Saturday, February 17
12:00 p.m. RESCHEDULED SCREENING
AMERICAN DREAM
Critics Choice: Great Documentaries
1991, 98 mins., 16mm. Directed by Cathy Caplan,
Thomas Haneke,
Barbara Kopple, Lawrence Silk. "In this searing,
probing film, Kopple examines the effects of corporate greed,
union politics, and the realities of putting bread on the table
in a small Minnesota town, which is torn apart by what turns
into a protracted strike at the Hormel meat-packing
plant."-Marshall Fine, Star

2:00 p.m.
Dennis Lim introduces
THE POWER OF NIGHTMARES: THE RISE OF THE POLITICS
OF FEAR
Critics Choice: Great Documentaries
2004, 180 mins., video. "This momentous essay on
the politics of fear parallels the rise of radical Islamists and
American neoconservatives: the mirror-image antagonists
who have long been allied in their hatred of Western liberalism and
their eagerness to politicize religion. Adam Curtis is a
historian of ideas who can bring them to life, not least because he
recognizes how they mutate, cross-pollinate, and succumb to
distortion. The myth-dependent "war on terror" needs its own brash
counter-narrative, and this is an invaluable start." -Dennis Lim

6:30 p.m.
F FOR FAKE
Repertory Nights
1974, 85 mins., 35mm. Orson Welles's playful con
game of a movie is about hoaxes, including Elmer de Hory's
forgeries of Picasso, Clifford Irving's "biography" of Howard Hughes,
and Welles's "War of the Worlds."
PLEASE NOTE: This is the only screening for F for
Fake. The February 16 screening has been cancelled.

=================================================================
Sunday, February 18
2:00 p.m.
AN AFTERNOON WITH RON VAN CLIEF
Fist & Sword Black History Month Celebration
Grandmaster Ron Van Clief, one of the first
African-American martial-arts film stars, will discuss his career
in a program accompanied by clips from his movies and TV shows
including:
Black Dragon, Way of the Black Dragon, Kung Fu
Fever, Bamboo Trap, Berry Gordy's The Last Dragon, Oz, Colorz
of Rage, The Sopranos, and Blazin. Van Clief has trained in
and taught martial arts for over 40 years and is a five-time World
Champion.
Moderated by Warrington Huldin, curator of the
Museum's monthly Fist & Sword program.
To view a NewyorkMag.com highlight, visit
http://nymag.com/listings/community/ron-van-clief/index.html
To view a story in the NY Daily News, visit
http://www.nydailynews.com/02-16-2007/boroughs/story/497894p-419709c.html
 
5:00 p.m.
Introduced by Joshua Rothkopf
LAKE OF FIRE
Critics Choice: Great Documentaries
2006, 152 mins., video. Courtesy of THINKFilm. "A
major event at last year's Toronto Film Festival, Tony Kaye's
staggering documentary on abortion in America is the
definitive statement on the subject. In production for more than sixteen
years and financed by the director, the film represents an
enormous act of social conscience, spanning from the historical
to the heartbreakingly personal. In its stark
cinematography and ominous orchestral scoring, it bears a strong resemblance
to Kaye's American History X. But his accommodation of
troubling details (including many harrowing shots of the procedure
itself) and eye-opening new interviews--one with "Jane Roe," now
an evangelical convert--reveal an investigatory spirit worthy of
the best journalism. Due for release in October 2007 from
THINKfilm, the
film will be presented in the same
work-in-progress cut that
stunned Toronto audiences." -Joshua Rothkopf,
Time Out New York

Monday-Friday, February 19-23: Family Programs
Museum open every day during Presidents Week
1:00 p.m.
"Catch the Motion" Workshop
Children learn the basic principles behind how
moving images move with a special emphasis on how motion-capture
techniques were used to make penguins dance in Happy Feet. 
 
1:30 p.m. 
HAPPY FEET
2006, 87 mins., 35mm., Warner Bros. Pictures.
Academy Award
Nominee for Best Animated Feature. Directed by
George Miller.
With the voices of Meryl Streep, Hugh Jackman,
Nicole Kidman,
Elijah Wood. With choreography by Savion Glover
and songs by
Prince, this animated musical about a lonely
penguin with an unappreciated talent for tap-dancing combines
dazzling photorealist landscapes, large-scale production
numbers, and inventive direction by George Miller, whose
eclectic filmography
includes Mad Max, Babe: Pig in the City, and
Lorenzo's Oil.

Saturday, March 10
1:00 p.m.
OFFSIDE
PREVIEW SCREENING
2006, 93 mins., 35mm print courtesy Sony Pictures Classics. A group of Iranian girls try to sneak into a soccer match even though they are forbidden by law to attend. Winner of the Silver Bear at the Berlin Film Festival, this richly metaphorical gem by Jafar Panahi (Crimson Gold, The White Balloon), was filmed during a real World Cup match.

3:00 p.m.
FLAGS OF OUR FATHERS
CLINT EASTWOOD'S IWO JIMA FILMS
2006, 132 mins., 35mm print courtesy Paramount Pictures. The first film released in Clint Eastwood's acclaimed diptych about the Battle of Iwo Jima examines the cost of war and the meaning of heroism from the perspective of the soldiers who were seen raising the American flag in the famous photograph. Also showing Sunday, March 11, 7:30 p.m.

6:00 p.m.
LETTERS FROM IWO JIMA
CLINT EASTWOOD'S IWO JIMA FILMS
2006, 141 mins., 35mm print courtesy Warner Bros. The counterpart to Flags of Our Fathers, which examines the Battle of Iwo Jima from the Japanese perspective, received Oscar nominations for Best Picture and Best Director. Also showing Sunday, March 11, 4:00 p.m.
