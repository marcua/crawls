Again, I don't know if this would be considered a "good" bar, but there is a bar on Hoyt Avenue South just west of 31st Street.   It is in the same block as Jimbo's, which faces Astoria Blvd., while this bar "Hot" something faces Hoyt Avenue South.   This place caters to a Latino audience and hasn't been open all that long. 

What interests me is the building itself.   For my of its life over the last 20 years it has been vacant, though another bar/restaurant tried to make a go of it and didn't last long.  I jogged one day and saw that the marshall's had seized and closed the place.    After another long period of no one in there, it is now open.   

I would love to know what the origin of the building is.  It looks like a building that housed a Con Ed power sub-staton or a Subway Power Station.  It has a unique shape and look.    I have no idea what its original use was, but I bet it is interesting.

I can actually recommend a pretty good little bar that I've actually been to more than a few times.   I think it is called "Pickwick" and it is two blocks west of the American Museum of the Moving Image on 35th Street.   When I first moved to Astoria and started to patronize AMMI for its weekend screenings the corner space was a deli run by an Irish woman.    The place went under -- back then that part of town was relatively austere -- and the space was empty.   Someone opened "Pickwick" and much to my surprise it has not only lasted, but seems to be thriving based on the crowds that have been in there when I've patronized the place.

They've got a huge number of beers on tap -- a dozen or more -- though no German beers last time I was in there much to my chagrin.   The food, while of the pub variety/quality, is pretty good.   The place hits the spot if I have time to kill between movies at AMMI or don't feel like cooking and want to knock back a few before heading home.
