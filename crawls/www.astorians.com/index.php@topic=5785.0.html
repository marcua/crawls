Ended up at Fattys- $11 for brunch and mimosa/limeade.
Had polenta cakes with provolone. Holy CRAP was it good. My veggie friend ordered the plaintain mash dish with cheese without pancetta, and it came with meat anyway. Our waitress was very understanding and sent it back to the kitchen asap. She offered my friend a mimosa for her trouble. Music slightly loud but not annoying. I'm definitely going again.

(Hey look! we're quoting each other!)

A little over a month ago I painted a bar mural at Bistro 33 (in the new location on 21st and Ditmars) and so have been watching as the whole process has been unfolding there. (Working with and around all of the different workers that have come in there to do different jobs to get it ready to open!) I haven't chatted with Gary for a few weeks, but I do know that they're having delays with the last bit of work getting done (I think things like the air vents were having setbacks). From what everyone says this is very very usual in the restaurant biz. There are just so many things that need to be done before a new restaurant can open. But it will open! I miss it too and I'm so eager to be able to go there again, and bring friends and family again! I'm sure we'll all get an email from them when it's ready to open, but if I hear anything before that I'll spread the good word  :roll:

The Creperie in Bistro's old spot ( 33-04 36th Ave) will be opening much sooner, perhaps even by the end of this week- I've also been chatting with Philippe (a TRUE foodie!) who is the owner of the new place and also the main chef. It will be called Cafe Triskell and will specialize in all different types of crepes (both sweet and with chicken/veggies etc). I'm so excited for them to open since I don't believe that I've found another really authentic Creperie in Astoria.

As far as Aces- the brunch thing is up in the air. I happened to meet one of the main chefs from Aces last weekend and he mentioned that he might be working on creating a brunch menu in the very near future for them...
