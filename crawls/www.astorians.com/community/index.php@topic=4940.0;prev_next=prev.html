Central heating/air doesn't refer to either gas or electric.  It can be either.  It simply refers to there being a heater in the house that send hot air through vents in the walls.  And central air there is usually a large airconditioning unit outside of the house, and sends cool air through the same vents as the heat travels through, thus heating or cooling every room in the house.  I believe central heat/air usually is only in smaller  mostly single family houses.  I don't think it's in too many apartment buildings.  But I suppose maybe brand new buildings might be installing it.

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_air

My mum's house in CA has central heating and air, and oh my goodness, how sweet it is!!!  The air conditioner runs on electricity which got expensive in the summer, but oh so worth it.  The heater runs on gas, and is definitely less expensive to run than the a/c.  But it would heat or cool our big ole house in no time.  I miss its most alluring feature - its adjustability (unlike the world's most ridiculous radiators in my apartment here.  When we asked the super how to adjust the heat, he looked at us like we were crazy and said, "uh, you just open a window."  How helpful.)  The window air conditioners here just don't cut it when you're used to central air.  I am dreading summer.

Hope you get to experience the magic of central heating and air, Daisy!!
