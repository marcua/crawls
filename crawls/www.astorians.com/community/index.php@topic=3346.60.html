Anyone know the one about the retired moyle and the tailor?

In a heavy Yiddish accent (think George Burns and Tevya).

SO a retired moyle takes a lifetimes worth of foreskin in a basket to the tailor and says, "tailor, ive been a moyle now for 60 yrs. I'm retired today and Ive brought this to you to make me a special special gift for my retirement."

The tailor looks at the forskin and the old man and says, "come back in a month and I'll have a beautiful gift for you!"

A month goes by and the excited moyle makes his way back to the tailor.  "Tailor" he says "what have you made for me?!?!".

Tailor reaches under the counter and pulls out a carved box with gold and silver inlay, he solemnly presents it to the retired old moyle and smiles.

The moyle eyes gleaming and moist at such a gift takes it in his shaking hands and opens it.  There nestled in a bed of blue satin is a lovely little wallet.  The moyle looks at the tailor dissappointed and upset, he says "tailor, whats this? I must have given you 30 lbs of forskin and all you have for me is a wallet?!?!"

The tailor smiles and says "yes, but when you rub it, it turns into a steamer trunk!"
