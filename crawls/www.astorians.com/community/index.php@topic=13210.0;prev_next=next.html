Here is the copy of the release the coalition for a better astoria sent out today.  We will continue to fight to ensure the community's voice is heard and that this land becomes green space. 


** For Immediate Release**
Coalition for a Better Astoria Applauds Con Edison Announcement Not to Sell Land to Fed Ex Developer

Contact: Costa Constantinides
Chair-Coalition for a Better Astoria
917-716-4540
11/25/2008

The Coalition for a Better Astoria, a group consisting of the Astoria Homeowners, Tenants, and Business Association, Greenshores NYC, Astoria Park Alliance, Long Island City Alliance, Democracy for NYC Astoria Linkup, the Queens Independent Living Center, the Astoria and LIC Waterfront Alliance, CHOKE, Greek Homeowners Association, Eleftheria Pancyprian Youth, Long Island City Rock, the Tri-State Transportation Campaign and other concerned citizens applauds Con Edison's decision to suspend plans to sell 22-acres of land in Astoria to developer Steel Equities to create a major FedEx distribution center. 

“This plan was deeply flawed from the start and a direct threat to our quality of life” said Costa Constantinides, chair of the Coalition for a Better Astoria, “we applaud Con Edision for adhering to the wishes of the community and pledge to continue to work to see this land is developed in adherence with the community's wishes as a park”.

"This is a tremendous victory for the residents of Astoria and for the city as a whole," says Kyle Wiswall, general counsel of the Tri-State Transportation Campaign. "The sale was a raw deal for the neighborhood, sending hundreds of exhaust-spewing trucks into the streets.  Hopefully the city is taking note that citizens demand rational planning and development that improves our quality of life."

The Coalition for a Better Astoria thanks Councilmember Peter Vallone Jr, Assemblyman Mike Gianaris, State Senator George Onorato, Congresswoman Carolyn Maloney and all of the groups and individuals who stood firm against this sale.  It will continue to advocate for this land to be transformed into green space for the community to enjoy.
