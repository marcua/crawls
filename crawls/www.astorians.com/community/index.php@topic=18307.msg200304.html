I thought I'd give my two cents on this as well.  A lot of this has already been said, but what the heck.

Went last night with my wife.  I had the classic burger and she had the Italian turkey burger.  Both were very good.  My burger was perfectly cooked (medium rare) and very flavorful.  I wiped off a lot of the mayo sauce.  It was tasty, but there was just too much on it.  The caramelized onions were awesome.  Just sweet enough without being overbearing.  Overall the toppings did a great job of complimenting the meat without hiding the burger's flavor.  I did put a little salt on the burger, but that's just me wanting to boost the flavor a bit.  The one downside of the burger was the bun.  I think the bun is a bit too big and not quite soft enough.  For me, a bun is a delivery method and shouldn't require too much attention.

My wife's turkey burger was good.  I only had one bite and it was very flavorful.  I'm not a fan of the toppings that come on it, but she was very pleased.

The fries have been somewhat controversial here.  I personally love this type of fry (McDonald's style).  My wife's were a bit more well done than mine, and she was kind enough to swap with me - I like crispy fries.

We drank only water as the beer was more expensive than I would have liked (and I already had a headache).  Speaking of headaches, I was really worried reading about the music volume...it was louder than your average restaurant, but not nearly as bad as I had feared.  Any bar would have been far worse.

In all, 2 burgers and tap water came to $35 with tip.  More than I'd like to spend on a hamburger meal, but if you think of it as a nicer meal than your average burger, then it's not too bad.

One thing I'd like is for them to add outdoor seating.  They said they are considering it for the fall.  Said it's too warm outside right now (though last night was perfect out) and that the permit takes a while to get.  I'm sure the latter is the major reason.

Overall...the service was fantastic, the food was very good, and I'm pleased that this is in the neighborhood.  It won't be an every day spot, but it will enter the regular rotation.

Had to find this topic because I finally went to 5 Napkin last Friday night after wanting to for months.  They must have tweaked the menu some because both the girlfriend and I got the original and it was $10.95 with no sides.  This was good actually because we were able to share an order of fries rather than each getting our own.

The burger I have to say may be the best burger I have ever had and with the high expectations I had going in really made me happy.  Mine was cooked medium.  I literally had to stop eating after the first bite and compose myself.  Also, I ate my burger with my hands.  What does that say about me?  :-D  We had the Tuscan fries (parmesan and herbs) which were good, not outstanding.  I'd like to try their sweet potato fries next time.  We also each had a wheat beer (forgot which one) which was only $5.50 which is actually cheaper than most places I go that charge no less than $6 for a draft.  Our total cost with tax/tip was $48 which for a high quality burger and a drink seemed reasonable even in Astoria.  Will definitely be back.

Oh and my girlfriend noticed at a table behind me a guy was eating the burger for 2 himself and he had a date with him.  We didn't stay long enough to see if he finished it.
