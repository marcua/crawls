Went here again (I try to go as often as I can, as I am a fan) for brunch on Sunday and tried the cheese grits as a side--hands down best grits I have ever had.

I think I should note to some here that the owner/chef trained under (I BELIEVE) the same head chef who began the Nobu restaurants; therefore, the cuisine is exactly what the menu seems to scream: Japanese/French fusion. In other words, I wouldn't really go here to order a BURGER. Don't get me wrong, I'm not defending the mistakes--a burger is easy as pie to make so it definitely shouldn't be coming out of the kitchen in a near non-edible state. However, I urge some of you who may have had bad experiences to go back and try some of the more different items on the menu. For instance, I am not usually a fan of grits but I opted to try their cheese grits and like I said: FANTASTIC! Even my mom agreed that they were some of the best she'd ever had.

I will say that, for the first time, I saw a TON of babies and strollers. I love children but I don't know how this place became a mecca for them; it's so tiny! Some of the kids that were there were running around the restaurant, bumping into other chairs, knocking coats over, and putting their hands all over the windows and doors.
