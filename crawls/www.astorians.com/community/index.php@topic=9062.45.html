Hi All.  Long time reader, not too many posts, but my Husband and I ate at Mojave last night, so I thought I'd post my review.
We arrived around 8:30, and the place was about 60% full.  We were seated against the wall, and I agree- the bench is a few inches too high, which was a little awkward.  They just need thinner cushions.
Unlike some others, our service wasn't too bad.  Our waiter was a bit harried, but polite, and our busboy/water guy was *very* attentive (my water glass was never more than 1/2 empty).
We must have been a lucky table, because we did receive free nacho chips with 2 types of salsa on out table immediately (the red one was limey and delicious).
My husband drank beer, but I had 2 cocktails: the Agave Margarita (on the rocks), which was good but too sweet for me, and the Hot & Spicy, which is a jalapeno infused tequila margarita. YUM. This was an A+ drink for me, I love spicy food and it was served with sliced jalapenos. We ordered the buffalo oysters and guacamole for apps.  The sauce I thought was a little overpowering for the oysters, but the texture was beautiful and they were yummy nonetheless.  I agree with the other poster who said order your guac sans sourcream- we had about 2 tablespoons of guacamole and a cup each of sour cream and tomatoes.  The guacamole itself was good, but not great (I make my own though and am admittedly a guac snob).
For entrees, my husband ordered the crab quesadillas and I had the short ribs.  My dish was wonderful and huge and he ended up eating half of it!  The quesadillas were good too- very full of crab meat and very, very rich.
We were given a dessert menu, but were too full (big shock from all of the food we ordered).
Overall, it was a nice night out and we will be back.  They need to work out some kinks, which I'm sure they will do.  I will definitely be back with some girlfriends to enjoy more Hot & Spicys :)

I also ate at Mojave this weekend.. and was thrilled.. Its a great addition to Astoria and a nice deviation from the typical greek, sushi and italian places. I got there earlier than my friend, so I sat at the "bar" which I would not recommend. the bartender didn't seem to want me there- not even asking if he could get me something until I asked for a drink menu.. the so called bar is also where the waitstaff get their drinks and submit orders.. its not a bar that you would want to sit in... but the meal more than made up for it. Once my friend arrived, we sat promptly in the cozy restaurant.. and ordered.. Yes, we were greeted with nachos and two salsa options.
The corn crusted calamari is the best i have ever had.. it actually beats out any Italian-Style fried calamari that i have ever had. it melted in your mouth, as opposed to being over cooked and chewy.. for being fried, it was light and crisp.
I had the fajitas and while they didnt come out "sizzling", they were hot and tasty.. The beef could have been cooked more, the standard is medium, where i prefer well done but the meal was delicious. the flavor was wonderful, the tortillas were light and served warm.
my friend had the tuna and enjoyed it.
I def recommend Mojave and will be going back... Try it out.  :mrgreen:

This place is very nice.  I just went to eat this evening.  I was seated right away and the place quickly started to fill up with people.  The blue chips are like an appetizer.  My sister and I had sangria to start.  Then we both ordered the corn on the cob.  This was lovely.  It was grilled just right.  I ordered the jumbo shrimp and my sister had the steak tacos.  Both dishes were great.

We decided to have a dessert.  We ordered the wonderful chocolate molten cake with banana ice cream.  YUMMY!  They left a nice amount of time before serving the dessert.  The chocolate cake also takes a little time to prepare.

I would definately recommend it!
