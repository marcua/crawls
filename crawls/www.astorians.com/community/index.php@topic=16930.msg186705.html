Sorry you don't like my take on that topic, but I'll stick with it.  When I say "older" I mean older than one's twenties ... not solely the senior citizens you're thinking I'm characterizing that way.  And you're taking me out of context -- my overall point being, a lot of property-owning native Astorians are being led to believe this is a wondrous thing going on that their property values have grown exponentially in the past decade.  In a very base, surface-value way, yes, ka-ching, but there are two greater, long-term realities at play that they may not be looking at in this scenario.  And this is, property values are meaningless to the property owner unless he sells his property (i.e., moves) and makes a small fortune  And when he moves, I can just about guarantee you, you're going to find people who have lived decades reasonably and affordably, understand that way of life, thrust into new, more moneyed living environments that they don't particularly like, and the over-riding theme of their lives will become: "Why did I ever leave that place?  It was home."  As I noted, the types of property-owning neighbors they'll have in 2020 or 2030, that's going to be a whole different class of people, and I strongly suspect there'll be a "damned if you do/damned if you don't" sense of going on living here with that more working-class mindset -- it will be like a neutron bomb going off, leaving the physical lay of the land as it was, only replacing everyone who had been there with people espousing completely different ways of life.  The only winners will be people who don't mind spending $800K on a deeply average two-story row house with thin walls and floors.  (I realize $800K is a little high right now for a two-story rowhouse, but that will be a steal five yeasr from now.)

I'm more with the various people who have posted here espousing what they've loved about growing up and living here for decades -- that stuff is great reading.  I can only hope a lot of these people own their properties and hang in there as long as they can.  But the "between the lines" message all of them impart is that they, too, sense that selling the one place in the world you call home for a fortune is a dream come true, with a lot of hidden strings attached.

As for the quality of posts here, man, let it ride, this place is for opinions ... which for the most part have been watered down and homogenized due to this inane "nod" system, i.e., people afraid of offending each other.  I'd rather read genuine thoughts and emotions than people walking on eggshells.
