Advice you probably already know on buying things off of Astorians.com
« on: June 06, 2010, 01:26:39 PM »
Here's my story if it can help someon else though right now, I'm just feeling pretty stupid.  I saw an air conditioner advertised on Astorians.com.  I made contact.  The young woman seemed so nice.  She even had her moving man bring the a.c. to me since my arm was injured.  I gave her $100 in cash & we said goodbye.  I then turned on the air conditioner and it seemed to work fine . . . for 4 minutes (okay, maybe it was 5 minutes) and then it just died.  I tried everything, read the instruction manual front to back, had a handyman look at it.  It's just dead.  Nothing wrong with my electrical outlets either.  So now I have no a.c. and I'm out $100.  When I contacted the seller, she implied that I was lying about it not working.  In case you're just as stupid and trusting as I am, here's my advice.  If you buy anything electric, insist on using it first and for at least 15 minutes before handing over cash for it no matter how nice the seller seems.
