It may be my personal opinion but I consider both the Daily News and The NY Post to be tabloids, somewhere rooted in real news, but exaggerated and blown way out of proportion for shock value.   I have a funny story related to this about this guy I used to see in the Park every day as I went to work.  He was cute and we would exchange glances every morning.  But as time went by, it dawned on me this guy was sitting there for 3 hours at a time reading the NY Post or the Daily News, and suddenly I lost interest.  To this day, I have colleagues tease me over this.  But I can't help that my perception of someone dropped over his choice of reading material.   :lol:

Actually, the New York Post proudly considers itself to be a tabloid.  Tabloid just means a condensed newspaper, with sensational reporting.  "Tabloids" aren't necessarily exclusive to the kind of papers that feature stories about Elvis being alive and are sold in the aisle of the supermarket.  And the pages read like a book, rather than the "broadsheet" format favored by the New York Times, for example.  The Post is also America's oldest newspaper. 

I find the Post to be a great paper.  The local coverage is second to none, and they are much more up front about their political slant, instead of trying to bury it within ostensibly objective journalism.  While the stories are sensational, you actually learn the key aspects of the news.  So what if you're entertained while doing it?

If I want a paper with stylistic quality, and lengthy articles about sometimes interesting and arcane subjects, I will go to the NY Times.  But for a quick read on the news of the day, I go to the Post.

I wouldn't lose interest in someone for reading the Post.  But I would lose interest in someone who spent three hours a day reading papers when they should be working.
