Looking for roommate for 2 BR in Astoria at 46th/Broadway M/R stop
« on: March 06, 2013, 02:49:50 PM »
28 y/o professional looking to find male or female roommate for 2B, 1 bath apartment for May move in.  The apt is brand new and completely refurbished - all new appliances (including dishwasher), wood floors, tiled kitchen, central air conditioning.  I have pictures of the apt, building, and neighborhood.  The apt is currently being refurbished but you can get a sense of the layout.

The owner owns several other buildings along the same street and lives across the street and two doors down.  Rent is $2000/mo ($1000 each), with a security deposit of one month's rent and a negotiable broker fee of one month.

It's a half-block/30 second walk to the M/R subway stop at 46th St. and Broadway in Astoria, Queens.  If you work in Manhattan, this is extremely convenient for the price.  It is 15 minutes once you're on the subway from the stop to the 53rd and 5th stop in Midtown.  The line bisects the 4/5/6 for an easy transfer at 53rd and Lex or 59th and Lex, and then continues down the middle of the island.  It's also an easy transfer to the E to reach the west side A/C/E line.

Pets are fine with a pet deposit.  It's a three story building, with a 2 bedroom apt on each floor, each with nearly identical layouts.   
 
The neighborhood is nice and quiet - a couple of bars/lounges, thai and chinese food, donut shop, cafes, health market, various bodegas, barbershops, the usual.  There's also a large 24 hour gym one subway stop/ten minute walk away, a movie theater one stop away, and pretty much anything else you'd need at the Steinway St. stop.

I'm friendly, neat, easy to get along with, non-smoker/drinker (but you do what you want), work full-time in Midtown, recent law school graduate, big sports fan, very sociable.  I'm looking for a relatively quiet atmosphere at home but I'm flexible and won't be there all that much during the week.  I have some furniture for the common area, but aside from the TV and couch, I don't have to keep it if you have stuff you'd rather put in there.  If you're a female and are concerned about living with a male roommate, I currently live with a female and she can provide a reference if you'd like.  I'm cool with pets but don't have any.  Obviously, plans change, but ideally, I'm looking for a longer-term roommate who plans to live/work in the city for a few years and isn't planning on moving. Anything I've missed, feel free to ask.
