(I know this is my first post, but I have nothing to do with Pistilli aside from living in the building.)


I have had a positive experience with the building, and less issues than with other apartments I have lived in. Maintenance requests are filled very quickly, and my floor is fairly quiet. The location is great if you like the park and don't mind walking 10 minutes to the subway. The park can get loud during the summer (especially at night time with the cars). Doormen are always there.


This windows sometimes leak (i.e., the window ledge was damp after Sandy), but they have fixed that issue with water coming in through the walls at least in my apartment.


***Oh, I wanted to add that I wouldn't buy there until they resolve their issues with the land lease.

I live in Riverview East and have since it first opened. We were fortunate to never have experienced any of the issues that others have posted about (leaks, etc).  Most of those building issues have been resolved as far as I know.  I very much enjoy living here, always have.  It's family friendly, right on the park and the apartments are huge.  We've made several very good friends with our neighbors here.  I love that there is laundry on every floor and a fitness room upstairs and that my balcony overlooks the park.  It is a bit far from the subway/stores and the park can be noisy in the summer, but that's only an issue if your apt faces the park.  And the other three seasons are very quiet and peaceful.  I can't speak to the Pistilli's management with their other developments, but they have been pretty hands on with addressing problems that have come up here.  They are most definitely not slumlords in this building.

OK this is my last comment re: Riverview.  It's pretty amusing how much interest this building gets with so many haters out there. 

As someone who lives here and not just someone who knows someone who know someone who lives here, I'll say that the apts are remarkably soundproofed.  It was one of the biggest and most pleasant surprises after moving in.  My neighbors could be having a raging party and I would only hear a hint of music.  The front doors are not as soundproofed.  If someone walks by your door talking, you can pretty much hear every word.

Two or three summers ago there were crazy loud parties being held on shore blvd.  when I walked onto my balcony it sounded like I had stepped into a dance club (with really bad music).  No amount of sound proofed windows could help with that.  The last two summers I have not noticed the loud parties.  Maybe cuz they've toned down or maybe because now I have small children and I am conked out in the middle of the night when the noise used to start. 

Contrary to local folklore, to my knowledge there aren't issues with
mold on the ext of the building, bedbugs, rodents and as I've said the leaks have been tackled. Our windows don't leak and never have.   I am no huge fan of the pistillis, they move at the pace of snails in addressing issues and their taste is.... Well, the building's design speaks for itself.  But for a young family that needs a good amount of space in an affordable and nice location, this is a pretty good choice.  I'm sure there are some apts that may be "lemons" with issues - it is a big building.  It's not a utopia and there are issues, just like with any other large development.  But I love our apt.  The super is awesome. And I love our neighbors - that goes a long, long way in dealing with any other annoyances.
