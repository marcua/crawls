Lamont came from far down in the polls as an unknown, riding Democratic (and some independent) voter anger at Lieberman.  I think now that he's the Democratic nominee, he well may be able to beat Lieberman in a three way race (especially given the Republican candidate being very disorganized and being attacked from within his own party over gambling issues).  Lieberman might well cost Democrats picking up the three contested House seats (his campaign manager refused to say if he would be supporting the Democratic challengers or the Republican incumbents), but I don't think it's a foregone conclusion that he's going to coast to re-election.  Keep in mind what happened in NY in 1986 when incumbent Republican US Senator Javitz lost his primary to Alfonse D'Amato.  He kept running on the Liberal line and got creamed.  I think it will take a few days to see what the new dynamic will be in Connecticut, but I think this race, coupled with the defeats of a number of other incumbents shows that voters are angry and taking it out on people currently in office.
