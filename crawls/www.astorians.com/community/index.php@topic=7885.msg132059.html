Quote from: alia on June 07, 2008, 01:59:57 PM
don't worry. all you *really* need is diapers and air conditioning. :>
Gee, have things changed that much since I gave birth almost 4 decades ago? Sure diapers and air conditioning were needed, but they certainly didn't feel like they were exclusives.

Now let's see if I remember that joyful period . . . ahh, yes . . . lots of caffeine was also needed. If you're lucky enough to fall asleep, the baby passing wind in another room sounds like an approaching tornado and will wake you in a NYC second.

Moms are needed too; yours, his, anyones who can take a few hours to come over and help a bit. A cook. Yeah, someone who not only likes to cook, but has the time and energy to cook some healthy and tasty food to help sustain your life while you try to make it through the baby's first 3 months of trying to communicate with the outside world! A part-time secretary would also be a great asset at that time. Someone needs to get those thank you cards written and posted ASAP and you sure as hell won't have either the time or the inclination. Please, whatever you do, don't forget to have a supply of chocolate in the house before you go into labor. You're going to be routinely craving it after every feeding. Don't be cheap, either. Buy yourself the good quality kind. And, you will also need a good package of flash cards. Yes, that is what I said, flash cards! If you are lucky enough to still have the energy to get out of the house for say, just a walk, you don't want to waste your energy answering all those dumb questions . . . that's where the flash cards come in. "Yes, he does have a lot of hair, doesn't he." "No, I don't think he is cold without a hat." "I actually wasn't thinking Harvard or Yale, I was kind of imagining Princeton." "Yes, the (dog(s)/cat(s) really like the baby and no I am not planning on getting rid of either." Same questions, different annoying people. And if you can arrange it, you will also need a good friend willing to do your mani/pedi so that you can feel almost human again. Try to get yourself one of those Hollywood personal trainers, that come over to your house when you call them, so that you won't have to deal with feeling lazy about trying to lose the weight that helped the baby be born so healthy. Last thing I vividly remember needing . . . someone to tell you every day how beautiful you look, how perfect you and the baby look together; someone who wants nothing more than to hold you in their strong, masculine arms while you nurse, or simply nap; who will wake you with a snack that you didn't have the strength to make for yourself; someone who will protect you from all the incoming calls that you would rather not have to answer; someone who will only allow visitors when they are there to be a buffer between you and the things you know are so much more important than company and the same someone who will photographically document every second of the the entire first year of your baby's life.

You know, diapers and air conditioning is not a bad place to start!
