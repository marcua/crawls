I am not opposed to biking as a green alternative.  But it is curious to me why there isn't equal attention paid to increasing and improving our mass transit system.  After all, isn't that the most green infrastructure already in place?  And personally, I'm a big walker.  I'm happy with these new streets-turned-pedestrian plazas.  I'd like to see more of them.  And they are also often shared with a painted bike lane, as they should be.  Even one on Shore Blvd, and other places in Astoria, would be nice  We sold our car after living a while in Astoria.  We find we can get around nicely on foot, by subway or bus, or the occasional cab.  It's actually very liberating.  City biking isn't my thing.  Just a matter of taste.  For me, biking is enjoyable in rural settings.  My point - probably poorly stated, was that I feel there has been a push to increase bike lanes and such, but not enough attention to widening and repairing sidewalks.  Not enough attention to painting safe crosswalks.
