I have not posted in a long time but this is making me pretty incensed. I cannot be the only person who notices all the yuppies in Astoria bringing their kids...and often BABIES into restaurants.  Everyone always bitches about the stroller moms and their bugaboos in Park Slope and the like- a problem in Manhattan as well, but not as much- (parents who feel guilty about getting a babysitter since they work all the time and take their kids to highly inappropriate places like expensive restaurants). Why is this ugly trend seeping into Astoria? 2 weeks I have gone into 2 different restaurants with my friend here in Astoria.  One is a WINE bar- where somoene came in with a 3 year old on a Friday night at like 9 pm.  1) Its a WINE bar- why would you bring a kid there? its not fair to other patrons OR the child  2) Shouldnt the kid be in BED at 9 pm?  Just putting it out there.  This was Winegasm, by the way.
Last night I was in another place in Astoria where a couple came in with a BABY. Yes baby. I don't even think a year old.  They proceeded to put the couple and the baby in the corner and I noticed no one sat near them.  That place also has a bar and is an ADULT restaurant.
Am I crazy here? Does anyone remember the days where parents were sane and left kids home with the babysitter, friends, relatives, etc. while they went to restaurants?  My parents would have never dreamed of taking us to places like that- its no fun for a child, the hours they are going to these places are ridiculous and if the kid gets fussy (understandably) - they piss the other diners off. Why would you do this? I feel like they are trying to prove something- like its possible to have it all? Well, fyi... it is not possible to "have it all" in a wine bar if that includes your two year old sticking his/her fingers in the brie on the fruit and cheese plate.
This is why Applebee's and TGIFridays are around. Two words: kid friendly.
