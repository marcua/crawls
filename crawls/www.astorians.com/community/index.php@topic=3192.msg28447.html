Quote from: erpotown on August 30, 2006, 01:14:45 PM
don't consider it as bad as the renovations that have been going on at the 35th and Madison NYSC bathrooms/showers for the past three months.

I was just there last night!  And I know what you're saying, being a guy, it meant I had to use the normal "ladies" lockerroom and couldn't shower.  What's really odd is that when you use the renovated men's locker room, which took them about two months, they don't seem to have done too much -- only retiling the shower area.  Hard to believe it took that long to do so little.  I gather the same is being done to the ladies' side now.

At this point, I'm used to using liberal amounts of Baby Wipes on those non-shower days, but I sure wish this would end.  If it's any solace, there were construction guys working overtime on the ladies side yesterday, which may signal that things are winding up soon.  I'm only in there one day a week, so I'm not as angry as I would be if that was my main gym.

Re: "NYSC at 34th and 6th is a gross dump of a gym and I couldn't be paid to go there."

Well, you're missing out on an amazing view you get when walk out on the veranda they have on whatever floor that is (25th?) -- you get an amazing view of midtown, and it's very relaxing on a summer's evening after a hard workout to go out there and cool down for a few minutes.  Granted, the gym itself kind of blows -- way too cramped -- but that veranda is a real plus, like having your own luxury apartment view for a few mintues every time you use it.

As noted in another thread, been to at least 20 branches.  The worst?  For me, it's the one on Broadway and about 74th (or so ... across from Fairway market).  People there are fine, it's clean, nothing technically wrong with the gym ... save it's the size of a broom closet!  Literally the smallest health club I've ever been in.  I gather it's some sort of stop-gap gym between the one on CPW and 73rd (or 74th), and the one on Broadway just up the block from Zabar's.  Frankly, their upper west side gyms in general are pretty bad -- cramped, and often over-crowded during rush hours.  The one on Broadway and 97th is another way-too-small gym.

There used to be one on 46th between Madison 5th that was sort of divey, but it closed.  (Got athletes foot there three times -- the only times in my life.)  Since I had a regular boxing class there, though, I went weekly, no matter what.  And I hate to say it, but the gem in their crown, the big gym in Grand Central, tends to be over-crowded (nearly impossible over lunch), the class schedule is lousy, and the damn building shakes from the traffic on that Grand Central overpass going right by the window.

But, again, allow me to reiterate -- for all my whining, I'm still going to stick with these guys.  Most of the gyms are reasonably well kept, they're located all over the place, and going there numerous times a week is a mainstay in my life I have no intention of giving up, no matter how much I grind my teeth when I get my monthly credit card bill.
