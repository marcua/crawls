Warning to Lurkers on this Awesome Message Board
« on: August 20, 2008, 11:27:42 AM »
I moved to Astoria in 2007 after lurking on this board for almost a year.  This board seemed so cool, diverse, and funny.  The members seemed engaged with the neighborhood and the world.   If the real neighborhood was even remotely like the online community here, then I had high hopes that I'd find a happy niche for myself in Astoria.

Well, flash forward to now.  How do I feel about Astoria, the neighborhood in Western Queens?   DO. NOT. WANT.   Limited finances are keeping me here for the time being, but I'm plotting my escape as I type this. 

I'm in the Broadway area.  It's serviceable but nothing great.

Random things that turn me off:

- It's ugly.  If you've been anywhere on the planet outside of NYC, and you're being honest, you know how unattractive this part of Queens is.  The utilitarian architecture is a downer.   All the red brick is overwhelming.   I thought I could get over it, and the neighborhood would offer other positives that would compensate for the ugliness.  I was wrong.

- Ignorant, racist neighbors who talk about the 30-something cleaning person in my building as the "boy" and on two occasions used an ugly ethnic slur in reference to him.   Cannot stand the Archie Bunker vibe some of the residents put out.

- Lack of trees on sidewalks.  Air quality is poor.   And wow, I can walk to parks or outdoor exhibits 30 minutes to an hour away?  How convenient ... not!

- For all the food talk on this board, the restaurant scene is lame.  The high-end places are mediocre.  The budget places aren't as exciting or yummy as food I've had in other parts of New York or even in Queens - like Sunnyside, Jackson Heights or Elmhurst.

- The elevated train makes the neighborhood look drab.  (How does Chicago pull it off?)

- Nosy neighbors who keep track of my coming and going and ask me about it, but don't engage in conversation that's pleasant or sincere.

- Local businesses that don't deliver services as promised and lie! lie! lie!  Boulevard fricking Decor, I'm looking at you. 


To READERS of this message board - If this message board is part of your decision-making process to move to Astoria, here's a tip:  lower your expectations.  Lower them drastically.  Take hold of the hype and all the Astoria-love floating on this board and regard with great skepticism.

Hi there, Rueful Rose. While I'm sure your time in Astoria has been genuinely unpleasant, it sounds like you have been very unlucky in your experiences regarding your living situation, and you shouldn't let that reflect on Astoria as a whole. We're not delusional when we say we like it here, so try to keep an open mind.

It's true that much of the architecture here, and in Queens in general, is ugly. This is why blogs like the Queens Crapper exist. We don't have the lovely brownstones that exist in other boroughs. A lot of the stuff here looks slapped together without any aesthetic consideration.

As for ignorant, racist neighbors, I do not have any of these. I'm sorry that you got crappy, racist, nosy neighbors. They are not special to Astoria.

Lack of trees - yes, there could be more trees. The air quality is not great. The city likes to build power plants here, and sometimes the whole area smells like sewage, which we have discussed on the board.

Mediocre food - I have to disagree. Not all the restaurants here are great, and everyone's taste differs, but we do have some fine offerings both cheap and expensive. I hope you've at least tried some of the better Greek restaurants, because that's what Astoria is known for.

Although it's kind of ugly and rickety sounding, I like the elevated train because I prefer being outside in most cases to being in the stuffy and smelly subway stations, and I like looking out the window while riding. Maybe I like it more on Ditmars because it's the last stop and it offers a nice view.

No, Astoria isn't utopia. It has its problems just like every other place. But I do really like living in Astoria, and my expectations aren't low. Western Queens isn't glamorous. I find living here to be practical and enjoyable, and I love the fact that I can get almost everything I need on a day-to-day basis within just 3-4 blocks. That's what I wanted. If my biggest concern was to live on beautiful tree-lined streets with lovely architecture, I would go to Brooklyn.

Rueful_Rose,

I find it curious that you have been lurking on the boards for awhile, and have lived in the area since 2007 but chose this to be your first posting (on the same day you registered no less).  Was there a specific event that compelled you to diss the area where you've lived for awhile now?  I'm curious...

I've been in Astoria for 11 years now and love leaving Manhattan behind and coming to what feels like a real neighbourhood in the Ditmars area.  Sure the architecture isn't the nicest but We like our neighbours, have trees in front of our house and, as someone else said, most everything we could ever need is within a few block radius.  We are also close enough to Manhattan that it is possible to come home after work, shower and change and return to Manhattan in a relatively short amount of time, provided the MTA is playing nice.  If you hate the Broadway area so much maybe you should check out other areas of Astoria.  Ditmars is a completely different animal than the Broadway area and may be more your speed as far as conveniences go.

We were in Chicago last year and the El train there is much worse to deal with than it is here.  At least in NYC the MTA runs most of the time and at faster speeds.  I actually like the train being above ground.  After living through events such as Sept 11th and the blackout it's actually comforting to know that it would be much easier to evacuate the train if it was above ground in Astoria than in a tunnel anywhere else.

As for not being happy here, I'm sorry to hear that and can only say that I hope you gave our little 'hood a fair chance and do find happiness wherever you end up. However, if that is in another area of NYC I can guarantee that there will be just as many negative attributes as there are here.
