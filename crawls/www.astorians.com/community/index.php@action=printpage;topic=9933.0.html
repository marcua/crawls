Title: Re: Stroller Survey
Post by: eblack on March 27, 2008, 11:49:59 PM
When our baby was first born we had a Graco car seat that snaps into the stroller bottom. It was handy and easy to fold up, once you figured it out.

We've got a Maclaren that I like a lot.  It's very common in the city, because it's reasonably priced (little over $100), relatively light, folds up easily and has storage underneath.  There are definitely higher end models out there, but ours has held up pretty well over the past year or so.

However, you might not have the same needs as we do.  We don't have a car, so we're lifting the stroller up stairs to the subway all the time.

Post by: danigirl on March 28, 2008, 08:21:49 AM
Congratulations!

When I had my son (almost 8 years ago) I had a pricy Peg Pergo.  It was nice, had the ability to lie him flat  in the stroller so I could use it while he was an infant.  However, it was big, wide & heavy!! I soon leaned that it was not that practical to walking around and frequent stores that are small.  I used it for a few months and then got a smaller Peg Pergo (I think it was called Aria).  That was much easier it use around Astoria & it was much lighter, easier to fold, etc.

Now with my daughter, I used the large Peg Pergo that I had used with my son but I only used it for a few months.  Once she was able to sit up, I bought a lightwight Maclaran.  So much better than the light weight Peg Perego (which I had gotten rid of because it did not hold up well after using it for 3+ years with my son).  I still use the Maclaren, it is light, so easy to steer (even with one hand!).  It folds very easy.  The only minus that I found is that the basket is a little small.

Hope that helps!!

Post by: sweetlilyg on March 30, 2008, 04:14:37 PM
I used a Combi City Savvy for the first 18 months.  It's very similar to the MacClaren already discussed, but you are able to snap in the infant seat when they are very little, and also fully reclines (I don't think the Mac does, but I'm not sure).  It's pretty cheap (around $100).
It was great because it is so light- I could easily lift it up subway stairs- and manuevers fairly well.  However, I beat on it- we walk a ton and the thing was pretty busted up in 18mos.  Also, I am 5'8", and I wish the handles came up a bit higher- I found it to be a little rough on my back.
Recently, I purchased a City Mini by baby jogger, and so far I love it.  It's a three-wheel type, but has the plastic wheels, so it's not technically a jogging stroller (they call it "all terrain").  The thing handles like a dream (haha I feel like I'm talking about a ferrari) and folds incredibly easily with one hand.  It's still fairly light, and I can get it up the stairs of the subway, but with a 25 pound kid in there, I try not to!
I have a lot of Mommy friends in the area now, and I'd say 90% started with either the MacClaren or the Combi, and most have purchased some kind of three wheel model after a year or so.  :-)
