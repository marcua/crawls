I'm not clear on how the pool works. Even if it is free, don't people need to have registered with the pool or have an I.D. card? I was a lifeguard at a pool on Long Island, and an open pool policy like this was dangerous in case somethng happened to one of the kids. Although we didn't have a fee for open swim, the kids needed to register.

I think this would be helpful as there would be a record of who comes to the pool. If they could swipe an I.D. card, the lifeguards would know who was there, if a child was injured, there would be a record somewhere of emergency contact info, etc. This may sound a little "big brother is watching" but so are a lot of cops standing around, which I agree, is a bit of an eyesore.

Also, I think that registering may prevent people from "crashing" over to the pool, and instead "planning" to go for a swim. Certainly seems like a better solution that giving up on it, or expecting that just because we don't live in park slope or cobble hill that we should expect this behavior.
