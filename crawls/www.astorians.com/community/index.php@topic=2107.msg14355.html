Ooh, me, I will!!!  Ok, so I don't actually have it, but based on my affection for his baking book, I can confidently recommend I'm Just Here for the Food.  I will be requesting it for Christmas this year (thanks for reminding me that I've been wanting it).  He explains EVERYTHING including all the science behind the cooking processes, gives you wonderful recipes with which to illustrate what he's discussed (a "master" recipe, and then variations), and there is a chapter or two about basic cooking equipment you should have (he even goes into brand names, at least he did in the baking one).  Plus, it's just really fun to read.  I've never read a cookbook like a novel, but I found the baking one to be hard to put down.  As for which version, the "Director's Cut" one sounds pretty cool.  Extra recipes, more information, and bonus feature-type stuff.  It comes out in October.  The original version did just fine on its own, though, so I guess that would be up to you.  Just my opinion.  I wish we used his books in school!  And if I'm not mistaken, at least for the baking one, he gives measurements in volume (standard for the home cook), and weight  (more accurate and what professionals do).  Plus, the cover says "Food + Heat = Cooking."  How could anyone not love that guy?!

Oh, yes, I'd definitely recommend I'm Just Here For The Food.  I don't always agree with Alton (to wit:  stuffing belongs in the bird, not beside it - that's why it's called stuffing - and besides, it just isn't the same without the hours of savoury melting fat and meat drippings soaking into it -- but even he finally came around to that), but even though I consider myself to be a fairly competent cook, I have certainly learned from him.  I love learning about the science behind what's really going on.*  Not only that - I have his salt cellar and his beaker-measuring cup.   :-D

Jim and I went to brunch at a friend's house.  We started talking about the food network.  Ah, Alton Brown!  And Iron Chef!  Jim and I got the starry-eyed look (well, perhaps not quite ;)).  His wife exclaimed, "Oh, no!  Alton Brown does all that weird stuff.  I love Unwrapped!"  It was all I could do to keep from simultaneously shuddering and cracking up.  We are complete opposites with food.

*If you like the science stuff, I also recommend What Einstein Told His Cook.  It's not a cookbook, but it demystifies a lot of what happens in the kitchen, giving you a background to many cooking techniques.  That, and the book that is arguably the Mother (pun intended) of them all, Harold McGee's On Food and Cooking: The Science and Lore of the Kitchen.

Incidently, all three authors - Alton Brown, Robert Wolke and Harold McGee - have conducted the Washed Mushroom Test.  Washing mushrooms is a perfectly acceptable way to clean them; they don't soak up enough water to make a difference in cooking, which vindicates my mother, which makes me happy.  :-D
