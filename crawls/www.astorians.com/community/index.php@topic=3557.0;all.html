Are they registered anywhere? If not, I'd go the dinner/gift card (Target is better than Walmart!  and if they are able to get there, it will be very useful once the baby is here for diapers and whatnot)/something non-tangible route, especially since they're apartment dwellers and probably don't have space for something they might not use. Are you good with kids? Maybe you can do a certificate or one of those little coupon books of favors that you'll fulfill - like babysitting on a Saturday evening so she and her husband can go out for a night without having to shell out extra dough for a sitter, helping to paint the baby's room or putting together furniture, stuff like that. Actually, if you know a lot of people that'd be willing to do stuff for them, you guys can make a pretty impressive book :)

Also, if you decide to get some little outfits or onesies or whatever, don't buy just newborn stuff - they're going to be bogged down with a ton of that stuff, and they grow out of it so quickly! Whenever I buy clothes for baby showers, I try to get 6-9 months, or 9-12 months. I don't mean to be a Target pimp here, but I found that they have a ton of great baby clothes cheap. And if they have any summer stuff left over, it would all be on sale around now, so (as long as you know the baby's sex) you can get some cute 1-year old outfits cheap for them to have for next summer.

Oh, also, this is just me, and its kinda dorky, but whatever - whenever I go to a baby shower, I always give a copy of Shel Silverstein's "The Giving Tree" - its my favorite children's book, and I want every kid in the world to have a copy of it! :)

Quote from: schmennifer on September 26, 2006, 08:24:56 PM
If she's a new parent, she'll probably appreciate not having to worry about cooking or going out to eat every once in a while. You can present it along with a pretty little book or box of their menus.
Okay. I'm gonna start the Mommy thread within this thread.

Yep. She would appreciate food gift certificates. But when I gave birth, I much more (ah! such good use of the language!) appreciated the cooked meals. You could give her your own gift certificates for home-cooked meals for her first week home after delivery. One night a lasagna; one night a stir fry all ready to microwave and eat; one night a stuffed, roasted chicken . . . got the idea? Friends did this for me, and I never forgot how much it meant especially when you are trying to get on some kind of schedule, and mealtimes for you and your partner are *not* in the plans!

The other most appreciated gift(s)? ANYTHING that will fit the baby 2 seasons later than birth. My favorite gift of all time was a one-piece snowsuit.

The least used gift(s)? Forgive me here, okay? Anything embroidered, knitted, crocheted. It is so hard keeping a newborn's clothes and bedding clean. If it needed to be hand washed, it wasn't used.

One other valuable thing, a book. A good parenting book. They're going to have a million questions in the middle of the night. Get them a good reference book that includes chapters on burping, passing gas and most importantly, throwing up! They'll read it all the time.

Whatever you do, give something that will be useful, helpful, practical and then you can be certain that it will, therefore, be used.

Another way to approach NYCMacUser's idea of cooking for them is to make a week's worth of meals that freeze well, like casseroles. That way they can just throw it in the oven for several minutes and it's ready. I've also heard that breakfast is often over-looked in these cases, so you can also throw together some breakfast food.

I'd also say not to overlook what they put on their register, if they have one. People tend to want to ignore baby registers and pick out cute outfits and toys instead. But remember that they have to buy all these things at once, and it really adds up. So while it may seem less creative to get the baby mattress or pack of one-pieces, these things are on the register for a reason.
