Quote from: TJT on August 07, 2008, 12:06:07 PM
Also a 2 second lag when going from regular to HD channels.
I am not sure what you're describing here.  A certain amount of delay is inherent in the process of encoding and decoding the digital signal. It is also possible that the digital and conventional signals arrive at the cable by different routes. Thus viewing a stations digital and conventional broadcasts side-by-side, it's likely that the digital one will be delayed.

If, on the other hand, you mean that there is a pause between when you select the channel and when the picture finally appears, that is inherent in the process too.  One technique employed to reduce the amount of data is that, most of the time, the encoded signal carries the difference between the previous frame and the current one. Then every so often, a full "key" frame is sent.  When you first tune to a digital station, the picture generally won't appear until the next "key" frame comes along.

If the signal is weak, the decoder will detect errors that it cannot "paint over". Typically, it will freeze the display until it can resynchronize on the next key frame.

I have read that some cable systems are decoding then reëncoding some HD signals at higher compression ratios (or lower picture quality) in order to cram more channels through their infrastructure.  I don't know which are said to do this though. The recoding would add delay to the signal chain.  And generally, decoding a more highly compressed signal involves more latency.

That about exhausts my knowledge on the subject.
