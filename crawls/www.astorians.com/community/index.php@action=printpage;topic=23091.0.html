July & August Walking Tours

MARINE PARKWAY-GIL HODGES MEMORIAL BRIDGE
GAHS Walking Tour
Saturday, July 7, 2012 at 12:00 noon

The GAHS and the MTA (Metropolitan Transportation Authority) are celebrating the 75th anniversary of the opening of the Marine Parkway-Gil Hodges Memorial Bridge.  Meet at the Rockaway Park/Beach 116th Street subway station, at ground level. The “A” or the “S” (Shuttle) train will get you there. You can take the “A” train to Broad Channel and get the “S” train to Rockaway Park (It runs at all times). We will then take the Q35 bus to the Rockaway Artists Alliance, where we wil stop by the MTA Bridges and Tunnels exhibit.   Once across, we can get another Q35 bus to Brooklyn College where the #2 and the #5 NYC subway trains are available. Check back on www.astorialic.org (http://www.astorialic.org) or www.forgotten-ny.com (http://www.forgotten-ny.com) for starting place and tour details. Fee:  GAHS members $15, $20 non-members
 
ST. MARKS & STUYVESANT PARK
Forgotten NY Walking Tour
Tuesday, July 17, at 6:30 pm

In this evening tour we will explore St. Mark‘s Church and churchyard, Stuyvesant Street, Stuyvesant Square and other landmarks in the area such as Stuyvesant Polyclinic and the original site of the 2nd Avenue Deli. Meet at St. Mark‘s Church, 2nd Avenue and East 10th Street.  The spirit of Peter Stuyvesant, the last director general of New Netherland, permeates the East Village around 2nd Avenue in the streets south of East 14th. Approximately two hours.  Call for reservations 718-278-0700. Fee:  $15.00 to GAHS members, $20.00 non-members

INWOOD PARK
Forgotten NY Walking Tour
Saturday, August 11, 2012 at 12:00 noon

Meet at Dyckman/200 St station, A train, corner of Riverside Drive and Broadway. See the Dyckman Farmhouse, the Seaman Estate Arch, and historic Inwood Hill Park and its rich Native American and colonial history in this tour through Inwood and Inwood Hill Park. Within the park is a secret Revolutionary War relic which is little-known to the general public. There‘s also Manhattan‘s only old-growth forest and spectacular views of the Henry Hudson Bridge.  Check back on www.astorialic.org (http://www.astorialic.org) or www.forgotten-ny.com (http://www.forgotten-ny.com) for developing tour details. Fee: $15.00 to GAHS members, $20.00 non-members

SOUTH STREET SEAPORT
Forgotten NY Walking Tour
Saturday, August 25, 2012 at 10:00 amVisit the Belgian blocked streets of the South Street Seaport area, featuring ancient sailing vessels and lightships, some of Manhattan‘s oldest private homes, remains of the Fish Market, and we‘ll finish at the longest continuously operating tavern in Manhattan.  Check back on www.astorialic.org (http://www.astorialic.org) or www.forgotten-ny.com (http://www.forgotten-ny.com) for developing tour details. Fee: $15.00 to GAHS members, $20.00 non-members
