Title: Re: looking for a room, and advice
Post by: daisy on December 28, 2006, 11:31:18 AM
My experience has been pretty favorable with Craigslist - for the roommates section only.  I have both looked for rooms and found roommates off of it, and it's been great.  I think she could find someplace in Astoria actually, as long as she's looking for roommates sharing a two/three bedroom, and not looking for a one-bedroom/studio just for herself.  But Woodside, Sunnyside, and Elmhurst would be great options too, and I think she'd be okay in either one.  The roommates section of Craigslist is thankfully, relatively broker free.

Unfortunately, it might be harder for your friend to find something without actually being here, because most people who are looking for roommates want an in-person interview.  Which was not a bad experience in the least for me, both as the interviewer and the interviewee.  It just helps everyone feel more at ease about whom to select.

Also word of mouth is a great way to find something too.  Send emails to all your friends, post it on your friendster or myspace account, or other lists such as this one.

Yeah, Craigslist's "roommates wanted" or shared (whatever its called) section is actually pretty good. I met LOTS of people (most of whom seemed normal) through there before I found my current place 1.5 years ago. Since I wanted to be in Astoria, and since lots of other people do too, it took me awhile, since everyone I was meeting with met with a bunch of other applicants too. But was offered the first place I looked at in Brooklyn - I woulda moved in too, it was $650 each, the girl seemed cool, and the building would have allowed my cat...but the neighborhood was sketchy - I wasn't comfortable walking around on a sunny Saturday afternoon, so I didn't think I should live there. Too bad.

Anyway, its the full-on rental listings on CL that are loaded with fake listings from brokers to lure you into working with them.

I agree that its going to be hard find a roommate without her actually being here to interview. (And I can't imagine she'd even be able to find a studio anywhere in NY on $600/month, which stinks, I know.) Maybe she can find a one-or-two month sublet on Craigslist (I've never used that section so I can't speak for how good it is, but I'd imagine its pretty decent) or stay with a friend to get her bearings here, and then she can find a more permanent place once she gets here?

The roommate section in Craigslist is great.  The only problem is when you are the person hoping to move in.  It can almost be like trying to make it in Hollywood.  There are at the least 20 people trying out for that one room.  Make sure she is ready to put on her game face and "wow" some people.  It won't be easy but she is bound to find something.
