My husband and 14-month-old and I just moved to a 3-bedroom in a 2-family house and the rent is $1900.  It is technically a 3-bedroom but the 3rd bedroom is pretty small and we are using it for storage since there are not many closets.  It's great for storing all the kid stuff, though, and the other bedroom that our son is in is big enough for 2 children, I think.

We're in the Ditmars area, about halfway between the train and the park, and the place is newly renovated with a spacious living area.

looking to perhaps buy a 2-family (and actually have an accepted offer on one, but the inspection didn't go as well as we'd hoped so we're not sure now)

We're so disappointed with the selection in this area- everything would require serious renovation, yet the prices are on par for a move-in-ready places. For something in the same condition, you can spend $250k less and get a place in one of the much better school districts in NYC. Just look a little further into Queens, a few miles away. House prices seem to be coming down to a more realistic level everywhere but Astoria, as today we noticed a few houses we were keeping an eye on have been sold, most of them months after going on the market.

Even New Hyde Park- just over the Queens border, it's right near the LIRR, maybe 45min into Penn, taxes are higher BUT one cute house in a top-rated school district was $399k. Even if you pay an extra couple grand in taxes, it's still 80% than private school each year.
