Checking in at my old neighborhoods L.I.C. & Astoria
« on: July 08, 2016, 06:39:34 PM »
  1951 we moved to L.I.C. from 125 Navy Walk Fort Greene Projects (Ingersoll Housing) Brooklyn. I attended PS 111 until June 1954, cool school, I was in the "Library Class" for 5th Grade and then the "Science Class" with Mrs. Solomon for 6th Grade. We lived at 12-50 35th Ave. Ravenswood Projects 1951-58 then 33-68 21st St. Queensview West until I joined the US Navy Feb. 1960. We attended Mass at St. Rita's, played PAL Baseball at the 110th Precinct, swam at Astoria Park Pool and in the East River at Rainey Park. Still in contact with friends from Queensbridge and Ravenswood Projects and Queensview & Queensview West. I graduated from JHS 126 June 1956 Class 9-SP1. I remember Mrs. Smul Homeroom; Mrs. Sullivan French and Mrs. Tedesco Algebra. Then on to Rice H.S. 124th St. & Lenox Ave. in Harlem, I hated that school. I wasn't the best student and always regretted my lack of enthusiasm, but enjoyed the social life in the Ravenswood/Queensview West neighborhood we lived in. I get back to L.I.C. & Astoria every once in a while, it's great to visit for a week or so, but my home is now California Home Sweet Home   If anyone is interested, there’s a reunion scheduled at Riccardo’s by the Bridge (riccardos.com) on July 30, 2016 9:00pm – 2:00am, check Queensbridge Oldtimers on FaceBook and google for details. Also, check out QueensBoard.com for Class Photos from many schools.
