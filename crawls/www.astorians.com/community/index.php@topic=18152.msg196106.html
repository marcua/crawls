I'm not sure how to think about this (how long *should* it take to stop an oil spill?) So I went looking for a comparable incident and found Ixtoc I. This was in 1979, in Mexico. It took 10 months to cap the spill.

I'm not against drilling. Unless we're all ready to stop eating raspberries in December (hauled from warmer climates in semi-trucks), give up on flying or driving, etc. - and most of us, however green we are, aren't quite *that* green - oil is here to stay. Until we come up with something better (hopefully soon).

But - just like I look both ways before I cross the street - proper caution should be exercised. And in a case like this, we should take a good close look to see if appropriate procedures (for both preventing and handling a spill) were in place and if they were being followed. And if not, we should fine the hell out of them.
