I get free hair services at bumble and bumble, sign up at their website. Free haircuts, blowouts, color. Christine Valmy offers deeply discounted facials, etc. when services performed by senior student. Same for NYU Dental School - awesome! If you want more info on that, please p.m. me.

Also rebates whenever I do online shopping. www.mrrebates.com.  I look for free stuff/deals on www.freesamplesite.com.  Last but not least, www.couponcabin.com. Take the time to clip coupons and especially look at the plastic bagged circulars they leave on stoops/foyers -often time there are FREE stuff coupons in there. In the last week I redeemed 2 free 1000 sheet Marcal tp roll coupons, as well as a free container of Wholly Guacamole. If I had not looked at the circulars, wouldn't have seen 'em. I'd say I save about $1,000 a year doing the above stuff.

Someone else mentioned Rite Aid - excellent way to get free after rebate stuff - just try not to LOSE your receipts as I tend to do. Also, if you forget to request your  check, they are nice enough to do it for you  and notify you by email that its on its way.

I've been happy with the ING direct savings account, but the interest rate may not be quite so good as HSBC. One problem is that when I really desperately need the cash I have to go online and transfer it to my checking account, and it can take a couple of days for that to go through. But otherwise I've been happy with it.

Another great thing about ING is that you can open a savings account or CD with any amount of money (I think their lower limit is literally $.01). If, like me, you have a little trouble scraping together enough cash for other places, this can be very helpful.
