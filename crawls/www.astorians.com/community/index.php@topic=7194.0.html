Confused about comments regarding cafes.
« on: July 22, 2007, 08:09:51 AM »
Folks - I am extremely confused about all the negative and deragatory comments I've read regarding Astoria's cafes.  For those of you who have recently moved into the area, this was at one time a community made up of European immigrants.  If you go to France, Spain, Austria, Greece, Italy etc. you will notice that there are many cafes where people meet, chat, read papers, drink great coffee etc.  Perhaps this is the equivalent of American Bars?  Anyway, cafes have been here for years.  They may have changed hands over time, and certainly there are more of them now, but to many of us they add a certain European flair to our neighborhood.  I am not sure what you would rather have replace the cafes.  Perhaps Starbucks?  or Denny's? or Burger Kings? or MacDonalds? or Chili's?  or any fast food chain that you find all over Americas suburbs?  I remember one Sunday, my husband and I had to go to New Jersey for a Memorial Service.  We were early and wanted to stop somewhere for a coffee.  There was no where to go.  Everything was closed except for one Diner with really bad coffee.  Why all the disdain?  These cafes are doing well, many are crowded with young and old, seemingly enjoying themselves.  I would really like to know why all the disdain?

I must say that the "Euro" cafe industry seems even bigger and more alive than ever. I've definitely contributed to some anti-Euro cafe comments on this board, but they never had anything to do with Europeans themselves (why would I be living in Astoria if I hated Europeans?!?!). It has all to do with sometimes mediocre food, way over-priced menus and occasional shoddy service. This is something that my wife and I have come to expect as we sampled (and continue to sample) the various Euro cafes in Astoria. We've both traveled Europe extensively and understand full well that, sometimes, you practically have to go into the kitchen and find your waiter to get your check and that it will still take a few waves of the hand to actually hold it in your hands. While I can understand this might seem like bad service to a newbie, it's really no big deal once their whole laid back, leave-the-customer-be attitude is understood. And If paying $6-$7 for a 12 oz. bottled beer is worth the price of sitting outside on a beautiful day then that's all there is to it.

But, I don't see why complaining about a restaurant/bar with overpriced, mediocre food, blaring dance music and bad service requires an explanation. If said criticisms seem to frequently land on so-called Euro cafes, then perhaps that's just an unfortunate coincidence. Perhaps.
