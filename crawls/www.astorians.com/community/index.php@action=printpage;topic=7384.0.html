Post by: purplecurlygirl on August 06, 2007, 09:38:27 AM
Quote from: eblack on August 06, 2007, 01:00:30 AM
If you can have hens, but not a rooster, how do you keep the hens laying eggs?

You definitely don't want a rooster around if you want hens to lay eggs you can eat!!

I used to raise chickens when I was a kid, and--forgive me for the metaphor--eggs are like a menstrual cycle. The eggs we eat are the unfertilized ones. You cannot eat a fertilized egg (at least not until it hatches and becomes a chicken :-P).

Most hens don't lay every single day, but you'd get four or five eggs per hen per week. They typically don't have a strong nesting instinct when they know the eggs aren't fertilized and they are not hard to collect. Try collecting an egg from a chicken who knows her egg has a good chance of having been fertilized, and you're likely to wind up with some nasty peck marks.

Hens are easy to raise and care for, and they'll eat just about anything (including mice!). However, the mess they make is vile, and there's a lot of it--make sure you have a place to dump it before you get a boxful of chicks. Hens can peacefully cohabit with cats--they're kind of vicious towards them and cats will keep their distance once they've been pecked a time or two. You also have to keep their wings clipped, which is not a fun chore (yes, chickens can fly--badly, but well enough to make it over a five-foot fence). I would *definitely* not keep them in the house.
