Title: Re: Aegean Cove
Post by: mronda on October 04, 2008, 03:02:28 PM
Finally tried AC. The owner was welcoming. The bar is well stocked and the dining area is warm and comfortable (they also have a fire place). There was a live songstress (this was TH night about 9 pm). My companion disliked her but I didn't mind; if I loved Greek standands I probably would have been happy as were the majority of Greek diners in the room.

Our food was delicious because it was well-seasoned, neither under- nor overcooked and packed with all the flavor you'd expect from this cuisine.

I had:
Creamy hummus with fresh hot pita on the table (came free as starter)
Grilled halloumi with chopped tomatoes, red onion and cucumber; drizzled with extra virgin olive oil and balsamic; outstanding flavor of cheese, vegetables and herbs without any one overpowering.
Masterful bifteki - two generous patties mixed with plenty of herbs and cheese, served with not-greasy lemon potatoes and garlic- and dill-loaded tzaziki
(What had to be) Fage Total yogurt with walnuts, honey and cherries as the house dessert; full fat, creamy and delish.

I sampled my friend's Makaroni - just-tender linguine with rich ground beef and tomato sauce; we thought we tasted a hint of nutmeg - that's what I will have for my own next time!

They had a decent international wine list. After dinner, I tried Greek port for the first time and loved it (ruby not tawny, served cold).

My only complant was that our waiter was very attentive to the large table ordering many bottles of wine and we had to flag down another waiter for after-dinner drinks, dessert and the check. The other servers seemed much better at keeping up with all their tables, but our guy's defense, you will never feel rushed through a meal here - our server even asked us if we wanted our entrees right after appetizers or if we preferred to wait.

I will definitely visit again.
