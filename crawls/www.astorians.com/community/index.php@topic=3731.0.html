Quote from: mcdirk on October 13, 2006, 02:20:41 PM
What was the LOTR game like?  I'd love to hear more.

Now that I'm home, I can write more on this. The one I played was The Battle for Middle-earth II. I mainly played the single-player game where you can follow the story line on either the good or evil side. I admit that I played in easy mode, so it was inevitable that I'd finish more quickly than if I had played in a more challenging mode.

Since I was playing in story mode there was a definite start and beginning to the thing. I'd play several different rounds either conquering or protecting various areas of the land and then the final battle would always take place with Sauron as an additional character. Both sides played virtually the same storyline, so when I played the evil side it was pretty much the same as the good, just different abilities, talents, and of course, different heroes and armies.

The heroes were specific characters from the LOTR story, and you had several that you would start out with. As you earned extra money/resources you could recruit new heroes or when they died you could also revive them pretty much the same way as recruiting them. The heroes had additional talents and abilities that your regular army characters did not have. So for example, if you had Tom Bombadil, you could make him sing or play a song (can't remember which) and it would scare the opposing side. This was good if you were getting a lot of damage points and you needed a little relief from getting your army completely destroyed. On the opposite site you could have the Nazgul and other typical characters.

I admit that the one thing I really liked about this game was the extra mythological/magical features that you won't get in a game like Rise of Nations (well, unless you play Rise of Legends or Age of Mythology). It certainly can give you an added edge if you use them correctly, whereas in RoN you have to wait for the appropriate technology to come along to kick ass.

It really wasn't too bad, but it was a little cheesy. I think that, as husband has put it, it is targeted more towards a younger playing audience. I would go as low as teenager, but not any lower than that. I did not play the other modes. There is a turned base mode which I never did try, but I may if I feel like it. I am not really used to the turn-based feature - at least how it is set up in this game, so I am not really sure if this makes the game more interesting or better.

I'd say if you were to try it out, download a demo first or play someone else's version. I'm not sure that I'd fork over money for it, unless the price has come down since it came out. The only strategy game that I'd personally pay for would be Rise of Nations, but that's just me!
