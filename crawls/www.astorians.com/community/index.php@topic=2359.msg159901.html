After staying over at my boyfriend's apartment last night, we had the fun of commuting back to Astoria in the morning to get to church services at Trinity Lutheran (31st Avenue at 37th Street - it was Christ the King Sunday and I wasn't about to miss it).

We got on the train at 14th Street and 8th Avenue, the A line headed up to 42nd Street where we were going to transfer to the 7 (such joy all this track work - I hope they're really doing track work).

We had the weirdest experience, really quite disconcerting.  We were sitting together talking and at one point held hands while making our plans for the rest of the day - nothing big, not even kissing or anything like that, but it set off a woman sitting across from us (I am not sure if she was homeless or coming/going from an airport - she looked unkempt but the bags could be from traveling).

After glaring at us, which happens - I get it, I'm gay, you don't like gay people, glare away, she started singing.  At first I figure she's just another subway preacher and she's singing "The Solid Rock", which is one of my favorite songs and her voice isn't half bad, so I just keep talking and ignore her, like most people on the train are doing (amazing how we New Yorkers can ignore strange behavior, isn't it?), but then she starts in on the gay nonsense and all this talk about s___, and how can you f___ where there is s___ (um, no offense, but heterosexual folks also do that).  She went on and on quoting scripture and yelling for a good 15 minutes as the train went slowly to 42nd Street.  Two folks told her to shut up, but we just ignored her. 

The funny thing is that she was clearly not going to church (though maybe she's of a denomination that meets on another day or time) and we were on our way to church - somewhat funny and ironic.

I do wonder what the best approach in these kind of situations is - confront a crazy person, talk back or just ignore the offensive and rude behavior?
