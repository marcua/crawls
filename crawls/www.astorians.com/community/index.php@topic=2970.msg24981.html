It's awful, really. I had a terrible time finding a place that was nice that accepted pets. I was lucky that I had the luxury of time so I could wait until I found one. And I certainly feel for those people who have no choice to give up their pets in order to live. I made sure that it was in the lease that cats were allowed. I couldn't live having to hide my pets and worry about whether I might be found out.

There have been so many studies that show how having and caring for a pet is beneficial to a person's physical and emotional well-being, whether the person is young or old. And while I guess I can understand the concern about noise or damage done to property, I have to say, that's not the animal's fault, it's the owner who doesn't take care of the animal or their home.

And frankly, I saw more than my fair share of "no pet" apartments that were absolute holes in the ground ... I found it ironic that these places allowed filthy slobs but not two little cats and a responsible, clean tenant ... :?
