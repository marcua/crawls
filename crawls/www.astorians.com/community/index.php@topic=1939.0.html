I have a great recipe for meatballs .. of course most of it is done by eye but I will try and give you measurements

Try to find the one pound package that has veal, pork and beef in it already.  If not, you can mix any combination of the three or just do all beef.

Half large onion finely chopped
4-5 cloves of garlic
2 eggs
1/2 cup - cup breadcrumbs (pre-seasoned)
handfull of pecorino-romano cheese

Don't salt and pepper the meat because the cheese and the breadcrumbs are usually salty.
You might need to add more breadcrumb so the mixture sticks together.  You want the mixture dry, but not too dry (make any sense?)  It should sort of be the consistency of meatloaf.
Mix all this up and ball each meatball and fry in a skillet with oil fillig half the pan.  Fry on each side until crunchy on the outside (don't worry.. they will soften up in the gravy).  You also don't need to worry if they are cooked all the way through because they will continue to cook in the gravy.

Give this recipe a try and let me know how they come out.. of course if you can adjust all the measuremtns to your taste.  I happen to like more onion so I add a little more.  The most important thing is to get the consistency right so the meatballs don't fall apart.

For what it's worth, I made Classic Italian-American Tomato Sauce II from the New Joy of Cooking for dinner tonight. It's your standard meatballs and red sauce, and it was rather disappointing, I must say. It's similar to DestineeNY's mixture, with less garlic, a handful of parsley, and only one egg. I used about 3/4 ground sirloin and 1/4 ground pork, and then panfried the meatballs in olive oil. The sauce is just onions (tossed over the meatballs halfway through frying them, which probably caused them to burn a bit), a bit of garlic and dried oregano, and then a big can of tomatoes, crushed. Simmer covered 30 minutes.

The meatballs were OK, if a bit too crumbly, but the sauce was not so much. I think the problem is trying to do everything in one pan. Perhaps next time I'll try to fry the meatballs in one pan and start the sauce in another, only adding the meatballs once the sauce is simmering... And if I have three hours, instead of just one, I'll make my dad's recipe for sauce. :)

Quote from: Harlan on April 24, 2006, 09:45:17 PM
I used about 3/4 ground sirloin and 1/4 ground pork, and then panfried the meatballs in olive oil.
I always find that when I use sirloin, or another lean cut of beef, the meatballs are too chewy. I generally use equal amounts of pork and beef. I also dust the meatballs with WonderFlour (a *very* finely milled flour—almost powder) before I fry them. This seems to help keep them intact during the cooking process.

I guess the whole idea of cooking the meatballs in the suace is to impart the flavor of the meat on the sauce, and meld the flavors of the sauce to the mealballs.  Single pan cooking doesn't allow for enough time for that to happen.

When my kids were still living at home, I used to buy 3 pounds of beef and 3 pounds of pork. Can you say meatballs and pasta, meatballs and rice, meatballs and noodles, mashed meatballs and couscous, meatball parmigiana and salad, etc. They would get up and *steal* a meatball, cold from the refrigerator, for breakfast.
