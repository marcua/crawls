Woe is me, where to take the 'rents...
« on: March 20, 2008, 01:37:01 PM »
So my parents are coming into town this weekend and we wanted to find somewhere to get food in Astoria at least Friday night and maybe a lunch sometime.

I've got the brunch places down pretty well, but I've already taken them to all my Ditmars-dinner staples. I was thinking about Il Bambino, but I wanted to see if there were any recommendations for a not too expensive but comfy and quiet place.

I am browsing through other threads, but I thought I'd ask. My mom is real adventurous and would love to try a different cuisine or something that she can't get in our rural hometown. My dad, however, can be a bit of a stick-in-the-mud, so I'm faced with the battle of finding a place where he can get a more moderated staple, but he usually finds something he likes. They both love seafood, but I'm not so crazy about it. Greek would be awesome, but something else would be cool as we usually go Greek (how can you not in Astoria!)

I would love to go somewhere in the Broadway or 36th Ave area as we've never eaten down there when they've been out. Y'all are the best!

What are the Ditmars dinner staples you've already taken them to?

Have you ever been to Kebab Cafe or Mombar?  I've never seen a menu at Kebab Cafe so that might be a little tricky for your dad, but Mombar does have a written menu and there are several dishes that I think are accessible for a less-adventurous palate.  The decor is really eclectic and I bet your mom would love it.  Here's the menu from NY Mag.

I'll also throw in a vote for Bistro 33 any day.  Neither Mombar nor Bistro 33 is dirt cheap, but both can be reasonable if you order with a bit of care.
