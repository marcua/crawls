There was a dog trainer who ran a training group in Astoria Park, although I don't know what ages they trained.  I think it was the same trainer who lived near my old apartment on 29th St. between 23rd and 24th Ave.  She had a sign out in front of their house that said "K-9 Training School".  I can't actually recommend them since I've never used them or know anyone who has. 

In general, whenever you find a trainer, I would ask what style of training they use.  Some dog trainers use negative training/discipline (think drill sergeant) which I don't prefer, it usually involves raising your voice or yanking on their choke chain/leash.  Positive reinforcement works much better and it's a kinder way to train your dog.  :-)

I wish you has been a little more specific. Are you having issues other than the dog not knowing what you expect of him? Is there any misbehaving that needs to be corrected?

Unfortunately, dog training is not simply a matter of cost. It is more a matter of YOU being in charge of the dog's behavior. The dog MUST accept you as HIS leader, and not even try to influence you.

My suggestion, as always, is to first take a few days to study NILIF and review if you are doing it correctly before you bring an outsider into the picture.
