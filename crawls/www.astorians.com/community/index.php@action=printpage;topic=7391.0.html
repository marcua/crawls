I wish to make a proposal!  I will personally organize an Astorians.com Meeting with our local representatives this fall.  It will be held at Riccardo's.  It will be sponsored by the Astoria Renaissance Committee (a Committee I had formed seven years ago made up of the residents around the park).  We would invite Giannaris, Vallone, representatives from the 114th Precinct, Sanitation, representatives from Astoria Park and perhaps the Transit Authority.

Noise polution (mainly from motorcycles)
Trash and littering
Graffiti
Dog walkers not picking up after their dogs
Astoria Park issues
Odd smells on Ditmars
Over Development of the area
Pistilli and various issues with his buildings

Since I do not wish to look like a fool in front of our representatives, I would like to get an idea of who from this forum would participate (obviously, if you are actually in town and do not have an emergency).  I just wish to see how much interest there is prior to organizing this.  Let's see exactly how many individuals are willing to make the effort to attend a meeting.  Once we post the date and time, l will come to you again for your commitment to attend.  We can then take attendance at the meeting and see who actually showed up. 

The only way I was able to get anything done in the past was to attend meetings, to organize meetings, and to get the local papers involved.  Alas, I felt that I was all alone in my efforts and became burnt out.  I am willing to give it another try, with your assistance.  Who is the first one to commit?

I'm just brainstorming a bit here so please don't feel that I think this is the way it has to be.

I think part of the reason that groups can't work together is that many groups have their own agenda and only work on one issue or two (i.e. "too much development"). I like the idea of having "Astorians.com" organized meetings where we choose issues that we as a community want to bring before our representatives (as is happening now) and then send the word out to any other groups that are also interested in presenting on those specific issues for that meeting. i.e. we pick three issues that we would like to address, we invite the representatives we'd like to be there, and then reach out to other groups who may like to join the discussion about those three topics (it could be two topics, or one topic even - doesn't really matter as long as it's organized.) Then the next time we meet we may have three different issues (or maybe two old and one new), and again organize it and then invite any other groups that would like to be involved in that discussion. It may be different people and different groups each time, but I imagine that you'll end up with a core community comprised of many smaller groups that work together on the areas of common interest, and yet each can still have its own role apart from any other groups as well. I see no reason for "Astorians.com" to have its own exclusive group, nor do I (currently) like the idea of one exclusive existing group taking "Astorians.com" under it's wings. Speaking personally, there are a lot of local politics that I don't really want to align myself with exclusively - though I may share areas of common interest with one or more of these groups.

I think one of the reasons this board does so well in terms of creating dialog is because it's a kind of "meta" conversation where people from many groups collaborate and discuss. If we can organize community meetings in the same spirit that exists already on the board here I think we may end up with a really dynamic yet effective community voice.
