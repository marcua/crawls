Yeah, I went to New England Culinary Institute up there when I was getting tuition reimbursement from Suss MicroTec :)  So I worked at the NECI restuarant on Church St. briefly, as much as my schedule would allow.

I lived with two people who worked at Stone Soup on College St.  That seemed like a cool place to work.  The restaurant workers of Burlington are one big family, basically... they all seem to take care of their own.  I felt like, in their eyes, I would never be a legitimate member of their family if I kept the high-paying corporate job so I kinda laid low in that scene, but occasionally worked as a saucier for a couple of places and a catering company outside of Burlington.  I was trying to keep a low profile at the time.

Plus, the girl I was living with at the time had a bakery stand at the Burlington Farmers Market, which was (in retrospect) really amazing.  I just resented being woken up early in the morning to help her, but it was a really cool experience.
