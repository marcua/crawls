Read the lease. It could potentially have said "$X for first 12 months, and $X+100 for second 12 months". More typical would be to say "$X*24 total, or $X/month, for 24 months." In the latter case, no, they can't raise the rent in the middle of the lease.

We just signed another two-year lease on my apartment. I forget the percentage amount of the increase,
but I do know it's locked in until it expires. Again, as others have said, your boyfriend's lease may be
worded differently, but in my experience the standard is that if your rent goes up, let's say 6% and your
increase is $50 per month, that stays put for the next 24 months from the day after the previous lease
expires.

Is your BF in a large building - mine is 6 floors w/10 units on each floor. If he's in two- or three-family house,
maybe the owner drew up the lease differently.

Hopefully he'll find the copy and solve the mystery.

Good luck.
