The 'sad block' reference...do you mean how that space next to Kyclades is it...?  and maybe between that Co-op apt. bldg...how the street level storefront has been empty for a few years now?  Yeah, it is really crappy looking...don't know what they could do to make it look better but some serious redesign seems to be in order...

Anyway, I know it's no big loss with Okeanos, but what I fear is that it will be another new yuppie/hipster place.  Granted, many of the new places that have opened are pretty good quality for the most part, but with each place closing, that's a little bit more of the old-school Astoria disappearing, and likely the LL will increase the rent for whomever the next tenant might be.

There really needs to be a place in people's hearts and minds, for the old school diners (like on Steinway near 30th Ave) and on 31st Street near the Ditmars subway....for such places to remain...Oh...and Tasty's on Ditmars.  I love the mix of the old and new in Astoria and would hate for it to continue at its current pace of gentrification.

This is why it's so important to patronize the old school places!  For e.g., the Bagel House's bagels are JUST as good as Brooklyn Bagel, yet all the hipsters go there instead.  Why??

Yippee, I'm in agreement with a lot of this. But in answer to your last question (and I'm not necessarily a fan of Brooklyn Bagel) -- having lived in Astoria for several years, I am grateful for some of the newer, albeit 'hipster-y' places like 60 Beans or NY Bagel and Coffee. Mostly this is speaking as a freelancer who works from home and enjoys being able to grab a coffee, snack and sit somewhere for an hour for a change of pace. Before these places came along, I would often feel at odds trying to carve out a little (unobtrusive, not douche-baggy!) spot for myself amid the the gregarious European seniors (bless them) who frequent all the 'old-school' places during the weekdays.


At times I feel very conflicted. The fancy-pantsness of all this new Astoria stuff is kinda out of control and I feel like it doesn't really target me as a normal person who's lived/worked hard in this city for so many years. Yet as much as I always admired the look/vibe of places like Lefkos Pirgos, never felt like I'd be welcomed there. Still bummed to see that place leave and turn into a Time-Warner (?!!) store...
