I'm a transplant from rural America (not quite the midwest, but may as well be), lived here since 1997, spent a decade before that living in a deeply rundown section of the Bronx.  Moved here because the rents were entirely reasonable, and if I'm being honest, I missed white folks, having lived so long being the only one one the block.

So I understand both sides of the issue to a certain extent.  In my mind, and what's not being noted here, there's a radical difference between someone who would move to a previously unheralded middle-to-working-class neighborhood for a basic, sound reason like cheap rent ... and someone who would move to a wildly overly-hyped, increasingly midde-to-upper-middle class neighborhood with what I can fairly describe as extortionate rents and inflated property values.  I wouldn't move here now based on that alone.  Since I'm here, I'll ride it out as long as I can, as I don't own my apartment and have been renting the whole time.  (I will not post my rent and make many of you weep.)

That's the difference I sense between me and newer arrivals ... and I'm denoting a basic difference in social class more than anything.  I came here with zero expectations after being raised in an economically-depressed rural area, then spending my 20s and early 30s in en economically-depressed and crime-ridden urban area.  I've always felt more at home, and identified much more, with working-class people, as that's how I was raised, and that's where I've lived most of my life.  Since I put myself through college and work in an office, I've surely been exposed to middle and upper-middle-class folks for a long, long time now.  Again, if I'm being honest, I don't like or covet the lifestyle and sense a deep emptiness about it based on too high an emphasis on status.

Of course, not everyone moving here is "like that."  And most of the "Midwest kids from the suburbs" are in fact well-heeled daughters and sons of tristate area suburbs (if you're paying attention to their accents).  It's a double-edged sword for me.  There's a lot I don't like going on, and a lot I do.  (I surely don't miss the lower-end scumbags who were over-populating this place when I first moved here, and a lot of them didn't get the memo yet about moving out.)  I try to keep an open mind.  But I surely understand where John B is coming from on this issue.

Casicua, yes and no on why people move here, or NYC in general.  I'd rather skip the Gordon Gekko "Greed Is Good" tinges of what you put forth -- there are countless reasons why people move to NYC.  Plenty of gay folks for the sense of community and liberation.  Plenty for artistic purposes and such.  That whole "best and brightest" thing, I bought for about two minutes in 1987, then slowly realized Manhattan was more like The Island of Broken Toys in the Rudolph the Red-Nosed Reindeer Xmas special than any sort of half-assed Atlantis gathering of uber-mortals.

I moved here because the prospect of doing so excited the hell out of me.  Based on literature (Henry Miller, Hubert Selby, etc.), movies (all those great NY-based 70s flicks), music (the CBGBs/new wave scene of the late 70s and early 80s) ... just the overall feel there was to NY at the time ... even in the later 80s when I moved here.

And that feeling is now just about gone thanks to over-gentrification, first in Manhattan, but surely here in Queens, too, and Brooklyn for sure.  Maybe it was a time and place, and maybe just an illusion to a dumb rube like me, but it somehow made sense enough for me to pack my crappy cardboard suitcase and come here with $200 in my pocket and not much else to my name.  I came here to live life the way I wanted to live it ... which has nothing to do with ambition or money ... if it does for  you, good on you, wish I could be more like that!

I don't disagree with you, but you're painting this with just as broad a brush as John is.  And lay off the agism ... a prejudice that makes no sense to me, because we're all going to age, unless we die young.

I'd say people come here to be themslves, more than anything.  THere are no easy answers on this issue for me on this issue.  But I can assure you, for me it has nothing to do with age.
