A few weeks ago I realized we (the world in general) were coming up to the 40th anniversary of 1969 (the year humans first walked on the moon, Woodstock, as well as an important year for me personally) and I got to being nostalgic for the time period.  I chanced to hear about Hair in the park on 1010 radio.

For a long time I couldn't bring myself to watch the movie.  Having lived through the times, it was hard re-live it by watching the movie.  A lot of people who were around at the time felt the same way.  It was too soon to be nostalgic, and we couldn't imagine we'd ever be nostalgic for anything anyway.  But one day I turned on the tv and there it was in front of me and I got drawn in.  Actually it was the sight of Treat Williams with long hair, dancing, that mesmerized me - in shock.  I think the movie was well done, and it's one of my favorites now, too.

TCM won't be showing it again till June 1, at 3:45 in the morning, so anyone else waxing nostalgic or just wanting to see the movie, might want to buy it.
