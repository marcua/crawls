Angelo's and the nearby Lorusso's on 18th Street are long time family businesses that catered to the long-standing Italian community that has lived in that area surrounding 21st Street for a generations.  While the neighborhood has changed in many ways, they don't seem interested in modifying their business model.  The Lorusso family are the nicest people but they just won't consider adding whole grain pastas or pizza crusts.  And, like Angelo's, the atmosphere and style of the place isn't that inviting (compared to say the new Antika on 30th).  Lorusso's has a liquor license and that area could desperately use a place with a nice bar with a few stools.  He could serve wine and bites of his amazing focaccias.   Angelo's remodeled not so long ago but not in a very tasteful way.  And there was hope there would be more deli and to-go Italian foods but low and behold, they missed the boat and stuck with thousands of cookies instead.  Who buys all those cookies?  These are good people but I think it's really too hard for them to change their ways.
