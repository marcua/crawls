I live on 24th Street at 36th Avenue.  If you don't walk below Crescent Street you won't be able to shop at the Trade Fair at 23rd Street/36th Avenue, get fried chicken at Palace 21st Street/36th Avenue, or be able to walk to Roosevelt Island.

Just avoid 36th Avenue when the kids get out of school.

Be bold.  If you live closer to 35th Ave, walk down 35th Ave past 24th Street and go to Trade Fair by cutting across the Ravenswood Housing Project.  Yes, the project.  Be bold - sit down on a bench and admire the tall leafy trees that will shade you from the hot sun.  If it's at night, pause at the intersection of 35th Ave and 24th St, and look up at the sky.  It's a surprisingly open area and on a clear night it's a nice spot to watch the stars or the full moon.

Speaking of unprovoked catcalls -- they happen with great frequency on 36th Ave between 35th Street and the N train. Which is mostly why I try to take the R or V from 36th Street whenever possible. It's not as bad in cold weather (obviously), but come spring and summer, I can't walk to the N or W without some kind of lewd gesture or comment made. I do not dress attractively at all, ever so I don't know why it happens. However, I have never once felt unsafe in my neighborhood (except for when the union dudes were all lined up across the street).

I have to second Starwind on avoiding 36th Ave when school lets out. Not that the kids are scary or anything, but there are definite traffic jams on the streets.  :-) Advantages to 36th Ave are the Thai restaurant (Arharn, I think it's called), and Cafe Triskell (even though I've not yet eaten there, I hear it's wonderful). For pastries, there is Oven Treats Bakery, which has pretty good drip coffee and a large assortment of sweets, and nice folks behind the counter.

Good luck to you!

Quote from: newastorian35 on August 02, 2008, 02:26:36 PM
Is the trade fair on 23rd st./36 Ave. any bigger or have even more items? 

The Broadway store expanded, but the 36th Ave store is more spacious.  Longer, straighter ailes.  More registers, more personnel in the ailes.  One problem with TF, at least on 36th, (I haven't noticed at the others), they don't have hooks on their shopping carts for you to hang a personal shopping cart/laundry cart on, so you have to put a carrying basket in your personal cart instead - and if you have a big cart, the basket falls in!

Maybe the 36th Ave store has more of some stuff, but Broadway has their canned soups lined up neater.  Shelves are more accessible on 36th Ave, so it certainly seems they have more stuff.  One of the joys of the 36th Ave store is that every few steps you're in a different country.

But a big warning about TF (36th Ave, at least):  THEY CHANGE THE LAST SALE DATES!  So, for meat, seafood, and cheese - LOOK CAREFULLY!  And never buy anything from these departments labled "Manager's Special"!

On the other hand, the deli at the TF on 36th Ave has the best "home made" roast beef! 

One of the great things about this neighborhood is that there are so many big food stores - the two TF's, Food World on 36th Ave by the train, C-Town on Broadway near the train, and Associated on 21st Street and Bway.  Each has it's advantages.
