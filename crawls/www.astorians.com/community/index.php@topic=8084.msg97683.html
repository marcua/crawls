It is SO funny that I found this thread.  I was just planning on posting something on this topic.  You want to know about crushes in Astoria...I've got a good story for you.

The girl i see once every two months

I have lived here in Astoria coming up on three years in March.  When I got here I was dating this girl, in fact, she moved in with me.  We lived together for a year and a half, but we both knew our relationship was falling apart.  It finally did last march. (amicably might I add)  Since day one of moving here, there has been this girl that I see once every two months, but when I do, it feels like my heart is about to jump out of my chest.  She's beautiful, seems intelligent and I would just LOVE to have a cup of coffee (or just go do whatever) with her.  She's got this beautiful curly hair and this incredible smile.  So, while I was dating my ex-gf, clearly the thought had crossed my mind that if we ever broke up I would want to find a way to meet this girl that I see and and ask her out.

Well, I've seen her a few times since the breakup and every time I swear to myself that the next time i see her I'll start chatting with her...So tonight I see her...I just got a haircut and as i'm walking out i see her walking into the Family Market next door.  I immediately freak out...I just got out of getting my haircut (which of course looks like I'm from the 70s) and I'm thinking to myself, "is this the time? Can I pull this off now? Can I go up and meet her?"

So I tried setting it up so that I would be walking past as she came out and it worked, but then I got nervous...so she wound up walking all the way down my block pretty much across the street and then behind me...I couldn't get the words :(  I have NO idea how to approach her...what the hell could I say?  So, I keep thinking now...maybe the next time...in two months.  Well, that was lame...hahaha...I guess I just wish I knew some way of meeting her...I was never good with talking to women I don't know...never good at the small talk because it always seemed so superficial...

I know this is about crushes on astorian strangers, but it was far too coincidental NOT to post my story given that this all just happened tonight and I just thought to look on here...

Anyone has any good ideas, let me know

Later party people,
Chris

I don't think that knowing whether a person is taken or not is that important.  I've definitely run into that wall talking to someone on the train and, usually, they were nice enough to drop subtle hints that they were taken.  That or they just made sure to say that they "moved up here/live with their boyfriend" or something along those lines.

Chris - good luck, man.  Just GO FOR IT!  What's the worst thing that'll happen?  She says go away.  What's the best?  You end up with a great relationship.  The potential gains far outweigh the potential losses...

Of course, it's easier said than done and I have dropped the ball PLENTY of times because I was too nervous to say anything.  But, it sucks MUCH more to kick myself for the next hour/day/week/month/year for not saying ANYTHING than it does to just feel like a tool because she has a BF or isn't interested.

But, I think it has to do with the chemical attraction, of which I am a 100% believer in...and totally powerless against.  Getting butterflies when I just look at someone is a situation that turns me into a total toolbag and usually leaves me hating myself for not saying anything.  Even a voice cracking, nervous "Hi" is better than nothing because if they feel the same way, it'll play out.

So, see if it'll play out.  The reward could be great.
