Gleason: This is not about overdevelopment.  Ditmars is one of the most underdeveloped areas in the five boroughs.  Most houses are running on 60 amps which is really nothing.  The demand hasn't increased significantly over last year.  Con Ed itself has admitted that this is not a supply problem (call their spokesman if you don't believe me), but an equipment problem.  Less development wouldn't have fixed the infrastructure (in fact, new development probably would have required certain upgrades to the infrastructure, and in this case, might have helped.)

As to questions about a class action lawsuit -- the hard part here is proving damages.  How have Astorians been actually hurt in the pocketbook (apart from rotting food, which ConEd does pay for)? That's what we'd have to figure out to file a class action suit.
