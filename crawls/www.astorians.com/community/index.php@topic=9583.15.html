My advice: don't call 311 unless the property owner is clearly never shoveling snow.  Because it's been my experience that the sanitation folks are ticket-giving hungry -- at least they've nailed my 73-year-old landlord a handful of times for infractions that were anywhere from deserved to outlandish. 

(And keeping her sidewalk clean is quite a task. In the past three years, I've cleaned up: a few dozens broken beer bottles, including two six packs purposely smashed, a kitchen sink, a water bed, vomit, countless plastic water bottles, used diapers, used prophylactics, discarded toys, human feces (unless someone in the neighborhood owns a very large shetland pony), dog feces (not a lot, but it's clearly getting worse), countless cigarette butts, countless glacine pouches for crank and X, a gigantic three-foot-long bail of 100-page blueprints that weighed about 50 lbs., christmas trees, a garbage bag filled with headless barbie dolls, a dead chicken, a soccer ball with a carving knife stuck in it.  The list goes on.  And this is a "nice" neighborhood!)

Take my word for it: when you're on the other end of this equation, the person who has to shovel snow or maintain a sidewalk, it aint always easy.  When a snow fall stops early morning, on a weekend, or evening between 6 and 10, sure, no problem.  But one like this past storm that didn't stop until early afternoon?  Who among us is home at 1:00 in the afternoon to get out there and immediately clear the sidewalk?  I wasn't.  (I saw a few people shoveling at 8:00 am when I left for work ... with another 4-5 inches of snow left to fall, ergo, a double shoveling session, which I don't do, the shoveling starts when the snow ends.)  I didn't get home until 8:00 pm Friday night ... at which time, I got out there and shoveled.  People got to work; we're not all retired.  As for getting kids to shovel ... unless this involves an X-Box and an animated character who looks like Rambo, shovels the sidewalk AND blows "bad guys" away with sawed-off shotgun, it aint happening.  I've never seen kids go door-to-door around here looking for work like that -- and I wish they would.

You'd be surprised how few friends you got when you need someone to help you perform hard physical labor.  Try it some time.  Everyone suddenly gets real busy, especially relatives.

So, my take, bust all you want on businesses, they should be shoveling in front of their places simply in terms of a sound business practice.  But rest easy on the old ladies.  Again, if you think it's that easy to snap your fingers and get people to do this stuff for you, even when you're paying, that's not always the case.  Besides, I shudder to think what Astoria will be like when all those old Greek and Italian ladies are no longer around.  Whatever kind of people move in and buy these nondescript row houses from their children for upwards of $700,000 a piece, I can guarantee you they'll be a lot less warm and sane than these women are.  I can see that happening already.

Back to lurking!
