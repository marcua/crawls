Please be careful when looking for studio apartments in this neck of the woods. The majority of them, in the 90% range, are not legal units with proper certificates of occupancy. A "valid lease" means nothing when the buildings department knocks on the door and forces one to leave the same day.  One indication of whether an apt. is legal or not is to see if it is advertised as utilities included. Just a heads up to all the people who want to live in this nice neighborhood.

Real estate brokers/agents should not be collecting rental fees on apartments that are w/o certificates of occupancy but it does happen. Good luck to all.
