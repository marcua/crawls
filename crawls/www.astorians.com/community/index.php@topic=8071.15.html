Everytime I go into the convenience store on 31st and 21st ave, I always swear I won't go back.
I moved here a year ago and last fall I went there often as it's pretty close to my apt. I don't carry cash really and I would pick up odds and ends with usually around an $8 tab and I'd pay with my debit card-no problem.
Around Valentines day, I go to pick up some gerber daisies and all of a sudden then have a $20 card minimum. That wouldn't piss me off if I didn't KNOW that they only imposed that minimum on me the second I came up in line since the person in front of me was able to buy their purchases (under $20) with their credit card. And every time since, they change the minimum for me. If I have $12 worth of stuff, the minimum is $15. If I have $7 worth, the min is $10. I finally stopped going, and won't even go in there when I have cash. I don't trust them.

i have to say, even tho 711 will most likely take business away from any near mom and pop shops, the slurpies will be great for the summer, add alittle vodka or something like that and your golden  :mrgreen:

That is probably not as true in this community as it might be in other neighborhoods.  Just based anecdotally on the discussions on this board, the crowd in Astoria is probably divided, with more than half of people hating on corporate chains such as 7-11.  Most of the coffee/bagel/deli type mom and pops have their devotees, who will not leave them for 7-11.  And the bodegas in the area already face tons of competition from other bodegas.  Plus, from my experience, items at both bodegas and 7-11 tend to be overpriced, as you're paying for convenience.  So it all kind of balances out.  It's not like 7-11 will be offering radically cheaper goods, as supposedly happens when a Wal-Mart opens up.  I don't think any of the businesses will lose much business.  Especially on Slurpees, since 7-11 will be the only business in the area serving such a product.  As a Coca-Cola Slurpee fiend, and a believer in freedom of choice I am psyched.  :-D
