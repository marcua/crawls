But, again, he's not pushing for us to be isolationist.  He wants to be MUCH more open with nations diplomatically and economically (through trade and information sharing, not gratuitous aid and/or harsh sanctions) and remove ourselves from so many of our obligations; military or aid-based.  This would mean that we stop funneling billions of dollars into Israel AND their enemy Egypt, of which both use to build up military strength to guard against/attack the other.  It would mean we'd stop sending money to future Saddams and Osamas while they are now our friends, yet tomorrow our enemies, to overthrow governments we don't like.

If the assistance we ask for isn't given; screw them.  Paul wants a strong military and it will be even stronger under his presidency because we won't have our soldiers all over the world.  Do we really need to have our troops stationed in Japan and Germany for their protection; spending millions (or billions) of our tax dollars?  Why can't they be given the task of protecting themselves?  Are they gonna spring up a batch of new Hitlers and Hirohitos?  Is someone going to jump on invading them once we leave?  Is the Red Army going to rise up again and go rolling across Western Europe?

It is THESE types of situations that Paul wants to get us out of; not the ones that are beneficial to the US.  If that's what everyone wants to consider isolationist; sign me up as an isolationist.  I don't, by any means, think that we should just blatantly allow suffering around the world; but there has to be a line drawn.  Our situation in the US direly needs attention.  What we spend just on our military and gratuitous aid activities OUTSIDE of this country would help bring our budget down, lower taxes, increase take home pay to families and help set up programs (private giving, not government) that can combat hunger, homelessness, or provide cheaper health care.

That's what Dr. Paul wants to get this country doing and that's why I support him.  If fixing things here by doing less in other countries gets that done, so be it.  If you're going to say it sounds selfish, consider something you want (environmental protection, cheaper and cleaner energy, better public transportation, lower taxes, cheaper college, etc.) and REALLY question whether you want to continue to send your money to another country, or whether you'd rather see it go to one of those things.
