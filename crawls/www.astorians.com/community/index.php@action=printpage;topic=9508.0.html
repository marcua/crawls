The IRS web site at http:/irs.gov is a good place to look.

One of the Frequently Asked Questions mentions Form 4852 for taxpayers who have not been able to obtain a correct W-2 or 1099.
http://www.irs.gov/faqs/faq-kw205.html

The form, like many others, is available as an Adobe PDF file:
http://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/f4852.pdf


First part of instructions for form 4852:

"Purpose of form. Form 4852 serves as a substitute for Forms
W-2, W-2c, and 1099-R and is completed by taxpayers or their
representatives when (a) their employer or payer does not give
them a Form W-2 or Form 1099-R, or (b) when an employer or
payer has issued an incorrect Form W-2 or Form 1099-R. Attach
this form to your income tax return."

Hope that helps.

I imagine it will - your situation seems to be exactly what it's for, but i am not a tax expert.

They are very serious about getting employers to withhold the proper amount, send it in to them, and furnish the required forms to employees.

The substitute form asks what effort you made to obtain the employers statement - I wouldn't be surprised if they follow up with serious questions to the employer.

If the employer claims you were a contractor rather than an employee, you would be responsible for paying your own withholding tax. In that case, he still owes you a Form 1099 stating how much he paid you as a contractor.

If you have any doubt whether taxes were withheld from your pay, or how much was withheld, or whether the employer considered you an employee or a contractor, you should get the advice of a tax accountant. You could be assessed fines and interest charges. Someone who knows how to ask for relief from these will be very useful.

I don't know any tax accountants. Someone else may suggest one.  You can also call someone at the IRS for advice.

Whatever you do, if the amount is more than a few hundred dollars, don't just settle for the advice of some guy on the internet, like me.
