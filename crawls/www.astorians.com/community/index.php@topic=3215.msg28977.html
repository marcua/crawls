Are we not allowed to say we had a bad experience at a certain restaurant?  I would rather know about someone's experiences at a restaurant, and I would welcome that establishment addressing the posts here, and perhaps changing for the better because of it.  In 2003, I wrote a review to Citysearch based on the bad service I received at Telly's Taverna.  Citysearch rejected my review but that's to be expected.  They don't touch controversy.  I had wanted to try the restaurant out based on all the wonderful reviews of the place.  I am of Latin descent and went with a few friends, who were also of Latin descent.  This should not have been an issue in the least.  From the moment we walked in, we were treated with obvious dislike or should I say contempt, to the point where we actually walked out of the restaurant for fear if we actually dared order something, they would spit in our food.  When the waitress finally came around to get our order, our questions about the vague menu were met with sarcastic remarks about rice being our favorite dish, and the like.  She was very rude and it was completely uncalled for.

I can think of no reason one should treat her potential customers so rudely, other than she didn't need/desire our business, and was fairly ignorant of Latin culture. The ignorance of a few doesn't speak for the masses, and I'd encourage others to patronize the many other fine restaurants in Astoria, Greek included, where customers are treated with respect and decency no matter their color.   I have not had an experience like this ever before or since then (and have not gone back to Telly's).  I am also half Italian, and have always been welcoming and sharing of other cultures especially food, being torn between two different ones myself.  Many establishments here have been quite happy to share those things with me, and I'm happy that Telly's is the exception here.   But also sad.

I think if the comments are tasteful that this can be a service to the community. If they are just personal attacks that's a different thing, but we are talking about local businesses here. I don't think there's any need to protect local businesses that discriminate, are unsafe in food production, serve bad food, or don't assist the neighborhood they live in (think the guy that did nothing to help the kids that were getting beat up a couple months ago).

I don't think restaurants deserve any special insulation just because their reputation is on the line. Anyone who owns a business puts their reputation on the line, whether they own a convenience store, restaurant, coffee shop, food cart, or grocery store. There's also a positive side to this kind of discussion in that it allows places to address the complaints/concerns of their customers - this can potentially boost business (i.e. fire the waiter that is turning away your customers, or fix your roach problem, or don't discriminate against people who are not your race).

This is just my opinion so far.

While restaurants shouldn't receive special protection, a considered view and careful description of an evening's events should be given.  Having never been in the restaurant business, I have no special attachment. Goodness, I'm not even a foodie.  You (plural) do go on about food on this board.  For me, it is all pearls before swine.

People tend to overreact to the most basic of situations and then relish in the pouting about it. When this happens it isn't fair to the restaurant.

Fatty's, mentioned in another thread, is a perfect example of this.  The owners are Sue and Fernando who know me by face if not by name. Wonderful and warm are great descriptions of them. Saw their establishment getting some crap about slow service and a general attitude about not caring about it. My experience is the polar opposite. Recently went with two others to eat there. We had a long wait and the waitress was being inattentive. We ended up leaving before the food came because we were hungry and people who had come in after us received their food before us. Drinking and talking with good company kept us busy until the hunger pangs were kicking in and we decided it best to leave.

The owners were informed and tried to comp our drinks and offer the food for free. They explained they had a newly hired kitchen staff and were working out the kinks and they were sorry for the mix up. We refused the drink comp and said no thanks, we were really hungry and needed to go eat but did not want something for nothing.

Next time I was there with three people they recognized me and insisted that the drinks be free for all of us and the meals were 2 for 1. I respect their commitment to being good hosts and concern for customers. If I had just stormed out and never come back to give them another chance, I would have missed out on a great meal and a nice evening for practically no cost. It is a tough business, but nothing and no one is perfect. Hopefully negative reviews will be tempered and one time bad experiences will not be presented as repeat poor service experiences.
