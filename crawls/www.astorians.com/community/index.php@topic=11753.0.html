apt notices in spanish only
« on: August 11, 2008, 12:29:03 PM »
Hey, was curious about what you all thought about this, even though it happened elsewhere in Queens.

I live in a Vantage building, and got home to find that a notice had been slipped under all the doors. Unfortunately, it was in Spanish only. I have no problem w. Vantage issuing the notice in Spanish and English, since it is an area with lots of Spanish-speaking residents. However, I was not happy to find a notice under my door that is not written in English.

I taped it up in our lobby w. a note on it: "Hey Vantage, how about in English next time, U.S.A." Immediately, somebody wrote, "I second that, we should all meet to discuss our issues w. Vantage." I don't really have other issues w. Vantage, but was glad somebody agreed.

Later that night somebody crossed out U.S.A, which I found to be disrespectful. I wrote "who crossed out?" and wrote U.S.A again. That was again crossed out.

Now, I have no problem w. the notice being in both languages. But I do have a problem w. somebody crossing out U.S.A. Without being disrespectful, my question is, Why spend your life here and wake up here every morning if it's such a bad country? It must be better than the alternative...

Just curious about any thoughts on this...
