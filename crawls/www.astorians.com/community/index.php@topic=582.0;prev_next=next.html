I never knew about the Lawn Gyland accent until I went away to college. Same with my sister.

I'd be walking (wawking) along (alawn) with somebody, and they'd start complaining about Long Islanders and their awful accents. "Where are you from?" they'd ask and I'd tell them- Long Island. They'd get a little flustered and apologize, "Oh sorry, you don't sound like it!"  :?

My sister and I were home on Christmas break, and realized we had the same experience up at our respective schools. WTF are people talking about? It didn't hit us until we went to Christmas mass, and suddenly we heard the priest say, "Let's awl give thanks and praise to da Lawd." We looked at each other with light bulbs going off over our heads. AHA!!!!

I definitely have a L.I. accent but it's not very strong for some reason. Coffee is "cawfee," but I still say "drawer" for example. Go figya.
