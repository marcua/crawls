your landlord may have cheaped out on the type of extermination. knock wood (no pun inteneded) mine are gone. and they weren't in my bed either. although my landlord maintains that there are no cracks in the walls and that since we don't have roaches we couldn't have a problem, and tried to pass the blame and bill on me(unsuccessfully). and it was my room that was infested, not the others. that was the worst ordeal i have ever gone through. october will be a year since we nuked the suckers in two shots. i did recently wake up one morning with a few welts so john and i promptly got bug spray and he went to work. here are a few upkeep tips:
raise your bed off the floor with risers. and keep it away from the wall if you can (my room is so small i cannot). the more room under your bed the easier you can spray underneath.
on the risers, put double stick tape. if they come up from the carpet the have to get to the legs and they won't get far. and since my bed is against the wall we lined the wall with double stick tape as well (right above the molding near the bottom). so if they come up from the carpet they get stuck there as well.
spray the crap out of your box spring and floor under the bed every few weeks. john went one step above for me though. since my mattress is encased in a plastic cover he took construction grade plastic sheeting and wrapped my box spring in it while it was covered in bug killing goodness. so i basically sleep in a plastic bubble. but i haven't been bit since.

A few other tips about bed bugs I can share as I had to battle them last year:

* don't feel bad about it, an infestation has nothing to do with how clean you keep your place
* Vaseline on the legs of the bed frame can also help keep them from climbing up.
* SteriFab is how I killed them.  It kills BBugs & BB eggs on contact but is inert when it dries, used in hostitals, etc.  Spray all over your bed, sofa matresses, wash all the sheets regularly. spray all the cracks and crevices and corners in the room.  Get a big container of it & a spray bottle, you'll want to have some around in case they come over from next door again.
* it helps to get in a Starship Troopers mindset: "it's an ugly planet, a bug planet"  us or them, etc. etc.
* No place is imune or too 'high class' I'm sure trump tower denizens have kids coming home from exotic trips with surprises in the luggage.

It's not even just NYC that has a problem.  BBugs were nearly extinct in North America thanks to DDT, but once that was banned and world travel of all classes became so common they made a comeback.

I am amused, maybe wrongly so, by the combination of the picture and your quote at the botttom.  Anyway, here are a few suggestions I had previously gotten from the googling my problem when I was at my wits end from crushing yet another lovely pestilent of my building. 

1) Caulk any crevices between floorboards, wall, doorframes, tub, toilet, sink, etc.  If you stop and look well you'd be surprised at how much you find to do this to.  Make sure you get a caulking lesson from someone who is familiar with it if you have never done it before.  Also make sure that there aren't cracks under your door, etc.

2) Depending on if you have animals or children in the apt. you can do one or a combination of things:  Bug spray, boric acid, peppermint oil, sticky glue traps (make sure they're made for bugs and not mice).

3) Preventative maintenance helps also.  Try to make sure that you don't leave dishes in the sink, trash in the trashcan, or crumbs on the counter.  Also try to make sure that none of your cats is stashing cat food anywhere, and also that you clean the litterbox really often as six legged critters can sometimes be drawn to the excrement.
