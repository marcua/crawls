I do sorta agree with the OP - I walk every morning from 21st to the train along Astoria Blvd - particularly as one gets closer to 31st, the amount of trash on the street just skyrockets. Having lived here for ages before I connected with any kind of community or found any cool bits of Astoria (my area not really being high on the 'cool' scale) this was part of what made me convinced I lived somewhere horrible. I've since settled in and found the awesome parts of Astoria, however I'm embarrassed to walk with any non-Astorian friends and relatives (especially from O/S) through that mess. There's very few trash cans, the few that are there are open and trash easily blows out of them even if they're not overflowing, which they usually are, and there doesn't seem to be anyone cleaning up besides the occasional shop owner who cleans their small patch only regardless of the fact its all about to blow back in. Very little civic pride and no cleaning crews which I notice other neighbourhoods in NYC have, paid for by businesses - why not Astoria? All that trash just ends up in the East River, and degrades our neighbourhood in the meantime.
