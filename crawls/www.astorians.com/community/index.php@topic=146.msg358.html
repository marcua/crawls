Allow me to elaborate (and, in the process, kill some downtime while I wait for a meeting to start and some proofreading to find its way to my desk).  Or, you can check out www.metopera.com.  Cheap seats are something like $36.  

Tannhauser is by Richard Wagner.  Don't get scared.  Tannhauser isn't The Ring and the Valkyries don't show.  Just Elizabeth (the beautiful and good daughter of the landgrave) and Venus (everything you'd expect from a goddess with a name like that) who represent the polar opposites between which the main character is torn (asceticism and hedonism).  Tannhauser is a talented minstrel, but a tad offensive to the good people of ... whatever that village is.  In the midst of a village talent show, he tears into a bawdy ballad praising the voluptuous charms of Venus.  Call the censors!  Really, the music is my favorite part - particularly the pilgrim's chorus - but this opera is a hotbed of discussion topics.

Yes, Wagner had a nasty side to his personality.  Still, his music is amazing, Tannhauser isn't as long as a Ring opera and doesn't require the same level of commitment, and is a good way to explore a fundamental question:  how a racist nut job can manage to create out a profound work on love, redemption, sacrifice and humanity.  

Still no meeting.

The Tales of Hoffman (Offenbach) Hoffman has very, very bad luck with love.  But the music is very nice.  I listened to this on CD a long time ago but I've fogotten about most of it.  Hoffman has to chose between a real woman and his muse.  Given his track record with women, he should go with the Muse.  You can read the synopsis here.  http://www.metopera.org/synopses/hoffman.html

Kat'ya I know nothing about, except that it's by Leos Janacek, a Czech composer.  The libretto is taken from a play by Ostrovsky and the synopsis reminds me of Lady MacBeth of the Mtsensk District - a young wife is tempted when her husband goes on a business trip, is mistreated by her in-laws, takes a lover, and suffers dire consequences.  I've seen The Cunning Little Vixen, also by Janacek, and it was good.
