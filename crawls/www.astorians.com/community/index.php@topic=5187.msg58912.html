Re: Disney

Disneyworld was so much fun!!  Exhausting, but fun.  We arrived on Sunday in Orlando.  The Disney Magical Express picked us up from the airport and brought us to the All Star Music Resort.  Along the way, we were fed a promotional video of Disney featuring all the characters on their way to Disneyworld, and all the magical things you can do there.  We were given a room facing the parking lot at first, but we asked for a room facing the gigantic guitar shaped pool instead, and they gave it to us!!  The resort is really nice, and they have 2 pools, the other is a piano shaped one.  They have an onsite cafeteria, which will slowly make you think of your school cafeteria, only with outrageous prices and less choices.  It is loud and even looks like a school.  But at least you can get food, because Disney has nothing in the way of a drugstore or grocery in their big city.  You need to get a car and go outside Disney for that.  It was at the Music Resort that we first discovered that something is terribly wrong with Orlando water.  It's tastes and smells really bad.  Even the fountain drinks are mixed with it and cannot mask the taste.  The shower and bathroom stunk from the nasty water.  Very strange.  As if they wanted you to purchase the bottled stuff.  Made me miss our NYC tap water, which is so much better.  The room was nice though and comfortable.  Yeah, it's the value one but it's decent, and you are entitled to use any of the pools or other amenities at other Disney resorts as long as you stay at one.

There are buses that pick you up right outside your resort every 10-15minutes and take you to the parks.  Epkot was probably my favorite park.  Soarin is a great ride which simulates flying over California's Golden Gate bridge, mountains, etc, it was really fun!  A lot of the rides at Epkot are actually films but they are fun nonetheless and we enjoyed them.  The lines weren't bad at all there, but that Fast Pass definitely came in handy. The thing is you have to get the Pass early, because they do run out of them, and you have to wait in the line after that.  The park was so big, we couldn't do it all, and we were so exhausted by midday even.  The fireworks are awesome!!  So pretty.  We visited most of the countries, the Mexico ride is being redone, but Norway's boat ride was fun!  They should have more finger food samples at the countries, instead of full dinners. Would allow you to sample more.  We had dinner at The Italian Pavilion - La Roma.  Excellent food, great service.  Not all the food sucks, I guess if you're willing to pay for it.  It was our one big splurge and it was worth it. 

It's the year of a million dreams in Disney, and cast members randomly hand out dreams to people in the park.  It could be a fast pass all day, a night at the Cinderella castle, a chance to be a host at a ride for a day- uniform and all.  We actually got a dream!!  We were so excited!  They selected 8 people or so to go ahead of the line in Soarin, and in the waiting room granted us our dream. You don't get to pick it though, we got collectible Disney pins that are apparently valuable (right), but it was very cool either way!

Magical Kingdom was alot busier, but we planned it well, because we took off on the train straight to Frontierland, where Splash Mountain is and Big Thunder Railroad Mountain.  We went on Splash Mtn twice, the second time convincing my mom to brave the 50ft drop.  Definitely in my top fave rides.  Hardly any wait in the mornings for these rides.  We went to go to Pirates, waited 20 minutes and the ride broke!! The audio went out, and they actually made everyone leave!  They gave everyone Fast Passes for any ride to come back.  So we came back later in the day and watched Jack Sparrow in a show, my bro got to be an honorary pirate!  Then we went on the ride.  They have incorporated Jack into it.  It was pretty cool.  Small World is a trippy experience but beautiful.  Had to skip some things, like the Haunted Mansion and Peter Pan.  Also such a big park, you can't do it all in a day.  They all are.  Rode Space Mountain twice. Awesome!  Monsters comedy show is funny, Buzz Lightyear shoot em up alien ride is hilarious, Stitch show is smelly fun, Nemo is cool.  Crush show is totally tubular man!!  Like totally!! Fireworks beautiful!  They have a light show parade that is great, before that.  It's strange though, they have that at 7pm, and the fireworks at 8pm, so you have to wait a half hour and do nothing before the fireworks.  We couldn't find a seat anywhere, and had to sit on the concrete.  Not particularly comfortable.  They shouldn't make people wait like that.  Everyone is exhausted, it's a 12 hour day!

Animal Kingdom got the shaft.  We were so exhausted we couldn't finish it all.  It definitely cannot be done in a matter of hours. It's huge!  We went straight to Dinoland, and rode Dinosaur.  Loved it!  You are put on a jeep in the dark, on a jungle ride to find the escaped t-rex.  This ride is so scary!   My family had such classic faces, I just had to buy the pics that they take at that crucial moment.  We could be on a poster for this ride.  It's hilarious!  Mount Everest roller coaster is pretty scary too.   The Safari ride is about the most waste of your time.  They ran out of fast pass, so you do wait the hour, and then you see hardly any animals!!  The driver goes so fast anytime you see an animal, you have no time for a picture, or even to study or get a good look at them.  Sometimes you don't even see the animals, they are hiding or sleeping atop some hill like the lion was.  It was such a waste of time.  You could have a better time going to the Bronx Zoo.  We were so exhausted we left shortly thereafter.  The gorillas on the trail walk are the most exciting - you actually see them, the tigers - they were hiding.  Couldn't find them.

Cirque De Soleil is the most amazing show you'll ever see.  It's a permanent show at Disney, called La Nouba.  We loved it!  I've never seen anything like it!  Downtown Disney is fun, didn't really go out clubbing.  Planet Hollywood is cool.  One piece of advice, make reservations.  Our first night we had none, and Rainforest Cafe had a 2 1/2 hr wait - that is unless you are rich people and bought into their members club.  Then you would get seating fast.  What a way to bribe the restaurant.  They won't seat you unless you give them money.  Nice.  All the restaurants had awful waits, we ended up at Wolfgang Puck Express that night, which wasn't great and still had a wait.  Thankfully, the rest of the trip went wonderfully because we had reservations everywhere.

Seaworld was fun too!  Lots of animal exhibits and awesome water animal shows.  Couple rides, they were fun too.  Still bad tasting water.  What's up with that?  We ate the Disney Boardwalk that night, was very pretty.

The last day we checked out, the airline takes your bags to the airport for you from Disney, which is really convenient.  We lounged by the pool all day before our flight.  It was great to finally relax in the sun.  We needed it.  All in all, a great trip.  I'd like to go back one day and do MGM and Universal.  I can't say I'd go back and do Disney every year.  It's not exactly a relaxing vacation, but I did have a great time.  It was magical.  We got a dream!  I have over 400 pics and a huge Disney vacation scrapbook website to create.  Wow.   :lol:
