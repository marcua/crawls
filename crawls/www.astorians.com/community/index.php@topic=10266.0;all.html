The standard of proof in a criminal case is much higher than in a civil suit -- though they were acquitted, they may still be found liable for damages under a less strict standard.

The prosecutor dropped the ball on this case, whether intentionally or unintentionally. The prosecution witnesses had criminal histories, making for impeachment material by the defense. The prosecution witnesses' stories contradicted each other, making for reasonable doubt. Remember, the defense can sit back and say nothing, it's the prosecutor's burden to prove the case. That they didn't prove their case doesn't mean the cops are innocent, it just means they weren't proven guilty beyond a reasonable doubt. When there are inconsistencies in testimony, when there are motives to lie and a shady criminal history, the judge is going to find reasonable doubt. And while any defense attorney (and most prosecutors) can tell you cops lie, for some reason, judges haven't quite gotten that memo yet. Cops will almost always be believed over civilians.

It's outrageous from a social standpoint to think these cops can get away with this, but from a legal standpoint, I can see how that happened.

As for the victims being bad guys in a bad place, that's just wrong. Even "bad guys" have civil rights, including the right to not be shot at by overzealous, trigger happy, ill-equipped to handle high pressure situation, cops.
