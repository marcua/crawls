I have seen the massive lots on Northern, though not since I lived in Sunnyside and had a car. I don't think enough of the other usual cohort stores are there for them to move into a big space with... it could happen though, but I'd think farther out on Northern going towards Woodside and such. Also not having a car I sort of don't even consider much of Northern -- it's easier to take the train in/out of Manhattan than walk or cab across the highway of Northern Blvd. It's not really a stretch of stores you want to amble down the way with -- it's like playing in the middle of I-80.

In general, shopping is a vastly different experience for those with vs. without a car. Even if they did open a B&N somewhere on Northern, without a car it would be basically useless to me -- training to the city would be vastly preferable.

I don't think Astoria has the volume of students the B&N on Court St does, with Brooklyn Law around the corner and Polytech a block away. Even if somehow the total numbers were equal, they are widely spread out all-over Astoria -- there's no central traffic point or any area as concentrated as LIC will be or Court is. There is also Brookyn City Hall and the Fulton St mall (which is actually very Steinway-ish) which generate more people and pedestrians. I just don't see any single point of concentrated traffic like Court St or anywhere in Manhattan -- Astoria just doesn't have the same geography.

I'm sure plenty of people here would welcome one though -- I sure would! I just don't think the conditions exist.
