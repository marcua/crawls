Why does the DoT so blatantly look the other way, and do NOTHING to stop the rampant double-parking in Astoria?  Whether it's Steinway Street, or 38th Street by Dunkin Donuts on Ditmars... or cars actually idling in MTA BUS STOPS!....and then today I actually saw some assholes double-parking on 30th Avenue.... an AVENUE (so very narrow!)!.... right outside a restaurant that was packed with diners....lots of cars trying to get by...lots of traffic...and this asshole thinks nothing of just parking himself right in the lane of traffic....making all the cars behind him wait...until no oncoming traffic is coming, so that they can then go out alongside him and into the OTHER lane of traffic.  It creates a huge domino effect and yet.... most people accept this it seems....

Why is nobody beeping?  Is it because all the other drivers are similar assholes, who do the same thing when they need to, and  so they accept it when it happens to THEM, because otherwise to complain would make them a hypocrite?

Most of these people who do this are from the neighborhood!  They can't walk?  Take the bus?  Or a taxi?  They must drive everywhere?  And what's next...they'll be whining that everyone 'has to double-park' now cuz the city is 'taking away' all their precious parking spots?   Well too bad. 

I've no idea how back in the day, a family of four managed just fine with ONE car.  Now every person in the family 'must' have their own car, and 9 times out of 10 it's a ridiculous monster SUV.  People are just so self-centered and lazy nowadays.

Astoria 101...what I mean about 30th Ave being narrow is that...if a car is double-parked, next to a Parked car, that means that that double-parked car is sitting IN the one lane of traffic for that direction, and that no one behind can proceed forward unless the opposite lane happens to be empty of traffic travelling in the other direction.  Clearly there is almost always traffic moving in both directions, so this means that a line of cars begins to build up behind the a$$hole double parkers, until such time as the opposite lane becomes empty, and then the entire row of cars must swerve out and onward, before more cars start heading towards them from the opposite lane of traffic.

As for scooters, yeah, I notice what they all do now is either blatantly go through a red light (I guess they think scooters aren't real 'vehicles') or else they will be real 'cute' and if they have a red light, they will instead take a slight right turn at the intersection, then swing left, so that now they are now facing a 'green light' and from there will proceed.  I do feel for these delivery guys...I mean, I get WHY they do it...they must get paid by the delivery and/or the tip for each delivery...and these guys get paid $hit and most folks look down on them.  It's tough work.  But that don't make it right.

Bicycles on the sidewalk.  I don't have so much of a problem with that so long as it's just the occasional bike, AND they are travelling slowly and taking care of pedestrians, AND they use a bell to let folks know they are coming (especially if from behind).  I mean, the streets (Steinway, 30th Ave, Bway, etc.) are very congested and dangerous for bicycles.  But when someone on a bicycle just speeds down the sidewalk and/or gives me no 'warning' they are coming, that really pisses me off.
