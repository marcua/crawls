Title: Another new place on Broadway
Post by: jennifer on November 20, 2005, 06:55:17 PM
We didn't even see this one coming -


Dandana it's at 42 - 21 Broadway
It's Egyptian (Moroccan maybe??) cafe/lounge

the logo has a music note in it.. but I asked the waitress about live music... and she said - maybe in the future.  They only had their grand opening 3 days ago.  So far just belly dancing  (on the weekends)

There is no liquor license yet.  

Food - was quite great.  We had... Lamb Tangine and two... I don't  know the name ...it's a chicken dish baked in Philo dough - it was sweet and savory.  Both dishes were a  little on the sweet side for my taste - but when mixed together with rice and the greek salad - it was really very good.  

service was... a bit underwhelming - but they just opened so i'll cut them all the slack in the world.  
On the other hand after my tagine with cous cous sat on the table for a few minutes with no cous cous... i had to ask.  the response was "oh... we actually ran out of cous cous.  Would you like some rice instead?"  So I had to wonder if she just wasn't going to bring me anything if I hadn't said anything.  They'll work the kinks out - hopefully after a few weeks.  

Prices were fair.  Most of the entrees were $15 or 18.  The salad was $7.  Egyptian tea - $3  

What's nice about this place is that although it has the hookah pipes like the places on Steinway - it also has a full menu.  Many of those cafes don't.  It's also a bit more upscale than some of the Steinway cafes - I think catering to a more mixed crowd.  Of course the prices reflect the differences - too.  I'm sure the pipes are more at this place than they are on Steinway.  But maybe not...   I wonder what it's like on the weekends -
