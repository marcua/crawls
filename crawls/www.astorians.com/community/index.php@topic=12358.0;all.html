We live thisclose to the bus stop on Ditmars and my girlfriend takes the bus at 7:30-8:00 three mornings a week and it never takes 8-10 minutes to get to the train.  The buses tend to bunch up or get caught in traffic with double parked cars especially between Steinway and 31st Street.  She budgets for 30 minutes so she is not late but 20-25 minutes is the norm.  If you drive by in the morning you will notice many bus riders hopping in the car service vehicles that pass by and charge $2.00 to the train  because the bus never runs on schedule.  The problem with this is you lose the free transfer to the subway.

I viewed some of the two bedroom two bath apartments with my friend who is a contractor/home inspector  when these were being built and they are large and nicer than most Astoria apartments.  If you are only renting and not looking to buy they provide the option of getting a two bath apartment (some apartments) or a small balcony.  I wouldn't buy here because the construction we saw  was pretty shabby in the units we viewed.  A friend of mine rented an apartment in one of these houses when it was just completed this past year.  She got a two bedroom two bath for $1,750 ( plus heat, electric and hot water).  She is scared how much extra that will add to her monthly nut when the heating season arrives.  She was able to negotiate the price down from $1,900.  She likes the apartment and the area because she drives to work but she hears a lot of noise from surrounding units (bad neighbors or shoddy construction?).  If you can negotiate a good price (everything is negotiable) and don't need to be close to the train this is a good option.
