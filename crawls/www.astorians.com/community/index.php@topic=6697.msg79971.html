Quote from: Ntrain on June 26, 2007, 07:06:27 PM

I also remember the blocks between 31st and 30th Streets.   On the south side of the block on the corner of Broadway & 31st  Gables was a mainstay for years and it was the first nice restaurant I patronized in Astoria.

On the other end of the block was a bar.   I remember a guy with a wheelchair was in there every time I walked by.

Also on that block was a jewelry store called "Goldstein's."   I remember the guy and his wife -- they were real old -- used to get on the bus on Broadway and go home.   I went in there once trying to get a Xmass gift -- at the 11th hour as usual -- and the guy tried to order a clock for me, but I didn't give him enough time.   I remember walking by when the owner retired and was closing the shop.   It is just a memory now.

On the other side of Broadway (north side) there were and I think still are two I don't know what you call them, health food stores maybe.   In any event, there was some nasty competition between the two businesses.   One may have opened after the other and the existing health food store took issue and there were some ugly racial overtones.   I think the owners of the "new" store were South Asian and the existing or other store started to tout its store's "cleanliness."   It got nasty and may have even made the papers.   I think the store still touts its cleanliness.

Each block has its own story and history.   What was the movie?   "Naked City" where the line was there are 8 million stories in the naked city?

Anyone remember Genovese?  It became Eckerd, which is now Rite-Aid.  So much for our neighborhood pharmacy.

When I was growing up in Astoria during the 1960s, my mom would take me to an ice cream parlor called Jacobi's near Immaculate Conception Church and when my dad was off from work he'd take me to one called Mehlhop's between 33rd and 35th Streets on Ditmars Blvd.  Apparently my mom's crowd from 21st Road used to hang out at Jacobi's when she was kid, so she kept going there with me after she was married.  In my dad's case, he grew up right near Mehlhop's, so that was his favorite place.  Jacobi's was gone by the time I was in high school but Mehlhop's, under a different name (which I've forgotten), lasted into the 70s.

wow,  lots of long forgotten places being brought up here..here are some more, forgive me if i'm repeating any.

-early 90's in the small storefront next to pollos mario across the street from the library there was a record shop. I was one of those people who loitered there and bought much great music in the short time the guy was there, This music critic Mike Gitter would go there and sell his promo music cd's and tapes after he was done reviewing them. I would buy new stuff for used prices instead of trekking down to St. mark's Sounds, that was great. I remember distinctly summer of 91 he was dropping a mega load of stuff and i asked him what was hot coming out music wise and he was adamant about it. He said Nirvana have a new disc and all the british music papers are calling it the second coming and by coincindence later that night I was listening to WSOU 89.5 where they played the first two tracks from nevermind and he was right, music was never the same again for me after that. I was sad when the record store guy said he was closing, i promised him to come by on his last day but I never made it. I kinda wanted to exchange numbers with him and maybe catch a show or something since we spent so many
hours jibber jabbering  about music. He loved Husker Du and the pixies I remember.

--the spot where pollos ala mario is was also a hopping bar for like a year back in 1990 called babareebas, i think?

--cookie kingdom with it's castle exterior and I think some sort of animatronic robot or doll that had a cookie that he put in his mouth?? located where queens county bank is next to the municipal parking lot.

--sampan chinese takeout right off 30th ave and steinway next to the old UA theatre. The owner sold out a few years ago, but i remember thier fried rice had real white breast chicken and bits of scrambled egg. His style of fried rice has been long abandoned for the new style that everyone has that sucks balls.

--across the street from sampan there was a chinese restaurant "dragon china"? that had booths and was like real fancy, at least to my meories from 30+ yrs ago. And i vivdly remember red..the place was RED decor and lamps etc.

--Steinway between 30th ave and 31st where kiddie world? is now was Playworld, OUR version of toys r us since we had none that was easy to get to until they set up on 48th and northern blvd. My mom joining in on the cabbage patch line and subsequent stampede, stands out for me. and to deter loiterers or shpolifters for some time they wouldn't let kids in without an adult accompanying them. So you woul dhave to wait for an adult to go in and follow closely behind them to sneak in.

--How about Eisenbergs a few doors down from where the dunkin donuts on steinway is now. They had china, chandeliers and all sorts of junk to litter a house with. They also had a full length 10'x6'mirror right next to their storefront which you could approach from like 10 feet away to get a good idea of how you looked walking. I always thought i looked too knock kneed and used to be self conscious and never missed a chance to walk to it  trying to add a lil bow leggedness to my stride to minimize it. at least in my mind..cuckoo!!!

--On steinway btwn 28th ave and 30th, but closer to 28th ave, Kind of where the kids afterschool tutoring learning center is now used to be S.L. Jacks, where you could buy anything electronic partswise made by all of humandom.
They had drawers full of fuses, wires, circuit boards. A hobbyists dream place. But I remember the staff really rude and intolerable of stupid questions, of which my 10 yr old science fair researching self had for them when trying to come up with a decent presentation one year.

--On 31st street near ditmars for only a year or two where Keyfood is now there was a rollerskating disco rink called Roller Royce? spent A few saturday afternoons skating to hits of the late 70's and early 80's there.

--How about astoria boulevard where now Burger King is a block before the 114th precinct. There was a huge Nathans of coney island..and in the early 80's they had a bonafide arcade room with like every video game ever made. Pure heaven!!!

--on steinway and 35th avenue Silverscreen's disco where the young greeks and italians wore their best neon greens and espadrilles and danced to TKA. But in like 89-92 they had a weekly friday night party called Berlin where alternative music was played. I remember moshing on the dance floor to fugazi's waiting room when the dj would take a request once in a while. and since we were poor 20 yr olds we would get liquored up out back where gold gym was on 40 oz Crazy horse malt liquor.

--on 23rd avenue and 31st street next door to sally deli was our neighborhood rundown stripbar Pennywhistles
skankaliciousness abound.

--broadway around around 44th street there was a corner store that was like an arcade that i remeber the kids from jhs 10 and bryant high school hung out at?
 
-- and finally not an establishment but a person. At the steinway subway station even to this day the panhandling light skinned black homeless lady with sunglasses. I remember seeing her back in the 80's and she still is around going strong, wow.

Wow, amazing post!  I still remember some of the things you mentioned, like Sampan and Playworld and Eisenberg's and the old dance club on 35th and Steinway, even though I was far too young to go back then.  Only one thing I'll point out: there was, in fact, a great toy store in Astoria before Toys R' Us rolled into town and aside from Playworld:  Valco, on Steinway between 34th and Broadway.  It was where the Lucille Roberts is now, I believe.  That place had a treasure trove of toys including some pretty obscure stuff that one would never find in a big-box toy store like Toys R' Us.  I think it disappeared, unfortunately, sometime around 1990.

I remember as a young kid going there with my mom, and often, stopping at the dry cleaners that was a couple of doors down next to what was the Modernage Diner (now the Astoria Diner)...Dandy Dry Cleaners was the name.  There was a friendly old guy behind the counter (Cuban, I believe) and I seem to recall there being lollipops there sometimes as well.

Also, on the same block but across the street, there had been a small Hershey's Ice Cream store for a few years.  I can't remember the exact location but it was closer to 34th Avenue than to Broadway.
