mirki2:
re: trail running shoes versus lightweigh hiking boots:

trail running shoes are probably fine so long as you don't have any significant elevation gain but you may be able to get a simple pair of ankle height ankle boots for the same price (and in some cases for even less money)

what you want to make sure you have for hiking is good support both for your feet and ankles. you also want to make sure your foot isn't sliding or rubbing - to prevent falls and to prevent blisters.

what I definitely would also think about is keeping your feet dry (from sweat, again to prevent blisters). you can purchase a pair of wicking liners at EMS (Eastern Mountain Store) that you wear as a layering pair between your feet and socks.

hope this helps.

Harlan:
I'm no expert on this, but my 2 cents anyway... There's a lot to be said for the steel shank in hiking shoes and hiking boots when walking on rough terrain. It really helps with comfort and endurance. I'd suggest getting a pair of relatively inexpensive and comfortable hiking shoes. If you're going to be walking on trails, the ankle support might not be as essential, and the shoes are cooler and more comfortable. I got a pair of Ecco hiking shoes recently that I really like. As for trail runners, I'm not sure that they have a steel shank, so I'd avoid them unless you're actually going to be train running... And I agree with mirki -- get a pair of non-cotton wicking hiking socks...
