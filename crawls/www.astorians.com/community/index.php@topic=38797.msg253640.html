While I have mixed feelings about the concept and menu, I have been wanting to give this place a shot (especially because it's from the Bareburger people, and I like that place a lot). There's just nothing on the menu that my kids would be even remotely game for trying, which seems odd.

We went and loved it.  We had the roasted broccoli (a must have - amazing).  The catfish was delicious.  Not Cajun style.  More meaty and flakey and a clean taste.  The mushroom ragu is also very good and filling.  I look forward to trying the ham in redeye gravy next time.  We sat in the bar area.  Super friendly staff and managers.  Great cocktails, whiskey, bourbon, and beer selections.  It definitely is a bit upscale but it's a sophisticated atmosphere without feeling over the top.  I thought it was very relaxing.  There were a few people with infants when we went but I don't see it being a great fit for kids who are a little older.  It's more an adult menu and setting.  I think they still want to capture the sidewalk cafe crowd, cocktails, and brunch.  That said, I wouldn't say it's not family friendly (saying from their perspective, not the OP)

That's good to hear, TJT! I was especially intrigued by the vegetables on the menu. Hope to get over there soon. To me (as someone who hasn't been inside to eat there yet), there's just a disconnect between the name/look of this place and the 'upscale' adult/sophisticated menu thing they are going for. The name "Burnside Biscuits" makes me think of places like John Brown Smokehouse, Queens Comfort, etc -- perhaps something even more casual than those. Which  definitely doesn't seem to be the case!


Astoria's got a lot of places like this -- some good/interesting things food-wise, but you can't quite figure out what they're going for with the signage/look/theme overall vibe, which seem 'off' somehow and either over- or under-sells the whole offering. One that immediately comes to mind is Nature's Taste (I think that's what it's called?) on Ditmars, which I think has good food, but I can't figure out exactly what's up in there and never feel tempted to find out.

I like Gothamist but find their restaurant reviews are almost always off the mark.  I do not care for Bare Burger either, I agree that it is overrated and falls way short of my expectations for burgers.

That being said, we had a fantastic meal at Burnside Biscuits.  The chicken was delicious - in fact, just two weeks removed from a highly disappointing meal at Root & Bone in the East Village, I was hesitant to get back on the fried chicken wagon.  Juicy, crispy and perfectly seasoned fried chicken, Burnside's chicken hit the spot last Friday night.  The biscuits were great also - flaky, fresh and buttery.

I was pleasantly surprised by how much I enjoyed Burnside Biscuits.
