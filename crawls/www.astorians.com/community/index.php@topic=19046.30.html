You know what- I too am suspicious of supermarket fish, especially the pre-wrapped kind. I'm always fearful of unwrapping it and smelling that dreaded ammonia stench. I asked the curly haired fellow if they carry cod (not scrod) and he said yes, but I didn't see any at that moment. I've gone to the fish market under the Ditmars stop for cod steaks. They've been very fresh in the past- haven't been there in a while, though. Lately, I've been buying frozen raw shrimp from Costco--Kirkland brand. I used to buy the shell-on deveined ones from Costco, which are great for New Orleans style barbecue shrimp, but the frozen raw (not cooked) Kirkland brand in 2 lb. bags are wonderful for tempura or scampi. No smell or off taste whatsoever-very fresh tasting.  I believe all shrimp from the fish mongers-no matter where you go- are previously frozen, then thawed, so might as well buy frozen to begin with. Best Yet's boneless chuck pot roast is great. I add some tawny port in the pot and we feast on red meat for three days...ugghh! Short ribs I've given up on because a few times I've had to trim off too much fat which is always on the underside of the rib, hidden from view until you open the package and flip 'em over. Their rib lamb chops are very good, but stay away from the manager's special! I feel sorry for the customer who has to resort to buying green meat to save a dollar.
