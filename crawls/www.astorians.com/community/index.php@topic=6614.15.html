I've read every theory and interpretation out there, and after alittle research and reviewing the last 5 minutes several times....

1. Tony is in fact wearing the same shirt when he walks in as when he sits down. One theory I heard was that he was wearing a different shirt, implying tony was imagining the scene..

2. As for the MO jacket guy being Phil's nephew from a previous episode, the actor, Paola Colandrea (from the credits) was never credited in any previous episode.

3. As for the 2 black dudes being the guys who tried to whack tony a few years ago (only got him in the ear) same as above, neither was credited before last night

4. Same with the truck driver dude, who i read was the brother of a truck driver christopher killed during a hijacking in one of the early episodes (the brother identified the dead driver).

5. Meadow does not have a look of dread on her face as she is running in. She isn't even looking into the restaurant, rather at the curb as she enters.

So my final opinion, and what i think chase was trying to get across, was that the 5 minutes of gut wrenching suspense we were feeling during those last moments was what tony will have to feel the rest of his life, constantly looking over his shoulder and having to think twice about innocent things like a regular guy walking into a restaurant. One other thing, the guy in the MO jacket looks around the restaurant a few times, but never seems to look at tony directly, more just a scan of the place......and yes, i have alot of time on my hands. I miss the show already.

That has to be one of the most brilliant episodes in the history of television. Absolutely brilliant. David Chase (show creator) has stated that he doesn't like episodic television where everything gets wrapped up because life isn't like that. Things are left unresolved, unfinished, ambiguous. People come and go and we don't know when or why.

The quick cut to black to me says that he got killed -- if not right there, then eventually. And that's how he'll go. Bang. Gone. No resolution, or chase through the alleys of Jersey City, or ferocious gun battle in night club. That's the life -- an evil life -- that this man has chosen. The violence goes on, the criminality goes on. The betrayal and paranoia. It's larger than Tony Soprano, who merely showed up on its gruesome stage for a spell... And it doesn't change the fact that he'd have it coming, as do all of them.

Hats off to Chase for pulling one helluva courageous move. He reminded us that while Tony's life was compelling, in the end this really wasn't someone we were supposed to care about, or sympathize with.
