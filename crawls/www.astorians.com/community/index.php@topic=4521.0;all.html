Okay, so I folded and used the last of my Christmas money to suck it up and go to Jeffrey at Devachan.

It was really amazing.  Unlike any other haircut I've had.  For once, I left thinking, "Hey! I can do that!" instead of my normal reaction to haircuts, which is to slink out of the salon with a lovely head of hair, knowing in my heart that it would never look like that again, and feeling guilty for it.

I barely noticed the scissors on my hair, although it's shorter and much neater than before (I wouldn't say "even", but my curls are at least even, which is more than I could say before).  We spent most of the time on styling techniques.  Things that I saw depicted in Lorraine's book made so much more sense when they were being done on my hair in person, and he also had some techniques I hadn't heard of before.

I tried them for the first time yesterday and it didn't look quite as good, but it still looked much better than before.   :mrgreen:  I also started to notice the problems with some of what I've been doing (specifically the clip trick, which I've finally realized I completely misunderstood).

I'm sold, much to the detriment of my pocketbook.  But at least I can now time my twice-yearly cuts for Christmas and birthday time, so that should help somewhat.

About a year ago, I discovered the Aveda Institute in SoHo.
