I do not buy the beans green and roast them myself, my supplier does that. 

I did a lot of research before picking them and tasted a lot of samples from different distributors.  Below is an excerpt of how they explain their roasting process:

"As one of the original Micro-Roasters on the Internet, We have earned a reputation of offering the freshest, highest quality coffee in the world. Our coffee is made from the top 1% of Class 1 specialty coffee beans and are carefully hand-roasted in small 'micro' batches and shipping the same day we roast it to all our customers. The difference in taste and quality between micro-roasting and other methods is like comparing fine gourmet food to cafeteria food. The quality, the preparation and the personal attention to the finest details is crucial is preparing great coffee.

We only roast in small batches. By roasting in small batches, every batch is roasted to perfection and there is nothing left over to the following day. Freshness is everything.


We know that coffee tastes best right after roasting, so we are committed to delivering or shipping our freshly roasted coffees immediately after the order has been completed. We often ship our coffees by next day Air all over the world. That is fresh and our customer do not mind paying a premium for great coffee."

"Costa Rica coffee comes from central Costa Rica with coffee grown between 4,000 and 7,000 feet above sea level.
