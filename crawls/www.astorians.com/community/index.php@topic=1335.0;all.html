Jim took me to Just Arthur's the day I finished the bar exam, so it's been awhile.  Like, more than a decade.  If I recall it was pretty old-school Italian plus steaks.  Knowing me, I probably had a steak.  I think I passed out after one drink, I was so utterly exhausted.  I've always been surprised that they've managed to hang on as the neighbourhood has changed.  Here's a review of it that's a few years old - no idea if that's before or after the renovations.

What about Amici Amore?  I've been to Butcher Bros. (their steakhouse), which was good but not outstanding.  The two restaurants share an entrance and a hostess, and you may be able to order from either menu.  Jim took me there for Valentine's Day last year, which was rather amusing.  I guess they assumed that the "ladies" would prefer lighter fare, and so were only offering a selection of Italian dishes.  Well, Jim had taken me to a steakhouse on purpose.  They did allow us to order from the steak menu after I complained, but it was funny.  Of course, the Butcher Bros. side wouldn't have many vegetarian dishes, but I think Amici Amore does.

I really would like to go to El Bouqueron again.

A couple of other possibilities:  Piccolo Venezia (haven't been; heard it's good); Kebab Cafe (can't resist putting it on there).  I've heard some not so good things about the Himalayan Teahouse, although I really wanted it to be good.  Has anyone had a good experience there?
