Think about it as a place you are BUYING into in addition to it being a place to live.  There is equity involved.

1) There is no board approval - that means anyone and everyone can live there.  You are buying shares in a place where no concern is given to who is moving buying, moving in, etc. 
2) They immediately allow subletting.  What percentage of apartments are actually owner occupied?  You're buying an apartment in a co-op that's essentially filled with "investment" properties, not into a community of cooperator owners.

Take a close look (or have an accountant) take a close look at the financials of the development.  How large is the reserve fund?  Have there been any assessments?  For what?  What kinds of major renovations need to be done that have not been done yet?

I've heard mixed about it myself.  Personally, I wouldn't buy at a place like that because it seems more about quick turnaround and apartment flipping rather than a true cooperative.  And I think things like no board approval just invites too much risk into your investment.

I love how people get so uppity about the acropolis and about parts of Astoria in general. The truth is, no matter where you are in Astoria, there are small time drug dealers, small time crime etc. I live on a very family oriented block, but down the street from me is that sketchy corner store. They have been held up twice. Ive also personally witnessed people selling drugs on that corner.

All along Ditmars a few years ago tehre was a rash of robberies. My friend who lives on 36th has seen small time drug dealing on her corner. People. We live in Astoria but we also live in NYC. Dont be nieve to think that because you live on a block with mostly houses or very quiet buildings that crap doesnt go on. Then you talk to people from Ditmars and they seem to think the Ditmars area is higher end than the 30th ave/Broadway area. Its all Astoria, people. Crap goes on. Thats not to say you shouldnt report such incidences or do your best as a member of the community to make it better, but hell, If you cant deal with the reality of living in the city, go to the suburbs.

My friend that lives in the Acropolis has a daughter and is very very happy there. Her apartment is so cute. French doors, and a great layout. She has never had bedbugs. Ive seen some DUMPS in the acropolis as well. And actually almost took an apartment there and while cleaning found a note from a previous tenant that they had a bedbug problem. From what I saw maintainance did their best to try to fix it, but... this happens in large buildings... Since they are all privately owned it just depends on the owner. Just know they are all small. I personally think you can totally work a small space if your organized and have an eye for design.

Dont let people scare you into thinking the Acropolis is like some housing project. It isnt.
