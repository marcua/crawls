Title: What's next?!
Post by: Christine on March 04, 2009, 02:32:51 PM
I was posting in the "closed hospitals" thread when somebody asked this question, and set me off on a rant. As Cannibal mentioned in the Dead Banks Walking thread, "de facto nationalization" appears to be the response.

Here's what I'd started posting as my rant:

It's like the fall of the Roman empire, American capitalism going awry. I always thought that when things collapsed, we'd have to become more like a socialist country (Canada, for example......or France.)

Sure enough, there's now talk about having the gov't step in to take control of the banks and other private companies until they're stabilized, then re-sold to the public sector. But talk like this is like blasphemy to many "red-blooded" Americans, who have been trumpeting our country's super-macho, world-power status and proudly-affluent lifestyle.

Now that the Obama-madness has settled down, looks like business as usual. He's all for asking the rest of us to make "sacrifices" for the sake of our fellow citizens, while our taxes go directly to the screwed-up companies that got us into this mess in the first place.

The gov't has turned into something like a parent that keeps buying new cars for the teenager who keeps wrecking them. WE- meaning, those of us who had been scrimping and saving ALL ALONG, never buying into (literally) the concept of consumerism- must now SUFFER so that we can give OUR SAVINGS to those parents!!  :x

The threat of the bad economy is what enables the gov't to keep us enslaved; enables our bosses to treat us like CRAP in the name of some "greater good" that is clearly a giant evil. We're told to trust the good name of America and its values, while the gov't exploits our national pride and trust.

But it's all so obviously WRONG.

Maybe nationalizing things wouldn't be so bad, even temporarily: Television would become public, and actually educational with fewer commercials (there's plenty of gov't propaganda snuck into TV right now, under the guise of "free press"); banks would be trustworthy; college would be somewhat affordable, as would housing and health care; celebrities might even be paid a little less, as income becomes spread a little more evenly......

As Colbert said the other night, "Nobody wants to use the 'N' word," but let's face it: Our country has fallen victim to its own greed, and nobody is enjoying the standard of living they should be due to corruption.
