Okay, so I folded and used the last of my Christmas money to suck it up and go to Jeffrey at Devachan.

It was really amazing.  Unlike any other haircut I've had.  For once, I left thinking, "Hey! I can do that!" instead of my normal reaction to haircuts, which is to slink out of the salon with a lovely head of hair, knowing in my heart that it would never look like that again, and feeling guilty for it.

I barely noticed the scissors on my hair, although it's shorter and much neater than before (I wouldn't say "even", but my curls are at least even, which is more than I could say before).  We spent most of the time on styling techniques.  Things that I saw depicted in Lorraine's book made so much more sense when they were being done on my hair in person, and he also had some techniques I hadn't heard of before.

I tried them for the first time yesterday and it didn't look quite as good, but it still looked much better than before.   :mrgreen:  I also started to notice the problems with some of what I've been doing (specifically the clip trick, which I've finally realized I completely misunderstood).

I'm sold, much to the detriment of my pocketbook.  But at least I can now time my twice-yearly cuts for Christmas and birthday time, so that should help somewhat.

Well, one brief disappointment with Devachan - the stylist very clearly told me at the time of my cut that he would do two touch ups for me for free before my next haircut in June (my cut was in January).  Sounded great to me.

I called the salon tonight, a little bit overdue, to see if he was there tonight so that I could pop in for it.

The girl who answered the phone and the manager both told me that I'd misunderstood, that was only for bangs.  "But... I don't have bangs.  He was looking at me when he told me this.  He could see I didn't have bangs.  He just cut my hair, for god's sakes."  It wasn't a miscommunication - we understood each other perfectly well at the time.  I have the card he wrote everything down on for me.

They insisted that I can either come in and get another $85 cut, or take a free cut as a subject for one of their classes.  No other options.  I can't afford another $85 cut, and was really looking forward to that touch-up, which I understood to be included in the price at the time.

Kind of a disappointment after I was so excited to go there in the first place.  I'll probably look into going back to David Ryan now, they at least have pretty good customer service.  And to think that Devachan almost had a customer for life over here.

About a year ago, I discovered the Aveda Institute in SoHo. I can't go any where else now.  It's a school, but they are training people to work in the very top salons in the city.  And the best part ever: $20 for a cut.  A real cut.  They do a good job every time on my curly hair.  The only thing that might be an issue for you is that you never get the same person twice so if you're the kind of person who wants one person to know your hair, this isn't a good option for you.  I like experimenting, especially if I know it will look great.  I highly recommend them.  When you call, you'll be on hold for a while and it's annoying but $20 for a really great cut.  I'll take it.  212-807-1492 ex. 2.
