I've been there twice since they've opened and I'm not thrilled.  So far their best feature is the open windows onto the street.


First time I had the Foie Gras sliders - which were excellent, followed by the turduckin meatloaf - which was so heavy I had 3 bites and I was done.  My dining companion had the cobb salad with pork belly.  The egg was deep fried - initially interesting.  the salad was so greasy we couldn't eat it.  Too heavy on the pork fat and too heavy on the tarragon.


Next visit I had the Foie Gras Sliders again - and was disappointed.  They were presented in a very different manner and just weren't good.  I had a light tomato salad and my dining companion had the PBLT - which was quite good, but again, kinda greasy.


I know pork is a fatty animal but I've had plenty of pork products that didn't leave me feeling like I had lead in my stomach. 


Service was a little slow and the place feels a little unfocused.  They're new, I get it. 


I'm a believer in supporting local so I will try them again - I don't wish failure on any business that isn't rude to me.  I just wish the food were just a little better.

My wife and I went out on a very special early dinner tonight to William Hallet.  It's been almost a year since our nugget was born and, therefore, almost a year since we've been out on a date. 

The atmosphere is cozy, I like the dark wood, nothing fancy but clean and comfortable.  The service was great, staff was very friendly and attentive.

And the food was incredible!

We started with the asparagus appetizer, wrapped in prosciutto with shaved (parma?) cheese.  Awesome.

My wife had the salmon with fingerling potatoes and citrus fruit.  She loved it.  She said it was the best salmon she's had in a while.

I had the NY Strip Steak with "wild greens."  I had no idea what wild greens would be so I was pleasantly surprised when I got swiss chard with a side of steak fries.  The chard was delicious with hints of orange, the fries were really good, and the steak was outstanding.  It had neurons firing in my brain like crazy - taste overload.

We finished it off with Whoopie Pie.  Now I'm not a big dessert fan but I actually enjoyed this.  Two pieces of brownie-tasting chocolate cake surrounding a marshmallowy cream filling with a shot of condensed milk on the side. 

I really enjoyed this meal.  The prices were about right for quality food in Astoria. 

Oh, and the beer was great too, good selection of drinks.

This place is now on my list of favorites.
