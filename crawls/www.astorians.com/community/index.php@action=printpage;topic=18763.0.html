Title: Re: 3 alarm fire on Astoria Blvd
Post by: oshma1963 on July 12, 2010, 09:24:05 PM
That's unfortunate about your experience in the RC office. If you were treated rudely, you should report the issue to the supervisor.

Here's the deal--I am a Red Cross volunteer responder and I got called away from my regular job to help out on the 6th. We had 14 fires and vacates that day, and at the time the Astoria fire was going there were three other fires waiting for responders.

The last I heard, the city cut Red Cross funding by 75% for this year. 40 people have lost their jobs in layoffs.

There just isn't the money to put everyone up in 4-star hotels for an unlimited time. The Red Cross has contracts with hotels that will house people on no notice, within the five boroughs. The housing is of last resort, for people who don't have friends or families who can or will put them up. The goal is to get people off the street, into a safe place, for the first 24 to 72 hours after a disaster. Beyond that is the realm of other agencies.

(We just closed the shelter we opened the next day for the dozens of families displaced by the two 5-alarm fires in other areas in Queens. You might have noticed http://origin.ny1.com/content/top_stories/121859/firefighters--union--fdny-sees-busiest-week-in-decades (http://origin.ny1.com/content/top_stories/121859/firefighters--union--fdny-sees-busiest-week-in-decades) this article, noting that the FDNY had its busiest week in 30 years. That means the Red Cross had ITS busiest week in NYC in decades.)

If you want to know what really goes in to the 2600+ responses every year, come and volunteer.
