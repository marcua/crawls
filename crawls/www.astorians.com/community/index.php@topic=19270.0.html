I have noticed that there are two distinct factors on this site, Natives and Transplants. I would think this sort of phenomenon is typical in any place. If you grew up with it, you're used to it. Go to the places the "transplants" are from, and you'll probably find all sorts of annoying little things about them that would never occur as complaint-worthy things to the people living there.

Case in point: growing up in suburban Connecticut, it never struck me as weird that I had to drive 10 minutes just to buy the littlest thing. Now that I live in a city where I don't even need a car, I get annoyed when I go back home and have to drive all the time. Also, lawns pretty much strike me as being a high-maintenance waste of space, time, and money, whereas when I lived there I thought of lawns as being a normal part of life. So yeah.

Re: Natives vs. Transplants???
« Reply #5 on: September 06, 2010, 09:01:58 AM »
It may seem that way but if you tally up the list of complaints voiced here and elsewhere, people new to the neighborhood complain about the same exact issues that longer-terms residents complain about – and have been complaining about for decades.

The only difference is that newer residents do not have the benefit of history and perspective that longer-term resident have and they haven’t had the benefit of learning to live with some of the problems that affect the area.

You can’t expect someone who’s lived in the area for 6 months to have the same perspective or reaction to things to someone who has lived here 6 or 60 years.

In fact I’m both relieved and dismayed that the complaints on this board are exactly the same ones that my grandparents, parents have made and I have made.

What you are attributing as differences between natives vs transplants to me is more a case of someone new to this website not having the perspective of some the people who’ve been contributing longer.

When I started to frequent this website I too thought there was this great divide between old and new…not so much any longer.

What I find more amusing than is that someone farts and within seconds a post will appearing saying what was that? I chalk that up to being a wonders of the digital age.

Re: Natives vs. Transplants???

I am a native.

IMHO, the difference between the natives and the transplants is as follows:

The Natives put their money where their mouths are. They not only make the phone calls, but they follow up by attending the appropriate Community meetings in order to publicly voice their concerns. They arrange to leave work early to be on time to attend these meetings; they get involved in their Community's affairs; they make certain the message gets out.

The Transplants walk the walk, talk the talk, but they sit on their hands for anything else. They also prefer a quiet, clean neighborhood, but won't make the valiant efforts necessary to participate in the decision making process because many of them are already planning on either going back to where they came from or are looking to get away from the things that are disturbing them.

Complacency, self-absorbency and self-righteousness serve no good to a Community.

It really does take a village.
