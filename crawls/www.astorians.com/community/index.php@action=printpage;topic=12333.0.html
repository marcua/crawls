Title: El Jamon: an opinion
Post by: 23rdSt on September 18, 2008, 05:23:27 PM
My gf and I went to El Jamon (2551 Steinway) today for a late lunch.  The restaurant was fairly empty, with spanish music playing in the background.  We set down and ordered from the prix fixe menu.  I got the lentil soup for app, then pasta with littleneck clams for main course, and flan for dessert.  My gf got the grilled pork for app, grouper in white sauce for main course, and chocolate mousse for dessert.  After the order, we got an amuse bouche of chorizo and croquettes, which was nice.  Bread and olive oil came shortly afterward (good oil, btw).  After that, the lentil came.  It was a nicely cooked in a delicious broth, with bits of chorizo in it.  I really wanted to finish the big bowl of lentil, but decided to save my appetite for the main course and desert. 

My main course--pasta--came pretty quickly after the soup.  There were about a half dozen clams in a white wine/(seafood?) broth with tomatoes, onions, and maybe a bit of saffron.  The pasta was cooked al dente, as it should be.  It was a simple pasta dish, but slightly too salty (not unforgivable).  It was okay, but any decent Italian restaurant can do this much better.  I ate most of the pasta, but did not finish as I was starting to get too full.  After a short breather, we got our dessert.  The flan that came was just standard restaurant flan, which is to say it wasn't mind blowing, nor was it bad.  While we were eating our desserts, the waiter brought out some sweet croquettes and a sweet dessert wine.  The gesture was appreciated, but we did not fully appreciate the whole thing since we were both bursting at the seams.

Overall, it wasn't an exceptional experience, but a slightly above average lunch for a very average price ($12.50/person).  After tax, tip, and drinks, we were still under $45.  While this is restaurant week, we learned that their prices are the same as with their normal menu.  The restaurant is about 6 months old, so it still has lots of room to improve--foodwise.  The service was very good for us (perhaps because we were the only people there).  We both left in a semi-food comatose state of being--our palates satisfied.  We do want to come back and try their dinner menu at some other time.
