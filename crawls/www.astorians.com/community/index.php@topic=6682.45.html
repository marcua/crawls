I don't think merm meant to come across that way.

I have hesitated to jump into this conversation because I've thought merm should be the main person to address this, but because I also consider this something I devote time, money, and energy to I'll go ahead.

Typically I personally don't like advertising that infringes upon a site visitor's experience. Some websites that use advertising become "cheapened", if you will, and it sounds like that is how a few people feel about it here. I would normally tend to agree, but being in the situation that I am (web/graphic guru, contributor of personal funds on a monthly basis for the entire existence of Astorians.com/.net, etc., etc.), I have to admit my perspective is a bit biased and relative.

While neither merm nor I spend a tremendous amount of time posting new topics or posts we do spend a significant amount of time maintaining the website and trying to make it better. Being the web people that we are we're always trying to figure out how to make Astorians.com better. We're asking ourselves "Does this work? Will people like it? Is it intuitive?" In order for the site to grow and to benefit a larger and larger group of people we have to spend more and more time working on it - and with increased traffic, there is an increase cost when it comes to server load/space.

Astorians.com would not be what it is without the continued participation from regular Astorians, but because there is an increase in traffic as well as an increase in the amount of time it takes to manage it, we do feel that we need to figure out ways to help with those types of costs. By no means is advertising going to make us a lot of money, but it will help to defray some of the costs. We also really appreciate the people who have donated money, either via direct donations or via the online store. Truly! Your contributions not only help us out financially while we try to manage this great big wonderful site, but they also help remind us that there is a great community out there that want to help us.

You're absolutely right - advertising is not the only way to get support, and I'll agree that it isn't ideal to the user's experience, but as merm has pointed out people are not forced to donate. I'll also point out that we're still playing around with ideas. Being in the web industry (not just here - my day job as well), I will say that things are constantly changing. Even advertising on the web is changing. Everything online is always in flux, so Astorians will likely continue to change and evolve as time goes on.

I didn't mean to make this so long, but my main point(s) here is that Astorians.com = really great website, and merm/wasabisam = really dedicated to seeing what works best for everybody involved.
