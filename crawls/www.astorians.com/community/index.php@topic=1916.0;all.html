Dry scallops are scallops that have not been soaked in the preservative sodium tripolyphosphate (STP), which severely irritates my mouth.  But besides its effect on me, it also brightens the colour (makes them whiter), plumps them so they are heavier, and extends their shelf life.  Since they have more liquid, it is difficult to sear them properly - they weep in the pan and you end up practically steaming them.  Generally, if the scallops you see in the market are white and glistening, they are probably "wet" scallops.  If they've been treated, they are required by law to be labelled as such, so if you are not sure, you can ask your fishmonger - or ask to see the box if they don't know what you are talking about.  However, if they don't know what you are talking about, I suggest you change fishmongers.

This is my fail-safe basic scallop recipe exactly as I gave to Flooz:
 
Lay the scallops out on a cutting board and remove (by hand) the tough little piece on the side.  It's inedible.
 
Set a timer for 1 minute but don't start it running.  Heat a nonstick pan and add about 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter per person. (12-16 oz. of scallops serves about two people.)  When the butter starts to brown (you want the pan pretty hot), quickly add the scallops, putting the biggest ones in first, and in the hottest part of the pan.  Start the timer (or start looking at the clock).  After one minute exactly, flip the scallops.  Cook for exactly one more minute.  Remove scallops from pan onto plates, leaving butter in pan.  Reduce heat and add juice from one lemon to pan.  It may spatter.  Swirl together, and pour over the scallops before serving.  You could make a cream sauce at this point instead of lemon by adding some cream and reducing it a little (it's also good with part of a fresh vanilla bean scraped into it - once, I did a lemon-vanilla cream sauce, but without the bacon).

For the bacon: brush each piece with maple syrup and bake until it starts to get crispy but is still flexible.  Cut each piece of bacon in half lengthwise, wrap around the raw scallop and fasten with half a toothpick.  Make sure the bacon strip doesn't hang over the edge of the scallop - that will prevent the scallop from making full contact with the pan when you sear it.

North end of Union Square, Monday, Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday, from 8am until about 6pm! Year-round, rain or shine! It's not the biggest farmer's market ever, but it's got very very good stuff.
