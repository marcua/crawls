I grew up on the Air Force base in Dover, DE... located next to a swampy/marsh area known as St. Jones.  The morning air was frequently filled with an "eggy" smell that came from the marsh.  Typically this was due to an environmental imbalance, and although it was possible for the local government to address this environmental issue and not only kill the smell but help create a healthier ecosystem, we all just got use to the smell and did nothing about it.

I've smelled the eau de poo wafting about Ditmars and although my childhood experiences with odd smells makes me feel a little desensitized to this issue, knowing that this is a man-made/industrial issue and not just an ecological imbalance makes me wonder why more isn't being done to rectify the cause.

It's a form of pollution, regulated or not, and needs to be addressed.  For those that live closer to the smell, attempting to either open a window or use an a/c unit bascially makes your entire apartment smell like a port-o-potty.

I haven't seen much media attention given to this, so maybe the next step is to try to involve them.  Especially if Vallone's response smells like a load of fresh Astoria air.
