I work for Parks answering correspondence from the public, among other things. This is actually an excellent era for the department since Bloomberg is all about improving the parks. So you can be sure that the e-mails and letters you write are being read and taken seriously.

It might take a while to get a response (about two weeks, sometimes more), but that's b/c each and every one is routed directly to the people responsible, then back up for the Commissioner(s) to approve and sign.

Each park gets inspected, and assigned ratings of "acceptable" or "unacceptable" for many criteria, including litter, cracks in the sidewalks, graffiti, even bird/dog poop. Pictures are taken as evidence for each inspection report.

In fact, somebody wrote in a few days ago regarding a lack of bike racks in one park. The Commissioner himself made notes before this e-mail was routed, saying that bike racks are absolutely necessary in every park, and that we should be including them in all current and future construction projects. I routed this to the construction teams for each of the five boros, and today received confirmation that all those responsible were made aware of this important requirement.  :mrgreen: How feasable it is, we'll see, but it's certainly encouraging. Shouldn't be too difficult.

What sucks is how the general public is just ignorant- there are people who think that by littering, they're providing jobs for people who need them (aka, people who are beneath them and *deserve* to pick up after them.) They forget there's only a limited amount of money in the budget, and we can't employ a clean-up crew for every man, woman, child and dog that visit every park.
