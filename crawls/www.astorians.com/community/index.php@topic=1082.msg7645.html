I was also disappointed in the new Health Food Store.  I thought it was misleading to advertise "Cafe" when there are no stools for the several small tables.  I inquired into the owners who said that they were planning to have a cafe in the back in the spring.  The prices are high.  Many of the items can already be found at Key Food.  It seems to me, that the items offered should not duplicate a major store, who can already offer lower price items.  One thing I can say, for my health needs I really like the home baked vegan food.  The lentil vegetable soup was really good.  It is really great to find food that is dairy free.  When I told them what my needs were: no nuts or dried fruits in addition to dairy free; they said they would tell their baker.

Just thought to add a bit of flavor to this mix (no pun intended); comparatively speaking the prices are not unreasonable, if you want to argue specifics there will always be one or two items in every store that you can find cheaper somewhere else.

The food (made onsite mind you) almost always has a crisp, clean and savory essence, I say almost because not everything is to my liking... The staff is amazing, from the owners down to the jack of all trades (whose name is Ricardo, he introduced himself) have an extremely positive and attractive vibe which has from their self-effacing beginnings brought me back time and again. In a neighborhood where community is a priority, these people have it sown up.

Their work ethic is tremendous, up until recently there have been very few times that I have not seen one or more members of the ownership there, working, not just overseeing.

I for one am glad to have a healthy alternative to key food, mcdonalds and pizza that nourishes not only my body but my soul; they usually have great music playing too  :-D.
