I was absolutely amazed at the crowds of parents and children trick or treating along Steinway and Broadway. I looked to see if there were any police at the intersection and of course there was not.  There seems to be a warped sense of the custom when parents take their children to commercial streets and stores instead of homes of friends to enjoy a treat. It is almost as if parents feel like their children are entitled to the candy. The worst I saw was a large group of parents with small children and carriages walk into Quinn's Funeral Home to trick or treat.  What kind of values are parents teaching their children these days?  It was a day I was NOT proud to live in Astoria!

I just don't see the problem with trick or treaters going to stores, particularly since not many people welcome trick or treaters at their door.  I lived near the main street of my town growing up and I went to homes and stores--I'm sure if I lived in a city with apartment buildings I would have only gone to stores.  The stores have candy, the kids want candy and it's probably a lot safer than knocking on doors.  I don't like rude children anymore than the next person, but trick or treating always comes with a small dose of entitlement--even if the children say please and thank you.  Also, it's a relatively cheap way for businesses to get people into the store and foster a little community support.

In jackson heights (where i grew up...y'all should be sick of hearing that by now) the apartment buildings are so large you don't have to leave. heck, my mom's building is so big, we only had to do half to get almost 2 full bags of candy each! most of the kids who lived in houses knew someone who lived in a building and tagged on. i never rang a house bell. and i never went to a store. but some kids did. i think that's why JH started the children's halloween parade (which is sooooo cute).
in our neighborhood we predominantly have brownstones or we live in multi family houses that aren't necessarily condusive to trick or treating. no one comes to my building. i don't even know if the 2 kids next door go out. so i can understand why kids would go to stores. if there's 3 other units in your building, that's not much fun. what i don't like about it is that it's become legalized begging. kids run into stores, with their parents (sometimes) and don't care that people are trying to shop, and in some case for candy for the little beggars. worse yet, as i said in another post, are the parents with their own bag who feel entitled to the same goodies. and then they get testy if a storeowner says that its only for the kids, or if the cashier or whoever takes too long to dole out the candy.
i miss old school trick or treating-at night with your parents and respectfully. i always said trick or treat and i always said thank you and smiled. even if it was the crazy lady.
