Yeah, I find getting up to be a mind-game, myself. I'll tell myself before I go to bed that I WILL get up earlier than I have been, but then when the time comes I'm totally convinced that I need to sleep more and I'm really tired and I have nothing to do anyway until I go to work (which isn't true - there's plenty I could do before work that I can't do after work because of the night shift thing). It's something you just have to make yourself do, and don't be tricked by your brain making you feel like you need more sleep, because you probably don't and you'll realize that once you get out of bed and start doing stuff. It might help to come up with something to do before work, maybe go to the gym or something, that you commit to doing every day.

Sadly I have no choice but to get up when my alarm goes off (or at least within 10 minutes) because I have a Metro North train I have to catch at a specific time. I wish that I could sleep in and be more lazy like when I was at my last job when I wasn't tied to the train schedule.

I've noticed that I have a tendency to fall back to sleep more easily, and thus make it more difficult to force myself out of bed, when I go back to my "go to sleep" position. It is a position I've always gone to sleep in virtually my whole life (and it makes me fall to sleep faster), and if I revert to that when I hit the snooze button it's torture in a few minutes when I really absolutely have to get up. If you have that sort of position, maybe try to lie there in a position that makes it easier for you to stay awake.

I have to say as well that it seems like you should probably find a more enjoyable job or find a way to tolerate your job more. I have a decent job, too - good pay, most of my coworkers are very nice and easy to get along with, good benefits - but I would much rather be at home. Mondays are nearly always frustrating to me because I've spent the last two days staying exactly where I'd rather be which is at home and with my husband (and my cat - can't forget about him). Of course, my objectives in life require me to be in the situation I am, otherwise I would most certainly be in a different occupation (likely lower paying, part time, and less stressful).

Anyway, I wish you luck. You should probably also try to get to bed earlier. Maybe 9:30ish. That could help you too.
