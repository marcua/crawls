« Reply #22 on: June 24, 2008, 11:22:00 AM »
Thanks everyone for all the well wishes. We're both okay. My cards are cancelled and they didn't actually get my bag, I just gave them my wallet, so I still have my keys, phone and ID, which life would be a lot harder without.

It's hard not to think of all the things that I could have done better or differently to avoid the situation. I know that the fact that it was late, that we had both had a few drinks (but if you know me, you know my tolerance! ha ha!) and that we were having a really good conversation made us less observant than I usually am. The walk we were making last night is one I've made alone many times. When I would make the walk alone, I was always a lot more careful and suspicious of others. Being with someone made me feel like I was safe. I guess what I've learned from this is that just because you are with someone, you aren't automatically safe.

Also, I need to find a way to thank the two gentlemen who came out of their homes at near 2 am to see what was going on. It's people who do things like that that keep Astoria from being the kind of place where these things happen regularly. They came outside and scared them away before anything bad could have happened and stayed with us until the police got there. While getting mugged will make me a little more wary around the hood, overall the experience has shown me why I love this neighborhood. When I was in trouble, my neighbors came out to protect me and my police officers responded quickly and did everything they could to help me. Beyond that, I have a group of people here offering their support. It really means a lot to me that this is the kind of community I live in. I'm really proud to be an Astorian.
