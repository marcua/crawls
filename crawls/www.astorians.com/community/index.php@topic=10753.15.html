Re: Have you been affected by the economy?
« Reply #22 on: June 12, 2008, 04:30:12 PM »
It has definitely affected this family in small ways. I send money to my parents in Europe every month and what used to be a decent amount is now nothing because of the weak dollar. In order to maintain our budget at home, (and its a tight one) I have to shop around a lot more now than I did before for the best prices. I don't buy much organic stuff anymore and if something is not a main ingredient in my cooking, I might buy the cheaper option. My husband has to drive around to do his work, so you can imagine that the gas costs are outrageous and he is driving a car that has good gas mileage. We did a lot of experimentation this past winter with ways to save money on our heating bills as well. Some things worked, some did not. We used to be able to save money every month, put something away for our children, and contribute to retirement accounts, but we have not been able to do so for many months. While we are not "struggling" the fact that we are not saving for the future has me very concerned. Also, almost every bill we pay (electric, gas, phone, cell, internet) has gone up this year. We will not take a vacation this year because we just don't have the money to do so. I hate that my children are missing out on the opportunity to visit a different place and experience something new. We will probably do some day trips and pack lots of food and drinks from home. like I said, nobody is struggling or starving here, but the prospects for the future don't look great. I am picturing me and my husband working into our 80's and never being able to retire. I think that is going to be the case for a lot of people.
