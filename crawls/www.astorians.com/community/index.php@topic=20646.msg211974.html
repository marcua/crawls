We had a really good meal here last night.  I loved the stuffed chili appetizer, a roasted poblano filled with quinoa, couscous, and beans and covered with a walnut sauce.  The filling has a coarse texture, rather like kasha, and the whole thing tasted great.  Very much enjoyed the fish and moo shu duck tacos, and the sweet potato side is smooth, creamy, and surprisingly savory.  We ordered the El Diablito cake to go, and it was graciously comped by the manager.  The cake has a bit of a slow burn to it, so if you get it keep in mind that it will be a little spicy.

We'll definitely be back!

I had a nice dinner here with my friend Friday night.  I won't be redundant here echoing everyone's love for the tacos.  They are excellent and the food is awesome.  What I will say is that this is a small restaurant and it's too small for the amount of people who want to eat there.  Its popularity isn't fun for those who wish to sit back and enjoy a meal.  I definitely want to go back but I'll go on a weeknight when it's not as busy.  There's barely any room to maneuver in there so depending on where you are sitting, you will be bumped all night by people trying to get by.  It can be hard to enjoy your food amid all the commotion.  And the busboys were a bit aggressive about clearing the plates.  Put your fork down for one second and they're already trying to bus the table.  I had to guard my plate and I hate that - you end up feeling rushed.  I understand they want to turn over tables, but at the same time - let me eat, lol!  We didn't even bother with dessert, it was so uncomfortable.  So, while a nice place and I loved the food, I'll go back when they're not so mobbed.

We were there Saturday night as well.  We got there early (6.30) on purpose because we had a toddler with us, and we got the last table available without a 20 minute wait.  It was clear as we ate that they were absolutely slammed, and there was probably a minimum of half an hour wait the entire time we were there.

We found the food to be excellent, the drinks (El Boludo, Pachanga Margarita) were very good, and the service was friendly and not annoyingly rushed despite the crowds.  We had the trio of salsas with chips to start, and the veggie jenga, duck moo shu tacos, and pork belly enchilada as entrees, with a shared side of pureed sweet potato.  The jenga looked enticing (and actually perfect as a brunch entree), I had had the duck tacos a couple of weeks ago (and they were awesome), and the pork belly enchilada was a knockout.  The chocolate and fig mole with the enchilada was outstanding.  The sweet potato puree was possibly the best I've tasted and hinted of lots of butter.  We finished the meal by sharing a piece of diablito cake, and we all agreed that it was a great ending to a delicious meal.  I find the Baby Jesus cake at Vesta too sweet for my taste, and the restrained sweetness of the diablito cake is perfect.  It lets the mild chocolate and aromatic spices come through beautifully.
