Not really -- or at least not enough to single out Astoria.  I have been "greeked" a few times, i.e., got the cold shoulder because I obviously wasn't.  But I just wouldn't go back to that particular place!  It's that simple -- treat me like an idiot, I'll spend my money elsewhere.  I've also had the experience of Greek shopowners being nothing but kind and wonderful.  I gather most businesses want your money?  If they don't, that's cool, too!

In terms of all-around racial crap,  I don't see all that much, aside from kids, who indulge for shock value and are as easily bypassed as dog excrement on the sidewalk.  I did get into a beef at my laundromat with a fellow customer, but that was far more an intelligence issue than any sort of ethnic/racial thing. 

(The place was crowded, dryers were getting to be scarce, I had my clothes ready, saw the last open one, save there was a sock in it.  I took the sock out and put it on the "lost" rack.  Put my stuff in.  Next thing I know this woman is all over me about taking her dryer.  "You stole my sock!" she cried.  "No," I responded, "I put it on the lost rack.  Where are your clothes?"  She stares at me.  Because her clothes are still in the washer, and she thought she was going to somehow "reserve" a dryer by putting a lone sock in one.  Er, uh, no -- the owner sided with me on this one, after she tried to drag me into all sorts of weird territory -- you're a bigot, you have no respect for women, etc.  I'm white, laundry owner Asian, and she was Latina.  I kept my cool and didn't play along.  Funny thing is, I see this woman all the time now, and we somehow have become friendly, probably because I came back and apologized to her later for causing a scene, even though I'd done nothing wrong.  You got to bend like that sometimes -- it makes a difference.)
