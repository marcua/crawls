Losing Waterfront to Developers
« on: April 19, 2006, 08:55:33 AM »
I understand a lot of people are upset that we are losing our waterfront to developers.

Their mouthpieces in media and politics talk about public access, but they hope we forget that Shore Towers 'public access' has been restricted for years.

They talk about community facilities, but look at Citibank's gallery: open until Citibank needed the space, then bye-bye (although the building remains, of course).

These monsters will add to traffic congestion, more power plants, more cell phone towers, and will block the views of the waterfront for long time residents.

They will be unhealthy. Living above a power plant will undoubtedly inflict all sorts of nasty illnesses to residents, particularly vulnerable children.

City maps show that much of these developments are in low lying areas that will flood when NY gets hit by a hurricane. Thats all we need, thousands displaced. Another Katrina bailed out by our taxes.

It is chilling to note that the new touted greenway is north south on Vernon along lines of proposed development. There are no links east/west between the community and the waterfront. I guess once again, unless you are living on the new projects on Vernon, the rest of us are just not part of the calculation.

They will spend millions on it so we can ride a bike along the ass end of a power plant, or look at all those rich people blocking our view of the waterfont.

A number of letters have been written to local papers expressing these concerns. They have not been printed.

Along the Hudson Valley (from the Battery to Albany), and the Brooklyn and Manhattan waterfronts, public parks are planned. Take a look at the East River Park on the lower East Side. Built in the 1930s by tearing down development and building ball fields.

Why doesn't someone on this board take a look how communities all over the country are being connected with their waterfront. Then explain to us why Queens is starkly different?

Why are we losing our waterfront to private developers?

Why don't we demand the waterfront get turned over to the community?

In March, this letter was written to the Western Queens Gazette which refused to publish it. It is currently on the website of www.licalliance.org

I am writing in response to the cover story in the Queens Gazette this last week about the proposed Silvercup West development project. As a member of the Screen Actor's Guild, the thought of more film production in New York is an exciting and welcome idea to me.

As a resident of Long Island City, I feel I must be circumspect about any such enormous development project and urge its promoters to realize that we have heard many such promises in the past of waterfront access, the attraction of visitors and all those wonderful things when similar projects have been erected.

One example is the City Lights complex, which did indeed provide a lovely waterfront park and then proceeded to block off its pier access last 4th of July to any person who did not have a special pass from City Lights. Can we expect this elitist attitude from Silvercup as well?

Another example is the Shore Towers in Astoria, which promised improved waterfront access for all, and then 6 months after it was built closed off the waterfront "esplanade" to all but its own residents.

What we are then left with are looming edifices that add to traffic and congestion and make it necessary for Con Ed to blast out more pollutants from their power plants to satisfy the increased need for electricity, but no real benefits for the existing citizens of Long Island City and Astoria.

You do mention jobs, but how many locals will really get those jobs? And don't forget the many cell towers that will have to go up on every apartment building in the area to provide cell phone access for all those extra residents.

When we citizens of Western Queens read these types of articles, which only give the developer's point of view on such projects, we are easily taken in and persuaded of the wonderful new way of life we will all have when the project is completed.

We need for you to print points of view from opposing factions, as well as objective projections about the reality of the jobs, pollution, traffic congestion, etc., so that citizens can really ponder the pros and cons.

Sincerely,

Georgina Young-Ellis


Member - Long Island City Alliance
LICalliance.org
