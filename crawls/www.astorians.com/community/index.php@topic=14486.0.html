What do you think the average electric bill for a newer 2 bedroom will be?

Pretty difficult to say. Electricity is quite expensive here, ending up around 20 cents/killowatt-hour, I think. A modern fridge uses a lot less energy than an old one. Do you have a giant plasma TV? Do you plan on using a space heater or air conditioner? Do you have CF bulbs in most of your light fixtures? (I like 1000bulbs.com for a good selection of high-quality CF bulbs.) There's a lot you can do to minimize your electricity bill...

I've been safe budgeting about $100 a month for gas and electric.

1 bedroom apt, heat supplied by the building, 42in LCD TV (I was actually surprised how much juice the TV uses), CF bulbs everywhere

Some months it's a few dollars more, but more often than not, it's less. It all depends on how much I have been home for the month and how much cooking I did, etc ...

The monthly delivery charges (the fees charged even if I don't cook a thing or turn on a single light bulb) for gas is about $20 and for electricity is about $35 anything on top of that is dependent on how much electricity and gas I use.

Hope this helps ...

When you're shopping around for apts, ask to see electric bills or maybe even call ConEd to see if they'll give you a history of usage & costs to get an accurate picture 

We're in a 2 BR, 2 BA, about 1000 sq ft, building is new-ish and $150 would be a dream bill to get.  Everything is electric for us, no gas lines in the building, so that means stove (which we use almost each day), heat/AC unit, hot water heater, etc all draw on electric.  We are not big heat users at all (full sun exposure almost all day), but come the summer we're running AC pretty heavily.  All energy rated appliances just a few years old & compact flourescent bulbs everywhere.

Highest we've gone over $400 last summer & least I've seen in recent winter is still almost $200.  So just offering another perspective for you to consider.
