Bartolino's Expansion
« on: January 14, 2014, 09:10:32 AM »
Hey All!

As I was walking to work the past few days, I noticed a somewhat significant change happening on Broadway b/t 34th & 35th Sts.  Bartolino's seems to have taken over the former Bartolino's-owned Tippin Inn (which was Rendezvous before that) next door as well as a real estate (actually I forget what used to be there) place next to the former Tippin Inn to now be one GIANT Bartolino's - but with three storefronts.  The new "Bartolino's" sign is up spanning over all three storefronts.

Here's the thing.  I'm baffled as to why.  I have eaten in Bartolino's a few times over the course of the past 10 years.  The food is good, decent NY Italian, the portions are not huge and the prices are high.  The original space itself was MASSIVE by Astoria (and NYC) standards and I never saw more than 3 or 4 tables occupied each time I ate there (many at peak meal times).  They also play music that wouldn't be sutied to the atmosphere (Maroon 5 as opposed to Sinatra) which I found odd.  Friendly and older waitstaff who have been there for years.  But, again, I am shocked about this expansion.  Due to the amount of clientele I have seen there, I would've thought we would be saying goodbye to Bartolino's rather than seeing it expand.
