S Prime Steakhouse
« on: December 10, 2012, 10:38:10 AM »
Will start with their website http://www.sprimenyc.com/  The place is in between Studio Square and Five Napkin Burger on 36th Street and off 35th Avenue. We celebrated a birthday there last Friday evening and had a party of more than a dozen people.  It was a phenomenal success.  We spent an hour and half in the bar area first enjoying their nightly drink specials and platters of oysters.  It's a great deal (1$ oysters) and they're fresh and delicious.  Then we sat to dinner and the food was truly incredible.  The foie gras appetizer was a huge hit.  As were the bone marrow pieces.  Portions are generous and the food is cooked to perfection.  I had the 28oz, 28 day aged rib eye (55$).  It was the best steak I had in a very, very long time.  Cooked prefectly and served piping hot and juicy.  It was outstanding.  My mother-in-law had the scallops and she loved it.  Very nice portion served beautifully and very flavorful.  The wine list is well rounded.  Even the desserts were a hit.  I can't say enough.  We dine out a lot and can be very critical.  But this place gets 5 stars from me.  They treated us like VIPs and there wasn't a single dish that we didn't absolutely rave about.  I can't imagine this place not being a gigantic success.  I can't wait to go back.

The service there is also some of the best I've experienced. I'm glad they are part of the neighborhood.


I like Christos just fine, but prefer S Prime.

Just wanted to post our fantastic experience at S Prime last night. 


I had purchased a groupon because it was mentioned in Fooditka's recent review and I had been meaning to try it.  I was a little skeptical that we would receive proper service because (a) I find most establishments who offer groupon-like deals diminish the quality of their service with groupon customers [Cafe Artistes [sic] RUINED a great meal with the worst service - and now they're closed], and (b) Fooditka mentioned the service was sub-par.


Our service from start to finish was FANTASTIC!!  The food was great - we had foie gras, porterhouse for 2, sauteed asparagus, and a trio of creme brule for dessert. 


To begin, they brought out an Amuse Bouche (sp?) of warm wild mushroom soup that was truly delicious.  The steak took a little longer than we expected but the waiter we had checked in often and explained that it was because it was such a thick cut (I had never seen such a thick cut!).  The plate wasn't sizzling like at Ben & Jack's but the steak was tasty.  The dessert was great, too, and they even gave us complimentary glasses of Moscato at the end.


The prices are steep - especially for Queens.  We paid what we would normally pay for a steak night at Ben & Jacks (or the like), minus the Groupon.  But that being said, I don't feel that it wasn't worth it, in fact, quite the opposite. 


Lastly, we met the chef, Joel, who was very friendly.  We had a nice chat with him in which he told us about some upcoming changes to his menu - like a different foie gras each quarter.  He also mentioned that as good as his steak is he thinks the fish dishes are even better.  They had a lobster special last night that sounded OUTRAGEOUS - and I totally would have ordered it if I wasn't fixated on steak. 


I know this topic hasn't been updated all year but I wanted to reawaken it.  Joel pointed out that they're in a terrible location with almost zero foot traffic and I wouldn't want a great restaurant like this to disappear.  For those of you who haven't seen it it's right next to 5 Napkin Burger (on 36th Street).  They have happy hour specials like 1/2 priced drinks and $1 blue points, too. 


The groupon is no longer available but I encourage you to go.
