Delivery Faux Pas?
« on: February 21, 2008, 11:51:25 PM »
OK, so tonight I decided to get some yummy delivery food because I was feeling lazy. I had a menu left here by the previous tenants, for a place that will remain unnamed (I don't to launch any specific attacks here and incur wrath as on the "good, but not as good as Sac's" pizza thread...). I place the order, give my address and phone number, no problems, right? Well, my food comes and the delivery guy CHEWS ME OUT when I answer the door because he says I live too far from the place and he had to ride his bike all the way to my place and the food got cold and therefore is not as good.

I had no idea what to say, he totally took me offguard as I didn't expect the delivery guy to yell at me when I paid him for the food (and I gave him a big tip too, before he started ranting). If I live too far away, why didn't they just say that when I gave them my address? I assume they've delivered to this place before, and that's why the menu was still here. I'd even made sure to do a little online research to make sure that delivery to the Ditmars area wouldn't be a problem (I'm weirdly obsessive about being as well-informed as possible about small things).

So that experience kind of ruined what should have been a really tasty dinner. And yeah, the food was a little cold, but a minute or two in the microwave and in the oven and it was good as new. Which was not actually that great. I guess I won't be ordering from them again for a few reasons.

Not sure if I'm looking for "here's how you f'd that one up" or a "That's happened to me too!" here in replies. Maybe I just had to rant a little myself.

While not revealing the name of the place, how far away are you from it? Regardless, it's not the job of the delivery man to yell at customers for ordering from far away.
