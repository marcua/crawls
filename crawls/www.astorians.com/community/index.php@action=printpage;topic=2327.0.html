Title: Re: Yikes!
Post by: wasabisam on June 11, 2006, 08:20:47 AM
I kind of rather think that this practice would be a no brainer. I have hesitated to put too much personal info online because of my paranoia about this sort of thing being implemented, although it is easy to do so, and I probably have. I don't have anything to hide, but the more that the government has to work with the more that they can use it to their advantage and possibly my disadvantage.

In some ways this is clearly a good thing. It seems that I hear about would-be-Columbine-copy-cats being caught through their candid posts on MySpace every month or so. Clearly some of these kids/adults/whoever are wanting to be caught, otherwise they wouldn't be so public with their plans - either that or they are just plain stupid, which is probably a good bet as well.

I do agree that there can be a lot of valuable and interesting research that can come out of this sort of practice. How they use this research is the scary bit.

However, the government cannot really be blamed for spying or snooping on MySpace (or Friendster, etc.) when the information provided there is provided voluntarily for anyone to read. It still feels like they are stepping on my democratic toes, though, but I recognize that I can't get fully mad when I have done half the work for them just by being social and candid online.
