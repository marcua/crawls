I work out there too and it's great. The equipment is very good and well maintained.

Lois is a very good trainer. I don't work out with her, but chat with her a lot and watch
her interact w/her clients. She's enthusiastic and there's always a lot of laughter - a good sign!

Dorothy is also terrific. I take her classes and she's awesome. Breaks down moves step by step
has a very common sense approach along with a dry sense of humour.

Like the previous poster said, if you join, wait a little while to pick a trainer. Watch the different
ones and see who appeals to you and even ask their clients what they like/don't like about them.

With a trainer, chemistry is important, so you want to make the right choice.

Good luck.

I finally broke down and cancelled my NYSC after they sent me a letter saying they're raising my fees another $2 for the umpteenth time. I checked with my new health insurance Oxford and they offer a discount for $57 a month thru WellQuest at the Rock.  They offer one at Synergy for $27.  So I joined the Rock, still a little pricey but cheaper than it normally would be and cheaper than my NYSC membership.  And it's convenient, clean, has lots of classes, and open 24hrs during the week.  One complaint, not all the cardio machines come with tvs.  Not too many meatheads at 10:30am, although one had his cell phone on him and took a long phonecall and no one ran up to him to tell him to get off.  If you have children, it must be awesome.  I think the childcare is free, and the playroom looks fun.  They have a tv set up in the gym so you can see your child in the playroom while you work out.  They also have this little old lady aerobics class, it was the cutest.  And I'll have to try that salsa class sometime soon :)
