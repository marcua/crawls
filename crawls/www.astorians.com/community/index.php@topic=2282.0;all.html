NOT!!  So I left work at 6pm today, and am just now getting home at 8:15pm.  I walked from the not running NW at 49th to 42nd to the 7, waited 2 trains to fit into the 7, sweated bullets, pushed my small 5'1 and half inch frame out of the train at Queens Plaza, only to be told the N wasn't running there either.  It was a mob scene.  We were all herded out like cattle to the street, the police weren't letting people even in Queens Plaza, only out.  No cabs in sight, buses??  I start the long walk home in the pouring rain to 27St and 20th Ave, proceeding to call every friend possible who has a car.  I finally reach a friend who proceeds to inform me he just got home on the now running N train!!  I get back on at 36th Avenue having walked from Queens Plaza, sweat some more, and after another half hour finally arrive home.

Now why is it this happens every f-in time it rains in this city??  The tracks flood and the transit system fails.  We have a drainage problem, nyc transit knows this, yet our money doesn't ever go to fix the underlying problem.  Hello politicians??  Hello Bloomberg??  Does anyone care about the lowly commuter??  So we just have to suffer every time it rains and our antiquated drainage system can't handle it.  My usual 20min commute home took 2 hours and 15 minutes.  Lovely.    :-P :-(

I apologize for the outburst.  That will be all for the evening.

I heard various rumours from other passengers, such as sick passenger, switch problems, signal problems, fire in the tunnel, flooded tracks, power outages, stalled train, terrorist attack, impromptu strike, and my favorite: there's a big party somewhere and they forgot to invite us.   :-D

To top it off there were no buses running into Queens from the other side of the 59th Street Bridge. I waited over 1 hour for the Q101, and got fed up and ended up walking home over the bridgein the rain with other pissed-off Queens residents. My poor dress shoes didn't even last one month this time  :-(

I got home just before 10pm tonight. I could have walked home, back then back to work, and then back home in the 4 hours it took me to get home today.

Why can;t the MTA get their act together and communicate to the riding public? If there is ever a serious life and death emergency, the riders don't stand a prayer in the subway because there nobody tells us anything.
