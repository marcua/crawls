I ate at this place twice over the weekend:  Dinner on Friday and brunch today.
 
I'm paraphrasing from the review I just posted on Yelp as I think this place is really great and will very quickly grow it's following:
 
On Friday night they were packed to the gills but very accomodating in getting us a table for four.  Because of how busy they were the food did take a bit of time to come out on Friday night.  That said, Christine (one of the owners) came by right after we ordered and explained that everything is made to order so it does take a little extra time (trust me - the wait is worth it!).  When it appeared that our food was going to take longer than normal both Christne and Kathy (the executive chef) made a pont to come by and apologize and offered us free dessert for the table (a very generous offer which we happily accepted given what we had heard about the desserts here!)  The food was amazing across the board and we all left with full tummies and smiles on our faces.


Today, I met up with friends for brunch and the food/service was equally as wonderful.  Again both Christine and Kathy stopped by the table to say hi and you can tell that they are very excited to be giving something unique back to the neighbourhood (I believe that all three owners are Astorians!).  Also, you can order from the regular menu if brunch isn't your thing.
 

As in all new businesses, there are bound to be hiccups in the first few weeks but this place has been going to great lengths to get things ironed out right away.  It's a welcomed addition to the neighbouhood and I can't wait to slowly eat my way through the entire menu!

I had brunch and dinner there over the weekend. They have a large selection of brunch sandwiches on excellent ciabatta from Leli, plus a few other brunch dishes, and also their full menu, as others have noted. My sandwich was very tasty, although not as spicy as the name (The Spicy) implied. I also tried their Australian iced coffee (despite the weather) because it has ice cream in it. How can that go wrong? (It can't.)

I went back for dinner because I really wanted to try their works burger, with toppings popular in Australia, including pineapple, beet slaw, and a fried egg. It was awesome. The meat (which is sourced from Australia) had outstanding flavour, and I enjoyed the toppings, even the pineapple, which I was a little uncertain about. At first I thought the roll might be a little too dense, but it stood up well to the giant stack the toppings created, and lasted the whole burger without disintegrating. My next burger there will be a plain one, though, simply to get more of the meat flavour. Definitely one of the best burgers I've ever had.
