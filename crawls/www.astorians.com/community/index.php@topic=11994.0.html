There's no hipsters in Astoria?  That's news to me.  It may not be Williamsburg or Park Slope or Greenpoint but I've seen *plenty* of hipsters in Astoria.  Somehow I also don't think the lack of indie music stores is it...I'm sure when hipsters first made their way to areas like Williamsburg, there weren't many indie music stores or organic food markets there either...Williamsburg was a pretty dumpy area, in fact (much more so than it is today!!!)...empty lots, abandoned buildings, burned-out buildings, etc.  Those things all came with time.  I think one difference, though, is that Astoria, unlike Williamsburg, isn't largely a "clean slate."  There were many thriving businesses in Astoria long before any "hipsters" made their way here, and Astoria, as a neighborhood, wasn't much different than it is today.  It certainly was not an economically depressed area, which would be one of the attractions for many (poor) hipsters to move in initially.
