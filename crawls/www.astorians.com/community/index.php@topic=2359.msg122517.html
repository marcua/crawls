LIRR and subway etiquette are different things

I don't know if you'd ridden the LIRR, but I used to commute everyday on it.  Let me tell you, it's worse than the subway regarding noise from people.  Cell phones ring non-stop and people are very, very loud on them.  It's enough to drive one mad.  I'm a real nice guy, but take this one time when this girl just began going through her ring tones during a ride home.  People trying to nap were now woken and people reading were disturbed.  You sometimes HAVE to say something on the LIRR because these people honestly do not realize they are being rude - it's like they were never taught about other people existing.  The girl was shocked when I told her to stop, like I was among a train full of deaf people who could suddenly hear.
