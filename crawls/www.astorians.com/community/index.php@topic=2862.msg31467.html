Take a look at photos from 50 or 70 years ago. The buildings were beautiful when built. Walk around Sunnyside Gardens or Boulevard Gardens. Queens was the nation's laboratory for planned communities during the first half of the last century.

Back 30 years ago some of the places you admire in Brooklyn were hell holes. Battered, graffiti covered, dangerous. They look different now. Why?

Today Queens had 10 times more illegal conversions than any other borough. Front yards are paved over, trees cut down, basements a rabbit warren of cells, and the cheapest lowest quality of housing is encouraged. Check out the blogs around the city: Queens in general, and Astoria in particular, is now a laughing stock when it comes to urban style.

The problem is our leadership is stale and complacent. The have bred apathy in the public. The papers churn out one real estate puff piece after another. You would think that Elmhurst is a delight compared to Williamsburg.

Its up to you to demand something better.
