We just stopped by after noticing this place for the first time the other day (strange, because we live around the corner on 36th St.).

Overall, I would give the experience a 7/10.  The food was pretty good, but there were a bunch of things that annoyed us.  I am going to sound overly picky here, but I think in order to really succeed in this area a place needs to be close to perfect.  I am very excited about all these new places opening up on 31st Ave and I want this place to succeed.

1) No real wine list.  While they had a little menu listing the beers and the by-the-glass price for a few generic wines, it was bothersome that they have a big bar set up with a ton of wine bottles displayed prominently, and no list of what they could serve or the prices.  We didn't bother asking for the list and my girlfriend ordered a glass of pinot grigio.

2) No other beverage list.  Aside from concerns about what I'd do if I had kids with me, I don't always want to drink wine or beer with dinner, and I had no way to know what else they had (tea? Lemonade? Soda?) or what the prices were like.  I ordered a Coke.  What I received tasted more like Pepsi (and it was listed as Pepsi on the receipt), which I personally don't have a problem with, but some people are picky about cola.  This brings me to the next and most irritating thing;

3) No free refills on soda.  If you have a full bar set up and I can see the soda gun, I really should not be charged for refills (and if the sodas were listed on the menu then I could at least have known about it ahead of time).  The waitress asked if she could "refresh" my soda, which I knew was ambiguous, but accepted anyway.  I had a total of 3 glasses (small glasses, which when filled with ice probably contained about 8oz of soda).  I ended up being charged $2.50 for each glass of fountain soda, which I find totally unacceptable and a complete ripoff.

4) Music was horrible and too loud.  They had some weird mix of recordings of live covers of popular songs, which were just plain not good.  We sat in the back corner and the music was too loud for me (though I am picky about loud music).  I think music should be ambient and not a distraction so that I can enjoy conversation with my company.  About halfway through, they switched to more appropriate oldies (Billie Holliday, Ella Fizgerald, Louie Armstrong, Frank Sinatra).  This was much more pleasant to listen to (even though it was still slightly too loud for my liking).

5) Fried shrimp/artichoke/zucchini was good, but almost entirely zucchini.  The appetizer cost $13 and had only 4 pieces of shrimp and 3 pieces of artichoke, and a heaping portion of shoestring zucchini.  Though tasty, this was just totally unbalanced.  Where was the rest of that artichoke??  And only 4 medium-sized shrimp?  Lame.


As I said, the food was pretty good for the money, at least as far as the entrees were concerned.  I don't think we'll go back any time too soon, but we will eventually.
