« on: January 12, 2008, 02:03:33 PM »
So, I'm not really sure what category this should go under.  However, since the I'm looking for the help of someone (a services) of someone that bought or sold something (a bar), I had my personal assistant monkey throw a dart and this forum was chosen.

Anyway, I'm looking to purchase a preexisting and profitable bar; preferably in Manhattan, but a boro would be considered for the right place and price.  I have quite a few years experience in the service industry, including some as a bartender, and I received a degree in Entrepreneurship, so I figure it's a decent business for me to get into.

But, I'm not really sure where to begin my search, both for the actual bar and for brains to pick about experiences.  So, have any of you bought/sold/managed/etc. a bar before and feel like sharing the experiences and wisdom with a budding entrepreneur?  It can be on here, to get a dialogue with others going; on the phone, if you've got a lot to say and don't feel like typing it all; or in person, if you feel like getting together for a coffee or maybe a beer or two (on me, of course, for info exchange payment).

Any help is appreciated.  Thanks for your time.

Brian

Well ive never owned a bar before but a have alot of friends in the business and ive basically hung in those bars/clubs alot thru out the years so i learned a thing or two abt them.

1. Manhattan is very expensive  rent wise , so u better be bringing in a ton of cash everynight . Competition is also very tough so  whatever gimmick or happy hour trick u can think of im sure someone  has been done it or them endless times.

2. If your are looking around astoria , as u can see the big time spenders basically go to greek bars. You can easily see this by seeing all the mercedes and bmw's outside and occasionally a ferrari or lotus .EVERYONE is out to impress or so it seems . I know alot of your may say its daddys benz or beemer but who cares , money is being spent in those bars  so regardless of how they got it , they spend it too !

3.Are u planning to go into this alone >? cuz if you are be prepared its a long long road getting home 6am  every night .( alot of cleaning happens even after the bar is closed at 4am)

After athens cafe was the main place for coffee on 30th ave  no one thought any other cafe would make it there.  So then up pops AVENUES and GRAND    and FLOW  as well . All basically next to each other down 30th ave.  They all seem to do very well for what they offer , as as soon as spring lurks in  im sure their cash flow skyrockets.

There are places in astoria that have been around for a long long time ,  like dna, caprice , central and cavo , but there are others that open and close  months or even weeks later .i can name like 10 off the top of my head but wont mention them. The ones that have stayed open seem to have a big following .   

I dont know much abt irish bars but places like McCans on ditmars and 37th(i think)  seem fun  since they get the happy hour crowd who also is hungry after work.

If you are looking for a bar that will  have VIP bottle service then irish bars arent the thing.

Think abt what u want exactly cuz there are many options to choose from , each with some risk.

Thanks for the reply, thanos.  I know the long hours.  I used to work, and close, a bar on the UWS.  Adding to getting out of there after cleanup was a train ride back to Astoria that hovered between bad and terrible, time wise.  I'm a night owl anyway, so it doesn't bother me. 

While Astoria seems it might be a decent place to start a bar, I think I'm aiming more for Manhattan.  McCann's kinda has the market cornered in the Ditmars-area that I live in, although there is a little room for another bar with the right idea.  I like McCann's, but I don't go there often because there is NO room.  If you get there after the seats are gone, it's kinda a pain.

So, it leads me to believe that there is potential for more bars but, on the flip side, Stini Yiamis (I dunno if I spelled that right) is never very busy and neither is Les Amis.  Both are great places, they just don't seem to have the business volume.

Personally, I don't want to deal with food; although I know it's usually a boon for getting more people in at more hours, which translates to more money.  But, I don't really want to deal with the excess inventory, employee, and clean-up requirements. I am not looking for a VIP bottle service type place, as I would like it to be a place that I would personally enjoy hanging out.  My middle class roots leave a bottle service place out of this inclusion, as I don't think I could charge what I hear they do (I've never been to a place like that) for a bottle. 

So, enough of what I don't want and more of what I DO.  I'd like to have a pretty chill bar/lounge mix.  There is a place and I can't remember the exact name (Music Box sounds familiar, but it doesn't have a sign) in the EV/LES area.  It's got a bar in the front, with a pool table and couches in the back and a smoking patio outside.  Perfect mix of a place, IMHO.  Good vibe, good crowd, etc.

We'll see what happens, I guess.  Only time will tell.  Thanks for the post and, if you have anything else, please fire it off.

I've never worked in or owned a bar, but as a potential customer in the Ditmars area, I thought I'd respond to some of what you've mentioned.

Quote from: SeminoleB on January 17, 2008, 11:14:03 AM

This is exactly why I think that McCann's *doesn't* have the market cornered in the Ditmars area.  I hardly ever go to McCann's for precisely what you mention - there is NO room.  I'm too old to go to a jam-packed bar where I get beer spilled on me and I have to yell to make my friends hear me.  When it's not crowded, I like it well enough, but that is so seldom that it's just not worth it for me at this point.

Quote
So, it leads me to believe that there is potential for more bars but, on the flip side, Stini Yiamis (I dunno if I spelled that right) is never very busy and neither is Les Amis.  Both are great places, they just don't seem to have the business volume.

I'll tell you why I don't go to Stini very much, or Les Amis at all:  Stini is nice, but their beer selection is terrible.  They have no taps and a very weak bottle line-up.  I'm not a fan of most mixed drinks, and that's what Stini focusses on.  I also enjoy wine, but they basically have just a couple of reds and whites.  That said, it is a nice space, the music isn't too loud (most of the time), and it's nice to go there to meet up with friends sometimes.

Les Amis -- for me, it's probably a holdover from when it was Matty's.  But they've never done anything since it became Les Amis to make me want to come inside.

Quote

I would love a place like that nearby.  I'm not familiar with the exact place you're referring to, but a chill bar/lounge mix is something that Ditmars doesn't have at all, and I think it would be a great addition.  Food for me would always be a plus, but from a business standpoint I certainly understand why you wouldn't want to serve it.  One of my favourite bars near Union Square doesn't serve food, but they have a stack of menus you can order from at any time - that can be a workable solution.
