Hi Astoria, my name is Hank I am 72 years old. I grew up in Astoria many years ago. I lived at 23-20 32nd St. which is between 23 Ave. and 23 Rd. It was my maternal grandparents home and we lived in the downstairs apt. We lived there until 1951 when we moved to 38th Ave. and 30th St. when the house was sold after my grandparents passing. We lived there until 1955 when I graduated from LIC High School and we moved to New Jersey. I still hung around with my friends in the Ditmars/31st Street area since LIC was dead.

Using Google's Street View I see that the Ditmars theater is now a post office and the Grand Theater is now a Key Food market, they were great places to spend a Sat. watching a bunch of cartoons, serials and a couple of current films. A bargain at 12 cents as was the Astoria Pool at the same price.

Hanlon's bar is still there but the rest of 31st St. is new to me. There was a small bowling alley of about 16 lanes beneath the East side El exit at the Ditmars station, one level below the street level when you came down the stairs. It closed in the Summer since there was no A/C and the wooden alleys warped from the heat. There was also a pool hall across the street one flight up above the street at the West side exit stairs. Neither of them were the family type bowling alley or pool room of today. They sold beer to anyone with the price and there were always big money games going on among the gamblers. Part of growing up.

The pool hall was above a night spot on the ground level where our local hero Tony Bennet sang under the name Joe Bari. Tony lived on 33rd st. right around the corner from me and his cousin was my best friend, Tony's brother John took us fishing. Tony was Mr. Cool with the first pair of mirrored sunglasses us local boys ever saw.

At the South end of 32nd St. at 24th Ave. the street went up a hill as it does now but it was not paved and ended up in a steep hill going down toward 31st St. where there is now a parking lot and a store, that was called Chinky Hill and was the neighborhood place for sleigh riding. The very un-PC name was a mystery since there were no Chinese families in the entire area. I since found out there were Chinese farms up there with the farmers selling their produce to Chinese restaurants in Manhattan. There was also a pearl button factory there before my time but the whole area was still littered with mother of pearl pieces that were what they used for the buttons. My grandparents told me about that so it was probably there in the 1930's. It might be the old building on the West side going up the hill, that was there when I was a kid but was never anything but an empty old building.

My last visit to Astoria was twenty years ago and I stopped in Bohemian Hall for a couple of beers and some memories. That was a busy place with parties, and big celebrations on Memorial Day, July 4th, Labor Day and any other reason for getting together to eat, drink and dance the polka. My moms family were Czech immigrants and Astoria had a large Czech population at the time. 

This probably belongs in a nostalgia forum but I could not find one. I found a lot of interesting info on my old home town while doing some research on my ancestors. There is more than meets the eye in our town's history.

Thank you for the responses, I see Astoria is still a friendly place.

Naturally I did hang out with a couple of Greek kids, plus some Italians, Irish, etc. it was a mixed bag especially during WWll when we had kids in P.S.85 from India, Africa and other countries who's parents were able to escape the war. Not near the park though, usually on 31st St. in a soda shop named Ringen's or the bowling alley or pool hall.

Chunky Hill is a new one to me. The hill was featured in a couple of films one being Goodfellas where it served as the neighborhood Henry Hill grew up in and met all his gangster buddies. Plenty of Astoria shots in that one. Another is an independent film that was written by a former local kid titled A Guide to Recognizing Your Saints. It never made it big but has Chaz Palminteir and Robert Downey in it and is an interesting story of growing up in Astoria, great shots of Astoria Pool at night and again Chinky Hill serving as home base for the principles.

All my old pictures are of family with many taken at 23-20 32nd St. some back when it was called Park Place or 3rd Ave. under one of Astoria's various old street naming schemes. 23rd Road had the exotic sounding name Pleasure Place, 23rd Ave. was Potter Ave. and 31st. Street was called DeBevoise St. or 2nd Ave. depending on the time frame. My favorite site for old Astoria pictures is http://astoriahistory.smugmug.com/  You can spend hours looking at old pics and seeing how empty parts of Astoria were with active farms even in the 1930's and early 40's.
