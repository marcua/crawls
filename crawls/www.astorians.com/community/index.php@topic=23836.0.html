I recommend postponing jury duty as well.

I'm surprised your call-in date is on a Friday.  Usually, it's a Monday or Tuesday.  You keep calling in until your number has come up for you to come in person.  Once you report at the courthouse, you have to be "on call" for up to 5 days.  If you're not put on a trial by then, you're excused for 4 years; if you are put on a trial, all bets are off.  I had jury duty last summer and the trial I could have gotten put on ended up going for 3 months.

I second what StephaniL says although I had to start calling in on a Friday this last time around.  If you just show up and then when you are called to the Jury Room and they ask if there is any reason why you can't perform you show them your travel arrangements that will get you off the hook.
Also wanted to add that if you are excused from a trial before Jury Selection begins you are off the hook for six years.  That happened to me the first time I had to serve here.  Of course, the second time I got blessed with three days of Jury Selections, and two days of trial before it settled - all in the beautiful hamlet of Jamaica, Queens!
