A POSSUM(!!!) in Dutch Kills!
« on: June 17, 2008, 12:28:22 AM »
Because of my weird working hours, I’m often in a position where I find myself having to do various mundane tasks at hours when most people are either asleep or watching their favorite TV shows, and about to get ready for bed. Today, I decided it would be most efficient and convenient for me to do a little grocerey shopping soon after getting off of work.
So I’m walking along the north side of Northern Blvd. towards Pathmark when I pass the Suzuki dealer parking lot near the corner of 37th Street. As usual, I was looking into the fenced lot at the various cars they have there, casually thinking which ones would be worth getting if I should ever decide to get a new or semi-new car in the very near future.
Well, what did I see but a freakin’ POSSUM right there in the car lot looking as if he were contemplating kicking the tires and making an offer himself!
OK, before we go any further, I shall add that I have partaken of absolutely no manner of mind altering substances whatsoever beforehand. But there he was - not a big specimen (about the size of a young cat, except very pale gray - practically white - and with the characteristic conical face, and having the long bald tail typical of possums.) And yes - I am absolutely sure it was NOT an extremely large rat. Believe me, I’ve seen enough of those around here to know the difference between one of those and something else. This was DEFINITELY a possum! He was only about two feet from me on the other side of the fence, so I got a real good look at him. No mistaken identity here.
After I recovered from my initial shock at seeing such a creature around these parts, I stopped in my tracks and said to him, “Hey, you possum! What the hell are you doing HERE?” His reaction was to simply saunter casually back under one of the cars in the lot as if he didn’t care to have any type of exchange - verbal or otherwise - with me, and who the hell was *I* to question *him* anyway?

So is this Animal Gentrification or something? Anyway, this is the first time in my nearly fifteen years of living in this particular area of Dutch Kills that I’ve seen a possum.
I feel like Aunt Bea on Mayberry or something now. What's next? Polecats?
