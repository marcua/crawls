Post by: Harlan on September 19, 2007, 10:44:01 AM
You can always blanch and freeze them, and delay your quandry until January...!

Yes, the crop has been ridiculously productive this year! Here's a really tasty dish I made recently:

Code: [Select]
Roasted Green Beans with Cherry Tomatoes

From Fineliving.com.

    * 1 lb. green beans, tipped and tailed
    * 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
    * 1/4 c pumpkin seeds
    * 1/2 c basil leaves, cut into thin strips
    * 1/4 c XV olive oil
    * 1 T sherry wine vinegar
    * salt and pepper

   1. Preheat oven (or for a half recipe, a toaster oven) to 375 degrees. In a dry skillet on low, toast pumpkin seeds until puffing up and browning, then remove from heat.
   2. Toss green beans in olive oil and some salt and pepper and spread them out on a baking sheet. Roast in oven for 30-35 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes.
   3. Transfer to a large mixing bowl and toss with cherry tomatoes, basil and pumpkin seeds.
   4. Season to taste with vinegar, salt and pepper. Serve warm or room temperature.

Serves 6.

Post by: lanseaux on September 19, 2007, 10:55:09 AM
Beans with pesto!  With last week's batch, I cooked them with scallions, minced poblano (both from the share), and pesto made from the basil share.  I sauteed the sliced scallions and poblano first in a little olive oil, then added the beans to heat them through (I boil them for 7 minutes and then shock them - they are cooked through but still a little al dente at that point, which works nicely for quick sauteeing).  I added enough pesto to coat a minute or so before the end.

1 cup plain yogurt
juice of 1/2 a lemon
1 clove garlic
5-6 anchovy fillet
pepper to taste

Blanch green beans for 30 seconds in boiling water (or even eat them raw theyâ€™re so tender!). In a small skillet on medium heat warm anchovy with garlic. Sautee and break down anchovy fillet for about 1 minute, until garlic begins to brown and a paste forms. While anchovy-garlic is warming put yogurt and lemon juice in a bowl, mix. Add anchovy-garlic and pepper, mix to incorporate.
