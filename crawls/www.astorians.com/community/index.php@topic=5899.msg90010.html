I agree with TAG.  I was just in Williamsburg for class tonight and the thing that always gets me is how everyone kind of looks the same (gelled hair, raggedy clothes that probably cost more than most of our salaries, etc.)

I'm sure that there are diverse areas of Williamsburg but if you compare Bedford Avenue to a Ditmars/30th/Broadway area you can see the clear differences.

I agree that Williamsburg and Astoria are extremely different.  But there seems to be an assumption that young people who enjoy fun and have money would only enjoy living in Williamsburg.  I just don't think that's true, and it's assuming that all young people are interested in the same things.  I think there are plenty of young people and fun to be had here.  Meg's right, people have different ideas of what fun is.  I remember clearly those days when I was 21, fresh out of college, in my first apartment here, and going out to cafes, bars, and clubs all the time.  In Astoria.  I loved going out and being able to get drunk locally, stay out late, and stumble on home.  I still go out, only now there are even more places to go.  I think Astoria can be anything you want it to be.  I realize it's not Williamsburg, but it doesn't have to be for young people to enjoy it.  It just depends on their taste.  That's a question only they will be able to answer.

Obviously you guys are in a great rush to find a place considering that it's been only three days since your original post. Being impatient isn't giving a great impression either, since there are over 2000 members on this site. First of all, look on craigslist http://newyork.craigslist.org/aap/ . Some say it isn't the best place to look, but it is a great resource. Secondly, I think you aim too high. True Astoria is an up and coming neighborhood, but I wouldn't call it "fun" in the just-out-of-college sense of the word. Yes it is affordable. Yes it can be inexpensive. Yes there a number of young people here. But fun? That is subjective.
You need to give yourselves more time cause you aren't going to find a place in a weeks time, especially in August. WALK AROUND, A LOT during the day and at night. When I say night I mean after 9 pm, usually that gives you a great idea of a neighborhood. Lastly, good luck!!

edit: Take a gander at this: http://newyork.craigslist.org/que/fee/408006803.html
