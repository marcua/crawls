I'd just be guessing, but I think it might change people's behavior.  Even those who are inclined to litter are less likely to do it when they are presented with a clean area and where people make an effort to clean.

When Robert Moses opened up Jones Beach, he intentionally forbade the beach cleaners to use the pickers.  He wanted to public to see them bending over to pick up trash, to see that a visible effort was being made.  Even his detractors remarked how it was effective in preventing littering.

Sometimes my schedule forces me to put my garbage out in the morning rather then the evening.  In those few hours, people see the garbage bags and make a mess out of the area.  They see garbage and add to it. 

Sort of like a breaking the seal type of thing perhaps.
