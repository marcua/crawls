I sense that we're talking about singles who are recent arrivals rather than Astoria natives. Here's a somewhat simple explanation drawn from my own circle of aquaintance: Astoria strongly appeals to young single females who, like families, are very often looking for affordable apartments in a safe neighborhood with lots of amentities like grocery shops, laundrettes, etc. Young single guys sometimes go for these things too, but they are also often willing to live in rougher places without all these things in exchange for cheaper rent - think Bushwick, Bed-Stuy, East Williamsburg, the South Bronx, etc. I know only two single women who live in those neighborhoods, but I know many in Astoria. I don't know any single men who live in Astoria, but I know many in those neighborhoods. Not a scientific sample, but maybe it's a start.

That said, there seem to be no shortage of single men in LIC, so maybe hit a WarmUp at PS1 before the summer ends!
