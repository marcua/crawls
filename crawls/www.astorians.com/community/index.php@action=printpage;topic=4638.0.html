Title: Re: Cable vs. Satellite
Post by: Snoodette on January 07, 2007, 09:55:44 PM
We got DirectTV back in 2001, and the Tivo box about 2 years later. We absolutely love it and I seriously don't know how we'd live without it! The channel selection is wonderful and I won't even get into how much a DVR changes your life.   :lol:

The only thing I would warn you about is the actual hookup itself. If no one else in your building has DirectTV, make sure you clear it with your landlord or coop board first. The techs that came out to install it back in 2001 drilled a hole in the street facing wall, with a thick white wire that goes from the roof to my apartment. When we added Tivo a couple years later, they had to drill yet another hole with a wire in the same spot. (I also have heard that if you plan on getting DirectTV in HD that will add yet another hole/wire...) The sound and vibration of the drill cutting through brick was enough to set off a car alarm right outside and caused the neighborhood dogs to freak out.  :|  Thank god I have an understanding landlord! I called them up about a year ago and asked if there was maybe another way to install it so that the cables didn't have to run down the front of the building, but they didn't seem to think there were any other options...maybe I just had a bad customer service rep that day?

Also, a couple years ago we intended to move, so in my calling around to various real estate agents, I asked if getting a DirectTV hookup would be a problem in most buildings. All of the agents said satellite hookups are pretty tough to get a landlord to permit. Maybe things have changed in the past couple years, though.

All that being said, if you can get DirectTV, I highly recommend it.
Title: Re: Cable vs. Satellite
Post by: aquaecny on January 07, 2007, 11:17:44 PM
I grew up with Direct TV at home and was always getting frustrated because it would go out often either due to weather, clouds, what have you. Since I moved out of my parents, I have always had Digital Cable (with HBO & DVR), and have been very pleased. I rarely ever have problems, definitely not as frustrated as I was with Direct TV.

A plus to Cable, you get your local channels at no extra cost, unlike Direct TV.
Besides, not so many holes either!  The cable can go through any spot, rather than a specific corner of the building for the dish.

As far as Tivo and DVR is concerned, they pretty much work the same for both platforms. It is a definitely a "requirement" nowadays. Besides, I think there is no sense in paying for the channel selection without having some sort of recorder.

Prices include estimated tax & fees

TIME WARNER
$125/month for Internet, Digital Cable with DVR and HBO
Pro: Convenient as I'm already an internet customer

DIRECT TV
$70/month for Digital Satellite TV with DVR and Showtime (still would have to pay $42/month for TWC internet)

Of course, I could be proven wrong and RCN could suck, but I'm willing to try it for virtually no risk.  At the moment, it seems clear that RCN is the way to go.  Again, thanks so much for the feedback, and I'll let you know how it goes.
