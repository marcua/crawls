Title: Re: Mother's Helpers: Need Advice
Post by: Bookgirl83 on July 05, 2008, 02:11:17 AM
The summer after my freshman year at college I worked as a mother's helper. The mom was always home with me, she just needed an extra pair of hands with the kids. They had just recently adopted a boy who was 1 years old when I came to work for them, and she had unexpectedly gotten pregnant with twins right after. So she had a 1 year old boy and 2 3-month old boys.

This was in 2002, and they paid me $10 an hour. I worked 2 days a week, from 8am-4pm, and she always provided me with lunch. I basically helped her feed the babies, change diapers, change clothes, make their bottles, play with them and did the babies laundry only (not their housekeeping).

I would say try to get a high school age student for this type of job, and pay at least $12 an hour. My mom found the ad in her church's bulletin actually. Maybe try craigslist, but I'd stick with someone who's in the neighborhood if you can find someone.

Hope this helps.
