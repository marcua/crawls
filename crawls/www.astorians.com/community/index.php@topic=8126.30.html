It was actually more the other way around. What we call "tex-mex" originated in the American southwest, and then spread to the rest of the country (and the world -- there was even a tex-mex restaurant in Budapest where I used to live.)

Food in Mexico is markedly different from tex-mex, and different regionally. For instance in Mexico City, the food was richer, more cheesy and saucy, but not as much spice. It was kinda like comfort food, and definitlely more prevalent in towns outside the city. In Baja, it's a lot less fried, more grilled with an emphasis on seafood. Easier on the sauces, too.
