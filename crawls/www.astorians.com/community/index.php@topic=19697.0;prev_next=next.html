 Ive been reading some posts on this site regarding where in Astoria is a good location to live in.
Commute time, parking, bars, cafes, shopping, seems to be what everyone is asking about.
The main question you should be asking is how safe is the neighborhood and how secure is your building.
Being near the train is great, available parking is outstanding, but these perks are not a way to decide where you should live. For the most part, Astoria is probably one of the safest neighborhoods in queens.What you should be looking at is how secure are your lobby and or front doors,can anyone enter the building? Do you have a pre-war apt door? How secure is the door frame, how accessible are your windows to intruders, is there an intercom system, is the hall well lit at night, and a host of other questions.This i believe is a good way to select a location to live, if you are near all the good stuff, even better, but don't elect to move anywhere unless your safe.

        Regardless of where you go, one of the first things to do is change the locks. Real estate agents, contractors, previous tenants or their friends might have keys which means you have a potential problem. For those of you in newly constructed buildings, secondary deadbolts are vital to get.The information i am giving is from first hand experience. I am a local locksmith in Astoria and i see misfortune daily in our great neighborhood. People are constantly getting broken into and burglarized because they avoid securing their premise properly. Its a minimal expense but the reward is great. Don't get cheap with your locks. Most of you have a pair of shoes that cost more than it would to put a decent lock on your door. Ive seen people set back by the loss of a laptop. All which probably could have been avoided.
