Re: Minimum income requirement for rentals? "Times rule?"
« Reply #1 on: April 22, 2012, 09:19:13 AM »
It's considered a rule because they want tenants who are able to pay the rent. If your income is 40k, then your actual monthly income after taxes is closer to $2,200. An apartment over $1,000 would start to strain your bank account, when you factor in food, entertainment, clothing, travel, and so on.


In Astoria most will request 40x rent, but some will have different requirements, like 33x. Other landlords will allow people with less income if they have a guarantor who makes 40x rent, or if they pay more money upfront.
