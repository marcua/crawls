WORST food experience in Astoria EVER - maybe the world
« on: February 20, 2010, 08:24:13 PM »
We went to Las Margaritas (38th & Broadway) last night for dinner. BIG MISTAKE.  We had been there once before ages ago and couldn't remember why we didn't go back.  I thought, "maybe they have a new chef - it's always so busy".

It was full of drunk people who were all celebrating their birthday - and they play a very loud mariachi rendition of "happy birthday" and bring out a piece of cake and make a big deal.  I guess it's a tribute to chain restaurants.  Plus,there only play like 4 or 5 songs in their mix - and repeat the Gypsy King's "Bamboleo" frequently.

But I really wanted to write about the food:  VILE.  I ordered a combination platter of a tamal, sope, and enchilada.  She asked cheese, chicken, beef, or mixed.  I chose mixed.  I tasted the tamal first and it was super sweet (?!) and really really really glutinous.  I felt like I had taken a bite of congealed wallpaper paste.  I spit it out into my napkin - I simply couldn't swallow it.  Then I ate the sope - which was ok.  Nothing to write home about but it wasn't nauseating either.  Then I tasted the enchilada which was *allegedly* covered in some sort of mole sauce.  One bite and it brought me back to my childhood when we had a dog that we fed milkbone dog biscuits.  That's EXACTLY what it tasted like - a dog biscuit.  I spit that out into my napkin, too.

The waitress was walking by and asked if everything was alright and I said no, that the food was inedible, that I couldn't eat another thing.  She was very nice, offered the menu to me which i refused (nicely).  She didn't charge us.  I spent the rest of the night with a quivering stomach - whether out of some sort of microbe that I might have ingested at that crap hole or (more likely) pure revulsion, I don't know.

How does this place stay in business?  That was BAR NONE the WORST restaurant in Astoria we have ever been - quite possibly the worst restaurant in the world. 


To be fair the chips and salsa were good and as i  mentioned the waitress was very nice.

The margaritas at Creek/Cave aren't "good" -- but they blow the margaritas at Las Margaritas out of the water. I don't think they even make blended margaritas. I recollect the drinks being rather strong, so you could taste the tequilla instead of being overwhelmed by sour mix. For the money, you get twice as much as you do at Las Margs, and the drinks are better.

Just consider the quality of food at Creek v. Las MArgs, and the drinks are pretty much on par.

For a good margarita (not in astoria) Canyon Road on the UES (77th and 1st, I think) uses fresh sour. Mojave's have been hit and miss depending on the bartender. For the record -- I prefer a stripped down margarita: 2 oz tequilla, 1 oz fresh lime, 1/2 oz agave, shaken and strained into a martini glass. The shaking waters it down just enough.
