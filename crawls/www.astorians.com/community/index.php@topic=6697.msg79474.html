God, Steinway has changed so much!   There used to be several movie theatres on Steinway.   That was long before my day.

I do remember that where the Starbucks is on the west side of Steinway between Broadway & 31st was either a Burger King or a bank.   I know there was a Burger King there back in the late 1980s and if it wasn't in the space where Starbucks is then it was right nearby.   There might have been a Blimpie sub shop over there as well, but I could be imagining that.

On the northwest corner of 31st & Steinway was a discount store much like the aforementioned Gem.   It is a bank now.

And the Sprint phone store on the west side of Steinway between 31st & 30th was a bank, Chase I think.   I think it closed when Chemical/Manufacturers Hanover bought Chase.  The building is a bit ornate for a cell phone store.

There used to be a small video store on the east side of Steinway between Broadway & 31st, real close to Broadway.   I think it closed shortly after the Blockbuster opened on Steinway @ 34th Street, which in turn has closed.

Quote

Burger King was on the other side of the parking lot, where the Vitamin Shoppe is now.  Before the Vitamin Shoppe, it was Nine West Shoes for a few years, which I think is what replaced Burger King. 

Burger King seems to have had bad luck in Astoria.  If I'm not mistaken, the original Steinway location was closed by the health department.  In the late 90's or around 2000 or so, a Burger King opened across the street but closed a few years later. 

And, of course, the one on 31st near Ditmars also went away after a few years.

Quote
On the northwest corner of 31st & Steinway was a discount store much like the aforementioned Gem.   It is a bank now.

Ah, yes, the S&A store.  That was there for as long as I could remember, but at some point moved a few doors down on Steinway to a much smaller location.  S&A just didn't look right over there.  I guess many customers agreed because it finally went out of business a couple of years ago.

Quote
And the Sprint phone store on the west side of Steinway between 31st & 30th was a bank, Chase I think.   I think it closed when Chemical/Manufacturers Hanover bought Chase.  The building is a bit ornate for a cell phone store.

I also seem to recall it lasting longer than that.  I remember going to the Blockbuster down on Steinway and 34th in the early 90s as a younger kid, and I'm pretty sure the small video store on Steinway near McDonalds was still there.  I think it lasted into the mid-late 90s. 

Anyone remember Stride Rite shoes?  Or the little Italian children's shoe store on Steinway near 31st?  I think it was where Benetton currently is or somewhere right near there.  How about Eisenberg's gift shop?  Or the neon-lit Top Ten sign?

Does anyone remember what was at the current location of the Gap, before it opened?  The pet shop next to it seems to have been around for quite a while in one form or another.
