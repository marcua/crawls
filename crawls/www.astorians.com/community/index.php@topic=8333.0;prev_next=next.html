Landlord's level of responsibility for fridge
« on: November 26, 2007, 08:17:04 PM »
I'm not sure if I'm posting in the right category, but this seemed closest.

I've been having some trouble with my fridge (it leaks water all over the floor, and has trouble maintaining an even temperature in the summer).  I mentioned this to my landlady today (who lives just downstairs, and with whom I've had a pretty decent relationship for the most part), and she said that they're not responsible for repairs or replacement since the fridge was left by a previous tenant.

Now who in their right mind rents an apartment that doesn't come with a fridge?  If I'd known that it was going to be my responsibility, I would have either looked elsewhere or negotiated for lower rent.  It was never mentioned to me when I took the place 4 years ago. 

Another wrinkle is, I don't believe a formal lease was ever drawn up.  I don't remember signing anything.  I don't know how that may affect me.

But it seems to me that this apartment was represented to me as coming with a fridge, and that it shouldn't matter how the fridge got here; it belongs to the landlady, and should therefor be her responsibility.

Thoughts?  Suggestions?  I'm hoping to get this resolved without alienating anyone.  It's not an emergency that the fridge be replaced right away; I just wanted to give her a heads-up that it was more than likely on its way out.

I would call the Attorney General's office or check out their website or Tenant.net--unfortunately these private houses situations don't always fall under those guidelines but it would be a good place to start.

You might point out to the landlady that at no point did she mention that the fridge was purchased by a former tenant, and you were under the impression that it was included with the apartment, pointing out that you wouldn't have taken the apartment if you'd known that. If she truly isn't responsible in the eyes of the law for replacing this, you have a couple options. You can threaten to move, hoping she'll see the sense in keeping a long-term paying tenant happy (since she'd likely have to replace the fridge before new tenants move in anyway, since most people wouldn't take an apartment that didn't come with a fridge). You can ask her to split the cost with you, since you won't be taking it with you when you leave, I presume.

I'd start by finding out what your rights are as a tenant in a private house, and the best way to do that is the AG office. And you signed NOTHING when you moved in? Not even a rental agreement? How did you find the apartment? My broker has a copy of our rental agreement, which is not the same thing as a lease, but at least it spells out some right and responsibilities for both my landlord and myself.

Hate to bring in a devious element to this, but....if she's saying that the previous tenant paid for it (with no way to prove it), then it's possible that she's simply not willing to pay for a new fridge. Is it likely that's the line she gave the tenant before you, too? If you leave and you haven't replaced the fridge on your own or with her help, I hope you get to warn the next person that the fridge is broken and you didn't buy it....

I think in general, it's pretty reasonable to expect the landlord to pay for the repairs/replacements a tenant makes even in your house situation since that is something they will be taking advantage of in their rent increase once someone else moves in. It's hard to believe that there will be a specific law stating "apartment must come with fridge" in terms of tenant's rights, but there must be something to the effect of it being habitable...implying fridge, toilet, etc...I'm sure your lawyer friend would know more. Good luck!

I contend that as the premises being rented came with a fridge that feature or service is to continue, and  if said fridge needs servicing or replacing it is the responsiblity of the landlord. It is an implied warrentee of habatability of the premises.

I would dispute this if I were you though. However be prepared to have say a 10 buck a month or so rent increase if they prevail.
