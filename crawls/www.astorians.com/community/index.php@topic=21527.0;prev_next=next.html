Gluten is in bread and wheat based products (pastas, breads, beer, and I think a few other grains like barley). If there wasn't the garlic caveat I would say that Indian food might be a good choice, but it seems that would be out. My fiance has been on a similar diet lately, and we've found that sushi (as mentioned) has been good, you can do mexican as long as you ask to omit dairy stick to corn tortillas and not flour (we did well at MexiQ - depending on how you feel about that place).
In the few weeks since my better half has been on her diet, we've managed to both cater to her diet and my not caring whatsoever. We've avoided bread, and stuck to rice and corn based dishes, to avoid the gluten, and she's had to avoid dairy in dishes. As for the garlic, that, I feel is going to be the tough part, since most things delicious have garlic in them.

BBQ might be a good idea, as meat and some veg sides might fit the bill. The garlic part is the real kicker, or a greek seafood restaurant would also be a good idea. Gluten-free is the newest incarnation of the low-carb trend, so more places are catering to it. I know that some pizza places are even offering gluten free crusts now, though I can't imagine they're much good ( and not an option for you since you can't escape dairy or garlic there!)
