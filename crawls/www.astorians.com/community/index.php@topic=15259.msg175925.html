As a native Astorian I have to weigh in on the boundaries thing... My western boundary has always been Crescent Street.  Between Crescent and 21st Street can be pretty dicey most of the time, although it does vary depending on what avenue it is, but in general the farther you are from 21st Street the better, especially at night.  I agree with what others said about walking the blocks and seeing what you feel comfortable with.

For an out-of-towner new to the city I'd try to get as close to a subway stop as possible, there's just more activity and it's safer when you get home late at night.  I would say try to keep it between 28th and 34th Avenues, and Crescent and Steinway streets, to be safest.

Hi there!
When I first moved to NYC as a 21 year old actress with no money, I decided to get a room at the Webster Apartments in Manhattan on 34th and 10th. This establishment was started years ago by Mr. Webster who wanted to create safe and affordable housing for women pursuing careers in NYC. They charge you on a sliding scale based on income, and it is quite low. At the time I was there, six years ago, it was close to the price your daughter wants. And included in the price is breakfast and a hot dinner. It is kind of dorm style. It's women only and this is very strict. They have a gorgeous roof top garden and backyard garden where the ladies like to eat their meals. They have rehearsal studios, laundry facilities (that you pay for...quarters...), a t.v. room and sitting rooms. I made great friends there and it was a lot of fun. Her room will be a shoe box, but will include a bed, a desk, a dresser, a sink and closet. The bed will have bedding and she will be provided with towels. A maid service will come in daily (unless she requests otherwise) to change her linens and vaccum the floor. For toilets and showers, this is dorm style with several on each floor to be used by all the girls on the floor.

It's very bosom buddies   :lol:


It was such a great place for me to live as a young women venturing into NYC. It was a great launching pad for me to explore other living options while having a safe place. Plus I walked to all my auditions and never needed a metro card due to the location.

I hope this helps! I just registered this morning for an account on here when I saw your question, because I got really excited "oh Webster is perfect!!!"

She needs to apply online and the sooner the better:
http://www.websterapartments.org/


There are other similar facilities in NYC too.
http://www.thebrandon.org/

I remember these being available but having trouble finding websites. Here's the info I found. Hopefully they aren't closed or something.
1. Gramercy Women's Residence : 17 Gramercy Park South : New York, NY 10003
Gramercy Women's Residence is located on the south side of beautiful and historic Gramercy Park just a few blocks from SVA's buildings on East 23rd and 21st streets. Gramercy provides female residents with a safe, comfortable living environment. Each apartment has a full kitchen and bathroom. Gramercy maintains a very stringent guest policy.


2. Evangeline Parkside. I think it's actually part of the Salvation Army  :mrgreen:
http://mynakedcity.com/entityprofile.php?gvEnt=31744

3. Jeanne D'Arc Home‎ - more info »
http://ephemeralnewyork.wordpress.com/2008/08/04/the-home-for-friendless-french-girls/
253 W 24th St, New York, NY‎ - (212) 989-5952‎
This is run by nuns/catholic charity, but you do not have to be Catholic to live there.


Anyway I can't recommend taking advantage of these housing facilities highly enough. It was empowering to be right in manhattan always knowing I could afford my rent and that I'd always have food to eat. And it put me in a healthy state of mind to search for where I wanted to live more permanently and to work on my career.
