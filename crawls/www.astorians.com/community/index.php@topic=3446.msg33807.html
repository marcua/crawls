Trattoria L'Incontro!  My favourite dish at Trattoria L'Incontro is their ostrich "tenderloin" -- struzzo in Italian.  Cooked to order (rare or medium rare is best because it is so low in fat), it is tender and flavourful, served sliced over a bed of lightly dressed mixed greens, topped with shavings of parmesan.  Very simple, and delicious.

It's been on and off the menu almost since they opened, mostly on, but when it's off, it's often a special.

I always bring my lunch and a few healthy snacks to munch on throughout the day. Almonds are a great snack, but the serving size is only about 17 almonds. I also bring baby carrots and hummus to snack on mid-afternoon. Popcorn is great too and they now make 100 calorie bags of just about everything. I also buy frozen bags of edamame, cook it the night before, and bring it with me to munch on. They taste yummy cold with a little salt.

I drink a ton of water throughout the day and always have flavored seltzer at lunch. I buy the seltzer in 12 packs and just pop one in my bag every day. Cutting out soda at lunch is a great way to cut down on unnecessary sugars. If you do need the caffeine fix, stick to tea.

I know making your lunch the night before or the morning of work can be a pain, but I eat much healthier and I am saving about $50 a week. (I work in midtown where there aren't many cheap choices for lunch). Same goes for snacks. I will buy a big bag of SoyCrisps and ration them out for the week. It is much better than buying a $2.50 bag at Duane Reade and then eat them all in one sitting.

Funny this thread came up because I've been trying to eat a lot better over the past few weeks (something in my head finally snapped when i saw what i weigh) and it's made a difference (7-10 pounds!) also, it's wicked expensive to eat out, even just salad bar, in the flatiron area.
this works if your office has a big fridge.
last week i brought some homemade tuna salad and healthy choice turkey breast along with whole grain english muffins(smaller serving and they were on sale, price leads me to most decisions)lettuce and tomato and just alternated the sammys everyday.
this week i have lettuce left over so i picked up a pint of grape tomatoes, a cucumber, a huge bag full of baby carrots-which i put into a small travel friendly baggie(i can also snack on these), 2 celery stalks chopped up, 1 green pepper chopped up, and some shredded cheese. i forgot to bring in the chicken i made yesterday. the only thing i don't prechop is the cucumber b/c i find it goes off faster that way. i get everything prepped sunday night while i am making dinner-it's just easier for me that way. put everything into individual bags that i throw into a drawer in the fridge at work. come lunch time i just pull out handfuls and prep the lettuce and cucumber. i've got a nice healthy lunch made in only a few minutes. if i eat it fast enough i can run out and actually enjoy a lunch break which i don't often get to do. i did the caloric math, and if i take it easy on the cheese and chicken and make the veggies my main focus, the salad is about 100 calories (a salad doesn't need a full serving of each veg or topping so i divided into the amounts)oh, and i keep a light italian dressing on hand since dressing makes it easier for your body to absorb nutrients, not so with fat free or naked salads. this usually lasts me all week. sometimes into the next if i get to order on the account, or if i wind up working merchandising and then i usually miss lunch.
we have waaaaaaaaay too many snacks here, on each floor(lays, choco chip cookies, pretzels, baked sour cream and onion chips and baked bbq chips) i've totally stopped having any. ok maybe one cookie if there's an open bag, or one pretzel. i've brought in a baggie of 4 medium apples and some cinammon to sprinkle on a sliced apple, then i microwave it and it's almost like a baked apple-so yum and smells so good. and cinammon naturally curbs your sugar cravings. and grapes big ole bag of grapes, this replaces the feeling of eating potato chips.
i have trail mix in my drawer that i eat sparingly.
so, that's what i do. cheap and healthy!

One of my fav lunches is something I used to get at a deli back home - if you have leftover grilled chicken and a microwave in the office, this is perfect.

Put all of these in a whole wheat wrap:
Hot grilled chicken, shredded/torn, salsa, jack cheese. Roll up and enjoy. The hot chicken will melt the cheese, and you can make it as spicy or mils as you like depending on the salsa. I know that wraps aren't as healthy as we'd like them to be, and cheese is cheese, but this is all "real" food, and its cheap and tasty :)
