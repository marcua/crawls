I have been reading the messages on Astorians for a while now, and I feel this is my time to participate.

I have been a long-time community activist; two years ago, I founded a group called NoiseOFF - The Citizens Coalition Against Noise Pollution, the website is http://www.noiseoff.org

I have attended the 114th Precinct Council meetings for the past three years and I would like to relate my experiences with them:

-- Ann Bruno, the president of the council has served for over 17 years and has cultivated an environment where dissenting or critical questions to the NYPD will get you booed and shouted down.  Concerned residents who have had issues with NYPD enforcement (or lack thereof) on specific issues have been made to feel their issues are invalid.

-- Ann Bruno has warned people against people from taking photographs or recordings of meetings, she will have police officers in attendance to confiscate any equipment she finds in use.

-- When I sought to deliver a short presentation on the issues of noise pollution in the community (NoiseOFF members have successfully done this all over the country), she fought against it and it wasn't until she was pressured after several months that she allowed me three minutes to make my presentation on the issue.

-- She unsuccessfully tried to bar the display of a NoiseOFF banner at the last Night Out Against Crime event held in Astoria Park.

-- In one meeting, she had two community affairs officers manhandle a woman for passing copies of a newspaper article to others about gang activity in Astoria.

-- At the November meetings, the meetings serve as a fundraiser for Ann Bruno's Christmas Party.  At the last meeting, there were no questions asked of the police on community issues, just politicos making donations one after the other to great fanfare.

-- The racial and age demographics of the precinct members do not even come close to matching the demographics of the greater Astoria community.  By contrast, the PSA #9 in Ravenswood and the 115th Precinct Council meetings in Jackson Heights (I have attended these meetings just to see the difference) largely reflect the community at large.  These meetings are well attended and there is a much better dialog with the public and the police.

-- Neither the 114th Precinct Council nor the 114th will make available copies of their by-laws.  These by-laws specify the rules and regulations that govern how the meetings are supposed to be conducted, including how the members can be nominated and win office at the council.  At the last election, no one even ran against Bruno and the nominating committee was comprised of Bruno's close friends.  Thanks to the work of a fellow activist, you can get the by-laws and the NYPD memo that amended the term limits at the NoiseOFF site (click 'Why Is Astoria, Queens So Noisy?') on the main page for your own copy.

I actually live next to the 114th Precinct and for years I have been dealing with the issue of illegal parking permit abuse.  If you walk by the area, you will see the officers civilian cars parked on the sidewalk, vehicles parked into a lane of traffic on Astoria Boulevard South and the sounds of car alarms going off at all hours.  The problem is worsened by the fact that Astoria Boulevard South is a busy truck route and the backed up traffic (caused by the police parking taking up a lane) has added noise and pollution that blighted the area.  The sidewalk and streets around the precinct is filthy because there they never clean up the property.  Did I mention the officers park in front of fire hydrants and crosswalks?  They have displayed an arrogance that cannot be believed; when I have complained, they blatantly tell me "well, we don't ticket our own."

At the precinct council meetings, I have been labeled as being "anti-police" because of my efforts to raise awareness of the issue.  To be clear, there is no parking permit in the world that allows this and this problem has been widely reported on by the media (not just in Chinatown).

Regarding Long Island City Alliance, I was a contributing member of this group and later quit when Bob and Debbie (they run the group as well as Greater Astoria Historical Society) threw this issue off the table because they were afraid it might offend the NYPD.  I have since spoken to former members who were disappointed with the group and never came back.  They also distinguish its members by whether they are new or long time residents.

There are a few of us activists in Astoria who have stood firm on the issues that affect us and I hope to meet more of you in the future.
