from the article:
"I think that it's unfortunate that kids' lives are micromanaged and there are social skills they'll never develop on their own," said Debbie Laferriere, who has two children at Willett, about 40 miles south of Boston. "Playing tag is just part of being a kid."

What she said. I'm only 8 years or so older than my peers at school (I'm almost 28, they're 20-21-ish), and even I see a huge discrepancy between me and them when it comes to certain things that could be derived from overprotecting kids and treating them like precious, breakable eggshells. Like, when I was a kid, we had concrete and steel playgrounds...they had plastic and foam. They're so used to being protected, and when something bad happens to them, they're always looking for someone else to blame. No one takes responsibility for themselves. I actually heard someone say in the B& darkroom once, "ugh...when I slip in that water," [that SHE spilled!], "I'm gonna sue school for not cleaning it up." God forbid they should take responsibility. I ended up getting paper towels and handing them to her. She looked at me like I was nuts and I said, "Oh, I grabbed them for you so you could clean up that water you spilled."

Of course I know that not everyone is like this, and that girl I mentioned is an extreme case...but if you don't allow kids to hurt themselves or make mistakes, how are they ever going to survive on their own? I have a big dent on my chin from kickball when I was 8 (my shoes were untied and I tripped on a pretty big crack in the pavement, landed straight on my chin...I'm lucky I didn't break my jaw when I tripped)...my mom probably could have gotten away with suing, but instead she said, "see? That's what happens when you run with untied shoes."
