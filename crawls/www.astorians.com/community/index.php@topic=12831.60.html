Red snapper in chorizo & anchovy puttanesca sauce.

Fine chop good dry chorizo & 1 medium onion, saute in 1/4 (or more) cup olive oil till onion wilts and oil is reddish.
Remove chorizo and onion to a plate.
Dredge red snapper fillets in flour and fry in the oil on both sides till just done.
Remove from pan and onto paper towel.
Chorizo and onions back into oil with sliced garlic, sliced good black olives and red pepper flakes.
Saute till fragrant and splash in 1/2 cup dry white wine. Scrape pan and reduce.
Add 28oz can diced tomato or crushed.  Bring to boil and then simmer to reduce slightly.
Check for salt and heat.

Serve over bed of sauteed green and yellow squash slices or pasta but I'm being low carb right now.

Astorians,
  Another quick and simple recombanista recipe for you all:

Thai Red Curry Vegetables with Spiced Thick Dried Tofu

Steam or Stir fry (to taste)
Snow peas
Carrots--sliced into thin strips with a Japanese mandolin 
Onions, cut coarsely into strips
An Indian/Asian red pepper (available at U Mart in Woodside) diced into small pieces
Fresh Baby Corn (Available at Pacific Market in Jackson Heights)

Chop Spiced Thick Dry Tofu (Available at U Mart in Woodside) into tiny cubes. Add to mix.

Quick and simple Thai red curry sauce:
One part of water
Three parts of Aroy-D's Red Curry paste (Available at U Mart in Woodside)
Stir and add to veggies.

Combine the ingredients above.
What a great meal in so little time!

You made my mouth water for Czech/Hungarian/Polish food. So I ransacked the kitchen and I came up with the following:

A Quick CHP Pseudo Goulash/Stew

Ingredients:
1+ lb slab bacon cut into bite sized cubes
1+ lb kielbasa cut into bite sized cubes
1+ lb fresh drained sauerkraut (rinse only if you prefer)
2 tps ground caraway seeds
1/2 large white onion roughly diced
1 green apple finely diced
1 large can diced tomatoes
6–8 small red potatoes

Directions:
In a 4 quart (or larger) pot, fry all the bacon until nice and crisp. Remove from the pot. Drain most of the bacon grease—which you might consider saving to make some eggs later in the week—and brown up the kielbasa. Remove the kielbasa from the pot to drain. Remove all the grease from the pot. Add the onion and apple; toss until just translucent. Add the whole can of diced tomatoes, scrape up the goodies from the bottom of the pot. Add the bacon, kielbasa, sauerkraut and ground caraway seeds. Bring to a simmer for 30 minutes.

Serve with the boiled small red potatoes and some nice Jewish rye bread with a mustard dipping sauce.
