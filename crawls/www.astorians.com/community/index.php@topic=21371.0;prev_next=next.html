Hey, Andrew--if you're a student, I recommend starting with no-fee websites first, rather than starting with brokers, as it can save you a serious pile of cash.

http://www.nybits.com (Probably the most robust search engine and the greatest range)
http://www.urbanedgeny.com
ETA: http://nymag.streeteasy.com/nyc/rentals/nyc/ (Make sure to check the "no fee" box)
Sure, Craiglist has listings, too, but (as someone who's done the same search from abroad and then had to sift through listings once I arrived here) the majority of them proved to be a waste of time.
Also helpful: http://newyork.timeout.com/shopping-style/apartments-home-design/51626/apartment-search-101

Also, if you'll take some unsolicited advice, it's best *not* to sign anything or send/wire any money without having seen a place first! See if you can CouchSurf or crash with a friend for a week or so while you hit the pavement. One of the best pieces of advice is to take a day or two to familiarize yourself with Astoria and with where you want to be--just walk around and look for management signs on buildings. Talk to supers and find out if someone's renting their place. See if you can leave your number if something becomes available. Call management companies. Etc.

There used to be an Astoria roommate-matching service, too, which I'm sure you can find if you Google it a bit. Sorry if this falls wide of the advice you were looking for.
