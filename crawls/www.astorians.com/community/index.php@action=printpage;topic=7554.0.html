http://www.wellquestonline.com/

Click on the drop down menu on the front page under sponsoring organizations.  All the health insurance companies and work companies that participate in the program are listed.  Oxford is one of them.  The application process is short and sweet.  And once you set it up, even if your health insurance changes, you can stay in the discount program.  It's a great deal.  The only two in Astoria that participate are The Rock and Synergy, I think.  You are allowed to use other gyms in the network as well (which are all over including Bally's) in addition 4 times a month.  But Global Fit is a similar program as WellQuest and worth looking into as well.

If anyone wants a 2 week pass to the Rock, PM me.  I also have 2 certificates for almost no initiation fee.

Post by: madama on January 07, 2008, 05:40:00 PM

As a long-time Rock Member (4+ years), there are plenty of positives and a some negatives associated with being a member there, in my opinion:

On the good side:

* Equipment is well-maintained and when things break, they are repaired quickly
* The gym and locker rooms are typically very clean and well-kept
* Hours can't be beat
* Classes are free, if that's your thing
* Members are generally pleasant, non-psychotic, and free of steriod-induced rages

However,

* It ain't cheap
* It appears the membership has taken a hit since Club Fitness opened on Broadway a few months ago, which might explain why the towels provided to members for working out have shrunk in size and quality
* Music, especially at night, is way too loud.  Staff must think they're managing a disco, not a gym.  Even blasting your iPod can't drown it out completely

Guest passes / visits are easy to arrange, so anyone interested in the gym should stop by and check it out.
