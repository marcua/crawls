Moving out - Security Deposit
« on: May 26, 2007, 08:53:35 PM »
Hey everyone,

I'm moving out of my current apartment (near Astoria Boulevard) in a month and my current roommate is staying behind.  While she is looking for a new roommate, I have spoke to our landlord about me leaving.  We are currently on an unwritten/unspoken month to month agreement, since our lease officially expired last winter.

So here's my question/dilemma.  I don't trust my landlord to give me my security deposit back.  He has a track record of being nice but kinda sketchy.  When we first moved in, we had to have our stove replaced and it took more than a month for him to do that.  Even then, I had to help him install it because he was too cheap to hire someone to do it for him and he couldn't do it on his own.  He also promised us that our apartment would be sprayed for bugs once a month as a precaution and that lasted all of 2 months.  He's also left it up to my roommate and I to take care of maintenance issues on our own, basically ignoring our requests or responding with phone numbers for local fix-it types instead.

I asked him to come inspect the apartment before June 1st so that I could find out his intentions regarding my deposit.  My roommate and I haven't made any damages in the time we've been here and the only thing that really needs to be done is a good repainting (which legally, I believe, is considered normal wear and tear).  Now...here my question --> how should I handle this going forward?  I want to ask him for my security deposit now (before I pay rent) but he has already mentioned that he wants to give it to me right before I move out.  Now, if he says he plans on giving me the deposit back in full but won't give it to me until the end of my occupancy, am I right in suggesting that he keep my deposit and use it toward my June rent?  This ensures we both have the money we are owed and I feel safe in knowing that he did not already spend my security deposit and cannot give it back to me right away.

Frankly, I can't afford to wait for him to give me the security deposit back, since I have to give it to my next landlord.  I don't want him to take me to court for that one month of rent, if that is even possible, and I wouldn't want to endanger a potential rental reference, but I really can't afford to lose this money or risk not getting it back in time.  Any advice on how I should approach this, or on what my legal rights are?  I do realize that I am the one being overly cautious and talking about not giving HIM the money HE is owed, but technically he should have the security money in a separate account and if he has spent it, that's not my problem.  If he sees our apartment and assesses no damage, then it stands to reason that I wouldn't be making any huge holes in the wall or blowing up the (brand new) stove in the next month or so.  Doesn't it make sense for him to just use the money he owes me (security) and put it toward my rent and call it even?

I really need advice.  I know there are probably other people going through this out there too, or who have been through it, so let me know!

Did the original lease that you signed mention your security deposit?  Normally, you are entitled to receive it back, and most landlords do not give it back until you move out.  Some may agree to accept it as your last month's rent, but both parties would have to agree on that.  It can't hurt to ask him to keep the security in leiu of next month's rent  But he doesn't have to, and you can't expect him to.  It's a security deposit, that is in case something happens to the apartment - you don't get that until you move out and they inspect it upon you leaving.  The next person that moves in should have nothing to do with it, you are entitled to receive it when you move out, not when he gets a new tenant.  If he does run off with your money, I assume the original lease would be enough to take him to court with, even if it has expired.  I would check in with these people, they should be able to inform you of your rights.

The security deposit is intended to cover the entire time you are in the apartment, so I can't imagine, legally, any owner would be required to give you the deposit back before you turn over the keys and leave.  Technically, no it is not contingent upon the next security deposit being received, but I imagine there is some reasonable amount of time the owner has to inspect the apartment after you leave, determine damages if any, decide how much to deduct and return the remainder to you.  Since apartments rent so fast here, it seems collecting the next deposit is also what prompts the owner to return the old deposit.  That's been my experience.  

And Daisy has a good idea, asking to use the security as your last months rent. Some will go for that, others won't. You should ask at least...

I think the landlord has 30 days to return the deposit after the apartment is vacated, deducting costs due to damage above and beyond normal wear and tear.

In your case, though, you and your roommate are (presumably) jointly and severally responsible for the lease, and so it's not clear at all to me why the landlord should return the security deposit to you. Instead, your replacement should pay you (instead of the landlord) for your share of the deposit. I don't think the landlord needs to return the deposit to anyone until the apartment is fully vacated.

Incidentally, you should know about the law regarding holding deposits:

Quote
y law, if a tenant resides in a building with six or more units, the security deposit must be placed in a separate interest-bearing account in a New York State bank... If a landlord fails to maintain the security deposit as required by law... the landlord may forfeit the right to hold the security. In such a case... the tenant can demand that the deposit be returned.

If the landlord declines to return it... the tenant can apply the amount on deposit to the next month's rent and use the landlord's failure to comply with the law as a defense in any legal proceeding the landlord may bring.
(source)
