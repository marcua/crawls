Donate Your Children's Books to a Great New Orleans School.
« on: March 24, 2008, 07:58:09 AM »
The Martin Luther King Jr. Charter School in New Orleans' Lower Ninth Ward is being rebuilt after having been completely devastated by Hurricane Katrina. The school is led by teachers and administrators who really care about giving their kids a measure of peace and a sense of hope after all they've been through. As far as the school has come, they still need one thing desperately: children's books. It would mean the world to them to receive 2,000 to 3,000 children's books to complete their school library and (in some cases) to give to the kids themselves, so they can build their own home libraries. They have about 500 so far, and still need more. They're looking for books for kids from Pre-K through 9th grade, running the gamut from the classics to contemporary fiction to all manner of non-fiction (biography, history, science, technology, art) to the Junie B. Jones books and R.L. Stine's Goosebumps series, which tend to fly off the shelves. Books with African-American characters are especially appreciated, and in short supply.

It was a great school in a troubled neighborhood and is now a school that wants to be great again in a devestated neighborhood. I'm not sure if any of the Astorians here can help, and I know there's all kinds of great places here to donate books, but I thought I'd pass this along.

Please send any books to:
Ms. Velva Rabb/Library
Martin Luther King Jr. Charter School
1617 Caffin Avenue
New Orleans, LA, 70117

Remember, you can use media mail to make shipping cheaper. If the cost of mailing is a problem or prohibitive for you, PM me and I can pick up and mail the books for you.
