I've been hearing a lot about burglaries, some robberies, and that rapist running around, and as a native NYer I can tell you it's nothing shocking considering the current economic times.  Conventional wisdom applies: try not to walk in desolate areas by yourself at night (girls AND guys!), get yourself some mace, take a self defense class, take a cab home, or get a big dog if your landlord allows it. Avoid living near "borderline" areas, aka, within spitting distance of the projects...because it doesn't take nearly as much effort to walk over 2 blocks to snatch your purse as it does to try to get a damn job. 

Have your keys in your hand--in fact, palm them and make a fist around them, with a key sticking out  between each finger.  Have your phone handy. Let your friends know when you've arrived home. Let someone know you're COMING home! Don't listen to your ipod when you're walking down the street in the middle of the night.  Travel in packs.  Look behind you.  Know where there is a business that is open late or 24 hrs in the vicinity where you're walking so that you can find someone to help you if you catch some creep following you.

I am a native Manhattanite cheating on the LES with Astoria.  We absolutely love our new home, and plan on raising our kids here.  I'm a bartender/cosmetology student by day and my fiancé is a graphic designer.  One of the best things about Astoria is all the great shops (especially the bakeries, YUM, MARTHA'S!) and the fact that small business can thrive here. There is a lot of stuff to do without having to go trekking into the LES or East Village like all the wannabes, college kids, and all of New Jersey, and it's more affordable and chill.  The charm of the area is that homey sense of old school community, like I remember the NYC of my childhood being before the Starbucks-ification began...just, you know, with fewer crack dealers. A LOT FEWER. 

This neighborhood is pretty tight knit and safe, but just remember that you're still in a large City and the rules of common sense apply.  You've made a good choice moving here!  Enjoy the area, Hope, and welcome!
