No apple picking, indeed ... a disappointment.

As for a quick fun day-trip, I don't know if I'd recommend the Hamptons, it's really best to do that in the summer (though I'd never really recommend going, but that's because it's not my scene at any time).

Greenport is a cute little town, there's not a whole lot to do, but there is a good restaurant and some cute shops, and there are a bunch of wineries along the North Shore that you can stop in on your way there.

Upstate day trips I'd recommend Woodstock, Sugar Loaf, Kingston, and Cold Spring, there are also searches you can do for most of the Hudson Valley Region that will tell you about the towns.

I think lanseaux mentioned Beacon as a nice destination, too. 

Wine tasting along the North Fork of LI, ending in Greenport is nice, too.  I did that around this time of year when I first moved to Stony Brook.  Orient Point, at the end of the North Fork, is cool because at one point, you can see the waves of the Peconic Bay intersect the waves of the Long Island Sound.  I loved seeing that.  As far as the Hamptons go, I don't find them to be such a big deal.  What I have heard, though, is how nice Montauk State Park is at the very end tip of the South Fork.  I've been wanting to get out there for some time now.

Port Jefferson is also a nice little place to check out on your way to the North Fork of LI.  I used to go there all the time, as I lived in the village next to Port Jeff while in Grad school.  It attracts a lot of visitors.  If you decide to visit the Three Village area (Port Jefferson/Stony Brook/Setauket), let me know, as I lived there for 4 years!

Orient Point is wonderful, I so much prefer the North Shore and the LI Sound part of the Island, and speaking of that ... I love Port Jeff!! I've gone there a few times recently, used to spend lots of time there when I was kid when mom and I spent weeks out in Centereach with relatives.

Well, there is, but the company has to want to do it.  The IRS allows employees to deduct a certain amount of pre-tax earnings towards a monthly commute cost -- $105/month, but it has to be offered through the employer.  There are several companies set up to do this - Transitchek is a common one in the NYC area, but there are others as well.  I've been enrolled in two of the others (currently through ADP, and I can't remember the other one) through employers.
