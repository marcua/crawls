Title: Dell Financial Services --- BOO!! HISSS!!!!
Post by: Ditmartian on July 25, 2007, 05:38:56 PM
I bought a new laptop 2 months ago online from Dell. Iv'e gotten stuff from them before, no prob. While I'm checking out, I get hit with this "No Interest for 3 Month" promo if I get a Dell Preferred Whatever account. I was planning on paying for the computer in full, but thought, hey I can budget it out over three months and not have to pay interest. What's not to like, right?

I make my purchase, get 'instant approval' and all that. Then my DFS card shows up in the mail with all the Terms of Agreement and stuff. First statement arrives some time later and I pay a hefty chunk.

2nd statement arrives -- and they're charging me intererst!!!!! And at 19.24%!!!!! :ticked: There was no explicit indication that they would do that. What kills me is that I feel tricked into getting a damn card and having to pay super-high interest, that I never would have otherwise. Had I known that I would certainly have never accepted the card! But I didn't find this out for two months. There was no letter saying "you got the card, but not the promo."

I call DFS and get basically nowhere, except they do credit me part of the interest charge. There's still this very slimy one-time "billing finance charge" I have to pay -- what the hell is that?

So I dash of a tersely-worded complaint and get a boilerplate response about how they calculate who is eligible for the promo and all that bullshit. I say bullshit because my credit rating is stellar.

I'm one of those freaky Americans who has (thank God) no credit card debt. I used to, but I started a very disciplined plan 4 years ago and I'm very happy to say that I have no debt. Not only that, when I had debt I paid on-time and well above the minimum. Sometimes multiple payments in one month! I also paid off a car loan in full a good 1.5 years before term.

I'm sure they never had any intention of honoring the promo, no matter how good one's credit rating is. In my opinion Dell Financial is a slimy organization that tries to bilk people out of more money. What a bunch of scumbags!

That's what you get for trying to be financially responsible. Effiing BASTARDS!!!! :ticked::ticked::ticked::ticked::ticked:

Do a little research on the Dell site, in particular the corporate side and find the email addresses for the customer service manager, credit manager, consumer sales manager, vp of operations, etc.  The higher up you go the better, email everyone.  Tell them your story politely and ask for help in solving the matter.  If you really want to lay it on heavy, tell them you are a loyal customer, but if you aren't credited for the interest, you'll be shopping Apple next time.  Someone will credit you for your interest and maybe offer something to make up for the inconvenience.  The lower people on the list will trip over themselves trying to help you so they can tell the higher-ups they took care of it.
