I've lost two dogs in my lifetime.  The first one was when I was a child, he was a pure bred German Shepard that my father brought in his coat pocket on the plane from Germany.  This was back in the early 70's and my father worked for the airlines.  I loved that dog because he was so tiny when I got him, we named him Wayne after John Wayne because he was so brave even though he could hardly walk.  I had Wayne for 5 years and one day I found him almost dead in the yard, we had let him out, he loved to go out in and run in the yard.  I went out to play with him and he was lying down and blood dripping out of his mouth, when I took him to the vet he informed us that someone had given him ground beef with glass in it and it had torn him up inside, there was no choice but to put him to sleep because he was in so much pain, the vet gave him pain killers but he said that Wayne could go on for days like this.  I was 13 years old, I'm 46 and I still cry when I think about him, he was family, the only thing he lacked was the ability to talk ... and he did in his way.

My 2nd dog was a cocker spaniel, I got her also when she was very young, her mother had died giving birth so the vet let me have her at 4 1/2 weeks.  I nursed her, fed her with a baby bottle, she was my baby (I already had my two kids and was pregnant with my third).  When my third child was born Dinah was 10 months old, the bond she had with us and the kids and especially the new baby, it was incredible.  I had two babies.  In 2000 I moved up and asked a very good friend to keep her until I found a place, that it would be no more than 3 months and I would go down and get her.  A month later I found out she was hit by a car and killed.  Dinah was my companion, she sat with me to watch TV, she woke me up in the morning (I didn't need an alarm clock) by literally washing my face with her tongue, she would jump in the pool with me at night when I got home from work and went for my daily swim, she was my baby...(crap I'm crying now).

So here's another person that understands, I will pray to St. Peter, he is the patron of animals and I'll also light a candle.  HUGS HUGS HUGS 

Pets love us unconditionally, they're better than a lot of humans.
