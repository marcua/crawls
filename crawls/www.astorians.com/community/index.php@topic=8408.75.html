I have several dozen locally/independently-owned restaurants to choose from within walking distance of my apartment.  I have 0 (zero) bookstores within walking distance of my apartment, whether chain or local.  I live by Ditmars so I wouldn't count Seaburn, and I'm not counting used bookstores, as wonderful as they are.

If I had no restaurants to choose from within walking distance, I'd probably feel differently about a chain restaurant opening (although I don't really know because I have not eaten at most of them) - I'd be glad for something, although I'd still prefer a locally-owned restaurant.  Likewise, if a B&N opened up near Ditmars, I'd be thrilled, although I'd really rather see a locally-owned bookstore.

Barnes & Noble is a New York original, btw.  Its current CEO, who purchased the name rights in the 1970s and expanded it from one store on Fifth Ave. to what we know today (love it or hate it) graduated from Brooklyn Tech.
