So have any of y'all actually ventured to La Papa and checked it out? I'm kinda surprised to see such vehement criticism of a place nobody has actually eaten at yet. We live around the corner, and my husband had a potato from there yesterday. Have a little faith in the Mundo folks!

I'm looking at the menu right now, and I can tell you that this place is super Mundo-esque fast food potato stuff. Their stuff is skewed to the Turkish, but they also have some Argentine stuff (dulce de leche crepe) and some innovative stuff (banana and nutella panini) as well as reasonably priced baked potatoes. ($7 for a potato with five toppings plus butter and kasseri cheese.) I had some of my husband's and the flavors were awesome. Tzatziki goes well in a potato I gotta say. :)

Hubster said that really nice friendly guy from Mundo was working there yesterday. I want to try the Turkish-style hot dog for lunch today, and maybe the red lentil Turkish soup? Wondering what "uludag" Turkish soda is, so might have to check that out as well. Tho the potatoes come with free coke or sprite.

This seems to be something new and Turkish. (The menu says "Kumpir, a baked potato filled with various fillings, is a popular fast food item in Turkey.")

Anyway, there's a review from some folks who actually ate La Papa's food. :)
