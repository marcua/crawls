hookah on Steinway...do they all have 'club-like' atmosphere on weekend?
« on: August 14, 2015, 04:51:59 PM »

I am trying to arrange an outing with some friends in Little Egypt, ideally for a Friday or Saturday night, for dinner followed by hookah.  But I've noticed when riding down Steinway...I think on Friday and Saturday nights...a real change on the street...people who look like they are out to go 'clubbing'...I suddenly see 'bouncers' outside many hookah bars, etc.

This is NOT the atmosphere I am looking for..  I just want to hang with a few friends and do hookah,  with the normal regulars (i.e., Egyptian men) who might frequent these places.  Don't want loud club idiots, don't want to contend with 'velvet ropes' and/or bouncers.

So...if I do want to try hookah on a Friday or Saturday, is there a particular hookah place anyone knows of that does NOT become club-like on the weekend...or is there a certain part of STeinway that might be less likely to attract club-going people?  Thing is, I want to have dinner at Mombar on Steinway near 28th Ave, and from there walk to a hookah place nearby...

Thanks!!

Big fan of El Khayam aka the Arab Community Center. It has exactly what you are looking for and I believe is the original hookah spot on Steinway.


They have a backyard that you could smoke hookah in the summer. The hookahs are great and the whole place is no frills and no alcohol. Suggest getting the mint tea to go with it. They also allow you to bring food from outside.
