2006 is an off year for NYC and NY State.  In Queens, elections are being held for the following positions:

Justice of the Supreme Court (court of general jurisdiction, not the high court of the state)
Judge of Civil Court - County
Judge of Civil Court - District
District Attorney

As is true throughout NYC, candidates for these positions are heavily controlled by the party machinery, and many candidates are running on most or all party lines.  The District Attorney candidate is Richard Brown, the long-time (since 1991) and current DA for Queens, and he is running unopposed and on three party lines -- Democrat, Republican and Conservative.

There is one ballot proposal for an amendment to the NY State Constitution - something about providing an acre of forest preserve land for drinking water for a town in exchange for 12 acres somewhere else.

There is more information here, from the NYC Board of Elections.
