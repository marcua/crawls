Post by: lanseaux on January 18, 2008, 12:34:53 PM
Trains work well in France, and I'd recommend the TGV (bullet train) over flying to head south from Paris.  Thing is, though, that the TGV doesn't really stop in small towns, nor does it take long enough to really need to do so.  It really is fast.   8-)

As for places to go in the south of France, there are several options:

1.  Classic Riviera/Nice region.  If this is your choice, Nice is a must-see, but I think my favourite smaller town is Saint-RaphaÃ«l, a bit west of Nice.  The whole region is choked with tourists as the weather gets warmer.  To the west, not really part of the Riviera but not too far away, is Marseilles, which has a totally different feel.

2.  North and west of there is the heart of Provence and surrounding region, which is lovely, with charming towns with a lot of Roman history - and ruins (sometimes still in use).  Aix en Provence, Arles, Avignon (which is a bit farther west), Orange - and my favourite, Vaison la Romaine, are all within the area I'm talking about. 

3.  The western part of the Mediterranean coast, basically starting west of Marseilles and extending to the Spanish border.  This region is called Languedoc-Roussillon.  Here, my favourite city is Perpignan, which is somewhat off the beaten tourist path (but which still has a lot of tourism).  It is quite close to Spain and in the heart of the French part of Catalunya, so it has a somewhat different feel to the Riviera region, but I love it.  A really interesting and beautiful ancient fortified city in this area is Carcassone, which I think is a World Heritage Site.

I've lived in France and travelled extensively all over the country, so I'd be happy to give further detail/impressions on any of the above or other points.  Feel free to PM me as well if you want to discuss things in more detail.
