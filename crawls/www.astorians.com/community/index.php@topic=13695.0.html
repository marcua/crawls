On Steinway Street, where the Municipal Parking lot is located, there used to be a school. PS 6. This was one of those really old buildings and the school went from Kindergarten to 6th grade. From there you went to your local feeder Junior High School which had grades 7, 8 and 9, except for those in the Special Progress (SP) classes which combined the three grades into only 2 years.

As a treat for the 6th graders we were allowed to get lunch outside of the building once a week. My girlfriends and I always went across the street to Woolworth which was located on the Southeast corner of Steinway and 31st Avenue.

Here is the lunch menu we used to sit over for almost half our lunchtime trying to make decisions.

Man, this is so not an Astoria memory, but when I was little, my mom used to bring me to Woolworth's back home (in CT) when she'd take me out shopping and I LOVED sitting at the counter on the stools. I would always order a hot dog and an orange soda. (And actually, to this day, I tend to order the same things over and over at places I like. If nothing else, I'm consistent.)

So for some reason, it took me a while to get the gist of being able to drink through a straw. I was totally normal otherwise, but straws baffled my little kid mind. I just couldn't work 'em. So one day, I was at Woolworth's with my mom, sitting at the counter, probably 4ish years old. My hot dog and orange soda were placed in front of me, and I decided that TODAY would be the day that I finally got it, instead of just taking the straw out and drinking the soda from the cup. After a few tries, I GOT IT! And I announced it LOUDLY. "MOM! I DID IT! I GOT THE STRAW TO WORK!" And, according to my mom, the other people at the counter clapped, and I got a scoop of ice cream on the house.

I miss Woolworth's. That store shut down when I was in junior high. It's now an FYE, if I'm remembering the layout of the mall right.
