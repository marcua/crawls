Ok, after all the odes to vegetarian duck, I have to come out on this one.  I do not like the vegetarian duck.

Quote from: Harlan on November 28, 2006, 02:05:27 PM
I'm decidedly not a vegetarian, but I honestly think that the vegetarian duck is the tastiest protein source on Wave's menu!

I think the real duck is much tastier.  The vegetarian duck tastes to me like a weird, dense, rubbery soggy raw piece of bread - which makes some sense considering it is made from wheat gluten.  It's not the fact that it is a vegetarian substitute (although I'm not really into vegetarian substitutes for meat - i.e. fake meat - I do eat them occasionally and have no objection to them, even though I am also decidedly a meat eater), it's just not appealing to me.  In a way, it's intriguing to me -- I'll have a piece now and then from someone else's plate, to see if I suddenly like it, but I haven't changed my mind yet.

I see that most of the descriptives I have used to describe the vegetarian duck relate to texture as opposed to taste.  I see that megc has referred to its "bit of sweetness" (I agree), and I think that is another issue I have with it.  However, I do not have a sweet tooth at all, so for a protein source to have some sweetness is very disconcerting to me.  The other night, I had Wave's real duck in spicy brown sauce, and it was delicious.
