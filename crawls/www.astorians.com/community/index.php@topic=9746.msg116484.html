In regard to the whole zip code thing, the first three numbers of your zip code is known at the prefix, and I think that's used to direct your mail through a general sorting facility.  Queens has three different zip code prefixes that I can think of (while I think the other boroughs only have one each): 111- zip codes go through Long Island City, 113- go through Flushing, and 114- go through Jamaica.  I bet the OP could put Flushing on his mail, and as long as he put the 11377 zip code on there, she/he would get mail.  My buddy always puts "Queens" on his mail (he's a die-hard Queens kid who's jealous of the other boroughs) and he gets his mail just fine.  The mail is totally driven by the zip code.

Long Island City encompasses Astoria, Sunnyside, Woodside. Dutch Kills, Ravensview, Bridge, and that really industrial  part that is booming now, that we have always called LIC. My guess is that it doesn't have another designation because no one really lived there before fifty years ago, and because it was its own incorporated town before it joined Queens.
So, at one point there was not the whole 5 borough thing, and I think LIC joined Queens/NYC in 1898. There's a few books by an historian Seyfried that better explains it (and I presume others). (i've always found this interesting)
Anyway, as far as 49th street- it is the last block in Astoria when moving towards the east. The zip is usually 11103 south of the BQE. North of the BQE 49th street is Definitely astoria- and very old school astoria, till it is bounded at the north by the water. In that area, just after 49 there is Hazen Street and then seventy-something street. This little trickery is attributable to that also being a really old part of Queens, and when they laid it out in a new grid that part just wouldn't conform. It is known as "East Elmhurst." (Which is code for "near the bridge to Rikers" --one of the oldest houses in Queens is by there.)
When traveling south on 49th, it stops being part of Astoria at Broadway. Many people who live south of B'way but north of 25A (northern blvd) say they live in Astoria out of convenience, but it is technically not Astoria, it is Long island City (and the zip at Steinway south of B'way is 11101). South of 25a is Woodside, only east of 49th street. West of 49th south of 25a it is Sunnyside.
I'd say Sunnyside is the most undervalued, btw.(and not in the following list of zips) But if you are into searching for real estate by the zip codes, you are looking for 11101-11106. If you look at it in zillow.com (site realtors use) you can really get a sense of it.
