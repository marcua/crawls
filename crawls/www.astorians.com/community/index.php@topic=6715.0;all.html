First, NYC is odd in that it's actually CHEAPER to rent from the airports.  Has to do with the competition.  However, for Enterprise at least, you can't rent from the airport unless you're flying and have a ticket to prove it. (though they don't always check)  Supposedly, it's some sort of airport regulation that says you can't rent from the airport if there's a local branch.  Weird.

Anyway, I almost always rent from the Enterprise on 31st near 36th Ave.  There rates are pretty good and if you're willing to make the effort when you call, they'll cut you a deal if you tell them about a rate you got somewhere else.  My wife just did that and we had a car for a week and a half for about $320 ($450 when you add in liability insurance).  I asked them about a frequent renter plan and they said they don't have one, but they'll "work something out", since I go there enough.

All the guys that work there are super nice and friendly and it always seems like they've got more people than they need, so they're pretty attentive.  The biggest problem with that location is that they aren't open after noon on Saturday and they're closed all day on Sunday.  That means you can't rent a car for just one day on the weekend and you need to have it for an extra day if you just want it Friday-Sunday.  They used to be willing to put on the sheet that you got it a day later to make up for that, but their policy has changed.

That enough info for ya?  ;-)

I rented from Avis at LaGuardia Airport recently. Everything about it was great except for figuring out where the hell the place was at the airport when I needed to drop the car off. Getting to the airport was very simple- just take the M60 bus, and there are free shuttles for each company that run at the airport to and from all the rental places, which I found fantastic. But you should know exactly where the place is when you go to drop the car off, or you will drive around in confusion like I did. Even the signs weren't very helpful because there were so many different ways to turn. But then, once I did drop the car off, they had shuttles going back to the airport and the guy would drop you off where you wanted to be dropped off. I got dropped off at the taxi area.

Also, I got unlimited miles and there wasn't any kind of tri-state area deal, plus they are open 24 hours. So I personally recommend the airport thing, even though renting from a more local place might seem more convenient. The airport is considered a preferred location, hence the cheapness here. Also, you don't have to be booked on a flight to rent from them. Not sure if that's true for all the other places.

By the way, I specifically chose not to rent from the local Enterprise because of the Sunday close and not 24-hour thing, because I knew I would be returning the car on a Sunday sometime around 11pm.. so that would've sucked for me.

I suggest you check out Zipcar.com.  I use their cars all the time.  The way it works is you can either rent by the hour, for $8-14. or by the day $69 and up for a maximum 125 free miles per day. The beauty is that THEY PAY FOR THE GAS.  Each car has a company gas card in it and all you do is make sure the car has at least 1/4 tank when you drop it off.  Zipcar has cars parked in garages and lots all over the city.  You go online, pick out the car you want.  Show up at the designated time, activate the rental by holding your issued card up to the EZ-pass-looking box on the windshield, the locks pop open, and off you go.  Super simple.  It's great for errands, and they have fun cars like Mini Coopers and Scions in addition to more practical ones for shopping.  In Astoria, they park them in a lot at Vernon Ave. at, I think, 30th Ave, and also over in L.I.C. and Sunnyside. It's not for everyone, but they do fill a niche for we drivers without cars in the city.
