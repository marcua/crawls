I too have had bad luck finding some common items at Rite Aid stores.  My girlfriend uses a particular brand of eye makeup remover, and they never seem to stock the stuff.  And it's a major brand, too.

I don't understand how this area can sustain so many Rite Aids.  I wonder what sort of density studies they do before opening a new store.  I read a book on the fast food industry, and there was a chapter on how Subway sandwich shops pop up all over the place because they put them anywhere, without respect to how many the population can sustain.  The inevitable result is that there are too many stores in a given area, and they choke each other out.  Maybe Rite Aid is falling into the same trap.

That said, I'm glad something is going in that old Blockbuster location.  I was worried it would sit vacant for a long time because that section of Steinway is no prize pig, and there are only so many retailers that can make use of such a huge space.  Plus, I noticed that Corcoran was the broker, which, at least to me, means a top dollar commercial real estate asking price.
