From Planned Parenthood:

Emergency contraception (Plan B) is now available without a prescription for people 18 and older, but we're still hearing reports of pharmacists refusing to provide it to their customers. This is outrageous. We need your help to ensure that every woman in every community can access emergency contraception (EC). We're asking people across the country to go to or to call their local pharmacies and to find out if EC is available. But we need your help —  join the Planned Parenthood Pill Patrol now!

We are already getting reports back from the field.

There's good news at a Target in Arizona:
"I called my pharmacy and Darcy was very helpful, obtained the price, let me know that I would need to pick it up at the pharmacy counter and provide my driver's license as proof of age. She said they had it in stock and were open until 9:00. Good customer service."

But not so great news at Walmart in Missouri:
Jack told us that at his Walmart, "If anyone in the Rx department wouldn't provide EC for religious reasons, they wouldn't provide it to the general public."

We need to know what's happening in your state. It's easy to sign up and easy to report back. Visit our website to find out how and then repost this bulletin so that your friends can join too.

P.S. We specifically picked Target, Walmart, Osco, and Costco because these pharmacies either have a thumbs-down policy on EC or have not responded to our requests to clarify their policies.

To counter the anti-choice link:  I found more information on the web regarding the difference between EC and a medication abortion.  An excerpt is below, and this is the link:  http://www.plannedparenthood.org/news-articles-press/politics-policy-issues/abortion-access/ec-mabortion-6138.htm.  It's from Planned Parenthood but I trust their information way more than I would ever trust The American Life League. They also provide sources to their information, something the ALL does not do.  Click on the link to read the whole article.  All of it is really interesting and I had to resist copying it all here for you to read.

 The Difference Between Emergency Contraception And Medication Abortion

There is considerable public confusion about the difference between emergency contraception and medication abortion because of misinformation disseminated by anti-choice groups. Emergency contraception helps prevent pregnancy; medication abortion terminates pregnancy. According to general medical definitions of pregnancy that have been endorsed by many organizations including the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists and the United States Department of Health and Human Services, pregnancy begins when a pre-embryo completes implantation into the lining of the uterus (ACOG, 1998; DHHS, 1978; Hughes, 1972; "Make the Distinction...," 2001). Hormonal methods of contraception, including emergency contraception, prevent pregnancy by inhibiting ovulation and fertilization (ACOG, 1998). Medication abortion terminates a pregnancy without surgery. By helping women to prevent unplanned pregnancies after unprotected intercourse, emergency contraception has the great potential to decrease the rate of abortion. By helping women terminate unwanted pregnancies up to 56 days after their last menstruation, medication abortion is a safe and effective option.

What is EC?
EC contains hormones that reduce the risk of pregnancy if started within 120 hours of unprotected intercourse. The treatment is more effective the sooner it begins. Plan B® is currently the only product marketed specifically as emergency contraception. Certain oral contraceptives taken in increased doses may also be used as EC ("FDA Approves...," 1999; Rodrigues et al., 2001; Van Look & Stewart, 1998).
