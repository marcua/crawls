I posted this somewhere else on the forum, but I can't find it.

Many years ago I poured myself a cold glass of water from my kitchen sink. As I recall, it was winter and the water was really cold, the way I like it. I put my filled glass on the counter to answer the phone. I guess the call was about 30 minutes long, and  I then *had* to leave the house. I came back home around 5-6 hours later and saw my water-filled glass sitting on the counter. At the bottom of the glass there was some (UGH!) sediment.

I washed out the glass thouroughly, and then I gave it a 10 minute soak with diluted ammonia—to be sure that the sediment was *not* caused by soap scum left in the glass—rinsed it out thoroughly and let it dry in the dish rack. When it was dry, I filled it, again, with cold water from the tap.

The next morning the bottom of the glass was again sediment laden. I FREAKED.

I don't use tap water from my building for *any* cooking or drinking purposes. I keep a Brita bottle, and a 2-quart bottle that I use for keeping the filtered water in. Coffee, tea, pasta, soup, baking, stewing—don't care what—no tap water. I also buy the filters at Costco at half the price of anywhere else. Is it cost effective to *not* use tap water? Probably not. But compared to what health issues might arise from contaminated water, or the cost of bottled water, I consider it, for myself, a necessary expense.

Try my little experiment for yourself. Make sure you use a clear glass glass. If there's gunk in the bottom of the glass, you decide if you want to drink it, give it to your kids and pets.

We have a skinny five litre container that I fill with unfiltered tap water and keep in the fridge for cold water, particularly in the summer when it's not so fresh and cold straight from the tap.  There is never any sediment in the bottom of it.

Sediment can indicate a problem with the pipes in your building.  If you are concerned about harmful impurities, you can call 311 for the test kit from the DEP.  I know for certain they test for lead, and they may test for other substances as well, but I can't remember.  (We had ours tested, and our water is completely lead-free.)
