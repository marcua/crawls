Quote from: jennifer on August 08, 2008, 10:38:59 PM

It would be very helpful if people would post what these day care centers are charging.  There are a number of us  looking and it could possibly save a lot of legwork. 

Unfortunately, in looking for daycares for your little precious legwork is always mandatory.  When my daughter was an infant I was looking into getting some type of day care place near my home for her.  I called the Day Care Council of NY 212-206-7818.  They actually send you a referral print out of all childcare providers in your area.  Unfortunately Astoria does not have a lot of "Day Care" places for infants.  What I did find was a lot of "Family Day Cares" where the day care is in a person's home (the provider has a license with NYS) and they only have 3 or 4 kids per adult.  The family day care that I went to charged $65 per day.  Unfortunately the hours were very inconvenient since we have to factor in commute time into the city. We decided to pass on it and opted for a nanny instead.  We got lucky with our current nanny (after having 2 other horrible nannies).  We met her by word of mouth through my mother in law's cleaning lady.  The cost for a nanny is dependant on what your agreement with her is.  We pay about $10 per hour.  Some nannies charge more and require that you provide them with breakfast and lunch among other things.  Again this is all  based on your agreement with her. 

A friend gave me a website - Care.com.  This website post profiles for a wide array of babysitters and nannies / their rates / and available times.  My friend was able to get a part time babysitter who picks up her son from his day care and stays at home with him until she get home from work. 

Hope this helps.   :-)

We have looked at 2 full time day cares so far (I'm due in Sept), both of which we liked: one in Astoria that was $1200 per month for infants, and one in Manhattan that was $2310 per month for infants. the one in Astoria though was 8-6, while the one on the rock was 9-5 -- the rate goes up to $2449 per month if you want an extra hour. At both the cost was less for toddlers, and in Astoria you could do 2/3/4 days/week or just mornings/afternoons for less. Oh, and the one in Manhattan charged us $150 to get on the waiting list... which we forked over even though we don't think we'll go with that place (mostly b/c of the logistics of commuting with baby seem insurmountable -- it was extremely nice), just so we have options when the time comes!

I haven't looked into nannies but $10 an hour is on par with what I've heard from friends. I've also heard that you can pay a bit less -- like $7 or $8 an hour -- if you share a nanny with another couple. I think we will explore the nanny option too but not until the baby is here.
