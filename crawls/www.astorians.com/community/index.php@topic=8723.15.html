Great easy mixing with Frank's. My dad always used to do a bit of a varient, adding some other spices he liked. I personally love making a tangy/chipolte BBQ rather than the straight up hot stuff. I don't quite have the tolerance for the really really hot stuff--at least not for more than a wing or two. I like to mix Worchestshire sauce, molasses, brown sugar, ground peppers (I have some ground tabasco peppers that are hard to get and these really make a kick), garlic and some cajun rub together. If you buy frozen wings and poke pinholes in the skins, you'll get thiner, crispier skins and the rub will soak into the meat of the chicken more. I actually like to poke the holes, rub the wings down and then lay them out on a cookie sheet and bake them at 450 until I get the skin texture I want. I make extra sauce so that some goes on when they're baking and some is left for dipping. It's more intense, but I'm kind of a perfectionist when it comes to my carnivorism.
