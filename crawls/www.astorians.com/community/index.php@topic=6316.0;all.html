I used to live near Fat Cat Tattoos when it was on 23rd Ave.  They had random shop hours (read: rarely open) and whenever they were open they were usually hanging out in front with their skateboards.  That being said, I have no idea how good their artists are, but that's all I ever saw from that place. 

My recommendation is New York Adorned on 2nd Ave on the Lower East Side.  It's a little pricier than some places  but they do great work (and, anyway, who wants to bargain shop for a tattoo?!).  I walked in on a Sunday afternoon with no appointment and was getting my tattoo about 30 minutes later.  I didn't have a picture, I just wanted a phrase in some pretty script.  I told the guy what I wanted (I wish I could remember his name), he went in the back for a few minutes and came back with a beautiful tattoo.

As far as the pain factor goes, I think it partly depends on where on your body you get the tattoo, and partly depends on your pain tolerance.  Personally, I love getting tattoos, including the pain (I know, this kinda makes me a freak).

Anyway, that's my two cents  :mrgreen:
