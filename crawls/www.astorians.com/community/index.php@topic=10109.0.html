Arepas Cafe...really yummy
« on: April 10, 2008, 07:48:09 PM »
I might be way behind the times here, but I didn't see many other mentions of this place...

 I decided to get off the train a stop early, at 36th Ave, so I could walk a little ways home and enjoy the warm day. I've heard pretty good things about this place, so when I walked by, I thought I'd give it a try and pick up something to take home. I ordered the arepa with sweet plantain, avocado, and some kind of yummy white cheese. It was delicious, and they give you a little thing of green sauce that I think I could have eaten with a spoon. Anything involving corn and/or cheese generally makes me happy, but I had never had an arepa other than the crappy street fair variety, so this was a real treat!  There were lots of other things on the menu that I definitely plan on trying, and they also do all sorts of tropical fruit drinks that sound awesome. Especially for when summer get here.
It was more of a large snack than a meal, but I was planning to throw together a salad and some leftovers anyway, so that didn't bother me. For $5, it was a nice amount of delicious, homemade food. The guy who owns the cafe was very friendly, and we chatted while I waited. He told me that he has had great response to the food, but that business has been kind of slow (maybe because of location?). I figured if I liked it, I'd give him a plug, so here I am. It's on 36th Ave, I think b/w 33rd and 34th Sts. I will definitely go back, and next time bring some wine and eat in!

Arepas Cafe is unbelievable and SO reasonable. Ricardo, the owner is so sweet and funny and from what I heard he's the one that delivers the food! I have found that some of the sauces in the meat arepas can be a little salty, but the flavor is great. Everything from scratch and their cane sugar lemonade is delish (especially as their lunch special). It's BYO now, but they're working on a license.

I'd have to say the "French Bakery" next door is horrendous. I miss Le Petit Prince. That place doesn't bake their stuff on premise (which doesn't mean it's bad, but don't call yourself a bakery) and last time I went there is smelled like urine and bug spray. bleh. The baguette was more like an Italian loaf.

But I have heard good things about the crepe place across the way.
