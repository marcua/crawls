I've been pretty disappointed by the pizza options in the Ditmar's area lately. We regularly order pies from Portabello's and Michael Angelo's, but they haven't been as good as they used to be. Sometimes the pie is good and sometime bad, but never "wow, this is great pizza!" Il Forno is very good, but I like a big classic pie. It's so hard to find a good crispy crust anymore. It's enough to make me want to open my own place, but that should be the last thing this hood needs--another pizza place, right?

Napoli makes a good, classic pie, but they're all the way over on 35th Avenue and won't deliver to me. That's part of my ongoing rant that Astoria is too damn large. I should be able to order pizza from Napoli because it is in Astoria and I live in Astoria, but I can't, and that stinks. End of rant.

There used to be a place on ditmars, next to MacDonalds, where the video game store was.  It was open only for about a year or two, but it had the most incredible pizza in Astoria.  Sadly when McDonald's burned down a few years ago (leading to it's rennovation) it took this place with it and they never rebuilt.  These days my favorites are Da Franco's (at the coops they built from the old Eagle Electric building on 21st st and 24th Ave down by Astoria Park), and Forno Siciliano (on Ditmars and 45th).  IMHO Da Franco's is better but more expensive and it's a bit out of the way.
