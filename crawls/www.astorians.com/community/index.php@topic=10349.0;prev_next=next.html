Quote
we walked all along the historical district area, but the line to see the liberty bell was outrageous. that should be on our agenda this time.

I don't know about that.  If you're really, really excited to see the bell, that's one thing, but there are lots of interesting historic sites that don't have long lines.  Check out Franklin Court, on Market St. between 3rd and 4th.  Lots of interesting stuff there if you're interested in early American history, as I am.  (probably pretty boring if you're not)

Actually, my favorite thing about Independence National Historical Park is how beautiful, green and semi-peaceful it is on a nice day.  It's relaxing.

Moviegoing in Philly is nice and civilized--you just wander between the Ritz theaters (three mini-multiplexes, a few blocks apart) and decide what looks good.  People who go there love movies and (almost always) know how to behave.

I lived in Philly for 9 years and miss it dearly.

Food:

You can get cheesesteaks anywhere and nine times out of ten they'll be good. I prefer Rick's in Reading Terminal Market (12th and Market near the Hard Rock). Pat's and Gino's are really for the tourists. Jim's on South street is pretty good too. If you like Japanese you must go to Tampopo's on 21st street between Chestnut St and Walnut st. Continental on the corner of Chestnut and 18th or 19th is pretty good. It's sort of family style for dinner but the lunch had good burgers. Plus it's cattycorner to Scoops De Ville. Jones on 8th (or 7th) and Chestnut is good comfort food. right near it on Chestnut is El Azteca...great mexican.

Places to see:
Mutter Museum is a must...although must people I take there lose their appetites.

Rodin Museum on the parkway is free and pretty good.

The Waterworks (behind the Art Museum) has neat looking buildings and the tour is kinda interesting.

Eastern State Penitentiary http://www.easternstate.org/ is really cool (21st and Fairmount)

In Olde City (down near Independence Hall) there are walking ghost tours (which I used to do) which are really fun. they start at 5th and chestnut 215-413-1996 http://www.ghosttour.com/philadelphia.html plus it ends at City Tavern which has good (yet pricy) beer.

A lot of people like to check out the shops on South Street (on South Street...d'uh between 2nd and 8th....beyond 8th it's projects and Harry's Occult shop).

If you have a car you can check out Fort Mifflin near the airport. http://www.fortmifflin.com/

If you need more feel free to message me. I love Philly.
