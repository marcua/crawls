I vote for the 30th avenue area.  It's got everything you listed.  I've lived here for about a year now, having spent eight or so years living near the intersection of Broadway and Steinway, and I like it much better around this neck of the woods.  There's way more going on and the neighborhood has a bustling, happening feel.  I particularly like my morning walk to the subway - past the restaurants and cafes setting up for the day, past the bodegas stocking up fresh fruits and veggies, etc.  Weekend evenings are great with everybody out and about having a good time, especially in the summer.

I love Astoria and wouldn't want to live anywhere else in NYC.  It has everything except. . .charm.  There I said it!  I live off 34th Ave and think my location is the best in Astoria: near culture (the movie theater and Museum of Moving Image and a bit more distant Socrates Sculpture Park and the Noguchi Museum), easy access to both the R/M & N/Q subway lines and to the Q101 bus to Manhattan), near excellent eating and drinking establishments (Cafe Bar, Studio Square, 5 Napkin Burger, plus everything on Broadway), and since we're slightly away from the main commercial aves (Broadway, 30th & Ditmars) it is nice and quiet. 

By the way, there is always a Ditmars v Broadway debate.  Not to engage in it  but check out all the threads on the abusive scummy folk in the Ditmars area, the smelly corner of Ditmars and 31st, and other unpleasantness over there.
