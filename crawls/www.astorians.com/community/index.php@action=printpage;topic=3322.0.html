edit after I typed this all out: Gah, I've obviously spent too much time thinking about this...

I used to do this when I was a teenager, maybe 14 or 15. It was probably because I was always reading about it, and my friends were way into astrology and whatnot (not that they're the same things, but they were in the same section at the bookstore :) ) I used to be so good at it that if I wanted to interact with a specific person in my dream, I'd say to myself something like "Johnny Depp will walk through that door now, and we're gonna be at 6 Flags and go on roller coasters" and there he'd be. But honestly, for me, the absolute best thing to do lucid dreaming is to fly.

I don't remember exactly how I trained myself to do it. Tthe fact that I read about it a lot and talked to my friends about it probably had a lot to do with it, because for me, a lot of my everyday life gets filtered into my dreams so at some point during my dream, I'd become aware that I was dreaming. One technique I remember was to ask yourself whenever you remember, "am I dreaming?" and then to test yourself. If you're dreaming, you may be able to force strange things to happen just by "willing" them to happen. (A specific thing I remember was to try to make the time on a clock change...if you look at it and it says 10:00, look away and "force" it to be something else when you look back.) However, I did get to one point when I did this back in the day where I would ask myself if I was dreaming, and since my answer when awake was always automatically, "no, I'm awake," it became my answer in my dream.

You know how in dreams, completely bizarre things seem totally normal? I think the key to lucid dreaming is REALLY thinking about what's going on around you, and when you realize that something strange isn't normal, you'll realize that you're dreaming.

Despite the fact that I've become lucid in my dreams a few times in the past couple years, I've never been able to take advantage of it - I usually start thinking something like "AWESOME! Now I'm gonna go fly and do this and that and hang out with this person, and its going to rule..." and I get so excited that I wake myself up.

Post by: mirki2 on September 09, 2006, 11:10:02 PM
Lucid dreaming is something academically reported and still being studied, actually.
Specific to what you're talking about, Merm, a recent study in the Peer Reviewed Journal "Dreaming" reviewed techniques for inducing lucid dreams. The study is small, only 20 participants, so they admit the limitations of the study straight of the bat but according to the abstract, the researchers found that the reflection-intention technique (using a journal) was to some degree effective. researchers again note that the study sample size was small and it would have to be repeated in similar conditions repeatedly until a large enough sample size was attained before any conclusions could be reached...

here's the reference (from PsychInfo, which I am thankful to still have access to as an academic nerd):
Paulsson, Timo; Parker, Adrian. The Effects of a Two-Week Reflection-Intention Training Program on Lucid Dream Recall. [References]. [Journal; Peer Reviewed Journal] Dreaming. Vol 16(1) Mar 2006, 22-35.
