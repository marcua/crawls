I know you are probably in a hurry to find something, but you shouldn't go through a broker and stay away from buildings. The brokers are going to show you expensive apartments or total garbage for the price range you are looking at. I pay 1100 for my 2 bedroom in a private house and I know plenty of others that pay similar prices in this neighborhood. (I always gasp when I see what people in Astoria are paying and wonder how you can afford to eat.) Anyway, try getting the word out that you are looking. When we were looking we went to several realtors that told us we would never find anything for the price we wanted and showed us lots of terrible apartments that were out of our price range. We got the word out with friends and acquaintances that we were looking and sure enough a friend of a friend knew someone who was looking for a tenant and we were able to hook up right away. Maybe it was luck, but I think you shouldn't rule out that way of finding an apartment.

Also, look at nearby neighborhoods if you are willing. My friend owns some homes in Woodside and had to reduce his rental fees on almost all of his apartments because everyone was moving out to buy co-ops or find cheaper rentals. Of course, he is the type of landlord that wants to keep his tenants for years, so that makes a difference.

Good LUck!
