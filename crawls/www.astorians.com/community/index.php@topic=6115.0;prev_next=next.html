Astoria-LIC Waterfront Parks - Upcoming Events
« on: April 18, 2007, 05:01:55 PM »
As spring unfolds, there are a lot of amazing events to get us all back out on the Astoria-LIC waterfront!  Gardening, walking and cycling tours, opportunities to learn about water quality, arts and crafts, and community-building meetings about parks are all happening in the coming two weeks. And, after that, there's even more fun to be had. Please check out the events below, and also visit www.astoriawaterfront.org/upcoming for information on events throughout the spring and summer. (Though please note that due to some technical issues, the site looks a bit distorted right now- we're working on that!).
 
And, for those of you who've not heard yet, Karen Overton is the new Coordinator for the Astoria-LIC Catalyst Project. She may be reached at 718-706-8044 or Karen.Overton@parks.nyc.gov.
 
Garden Days at Two Coves Garden/Goodwill Triangle!

What: Come meet friends and neighbors to help start a new community garden and keep the garden beautiful. On April 21st , we're kicking off the garden season with regular weekly gardening days every Saturday. We'll be planting, weeding, building raised garden beds, and more!
When: Saturday, April 21, 10am-1pm and Sunday, April 22, 10am-1pm
Where: Goodwill Triangle/Two Coves Garden at 30th Avenue, 8th Street, and Astoria Blvd
Who: Friends of Two Coves Garden
More info: Contact Karen at Karen.Overton@parks.nyc.gov or 718-706-8044

 
Astoria-LIC Waterfront Parks Alliance Meeting

What: Join us to help figure what we can do to improve our Astoria/LIC waterfront parks and our waterfront as a whole.  This month we'll finalize our slate of organizational positions, plan It's My Park! Day, and more!
When: Thursday, April 26, 6pm (4th Thursday of the month)
Where: Greater Astoria Historical Society , Quinn Building, 35-20 Broadway, 4th Floor,  Long Island City, NY 11106
Who: All Are Welcome!
Why: To join together with friends and neighbors to re-envision our waterfront and improve our neighborhood.
More info: Please call 917-902-9165 or email Emily.Maxwell@parks.nyc.gov

 
Astoria-LIC Waterfront Walking Tour

What: Join Richard Melnick, from the Greater Astoria Historical Society as he divulges a wealth of information about his local neighborhood. The pace will be leisurely, with plenty of stops to point out places of interest.
When: Saturday, April 28, 11AM-1PM
Where: Start at corner of Ditmar Blvd and Shore Blvd, and end at Rainey Park
Who: All are welcome. Make sure to wear comfortable walking or hiking shoes.
Sponsored by: Greater Astoria Historical Society and Partherships for Parks
More info: Please RSVP to (718) 278-0700 or visit www.astorialic.org

 
Kite Flight: SkyScrapers!

What: Socrates Sculpture Park and the Noguchi Museum present KiteFlight, a special workshop for children to build and decorate a kite, then fly it in the Park. The workshop is free and supplies will be provided.
When: Sunday, April 29, 2007, 11AM-2PM (rain or shine)
Where: Socrates Sculpture Park, 32-01 Vernon Blvd. at Broadway
Who: It's a children's workshop and everyone is welcome!
More info: For more info, please visit www.socratessculpturepark.org, or email info@socratessculpturepark.org

 
CityParks Seniors Fitness

What: Have fun getting fit with tennis, yoga and walking. Exercise, even in moderate amounts, can help us feel and look better, maintain or lose weight, reduce our risk for heart disease and diabetes, and minimize symtoms of arthritis.
When: April 30-June 22 2007
>Tennis T/TH 10AM
>Yoga T/TH 9AM
>Walking M/W 9AM
Where: Astoria Park, Tennis Courts at 21 st. and Hoyt Avenue South
Who: Free for seniors age 60 or over!
Sponsored by: City Parks Foundation, www.CityParksFoundation.org, (718) 760-6999
 
Astoria Park Alliance Meeting

What: The Astoria Park Alliance began this year. We are planning great events (Health & Fitness Fair and clean-up days in the park and along the shore, as well as discussing important issues that affect those who use and enjoy the park.The group represents local residents and organizations who are working together to ensure the overall health of the park. Please bring your ideas and positive energy and enjoy our healthy snacks too.
When: Tuesday, May 1, 2007 6:30pm
Where: Steinway Reformed Church, 41-01 Ditmars Blvd at 41st Street
Who: All are welcome!
More info: If you have questions please call Helen Ho at 718-520-5913 or e-mail at helen.ho@parks.nyc.gov. Helen works with Partnerships for Parks and also happens to be an Astoria resident.
 
Green Queens: Estuary Wading & Cycling Adventure

What: RIDE (10AM-1PM): Join us to ride the proposed Queens East River Greenway, learn about local geology, and check out local environmental projects in action. We'll explore the local waterfront parks, visit a fantastic natural beach on the East River, and tour Build It Green! The relaxed ride is under 5 miles, and will take up to 3 hours, discussions and stops included. Stay after for a great workshop to learn all about water quality! This tour will be led by the Astoria/LIC Waterfront Parks Catalyst Project of Partnerships For Parks, www. itsmypark.org, in conjunction with http://cityreliquary.org/, and http://bignyc.org/. Feel free to ride with us to warm up for the Five Boro Bike Tour.

WATER QUALITY WORKSHOPS (11:30-1PM and 1:30-3PM)

The introductory workshop will explore human impacts on water quality through hands-on tests of physical and chemical water properties using scientific field instruments.

Please join us for the bike ride, the workshop, or both, but make sure to save the date for this fun, neighborly ride!

Presented by the New York Soil & Water Conservation District and Partnerships for Parks.

When: Saturday, May 5, 10pm-1pm. Workshops at Hallets Cove at 11:30AM-1PM and 1:30-3PM.
Where: Start at Gantry Plaza State Park, 48th Avenue and the East River
Who: All are welcome. Children under 18 must be accompanied by a parent or guardian.
More Info: For more information or to register, please email Channaly.Oum@parks.nyc.gov or call (212) 676-1929. For updates in case of inclement weather, please call 917-902-9165.
