Title: Re: AC or Oscillating Fan?
Post by: amsci on April 27, 2009, 09:36:23 AM
I pretty much melt in hot weather, but pretty much try to stick to using a fan in the daytime and use my AC only in the evenings so I can sleep.

You might want to look into getting a small AC at least for when you sleep. Two things to consider: One is that what AC does that a fan can't is pull the humidity out of the room, which can greatly affect your comfort level. Second, you're on the top floor of your building... hot air rises so your place will feel warmer. I remember living in a 4th floor walk-up and there was a 15 degree temp difference from the first floor to my apt! Ughh.

Another thing I like to do is to keep my curtains closed during the day (especially if I'm not home) so the sun doesn't heat up my place.

Whatever you do, hope you keep cool!  8-)

Post by: ManicMaurice on April 29, 2009, 03:01:38 AM
Quote from: RayG on April 28, 2009, 11:41:17 AM
How many windows in the room? You can get a window fan that sucks out- this will pull air IN the other window and make it cooler even if it is pulling in the warm air. Just having the air circulate can be better than a breeze blowing on you all night.

I'm with RayG on this one. It's exactly what I did at my last apartment.

If you only have 1 window put the window fan unit in the top of the window blowing out and open the window at the bottom -- this way the window fan blows the hot air out and sucks in the much cooler outside air. You could try it the other way around, window fan in the bottom and window open at the top, but I suspect that since hot air rises and all you'll be more successful with it the opposite way.

Hope this helps, I completely understand wanting to avoid a window hogging air conditioner. I'm from FL, so I might be a little warm weather biased, but I've successfully avoided them for 5 years and counting now. :-)
