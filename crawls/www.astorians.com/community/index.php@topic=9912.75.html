Hello fellow Astorians!
This posting has recently been brought up to our attention. It seemed like something we would personally like to respond to. Firstly, we’d like to apologize to the person who was asked by our waiter to move to another table for that inconvenience. Although we are a small place, we try to accommodate as many patrons as we can given our size, moving to another table happens very rarely, going up or down a chair on our communal table maybe slightly more often but even that is rare…Regardless, it should always be assisted by one of the staff and handled in a way common in the service industry. So we would really like to invite the person who wrote the original posting to come join us again here at winegasm(our treat!) to provide him/her with a much better dining experience, one they’ll hopefully have good memories of.
Secondly, our food is something we have worked on a lot in the past and right now our kitchenette is headed for a second change since we opened  (6 months) it will feature more of a fall /winter selection of small and larger plates better suited for a small wine and cocktail bar / eatery. This edition will have many great suggestions and ideas we got from our patrons. Lastly, all of us at Winegasm are working very hard to deserve Your attention and our little spot in a vast array of culinary choices in New York City and Astoria and we hope that with the addition of our tasty fall/winter cocktail list, expansion of our wine list (which will now include flights of wine as well) and a revamped menu under the helm of our new chef, our efforts are going to be recognized.
Cheers Everyone!
Laris, Richard and Dean

I finally tried it out today.  The space inside is beautiful, very well done.  The skylight is a nice touch and it's very artistic.  The staff are extremely friendly and accommodating and treated us very well - we were a party of four which morphed into a party of five and they were very gracious.  Winegasm has a pre-fixe brunch for just over $15, which includes THREE mimosas (you read that right, three full size mimosas - and they were tasty!).  I had the chicken sandwich which was nice and the rest of my group had pancakes and french toast (which looked the best of all of our choices, it came with nutella!).  It's a great addition to the area - go check them out!

My roommate and I had brunch at Winegasm last Sunday and it was delicious!  We had the croque madame (which I found out is a croque-monsieur plus a fried egg) and the quiche, with a side of potatoes - both dishes were generous portions, and we loved the mimosa deal!
