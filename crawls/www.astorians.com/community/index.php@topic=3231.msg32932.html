Quote from: StephanieL on September 16, 2006, 06:26:26 PM
OK, I'll sound a note of dissention here.  I wasn't impressed with the cross-cutting at all, and some of the dialogue was unintelligible.  The constant errors in geography and anachronisms also got distracting after a while.  I think the only thing I was impressed by is that I saw my apartment building in one shot--like when is that ever going to happen again?   :-D 

I'm not sure what geographic errors you noticed.  And as far as the anachronisms, I think we have to understand the difficulty of shooting on-location and trying to make the surroundings as accurate to the time they are meant to represent as possible.  I think they did a good job overall.  How, exactly, are they supposed to get an old RR train up to Astoria?  They don't exist anymore.  Or the idea of graffiti not being present on the trains.  And besides those of us who actually live in Astoria, who else will notice these discrepancies?  I just think it's something that needs to be noted, and accepted.  To me, the important part, which is the story of the characters, was told very well.  Astoria served as the backdrop for it, but was not the central piece of the film.

I don't necessarily believe that all the dialogue was meant to be heard completely clearly.  I think that was part of the point of the film.  Not all the cinematography was clear.  I think it was meant to serve a specific purpose of the uncertainly surrounding their lives.

Just my take on things, though...
