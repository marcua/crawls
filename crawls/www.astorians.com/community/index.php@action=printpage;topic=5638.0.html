Title: Playing with your food: New recipe ideas
Post by: Jonathan on March 21, 2007, 11:36:58 AM
Wine cured dry sausage and pasta:

Been working out a new recipe based upon talks and tastings with Ralph at Dave and Tony's Salumeria.  They have a wine cured dry sausage that has a deeper earthy flavor than most other types.  SO I went back to my kitchen and played around. Have made it 3 times now for clients and fairly happy with it. Please keep in mind that I work with recipes for 30-120 people. That's why I usually don't give exact measurements.

Have them peel and slice paper thin 1 wine cured dry sausage
1 marinated whole hot cherry pepper (optional)
1 bunch parsley washed. Pluck whole leaves.
1 bunch basil washed. Pluck whole leaves, cut off roots and tie up stems into a bunch.
4 cloves garlic sliced not chopped
Good olive oil
Salt and pepper to taste
Grated parm cheese (best you can get). For addition at table if desired.

Cooked pasta of choice.

Put wine sausage, cherry pepper, basil stems and garlic in pot with enough oil to just cover. Bring to boil and reduce to low simmer for 1/2 hour.  You are making an infused oil.  If you keep it at a low simmer the sausage will wilt but not overcook and shrivel up.  Taste it. Should be deep earthy meaty with back round of garlic and basil. Heat is from cherry pepper.  When it's done, remove cherry pepper and basil stems.  Season to taste.  Put cooked warm pasta into large service bowl. Using slotted spoon get all the sausage and garlic into pasta and add enough of the oil to coat.  Add in all basil and parsley leaves. Mix well. check for seasonings. Let sit a few minutes for leaves to slightly wilt. Serve with parm cheese on side. 

Save the oil for next meal.  It's awesome.
