For the last two years, we have waited for the corner of Steinway/28th ave to be filled. At long last, the corner has been filled by a resturant that Astorians can be proud of.

Luna de Juarez is an experience bar none. My partner and I have been talking about this place since the moment it opened and tonight we finally made the plunge.

We started with a pitcher of real Margaritas (no half stepping here). Then we got the Guacamole made at the table which was an experiance we had never enjoyed. They asked us what we wanted and blended everything there to our specifications.

We ordered, for our main courses, the Steak fajitas and the Parrillada Luna ( a mixed grill plate including Steak, chicken, shrimp and chorizo). Both dishes were well cooked and tasteful. We had our own plates that allowed us to sample from all the mini plates and main dishes that they placed before us and we felt like every bite was full of individual flavors that encouraged us to enjoy more.

However, what topped off the evening was the experience. Our waitress was new, but sweet and very attentive. And the owners came out to talk to us for quite a while, hopefully not something that will happen in the future because they will be too busy. they are very proud of what they have created here and I think Astoria will enjoy it.

But more important will be this summer. When, during the hot months, we can stop by and have a pitcher of Margaritas and some guacamole in the outdoor seating area. Ahhhh....what a treat.

Stop by....you won't be disappointed.

Just wanted to add to this discussion, as I went with a friend to this restaurant this past weekend.  Great to see such a beautiful space on this corner.......a wonderful visual addition to the neighborhood!

However, I was really disappointed by the food here........so much so that I felt embarassed to have brought my friend there after boasting about how great the restaurants are in Astoria!  I'm not gonna describe every last thing we ate, as I realize they're just starting up, and things will probably improve.  However, I was really taken aback at how expensive things were on the menu.  If I'd looked at the menu before going in, I would have just kept on walking down to Ali's or Mombar........the prices are comparable, but I've never been let down by the food at either restaurant.

Due to a pretty empty bank account, I opted for one of the cheapest things on the menu (as far as mains are concerned) beef tacos ($13 or $14 I think?), and my friend got the steak fajitas ($16).  my beef was really bland, so I tried my friend's fajitas, hoping all those peppers might have spiced things up a little.  no such luck :-\

I can say that the staff were very attentive, and the owner DID come over to ask how our meals were.........unfortunately I didn't have the guts to be honest, and mumbled a meek "It's good, thanks."  Hopefully they'll check out this board and try giving their patrons a little more bang for their buck.

We went last night.  We thought it was really terrific.  Portions are huge and we over ordered.  I'd start by saying what an extremely friendly bunch of people they are.  From the servers to what seemed to be an owner.  They really aim to please.  Though it could be that we were overwhelmed since we just suffered horrendous service at Mohave!  Anyway, we both had the soup that is described as a cream soup with mushrooms, vegetables and cheese.  It was pretty incredible.  Very delicious.  The bowl was about 14" in diameter.  Rather than just a soup that you eat in 2 minutes and forget - it was really a course to be enjoyed.  With that I asked for the fried avocado appetizer.  It's filled with shrimp and seafood.  We thought this was absolutely delicious.  It was rich without being too heavy.  And very flavorful.  The shrimp were perfect.  Then we had two large entrees.  One was the seafood stuffed chili rellenos and mine was the mixed meat burrito platter.  They were both very good.  My meat platter was gigantic.  The churisso was amazing.  The chicken was still moist and nicely char grilled.  The steak, however, was cooked to death.  The shrimp were amazing.  Again, a huge table-filling plate.  Soft, homemade, steaming tortillas on the side.  That meal could feed three.  The stuffed chili was delicious and also a very large portion.  For sides we had a green salad.  How good can a salad be?  This one was cold and fresh and delicious.  The refried beans were authentic (I'm just not a huge fan) and the fried tostones were delicious (big plantain chips).  The whole experience was a delight.  The prices are not fast-food tex-mex.  But that's a good thing.  This is a moderately upscale venue with delicious food and incredibly friendly service.  We will be back again and again.  I'd also add that the atmosphere is not overly ethnic.  What I mean is that the football game was on and this place is a totally cool spot to drop in and have a beer, some empanadas, and watch a ball game.  I say that because I think there's a real shortage in bars that look, act and feel "normal" in Astoria.  They have happy hour until 8 every night.  Another smart move in today's world when most people don't end work at 5 anymore.
