Fellow Astorians
 
With a heavy heart,I have to say from a reliable source, Chris is dead.
A personal friend of mine for 18 years. We shared a common bond, we were homeless together.
For 25 years and I for 13 years. Though I got off the streets 5 years ago. Perhaps you may have seen me, I lived under the viaduct on 33 Street. So you see we were close to one another and we spent a lot of time talking when he was in the mood.
I'll tell you some thing about the man you didn't know.You can interpret it as fact or fiction.  Either way it was told me and I believed him.
He came from a wealthy family,college educated,father was a famous Greek general and he had a wife and two kids.
He left home because something happened within the family. Whatever it was he wouldn't reveal to me and I didn't try to force it out of him. I too had a similiar situation and my only option was to live on the streets. So a combination of circumstance and choice.
On some days he acted irrational but that was to keep people away. A few might consider this as mental illness and they're partially right. When you been out here as long as we have, lack of sleep,improper diets,and danger everywhere. The mind plays tricks,hence irrational thoughts.
Other times we can hold an intelligent sane talk with everyone.
Several months ago we had coffee. "Cadillac I can't make it out here another winter."
I know what he meant I had the same feelings over the years. I wouldn't dare tell him to go to a shelter because all street dweller know how bad they are. Too risky. We rather go to jail. Crazy? No, at least in jail you knew who your enemies were, everyone. That's why we took our chances out in the elements and picked our own places to hide. Kind people giving us food, clothing etc wasn't the reason for us staying here, we felt safe.
You saw the shrine, it's the street way of rememberance. A few of you left something, said a prayer or said a kind word about him to me.
What will happen to him? I almost forgot, he served in the military too.
Being that he didn't want his family notified, his body hopefully buried in a VA cemetary. Otherwise,potters field an unmarked grave.
That's it ladies and gentlemen. Believe what you want to about Chris the man.
He was my friend and I miss him.
 
                                                 Cadillac Man

This is really horrible.  I have lived in Astoria for my whole life and I have seeen Chris under the train on 31st for quite a few of those years. I never saw the man bother anyone and he always seemed like a nice guy. However, I always got the impression that he didnt want to be bothered, so I never tried to speak to him. Probably about two months ago I decided to speak with him and found out that I was completely wrong. We had a nice conversation and he was more friendly than a lot people I come in contact with on a daily basis. He refused to let me buy him food or anything though no matter how hard I insisted. His reasoning was a lack of bathroom access and that being so used to NOT EATING, his stomach would get upset. His reason really just broke my heart. I was just honored and humbled by his willingless to speak to me. I know its strange but after having seen him for so many years he has become a part of Astoria and he will be missed. I wish I could have and would have done more for him. Let us all think of him the next time we pass by a homeless man or woman. We never know their struggle and a little help can go a long way.
