From the City Clerks website:

New York State Law requires any person who performs a Marriage Ceremony within the City of New York to register with our Manhattan office located at 141 Worth Street, New York, NY 10013.

If you are planning to wed anywhere within the five boroughs of New York City, you should ask the person who is performing your Marriage Ceremony if they have registered.

The registration requirement does not apply to Marriage Ceremonies performed anywhere else in the State of New York.

More info here: http://www.cityclerk.nyc.gov/html/marriage/officiant_reg.shtml

We were married in Nassau County, so I'm guessing "marriages within the city" apply to people who *live* in the five boroughs? Hmmm......! Looks like outside of the five boroughs, the officiant need NOT be registered with the city.....

State requirements here: http://www.health.state.ny.us/nysdoh/consumer/vr/married.htm

I wish I had an answer for you. I am a pastor in Suffolk county - but not out in Montauk, but am ordained as a Lutheran.  I am moving to Trinity in LIC/Astoria in early May.  In order to perform a marriage in the 5 boros I had to officially register with the NYC Marriage Bureau which is VERY strict.  But for out here in Suffolk?  On the marriage license form, which you or your friends would have to get from a municipality, the clerk of the village/area will sign, they will sign, and there is a place for witnesses to sign.  The officiated clergy/ or even perhaps a Justice of the Peace (or in Florida, even a Notary public could perform as long as they put there official license number on it) had to sign.

When I have signed marriage certificates in the 8 1/2 yrs I have been here in Suffolk - doing both weddings at my church and at catering halls, etc., I have to indicate where I am the pastor, what village and church (even if it is NOT performed at a church) and what village/county, etc the wedding actually took place.

Do NOT have the couple get a license in the 5 boros unless the person doing it is registered with the city.

Hope this is not too confusing.
