IANAL, but I don't believe that under NY law you have to give consent for a property manager to show the apartment. I think the property manager/super only has a right to enter over your objections in the case of urgent maintenance problems (plumbing leak, etc.) I think you can refuse permission, but expect more trouble getting your deposit back...!

This said, it's not a big deal. The realtor's not gonna steal your CDs. Just make sure they give you 24 hours notice before showing so you can pick your drug paraphernalia off the floor. It costs a buck or two to duplicate most keys, it's legal to do so, and it's certainly worth having a spare or two anyway... (The super doesn't have a key?? What's up with that?)

I have, several times.

I think there are 2 kinds of landlords, those that want to clean up and renovate the apartment before renting it again, and those who want to have another tenant moving in the day after you leave.

Yeah, and cultures are totally different in different places. In midwestern college towns, almost all rents turn over on the same day (August 15th), and people frequently sign leases six months before moving in. In that situation, property managers have to have access to the property to show it, and in fact the standard leases (at least in Champaign-Urbana, IL) say that they have the right to do so with 24 hours notice. I don't think such a clause would be legal in NYC's very tenant-friendly legal structure...
