Update on Taxi Hearing...

So my service refusal happened way back in June 2010. My hearing was today, and although I don't know what the verdict is yet, I can say that it went smoothly.

[I had hailed a cab back in June in midtown west; got in, closed the door, and asked to be taken to astoria- give him my address. He refused, said he'd take me to 59th and Lex but not to my address. I said that wasn't fair and he then started cursing at me...I got his medallion and got out and left.]

Anyways, the advocate for TLC was really nice and we showed using the GPS records and his fare records that the cabbie was indeed heading uptown, and had plenty of time to drop his last fare off, refuse me service, and then continue. The cabbie denied it all, said he never picked me up, doesn't know what I'm talking about, etc.

What I don't understand, forgive me for being naive, but how can someone who is sworn in  just freaking lie like that in front of a judge... I can't fathom how that is possible. He event embellished and went on and on about how he never would ever refuse service, etc. Blah blah blah. I really hope they find in my favor because it is so obvious from the GPS records, the trip records, etc. that the guy did it. Anyways... I will repost with my verdict. I am glad I stood up for the people of outer boroughs. We can't be discriminated against! :)

I had an incident about two years back where a cabbie picked up my out-of-town parents at La Guardia and promptly turned off the meter when they gave him the Astoria destination (my apartment). My parents, not knowing any better, didn't speak up at this. He then proceeded to go in the opposite direction they requested, toward Flushing, pretending not to understand my mother asking him to go the opposite direction several times. (When she called me on her cell phone, she said she could see a giant metal globe. Um, yikes!)

I listened to the conversation in the car as the cabbie continued to repeatedly ignore their directions to turn around. He took them down into Brooklyn and proceeded to go in circles for a bit before they finally managed to get him back up to Astoria. Mom was reading street signs to me as I followed nervously on Google maps from my apartment.  She read off the medallion number to me in code, as I was starting to become very concerned by this point and wanted to make sure I had it if things got worse.

Upon finally reaching my apartment, the driver asked for $45, since remember, no meter. They refused and gave him $20 (which was even then a little high for what the ride should have cost, but they wanted to get rid of him.) He argued that it wouldn't cover his gas, but at least they stood their ground on that one - it more than covered the gas for the trip they'd requested in the first place.

Since I'd written down the medallion number, I talked them into reporting it. The TLC allowed my mother to testify over the phone from her out-of-state home. And her story was similar to the one above - the driver claimed it hadn't happened, but his log and GPS suggested otherwise and matched her story.

The driver was fined and issued points.

What it all boils down to is - points don't matter much in moderation, and the fines are small. It's when these guys get reported and get multiple points that they'll stop doing these kinds of things. They wouldn't do it if they didn't get away with it most of the time. So report them! Thanks to everyone who has.

UPDATE:

Just got my hearing verdict in the mail. They found him guilty of refusal of service and being discourteous. They fined him $150 and $200, respectively. That's a lot of money for anyone in this economy and I hope they will think twice before refusing to go a few measly paces across the QB to take a passenger home. What a stupid waste of everyone's time when a five minute cab ride could have prevented this.

As an aside, for anyone who ever gets refused service, I would advise to use your camera phone if possible, or punch in the medallion number. All you need is the # but it also helps seemingly to have the guy's name if at all possible. Make sure you get IN the cab before stating your destination.

Probably good news to almost everyone on this thread:

http://gothamist.com/2011/02/24/higher_fines_coming_for_hacks_who_r.php

Higher Fines Coming For Hacks Who Refuse Fares

Right now a driver convicted of a refusal gets a fine of $200-$350 for their first offense, $350-500 for the second (with a possible 30-day suspension) and if they get three offenses within 36 months they flat out lose their license.

Under a plan the TLC is proposing, which will need to be approved by the City Council, a first offense will soon cost $500 and a second will cost $750 with a 30-day suspension if it is the second offense in 24 months. The penalty for a third offense would remain the same. We think this is a great idea.
