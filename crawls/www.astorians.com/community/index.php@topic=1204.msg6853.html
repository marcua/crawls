As it's your birthday I think you shouldn't have to cook.  But maybe you enjoy cooking (I do enjoy cooking but not enough to do it on my birthday) so I shouldn't jump to conclusions.

Anyway.  Soup.  I'm a big soup fan, especially at this time of year.

If you have any surplus chicken, you can always toss it in the pot with some carrots, celery, onions, parsnips and dill.  Or a nice lentil soup would--no.  scratch that.  You are not allowed to stand there cleaning legumes on your birthday.  How about...OK, this is not to everyone's taste but what about picking up a couple cans of baby oysters, some light cream, fresh garlic, old bay, cayenne pepper and a little parsley or cilantro, cook up a nice oyster stew?

If you don't want to go the soup route, perhaps a fish course.  Maybe some pickled herring--that's easy.  Does it go with roasted chicken?  Probably not, but I don't know what goes with what.

Oh, we're overlooking the obvious here--your one extra thing could be dessert.

happy birthday again.

If you do not have the terra cotta dont buy it.   sounds like you need plates and basics anyway.  ikea is good for that.

two great ways to roast garlic:
1. wrap it in foil with some good oil, salt and herbs as options.
2. the way we do it at the kitchen is to peel tons of cloves, put them in a baking dish or overn proof container, cover with good oil and bake till lightly browned, soft and fragrant.  it goes into all my dishes.
