The building never advertised their amazing appliances and fixtures. The building initially opened up for purchase, not rentals. As a purchase building, home owners usually, for the most part want to personalize their space. Not every home owner wants the same counter top or cabinet wood color or bathroom tile. The building decided to give us just the basics, then offer the space at a low purchase price and when coupled with the amount of money to spend on the fixtures of my choice, I still come under what other buildings are charging up front to provide its buyer with granite counter tops, marble tiles and stainless steel appliances. I don't want a building to dictate my choices for me. I just want my mortgage to be low overall. In LIC, the new condos going up, a two bedroom with amazing fixtures is going for 750K. I can get a THREE bedroom here for less than that. It's not a bad message, just a basic bare bones one.

I took a look at the smaller apartments, one bedrooms just out of curiosity. I guess I was trying to live House Hunters in person. Really cute with nice sized bathrooms, same sized kitchen as the two bedrooms and it depended on the unit that dictated the size of the living and bed rooms. But there are still plenty available, but I see people coming everday to look. As for the two bedrooms, the one for 2050, both bedrooms are a nice size. In my sons room I have a twin sized bed, a desk and a wall unit with his television with still some room to play in. Hey, maybe not as big as in a house in the suburbs, but very comfortable. I hate feeling claustophobic and enclosed, and I don't feel that way in my unit.

Yes, I find parking after 9PM. Maybe not right in front of the building but definitely along the park.
