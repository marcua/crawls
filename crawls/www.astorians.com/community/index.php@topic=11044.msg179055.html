The landlord has the right to enter the premises if there is an immediate danger of damage/danger to property or life.  If there is a water leak coming down, smell of gas or something similar.  Check what your lease says...some will state that if there is a gas/electric meter or some sort of main valves the landlord has the right to enter the premise to address the issue pertaining the the specific task at hand.  Other than those handful of situations the landlord would be a moron to enter the tenants premises with no valid reason.  I have never ever entered my tenants premises and my lease specifically gives me the right to do so for a number of reasons, including to allow the exterminator if they fail to list themselves once per month.        :lol:
