I can't believe I missed of these posts -

uhm guys/gals - while liter is annoying, I don't think I'd go confronting anyone on it. Or if you're going to insist on doing that, do it in a non-hostile way. Yelling 'trash can' at someone is not the best approach. If they are a crazy old native like me (not that I litter mind you...), old instincts might kick in and yeah, you might get hit.

You have to choose your battles. Do you want to go to a hospital because someone littered? Just saying...

If you approach someone nicely about it, you'd probably get a better response... and even if not immediately, that person will probably think twice and not do it again later on, which is a good goal...


It reminds of one day, this guy gets on the N with a boombox. he's chaging stations and being annoying by having it on in the first place. some wanna be hero girl steps up to him reaching at his radio trying to turn it off. soon enough the guy is standing their yelling at her and I'm thinking great, I'm going to have to save this dumbass? Thankfully the guy got off the train at his stop still yelling and what not. Lots of bark and no bite. I suspect he just got out of jail and didn't realize people don't like people with boom boxes on the train anymore (not that anyone ever did).

Ya got to pick your fights... and your approach... and appreciate the fact that in a city of 8 million, there are going to be all kinds, even the occasional literbug.



Anyway. I actually paid attention walking home yesterday, and I would say 95% of the liter seemed to be from these retail places that dump menus and flyers on doorsteps. Not a scientific survey by any means, but I would say these stores are a large source for rubbish and should be acted upon in some way. I just wish I had the time to go down a few streets, fill up a car truck full of these flyers, and dump them on their door. actually, maybe a wheelbarrel... i don't want to be accused of dumping and have my car impounded (which they do on the off chance you're caught dumping).

I mean damn, if i want a coupon, i'll look throught the flyer in the store. if i want a menu, i'll stop and get it on the way home... wish these businesses would understand that and save themselves some printing costs...
