Ok, I really had high hopes for this place, especially since I live a block away, and I loved just sitting there and having a beer or a glass of wine back when it was Santorini.  Its a great corner and the new owners have really spent a good deal of money renovating the place.  It looks great.  Also, the owners are great people.  They make you feel like you are family, that being said, I've now been there two times with my wife, and two times for drinks with friends.  My verdict:  Its a good place to have a drink, due to the nice owners and wait staff, but don't waste your money on the overpriced and underwhelming dishes.

As you can tell, I really wanted them to succeed, I kept giving them another chance, hoping that the first time, and then the second time, was just a goof...but it wasn't.  The food is bland.  Not bad, just bland.  But the food should be better given the prices.

The first time I was there I had the steak fajitas.  First off: the platter wasn't sizzling, the steak strips looked and tasted warmed over, and the peppers and onions were drenched in oil.  Taste: bland.  The second time I was there I had the lunch special pork chop and the "pre-columbian soup".  The soup was milky, and not creamy, and just bland and the pork chop was salty, not flavorful, just salty and small.

Its a shame.  Such a great corner, such a great decorating job, such nice people you just want them to succeed, but I cannot tell a lie.  The food isn't worth it.  This is definitely NOT authentic Mexican food.

We went just last night for the second time.  And brought friends.  We all loved it and had a great time.  This time we all enjoyed a large number of the Margaritas, which were excellent.  I think it's a great meal for the price.  The atmosphere is very nice.  I've never seen more varieties of artificial stone in one place in my life.  But somehow it works here without looking ridiculous.  The food may not be authentic Mexican.  And it may not be of 5 star caliber.  But that's not what they're trying to do.  It's a casual local place.  In my opinion the prices are just moderate.  If not low for the quality and quantity of food.  I think if you go in expecting a very good meal at a fair price, you'll love it.  If you expect to be overwhelmed by gourmet cuisine and think you're going to have a very ethnic experience, then you'll be disappointed.  We will be back again and again.  The owners and staff also make it a very pleasant experience.  The service can be a little off but it's more due to a slight language barrier and maybe an unfamiliarity with certain dining practices.  But we found it more endearing than offensive.  The bottom line is that they bend over backwards to please the customer.  I hope they make it.

Luna de Juarez
« Reply #43 on: April 03, 2009, 10:47:26 PM »
My wife and I tried Luna de Juarez tonight based on so many recommendations that we've gotten both from this board and from others.  We walked in Friday night at 9:00PM.

There were some redeeming qualities about it but overall, it plain old sucked.  This is my third review that I've posted now and I hate to be such a downer, but I simply can't endorse a restaurant that belongs on Ramsay's Kitchen Nightmares.  It's that bad.

So here we go from the beginning.

From the moment we walked in, there was a live band.  I immediately got excited until I realized that the band was awful.  Really, it was one member of the band ruining the performance for all, namely, the trumpeter.  He was horrible.  Not only did he not realize that he couldn't play a note with any kind of purity and he couldn't sustain a note, but he tried to do solos all night and routinely tried to "trump" his band for volume.  Everyone in the restaurant was squinting and wincing when he played.  And boy did he play a lot.  So the mood was bad.  I'm not blaming this on the restaurant per say, but it was BAD.

Next, the service.  They asked us if we wanted a drink.  I said, "Diet coke, please."  My wife went with a water.  After tasting my drink, I realized that I got a Jack and Coke instead.  Because I didn't want to get charged for the alcohol, I told the waiter that I had ordered a Diet Coke but got an alcoholic Jack and Coke.  He apologized and said he'd be right back.  Sure enough, he showed up 10 minutes later and brought me...what do you know...a Jack and Coke.  I tasted it again and told him it wasn't right.  I wanted a DIET COKE.  He said he messed up and would be right back.  He came back later with a JACK AND COKE again.  So I thought it was fate that I'd be drinking a Jack and Coke instead.  And I did and ended up paying for it.

Now onto the most important part: food.  My wife ordered their "famous" steak fajitas and I ordered the beef enchiladas with verde sauce, rice and beans.  The waiter told me that the verde sauce was a great choice for spicy food lovers, which I am, so I was excited.  The order took 40 minutes to get to the table.  Now ordinarily I wouldn't complain about 40 minutes for food, but we were one of only two tables that ordered food.  It was kind of funny because based on body language both my table and the other table were wondering why the food was taking so long.  Oh well.  When the food finally arrived, my wife looked horrified.  Her steak fajitas ($17.95) were completely overcooked.  The meat had shriveled along with the onions and peppers.  They almost looked dehydrated if you could get past the fact that most of it was burned.  Her side of pico de gallo was shriveled as well, most likely due to refrigerator burn.  You could instantly tell it was probably made days ago with both the tomato and onions translucent and having no juices.  On the other hand, my enchiladas ($13.95) were plump with cheese and verde sauce poured all over.  The rice and beans looked "out of the can," but it didn't matter because the enchiladas looked good.  Until I ate them.  The beef inside was completely dry to the bone, like jerky dry.  Also, the sauce was as mild as milk, there was no spice in it whatsoever.  I asked my wife to try mine to see if it was more edible than her burnt, dehydrated food.  The sad part was that she wasn't sure which one she preferred more.  I flagged the waiter down and asked for some hot sauce.  He brought me tomato water.  Again, no spice or taste whatsoever.  The waiter later swung by and told me that he was going to bring me a side of sweet plantains.  Oh great, I thought, a complimentary dish to win over customers.  At least service was picking up points.  As it turns out, the sweet plantains arrived after we were done eating.  At least they were decent although lacked crispiness.  I was willing to overlook that.  Until I found out that he charged me for it.

By the end, I paid $50 for quite possibly the worst Mexican food of my life.  I highly prefer Chipotle over what I ate.  On top of that, I think I got a mild jaw sprain from wincing so much due to the HORRENDOUS trumpet playing.  When the meal was done, my wife got the check as fast as she could and we jetted out of there ASAP.

Food: D
Portion Size: B
Value: F
Would I Recommend?  NO WAY.  Service, atmosphere, and food were very very unsatisfactory.  Plus I got charged for things I didn't even order, not just once but twice.  But even then, I can overlook everything as long as the food was good.  But it clearly wasn't.  I felt as if perhaps the head chef had stepped out and instead let the kitchen porter/dishwasher cook instead.  And that would have been fine if he even half knew how to cook.  Quite simply, this was one of the top 3 worst restaurant experiences in my life.  Sorry, but I have to tell it like it is.
