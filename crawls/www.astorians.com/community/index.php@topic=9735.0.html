Am I Really Priced Out of The Neighborhood?
« on: March 05, 2008, 09:41:38 PM »
In a few months I'll be leaving my shared 2-bedroom apartment to get my own place. I live on Steinway near 36th Ave.--and I love the neighborhood. Good people everywhere (not enough green space--but I can live with it).

I'm willing to pay up to $1300 for a decent studio/one bedroom. I'd love to stay between Broadway and 36th Ave. in the vicinity of Steinway. I've talked to a few brokers and they all seemed to think I'll never find anything. Do I need to find some new brokers? I know I'm being pretty specific, but getting my own place (after 6 yrs. of living with someone) is enough drama. I want to keep using the same laundromat! I want to be close to Valverde!

Am I crazy? Is this really as impossible as the brokers are making it out to be??

Any advice at all would be appreciated. . .

Each time I looked for an apartment in Astoria, I always tryed to get an apartment with exclusive use of a backyard.  Each time, brokers seemed to be working against me.  I can't tell you how many times I was taken to a place which did not have what I was looking for.  They just kept telling me that an exclusive backyard in Astoria was impossible.  All it was is that they didn't have what I was looking for in their rolls, and they would just take a shot at unloading something else.  In the end, I did manage to snare places with backyards.  So it might be difficult, but it is not impossible if you do the snooping on your own.  I do not think 1300 for what you are looking for is unreasonable at all.

Good luck!

I know you have a specific area that you are targeting, which might make it a little more difficult, but you are not being unreasonable as far as price goes.

When I was looking for something under $1,000 in western Queens, I was told by a couple brokers that it was impossible.  A few tried to show me places that were way over $1,000, hoping that I would like the place so much that I would somehow find a way to afford it.  In the end, I went to Alexiou Century 21 at 31-23 23rd Avenue (gratuitous endorsement) and met with George who showed me a few places that were in my budget.  

Depending on the amount of time, patience and perseverance you have, I really believe it's possible.

Good luck!
