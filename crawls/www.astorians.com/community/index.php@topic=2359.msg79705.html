Sorry, Alexa, I guess I interpreted your comment, "I will NOT give up my seat to someone that has a baby carriage" incorrectly.

I think we can all agree (I hope) that it really comes down to just plain being considerate.  I try to be as considerate as possible when I have the stroller on the subway.  I make every attempt possible to get a seat that's out of the way and pull the stroller in to me as closely as possible. 

If someone has their stroller blocking the doorway and have the opportunity to move out of the way, they should.  If they don't, then they suck, fair enough.  But if there's no room for them to move on a crowded subway, they don't have a choice.  As another passenger, at that point, I would give them my seat, not so much so that they can sit, but so they can get out of everyone else's way.  I've had people shove past me to get the two person seats at the ends of the N cars when there are plenty of other seats and that's the best place to get the stroller out of the way.  It takes all kinds, I guess...
