My dad took me to see Jaws at the Astoria. He loved that theatre because he could sit and
smoke there to his heart's content.

Anyway, I was terrified - think I was about 8 or 9. My dad had dozed off with a cigarette still hanging out
of the side of mouth. I kept fidgeting and poking him because I was scared. I finally poked him really hard
and he woke up to give me this brilliant explanation:

"It's all mechanical see? The shark's a machine, he's not real! Look at him—see the hinges there
where his top and bottom teeth meet? What's wrong with you - you're a smart kid.
You should have figured that out by now..."

I calmed down after that - don't remember if I really accepted that completely. My dad lit up another
cigarette and promptly dozed off again until Roy Scheider got him at the end.
