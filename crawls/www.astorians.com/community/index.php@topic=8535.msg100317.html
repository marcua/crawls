Street Noise - Rant and Any solutions?
« on: November 05, 2007, 03:04:49 PM »
I live on 34th Street and we have always gotten our fair share of street traffic from those who take it instead of 31st Street off the Triboro.  I have lived in the same building, facing the same street for almost 6 years and have noticed that this year the horns and street noise is getting ridiculous.

People honk at garbage and recycling trucks in the morning - so if you manage to sleep through the noise of the pick ups you get woken up by the honking. People don't really think their honking will make the garbage truck move do they? And what about the person who use to honk at 6:15am for their pickup...get a clue and call the person you are picking up or ring the buzzer for them! I guess that might wake up other people in the apartment, which would be really inconsiderate - let's just wake up the entire neighborhood instead!  I know there will always be noise in NY - but do we really need horns waking us up every morning??

I know there are areas in Manhattan where they have the signs posted regarding the fines for unnecessary honking. What would it take to get these signs in Astoria as reminders of the potential fines and more importantly to have noise ordinances enforced in Astoria?
