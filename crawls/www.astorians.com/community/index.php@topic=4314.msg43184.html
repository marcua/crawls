I frequently make jokes about the potential for having the Smoke Police who will ring your doorbell and give you a ticket for smoking in your own home.

I'd be against that because it would infringe on your personal freedom to do what you want in your own home. But banning companies from selling trans-fats to children as fast food is a restriction of a company's rights not yours. You can still get trans-fats and eat them or feed them to your kids all you want, companies just can't profit off killing you using this method. I see no problem with banning a poison from being manufactured in mass, and sold as "food" when it really isn't food and the are are no health benefits. If you want to use trans-fats you are still well within the law to do so. There is a huge difference.

Let's not confuse the government's right to regulate corporations, with individual freedom. Next you'll be telling me "but a corporation IS an individual." I'd be against it if they made trans-fats a controlled substance, (i.e. illegal for individuals to posses), and I'd agree with you were that the case, but the whole argument (that I hear a lot of people using) against the ban being an infringement on individual freedom is a real stretch IMHO.

I completely support this ban - for once we have elected officials that are standing up against corporations who are making a profit while killing people. Corporations always spin regulations like this into "infringement on personal freedom", but I don't buy it. I'd gladly support the same type of "ban" on HFCS as well for the record.

The reason why HFCS is so ubiquitous is because (a) American corn growers are subsidized so there's too much corn on the market, making the cost of making HFCS extremely low, and (b) American sugar growers are protected by tarriffs so there's less sugar on the market than there would otherwise be, making sugar in the US something like twice as expensive as in most of the rest of the world. Fix these two annoying policies, and you'd see a lot more sugar in soft drinks and a lot less HFCS in processed foods.

I'd much rather see that done than any sort of ban on HFCS. It's bad for you, yes, but mostly because it's cheap...
