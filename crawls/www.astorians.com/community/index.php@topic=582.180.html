(Bear in mind that I mean the following as constructive criticism) FearNLoathing, if you're looking to meet women, perhaps you shouldn't link to articles you've written that highlight sexist, outdated, stereotyped views of men and women and the interactions between them. Not all women act the way you describe, and the sooner you realize that and stop behaving as such, the sooner your dating prospects should dramatically increase. Those articles don't present you in a very flattering light, and they might give someone the wrong impression about you.

As for the general question of what women look for in a guy (based on personal experience): yes, machismo is a huge turnoff, as is any sign of a huge ego that constantly needs boosting/stroking. The cocky and confident act gets annoying, if not downright creepy, real fast. No one wants a guy who comes on too strong and feels the need to assert control all the time. A guy should not be narcissistic or spend too much time on his appearance or be blatantly insecure, because there's nothing less sexy than a guy who feels the need to overcompensate.

A guy should be considerate and respectful, which in my book means he treats the woman as an equal rather than setting her on a pedestal. He respects her opinions and listens to her concerns, and sees her as an individual rather than treating her simply as part of the monolithic group known as "female". He doesn't have a problem with her being independent and making her own decisions. He has some interest in and pays attention to current events and politics, and can hold an intelligent conversation (and is at least open-minded about these views, if not like-minded). He enjoys going outdoors, trying new things, and being physically active, instead of sitting on the couch all day. Sense of humor and a passion about some kind of interest (such as history, music, or computers) is a huge plus. Also, he can still be living at home but he needs to have motivation in life--at least some idea of what he wants to do--and if he's not happy with his job, then at least he should have some plan for his future.
