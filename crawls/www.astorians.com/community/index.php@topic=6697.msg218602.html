I grew up in the area of Old Astoria Village and have played in the tunnels that you mention.  I had friends who lived in the mini-mansions by the water - in the basement there was a section w/ a wooden floor w/ a hatch, which opened up to stairs which led to the sub-basement then the tunnel network.  We had a GREAT time exploring the underground, now mostly gone (I assume).  The Astoria Historical Society denounces such history in the area.
There was also two cemeteries known as Astoria Village Cemetery #1 & #2 in the area we are talking about - now 14th street, 14th place, 18th street & part of 25th rd from what I was told.  Supposedly the graves were moved, but unsure.  I was raised to ALWAYS say a prayer to a Great, great, great uncle who was buried on 25th rd - to this day I say a prayer when walking down that road.

Most of the entrances (within) the houses have been sealed up years ago by the previous owners. Most new owners of the homes have no clue they exist.  I was told that this was done bec. the tunnels allowed easy access to all the homes via the underground and that robberies where high, nevermind the rats.
If you speak to the old time residents of that area they will tell you that the tunnels do exist.  I would imagine that the original network of tunnels no longer exist, but the ones that do would lead to dead ends - due to all the construction/tear-downs of the grand homes that once belonged to Old Astoria Village.

The so called "tunnel" that everyone is talking about was what we use to call the "a**hole". It used to dump raw sewerage into the east river untill they closed it off. There were two huge concrete doors that opened with chains at high tide and raw waste would flow out into the east river. That is why there used to be a god awful smell at low tide in that area. I lived across the street from the tennis courst from 1961 til I got married in 1992. They also closed off Shore Blvd. some time in the early 80's to vehicular traffic and the area was so much nicer. Only foot and bike traffic sere allowed on shore Blvd. The area was so much quiter in the summer time.
