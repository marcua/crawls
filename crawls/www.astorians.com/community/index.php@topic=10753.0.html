We've been pretty lucky. Our jobs are secure -- unfortunately, in an economy like this, crime goes up and people are getting evicted more and more (I do defense, my gf does tenant-side landlord/tenant law). Personally, I'd rather the economy be better.

Gas-wise, I figure it's costing me about an extra $40/month, or an extra $10/week to fill up. We desperately need a new car (especially now that my stupid check engine light keeps coming on), but my car gets great gas mileage so I'm hesitant to trade it in. With the economy as it is, I'm also hesitant to take on a car payment and increased insurance costs.

(Un)fortunately, the bulk of my income goes to my student loans and those have actually gone down with the drop in interest rates. I still won't have them paid off until I'm well into my 60s at this rate, but a little bit of relief is nice.

The one place I can see the economy affecting us is, as others have mentioned, with travel plans. I'm not a big fan of going on vacation to begin with (leftover scars from childhood family vacations... ), but with the cost of airfare these days, I don't see myself going anywhere for fun anytime soon.
