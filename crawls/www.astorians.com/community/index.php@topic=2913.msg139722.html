Lurker, first-time poster here. Decided that I should reply since I used the criticisms and advice from this thread to fuel my broker search. I'm moving back to NY with my husband and cat, and have settled on Astoria. We went up a couple of weeks ago and met two brokers (saw four apartments). Since returning home and haunting this thread, we've changed our minds and are working with Mary from ReMax. Here's our story:

NY Finest Real Estate (Forest Hills) - Cute office, cute lady. Bad attitude and no change. We had to supply our own quarters to pay for her daily rent-a-car to park. Tacky in my out-of-state opinion. She introduced us to Lionel of J C Management or Coco Realty. That's about the only good thing she did. She advertises herself as a realtor, but operates like a broker - being that her main source of income is Craig's List.  :-(

J C Management (31st Ave Astoria) - Nice office, sarcastic employees but fun atmosphere. We are working with Lionel who seems fairly reputable with just about everyone in Astoria. So far has been pleasant to work with, but I'm getting tired of hearing sentences with the word "tomorrow" in it. We have to move by July 1 and the sands of time are flowing . . . I got nervous and fell to the mercy of this thread and Mary of ReMax.

ReMax (Astoria) - Seems to know her stuff. Very nice and willing to work with out-of-staters, which is a blessing since I'd lose my lunch if I had to fork over more money for another rape date with LaGuardia.

Will post an update once I have one. Waiting on promised email, which Lionel told us we'd get "tomorrow".  :x

I've read some comments about Metropolis - that people have put money down, etc, and then didn't get the apartment. As of now, I recommend Angela highly. 9.5 out of 10.

Historyteacher, it depends on your price range if you'll be able to afford the places Mary has to offer. My top was $1650 and she was telling me all apartments with elevator, laundry and live-in super were 1800+. Well, that wasn't true because I had just looked at a place for $1550 with all of that and then some. We wanted something around 30th, close to trains and she had mostly Ditmars. It's possible we were a mismatch. I would make more than just one appointment. I made that mistake when I came up mid-June. You really really need to be there to place the deposit and see the place you're signing off on for a year of your life. I had to rely on family and while it's not everything I had hoped for, it was well under my price range and in an awesome location. Good luck! Oh, and make sure you have all the proper documents with you! Ask each broker what's required.
