Grew up on 37th Street (right across the street from Most Precious Blood - took my first communion there).

Used to go there during summer break, at that time it was FREE, you could go to the gym and they had tables set up to do different things and they used to keep the play area open outside and the kids played street hockey...those were the days (mid 60's - early 70's).

The moved to Florida in 1973 (hated it).

And in 2000 the new Millinium...blew me back to Astoria and I've been here ever since (will be 7 years in May).

Will I ever leave Astoria...I never left Astoria, even when I didn't live here. (Awwwwwwwww)

Since coming back I lived for 5 years around 30th Ave. and 14th Street (WOW THAT AREA CHANGED!  I remember the Italian Festival on 14th Street -- that's long gone).

For the past couple of years I've been living on Ditmars right by the park.

I will say one thing, Astoria has changed.  I do miss the way it was, it was like living in a small village/town.  People used to sit on their doorsteps during the summer, everyone knew everyone.  All the parents watched out for everyone's kids.

It's still a great place to live but those of you that were around way back when...you know what I'm talking about
