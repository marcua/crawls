Digital TV vs Amtrak trains
« on: October 30, 2008, 12:24:53 PM »
I visited my mother (33 Street near 23 Road) recently and bought her a Sony digital LCD TV that's compatible with the simple Sony remote that she's used to using.  Every time the electric Amtrak train or the diesel freight trains go by near 23 Avenue, the TV loses signal and goes blank. The picture returns once the trains are down the line.  This TV is hooked up to an antenna on the roof.  I also connected her nondigital TV, which uses an indoor antenna, to a converter box; this TV pixellates when the trains go by.  I dropped by the RadioShack on 31 Street and asked them if any customer had mentioned this, but none had.  Has anyone here experienced this?  (Next time I visit, I'm thinking of replacing the old rooftop antenna with a directional one and using quad-shielded coax, but what to do about the indoor set?)  Ain't progress grand?

Some of the digital signals are not at full strength, particularly the PBS stations...they won't go to full power until the switchover, to save money.

And definitely having a rooftop antenna does not guarantee good reception of the DTV channels, as most signals are in the UHF band, whereas many older rooftop antennas are VHF-only (channels 2-13) and will not improve performance for the UHF stations as a result.  In most cases, any simple set of rabbit ears from a store like Radio Shack will do the trick though, if you're not interested or able to install a new VHF-UHF rooftop antenna.
