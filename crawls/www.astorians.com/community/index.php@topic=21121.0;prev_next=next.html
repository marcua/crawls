We just had another delicious meal from this place yesterday, but this time we ate in for a change.  If you live in this part of town and like really good Spanish food (Not Latin American but of Spain), then you have to try this.  Their Garlic Soup is delicious and not exactly like every other Spanish restaurant.  The poached egg they add puts it over the top.  The hot octopus appetizer is very good with olive oil and Spanish seasonings served on a wooden platter.  The beef and veal meatballs in a saffron sauce are really amazing for meat lovers.  The chicken croquets are extremely rich and more cheese than chicken.  Very addictive.  There were three of us.  One had the Grilled Sirloin Steak with Peppercorn sauce.  It was much nicer than the description.  A huge strip steak arrived in a dark, flavorful sauce that was not heavy at all.  He ordered it medium rare and it was spot on perfect like at a fine steakhouse.  Another had the codfish in sweet pepper sauce.  A huge portion and a bit boney.  Not the favorite on the table but not exactly bad either.  The other dish ordered was the Pork Adobo.  Moist and marinated earlier in some hot peppers.  Then the garlicy adobo sauce toned it down but added intense flavor.  A winner.  The chef is terrific in this place.  The servers are so happy to help and really very cheery and polite.  It has some "off the beaten path" rough edges.  Like music that's inappropraite for this level of food and blasting loud (club/house/techno).  But when we told them it was too much, they responded immediately and happily to tone it down.  There is a menu item that says Chicken with Rat.  We pointed it out and they were stunned.  It was shortened for ratatouille but no one had noticed.  But for a place with this kind of amazing food in a part of town lacking great restaurants (Bear is nearby but that's it), you can easily forgive these things.  They have beer, wine and liquor.  And some very delicious sounding deserts but we declined.
