Well, first of all, I have been there only once, so this is probably more impression than fact.

The place is primarily take-out: it's narrow and has maybe a short ledge along one side wall where you could stand and eat.  It's dark brick and wood industrial looking, like a blacksmith's shop or maybe an 1850 general store. One fellow was working the counter, and taking pies out of the oven. Two or three others were working in back doing prep work & assembling pies.  There were 4 items listed on the chalkboard menu - Margherita, Artichoke + Spinach, Crab, and Sicilian. I don't know if they do customs, or if you have to choose whatever is cooking today.

I got an Artichoke for me, and a Sicilian for my gf.  The fellow working the counter had a great smile, that seemed to say, "Welcome back, or if it's your first time, welcome back next time in advance."

The artichoke is a large slice. The crust is thick, with a wonderful crunchy bottom that can support the whole slice easily. The word rustic comes to mind.  The topping is, I think,  a couple of layers.. a savory creamy cheese layer the consistency of thick pudding, the artichoke & spinach bits, and cheese on top. It's a lovely light greenish yellow color. I saw someone's photo of a slice on flickr but it didn't do the thing justice so I won't bother to link. The flavors blend really well.  One online reviewer compared it to cream of asparagus soup, but I don't think that's quite enough: the pie is a lot richer than any soup I have had, and the variety of textures adds a lot. And artichoke + spinach does not precisely equal asparagus by my algebra.

The Sicilian square was beautiful -- slightly misshapen with rich blood-red sauce. Considerably smaller than the other slice. I had only a tiny taste of it, out of fear that if I had more I would be obligated to share mine.

I'm sure the place isn't for everybody. Some of the online reviewers seem to be bothered by the looks of the kitchen. If indeed the choices are limited to the pies on the board on a given day, it's probably not a good place to take a whiner.  And theres the matter of the line - though we stopped early to mid-afternoon of a Saturday and there was no significant wait.

I love really rich food, and I eat too much.. kind of a pig really.. yet I didn't finish the whole slice at once. Thus, I can report that it reheats just fine. I use a gets-hot-in-the-microwave "browning" platter to reheat pizza.. nuke the dish for a couple of minutes to get it good and hot, then put a slice on it and go for another minute or two. It keeps the bottom nice and crisp, and manages to get the slice hot before the uwaves turn the crust to rubber.

Here is a link to the chowhound thread about the place. Those messages are 6 months old, so maybe some of the kinks they note have been worked out.
