Host a Party in Astoria -- From MoveOn.org
« on: July 05, 2005, 02:36:43 PM »
Some of you have probably already gotten this email from MoveOn.org but thought I'd pass it on for those who haven't seen it.

President Bush is under intense pressure to appoint a radically conservative Supreme Court justice. One Republican 527 group already announced that they plan to spend $18 million in support of Bush's nominee.

We're going to have to fight hard and smart to balance the right-wing mobilization and protect the court. That's why this weekend, MoveOn members will hold more than 1,000 house parties around the country where we can get together to plan our campaign.

Momentum is building, but right now, we still need more party hosts in Astoria.  Would you be willing to host a Supreme Court house party this coming weekend? It's easy, and doesn't take much time. By providing a place for MoveOn members in Astoria to get together, you can make a big contribution to this campaign. People will really appreciate it.

You can learn more about hosting and sign up right now at:

http://www.moveon.org/r?r=763&t=4&id=5744-2871443-Lcn_35gMJVFUpEZl3LYZNw&t=1

Here's how hosting works: you just need to set up a party online, and invite your friends. It only takes a minute, then we'll invite other MoveOn members to join (if you open your party to the public). We'll provide a detailed guide and agenda for your house party, walking you through the party minute-by-minute.

Even if your senator is likely to vote to defend our rights, it's still important that folks in Astoria mobilize. Winning this battle will require a national campaign that explains what this fight is really about and pushes the entire Senate to stand up to Bush if he nominates someone who's out of the mainstream. And we need to be ready to jump into action the second an extreme nominee is announced, because the following 48 hours are when the public will form most of its opinion.

If you can't host, but you want to get involved in the big Supreme Court campaign and meet MoveOn members in your neighborhood, you can find a party to attend in your area at:

http://www.moveon.org/r?r=769&t=2&search_zip=11105&id=5744-2871443-Lcn_35gMJVFUpEZl3LYZNw

Originally, we'd planned these events as a progressive movie night—a way for progressive folks to get together and meet each other in communities around the country. We'll still be watching a movie, but with Friday's news we've also got some planning to do.

The stakes are pretty high. If President Bush replaces Justice O'Connor with a conservative hard-liner, the balance of the court will shift dramatically.  That one nomination could strip millions of Americans of constitutionally guaranteed rights—among them our rights to privacy and our civil liberties.

It's up to the millions of us who care deeply about protecting rights to stop that from happening. That means mobilizing now, before a nominee is chosen, to let Bush know that an extreme nominee simply won't pass.  And it means getting ready in case he does nominate someone who would infringe on our rights.

It's all going to start this weekend, and we want you to be a part of it. Can you host a Supreme Court house party this weekend? Sign up now at:

http://www.moveon.org/r?r=763&t=4&id=5744-2871443-Lcn_35gMJVFUpEZl3LYZNw&t=3

This will be one of the biggest domestic fights of the Bush Presidency, and for us, it kicks off in earnest this weekend. See you there—it's going to be a wild ride.

Sincerely,
—Ben, Tanya, Matt, Eli and the MoveOn PAC Team
  July 5th, 2005
