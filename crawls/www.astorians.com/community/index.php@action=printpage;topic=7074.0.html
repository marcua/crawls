Mom had gone oversees to visit her parents and poor Dad was stuck overnight at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, where he worked.  They had to mobilize because they were afraid of looters.  My Dad told me that it was such an eerie night.  He had to walk the miles of hallways with a flashlight all night long.  The Egyptian Galleries were especially scary.  My brother and I had purchased lobsters that night and were planning to surprise Dad with a nice meal.  We didn't know what to do with them so we set them free in the East River.  Who knows?  Maybe they made their way back to Maine!

Title: Re: 30 years ago today - The day the lights went out in NYC 07/13/1977
Post by: T.J. Connick on July 17, 2007, 08:54:24 PM
Things went black at LaGuardia Lanes that Wednesday night.  Was due to begin the night shift at 11pm at United Parcel Service in Maspeth.  No juice, no work; radio of the old '67 Camaro reported power in Rockland county.  Made tracks.  Found lights, a cold beer, live music, and air conditioning (it had been 93 when the lights quit).  Returned with sunrise on Thursday via Harlem River Drive and Triborough; plenty of smoke from the night's hijinx clouding the Bronx. 

Not a witness to lobster liberation in the East River, but saw some twisted humans by the Hudson.  They caused a traffic jam at Palisades Parkway overlook so they could gaze through the night sky across the mighty Hudson and see...nothing.

A few years later was working in Bushwick and East New York.  Locals said that most stores folded for keeps that night.  Picture the full length of Ditmars or 30th Avenue or Broadway -- open for business before, but mostly cementblock, rusting scissor gates and busted windows thereafter.  That's what shopping was like for nearly twenty years.  Astoria had remarkable fortune to ride out the worst days, but for a lot of New Yorkers, those that were elsewhere or not yet born were to be envied.
