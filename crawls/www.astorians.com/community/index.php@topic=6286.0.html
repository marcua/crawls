Advice on Signing a Lease
« on: May 09, 2007, 05:30:41 PM »
Hey everyone. My wife and I are going to be moving from Rochester, NY to Astoria at the end of May. We found a great apartment that we are really excited about. It's in a pre-war building on the corner of 28th Ave and 33rd street. It is currently being renovated and we are going to be in town this weekend to meet with the owner and sign the lease (we already put a deposit down). The building is rent stabilized.

The lease has an "as-is" clause that states that we (the tenants) have inspected the apartment and it's condition is acceptable. Since the renovations won't be completed by this weekend I am hesitant to sign the lease with that clause in it. My fear is that they'll stop working on it and it won't be ready for our June 1st move in date.

On the other hand, I have every reason to trust that they'll finish it (they were literally working on it when we viewed it) and our Broker is a very nice lady that I feel we can trust (Avanguard realty). I'm kind of thinking that we should just sign it, so that we know the apartment is ours. We looked at like 15 apartments last weekend and this was the best one by far. Anyone have any suggestions or thoughts?

Thanks!

I would be very weary of signing a lease for an apt "as is".  The whole point of renting is that you are not responsible for repairs and the landlord actually has many responsibilities to the upkeep of the place.  By saying you will take it "as is" you are giving up you rights as a tenant.  In my opinion, keep looking.  There are plenty of places out there.

I just got ripped off this weekend from an agent who showed me a remodeled apartment that was supposed to be "similar" to the one I was supposed to be taking.  When I got the key to the apartment I was supposed to get on the day of the move itself, I was in shock.  I was shown a totally remodeled apartment, modern, updated, etc.  "My" apartment  was last remodeled 40 years ago (yes, they told me that).  The only thing similar was a new kitchen and sanded/stained floors.  Everything else....walls, bathroom, electric wiring, fixtures, etc were as they were during the Johnson administration.  Except they did throw some paint on the walls in areas. If you can imagine, each room had only one electric outlet and they all had only 2 prongs (when I complained I couldn't plug in a tv, computer, air conditioner, etc they offered to run an extension cord along the wall to a window so I could plug in a fan).

I am now fighting with them to get my first months rent and security back (they did refund the broker's fee) and am not even sure I will be able to stay in the apartment I was supposed to move out of. This was with an agent I never used before, but with an agency I have gotten an apartment from in the past and have referred many people to.  It should have been a red flag when the agent told me at the lease signing that he was also the property manager, super and lived in the building.  I figured...I can trust him, I'll see him every day.

The point is, be very careful of not seeing the actual apartment.  I've learned my lesson. I will probably have  a few days notice to put everything in my apartment into storage and move away until I can find something new.
