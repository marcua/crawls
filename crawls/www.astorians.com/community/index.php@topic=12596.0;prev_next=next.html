Friends,
  I thought that I would start a POSITIVE thread about experiences that we, Astorians, could only have encountered here in da QNZ--a thread about situations that could happen "Only in Queens".
  I'll offer up the first anecdote.
  I've been working with a student at the college the past few weeks, who was, to say the least, of a very ambiguous ethnicity.  Of course, here in Queens and at the college, "ambiguous ethnicity" is a given, since we serve the Queens population of about 180 different ethnic groups.
  The female student that I've been working with has "Latina" features (please no racial profiling comments)--black hair, brown eyes, and olive skin, but she speaks English with a pronounced Eastern European/Polish accent and uses a definite Eastern European syntax.  For example, she doesn't use definite and indefinite articles and her word order is definitely Slavic in nature.
  For a while, I thought that my ears were failing me and I wasn't discerning a Russian accent from a Polish one (both very distinct) and that she was probably Bukharin--we have many Bukharin Jews at the school.
  As I was instructing her today, our language barrier became apparent, and she admitted that part of the problem that she was having with her classes was due to her "limited English".  So, I asked her what her native tongue was to access how I could help her.
  She, simply said: "Guess!"  Of course, I had no idea.
  She later told me that her native language was Spanish, but she learned English from the Polish women that she worked with here when she first immigrated to the US.  She also told me that everyone assumes that she's Russian, which is alright by her.
  Only in Queens!  A Latina that speaks English with a Polish accent! 
  AAAHHH, you gotta love it!

-=:Astoristani:=-

  One day several years ago, I walked into the International Meat Market down on 30th Ave., took my number, and asked if they had any ham hocks.  I wanted to make a bit of white bean soup.
  The butcher behind the counter told me that they didn't have any demand for it, so they didn't carry it and politely referred me to Pathmark!
  Two weeks later, I was coming home from work, and paused before the meat market's window and was surprised to find it filled ham hocks (as it is today--anyone passing this establishment will atest to this).
  But this isn't the true Astoria/"Only in Queens" moment.  The true Astoria moment came when I purchased a ham hock and the apprentice butcher asked me how I wanted it sliced!
G-d, I love Astoria!
