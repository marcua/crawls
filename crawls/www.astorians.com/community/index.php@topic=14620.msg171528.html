Hi neighbors,

Brandon here. Our state senator, George Onorato, is out of touch.

He believes District 12 (Astoria, Sunnyside, Woodside and LIC) doesn't support gay marriage equality for New Yorkers.

I think otherwise. There's currently a gay marriage bill proposed by Gov Paterson and Oronato has said he'll vote against this historic civil rights bill.

The Democratic Party now controls the State Senate which allows for a gay marriage bill to be introduced onto the Senate floor for debate. While formerly in Republican party control, no marriage bill could have been introduced for debate.

New York Governor David Paterson publicly supports gay marriage equality as does the House of Representatives and most of the Senate.

However, the Senate is a few votes short of reaching the required number of supporters to pass the bill.


State Senator George Onorato, who represents western Queens county, is one of the senators who doesn't yet support gay marriage rights for his constituents.

Astoria alone is one of the most gay-friendly neighborhoods in all of New York City. Every voting gay and lesbian resident of Onorato's district must contacted his office and demand that he support gay marriage in New York state.

He might prove to be the missing link that will allow New York state to join the ranks of Massachusetts, Connecticut, Iowa and now Vermont




If you support gay marriage equality in New York state, call his office.

Contact State Senator George Onorato and tell him to support gay marriage equality in New York State:

Tel: (718) 545-9706

Contact me here if you own a local business and would like to sign a signature form full of local business who support marriage rights. This will be given directly to the State Senator on April 28th on Equality and Justice Day in Albany. (several gay rights grassroots orgs are going to Albany to talk directly to their representatives about marriage).

If you're on Facebook, there's a group for this cause. Please join and promote this with your friends and family:

Yours in equality,
Brandon Brock

While Senator Onorato has now declared his opposition to the marriage equality bill, I think those of us who support the bill need to keep in touch with him and his staff, on a regular basis, respectfully making our points.  We need to convince him that he has come to the wrong conclusions.  I do worry that the issue could get heated, especially with many people feeling quite passionately about the issue, but civil discourse and even polite demonstrations are more likely to make the point than more provocative actions, which I contend could be counter-productive.  Letters, calls, petitions, meetings, these are all tried-and-true methods to move elected officials on an issue and should be done.  Other tactics, including berating staff members and being less pleasant are less likely to see good results.
