Homeowners aren't allowed to block the alley. There's a document called "Declaration of the Right of Way" that should be attached to everyone's deed which restricts the owners from obstructing other owners and occupants of the block to their property, garages, parking spaces. You should call DOB, tell them your problem. Go to ACRIS website or the office in Kew Gardens to see if the Declaraton of Right of Way is attached to the deed-info is public access. And if a vehicle belonging to a resident of the block is constantly and for long periods of time blocking the alley -not just loading, unloading-you most definitely can call 311. It's a priority A complaint and someone will be sent out there that very day. Problem with that is that if the car isn't there when the inspector comes no violation can be issued. You should also talk to other residents of the block who I'm sure are just as frustrated as you are. Maybe some community agitation is needed to resolve this problem.  Greek Restaurants Under Fire for Illegally Valeting Cars, Astoria By Eleni Bozos (http://usa.greekreporter.com/author/ebozos/) on October 25, 2010 in East Coast (http://usa.greekreporter.com/category/east-coast/), New York (http://usa.greekreporter.com/category/east-coast/new-york/), News (http://usa.greekreporter.com/category/news/) Tweet This (http://twitter.com/share)  (http://usa.greekreporter.com/files/2010/10/Astoria-NY-300x225.jpg) (http://usa.greekreporter.com/files/2010/10/Astoria-NY.jpg)The New York Daily News stated recently that several Greek restaurants in Astoria have come under fire from local residents for illegally using street parking spaces for its private valet service. The parking situation has caused an uproar from the residents.
The owner of Telly’s Taverna said she didn’t realize her valets were causing a nuisance. The Daily News observed a valet worker for the restaurant on a recent busy Saturday night. The worker placed a cone in empty spaces on 23rd Ave. as cars left and replaced them with customer’s vehicles as they arrived. The practice of reserving a public parking space with any type of device is illegal, according to the city Department of Transportation. The Police Department can enforce the law and ticket anyone officers find breaking it.
The News observed Stamatis Restaurant reserving spaces with the traffic cones as well. “They are only doing it on Friday and Saturday night,” said one resident who did not want to give his name. “These are small businesses and hardworking people. All the restaurants do it. It’s really not a big deal.”
Telly’s owner Nana Loiselle, insists she would never intentionally cause a problem for anyone in her neighborhood. The restaurant began its valet service about six months ago and uses a parking lot around the corner Loiselle stated. She pays $450 every weekend to use the space. “I don’t think I own the street and I just want to make my customers happy…If one of my valets was blocking a spot with a cone and someone needed the space, all they would have to do is ask, and I would move it and gladly give them the space.”
Stamatis owner Anna Bililis said she also rents a parking lot for her valet service. “What can I say, you say you saw it, but I honestly didn’t know that happens” Bililis claimed.

If you live across a private establishment with high client traffic or city community center or daycare and the parents and/or employees of the establishment have blocked your driveway:

They end up having a parent meeting over it (but be prepared to file annually- new kids attending new parents dropping off.)
 
 3. Even if it is just a regular person or neighbor i would still file a complaint with the mayor's email. The more you file the more a pattern is formed and the more folks like you and I have a chance of changing and having the law enforced.
 
 4. Write to dmv in albany about the law "Parking or standing is not allowed: In front of a driveway." It is not sufficient and needs to be more detailed like: Parking or standing is not allowed: In front of a driveway and needs to be 5 feet away from the driveway on either side.

 5. Have the number of a good towing company that tows within 5 minutes usually. Call them as the officer is writing the ticket.
 
 6. Place a private reflective: No Parking in Driveway Violators will be towed at owner's expense sign.
 
 7. Request from DOT a No Parking anytime sign be placed on either side of a driveway. Forewarned they deny the first request. So you refile stressing the problem and your experiences and take pictures of you driveway and either side of it and of the infraction violators. Once the request is approved check on its placement. One person checked and DOT records showed that the sign was placed when it wasn't. The individual had to file with DOT and 311 a complaint and make a request for a replacement. Even when the sign was to be placed the city employee was unsure about placing it and was not going to.
 
 a. either sweet talk or calmly explain the problem and why the sign is being placed to convince the employee or
 b. call DOT where you requested the sign and report the individual immediately or for afterhours 311 and the next day call DOT. Take his name or description, date, time, plate of vehicle s/he's driving file it with the complaint.
 
 8. Don't bother talking to folks about them blocking your driveway. They disrespect you by blocking, they might end up cussing you out as I've seen. They will respect a ticket and tow more than you talking to them. You do not owe them anything. They owe you for blocking your driveway in the first place!
