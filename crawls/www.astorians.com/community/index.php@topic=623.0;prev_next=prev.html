Anyone notice their ConEd electric billing going way up?
« Reply #6 on: December 12, 2005, 09:25:14 AM »
Energy prices are tied to the price of crude oil. Natural gas is a byproduct of the oil industry.[Corrected: not a byproduct, but a similar product] When 100 oil [and gas] platforms in the golf were destroyed or damaged by Katrina that meant that the supply of oil and natural gas was threatened. This makes oil/gas/energy traders in the market go crazy and start buying contracts because they think that the price will go up with demand once we run out of oil/gas/whatever. This makes the price go up in and of itself. Once they get those oil platforms up and running again it will ease the market and the price should drop a bit, but this will be months not weeks away.

It's important to look at the cost per KW hour for electricity (somewhere around $0.10 to $0.20 per KWhr), and the cost of your gas per therm (somewhere around $1.30 - $1.70 per therm) currently. This is the only real way to see what exactly is going up or down. Also pay attention to your usage. Saying that your bill went up is hardly a way to know if it's because you left your computer on, or if the spot price of energy has just gone through the roof. I recommend taking a minute to look over you bill and try to understand what is going on. Also, that's how you'll know if any habbits that you've changed energy wise are actually making a difference.

Much of the electricity we use is generated by gas turbines. Some from nuclear, and some from coal. Of these options nuclear is the only energy that is not suceptable to these price spikes because it generates a consistant amount of energy over a long period of time. However, nuclear only accounts for 12% of the energy we use in New York City.

I'd recommend that people take the time and research ESCO's. These are third party energy suppliers who will bill you through your conedison bill. It can save you money. List of New York ESCOS

I would personally recommend changing to an ESCO that can serve both your electric and gas needs. I've done a bit of research on this and believe that one of the best options is IDT Energy. Their prices the last few months have been consistantly lower than conedison.

Also, there's the option to lock in your energy prices for a specific amount of time through other providers. I'm personally thinking of locking in a flat rate for 5 years through Energy Savings Corp (you will see this on the list of escos.) The advantage is that your price will not go up, but the disadvange is if the price drops then you are locked in at a higher price. I personally feel however that limiting the upside in price is more important to me.

That's all I have to offer at the moment.

The level payment plan can be dangerous. It doesn't really save you any money, they just take one year's worth of usage and spread it out over 12 months. So that way in the summer you are paying less, and in the winter you are paying more than everyone else. But in the end you pay the same.

Of course this gets thrown off when the prices spike (like they did in the last few months since Katrina) so that's why they are telling you you have to pay more. We are all paying more. :(

If you really want to lock in your energy prices you have to get a contract to purchase your energy up front. I'm going to do that with my electric, and then sign a contract for gas over the summer when the prices are lower again.
