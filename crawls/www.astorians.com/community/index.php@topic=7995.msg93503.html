Fiscal responsibility != country's economy. 

There is overlap, but you have to really distinguish the two of them.  Spending 1 billion on a bridge to nowhere in Alaska does not mean that inflation will go down or interest rates will go up.  What it does mean is that the money will come at the expense of other social programs with larger implications down the road.  I'm trying to think of the most direct correlation between gov't and economy, I can't think of any more direct than taxes, an increase of which may result in a decrease in consumer spending, purchasing of cheaper products overseas (trade deficit), etc.
