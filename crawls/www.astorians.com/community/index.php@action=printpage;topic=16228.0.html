Title: Re: Top Tomato Space will be.....
Post by: amandax on October 02, 2009, 10:39:28 AM
Local farmers meaning farmers in Jersey and southern NY State with small farms rather than giant industrial farms. There is also an urban farming movement afoot in NYC. There is rooftop farms in Brooklyn that grows all kinds of produce on rooftops in Greenpoint and sells them at farmers markets and to local restaurants. The local farms are mainly organic and don't use pesticides in their growing much of the time and grow fresh, seasonal ingredients that don't get shipped for thousands of miles and use fossil fuels to ship them. It's called the slow food movement or ethical consumerism.

This afternoon, I stopped by Astoria "Farmers" Market with the intention to buy some vegetables for the first time, since sadly the CSA season has ended until next spring.  I was so disheartened by what I found.  I was specifically looking for seasonal veggies, although I didn't expect to find local or organic necessarily.  I wanted root vegetables, winter squash, cabbages - that sort of thing.

In the refrigerated case near the entrance closest to 35th St., where I had noticed some organic items being displayed when they first opened, I saw only a random assortment of non-organic fruit and vegetables, including slimy-looking mushrooms, brussels sprouts, and California strawberries.  In another section, all of their herb bunches were wrapped in plastic - so not only could I not check the quality, but plastic spurs spoilage for sensitive leaves like that.  Aside from generic green cabbage, they had Napa cabbage that was also wrapped in plastic.  I couldn't find any parsnips or rutabaga.  The cauliflower was gross - they had both white and purple, but all of the heads were turning black, and on closer inspection, nearly all of the heads had already been scraped - which means that the store had already scraped off one layer of spoilage and put them back out for sale.  At that point I turned and walked out - and went to Trade Fair and got everything I was looking for, all of it fresh looking.  I never thought I'd prefer Trade Fair to a vegetable stand, but today it was sure better.

Has anyone else shopped there, and if so, did you have a better experience?  I'd like to think it can and will be better than this, even if it isn't ever a true farmers market, but today was a very poor shopping experience there.
