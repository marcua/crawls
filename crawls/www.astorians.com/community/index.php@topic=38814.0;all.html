Fall has its advantages. Cooler temps and rainfall (fingers crossed) actually stimulate growth where it's needed most in young trees-the roots. A strong root system makes for a healthier, stronger tree. The tree will direct its energy to the roots in the Fall rather than to the foliage. Planting shouldn't be done too close to the first hard freeze, however. Mid October is still safe, though. Sometimes Spring is tricky, a crap shoot-too cold for too long with soil that is too wet and compacted for planting, followed by a sudden heat wave and then boom it's summer, really stressing the young trees.
