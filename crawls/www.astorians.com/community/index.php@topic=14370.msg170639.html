I thought I'd list my recommended restaurants here so that people can get a sense of the kinds of food I'm into but also so that everyone has an aggregated list for reference.  Just so you guys know, I'm generally into authentic ethnic and regional cuisines executed with a lot of precision and/or passion.  I don't like my foods greasy and I'm an accomplished cook in my own right, so there are times when I get a little critical of sloppily executed dishes.  Also wanted to mention that I've been eating mostly at home lately to cut back on expenses, so I haven't exactly splurged in the neighborhood just yet.  Places I'd recommend thus far being in Astoria for almost 2 months now:

Agnanti - exceptional seafood, good mezes, mediocre meat dishes, moderate portions, moderate pricing.
Taverna Klycades - more seafood variety than Agnanti, good mezes, overly generous with olive oil making dishes generally a little oily, moderate pricing
Zenon Taverna - large portions, moderately low prices, solid food with some of it being very good
Last Stop Restaurant - large portions, low prices, good/solid food with nothing exceptional but nothing bad either
Neptune and Mike's Diner - low prices, decent food, you can't go wrong but you're not going to get dazzled either

There are some that I can't recall right now and there are a whole bunch that I would NOT recommend, but I'll keep adding to the list as I go along.

Re: diners - fair to say but the thing about diners is that you don't really go out of your way to eat at them.  For the most part, they serve standard fair executed "standardly."  I can't really think of a diner that's a destination eatery, they're more like places you go to grab a quick bite to get full.  Hopefully the food is decent and cheap.  There's no wow factor and the guys at the kitchen are more cooks than chefs.  That being the case, I don't travel outside of my neighborhood for a special occasion to eat at a diner.

Typically, at a diner I expect a well-prepared egg dish and some fluffy pancakes, etc., so I'm not asking for too much.  I'll occasionally stray off the breakfast menu for something adventurous, but I typically get regret doing so.

In my post, I'm simply recommending the places, meaning that you won't lose by eating there.  I didn't say it was the best by a long shot.

More places to add to my recommended list:

Cafe Soleil - excellent coffee, amazing almond croissant, cheap, haven't tried much else
Halal Sandwich Shop - very generic name on an orange awning/sign, fresh meats grilled on sandwiches with a mediterranean "caponata" relish, moderate price, mediocre portions, pretty decent overall
Tierras Colombianas - simple ethnic, rustic, honest food that's tasty, large portions, moderate prices, meat can be dry sometimes, a fun experience

The one observation I will make is that of all the Greek places I've tried, they've all had very lackluster lamb dishes.  It's very funny to me that Greeks in Astoria can't seem to cook their national staple food.  Very disappointed with lamb dishes in Astoria in general.

The other incredibly obvious observation I'll make is that Indian in Jackson Heights is much better as is Chinese in Flushing.  I'm not sure why those two ethnic minorities haven't made more of an effort to open restaurants in this location.

Quote from: riteus on March 20, 2009, 11:47:05 AM
The one observation I will make is that of all the Greek places I've tried, they've all had very lackluster lamb dishes.  It's very funny to me that Greeks in Astoria can't seem to cook their national staple food.  Very disappointed with lamb dishes in Astoria in general.

While lamb is stereotypically associated as a Greek staple food, the truth is that it's far from it.  I would venture to say that most Greek households, both here and in Greece or Cyprus, do not eat lamb very often.  In fact, until recently, the Greek diet was known for de-emphasizing meat and being based around vegetables, legumes, grains, some fish, and limited meat.  Lamb was something reserved for holidays and special occasions, and in many ways, still is. 

That said, I do agree with you completely that the lamb dishes at most Greek restaurants in Astoria are indeed lackluster (and, I will add, usually nothing like what you would find in Greece).

Quote
The other incredibly obvious observation I'll make is that Indian in Jackson Heights is much better as is Chinese in Flushing.  I'm not sure why those two ethnic minorities haven't made more of an effort to open restaurants in this location.

Good observation.  It's surprising more Indian or Chinese restaurants have not opened in Astoria, especially with the growth of other types of cuisines in the area, especially Thai.  It would seem to me that such restaurants would do well if they opened in Astoria, even in the current economic climate.  That said, the same could be said about, say, Greek cuisine in Flushing or Bayside...there's very few restaurants and the quality isn't as good as one would find in Astoria, even though those regions also have a substantial Greek population.

Mezzo is an interesting pick.  I can't wholeheartedly recommend it although I've had some decent dishes there.

The first time we went, we ordered the mixed grill meats platter, which consisted of tons of grilled meat varieties and potatoes.  We also ordered the seafood pasta and a cold mezze platter.  The pasta was superb, the meats were delicious if not a little heavy, and the dips were good too.

The second time we went, we ordered the fried calamari and seafood pasta.  The calamari was greasy and had no seasoning.  The seafood pasta was atrocious, the seafood was completely overcooked and shriveled, and the pasta was mushy.  I looked at my neighbor's lamb dishes.  The lamb chops they ordered for nearly $30 an entree were overcooked, dry, and were tiny portions.  I was sorely disappointed and if I were them, I'd be pissed.

After the first time I ate there, I'd say I'd recommend it.  After the second time, I'd say I'd never go back.  If anything, it's wildly inconsistent.
