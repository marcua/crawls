A Prayer for Owen Meany
My mom gave me this book when I was about 17 and I told her that I thought I didn't believe in god. It means so much to me in that it was what made me realize I believed in God, I didn't believe in other people telling me how to believe in God. I started finding my spirituality in all things around me, and not just in old scripture.

The Kite Runner
Anyone who's ever had a friend they genuinely loved should read this. Starting out as little children, exploring the themes of learning mistakes, paying for your actions, redemption and growing up are all prevalent themes that touched me across the spectrum. I was able to relate to the characters easily, the storyline was very cohesive and believable, and I couldn't put it down from the second I picked it up.

The Giving Tree
Really short children's book. Read it and you'll see why it's a must-read.

Crime and punishment. It's a classic of Russian Literature and an example of the power of words. A story of a tortured soul as only a 19 century Russian Author could do it.  A bit heavy, save for winter.

Kite runner. Just an amazing exploration into a world and life that we will never ever see or experience. Kabul Afghanistan.

Auntie Mame. Hilarious story of eccentricity and relationship between Crazy aunt and her nephew. Set in NYC, 1930"s??? 

Time travelers wife. relationship between and woman and the time traveling man in her life. One of the best sci fi topics to be written about by a woman.  Excellent characters and good modern story.  It's got everything: sex, Violent femmes, drugs, booze, time travel, great writing, some crying involved (not me of course). My favorite classic.

Life of Pi. A fantasy of sorts as told by a child marooned at sea and what happens to him and a host of characters (some animals too). A beautiful story, fun too.  Very very fast read because it is so good.

Winters tale. The world of NYC early 20th century. Story of a thief and his horse mixed into the world of wealthy families, industrial age and art deco NYC. The Beauty of the Hudson valley, love story, some fantasy, 5 point gangs, building NY.  My favorite book.

So here are mine, and I'll try and explain the best I can why you should read them.  I will include a fourth for youngsters like myself.

1) The Hitchhikers Guide to the Galaxy: If only for the perfect melding of science fiction, humor and insights to the human psyche, this book, and to be perfectly honest, all 5 books in the trilogy, should be read.  They're easy enough to read, funny enough to keep you interested, and more enjoyable than both the BBC mini-series and the recent movie.  Plus it will hopefully teach you to not panic.

2) A Clockwork Orange: Another book that is far better than it's cinematic rendering.  My favorite part of this book is how it deals with language, and how it utilizes a person's ability to learn a language to tell a horribly frightening story. If you buy a copy with the glossary and use it, it's cheating.

3) How to Talk Dirty and Influence People: Lenny Bruce's autobiography.  I actually want to go back and re-read this, but it's just a top notch book about censorship and how the taboos of one person can effectively ruin the life of another. 

4a.) The Automatic Millionaire: This is a book that anyone who ever plans on retiring should read. It's basically a step-by-step rundown of extremely simple things that a person can do in order to retire with a million dollars (when you actually retire, a million bucks isn't that much).  Unfortunately, some things, like property ownership, aren't extremely realistic in NYC, but I would say 90% is.

I read an eff-load of books, so it's pretty hard to narrow it down to three. But here are some (SOME!) of my favorites:

The Lovely Bones - This is just so interesting. It's from the perspective of a murdered girl watching how her family is dealing with her death.

Einstein's Dreams - By far one of the most fascinating reads on time. Gives little snapshots of how the world would operate if his theories were true.

The Wideacre Trilogy - These books are just delicious. By Philippa Gregory.

Anything by Jasper Fforde (especially the Thursday Next novels) - If you enjoy word-fun (grammar, puns, etc.) these books are totally awesome and totally cheesy. By far one of my absolute favorite authors.

Some others I'd recommend - The Golden Compass (loved this - still have to read the others in this set), Harry Potter (of course!), Conservatize Me (if you're a liberal this is a fun read), A Spot of Bother (recently read this - was fun), The Pale Blue Eye (if you like Edgar Allen Poe this is a fun little detective novel with him as one of the characters), Chronicles of Narnia (grew up with these - love them!), and I could go on...
