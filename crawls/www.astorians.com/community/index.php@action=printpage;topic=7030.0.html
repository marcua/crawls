Title: Re: Montreal Restaurant Recommendations
Post by: lanseaux on July 11, 2007, 12:38:42 PM
Six days?  I'm envious!  I love Montreal, and the food and restaurants are fantastic.

First on anyone's list should be Au Pied de Cochon (http://www.restaurantaupieddecochon.ca/index_eng.html), specifically their poutine topped with foie gras.  It's a heart attack on a plate but entirely worth it.  They have a whole section on their menu devoted to foie gras, in addition to many local/Quebecois specialties, including lots of game.  The venison tartare is also delicious.

Another restaurant we enjoyed very much but that seems to be off the tourist radar is Aux Deux Canailles (http://www.hour.ca/food/food.aspx?iIDArticle=766).  Reasonable and delicious (but we didn't try the horse tartare).

Aux Deux Canailles
256 Roy E., 844-0222
Dinner for two, not including wine, tax or tip: $40-$60 (prices from 2004)
Lunch tables d'hôte, Mon. to Fri.: $13-$17

(I found one online reference that indicates that Aux Deux Canailles closed in that location and may now be a Basque restaurant called Pintxo.  It was supposed to have reopened somewhere else - I have found it in current online lists at 250 Roy E., with the same phone number.)

Regardless, though, almost anywhere you go it will be good.  Enjoy!

Post by: Sabeers on July 11, 2007, 05:34:45 PM
For Montreal meals there are two things you definetly need to have.  Poutine and smoked meat.
Poutine, I would suggest La Banquise which is on Rue Rachel right off of Parc Lafontaine.  Good news is is that place is 24 hours, the last call rush is always amusing.  And for Smoked meat, Schwartz's on St. Laurent cannot be beat, there is usually a line on the weekends but definetly worth the wait.
