Post by: Jonathan on March 27, 2006, 03:38:05 AM
frozen veggies do have their uses.

I am certain that the crepe dish at beer garden uses frozen spinach and its a great dish.

For catering we use frozen spinach for creamed spinach and with pasta dishes.  using fresh costs way too much and 4lbs of fresh spinach sautees down to about 4cups.

Frozen peas a great year round. never use canned. try a fresh pea soup with frozen peas and you will be pleasantly surprised.

frozen rasberries, strawberries and blueberries are excellent products.  Jaque Pepin suggests using them for baked desserts and fruit sauces.

Just be sure to by the best brand possible. cheap frozen spinach might have some sand in it.

whats a bad brand?  any generic or cheap supermarket house brand. 

Birds eye, bjs and costo house brands are best.

Title: Re: Frozen Vegetables
Post by: lanseaux on March 27, 2006, 01:51:48 PM
I really, really like TJ's frozen green beans.  They are excellent - and leave me with no prep work, to boot, which I appreciate when I'm getting home at 8pm and preparing dinner.  I nuke them, covered, with no added water for four minutes, and then saute them with slivered garlic in olive oil for two or three minutes.  Toss in a little red pepper flakes and/or a little grated parmesan for a great dish.

During the winter, we occasionally buy frozen broccoli and cauliflower.  Surprisingly enough, the Key Food brand of both contains more florets and fewer stem pieces than the name brands - can't remember whether it was Green Giant or Bird's Eye.  But I bought them side by side and poured them into bowls to compare.

TJ's mushroom blend is also very good, and cost-effective, although you have to be patient and careful because frozen mushrooms exude even more water than their fresh counterparts.  And TJ's frozen shelled edamame!  Other brands of edamame are good, too.

Frozen spinach is probably the most frequent visitor to our freezer, though.  I have to say I've never had any problems with Key Food brand, although when I can afford it I buy organic.  We also buy frozen berries - that's what I make my blackberry/raspberry, sage and caramelised shallot reduction with.

I can't remember the last time I used a canned vegetable, except for tomatoes and hearts of palm.  I didn't grow up with canned vegetables, so it's not something I think of.

Title: Re: Frozen Vegetables
Post by: Christine on March 28, 2006, 09:19:55 PM
Frozen vegetables are said to be more nutritious than the fresh produce you get at the market. That's b/c freezing is done not long after the vegetables have been picked, and therefore more vitamins are retained.

"Fresh," OTOH, has been sitting around after being trucked in and gassed with ethylene or whatever it is they do to force ripening/maintain color etc.

Besides, you think they use fresh produce at fancy restaurants? Edamame is a GREAT frozen food to have on hand- toss in soup or quickly boiled and eaten like popcorn. I'm also partial to other small and cut-up frozen vegetables that can be added to many things.

Oh yeah, bananas can be frozen for a while rather than left to turn black and then tossed out. Great for smoothies.
