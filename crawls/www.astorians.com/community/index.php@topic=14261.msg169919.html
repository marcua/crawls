My lease is up for renewal this spring and I'm considering asking my landlord to discuss re-negotiating the rent. Unless he's going to lower your rent out of the goodness of his heart-I just don't see that happening. Maybe the best you can  do is ask him to keep the same amount (lock yourself in for at least a 2 year lease, then)...if you know he's definitely raising it; do you suspect he might? Otherwise, if he does, maybe you can point out that you've seen lower rents for better/bigger spaces-be honest here. Or, if he really insists that it's going to go up, maybe you can ask for a more efficient appliance-a stove or a fridge. But, you know your own landlord-is he a nice guy that would negotiate or would you be stirring up a can of worms with him? What about a fresh coat of paint, floors, etc...?

Any landlord who hopes to raise rents in this economy is only bluffing. Rents on average are down 8%, and I think that the number is higher in the boroughs. Look around (but do some serious research, get written offers that you can bring to your landlord) and threaten moving. Sure, the moving cost may cut into the first 3-4 months of savings, but if you can get a nicer or cheaper place, it will be worth it.

If it's that Astoria landlords are missing something about the greater economy, I think they are in for a rude awakening when a lot of leases come up in July/August and people move across the river to the UES.

Anecdotally, I had interest in someday taking over my friend's rent-controlled lease in Chelsea, but it really isn't that much of a deal  now...
