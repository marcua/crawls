ATTENTION COLLEGE STUDENT!!!!
« on: June 22, 2011, 04:44:46 PM »
 
In New York state, there has been a tuition-refund benefits program for students for years.
Since 2009, the Feds has announced that they are broadening it for the students who even do not have an income.
Therefore, more students can be benefited from it up to $1,000 depends on their tuition payment records,
and $2,500 if they have additional income and etc. via filling a tax return form for it.
Many international students who are not eligible to have a job to earn an income due to their visa status tend to regard it as a tax return
since they fill out the a tax return form for it, and often express their concerns and frustrations about it.
This program is a voluntary program for the students who are eligible to be benefited from it,
not mandatory for all international students, and also not against any form of immigration or tax laws.
It’s similar to a student tuition-rebate program for getting back a portion of tuition they have paid,
and even a very valuable opportunity for eligible international students since it’s recorded via a form of tax return
to prove that they have indeed been full-time students who pay their required tuitions.
The following links are to help your understanding :
1.     http://taxes.about.com/od/deductionscredits/qt/education.htm
 
 2.     http://www.themiddleclass.org/bill/american-opportunity-tax-credit-act-2009
To be eligible for this program, you have to be a full time student or at least a part time student with 6 credits
who has a tuition payment record to prove it. No inquiry about your status, age, or nationality.
If you are a New York resident going to a school in New York City area without a Social Security Number,
you can apply for this program without it for the first year only.
If you are not eligible to apply SSN for next 2-3 years, we will help you to apply for the Tax ID which works similar as SSN.
When you receive the Tax ID 2-3 months after applying it, you are now eligible to apply for this program.
CALL or visit our office in Manhattan with it if you prefer to discuss it in person.
