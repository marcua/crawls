Title: *JOB* Traders' tech support at day trading firm
Post by: Tradeology on July 20, 2007, 08:34:59 AM
Requirements: Series 7 License, Knowledge of some tech, able to lift 50+ lbs.

Description: The job entails daily office routines, tech support when needed by traders, and occassional projects that may or may not require some manual labor (This isn't very often at all. Maybe 2 or 3 times a month.)

Responsibilities:

Tech support for traders (assist with creating layouts, troubleshoot functionality issues)
Monitor P&L Risk
Execute trades for customers
Allocate trades
Email Blue on allocation breaks
Check ACT Workstation for trades/clearing/reporting on ACT
Keep on-going records for trade breaks, open items, MPTG activities, BLUE activities
System check for short sale rule
System check for long sale rule
Check for breaks/order discrepancies/price discrepancies for MOG before the open and after the close
Filter through resumes for prospective traders and schedule interviews
Check new user (prior to active trader status) accounts for proper data feeds and system functionalities
Assist remote traders through VNC and similar programs / often travel to remote facilities to address issues
Responsible for moving/setting up trading stations
Responsible for repairing trading stations
Excel programming (VBA), data manipulation*
Assist in various projects on various capacities
* Not Required

Everyday is different and variety is always a plus here. The staff is great and it's more of a family atmosphere than a business. I put in bold the tasks that are most frequent. The salary range is from 35-40k to start depending on experience, but there is a bonus and you can move up pretty quickly if you're good. The CEO is in a multitude of lucrative businesses. So, this opens a lot of doors as long as you can get your foot in the door.
