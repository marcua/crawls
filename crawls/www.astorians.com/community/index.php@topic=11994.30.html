  first off, i see everybody seems to think what makes you hip is how you dress.  thats pretty shallow.  what makes someone hip is what they do and their beliefs.  i guarantee if you look at the back of the closet of these fedora and Vans wearing "hipsters" you will find a green John Deere tee and a trucker hat, which they would never wear nowadays because those items aren't "hip" anymore.  since when is it "hip" to follow trends?  they dress this way to look like they are hip because they've seen other people who they thought looked hip wear it, or because they media subliminally dictated it to them.  true hipsters stand out and don't follow trends and look for the "next big thing" before it happens.  that is the usually what happens, true hipsters find the next big thing, then the so-called "hipsters" ride on their coattails to try and be like them.  it happened in Williamsburg.  the "starving artists" first moved in because it was cheap run-down neighborhood, less rent means more food and art supplies with their already thin paycheck.  these true "starving artisits" are at the cusp of hipsterdom and set the trends. the wanna-bes wanna be just as hip and move in too.  eventually the spot is blown and everybody and their "cool" cousin want in, and the rents go through the roof.  next thing you know, you have some rich wanna-be's skating around town on their extra-long skateboards looking for the next big thing in Time Out New York..
