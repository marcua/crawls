Quote from: pink_floyd on November 28, 2007, 10:38:56 AM
what is an "american" restaurant? McDonald's?

:roll: No. I am talking about restaurants that aim to serve whatever is thought of as American food with an American atmosphere. Yes, these places do exist, just like places that aim to serve Japanese food with a Japanese atmosphere exist, Mexican food, Italian food, etc.

I've had good meals from some of the Japanese restaurants on Ditmars, but I've been to Japan, and they don't seem like Japan. Harlan pretty much hit the nail on the head with what I'm saying: often it seems places just read a how-to guide on Japanese food and restaurants. The food might be good, but it's generic -- primarily sushi and teriyaki, with a few yakitori items and a couple noodle things thrown in for good measure, and a standard miso soup made with tofu cubes and seaweed -- and the atmosphere plays to what Americans (or maybe foreigners in general?) think Japanese restaurants should look like: plain but elegant, with lots of pretty wood and references to cherry blossoms.

Bringing up JJ's Fusion and Lil Bistro doesn't really fit in this, though - JJ's Fusion may serve sushi, but it doesn't claim to be a "Japanese restaurant" and neither does Lil Bistro. I don't believe the Japanese have a monopoly on sushi, and I love creativity with food.
