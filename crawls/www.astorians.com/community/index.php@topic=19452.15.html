If you like Chilean Sea Bass, have it at this place.  A friend who is very particular about food told me it was possibly the best sea bass dish she'd ever had.  I have to agree.  Cooked perfectly.  Buttery and salty on the outside with a spicy blackened crust.  But not Cajun blackened.  More lightly from the saute pan.  Over spiced mashed plantains.  Everything we had was really delicious.  The service was very good.  The process moves a little slow but they are clearly surprised by their popularity.  They even called a waitress in from home to help with the crowd.  So they're trying very hard but if it stays this much of a hot spot, they'll need more staff - on the floor and in the kitchen.  The only other thing I might say - and remember, I had the fish and not the ribs - is that the portion was small.   And we had a quesadilla (the devil corn which was super!) as an appetizer.  So if you're a big eater like me, you may not find yourself leaving too satisfied unless you order a few items.  This place is a winner and I can see it being becoming a very popular spot and hard to get a table.  Like others said, it's not a huge in terms of seating.

Ate here with some friends last Friday.

The beer selection on tap is ridiculous - so if you are a big fan of beers then this is a good place to go.

For a place that had only been open a few days, the service was much more on point than I would have expected.  Our waiter (a guy with short reddish hair) was super attentive and personable.  The hostess was also really outgoing and friendly.

We had the guacamole and the fiesta nachos to start off.  Both were very good.  One issue with the presentation of the fiesta nachos (it was more like a 7 later dip) was that it came with flimsy tortilla chip strips that would break every time you tried to scoop up some of the dip. Fortunately the guac came with more substantial chips that were able to withstand the dipping so we were able to use those for both.  Both apps were very tasty.

For the main we ordered the meat tasting platter.  In a mix up they brought out the quesadilla tasting platter by accident.  Our server immediately noticed this, apologized, said our main would be out asap and took away the wrong dish.  An owner (or supervisor) also came over to apologize, and our waiter came right back to offer us a free round of beers.  So they made a mistake but immediately rectified it and provided compensation without prompting.  Hopefully they'll maintain this level of service/care over time and it's not just a "it's our first week we are trying to make a good impression" kind of thing.

In the tasting platter I liked the brisket and the short ribs the most. The carnitas was a little too fatty for my taste; additionally, since it's braised in coca cola I just didn't like the sweetness of it compared to the spicy and tangy flavors of the other two meats.

For dessert I had the toasted coconut fried ice cream - it was really good - not greasy or anything.  My friends shared the tres leches cake (which I also sampled) and it was fantastic.

I would definitely return here.

The missus and I went there Friday for date night.  We got there around 8.30, and it was definitely a full house.  We had a few drinks, I opted for beer, of which they have a great selection, and Ms Murdock had a decidedly non-sangria sangria. We waited for about 10 minutes, and were seated.  The staff was solid, attentive, but not annoying.  Neither of us were particularly hungry (I way overdid it on lunch) so we split one of the guacamole things and the quesedilla sampler. For the price, I was expecting a lot more guac. It was good, but for $8, it was like a tiny ramekin that was about 1/2 an avocado.  Luckily for us there was a little squeeze bottle of delicious sauce that we generously squirted on our chips when the guac ran out. The quesedillas were okay, but they were just quesedillas.  My favorite (the chorizo and goat cheese) was not appreciated by my better half who likes neither chorizo nor goat cheese (I WIN!). The other two were forgettable. The chicken tasted not unlike something I could have gotten at Taco Bell, and I'm not sure what ingredient was listed as "Mexican Truffle" in the third, but it tasted nothing like tuffle (which is a HUGE disappointment).
As for the ambiance, the space definitely gives off a hip, modern vibe, without being pretentious (although the waterfall with the John Wayne collection playing behind it seemed a little out of place), but the music was a total head scratcher. It was completely off the wall random.  They were playing War's "Low Rider" followed by somewhat quiet indie rockish stuff, and then randomly interspersing cuts from Billy Joel's Greatest Hits Vol. 3 (I know for a fact that it was BJGHV3, because I have it, and tend to avoid it). For all the thought that seemingly went into this place I don't know why they didn't do a little more thinking when it came to the sound track.
Overall, it was okay. For what we spent, I can't say I'm rushing to go back, but I probably wouldn't say no if it was suggested.
