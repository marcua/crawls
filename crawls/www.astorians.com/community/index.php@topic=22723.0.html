Berry Fresh Farms 2.0
« on: May 15, 2012, 10:43:59 PM »
I spun this thread off of the Newtown Farmer Market Thread. I hope that this thread can be merged with other threads that discuss the same topic.

For those of you that haven't visited the new Berry Fresh Farms, I wanted to mention a few highlights.
First, they really expanded their deli section. It's no longer a few glass cases in the old "meat room". Now it is practically the entire length of the eastern wall of the old nightclub space. It features reasonably priced prepared gourmet Italian foods (Asparagus Parm, anyone?); a whole array of American and Italian meats and cheeses ranging from this week's special 4.99/lb Boer's Head pastami (what a steal)  to 20.00/lb imported italian speck and everything in between; an extensive olive bar; and soon to come rotisserie chickens. The key to the Deli is that it is reasonably priced and seems to be far cheaper than Key, C-Town, etc. for far better quality and selection.
Their fresh bread selection is amazing! Bagels, etc. from Island Bakery, Rolls and loaves from Astoria Bakery, Pan Latino from Teixera's, and bread and pizza dough (.99 a pie) from long Island's Polito's. where else are you going to find this in the neighborhood?
"Ethnic" food is well represented as well--Indian, Arabic, Greek, Italian, etc. Not quite as good as Tradewind, but decent never-the less. I'll give them time. They even have a jewish/kosher section unlike most of the local chains here in Astoria that only have seasonal items. Has anybody lived here long enough to remember when a particular  store--that will remain unnamed--on 30th Ave. in the 40s actually located it's "Jewish" food in the pet food and pesticide aisle? For Italian items this is the go to store in Astoria in the same way that Titan is for Greek and Euro market is for Balkan.
Berry Fresh is still getting their frozen food aisles up and running as of this weekend with empty freezer units. But I'm sure that they'll be first class once finally installed.
I really don't understand a call for Trader Joe's in Astoria given that we have so many great locally owned markets on our door step.

If you've never shopped at a Trader Joe's, then you don't understand what you're missing. I've not been to Berry Fresh, but I'm positive I'd still get more for my money at TJ's.
