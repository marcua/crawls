This is a very interesting situation. Under Federal law, a person cannot be required to serve jury duty more often than once every four (4) years. If you have served in the United States District Court or a New York State Court within the last four (4) years, and wish to be excused, you would simply mail a copy of your official jury certificate, together with the Federal summons, in the return envelope. But, you really didn't serve. You were simply on notice. So I believe you are required to serve, if called, on this Federal jury.

So I ended up getting called for jury duty at the Federal Court House in Islip, Long Island this week.  Let me tell you, it is a schlep!  Took quite a while to make it there and made the mistake of getting off at the Islip stop on the LIRR as opposed to the Central Islip stop.  Different line, etc.  Almost got selected for an armed robbery case scheduled to last from 4-6 weeks (egad).  The people at the court house were cool, however.  It's a nice, modern building almost 5 years old clean to the point of being antiseptic and they have a pretty decent cafeteria there.  In the waiting area they have books and magazines you're free to take home if you get engrossed in them.  When I finally got done it was 4:30 and the lady at the desk told me I'd be waiting a long time for the bus (she knew how long it took me to get there from Astoria) so she called a cab to drop me off at the Babylon station.  The court picked up the tab and after a short wait I was on an express train back to Jamaica.  Still, though, I left my house at 6 and got home around 7:30.  Long day.  Just a heads up in case anyone here gets called.

Well, up until this experience I had had the same understanding -- that you only get called for Queens County jury duty.  But this was for a federal circuit court, which is an entirely different system of courts.  The judge said this was the Eastern District court and our areas of residence were included in that district, which is why we were called to serve.  I asked my coworkers and they confirmed that it is possible to get called for federal jury duty once in a while.  I did have the impression, though, that I was a rarity among the jurors selected and that most people were from areas nearer to the court.  If I don't get called back during my standby period the letter I received says I'm exempt from federal jury duty for two years and from New York State Court (I suppose this is yet again different from Queens County?) for six years.  There was apparently a misunderstanding among many of the jurors who had been there before and had been told that they would be exempt from federal jury duty for ten years, which is not true.  The judge said if it were up to her she would make it a ten year exemption but she doesn't get to rule on that issue.   :|
