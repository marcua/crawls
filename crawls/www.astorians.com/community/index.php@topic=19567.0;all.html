Torture of Gays in the Bronx
« on: October 09, 2010, 06:22:24 PM »
I have been torn up for the last two days over what happened to those three men in the Bronx. What we are hearing about goes beyond anything I have read about in years. They went through HOURS of torture.....not gay bashing.......pure and simple torture. And the reason they were tortured was because they were gay....pure and simple.

I can go on and on about gay rights and individual bills being forwarded through the legislatures of this country....but I will repeat what I have said before.

There are political and religious leaders who's very words continue to make it okay to engage in these acts. They stand before their pulpits and their podiums and condemn the LGBTQ community with a hatred that corrupts our society.

I just don't understand why ANYONE continues to support those who spout such hatred. How many more will die or be bashed or be tortured before we, as human beings, understanding this issue in a compassionate nature....and say......ENOUGH IS ENOUGH!!!!!!

I do want to spend a moment and thank Bloomberg, Quinn and Kelly for the press conference I just watched on NY1. I watched the 3 main elements of our city's power structure stand firm against the torture that occured in our city.

One last point....on a personal level.

I am very glad to be here in NYC. But there are moments when I am very afraid. Not because of the city.....but because there is an element here that would hit me upside the head with a baseball bat. I will give my partner a quick kiss as he heads to the N line and I head to the R line and I can't help but wonder if I will get bashed for it. I'm not doing it to prove a point.

Tortured......

For hours.......

And I know that there are those that would do the same thing to me.

UCMJ Sec. 925, Art. 125. deals with Sodomy and it states, “(a) Any person subject to this chapter who engages in unnatural carnal copulation with another person of the same or opposite sex or with an animal is guilty of sodomy. I know it sounds funny because the way the UCMJ defines carnal copulation, this would include oral sex as well.  If the military wanted to bust someone for such activities, it could and the service member could not do a thing about it because the service member is bound under the terms of the UCMJ. Extend this to homosexuals now. Since homosexuals do not have a means by which they can engage in natural carnal copulation (penis/vagina sex), then if they want to engage in sexual activities, this would limit them to oral and anal sex only – the two sexual acts that are explicitly singled out by this article.

That being said, lifting the “Don’t Ask, Don’t Tell” policy forces openly homosexual members into a Catch-22 situation. While they are free to openly express their homosexuality – without fear of retribution, they must still conform to the Uniform Code of Military Justice and not engage in homosexual acts. Now, does the military enforce it? Generally speaking – No.… but it is written law that is enforceable, thus creating the catch-22 whereby open homosexuals would be forced to not engage in homosexual activities – still an infringement of their personal liberties. BUT without addressing this article, lifting Don’t Ask Don’t Tell still does not lift the veil of the root of the problem — Being homosexual is not the problem — engaging in homosexual activity is according to the UCMJ. If a superior knows their subordinate is a homosexual, and catches that person engaging in a homosexual act (ie, surprise inspection in a dorm room or something), then that superior is obligated through their bound duties to still hold homosexuality over that person’s head.

So, to that end,  to just lift the policy without making sure ALL ducks are in a row, we would need to make sure ALL barriers and restrictions are removed on the homosexual lifestyle. Since the UCMJ is a Congressional Code of law, then only Congress has the ability to repeal or amend the article – and this needs to be accomplished before it is pursued. And honestly, I am perfectly fine with Congress amending or repealing the applicable article of the UCMJ (925.125), and making sure there are no other Catch-22s so that President Obama may repeal the policy,
