Post by: sarahespanol on March 29, 2008, 04:03:29 PM
that's the age old question, isn't it?  if you hire a sitter to cover YOUR schedule, you will have to hire her for hours that your husband may be able to cover instead- and then you might feel like you are "wasting" money.  Though, if you do- then your husband could take some of those hours to clean the house, shop, cook, etc without a baby to watch- and that might be really nice.

You might find a family that would be willing to "share" the sitter with you- especially good if they have a full time sitter and you would share on an "as needed" basis (maybe agreeing to "sharing" for a set number of hours per week- and if they are very flexilble, maybe per month so that during busy weeks for your husband, you could use your surplus hours, and in less busy weeks, you could rack up hours as your husband would be home). 

If you are hiring a sitter, regardless, they are counting on your money, too.  So, even if you are on vacation, you still pay them- if you stay home because you are sick (or your kid is sick- more often), you still pay, etc.  At least- that was how it was when i had a full time sitter.  And in a share, the sitter will agree to do it because they will make more money during the "share" part- and will probably count on that money, too.

I have done a share, and it can be great!  I have also had my own sitter who came to my house, and that was great too!   

Sitters in astoria usually charge 10-14, and a share is usually 16-20 per hour.  some sitters prefer a "per week" minimum. 

Now I use daycare- I have a soon to be 4 year old and a soon to be 1 year old.  It ended up being cheaper for us (and now that my older kid needs the school/socialization it makes more sense for us).  Day care is 1000-1600 for an infant (some daycare centers start at 6 months).  18months and up is 800+.
