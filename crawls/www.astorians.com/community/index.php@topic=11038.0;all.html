Regardless of who you think makes the best bagel, the key part of the question was about lox. And the answer is, regretfully, don't eat lox. Almost all salmon is either severely endangered (the West-coast salmon fisheries are closed this year because they're so depleted!), or farmed in unsustainable ways. Regarding farmed salmon, take these quotes from this op-ed from the Times:

Quote
I have come to believe that the farmed product is not a healthy alternative to wild.

Three Norwegian-owned companies dominate the salmon-farming industry in North America, and their offshore net-cages dot long stretches of the west coast of the Americas. In Chile, overcrowding in these oceanic feedlots led to this year’s epidemic of infectious salmon anemia, a disease that has killed millions of fish and left the flesh of survivors riddled with lesions.

The situation in Canada, which supplies the United States with 40 percent of its farmed salmon, is not much better. In British Columbia, offshore net-cages are breeding grounds for thumbtack-sized parasites called sea lice. In the Broughton Archipelago, a jigsaw of islands off the province’s central coast, wild pink salmon are infested with the crustaceans. Scientists think that the tens of millions of salmon in Broughton’s 27 Norwegian-owned farms are attracting sea lice and passing them on to wild fish, killing them. They say that this infestation could drive Broughton’s pink salmon to extinction by 2011.

Unless you can get lox made from wild Alaskan salmon (which is in good shape), the ethical thing to do is to avoid it entirely. :(

Question...have you ever tried whitefish?  OMG it is soooooooooo good and they have it there and it's fresh, try it, it's a bit on the salty side but it's really good.

Or, you can make it...
Smoked Whitefish Spread
Yield: 1- 1/2 cups
Ingredients:
8 ounces Whipped Cream Cheese
1/2 pound smoked whitefish, skinned, boned and flaked
2 tablespoons finely chopped scallions (green onions)
1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill or use 1 teaspoon dried dill
1 teaspoon lemon juice
1/8 teaspoon freshly ground pepper, (1/8 to 1/4)
 
Mix all ingredients until well blended. Refrigerate. Serve with matzoh or bagel.
Garnish with quartered lemon and lime slices and fresh dill.
