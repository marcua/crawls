Re: Anyone have an opinion on new Hibachi/Sushi on 23rd Ave?
« Reply #5 on: April 29, 2008, 12:01:36 PM »
I went there last night with a couple of friends and was not very impressed.  Mind you, I'm not really a foodie, but even I noticed it wasn't so great.

We had hibachi, not sushi, and it was sort of "un-traditional" if you're used to the typical Benihana-type hibachi.  It was served with fried rice, which was good, but their idea of vegetables was some shredded lettuce, scallions, and onions.  The meat was cooked fine and quite tasty, but it was cut in long strips and not "bite size", chopstick-friendly morsels.  It was also served in some kind of teriyaki sauce and not with a side of ginger sauce, which is what's usually given.  The place that used to be open on Steinway Street (right next to Victoria's Secret) was MUCH better.

They have a traditional hibachi table in the back that can seat a large group, but it wasn't being used last night so we just had it served from the kitchen.  The worst part was that it was EXPENSIVE.  The bill was about $100 for 3 people.  Granted, we had an appetizer, 3 drinks, and 3 entrees, but that seems like a little much, especially for mediocre food in Astoria.
