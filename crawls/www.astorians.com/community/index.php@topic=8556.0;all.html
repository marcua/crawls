Folks on cell phones
« on: November 06, 2007, 05:09:29 PM »
I'm starting to think that some people, when using a cell phone, lose their ability to do anything else.  I'm sure we've all seen them wandering aimlessly on the sidewalks, in crosswalks, on the stairs in subway stations and they seem to think, I'm on a cell phone, I'm too important to worry about anyone around me - I can walk into folks, I can cut them off unexpectedly, I can do anything I want.  Can't people multi-task?  What about talking on cell phone shuts down the other parts of your brain, especially the parts connected to human courtesy?  If you're going to be on a conversation and don't want to pay attention to anything or anyone else, why not step over to the side so you aren't in the way?  Did their brain already disconnect the minute the call went through?  I don't get these folks at all.
