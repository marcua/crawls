I live on a block that seems to have more dogs than people. I cannot remember the last time I saw a dog off-leash, or an owner that didn't clean up. I've always believed that it was a Community thing. If people where this happens do nothing, it will continue to be a problem. On my block if someone sees a dog do their business and the owner walk away, someone will politely remind them of their civic responsibility. But on my block, people are also deterred from feeding pigeons.

Being a good dog owner is the responsible thing to be. There are a few rules of good ownership. Having your dog spayed or neutered, even if it is a purebred dog. (We don't have many professional purebred breeders here in Astoria!) Getting them an $8.50 ASPCA license tag. Keeping them healthy (vetted) and socialized; on leash at all times, when not in the park during off-leash hours, and cleaning up after them.

Form block committees and take action. Post signs. Take pictures. Let dog owners know that their bad behavior is unacceptable and will no longer be tolerated. Be united in your efforts.

And please, don't blame the dogs. They don't know any better. The owners are supposed to be the responsible ones. Confront *them* about the messes they leave behind. Confront them about their lack of license tags. This is most certainly a quality of life issue that *needs* to be dealt with.

I don't want to burst your bubble, but the collar with the tag is not the best way to recover a dog. Have you ever considered chipping the dog? The cost is not prohibitive, but well worth it. I no longer keep my dogs tags on their collars. I put them on their leashes. I put this (http://www.boomerangtags.com/page.php?c=collartags&k=h) on their collars. I recommend doing it this way, especially for mixed-breed dogs, so you retain evidence of ownership ( ASPCA & Rabies) in case your dog is ever lost. With a purebred dog you always have your AKC registration papers.

Let me tell you a short story.

About 2-3 months ago I went shopping at Best Buy. When I came out of the store with my groceries I saw a beautiful Shih Tzu roaming the parking lot. He saw me and charged towards me. I bent over to pet him and saw he had no collar on. He probably slipped the collar or ran out of the door. When I got into the car, I invited him in and he just jumped in. I drove directly to the nearest vet and had him scanned. We called his registry, and the owners and they came to pick him up within a few hours. Chipping or tattooing a dog is the very best way to be certain that, if lost, your dog will be rehomed.

As far as licensing is concerned, the ASPCA does not have the funds to do proper animal control. People don't realize that their license fee goes to protect all the city's abandoned and stray animals. I have never understood why everyone who owns a dog doesn't license their pet for the health and safety of all those poor shelter animals. Regardless of the fact that it is the law, why would anyone who has a best friend in a canine not want to help all those other poor creatures without homes of their own.
