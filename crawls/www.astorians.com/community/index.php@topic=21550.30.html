Even if it's not a particularly strong hurricane, it is still a hurricane and it is a large-sized one. As far as I know, it's not often that a storm like this hits NYC directly as this one is forecast to do. I'm pretty sure that's why it's getting hyped up so much. I doubt the MTA would be completely shutting down a day ahead of time and Con Ed possibly shutting off power for lower Manhattan if this was mostly about selling newspapers and getting high poll numbers for Bloomberg. It is disrupting way too much across the city. Even the Starbucks on Ditmars was closed this morning, even though the hurricane isn't supposed to start until 2AM tomorrow. Think of all the people who could not get a toffee nut latte.

But aside from the wind (which the NOAA is still saying will be a sustained 50-70mph tomorrow morning, with gusts up to 80mph), they're still predicting at least 8 inches of rain for the city when we've already had a very rainy month, and the risk of a storm surge on top of that. I'm not exactly prepared for post-apocalyptic conditions and I don't expect those to happen - even if there is flooding and our water supply gets contaminated for a while, I'm kind of still expecting to be able to easily walk down the street and get some bottled water - but there is an understandable element of "fear of the unknown" here because people, even the experts, don't seem to really know how bad it might get.
