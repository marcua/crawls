Quote from: Frappe on March 16, 2007, 04:17:46 PM
So first you called my suggestion about not giving aid to areas which cannot sustain it as absolutely wrong, but now you are agreeing with it? (clearly referring to our government, as private individuals and groups can do as they please)

Looks like I'm agreeing with you about governments not giving aid, but I'm disagreeing with your rationale. You're saying that such aid increases poverty by increasing the local population to an extent that "the area" can't handle. Granted, you note that societal factors are relevant, but you also mention geography as if we can say of a place itself that it can only support so many people, which was why I talked about NYC and reiterated my emphasis on legal obstacles to wealth generation/prosperity.

Quote from: Frappe on March 16, 2007, 04:17:46 PM
So are you saying that if a million person population has access to resources that can support a half a million people, then there shall not be an expanded level of poverty?

If one put a million people into a cage with enough life-sustaining goods for half of them to live a full year at a subsistence level, more than half of them would be killed/die long before the end of the year from the hoarding and killing behavior that would ensue. This is what we see in failed states all over the world. 

I'm saying that the problem is the cage, not the number of people in it. Take away the cage, people would be free to move, to trade, to save and conserve, and all of them would and should survive and thrive. They would be free to create and trade for what their cage conditions had denied them. The limits of local resources would not be limits on their access to resources.

Quote from: Frappe on March 16, 2007, 04:17:46 PM
So you believe that there is a limitless number of people the world can support?


For all moral and practical purposes, the notion of imposing a population limit for "the world" is a disastrously bad idea. But it's also not even remotely an actual problem. Population does not grow geometrically, which was the faulty basis of overpopulation alarmists like Erlich. Moreover, empirically we see a causal link between increased wealth and slow-downs in birth rates. As the global trend towards liberalization continues, several predictable and unpredictable effects (increased life-spans, lower birth rates... impact of cloning? consciousness transferrence to "bodies" that can function forever?) will continue to play out, with population numbers fluctuating in ways only time will tell. No worries.

"...limitless number of people the world can support...."

From the perspective of a future human to whom "the world" clearly encompasses the Earth, the Moon, Mars, some moons of Jupiter and Saturn, not to mention Dyson spheres and man-made planets, your question might seem odd. Between now and then, as global markets ignite new waves of discovery and innovation, we have reasons to believe we're simply not going to come close to exhausting global resources.

Btw, astronomers just figured out that the frozen south pole of Mars contains enough water to cover the entire surface of the planet with water 35 feet deep. This will significantly facilitate expansion to that planet for our descendants. :-)   

Quote from: Frappe on March 16, 2007, 04:17:46 PM
So China is barking up the wrong tree with their population control measures?

Yes. Horribe govt., wrong tree.

China's population fell victim to the rigors of totalitarian socialism for most of the 20th century. Over 73 million people died in China between 1949 and 1987 as a direct result of Chinese governmental practices, approximately 50% of them in labor camps. 73,000,000. Gut-wrenching.   <Moment of silence.>

Regarding the one-child policy, by now you realize I consider it reprehensible. I think I heard that the Chinese govt. is backing away from it right now. Also, reformers there are trying to implement legal protections for private property rights (http://www.nytimes.com/2007/03/16/world/asia/16china.html). The current thug government of China wants to manage its people's inexorable embrace of freedom while still retaining as much power as possible, like the power to take its citizens' wealth without any legal consequences. Talk about a country on the brink.
