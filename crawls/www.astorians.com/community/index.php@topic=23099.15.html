The more things change...I worked at the pool in the mid and late sixties.  We had our share of trouble makers in those days too.  The pool was open from 10:00am til 10:00pm every day.  We also had a detail of 8-10 cops who worked there the whole summer and got to know the wise guys.  Our ace in the hole were the Parkside Gents, who the older Astorians will remember.  They were always willing to lend a hand if there was trouble brewing; things never got out of hand.
 We'd ask kids from Manhattan and Brooklyn why they came all the way to Astoria.  They told us the pool was so much nicer, and safer than Thomas Jefferson or Betsey Head pools in their neighborhoods.  In 67 or 68 Parks installed the lifeguard chairs that are there now. Before then, the lifeguards walked their beats.  The chairs cut down on the bs with the would be tough guys, as the guards were above the crowd.
