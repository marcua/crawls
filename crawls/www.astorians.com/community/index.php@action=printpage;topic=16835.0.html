Title: Re: new condos on Hoyt South
Post by: Christine on January 23, 2010, 07:30:04 PM
Dropped in to check the building out for ourselves today. We walked in and called the number advertised, and the owner was by within a couple of minutes.

He was gracious enough to give us the full tour at the last minute, knowing that we weren't likely to take something (just browsing.) Very nice guy, seemed to be on a friendly basis with the tenants we saw.

19 total units, both 2brs are taken. We saw the 1brs and penthouse studio. There are four different layouts to choose from for the 1brs (661-701sq ft.) Central rec areas, gym and laundry room which seem to facilitate social interaction, including rooftop access (landscaping in near future.)

Pros: Contemporary decor, hardwood floors, stainless steel kitchen appliances, hot water heat included. Cool views, I enjoy being able to see the Triboro and across to the train trestle, plus fun people-and-traffic watching opportunities. Ample windows and natural light throughout w/good noise insulation. Some great views of NYC skyline (Empire State Bldg.) High ceilings, stone countertops. Nice closets w/solid wood single-panel doors. Exterior doors, thermal pane w/weather-seal. Jaccuzi tubs. Nice hardware/fixtures. Kitchen appliances and countertops are beautiful. Small terraces, room for seating. Wired throughout for cable. Ample outlets and cabinet space in kitchen. Close to train. ADA compliant. Video security door buzzers (you can see who's buzzing.) Elevators. Parking available for extra cost.

Cons: Traffic noise (on Triboro side; tradeoff for the views), some face directly across from other apt windows, new building construction nearby might block views eventually. Small size- you can see the entire place while standing in the doorway. No pets allowed (could also be a "pro," depends!) Roof is not ADA accessible (not a con for everybody.) High price compared to local average.

Our overall impression: This is a sleek, friendly place that would suit high-earning single people who are not home much. The space is a little too small for couples IMO (at least, until the 2brs open up again.) Kinda like a vertical gated community for young people! I still wouldn't call it "luxury" due to the size and location, but I'm sure it's better than the Pistilli and other new bldgs in the area with tiny kitchens and less closet space. We were impressed with the attention to detail, which was refreshing. "Upscale contemporary" is more like it.
