Just thought that you might want to hear from someone who lives at the Pistilli building on 24th Ave. Yes, I did see all the negative threads on this place and I do understand where some folks are coming from. The materials used aren't the most high end, but given that the rent and even the purchase prices are really low, it doesn't concern me. We do enjoy living here. We love having a huge balcony, great closet space for low rent. We came from Manhattan, the Financial district, so I know what it's like to pay high rent. The kitchens are small I'll agree, but given that I spend all day at work and when I get home I whip up something really quick for dinner, the space is good for us. It's still comparable to what we had in the city and we had a huge place in the city. But if I wanted a jacuzzi apartment in the city I would have been paying more than my initial 4,200 a month. We currently live in a spacious two bedroom, two bath for 2050, which is an amazing savings given that we live in front of an amazing park, with a free pool in the summer, we have a balcony, we have a jacuzzi, we have a dishwasher, we have lots of closet space, we have a doorman until midnight, we live near the city (quick commute), we have plenty of parking in the area, we live near great restaurants, there's a great school for our son, they accept pets, it's a great neighborhood and community. Yes, like any building there are some things that I've called management to complain about, but they listened and addressed my issues. They may an initial runaround, but I think that most places do.

Too many folks complain about too many things without looking at the more important factors and at the bigger picture. Im not here to advertise for Pistilli, i'm sure he does fine without my opinions. I just mention this because it would be a shame for others that don't want to head out too deep into the suburbs and still want to enjoy a community like Astoria and miss out at an inexpensive opportunity to do so. Hey, we can all still go out to LI and get a house and upgrade our kitchens and bathrooms for thousands of dollars, but I think life is more that just if my counter top is made of granite or laminate. And yes, it might be cheaper in Woodside or Jackson Heights. But I'm here to stay.

You can ask me anything about the building if you want an inside take of it. I'm more than happy to share.
