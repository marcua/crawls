Quote
Actually never had it beforfe.  Tell me more.

You get a variety of mostly stewed dishes, beef, lamb, chicken, eggs, vegetables, etc. Somewhat spicy sometimes, in the way that say Persian food can be spicy, but not wicked hot or anything. The dishes are served in little piles on top of a spongy flatbread called njera, made from the teff grain. You also get additional njera, and you tear off chunks and use it to eat the meat and vegetables with your hands. Yum. My two favorite Ethiopian places are near Columbia. One's on Amsterdam and about 120th, and the other's on Amsterdam and I think about 107th. The latter's called Awash, but I don't remember the one higher up's name. Ghenet and Meskerem in the city are OK, but pricier, not as friendly, and not as good, in my opinion. Ah! Google says that there's a branch of Awash on 6th St., between 1st and 2nd Aves! Cool!
