I have a fairly high tolerance for chain restaurants... I think growing up here in Astoria, or even in NYC for that matter (especially in a family without a car) chain restaurants were somewhat of a novelty for me. Once I started driving and ventured out of the city, there was a whole world of food I'd never experienced! I remember when I had my first mozzarella stick -- such cheesy goodness! Never before had I tasted such deliciousness... a log of cheese? Battered? And deep fried? Wow. My arteries clog with excitement at just the thought.

Chain restaurants are safe food... relatively consistent, you know what to order, nothing is especially bad tasting, though none of it is especially good for you. Of all the chain restaurants, though, Applebees is the one I think sucks the most and has the least vegetarian options. They're always kind of sticky and dirty. Chili's, Outback, Olive Garden... all much better -- everything is relative (and yes, I know that Olive Garden is hardly real Italian... but  who can pass up the unlimited breadsticks and salad!).
