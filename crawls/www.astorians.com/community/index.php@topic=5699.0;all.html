The ONLY argument that can be made for 'all along the watchtower' is that it has started us thinking about Earth. What is it going to be like when they find it? What year is it on Earth? Will we recognise it? after all, why do colonists wear clothes like ours? What's up with that? (apart from easy wardrobe stuff!) they look heaps like our modern society, and yet the 'all of this has happened before and will happen again' stuff could suggest some antiquity. The greek/roman pantheon is curious too. And Baltar's storyline has suggested 'Jesus' to me more than once. Now he's got his own cult of women rushing him away to his new life.. Meanwhile, I believe the four who heard the music heard it because they have some link to the thirteenth tribe. I'm not sure if this means they are cylons or not. I'm kind of curious about the whole bulletheads into androids thing. If the bulletheads are incapable of telling them apart and have no real consciousness, but they were first - how did the new cylons come about? Who or what made them? Or were they made at all?? What if they're the thirteenth tribe? Or clones of them. Or some group of them sent back to watch, who had a schism between 5 and 6 (isn't it odd that now there are 12 cylons, and not 13? 13 tribes, is there a missing cylon?) about how to do so.. or the 6 discovered rebirthing and the others didn't..

Starbuck may well be the first human-cylon hybrid, not Hera. Her mother was in the first war. Her mother insisted on her 'destiny' from day one. Her father is completely absent. Her aptitude may be no normal human thing. Or perhaps, if my theory about android-cylons is correct, Starbuck is in fact a thirteenth tribe member as well. The signposts to Earth are awfully handy, Tyrrel's already shown his ability to recognise them (eye of jupiter), and the beginning of the music could have been in the maelstrom episode, which sent Starbuck into the mandala.

Anyway. We have a year to make s___ up. Woo!
