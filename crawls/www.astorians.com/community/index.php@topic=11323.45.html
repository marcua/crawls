Re: Get rid of the Nods

In general, I'm not bothered by the nod feature.  I absolutely do not find it to be a rude form of communication unless someone posted something completely neutral and unoffensive like "Today I am happy" and someone gave them a neg nod.  That would be clearly stupid and should be taken for what it's worth (especially if the post was in a "Are you happy?" thread).

I personally don't want to go through post after post of "I agree", or "what she said", or "I disagree".  I think that some people that are advocating for the nods to be done away with are trying to encourage a more lively debate or discussion but the reality of the situation is that maybe a poster summed it up so well that you don't have anything to add because you are in complete agreement.  So if you don't want the pos/neg nod feature then you also would not want the "I agree", "I disagree", "what he said"  posts.  So why then limit the thread to a discussion of a handful of people without inviting the masses to "vote", so to speak, on what position they are liking the best?  On the floor of some governmental bodies you take votes...members clap/don't clap, say "aye" or "nay", raise/or don't raise their hands, or otherwise vocalize/express a short form of agreement/disagreement.  What's the difference here if you agree or disagree with a particular post and really have nothing else to add?  After all, are you not, in essence, vocalizing your opinion to an audience?  People don't like tomatoes being thrown at them but they can't hold back a smile when the crowd roars....i get it.

As one of the moderators of this board, the other reality for me is that some members just don't know how to communicate without being rude and offensive.  If the goal is to promote intelligent dialogue then let's do it intelligently without all the attacking and name calling that sometimes goes down.  For me, the nod feature is also a way to express agreement/disagreement to an overly aggressive or inappropriate post without getting sucked into the nonsense of it all.  The group addresses the problem collectively without allowing the loose cannon to engage the majority of members in a senseless dispute.  

I'm also not sure what we gain in removing the anonymity of the feature.  Does it really make a difference?  So you know who agreed or disagreed with you...what's the importance?  

Notwithstanding any of the above, I'm totally open to changing my opinion on the nod feature.
