Is there? I've done searches on here and have only stumbled on a single post about Michael Angelo's back in a 2005 threat that mentions it briefly. Oh well, guess my board searching skills have gotten rusty.

Thank YOU for writing it! We were quite glad to have read that post when we were enjoying the pizza there! Their non-pizza items are quite good, though not to the measure of the pizza, IMHO. However, their gnocchi is delicious - I definitely recommend that. Plus, of course, the veal if you have a large appetite!  :-D They were quite nice with us when we were there too - the four of us were having trouble settling on an appetizer, so the chef offered to make something special for us. It was the most amazing eggplant I've ever had in my lift and zuchini that I could actually eat - as could my boyfriend who hates almost all vegetables. Of course, we still ended up getting two other appetizers because it all sounded so good (there was waaaaaay to much food for us at the end), and in the end were quite pleased. And drunk - after we bought multiple bottles of wine they brought out complimentary sambuca for us too! Oh, and the homemade desserts are really quite good. I don't like tiramasu and even I could eat some of it. I'm a big fan of the cannoli there, though. Okay, must stop drooling over their food at 7:30 in the morning!

Hi All.....I'm a 3rd generation Italian here in Astoria and I've seen many pizzerias come and go.
For the longest time, Rose & Joe's was THE place for thin crust Sicilian pizza. But I recently had
a slice and something has definitely changed.  The sauce and the cheese just didn't seem to have
the sharp delicious flavor that had made it so famous.

So - (as much as I love Rose & Joe's bakery), for pizzia, I recommend The Last Stop Cafe on
31st Street btw. 23rd Ave & Ditmars.  I'd like to know if others have tried it.[/font]
