Oh my gosh, I love this. A whole thread about supermarkets (with a good dose of L&O). I used to be the biggest supermarket snob back in Texas. In college I was poor as dirt but I wouldn't shop the HEB near campus because it was so trashed out and run down. So instead I went to Albertsons (the NICE/slightly more expensive store in town).

But true supermarket heaven (for me) is the Central Market on North Lamar in Austin. Oddly enough, Central Market is owned/associated with HEB. But it was a world apart... think of a hybrid between Whole Foods and a local greenmarket and a Fairway -- so there was extensive selection of organic and fresh stuff with all of the "regular" grocery store stuff as well. Massive produce section, cooking displays, extensive meat and seafood, and a huge bakery. Plus an attached outdoor cafe that overlooks a park and playground where they had LIVE MUSIC during the warmer months (which is 3/4 of the year in Austin). Cooking classes, amazing wine section with lots of good staff reccomendations, etc. Plus their newsletter is called the "Foodie"! The only down side was finding a parking space on weekends. We'd get really creative when it came to that.

Okay now I'm just making myself homesick. I miss Central Market so much!
