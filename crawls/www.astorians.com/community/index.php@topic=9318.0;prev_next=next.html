While I don't run outside in the cold (I wait till springtime), I have noticed a lot more people running outside in Astoria during the winter.  That said, yes, the drivers around here are notorious for being reckless.  From what I've seen, this is especially true of Livery cab drivers.  I have seen livery cabs cut off joggers, bicyclist, even people with baby carriages.  They simply don't care.  The road and corners are for them, or so they believe.  But of course, this type of behavior is definately not limited to the cabbies.

Problem is, the cops can't be everywhere.  And since there are now fewer cops in the area, drivers believe they can get away with a lot more.  Honestly, even when I'm jogging outside in the summer, I stay on the sidewalk.  Yes, the concrete is not as forgiving as asphalt is on the knees.  However, the concrete under my feet is a lot more forgiving than a fender comming towards my kneecaps.

Just my .02.

Some tips on running in Astoria:

1.  I see runners running in the street on the asphalt, especially on 20th Ave in the bike lane.  Yeah, I know the asphalt is way easier on the legs than running on the concrete sidewalk.  Forget that, use the sidewalk.  Drivers usually don't give a crap about you in the bike lane, and all it takes to wipe you out is someone yapping on the cell phone, playing with the GPS, etc. etc. etc.   It's not worth it; stay on the sidewalk.

2.  Astoria Track.  Nice track, easy on the legs.  Like one of the other posters said, they don't enforce the rules as much as they should and you will see 1. Dog walkers  2. Rollerblades  3. Strollers  4.  Bikes  5.  People walking 3 or 4 abreast   

3.  Astoria Park.  Lots of paths, watch for the dog walkers with the 50 foot leashes

4.  Along the river.  In the winter, not bad.  In the summer, crowded.

5.  Around the Park.  OK, but if you stay on those little dirt paths by the pool, watch out for the tree roots.

6.  The Streets of Astoria.  Pay attention to sidewalk cracks and when you come to an intersection you had better expect someone to pull right into the crosswalk whether they see you or not.

7.  Best place to run (I'll give you the mileages if you want):  go over the Triborough Bridge (you get on it at 27th Street) and run over the bridge to Ward's Island.  Once you get on the bridge, you're set to go, especially when you go on the main span and are running above the traffic.  Great views.  Be careful on windy days.  You get over the bridge and can run all over Ward's and Randall's.  On April 1, they start lowering the pedestrian bridge over to Manhattan and you can run over there.  For the girls and women (whatever the age divide is), you might want to be a bit careful when you get near the pedestrian bridge, there are sometimes some (since I don't want to offend anyone) I'll say, "shady" people that may cause you worry hanging around that area.

8.  Other places to run:  Vernon Blvd.  Not bad, not bad.  Go past Costco up to, what, 36th Ave (Roosevelt Island) or continue on down to LIC Bar.  Not a bad route, on Sundays, it's great - no traffic!  Trucks on the weekdays, so you get the diesel, but very little crosstraffic if you stay on the river side.  If you want to go crazy, when you get to the 59th Street Bridge, make a left and go all the way down to where you get on the bridge and then go over to Manhattan.  It's a pretty long slog up the bridge, but you get rewarded coming down the other side.  Go really crazy and get on the path at 61st and go up to the ped bridge and then back to Ward's to Triborough and back to Astoria.  You can also go all the way down to the Pulaski and into Brooklyn and along Kent Ave, I think it is.  Do this on a Sunday.  Or, go over to Roosevelt and do some loops, or run around Smallpox Hospital.

9.  Big Dream #1:  When St. Michael's Cemetery allows running.

10.  Big Dream #2:  Open track at Icahn Stadium.

11.  Big Dream #3:  Open track at the Catholic School on 21st and 27th.

12.  Big Dream #4:  Can I be Client #10?
