Best Chicken Wings in Astoria?
« on: November 15, 2010, 08:10:39 PM »
Actually, what I really want to know is who has good chicken wings (aka - Buffalo Wings) near the Ditmars train station, since that's where I live.  However, I did a search and didn't find a previous thread on "best chicken wings" on the board.  So I figured, why not post a thread and see what people have to say over what they've found in the great Astoria/L.I.C area.  I mean, why limit the topic to a small area?

I know that, usually, wings are bar food.  However, truth be told, my bar days are over as I think it's in poor taste to go into a bar with kids in tow...which is usually how I roll these days.  So since I'm not in the bar scene around here, I don't know what the local "adult beverage establishments" have to offer in terms of buffalo wings.  I mean, there have to be some wings that are crummy, most are probably "ok", and a few may be exceptional.  I just don't know who offers what.

So please feel free to post on this thread.  All input will be greatly appreciated.  Please, let the opinions on wings fly (crappy pun intented).

The best options in Astoria are McCann's or Dillinger's. Neither is mind-blowing, but they're good.  Someone from Texas told me that Wingstop (which originated in Texas) has great wings (but I don't know if a Texan trying to tell me about great wings is like a New Yorker trying to tell me about great barbecue).  Their Steinway St. location has been in the works for about a year now.

If you don't want to hang at McCann's with the kiddos - you can get them as takeout. (The wings, that is.  The kids are extra.)
