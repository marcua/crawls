Anyone else been to Kitaku - the new sushi place on 23rd?

We went on Friday night and weren't too impressed.  When we saw the fish menu, we had great hopes for a good quality sushi place near us, but we were pretty dissapointed.  Quality of the fish (the Uni and Ikura was so, so and possibly a bit old which is NOT a good sign) and sizes of the portions were meager considering the prices (thin, not very big slices - hard to actually taste the fish).  The only thing we had that got high marks was the Age Dashi Dofu and the Vegetable tempura apps.  We also tried the Tom Yung Gung , but I didn't think it was worth getting again - the broth was not very spicy and they put fake crab into it, which fell apart and threw off the taste and texture.

We're not going to totally write them off just yet.  We're hoping they'll realize what's going wrong and work out the kinks.  We'll go again in a couple of weeks and see how things are going.  Until then, I guess we'll continute going to Watawa as our local joint.

Zeke

Personally, I have been going to Kitaku nearly once a week throughout the fall.  I can't speak to the sashimi, since I haven't tried it, but I have thoroughly enjoyed everything that I have eaten there:  every appetizer and every house specialty roll on the menu, plus a few of the standard rolls.  There is a mango lobster specialty roll that is particulary delightful.

By comparison, I recently tried JJs Asian Fusion, and actually preferred the sushi at Kitaku.  (Not sure about comparisons to Watawa; I haven't been there since it opened a few years ago)

The wait staff and sushi chefs have also been extraordinarily kind at every sitting.

And honestly, I don't really get the gripes from early on in this thread about their prices.  I have always felt the pricing was actually very reasonable.

Last point:  their chocolate cake is outrageously good.
