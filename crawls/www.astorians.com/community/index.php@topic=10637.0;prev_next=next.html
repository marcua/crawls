beer gouging
« on: June 12, 2006, 09:45:59 AM »
on thursday night my wife and i stopped by the beer garden for a late evening beer. the crowd was lively and pleasantly sparse. as we've done so many times before we produced $8 ($10 with tip) for two mugs of beer.

against my better judgement, we went again yesterday to catch a little world cup action. arriving at 230pm we found plenty of seats; although not with the greatest viewing angle. once situated, i went indoors to wait on line for 20 minutes for a pitcher of beer. i was stunned when he said it would be $14 (previously $12). single beers are now $5.

does anyone know if this is a permanent price increase that coincides with the world cup or if it is indeed price gouging during the world cup? i would have asked myself but there was no room for idle chit chat amongst the thirsty.

please say the days of the $4 draft are not limited in queens.
