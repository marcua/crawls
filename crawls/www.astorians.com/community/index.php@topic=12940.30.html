That's exactly what I've been saying.  It's easy for these landlords to be greedy and sit back and wait for some (fool) to come along and take their ridiculous asking price.  In the meantime, however, you have a situation where:

1. Many stores are vacant
2. Due to the vacancies, there's less traffic - vehicular and pedestrian - to your area
3. The many vacant stores give any new prospective business a choice of where to move in, and the opportunity to haggle and lower rents (assuming the landlords want to budge, which they often don't)
4. The area as a whole begins to look less attractive to new businesses, as many empty storefronts and reduced traffic serve as obvious warning signs that the area isn't in the best of economic health.
