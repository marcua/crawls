The Sox picked up a game last night as the Rockies shut down the Yankees.   It shows you have tough it will be for the Yankees to cut that lead.   The Yanks recently won nine in a row and are still nine games out.   A big disadvantage for the Yankees is that they don't play the Sox very much the rest of the way.

Beckett has won 10 and has to be on the short list for the American League Cy Young.   That trade that brought Beckett over from the Marlins is one of those trades that may benefit both teams.    The Sox gave up their top prospect, Henley Ramirez, a shortstop, who played well for the Marlins last year.   The pitcher the Sox also gave the Marlins has pitched well.   His name is Alay Solar I think and he threw a no-no last year as a Rookie.

Meanwhile, I noticed in the box score last night that David Ortiz made a rare start at 1B after sitting out Monday's game.   The Sox get crucified in these interleague games when they can't use the DH because they have to sit Big Papi, Youkalis or Lowell. 

Hopefully, Coca Crisp is coming around.   He hit two homers in that Monday night loss.  He's been a bust since the Sox shipped another top prospect, Marte, who they got from the Braves for Edgar Renteria, who bombed in his one year in Boston.   J.D. Drew, a real curious off-season free-agent signing, has also not exactly lit the world on fire.   And in the Sox revolving door of shortstops, Julio Lugo has flirted with the Mendoza line this year.    Hopefully, these guys will get untracked.

What is up with BoSox and their shortshops?   Since Garciaparra was traded in '04, the Sox have had Orlando Cabrera, Renteria, Alex Gonzalez and now Lugo.   

Also, Schilling, after his near no-no two starts ago, has bombed in his last two starts and has been sent back to Boston to have his arm checked.     Hopefully, all will be well.    The Sox have Jon Lester waiting in the wings and they could always move Papelbon into the rotation and make that "other" Japanese pitcher, I forget his name, the closer.
