What kind of building do you live in?  Is it a two or three family house?  Or do you live in an apartment building that may be rent stabilized?  This may effect some of your rights regarding your situation.  At face value, it doesn't seem like you should have to live with some funky orange mold. 

You should try to consult a housing/tenant attorney.  In some cases, tenants can withhold rent and put it in escrow until conditions in an apartment are fixed.  I am NOT an attorney, and am not suggesting you do this.  It's merely an option in some cases.  Watch the landlord get the hole fixed once the rent isn't coming in!

Law Help's website provides listings of legal agencies that assist with various civil issues.  Only kicker is that you often times must have a moderate to low income to qualify.  You may want to check them out anyway - http://lawhelp.org/NY/, click on the "Housing" option.  The New York City Bar can also provide legal advice, but again you need to have a limited income - http://www.abcny.org/Public/index.htm.

Good Luck!
