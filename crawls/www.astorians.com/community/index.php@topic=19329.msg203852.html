Re: Astoria NW building @ 21st Street
« Reply #10 on: October 15, 2010, 01:33:49 PM »
So I have lived there since Sept 7th, probably one of the first people to move in. Overall the apartments are nice, I pay about $2300 for a one bedroom with price for Parking garage included. However I'm not impressed at all with how the apartment was not clean or ready on my move in date. I just got blinds last week, all the items that I wanted fixed are still not repaired. The painting was done badly, so I had to ask to repaint.

keep in mind, I was one of the first people to move in, so that might have to do a lot with it. But one the things that I was highly disappointed with, was that I thought the leasing agents were nice and I was comforted by the fact that they were in the building. Come to find out, once you sign your lease, they have nothing to do with you!! They are just sales, separate than Criterion, which is who handles basically everything else. :-( and they are not as helpful, accessible or nice.

Also it is a walk to the N/Q but there is a bus that will take you there.

I'm in the D line, the room is good size with two closets, but the living room though a good size, doesn't have enough wall room, so the furniture is odd to setup I think.

The Parking Garage is small, and hard to park and get out of.

The gym is fine, I used it a couple of times.

Overall I think they are getting better and I like it.

Quote from: rene0226 on October 16, 2010, 12:43:08 PM
How's the noise level?  A lot of these new buildings have thin walls and you have to listen to your neighbors' televisions or music.

My apartment faces 21st street. Street noise can be loud during day but I don't really mind.  With windows closed, I hear almost nothing.  They are double glass and that certainly makes a difference.  At night, I hear some movement over the bridge but just a bit and, again, almost nothing with windows closed.  The apartment next to me is occupied and I've yet to hear anything.  I don't hear anything above or below either.  I'm not one to notice but I honestly haven't heard a peep.  Lots of young professionals who work long hours, couples also working long days, and some families (with small children). The apartments aren't really big enough to accommodate more than 1 child (there are no 3 bedroom to my knowledge), so I've yet to see more than a few.

Building staff has been extremely accommodating. There were a couple of issues when we moved in (small things like a toilet part that needed to be replaced). They were at my place within 1 hour of reporting it.  I kid you not!  I've lived in other ammenity buildings and have not had such luck!
