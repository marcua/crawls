First off, this is a terrible tragedy, and we all hate to see it happen in our own neighborhood... such a shame.

Secondly, I think we need to curb knee-jerk reactions calling for closing down of shore blvd. To be honest, when I have been there walking my dog late at night, it's the fact that people are around that makes me feel safe. While I do think the loud music and cars/bikes etc does pose a problem, I'm pretty sure that there is only one obvious solution: More police, and not just that, but more police who give a sh*t
I have seen cops drive by cars blasting music at unreasonable levels at 2AM there and just keep going like there isn't a problem with that. I'm ok with people being around at whatever hour of the night, it's there prerogative to do so. If police aren't there and enforcing, however, people can just get away with whatever they please without fear of consequence. If there were a noted police presence there, and people in the area didn't feel like they could get away with doing whatever, this incident may have been preventable.

I love Astoria, and even in the midst of this, I still feel safe walking around alone here at night- it's part of why I love this neighborhood so much. I hope this incident is a kick in the arse for local authorities to serve as a reminder that cops aren't parking ticket dispensers, and that their job first and foremost is to keep our citizens safe. The only way that can happen is if cops are around and they give a sh*t about what they do.
