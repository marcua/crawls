Also, people who have property to rent are used to dealing with renting to new tenants as a regular part of their business, while those selling a home do so infrequently - sellers need more professional help.

Quote from: Bentleys Dad on March 19, 2008, 07:26:05 PM
When you say "marching this way", do you say this because of the cost of the rents going up or because that is what people will pay regardless of the fact that they can negotiate the fee? Or try going directly to the management company (if there is one).
I just meant as such practices become more common in the more pricey/competitive boroughs it's not a stretch of the imagination to expect they will be here soon if/when demand continues to go up for apartments. I paid a month's rent fee 3 years ago for my current spot and at that time was starting to see 10/12% fees being asked for... which I didn't see a few years prior when I had looked (and ended up in Sunnyside).

As for going directly to companies, in many cases that's at least a little risky (in terms of losing the place) as some brokers nowadays make you sign docs before they'll show you anything stating you can't do exactly that -- make a separate deal with a property they show you (at least in Manhattan, likely elsewhere). Of course getting busted is probably not all that common, but it's not like it hasn't occurred to brokers either... and of course negotiation is always an option, but that of course depends on leverage, which if you're trying to get a good deal on a good place you generally don't have in this city -- they generally ain't gonna have trouble renting out a good place for a good price, and if you don't want to pay it's not hard to find someone else who will.
