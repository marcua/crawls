Trader Joe's is NOT about produce.  As I see it, the produce is the weakest side of the store and I'm still mystified as to why they keep it up. 

Trader Joe's has a lot of the things you can find at Whole Foods.  But for way, way less (price-wise).  I'm talking nuts, dried fruit, granola, dutch process chocolate chips, yeast, canned organic beans, cheese (no, it's nowhere near Murray's, never fear!), salsa, nut butters, Valrhona chocolate, Cliff bars, and brown rice.  These are staples I get there.  Of course they have some "bad" stuff - nacho cheese dip, frozen buffalo wings, and brownie mix.  They get interesting juices in, too (I'm a big fan of the apricot juice in the box).  And now they will have wine here, and at least in CA it wasn't all swill (but I'm leery of 2 buck chuck, I must say).  Trader Joe's is REALLY GOOD for post concert receptions and the like!  They are also one of the top sellers in the state for certified organic products.

I first remember TJs about 20 years ago.  My grandfather took me there and we bought almond butter.  I thought, "almond butter??!?!?!  how wierd!"  But now I love it!  And a jar for $3.99 sure beats it for $8.

I also like the fact that they don't seem to take themselves so seriously.  Love the Fearless Flyer.  And they do exchanges/returns without an attitude.

I will also put in my disclaimer regarding my feelings about Whole Foods, that I've never truly been comfortable shopping there.  But that is because they were in competition with two AMAZING groceries in Berkeley, where I became aware of accessibility to products of this kind.  Oh how I miss the Monterey Market and Berkeley Bowl, true wonderlands of food!  Especially Berkeley Bowl, OMG.  It's up there with the SF Ferry Building Farmer's Market.  And Monterey Market had produce curiosities and incredible prices on produce, not to mention Food Mill peanut butter bars!!!  Whole Foods couldn't hold my interest with that kind of competition and fascination.  So, I never developed the WF/WP shopping pattern.  Trader Joe's, on the other hand, was unlike either of those (BB or MM).  And the TJs out here aren't a cookie cutter layout of their stores in the West.  It's interesting - each of their stores I've been to feels very different.

I, for one, welcome our Trader Joe's overlords.
