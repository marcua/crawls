Personally, I would never hand a broker cash. Putting aside the fact that I just flat out don't trust brokers in general, there is no reason to not accept a bank check. A bank check can be cashed on the spot, and it leaves a paper trail. This way, you have a paper record of the monetary transaction- whether it be for taxes, records or a deal gone sour.

IMO, if that were me, and I could avoid paying in cash, I would really prefer to pay via check, bank check or money order.

I've paid a broker cash before - it was either that or bank check.  And considering the damn bank wanted another $30 just to issue me a certified check, I went with the cash.  I have never been more nervous on the way from the bank to the broker's office with that much cash on me.  I hated how nervous it made me feel, as if I was walking around with a big sign on my forehead, saying "Rob me!!"  But I did make sure I got a receipt from the landlord and the broker,  and I had her cell phone and office number.  I also made sure that I could pay by check after that first influx of money, because there's no way I should have to walk around with that much money all the time, just to pay the rent.  I had them stipulate that the stove and other repairs would be done before I moved in, and since I had her phone number, I was able to call and speak with her to make sure it was done.

I've lived in a lot of apartments over the years and I've paid by both cash and bank check and I prefer the bank check because like Daisy, it made me nervous to walk around with that much cash on me and as casicua said, it's guaranteed money.

If a broker or landlord won't accept a bank check I'd walk away. That's not to say they aren't legit but it's a warning sign they may be difficult to deal with and if a bank check is a problem in my opinion it will be only the first of many.

A legitimate broker or landlord will always give you a receipt for any monies exchanged. So for an initial deposit (i.e., a couple hundred bucks to hold an apartment, if required) I think a receipt is fine but I would never, ever hand over the rest of the security deposit, 1st month's rent or broker's fee without signing a lease and getting the keys.

For me, I wouldn't sign a lease for an apartment undergoing extensive renovations unless there's a back-up plan in place in case the work overruns. Do you have a place to live if it overruns a week? two weeks? a month? What about storing your stuff? It all depends on the amount of work being carried out but it's risky in my opinion.
