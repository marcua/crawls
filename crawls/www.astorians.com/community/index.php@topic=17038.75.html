That Woolworth's picture brings back nice memories of my early days growing up at 23-20 32nd St. South of 23rd Ave. in the 1940's and 50's.

A strange situation was that there was a McLellan's five and ten cents store on the next street heading East on Ditmars that did about as well as Woolworth's even though they both sold basically the same products.

Woolworth's had booths where you could listen to records before you bought them, even if you were a
kid who obviously did not have any money to buy anything.

There was a NYPL branch on 31st St. just North of Woolworth's that had a lady who read stories to young kids on Saturday mornings. The Southeast corner opposite Woolworth's housed a store called Famous Fashion which was a very popular women's clothing shop.

The whole shopping area of 31st from 23rd Ave to Ditmars Ave. was referred to as "Ditmars" in addition to those stores actually on the avenue.
 
My Mom worked at the Woolworth's and it is where she met my Father.
