There's one key difference between the two posts you reference and Bill's.

That is that we've had an ongoing issue with Waltz posting dupes and advertising on the board without contributing to the community dialog, and we had messaged them about it on more than one occasion.

While the above two users did indeed post links to their blogs (perhaps in bad taste), it was far from an ongoing issue, and different than just plain business advertising.

I don't think the rule of thumb for this board is too hard to understand. There is a tolerable ammount of self-promotion that is okay, but there comes a point where it crosses the line and just becomes spamming.

I think there's a distinction between posters that simply use the board to spam or self-promote (i.e. the Waltz/Bill posting series), and a regular contributer and participant to the board who announces their personal website once among other posts (see the posts of the two you linked two who mentioned their blog.) Even so, Walt'z posts were self-censored, not deleted by me or anyone else. I'd say that's pretty open.

"Open discussion" does not mean anarchy or free for all for me. You may be comfortable allowing a community board to have more blatent advertising on it (i.e. look like a "classified" bulletin board for "listings", or look like craigslist), but this board is structured more for conversation rather than classifieds or advertisements. "Open discussion" is what this board is about, spam is not the same as conversation.

If you have a hard time telling the difference, or you think our rule of thumb is too difficult to understand, please feel free to share your thoughts either here or in a private message.
