Speaking of a B&N and Steinway...the former Dr. Jay's would make a fantastic location for a B&N or Borders or any bookstore.  Definitely not happening, however.

This discussion has taken place here before, but Steinway has fallen into a vicious cycle: high rents have led to vacancies...those vacancies have reduced foot and vehicular traffic to Steinway...that, in turn, makes it a less attractive option for chains to open up shop on Steinway, as they do tend to perform viability studies prior to deciding whether or not to open a new location.  I think Astoria's proximity to Manhattan also doesn't help either, since it seems to be assumed that Astorians can just hop on the train and do their shopping easily in the city.
