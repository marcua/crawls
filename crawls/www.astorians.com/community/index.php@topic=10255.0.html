Two questions:

1) I'd like some suggestions for food that's easy to carry to the beach, if you're taking mass transit.  I want to carry food that's light weight but filling, and not messy or smelly, since I'm going to be carrying it in my backpack, and preferably ready made that you can just buy at the grocery store on your way.  (A grocery store in Astoria, not the over-priced beach communities.)

What do carry when you go to the beach, especially when you go by mass transit?  No matter how I try to pare down, I always feel like I'm carrying too much.

I also drive to the beach but still try to pack light food wise.  I would suggest wraps, pasta salads, fresh cut fruit and nuts to nosh on (not necessarily all of those things of course).  All are pretty much no mess and easy to contain.  No idea what a friggin Sigg was till I clicked on your link.  Bring a bottle or 2 of water or whatever so when you finish it you can trash it and carry less on the way back home.

We take the LIRR out to Long Beach. I do grilled chicken salad. I get the packages of the
Perdue grilled chicken strips and do a salad in one of those ziplock containers. When we get
to Woodside we pick up fruit at this great Korean produce market on 61st before we catch
the LIRR. And of course 2 -3 big bottles of water. That's for myself and my boyfriend.

We find that's enough for us. The salads only take a couple of minutes to make the night
before and I have a couple of soft coolers that the containers and the fruit fit into.
I put them and the towels in a tote bag and carry that w/my beach chair.

This might sound like a lot to carry to some, but Long Beach doesn't offer much on the
boardwalk and I'm not a hot dog, chips, etc. person. We like to eat light and healthy and
this is filling while also being a lot cheaper than buying stuff out there.
