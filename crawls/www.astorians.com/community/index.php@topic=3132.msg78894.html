one more link ... when I browsed a few sites on cleansing/fasting i saw comments about feelings that reminded me of the response to anorexia that was described in this article:

http://www.slate.com/id/2164436/entry/2164437/
Quote
More importantly, once someone has been restricting food for a certain amount of time, she discovers advantages, not appearance-related, in the way that her mind and body respond to starvation. After this point, the anorexic is motivated by a cost-benefit analysis that sounds very much like the calorie restrictor's: "This is worth the effort/pain/fighting with my parents, because of X benefit." In my case, there were several noticeable pluses. Being hungry gave me energy. It helped me focus on my work. It made me feel emotional and creative in a way that I enjoyed. At the time, in fact, I believed I was eating as I did much more for the intellectual and creative edge it gave me than to look thin.

Now I understand what happened better: My starvation triggered the release of endorphins. In evolutionary terms, this is designed to give humans heightened coping powers in an emergency. In my case, it gave me a sense of well-being and made me feel sharp and energetic. This feeling became an addiction, so that I pursued it even as my tolerance level rose and the same amount of endorphins no longer produced the same effect, at which point I became quite depressed.

Hang in there.  You'll be glad you did. .

Thanks for the support MagicP. 

For those of you who seemed concerned about this NOT being a good way to lose weight - I didn't go into it (and it's not billing itself) as a way to lose weight.  However - weight loss is a very measurable thing and I can't see any other side effects right now... so the weight loss is inspiring.  I realize that if I go back to my regular eating habits when I stop this I will put the weight back on.

However - one of my big reasons for doing this is to find out what's the deal with my daily stomach aches.  So far no food = no aches.  Now its a matter of introducing food back into my system and finding out what causing the problems. 

Also - the weight loss is pretty good incentive and jump start to get back to eating properly a la South Beach.  I think keeping sugar and coffee out of my diet (or at least to a minimum) seems like a pretty good idea.  Knocking those items out as well as everything else has been much easier for me by doing a NO FOOD regimen rather than actually eating carefully while on a diet like South Beach. 

As for the other side effects... increased energy, clearing skin issues... I'm not seeing it.  Though I do look pretty good if I may say so myself.  MC or end of the school year happiness or new hair product or 5k training or or or... so many possibilities. 

I certainly don't think I'm hurting myself by doing this.  It's been an interesting week of food avoidance, food discussion avoidance, food picture (moving and still) avoidance.  If you know me beyond this board - you know that these are all very difficult things to avoid in my home. 

And then there's something else to look forward to - when its all over we're celebrating at Pure Food.  Yum
