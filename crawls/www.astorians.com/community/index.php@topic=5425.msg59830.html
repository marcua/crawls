I'd buy an apartment with a nice plot of outdoor space, and get my car back up here, and I'd have a garage space for it. I love astoria dearly - truly I do - but if money were no option I'd buy a place in the west village. Wandering the streets over there feels like being in a fairy tale. I'd go places a lot on the weekend. Like fly down to Austin or to my dad's place in Louisiana. Just wherever I feel like going.

I'd pay off my brother and sister-in-law's house, and put aside money for their kids' college fund. Buy my mom a condo in Austin. Give my dad the funds to renovate my grandparents house, plus buy him a nice new pickup truck. Pay off my friend's debts. I would eat sushi like 3 days a week. I would take lots of cabs. I would go see broadway shows and plays, and go see the opera. Replace almost all the clothes and shoes in my closet with new ones. I would buy this sofa and reupholster it in a fabulous fabric. Pay for me and my gal-pals from college to go to Greece (was waiting to do this for my honeymoon, but forget that).

And, surprisingly, keep working in New York - at least for the time being. But when the time was right, I'd sell the village apartment and use that money to buy a bungalow in Austin. Eeeee.

Oh yeah, and put some in savings. I've always wondered what that wealth management service is that advertises on NPR. Maybe I'd give those folks a call.
