You can set up an email address to use only with your building's management.  Nobody says you have to use one email address for all your correspondence.  Your real concern would be paying your rent/whatever bill online.  You certainly wouldn't want to allow them to make automatic deductions from your account.
 
One big advantage to paying rent online, usually ONLY applying to a management company that lets you use a credit card for payment, is that if your credit card gives you rewards, especially airline miles or something else that you value, you could rack up a lot of points annually just paying your rent.  So, you would first check with your credit card (or one that you would be applying for) to make sure they award rewards and make sure how the payment to building management has to be processed (some cards say the payment has to be billed as a purchase, some cards will give the same reward for paying a bill that's a service), then find out if management will let you pay by credit card (because most debit cards don't offer point rewards) and if they charge a fee for paying by credit card, and make sure any fees from management and from the credit card itself come to a total that still makes sense to you to use the card in this manner.


As for e-mailing rent invoices, it may be a cost cutting move (no paper, no postage), an effort to prove items were sent and on time, and if you pay online they get their money quicker.
