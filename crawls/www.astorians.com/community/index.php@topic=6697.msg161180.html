Actually, you are almost correct. The site where PC Richard is now, and the surrounding 6–8 block area was once the Borden Milk Farm. The cows roamed the fields until milking time. I remember as a little girl when the farm was sold and the developers divided the property. Borden held a HUGE party for Astorians. They gave out gifts and horse rides. They served 'soda pop' and goodies to all the kids and their parents. We all got our pictures taken with (I believe her name was) Elsie The Cow.

One of my most memorable things was that Borden sold most of their heavy cream to the Willner Family who owned the shop next door to what is now Frank's Bakery on the corner of 30th Avenue and 36th Street—it used to be a Kosher deli—and the Willner's churned the cream into butter. They also sold fresh eggs. Right from the chickens. They were graded by weight. Mama used to send me up to Willner's for fresh churned butter to be put into Mama's crystal butter dish, and the eggs were put into a wire basket which I had to bring from home. Although the Willner family were Orthodox Jewish folks, they sold cracked eggs at Easter time for a penny apiece so that they could be colored for Easter baskets.
