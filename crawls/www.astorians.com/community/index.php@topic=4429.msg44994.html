I thought I would get this in while there's still time (one candle left!) - Happy Chaka-khan-ukah! 

I ate about thrity latkes on the first night, so I've sworn off them till next year.

Also, my Bet Hillel/Bet Shammai menorah broke, so I was kind of bummed (my wife fixed it, but it's just not the same if you don't have it for the first night).  To explain: in the Talmud, the students of Shammai and the students of Hillel disagree about how to light the menorah; the former argue that 8 candles should be lit on the first night, seven on the second night, and so on, while the latter argue that we should light 1 on the first night, 2 on the second, etc. (which is our current practice).  I made a menorah with two sets of candle holders so that I could light candles according to both methods (but I put the candle holders too close to each other, so the relatively intense heat given off by the collective conflagration melts the puny hanukah candels in about three minutes).  But at any rate, it broke.
