Finally tried this place and I have to say that I am, sadly, disappointed.  I live less than 2 blocks away and have been really excited about its opening - it's taken me so long because I just haven't been in a wine mood lately.  That being said here are my comments:

1.  It's GORGEOUS!  It's so beautiful - it's so totally different than anything else here in Astoria.  I'm very impressed with the atmosphere.
2.  We decided to get a bottle of white but I didn't want anything sweet - so I asked the waitress for advice.  While I wasn't expecting the expertise of a sommelier I think it's reasonable to expect the wait staff to really KNOW the characteristics of the very small wine list they have.  She seemed confused by my question and could only recommend 2 out of 13 white wines that wouldn't be sweet.  Really?  Out of 13 whites only 2 weren't sweet?  And here's the kicker:  the wine she recommended that we chose was SUPER fruity - not sweet, really, but over the top fruity - really should have been told that before hand.
3.  We ordered 2 apps and a mixed platter of meats and cheese. 
       a.  App 1 was bacon wrapped prunes.  I had these as a mise en bouche in France last year and loved them so much I make them at my dinner parties now.  These were good - my bf said he liked mine better.  I would recommend these even though they didn't knock my socks off.
       b.  App 2 was Tiger shrimp in garlic and pepper sauce.  WOW.  That sounds good doesn't it?  It really wasn't that great.  First off, they weren't tiger shrimp - too small for that.  Secondly, the sauce was basically watered down wing sauce - I KID YOU NOT.  I think there were MAYBE 3 shrimp on the plate (covered with a lot of garnish) and this cost $10!  There was also a sliced roll for dipping and it tasted freezer-burnt.
       c.  The platter.  They have these meat and cheese platters for $4 to $6 each and you can combine any 3 for $15.  We ordered smoked duck, maple ham, and a French cheese called Petit Basque.  Everything on the platter tasted great.  The smoked duck was sublime, the ham was sliced perfectly (I believe thin slicing enhances the ham tasting experience), and the cheese was quite nice.  HOWEVER, for $15 I was shocked by the small portions.  There were only 2 very, very small slices of cheese on the platter - they were about the size of a small tortilla chip in dimensions.  Is this worth $6 (the price of the cheese plate by itself)?  The cheese was good but that was ridiculous. 

I think this place is beautiful and I think they have some worthy foods.  I would not recommend this place to anyone watching their budget because I don't think it's a good value.  If you have a fair amount of disposable income to blow on a night out to try a pretty place I say go for it.  Otherwise, I don't think I'll be talking this place up.
