Two guys with guns at Astoria Blvd
« on: September 23, 2006, 01:36:39 AM »
So I was walking home from the Astoria Blvd train station tonight around 1am, going across the elevated walkway with my friend, when a guy speeded by, weaving urgently through everybody as if going towards some big emergency, and I immediately saw that he had a gun in his right hand facing down. Then another guy followed suit, running by with a gun in his right hand too.

Bizarre. I've never seen handguns in plain sight like that before, held by people in plain clothes running by. Made me feel uncomfortable, and I held back so as not to be near guys with guns.

Could they have been police in plain clothes? Or what else could it have been? Anyone hear anything? My friend said she'd heard sirens right before we saw these guys run by us.

Hey all - thanks for the kind messages. It's funny, I never felt all that unsafe, but definitely uncomfortable and unsure what to do seeing guns in plain sight like that without knowing for sure who these guys were.

However, now I'm pretty sure they were police officers. I agree with Jtrane, the fact that their guns were facing down, and also - the air about them was of responding to some emergency. Also I never really saw their fronts since they were zipping past us, so they could have had badges around their necks as jayme said, I just couldn't see them. Plus, they didn't really seem like criminals who would probably have been more reckless with people in their way. And lastly my friend later said that she saw the 2nd guy drop a pair of handcuffs when going down the stairs and he had to turn around and pick it up.

So I felt better feeling sure they were police officers, though they didn't make us feel all that confident in the cops since the 2nd guy seemed a little discombobulated dropping his handcuffs and having someone else help him pick them up.

A strange experience though - don't think I've ever been in the middle of a police chase before like jayme has! We should invest in one of those police scanners so we can find out what goes on around here. I'm so curious what it was, and I'm sure I'll never find out!
