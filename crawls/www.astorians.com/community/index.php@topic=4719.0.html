I love Thai Elephant
« on: January 13, 2007, 06:23:12 PM »
Seriously, I love this place. The people are nice and the food is so delicious. Well, I only ever get one or two of the same things there, but they're the best of those things I've ever had so I would imagine they have other delicious food items. The thing I got tonight, Pad Kee Mao, was spicy to the point that I had to blow my nose while eating it. I don't even like really spicy foods, but this had such a great flavor that I needed to keep eating it even though my tongue was burning. It was like Bart on The Simpsons when he was trying to get the cupcake that was hooked up to the shocking device. I work in hell's kitchen, where there are numerous Thai options, but this is much better than any of the stuff I've had around there and cheaper too. Yay, Thai Elephant.

I tried Thai Elephant tonight.  Got the Pad Kee Mao, and it was really good.  It had a kind of peppery flavor that I liked, but I did not find it to be very spicy.  It was a nice spice level, certainly not mild.  Good flavors.  I also tried the mango salad, which I also liked very much.  The curry puffs were ok, but I much prefer those at Wave.  Had the mango sticky rice, which was uninteresting - tough mango flesh and the rice was not very sweet and was a bit mushy.  A coconut cream was on top of the rice, which was a nice touch, but I could have done without it. 

For me, Thai Elephant serves as a good backup to Wave, which I find at this point to be superior to Thai Elephant.  That being said, I'd still got back to TE to try some other things.  But Wave will be my default and first choice in Thai food in Astoria.

I really need to make it to Sripaphai, though.

I've noticed that the dish is a bit inconsistent, i.e. one night it'll be pretty spicy and another night more mild. It's always tasty though. There was a lot of minced garlic in it this last time I got it, which I really liked and don't remember it being like that the other times. Then again, I'm extremely sensitive to spiciness so something that's really spicy to me is probably not terribly spicy to people who are into really spicy food. So for anyone who's actually been to Thailand and eaten real spicy Thai food, this probably doesn't compare.

I do need to try more things at both Wave and Thai Elephant. The good thing about them being so close to each other is that I can pick and choose what I want from each place and call the orders in, and then pick up the two orders at the same time. I have no problem going for the best of both worlds.
