Title: Wassi coffeehouse
Post by: goldfish boy on May 20, 2006, 09:33:00 PM
28th ave. btw 37th/38th sts, not far from the Irish rover.

I stumbled across this place this afternoon and I was very glad I did!  It's no Freezepeach--it doesn't have that homey vibe--but for us mittel-Astorians, I think it can partially fill the same need--it's a nice little coffeehouse with good coffee, outdoor seating, free wireless and no Eurodisco.   The pastry prices are a little high, but the coffee drink prices are very reasonable.  It was pretty empty, though, and running into flooz and xahc later they confirmed that it's usually empty there.  Too bad.  The pretty young server said they'd been open about a month. 

Anyway, I like it and unless someone knows of a reason that it doesn't deserve support, I'd like to see the place get some more business, so I'm letting youse know about it in case you don't.

Post by: billyboy on August 29, 2006, 12:59:14 PM
Quote from: goldfish boy on August 29, 2006, 12:15:48 PM
I, er, uh, think they need to better project* from the outside what they really are...

It's hard to tell what they really are even when you peer through the front door.  Every time I go by there (usually on a Saturday afternoon, after hitting the NY Sports Club on 30th Ave), I get "neighborhood" vibes from the few people hanging out, i.e., older middle-aged guys more prone to fisherman's caps and walrus mustaches than bedhead haircuts and wallet chains.  Granted, I'd rather hang with older guys like that, but also recognize that vibe aint going to put asses in seats.

I think they'll have an identity issue that a lot of small places like that have, be they bars/restaurants/etc.  And that's figuring out who they want their customers to be, and who their customers ultimately are.  Right now in Astoria, that's a very push/pull thing between older and newer neighborhood folk.  I remember when that Greek bar on Ditmars just off 31st (Les Amis? ... never get the name right) first opened, after being a classic dive bar (can't even remember its name!  Kellys?), no one quite knew it was a "Greek" bar at first.  But after awhile, it just sort of faded into that.  A few blocks up, 718 has struck a nice balance, attracting mostly newer folks, but every now and then, you catch older neighborhood types in there.  Jury's still out on Stini Yiamis -- the look of the bar is generic/any bar/any town/USA -- but could well end up being a nice/good bar for all I know.

In terms of coffee houses, we got enough Greek coffee houses, and Wassi should be going full gun for the younger/more affluent folks moving into Astoria.  And as noted above, I get weird jolts sometimes when I pass and see this isn't necessarily the case.  I think they're doing what they can -- wine lists, wireless internet, etc., but it's hard to say how that one's going to pan out.  Understand that I have nothing against older or newer folks -- it's just that a coffee house, like a bar, strikes me as being very much an "image" establishment, where you want to go some place that matches the image you want to project.  And I'm confused about Wassi!

Title: Re: Wassi coffeehouse
Post by: Lili on October 12, 2006, 11:12:06 AM
Hey, people from Astorians.com...

I'm the one in charge of Wassi, and guess what... You are not the only ones confused about the place - we are confused too!

When we first opened the place, in April, it was suposed to be a coffee house. But things started to change, and now we want the place more as a lounge than anything else. We do have liquor license - but only for wine and beer. The full liquor license is in its way, but it's hard to predict when exactly we'll have it. Our hours now: 4pm to 12am on, depending on the crowd. We don't really serve food. Our menu has  paninis, desserts, coffee, wine, sangria, beers.

We started with live jazz and bossa nova (I'm Brazilian and my fiance is Peruvian) but now we are trying new things to see what is better for the place. I have movie nights now, every Tuesday. Karaoke any day, DJ on Fridays and Saturdays.

And YES, we'd love to have a meeting for Astorians.com over there - just let me know in advance. I already had some meetings over there and they worked.

I built our website last week - www.wassilounge.com (http://www.wassilounge.com) and I'm really expecting that people start to give their suggestions for the place. We have all kind of people hanging out over there, but the place doesn't  have a face. I try my best, but I have 2 jobs and I'm charge there because I really like my fiance's place and I know it has a great potencial.

Well... sorry if this post is sooo long, but people keep wondering what the heck we are doing there... I hope it's a little bit clear now! :mrgreen:
