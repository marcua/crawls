New Yorker magazine - the Obama cover
« on: July 15, 2008, 02:28:35 PM »
Well, I received my issue of The New Yorker today with the controversial Obama cover. Now, finding the cover humorous or non-humorous is one thing. What I don't understand is why people are in an uproar because they see this as a terrible attack on Obama and his wife.

It reminds me of a Saturday Night Live cartoon by Robert Smigel that aired this past season, which was called The Obama Files. You can see it here: http://hotair.com/archives/2008/03/03/video-the-obama-files/
To summarize, it shows Obama on the campaign trail, all the while trying to avoid association with Al Sharpton and Jesse Jackson. Now, it was clear to me that this cartoon was saying those two are divisive figures and that Obama would prefer not to be associated with them. I took it as being a positive commentary on Obama and the campaign he's trying to run. But a lot of people, not so much on that page but in other places, have been upset by the cartoon because they think it's anti-Obama and racist. I don't get that at all. It's not anti-black; it's anti-Sharpton/Jackson.

What do you guys think? Do you find the New Yorker cover and that Obama cartoon to be anti-Obama and/or racist?

Gemelli Lemming

Begin to boil one pound of Gemelli.
Saute garlic, onions, salt and pepper in olive oil until garlic begins to brown.
Add parsley and basil and cook until garlic is fully browned.
Added half pound of shredded lemming meat and raise heat (be sure to half lid the pot to prevent oil splatter).
When meat is well browned, add sun dried tomatoes* and reduce heat.
When macaroni is fully cooked, add to lemming mix.  Mix well.
After a couple of minutes, turn off heat and let food sit for two hours.

When ready to serve, re-heat on a low flame, adding water if needed.
Add shaven locatelli.  Sprinkle with oregano.  Serve with any hardy red wine.  Loire valley reds are my favorite. 

* can use thinly sliced mushrooms instead.
