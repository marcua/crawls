Title: Come to a free Broadway concert this Monday at 6:00 in Athens Park
Post by: JeremiahFP on June 04, 2010, 10:22:05 AM
Hi all,

I am sponsoring a free concert Monday night at 6:00p in Athens Square Park.  The concert will feature amazing performers - including local Broadway stars and high school talent.  We will have free food, a free raffle, and kid-friendly activities.  The entire community is invited; it promises to be a fun night for all!  The concert features:

Tristen Buetel, a high school student and one of Astoria's rising stars
Janine Divita of "Grease", an Astorian
Erica Dorfler of "Avenue Q", an Astorian
Demi Fragale, a local Astorian up-and-comer
Jene Hernandez of "The L Word", and "In The Heights" workshop, an Astorian
Karla Mosley Frei-Pearson of "Guiding Light" and "Hi-5", an Astorian (I'm biased, but Karla is my favorite performer - she is also my wife!)
N'Kenge Pacurar of "Sondheim on Sondheim"

Two of Astoria's greatest strengths are our diversity and our talented artistic community.  This concert is a celebration of both, and we hope to see you there!

For those of you who don't know, I am running for State Assembly.  I think we, as a community and as a State, are at a crossroads.  You all, the voters in this election, have a very serious choice to make in this election.  I'm not going to make a political speech here.  But I do want to say that there are real differences between all 3 candidates (lots of differences - John and I differ strongly on social issues; Aravella and I differ strongly on reform), and I hope you will meet us all and then judge for yourself whom can best represent you.  To learn more about me and my campaign, go to www.jfp2010.org

Also, given some of the comments that have appeared on this blog and elsewhere, I want to stress that I think both of my opponents are, at bottom, sincere people who want to serve.  I have serious disagreements with them on policy, but I will do everything in my power to have an honest debate and to avoid mud-slinging and personal attacks.  I hope to earn your support, but, more importantly, I promise to run an honest campaign that will earn your respect.

Enough politics for now.  I hope I see you at the concert! 

(Mods, if I posted this on the wrong board, I apologize and please move it.  Thanks!)
