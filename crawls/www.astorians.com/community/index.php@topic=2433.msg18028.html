Books, bookstores, book-swaps, and books!
« on: June 23, 2006, 05:18:10 PM »
I know I'm a newbie, so let me know if this post is in the wrong place. But browsing around, I've seen a lot of wistful talking about "wouldn't it be great if Astoria had a real bookstore" and "could we set up a book-swap" and "is anyone interested in a book club".

Now, I think these are all great ideas. If I were really brave (or really rich), I'd give up my job with health benefits and open up a little used bookstore and be really happy. Or I could start a petition to get Barnes & Ignoble to bring the chain to Astoria. (After all, we have 3 Starbuxom here now, and they are thriving...)

I visited a great place in Baltimore called "The Book Thing". It's like a book-swap. It gives away thousands of books every weekend, operating out of a basement. It survives on donations and grants. See http://www.bookthing.org.

Brother, can you spare a basement?

Of course, if there were an existing location (Freeze Peach, anyone?) with space or interests in expanding, that would be ideal, but somehow I doubt this is feasible.
