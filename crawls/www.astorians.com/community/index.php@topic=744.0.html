Himalaya Teahouse 33-17 31st Ave
« on: September 13, 2005, 03:45:51 PM »
There is a new little spot that just opened on 31st Avenue between 33 - 34th St. It was delicious!! You must go and try it.

We stopped in for lunch Saturday afternoon and had wonderful beef dumplings - a great hearty portion served with a very tasty slaw of red & white cabbage. Also had the edamame. Service was always with a smile and the beef soup was amazing too - great clean taste to the broth, with a bit of a spicy kick to it. The noodles in the soup were delicious and hand made just before they went into the broth to cook. Same with the dumplings - everything is hand made and totally authentic Tibetan. Even down to the Tibetan cookies -the only dessert they have, but they hit the spot.

We had iced tea, cherry rose I believe it was called and it was just a delight of flavors. The owner is a very sweet local Astorian woman and her staff are all from Tibet. I would totally encourage you to visit and support this great place.

We went last night, to check it out. My husband was pretty happy with his dinner, chicken dumplings and a rooibi tea, and I had the beef dumplings and a ceylon tea. The dumplings were good, the dumpling-part itself obviously fresh and handmade, but the filling was a little bland. Then again, I didn't try any of the other dishes, like the broth and the edaname.

However, I have to say, I don't think he put enough leaf in my ceylon tea, even after I asked for it to be made a little stronger (I'd ordered the pot). I'm not an expert on tibetan food, but boy, I know my teas (many years lived in Kenya and a British husband, woo boy do I know tea), and my tea wasn't strong enough even though I let the pot set for at least fifteen minutes.

I'm not so much criticising the place (perhaps that's how they brew their black teas on purpose), I'm just expressing disappointment that I didn't get the kind of tea that would stand up in inclement weather.

The service was stellar, probably because we were the only ones in there, and the place is REALLY charming. I suggest sitting by the window, like we did! Or the garden.
