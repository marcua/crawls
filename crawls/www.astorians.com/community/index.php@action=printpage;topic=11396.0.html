Title: Re: LIRR station in LIC?
Post by: Frappe on July 28, 2008, 10:34:43 AM
To get a better sense, you can also look at the maps on yahoo.com.
http://maps.yahoo.com/;_ylc=X3oDMTExNmIycG51BF9TAzI3MTYxNDkEc2VjA2ZwLWJ1dHRvbgRzbGsDbGluaw--#mvt=m&lat=40.756141&lon=-73.906058&zoom=16

I would recommend the following route:
Starting at Steinway and 30th, walk down Newtown Avenue all the way, until you get to Northern Boulevard.  Cross over the boulevard and continue along the same road, which is now called Woodside Avenue.  Follow Woodside Avenue as it curves to the left past the park and you will get to the triangle intersection of Woodside Avenue, 58th street and Roosevelt Avenue.  Make a left along Roosevelt and the train is 3 blocks away.  Milage is a little more than 1.5 miles.

This route avoids walking along Northern Boulevard and it also, mostly avoids the Woodside Houses (which are extremely safe as housing projects go).  A slight amount of distance is added by continuing along Woodside Avenue instead of turning down 39th Avenue, which is a safe avenue, but it does have an industrial element to it as well as a dank overpass at 58th Street.  (I personally love 38th Avenue.  Its like a little road from the past).  Newtown on its own can seem a bit desolate, but it is lined with houses and its Astoria and I've found it to be a very safe and super relaxing walk.  Shadest part of the whole walk is probably at the intersection of Broadway, Newtown and 48th Street, which I imagine most folks on this board will agree that its no sweat at all.  

As for buying the train/beach tickets, you can purchase them from either the station agent or from the machine, though I do believe if you want to use a credit/debit card, you have to use the machine.  The agent booth and the machines are right near each other, so you can use one or the other
