Post by: JustMe on June 08, 2012, 12:02:35 PM
There was an interesting article published by the NYTimes ("You Say You're a Dream Renter? Prove It" by Jim Rendon July 15th, 2011) that discussed these challenges.  One of the most interesting things I learned from reading that article was their reference to a company, Insurent, that insures leases.
In a nutshell, a Renter who might not be considered qualified (student, low credit score, no guarantor, etc) can pay a fee to purchase a policy that will guarantee the lease and rent payments in the event of default.  If I recall correctly, their fee was a month's rent, or something like that.
Here's a link to the article:  http://www.nytimes.com/2011/07/17/realestate/prospective-renters-have-much-to-prove-to-landlords.html (http://www.nytimes.com/2011/07/17/realestate/prospective-renters-have-much-to-prove-to-landlords.html)  and here's a link to the Insurent website:  http://www.insurent.com/index.shtml (http://www.insurent.com/index.shtml)
Hope this info helps.   Good Luck!   Cheers
