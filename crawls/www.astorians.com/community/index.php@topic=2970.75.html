if you have a pet for more than 3 months in your residence the landlord can't force you or the pet out. he/she can try but you can get away with it. especially if you have cool neighbors who are willing to know and keep there mouth shut. something about plain sight.
this doesn't work in private family homes or buildings with less than 6 units.

The pet needs to be "open and notorious" -- meaning that the landlord or super needs to know about it, offhand I don't think a neighbor is sufficient. Once the 90 day period is over, then you're good. Usually landlords don't care about cats -- they're quiet and tend not to annoy people (unless it's my cat who annoys everyone); and I can't imagine anyone getting evicted over a 10 gallon aquarium (unless there's some sort of vicious, noisy beast living in it). With dogs it's easier to satisfy the open and notorious requirement -- they get walked and leave the building. With cats it's a bit more difficult since the only time someone would really know is if they enter the apartment.
