And let's not forget to mourn the "First Run In Queens!" porno theater.

The arabic community appears to be anything but a ghetto. It's full of businesses (that appear to be) owned by the local community. One space or another seems to be under renovation all the time.  Certainly an improvement over the moribund businesses and seedy bars there in the 1980s.

I think Steinway from N. Blvd to 30th is doomed by geography. It would take some ambitious creative development -- trees, maybe some curves or hills added to the road -- to hide the fact that it's a straight boring main-drag; divide it into chunks that feel right on a pedestrian scale.  The avenues have trees and some contours that make them more pedestrian friendly.

Well, to be fair, the part of Steinway between 28th Avenue and Astoria Blvd., where all the Arab places are, was never really part of the "core" shopping district of Steinway Street.  What I remember is that it was basically home to various mom-and-pop type shops...a couple of Italian cafes, a couple of small clothing stores, a couple of travel agencies, a couple of salons...a mix, but really stuff that wasn't very "central."  Ditto Steinway below 34th Avenue. 

Steinway could definitely use some beautification, I'll agree.  Maybe a brick sidewalk, more trees and shrubs, regulations over the types of signage that businesses can put up towards something that's more aesthetically pleasing....would all do wonders.  Unfortunately though, more pressing things for Steinway don't even seem to be happening, beautification is probably somewhere down that list.

First thing is steinway north of 28th is not considered part of the Steinway shopping area also it was never part of the BID of steinway so its not relevant to "shopping" discussion.
Second Steinway has the core 2 blocks between 30th and broadway where all the main retail is going on, the other block north of 30th ave and south of broadway was always considered secondary and tertiary blocks and only became impotent in the last 5 years when the primary blocks became too expensive.
As for the assumption that steinway was never considered upscale shopping area is wrong. In the 70's and 80' it was known for having 1 of the best bridal stores in new york "saymals" it had the number 1 selling Tahari label in new york in "modesty" and people came from allover to buy prom dress from "top 10".
that is just a few samples.
