Queen Margot.  I've only seen it twice, once in the theatre and once on VHS.  I saw it in the theatre first with a film-buff friend.  We used to go to movies and then sit around chatting about them for hours.  After Queen Margot we walked out in silence, looked at each other, made a few attempts at speech that were more like neolithic grunts, and then went home to absorb it all.

When I rented it years later it was because another friend asked me that "what's your favorite movie question," and I gave the same answer.  The only way to explain Queen Margot to people is to make them watch it.  The closest I've come to describing the experience is like getting smacked in the face with a heavy but fluffy pillow that's been laced with a light amount of some psychotropic drug.

I've owned it on DVD for like 2 years and just can't bring myself to watch it again, but it's good to know it's there.  Some day I'll be ready for it again.
