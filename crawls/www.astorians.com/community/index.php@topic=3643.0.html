Re: Shampoo w/o sodium laureth sulfate?
« Reply #8 on: October 02, 2006, 05:28:34 PM »
About 2 years ago my stylist suggested I use Devachan products since my hair is naturally curly and I really shouldn't be using any kind of detergents or silicones on an already difficult texture. So I checked it out and haven't used any other line since. It took awhile to get used to not having the lather when I wash it, but it's definitely improved the health of my hair.

Anyway, I've had no problem buying it at the beauty supply store on Steinway St. (close to Broadway), just as you come up the steps from the subway. It shouldn't run you more than $14 a bottle. I'm pretty sure they might have other brands of lather-free shampoos, but this is the only one I've really ever used. Also, as previously mentioned, Ricky's has a fairly good selection of shampoos, as well.

(Be warned, though, the first few times you use any shampoos without the detergents, your hair might feel greasy and dirty. It seems to take a few washings before your scalp and hair adjusts.)
