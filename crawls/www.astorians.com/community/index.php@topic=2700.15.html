Just want to second the vote for the Queens Library.  You can get books for free, browse, exchange, etc.  The interiors are a little depressing, but the staff is friendly and helpful, and the new technology is fantastic.  I've started using them a great deal for research and have found that they generally have most books that I want at some branch, and I've been able to request the books for pickup at the Broadway library. 

A few nifty features that weren't around in my childhood days of library-going:  The ability to search and request books online (from any location), email notifications of when my books come in, online renewals if I can't make it back to return the books in time... seriously, support your local library!  They're in our community already and have a lot of great books there to be read.

That said - I've patronized Seaburn on occasion, but I would also love a Borders or B&N in the area for the previously stated reasons.
