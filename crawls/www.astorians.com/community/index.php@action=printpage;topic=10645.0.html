The Sakura lunch does sound like a very good deal, then.  This is giving me a terrible craving for orange soda, which, if I'm not having hot tea, is my perfect-sushi-go-with beverage.

Oddly, I can go out of my way after work to Flushing and transfer to a bus that gets me home to Astoria for same price, so in summer, when I have time after work, I go to Flushing for take-home sushi.  My beloved regular place disappeared while I was away with a broken leg, so I now I go to the higher priced sushi take-out place in the Hong Kong Mall on Main Street.  When I compare prices on sushi, this what I'm comparing it to:

Today I got a 6 pc variety sushi (1 shrimp, 1 whitetail, 2 tuna, 2 salmon) for $6.  I also bought an 8 pc tuna roll and a 5 pc inari - I think each of those dishes was $4.  This is an exclusively take-out place, and downtown Flushing suffers from not having an out-door space where you can sit and eat.  This might sound like a high price for the rolls, but I think the rolls are bigger (more fish) at this place. 

The place makes up packages in advance, but it's not to be confused with mall sushi!  You don't get little packets of wasabi, you get a ball of the real deal, and ginger that pleasantly bites you back.  Since I tend to get there near closing, after I've picked what I intend to buy, they put a few other packages on top to show me what I might get at a discount if I round up to, in this case, $20.  I couldn't take advantage of the deal today, though, because I had just indulged my other craving, Blimpies, and it was tooooooo much for one night, and my home fridge is just too cold for sushi storage.

Based on my own experience, I disagree.  Sometimes it depends on location.  As I said, the place I go to now is a little store within a Chinese mall in Flushing.  Perhaps they charge less than restaurant prices because they don't have any place for you to sit - you have to eat somewhere else, presumably at home.  But their prices were higher than the place I used to go to.  This was also sort of take away, in the sense that they didn't have waiters to give you table service, but the Chinese mall they were in had a lot of little food places with common areas for seating.  Like the place I go to now, they make up trays in advance, but also like the place I go to now, it's not American mall sushi - the food is well made and they give you real wasabi.  This other mini-mall, the one with the common seating, slowly underwent improvements in appearance, but the last time I was able to go it still didn't have a pretty atmosphere.  The locals (I guess that would include me, since I went so often) would rather put up with non-prettyness and get lower prices.  The place where I first started eating sushi charged such low prices that it was cheap compared to anything else you could think to eat, and it was only when I started going to other Japanese restaurants that I found out how incredibly low their prices were.

So, there's the location, location, location argument to be made for low prices.
