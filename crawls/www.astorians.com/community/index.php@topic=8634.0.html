Hello,

My name is Yelena and I am a student at Baruch college. I came upon this forum after researching about the Greek population in Astoria. I am doing a project in school about how traditional Greek dance keeps the Greek community in Astoria together. I was wondering, would any of you be willing to get interviewed? Preferably if any of you spoke Greek. The project tries to focus on 3 Greek dances, Kalamatiano, Hasapiko, and Karsilamas. The interview would not be very long and can be done in a public place as meeting someone from the internet can be frightening. The reason Greek speaking would be preferable is because this way I could record some of what you say in Greek and when shown to the teacher for grading I edit in subtitles.

So anyone willing to do this?

If not do you have any suggestions as to where I can go to record people dancing? Is there any type of Greek festival that will be coming up in the next week in Astoria by any chance?

Thank you guys and gals in advance.

I would definitely check out the following organizations:

HELLENIC MUSIC FOUNDATION: http://www.hellenicmusic.org/

GREEK-AMERICAN FOLKLORE SOCIETY: http://www.emoreiro.com/GAFS/  (They do lots of traditional dances in costume in various places throughout the region, and performed in the Greek Independence Day Parade on 5th Avenue last April.  They even have some well-known Greek Americans as part of the group, including Fox 5 meteorologist Nick Gregory).

HELLENIC PUBLIC RADIO: http://www.gaepis.org/ (with a daily program on WNYE 91.5 FM, they also sponsor various events throughout the area and are in touch with various Hellenic cultural organizations throughout the region).

Good luck!
