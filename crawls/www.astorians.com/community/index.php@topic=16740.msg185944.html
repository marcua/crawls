And what's wrong with "Happy Holidays"?! Not everybody celebrates Christmas. It's not even Jesus' real birthday.

I'm all about the Winter Solstice myself: Before there was electricity etc., humans paid close attention to nature, and figured out that this is the shortest day of the year.

As a way of celebrating longer days and more sunlight ahead, they celebrated by bringing evergreens indoors, lighting candles, and feasting on meat (cattle that couldn't be fed through the winter were slaughtered.)

I find that THIS is something worth celebrating: It's a holiday that unites all humans regardless of religious beliefs, and doesn't involve strictly imposed traditions- in other words, there's a spiritual component to it. Despite the fact that it pre-dates holidays like Christmas and Chanukah (which all happen to be around the same time of year, gee whiz what a coincidence )
