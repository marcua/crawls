I'm being evicted!
« on: November 10, 2006, 02:26:41 PM »
I've been having problems with ConEd. They can never get access to our meter so most of the time we're paying estimated bills. This is my landlord's fault as the meters are in his basement and he's never home (this is a two-family house). So finally he calls in a reading (after four months!) and we get an adjusted bill and it is absolutely astronomical, especially considering we lost power for eight days in July.

I talk to ConEd, they agree to verify the reading the next time they're scheduled to come out, which is next month. I shoot an email off to my landlord explaining the situation, and he responds a few minutes later saying he can't think that far ahead (three weeks, for the record) and that he's very busy. So I respond saying that we're all busy, but I'd appreciate him working with me on this, and I mention that the meter hadn't been read for four months. Well, he gets a bit hot, I get a bit hot, and it ends with him saying that he wants us out at the end of this month.

Now, we're not leaving at the end of the month, as that's not even 30 days' notice. Plus, we have a lease that doesn't expire until September 1 of next year. I plan on reminding him of this and telling him that unfortunately there's no way we're leaving by the end of the current month. Because of this insanity we've decided to move anyway, but I have a feeling he might try something like changing the locks on us. I have no qualms about calling the cops if he does that.

What a fucked up Friday this turned out to be.

There is a housing advisor in Astoria, call her, she may be able to give you advice on how to proceed with this.  I'm not sure if private residences fall under the 6 month eviction rule, but I think so.  He is not allowed to evict you just like that, and legally you would have 6 months to vacate the apartment.  I would move too, but I think you'll be able to get a court order to be able to move within the 6 months.  Look into it and good luck.

http://centralastoria.org/services.htm

http://www.housingnyc.com/  -This site may give more info on your rights.
