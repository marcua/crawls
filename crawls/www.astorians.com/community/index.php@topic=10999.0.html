Hey Chris,

I don't live on Ditmars, but many of the folks that I have met on the board do and I hang out there occasionally. It has a lot of nice restaurants, stores and bars.  It's the last stop on the N/W train, which is the major Astoria line (I live off of the R).  The average price for a one bedroom in that area of Astoria is $1,400, I believe, but I can be wrong.  A studio should be around $1,200.  Anyone else who has a better idea of the prices, let us know!

To be honest, a real estate agent would probably be your best bet.  I didn't want to use one either, but after searching by myself, I broke down and went to a guy on Ditmars. 

How would you define "somewhat of a nice place?"  Most of the apartment buildings are old and some of these, not all, have fell onto hard times  Depending on how landlord takes care of their property and if the residents respect the property, the buildings can be immaculate or a tenement, like I live in right now.  I pay well below market, so I'm not really complaining, really!  You have to see the place for yourself, obviously, but you should know that there are just certain conditions that I have gotten use to, and, at one time I considered myself picky. 

I hope that doesn't put you off to the area, it really is nice.  I'm quite certain that other members have had better apartment experiences, but personally, I have just learned to let certain things go because I want to stay here and I can't afford one of the many new "luxury" buildings. 

Anyway, good luck with your search and let me know if you need any more help.
