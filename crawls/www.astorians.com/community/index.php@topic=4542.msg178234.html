I was in tears earlier today (Friday) because it seems every time I try to make my life better, something gets in the way to stop or undo the good I'm doing.  After Mr. Not-A-Bunny took off I realized it was important to make changes in the apartment because while the person who leaves has the fun of starting over in a new place, the person left behind has to deal with the same old s___.  So I was working on making the place look better but day by day the sciatic pain was getting worse.  Last Sunday night and for the 3 nights following I took half of a prescription muscle relaxer previously prescribed for the condition.  In the past a whole tablet would leave me unable to really wake up the next day, but it turns out half a tablet does nothing to relieve the condition.  Last night I took a whole tablet but it didn't do anything either (it didn't even make me groggy today), so I was in dispare because I was losing ground on getting anything accomplished, and had no reason to believe the sciatica was going to let up.  Then I recalled that I had one Celebrex capsule left that I'd been saving for an emergency and when I looked at the bottle I saw that I only had a few days left to get any refills.  I took the capsule - it's something you take every 12 hours so I guess it peaks at about 6 hours.  What a difference!  It's truly a miracle drug.  I was very unhappy at the thought of putting anything on a credit card, but I looked through those that I had and called Bank of America.  They gave me 2.99% APR through October, and Walgreens took off about $70 of the total price of my remaining prescription with the $20 purchase of their drug discount program.  So I'm hopeful for the future again, even though I did feel like s___ coming home from the pharmacy because once again, it's Friday night and I don't have a date.  Not that I could have gone anywhere or done anything, being in pain, but that's besides the point.  Anyway, thank goodness for modern medicine, because Friday morning was a nightmare, but Friday night was not.
