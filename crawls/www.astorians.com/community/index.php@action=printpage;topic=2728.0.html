I don't speak Italian or Greek but I'm quite sure that it's a take off on the root "meze".  In Greek, Armenian, Turkish, Arabic, etc. variations on the word refer to lots of plates of different foods served to the table.  Usually in smaller portions.  Spanish places call this Tapas.

That said, as a person who knows a little something about this kind of food, my impression is that Mezzo Mezzo is the ultimate tourist spot for "Greek" food.  I don't consider it authentic or even that good.  But a cool experience to take friends from out of town who don't know better.  For the real deal, try Agnanti on Ditmars and 19th.  When everyone in the place is speaking Greek and the line is practically down to the river, you know something good is happening.

Post by: lizluv43 on December 19, 2006, 11:52:14 AM
My List is as follows:

Steinway/Broadway Side of Astoria:

El Sitio - 36th Ave under the "EL" - If you go on a Tues., Wed., or Thursday the owner is usually there and he does the cooking.  Food is really good, especially their "Ropa Vieja" (translation is shredded clothes!).  What it is, is shredded beef in tomato sauce.

Agnante - Ditmars & 19th St. (Across from Astoria Park) Great food, I've ordered just about everything on their menu and it's all good and the service is great, very friendly.

El Olivo - 31st St btw Ditmars & 21st Avenue - ORDER THE PAELLA AND SANGRIA...all I can say is OH MY GOD!!!!  The portion of paella is so large that you can take home a doggy bag and have a full meal for the next day!!!  The Sangria is homemade, order the pitcher, you're going to end up drining more than one glass.

Tellys Taverna & Taverna Kycylades - The best seafood, if you like octopus....

Cavo - The bartenders are very tasty

Fatty's Cafe - They have the best Sunday Brunch in Astoria.

Mary McGuires - The place is pretty rough BUT on Tuesday nights it's buffalo wing night and they're not bad, actually they're good and you can get 20 pieces for 4.60 (and happy hour beer to go along with it).
