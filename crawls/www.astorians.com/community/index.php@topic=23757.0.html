What do I need to prepare for a new apartment?
« on: November 23, 2012, 04:46:23 PM »
Hello Astorians!

I am looking to move to Astoria eventually and am looking for some advice or information from someone who lives there or knows the area. I graduated from a master's program in May and started my first job in Manhattan. I am currently living at home in the suburbs with my parents, commuting almost 2 hours each way to get my feet on the ground and pay off some of my student loans  . I am hoping to stick it out for a while longer to save up some money, but am obviously looking to move in closer eventually. At this point, I am pretty certain that when I do move it will be to Astoria, as it's nice and close to my job in Manhattan but a lot more affordable.  Currently, I am sending almost my entire paycheck directly to Sallie Mae (ouch), but I am mapping out a budget for the next few months to make sure that I am holding on to enough that I can move out when the time comes (probably July or August).

I am looking to save money, so will probably be looking at either a studio or 2BR with a roommate. Can anyone give me some information on what is typically required? How much will I need to have saved up just to get myself into an apartment? What type of information are landlords typically looking for (e.g., pay stubs, bank statements)? Just looking for a general idea so that I don't end up sending all of my money to my loan lender and stuck with my parents for a few extra months. Thank you!

You should have more than that unless you know for sure your parents will back you up in case of an emergency .   Someone can lose their job and not have success right away finding something new . When rent and bills are due thats when your real troubles begin .  I would always have 6 months rent available just in case . It might sound like alot but you dont need your credit getting hurt if something like that happens and you cant pay .   
 
1 month security deposit
1 month rent
If you are gonna pay a broker have money available for that
min 6 months rent and extra emergency money avail on hand .
Also dont forget besides rent , bills ,  food , and im sure you might want to buy something like furniture for your new apt . So  additional money is needed for that and more .
 
I see so many people going looking for apts with barely anything in the bank and they wonder why they cant lock down the apt when they go see it .  I had a woman yesterday come in looking at an apt i had with the security and first month and not a dime more in the bank .Her job was new and she was on probation .    A month ago someone tried to scam me by renting an apt with ZERO money in their account . And i mean Zero . They wanted to get in and live for free im sure .   
 
Im not saying you would do this  but you have to see how landlords look at things since they happen all the time .
