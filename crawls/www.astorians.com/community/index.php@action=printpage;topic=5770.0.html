Here's (http://www.astorians.com/index.php?topic=4225.0) a thread about organic/free range meats available in the Ditmars area.

More information (http://www.astorians.com/index.php?topic=377.msg1433#msg1433) about some choices, including cheese, around Ditmars.

There is an organic food market, Fresh Start, on 23rd Ave. between 31st and 29th that carries organic chicken and a few other meat products - Applegate farms bacon, that sort of thing.

A couple of other options in the Ditmars area:

-Espresso/Coffee shop - Freeze Peach, on 29th St. just off Ditmars.

-gelato - La Guli has a wide range of homemade gelati and Italian ice in the summer months.  I much prefer their stuff to what Martha's offers.

Elsewhere - a couple of flavours of 5 Boroughs ice cream is available at the Bravo supermarket on 34th Ave. and 35th (ish) st.  The spicy chocolate is deadly.  The espresso at Il Bambino, 31st Ave. around 35th St., is very good.

Most of your other questions have been answered, so I will deal with my area of expertise.
The Salvation Army at the corner of Steinway and 34th Ave., right over the 34th Ave. Steinway R train station exit.
This has been touted as the largest Salvation Army store in the country (I read that somewhere once). It is bigger and more pleasant than most of the others in New York City.  I used to travel to a couple of other SA's, but now I just figure if God wants me to have it, it will find its way to this Sally's, and I will hear it calling to me as I walk out of the train station (that's usually how it works, too)
The furniture selection is good, but the best part about this store is the good selection of quality clothing for men, women, and children (I can dress exclusively in Ann Taylor, Banana Republic, and J. Crew, or I can spice things up here and there with a Prada, or Christian Dior, or Armani....  Okay, you can find Gap, H&M, Zara, and others, as well, and they do have a good selection of larger sizes for those of you who are leaving the small sizes for moi.)  I love it... I don't have to go to Macy's or Bloomingdale's and suffer the stress, and I can find Bergdorf Goodman and Barney's merchandise half the time and walk out with a sack of clothes for under $30.
Make it a stop on your busy route.
