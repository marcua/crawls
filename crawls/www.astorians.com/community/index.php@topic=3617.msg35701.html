Well, Merm, for those of us living a bit more than 5 years here you can be assured that  Astoria has gotten worse, much worse, if only from fact the the population has increased. More people, more trash.

Big question is, despite the fact that the city has been reporting the sorry state of Astoria streets with numbing regularity, why the politicians do not seem to share the concern of the posters on this board. Everyone of our leaders are already aware of those statistics. Everyone. The big question is why no one in a position of authority seems to believe it important enough to do something or even comment publicly.

Do you really believe that by sending politicians pictures you will show them stuff that they have not seen or that they do not know? Or do you you imply they are insensitive or oblivious?

Or will your good intentioned efforts at contacting them simply give rise to a few headlines in the papers, a topic to only dissappear again while we are lulled into thinking that something of substance is being done? A year from now, anyone want to bet that things will be better? I have a war in Iraq that will be over in a year, too. Another politician's promise.

And again, I must stress, dirty streets are not a single issue, but merely a single red-flag to a host of linked local urban ills that seems to rise in our community with the passage of time.

Go ahead, take pictures, but post them in a location for everyone to see. How about a gallery on this board, trash and graffiti?
