I see I'm a little late on this thread, but I really gotta say, the hookah places on steinway passed 28th ave used to rock hard. And they've gone SUPER downhill in the last couple years.
I have no idea about the names of these places, but on Steinway, north of 28th ave on the west side of the street,there were two hookah places a couple doors down from each other. There was the one with the blue awning, the Arabic Community Center or something, which had a really nice outdoor section which you had to walk through the kitchen to get to. That place was cool but it was also very much a local hang out for middle aged Arabic men, so I always felt out of place there, and went to the place a couple doors down. This one had a red awning, with a string of white christmas lights hanging from it, and a big screen tv. Never bothered to get the name of it. It was AWESOME like 2003-2004. It also had a nice backyard section, with a huge heater in the middle so it was still open in the winter. This place was more for like 20something locals, which is why I was more comfy there. They still played obnoxiously loud arabic music, but it was still really chill. And guess what? Hookahs were $4. No thats not a typo. $4.
And then all these trendy people who justed moved to astoria because they couldn't afford the city any more started going there, and the owners realized they had been sitting on a goldmine. They re-did the backyard, and yes it was much nicer, but they also doubled the price of the hookahs AND instilled a new rule that no one can share a hookah and everyone at the table must order a drink. They also made a big stink when one of the guys in my party left. He had gone to meet some friends at the corner and bring them to where we were, but the guy serving us came over and made a big deal about 'why did he leave he did not pay you are responsible to pay for his order blah blah blah' we told the guy to relax and that he would be back and it wasnt a big deal, but he kept carrying on, and the funniest part was all he ordered was a $2 bottle of water!!!!!
Sorry about the rant, but it's still a sore spot since I used to go to that place once every two weeks before it got 'trendy' and the service in turn decided to suck. If you want reasonably price, authentic hookah, go find another arabic community where the gentrification hasn't begun to take over yet.

Yep, with it being Ramadan right now the businesses on Steinway are thriving.  I actually meant to tell the fellow Astorians about the dinner deals that can be had.  Almost all the restaurants and cafes have all-you-can-eat buffets at sunset for "iftaar" or the breaking of the fast.  I went to Aldar (next door to the Mexican restaurant on the corner of 28th ave and Steinway) and for $25 they had an absolutely amazing selection of food.  Lamb, chicken, squab, beef, shrimp, fish, pasta, vegetables, soups, desserts, etc. etc.!!!  There are other places that are a little cheaper (I think Jerusalem Nights has a $15 buffet).

You'll be immersed in an all Arabic crowd but it's fun.  I'm not middle-eastern myself and I thought it was a great experience.

The residents who live on the other side of Steinway Street are adversely affected by the smoke from the hookah cafes.  In NYC, there is a no-smoking policy but these businesses get a pass from the local political establishment, and the result is that there is a no-hit policy on enforcing the law for these businesses.  Many of the affected neighbors were forced to move from their apartments and the pungent smoke (it gets into their apartments even with the windows closed and the odor is even absorbed into their furnishings) because the hookah cafes set up open tents in the backyard.  These same residents had organized and complained at CB1 meetings (and even got coverage on the local television news) and were ignored.
