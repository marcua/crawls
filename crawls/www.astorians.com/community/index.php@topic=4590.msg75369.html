I'm surprised by all the negative comments about the Acropolis. I've been there over 3 years and I don't think it's as bad as so many people say it is. No place is ever perfect but I think it's a nice place to live overall.

They are trying to renovate all the older apartments when people move out. The grounds are well cared for. Lots of flowers and trees in the courtyards.

I will admit that they have a problem with people picking up after their dogs. It's one of my gripes. It's a big problem there. 

I've never had a problem with bed bugs and don't see all of these mattresses that people say are on the sidewalk. Unfortunately because it's such a big complex, when garbage day comes, the sidewalks are overflowing at times with garbage. If they had dumpsters or something, I think it would be better.

You can't beat how close you are to the train, restaurants, shopping, etc. They have their own security which is a bonus.

I would recommend people to check it out if they are interested.

I've lived in Acropolis Gardens since 2003 and have never had a problem. We bought our one-bedroom and completely renovated. The location is fantastic, the grounds are constantly being maintained, a new much-needed laundry room is under construction and we have never had bed bugs. A definite plus is they allow dogs, although some owners don't care enough to pick up after them. The only drawback is the size of the apts -- most are on the smaller side. But with such a short commute to the city, you don't mind after awhile.

Just my 2 cents here because I have no friends who rent or own there. While it's great that the management has changed recently, what will always be an issue in a HUGE coop complex like that if there is no board to go through, even for renters, is the people living next door to you. Living near buyers or other renters might be an issue in terms of dogs, other noise, cleanliness, bed bugs, etc... I think it's pretty difficult to control who lives around you unless they go through a board approval process, and even then....  While you may buy your own place, renovate it and it can look fantastic, there are other things to consider than just your own 600 sq. foot of space. I would be weary of getting into a complex of that size with no board screening and a managment that has just changed hands, I think I'd want to see a couple of great years go by for the majority of the tenants and a low % of renters to buyers before investing in this kind of property. Unfortunately, there aren't a wealth of places to buy in Astoria, but there are more now than there were 3 years ago. I'd seriously consider other places before settling on the Acropolis-it would be my last choice if I had no choice. Also, has anyone mentioned, this is a walk-up?

p.s. And no offense to those who bought there already, but for future buyers, you have to take everything with a grain of salt. I'm sure the buyers also have a vested interest in making sure that the image of this place is favorable, since they'll eventually want to sell it for more than they bought it for.
