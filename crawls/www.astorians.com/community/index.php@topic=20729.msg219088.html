What if?
« on: March 20, 2011, 11:59:09 AM »
Sometimes my friends and I like to pose "what ifs" and then talk out all the possible scenarios.  Here's one that grew out of a conversation about Libya's Civil War.

What if the South decided to secede?

Before anyone notes how very different the situation is in Libya and any reason why the Southern States would want to secede, I know!  Just play along.

If the Southern state legislatures and Governors all sign a Declaration of Independence and send it to the President's desk - what happens?  What does Obama do?  What does the rest of the world do?  Which countries support which side?

"It'll never happen" responses are not allowed.  Trying to have fun playing nerdy political games here.

Discuss.

Quote from: kempsternyc on May 03, 2011, 02:01:57 PM
One certain thing that will happen is that Us New Yorkers will get more back from the federal government instead of it going to Southern States.

Well, that's a case of being hoisted on one's own petard.  The higher one sets the tax rates of earners in the upper income brackets, the more those areas with higher average incomes contribute to the treasury.  On the flip side, since most benefits programs are means-tested, those same areas draw less on the treasury.  The really pernicious thing is that, due to the higher cost of living here, a dollar in New York City is worth much less than a dollar in Savannah. 

If one could ask "What's the matter with Kansas," one could also reasonably ask "What's the matter with New York?"
