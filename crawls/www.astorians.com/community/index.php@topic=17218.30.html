Example (how the mortgage banks do it)

Salary $72,000.00
Front end ratio used by banks is 28% (mortgage payment)
Back end ratio used by banks 36% (mortgage payment plus other debts {they do not include utilities but you should})

Formula is:  GROSS YEARLY SALARY/365*30*36%

The monthly payment you can afford is $1,656.99 (includes principal, interest, taxes, and insurance)

and your total payments (mortgage, credit cards, car loans, etc.) cannot exceed $2,130.41

So if you make $72,000.00 you qualify for a mortgage of $220,000.00 (@ 4.75%)

You would need 3% down (minimum)
In addition to the 3% down you need some money upfront such as:
Inspection ($400 and up)
Homeowners Insurance (first year needs to be paid upfront)
Someone can gift you money and there's no limit as to how much
Seller can pay some closing costs but NY is one of the highest in closing costs.

In Astoria you can probably find a co-op for that price.

Also, if you buy a home that's a "legal" three family the bank will include 75% of the rents collected to your income.

So if you have a home where you have 2 apartments rented out at 1,500.00 each, that's 3,000 per month so $2,250 is added so now your monthly income goes up from $5,917 to $8,167.00

Now your mortgage payment cannot exceed $2,287.00
Your TOTAL housing payments (mortgage and other debt) cannot exceed $2,940.00

So how much house can you afford now? $400,000.00

Still falls below what prices are in Astoria for 3 family homes but you still CAN find these prices on the Queens/LI or Queens/Brooklyn borders.

You also have to take into consideration that you'll be paying water for your tenants, and electricity.  That's an extra $2,000 (could be a bit less, could be a bit more depending on your tenants) per month that you'll need to add to your monthly expenses. 


P.S.  This is just an example rough estimate not a direct quote of any type but pretty close to give a general idea of how it works.  :)
