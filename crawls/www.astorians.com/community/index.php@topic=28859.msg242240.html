My partner and I were long-time regulars at the Brick Cafe lounge. We knew about the sale of the entire place and were there on the last weekend before the lounge closed for remodeling to become Halseys at the end of the summer.


The new manager told us he anticipates Halseys becoming the 'craft beer capital' of Astoria - which meant nothing to us as neither of us drink craft beers. He promised us the changes would be minimal, but it no longer appeals to us.


As a previous poster said, it's way too bright for a bar. I've passed by on a few evenings and even from outside it's too bright. They've also done away with the extensive wine selection that was a standard for Brick. The new manager had told me they'd have 'wine on tap' which didn't appeal to me at all.


The lounge used to have a charming, rustic appeal and it was a great place to relax for us to relax without the glare of televisions and bright lighting. I know everything has to change over time, but the lounge did great business and was always busy. What's there now doesn't appeal to either of us.


We've been enjoying drinks around the corner at Pachanga Patterson, which has a great staff, good wine and beer selection and a comfortable atmosphere (including the right lighting).

I'm so sad to hear Marketa closed.  I found the place to be somewhat different from the other places in the immediate vicinity.  It seemed a bit more er...discreet?...whereas some of the other places (like the one directly on the other side of 30th Ave.) feels more like a Euro type club/restaurant.  What I liked about Marketa was (beyond the great music the DJ would often play) ...their grilled salmon dish on a bed of micro greens and peas and fingerling potatoes.  The first time I went there and got that dish I was actually not expecting it to be so incredibly good...on par with other real high-end restaurants.

I agree that overall, 30th Ave has become overly saturated with bars and restaurants, many of which have no original theme or charm.  I DO like SugarFreak....great ambiance and vibe there.  And Butcher Bar is usually pretty good.  Ovelia can be ok, and that juice bar that is attached to/behind that other restaurant on the corner of maybe 35th?  ...that's a great juice bar.  But those are probably the only places I'd go to.

I do like that there are still some old school establishments that make it still feel like Old Astoria....that guitar shop that offers lessons...that strange hobby/religious shop....I hope they aren't forced out due to higher rents all to make way for more bars and restaurants!  I'm really sad at how oversaturated Astoria is becoming.
