Title: Re: Jimbo's
Post by: LHT on September 18, 2008, 05:48:38 PM
I don't know that I'd call it a typical American dive bar.  It's a very old-style Greek place.  They've recently renovated it a few times in the past couple years, so it's not a smelly run down kinda place, but it's definitely got a 'different' kinda bar vibe.  Latin girls work the bar and don't speak the best English, but they're nice enough and friendly.  Sometimes seemingly too friendly to the older guys, but definitely harmless.  They actually have amazing food, no real menu but their biftekia, saganaki and steaks are pretty incredible.  Same for the fried calamari - some of the best I've had in town.  We actually ran into friends from Bayside who had made the trip over just because they heard how good the steaks are - go figure.  Expect Greek soccer or soap operas playing on the TVs unless there's a game and if you hang around until after 10pm, get ready because that's when the music & live floor show starts.   :roll:  It actually gets a bit crazy and crowded on Fri & Sat nights, but I believe they have music almost every night.  If you're into live Greek music, it's pretty good and the dancing can get fun too with stacks of dollar bills flying overhead and champagne popping.
