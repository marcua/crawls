I think I do remember this place, but I never went in there.   I would pass by it on my way in a cab to Laguardia.   Then one day I went by and there was this high-end restaurant -- or what I presumed to be a high-end restaurant -- that had already gone out of business.   I said to myself "wasn't there a bar on that corner?"   Boulevard was open after I moved to Astoria in '87, but I can no longer remember when it went out business, some point in the early to mid 1990s I would guess. I am speaking of the space across 33rd Street from the Roy Rogers cum Burger King.   The space became a deli and is now a real estate office.

I may be dreaming this as well and I hope someone can straighten me out, but at the corner of Hoyt Avenue South & 31st Street was there ever a bar there before the Dunkin' Donuts moved in?  That Dunkin' Donuts wasn't always there was it?   I seem to remember a neighborhood -type place that had a lot of plants in the window.   I never went in, though, and sometimes I think I dream these things up.

Finally, I've asked this before and no one has confirmed it, but does anyone remember a dingy-looking bar on Astoria Blvd. just west of 21st Street on the same block where Fishbein's and the bottle shop used to be.   It would have been on the south side of AB a few doors west of Fishbeins.   It was a forboding looking place with a couple of small windows.  It looked like a scary place.   One day it was gone and I don't know if it ever existed or if I was imagining it.

Well, kiss the Glen Oaks (I think that was the name of it) goodbye.   It was an Irish/workingman's/old man's bar on 30th Avenue and 37th Street.   I walked by on Saturday and there was a "for rent" sign in the window.  I never went in there myself, but these types of places were all over Astoria and are still fairly prelevant, but one after another they are closing.

It wasn't really a bar, but I noticed the Spanish restaurant on the corner of 28th Avenue and Steinway has closed.   Whatever opens on that corner has a tough time of making a go of it.  Before that restaurant there was one of those "Athens Cafe" type of places with outdoor seating, but that didn't last either.  I can't remember what was there before the coffeehouse, but I know a number of eating/drinking establishments have come and gone in the 20 years that I've lived here.
