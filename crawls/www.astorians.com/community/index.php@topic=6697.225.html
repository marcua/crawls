Mr. Astoria For Life. If you referred to my post about the pool hall and bowling alley they were two separate places on opposite sides of 31st St at the Ditmars station in the early 50's. The pool room was on the West side upstairs and was a real dump with dirty windows and a bunch of us wanna-be juvenile delinquents with DA haircuts smoking Luckies and shooting pool .

The bowling alley wasn't exactly Mayberry either it was down one level below ground on the East side of the station if you kept going down from the station through the office building. I set pins there sometimes to make a few dimes before I went to my regular 5PM delivery boy job in Manhattan. 10 cents a line so if you set for two lanes with two bowlers each you made 40 cents for each game and you might score a big dime tip for a big half a buck total for your half hour's time. Lou the owner would not let kids set pins for any big money games so you could not mess up a payday for one of the few hustlers who made a living there. I saw guys lose five or six hundred dollars over the course of an afternoon to Albie the Phantom who looked like a bum but bowled like a pro. ...the good times.

Looks like the Zabb Thai Restaurant on 30th Avenue between 34th and 35th Streets is closed.   Whether permanent or temporary I don't know, but they renovated fairly recently.   

That block has changed since I moved into the neighborhood in '01.   In addition to Zabb Thai, Flemings one of those "old men's bars", closed its doors recently.   When I moved in in '01 the corner store at 34th and 30th was a hardware store that was closing.   A pretty good restaurant -- with a dynamite breakfast as well as good burgers -- opened in its place, but went out of business after a couple of years.   They kept changing the format, at one time having belly dancers in a bar at the back of the restaurat.  Another restaurant replaced it and seems to be doing very well.   On the other end of the block at the corner of 35th and 30th Avenue was a candy store run by South Asians.  Occasionally, I'd buy my "New York Times" there when the paper boy failed to show up.   That closed and a high end seafood restaurant, Akli, opened.   Akli, for the record, is terrific based on the one time I ate there.  There is also a tiny Indian sweets shop on the block that has closed and reopened at least once since I've lived there.   And there is another South Aisian-owned business, a printing shop, that also included a cell phone store at one point.   The cell phone store moved to the northwest corner of 34th & 30th Avenue.  There is even a strange bank, called the Somali Exchange Bank, which I've never been in.
