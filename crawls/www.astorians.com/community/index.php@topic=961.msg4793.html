Jtrane, refresh my memory--who was Otis in Superman II?

That was a good movie.  My favorite aspect of it was the light touch with which Christopher Reeve and Margot Kidder played the two principals, setting the precedent for Dean Cain and Teri Hatcher on TV.  My least favorite departure from the comic book was when Lara said that without his super-powers Clark would be mortal.  Everyone who read the comic book knew that Superman would eventually die.

But what was creepy to me about that movie was Terence Stamp as General Zod.  This was the actor who had played the saintly Billy Budd, the innocent John Soames and the lovable cad Alfie (on Broadway).  Now he was playing a totally horrid, diabolical villain with super-powers and without a sympathetic bone in his body.

James Dickey, who was primarily a poet, told an interviewer that he was not a fiction writer because the only thing he could think of with a plot was Deliverance.
