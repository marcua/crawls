Quote from: jp-morgan8 on December 01, 2008, 08:17:35 PM
I think because they are rent stabilized and have special stipulation allowing nice places for low rent. I think they renovated them all too.

I’m sorry to say it sounds like you’ve been seriously misinformed. The term ‘rent-stabilized’ means nothing nowadays. Rent protections have been eroded over the years and though an apartment may fall under rent-stabilization regulations it doesn’t necessarily mean the apartment is less expensive than those not covered by regulations (i.e., in private houses or buildings constructed after 1971).

Apartments that are considered rent-stabilized do offer additional protections in regard to rights to an apartment and how much the rent will increase each year but the regulations do not stipulate that an apartment must be renovated or that they will be offered at a low rent.

Hey, we’ve all been there. I know it can be rough going trying to find a place to live, especially if you’re new to the game, all I can say is to be careful.

The medium may have changed but the scams are still the same. Back in the day when people looked in newspapers for apartments, there were loads of ads touting ‘rent-controlled’ or ‘rent-stabilized’ steals offering so sweet of a deal apartments in the heart of the Village for an unbelievable price. I don’t want to be a buzz kill but I’m sorry to say those places do not and have never existed.

The only difference now is that, compliments of the internet, the scams come complete with pictures making them more enticing. Before you would lose your deposit and a bit of your dignity, now they steal your identity and clean out your bank accounts.

Legitimate management agencies have physical offices, land-line phone numbers, and identifiable people you can talk to. They are registered with the city or state. Any entity with just a web presence, on a blog no less, is extremely suspect in my book.

And for what it’s worth since you’re still learning, basement apartments may be less expensive but they are almost always illegal.
