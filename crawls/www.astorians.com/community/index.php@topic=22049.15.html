I'm going to chime in here to give another perspective on car ownership. I own a car because I work in Nassau County 6 days a week. I live in Astoria so my husband can commute to his job in Manhattan by train (and because I love it and don't want to live anywhere else!) I don't worry about alternate side parking because it happens while I'm at work. I live near Astoria Park, and I find the distance from the subway and shopping means I always find a parking spot on or near my block. On the rare occasions that I don't have my car (and I take the N to the 7 to Woodside to Jamaica to my LIRR stop and walk the mile to the office) it takes nearly 2 hours vs. the 30 min driving commute. Yes, it does mean I fork over a lot of money for insurance, maintenance and payments. Yes, it does mean that my friends ask to borrow my car. But it also means that I can food shop at Best Yet with ease, which I enjoy. And I can easily visit my parents and in-laws on LI and in CT at a moments notice. Trust me, when I get that mechanics bill or Progressive statement, I want to push my car into the river. But at the end of the day it is a necessity for me and I am thankful that I am able to afford and maintain a car in the city.

Thanos, you make me LOL!


I will satisfy your curiosity, though. I lived in Stony Brook (actually East Setauket, one of the villages in the Three Village area), and attended SUNY there. I did live on 25A, but it wasn't bad.


I lived 25 minute walk/10 minute bike ride door to door (home to studio). 15 minute walk to the Stony Brook LIRR. I lived across the street from a deli and a number of restaurants (Indian, Subway, Sushi, Chinese). Beer and Wine store was there, too, as well as a fish market. I biked up the hill to get my groceries, or walked to the farm stand a few blocks away. I biked my laundry up the hill to do my wash. My bank was up there, too. Library was biking distance. Friends were mostly nearby, plus I'd see them at school.


I could bike to a number of beaches (West Meadow, Crane Neck, Cord Wood).


Bars were close and sometimes I'd carpool with a couple of other students to one far away (see - I am not 100% anti-car, though I prefer carpooling to single driver). Also, I carpooled to my church job (I was a church choir director in Bayport). There is a taxi service out in Stony Brook, too.


I fully realize I was very fortunate to be where I was. I made choices appropriate to the lifestyle I wanted to lead at the time.


I was an urban cyclist in CA, where I met lots of different people and heard lots of points of view regarding transportation. It was enlightening. I was shown that there are viable options to driving, and I loved creating a lighter footprint, environmentally. I biked a lot in Berkeley - when I worked at Cal (UC Berkeley), I biked or walked 99% of the time. If it was hard core raining - like monsoon weather - I would drive to a neighboring parking lot and pay $5 for the day. It was extremely rare, and I probably did that 5 times max. I walked and biked all over Berkeley, Albany, and parts of Oakland, too.


The scenario you describe with Old Navy and the Supermarket - I don't believe I consume as much as you, so that is an unrealistic scenario for me. Our shopping and buying habits are just different, that's all.

I couldn't be without a car for purely selfish reasons. I LIKE to drive places other than a walking distance from a subway/bus which limits your experiences. I can get in my car and go surfing in less than an hour- 30 minutes if I go to Rockaway. 45 minutes to Long Beach. The trials and tribulations related in numerous "NYC surfer" stories about 3 hours on a train and bus dragging a board and equipment to Rockaway are all true. the A shuttle is messed up most of the time on weekends, little or no warning about service changes, etc... And that's if you only want to go to Rockaway. So if I want to get to the beach for a 7am start, I'd have to leave a 4am to get there on time. Getting to Long Beach is actually shorter- IF you get a nice enough conductor that will ALLOW you to bring a board on the LIRR. But aside from that scenario, I can go to any number of interesting places at MY discretion without the rental/Zip car hassles. I've had the same garage for over 20 yrs a couple of blocks from the apartment that I can access 24 hours. I don't have to move for alt side, and I rarely get plowed in for long since I have AWD. Sure- there are plenty of people who don't need a car or don't want a car and that's fine for them. I don't need to rely on the kindness of friends or the scheduling vagaries of the MTA 'improvement' programs that mess up trains when and if I need to get somewhere in a hurry.
