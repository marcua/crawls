Scribbles (and all)
the rise of bed bugs (where's your source on this?) has nothing to do with warm weather. I had bedbugs in the dead of winter. They are notoriously difficult to kill with heat or with cold.
In order to kill bed bugs and their eggs with cold, you must place infected items in a subzero freezer (-29 degrees at least) for several days.

If bed bugs seem to be on the rise, it has more to do with international travel, more people vacationing and visiting friends, and ignorance, not because of the spring.

Turn your nervous energy into something positive.  First, research all you can on them.  I've survived them in the past (in an old apartment), and sometimes a preventative treatment is a good thing to have to give you peace of mind, even if you haven't been bitten.

Also, to ALL NY'ERS: Protect your mattress and box spring.  There is a wonderful product called Protect A Bed mattress covers.  We should all be using them.

To the poster: "Can you get bedbugs walking down the street?"  I would say that's very unlikely.  They mostly come out at night, when you're sleeping and the reason they are drawn to humans by the carbon dioxide we exhale.  That's why after a treatment, your exterminator will probably tell you to continue sleeping in your bed as 'bait' for the bugs.  They will come towards you, and come in contact with the insecticide and die. 

Bedbugs are a challenge, but if your building management has a good plan of attack, and you are willing to participate in the program, you should be fine.

Don't stress, but if you need a drink and need to chill, let me know and I'll meet ya at Crescent Lounge for a beer or two.  We're all in this together.  It's just part of living in NYC.

There aren't many bedbugs in suburbia, but if I lived there I'd slit my wrists...
