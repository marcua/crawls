Title: Re: Chained Supermarket Carts
Post by: psychopompos on August 12, 2014, 01:42:41 PM
Most supermarkets have some sort of means of making sure that the carts don't get taken away or left in inopportune  places, for example, in the middle of the street. Especially important when they're situated in dense urban areas and don't have parking lots. Ugly metal bars outside the door which are big enough for a human to pass through but too narrow for a shopping cart are common in Astoria. Chaining carts together and asking for a quarter in order to unlock the chain is less intrusive visually, but probably less effective and more annoying for customers.
