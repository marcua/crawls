Quote from: Starwind51 on June 01, 2008, 01:36:30 AM
What was Delphi?
I didn't realize how long ago it was - I was a habitual nightly user of Delphi around 1984-1986.

At that time several companies who offered time-sharing computer services for business also offered personal accounts for late-night and weekends, when their equipment was under-utilized.  The biggest was Compuserv. It was, in a sense, the AOL of it's day. A second service, more serious (or a lot duller) was The Source. The Source was the first service I paid to use.

Access was through modem on a telephone line from a dumb-terminal, or a PC with a terminal emulation program. 2400 baud was high speed access. Everything was text - no graphics, unless you count :-)

Each service had modem pools in some major cities. A couple of X.21 network providers offered wider coverage - Uninet and Tymnet were two of them. You would dial the local Uninet or Tymnet number, and when it answered, you'd request to be connected to Compuserv, or whomever.

Services offered were e-mail, special interest document libraries, and bulletin-boardish things, organized around interest groups. People edited and moderated these things in exchange for free usage, and for the opportunity to push people around.

Compuserv also had an online chat thing called "CB" (after citizens-band radio, popular around the same time.) CB felt sort of like IRC, but there were a limited number of channels, and they were numbered rather than named. After a week or two, you'd learn what peculiar interest found a home on which channel.

Minicomputers got cheaper, and Uninet and Tymnet were looking for more business, so other services appeared. Many targeted individual consumer and small group use as a primary product. Delphi was one of them.

I joined Delphi as a lower-cost alternative to The Source - a handful of Source users jumped ship around the same time. It was a real bargin. I think $6/hour when I started, later falling to maybe $4.50. The Source cost a lot more, maybe $12.00/hour, or $10.50at the lowest, and really expensive during business hours.

One month on the Source, when I fell in love with some other user, my bill was around $650.00. Not as expensive as Spitzer's love life, but it seemed an awful lot at the time. After that, I learned to logon, download everything of interest, and log off to read it.

Delphi offered special-interest message boards, e-mail, and a good online chat system. The chat was better than Compuserve's: named, user-created channels. And it had a smaller, and (so far as I could tell) more intellectually sophisticated user community.

Delphi kinda fell apart from fragmentation - competing services for specialize interests appeared - and from the rise of the internet. I didn't realize it had become an internet site.

That is a probably more than you or anyone wanted to know - a real Abe Simpson answer.

I will now resume rocking in my chair, grumbling, and whittling toothpicks.
