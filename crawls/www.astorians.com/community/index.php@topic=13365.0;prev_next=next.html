I assume you need to copy a rare recording that you could not otherwise get online.  There are a few ways to do this on the cheap:

Option 1. Connect an audio cassette player with a line or phono output to the microphone jack of your computer, use an audio recording program and save as a file.  You can get the cables at Radio Shack.  You will likely need a "Y" connector (1/8" stereo plug on one end and a white (male) and yellow (male) plug on the other end.

Option 2. Connect an audio cassette player with a line or phono output to a video capture card or device and copy to your computer.  Open your video editing program and export the audio to a file.

Option 3. Connect an audio cassette player with a line or phono output to an MP3 player with a line input (a few models have this) and save as an MP3.  You can also do this with a voice memo recorder that will connect to your computer via USB.

You can get an audio cassette player (even a cassette walkman will do) at any Salvation Army / thrift shop / eBay inexpensively.

Option 4. If you need to copy a ton of audio cassettes and need a more permanent solution, there is the Ion Tape2PC USB Cassette Deck that sells for about $100.
