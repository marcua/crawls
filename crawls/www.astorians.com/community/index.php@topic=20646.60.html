So, finally got around to trying this place with three friends last night, and I find myself somewhat on the fence.
First the space, service, and general vibe was pretty right on. It's a nice space, our server was attentive without being overbearing, the music was there, but not overly noticable. All plusses.
Then there's the food.  We started with chips and guac.  The guac, while fresh, was nothing that couldn't be replicated at home, and certainly didn't live up to the $9 price tag. Unfortunately, I think that pricing issue was the theme throughout the meal.
For round two we all split the mussels, which were good, and I loved the chorizo broth. I would say that was probably one of the high point of the meal for me.
For a main course, we split a bunch of tacos. The only ones we didn't get an order of were the bean tacos. Here is my impression:
Mu Shu Duck: Absolutely delicious. Unfortunately, nobody at my table tried one of these but me, so I think they missed out. Tangy pickled stuff, with the sweet fatty duck just hit the spot for me.
Vietnamese Pork Shoulder: These were only okay. Someone in our party complained about the meat tasting like lamb, but I didn't get that at all. That said there wasn't much to it, I didn't really taste the vietnamese marinade, or the siracha, and I had to add hot sauce for flavor.
Lamb: My first bite of the lamb contained something so sour that I was immediately turned off, but I finished the taco, and by the end I was enjoying it a bit more, but that first bite was just off putting.
Pollack (aka fish): I don't know if it's just that pollack is exceptionally mild, but I couldn't taste the actual fish, but only the batter and the fry grease. I was underwhelmed.
Portobello: I don't particularly care for portabellos, so I passed, but didn't think it was a good sign when, at the end of the meal only one was eaten.

We passed on dessert, and got the check, which for a party of 4 wound up being $115 (we also had 2 beers and a pitcher of sangria). For a meal of tacos, chips and guac, and mussels that's steep to me, especially as I still think that the tacos that are being put out by the taco truck are better overall tacos than what PP is putting out. And I get that PP is doing forward thinking tacos, fuzing other cultural elements and using fresh / seasonal / local ingredients, but I only found 1/4 to be worth ordering again, based on the price and flavor.
All in all, I seemed to have the most positive reaction in my group. My girlfriend swore that she wouldn't go back, and honestly, for the price and other options for tacos in the neighborhood, I can't fault her for that. I'm in no rush to go back, myself, which is quite the opposite reaction from what I had when we visited Vesta.

Based on many of the glowing reviews of Pachanga on this board, I tried it last night, and absolutely loved it.  What a great addition to our neighborhood.  Based on a number of the reviews here, I went in preparing to be unhappy with the bill, wary of the overaggressive busboys, and cramped in the space there.  I was pleasantly surprised to find a reasonable bill and comfortable accomodations.
 
My dining companion and I ordered one order of guacamole and chips, black bean soup, moo shu duck tacos, a side of sweet potato puree, one margarita each, and a dessert, all of which came out to $57 before tip.  I thought that was fair, especially considering that the drinks were $17 of the total.  And the tables, while somewhat close together, did not make me uncomfortable, and I did not feel that anyone was in my space.  Finally, I found the service to be brisk but not overbearing.  The waitress was very personable and friendly.
 
And most importantly, the food was incredibly delicious.  The guacamole was very mild and had large chunks of avocado, just the way I like it.  I do agree that it should cost less than $9.  The homemade chips were very unlike any others I had before -- they were very thick and crunchy and not too greasy.  The black bean soup was smooth and tasty.  I enjoyed the crunchy tortilla chips on top.  The duck tacos were also delicious and not too fatty, although they could have used a little more flavor to them.  I agree with an earlier commentator that an orange type of sauce would be called for.  I think hoisin sauce is supposed to be in the tacos, but perhaps not enough was applied.  The sweet potato puree was delicious, and the el diablito cake was outstanding.  It had a slight kick to it from the chili sauce, but not enough to need to drink after each bite.  However, a sip of water did cool things down.  It had the perfect amount of sweetness.
 
I've been debating as to whether I like Pachanga or Mexi Q better, and it's a difficult call for me.  Does anyone have an opinion on that?  I'm just glad to have both in the neighborhood.
 
Overall, I am really excited about Pachanga and can't wait to bring friends to try the place.
