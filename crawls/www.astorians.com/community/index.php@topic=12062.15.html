One of the things that NYC residents should know is that they have a choice of where the EMTS transport to. FDNYEMS is in radio touch with almost all ERs. They can ascertain what types of traumas will precede the one they are transporting in. If they deem the wait too long they can request permission to transport elsewhere. Since most automobile accident injuries require extensive triage they try to bring victims to facilities that meet the need: have available trauma teams and are not overwhelmed with other prioritized traumas. In your case, the fault lies not with the hospital, but with the ambulance service who did the transporting.

I have a medical condition that sometimes requires ER intervention. I ALWAYS call my preferred ER to A) inform them that I am on my way and B) ascertain that they can be made ready for my arrival. If I can do that as a patient, certainly the EMTS can as long as they explain what they are dealing with and how they have treated the patient thus far. Emergency Rooms must reprioritize as each new patient walks through their door. Having said that, I fully understand that major cardiac and cerebral incidents, stab and gunshot wounds or an intractable asthmatic condition will almost always precede anything else.

It certainly is allowable to carry such a card, but it is also highly unlikely that an EMT would even notice it. If they arrive on the scene and find someone unconscious, their prime objective is to determine the cause; then stabilize, and finally transport. When they do a search for medical information—like a MedicAlert tag or bracelet—they generally just look around the neck and the wrists. It is usually NYPD who will look for personal papers, like a wallet, to identify and then to find out who needs to be called.

I keep a series of listing in my cellphone under ICE (In Case of Emergency). The first listing (ICE #1) is my personal physician. If he is called, he will direct them to transport me to a specific hospital. ICE #2 is my POA (Power of Attorney and Health Care Advocate) she will come to my side immediately and bring all the necessary paperwork with her. Her signature and word is as legal and binding as if it were mine. These are things that should be thought out well in advance of any need. I would be glad to discuss this with you off thread, PM me.
