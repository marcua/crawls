Yes.  If it appeals to you, definitely order from the specials of the day.  Emilio, the chef, shops daily and buys whatever fresh ingredients strike his fancy that day.  Then he devises the specials.  Not to say that other menu items are stale or lousy!  His lasagna is huge.  Six or more layers of meat and cheese.  Not for a light eater.  And if he bought tons of clams, mussels, and shrimp, be sure to get the seafood combo and he'll do any sauce you want.  And put about a pound of pasta underneath.  You'll notice a lot of pretty big guys eating here - for good reason.  If you're the type to order a salad, beware. I'm not sure they'd even have lettuce.

Had a really fantastic experience at Hellgate on the Park last night.  I can't wait to go back!

Service was great, our server was attentive and nice and the chef and/or owner came by a few times to say hi and ask us how everything was.  They gave us each a free glass of wine!

As an appetizer we had the fresh mozzarella and tomato, it was awesome. 

I couldn't decide what to get so I asked the server what her favorite meal was, took her advice and got the seafood fra diable.  It was a huge plate of shrimp, calamari and clams over pasta and it was the best meal I've had in a while.  I ate every bite.  My friends didn't think I would be able to finish it... I showed them!   :mrgreen:

Of course I made a point to try everything that everyone ordered, and there were five of us.  My sampling included the eggplant parm, penne with vodka sauce, veal marsala, and meat balls.  Every single thing was awesome. 

Here's the best part, the five of us had 4 rounds of drinks, giant meals, appetizer, bread... and out bill was $130!

So we ate at the Hell's Gate Restaurant last night, and it was delicious!

The service was very pleasant (we had a very muscular woman waitress). The food was slow to come out, but we were totally understanding, considering the kitchen was a corner room with two guys in it.

I had the shrimp fra diablo, which was amazing. My girlfriend had the shrimp parmigiana, and our friend had calamari over linguini. All of us loved the food.

There were a couple of things we ordered that they did not have, like clams and manicotti, but we sort of knew going in they had a limited menu at all times.

I would DEFINITELY reccommend going there if you like Italian food and bars. It is a perfect combination, a good date spot, and also a nice place to grab some beers and pasta with your friends.
