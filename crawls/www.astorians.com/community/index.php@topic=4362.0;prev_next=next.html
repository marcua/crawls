Has anyone been to this new place on Hoyt?  It's about a block down from the Astoria Blvd. subway stop.  It's been sitting unopened for a few years.  Now it's finally open for business.  It's a good location for a decent pub.  Lots of foot traffic on Hoyt and more to come when Eagle becomes occupied (I'm not starting anything or suggesting I'm in favor of the Eagle conversion!).  However, it's next door to a partially abandoned building that seems occupied by the homeless.  Also, I was told it's owned by the same owner as the bar Tsolias which is further down on Hoyt.  Tsolias is scary on a good day.  These things make me wonder how hot this Hot Spot can possibly be.  If anyone dares to open the door and go in, please report back - in detail.

I started this so I'll submit my review after actually going in the place.  I was pleasantly surprised.  VERY friendly staff.  We had a great bartender.  Young female.  Very friendly and knew her drinks and how to make them.  Spoke perfect English.  I say these things because I find this not to be the case at many Astoria bars.  The decor is almost comical.  They spent a lot of time and money on the place but the taste is somewhat garish.  It's colorful.  I can say that much.  An awkward looking ceramic tile floor and tons of chrome tables and chairs.  Who will sit there, I have no idea.  It's not a restaurant.  But expecting something far worse, the place is really not all that bad.  They have a great jukebox (in terms of selection) but you need a user manual to operate it.  And it gets worse after each drink.  I would give it a shot if you live in the area of the Astoria Blvd subway stop.  The semi abandoned building adjacent to it still puts a damper on the whole experience.  (When will they finally bulldoze that mess?)But they're trying and they did a pretty good job.  I hope it makes it and draws a decent after work crowd.  There is also a huge screen TV for sports fans but it's hardly a sports bar.
