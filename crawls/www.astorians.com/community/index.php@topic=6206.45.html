The strongest, most vibrant neighborhoods are the ones of great diversity.  This extends to gastro-entertainment.  So let there be the chain restaurants along with the bistros.  Both fulfill a different function in the neighborhood and both strengthen the neighborhood.  Not every teenager coming from the movies or every multi-rugrat family wants to eat quaint after seeing a flick.  They want simple, relatively cheap.  Too much of any one thing, including boutiques and eateries, is never a good thing.  Give 'em the choice to make on their own.

And when it comes to health related concerns: Eating out is almost never a healthy decision as compared to eating at home.  This includes the quality of food, additives and the handling and preperation of food (if you eat out, don't waste your money on purell).  If you are at a point that your personal health is dependent upon the quality of fare dished up at restaurants, then it is time to re-evaluate things.

And this is coming from someone who would never eat at a place like that.

First of all, to me, PETA stands for "People Eating Tasty Animals".  I am not some ultra organic barefoot wannabe who makes my own clothes and fights for greenpeace.  I'm just letting you know some information from a different perspective.  Some people have never been to an Applebees, and that's great... don't bother.  I know every small mom and pop place isn't the best either.  My point is that at least they're cooking things.  Chain places like Olive Garden and Applebees don't even put items on plates half the time.  That manicotti they serve comes cryovac sealed from the factory ON THE PLATE and they put the entire thing into a steamer.  That to me is sad.  Also, eating out is totally unhealthy.  From zero stars to 5 stars, it's not good for you.  That being said, I love eating out, but the places I go to aren't loading their cheese filling with Xanthan gum and Ultrasperse.  And the meat is actual meat and not mechanically pressed with wheatex or some other soy derivative.  Believe me, it might taste like meat, but it's not.  I'll admit it, when I was poor in college I ate the hell out of some applebees wings and mozzarella sticks.  It was great.  Knowing what I know now, the chemicals they put in that stuff really gums your system up.  I won't touch it.  Make your own mind up.  Go there and see if it's worth it.  Just know from a physiological perspective, it's not good food.
