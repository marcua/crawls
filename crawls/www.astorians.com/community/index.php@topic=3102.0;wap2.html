Harlan:
And the "funny" thing is that as far as animal cruelty goes, foie gras isn't nearly as bad as what they do in industrial chicken farms. The ducks fed for foie gras tend to be fairly free-range for all but the force-feeding phase of their life, and ducks don't have a gag reflex so putting a tube down their throat isn't all that cruel. In contrast, most chickens spend their entire lives in a cage the size of a sheet of paper in a huge barn that reeks of ammonia, and have their beaks snipped. I'd rather be a foie gras duck than a McNugget chicken any day of the week...
