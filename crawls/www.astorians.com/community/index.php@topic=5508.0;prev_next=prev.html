What style do you want to study?

There's a traditional, pretty formal Shotokan place b/w Bway & 34th Ave on Steinway (2nd floor).  It's very friendly, pretty formal, and it's pretty much up to you to push yourself as hard/easy as you want to go - really good if you want the formal, traditional and philosophical approach to martial arts.

There's a mixed martial arts place on Steinway at 34th Ave - each style/teacher rents out space, so there's lots of different styles happening at once, in different parts of the studio. Don't know much about it personally, though.

There's a Muy Thai place on Steinway at 28th Ave - real tough, real good fighters, real loose environment (go there if you already know what you're doing and want to train hard & spar).

If you want to commute a little bit, there's a Jujitsu/kajukembo place in Kew Gardens - one particularly amazing sensei, will push you super hard if you're into it, but the commute can be a pain.  be prepared to fight & fight hard.

There are also a lot of Tai Kwan Do places, but most of them are really commercial and cater mostly to children. 

hope this helps - welcome to the neighborhood.  any more info you need, happy to help.
