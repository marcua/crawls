Title: Re: PS 234
Post by: Gretchen on January 24, 2009, 07:25:43 PM
I have one son there, currently in 4th grade.  He's been pretty happy there.  He was in the Talented and Gifted program at PS 150 in Sunnyside for a year and hated it.  This is obviously not as challenging for him, but the teachers are so far all good and the school has a pretty good atmosphere.  It's much more welcoming than PS 150 or PS 85 (where I have one in preK) in my experience, they don't treat parents as dangerous interlopers in the school as those others do.  I also figure that even though he's not getting all the accelerated curricula he would have in the gifted program he has time to pursue his own interests and learn the stuff he really wants to.  They have a good art program and music as well.

That said, the 4th grade is brutal for State tests and they hammered the prep pretty hard. He's got the ELA behind him and just starting on the math prep now, 6 weeks before the test.  I understand that a lot rides on the scores for the school but I think they're prepping the kids too much.  It stressed them out and it's boring (at least for my son who does pretty well on tests.) 

All in all given the options in the area I think it's as good a school as you're going to find.  I was not impressed with the Charter School, but I know a lot of people are, though it's hard to get into.  If it's your zoned school I'd recommend it.  There's a lot to be said for having it close to home.
