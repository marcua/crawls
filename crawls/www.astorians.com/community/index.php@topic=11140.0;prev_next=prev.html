Thai Pavilion
« on: February 07, 2009, 02:10:15 AM »
So I've been to the restaurant and I decided to order in. 

The last time I had gone I remembered the fried rice being excellent.  But I also remember the portions being rather small and somewhat pricey for what you're getting on the plate.  The other thing that sticks out in particular is that the meat in the curry dishes are tough and dry.

My impressions from tonight:

Steamed mussels were tasty although they had a little too much broth.  The container it came in was actually filled with liquid to the brim.  I was surprised because I had thought that the point of steam mussels was to steam them, not boil.  Either way, the taste was great and the mussels were fresh.  I'm not going to complain too much although, once again, I was shocked by how much liquid there was.

The Seafood Yum Salad had tender seafood, but the flavor was lacking.  Yum is supposed to be more sour and spicier.  In general, the taste was too bland.  In fact, the dish was just flat out bland, which is a very disappointing descriptor especially for Thai food, which is known for its strong flavors.

The red curry I ordered wasn't red curry at all.  They decided to give me Massaman curry instead.  Although I was disappointed, I do love a good Massaman, so I wasn't about to complain...until I realized that the dish itself was lacking.  They had used too little curry powder.  The final taste was a rather smooth but tasteless curry.  And again, the meat was tough as nails.  It was actually a lot like eating jerky in my curry.  This makes me wonder if they know how to prepare meat at all...

The Pad Thai I ordered was tasty.  In fact, it was slightly too tasty.  But at least it had taste.  It was a little sweeter and saltier than I would have liked it, but in general this was my favorite dish.  The one thing I couldn't help but notice was the portion size.  I'm not sure how you're supposed to get full eating the entree portion, especially when half of the take out box was bean sprout and lemon.  To me, it would have been a decent appetizer portion and nothing more.

My final bill came out to be $44.  I think this is way overpriced especially for the food.  I know that the base ingredients for the food doesn't amount to $10 and I think that I could have made better renditions by myself at home.  Overall, I wasn't impressed with what I got for how much I paid.  I would have been more satisfied, if perhaps, I had only doled out $25 instead.

Final assessment:

(Out of a 10 point scale, 1 being poor, 10 being spot on)

Taste: 6.5
Value: 4

Would I recommend?  Based on tonight's impression it would be a firm "NO" unless you're really hungry and don't mind getting charged extra for some slightly above average Thai.

"Slightly above average" is my estimation, too, though I don't find it overpriced and I don't think the portions are too small.  I get food there often because I live around the corner; I don't consider it one of the best Thai places in the hood but I do find it consistently pretty good.

In the past I found the food less spicy than it ought to be.  Recently they've upped the chili level.  It's still not "Thai spicy" but it's spicy enough to satisfy me.
