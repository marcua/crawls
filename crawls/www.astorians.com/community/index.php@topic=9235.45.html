I have to wonder....

So many on the board lament all these 99cent and discount stores popping up. But it takes money and profit to pay the rent. Which means these stores must be making enough to pay rent and employees. Which in turn means there must be plenty of people shopping there. Ever think the reason that places like El Mundo discount open up is because there is actually a market for that sort of store in the area? It's not like everyone in astoria is rolling in dough. There are plenty of families and immigrant families in the neighborhood. It costs a lot to raise a family in this city and discount stores like this must offer some sort of relief or offset some costs for these people. I know my (single working)mom certainly took advantage of all the discount stores in Jackson Hts when my brother and I were younger-I imagine it to be the case here in Astoria.
As long as the stores we have here are profitable more will pop up. If the store is awful, they won't make money and they will close. Simple as that. They aren't going to stay in business if no one is shopping there.

And as for El Mundo...that place is ALWAYS packed when I pass by. They aren't going anywhere anytime soon. And if it means a few families can stretch a buck or two, then good for them.

Proud discount store shopper who would have an orgasm if Jack's 99cent store opened in Astoria. Because guess what-I like to save money at these places!
