Hi Neighbor!  I live at 42nd St & 34th Ave.  Here's a very "local" start for you:  The Brazilian by the pound place on 34th Ave at 45th (or so) is great even for a non-meater like myself.  Cafe Bar (currently closed for revovation), my home away from home, is on 34th Ave at 36th St is my go to place for brunch, drinks, dinner, hang-out.  The dry cleaner at 34th Ave & 42nd St is reliable.  Pathmark is just up the street at 42nd & Northern Blvd.

I also live nearby on 46th.
There are 2 laundromats on the same block on 34th ave btwn 44th and 43rd. i use the one with the blue awning since its the closest to me.
the gyro place on 45th street is pretty good. i don't want to see them close! it's kind of a random stretch.
i usually get my haircut at studio 34. they don't take plastic but mina (or mila...i forget) is good.
only place i've ever had my nails done is angel spa on steinway just off 34th ave.
pathmark is just up the street, but there's also the shopping center on 48th with the stop and shop (in addition to the marshall's, old navy, aj wright, conway, closeout paradise etc). typically the stop and shop tends to be cheaper. so i check their circulars online weekly like the uber nerd i am.
time cafe on 45th and bway isn't super great, but its pretty good.
I wish we had a good diner nearby but bel aire delivers this far. well, there's a new one on norther blvd btwn 45 and 46 but i'm a little scared since the last one was pretty sucky.
if i'm going to get pizza/past delivered i get it from lentini's. i have frequently been disappointed in gino's which is much closer.
if you like indian, seva delivers in this direction. a lot of places don't seem to like to deliver to 34th ave.
King Garden on 43rd and 34th ave has all your chinese and japanese food needs met.
that's what i can think of. feel free to ask anymore about this end. i was beginning to think i was the only person on this board in this neck of the woods.

You know about the Entenmann's outlet for bread (and delicious baked goods) right? It's not exactly a "hot spot" but I've saved a ton of money buying my bread there since I make my lunch every day. They sell them 3 loaves for $3 - I take it home and freeze what I'm not using since bread freezes well. They get deliveries Wed - Sat. I actually just came back from there and their bread shelves were full, and they had additional carts that they were stocking. It's not just boring/unhealthy white bread either - I got a couple loaves of whole grain and one of seedless rye.

I made the mistake of going there a couple times on a Tuesday and they had barely anything. But if you go later in the week, you should be good.

I'm in this neck of the woods also.  I would add to the above:

There are many, many very good restaurants in these parts.
