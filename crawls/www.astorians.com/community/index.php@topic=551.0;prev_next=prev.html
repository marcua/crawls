One of the photographs in a new photography show by local artist Paul Takis currently going up at freeze peach cafe has triggered a controversy surrounding the Hell Gate railroad trestle.



The controversy surrounds the name of the trestle itself. Where does the name come from?



Two theories sofar include a derivation from the dutch for 'beautiful passage' and the idea that the trestle got its name from tragedies surrounding the whirlpool under the bridge that took several lives during its construction...



Anyone?

That part of the river was called the Hell Gate long before that bridge was built. It's called the Hell Gate because of the fast and treachorous current there. I have also read that "Hell Gate" roughly translates to "beautiful strait" in Dutch, but I don't speak Dutch.

At any rate, the name long preceded the bridge.
