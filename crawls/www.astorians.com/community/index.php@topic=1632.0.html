Historically, Astoria stopped at Washington Avenue (36th Avenue to you young uns). Beyond that, it became Long Island City.

When Zip codes came into being (if I recall correctly, that was around the summer of 1963, but did not come into regular/required use until somewhere around 1967-68) 11101 and 11106 were defined as Long Island City; 11102, 11103 and 11105 were defined as Astoria. Interestingly, 11104 became Sunnyside although it borders on Long Island City's 11101 but it had all those pre-war 6 and 7 story buildings that it was thought it should have its own zip code because of the large residential properties.

When I was a teen growing up in Astoria, there were turf (READ: gang) wars between the groups from Astoria and LIC. Your home address defined which high school you went to. LIC residents were automatically sent to Long Island City High School. Astoria kids went to W.C Bryant High School. The private and religious-based schools had their own wars. There were no race problems; nah! it was all about turf. You *had* to chose. Your personal safety depended on it.

When Bryant and LIC played in the citywide final basketball championship, every cop in the city was needed to maintain peace. I don't even remember who won—I only remember the guy who shielded my girlfriend and me to get us out of there alive!

Who the hell said *those* were the good old days?  :?
