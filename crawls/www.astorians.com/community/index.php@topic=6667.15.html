Well, the MTA ran the N Express tonight and I got off at the Astoria Blvd. stop and noticed the former lumber yard at 32nd Street and Astoria Blvd. is being torn down as I write.  I assume an apartment will be going up, but that isn't the most appealing place to live. It is at the foot of the Tri-borough Bridge & the GCP.

And development in Hunter's Point continues unabated.  I noticed the skeleton of a new building that is going up on Jackson Ave. (I saw it from a distance so don't have the exact address).

And the area along Queens Plaza continues its frenzied redevelopment.  There are four or five buildings right near Met Life's soon to be former HQ going up.  And the area right around the 39th Street subway stop is rapidly being developed.  There are at least four apartment buildings being constructed.

One interesting building in Hunter's Point that is done or just about done can be seen from the Queensboro Plaza subway stop.  It is kind of behind or south of that ugly parking garage.  You can see the "for rent" sign or banner and the building has a clear glass facade.  I assume it is some sort of office building.   It almost looks like a storage facility.
