Title: Re: Whats Missing In Astoria
Post by: essen on April 10, 2012, 11:02:05 AM
Are you going to open a place? If so, are you seriously suggesting that Ditmars might desire additional bakeries, or that 30th Avenue might want "a Watawa"? If you are looking for ideas, a good rule to follow is that if it's some sort of ethnic cuisine, it should be authentic and something the owner is really passionate about. Don't open a place just because it sells food that seems to be trendy and then fail in the execution because you really don't care about the food. In general I think the people who post on this board just want stuff where it seems like the people running our local businesses care about the quality of what they're selling, and that it's not an act just to attract the kind of customer who cares about that sort of thing. We can tell the difference.

I would appreciate a kitchen store that sells good quality cookware for a reasonable price on Ditmars. You'd never know by looking at the businesses in Astoria that anyone actually likes to cook at home. But a lot of people do, and if I want to buy good quality cookware for a reasonable price, I have to go into Manhattan.
