 http://www.ny1.com/ny1/content/index.jsp?stid=10&aid=78302

Brazilian Immigrants Open Coffee Shop In Queens
February 08, 2008

Two women from Brazil have joined forces to carry out their American dream by opening Brazilian cafe called Chocolate Spoon in Queens.

City Councilmen Eric Goia and Peter Vallone were on hand for the official ribbon cutting in Astoria. The cafe has been serving the community for about a month now.

"It's a lot of work, many hours working, and it's a blessing," said co-owner Odette Bernades.

Odette and her business partner, Patricia Faria, decided to pursue their dream after Faria backed out of a flight to Brazil that later crashed – killing 200 people on board.

"Take that chance or never again," said Faria.

The two enrolled in the Artisan Baking Center -- a city-sponsored program at LaGuardia Community College.Bernardes and Faria learned how bake, and how to make their business work, and most of the classes and workshops were free.

Vallone says the shop is a product of some very motivated individuals and government funding.

"It's always great to see when the money that we're able to provide to the community bears fruit, or food in this case, and we're very happy about that," said Vallone.

- Ty Chandler
