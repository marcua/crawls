Back to the original discussion:

I have also had very frustrating encounters with the 114th, and so has my wife:

I had my wallet pick-pocketed two years ago.  Within two hours someone had charged several thousand dollars on my credit card and and emptied my checking account.  My bank at the time was Citibank and Citibank wanted me to file a police report in order to credit my money back to me, which sounded like a reasonable enough request.  It all seemed very routine from Citibank's end. 

When I went to the 114th they demanded to know where I had been pick-pocketed.  How would I know where I was pick-pocketed?  Thats the whole point of picking someone's pocket.  They don't know its happening.  Thats like asking someone "Where did you lose your keys?"  The obvious answer is that if I knew where they were they wouldn't be lost!  Anyway, they insisted and I told them it happened somewhere between my commute from Astoria to Manhattan.  That was all they needed to hear.  "So then, what you are saying sir, is that you realized you were missing your wallet when you were in Manhattan.  You need to go to the precinct in Manhattan to file your claim.  We can't help you."  With that, they basically refused to discuss the mater further.  A week lost without my money.  Of course, the Manhattan precinct said that I had to file the claim at the precinct where I live.  ANother week lost without my money.  I went back to the 114th, this time armed with a print out from the NYPD web site saying that it was THEIR responsibility to take care of this claim because I live in their precinct.  They grudgingly accepted, but then came the worst part, dealing with this woman called PFC Williams.  I guess she's the data entry person. 

You have never met a more lazy, sullen, bureaucratic, entitled, condescending, and useless person in your life.  Think DMV poster child.  When people say the police department is under staffed I laugh.  If the staff they have, actually worked like we do in the private sector, they would be OVER staffed.  PFC Williams made me wait while she got back from her one hour lunch.  She then waltzed in for five minutes and expressed surprise that I was still there.  She then disappeared for another fifteen minutes.  When she finally, reluctantly, sat down in front of her computer to input my claim she kept insinuating that she had never heard of a person making a pickpocket claim.  She then asked me why I needed it.  I said it was necessary for the bank to restore the money to my checking account.  She asked me if I had a letter from the bank manager stating that.  I told her I did not have a letter from the bank manager, but that I was making a report of a theft.  She said I needed the letter from the bank, and immediately stopped typing and left her chair.  With that, she refused to take my police report.  Another week lost.  The bank couldn't believe my request.  THey had never heard of such a thing, but they wrote me a letter anyway.  The following week I went back with my letter.  I was there at 9 am.  She was supposed to start at 9 am.  I had called ahead to find out.  She didn't show up till 10:15.

I eventually forced them to file my claim, but it took a month and a half.  I had to dig into my savings to pay for my bills that month thanks to the 114th's refusal to do their job.  I'll never forget pfc Williams.  I can't believe my taxes pay for her salary.  The 114th is a disaster.
