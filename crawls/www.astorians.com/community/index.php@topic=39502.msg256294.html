Another observation I've made about the car culture in Astoria is...

most cars seem to be gas-guzzling SUVs.  Why do people need such huge cars?  How did families get by without SUVs back in the day, and when families were even larger?

It's not uncommon for me to see a car 'parked' on a residential sidewalk, facing the home's garage, with the back of the car in between two legally parked cars, and the front of  the  car on the sidewalk, allowing just enough space for pedestrians to walk onto their driveway and curve back around onto the sidewalk.  In such instances, there is a SECOND car that this same family has, parked in their driveway.  So you might ask... 'and what about their garage?...is there a third car in the garage?"  No, usually not.  And why not?...oh, well that would be because their garage is full of JUNK...  Stuff, stuff and more stuff.

Then these people have the audacity to demand 'more parking spots'.

Everyone  in a family apparently must have 'their own car'.  Folks can't rideshare.  Folks can't pick up or drop off a family member.  Folks can never use publlic transportation or a taxi.  Everyone 'must' be able to get in their own car and  Go, whenever they want, 24/7.  Everyone buys stuff on Black Friday, just for the sake of it.  QVC.  Everyone wants large flatscreen TVs, and  the  next year they want an even bigger one.

Put it all together, and it's just a really disgusting way to live.  Self-centered and such an utter waste of resources...clogging our streets, polluting the air, filling our landfills with more unwanted/old stuff....

I get that the old-school Astorians resent newcomers moving in and trying to alter the neighborhood to their preferred way of living.  But just because that's the way Astoria has always been (a car-centric neighborhood) doesn't make it right.  People need to really think about their  lifestyle choices and ask themselves why they are so resistant to considering change, or other ways of being.  The thing is, after a certain age, most people should NOT be behind the wheel of a car.  Eyesight goes, people literally 'shrink' and can't see over the wheel, and reaction time slows down.  Certain older people behind the wheel put themselves and  others in danger.  After a certain age, drivers should be re-tested every year in order to maintain their licenses.

So wouldn't it be in drivers' own interest to not be so dependent on  their  cars?  Because one day, your license could be totally taken away from you.  When this happens to drivers, they often say they've 'lost their independence', and then they become depressed.  True independence and freedon however, IMHO, is in NOT owning a car.  I've never owned a car in my life and have no interest in  ever owning one.  I manage just fine getting where I need to and want to, and I save thousands of dollars each year  in the process.  No parking fees.  No shoveling.  No registration,  inspection, repair, gas, toll fees.  No AAA membership fee.  No parking headaches.  No traffic jams.  Not feeling guilty about contributing to pollution.  No having to work my schedule around opposite-side parking.  lol.  Etc.
