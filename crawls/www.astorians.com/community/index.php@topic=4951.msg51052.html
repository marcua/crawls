How complicated are your taxes? If you're salaried, rent a house, don't have significant investment income, and are taking the standard deduction, you can almost certainly do it yourself. If you make less than (I think) $55K, you can download free software from the IRS to fill out the forms for you so you don't have to worry about math errors. You should have a copy of last years' tax forms that H&R submitted for you. Assuming your situation hasn't changed much, you might walk through it to see if you can figure out what they did, and then try doing the same things this year.

If you own a house and have lots of investment income, (a) lucky you, and (b) I guess you should hire a real accountant... :) Preferably not one wearing a costume!  :crazy:

(I'm doing mine by hand this year, as usual, and hoping my Federal refund is bigger than my State/Local amount-owed!)

Honestly, I would just use an online tax program.  I've used Tax Act online and I've been pleased.  You can do it for free, or fork out a little extra (I think like 10 bucks) and they'll remember your tax information from the previous year and add it to your current.  I actually ended up getting an extra couple hundred back because it remembered that I'd had an income loss with Student Loans and Investments that I was able to carry over.

It's step by step and easy to use and, seriously, if you need help, I could probably walk you through it.  Your taxes would probably take about 30 minutes if you don't have anything crazy. 

And I STRONGLY advise against H&R.  I paid them $210 and the guy missed out on about 500 bucks that I should have gotten back and actually found on my own the next year when I decided to do them myself.  The guy didn't put ANY of my education expenses on the form.  I guess I could have figured it out when I watched him try to dial out on the telephone USING HIS COMPUTER KEYPAD!

Honestly, most of the tax preparers are seasonal and go through a, relatively, short training process.

I e-file mine using TAX SLAYER.com.  It is free if you are 25 or under.  Otherwise it is only 9.95.  It is quick and easy.  They ask you questions through an interactive wizard.  I guess it is much easier if you just have some W2s, standard deductions, and a couple things here and there.  This also includes the FEDERAL and STATE returns.  There are questions about NYC tax, too so that will be included.  I have them just put the refund right into my bank account.

The Student Loan Interest stuff is definitely a good thing to remember.  Max is $2,500 a year.  Also teaching expense of $250 max a year!
