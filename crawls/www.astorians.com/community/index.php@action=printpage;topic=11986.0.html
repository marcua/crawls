Title: Re: NYT Op Ed : the need for nation building
Post by: RedSoxfanNYC on August 27, 2008, 04:21:29 PM
As a quasi-regular reader of Friedman, this is pretty typical of the stuff he brings to the table. I think there is certainly a point to this all, as he points out our infrastructure in many places is indeed crumbling. I don't think using developing countries is necessarily the best example, especially China. It is far easier to go straight to the newest technology when you are working with a blank slate. As an example the phone system in this country started out with copper wiring, as technology changed (to fiber) all of that infrastructure has to be upgraded. In a place like NYC a lot of that is buried beneath streets, buildings etc. Far more expensive to do upgrade that than it is to say run the newest lines to a place that hasn't been developed yet. On top of that you have to deal with opposition from the public that you wouldn't really have in China. Watching the olympics on TV it was apparent that the land they used for the Olympic village wasn't originally vacant. The opposition there has no voice, here people would be protesting in the streets, there would be lawsuits galore etc etc. Then of course there is the issue of funding. Who is going to pay for it? Are they going to raise taxes?

I certainly support ideas like this in earnest as I feel steps like this are necessary for protecting the USA from becoming a has-been in the future. There are a lot of shortsighted "penny-wise, pound foolish" people around that might not though.
