It's a little wacky, but surprisingly refreshing and delicious: avocado and ruby red grapefruit. You slice the avocado into wedges, slice the peel off the grapefruit and segment it (the resulting pieces are called supremes), and you alternate the grapefruit and avocado like a caprese salad. Dress it with a little sprinkle of good olive oil, salt and pepper. The creaminess of the avocado works beautifully with the tart grapefruit. Really good, and unusual. And soooooo easy!!!

Ok, try this.  Get some Champagne vinegar.  Thinly slice some red onion and marinate in the vinegar for about 2 hours.  Drain the vinegar into a bowl and add olive oil and some spices (making a basic italian dressing).  Clean some Belgian Endive and place leaves on a plate.  Place some red onion on a leaf and top with some of the dressing.  A nice simple finger salad.  You can garnish with whatever you want.  You can try putting the tabouleh in a Orange or Yellow pepper, put that in the center of the plate and put the endive cups around the pepper.  Maybe put some grape tomatoes around the endives.  Just a thought here.  Good luck with the party.

This will go with the couscous. It's my favorite early Spring salad, very colorful and adaptable. This feeds 4-6

Beet-Orange-Mint Salad w/ Cheese (optional)
4 beets, golden or red, sliced
2-3 oranges (grapefruit, clementines or tangerines would work too)
1/4 cup crumbled feta or goat cheese (or more)
1/2 cup mint, chopped
2 teaspoons fresh ground cardamom
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon pepper
juice of 1 lemon
2 tablespoons olive oil

You can add pinenuts or walnuts too. The cardamom is the real wow factor here, though it can be done without, but you should use fresh b/c the flavor is really pow. You can also use a vinegar (like champagne or red instead of lemon juice). I also like salads with grilled fruit, like peaches, but alas, no good fruit just yet. :-(
