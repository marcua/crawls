Title: New Book Discussion Program at Greater Astoria Historical Society
Post by: Al122 on August 31, 2010, 02:51:07 PM
Reading Between the Lines – New York Council for the Humanities: Understanding Today’s Activism

A free reading and discussion group at Greater Astoria Historical Society
   
Join us for a series of lively conversations exploring the history of social movements in the United States. Each 90-minute conversation will be facilitated by Al Ronzoni, co–coordinator of our popular “History Roundtable Series”. Participants are asked to attend and contribute to all four sessions. 
____________________________________________________________________________

·Pre-registration required—space is limited. Call 718–278–0700 or visit www.astorialic.org to sign up and get your books now.

·Once you are registered for the series, you can pick up your books at the Greater Astoria Historical Society prior to the first session.

·All meetings will be at the Greater Astoria Historical Society, 4th Floor, 35–20 Broadway, Long Island City, NY 11106
____________________________________________________________________________

Session #1 – Wednesday, September 22, 2010 at 6:00 PM
Title #1 – American Reformers: 1815–1869 by Ronald G. Walters

Session #2 – Wednesday, October 20, 2010 at 6:00 PM
Title #2 – Ahead of Her Time: Abby Kelly and the Politics of Antislavery by Dorothy Sterling

Session #3 – Wednesday, November 17, 2010 at 6:00 PM
Title #3 – Eyes on the Prize: America’s Civil Rights Years, 1954–1965 by Juan Williams

Session #4 – Wednesday, December 15, 2010 at 6:00 PM
Title #4 – Antiwarriors: The Vietnam War and the Battle for American’s Hearts and Minds by Melvin Small
