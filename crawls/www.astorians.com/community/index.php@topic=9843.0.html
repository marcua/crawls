Taxi Refusing Service- hearing?
« on: March 17, 2008, 10:18:41 AM »
Over the weekend I was trying to get a cab back to Astoria from the UES.  This is around 3PM on a Saturday.  I get a cab to pull over (the light said it was available and not off-duty). The driver lets me in, and after I tell him where I'm going he says "I'm off-duty and going home, didn't you see my light?".  Now, I'm not a tourist...I know how to hail a cab correctly.  So the guy was clearly lying because he didn't want to go to Queens.  Unfortunately I neglected to get his medallion number to report to 311.

Are yellow cabs allowed to refuse service like that?  I was under the impression that you were entitled to go anywhere within the 5 boroughs.  Plus, I'm like a 5 minute drive from the Queensboro so we're not talking about the other end of Queens.  This is the second time in about 6 months that this has happened.  That time, the driver just flat-out refused to go to Queens.  WTF?

Re: Cabs refusing service to Queens?
« Reply #1 on: March 17, 2008, 10:29:17 AM »
No, they're not supposed to refuse service to any of the five boroughs.

A few years ago I reported a driver who did that to me - wouldn't go from
Times Square to Astoria late on a Saturday night. I reported him with his
medallion number to the Taxi & Limo commission. They gave me a hearing
about 2 months later with an arbitrator. The person listed to each of us and
found in my favor. The driver was given a warning since it was supposedly a
first offense.

But I was pleased with the process because it was good to know that this
is taken seriously. Plus I found the people at the TLC very helpful  - a guard even
walked me out and to the train station because she didn't want the driver to try to
speak to me afterward.

Next time - hopefully there won't be one - get their number and report them.
If enough people do that, hopefully it won't keep happening.

Check out page 36 in therules for cab drivers.

The only way that the exceptions apply is if his off-duty light was on before you got in. If he'd turned the light on after the last passengers left and then you got into the cab, he's allowed to refuse.
