Volunteers Throughout The Five Boroughs Beautify NYC
 
April 22, 2007

Many people took advantage of the great spring weather Saturday while participating in a little spring cleaning. As NY1's Ruschell Boone explains in the following report, the volunteers say they didn't mind breaking a sweat because it was their way to give back to the city.

It was a great day to be in the Astoria Park Pool, even though those inside were just cleaning it out.

Saturday dozens of volunteers from New York Cares spent several hours getting rid of the debris that accumulated over the winter.

One rubber ducky found was probably from last summer.  

"It's fun actually, because I've never volunteered for anything before,” said participant Katrina Thomas. “This is really cool, a hands on project. I like it."

"I love to swim and what better way to help out than coming out here and helping out cleaning out the pool,” said Alejandra Acosta, another volunteer.

The cleanup was part of the New York Cares 13th annual Hands on New York Day.

Volunteers worked to revitalize community centers, parks, gardens, and other public spaces in all five boroughs. More than 4,000 people signed up to help out.

"It's our biggest turnout ever and the volunteers are willing, so we're just thrilled to make today happen,” said New York Cares Executive Director Ariel Zwang.

In Manhattan, more than 200 volunteers from HSBC worked to improve the Lower East Side Ecology Center. They painted fences, renovated benches, planted flowers, and spread wood chips among other things.

"I'm sort of covered in black,” said HSBC President and Chief Executive Officer Paul Lawrence. “I've been moving soil and spreading mulch, but it's great."

"This is the largest waterfront park below 59th Street and like with many big parks, we have a lot of areas that need more maintenance than the Parks Department can really provide,” said Christine Datz Romero of the LES Ecology Center. “So today we have a fantastic opportunity to really get a head start on the season."

The event marked the end of national volunteer week, and many of the workers say they will be back next year to do it all over again.

-Ruschell Boone
