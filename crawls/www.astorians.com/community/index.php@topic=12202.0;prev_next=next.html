I have a five year old (!) Creative Zen Jukebox (20 GB).  At the time, I refused to buy in to Apple's marketing machine and the hype surrounding the iPods, and I still feel the same way today: iPods are a product of marketing, are expensive and overrated.  When I purchased the Zen, the comparable 20 GB iPod was $80+ more, depending on the retailer.  I've had no complaints at all with the Zen...it has a great battery life (something many iPod owners seem to complain of), the navigation is fairly easy, it plays .mp3/.wma/.ogg files, the sound quality is good with numerous EQ settings, and while it is a bit bulky by today's standards, it still fits easily in my pocket.  I'd recommend a Zen or some other non-iPod to anyone anytime :)

Was a long-time Creative Zen Jukebox fan - 30GB player and then the 60GB player, from about 2000 through early 2007.  Pluses: easy back-and-forth file transfer, tolerable sound, compact. Minuses: incredibly small viewing window, had to reboot far too much, had extremely awful customer service experience when I bought the second and it malfunctioned within weeks -- had to call in the Better Busines Bureau to get results.  But eventually had a fine player.

In early 2007, made the switch to iPod, the grandaddy 160 GB player.  And I have to say, for all those years I pooh-poohed iPods, this thing is phenomenal.  The best design I've seen on any player, large clear window, incredible battery life (I've let it go for 10 hours and still had plenty of juice), tons of accessories available (a downside of Creative players), and with the Bose ear plugs I got with a gift certificate, sounds great.  I love this thing -- am up to about 70 GB of music and am running out of ideas, so I doubt I'll ever fill this thing up unless I start pumping in my CD collection wholesale.  Can't stand the closed/exclusionary system, but now that I'm into large external hard drives and a laptop with enough space to handle the files from this player, not such an issue.  Of course, wasn't too nuts about the price of the iPod either, but who else is putting out a player with that size hard drive, that compact a player and that much battery life?

Never used iTunes and never will.  (Their DRM restrictions aren't so bad, but I'm a firm believer in no DRM at all.)  With EMusic, Amazon, my own CDs and a solid group of like-minded music-fiend friends, I've had more music over the past decade than I know what to do with.  Hardly a Mac acolyte - tend to blanche in their company - but this is one thing Jobs got radically right.
