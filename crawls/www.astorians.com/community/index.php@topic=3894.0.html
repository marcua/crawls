Ever since the weather got cold I have been making soup like a fiend, here's a simple way to make any veggie soup:

Onion, carrots, and celery, and chicken or veggie stock. These are the basic stock ingredients, keep these on hand at all times. I prefer chicken stock over veggie for flavor.

Chop an small onion, two stalks of celery and one large or two small carrots.

Use a root or squash veggie as your main ingredient. I use pumpkin, butternut squash, sweet potato, potato, etc. Peel, de-seed, and chop up about two-three cups.

Heat a large soup pot and a tablespoon of butter, or olive or canola oil, whatever you prefer. Throw in all the veggies and saute for 5 minutes. This "sweats" the veggies releasing their flavor.

Pour in the chick/veggie stock. Pour just enough to cover the veggies, usually a 32oz container is more than enough. Simmer the veggies for about a half an hour until the carrots are soft. Let the soup cool a bit.

Put soup in blender and blend in small batches until smooth. Pour back into pot. Add salt and pepper to taste. If you want it creamy add some whole milk or half and half. I usually don't put cream or milk in it.

This batch will make about 48oz. 1 1/2 of those large chinese soup containers.
