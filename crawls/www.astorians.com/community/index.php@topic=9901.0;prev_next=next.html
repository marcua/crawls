This may be the first Tasty's review. I was doing some errands, passed by, and it was open and bustling.  I decided to order the Chicken Souvlaki Platter, figuring that covers grilled meat, fries and salad, three things I look for in a good diner experience.  I am happy to say they didn't disappoint.  The chicken kabobs were tender, juicy and plentiful.  The fries were also tender, not tough or "twice-fried," and the greek salad was huge with plenty of feta, but oddly, without olives, and with shredded lettuce instead of pieces.   It arrived with delicious grilled pita triangles.   I couldn't finish it, although I gave it the old college try.  Service was good, the ambiance pleasant, an overall good first experience, I'd say.

Here's the best part for me.  This platter cost $7.25 plus tax.  The same platter At Mike's Diner costs $9.60! When I got home I compared other menu items price-wise and discovered that a bacon and eggs breakfast is $.80 less than Mike's, as is a hamburger deluxe; a Roumanian Steak entree is $15.95 vs. $19.80 at Mike's.  Don't get me wrong, I love Mike's but I love saving a buck here and there too, and I have always felt that some of their food is overpriced.

I also think that the folks at Tasty's deliberately set their prices lower so as to compete with Mike's. 
My second acid test will be to read more reviews, and to try a breakfast there, as I am a fan of Mike's eggs and home fries.  Tasty's offers something called a "Ditmars Frittata," with sausage, basil, tomato & mozzarella.  Yum! Their smoothies and desserts look good too.  Their hours are listed as 6am-midnight, 7 days.  Welcome to the neighborhood, Tasty's, your prices are refreshing in these tough economic times.

p.s. The takeout menus are ready and deliveries were available when I was there today.
p.p.s. I do not know anyone at this restaurant or have any affiliation to it whatsoever.
