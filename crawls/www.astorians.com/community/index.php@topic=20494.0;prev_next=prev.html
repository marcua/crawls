Great video, I can add memories of what was where in the 23rd Ave and 31st St.-32nd St. area where I lived back in the 40's and 50's.

The Southeast corner of 23rd Ave. and 31st St. which now is home to a Greek restaurant was the home of Beebe Radio although it really only occupied the corner not the amount of space the restaurant does.

Mr. Beebe was the neighborhood radio repair man and later started to sell TV sets when they became popular in the late 1940s. He put a 10 inch TV in the window of his store facing 23rd Ave. and even piped out the audio so the locals could experience the new phenomenon that was just becoming available. Folks started to bring their folding chairs to claim a good spot each night even though the available fare was spotty and about the only film showed repeatedly was the Jack London Story a 1943 movie that one of the broadcasting stations must have owned the rights to.

You could easily count the number of TV owners on a street by looking for antennas mounted on the roof of private homes or on the roof of an apt. building since each receiver needed it's own antenna, we had eight antennas on our block of private homes and apt. houses that probably housed at least a hundred families.

A couple of doors just East of Beebes was Joe Pospisil's bike sales and rental shop which was a good sized store where Joe repaired, sold and rented bikes by the hour since not many parents could yet afford a bike for their kid and a parent could rent a nice bike from Joe for 20 cents per hour so Dad could teach his son to ride as my father did for me. After I learned to ride and could not come up with the rental fee sweeping Joe's store or packing up the cardboard new bikes came in would get you a credit worth an hours riding time.

After a few months or a year it usually came to be that your favorite rental ride was your birthday gift through a deal worked out between Joe and your folks that was affordable by each party and was never forgotten by the lucky kid. Joe was a neighborhood hero and right-fully so. Astoria was the best place in the world in my eyes.
