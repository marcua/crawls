Title: New Brazilian Restaurant
Post by: Samba Grill Brazilian BBQ on June 26, 2008, 11:33:48 PM
Ola!  We just opened on Tuesday, June 17th.  For those familiar with the area, we took over the old Dragon Gate restaurant.   Each day here at Samba Grill we prepare various Brazilian dishes that you will find in all regions of Brazil such as Shrimp sauteed in a yuca cream sauce (Bobo de Camarao), Chicken with okra (Frango com Quiabo), Chicken Stroganoff, Thinly Sliced grilled steak with onions, Feijoada which is mixed pork with black beans, Empadao (similar to chicken pot pie), Tilapia fish in coconut/tomato sauce, Codfish with potato (Bacalhau), etc.   We have over ten types of meat on our rotating BBQ Grill... skirt steak, sirlion, beef ribs, turkey wrapped in bacon, chicken, brazilian sausages...  You can choose to eat by the pound (self serve) or rodizio style service (all you can eat) where our servers will come around will all the meats and cut right off the skewers onto your plate and the buffet table is unlimited ($22.95).   
Our desserts....Brazilian style caramel flan, chocolate cake with coconut filling, passion fruit mousse, chocolate mousse, etc.

We will have our liquor license soon....beer, wine, sangria!   We hope you enjoy our food, atmosphere and service! Obrigado!
P.S. Our delivery service will be up and running soon and will post update when we will begin!!

Title: Re: New Brazilian Restaurant
Post by: Samba Grill Brazilian BBQ on June 27, 2008, 11:08:07 AM
Hello! Yes, I am glad to see that this site really brings the people of Astoria together. It not only helps to promote business but it also allows us to see if we are doing the right thing through people's input, questions, etc.
So to answer your questions...when you step into our restaurant our waiters will ask if you would like to serve yourself or to have sit down service.  If you choose to serve yourself, you fill your plate and then weigh it on the scale by the register.  You can pay on the spot or pay at the end of your meal by giving your name at the counter and they will leave a check open so you can add items instead of paying each time.   For those not familiar with Brazilian food paying by pound is actually a good way to familiarize yourself.  You can get a little bit of each food and then on your next run at the table you can fill up on what you really like.  It's also good for people who want to take out.  Or go for the other option, Rodizio service, you take your buffet items like other buffet restaurants and the meats just keep coming from the waiters.  You can ask for more or less of any type of meat you like.  The bill comes to your table so no need to stop at the register.  If someone at your table chose by pound they are the only ones who have to stop at the register to get their plate weighed in and it will be noted your on check.  It sounds complicated but it actually works out pretty smoothly! There are many restaurants in Brazil that work this way and they are very popular.
We are working on our delivery menu and will distribute them once they are ready.  Items on that menu will consist of foods we have everyday such as rice, beans, french fries, yucca and plantains.  Together with your choice of meat(s).  We will also have a special of the day that you can order.   I hope I answered your questions....thank you for your inquiry and hope to see you soon!
