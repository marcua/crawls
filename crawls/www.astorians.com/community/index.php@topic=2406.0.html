chowhound
« on: June 21, 2006, 11:27:28 AM »
Someone, I think it was Jonathan (or maybe someone else), was wondering the other day about Chowhound. If you hadn't heard, Chowhound was bought out by cNet (a tech news and review set of sites) back in March. After spending another 30 seconds of my life waiting for the 10-year-old Chowhound software to provide its massive, horribly organized page of comments on my computer screen, I decided to do a little google searching for any information on when cNet's new software for Chowhound will be up and running. I wasn't able to find an answer to that question, but I did find a blog written by a developer of the new site, PJ Hyett. Mr. Hyett sayeth:

Quote
Stay tuned for some interesting stuff on the Rails front, it's been a very productive month at CNET working on the new-and-improved Chowhound. Lots of caching, recursion, testing, and integration with a Java-powered search engine (gasp!).

We also have an interesting approach on how we think forum software should operate, and that's why we're rolling our own as opposed to using any existing application. Chowhound, as it stands, may use software that's almost ten years old, but no one can deny the quality of the content on the site, and we have every intention of keeping it that way.

"Rails" refers to Ruby on Rails, the hip new system for developing Web 2.0 applications. The "gasp" refers to the fact that the existing Chowhound system has no searching or organization at all! I'll be interested to see how their forum software differs from, say, the Simple Machines Forum software that runs this place...
