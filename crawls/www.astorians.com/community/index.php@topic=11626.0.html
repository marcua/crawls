Wow. I guess I'm in the minority here. This site is actually the only moderated one I post on.

The other sites I post on don't have many "trolls" but regular members will occasionally post a message that is "trolling"--basically saying something ridiculous you don't really believe just to get laughs or spurr on conversation with a new guy who doesn't know you or know your political agendas. It's not nearly as brutal as this stuff is.

But I've always felt that the best way to deal with trolls is through the other membership. People call trolls out. On one of my favorite places to post, trolls frequently receive scores and comments as to whether their "troll" was effective. This calls the posts out as what they are, but still acknowledge the humor. Troll is happy for the lulz and the community gets to make light of it.

Also, 4chan, /b/ board or not, is a big messy screwed up board. It shouldn't be viewed as the "norm" for the 'net.

And beyond that, these kids that kill themselves over their MySpace hack jobs or lost iPods need something a lot more than protection from an internet troll: they need parents that take a more active roll in their lives and get them help. Meier didn't kill herself "because" of the internet. She had problems before the fake internet boy and her parents thought sheltering her more would help. Granted, cruelty after a kid's death is whack, but this isn't the internet's fault.
