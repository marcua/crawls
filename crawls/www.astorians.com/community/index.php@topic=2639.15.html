Yes, I've worked for and with real estate developers.  Some are good people, some aren't.  When I worked at Kathy Zamechansky and Associates, I mainly wrote newsletters for health care facilities and nursing homes, and press releases for ribbon-cuttings.  I think that has little impact on my opinions about what is going on in Astoria or development.  In addition, I don't see where I or anyone else should have to post caveats about ourselves when posting our opinions in any thread.  I don't see any personal information about you, who you are, your jobs over the past two decades, your affiliations with any groups or anything in your profile or in the hello thread, I just see you snipping at others and engaging in negativity and personal attacks, as well as hijacking threads regularly to discuss "over-development".
When I served as the Chief of Staff to City Councilmember Alan Jay Gerson, I worked with the GVSHP quite a bit.  I know them well and am a huge fan of their work.  GVSHP has next to nothing to do with Astoria and development.  GVSHP exists to fight in-appropriate development and to fight for expansion of historical districts in their catchment area, and they do incredibly wonderful work.  I worked alongside them to defeat many proposed buildings and to secure new historic districts - I know them very well.  They are taking on real problems.   Manhattan and parts of the city are facing real over-development.  Towers, every parcel of land being developed, blocked sight-lines, all of these can be big problems.  I don't see that happening in Astoria myself, but if I did, I would be concerned about it.  
I do see a lot of small in-fill projects on vacant lots.  In some of your other posts, you've combined Astoria and Long Island City (which, if I remember correctly, is where you live - wasn't it you who said you lived in one of the highrise buildings down there in the elitist fireworks thread?).  
I think the issues facing the two neighborhoods are very different.  The tall buildings being proposed and built are all down in your neck of the woods and in Community Board 2, not up in Astoria and Community Board 1.  I have never heard anyone refer to CB1 as the Wild West.  I don't know where you get that.
The low rise nature and community feel of Astoria is why I've made it my home and why I remain here.  I think there are lots of big buildings proposed and being built in both Elmhurst and Washington Heights - maybe not 30 story buildings, but then their zoning wouldn't permit that, neither would zoning in Astoria, which generally limits buildings to 6 stories, though around some of the big commercial strips you can get up to 9.  
I still don't get why you always bash Trinity Lutheran, but after your last post, I did some asking around, and they only were talking about a 9 story building, if even that - which is what they can legally build under the current zoning, which is hardly tall, let alone, "very very very tall", as you characterized it especially given the 6 story buildings on the block and adjacent to the lot, and the project has been on hold for sometime.  Do you have something against Lutherans or just churches?
In any account, I've heard that the City Planning Commission is already looking at downzoning in Astoria, so your concerns about overdevelopment may end up being much ado about nothing.  I think there are serious infrastructure problems vis-a-vis ConEd and agree that if we continue to see new building in Astoria we need better sewers and power capacity.
