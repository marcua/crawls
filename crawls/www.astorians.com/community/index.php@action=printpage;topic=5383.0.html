Title: Re: Avoiding brokers & expensive fees
Post by: daisy on March 01, 2007, 10:43:59 AM
I've gotten 2 apartments out of 5 in Queens without fees.  But it's hard work.  Back then, I used the newspapers, I knew which days had the listings and which to use.  But now, all those listings are online everyday.  So check nytimes.com, newsday, village voice every day, I want to say all the time, or set up some email alert system.  Some sites have this where you select criteria, and they email you listings fitting them as soon as they come in.

As soon as you see a listing you like, get on the phone and be prepared to see it asap.  Have a credit report and references handy.  Be prepared to take it if you like it.  Ask questions.  Make sure you get a lease with the monthly rent in writing. 

It's probably the hardest way to find an apartment, but if you have lots of time on your hands, go for it.  Otherwise, most realtors around here do charge only one month's fee, and the ones that ask for more are screwing you.  See someone else who will charge you a month.  And either have your own current credit report, or don't let them charge you more than $30.  Credit reports are never $100, I learned this the hard way.

Good luck!
