first off, let's agree to disagree on the issue of this mandate.  secondly, it's quite possible i'm not explaining myself properly, so here's a little clarification using your points as reference...

"Is it really a good idea for people to make a decision to adopt a pet based on a tax subsidy?"
     --i don't think anyone who doesn't want a pet (or likes animals) is merely going to get them for a $3500 annual subsidy/tax credit.  as foolish as people can be, i don't think many are THAT foolish.  i do think however that RESPONSIBLE people who want a pet (or an additional pet) may be "enticed" to do so with a little relief.  MUCH more importantly (as mentioned previously), the people who have had to give up their pets due to financial reasons, may now be able to afford to keep them with such a break and keep them from being euthanized or out of shelters (it's been reported that this is happening more and more often during the recession).

"Abuse of animals is actually one of the last signs of a sociopath. Are you advocating putting animals in homes to prevent people from becoming a sociopath? That seems somewhat cruel."
     --are you kidding me?  is this really how you read my comments?  don't be daft!  of course i don't advocate putting animals in the hands of sociopaths.  i believe (from my own experience) that people who grow up around animals are more likely to have a tremendous respect/love for them, and may think twice about what they do to them through the course of their lives (domestically and in the wild).  i think pets in households reinforce these "humanity" issues in human beings on a daily basis, especially in the instances of families/kids having pets (it also promotes a sense of responsibility).  as an (extreme) example, there are prison programs where inmates care and train dogs for assistance programs, and as a result their whole attitude on life changes...i think this says a lot for the power of animals in one's life.

"You say that you will not benefit from others children. That is not true.....As for the funding of education; there are many arguments about the cost of an educated vs an uneducated one. The one I'll make is this; somebody paid for your education."
     --yes of course children are the future...yadda, yadda, yadda...  i never advocated that my taxes shouldn't go to schools, etc.  i used that as an argumentative point towards you not feeling that society should contribute to my pets.  to me the comparison is still the same though.  i don't have kids, never will, so in return for MY lifetime contribution to our society's educational system, i want some sort of break in my taxes for the creatures that i consider my children.  it's not like it's a big chunk either.  in the lifetime of a pet, a $3500 tax break isn't going to pay for the total health and welfare of my pet.  it also isn't like it's an annual cash handout...it's a tax credit.  the government wouldn't be paying for me to have pets, it's just a break.  and as to someone paying for my education, as you put it, i'm sure my family's taxes had some contribution to their only child's education, just as mine goes to yours (maybe not literally yours, but you know what i mean).

i'd further argue that in addition to pets being a big quality-of-life plus for our society, that they also have a huge financial value to it.  i'm sure you know that pet care is a billion dollar industry.  there are millions of people out there who make their living from all the money that people spend on their pets (from the production and retail of food and accessories to their medical care).  i'm sure all those people would also agree that pets are pretty important to our society.

by the way, i'm not sure what you meant by "proper role of government" but i have a feeling we might disagree on some of those issues as well...     besides the government does have a role with our pets.  we have to register them, and follow their laws about them, don't we?  albeit a small amount, the city gets money every year from me for my pets.  part of this mandate is that in order to get the break, they would need to be registered.  i think this act alone could also promote more responsibility in people for their pets, as i'm sure not everyone is registering them.  as i also originally mentioned, i think there should be a further clause that the animals should be adopted (not bought), but that may be stepping over the line of a person's right to choose where their pet comes from.
