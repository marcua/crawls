Quote from: Christine on January 26, 2010, 01:23:17 PM
It was a house with an apartment upstairs and a "finished basement" downstairs (wink nudge.) Even with the additional tenant income, just not worth it.

So, a legal two-family and illegal three.  $860 is still high given the condition you describe, but it makes more sense.  The additional tenant income for that could be $2000-$3000/month depending on the size and condition of the apartments and the exact location of the house.

Using property sales information maintained by the city, I did some quick number crunching.  I used Rolling Sales data for Queens (January 2009-December 2009) (links provided to either a PDF or XLS).  Median sale price for a one-family in Astoria (for all properties with a reported sale price of over $100,000) was $545,000.  Two family was $700,000 and three family was $737,000.  (Even though I excluded properties reported as sold for under $100,000, there are probably still family and other under-market transfers that skew the data.  It would probably be more accurate to have the cut-off be much higher - $400k or $500k.)
