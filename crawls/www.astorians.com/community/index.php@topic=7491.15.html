Fruitbat is absolutely correct...The first thing I learned in self-defense class is not to try to be a hero. You do not argue with the guy holding a gun or knife. If he knows what he's doing, he's dangerous. If he doesn't know what he's doing, or if he's totally insane, he's even more dangerous because you cannot predict his actions. Just do what he (yeah, most likely "he") says and try to stay alive.

As far as people responding to shouting or screaming: My old Psych 101 class said that unless people are addressed directly, they tend not to want to get involved in other people's problems. So if you shout "Help!" to the world in general, people are less likely to respond than if you are making eye contact with one person and saying "Help me!" This scenario was explored in a crowded public place, so I really don't know how your average Astorian would react to hearing noises out on the street at night.
