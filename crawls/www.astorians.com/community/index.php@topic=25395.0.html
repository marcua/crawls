Yes - when I say one of us, I mean a longtime member of Astorians.com - He is Costa01 here, but not been as active while he's been campaigning.  He's always been accessible, and predict he will remain so as Councilmember.


Costa was one of the lead organizers (and one of only two heterosexual ones) of the big marriage equality rally here in Astoria.  He stood up for us when elected officials (thinking of you Senator George Onorato) did not. 


He led the fight to save the Grand Avenue Post Office, rallying Congresswoman Carolyn Maloney and her team, and our local elected officials - Senator Mike Gianaris and Assemblymember Aravella Simotas, to keep it open.


I was proud when he became our District Leader and have watched his organizing.  He helped put LICA (Long Island City Alliance) on the map and is now working on sanitation issues.


I've known him, his wife Lori, their son Niko and their dog, Coco, for a decade and am very excited about this election.


Speaking to the phone calls - I've run city council campaigns and was the deputy campaign manager for Carolyn Maloney a few years back.  In contested primaries and elections, any competent campaign will call prime voters.  It serves three purposes - 1 - to remind voters about the upcoming election, 2 - to gauge whether a possible voter is a supporter or not (so you know if you want to work to pull them to the polls) and 3 - to make sure voters know the name of your candidate.  Usually you want 5-7 "touches" from personal contact to email, to phone calls to mailings, and a few more are usually better.  It's definitely annoying, but it's part of the price of democracy.  Without the outreach, people forget about the election or have no information on the candidates.  The folks actually paying attention often tend to be the folks who get too much contact.


Costa has been out at subways every single morning and his volunteers (all unpaid) are door knocking every night.  He's working hard and I predict he continues to work hard for us in the City Council.
