Hello fellow Astorians, I am a Midwestern transplant to Astoria and so far I love it however, I am looking for a
grocery store that can hopefully meet or exceed the following expectations in no particular order:
CLEANLINESS: obviously I want to buy food from a clean place. Or at least strives for the appearance
                    of clean.
Cost: something that is not crazy with their prices but not necessarily a $.99 store.
Quality: I am not against generic food but I'm a food snob when it comes to certain things. (Dairy, Meat, etc)
Choice: I would like a place that has healthy, natural and organic options at a reasonable cost.
Location: Located in or at least very close to the heart of Astoria.

I hope such a place exists! Thanks neighbors!

When I lived in that neighborhood, I liked the Trade Fair on 30th and 49th Street. It's a bit of a walk for you, but it's a nice walk and anyway the bus goes down there. While it is lacking in a reasonably priced organic department aside from things like cheap Brad's pasta and occasional sales on organic beans or something, for general groceries it's got a good selection and the best prices in the neighborhood. And as far as Trade Fairs go, that one is relatively clean (you would not enjoy the one on Ditmars). In that neighborhood I never thought of any one grocery store as a one-stop-shop. Trade Fair, Key Food, and the produce places towards the N train were all frequented by me depending on what I needed.

effort for me.

I've shopped at that Associated on a number of occasions.  We do a lot of the food purchases for our church there, and it's across the street.  It's not the prettiest store, but it's definitely cheaper than most of the other supermarkets in the area, it's clearly one of, if not, the closest to the original poster and I've never had a bad experience there.  The clerks are actually friendly, the check-out lines not usually that backed up and they have wide aisles, which I like.

I know exactly what you mean!  I am a Midwest transplant too and really miss my former grocery store.  I miss it more than anything else in my hometown.
I have been to every grocery store in my area.  The only store that really is remotely close to what I miss is the Stop N Shop on Northern.  It is a 30 minute walk for me, but it is soooo worth it.  The prices are so much lower.  I can't even consider buying certain things at Key anymore.  And they have their own private label organics, much like Trader Joe's.  The people that work at Stop N Shop are super friendly.  They really seem to want to help you find what you're looking for.  It's clean with wide aisles and a large selection.  And Stop N Shop doubles coupons under 99 cents.

Having said all that, in my 14 years in Astoria, I have come to the conclusion that when it comes to meat and produce, the best choice is always your local butcher and produce stand.
