Hi folks,

Just adding to the reviews here.  My recent apartment search went a little something like this: was living in the Steinway area and wanted to stay around here but move into a place of my own.

Based on the rave reviews for Kim at Halvatzis I figured I should call her first.  She was organized and professional but it soon became clear she didn't have much inventory around the Steinway area - mostly Ditmars instead.  I called Global City hoping to speak to Dina, who helped get me my first place in Astoria, but she no longer works there.  I then called George at Century 21.  He was nice as others have said but distracted at times and I got the sense that apartment rentals weren't his/their main business.  Someone said he wasn't pushy and I can certainly back that up.  Not pushy at all but kind of on the opposite side of that spectrum. 

The apartments he showed me were in the right neighborhood but it didn't quite work out so I called Aly from Aly's rentals and left a message.  She never returned my call.  Also called Astoria Apartments and got Awilda on the phone.  She and I saw a host of apartments the next day. They did fit the criteria - right price range, right part of Astoria, also mostly prewar with high ceilings and wood floors.  I saw two solid apartments that I liked very much, one that I flat-out adored. I'm hanging out in it now having moved in two weeks ago and happy as can be. 

Overall thoughts/impressions? (A) Be clear on what you want (B) Be willing to call around a lot and see a lot of places - I'm so glad I followed my friends' advice and didn't just take the first place I thought was all right (C) Be organized and keep detailed notes of the financial transactions you engage in so you can make sure everything you're doing is within the budget you set out. 

Good luck to anyone who's apartment hunting now. Glad I don't have to go back out there for a while!

Quote from: jeffc2 on November 06, 2009, 08:32:37 PM
I've seen a total of 5 apartments

With all due respect, that's just the beginning.

The best result by far will be if you actually do the legwork, as Yaxpac says, and go visit not just the apartments but also the brokers themselves.  Most of my apartment hunting was done in pre-web days (or at least, before everyone had a website and email), but even so I'd say that you're better off getting a list of brokers in hand and setting out on foot and going to every last one (except, perhaps, the ones that are not recommended in this thread).

Also, if you really study the Craigslist offerings, you will begin to figure out which are the legitimate ones and which are bait/switch, fake, or otherwise misleading.  It doesn't take too much effort to send out an email to a bunch of possibilities, and you can narrow your search even further once you have their responses.

I'm not trying to slam you or be rude, just realistic.
