The offer is legit, but since I had to call Time Warner for something else, I clarified what "Everyone" is.


Anywhere in New York State, if you have basic cable with Time Warner, have basic with one other service, or do not have Time Warner for any of your services, you are eligible for this price structure. 


If you have DTV, meaning, among other things, you currently have the ability to view on demand channels, you would not be eligible, and if you already have all 3 services you would not be eligible.


I didn't call the number on the flyer, I called the number I usually call when I call Time Warner, and the computer automatically told me the date and time of my appointment, which I had been given during the follow-up call made to me earlier today by the division that makes the appointments.  The computer asked if I were calling about the appointment, and I wasn't, so I went on to tech support.


I was calling tech support because I can't view the onscreen channel listings on either of my cable boxes, even after rebooting a couple of times - they've been getting a lot of calls from people about this problem.  They have different dates on which to upgrade their software in different locals, and the signal goes out to 10 boxes per minute, triggering an automatic reboot, so you have to wait for your area to come into effect.
