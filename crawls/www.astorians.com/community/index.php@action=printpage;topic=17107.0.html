Title: Re: Rats taking over
Post by: ladydi on January 12, 2010, 02:42:55 PM
Where do you live? What an awful situation.

I live next to an abandoned building and we had a rat problem for a brief time. They were nesting in the backyard and
would come out on the sidewalk. But I called 311 and they put those bait 'boxes' - I don't know what else to call them! -
and we didn't see them anymore.

I would take photos as another poster suggested. You can also go on the NYC Dept. of Buildings website and find out who
owns the property. I did that with this building because vagrants were starting to use it and were climbing out of windows
at all hours. I reported it and the owner was forced to board up the windows and put high boards around the property.

I'd also call 311 again. It can't hurt. And if you still don't get anywhere, contact your local representative. I contacted
Vallone's office when this owner refused to shovel their snow and our block was a sheet of ice. His office followed up
with the Dept. of Buildings and got after him.

You don't have to live with this and shouldn't. But the cat idea is also good. If you can have them, it wouldn't hurt!

Good luck.

Title: Re: Rats taking over
Post by: AstorianBoy on January 13, 2010, 12:37:27 AM
To keep them from coming inside, try not to leave any food lying around. Also, try to get some peppermint oil and soak cotton balls with it. Then put the cotton balls in areas where you think rats may come in. Rats hate the smell of peppermint and when they smell it, they will leave because it stings their noses.

Although the peppermint oil idea may be a good temporary solution, you should really try to contact the city and report the landlord.
