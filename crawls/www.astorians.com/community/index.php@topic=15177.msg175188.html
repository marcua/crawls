Kyklades is overrated, IMO.  The food is good but I just don't see how it's so much more appealing than the several other Greek seafood restaurants in Astoria.  It's also limited to just seafood, so if you're interested in trying other aspects of Greek cuisine, you won't find it there.

I would recommend Philoxenia, Zenon, S'Agapo, Stamatis, and for something out of the ordinary and very bare bones but with good food, Corner 26.  I still haven't tried Loukoumi or Ta Vlahika, so I can't make a recommendation for those either way.

Quote from: AlexNYC on June 18, 2009, 09:34:31 PM
I don't think there is any singular standout Greek restaurant in Astoria anymore (Esperides and Karyates were but they're both closed). However, there are a handful of good ones. Stamatis, Zenon, Taverna Kyklades are all good. Tony's Souvlaki Opaa is almost as good and with better prices.

I don't really think Opa! could be compared to any of the above restaurants, in the sense that it's more of a gyro/souvlaki joint with some additional dishes on the menu, whereas Stamatis and Zenon are more "traditional" sorts of restaurants with a much more diverse menu, while Kyklades is a straight-up Greek seafood menu (albeit with an unfortunately limited menu...there are many delicious and Greek seafood meze dishes that for some reason no Greek restaurant in Astoria touches).  It's kind of like comparing a neighborhood pizza shop to a full-fledged Italian restaurant.

I also never really considered Esperides a standout Greek restaurant.  What Karyatis had going for it was more of an upscale Manhattan ambiance, but I don't recall the food being particularly special.
