I just had my second meal at eTaverna tonight. I admit I've been slow to check it out because of my perception of it in association with Foodoni, the previous resident in that location.

That was a mistake. My second time there was even better than my enjoyable first visit a few weeks back. This time I went with my omnivore husband (I'm vegetarian). We took advantage of one of the great unique characteristics of this place, their offering small plate versions of many of their menu items. This made it possible for us to pile up our table with a variety of dishes to sample, and still keep the price reasonable. With a generous glass of retsina (my weakness) and 6 dishes, our bill was only $37 and change:

Greek salad
Avgolemono (Chicken, egg and lemon soup)
Lentil Soup
Greek sausage (loukaniko?)
Okra (Bamies) in tomato sauce
Giant Beans (Gigantes)

After we were seated, they brought us some good, fresh Greek bread with tyrokafteri (spicy feta spread - delicious). Each of the dishes I tried were excellent, including one of the tastiest Greek salads I've had, in a neighborhood with many good renditions. The omnivore, who also went with me with great skepticism, was just short of moaning with pleasure eating the sausage dish AND the avgolemono. It's been hard to get him there after my initial good solo experience. Won't be hard to get him there again.

Both times I was there the hospitable service was notable, maybe even more tonight. It's a gorgeous, warm night, and we'd gone intending to sit outside, but the outside tables were full, except for one in between others - and near a family with two small boys boys. I think the proprietor sensed that we were hesitant when we arrived and hovered by the posted menu in the window, ambivalently. He scooped a free table up and placed it outside the crowd with some space around it. Much more comfortable.

I encourage folks to check out this gem of a small business in our rare neighborhood. I hope it does well!
