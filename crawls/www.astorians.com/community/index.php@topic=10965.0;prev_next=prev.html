Aren't they in the freezer section of most food stores?  If not... here's a solution that will take a few extra minutes, but it's guaranteed to work:

Buy a can of refrigerated Pillsbury crescent rolls and a package of Boar's Head mini hot dogs.  Once home, open and separate the hot dogs, pop the Pillsbury can and unroll the dough.  Cut each triangle into three or four pieces and roll each hot dog in a smaller segment of dough.

Heat oven according to Pillsbury directions, and monitor closely until the dough is puffed and golden.  If you want to make sure the hot dogs are cooked thoroughly, boil them beforehand.

Crescent Rolls. The superior pigs in blankets wrappers! Each "roll" (the dough is perforated) provides 3-4 wrappers worth. They are so easy, and take about 15 minutes to bake. Just wrap them up and throw 'em in the oven at 350 for 10-15 minutes til they're golden brown.

One day, my dad accidentally called them "Franks in Blanks". This is now their official name in Alisonland.

I saw someone somewhere who wrapped up a full size hot dog in a full-size amount of crescent roll dough and then baked them that way. So simple, but so genius. A ginormous Frank in Blank!
