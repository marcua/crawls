Fundamentalist Christians - at least some of them - believe that the U.S. is supposed to be a Christian nation, that we have lost our way, and God is pissed and going to kick our asses (Just like he kicked the asses of the Brits when they began to permit civil ..hmm, well... or the asses of Danes for being so sociali... hmmm). Obama is a terorrist!!!!) and half believing them and experiencing that thrill of fear and alienation. This sense of fear and alienation keeps us from examining our assumptions and the information we are given. Other Americans don't just have different priorities or ideas, they are evil - or stupid.

I'm not saying that fundamentalists are prone to conspiracy theories - there are people of all political stripes with the same predisposition. But I have sat through dozens of church services running exactly as I described above, where the minister says (for example) that America has turned its back on its Christian foundation, and God will not let this stand, and Armageddon is at the door because George Bush* (sr) is collaborating with the Russians to bring about a New World Order which is represented by the Beast in the book of Revelations, etc. Oh, yes .. that sermon was where I first heard about the Illuminati. When I was a kid, I got quite genuinely worried about this stuff. And yet, I kind of enjoyed the rush of hearing and entertaining those ideas........

I do not think that everyone in these churches actively believes all of that. Looking back, I don't think most of the people in the congregation I sat in really believed it. But I never heard anyone say "I don't think that's true." So as a kid, I believed it - for a while at least. Hence, the trickle down effect.

One somewhat troubled person (let's assume a green in a country of greens and oranges), comes up with a new scary conspiracy theory - the oranges are zombies. They send out the mass emails or preach the sermons. If other greens who disagree simply ignore this and don't dispute it, the only people who do dispute it are oranges. And of course we all know they have a vested interest in disputing it and therefore can't be trusted, so that's ignored. And so the more credulous, less educated, more anxious greens hear only one message coming from their group. And even though it's a lie, and most of them deep down know it, the lie wins within the group as a whole. Because no green stood up and said "This is simply not true, and here is why."

*We had a republican president at the time, the evangelist had to work with it
