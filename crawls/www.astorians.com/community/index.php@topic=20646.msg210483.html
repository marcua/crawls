Vesta is most overated place in Astoria right now. If new place is like Vesta, I'm skipping it. Small portions, high prices, and inconsistant cooking.       Now go ahead, shoot me.  :mrgreen:

Overrated is a strong word.  I think a lot of restaurants fall victim to what is probably going on at Vesta.  Namely, they get complacent and stop pushing themselves in the same way when the restaurant first opened.  I'm sure most of us can understand the temptation once a particular place has a steady flow of customers and can "take a night off" here and there.  I'm not trying to make excuses, I'm just saying that Vesta's not alone in this regard.

Another issue may be that as a restaurant gets more successful the original kitchen (or other) staff is afforded more time off or they try to branch out for new opportunities.  This leads to new blood in the restaurant and introduces a whole new learning curve to the mix.  If you're one of the unlucky customers getting a new cook's first attempt at something, I can probably understand your reluctance to go back.  It just doesn't make that particular plate of food indicative of the restaurant.

And with that said, I've never had a bad meal at Vesta.

I also understand that this place may not be everyone's cup of tea, the decor and general ambiance definitely gives off a younger, hipper vibe. Putting that aspect aside, what would you expect to pay for a similar meal, eaten and served in the context of a date? I'm honestly curious, since I don't eat out much, and only splurge on meals out once in a while (this does not include take out) I thought the pricing was par for the course.
