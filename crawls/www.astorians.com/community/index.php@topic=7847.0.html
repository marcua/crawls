I'm also down for the day of service.  I was also here on 9/11 (still a new transplant) and I remember wanting to do something, but not really being pointed towards anything by the powers that be at that time.  In fact I seem to remember the best suggestion anyone in government came up with was to go out and shop.  More Americans are volunteering or spending time in service now than ever before, and I think its time for our government to take that momentum and do something with it.  Time had a neat cover article about setting up National Service that had some really great ideas.

I don't think we can or should continue treating 9/11 as a day of mourning forever, but I will say that some of the comments in that article of people that think we should get over it really pissed me off.  Its great to know that somebody in Raleigh NC or wherever is ready to sweep all the dust under the rug, but I'm not sure that everyone, particularly those of us that were here and the rescue workers who are still dealing with health and mental ramifications from 9/11 feel that way.  I know I don't yet.

I agree that we should always have some sort of commemoration or memorial service on that day.  I like the idea of charity work, and making some good out of the tragedy.  I do wish we could move on in other ways though, and I think we are.  Each year, I couldn't turn on the tv because every program had to do with the mourning, or a replay of the footage showing the planes hitting the towers, the naming of the names, documentaries.  While I agree there should be coverage of a service, I personally can't do 24 hours of it.  It's not good for me, I can't deal with it, and the TV coverage forces you to constantly.  So I turn the TV off.   I was here that day, and it was awful.  I can only lift my head up and move on.  But I think TV coverage is trying to move on as well.  It's been many years and I'm glad the infighting has finally stopped and we are rebuilding.  I hope New Orleans doesn't take this long to recover, there was an interesting news article about both tragedies the other day.  We need to rebuild our cities.
