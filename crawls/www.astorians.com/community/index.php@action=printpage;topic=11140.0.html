Title: Re: Linn Restaurant on Broadway/29th
Post by: SockMonkey on December 03, 2008, 08:09:46 AM
I went there in late Spring a few weeks after it opened & once since.  Service was truly awful the first time - after waiting over an hour we asked if we could cancel orders & leave, but just got general looks of confusion due to language barrier & stayed out of guilt.  Second time around sat at the counter & things were much improved from a service standpoint.

The menu is pretty limited, which for me is a good thing since I get overwhelmed with multi-page tombs of choices & am often reduced to saying 'I don't know' when asked what I want & then have food envy of my dining companions when they make better choices.  But I digress...food is nice - kinda expensive and a bit more traditional Japanese with a focus on sushi/sashimi rather than a lot of other places that cater to the spicy tuna roll penchant of many American sushi-goers. 

At first was put off my the odd decorating, but it's grown on me. 

I thought Shima was awful, so a welcome change, but for me Linn is too pricey & inconsistent to become my staple sushi establishment (that will remain JJs)
Title: Re: Linn Restaurant on Broadway/29th
Post by: doubleL on December 15, 2008, 02:54:26 PM
I checked out Linn last night. The place wasn't packed, but pretty lively considering it was a Sunday night.
When you look into the place, theres a huge white wall which reminded me like I was walking into a gallery. The interior design is nice, and I thought the food was above the average Japanese restaurants in the area. I don't think this place needs to expand on the number of items on the menu, but rather the options within "chef specials." With a chef who has worked in a renowned Japanese restaurant, I would like to see menu items, ingredients or combination  I haven't seen before at Japanese restaurants. The service was on par as well. Food came out quick, and our waitress was attentive.
One more critique would be the lighting. I like warm lighting, the restaurant leans towards "cool" lighting. Though it made everything seem clean, I prefer dim and warm lighting.

Also, the space holds artwork, which probably explains the whole "gallery" look. The artwork was cool. I did some research and found out the artist info here... http://www.thenoneart.com/
Pretty cool that they are embracing street art as I am a fan. Will go back soon and try other menu items.
