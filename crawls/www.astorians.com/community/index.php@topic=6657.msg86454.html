So I'm back from Rhode Island!!  I had such a great time (oh except for my stay at the Seacrest Inn - YIK!! review below).  We saw the Waterfire art installation in Providence, it was awesome!  I took a lot of video and will try posting some excerpts somewhere when I can.  Newport was great too, we did the 5 mansion pass and Cliff Walk in the two days we were there.  It's really beautiful and the mansions are spectacular!!  Pictures soon to come.  Newport's fun with all the great shops and restaurants - I must have gained or lost weight or just maintained because it was hot everywhere, lots of exercise, drinking every night, and lots of eating to be had.  Had so much seafood!!  Did try frozen lemonade - it's kinda like a big icee - in Block Island.  We stayed at the Marriott in Providence - was really nice, although the pool was mobbed by children.  Explored the city all day, and waterfire at night.  We stayed at the nicest Bed and Breakfasts in Newport - the Belle View Inn.  The rooms are great and the staff so friendly and helpful.  A wonderful contrast to our horrible stay in Block Island.  Block Island itself is amazing, so beautiful, the views are amazing, the lighthouses fun to explore, lots of cute shops and restaurants, Island full of natural beauty and beaches.  We rented bikes the first day, a bit of a mistake due to how incredibly hot it was - I swear I almost got heat stroke, with all those hills and the sun beating down on you.  We rented mopeds the next day, but I'm a scaredy cat and rode on the back of my friend.  I don't like motorcycles or mopeds so it was nervewracking, I was anxious the whole time.  But it was the only way to see the entire island.  If I went again, I'd want the car.  It's really worth it to see the whole island, it's just so beautiful!  Pics coming soon!!

My friends and I stayed at the Seacrest Inn in Block Island.   I wish I had read Trip Advisor reviews before booking it, because this place really should be condemned.  It is falling apart, and looks like the place was built out of cardboard.  There is mildew all over the bathroom, on the curtain, and on the walls and ceiling.  There is no tv channel service and no phone.  There was a large stain on the carpet, and the bed quilts hadn't been washed in decades.  The towels are old and ratty.    We didn't bother complaining because the entire inn looked like this, and there would be no better room to be had.  It is run by a little old lady who could care less about her customers, just that she is making a lot of money off them.  The place is not cheap, it's $189 a night, and the ad on the web is deceiving.  They prey on unsuspecting people, who have never been to Block Island before, and don't know what they are getting into.  Upon touring the entire Island, and seeing the many beautiful B&B Victorian houses of comparable value, we realized we really were staying at the worst place on the entire Island.  Even the breakfast was disgusting, it consisted of pre-packaged danishes that you can buy at 7/11, full of fat and preservatives.  They make you secure the room with a deposit on a personal check - which they cashed ahead of time - and then make you pay in full upon arrival - no refunds.  Their workers are exchange students who live there for free  - in exchange for work.  It's like a hostel.  The place is awful  - Be warned!!!  I have posted pictures.

I have posted this on Yahoo Travel, and Trip Advisor but the reviews haven't been approved yet.  Is there a way to report a hotel on violations of having mildew everywhere?
