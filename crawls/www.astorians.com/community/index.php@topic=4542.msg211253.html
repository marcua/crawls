It's still early in the ownership period but something that annoys me already - I had a bunch of scraps of paper left over from when I didn't have a working shredder.  Since they're small scraps they don't all fit across to the middle part where a tiny pin senses the presense of paper.  My old shredder would grind till everything in the teeth was gone, but this shredder stops with paper left or right of the pin still in the teeth so I have to switch from auto feed to foward.  Even when I small scraps seem to have been ground, if I sit back and look at the basket and put the machine in forward I see more paper falling out of the teeth.  Interestingly, though, if I put the machine in reverse I also see shreddings fall. 

2 points about shredders:

1) The left and right of the pin thing is important because if you just place paper in the center, where the sensor pin is, you get a mountain of shreddings in the center of the basket, and have to lift the shredder level out the mountain so you don't have to empty the bin as often
2) I made sure to buy a machine that sits on a metal basket because the metal basket is very solid - it doesn't sag, doesn't dent; and plastic bins attract dust.

Another point - this is a cross cut shredder but inserting paper in the usual manner leaves too much consectuive information (numbers, names) so I always put info in sideways.  This is probably more than I need to do since I'm putting my garbage down a garbage shoot in an apartment building and it's going to mix with a lot of other people's garbage before being compacted, but it's a good practice anyway.  Copnfetti shredders are more expensive.
