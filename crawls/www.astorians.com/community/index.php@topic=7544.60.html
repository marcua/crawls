We ate there tonight. It's called Oceano. It was a mildly negative experience. The menu is a mess. There are too many peculiar dishes that don't belong on the menu (e.g., pasta and cajun dishes), and not enough stand-out choices. Maybe they can sort things out in the coming months.

According to the manager, the "speciality" is seafood. We ordered the seafood ceviche, salmon, and one of the chicken dishes. Each was priced between $15 and $20, and were among the less expensive items on the menu. None of the dishes were memorable, and each included the same side of slightly warmed-over carrots, celery, and zucchini. I couldn't wait to leave.

I'm perplexed. Why would one open an overpriced, mediocre seafood restaurant across the street from Kyclades?
