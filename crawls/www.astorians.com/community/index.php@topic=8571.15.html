Meg, it all depends on how quickly we are able to make decisions, so we should be on time and remain focused on the task at hand, which is to come up with a forum for the meeting.  There are two schools of thought - one is to have questions which are prepared ahead of time, which encompass everyone's concerns - noise, trash, crime, over development, transit etc.  The other is to have the questions, but to allow follow up questions.  My feeling is that we should combine the two but to limit the follow up questions to two, the most.  This is so that we do not get stuck on one issue.  Also, if a person is selected for a follow up question, they cannot have another one during the meeting, so as to allow everyone an opportunity.  We can come up with final decisions at the meeting on Monday.  Also, I will try to post some questions on line to get us going.
