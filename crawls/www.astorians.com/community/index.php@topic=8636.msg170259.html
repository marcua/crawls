Great point about paying...for both things I didn't order and for the food and service in general.

Honestly, I felt bad.  I could tell they were trying but just couldn't seem to get it right.  First off, my wife and I debated quite a bit whether or not we should pay and if so, whether or not we should leave comments for the manager.

The thing was this: it was a Friday night and I was taking my wife out on a date.  I didn't want to make it into a big deal or make a big, nasty scene out of all of it.  I wanted to try and smooth it over as much as possible.

When they walked by and asked if things were ok, I'd always say "Yep, great."  I did that except for when they repeatedly got my drink wrong.  I was on the verge of making it into a big scene (honestly, how can you get DIET COKE wrong not just once but over and over and over again?!) but decided to just play it cool and internalize the frustration.

Also, you could tell they were having a tough time with the trumpet guy in the band.  Everyone including the customers, manager, bartender, waiters/waitresses was going bonkers.  Honestly, that guy would have done humanity justice if he just never picked up another brass instrument (and most likely another instrument period) for the rest of his life.  There was only 1 person who loved the trumpet, an older woman sitting at a table by herself.  She somehow was enjoying the music and kept encouraging the trumpet to "carry on the tune."  That encouragement really boosted his confidence to trick himself into believing he was playing well.

After that, when paying, we talked about whether or not to tip.  I'm not sure who's fault it was, the wait staff, the bar, or the kitchen.  In this economy, it's hard enough for a lot of people to make a living, so I just ponied up the cash and exited.  I didn't want to make a scene and I genuinely wanted to help others.  But you're right, it doesn't convey the point that the service, atmosphere, and food didn't merit any payment.  I might have been better off staying at home with the wife and eating a bowl of cereal instead.
