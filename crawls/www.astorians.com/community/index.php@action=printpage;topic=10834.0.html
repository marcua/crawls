Title: 311 Success!!!!
Post by: Missmarty on June 13, 2008, 08:02:18 AM
After a night of incessant blaring from a car alarm, we called 311 at 6:30 in the morning. They connected us with the 911 operator, who called out the police. They arrived in less than 15 minutes...after some investigating, walking around the car and looking inside, they actually broke into the car and disabled the alarm!!!! It was fantastic. A ticket was issued, though I don't know what for or how much.

I'm always intimidated by the notion of calling 911, but it was very nice to see 311 prioritize it as something to call the cops for. For the record, car alarms that don't shut off in 3 minutes cause "Quality of Life" violations which are under the DEP's jurisdiction. Cars can actually be towed for this.

Thought you all might like to know, as you seem to be "Quality of life" people.

Post by: Spaulding on June 13, 2008, 11:58:56 AM
    I have a great story too.  Earlier this spring, I began to notice a very high pitched beeping coming through my bedroom window.  After some investigation from my roof, I realized that a neighbor behind me had plugged in an electronic bird/critter repellant next to a patch of newly planted grass.  I guess they were trying to protect their seeds from pests.  I didn't mind this running during the day since I am not in my room very much. However, they ran it all night as well and it was starting to drive me nuts.  I called 311 and after some difficult explanations to the operator, she transferred me to the Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) since this was an "environment" issue.  I re-registered my complaint and they said an investigator would reach out to me within a week.  I must admit I wasn't too optimistic. 
     Well shame on me!  I got a call from an investigator the next day.  He was understanding but also realistic.  He said he would visit the neighbor but admitted that he couldn't legally do anything unless the sound was above a certain decibel level.  However, he said he would ask the person to be a good neighbor and shut it off, which I appreciated.  By the next day, I never heard it again!
