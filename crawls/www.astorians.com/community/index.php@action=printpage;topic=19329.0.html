Title: Re: Astoria NW building @ 21st Street
Post by: KateNW on December 04, 2010, 11:51:50 AM
I'd be happy to answer them!

It's a no fee building.  We found the apartment through a broker but the building pays the broker's fee.  I think you can also make an appointment with the building itself but I'm not sure about that, it's kinda an odd arrangement (citihabitants manages the building but Criterion owns it). The building management ran a credit check and wanted a pay stub.  I believe you also have to supply your last landlord's (if you have one) #.  I'm not positive though because my significant other did some of the paperwork. It's a 15 minute subway ride to midtown.  It's under 10 minute walk to the subway. I can make it in 5 minutes but I remember thinking it was "longish" for the  first couple weeks.  It's about 7-8 blocks? Anyway, I'm not complaining.  I wouldn't want to live farther from the subway, though.  You get 2 months free upon signing the lease and don't pay rent until the 3rd month.  You do put down a security deposit. One bedrooms, if you figure in the 2 months free, I think are in the 1900-2500 range and 2 bedrooms at 2500-3,000+. We pay about 2800 for a 2 bedroom. There are tons of layouts (we looked at all of them before deciding) and 1-2 bathroom options, plus price depends on what floor you live on. The roof deck is awesome.

And just because I don't want to seem mean re: providing pictures. I think the management is pretty internet savvy and this is my real first name.  I just didn't want to make any waves for the first months living there.  The fixtures are beautiful and so are the floors.  Yes, the photos online are after an interior decorator worked some magic. You just have to imagine the apartment empty.  I know, hard to do sometimes.
There are plenty of healthy food options but don't expect a Whole Foods, or whole foods-like place.  There's some great farmer's markets in the area.  And 30th avenue (between like 30-35th street), which you'll be close to every day if you take the subway, has these awesome cheap produce stores.  I get the best fresh food for a great price.
