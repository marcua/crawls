Well, I'm not quite sure the name of it, but it is two doors from Tournesol, which is 5012 Vernon Blvd in LIC.  I wrote Jackson Ave above, but that's not correct...it's almost at the intersection of Vernon and Jackson, but definately on Vernon.

They only have wine, and I believe maybe one beer on tap.  Wines are available by the glass ($7 and up), the carafe ($16 and up), and the bottle ($24 and up).  Rather than bar peanuts, you are served some great olives while you sip your wine.  They also have a nice looking cheese menu and raw bar selection.  Strangely, there is a foosball table in the back.

The place isn't huge, but it's very laid back and comfortable.  Dim lighting and warm - both in a good way.

Hope that helps.  Definately worth a visit.
Title: Re: Steakhouse? Desperately seeking advice!
Post by: lanseaux on January 28, 2007, 11:25:09 PM
The wine bar is:

Domaine
50-04 Vernon Blvd
718-784-2350

Their card says "depuis 1938" -- since 1938, but I'm not sure what that is in reference to, since they just opened this fall.

Jim and I ended up there one chilly night in early December because Tournesol was full, and we decided that all we really wanted was wine, cheese and oysters, anyway.  We were pleased to see the wines by the glass/carafe/bottle.  The wine we had (which I enjoyed but unfortunately did not write down, but I think it was a Spanish white) was $26 per carafe, which I think was 500 ml.  The cheese selection I would describe as just this side of daring, but good enough.  We had an excellent Camembert.  They also have oysters, clams and shrimp.  There is no kitchen, but since they are owned by the same guys as Tournesol, there's a certain amount of running back and forth, and it more or less works.

The small cheese plate was $10.50, and a dozen oysters were $24.  They had, if I recall, three kinds of oysters the night we were there, including Kumamoto, which were very creamy and contrasted strongly with the sharper east coast oysters.  We talked to them about local oysters, and gave them the name of Pipes Cove oysters that are now being grown on the North Fork near Greenport as part of a seed program.  I don't know the range of where they are being sold, but they are delicious, and the guys said they would ask their oyster guy about them.  All of the staff were French, and friendly.

Towards the back they have high tables to stand or sit at with bar stools, and in front they have two or three small round tables with chairs.  Subway tiles on the walls.  I'd agree with the afarber's description of the good dim and warm atmosphere.

All in all, I'd say it's worth checking out.  When we went they didn't take credit cards yet, but were expecting to within a few weeks.  I'd like to see a slightly better cheese selection, and more local oysters, but it's definitely promising.
