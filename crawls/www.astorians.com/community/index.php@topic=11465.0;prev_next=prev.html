Am looking for (ideally) August 1st; could possibly do August 15th or September 1st, but no later.

I'm looking for an UNFURNISHED room in Astoria in a cat-friendly, gay-friendly household. I'm a financially-responsible gay woman, single, friendly and a non-smoker.  I've got a great sense of humor and I'm fairly laid back. I know how to be sociable while also being respectful of your privacy in a city where private space is at a premium.

Workwise: I'm currently a literary agent at a small but well-respected agency in Manhattan. I work primarily with science fiction, fantasy, pop culture and gay/lesbian writers. 

When I'm Not Working: I work about 80-100 hours a week so lately I haven't had much of a social life. I'm pretty quiet when I'm at home. (Mostly reading books or editing manuscripts.) But sometimes I'm too busy for my own good. I like to spend time enjoying the city. I confess to being a big nerd; I love board games like Scrabble and Risk. (Although occasionally I get Monopoly and Risk confused, thus resulting in the unfortunate placement of hotels in the Ural Mountains while my army unwittingly invades Park Place. Tragic!) I enjoy movies and cooking for friends and - when I'm not too lazy to drag my ass out of bed in the morning - lifting weights. I play pool (badly), and I'm a big fan of just relaxing on the front stoop with a book in the summer. In fact, books are my livelihood and my life; you'll never lack for something to read if I move in.

Some things for you to know about me:

* I have furniture to contribute to the common space, and kitchen things if you need them. If not, no worries!

* I love Astoria; have lived there for ten years. I was recently priced out of my awesome one-bedroom, thus the move back into a roommate situation. I prefer the area around the Broadway N/W stop but am open to anything really.

* I have two cats. They're affectionate, very friendly, well-behaved and don't scratch furniture (they use a cardboard scratching post) and have lived with other cats in the past.

* I really like to cook for other people so if you're around and up for trying one of my experimental dishes, awesome! I make great oatmeal chocolate chip cookies and the world's best three bean vegetarian chili. (But I'm biased, of course.)

* I'm extremely tidy and respectful of common areas

* This is a big one for me: I have asthma, which is pretty much under control. But I really need to live in an apartment that's totally smoke-free. (That includes no 420 in the house. Although you're always welcome to bake it into your brownies.)

Anyway, let me know what you think.

Thanks very much for your time.

All the best,

Colleen

Hey Colleen,

So, I believe that the apartment you may be looking for is, in fact... ours.

We love a kitty, love a gay, love a non-smoker, love an asthmatic, love a Scrabble addict who tries to place property in Eastern European mountain ranges--the list goes on and on. We love most everyone, except for those who club baby seals. Because that is just not nice.

Our apartment is three short blocks from the Steinway station--STREETS, none of this Avenue noise. Two laundromats are located downstairs--you choose whether you head left or right. We have a large kitchen, perfectly suited to cookie-baking (genuinely large--loads of cabinets, kitchen table for four, more appliances than we could ever possibly use).

It is likely that you will have the option of taking over two rooms--a bedroom that is about 13 x 11, and an attached office that is roughly 7 x 11. What is your price range?

We have high ceilings, your rooms have 3 windows total (with a tree outside of one!). You can paint the walls whatever color you'd like, and leave naughty messages on the fridge--we have a love of creativity and simply CANNOT live with someone who does not boast a sense of play.

The landlords live in the building. They are loud. Seriously. They will likely try to feed you and make you a part of their huge Italian family. They might also drive you a touch crazy, but their hearts are in the right place and their food is brilliant! (Homemade pizza, zeppoles, stuffed peppers... it's pretty incredible. They used to own their own pizzeria). However, they go to bed early, so if you are working such long hours, your paths may not cross quite so much.

We just got a brand new heating system put in, so we control our own heat (which we pay for), and we have cable and wireless internet.

You would be living with: two ladies in the winter of their twenties, one actor/writer/professional temp from rural upstate New York, one hip-hop artist from Toronto (I called her a "rapper" until she set me straight). Mostly she raps about hockey, maple leaves, and LM Montgomery novels.
