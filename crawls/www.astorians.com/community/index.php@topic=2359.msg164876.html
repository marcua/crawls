A few months ago, my wife and I took a trip to Buenos Aires. At the time her pregnancy was just barely starting to show, and - no matter how crowded a subway train was - every man (in a seat) that we stood near would offer her the seat. Little stickers on the train windows revealed that offering seats to pregnant women was mandatory by law, but I believe there is a cultural pride and courtesy too around pregnant women there. Men were offended if my wife didn't take the seat they offered; quite a few times they stood and left the seat empty rather than be seen sitting beside a standing pregnant woman.

Of course now back in NYC, with my wife is entering her 9th month of pregnancy, it is rare when she is offered a seat. I think I may petition the MTA to put up some of those stickers from the B.A. Metro! ;)
