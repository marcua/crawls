I consider myself friendly with both Jeremiah and Aravella.  Jeremiah goes to Trinity with my friend Dirk, and I was part of the advisory committee that ran Western Queens for Marriage Equality. Aravella and I met through some common friends on Community Board 1 and some common friends through the Helenic Society.  She also exchanges recipes with me often.

On personal levels I get along with both and think they are genuinely nice people.  There are many of us who have to maneuver our way through this election because of the insular nature of a small community like Astoria.

I have no desire to speak against either candidate.  I have been helped last year by the Aravella/Gianaris camp on some unemployment insurance troubles--and am extremely grateful for the diligence in helping me deal with a very bad employer separation situation.  I also respect highly Mike Gianaris additionally because of his constant support for LGBT issues, and the change he has brought to the political spectrum here in Astoria.  Further, I respect Daniel Dromm and Jimmy Van Bramer, both personal friends and leaders of the LGBT community that have integrity and do not just go with the political machine--but actually stand for what they believe.  I am surprised at the dismissal of their endorsement by my friends who support Jeremiah, and accusations tantamount to calling the same leaders political hacks.  Jimmy has been integral in molding the Queens library system to the flagship that it is now, and he has been actively seeking office for many years, and has dedicated himself to our LGBT causes.  Daniel Dromm has been an activist well into the 80s and served our Education system and the UFT when our County leaders did not.  He lead a campaign against the County candidate this past year and won. Neither of these two are county "Yes" Men.

Their endorsement speaks words to me about Aravella and the work she has done with Community Board 1 and the UACC.  And if you know anything about Rose Marie and the UACC, you know that they are a civic organization that does not cow-tow to political leaders.

I like Jeremiah, and I have worked with him on marriage equality.  He was an active advocate, like so many of us; and he was a leader in 2009, like so many of us that joined the Coalition that is Western Queens for Marriage Equality.

I am fortunate to know both of the candidates, and grateful that both are on the right page with the issues that mean the most to me.  I know that whoever wins, this district will be a vote for YES on marriage equality.

However, given my experience and friendship with both, the work of Aravella on Community Board 1 and the UACC, and the endorsements of leaders I respect, and the help I've received from the Aravella/Gianaris camp, I am going to endorse and support Aravella Simotas.
