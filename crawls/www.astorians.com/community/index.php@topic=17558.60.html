Sorry so slow to respond - I usually get email notice of new posts in this thread  I didn't about these last few, and hadn't checked the site in a while.

Corner 26 Taverna was a very pleasant discovery.  We split tomato croquettes, he got the meatballs, and I had a stuffed peppers special.  He loved the meatballs, and we both were impressed with the croquettes and the stuffed peppers.

The place is so not Manhattan/Williamsburg/30th Avenue glitzy or polished.  It is seriously committed to being an unpretentious neighborhood restaurant.  Better-traveled folks could tell us if it's reminiscent of authentic tavernas in Greece.

The prices were reasonable too, and if I remember correctly, I enjoyed a glass of wine for something like 4 bucks I think.

Check it out.  Don't let the non-hyped places escape support for lack of fanfare.
