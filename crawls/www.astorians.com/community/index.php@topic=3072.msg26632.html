I don't know why your landlord can't get them evicted.  It sounds like they are living there illegally (unless their apartment is huge).

HOUSING MAINTENANCE CODE

Every person occupying an apartment in a Class A or Class B multiple dwelling or in a tenant-occupied apartment in a one- or two-family dwelling shall have a liveable area of not less than 80 square feet. The maximum number of persons who may occupy any such apartment shall be determined by dividing the total liveable floor area of the apartment by 80 square feet.  For every two persons who may lawfully occupy an apartment, one child under four may also reside therein, except that a child under four is permitted in an apartment lawfully occupied by one person. No residual floor area of less than 80 square feet shall be counted in determining the maximum permitted occupancy for such apartment.

A little lesson for all you newbies who think that 311 or calling the community board/politicians will do something on noise.

Here is an example of a noisy bar in Hunters Point. This problem has been going on for years. But note the resident said in effect, 'we will protest but there is nothing we can do about it.'

It is sad that unless something is within a narrowly defined agenda set by our politicians (that benefits them, and not us necessarily,) grassroots concerns are simply ignored. Years of false promises and lip service by the politicians (noise/motorcycles at Astoria Park, anyone?) have bread hopelessness and apathy in the local community. May explain all those empty rooms at the power outage meetings.

In Queens you will never see 'unseemly' events like the recent protest in Brooklyn against Ratnerville. If you don't like things shut up and sit down ... or move.

Apathy is the politicians' best friend. 'Public' initiatives come in one direction: from the top down.
