The way I explain "South Midtown Astoria" to other Astorians is that there's less "stuff", but the stuff we do have is all pretty consistently good (as opposed to the commercial strips on Broadway or 30th Ave that are certainly busier, but I think they tend to vary in quality... YMMV.) 

Beyond Cup, Sunswick, Cafe Bar, Pizzeria Uno's, S'Agapo, and Locale (and I haven't tried the latter two), we don't really have too many other restaurants.  But I'm a big fan of the menus and environment at Cup and Cafe Bar, and the beer selection at Sunswick.  We also have the Starbucks, the Museum of the Moving Image, and I hear Little Bistro is good, though I haven't been there myself.

Back to books... I read just about any genre, I'm kind of predisposed to strong character writing and strong storytelling.  I do have a love of fantasy/sci fi too, but it has to be done well.  There's plenty of sci fi that I would chuck out the window.  I think it comes from taking writing classes in a southern university fiction department!  Although my teachers there hated sci fi/fantasy...

I devoured all of Diana Gabaldon a few years ago, I need to sit down and reread all of her stuff so I can check her newest out of the library, but it's so daunting.  Those novels are huge!  Also Orson Scott Card (going many many years back), J. K. Rowling (a given?), E. L. Doctorow (love some of his stuff, can leave some of his stuff), and lately I've been indulging and reliving some older childhood favorites like Susan Cooper, S. E. Hinton and Zilpha Keatley Snyder. 

Phew!

I'm really glad that so many people are interested in a bookswap. Although I don't know a lot of the places that were suggested, let me try to sum up the requirements.

We need to meet someplace that can handle:

-a largish group of people (Starbuxom are too crowded)
-a block of 1-2 hours (most cafés/restaurants don't like a group to monopolize a table over such a long period)
-moving around to exchange books (tables in cafés/restaurants are usually too tightly packed for free movement)
-talking (most bars I know are too loud)
-reading or at least skimming (all bars I know are too dark)
-near to public transportation preferred

...Which is why I suggested Freeze Peach... if they're busy this Thursday evening (has this been confirmed?) maybe we could do a weekend afternoon/evening instead. Let me know if the other locations are vastly different from the bars/cafés/restaurants of my experience, and we'll go from there.
