duuh...how could I forget caviar. I don't know how plentiful it is now, but back during the Soviet days that was one of the foods Russians would eat like we eat peanut butter: slathered by spoonfuls on black bread, or of course rolled up in a blini- atleast according to my old Soviet era ex-pat friends. Mushrooms sauteed with sour cream and chopped onions are good too. Kvass is a fermented black bread drink (low in alcohol-served even to children) real popular in the old days.
