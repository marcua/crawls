First off, the Freeze Peach thread wasn't exactly just a "promotional" piece, by the time I actually read and responded to the thread (11 hours after it was started) it had already become a "conversation" about how this person's tone was negatively affecting the cafe. I wasn't praising or defending the promoter, I was just saying that it wasn't the end of the world. From what I was able to glean on my own this person isn't an owner, founder, or attached to the cafe in any way other than volunteering for it on one occasion. Besides, even according to my most vitriolic critics the thread wasn't very promotional for the cafe anyway. If I'd have moved the thread I'm sure I'd be criticized by the same for "silently" moving it to hide any negative comments about the cafe. I moved the Astorialogues thread because first off it was posted on the politics board as promotion of another website. Second, I perceived the OP of that thread as one of the founders of that site (a fact that she denies). When I saw the thread (a few hours after the post) it hadn't generated any non-promotional "conversation" and made more sense to me in the business listings section than in "politics". This was the difference I perceived at the time.

Frequently we will 1) remove completely or 2) re categorize threads that are solely promotion of another website (promote their own blogs, link promotionally to their website etc). Links to, and discussions about local businesses are more difficult to categorize imho because these are often not just an advertisement, but invoke lots of discussion from the patrons of that place both positive and negative.

It was my act of re-categorizing this one misplaced astorialogues thread that prompted the start of this vitriolic and abusive thread on astorialogues 10 days ago. Ironically, this comes after two other posts that were appropriately categorized on Astorians remained in place pointing to Astorialogues. Yet it's angrily claimed that I'm coming out from the shadows to "censor" knowledge of the site. If indeed that was my interest don't you think I'd just permanently ban my critics, and remove all references to the site?

Of course for a hand full of people the answer is "no" because it's too simple and not sinister enough. It's a lot more fun (and I think that's evidenced in the thread on Astorialogues) to create a huge controversy at my (and other member's expense) to try and drive people away from Astorians. There is no reasoning with this kind of argumentation, it's not a discussion, the "facts" don't matter because with every post from me describing my actions and mindset at the time of those actions comes only more name calling and distortions. What matters is keeping this issue alive an at the top of the thread list on Astorians for as long as possible.

Quote
The intense negative feedback you are receiving has everything to do with your moderating approach and nothing to do with the people who make up Astorians or Astorialogues.

This intensive negative feedback is coming from a hand full of people who are upset about two things. 1) mis-cateorization of a thread (even though I left two other astorialogues posts that were properly categorized in their respective spots on more heavily trafficed areas of the site). 2) over my removing of the "u" for some users over a period of less than 24 hours. "Other" negative feedback is primarily due to my enforcement of the rule of no name calling on the site - a rule that a few people can't seem to manage when it comes to the discussion of politics.

Quote
You're focusing too much on the names the site owner is being called and too little on the underlying causes of people's dissatisfaction.

It's individuals that use the politics section that are the most dissatisfied. This was the reason that Astorialogues was started, because its now founding members had all been temporarily suspended in the past as a result of violating the rules on the politics board.

For the record. According to Google Analytics, in terms of Unique Page Views the politics section of this board makes up 0.64% of this site's total traffic (by comparison the business listings section makes up for 0.37% of the site's total UPV.) Yet, as I've said before, the politics section makes up for approximately 90% of all user suspensions. The remaining areas of the site (especially Local Chat, Restaurants etc) comprise a huge bulk of site traffic, drive the most conversation, require little if any input from moderators, and result in the fewest problems between members. You've heard of the 80/20 rule, in this case it's more like 90/1. Something like 90% of the moderator issues, come from about 1% of the monthly site traffic (not exact numbers, but hopefully a fair representation of my perspective).

Please understand that from my perspective the problems that exist in the area of politics are not representative of the site as a whole, and that while I'm committed to finding a way to promote a consistent user experience for all board members, I'm not willing to allow a hand full of people who insist on being able to call each other "racists" and "bigots" "assholes" and "nazis" to set the tone for the entire site unless the tone of the entire site indeed organically shifts. Over 4 years of running this site the bulk of users of Astorians consistently reaffirm that they find the rules and moderation activities in general to be acceptable (although I'll be the first to admit there are problem areas, and I'm committed to improving these.)

It's difficult to please everyone, and this is why I can genuienly say that I'm glad there's another place for anyone to go who is less than satisfied with the rules or application of the rules here. I believe that the addition of Astorialogues to the community will make the discussion in Astoria more diverse, and can make Astorians better for it. For anyone who is attracted to what Astorialogues has to offer, please know that there is no hostility towards members of that (or both) sites. All are welcome as long as they abide by the rules they agreed to upon signup.

In conclusion I do hope that Astorialogues finds a way to build community without tearing others down.

I've been reading this thread with great interest. Though it's obvious that many people who've posted feel passionately I'm still trying to work out what exactly are the motivations driving people. For what it's worth here's my perspective.


Astorians.com is essentially an online publication covering Astoria , a highly-interactive one yes, but in essence it’s a magazine where contributing writers (i.e., the users) provide the content (i.e., the threads) within a set of editorial guidelines (i.e., the rules) set by the owner and editor-in-chief of the publication (i.e., merm) and the editors (i.e., moderators).

Some feel the editorial guidelines or ‘The Rules’ are too loose, some think they are too strict, others feel they too arbitrarily enforced. You can’t please everyone.

As previous comments have noted, if the editorial guidelines are not to a contributor’s liking, he or she is under no obligation to contribute. It’s not mandatory.

If the editor-in-chief or subeditors object to something that a contributor has written – it their prerogative to either edit it or delete it from the publication or in the case of astorians.com, since it is published in real-time, ban the contributor from further contributions for a period of time.

This is a community-minded publication but it is not governed by the community. The rules are not “law” and no one he has “rights.” I don’t see the point in debating rules and their enforcement (or non-enforcement) from the point of view of 'that's not fair' or cries of censorship because when it comes down to it, what merm says goes as far as I’m concerned, whether it thought to be fair or not.

Astorians.com does not exist to promote the cause of freedom of speech. It is a privately-owned electronic publication that not only serves those who contribute but many, many others who read, but choose not to post comments. Merm has to consider these readers as well. I think that's what many of you forget. As editor-in-chief he has to think about the bigger picture -- it's not all about the needs of the contributors.

As a writer and contributor, you may feel you’re being censored when your posts are edited or your user priviledges curtailed, which is not surprising because you are being censored. But it’s not your publication and you’re more than free to contribute to another one of your choosing or better yet start your own and write anything you wish to.

Let me pose this question: Have any of you ever written for a newspaper, journal or magazine? have you ever had a letter to the editor published? Do you think you have the right to have your words published without any oversight from those in charge of the publication? It's both naive and laughable if you think you do.



Merm – I think you and the moderators have done a fantastic job with this website, outstanding actually. You’ve been more than fair in the handling of the contributors in my opinion. Because it's in the interest of astorians.com for you to do. Contrary to what some people think, you wield a very, very light editorial hand in trying to keep this site fair and enjoyable to EVERYONE (sorry for the caps but it's an important point).
