Just for the record, its not only Ikea's kit furniture that has defects.  We just bought one of those butcher's block kitchen carts from Home Depot over the weekend (last one - negotiated and got it 20% off!) .  It was crazy hard to put together, i.e. you had to screw stuff in by hand in places where it would be impossible to use your right hand etc... Say what you will about Ikea but at least they make an attempt to design their stuff easier to assemble yourself. Anyways, we get to the final piece (a drawer)...  and guess what? The drawer won't close!! I'm guessing whoever was cutting the wood that day missed the mark and made the drawer a centimeter too wide! Well, after putting this whole thing together and getting excited I wasn't about to take it apart to bring back to Home Depot. I run out to the 99 store buy a pack of sandpaper and start sanding away for like 2 hours!!! At last, the drawer fits, and the spouse is happy. So yeah, enough said...kit furniture has its pros and cons.

I agree wholeheartedly with the you-get-what-you-pay-for theory.  I've have a mix of kit furniture over the years.  I had several items made of  particleboard, including a wardrobe from Ikea, that fell apart within a couple of years.  I've vowed off particleboard for life.  However, the solid wood wardrobe (also Ikea) I got to replace it is really nice and sturdy.  We also have several of their solid wood bookshelves.  We also have a few items from Target as well that are really solid after 8+ years of use.  I think you just need to be choosy about the quality and pay a bit extra for solid wood.
