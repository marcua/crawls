Statistically, this is not correct. Across the country, the number of privately owned coffee shops has declined. Starbucks has an aggressive policy of expansion, with direct intentions to drive out local shops. This is why you can see multiple stores so close together. There are specific instances of this in Boston and Pittsburgh that I can think of off the top of my head.


There is (or used to be) an intersection in Seattle where there was a Starbucks on each of the 4 corners (Queen Anne) - and I may be making this part up - but I sort of think there was a Starbucks cart at the intersection too. 

I don't have any stats or articles - but I had a friend who was in management with SB and she definitely confirmed that at least in the 90s SB would wait until an independent was doing well in a neighborhood before they would come in and open a shop.  There was lots of evidence of this in the Pacific Northwest and Boston (the only places I lived) in the 90s. 

SB original location has no seats.  When they started to grow they put in intentionally uncomfortable seats with really low backs to dissuade people from hanging around.  They wanted folks to "eat it and beat it."  High turnover would = more profits.  But apparently this wasn't true.  DOn't know when they switched to comfortable seating. 

I don't think their coffee sucks.  What I DO hate is that they only carry vanilla flavored soy milk.  What's a girl to do if she doesn't want her soy latte flavored and uber sweet??  Answer: go elsewhere

One more thing.  Possibly more important than the coffee itself is - the freshness, the grind, and the brewing technique.  I find the best way to make coffee is the simplest - I grind it as needed, put it in a melita filte, pour over the hot water - slowly.... and drink.  (with plain soy if you please)
