New Monthly Open Mic for Poets and Writers at Waltz
« on: August 05, 2005, 06:23:42 PM »
Words at Waltz is a new monthly reading series at Waltz on Ditmars Boulevard (corner of 24th Street).

This free series, which will be held on the last Monday of every month at 8pm,  will include one featured reader and an open mic for poets and writers.

The first reading will happen on Monday, August 29th.  While I hope to feature some of this city's best writers, I'd also like to showcase Astoria talent, so come on out and read a poem or two or an excerpt from a short story.

Hope to see you there!
Michele Battiste

September 26th reading
« Reply #6 on: September 20, 2005, 05:28:20 PM »
Hi all.

Hope you can make the second "Spoken Words at Waltz" on September 26th. Come out and bring some poems or a story excerpt to read at the open mic!  There will be a door prize, too.

Here's some info about Mary Golias, the featured reader:

Mary Golias is from Massachusetts where she did her BFA at Emerson College, an MA/MAT at Rivier College, and her MFA at University of Memphis where she was awarded a scholarship and teaching assistantship. During this time she taught composition, poetry workshop, and literature while serving as poetry editor of River City and president of the creative writing club where she coordinated a literary event to benefit the Memphis Literacy Council. During the summers she taught ESL to students from Mexico. Last July, she was awarded a partial scholarship to attend the Prague Summer Program through Western Michigan University. Work has appeared in Red Brick Review, Peralta Press and Oyez Review as well as book reviews in Paterson Literary Review. Specific poems have been finalists for magazines such as Phoebe and River Styx. Currently she lives in Astoria, NY and teaches language arts and ESL in NYC public schools.

MONDAY, OCTOBER 24th Open Mic and Feature at Waltz
« Reply #8 on: October 03, 2005, 05:51:58 PM »
Hi all.

While the "Words at Waltz" reading series normally occurs the last Monday of every month, the next three readings will be on the penultimate Monday in October, November and December.  This is due to the readings coinciding with or brushing perilously close to some major holidays (and yes, i do consider Halloween a holiday).

So the next reading will be on Monday, October 24th at 8pm.  Andrey Gritsman will feature.  In addition to being a highly respected and published poet both in his native homeland of Russia and here in the US, Andrey edits the online bilingual poetry magazine "Interpoezia."

http://www.interpoezia.net/

He also runs the monthly "Intercultural Poetry Series" at the Cornelia Street Cafe.  Come on out and bring some poems or a story excerpt to read at the open mic.  Hope to see you there!
