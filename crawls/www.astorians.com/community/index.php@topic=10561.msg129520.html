i don't know.  no offense to anyone in the business, but i just don't trust employment agencies.

in my experience, it seems they just want to get as many people into their ledgers that they can so that they can tell their clients that they have all of these people to choose from.  a long time ago, i went to a ton of agencies and filled out a bunch of applications.  they would give me a typing test, and say, "thanks, we'll call you when we have something."  then i never heard back from them.  i guess it was this experience that left such a bad taste in my mouth for these places.

granted, this was a long time ago...before i had any real work experience...but i have a feeling that it also had something to do with the fact that i've never fit into this conventional fortune 500 look.  now years later, i'm in-between jobs, and i refuse to even consider the agencies as a viable job-hunting option.

today, i'm pretty much mid-career and my experience is basically in trafficking for ad/direct mail agencies.  i don't want to do secretarial work and ideally, i'm looking to go from the print medium to video/tv/film/radio/internet/etc.

is there anyone that can make a suggestion of some sort...especially someone who may work at an employment agency?
