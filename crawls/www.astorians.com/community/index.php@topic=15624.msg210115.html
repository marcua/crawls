I had to revive this after giving this place another shot over the weekend.  I noticed it was still hanging on and that people were inside.  We went in to place a take out order.  It seems to me that the people have changed and the menu has gone much more Indian than before.  The interior is exactly the same.  So we ordered several items, mostly traditional Indian.  We came back and the order was neatly wrapped and ready to go.  The woman who took our order was very pleasant and professional.  I ordered the Fried Chili Lamb.  It is super hot and very tasty.  Really enjoyed it.  The Cauliflower Manchurian was simple but delicious.  Shrimp in coconut milk was very good.  Shrimps were large, fresh, and cooked perfectly.  The Palak Panir was also very good.  I'm glad we tried again.  It still seems a little on the fringe but something has changed for the better.  I know everyone loves Seva.  But if you live in the Ditmars area and you like Indian, give it another shot.  They still have Thai and Chinese Items as well.  But it seems that Indian is where their strength is.

I gave Spiced a try for lunch today.  I ordered through Seamless Web, and after some deliberation, chose the "Indian Prefix [sic] Lunch Special" with mixed vegetable pakora, chicken saag, pilaf rice, and Coke [which I forgot was part of the order and didn't come anyway, which is good since I don't drink soda]; the "chicken 911" appetizer (which I think is a smaller version of their chili chicken entree (dry)); and a butter naan.

Short verdict: there was nothing wrong with the food itself - I enjoyed it generally, but it was definitely lacking the complexity of a good Indian restaurant.  The lunch special, I am well aware, may not be the best test of a restaurant, but I've ordered lunch specials from many Indian restaurants and this didn't come close.  The vegetable pakora was tasty (but had no dipping sauce, which I wanted - a coriander or tamarind sauce would have been nice, or even some raita), but had a certain blandness, and the chicken saag was the same.  The chicken itself was dark meat (good in my book) and very tender, but it was just lacking in some of the spices (not heat spices but general) I appreciate in good Indian food.  There was nothing pilaf at all about the rice - it was plain white rice, somewhere between Chinese takeout rice and plain Indian rice.  The chicken 911 was good - the dry chili chicken was one of my favourite dishes at Spiced when they first opened, and it seemed about the same.  The naan was good.

I am by no means writing them off, and I have definite plans to order the fried chili lamb (I came thisclose to ordering it today) as well as a few other items on the menu.  At the very least, it may be a good option to have nearby for those times when I am just craving Indian food and can't wait and don't have time to cook it myself.
