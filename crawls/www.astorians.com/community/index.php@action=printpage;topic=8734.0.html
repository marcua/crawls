Title: Re: Power Point Class?
Post by: essen on November 28, 2007, 02:11:24 AM
I haven't used any of the newer versions of it, but if you just want to learn the basics, it's very simple. Just set an hour or so aside for yourself and start messing around in it. I taught myself how to use it a few years ago while waiting in the airport for a delayed flight, and by the time I had to pack up and board I felt I knew the program well enough, and I'd made a presentation for personal amusement. I would recommend coming up with something dumb to make a presentation about and then figuring out how to make it. Aside from it being a sort of rewarding educational experience, you might start laughing hysterically. You will want to involve clip-art and text effects in some way.

Robert Grauer's Exploring Microsoft Office 2003 series is a really good classroom-type series to help learn any of the office products.  It comes with a CD that has files you can practice with step-by-step and plenty of on-your-own exercises, too.  I use it with my seventh graders.  It helps people develop basic skills and proficiency.  If you are okay with used books, the cost is only $8.00 and the files are also downloadable from the internet.
