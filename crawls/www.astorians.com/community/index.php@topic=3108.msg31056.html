OK, here's the recipe as it was printed in 'The Armenian Kitchen'.  It was a done by the Ladies Guild of an Armenian church in Boston, MA.  This recipe is a 3-day process, which would be way too much effort for a dip, in my opinion, but hope you enjoy. 

Syrian Mouhammara

1/4 c. ground red pepper
1/2 c. lemon juice
1/2 c. water

1/2 c. olive oil
3 tablespoon ground cumin
1 1/2 tablespoon sugar
1 1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 c. water
2 c. coarsely ground walnuts
2 c. plain bread crumbs

seeds from 1 pomegranate (divided in 1/2)
1 tablespoon pine nuts

Soak ground red pepper overnight in lemon juice and water.  Store in refrigerator.  Next day, mix well with next 7 ingredients.  For more vibrant color, add a teaspoon of red food coloring paste or a few drops of liquid red food coloring.  Taste for flavor - for more spicy, add more ground red pepper or a few dashes of Tabasco sauce.  Refrigerate overnight to blend spices and flavors.  Next day, taste and adjust seasonings (lemon juice, salt, etc.).  Mix in 1/2 of the pomegranate seeds.  Spread mixture in a shallow serving platter and garnish with remaining pomegranate seeds and toasted pine nuts.  Yield: 4 cups.  May be frozen for short term future use. 

I often use this site for recipe searches and they have a few other recipes that seem to take less time.  They don't use fresh pomegranate, but instead use pomegranate molasses or syrup.
