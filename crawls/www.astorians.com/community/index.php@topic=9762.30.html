At L'Incontro, I usually just listen for the main ingredient of the specials, and then I ask them to repeat the details of the ones that catch my interest.

But my favourite dish there is the ostrich - struzzo.  It's very simple, but ostrich is a meat that's very easy to cook poorly because it's so lean.  It's always tender and flavourful - just sliced "tenderloin" around lightly dressed field greens, topped with generous shavings of Parmigiano.  It started out as an occasional special, then went to the menu, but went back to being a special because Rocco wasn't always happy with the quality of the ostrich he could get.  I think it's still a special, but he does almost always have it.

I've never been to Piccola Venezia so I can't comment on the restaurant comparison, but I love L'Incontro.
