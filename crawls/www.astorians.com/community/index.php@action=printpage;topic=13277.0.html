Title: iTunes Dilemma
Post by: TJT on December 02, 2008, 05:32:18 PM
Maybe someone out there can help a guy from the 80s who is more at home with records, tapes and CDs!

We made the leap to an iPod about 2 years ago.  I successfully downloaded all the music we wanted to keep forever from our CDs.  Then gave away the CDs.  (I know - but I do things like this all the way, or not at all).  So my iTunes library contained all the music as does the iPod.  Since then we purchased new music via iTunes as well.  In addition, a friend of mine downloaded the music files to a DVD that I keep in the apartment.

Since then, the hard drive on my laptop burned up.  The laptop is nonfunctional.  The same friend thinks he might be able to salvage data from it.  But no guarantees.  And maybe he can move files we downloaded to the disc into a new iTunes library.  But again, they may or may not be accepted.

I bought a new laptop and downloaded iTunes.  I plug in my iPod which contains all my music.  I carefully select NOT to sync the iPod.  So it shows my iPod's music library when it's plugged in.  But I cannot migrate the music from the iPod into the new, empty iTunes library.

What does a person do when everything is gone except the iPod containing all your music?  I've done some research and see that some people say you're doomed.  Others say there is a 50 step method in iTunes.  But if you press one wrong key, you wipe the iPod clean.

I still have the original Apple CD that came with the iPod.  Does it help any if I install that CD on the new laptop, rather than downloading iTunes from the Apple site, as I did?  I doubt it, since again, the music resides on the iPod and not in my iTunes.

If anyone has any advice, let me know.  I haven't gone to the Apple store to inquire.  Honestly, it's not a huge issue but I think I should resolve it before giving up on all that music.  That iPod won't last forever.  There must be other people who have a laptop get damaged or stolen and they have nothing left but the iPod.  These things never happened when you had hundreds of 45s.  Only if maybe your house burned down.

But we're just not that much in to collecting and owning music, per se.  Our iPod only holds songs from CDs we bought since we were in High School.  I think we have about 710 songs on the iPod.  Which must sound nuts to most of you.  And honestly, if we never hear those songs again, it might not be a bad thing!  It's just that this circumstance arose and it felt uncomfortable to be forced to part with the music when it seemed so close to being able to be loaded back into iTunes.  It was more about breaking the Apple code than saving the music.  So I will take those steps and try and migrate the music files.

Also, the whole iTunes thing was new and mysterious a few years ago.  Now there is a much different comfort level as far as finding any song in the world online.  I remember at the beginning everyone was convinced that they had numerous CDs that would never be available on iTunes.  That may be true for some people, but not us.  And I think less so for most people - and even more so as time goes on.

By the way, we purchased a Sirius satellite radio and boombox for the apartment since this whole mishap.  For us it is a perfect solution.  It sounds fantastic.  We can choose any music you can imagine - depending on your mood.  And they pick the songs.  Not just picks from my dated collection.  For us, it's a perfect solution.  I prefer hearing some new music rather than my own.  And honestly, I don't care anymore about owning it to listen to later.  And no more running over to the iPod to move to the next song.  And the playlist organizations.  The iTunes.  The charging.  And all the rest of it. 

Life was so simple when it was just a couple hundred albums and 45s!  But I can remember there being problems when you mixed parties, drinks, turntables, needles, and records!
