she can't touch the dog because it is a working dog who was trained east coast style, meaning that the reward for doing things correctly is things like "good boy" and petting.  This makes it difficult for the owner if they let people talk and/or pet the animal. 
That is only only when the dog is working. When the dog is not in harness it is to be treated like any other pet dog. If they aren't, they become depressed because they lose their canine identity and then have the potential to start resenting working. Please discuss this with the owner. I have fostered working dogs and was trained to teach the dog how to be a dog. After the first year the dog is returned to the fostering organization where they specialize in training the dog to become a companion working dog. Once the animal is certified, they are retrained with their new owner who is instructed to NEVER forget that the dog must be treated like any other dog when not working. Some of my fosters have gone on to become guide dogs, drug sniffing dogs and police dogs. This little guy is going to be missing it's owner very badly, and MUST be petted, played with and given treats to make it as comfortable as possible, under the circumstances.
