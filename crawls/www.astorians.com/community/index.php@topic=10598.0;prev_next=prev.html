We had a nice group of 6 and did have a good time.  But the service definitely needs some work.

We've been twice, once the week it opened, and once this Tuesday to celebrate my new job (we were on our way to get wine and eat at home, but I wanted a good draft beer, and at this point, I feel like that's all they have going for them. To make it easy to reference this post when considering your next game watch, supper or corporate event, my pros and cons will be bullet pointed.

Pros:
+ I'm not going to lie, I'm a straight guy and the waitresses are easy on the eyes.
+ The beer list.  There are 40 good drafts and like 200 options in cans.  Some prices are good ($6 for Ommegang Hennepin) and some are bad ($4 for a can of Gennesee Cream Ale).
+ The nachos. A plate of nachos is easily enough for two people and they tasted pretty good.
+ TV's. There are enough TVs to watch your game from any seat in the place. HOWEVER, it pissed me right off when I was watching both the mets and yankee games and they switched ALL the TVs to the NBA finals.  They changed it back when I asked, but seriously, leave the local games on a few TV's.
+ Location, which happens to be like 2 blocks from my house.  Because I'm lazy.

Cons:
- The first night we went it was like an Ed Hardy convention, which was irritating, because two douches kept making comments to me and my better half.  I'm a lover, not a fighter, and even if I wasn't I'm not about to start a fight with a table full of roid ragers.  The second night we went it was much quieter, but I can see this joint drawing that kind of annoying douchey crowd.
- The food. I won't say that it's all bad, but both times we went we had issues with our meal.  The first night, we had ordered add ons or subs (they offer sweet potato fries, which is what I will order any time they're available) and that screwed things up royally. The second night, the order was right, but when I bit into my turkey burger, the bun was so hard I thought I was going to break my goddamned teeth - and I was kind of irked that initially another server was trying to sell me on the idea that it was toasted ( I know the obvious difference between toasted and rock hard stale). Both times the waitress / management did everything they could to make up for it (comped drinks, free sweet potato fries, etc) so at least there was that.
- The wait.  The first night we waited 3 innings (my standard measurement of long amounts of time) for our food.  My girlfriend tells me that three innings is about 45 minutes, but I'm not sure i believe her. This stems from all the food being "prepared from fresh ingredients / cooked to order."  The second, less crowded night, the food came quicker.  So if you're in a hurry, this place is probably not for you, even on a slow night.

So to wrap things up, I don't see Canz being a place I'm going to spend a whole lot of time at, because, like Studio Square, it's not my type of crowd.  I'd also recommend giving them some time so that they work out some of their initial kinks.
