I've had Verizon since, well, before it was Verizon (Bell Atlantic), and the main reason I have stayed with them is because of their call quality/reception.  Their phones aren't great (although perfectly adequate, they're not usually cutting edge) and their plans are average, but what's a great phone if you can't actually talk to anyone on it?

The only place I have ever had consistent reception issues was when I worked on the 46th floor of a building in Midtown.  But for several years, I worked on the 35th, and then 26th, floor of another building in Midtown, and my reception was fine there.  I've never had trouble getting a signal in Canada - as far north as Labrador - and I can also get a signal in the more touristy areas in Mexico.  My only real complaint with reception is that most of their phones are not GSM, so if you go to Europe or other places that CDMA coverage is not standard, you have to rent a different phone from them, or buy a pay-as-you go phone for your trip.  That said, I have heard that they do offer a few dual-band phones, so if you often travel to countries where GSM is prevalent you should get one of them.

I've had AT&T/Cingular since always, and I've had reception problems at times. Sometimes it was the phone (I think the devices are programmed to start failing about two months before your contract is up, so you're so fed up you'll renew just to get a free/discounted phone!) and sometimes it was the service. If you call and complain to them every time it happens, it seems to get better (assuming, of course, that you can keep a call long enough to complain!). They'll sometimes credit your account as well if there's a known problem, the way cable companies do.

It's been a few years now since I've had those problems, and I haven't had any consistent problems with connectivity or dropped calls in Astoria using three different phones. I don't think lack of reception in an elevator in a high-rise is a cause for concern, though--I don't know anyone who gets reception in my building's elevator.

I have been with AT&T for my personal cell service for as long as I can remember. All the way back to when they were Cingular. And back when Cingular and AT&T were merging their networks I had my fair share of dropped calls, but in the past two years I can't say that I have any real complaints.

For a little over a year I had a work cell phone with service provided by Verizon and I had way more dropped and missed (direct to voice mail) calls with that phone than I have ever had with my AT&T service. Sometimes it would even take hours for a txt msg just to be delivered. I can however say that the coverage appeared slightly better and more often than not with Verizon I would have 5 "full bars" of service. Plus the audio quality was slightly better, and their data service was noticeably faster. While on the other hand, with AT&T I often notice that the majority of the time I am usually riding somewhere near 3 to 4 bars, the audio quality isn't as perfect (but isn't ever unacceptable), and the data service varies greatly. But more importantly I can also say that with AT&T I haven't had a dropped/missed call or delayed/undelivered txt msg while using my phone in NYC in about 2 years -- and in the end that's what matters to me most.
