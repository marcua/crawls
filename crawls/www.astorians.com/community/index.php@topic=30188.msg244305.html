Went with a friend tonight. I have high hopes for this night market but the first night was a little tough. We got there at 6:30 and the lines were so long (and barely moving) that we had to leave. It was very hard to tell what food was at each stall, particularly from a distance with the long lines, and my vegetarian friend couldn't find anything he could eat. The food looked good, though--we just couldn't get to it. There was Burmese, Japanese, Chinese, Taiwanese, arepas, some kind of mystery crepe situation, grilled corn & lemonade, tasty looking sandwiches stuffed with sliced meat and onions, and some interesting tea as well. They had a couple of dessert options too. As we left there were still people streaming in, so it looked like the situation was only going to get worse, not better.


This market has a lot of potential. Clearly the interest is there, but they need more vendors who have enough food that they can quickly turn around. I hope to go back another time, but I may go with a plan B ready just in case.
