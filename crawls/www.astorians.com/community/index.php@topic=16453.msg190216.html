I appreciate reading everyone's sharing of their believe and religion on here...believers and non-believers alike, rather you are Christian or not, an atheist or believer, you are holding by FAITH a set of believes and principles.
Since everyone is so open and generous to share, I am privileged with the opportunity to share my faith as well, I'm a Christian what does that mean?  It means first and foremost, to quote John Newton, "I am a great sinner and Christ is a great Savior". It means that I am saved by grace and no amount of good works will entitle me a ticket to Heaven. It means my best human efforts to be a moral and just person falls short of the glory of God, I am justified and cleansed by the blood of Christ on the cross. It means I am to follow Christ's commandments to love God with all my heart and love others as myself (Mark 12:28-30), regardless if others return that love or not. It means to forgive others as God has forgiven me. It means that I am to show the same mercy to others as God has shown to me. It means that I don't take God's forgiveness for granted ( John 8:11). It means that God loved me before I loved Him and He pursued me before I opened my life to Him. It means I have been shown the magnitude of God's love (John 3:16). It means that I strive to see others through the eyes of God. It means I may be judge and hated for who I am, but I am to respond with love nevertheless (John 15: 18).

Being a Christian doesn't mean that I was born into a household with Christian parents nor entitled in anyway. Likewise being a Christian doesn't mean that I will have children who will automatically be Christians, I will do my best with my duty to share God's love and grace with them, not to convert, only God can do that.
