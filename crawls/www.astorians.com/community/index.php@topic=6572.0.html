Indoor Feral Cat
« on: June 06, 2007, 08:34:49 AM »
A couple of years ago, one of my two cats (who were sisters) died. We went to a shelter and picked out a little kitten to save the surviving sister from what appeared to be never-ending depression (I should have prefaced this by saying it was a "crazy cat lady" topic).

The kitty proved to be successful to save the one cat from depression, however I think we ended up with an indoor feral. He has never allowed us to pet him, is incredibly skittish, and only after two years of intensive 1)pairing the environment with food, 2) providing treats any times he comes closer with more treats for closer approximations, etc. etc. he will he finally sit in the same room with us, but if you move your foot, he takes off.

He gets along with the cat though. Sometimes they roughhouse, but will often cuddle together and sleep. They will also groom each other.

Now circumstances are that we need to get rid of the cats, and I don't know what to do with this little guy. He isn't a friendly cat, and I'm not comfortable giving him to someone knowing that he wouldn't be good with children, or families, or people for that matter. Any suggestions?

If you decide you need to give him up, try Animal Haven or Bide-A-Wee.  They are  no-kill animal shelters so hopefully they'll find someone who will be patient with him and he won't be euthanized.

If you got him when he was a kitten, I don't think he was actually feral, but if kittens have limited human contact during their first 8 weeks they tend to be less interactive with people.
