Quote from: juniperbean on May 19, 2009, 11:29:59 AM
a friend and i have been seriously throwing around the idea of opening a southern inspired spot in the astoria area. our concern continues to be whether there would be enough interest/business in this area to sustain us. we're thinking pulled pork, ribs, some fried chicken.... great sides. in a cute spot, ideally with some outdoor space. sweet tea. etc etc.

not ditmars area.... either around 30th ave, broadway.... seeing this board definitely makes me excited.

what are your thoughts as residents of astoria?

Juniperbean, I do think your idea could definitely work with some smart planning, regarding the food, atmosphere and service.  In my own mixed marriage (he's an omnivore, I'm vegetarian) of former southerners, we'd love a place like you describe and bring a lot of people there if:  the menu worked really well both for the meat-eaters and the meat-abstainers (include good sides w/out meat); you had reliably good, fresh biscuits and cornbread (with good, homemade spreads: preserves, maple butter, the like); reasonable prices (like Vesta); consistently welcoming, friendly, efficient and competent service (like Vesta); comfortable seating; enjoyable music (but not too loud, discouraging conversation).

We went to an extremely popular new restaurant in Cambridge, MA recently based around a chef's interpretations based on his Virginia childhood, called the Hungry Mother.  Great place, maybe more formal and higher priced than I'd recommend, but WOW, the food.  They had a memorable-for-life cocktail made with Makers Mark and Sorghum.  That kind of unique item can alone bring people coming back again and again.

Do all the traditional meat/fish things really well, but show genuine care, attention and interest in the vegetarian options, and you'll get loyal customers and great word of mouth.  No group looking for a place to go out will think the place won't work for them.

I'd love to be in your group of guinea pigs!

Any restraunteurs on this forum?   Please answer my plea for good smoked BBQ in the 'hood.  I would love an easy place to pick up some ribs/pulled pork/brisket whenever the craving arises.  With the right set up you could make a killing. 

Agreed, Parkie!
  What Astoria (and NYC) needs isn't necessarily a restaurant, though.   I'd be happy with a guy with an oil can grill and a good BBQ recipe camping out in a local parking lot or street corner--real down and dirty BBQ ribs and chicken.
  But that's just the way I am... :-o  I like my "street" food.
  I don't know about you, but how much souvlaki or chicken and rice can anyone in Astoria eat?  Bring on the BBQ!
  I remember my misspent "salad days" in--of all unlikely places for great BBQ, Minneapolis, MN--and the best BBQ to be found was in a little shell of a building on Lake Street (Think the old Carvel Building on Steinway Street).  Nothing fancy: just a counter and a few tables AND several oil can grills in the back.  But, dang, if they couldn't pull off the best BBQ chicken and ribs, baked beans, and slaw in the Upper Midwest!
  Unfortunately, I would imagine that the NIMBY crowd, wouldn't let such an establishment exist in Astoria. Too much smoke, too much fragrance-such an imposition on the sensibilities of others.  Someone would have to raise a "quality of life" issue and call one of the Vallones for legal counsel.
   We have to keep in the mind the mindset of those in Manhattan that tried to shut down an upscale eatery like Blue Smoke BBQ due to "pollution" issues.  I ask you: How is Astoria different?  Are you different?  Please make a difference.
Bring on the BBQ!
