« on: November 07, 2008, 02:24:37 PM »
The thread on Arcos got me to thinking and I'm starting to get alarmed by the number of vacancies on Broadway.  I think some of the landlords have miscalculated and pushed out tenants seeking higher rent only to be hit by this economic downturn and now we have a large number of vacant stores on Broadway, four on Broadway between 33rd and 34th Street alone.  The Cafe at Broadway and 38th has been empty for some time, the 99 cent store across from Brooklyn Bagels now sits empty (though it looks like something may be going in there) and the Raleigh store and two stores (as well as a restaurant) on the North side of Broadway between 32nd and 33rd are empty).  It's getting depressing.

Those are valid points, but I think it's pretty silly to have the same expectations for Broadway as you would for 30th Avenue in terms of cafes.  What I mean is....there's so many cafes on 30th Avenue now that the entire strip has become a huge draw, and in essence, they feed on each others' success.  On Broadway there's really just Omonia, which seems to attract a different crowd and doesn't have the same "vibe" as the places on 30th, and until recently, there was Kolonaki, which seemed to be lagging far behind Omonia in terms of business and which also lacked the vibe of the 30th Avenue cafes. 

I remember when Grand and Flo opened, that actually gave a boost to Galaxy Cafe, which had been there longer than anyone but which wasn't really very busy.  Once those two places opened up and drew crowds, Galaxy began to draw more customers as well, until it was sold...now Balu is in its place and it's as busy as all of the others.  It's the power of the collective draw these places have.  The former Kolonaki location doesn't have the same "draw" at the moment, though obviously that could someday change.
