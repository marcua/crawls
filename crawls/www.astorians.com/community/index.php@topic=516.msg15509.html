Aww .... <sniff> You guys are gonna break this cynical girl's heart ... (so be it, means there's still a little left ...). Okay, so here is my official "hello":

Gave away my real name somewhere on the board early on, since then, prefer to be called by my TRX-christened name, "dc" though the alternate "Kerouac Kitty" still makes me laugh ... (google duluoz).

I am a NYC provincial: born and raised in Queens; grew up in Corona, moved to Rockaway, then Forest Hills. Educated fully in the NYC Public schools and attended a CUNY college. Moved to Astoria in February, and it is a rare understatement from me to say that it was one of the best decisions of my life.

As I have said to some of you, I don't know what I would have done had I not found this board. I arrived here a few months after ending a 12-year marriage and after lurking for just a brief while (the posts I place here are my first on any board, too), I was completely taken with the community here; how everyone wrote and interacted; the variety of interests, backgrounds, and personalities; and the topics serious and silly.

Those of you who have met me know I'm pretty much a stubborn, opinionated, melodramatic, wise-a*s, chain-smoking, atheist, only-child, with a fondness for black t-shirts and jeans, mopey song lyrics, books, Smithwick's beer, my zen cat (& original avatar ...) Siddha, her snaggle-toothed brother, Taylor, and hot chocolate with caramel (in lieu of a pot of black coffee).

My personal line of "misanthrope" is (for the most part) said with a wink, but it took every ounce of strength to go out and meet the board members that first night (braving Uncle T's warnings, and all ... heh). And that was perhaps my second best decision. In just four short (long) months, this board has introduced me to some of the most generous, intelligent, fun (and funny), talented, and just plain nice people I've ever come across. It has also brought me to a few people who I hold very, very close to my heart and who I now can't remember having ever lived without (and oh, yeah, I'm a sentimental fool).

And finally, to answer a question I get asked a lot: yes, the avatar drawing really is me, as interpreted by Kyle Baker in his mid-90s New York Magazine comic strip "Late Hit."

So, hello, and a much-belated thank you for my wonderful welcome, and a continuing welcome to those of you who have joined us since.
