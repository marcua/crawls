One thing to note here that I think makes the debate really difficult is the fact that some of us think that life (sentient life) starts out of the womb, others think that its starts at the moment of conception, others think it starts weeks after the child is born.

I think the key problem is that while we may say that we "know" that life doesn't start until birth, or that we "know" that it starts at conception the truth is that there isn't any evidence to support either view - this is entirely a philosophical debate and not necessarily a scientific one.

For all those liberals that claim that it's the unfounded beliefs of the right wingers that are forcing their religious views on them, the same can be said for the reverse - there's no evidence of the moment of self-awareness when a baby becomes a human.

I happen to imagine (unfounded "belief") that it's okay to use embryos, and I start to get more and more uncomfortable with the harvesting of said embryos the older they are. However, some people have a different imaginary idea about when it's "okay" and not "okay" based on their feelings which some attribute to their religion (which BTW says nothing on the subject, or when it does seems to eludes to the fact that a fetus is less than a human), and some attribute to the god of "common sense". I don't really buy either.
