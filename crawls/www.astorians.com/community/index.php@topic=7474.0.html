First of all, congratulations.

I can't actually recommend one to you, but I do have a suggestion for you. Thirty-seven years ago I gave birth to my first—twins! Everyone told me about this really great local Pediatrician. So, of course, I made our first appointment to see him. We had great differences of opinions about raising children and my children's first 3 months were quite a hellish experience.

My suggestion: make a list of your priorities. Do some reading on child raising; speak to your friends and families. Even simple or dumb things count. Do you believe that a baby should cry for X minutes before you pick them up? After you have a list, call all the local Pediatricians and ask them for a no-fee consultation interview.

I do this on a regular basis each time a new specialist is required to manage my physical disability. You'll be very surprised how many doctors think they are some sort of deity and refuse you the appointment. But, you will also be very gratified to find out those who allow this interview and enjoy your questions and your logical way of making such an important decision.

I wish you all the luck in the world. Have a safe and comfortable delivery and a healthy baby.
