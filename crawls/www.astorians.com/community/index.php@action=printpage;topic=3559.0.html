It is the planes. When the wind blows strongly from the airport into LIC they can be heard clearly. Also, when there is little traffic, as an evening when they play the Superbowl and many people are in front of the tube, the city gets quiet and the airport is heard clearly. Its eerie. Check it out.

Groups like UCCA routinely have meetings -- with seemingly little effect except to plant in people's minds that something of substance is being accomplished by talking talking talking about this. Newspapers going back to the 1940s had complaints about airport noise with a big spike on the issue when they converted to jet traffic about 1960. If you think things are bad, go out the Flushing or East Elmhurst. I recall the Concorde thundering overhead once while near Kennedy.

Again, Queens benefits from really great planning. Get used to it. The bottom line is indifference by our leadership on both noise and pollution from the airports (except getting headlines as they talk about it.) They lack the ability, or perhaps the desire, to do anything of substance about this problem.

And yes, the airports rank right up there with automobiles and power plants adding to our already dangerous levels of pollution (off thread, did anyone see all those dead birds at the power plant up in Steinway?)
