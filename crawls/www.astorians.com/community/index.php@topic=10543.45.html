Re: Waterfront parking to be eliminated

As it stands now, this is one of the unfriendliest cities for bicyclists there is.  Lack of bike lanes, lack of bike parking to lock up, lack of motorist awareness of bicyclists, etc...

As it stands now, there is no sharing of the roads for cars and bikes.  Creating bike lanes on 35th and 36th streets is nice, but is really not much more than a token effort, and falls significantly short of the real need to make biking easier, and more importantly safer.  Almost every restaurant that delivers uses bicycles.  That alone should create a demand for safer bike routs.

As a non-driver, who doesn't actually bike that often, I would really appreciate seeing better enforcement of basic traffic laws, in particular, use of turn signals.  I got in a shouting match with a driver last weekend when he nearly clipped me because he didn't use a turn signal, and I was on his right at 21st Street and 23rd Ave.  If there was a bike lane on either of those streets, maybe the driver would have taken time to see if there was a rider by him, and perhaps he would have used a turn signal.

The more bike riders, the safer it is to ride.  But for there to be more bicyclists, more bike lanes need to be created.

And I also like the idea of fewer cars on the road.  Less pollution, easier for the vast majority of pedestrians and bike riders, easier for buses, etc...
