Handyman, did you get your information from the Department of Building website, or the city map, which links to the building site.

Anyway, I don't know if anyone else had posted this before, but I attached a link to the interactive New York City map because it's pretty darn helpful to get information about the area and sometimes, even the building before you make any decision.

http://gis.nyc.gov/doitt/cm/CityMap.htm

If you go to the top right of the page, you will find a place where you can enter an address or intersection.  Once you enter the necessary info (make sure you include the borough), the map will zoom to the area, if the address is valid.  From there you can check out the aerial view, find out if there is a train near you or a library, or connect to a link that will take you to a statistical profile of the area. 

When I was searching for an apartment recently, I used the Lot Info link, which sends you to the DoB website, and discovered that a place I was about to put a deposit on, was an illegal occupancy.  In fact, the landlord had a bunch of violations for the same place.  I was wondering why I wasn't able to sign a lease. 

Good luck on your search and I hope this information is helpful!
