So anybody loving lower Astoria?

I do.  For one thing, I think it's more convenient for transportation - we usually take the R/V, but if there are weekend service interruptions, we can also take the N, although it's a farther walk.  We love the proximity to other parts of Queens, like Jackson Heights and Flushing.  I might be overgeneralizing, but I also think that lower Astoria is more culturally diverse than the Ditmars area - we have the old-time Greeks and Italians, but also a vibrant Latino and Asian community.  We're within walking distance of the Astoria Kaufman 14 and the AMMI, and all the restaurants on 31 Ave.

So yeah, we love it here.   :-)

I lived around 48th and Broadway for 4 years, and now live in the Ditmars area for the last 6.  I have no beef with either area.  Both have their pluses and minuses.  I would actually say both areas are diverse, with more Latinos and Asians on one end, and more Greeks and Italians on another, and more Brazilians in another, and so on and so forth, and sprinklings of other ethnicities mixed in all over.  Some pluses with the 48th Broadway area, that is quite suburbia like: the mega stores on Northern Blvd featuring Stop N Shop, Best Buy, Old Navy, Sports Authority, Home Depot, and Pathmark.  All featuring parking lots!  Woohoo!  Looks just like Route 9 upstate near Wappingers.  And it's 10 minutes train to Target and the QueensCenter Mall!!  To get your urban dose, stay on Broadway.  I liked the area and found it very convenient for everything.  Cavo was within walking distance, yay!  The movie theatre's pretty awesome.  The MOMI.  There's even a 24hour laundromat.  The R does suck on the weekends in that it doesn't run locally in one direction often, but the NW is no picnic either.  Basically, I think either side is pretty good, just depends on if you want more residential - Ditmars, or more businesses - Broadway.

does anyone know anything about the 48th st/28th ave area? I'm looking at moving from 21st street and 26th ave. any suggestions? parking? BARS? train? Thanks!

Hello,

I live on 48th Street and Broadway, right next to the 46th Street stop on the R/V/G line.  I've been going to a bar on 30th Avenue and 47th Street called Dillinger's.  IMO, it's a pretty cool neighborhood bar, although you may want to check it out yourself. 

The Irish Rover, which is another great neighborhood bar and repeatedly gets good reviews, is located on 28th Avenue on the other side of Steinway.  Check this out:  http://newyork.citysearch.com/review/7326558  It's worth walking to, even on a rainy night, as I found out recently.

I don't know about the parking situation, but the train stop is kind of far.  You could walk to 30th Avenue and catch the Q18 to the N/W 30th Avenue stop, or you could continue walking down the hill and go the same stop that I use, which is the 46th Street stop.  There's an entrance/exit on 48th Street.  Other members who actually take that walk on a normal basis can probably give you a better feel for the time it takes, but I would guess it's about 8 to 10 minutes.  Personally, I'm not a big fan of bus to train or long walks, but that's probably why I am so unhealthy and obscenely big.

The key though is to walk around yourself.  Explore the area and see if you like it.  Good luck!

Hi- 46th and B'way is certainly part of Astoria, and near Cavo and some pizzerias and some other places. It is, by the stats and from personal experience, very safe (but I grew up here, dunno what you consider safe), and seems to be benefiting from more little bars and restaurants. 
Regarding the Woodside Houses ("The Projects"): they were cited about ten years ago as being a model project- in the sense that the residents were generally employed and you can walk around there without having an issue.
If you are not used to projects in general, wait till you get a feel, but the Woodside Houses are no movie stereotype of people assaulting each other and passers by. At all.
You can find the crime stats online.
There's a one family house half a block down from "the projects" on the market for 950k.
