Title: Re: Truva Mediterranean Cuisine
Post by: round2 on March 30, 2015, 12:14:14 PM
Went there on Friday night, and left impressed. Will become one of my regular destinations if it maintains the current quality of their food.

The atmosphere is kind of comfortable-diner, which suits its moderate prices. So VERY happy to discover they weren't playing generic American pop music. I believe it was a mix of Turkish and Greek music, if I understood correctly. The staff seemed attuned to being welcoming and contributing to an enjoyable meal.

My omnivore husband and my vegetarian self both had plentiful options to choose from. We both fully enjoyed what we ate. He had a chicken dish (chicken adana kabab, I believe), and was very happy. I had the excellent Turkish lentil soup and we split a combination plate of mezze, including a truly special ezme, a tomato and mixed vegetables chopped dip (http://www.giverecipe.com/turkish-spicy-ezme-salad.html) and very good smoky babaganoush, hummus, super-fresh tabbouleh, and cacik, a Turkish yogurt, cucumber and (I think) spinach dip.

Got to take a second to talk about the AMAZING hot, fresh Turkish bread that they put out on your table. No doubt that alone will be responsible for many return visits of folks checking Truva out. Reminds me of what I wish Balkh Shish Kabab would do (their bread never seems fresh, always reheated).

Also ordered a dessert to go, Kazandibi, a caramelized milk pudding sort of rolled up into layers. Very nice!

Kudos to this new restaurant, exactly the kind I always hope is opening in the neighborhood. Cuisine-specific, affordable, welcoming, comfortable, unpretentious with a major focus on freshness and quality of ingredients and dishes.
