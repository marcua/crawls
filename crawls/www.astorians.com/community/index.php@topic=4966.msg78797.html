We LOVE our apartment.

we're on 41st/Newtown

Our building is kept in immaculate condition by our landlord.  In fact our front stoop is SO appealing that it is frequently used by....the neighborhood teens as a hangout, random passersby, the democratic club going to AC once a month....

It's a three story - three family Brick corner rowhouse.  We have windows facing east, west and south.  The front of what should be an alley is blocked by our neighbors garage - so there is no access to our yard except through our apartment or our basement (or over the neighboring fences) - excellent - privacy and some security.

Interior...
It's basically a railroad +.  All very long and narrow - but oh so comfy...
front room is our living room - our furniture is way to big for the size of the room - but there's room to seat 5 or 6 plus a few folk on the floor if we wanted to.  Jonathan's closet is also in this room.

Middle room is the office/dressing room - two desks, a couple of dressers and my closet. 

Dining/Living space opens up in the middle of the apartment - This is the only wide room  (the two front rooms share the width of the building with the hallway).   We have our dining room table seats 6 (8 if we crowd) - we can open it up and fit 10 (more if we had too).  A china cabinet, two upholstered arm chairs with ottoman and small table, bookcase/cabinet.

The kitchen - is long narrow - we have a small table in there that we never use - a couple of butcher blocks lots of cabinets - but never enough space - counter, cabinet, refridgerator or anything....

The bedroom is very small.  We have a queen size bed with two night tables - a blanket chest and tall dresser (sounds like it's big) - there's about two and a half feet on each side of the bed I suppose.

The bathroom is miniscule - the toilet and the sink don't actually both fit - so as you sit on  the toilet you get the sink jabbing into your arm if you're not careful. 

The best part of the apartment at this time of the year is definitely the backyard.  Jonathan has created a beautiful warm welcoming garden/living space.  The flowers are great - the grapes are intimidating us with their volume this year - we have a nesting mourning dove in our grape arbor - a grill - room to seat 12+ for a sitdown meal or room -

and in general the whole place is QUIET.  Beautifully - peacefully quiet. 
My neighbors with the perpetual yard sale SUCK (see other thread for that info).  My neighbors with the perpetually yapping little rat dog SUCK.... but other than that.... our place ROCKS!!!  We love our home.


location - I thought the walk to the subway was going to be bad - and in the winter - it is a miserable 7 minute walk - but that's not so bad.  We're too conveniently located to NYSC - I hate the place - but I know that I won't travel the 10 minutes to another less conveniently located gym. 

I do wish there were food purveyors between the Steinway St stop and our house. 

All that for $1850/month.... I used to think it was high... I'm beginning to think that we've got a bargain.  Shhhh - don't tell our landlord.

I rent a 2-bedroom on the first floor apartment of a house...row house, I think it's called?  I have a roommate.  There is a neighbor in the basement apartment and two more neighbors above us in the top floor apartment.

It's my first apartment.  I like it.  Our landlord kind of pulled a "flip this house" deal.  He re-tiled it and re-painted the whole place before we moved in, so it looks like it's worth more than we pay.  Our rooms are a decent size and so far everything is running well.  Killed about...five bugs in my count, but after 2 1/2 months, that's pretty good.  Plus the commute to my job in Manhattan (if everything runs on time) is only 20 minutes!

Downside: I live off of a major street (33rd, right before you hit the GCP) and the cars tend to be horn horny, litterbugs, or have incredibly loud engines.  One time I was sitting on my stoop and this lady lit up a cig and just tossed the empty pack onto the street as she sped by.  Not even a second thought.  To me, 33rd has a feeling of being dirtier and noisier than the other parts of Astoria.  Most other streets have more trees and only one lane, but my street is a three lane drag filled with cars racing to get to the green light (which is nearly impossible by how the lights are timed).

I live in a one-bedroom in a prewar co-op near the Broadway stop.  The apartment is designed very rationally: you walk into a large foyer, with the living room straight ahead, a small hallway (containing a broom closet and pantry) and the kitchen off to the left, and the bathroom and bedroom slightly to the left and ahead.  Archways separate the hallways and living room from the foyer.  I just had my kitchen redone, so it's completely brand new, but the bathroom will need work eventually.  It still has much of the original details--black and white tile floor, clothes hamper built into the wall--as well as the original faucets in the shower.  The bedroom can easily hold a king-size bed and dresser with room to spare; I have 2 closets that I would like to combine into one big one someday.  The living room is a good size too.

The acoustics are such that I can hear everything in the hallways, but generally it's quiet.  Most of my windows face the parking lot of the apartment building two doors down, so occasionally I hear someone's car stereo at odd hours.  (Too bad the lights of the parking lot are on until just before daybreak.)  The building is generally well run, and I think they're planning to redo the lobby this year.  I have a storage unit and there's laundry in the building.  I hope to be there for the long haul.

Me and my boyfriend live in the apartment complexes on Crescent Street between Ditmars Blvd. and 21st Ave.

It's a 1 bedroom and we pay $1300 (it's being raised to 1325 this year, bleh!). The buildings are older, but really well maintained with a live-in super. We live on the 2nd flr of a 4 flr walk up, there are 8 apartments to every floor.

The inside of our apartment is completely renovated as the landlord company renovates all vacated apartments. The hardwood is new, the kitchen has new ceramic tiles, nice dark cherry wood cabinets, and brand new appliances. Bathroom has new features as well, except for the old white & black floors (which I actually love!). It's sort of set up RR style. You enter a long narrow hallway and the living room/kitchen open up to each other, only separated by a "bar" which is the perfect place to eat. Next is the bathroom and bedroom.

Pros: All new! Quiet at night, close to the park and the train, no mice, no bed bugs. I've only seen 6 bugs in 7 months! The building has an exterminator walk around the building on the 2nd Saturday of Every month to see if anyone wants their apartment sprayed. We've only had him come in once just for the heck of it. He just squirts this stuff in the kitchen and bathroom (take about 30 seconds).

Negatives: Directly across from St. John's Prep HS and the kids can be LOUD. Actually it tends to be the annoying parents who blast really bad music from their cars as they wait to pick up their kids. It's really hard to get packages delivered because the front door is locked and Fed Ex/UPS never leaves the packages outside. The buildings have a No Pet policy (we really want a dog!). The guy who lives below us is this older gentlemen and the two times we've had a small party (everyone was out by midnight) he banged on the pipes to get our attention. I thought it was pretty rude considering we're quiet 98% of the time we've been here.

One bedroom Broadway/31st Ave area, top floor of 4 story building with 24 apts in it.  I really like living here in my first apt in Astoria/Queens - I grew up in Manhattan and then moved around a lot in Brooklyn.  I like the laid back feel of the place and yet it's so close to the action!

Pros:
-  Exposed brick walls in the LR and kitchen and the rest of walls painted a sunny yellow - recently renovated when I moved in
-  Close to everything on Broadway but not on Broadway
-  Apt faces the back, away from street noise
-  Heat and plumbing is great
-  Rent just went up to $1231, which no longer seems outrageous after reading what you all are paying

Cons:
-  No sound insulation whatsoever.  I'm on the top floor bec I thought I would be protected - no such luck.  If my downstairs neighbors really want to get into it, they will keep the volume up way past midnight!  So far, there have been 3 different neighbors and these latest ones are the most quiet.  I envy the person who posted that tenants could have parties in the building and not bother each other!
-  For some odd reason, if I want to turn on the oven, the (smoke?) alarm goes off.  Also, there is no exhaust over the stove.  I don't think this kitchen was made for cooking.
-  Tiny closets (2). I had to get rid of quite a lot to move in here
-  Tiny overall space, I think my place is less than 450 sq
-  The rent just went up to $1231!

Rentometer is a really neat website for comparing rent to other people in the area. Upper Astoria has a lot of entries on here.

Our apartment is in a 3 family home on (in the 40's). We're on street level, so we get a lot of noise, but the backyard and back porch make it all worth it. It's almost a studio apartment - the only thing seperating the bedroom from the living room is a set of those folding doors (large cracks around the edges). The house was built in the 1920's so there are a lot of cool little woodwork details around the windows and stuff. At the same time there are a lot of unrepaired holes in the walls and other similar problems you find in old homes.

Our rent is almost $1400 a month, which is a little high for a 1 bedroom, but, again, I was willing to pay for it due to the use of the backyard. We have a nice little tomato garden out back.

Our neighbors are excellent. The guys upstairs must work all of the time because I barely hear them (they get in late, but it doesn't bother me). The guy downstairs threw one party at the end of the semester earlier this year, but he invited us and was highly apologetic about the noise (who cares if somebody throws a party once in a blue moon? If it were every night I'd be pissed, but this guy is pretty friendly so I can't complain).

Over all I like our apartment. I wish it were cheaper, but I'm glad to have a little greenery out back.

I also don't love my apartment.  I dont' hate it, but I'm always looking for something affordable that I might like more, so I cruise CL all the time.

As for the apartment itself:
1 Bedroom on the second floor of a 4 story building, 16 units in total.  It's your standard prewar railroad, kitchen to your right as you walk in the front door, then the living room and as you pass the water closet to your left you'll spot one of our two very, very small closets and enter the bedroom.  The apartment has lots of charm in the way of cool detailing on the walls, and the landlord keeps the building up very well.  It's in the back of the building so it's nice and quiet, which I appreciate. 
I've been in there for just over 4 years at this point, and while it was great when it was just me, it can get cramped with two people living there, mostly due to lack of storage space.  We only have two small closets (28"x28") for the whole apartment, and next to no storage / counter space in the kitchen (we could use new appliances and flooring in there, but I have no clue when a landlord is supposed to replace those sorts of things, so I'm hesitant to ask).  Our sunlight situation kind of stinks too, since the only window that gets direct sunlight is the one in the rear of the bedroom, which tends to wake me up around 7AM on the weekends.  All of our other windows face the building next door, but I have never seen anything interesting going on in there.
Every time my lease comes up, I inevitably look for a new place, but my landlord keeps hooking me up, so I still pay under $1200 a month, and I haven't seen anywhere else I have really wanted to live for under $1500, which is steep for us.  Plus I would only move for somewhere with considerably more storeage and / or a backyard.
