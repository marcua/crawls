I ate at Iji last night and really enjoyed my experience there.  I was very happy to see that the restaurant was filled to capacity -- apparently the good word is spreading.  The menu is reasonably priced, and the food is fresh and delicious.  My 2 dinner companions and I were sitting at the sushi bar so we could see all of the rolls being made -- the two sushi chefs took such care and precision in making every unique roll, it all looked delicious to me even though I don't eat raw fish!  I had the beef teryaki, which comes with a choice of white rice, salad, or miso soup.  I had the salad, which was not too heavily covered in dressing like most Japanese restaurants do.  The beef teryaki was cooked to order, and it was more than an ample-sized portion.  It comes on top of some type of tasty mashed potatoes with sliced vegetables.  One of my friends also got the beef teryaki while my other friend got a California roll and an avocado roll, and she appeared to enjoy both.  Desserts are homemade and very flavorful.  I tried the banana tempura, which is an entire fried banana with a small amount of berries.  My friend had the chocolate creme brulee (I think), which he could not stop raving about.  Overall, I would highly recommend Iji.  It's a welcome addition to the neighborhood.

Ate at IJI for lunch today. Had the Unagi don bento box $12,

The miso soup was delicious. Clean, actually tastes non packaged and it was not overly salty. Did a chef make it???

The bento box arrangement was on a quartered modern plate.  3 lightly fried shumai, a California roll that was mostly wonderfully fresh and bright avocado, a decent sized portion of eel broiled with sauce and sauteed veggies underneath and finally a round of steamed rice that was unfortunately under done.

That said, it was excellent quality, service and dining area. Would gladly go back. Everything was just so fresh, clean tasting AND NEVER TOO MUCH SALT which is a big thing for me, I have to watch my salt intake.

Waitress says they will deliver to 41st street and Steinway. Hard to believe but might try it for dinner one night and see how it rates next to JJ's which is the best around here but not cheap.

I have now been to Linn, Osaka, and Iji, and can say without hesitation that Osaka is my favorite of the three.  The food is fresh, reasonably priced, and they offer just enough special rolls without going crazy.  The staff is also extremely friendly and the service is great.  The food at Linn was just so-so.  The menu is very small and not very imaginative.  My boyfriend and I disliked Iji.  We found the service to be slow and the food to be terrible -- not very fresh and filled with mayo.  Maybe we were there on a bad night, but with Osaka so close, I doubt we'll be going back.  Samurai Sushi on 34th Avenue at Crescent is also decent, but has strange hours.
