Welcome.
My first advice (and I mean this in the most helpful way so please don't be offended) would be to help your son with speaking and understanding English. My son has a friend that started in a 3 year old program not speaking or understanding English and the poor little guy was throwing up every school day morning (3 days a week) from the stress. It was VERY difficult for the teacher as well because he wasn't understanding what she was saying. He was much better by the end of the year but it was a very rough year. Last year in my son's preK class the students that didn't understand and speak English also had a difficult time. It is very stressful for a child to not understand and be able to communicate with others in the classroom.


As far as pre k I can only tell you about those around me (30th Ave area) PS 234- I have a friend that is there and is very happy. I toured the school for K (it's our zoned school) and found the staff there very nice and pleasant the school is new and very clean. I would have happily sent my son there if he hadn't tested into the g&t program.


I sent my son to PS 70 for preK because I heard wonderful things about that program. I was very pleased with the program but I didn't like the principal and the overall feeling of the school. I still recommend the pre k program though, I think it's one of the strongest. As far as I know it's the only one that gives homework, not much just practice writing a letter of the alphabet over the weekend and bring in objects that start with the letter and talk about them on Monday. I think this really helped the children speak in front of the class. The teachers were warm, caring and very good with the children.


I have heard good things about Queensview Nursery School too.


The children that I know that went to preK at daycare centers didn't learn much if anything academically it was more of a playtime.
I wanted my son to have the 2.5 hours to be more academic to help prepare him for Kindergarten.


You can check out public school at schools.nyc.gov all schools have grades and you can also see how parents grade the school and principal.
also insideschools.org
Good luck feel free to PM if you have any other questions.

Title: Re: A good pre-K in Astoria
Post by: gargo on January 24, 2012, 09:48:57 AM

As far as I know there is no early registration for public kindergarten and there is no waiting list for general ed. kindergarten.

If you decide to go with public pre K I would check: schools.nyc.gov to check out the registration deadline and get more information.
When I registered my son I had to do it at the school and bring him with me, I also had to bring proof of address and his birth certificate. You will get a form to take to your Dr. for proof of immunization and a physical, this is to be returned at the start of the school year.


As far as kindergarten registration- there is a time frame (check the website for the dates) in late winter or spring for the following school year. Again you have to go to the school in person and apply with your child (at least that's how it was last year).
You may apply to any school you wish but if it is not your zoned school your chance of getting in can be slim to none depending upon the school.
You can check the website to find your zoned school or call 311 (when I was checking on schools the website wasn't working that well).


I think the only registering you can do online is for charter schools and to sign your child up to take the Gifted and Talented test.
Charter schools are chosen by lottery- they have a waiting list so even if you aren't chosen at first if you have a low number you may still get in.
 
You have the option in pre K to have your child tested for the "gifted and talent" program if your child does well you can apply to selected schools that offer the program.
Slots in those schools are given by score (higher the score the better your chance of getting the school of your choice) your child is guaranteed a spot in the program but it may not be your first choice.


I am probably giving you too much information and your head feels like it's going to explode!


You have time.  Talk to parents about schools, check out the school websites, for kindergarten you can tour the schools. Luckily there are a lot of options in the area, and some really good schools. There are also plenty of private options but I am not familiar with those.
