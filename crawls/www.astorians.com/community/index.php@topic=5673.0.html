Courtesy of Streetsblog, a Queens Gazette editorial about Queens Plaza:

Quote
The city struts itself bragging that they are going to tear down the grotesque municipal parking garage and they are throwing into the mix a few spindly trees and a couple of concrete benches. They promise to address the difficulty that pedestrians have crossing the plaza.

I chuckle about Queensboro Plaza and those "luxury" condos/apartments that are being constructed right on the plaza.   Who is God's name would want to live there?   In addition to the traffic, noise and unsightly subway staton, there are no amenities at all.    One other little perk of the neighborhood is this is where the released Riker's Island prisoners are dropped off.   Oy.

And Met Life didn't like it so much.   They are already bailing out.   That area was supposed to be another downtown Brooklyn/Jersey City due to the fact that it is a  transportation hub and the proximity to Manhattan.  The City was hoping to entice dotcoms to the area, but we know what happened there.

On the other hand, there is a ton of construction going on.    There are several office buildings being constructed near the Citibank Tower and one apartment building has gone up and is occupied right next to Met Life.   Two other apartment complexes are under construction right on the plaza and there are two or three buildings going up right after the 39th Street stop on the N/W line.
