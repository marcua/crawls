My parents are coming in for the weekend and wanted to go out to dinner on Saturday and get brunch or lunch before they left on Sunday. They don't care if we're in Manhattan or Astoria (I figured with the holiday, Astoria would be easier), but my mom just got done with chemo and she did say that our normal search for strange and spicy food should be put on hold for the weekend. Which means that the Indian and Thai places I checked out (hmmm taste testing!) are out. My mom just needs something that will go easy on her tummy.

I'd really like to take them out to a nice dinner (they're not from around here and they're all proud of me for moving to the big city and working in a big office etc, etc), and don't want to pick a place blind. I was hoping to spend $40-50 per person. They're staying in Manhattan, but will go wherever I want to. I was thinking about Trattoria L'Incontro--but can I really trust what Zagat says?

Oh, those with such great knowledge of food and libation! I beg thee for thy advice!

I figured you guys sound incredibly food savvy and someone must know just the place.

Trattoria L'Incontro is very good, but it is also very loud. With that said, it is an attractive restaurant and the waiters are pretty attentive (but be warned that they do recite a long list of specials).

There are also great Italian restaurants in Manhattan that have a quieter atmosphere, but sorry - can't think of any of their names at the moment.

If you are looking to save money as well, maybe grab a coupon from www.restaurant.com. We have done that a few times and have enjoyed several of the places we have tried.

For brunch on Sunday, I'd highly recommend Tournesol in LIC.  They have a really lovely brunch - very French oriented, but with an assortment of typical American choices.  The food is delicious, but definitely not too strange or spicy. Neither of the web addesses they've had seem to be active right now, so here is citysearch's page on Tournesol.  Reservations are recommended, but I have found if you get there as soon as they open for brunch, you can get a table without one.  I, too, hope your mom recovers fully.

Tournesol
50-12 Vernon Blvd. nr. 50th Ave.
718-472-4355
