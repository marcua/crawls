JustDesserts & firelight79: When I was a little girl "they" said I had weak ankles. As I grew up I was living proof that you could sprain an ankle sitting still! I was plagued all my life . . . until an Emergency Room doctor suggested that I train my ankles to be stronger. He recommended a Wobble Board. I searched everywhere. (Remember this was long before the internet.) I finally found one on a business trip to Chicago. Once I started using it regularly, I *never* again walked off a curb and ended up with a sprained and swollen ankle.

Mine looked very much like this:
(http://us.st11.yimg.com/us.st.yimg.com/I/net2fitness_1896_5281780)
It has a sorta round-ball on the bottom and you stand on it and balance. I was able to ski, ice-skate and jog after 6 months of use. Now, I have bilateral hip and knee replacements and consider myself lucky that I can still walk.

If you are interested, you can search for an inexpensive one here (http://froogle.google.com/froogle?num=100&hl=en&lr=&newwindow=1&btnG=Search&scoring=p&q=Wobble+Balance+Board&lmode=online&cat=1706&lnk=catsugg).

Perhaps, if you 2 live near each other, you can share one. Morning for one, afternoon for the other.

Remember, if you can wiggle your toes *without* screaming, nothing is broken. It you feel the need to scream, you *must* see a doctor for an x-ray.

Get well soon.
