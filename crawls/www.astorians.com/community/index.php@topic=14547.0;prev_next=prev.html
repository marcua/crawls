I've tried it as well.

Everything was so mediocre that I wasn't sure if a chef was cooking the food or a bachelor who just nukes things in the microwave straight from out of the box.

We ordered a burger, chicken tenders, and a half chicken, mac and cheese, and fries.

First off, the burger lacked salt and spices.  There was clearly no salt on the patty and no spices either.  Although the burger did retain some gas grilled flavor, it was very bland and underwhelming.  Slathering ketchup on it only helped a little.  The fries also had no salt or seasoning.  Again, this meal turned into a ketchup fest.

The chicken was ok.  Not good but not bad either.  It was moist but didn't have much flavor.

The chicken tenders were cooked golden brown.  But again, no salt or seasoning.  It was literally just chicken dunked into the fryer.

The mac and cheese tasted like something I always get from a supermarket.  You can't lose with it but you're not going to wow anyone either.

Overall, the meal ended up costing $40-ish.  I would say that it was very lacking for a $40 meal.  I might have only paid $20 for it looking back.

Food: C+
Portion: B
Value: C

Would I recommend?  Maybe.  Only if you're too lazy to cook at home and want to overpay for food that your non-cooking friend can do in their own kitchen.  It's very blah, bland, and under-seasoned.  It's like the kitchen staff wants to make the food look like it's supposed to look in the picture, but other than that, don't know how to cook.
