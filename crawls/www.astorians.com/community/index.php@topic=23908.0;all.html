For some reason, although it's not far from me, I never got in the habit of going to Best (Yet). I have usually made it there only once a year or so, when I remember to check it out again. Last month it must have been time for my yearly visit. I hadn't known they dropped the "Yet" from their name, and I don't know if that was the difference, but I found the quality had dropped significantly from my last visit. They also seemed to carry only the major national brands, with little space for local or organic brands (which was true to some extent of Best Yet as well). I ended up getting mostly household cleaners and other non-food items. I doubt I'll be back (although I agree that they have a good beer selection). I don't buy supermarket meat, so I can't comment on that. 

On the other hand, I have been more pleased with Berry Fresh Farms (or is it Fresh Berry Farms; I can never remember) since their expansion into the laundromat space. They still don't carry a lot of organic produce, so I usually end up buying less than I intend (I don't buy exclusively organic, but I try to for the Dirty Dozen), but they have a better selection brand-wise of other items, often with better prices (for instance, they often have Rao's sauces for several dollars less than other markets in the Ditmars area). They've also expanded their selection of organic or less processed brands, like Bob's Red Mill (as has the Trade Fair on Ditmars, for that matter), have a good selection at good prices of mainstream organic dairy products, and a great selection of butter, including butter from Ireland, Fance, and Italy, as well as better quality American brands like Cabot, Kate's and Plugra.

I was at Food Bazaar today. They are still in grand opening mode, celebrating with a DJ, a couple of people dressed up as circus performer making balloons animals  for kids, and a mariachi band.
