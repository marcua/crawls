Quote from: goldfish boy on July 24, 2006, 07:53:52 PM
Well, Michael Bloomberg has been praising Con Ed, which means he is either stupid or corrupt. 

Actually, I'm curious to see what he's saying in another 3 or 6 months.

I could wish that he were being a bit more reserved in his praise and took a line more like "Right now our first priority is to get power back to everyone and make sure that the system is working properly - THEN we will be looking at who is to blame and holding them accountable." But I think he has a good reason for this tactic.

I can't see what it would do to fire the CEO of ConEd right now. I've heard nothing that clearly points to his being to blame. His henchpeople, who probably contributed to the problem if the problem is with the leadership of the company, will still be there. Firing the CEO or all of the top people in the company- with no clear picture of exactly what went wrong and how, and no plan for bringing in better company leaders and transitioning them in smoothly - would do absolutely nothing to help with the current situation. It would simply create more confusion and chaos. We NEED ConEd to work as smoothly and efficiently as they can right now, and changing their leadership or even announcing a future change in leadership would only be detrimental. Not to mention the fact that hiring and firing high-level people is very expensive. A quick reaction in this situation is simply not desireable.

I imagine that later, when power is restored and people have calmed down, questions will be asked and, hopefully, the guilty heads will roll - especially if citizens demand accountability. But right now I think it's good leadership to support ConEd as much as possible in getting power back to everyone. Blame can wait. Justice is best served with deliberation.
