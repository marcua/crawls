Meg - I must have just missed you guys!  I got there around 8:45 for dinner last night.

I had high hopes for the lamb vindaloo myself, as it's probably my favourite dish (well, goat vindaloo is my favourite dish, but it's harder to find).  I've only ever had it once where it was spicy enough for me - as in knock your socks off-make your head sweat-stun you with its complexity that shines through the heat.  Last night at Seva I asked the waiter to have them make it truly, really spicy.  While it tasted good and the lamb was tender, I was truly, really disappointed in the spice level.  It had some heat, but what I would consider average.  I told them I was disappointed, and they offered me hot sauce.  What they initially brought wasn't any hotter.  I sent that back and they made it hotter finally -- but it still didn't make my scalp sweat.  That's what I was looking for.

Of the appetizers I tried, I liked the bhel puri and the chicken malai kebab very much.  I also liked the taste I had of mulligatawny, but as gfb noted a while ago, the crab cakes were subpar - and not very Indian-tasting to me.  The chicken malai kebab had a very nice heat level.  We also all really liked the sesame naan.

Service was lacking, although our waiter was pleasant enough.  It was incredibly slow, and they told us it was because they had a lot of delivery orders.  I can understand if they had just fired a bunch of orders initially, but the pace of deliveries clearly slowed and we didn't get our food any quicker.  At this point, we were the only table seated in the restaurant.  They brought the appetizers out one at a time, with minutes passing between each dish.  We waited an inordinately long time for our entrees, and when we finally caught our waiter's eye and asked what was going on, the waiter said, oh, you were busy talking so I was waiting to serve you.  ??? We all found that to be a really bizarre statement.  He said the food was ready in the kitchen, but it still took 5-10 minutes for the dishes to start coming out after that. 

I would like to try other dishes there, particularly the green chili chicken, and I really want to push them on the heat levels.  Don't get me wrong - I'm glad they can adjust the heat level for those who prefer it less spicy.  But given what I've read on this thread, I was surprised at how timid the vindaloo was since I ordered it spicy.
