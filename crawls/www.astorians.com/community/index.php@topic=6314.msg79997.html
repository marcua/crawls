Re: Panera in old Gold's Gym location
« Reply #33 on: July 02, 2007, 09:30:13 AM »
B&N will buy books at a price and pay the author full royalties on a certain quota of books, but they have a clause in the contract that if they can't sell the books at full price, the author will have to pay them back the royalties on any unsold or discounted books. It's a pretty nasty practice and I've seen it bankrupt writers. It's really only B&N that does it, as far as I know, but their purchasing power is too immense to get it out of the contract. The only authors that have the power to turn them down can pretty much be assured their books will sell enough copies to meet the B&N's inital quotas and won't risk their relationship with the retailer.
