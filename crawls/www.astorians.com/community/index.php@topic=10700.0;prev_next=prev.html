Stewart, I fully realize the wrong message it sent. Also, I've apologized for that. My intention wasn't to stop driving traffic to the site, but rather than keep removing the post of the "bumper" to make the link temporarily not work for them. In short I was annoyed and made a bad call. I genuinely feel bad that some took it like I meant harm towards the site. This couldn't be further from the truth, and any of my friends can vouch for that.

Generally, I sincerely have nothing against astorialogues as a whole, just people who keep bumping posts is all. The existence of this thread I hope shows that I have no objection to the link - just felt it was overtly promotional by someone I perceived as a founder of the new site (apparently I was wrong, my "research" on these kind of things isn't really scientific. I generally try and get a sense for if something is self-promotion, or a genuine endorsement of a business by a third party).

In sum, I've never had a problem with the site, and I greatly respect what Adam and his team is trying to do (as I've done it myself and it's a lot more difficult than it would appear). I fully endorse the site, and their proposed ideal of "free speech" that they are striving for - it's not unlike the ideals I had for Astorians when I first launched it. I've since discovered that I don't indeed believe in completely free speech for a variety of reasons, and after much discussion with others rules were created to create a framework for the dialog on this site. While it's been bumpy, I believe it's largely been a success given the diversity of the crowd both here, and in our neighborhood. In no way do I think Astorians represents the ideal however.

Speech is a messy thing, and this site (as do I) remain a work in progress (current example notwithstanding).
