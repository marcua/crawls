Zeno says:  " 'Those who don't learn from human history are doomed to repeat it.' "

Frozenfoot says:  "i don't think it is 'natural' to make assumptions about a person's character, intentions, nature based on their appearance..."

I'm going to coopt that very broad first statement and apply it at a micro level.  I'd suggest that Enigmacat applied the lessons of history to her plane situation in a very real and understandable way.  Her learned expectation was not based on learning from a parent.  It was taught by the (mostly Saudi, remind me how we ended up in Iraq again?  Oh yeah Bush the younger's vendetta and oil, etc.) psychos who were all muslim and very middle eastern looking young men.

I will not even address genetic factors here, but will stick to learned behavior.  Let's not forget we are really our own first teachers and we never stop learning.  We observe and explore and experience the world around us.  We make many of our decisions based on these experiences.

In this case we have a clear example of a subset of people that are clearly deranged.  They do not represent Islam as a whole.  They do not represent people of the middle east as a whole.  However, they are from a narrow band of humanity with a distinctive look.  It is human nature to assign categories.  Early psych class brings to mind how kids with brown dogs that are fuzzy will call anything a dog that has four legs and fuzz like a bear.  Bears are dogs until someone corrects them.  Cars and trucks are all cars or trucks depending on what is learned first.  But I digress.

It makes no sense for people to see a pattern and not recognize or try to categorize it.  Can everyone not agree that all the 9/11 related activities including the recent subway bombings were by young radical muslim men?  Why are we wasting time searching 85 year olds and 5 year olds.  Random searches of bags in the subway.  Brilliant.  It makes no sense.  Not that I'm a fan of the searches at all, but if they are to be performed, why not be smart about it?  The rights of young muslim men should not be reduced, but focusing on searching their bags and exercising extra scrutiny of them on airplanes isn't exactly trampling their rights any more than anyone else's are being trampled.

Back to E-cat.  It just makes clear sense to me that a person can take the facts as they know them and make assumptions on them.  There is justification for her fear.  Considering high minded principles doesn't translate well in an airplane where one's arse is on the line.

Profiling is great to help find those pesky fundamentalist Muslims, but what happens when our target becomes Iranians, North Koreans, Chinese?...  My wife is Chinese - I can't *wait* for China to make a move on Taiwan, as the US has pledged military aid to Taiwan.  Woohoo, profiled Asians everywhere!  Canada, here we come.

OK, a counter argument is out, and it's the basis of the high minded constitutional principle that everyone *must* be treated equally.  Of course, this principle has no place in a survival situation - for instance, the man in the plane with the box cutter is *not* to be treated equally - he should be jumped at the earliest possible convenience.  

So how can we reconcile our fear of the “jittery Muslim on the airplane”, uphold our lofty goal of equal rights for all, and furthermore, keep our nation a safe place to live in?

The problem is while our instinct to survive has remained constant, our sense of paranoia has been dramatically and unfairly increased.  Adding fuel to the fire is the way that Muslims look - because Muslims are very foreign “looking” to many Americans (i.e. there are lots of noticeible differences).  Therefore, its easier to make hasty character judgments and harbor negative associations upon Muslims and Middle Eastern “looking” people, as opposed to French, German, or Russian folks.  
 
Is enigmacat's fear justified, and would I be jittery in the same situation?  You bet, though we've been given good reason to feel so by our friends in the media.

IMHO, a good start to fixing this fear would be to shut down the New York Post, the Daily News, and CNN.  I've never read an entire issue of the New York Post before, but I get nauseus from looking at a) what is called news by that publication,  and b) the millions on New Yorkers that read it.  It has a "War on Terror" section!  Come on, people!! #$@#@$!   Another gross example:  I was living in Richmond while the DC snipers were on the loose.  CNN had 24 hour coverage of gas stations with tarpaulins over them, interviews with Chief Moose, and bullet point lists of what to do to "stay safe".  And of course, we were afraid.  

We have been conditioned to forget that smoking, car crashes, and HIV kills more of us each day than all of the fringe Muslims, John Mohammed (sniper),  Timothy McVeigh, and good old white, Christian, and very fringe Eric Rudolph (abortion clinic bomber).
