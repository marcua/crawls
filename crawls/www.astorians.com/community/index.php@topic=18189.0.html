I'm looking to rent a car for about a 24-hour period, to drive out of state, in an upcoming weekend - from after work on that Friday to the late afternoon on Saturday, or about the same timeframe on a Saturday-Sunday. I'm having a surprisingly hard time finding a company that stays open late enough on either day to do this aside from the ones at the airports, and then the ones at the airports say the shuttle to the rental car places are for flight passengers only, so I wonder if I'd have a hard time getting to and from the car rental place. I'll be driving nearly 200 miles each way, so Zipcar wouldn't be practical. Anyone have any recommendations?

Hotwire.com come usually ends with the Avis at LaGuardia Airport which is open 24/7.  Then we either walk over, or, if we have luggage, hail a cab.  (The money we save doing Hotwire pays for the cab.)  It's only a three mile walk to the Avis.  My bf will often rollerblade over there, pick up the car and drive back to get me and our luggage.  We've also gone to LaGuardia airport and gotten on the shuttle but that takes more time than just taking a cab over to Avis directly.  The shuttle people don't check for air tickets.  You just have to stand in the line.

There are a few rental car places in Manhattan that have decent hours -- I've rented from an Avis on the UES and in midtown, both easy to get to by subway. While the base rate at the airport is often cheaper, there are additional airport fees/taxes that sometimes make the rental ultimately more expensive.

I try to time my rentals so that I can use the Enterprise on 31st St & 36th Ave. Their hours suck, but the rental prices are reasonable and the guys in there are very nice.

I always rent from the airport - I take the M60 from astoria blvd.  Very quick process and usually pretty cheap.  I priceline for rental cars and I feel I get very good deals that way. 

I have taken the airport/car rental shuttle bus for Hertz a few times before - never a problem not being a flight passenger.  The guys driving the bus could care less in my experience.  

There are a couple rental car places that are on the bus route that don't require taking a shuttle.  Enterprise is on 23rd Ave so you could get off the bus before you cross the grand central and enter the airport.  It's a 2-3 minute walk to Enterprise from the bus stop.  

I think Dollar is also on the M60 bus route but earlier than Enterprise.  

Taking the shuttle bus will probably slow down your process.  Last time I waited 20-30 minutes for the Hertz shuttle bus.  Next time I rent I'll be aiming for Enterprise or Dollar if their prices are good so I can avoid the shuttle.
