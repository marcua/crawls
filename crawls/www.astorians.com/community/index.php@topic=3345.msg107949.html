Hello, hello.  I've been getting all the hair ripped out of my nether regions for about 3 years now...here are some places to check out..... 

Currently, I go to Unique Eyebrow Threading Salon on 6th Avenue between 24th and 25th Streets in Chelsea/Flatiron.  While obviously not in Astoria, it's around the corner from my office, so every 3-4 weeks, I am there on my lunchbreak wincing my way through Brazilians.  To be honest, it's less painful each time since the hair grows back much "thinner".  The good thing about them is that they use "Sugar Wax," which is an all-natural, honey-based wax that is free of chemicals.  My skin is so sensitive every time I used  "regular" wax (hello, J Sisters), the irritation was much worse.  Also, some claim that sugaring tends to be less painful because it sticks to the hair and not the skin, so when they pull it off, they are ONLY pulling the hair and not pulling the skin as much.  The women there are primarily Indian and they are so sweet...if you get it done by Heena or Roma, they will talk to you the whole time and trust me - any diversion helps.  Talk to them about the weather, their kids, where they live, your job, your fiance, ANYTHING. There, it's about $35 pre-tax and tip.

Other experiences I've had it done have been at J Sisters (I, too, went there for my 1st brazilian ever.  SO EXPENSIVE - $85 - and the woman barely spoke to me at all other than to reprimand me for shaving up until that point and then again when it was over to try and hock some $20 jar of "serenity" cream or something ...just rip rip rip, ultra-painful, very impersonal - at least it was fast) and I, too, went to Eve's Nails on 30th Ave once (okay, around $30-35, the women there are nice, but they use chemical wax as well).

All in all, you just have to grin and bear it, not be modest at ALL, and be ready to tip well if they're quick and pleasant in the midst of some not-so-pleasant moments!  Good luck!

UPDATE:  Based on astoriagirl's tip about Spa Hydra and the reviews I read on Citysearch, I made an appointment for a bikini wax today and it was great!  It is pretty tucked away, above Cup Diner, and I think I was the only client there first thing on a Monday.  Anyway, the women working were very nice and I was escorted into a wax room with serene music playing and a very calm atmosphere.  I had a French Bikini Wax (no Brazilian this time).

Barbara was my "wax technician" and did a great job.  She was REALLY quick, friendly, personable, talked to me throughout, gave me tips on avoiding ingrown hairs, etc.  Plus she wore gloves (big plus) and I couldn't really tell because I was facing the opposite way, but I think she used a separate "popsicle stick" each time she dipped in.  The wax itself was different than most salon wax - it was blue in color and not too hot.  I think they use a more all-natural type that is very non-irritating afterwards.

The whole thing was over within 15 minutes.  I'm assuming if I had gotten "That Kind" of wax it would have taken longer but only by a few minutes.  Next time I go, I'll give it a shot!
