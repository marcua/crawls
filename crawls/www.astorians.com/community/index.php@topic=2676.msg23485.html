2006 Primaries
« on: July 22, 2006, 03:40:18 PM »
Is anyone following the three statewide Democratic primaries this year, or the Republican US Senate Primary?  Our Attorney General, Eliot Spitzer, is facing off against Nassau County Executive, in the Democratic Primary for Governor.  We have a four way fight for the Democratic nomination for Attorney General with former HUD Secretary Andrew Cuomo, former Public Advocate Mark Green, former Clinton Administration staffer Sean Patrick Maloney and Charlie King all seeking the nod.  Senator Hillary Clinton is facing a challenge from Jonathan Tasini, predominately on the basis of her position on the situation in Iraq.  The Republicans get to chose between former Yonkers Mayor John Spencer and former Reagan Administration staffer KT McFarland.
Is anyone following the races and up for discussing them?  They're all pretty interesting and will have real impacts on our state.

(1)  NY1 bars Tasini from US Senate Debate, claiming he has not raised enough money


As you may have heard, New York 1 Cable News, "NY1" has refused to schedule a debate between Hillary Clinton and Jonathan Tasini.  They claim the reason is financial.  Tasini has collected 40,000 signatures and will be on the ballot. He is tracking in the polls at 13%.  In the past, NY1's threshold for a debate has been that the candidate had to be polling at at least 5%. Now, it seems that they are changing the rules and adding the proviso that the candidate must have also raised $500,000.  This is outrageous and calls for action.

The Tasini campaign held a press conference about this yesterday.  The following is an excerpt from a letter sent by the Tasini campaign today:

 
We are asking you to immediately call Robert Hardt, NY1's Director of Politics, who was one of a few media power-brokers who made this decision that is closing off discussion and censoring debate. We are calling for a simple criteria for participation: any candidate who has qualified for the ballot should be allowed into the debate. Call or e-mail him TODAY: 212-379-3330 or Robert.Hardt@ny1news.com and demand that NY1 revise its criteria.

Democracy for NYC believes in fair participation and accountability in our political system, and we urge our members to contact NY1 today.
