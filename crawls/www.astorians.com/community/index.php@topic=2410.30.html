This brings me to wonder however, just what the store's policy is concerning supplying made up data when it comes to warranties and whether or not they are bound to honor them.

@mcdirk: I may be considered paranoid, but giving one's name may be just as significant as giving one's address, telephone number, social security # and medical history as all of these things can easily be deduced from it. A name is one's identity and if I'm not mistaken one's "property" as well. Of course I may be wrong here in this regard, but having my name bought, sold or traded between companies without my permission gives me a dirty kind of feeling, like I've been violated somehow. But this is just me. I don't mind giving my name to people that I like or trust. But if some stranger came up to you on the street and wanted your information, what would you say?

On the warranty I paid cash. I got a receipt. I am also curious if they will honor it. I doubt that there will be an upgrade to the tape recorder :) but I am confident that if it breaks (through normal use) that they will honor it (the warranty).

But surely someone else has stories of their own to relate: Jcy? Mcdirk? Enigmacat? Surely at one time or another during your lives you have had your privacy violated. What were the events? How did it make you feel? Was there a way to prevent such violation? And if so, should such measures be practiced in the future even if such procedures are not practiced or accepted by the population at large? I have had experiences in the past involving a stalker who used my name in order to establish where I lived and so pester me. There is a distinct reason (besides being a fundamental right) why I choose to treat my name with the respect that any other form of identity deserves.

Also, I don't think anyone has tackled the dichotomy between the right to privacy and the right to free speech. Many times these two rights come into conflict with one another, such as in cases of National Security versus journalistic expos√©s. Should private individuals have the same or similar rights to government agencies or corporations in regards to privacy?
