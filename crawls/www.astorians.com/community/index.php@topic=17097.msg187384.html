I had both my kids at NY Presby on 68th St. and York Ave. Had complications with both, one had to stay in the NICU, and I am so glad I went to that hospital as opposed to any other. They took care of me and the babies very well. I have a car so I ended up getting a monthly parking pass and going there day and night to be with my baby in the NICU. It was a quick drive even with traffic and bad weather.

The only time it got a bit hairy was during the holidays with holiday traffic and lack of parking, but since I was having complications, the subway (stairs) was a no-no for me.

I was suprised to hear about the high C-section rates that another poster stated. I had major complications and they were able to avoid a C section. I think that has a lot to do with the doctor as well. Once you find a good hospital, you need to find a doctor that is on the same page with you. Although they are a very busy practice and you might get any one of the 4 OBs to deliver you depending on who is on call, I can recommend Cornell Medical Associates.

If you want to avoid the city, I understand that there is also a wonderful labor and delivery unit at Long Island Jewish which is not that far away.


Congratulations and  Good Luck!
