C’mon guys do you all really consider Steinway Street far from the subway? It’s 6-1/2 city blocks, 7 if you count the measly distance from 31 street to 32 street. My 86-year-old grandmother used to be able to clock that in less than 10 minutes – and that was after she loaded up a couple of shopping bags and a push cart at Key Food.

Sarah -- The 22-block on Steinway is prime Ditmars territory and definitely not a far walk from the subway – at least by my standards – and you’ll have the choice of mixing it up by walking down either Ditmars with the shops or 23rd Avenue with houses. Ditmars stores, Trade Fair supermarket and bakeries and restaurants are steps away, take a left at the corner actually and there you are.

As far as safety is concerned, Ditmars is usually busy, late into the night and the Ditmars area is as safe as any in Astoria.

Steinway Street though is one of the main north/south streets in the neighborhood, and is busier than most side streets. It’s not a super highway but it is two-way and has more traffic, including truck traffic than a side street.

The block where the apartment is located is mostly residential, with some small apartment buildings at the corners and a long row of 3-family houses (where I’m assuming the place your husband is looking at is located) with garages/off street parking, which you’ll need because with all the driveways, on street parking is tough. There’s a small park playground mid-block across the street from the houses.

You’ll have very easy access to the Grand Central, making your commute to Long Island somewhat straightforward than if you lived in more southern and western areas of the Astoria.

(And Astoria Park is a long walk or a short car ride away.)
