So many events...phew! Anyway, again, thanks for supporting the Astoria Music Now Festival. Continue on to this one and I swear a good time. This event is my baby at Waltz-Astoria every month and I'm really excited about this Thursday. Tony O'Neill is not only an excellent writer but an engaging reader. I'm actually really proud of every one that is performing this night. It's a very unique experience because it's more than a reading and just underneath a balls out party. Here's the info...there's nothing in Astoria like this.

Thursday July 31
A Night With The Whiskey Dregs
a one night stand
Thursday, July 31 @ 8PM - 11PM
at Waltz-Astoria
Enjoy edgy and contreversial poetry, short stories, and musical acts with A Night With The Whiskey Dregs
Hosted by Carlos Detres
Thursday, July 31, 2008
at Waltz-Astoria (www.waltz-astoria.com)
(71 8) 956-8748
23-14 Ditmars Boulevard

The Waltz-Astoria is an intimate setting with a vast selection of beers and wine as well as tea and coffee. This unique reading is accented by a party atmosphere where drinks are served along with raw language. We have a great line up for this evening. Here are your performers:
Tony O’Neill - writer of Diggin’ the Vein and the upcoming books Hero of the Underground and Down And Out on Murder Mile.
Nate Metzker - Musician. I heard him play at Freeze Peach Cafe and knew that I had to get this guy to do at least one of these.
Carlos Detres - Writer, editor and publisher of The Whiskey Dregs for both Plugged and Unplugged versions.
Grant Gordon - Comedian and one of the funniest guys in Astoria.
Steve Falcon - One of the Whiskey Dregs newest friends.

Hey, all. Thank you SO much for coming out and supporting last night. It was a really great turn out and almost everyone who attended was from Astoria, which strangely has never happened before. They're usually from all over the city so it was great to see people from our neighborhood.

A lot of people asked about Tony O'Neill afterward. The books he's written taht you can buy are:

Seizure Wet Dreams (poetry)
Songs From The Shooting Gallery (poetry)
Diggin' The Vein (novel)
Hero of the Underground (novel)

You can get all of his work from Amazon.com. I'm reading Songs From The Shooting Gallery and Hero of the Underground now and they're really f'ing good. I really believe that he's one of the most important writers out there right now.
