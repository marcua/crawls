Title: Re: Theatre Ensemble
Post by: SpecialK on May 20, 2007, 11:34:02 PM
I think it's actually harder to do community theater in NYC than it is in, say, Cape Cod, or in North Carolina (where I come from). 

Astoria is chock full of terrific, talented theater people.  I know quite a few of them.  Actors, designers, box office staff, company managers, stage managers, singers, dancers, musicians, etc etc etc.  This is a great neighborhood for theater people since it's so inexpensive, close to midtown, and arts-friendly, but it tends to support a commuting theater population rather than a population that wants to actually do theater out in the neighborhood.  The problem is that so many of them are pursuing professional theater careers over on the other side of the East River, and for various reasons (some of which relate to schedules and some of which are more complicated*), it's difficult to do community theater.

I don't want to make it sound impossible, though, because Astoria has supported some great theater (APAC for one) and I'm sure there's room for more, plus hopefully we'll have some community access to the performing arts facilities they're building at the Frank Sinatra School of the Arts on 35th Ave?  Seriously, there are challenges, but I hope you can overcome them and pull something together.   :mrgreen:  There have to be people with other jobs who would be interested in community productions, and hopefully you can get them together.

(*I'm contractually forbidden from getting involved with any producing organizations because of conflict of interest issues from my day job.  That's just my own example.  :|)
