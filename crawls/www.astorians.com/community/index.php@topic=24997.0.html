Why are there so many car accidents @ Crescent St. & Hoyt Ave. S?
« on: June 12, 2013, 09:43:09 AM »
I have been jogging down Hoyt Ave. South for years and I've seen so many car accidents at that corner I couldn't count.  I've never actually seen a collision, but I've often seen the crashed cars, the police, the ambulance and the people involved.  Many signs I've seen the broken glass and car bumpers left on the side of the street.


I have often wondered why there are so many car accidents at this corner.  For years, when they were working on the bridge, they had those Jersey barriers along Hoyt Ave. S. blocking several lanes of traffic. I thought that had something do w/ all the accidents.  But they removed those barriers years ago and the cars still crash into one another w/ great regularity there.


I have no idea why this happens. It doesn't appear to be a particular bottleneck or designed badly.

If I recall right there are some really badly timed lights there. Coming south on Crescent, Hoyt North goes green and you have a few seconds to get past Hoyt South. Badly timed lights are really an epidemic in astoria/LIC - it causes people to speed trying to make lights. In manhattan and brooklyn, you can generally drive quite a distance within the speed limit without having to stop at a red light at every other corner. I suspect the frustration from this makes people around here drive a lot more aggressively.
