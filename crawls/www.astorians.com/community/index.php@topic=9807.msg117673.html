AMMI was for me the best thing in Astoria.   I've been an AMMI member for almost as long as I've been in Astoria, now over 20 years.   I've literally seen hundreds of movies there.  Coincidentally, the first movie I ever caught there was the last movie that screened before it closed its doors for the renovation:  HOW GREEN WAS MY VALLEY.

I am going to miss this place.  In the early days -- the early 1990s -- they used to have weekday screenings and I remember once slipping out of work one afternoon to see a film, BAD COMPANY, and there were about two people in the theatre besides myself.   They quickly stopped those screenings.

As Astoria started to gentrify, AMMI tried to start a series called "Cinema Classics" and had a Thursday night screenings as well as weekend screenings of a classic film.   They didn't draw on the weeknight and refined the schedule so the classics, which supplemented their regular repertory programming, screened early Saturday and Sunday evenings.   Those were fairly well attended.

At first, in days before the Regal 'plex opened and P.C. Richards was still King Kullen there was absolutely nothing around AMMI.  The area was real quiet.   The bar Southwick was a deli run by an Irish woman, but that went out of business and the storefront was vacant for awhile until Southwick moved in.   Now the chain's have arrived and the area is bustling with activity at night
