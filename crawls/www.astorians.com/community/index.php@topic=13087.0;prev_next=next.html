Are there sexist people in Astoria? Yes.
Are there sexist people in Harlem and Washington Height? Yes.
Are there sexist people everywhere? Yes.

Should Astoria, Harlem, or Washington Heights be singled out as sexist neighborhoods? No.

While I whole heartedly agree that you shouldn't be subjected to such behavior I don't think it is something that is indicative of Astoria, Queens, or even New York City in general. But unfortunately is instead an issue that is more universal.

The thing that always gets me is, what do these guys expect as a response? Do they expect that you're going to think to yourself, "wow, he thinks I'm pretty" and then turn around and walk over to him and start a conversation? I mean really? And if it gets out of hand, call the cops and file charges of harassment.
