Yep, those reviews are pretty bad. Maybe a down-to-earth Astorian staff will improve this location. Seems like it would be a nice complement to Nook and Cranny next door. Still, I was sad to see the Hallmark store go. It's disheartening to see some of the changes that have gone on on that block. The hairstylist The Place was replaced by that cell phone store (which of course replaced Oleput a few years ago before moving to the current location a block away). The corner deli space on 29th Street has been vacant for almost a year now. Not to mention Frieze Peach being replaced by a bank.


On the flip side, I'm cautiously excited about some of the changes on the far end of Ditmars near 21st Street. The Place relocated there next to Bistro 33. A coffee shop opened up in the last year, and a candy store is set to open soon. I remember when Bistro first came to that desolate strip, replacing, I think, a laundromat. It's a hard spot that you need to go out of your way to get to unless you live in the immediate area--although being close to the park is a boon in the warmer months. I had high hopes when they opened that coffee/ice cream place next to their restaurant. It never seemed to get off the ground. I don't think they ever fully committed to it since they already had the restaurant. They turned it into a Sushi place before closing that as well. I think they still have a lease on the space, so maybe it will turn into something else. Hellgate on the Park is also there, but I haven't been since they stopped serving the great Italian food. I heard a rumor that they had Guinness on tap, which would be awesome if true. Does anyone know if they still serve food? The city just planted a bunch of trees on that strip as well, and I think that could go a long way in making it a more pleasant, leafy destination.
West Ditmars, maybe? Or is that just too ridiculous and pretentious. I'm not in real estate, I swear!
