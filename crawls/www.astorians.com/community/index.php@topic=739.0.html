The only way I can more succinctly put it besides what I posted on the petition is that Seaburn left me feeling icky, like I was in some sort of cult bookstore. At first it was the lack of organization which, while not a huge problem, gave me no compelling reason to return to the store, whether to browse or look for something specific.

What did it for me was the basement where there were stacks and stacks of Black Power/Black Nationalism/Black Racism/who knows what books. These were the only thing organized about the place (besides the Greek-language shelf next to the front door on the main floor). And I got the feeling that there was some sort of political agenda at work here behind the business, and I like my bookstores neutral and non-partisan, thank you, not agit-prop centers.

My girlfriend as about as liberal as they come and even she was turned off by it. Me, I'm your average bourgeois libertarian, and proud of it, so you can see why I wouldn't want to do business with Seaburn.

I know the previous store was left-leaning, and that didn't really bother me. However with Seaburn, after having seen the stuff in the basement, I (being white) had the distinct feeling that I was on some sort of hostile territory...

I certainly can't comment on the store's organisation, or lack thereof - or their stock of books generally, but it seems that the books that turned Daveed off are part of the publishing side of their business.
