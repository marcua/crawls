Quote from: merm on September 23, 2006, 06:23:04 PM
I just don't think their management gives a rip about how their store operates.

I simply hate that store. If there was another one I could go to I absolutely would. It's in moments like these I wish I had a car.

Of course the owners/managers don't care...they have so much traffic, why should they?? 

The bottom line is: they have a monopoly due to thier location.  Thier convenience wins out above all else.  And truth be told; most of the supermarkets nearby are not that much better.

That's one thing I'll give the Long Island Suburbs; thier Supermarkets are usually very good.  They typically have large Deli departments, a butcher on premises and large, well stocked isles.  And they are usually very clean and well maintained.  Not to mention adequate parking.  Others even have amenities like Wine and Cheese sections or an in-house bakery.

But then again, suburban Supermarkets have the luxury of being built on larger plots of land.  And since they're usually the ONLY supermakets within 10-15 miles or so, they almost need to be that accomodating.  It wouldn't be fair to then compare our local supermarkets on the same basis.

So you don't get the big isles, tons of brand options or "expert" service.  But, what you do get is the convenience of getting your food on your way walking home; instead of driving 20 minutes after you get home. 

I guess you have to take it for what it's worth.
