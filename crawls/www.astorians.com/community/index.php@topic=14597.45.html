Went Saturday afternoon and had the lamb burger (this one doesn't have cheese on it). Before I ordered, I asked our very gracious and friendly waitress, how many ounces the burger was. She said 8 oz. Ok, now because I'm always weighing my food, there is no way that was an 8oz. burger, it was more like 4-6; 8 maybe if you include the bun, toppings, etc! Ha. I asked for medium and my husband, who got their signature bareburger, asked for rare. Both of ours came out well done. My lamb was very tasty and very very lean, not what I was expecting, but still good. Would I order it again? Probably not unless I really tell them to watch the cooking on it. My husband's burger probably had a bit more fat on it and it was really good. We didn't order any extras like fries, onion rings or shakes, so our bill for two burgers was $23. Not exactly cheap for burgers, even organic burgers. At this price point, I'd throw in the damn fries.

mcgowan, I don't want to belabor this point because I probably won't be going there very frequently. BUT, I do cook on a regular basis and I know that ground and cooked will weigh differently. Setting aside for now whether she answered me in oz for ground or cooked, at the most, the difference would have been 1 oz, not 2. If we give her the benefit of the doubt and say the burger was 6 oz instead of what it looked like to me, more like 4-5, then that's a lot of juice and fat that left that little burger! I don't expect her to know this, but I thought it was conveniently not advertised off of the take out menu. (Didn't see the regular menu.) Most places, burger, diners, and restaurants list what their burger size is; it's a matter of pride to advertise the size. If it actually had fat in it, then I could expect there to be a bigger difference between the ground and cooked, but like I said, the meat was really really lean, very little juice to speak of. I don't begrudge them the restaurant. I think it's gorgeous, has a very hip vibe, close to another good restaurant, is very family/baby friendly (there were 3 babies and 2 kids in the restaurant when I ate there) It uses recycled material, organic food, etc... BUT, in this economy (and frankly in any), I can't understand the stinginess of separating all the things you'd get in a typical burger place and charging too much for all of those extras. I love eating out as much as the next person, but this place right now with what it's offering, is great when someone else is picking up the tab. For the same price of $12, I'll be going back to Brick when I want a real burger that I can't make myself at home, and it will come with fries.
