Attention all cyclists:

I just heard about this great new website this morning: Ride the City

http://www.ridethecity.com/

It's kinda like Hopstop, but for bikes. You enter a starting and ending address (or just click on 2 points on the map), and it will map the best route according to the city's bike paths (you choose whether you want the safest or most direct route). It also shows locations of bike shops.

Good stuff!

I discovered this site about a week ago and was really excited about it. They're supposed to give you the safest bike route based on the locations you type in. What a great concept! I bike a ton around the city but am always looking for different places to go and sometimes tire of pouring over maps.

I actually wasn't impressed with the route the site came up with for me. I live off 24th avenue and wanted to go to cunningham park and it made a route for me. It started out okay but then on 34th avenue things started to go wrong. It said "turn right onto 107th street and then left onto 34th avenue..." and then went on to say to make another right onto 109th then a left onto 34th street. That is not possible. I figured that part out but then after Shea stadium it told me to grand central parkway or something and there was no turn.

Basically, use the site with caution. I think they have some bugs to work out.
