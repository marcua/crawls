In 1995, our Zoning Resolution was amended to make it easier for big box stores to settle within the city.  This was because city residents wanted to shop at them and they were leaving the city to frequent them.  Now the city gets tax revenues from Lowes and Home Depot, unskilled labor is employed at a relatively good wage, contractors who are based in the city can compete with those from the burbs because of the cheap supplies they can get.  Property owners, outdoor markets vendors, construction projects, do-it-yourselfers all benefit from lower costs.   Some hardware stores did go south, but the ones that evolved serve the neighborhood better now then they did before.  And the shops which replaced the ones that did fail now service the insatiable demand of our hood's foodies.
