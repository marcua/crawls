I don't condone the squids who feel the need to rev their engines super high in a residential neighborhood. I personally don't like those jackasses who feel the need to do it- it's un-necessary, disturbing to neighbors and gives the rest of the motorcycling community a bad name.

Every other block, you have to swerve to miss some jackass changing lanes and not paying attention. For most cars, it's a light scratch. For anyone on two wheels, it could mean broken limbs, totalled bike and death.

Is it necessary to high rev down quiet one lane streets? Absolutely not. Is it necessary to stay visible among other motorists? Definitely.

I don't think the raw data is a fair assessment of motorcycle accidents altogether:

As somebody pointed out before, there are what we call "Squids" in the motorcycling community. These are generally the people who never took the time to learn proper motorcycle safety, and got a bike to basically showboat, rev loud and go fast without having proper respect for the dangers and responsibilities surrounding motorcycle ownership. I'm willing to guess that a majority of those Unsafe Speed and Failure to Yield accidents were caused by motorcyclists who have either had their license for less than 6 months, or were riding on just a permit and fall under this "Squid" category.

Almost every salesperson I have spoken to when I've shopped for motorcycles has told me the same thing: close to half of new motorcycles that make it out of their showroom floor come back damaged within the first few months.

I've been riding since I've been 18 years old and (knock on wood) have never had a bad accident. I practice safe riding technique, wear my leathers, helmet and gloves and ride with the mentality that everything surrounding me on the road is potential death. My exhaust is louder than stock, but when I'm in a residential area, I keep the revs low, and it's basically just as loud as any other car driving by. When it's on the highway, cars can hear me approaching.  (Not gonna lie, there's a little ego in there too-it sounds pretty cool)

Back to the original topic: PLEASE don't think bad of all of us on two wheels. I have a more heavily vested distaste for those obnoxious loud-revving, reckless show-boaters than any of you do. They not only give us a bad name, but they also raise rider insurance payments because of the constant reckless driving and accidents.

I own what's classified as a "Naked Sport Bike". It's basically the same setup as most sport bikes without the side plastics and a more upright riding position.

Yes- most squids tend to ride sport bikes. Generally, they are the high revving racing bikes that have more power than most new riders can handle. I think they tend to like them because they are flashy and fast. I do, however, have many responsible sportbike riding friends also.

If you really want to identify one of these guys look for any of the following:
- Full helmet improperly perched on top of their head so people can see who they are while riding
- Unsafe Apparel: Tanktops, flip flops, shorts
- The need to twist the throttle and rev loudly at stoplights or stopsigns.
- Wheelies or Stoppies (Grabbing the front brake hard to raise the rear wheel during a stop) in public areas

These guys are the ones who are screwing most riders over. They raise the accident rates and give the public opinion of motorcyclists a big blemish.
