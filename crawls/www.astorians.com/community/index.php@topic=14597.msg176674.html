Generally agree with  most of the comments so far.  Went on Sunday for an early dinner (around 5pm) and tried to be very understanding of the place just opening and expected there to be some things that they would need to work out or not have yet (for instance, beer?  Anyone know if they'll be getting a liquor license? There appears to be a place for taps.)  While the food was, frankly, pretty average I think the concept is still great, the atmosphere is good, and there is a lot of variety and potential for the place. 

The most notable downside was the service.  As a former server, there is NO WAY an inexperienced server could handle on their own that number of tables once they were all full (which they were.)  I felt terrible for the poor girl that they had running around as this was clearly her first time waiting tables and she was struggling.  And I certainly didn't feel like this was entirely her fault.  The electronic ordering is a good idea in concept but in actuality there seemed to be a lot of problems with it - for instance we decided on a minor order addition about 30 seconds after she hit send on the machine and couldn't get this relatively easy change made because of their computerized order placing system.  The change we wanted to make was shrugged off by the girl who was supervising the server who said we'd "have to try it next time."  I think what really bothered me was that this more "experienced" girl who was training the main server really wasn't a help at all.  We had to ask her for water.  Twice!  We also had to seat ourselves and get our own menus.  Also it would've been nice if someone mentioned that they did not take credit cards yet before we got our bill.  A friend who went Sunday night after I was there also mentioned they ran out of the brioche buns and weren't informed of it until they tried to place their order.

The burgers didn't wow me for a burger place - overcooked, pretty small in size/overwhelmed by the bun - but I would be willing to give the food a second chance... except I'd get take-out this time!

Walked by last night and they were packed which is remarkable in this economy for a new place in the middle of the week.  I'll be curious to see if they can shake off these issues.  One of the things that I think is so great about Astoria dining is the high level of service that you get and here Bare Burger was a serious miss.

My BF and I finally tried BB this past week.  I had the bacon cheeseburger with beef and he had the same but no cheese and he chose bison.  We both ordered our burgers medium and both received overcooked burgers.  I didn't send it back because I hate wasting food but next time I will send it back - I hear they're having a problem with that.

Honestly, I don't know why people are up in arms about the price.  I thought $9 for a burger that's made from organic meat is a fare price.  If you add on the fries and everything comes to $15 - I've been to plenty of places that charge that for a regular conventionally-farmed burger.  Plus, where else in NY can you get an organic elk burger around the corner?   

I didn't think it was as good as I had hoped it would be.  I will try them again and beg them not to over-cook the meat next time.

Lastly, they really have to get a liquor license - or at least some sort of temp permit so we can bring our own.  I don't know about you, but I love burgers and beer - organic root beer doesn't cut it.

and, in response to the baby question:  I get really uptight about babies in nice restaurants but this is a casual burger joint, anything goes.
