Don't know if you're driving or not. If not, you  might want to go with Metro-North.

They have a package deal with rail fare and admission to the zoo. Here's the link
for it on their website: http://www.mta.nyc.ny.us/mnr/html/getaways/outbound_bronxzoo.htm

Price is $18.25 for adults, $15/seniors. Kid prices vary by age. The price you pay includes the fare,
roundtrip trolley transfer to/from station and zoo admission.

A friend of mine did it and said it only took about 35 minutes from Grand Central Station and was
much easier than the subway.

My advice is to get there as early as you can so the animals will be more active.

I took the subway up when I went two years ago and it only took about an hour from Ditmars.  If you go that route then when you enter the Zoo head left and it will take you in a nice loop.  This way you can see the cheetah and giraffes first before they get too tired and just start lying around.

Also, we did the POP pass last time and it ended up working out pretty well since you have several options and it's cheaper than paying for each thing separately.

Have a great time!
