Title: Re: Good Caribbean?
Post by: bennyt on January 15, 2007, 04:15:31 PM
With the caveat this is NOT in Astoria-
I had incredible vegetarian Caribbean food in South Richmond Hill.  It was at a place called "Veggie Castle II" on Liberty Ave. near the Van Wyck.  We had faux oxtail, jerk chicken, ginger chicken drumsticks, mac and cheese, rice and black-eyed peas, etc.  It was one of the best fake-meat experiences I've ever had (on the level of Veggie Dim Sum).  And cheap as all get out (for seven bucks we got two entrees and two sides, which were lovingly heaped into a pie-tin container).  We had to eat in the car, though, as there was nowhere to eat (there was one stool in the place, I think).  Sybil's bakery next door was also quite good!

Title: Re: Good Caribbean?
Post by: bennyt on January 16, 2007, 11:00:29 AM
Quote from: Rachel780 on January 16, 2007, 09:56:31 AM
Is Veggie Castle anywhere near a subway stop? Sounds great, I want to check it out, but don't know the area.

I think the closest subway is the Lefferts Blvd. A stop, but it's a bit of a hike from there.  The original Veggie Castle is in Flatbush (near a bunch of different subway stops), and there are plently of other veggie Caribbean places in Flatbush and Crown Heights (among other Brooklyn nabes) that are supposed to be great (but alas, I haven't been to any of them).  My source for these is Veg-Out (Vegetarian Guide to New York City).  A word of advice, though -- there's probably a reason why you "don't know the area"; a trip out there should not be made alone (and probably not at night either).
