We have lived within view of that intersection for 4 years and it's always treacherous. People roll through the stop sign like it's optional, fail to yield to pedestrians and generally drive like they are the only people on the road. And that's just the people on 27th Street! Before the stoplight on 28th Street,  24th Avenue traffic was much worse. People still drive to fast there (the accident is a case in point) but at least it's a little easier to cross if you time it with the light.
I cannot imagine how fast that BMW had to have been going. He totally destroyed one car and busted the axle on his own vehicle. That takes some force! We heard a god-awful "bang" and I actually thought it was the transformer blowing like it did last year. Except it wasn't a hot day so that didn't totally make sense. He said a lot of people were screaming at the driver, which was really pointless. Yes, he drove like an *a*h* but screaming at him doesn't help anyone.
In any case, drivers need to step back and realize this is a neighborhood full of families and elderly folks and that kind of speed is just not ok!
