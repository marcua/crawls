Title: Re: Yarn stores
Post by: megc on December 30, 2006, 12:17:57 PM
There is a little crafty store on Ditmars near 36th Street.  I was just in there buying needles and they had a lot of stuff, for such a small store.  It so close, definitely worth checking out.

I've been to the Michael's on Northern Blvd (thanks, flooz!), and they had some yarn there.  I bought some and made a hat (http://farm1.static.flickr.com/128/336960317_f610784b0c_o.jpg) this week from it.

I just went to Purl in SoHo (http://purlsoho.com) and they had beautiful yarn.  I bought some gorgeous stuff from Manos del Uruguay.  Here's a picture of the colors (http://farm1.static.flickr.com/143/336960314_bb70f6e6ac_o.jpg).  I like their statement on the tag:
Quote
Manos was established with a very clear objective: to give support to Uruguayan craftswomen acting as a net of social promotion all along the country. Its purpose was and is the economic, social and cultural development of its members and the environment in which they live.

It is pricy, though.  But if I like it a lot, I'd go back from time to time for hot yarn.   I'm also curious to see what the yarn costs at the USq Greenmarket, too.  I know there are wool people there sometimes.

I've also heard that Smiley's is a good place, too.
