I have a question for anyone who might own an actual house in Astoria.

I'm really bad at math... ;-)  ...and am trying to figure out if I could afford say a two-family in Astoria.  Ideally I'd like something within the square area between Steinway and 31st Street, and Broadway and 30th Ave, though a few streets away from this square area in either direction would also be ok.  I'm thinking something closer to Steinway or just East of Steinway will likely be less expensive than the blocks that are WEST of Steinway and heading towards 31st Street.

Approx $3k a month for Mortgage, Taxes and Insurance. You pay that 3k to bank who will pay the taxes and insurance for you (escrow balance you accumulate)

That leaves you with utilities and water. Water is billed quarterly, figure ~ $150-200 every 3 months. Electric/gas not much different than you might pay in an apartment, with the exception of hot water (figure an extra $80 month gas) and for heat if using gas, could range between $200 or as high as $500 in one month depending on the weather and how high you keep the thermostat.

Figure low end $3,500 a month, high end $4,000 a month. (based on your purchase price and down payment)

If u wanted a 15 yr fixed you would have to put down more than140k , which is 20% of the purchase price (700k) or you are looking at close to 5k a month with insurance and taxes . It would be tough for a bank to go for that with todays rules . Plus it makes it tough for you god forbid you lose your job and or tenant .  On a 30 yr , with taxes being roughly around 5k for a 2 family and isurance abt $1750 a month your total payment before any other bills would be around $3250.  Thats with a mortgage rate of 4% .   Multi family homes dont get the same low rate your bank advertises , its always a little higher . Read the fine print and you will see that rate applys only to single family homes . 
Areas like Ditmars , you can go all the way down to 21st and its fine . On 30th ave its another story . The projects and other less wanted areas start around there . Not saying you cant find a home and rent it out but it becomes a little more sketchy .  If you are looking for a duplex 2family , then it will be more expensive . True duplex homes are like the ones found on Ditmars , in the 20's up to 29st . They are among the largest homes in Astoria . Some have 2 bedroom rentals on the top , some have 1 .    The duplex (1st and 2nd floor) has 3 bedrooms . Some people if they are single end up living in the basement and rent out the duplex for really good money , plus the top floor apt to make ends meet .
 
Electric is always seperate  so it shouldnt be much if you use your brain and shut lights that arent needed .  Plus not running AC the min it goes over 75 degrees helps too. Open a window instead .   As for heat , most older homes are 1 boiler , but more and more are converting to seperate boilers so the tenant controls and pays for their own usage .  If its not that way , you have to provide heat  but also be sensible about it .  If the tenant goes to work and you know they arent home , you dont need to put it on  esp on days it isnt too cold .  No one is should be in a tank top and shorts in Feb in their apt and complain its too cold .
 
In the end you get great tax write offs , and can write off any expense or bill twds the house as long as it involves your tenant. Heat , any hall way  electric  or front/back lights  ,, water , repairs , advertising , even xmas and halloween decorations and planting flowers .
 
Also i have to say , never ever think you wont rent an apt out in Astoria . Losing one month is not acceptable and EVERYONE rents out in Astoria . People in Whitestone with no public transportation rent out apts for good money . I have never lost a single month ever . Just have good leases that say you need 30 days in advance notice when the tenant wants to leave  and only after their currect lease expires . Breaking a lease is illegal anytime before that .
