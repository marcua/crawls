Let me tell you a tale of the efficiency of the private sector.

The UK privatized it's railways and partially privatized the London Underground about 10 years ago in a bid to run the services more efficiently and provide relief for the taxpayer.

The result, at least on the Tube, is a service thats as least as good or bad as the New York City Subway, depending on your viewpoint, and fares that cause grown men to weep. They are astronomical, which would be fine except taxpayer subsidies are as high as they were before the systems were privatized.

Why is this? Because when you privatize, you add another interest group: the fatcats in the financial sector who need to get their cut of the transit dollar(pound) pie.
   
A weekly ticket on the Tube for Zones 1 & 2 (central London only) is 24.20 or nearly $50; a monthly will set you back 93 or just over $180.

A weekly ticket on the tube for Zones 1 & 6 (which covers the entire city) is 44.60 or nearly $90; a monthly will set you back 171.30 nearly $350!

This is in supposedly socialist Great Britain.

In New York: system-wide a weekly MetroCard is $25; a monthly $81
