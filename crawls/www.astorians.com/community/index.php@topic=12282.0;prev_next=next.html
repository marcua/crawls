My better half and I went to the New York Historical Society this past Sunday
and it was great!

They have an interesting exhibit on Grant & Lee and the Civil War.

Also, if you're into furniture and decorative pieces, they have a great collection of
Tiffany lamps as well as furnishings, pottery, sculpture, etc.

There are also exhibits on the Hudson River School of Painting, Slavery in New York
and politics in New York.

Admission is $10 - very reasonable and worth it. I think free admission is on Friday nites.

Location is Central Park West & 77th Street.

Another place we loved the previous weekend is the Museum of the City of New York at
103rd & Fifth Ave.

We saw some really neat exhibits on Catholics in NY, a new one on Paris & New York design and
politics in NY (guess it's the season for that!)

Upstairs they recreations of rooms from different eras in NY history also a history of NY ports and
the maritime trade.

Admission there is $11 - don't remember when their free time is. A bit of a trip but again, very enjoyable!
