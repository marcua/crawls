Overdevelopment = floods, blackouts, etc.
« on: August 29, 2007, 02:43:05 PM »
Found this article interesting:
http://www.nytimes.com/2007/08/29/nyregion/29damage.html?ref=nyregion

Kinda reminds me of the 1970s when there was that huge winter storm and the city didn't plow any of the streets here.

Anyway, that article talks about the people that were flooded in the storm earlier this summer. The theory is basically that all this development that's been going on, new buildings where there were none, huge buildings replacing much smaller old ones, is basically taxing the already inadequate sewer lines in queens. The article doesn't mention   last years' Con Ed disaster but it should have. More apartments, bigger homes = more demand for electricity on a grid that con ed barely seems to bother maintaining. It's only a matter of time before this seriously starts to effect the subways too. People complain about the N being packed at all hours these days, and with more and more people moving in, it's not going to change any time soon. Getting the MTA to increase service is like asking for a tax break... it rarely happens.

Is it just me ( which I doubt, Queens Crap complains a lot about this: http://queenscrap.blogspot.com/) or is high time to stop this development insanity, at least until some of this infrastructure can be expanded? Perhaps there should be a surcharge on these developers so the city can collect funds to use towards better sewer lines when a new building is build or expanded?

The bad old days of NYC didn't make me or my relatives leave the area... but I think if this overdevelopment/under investment continues, I'll be heading for the hills... kind of ironic that the 'success' of NYC is what is currently causing so many people so much grief.
