Hi George...not to be picky, but I think you are a bit mistaken about oily coffee beans.  Oily beans are the result of degradation of the bean from roasting.  Dark roasting causes more oils to be released.  An oily bean could be far along in the degradation process and not be fresh at all.  Dry beans can indeed be completely fresh/recently roasted.  I don't think that I have ever seen "wet/oily" beans at either Stumptown or Blue Bottle and both companies provide the roasting date right on their package.  Starbucks beans are usually very oily because the burn their beans.

I couldn't agree more about the use or lack of butter in most bakeries. It's easy to understand why most substitute oils and shortenings for butter, it will stay fresher tasting longer. But the taste, yuck!
We'll be serving both American and French influenced pastries made with BUTTER AND HEAVEY CREAM! We won't keep our display cases packed to the gills with product because we want to offer as fresh of a product as possible. I'll try to keep NYCMacUser in astoria with my red velvet cake, or maybe I could win you over with our eclairs which are filled with fresh Tahitian vanilla bean flavored pastry cream made with milk, eggs, sugar, flour and the beans, not the oil based artificial whipped cream that most of us had at our local bakeries growing up.
We'll have cupcakes for the neighborhood kids a more  indulgent Valrhona chocolate mousse-praline torte for those who wants something a little more decadent. In the morning we'll offer croissants, pain au chocolate, blueberry, carrot and bran muffins, scones and a really yummy herb biscuit made with creme fraiche and fine herbs. It goes great with eggs. We'll have apple wood smoked bacon and thick country pork sausage on the AM menu (6:30am-10am weekdays 9am-3pm weekends).
We're not just a bakery, we're going to serve a modest but tasty menu including burgers (made with 50%brisket meat), slow roasted chicken sandwiches flavored with Mediterranean spices, lentil burgers, a garden salad, and fresh iced tea and our gingerade (a honey sweetened lemon-ginger-anise iced brew). Our burgers will be on soft egg rolls and our other sandwiches on Portuguese or wheat rolls baked in house.
Hope to see you soon,

George
