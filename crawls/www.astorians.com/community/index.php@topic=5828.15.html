I have always bought Kryptonite u-locks.  They were an excellent investment, and yes, about as much as I paid for my last bike.  When I lived in Berkeley, there were a rash of thefts of bikes that used the "wide" u-lock.  Thieves were able to fit a small car jack between the two posts of the u-lock and jack it apart.  Crazy.  That's when we were advised to buy the "narrow" u-locks, and do the lock-front-wheel-to-back-wheel-to-post kind of locking.  I never had a bike stolen that way.  But this is NYC and everything is different!

Used to be that you could unlock a u-lock with a ballpoint pen.  Thank goodness the Kryptonite people learned how to make locks that foiled that.

The return policy on Kryptonite locks is sweet, too.

Heff, I feel yer pain. If anyone's been following the "Bike shop in Astoria?" thread, you may have read that I recently bought a new bike from Tony's Bicycles. It's a Trek hybrid (not orange, though!) and is the mirror image of my previously stolen Gary Fisher Tiburon hybrid, which had cost me about $400 bucks. I can't tell you how upset I was to find that it had been stolen out of the locked basement in my old building in Manhattan. I was the dumbass in this situation, having locked the bike to itself and not the bike rack. I used a square-link chain with an American city gate lock run through both front wheel and frame, but someone just picked it up and carried it off to where they could leisurely work on the chain or lock with the power tools needed to cut through. The moral of this story: Don't relax for a second in this city. Lock your sh!t down like it was the last bike on earth and then, perhaps, you will still have one to ride when you come back to it. Oh, and go to Tony's and have them chain your seat. That's the first thing that goes--even in broad daylight.
