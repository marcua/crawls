If you really want to do something about motorcycle noise in Astoria, contact Councilperson Peter Vallone's office to sponsor (reinstate) Intro 416A.  The bill allows police to inspect and ticket motorcycles that do not have the required EPA stamp for noise emissions on exhaust systems.  The only reason why motorcycles are loud is because riders change out the stock exhaust for an aftermarket model, essentially a straight pipe with a removable baffle.  The claim "loud pipes save lives" is a myth.

By that, I mean the author of this article is arguing on a speculative level, much like any other person with motorcycle experience would. I don't think anyone is comparing loud pipes to cancer research, or even putting it on that level. I do, however, stand by the fact that louder exhausts can save lives. There is a fine line between creating visibility and being a disturbance to those around.

It is, however, an indisputable fact: if other motorists are aware of your presence, they will be less likely to accidentally hit you or swerve into you.

Like I said, I hate the people that feel the need to rev their motors super high for no reason. They are disturbing to others. Most aftermarket exhausts, when driven at normal RPMs should be barely louder than most cars. You can't blame the fact that they exist, you have to blame the idiots driving them at high RPMs. I could be just as much of a disturbance in a 93 Camry with a heavy foot and a loud stereo system.

Everyone - I have been personally involved in this issue for years.  Phone calls / meetings / letters etc.

The police are very clear on this matter - they do not chase bikes because to do so creates dangerous conditions.

Personally, I call straight into the precinct when I see the bikes racing.  They race down 19th Street in groups.  This is a residential street where lots of kids cross to gain access to the park. 

Two things you must do:

Call the precinct directly at 718-626-3911 and call 311.  If anyone from the precinct tells you to call 311, explain that this is a local problem and needs to be dealt with directly and immediately by the precinct.

Also, it would not hurt to attend one of their meetings at Riccardo's as a group.  Only if we go as a group can we get them to listen.  One or two voices do not really make a difference.

Please read this carefully and make up your mind about what you want to do:
Years ago, a cop was posted on the corner of 19th Street and Ditmars Blvd all summer long.  That one cop made all the difference in the world.  There was no noise that summer.  I know why the cop was posted there but do not wish to put the information on this forum.

Now, they are busy targeting anyone making any sort of parking infraction.  They are not interested in quality of life issues and no longer bother.

Who would like to attend a meeting with me and have our voices heard?  The next one is June 22nd.  We need a group of at least 15 people.  Who is coming with me?  If you commit, please stick with it.   I have had this happen before and wound up with only a few people although many had committed.
