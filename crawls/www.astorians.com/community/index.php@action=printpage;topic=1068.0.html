Title: On this Monday
Post by: zeno on November 14, 2005, 03:41:51 AM
I chose this episode of Binrev because it is a logical follow-up of the last episode. I have more personal reasons as well, having to do with the contsitutional right to privacy: see the P.A.T.R.I.O.T. Act (http://astorians.net/phpBB2/viewtopic.php?t=623) thread (hopefully I have matured a little since beginning that thread).

I still only use rudimentary encryption algorithms for my communications (if at all) and the reasons for this, although there are a plethora of open-sourced tools available to me only a small download away are simply ignorance and laziness. Many people avoid using passwords because remembering them is a tiresome task, or typing it in simply takes too long. Now, through the modern miracle of mnemonics I can remember a password 40+ characters long! And so can you! Take this example:

 "Sometimes I Go To The Refrigerator Very Late At Night To Get A Late Night Snack. Whether I Eat Cheese Or Cereal It Really Makes No Difference Just As Long As I Remember To Brush My Teeth Afterwards Because Tooth-Decay Is An Unpleasant Thing, Just Like Bad Breath.(my phone # with the last two digits changed (and a different area code)"...

Renders this relatively secure password: ["SIGTTRVLANTGALNS.WIECOCIRMNDJALAIRTBMTABT-DIAUT,JLBB.(my phone # with the last two digits changed (and a different area code)"] =
Sixty-five (65) characters

By using numbers and punctuation marks in addition to alphabetic characters you increase the sophistication of the password. Add to this case sensitivity then you may have something very daunting indeed. Of course, the above example may be a little extreme so you'll have to adjust the strength of your passwords for your own needs. You could measure the time taking to type the password in against how much the information you are attempting to protect is worth. But to not use them at all or a password that is easily cracked or guessed (like a pet's name or your favorite food) may not be advisable. I guess, what I'm trying to say is that with a little information, solutions are only a small act of volition away.

I hope you will join us this week as we learn how to protect our privacy together and am sure you will see me taking notes.
