Title: Re: Time to quit smoking
Post by: AlexNYC on June 06, 2010, 12:20:34 AM
Many people have quit smoking successfully. It's a combination of good planning and being properly motivated. The first thing you need to do is cut back to a more acceptable amount of cigs a day. Try to knock it down to 20 a day, and then 15 and then 10. It will be easier to stop if your body is used to taking in less nicotine. Some people find the nicotine patch a good method, others don't. Once you do stop, replace your bad habit with a more acceptable one like chewing gum or hard candies.


It is doable if you really want to stop. Good luck and keep as posted on your progress.

Title: Re: Time to quit smoking
Post by: ATB24 on June 10, 2010, 12:08:38 PM
This is probably one of the best things you can do for yourself. I quit almost 2yrs ago after smoking 10yrs. I quit because I was becoming disgusted with myself physically.

I turned to sports and cut down drastically only smoking a few a day. Then finally one day, I said, it would be my last day and it was. You have to be hard on yourself, you shouldn't say, August, you should say now.

I feel better than ever and now get disgusted by the smell of smoke. Here are some tips that might work for you:

Get into sports to keep your mind of smoking and doing it with a partner to help each other during week moments
Indulge yourself in other ways (cake, candy, sex) to keep yourself sane during a craving (cravings will kick in, but will go away)
Drink a lot of water (especially when you get a craving)
Don't give yourself excuses (oh, it's just ONE smoke, no! that is how you go back)
Don't think about a year, month or week from today. Just make it a day at a time, eventually it gets much easier
Stay away or cut back from activities that makes you smoke (drinking is usually one of those)
If you messed up one day, move on, consider it a big mistake and go back to being 100% smoke free

Any other help I can provide, just ask, I'm glad I quit when I did
