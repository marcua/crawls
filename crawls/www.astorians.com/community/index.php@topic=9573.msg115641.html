I wouldn't avoid Locale personally - but i wanted to share my latest experience there.  We had a reservation for our party of 8.  When we arrived I saw a "reserved" sign up in the lounge area.  I was thinking to myself hmmm no way they're going to seat us up there.  Everyone in the party arrived and we were escorted to that exact table.  We all just looked at each other bewildered.  Are they really putting us here?  The hostess looked at us confused, we all stood there and she proceeded to try and convince us that the lounge area was a fantastic place to eat dinner.  We will have a great full view of the bar and we have the ceiling fan directly above us so we'll be cool.  She hands the menus over and walks away.  Ok? We're all couples so who cares about the full view of people at the bar?  Anyway, we decided to make the best of it and.....it was pretty horrible.  The food was great (as usual) but there is no way I'll ever eat there if that is the available seating option. 

If you don't have the room to accommodate a party of 8 in the "dining room" then i think they should at least inform you that you'll be sitting in the "lounge area" hunched over on a velvet ottoman (when its 90 degree weather outside) for 2 hours+.  Half of us couldn't even fit our legs under the table.  Love the food, the wine....hated that one experience.  I think the hostess should know that a bunch of 30 year olds probably know when they are going to be miserable.

Just went to Locale for dinner for the first time last week.  We went because we had a $25 Restaurant.com gift certificate.  However, when we got there, the waiter (and later the owner) informed us they cancelled their account with Restaurant.com and were not accepting them any more.  We were very disappointed, and when we complained to the owner he was gracious and offered a 15% discount.  I thought the food was excellent, but a bit overpriced to be a regular go-to place.  Only planning on returning for dinner for a special occasion or to impress friends who are new to the area.  Also, drinks only would be much less expensive and fun (lounge area is cozy and inviting).  Service was fine, but the waiter could have been a bit more knowledgable about the food.  Brunch, on the other hand, is a bit less expensive (and more comparable to other restaurants in the area).
