The thick white cables would be for cable internet and/or TV.

We've had Earthlink hi-speed cable internet through Time Warner since January 2002 and have had mostly good experiences with it.  If there are tech issues you usually have to deal with Time Warner rather than Earthlink, though.  We pay $41.95/month.

We do not subscribe to cable TV, although we have a splitter (installed by Time Warner) and use the other cable as an antenna by screwing it in directly to the TV.  Through this we actually get many basic cable channels but in random locations -- for instance, NY1 is on Channel 3.

I had AT&T High Speed Internet for a little over 4 years.  It was good, although getting it installed and trying to get someone to help when it wasn't working was a real pain. 

First, because Verizon owned the cage that the service had to be initiated from, AT&T had to arrange a "vendor meeting" to gain access.  AT&T told me that it was going to be awhile to hook it up because, well, it's Verizon.  I don't know if they still do it like that, but you might want to consider that before choosing.

Second, I had a couple of issues with the customer service department.  Apparently, AT&T, like many other companies, had contracted out their technical services and they were awful.  The few times that I needed to call them was an adventure.

Overall, it wasn't all that bad.  I prefer my Time Warner cable internet, though, and I don't think I will ever go back to DSL.
