Title: Re: Stuck on a Plane for 11 hours
Post by: Frappe on February 16, 2007, 11:30:48 AM
A bag can never be left on a plane if its owner is not with it.  So to deplane, all the bags must be removed, which means more overtime, more lost bags and more delays.

Then there are the work rules.  A pilot is not permitted to work a certain number of hours in a row.  So if you have a plane all loaded up, you'll do what you can to get it out. If you alight the aircraft, which is an hour long, and then immediately board it, thats another hour.  If the math works out that the pilot will be in the air for one minute longer then the regs say, then the pilot must withdraw from work, and another pilot be found, which usually means the flight is cancelled.

Then there are logistics: aside from servicing the masses of arriving/depart and delayed passengers, the airplanes do not have gates to dock at nor taxi space to manuver.  Sometimes flights which arrived at the gates do not have pilots to move the empty planes away due to the above noted work rules. 

I wouldn't call it incompetance.  I'd call it a string of bad incidents which forced bad decisions.  I got most of this from talking to my friend who works at JFK.  Interesting to hear its inner workings.  A huge byzantine set of rules which at times seem ridiculous all to prevent a lawsuit.
