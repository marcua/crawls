I know it's a park and people like using it to play sports, but truthfully Astoria Park is unlike other parks in NYC like Central Park and Prospect Park in that the fields are non-sustainable. There are no sprinklers to water the lawns ( only rain) so if organized sports are played on the lawns, they will get ruined in a very short amount of time. Plus, technically, if you have groups bigger than certain sizes, you will need to get a permit to play on the field. You can go to places like Flushing Meadows Corona Park where there plenty of soccer fields with astro turf no less that you won't have to worry about killing-LITERALLY. The lawns at Astoria really can't take much more of people playing organized sports on them who don't know, or even worse, don't care that soon we won't have lawns to play, rest, or picnic on anymore. And it really doesn't take much for it to turn into a dustbowl. Have you ever wondered where those bare patches came from, which we have at the top of the great lawn in Astoria Park next to 19th Street? Or the one north of the Hell Gate Bridge, which has slowly been repairing itself, due to the huge amounts of rain that we have had lately? I am not against sports by any means, but we have parks nearby with purpose built facilities, like Whitey Ford field ( 1st Street and 24th Ave-formerly Hell Gate field) http://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&hl=en&geocode=&q=whitey+ford+field&sll=37.0625,-95.677068&sspn=31.095668,59.765625&ie=UTF8&ll=40.774724,-73.933439&spn=0.007247,0.014591&z=16 (http://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&hl=en&geocode=&q=whitey+ford+field&sll=37.0625,-95.677068&sspn=31.095668,59.765625&ie=UTF8&ll=40.774724,-73.933439&spn=0.007247,0.014591&z=16)
