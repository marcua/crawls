My roommate and I both joined Lucille Roberts last year and still go several times a week. They recently installed 3 flat-screen TVs in the cardio room (the ground floor), as well as added several new elliptical machines. It's only gotten better since we've been there - and the price has gone down from $18/mo when we joined to $10/mo (not that it helps us).

We don't do the classes, so I can't comment on them. The classroom is downstairs, and I like natural light. The weight room on the second floor is decently stocked - lots of machines (often 2 of the same), free weights, different size exercise balls, mats, a wall of mirrors. There are plenty of lockers - you just have to bring your own lock (day use only). They have personal trainers, but we haven't used them either (we are both kind of "lone wolf" workout chics).  8-)

The only drawback for us has already been mentioned - the hours. It's only open until mid afternoon on weekends (3?) and doesn't open in the morning until 8:30 or 9 depending on the day. Neither of us have traditional 9-5 jobs, so it's fine for us. If you do, your only option is probably after work. We tend to go during off-peak hours, so I can't talk about getting a machine at the peak times.

Overall, it's a great no frills gym. We both like that its women-only and very casual. Most wear sweats. It's a wide variety of ages, ethnicities, and sizes, all just there to work out, and sometimes chat with their friends.

They always have guest passes available - if you're interested, pick one up and try it out for yourself. I believe you have to give out some info about yourself, but it helps to experience the gym yourself before committing. I joined after my roommate gave me her guest pass.
