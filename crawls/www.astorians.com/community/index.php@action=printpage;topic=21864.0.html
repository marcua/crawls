Post by: thanos27 on October 28, 2011, 02:34:49 PM
Hi , welcome . One bedroom apts in astoria , depending on how updated you want them usually start at abt 1200 and up . To get something nice and modern , stainless steel appl's plus an updated bathroom , expect to pay twds 1400+.  Anything near the train will always cost more .  Parking spots or garages are abt 150-200 a month .    You can always look on craigslist and avoid fees , but ofcourse you should be carefull and ask plenty of questions . You would need to have current credit scores ,drivers  license  and possible references .   Apts are usually rented on the 1st of the month , so unless someplace has been vacant for months it would be hard to get in on the 28th of the month , but you never know .  As far as quite goes , the farther you are from the train and the main areas like 30th ave and broadway , the more quite you should find . Ditmars Blvd is one of the most residential areas with less commercial traffic and less bars and clubs than the other mentions areas .Upper Ditmars is the most quite area in Astoria with zero nightlife going on , meaning no noise , but it is farther from the train . Plenty of people choose this area for its saftey ,family life and available parking . You should come and feel the area out , see what you like and dont like . There are plenty of real estate places all over and you can check them out as well but be prepared to pay their fees if using them .

Title: Re: Looking for a 1Br Apt.
Post by: BoredTemp on November 10, 2011, 01:17:37 PM
trains are indeed 24 hours - they just have reduced service overnight.
 
You can find all the train schedules and maps on the MTA website.  http://mta.info/ (http://mta.info/)  The 1/2/3 and A/C/E all go to Penn station, but none of them go to Astoria.  You would need to take the N/Q/W to 42nd and transfer.  Or, on a nice day you can take the N/Q/W to 34th street and just walk over - which is my preference.
 
The time between Ditmars and 30th avenue can be less than 5 minutes, it's not really a factor.  You can even walk between the stops in 10 minutes or so.
