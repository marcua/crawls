Title: Re: Astoria Coffee
Post by: hikaruhana on October 16, 2014, 10:48:28 AM
+1 on the library idea. Great concept.


As someone who is also self-employed, I also like to switch it up as Philo-Junius does. I work from home some of the time, but too much of that gets claustrophobic and isolating. I also work at Create NY Space, a great coworking space in the neighborhood, and some times I like to drop by a coffee shop for a different vibe. Part of the reason I go is that I actually *want* to be more social.


The folks from Astoria Coffee are good friends of mine and I think their coffee shop is a wonderful place to stop by to get a bit of work done or, if you're feeling social, just pull up a stool at the bar and make a new friend. Late in the day is a good time for doing that--the vibe there is pretty relaxed around 5 or 6 and they usually have some nice classical music or jazz playing.


I'm a little surprised by the hostility to us laptop people. Although I've seen the 'campers' out in force and think that's a horribly selfish and antisocial way to approach things, plenty of us are just trying to be productive and social. I often go out to coffee shops to write by hand in a journal and somehow I suspect that would never be called out as antisocial or rude. Not sure how writing on a laptop is different. I also think the plug compromise is a good one.


I think each coffee shop has a right to set its own rules about WiFi use. After all, they're the ones who will be financially impacted the most by the folks who think it's somehow all right to ask for a bottomless cup of coffee and stay there all day. It also depends on what kind of atmosphere and community the shop owners want to create. That's up to them. If I'm right, Queens Kickshaw now shuts their WiFi off at noon. Other places have different policies. I remember being asked not to use my laptop in a coffee shop in Manhattan since that was their policy, and I thought that was perfectly fine. Clearly they were going for a different vibe, and that's their prerogative.


If you're looking for something less laptop-oriented, there are certainly places you can go in the neighborhood. When I want a different vibe, I sometimes drop by Cafe Omonia. Great people-watching, not a place I would really feel it would be appropriate to bring a laptop, and that's perfectly fine.
