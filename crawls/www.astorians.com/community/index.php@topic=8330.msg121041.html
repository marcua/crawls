He doesn't seem to be The Scary Man.  What does The Scary Man look like (besides scary)?  Is he white, black (light or dark), Asian, etc?  What sort of build does he have?  What is his hair like, or does he let any show?  Etc.?

How about you, Johnny4evah?  Who's the homeless man you're referring to?

I've had some thoughts reading this thread.  One was a story in the New Yorker, I think it was last fall, about homeless services in a city in northern California.  Basically, there are the soft-core cases (most of the homeless), and a few chronic, hard-core cases, those who are very seriously mentally ill.  Those few hard-core cases cost more than all the soft-core cases put together, and it's because of a moral Catch-22.  It would cost very little to put them up, permanently, for free, but no one likes a freeloader.

As for the homeless being mentally ill or vice versa, there's mental illness and there's mental illness.  Not everyone with a mental illness is out-and-out schizophrenic.  There are always those on the borderline who can hold down some kind of a job and live independently if there's affordable housing for them.  But if there isn't, or if they lose their jobs and can't get a new one (why hire a mental case?), they provide a handy excuse for the free-market fundies to wash their hands of the whole problem of homelessness.  They're crazy.  Let the market do its thing.  End of story.

As for conducting a dragnet of possible killers, that's just what a lot of faculty at Virginia Tech tried to do after the killings there.  But what criterion can there be that will weed out all the homicidal maniacs, and no one else?  It was suggested, for instance, to weed out everyone who writes a story ending with a suicide.  OK, that will get you Leo Tolstoy (Anna Karenina), Matthew Arnold (Empedocles on Etna), Willa Cather (Paul's Case), Aldous Huxley (Brave New World), and others who don't come to mind right now.  It will also get you William Styron (Sophie's Choice) and Kurt Vonnegut (Mother Night), two authors who really did face inner demons but who AFAIK never killed a fellow human.

Ok..just some background... I usually am not that afraid of homeless people.. grew up in San Francisco and Berkeley.. where there are homeless people all around.  I usually cross the street, don't make eye contact and I hardly have any problem.  But there is this dude on 31rst and Astoria Blvd (Dunkin Donuts)... I don't know what is ethnic/racial background is.. wild guess is middle eastern, Latino, southern European.. he is about 5'7" light olive skin.  Sometimes talking to himself.. mostly glaring at women.  I am assuming he is sometimes homesless because sometimes he is cleaned up, shaved and with a hair cut, and othertimes I have seen him on the floor in the street sleeping.  It is the WAY this guy makes eye contact with me when I am trying to avoid him that freaks me out.  He sometimes yells directly towards me, so he is speaking to me and asks if I can help him out.  Then he smirks with this look on his face like he is thinking about something and it isn't good.  I try to avoid it, but he is at the bottom of the platform of my train at Astoria Blvd.  I really think one day he is going to do something.  For now I have decided to walk on the otherside of the platform... but sometimes when it is raining or I have had a hard day at work, I just forget.  The other problem with walking on the north end is that it is basically more desolate and then I would have to cross under the bridge... again not totally safe late at night...so I weight my options and decide to just face him and risk him following me since there are more people on this side and I can run into a restaurant or something.  Funny thing is that I can't call the police because what am I going to do tell them I have a "gut feeling"....  He knows where I live because he wonders around and one day saw me get into my apartment. I also saw him going into my trash.  It really freaks me out.  I want to be able to get off the train and just go home. I guess that is the price for living in NYC.... I never have seen him bother men and every time I see him he seems more and more "comfortable" firecting himself towards me... Sorry I am having a hard time putting my finger on the issue and describing it....
