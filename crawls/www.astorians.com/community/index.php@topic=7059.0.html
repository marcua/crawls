I personally would put my stuff into storage but it might be easier to sublet your place if it as furnished. However, I would really scrutinize whom ever you are going to let live there if you do leave your furniture in the apartment. Make sure you are given references and check them. You do not want your personal belongings and furniture ruined. Maybe put your favorite items into storage and leave the rest in the apartment. Good luck.

I would keep any non-heirloom furniture in the apartment and have the subletter put down a furniture deposit. Also- if someone smokes, that will seriously change the smell of your apartment, and even if they "look" like they don't smoke or promise that they only do it outside etc, just assume they will smoke/drink/lounge in your apartment and adjust the furniture deposit to that. And write up a contract saying that cigarette smoke smell in your furniture, rips, tears, visible damage will be covered in the deposit. I know that when I was a subletter, I was very conscious of the furniture deposit because it was pretty steep and I didn't want to lose that money due to stupidity.

And even if you don't do anything to that extent, at the very least have a contract written up and make two copies of it.
