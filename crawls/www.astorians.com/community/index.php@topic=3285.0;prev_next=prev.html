They usually have egyptian TV on.  My experiences there (I live around the corner) have been decidedly un-spicy.  1st time I had Chicken Cous Cous.  It was a lot of food for the money, plate must have weighed 8 pounds, but it could hardly be desribed as spicy.  A little on the plain side of savory is how I'd describe it.  Another time I had a chicken sandwich ona toasted baguette w/ fries.  That was pretty good.  A third time I domn't recall what I had, but it was back to kind of bland again.

They've got a full bar now, and music on the weekends.  Occasionally I've seen a belly dancer in there.  It seems to be a hot spot for slick middle eastern guys and their trashy dates.

All that aside, I like the place.  It's a big step up from the depressing little real estate/apartment broker office that was there for the 1st 5 years I lived here.
