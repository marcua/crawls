Likely they are American Cockroaches. Which always live near a source of water and can come in through the sewer as well (they go in a drain outside and come up in your house). The can live in walls or floor as well if it's damp enough but you'd need to have a leak in there for them to be happy. Most of the time they live outside or in sewers and come in looking for food. They are pretty common in NYC during the summer, you see them even running along the street at night.

I grew up in S. Florida, home of the worlds largest bugs.  I happen to know a thing or two about keeping them away!

You are seeing waterbugs because there is a hole where they are coming into the apartment.  Esp if you are on a 2nd or 3rd floor thats the only way they can get in other than "hitching a ride" in a box or something.

Immediately get steel wool and use it to seal the spaces where your radiator pipes come up thru the floor. 90% of the time thats where they come from in NYC apts.

Combat (the bug trap people) make a caulk that contains an insect repellent; I think they sell it at the Square Hardware at 37/ditmars.  When I started to see bugs in my former apt, my landlord caulked our floors/corners with it and we NEVER saw another bug again.  Regular caulk will work as well; double check the windows, where your air conditioner may be in the window...SEAL UP EVERY CRACK.

Also dont ever leave water in the sinks! wash dishes immediately after using and wipe down your bathroom sink with a towel after you are done using it.  Get some bleach and dilute it 50/50 with water .... spray it in your shower after you are finished...that helps too. That's why they are called WATER-bugs...they are attracted to it!

Keep sugar and all food in the fridge once you have opened it; pet food should not be left on the floor when you arent home.

Buy like 23 packages of bait traps/roach motels and put them everywhere....they do work but take time.

As long as you arent seeing baby bugs the ones you are getting are rogues in search of food and water.  The big ones are nasty but not cause for terrible concern...the little ones, on the other hand, are. 

Im sure your landlord will call an exterminator if you tell them there is a problem. 

good luck...eeeew it stinks but you can eliminate the problem with perseverence!
