Not getting into the morality of the issue, but NYC has pretty strict rules on what can call itself a Spa and what can't (licensing is required to be able to put the word Spa on your business).  As a rule of thumb, if the place uses the term "Bodywork" it is an unlicensed spa.  The majority of the time this means that they are not catering to the typical Spa crowd, but to men seeking sexual release.  Almost all of these place provide masturbation for money, but some (actually a great many of them) also provide sex for money.  An easy way to tell whether the place is offering more than just a back rub is to look at their hours of operation.  These places make their money on turnover and since their clientele is primarily men (who are typically working during the day, they have to stay open late in order to attract business when the men have free time.    If a Bodywork place is open late at night (most legit Spas typically close by 7 or 8 PM at the latest), or 24 hours, it is almost surely a "rub & tug" joint.  Another sign is the physical plant.  These kinds of places invest as little as possible into the spaces they occupy.  Making the place look high end and zen like is not their priority, so they tend to not be anywhere near as nicely finished as legit spas.  Also since they don't want to attract too much attention from the neighborhood, they will often have a tiny sign and have their windows covered at all times, so that you can't look in from the street (legit spas tend to have clearly visible and inviting lobbies).


Sadly Astoria has been seeing increasing numbers of these Prostitution joints opening up.  There are a couple of major ones operating pretty openly in the south side of Astoria (wondergirls spa being the biggest and most flagrantly obvious), and there are fewer ones in the north side of Astoria.  Recently there was one right next door to the Vallone school which kids hat to pass on their way to and from school that was shut down.  The operators simply relocated down the street and now they are operating out of a second story around 23rd ave and 31st street (which I guess is better, but I hate to be a tenant in that building).  I think a new place just opened up somewhere around Ditmars and 33rd as well.  Many of us have been tracking these place for years, and by and large Astoria has remained generally spa free (though not sex work free).  As the population has changed, more and more of these places are moving into the neighborhood chasing the higher incomes.  The fact that despite being reported to the police these places remain open for as long as they do, leads me to believe that unless they are blatantly obvious and become a public nuisance, the police is willing to look the other way.  It wouldn't surprise me if it might not have something to do with organized crime.
