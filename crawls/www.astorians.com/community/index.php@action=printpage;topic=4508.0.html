Post by: lanseaux on January 02, 2007, 11:39:24 AM
Unfortunately, I let my notary license lapse.  As firelight79 said, notarial fees are $2 in New York State and are set by law (http://www.dos.state.ny.us/lcns/lawbooks/notary.html):

Quote
ยง136. Notarial fees.
     A notary public shall be entitled to the following fees:
     1. For administering an oath or affirmation, and certifying the same when required, except where another fee is specifically prescribed by statute, $2.
     2. For taking and certifying the acknowledgment or proof of execution of a written instrument, by one person, $2, and by each additional person, $2, for swearing such witness thereto, $2.

Also, the fee to become a notary is $60, not $75.  Here's a general FAQ page (http://www.dos.state.ny.us/lcns/ntryfaq.html) on notaries in New York from the NYS Department of State.

Title: Re: Notary Public?
Post by: lanseaux on February 10, 2007, 01:05:03 PM
The only thing a notary does is authenticate the fact that the person signing a document is who he or she says, which is why you are supposed to have to present ID to the notary and sign the document in front of them.  It has nothing to do with the authenticity of the document itself, or any organisation or person behind the signature (but it does reinforce the signer's legal responsibility for the document's authenticity).  The fact that a notary has no interest in the identity he or she is authenticating is supposed to make it more reliable and strong, not less.

A notary is a holdover of an ancient office.  Wikipedia has a good look at Notaries and the history (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Notary_public) of the office (here is info specifically on New York notaries (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Notary_public_%28New_York%29)). 

You can also get things notarized for free (http://www.nycourts.gov/supctmanh/notary_public.htm) if you go to the NY Supreme Court at 60 Centre St.  The notary office is in the basement.
