Hi there everyone,


I'm considering taking a job that would have me working from home here in Astoria. While that sounds awesome on the surface, I think that it could also be pretty isolating over time.  As a people person I like to have a good balance of community and individual time.  The company I would be working for is pretty progressive in trying to help its staff work this stuff out and I wouldn't be the only one telecommuting, but all the same I'm trying to think ahead and consider how might be best to navigate this new work life should I end up joining them.


Is anyone else working from home here?  Are there any groups for folks who are?  How do you break up the day so you don't feel isolated?  And also, have you checked out any co-working spaces in the city?  I looked at New Work City the other night.  Appeals to me since I'm in IT but if there were something closer to home that'd be nice too.


Cheers,

I used to live in Astoria and worked from home as well.   Unfortunately, there are just not that many options for coworking in Queens itself and the cafes haven't been a wonderful alternative.  The Kickshaw seemed like a good one but they do have private events during the day sometimes like an impromtu flim shoot and their wifi is very sketchy at best.  Plus you get the distractions of other people's phonecalls and in Starbucks - you get the homeless as well. 

I'm off the 7 line in Jackson Hts now and I recently joined a coworking studio called The Productive.  It's quiet and geared towards other creative entrepreneurs like myself but also writers, developers, and other freelancers.  It's also really affordable and a great option for me, as it gets me out of the house and around other inspiring and motivation people.  You have access to a printer and conference room as well.

I'd highly recommend using a coworking space and if you have any questions, do let me know.  I worked from home for over 5 years, and while I got used to it over time, it defintitely does take a certain amount of discipline and it can get lonely.  Do what you can to make yourself leave the house - at least after work - see friends, join industry organizations or other groups that interest you.  Get dressed every day, even if you don't plan to leave the house.   Somehow it makes the workday seem more official.  If you can, work out in the morning and come home to work - then it also feels like more of an office.  But the coworking studio has done wonders for my productivity - it's less distractions and you are just more focused on getting work done in general.  The Productive is just off Grand Central area and very convenient to most trains.  Most other spaces were much more expensive and in downtown Manhattan or Brooklyn.  Feel free to im me if you have other questions.  http://theproductivenyc.com/
