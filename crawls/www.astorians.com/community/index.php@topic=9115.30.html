You can resent an entire group of people based on political affiliation if you want to.  But it will only serve to marginalize your own viewpoints.  Besides which, it is un-American in what is supposed to be a country of tolerance.  Disagreeing with the average Republican is one thing, but to write someone off because of party affiliation is nothing more than petty.  People hold Republicans accountable for their actions?  The average Republican is not a policy maker.

The policies of the Bush administration are the policies of the Bush administration, not of the Republican party.  A party is an amalgam of people with different views.  The Republican party, especially since the days of Reagan, has been a somewhat uncomfortable marriage of religious and moral conservatives, and fiscal conservatives.  Bush and the neocons happened to have a period of ascendancy, but that does not mean that they are the end all and be all of Republican politics.  Nancy Pelosi and Hillary Clinton have differing viewpoints, yet they are from the same party.  I would never assume that because they are from the same party, they must push the same policy agenda or have the same belief structure.

Also, of course Bush was going to dismantle the policy decisions of those before him.  You act as though that is a surprise.  Now, many people (myself included) believe that he was flawed in his execution.  But it is axiomatic that the next person will rework what the last person did.  This remains true no matter who comes in and out of office.  And I am sure that everyone thinks that their policies are decent.

Finally, your hope that the Republicans would nominate someone else to represent them in 2004 was preposterous.  That is not the way the political process works, and you know that.  Change comes about slowly in our political system, and most of the 2008 Republican candidates have distanced themselves from Bush. 

Just because you, from outside the Republican party, have branded what is going on as zealotry, does not mean that the Republicans will clean house in the way you want.  That reminds me of the time another poster on this board went off on how Republicans had become so extreme and needed to shift towards the center.  That might be so, or it might not.  But it is a decision for Republicans to make amongst themselves.  I would never presume to tell liberals that they need to shift rightwards towards the center.

If Democrats want to be relevant, they will have to extend the hand of friendship.  And so will the Republicans.  When you are elected, you represent all of your constituents, not just the ones who are from your party.  To have bitter feelings now will only lead to comeuppance once the other guys are in power.  Perhaps we will see that happen when Bush is out of office (although my personal prediction as of right now is a McCain win).

But eventually, we're going to have to work together regardless of our various beliefs.
