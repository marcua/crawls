Re: 25 reported murdered at Virginia Tech
« Reply #14 on: April 17, 2007, 10:03:34 AM »
Lockdown, from what they were talking about in the news, would have entailed shutting down all classes, essentially getting to all the buildings that had classes and keeping those students in the rooms, keeping the doors closed (locked) so no one could enter until the situation was resolved, somehow getting information to all students to stay in their respective dorms and to not leave. Cancelling the rest of classes (they hadn't done this).

There was a lot of criticism about the campus security, but working at a university, I can see how this is incredibly challenging. I don't necessarily check in when I teach a class. Security would have had to identify all the classes that were in session across the campus and notify the classes of a lockdown. However, much of the security would probably have been focused in apprehending the first shooter. I think it is easy to criticize the way it was handled, but logistically, how could the information have been disseminated faster? There is no intercom, there are no departments necessarily congregating in the morning that could have then relayed the information to their students. Email must have been the most consistent methods.

Maybe what could have been done would be a call to every R.A. on campus to notify all the students in the building that they needed to stay in their dorms, but what about the commuter students? I"m sure there could have been a more effective way, but on the spot, this must have been challenging to troubleshoot.
