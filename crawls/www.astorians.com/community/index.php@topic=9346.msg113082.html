Quote from: essen on February 12, 2008, 08:39:50 PM
And why again is it the customer's responsibility to determine the wages of restaurant industry employees? If restaurants can't be bothered to pay their employees even minimum wage, why not have an established service charge or delivery charge, which would guarantee a specific amount to these people? I mean you end up paying for it in tips anyway, and I'd rather just have an amount spelled out on the bill and not be expected to pay any more. That way they'd get the money without the restaurant having to raise the prices of the food.

It's not your RESPONSIBILITY, it's you CHOICE.  That's the point of tipping and why, here in the US, we have a tipping system.  You don't HAVE to tip a thing if you don't want to.

The reason they don't remove tipping and put this in the cost of the food is because, consciously and subconsciously, MOST people look for a deal.

Imagine two similar restaurants, both that have food you enjoy and you wouldn't pick one over the other for taste reasons.  One charges a delivery fee of $3 and, because they pay their delivery/service folks minimum wage, they have higher food prices compared to the similar restaurant because they have to make up those costs somehow.

Now, you pull out a menu and, taste preference aside, which one are you (or someone new to the area) likely to choose?  The cheaper restaurant where you can make your own choice as to whether to tip or not; or the one that charges more for the same sandwich and a delivery fee?

Consciously or subconsciously, you're likely going to be picking the cheaper one.  Most of the time, you and most others are not going to factor the potential tip in the price or, if you're cheap, you'll find the delivery fee and the increased price of food to be too much; making it cheaper to just buy from the place that doesn't have the fees and just tip.

Free choice.  We don't live in a socialist society (yet, anyway).  While you might be berated by others and cursed by the server/delivery person for being cheap, it's your choice.  I don't think most here would be ready for the costs and product price increases required with removing the voluntary tipping system.

It's all your personal choice.  I'm just saying that you can't look at everything, especially when it comes to delivery, in a % point of view.  While %-wise, $2.50 might be an "accepted" good tip, would you be happy pedaling your bicycle 10 blocks (at least...consider round trip), walking up to a dark porch or into a big apartment building, hoping that you don't get robbed, and potentially having an angry customer that's going to complain to you that it took too long, even though the restaurant is extremely busy and it wasn't your fault it took them that long to cook the food, all for $2.50?  What if the next person isn't so understanding and leaves no tip?  All that work was for naught...

When I'm ordering, I am usually ordering just for myself and I'll give $2 (which is usually more than 15-20%) if I am paying by card.  If I'm paying by cash, I'll give $2 plus whatever the change is, to make it even.  While it might not seem worth traveling all the way to my apartment and back, that is the job they chose and if they don't like it, there are A LOT of other jobs out there.  They might not be desirable jobs, but I've heard garbage men get paid a ton of money.

I think one of my biggest problems is that I usually tip pretty highly compared to the "standard percentage" and I have consistantly received poopie service from the places in my neighborhood.  You can say "well don't order from there anymore" but that's easy to say when you aren't having the problems I've had with the places in my area.  I've had to wait 45 minutes for an order of DOMINOS CHEESY BREAD and that's from someone who is given a Dominos car to deliver in.  I had one delivery woman call me from the street and tell me to come outside because there were no parking spots and she was too lazy to park around the corner.  I was told later, by a friend, that she probably did that to see if she could get away with it.  When I wouldn't comply, she was up my stairs and at my door in under 5 minutes.

I just don't see why I should have to pay almost double for my meal because these people aren't paid properly by their employer.  ESPECIALLY chain places like Dominos (though the privately owned places haven't been much better).  And don't tell me they would raise the price of the food...most of these meals are obviously suffering from inflated prices already.  Somehow I can't see how a restaurant can justify charging over $8 for a cheeseburger while the place next to it can charge less for a larger portion.

Either way, I don't think I should be punished or called a cheapskate for the way I tip.  $2 might not seem like a lot if you have to bike a 20-block round trip with a plastic bag hanging from your handlebars, but it does seem like a lot when you're already paying more for your food than you really should be paying.  You can say "well don't order out" but that's just not happening and it's ridiculous.

People can argue all they way but if 15-20% is standard, that's what these people should be expecting.  I don't understand people who expect above average rewards for (typically, for me anyhow) sub-standard service.  I am not a cheapstake.  In fact, I often feel immensely guilty because I tipped more than I should and it is just that much more money that I can't afford to be spending.  I don't deserve to have someone spit in my food, or put their dick in it, or what have you.  No one does.  It's suck, vile, and, to be frank, it's ghetto.  If food service people want to be tipped properly, they should have some class.  It might not bring in significantly more money, but it will certainly guarantee that decent tippers like myself won't be taking out of their tip.

Also, I should mention that I had an incident the other day with a delivery guy where he brought me the wrong soda and I said "this is supposed to be x and it's y."  His response was "uhhh" and I said "well, can you just swing by later when you're back out on your rounds and bring me the right kind."  I didn't expect him to rush back with it, though I would think the restaurant (it was a chain) would send someone in a car to correct the error.  After all, I'm PAYING for this service.  The guy's response was "it's too far."  I said "but I ordered x and you are trying to give me y."  He goes "it's too far.  Here, take my tip instead.  The soda will be free."  I was aghast.  He would rather give up his tip to cover the cost of the soda than go back to the restaurant and say "this is the wrong soda for this apartment and he wants it now, someone should bring him the right kind."  I wasn't going to take his tip back because I don't believe in not tipping at all, and I don't think he should be paying for the soda...the restaurant should.  But I was livid and I ended up slamming the door in his face for being so unprofessional as to say "it's too far."  The saddest thing is that I probably would have tipped the guy again for taking the time to come back and give me the right soda.

It's a sh*tty system but it's not my responsibility to supplement these peoples' salaries.  I have an office job that I have to bust my hump for.  I stay late, come in early, take work home almost every night and on weekends.  And I get paid less than an executive assistant would.  It's the nature of my industry and I accept that, but if the company didn't provide so much free food at meetings and if part of my job wasn't to wine and dine potential clients, I would probably go hungry many days and nights.  So it bugs me to think that I should have to give up money I barely have, after working just as hard emotionally and mentally as they do physically, because their employer pays them less than what is livable.  My employer pays me less than what is livable and I don't expect my clients to supplement my income whenever we close a deal.  If you can't accept the nature of the beast, don't apply for an effing job there.  That's how I feel.
