To all:

In answer to the original post:
Can someone please explain to me why broker's exist?

I can sincerely respond that as a Realtor I am appreciated and respected by most of those that I have worked with.  And I am happy to exist as a Realtor as it has allowed me the time to care for my little one while paying the college tuition for the older one.  I am an honest, responsible and a hard working Realtor.  I'm not perfect, but for the time that I spend on each and every transaction, I am worth every penny of it. 

I take my work seriously, as finding or selling a property is a difficult task.  Especially in this market and with everyone running around with a busy schedule.  The process of securing a new home makes everyone quite anxious, and there are moments when everyone is frazzled, but with patience and understanding we/I make it through.  It is often a bumpy ride, especially when purchasing a co-op, as board approval is required.

For the Buyer:  I consider myself a personal shopper, in that many will tell me what they qualify for, and what they want and I keep an eye out for that particular property or those particular properties, which may work for them and their family

For the Seller:  I consider myself their hired gun in that they can leave the responsibility to me, and I will take care of the marketing, open houses, and scheduling of all showings.  And of course there is much more that is involved.  A million calls and a million emails at least.  LOL

This is the side that many do not know about:

In a co-op transaction I can easily spend 50-75 hours.  During this time I do not get paid.  From the time a buyer makes an offer until the time a buyer closes on a property, three to six months may go by.  And again I do not get paid.

These transactions entail housecleaning, photos, phones calls, emails, many showings, negotiations, open houses, board application review, mortgage assistance, attorney referrals and communications, walk-thrus, emails, communications with other Realtor's and the list goes on.  But, if something goes wrong with the financing or the board rejects the buyer there is no closing and I do not get paid.  And we're back to square one. 

So I can actually wait for three to six months, spend 50-75 hours on the transaction and then find out that the buyer has been unable to secure financing and/or has been board denied.  And that is the luck of the trade.

Now, if there is a closing, and a commission of 5% has been negotiated with the seller and the unit is 300K the commission will be $15K.  Of that $15K commission check a split will go to my broker, and a split will go towards taxes.  The remaining balance is my cut.

Now if there is a closing, and another Realtor brought the buyer, the commission will be 2.5% or $7.5K.  Of that $7.5K commission check, a split will go to my broker, and a split will go towards taxes.  The remaining balance is my cut.

And that is my life as a Realtor in a nutshell.  So I will continue to work as a Realtor with those that appreciate my time, honesty, and hard work.  I enjoy spending time with my children and I'm so very proud that I can pay the college tuition for my son. 

Know that many respect and appreciate Realtor's and I have established many great relationships along the way.
Chemistry is always key in real estate transactions, so if you don't find someone that you like and work well with, move on to someone else.  There are many people out there that are terrific at what they do.

There are three sides to every story.  I've shared mine!!
