Letter to Assembly Member Gianaris Re: Enhancing Sanctions
« on: August 31, 2008, 02:34:17 AM »
August 25, 2008

The Honorable Michael N. Gianaris
New York State Assembly Member
36th Assembly District
21-77 31st Street, Suite 107
Astoria, NY 11105

Dear Assembly Member Gianaris:

In response to the incident that occurred on August 17, 2008 that involves the death of NYPD traffic agent Donnette Sanz and her infant son, Michael, I believe it is time for State lawmakers to tighten the screws on motorists who have an extensive history of vehicular offenses and who continue to drive in our State.

The motorist who struck Ms. Sanz, Walter Walker, has an extensive history of driving offenses and arrests that date back to 1989, to wit:

• Walker has been driving with a suspended license since 1989 after having his license suspended numerous times for intentionally and recklessly ignoring traffic laws.

• Walker was arrested in 1999 after being caught at a police checkpoint driving with a suspended license.

• Walker was again arrested in 2005 for driving with a suspended license. He was convicted of this offense in 2006.

• In total, Walker had his license suspended at least 20 times, according to police.

When we consider the numbers of other drivers on the roads today with driving records similar to Walker's, this should force us to consider measures to enhance sanctions for those who habitually violate the law by continuing to drive with suspended New York State licenses.

I am confident that the New York State Assembly has the concerns of millions of motorists and pedestrians in mind, and I'm certain they are aware of their responsibility to provide for the safety of the citizens it serves.

Sincerely,

Francis J. Balducci
