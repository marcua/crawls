Quote from: fredo on December 22, 2007, 08:39:36 PM
The Euro-Market used to be a pool hall.

I remember when this pool hall opened, but what was here before the pool hall?   I can no longer remember.   For those who don't know where th Euro-Market is it is on the west side of 31st street just below 30th Avenue.

I wrote this before but the guy who opened the Euro-Market ran the Cyprus Deli on 30th Avenue between 34th & 35 Streets.   He closed the deli, though no other business moved in and a new deli opened next door.   I really missed the Cyprus Deli and lo and behold it has re-opened, though they no longer stock the great selection of beer the Euro-Market reportedly has and Cyprus Deli used to have.    Now there are two deli's next to each other, though the other one always smells like cat and that is big turn off for me.

Speaking of deli's, anyone patronize the deli on the south side of Broadway between 32nd & 33rd Streets?   A family used to run the place.   A father and his sons and one daughter ran it.   It had great character.   It was real cluttered and they had all sorts of exotic candies and delicacies from Greece.    When I lived on 32nd Street (until March '01) I would patronize that store.    It has had at least two sets of owners since the family sold out and has lost all its character, something that Astoria is losing with all the gentrification.

I noticed the Zodiac Cafe @ Newtown & 31st is closed "for renovations."   Kind of odd for a cafe to close in July -- prime outdoor seating season.   New Yorkers -- at least Astorians -- seem to love their al fresco dining so it seems an odd time to close.   

Across 31st Street from the Zodiac a tiny new restaurant opened in the space that the Himalaya Deli had occupied.   The Himalaya, under a new name, moved to the corner of the same block where the print shop was for years and the internet cafe was for about a minute.   I don't know the name of new restaurant, but it is some exotic ethnic restaurant.   It is tiny though.

Around the corner on 30th Avenue and I believe 30th Street there was a green grocer that had been there for years.   It was odd that it was run by Asians -- Koreans I assume -- but they had a Greek or some sort of European woman working there.   I don't know if she owned the place at one time or had worked there for previous owners, but the place finally closed and a restaurant named Table 30 is opening, but progress has stalled.  I don't know what the deal with the new restaurant is.

And on my block there is a new restaurant called The Montenegro Grill that is about to open where the defunct Bangladesh Cultural Center used to be.   It is also taken up the space on the corner of 34th Street & 28th Avenue that has seen a succession of restaurants/cafes come and go in the seven plus years I lived on the block.  Next door to the Montenegro Grill was a Chinese take-out place that closed, re-opened and then closed again.   It won't re-open because the Brazilian Restaurant next to that took over the space.

Apropos of nothing at all, this social note from the Brooklyn Standard Union in 1906:

[The Shuetzen Park was, I understand, located south of Broadway, between 41st and Steinway.]

12 December 1906
HER EYE BLACKENED SEPARATING LOVERS

Despite the fact that Miss Blanche DOWNEY, 19 years old, of 60 Lawrence
street, Astoria, has a black eye, her wedding, which is set for Christmas
Eve, will not be postponed. This announcement was made in Magistrate
CONNORTON'S court, Long Island City, where Miss DOWNEY, accompanied by her
affanced husband, Andrew HOPPE, 21 years old, of 320 Hamilton street, and a
bevy of girl friends, appeared this morning to prosecute her assailant and
former admirer, a young Frenchman named Charles RABOUD, of 65 Bodine Street.
RABOUD and HOPPE had a set-to over the girl at a ball in Schuetzen Park last
Saturday night, capitulated and started home with Miss DOWNEY. On the way
home the quarrel was renewed, and when Miss DOWNEY interfered she received a
black eye. RABOUD was held to-day in $500 for examination next Monday.

[Lawrence St is now 28th Ave.   Bodine is 43rd Road in Hunters Point.
