Words @ Waltz poetr open mic + Sharon Olinka
« on: January 14, 2007, 07:24:31 PM »
Hi all.
Best wishes to everyone for a new year filled with kindness, happiness and health.  After a break for Christmas, the Words at Waltz reading series is starting 2007 with a featured reading by Sharon Olinka, an accomplished poet and Astorian.  I first met Sharon in 2005 at a reading at Cornelia Street Cafe where she wowed the room with her evocative imagery and mesmerizing language.  So come on out, bring a poem, short story or any literary meanderings to share, and take part in a celebration of literature in Astoria.
 
Here's the basics, and read on for Sharon's bio and a sample poem
 
Words at Waltz reading series
Monday, January 29
8pm Sign up, 8:30 Start
Waltz-Astoria Cafe
23-14 ditmars boulevard
$5 minimum purchase

Take the N or the W to Ditmars, turn west/left onto Ditmars, and Waltz's is about 7 blocks down on your left.
www.waltz-astoria.com
 
Sharon Olinka aims to create greater understanding between different cultures through international publishing projects.  THE GOOD CITY, her second book of poems, was published by Marsh Hawk Press in 2006, and her poetry was included in the anthology Bum Rush the Page: A Def Poetry Jam, from Random House.  Her work An Eye For An Eye Makes the Whole World Blind  won the PEN Oakland Josephine Miles Award, and in 2005 she won a Barbara Deming Memorial Award grant.  Her first book of poems, A FACE NOT MY OWN, was published by West End Press in 1995.  A literary critic who has written for American Book Review, Sharon worked for WDSU-TV in New Orleans in Programming and wrote features for The Baton Rouge Morning Advocate.  She came to NYC in 1990.
 
Rembetica Song
 
 
             Unmusical
chainsaws screeching from
imploded buildings.
                      Then shattered glass.
                       My little ship in a bottle.
With no country.  Alone
in water.  Cries
of the dying.  My breasts
in ceremonial black velvet.
Towers of language.
Hotel that crumbled
into flames.  Secret kitchen
of the dead.   
Water of smoky ash.
Bar where I’d
sing the blues over some man
tied to an extinct,
juiceless vulva.
 
I will sip wine in a rebuilt city.
Someone will say I’m sexy.
I will learn to speak Turkish.
I will pretend love matters.
 
Copyright 2006, The Good City, Marsh Hawk Press
