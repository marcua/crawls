It's worth pointing out that buying lottery tickets for a payout this big is actually a good investment for a change! The odds of getting the right number are 1: 175,711,536. If the jackpot was only $20 million, then your expected return on your $1 investment would be about 20/175, or about 11 cents. Bad investment. But now, with a $355 million jackpot, your expected return is about 355/175, or about $2 on your $1 investment! If you had a million dollars you didn't mind losing, you could do a lot worse than buying a million lottery tickets and having a 1/20 chance of a 3550% ROI.

(These calculations do not take into account the amortization assumptions used to get the $355 million figure, nor do they take into account the huge tax hit you take on any lottery winnings... I suspect that in reality, your expect return is closer to $1/ticket than $2/ticket. But it's lunch time and I'm not going to crunch any more numbers now!)
