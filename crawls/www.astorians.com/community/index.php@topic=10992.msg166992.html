Some parents are good and immediately take their crying child outside to calm them down. In those situations I feel bad for the parent (particularly if it's a movie). And I appreciate that they're proactive the moment the child starts acting out and potentially (more than likely) ruining the movie or dining experience of the other patrons. Also, I understand that sitters are expensive and/or hard to find. With a sitter a $25 movie for a couple suddendly costs $60+ for two new parents.

Until some of the people in the back starting yelling at the parents and then the PARENTS started throwing a fit about their right to be there and yelling back and making threats to the people in the back until even more people (just about everyone in the movie theater) chimed in and FINALLY the father ended up taking the baby out of the movie theater to calm it down (or more likely change its diaper since they came back 15 minutes later and the baby didn't cry through the rest of the movie).
