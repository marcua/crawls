« on: February 04, 2016, 05:25:11 PM »
For those willing to share, I'm curious what others pay for rent in Astoria and what they are getting for it so that I (and any others reading) can get a sense of true market rates.  As we all know, what brokers tell us is  'the norm' for rents is often inflated, since it's in their interest to get us to pay higher rents.  Higher rents = higher commission).

As they say, Knowledge is Power.  I've listed below what I think are key points to take into account, followed by my own info:

+++++++++++++++++++++++++

Monthly rent:  $1750

Type of building (private home owned/managed by an individual person/LL? .....small apt building run by a management company?....city-owned building with a Super who lives on-site?....doorman/luxury apt bldg.?)   SMALL APT BLDG WITH 8 UNITS OWNED BY A PRIVATE LL

Approx age of building/house?  SEEMS TO BE MAYBE 50 YEARS OLD AND WAS GUTTED/REDONE MAYBE 15 YEARS AGO?

Approx how old is kitchen/bath?  KITCHEN MAYBE 15 YEARS OLD...BATH MAYBE 30 YEARS OLD?

How many rooms in the apt? (bedrooms?  living area?  dining area?  bathrooms?)  1 BEDROOM, 1 LIVING AREA, SMALL KITCHEN, 1 BATHROOM

Overall condition of apartment/building (old feeling/cracks here and there.....pretty nice....very modern/luxury?)  PRETTY NICE

Private outdoor space?  PRIVATE BALCONY

Extra storage space?  NO EXTRA STORAGE.  ONE CLOSET ONLY, IN THE BEDROOM, BUT IT IS A LARGE WARDROBE LENGTH CLOSET.

Dishwasher?   Microwave? YES TO BOTH

Laundry in apartment?  elsewhere in building?  No laundry in the building?  NO LAUNDRY IN THE BLDG

Pets allowed?  YES CATS ONLY.

Heat included?  NOT INCLUDED

How long of a walk to nearest subway station?  12 MINUTES WALKING AT FAST PACE

How many train stations from your Astoria station to first station in Manh?  EIGHT

Market rate, rent-stabilized or rent-control? RENT-STABIL.  BLDG IS 421a

How long have you lived in the apartment?  TWO YEARS

If you have lived in Astoria less than three years, what is your typical approach for finding an apartment in NYC?  ...do you always go straight to a broker?...do you try a do-it-yourself search using word-of-mouth, friends, building Supers, etc?  Or is your current apartment your very first rental apartment in NYC? (i.e., how much experience do you have navigating the NYC rental market?)  I EMPLOY DO IT-YOURSELF METHOD AND ALSO USE BROKERS, AND START SEARCHING 3 MONTHS IN ADVANCE SO I CAN GET SENSE OF THE MOST CURRENT TYPICAL RENTS, AND WHAT I CAN EXPECT TO FIND....

If you have lived in Astoria more than three years, how often does your rent typically increase?  With every renewal lease, or only every few years when LL feels the need?  Have you noticed any changes/new trends in the rent increase pattern of your building,  and as Astoria has become more popular?   N/A

Monthly rent:  $1665

Approx age of building/house?Built in 1920s, so nearing 100.

Approx how old is kitchen/bath?  10-15 years old.

How many rooms in the apt? (bedrooms?  living area?  dining area?  bathrooms?)  1 bedroom, 1 bathroom, living room/kitchenette in same area.

Overall condition of apartment/building (old feeling/cracks here and there.....pretty nice....very modern/luxury?)  Old but nice, although plumbing issues are a constant concern (poor flow, backed up water coming up from drain, especially on rainy days).  Bed bugs and other critters seem to be a constant problem, according to an exterminator I know.  Cracks from around ceiling where pipes run vertical through some corners of apartment.

Private outdoor space?  No

Extra storage space?  A set of cabinets by front door which seem recently installed holds majority of clothes.  Without it, only closet in bedroom would have storage.

Dishwasher?   Microwave? No.  Microwave provided by me.

Laundry in apartment?  elsewhere in building?  No laundry in the building?  Laundry in building, but closes early (8pm) due to unsecured entrance.

Pets allowed? Cats and dogs.  Dogs are recent (probably to persuade people to pay such a high price).

Heat included?  Yes

How long of a walk to nearest subway station?  5 minutes to either 30th or Broadway

How many train stations from your Astoria station to first station in Manh?  4-5, depending

Market rate, rent-stabilized or rent-control? Market rate.  Rent originally was $1150.  One year it jumped to $200 more per month

How long have you lived in the apartment?  Six years

If you have lived in Astoria less than three years, what is your typical approach for finding an apartment in NYC?  ...do you always go straight to a broker?...do you try a do-it-yourself search using word-of-mouth, friends, building Supers, etc?  Or is your current apartment your very first rental apartment in NYC? (i.e., how much experience do you have navigating the NYC rental market?) N/A

If you have lived in Astoria more than three years, how often does your rent typically increase?  With every renewal lease, or only every few years when LL feels the need?  Have you noticed any changes/new trends in the rent increase pattern of your building,  and as Astoria has become more popular?   Rent increases every renewal.  At first it was small (~$50 or not at all), but when Urban American took over, rent skyrocketed and it's been going up steady since.  Doubt I'll stay longer; aging apartment just isn't worth the price.  I see that when someone moves out, they are replacing all kitchen cabinets and fridge, plus a microwave.  I imagine this is all to match the price they're demanding, which is astronomical.  I'm sure my rent will be $1,765 come August.  Rent increases used to negotiable, but there is a veiled "threat" in each notice of increase, saying the area is in high demand and that others will be more than happy to pay.

Yeah...that of course is a common ploy and mindset of many LL's...they now have $$ signs in their heads as they see more and more young'uns moving in.  I guess I'm lucky and 'hopeful' on one hand that...because my LL lives with his family in the bldg., that his desire to retain a good tenant like me will trump his desire to try and flip the apartment by giving me a big enough rent increase that I'll consider leaving, and then he can start all over with a new (higher rate) 'vacancy lease' for the next tenant.  At some point, if the rent just keeps going up, I may consider just being straight with him and saying that I don't 'need' to live in an area that's 'popular'...and that while I know Astoria is becoming that way, it doesn't matter one way or the other to me...that I like the apt as a whole, and the bldg. as a whole, but that I don't 'need' to be in a hot neighborhood like Astoria.  (...which is true...all I need is a few decent amenities...but not necessarily as many as we now find ourselves with here in Astoria...not if it means I'm dishing out more for rent than I care to... ;-)
