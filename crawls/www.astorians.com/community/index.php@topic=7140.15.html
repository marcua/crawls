Re: Explosion on 42nd St.
« Reply #25 on: July 18, 2007, 09:22:28 PM »
I work in a building right in the area. It was 5:56 I had just picked up my briefcase to leave my office and the lights flickered twice. Then I heard a loud noise like a thundercrack, and then I heard rumbling and I went to look out the window on Lexington Ave, and as I approached the windows the noise was getting louder, it sounded sort of like an airplane engine which gave me the creeps. I looked outside and down in the street people were running up Lexington, some of them in a panic. I knew something bad was transpiring, and I told a cleaning woman who was right there that we should check with the security guy at the entrance to find out if he knew what happening. Just then the building security announced over the PA system that everybody in the building should evacuate right away, using the stairwells. So I yelled out for everybody to use the stairs, and with alot of others, we ran down 10 flights of stairs. Once I got out the building, I looked down southbound on Lex and saw just south of 42nd Street huge billows of gray and black some, some rising at least 15 floors or more, and the ground was trembling. I did not know what to make of it, it seems to be coming either from undergroudn or the adjacent building. So I walked fast north and didn't slow down until I hit 47st Street. Alot of people assumed a terrorist bomb attack, which was my first gut instinct since I never seen anything like this since 9/11. I was then thought it may be an underground electrical explosion of some sort. I was somewhat relieved to hear later it was most likely a steampipe explosion. I ended up walking up to 60th Street, and took the W home. The Lexington Ave lines are currently out between 86th and Brooklyn Bridge stations. The streets are currently roped off between 40th - 43rd Street, and 3rd Avenue and Vanderbilt Ave. I had worked on Wall Street during 9/11 so like alot of people who experienced that day up close, it's easy to get the jitters and jump to conclusions.
