Good morning,

Service on the QM22 Express Bus has been suspended because NYCDOT issued a restraining order against TransportAzumah from operating the X90 and QM22 Express Buses. NYCDOT shut them down for no reason whatsoever, this move is sadistic on their side because TransportAzumah was a legal annd licensed carrier and used contract buses and drivers from Skyliner and Campus Coach. Furthermore the TransportAzumah services are Apple Core routes which means one would have to join a private transportation club (although there is NO personal information required or requested) therefore subjecting the X90 and QM22 routes to NYSDOT regulations and NOT NYCDOT regulations. If the MTA screws around with YOUR money and mismanages YOUR money, you should have the right to use other transportation options that are available to you because you don't want you money mismanaged and screwed around with. Also riders are upset at the MTA and must have a way of taking out their anger of the MTA and the wallet is a perfect way to do this. Please sign the petition so NYCDOT could operate the routes or TransportAzumah could reassume operations of the route because the United States is a free country and in a FREE MARKET economy YOU have a right to whatever choices are available to you. NYCDOT is screwing around and wasting YOUR taxpayer dollars on stupid lawsuits like this for no apparent reason. There is no law broken and NYCDOT bus routes were transferred to the MTA Bus Company in 2005, therefore there is no reason for NYCDOT to be screwing around and wasting YOUR taxpayer dollars if you decided to use other transit options that don't mismanage YOUR money the way the MTA does. Please sign the petition and fax it to 1-347-673-0094.

Hey QM22, I just got this email from Joel Azumah as I was one of the petition signers for the QM22 Service. What is going on with this email. Is there anything I could do to help.

TransportAzumah has retained legal counsel to oppose The City of New York’s interference with our bus services. The temporary restraining order obtained by the City is in effect until at least July 15. On July 15, there will be a hearing held on this matter. No service will operate on the X90 or QM22 before July 16.
 
A number of requests have been received for refunds. We are going to wait until after the initial hearing to determine how refunds are going to be handled, as we will have more information by then on how to proceed. This matter is now a part of the litigation and the events are still unfolding.
 
TransportAzumah’s main concern is that the assorted actions by the City on these routes are intended to completely destroy the passenger base on these services. The goal is to chase all of you away before we get the ability to resume service. The easiest way for them to achieve this was to obtain a restraining order and ensure that you had no replacement service whatsoever. To a commuter, uncertainty is unacceptable and they have done their best to create uncertainty and disruption and their goal is to do it long enough so that you find and stick to another way of going to work. We understand and regret the frustration that many of you are going through and intend to use the legal process to deal with those responsible.
 
If you are interested, you can provide a signed and notarized affidavit for the judge, Honorable Karen Smith. This is different from the petition. A suggested text is below:
 
Affidavit of passenger(s)
 
1. Name is [name], residing at [address].
2. I work at [company name] located at [address].
3. For [period of time] I was a passenger on the bus line operated by the MTA Bus Company (“MTAB”) between Jackson Heights & Astoria in Queens and midtown Manhattan . I used the bus line in order to travel efficiently between my home and workplace.
4. For several reasons OA decided to discontinue the bus line that I had been using, the last day of operation being June 25, 2010. Its discontinuance represented a hardship since no other transportation would be able to carry me as efficiently between my home and workplace.
5. As the date of discontinuance approached my fellow bus riders and I became aware of a commuter club that was being formed. The name of the club is the Apple Core Commuter Club (“Apple”).
6. As advertised to me, the Apple club would be exclusive, and only its members and guests would be allowed aboard the private coaches traveling between the upper east side of Manhattan and lower Manhattan . The club’s organizer, Transportazumah LLC (“TA”), made the arrangements to charter buses for the club’s use.
7. I have been a member of Apple since June 28, 2010, and have been regularly traveling aboard the club’s private coach.
8. I pay a fee to TA for using the Apple’s private coach. The fee is paid [using TA’s website and/or when traveling aboard the coach].
 
If other passengers are interested, they can also submit them. They can fax them to us at 347-673-0094. They should be signed and notarized.

I spoke to one of the QM22 riders who happens to be a friend of my wife. She said that the order was issued because NYCDOT believed that TransportAzumah violated the law by picking up people off the street which is illegal. However, TransportAzumah's services are rendered through the Apple Core Transportation Club. Under NYSDOT guidelines a private transportation club can pick people up off the street. Technically, the X90 and QM22 are operated by a private transportation club. It is legal to start an intra city bus service as long as it is a private transportation club, which is subject to NYSDOT regulations and not NYCDOT regulations. So there is no reason for NYCDOT to go after Azumah if he is subject to NYSDOT regulations. You are also allowed to charter a bus for your "club."
