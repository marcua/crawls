A Google search came up with this - http://www.archive.org/stream/dininginnewyork002707mbp/dininginnewyork002707mbp_djvu.txt, which is an online text version of "Dining In New York" by Rian James, published in 1930 (I think).  Anyway, this lists Ye Pig 'n' Whistle at 175 West 4th Street.  I don't think the restaurant is there anymore.  You can check out the address on the Songlines website - http://www.nysonglines.com/4st.htm.

WOW!  Thanks for the research.  Using that address, I checked my Streets & Trips map.  Assuming the addresses are still the same, it was at the corner of West Fourth and Jones, two blocks west of the southwest corner of Washington Square Park. 

I'm reading a 1930 printing of a 1924 book, NICHOLAS A Manhattan Christmas Story, by Anne Carroll Moore.  In the book The Pig and Whistle is described as "a Christmasy, Dickensy looking place", where the characters in the book had a "very jolly dinner, half of the party sitting on a long bench against the wall - the other half sitting on another long bench at the opposite side of the table".  It's just a brief mention, but the name of the place sounded familiar from my years in the Village.  The whole book is a must-read for anyone who loves the history of New York and would like to virtually-time travel and read about familiar New York as it looked in the past.
