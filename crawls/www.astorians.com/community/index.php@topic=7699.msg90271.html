Re: Politicians getting caught in the bathroom
« Reply #37 on: August 29, 2007, 05:50:12 PM »
This whole situation has seemed really bizarre to me, ever since I first heard about it. I mean...okay, well, I can't claim to know all about mens' restroom habits, but his behavior seems kind of bizarre. I wouldn't think of those actions as cruising for sex, necessarily, but I am not a gay male. Then, when he's confronted, he CONFESSES--pleads guilty, pays a fine, says both that he did something he knew or should have known was engaging in lewd behavior (I forget the wording, but "knew or should have known" was definitely in there) AND that he will not later plead innocent of the charge, which is exactly what he's doing now. I'm sorry--I don't know a single straight male in my life who would a) likely know that those actions were a code for cruising, b) if they did know, would actually MAKE any of those actions, c) if they didn't know and accidentally made those actions, ADMIT the actions and pay a fine, especially if they were in a public office. They would be protesting their wide stance or whatever and would almost go overboard in exclaiming their hetero-ness.

So, I must assume that the senator is, in fact, a closeted, self-hating gay male (self-hating determined by the legislation he's pushing so hard for).

However, what I find really abhorrent is the public's reaction to it. In general, the last several years, I've noticed that when elected officials do things that are either against the law or the public find abhorrent in general, people do a lot of talking but no one demands anything be done. What's wrong with the public that we can't stand up and say, "Hey, what you did as an elected official was wrong. You were speeding/doing drugs/laundering money/employing illegal immigrants/cruising for sex/cheating on your wife* in defiance of what you say you stand for. I do not want you representing me any more, you're fired!"  I don't believe that it's because the split is 50/50 or that most people don't mind that the politician was doing something wrong, but because we see corruption in government as so ubiquitous we feel helpless to do anything about it. I mean, seriously--if the public called for it, COULD we even fire our own representatives? (Aside from impeachment for the president, I'm not sure I've ever heard of it. Only that elected officials sometimes resign for various reasons, most of which boil down to "people don't like me anymore").

*I should note that my politics are not showing here, as there are a number of these instances and others that I don't care much about and/or don't think should be illegal in the first place. However, an elected official should be held to a higher legal standard than the average Joe, even (or especially) regarding common laws like speeding or basic societal courtesies like cheating on your spouse. And when politicians fight for particular regulations, I think they should be held to an even higher standard. If we don't like the laws and don't want to hold either average citizens or elected officials to them, get them off the books!

I hate that we have placed celebrities in higher positions of esteem than those we elect to govern us.
