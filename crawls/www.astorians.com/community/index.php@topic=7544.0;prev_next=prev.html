Bravo is C-Town South!
« on: March 04, 2008, 08:08:12 PM »
I was in the C-Town on 28th Avenue this past Sunday, shopping for party treats, and I was thunderstruck at how much it resembles Bravo on 34th Avenue!  I've never seen any two supermarkets that were so nearly identical--if you're in one you might think for a moment you're in the other.

Same layout, except for the seafood section which I never found.  A two-aisled produce section to the left as you go in.  On the right side, an island for frozen pizza and another for cheese and bread, between the freezer on the left side and the dairy case on the right side.  In the right front corner, a bakery and deli section with a black-and-white striped awning and big barrels of nuts and similar things.  Across the aisle from that to the left, coffee beans.  In front of the aisles, displays of European exotica.  Seldom-seen Goya beans.  And NO frozen blintzes or latkes.

Best Yet is Best in Nabe
« Reply #11 on: March 06, 2008, 03:31:07 PM »
For purely local places, the best by far is Best Yet on 20th Ave & 37th STreet.  The only caveat is that you need a car to get there.  However, it is a true supermarket.  Clean and well stocked, with a very good staff.

Other than that, I like the Super Stop and Shop on Northern Blvd.  The prices are better, by far, than any local places, but the produce section, while huge, can be touch and go.  Great fruit, mediocre at best fresh herbs.  I usually hit our local veggie stand (at the Ditmars Blvd N/W) instead.  Using their frequent buyers card, and double coupons, we make out like a bandit some weeks.
