Title: VEGESTORIAN RECIPES!
Post by: brownbird on October 12, 2004, 12:23:26 AM
Since fall is upon us, I can think of no better way to celebrate the occasion than with a convenient, fast, good, budget-conscious (read: cheap!) soup to warm up to after a day spent in the throngs of wind chill and the like.

This recipe requires little if any effort and can usually be made within 10-15 minutes (including prep time) and is absolutely delicious! We have enjoyed this many of nights. It also doubles as a great sauce for steamed vegetables and brown rice.

15 MINUTE BLACK BEAN SOUP  

2 (15 ounce) cans black beans, drained and rinsed (or fresh if you prefer)
1 1/2 cups vegetable broth
1 cup of your favorite chunky salsa
1 teaspoon ground cumin
Lemon to taste
Garnish of some sort if you like (cilantro/onion/soy sour cream/soy cheese)

In a food processor or blender, combine beans, broth, salsa and cumin. Blend until fairly smooth. (Or pulse for a thicker consistency)

Heat mixture through out, Ladle into bowls, immediately squeeze desired amount of lemon and garnish as you please.

TOFU HOUSE CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES

Servings: Makes 6 dozen cookies

 

From Akasha's Richmond's The Art of Tofu, (c) 2001 Morinaga Publications

 



 

Ingredients
1 cup unsalted margarine
1 cup unrefined cane sugar
2 Tbs light molasses
4 Tbs Mori-Nu Silken Lite Tofu
(firm), puréed
1 tsp vanilla extract
1 3/4 cups unbleached flour
1/4 cup whole wheat flour
1/2 tsp baking soda
1/4 tsp salt
12 oz. (2 cups) chocolate chips


 

Method
1) Position 2 racks in center of oven and preheat to 350°F. 2) Cream margarine, sugar and molasses with electric beaters or in heavy-duty mixer until light and fluffy. Add tofu purée and vanilla; beat for 1 more minute. 3) Mix flours, soda and salt in small bowl. Add to creamed mixture and mix at low speed with the electric mixer or lightly with a rubber spatula, blending in all flour. Fold in chocolate chips. 4) Drop by rounded teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes. Cool on wire rack.

 

Nutritional Notes
Nutrition Facts are based on 1 cookie. Provides 0.33g of fiber per cookie.

Fat: 4g
Carbohydrates: 7.6g

Sodium: 18mg
Cholesterol:: 0mg
Protein: 0.6g

Pumpkin Muffins
(as found on veggieconnection.com)

Ingredients:

1/4 non-hydrogenated vegan margarine
1 cup vegan brown sugar (or white sugar plus 1 tablespoon molasses)
1/2 cup applesauce
2 cups pureed pumpkin or squash
1 teaspoon cinnamon and nutmeg
2 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
2 teaspoon baking soda
Directions:

Preheat oven to 350 and grease muffin tin. (This recipe makes 12 muffins.) Cream together margarine and vegan sugar. Add applesauce, pumpkin, and spice. Mix well and add flour and soda. Fill muffin tins and bake for about 20-25 minutes. The muffins don't rise much, so don't be shy about piling the batter in.

These muffins are great warm, but keep for days. Most people don't believe me when I tell them these muffins are vegan, because they cook up so light and fluffy. Also, people are shocked to find they are made with 100% whole wheat flour.
Title: Date Nut Torte
Post by: wasabisam on April 03, 2005, 10:46:57 PM
Here is the recipe for the raw torte I brought for Vegestorians tonight. It is very easy and very quick. The dates probably should be soaked for about 3 - 6 hours first, though.

This recipe is from Alissa Cohen's website (http://www.alissacohen.com).

Date Nut Torte

Fudgy, creamy and sweet!
I bring this with me when I’m visiting someone I’d like to introduce to raw food. People can’t believe it’s raw! And it’s one of the quickest and easiest desserts to make.

Base of Tort:

    * 2 cups raisins
    * 2 cups walnuts

Frosting:

    * 1 cup dates, pitted and soaked
    * 1/2 lemon, juiced

For Base:
1. In a food processor, combine raisins and walnuts and blend until well blended and moist. (This will take a few minutes and you may see it forming a ball. Just make sure the raisins come out looking like a fudgey mixture and are not still grainy)
2. Remove from processor and mold onto a plate in a round circle about 1 1/2 inches thick.

For Frosting:
1. In a food processor, combine dates and lemon juice until smooth and creamy.
2. Spread the frosting on top of the torte

Note: I like this served at room temperature as the frosting and torte are still sticky, but if you want a firmer texture that will be easier to slice, refrigerate it for a few hours.
