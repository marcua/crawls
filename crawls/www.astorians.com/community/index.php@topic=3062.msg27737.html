[DFNYC] Tonight - AG candidate forum, Aug 21

(1) Tonight! (Mon 8/21) Attorney General Candidate Forum with DFNYC, NDM & Young Dems

Join us tonight, Monday, August 21, along with NYS Young Democrats and New Democratic Majority for a forum with the candidates running for Attorney General in the Sept. 12 Democratic Primary.

When: Tonight, August 21, 7-9pm

Where: 420 Bar & Lounge, 420 Amsterdam Avenue @ 80th Street.

All four Democratic candidates on the ballot for Attorney General have been invited to attend the forum. As of this email, the following candidates, listed in alphabetical order, have confirmed:
Mark Green
Charlie King
Sean Patrick Maloney
Please join us for an exciting evening! The candidates will give speeches, take questions, and mingle with attendees.

We will begin our endorsement vote for Attorney General at the forum.  The candidates have made statements to DFNYC members, which you can read at this page of our website:

http://www.dfnyc.org/cms/node/87433


http://www.DemocracyforNYC.org - local DFA group
http://www.DemocracyforNewYork.org - state coalition of DFA groups

I wonder when or if an endorsement would be announced.
While I really do not like that bar location, it was a great opportunity to hear up close from many of the candidates. (Would strongly encourage likely voters to take advantage of similar future events.)
No participation from the cuomo campaign.

It was not a debate. And I wonder if the order of presentations was by chance:
Maloney, Green, King

I thought I would favor one but my personal view was that all had merits and a preferred endorsement would not be a necessity.
Candidates were asked to speak briefly. There was 25 minutes of q&A each for 6+++ questions. A brief closing encouraging an endorsement.

I could go into some details but my reporting is extremely subjective. Issues included, health/insurance/fraud ; housing; education; political reforms; voting systems; the death penalty; fundraising concerns; national and foreign concerns/ constitutional issues

On a basic number scale 1-9, my views
King 6, Maloney 7, Green 7, (cuomo 3)

Green is the experienced leader of yesterday for the problems of today.
Maloney is the leader of tomorrow for the problems of today.
(see his site concerns regarding myspace.com and on con ed/PSC)

While King made a lesser impression, I greatly appreciated his pragmatic interests and tieing responses to real-life individual stories. (Thankfully candid)

Some are concerned that the next officer would only make minor changes for NYS.
I came away thinking that I would remember the next AG more if it were King or Maloney, and that Maloney would likely do a much better job.

If either of those get the endorsement or nomination, they would have my interested support. (surprisingly)

***
None sold me on sincere concern of property takings under eminent domain.

Green made an impression on political reforms; campaign financing
King on education and diversity issues (including women's business opportunities)
Maloney on domestic violence, transparency in govt.

I was a Green for years in CA, and while I was living on Long Island.  I switched to Democrat when I moved to Astoria a year ago.  The Greens feel much more disorganized and spacey out here than they did in CA, but I still like the idea of supporting some of the mission within the Green Party, especially environmental issues.   There are certain aspects of the Democratic Party that I like, but find them overall to be frustrating.  I may continue to stay registered D but study the independent candidates.

These days, I find myself open considering candidates from various independent parties, including the Greens and Libertarian.  I am intrigued by the Liberal Party.  Working Families doesn't appeal to me, because I don't see them as being particularly effective, and they remind me of the various Socialist parties that really do not have an appealing message for me:  the far, radical left (which I have had experience with) is just as narrow minded as the far, radical right, as I see it. I am repulsed by the Constitution Party and hope they have no candidates on the ballot.
