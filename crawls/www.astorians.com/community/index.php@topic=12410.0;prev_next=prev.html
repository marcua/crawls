Hi,

I'm just stumbling on this thread, so I'll add my .02 (whatever happened to the "cents" symbol?) I've been a member of both Astoria CSA and Hellgate (current member of Hellgate) so I'd like to offer my perspective. The shares at Astoria CSA cost more, so yes, you do get more because you're paying more. I don't know that the overall value is all that different. In both CSAs the amounts in the early weeks are considerably lighter than they will be for most of the season.

Personally, I prefer the inclusion of herbs in the regular vegetable share, I'd rather have the herbs than a second head of lettuce, but this is my preference. Different farms grow different selections of vegetables (with considerable overlap) and to some extent, in a neighborhood as rich in CSAs as this one, you can try to choose a CSA based on how well what they grow fits into your diet.

Another issue the farmers deal with is the balance between variety and quantity, so that in some CSAs they will offer a greater variety of produce in smaller amounts and in others there will be fewer items in greater quantities. Again, this is something that's worth looking into in terms of your eating habits and the offerings of each CSA. While the vegetable shares are less expensive and smaller at Hellgate, the fruit shares are more expensive and include both more quantity and variety than the fruit shares at Astoria CSA.

And, just a note about sunchokes, they can be either an early spring or late summer/fall vegetable.

I've been a member of the Hellgate CSA since its first year at Freeze Peach (I'm old. Is that four years or five?); what I seem to remember is that way in the way back, when Hellgate CSA was the only CSA in town and was, I think, held at the Arrow community center (?), it was started by people who had a particular interest in making the share affordable for low income people. They specifically worked with a farmer who was willing to do smaller and therefore cheaper shares: most CSAs give you a share big enough for a family of four; Hellgate CSA suggests it's good for 1-2 people. (Believe me, my friend in Brooklyn has a huge share of which I am deeply envious.)

This is the first year they've done dried beans, and I was a little surprised by them too-- but my toddler was helpful with shucking them and I figure the dried pods will do great in the compost bin. (obligatory shout out to the Two Coves Compost Team)

I think if you have a concern, make a note of it-- you might want to wait and see if you still are concerned at the end of the season, when they send out a survey to all the members.

The CSA that was at ARROW was the now defunct Queens CSA.  The Hellgate CSA was originally called the Ravenswood CSA and pickup was at the senior center in the Ravenswood Houses.  It moved to Ditmars when a group of us decided we wanted to take it over (that is the usual process - the first year it's run by an AmeriCorps Vista and JustFood, the second year it's passed on to volunteers from the CSA).  We all lived in the Ditmars area so that was a natural fit.  It was great at FreezePeach, and I miss FreezePeach itself, too, but having it at FreshStart has its benefits.

Actually, I remember getting those dried black beans last year, too.  I sat on my futon couch and shucked them while watching something on cable.  Thank goodness both the couch and cable are gone, but I'm happy for the black beans again.

Hellgate CSA is currently in its 6th year. I've been with the CSA since before it was a CSA - I attended some of the earliest exploratory meetings run by Cara Fraver and JustFood, and was an early member after signup commenced.  I'm so glad the CSA started and has been around for as long as it has been.  I just love it!

I personally like the smaller share because it's more manageable for me and my boyfriend.  I always feel badly that mid-season or so, I can't keep up with all the veggies and some stuff goes bad.  :oops:  There, I admitted it!  I always have plans at the start of the season to eat everything I get, and now that I'm doing meal planning each week, perhaps I'll be better this year!   :lol:
