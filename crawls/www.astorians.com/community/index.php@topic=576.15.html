Well, I'm definitely no lover of "spin", but I have to disagree about the purpose of a memorial. To me a memorial is something that shows respect for the dead and by which we can remember them. Like a tombstone.
And I guess it does soften the blow for the living.

For some reason I am reminded of the Lennon memorial in Central Park. When I see that memorial I am reminded of that man's life, feel sadness that he's gone, feel camraderie with the others that are there still remembering, feel hope when I see the next generation looking in puzzlement at the flowers.

And then you look around the space of the memorial.  It's not ostentatious, it's not unapproachable and it doesn't dominate Central Park. People come, people linger then people move on, maybe a little more reflective than usual.

Both sides have used 9/11 as a launching point for their agenda. And the result is that the families are again having a deep pain brought to the surface and shoved in their face. Witness the many movies that are based on true crime, and imagine what the families of the victims of those crimes must be thinking seeing the murder of a loved one sensationalized and distorted in the name of entertainment & profit, under the guise of a "factual" portrayal.

I am not saying that we should stop talking about 9/11.  Just as we should never stop talking about each and every atrocity through history at the risk of appearing insensitive. We have to examine these things thoroughly, hold those responsible accountable, and try to prevent these things from happening in the future. Iow, learn from our mistakes. A memorial can serve to remind us of the past when otherwise we may forget.

I do not think the site should become a giant graveyard. By nothing being there, the city is loosing money. By stamping out free speech we are capitulating to terror (whether it's coming from external or internal sources).
I do think that we (all of us) have a right to determine what (if anything goes there) however. This is still our city after all.

just my 2 cents,
----zeno.

While I'm not throwing my opinion on the side of "build a memorial", I will say this -- markers (gravestones, memorials, plastic crosses at the side of the road) are intended for the living and not the dead.  Thought we might not all want/need something concrete to remember them, it can be a part of the grieving process.  Things become sticky, however, when the death becomes sacred -- the marker, not a token for the living but a shrine to the martyrdom of the death it self.

There has to be a way to mark a tragedy, to put it in it's proper place for the mothers and daughters, husbands and sons who are grieving -- without dramatic grandstanding and gaudy public display.  

Building a 'ground zero' memorial is almost counter productive to grief when it memorializes the event -- making the happening more important than the resulting deaths.

I wish we'd (collectively) get off the death-worship wagon and make some part of Ground Zero a peaceful, contemplative and neutral place. I don't want to see images or depictions of that horrible day, nor do I want to see pictures and names of the dead like some cemetary.

I believe that the 9/11 tragedy belongs to the entire world. No one group has "ownership" over 9/11, not even the dead. Between the victimhood-worshipping family-members (not all are like that, mind you), cultural warriors of all stripes, mercenary real estate moguls and a bevy of politicos, I'm sick of it.

Look at the mess the whole project is in. Disgusting.  :x

I say an apt memorial should not be obsessed with the past or George Bush or Al Qaeda. Make it in the now. Keep the footprints undeveloped -- frikken Larry Silverstein and the Port Authority can work around them -- and put life back on them.

Make them simple and elegant like a Japanese garden. Seed grass and a plant a single Oak tree on each footprint. Connect the two with a footbridge over a fountain or stream or some sort of water. Keep it organic and airy so that we have earth, water and air as the primary design elements. Put a few parkbenches maybe so anyone can sit and contemplate what it takes to finally heal.

OK, I'm getting off my soapbox now...
