Title: 7th annual Musical Saw Festival
Post by: AstoriaArts on June 22, 2009, 10:57:06 PM
Please come to the 7th annual Musical Saw Festival: a concert and art exhibit centered around the 300-year-old art form of making music with a carpenter's handsaw.
Watch an attempt to set a Guinness World Record of the "Largest Musical Saw Ensemble."

(http://musicalsawfestival.org/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/sawfestflyer09.jpg)

The art form of making music with a carpenter's handsaw has been around for 300 years, but has become almost forgotten since the 1930's. The festival set up to make sure this unique art form does not disappear.
For the past 7 years Astoria (Queens) became a pilgrimage spot for musical saw players from all over the world.
 This year 40 musical saw players have registered to perform. They are coming from Japan, Belgium, Germany, Canada and all over the USA.

The festival, which is supported by the Queens Council on the Arts, NYC Department of Cultural Affairs and the NY State Council on the Arts, includes a concert, an art exhibit and a workshop - all centered around the musical saw, of course.

A Guinness World Record attempt for the 'Largest Musical Saw Ensemble' will take place at the festival. The current world record, set in Poland last year, is 27 musical saw players playing together.

Four world premiers of music written especially for the musical saw will be featured, with participation of a string quartet, handbell group, piano and soprano singer.

For some truly "cutting edge" music, come see the saw players and say you saw it here first!

When: Saturday, July 18th, 2pm
Where: Trinity Church, 31-18 37th Street (37th street at 31st Ave), Astoria
Admission: $10
More info: www.MusicalSawFestival.org (http://www.MusicalSawFestival.org)
