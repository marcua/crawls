Does anyone have any recommendations on how I can find this stuff? Or just advice based on how you all found your niches in New York?

I would recommend subscribing to various email events calendars.  I did that when I first moved here.  I have cultural and polictical leanings, and I did not limit myself to just Manhattan but wanted to get a feel for what was going on throughout all of NYC.  What I've found useful and interesting is (in no particular order):

Nonsense NY
nonsense nyc is a discriminating resource for independent art, weird events, strange happenings, unique parties, and senseless culture in new york city.

Flavorpill
Twice monthly, the Network Update delivers a sampling of the stories Flavorpill covers across our 11 publications on art, books, music, fashion, world news, and events in NYC, LA, SF, CHI, MIAMI, and LDN.

French Institute Alliance Française
French Institute Alliance Française is an American private, non-governmental, not-for-profit organization. Its mission is to promote and enhance the knowledge and appreciation of French and Francophone culture, to increase the knowledge of the French language, and to encourage interaction among French, Francophone and American people through programs in education and the arts.

MOMA
Events at the Museum of Modern Art.

Moving Image Weekly
Weekly events listing from the American Museum of the Moving Image in Astoria/LIC.

Issue Project Room
Acknowledging the critical deficit of art centres in our culture, The Project Room emerges as a vital meeting place for the most disparate forms of creativity whose sole criteria embodies the integrity and spirit of artistic expression and exploration.

Bluestockings
Bluestockings is a radical bookstore, fair trade cafe, and activist center in the Lower East Side of Manhattan. We host readings, workshops, performances, discussions and films almost every night. Please see our events calendar for details on upcoming events, and when visiting the store, take a look at our community bulletin board to learn about other events happening around the city.

The Danger List
Alternative goings-on in Manhattan and Brooklyn.

Times Up!
TIME'S UP! is a grassroots environmental group that uses educational outreach and direct action to promote a more sustainable, less toxic city. For more than 15 years, TIME'S UP! has worked to educate people about the environmental impacts of everyday decisions, from the food we buy to the means of transportation we use.

Waltz-Astoria
Local cafe and music space.  Concerts, shows, readings, wine tastings.

Parks and Recreation
Events at the city's parks in all five boroughs.

I also paid attention to this site, which has a calendar section; Joey in Astoria for the weekend events listing; queens.about.com for stuff going on in A/LIC; and Freeze Peach has an ongoing calendar.  The L Magazine has listings, as does, of course, the Village Voice.
