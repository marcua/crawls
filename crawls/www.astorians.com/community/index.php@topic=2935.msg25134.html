Quote from: annie on August 10, 2006, 04:56:45 PM
I'm not about to go dipping my grandmother's rings into that cheap-ass chemical soupy stuff they sell in DIY jars from Target.
With the exception of their pearls, I clean my grand- and great-grandmothers' jewelry with a soft toothbrush and just the slightest amount of toothpaste. I allow it to air dry and buff it up with a jewelers cloth. The stones shine and the metal sparkles, but it doesn't remove the original patina.

The pearls, I take them to a retired friend who used to earn her living stringing pearls. My maternal grandmother's pearls are more beautiful today than when my mother gave them to me and my paternal great-grandmother's pearls are not strung on silk, but on gold wire and are extremely fragile, so cleaning them takes great patience and care.
