Playing hookie from work, I am currently sitting at home eating lunch from this place. I've been in there twice before, and I LOVED it both times. I might have posted about it before on this thread but ... I have to tell you about my sammich ...... its just soooo good!
First of all I don't live in Astoria, but I live on Skillman ave just the other side of Northern. I was a little unsure if they would deliver to me, even tho it's extremely close, I wasn't sure if they'd want to send the guy over the bridge that overpasses the train yards. But the guy on the phone assured me it was NO problem at all, and that the delivery will be very quick. He was so nice about it! I ordered a mozzarella and prosciutto panini, which is part of the lunch special. It's not grilled, its cold, but it is SO good. The prosciutto is tender and thin and theres a healthy amount of it, and it's not too salty either. The mozzarella is fresh and moist and soft and SO good. The bread is what kills me though. I have no idea what it's called but the crust is flaky and buttery and it melts in my mouth with each bite. The lunch special also came with salad, but it's mixed greens, and I'm an iceburg kind of girl, but the greens seem very fresh and they included a little side thing of oil. For $6.95 how can you go wrong? They also included for free a drink, which it did not mention on the menu, and I'm not quite sure what it is. I keep sniffing it and sipping it and I can't place it! It's deep red, kinda lemony, sorta sweet, not carbonated and definitely some type of juice. It's got a very distinct scent and taste, but I can't place it for the life of me. This is gonna bug me all day.

Finally got to this place last night after weeks of good intentions. (Walking from 30th Ave often seems like a good idea, then I get hungry.)

The special was pork loin, I guess roasted, sliced, and finished quickly in the broiler, stacked with golden raisins, mushrooms, a bit of spinach, a bit of carrot.  Some yummy polenta on the side.   A bit of frothy sauce over the top was a little odd looking, but everything was delicious, and cooked perfectly.

Nice people there too.  I think I am going to be able to make the whole long walk down there often.

It has so few tables (seating for maybe 20 people, max,) I hope just enough people believe me to keep it going. I don't like to wait in line.
