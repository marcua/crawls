Bentley, I've responded to your request via PM.

Aqua & Lobber. The reason we've not made it public in the past is because the purpose of a temporary suspension isn't really to "shame" someone into behaving, but to force them to step back from the keys for a while before posting again. Early on we got very negative feedback (and lost a few members) when this information was made public because they felt we were trying to shame them.

I do agree there are positives and negatives for this information not being public. One negative for me is that it encourages gossip as to the reason the person was suspended, and allows people to hypothesize about the reasons for it. Often this gossip casts the moderators in the worst possible light. But, if an individual who loses their cool doesn't want that information public, currently we will honor that request despite the negatives.

I'm completely open to changing the approach if a majority is interested in it.
