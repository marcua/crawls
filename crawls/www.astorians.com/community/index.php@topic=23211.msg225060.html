Feature Presentation - Tuesday 31 July, 6:30 PM

 
Michelle Alexander: "The New Jim Crow"

Presented by Dr. Michelle Ronda, Professor of Sociology, Marymount Manhattan College
 

“There are more African Americans under correctional control today—in prison or jail, on probation or parole—than were enslaved in 1850.” In this incisive presentation, former litigator turned legal scholar Michelle Alexander provocatively argues that we have not ended racial caste in America: we have simply redesigned it. The New Jim Crow is a stunning account of the rebirth of a caste-like system in the United States -one that has resulted in millions of African Americans locked behind bars and then relegated to a permanent second-class status. The New Jim Crow challenges the civil rights community—and all of us—to place mass incarceration at the forefront of a new movement for racial justice in America. (60 minutes)
The program will be presented by Michelle Ronda, Professor of Sociology, Marymount Manhattan College, a gifted teacher and lecturer. 

 
All Are Welcome Tuesday Evening July 31!

Although this event is open to the public, and everyone is welcome, I should clarify that Alexander, in the introduction to her book on "Mass Incarceration in the Age of Colorblindness," is clear that the intended audiences for the thesis and data therein are people who care about racial and social justice in the US today.  Sometimes "preaching to the choir" is a goal rather than a critique (at least the history of successful social movements tells us so).
Alexander once believed that to equate mass incarceration with slavery or Jim Crow was hyperbole that would only damage a movement for equity in punishment, but her own research and engagement with data led her to a different view. As a discussant, I will pose some questions following the video viewing of Alexander's lecture on her book (whether I am gifted as a discussant seems wholly irrelevant to me given the format).
If you are interested in learning more about incarceration in the US and more about Alexander's argument on racialized social control, I hope you will join us.
