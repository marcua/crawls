Spring/Summer Photo Contest!
« on: April 25, 2009, 11:10:06 AM »
As much as I like the snowy picture above, I'm afraid it's that time of year again boys and girls - for a new picture on astorians.com!

Here are the rules:
0. Two submissions per person only - show us your best work.
1. Your image should be in digital format.
2. The picture needs to be taken with at LEAST at 3mp camera. Scanned images must be HIGH quality. (the bigger the better)
3. A small version of your image should be posted to this thread with a brief description of what it is. Please embed the image in the post if you can (use Flickr's link feature, or wherever you want to host it.)
4. The contest starts immediately and will run until May 21. At which time the contest will be closed to entries.
5. Astorians will then vote on the submitted images.
6. A winner will be announced and the graphic will go live on the homepage shortly thereafter.

Further details:
Your image will run for a period of weeks or months. It should be a picture that captures some aspect of Astoria, and ideally, should be a spring or summer theme. On the bottom right hand corner we'll provide your name/username/link/email whatever you want (in text) so people can link or contact you. Obviously you are giving us permission to use your image on the Astorians homepage for a duration of our choosing by your submission. We reserve editorial right over your photograph's use on the website, however we will not resell or claim any other rights to your work.

Nice work Harlan! :mrgreen:

Today actually turned out to be perfect weather for snapping a few shots. I was kinda bummed when I realized that the sun was sticking around because I was already out for the day and I didn't bother to bring my camera, but there's always tomorrow ...

I'm really digging the fence shot. The only thing that I found distracting (and I'm really digging here, because it's a really cool shot) is that the trefoils in the left hand portion of the fence are just slightly outside of the depth of field, and as a result, they appear a little soft for being so large and prominent in the shot. You might try reshooting it at the same distance from the fence, focused on the same spot you focused on the first time, but adjust your aperture so it is slightly smaller. I noticed you used f/3.7, maybe try it at f/4, f/4.5, or f/5, so the depth of field will be just a little greater.
