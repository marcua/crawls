Yes in hindsight, my statement about the clothes and shoes selection in Astoria being a "joke" was completely overgeneralized, I will fully admit now.  Instead (you're right), I should have said, to ME, shopping in Astoria is a joke.

I am not a girly-girl in any way shape or form and do not wear cutesy little boutiquey-type dresses...nor is my style "vintage".  My style is immenesly casual...jeans, sweatshirts, tshirts, etc.  However as casual as I am, I will fully admit to being a BIG 'ol namebrand snob...Lucky Jeans, Levis, Carhardt, Ralph Lauren/Polo Sport, etc.  As for shoes the girliest shoes I own are platform Steve Maddens....but for the most part you can see me rockin' my Uggs, Birkenstocks or Clarks' Wallabees (or "kicks" (sneakers)....Vans, Adidas, etc).  As for accessories, I usually have something Hello Kitty (as cutesy as I get) or Paul Frank's Julius.

But my biggest thing is that I won't pay full retail either (rarely at least).  I will usually head for the outlets or more likely, stores like Marshalls or Century 21 or the discount racks at department or retail stores.  I would rather have a name brand than something some local designer has manufactured.  And to pay a premium for something that's not a brandname, isn't my thing either...but again, that's just me.  With my description above, you might be able to see how there is very little in Astoria to my liking. 

With the exception of my time in college and living abroad, I have lived in Astoria ALL of my life, and have never found Astoria to be a place where I would think about buying clothes (with the exception of the gap, or in jhs when benetton was the place to shop).  I think I might have bought something in easy pickins once but that was a total fluke as I was in the 6th grade.  Being in this community for as long as I have, I think I am pretty aware of what's around, and am more likely to spend my money here than anywhere else.  But at the same time, for these type of things, I can't think of one place where I can find something I would want to wear.

Shopping for jewelry is an idea though....that could be kinda cool...and something I wouldn't have normally thought about.  And I do agree with you about sales people in some stores...they are pretty clueless sometimes.  I find that to be a retail problem in general...not just in Astoria or in a chain store...customer service lacks in a lot of places.  And, for the record, I don't think Foot Locker is necessarily a "quality establishment" but based on my taste, if I am looking for something for my feet, and I don't feel like going into the city, it is one of the only places in the neighborhood I can go.

i appreciate the effort put forth by listing all the shopping options on steinway; sad to say that looking at it really provides an overview of how little *variety* there is in the shopping options. as an example: there's not much for women that falls in between the super-cheap polyester crap that isn't even all that of the moment (a la pretty girl, cool jeans, and easy pickins) and wayyyy overpriced clubwear. i do like the boutiques that have been listed earlier in the thread; it's interesting that none of them are on steinway.

one thing that also worries me (and further underscores the lack of variety on the strip) is the number of businesses with two outlets that are within a block of one another. two at & t stores, two payless outlets, two rite aids (one's not on the list because it's across broadway from the corner where this list starts -- and at least one is going to be vacant within the next few months if the thread about the new rite aid at 34th and steinway is any indication), two sprint stores that are a few storefronts apart from one another! and there are a couple of fabcos as well. surely those outlets are ripe for consolidation soon? 

that said, i would like to add that madeline hosiery, the corsetiere on steinway and 30th, is GREAT for bras and other foundation garments, a hidden gem that any woman looking for a bra should definitely check out.
