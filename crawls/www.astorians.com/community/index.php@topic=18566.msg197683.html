I have always said that the Realty business is the most lax business in the U.S. (especially NYC) in terms of regulation.  Even the oil industry is better regulated...and that's an industry that CLEARLY needs more regulation.

People are so willing most of the time to put up what realtors have to say and even if you question thier business practices, the common answer is "well, that's ust how real estate works"; or something akin to that.

To me, that's crap. 

Money like this should immediately have be held in an escrow account where the rented would have placed the money into DRIECTLY; there should be no need to hand any money directly to the realtor.  When the transaction is finished, THEN the realtor would be allowed to take thier fee from the escrow account, not before.

But if you ask any realtor, this type of setup would be "horrible and detrimental to thier business" (or some other BS like that).  At the very least, harsher laws should be in place to punish those realtors who missapropriate money in this fashion. 


To bad the real estate lobby is so strong.  And the worse part is, most people seem not to care about this issue.
