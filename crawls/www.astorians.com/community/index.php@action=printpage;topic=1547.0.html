You must be kidding. Mac users are just as much prisoners to proprietary software as Windows users. I tend to see this level of confusion among Mac users quite frequently though. The bunch tends to think that they're not exposed to the issues of EULAs, DRM, Trusted Computing, and other violations against personal freedom just because they're running a more "capable" OS (than windows at least). Do you know that OS X is nothing more than a bastardized copy of FreeBSD with a pretty GUI on top? I use the word "bastardized" to indicate my distaste for the fact that FreeBSD's license allows a company like Apple to take the code and redistribute it under a freedom-restricting license. All in all, some people may think that Macs are pretty but once you learn to value freedom, it's easy to avoid the trap by just admitting that all the eye candy in the world doesn't justify enslaving yourself.
