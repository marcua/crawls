Briefly: artgirl and I liked it a lot on our first visit.


Cocktails:


--Sazerac, very nice, sweet but elegant.


--Some kinda lemonade-hibiscus-tea-whatever thing that artgirl had, my sip was refreshing and complex.


--I also had a pint of the IPA they have on draft, which I think they said was from Abita or someone down there. Quite good, seemed a little more robust than other IPAs.


Apps:


--Boudain balls, the crawfish ones, awesome and decadent. Instant hit.


--Gumbo, just perfect, smoky file flavor and tender meats. As with the black bean soup at Pachanga Patterson, a bowl of this could be my dinner most nights.


Entrees:


--Fried oyster po' boy, really good, though I agree with someone who said that they should use a less-substantial roll. The stiffer outer parts of this one kinda got in the way of the filling. I resorted to opening the roll, sticking my face in and eating the oysters along with the inner parts of the roll -- which I guess tells you the oysters were very good.


--Buttermilk fried chicken, really good, nice meat, serious batter. I will warn you that this is some hard-core fried chicken, with plenty of batter and even more salt (which I like). If you're looking for light and airy, this ain't it.


Desserts looked good but we were too full, not really surprising.


Service was friendly, reasonably fast and accommodating. Interior is very cute. We liked the big high tables opposite the bar.


Left sated and happy, will be back.

I've eaten there twice already and like almost everything I've had so far - the balls, the fried chicken, the Mac & Cheese, fried oysters, gumbo, etc.  I wasn't a huge fan of the blackened catfish; the spice mix was too salty and lacked heat.  The Colllard Greens were odd; they tasted pickled, kind of like sauerkraut.  But the food is very good and very different for NYC.  I'll definitely be back.

My main complaint right now is the drink price point.  Those lovely Sazaracs people are mentioning are $11.  In Astoria.  Where I can walk down 7 blocks to the Quays and get a single malt Scotch for $7 (with a buyback most nights).

Top shelf liquor is $12 and $13.  These just seem way too close to Manhattan drink prices to me.  I know the hipsters are coming with their fancy food and their money...but I wish drink prices weren't out of control so quickly.

Ironically the food prices at Sugar Freak is quite reasonable for the quality.  Portions aren't huge but the food is heavy so they don't need to be.

They might have heard y'all on the cocktail prices/specials complaints: artgirl and I went back for our second visit Sunday night, and they had a "heat-wave special" of $8 on all cocktails and $5 draft beers.


I had a very nice hurricane, with lots of real fruit juices, as opposed to the frozen-drink-machine kind you get in the cheaper Bourbon Street tourist traps. She got a watermelon punch with a savory note that she thought might be cucumber, very refreshing. I also had an Abita Purple Haze draft.



The food was great, again.


We shared a cup of gumbo, wonderful again. Also the pork boudain balls, another hit, rich and moist and very distinct from the crawfish ones.


I got the blackened catfish, a dish that can be an afterthought in some Cajun places, but here the seasoning was bold and salty (which I love) and the fish was tender. It was also served truly hot, another test in a NOLA-centric place, because a lightly coated piece of finned fish can cool very quickly if it's left waiting around for its deep-fried menu neighbors.


I got it with the side of lima beans and tasso ham, which was buttery and smoky and made artgirl a fan, even though she doesn't like limas much.


I added the Southern slaw. This had a thicker, creamier sauce than standard cole slaw, with a vinegar kick to keep it bouncy. Artgirl "suggested" that I put the dish between us so she could steal more of it.


She ordered the entree listed as "grilled shrimp," which sounded a tad dull to me, but the waiter recommended it. Turns out it's only the menu description that's dull: this should really be called "grilled half-stuffed shrimp" or something; each one had a generous cooked-on dollop of ... I dunno, a savory cheese, maybe parmesan? Some parsley? Not sure what was in it, but it was insanely tasty, with the smoky grill flavor carrying right through it. One of the better shrimp dishes I've ever eaten.


For dessert we shared the chess pie, which was more than enough for two at that point. It reminded me of a pecan pie, but with peanut, and it was rich and sweet but also had a nice nutty tang.


On a side note, I left my umbrella there a couple of weeks ago, and called to report it the following week. As promised, they still had it behind the bar for me. The little things.

Count me as another fan of Sugarfreak! I was really impressed with the food and service.

Starters: crawfish boudin balls (deliciously and perfectly fried, not too bready) and gumbo (I thought this was the weak link of the meal, not bad, but would have liked it thicker and I thought it lacked some depth of flavor)

Didn't have dessert because we were too full, but can't wait to go back and try the chess pie, among other things.

Such a great addition to the hood. Can't wait to go back! Hope they open for brunch sometime too, think they could do some great things with that.
