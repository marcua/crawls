Another thing, much to my chagrin, that disappeared are Chinese hand laundries.  When I first moved to Astoria I used to go one on the corner of 34th & Broadway.  The restaurant, Uncle George's or whatever it is called expanded its space and took over my Chinese hand laundry.   They moved to the corner of 31st Street and whatever the street is that is just east of Steinway.   

I liked the way they did my shirts so much I made the trek and continued to patronize them.   I eventually got sick of the walk and started to use a Chinese hand laundry that was on Crescent just south of Broadway.

Then I moved and used the people just east of Steinway, but they went out of business.

I walked by Crescent Street one day and that guy was gone.

The only other Chinese place was "Al's" on Broadway a block or two west of 21st Street.  I remember going in there once to find out how long it would take and they told me there was a two-week turnaround on shirts.  I never bothered to go there and the place never seemed to be open when I went buy there.   It too is long gone.

I've been searching for a good Chinese hand laundry since.  There doesn't appear to be any more of them in Astoria and from what I understand from people here long before my time, they used to be all over the place.

I received a little bit of Astoria history last night.   I've always wondered why there are two first-class fruit stands on 30th Avenue between 32nd & 33rd Streets.  I think there is only one or two sores in between. 

I asked the guy in the fruit stand that is not on the corner about this and he told me they opened in 1978.   He said the other fruit stand, the one on the corner, had been located across the street where Key Food now is and bought the building and moved to the corner of 30th & 33rd. He said that occurred in 1982, well before my time in Astoria.   From reading this board the space on the corner of 33rd St. & 30th Ave. had been a bar.

I went out for my jog on Saturday and decided to vary the route home to break up the routine and took a run down I think it is 23rd Street or whatever the street is two blocks east of 21st Street.   I hadn't been on that block in a while and there is an old deli -- called the Parkside Deli even though it isn't even near the Park -- midblock on 23rd between Hoyt & Newtown Avenues.   I think it was closed when it first appeared on my radarscope and then was open for a while and now it appears to be closed, though you can still see some of the wares in the window.   That is kind of an obscure block and I always thought it was an odd spot for a convenience store/deli.   It is midblock and not on the corner or anything.

And on the southwestern corner of Newtown & 23rd there was one of those once ubiquitous auto repair places that appears to be closed and a "for sale" sign has gone up.   It looks like that will be the next apartment building.   Right across the street, where there was a flea market/second hand shop/used furniture store -- is a new apartment building.   In fact there are several new residential buildings/buildings under construction between 21st and 31st Streets on Newtown.
