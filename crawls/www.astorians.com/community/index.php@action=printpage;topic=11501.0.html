Suicide is actually one of the biggest concerns in the holding cells, not only in NYC, but across the nation.  Many of those were just arrested have never been through the system and literally have a breakdown.  I know that NYC precincts are required to keep a log and check on the prisoners every so often. 

http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=950DE4D61F39F935A15751C0A96F948260&sec=&spon=&pagewanted=all

Below is an article taken from the NY Times in 1989. The article addresses the suicide issue.  Of particular interest is the following:

"Psychologists and correction officials say potential suicides tend to fit a pattern. They are usually young men from the ages of 19 through 25, first-time offenders - many charged with driving under the influence of drugs or alcohol - with histories of psychological instability and trauma at home or work. Many of those being held are awaiting arraignment or trial.

Upon entering a jail or prison, officials say, prisoners are routinely searched for objects with which they can harm themselves. Shoelaces, neckties and belts are taken away to prevent hangings, and prisoners showing signs of depression or erratic behavior are secluded and kept under constant or periodic watch. Such prisoners are also sent to psychiatric facilities as quickly as possible for evaluation."
