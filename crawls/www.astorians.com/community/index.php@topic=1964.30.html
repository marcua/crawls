So I tend to think that money will at least get the ball rolling for poorer students.  Why do you think Pataki is so against the campaign for fiscal equity rulings to fund NYC schools closer to what upstate schools receive?  Because it's the upstate and western new yorkers that already have good schools that voted for him.  He's fighting tooth and nail to keep the NYC schools from getting the funding that is fundamentally necessary to make improvements.

But I do agree that the problems run far deaper that just dollars and cents.  There are a whole slew of socio-economic issues that run incredibly deap that would need to be addressed that I have no idea about.

But one last thing, JCY...
I take issue with the generalization that " the reality however seems to be that most of the people on government assistance are taking advantage of the system."  DO you have numbers to back this up?  Because as I've stated in previous posts, I know several people that wouldn't be where they are today without this safety net, and I guarantee you that they weren't "taking advantage" of the system.  Unless you mean "taking advantage" to mean being given an opportunity to succeed.  Food stamps allowed them the chance to feed themselves while they went through rehab to clean themselves up.

So, as we seem to have both come from situations where neither of our families were rich growing up, we each have different experiences of success and getting by in society.  Understandably, your folks pulled themselves up by the bootstraps, and built a succesfull business.  I benefitted from (albeit not a lot) of government support when there was difficulty, which I attribute largely to how I've gotten to where I am today, which I'm quite proud of, thank you very much. 

So our experiences, undoubtedly lead greatly to our personal/political oppinions.  And as a result, are unlikely to be swayed to the other side.
