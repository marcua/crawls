Title: June 20 & Aug 29- Astoria Food Tour!
Post by: belle on June 11, 2009, 04:42:48 PM
Hi everyone!

I'm happy to announce I'm leading a food walking tour of our favorite neighborhood (duh Astoria!) through ICE (Institute of Culinary Education). Pass the word to all your friends who ever wondered how delicious Astoria is!

The tour is $75. I don't set the price, but we'll be eating fairly constantly throughout the 5 stops (4 hours) of the tour and I think it ends up being worth it.

The teaser is below and you can sign up by heading to ICE:
https://web.iceculinary.com/icereg/details.asp?cid=ASTORIA (https://web.iceculinary.com/icereg/details.asp?cid=ASTORIA)

Culinary treats reach beyond Grecian shores in Astoria, as you will sample during a flavor-packed stroll through the Queens neighborhood. Each stop of the tour features a demonstration, shopping opportunity, or nibbles-- such as honeyed baklava, nutmeg-laced bureks, fried haloumi, house-cured salumi, apple sheesha, and cardamom coffee. Start the morning with Greek pastries and coffee, then it’s off to sample Bosnian bureks. Continue to a Mediterranean grocery and fill your bags with assorted feta, olives, nuts, oils, and other culinary delights. Walk by “old world” fish mongers and butchers whom locals favor, and enter an Italian market for a demonstration in sausage stuffing. A turn into the heart of Egyptian, Moroccan, and Lebanese cultures will set you among hookah bars and Middle Eastern specialty shops. You will rest your feet at a favorite funky Egyptian restaurant with a sampling of small plates.
