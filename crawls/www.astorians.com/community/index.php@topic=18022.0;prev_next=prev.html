I retired from construction as a member of Local Union #3 and it was a good job with good pay and benefits during my time when there was plenty of work. My son is also a member and has not worked for months.

The $40-$50 per hour rate comes after a six year apprenticeship with mandatory school after work twice per week foer the fist four years. I don't know what starting pay is now but it's probably less than most other available jobs. You don't hit a half way decent pay rate until about five years with a big jump after six.

These applicants have a long way to go to begin work. Their background will be screened and those that get past that will be scheduled for an interview where they must bring their high school transcripts and sit at a table with six people both contractors and union members who will ask questions, look at grades and attendance records and rate the applicant based on school history and desire to enter the field.

Standing in line is just the beginning.
