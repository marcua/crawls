The white cat looks good, but some of the others...well, I dunno - the shave is too close.
I have 5 cats . One has a very thick undercoat that gets matted and sheds all the time, even with constant combing. It's the kind of coat that no one wants to pet, so she does not get the attention of the other cats. I had her shaved last Spring for the first time - the vet called it "the lion cut." I was a little shocked at first, but then grew to love it as I can really give her so much more physical attention...no more shedding. She honesltly seems happier, too. IMO the only drawback is the fact that the vet has to tranquilize the cat in order to do a nice shave.
