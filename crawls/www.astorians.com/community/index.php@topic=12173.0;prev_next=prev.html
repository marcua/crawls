I don't think it's common. I see it as more of a credit or banking issue. People aren't required to have a bank, but in order to cash a check from someone's (a tenant's) bank the recipient would have to physically go to that specific bank or pay a check cashing fee. It also prevents a bounced check issue, which the landlord may have been bitten on in the past.

The question isn't the tender, but rather the receipt for payment. I would want a receipt each month for the payment I made so that I couldn't lawfully be evicted if the landlord chose to play dishonestly.

It is illegal for a landlord (I believe ANY landlord) to INSIST that the rent be paid in cash. They can request it, but they can't refuse to accept any other form of payment.

Cash shouldn't be a problem as long as you get a dated receipt each month. I would however advise you to check out the building with DHCR. THE DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND COMMUNITY RENEWAL. They are located in Jamaica an you can call them to make your inquiry. They will be able to tell you if the apartment is rent stabilized and what the legal rent should be. If the apartment is stabilized the landlord is required to provide you with a standard stabilized lease. How he wants to be paid is less important then weather or not he is renting within the law. Often times when a landlord is taking cash only for a stabilized apartment it is because he is trying to get more then he is legally entitled to. It could also be that he has had problems with bad checks in the past so he now has a cash only policy. Either way educate yourself as to what your rights as a renter are so you can protect your interests going forward.

Good Luck
