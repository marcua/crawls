I agree with Alexa (I did positively nod you for that statement).  Positive nods are easy to give because you like what someone said.  I don't think people go around positive nodding people because they like them, then there would be no 0 Nods on any posts.  Everyone would have either a net + or a net -.  The negative nods seem to be really what people are having issues with.  This is where the consistency gets messy, people disagreeing with or not liking the person.

I think I remember merm saying that he was just trying it out and seeing what comes of it.  Well, I do tend to agree that nods lead me one way or another when reading a thread (prejudging).  I hate it.  I want to come up with an opinion on my own.  I also like my aforementioned idea of simply ridding the negative nod, or adding a requirement for the reason why you disagree (as Alexa suggested).

Enigmacat also hit it right on the nose with the idea of the anonymity of handles.  Many of us that have been around for a while have met other people in person and the username is not so anonymous anymore.  Some of us can at least put faces to user names.

Basically, I think that there is an overwhelming amount of community members that do not agree with the nodding system as it currently stands.  Whether they stay or go, something does need to be done to address the situation instead of keeping it the way it is.

I've always pretty much disliked the nods, but at this point, I've put my issues behind me, and decided not to get so frustrated with them.  I figure I have better things to do, than get all bent out of shape over it.  I still reflexively pos nod a post that got a negative nod, when there is no legitimate reason given for doing so.  I understand the nods intended use, but I'm not sure everyone uses them the way they're intended to be used.  I do know that I don't like the idea of getting rid of the anonymity, because I feel that the eventual consequence of that would be that the negative nodder would be attacked and the thread would degenerate into name-calling, insults, and general mayhem.  I think that result would be far worse.  I do like the idea of getting rid of the negative nods.  But at this point, they've been around for two years and I've chosen to just do my best to ignore the feature.  There are good and bad things about every web site I visit, and I really can't expect to have everything to my liking.  There are some things in life that just aren't really that important in the larger scheme of things.  And to me, this is one of them.
