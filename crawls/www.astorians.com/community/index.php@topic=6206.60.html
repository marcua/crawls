Quote
What is panera?  I've never been to one.

Panera was actually originally a local place in St. Louis called the St. Louis Bread Company.  The food is actually pretty fresh and delicious.  Sure, it's not local anymore, but as far as chains go, it's actually really nice.  They've maintained a good quality ever since expanding out of the St. Louis area.  I would equate a lot of the stuff to what you get at Cosi. 

I'm not a huge fan of chains, but the great thing about this area is that it doesn't seem like many local establishments are being shut down by a horde of chains.  Sure, it still happens, but, outside of the city, this is a huge problem.  My parents visited a few weeks ago, and they couldn't believe how many local establishments are around.  It's totally not like that anymore in most of the country.  In the town where my wife is from, there is no grocery store(there used to be).  So, where does everyone go for groceries?.......Wal-Mart.  Ugh.  In my home town, they DO still have a grocery store, but MANY people drive to the next town to shop at Wal-Mart because of the lower prices.  It's unfortunate.  I really wish people did not always think with their wallets.  Some people are forced to, but many people that CAN afford to shop at local establishments still go to Wal-Mart to save money.  Makes no sense.  I really enjoy the fact that a lot of the money I spend here in Astoria goes to local establishments.  It's very refreshing.  Anyway, Panera is not Wal-Mart, so it'll probably be cool to have it here.
