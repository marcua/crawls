I'm going to California for a couple of weeks.  The debit card I had intended to use for purchases while there won't be available, so I have to decide where else to park my money.  I'll take some cash on me, but should I put the rest:
 
A) In my debit card from a bank that doesn't have CA locations, but can be used as a debit or credit card anywhere in the USA (I already notified them I'll be in CA), but I can't get cash without paying a fee from each bank

B) In my savings account in a bank which does have ATM's & branches where I'm going but I can only use their ATM's/branches or I'll be charged a fee when I withdraw cash, and it's not a debit card so I can only use it to get cash

or

C) Just put everything I possibly can on a credit card and pay it off when I get back?

(or)

D) Get traveler's checks, because, gee, doesn't everyone just love them?

That could be useful for not carrying a lot of cash, but the question was really how would I get cash once I'm there.
   
And here's the answer!

I called one of my credit card companies to let them know I'll be using the card (I haven't used it in a long time) and that I'll be using it outside my usual area.  I asked about using the card for cash withdrawals (don't! it has an incredibly high APR!) and the rep pointed out that I could get "cash over" the amount of an actual purchase.  [And then I asked myself, Duh, Starwind, what part of the dark ages do you actually come from?]  I just have to check with the cashier or look for a sign that tells you what the cash back limit is as set by the store.  The rep said sometimes people have to make a few tiny purchase separately in order to get all the cash back they actually want.  The whole total (item purchased plus cash back) is regarded as a charge, but the cash doesn't earn you any bonus points, just what you actually purchased.)
