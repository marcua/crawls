Why aren't liberals more politically active.
« on: May 14, 2005, 12:15:48 AM »
Last night my boyfriend & I came back from yet another 'liberal' meeting -- this time a mock trial, putting the war in Iraq on trial.  It was interesting but preaching to the choir.  Afterwards, we fell into a discussion about why people aren't doing more to bring about change in Washington.  My boyfriend and I and most of our political friends are what I call 'old lefties' from the late 60s & 70s (we're in our 50s now and just as radical -- at least with our mouths  :lol: ).  

I personally feel that the neo-cons don't mind at all how many rallies and protest marches we attend because it doesn't impact them at all.  They've got control of the media, most of the government and it seems to me they do what they want.  I'm growing more and more frustrated by what I see happening each new day and I --this may sound naive or misdirected -- but we wonder why people aren't taking to the streets like we did in the 60s and 70s.  It seems to me that we brought about some real change then that just isn't happening now.  

Some of the reasons I see (& welcome discussion about) are that times are harder, young people have to spend most of their time working to keep a roof over their heads while my generation was pretty much supported by our parents and had a lot of free time to 'dream big'.  Also, we watched less television and there seemed to emerge more 'leaders' (apologies for my quirk of putting so many words in quotes but I do that when the terms I use are debatable) -- Martin Luther King, Jr., the guys in the Chicago 7, the black panthers, students for a democratic society, the Berrigan brothers.  Even my aunt, who is in her 90s now and was a L.I. housewife & mom at that time went off and chained herself to a nuclear reactor with the priests Berrigan and was arrested!

I dont' want to be one of these older people who paint the past in golden terms -- that's really not even the issue -- but am just trying to figure out why every day, it seems to me the neo-cons in Washinton keep moving us towards a theocratic (?) dictatorship, a foreign policy that endangers us all, and there isn't the uproar there was in the 60's-70's.

Any discussion of this, I would welcome.  Hope there is some interest in all this out there.  Thanks for letting me ramble and vent a little!   :)
