And by doing so you just might be the only tenant in your building that doesn't get bedbugs.

I got scared of reading all about these pests, and decided to do something about it prophylactically.

I started by protecting my bed. I bought a bedbug mattress protector and also pillow covers. (http://www.bedbathandbeyond.com/search/search.aspx/bedbugs/?order_num=-1&sstr=bedbugs&dim=1&nty=1&) I used all my 20% off coupons and saved a real bundle.

Then you need to clear caulk (http://www.homedepot.com/webapp/wcs/stores/servlet/THDProductCompare?prodComp_3=100024624&prodComp_0=100173080&langId=-1&storeId=10051&catalogId=10053&remProdId=100657595&prodComp_1=100662614&errorURL=ProductAttributeErrorView&keyword=clear%20l) all around the floors in your apartment. Between the floor and the baseboard, and if you have high molding in a room, get up there and do the same to the top side. You will need a caulking gun (http://www.homedepot.com/webapp/wcs/stores/servlet/Search?keyword=caulking+gun&langId=-1&storeId=10051&catalogId=10053) but as they tend to be expensive, try to borrow one.

I have two small dogs and I was VERY concerned about the use of the following item. I did my research and suggest you do the following in specific locations around your apartment. Buy human grade Diatomaceous Earth (http://www.earthworkshealth.com/detail.php?id=1) and a plastic applicator bottle (http://store.hello-gorgeous.net/apbowimesc6o.htmlsmall) from any of the local beauty supply stores. Lay down a small line of this stuff all around the bed, the doorway, electric outlets, telephone jacks, on shelves and floor of all closets and kitchen cabinets. Repeat this as the dust disappears.

While my neighbors have had repeated outbreaks of bedbugs, I have not seen any. The exterminator checks my apartment when he is in the building doing extermination in other apartments and he gets down on the floor to look under bedroom furniture and still finds nothing. This was all recommended to me by another member of this message board (RB) and I will be eternally grateful to her.

Interestingly, I haven't seen a roach or a mouse since doing this.
