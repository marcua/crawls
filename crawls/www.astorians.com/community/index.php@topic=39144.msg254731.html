I love soccer, and I think a lot of people in our neighborhood do.  So from my perspective a pitch would be good. Also, people don't care at all about the rules posted outside of the track, so perhaps it makes sense to change the default usage of the field.

I hope they can find a way to still allow runners to use the track to some degree however. A high fence around the soccer pitch for one, and then big signs with emphasized rules for behavior. As it stands running at the track is incredibly annoying at times because of all the people who have zero respect for runners, and I for one would fear that without a high fence and more soccer balls using the track would get even more difficult.

I'd rather have seen a pitch elsewhere to be honest. Come to think of it, I think there are a few pitches in the area already, so not sure why they can't be used. At least one up by the power plant, and I think one along the water, south of the park.

Say goodbye forever to a nice quiet jog and workout at the track.


I remember as a kid in the early 70's watching baseball, softball games in the grassy area. I also remember in the 80's jogging on the dirt track and dodging soccer balls and stampeding soccer players. Later there was a time in the 90's when the field house supervisor-a young guy named John who oversaw the tennis courts-ran a tight ship and enforced the rules. No sneaking on the tennis courts without a permit; you had to sign up for a court at the designated time, no ball playing in the grassy area, no bicycles on the track, etc... Anyone notice how the grassy area has been ravaged ad turned into a dust bowl? I wouldn't be surprised if the parks dept. is intentionally letting it fall to ruin.


In the summer it's always nice to see neighborhood children taking part in organized track and field activities on the weekday mornings. The FIFA-sized pitch will put an end to that.
