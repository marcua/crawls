I just recently did the gym search in Astoria...

I had started running outside before it got super cold, but I didn't want to continue in the winter weather.  I went over to Astoria Sports club with every intent of joining at the cheapest level solely for the treadmills.  But I went to the wrong block and ended up at Evolution, which I had no idea was even there!  I walked in and spoke with Anthony, the owner and he was doing a "special holiday deal", of $575 for the year.  I walked around, and everything is brand new, very clean.  I should also mention that this gym is open 24 hours on the weekdays, which is hugely appealing to me since I get up at about 5 am to work out. 

I then went over to ASC after this.  I had looked at it once before about 2 years ago and had remembered that it wasn't super great, but I figured for their cheap rates it would be fine.  But...I really really did not like it when I went in this second time.  The main thing that got to me was the smell.  I don't think it's very clean in there, and it smells very damp (on account of the pool, I know) and sweaty.  I don't know how else to describe it, but it's not the kind of environment I want to be exposing my lungs to as I run.   But I'm a bit of a germophobe as I get sick easily, so this really kind of grossed me out.  Also, the people who work there have never been super friendly - they seem to "put up with" the people who come in.  I especially noticed a difference coming from Evolution, where they were very happy to talk to me and answer all of my questions, without being too "sales-y".

The conclusion: I ended up joining Evolution.  $575/yr is super cheap for a full gym (he ended up extending the offer, and seems pretty easy-going about it).  It being open 24 hours lets me work out whenever I want, which is hugely important since I wanted to start working out in the morning (I know from past experience I won't work out at night, too many other things to do!).  I am happy to wake up and go there in the mornings and I look forward to it.  I have the feeling that if I had joined ASC, I would have dreaded it every time.  I'll also mention that the staff there are very friendly, even at 5 am!

Good for you!

I joined Club Fitness on Broadway and 31 Street in Sept.

So far so good. Not really cheap...$79/mo. So I do my best to get my money's worth. I thought the price for their private training was high...(like in most places truthfully.)

It is quite clean. I mentioned to the owner...or one of the owners...Yanna, nice lady, that I was concerned about them clumping the classes in the middle of the day while I would be at work. They are shooting for a six person minimum per class to make it worth their while. This can be a stretch for a 6:30 AM Yoga class on Monday mornings. (The instructor, Antonia is one of the best instructors I've ever had....her husband does the Friday 7:00 PM classes, a card carrying 'swami yoga master'.

So there in would lie a problem for somebody shopping fitness clubs.

What should you look for?

1) Cost
2) Convenience ( If it's a pain in the ass to get to...you'll never use it ) Club Fitness is right next to the Broadway subway stop for the N/W on 31 St.
3) Safety ( I'd also add comfort in here....who wants to go to a gym with a bunch of 'roid raging barbell throwing jerks who 'Own' the gym)
...incidentally....I never really felt safe at the BQE....it was like working out at Riker's....they had a sign in the locker room advising you to use a substantial padlock on your locker....Gee...that fills me with warm and fuzzies!

4) Quality and hospitality of the staff.

The thing to really watch out for, if you can, but it may be moot after you ink the contract, is does the gym 'slip' after you sign on...like the Rock for instance...I was one of the original patrons.... I waited in line out front when the Soho restaurant was not in yet and they were doing their enrollments from there....about 4 years ago...then...well...it slipped, steadily. The lawyer owners grabbed some of the interior space for their offices and the staff turned into 'Bill and Ted' kids.

The Matrix really isn't bad at all....just a bit of a walk in the wrong direction for me if I'm trying to get to work in Manhattan on time. Nice gym for the money though. Ditmars Blvd down in the strip mall in the 40's.
