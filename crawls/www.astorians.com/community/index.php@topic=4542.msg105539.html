Well, if someone else is footing the bill, then that certainly has awesomeness potential. 

Once, I was stranded in Frankfort, Germany over night after a layover went awry.  I was so completely beside myself and upset.  ... Until I realized they were giving me money for food and putting me up in a five-star hotel I could have never afforded on my own dime.  It turned out to be a fabulous night. 
That's how I felt about being stranded in Paris. We were all transported to different hotels, and I was lucky enough to be dropped off at the Saint-James & Albany Hotel in the heart of Paris. I stayed there with the flight crew and 12 other passengers. I had a suite overlooking a magnificent garden. I had never heard of the place, but I probably had never heard of most 5 star establishments. One of the crew rounded us all up and took us on a private tour of HIS city! We ate and drank, courtesy of the airline, and barely slept before we departed to JFK the following evening. The crew was scheduled for a 30+ hour layover in NYC and I returned the favor with a private tour of MY city. Many of us still exchange holiday cards.

I got delayed in Chicago at O'Hare twice. The most horrific experiences of my life. One of the delays was an overnight and we got cots at the airport and cellophane wrapped tuna or bologna sandwiches. I've never flown with Delta since that time! (I won't even mention the conditions of the ladies' facilities at the airport.) And they wouldn't give us our luggage back so we could use some the personal hygiene products we had packed!
