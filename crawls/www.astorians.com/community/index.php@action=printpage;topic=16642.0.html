Title: Freelance Moms Unite! Childcare Swap Group?
Post by: amh821 on November 12, 2009, 03:27:37 PM
Hi all -

I am a freelance Museum educator/artist with a wonderful little 7 and a 1/2 month old boy, and I am in desperate need of working out a safe, dependable childcare situation for him that works with my schedule - which is predictable over the long term, but shifts hourly from week to week. 
Currently I divide our childcare needs between two sitters.  One is working like a charm a couple days a week, the other is becoming less and less reliable.  My husband and I have been searching now for well over six months for a perfect sitter scenario, but to no avail - either the sitters are way too expensive (requesting $15-$25/hr for one child - which I am told is too much even by Manhattan standards), or they are unable to gel with my variable hours.  As I'm sure some of you feel, too, I'd really love to just get settled with sitters and not have to worry about scrambling at the last minute to find a fill-in.

The sitter that really works for us is another mother with a relatively flexible part-time schedule (and we've agreed on a rate we both feel good about at $10/hr), so this got me thinking...I've met several other mothers in the Astoria area that are freelancers and have childcare struggles, too; I wonder if perhaps a childcare swap group fueled exclusively by moms would be the way to go? 

I'm kind of testing the waters here so to speak.  I'm not even sure how this would work - or if schedules would mesh exactly - but I would really love to try and set up a time to meet and chat with anyone who might be interested in this idea.  Unfortunately I neglected to get the contact info of the other freelance mothers I've spoken with in the past, so hopefully this post will get their attention as well, and we can reconnect. 

I look forward to hearing from you, mamas!

Amanda

Post by: litza on October 28, 2010, 03:26:00 PM
Another freelancer here, with a 10-month old boy. Child care has been kind of an adventure; I've had a regular babysitter 3 days a week (whom I found through Sittercity.com) but that does get expensive and doesn't really provide enough hours!

I'm part of the Astoria moms Meetup group (http://www.meetup.com/ASAHMGroup/), which I heartily recommend for networking when you've got a tiny baby! I have been trying to help organize a babysitting co-op through that group (hopefully expanding beyond that group eventually). The main intention has not necessarily been for workday sitting, though there's no reason why that wouldn't be possible. If you're interested, I'll get in touch with you both with any developments.

(One thought: taking care of multiple newborns is probably a lot harder than older babies, since there's just so much holding involved; I was really glad to have one-on-one care until my son was about 5 months old. Now I think it would be pretty easy to take care of him and another child.)
