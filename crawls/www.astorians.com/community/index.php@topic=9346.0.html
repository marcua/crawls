+1 on no such thing as "overtipping."

Having worked through college and after in food service and bartending, I really got a good feel for what it's like and know how great it can feel to get a great tip.

As a server, getting that little bit above 20% could put a smile on my face and make me not feel so ticked at a table of jerkoffs that ticked me off earlier.  That extra buck might not be a bunch to you, but to a server/bartender that has to tip out on that (busboys, barbacks, door guys, etc.), it puts them back into the 20% range.

I always overtip, too.  As stated above, the server would have to be TERRIBLE to get only 20% from me because, even then, I understand that they may be new or just having a bad day.  Granted, it helps to tell the customers and be apologetic; but some people are stubborn.

Some of the best tips I've gotten were when I was doing horribly, but apologized to the customer and they could see that I was getting run like crazy by the other tables or that I just had someone bump into me and spill a tray of drinks all down the front of my shirt.   

I don't know what the server minimum here is but, in TX and FL where I waited tables the most, it was $2.13/hr and I had to tip out 4% of my total sales to busboys and bartenders.  So, even though you were nice and tipped 20%, I was knocked to 16% immediately.  That doesn't even count taxes and the people that don't tip.  The hourly rate definitely doesn't make up for that, either.
