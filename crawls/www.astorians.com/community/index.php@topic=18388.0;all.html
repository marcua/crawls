I can explain everything.  These are feral helicopters you’ve been seeing around the area, particularly at night.  Yes, feral – they’ve broken away from their various TV/Radio affialiates and police/transportation groups to live “ in the wild” blue yonder.  A lot of people don’t know this, but helicopters are nocturnal beings who tend to be more active at night.

Why are they so active around Astoria lately?  There’s an old Greek lady living on 30th Road who has been quietly feeding them barrels of oil every other night.  I’ve seen her, with her nephew, wheeling out the barrels on a hand-lift truck.  The old lady makes a strange whispering, purring sound.  She also has a portable electric can opener which she turns off-and-on.  The sound of this apparently attracts the feral helicopters.  They’ll hover over her backyard, lower a rope harness, which the nephew will attach to the oil barrel, then they’ll fly away a few minutes later.

She has names for each feral helicopter: Felix, Duchess, Leo, Circes and Vallone.  Sure, the neighbors complain about the lights and noise, but if it wasn’t for her, these helicopters might possibly perish on their own, or at least find their existence much more hard-scrabble than it already must be.  So, forgive an old woman her compassion in a world that’s running short on this valuable commodity.  The noise and lights fade, but hopefully her good intentions will remain, even after she’s gone.
