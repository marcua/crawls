Re: wi-fi, laptops at Queens Kickshaw: I asked Ben (one of the owners) how he felt about laptop hanger-outers, and he said, "oh that's fine, we want people to feel comfortable here." And he mentioned that he envisioned the back "communal table" as places where people could work (to that end there are four plugs along back side) and all four other back tables have plugs too. No plugs up front though.

If you're like me (and a freelancer) you don't feel guilty about camping out at local Starbucks, but WOULD feel guilty camping out all day at neighborhood haunts. BUT. Things are a bit dead after about 1:30 at Kickshaw and I have had my pick of tables here the past few days I've worked here.

So I think the moral is, if you bring your laptop, be kind and hang out at communal table, leave other tables for groups to sit on. :)

Just want to add that I had my first meal there today and I was more than impressed. IM IN LOVE.

I hit up the Kickshaw with a friend after yoga last Saturday, you know, so that we could fill the void of any calories that we had burned previously....and I couldn't have been more happy with how I choose to fill my belly.

We both stayed traditional and got the cheddar and mozz sandwich, that's served with a cup of tomato soup.  The bread was toasty, like any good grilled cheese, but amazingly moist and chewy at the same time.  Melted to perfection.  The tomato soup was a wonderful compliment and had a hint of indian spice.  $8 very well spent.  They do cold-brewed iced coffee too.  While it was delicious, I wish it had been bigger for $3.50
