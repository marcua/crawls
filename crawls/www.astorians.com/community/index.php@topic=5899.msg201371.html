I've just moved here myself a few months back, and I live not far from the 30th Street subway ("el"? station), and yes,the neighborhood seems safe enough, although in any major city nothing is completely safe. I am not from NY originally, I am from Chicago, although all in all I have lived in eight other cities all together; so I am not sure where I am from anymore; but in all honesty, this place is not my favorite city. I came here for work, but have no intention on staying past one year (I do not like my bldg., and I am about to talk to my atty. about getting out of my lease as there are violations all over, then I would leave ASAP, and go back to the West Coast), but if that doesn't happen, I'll wait out the months left and leave.
I have lived in San Francisco too; which is not cheap either, it is NY prices, but in this neighborhood you get no where near the value or quality of life as you would in SF.
I have been here six months, but I  basically go to work only and come home, as work eats most of my time so maybe I haven't seen much of the places beyond grocery stores and the like (BTW Trade Fair is a dump) so that might be part of it, but in the very little spare time I have, the people, places here; no thank you. This was a mistake, a big mistake, BTW real estate brokers are low lifes all of them.
Moving is a pain ( I will have moved a total of 6000 miles in two years) ,but I need to go, I am sorry to all who do enjoy it here, but I can't stand it here.
