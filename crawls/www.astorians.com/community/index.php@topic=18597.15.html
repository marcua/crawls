This is obviously a problem residents who live around the Park (myself included) have been having to deal with summer after summer after summer. (http://www.astorians.com/community/index.php?topic=15043.0)

There has to be a policy change of some sort and organizing is the only way to do it. Closing Shore Boulevard to vehicular traffic between midnight and 8:00am, Thursday - Sunday would be a great way to quell the noise, limit the garbage, and most notable to Politicians, improve the Quality of Life to the community.

The noise that has emanated from the Park after dark the past three weeks has been uniquely intolerable, and though my countless call to the 114th (along with those of many in the community) helped to draw attention to the disorder and unlawfully loud 'music' being blasted from Shore Boulevard by those who loiter there at ungodly hours,  we cannot continue to rely on the whim of the police to preserve the peace. We, as a community, MUST organize and become a political force. The Police will only respond to the policy of the administration, and the administration will only respond in fear of losing votes. If we can organize via petition and become a presence at community meetings we can make our voice and our desire for policy change heard.

In the next week I will be drawing up a petition and collecting signatures around the neighborhood to support action on this issue. A digital petition online would also be extremely helpful. I know there are enough of us to invoke change and if Mr. Vallone, Jr. does not want to recognize us and our concerns I am sure we can find someone who will.

If you would like to take active roll with me, I invite you to contact me at JohnQ.Astoria@gmail.com and let me know. In the meantime, please keep phoning the 114th (don't bother with 311, it's a joke) and if you can reach P.O. Surami, she had been especially understanding and helpful in getting patrols to the area. The numbers are below.

Let's keep this thread vital and let's get something real accomplished for our neighborhood.

John Q - that's excellent info and I am totally in for organizing and getting something done. 

I also think Shore Blvd should be closed to vehicles, although I wouldn't mind seeing it closed permanently and making it pedestrian only.  It would cut down on garbage, noise, and huge gatherings of people.  There's still a parking lot for people who want to come and visit the park via car and anyone on foot or bike would be welcome.  I just don't see the need for that street to be a parking strip.  And if the cops are not doing a good job now of stopping them, then how well are they going to enforce a curfew?

I have called the police several times regarding these groups blasting their music.  And we're not just talking one loud car radio.  There is a white pick-up truck whose entire flat bed is filled with huge speakers that blast this music as if it's a concert hall.   The police arrive late, break it up for just a little while until the group sees the coast is clear and then they're right back at it. 

 I think going to a community board meeting is probably the most effective way to get something down.    But as Debbie pointed out, it's only effective if there's a group that bands together and goes.
