Quote from: urrisgirl on July 25, 2008, 01:36:27 PM
Para- you hit a real nerve with this comment. Last summer my husband got hurt playing football. He ended up being out of work for 6 months and having to have surgery to repair his torn ligaments in his shoulder. I fortunately have a stable job & health benefits which is more than a lot of people in this country have. However because my husband was unable to work and my job was unable to cover all our bills it was necessary for him to collect disability in order to keep up with bills and not fall into debt. We were fortunate that he was not permanently injured and eventually able to go back to work- which believe me he did the second he got the ok from the doctor.

My point being is accidents do happen. I hope you never end up having an accident- because trust me it is pretty scary not knowing how you are going to pay your mounting medical bills, rent, etc. It is much more complex than saying "pay your own damn bill".

It is more complex than that, but part of the reason you got what you got is that you and your husband were both working prior to the accident. I have no problem with helping out people when they hit hard times, provided that these are "hard times" and not the way they live perpetually.

When my father was injured, he was in the ICU for a week and then in rehabilitation after that. My parents don't have what most people call insurance, they only have what insurance actually is--stuff for serious injuries. It didn't cover anything and he couldn't work. I left college to take care of the family business. I don't even know if they offer disability for farmers, but my parents didn't get any money then.

I'm not saying it would have been wrong for them to take assistance had it been available, but I am saying that there are lots of ways to work situations out if you're hard working and determined. I don't mind helping out the working people who are struggling. I do mind having systems that allow people to perpetually not work, not pursue their own methods of sustenance and rely on the working citizenry to support them for their entire lives.
