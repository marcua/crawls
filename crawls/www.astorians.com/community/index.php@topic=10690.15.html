Quote from: essen on June 03, 2008, 12:44:03 AM
Well, it supports the authors, right? Do authors get any kind of compensation for their books being taken out of the library?

In the U.S., libraries generally do not pay royalties to authors each time copies of their books are loaned out.

It’s been a minor issue in the industry that occasionally surfaces though I think it’s generally agreed that lending books from libraries is a good thing, much like playing music on the radio – a great promotional tool.

Rather than making libraries and library users pay, many author/writer groups would like to see a method of compensation implemented modeled on what is done in some European countries, which have systems in place where writers/authors are paid a small royalty on behalf of libraries and library users from a fund financed by the government (i.e., taxes) each time someone borrows their books from a library.
