Hello all!

Below is a copy of the agenda for our first meeting.
Thanks to all who attended and participated.
If you were unable to attend our meeting, check out the agenda below, go to our blogspot, or contact one of us.
Thanks!

Let's make this happen!

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Community Garden Interest Meeting

Date: February 5, 2005

Meeting Site: Freeze Peach Café, Astoria, NY

Co-Leaders: Robyn B. (enigmacat@yahoo.com) and Rosemary C. (cabanird@yahoo.com)

Blogspot: http://www.astoriacommunitygarden.blogspot.com


* Welcome!
* Introductions
  Who are we? What kind of garden do we want?
     o How might we fit into the community?
        Vegetable? Flower? Other?
* Resources
     See our blogspot for the research and resources available to us
  + We have established contact with Green Guerrillas
     Robyn plans to attend Green Drinks meeting, see dates below
   o Do you know of any resources?
* ACTION STEPS—What we need to do next…
  o Find a spot!
  o Spread the word about our efforts to other Astorians
  o Connect with other organizations/people who can offer us support, might    be interested
     What can you help out with?
*Upcoming Dates:
   o Our next garden meeting will be February 19, 2005, check blogspot for more details
      Green Drinks meeting on Tuesday, February 8, 2005. "Kabin", 92 Second Avenue (between 5th and 6th sts) New York, NY tel: 212 254 0204 www.greendrinks.org

 Let’s Get Motivated!
Why a community Garden?
   o Many of us rent and don’t have much space, if any, to grow anything!
      Some facts taken from CommunityGarden.org:
      + Gardens create opportunities for people to work together to improve communities in many ways.
      + Studies have shown that for humans, just looking at a plant can reduce stress, fear, and anger, and lower blood pressure and muscle tension.
       + Visiting a small garden can give a person the feeling of “getting away” from stressful places such as work, etc.
          Gardens can bring communities benefits such as, reduced crime, nutritious food, and increased business activity
    o Gardeners themselves gain a sense of accomplishment and success from caring for their own garden.

Feel free to make copies of this agenda and pass it on to anyone who is interested!

Bring some more neighbors to the next meeting!

Most importantly—KEEP IN TOUCH! LET’S MAKE THIS HAPPEN! :)
