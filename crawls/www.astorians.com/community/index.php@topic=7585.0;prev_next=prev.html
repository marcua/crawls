I was rather disappointed with Freeport as well - You do just as well out on Long Island.  Two places in Maine I always stop at is Moody's Diner (http://www.moodysdiner.com) in Waldoboro and The Big Chicken Barn (http://www.bigchickenbarn.com/home.html) between Bucksport and Ellsworth on Route 1.

For a good taste of down-east, Moody's has great home-cooking type dishes and lobster rolls, don't forget the Whoopie Pie - and a bakery on premesis.  Try not to get there during traditional lunchtime, or you'll be on a line out the door.  They also have some really funky cabins up in the woods, up the hill in back of the diner for around $60 a night.  I love it there.

The Big Chicken Barn is pricey but huge, and you can spend a whole day there checking out the antiques.  There is also a second floor dedicated to vintage books, magazines and records.  Although expensive, you can find a good bargain there occasionally.  I bought a non-working Westclox art deco alarm clock there for $14, took it apart and cleaned/oiled it and it keeps perfect time.
