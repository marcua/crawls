I have a friend who has been using one for a couple of years now and has been very satisfied with the connections and service.  I think she uses Vonage but I'm not sure.

I actually want one, but I am not willing to give up my land line.  In cases of severe emergency or a blackout (or whenever your cable goes out), a digital cable-based (VOIP) phone will not work.  There's cell phone backup, of course, but on days like September 11 (don't know if cable was out that day because we didn't have it), the cell phone circuits were jammed, as they were on the day of the larger blackout in 2003.  Land line circuits were also overloaded on September 11, but they cleared more quickly.  In 2006, the Astoria blackout was so long that people's cell phones were dying.  I brought my chargers into work, but it was still a hassle.  Then, my cable/modem went out for a week sometime in the past six months or so.

Does it happen often?  No.  But it does happen often enough, and in situations where I specifically want to know that I have a way of getting in touch with people.
