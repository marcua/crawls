« Reply #62 on: January 21, 2009, 03:54:03 PM »
I joined about three weeks ago. I've been there several times and here is my impression:

1. Yes, this place is definitely no-frills. There's two levels, upstairs is cardio and free weights. The cardio section is impressive, and I mean it. Maybe it's because they're all shoved in one place, but NYSC on Steinway can't compete with this number of machines. There are easily 20+ bikes, 20+ elliptical, and 30 or maybe 40 treadmills (there may be even more of each category, I've never really counted!) But I've been there at their busiest time, and never had to wait (take that, NYSC!) for ANY machine Downstairs has locker rooms (with showers, lockers, and toilets/sinks) and the weight circuts. There are a ton of these as well. They also have the massage chairs and tanning booths downstairs. All is clean so far.

2. Because the machines are new, they are clean. They have a policy of wiping down the machines when you're done (which a lot of gyms DON'T do, and it skeeves me out)

3. In the cardio room there are about 20 TVs mounted on the wall, and you can plug your headphones in to listen to them, not bad

4. The type of people working out at this gym def. seems to be younger (sometimes there are young teens "hanging out" in the basement weight room, kinda bothers me, as they are taking up the machines but not working out)

5. There are not many (or any) trainers just hanging around at this gym compared to NYSC. I believe you can schedule a group workout with a trainer but I've never seen one actually in action

6. There are no classes, no towels, and NO SCALE in the locker room (believe me, I looked!)

All in all, you get what you pay for. I think for $20 a month its an excellent deal, and they make good on the "unlimited guest privileges" too, I have brought my boyfriend for free every time I have gone with no problems!
