Quote from: Christine on March 27, 2008, 10:37:16 AM
FINALLY tried Oleput last night for the first time, and it was terrific! Helps that it was uncrowded.

At first, we wanted a bit of coffee to wind down the evening (well, we were set for a night bike ride, but then got comfy.) Sat down and the BF noticed people drinking beer, so we got that instead. Then another. Then we got hungry. Then we had coffee (decaf).

The beer selection is unique, and we enjoyed the dark German selection. The sandwiches were not cheap but very tasty and again, somewhat unique/ upscale (kinda like what I've eaten @ Sparrow.) BF had the chorizo and I had the grilled cheese. Both came with bitter greens which I love. Coffee was fine.

Best of all was the service. The waiter was friendly and knew the beer well. The woman was also friendly and when we asked about the music, not only did she check on it for us, she asked our opinion of it. That was cool.

So I can fully understand why they'd serve drinks and food rather than just coffee: We were going to spend a few bucks on coffee, maybe a few more on a snack, but ended up spending nearly $50. Which is not something we'd make a habit of- for a few dollars more, we could've had a full-blown meal. But the atmosphere, given all its hipsterness, was definitely welcoming and chill. At least mid-week.
