I've lost count here... With the help of a dining companion yesterday, I made two Chinese dishes. One was only fair, with tofu, CSA bell peppers, and black bean sauce. The other was fantastic, and uses up CSA summer squash in an admirable way! Here's the recipe...

Squash Pancakes (from From the Earth)

  • 2 T roasted unsalted peanuts
  • 1 1/2 c zucchini and summer squash, peeled, sliced into 1/2" rounds, then julienned into 1/4" sticks
  • 1/2 scallion, trimmed, cut into 1/4" rounds
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/2 t sugar
  • 1 T soy sauce
  • 1/2 T Shao-Hsing wine or sherry
  • pinch of white pepper
  • a bit more than 1/4 c flour
  • 3 T peanut oil

1. Crush the peanuts coarsely between sheets of waxed paper.
2. Put the peanuts and all other ingredients aside from oil in a mixing bowl, and mix until batter is smooth and evenly distributed.
3. Heat a non-stick 10" skillet over medium-high heat. Add oil and coat the pan. When quite hot, pour in the batter and spread into a layer, with the egg mixture evenly distributed. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until bottom is lightly browned, shaking the pan occasionally to prevent sticking.
4. Slide the pancake onto a large plate. Cover with another plate the same size. Flip the pancake over and slide back into the pan. Reduce the heat to medium and cook until squash is tender when pierced with a fork.

Serves 4-6.

Admittedly, I do not cook as much of the food of my ancestors as I should... most of it comes down to recipes. I like to make things the way my grandma used to make them (who doesn't?) but a lot of those recipes just weren't transcribed. She passed away earlier this summer and I've been thinking of having a Chinese food party to memorialize her enormous contribution to my personal culinary repetoire... but of course I'll have to practice some of those dishes first.

Some of my favorites are dishes that can't be found on any restaurant menu (unless you're in a Shanghai place and bother the waiter in Mandarin, I find). I don't even know what the english monikers of many of these dishes would be:

meatballs steamed and covered in sticky (pearl) rice
noodles with ground pork and tofu in a black bean/tomato sauce
long beans with pork belly
the Chinese charcuterie (including whole eggs boiled in beef broth, sliced)
pickled mustard greens (with wheat gluten blobs)
fried dough stick with shredded pork tucked into a sesame bing

Lots to figure out and practice!
