Welcome to the neighborhood, Sarah!  I live just down the street from your new apt.  You'll be in the neighborhood of the St Irene Chrisovalantou Greek Orthodox Church, which has unusual and (IMO) wonderful bells at various times during the day.

Definitely avoid Dahn Yoga.  As far as yoga studios, I hear good things about The Yoga Room on Steinway (32-32).

You'll be a block from Trade Fair, which has some interesting things, though odd smells at times.  Top Tomato has ok produce (not great, but decent) and is open 24 hours, which is very cool.  Key Food is over on 31st Street by the train.  Fresh Start on 23rd Ave between 29th and 31st is a good natural foods store.

I go to SPAce (Ditmars by 29th Street) for my haircuts.  I like Edna (I have an appt with her next weekend even).  I went upstairs once, where they do the massages etc, and it's really nice up there.  Like a totally different world from downstairs.

As far as food, I'm sure you'll get lots of opinions.  Favorites in the neighborhood for me are Wave Thai, Golden Chopsticks (mediocre yet better than a lot of Chinese places), Zorbas (for Greek and general diner type food), and Michael Angelos for pizza (get the Sophia Loren).  One interesting place nearby is Balkh Shish Kebab, an Afghani restaurant.     Their dumplings are good, kebabs are tasty, and they do deliver. I also order from Kumo, which makes fine sushi.  I've heard good things about Sakura, though, a new Japanese place.  I think a Brazilian place is opening up on 23rd Ave soon, too.  Oh, and you can't not visit the Sandwich Kings on 23rd Ave near 35th Street.  Total yum.

Oh, there are some good things to be had at Soho, especially the whole wheat pancakes.

I'd recommend avoiding Los Amigos.  I had, um, GI distress last time I went there.  Yuck.  Also be careful of the bird crap bridge on 23rd Ave.  I've not been crapped on... yet.

Freeze Peach is great, and a new coffee place just opened on 31st near the Ditmars station.  They will be open from 6:30am to midnight 7 days a week.  Wifi is coming, apparently, but not there now.   

essen is right, there are a lot of discussions about all of this stuff.  But enjoy the neighborhood!  It's a great part of town.

For a Vet, I like Astoria Veterinary Group, on Steinway just North of the highway. I don't much like the place on Ditmars and roughly 34th St.

I adopted a cat from Francis' Friends. They take good care of their cats, and place this with good foster families. Their adoption contract is the standard one for animal adoption agencies: fascist. You agree not to declaw the cat, not to give the cat to anyone else without the agency's prior permission, and about 300 other limitations. I'm pretty sure there was something in there about their being allowed to use deadly force (against people) if they get even slightly suspicious. It's understandable, but obnoxious.

Meg got most of the other highlights for this neck of the woods! You may have to learn to like seafood, though...! :)

Welcome to the neighborhood!  You'll love living here.
I have seen all the Vets at Astoria Vet Group (one of my 2 cats has a chronic disease, so they basically can build a wing named after him)- they are all great, my personal favorite is Dr. Kamer.  Our other cat (not the sick one) was adopted from Francis Friends.  We had a great experience with them.
I can also recommend SPAce for haircuts & decent color.  I go to Rejuvenate on 23rd ave & 27th (?) for massages and facials, and have been happy with them.  It's a small space, but the massuese is great (Beatrix). Plus I beleive they only use organic products.
I get my fruits and veggie at the stand on 23rd Ave and 29th street- it's smaller than Top Tomato, but I think the quality is a little better.  Fresh Start is great if you're looking for a Natural Foods option.
I like Watawa and Sakura for sushi, Wave Thai and Bangkok Tasty for Thai, Michaelangelos has great pizza, and Zorba's for greek salads and gyros.  We love to go out for breakfast, and can usually be found at Igloo on the weekends for brunch (or Fatty's if we feel like a little something special).
I belong to the Rock, but haven't done a yoga class- I hear good things though.
So many great options here- have fun exploring!
