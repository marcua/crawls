“…this wonderfully gripping dance..." -Robert Johnson/Starledger

ANGST
What is fear? What are we afraid of? Why?

Inspired by a visit in the Holocaust Museum in Berlin, Daniela Hoff explores in her choreography, ANGST (working title) different layers of
fear. There are simple fears, almost just worries, and fears that are deeply rooted, primal fears. Performers are Tomomi Imai, Lize-Lotte Pitlo, Sarah Pope and Daniela Hoff. The music is by Live Footage.






“…performances that draw you right into the path of the movement…” - Anne Pierce, Washington DanceView

This marks the premiere of Appel’s first extended small group work in over twenty years. Consisting of short dances that mesh episodes of set and improvised dancing in intricate and intriguing fashion, the piece unfolds as a paradigm for some of the singular and shared aspects of human interaction revealed through encounters with the invented and intuited ways we structure our lives. Tied together and yet standing alone, unfurling with extreme definition or virtual disrepair, these dances evoke memory and hypothesize about possibilities—but most importantly celebrate our ability to reject rigidity in favor of pliable and vibrant responses within the worlds we engage and re-create. Performers include Cara Heerdt, Ava Heller, Suzanne Thomas, Mindy Upin, and David Appel. (www.youtinydancer.com)
