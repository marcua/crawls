My bf and I went last week with friends so we were able to try several dishes.  Our review:

1.  the service was great - which isn't always the case in a new restaurant.
2.  it's very lovely inside - we REALLY like the atmosphere
3.  the music:  inappropriate and better suited for a 30th avenue pseudo cafe and too loud at times (although it was mainly the 160 bpm that grated on us most)
4.  the food was OK - I'm willing to attribute the lack of "Wowness" to their need to work out kinks but we were just "meh" about the food.  what we had:
            Country Pate - I thought this was one step above Alpo, the bf thought more of Fancy Feast.  To be fair, country pate's can be a little funky.  Would NOT try this again
            Escargot - not bad, not great - and we don't know why, it was just not great
            Foie Gras - a personal favorite of mine and I was PSYCHED that someone in Astoria was finally serving it.  Similar to the Escargot in that it was good, not bad, not great and we don't know why, it just missed the mark somehow.
            Lamb Shank w/Vanilla mashed potatoes - good
            Mixed salad in a tortilla shell - tasty, don't really understand the Brasserie/Tortilla connection but OK
            Hangar Steak w/Frites - OK, not bad, not great - we think we were not wowed by this because we just recently went to Ben & Jacks and that's a hard act to follow with stead, esp. w/ Hangar steak which isn't a great cut of meat to begin with

Overall, I would say give this place a try - we will again.  I have every hope that the food will get better and this is just "opening pains". 

Also:  the bread was AMAZING AMAZING AMAZING.  Several different kinds, all super delish!

Sorry if I'm repeating a thread, but have we talked about Pomme Cafe yet?  This place just opened on Broadway and 38th.  I tried it for brunch on Saturday and it was pretty good.  I think they are still working out the new restaurant kinks, but man, does this place have a ton of potential.  The dinner menu looks to be a half-click above regular Astoria dinner prices but still lower than, say, Brick which is the only other Frenchy place around.  Has anyone tried it for dinner? 

http://pommecafeny.com/Pomme_Cafe/About.html

MagicPie's quick and dirty brunch review:

$14 prix fixe.  Good coffee (bottomless cup), amazing Bloody Mary, delicious little croissants, attentive service but the main courses took a really long time.  My smoked salmon (+$2) benedict was inedibly salty but tasted better when I took the salmon out, eggs were properly poached and the hollandaise was quality.  My partner's rice crispy French toast was delish.  Home fries are perfect.  Side of bacon was neutral, neither good nor bad.

All in all, I'd try it again, but it's too pricey to be the default brunch place.

Re: Pomme Brasserie

I must say, after reading so many negative and mediocre things here and elsewhere, we weren't sure what to expect when we decided to have dinner there last night.  But I'm pleased to say that we're very glad we went.  Our experience was a very positive one and our meal was really very delicious. 

We had two appetizers.  Escargots and the Country Pate.  I always get the pate at a French place.  I happen to like Brick's very much.  So I was curious about Pomme's.  I was impressed and pleased.  I'd get it again for sure. I agree, the texture is not what you'd expect.  It isn't the livery, fatty kind of pate that you most often wind up with.  Not that I mind that.  But this one was light but substantial at the same time.  More meaty but not fatty.  Very flavorful.  I can see why it would disappoint some people.  But I really enjoyed it.  And it was as enjoyable to look at with all the garnishes, as it was to eat.  And pate is not usually a pretty thing.  The escargot were perfect.  Rich and buttery.  Tender and delicious.

We both got a soup.  Me the Creamy Tomato.  My wife the Onion.  Mine was very good.  A complex flavor that I couldn't put my finger on.  Maybe a dash of cognac or brandy.  But I really enjoyed it.  If it were a few degrees hotter, I'd have been happier.  My wife liked the onion soup very much.  And commented how it had just the right amount of cheese without being a molten cheesy mess that it impossible to eat without stretching cheese halfway across the dining room.

I had the Striped Bass served over spaghetti squash and white and fava beans.  It was flavored with a blood orange reduction.  The fish was incredibly fresh and delicate.  Cooked to perfection.  The beans and squash were rich and amazingly flavorful without being heavy and overwhelming.  I was really happy I ordered this dish.  My wife got the duck confit and loved it.  It was served with a bread pudding that she really like with the duck.  For me, it was too much like dessert to go alongside the duck.  But I tasted the duck and it was amazing.  We skipped dessert this time.  But almost everyone else in the entire place seemed to be getting dessert and loving it. 

The place was crowded and I must say, everyone seemed happy. Our server was very good.  Smart and attentive.  There were no mistakes or forgotten items.  And we loved the breads.  All whole grain and multigrain.  There was a bit of a gap for our main entree to arrive but it wasn't an unacceptable wait.   I was listening for strange music but it was so full that I couldn't hear it over the voices.  Candles came out as we were leaving but I didn't smell any scent.  The decor is very nice.  And the chairs are very comfortable.  They fit a lot of people into the space but it isn't cramped.

We dine out a lot and can be very critical.  But I must say, this restaurant served us one of the best meals we've had out in a while.  I'm very happy we tried and we really look forward to going back soon.

Re: Pomme Brasserie
« Reply #26 on: August 16, 2010, 02:33:07 PM »
So my better half and I went there yesterday for our 5 year anniversary, and it was...okay. First, the place looks like a million bucks.  It's a gorgeous space, and I felt like a rich millionaire (a French one at that) just walking in.  And in the entire restaurant, the attention to detail isn't lost, since even the restrooms were gorgeous.
So granted we went in just after brunch on a Sunday, and the entire restaurant was pretty slow, in general, which we didn't mind.  Our server was great, and they kept our water glasses filled (which is important since we ran the crazy spartan race that morning) and the food came quickly.
The food was the biggest letdown, which again, could very well be due to the fact that we were sitting down to eat at 4.30 on a Sunday.  The charcuterie plate was okay, a few slices of prosciutto, and a couple thin sliced meats that seemed to be capicola or sopressata, again, okay, but not as good quality as you'd get from Sorrisso's say. For mains I had the french burger and my better half had the steak au poivres. The fries were great on both ends.  My burger was cooked perfectly, but lacked any trace of salt or pepper. My girlfriend said that she thought she should have ordered the steak with the bearnaise, as she wasn't thrilled with the sauce, but again her meat was cooked perfectly. For dessert we split the mousse, which was nothing to write home about.
All in all, it was a pleasant experience, but afterwards, over drinks at The Quays (the spot of our first date) we discussed how Bistro Les Minots lacked the amazing ambiance of Pommes, but had really terrific food, and how the ambiance at Pommes was terrific, but the food felt like it was lacking a bit, and then we got bummed that bistro was no longer there.
All in all, I would definitely give it another try, probably during the week.
