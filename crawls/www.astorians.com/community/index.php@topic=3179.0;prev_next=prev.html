If your iPod is under warranty, you can get a shipping box sent straight to your house.  Go to depot.info.apple.com/iPod, and setup a repair.  If you do not have Applecare, and have had the iPod for more than 6 months, you will have to pay around 31.00 shipping.  If you have Applecare, it will be free.  Using this method, you will get a box via DHL overnight.  You put your old iPod in the box, and you'll get a refurbished one in about a week.  As far as if Apple keeps batteries in the store, I have no clue.  I doubt it.  They would probably have to send it off just like you, so you might as well do it yourself and not wait in line.  

If it is not under warranty, you might try Tekserve.  If they won't do it, there are a couple of places you can send it:  http://www.brokenipods.com and http://www.ipodresq.com.  Both of those options would require you to send it off, I believe, however there's gotta be a local Macintosh consultant that can help you out.  Sometimes these consultants will replace iPod batteries.  Apple will probably be the most expensive option if the iPod is not under warranty.  If you are not sure about it's warranty status, send me an e-mail with the serial number and I can look it up for you(I currently work at a reseller, and have access to that sort of information).  It is possible there is a location on apple's website that allows you to lookup your warranty status, but I've never seen one.  Let me know if you have any other questions!
