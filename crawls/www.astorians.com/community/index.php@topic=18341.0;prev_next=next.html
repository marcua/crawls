Multi-Vehicle MVA on 36th Ave
« on: May 25, 2010, 01:12:55 AM »
What I saw at approximately 11:40 PM standing in the middle of the block at the laundromat 36th Ave 31st Street.
Black Lincoln livery vehicle traveling east on 36th Ave at 29th Street intersection.  White luxury SUV following too close behind.  Police lights appear to be behind both these two vehicles.  What made me look to my right were the squeeling tires as the Lincoln appears to swerve to the right after possibly being struck from behind, and the suv appears to pivot to the left into the opposite lane to get out from behind the livery to pass it.  You hear more metal crunching as that livery may been the one to rear-end a parked car, but it then definitely climbs the south-east curb at 30th Street and hits a building front door adjacent to a mosque.

It's at this point where your Cannibal finds discression the better part of valor.  Not a good judge of physics or inertia, I duck back into the laundromat three storefronts away from where the livery came to a stop.  By the time the crunching subsides I peak back out of the doorway and find the white suv has side-swipped a parked taxi mid-block (also a smaller suv) which puts a stop to that vehicle.  In the confusion people are exiting both vehicles, but the ones eminating the white luxury ride are being followed by PD.  There's an injured person in the suv that FDNY eventually extricates.

What follows were large contingent of emergency service and other PD, FDNY, and gawking onlookers.  A police vehicle parks past the el station on 31st street, and others report seeing a foot pursuit take place.  Of course, nobody knows what all of this stemmed from.

I experienced all of this in the name of clean laundry.  Sheesh!
