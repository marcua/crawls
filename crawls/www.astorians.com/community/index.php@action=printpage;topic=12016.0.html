Title: Re: Wireless Internet Question
Post by: merm on August 30, 2008, 12:48:22 PM
Those are other people's wireless routers, if you use their internet you are "stealing" their broadband (at least that is what some would say.)

By the way, there is absolutely nothing less secure about surfing over a wireless connection than a wired connection. Both are equally encrypted (since encryption happens in the browser BEFORE it's transmitted through a wire or antenna), and second because if someone is going to snoop there are about a million times more people that could snoop the connection through the "wire" than through your small wireless connection.

Also, I'm not sure what you mean by "phone jacks", are you using dialup internet? If you have broadband, you don't connect to any phone jacks, and there wouldn't be multiple ones in your house. You would connect your computer to an external DSL or cable modem - your computer wouldn't be connected directly to the wall, but to one of those devices, and that device to the wall.

Yes, that is true. In fairness to the computer, it doesn't know which of them might belong to you.

The router broadcasts a name so people who do have reason to know of it will be able to see that it is turned on and in range.
