Regarding Miriams - I've never actually eaten there.  I mentioned it as the only Black owned restaurant in Astoria that I know of, because of the reference to Harlem.  I was also hoping that if anyone else had eaten there they would give their opinion.  I'll try to eat there tomorrow. 

When Miriam's first started setting up shop they never seemed to be open, but whenever I pass by now they are, but not late at night.  I gave the phone number, so anyone interested can just call up and ask what time they're open till.  They certainly don't seem to be a fly-by-night operation - they had their menu printed and offered a coupon in the Yellow Pages or Yellow Book, whichever one we last got.

I've eaten Ethiopian food at a friend's house - it's similiar in spiceyness to Indian food, so if that's the kind of thing your friend is longing for, try an Indo/Pak restaurant.  A big difference is that self-rising flour is used for Ethiopian food, so I would substitute naan for it.

My West African friends also enjoy Indo/Pak food.

It's "outside the pale" (beyond Astoria), but the Q66 will get you there - when a tourist asked a Puerto Rican guy I've seen often on the bus, where's the best place to get Puerto Rican food, he said Caridad, on Northern Blvd.  I justify mentioning this restaurant because one of the great things about living in Astoria is taking the Q66 on a magical trip to other places, and then coming home to  :smitten: Astoria :smitten:

http://new.york.diningguide.com/data/d106369.htm
They're always busy when I pass by on the bus.

The O.P. really will have to more specific with what her friend is missing, as others have mentioned, Harlem covers a big area with a lot of nationalities.

Living next to Astoria Blvd or Hoyt can be a loud ugly mess.  If she doesn't live far enough away from the noise and ugliness of it, I don't blame her for disliking her first taste of Astoria.  You'll need to take her to Astoria Park, all of Ditmars, all of 30 Ave, all of Broadway and maybe even to the movie theatre to counteract the blah that is her backyard.  Then she'll see the real Astoria.   I have a friend who also moved here from the Upper East Side at my encouragement.  She loves her apartment and I think she likes it here sometimes, but on more than one occasion she's told me she doesn't like hanging out here, or that she hates it here, or that she misses the Upper East Side, and usually prefers to hang out in the city.  People like what they like, and if she's truly missing the city, then she'll continue to miss it.  Maybe it'll wear off, maybe it won't.  I think in my friend's case, it's wearing off a little.  Maybe as Broadway picks up, and doesn't have hoodie hangouts like NuBar anymore, she'll feel more safe around her area and it won't be as chaotic.  At first I was hurt she hated it here, but it's really just personal preference.
