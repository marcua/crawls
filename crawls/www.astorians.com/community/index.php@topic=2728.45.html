Quote from: lanseaux on August 21, 2006, 12:06:15 AM
Mezzo mezzo (the phrase) is actually Italian, which has always puzzled me a little.  (It could have a Greek meaning as well, but I'm not aware of it.)  Mezzo means half in Italian, and I do have the impression that mezzo mezzo means so-so in Italian, but I understand Italian better than I can speak it.  Maybe one of the board's resident Italian speakers can tell me how far off the mark I am.   :-)

I don't speak Italian or Greek but I'm quite sure that it's a take off on the root "meze".  In Greek, Armenian, Turkish, Arabic, etc. variations on the word refer to lots of plates of different foods served to the table.  Usually in smaller portions.  Spanish places call this Tapas.

That said, as a person who knows a little something about this kind of food, my impression is that Mezzo Mezzo is the ultimate tourist spot for "Greek" food.  I don't consider it authentic or even that good.  But a cool experience to take friends from out of town who don't know better.  For the real deal, try Agnanti on Ditmars and 19th.  When everyone in the place is speaking Greek and the line is practically down to the river, you know something good is happening.
