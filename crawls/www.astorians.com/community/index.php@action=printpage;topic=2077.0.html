Post by: ddanny999 on March 27, 2007, 11:42:37 PM
Your ears are not genetically different because you are a lifetime New Yorker and claim a greater tolerance for noise.  Noise pollution is not a quality of life issue, it is a human health issue. 

Long term exposure to noise pollution results in reduced hearing, increased stress levels, lost sleep and productivity.  The cacophony of noise that many of us are exposed to is harmful.  Noise pollution is nerve shattering to adults and frightening to young children.

For some people, the levels of noise are so great that they are forced to move from their apartments or suffer in silence.  A few even resort to medications as a means to cope with the noise even though there is nothing wrong with them.

Ten years ago, no one knew what a boom car was, now they are everywhere.  Motorcycles and hot-rods are much louder than they were because the EPA has not enforced noise emissions from aftermarket exhaust systems manufacturers in twenty years.  For all the screaming noise of car alarms, they just don't work - thieves intentionally trip the alarm to mask the sound of breaking glass.

Astoria is flooded with noise and if you live near a busy thoroughfare as many of us do, it is a nightmare.
