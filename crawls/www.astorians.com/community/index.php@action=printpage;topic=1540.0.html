Title: Re: an Astorian in Philadelphia?
Post by: goldfish boy on February 07, 2006, 11:31:59 AM
Where in Center City are you staying?  It would help me make restaurant recommendations.

Yes, Reading Terminal Market is a lot of fun.  Two caveats, though: 1) it's closed on Sunday; and 2) if you're planning to eat lunch there (and you should), you ought to try to see everything first because otherwise you'll finish a delicious meal and two minutes later see another stand that would have offered you an even more delicious meal (depending on your tastes).  Be sure to check out Mueller's chocolates if you go.  It gets reeeeeally crowded and there's a lot of walking, just in case your mom isn't up to that.

It's at 12th and Market.

The Mutter Museum is great too,  but I don't know that it's such a "mom" thing, unless your mom likes to be grossed out.

Museums: If you want to visit museums, here's a guide by subject matter

University (Of Pennsylvania) Museum: archaeology
Academy Of Natural Sciences: geology/biology/paleontology
Franklin Institute: earth and space science
Mutter Museum: medical oddities
Philadelphia Museum Of Art (a.k.a. "th' Art Museum"): the kinds of stuff you would find at the met, plus cheesy Rocky-steps photo ops
Rodin Museum: um, Rodin stuff
Independence National Historical Park: enough colonial/early US history stuff to keep you happily busy for a month even if you don't think you're interested in history

Regarding Capo's voluminous restaurant list: sadly I must inform you that Lombardi's pizza has closed (but then again you can get that in NYC).  And I respectfully have to dissent from the Pat's Steaks recommendation.  Go to Tony Luke's instead.  The old traditional one is way, way down in South Philly on Oregon Avenue, but there's another one called Tony Jr.'s in Center City, on 18th St. near chestnut.  Pat's has kind of become a tourist trap--high prices, smaller sandwiches.  Tony Luke's is the real deal.  (and now you can get that in NYC, too)

I'll say more about restaurants when I find out where you're staying and where you'll be for business.  If you want a beverage after work, I heartily second a couple of capo's recommendations: Monk's (16th St. at Locust) for Belgian ales, and The Bards (Walnut St. btw 20th/21st) for relaxed Irish bar ambiance BUT ONLY IF YOU GO EARLY EVENING BEFORE THE COLLEGE STUDENTS INVADE.)

More later.
