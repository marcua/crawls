"Educator's happy hour".....may I suggest that fine day in June, some kind of Tuesday, probably, when you just realize that you are *truly* done with the semester, and have recovered enough from the trauma of finals to start to remember your self, and then it hits you! that everyone else in the world has to get up and shower and put on a decent mood and go in to The Place where autonomy is expressed solely through expressive coffee cups, and have a full 8-hour work day, whereas you: You don't even have to SHOWER if you don't want.  Because the school year is OVER.

(Happy hour?  Get it?  HAPPY hour?)

At least it beats that *other* kind of summer day, usually about a week later, when you hit the local bookstores/coffee shops/home depots/stapleses to fill out applications because you can't live in a teaching salary.  That's less Happy.  Particularly when you realize you'll be spending the summer working in a hyper-air-conditioned place instead of writing The Book that you know would get you tenure and/or a better teaching job, but then you know you can't work on that The Book right now because you can't.  Afford.  Paper.

But then, maybe that's just more me.  Hm.

Look up Connected Math Project to find out the local distributer - call them up and ask for a sample of the books.  When I've done this in the past they have provided me with all 24 Teacher's editions and one copy of  each of the student editions.  Plenty to dig through for years!  And while you're at it ask for samples of other books - there's lots of free stuff available for your perusal. 

Not that I think you NEED a text book.  But this particular series has AWESOME ideas and lots of materials that you can make fit your needs.
