Post by: lanseaux on July 01, 2008, 09:57:56 AM
If you are making ice cream without a custard base (i.e. without eggs - also called Philadelphia-style ice cream), the end product will freeze extra hard.  The custard emulsion is what gives regular ice cream a more supple texture.  To help with the texture of egg-free ice cream, you can add a tablespoon of vodka or a tablespoon or two of corn syrup to your base.  The vodka, which you cannot taste in the final product, makes it more scoopable, but the texture is still a bit rough.  The corn syrup is really the best (it's what the Brooklyn Ice Cream Factory (http://nymag.com/listings/restaurant/brooklyn-ice-cream02/) uses).  (I'm not talking about high fructose corn syrup here.  I try to avoid all corn syrup myself, but a tablespoon or two in a quart and a half of ice cream really isn't going to hurt.  I think invert sugar also may do the same thing, but I don't know much about it.)
