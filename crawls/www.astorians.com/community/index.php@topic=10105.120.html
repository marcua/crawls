Restaurants that wash their floor while you're there. 

Once I was with a boyfriend in a Chinese sit down restaurant.  They had not said it was close to closing when we sat down.  Then in the middle of our dinner, out comes the amonia.  I work in the afternoon/night and get to my local take-outs at about 10 pm.  I check the menu to see what time closing is, generally 11 pm.  I have made up my mind - if they start washing the floor while I'm deciding what to order, I'm deciding NOT to order.  I walk out.  Once I was sitting at a table to deciding what to get (for take out) and they guy started washing the table while I was sitting there.  I've not yet said to the workers why I'm walking out, but some day they'll put two and two together.

McDonald's is another offender.  I guess someone told them not to use amonia during serving hours, but they do start washing the floor and emptying garbage cans right in front of you. 

By contrast, I was once in a pizzeria when an over-zealous new employee starting washing the wall tiles in the afternoon.  The boss came out and told him to stop.  He told the guy to do it after closing, when the customers are gone.

I keep meaning to write to the city council about this.  In the case of amonia, it's nausiating to smell that while you eat, or while waiting for take out.  Overly floral cleaning products are not better.  If you are sitting and eating, you should not be expected to lift your feet so they can clean under your feet.  And if you're standing, it's dangerous to have to walk across the wet floor.  I thought the City Council would be a good place to take this issue to because it surely must be going on across most of the city.
