As a native Astorian I have to tell you that the noise level has always been the same. From the bikers to the ice cream trucks. It is really part of the Springtime and Summertime charm of the neighborhood. Groups of roving teens blaring their music; car washing with the car doors open and the music blaring; spontaneous foot- stick- baseball games with the local girl cheerleaders screaming; dogs yapping; people who know each other yelling to each other; Moms calling their kids from the windows; the little girls squealing playing hop-scotch or potsy; groups playing handball or stoopball.

Those bikers you see in and around the park today, are the offspring of the last generation who did the exact same thing.

The passing red lights, stop signs . . . well that has always been illegal, and should be reported; but it won't stop it. It happens everywhere.

I love this neighborhood. I also love change. But if you remove the bikers, and the teen groups the change might be that the flavor of the entire area will change forever. I also have to admit that the sound of the ice cream truck makes me smile. It reminds me of hearing it and having to run for my wallet because my children would start their begging. They were really good times for kids. The music alerted them to the treats waiting for them outside. Don't you have things from your childhood that bring back sweet memories of the summers of your past?

Seek out the help of the local precinct to more closely monitor the area around the park—night and day.
