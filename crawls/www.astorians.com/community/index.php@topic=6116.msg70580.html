The steps are alongside the ramp on the Hoyt Ave. North side around 27th St. You can ride along the path, just pay attention to the spray-painted warnings as you approach the steps. Don't worry, they're usually in orange and are definitely not subtle (usually they read "STEPS!!!! DISMOUNT!!!!")

There's a rail set up on the steps where you can position the wheels of the bike and push it up. It's clumsy but helpful!

In a couple of years, there's going to be a big ol' water park there. Be careful what you wish for.....!

At the moment, there's a men's rehabilitation facility (jail?) and a fun (if seedy) pedestrian bridge across to Manhattan.

By riding over the Triboro itself into Manhattan, you can ride across 118th/119th where the bike paths are, over to St. Nicholas Ave. where there's a very popular bike path.

Up St. Nick's to the Harlem River Greenway (very busy crossing) to Dyckman St., where the new Highbridge bike trails are located.

Or, continue past Ft. George Hill and over to Broadway, make a right and ride up to da Bronx where Van Cortlandt Park is.

OR, cross over Broadway and make a left....Cloisters are around there somewhere. Continue southwest for the Greenway that takes you (almost) all around NYC.

Possibilities are endless  :lol:

Re: Anyone walked to Randall's Island
« Reply #10 on: April 25, 2007, 10:41:26 AM »
Get off Astoria Blvd. at the Neptune Diner side. Walk toward the bridge, just past the car entrance ramp- steps should be easily visible.

Great little walk, I think the path from Astoria to Randall's is like 1-1/2 miles.

From Randall's, there are three choices:

- Link to ped path to NYC
- Link to ped path to the Bronx
- Pedestrian bridge to NYC (96th St. area & river walk.)

Those connecting links are tricky to find, and you have to be careful on the road between the paths. Once you do it, though, it's easy.
