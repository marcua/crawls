Since others have addressed the factual error in the post, I want to address the tone of the post.

Simply put, no one here, that I am aware of, has accused the Senator of condoning, supporting, establishing or perpetuating Anti interracial marriage laws. That has never been the point of the argument.

The point that we are making is that many arguments against same-sex marriages MIRROR the same arguments of those that opposed the mixing of races. I want to take a moment today to address one specific argument.

Unnatural. That is the way our society and our government considered interracial marriages. The mixing of the races was unnatural. Simply put, The Whites of the nation were superior and must never be mixed with Negro blood (A term they would have used).

Now fast forward to today. The union of two people of the same sex is considered unnatural by some. And, according to the latest NY state poll, now it is less than a majority. 53% of New York supports gay marriage. There are two ways to look at the "Unnatural" argument. Physical and emotional. Physically, I am attracted to another man. Is it natural? I can only answer that in this sense. There are studies that show that homosexual activity occurs in the natural world. Emotionally, I have already addressed my love for the man I live with in this thread. And quite frankly, I am uncertain of how to defend my love for him. It is not something I really intended or thought could happen. But, it is real. More real than some could imagine.

What made me the way I am? What made me fall in love with another man? I don't know how to answer that. For me, It simply what is.

What does this have to do with me seeking the right to marry him? Simply put, I want to provide and protect him. And all I am asking for is the ability to do this before the State of New York.
