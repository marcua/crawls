"W" Stands for Worst!
« on: July 30, 2008, 06:34:20 AM »
So there was Never (N), the Rarely (R), and now, the Worst (W)!  According to a new report by the Straphangers Campaign, the W came in last.  The link to the AM NY site is below, along with the relevant paragraph.  The link to the Straphangers report appears below the link.

http://www.amny.com/news/local/transportation/subway/am-subway0730,0,4847785.story

"Besides giving an overall snapshot, the report ranks the lines individually. For the third consecutive year the W train came in last. The W has the longest scheduled wait at 10 minutes, has one of the worst breakdown rates at every 87,000 miles, and shows up on time just 84% of the time."

http://www.straphangers.org/statesub08/

Seriously even with all the bottle necking... i would be ok with a few more express trains in the morning stopping at Ditmars, Astoria, and Broadway.... sorry for everyone else but it would make the trains flow a little better.

Bottle necking means that only a certain number of trains can go through a particular stretch of track during a particular period of time.  With the tunnel at capacity, all you'll be doing with the express trains is have them zip along to the 60th street tunnel where they will have to wait and que for a chance to go through.  I'd give odds that your transit time shall not improve. 

The real solution is to build another subway connection between Queens and the city.  A cheaper solution would be to upgrade the singalling system which might be able to squeeze an additional few trains per hour through the tunnel.
