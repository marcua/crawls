Quote from: stringpicker on March 08, 2009, 12:54:57 PM
I know people who are paying $1,200 for a two bedroom close to Broadway. I'm not saying that's the standard price, but I suppose $1,000 should be good enough for a studio.

Alas, it's not.

Although my understanding is that real estate sales prices are modestly down here in Astoria, I've yet to hear anything about rents decreasing yet (except for high-end stuff in Manhattan and LIC, which is down drastically).

Unless you know somebody, or are very lucky, your best bet really is to go with a broker. Many landlords here have never even heard of this new-fangled "internet", and they exclusively work with brokers. Also, you may get a somewhat lower rent with a broker than with a direct rental, because the broker screens people for the property owners, reducing their risk a bit, and their time commitment a lot. Your only other real alternative is to come out here some day and bicycle around all day, looking for For Rent signs. But, again, most landlords don't publicly post signs here. Good luck.

Hi, i have looked around astoria for friends who were searching for apts and werent from the area and i think its pretty much safe to say that rents are pretty much on the high side here /.  For $1000 a month a 1 bedroom might be out of the question  and even a large studio might be on the tight side .  Depending on how flexable u are with that price and range , u might be able to find something a couple of hundred dollars up .  I dont mean to discourage you , u never know u may find something , but if you are looking at something on the newer side and with totally new baths(2nd highest selling and renting point after a newer kitchen )  landlords will charge more  than 1k and the reason being is because they can .  Demand for astoria is very high these days and with less and less people buying , renting is the only option .   I know one of my friends just rented a studio on broadway and 47th street for 1200 a month but its not that big and not that  close to the train she wanted to be near (n or w train) . I know another person that rents a basement apt with a small window for $  950 a month  but she is close to the train and its very safe being its in a private house instead of an apt building.  Small window is the downside . If u are willing to be a little further from the train  u may find something closer to your price range but like i said astoria prices have really gone up  and dont look to come down anytime soon even in this crazy economy .   Any other places u might be interested in ? jackson heights , east elmhurst  , woodside  and sunnyside are all pretty close by and u may find rents closer to your liking .

Well those above areas all have their good and bad i guess  , just like most places do .  Jackson heights used to have a really bad rep , and recently in the last couple of yrs  has become alot safer as they added police patrols in the scetchy parts . It is a busy area for the most part and alot of bars and 24 hr delis and stores.It has a very large hispanice population  , and some people fit in while others might not . Woodside is alot of irish /german decent with alot of hispanics also in the last dozen yrs  having moved in .  Rents are lower than Astoria and there are alot of stores around.  Again depending on the area , u might have a little caution walking home at night alone . Sunnyside is close to Astoria and might be your best bet from the 3 above . I say this cuz the trains to manhattan run well and there are alot of bars and pubs around . Its a busy area and people always seem to be out and abt .  East elmhurst is also similiar to Sunnyside as far as safety goes but u wont have as many bars/24 hour stores and night life around . One area in East elmhurst that i would look into is called the "upper ditmars" area.  Its a very quiet and safe spot located right on the border of astoria/east elmhurst (some people still call it Astoria) and it has some of the newer and larger apts .There are stores/supermarket/deli/cleaners/salons etc all nearby but they wont be open 24 hours . It is very residential and u wont have people hanging out drunk or alot of noise at 4am as people pile out of bars and clubs . A short distance away by car or bus u can go to bars or find nightlife .  i guess it all depends on what u want ,  but just be aware that if u do find something next to everything , chances r everyone else will be there too , and spring time with windows open at night it can be hard to sleep with the noise . For me i prefer to be  a little away from everything , but near enough by car or a nice walk .
