I just want to add that it's a good idea for everyone to take inventory of their wallet.  Make a list of every credit card number and the 800 customer service number.  Your driver's license number.  Everything in there that can be cancelled and/or replaced.  And if it is irreplaceable, then ask yourself if it really belongs in your wallet.  Forget theft as in credit cards - but if there is anything in your wallet that would make you uncomfortable if it wound up in the hands of a stranger, then maybe you should leave those items home.  I always did this, but I'm pretty neurotic.  I dropped my wallet in a cab in South America.  When I realized it, I actually had a list of all the contents and all my credit card information.  And written in a fairly unusual foreign language as a second level of security.  In 15 minutes I was able to pick up the phone in Santiago, Chile and cancel all my cards and have AMEX checks waiting for me down the block.  Of course my travel companion was outwardly impressed but inwardly freaked out, probably.

Thanks for sharing this awful story.  We often go out for drinks and stroll home like it's Mayberry.  I guess the increase in prices of certain items has made some people desperate.  Sadly we'll all have to watch our backs.  And after late night cocktails, spring for a cab for safety sake.  Spend a few extra bucks to avoid the bum who will harm you to get a few bucks.

I know I am late to the party on this one, but I am glad that Para and her better half are ok.  I have to admit that I have this fantasy scenario where some tries something like that on me, and I dispatch them terribly and swiftly.  But I know that in a real life scenario, it's different and it doesn't always do down the way it does in your head.

It makes me really mad when things like this happen in my town.  And as someone who is constantly harping on quality of life nonsense like the bums who harass people and ask for change on Broadway, or the uptick in vandalism, I am extra disgusted.  I see a connection between petty street crime like this and QOL violations.

Some of you have said that price increases and economic hardship help to explain this.  That may be so, but it does not justify it or mitigate it.  By brushing it as people falling on hard times, it's like saying that these thieves are otherwise ok people.  They're not.  Good people get second jobs in hard time.  Criminal scumbags steal from people.  Quite frankly, they would do so without economic hardship.  Eventually, someone around here will defend themselves, terminally.  And I will have zero sympathy for whatever petty thief gets it.
