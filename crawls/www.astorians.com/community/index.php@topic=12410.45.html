I just wanted to take a moment to clarify some confusion about Council Member Vallone's response to Grant's posting on Queens Crap.  The Council Member did indeed reply to what was an open letter to him on Queens Crap from Mr. Brandeis.  In his letter to Council Member Vallone Mr. Brandeis referred to Astoria as a place that was slowly turning into a ghetto, a statement that Mr. Vallone obviously does not agree with.  In his response to Mr. Brandeis Council Member Vallone simply stated:
 
"I have spent virtually every day of my life in Astoria and could not disagree more with you about your assessment of our beautiful neighborhood as "becoming a ghetto". If you really feel that way, you should move into some non-ghetto area and make room for one of the many who are clamoring to live here."
 
It is clear the Council Member obviously took umbrage with this characterization of his district and home. The above comment is one of indignation, making the point that Astoria can't be so bad if so many people are moving here. It was intended to be more ironic than accusatory. However, while he sees the ghetto remark as a gross misrepresentation of the truth, it is not to say that Astoria does not have its problems. In fact, the Council Member works hard every day to correct these problems by working with constituents and government officials. He takes pride that this work, and his father's before him, which have actually managed to keep Astoria one of the most desirable communities in the entire city. Some things are beyond the control of any City Council Member, but Peter will stake his record against any other representative in the city.
 
To that end, the Council Member will be holding a rally this weekend to fight one of the most pressing problems in the area, the threat of air pollution caused by the many power plants here. The state has approved a new power plant to be built along 20th Ave., making it the 6th, and he is calling upon the community to voice their opposition to this proposal. We ask everyone who cares about Astoria to make their opinions heard, not just on this message board, but to every New Yorker, that we will not stand for this. We need your help to make this community the best it can be. We hope to see you there.

http://www.awqinc.com/h2s.html

Hydrogen sulfide is a naturally occurring gas that causes an odor that can be described as anything from rotten eggs to that of tidal flats at low tide.
   The gas can be generated in a number of different ways. Decaying organic matter, like that which forms coal or peat moss, can create this gas. A second way to generate this gas is from the reaction of acidic water with aquifer that has a sulfur content. The third way is from the by products of bacteria that use iron or manganese as part of their diet. These bacteria are sometimes referred to as iron bacteria.


http://www.armofthesea.info/flora_fauna/ff_kingdomspp/monera.htm

The heavier decomposing organic materials drop to the bottom of the water column and end up within the sediments. You have probably been aware of their decomposition if you have ever smelled a rotten egg aroma near the tidal flats and the marshes at low tide. This is hydrogen sulfide, a by-product of the decomposition process in sediments with little or no oxygen. To live in such an environment decomposer bacteria must have anaerobic metabolisms to digest the organic material. In the same way we breathe oxygen and exhale carbon dioxide, anaerobic decomposers inhale sulfate and exhale this hydrogen sulfide.


In regard to local waters: The low-tide smell is due to the decomposition of organic matter. This is scientific FACT.

It’s particularly bad in Flushing Bay because of pollution, including run-off and that the airport runways restrict the natural tidal action that would ‘flush’ out toxins.

The sewage treatment plants, at Bowery Bay and on Ward's Island across from Astoria Park, add to the smell, but as you say they've been there for more than 70 years and it's something residents of Ditmars, and Ditmars is the main area of Astoria that's affected, either have adjusted to or have to adjust to because s___ stinks, even when it's being treated.

The recent problems at the Bowery Bay plant, which intensified the odor, have been fix (supposedly).
