« Reply #5 on: June 09, 2008, 03:06:40 PM »
First use Consumer Reports online to determine what size you need (very important, bigger is not always better), which products they recommend, and the going prices.  For instance, I used them and found out that some ac's direct air better to center, left, or right.  Then I looked for places selling what I wanted, called up PC Richards and bought 2 (different sizes) sight unseen, based on the CR recommendations.  PC didn't have them at the Astoria store so they said I could have next day delivery, free.  Installation would have cost more, though.

Some small ac's can run on regular electrical current (plug in anywhere), and some need a heavy duty outlet.  (Mine are one of each.)  I bought LG's and have been very happy with them.  I don't know if all ac's now come with remote controls, but both of mine did and it's a wonderful feature. 

AC's are an expensive item and the chain stores can buy in bulk and sell for less, so it's one time when you probably wouldn't go to a Mom & Pop.  Another thing about small places, sometimes they sell "refurbished" ac's.  The first ac that my husband bought looked brand new in a factory sealed carton, but looking closer I saw the word "refurbished" or something like that, and even the factory couldn't or wouldn't tell me what exactly it meant in this instance.

Also, I don't know if all ac's are now made with lift out screens, but my LG's have them and they are a cinch to wash!
