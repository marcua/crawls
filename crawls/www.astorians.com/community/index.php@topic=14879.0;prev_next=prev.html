there use to be one.   the guy lived in clutter, holes in the floor, rodents crawling around, lived in darkness, the lights never worked. i don't know if he had running water.  the door was halfway open it couldn't close at all. you'd see a candle burning in the window.  everyone called him joe the rat.  i think his name was willard but i'm not sure.  he would be walking around talking to people, very nice but he just lived the way like that.  people checked up on if they didn't see him.  he's in a nursing home the last i heard, he wound up there after a neighbor didn't see him for a few days and went into the house and found him passed out on the floor.   this is going back about 10 years maybe, and now the house is fixed up with new people living there.

it was like the movie grey gardens for REAL!   i guess to someone randomly walking by it was scary but it was sad to everyone who lived nearby.  :-(

Back in the early 90s there used to be one on 38th Street right off 30th Avenue next to the Astoria Federal parking lot. I shouldn't call it "spooky" per se - it was more like a crack house type place - with disreputable looking guys outside at all hours. The house itself creepy as hell - wood frame in terrible repair - tattered blinds, etc. etc. One day it got knocked down and a nice brick house went up....which is still there today. It was a relief for that horror house to be gone - does anyone else remember this Tobacco Road-looking house?
