Ike Turner was a rhythm and blues & rock and roll pioneer. He started with Ike Turner's Kings of Rhythm, played piano on the first rock/blues record to be a hit in 1951 titled Rocket 88 with Jackie Brenston at Sun Studios in Memphis, preceeding Elvis by 3 years. Actually, the New York Post proudly considers itself to be a tabloid.  Tabloid just means a condensed newspaper, with sensational reporting.  "Tabloids" aren't necessarily exclusive to the kind of papers that feature stories about Elvis being alive and are sold in the aisle of the supermarket.  And the pages read like a book, rather than the "broadsheet" format favored by the New York Times, for example.  The Post is also America's oldest newspaper. 

I find the Post to be a great paper.  The local coverage is second to none, and they are much more up front about their political slant, instead of trying to bury it within ostensibly objective journalism.  While the stories are sensational, you actually learn the key aspects of the news.  So what if you're entertained while doing it?

If I want a paper with stylistic quality, and lengthy articles about sometimes interesting and arcane subjects, I will go to the NY Times.  But for a quick read on the news of the day, I go to the Post.

I wouldn't lose interest in someone for reading the Post.  But I would lose interest in someone who spent three hours a day reading papers when they should be working.   :evil:
