I did a little research on this topic.  Make sure the wood frame is "kiln-dried".  This means it won't warp - an indicator of a good-quality couch, usually.  Pottery Barn has very good quality stuff.  I have a Pottery Barn couch and did a lot of shopping around for quality.  The only problem is that even a drip of water makes a mark on the fabric that I selected - very annoying!  A lot of IKEA's stuff, while cute, is not good-quality.  Their wood is not kiln-dried (last time I checked) and they use cheap foam for cushions that can flatten after a while.  All-down cushions can also completely flatten and look pretty sad after a while.  Supposedly the best kind of cushion is one with a medium-density core that is surrounded in down.  Hope these suggestions help!
