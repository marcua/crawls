So tonight we went to Mojave to celebrate my hubby finishing the NJ Bar today.  As we are eating a table of three adults and two children are seated across from us.  As soon as they are settled the mother figure whips out a portable DVD player and places it in front of the two children and they proceed to watch a movie the entire meal while the parent's drink and visit.  I know that times are a lot different from when I was a child "way back when" but I would never think it appropriate to do this.

Why so quick to judge these parents from this one encounter?  Meaning perhaps most nights they have a quality family dinner without electronic diversions, but, as it happens, that night because of something we are far from aware of it was a DVD player at Mohave.  I'm sure every single family has isolated moments when if someone was looking on would think to themselves "wow, what a disfunctional family."  I've been with cousins, for example, who since the time they were little were very unbratty.  But on occasion would stand screaming & crying in a store yelling "if you don't get this for me, I'll hate you forever."  My grown-up cousin, their mother's way to handle these occasional outbursts were to completely ignore them (to not give such behavior power).  This worked, I'll tell you, and these kids are now polite, non-materialistic, non-bratty teenagers.  But someone looking on at those moments would probably say "I don't get it, what brats these kids are."  So, unless we see something obviously abusive, let's not judge parents for there parenting choices (unless we really know the full extent of their parenting).

Wow - this has turned out to be an interesting dialogue! 

To AstoriaMommy - you are correct, I do not have children of my own. However, I do have a godson, and several nieces and nephew, brothers and sisters.  Growing up were were taught that dinner was time for the family to come together to talk about their day, and be together.  TV's, radios, etc were turned off when it was time to come to dinner -even the phone went unanswered.  As an adult I've had the wonderful opportunity of seeing two of my three siblings raise their children.  When we all go to dinner the children are included in the meal as much as the adults and not left out of everything.  Granted, not all children act the same and I've seen the same thing within my own family.

I did not start this thread as a personal attack on this family.  The whole situation, to say the least, really threw me for a loop.  You are all correct in that I have no idea what goes on in this family's life outside of the few moments we were in the same space at Mojave last night.  However, it also didn't seem to surprise the two kids in the least when the DVD player was presented to them (and they appeared to be well behaved in general).  The thing that bothered me though is that the three adults were so caught up in their own conversation, ordering drinks etc that they really weren't paying one iota of attention to the two children. 

Anyhow,  I certainly did not mean to offend anyone out there as people do have their own styles for rearing children.  If we were to ever have kids I will likely follow in my brother's footsteps and treat the child as if they are on the same level as everyone else around them - regardless of age.
