Any day of the year is a good brunch day for me except July 30 & 31.

Brunchaholics (these days it sounds better than Brunchofiles), when I said I hadn't been to brunch in 25 years I meant that once (and only once), about 25 years ago I was invited by my younger brother to brunch, at brunchtime, in a restaurant serving brunch from a brunch menu, so strictly speaking, I've only had brunch once, about 25 years ago.  But that isn't exactly the only time I've been to brunch.  First of all, when I was a little kid, if my family had a bizarrely lazy sleep in day my mother would cook brunch, even linner.  But for the past 25 years a friend of mine has had a party every July 30th and I've been to most of them.  The party ends when the last guest falls down or walks out the door.  That would be me, walking out the door, coming home to Queens.  The custom arrose of whoever could stand up early enough the next day would go to brunch - originally it was dim sum, so in the classic Euro-American sense of the word it was not brunch, not being a brunch menu.  One day I realized that it was better to wait for daylight to come home from the party because it would take a fraction of the time that it takes at 3 AM.  These past two years other people have stayed up or woken up and kept the conversation going late enough, or early enough, that we've just gone out to breakfast, and simply fallen down later.  It may be too soon to see if breakfast is the new brunch for us, but, during the brunch years, I have sort of been to brunch.  But never in my own neighborhood, so I'm still a virgin.
