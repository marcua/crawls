"Mr. Dodd, who spoke on the floor for more than 20 hours in recent weeks in an effort to stall the bill, said future generations would view the vote as a test of whether the country heeds “the rule of law or the rule of men.”"

"Among the presidential contenders, Senator John McCain, Republican of Arizona, voted in favor of the final measure, while the two Democrats, Senator Barack Obama of Illinois and Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton of New York, did not vote. Mr. Obama did oppose immunity on a key earlier motion to end debate. Mrs. Clinton, campaigning in Texas, issued a statement saying she would have voted to oppose the final measure."

So, Dodd frames the issue correctly, recognizes what is at stake, and mounts a serious effort to oppose it. But Obama and Clinton were missing in action. F--- them!!!  And, of course, McCain did manage to vote for this law, which ought to provoke the press to ask him, "Excuse us, Senator, but why are you eviscerating the Constitution that soldiers are presumably sworn to defend?" McCain, what an ass.

But Obama and Clinton are supposed to be the kinds of leaders who want to protect our civil liberties, right? And with a Democratically controlled Sentate, and with all of the press focusing on them, one or both of them could have drawn national attention to this issue and maybe even turned the tide against this law. But not only did they not fight it, Obama and Clinton didn't even vote. SHAME ON THEM!

So we live in a world where, when Republicans control Congress they push through massive expansions of fiscally irresponsible entitlement programs and corporate welfare, and when Democrats control Congress they rubber-stamp laws that immunize corporations from lawsuits arising from their assistance in violating civil liberties of people being wiretapped by the State without warrants.

Nightmarish.

I repeat: it is not republicans versus democrats, it's professional politicians versus citizens, versus you and me.

Warrantless wiretapping? Nightmarish.
Immunizing corporations that comply with government trespasses against citizens' individual liberties? Nightmarish.

This government didn't need these powers to prevent 9/11 from happening. They had the intelligence they needed. There were even rules against passengers paying in cash for one-way tickets at the gate which should have prevented the evilmeanies from getting on the planes the way they did.

I'm not opposed to wiretapping. Just to the process whereby it happens in lieu of any checks and balances designed to ensure that our government, which is only instituted to protect individual liberty, remains limited by that principle. I would understand if logistical changes need to be made to accelerate a review of wiretapping requests. But there's still a correct order; warrants should be granted before not after wiretapping commences.
