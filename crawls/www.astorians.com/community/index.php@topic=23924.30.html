Many people here are confusing waiter service with delivery service.

It is widely known in New York that for small orders delivered, tipping $2  should suffice (10% of a 20$ order). But, for a larger order with several items of food, tipping $4 or $5 should cover it. If the delivery person had to weather rain or snow to get to you, being extra generous is always good.

Now for waiter service, it's a different story. Wait staff at restaurants expect 15%   to 20% gratuity on the total bill. It's also written on the bill.

Here's a few good sources for all those wondering:
1- New York City Tipping Guide.
2- New York Trip Advisor
3- CNN - How much to tip

Now if you want to brag about giving delivery boys 5$ and more on a 20$ order, than that's good for you, but don't go ahead and call people stingy or whatever negative comment. If the delivery boy is giving you an attitude, then tell him off and don't even order from there. Or just simply call up the place anonymously and complain. You shouldn't feel obligated to pay delivery boys equal to what waiters are getting.
