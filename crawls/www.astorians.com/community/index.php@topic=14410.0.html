Garbage bags found dumped on my sidewalk
« on: March 24, 2009, 11:11:12 PM »
As I went out tonight to take out the garbage for tomorrow's pickup, I noticed 6 large garbage bags and a TV dumped on my sidewalk. Why would someone dump their garbage on another person's sidewalk? The bags contain twigs and branches and dirt, apparently someone cleaned the garden and decided to dump their trash in front of my house. I'm concerned that I may get a ticket since I don't know if there's also recycleable garbage inside.

My question is, how responsible am I for this, and is there anyway I complain about it? This is not the first time people have dumped their trash on my sidewalk.

I've dealt with this problem for years. There are people who commute from the suburbs into the city and park their cars here on the street, and then take the subway into Manhattan. Some of them will bring their trash too and leave out. But I've caught local long-time residents dumping trash at my place just because it's more convenient than theirs.

Yes, you will get tickets. You can complain about "illegal trash dumping" to 311, but it doesn't do anything. You'll still get tickets, and you'll have to pay them because according to Dept of Sanitation - you are responsible.

I've dealt with this sporadically with my landlord's property, a dimly-lit corner lot.  On top of the graffiti on her cinderblock wall running along that side street.  And the dog (and sometimes people) droppings.  And the abnormally large amount of crap people throw on the ground, being next to a schoolyard and near a nightclub.

Bottom line, she's held responsible for anything anyone leaves on her property, so I get on top of these things ASAP to keep in her good graces and help out.  She's had the exact same problem numerous times with construction debris being dumped on her property -- I'm talking heavy-duty garbage bags filled with 75-100 lbs. of broken bathroom tile and such.  In that case, I know it was her neighbor, as he eventually left the discarded toilet that was sitting in front of his house two weeks earlier.  (He had been refurbishing one of the apartments in his house.)  But I let her hash that one out -- it's my understanding the D-bag has lied to her face about this, but I stay out of it.  Obviouisly, I'd beat the guy like a red-haired stepchild if I could get away with it, and the thought crosses my mind routinely.

Call 3-1-1, immediately, when it happens, and pitch a fit.  Make clear that someone has illegally dumped garbage on your property -- raise hell.  If you just meekly call in and report it, chances are you could get ticketed.  And one of your neighbors just might call it in on you -- this has happened a few times with my landlord.  We've learned -- get on the horn immediately and make abundantly clear what has happened.  That's the only way you can beat this rap, unless you pick the stuff up and dump it elsewhere yourself.  I don't care if it's locals or suburban folks doing this - it's a lousy thing to do, and in this case, to a 70+ year old woman.

Believe me, I know how angry you are right now!  I've recently been doing the graffiti dance with the neighborhood artistes, and you can find me some mornings at 6:30 am, out there with my brush, covering over their previous night's masterpieces.  Bad rash for about two weeks there, but I think they're getting the picture that I have more paint, more money to burn and am not doing anything illegal.  You learn some nasty things about human nature when you help maintain a property in an urban neighborhood.
