I live within a block of Crescent and Vine and have not been able to stop in until last night. There had been a couple attempts prior, but it had been so crowded, it wasn't the type of experience I wanted. Last night was perfect, with only a few patrons standing at the bar and only myself and my boyfriend at a two seater table. The owner(?) told us the deal right away, with olives in hand, and I ordered a rose wine which was pretty nice for around $8. It came in a mini caraffe, so it was about two glasses of wine. My boyfriend also had the wine and a C&V panini, which he said he liked. If I remember correctly it contained gouda, arugula and olive tapanade. I ordered a plate of chorizo, prosciutto and gruyere cheese. It was a much bigger plate than I was expecting for $6 with three pieces of flakey bread.

To give my review some context, I had recently been to an amazing undiscovered fromagerie on 51st and 9th called Casellula where I had 2 glasses of wine paired with 3 cheeses (artisanal cheeses from Switzerland, France and Egypt) with a friend. The cheese portions were very small, yet amazing. Anyway, the bill, for two, came to $72. My sweet little outing last night- $32. Very much worth the price and the music was not too over-bearing.

All in all, I really enjoyed my experience and although it isn't necessarily a fromagerie, there is a cheese and meat selection where you can order 3 (meat or cheese) for $6 and have a very nice assortment. I'm really happy that it's there and having been to Casellula, I feel like Crescent and Vine is really doing a good job for what you spend there.

I went there again sometime last week and it was cool, the guy (perhaps the owner) sporting a sports coat, funky tshirt and sneaks was nice and friendly though kind of pushy.  I tried their Riesling which was yummy and we had some bruschetta and a meat and cheese plate, all were awesome, light and yummy. 

I did also gave Fattys a second try and it was awesome, man their chicken with avocado sandwich and maduros were off the hook, as was their mojito.  Both are cute places over all.  I think there is more than plenty of "your average pubs" in the neighborhood, yet what we seriously lack is good lounges, for a more art/bohemian crowd, with space, plush couches and nice art along with killer Djs    Alot of these places are very tiny and there are not enough drink specials around, at least by Ditmars.  While the food at 718 sucks, I still have to say that their 2 for 1 M-Thurs till midnight, is a great deal all around. 

I will be going on another "scoping visit" in Astoria today, check out any spots along steinway maybe that have potential, any suggestions?
