Title: ASTORIA PARK VIGIL A COMPLETE SUCCESS!!!!!!!!!!!!
Post by: zeno on August 29, 2005, 02:17:40 AM
I must admit that being new to the excercise of civil disobedience and peaceful protest as outlined in the Bill of Rights that I was a little nervous about showing up in a public park late at night with candles and pit-bulls.

Was it a coincidence that the Vigil was set to end at 11:59 pm, exactly one minute before midnight? What would happen at that dreaded hour? Was there some clause in the law books in Rome about candles (read: open flame) that punishes such indiscretions in ways too horrible to mention? And what time does this park close anyway? If I overstayed the dreaded minute would my peaceful vigil be instantly transformed into trespassing or criminal mischief by the armies of peace officers and private security agents who would be hired to crack down on the protest?

My heart litterly quivered in trepidation as I contemplated the Rave in Utah where helicopters descended while military men in camoflage made short work of those dangerous teenagers and dancers, tackling them and screaming them into submission.

And what about the other protesters? Would they look on me unkindly when I tried to give them a flyer concerning the September 24th March on Washington to end the War on Iraq? Would screaming arguments ensue and more importantly, would I get bit?

My, my, between the stealth helicopters, the armies of cops, cannibalistic
leftists, pit-bulls, snipers, angry townsfolk, neo-cons, tear gas, plastic
bullets, real bullets in M16s, sonic weaponry and open flames I was
really in for it. No surer way on the path to destruction a direction was headed I.

Luckily, my fears were unfounded. Here are the minutes of the action:

23.12: I arrived late to the protest, but didn't see anyone there at the risers, so I walked around to the other side of the track just in case there was confusion about which riser to meet at, then found myself back where I started at the first riser.

around 23.23: I courageously lit my candle amidst astonished and puzzled onlookers (all three of them) and placed the aforementioned candle on the top riser then sat down. I immediately lit a cigarette to demonstrate to the onlookers that I was not about to pray, but was in fact a depraved liberal, "one of the people".

23.28: I noticed that my right ankle was starting to itch quite badly then deduced that I was indeed being eaten by a mosquito when it flew past my face.

23.29: I ground out my cigarette, careful not to litter.

23.30: After ruminating on how large the mosquito was I decided to stand up. Not only would this help me from being bit, it would also help me become more visible to the other protestors who by this time were arriving in droves (albeit unseen by me), it is after all a very big park.

23.36: Representatives of New York's Finest started showing up, along with dozens of private security "personnel" sporting the latest in technological crowd control devices and weaponry, but they were so cleverly hidden that I couldn't spy a trace of them (must be their training), although I can't say I didn't try. In response to this, the (by now hundreds of protestors) also remained hidden. As newly installed Comptroller I felt it was my duty to stay out in the open and if necessary, take the blame as well as the full wrath of the Administration if they were so inclined. I gritted my teeth, tried to look relaxed, confident and reminded myself that this was a necessary burden of leadership. With great leadership comes great responsibility, a willingness to sink with one's ship. I would show the world and George W. Bush what it means to be a commander!
Perhaps, after hearing of my bravery he would award me some Medal of Honour or Reward for Courage. Such a trophy, bestowed by him would really mean something, he is after all a Great War Hero and a fighter pilot to boot.

23.41: Tragedy! It struck without warning. My solitary flame in the darkness was snuffed out by a malignant wind. I quickly relit it should others be confounded in the night without a beacon to guide them.

23.42: Someone ran past me on the track. I looked at my watch and saw it was 46 seconds after the minute.

23.47: I scanned the track to look for the lone runner and became suddenly disappointed to see him leaving the track without the benefit of having been timed sucessfully.

23.51: Lit another cigarette and admired the skyline of Manhattan as well as a gorgeous terraced condominium complex in the distance. I thought to myself how little time I actually spend with my thoughts in such pleasant surroundings.

23.53: Received communication from the current Comptroller that I was fired and would be ineligible for further election to office due to (what I believe) was a curious use of a technicality. Specifically, due to the fact that I was no longer a dog owner I could no longer lead. So with great regret and remorse I submitted and peacefully stepped down. Let me just say, that during my term it was a great honor to serve you all, but due process must be observed even if it causes great discontent with those who would rather hold on to power beyond their lawful term if democracy is to be preserved.

23.55: Relit my candle and prepared to grind out my cigarette. Also started to prepare for the "dreaded" hour.

23.57: Some skaters joined the vigil and protested dog breed bans everywhere silently in keeping with the solemness of the occasion.

23.58: Staring at my cell phone which showed the "correct" time.

23.59: Blew out my candle and started the long walk home.

Well I must say that I had expected much more mayhem and opposition, but now that I know how serene these vigils and demonstrations can be, as well as how conducive to silent meditation and mental well-being they are, I for one will not be afraid of attending more protests. By seeing how successful this Vigil turned out (as controversial as it was) I have no reservations about saying with certainty that when it comes round time to protest the final phase of the U.S.A. PATRIOT Act, I am quite sure it will also be a Complete Success. :-)

----Zeno.
