Here's a few more interesting ones that haven't been mentioned yet:

For years, there was this blind guy who would sit on the southeast corner of Steinway and 31st Avenue outside of what was once Woolworths (later it became a drugstore, RX Plus or something along those lines, before becoming a clothing store), who would sing, accompanied by a radio.  I remember him for many, many years going back to when I was a little kid.  Any time someone would drop a coin in his plastic collection cup, he would say "thank you, God bless you."  After a while he disappeared from that corner but then for a short while I saw him turn up on 31st Street and Ditmars, in the Citibank-Starbucks vicinity.  I haven't seen him in several years, however.

There's also this allegedly homeless woman who is always covered up in a jacket and sunglasses with her face barely visible, and walks with a bit of a hunch.  Her method of begging is sticking her cupped hand out at passersby.  I've seen her all over Astoria but most frequently around Steinway and also on Broadway.  A few months ago I was in the Genovese, err, Eckerd on Steinway and 31st Ave. and she was actually in the store, and had apparently urinated on herself, as her clothes were wet and she smelled like, well, urine.  The store manager escorted her out.  Over the years I heard a lot of rumors that she was actually fairly well off but mentally ill and would beg for money regardless but I have no idea if that's true.

There was also a slightly mentally ill but very nice fellow named Sammy who would be in the 31st Avenue and 41st-42nd Street vicinity, usually holding an American flag.  Haven't seen him in many years either.

A lot of the faces up in the Ditmars/31st St. area are familiar, such as this guy that has a pretty long beard and tends to sit on the benches on 31st Street right off Ditmars.  Funny story: I was once in the library on 31st St. waiting to use the computer as my PC had crapped out at home.  He actually came in and cut in front of me in line to use the computer, and then caused a scene when I politely told him that I was there first. 

Also, there's the guy that seems to live under the railroad bridge on 33rd Street just off of 23rd Ave., who has the giant cardboard cutout of the New York Lottery guy.

On 30th Avenue every so often I see this short chubby guy riding quite a...blinged out bike.  It's a very low bike, and seems to have brass handlebars and it even has a radio.  He tends to ride it up and down in front of the cafes to show it off.

A few years ago I kept seeing this tall guy with short gray hair on Broadway who was definitely mentally ill, he'd randomly start shouting or going after people and one time he just walked out into the middle of traffic on Broadway and started halting cars.  I remember the police actually came and picked him up that time. 

Also on Broadway, a few years ago I would see this guy with dark hair who was apparently a Vietnam vet, who would pace Broadway up and down all day long, talking to himself or carrying a stereo.  Haven't seen him around in a while either.

Here's one final one.  When I used to take the N to school in the city each morning, I would tend to arrive at Queensboro Plaza by around 7:40 ish.  Since I was usually in the center car, I often saw this woman who would enter the train at around that time at Queensboro.  She was on the short side, with short hair, middle aged, clearly mentally ill, and she would always have with her a brown paper bag that would invariably contain a juice box and a package of twinkies or some other related industrial-grade junk food.  She would then proceed to fumble while trying to get the straw from the juice out of its enclosure, muttering expletives while doing so, and when she finally succeeded, she'd take a nice big sip, before ripping open the twinkies with an amazing passion and literally cramming them into her mouth.  It really was a pretty disgusting sight.  I stopped seeing her when I graduated and stopped taking the N in that direction that early in the morning.
