We need a new front door for the 3-family building in which I live.

The existing door is 38" wide, but today's standard residential doors seem to be 36" wide. A couple of contractors we've talked to say we can get a commercial door to fit, but the stuff they offer doesn't look so great. We also want a fair amount of glass to see who is at the door, and a mail slot.

If only a few of the old German woodworkers who developed this neighborhood were around to make one...

Surely others have dealt with this problem before. Anyone have a recommendation?

The door itself is 38"??   That's awesome.    If you're looking to save money AND make the job come out nice, you just get a 36" pre-hung door from the Depot or Lowe's.  With the frame it comes 37 1/2" so that leaves a nice space for shims and leveling.  This is of course provided that the frame you have is in good shape.   Is it?  Does the door and frame take up the whole brick opening or is there a side-lite or panel?     If you're looking to cut a mail slot, I'd recommend getting a fiberglass door.    It's easier to cut, and won't leave the hideously dangerous sharp edges like steel will.  (the security of the door is in the lockset and jamb of the door.  The door itself makes little difference.)

Of course, if your door is 38", chances are it's also 84".  (a common size, but not stocked locally)   With the saddle of the door and the frame, you're looking at nearly 82" in height.   Fill the top with a 2x4 and some insulation and you'll hardly notice the difference.   Down the sides, you can fill/cover with any number of moldings.   Some caulking and paint, and you'd hardly notice.

Now if you end up ordering the door for it's style, just go ahead and get the right size you need, but if there's one at the Depot or Lowe's that you like.  Go for it.
