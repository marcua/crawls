In regard to the whole zip code thing, the first three numbers of your zip code is known at the prefix, and I think that's used to direct your mail through a general sorting facility.  Queens has three different zip code prefixes that I can think of (while I think the other boroughs only have one each): 111- zip codes go through Long Island City, 113- go through Flushing, and 114- go through Jamaica.  I bet the OP could put Flushing on his mail, and as long as he put the 11377 zip code on there, she/he would get mail.  My buddy always puts "Queens" on his mail (he's a die-hard Queens kid who's jealous of the other boroughs) and he gets his mail just fine.  The mail is totally driven by the zip code.

Here's background info on Long Island City.  It was created by the consolidation of several communities in 1870, just as NYC was created by the consolidation of the five counties into the five boroughs in 1898, and it was actually an early part of the larger consolidation.

From the link:

Quote
Long Island City was created in 1870 from the communities of Astoria, Hunters Point, Blissville, Ravenswood, Dutch Kills, Bowery Bay, Steinway, and Sunnyside, all in the western part of the Town of Newtown, westernmost township in Queens County. The neighborhood of Middleton was dissolved because the U.S. Post Office refused to recognize two locations with that name in the same state, and there existed a town of that name in Orange County. The heavily industrialized Long Island City was one of the three cities whose approval was necessary to create the City of Greater New York in 1898.

The term LIC is now mostly used for the south-western part of the former city (ZIP code 11101). The border with Astoria is very vague; in ZIP codes 11106, 11102 and 11105 some locals prefer the term "Astoria" (for the convenience or the status), while others (esp. long-time residents and artists) prefer to be associated with the long history of Long Island City.

So yes, even those of us who live up by Ditmars technically live in Long Island City.  "Astoria, NY" is not recognised by the USPS, and I've occasionally had the issue come up when I've ordered things online or over the phone.  I tell them, "Astoria, NY 11105", and if they are using the USPS data, they say, "there is no such place."  My other half's license says "[our address], Long Island City, NY 11105", and mine says, "[our address], Astoria, NY".

Apologies in advance for the long-ish post...

I’m sorry you’re feeling duped and I don’t want to tell you how to feel but it seems to me you are splitting hairs on this one.

You live right near the ‘technical’ border between Astoria and Woodside (I’m assuming you are on 30th Avenue between 49th and 50th Streets) some people put the border at 49th Street, others at 50th Street or Hobart Street.

Regardless of where the border is, given the irregularity of the street grid in this area, I would, and I think most people who live around you consider this part of Astoria -- not Woodside -- since geographically, commercially, socially it’s more closely linked to Astoria.

No one ripped you off. Though you may not agree, Woodside isn’t any less expensive than Astoria and you aren’t paying more for a low-class address – in fact you’re living in one of the best parts of Astoria my opinion.

You have the choice of two subway lines (RGV and the NW), are as closely linked to Manhattan as the others parts of the neighborhood and additionally to the rest of Queens, which the majority of Astorians are not.

And you’re in walking distant of Dilliger’s bar, Sorriso’s deli and Gian Pierro’s bakery – which are all tops.

Note: Postal zones do not always follow traditional neighborhood boundaries or carry the same names for that matter. Queens is divided into three main postal zones:

LIC -- zips starting with 111
Flushing -- zips starting with 113
Jamaica -- zips starting with 114

(That’s why LIC, Flushing, and Jamaica outrank individual neighborhood names on your mail.)

So even if your zip begins with 113 it doesn’t mean you live in Woodside, only that your mail is delivered through the Flushing postal zone. One thing is incorrect though, run your address on the usps.gov website. If you’re in Astoria/LIC you should be in 11103 not 11106. 

The same thing applies to the upper Ditmars blocks between 49 street and LaGuardia airport. They are technically considered Jackson Heights, though most residents would call it Astoria, given it’s linked more closer to Ditmars and Steinway and since the area is cut off from the rest of JH by the Grand Central.
