Astoria Rents...a survey.
« on: January 28, 2013, 06:12:39 AM »
Hello Astorians!


What are we paying for our rentals? I'm interested in everyone, but especially anyone who's moved in the last year or two.


I've lived in the hood for 6ish years, a lurker on the board but first-time poster. I'm also about to move to a new apartment in the neighborhood for the first time since 2009, and I'm having a little bit of a heart attack over the prices. The husb and I looked at one and two bedroom apartments over the last year, anywhere from 36th Ave up to 21st Ave and 19th Street over to the low 40s...holy crap! When we moved into our current apartment in early 2009 (a large one bedroom in a nice building, separate kitchen, good closets, three blocks from the N/Q at 30th Ave) the rent was stabilized at $1300. In the last year some nutty stuff has happened in the building as well as the management company kind of getting neglectful, and we're wanting a little more space, so we're looking for a 2-bed.


From an extensive apartment hunting survey, decent-sized true 2-beds (by this I mean, separate kitchen, living room, true bedrooms that each fit full-size beds or larger), seem to be priced anywhere from $1800 on the low end to up to $2800 for luxury rental condos. Small, true one bedrooms seem to start around $1500 (give or take a few bucks) up to $2000 for condos with amenities.

I ask partly because we just signed for a place on 21st Ave/35th Street for $2000 - nice, three-story private house, two decent bedrooms, 5 closets + kitchen pantry, galley kitchen with small dining area, and an approx 12x14' living room. There's also a washer/dryer in the house's shared space that's used by only one other apartment, a rare find. Does this seem reasonable to you Astorians? Are we in the ballpark, or are we huge suckers?

And pray tell, what are you paying, friends? I'm not so sure that brokers / Craigslist are always the best indicators....

$1800 average for a 1BR seems a bit skewed. Just looking at Craigslist, the majority of 1BRs in Astoria are still under $1800. Maybe the new luxury condos are bringing up the average price, but for most of Astoria it seems the average price is still well below that. I'm getting the impression some people advertising Astoria are trying to artificially boost prices around here to those not familiar with the area who don't know any better. That $1600 1BR 39th Ave apartment mentioned in another thread struck me immediately as being off, and lo and behold the price went down after the poster decided she didn't want it, despite her being told she'd have to act fast if she wanted the place.

Also, with brokers, a lot of times they are able to bring prices down at least a little from what they were originally stated as, if they think they have a good candidate for an apartment and if they have a good relationship with the landlord.

And daisy, regarding that formula - I've often found they aren't strict about that at all. They are more concerned about the tenant having a good track record of paying rent on time and having good credit and a job that comfortably covers the rent. Making the rent every week has struck me as being less important than that other stuff.
