two suggestions:

tia pol in chelsea (205 10th avenue between 23rd and 22nd) is small, intimate and the tapas are extraordinary. be sure to try the fried chick peas, chocolate and chorizo and any fish or game they have as special. see their menu at http://www.tiapol.com and don't forget to wash it all down with sangria.

momofuku s'sam bar in the east village (207 second avenue at 13th st.) may not be all that healthy but i challenge you to find better quality food, menu innovation, deliciousness and reasonable value in the city. my wife and i went there for our anniversary and can't stop thinking about the delicious steamed pork buns, fried cauliflower and skate. see their menu at http://www.momofuku.com and definitely get the whole bottle of sparkling shiraz.

cheers.

Chow hound is a great resource and people are always glad to give advice. 

Javajay: Mundo might be a good local choice for the low fat dinner, very interesting food and not too expensive.  In the city, Vatan on 3rd ave by 29th street???  is a great choice.  Vegetarian Indian food served all you eat style.  Very comfortable fun seating, quiet place with warm vibe.  Women wear the full Indian outfits.  Not a buffet.  They bring around dishes one at a time and you can ask for more.  Really great and different sort of place.  Even more fun with 4-6 people.  You have to make a reservation. Ask for the romantic tables toward the back on lower floor tables or in booth.  Not expensive at all.  I am not vegetarian at all and love this place.  Also I am not a huge fan of curry but thats fine here since it is not a huge part of the menu.  I promise your wife will really enjoy this.
