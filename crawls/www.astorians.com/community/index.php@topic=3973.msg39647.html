Zen Palate is essentially like two restaurants in one. The downstairs is good for a quick and fairly inexpensive bite to eat with mostly little two- or four-person tables. The upstairs is more like a regular sit-down restaurant with larger tables, more attentive waiters, and a different, more gourmet menu. Entrees cost anywhere from $12 to $16 (if I remember correctly).

I would still choose VP2 over Zen Palate's upstairs menu, and you can walk away from VP2 with better food and more money in your wallet. I still enjoy Zen Palate, but I much prefer their upstairs menu to their downstairs one.

Quote from: megc on October 31, 2006, 10:23:02 AM
Someone who brings in flavors of the mediterranean, central asia, and south america, to name a few.  I understand why asian flavors lend themselves so readily to meatless/dairy-less fare, and I enjoy it, but I find it repetitive after a while.

Burritoville has good vegan options, and we make Mexican all the time at home. Mediterranean is a no-brainer in my opinion, although many associate fish with that diet. You can easily make (and probably buy) vegan paella (same as vegetarian), as well as make a lot of Mediterranean inspired dishes. I have an excellent cookbook called The Mediterranean Vegan Kitchen, which has wonderful pasta, focaccia, salad, soup, and dessert recipes.

VP2 and Red Bamboo both have a lot of dishes other than asian, although they would probably be best categorized as asian-fusion.

Other types of restaurants also cater to the vegan crowd, sometimes without meaning to, I think, because many of their dishes are automatically vegan. If you go to Ethiopian, Indian (still asian, sorry), Italian, and Spanish restaurants you can get many fantastic dishes that are either vegetarian or vegan. Middle Eastern food also has wonderful options for vegans - falafel, hummus, baba ganoush, tabouli, etc.

But you're right - many vegan/vegetarian restaurants do tend to go the asian route, which, for people who don't like asian food is unfortunate. I LOVE asian food, so I can't complain too much myself.
