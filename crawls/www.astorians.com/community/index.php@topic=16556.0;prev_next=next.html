Apartments in Astoria with "no lease"
« on: October 19, 2009, 10:40:21 AM »
We found out what not having a lease means.  It means you pay rent and OF COURSE, security, but  the private homeowner (of which there are legions in the neighborhood) can lie about how much was paid, when, and even the amount of rent at the move in date!  An old buyer beware story!

We lived in a 3 BR apt in an attached house at the same address for 22 years and since it was a private house-- got to be the virtual supers after awhile. (I mean we all WANT to be good neighbors.)  These things happen slowly over time and as "situations" come up: the nice, getting-older, immigrant landlords fr the "old" Astoria require a little help every now and then, their first-generation children have ATTITUDE; they don't get their hands dirty, the immigrant landlords DON'T maintain your apt.(they still live in the "old country" and it's not DONE there--these American customs are so strange), and the rent, geez, it still IS lower than all the new apartments being built (DON'T want it going up by refusing to shovel or doing ALL your own repairs over the years (IN your apt., IN the landlords apt. and IN the illegal mother-in-law apt. on the first floor). 

Time passes and before you know it being a good neighbor has you handling many things in the house AND since you're (almost)part of the family you get to help solve family problems too BUT, they still can raise the rent....after all, renters are lower class. Their nouveau-riche children told them so.

So, yes, after periodic raises over the years, we had our rent raised 3 times in less than 2 years.  It was around the time we stopped being so available (the betrayal!) for every little thing.  We also found that for the SAME rent, there are A LOT of OTHER apartments in Queens!   

We left with 1 1/2 months notice for them all--move out date established. 
Day of move:  absolute rage directed at my husband WHILE the movers are working!  "WHAT security--you never paid it!  "I was going to give you $250--the rent you paid when you moved in in 1987! but NOW, you ASK for more--I give you nothing!" "I pay you NOTHING!"
(Was there a 3 BR, even in Astoria, for $250 in 1987???) 

We expected dithering over security -- but not a lie about the actual RENT at the time and the contention that NO security was ever paid. The rent in 1987 was $800 and that is what my husband paid then.

So, the rage was a ploy that worked--we were rattled enough with the moving. Relations had ALWAYS been polite and respectful UNTIL move out day! (and THEIR great deal was over.)  There was NO LEASE so it was anything the "old country" landlord said it was.  They actually told us later (went back for some stray mail) that they were not going to do a lease again either----they might not like the new tenants.  And, an actual lease is not required by law.

SO BE AWARE--
Of course we laugh:  a tenant is going to pay a broker fee, security and 1 or 2 months rent with NO lease?  But....

We are so DONE with Astoria--BAD tastes in our mouths, now. BUT, the new place is gorgeous and living well is the best revenge, you know?
