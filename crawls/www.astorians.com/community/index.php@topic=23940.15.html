What was said at the door vs. What is on the flier vs. What happened at the installation appointment vs. What customer retention is saying on the phone:

1. Everyone is eligible for this price
2. Price includes the movie channels (HBO, etc.)
3. Price includes Turbo speed internet
4. Price includes DVR (record-able television service)
I mentioned several times, at the door, I had two cable boxes, a wireless router, (my Vonage modem), and my old Time Warner phone modem was still connected to the cable - if you flipped the switch to turn on the service right now that phone would work right now.  Both the guy at my door and the other rep on the floor said "But you need the appointment to get the new box(s) (DVR) and the Turbo (modem?).


Without the installation of new box(s) of any kind there would be no need of an installation appointment, because all they would have to do is turn on my phone service, call me on my home phone to make sure it's working, and that would be it.


The installation appointment:
The person who does the installation is not given a print out of your services.  It came as a surprise to him that I was supposed to get a DVR box.  He told me to call customer service about that.  As it happens I've had problems with my non HD service for years - the video and audio signals separate and I have to refresh almost daily, and there are many days I have to refresh the signal several times.  Since other techs had tried other things without me getting better service, I asked if he could change that box that day.  He asked if I really needed a new box and I said I really did.  The new box is an HD box and the signal on both boxes now holds its integrity.  I didn't even know my living room tv was HD till I got this new box.  (It turns out the tv is 480p.)


After the appointment I tried looking at YouTube and it took as long as ever for a video to load, which means it buffered, buffered, buffered, and I gave up.  On my wide screen tv, old HD box, I looked for the movie channels but only got the message I could buy the movie packages.  I went to my living room tv, new box.  The movie channels are working, but in letter box because it's a square screen.


Many questions, so today I looked at my account online.  Standard internet service.  I called customer service and was told my service was $134 a month.  Customer service connected me to a customer retention rep who had me read the flier to him.  I told him about the discrepancies between what was said at the door, what was on the flier, what was said at installation, and what service is actually in place.  He said he couldn't change what I'm charged - I have to take the flier to a local office (Queens Center Mall) because they have to look into the matter and they're the only ones who can change my rate.


He also said I wasn't currently paying for the movie channels, that the movie channels hadn't been disabled on the new box, rather than the service being turned on.  Since I wasn't paying (the movie channels are not included in the price) I couldn't switch them from one box to the other.  (I also can't switch boxes because they might get turned off in the process.)


So, I have to Queens Center Mall and get this business straightened out.

Had TWC for about 3.5 years here in Astoria.  The price kept going up and up and up.  I just felt like $150 a month for cable and internet (no phone) with 2 dvrs and no premium channels was too much.  I felt like there had to be a better deal out there. 


So I looked at DirecTV.  I was reluctant at first because my parents had DirecTV when I was in high school and it was terrible.  The signal was bad and rain/snow pretty much meant no TV.  And with the wind here and the fact that the dish has to sit on top of the building, how would that work?  I had read a lot of bad reviews about the customer service and the 2 year contract. 


I decided to move forward anyhow and it was a fantastic decision.  I've had DTV for about a year and a half now and have only had maybe 2-3 outages for like an hour.  I did decide to keep Time Warner's internet because it was the only real solution I had there.  I was able to negotiate the turbo speed with them for $30 a month though.  I pay around 50% less than what Time Warner charged me for the same services plus I get the Encore premium channels, a better DVR, and I also received the NFL Sunday Ticket last year.  In total, I pay about $75-80 a month for TV/internet, which seems like a fair price.  DirecTV also has better customer service.  Essentially, they do backload their contracts so the first year is much cheaper.  However, when year 2 comes around, just call up and tell them you are looking to switch because you have a better offer and they'll give you discounts to get your pricing closer to year 1 prices.
