I accept your invitation to correct you. First of all, it's "Your point of view, and I think you mean "respectfully disagree." :mrgreen:

I see a tremendous role for moderation. I made a sincere pitch for moderation back in February's Politics to be Discontinued thread. My response to having my site censored was a moderate, principled plea to merm. I agree with Paine that moderation in temper is a virtue, but moderation in principle is a vice.

To my way of thinking, and this is backed up by my overall life experiences (including this most recent hubbub), there is an inherent contradiction in placing so great a value on the appearance of any virtue that one engages in all manner of abhorrent behavior to foster it. You can't get to decency via vice. Not only does a moderate end not justify suspensions and editing and censoring, a moderate end is impossible when such techniques are employed.

To quote the Bible,

He that troubleth his own house shall inherit the wind:
    and the fool shall be servant to the wise of heart.

I believe wholeheartedly that the only moderation truly worth anything is the moderation that results from free and open communication. I call this voluntary moderation, people sorting out their differences as adults.

People will always say emotionally intense things, some things that they wish they could take back. And I believe that, in most cases, they should not be interfered with or edited or suspended or censored before they have a chance to experience the effects of such actions. This is how we learn and grow.  Freedom and responsibility promote growth; self-censoring and expecting external interventions infantilize (eg, people refer to themselves as being in the naughty corner, they plead with the moderators to let them back in, and the "nods"... :?). The feedback loop works when people are not prevented from experiencing it. Establish rules that remind people that they are free to express themselves and responsible for the content of what they say, they will become better communicators, achieve empathy with those who hurt them or whom they offend, and they will mature, and their relationships will be deeper, truer. 

Does this work perfectly every time? Of course not. But perfection isn't the point. Respecting people's rights as adults to learn from the effects of their choices is a modus operandi I can live with. You see a vicious tantrum, I see a genuine crisis that is a moment along a continuum, one that can only balance itself out in a meaningful way if its participants are left free to communicate and held responsible for what they say by each other.

I told merm in February that he was chasing a degree of control that was neither desirable nor feasible, and I stand by that.

astorians.com is pretty informative, and i think that's the main reason i check in so often.  on that note, i think it serves it's purpose.  on the otherhand, i think when it comes to the issues that are debatable, these recent events have hindered the forum...especially more recently.

since reading all of these recent comments about the rules and regulations, i find that i'm more tentative in the postings that i make, and i'm not so sure that's a good thing.  it almost defeats the purpose of some of these threads.  it's one thing to be blatantly disrespectful or insulting toward others, but it's another to be passionate about one's (differing) opinion.  i can understand how the former wouldn't be tolerated, but the latter?

i'll be the first to say that i don't know all the facts that started this line, but it just seems that a lot has been made of some comments made on a forum where there should be a lot A LOT of room for expression.  i think it's an almost impossible task for the moderators to be accurate all of the time, as what's insulting to one person, may not be insulting to another.  instead of having the moderators making these decisions, it should be more dependent on the the community reporting/commenting what they deem offense.  then in those grey areas, the moderators can be the final word.  i think when it's left up to the people, things eventually sort themselves out.  and i also think this is far more effective than the "nod" system, which i'm not convinced works at all, really.
