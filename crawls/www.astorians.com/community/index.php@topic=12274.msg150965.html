Imagine living in homes behined the church and being woken up at 8:30 in the morning by resonating church bells that ring on for sometimes nearly two minutes with a noise so deafening that even with the windows closed you cannot muffle the sound.  Imagine that one Sunday you are in a dead sleep and "double" sets of bells startle you out of a peaceful sleep, being rung 175 times at 8:15 am, then fifteen minutes later 152 times, then about 45 minutes later 35 times.

And imagine that there is absolutely no rhymn or reason to the ringing of these bells, no dogma.  The numbers of rings change from week to week; how long the rings last from week to week and the times they are run changes from week to week.  This is what the neighbors who live behind this Monastery have been dealing with. 

Then there is the issue of this huge light that is posted at the top of the church that shines brightly into the bedrooms of its neighbors, so bright that even blackout curtains do not completely diminish its brightness.

Despite numerous complaints and despite there "promises" to ring the bells later on Sunday and decrease the amount of times the bells are rung, they have not done so.

Instead, they have forwarded complaints to their legal representative who has in his naivity believed St. Irene's when they told him they have made changes.  They have not.

It is a shame that, in the name of Christianity, that St. Irene's feels they can do whatever they choose. 

Is that they way your God wants you to treat your neighbors?  Shame on you.
