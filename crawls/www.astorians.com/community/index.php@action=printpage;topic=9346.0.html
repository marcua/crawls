+1 on no such thing as "overtipping."

Having worked through college and after in food service and bartending, I really got a good feel for what it's like and know how great it can feel to get a great tip.

One call from a customer complaining about cold food can be enough to get a deliveryman suspended, so Justin always pedals fast, running red lights and cutting in front of traffic to cross the street. Stopping on 85th between West End Avenue and Riverside Drive, he wraps his chain around a pole, snaps shut the rusty padlock, grabs the bags of food, and hits the buzzer—all this takes just four or five seconds. It's not your RESPONSIBILITY, it's you CHOICE.  That's the point of tipping and why, here in the US, we have a tipping system.  You don't HAVE to tip a thing if you don't want to.

The reason they don't remove tipping and put this in the cost of the food is because, consciously and subconsciously, MOST people look for a deal.

Imagine two similar restaurants, both that have food you enjoy and you wouldn't pick one over the other for taste reasons.  One charges a delivery fee of $3 and, because they pay their delivery/service folks minimum wage, they have higher food prices compared to the similar restaurant because they have to make up those costs somehow.

Now, you pull out a menu and, taste preference aside, which one are you (or someone new to the area) likely to choose?  The cheaper restaurant where you can make your own choice as to whether to tip or not; or the one that charges more for the same sandwich and a delivery fee?

Consciously or subconsciously, you're likely going to be picking the cheaper one.  Most of the time, you and most others are not going to factor the potential tip in the price or, if you're cheap, you'll find the delivery fee and the increased price of food to be too much; making it cheaper to just buy from the place that doesn't have the fees and just tip.

Free choice.  We don't live in a socialist society (yet, anyway).  While you might be berated by others and cursed by the server/delivery person for being cheap, it's your choice.  I don't think most here would be ready for the costs and product price increases required with removing the voluntary tipping system.
