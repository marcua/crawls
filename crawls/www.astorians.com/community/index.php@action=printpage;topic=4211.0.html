Title: Re: Searching for "Astoria 1985" poster - please help!
Post by: sorabji on December 16, 2006, 05:53:51 PM
That poster was published in the Western Queens Gazette (now called the Queens Gazette). The poster was an ad-sponsored thing, not really a map of legitimate historical merit, but it is still interesting and fun to look through.

You could try contacting the Gazette  -- (718) 361-6161, qgazette@aol.com, 42-16 34th Avenue, L.I.C. N.Y. 11101 -- but I know their headquarters somewhat and would be surprised if they had any quantity of these posters from 20-odd years ago. They more likely would have it as microfilm or something similar, so perhaps you could summon a printed version from that.

Another option would be to visit the Queens Library in Jamaica. Their online catalog -- queenslibrary.org -- says they have all copies of the Gazette from v. 3 (1983) - v. 14 (1995). Assuming that's true, and assuming their copies are complete, you might be able to at least see the thing if you can determine which edition it was in.

I don't know if it was printed annually, but there were several iterations of that map. Until recently the Raleigh clothing store on Broadway (somewhere between 31st and 33rd, can't remember exactly where offhand) had later editions of that map. I think theirs were from the early 1990s. But if you blinked you'd miss it in their window, it was that buried. It looked like the maps were all over the base of the display window, but then they put other window display stuff on top of those maps. More recently it looks like those maps have been removed altogether. Hey, maybe the Raleigh store saved those maps for later use. It would be a shame if they threw them out.

And in case anyone went to check out this very cool map at Dino's and didn't spot it, that might be because they recently moved it into the bathroom. I know they did this because they recently renovated the main dining area and during the renovation they moved that poster and the Sopranos autographed photos into the restroom. I would have thought they'd move those items back to a more visible spot by now, but I guess not. I used to like pointing that map out to people but I'm less inclined to do that now that it's in the bathroom...  :?

Hell Atlantic, since you're probably happy to see this any way you can, and since I was actually *at Dino's* when I happened to read this thread on my Treo, I took a few pictures of the map. I did not get enough shots to get the whole map but maybe I'll try and finish the set next time I'm there:

http://www.sorabji.com/_/Astoria_1985

Hope this helps.

-mt
