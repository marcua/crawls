I highly recommend the Astoria Veterinary Group at 23-54 Steinway.

Not to be confused with the Astoria Animal Hospital on Steinway, which I've also been to (and suspect is the one mcdirk was referring to)-- they have a clean, modern facility, but are expensive and the doc is not very friendly-- the assistant did most of the examination, and then the doc came in for 5 minutes and didn't even introduce himself to me.

I just brought my cat to the Astoria Vet Group last week (after a glowing recommendation from my friend who brings her cats there), and had a very good experience. Everyone there is very nice, and seem to be very concerned about your pet. And it was much less expensive than the Astoria Animal Hosp.-- the same procedure (full teeth cleaning) cost $200 less at the Astoria Vet Group.

Dr. Smith is wonderful. She's very knowledgeable, and was very patient about answering all of my questions.

Their info:

I've been taking my little guy to Animal Clinic of LIC mostly because of the convenience (we live very close by).  So far, no complaints with the standard kitten immunizations and procedures, and he's relatively calm when we're there.

Last week he went in for neutering.  I dropped him off in the morning in his cat carrier, which I usually leave open around the apartment with a donut bed inside.  (He sleeps in it all the time at home, and then when we actually have to travel with it, not only is he perfectly calm, but the donut bed keeps him from sliding around if the carrier gets bumped.)

When I returned to pick him up post-op, I happened to be anxiously peeking in the back room while they took him out of the recovery kennel. 

I was surprised - and very pleased - to see that they'd taken his donut bed out of the carrier and moved it to the recovery kennel with him for the day.  I'm sure it made his recovery experience less stressful since he had his familiar bed from home, and it wasn't something they had to do.  It was a small gesture, but I wouldn't have even known about it if I hadn't been peeking in the back. 

He's now fine (if sterile), and I'm feeling pretty satisfied with conditions over there right about now.  Definitely recommend them based on the appointments we've had so far.   :mrgreen:

This will probably drive you nuts with so many conflicting opinions, but I recommend
the Animal Clinic on 36th Ave.

For several years, I took my dog to Astoria Veterinary Group. A year he developed a huge
cyst on the right side of his mouth. They drained it, put him on a lot of meds, but never gave me
a straight answer about the cause or prevention of reoccurrence.

The cyst returned as well as a number of large, fleshy bumps. I was told they were 'fatty deposits',
common for an older dog.

Finally I went to the Animal Clinic for another opinion. Turned out the cyst and fat deposits were
lymphatic cancer. This was in August and he had to be put down in mid-September.

I know cancer can't be prevented, but the vet at AC told me that even a first year vet would have caught this
and at least given me straight answers.

The staff at the AC are great. They called me the day after my first visit to check on my dog
and when he was euthanized they couldn't have been nicer or more helpful.

Astoria Vet Group just keeps sending me reminders to bring him in for a check up.

Since it's  near you, why not just go in and check it out? You'll know by your gut reaction if it's
right for you and your pet.

Good luck.
