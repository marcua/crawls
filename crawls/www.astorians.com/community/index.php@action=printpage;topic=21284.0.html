Starwind, I think you bring up two strong points, and both have been commented on only occasionally in the media. First I agree that the prosecutor pressed charges that were too much to be able to prove, without a reasonable doubt. I think that is the part that 99% of the outraged Nancy Grace types have missed.  Had she been charged with something like criminally negligent homicide and tampering with evidence, I think she'd be behind bars right now. Murder 1 is a very difficult thing to prove, but I never followed the case close enough to be able to point out the missing links.
Secondly, I think that the death penalty is also something that certainly played a part. Personally, I would rather have a guilty person walk free, than have an innocent person killed (I don't want to see the guilty killed either, but that's beside the point). As a juror, knowing that a case that seemed to have its fair share of holes would end a persons life, I can't say I'd vote to convict.
I think that Casey Anthony probably did it, hot or not, but this is how the legal system works.
