Title: Re: Advice on piercing
Post by: megc on April 09, 2008, 09:13:42 PM
Getting it done at a tattoo/piercing place is just fine.  I had my very first piercings (I have 3 in each ear) at a jewelry place and they used a gun.  I was 13.  I had the other 4 done at a tattoo place on Telegraph Avenue in Berkeley in my early 30s.  They used a needle.  I believe the needle is better - the gun slams the stud through your ear.  The needle actually removes skin.  Many people believe the needle is better - the recovery is quicker, it's a cleaner hole.

After I got those 4 holes, they gave me an orange soda to drink, which was supposed to bring my blood sugar back up (?).  It might have been some weird urban myth kind of thing, but I sucked down that soda like there was no tomorrow. I don't like soda all that much.

I think mine were/are all 14K gold.  Hypoallergenic is good.  Plated metals are not good.

These days I keep gold hoops in all my piercings.  I've thought about changing them. At some point I will.

I think the amount of pain just depends on who you are and what you tolerance for pain is.  Me, I have a low tolerance for pain.  So, my 4 hurt pretty bad by the last piercing.  They ached for a couple of days but tapered off after that.  I will never get my nose done because 1) I don't believe it hardly hurts, like friends have told me, and 2) I believe I would look ridiculous with a nose piercing - I'm way too pasty looking.

You're certainly not too old!  Have fun!  It goes fast.

Post by: lanseaux on April 09, 2008, 10:09:41 PM
I've mentioned them in a couple of other threads I can't find at the moment, but I would highly recommend Venus Modern Body Arts (http://www.venusbymariatash.com/index.php?section=piercing) in the East Village - 199 E 4th Street, just east of Avenue A.  I took a friend there for her first ear piercing, and I think she was really pleased.  She was roughly the same age you are, so I don't think you are too old.

I don't think you need to go to a doctor's office (I have to say I didn't even realise that you could get your ears pierced at a doctor's office!), but you should definitely go to a reputable place (not like me at 17 at the Jersey Shore...).  I got a fourth ear piercing at Venus about two years ago, and it was the first one I had done that used a needle rather than a gun.  I expected it to hurt more, but it hurt less.  It wasn't painless, but honestly, 99% of the pain was after the earring was in, and it wasn't a sharp pain, but rather more of a general achiness as others have mentioned.  I didn't even realise she had done the piercing until after it was finished - it was that quick and smooth.

As a couple of people mentioned, be careful of the metal used.  I developed an allergy to nickel that I still have because of my first piercing (at age 12), because either the gun or the cheap studs contained nickel.  That's one thing you won't have to worry about if you go to Venus - they use only surgical steel.

Post by: paratactical on April 10, 2008, 09:59:48 AM
It seems like you're leaning toward going to a professional peircer anyway, but as someone who knows people in the business and has had lots of tatts and peircings, never never never never let someone peirce you with a gun. Always have it done with the hollow needles the pros use. I could really go on and on about why, but the guns cause trauma to your body and because they don't remove the flesh where the hole is going (hollow needles actually remove the skin) they are more likely to get infected or be unable to grow closed normally.

The one peircing I had done by a gun would not heal right.

Also, make sure to start keeping salt in the bathroom. A soak or rinse in hot salt water can do wonders for sore or swollen new peircings.
