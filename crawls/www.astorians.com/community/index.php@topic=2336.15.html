I pretty much live on the aformentioned corner (on 34th street, past the Dunkin Donuts a few houses toward 31st ave) and I can vouch for the fact that this particluar corner on Broadway has gotten out of control in the past few months. Its steadily getting worse and no amount of 911-calling seems to remedy this decline in safety.

My husband and I have also called the local precinct directly (114 Precinct: 718.626.9311), when, upon multiple instances we look out the window onto the street and see brawling teens, fistfighting, yelling, taunting passersby, kicking parked cars, etc. We have left for work in mornings where we see blood splattered all over the pavement in front of our building.

There is a real problem developing. The cops always eventually come but usually its too late, after the fact. We really should use a collective voice to tell these craven hoodlums that they are not above the law just because they are frustrated that their families are being pushed out by hipsters or whatever. Who cares? There's no excuse for this kind of violence.

We should start by attending the community meetings (the ones upstairs from the Quinn Funeral Home on Broadway) to figure out what a course of action should be.
