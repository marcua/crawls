Here's the reason why people are so up-in-arms about Palin's lack of experience: Obama's lack of experience was one of McCain's main criticisms of him up until the point Palin was chosen. The other main criticism was that Obama is a better celebrity than politician. So then, after all that criticism of those two things, McCain now has a VP choice who is no more experienced and has become no less of a celebrity than Obama is.

Now McCain attempts to explain it's different in her case by emphasizing all of Palin's "executive" experience, but give me a break. Executive experience as mayor of a very small town in Alaska, and then not even one full term as governor of the most sparsely populated state in the nation, which is also hundreds of miles away from the rest of the country. She never even got a passport until 2006, at 42 years old. This is not to demean her small-town roots or small-town people in general. But, by watching interviews she did before her being chosen for VP, it was clear she wasn't interested nor knowledgeable in how things were in the rest of the country or the world. She cared about how things were as they related to Alaska. Understandable as an Alaska governor, but not as a politician on a national level.

To me, though, experience isn't the biggest of my concerns. Someone could have all the experience in the world, but I'm not going to vote for them if I don't agree with their views. Obama supporters like Obama because they like his ideas, not the number of years he's been in the Senate. You can bet that so many liberals have reacted so harshly to Sarah Palin not only because McCain was hypocritical in his choosing someone no more experienced than Obama, but because she is viewed as being an ultra-religious ultra-conservative who wants pregnant rape victims to be forced to carry to term and doesn't care about polar bears.. among other things. Her being so inexperienced, and sounding so confused in interviews, is just the icing on the cake.
