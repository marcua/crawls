We adopted our cat just over a year ago from a Manhattan shelter on the UES. He was about 8 months old at the time, but there were cats and kittens there of varying ages.

While we had to put down money for him, it was still pretty minimal. The only thing is that they require that all animals leaving their shelters are spayed or neutered, if they are not already. So in some instances, such as ours, you are unable to pick him or her up until s/he has had the surgery (performed there by one of their veternarians).

They also didn't do an aggressive background check as they supposedly used to do. We essentially could go up there, look at their cats, say "I want that one" and then that one would be ours after paying, and taking care of the smaller details.

All in all, it was a pretty straightforward process and we are very happy with our cat. Here's the link to the shelter we went to: http://www.nycacc.org/site/c.ikLTJ9MUKtH/b.540513/k.CB5A/Home/apps/s/inline.asp

The Brooklyn Animal Rescue Center (BARC) is a wonderful place.  They are a no-kill shelter on Wythe Avenue in Williamsburg.  I got a wonderful cat from them several years ago -- she was a feral, but blossomed into the most fantastic roommate one could ever hope for.  And the people there are (or at least were) great!  

They get in a lot of kittens, but they have a pretty "high turnover."  They have a lot of older cats (which I actually prefer), but I can't imagine them trying to force one off on you if that isn't what you are looking for.  From what I remember, they do ask for references, but nothing excessive.  I believe that there is a link to them on PetFinder as well.

I had a wonderful experience adopting a cat from this place in Astoria:

http://www.francisfriends.org

As far as I can tell, it's sort of a small network of people who foster cats until homes are found for them.

I looked at their listing of cats, saw one I was interested in, and called them up. The woman actually brought the cat over to my apartment, and offered to let me keep her for a couple days to decide if I wanted to keep her. If not, she would come back and get her. Fortunately for me (and kitty), she was just the right cat for me and I kept her!

The whole process was very informal, I didn't actually have to submit the application (or have reference checks!), we covered that all in conversation.
