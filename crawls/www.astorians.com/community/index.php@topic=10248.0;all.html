Expelled
« on: April 23, 2008, 01:20:42 AM »
If you want to please the film critic community, you don’t make a movie that suggests life may not have happened by pure chance.

Ben Stein apparently doesn’t care a lot about  the critics because that’s exactly what the  man has done, made a film that suggests life may actually have been planned and have a purpose.

The critics have been almost unanimous in their hate for Stein’s latest film, 'Expelled: No Intelligence Allowed.'

The Rotten Tomatoes site shows only 9 percent of film critics with positive reviews. In contrast, Al Gore’s 'Inconvenient Truth, which has glaring errors and out lies, is adored by 93 percent of the critics.

Despite the steady stream of sex and violence coming out of Hollywood, The New York Times says that 'Expelled'  is 'one of the sleaziest documentaries to arrive in a very long time.' The newspaper of record uses some choice words to describe the movie, stating that it is a 'conspiracy-theory rant masquerading as investigative inquiry.' You mean like the dozens of 'documentaries' on the  current conlict? 

Critics weren’t satisfied with merely reviewing the film. Instead they apparently saw it as an opportunity to slam its star, Stein.

Time magazine fumes that 'the man made famous by Ferris Bueller . . . quickly wades into waters far too deep for him.'

Former publisher of Stein’s Hollywood column, E! online, warns that 'Stein's credibility is blown on this poorly constructed diatribe' and advises filmgoers, 'You'd be smart to save your bucks.'

The public apparently isn’t listening.

'Expelled' had a huge opening weekend for a documentary with a radioactive topic. It placed ninth with a box-office take of $3.1 million.
By comparison, Morgan Spurlock, director of 'Super Size Me,' debuted his 'Where in the World Is Osama bin Laden?' documentary on the same weekend. Spurlock’s film took in an embarrassing $143,300.
