When I wrote a research paper on Corona history years ago, much of the reference I used were books by historian Vincent Seyfried. I looked up his additional books on the Queens historical site and saw that he has written a number of books on LIC as well. Their URL with all available titles is:

http://www.queenshistoricalsociety.org/publications.html

And these are some of the books:

300 Years of Long Island City: 1630-1930 Vincent Seyfried, 1985 Queens Community Series, (paperback), 188 pp., illus.

Long Island CityQHS publication (brochure). One of a series of brochures about historic Queens neighborhoods

Old Queens, N. Y. in Early Photographs Vincent Seyfried and William Asadorian, 1991, (paperback), 175 pp., illus.

A Research Guide to the History of the Borough of Queens and Its Neighborhood Jon A. Peterson and Vincent Seyfried, ed., 1983

These books are particularly wonderful because of all the photographs that contain. I used the Corona title when interviewing my grandparents, and it was a wonderful tool.
