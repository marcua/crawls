So one time on the subway, Im sitting there in a full car, a woman comes onto the train.  Is she pregnant, is she a little overweight?  Cant tell.  Im guessing she is overweight and just know that if I offer her my seat and she is not pregnant Im going to seriously offend her in front of the whole car.  So I do nothing.  Few minutes later the woman next to me gives me a big "HRMPH!" gets up and gives the woman her seat. I know 100% what you are talking about. My rationale for women whom you can't tell with is that I'd rather play it safe and not offer her a seat for the following reason: I believe it is a far worse offense to imply a woman is overweight than to not offer a seat up to a pregnant lady who's not that far along. Most pregnant women will just assume that it is NY and people are generally rude, but I wouldn't want to send some woman home feeling bad about herself because a guy on the subway mistakenly thought she was preggos. 
When I can tell a woman is pregnant, though, I will always offer my seat up. Same goes for elderly folks and women with babies strapped to those weird backpack slingy things. It amazes me how rude people are in that respect.
On that same note, I always help mothers carry strollers up and down subway stairs. It baffles me how so many people walk by and see a mother struggling like that. It takes like 30 seconds out of your day and it's not like you run into that situation that often.
