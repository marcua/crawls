I know that many things have been said but I just don't understand why the prices are so high in Astoria. Don't get me wrong, I love the area but the prices seem artificial to me. I know the area is popular but it seems that prices have fallen more in Manhattan. Someone please enlighten me as to how this makes sense. I live in the Ditmars area and affording a home or even a condo for that matter is beyond approach. If one looks at the median household income for the area it doesn't jibe with people being able to buy. I'm certainly under no illusions that everyone falls into 50K range but six hundred and seven hundred thousand dollar single family homes! Have the majority of home owners been here forever? One of the many things I like about this area is that it is very working class. Maybe I am delusional but, how do we afford anything?

If I have offended anyone this is not my intention. I'm just stating what seems obvious to my naive eyes.

Re: Astoria housing prices immune to economy?
« Reply #7 on: May 01, 2009, 08:34:17 PM »
You're under no illusions when you say many home owners (1-2 family) have been here a long, LONG time. However, one can't compare co-ops/condo prices w/ those of 1 or 2 family homes because of the crappy new construction of the developers of apartments for sale.

For the most part, old time neighborhood 1-2 family home owners don't have at risk mortgages. Many don't even have mortgages. That means there are not many  "motivated sellers" who are suffering from unemployment or scam mortgages.

When you mention 600-700k for 1-2 family houses, I have to make a point. While some have sold in the last 4 years at ridiculous prices, there haven't been many transactions in that league for some time. In fact not many transactions have happened at all for houses in the 4 or 5 zip codes of Astoria in a year. Check the comps. Also - many of these retired folks  put houses on the market for no other reason except to brag (lie) at the cafes to friends that they could get a king's ransom for 2000 sqf. If one goes on an appointment for a house for sale, ask the selller(s) where are they relocating. An answer of I don't know yet means..... the house ain't for sale and the owner is looking for attention with nothing better to do.
