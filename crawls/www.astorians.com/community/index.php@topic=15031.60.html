That reminds me, I stopped by a week or two ago for the first time.  Had the "classic".  It was absolutely delicious, and very filling, especially for the price.  Definitely not your standard baked potato, and it's definitely different from the sort of potato you might find at, say, a buffet potato bar.   They sort of mash the insides, and combine it with all the toppings you get, and there's this great mix of hot and cold and smooth and crunchy in every bite.  I'll be back for sure.

I'd been meaning to go to this place for ages, and finally trudged out here tonight to pick up dinner.  I had the veg and my partner had a "make your own".  It's a huge amount of food for $6--I only managed to get through half.  My only complaint is that I like to eat the skins, and the skins were just too tough, like the potato had been cooked for a little too long.  (BTW, they definitely don't microwave the potatoes--there's a big oven in the back.)  The chocolate pudding I got for dessert wasn't too bad, but it was actually a small, flat cake with pudding on top--not quite what I was expecting.  The counter guy gave me a taste of one of the daily soup specials, lentil with pasta and vegetables, and it was really good.  Next time, I might get a soup and a "Turkish panini" instead of a potato.
