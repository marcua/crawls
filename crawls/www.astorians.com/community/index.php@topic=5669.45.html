Quote from: 28Grand on March 26, 2007, 08:15:55 AM

1. When dining out, it’s usually never OK for one person to sit at a 4-person table, especially during busy hours.

2. In my experience, if you have to ask for something three times the answer is usually no -- but you knew this already, otherwise you wouldn’t have asked three times in the first place.

3. People are rude. Get used to it. When did compensation become compulsory for being shabby treatment? Does poor service at a department store entitle someone to walk out with the merchandise as compensation for their feelings being hurt?


If I can just add my few cents here . . . as both an avid consumer and a small business owner I just can't contain myself.

To #1 - as a consumer it is NOT your responsibility to figure out what is or is not convenient for the business - if you want to sit at a large table then ask - it's their business - only they really know how they want to run things and it's entirely up to them. 

To #2 - if you ask and they say no then the answer is no - as a business owner I'm very aware of the technique known as "ask a million times and maybe the answer will change" - it WON'T, and it's annoying as all hell when you're asked over and over again.

To #3 - I absolutely refuse to accept this as a social "truth".  Accepting when people are rude perpetuates the problem and I, for one, am sick of it - like the cab driver last night who complained incessantly as to how much money he was going to lose by driving ALL the way to Astoria - as if I was going to change my mind if he complained enough to me.  I don't ever want to get used to people being rude and I think it's our social responsibility - particularly in a business situation - to let the people around us know when they are being rude and how that affects us.  Rudeness snowballs, by accepting it I think we're condoning it and I won't condone rudeness.

To #3a - giving discounts/freebies to dissatisfied customers only hurts when it's been manipulated out of you.  The fact is that businesses exist because of the continued patronage of customers - there is a very real threat that pissing off a customer will have a negative impact on your bottom line.  They told her it was OK to sit there - then they asked her to move - this is an inconvenience and a free soda or dessert isn't going to gouge the cash drawer but it might maintain goodwill.  It's often the effort that counts more than the freebie.

OK - I admit, much more than a few cents.
