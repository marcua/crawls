This accumulation of text reminds me of something that used to go on at CUP, the defunct diner at 35th Avenue and 36th Street (where Five Napkin is now).
 
CUP programmed its cash registers to round up checks to the nearest dollar, adding as much as 2 or 3 cents to every check.
 
I have evidence:
 
http://dailyreceipts.com/cup_diner_queens_050102.jpg.html
$4.95 + .43 = 5.38. Right? So why was I charged $5.40? I WANT MY 2¢ BACK!
 
http://dailyreceipts.com/cup_diner_queens_050311a.jpg.html
1.41 + 0.12 = 1.53. And yet I was charged $1.55.   THOSE BASTARDS!
 
http://dailyreceipts.com/cup_diner_queens_060113a.jpg.html
10.75 + .93 = 11.68. And yet, these criminals charged me $11.70!  HOSE ME DOWN! I AM SO ANGRY ABOUT THIS!
 
http://dailyreceipts.com/cup_diner_queens_060806.jpg.html
10.95+ .94 = 11.89. And yet I lost 1 precious ¢ on the deal! I weep.

But wait. Here is where the penny-pinching OCD gets really really REALLY interesting.
 
CUP's penny-skimming went both ways. Sometimes CUP rounded up, sometimes CUP rounded down, in the customer's favor. IN THE CUSTOMER'S FAVOR! Can you imagine? You stomp into a dining establishment as if entering combat, poised to destroy a business at the drop of a fork with your angry angry angry online review (really apoplectic angry spit-and-bile flowing from every righteous orifice and FROM THE TIPS OF YOUR FINGERS AND TOES as you flail your gift of life into the frenzied madness of a really really reaaaaaaaly vitriolic online review), but then you look at your receipts and you find that you actually got a break once in a while.
 
At CUP the rounding also went down. The place I thought was robbing me of precious ¢s probably broke even on what a lesser man's blogging butt (heh  ) might have hoped was the first indicator of another global financial meltdown.
 
I have evidence:
 
http://dailyreceipts.com/cup_diner_queens_050309.jpg.html
12.95 + 1.12 = 14.07. Why was I charged 14.05? Should I have left 2¢ at the altar of CUP Diner?
 
http://dailyreceipts.com/cup_diner_astoria_070828.jpg.html
10.50 +0.91 = 11.41. Why was I charged $11.40? WHY? Should I have left 1¢ at the altar of CUP Diner? Should I have invested that stray 1¢? What would Madoff have done with that 1¢?
 
http://dailyreceipts.com/cup_diner_queens_071213.jpg.html
10.50 + .91 = 11.41. BUT I WAS CHARGED A MERE $11.40!!! SCAM-A-LAM, SCAM-A-LAM, OH BLACK BETTY, SCAM-A-LAM. Oh, wait, I actually made a ¢ off of this deal. Nevermind.
 
 
I noticed it, which is not to say that I gave a donk about it. I noticed that the math was bad, but at 1 or 2 cents per episode I figured this exercise was probably in the interest of saving the prestidigitational exertions and time exhaust of dealing with pennies. This scam sometimes cost me nothing, other times it cost me as much as 3¢ (gasp!) per visit to CUP. Over a year the CUP rounding scam, on average, might have cost me (take a deep breath) …  TWENTY CENTS!
 
Similarly, I sometimes buy those tall cans of Arizona Iced Tea, clearly on sale for 99¢ a pop. Almost invariably the cashier charges me $1. ONE FULL DOLLAR and I lose a precious ¢ on the Arizona Iced Tea scam. I would bet that in the last 5 years I lost TWENTY CENTS on the Arizona Iced Tea scam. Do I care? Do I care? I do not.
 
I know that operators of food carts and places that refuse to issue receipts will commonly "guess" at totals for purchases. I saw a SHOCKING EXPOSÉ of this practice on one of the local tv newsies, which revealed that certain hot dog and falafel vendors were scamming as much as 6 DOLLARS A DAY from this crime-which-should-be-punishable-by-death.
 
The continuous outrage never ends, is all I am saying. Why do we submit to and thereby contribute to it? WHY? EVERYBODY JUST STAY HOME.
