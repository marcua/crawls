I'm sure that all basement apartments are not created equal. And while you may have had a bad experience with one, out of the 4 people I know who have had basement apartments, only one had problems and that apartment and that landlord were sketchy to begin with - but they were moving to NYC (they got jobs here out of college) and were desperate and took the first cheapest apartment they could find. And experienced difficulties. The other three people lived in nice, clean, carpeted, warm, non-flooding basement apartments and didn't experience any problems. Although one of them did have ugly wood paneling. Oh, the horror!


Personally, I have had mostly good experiences with finding apartments on craigslist. Of course, I avoid the ads posted in broken English or that sound sketchy (or have limited to NO information besides a general area and price tag). A lot of other people I know have used craigslist to find apartments and I've heard an equal number of success stories as horror stories. The difference is that the success stories involve people finding a nice apartment and the horror stories involve people losing time going to see a horrible apartment (which will happen even with brokers), but they got lovely stories about the horrible apartments they went to see to tell to friends. I don't know of anyone who went to see a horrible apartment and were somehow forced to take it.

I just feel that your blanket statements such as "You will not find a 1BR for that price" and then pointing to the worst apartment on craigslist you could find - is misleading and wrong. You seem to be discouraging people from bargain hunting. I can't understand why you would do that.

You are also alleging that craigslist is not a worthwhile resource to use while apartment hunting. Yes, you will see many bad apartments on craigslist while searching for a good one. But if you go look at the thread someone posted about all the brokers he dealt with who showed him apartments that didn't meet what he was looking for and tried to upsell him, I'd say that can be said of any other apartment hunting resource.

You can find some of the best deals on "by owner" apartments. However, you can also find some of the sketchiest. The most important thing to do is be careful, I'd recommend bringing someone with you when checking out apartments and make sure you feel good about the apartment and the person you are dealing with and don't sign anything without fully understanding it. And don't be pressured into making a decision you might regret.
