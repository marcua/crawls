astoristani...i consider myself a "real astorian" as i have lived in different parts of the neighborhood all of my 37 years (with the exception of the time i was away at university, or the 6 months living in london)...and on this topic you don't speak for me at all. 

i don't see these larger (chain) stores as necessarily a gentrification of the neighborhood.  the areas in question are more diverse than ever. 

yes, we certainly have our share of "yuppies", but there are still a lot of old greeks and italians, as well as a lot of new immigrants (ie: spanish, middle eastern, pakistani, indian, etc). 

business is business, and if these larger, more established places keeps the business in the neighborhood, then so be it.  this is the more important priority.

for example, there is this dead zone of restaurant business on 20th avenue and steinway street.  these two places in particular keep bringing in new owners and then go out of business.  they're already on their third or fourth owners in the nine years that i've been over here.  it's a shame to see these places fail, but there is nothing really in the area to keep them viable.  sure there are a lot or residences, but i guess they're not recognizable enough to get people to go to them.  would a chain do better over here?  i don't know the answer to that.  but since they'd be more likely to bring in some traffic, it wouldn't hurt the community (as you seem to imply by your line of thinking).

i mean look at the area where unos is.  a long time ago, there wasn't much there...except maybe that club (if that was your thing) or the pool hall.  then pc richards opened there...then the movie theater....then the studio started to expand (and get more in the way of visitors)...then unos...then applebees, starbucks, carvel, panera, etc...  now that area is thriving like never before....including that diner which i believe is an independent of sorts (or is that owned by the studio?).

what would have happened to that area if none of these places opened up?  it probably would have remained fairly barren. 

think about it, and tell me which scenario would you prefer?
