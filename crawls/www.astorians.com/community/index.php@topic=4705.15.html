I think the Ditmars/Astoria Blvd has such variety in its drinking establishments:

Hellgate Social:  hip, fun place with great mixed drinks, nice beer selection, and movies, bands, and djs.  [aside: I wonder if they will start a quiz night there?]

Sparrow:  relaxed vibe with good beer on tap and wonderful bottled beer selection.  New food and coffee is a plus.  How're the sandwiches?

Beer Garden:  only remaining one of its kind in the area.  Good beer, good food. 

Albatross:  gay bar and friendly to all.  Only gay bar I know of in the area.

Stini Yiamas:  loungey/bar with a good liquor selection.  Beer selection is lacking.

McCanns:  Sometimes a bit like Animal House but with good beer and food, good place to watch a game.  Best wings in the area.  Pool.

Waltz:  cafe with beer and wine and music.

Fatty's:  nice bar in a sometimes loud restaurant but a + is the related beer/wine place opening up soon.

Remote Sports Bar:  from a fellow MeFite,
Quote
The Remote is a cool place, they even let you smoke there. But it is very old school. 40/50+ alkies during the day, outerborogh greaseball football fans on game day. But the beer is cheap, they have games on the TV, and the crazy old drunks have great stories. But they can get rowdy. But the bartenders are big on free drinks.

Irish Rover:  excellent pub with a great buy back policy.  Best place in the are to watch a baseball game.

Gussy's:  only biker bar I know of in the area (near 20th Ave on 29th Street).

Unknowns:  Black Dog Bar (name isn't quite right, but it's on 23rd Ave near 27th Street); Club 23 (eeuw), Central Lounge (waaaay north on Steinway); No Partners on 21st Ave; that bar near the Ditmars subway stop, whose name escapes me...O'Hanlons?

Les Amis on Ditmars creeps me out.  Never been to 718 - it has a bar?
