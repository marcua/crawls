Quote from: Newtownian on February 05, 2007, 04:09:41 PM
Where was the triboro theater? What's there now?





In June 1974, the Loew's Triboro Theatre, which was situated on the corner of 28th Avenue and Steinway Street, was slated for demolition, but a community drive to save the theater and use it as a community cultural center got a boost when the City Landmarks Preservation Commission voted to grant official landmark status to the theater. We celebrated and called off any further protests, and that night, in the darkness, the new owners of the property brought in the wrecking ball and demolished the building. I can remember standing there and sobbing along with hundreds of others who had been so active in the protests to save this magnificent structure.

It was such a beautiful building. My Mom used to send my sister and me to the movies almost every Saturday morning. She would give us a little slip of paper and tell us which dishes she still needed. They used to give out free dishes for every ticket purchased. When she needed the serving pieces, she and my Dad would go themselves because we didn't know the difference between a coffee pot or a teapot!

I also graduated Bryant High School from the Loew's Triboro Theatre.
