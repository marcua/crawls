What interested me about this case is that Sean Bell sort of became a poster boy for racial injustice.  Obviously, this was a horrible tragedy, and no one deserves to die in that way.  So the narrative became "innocent person of color gets gunned down."  I can't help but think of the surrounding circumstances, though.  It is not as though he was a Boy Scout who died working on a merit badge. 

He was a bad guy in a bad place where bad people do bad things.  We all make decisions in life, and his was to associate with whom he associated with.  When you hang out with criminals and engage in criminal activity, there is an implied risk of something terrible happening to you.  That is a risk level that regular people don't have to assume.

For the community "leaders" to have to stand with an unlikely poster boy like Sean Bell, it shows how rare an occurrence something like this really is.  Despite the preferred narrative of the maniac gun crazy cops taking matters into their own hands.  The verdict might not be what Sean Bell's family wanted, but a more favorable verdict would not have brought him back.  I hope this verdict will be a setback to racial hucksterism.  Maybe now those community leaders can focus on better things.  I never hear them protesting against someone who has beaten or raped an old lady, for example.
