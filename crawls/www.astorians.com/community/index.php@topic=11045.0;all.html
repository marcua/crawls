Hi everyone, I'm new here, this is my first post!

I researched daycare in the 11103 area.  I visited Learning Odyssey, Happy Days and Perfect Angels.  All quoted $1,600 per month for an infant with only Perfect Angels offering a part-time option with a reduced rate.  Of the 3, Learning Odyssey was BY FAR the best.  The staff are all trained and continuing their education in childcare, it was exceptionally bright and clean, there is a monthly curriculum - even for the babies, the ratio is 2:1, there's a large backyard play area, the play room is separate from the infant nap area... I really could go on and on.  Of the three, it was most run like a business which I liked.  The Director was very nice and the large staff spent the entire time we were there cleaning, sanitizing and preparing for the next day.  We were very lucky to be able to enroll our baby since the wait list is quite extensive.  Only 12 children can be enrolled at any given time.  Again, another plus.

Happy Days had all the same features - 2:1, 12 children - my drawbacks to their facility were that it was only one large room - no difference from play and nap, I only saw one additional caregiver besides the Director and it is very close to Astoria Blvd. which made me nervous.  Also, they play the TV during food prep and while they said the programming would be educational, it's parking the kid in front of the TV to get something done and it made me wonder if it was done more often than they said.  They also didn't seem to have a set learning curriculum and while my baby is only 7 weeks old, he'll be in daycare until he's ready for school so I want him to be in a facility that will prepare him for that. They did have a backyard with lots of toys and they were sanitizing while I was there.  Also, food is provided which is a huge plus.

Perfect Angels, I'm sorry, was a hot mess.  Literally.  The rooms were very tiny and cluttered.  There were post-it notes every where reminding the staff simple things like to wash their hands and the 'play area' was a Little Tykes tree house crammed in a small room.  There WAS an outdoor play area but you had to go through the Director's house and even then it was just their driveway.  No toys were visible and there wasn't a safety gate.  There was a rubber floor installed though.  What I will say about them is the Director was very passionate about the children and you could tell the toddlers were kept very busy.  If our child was older, I believe he would thrive there.  It was set up like a school and they had a full curriculum.  Just not a place for babies and the post-its made me wonder about the quality of the care givers.

I would recommend anyone looking for daycare to visit the NYS Office of Child and Family Services.  http://www.ocfs.state.ny.us/main/becs/  All three above are licensed by the state and subject to its policies and regulations which are quite extensive.

While you could get a nanny for possibly the same rate or less... most are paid under the table so you couldn't claim it on your taxes.  They're not licensed care givers so you really not sure what you're gonna get.  Also, since they are individuals if they are sick or cannot watch your baby for any reason, there is no fall back.  For us personally, day care was a better option.
