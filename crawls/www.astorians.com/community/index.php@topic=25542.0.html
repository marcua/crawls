Who the hell put winter decorations up on 30th Avenue?
« on: November 06, 2013, 11:03:13 AM »
Anyone else notice this?

There are giant, ugly snowflakes and what looks like snow angels hanging from the lampposts across 30th Avenue for several blocks (at least from around 30th St. through Steinway or so).


It's November 6.

We're barely halfway through fall, and now they've got these things up, for what, the next two months at least? Winter in this city is long and depressing enough--this only makes it feel longer. What's the point? Seriously, who's in charge of this, some neighborhood organization? Why would they string up winter decorations right during the peak fall foliage? It looks awful and just stretches out the misery of winter. I remember them doing this last year, too. Anyone know how we can change this?

Guys, please, read the original post (and my follow-up comment) a little more carefully. It's not about the existence of winter/Christmas decorations. It's about the timing and how it's way too early. I even put it in bold and italics in that first message.

These things are all fine in moderation, when it's, you know, actually Christmastime and/or winter. Like in December. However, this being the first week of November, 'tis not the season. 'Tis halfway through the fall. 'Tis the case that there are still leaves on the trees! 'Tis, in other words, yet another example of the creeping length of the Christmas season, which seems to get earlier every year, which makes it drag on forever, which ruins the fun of it all.
