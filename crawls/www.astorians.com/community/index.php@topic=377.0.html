Kil - I can understand where you're coming from. When we were both interns and making very little money ($400 - $500 altogether in a month) we had to go and buy $0.20 boxes of pasta (they were on sale), and live on that for a long time. We cooked A LOT, and were able to make some pretty good and satisfying meals even on our low income. And we didn't exactly work full time either.

Now, though, we're both working full time - and we rarely buy instant, microwaveable foods. And we rarely, if ever, buy fast food. We still cook a lot, and we make pretty good meals. Sure, sometimes we don't eat all that healthfully, but a lot of times we do, too. I'll be honest with you - I am exhausted when I come home from work. All I want to do is curl up on the couch and watch a rerun of Charmed. But I'm also starving. So I usually snack for a little bit as I watch that episode of Charmed (winding down usually comes first with me, and I can't resist the fantasy world of Charmed for that), and then I usually make something after that for supper.

I don't always cook for us, but it's better. I honestly think that if people want to eat well, and know what they are putting into their bodies (not just buying whatever chemical-filled processed food is on the grocery store shelf), then they will *make* time. Even when I do make supper I honestly don't always want to. I used to enjoy cooking a lot more. I don't that much anymore because it has become a burden to a certain extent - but I owe it to myself and my family to make dinner every now and then. So many people are using the "I don't have time or energy" excuse to not take care of themselves. Just think of the diseases that people have that are probably owed to the buildup of artificial chemicals in their bodies. It's "Supersize Me" played out for so many people every single day. Ugh.

While in theory shopping on 30th Ave is possible for me, in practice I just don't make it down there often.  My own laziness.  I do like the International Meat Market, and it's good to know they carry d'Artagnan.  

Here are a few of my picks closer to Ditmars.

Rosario's under the Ditmars N stop has a good selection of cheeses - I'd say primarily Italian and Spanish but some others, too - as well as salumi.  His prices are good.  Also a good selection of imported and gourmet packaged foods.

Mediterranean Foods II in Agora Plaza.  Anybody ever tried the butcher shop next to them?

I second Top Tomato.  I just wish they carrried more (rephrase that: any) organic produce.

I go to the butcher on Ditmars between 33d and 35th.  They don't have a huge variety, but at least I can pick the piece I want.  The quality is fine.  They carry Bell & Evans, but only whole chickens.

I don't eat a lot of pasta, but there's a (I believe fresh) pasta shop on 23rd Ave. between 31st and 33rd.

Fish I buy in Manhattan.  I do not like the choices around Ditmars.

Liquor store - Dominicks on Astoria Blvd and I think 29th St. is supposed to have a really good selection, but I've never been personally, so I could be proved wrong.  I usually go to Jolsson's - their selection is average, but they'll order you anything you want that they can get.

Little Bangladeshi place on Ditmars between 32nd and 33rd - more or less next to Fresca Tortilla - carries freshly made samosas and a good, cheap selection of dried lentils, chana dal, etc.

And finally, you can sometimes unearth some interesting things in the Trade Fair on Ditmars between 37th and 38th.  I just go in and poke around sometimes.

Oh - really finally, except this isn't near Ditmars - the place called Original Mexican Food on 29th St. just off 30th Ave. carries quite a wide array of Mexican fresh produce, dried chiles and canned goods.  They also have a full menu of very tasty Mexican standards.  Ok, so I've only eaten there once, but it was good.
