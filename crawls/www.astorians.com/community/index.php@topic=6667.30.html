The transient nature of things has definitely not been good, though some of these landlords...  the last few years has made it so that most people moving here don't even know what rent-stabilized means. Heck, people think "key money' is the norm and isn't what it really is: Extortion. I never heard of key money until I started reading this board.

Not all landlords are evil, I know of some that only bump up the rent a little when people move, and they usually work on the apartments before renting it out again - but then there are those that just jack up the rent every chance they get, and these developers building these new buildings and expecting them to all rent out at these super high prices. I'm wondering how much money these developers have and if they might not run into serious trouble if no one takes their bait and rents with them. The subprime fiasco is set to land a lot of people in southeast queens in foreclosure soon, will overdevelopment/not enough people willing to rent at these high prices result in foreclosed brand new buildings? We'll see I suppose. That new building by the subway btw Bway and 31st av still has a 'for rent' banner on it months (or has it been a year already) after being completed. Who can and wants to move into these places with the high rents? Who's going to move into all of these QP places, around the corner from the strip club and where the Rikers bus drops off the newly released inmates?
