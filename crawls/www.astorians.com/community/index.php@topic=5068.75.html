I just remember the protest signs around Astoria and the heated townhall meetings.
Months and months of planning went those protests. I felt then, the same as I do now, that the line would have changed the Astoria community immeasurably and all for the convenience of Manhattanites. It was during a period when the Astoria/LIC neighborhood was referred to as the parking lot of Long Island. I always figured, if they can afford Manhattan rents then they can afford to pay for a flat-rate cab, yellow or otherwise. At the time we were willing to make a concession if they would have agreed to go underground. They probably would have run out of money anyway, but the longer, and louder, we protested, the surer we were that the plan would be abandoned. And, it was.
