The exit polls at closing time in Ohio during the 2004 presidential election had Kerry winning 51% to Bush 48%. Strangely enough the percentage flipped immediately after. Exit poll discrepancy are almost never more than 1%, and yet they flipped drastically after voting ended. Astoundingly this happened mainly in Democratic majority districts. Election fraud by tampered Diebold voting machine was suspected. Various independent audits made afterwards concluded that the odds of such a dramatic about face for Republican presidential candidate by those having cast their votes for Democratic local positions are almost astronomical. All investigations conclude that there was voting machine software manipulation. Alot of Republicans leaders have been advocating against voter fraud, and are pushing for voter ID requirements at the polls, which would greatly disenfranchise minorities and senior citizens. Many people who fall into these categories are living on a limited income and do not have the accepted photo ID, such as a drivers license, a state issued ID, or a passport, and are unlikely to go through the expense and the sometimes difficult process of obtaining one. In reality, true instances of voter fraud are scarce. The truth is election fraud is what we as citizens have to be seriously alarmed about.
