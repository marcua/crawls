Title: No such thing as rent stabilized anymore...
Post by: Jtrane on January 10, 2006, 12:20:16 PM
http://www.housingnyc.com/html/resources/dhcr/dhcr40.html

Good ol' Pataki.  He managed to sneak this in under the radar back in 2003.  To summarize, what this essentially means is...

Chapter 82 of the laws of 2003 for New York State.

If you sign a lease with a "Preferential" monthly rent (a rent lower than what the landlord can legally charge),  when that lease expires, the landlord can raise your rent to the maximum legal rent on the renewal lease.

So for example, if back in 2003, before the big boom started in Astoria, landlords were having a slightly more difficult time filling vacancies,  they would give you a preferential rent.  Let's say the legal amount they could have charged was $1100/ month.  But they needed to get someone in, so they only charge $600/month.  Late in 2003, Pataki sneeks in chapter 82.  2005 rolls around, Astoria is now super hip, people moving up here all the time.  Apartments are now hard to find.  Landlord decides they can get a lot more for that previously undesirable apartment.  Lease renewal received.  Boom, they want an additional $500/month.  No legal protection for the tenants.

I'm fortunate, and my landlord is not killing me in this way.  But if they wanted to, they could essentially price me out of my apartment, i.e, my home. 

So, questions...
How does this kind of thing slip into the law of the land?  Why weren't people outraged by this?  Is it a coincidence that this was passed by a republican majority NYS legislature, and signed by a republican governor?  Yeah, maybe I'm being a partisan hack, but hey, this just seems to be another big example of the lack of concern towards "regular working folks" like myself, and most, if not all of my friends while tilting the platform again in the favor of the rich.

I'm not saying landlords shouldn't  have rights to make money on an apartment.  But to be able to force mid to lower income people out of their homes so easily, even if those people lived there for a very long time,  at the time of lease renewal seems highly unethical.

Also in this law, all apartments above $2000/month have been deregulated.  There is NO rent protection for any apartment above $2,000/month in New York.  Makes you feel all warm and fuzzy inside, huh?  Way to look out for your fellow human!
