Gramiltants can be so tiring sometimes . . . does it really matter?

To me it seem tht the usage of depends on whether the situation is formal (appointment), or informal (date).

Appointment is simply an arrangement to meet, usually within a business context.

Date on the other hand, is an agreement to meet, with a specified time being an important consideration, usually within a social context, especially a romantic/sexual one.

I gather the trouble creeps in when the situation is neither entirely in the realm of the business or the social.

For instance, would you be make a “date” to meet your ex to discuss your children or would you make an “appointment”? I guess it all depends on how well you get on with your ex!

For meeting people you know well -- it’s a date; for business and for people you don’t know well (or know well but do not look forward to meeting) -- it’s an appointment.
