I wouldn't support Palin as a candidate in 2012, personally.  At least not in a primary.  I think she has a long way to go before being Commander-in-Chief material.  But there are large factions of the conservative movement who are unequivocally for her and are psyched at the possibility of 2012.  Particularly the people of faith you mentioned above.  For my part, I would like to see a ticket involving Governor Jindal of Louisiana.  There is a smart young guy with a real American story and a bright future.

Image and core values will both take care of themselves.  The conservative movement has always been a movement of opposition.  Because of the last few years, people have forgotten that and are speaking as though the entire Republican party is sunk.  Remember, close to 50 percent of people voted Republican, and much can happen between election cycles to swing people on the cusp.  Also, I think this will be a good time for the Republican party to clean house of the excesses of being the party in power.

While I will concede that Obama is more of an intellectual type, I am concerned about his lack of experience.  His level of experience is basically comparable to Palin's.  Also, he is not totally innocent of demagoguery in his own right, I'm sure you'll agree.
