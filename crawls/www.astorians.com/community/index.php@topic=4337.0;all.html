Those speakers are likely to have their amplifiers powered by a dc converter that plugs into your electrical powerstrip and is kinda wide, you can't miss it.  Maybe that thing has shorted out and stopped providing dc current to the speakers?  Then the problem might just be where the audio cable plugs into the sound section of your computer (either a separate PCI card that plugs into your motherboard, or it's built into the motherboard and is a plug next to the parallel printer/serial/usb plugins).  A third possibilities are that you may have had other software or other bugs in the operating system recently dealt with.  After whatever changes were done, the software driver that runs the soundcard / motherboard sound components were erased or changed.  It'll be possible to download new drivers over the internet, install them and make the sound return.

So, first try the ipod-type wire that plugs into the computer, make sure it hasn't been inadvertantly pulled out.  Try the other end at the speakers.  Then make sure the dc adapter is plugged into the AC current, and that any wire leading into the speakers is also securely attached.  If all else fails, post here and I'll respond.  OR JUST OFFER ME SOME YUMMY TRUFFLES AND I'LL COME OVER. :angel:

Before you start to panic that it's the speakers or the computer...  Take the mini plug out of the computer and plug it into your iPod or some other portable type device and see if you get any sound.   If you do, then your computer settings are screwed up somehow.   If you don't, you may have a cable or power issue with your speakers.   Always dumb it down as much as possible and do the simplest things first.

Good luck....   and by the way, it's "sharing" not "downloading"    :angel:
