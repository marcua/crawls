Also, clean the filter. Or go to a harware store and get a new filter.  A small amount of obstruction can make a considerable difference.

In regard to your item #2, the work an ac does is related to the difference between the temperature in the room and that outside.  A machine that keeps the room at 72 when it's 80 outside may keep it at 82 when it's 90 outside.

It also takes work to remove moisture from the air, and the ac does that as a side-effect of its effort to cool. When it is very humid, much of its capacity will be used removing moisture until the inside air is dry. It is wise to keep the a/ced part of the apartment closed off from the rest. Now you know why your dad always yelled at you to close the damn door.

If you have a top-floor apartment, the sun on the roof is a major contributor of heat. Depending on whether your roof is well-insulated, it may be that you couldn't afford enough air conditioning to keep it comfortable on a summer afternoon.

Starwind posted a link somewhere to the consumers union air conditioner requirements estimator form. That thing is pretty accurate. The requirements are related to the area of each boundry between the inside and the outsize, what that boundary is composed of, and what the temperature difference should be, plus a factor for each person and each power-consuming device in the air-conditioned area.
