Dear Handyman!

As the kind fellow who helped me put up my CD shelves 18 months or more ago, the least I can do is to contribute some of my faves!

YES to previous comments about The Pogues' "Fairytale of NY" (with the sadly missed Kirsty MacColl), YES to Nat 'King' Cole with "The Christmas Song" (just so timeless with all his chestnut roasting), and YES to Andy Williams "Most Wonderful Time Of The Year" (even though - like everyone else - he mimed it at the Macy's Thanksgiving Parade...) but a few more to toss into the mix...

Smashing Pumpkins - "Christmastime" (cheesy, sappy, but hey, it's Christmas!)
Counting Crows - "A Long December" (not Christmas strictly speaking, but a lovely, moody track)
Eels - "Christmas Is Going To The Dogs" (rarer, but such a cool sentiment for us cynics...)

- and of course ANYTHING from the Phil Spector Christmas Album (whatever your thoughts on his guilt / innocence!)

Happy Holidays one and all!
