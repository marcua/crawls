Tasos charged us $50 dollars EACH for two credit reports, and never thought to MENTION a discount in his broker's fee. We put a down payment on the place, paid for the credit report, and went home leaving it up to Tasos to contact the landlord and set up a meeting...
We met the landlord's wife who gave us the ok and signed the lease. Tasos gave her a copy. We also paid her 1600 dollars as a security deposit. Tasos told us that he would set up a meeting so that we could meet the Landlord in a day or two. He did not give us the keys, or the copy of the lease, just a receipt for the down payment, and one for the $100 dollars in credit checks. (Other broker's charged us $20 bucks for two, last year, and provided us with the paperwork, meaning our private credit information & Social Security) Tasos didnt give us any of that, he kept it. Not minding his own business, he keeps complaining about us not having his "fee." His $1600 dollars for driving his farty Benz around the block, ONCE. He starts telling my wife "well this is what you should do, have your husband go to his bank, and take out my fee and you can pay him back when your check comes from out of state. When I heard this i knew the guy had problems. First of all, the law says that he can charge a commission for procuring and obtaining the apartment for us, as of right now there is someone else living in it, yet he's harrassing my wife when Im at work talking to her like she was late in paying him. I yelled at him enough for him to back off. He started with his little attitude on the phone with me telling me "thats not how it works in this office." Yeah Tasos, i hope youre reading this because now the Department Of State wants to know exactly how it does work in that office!!!
So the last time I saw him I put the original listing under his nose and i felt him grow uncomfortable, the way people get when you catch them in a lie, jittery, nervous a bit. He starts with his slick talking act, then explains how this was "just a promotion for that one apartment." Yeah Tasos, go figure.
