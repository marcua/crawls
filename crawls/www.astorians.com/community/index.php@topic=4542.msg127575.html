 :-o It's been one of those 24 48 72 hours weeks.  

I came home from work Thursday, swearing I would not stay up late again.  I did.  I don't work on Fridays, so I should have been able to sleep late, wake up refreshed, and get things done.  No!  I spent all day in front of the tv, all evening in front of the computer.  In between I did my exercise routine 3 times in an effort to be able to bend over and take the polish off my toenails.  The night was about to become the next day when I had to make the crucial decision - put it off for the next day and go to bed then and there (the intelligent thing to do) or push myself, literally, and get the polish off, take my shower, and loosen up my muscles enough to put another coat of polish on (the whacky thing to do).  I went for the whack.  Freshly showered, girlishly shaved, with a fresh coat of Iced Spice on fingers and toes - I went back to the computer.  And the tv.  At 5 a.m. the birds started singing.  I couldn't resist staying up - I had two weeks of laundry to do.  I would get an early start.  I went to the bathroom and started putting on my makeup.  I had bought this color scheme a few months ago, and was finally trying it for the first time.  I was also trying a new product for the first time - a loose powder eyeliner that comes with an applicator the diamter of a pencil barrel.  It makes a fat line.  It's for the "smokey eye look".  It looks like Johnny Depp's makeup in Pirates of the Caribbean.  Now in all the time I've been putting on makeup, and for all the times I've read that you should hold a tissue against your cheek to catch any loose powder shadow falling on your face, I've never done that because I've never had any eyeshadow powder fall on my cheek.  Till today.  A few grains of black powder fell on my cheek, and I knew that nothing but a vacuum cleaner would get it off without smudging.  I tried to lightly brush it away, but no, it smeared.  Not only did I have Jack Sparrow's eyeliner, I had his 5 o'clock shadow.  I put on more face powder to cover it.

My one son, the genius, you know the type, G-d knows everything but Michael knows it all better . . . kept putting off sending me a copy of his wedding video. It cost too much to make a copy, it is encrypted with expert copy protection, what happens of I send it to you and it gets lost in the mail . . . CAN YOU SAY BULLSHIT?

So yesterday I get a bubble bag with his return address. Inside is a post-it note from my daughter-in-law stuck on a plastic cover of a DVD. It says, "Mom, Sorry it took so long. Happy Mother's Day. Love, Wifey & Mike" So, I called her to thank her and promised to send it back by Tuesday. "And, oh, yes, I made a few copies so that you can give your Mom & Dad a copy, and perhaps Mike's best friend who lives 3500 miles away a copy, and maybe even the Best man and the Maid of Honor copies . . . and please tell your genius husband he should buy a Macintosh!

Then I sat down and watched the damn thing.

I raised my kids in a somewhat sparse and simple lifestyle. I taught them manners and humility with grace and self-dignity. I taught them to appreciate having each other and how to give love and be charitable without any expectations from life.

From this relatively non-materialist world came the child who had the most lavish, wedding ever imaginable. I am so glad that I was home alone today because I was yelling at the screen.

I guess I should also explain that they live in Los Angeles. That they are both involved in the HOLLYWOOD industry. So, the venue was gifted to them by Warner Brothers Studios. They were given an acre of a lot plus the use of a fully equipped restaurant set and a sound stage. The wedding planner had to hire a union stage crew and a florist to decorate the outdoor venue; a chef for the pre-wedding hors deurves served by a couple dozen little college students dressed all in lavender, along with the string quartet; a chef for dinner and a crew of union electricians to hang the the ambient lighting and the hanging monitors when they played their wedding montage (you can watch it without having to schlep yourself to CA). The money this affair cost could have fed a family of 5 for 10 years and still had enough left over to make half their mortgage, car and college fund payments. Everyone thought it was magnificent. I thought it was ostentatious, but I kept my mouth shut. I did everything by the mother-in-law book: I offered no opinions, I agreed with all the plans (for all four days of this affair) and I wore grey!
