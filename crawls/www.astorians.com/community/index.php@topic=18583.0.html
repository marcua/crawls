I've been a vegan for many years. I can usually find at least a couple of options on most restaurant menus that will suit me. When eating Italian, order pasta with vegetables and hold the cheese. For Greek, one can feast on a good portion of the appetizers. Mexican cuisine there are bean tacos and burritos, as well as a few veggie options. At standard diner places, you have veggie burgers and grilled veggie sandwiches.

There are of course some nice vegetarian restaurants if you know where to look. Here's a good link to the best NYC has to offer:

http://supervegan.com/toprated.php

I had dinner at Brick a few nights ago, and was SO excited to see printed on the bottom of their menu: "Ask your server about our vegetarian and vegan dishes". So I inquired about their vegan dishes - and was given an entire separately printed menu! While significantly smaller than their regular menu, I was extremely impressed to see the amount of selections they have - more than one choice in every category (appetizers, entrees, and desserts).

As a vegan, I am used to ordering salads, pasta in oil, etc. at most places. (When socializing, it's always more about my company than the food anyway.) But whenever I do find places (especially local) that offer vegan options, I am beyond thrilled and always make a point to spread the word.

I think this was posted in previous "vegan" threads, but Seva as well as Mojave also have some nice vegan options.
