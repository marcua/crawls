Title: Astoria: The next development wave -- Durst's Hallet's Cove
Post by: 28Grand on September 29, 2014, 08:27:51 AM
As we all know, the big city real estate types are now looking beyond Manhattan (and Brooklyn to some extent) to Queens in general and Astoria in particular.

Much of the media attention so far has been focused on the Astoria Cove project, which has become the poster child in the Mayor’s battle to secure more affordable housing for the city.

But there are several other projects in the pipeline including another Alma mega block in LIC and now the Durst family.

From the link in the NY Times article below:

“Times are changing,” said Douglas Durst, a third-generation developer. “Large-scale office development opportunities are sparse and Manhattan land is cost-prohibitive to build rentals. It is time for the family to go deeper into residential and to cross the ocean to Astoria.”

I’m a pragmatist, these project will go ahead and I don’t think there’s much anyone can do to stop them, there’s too much money involved.  Plus, I’m not certain they should be stopped. The city needs more housing.

But, I do feel that the developers need to give back more to the communities they build in. For this project, 20% of the units are labeled as affordable and Durst is building what appears to be senior housing in a deal with the NYHA on a portion of the Astoria Houses. In addition, the NY Times article states that this new complex "will open up the waterfront to local residents with an esplanade and include a supermarket, retail space and a school. The city has promised better bus service, and there is talk of a ferry to Manhattan".

But I’m left wondering, is this enough? What do other people think could be done to benefit the neighborhood as a whole?
