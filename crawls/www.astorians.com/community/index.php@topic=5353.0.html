I live on Theodore Avenue (42nd Street)... that's not on your list, I see.  So if anyone else can add on, that'd be cool to see all the varieties.  I do know that Queens was made up of individual villages with different governments, so that is why a street with one name now, used to have a few different names.  They renumbered the streets to make it easier to navigate.  Also, the roads/drives/territories come into play because they made the grid similar to Manhattan: Avenues and Streets.  Soon, people realized they needed more streets in between, so roads/drives/territories were added.

if you're interested:

Theodore (42 street) and Albert (41 Street) were named after Steinway's sons.

The Briell family were gardeners who lived at the corner of Newtown and Grand (30th) Avenues.
