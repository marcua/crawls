The little flowers are a reward for doing the right thing - getting up early and getting things done, or encouragement to stay on the path of doing the right thing.  No little flowers are awarded for falling asleep with the lights, tv, and makeup on and then sleeping till noon in an attempt to recover.  I eventually went across the street and bought Breyers strawberry ice cream, and it fed my brain nicely.

The sugar that glues the bouquet, and the sugar that surrounds each piece of the bouquet, make the pieces brittle, so when I take a piece off it's usually part of flower or leaf, not the whole thing, so a tiny bouquet lasts longer (to eat) than one would think, and of course I don't eat a piece every day, so I have a lot of the eating bouquet left.

I'm not sure if I mentioned this before, but when the artist put the bouquet boxes in a bag for me, he pointed out that the bag was not made of plastic, it was made of corn.  Not that I'm going to eat the bag, just saying that corn is a viable alternative to plastic.
