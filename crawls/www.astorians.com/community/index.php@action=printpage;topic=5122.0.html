Title: Re: Diamond Rings: How Much?
Post by: NYCMacUser on February 11, 2007, 09:31:46 PM
Quote from: enigmacat on February 11, 2007, 08:55:05 PM
I believe that the Atlantic Monthly article referenced in the blood diamond thread showed that it is very difficult, if not impossible, to sell a diamond ring for anything near what you paid for it.

I had believed that women made such a fuss about the size/quality/cost of the ring because if the guy walked under a bus or ran off with his secretary, they could sell the ring and at least cover a few month's rent.

But it sounds like they aren't a good investment.
Well, it all depends on what you would consider a good investment. An included stone of any type is not a good investment. A practically perfect stone, with a perfect table, with flawless facets, set in a better quality metal, can be a great investment. When you go to buy a diamond, you *need* to know what makes a good stone; you need to have a relationship with your gem dealer; you need to have at least 2 independent appraisals from accredited gem labs that value the stone at least 3 times what you will be paying for it—that is a very good investment quality stone. There are 2 sides to the Blood Diamond argument. I fully understand the one that says diamonds only devalue. That stems from the activists who would rather not see any diamonds mined. The other side of that argument is history itself. People who make an investment in a quality diamond understand that you *never* buy a stone from a jeweler; you buy from a gem dealer. And you *never* sell a stone to a jeweler or a gem dealer, you sell it to either a buyer or at an auction where quality estate jewelry is being sold.

If you bought a fine quality 2 carat stone in 1976 for $2,500—unset—and then spent an additional $1,500 on the casting, that ring today would probable fetch $35,000–$40,000. Remember you haven't been paying taxes on it like an ordinary investment. A diamond is not a short-term investment by any means.

I personally, have very mixed feelings on the subject, but I do understand both side of the argument.
