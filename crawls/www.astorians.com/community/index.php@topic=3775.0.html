My new airplane rant
« on: October 12, 2006, 09:13:28 AM »
So I got back from Miami this week, and let me say it was the absolute worst experience flying with American Airlines.  The return flight was supposed to leave MIA at 8:45pm, and get into LGA at 11:35pm Sunday night.  About halfway into boarding the plane, the airline announced they were performing maintenance on the plane, assured us all was well, and just to sit tight.  About a half hour later, another announcement came on that the plane was going out of service, and for us to go back to the gate while they "found us another airplane".  About two hours later, we finally took off at 10:45pm.  About an hour into the flight, the pilot comes on.  He says he has "bad news".  Due to budget cuts, LGA is now closed after midnight, and there will be no one to direct us on the runway.  Therefore we are being diverted to JFK.  During all this, the staff was rude and impersonable, no food or compensation was offered in any way, the only consolation we seemed to have was the now free $2 headphones so we could watch the inflight movie.  The staff still hawked their $4.00 snackboxes, and about half the plane didn't even get beverages due to turburlence.  It was 12:50am when we finally got to JFK, and they announced that 2 vans were outside to take passengers back to LGA.  2 vans for 18 people, yet there were 200 people on the plane.  What kind of logic was this??  No apology, and once we deboarded no staff remained in sight.  It took a long time to get our luggage, then we had to find where these vans were with no help from American Airlines.  Upon arriving at LGA, we had to coax the driver into dropping us off downstairs near the cab station and car area, instead of the now closed entrance to the American Airlines terminal.  Four of us had to pile into one cab, because there were not many cabs coming into a closed airport at that time of night.

I live about an 8-minute drive away from LGA airport.   Arriving at JFK at 1am was not what I had in mind.  I had to go to work the next day.  I did not get home until 2:15am, and subsequently had only 5 hours of sleep before work the next day.  I was also sick, and being that I had not eaten anything throughout that ordeal, my condition was made worse.  Throughout all this, no apologies were made nor any form of compensation offered.  Not to mention, on the flight to MIA from LGA, the staff was also very rude and unfriendly.  The response to my innocent question about pillows, was a condescending look and “We don’t do pillows”.  The least he could have said was “I’m sorry, but we no longer stock pillows”, but he was instead rude, and it was completely unnecessary.  The same treatment was given to questions about blankets, only 20 of which were provided for the entire packed plane.

What kind of company is this that treats their customers like this?  I have written a letter to their corporate offices, and don't expect much of a response.  But I will let you know if I get one.  Countless people have told me how much better JetBlue would have handled this, and I'm not likely to fly American Airlines anymore.
