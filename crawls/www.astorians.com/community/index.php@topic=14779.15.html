Housing prices are (almost) always a lagging indicator. Just like jobs, but they're the last thing to rebound in a down economy. If the jobs come back, then house prices follow.

Sure, Astoria might be a little bit of an "in-demand" area, but there is no way that prices continue to be so high. Either people don't sell and the surrounding areas go lower in price, and that results in a price drop here, or people do sell at lower prices, pushing prices down. With the possibility of deflation, wage-cuts, higher unemployment, and lower rental costs, housing prices have nothing to do but fall in this area. I've always heard Astoria likened to Manhattan as far as the market goes, and Manhattan's prices have fallen nearly 25%. Factor in the glut of new building here...and I think it's all on the downslope.

Now, the depreciation of (artificially, perhaps never existent) prices is a good thing. It allows more equality for those who don't have homes to be able to purchase, not just the ones who have been here forever or the super rich.

For those who are interested, median prices in the Astoria zip codes 11102, 11103, 11105 and 11106

Past year: -20%
From peak: -30%

I gather this includes different types of properties, single family, multi-family, condos/coops etc.

Ditmars area is the one bright spot in the Astoria real estate landscape. Prices went up in the last year and it has had the smallest decline from peak of all the zip codes.


11102
Median price 519K -- down by 18% (from 635K) year on year from April 08 to April 09.

The market peaked in early 2008 with a median asking price of 823K making current prices almost 37% lower than peak.


11103
Median price 540K -- down by 25% (from 762K) year on year from April 08 to April 09.

The market peaked in mid-2008 with a median asking price of 832K making current prices about 35% lower than peak.


11105
Median price 715K -- up 3.5% (from 690K) year on year from April 08 to April 09.

The market peaked in late 2007 with a median asking price of 797K making current prices around 10% lower than peak.


11106
Median price 430K -- down by 40% (from 700K) year on year from April 08 to April 09.

Statistics for 11106 are odd. There must have been a big sale in 2001 that skewed the numbers because that looks to be the peak year with a median asking price of 2.1M making current prices over 80% lower than peak. I used 40% lower from peak as the average.



The number of sales have plunged in all zip codes.



edit: numbers from www.trulia.com
