Anyone have troubles with Aris Crescent LLC?
« on: August 06, 2009, 12:53:07 PM »
We recently moved to an apartment owned/operated by Aris Crescent LLC.  Our first rent check was due by August 1st.  After finding out that their office closes @ 5pm, but they have a drop-off mail slot for afterhours, I dropped the check in their mail slot on Friday evening (July 31st).  The check was in an envelope that was given to us by management a week earlier and I clearly labeled it with the proper information.

My concern is that I've called several times this week to confirm they recieved my payment, yet I always get shrugged off.

1st Call - Monday: I was told that checks are being entered into the system and I would have to call back at a later time to verify they received it.

2nd Call - Tuesday:  After giving them my address, they told me that it was another person's (Christine?) property and that she was not in the office... I was told to call back on Wednesday to speak with her.

3rd Call - Wednesday: I was told the same thing as Tuesday.  When I made it clear that I was told Tuesday to call Wednesday, they said "yeah, but she (Christine?) didn't come in today.  I also made it clear that the payment was due by August 5th otherwise it is considered late.  If I can't verify if you recieved my payment from Friday evening, then I can't know if I need to give you another check.  The person had no response, and again simply told me to call back tomorrow (Thursday).

4th Call - Thursday:  Today I am told that checks are being entered into the system and should be processed by Friday... so now the responses have come full-circle and I'm back to what I was told on Monday.

So now all I can do is wait to see if either the funds are taken from my bank account, or if I get a past due notice from management.

Anyone ever have a similar problem?  If I end up getting a past due notice, then I have no way of proving that I actually submitted a payment that was lost/not received.  The carbon copy of my check only proves that I wrote the check, not that I delivered it.  I'm curious if others have similar or even different problems with this management company that I can protect myself from in the future.

Thanks.

From the rent guidelines board FYI

Am I entitled to a receipt for my rent payments?

Landlords must provide tenants with a written receipt when rent is paid in cash, a money order, a cashier's check or in any form other than personal check of a tenant. Where a tenant pays the rent by personal check, (s)he may request in writing a rent receipt from the landlord. The receipt must state the payment date, the amount, the period for which the rent was paid, and the apartment number. The receipt must be signed by the person receiving the payment and state his or her title. (Real Property Law §§235-e)

We lived in the same complex for about 2 years until this past May, never had a problem with paying rent. We always used those envelopes they stuck in the doors and mailed in a personal check around the 1st of the month.

However - good luck getting through to ANYONE at Aris Crescent. They never return phone calls, Christine (the person in charge of our complex) is impossible to reach. While the receptionists there know the answers, they never would volunteer to answer your questions. I remember calling 2, 3 times with a simple question about our lease, and I was told "Christine will call you back". Nothing for a whole week. Then my fiance called and it occurred to him to ask the receptionist, and he got an answer right then and there.

Other than that, it's a great apartment complex. When we first moved in, there were some small bugs, and immediately the exterminators were sent in every week, and not a problem since. The heat and hot water went off for about 12 hours in the winter two years ago, but whatever it was, it was fixed and not a problem since. The Super (Stanley) was prompt and friendly in fixing anything. Right before we moved out they renovated the laundromat, a shame we didn't get to take advantage of those.

Good luck!!
