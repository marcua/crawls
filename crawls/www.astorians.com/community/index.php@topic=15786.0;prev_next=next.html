Quote from: ATB24 on July 16, 2009, 09:00:48 AM
With services like a cable modem you are "sharing" the internet with the whole neighborhood, so if everyone else is on at once, you might be able to feel it.
Absolutely correct.

Oversimplified: with a Cable/coaxial connection, there is one large virtual "pipe" which everyone in a certain radius shares.  If very few people are accessing the internet via this "pipe", your share of the dataflow through the pipe is only capped by the download speed you pay for (10 mbps, 30 mbps, etc.).

However, if more people start are accessing the pipe (say, like, a whole bunch of kids who are out of school and now online either updating thier facebook pages, twittering or playing World of Warcraft), then everyone's share of the pipe is throttled down in order to allow everyone access.  So you may be getting 10mbps speeds in the morning, but 2mbps speed in the evening.  So in this case, you are getting far less bandwidth than you are paying for.

This is why I have DSL.  With DSL, your bandwidth is capped at far less than what cable is at the same price (3mbps vs. 10mbps, usually).  But, since you are accessing the internet via your own phone line and not a shared line, your bandwidth speed is maintained no matter what time of the day you go online.  So you actually get what you pay for.

The bad part about DSL is: it's more expensive and the speed is decreased the further away you live from the "Central Station".  Our Verison Central Station is located on 30th Street near 30th Avenue, so I'm good.  So the closer you live to 30th and 30th, the better.
