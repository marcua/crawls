Wow, I missed out on this whole jean discussion today! I'm very pear-shaped, so I've had my problems as well. My $0.02:

On Gap - I have a pair of their "curvy" fit jeans and like them a lot. I was never able to wear their jeans before those, and I must admit they fit quite nicely. And price-wise, I don't think they're bad at all. Considering everyone I work with wears these $150 - $300 jeans, I consider $60 or so a good price for a good pair of jeans.

On Lucky Brand - I have a pair of these. There's a store at 22nd and Broadway. I found when I tried them on that they weren't fitting me too great, until I landed on the right cut. They have a lot of different cuts, so you have to experiment. I was kinda mortified spending $100 for jeans, but the sales lady told me they'd be my favorite pair -- and she was right.  :-) So that was well-spent I think.

On H&M - I have horrible luck with their pants. I stick to skirts and tops there... but maybe they've started to make more sizes and cuts. I usually found everything there seemed to be made for a stick-thin model type.

Other - My favorite place for pants/jeans is the Limited, but there isn't one in the city. So anytime I'm near a Limited (home, or in DC), I always hit one up. Also Banana Republic, oddly enough, fit me pretty well. But I always have to get their stuff taken up. I feel like lately, manufactuers have been responding to the concept of women having hips, which I truly appreciate. Or maybe I'm just getting more adventurous with brands (Gap sorta opened the door on that one - that and not living near a Limited anymore)!

On handbags - I LONG to drop a decent amount of money on a really really great one, but feel like the trends change so much that it would really need to be a well-thought-out decision. The Dior saddle-bag makes me salivate, but it's also approximately 1.5 month's pay, so that's not going to happen.

Meanwhile, I raid Target, or Canal, for my quick-fixes. I love how on Canal now, it's this whole covert operation where they take you to a hidden room to buy bags. Since they got sued by the expensive bag designers, they can't sell them in those booths facing the street anymore, so instead you get to feel like you're breaking the law or something, which I must admit is too fun.  :-D

And I've got no guilt about not giving my money to LVMH or Prada or whoever. Nobody buying these bags is buying them INSTEAD of a real Chanel bag. They're buying them because they CAN'T AFFORD a real Chanel bag. So they need to get over themselves!
