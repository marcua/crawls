Well in an attempt to establish a brief glossary for those who asked:

Authentic Mexican Food Items for the New Yorker

1) sopes = grilled corn tortillas served open-face with pinto beans, cotijo (crumbly white cheese), lettuce, pico de gallo and grilled meat of your choice piled high. Do not confuse this with "sopas" or actual soups.
2) pozole = stew (soup!) that's pretty hearty and has a corn-meal (harina) base-- its like polenta... lots of chorizo, veggies, or albondigas (meatballs) traditionally. Garnished with radishes (a oaxaca favorite).
3) nopales = cactus leaves (great in the aformentioned pozole!)... often times grilled. Sometimes has the texture of okra. No needles, don't worry!
3) huaraches = thick "cradles" of masa (corn meal dough) filled with the usual suspects, and sometimes freshly shredded cabbage... the shape of the little corn meal cradle resembles the shoe  and so the "huarache" sandal.
4) tortas = mexican sandwich (the chorizo at Tacos Mexico is the BEST!) with all the usual food groups including mayo or chipotle sauce oftentimes. The bread is key here: they should use a roll that's not too dense and slightly sweet (like a fluffly pan de leche)
5) bunuelos = deep fried tortilla triangles (they puff up) sprinkled with cimmamon and sugar. Usually a party treat with other postres (desserts).

Is that good? My oblique dream is to someday bring the authentico fish taco to the plates of New York at a street-stand price... anyone want to put up the capital for Annie's Fish Taco Stand? Don't forget the fresh chopped radishes and cilantro... You'll never go to Fresca Tortilla again!

hasta luego!
