A few friends and I tried this place Friday night.  I had a bad experience the first time around but was giving it a second chance.  Although we were again seated in the awful table right near the window, the service was at least a bit better this time around.  We actually got our waters and silverware, so I suppose that's a step up.  I really want to like this place but damn, it just needs a lot of work.  The layout is so strange - not a lot of seating, and the seats next to the window in the front by the bar there - those are just stools next to a table.  So when it's winter and you have a huge coat, scarf, hat and bags, guess what?  There's no where to put it all except to either wear the coat in the restaurant while you eat, or sit on it, and put the bags on the floor.  Neither of which is very comfortable.  It took a long time to get drinks - I think this was because they don't have a dedicated bartender doing table drinks, so you end up competing with all the folks at the bar - either that, or they are just slow bartenders.  And the drinks aren't that amazing - I've had better margaritas, etc elsewhere.   And now we come to the food - us 5 people got 3 round chips and a squeeze bottle of sauce - and that's supposed to serve as salsa and chips.  Lame.  Then we order entrees.   All meat and no sides, unless you count the half a slice of cornbread they give you.  No greens, no corn, no coleslaw.  Just meat - and the meat is good - but it'd be nice to have a side with it, especially when you're paying so much for it.  The half slice of cornbread is a tease. 

I don't know.  I want to like this place but there's so much better in the area.

Last night was my 3rd visit, but our first with friends.  The booth we were sat at was cramped - although maybe it was because of all of our bulky winter coats ...

Anyway, the food here is good.  I've been really wanting to try their guacamole - maybe they changed the serving size because I thought there was plenty for us - we ordered the triple one.  It was very tasty.  We also ordered the cazuela tasting - delish but not religious.  My sister ordered the fried chicken which is what I had the first time I went and thought it was the best fried chicken I'd ever had.  This time it was a bit greasy.

I think this place has a great look and the food is good enough that I feel like I should like this place more than I do.  I wouldn't not recommend it but there's just something ... we had a hard time putting our finger on it but I think we figured it out.  It's the service.  They are understaffed for their size and volume of business they were doing last night.  If they improved their service by (a) having more staff, and (b) having better trained staff, then I think this place could be great, instead of just OK. 

The service wasn't bad last night, they were just rushed and I thought just a touch inattentive.  For example, when they took our drink orders they told us they didn't have one of the things we asked for and then suggested the same exact thing to us - claiming we said something else (which we didn't).  That's fine, it's a misunderstanding, happens all the time - but over and over?  The bus boy came over and asked if we were finished with our cazuela we said yes and then he disappeared without clearing the table.  This happened several times throughout the meal.  Weird.

The impression I got was that they were overwhelmed and undertrained. 

If you haven't been here yet I definitely recommend it - just maybe not on a very busy night if you can help it.
