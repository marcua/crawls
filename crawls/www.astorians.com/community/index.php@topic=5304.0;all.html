My new year's resolution was to drink less often, and so far it's been pretty easy.  Maybe I'm just getting old (!),  but I don't react well to alcohol anymore - I will spare you the details :)  It's saving me money big time - which is great because I'm pretty broke these days, and it's saving me calories.  I usually just have a glass of wine when out with friends and then water the rest of the evening.  You can have fun without drinking, I still go out with my friends, whether to a bar, club or movie, I'm just not much of a drinker, and it's perfectly ok.  I still have tons of fun, and I'm better in the morning because of it.  Plus with work killing me all week, I hate to waste my weekends because of some stupid hangover.    Best of luck to you, I'm really happy I made this decision, and I hope you will too.

I cut down on my drinking significantly when I started dating someone who didn't really drink.  It wasn't a deliberate choice but I just starting spending so much time with a non drinker that it wore off on me.  A few months into it I found myself having fuller, non-hungover, wasted weekends.  I'm an artist and also became much more prolific from having more time and energy.  My skin looked better and I also lost weight without even trying, what girl doesn't love THAT!  It also has made me reflect on the time when I did drink more, I never realized it but I really had become a binge drinker, drinking WAY more than was necessary to have a good time. Lots of good suggestions so far. I haven't drank or done any drogas in 7 years (long story, predictable ending). It can still be difficult for me to attend some social functions where libations and favors are abound. I think people should do whatever they hell they want in whatever quantity they like, I just can't.

I concur with the specialty non-alcoholic cocktail suggestion. They make me feel pretty (ha ha). My favorites are soda water with bitters and a twist, cranberry juice and ginger ale, and a sprite screwdriver. I recommend tipping well, since the bartenders generally won't serve the person getting a coke before the gang of party-goers ordering fifteen shots of Bacardi.

In the beginning I just started playing pool (hobby) by myself a few nights a week. I was out and about, but had a task at hand, plus side is that I am lethal on the felt now.

I would also have an exit plan wherever I went, meaning that I wouldn't rely on someone else for transportation. If I went to a party, I would get myself there so I wouldn't have to deal with hauling drunk people around or waiting for people that were really into the scene. It was pretty simple, if I felt uncomfortable, I would give everyone a cordial goodbye (pounds and daps) and tell them I had a prior obligation and go home and read. Kept me from slipping many times.

Another great resource is having/finding some friends that don't like to get wasted. It can be hard (was for me, my entire social circle was looped out all the time), but eventually I got really close with some people that were fun and operated in moderation and respected my choices.

Final point of my diatribe, I had several things (locations, moods, activities) that I found were "triggers" that would make me want very much to indulge in something or other- I found that simply being aware of those triggers allowed me to navigate sticky situations with a minimum of discomfort.

Sorry if I sound preachy, feel free to send me a PM if you'd like.
