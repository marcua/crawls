I commute by bike over the bridge pretty regularly. During weekday rush hours, at least, it's fairly busy with cyclists and walkers; I imagine it would be fine Sat morning as well.

The thing that caught me off guard about it the first time - it's high! Somehow I forgot that going over requires going up...

Access on the Manhattan side is on 60th between 1st and 2nd. Theoretically you're supposed to loop around from 1st ave, but it's quicker to come the wrong way. From Astoria, just take Crescent straight down - the entrance is right next to the auto onramp for the lower level.

Maps: http://www.transalt.org/bridges/bridgif/map_queensboro.jpg
Info: http://www.transalt.org/bridges/queensboro.html

Enjoy...

i walk from astoria to rockefeller center and back every week or so. i almost never use the subway, but if i get too tired to walk back i take the q32 bus from midtown back.

my only warning about the bridge is that as you're entering or exiting the walkway watch out for bikers who ignore the warning that they must dismount and walk their bikes off the bridge. i have never seen a biker do this on queensboro or any other bridge in town but i've been buffeted by the wake of bikes roaring past at what i guess is 35-40 mph, maybe even faster. it seems dangerous as hell and i usually make sure the coast is clear of inbound speed demons before entering the walkway.  

and charge your cell phone if you're worried about this sort of thing, because all the emergency callboxes are gutted out and useless.

oh, and wave at the tram passengers if a roosevelt island tram happens to pass by. it's required.

I've walked and riden a bike over that bridge too.
