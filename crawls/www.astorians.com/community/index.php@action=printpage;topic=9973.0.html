Post by: NYCMacUser on March 30, 2008, 11:04:05 PM
I swore up and down that I would never own another dog again as long as I lived. I lasted 2 years. Watching a beloved pet get old or sickly is the worst thing in the world. I know. BTDT. My last little guy was totally blind and deaf; had to be hand or spoon fed; had no control of his bodily functions; howled when he walked into walls that he could no longer see; bit your hand if you went to pick him up and I still loved him. I swore I would never go through that ever again. I understand what you may be feeling.

I now have 2 dogs—they're little, so I got 2 of them—and, although I have never forgotten any of my pets that passed over the Rainbow Bridge, I am grateful that I still have the capacity to be loved.

Take all the time that you need to feel free to love again, and then go get yourself another companion.

The rainbow bridge poem was sent to me by a friend when my dog died and it gave me some comfort.  For those who have never read it here it is.

Just this side of heaven is a place called Rainbow Bridge.
When an animal dies that has been especially close to someone here, that pet goes to Rainbow Bridge. There are meadows and hills for all of our special friends so they can run and play together. There is plenty of food, water and sunshine, and our friends are warm and comfortable.
 
All the animals who had been ill and old are restored to health and vigor. Those who were hurt or maimed are made whole and strong again, just as we remember them in our dreams of days and times gone by. The animals are happy and content, except for one small thing; they each miss someone very special to them, who had to be left behind.
They all run and play together, but the day comes when one suddenly stops and looks into the distance. His bright eyes are intent. His eager body quivers. Suddenly he begins to run from the group, flying over the green grass, his legs carrying him faster and faster.

You have been spotted, and when you and your special friend finally meet, you cling together in joyous reunion, never to be parted again. The happy kisses rain upon your face; your hands again caress the beloved head, and you look once more into the trusting eyes of your pet, so long gone from your life but never absent from your heart.

Then you cross Rainbow Bridge together.... 

Author unknown...

Post by: ladydi on March 31, 2008, 08:45:09 AM
I'm sorry for your loss. I know just how you feel!

I just put my dog down in September. He had lymphatic cancer and was suffering a great deal.
My vet was wonderful - he and his staff were just great and helped me accept the fact that at
14, he'd had a really good, long life.

But it was SO hard. Although he was my third dog, my dad had always "done the deed."
Luckily my boyfriend was right there next to me. He held my hand while his other petted
my boy's head and told him how great he was.

It's been a few months now and while I miss him terribly, I'm not sure about owning another
one. I don't know if I have the strength for that again. A friend of mine told me to wait two
years to fully get him out of my system so that whether I decide to get another animal or not,
I make the decision with a clear head. And I think he's right.

But let yourself grieve. Don't deny the feelings. Thank god people at my job too were wonderful.
A couple of women here brought me flowers (!)  and others gave me cards. Sounds silly to some,
but it was devastating and having that support and show of understanding was helpful.

My dogs pix are still on my desk and even if I get another one, I wouldn't put them away.
They're a part of you - good and bad - and the loss is very painful.

Wait awhile and see how you feel.

Good luck.
