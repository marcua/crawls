I don't think employment and shopping are mixed in with residential space anywhere nearly as much as in New York.  That being said, from my personal experience in Chicago/Chicago burbs, it's not always the case that employment and shopping are restricted to office parks or shopping centers.  And there are certainly office parks and shopping centers in the city itself.  There is public transportation in the suburbs, but the network is less dense, making a car more convenient but not absolutely necessary.

However, I think that suburbs tend to be younger than the cities that they grow around, and more often than cities display characteristics of planned versus organic development, in that certain areas are set aside for business/shopping districts.

My honest (un PC) feeling is that suburbia totally sucks. I was suburbia-born and raised. The real OC, on top of it all. As far as I can tell, suburban living is safe, clean, cheaper and part of the American Dream. But it can also be insular, narrow-minded, lacking in cultural activities, and frought with socioeconomic prejudice. On most subway lines, I see stockbrokers, lawyers, and other affluent suits riding alongside homeless winos, students, labor workers and Jesus freaks.  Do you see all those people carpooling together in suburbia? The suburbanite lives most of his daily existence with blinders on, and those blinders are the buffer-zones of his lawn, his home equity, his cul-de-sac, and his home owners association. He doesn't have to deal with looking a panhandler in the eye on the way home from work. So why think about those unpleasant things? Just keep driving your Hummer to the Applebees and back...

Sure, we were all safe and sheltered little suburban kids... but hanging out in the Chili's parking lot smoking and drinking because all the strip malls close at 7pm isn't exactly more culturally enriching than a night in the city. I mean, what if I grew up with independent film houses, cheap ehtnic food eateries, real libraries and concert halls? Maybe I wouldn't have been scarred by the goddamn 'bloomin onion'.
