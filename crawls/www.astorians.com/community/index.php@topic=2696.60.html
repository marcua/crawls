I had friends visiting from London in December, and my one friend wanted to have Chinese takeout so she could eat from the white box -- she's seen it in movies and wanted the experience.  I ordered something like $60 worth of food from Golden Chopsticks (I didn't know it was possible to order $60 worth!) -- just an assortment of dishes.  It was a lot of food - we had leftovers for days.  Everything was great and my friend was tickled to be able to eat from the classic Chinese food takeout container.  I agree, Meg, the portion of cold sesame noodles is huge!

There are actually two different ways they make the General Tso's at Golden Chopsticks.  The more common way is as you had it, which I think is a more standard representation of the dish.  I agree that it's a little sweet, but the chicken is always tasty, and the broccoli with it nice and fresh.  The other is much better -- must be a different cook who only works there sometimes.  It's much less sweet (my preference), and spicier (even if you don't ask for it spicy).  It's what I think General Tso's should be all the time.

Their hot and sour soup is fantastic in cold weather or when you're sick.  They are very consistent.

Life is very strange.  I just checked this thread last night to find somewhere to order from, but didn't see anything that appealed to me.  I went through my menus and found one for King's Chef on 43rd and 28th.  I think they must have a wide delivery range.

The food was actually pretty good.  And they sell in small (pint) or large (quart) size, which I love, because I can sample without spending too much money.  No white cardboard containers, though.  All plastic.

When I got my food it came with a menu and I realized I had an old one and two of the 3 items I ordered weren't on it, so I don't know what I really got.  I ordered "Hot and Spicy Chicken" which is always a gamble.  It ended up being chicken in a slightly spicy sauce, but it wasn't super sweet like many of the chinese places in Astoria make their sauces.  It had a lot of green peppers and onions (maybe too much) that were fresh.

The steamed Shrimp Dumplings may not have been shrimp.  I'm not really sure what was in them, but they aren't on the menu anymore, so I may have eaten something I didn't really want to eat (I don't eat mammals), but there was definitely a piece of shrimp in one of them.  I'm not asking questions and it was tasty all the same.  I won't be ordering it again, 'cause it's not on the menu.

The plain mai fun was... plain.  It was a little dry, too, but I guess you get what you ask for.  I knew I should have gone with the chow fun...

Overall, it was probably the best chinese I've had in astoria so far and will give it a shot again with my new menu.
