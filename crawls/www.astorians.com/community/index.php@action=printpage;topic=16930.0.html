In the other apartment on the top floor lived a couple and their daughter (the parents died, the daughter -- now in her 50s -- still lives there), growing up, the other apartment on the first floor lived a young Greek couple who raised their two kids there for about 10 years or so, the other apartment on the second floor had a single mother and her two children (the now adult children have moved, but their mother still lives there). Now, I don't know my neighbors (except for the landlords who still live on the premises up and down the block), there are always moving trucks dotting the landscape at the end and beginning of the month, there's been a revolving door on apartments in my own building. It's kind of sad.

So I think what the OP was getting at was that Astoria used to be the town where families raised their kids and the kids grew up and moved on, but now Astoria is the place where the kids from other places move on to, albeit temporarily at times, en route to the place where they will ultimately settle down and raise their families. I do have an issue with small houses being torn down for the sake of building multilevel apartment buildings -- and when they tore down the old Presbyterian Church on 33rd Street that was like a dagger to the heart! -- in that respect, we lose the flavor and character of the neighborhood.

Blaming it on newcomers from the Midwest, though, is just scapegoating and unnecessary. I'm sure there are much more worthy recipients of the OP's ire.
