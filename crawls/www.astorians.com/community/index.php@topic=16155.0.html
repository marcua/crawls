Encore Re: Decemberists at Terminal 5
« Reply #6 on: September 20, 2009, 12:14:47 PM »
First, Im gonna say thanks.

A little about the concert in a bit.
This was my first time checking out Terminal 5. (Not the one at JFK.)
I was prepared for the worst, and it turns out to me to be only slightly less of a venue than most general admission others. Isolated on the far west side of midtown.
I would rather bands play elsewhere, but if I want to see a band, I would go back to T5.
The sound was better than expected. Did not see much of the band though.

For the music, I think I have underestimated the Decemberists. I love U2. This band may be having a grand career like them.
A band has to be pretty good to have a concert based on random pickings from their list of songs.
Their song list is broad and strong.

I do wonder though whether the song choices were manipulated in some ways.
The MC picked from a lottery wheel 3 songs at a time. An evening of several sets of 3 songs.
I think once or twice the band did change the order of songs played. The element of surprise did add to the evening, so I hope they would do something like this again.
Almost forgot one fun silliness, was hearing a song improvised about playing in NY near the Miracle on the Hudson.

Some songs were in the middle when they more likely would have been played at the end of a concert. And I think by luck the song choices early on were more interesting than later. Quite a few during the concert were from Crane's Wife. (My they have a lot of song titles starting with the word The)
New songs sounded great. The band was great with wonderful versatility of instruments.

Very early picks included Raincoat Song and July July.
I did not notice if they did any cover songs. Rake's song is a live standout.

Another superb concert in a year of quite a few very good ones.
Hope to see them again another year.

I had a great time at the concert! 

This was my 6th Decemberists concert and they are by far my favorite band. My favorite concert was their Hollywood Bowl performance with the LA Philharmonic.  It's too bad they didn't cut a live album of that or any other of their orchestral concerts because their music really lends itself well with a symphony.   

Saturday night they did play one cover, one of my favorite covers: Bridges and Balloons by Joanna Newsom.  Her lyrics are very Decemberists-like in that they are all rich, lyrical and slightly tongue-twisty.

The sight of bridges and balloons
Makes calm canaries irritable
And they caw and claw all afternoon
Catenaries and derigibles
Brace and buoy the living room
A loom of metals warp woof wimble
And a thimble's worth of milky moon
Can touch hearts larger than a thimble

I was very happy with the picks:  I Was Meant For the Stage, The Raincoat, The Rake and The Cranewife #3.  Good stuff.  I could have done without The Tain.  It's a great piece, but at 20 mins at length, I'd prefer a few more sing-a-long type songs.  But they, that's apparently what fate (or the MC) has in store for us.
