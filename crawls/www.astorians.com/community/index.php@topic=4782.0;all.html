First of all, from your photo, I can't imagine that you'd need accutane. But anyway, I've been to many dermatologists over the years, none of them ever seemed to want to put anyone on accutane. There are just so many bad side effects, the most notable of which will come into play if you want to have kids someday.

A friend of mine used to have really horrible cystic acne (worse than mine by far) and her derm seemed to be able to knock it out quite well with antibiotics. It's not great being on antibiotics all the time, but this is probably a 'lesser of two evils' situation. I'm not sure how attached you are to your doc, but you might try a second opinion.. I like my current derm very much, if you'd like a referral.

I saw an ad for a book the other day - diet for clear skin or something like that. I looked over the summary and it seems like the diet for clear skin is a well-balanced, good diet, period. You know fish and leafy greens and such. Too bad that's not MY diet.  :-P

As for my doc, his name is John Romano and he's part of a dermatology practice in the west village. I think it's called West Village Dermatology or somesuch. I had been seeing a derm that my primary care doctor had referred me to but wasn't particularly keen on. Some derms I feel like treat acne dismissively. And granted, it is a minor health problem in the grander scheme of things. But if I'm in their office, it's because it was bothering me enough to make an appointment, and I really want them to focus on it and not just throw perscription slips at me.

I found Dr. Romano by cross-referencing highly rated derms with my insurance plan. He was IT. It took a while to get in to see him, but luckily I like him so far. The way he looks at it, and the way I look at it, there are a LOT of options out there to try before one gets to accutane. Including that "Diet for Clear Skin" book. Even I would try revamping my diet (and that means a lot considering how much I love food!) before I would let a doctor put me on accutane.
