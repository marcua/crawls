I hope the following doesn't come out like too harshly but I am bothered by the argument some have offered here about not glaring and why not just ask for the seat.

I've learned in recent times that ignorance is no excuse for a lot of things in life. If I've heard correctly, people can be ticketed for occupying more than one sit with packages etc. So I'd think that information might find it's way to a fair number of riders. At least the adults who ride the train.

This train is a public space. We're all paying for it in some way or another. I'm a native New Yorker and a homegrown Astorian and I'm fully aware of the circumstances that would make someone occupy more space than they need on the train. I understand and accept mental disabilities or other handicaps as a reason. But as stated before, there are a sickening number of people in New York who have an insane amount of self entitlement and expect others to ASK for a seat or worse yet the seat hoggers get offended that another rider asks for some space.

In my head I can only think "Get a clue" and have some courtesy for fellow passengers. I'll have all the space in the world on the train some times and the moment I know we're hitting a stop I further prepare myself for anyone who needs the space. On the N/Ws, the new ones that have a bar in the middle of the long row you can fit three adults on either side of the bar. I know this and I take it into account when I occupy a seat.

Don't expect people to have to ask for some space. Be aware of your surroundings and know that you should share the space REGARDLESS of whether someone speaks about it or not. These people who sit there taking up two seats, with legs spread out or packages while others stand by deserve to be glared at.

I remember seeing to young guys in suits on a ride home from work I assume, Ditmars bound. Crowded train. One of them took up two seats practically lounging, legs akimbo like nothing mattered and no one on the train existed except for his friend. WTF I thought. He was talking loud and acting hard. You mean to say someone has to deal with this guy to get a seat where you'll either be turned away or have to deal with being next to him for however many stops? Ridiculous.

The reason I believe that people don't "just ask", is that a lot of people don't want to deal with a potential public confrontation.  I've seen people flip out on more than one occasion, and sometimes it has ended violently.  Some of us are less assertive when it comes to seats, or even asking someone to turn down the music (I fear for a whole generation of people with tinnitus in their future).

These days, if I see what looks like a space, I politely say "excuse me" and sit down.  It was harder when I was using a cane, for some reason.  My suspicion is that it was because I was already feeling self conscious, and didn't want to bring more attention to myself.  My issue, I know.  

These days, I look up and around much more and give up my seat more often to pregnant women, parents with children, and the disabled (including those with canes).  When I'm alert and paying attention, it feels good to help in this way.
