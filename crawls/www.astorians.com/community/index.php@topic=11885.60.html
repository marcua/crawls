I like the Broadway area, but then I've been here almost 15 years and seen all the changes, some good, others not as good, but on the whole positive.  I love being between two train lines so I can get home easily.  I love Michael's - the 24 hour diner on Broadway and their food and their staff, I really like the new restaurants popping up, though not all are to my taste.  I'm happy that we have a Bravo nearby now and that it's kept clean.  I adore S'Agapo and am going to try Philoxenia eventually.  My block has a good number of trees, though I'd love more.  I love being able to walk to church and agree with Enigmacat, that bumping into folks I know makes my day (and it happens at least once if not more a day in Astoria).  I do agree the architecture is lacking in our area, though that doesn't bother me as much.  Would it be nice to live in an area that had brownstones, yes, do I need to be happy, no, not really.

Sorry to hear you haven't enjoyed Astoria so far, Rueful_Rose.  Hope you find elements of the neighborhood that you like.  I'd check out the Sculpture Park and the walking paths along the East River, they always cheer me up, and while Ditmars and Astoria Park are a bit of a hike, it's a nice hike in good weather and one I do regularly.
