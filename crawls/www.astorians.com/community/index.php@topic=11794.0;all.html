Hi everyone!

I just moved to Astoria and one thing that excites me about the area is all the Mediterranean food. In particular, I am on a quest to find the best falafel in the city. My first taste was at Athen's Cafe, which was a complete disappointment, considering how excited I was to have good Greek food. The falafel, though, was so fried that it didn't taste like anything and it was very dry. Then I happened to find some frozen falafel balls in Costco, which were pretty tasty for a quick and easy meal, but coming out of the microwave just isn't the same. Any suggestions on where I should try next? I've seen signs for falafel all over the place, but do not know what to expect after my first two attempts.

Thanks!

Shawarma is a Middle Eastern-style sandwich usually composed of shaved lamb, goat, or chicken. Less commonly, it contains turkey, beef, or a mixture of meats. Shawarma is a popular dish and fast-food staple across the Middle East, and is consumed across the rest of the world as well, being especially popular in Russia. Shawarma is known as guss in Iraq; it is related to the gyros of Greece. Gyro, however, are typically made of pork meat, or less commonly chicken, although beef or lamb is usually used outside Greece and Cyprus. The classic shawarma combination is pita bread, hummus, tomato & cucumber, and of course the shawarma. The additional toppings includes tahini, and amba.

Alright now - I'm a bit of a fallafel connoisseur.

Mamoun's is always great if you are in the West Village (on Macdougal/W 3rd I believe) and there is also one on E 8 now, as someone mentioned. They're super cheap, but they serve smaller falafels too.
I tried Moshe's fallafel a few times but wasn't overwhelmed...it tasted oddly buttery or something.
Barahket, in the LES is consistently good - a Turkish spot.
As for local spots, the Egyptian guys with the cart on the corner of Steinway and 34th Avenue (it's near the 34th Ave exit on the Steinway stop)
never disappoint. They put fries and eggplant in there too.  :-)
