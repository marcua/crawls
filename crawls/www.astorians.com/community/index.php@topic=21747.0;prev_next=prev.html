Uh, no, I don't think Greek olive oil is any better (or worse) than any other.  I've had freshly pressed extra virgin olive oil from Greece, Italy, and Spain, and all three can be excellent, although there are big differences in taste.  But the differences in taste aren't necessarily consistent from one country to another -- I've tasted big differences from the same olive field from one year to the next.  There is no health difference.

One thing to be wary of is adulterated olive oil that is labelled "extra virgin olive oil".  Very often, it's not, and there is not a good way for the consumer to tell (except perhaps price --- if it seems to good to be true, it probably is).

Slippery Business: The trade in adulterated olive oil.

 Olive oil is classified by how it was produced, by its chemistry, and by panels that perform olive oil taste testing. Tests in Europe were always stricter than here in the states . Now the States has also gone deeper and have done more tests to keep up with the thieves out there .
I Remember like 4 years ago tons of Italian farms were busted trying to do the ol bait and switch with their oils . They would get cheaper oils from Tunisia and Turkey and pass it off as their own .  Italians got a very bad name in the business after that . Not saying all Italians would do it , but thousands got in big trouble .
 
Most of the top Italian oils sold in Italy as "premium" olive oil has partial greek olive oil in it . Check the label on the side despite what the front says and you will see the GR .
 
Only 10% of the worlds Oliveoil is labeled as extra virgin while about 70 percent of Greece's olive oil is extra virgin.
