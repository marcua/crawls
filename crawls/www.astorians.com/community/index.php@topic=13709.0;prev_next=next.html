They way I'd figure it out is look at your existing utility bill and look at how many KWH you are using and find your current rate per KWH. I'd use $0.25/KwH as an average NYC price for electricity. Chances are whatever your usage is in your current place, that your usage will be similar in the new place. You can estimate how much your current usage would cost you here in NYC.

Getting quotes in $ can be misleading because of so many variables. I've paid as much as $250/mo for electricity for a three bedroom apartment in NY, and as low as $40/mo for a 3 bedroom.

It really depends a lot on the apartment. Are the seals on the windows good? What about the door? How high are the ceilings? Where are the heaters? How many outlets do you have? How many computers are constantly plugged in? Big TV you watch frequently?

I have had apartments where my total utility bill (with gas heat) averaged around $60/month. I also living in one where the total bill was over $450 one month!

There are lots of things you can do to lower your bills, though. Plug things into power strips and turn off or unplug the strip every time you're done using what's plugged in there. Keep one computer on as the primary on in the house and only turn the others on when necessary. Don't leave your receiver on. Use energy efficient bulbs. Yada yada yada.

It's a good idea to look at previous bills, but remember that different apartments and different roommates will always produce different results.
