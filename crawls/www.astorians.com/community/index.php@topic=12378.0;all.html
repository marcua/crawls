Interesting topic and questions.

Personally, I don't feel the Ditmars area is lacking in quantity of stores that sell fruits/vegetables, however I think that we're lacking in quality. Much of what is available isn't local, and it isn't that great either. It bruises easily or tastes bland or clearly has been grown cheaply.

Here's what I'd love to see:

- A store that sells local produce. Doesn't necessarily have to be organic, but I'd love to buy food that was grown in the tri-state area.
- Kind of goes against what I just said in some ways, but I'd like to see a store that specializes or at least sells a good amount of Asian fruits and vegetables. I really enjoy eating Asian foods, and wish that we had something here so I could indulge more frequently in Asian cooking. This is sorely lacking IMHO.
- One thing I've been dying to try for the last year is a pawpaw, but for the life of me I can't find it anywhere, and supposedly they are grown locally. So, perhaps seasonal unusual fruits/veggies would be nice.

If the first one could be done, I'd be pretty happy.

I agree completely with wasabisam.  (Well, I haven't been dying to try a pawpaw, but I wouldn't mind.  ;) )  I would really love to be able to buy local fruits and vegetables more easily.  I'm a CSA member and I also buy other local fruits and vegetables whenever I can, but that means generally that I make a trip to Union Square, or that I'm away for the weekend, usually on Long Island, where we stop at local farms on our way home.  During the CSA season I feel that our needs are mostly met, but sometimes I want something that wasn't in the share that week, and of course the CSA season only covers half the year.  I know that there is less variety in the winter, of course, but there are still local vegetables available during winter months - they're just harder to find.

I don't think it's completely unrealistic to think we could find locally-grown Asian produce.  Many local farmers (including ours) have expanded their crops in recent years to include many previously "exotic" items because of their customers' demands.  Some new farmers are from different countries, and they often start by growing produce that they crave but cannot easily find.

(As an aside - Sang Lee Farms out on the North Fork grows a wide variety of Asian produce (and other items), which they sell mostly to CSAs and farmers' markets, as well as very limited wholesale.  This year they joined Just Food and started offering vegetables to their first CSA in NYC - it's in DUMBO/Vinegar Hill, Brooklyn.  Their produce is excellent, and I wish it were more readily available in NYC.  JavaJay, maybe you could get in touch with them if your plans start moving in this direction.)

Top Tomato occasionally carries local vegetables in-season (right now they've got local tomatoes, for instance), but it's not often and it's a very limited variety.  I'd love to be able to have ready, easy access to local produce.

A one stop, awesome produce place does not exist in Astoria...that i know of.  Who doesn't want great produce?  The fruit/vegetable markets on 30th ave are good (United Bros and the one next door to it..Elinak...something or another, Enigmacat ...help!)  The produce at the Bravo on 34th ave is good.  But I want a one stop place that not only has great fresh vegetables and fruit but has an international selection as well.  Asian veggies as have been described, and Latin as well.  If I'm making a sofrito, for example, I don't want to settle for a sweet pepper because Western Beef is out of aji dulce, and no one else carries it.  I don't want to bounce around from veggie market to supermarket looking for what I want.  Even though they are next door to one another I don't want to get veggies at United brothers and then go next door to get the awesome fresh herbs they have that i can't find at United or anywhere else.  I don't want to settle for scrimpy looking herbs in plastic containers at the supermarket.

Now let's talk about space.  Make it look nice.  Clean, well organized and inviting.  Give me enough room to navigate with a cart if I need to use one.  Don't store the veggies and fruit in ugly, old torn containers.  Clearly label everything.  Give me easy access to plastic bags and scales.

I by no means cook often.  In fact, I only cook for a handful of dinner parties a year, in addition to Thanksgiving and Christmas.  But when I do have to shop for a dinner party, I find myself going to at least 3 different places to get what i need/want.

It's funny--I moved to Astoria 10 years ago precisely because the produce was so good. Since then, of course, the desire for more local stuff has come up, and I too wouldn't mind some Asian specialty items (lemongrass, mostly) all in one spot. But I have found that _almost_ everything can be had here in the neighborhood already. 

Definitely great herbs at Elliniki Agora on 30th (this is generally the best produce place around, I think--it's worth making yourself a known regular there), as an earlier poster said. Good bean sprouts and a few other Asian vegetables at the little produce place by the Ditmars train stairs (west side of the street).  Trade Fair on 30th for pretty much any shape eggplant you could ever want, plus all kinds of Indian/Carib greens.  And Family Mart, the Japanese store on Broadway, has some produce too--daikon, and some other things.  In general, I've been able to find a lot of things by thinking beyond the usual ethnic labels--Bangladeshi joints will often have things I'd normally look for in a Chinese store, for instance.  (And for really Asian, U-Mart is very close.)

Of all the things Astoria could use, I don't think a fancier produce place is top on the list. (Remember: clean, shiny, organized, etc. also means higher prices.)

Now...whatever happened to someone's idea of opening a nice bookshop?
