How about directly addressing the handful of posters who offended you while refraining from broadbrushing the everybody here?  And while I am at it, I should probably mention that I am related to a few people who were somewhat more infamous than John.  It would just never occur to me to be outrageously outraged over somebody expressing satisfaction that my great uncle died in prison while doing a life sentence for racketeering or that one of my more distant relations just up and disappeared.  That was the life they chose.

That was the life John chose.  Sure, he was a tattoo artist, someone's son, brother, uncle, nephew.  He was also a career criminal, with a long arrest record and convictions for violent crimes.  Even assuming that his "record" accounts for all the crimes he committed which-I think we can all agree-is highly unlikely, this is a man who left many victims in his wake.   

Do I rejoice in his death?  No, I do not.  NOT. AT. ALL.
Do I have empathy for him as a fellow flawed human being?  Yes, I do.
Do I have sympathy for his friends and family?  Sure.

That said, let's not overdo the emoting and self-flagellation here, Astorians.  This guy was a fellow human being, but he was also a predator.  And, while we are busy tossing those who expressed satisfaction in John's death under the bus and blubbering about "mean people," let us consider the notion that perhaps the posters some of us find it so easy to condemn without a second thought may very well themselves have been victims or the loved ones of victims of violent crime.
