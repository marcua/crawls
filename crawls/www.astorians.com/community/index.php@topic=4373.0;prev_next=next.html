I've been living in the 30th ave / Steinway / Broadway area for 5 years and haven't noticed the sewage smell.  It's also important to note that in addition to the N/W going north / south on 31st st, we've also got the r/v (and sometimes g) trains that run east west on broadway.  There are stops at Queens Plaza, 36th Street, Steinway St, 46th St, Northern Blvd. 
In addition to the amenities you'll find close to home, there's a Best Buy, Old Navy, a HUGE Stop & Shop Supermarket, Marshall's, Staples, and other various shops.  Plus you'll be about 6 or 7 stops on the r/v from the Queens Plaza mall which has a Target and most other mall stores.
I absolutely love the area, and wouldn't leave unless I was getting out of NYC altogether.  That said, I personally feel that the nightlife is lacking in the neighborhood (it's extremely easy to get to Manhattan, though).  I'm sure folks will disagree with me on this point, but a lot of places are bad dance clubs.  There is the Beer Garden which is a summertime institution, and a couple places that I do love, but most of the time I find myself in Manhattan when out with friends.  Every fall I get bummed about the lack of a good sports bar in the neighborhood where I can watch football.

Hey there 

I thought I'd throw another area into the mix while here! My boyfriend and I have lived off of the 36th ave stop (closer towards manhattan side) for 2+ years now and love it! We don't have kids and are in our mid 20's, and that specific area is actually more family oriented...Also the street I'm on and near us are a lot of Bangladeshi families (actually our landlord and his family live below and are Bangladeshi:)

We do wish that there were more young people in that specific area, but lately we are starting to find younger people in that area coming out of the cracks which is great! We actually live right near the Ravenswood projects, but I have never felt unsafe there....apart from the obnoxious teenagers that occasionally try to harass me to prove their "coolness" to their friends...but it's something you'll find in any neighborhood where teenagers are going through their awkward (read: annoying) stage. But I feel like the neighborhood really cares for itself and it's a nice feeling.

I typically walk to Broadway for basics- which is just a few blocks away from me....also the movie theater is an easy walk (astoria kaufman studios), as well as many great restaurants of all types (especially Greek-  yum!!!!!) Actually- on the other side of the tracks on 36th there are a bunch of newer wonderful restaurants- Cafe Triskell opened recently and Aces is there as well...(talked about on these boards).

We have a car- and tend to drive to Northern Blvd for grocery shopping at Pathmark, as well as Petland Discounts there for cat food/supplies. And if you like cheap shopping like I do- there's the basics like Marshalls and Old Navy there too.

I think it's wonderful that you're a social worker and interested in living in a community that you could also bring new energy to! There are many local organizations that I've learned about in the area right in our neighborhood.

I could keep going....but another positive is that it only takes us about 20 min (or even 15 on a good day) to get from our apt. to midtown manhattan:) That's a great thing about Astoria in general. Of course it helps if you live close to the subway as you mentioned- we live about 5 blocks from it- and it's really nice.

Keep asking questions as they arise and an early welcome to you!
