tax filing status question
« on: March 05, 2009, 11:42:19 AM »
Ok, so I'm trying to figure out if i can file my taxes as single even though I am married. See, I can't file jointly with John since he is self employed, and I can't file as married filing separately bc I lose all my deductions and will get about $50 back. Everything I've found online says that if you got married on 12/31 you are married and must file as married. But everything I have found presupposes that your spouse is a legal resident or citizen. John only just was approved for a change of status to permanent resident on Tuesday (wheee, a government pleed validated our relationship as legit  :roll: ) and he did not have an alien registration number or social security number until January/February of this year, thus he was not a legal resident in 08. When I was previously married, my then husband was in the same boat-overstayed visa with no status during the tax year. I had figured then that since I was married I had to file as such because technically I was (which is what everything online was telling me then and now). So that's how I filed expecting a nice juicy refund. However, I got a letter from the IRS saying I was ineligible to file as married because my then husband had no legal status during that tax year. I called them and spoke to someone who explained that in order to file as married the spouse has to have some sort of government registration number and be a legal resident. I was only allowed to file as single.
Technically, John was not a legal resident for the 2008 tax year, and if I follow the logic from the information previously given by the IRS itself, I should be allowed to file as single for the last time. I figure the worst scenario is that i get audited and then explain my logic. I determined my status based on information the irs gave me regarding my first marriage.
I just want to make sure this makes sense and sounds logical. Everything I have found online doesn't mention what to do if your spouse was living in the US already as essentially undocumented. they all assume that the couple as gone through the proper legal channels and the immigrant spouse has some sort of status within the country at the time of marriage and thus the tax year. I don't assume that anyone on the board has gone through this exact situation (but if you have any info would be great). I guess I just want some sort of validation that what I have said makes sense and that I should just go ahead and do it.
So, who thinks this makes sense?
