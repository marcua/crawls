                                                          We need your help Astoria!

      If many of you have walked in Astoria Park, which I'm sure you have, you've probably seen all the beautiful plants, tall trees, and the wonderful view of NYC. I myself have walked through Astoria Park plenty of times and enjoy walking from one end to the other, but each time I walk by the track something always catches my eye. A small, non-grassed, short fenced, dog run called Bugsy’s Dog Run. It's across the street from the track, hidden slightly behind 5 0r 6 vertical parking spaces right by the East River, its hideous! I've seen many dogs in Astoria Park, I think its “dog heaven” but Bugsy’s doesn't look very well kept and rarely used. So here's where you come in, we need your support and donations to change this place around. We need volunteers to help restore Bugsy’s Dog Run into the beautiful dog park it deserves to be. I see much potential if this project succeeds, including events and fundraisers for dogs and our wonderful community.  =)

I would respectfully suggest that before you gather volunteers or solicit funding for your project, you determine ownership of that parcel of land.

You see, if that land is privately owned you will have to negotiate with either the owner or their heirs . . . in which case I would suggest an attorney be present.

If that parcel is part of Astoria Park you will be confronted by the dozens of dog owners that fought for years to have Astoria Park made part of the City's off-leash parks. An off-leash park is one that allows dogs to be off-leash from 9:00 PM to 7:00 AM. But, there is a caveat to that law: a park can have only one—a dog run or off-leash hours—and I feel comfortable in assuring you that those off-leash dog owners are prepared to fight City Hall as they did once before, before they allow this privilege to be taken away from them.
