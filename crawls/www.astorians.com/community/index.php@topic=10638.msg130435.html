Coffee Bar is a bust for those hoping for coffee work area
« on: May 28, 2008, 01:40:49 PM »
So I came to Coffee Bar in Astoria for lunch, hoping the hope of the freelancer tired of having to go into the city to have a nice place to have good coffee and work during the day...but alas, my hopes were dashed. Plenty of tables, but no wi-fi, and the nail in the coffin is no plugs...anywhere.
On the plus side, the place is clean and bright, the service was friendly, and my steak and cheese panini was quite good (not great).

For those of you curious about the menu, it's pretty basic - for breakfast, they have bagels and schmears, egg "platters," omelets, waffles.
For lunch, salds, paninis, wraps, sandwiches, burgers, and a few "house entrees" like grilled chicken.

I tried the coffee last night.  I can't comment on the espresso or cappuccino, but the regular coffee was a bare notch up from generic deli coffee.  I should say, though, that I like quite strong coffee, and I drink it black.

As for food, we tried a few things.  The smoked turkey sandwich was pronounced quite good, if spicy.  The fries were thin and crispy (a bit overdone, but not terribly so), burgers were thick, although cooked well-done unless specified otherwise (no one specified, so I don't know how accurate they would be).  The "traditional salad", described as "mixed greens with croutons, chopped eggs and smoked bacon", was more like a breakfast salad -- three hard boiled eggs and a tremendous amount of crumbled bacon.  Quite tasty, but huge, far more than I expected from the description (not always bad, but I really only wanted a small side salad).  The beer selection is anemic (Heineken, Corona, Stella and Sam Adams).

The service was a bit chaotic and slow, although they were friendly.  We had to ask repeatedly for several items, including napkins, forks and water.  Not all the food came out at the same time.  A beer was forgotten until late in the meal.  I'm assuming these are kinks they will work out, since they just opened.

Coffee and several plates of desserts (mostly pastry items -- apple turnover, a chocolate babka relative, fruit cake, a cake with some ricotta) were offered on the house at the end, with smiles and apologies.
