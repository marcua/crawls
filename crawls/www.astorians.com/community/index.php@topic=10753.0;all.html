i just did that recently.  call the investment company and tell them that you want to rollover your money into an ira.

first though find out from your bank how the check should be made out.  for example, citibank wanted the check from my investment company to say, "citibank fbo" (for benefit of) then my name.

i left my company last year and it took me the realization that my 401k was losing money to move it to something more stable and fdic insured.  luckily i did it early on so i didn't lose all that much (relatively speaking).  now it's in a short term cd where it's fdic insured (up to $100,000), and gaining a steady 4% (measly, but better than losing money).  also in the worst case scenario, i'll keep every penny that i put in.

be careful though because the bank will probably try to convince you to roll it into something where they are investing it.  they'll tell you that in the short run, you'll lose some money, but in the long run you stand to gain a lot more than the 4% that a basic cd is offering.

good luck.
