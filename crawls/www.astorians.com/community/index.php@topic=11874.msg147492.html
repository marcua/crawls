Well, of course you cannot expect silence in a gym like a library.  You'd expect to hear music, and the sound of machines running and weights being lifted and put down (not thrown down).  I personally don't mind the "meatheads" grunting, but sometimes the loud sound of a weight being thrown down is a little startling.  Anyway, my take on the so-called meatheads is that at least they are working out.  I am much more bothered by the person talking on their cell phone while they're "working out" (notice--it's never the fit people who do this) and those who are not considerate of others and get in the way (again--not usually the fit ones, usually the new years resolution, or get-ready-for-bathing-suit-season nuisances who will show up for a month or so, get in the way and then disappear without achieving their goal).  I'm also not fond of people talking to me beyond a friendly hello, because the gym is my time to be alone and complete my workout, not a place to chat.  So yes, I do expect silence beyond the basic gym noises.

Just curious--what is the purpose for dropping weight?  I think I can see the purpose of the grunting.  I've been to many gyms over the years (and dated my share of trainers) and I've never heard there was a real reason for dropping weights.
