No, their 2 bedroom (converted to 3 bedrm) doesn't have a balcony.

The master bedroom has it's own bathroom and walk-in closet. There's another full bathroom in the apartment as well.

I believe their total rent comes to $3,100, which they split (not evenly since the person with the master bedroom has to pay more than the others for obvious reasons).

They also had to pay a one-time amenities fee of $500. Luckily I saw an ad at the movie theater that said if you bring your movie stub into your lease signing they'll take $200 off your amenities fee. I gave it to them and they saved the money. I now have free access to the swimming pool in the building when it opens (yes! haha).

I don't know anything about these specific apartments, but there are some Avalon properties back home, and they always (seemed like) really nice, well maintained places and the management company was decent to deal with (I had to for work for a little while, we housed some of our out-of-town seasonal staff there). When I was 20-ish, I even knew a few people from the local college that lived there - the university worked it out so that some kids could dorm there (they knocked down and rebuilt some dorms as part of expansion). Imagine having your dorm being this really nice condo-type complex! I was all, "wow, maybe I *should* go away to school," haha.

Just thought I'd add that it seems like Avalon is a decent company to rent from, if you're looking for this type of place. But I do think that $2200 is a LOT for a 1BR. Gah. (But yeah, I guess they can get away with it with nice views and whatnot.)
