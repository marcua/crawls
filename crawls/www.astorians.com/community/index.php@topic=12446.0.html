What are some good places in Astoria to have a 5 year old birthday party? Cost is definitely a factor. I know people spend tons of money on this stuff and I  really cannot. Astoria Sports Complex. The package comes with two hostesses, pizza and drinks. You also get to choose from several sports - including soccer and batting cage....there is a giant blow up slide for the kids and parents. You just have to bring a birthday cake and that's it. They supply all the tableware, etc. unless you want to bring your own decorations - I did that, and the hostesses did a nice job decorating the eating area.  I had my daughter's party there last year and it was not too expensive, all things considered. The party area is very large too, so that would not be a concern.

I have had my sons birthday parties at the sports complex on 38th st. & at Maric Bowling Lanes (I think it is on 47th St off of 20th ave).  By far, the bowling alley was the best party & it was much cheaper.  The sports complex was a bit expensive - you have a choice between 2 sports (my son chose swimming & the big infatable slide).  They had a hostess, food (pizza) & drinks.  I supplied the cake. There is room for adults & you can order pizza for the adults as well. It was nice but expensive.

Maric lanes was great.  They had 3 lanes to themselves and bowled for an hour then had a choice of pizza or hot dog & fries, drinks & cake.  There is also a hostess & I also supplied the cake.  There is room for adults & you can also order pizza for the adults.  It is much cheaper than the sports complex.  They also have a larger room upstairs if you are having alot of children / adults.

Also, don't forget that you can get birthday sheet cakes at Costco for around $15 - $20 & they are tasty.
