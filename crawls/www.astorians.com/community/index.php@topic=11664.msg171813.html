I suggest choosing an avenue or a street and walking the length of it (predetermining what the outer boundary is, if it goes out of our neighborhood).  There's always parts of a street or avenue that someone has never seen, and parts that you wouldn't want to walk through by yourself, and it's a guarantee that you'll see some interesting little thing that you can only find by walking past it. 

I suggest starting at the outer end and concluding at the river end.  I think most streets and avenues need two walks because continously crossing the street can drive you crazy, and you really want to see both sides.  Walking is the greatest joy of living in Astoria.

As for eats, people can bring nibbles to have along the way, and an agreement should be made in advance that any place the walkers stop in to eat that day should be the kind of establishment that accomodates very small budgets (such as one slice of pizza and a soda).
