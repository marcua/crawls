I just got this from Simotas website

Aravella Simotas is a lifelong resident of Astoria with a strong commitment to her community and public service, and has devoted herself to making a positive impact on the lives of others.  Aravella’s career in public service began at the New York City Council’s Office of Oversight and Investigation, where she focused on various public safety, youth and environmental issues, and was followed by community affairs work at the offices of Council Members Peter Vallone, Sr. and Peter Vallone, Jr. Now, she is running for the State Assembly to continue delivering results for her community and middle-class families because she understands the serious issues facing western Queens.

A graduate of the New York City public school system, Aravella appreciates the value of a solid public education.  Aravella attended Public School 17, Junior High School 126 and William C. Bryant High School in Astoria, and continued her education at Fordham University and Fordham University School of Law.  Aravella is dedicated to ensuring that our children receive the same first-rate education that she did.  That’s why, as our next Assembly Member, Aravella will ensure that critical education funding is not cut during these tough economic times.

Aravella fully understands the environmental issues facing western Queens.  Over the years, Aravella has demonstrated a relentless commitment to our community’s environmental health.  While at the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation, Aravella advocated for environmental causes with New York State Assembly Member Michael Gianaris and fought to curb construction of highly polluting power plants.  Aravella’s hard work in this endeavor resulted in a reduction of over 250,000 pounds of our state’s annual toxic emissions.  In Albany, Aravella will continue her diligent environmental oversight so that western Queens remains a clean and safe place to live.

Aravella’s dedication to public service is rooted in her commitment to protecting the civil rights of all citizens.  Irrespective of one’s race, age, ethnicity, religion, gender, disability or sexual orientation, Aravella understands that equality for all should be the foundation of our society.  That’s why Aravella has volunteered a significant amount of her time to providing legal representation to the economically challenged, and has handled cases that have had a widespread impact on the local community.  Recently, Aravella was honored by the United Spinal Association for her part in representing an individual in a wheelchair who was denied access to his housing development and discriminated against on the basis of his disability.

Aravella is also a staunch supporter of LGBT rights.  Aravella knows that for too long, LGBT couples have been denied the rights and benefits they deserve, including social security benefits, hospital visitation rights, co-parenting rights, tax equity, and bi-national family-based immigration rights.   As our next Assembly Member, Aravella will fight to ensure that equality for all is finally enacted by our Legislature.

Aravella knows that working families are the cornerstone of New York’s economy.  As an attorney at the United States Court of International Trade, Aravella addressed disputes involving domestic companies and American workers who lost their jobs because of outsourcing.  In turn, these hardworking men and women received the benefits they deserved. In the Assembly, Aravella will continue to look out for working men and women and will fight to take away tax breaks for companies that outsource jobs overseas.

Aravella is a long serving member of Queens Community Board #1.  Aravella also serves on the boards of the United Community Civic Association and the Hellenic Times Scholarship Fund.  She was presented with a Citation of Honor by Queens Borough President Helen Marshall for her commitment to these and various other community organizations.  Aravella currently lives in Astoria with her husband John Katsanos.
