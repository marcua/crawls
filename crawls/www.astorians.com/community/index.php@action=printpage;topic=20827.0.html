My wife and I, and a group of our friends have been Astoria residents for 3-4 years now, and came up with the idea to "eat Astoria through the alphabet." Basically this means every month we go to a different restaurant, and each time the restauarant starts with the next letter of the alphabet we are up to. We want to do some classy and expensive places, some dirt cheap delicious grub, and everything in between.

We started our journey with the letter A this past Sunday and had an amazing meal at "Astor Room" by Kaufman Astoria Studios. Up next is B, and so far the favorite is Bareburger. But we vote before a final decision is made, and I thought good old Astorians.com would be a good place to post updates and have people give suggestions. Keep in mind we are looking for a place a little less expensive than Astor Room, but does anyone have a good suggestion for an Astoria restaurant that begins with the letter "B"????

Title: LETTER F IS DONE
Post by: liman73 on September 13, 2011, 12:19:27 PM
In a strange twist, we were on our way to go to LETTER E last night, and were going to do the tapas place "El Boqueron" on 34th Ave. Well, unbeknownst to us El Boqueron is closed every Monday in the summer, so our plans were halted.
 
We were all starving, and started wandering the neighborhood looking for a replacement.
 
In the end, Five Napkin Burger found us and sucked us in, so we decided to temporarily skip E and go right to F.
 
I had never been to Five Napkin (I tried to go once to one in Manhattan last year but it was like a 2 hour wait), and I must say I was a big fan.
 
The price may have been more than a typical suburban burger joint, but I was not bothered at all by the cost. The decor inside gives the place a feel of a nice restaurant, and it's on par with other "fad" burger restaurants. I had a bacon cheeseburger (which was quite filling), and split tater tots, french fries, and fried pickles with the table. A few people got a beer, and the rest of us got sodas, and it came to 23 dollars per person. Really not bad.
 
The quality of food was very good. The fries were good (nothing great), the tater tots were awesome (they are like mini knishes!), and the fried pickles had pastrami and sour kraut in them so... enough said. My burger was very good as well. I really have no complaints.
 
I went into this meal expecting it to be tasty, but expecting a lot of the negative reviews to resonate with me. On the contrary, I was pleasantly surprised and would definitely go back to Five Napkin Burger.
 
We will go back to the letter E soon, but for now we have moved on and knocked off letter F.

B- BAREBURGER
C- CAFE TRISKELL

Title: Re: Eating Astoria through the Alphabet- Suggestions Welcome!

Cafe Triskell was really good- it was absolutely pouring rain on the evening we went- as a result (I think) the place was totally empty except for us- this is always a weird feeling, but whatever.
 
Also, I was feeling a little sick that night, so it was going to be tough for me to love the food. My dinner crepe was hame and cheese with spinach, and it was fair. My appetizer was a french onion soup that was good, but not quite as good as the menu hyped it up to be.
 
Then we ordered dessert crepes, and this is where Cafe Triskell really shined through IMO. I had some of my buddy's crepe which had peanut butter, nutella, and bananas in it. DAYUM! It was awesome. I wish I had just gone and gotten dessert.
 
The waitress was very pleasant and the service was good, but then again if you are the ONLY table being served you would expect nothing less, right?
 
Overall, I was satisfied, though not thoroughly wowed by Cafe Triskell. Again, inclement weather and a stomach ache put me in a bad spot going into the meal, so I would be willing to give it another meal for sure, but of all the places we have been so far Triskell may have been my least favorite. That, however, is more of a testament to how good everything else has been that we've tried.
 
If you go to Cafe Triskell, make sure you try the dessert- its money!
