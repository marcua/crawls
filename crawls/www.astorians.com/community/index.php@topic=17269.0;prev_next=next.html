I dont know if it is the same, but it looks a bit like a Toddy coffee maker. The Toddy is usually used for cold brew coffee and creates a concentrate that you then dilute with either water or with milk. Hi,

Thought this thread had run its course..

I really do like the Chemex a lot, what I have found is that whatever types of coffee tasted really good using my drip machine, dont necessarily work as well using the Chemex.  For sure you should not grind the beans nearly as much as your would for drip as the best results seem to be with a course grind.

I was really enjoying Freshdirects "Ethiopian Yrgacheffe" with the old drip machine, but I am less excited about it using the Chemex.
I have tried a few others, including "El Salvadorian Rain Forest" but still havent found something I am enthralled with.   I do love the fresh smooth taste that the Chemex provides and its also pretty easy to clean, I have yet to use any soap and hope not to.

Guess I need to stop being lazy and visit a Whole Foods or Porto Rico or something and try some good beans.  Where is Dallis Coffee?
