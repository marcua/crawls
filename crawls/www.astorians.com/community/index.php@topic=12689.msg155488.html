Vesta - 30th Avenue and 21st Street
« on: October 17, 2008, 10:46:22 PM »
My wife and I went there tonight to try it out.  It was a fun place, with good food, good wine, and an awesome dessert.

The place is cozy with exposed brick walls, art and photos on the wall, and wine bottles on high shelves.  The staff was friendly, funny, and honest. 

The menu spoke of Italian cuisine and local markets, combining orrechietta, kale, and sausage in plain wonderfulness.  I had a striped bass over couscous that was pretty good.

I recommend their Fennel Orange salad.  Tasty and healthy as an appetizer. 

BUT, the cherry on top was the dessert.  Joe, who seemed to be the head honcho, recommended a toffee cake which he called the Baby Jesus.  He said it was a homemade cake that received a "Baby Jesus!" exclamation the first time someone tried it.  We had it and it lived up to its name.  We heard somebody at a nearby table describe it as Jesus in your mouth.  It had the atheists proclaiming the holiness of Jesus.  It was that good.

I think y'all should check it out.  It seems like a nice place, with friendly folks running it.  I'm looking forward to going back there. 

Oh, and the wine was good too!

We went on Friday night, knowing they had just opened.  Frankly, we didn't expect a large crowd.  Although 21st Street undergoing a pretty big transformation, it's still off the beaten path for some.  However, with this place being only 2 store fronts from the very successful Club 21, it gets more attention.  We walked in on Friday night to a surprisingly large crowd.  Nearly every table and every seat at the bar were taken. Really great atmosphere.  Very friendly people - owners and the customers there that night.  As others just said, they have a great wine list by the bottle or the glass.  We ordered a bottle of chianti that we didn't care for.  Way too young.  But no harm, they turned it into a bottle to be used for glass orders and we got a different bottle.  We wound up ordering a pizza and staying quite a while.  The pizza was so tasty that we decided to go back again yesterday.  And this time we also got the striped bass dinners.  The fish was cooked perfectly.  Delicious.  Another minute and it would might have been dry.  The fresh mint cous cous was light and tasty.  A nice compliment to the bass.  I highly recommend the side order of sauteed kale with garlic.  Just the right amount of oil, garlic and salt to make it delicious.  The roasted parmesan brussels sprouts were also very good.  We also had the baby jesus cake both times.  I wasn't that impressed with it on Friday but I thought I better try again on Sunday when I didn't have an large quantity of wine prior.  Frankly, I didn't like it much better.  But I'm really not a cake guy.  But I can't help but think that it's zapped the microwave for 30 seconds.  It has that odd hot center and funny texture.  But the taste is very good.  I'd rather have another Margherita pizza than that cake.  They make the pizza on a stone and the crust is so thin and Delicious.   You only get four slices out of it.  I'm not saying it's too small or a rip off.  But I'm a big eater and I can easily eat about 10 of them when it's that thin crust, feather light pizza.  I think the prices are reasonable.  That's a very subjective thing.  I think the pizza might be 8 or 9$ and the striped bass entrees may have been in the 18$ range.  For the quality of the food and the experience, I think the prices are very fair.  Mangopaco, I think we saw you there.  Your description indicates comments that we both made and heard ourselves!  This location is convenient for us.  And like with Club 21, we're thrilled that there are finally some great places opening up on 21st!

My boyfriend and I have been anxiously awaiting the opening of this neighborhood trattoria, and let me say, we were not disappointed. We have been twice in the last week and a half, and I look forward to another visit this weekend. We would like to think of it as our "Cheers"-it really does have that neighborhoody-where everyone knows your name-feel.  The owner and staff are very friendly and accommodating, and the food speaks for itself. We have had the pizza every time. Thin and crispy crust, not overloaded with cheese-fabulous!!! And all I have to say is thank goodness for some "little baby jesus" in our Astoria hood. We always went to Schiller's on the Lower East Side to indulge in their toffee cake.  But now we won't have to make the trip.  I actually think that Vesta's is better.
Just order it for dessert. You will thank me. And Jesus. A perfect addition to the neighborhood.

Was so excited for a new place to come to the neighborhood... and finally checked it out ...also on two occasions last week.  I went once with a larger group of six, then went again on Friday with a friend and sat at the bar for dinner.  Both times were really fantastic. 

The bartender was super-knowledgable, friendly, was interested in finding a wine that we would love.  On Friday I tried two whites, and ended up deciding on the Pinot Toi. It's one of these places that invite you to have a taste with one of their 'shots' then move onto a glass or bottle once you've made a decision.  Great concept.   

The wine list isn't your typical Italian list and the food isn't typical Italian either.  I loved the striped bass and lemony cous cous (cooked amazingly), the lasagna is awesome (comes in a personal baking dish, the sauce is delicious and meaty), the pizza is also great (super, super-thin crust), the steak was cooked perfectly, and the brussels sprouts are my favorite.  A couple of misses were the chicken, which was okay, but paled in comparison to the other entrees I tried, and an olive oil cake that was on the menu the first time I went, then off when I went again.  Was kind of dry...and next to the Jesus cake was a miss.  Jesus cake is ridiculously good.

Will absolutely be back - I really love the vibe.
