I've been to an orthopedist, but I don't know if he's a spinal orthopedist.  He's treating me for the achilles tendonitus, and gave me the pills for the sciatica.  The sciatica is a temporary thing from having walked so off-kilter for so long.  I've had sciatica before, but it's not chronic.  Today I even considered not taking the pill because I felt so much better, but I really needed to clean the bathroom (I hadn't cleaned it in a month) so I took it.  I'll probably take it tomorrow too - I'm doing my usual exercise routine, but I'm nowhere near back to touching my toes when I do "round over" (roll down from standing position till you touch the floor, roll back up - a slow count of 8 each way).  Last week it was taking me 5 minutes to get out bed - 5 minutes till I gave up, then a few more to try again (cry again!).  Now I can pretty much just roll up and get up.  I took Back Care and Posture class at the YWCA, and they taught us how to get up out of bed, which would sound funny to anyone who's never had back problems!  (Roll on your side, put both hands on one side of you and push yourself up to sitting position.)

Cyclo/Flexeril is definitely not a fun drug.  It puts me to sleep, wakes me up 2 hours too early.  It enables me to sit and stand, and makes me so ditzy I can't keep my mind on what I'm doing.  It enables me to do my exercises because I'm not in pain, and gets in the way of me doing my exercises because my muscles are relaxed!  I had two cups of coffee this morning (and some chocolate cake) and it boosted the caffeine effect unbelievably, but I was too wired to accomplish anything for hours!

But Percocet!  I guess I had a hospital experience so traumatic I forgot all about it, but when I broke my leg a couple of years ago and they wanted me to take Percocet I instantly recoiled from the idea, though I couldn't say why it sounded like such a bad idea.  I went for codine instead, but after I had my surgery my doctor didn't know I didn't want Percocet and prescribed it.  A few days later I was in an abulence being transferred to another location and had auditory halucinations real enough that I turned my head in the direction the voice came from, even though I knew there was nothing there!  No Percoset for me ever again!

By the way, I was hobbling for so long with the tendonitus because I kept anticipating needing to buy Celebrex after my next steroid shot [my third shot since last summer], and even at a mail order pharmacy (Better Business Bureau approved) it was still beyond my means.  I seem to be doing ok this time with just the shot, though, and my doctor doesn't charge much for an office visit.  So, here's hoping I won't need anything else.
