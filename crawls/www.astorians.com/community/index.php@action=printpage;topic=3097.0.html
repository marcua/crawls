3. Two deep fried thingys (fatiya (fuh-TEE-uh)?), one meat, one cheese. Both wrapped in phyllo dough and deep fried.
4. Octopus with potato and lemon
5. Baby eggplants stuffed with a loose mixture of rice, meat and spices
6. Meze plate: hummus, a deeply smokey baba ganoush, and foul.  Served with a garnish of deep fried escarole (wow!).  Also with falafel, served with warm pita.
7. Kofte - ground lamb (and beef?) stuffed with pine nuts and raisins, formed into elongated meatballs, served with a tangy yogurt sauce.
8. A dish with two other thingys on it: One was served in wedges, and was sort of like an Egyptian frittata (eggah?), possibly containing finely ground veggies, with a slow, sneaky heat. There was a thin layer of coconut cream on the top of this. The other thingy was with potatoes, chickpeas, turmeric, and was also kind of in a cake form. I don't know what bound it.

The three main courses:

9. Apricot chicken. Oh.my.god. It literally melted in your mouth - both the chicken and the apricot. The dried apricot melted. The chicken melted. Wow. I think this was my favourite dish.
10. Lamb shank. Served with rice. Tender, falling off the bone.
11. Thick, incredibly tender strips of beef with a very tangy flavour. At this point I could hardly take another bite, but I took several.
