Title: Re: United Miners SC "Rudar"
Post by: QuentinC on August 13, 2008, 10:21:38 AM
I have passed through those doors and lived to tell about it.  They serve Istrian food, which is sort of like italian with a german austrian bent.  Think hearty gnocchi, simple fried fish.  it is very good very inexpensive and can be pretty heavy.  It is intimidating to go in there the first time-- you walk inside to a bar where there may or may not be patrons, and ask where the resto is and you are pointed downstairs.  Sometimes the place is completely empty, other times it''s like walking into a party.  The room looks as though it hasn't changed since the 50's (in a good way) and the women running the restaurant are very sweet.  It's only intimidating to walk in the door.  There are often large parties- weddings, showers and the like, but even then they will manage to feed you.

Title: Re: United Miners SC "Rudar"
Post by: TJT on August 22, 2008, 03:32:39 PM
We've been a few times for dinner with a Croatian relative.  Excellent food.  But I can't be sure how comfy we'd feel without being with them and him speaking Croatian to the waitress.  Order anything they make with veal.  It will be delicious.  The gnocchi is usually lighter than a traditional Italian restaurant.  And if you're daring, order any dish made with tripe.  They do a nice job with it. 

As for the weirdness - I'm getting used to these places in Astoria.  Though I never saw them anywhere else I lived in Queens.  All these so-called soccer and sports clubs.  It seems odd to me.  I guess they really don't want just anyone coming in.  So in that sense, it isn't welcoming to "the rest of us".  But if they do - then why not drop the silly soccer business and put up an awning that indicates Croatian/Istrian food and entice the public to come and eat?  The people going in there clearly haven't been in any condition to go near a soccer ball in at least 20 years.  I notice in many instances these clubs are just there to skirt smoking laws.  They also seem to be hang outs for old men who want to get away from their wives for a while.  I guess the ethnic version of an OTB.  I also suspect that once their "membership" dies out or moves to the burbs, they will have trouble keeping this up.  For now, give it a shot.  But don't be surprised if you don't feel like they roll out the red carpet.
