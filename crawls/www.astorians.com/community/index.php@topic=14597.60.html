was at BareBurger on a Monday night.  Wasn't that crowded - they seemed to be making an effort on the service which addresses some of the prior commentary - although again it wasn't a full crowd.
Had the Jalapeno Burger (piedmontese beef) and the ranch swiss burger (bison) - both ordered medium with fries and onion rings.
The 7 grain bun was a definite winner but as for the burgers - they came out slightly overdone - (although quickly) - and were a little disappointing overall.  The Jalapeno burger wasn't very spicy for some reason and the beef while good was a little overcooked to really tell.  Liked the Swiss burger (bison) better - thought the meat was leaner and more flavorful.  As for the fries - kinda weak - overcooked again - and generally not flavorful - and the onion rings were burnt to a crisp making them almost inedible.

Still not a terrible experience but not one that I'm in a rush to try again for $20 a head (w/tip).  If the prices came down I'd be willing to try them a few more times - in the meantime - I'll wait for better reviews.

Just tried Bareburger for the first time tonight.  Got their primary Bareburger #1 to go.  Overall, the only complaint I had was waiting a little over half an hour for take out.  (Not necessarily their fault - they got an onslaught of dine-in, take-out and delivery orders literally at the same time.  People seated right around the time I ordered still hadn't received their food when I left.)  I got an apology twice from a goateed guy in a more beige shirt who works there.  Told me the delay was due to hold-ups in the kitchen.  I looked back and saw about 10-12 slips hanging, and with that small space they have, could imagine the chaos. 

Anyways, my burger and fries were hot and fresh - so they certainly weren't sitting in spite of the long wait.  The burger - pretty good - could definitely make a run for the best in Astoria.  This place is definitely tops for designer burgers, with all their meat and topping options.  The fries - simply unbelievable!  They need to get their liquor license in tow to deliver on the beers part of their tag line.  Overall, would I go back, yes! (Will bring a friend from Sunnyside there soon.)  Though, at $11.95 and up for a burger and fries, it won't necessarily be a regular choice (certainly better burger deals can be found in this hood, though the portion sizes at Bareburger are plentiful to go along with their top quality ingredients).

Overall, I liken this to an Astoria/Queens version of Rare and several of the other upscale, designer burger spots that have popped up in Manhattan in these past few years.  Next is to measure this up against Donovan's in Woodside, which to my knowledge, is the most talked about burger spot in the borough.  This place still has some service and capacity kinks to work out with the kitchen, but provided that gets dealt with in time, looks like we have a new neighborhood spot.

I finally made it over to Bareburger today and was really glad I did!  The food was terrific and just exactly what I needed: a real, satisfying burger that had that "whole" taste you get from food prepared the right way.  I got the ostrich burger with blue cheese and bacon, among other things.  Yum.  Also got one of the specials of the day, sweet white corn on the cob.  Yummm.  Didn't need any seasoning at all.  My mom got a veggie California avocado burger and loved it.  We both got iced tea without sweetener.

Service was great, top notch and really welcoming.  I always take note when the people who work at a restaurant really believe in what they're doing and are excited and proud of the product they're putting out there.  You could tell they were trying to make a good impression.  Mission accomplished!  Sunny people, sunny decor, good food.  I'll be back.
