Quote from: pineapple on March 30, 2008, 03:51:18 PM
Also, Essen, it is symbolic.  An action to bring awareness.

I get that, but what does it really accomplish aside from smugness among some of the participants? A bit of "I'm doing it. Are you?!" which is an attitude I can't stand and what turns a lot of people off from this kind of thing. I would rather see a worldwide effort to get people to do things that actually do make a positive difference and are reasonable as solutions to our wastefulness as a species. Shutting all the power off for an hour isn't a practical solution, and I don't believe it helps to lead to one. And just the idea of an "Earth Hour" is silly to me. I would be more supportive of, say, an "Earth Night" or "Earth Weekend" where people turn their things off and communities host events that don't involve much electricity usage (or that which has been solar generated or something). But I would be most supportive of incentives to actually get people to invest in things like solar power for their homes and better ways of recycling.

As for the CFL bulbs, I'm torn. I have a lamp that doesn't work with those - the shade clamps on to the bulb directly, and I doubt it would fit on of the CFLs, and I don't want to throw away the lamp. Also, they contain mercury, which is bad if/when they break, so they're supposed to be specially disposed of at places that handle hazardous waste. I wonder how many people bother to do that.
