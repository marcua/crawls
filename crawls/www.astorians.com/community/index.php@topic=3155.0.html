City Council Votes Itself 25% Pay Raise
« on: August 26, 2006, 08:58:08 PM »
CHRISTINE PRESSES FOR POL PAY RAISES
By DAVID SEIFMAN
August 20, 2006 -- ELECTED officials who haven't had a raise in seven years may finally get one.
Council Speaker Christine Quinn has suggested a $22,500- per-year raise - or 25 percent - for council members in a letter to Mayor Bloomberg's salary-review commission.

The panel is scheduled to make recommendations next month on salary increases for council members - along with the mayor, comptroller, public advocate, borough presidents and district attorneys.

"Compensation levels for elected officials should be increased by 25 percent simply to keep up with increases in the cost of living," Quinn wrote commission Chairman Tom Bernstein.

If the panel takes her advice, the 51 council members will see their base paychecks jump from $90,000 to $112,500. (Many council members also receive bonuses of $10,000 to chair committees.)

On a percentage basis, the raise would be less than those negotiated by municipal workers over the six-year period.

The mayor seems in no hurry to act.

He didn't get around to appointing the salary commission until April - three years late.

"Some people are getting frustrated," admitted one council member.

But no one is complaining publicly, since such squawks aren't likely to elicit much sympathy from voters who earn considerably less.

The ultimate decision rests largely with the mayor, who can accept, reject or modify the recommendations of his three-member panel.

One elected official said it makes sense for Bloomberg to approve raises, since he could then also boost pay scales for deputy mayors and commissioners.

They are now effectively capped below the mayor's own official salary of $195,000 - of which the billionaire Bloomberg only accepts $1 per year.

As a bonus, the elected official said, the hikes would put pressure on Albany leaders to push through raises for state legislators, who earn $10,500 less than their city counterparts.

"It would drive Shelly nuts," chuckled the elected official, referring to Assembly Speaker Sheldon Silver, who has a strained relationship with Bloomberg.

 
August 25, 2006 -- THE ISSUE: City Council Speaker Quinn backs councilmembers' demands for higher salaries.

The pure lunacy of our elected officials never ceases to amaze me ("Christine Presses for Pol Pay Raises," Aug. 20).
City Council Speaker Christine Quinn wants a raise?

She already makes over $100,000, yet is in desperate need of a raise to keep up with the cost of living in this city?

Has she seen the starting pay for a New York City police officer? It is exactly the same as the raise she is suggesting - $22,500.

If Quinn thinks that compensation levels for elected officials should be increased by 25 percent simply to keep up with the cost of living, I wholeheartedly agree. Let's give New York's Finest what they deserve for keeping her safe.

Oh, I forgot. When it comes to raises for the NYPD and the FDNY, the city is all of a sudden bankrupt.

Christopher DiToro
Staten Island
