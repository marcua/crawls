Kitaku shout-out
« on: March 30, 2010, 09:46:52 PM »
Just got back from treating ourselves, a way to ease the gloominess of the weather. Original plan was Watawa, but walking Watawa-way was wet- weather-y.  :roll:

Kitaku was on the way so we stopped there, and tried "omakase." Started with miso soup, then the crab coleslaw; next was spicy eel with cream cheese etc.; then shrimp with benito crunch and rolls (I'm simplifying the descriptions- plenty of food each course); then sashimi with the good stuff; fried green tea ice cream for dessert.

$50 for us both (before tip, green tea to drink.)  :mrgreen: While it's not Nobu-quality, I was just jonesin' for a fun evening out in a cheerful setting, and Kitaku delivered. Steven was our chef- very nice and talented guy. Tell him we sent you  :smitten: or not, but sit at the sushi bar on a quiet, rainy evening and it should bring a smile to your face.

its on 23 ave, not 23 road.  same owners as Dragon Gate, they expanded into this place.  this is my go-to spot for chinese, and i eat alot of chinese.  best by far.  one of the very few places that still puts egg in the fried rice, i dont think anybody does that around here still.  the japanese food is a new venture for the owner, and i think it misses the mark.  i tried it because it was a few dollars cheaper than watawa, and because they have tuna tartare on the menu, but i could taste how watawa is worth the extra 2-3 dollars.  i remember when they first opened, they had a thai menu also, but i think that got phased out due to people going to Bangkok Tasty instead, which is right across the street.  if you want THE BEST chinese food, order from here, open until midnight on fri-sat, and 11 every other day..
