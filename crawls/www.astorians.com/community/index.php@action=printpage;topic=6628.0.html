Title: Re: keeping your veggies fresh.
Post by: megc on June 11, 2007, 04:32:43 PM
Often when I buy lettuce by the head, I prep it right away.  Clean it and tear the leaves off (sometimes break up the leaves) and store them that way.  Takes up a little less space.  I find the CSA veggies don't wilt or go bad nearly as fast as the conventially grown stuff does.  Keeping the veggies as close to the bottom of the fridge helps, too, even if they're not in the crisper.  The paper towel technique works, too.

Oh, and you can often "refresh" lettuce by plunging it into very cold water.

Title: Re: keeping your veggies fresh.
Post by: Cassiel on June 28, 2007, 10:32:45 PM
Summing these up a bit and seconding several of the suggestions (since I just finished prepping and storing my share from this week):  greens and leafy things will go south the most quickly and require the most care in storage.  Wash them well first (you may as well cut/tear them up and remove any stems or other parts you're not going to use when you do this), and then get them as dry as possible (salad spinner, or patted with a towel -- paper or regular).  Store them wrapped in a paper towel or dishtowel, or in one of those terrycloth or muslin bags you can get for the purpose, and put this inside a plastic bag in your fridge, in the crisper if you can.

For herbs, it's best not to wash them ahead of time since it accelerates their breakdown, but otherwise the same storage method is good.

Root vegetables, zucchini, broccoli, etc. -- no need to worry much.
