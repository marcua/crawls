Hey parents!
My cousin is having a baby girl in a few months and I'm attending the baby shower in a few weeks.  Very exciting time.  I've already bought some standard baby stuff but I'm wondering if anyone could recommend a great gift that might not be on the registry.  Something a new mom-to-be might not think of.  What's the one thing you wish you'd had for the first few months after the new arrival?  Or is there something you wish you'd had for yourself at a time when the new baby gets all the attention?  Or, just finish this sentence: "When you have a new baby, you can never have enough _______." This is really sweet of you.

There are 2 gifts I received that I didn't register for and absolutely LOVED.  The first is a Miracle Blanket- it is for swaddling newborns and makes swaddling so much easier (especially in the middle of the night after a feeding!)  I think they sell them at Buy Buy Baby and you can find it online. They are around $25.
The second is for when the baby gets a bit older- the Me Too Portable Highchair.  It is a little foldable chair that you can hook on to almost any table, and it's much more secure than the highchairs commonly found in restaurants.  My daughter starting using hers around 5 months.  It is small enough to fold up in my diaper bag.  It's great for going out to eat, but also wonderful for travel- no need to worry about high chairs.  I haven't seen it sold in stores, but you can find it online.  I think it retails for around $40.

You can never have enough diapers!  She'll probably receive plenty, but if you do decide to purchase some, I would go for 1's or 2's- most babies grow out of the N's pretty quickly, and they send you home rom the hospital with a bunch.
Congratulations to your cousin!

If you go to Target now, you will get a great deal on winter clothes that are on clearance. You can buy the 6-9 month size and the baby should fit into this by next winter. My sister had twins 9 months ago and she got tons of clothes for ages 0-9 months. Last minute she had to go on a major shopping spree for new clothes for the girls since they had outgrown the smaller baby clothes. The Children's Place on Steinway also had a large clearance section about 2 weeks ago, mostly winter clothes.

I also bought my sister something called My Brest Friend. It is a pillow that you can use while breastfeeding the baby. Since she had twins, this was especially handy for feeding both babies at the same time. I guess it is similar to the Boppi, but she has suggested this to every new mom-to-be she knows.
http://www.mybrestfriend.com/

Ohhh,those are some great ideas.  I love my My Breast  Friend -- it's the best nursing pillow out there, IMO.  The swaddling blankets are hit or miss -- only one of my four liked to be swaddled but boy am I LOVING  them now!!!  I got half a dozen or so with some handmedowns and she sleeps in one every nap and bedtime.

If I was going to give a baby shower gift with unlimited funds, I'd probably give her a basket with a sling (cloth baby carrier, I'd choose a wrap style sling), some books (Elizabeth Pantley's No Cry  Sleep Solution and the Happiest Baby on the Block, author unknown, and the Baby Book by Dr and Mrs Sears, So That's What  They're  For,author unknown and Take Charge of your Child's Health by Dr Wootan), and some cd's with lullabyes.  If you know she's going to nurse, super soft LANA breastpads are wonderful.  Miracle Salve is fantastic for mom for after the birth *and* for using on the baby.  A bed tray with remote control for a lamp, a nalgene or stainless steel water bottle would make a nice themey gift,too.

I could keep going...
