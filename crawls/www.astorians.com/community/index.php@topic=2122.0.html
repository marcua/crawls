May, 16
DFNYC Monthly Mixer

Where: Light, 125 East 54th Street (btwn. Park & Lexington) Mix and mingle with DFNYC. Click here to RSVP Admission: $5 per person (with RSVP), $7 without Admission price benefits Democracy for NYC. It's a good cause, so bring a friend.

May, 16
Benefit for Voters' Rights with Gov. Howard Dean
Gov. Howard Dean, Chair of the DNC, will be the special guest at a benefit this Tuesday evening for the New York Democratic Lawyers Council. The Lawyers Council is a coalition of lawyers and other volunteers dedicated to preserving the right to vote, both by fighting obstacles to voting and ensuring that our votes are counted. When: Tuesday, May 16, 7:30 to 9:30pm Where: The Yale Club, 50 Vanderbuilt Avenue, between 44th & 45th Streets Tickets: Sponsor - $500, Patron - $250, Guest - $125 RSVP: I have just joined the host committee for this great event, so you may RSVP to me, Tracey, at tdenton-at-dfnyc.org (replace -at- with @) or (646) 251-0680.

May, 17
Comedian with Disability at Carolines - DFNYC Field Trip?
Damon Rozier, a professional comedian who uses a wheelchair, will be performing at Carolines this Wednesday night. Read more about Damon at the United Spinal website. When: Damon will be performing on Wednesday, May 17th, for the 9:30 show. (People should arrive at Caroline's no later than 9pm.) Where: Caroline's is on Broadway betw. 49th and 50th Sts. Format: Damon will be part of the "Bob di Buono" show featuring 5 - 7 performers, lasting 1 1/2 hours.
May, 18
Conf. Call with Greg Palast, and message from Greg to DFNYC

Conference Call with Greg Palast and DFA Activists. Discuss the challenges for the upcoming ’06 and ’08 elections. Thursday May 18th 7:30pm-8:00pm EST. Dial in number TBD
A message from Christy Speicher of DFNYC: Greg Palast has a new book out called Armed Madhouse: Dispatches from the Front Lines of the Class Wars. Join us for a conference call this Thursday with Greg, as we discuss Greg's book about how the Republicans plan to steal the '06 and '08 elections and what we can do about it. Greg Palast is the award winning BBC investigative reporter who wrote the New York Times best selling book The Best Democracy Money Can Buy, about the Republican’s fraud in the 2000 election in Florida, including purging the voter roles and preventing the counting of votes.

Murderball Screening to support the US Paralympic Team

Screening Liberally, Participant Productions and Democracy for NYC invite you to a fundraising screening of the Academy Award nominated documentary MURDERBALL
Join us Monday, May 22nd at 7pm at Prey Lounge as we raise money to purchase new quad-rugby chairs for the US Paralympics team. Come early to get a good spot, enjoy drink specials throughout the event and stay for a Q&A with Keith Cavill (http://www.murderballmovie.com/players.html) from MURDERBALL.

May, 24
Al Gore's An Inconvenient Truth in Theaters
Al Gore and Davis Guggenheim's documentary about global warming entitled "An Inconvenient Truth" opens in theaters on May 24. Those who have had the benefit of seeing Al Gore's brilliant presentation on global warming, know this is a must see film for everyone. Join the DFNYC "field trip" purchase your tickets today: The Sunshine Cinema 143 East Houston Street (btwn. 1st & @2nd Aves.) Wednesday, May 24 7:15 Showing Click here to purchase your tickets. AMC Lincoln Square 1998 Broadway (@ 68th Str.) Thursday, May 25 7:35 Showing Click here to purchase tickets. Although the film opens on Wednesday, there is no public early evening showing with available tickets. We have therefore moved the DFNYC field trip to a more convenient time on Thursday. If you can only come Wednesday, please join us at the Sunshine. Then RSVP so we know you're coming. "An Inconvenient Truth" eloquently weaves the science of global warming with Al Gore’s personal history and lifelong commitment to reversing the effects of global climate change. A longtime advocate for the environment, Gore presents a wide array of facts and information in a thoughtful and compelling way. The film is not a story of despair but rather a rallying cry. Watch the trailer. (Quicktime required.) www.climatecrisis.net
