I went to Angelo and Son's for a sandwich yesterday.    I forgot who, but someone posted they were almost as good as Sal and Charlie's on 23rd Ave.    I've had them before at Angelo and Sons and yes, they were very good.    I usually call ahead so it's waiting for me when I get there.   Yesterday was a spur-of-the-moment kind of day, so I ordered it at the counter.   The guy that was making the sandwich came up front twice to look for a hero-roll and both times, shuffled all the bread around (like one  would mysteriously appear after the first time he checked) and wasn't wearing gloves.  Once I realized this I started paying attention and noticed him go to his brow several times and didn't wash his hands before begining to make my sandwich.   Needless to say, I stopped him and went to go see the owner/manager.   While waiting for him to get off the phone, one of the girls there asked me why I wanted the manager and when I told her she said she was glad I was saying something because it seems he NEVER wears gloves.   *YUCK*   When I spoke to the owner/manager he seemed indifferent and said something to the effect of "let me find out what happened"   Huh?   What's to find out?   I just told you what happened.   No offer of an apology, nothing.    I went back this morning to get coffee and find someone who at leat gave the impression of giving a damn and the same guy was there.   I hope I catch the other guy I see there tonight.  (I'm assuming they're the "Sons")

The only good thing is that the guy making sandwiches this morning WAS wearing gloves.  (different guy tho)

For those interested, the gloveless guy was Italian looking, with a grumpy disposition and a mustache.

It takes a LOT to gross me out, but this grossed me out plenty.   

I'll keep you all posted if I get a hold of the other "Son".
