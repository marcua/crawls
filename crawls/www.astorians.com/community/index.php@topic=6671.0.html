On one of the other threads there was a mention of a father/son team who for years ran a kabob stand on Broadway & 36th.   Until I read that post I had completely forgotten about them even though I always walked by and even bought kabobs from them.

There are many people I see, if not daily, often in Astoria.   I'm sure many of you have as well.   Here are a few.

There is a blind guy, who as long as I can remember, has been playing an accordion on the N trains at night for years.   In recent years he's been playing a recorder I think it is called.  I haven't seen him recently so I don't know if he's still around.   He presumably gets on/gets off at Ditmars and always would get off 59th Street to wait for the N train to go back to Astoria.   I know nothing about the guy, but he speaks with a slight accent so I assume he was born somewhere in Europe.   In '04 I was standing on the corner of 28th Avenue and 35th Street and saw him walk up 35th Street (between Astoria Blvd. & 28th Ave.) and get in a cab.   It is possible he lives on that block, but that is the only time I ever saw him anywhere but on the subway and always at night.

There was another guy, who clearly was homeless, who late at night on the N line going into Astoria would sing.   He'd stand there in the car and sing and dance.   He was filthy and clearly mentally ill, but harmless.   I would cringe if I got stuck in a car with him, ah, performing, but other riders seemed to find him amusing.   I found him annoying.    I haven't seen him in years.

There is another guy who panhandles on the N line.   He's used the same line for years and years and tells people to smile even if they don't give him change.  He says he coaches a basketball team.   I've seen him fairly recently.  He's been working the N line for years.

When I lived closer to Broadway there used to be this older guy who was short and wore glasses who I nicknamed "the mayor of Broadway."   I don't know who he was, but he seemed to earn a living doing odd jobs for the business owners on Broadway between, say, 31st and Steinway.   I haven't seen him in years but he was a familiar figure for years and years.

Way back when I moved to Astoria there used to be a group of homeless men, alcoholics, who used to sit outside of the then Chemical Bank branch on the corner of Steinway and Broadway.   This would have been late eighties/maybe into the early 90s.   I never saw many homeless people in Astoria like you do in Manhatan and those I did see were not transient.   They seemed to have found a spot and stayed there for months and even years.   I have no idea what happened to these guys, but they seemed harmless.

There also used to be a slighty heavy homeless and mentally ill woman who would sit outside of the Broadway subway stop in the 1990s for what seemed to be an interminable period.   She would sit in the parking lot on the east side of 31st and would sit there eating takeout.   She'd leave the detritus on the ground and made a mess, but she seemed to be there for months if not years.   I have no idea whatever happened to her.  One day she was just gone.

More recently, I noticed there is an older guy, who is South Asian and is no doubt an immigrant, who sells newspapers to passing motorists where Hoyt Avenue South, Astoria Blvd. and 33rd Street converge.   He's out there weekdays and leaves by early afternoon.   I have no idea who he is, but every time I see him I feel a bit bad wondering how hard it must be to make a living doing that.   But he is a real trouper.

Finally, I see the same or a few Indian woman.   They must hail from Peru or Chile and they were fedoras.   They collect returnable soda bottles and cans from garbage bins and push around a shopping.    It just makes me wonder how tough life must have been for her back in South America if the way she makes her living here is through collecting returnable bottles.   Another thing that makes me sad.

The newspaper guy is ancient, but he must make a decent buck.  I always see a motorist getting a paper from him when I pass.  He must get 2 or 3 drivers at every red light.

And the bottle ladies are out all night come Tuesday night and Friday night.  My first night in Astoria, I asked my roommate how long he think it'd be before someone took our cans (which we just put on the sidewalk).  Immediately we heard a rattle.  We look outside and the bag was gone.

Anyway, I notice there's always this domestic cat outside the laundromat or the bodega on 28th and 33rd.  It just sits there and it must belong to someone who works or lives nearby.  It's totally tame and sleeps on the sidewalk

Ditmars has quite a few regulars.  The blind accordian play is rumored to have a foot fetish and apparently tries to touch the feet of women when sitting on the train.  He's still playing, but I haven't seen him in about a month.

There is the Vietnam Vet Cadillac Man but no one knows where he is right now.  Perhaps he ran off and got married to his girlfriend.

There's the little old man who's Latin I think that is usually outside the Ditmars station by the stairs.  He sings alot and waves his hands from side to side very fast.  He's harmless and never seems to be asking for money.  He just sings.

Then there's the black guy with an afro that usually stands outside Commerce with his radio.  People seem to know him, he's pretty friendly.  Perhaps a little slow.

The old lady on Ditmars who used to sell books is gone.  She was a mean, angry woman who always fought with her boyfriend, another homeless dude.  I heard shopkeepers complained and she was deported back to Greece.
