I must say, I really wish that the MTA was better about publicizing outages. Not just what's currently out of service, but what outages there have been in the past, say, 24 hours. It would be nice to know that there's a stalled W train, and it would also be nice to know that the stalled W train has been cleared, and that there are residual delays. That information should be updated by the minute and should be available on the web. (And booth agents should have a little computer with that information too.) Aggregate information should be made available in a downloadable database for the Straphangers folks to analyze. If the MTA was a city agency, you can bet statistics-crazy Bloomberg would have made that happen. Way better information is available for drivers than for subway riders.

My other gripe is that there should be buses in constant reserve to pick up the slack when there are outages. East river tunnels flooding again? No worries, just triple the number of busses going over the triborough and queensboro bridges.

Failures are part of any complex system like the world's largest subway system, and the MTA does relatively well at minimizing them given the age of the tracks and some of the trains, but they're not very good at working around problems or making customers feel like they're being taken care of.
