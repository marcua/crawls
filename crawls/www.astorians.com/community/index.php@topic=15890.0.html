La Campagnola on 35th Avenue
« on: August 16, 2009, 08:25:55 PM »
OK, they're finally open although they don't have a liquor license yet.

They're doing the same thing as the former owners (Cinema Paradisio) with the pizzeria in front and the "fine dining" restaurant behind it; I'm not sure this combo works in the long term.  The green-seat benches of the pizzeria is at variance with the restaurant, which has a cool iron chandelier, tapestry-style paintings on finials on the walls (at medieval above-eye-level positions), and black cloth dinner napkins.  The dining room was extended further back and looks nice.

Entrees are reasonably priced, but the soups and salads will drive up the final bill.   I had the spaghettini piccante with seafood and it was enough.  5 mussels, 3 clams, 4 scallops and about 8 shrimp for $15.  Delicious, but large enough so that I predict they will reduce their portion sizes within a couple of months. 
Bread comes with a small drizzle of excellent extra virgin olive oil; ask for more to go with it.  Gelato dessert is 3 large scoops.  Triple espresso flavor was delicioso.

They do need to change the music, which is post-modern Italian pop and electronica.  This is not really dining music in my book.
