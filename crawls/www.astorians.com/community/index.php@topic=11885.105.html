Excellent point.  It's also a mistake to take averages over too broad an area.  20% of Queens' population may be African American, for example, but in certain areas of Queens, the African American population is way more than 20%, and in certain others, like Astoria, it is way below.  In the end it averages out to 20%, however, but that doesn't mean that there is a more or less even distribution anywhere with Astoria being the glaring exception.  Certain areas tend to attract certain ethnicities/races more than others...e.g. Astoria has a higher than average Greek population, Flushing has a higher than average Chinese and Korean population, etc.  While there may be cases of racism in individual buildings, for instance, I think it's a stretch to say that Astoria, as a whole, is an area where African Americans are discriminated against just because they make up a relatively small percentage of the population.  Sometimes it does just boil down to self-selection, and that self-selection could also account for the fact that no African American family has moved in to a specific apartment building in Astoria.
