Quote from: paratactical on July 14, 2009, 04:10:02 PM
After going to the Dr...

Did the doctor give you exercises to do?

One good one:  While you're lying on your back with legs straight, draw one knee up toward your chest, wrap your arms around your bent leg pulling it gently closer to your chest.  Hold for a few seconds, release it, and gently stretch your leg back to start and do the other leg.  While that action might sound like a nightmare when you're in pain, it stretches out your lower back, releaving pressure on the painful spot.  If, while you've got your leg bent, you can curl your head toward your bent knee, you'll get an even better stretch.

I tried to find a good website that demonstrates back care exercises, but the only one I found had drawings of people in red that looked like the pain you're trying to alleviate!  I learned my exercises in back care and posture class at the YWCA, but they don't teach anything good like that anymore because "you can get that kind of information anywhere".  Thanks, Y.

I hope you feel better soon.
