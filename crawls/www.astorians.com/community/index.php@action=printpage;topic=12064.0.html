Title: Re: ? for runners
Post by: lanseaux on September 03, 2008, 09:30:26 AM
I did the couch to 5k, starting from ZERO running, and in April I ran a half-marathon.  If you stick to it it really does work.

When I started, I had trouble running for the first 60 seconds.  I couldn't run a block.  I found a few runners at approximately my level through this board, and we would meet in the mornings and do the program together.  I also pursued it on my own later.  I worked up to running to the track in Astoria Park and back, and then added distance by adding laps.  Sometimes on Saturdays I do long runs through Astoria, going anywhere I feel like.  If you're running in the streets and want to calculate distance, you can use Google Ped (http://www.gmap-pedometer.com/), or if you want real-time data, something like a Garmin Forerunner (http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_ss_e?url=search-alias%3Delectronics&field-keywords=forerunner&x=0&y=0).

I agree with Frappe about buying good running shoes.  Jack Rabbit Sports is good, or you can go to Super Runners Shop (http://www.superrunnersshop.com/Lex.htm) on Lex and 89th.  They also take your time to check your gait and recommend shoes based on that.  The first pair they had me try on turned out to be perfect for me, and I've been using the same brand/model ever since.

There is a running club based out of Astoria Park - Hellgate Road Runners (http://hrr-online.org/).  The google cache of their site says they sponsor a 5k race every June, but I can't find any information about it on their site, so they may not have done it for the past couple of years.

Otherwise, check out New York Road Runners (http://www.nyrr.org/).  They have the most races, and a lot of 5k races.  Occasionally they have races in Queens, but I'd recommend sucking it up and going to one of their races in Central Park one weekend morning.  It's really not far, and it's so beautiful running through Central Park early in the morning.  No matter how I do in the race, it always makes my day to be running, and then walking, through the park so early before the city wakes up.  It's just beautiful.

I'm still not a fast runner, and I never will be.  But I get the job done, and as I've qualified for the 2009 Marathon, that is my next goal.

Post by: bryanc on September 07, 2008, 04:33:35 PM
Just passed, but every August the Federation of Italian Americans of Queens hosts a 5K run here in Astoria. This was the first year I ran the race and it was a lot of fun. Another way to connect to the Astoria community.

On the topic of why to find a 5k race rather than run the 3.1 miles on your own...community and motivation. Although running in a tight pack of runners takes some getting used to, it's definitely inspiring to run among others that probably started running for similar reasons to get in shape. One of the perks of running is that you can do it on your own and on your own schedule, but if you haven't done so, I strongly suggest throwing an organized race into the mix every now and then. It will help keep your running energized.

Also, if you plan in running outside, it will be difficult to start running during the winter, but not impossible. The colder weather and short days are the main challenges. If you run on a treadmill, you can avoid the weather and light issues, but for some treadmills can be incredibly boring. It's all about knowing yourself and what your "excuses" are, then you can stay motivated and start to really love running.

Best of luck to you!
Bryan
