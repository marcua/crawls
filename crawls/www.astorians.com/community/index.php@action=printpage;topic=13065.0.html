Title: Pediatricians & Hospital Affiliations
Post by: domenica on November 13, 2008, 10:35:52 PM
I recently had to take my child to the emergency room. I've had such negative experiences at the local emergency rooms (Astoria General, Elmhurst) in the past, that we decided to take the baby to the Komansky Center at New York Presbyterian. As usual, they were outstanding and made us all (including my child) feel at ease. I love that hospital and the doctors that I've dealt with there, but its such a pain to get to when there is traffic or during the holiday season.

We go to a pediatrician in Astoria who is affiliated with Flushing Hospital and North Shore. The pediatrician is OK, good for coughs and colds, and the regular shots, convenient location, but I wouldn't want them to care for my child with a serious illness-I just wouldn't trust them because they are not as thorough or knowledgable as the NY Presby doctors were. Does anyone know anything about the Flushing or North Shore hospitals and how they fare?  They also seem very far away from Astoria. My experiences at the other ERs I mentioned above were bad because everything was rushed, overcrowded, not very thorough or seemingly scientific, dirty conditions, nobody seemed to care, and a large proportion of the staff didn't speak English and then to top it all off years of billing issues because someone at the hospital let the ball drop and didn't bill the insurance in time. Is this what I can expect at the other hospitals in Queens? I want to be prepared and informed should there (knock on wood) be another trip to the ER. Would love to hear your experiences.

I couldn't agree more with you re: Mt. Sinai Queens ER- I have never had to take my daughter there, but I've been there 2x, and it has been a disaster on all levels.
