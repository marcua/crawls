Let's consider a hypothetical conversation going on in a cafe in Baghdad between two Iraqis who are members of the 65% of Baghdad residents who, according to the most recent polls, want the US out immediately.  What has happened is that the US has withdrawn.  The final troops have just flown out of the airport in Baghdad and Iraq is officially free of a US presence.  How has this happened you ask?  Well there has been a military coup.  A group of US Generals had finally had enough of W sending troops wherever he wanted and they were putting an end to it.  One of the Generals then declared himself President, for the good of the nation and to protect said nation from neo-con extremists of course.  The new General/President then declared that there would be free elections.  What he didn't say was that most of his opponents would be suppressed or arrested and that popular protest would be "highly discouraged."  Then surprise surprise he wins the next election with over 90% of the vote.

Now our friends in the Baghdad cafe are good democrats and say that they definitely don't agree with a dictatorship, but they must admit that any semblance of a functioning democracy being crushed in the US is good for the Middle East.  How would we feel about this?  And if we wouldn't want a dictatorship here to prevent our extremist president from doing anything else crazy, then how can we just shrug and say, "well, it's better than the alternative" when it comes to another people's country?
