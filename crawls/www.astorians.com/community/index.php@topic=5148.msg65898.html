Great thread, excellent information.  I'd be really interested in making cleaning products, I have an 8 year old who might also be interested in helping out. 

I read a really interesting book called "Not Buying It: My Year Without Shopping" by Judith Levine who lives in Brooklyn.  She did a fairly radical not buying thing for the sake of the book, but certainly an interesting read for anyone interested in withdrawing from mad (or even just habitual) consumerism.  The amount of money she saved is truly inspiring.  And the reassessment of "necessity" is probably something we could all benefit from periodically.  I think it's a bit like tv, you think you'll miss it more than you actually do, once you get going, though there are times when it's really hard.  I lived without tv for about 10 years and now I find I miss not-tv.  My family is adamantly opposed to chucking the thing, though we have only broadcast so that limits the stupidity.  Plus saves loads of money.  And I find I figure out much sooner that there's actually nothing to watch.

I'd love to switch to energy efficient light bulbs but I like the full spectrum light.  Does anyone know if there is any such animal?  And hopefully conservation can offset the higher cost of green/wind power.

I read last week that SF has an ordinance to ban plastic bags altogether, although it would allow recyclable ones.  Also the Container Store has a garbage pail with notches on the side to hold the handles of plastic shopping bags so I just use them for garbage bags, though I do try to use canvas, string and other conveyances as much as possible. 

As for the water issue, you can get a test kit for about $20 that will test what actually comes out of your tap for all kinds of things, and then you know.  If you let tap water sit for about 24 hours the chlorine evaporates out of it.  I suppose you could test it again and see what the difference is. I don't know how we all got sold on this idea of bottled water being so much better.  In some places it's probably true, but not in NY.
