That place had been there as long as I can remember.  Boy is 30th Avenue between Steinway & 31st changing!   We also lost in recent years Marino's, the terrific fish store in that same general area.
 
The green grocer on the corner of 35th I think became a restaurant that came and went in a minute and now that became a grilled chicken place.
 
The candy store diagonally across the street became Akli (sic) a terrific, if short-lived, high-end Greek seafood place, and then became "Bar 30", which didn't last much longer than Alki.
 
And between 32nd & 33rd, the chinese take out place on one side and another fish store closed to become another bakery.
 
That new hamburger place on the corner of 32nd & 30th Avnue replaced a short-lived donut shop that replaced Pinocchio, a restaurant that was originally between 31st and 32nd.  It literally moved across the street when they put up that bank building on the southwest corner of 32nd & 30th Avenue.  I heard Pinocchio was going to move down 30th Avenue on the "other side" of 31st Street, but I don't know if that ever happened.
 
And I received a flyer in the mail for a new place "on the other side" of Steinway for a sports type place that is partially owned by C.C. Sabathia, the Yankees' pitcher.  I think it is art of a chain.  I haven't made it over there yet, haven't even seen it, but it sounds like the kind of place to watch the Sunday football games.

Quote from: neo11 on July 09, 2007, 12:51:29 AM
Wow, amazing post!  I still remember some of the things you mentioned, like Sampan and Playworld and Eisenberg's and the old dance club on 35th and Steinway, even though I was far too young to go back then.  Only one thing I'll point out: there was, in fact, a great toy store in Astoria before Toys R' Us rolled into town and aside from Playworld:  Valco, on Steinway between 34th and Broadway.  It was where the Lucille Roberts is now, I believe.  That place had a treasure trove of toys including some pretty obscure stuff that one would never find in a big-box toy store like Toys R' Us.  I think it disappeared, unfortunately, sometime around 1990.

I remember as a young kid going there with my mom, and often, stopping at the dry cleaners that was a couple of doors down next to what was the Modernage Diner (now the Astoria Diner)...Dandy Dry Cleaners was the name.  There was a friendly old guy behind the counter (Cuban, I believe) and I seem to recall there being lollipops there sometimes as well.

Also, on the same block but across the street, there had been a small Hershey's Ice Cream store for a few years.  I can't remember the exact location but it was closer to 34th Avenue than to Broadway.
