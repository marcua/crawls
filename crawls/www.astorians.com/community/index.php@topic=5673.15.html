With daylight savings time and the fact that I came right after work and switched from the 7 to the N I was amazed at the pace of development on the plaza, particularly on the north side.   Those apartment complexes are huge, but it still doesn't look like the most appealing place to live. 

And I noticed on the south side of the plaza there is scaffolding surrounding a building (that used to contain a bar that is now a convenience store) and I wonder if that building is coming down.   It is right by the stairs on the south side of the plaza.

Because of the high rents in Manhattan -- I'm convinced the streetscape in Manhattan will contain only Starbucks, banks & Duane Reades -- a piano showroom moved its digs to a street one block south of the plaza off Jackson Avenue.   They began having jazz concerts.   Since I attended I was able to for the first time get a close up look of that completed apartment building right off Jackson with the ceiling to floor glass windows.   I think someone said those units were going for $450K.   Directly across the street is that parking garage that one wag called the ugliest building in NYC and that I think is going to come down.   I don't know if anyone is even living in this building.   You can see it from the Queensborough Plaza elevated platform and at night lights are on in some of the apartments, but I bet the building owner put them there.

There was a decrepit industrial building that was directly across the street from this building that recently was torn down so look for more development.

Do they still release Riker's Island prisoners at Queensboro Plaza?   I think they probably get discharged in the early morning hours because their sentence would end at midnight.   They spend the overnight hours getting processed.   By the time that happens hours have gone by.

I bet they will find a new place for that once those new buildings start to open and the area continues to gentrify.   I remember that before the Time-Warner Center was built on Columbus Circle the old New York Coliseum stood there.   Since prisons are about the only industry left in Upstate New York many New York City prisoners end up getting sent way, way, way upstate.   An entrepreneurial sort started a bus service so the lprisoners' women could visit them.   The guy would pick up the women (and their children) at the Coliseum.   However, once Time-Warner and the upscale mall (called an "urban shopping experience" by the complex's PR flacks) the guy had to find a new spot to load his passengers.   They are now picked up at 58th Street and Broadway.   The same thing will no doubt happen in Queensboro Plaza if it hasn't happened already.
