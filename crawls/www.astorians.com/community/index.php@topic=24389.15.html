I moved from the West Village to Astoria (Broadway & 32nd) way back in '87 and paid $600 for a 2-bedroom apt. in a private home.  The house was sold twice while I was there and I was paying $800 or $900 a month in 2001 when "The New York Times" ran a piece that talked about Astoria was an undiscovered gem.  After that article Astoria was "discovered" and my landlord wanted to raise my rent to $1,700/$1,800 a month.  From what I understand, after that article, many landlords raised the rents they were charging.

So, time to move.  I found a rent stabilized apt. in what was then a hot market for a one-bedroom apt. (yes, I was forced to "downsize") on 30th Ave./34th Street and now pay $1,500 a month.


My biggest regret is that I didn't get a rent stabilized place in Astoria back in '87 when I first moved here.  I had no idea then that I'd be staying. 

That is my advice to anyone moving to Astoria now -- especially if you plan on staying -- is to get a rent-stabilized place.
