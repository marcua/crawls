Kitau (?) the new sushi on 23rd Ave.
« Reply #16 on: March 07, 2009, 10:39:58 AM »
Decided to try it last night. It's right next door to Club 23, yet worlds apart of course! Although I think the music seeps through, which is too bad.

Decor is fantastic, whoever's renovating the restaurants on 23rd Ave (Bangkok Tasty for example) is doing a fabulous job  :mrgreen: Kitau used to be the Greek supermarket that I liked, but you'd never know it. Creative interior. Lights above the tables could be shielded a bit more- no big deal.

Food, however, was just decent. I loved it; presentation as wonderful as the interior design, but my sushi-savvy dining companion wasn't as impressed. He tried the $13 bottle of sake and again, my less-experienced palate thought it was fine, while he deemed it "cheap." We had the black angel and spicy girl rolls (excellent); split one of the sushi entrees with the three types of sushi (came with soup and salad, pretty standard); were offered a sample appetizer consisting of cream cheese with.....oh I forget, it was tasty though.

Still, the prices are normal, service was great (they allowed us to linger which was nice) and they appear kid-friendly for those of you who like that sort of thing (biting my tongue)

Verdict: Wonderful treat on payday after a very rough week if you're not much of a sushi snob. Also, this would be a fine place to impress a date (who's not a sushi snob!) or just relax with a group.

My wife and I went to this place for lunch today for the first time and were pleasantly surprised given some of the negative reviews in this thread. We had the 3-roll lunch special, tried the "age tofu" and "tempura" appetizers.

The prices are definitely not more expensive than I'm used to paying in Manhattan for a similar lunch ($8 as of today for 3 rolls, salad, and miso soup). We found the staff were attentive, and the food was fine. For an $8 lunch though I'm not expecting to have the red carpet rolled out. The food quality was certainly on par with anything I've had in Midtown or Murry Hill for a Sushi lunch (actually better than a couple places I've been to in Midtown). The place was clean, and the decor is nice.

After today's lunch I don't have any complaints about Kitaku.

Agreed.  We also tried it for lunch over the weekend.  And we both enjoyed everything we had.  The miso soup was very tasty and hot.  The salad was ice cold and fresh.  I had a dinner roll as an appetizer.  The name was something like Spicy Mountain.  It was delicious.  Stuffed with crabmeat and avocado.  Then actually battered and fried like tempura.  Then smothered with crawfish and spicy Japanese mayonnaise.  It had to be the most unhealthy and delicious thing on the menu.  I highly recommend it.  Then I had the beef negimaki.  Very good.  Tender beef.  More scallion than I could eat.  But I enjoyed the flavors.  And a nice portion.  My wife got a mixed bento box.  There was over a square foot of plate with little mounds of items here and there.  So not my thing.  But she loved it and said each item was delicious.  The main part of the combo was a salmon terriyaki and she said it was cooked perfectly and very tasty.  We will go back for sure.  And we might even try their Chinese menu since the Chinese choices in town tend to go from bad to worse.  Maybe this place is good.  But their Japanese is very good.  And I also think the prices are moderate and fair.  And for a change, they had a nice crowd.  And the service couldn't be friendlier.
