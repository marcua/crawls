Waiting.. Waiting.. Waiting For This Song To Change.
« on: March 19, 2008, 12:45:03 AM »
So, I was in a  store  yesterday, and the  song by  John Mayer.. Waiting For the World  To Change.. this years  'Anti-War' tune. Besides  being a pretentious bore, it did remind me of the history  of the Protest and Patriotic  song. I guess the furthest back one can go, in the USA at least, would be Woody Guthrie. In my youth I found many of his songs  inspiring and great, of course, at one time  I watched  Bozo and believed  Spiderman was swinging through NYC. :lol: But, Woody, did have some great songs,best known for  'This Land Is Your  Land", Woody  was  a great American folk singer. Later, I found out Woody used this as an alternative  to Irving Berlins Flag-Waver 'God Bless America'. He originally wanted to call the song 'God Blessed America."  Now, Woody grew up at  a  time  when Socialism and Communism had a 'human' face still, and he did throw in, but, to his credit, was  never  a member of either. Actors  like Will Greer, best known as Grandpa  Walton on the Waltons, first introduced  Woody to members of  the Communist Party. Still, Woody Guthrie  was not a  'joiner', more  stalwart  'protest' singers like  Pete  Seeger, who was an avowed Stalinist and  who took 50 years after Stalins  death to admit he was  'wrong', were much more  than just fellow travelers. Woody was married  three times and fathered  8 kids, Arlo his son, was most famous  in the late 60's  with Alices  Restaurant, but, no way as talented as Woody. Woody died  of  Huntingtons  disease.He carried  with him a guitar, from the Dustball Era of the 30's, which had  a  sticker 'This Guitar  Kills  Fascists', unfortunately, Woody himself did not want to actually  kill Fascists, as he avoided  being drafted  during  WW 2, cause he thought he could do more  harm against them at home by writing songs?    Eventually, he joined the Merchant Marine, but, was bounced  into the Army, oddly enough the Army  was OK with him being associated  with Communists, and the Merchant Marine  was not, go figure?  :lol:

Although, the 50's  are many times  associated  with 'repression'  of Folk Singers with 'left'  leanings, it was only early in the 1950's, with the Iron Curtain descending in Eastern Europe  and  the crimes  of 'Uncle' Joe  Stalin came to light, that many proud, 'liberal/progressive' Folks  began to distance themselves from the Communist Party. Woody was not one of  them, he continued  on till his death sadly in the late 60's. I consider Woody, even though  I vehemently disagree with his support of Communists, the  quintessential  'Protest' singer/writer.

Fast foward  to the  late 60's, Country Joe and  the Fish, "I Feel Like I'm Fix'n To Die Rag.'  In response  to the  lingering Vietnam conflict, many  songs of protest emerged, but, unlike  WW 2, or even the early Cold  War, when many actors, singers  also  supported  the conflicts in song and movie, Vietnam spawned  louder and more angry protest songs. This song by  Country Joe and the Fish, to me, epitimised one of them. Although, the  'Whoppee  We're  All Gonna Die" ending added  a Carnival like  atmosphere  to a deadly serious  War. Still,  there  were  songs  and movies  supportive, like John Waynes 'Green Berets' and the song "Ballads  Of The Green Berets."  The song was written by Robin Moore and Staff Sgt. Barry Sadler, while the latter was recuperating from a leg wound suffered as a medic in the Vietnam War. Moore also wrote a non-fiction book, The Green Berets, about the force. Contrary to popular  belief, many were  supportive of the Vietnam Conflict, at least the first few years.

Certainly, John Lennon, after leaving  The Beatles, wore  some of  his politics  on his sleeve, but, even he would admit  that Instant Karma  and Imagine  are notable  exceptions  and one can say it was more his inflated ego  on display than any serious  song  on a better  world. Sadly, John should have been more careful, used to see him quite  a  bit  when I was a kid, even shook his hand once.

As the Vietnam Conflict faded, so did the Protest song, and Patriotic  songs slowly  started  making a  comeback, not seen since  the early 60's. The Hostage  crisis  ending in Iran ,with release of the hostages, after 444 days, made the song 'Tie  A  Yellow  Ribbon' by Tony Orlando And  Dawn a hit.  The song was  really about a convict let free, but was soon used by folks  tired  of the USA  being run down  and attacked, and they tied  yellow ribbons as  a show  of solidarity for the hostages.

Over the years Country music  has always  been a mainstay of Patriotic songs, whereas  'Rock' was always  considered more 'protest' music  of the 'system.' Although, this is far from the case. Many a  'Metal' show, particularly  bands  like  MANOWAR, or even the more  recent  METALLICA and THREE DOORS  DOWN, have issued  records  songs supportive of the armed  forces  in combat. An exception to Country music was a band like  the Dixie Chicks, who seemed  to ride  a wave of discontent, but, as many of  the 'protest' songs artists discover, most end  in obscurity or  a footnote  in history. Which brings  me to this song.

I never  heard of John Mayer, and this  song is  pretty tepid and not much of  a 'protest'.. waiting for the world  to change?' Hmm, let's  wait for  the leaves  to change?  :lol:  Pretty bad, what's  most disturbing about this era is  not  the plethora  of protest songs and films, which I mostly detest, and find  terribly narrow minded and full of self  importance, but, the lack of  real movies and music supportive of say the Troops  in Afghan and the  War on those  who attacked us.

In spite  of the characterisations by some of my posts and thoughts here, I don't want to eliminate  anyone  from speaking  or  singing, writing or running down nude  in the middle of Times  Square, OK, maybe  if the look good on the latter.  :lol:  No, citizen soldiers  should  speak up, but, do no harm to what is good. The culture  seems  so  one-sided  today  as it is stifling. Everywhere  I go I am greeted  with one version of the days events  in one of the largest Cities in the nation. If I dare to criticise, I am attacked, a mob mentality ensues. In days  past, alot  of things were in the American Mix. Woody Guthrie was around the time  of the Andrew Sisters and  a  Hollywood highly supportive  of that conflict. In the 50's, falsely characterised  as  this blanket repressive time was  when Rock N'Roll  took off and films abounded  pushing the envelope. The conflicting sides came to head  in the late 60's, but, even as  rioting broke out in the streets  of Chicago  and a  whole generation lost their direction in Upstate  NY at  WoodStock, US  Astronauts  landed  on the Moon and planted  the stars  and stripes. 70's  Chaos and the reality that  the Woodstock generation was also  Charlie  Mansons soon had  further  blows when  'Free' Love  spawned an explosion of unwanted children and eventually  the tragedy  of AIDS of the  mid-80's. Still, the long gas lines  and hostage crisis led me and others who wanted to see the best  for  the nation turn from  the late 70's  down turn to the optimistic  'Reagan Era.'  The 80's  were mostly a happy time  for this  nation, although the Cold War conflict provided  some drama. The removal of Saddam from Kuwait in 1991 provided  a perfect cap, with an almost WW 2 patriotism. The  Iron Curtain finally came down at the end of  that era  and by 1992, we became so comfortable, that  we had forgotten that enemies  of  freedom still lurked  out there, the WTC bombing in 1993 for  example. Saddam would torment  our pilots, his people  for another decade, and the attacks from Al Qeada would increase, culminating  with 9/11. The enemies of Freedom. Woody Guthries  Freedom, to  John Waynes Freedom to your Freedom and mine, is not a  Freedom to be  taken lightly, nor does it mean we all have to agree on everything. But, no matter  what side you are on, in the end it was all happening here, the good Ole' USA.

Hamas Cleric Predicts 'Rome Will Be Conquered by Islam'
Monday , April 14, 2008

A sermon last Friday by a prominent Muslim cleric and Hamas member of the Palestinian parliament openly declared that "the capital of the Catholics, or the Crusader capital," would soon be conquered by Islam.

The fiery sermon, delivered by Yunis al-Astal and aired on Hamas' Al-Aqsa TV, predicted that Rome would become "an advanced post for the Islamic conquests, which will spread though Europe in its entirety, and then will turn to the two Americas, even Eastern Europe."

"Allah has chosen you for Himself and for His religion," al-Astal preached, "so that you will serve as the engine pulling this nation to the phase of succession, security and consolidation of power, and even to conquests through da'wa and military conquests of the capitals of the entire world.

"Very soon, Allah willing, Rome will be conquered, just like Constantinople was, as was prophesized by our prophet Muhammad," he added.

Al-Astal last June preached how it was the duty of Palestinian women to martyr themselves by becoming homicide bombers.

"The most exalted form of jihad is fighting for the sake of Allah, which means sacrificing one's soul by fighting the enemies head-on, even if it leads to martyrdom," he said in a June 23, 2007 interview.

"When jihad becomes an individual duty, it applies to women too, because women do not differ from men when it comes to individual duties," he said, calling Jews "the brothers of apes and pigs" who should "taste the bitterness of death."

Friday's rant repeated that theme: "Today, Rome is the capital of the Catholics, or the Crusader capital, which has declared its hostility to Islam, and has planted the brothers of apes and pigs in Palestine in order to prevent the reawakening of Islam.

"I believe that our children, or our grandchildren, will inherit our jihad and our sacrifices, and, Allah willing, the commanders of the conquest will come from among them.

"Today, we instill these good tidings in their souls and by means of the mosques and the Koran books, and the history of our Prophets, his companions, and the great leaders, we prepare them for the mission of saving humanity from the hellfire at whose brink they stand."
