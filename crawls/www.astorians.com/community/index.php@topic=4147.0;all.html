Gamer's Playin' after 80 hours at Sony station


Extreme gamer Angel Paredes spent nearly 80 hours on line then shelled out $900 in cash to get his PlayStation 3 last night.
Now comes the hard part.

"I'm gonna go home, take a shower, then hook it up," said Paredes, 31, of Manhattan. "Then I'm gonna play the rest of the night and this whole week."

PlayStation fanatics apparently don't need sleep.

Paredes was the first to get his PS3 at the Sony Style store at E. 56th St. and Madison Ave. Thanks to Sony, he and Nos. 2 and 3 on line all got courtesy limos to take their toys home.

The throng was also entertained by rapper Ludacris, who performed several songs to help pass the time. Before the midnight launch, video-game geeks nervously guarded their spots in long lines at electronics and video stores around the city, checking their watches compulsively.

"Everyone is getting restless," said Jeremy Teacher, 27, of New Jersey, who waited outside Sony Style for more than 28 hours.Valencia Torro, a hairdresser from Mount Vernon, said she wasn't getting out of line come hell or high water.

"I need this and I need it now. My kids won't sleep at night because they keep thinking about it," said Toro, 33. "I can't go home empty-handed."

Tempers flared briefly at the Circuit City store at Union Square, where gamers traded accusations of linecutting. "You can't even sleep unless you have your eyes open," Wataru Nada said.

Sony has promised to make available 400,000 PS3 machines in the U.S. today and about 1 million by year's end.

I think that people (in general, but most certainly not all) equate things that others do that they would "never" do themselves with being a "loser." It just so happens that many women (not all - I am obviously a woman who loves her WoW) and some men (I know many personally) that think that playing a video game is a complete waste of time. I have had some people get mad and throw this argument in my face, "Well, you don't see me playing World of Warcraft when I go home. I do something worthwhile like going to theater." In my case, I have no real interest in theater, and I will never truly understand why people spend hundreds, if not thousands, every year to go see the same shows over and over again (or a myriad of shows). The one show that I really have liked has been Spamalot, in part because it was able to poke fun at itself (and it is Monty Python for goodness sake!). Of course, the same person who thinks I am stupid for playing WoW on occasion (I haven't played it in the last few days in fact), says that Spamalot is not true theater and isn't a very good show anyway.

So some people think I am a loser for playing video games, but I'd like to think that those same people don't judge me entirely for that one thing that I happen to enjoy as a past-time (I also enjoy other pasttimes as well). If they judge me it should be based upon lots of things I do! (slight sarcasm there) I have people (sometimes the same person/people) who think it is a novel and strange idea that I like to buy my summer vegetables from a farm and "what is a CSA again?" They also think it is strange and makes me a bit of a loser because I like gardening and composting, and try to live a greener lifestyle. Or "You read a lot of books. The last time I read a book was...oh I can't remember. I can't imagine reading anything but the newspaper on the subway." A really good one is the argument that I must not enjoy life because I don't spend as much money as I should on "things" and "restaurants."

So therefore, I think that there will always be someone out there who thinks I am a loser regardless of what I do.

I also think that the people who say that video games = loserness should actually sit down and play half a dozen different kinds of video games in one day. I guarantee you that you'll get hooked on at least one kind. I personally recommend MMORPGs (WoW of course), strategy games (Rise of Nations or Age of Empires, and Supreme Commander is in beta right now), first person shooters (any of the Battlefield games, Half Life, etc.), simulation (Sims 2) and even pogo.com for the short diversions and pointless badges. I personally have over a hundred badges. :-)
