I don't understand why people are so upset at Frappe's post. This is a "rant & vent" forum; he appears to be venting. He also said he was on the train that hit that person. I think it is completely reasonable for him to be upset.

I also don't understand some of the criticisms. Everyone knows that the person who committed suicide was suffering and was in a terrible state, but this doesn't mean that all anger directed towards that person is somehow cruel or inhuman. I think it is also unfair to suggest that we ought to "think of what this poor person must have been going through in order to do something like take there own life," without also acknowledging what those people on that train went through. We can and should make room for those people to express their feelings, and many of those people, like it or not, will feel anger. Just imagine how terrible it would have been if you were a parent sitting with your children in the front of the train. I think most people in that situation would feel a deep sense of anger and frustration afterwards. That's completely natural. Why belittle or criticize those feelings?

Also, I think it's wrong to take Frappe's suggestion to imply that it's possible to commit suicide without affecting others. I think his point has to do with the degree to which it affects others. All suicides affect others. If you jump off of a building and land on a group of tourists though, it affects others in a much more egregious sense. I think this was what he had in mind concerning this particular suicide.

I think everyone should keep in mind that Frappe was on a train that killed someone two days ago. If he is upset about it and expresses his anger in the appropriate "rant & vent" forum, why not just see his post as a way for him to say what's on his mind and to get it off his chest?
