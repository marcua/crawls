I still hear about guys who were supposed to be mafia guys from Astoria.  I hear people talk about an assh*le named Sonny, who had a bar on 29th and 30th, and then sold that and had a couple of candy stores.  I saw him a few times as a small kid, he had Lon Chaney eyes.  Average height, dark complexion, and tattoos.  Some thought he was gangster, some still do.   I have a Sony story that my uncles and friends tell. If you are wondering why I call him assh^le, story will explain:

About 1982, a guy I will call Vinny (He is still alive)  came into OTB on Broadway. He is on some of those crime charts they occasionally show, and is listed as Genovese Captain.  He wa sabout 50 at time, big guy, tough, but easy going. I thought he looked like the late Sam Kinison.  Did not dress up much either, looked like a truck driver.     

Vinny walks in, and an old man, a retired, on apension, is one of the people he says hi to. Vinny just wants to make  a bet, but old man has problem.   He tells Vinny that he is owed money by Sonny, a local guy, bookie, who took money and won't pay him on a few sports bets.    Sonny, the 'Gangster" is blowing guy off, telling him to get lost.   Vinny say's he'll see what he can do. 

Vinny approaches, and Sonny is with friends, who know who Vinny is.  Vinny asks Sonny for  amoment to talk, Sonny always had a scowl, nasty f#ck, just stares.   Vinny, in his easy going way, explains he knows old timer, and asks if Sonny owes him money, and if so, can he 'Help him out, and pay him?"     Just like that. Sonny replies 'Go f#ck yourself."   
Vinny hits him.   Sonny goes flying.  I like the way a bystander explained it to me: 
"Ever see the Three Stooges, or Abott and Costello, when a guy gets hit with a flowerpot on the head, and just slinks down a wall, and they make that a cuckoo bird sound as guy's roll up---that's how Sonny went down." 

Vinny ran out.  When Sonny woke up (they had to get ice from bar and throw some  on him)   he was told who he had said f#ck off to.   He recognized the name.  The old man who was owed the money got it next day.    :-)


To this day, on occasion, someone will mention old Astoria guys, and Sonny's name will come up. "He was big Mafia guy, very connected," someone will say.   I wish I had that  knockout on video to show them.

I believe they're still in cahoots with one of the main Italian families.
