As the owner of a very feisty, high energy,  Husky / Shepherd / Labrador Mixed puppy, I am all too well aware of how hard it is to select the most durable and safe leash. My puppy went through 5 leashes in her first year, and they weren't cheap on the wallet either!  One broke while I was in the park with her and thank goodness she didn't run out into the traffic. Another one scratched the top of her nose so bad that her fur came off. And they were all from Petco! While looking to replace the last leash my little rascal wear and tear through, I decided to take my pup with me and check out the new store in my neighborhood My Little Buddy Pet Boutique.  The ladies there were so incredibly helpful and they educated me on selecting the right leash. When I used to go to Petco and other chain pet supply stores, I had the mentality that one leash fits all, at least that was the attitude I got from the sales personnel that worked at those stores. All I can say is that I experienced the best customer service whenever I visit My Little Buddy Pet Boutique, they remember my name and my pup's after the first visit and they've always gone out of their way in helping me select the right food, snack and most recently the right collar for my pup. Sorry to hear about your bad experience, it really does sound out of character to me. Good luck on your future shopping expeditions for your dog.

Sometimes you get one chance to make a good impression and shilling on the message board is not going to make that go away.  Even if the store owner or employee was having a bad day, they should have a clear policy on returns or at least make an effort to resolve the issue.

Maybe the leash is too small for the dog or maybe the leash in construction or design is defective, but what matters is not letting the customer go away with something that is broken.

Running a business like a local pet store is really about relationships and loyalty, in so much the customer can feel confident to go back and shop for additional pet supplies time and again.  The retail environment is a very competitive with online shopping, big big box retailers and chains.
