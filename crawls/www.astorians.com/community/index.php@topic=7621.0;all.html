I love Briemere pies as I grew up very close to their farm on Long Island.  I will always be able to smell that wonderful scent of walking into their bakery...

However, be aware that Briemere uses Lard to make their crusts.  Also, although $6/slice is steap, Briemere charges a pretty penny for their pies.  I believe that the apple pie is the cheapest pie at $15 (most pies are in the $20 range).  I suggest taking a ride out there one day.  The pies and breads are usually warm when you get them.  There produce is just as good as their pies.

Separately, and I know this sounds odd, but the frozen apple and blueberry pies from the Vermont Baking Co (sold in Whole Foods) is fantastic.  I was informed of this on chowhound and the person was totally correct.  They are flash frozen with fresh fruit.  I was in search of a good apple (or Blueberry for that matter) pie in Astoria and I could not find anything edible. I thought Martha's pie was disgusting...it tasted like gooey canned apple compote.   

trust me on the Vermont Baking co pies...they are awesome.

Oh, Briermere's strawberry rhubarb is my absolute favourite.   :smitten:

If you go right to the farm stand (which is about an hour and a half from Astoria), the prices for whole pies this summer range from $14 for the apple, to $18 for the lemon meringue and fruit cream pies.  They also have two pies that are $26 each.  They are expensive because they use good ingredients and are hand made. 

A neighbour of ours bought a whole Briermere peach pie from Oleput; I think they charged him $24.  If you buy it direct from Briermere, it's $14.50, plus about $15 worth of gas (150 miles r/t; 5 gallons at 30 mpg, $3/gallon) . . . so it's actually cheaper to buy it here in Astoria.  (A trip out to the North Fork is worth it anyway - not talking about that.)

$6 for a slice is cheaper than a lot of desserts in a lot of restaurants, and I think it's great as a treat.  I also like the fact that I can get a slice of Briermere pie without a three hour drive, and without the rest of the pie sitting on my counter to tempt me!

I'm sure Oleput would be happy to answer questions about the freshness of any of their pies.
