OK, As  everyone  here knows, my politics  veer to  the Right. Still, I always enjoy  Political  Conventions, speeches  that make me cringe, infuriating and  even emotional.

First... the DEMOCRAT  NATIONAL CONVENTION 2008

The first  day had some  'religious' woman speaking about  how  'we'  mistreated  Indians, had Slaves and are in no position   to remove  'evil' people.  This was  a bad start in my view, it reinforces the  feeling by many that  the party is run by radical  leftists. Still, this was early afternoon, by the first evening, we heard the usual  fluff, nothing special.

Day 2, Hillary Clinton, in an orange pantsuit, with Chelsea  in tow, my, unfortunately, Senator :D   gave  a  speech, mostly, about what else.. herself.. oh yeah, and Vote  For  Obama.  ;)  Obviously she felt it was hers and it was taken away.

Day  3, Bill takes  the stage, and rips  into Bush, of course, most of his points  were  exaggeration and outright falsehoods, but, it is  a  political  conventions and he is 'Bubba.' ;)  This over shadowed the 65 year old  Biden, who  for some reason represents 'change'  like Obama, even though Biden has been a Senator  longer than McCain and is only 6 years  younger. Apparently, 'age' only matters  for Republicans?  ;)  Anyway, the mans  speech was  incoherent and full of  stumbles, if Bush had made  this speech it would be  on the nightly news  as  a  'gaffe'. Of course  the Press  being on the democrat  side, except for FOX News, which at least offers  the other  side, only featured  Clinton.

Which brings us to Day 4, and the Obama 'Rock Concert.' This has to go  down in history as one of the most excessive, megalomaniac  event in Convention history. I mean using a separate  stadium is not even done by sitting Presidents  for reelection, let alone someone who has been barely in politics. It was pure Hollywood, no joke, Spielberg and others  have helped set up the effects, I half expected  R2D2  to show up with  C3PO. If the democrats think this  makes them palatable to  the 'common man', well, I just  don't  see it. Obama made a list of what he says  is wrong and how to fix it. IMO, It was  worthy  of a great  Greco-Roman Coliseum event, but, in the end it was a  'hollywood' extravaganza, with little substance.

In all, the Democrat  Convention inspired  me, once  again, to vote Republican.  :D   Seriously, anyone else, even if you dont agree, let me know. I promise  I won't  'bite', just 'growl'  a little.  :D  Now on to the  Republican National Convention and  Nov.   :-)

REPUBLICAN NATIONAL CONVENTION 2008


I am a Republican, so anything I say here will self-destruct in five seconds... ;D  Just kidding.

Well, The Republicans had their turn and it started  off  with a Hurricane  and ended  with a Warrior. Sounds  like  my kind of folks.  :D

Day 1) This was  dominated  by the 'news'  of Gustav the relatively mild  Hurricane  that was finally  handled  correctly, by those  MOST responsible for  such things in a state. The  newly  elected  Republican Gov. Jindel who was  on McCain's  short list for VP, in Louisiana. Mrs. McCain and Mrs. Bush urged conventioneers  to put off  the politics  and help fellow  Americans  in the Gulf Region. Sen. McCain went to the region and  it showed  a great American spirit.  The press spent  the day  in what became  a disgraceful  tirade  against the pregnant 17 year-old daughter  of the VP, cause  she  was unmarried and *gasp* her mother  is  not in favor of Abortion and Free Condoms  for everybody  in school.

Day 2) It really started  to get rolling. Highlight  for me  was former Senator  Fred Thompson. The one time actor, really laid into the empty suit at  the top  of  the democrat  ticket and  the 30  year  career 'yes'  man as  VP for  the democrats. He also recounted  in sobering detail the top of  the GOP Ticket McCains, Vietnam experience and how he went to Washington  and fought  for what  he believed, even as it bucked  his own party. Fantastic.

Day 3) Guiliani, the Mayor  of NYC, on 9/11, much maligned  by a horrendous  one-sided  press  that controls, sadly, my fair city of NY, proved once  again, why he was able to clean  up a city, run for decades by the corrupt democrat machine. A city of millions which had  2,500  murders  a year and  turned it into a great town by the time he left.  He highlighted  how the democrats  had lost touch in what had happened on 9/11 and how the top of their  ticket had less executive experience than Palin. Still, this was Palins  evening. Palin, who went from relative  unknown to VP pick and had the sharks  of  the press  trying to pick her apart, proved why she was  called  'barracuda.'  She lay into  Obama's  'styro-foam' Greek Temple  Spectacle and showed  exactly  what it was, a Hollywood  production with no substance. :D She laid  out her personal experience and showed that she is no God, but, certainly  God Fearing and a woman who rose  from Mayor to Governor, in a  state  vital to our energy  needs and even Northern Defense. She  proved  to  me she is more capable  as the VP for  the GOP, than Obama  could show  as  President for  the democrats. Nice  going, Palin.

Day 4)  This was McCains night. He  was preceded  by Cindy McCain, a true  class act.  As McCain began to speak, a couple of  the Left-wing Thugs that have been breaking windows and causing mayhem outside tried  to disrupt  the man from speaking to which McCain responded after  one of  the nuts, a woman, who had to be dragged out, 'Not To Listen To The Static.'  This is  the face of the so-called  'Anti-War'  movement? LOL!!  Anti-War  MY *** . Anyway, Senator  McCain laid out his domestic  programs   of  lowering taxes and holding the feet to the fire of the bureaucracy. He listed  the striking lack of experience of  Obama as opposed  to his obvious  experience. Finally, he  recounted  why he HATES  war and why any military  person knows  that to be free is not easy and just  be 'anti-war' , but, to actually confront evil for REAL  peace.  He ended  with  the crowd up  on it's feet cheering. No columns, no  fireworks, just some balloons and confetti of  the Red White and Blue. A simple warrior who loves his country like his family and above himself.

The Convention showed  to me TWO  genuine  people, one  a  true  outsider and one  who has  bucked even his  own party and has fought  for  his nation, here and abroad. The  Choice Is Yours Folks...

I Have MADE MINE..

McCAIN/PALIN    :-)

The problem is, people actually actively look for, and pay attention to, information that fits with or confirms what they already know. And we reject or skim over information that contradicts it.

So when we hear or read about something "great" that the candidate from the opposite side we're on did, we discount it because it doesn't fit with our idea of how horrible he is, but when we hear something "great" about our candidate, we embrace it.

The other problem is, Sarah Palin is getting all the media attention now. Before that, Obama was getting all the media attention. And the polls reflect the name people are hearing the most about...

Unrelated to the media attention, and breaking away from the impartial nature of my post thus far, the problem that I see with Republican thinking is that the "trickle down" economic effect - help the rich get richer and the money will trickle down to benefit everyone - no longer works. Sure, maybe 50 years ago it made sense to give big companies tax breaks because then they could employ more people in their factories or whatever... But now the tax break helps to pay for more sweat shop labor overseas. How does this help all the unemployed people in America?
