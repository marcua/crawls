One other thing, Diamond Nexus Labs only produce a diamond-simulant. Far inferior to a real diamond in brilliance and refraction. There is, however, a man-made diamond being produced in small quantities from Charles & Colvard called Moissanite. Many of the certifying agencies (GIA, AG, EGL) had to have special equipment made for them in order to distinguish between a real stone and the Moissanite. These stones are not inexpensive, yet they *are* far less expensive than their diamond counterparts.

Moissanite is not diamond, it's silicon carbide. Diamond is pure carbon.

After a bit of additional surfing, it appears that yes, the Diamond Nexus Labs product is not a true diamond either, but a mixture of cubic zirconium and a number of other chemicals (ZrO2, YO2, CaOx, MnOx, TiO2, and C). They claim that its optical properties are equivalent to an excellent mined diamond.
