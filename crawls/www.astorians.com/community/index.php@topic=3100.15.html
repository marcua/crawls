Hey, sorry Rachel for intruding on this thread (I don't know you), but I'm a U of C alum, and I wanted to offer my two cents.

Depending on your food tastes, Hyde Park (and Chicago in general) is the king of great cheap food.  You will find incredible Mexican food, BBQ to make you weep, and ridiculously good stuffed pizza.  For the first, go to Pilsen (in Hyde Park for a burrito, try the steak burrito at Maravillas on 53rd st.).  BBQ- the best is Leon's on Stony Island (the the 70's I think)?  You need a car to get there.  Don't let the bullet-proof glass scare you.  Hot links, chicken and ribs are the bomb.  In Hyde Park for BBQ, I would try Ribs 'n Bibs (BBQ cheeseburgers, rib tips, etc.).  Also good for burgers 'n beer is Jimmy's (where you'll more likely than not run into U of C grad students discussing Tolstoy over MGD.  For pizza, in my opinion the best is not Gino's East, but Giordano's.  Connie's is also good.  And Gino's East is passable  :-)

And don't forget all the amazing diners.  The Golden Nugget on the north side (on Clark, I think - its been a few years), is the best diner I've ever been to.  Buscuits and gravy, chicken-fried steak, and their skillets....  and 24 hours, of course.

If you're really in the mood for junk food, try the mozzerella sticks at Leona's (one dollar each, and probably a pund of cheese each.  amazing).

And if you miss Astoria too much, head for Greektown.

I've only been in NYC for five years, but in my experience so far, Chicago can hold it's own and certainly excels in many areas (Mexican and BBQ especially).
