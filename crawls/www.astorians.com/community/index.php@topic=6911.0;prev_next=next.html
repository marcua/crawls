Not only have owners made a significantly larger investment then the customer, but they also know their business better than the customer.  They have a much better idea of what makes good business sense in their establishment.

I've worked in restaurants long enough to know there are plenty of situations when its not desirable to seat a single at a four top.  Maybe the rush is coming, the bread and butter times when a restaurant needs all the tables and turnover it can get.  How long is this person going to stay, especially with a computer?  I do know that once one person sits at a table that big, other patrons that see it want to do the same.  I've also heard the "well, I did it the last time I came here" enough times for a lifetime.  Maybe the person took the four top with a sense of entitlement, without being polite enough to ask.  Being polite and asking gets one so far.  Maybe the person is a crappy tipper or always has some sort of complaint and they just aren't worth it.

If you are a single at a four top and the owner askes you to move, then its clearly not okay.  You should be asked, of course, to move in a respectful manner, but then be respectful in return and move.
