I've lived in Queensview for 6 years.   

Pros: 
Nice Apartments
Great Views
Cheap Parking
Quiet
Cheap (great for buyers)

Cons:
Cheap (bad for sellers)--and the board will not take the open market issue to vote again
No dogs allowed
Sometimes it feels like a retirement home--new obituary seemingly every week
No assigned parking spots
Across from the projects
Bad heating system:  cannot regulate heat in individual units
Maintenance staff that used to be very tardy/uncaring.  They seem to be getting better though
Security staff spend more time inside that patroling the grounds.  After a recent mugging, they are more vigilant though

I started in the coop as a young buyer 6 years ago, perhaps the youngest buyer they had in a long time.  The price and the views have been great perks, but there are negatives too.

There are some distinct differences between Queensview and North Queensview.

North Queensview is a market rate housing cooperative.  There, owners can sell their property for whatever value they deem appropriate.

Queensview is a limited equity cooperative.  Here, the price of the apartments is controlled by the board.  This type of system is used by developers who wish to create affordable housing but still allow people to own equity in their homes.  The issue of switching to open market went up for vote several years ago and was voted down, so the situation there is not likely to change any time soon.

Most, if not all co-ops take a "flip tax" when you sell your apartment.  This is not limited to the Queensviews.  This flip tax is used to renovate the apartment when the owner vacates it.

North Queensview and Queensview's apartments differ in some important ways, at least in the 3BR layout.  In North Queensview, the kitchen is walkthrough and open on one side to the dining room.  In Queensview, the dining room is right when you walk into the apartment.  Queensview buildings 13 and 14 3BR apartments share the same layout as North Queensview apartments.   

North Queensview apartments (at least that have not been renovated by their owners) do not have wood floors.  They have this vinyl-ish sort of tile.

Queensview basically does a complete renovation of apartments.
North Queensview only plasters, primes, re-grouts the tile and re-wires the apartment for circuit breakers if it still has the original fuse box and wiring.  Any other renovations - new kitchen cabinets, bathroom, floor, etc. are the responsibility of the owners.

The maintenance in North Queensview is lower than in Queensview.

Neither cooperative has submetering, so maintenance includes "unlimited" utilities - gas, heat, hot water, electric. In North Queensview any new appliances must be energy star compliant; I am not sure about Queensview.

I would say the apartments are pretty substantial in size.  A 3br apartment is about 1100 sq ft with closets.  Unless you horde tons of crap, they are a really good size.

Both cooperatives have minimum income requirements, but they are not that high.

The neighborhood is pretty quiet.  I would say Broadway is nicer now than it has ever been with a lot of new restaurants and lounges and stuff.

Apartments on the upper floors do provide pretty nice views.

Queensview has recently been giving away a seemingly unlimited amount of reserved handicapped spots in their lots.  Queensview basically sells an unlimited amount of parking stickers and works on a first come, first serve basis.  But since they have given out so many reserved spots, it has significantly decreased the overall number of available spots.

North Queensview has a parking lottery each year.  If you are picked in the lottery you are given an assigned spot that is yours for the entire year.  Residents that spend part of the year in Florida or warmer climates must surrender their parking spots when they leave.

Queensview does not allow dogs, but you can have 1 or 2 cats, I forget.
North Queensview allows one dog or two cats, I believe.
