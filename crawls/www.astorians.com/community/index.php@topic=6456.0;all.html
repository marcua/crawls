Dishwashers, laundry facilities, and elevators are pretty hard to come by in Astoria.  You might luck out and get all three, but you're more likely to get just one of those if at all.  Closet-space shouldn't be a problem, nor should well-lit, and much bigger and cheaper than anything you'd find on the UES. 

If you really must have laundry and elevator on the premises, the suggestions of Jackson Hts, Forest Hills, Elmhurst, and Rego Park are right on the money.  This is because there are more buildings over there such as coops, condos, and apartments which have these amenities. 

If you are deadset on Astoria, try Metropolis Realty.  Alex does show buildings with dishwashers, elevators, and laundry on the premises.  My friend has one, and it's the only building in Astoria that wasn't called "luxury residence"  I've ever seen with such things :)

 :-D Wow!  First, thanks for all the advice guys - really appreciate it.  However, who'd have thought that the word 'yuppies' would create such angst?!
To explain, I'm a child of the 70's, which means I came of age in the 80's when the word was first coined.  Originally, it was a marketing demographic to describe individuals 27-35 years of age, college-educated, working in a professional industry (i.e. marketing, finance, sales, medical, etc), who lived in the cities (New York, California, Miami, Atlanta, etc.), and whom(marketers believed) lived to enjoy the good life.  Hence, the show "Melrose Place" and its ilk. It is the shortened version of 'young, urban, professionals'. 'Hipsters' are a marketing demographic of the same age, education, profession, and geographical placement who shun the ostentatious behavior of 'yuppies' preferring a more laid-back enjoyment of life.  Of course, these labels only tell a part of the story of any person.
Perhaps, if I find the apt I want, I'll throw a housewarming and you can get to know the conservative yet wacky, detailed-oriented, love-to-entertain but craves silence-to-think (and all the other oxymoriphic sides of me and my husband).  After all, you may end up being our new neighbors!
Again, thx for the advice.
