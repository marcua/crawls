I also met up with the AFPJ group at Queensboro Plaza.  TRX was there, too.  Alexa and Frappe, we must have met!

The doves looked great, and Ian the Dove Dr. helped me fix my ailing, floppy dove.  The march was less colorful than ones I've been to in the past.  The energy was kind of muted. I was still happy to be there.

I was tired and hungry by the time we arrived at Dag Hamarskjold (sp?) Plaza.  I took a bunch of pictures, and will post them up on flickr tomorrow if I can get my computer up and running. 

We did have a beautiful day.  Thanks to AFPJ for organizing the meeting up at QB Plaza and for the events approaching the march (vigil yesterday and the talk on the Iraq War on Thursday).  Was nice to see some familiar faces out there today - just wish I had been able to recognize other astorians (except Bookminstrel and TRX, who I already knew)!

Protestors Take To The Streets To Mark Anniversary Of Iraq War
 
March 18, 2007

Protesters took to the streets Sunday to call for an end to the Iraq war as the conflict's fourth anniversary approaches.

The group United for Peace and Justice organized the afternoon rally in Midtown, which is passing by the offices of Senator Hillary Clinton and Charles Schumer.

Activists are calling for Congress to set a hard deadline for troop withdrawal.

A group called the "Peace Actions Coalition" is planning a protest near Wall Street Monday morning.

Organizers say the group will engage in peaceful civil disobedience to bring attention to the economic and human costs of war.
