I think it really depends on the dog.  My cocker will freak out if you put anything on him.  My labrador seems to revel in it.  Just like people, some dogs like it and some don't.  Common sense and knowing your animal is what counts here.

As for the clothing, saying that dogs have their own natual coat is like saying humans have their own natural skin.  We've both evolved and moved into environments which require the need for protection from the elements.  Many breeds, in particularly the smaller breeds, have been bred by humans so as not to be able to stand the cold as their ancestors could.  Even bigger dogs are alot more comfortable with a sweater on during the frigid weather, or (especially) a slicker when those cold winter rains/sleet are coming down.  Again, its just common sense.  And if your dog needs a sweater, shirt or slicker and as long you make sure it will not chaff the dog with shoddy materials or ill-sized clothing, there's nothing wrong with making it stylish.
