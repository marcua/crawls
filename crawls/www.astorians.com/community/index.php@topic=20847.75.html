Quote from: TJT on May 09, 2012, 10:40:20 AM

Yesterday was the last time I will ever set foot inside this sinking ship.  What a mess this place is!  The dry goods are only one item deep on the shelves.  Fewer and fewer brand, size, or flavor choices.  The meat selection continues to be pathetic and even scary looking.  Other display areas are either empty, shut down, or turned off (as in refrigerator cases).  Every single freezer is filled with frost.  Everything inside is covered in ice crystals.  The dairy area is nothing but a sad display of mostly expired items that don't feel cool to the touch.  In short, this place is on it's last leg.    He's a disgrace and has no interest in providing a good service to the local community.  I hope it happens fast so that maybe someone will buy it out and do this right.  We need a quality market in that exact location.

I usually take most reviews from the posters as hyperbole tinted with a grain of web forum-mediated snobbiness, but I have to say that this is dead accurate.
I rarely get over to that part of the neighborhood, but today my Sunday ramblings led me to long trip that ended its final leg walking down Crescent from Ditmars to Broadway. When I hit Crescent and Newtown, I saw a store that I hadn't been to before and was surprised to find out that it was the infamous, Newtown Farmers Market that I've read so much about on this board. "Surely, it can't really be as bad as everyone makes it out to be on Astorians", I thought to myself, "It's probably no Trader Joe's but it can't be that bad". Upon entering the store and walking down the first aisle, I knew that I was definitely mistaken. The shelves were half empty. The product was, in several cases, place one item deep and spread out to take up as shelf space as possible, so as to not look empty. The red meat was a strange grayish color with a little pink here or there. The fresh produce was practically non-existent, especially for a "farmer's market" to consigned to a table next to a stainless sink of questionable cleanliness with brown runny heads of iceberg lettuce dripping into it. The only thing that seems to keep them going is their location and the few Mexican staples that they to seem stock and sell on a regular basis. Everything else seemed to be expired, frost covered or potentially dangerous. Gray meat?
On a positive note I made my way to Berry Fresh Farm 2.0 a little later. What a great transformation this store is under going,
