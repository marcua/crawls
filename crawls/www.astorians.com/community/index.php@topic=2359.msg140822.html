A man on the subway once asked everyone within a two-foot radius for a tissue to give me when I had a contact lens malfunction and my right eye streamed like a leaky faucet. I was too embarrassed to ask around myself, so I was extremely grateful to him. The fact that he noticed I was suffering was pretty amazing, since most folks on the subway seem to go out of their way to not notice the people around them (like I do). I don't think he did it because I'm a woman; I think he was just a plain ole nice guy. It seems a shame to be suspicious of other people's random acts of selflessness.

Same kind of deal on the airplane, on my way back to NY from CA. As soon as that seatbelt sign is turned off, everyone jumps and grabs their crap out of the overhead bins. My bag was small and not heavy, easily accessible and I was in no one's way to get it. However, the gentleman in my row asked if he could get it for me. Could be because I'm 5'4" and he was 6" plus and he figured it was just easier for him ... could be because I'm a woman, or he figured out I was depressed being back in NY, I don't know, but it warmed my heart a bit. It was just a nice thing for him to do ... I thought.

I dig chivalry. Makes me feel a little special when I'm so used to feeling very much the opposite. A door opened for me or a seat given up to me by a man because I'm a woman actually kind of makes my day. Maybe I need to lay off the Austen.

All that said, I don't think (and I believe I have stated this before) anyone should feel obligated to relinquish a seat on the subway (or open a door, or let a woman go into the building first, etc). Sure, it's nice, but the first-come-first-served adage applies to the train. In my opinion, humble though it may be.
