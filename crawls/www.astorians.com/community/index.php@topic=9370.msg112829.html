I don't know if you ever got an answer to this but, from my experience, the answer might be "Yes."  I used to get strep 5+ times a year as a kid.  The doc told me that, if I got it one more time, he'd recommend removing my tonsils.  Wouldn't you know, I didn't get it again for 3 years or so.  I've had it a few times since then very sporadically and sometimes years apart; once so bad that my throat turned white and closed up so painfully that the Doc actually prescribed Vicodin and another time as a wonderful side effect to having mono (super awesome...).

Still, I'm thinking I'll have to have my tonsils removed before too long.  I've noticed, as has any Dr. that has taken a gander at my throat, that my tonsils have gotten quite large and a little bit misshapen and bulgy these days.  They haven't said I should have them removed, but I'd only imagine one serious bout of strep and I'd be done for. 

Hopefully, it's when I have insurance...
