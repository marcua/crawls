I don’t know why you posted this twice Steve Crawford, but I am responding on both threads so in case you are only checking one.

I can’t believe anyone can sincerely state that Simotas would have more support from the gay community than Jeremiah Frei-Pearson! Few if any Astorians who are politically active in the marriage equality movement or who were involved in the local movement to pressure George Onorato have heard of Simotas. Simotas may have secured approval from prominent gay politicians but I have yet to hear any praise from any actual Astorians involved in LGBT issues.

All we know about Simotas now is that she has loyally served Mike Gianaris and the Queens Democratic party, but we know little to nothing about her community involvement and have yet to see if she has gone to the streets to stand up for the rights of any group, let alone the LGBT community.

Frei-Pearson on the other hand has been an inspirational and dependable ally to the LGBT community and the marriage equality movement. It is absurd to accuse him of not being involved. I can only assume that the person posting that is ignorant of Astoria LGBT issues or is intentionally lying.

Frei-Pearson has put in more time than most LGBT people on marriage equality! It is an issue near and dear to both he and his wife, both of whom could be seen regularly last summer handing out fliers on street corners, attending rallies, and at many meetings. Let me emphasize the point that Frei-Pearson has not just stood up in front of crowds of thousands arguing for marriage equality. He is not one of those “gay friendly” politicians who just shows up at the right PR moments. Frei-Pearson has done the truly thankless, gritty, at times personally risky task, time and time again of standing on the street asking strangers to support marriage equality and to become more active in their government. If there were more straight people like Frei-Pearson, there would no longer be “gay issues” because LGBT people would have full equality and complete social acceptance.

Both Simotas and Frei- Pearson are pro marriage equality and this is a blessing for the LGBT community so now we need to see where they stand on all the OTHER issues. Challenge Frei-Pearson on a real issue in play but for God’s sake, don’t question his incredible service to the LGBT community. After all the time and work he has put in standing along side us, after all the times he has put himself out there to publically defend us, it is offensive to say that he isn’t involved in “any real community issues”… unless of course, you don’t think protecting the relationships and families of a growing number of Astorians with marriage equality is a “real” community issue. I hope the rumors of Frei-Pearson running are true. We don’t need more politicians whose records show consistent service to a party, we need more politicians whose records show them consistently standing for what is right.
