I am sorry for not responding sooner. I haven't been feeling well, and it
has been difficult for me to get on the computer.

The reason this vigil needs to be held on this particular night is because
there will be little vigils held all over North America at the same time
everywhere. 8:00 P.M. on the East Coast; 5:00 P.M. on the West coast;
etc. The organizers are hoping to get some press from the vigil so that the
public will come to understand that breed banning is NOT the solution to
vicious dogs. That to enforce laws requiring people to PTS (Put To Sleep)
loving, healthy, gentle dogs is not a solution to anything. They are trying to
get the public to understand that we need to PUNISH THE DEED NOT THE
BREED. This year Pit Bulls, next year Toy Poodles. When will it stop. We
just need to enforce the laws already on the books so that the government
cannot get any closer to our bedrooms!

There are no inane vicious dogs. There are bad owners. There are bad
(READ: Puppy Mills) breeders.

This Canadian law is also now in the Assembly of the State of California.
AND it looks like it is going to pass.

When will it stop. After dogs—what's next?

The real issue here is not necessarily dogs—although I feel very strongly
about banning ANY breed just for the sake of destroying something
someone has had a problem with.

Personally, I am sick of the government, whether local, state or federal,
legislating MY lifestyle.

With or without a dog, please come to Astoria Park by 8:00 P.M. Sunday
night and light a candle so that we may all recognize each other.

The politics of dogs is just the beginning of the beginning of the end  of
being allowed to own them.

Landlords got their way by being able to ban pets—but there is a loophole
in the law I will be glad to share with anyone who has a need to know—
now the police are expected to enforce housing laws!

BTW, I will probably NOT be able to attend Sunday night due to health
issues. If anyone would like to borrow one of my 2 dogs, just let me know.
They will be more than happy to accompany you to the park. I have a
Bichon and an Affenpinscher.
