Title: Astoria Pool Sentinels
Post by: 28Grand on October 29, 2014, 06:00:18 AM
In my travels around the web this week looking for all things Astoria, I found this article from the Republican Herald of Pottsville Pennsylvania:
http://republicanherald.com/news/relatives-of-designer-visit-mother-s-memorial-statue-1.1778304


The article describes a visit to a sculpture, Mother’s memorial in Ashville PA by the relatives of the designer Emil Stiebern, who was an Greenwich Village based artist known for his works in public buildings and spaces in New York and elsewhere.


The interesting part of this is that the article mentions that he designed a work that stood in ‘Prospect Park’ in Astoria. This intrigued me (aside from the fact that Prospect Park is in Brooklyn). I dug around some more and it turns out that he designed two large stainless steel Sentinel-like sculptures that stood on the top deck and overlooked Astoria Pool.


Here is a picture:
http://collections.mcny.org/Collection/Astoria-Pool.-2F3XC5UJWITZ.html 


For some reason, I’m a bit obsessed now. Does anyone know more about these sculptures and what happened to them?
