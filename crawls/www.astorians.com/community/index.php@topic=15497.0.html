Your party may require a Special Event permit:

From the Parks Dept. Events Frequently Asked Questions

"For which activities are special events permits needed?
Special events permits are required for events/activities where twenty or more people will be present. School picnics, birthday parties, and field days all require a special events permit."

This page describes how to apply.

Also, I don't think Astoria Park has any areas that allow a grill, so cooking is right out. (I guess you could tuck meat under the hood of your car on the exhaust manifold or something.)

Other pages around http://www.nycgovparks.org/ give the rules.

The park on Roosevelt Island has grills.   

Directions
# Walk/Car:You have access via Queens along 36th Avenue. Just head towards the water and you can cross the Roosevelt Island Bridge.
# Subway:Take the F train to the Roosevelt Island stop. There is no other choice.
# Tram:If going under the river via the subway isn't your thing, try the tram. Take Tramway Car at 59th Street & Second Avenue Station.(Travel time is 4-5 minutes.)
# Bus: You'll only have access in Queens. From the Queensboro Plaza overpass, take the stairway down to the north side corner of Crescent and Bridge Plaza North. Take the Q102 to Roosevelt Island.
