Just had a bad experience there a couple of days ago involving one of my supermarket pet peeves, but it turned out kinda funny:

There were two regular lanes open and one so-called "10 limit" lane. The store was busy and everyone in the express lane had their carts full, way more than the "limit", but the cashiers never say anything, so when you have 2 or 3 things ya have to wait half an hour.

This really, really irritates me.

So then they announce the "office" lane is open for 10 items or under. Great - I dash over and am third in line. Then I notice the woman in front of me has her mid-sized wheelie cart full of all kinds of frozen, canned, veggies, etc and starts to put them on the counter. I call her on it and she asks the cashier can she come through the line. The cashier only sees the 2 items in her hand and says yes. I point out the cart-full of stuff out to the cashier who looks over the counter and says "Oh I didn't know you had so much". The customer starts griping and I pointed out the announcement said 10 or less and asked the cashier, who I'm pretty sure MADE the announcement, "Isn't that what you said". She just stared at me. So I just stepped in front of the customer and told the still-staring cashier "It's okay, ring me up." I see the other 5 or so people in line, each with a couple of things, bristling at the sight of the cart. My transaction is done and I'm rounding the corner for the back door when the customer starts "you have a lotta nerve, blah-blah-blah" at me and - this is so beautiful - almost in unision the people in the line practically scream "10 OR LESS!!" I busted out laughing, it was soooooo hilarious, I laughed all the way home.   Shoppers revolt!

But yes, that store sucks big time.

I can't stand shopping at Key Food for that exact reason. Aside from people cheating the express lane (which is just pathetic), the cashiers are maddeningly slow. Well, and their prices are noticeably higher than Trade Fair's.

I had a ridiculous cashier experience at the local Eckerd recently also. I get up to the counter and the cashier is a young guy. I had like three items. As he goes to scan them, he very deliberately wipes his finger across his nose and he was sniffling. So that was disgusting. Then he boredly scans them and I heard one of the items, a toothbrush, beep twice. I suspected this meant it got scanned twice, but as he acted like it was normal I didn't say anything. The price came to higher than expected, so I paid and looked at the receipt when he handed it to me. Lo and behold, two toothbrushes were on the receipt.

So I went back in line because he was ringing up some other people, and I got up to him again and he just looked at me. I said "The toothbrush scanned twice." I had to explain this a few times and said I needed a refund. He went to get a refund slip and asked if I needed a new receipt. I said "No, I just want the money." He then said "I'll need to get a manager if you need a new receipt." And I said, "No.. I just want the money."

Then he needed a pen to fill out the slip, but he was not interested in using the one on the counter by the credit card scanner. He asked the cashier behind him for a pen. She seemed tired of him and pointed out the one on the counter. He said "I'm not using that." So she gave him a pen and he used it. Then he passed the pen and paper to me to fill out my part. I was disgusted by his nose wiping and sniffling so I used the other pen.

So then he gave me my money back. Then he went to put the toothbrush under the counter. I said, "I still want the toothbrush." He was very confused by this. I once again explained that I needed the refund because the one toothbrush rang up as two. So he gave it to me and I left. I've never been so annoyed by a cashier.
