Title: The Steinway Shopping Experience
Post by: 28Grand on February 14, 2008, 06:22:37 PM
There’s been several threads on Astorians discussing the state of shopping in the neighborhood, especially on Steinway.

At times it seems to me that the discussion seems to overly focus on what Astoria doesn’t have instead of what it does. I’d like to point out that Astoria has four, prime, diverse retail strips: Ditmars, Grand, Broadway and Steinway. As well as big box retailers on Northern Boulevard and smaller shopping areas scattered throughout the area.

How many other neighborhoods in this city can lay a similar claim? How many of people come from places with no “downtowns” or one’s that are empty?

As an FYI, at the end of this post you’ll find a census of the stores on Steinway prime: the blocks between Broadway and 30th Avenue, in order from north to south (west side of the street in blue and the east side in red).

Steinway between 30th and 28th Avenues was, in my opinion, considered to be of lesser importance than the prime blocks, though you can find some varied businesses, including restaurants, clothes shops, nail salons etc., and a few of Astoria’s long-time enterprises including Schtaz’s paint, She Goes to Your Head hair salon, Joanne’s Bakery, and Gyro Uno.

Though this may be debatable to some, Steinway between 28th Avenue and Astoria Boulevard has actually improved with the rise of Arabic-owned businesses, though it looks like this stretch is again in transition. I counted approximately 8-9 empty storefronts, which I find troubling.

I deliberately left out Steinway between Broadway and 34th Avenue because it was never prime and always a bit of a hodgepodge (and a mess).


Though I think it’s important to support local shops, I make no moral judgments as to how and where people shop. And I’m not advocating  that one must shop locally to be a “good“ Astorian. I’m only hoping people enjoy what is on offer and perhaps think twice before making a complaint and maybe give Steinway merchants a chance at capturing some of your disposable income.
