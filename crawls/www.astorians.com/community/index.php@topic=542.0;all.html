Hello,

I really love sourdough bread - you know the fresh kind you can get at some bakries in San Francisco, Germany, Italy, France etc. But I've been reading recently that this form of bread has suffered greatly because of the commercialization of breads. I was suprised to find out that 75% of all bread consumed in the United States is produced by three baking companies, it's a multi-billion dollar industry. Anyway - these companies need to have a consistant product so they have "manufactured" yeast to use in making bread. This is the yeast you and I eat all the time - it has no flavor whatsoever and is completely engineered.

So I've decided I'm going to start my own sourdough culture. Is there anyone else that's interested in starting a sourdough culture and making bread with it? I'm very willing to share some of my San Francisco sourdough culture with anyone who would be interested. Or if you have your own culture I'd love to have some.

My first 5 loaves turned out pretty bad :(

My problem seems to be how to know when to use the culture. You have to store it in the fridge (unless you want to feed it daily which costs $$) and then when you are ready to use it you take it out, feed it, and then wait for it to get active again. Then when it is at the "peak" of its cycle you use part of it in the bread, then put the rest back in the fridge.

If you don't catch the culture when it's really active, then the bread doesn't rise. But my last loaf was pretty good. The taste is there, but I still need to work on the texture and getting the maximum rise out of the natural yeast.

I'm happy to share some of my culture. When would you like it?

When it's at room temp you have to feed it (it's alive muhahahaha).

You add 1 cup flour (unbleached white is GOOD) and add water (filtered is GOOD) and mix/shake.  Let it sit at room temp.

Then 24 hours later, feed again.  1 cup flour, some water.  Let it sit (still at room temp).

THEN put in fridge.

Shake up once a week or so.
