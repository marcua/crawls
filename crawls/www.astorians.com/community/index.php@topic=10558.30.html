I pretty much agree with ladydi. We shop at the TJ's in Brooklyn and also at the one at 6th ave. Sometimes we shop at the Fairway in Red Hook. I like Fairway much better than Key Food and Best Market and Berry Fresh (a very depressing store, but better than it used to be), but it's a bit of overkill. The store itself is too big and redundant with the different aisles stocked with the same items-a bit of a hard sell- and I get claustrophobic in there after a while. The meat and fish selection is larger and more varied than at TJ's. The prices are higher than TJ's. Fruits and veggies are more expensive than TJ's and California Farmer's Market and not necessarily better quality. Of course the staff at TJ's seems happier than anywhere. It's just a more pleasant shopping experience at TJ's and I find that I don't over spend like I would at Fairway because TJ's brand items are cheaper and better than most of the national brands at Fairway and other supermarkets. I'd still be thrilled with a Fairway in Astoria. They'd need an open, empty space for the store and parking lot, so the area around 20th ave seems like the best choice. I hate driving around Northern Blvd.


Now if a Stew Leonard's were to open in Astoria, I'd be chanting "Why Leave Astoria" all day. That's for another thread.
