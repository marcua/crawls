Oooh, ladybugs are vicious and swift when it comes to eating those other bugs.  It's a good thing.

Composting is good because it helps eliminate the waste you might usually put in the trash that then goes in the landfill.  The worms eat the food scraps that you feed them, process it through their bodies, and the resulting compost can be used in houseplants and other plants. Title: Re: Composting in Astoria?
Post by: belle on September 08, 2007, 12:02:46 PM
I'm in the CSA so they basically get all my veggie scraps and coffee grinds (tea bags are okay too). Dead house plants/ leaves are also good, though I was told no store bought flowers (b/c you don't know what is sprayed on them). No citrus, no dairy (but egg shells are okay), no oil (so nothing if it's been cooked in oil), no meat/ fish or bones, and I think no processed sugar. I also give them most of my junk mail-- take off stamps, plastic, staples and gummy seal and no glossy paper (no magazine paper) but newspaper is great and keeps thing dry if it's getting too wet (though they def like a moist environment).

I have a lot of tomato scraps but try not to give them too much b/c I don't want the soil to get too acidic (same if you drink a lot of coffee). But Two Coves will take your citrus and extra acid scraps b/c they have a larger area for matter. Cores and rinds they really love-- avocado peels and pits, apple cores, melon rinds and pits and corn husks (dried out for 24-48 hours)/cores, but these items take longer for them to eat through.

I cut everything up to about 1-2 inch pieces b/c it helps them eat it faster. They def don't eat all the food I give them every night, but it doesn't stink at all, just sort of smells faintly of soil near the bin. I sometimes skip a night or two just to let them some time to eat through if I've given them a bunch. (Or like when I went on vaca for 10 days I just put 3 big handfuls and mixed it around and they were fine). If I have a lot of scraps one night I stick it in a bag in the freezer to use when I have nothing or when I go on vaca.

Also, if you find an overpopulation of mites (not the nasty ones just very minute reddish ones) avocado and larger fruit pits are a great way to get rid of them-- just stick it in for 24 hours or so and remove it if you see little red guys crawling everywhere. It's a living environment so you'll always have more than just worms in there, but you don't want something to get out of control. I have had no problem with fruit flies, but the person who taught me about worm bins said she puts everything in the freezer for 24 hours (esp banana peels) to kill anything from the outside that is living in her peels and then feeds it to her worms.

Here's a really good site for more info:  http://www.nyccompost.org/how/wormbin.html
