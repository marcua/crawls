I agree that it's absolutely unethical, but state and local Human Rights Laws do not necessarily cover two unit owner-occupied dwellings.  In other words, "The Law does not extend to residents of two-family houses if the owner or a member of the owner’s family resides in one of the housing accommodations and the available housing accommodation was not advertised.* In addition, you are not covered by the Law if you rent a room or rooms in non-government assisted housing where the owner resides."

*The non-advertised restriction to the exception is not covered by the NY State Division of Human Rights, only the NYC Commission on Human Rights (where I worked in 1995 and 1996).
