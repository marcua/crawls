Funny, I was just about to post a suggestion for a local bike club, as everybody I know is either out east or up north or NJ.

Have you tried Van Cortlandt Park? The Croton Aqueduct trail is like 40 miles long. Take the 1 all the way to the end.

You could take the LIRR to some nice places out east, say the Port Jeff line to Kings Park, from where you could bike or take a cab to Sunken Meadow State Park. Same train line takes you to Northport for a nice charming village experience, or Centerport for the Vanderbilt Mansion, Cold Spring Harbor has a hiking trail (can also be reached by Syosset stop I believe.)

You could also take Metro North to places like Sprain Ridge (1/2 hr from NYC) or Blue Mountain-these are the only ones I know offhand.

There is a book called "60 Hikes Within 60 Miles: New York City. I have a copy. Most of the trails give directions on how to get there via mass transit (some require taxis for the last bit).

Also, check out Meetup.com... there are several hiking groups on there (and a few on yahoo) that organize regular trips. If you don't have a car, you can always carpool with someone that does.

The NYNJ trail conference is a great organization. They have maps and all sorts of goodies for anyone interested in hiking in the area.

I belong to two hiking clubs.  Shorewalkers (http://www.shorewalkers.org) runs many hikes in NYC and the tri-state area, and sponsors the annual Great Saunter around Manhattan.  The Outdoors Club (http://www.outdoorsclub.info) runs more hikes and most of them are outside the city, though easily accessible by public transportation.  I've gone on hikes with them in Beacon, Garrison, Peekskill, Croton, Tarrytown, Scarsdale, and Staten Island.
