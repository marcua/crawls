I don't doubt the motivation behind the poster defending Igloo, nor do I think if any of us were store owners that we would do anything different.

Picture this:

A lone diner goes into a local restaurant to grab a bite to eat. Spotting an empty booth up front, she asks the waitress if it would be alright to take it even though she knows it seats 4 people. Though it’s not particularly busy at the time, the waitress declines and politely suggests a smaller table given that it’s the evening, primetime, when many people eat their dinner and the booth may be needed to accommodate a larger party.

Undeterred, the lone diner scans the restaurant and spies a large, empty 4-person table in the back and asks if she could have that one. The waitress, confronted by the customer’s insistence, weighs the options: the day, the time, number of tables available, etc. and against her better judgement decides to give in to avoid a scene.

The diner, still undeterred and now sensing the waitress’s hesitation, suspects that it is still not alright to occupy such a large table, asks the waitress not once, but three more times until she receives the answer she wants. Feeling a little less guilty, she takes her seat and spreads out…

Do I need to go on?

I took liberties filling in the blanks and maybe I missed something but to me the question was asked and answered – and the reply was ignored.

Can I suggest something:
 
1. When dining out, it’s usually never OK for one person to sit at a 4-person table, especially during busy hours.

2. In my experience, if you have to ask for something three times the answer is usually no -- but you knew this already, otherwise you wouldn’t have asked three times in the first place.

3. People are rude. Get used to it. When did compensation become compulsory for being shabby treatment? Does poor service at a department store entitle someone to walk out with the merchandise as compensation for their feelings being hurt?

4. Bad service -- in my book it means pack it in, pay up, move on.

5. Before you ask for sympathy make sure you’ve examined your own behavior in any given situation.
