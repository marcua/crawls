Post by: rg on September 30, 2010, 10:16:23 AM
Think about it as a place you are BUYING into in addition to it being a place to live.  There is equity involved.

1) There is no board approval - that means anyone and everyone can live there.  You are buying shares in a place where no concern is given to who is moving buying, moving in, etc. 
2) They immediately allow subletting.  What percentage of apartments are actually owner occupied?  You're buying an apartment in a co-op that's essentially filled with "investment" properties, not into a community of cooperator owners.

Take a close look (or have an accountant) take a close look at the financials of the development.  How large is the reserve fund?  Have there been any assessments?  For what?  What kinds of major renovations need to be done that have not been done yet?

I've heard mixed about it myself.  Personally, I wouldn't buy at a place like that because it seems more about quick turnaround and apartment flipping rather than a true cooperative.  And I think things like no board approval just invites too much risk into your investment.
