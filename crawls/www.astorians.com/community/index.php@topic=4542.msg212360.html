Did you watch the royal wedding?

ZZZzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz.... Are they on their honeymoon yet?  I was up all night.  I started watching at 2 AM.  I saluted their marriage with a cup of coffee. 

I wore a sapphire and diamond ring, matching earrings, Kirks Folly Queen for a Day ring, Kirks Folly tiara (double row of crystal flowers), long flowing Eileen West nightgown, and Sarah Jessica Parker Lovely perfume.  When I realized my husband had woken up for the morning I went to watch it on the big screen in the bedroom.  I got into bed dressed like that and my husband didn't say anything  :-o

Back when Diana got married my mother didn't wake me up for the telecast.  I was so mad that I missed it!  I made sure I wasn't going to miss this one.  It doesn't matter what I think about the concept of "royalty", this was a world wide phenomena.  I feel especially bad for the people in our country who lost their homes in the tornadoes.  Days ago they were planning to watch to this wedding, too.  Then this disaster happens and whenever they hear people talk about this royal couple all their awful memories will come back.

I would have watched the wedding without caring if William and Kate had not known each other for so long.  So many celebs marry people they don't know well or for long and the media makes such a hoopla and all the time we know it it isn't going to last.  This couple seems so real.  And likeable. 

It was funny when people started entering the Abbey, and we saw... folding chairs?  Hmmm, just because you're invited doesn't mean....

I loved the trees in the Abbey.  It reminded me of Christmas at Rockefeller Center when you look down the lines of trumpeting angels and they frame the tree.  The trees in the abbey framed the setting and added to the fairy tale feeling of the wedding.  It also reminded me of an episode of Sabrina the Teenaged Witch when Sabrina is told to bring in a tree for somebody's wedding and it was funny because it seemed so absurd.

I loved the choice of dress.  It was pretty.  And no "crazy train".  Maybe Kate will usher in an era of general good taste.

The best thing about watching the wedding was watching the way William looks at Catherine.  That's what we all really want in our lives.

There's more I can say in regard to the new Duchess, but it's on a different tangent.
