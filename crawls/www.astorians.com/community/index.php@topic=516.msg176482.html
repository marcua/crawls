Hello All

     I just want to introduce myself. Most people in the neighborhood know me as Fane! Those that do are more than likely in thier 40's or 50's. Anyone who has hung out in astoria park in the last decade would recognize us. Us being me and the couple of old friends that i still get together with. We usually hang out in the area that we refer to as the triangle. This is becuase the foot paths roughly form a triangle. Its above the meadows towards 19th street near the hellgate train trestle. We usually listen to music out of a boom box and its usually old obscure music from the sixtees and seventees. Most responses are posative however rarely there might be a complaint from people who think that they are in a library. We are old school astoria. A throwback to the way cats hung out in the sevetees when music was shared, experienced together almost like a religious experience. Back then there would be people hanging out all along what was once called the strip along shore road listening to music out of boom boxes. we carry on that tradition. It has taken me a long time to use this medium becuase admittedly i am resistant to this overwhelming assault of technology which in our opinion is diluting humanity itself. Where people once socialized face to face in person now is through electronic gadgets. Admitedly i am finding myself increasingly isolated becuase of my reluctance to accept the changes taking place. But ther are a few of us old timers left in Astoria. In fact i am working on a series of graphic novels about the neighborhood and the guys i hang out with. The stories include most Astortia landmarks and life today from the perspective of us cats in our fourtees and fiftees. Anyhow i thank all who acknowledge this and i am glad to be a part of something that i hope others can relate to.


Peace to all


Fane
