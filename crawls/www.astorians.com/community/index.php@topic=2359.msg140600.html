I once pulled an all-nighter at work, and didn't manage to leave to head home until around 4pm the following day.  This was back when there was no such thing as "office casual", so I was in a suit with a briefcase.  I caught the early rush home, so the car was moderately full - although I got a seat.  Somewhere after QBP, I simply fell sound asleep from sheer fatigue.  When the train pulled into Ditmars, a woman gently shook me awake, and I found myself with my head resting on her shoulder - without another soul in the car.  When I had fallen asleep, I apparently leaned against her.  Instead of waking me or jumping up, she let me rest there, and didn't even move when other seats opened up.

Very different scenario to yours, mcdirk, and I don't recommend falling asleep on the subway, of course, but I'll never forget that woman's kindness.
