Not tipping is not a form of theft.  If you choose not to tip then that is your business.

Giving the tip to the busboy with the intention of him or her keeping the tip is not theft either.  What it is is an active promotion of theft.    All tips have to go through the waiter.  The waiter is responsible to the house for money.  The waiter is responsible to the other staff to tip them out as well.  The busboy is required to give any tips which they get independently and give them to the waiter.  If they do as the patron intends, and keeps the tips for themselves, then it is a form of theft, plain and simple.  If they are dishonest and smart, they will keep part of the tip and give the rest over to the waiter.

The service you get is part of a package.  The waiters and bussers work as a team.  There are times my waiter service must have sucked because my busser was dragging a$$ and I was rushing around trying to pick up their slack.  As a waiter, I did not have the right to short my busboys on the amount I tipped them, even if I didn't think they deserved it.  If I did, it would be considered stealing and it was a fireable offense.  A busboy withholding tips is a fireable offense as well.

Ladydi, I don't cafe to argue either, but your pop was in the back of the house and wasn't in the front.  It shows an extremely poor grasp on the mechanics of a restaurant.  As a patron, you have no idea of the real reason behind the service given to you and to promote theft like that based on un-informed presumption is disheartening.

Yes I would be an uninformed patron as well.  That's why your comment about condescending all-knowishness baffled me.  I have no idea about the service in the restaurants I goto.  I do not want to give off that impression.  But I can be extremely comfortable that giving the tip directly to the busboy is not acceptable in almost any restautrant you goto.

I had to deal with it as a waiter and it was a problem in certain places, so I can buy into the defenseive comment, for it was my paycheck that was getting screwed with.  In some places waiters are required to tip out our bus and bar based on gross sales.  So now if someone gives the tip to the bus it not only costs the waiter in terms of gross tips, but now the waiter has to additionally tip our the bus from their own pocket the tip which the bus got from the patron. 

Another scenario is when the tips are pooled by the waiters.  So all of my tips go into a jar and then are split equally amoung the waiters, bus and bar.  So if a busboy gets privately tipped, then that money doesn't go into the tip pool.  That means every worker in the front of the house is getting stolen from.

Also, just one other thing to think about is that a level of trust has to develop between the bus and waiters.  As a waiter, I have to be confident that when the bus does take the money off of the table, I'm going to get every dime of it and not worry about he or she peeling off a fiver or something like that.  If a busboy gets the tip instead of me, naturally they have to hide the fact that they were privately tipped.  Once I start suspecting something like that, now I have to stay on top of them, which hurts my service then.
