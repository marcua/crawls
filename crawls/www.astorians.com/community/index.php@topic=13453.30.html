That is not a delay of 2-3 hours of waiting for their website to update. That's called lying.

How else did the supervisor have one to send out the next day when they told me two minutes beforehand they they didn't have any available for a full week until I started making scathing and angry comments at the poor woman? It's not like she said "Oh, one just became available in our warehouse" she just said "We will ship one out tomorrow" in a dead voice.

So they were able to change the date of their delivery but not cancel it? Does _that_ make sense to you?

Drakr, I really don't think there was "shady business practice there". Poor execution or mismanagement- maybe, which granted is still not a good thing. It sounds to me like there was an inventory mix-up, which can very easily occur amidst hectic holiday ordering. I doubt a large company like B&N has any reason to try and deceive you.
From my experience in customer service (not at B&N specifically, bit other places), the front line of customer service people have their hands bound by policies set in place, which usually exist for a reason, or just plain lack of authorization to do things.
My best advice to you for any situation like this, no matter what company you are dealing with, is to immediately elevate your issue up the supervisor chain. Supervisors generally have more power to do certain things, such as expedite shipping, discounts, etc. that floor level customer service people don't.

Like I said, I don't speak for how your customer rep at B&N handled this situation, and I am sorry you had that experience, but I hope you come back and shop with us again.
