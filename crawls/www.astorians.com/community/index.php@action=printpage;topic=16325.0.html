EXHIBIT OPENING:  Sunday, October 18, 2009 at 2:00pm
“The Photography of Max Drucker”
Max Drucker was a founding member of the New York Photo League, later the Film and Photo League.  Its goal was to illuminate the social issues of the Depression by exhibiting documentary work by members and prominent photographers.  Documenting the Depression, they captured the labor rallies, marches, demonstrations, breadlines, and Hoovervilles during the 1930's.


SYMPOSIUM & EXHIBIT OPENING:  Saturday, October 24, 2009 at 1:00pm
“The Ethnic Social Clubs of Astoria:  Their History and Role in Community Life”
Through a symposium hosted by the Greater Astoria Historical Society, accompanied by an exhibit, the contemporary phenomenon of Astoria's ethnic social organizations will be explored in depth.  As the Astoria of today typifies an ever-expanding melting pot, the scope of these organizations has come to reflect the demographic changes of that community.


HALLOWEEN WALKING TOUR:  Saturday, October 31, 2009 at 3:00pm
“Haunted Waters Walking Tour”
Join us for a tour of murder, mayhem and mystery along the Long Island City waterfront.  Our walk will take place along the treacherous East River as tales are told of those lost long ago amoung the churning whirlpools and electric eels.
Meet in Astoria Park beneath the Hell Gate Bridge.
$10  ($8 for GAHS members and anyone in costume)
