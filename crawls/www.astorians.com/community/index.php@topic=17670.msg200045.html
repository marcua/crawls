Here is Jeremiah's statement on the decision to end the race:

Quote
Together, we collected a massive number of signatures and raised more money than all but one non-incumbent Assembly candidate in Queens. More importantly, we helped frame the terms of this debate. We lead the fight for marriage equality, for good government, better schools, more hospitals, a sane energy policy, and a government that doesn't ignore the 80,000 children in Queens who live in poverty. I am very proud of our what we accomplished and of the values we fought for.
 
Unfortunately, the dynamics of the race have changed in the past week. After this past week's developments, we still have path to victory - but that path would require us to run a negative campaign against a fellow Democrat.  This is a path that I am unwilling to take.
 
As a result, after many discussions and lots of prayer, Karla and I have decided not to go forward with this campaign. This was a very tough decision - and it was made even more difficult by the amazing outpouring of support we received.
 
Although this political campaign is ending, the causes for which we fight endure. I am looking forward to working with Marriage Equality New York to help all New Yorkers get the right to marry; I am looking forward to continuing to fight to ensure that more children get the opportunities they need; so that our community's schools improve, so that we get more hospital beds, so that immigrants are welcome throughout our country, and so that we can finally fix our energy policy. We have lots of work to do and I hope you will continue to work alongside me.

Of course I'm saddened by this decision. But even so, I understand and support Jeremiah's reasons for ending the campaign. His only remaining path to victory required a negative campaign against a Democrat with a considerable following in the District. He's put our community and our shared values ahead of this single election and I can assure all of you that he isn't going anywhere. We will continue to have his leadership, his support, and his advocacy on all the important issues facing our communities.

I strongly believe that competitive primaries are good for our Party and good for the people. A robust contest allows the best candidates to emerge, vetted out in the open by the people. I believe that we've achieved that for Astoria. We know a lot more about all the candidates and their views than we would've if Jeremiah hadn't made his bid. There are 36 pages of discussion on this forum that never would've happened if Jeremiah hadn't run. This race has also proven that Aravella Simotas has tremendous support in our community. I've had the opportunity to speak with her on several occasions and I have found her to be intelligent, interested, and open to good ideas from wherever they emerge. She will be Astoria's voice in our Assembly and I hope we can all come together to work with her on the issues facing our community.

I feel bad for the people who campaigned for Jeremiah and I think he let them down.  The primary was only a little more than a month away and his candidacy at least offered a choice for Astoria residents.

Jeremiah did not engage in a negative campaign, but it did not make him look gracious, he seemed dispassionate and boyish.  As if his innate goodness could overcome an inane campaign that was more cabaret than caliber.

Aravella's campaign had no compunctions attacking Jeremiah as evidenced in this message thread.  In a political race, there is no second place (unless you are Hillary Clinton).  You play to win.  Aravella knows that.

I take stabs at the Astoria Political Machine for its evident entitlement, obsequious self-praise (on this message board and in print), and for positioning a woman for the Assembly whose greatest accomplishment, frankly, is being Greek.  However, Aravella's campaign is effective and she communicated better than Jeremiah.

Here is how Aravella described herself on her campaign website (first paragraph bio): "Aravella Simotas is a lifelong resident of Astoria with a strong commitment to her community and public service, and has devoted herself to making a positive impact on the lives of others."

Here is how Jeremiah described himself on his campaign website (first paragraph bio): "Jeremiah Frei-Pearson lives in Astoria with his wife, Karla Mosley Frei-Pearson, and their two cats: Scooby and Scrappy."

This is not a contrast of style, this is a WTF gaffe.

Jeremiah is grassroots and organic, while Aravella is establishment and systemic.  She has an enormous warchest (with a billionaire organizing her fundraisers), political connections, advisors and endorsers at her side, and a local newspaper that celebrates her every move.

I guess Aravella's supporters are relieved since there is no other candidate to stand in her way (and John Ciafone doesn't count).  They can breathlessly swoon over Aravella as she is "changing the way Albany does business" ...for the next twenty years.
