Yes!  It's a great idea, I always take advantage of Restaurant Week NYC.

Here is my suggestion:

I can't afford to eat at most of these restaurants under normal circumstances, so I like to find the special restaurant week menu online (not always available for every place, but usually you can find it) and then go to menupages.com or something and compare prices.  You will see that sometimes it's not that great of a deal, and sometimes it's an incredible deal.  I remember being really impressed with a Restaurant Week lunch at Smith and Wollensky last time around.  Oh, and Tao, I had a really good Restaurant Week experience there.

Oh yea, one more tip: keeping it down to one glass of wine is best because at most of these fancy places, they're pretty expensive.

In the little experience that I have had with NYC Restaurant Week, your assumption has been correct. The menus dont usually reflect what the restaurant is like most of the time. I think that can work for them or against them.

Generally there are 2 to 3 options for appetizer/entree/dessert and most of the time, none of those options encompass what the place is known for.... a meat dish, a poultry dish, a seafood dish.... the menus seem (my opinion) to be items that are easy for them to mass assemble.

But I can see how if someone was going to the theatre or some event, that you would take advantage of this kind of prix fixe. They can get you in and out and on to the next reservation pretty efficiently during this time.

I am probably not the norm though....I like to go somewhere and order whatever that restaurant is know for...i like to try things that I dont know already know what it is going to taste like. Does that make sense?
