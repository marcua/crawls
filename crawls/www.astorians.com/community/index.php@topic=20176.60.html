Those do it yourself tub kits are hit and miss.  Even having it done by a professional is hit and miss.  At the end of the day it's still paint, so unless it's completely sealed it will start chipping at some point.  No abrasive cleaners, no suction cups, no more leaving cans or bottles on the sides of the tub, etc.  Once you do this to a tub you need to be very aware of what you are doing in there.

Again, keep in mind that you're not really "resurfacing" the tub.  You are only painting it.

In the case of the user whose landlord painted the tub with house paint, that would all need to be stripped off before you could even proceed with a tub resurfacing.
