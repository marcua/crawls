I agree with Yaxpac on the sort of awkward feel with patronizing Waltz and Freeze Peach. There've been coffee shops in other cities and towns that seem to get down what I think of an ideal combination of homey and a business, where it doesn't feel like you're intruding or asking the barista to do you a personal favor by making you some coffee. In that respect, I feel like designing the service area to look like somebody's home would invite awkwardness, especially if there's only one person taking orders and making all the drinks. I personally would prefer the seating area to look homey as opposed to the kitchen/service area.

In terms of local places that are easy to check out, I think Grounded, in the West Village, has a good vibe to it. I can't think of any others offhand.
