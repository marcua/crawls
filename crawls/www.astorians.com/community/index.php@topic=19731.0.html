I was there last night with some friends, and we all enjoyed it very much.  Nice staff and friendly service, and the space is cozy.  We were given an amuse of chicken liver and bacon, topped with some pea shoots (I think they were pea shoots) on top of a housemade chip - delicious!  It was adjusted for our vegetarian friend with hummus with olive, which she liked a lot.  There was a little bread and tapenade at the table, too.

We tried both the beet and goat cheese salad and the baby spinach salad with fennel, citrus, almonds, and a light creamy blue cheese dressing, perfectly dressed. Out came a little espresso cup filled with a kind of ricotta and almond soup with a touch of truffle oil, which was wonderful. Then we had a seared scallop over creamed spinach and mornay sauce, topped with a cute little fried egg with another drop of truffle oil on it.  Wow!!

For our mains, I had the duck breast, which was served with spaghetti squash, which was just lovely.  My friend had the poulet roti, which was very tasty and moist.  Our veggie friend had the open mushroom ravioli in a cream sauce that was also delicious. Everyone liked what they got.

For dessert we had profiteroles, a fig tart, and a coffee panna cotta with caramel ice cream.  All were excellent but the panna cotta really stood out for me - it had some chocolate toffee crunchies between the panna cotta and whipped cream and ice cream, and I absolutely loved it.

The wines were very enjoyable, too.

I left the restaurant satisfied but not overstuffed.  That's a win in my book.  Food and wine were just great.  So nice to have a good French option in town. I'll definitely be back!

We've been there three times.  So far everything has ranged from good to excellent.  It's a little pricey for the area, but the food quality is definitely there.  They appear to have a pretty serious chef; we've gotten an amuse all three times and all of the food has been pretty sophisticated.

I've read reviews suggesting that the steak isn't that great.  I haven't had it, so YMMV.   

As of a week ago they were cash only, so bring cash.  They also let us bring a bottle of wine, which saves a bunch of money; I'm not sure how long they'll allow that without a charge, of course.

If you live in the area you should definitely give it a shot.  We need more good restaurants around here!
