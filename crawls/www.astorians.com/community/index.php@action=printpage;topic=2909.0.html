P.S. If your joints get cramped, perhaps you are running at a pace that is too fast for you. When starting out as runner, it's good to use the "talk test". This means that you should be running at a  pace at which you could carry on a reasonable conversation without being too winded to speak. I'm not saying you should be able to deliver a Shakespearean soliloquy, but you should be able to carry on a conversation. Just a tip....

Post by: lanseaux on September 26, 2007, 03:03:50 PM
There's always the streets - I used Google Ped (http://www.gmap-pedometer.com/) to caclulate distances until I got my GPS.

But better than that is the regulation track (http://www.nycgovparks.org/sub_your_park/park_of_the_month/2005_07/html/astoria_park.html) in Astoria Park.  It's a 1/4 mile track with a rubberised running surface.  It's completely free and open to the public.  It's close to the intersection of 21st St. and Hoyt Avenue, right next to the Triborough Bridge.

astoriantobe - I've gone jogging as early as 6:30 in summer months, and I've never had a problem.  I live near Ditmars and 31st St., and I usually head to Astoria Park and sometimes tack on a loop around Ralph Demarco Park, which is the little strip that extends north from Astoria Park along the water.  I've set it up so that I have a 3 mile round trip to the entrance to the Astoria Park track, and then I tack on however many laps I need to get up to the miles I want.  I also have a 4 and 5 mile loop to the track mapped out.
