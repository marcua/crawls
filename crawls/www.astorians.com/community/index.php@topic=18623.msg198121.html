Can someone please explain to me why brokers exist?
« on: June 22, 2010, 01:25:42 PM »
I have been apartment hunting for the past few months, and don't want to pay a broker's fee just on principle. I have seen a few good places that I liked, but I really do not want to pay a month's extra rent nor do I want to pay 15% of my annual rent to someone for essentially doing nothing except put me in contact with the person I am actually paying rent to.

I may get flamed for this, but from what I am seeing, these guys do nothing but swoop in and claim apartments as their own exclusive listing, and then rent them to you at a ridiculous markup, calling it a "broker's fee". In this day and age, it is not difficult to go online, find a listing and go see it yourself- I simply do not understand how a broker justifies charging an exorbitant fee for doing something I could just as easily do myself.

I actually thought it was the opposite- it was my thinking that there was more involved in purchaseing property (legal paperwork, surveying etc.) than in renting it.

In my experience, any decent apartments I've seen have one month or 10-15% annual rent tacked on as a "broker's fee". I can see how a landlord would want a tenant to provide a credit report and references, but this stuff is easily furnishable by the potential tenant. Additionally, I have never encountered a broker who ran anything other than a credit check.
They basically do nothing that anyone else couldn't easily do him- or herself.
What also irks me is that there is not even an option to deal directly with a landlord or management company in many of these situations. I can understand that some people can use a broker if they are unfamiliar with the territory, but it's not necessary if you know where you want to live and what you want to pay.

This is NY- most apartments can basically rent/sell themselves. It's a cutthroat market out there, and these scavengers just drive up prices and then sandbag you into paying fees for basically snaking in and claiming a property as "exclusive" to that broker.
