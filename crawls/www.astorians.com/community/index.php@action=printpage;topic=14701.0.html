"Although the number of properties available for sale declined 7% to 10,421 from 11,206 listings in the same period last year, inventory expanded 6.1% from the prior quarter total of 9,822. Quote from: webmamma on May 04, 2009, 06:42:13 AM
I remember reading somewhere that the value of real estate in New York has historically doubled every five years, even during the great depression. I remember seeing a chart somewhere...

That is historically untrue and mathematically falsifiable.
First, Robert Shiller has shown that housing in this country (including NY) has not increased in value beyond inflation from 1890 to 1999. It then grew into the biggest bubble in world history.
Second, if a house cost $5,000 50 years ago and doubled every 5 years, it would now be worth over $5,000,000. (5,000 x2, x2, x2.... you can do the math)
And we can add periods in the 70s and 90s when prices fell up to 50%.

Do you think the small, wood-sided, 80 year old single family homes that people in Astoria are trying to sell for $600,000 will be worth $1,200,000 in 5 years?
