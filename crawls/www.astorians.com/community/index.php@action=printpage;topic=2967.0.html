I've always been curious about the Rock -- is anyone here a member/have a take on it?  My only qualm is one location.

I'm an NYSC member, too -- with the most expensive monthly membership that allows me to go to any gym at any time.  (Don't ask -- a good gym membership is the one luxury I allow myself.)  I've been to NYSC branches all over New York, literally about 20 of them, and I can say that one on 30th Ave is one of the best I've been in.  This, of course, after they broke out to two floors -- it was a little cramped on one floor.  Now it's one of the best -- I'm mulling over the possibility of trying pilates just because they built that one special room with the machines.

As for your complaints, sure, some of the trainers are lousy (horndogging pretty women or just not doing much at all), but some are great -- the women trainers tend to be better/more ethical.  Last thing I want to do in a gym is watch TV (although I understand some folks prefer this on the cardio machines).  There's no pleasing anyone with the gym music (which is geared towards kids and does suck ... but if they piped in the bluegrass, blues, old-time country, alt 80s and cheesy 70s pop I often listen to on my MP3 player, you'd probably have a riot).  And the elevators run hot and cold (both were broke down when I went yesterday), but as I walked up the stairs yesterday, I thought, "I'm going to a gym ... shouldn't I be taking the stairs all the time and not complaining about it?"

I saw an earlier complaint on this thread about the quality of the workout you can get at NYSC.  I don't know.  For the past few years, I regularly take boxing and conditioning classes that you have to be in pretty good shape just to make it through one class, and in top physical condition to excel at.  Granted, the free weights area is too small, but I'm much more a proponent of these harsh classes than weight lifting (although I try to do both).

My main complaint regards that special circuit, the "20 minute workout" of weight machines -- which are actually pretty good machines.  (Most of the gyms put a white plastic chain around this circuit during peak hours ... which has to be one of the dumbest things I've seen in any gym.)  And it's not really the gym's fault.  But I've had this experience in every NYSC Branch.  Believe it or not, gym members steal those little 5-lb. weight pins that are used to adjust the smaller side weights.  Those things come in handy when you become familiar with a machine and want to slowly add on more weight in 5-lb increments as you go along.  Most of the machines, you can see that little metal pin has been snapped off.  I've talked to the trainers watching these areas, and each has said the same: people are stealing the pins.  WHY?!  There's just no logic to it -- it's a small metal rod with a small yellow round plastic handle.  It serve no purpose other than to help people add incremental weight to a machine.  Granted, I'm used to white-collar kleptomania, but this is ridiculous.  And my complaint against NYSC is that they don't readily replace these things -- I'm on the verge of going to a hardware store and having my own made.

Other complaints: at least at 30th Ave, they're obviously not clipping locks left on overnight (went in Saturday, at least 50 locks on personal lockers, about 20 guys in the gym, you tell me), the sauna is always closed (I picture wild gay orgies causing the shut down, but the truth is more likely guys messing with the controls or simply a failed health dept inspection ... besides which, all NYSC saunas and steamrooms are way too small/cramped), always seeing chewing gum in the urinals (not the gym's fault, just the lousy behavior of spoiled kids in desparate need of an ass-kicking who grew into spoiled men ... I see this at all the branches), and still see plenty of folks flaunting the cellphone rule (had to shoo away a guy with a cellpone who was sitting on a machine I wanted to use ... gave me a dirty look ... got a piece of my mind and a stern lecture from the trainer on guard duty ... will never understand the absolute idiocy and total self absorbtion of many cellphone users).

But overall, I give the 30th Ave NYSC a huge thumbs up, and not just because I can walk to it.  Overall, it's pretty well run and has a very good vibe about it.  I enjoy going there.
