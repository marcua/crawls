Quote from: johnny4evah! on June 24, 2008, 12:29:32 PM
We are one block away from the park, so we get a lot of transient types coming through now-even though the pool isn't even open yet.
Be careful out there everyone-it's going to be a long summer!
It's been very obvious to me that part of the problem is that Astoria is attracting a lot of younger, white-collar resident (YUPPies, if you prefer that term) to stay.  As a result, a lot of bad guys are taking notice.

To add to what the original poster wrote about our neighbors...my sisters' car was broken into in broad daylight on 31st street....right in front of Mandee's!!!  All they took was the GPS system.  But a girl was walking by and got a description of the guy.  Not only did she stay and tell the police what she had seen, not only did she provide information to the insurance company, but after the cops left and tried to chase the guy, she waited almost two hours for the cops to return (unfortunately, they never did).  She didn't have to stay as long as she did and didn't have to offer any help to my sister, but she did.  That was awesome.

Yes, it's true that we live amongst a lot of quality people in this neighborhood.  But it's obvious that the bad guys are taking notice.  

It's unfortunate that this sort of thing is happening with more frequency in our neighborhood.  Ever since the rape that happened near Ditmars and Crescent a short time ago, I've definately read about more crimes happening in the neighborhood than ever before (or maybe I'm just more aware of it now).  

I'm happy that the damage to the original poster and her boyfriend was minimal.  And I'm even more thankful they weren't physically hurt.  This should pose as a reminder that we all need to be more cognizant of who's walking around in the neighborhood.  And if you see anyone who you don't regularly see, keep your suspicions up.  

Let's keep up the dialoge and be on the lookout for anyone who meets the description of someone who's committed a crime.  Because chances are, they'll try and do it again.
