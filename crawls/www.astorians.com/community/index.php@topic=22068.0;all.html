But there's another variable that the building doesn't fully disclose.  Do you know about the PTAC units that heat/cool the apartment?  Well I just had to learn up on it.  Apparently in some instances the building will supply the heating fluid and the coolant, and the PTAC unit essentially just runs as a fan, to help circulate the cool or hot air.  And so you are paying for electricity to run those fans.
 
Apparently in this bldg however, I learned that they do NOT supply heating fluid or coolant for the PTAC units, and so the units themselves must create their own heat (via gas) or cool air (via electricity I thing), and then also run fans to then circulate that air.  So I understand that what that means is that, because the PTAC units are doing so much work, and they run on electricity, our electric bills will be that much higher.  It was difficult for others to tell me exactly what we might be paying each month, but the concensus seemed to be that our electric bill could be $200 in the higher use months.  So as far as I'm concerned, this is just another additional part of the 'rent' cost (most rental apartments DO provide heat).

Hi there, hoping I can offer some nice insight to this discussion!
 
I've been a strictly Ditmars girl for 7 years, but after a lump of bad apartments and bad landlords, we decided to up and look for a new building; and we looked a LOT.  We ended up going from the first floor of a townhouse on Ditmars and 37th to one of the new buildings on 21st (We're next to the N/W Astoria Building).  It was the best decision we ever made.
 
We pay the same in rent as we did for the much larger apt (the new one is only 1 bdrm), but since we're a couple, it doesn't matter.  We just moved Dec 1st, so we've yet to get our first heat bill, but I don't anticipate it being that bad.  We have 3 seperate units (one in kitchen, one in LR, one in bedroom) so usually only one is on at a time, and we keep it pretty cool; like 68 degrees.  This weekend we are going to get a space heater though as other people in our building reccomended them as being a little more cost efficient.  Fact is, most new building (we found) do not include heat.  It's just a downside to the new buildings. My partners dad did recently tell us that before we run the dishwasher, you should run the hot water in your sink, this way you don't use as much electricity getting hot water.
 
Okay, so the walk to the train; yeah, it's longer than usual.  But we don't mind at all, because once you're home, you're home! And when you get the view, it's worth it! We love our roof, New Year's Eve was great to watch fireworks and I can't wait to bbq.  Our friends don't mind walking over at ALL! No one has yet to complain and everyone loves the apartment. PLUS we use the busses a LOT! It's faster for me to take the bus down 21st to Queens Plaza and catch the train rather than walk to the train.  From Astoria Blvd to Queens Plaza is about 10 minutes.
 
The ammenties once you are home also are part of why you won't mind the walk; you have the washer/dryer, the view, the balcony.... we have a bodega right next door, so it's fine. Plus, the community beyond 21st St is really cute and up and coming (Hell Gate Social, Vesta, Astoria Bake Shop, etc). 
 
Last year when we were moving to our new apartment on Ditmars, I had said I refused to look at anything on 21st St and Astoria Blvd, but boy I'm glad we made the change!  There is also a new grocery store on Newtown and 23rd Street that I love.
 
I have been trying to get a lot of people I know to move into the building you are looking at, it looks and sounds great! If you can afford it, do it!
