Went last night, as far as the food goes, I was very satisfied.  I really liked that there were items on the menu I'd never seen before; bok choy pakora, green chili chicken, etc.  The shortened review - We had the pakoras, green chili chicken, saag paneer, mulligatawny, rice and nan.  All was really tasty and well seasoned. The mulligatawny was rich and smooth, nan (on time for us) was buttery, and the bok choy pakoras were light and crispy.  Entrees were very good, although I've been spoiled with the saag paneer from Mina's in Woodside, I would order both again.

Our service was a little slow but nothing crazy and the owner(?) noticed and repeatedly apologized. I will definitely be back.

They had lots of covers for a Wednesday - the power of astorians.com perhaps?

Another good review for Seva! We went on Sunday afternoon, got the bok choi pakoras, the bhel puri, the malai kofta, and sesame naan. The pakoras were really good, with two excellent sauces. The bhel puri was strange but strangely compelling; the only complaint is that one order serves 4-6 people! The malai kofta was (according to my GF, who claims to be a conaisseur of this dish), particularly good, with nice rich flavors. And the sesame naan was just the right mix of salty and oily and toasted. Friendly service too, and a pleasant space. Definitely worth a trip from anywhere in the neighborhood!

yum! i like this place a lot, though I didn't try very many menu items.  I got Malai Kofta, vegetable samosas, and a sweet lassi.  Samosas were very tasty, although the dark brown sauce that usually comes with samosas wasn't as good as some other places I've tried (i'm big on condiments).  but considering that the samosa itself was crispy on the outside, delicious on the inside, it didn't really matter.  not too doughy, either--that's key.

My serving of malai kofta was massive! as in, I got three meals out of it.  Usually you only get two little vegetable croquettes and some sauce, but with this there were four vegetable croquettes, and a ton of sauce!  and it was like $8.  that's a steal at twice the price. It wasn't the BEST malai kofta I've had, but it was yummy.

and finally, the lassi.  The clincher.  if it's a bad lassi, count me out.  this, however, was sweet without being too sweet.  Good consistency too.

I got take out, and the ladies at the counter were super nice.  I obviously need to try more items (will go with friends next time and share--am dying to try the chicken tikka), but I really enjoyed my meal.

I feel like I'm pretty spoiled when it comes to Indian seeing as I just moved to Astoria a few months ago from Woodside on the border of Jackson Heights so my basis of comparison is Jackson Diner and the like. Seva is good but could be better. It's the best I've found in Astoria so far (I'm not a fan of Roti Boti, their Jackson Heights location is way better). The garlic naan is not garlicky at all, I was convinced it was regular naan. The tikka masala was pretty good, not the best, but good. The samosas were good too. I thought overall it lacked spice. The chutney was strange, kind of watery and bland. Their rice pudding was really lame. I will not order that again. The portions here are great and the prices are awesome. I think overall it's good but not great. I still drive over to Jackson Diner and get takeout once in awhile though.
