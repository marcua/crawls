Hi, y'all!  My husband and I (29 and 30, respectively) moved to the Ditmars/Steinway area in September.  I found Astorians.com during our apt search, and it has been an invaluable resource since.  I've been lurking here for recommendations for everything from apartment brokers to our vet, all with great success.  Thought I'd finally jump into the fray.

I'm originally from the Philly suburbs, moved to NY three years ago (almost to the day!) when my now husband got transfered back to NY for work.  My husband was born in Queens, grew up on LI, so NY really feels like home to him.  We lived in Marble Hill (the Bronx) and Briarwood (way far out in Queens), which were both OK, but now that we found an apartment / neighborhood we both really love in Astoria, it finally feels like home to me too.  I love the quiet, family feel of our street, but it's just a couple blocks away from all the restaurants, bars, and shops on Ditmars.  We both love to cook, and the availability of fresh and unusual ingredients is so incredible that we've forgone exploring the amazing array of restaurants around here.  I figure, we'll leave that until the summer when it's too hot to cook.  Plus, we have plenty of time for all that, because plan to stay here for a long, long time. 

I've been working as an event planner for the last six years, and I love it.  I'm actually looking for a new job, so if you know anyone that could help on that front, I would be eternally grateful...unemployment has me more than a little stir crazy at the moment.  My husband is a 2L at CUNY Law, a total overachiever who works/studies constantly.  So in addition to killing time on the boards here, it would be great to make some new friends in the neighborhood to hang out with while he studies. 

Thanks for such an awesome resource on our awesome community!

Hi to all my fellow Astorians!

I'm a 3rd generation Astoria native.  My Grandparents migrated here from Italy.  After getting married I moved to Bayside, and after getting divorced I wised up and moved back to Astoria.

To all those who are new here....Welcome!  You have made the wise choice to live in a neighborhood that welcomes the new yet continues to keep and cherish it's old world traditions. 

We have the beautiful Astoria Park as our backyard, complete with our ever present towering parents of the neighborhood: the Tri-Borough and Hell Gate Bridges.

And to those who attended PS 122, or took religious classes at Immaculate Conception or who remember what once was "up Ditmars" (aka as 31st Street): The bowling alley (now Keyfood), the movie theater (now a Post Office) Phil's Deli - who had THE best hot dogs and (now a fish store)  I'd love to hear from any of my fellow old time neighbors.  (please tell me 45 is not that old!) 

Jenny
