OK, Here's  how I see it. As  someone who has for years dealt with both Govt sponsored and private insurance  with people  I love and respect, from my parents to veterans and finally myself, while  I was going through the worst health crisis  in my life, which happened  to be literally life and  death, without insurance. I have come  to the following conclusions.

First, having 'great' insurance  is  no guarantee of great  care, matter of fact, many Ultra Wealthy are purposely  put through unnecessary  tests and even surgical  procedures, due to the fact they can pay and have great insurance. Now, one can say, who cares, they have 'bucks', screw 'em. OK, Let's  go to the other  end, illegals nearly 15 million and those  with no 'traceable' bank accounts, assets, get 'Free', actually anyone  who works pays for them, health care  and  they can pick and choose  from a  myriad  of  state  sponsored  plans that best suits them. This is not available in all states, which is why  you see  most of the Medicaid fraud  drift to the larger, less able to trace fraud,  states. So, you have people  who are riding around, and in public clinics with cell phones and gold watches, and then later on line at BESTBUY buying wide  screen plasma  TVs, going in for routine checkups  and even cosmetic  procedures, while  'saps' like me and you can't get the same, cause  we 'earn'  too much, or more to the point, we  are honest  about what we earn cause we feel that, rightly,these programs were designed for the genuinely poor and 'homeless'  people. In NY, they advertise  to sign up the whole family to these  programs and there have been statistics  that these programs  have been defrauded , nationwide, for decades, to the tune  of  nearly 2 trillion dollars.  As the Govt. spends more  for  "health' care  than any other federal or state  program, including military and  disaster  relief combined, more money and larger  programs  are not the answer.

I have found, for myself, that  negotiating with doctors, and more important the billing sect. about your ability  to pay, before  a  visit, is essential. You  will discover, when you pay out of pocket, that  it's  not just  the  Military that is  'screwed' by  state/govt  funded  programs, but, in the end, me and you. See, when someone , anyone, provides a service, knowing the Govt  is paying, they jack up the price and even bill multiple  times, which is  illegal, but, hard to track. One 'solution', one of which is  the McCain 'health' program in ,http://money.cnn.com/2008/03/10/news/economy/tully_healthcare.fortune/.  McCain himself  a man who lost  alot of  his body fighting for his nation, knows full well, the trials  and tribulations  of dealing with Govt. 'health' care. 

Now, How to deal with doctors and what I  have found  is the best way. First, if  you go to a doctor  and he  makes you wait more than two hours, spends five minutes with you, and only hands you a prescription without explaining anything, except getting your insurance card, run, don't walk, away from him/her.  They are hacks  who are taking more  than they can handle. Second, pick a doctor, for your condition, based  on YOUR OWN RESEARCH. I can't emphasis enough, how important it is for folks  to take control of their  own health care. Do not go in uninformed, or  clueless, have  a list and be direct with the doctor. They are only human and do miss things. Third, boost and add things  to your own immune system, OK it first  with the Doc, if they seem  adverse  to ANY  'natural'  means of boosting your immune  system,again.. run, don't walk away. Fourth, find out side-effects, before  you start any therapies, and if it is worse than what  ails you find out if there is  a less damaging, 'cheaper' alternative. Finally, don't take everything a Doctor tells  you as Gospel, they are not always correct, and just  like insurance  companies, may have vested interests. I could tell you horror  stories  of Doctors, nurses and insurance, both VA/Govt.  and Private  I have run across  for loved ones and the last few years, myself, but, the bottom line  is there is  only one set  of people  who can get you the 'best'  health care, YOURSELF by being informed, negotiating price and care for yourself, and those  who love you and take care of these wishes  if you are unable to speak. In the end, keep your health care  as  close to you as possible, cause once  it's out of your hands, it becomes a matter of someone elses  conscience,  ego, greed and logic.
