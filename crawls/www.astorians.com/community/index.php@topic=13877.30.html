Re: Chicken Shack
« Reply #41 on: March 20, 2009, 11:07:02 AM »
I got take-out from this place on March 18.  I was pleased.  It's a nice, bright space with an extensive menu.  The counter service was friendly and efficient.  After staring indecisively at the menu for several minutes, I ordered 1/2 white meat chicken (which comes with pita bread), side order of mixed vegetables and side order of roasted potatoes.  The total was about $15.  I ordered much too much food.  I could only finish about half of it (and many people on this board know I can put away significant amounts of food) and then I had a second filling meal yesterday.

I remove the skin from chicken for health reasons and, of course, without the skin there's a risk of the chicken being dry and flavorless.  However, this chicken was marinated or something.  It was moist and flavorful without the skin.  The pita bread was fresh and warm, though I put it in the toaster anyway for some crunch (and partly because I bought a new toaster oven and wanted to play with it).  The veggies were cooked soft, not crunchy, but they were still good, nicely flavored.  My only misstep was the roasted potatoes.  I didn't like the way they were seasoned.  I saw the fries coming out and they looked really good, medium-thick fries with skin on.  I should have ordered fries instead.

Anyway, on the whole, if they keep the quality up, I'll probably go there a lot.  But I don't know whether they have any connection with Christine McVie's band before Fleetwood Mac.
