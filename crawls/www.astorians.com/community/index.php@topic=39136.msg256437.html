Yes Queen, as you say, lots more young couples moving in, and indeed, a 'fancy, luxury apt' at even $2600, is very doable for a couple to swing, versus a more basic apartment at $1900, for a single person to swing.  (That example right there is a differential of $600/month being paid by any one person...)

When I first moved to Astoria 4 years ago, I was one of those people that lived in a luxury building (on 21st St. right near 30th Road).  Prior to that I'd never lived in a doorman building, and the only reason I took that apartment was 1) I was desperate to get out of Glendale/Ridgewood, Queens as the commute was horrid and none of my friends would come out to visit, NOR could I ever get taxis to go there from Manhattan (too far).  2) The realtor that showed me the Astoria place was 'smart' enough to show me the place at night.  I got one look at the view of the Manh. skyline from my windows and I was enthralled and....3) I figured I'd do a bit of Airbnb to help swing the rent.

Well after two years of living there I decided to move out.  It was too stressful 'having' to do Airbnb and hoping no one would find out. (Whenever I rented out via Airbnb I was always there at the same time to host/supervise the guests.  I'd sleep on futon couch in living room while they got bedroom...) 

Also, the high rent simply left a bad taste in my mouth...the fact of whether or not I could actually afford it aside.  Paying $2400 (and this was two years ago) for a 1BR in Astoria seemed unconscionable to me.  Obviously I love Astoria but still, it's Astoria.  Who do these places think they are charging that kind of money?  In addition, the building loved to tout itself as offering rent-stabilized apartments, but again, that term in and of itself can be loaded with confusion.  They still raised the rent every year and while granted there was a cap on how much they could raise it, when the rent is already $2400, well you can see that even with a 2% increase each year, the new rent price begins to border on insane.

Then there was the building itself...how it was made...the quality of the fixtures, the design, etc.  I began to eventually see beyond the 'modern, clean lines, all white decor', and that this was just another cookie-cutter modern soul-less building, with all the clich√© 'modern amenities' thrown in to appeal to a certain clientele (doorman, very generic 'fitness center', finished rooftdeck complete with gas grills, outdoor couches, and even...outdoor showers??!), washer/dryer inside every apartment (which to me was utterly wasteful.  I'd much rather have another closet versus a washer/dryer taking up space in my apt.  I would have been very happy to just have a small laundry room on every floor.  Even THAT would have been a luxury to me, as my entire life I've been a Laundromat person. ;-) 

Then there was the fact that within one year or so of the building being built, opening up for rentals, and being filled with tenants...within a year or so, new owners/management bought the building and there was a changeover of some rules and some of the staff. 

The doormen were clearly treated like chattel by management.  Of course, I was always very nice to the doormen...would sometimes offer them a plate of food or a cold drink in the summer...they didn't even have a mini-fridge for the doormen, so I actually found one for them on Craigslist and arranged to have it delivered and kept in their little 'closet' of a room, where the doormen could keep their bags, jackets, etc.  The doormen would tell me of instances where they were 'told' they had to work a double shift because another doorman called in sick, etc.  Many of these doormen also had second jobs where they were doormen at other buildings, and most of these guys lived in other boroughs (i.e., the Bronx or Brooklyn) and  so travelled great distances to/from their various jobs.

Add this all up, and I decided that while I could technically afford to live in such a building, I just didn't like spending that much of my income on rent, and in such a soul-less building.  I also never liked living on 21st Street with all that traffic and industrial feeling.  Blech.

That's when I went on a very serious mission to find a new place.  It was exhausting...I posted on FB and told all my friends to ask all their friends for any leads.  I walked the streets of Astoria, peering into apt buildings for bldg. mgmt. info....I'd wait outside buildings and question tenants.  And I also worked with a few brokers, telling them my criteria.  (I was looking to pay $600 less, or $1800, for a 1BR.) 

It was an extremely stressful two months of searching, but I finally found a place... a 3rd floor walkup in a small apt. building with 8 units, and where the LL lives with his family.  I'm in a totally different part of Astoria...by Ditmars/Steinway...and much prefer it to 21st St./30th Ave.  I'm now back to Laundromat life, and while it sure was nice for 2 years to be able to do my own laundry buck-naked, any hour of the day or night, in my own apartment, I'll take this new living situation over my old 'luxury' one, any day.  ;-)
