Quote from: janelane on September 10, 2006, 09:22:22 AM
I'd have to say Cup.  I know a lot of people like it.  We've tried it 3 times.  Everytime the service was horrible.  Brunch is definately overpriced as well!  I know a lot of people like cup, but it's not for me. 

I'm with you on this one... when we first moved to Astoria more than a year ago, CUP was awesome (they served quirky yummy things like roasted corn-on-the-cob with a chili rub) but then, sometime last winter, they changed management. No obvious announcement, just a revamped menu that got rid of all the distinctive, high-quality items (homemade chili topped with avocado chunks) in favor of all the mundane typical diner-fare (greasy Roumanian shell steak with a side of pallid frozen vegetables).

They used to have great sandwiches with fresh ingredients. They used to serve real Kesso yogurt with fresh fruit and homemade granola, drizzled with greek honey for brunch. All that is gone. Now you'd be hard-pressed to differentiate them from any other Queens diner. In this vein, the food wasn't bad, but having known its prior incarnation as a real eatery, the overwhelming disappointment is what makes this the worst (and saddest) eatery now.
