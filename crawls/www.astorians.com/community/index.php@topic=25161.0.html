Anyone live near 20th Ave and 43rd Street?
« on: July 06, 2013, 07:22:14 PM »

So I've been looking around for a house to buy in Astoria.  Today I checked out a house near 20th Ave and 43rd Street.  I'd never been in that area before.  I did walk around the entire block, and then walked back towards Steinway and Ditmars. 

Sometimes you need to walk around an area a few times at day and night to get a real feel for it, but my first instinct is that it's rather sleepy and quite suburban feeling compared to other parts in Astoria, I guess partly because it's quite a bit removed from the subway, and also because just on the other (Northern) end is what seems to be all industrial/waterfront, yes?

And I would imagine that if you are single and carless (as I am) that it will feel quite isolating.  I didn't have my phone/watch to time it,but it seemed it would take a good 15-20 mins. to walk to the N/Q at Ditmars no?

So I'm thinking this area/house is a no-go for me unless someone can convince me otherwise as to other amenities/virtues that I perhaps missed during my one jaunt around the area?  ;-)

I grew up on 43rd st. between 20th road and 20th avenue. My parents still live in the same house. The area is a quiet, peaceful, safe neighborhood. There's a diverse ethnic mix and people are generally friendly and look out for each other. The area hasn't changed much since the 1960's. My father commuted to mid-town Manhattan every day for 40 years. He walked to the elevated train station (the "EL") on Ditmars. It was a brisk 15 minute walk that he made twice daily. It kept him in shape. Once on the train, he was in the city in 15 minutes. Shopping and good restaurants are in walking distance and you are close to the airport. I would say that it would be a great investment to buy in that area. Good luck.
