Went there 2 weeks ago and the place is stunning.  They did an amazing job with the decor and transforming it from the former Canz.  Staff behind the bar was dressed in ties and vest - very impressive - reminded me of Dutch Kills.  There were 5-6 bartenders behind the bar - so many indians and no chief.  They were extremely friendly and helpful.  I agree with a previous post - it reeked of gas, to the point we inquired and were told they didn't smell it. 


The music completely there me off.  They were playing 70's music - don't get me wrong - I love Earth Wind and Fire as well as Elton John and Fleetwood Mac, but the ambiance didn't call for 70's.  It has a jazz, modern 20's music vibe, but it's obviously going thru an identity crisis.  When we left there was a bouncer outside - why must a restaurant have a bouncer?  It wasn't packed nor was there a line to enter - a bit pretentious.  We were told that they plan on changing the music to R&B and Hip Hop - don't mind the music at all - it's just doesn't have the right fit.  I hope they make changes to ensure longevity, otherwise, it'll only last a few years.

We stopped in yesterday for brunch, and as others have said, you wouldn't know that it used to be a second rate Hooterz. It's gone from the feel of a barn, which is how I'd best describe the decor at Canz to completely redone in dark wood, with low lights. I think the best comparison, decor wise, would be Sweet Afton, but maybe a little more upscale as they have leather banquettes, and a couple other nicer touches (the "uniform" the wait staff wears is great, and feels like a throwback to the 30's / 40's). The music they were playing was mostly soul records (I specifically noticed the snapping Stax logo on the screen of the music player when we walked in) which I love. Don't know if jazz would be better or not, but it seems more appropriate than say hip hop or club music or whatever. We went for the punch brunch, which overall was pretty damn good.
I had the duck and beans, which was so over the top delicious, and certainly the best of the three dishes we tried. My wife got the eggs benedict, which didn't have the ham / canadian bacon (we couldn't remember if that was mentioned on the menu or not) and it didn't come with any sides, so she wound up ordering some extra fries, which were great. Our friend got the burger, which was a bit over cooked, which happens. We all enjoyed the bourbon punch, and tried a couple other cocktails as well.
That said, the place is not without it's faults. First the complimentary punch is only served with your brunch entree, and you can't order more if you'd like it (we did). We thought it was kind of unclear, even though there is a list of rules on the brunch menu, which we all seemed to fail to read / retain.  Second, as others have mentioned there's definitely an odor in the place. Not sure if it's the varnish from the wood walls or what, but my wife and our friend both noticed it. I didn't since my sinuses have been stuffed for the last few days. Third, at some point during brunch they dropped a projection screen down from the ceiling. We were talking about how nice it was that they didn't have TVs. I hope it's there for like screenings or something, and not for "the big game." There are plenty of places to watch sports or whatever, and this place really doesn't need that - at all. Fourth, I think the bouncer is really off putting, but then I"m not sure if I'd know the place was open without someone standing there. Lastly, I think the lights could be just a touch brighter. It was dark to the point that the menus were a little hard to read.
Overall, I think this place caters to a growing crowd in Astoria, and I'd compare it favorably to Sweet Afton, Dutch Kills, and NY Doghouse. As it's close and doesnt' seem like it will attract the throngs of Affliction afficionados that Canz did, I'm quite looking forward to visiting during the week, or even just stopping in for drinks after work.
