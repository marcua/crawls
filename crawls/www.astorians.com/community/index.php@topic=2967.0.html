I was a member of the Sports Complex about 6-7 yrs ago, so things could have changed by now.  Back then, it certainly was a model of "you get what you pay for".  They didn't have updated machines, things broke down, and it wasn't so modern.  But it really wasn't too busy either, so you didn't wait long, they do have a pool and hot tub from what I remember, and raquetball, and maybe tennis?  The only thing about the year commitment, is if you don't go, you're stuck with having paid for the whole year.  

The Matrix looks like a great gym, offers month to month, but is far- 45th and Ditmars.

The Rock is good and 24hrs, but maybe expensive?

I'm a member of NYSC Astoria, and it's great.  Plus they're all over the city so you never have an excuse not to go, cept your lazy self  :-D  (I'm talking about me here)

Re: Astoria Sports Complex?

I was a member of ASC for about 2 years. The setup is fine and I love the fact that they have a pool. I didn't get there that often, but I didn't feel too bad about it b/c I had paid so little for it. The people are realy nice, and the equipment is kept up pretty well, and I love the fact that it is very much a community gym, with kids and older people, not all yuppies like NYSC.

The only thing is that the locker rooms and showers are a little bit grody. It's fine if you can go home and shower afterwards, but if you want to shower at the gym and then go, it is not too appealing.

But in the end, it was the distance and the lack of a gym partner that made me give up on it.

Re: Astoria Sports Complex?
« Reply #8 on: August 14, 2006, 10:10:03 AM »
I'm an ASC member, but only for the cardio floor-and both my roomies jumped on my bandwagon.
I dig it. I go in the morning, when motivated (see thread in social networking for my attempt to be shamed into more frequent visits...i should check that one...)and find its easier to get a machine, and there is no time limit. After 5 the treadmills are only available for a half hour per person.
The machines are all in good condition and theres quite the mix of people. not that i am particularly interested in meeting someone while sweating with my ipod....
The rates, i find, are very good. It's $99 for the year for either the weight floor, or the cardio floor and a $59 start up fee. if you re-sign with them the $59 is waived the following years. I think its $259 for 2 floors. The pool is 250 on its own, but 259 if you add another floor, and i want to say 300 for the whole shebang. My roomie was paying 80/mo for NYSC, so in comparison, it's a steal.
