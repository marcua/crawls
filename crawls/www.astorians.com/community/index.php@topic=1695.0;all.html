Yaaaay thats a real adventure... I've only gone a few times but with my grandmother (old skool chinese lady, yo) and then with my parents (newer skool but grew up in Taiwan) and we've found some really great places. You can't go wrong with the fried food windows that are right in front of the LIRR station on Main St. as you come up from the subway station: good place to start for scallion pancakes, fried dumplings, and of course the hot soy milk drink.. all to go, eat while you walk, naturally!

Then I would recommend walking up Main Street, toward the big ol' Queens Public Library to this place called "East" for dim sum or BUFFET if you're brave (theres a lot there and I think it might be kinda expensive). Its a westernized ambiance (so skittish white people wont be scared by what must be the de rigeur scuzzy chinese restaurant) but yet the food is really good. A bit pricier but good for impressing that hot Chinese dissident you've got your eye on.

But if you're not up for a buffet/sit down nicer place, there's a great "food court" like place thats gloriously dingy and closer to the station on Main... there's a man in the back who hand throws and then cuts noodles and cooks them fresh for you in like 2 minutes, tops it with traditional stuff like roasted pork and scallions, veggies, etc. Its an amazing sight. Better than watching pizza tossers, in my humble opinion.

Yay! let me know when you want to make an afternoon excursion to the end of the 7 line, I'd love to go (when the weather warms up, of course)

My favorite standard place is harmony palace on mott just north of canal street. Im friends with one of the managers there and he takes good care of me and introduces me to new dishes.  he is currently showing off his new chef from shanghai (or so he claims).

Love Jing fong just get there eary. this is probably the best in food, cleanliness and overall taste, plus there are stations you can walk up to as well.

Trimple eight under the manhattan bridge anchor (east bway?) is probably equal to or just behind jing fong in my opinion.

Now i have to disagree on the pork dumplings and here is my reason.  If you eat a pork bun you get full and cant eat all the dumplings, beef tendons, chicken feet and occasional sticky rice stuffed with sausage.  Ill save the pork buns for just walking around chinatown. and whats more important than shark fin dumplings, har gow and shu mei?  well except beef tendons that is.  damn you annie, now im hungry. 

By the way, friend at harmony introduced me to crysanthymum / black tea mix which beats the crap out of the regular stuff on the tables.
