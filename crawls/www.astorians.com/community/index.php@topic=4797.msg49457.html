I gotta get this off my chest. You know how when you buy a new coat, or blazer, or skirt, and the flap in the back is stitched shut? You're supposed to undo that before you wear it. PLEASE undo it. It's stitched like that to make sure it gets to the store without getting all rumpled or creased, but you're not supposed to wear it like that. I saw a grown man with a nice long wool coat on yesterday with that damn flap stitched shut.

Same as when the pockets are stitched shut. Obviously you're supposed to have use of your pockets, so you're expected to undo that stuff before you wear it. But I can't see your pockets. I SEE the flaps.

If it's a friend, I tell them, or even take it upon myself to undo it for them. But strangers are out of range. And aside from the urge to run up and grab their coat and undo it for them, I simply simmer with irritation. So please help me out and undo that thread on your coat or skirt. All it takes is a pair of scissors, or a good tug.

Many thanks.
