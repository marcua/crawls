My grandmother and mother would tell you that it is a dense bread made with flour, baking soda, buttermilk and salt. It has a slight tang to it and is usually served warm with butter with stews, soups, or just on its own.

However, there are so many variations that you come across here in the States. Some that add caraway seeds, raisins, even a dusting of powdered suger on the outside. You will find a million different recipes online. Some people even refer to brown bread or "wheaten bread" as soda bread.

My grandmothers is very basic...my mom likes to add raisins sometimes (because she likes to eat it for breakfast)....The kind that I had at Dolce Italia was sweeter than I have had it and did have raisins. I practically ate the whole loaf myself with whipped butter. So good.

I would imagine that you could wander around Woodside and find a lil bakery with traditional soda bread.  :-)
