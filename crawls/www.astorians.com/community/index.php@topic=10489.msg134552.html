Quote from: duska3419 on June 18, 2008, 11:03:11 PM
is there like a (safe) parking lot and then you can get out and walk around ?

Yes.  There is a large parking lot, a welcome center, and walking trails (which are level, graded, and not particularly long, but they make a delightful afternoon stroll).  It's part of the Gateway National Recreation Area, so it falls under the auspices of the National Park Service.  The National Park Service website for Gateway is cumbersome and not particularly helpful, but you can get a lot of other information from google, including the Brooklyn Bird Club's page on Jamaica Bay, with rough maps (look specifically at the West Pond map - you will see the parking area on the lower right of the map, next to the visitor center).  Very limited information here.  Other information here and in linked search.

We went last year. Had never been before and have to say that it was a beauty of a park. Nature and wildlife everywhere. I think it's what the wetlands around NY looked like 200 yrs ago. You get to walk on paths skirting inlets, marsh and bays. As you walk around one bend you see geese flying overhead, tall grasses ringing the inlet and then off in the distance the gleaming top of the Empire State building off in the distance.  The place is serene and awesome.  Very safe, good parking in a lot and not that far away (by car on an off time).
