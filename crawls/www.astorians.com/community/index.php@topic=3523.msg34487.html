Key Food (31st Street between Ditmars & 23rd Ave) sucks
« on: September 23, 2006, 05:45:05 PM »
:x

Is it just me or does Key Food suck? With exception to a handful of employees, their cashiers are rude and have not been trained properly. And if they have been adequately trained, have not been corrected (or reprimanded) sufficiently when they have done something wrong or out of line. I have worked as a cashier in three different grocery stores and each one was very careful how they trained their employees. There were three things which held true for each:

  • You must respect your customer, and essentially your customer is always right.
  • There is a proper way to bag items, i.e. bread and eggs don't sit on the bottom of the bag with cans of food on top. Or chemical items such as toilet cleaner should not be bagged with food items. And don't throw produce around!
  • You need to wear your uniform, and it needs to be worn correctly.

So today our cashier was disrespectful. He didn't even pay attention to what he was doing. When it came to the end of our stuff there was a very obvious break in between our stuff and the next person's groceries. Yet he grabbed their stuff and started scanning. I kept asking him to stop, but he kept doing it. He even did it with the person in front of us with our groceries. He also was getting visibly upset when one of our items wouldn't scan, and seemed very put out when he had to type the item in.

When he went to bag our items he was throwing all the wrong stuff together - chemical cleaner with produce, and he was banging our produce around. Our bananas scarcely last long enough as it is, so banging it around isn't exactly going to help further its existence.

And hello - he looked terribly sloppy. He didn't even have a proper name tag. He had taken a permanent marker and written his name on the front of his Key Food apron (which wasn't tied even).

I may seem to be really nitpicky on the last item, but the fact that you don't even present yourself properly just makes the whole thing worse. The grocery stores I worked in would have not stood for that sort of appearance.

I know that there are other things as well - things being priced incorrectly, but the cashier thing really really bugs me. I have found myself fuming on more than one occasion after dealing with their crappy customer service. I feel like most of the people that suck are the teenagers that are just floating through. I know that isn't entirely fair because there are actually a couple of teenagers that do a decent job, but most don't care and think that it is cool for them to be gossiping with the next cashier or even on their cell phone as they scan your stuff (not kidding - that has happened). I can think of about four or five people that I actually like in that store and they are all over 40, maybe even 50.

Sigh...young people these days... :roll:

I think Key Food sucks for the following reasons:

  • Nearly every time I go through checkout at least one item rings up as the wrong price. It would be one thing if this happened occasionally, but when it happens over and over and becomes "expected" it gets a bit old.
  • Things are not shelved right. Frequently you pick an item off the shelf and then have to go hunting for the price tag for it.
  • The check-out people frequently bruise up all my produce. They slam it around while gossiping with each other about who knows what.
  • Find an item you like? You might not ever see it again. Then 6-12 months later it shows up again on the shelf.
  • The check-out people never ask for a Key Food card. So if you're there buying some sale item and you forget, too bad. Other stores always ask for the card.

I just don't think their management gives a rip about how their store operates.

I simply hate that store. If there was another one I could go to I absolutely would. It's in moments like these I wish I had a car.
