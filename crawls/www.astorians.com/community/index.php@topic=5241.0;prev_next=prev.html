The late night crowd consists predominantly of Greek males, many of whom come off as drunk and aggressive. 

The last time I was there late at night, my friend and I sat at the counter.  To my right was some junior meat head (I say junior because he was pretty short) and his girlfriend.  I don't know what the guy's issue was, but he spent the majority of his time there staring straight at my friend and I like he was interested in killing us.  I think he was insecure about his girlfriend, because as she went to the bathroom, he turned and started eying everyone between him and the bathroom door like he was going to attack them if they looked at her or she looked at them.  Earlier in the evening I looked at her very briefly and I'm assuming that he got the idea I was into her, which I was not.  At all. 

Anyway, I'm a fairly passive person, and considering that he seemed to know just about every single person in the restaurant, I made a point of not trying to make eye contact with him.  Still, I could see him leering at me out of the corner of my eye.

The incident was annoying.  Not enough to keep me from not going back, but enough for me to mention it here.

Neo's initial theory is correct, I believe.  It fills up after last call and the bouncer is needed because a lot of the crowd is drunk. 

While we were waiting in the vestibule in a mob of people, the door would open, a group would walk in and shove through the crowd and assume that they could just walk right into the restaurant even though there were 20 other people waiting around and a gigantic fellow standing in front of the door.  And every so often the crowd would start closing in on the door like they were going to rush it when it opened and the bouncer would start yelling for them to move back. 

I think everyone goes there, because Astoria doesn't have nearly as many 24 hour diners as Manhattan does.
