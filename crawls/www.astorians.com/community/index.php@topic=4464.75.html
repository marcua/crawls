OK, I had the peanut butter-chocolate cupcake and I now think it's the best cupcake I've had in Astoria.  The cake is nice and light, not overly sweet, chocolatey, and of a pleasant texture, plus the loads of peanut icing on top studded with a few halves of mini peanut butter cups is delicious.  It's velvety and peanut-buttery, with no graininess at all.  It's a silky peanut buttecream, basically.  This cupcake and the oreo cheesecake cupcake are both made in house, whereas the others are contracted out.  I've had them elsewhere and they are pretty tasty.  But this peanut butter one is pretty fantastic.  I've also eaten the brownie, which is fabulous.

I see no one has raved about the soups Darren is serving now. Guess I'll step up to the plate!

They are amazing.

Yes, it's just that simple. I don't know what magic is in that man's fingers, but he turns what should be the simplest meal into an incredible culinary experience. All of his tomato soups are wonderful - word is that Saturdays are going to feature them now because they are so popular. Then again, he mentioned the other day that he has sold out of soups every single day save two since they started serving them this winter, so that should say something about how great they are.

Oh! Best part - soup and crostini designed to match the soup is $4.50. Definitely enough for a meal, or you can split another crostini or a panini with a second person, and be very satisfied. The first time I got the soup I was with two other people and we made the mistake of each ordering soup (which we didn't realize came with a crostini at the time), a crostini to share for the table, and a panini each. None of us could finish the paninis we were so full on everything else.

Oh how I wish Darren did catering or rented out his restaurant for parties. It would be amazing. I'm just praying for a lobster bisque to show up on the menu one of these days.
