mcdirk and stephaniel used their wiley charms to lure me over from the general introduction thread... so I'll reintroduce myself here. As far as my LGBT-related intro goes: I've been out as bi since I was about 18. I've been in serious, long-term relationships with both men and women and am currently single. 

I'm fairly committed (there's always more that can be done) to LGBT rights and issues - particularly in the legal realm (simply because that's what I'm studying). Just as an example - I've recently found myself embroiled in the midst of a heated debate about the FDA's policy which restricts blood donation from men who have had sex with men (and women who have sex with those men). The question is whether having a blood drive on our lawschool campus would be a violation of the our human rights policy which forbids discrimination on the basis of sexual orientation.

As far as being the B in LGBT, I'm well practiced in explaining my sexuality to both straights/queers who stereotype bisexuals as indecisive, selfish and unfaithful. I'm almost always happy to engage in the friendly debate on the topic. I sometimes find myself (don't all LGBT people to some extent?) as somewhat of a (hesitant) ambassador for being bi.  I end up just trying to explain -my- own particular experience and feelings and try to explain that I'm not trying to prove that all bi people are like me but that the danger lies in making presumptions about people's attitudes and behaviors on the basis of their sexuality.

The length of this introduction/mini-rant prolly demonstrates that I'm procrastinating from studying for my finals again. Back to it!

Hi everyone.  I'm new to the board but I've been in Astoria for about 2.5 years now.  I just heard about OutAstoria and I'm glad there is a message board.

I guess I'm here because I have trouble meeting gay men (for both friendships AND dating).  I don't know why, but I get really nervous around men, esp. gay men.  I'm usually pretty out-going, friendly, extroverted and fun.  The idea of going to one of the OutAstoria dinners/brunches sounds like a lot of fun, but it also terrifies the hell out of me.  So I guess it is good that there is a message board where I can get to know some of you all first, because I need more gay friends.  No offense to the straight women of the world, but they just aren't enough.

A little about me?  I'm a book editor, an admitted workaholic, a netflix addict and like most people in NYC, I can be found with my MP3 player permanently attached to my ears.  I'm not a huge fan of bars and clubs (too dark, too loud, too shallow) and I'm not a big drinker (I know, what kind of gay man am I?) so I haven't met many gay men.  Most of my interactions with the gay community are with people who are only looking to hookup or a few one-time conversations where I wasn't sure what to say.

I'm looking at this place as somewhere I can be myself, where I can get to know some friendly people and eventually work up the nerve to come to one of the dinners once I'm ready to meet those friendly people face to face.  And hey, maybe I'll find a few people who understand the nervousness.
