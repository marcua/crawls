As a non-practicing/non-religious Jew, the Jewish community in Astoria is pretty much invisible to me. The supermarkets do have a small kosher food section and they carry a few special items for Passover, but, for example, I had a really hard time finding potato starch a couple years back. The liquor stores generally have a dusty selection of Manischewitz. No kosher delis or knish places or anything like that, which saddens me.

For religious observances, there is the Astoria Center of Israel, though: http://www.astoriacenter.org/index.htm

Hi Pinchas!


My husband and I are managing to be fairly observant Conservative Jews in Astoria. Obviously it's not exactly a thriving hub of Jewish life but that's not exactly a bad thing, either -- I wouldn't want to live in a culturally uniform neighborhood! We keep kosher at home (though we do eat out at non-kosher places, as there are no kosher restaurants nearby) and we're members at Astoria Center of Israel. I think we probably had the only sukkah in Astoria this month :)


We live near Ditmars and here are my local kosher tips:
  • There's a good-enough selection of kosher wines at the three local liquor stores, though we don't restrict ourselves only to kosher wine (we've also ordered a lot from Sherry-Lehmann, which has a great selection & free delivery).
  • The best local selection of kosher staples (cheese, baked goods, chicken broth, etc.) is at Best Yet at 20th Ave and 37th St, and there are more limited selections at Key Food.  Parrot Tree on Ditmars has a surprising assortment of pareve desserts -- I think it's run by Israelis.
  • There's no place local to get kosher meat. We don't eat very much meat, though, so we can get what we need via Fresh Direct and by making occasional trips to Aaron's in Rego Park. Costco in LIC also has kosher chickens and quite a few other kosher products.
  • Oh, and essen -- though I've never found potato starch at the grocery stores, the health food stores stock it year-round. I think I get mine at Fresh Start on 23rd. Key Food's always got matzoh and matzoh meal during Passover (at much better prices than in more Jewish neighborhoods, as it happens).
I believe that there is an Orthodox synagogue in LIC, or maybe a Chabad, but I don't know much about it. ACI on Crescent is the only Conservative shul in the neighborhood and has been experiencing an encouraging revival in recent years since Rabbi Jonathan Pearl arrived, especially attracting a good number of families with babies and toddlers. The rabbi has also held at least one conversion class, so that's attracted some young singles and couples. Not in huge groups, mind you, but enough to provide for a rewarding community that continues to expand. There are kids' programs on Shabbat and the occasional community/potluck on Friday nights.


Feel free to message me if you've got any other questions about the neighborhood!

It is a multi-ethnic area, everyone is welcome in Astoria.  That said, there is a Jewish population, albeit, maybe not as large as other Queens areas.  We are close enough to other neighborhoods that claim a stronger Jewish population, that you could always visit to immerse yourself in whatever portions of your culture might be lacking here.  Regardless,  you will find a warm welcome should you decide to move to Astoria.
