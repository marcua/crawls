Sorry to hear about this.  The board's powers depend upon whether the nuisance neighbor is an owner or a tenant on a sublease.  Your status as an owner or a tenant could affect your strategy too.  Broadly, you have a right to quiet enjoyment of your apartment in either scenario and anyone who is causing a nuisance is either violating their lease or the shareholders' agreement.  It is a breach of contract situation and the board does have remedies available to them.  You need to keep a contemporaneous log of the problems and provide more information about whether you are dealing with sub-lease tenants or owner-occupiers.  Hope this helps.

If the neighbor is causing a noise nuisance, you should contact the coop board (in writing) with specifics on the type of noise, the hours that it is occurring and so on.  You should carefully read the coop board rules or by-laws to see what obligations the board has to investigate or otherwise take action to remedy the situation.  If your property is being damaged, you should file a police report.  I also recommend maintaining an incident log (noise incidents, contact with the neighbor, calls, letters, calls to police or reports) that you might need to show to any third parties to prove your case.

I am not sure what you mean by 'contentious email thread', do you mean this neighbor is writing you email messages or otherwise harassing you by email?
