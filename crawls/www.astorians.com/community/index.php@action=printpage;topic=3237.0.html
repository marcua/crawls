YES, or like someone already said, I will gladly take my business elsewhere. And I won't have a problem telling the manager, with a  :-), why I'm not shopping there anymore. It's pretty obvious managers know this, almost every want ad I read, no matter the business, wants applicants who are  "friendly/personable/upbeat".

I know all of my immediate neighbors. (Part of this is from my policeman dad - "Cathy Jo, go meet those folks so if you have to pound on their door at 3 a.m., without a stitch between you and the world, they'll know ya and open the door!" Which has turned out to be good advice....but that's another story.) But also because I don't want to be the "average" New Yorker, being so removed from their neighborhood, just existing and not living WHERE they live. Ya know?

I eased into it. I started by smiling at them a few times, then exchanging "Good Morning's" and finally introducing myself when I saw them outside. Did they all think I was el loco? Of course! Italians next door, Greeks across the street, Spanish and Indian next door. We all have accents! None of them knew what to think of me, but now, a year later, we wave and have actual conversations. Talib, the little boy next door always asks questions about Texas and, like any good Texan, I'm always willing to brag share. Do I talk to his dad and uncle? Not usually more than a respectful nod and hello, but I've gotten pretty friendly with his mom and aunt. It's not always easy - their English is a lot better than my Urdu!

Do I walk down the mean streets of NYC with a huge grin directed at every passerby? Of course not. But I often do have a big smile because my husband is making me laugh, or we see an awesome old building, or if we get lucky and have one of those magical only-in-New-York moments or simply because I'm happy. I am in love with life, my husband and New York City and I express that, I don't understand how one can't. And, if one is enjoying life, how and why keep those wonderful feelings bottled up inside? Then what is life? Does being constantly solemn and void of expression make one more pious? Some may see this as the musings of a simpleton, but it kind of is simple....if you're happy and you know it - show it! Not for others but for yourself - to feel.
