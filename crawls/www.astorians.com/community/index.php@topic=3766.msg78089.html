To address a previous post, there's a GORJUS and accepting Presbyterian church in the City (i know, wouldn't it be great to have something in the NEIGHBOURHOOD, tho?) http://www.fpcnyc.org -- 5th Avenue @ 12th Street. It's SO FUNNY to hear the ladies talk about PDA!  We don't, really, we're a bit reluctant ...  I'm from farmland in way southern Maryland by the Eastern Shore, been out forever and up here since '82, and my husband is from Jersey and went to a Jesuit High School in the City, so it's not like we're shy or new to The Life ... but it just seems weird.  To look at us we look absolutely to type for 45 year old bears and I KNOW everyone in the neighborhood knows we're married.  In the last year, though, we've see two or three different couples of womyn walking down Ditmars holding hands and we're like DAMMIT ! let's DO IT !  :-) and if we've had some beers, late at night, we WILL ... so ... baby steps and thanks for the courageous example, ladies!  We've noticed in the years we've been here what we PRESUME to be a nice and growing gay presence and I could not be more pleased living where we do.  Can't wait to put our name in for the Dinner emails!  Sounds very very exciting.
