Mace is tricky. And pretty much totally useless when there's more than one assailant, which is the situation this poor girl found herself in.

Really it all comes down to staying alert, using a buddy system like the prev. poster did, and improvise. Also, never second guess your gut instinct -- you get the "heebie jeebies" for a reason, so trust it and err on the side of caution.

Learning basic self-defense can be useful, too. A good class will also emphasize how to avoid or get out of a situation w/out resorting to violence. Again, self-defense isn't likely to be effective against more than one attacker (unless you're a martial arts guru).

And if someone pulls out a weapon, knife or gun, just give them what they want -- that girl's lucky all she got was a busted nose. It could have been horrifically worse. Only if you feel that they will harm you regardless, then you have to fight back.

That reminds me, I could use a refresher self-defense course, and maybe get my wife to take one -- does anyone know where to go?
