It was great celebrating Pi Day at school yesterday.  We had our students create a paper pi-chain that was color-coded by digit.  This was a great way to show there were no patterns in the digits.  We also held a pie baking contest open to students and staff.  In order to become a judge for the contest, you had to take part in another contest:  Pi Digit Memorization.  Students had spent time memorizing the decimal digits of Pi.  Our top winner, an 8th grader, had memorized 92 digits on command.  Another 8th grader came in second with 86 digits in her memory.  Tied for third place were two of my 7th graders memorizing 70 digits each.  My vanilla chocolate chip pudding pie came in second place!  A great time was had by all!
