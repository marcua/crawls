You can have a very strong economy with a weak USD. The recent changes in the FX rate (The EURO is at 1.4) are because of the lowering of the fed fund rate on tuesday. The Macro result of this is that USD denominated securiies will not be in as strong of demand since they will be paying less in interest. As a result, foreign govt's who may own USD bonds, may sell them (and therefore sell USD) and buy higher yielding securities in other currencices. The benefit of having a weak USD is that goods produced in this country are cheaper to overseas markets. There are too many reasons to go into right now as to why this decision was taken, but the gyst of it, inflation not a concern and it is proving hard to borrow money right now with the sub-prime mortgage crisis.

The CAD has been very strong for a while. Their economy has been doing very well, primarily driven by the many raw materials, primarily oil and wood. So basically if someone wants to buy a canadian oil stock, they will needto buy CAD, which results in that currency appreciating.
