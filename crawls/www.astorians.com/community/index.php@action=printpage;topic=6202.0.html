The split makes sense to me.  Think about it - posters on this board go to 36 Ave, Broadway, 30 Ave, Astoria Blvd and Ditmars to catch the N/W, but anyone who takes the R is either at the Steinway or 46th St stations.  Less to choose from, and if you've seen how packed Broadway and 30th can get in the morning, well - I'll buy that more people who consider themselves "Astorians" are riding the N/W train.

I do like the flexibility.  With the way the MTA has been the past few years, it can't hurt to have backup options.

I'm three blocks from the N/W.   Yeah, as a kid I lived for about 4 years under the rusty glare of the J train at Cleveland Street, where you could just look up from the second floor true railroad apt., and see the wheels and the sparks fly far closer to the Brooklyn digs than they do here.  As a 10-year old kid I don't ever recall being roused from sleep by the train;  sure, it was always there but you sort of put it out of your mind.  The other day while walking below the south-bound N/W track I was speaking to someone.  I could no longer hear what the person was saying when it was directly overhead.
