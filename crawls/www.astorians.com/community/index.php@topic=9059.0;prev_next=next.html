GRR! My bike got stolen today.
« on: December 22, 2007, 04:57:39 PM »
Right outside the Best Buy on Northern Boulevard. Broad daylight, plenty of people around. I was in the store no more than 10 minutes. Of course it was locked up, but maybe my lock wasn't up to LIC standards. They didn't even leave the cut cable. The bike was expensive too, not just a beater. Meant more to me than the money alone. If you see a red full-suspension Mongoose being ridden by someone who obviously can't afford it, call the cops for me! I'll reward you!

I reported it to the 114th precinct, though I know that won't do anything but make myself feel better. Maybe they'll bust some kid for drugs one day and find a garage full of hot bikes. Wishful thinking. Now how am I supposed to hang out at Randall's island next spring?

Owning a bike in Astoria is great, just make sure you buy the biggest chain you can find!

</end rant>

Unfortunately BigBryan is mostly right.  It is mildy worthwhile to register with 114th since it is a free service (and one can hope that if enough people do register their bikes maybe the service will get more attention) but basically unless you find the stolen bike yourself or the police bust a ring of thieves its not going to get your bike back to you the same way your license will get your car back to you.  Cops just don't have the time to go looking for stolen bikes.  But if you have the time, register you bike anyways.

Every so often you see these "my bike got stolen" threads, and for any potential bike owners I'd like to say, don't let this discourage you from riding in the city.  People ride in the city every day.  A little common sense; spending some dough on a quality bike lock (like the one kidmurdock linked), making sure that things that can be easily removed aren't left on the bike (I hate easy release tires either don't buy a bike with them or spend the money on the tire locks at your local bike shop) and don't leave your $3k racing bike laying around and you should be ok.  When my bike was stolen it was mostly my fault for being careless, the way I locked it I was asking for it.

But you should ride your bike whenever possible.
