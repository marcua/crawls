Title: Fairway in Red Hook - what about Astoria?
Post by: lanseaux on May 22, 2006, 01:05:26 PM
So now that Fairway has opened in Red Hook, of all places, (and Whole Foods is coming to Brooklyn - Gowanus) it got me to thinking:  what do we have to do to attract a serious supermarket to Astoria?

Astoria's been getting plenty of "buzz" over the past few years, and we've got small specialty markets - which are great, don't get me wrong - but we're still stuck with crappy - average at best - supermarkets.  I do a lot of local shopping, and I don't think that would change, but I really wish I didn't have to shlep into Manhattan when I do want to go to a real supermarket.  I mean, how ridiculous that in a Greek neighbourhood there's no decent place to buy fresh fish?  (I'm talking up by Ditmars, not 30th Avenue, which is too far for me to walk just to grab some tuna or dry scallops for dinner.)  That's just one example.  I'd also really like reliable access to organic produce and meat, not to mention ingredients that aren't usually found at the local Key Food. 

What would it take to get one (any one, not necessarily Fairway or Whole Foods) here?  Has anyone heard any rumours?  Anyone interested in starting a petition?  Would that have any chance of success?  Should we focus on convincing stores already here to improve their offerings?
