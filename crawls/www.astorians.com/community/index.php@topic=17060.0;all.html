As far as I know, to post the image you have to save the image to an online photo-share site, such as PhotoBucket.  Using that site, I've always saved the image to my computer first, and uploaded it from there.  I'm not sure if you can upload it straight to PhotoBucket from the internet.  Once you've got the image stored in your album a menu will appear when you mouse over it, and you can select "direct link".  Copy the link, and back here on Astorians click the image icon.  What you'll be pasting in is the url of the image from your photo-share album.  What you pasted was the url of the website.  Whether you use PhotoBucket or another photo-share website, make sure you store your image in any subfolder you might want to create first, otherwise the link will probably break if you move it later, and all that will remain in your Astorians post is a little red x in a box.  So, it's choose a photo-share website, open your account, set up your main folder, set up any subfolders, save the image to your computer (if neccessary), upload it to the photo-share website, move it to a subfolder if you want to, choose "direct link" or whatever it says, copy that url, paste it here using the image icon.

This is my source for all candies nostalgic:

http://www.nostalgiccandy.com/index.aspx

I like them because, unlike a lot of other websites, you don't have to order a box of something.  It's like an old-fashioned candy store where you can really pick and choose exactly what you want and exactly how much you want.  If it hadn't been for this I might never have tried an Abba-Zabba bar.  I see it comes in chocolate, too.  They are very generous with coupons.  They frequently e-mail you 10% off coupons, which can make up for the shipping cost.  Unfortunately, they don't have Ice Cubes, at least not at present.  You can ask them to carry them.

I loved candy cigarettes and candy lipstick when I was little.  I bought some from Nostalgic Candy a few years ago.  They're still fun.  One thing about the candy necklaces they sell - they're made of Smarties.  The original candy was a hard disk, Smarties are soft.  Maybe too many kids choked on the original?  I preferred the original, though, it lasted longer, in terms of eating it.  Smarties dissolve too quickly.

Remember Flying Saucers?
http://www.nostalgiccandy.com/search.aspx?find=flying+saucers&log=false&category=2

When life gets crazy, there's nothing like a flying saucer to put the fun back in dysfunctional.
