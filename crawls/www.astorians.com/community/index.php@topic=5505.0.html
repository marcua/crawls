Am I the only one who thinks these new luxury condos going up right across from Queensboro Plaza are a strange business venture? When I first saw the banner on the corner of the block that contains Scandals, I thought it was an ad for something that would be somewhere else. Nay, the condos are going up right on that corner. Their website, www.view59.com, gleefully ignores what the immediate area is like. They have some pictures of the 59th Street bridge, kind of overlooking the fact that it's right outside your window which doesn't seem like a big draw to me. Not to mention that at least 1/4 of those condos will have a terrific view of Queensboro Plaza. I'm intrigued by whoever thought this was a good idea.

Christine said:
Quote
I don't believe it's a secret that the neighborhood is currently seedy. So was Times Square not so long ago. So was the Meatpacking District (still is, but not by luxury real-estate standards.)

The Queens Plaza area of Long Island City isn't really comparable to any neighborhood in Manhattan. One key difference is they don't have above-ground subway stations that are rusting away, with various trains squealing by every couple minutes. I can't imagine being happy living that close to Queensboro Plaza unless I was very poor and that's all I could afford. But the idea of buying some sort of luxury apartment there is ridiculous to me, regardless if they install some SimCity trees and park space.

The planning of that area when it was first developed apparently didn't take into account that it might ever be a desirable neighborhood, and it's going to be a long, expensive battle if the city hopes to turn that area around. It's like that episode of The Cosby Show where he was talking to one of his kids about the importance of presentation, giving an example of cooking up a delicious steak dinner and then pulling the lid off a trash can on which to serve it. Unless they completely flatten those blocks and build everything from anew with a different mindset, that station will always be the trash can lid. But with the frequency and proximity to the station that these new buildings are going up, developers don't seem to see this as a problem. All I know is I wouldn't want to hear trains screaming to a stop every two minutes while I'm trying to sleep, watch TV, entertain guests, or whatever else I would choose to do in a luxury apartment.
