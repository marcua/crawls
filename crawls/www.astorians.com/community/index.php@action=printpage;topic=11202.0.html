I am steadfastly against the construction of affordable housing in Astoria.

The construction of affordable housing will result in a small percentage of the rental units being "affordable housing".  They will be ugly, they will be poorly designed and they will soon become the highly coveted domains of lucky citizens who pass these rental units down from generation to generation until the city decides to let them go condo, whereupon they become subject to market forces and allows a handful of people to fall backwards into being millionaires.  Furthermore, the construction of these units will not lower the overall rents in Astoria.  The existence of affordable housing will increase the demand for housing in Astoria, so that the pre-construction market equilibrium will be obtained within a few years (a great parallel are traffic studies and the traffic generation which accompanies each new traffic lane or redundant bridge).  This will cause an increased population density in Astoria, which, without a massive public investment in our mass transit options, will result in increased number of posters calling myself and my co-workers corrupt incompetents in the "my subway ride to work sucked" thread, which of course, is always an informative pleasure to read.

I know its not a trump card, but as an urban planner, I have seen the government attempts at housing, either low or middle income.  At best, they are grudgingly passable uses of public space and monies.  At worst, they are absolute train wrecks which destroy neighborhoods and make a few contractors rich at the expense of schmucks like me.  For the most part, housing is a private matter and ought to be built by private interests who are regulated by the government.  Public housing is a viable option not in neighborhoods which have a robust housing market, but rather in ones which do not.  They can also be useful when it is advisible to increase population densities and a conscious effort is made to do so.  Finally they can be viable in areas where the private interests are not willing to invest in and the use of such public monies is actually an investment as a pallitive for the lower classes.  Astoria does not fit into any of those three.  Building affordable housing in Astoria will have the same effect on housing prices as us destroying our national treasures to drill for oil.
