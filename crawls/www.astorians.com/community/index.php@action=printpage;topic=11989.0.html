Title: Re: Best Cheesesteak in Astoria (Is there even such a thing?)
Post by: goldfish boy on September 28, 2008, 02:03:52 PM
Quote from: Pulled pork on September 27, 2008, 10:12:44 PM
Ribeye, really?  I thought people are sayig it's a low quality meat.  Ok, so what's the cooking process to get the final meat product?

It's probably Grade Z ribeye.

The cooking process varies.  Start with steak cut extremely thin, then:

(steak shop style) pile masses of slices up on the grill with chopped onions and veg oil (water at Ishky's), portion off to order and drop it on the grill, flip a few times until it's cooked, slide some onions over to join the meat, put slices of cheese on top, then put the open roll on top and turn the sandwich over with the spatula

(pizzeria or diner or outdoor pushcart style) squirt some oil on the grill, drop fresh or frozen meat slices on, drop chopped onions on, use metal spatula to chop the meat up while it's cooking and scramble it with the onions, drop cheese on steak and onions, then then put the open roll on top and turn the sandwich over with the spatula


The main difference is whether you end up with intact or nearly intact slices or whether the steak is chopped up in little pieces.  Both are authentic; you know what to expect depending on where you purchase it.

The roll is crucial, and it's the hardest thing for out-of-towners to get right.  It's got to be an Italian roll, with lots of air on the inside and crusty on the outside.  It takes some effort to tear the crust, it shouldn't be soft.  I went to the other 99 Miles location once, and the roll was totally wrong.  It was more like a hot dog roll, with a soft crust and wonder bread-y insides.  Wrong.  But from your post it seems they may have rectified that.
