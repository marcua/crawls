When I used to make drip coffee while in grad school on LI, I used 2 Tbs. of beans per drinking cup of water.  I'd often fill my mug with water to measure out what a "cup" was.  Then grind the beans right then and there, dump them in the basket and brew the coffee.  Was pretty good!  At home we now use a Bialetti stovetop espresso machine, which works well, too.

I'm a purist - I don't like any flavourings in my coffee -- just beans and water.  I've been hooked for a few years on an organic sumatra dark roast I get from a little coffee store in Park Slope called Java Joe's - they're on 8th St. just off of 7th Ave. (betweeen 7th/8th).  They've got a website but I can never find it.  If I can't make the trek, they mail the beans to me the day they get them in, for about the price of a round trip subway ride.  I use 56 grams of beans (6 scoops) for 36 oz. of water.  We have a burr grinder.  We use a French press (and sometimes alternating with Toddy), so I don't know how the coffee/water ratio would translate to a drip maker, though.

Ok, how about one shot espresso, one shot condensed sweetened milk, touch of ancho powder, touch of ground cinnamon all mixed together (warm) and poured over home made vanilla ice cream.  Or espresso powder instead of liquid espresso coffee and use 2 shots condensed milk???

Damn, Jonathan, you need to set me up with some of that. Sounds delicious! I'll bring the espresso (I got an espresso maker for Xmas and it has become my new best friend)!

As for tips, obviously fresh ground is nice if you can get it. I don't have a grinder, but I love Illy, so I buy that. And this may be a "duh" for everyone else, but I was clueless: don't keep coffee in the fridge/freezer. Keep it in an airtight container on your counter. Only keeps about a week this way, so don't grind (or buy) more than you can use in a week. I break this rule regularly, but that's the ideal I guess.

Also, a friend of mine swears the water has to be nice and cold. I think tap cold should be fine.

And speaking of condensed sweetened milk, one of my great indulgences is thai iced tea, which largely consists of it. Yum!
