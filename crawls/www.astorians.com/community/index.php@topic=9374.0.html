Bikes, Parks, & Truck Routes: Are we Heading for a Greenway Collision?
The Queens Greenway: How to Get it Built the Right Way

What: Spend an evening with our guest speaker, Milton Puryear of the Brooklyn Greenway Initiatives. He offers to share with us the valuable lessons he's learned as a community activist while working with government officials, real estate developers and funders over a ten year period. We will spend the last half hour strategizing about the challenges presented in the proposed Queens East River Greenway and how to get it built the right way. The time is ripe to make a difference!

When: Thursday, February 21 from 6:30pm-8pm

Where: Greater Astoria Historical Society, Quinn Building, 35-20 Broadway, 4th Floor (at the corner of 36th Street)
Subway: Take the N/W train to Broadway

Sponsored by: Green Shores NYC, www.greenshoresnyc.org and the
Transportation Alternatives Queens Committee, www.transalt.org and http://groups.google.com/group/TAWQ

Please RSVP to Karen.Overton@parks.nyc.gov

There is a proposal for a greenway along the waterfront, although along is a subjective term.  Depending on where along the proposed greenway we are talking about it's either some waterfront access or Vernon blvd or squeezing along the 40 feet of space that Costco had to give use (from dusk till dawn only though).  Our waterfront, like Brooklyn's, is a combination of commercial, residential and industrial, some owned by city, some owned by parks, some owned privately, and requires a lot of maneuvering to get things done.  Milton Puryear, the fella who is presenting tonight, has already been through this in Brooklyn and has is more than willing to give out pointers and advice to avoid the same road bumps he's had to master. 

But the short answer is, yes there is a proposal for an east river greenway, its just not a very good proposal in my opinion.
