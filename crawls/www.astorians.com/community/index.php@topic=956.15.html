Roast it, baybee!

Peel, de-seed, and cut up the squash into 1/2 cubes.  Peel a head of garlic and cut each big clove in half, the other ones leave alone.  Douse with exviroloil, kosher salt (can I just emphasize the awesomeness of kosher vs. iodized salt?!?!?), and thyme.  Dump into a covered casserole dish or glass pan/foil and roast at 400 degrees for an hour and 15 minutes minimum (I like to cook it for an hour and a half, myself).  It will be the best freakin' squash EVAH.  The salt helps bring out the sugars and things carmelize, yum.  The squash gains a buttery texture, and has the most amazing sweet yet savory taste.  The thyme is magic.

This is what I'm going to do with my squash this week.
