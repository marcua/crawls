Title: Vesta - 30th Avenue and 21st Street
Post by: mangopaco on October 17, 2008, 10:46:22 PM
My wife and I went there tonight to try it out.  It was a fun place, with good food, good wine, and an awesome dessert.

The place is cozy with exposed brick walls, art and photos on the wall, and wine bottles on high shelves.  The staff was friendly, funny, and honest. 

The menu spoke of Italian cuisine and local markets, combining orrechietta, kale, and sausage in plain wonderfulness.  I had a striped bass over couscous that was pretty good.

I recommend their Fennel Orange salad.  Tasty and healthy as an appetizer. 

BUT, the cherry on top was the dessert.  Joe, who seemed to be the head honcho, recommended a toffee cake which he called the Baby Jesus.  He said it was a homemade cake that received a "Baby Jesus!" exclamation the first time someone tried it.  We had it and it lived up to its name.  We heard somebody at a nearby table describe it as Jesus in your mouth.  It had the atheists proclaiming the holiness of Jesus.  It was that good.

I think y'all should check it out.  It seems like a nice place, with friendly folks running it.  I'm looking forward to going back there. 

Oh, and the wine was good too!

Both salads were great (and the portions were a bit more generous than I was expecting, which was nice). The only thing that perplexed me was that I couldn't detect any fennel in my salad (it has a pretty distinct flavor). When I asked about it, I was told that the fennel was "shaved very thin", but for the life of me I couldn't taste it. But it was a very good salad anyway.

For entrees, my bf had the gnocchi with mushroom cream sauce, and I had the salmon with lentils and prune reduction. WOW-- both were fantastic. The gnocchi was tender and fluffy, the mushroom sauce very mushroom-y, and it had some toasted pine nuts on top which gave it a nice texture. The salmon was cooked perfectly-- tender, with a nice crispy crust around the edges. The sweetness of the prune sauce balanced nicely with the lentils. For sides, we got the grilled asparagus and creamy polenta-- both very nice. With all of these great dishes around me on the table, I couldn't decide where to stick my fork :)

Then of course, we had to get the jesus cake... and oh. my. god. YUM. I've never had a dessert quite like it. It was kind of like a spice cake, but not overly spiced. Nice toffee flavor. The cream on the side was perfect-- not overly sweet, which was a nice balance with the sweet toffee sauce. The cake was moist and warm, and had a nice little crust on top. Had a cappuccino too, which was really good.

Also, nice wine list, and I dig the "shot" option... you can order by the bottle, glass, or "shot" (a 2 oz. portion for about half the price of a glass). I often can't drink 2 full glasses of wine, so I love that I have the option to have just a little more, without having to buy a second full glass that I know I won't finish. Or, if you want to taste a bunch of different wines, that gives you a good option. Maybe Vesta should look into introducing flights?

Very happy to have this place in the neighborhood. We will definitely be back!
