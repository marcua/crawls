Title: Re: Switching to Green Power Electricity
Post by: merm on July 10, 2006, 10:15:27 PM
Those numbers don't look right to me. I think the costs you might be looking at are the supply + delivery costs. You need to be looking at the supply costs only, delivery costs will be in addition to the supply.

When you get a quote for power (even through ConEd Solutions Green Power) from any ESCO they are only quoting you the supply costs. ConEd then adds the delivery costs to that when you get your bill.

So, to compare apples with apples you need to get the supply cost for the green power, and supply costs for the non-green for the same month's period.

I'm VERY interested in finding out more about this. If the cost is close I'll switch too.
