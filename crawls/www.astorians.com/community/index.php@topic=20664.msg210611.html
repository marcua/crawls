Monday, March 7 at 7:00 pm - Greater Astoria Historical Society - THE SMITHSONIAN MUSEUM:  WILLIAM STEINWAY DIARY

A Gateway to the 19th Century: The William Steinway Diary 1861–1896”  Discover the history of the William Steinway Diary Project, one of the Smithsonian’s largest and longest-running volunteer efforts. Listen about first hand accounts of events that shaped our city’s history: Williams’s role in the creating of the New York City subway, the 1863 Civil War Draft Riots and Lincoln’s assassination, and the utopian community they created at the end of Steinway Street: the Steinway Settlement! 

Free and open to the public.

35-20 Broadway, 4th Floor, 718-278-0700
