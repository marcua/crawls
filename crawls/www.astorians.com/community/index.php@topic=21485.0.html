Health threatening and dangerous issues affecting our community.
« on: August 14, 2011, 02:01:32 PM »
As a concerned resident of Astoria Queens I would like to bring everyone's attention the health threatening and dangerous issues affecting our community.

http://www.flickr.com/photos/concernedastorian/sets/

1. Berry Fresh Farm Inc., located between Ditmars blvd. and 23rd. Ave. in Astoria Queens.

This supermarket, while providing an import resource to the community has been severely negligent in maintaining clean and safe garbage disposal practices on 43rd. Street between Ditmars Blvd. and 23rd. Ave. Over the years their business has grown and prospered. What has also grown is their volume of garbage and lack of community responsibility. I am submitting recent photos (link above) taken over a period of time that demonstrate the practice of substantially overfilling their small, few and broken garbage receptacles. The garbage carting company should also be held responsible and should refuse to remove their garbage if it overflows the top of the receptacle. By not doing so, when the garbage is picked up, the substantial overflow lands all over the sidewalk next to the entrance of resident’s homes. It then runs into the gutter and down the block. This turns 43rd. St. into an open sewer that attracts rats and all manner of bugs. The carting company does not clean this up. So this "Garbage Stew" remains for many hours putrefying on the street. During the hot summer months the odor from this "Garbage Stew" is unbearable. The block always smells like garbage. There are apartments and resident windows right next to the garbage and loading dock area and private homes located across the street. The residents, which include elderly and families with children, should not have to accept this irresponsible and disgusting practice. As the residents have helped in prospering this supermarket with it's patronage, Berry Fresh Farm Inc. in kind, should have a responsibility to the community by adopting safe and sanitary garbage disposal practices.

2. Berry Fresh Farm Inc.

Again, while providing an import resource to the community has been severely negligent in being sensitive to the community with regard to accepting deliveries from suppliers. As their volume of business has grown so has their delivery traffic. The large delivery trucks and Semi-trailer trucks double park sometimes 2 to 3 trucks at a time. There is no room for them to pull into the tiny loading dock that’s filled with refuse, supplies and empty crates. This practice prevents a resident’s ability to get to their car quickly especially in an emergency. Besides blocking access to resident’s cars the many idling trucks and exhaust also create a health concern for the community.
 
3. The Astoria Ditmars Subway Station Stairs, N & Q Trains..

Most of these stairs are potentially dangerous. Many are loose, uneven and cracked. Most of the metal and wood that makes up the stairs are rotting. They can no longer be repaired with more pieces of wood, screws, nails and paint. The attached photos (link above) are only a small sample of the many affected stairs. Does someone need to be injured before something is done? For the safety of the thousands of paying subway travelers to and from Astoria, every day, and the revenue they provide to the MTA the stairs need to be replaced.
