Coughing and Sneezing in Public
« on: April 23, 2007, 11:26:41 AM »
I was raised in the South and predominately on military bases, so maybe I have a different take on this than other folks, though I suspect not.  I was on the subway platform this morning with a guy about my age, and he was coughing up a storm, not covering his mouth or anything.  He did not seem to care in what direction he was hacking or on whom.  I watched him cough on at least three folks, one of whom was eating and saw person after person glare at him, but no one said anything.  What is up with these folks?  Just poorly raised?  I don't get it.
When I finally got to work, I was walking down 35th Street and had the person behind me sneeze all over my back.  I could feel the moisture.  I turned around somewhat startled and not only did he not apologize, but he glared at me.  I just turned back around and hurried to work. 
What happened to manners and common decency?  I would have been absolutely mortified if I'd sneezed on someone.
