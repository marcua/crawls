Knowing the difference between you and I and you and me.  It's a really simple rule.  Think of it the same as if you were only referring to yourself.  Seems like everyone says "you and I" exclusively these days because "I" sounds more propper or something, when in fact they are wrong. 

example...

"John gave free drinks to Bob and I" is WRONG!!!!!  You wouldn't say John gave a drink to I.  It would be John gave a drink to ME.  So when you add another person to the situation, the rule stays the same.  Propperly would be "John gave free drinks to Bob and me."

or John and me went to the store.  WRONG.  You wouldn't say "Me went to the store."

So next time you are referring to someone else and yourself, think about how the phrase would sound if it was only you that you were referrencing.
