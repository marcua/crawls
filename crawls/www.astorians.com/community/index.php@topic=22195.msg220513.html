Blocking a driveway isn't something that is normally ticketed for because it isn't illegal. They can only ticket or tow if someone complains about it. People who have driveways park there if they are multi car owners and it becomes almost private street parking. Best bet is to call 311 and ask what the procedure would be. Firstly- you didn't say if you have a sign posted. If not, then get one before you do anything. You might have to arrange with a Tow company that is registered to tow parking violators. Next time he parks there, leave a note that he will be towed the next time. Then arrange for the tow if he does.


You mention 'tension in the neighborhood', is he some neighborhood mob connected wanna be? If he blocks your driveway, he probably blocks others and never had anyone do anything about it, so he's not worried. Ask your neighbors about it- all it might take is one ticket or tow to stop. And then you would be the hero among the neighbors.

By driveway do you mean alleyway? There seems to be two problems here that need to be solved. The first one - someone is parking on your property, right? If that's the case tell them to move or leave a note that they'll be towed next time. If they don't stop you have a right to get them towed, like Thanos said. The other problem is - he is blocking the egress/easement. You or other neighbors most definitely can call 311- complain that a car is blocking the alley and you or they can't get through. An inspector will come out there-the same day. Hopefully the car will still be there when the DOB guy arrives. No one is allowed to block any of the residents' right to pass through the alley in case of emergency, fire or otherwise either with cars, or fences or gates. And no homeowner can block the other residents' right of way when they want to pass through by saying "It's my property. Back up and go the other way!" They cannot make an alleyway that's used communally by other property owners of the block to pass through their private parking space.
