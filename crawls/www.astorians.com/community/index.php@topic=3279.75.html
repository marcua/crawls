I think if we are to do anything as a community it needs to be intelligent and well thought out. I'm hearing that from you guys too. We have time to choose a response, I don't think we're at risk of losing the domain. If any action that we take is seen as "retaliation" it will just justify the very kind of behavior that we all loathe in the first place. I'm not against the community having a response, but I see Jayme's point in making sure that our response isn't in kind. I do not want to legitimize this person's paradigm by behaving the same way. I think that having a discussion about it here though might help us to decide what (if any) response we might want to have as individuals or as a community, so I welcome open and thoughtful discussion on what that might be. I genuinely appreciate all the input so far.

As far as what the community can do to help I think the best thing for us to do right now is just wait and see what kind of response we get from eNom. I do not believe that our domain is at risk, this is a simple matter of eNom believing (falsely) that I've misrepresented myself on the domain registration. This will be easy to prove once they look at the records. They just seem to be a bit slow (and a bit gullable). If people contact them directly it might make matters worse. Should this process drag on for a long time however a community response might help. Until then I think it's best to keep it simple, because it is still a fairly simple matter.

I also agree that the businesses listed on this person's site may not actually have given permission for their listing to be there (just as many businesses that are linked on astorians have not). Even if a business asks to have their link removed from a site, I don't think there's anything they can really do about it if the person doesn't comply (unless you can prove trademark infringement or something). Many websites simply link to lots of other sites to give them a higher rank in the search engines. By cross linking sites you can gain a higher search rating for sites you want to promote (say, sites you are responsible for designing or maintaining). I'm not saying it's a bad idea to notify business that they are linked there and let them know what you think, but, I wouldn't hold them responsible for being there on the first place since they probably don't even know they are listed there.

As for donations, they are welcome at any time. Luckily I haven't needed to retain the services of a lawyer or anything yet so related expenses are still reasonable.

Overall though I'd like any response to be one that mirrors the kind of behavior we wish we'd have gotten from this person in the first place. I'm personally tired of the tunnel-vision that "revenge" gives, and would like to see its effects minimized in our community. I'm not sure how to accomplish that (maybe it's not even possible, the mods know how I've struggled with  what to do), but I do like to think that when one (or many) have been wronged it presents a unique opportunity to raise the bar.
