Hi everyone:
 
I know that this issue has been discussed on these boards in the past few years, but I would like to get your informed opinion.
 
I just moved to Astoria this week, and a long-time resident of Astoria suggested that I get a window gate for my bedroom window that is accessible via the fire escape.  The window faces the back of the building and is on the fourth of five floors.
 
I have gotten estimates from several locksmiths in the area, and the consensus seems to be that the total cost would be $350 to $400 for one window (35 x 70).  I suppose this is a small price to pay to prevent burglaries and peace of mind, but your input is greatly appreciated!

Re: window gates -- worthwhile?
« Reply #10 on: June 03, 2011, 12:44:18 PM »
A far less expensive option would be a wooden dowel cut and insert between the top of the bottom window pane and the top of the frame, to prevent someone from sliding the window up from the outside and renter's insurance. I've been in Astoria for almost 10 years, and haven't heard of many burgalries, but with dowel that I mentioned, the burglar would have to smash through the window, which if they're dedicated enough to do that, the gates aren't going to stop them.
If they're really that dedicated, your renter's insurance (mine comes out to be under $20 a month) will cover any losses, something a gate won't do.
That said, if the gate makes you feel safer, go for it, but personally, I don't see them as necessary.
