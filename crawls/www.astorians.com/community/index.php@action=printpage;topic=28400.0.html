Title: Re: Tru Astoria cafe on Ditmars
Post by: TJT on November 11, 2014, 09:58:48 AM
We made it over to Tru last Friday evening and really enjoyed it.  It's definitely a mini-Avenue Café in every way except it's definitely toned down a notch.  No ridiculously sexy, overdressed hostess.  No pounding music at high volumes.  Actually really down to earth and very friendly service.  The décor is obviously the same designer as Avenue Café but I don't mind it.  The grilled halloumi was delicious as were the zucchini fritters.  My wife had pork chops that she really enjoyed.  My chicken kebob was delicious.  The rice was a bit bland but the portions are generous.  Cocktails were made just right and service was swift and they showed a lot of concern that we were pleased with everything.  We had delicious coffee drinks but things took a turn for the worse with the desserts.  The cheesecake was - honestly - disgusting.  Some ladies next to us got the brownies.  They looked so rich and delicious so I asked them what they thought.  They all made a face and said they were horrible.  Obviously they need to work on this.  Why bother trying to do something you're not good at when you can get delicious desserts from Artopolis or even Martha's and keep it local?  It looks like they do a very good breakfast and brunch.  From the crowd, it would seem as though the Ditmars area was dying for this kind of café.  It is incredibly popular already.  It can understand it being jarring for Ditmars at first, but I think they're trying to make it work without being over-the-top.  I will be back for sure.  It was an enjoyable time and nice to have that experience on Ditmars.  By the time we left at close to 9:30, there was not an open seat in the place.
