I dont' think it's necessary to throw everything out. Fabrics can be salvaged through washing and drying. Some items can be sprayed down with sterifab or another contact-kill spray. You can also wrap things in plastic and put them away - I dated everything to be opened 18 months from the storage date and put two layers of plastic around them and taped up everything so that NOTHING could get in or out.

Some things you may have to throw out, but if the problem is that your neighbor has them, or they are living in your walls, you only increase your costs and do not solve the problem. I threw a lot of things out just because I was so frustrated and paranoid. I later regreted it. Some things really did have to go - how was I going to sterilize my clock radio? - but I threw out a gorgeous quilt that I'm still bummed about losing. I wish I had just wrapped it in plastic for a few months.

I cannot recommend caulking highly enough. Once I caulked, sprayed, and put DE along all the baseboards (and glue traps under the legs of every bed) and we sterilzed or stored everything in the apartment, the problems stopped. I keep an eye on the caulking - it developed cracks during the winter, and I had to recaulk. A small price to pay.
