Title: Alternate route from Queensboro Plaza?
Post by: artgirl on October 04, 2005, 12:19:53 PM
At one time or another (and almost certainly more), this has happened to all of us:

N/W service gets screwy and the line shuts down between Manhattan and Queens. Sometimes, you can take the 7 to Queensboro Plaza, where they'll shuttle an N/W train back and forth between there and Ditmars.

:?: Question is, what to do when there's no N/W service at all? What's the best way to get to 30th Ave (and points North) along the N/W line?

It seems like the only way to get close to there is by taking a cab from Queensboro Plaza (which is what I end up doing). Yeah, it's only like $5, but that's still money I wasn't planning on spending. (I use a monthly Metrocard, so any other subway or bus rides are "free" to me)

Sure, I could take the R to Steinway & Broadway and walk from there, but it's about a 15-minute walk to 30th Ave... which I don't mind so much except when it's pouring rain or freezing cold (often the case when the subway gets screwed up).

Is there a bus that approximates the N/W route?

Or should I just suck it up and keep taking cabs in this situation? I know that's probably the "best" way, I'm just curious if anyone has alternatives.

thanks!

You can head over to 21st Street across Queensbridge Apts. and grab the 19 A bus. It goes straight up 21st Street all the way to Ditmars and then on to LaGuardia Marine Terminal area. Take caution, though -- Queensbridge can be an unsafe area.

Title: Alternate route from Queensboro Plaza?
Post by: artgirl on October 04, 2005, 02:38:01 PM
tuesday,

I've actually had good luck hailing yellow cabs there... I've found the best place to be is under the tracks where they bend around to 31st Street. Go to the East side of 31st Street (the right side for Astoria-bound cabs coming around that bend), where they'll be able to pull off to the side. If you try to get a cab before this point, there's not really a good place for them to pull over, and they'll pass you by. (Don't try to hail a cab headed toward Manhattan because at a certain point there's nowhere for them to turn around)

Just be careful walking over there, as you have to cross some turn lanes with cars whipping around corners... wait for your walk light, even the traffic signal is red.

(Since so many cab drivers are based in Queens, sometimes they'll stop for you even if they're off-duty because it's not really out of their way to make a close stop.)
