yeah marathon realty sucked and the women in there acted like the poop dont stink!  I found a great 1.5 BR for 1100 people!  There is HOPE!

To all the shady realtors out there that tell you there is no way you can find a decent 1br for less than 1200, i say bs.  Granted it all depends on your circumstances and how much time you have to look and how persistent you are with your search.  If you are careful with your planning and know Astoria is where you want to move, start looking at least a month and half before or else you are just gonna have to take whatever you cant get.

You cant expect the real estate agents to call you to follow up, because many of these people dont event get much of a cut from the fee they charge you, and they have tons of other people calling them to find a place.  I must have met every real estate agent in astoria (seriously), and I made a list and would call them every monday to check what they had, or check on their websites.  It took me 3 months and I must have seen about 60+ apartments.  If you have the time then you can really find a good deal, craigslist is by far the best source. 

Sometimes just walking in to the real estate offices on a sat afternoon helps, because something always pops up (like a cute $950 small 1br i saw right by 35th and 30th ave one time).  Its a renters market, and if people are paying right up front what the landlords want, then landlords are only going to demand more $$.  Also, a good real estate agent will NEGOTIATE on your behalf, most of the time every apartment is negotiable (unless its already under market value and has lots of renovations).  However be mindful of the "I can get it down $50 less if you sign a 2 year lease" schemes. 

People's experiences will probably vary with agent and agency, important is to find an agent you like, that is patient and that knows what you need and wont waste your time showing you stuff that is not for you.  That being said you still have to call them and follow up and cant expect that they are going to be calling you.  The early bird catches the worm not the one that was waiting for the worm to pop up.
