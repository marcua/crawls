I always assume low flying helicopters mean someone's been firing a gun and the police are after them.  You have to check what kind of markings the helicoptor has (NYPD, for instance) and if it's circling.  Of course a searchlight is a dead givaway - that the criminal out to give up and give themselves away!  If the helicopters are going in a straight line between Manhattan and LaGuardia, then it's probably a commuter craft.  If they stay mainly over the river it could be a sightseeing helicopter.  If they're hoving over a bridge it could be a movie being shot.  3 helicopters at about the same height, hovering or moving around in a small area - I used to assume baaad traffic tie up, then I saw this scene over the area near Target in College Point and figured out long later this was the first report of West Nile and they were filming the insecticide spraying, and now when I see 3 helicopters, sadly I would assume crane collapse.  One big dark helicopter with two sets of blades on top - either we're at war, or hopefully, it's just an arial demonstration.
