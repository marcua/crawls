Here are some favorites of mine:

Julie Andrews, Greatest Christmas Songs.  This album used to be called A Christmas Treasure, and for reasons unknown to me, RCA has tacked on 3 year-round songs at the end, which you can play or not.  She has another Christmas album called Christmas with Julie Andrews, which ought to be good.

http://www.amazon.com/Greatest-Christmas-Songs-Julie-Andrews/dp/B00004XR5U/ref=pd_bxgy_m_text_b

The Holly and the Ivy, sung by the choir of Clare College at Cambridge U., conducted by John Rutter.  It also had a former name, Christmas from Clare.

http://www.amazon.com/Holly-Ivy-Carols-Clare-College/dp/B0000041VV/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=music&qid=1228626590&sr=1-1

And this is an anthology, but a delightful one: Sleigh Ride!.  My favorite tracks are Victor Herbert's "March of the Toys" and Handel's "Or Let the Merry Bells Ring 'Round."

http://www.amazon.com/Sleigh-Ride-Classic-Christmas-Favorites/dp/B000003FCB/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=music&qid=1228626833&sr=1-1

This album I've heard only in the 30-second sample clips at Amazon.com, but they're enough to make me want the album:

Yo-Yo Ma et al., Songs of Joy and Peace.  Not all the songs on the album are actual Christmas songs, but when taken together they have a Christmasy feel.  And no, it does not include  "River."

http://www.amazon.com/Songs-of-Joy-Peace/dp/B001BN1V8U/ref=pd_sim_m_69
