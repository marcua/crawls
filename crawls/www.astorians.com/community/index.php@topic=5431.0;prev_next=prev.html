The good, liberal shredded wheat side of me can't help but agree to a point that women don't always get a fair shake in comics.  But the I've been reading Marvel since I was ten sugary sweet side can't help but think of Storm, Ms Marvel, She-Hulk (a tough hero AND a lawyer and one of my personal favorites), Elektra, Sue Storm, the Black Widow and others. 

I've heard from other female friends that complain that comic books are mainly for boys, and they're not wrong, but most of those friends never read comic books in their life, let alone the indies that don't even feature super heroes and probably would be more in line with what a non-tightwearing fan would want to read.  What do you feel mainstream comics should do to entice more than the handful of women I see at my comic store on wednesdays?  And have you read the now defunct Alias by Brian Michael Bendis?  Jessica Jone is a failed costumed heroine who gave up "the biz" and now works as a P.I and deals with weight and image issues, relationship issues and has a bit of a drinking problem.  I was a big fan and I felt that since it was high on personal issues and low on action it could be termed as, for lack of a better word, girlie.

The good news re: 'ol Wing-Head, is that the issue where he dies is called The Death of the Dream Part 1, which means there will be a part 2, and indeed Captain America #26 is due sometime in May.  So here's hoping Steve Rogers will be back soon.
