Why There Are Not Many Bookstores In This Area
« on: June 12, 2007, 06:03:08 PM »
I think there are not very many bookstores in the area because there are a lot of libraries.  A LOT of libraries.

In case anyone doesn't know, the Queen Public Library System is separate from the other boroughs, so in New York City you need two library cards to experience true library joy.

Here's a bonus point connected to the subject of libraries:

If you look at the QPLS (and NYPL) you can get a list of all the locations of the library and their hours.  Going to a branch you've never been to before is a great way to get to know a neighborhood.  It gives you a starting point, and, important for walking around - there's sure to be a bathroom there.  Cheap entertainment (walking around) and you can take out books from any branch and return it at your local one.  I'm not sure if they made that return-locally policy available for movies took, though I think so.  And, speaking of cheap entertainment - there are always events going on at the libraries, and most of them are free.

I was actually shocked when I moved here to see how bad the library system is in New York. I came from Pittsburgh, a much poorer city, but they had an absolutely amazing central library and terrific branch libraries.

Stupidest thing I've ever heard of.

Most of the time I just go to the Brooklyn Heights library near work, which isn't much better.
