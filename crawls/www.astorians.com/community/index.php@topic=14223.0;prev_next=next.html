The Mitten King at Ditmars N/W Stop
« on: April 21, 2009, 10:39:42 AM »

My name for this fellow is a little bit of a misnomer, but I always liked how it sounded better than The Glove King.

Does anyone know anything about the guy who stand in the east side of the Ditmars subway platform and looks down at his glove covered hands? I'm always really worried about him. He wears the same clothes days each day and spends a good portion of him time in that station. When I have seen him coming or going, he goes down Ditmars Blvd., but I'm not sure were to. He never really speaks to anyone (only himself from what I have seen), much less asks for money, so maybe he has some family that help support him?

My heart really goes out to him. Maybe it is because he seems less able than some homeless/mentally off-kilter people that I see on the streets.

Once I picked up on the name "Jazz Hands," I was able to find a bit more about his from this board.

The Cadillac Man made this comment on him a year or two ago. This quote is for the rest of us that missed that discussion.

"My dear friends
A quick lesson about street life.
To those who had harsh words for the singer at the Ditmars station,something you should know.
Name is Harold.early 60s and lives in a furnished room no bigger than a closet,(I know where,but not telling.)
No family,no source of income other than what he receives from singing.
Once had a life,working for a major record outlet as a floorman/salesman for over thirty years.
He knew where every 45 and LP albums was within the store and had a vast knowledge of music etc.
Automation was his downhill,when music was placed on CDs.
Told that his services were no longer needed and like albums and such,tossed in the dumpster/street.
Indeed he has slight mental problems,not maintaining his appearence or eating properly,he just doesn't care anymore.
Sole purpose now is to make enough to pay rent and a few dollars for food
Looks are everything to some but to me Harold to still a human being.
Cadillacman"
