Title: Re: French-friendly establishments?
Post by: Songbird on July 26, 2006, 02:07:25 PM
I'm sure your friend has already come and gone, but I have to sing the praises of Le sans Souci. 

Let me preface this by saying that I went to a french school from age 2-12, and have been mistaken for French most of my life as soon as I start speaking it.  I've lived in a small town in southern france for five summers, and feel more comfortable in France than in many states of the union (like Texas).

When I walked into Le Sans Souci, I was greated by a hansome young man who spoke to me in French.  We had a long discussion about music as I ate some of the best food I'd had since moving to New York.  It was a croque Monsieur, and it tasted just like the ones I'd had at the local diner in Villeneuve-sur-Lot. 

I was invited to come back and hangout, and it's been my favorite hangout ever since.  It's a wonderful place to just go and have a glass of wine, and enjoy the French atmosphere.  The garden in the back is lovely, and hopefully they'll be starting up French film night again soon.

Chef Eric is a genius, making incredible food with the perfect balance of fresh ingrediants.  I recomend getting the Prix Fixe, which is always they're specials for the day. 

Le Sans Souci rocks!  Try it out.
