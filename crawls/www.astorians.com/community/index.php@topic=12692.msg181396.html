I stopped in about 7:30 on Saturday evening. A sign on the door said that last call for food was 8:30 as there was a private party at 9 or 9:30. Funny, I had just checked out their menu on line and saw now mention. The bar was playing rather loud headbanger alternative music, which I found incongruous with the very German themed interior. I really didn't care but it was a little strange. The Met / Yankee game was on -several TVs- and there was a definite leaning towards the Yanks with the interior wall Yank pennants and player photos and the waiter's Yankee logo-ed shirt and cell phone holder ... really!

I went with my gold standard of honest Teutonic sit down stick to your ribs fare: Sauerbratten. $16.00.  I got the "Big Boy" 1 liter beer, I think it was like $11.00 (Imported, potent and took me all night to finish)

It came with a kind of noodle side ( I really can't remember it's German name but it took up half the plate and was satisfactory.), a 1/2 slice of rye bread and a side plate of white and red saurekraut. The meat itself was tasty, but I've had better. I found the beef to be vaguely soggy/spongy and the gravy, as I like it, was a bit thinner and lighter than I am used to and prefer. It wasn't horrible, I've just had better. Maybe next time I'll try one of the 'vursts.

To compare what I mean by a darker, thicker gravy ... Koliba on 23 Ave off of 31 St ... the Beef Goulash.

http://www.kolibarestaurant.com/Main.php?do=Menu

It could be a regional thing.. I like the more robust gravies with my Sauerbratten.
