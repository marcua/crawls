I'm not seeing a "how" they are going to do, I'm seeing a "what" they are going to do. Obama has an even more comprehensive overview than they do:

On the Economy:
- Pass the STOP FRAUD act
- Obama will create a Homeowner Obligation Made Explicit (HOME) score,
which will provide potential borrowers with a simplified, standardized borrower metric (similar to APR) for
home mortgages.
- -Obama will create a fund to help people refinance their mortgages and provide comprehensive supports to
innocent homeowners.
- As president, Obama will work to eliminate the federal bankruptcy law’s Chapter 13 provision that
prevents bankruptcy courts from modifying an individual’s mortgage payments.
- Provide a Tax Cut for Working Families
- Eliminate Income Taxes for Seniors Making Less Than $50,000
- Obama will expand the existing Savers Credit to match 50 percent of the first $1,000 of savings for families that
earn under $75,000, and he will make the tax credit refundable.

Under each of those points there's a full explanation of exactly what his plan is, and what laws he would change or enact. If you spend some time looking around his site, you'll find complete reports at the bottom of each "issues" page that goes into great detail about the points that are summarized on each issues page. At minimum, these reports are just as detailed as any other candidate, and on most issues they go into more detail than the other candidates do.

But, based on what you've quoted above, I'm not seeing a "how" any politician will get their plans done (including Barack). How will Hillary "commit $1 billion annually in a Family Leave Innovation Fund" for example? It's not like the president gets a blank checkbook, how is she going to do that? She's going to have to convince a lot of people to write that kind of a check, and she doesn't say how she'll do that.

My point is just that Obama has laid out a pretty comprehensive overview of his approach, and that if you read candidate's plans side by side you'll see that none of them show step by step how they'll do it - but I don't think that can be expected anyway because the presidency isn't an all-powerful position in the U.S. The president will have to convince congress to approve all this stuff and write the check for any of it.

I'm more interested in seeing where a candidate's head is on issues. I don't expect them to give me step by step how they'll achieve it, they are only one piece of a much larger picture after all.
