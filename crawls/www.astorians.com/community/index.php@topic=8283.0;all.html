Athens Taverna
« on: October 14, 2007, 09:35:02 PM »
My husband and I went for a tasting tonight.  This is definitely not the norm.  It is quite high end, with a Chef brought in from Athens.  We tasted a number of items; they were all eclectic and delectable.  It is not cheap, but for a special occasion, I would recommend this.  Some of the items on their menu include:

Stuffed onion pearls with octopus in mavrodafni reduction
Grilled grouper fillet with basil sauce, served with black eye bean salad.
Roasted beef marinated with herbs & orange, served with mushroom mashed potatoes.
Rabbit with bergamot and mashed sweet potatoes.
Grilled dry aged black Angus steak with orzo pasta and mushrooms.

It is wonderful for a special occasion.  I believe that dinner for two, with a drink would run you between $75 to $95, depending on what you have.

The only other high end restaurants that would compare are Christos and Piccola Venezia.  I don't know of any others of that caliber in Astoria.

To return the thread to its original topic, I had the chance to eat at Athens Tavern last weekend.  There were definitely some very strong points about it but also some cons, in my opinion.

PROS:
- The menu is very out of the ordinary for a Greek restaurant, especially those in Astoria, which have mostly played it safe with their menus.  There's lots of innovative dishes here and a lot of creativity in selecting the menu.
- Most of the food I ate was delicious.  I particularly enjoyed the Yiouvetsi Thalasinon (Seafood Yiouvetsi, a variation of the common Greek dish Yiouvetsi, with is made with orzo pasta in a light tomato sauce).  No one at my table happened to try the rabbit, however, so I can't comment on it!
- The service was excellent, very prompt and attentive.
- The desserts, which were treated to us, were exquisite, I must say.
- Extensive and reasonably priced (compared to the food-see below) wine list.

CONS:
- The place is pricey by Astoria standards.  Don't expect to come here and have an affordable, filling Greek meal like you would at a place like Stamatis. 
- The portions are not terribly big.  I was especially surprised by how small the Horiatiki Salata (Village Salad) was, and for $11.  I understand that this isn't a Stamatis-esque type of establishment with heaps of home-style food on your plate, but this is not the place to go if you're stomach is totally empty!

Overall though I would recommend it just for the unique menu alone, as well as the fact that the food we did have was really well prepared, and the desserts were amazing.  But be prepared to splurge a bit....and if you're starving, you might be better off crossing the street and going over to Stamatis or Telly's if you still want something Greek :)

Anna, of Anna's Corner, sold it sometime in 2005 or 2006.  New management took over, and it really began to hit rock bottom.  Originally, with Anna, it was a Greek-style "psistaria" (grill is a rough translation) focused mainly on meat.  When new management took over, they changed the way a lot of the food was prepared, and it was much less authentic.  They also added bizarre items to the menu like shrimp scampi and penne a la vodka, which absolutely did not fit in at all.  Apparently the new management hopped out at the right time though, managing to sell it to the owners of Athens Tavern.

Athens Tavern was nice but way overpriced for Astoria, and some of the portions were very small.  The Greek Salad I ordered there, for instance, was unbelievably small...barely enough for 1 person...and it was the "large" (and the pricetag was large too, $10).

The thing that disappoints me is that no one has explored the full breadth of "authentic" Greek food.  It's more than just pastitsio, mousaka, souvlaki and grilled seafood.  When I've traveled to Greece, I've had a chance to eat at "mezedopoleia" (the Greek equivalent of tapas bars), which often have close to 100 different cold and hot "mezedes" (appetizers) to sample, much much more than the usual dips, calamari and grilled eggplant/zucchini that every Greek place in Astoria has.  I've been to "ouzeri" (like tapas bars but focusing on seafood-based appetizers, often served with ice cold ouzo).  "Psistaries" (grills) that have unbelievable meat dishes, more than just souvlaki and lamb chops.  Regional restaurants focusing on the cuisine of the different parts of Greece...some amazing dishes there as well.  The place that's come the closest is Agnanti but they've only scratched the surface (also, every time I went, service was AWFUL, but that's a different story).

I've found none of that stuff at Stamatis or any other Greek place, in Astoria or in the city for that matter.  It's really unfortunate, because people get a very limited view of what Greek cuisine is.  And all of those types of places I mentioned are pretty affordable...they're the types of places an average Greek family can go to in Greece.

Hopefully someone will try something new as far as Greek cuisine goes, without resorting to the tried-and-true (souvlaki! mousaka!) or going to the extreme opposite and bringing in something that's obviously too expensive for most people.
