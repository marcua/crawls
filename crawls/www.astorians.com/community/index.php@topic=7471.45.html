I took a date to JJ's on a Friday night because I'd heard such great things about it and it was a TERRIBLE experience. I freely admit that it was mostly due to having the worst service I've ever seen. I think it took the waitress 30 minutes from us sitting down to come over and take our orders, it took a long time for the food to come out and after another 30 minutes of waiting/trying to flag down the waitress or someone, finally I got up and went to the front to ask for our check and it still took another 10 minutes to get it.

It was early on a Friday night and it wasn't full yet and it was semi-busy but not packed (no wait, some empty tables) and they seemed to only have two servers for the entire place. It was clearly not enough.

The food was good, not spectacular. We had two appetizers which were both good. The pot stickers were tasty, but seemed overpriced for what they were. The sushi was good. Again, not amazing. The price seemed higher than the quality, especially considering the poor service.

I will have to go back again in order to give it another chance since everyone seems to think it's the best in Astoria. I really used to like Watawa, but I don't think Watawa is as good as it was two years ago. At this point I think I still prefer Watawa over JJ's though.

My favorite sushi place at the moment is Ten Sushi on 31st street between park and...3rd? Always excellent quality, good prices, nice atmosphere and I've never had a problem with the service (yet). Mind you, I go there for lunch and I'm not expecting terribly much from it. Just tasty, quality sushi at lunch special prices. I don't think I'd take someone there on a date or anything - it doesn't have much atmosphere.
