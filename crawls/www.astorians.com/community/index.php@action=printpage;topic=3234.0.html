As a result of moving to a new server several Astorians members have inquired about ways that they can help support Astorians.com

Wasabisam and I have come up with three ways that members can help.

1. Donate (https://www.paypal.com/cgi-bin/webscr?cmd=_xclick&business=support%40astorians%2ecom&item_name=Donation%20to%20Astorians%2ecom&no_shipping=1&return=http%3a%2f%2fwww%2eastorians%2ecom%2findex%2ephp%3ftopic%3d3234%2e0&cancel_return=http%3a%2f%2fwww%2eastorians%2ecom%2findex%2ephp%3ftopic%3d3234%2e0&cn=Your%20Astorians%2ecom%20Username%3a&currency_code=USD&bn=PP%2dBuyNowBF&charset=UTF%2d8) - The most direct way to make a difference. 100% of donations go directly to support the site.
2. Visit our online store (http://www.printfection.com/astoria) - Approximately 20% of all sales made through this store go to support Astorians.com. We'll be adding new products periodically so check back often!
3. Host your website on the Astorians.com server - Send a PM (http://www.astorians.com/index.php?action=pm;sa=send) to merm if you are interested in sharing server space.

More information: Astorians.com is hosted on a Virtual Dedicated Server, which means we have guaranteed resources and cannot be kicked off or slowed down by other random sites (like was happening on our old "shared with thousands of other sites" server). The yearly operating cost for this server is approximately $600 and it's our goal to keep the site banner-ad free. Donations, and sales that are made on this site will first go directly to support the cost of operating the server. Should there be funds available after all existing operating expenses are met, extra funds will be applied first to upgrading the server or software should there be a need, or towards the following year's hosting expenses. Should there be funds after all base costs, and upgrade costs are met then the remainder may either be held for future possible expenses, or used by merm to purchase Chinese takeout after many hours of working on the server keeping things updated. :)

Thanks to all who have expressed interest in supporting this site! If you do decide to support, please let us know by posting here!

Title: Re: Supporting Astorians.com - New Store, Donations, Site Stats
Post by: merm on September 01, 2006, 03:26:25 PM
At this point there's no plan for a donation meter, though I've (and the mods too) have done quite a bit of thinking and talking about it. I'm a bit resistant to that kind of thing for this site for two primary reasons:

1. Every site that I've been a part of that does that falls short every single month of the fundraising "goal". I don't want astorians.com to be in perpetual fundraising mode trying to reach a goal. It's depressing to see that the board administrator is having to foot the bill month after month, when this happens I'd rather it not be public (as has been the case for this board for the last 2 years.) I'm not looking for people to feel sorry for me, or for people to be motivated to support this idea out of guilt or because we're running short (see number 2). :)

2. Because I don't think of Astorians as a charity. It's a project I started, and then was assisted (sometimes in very significant ways) by people I've met along the way. I do it primarily because I love astoria and because when I first launched the site there really wasn't a board out there that I wanted to join. That being said however, if I was able to do Astorians (or other community building boards) for a living I'd absolutely do it. I'm totally into online community building. So if we ever grow to the point where we could support a full or part-time person (like me, or someone else) I don't want to set a precedence that the people that run this board must do it for free, or that they'd be working for a charity, or that the only costs to be considered are the hosting costs.

Which brings us back to number 1 really; that the "real" cost to operate this site is a lot more than $600/year because it involves the labor of professional people who give a significant portion of their time to keep things running and make things better. If we were to be honest and transparent about that, there's no chance at all that we could meet the "real" expenses that running such a board entails.

So at this point I just prefer to focus on transparency about how the funds will be used (see original post), but not focus on whether or not we are meeting any goal. If we meet expenses great, if we don't that's ok too. If we're over, then I don't consider that a waste.
