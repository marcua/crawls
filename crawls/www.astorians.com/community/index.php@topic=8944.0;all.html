Mace is tricky. And pretty much totally useless when there's more than one assailant, which is the situation this poor girl found herself in.

Really it all comes down to staying alert, using a buddy system like the prev. poster did, and improvise. Also, never second guess your gut instinct -- you get the "heebie jeebies" for a reason, so trust it and err on the side of caution.

Learning basic self-defense can be useful, too. A good class will also emphasize how to avoid or get out of a situation w/out resorting to violence. Again, self-defense isn't likely to be effective against more than one attacker (unless you're a martial arts guru).

And if someone pulls out a weapon, knife or gun, just give them what they want -- that girl's lucky all she got was a busted nose. It could have been horrifically worse. Only if you feel that they will harm you regardless, then you have to fight back.

That reminds me, I could use a refresher self-defense course, and maybe get my wife to take one -- does anyone know where to go?

A bigot is a prejudiced person who is intolerant of opinions, lifestyles, or identities differing from his or her own.

Racial discrimination is treating people differently through a process of social division into categories not necessarily related to "race". Racial segregation policies may officialize it, but it is also often exerted without being legalized.

Xenophobia is a fear or contempt of foreigners or strangers and people.[1] It comes from the Greek words ξένος (xenos), meaning "foreigner," "stranger," and φόβος (phobos), meaning "fear." The term is typically used to describe fear or dislike of foreigners or in general of people different from one's self.

Hate speech is a term for speech intended to degrade, intimidate, or incite violence or prejudicial action against a person or group of people based on their race, gender, age, ethnicity, nationality, religion, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, language ability, moral or political views, socioeconomic class, occupation or appearance (such as height, weight, and hair color), mental capacity and any other distinction-liability. The term covers written as well as oral communication and some forms of behaviors in a public setting. It is also sometimes called antilocution and is the first point on Allport's scale which measures prejudice in a society.

Antilocution is a term defined by psychologist Gordon Allport in his book the Nature of Prejudice, 1954. Antilocution defines verbal remarks against a person, group or community, which are not addressed directly to the target. Generally referred to as "talking behind someone's back," the impact of this is often overlooked. However because antilocution creates an environment where discrimination is acceptable, it frequently progresses to other more damaging forms of prejudiced behavior. Its use is overshadowed by the more modern term Hate speech which has almost the same meaning.

Allport's Scale is a measure of the manifestation of prejudice in a society. It is also referred to as Allport's Scale of Prejudice and Discrimination or Allport's Scale of Prejudice. It was devised by psychologist Gordon Allport in 1954[1].

Allport’s Scale of Prejudice goes from 1 – 5.

Scale 1, Antilocution- Antilocution means a majority group freely make jokes about a minority group. Speech is in terms of negative stereotypes and negative images. This is also called hate speech. It is commonly seen as harmless by the majority. Antilocution itself may not be harmful, but it sets the stage for more severe outlets for prejudice.

Scale 2 Avoidance -People in a minority group are actively avoided by members of the majority group. No direct harm may be intended, but harm is done through isolation.

Scale 3 Discrimination -Minority group is discriminated against by denying them opportunities and services and so putting prejudice into action. Behaviours have the specific goal of harming the minority group by preventing them from achieving goals, getting education or jobs, etc. The majority group is actively trying to harm the minority. (e.g. Jim Crow laws)

Scale 4 Physical Attack -The majority group vandalise minority group things, they burn property and carry out violent attacks on individuals or groups. Physical harm is done to members of the minority group. Examples are lynchings of blacks, pogroms against Jews in Europe, tarring and feathering Mormons in 1800s and British Loyalists in the 1700s.

Scale 5 Extermination- The majority group seeks extermination of the minority group. They attempt to eliminate the entire group of people (e.g., Indian Wars to remove Native Americans, Final Solution to the Jewish Question in Germany, Ethnic cleansing in Armenia

Overall guys you should know that the moderators do care about this kind of thing, and that we do take action. We do not always publicize what actions we take, and when we do it's different depending on what's going on. Moderating is more art than science.

That being said, while the board is not intended to be a voice for intolerance or racism - it is important and even beneficial for the community to address those kinds of issues head on and avoid censorship and removal of posts as much as is possible. It's very difficult as a moderator to find a balance between constructive community dialog, and abusive and intimidating language. I believe that by responding publicly, and as a community to this kind of language that we make the community a better place, but that if we just sweep these issues under the rug that they are given power to fester.

This is why it's important that each of you express your thoughtful opinion in matters like these. I believe it makes the community a better place when we respond intelligently and thoughtfully to racism and bigotry and prove that these ideas (though they may exist) are in the minority and are not representative of the community we've created.

If this board is to be representative of Astoria, please understand that it will not entirely be representative of your own views - and that some things you may find offensive (as may I). But that you have as much, or arguably even more power to shape the collective voice of the community than you may give yourself credit for.

All this being said, I (we) will not let this board be used simply for the purpose of spewing hatred, but that doesn't mean that you'll never see hatred on the board. Despite how it may appear, we actually do consider and in most cases address (or take some action on) the issues that come up. Sometimes that results in a suspension - but we've decided not to discuss that publicly when it does happen. The goal of Astorians is to facilitate community dialog, that may mean that the community has to address some discriminatory ideas as well.

Please help us shape the community voice by 1) reporting posts you find are in violation of board policy and 2) expressing your thoughtful and respectful views in contrast to ideas you disagree with - this is especially important when it comes to issues of discrimination.

As always, we are open to suggestions as to how to shape the policy of the board, and how to best execute that policy when there are violations. You may PM me, or post under the "Board Information" board if you feel something should change.
