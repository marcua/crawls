I've never dealt with a situation like this before so please forgive me if this sounds stupid but....

 For those who are responsible for utilities, how do you work heating your apt. in the winter? (What do you keep it set at? None at all? etc. ). Our bill keeps going up and up (literally doubling each month) and we just don't get it. We don't even have the heat on really...it's set at 59. I realize this is gas usage but if we turn it all the way to the lowest, our apt literally gets to 40-something. Isn't the landlord supposed to keep the building at a certain temperature? Is $400 really a normal amount!? It seems absurd to me that paying $400+/month is what everyone does.

It's a 3-bedroom and the back bedrooms don't even get sun so are several degrees cooler than the rest of the apt. I sleep in 4 layers with 4 blankets I'm just sick of freezing and still paying ridiculous amounts of money for gas.

First off, landlords are required to keep apartments a certain temperature if they control the heat in the apartment. When tenants control their own heat and can choose the settings, they care responsible for the heat. This is the difference between an apartment where you pay for your own heat versus one where you don't.

Second, heating water in the winter takes more gas than in the summer because the water is colder to begin with. Showering, cooking and such in a normal fashion is more expensive in the winter because it takes more energy to heat water when it starts at a lower temperature.

Third, while $400 is a lot, you're in a three bedroom. When I was in larger apartments like that and we had control over the heat it was completely normal to pay upwards of $100/per person for heat during the three coldest months of the year.

So, basically, unfortunately, that's the way it is and what you're being charged and the more expensive rates are completely normal.

There are, however, a few things you can do.

1) Check to see that ConEd is doing actual readings of your meter. Occasionally, they do "estimates" which are based on what was used in the past and then only do real meter readings every so often and adjust your bill. This, however, won't always mean you should pay less.

2) You can try getting an electric space heater and using it in the bedroom at night. I have done this in previous apartments where electricity cost significantly less than gas.

3) Go to Home Depot and get plastic for the windows. Get curtains. Fill pantyhose with trash bags and put one leg on each side of the bottom of doorways. Insulate the house so that you aren't loosing as much heat.


Good luck.

As it gets colder, it will take more heat to maintain a temperature of 59.  It's been so cold lately that perhaps that is happening.  It does seem unreasonable that you are freezing and paying so much, though.  My landlord pays for my heat, but often before I go to bed at night and in the mornings when I'm getting ready for work it is very cold.  I turn on a small space heater in my bedroom and close the door during these times.  The heater is on for less than 2 hours a day and it makes things more comfortable.  My electricity bill is only in the $50 range.  And I haven't removed my A/Cs from the windows and can clearly feel a cold draft coming from the windows.  An energy-efficient small space heater is usually under $50.
