Re: Which halal food vendor is better....
« Reply #14 on: October 21, 2009, 08:26:35 PM »
the proceeds go to the street vendor project, which is a non-profit that works with vendors (not only food vendors, but all street vendors) with legal battles, insurance, permitting, etc.  It's a cause that I feel is a worthwhile one, but it's become cost prohibitive in the last few years. 
At the same time, in the last few years they've definitely raised the bar, doing a dessert competition, having more vendors and opting for a little more established venues for the Vendy's.  The first year I went it was Sammy's Halal from jackson heights, the arepa lady from Roosevelt ave, the Dosa Man from Washington Square and Calexico tacos from soho in the yard of St Mark's Church.  It's definitely come a long way, and I wish that it was something that was a bit more wallet friendly, but even at $100 a ticket, the vendys sold out this year, so there's no shortage of food cart enthusiasts who have a couple more bucks to burn than I do.
