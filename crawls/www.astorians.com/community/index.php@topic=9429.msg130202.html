Quote from: Drakr2000 on May 27, 2008, 04:04:40 PM
In smaller towns, I'd say yes - dive bars usually stick around for a long time for whatever reason. I have no idea about in NYC.

I think that dive bars exist for lengthy periods of time for a reason...much as they do in small towns and as I've seen in NYC, they attract 'regulars.'  Those people who go there on a daily basis and get to know each other.  Dirty old men, locals, regulars, whatever you want to call them can keep a place in business for an amazingly long time.   

It's just interesting to think that dive bars were once "not dive bars."  I mean, when a someone opens a new bar do they have the intention of being a 'dive' bar?  How does a 'dive' become a dive?

I consider myself a regular at the Rover -I've made many friends there and I know all the bartenders and way more information about people there than I sometimes care to know.  I've seen the place evolve over the last 2 years; some good, some odd and some bad. Honestly, I think a bars ability to change with the times keeps it from becoming a dive -that and the love and care of the establishment by the owners and patrons.
