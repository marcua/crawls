Quote from: Astoria Luv on February 09, 2007, 10:55:05 AM

I just moved from 36th Ave. and 32nd Street to the above-mentioned area and I was wondering if there was any good diners within walking distance of my apartment.  I used to go to the Cup and I guess I could still walk there, but something closer would be a lot better as I like to roll out of bed on a Sat. or Sun. and make a quick dash to get the corn beef hash platter (yum, my mouth is watering just thinking about it!)

I just wanted to thank everyone and their great suggestions!  I have to report that the Orange Hut, while having a tasty breakfast (sausage platter) was one of the worst environments I could have eaten in.  I might be a little snotty and uptight, but I enjoy spreading my Sunday Times out and kicking back.  At the 'Hut' I could barely move because I was wedged between two people at the counter and the draft from outside forced me to wear my jacket during my entire 15 minute stay!

Oh well, at least it was better than the place I went to on Saturday morning!  Located across from the Home Depot on Northern Boulevard is a diner that looks and feels something from a 1950's movie.  Unfortunately, the food tastes like it was made in the 50's and was just left over and the service was HORRIBLE!  My corn beef hash was only edible after dumping a lot of ketchup and salt and pepper on it.  However, it quickly turned into a big red pile of mush!

I think I'm going to try that place on 30th Ave.  It's a little far, but I'm sure it has a better atmosphere than the Hut and better food than the wasteland on Northern Boulevard.

any reports on the Thai restaurant on Broadway in that area (40s) ... Thai Chile I think it's called...

I'm a very big fan of Thai Chili (formerly Dow Thong) on 45th & Broadway.  There are a variety of dishes with unique flavors, plenty of vegetarian options, including "vege duck" if you want a protein besides tofu, and the dishes are not overly saucy.  They are very friendly in person, although I most often order delivery.
