Ditmars Boulevard is the name of the street that runs east-west at the terminus of the N/W line, and as mcdirk says, it is in Astoria.  People say they live in the "Ditmars area" (or simply "Ditmars") to pinpoint which area of Astoria they live in -- other main drags are 30th Ave., Broadway, and Steinway (all run east-west except for Steinway, which runs north-south).

Ditmars is comparatively low-key; there are many restaurants and a few bars, as well as an extensive assortment of other commercial establishments - supermarkets, dry cleaners, delis, butcher, bakeries, dentists/doctors, coffee shops, a few wine bars, drugstores, gym, etc.  Oh, and a few nail salons, 99 cent stores, and banks.  There aren't really any clubs or cafes as there are around 30th Ave. or Broadway.

You definitely don't need a car.  All amenities are within walking distance, just as in other parts of Astoria.  And it is an excellent commute to midtown - my office is between 5th Ave. and Madison, and my commute (from Ditmars) is 30 minutes door-to-door.

I can't really comment on apartment prices because we haven't looked in years, but I would think that $1250 is in the 1 bedroom range.

Another comment about Ditmars as you're looking--

There's the Ditmars area near the N/W and then there's what is called "Upper Ditmars" where you have to take the bus to the subway. Make sure that when you're looking at places you know how close it is to Ditmars and 31st St, where the subway is. I know people who live in Upper Ditmars and take the bus to the subway and they don't seem to mind, but I know that I wouldn't want to.
