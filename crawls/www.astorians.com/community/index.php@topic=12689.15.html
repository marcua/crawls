Just got back from our first meal there.  :mrgreen:  :mrgreen:

Truly impressive. What a restaurant should be: Casually elegant. Elegantly casual. Borderline bistro. I didn't feel like I was being rushed, upsold, or bamboozled. Had the chicken w/kale and "life-changing" polenta (normally I don't eat chicken, but made an exception since it was a FreeBird(tm) but will go vegetarian from now on); BF had the pasta w/homemade sausage. Each had a glass of the waiter's favorite red (from the cheaper options) and it was great. Appetizer was fried Brussels sprouts (mmm) and fennel salad w/oranges.

Of course, Baby Jesus for dessert, and BF said it looked exactly "like a meatloaf." But tasted heavenly  :lol:

Indeed a great place. Not cheap (I think the bistro-like setting lulls people into complacency, "ah it's not fancy so it's not expensive!") but reasonable.

We went last night. It certainly was hopping, for a Wednesday. Congrats to the owners.

Christine - we must have just missed you; we got there shortly before 8:30 tonight.

It was a very nice meal.  I had the squash soup and the lasagna, choices I based on reading through this thread a couple of times.  The squash soup was a little too sweet for my taste, but otherwise had a very nice balance of flavours.  The lasagna was a deceptively large portion - I brought part of it home with me.  It was a hearty dish that had very tender noodles in the middle and nice crispy edges, and overall good flavour.  I've been on the hunt for a lasagna as good as a mind-blowing one I tasted in southern Switzerland nearly 20 years ago, and this was one of the closer ones.  The individual pan was a nice touch, but I was surprised that it was lined with a non-stick, Teflon-like coating.  I had a taste of the polenta, and it was delicious and cheesy.

The Baby Jesus cake was achingly sweet but otherwise tasty.  Normally it would be way too sweet for me, but I did enjoy my share until I developed a real sugar buzz and had to stop eating it.  It was served with a dollop of unsweetened whipped cream on the side, which helped cut the sweetness a little.  We decided that the cake itself is a very mild spice cake, and the taste and texture of the cake itself was reminiscent of warm chocolate chip cookie dough without the chips.

I'd like to go back and try a couple of other things on the menu.
