Title: Participatory Budgeting voting -- March 28 to April 3
Post by: 28Grand on March 16, 2016, 11:34:50 AM

District 22 Participatory Budgeting Project proposals have been published and voting runs from March 28 to April 3 at various sites across Astoria. See the link below for more information as well as a list of voting sites.

http://www.council.nyc.gov/d22/documents/030416PBProjectsVoterGuide.shtml (http://www.council.nyc.gov/d22/documents/030416PBProjectsVoterGuide.shtml)

The Participatory Budgeting process allows local residents to vote on how to spend $1.5 million on their top five projects to improve the neighborhood.

There are 20 projects on the ballot falling within the following categories: Education, Arts, Culture and Community Facilities, Housing, Parks & Recreation and Transit. Each project is listed and costed in the Voter Guide accessed here:

http://www.council.nyc.gov/d22/documents/Voter%20Guide%202016.pdf (http://www.council.nyc.gov/d22/documents/Voter%20Guide%202016.pdf)


More information can be found on Council Member Costa Constantinides's website:
http://council.nyc.gov/d22/html/members/home.shtml (http://council.nyc.gov/d22/html/members/home.shtml)

The results are in and as reported by Angela Matua in the Queens Courier / QNS.com local parks will greatly benefit from the Participatory Budgeting process.

In District 22, Costa Constantinidis will be putting monies toward improvements in Astoria Park, to repair water fountains and install new ones. Also, picnic and gaming tables will be provided in locations in the park to be determined.

Bulova Park in Jackson Heights will also receive monies for improvements. Funding for countdown clocks on several bus routes has also been selected.
