Are you talking about House Centipedes or regular old centipedes? House centipedes are good because they eat roaches and other bugs and aren't interested in you or your food. I'm guessing you're talking about those because they're a lot quicker than the non-house centipedes. I would say to try to leave them alone if you can stand it. If you have a lot of those and no roaches, it could be because they're eating your roaches. Sucks that they're in your bedroom though, that's the worst.

Here's an amusing account of life with house centipedes that I just found on google. unfortunately it's not very encouraging in regards to ridding your life of them: http://www.math.umd.edu/~schris/scutigera.shtml
If anything, I would say as opposed to trying to kill them, try to eliminate whatever gap you think they're coming in through.

Quote from: mcw on August 05, 2007, 08:44:48 AM
essen, how can you tell the difference between house centipedes and regular centipedes?

House centipedes have much longer legs than regular centipedes. The legs form an oval shape (short towards the front and back and longer in the middle) and they are sort of gangly; centipedes are just long with a lot of tiny legs. Also, house centipedes are much faster.

Handyman, house centipedes are really fast and a lot bigger than silverfish, so once you've seen both it's easy to tell the difference. These things are very distinctive. And silverfish suck because they eat book binding and various fibers. You definitely don't want those around.
