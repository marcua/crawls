I really disagree with this approach to the nods.  I will preface the following with the caveat that this is my observation and opinion of the act of nodding and I am not aiming to personally attack daisy the person; I apologize in advance if it comes off that way.  I am also NOT speaking as a moderator here, nor do I represent any of the moderators with my statements.

What I don't like is the use of positive nodding as a reaction to not understanding the negative nod itself, rather than positive nodding based on one's own opinion about the subject/conversation at hand.  I find this kind of approach be be rather arrogant and completely against the point of using nods constructively.  To me, it's as bad as negative nodding someone because you don't like them.  Assuming a negative nod contains malicious intent is certainly thinking the worst of things and the people involved with the thread.   I think that's what sticks in my craw the most - automatically assuming a negative nod is a personal attack; that gives no benefit of the doubt and does not contribute to the community in a positive way whatsoever.

When I see people reacting to something because they don't or can't understand it, it troubles me.
