Quote from: Frappe on July 14, 2008, 10:58:22 AM
With regard to the original topic, all folks stereotype all groups which are different from theirs.  I'd imagine it won't be popular to say on this thread, but frankly, I do not believe someone when they say they are devoid of stereotypes, in particular in the areas the OP brought up.  You have an honest discussion with them and you'll see it.  A nice chunk of self deception is usually at work. 

I dunno. I'm not going to claim to be devoid of stereotypes, but I think I reserve judgment until I see, at least somewhat, what people are like as individuals. We're talking about entire races and religions here. I grew up in a multi-religious household and never felt religious growing up. I wasn't an atheist either; it just wasn't something I thought about. I finally went through a personal religious-type crisis in college and took some philosophy courses, and I ended up feeling like people are just going to believe whatever they believe, and people don't really have control over that. I'm not concerned with people's religious beliefs as long as they don't use them as a weapon, which is something I can't stand no matter what the religion is.

As for race, I grew up in a mostly white suburban town, but we didn't talk about race in my house. I watched The Cosby Show when I was a kid, and I never thought much about the fact they were black. It was the only current sitcom I liked, and watching reruns these days I still think it was a good show. I understand some people didn't like that show because they didn't think it was a realistic portrayal of black people, but at any rate I think it brought me up with an open mind towards black people, feeling like they, just as any other people, should be seen as individuals rather than judged by the color of their skin. I didn't have any particularly negative experiences with black people either which could've caused me to feel differently as a child. Perhaps because I was in such a white-majority town, my bad experiences were generally with other white people, which again taught me to judge people by their actions and not by their skin color.

And trust me, I don't love everyone either. I'd go so far as to say I'm vaguely misanthropic. I can't say what paratactical did, that every time someone upsets me I remind myself that they are having a personal crisis and try not to judge them. But my broad negative stereotypes are usually regarding people in general and human nature, not their skin color or religious beliefs.
