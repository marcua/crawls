Had TWC for about 3.5 years here in Astoria.  The price kept going up and up and up.  I just felt like $150 a month for cable and internet (no phone) with 2 dvrs and no premium channels was too much.  I felt like there had to be a better deal out there. 


So I looked at DirecTV.  I was reluctant at first because my parents had DirecTV when I was in high school and it was terrible.  The signal was bad and rain/snow pretty much meant no TV.  And with the wind here and the fact that the dish has to sit on top of the building, how would that work?  I had read a lot of bad reviews about the customer service and the 2 year contract. 


I decided to move forward anyhow and it was a fantastic decision.  I've had DTV for about a year and a half now and have only had maybe 2-3 outages for like an hour.  I did decide to keep Time Warner's internet because it was the only real solution I had there.  I was able to negotiate the turbo speed with them for $30 a month though.  I pay around 50% less than what Time Warner charged me for the same services plus I get the Encore premium channels, a better DVR, and I also received the NFL Sunday Ticket last year.  In total, I pay about $75-80 a month for TV/internet, which seems like a fair price.  DirecTV also has better customer service.  Essentially, they do backload their contracts so the first year is much cheaper.  However, when year 2 comes around, just call up and tell them you are looking to switch because you have a better offer and they'll give you discounts to get your pricing closer to year 1 prices.
