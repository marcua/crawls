Blogs still can't beat a good newspaper
08/02/2007

Some weeks ago, your newspaper published a letter of mine titled, "Four clues your community is facing urban deterioration" (June 28).

The letter was picked up by several local Internet blogs and created an enormous amount of controversy.

As I watched the debate play out, it occurred to me that the only reason the letter was taken seriously is that it was published in a real newspaper.

Insofar as my main point was that Astoria, a once-charming community, is in a state of decline and that this is happening in large measure because once-cohesive neighborhoods have become fragmented and atomized, I found it ironic so many people told me how wrong I was and used as proof the fact that there are so many "community blogs" taking up this and other issues.

Now, if Astoria were still a real community, wouldn't neighbors meet face-to-face and discuss things rather than hide anonymously behind their computer screens?

The fact that community newspapers like yours still exist means that whatever is published in them will be taken seriously because there is still a sense that material that is published has been mediated by a respected institution.

It is not about ranting and raving on the Internet, it is about thinking an issue or point of view through and expressing thoughts in a way that is fit to print.

John Borrillo

Astoria

--------------------------------

I agree with what this guys says.

The fact of the matter is that civic groups ARE meeting in Astoria, people ARE trying to do something about the slippage of our community (sanitation, development, noise, the power grid, cell phone towers, etc), but people, particularly young people, ARE NOT getting involved. For example, of the 1000 regular visitors to this board, how many of you have actually gone out to a community board, a UCCA, an Astoria Civic, a LICA, a Transportation Alternatives, ALWPA, or a precinct council meeting? One of the town hall meetings held by elected officials?

Your lack of involvement does not auger well for this community and its future.

I challenge you to get involved. Simply thinking that posting and ranting is effective will, without the follow-up to real human interaction, in the long run, simply wastes your time and gets our community nowhere.  Blogs, although very useful and fulfill an important niche, are never a substitute for meeting face to face. A screen never trumps a human face. A keyboard, a handshake.

Organizing, meeting with others that have a similar concern, and collectively deciding on your next course of action is the only way to truly be effective and to for you to demonstrate to this community, the one that you are making your home, is worth your effort to make it a success. If something is important to you, you will find the time. No excuses.

The next time a civic group posts on this board, let’s see how many of you find the time to make the effort at showing up. Night out against crime is in Astoria Park this Tuesday. That is a good start.
