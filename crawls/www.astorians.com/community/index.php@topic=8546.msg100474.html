This is why an indoor/outdoor thermometer is so important. The law clearly states the inside temperature that is to be maintained. However, that temperature still feels ridiculously cold to me. But if you want to be taken seriously when you complain legitimately, you need proof that your complaint is legitimate. The legal required temperature doesn't feel "reasonable" to me at all--but the landlord might be meeting the letter, if not the spirit, of the law. Besides, he doesn't have a thermostat in your apartment--now that he's turned it on and knows that you know the law, maybe you just need to remind him, "Well, I know you turned it on, but the law states it needs to reach X at these hours, and I'm looking at my thermometer and it's not there yet."
