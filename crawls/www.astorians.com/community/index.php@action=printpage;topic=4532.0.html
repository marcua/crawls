Title: Re: a frame for an art print
Post by: hikaruhana on January 06, 2007, 11:59:24 AM
I just went to Astoria Art & Framing, a recommendation found on one of those links shared by a previous poster.  It's listed as being on 35-04 30th Ave but I think I had to snoop around a little to find it because the address was slightly off... it is on 30th Avenue, though.  I went there to get a gift framed just before the holidays.  The man there was very nice and they did a professional job.  I ended up going with a slightly pricier option than usual because of the importance of the gift but they have some reasonable options there if you don't want to spend too much money.  Also, if you pay in cash you don't have to pay tax.  He asked me to put down a deposit which was deducted from the total price.  I was very happy with their work and plan on going again when I have something special to get framed.

Hi,

If you have not found a frame already you might want to check out American Frames.  They're on the web and have many inexpensive frames in different styles and colors. The web site is fabulous!  You can upload photos of your art and try out different frames on it.  They even have a feature where you can "paint" the wall behind the frame.

I believe it's americanframe.com but you might want to do a search first.

They will even cut plexi and backing board for you but they don't ship larger sizes.
