What can I say?  I happen to like the pedestrian plazas.  Especially the one in Madison.  I also like all the green spaces the city is building throughout the city.  Why?  I don't know.  Maybe because they are nicer looking than the traffic clogging our streets every day.  Maybe because it's nice being able to walk around Times Square without stopping all the time to wait at the lights with 50 million other people on the sidewalk.  It's really opened up some really pedestrian congested areas.  I know when I worked in Times Square it was a huge pain in the ass having to get anywhere due to the masses of people.  Opening the streets seems so much better to me.  I like being outside.   I love outdoor cafes.  I like people watching.  As far as accidents go, I can't go around being worried about that all the time.  That can happen on any pedestrian crowded sidewalk as well.  As far as the tables taking up so much space on the sidewalk, there are regulations on how much space they can take, and if they are violating that, you can always report them.   But if we have an open space in the street to walk in instead, that negates the problem right there.

Traffic's always going to be a problem in this city.  There are no easy answers to the rerouting that will occur.  It's always the cars vs the pedestrians in major cities.  I don't have the answers but that's why I'm not on the committees that make these decisions.

I too, also like the pedestrian plazas. I had family from Philly in town, and we walked down Broadway in Midtown in the "street" and it was so much nicer of an experience than everyone cramming on the sidewalks with the vendors. In high foot traffic areas, the pedestrian plazas are worth the loss of street.

In Astoria, southern more so, I think it would work great. And store owners who lament the loss of car traffic affecting their business, well I think the increase in foot traffic is more beneficial. Up north it's not nearly as dense, and I don't sense the need to shut roads to make room for people. As a driver, I do sometimes take my car from Ditmars to more southern avenues because it's convenient. But I do not freak out if I can't get parking directly in front of my destination. I go for the nearest available spot. If that's 5 blocks away, so be it.

And speaking about cafe/restaurant tables outside and lack of sidewalk space, has anyone been on 2nd Ave on the UES lately? I feel like in that scenario they really shouldn't have tables outside. Besides, who wants to eat next to a bunch of nasty construction?
