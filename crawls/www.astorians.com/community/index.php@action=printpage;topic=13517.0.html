Title: Bakery Love: Top Five in Astoria 2008
Post by: round2 on December 31, 2008, 02:07:25 PM
Couldn't rest without tipping my hat to my favorite bakeries in Astoria:

St. Honore Patisserie (Ditmars and 32nd St.)- there are a few different things here that I love: the excellent cookies, apple or pear tarts and pastries, fab bread pudding, and (hmmm...) the coconut custard pie. I love the people here too.

Artopolis (31st St. and 23rd Ave.)- this place just looks kind of dreamy, doesn't it?  Sort of like you've died and gone to Greek bakery heaven.  The make incredible quality stuff here, not to be taken for granted.  The cookies are truly amazing, as is the bougotsa (sp?).  Really, there's about 500 different things to try here - I have barely scratched the surface, and seriously, I'm in here WAY too much already.

Laziza of New York (Steinway and 29th? Ave.) - this Lebanese bakery is not only a visual feast, but has incredibly delicious, extremely fresh pastries and cookies, the equal to which I don't think you'll find in NY.  Go, and be adventurous.

La Guli (Ditmars and 31st. St.) - I love La Guli, although I seem to only work a narrow vein within all of what they offer.  I love the chocolate hazelnut biscotti, and the, oh what are they called, ricotta ridged shell pastries.

Yaya's (31st St. and 30th Ave.) - this place also puts out some delicious stuff, with excellent sweets.  Don't overlook their excellent spanokopita (spinach pie).
