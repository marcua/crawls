Quote from: Christine on July 18, 2007, 11:49:30 AM
Kindly explain the difference b/w Tex Mex and just plain ol' Mex? Somebody? Anyone?

One specific difference is that in most of Mexico, tortillas are corn, not flour. Authentic tacos are always soft corn tortillas, for example. Flour tortillas are basically only used for burritos, which are common only in Northern Mexico, near the border, as street food.

Real Mexican food uses a lot less melted cheese and a lot less beef. Ingredients like fish, radishes, nopales (cactus), epazote (an herb) and squash are much more common. Beans are very common; rice, less so.

There's also a lot more variety in real Mexican food compared to what you get at Tex-Mex restaurants, which seem to only have variations on burritos, enchiladas, and tacos.

There aren't many good high-end authentic Mexican restaurants in NYC, but the cheap taco places in Astoria are quite authentic.
