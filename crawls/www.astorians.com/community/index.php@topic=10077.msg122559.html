I think you have to form a LLC, a Limited Liability Corporation, to buy a property between several individuals.

I've thought about this a lot since where I live it'd be impossible to buy a multi-family unit by myself.  I actually had an opportunity to get a 6 family in the Hunters Point section of Long Island City this past fall, but 2 of the apartments needed to be re-done.  So besides the initial cost of the actual building it self, I'd need money for those 2 apartments.  The building itself though was owned by the same family for 3 generations and was kept in tip top shape, except for those apts. that needed work.

By myself, impossible, but with one or 2 or more people it would've been relatively easy.  But my friends are broke and doing something like that with a stranger was too much of a risk.

You always hear about people doing it years ago in places like the Village/Tribeca and now the parties that did it are all rich.  A lot of work, but worth it in the end, IMHO.

For rent control, it looks like in most situations, the building had to have been built prior to 1947, and the tenant has had to lived there continuously since before 1971!!!

Rent stabilization is related to number of apartments in a building.  In many cases, if an apartment building built after 1974 has 6 or more units, it has to be rent stabilized. 

http://www.dhcr.state.ny.us/ora/pubs/html/orafac1.htm
