Tell me if I'm crazy.

Let me explain:
I grew up hating fruits and vegetables, and though I no longer feel that way about them, I have to make an effort to remind myself to eat them.  Ironically, fruits and vegetables are the first thing you see when you walk into Trade Fair on 36th Ave, and one day I realized that all along I had been makeing an effort to walk right by them!  So, even though Trade Fair generally charges more for lesser quality, I've been reminding myself lately to stop in the fruit and veg section and get something before moving on to the other departments.  So far this season I have bought 4 packages of strawberries - more than I've bought in whole seasons in years gone by.

As I was passing Top Tomato (Ditmars) on the bus tonight I noticed cherries had arrived, and after picking up some seedless grapes outside the store, I picked up a package of cherries, though they were priced at 6.49/lb.  I weighed the package on the customer's scales and saw that it weighed about 1 1/2 lbs.  I also saw that bigger packages of blueberries had also arrived, and priced at what I thought was 4.99/package, although it might have been 4.99/lb.

Now, if I feel that a store is really charging too much for something I usually don't buy it because that only encourages them to charge whatever they feel they can get away with, but I'm really trying to eat more fruit and vegetables and I've come to the conclusion that instead of shopping around, I succeed more if I shop where it's convenient.

Happily, the blueberries rang up at less than I was expecting, the grapes about what I expected, but what do you think of spending $10.19 on cherries?

Am I crazy?
