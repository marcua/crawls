Hi George...not to be picky, but I think you are a bit mistaken about oily coffee beans.  Oily beans are the result of degradation of the bean from roasting.  Dark roasting causes more oils to be released.  An oily bean could be far along in the degradation process and not be fresh at all.  Dry beans can indeed be completely fresh/recently roasted.  I don't think that I have ever seen "wet/oily" beans at either Stumptown or Blue Bottle and both companies provide the roasting date right on their package.  Starbucks beans are usually very oily because the burn their beans.

So, a few of us walked over there (post-4 mile run!) and really enjoyed ourselves!  We tried a bunch of things - iced coffee, iced tea, and a croissant, slice of maize almond cake, and a carrot orange muffin.  Everything was delicious.  First, the iced coffee was nice and strong and certainly not dumbed down (iced coffee can be so weak, in my experience) - it's one of the best iced coffees I've had.  I loved its rich, smooth flavor.  The iced tea - I can't really speak about it too much as I only had a sip, but I do remember it tasting sweet, which surprised me.

Of the three baked items we got, I absolutely loved the maize almond cake.  It's moist, but still a bit crumbly as corn cakes can be, with a lovely citrus flavor to it.  Not too sweet either. It was wonderful!

The carrot-orange muffin was also great.  It was light and moist and I loved the combo of orange and carrot.  Again, it was not overly sweet. It had a crunchy top which was due in part to the large crystal sugar sprinkled on it. It does have raisins, in case that matters to you.

The croissant was buttery and not like those overly bready croissants you get most places. It was beautifully layered inside and pleasantly browned outside.  Basically, it's how I imagine a croissant should be.  I noticed they had pain au chocolat as well, and I'll give that a try at a later date.

Oh!  We also had the egg and cheese on a Portuguese roll, which was outstanding!  I've never had the egg on a roll on anything but a kaiser roll before, but I very much liked Astor Bakery's version.  It was a large roll and thus a substantial sandwich. The egg and cheese were cooked together and it was fabulously savory.  Next time I'd add bacon.  My friend asked for butter on the roll, and we did like that addition.

Overall, I loved the fact that the sweetness of these products is balanced and not overbearing, and that things are moist and not dried out.  I don't eat a lot of sweets these days, so this was exactly the kind of thing I look for when I do indulge - high quality, delicious, well-balanced sweets.

I was very happy with things!  I'd love to hear what other people thought.  I'll certainly be back.  Looking forward to trying their savory things, too.

I am a weekend brunch regular at Astor Bake Shop. Yes, the service can be slow but the three regular staff members (one is the owner) are very sweet and accommodating people.   Recently, the service has gotten a lot quicker. They've hired one new person to help at least on the weekends during brunch (I've never visited during the week).  The pancakes, egg sandwich, and burgers are phenomenal.  The cakes and cupcakes which I've brought to gatherings are delicious. I'm extremely picky about Red Velvet Cake and Astor Bake Shop's is moist and flavorful.  The one item I don't enjoy is the baked chicken sandwich-the meat is mostly dark and the flavor is odd.
