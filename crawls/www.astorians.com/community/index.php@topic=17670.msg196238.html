Regarding the ConEd issue
« Reply #319 on: May 23, 2010, 12:55:06 AM »
The ConEd issue has been a confusing one for me.

The first question is who was/is Western Queens Power for the People. How did this organization come about? I've asked before and haven't received an answer.

The second is how the settlement came about. I just googled and came up with this link that contains a treasure trove of information from the Public Services Commission.

http://www3.dps.state.ny.us/W/PSCWeb.nsf/All/0F519E7284794F3085257687006F38D3?OpenDocument

There are two things that intrigued me as I clicked on the various links.

The first thing is that there was a final report released concerning the investigation into the power outage. It was released Feb 9th 2007 and is a 190 page document. So it isn't completely true that the settlement ended the investigation. But, interestingly, PCS wanted to continue the investigation. April 2007, they called for a Prudence Investigation that would, as far as I can tell, move from the factual investigation into more of an investigation of the culture/attitudes of ConED that would have created the process that would have lead to the blackout. I think that means looking into whether ConEd cared more about pleasing the shareholders then updating equipment.

Anyway, on April 24th 2008, a settlement was announced. And as you can see WQPFP wasn't the only group to sign off on it......Here's a link to a page that shows that four other groups signed off on it. http://www3.dps.state.ny.us/W/PSCWeb.nsf/All/ACD11A54EB8E5B7E85257696006D4A78?OpenDocument

okay, now I just found an article from the Queens Gazette stating that state law actually doesn't allow for much more of a settlement.
http://qgazette.our-hometown.com/news/2008-04-30/Features/Lawmakers_Denounce_Con_Ed_For_Paltry_Blackout__Set.html

Here are two quotes
Quote
"This proposal must still be reviewed by the Public Service Commission before it goes into effect, and some have pointed out that we would not even be considering this settlement plan if it were not for the inadequacy of state laws that stymie efforts to hold private utilities truly accountable for their failures. No matter how this issue isultimately resolved, it is very clear that we need to make changes in our laws to better protect all of New York's communities from the type of devastation we experienced here in Queens."Vallone (D- Astoria) stated,

Quote
Alyssa Bonilla, of Sunnyside, a member of the Power for the People organization, stated: "The laws that currently exist to seek justice against a privately owned utility company are inadequate."

And to end this long post....Our retiring Sen Onorato had this to say.
Quote
"On the other hand, under the terms of this proposed settlement, Con Ed will not be able to pass along $40 million in outage related costs and $6 million in other charges to its customers, and it will provide $17 million from shareholder funds to pay for the refunds, community environmental projects, and a study on the economic and health impacts of the blackout. Members of the Western Queens Power for the People Campaign worked hard to win these concessions, and are to be commended for their efforts.

My conclusion:

An investigation was done and completed....but ConEd didn't want the prudence investigation.

The laws of our state didn't really provide for a more satisfactory settlement.

WQPFP worked hard to get terms beyond what the law allowed for.

And for all the carping by Maloney, Gianaris, Clinton et al........State Law Sucks.....and Western Queens Power For the People got us more that we actually would have gained.

ps....I just spent an hour creating this post because it is an important issue and not an attack on anyone...I do truly want to know what happened.
