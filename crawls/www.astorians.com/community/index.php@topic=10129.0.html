Its not a free beach but its a really nice beach... try Long Beach on LI. Just take the Long Island Rail Road from Woodside- 61st street Station to Long Beach- it is about a 50 minute ride. The train ticket and beach pass will cost you about $18 altogether.

Water Taxi is a bar, that happens to be outside right by the water and amazing view of Manhattan.  They imported sand to the area, and you can run around barefoot and play volleyball.  They sell hotdogs and hamburgers and sangria, among others.  During the day, kids are allowed.   There is no access to the lovely Hudson River water, so there is no swimming.  You can wear whatever.  They have weddings there sometimes.  They have a DJ too.  On weekend nights, they usually have a cover charge, and a long line out the door and around the block, from the crowds who bombard the place after the PS1 party.  This line is made worse by the fact that they actually scan everyone's ID before you can go in, so if you forget your license, you are out of luck.  Even if you are 50 years old.

Go during the week, it's much easier to deal with the annoying door issue.

Just a few minor point-

Water Taxi Beach is actually on the East River, not the Hudson.  And they have to scan ID's, even if you're 50, b/c some neighbors are accusing them of encouraging underage drinking and you can't just scan some people, if you scan anyone, you have to scan everyone.  Most popular places in Manhattan and Queens scan them too nowadays, it's pretty common.  They just protecting themselves.  The Astoria Beer Garden did this last summer too.

And they've doubled the size of the place!  With new LI sand, as opposed to the NJ sand they had, which was much courser.  They had to use NJ sand initially b/c there was a strike of truckers on LI when they opened and couldn't get any LI sand.  But now they have it. 

Also, there's only a cover when they have a 'famous' DJ...usually on Saturday nights.  People come from all over the tri-state area and internationally for some of their DJ's, if you can believe it.

I second Long Beach. I've been going out there since I was little and it's my favorite.
Nice crowd—families, singles, couples. Never any trouble and very clean.

Lifeguards are top notch -- always on the ball and also friendly.

It's a nice one-day getaway when you need a break.

I introduced my boyfriend to it a few years ago and his kids love it.
They've been asking when we're going to start riding "the blue train" out to the beach!

And for ladies like me who end up going alone - no one bothers you. I tend to stick to the same
area and see the usual 'regulars' who I 've known for years. No one ever harasses you and there are
also beach police on foot patrol regularly so if you did have a problem, you can summon help.

Last year the combo train fare/beach admission ticket was $18 but it may have gone up.
But totally worth it.
