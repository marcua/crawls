I want some freaking delicious coffee to make at home
« on: July 24, 2008, 01:00:06 AM »
All right, people. I currently have no job, so my usual coffee shop trips are out. However, I still want coffee. I have some beans and ground coffee at the moment that I find underwhelming. I have a grinder and a french press, plus one of those melitta one-cup things that's good in a pinch.

What I want is coffee that tastes as delicious as it smells. The coffee I have now smells great, but when I make it the taste is uninspiring. I've tried changing the amount of time it's brewed and the amount of coffee I use, and I've tried it both in the french press and the melitta, but it's still not very good. I do not like a bitter or burnt edge to my coffee, but I don't want it to be so smooth that as soon as I add half & half I can barely taste the coffee anymore. I do not want decaffeinated. I just want coffee that tastes like coffee, with a full-bodied, bold flavor. For reference, since coffee taste is so subjective, a place that I think has good coffee is Veselka, at 2nd Ave and 9th Street. (They also have good tuna sandwiches and cold borscht for summer, btw.)

Flavored coffees are acceptable as an alternative to traditional coffee, but I want them to really taste like the flavor they're supposed to be - none of this "chocolate & peanut butter" stuff I bought that tastes like neither chocolate nor peanut butter - and I'd prefer them to not be artificially flavored. Now, I'm not sure a flavored coffee that fits that description actually exists. Recommendations for tasty, all-natural flavor syrups that people have tried would not be out of place in this thread, as then I can make my own flavored coffee.

Thanks everybody.   :mrgreen:

Kona is like my favorite coffee. If you are not working, take the 7 to 40th street, get off on the NE corner, and the 4th storefront off the corner is a coffee shop that roasts their own beans. The name is like Bareurs or something involving a B an A and a couple R's. If you get there around 930am, the guy should be roasting beans, if he is, you'll smell it as soon as you get off the train. You can always ask for whatever they have fresh0 and/or ask their advice. I am currently enjoying a Kenyan roast, which definitely has a robust flavor. They also have the Jamaica Blue Mountain there but its pricey. I would have recommended Costa Rican but if you don't like Kona I don't know how you'd feel about the Costa Rican.

Well the last several times we have been getting the organic bird free Mexican. It has a nice light flavor, but don't let that fool you into thinking that it isn't strong, which it is. Try auggies blend too, not to be confused with aggies blend. Or you could trust one of the workers and ask them either what they recommend and they'll probably ask you what you like, how often you drink it, how you prepare it etc. Or you could ask for the flavor of the day/week. Just don't feel intimidated, let them know what you want ( if you figure that out in advance) or let them decide for you.

Porto Rico also has the Blue mountain, but it's $45/lb and I don't think you're willing to drop penny for that.
