Look, I don't want to argue, but I grew up around the restaurant business.
My father was a chef and often took me to work with him when I was young.
Later on I would visit him at lunchtime or sometimes come in for dinner with a friend.

We talked a few times about good service, how to tip, what to expect, etc. And I had
a couple of incidents like the one described. He always said "tips are earned, not expected.
You don't have to tip if the service doesn't warrant it." And he always told me to give the
the busboy (or girl!) something if they went did something for you. Like if I get my food
wrapped to take home, usually the busser brings it to you and I'll always give them a
couple of bucks.

So if the busser is the one who doing his/her own job and that of a waiter/waitress who's
slacking off, then tipping them is giving them their due, not promoting stealing.
