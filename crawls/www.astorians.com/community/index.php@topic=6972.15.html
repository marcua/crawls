I have a feeling that this is a case where the regulations were written prior to the existance of the blue recycling bags (which I have only started to see in the past two years or so).  I would imagine that since other regulations refer to fines for putting regular garbage in blue recycling bags, they are acceptable for recycling use.  The spirit of the law is likely that sorters must be able to see through the bags once they reach the recycling facility.

This kind of reminds me of a story a friend of mine who worked at TLC once told me.  When there was a recent taxi fare increase, it caused all sorts of bureaucratic entanglements because taxi cabs are required to have those signs on the side of the doors announcing the fare.  Apparently, the regulation codified not only the need to have those signs, but specifically the fare at the time the regulation was made.  In other words, to update the signs with the new fare would have been breaking the law because the law stated that the taxis must have signs quoting an old fare!  This kind of stuff makes my head spin.
