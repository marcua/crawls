Title: Re: bird flu caused by factory farming
Post by: Harlan on May 09, 2006, 03:35:18 PM
No, "bird flu" was most decidedly not caused by inhumane treatment of animals. The author of that non-scientific article confuses the origin of a virus with issues of transmission.

The threat of bird flu as a pandemic, however, is very much related to crowding, both of birds and people. It's certainly true that crowding of animals, including people, makes it much easier to transmit certain types of disease. More birds will get H5N1 because they're living in close quarters, certainly. This doesn't mean that if chickens were all wild birds, H5N1 would never evolve or jump to people, it just means that there would be less opportunity for the virus to spread. Same with people. Cities are a really efficient way for diseases to spread. Back in our hunter-gatherer days, although life would still have been nasty, brutish and short, we certainly didn't have had nearly as many infectious diseases as we do now (or, particularly, as we did 100 years ago before antibiotics and vaccines). Jared Diamond covers this quite nicely in Guns, Germs & Steel (http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0393317552/002-0749109-8693627?v=glance&n=283155)...
