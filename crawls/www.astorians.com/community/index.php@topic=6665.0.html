What happened to Pizza Palace? :(
« on: June 14, 2007, 02:35:42 AM »
I'm talking about the one on Ditmars and 31st. What has happened to this place over the last two years? When I moved here, a slice from there was delicious, the perfect size, well-balanced and nothing fancy. Just a plain old slice of pizza that tasted good. At some point, they decided to start selling "The largest slice in New York" or something fitting that description, and I don't doubt that it might be the largest slice- but now when I get a slice from them, not only does it cost a dollar more than it did two years ago (along with all the other pizzas being much more expensive), but it's gross. The blanket of cheese has become very thick, not enough sauce, and the slice is just unreasonably large. I would rather pay less for something much smaller. Crust is still nice and light, but I barely made it through half the slice before I started to feel disgustingly full of cheese, and I wasn't tempted to save the rest.

Has anyone else had this experience with Pizza Palace? It makes me sad because I loved that place when I moved here, but I feel that the pizza has changed for the worse very quickly and it makes me not want to go there anymore.  :-(

I used to love Pizza Palace.  When I was a student at PS 122 for a year during the 6th grade, every time we had a school trip someplace, it was the official last stop on the school trip.  I'd put together some money with my friends so we could afford an entire pie, or sometimes we opted to get souvlaki and some french fries with cheese.  It was junk food heaven, and things were even better with the arcades in the back.  One friend of mine was just amazing at Cruis'n USA. 

However, in later years the place just came across as expensive for me.  The slices of pizza and everything else seemed to be everywhere from 50 cents to a couple of dollars more than at comparable establishments elsewhere in the area and the food wasn't really anything special.  I haven't been there in a while so I haven't seen these new large slices, however, but the high prices turned me off to the place.  When I still lived in that neighborhood, I tended to prefer Frankie's for pizza, or sometimes I'd trek up to Alba's, or Michaelangelo's in the last few months before I moved out, when it had first opened.
