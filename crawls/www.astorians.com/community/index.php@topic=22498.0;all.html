why report dumping?
« on: March 29, 2012, 02:25:38 PM »
as I was leaving my apt today at around 11AM,  I watch a man sliding part of a sectional sofa down the sidewalk and leave it to rest next to two mirrored doors under the trestle on 33 Street off of 23 Ave.

I follow this guy to back to his place (just 4 houses away) and as he strolls up his driveway i confront him about  dumping the couch. He goes in to his building and shuts his door.

I call the cops and wait around just 15 minutes before they arrive. I describe the man to them, point out the house he came out of and figure something might actually be done about it. I go back to my apartment to pick up a book i had forgotten and by the time i came  back downstairs (all of maybe 3 minutes) the cops had left.

i wasted my time. and what's worse -  it was only part of a sectional, when I get home tonight I'm sure he will have shuffled the rest of it under the trestle too.

I suppose reporting it to 311 will get it cleaned up eventually, but don't waste your time calling the police about it.

the exact details are probably listed on the sanitation website, but basically all large items like furniture, sanitation is *supposed* to haul away on the 2nd collection day of the week (like the day recycling gets put out). Mattresses must be wrapped in plastic. Items like AC units and refrigerators, or anything else that has refrigerant you must call to setup an apt, they will tell you to put it out the evening before pickup so that it gets drained, slapped with an OK sticker on it, then sanitation guys pickup
