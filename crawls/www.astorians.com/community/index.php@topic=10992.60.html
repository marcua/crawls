I guess, Alexa, that part of the problem is that a lot of Astoria places aren't just a bar or just a restaurant. Crescent and Vine is a semi-regular hang out of mine. While I'm not going to puke on the table or get really rowdy, I do swear loudly and tell inappropriate jokes.

A restaurant is, IMO, a fine place for kids to be. Provided that strollers aren't blocking aisles for other customers and the kid isn't crying, I can always ask for a table a bit away from the parents if I'm worried about the kid. The problem is the borderline places like C&V or restuarants that become more of a bar as the night gets later.

I also think the time matters more to the adults than the kids.

I've seen kids in the Irish Rover in the late afternoons and early evenings. I'm okay with that and the kids in the Beer Garden in the afternoons. I just think that as it gets later, I have a tendency to get rowdier and (obviously) drunker and it makes me feel uncomfortable to think that I would say or do something in front of a kid I shouldn't.

This is my problem that I feel this way, but I doubt I'm the only one. I think most parents on this board seem to have an idea of the difference between when is okay and when starts to get ackward. I think that if people pay attention and try to be considerate of the others around them, there isn't a problem at all. The real bugger is when people get too caught up in thinking about what they want and don't try to consider others as well.

What I think is weird, though, is that all the pro-kids-in-adult-places-late posts haven't made any mention of everyone else. (Save the one about the entertaining kid).

By "everyone else", do you mean people without kids?  If so, I am part of "everyone else".  But I think parents have rights, too - it's their community just as much as yours and mine.  That includes the right to be on the street at rush hour with their stroller if they have to (I'm sure they enjoy the crowds and being yelled at, too), the right to go to a halfway decent - or better, yet, a nice restaurant or restaurant/bar or *gasp* even bar with their child.  Do they have more rights?  No.  Do they have the right to sit there if their child is screaming its head off?  Well, maybe technically, but most would have the courtesy to step outside with the child, or leave if need be.  And I believe a restaurant or bar has the right to ask a disruptive patron to leave, and that probably extends to disruptive children. 

Not all kids are well-behaved, but a lot are, and if they can handle it, why not?  A friend of mine has a daughter who loves going to restaurants with her parents - her favourite meal was seared duck breast at an upscale restaurant near their house when she was five.  Why restrict her from that?

And "late at night" really isn't what most others have said in this thread.  Granted, "late" is open to interpretation - it may be 8pm for some or midnight for others, but for the most part people here aren't talking about much past 10pm or so.  And I really don't think that people mean places like McCann's or Dillinger's or the Quays when they're packed with drunk people at midnight.

Since smoking has been banned indoors at bars and restaurants, it's MUCH easier to bring children into bars without feeling like you are putting their health at risk.  Infants can also sleep through a lot and don't sleep at normal bedtimes, because they are awake and asleep on and off throughout the day.  So, why not put your baby in your carrier or car seat or whatnot and take them to a bar? Language won't do any harm to the child who can't understand curse words anyway.  Rowdy crowds and drunkards could be an issue though, so parents just have to be smart about how late they have their kids in the bar.  Toddlers and older are different, because they most likely won't sit still and should have a set bedtime, IMO.   

Some bars do have curfews for kids.  The Beer Garden's curfew is 9pm, which seems fair to me.  You won't see my kids in there past 7pm anyway (usually).

The bigger problem with restaurants is irresponsible parents.  I went out to a restaurant with my wife, brother and newborn a couple years ago.  My brother said it was a family friendly restaurant and it was, but a nicer, sit down meal than we really expected. My baby decided to start crying soon after the food came and I spent the good part of an hour in the parking lot walking her back and forth while she wailed.  We didn't bother other diners, but I didn't enjoy my meal either.  That's a good reason not to take your kid out for a nice dinner.  But if someone has a wailing kid and doesn't leave, that just sucks and they shouldn't have their kid out.  Same with people who are inconsiderate about strollers, etc. 

But if a kid is well behaved, why shouldn't they be able to go out as a family?  Again, if we do go out for dinner with our children (well, we haven't taken the new one out for dinner yet), we do it on the early side for bedtime's sake and for other diner's sake, but if you know your kid can handle it, they have a late bedtime and you're willing to get your dinner to go if the kid melts down, then I see no reason why you should feel you can't take your kid out to a later dinner.
