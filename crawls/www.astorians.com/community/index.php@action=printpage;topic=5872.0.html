When the ancient Persians captured a town which resisted it, all of the men were often killed, unless they had a specific skill which the satrap might find useful.  When an unskilled Greek captive was facing the executioner, he begged the satrap to spare him.

"Why should I spare you?  What skill do you have?"
"I can teach your horse how to talk" the Greek responded.
The satrap held up his hand and asked how.
"It is a skill my father taught me.  In one year's time, I can teach your horse to talk.  If I cannot, I shall gladly submit to any punishment you deem fit"
The satrap then agreed and the life of the Greek was spared.
Later, his friend came upto him and admonished him.
"You can't teach a horse to speak, that's not the truth"
"The real truths are that over the next year, I may die, the satrap may die or the horse may die"
