No.  No way, man.  If you'd seen what I did one summer night at the beer garden a few years ago ...

It was pretty crowded, as usual.  Tables filled with people drinking, eating, chattering away.  When this gang of about a dozen babies came sauntering in.  They were a unified gang -- all wearing top hats and diapers, nothing more.  A few had small black walking canes. 

You know that feeling when you get on a subway car or street, and "bad people" are there causing trouble, that sense of fear, of something bad about to happen?  You could feel it when those babies walked in.

Sure enough, they weren't there more than a few seconds when the babies started taking pitchers of beers from tables and drinking them, straight from the pitcher, no cups.  I think people were too shocked to do anything.  One girl went up to one of them and said, "Aw, that's a cute little baby" and went to pinch his cheek.  He grabbed her arm and flipped her full-bodied over his shoulder onto her back.

She was with a table full of aging frat-boy types ... not surprisingly, most of the crowd was like this ... and all hell broke loose.  "Dude, going to kick your little baby ass" and such was yelled out ... the way guys afraid to fight start carrying on in hopes of their opponent backing down.  No such luck.  In fact, the verbal sparring caused the babies to immediately start kicking ass.

If you’ve ever seen the movie Kill Bill, the scene where Uma Thurman takes on that huge gang of martial arts bodyguards in a Tokyo bar, it was like that.  The first group of frat boys descended on the babies, and the babies just cleaned their clocks: punching them in the crotch, popping knee caps with their canes, smashing their heads off the picnic tables.  And their top hats never came off – they weren’t even breaking a sweat.

Well, beer muscles being what they are, rather than fleeing, even more guys tried to fight them, and it turned into a melee – fists flying, blood, groans.  Guys were flying away from this group of ass-kicking babies just like in a kung fu movie.  It was then people realized there must have been something supernatural occurring, and they started to flee, as if from a blazing inferno, jamming the exits.  You couldn’t get out.

I ran like hell and scaled the wall by the side of the street – I saw others doing the same on the chain link fence to that playground-type area next door.  Got out on the street and just ran.  A few minutes later, crouching underneath a Fedex truck, I could see a dozen pair of small legs and walking canes sauntering down the block, laughter, beer spilling on the street as they walked.  Sirens were approaching, but they didn’t seem to care.  I took one last look and saw those babies hang a right down by the fish place, obviously walking to the subway to go into Manhattan to cause more mayhem.

No babies in bars, please.  I walk into a bar and see a baby, even if it’s in a harness, I get the hell out.

My other baby friends and I were in the mood for a relaxing beer at the local beer garden.  It had been a while since we stepped out so about a dozen of us walked in wearing our Sunday best – top hats and diapers and nothing more – less is more when you’re a baby.  Some of my friends had black canes since they had just learned to walk and were a bit unsteady on their tiny feet.

We decided to drink the beer from the pitchers instead of using cups as it saves time and energy on clean-up and we wanted to show off our new drinking skills since most of us had recently graduated from sippy cups.  Then out of nowhere, this girl came up to my friend and said, “Aw, that’s a cute baby,” and raised her hand like she was going to punch him.  Who punches a baby?  So my friend grabbed her arm and flipped her full-bodied over his shoulder and onto her back.  It was instinct. 

She was with a table full of frat boys and they got all bent out of shape and started yelling threats at us.  All we wanted to do was have a nice drink.  The frat guys threw the first punch and we defended ourselves. 

If you’ve ever seen the movie Kill Bill, the scene where Uma Thurman takes on that huge gang of martial arts bodyguards in a Tokyo bar, it was like that.  The first group of frat boys descended on us and we did our best to defend ourselves.  We punched them in the crotch and tried smashing their heads off the picnic tables with our tiny baby hands.  And they kept coming and coming!  It was so scary.  We did our best to hold onto our expensive top hats. 

Well, beer-drinking frat guys being what they are, rather than fleeing, even more guys tried to fight us, and it turned into a melee – fists flying, blood, groans.  Babies were flying away from this group of frat assholes just like in a kung fu movie.  It was such a mess that the rest of the patrons tried to get out, jamming the exits.  There was a crush of people.

People were scaling the wall by the side of the street and doing the same on the chain link fence to that playground-type area next door.  Everyone just ran.  A little while later, we were able to escape with our lives, trying to maintain some dignity with our hats and canes.  Sirens were approaching, but we just kept walking.  We hung a right down by the fish place, trying to get to the subway to go into Manhattan where there are fewer frat guy morons.

No frat guys in bars, please.  I walk into a bar and see a frat guy, even if it’s in a harness, I get the hell out.

Funny, my mom loves to tell a story about how when I was around 2, I finished off all the left-over drinks
after a party. She said she found me swaying back and forth in our living room. It was only a few, maybe 3 or 4
and just small amounts that probably amounted to half a glass. Doctor told her I'd be fine.

Supposedly I slept for 10 hours and awoke w/no hangover.....

But seriously, my partner and I were in Brick a couple of years ago. This was when they had sofas and loveseats in
the back lounge area. We were sharing a sitting area w/ a couple who had their baby there. The little one started crying
and fussing, which eventually went to shrieking. They asked for the music to be turned down, asked another group nearby
to "keep it down" and we were just amazed. Finally, the manager came over and asked them to leave, which they did but
were very annoyed about.

If you have the money to go out for drinks, then you've got money for a sitter. This thing of bringing small kids and
babies everywhere is ridiculous - it's not fair to the little ones and not fair to other patrons. And I don't hate kids - my
partner has an 8 and 11 year old and I'm about to become an aunt. But there are places children don't belong and
I think bars are in the top five on that list.
