Thoughts On The State of Astorians
« on: June 18, 2008, 10:30:42 PM »
(I know how our administrator, merm, and our moderator, mcdirk, hate to have to plow through long posts, but this is going to get personal, very personal, so I suggest you loosen your ties, relax and continue to read.)

When I joined this online Community of Astorians.com—and yes, I know that this is the internet and that capital letters are not needed, but I prefer to always put my hometown in a respectful light whenever possible—somewhere around March of 2005, it was a promising avenue into the soul of the Astoria Community at large. The only other Community-oriented site at the time was MyAstoria. Astorians.com had a few hundred members on the forum and the hot discussion was about the best bagel place around! Now Astorians.com boasts over 4000 members and averages around 140 unique posts per day. The site had far less software features and far less dissension. There was also little to no moderation. (I recently read something which profoundly resonates on this discussion: A thing moderately good is not so good as it ought to be. Moderation in temper is always a virtue; but moderation in principle is always a vice. —Thomas Paine) There were no little red and green thumbs-up and thumbs-down. There were no nods. I would personally very much appreciate it if this remained a nod-free thread. If you want to add to this thread, please post your comments. IMHO, the prolific nodders have added nothing to this forum and have in fact detracted from far too many vibrant, interesting and necessary conversations. To the neg nodders who go from thread to thread, who haven't got the pertinaciousness (READ: balls) to post their own opinions, who always neg nod certain people for whatever their petty reason, please go back to lurking because I am asking that either the nods feature be disabled or people start being held responsible for their neg nodding usage so that those that are offending the system can be held accountable for their actions to disrupt the forum. When you see a whole lot of nodding going on in a thread, doesn't it change the thread's tone into a we-against-them kind of thing? Their presence alters the discussion itself and discourages people from posting. When did flaming politely become so acceptable? No-one can identify what neg nodding ultimately produces, except a lot of hurt feelings and I hardly think that's what this forum is all about.

Merm has done an outstanding job in the development and upkeep of Astorians.com, and I personally want to take the time to applaud him on that. mcdirk recently, unbeknownst to him, got a standing ovation from me on his recognizing that a thread was getting too hot and locking it up until cooler minds prevailed, and on a few occasions asked that a conversation get toned down. That should be the extent of how this board needs to be handled. Have you noticed that since it was recently stated that it is okay to use curse words spelled out in posts that they are occurring now more often? Besides being somewhat amusing, it also succeeded in calling peoples attention to the little known fact that this, just like any other, forum has default software parameters and that they can be changed by changing the fields of your own personal profile. (Here is a little test for you: f___ if the preceding word is PANSY you should go to your Profile —> Look and Layout Preferences and put a check mark next to Leave Words Uncensored.) Then you will be able to read the word F•U•C•K and all discussions in their entirety without any artificial sweeteners.

Until now I have been a strong supporter of this forum up to and including urging people to financially support this site. For the requested donation of $12.00 per year we supporters were promised an advertising-free site and some additional perks. I was of the opinion that supporters would have a stronger voice in the goings on around here. After all, how many private clubs allow non-members to vote? But apparently membership in this club is supporting the non-donating member, ass-kissing flocks of sheep that will maintain the status quo regardless of the consequences.

We have been reminded time-after-time that this site is the private property of the owner of the site. I've always read that like, "it's my ball and I'll make up the rules for the game or I'll just go home with my ball." Somewhere among the threads there is even an analogy comparing this site (or was it all internet forums) to a magazine with an editor and a publisher and how things get published at that type of venue. I recently read a very interesting true story about public rights on private property. Back in the 1970's a gentleman who was quite the radical about building a nuclear plant on Long island, went with his little local anti-nuclear plant group and started passing out leaflets inside the Smith Haven Mall in Lake Grove, Long Island. They got themselves kicked out. The Mall rules stated that since the Mall was private property, the management can say who stays and who goes. The leaflet group initiated a lawsuit in New York State and they won the suit based upon the concept that a mall is pretty much the same thing as the "Town Square" of old. The Town Square was where people gathered to meet and discuss happenings of the day and where the local Government posted things of Community interest. The mall may have been private property but it had become more than that.

So has Astorians.com.

It is now so much more than a privately owned website. It has become a public space where local residents of the Astoria Community meet to discuss and share information. This suspending and banning of members of the forum is absurd. Even in the days of old, when one was punished they were put into stocks in the Town Square where they still had access to their Community. Suspension and banning denies the basic principle of this forum: to meet and share thoughts and ideas through input with friends and neighbors. This online Community has both the intelligence and the tenacity to moderate itself without any High School hall monitors like the simple- little-minded snitches who obviously find use for the 'report to moderator' link. I was recently told that suspensions are usually 24 hours, then 48 hours, then 7 days, then 30 days, escalating with each suspension. But, that doesn't seem to be the case. And why, among the pages of the Board's Rules, is this not mentioned? The rules themselves may be fine, it just seems that they are very unreasonably and inconsistently enforced, and that their enforcement has become a subjective statement by the enforcer rather than any other issue. Inconsistent moderating has led to so much discontent of this site, leaving the only consistent issue being the inconsistent acts of the Administrator and the Moderator(s).

How telling is it that in the Open Discussion of Board Rules and Policy, as of this writing, there are a mere 37 posts expressing their opinions and yet there have been 50 people who responded to the poll. As I perceive it, the problems that this forum is currently experiencing is not so much from the having of rules as it is the way these rules are both enforced and interpreted, and the inconsistent way they are moderated.  I am constantly reminding myself that we would not have racial integration if it were not for the minority voices being louder and more willing to suffer the consequences of their actions to just be heard. Even more interesting than the aforementioned poll, is the poll in the nods thread. With only 38 posts in the thread there have been 54 people who voted and almost 25% of those voted I don't really care for it, but I use it anyway for those times when I disagree but don't feel like posting about it. I would have liked to see how those folks would have voted if there had only been 3 choices: I Like The Nods; I Don't Like The Nods or I Don't Give A Flying f___ About Nods!

I would like to take this opportunity publicly like thank those of you who have been so supportive and loyal to me throughout my little ordeal, and to the folks whose words and thoughts I have used, thank you for your permission to do so.

This is what it looks like, just so you recognize it should it confront you someday:


No warning; no explanation; no discussion; no avenue for appeal . . . no telling you what you did/said; no informing you how long this bullshit will last; just get yourself into the naughty corner, like a child and think about your sins because someone subjectively decided that you did/said something that they interpreted as being against the rules—for YOU—while the baiting and inflammatory rhetoric is overtaking the forum.

What saddens me the most is the self-indulgent, smug, cavalier attitude expressed by those in charge. In general, I have been a huge merm fan. The concept of this forum, what Astorians.com has done for the Community has had a huge impact on our lives.  This isn't just another board, this is our neighborhood and the people who frequent this board are our neighbors; many of them have become friends. So, now there is another local forum who might be perceived as giving chase to this one. I personally welcome any venue that can attract the diversity of Astoria to help restore the cohesiveness that once was my Community.

If things around here don't start healing, begin being open to less moderation, less of this vicious neg nodding, or ass-kissing crap, I am going to take the advice of a friend who told me get a hobby and get the hell off the computer.

I've been addicted to what once was Astorians.com. I've got to quit. Or, minimally, I've got to cut back.

Some of you will recognize that there are larger issues at stake here than a few people name calling, being suspended, or being mad at the moderation of this forum. Sadly, some will not.

Mamamac -- I’m glad you’re back and I’m sorry you seem to be hurting. It’s quite obvious that there need to be some changes in the way this site oprates, which as merm has said he will address.

I’d like to respectfully respond to some of what you written.

The astorians.com as a magazine model you cite was mine and though I can see parallels between your model, astorians.com as a town square, in my mind the two are similar but not the same. Perhaps in my example I didn’t go far enough so I would like to add that I also believe that astorians.com would be nothing without the people who contribute to it, or it’s writers, because those contributions are the whole purpose of this site – at least in the way that I see it.
 
As much as I love astorians.com it is not a replacement for Astoria. A website is not the real world, at least not yet, because in the real world you do not have the privilege of being anonymous. It’s the anonymity that is key to this discussion and often left out of the equation.

Online, at least on this website most of us are faceless, nameless personas. We are self-edited versions of ourselves and how much of the un-edited portions are revealed depends solely on what we choose to show, though you can learn a lot from what a person writes and what they respond to.
  
Anonymity skews the comparison. It’s empowering, almost like a drug, because it gives us the ability to say things and behave and act in ways we might not normally say, behave and act and therein lies the trouble.

You cannot be anonymous in a physical town square. You have a name, a face, an address, etc. are seen and are identifiable, which allows your words and actions to be called to account in ways not possible online.

So to all who were hurt and confused by suspensions and “banishment” from the town square have you ever considered that some of the things you say, from the flip comments to the down right nasty, may have hurt other people’s feelings? Or when read by third parties intimidates them and inhibits that from taking part in the town square discourse?

Has the thought occurred to anyone who advocates a lighter approach to managing this site that a more free-for-all, no holds barred philosophy effectively makes you one of “those people” who alienate and in effect banishes people from the town square?

It’s easy to say to people they should get over it, not take it so seriously or they should toughen up. If that’s a person’s immediate reaction I respectively ask “who are you to decide what people should be or how they should react?” It smacks of the same judgemental attitude the supposed “those people” who run this website are accused of.

It may be harmless banter for some but as I’ve maintained words matter especially on a website that consists only of words. We’ve all said things we regret, myself included, but I have to tell you I always think hard before hitting that save key and publishing my comments. Some of you don’t.

People may say ‘we don’t need rules, we’re adults and can manage ourselves’ but when many feel aggrieved the first thing cited are the same rules you say are not needed in the first place.

I agree we don’t need rules, when we’re all playing nice that is, but when the discussion turns ugly as they do from time to time, it’s not so easy to sort out.

Those who hold the attitude we don’t need rules I say really? Have you seen some of the comments on the alternate website – one word: vicious – and for what should be a neighborhood website a shameful way to treat one of our own.

In regard to those who feel they have “rights”  – really, and your right trumps someone else’s?

It’s funny, MamaMac cited negatively “those in charge” as a sort of  “enemy.” Until this week I too thought there was a “those in charge” and some of you will be surprised to know, as I was surprised, that there isn’t a those but a who – merm – one person (with help from mcdirk) one human being who’s trying his best to manage a diverse group of people and at the same time make astorians.com an enjoyable place to visit.

Few people have the means to create their own town square by building a shopping mall, and those who do one must work very, very hard to create an atmosphere where people want to congregate.

But each and every one of us has the means to create a virtual town square such as astorians.com from the privacy of their own home. So I applaud anyone who is brave and crazy to do so.  

The hard part is making it a place where people want to visit. It didn’t just happen. So let’s give merm some credit and let’s pile-off and let him get on with trying to improve the site for all. Its time to acknowledge he is one human being who’s trying to do his best.

Lastly, Merm has been very open to hearing from everyone that has an opinion on this matter. I'm looking forward to the changes and hope we can all move on.
