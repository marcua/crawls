As long as Greeks, Greek Americans and Eastern Europeans (or many other European cultures) live and travel to Astoria (wealthier Greeks may have moved away, but they still shop and play here) cafes will be a part of this neighborhood.  It is not a matter of 'novelty', it is a matter of culture. 

This may seem strange to you, but just as having your first drink is for Americans, having your first frappe is a right of passage for Greeks.  My niece, who is third generation Greek American was so excited to sit at a cafe for the first time and have her first frappe (on the light side).  She had always had sips of wine and champagne, which I feel helped her develop a healthy attitude towards alcohol.  It is no longer a forbidden substance that she could not wait to drink.  She knew what it tasted like, and when she is older I hope she will be able to handle it without issues.  Since this love of coffee extends to even third generation European Americans, I do not feel that 'cafe society' will wear off any time soon.
