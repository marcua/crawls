What kind of restaurants are you looking for?  I don't know the Greek restaurants, but I can recommend for Mexican: Luna on Broadway between 43 and 44 Streets.  Also there's Los Portales on Broadway near Crescent.

There are a bunch of great restaurants on 31 Ave. - J.J.'s (sushi, Asian fusion) and Il Bambino (Italian, paninis).  I'm going to try Djerdan for burek this weekend, also on 31 Ave.

Another good restaurant is Valverde (Italian), at the corner of Steinway and 36 Ave.

If you like juice, there's a place called Latin Cabana on Steinway near 35 Ave. that does fruit juices.  I believe that Sai's Organics on 30 Ave. also has freshly-squeezed juices.

The whole chain restaurant thing horrifies me.  Clean?  If you mean most of them reek of cleaning fluid when you walk in the door, sure, they're clean.  Overwhelmingly I think they cater to the lack of imagination, keep it safe mentality of American food that makes it the laughing stock of good cuisine around the world.  It's invariably unhealthy - to a degree that is difficult to fathom and I believe borders on abuse.

A few years ago I read a book by Eric Schlosser titled, "Fast Food Nation, the Dark Side of the All American Meal" - that focused on the evils of the fast food industry.  Applebees and Panera's and all that other crap is fast food - just better looking than McDonalds. 

After all the hoopla on this site about the opening of Panera's I tried it and I could not believe what they were foisting (sp?) on us as food.  It was barely edible and I would think twice before offering that to a dog, honestly. 

The problem with giving our business to chain restaurants is that they have an unfair advantage over mom and pops (through various government endorsed incentives) and sooner or later the mom and pops disappear.  then we wind up with entire towns made up of nothing but chain restaurants - and it's these places where people forget what real food tastes like.  It's places like these where people are consuming multiple liters of soda a day, eat garbage and weigh 400 pounds.
