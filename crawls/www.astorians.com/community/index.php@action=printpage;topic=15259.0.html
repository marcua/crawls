Post by: Alison on June 16, 2009, 02:40:00 AM
As someone who recently got herself a new roommate, I'd say she shouldn't bother looking until she's close to be able to move in, and she really does need to get here to meet for in-person interviews. I appreciate that people may not be able to get here for a roommie interview immediately, and I gladly set up appointments for when they would be in town, but I wasn't about to invite someone to live with me if I couldn't meet them in person. I had some very nice-sounding people email me saying that they wouldn't be in town until June 1 (their desired move in date/when I wanted the room filled), but that they wouldn't be able to meet in person. From my perspective, that just wasn't safe/sane. There is also a lot to be said for two people who are living together to be able to be compatible....not necessarily "friends", but it's important to be able to be comfortable with the person you're sharing an apartment with. And from their perspective...how would they know that they'd want to live with me/in this apartment/on this block?

If your daughter can crash on one of her friends couches for a weekend or two so she can go meet potential roommates (that she's already emailed and set up appointments with), that would probably be the best plan.

For an early August move in, I'd say she should start seriously looking in early/mid-July. Like, after the 4th. Start scanning Craigslist earlier though (it's pretty much the only thing people use here to find roommates, other than word-of-mouth), and email if someone says they have an opening for August 1. That said, there will be more listings for 8/1 toward the end of the month than the beginning. Email everyone who has an apartment to rent in Astoria, even if it sounds crappy/has no photo/whatever. A lot of them won't write back. If she finds a place she likes and is invited to move in, she should sign paperwork/get keys ASAP after agreeing, since things can fall through (it happened to me).

And finally, and I promise that I'm not saying this to be snarky or judgemental or anything, but....if someone emailed me looking for a room for their daughter, I probably wouldn't invite them for an interview. From my perspective as a leaseholder/roommate, it's not really in my best interest to have someone live with me who (apparently!) isn't taking the initiative to look up places themselves (am I gonna have to hound them for rent/to clean up after themselves/etc? there's also the "every mom says their kid is great" issue.) Again, I'm just trying to be honest/helpful, and for all I know, she's already looking up places on her own and emailing people as I type. Plus, there's the "are we compatible" issue, which I wouldn't get from a 3rd party (mom) emailing me. Sure, you may have to co-sign for financial reasons or something, but if I had a room to rent, I'm going to primarily deal directly with the person I'd be living with.

The N/W subway in the 'hood goes up 31st St. The R/V stops at 36th St and Northern Boulevard, Broadway and Steinway and 46th St and Broadway.

If I were looking for an apartment, my preference would be to try to be in a box with a Southern boundary on 34th Ave, eastern boundary on Steinway, western boundary on 21st St and a northern boundary on 21st Ave. There are several places outside of that box that are safe and awesome to live--I'd live past Steinway on 30th Ave or 28th Ave--but it's a good parameter to look in that will keep you close to the subway and plenty of vibrant, open late stuff.

I am a night owl and I come home late alone pretty regularly. I lived off of the Ditmars stop for a while and really enjoyed it for many many reasons, but I feel more comfortable coming home late now that I live on 30th Ave. 30th and Broadway seem to have more places open late, and that makes me more comfortable about my walk. Oh man, do I understand what your daughter is going through regarding apartment hunting from out of town while working crazy hours. :) (I worked full-time in baseball up until I moved here, which meant 9-5, then working the games as well. Arg!)

She should write up an email introducing herself that is generic enough to apply to whatever apartment, but friendly and personable. It can be copied & pasted to ads. She doesn't need to ask too many questions right off the bat (that's what the in-person interview is for), but a paragraph or two introducing herself, saying what she does, when she's moving, and what date(s) she'll be in town to meet would be good. Then, if the person wants to meet her, they can set up an appointment. Even if she is working crazy hours, it really only takes 30ish minutes a day of scanning Craigslist and copying that email to people to get the ball rolling. And, she'll start to see the same apartments being listed several times, so she'll know who not to bother emailing again. Then, a few days before she gets here, she should email the people that she has appointments with saying "hey, just confirming we're meeting on Saturday at 1" or whatever - several times on my hunt, I'd show up to find that the room was already filled and they hadn't bothered to tell me.

As a walking time reference, I live at Steinway & 21 Ave, and it's a 10 minute walk to the Ditmars stop on the N (which is actually closer to 23rd Ave, since the tracks don't go all the way to Ditmars. If you zoom in on the satellite, you can see the stairs just north of the Amtrak tracks. All the other stops are actually at their intersections - 36th Ave, B'way, etc.) Google maps does walking directions now too.

Hi there!
When I first moved to NYC as a 21 year old actress with no money, I decided to get a room at the Webster Apartments in Manhattan on 34th and 10th. This establishment was started years ago by Mr. Webster who wanted to create safe and affordable housing for women pursuing careers in NYC. They charge you on a sliding scale based on income, and it is quite low. At the time I was there, six years ago, it was close to the price your daughter wants. And included in the price is breakfast and a hot dinner. It is kind of dorm style. It's women only and this is very strict. They have a gorgeous roof top garden and backyard garden where the ladies like to eat their meals. They have rehearsal studios, laundry facilities (that you pay for...quarters...), a t.v. room and sitting rooms. I made great friends there and it was a lot of fun. Her room will be a shoe box, but will include a bed, a desk, a dresser, a sink and closet. The bed will have bedding and she will be provided with towels. A maid service will come in daily (unless she requests otherwise) to change her linens and vaccum the floor. For toilets and showers, this is dorm style with several on each floor to be used by all the girls on the floor.

It's very bosom buddies   :lol:


It was such a great place for me to live as a young women venturing into NYC. It was a great launching pad for me to explore other living options while having a safe place. Plus I walked to all my auditions and never needed a metro card due to the location.
