Just to clarify, the president does indeed make new laws -- by enacting them. And as the chief executive, he is responsible for executing and upholding laws through the government bureaucracy.

Moreover, each major federal agency or department has a legislative affairs staff whose role is to liaise with Congress and congressional committees for the purposes of drafting legislation (bills). For instance, the Patriot Act was largely initiated by the Executive Branch, specifically the Dept of Justice.

While a president cannot use his legal authority to impose legislation on Congress, he can persuade Congress to enact legislation based on his agenda. Thus the Pennsylvania Avenue two-step.
