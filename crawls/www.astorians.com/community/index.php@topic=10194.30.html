Agreed!!!

As a fellow Astorian, who owns a motorcycle, I find most comments in this thread to be offensive.  Reason being is because you can't blame EVERY motorcyclist for what a select few choose to do. Yes, most of the motorcyclists who race around Astoria Park and pop wheelies, endos and do burnouts are not from around here. But here I am, a motorcyclist who enjoys the sport, and loves to cruise around, who is being labeled solely on the actions of others. I have a right to ride my motorcycle any where I choose to. And no, I don't do wheelies, endos or burnouts. I don't ride my bike in shorts and a t-shirt with my helmet perched on top of my head. I KNOW the risks of riding, I KNOW what can happen if you go down. Road rash is NOT something I would like to get. Instead, I gear up. Full face helmet, which is always on the whole way and strapped, full leather, Kevlar reinforced gloves, leather jacket with protective padding, riding pants and of course riding boots. If I want to race, I'll visit a track.

The street is obviously the last place I want to open the throttle and see how fast I can get from 0-60. With the amount of bad drivers in the area, I always have the "I'm invisible" attitude when I'm out riding. Also, the best way to report someone doing illegal stunts on public roads is to report the license plate. The last thing I want to be is targeted because of the color of my bike. It's happened before on the GCP, got pulled over because I "matched" the description of someone driving recklessly. I don't need to get hassled when I'm out riding like a normal person.
