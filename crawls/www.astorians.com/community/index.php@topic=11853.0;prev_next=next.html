Two years ago I checked out Kid Krazy after being really really impressed by the owner, who had taken the children to the playground while I was there with my 18 month old.

Good things: The owner is fantastic, the toys are cool. Also, they didn't freak out if the kids weren't potty trained, and made sure everyone went to the bathroom and were clean before their folks picked them up.

Bad things: the area for the 2-3 year old set (which had been very spacious) got cut in half a little over a year ago so that they could make room for a group of even younger kids. I haven't visited again (my son was too young, and then the times weren't as convenient for me as the place i ultimately ended up with), but I would definitely check the physical space out before you commit.

Other things: The number of childcare options around here have really increased in the last three years. There's a Montessori school that I believe starts at 2 yrs, if you're interested in them, located next to Astoria Park. (Blossoms, I think they're called...) Have you checked out the Astoria Stay At Home Moms' MeetUp? They can be a good source of info.
