From my understanding the only use at this moment in time for an HDMI cable is if you have a blu-ray player or a ps3 or xbox 360 where the games are output at a 1080p resolution.

Movies and TV is normally output at 480i resolution.  Unless you have a HD cable box which the output is actually Hi-Def (1080i/p).

So the HDMI is great if you have a video game system or blu-ray player to view full HD on it.  Otherwise, normal output resolution from tv/movies even with HDMI cable will still be 480i resolution.

Title: Re: mounting a lcd flatscreen ?
Post by: Bentleys Dad on September 06, 2008, 08:24:04 PM
All you have to do is get yourself a stud finder, locate the metal studs, get some toggle bolts (http://stores.steelworksstore.com/catalog/toggle.gif) with washers, affix the lcd mount to the wall with the toggle bolts, mount the tv and you're done. It will hold. If you're not sure you'll trust a few toggle bolts, then mount a piece of 1/2" plywood, then the tv mount on top of that and then the lcd. Trust me, it'll hold. I've done it hundreds of times.
