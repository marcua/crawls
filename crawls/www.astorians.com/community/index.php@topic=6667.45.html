I wonder what impact the softening of the real estate market is having in Astoria?   The reason I ask is that I live on a block that includes large apartment buildings.   In my building when one tenant moves out another is in immediately.   I noticed for the first time since I moved onto this block in 2001 that another one of the buildings on the street had posted a sign on the front of the building advertising vacancies.   

I don't think the real estate market in NYC or the Northeast has collapsed the way it has in other states.   I think in California the median price of a home dropped something like 63% and there is an interesting piece on the front page of the Times today talking about some of the real estate deals out there.   Unfortunately, none of them are in the Northeast.

For example, a woman in Florida bought a house that sold for $370K in '06 for $187K and had her closing costs taken care of by the seller.  Another guy bought a townhouse for $87K that sold for $260K in '06.   In the same development another co-op sold for $75K and a third for $65K.   So, deflation has hit.
