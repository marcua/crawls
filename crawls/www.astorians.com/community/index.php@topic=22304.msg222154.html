Went yesterday.  I also think it's a great addition to the neighborhood, and especially Ditmars, which generally lacks more interesting food options.  But being more of a meat eater, I can only enjoy this just so much. But I will say that what I had was very flavorful and delicious.  I got the Tonkotsu Shoyo (creamy pork broth ramen) and added extra pork and a poached egg.  It wound up very very rich and incredibly tasty.  We had both the beef and pork Gyoza.  They were great.  Better than most places other than maybe JJs.  And I really liked the sweet mashed potato they put underneath.  The Niko Man little sandwiches are very tasty.  The pork belly didn't seem appealing to me.  But the shrimp was great.  Served over a very rich egg salad.  The servers are young and they move quickly from table to table.  English abilities vary.  We were asked over and over for our drink order.  I wouldn't call it bad service, just a little sloppy and too rushed.  And the yelling hello and goodbye to people coming and going is cute, but then can become annoying if you're not in the mood for it.  And yeah - the benches are not at all ideal, in my mind.  Not terribly uncomfortable but it was about the jacket and whatever else you have.  Considering how tasty the food is, I hope they up the game a little bit with the atmosphere and furnishing.  If so, then I think it would really be a winner.   The owner, or senior manager visited our table at the end and was very kind.  She described all the foods and was interested in our opinions.  She mentioned some specials, but sadly, none of the many servers we had said a word to us about specials.   It also seemed to me that the customers they draw are a bit younger and more diverse than normally drawn to Ditmars eateries.  I think that's a good thing.
