So I made Indian food for myself last night. Some puff pastry potato samosas (baked puff pastry is an excellent substitute for the usual deep-fried wrappers!), and some cabbage and carrots with various spices. Quite tasty. I make a fair amount of Indian food, probably every couple weeks or so.

A bit after I finish eating, and settle myself down in front of the DVD player, my landlady downstairs calls. This is usually a bad sign, as she only calls when she's angry, but the cat's not running around on the kitchen floor, so it's hard to say what's up.

Here's what she had to say (paraphrased): "Harlan, what are you cooking? Are you making Indian food? I can't have you cooking Indian food! The spices get into the house and they don't get out. I won't rent to Indian people, and I can't have you cooking Indian food. You must open the windows and get rid of the smell! And this summer, don't run the air conditioners [they're hers -H] when you're cooking Indian food, because the smell gets into them and you can't get it out! You can't cook Indian food!"

Argh. I'm tempted to cook Indian every day for the next week. She has no right, legal or otherwise, to keep me from cooking what I would like. And saying she won't rent based on ethnicity? Not a classy move on her part...
