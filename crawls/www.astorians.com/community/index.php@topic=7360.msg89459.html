I'm sure I'm butchering this but I believe the reconstitution in 1990 had to do with switching
from a public Co-op to a private one. Meaning the Co-op was no longer eligible for
government funding and there were no more restrictions on what could be charged
to buy in and become a shareholder.

Prior to 1990 the asking price was REALLY low, several or so hundred dollars per room to buy in.
I've heard before 1990 it was IMPOSSIBLE to get in here as no one ever left knowing that sooner or
later they were going to go private. Once the reconstitution took place, lots of apartments became available
as people had been waiting for the price increase to sell.

That was long before my time and someone could do a much better job explaining it all.
