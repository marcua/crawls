Kabob Cafe
« on: February 28, 2009, 11:49:25 PM »
I thought I'd go ahead and make this post while I'm at it (might as well since I'm ranting).

Kabob Cafe was a letdown.  Don't get me wrong.  The food isn't bad, it just didn't live up to the hype by quite a bit.

I think the price was definitely on the high side.  We ordered 3 appetizers (beet salad, lamb cheeks, lamb liver) and 1 entree (lamb shank) and 1 bottle of water.  The total was $72.  We went on an empty Sunday lunch.

Food: Hands down, they have the BEST hot sauce.  You can tell they make it fresh.  There's a strong hint of garlic, chilis, fennel, and some kind of vegetable that gives the sauce body, perhaps eggplant or tomato.  The beet salad was well prepared.  It was light, refreshing, and the beet was paired well with dill.  It really was a very simple salad, almost too simple, but I appreciated it nonetheless.  I love how they dust zatar on the plates, it's very beautiful and appetizing.  Then the meal started going downhill.  The lamb cheeks were cooked as confit and served with raw egg on top.  I didn't taste the cheeks, there was absolutely no meat texture of any sort.  In fact, I only tasted fatty mush with a raw egg on top.  At first I liked it.  I really wanted to like it.  My wife gave up eating it after her third bite and passed her plate to me.  I finished both plates but was very reluctant to do so.  Then the liver came out.  I think there might have been 2 ounces of liver total on the plate, and it was diced up into little cubes.  The liver was stir fried with green peppers, onions, garlic, and spices.  It was very greasy (red oil running all over the plate) and way too salty by a mile.  I was too embarrassed to tell the chef that, I didn't want to offend him especially since he seemed like such a nice, expressive guy.  Finally, the lamb shank came.  It was mediocre to decent.  The meat wasn't tender enough and the flavor of the sauce did not penetrate.  Overall, it tasted like a piece of meat that was cooked separately with sauce ladled on.  Maybe this is what the chef intended although I highly doubt it since it's supposed to be stewed/braised.  The sauce was nice and rustic, I loved the escarole and frisee in the sauce.  Pairing it with the hot sauce and zatar made the lamb taste much better than if it was just served alone.  Overall, it was a meh.  I know I can do better at home.

Portions: Portion sizes were good.  That being said, it was unfortunate that I could not taste the cheek in the cheek dish, and there was so little liver on the liver dish that it almost seemed like a garnish.  The beet and lamb dishes respectively were fine.

Value: Commanding high prices, you'd expect better cuisine.  $72 for lunch with no beverage is quite expensive.  I mean, their prices afterall are very close to Manhattan prices, but the taste was far from impressive.  At best, I'd say the taste was decent.  But for decent cuisine, you need to lower prices and make it more into a nightly neighborhood type spot.  Just my opinion.

Food: B (there really is no excuse for a completely oversalted dish, it just means the chef isn't tasting his cooking anymore)
Portions: B+ (no complaints since upscale food isn't designed to get you stuffed)
Value: B- (I'd recommend this place as a novelty cuisine but it isn't worth going back too often)
Recommend: Yes, for an adventurous dining experience where it's more about the experience than the food
