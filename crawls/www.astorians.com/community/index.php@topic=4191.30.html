Dude, we live in the outer boroughs, smoking is a way of life here.  I hate it, but I've learned to accept it and deal.  I went to Woodside to a bunch of irish pubs this weekend, and there was smoking inside the bar like no tomorrow, from beginning to end.  It's the same in Sunnyside, and there isn't a bar in Astoria that I've been to after midnight, that I haven't seen smoking.  I would name them but that would be all of them but the Beer Garden, and that's only because of the massive security and its historical landmark status.  Queens has a highly immigrant population and the bars are loath to tell their customers to leave, they'd rather pay the fines.  I'm sure this happens in parts of Brooklyn and the Bronx, and even Manhattan as well - although in less obvious spots. 

If I don't want to smell smoke while I'm eating, I leave.  If I don't want to drink and smell smoke, I leave.  There are enough places that there is not smoking going on that I'll be quite fine.

That being said, there is something to be said about your neighbors not smelling the smoke.  I lived above The Batcave on Ditmars and 38th for 3 years.  Their windows opened into our courtyard and the smoke came into our apartments at all hours.  It was impossible to escape, and it took years - years- of calling 311 to complain, for the landlord not to renew their lease.  We couldn't stand them.  It really sucked to smell that all the time.  I really do wish the laws were enforced in the outer boroughs.
