As many of you know I'm a big supporter of Barack Obama. But I'm deeply disappointed in him today after reading this:

Quote from: Article
http://blog.washingtonpost.com/the-trail/2008/06/20/obama_supports_fisa_legislatio.html

In his most substantive break with the Democratic Party's base since becoming the presumptive nominee, Obama declared he will support the bill when it comes to a Senate vote, likely next week, despite misgivings about legal provisions for telecommunications corporations that cooperated with the Bush administration's warrantless surveillance program of suspected terrorists.

I am especially uncomfortable with the free pass it gives telecoms that violated the privacy of many Americans by allowing the government to "wire tap" telephone and internet connections unchecked and without a warrant.

This is the kind of thing that makes me deeply concerned that maybe my candidate really isn't about "change". I sincerely want to see Washington change, but I don't think we're going to get that by supporting legislation that legitimizes the type of behavior we've seen for the last 8 years.

I wrote Obama and expressed my concern through the contact form on his website, or his DC office, and would encourage anyone else who is interested to do the same.
