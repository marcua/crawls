Embassy Suites in Maui is pretty nice, as well as the Hilton Hawaiian Village in Honolulu.  My all-time favorite was on the main island of Hawaii, the Hilton Waikoloa Village which was amaaazing.. it had a train that ran through the center of the resort to get you from one end of the resort to the other, with stops in between for the shopping area, etc. It also had its own beach where you could rent out all these different kinds of water bikes, kayaks, snorkel gear.  It had a small, very plain little chapel on a hill where I actually saw a small wedding ceremony, and it had its own flock of flamingos that grazed the lawn. It was probably pretty expensive, but I have no clue as I was around 15 or 16 years old and wasn't paying. Don't go to Kauai unless you plan on being really bored haha... although they filmed Jurassic Park there, so that's cool.  Hope this helps?  :-)

I suggest renting a condo instead of staying in a hotel or resort. You get a more authentic experience and don't have to deal with the honeymooner/touristy stuff. We rented a condo on Maui and because it was so much cheaper than the resorts we were able to stretch a 1 week stay in two 2 weeks. We made our own breakfast every morning and sat on our lanai  to enjoy it. Really nice and peaceful.

We did all our research on tripadvisor.com. They are an amazing resource. Good luck.

Hey, don't know if you've booked yet or not, but I emailed my friend when you posted this (who honeymooned in Hawaii, she went to Kauai and Oahu), and she wrote back :)  Not sure how much info is helpful/useful to you, but I thought I'd post it anyway. Their honeymoon was for 2 weeks in late August 05, so right around the same time of year you'll be going.

(Tim is her husband, and Liberty Travel is in CT, although there may be Liberty Travel in NY too, not sure.)

----------------------

In Kauai my Aunt and Uncle gave us a timeshare to stay in for the week.  So thats unhelpful.  But there was a really nice hotel near us called the Princeville Resort http://www.princevillehotelhawaii.com/.  I am pretty sure that is where Tims brother David and his wife stayed on their honeymoon and they loved it.
 
I loved Kauai so much - Tim and I are going back there for our 10yr anniversary.  Kauai is the most relaxing place on earth!
 
The second week we went to Oahu and stayed at the JW Marriott and Ihilani Resort.  http://www.ihilani.com/.  The place was awesome.  We had a great time.  Oahu wasn't my favorite - it reminded me too much of home (bustling city, rush hour traffic which we were stuck in 3 times (including the rush to the airport)).
 
I don't know how much each place cost per night but we paid about $3000 to Liberty Travel which included 5 days, 6 nights at the JW Marriott, car rental on both islands for the 2 weeks, breakfast buffet at the JW (which was freakin awesome - it was a $40pp buffett breakfast) and 2 round trip tickets from NY to Hawaii (with a stopover in LAX) and the cost of the small plane to go from Kauai to Oahu.  Also we got free leis on the island jumping plane (which we missed because we took an earlier flight), and a voucher for a free pair of pearl earrings which were really nice.
 
Also, I don't know if your friend has ever been to Hawaii, but if s/he is going to rent a car make sure they get the full insurance.  Tim and I somehow destryoed the car on Kauai (we went down alot of rocky roads) and the place was ready to rip us a new one until Tim showed them we payed for the full insurance.  Also don't leave anything in your car - the local punks like to target tourists and break into the cars to steal your stuff.
