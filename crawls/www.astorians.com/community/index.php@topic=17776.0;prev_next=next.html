I spent a decade in the Bronx, 87 to 97, around the Fordham area (nowhere near the college), and people talked about petty crimes like this all the time, because they surely happened there, too.  This was pre-internet, so I guess there was no online gathering place to mention them when they happened.  The feeling there, for me at least, was far more rural than Astoria -- more greenery, and hills, a lot of people living there straight from the countryside in the Dominican and Puerto Rico.  The Bronx is a great piece of land that got a bit seedy over the years, probably the most beautiful borough of NYC when you scrape away the grit.  I lived through the prime crack years to the point where walking down the street at night was a crunchy experience -- you couldn't avoid stepping on the empty capsules around certain known dens.

These things were never ignored in my neighborhood.  Someone smashed your neighbor's car window and stole his cassette player from the dash, he told you about it, and you commiserated, and looked out for each other.  Not sure what you're getting at here, other than a faux toughness related to an image of a place most people know nothing about.  For those of us who do, we know there was a lot more going on than that there.  Could I ask where you're from in the Bronx and how long you lived there?
