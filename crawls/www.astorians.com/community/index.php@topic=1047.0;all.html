The fine print of NaNoWriMo
« Reply #20 on: November 07, 2005, 05:07:05 PM »
we only have to finish a novel, not a good novel.  for me, last year, it was going to be an exercise.  if you want to finish you just write.  the side effect is that you enjoy it more because you don't second guess anything.  if you're plugging along and you're like, "man, this sucks," instead of being all down on yourself you just keep pecking along.  likewise when you have a couple of sections where you're like, "wow, this actually is not half-bad," you don't take that seriously either.  you just write.  very zen.  in the absence of judgment, it's easy.

That's an excellent approach to any form of writing. Get it out there and edit later. Whenever I'm stuck on something (I'm a copywriter), I think that I'm writing an email to a friend. That way, I'm more in the moment (yes, and it's very "zen"), openly communicative and conversational.

I remember an article or a book on writing in which the writer said something to the effect that sometimes it's OK for her characters to say, "ca-ca poo-poo" from time to time. :lol:

Right now I'm stuck on a freelance assignment I have. Ugh. I think what I'll do is actually start typing my copy in an email message to myself. That should be interesting...

That's cool.  Are you going to be at the NaNoWriMo meet tonight?  I don't know the "rules" but even if you're not doing it this year it'd probably be fine to join us (are there really that many of us?).

the email is a good point.  last year, my first year doing it, it was a collective effort in a writer's group i was in.  we decided to do it on a whim with 18 days left.  we just grabbed the ball, wrote, and then emailed it to everyone.  then someone else would say they were taking it for a while and add, then email again.  it was always getting worked on and we finished (just barely) by the deadline.  this year i'm doing it solo, but i keep to the email.  i work on it at work, email the latest draft, then pick it up at home on my desktop, then email it again, pick it up on one of my laptops, email it again...   it keeps it casual.

you should try it amigo.  it's not as daunting as it sounds.  and it's fun.
