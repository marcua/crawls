Thanks, Merm.  I really am uncomfortable with confrontation.  And as enigmacat said, changing the subject is a defense mechanism for me.  And while I really enjoy some of the heated debates on here, I really dislike when namecalling takes place.  While I admit to some borderline tactics with gatorboy/gleason a while back, I generally try to stay clear of those conversations.  I have really enjoyed being a part of this online community for the last year, and hope that it continues to be the great community asset and social builder that it has been, at least for me.

I can understand when someone might lose their cool when seeing questionable tactics used in discussions, or hijacking of threads, as I have myself.  But the thing we have to understand, as has been stated many times, reading words on a page puts a barrier between us.  It's quite easy to attack someone when you aren't looking them in the face.  As merm properly stated, it's best to give the benefit of the doubt on here, as it's 100% impossible to fully understand someone's purpose by only having the words on a page to form a judgement.  Sarcasm is undetectable in most cases.  Sexual innuendo can often be completely lost, as well.  :-o  So I think a good practice is to step away for a moment.  And think reasonably and logically about the person on the other end.  Chances are, they don't hate you.  And in most cases, probably are in general agreement with your oppinion, but semantics get in the way.

OK, that's my $.04.3333.
