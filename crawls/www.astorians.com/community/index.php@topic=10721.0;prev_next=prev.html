Montauk is fabulous and I'm surprised that no one mentioned Gosman's Dock - my all time favorite fish place until I moved to Astoria and discovered Elias Corner for Fish.   You can also stay at the Driftwood motel for not much money. It's right next to Hither Hills State Park and some rooms even have their own grill and a short walk to the beach, pool, tennis courts, etc.   It's still there and was there when I was a kid in the 1970's.   

We went for the day to Montauk last year and concluded that it's way to long a drive for a day trip. However we are not morning persons and it took us three hours to get there and we missed the turn onto the LIE coming back and it was 3 hours.   Going through East Hampton during the afternoon is a nightmare.  Actually from Southampton to Easthampton is a nightmare. 

As an old Hamptonite - I also went to school there in the 1980's - duck and close your eyes as you pass through East Hampton.  What used to be an old fishing town has become even more goash than the Short Hills Mall.   Imagine my surprise, not having been there in over 15 years, to see a Tiffany boutique right next to Coach Bags.  It looked like Madison Avenue.   Also we saw a Fresh Direct truck on the way out.

All of that said the few hours we had basking in the sun at Hither Hills and eating fish at Gosman's was pure bliss.  One warning.  Do watch out for the seagulls the size of boats.   I mean those guys could take down a blue fish or, I dare say, a shark!   Don't get on their bad side and don't feed them.  :-)

Yeah, we went out there several times last year.  It's awesome!  Our drive usually takes about 2 and a half hours, but I can see where it would take people 3 sometimes.  We went on the ends of the Summer season(twice in like April/May and once at the beginning of Sept).  If you hit the tide just right, the waves will be crashing against the rocks that go all the way around the point.  Really beautiful.  Also, at certain times, on the south side of the point, the waves will come in and grab all of the small, smooth rocks and pull at them.  It makes the most awesome crackling sound.  You can walk all the way around the point on the rocks, just watch your step!  If you drive out that way, I would also recommend Cooper's Beach in Southampton(ranked 4th best in the country, I believe) and The Clam Bar: http://www.wordhampton.com/clam_bar.html.  I would also encourage you to drive off the hwy and check out some of the houses.
