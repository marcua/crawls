Three of us went to this new place, called Wave Thai, last night. Mostly very good! Here's the full review...

Food:

This was one of the better Thai places we've been to, and may be the best in Astoria. It's definitely aimed at American audiences, and is not aimed at extreme authenticity and exoticism, as is Sripraprai. Definitely better than Ubol's Kitchen, and I think better than Arnhem and Thai Angel as well. We got five dishes: Vegetarian Dumplings and Calamari Crispy as appetizers, Grilled Flank Steak salad, Chicken with Eggplant in Spicy Brown Sauce, and Street Noodles with Tofu. Excellent flavors in all. The dumplings were good, with a flavorful diping sauce. The calamari was excellent. The salad was admirably pungent, with the steak correctly cooked medium. The chicken in the chicken dish was perhaps a little dry, but the dish was otherwise quite good, even when ordered mild for a dining companion. The street noodles with tofu were quite good as well. I'll be interested in trying some of their more ambitious dishes, including several types of whole fish, and a roasted duck curry.

Atmosphere:

The restaurant is very modern, with a plate-glass front wall, red and gold colors, bullseye lighting fixtures, and a Buddhist fountain near the front. However, it has a real problem with drafts, there being no airlock with the door. In addition, along one wall, the (cool-looking) bench is made of concrete, with no pillows, which is both cold to sit on and very hard. It's going to be hard to persuade me to go on a weekend winter evening, when it'll be crowded and being stuck by the drafty door is a real possibility. However, summer's just around the corner, and off-hours probably allow for better seating.

Service:

The service was quite attentive and friendly, however we had one problem. Our bill was $51, and we paid them $60, including tip. As we were leaving, I noticed that they had put only $4 back on the table, so I suggested that they had mis-counted. The waiter said that we had only paid $55, but I had counted twice and was quite sure. A white guy (the owner? not sure) came out and assured me that the $5 was there, and that they had probably just mis-counted. Either they did in fact mis-count, or else one of the employees was trying to boost his tip a bit, and got caught... Keep an eye out for that if you go!

Overall, a very pleasant addition to the neighborhood!

Ate at Wave today
« Reply #14 on: November 26, 2005, 08:45:55 PM »
Having had enough Turkey, ribs, potatoes, and pie to feed many small armies over the last couple of days we wade our way North of GCP to check out Wave.  I'm always on the hunt for new Thai - I find our options along 30th only fair.  We had four excellent meals (there were FOUR of us eating so that worked out well).  I really loved the street noodles and I think their version of Tom Kai Gai (coconut chicken soup) is one of the best I've had in this city.   We lucked out on strolling in at 3:10 and finding there is a lunch special available until 3:30.  low price, includes a bite size spring roll and soup of the day.
 
The owners were very nice and friendly.  They couldn't believe their luck at finding a NYC neighborhood that has the population to support 2 japanese restaurants but no THAI!!  I think the locals are the lucky ones in this deal.  Go.  Eat.  Support them.   :)
