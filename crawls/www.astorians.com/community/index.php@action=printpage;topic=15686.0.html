Title: Re: Anyone down to bike ride?
Post by: ManicMaurice on July 30, 2009, 12:17:32 PM
^^ What he said ^^ :wink:

I would call my bike fast enough for me. I'm sure it wouldn't win a downhill road race with a dedicated road bike, but that's not really what I was looking for. I wanted a bike that would work for riding around the city and the variety of different terrain we encounter here. I opted for the 29er rims and tires because they are a bit larger in diameter than a typical off-road / mountain-bike so they take pot holes, bumps, and curbs a bit better. I also choose a bike with a steel frame because I liked how it rode over the felt-stiffer-to-me but slightly lighter aluminum frame bikes. I pretty much looked at bikes from the manufacturer's "urban" and "city" lines because they actually fit what I was looking for -- hybrid / mountain-bike frames, maneuverable mountain-bike handle bars, slightly more upright but not cruising riding position, semi smooth tires (definitely not knobby like off-road tires), mechanical disk brakes, and upper mid hardware components.

It's all what your looking to do. If you want to zip around the roadway in central park then you are probably going to want a dedicated road bike, but if you are looking for something that will get you around town and allow you to explore areas that you might not venture to on foot then a "urban," "city," "cyclocross," "fitness," or "commuter" bike are probably what your looking for. IMHO :mrgreen:
