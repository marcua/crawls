First off I've been here a handful of times and never had any issues...until tonight.  I got there with a small group (there were 3 of us) about 10:45 and we order a round...the bartender "mistakenly" shorted us $10 on our change.  About 1/2 hr. later we ordered another round and again, he shorted us $10 on the change.  I would  have forgiven him once but twice is just ridiculous, not to mention he NEVER apologized but at least he gave us our money both times when we called him on it.  A bit later our group had grown to about 10 people and the bartender put down a round of drinks on one person's bar tab...the problem was no one had ordered the round so it was a pretty good chunk of money we had to all lay out for another round.  Needless to say, he didn't make out too well on his tips tonight!

I hope this is not a sign of things to come and no, nobody was intoxicated because I've certainly seen this trick tried in the past when the bartender assumes you're drunk enough not to notice they short you...granted, I've only seen it in the city - never in Astoria.

If a transaction is going through on a credit card they must charge you tax. All drinks are taxable and when using cash for a transaction most bars won't charge tax (or have tax built into the price of the drink already if they're smart so you don't notice the odd number) because the government can't track cash transactions.

Yeah, the government can't track them up until recently,  New York State imposed a new law on reporting inventory purchases, bars have been clamping down on not taxing cash transactions. My friend owns a place, and he told me that it is really serious now, the state is low on cash so they are looking to plug all the holes in their tax revenue. So just because you got charged the tax you were supposed to be charged on a transaction doesn't mean you should write a place off, the place does not want to get shut down by the state.
