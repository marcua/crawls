This might sound too technical and convoluted, but I was bored at work and got interested in the different posts.  I thought about it in a legal framework.

The NY State Social Service law has a lengthy defintion of neglect and it appears, as the law usually is, based on what would be considered "reasonable." http://public.leginfo.state.ny.us/menugetf.cgi?COMMONQUERY=LAWS  Is it reasonable for a parent or guardian to allow a 9 year old child to ride the subway alone?  Our resident lawyers Barrister and lanseaux or perhaps a social worker who deals with children can probably can explain it better, but say the kid was picked up by the police based on a citizen being concerned about the safety of the young child.  Would this would be referred to Family Court and a social worker asked to evaluate the child?  I might be way off base here, but it doesn't seem like the law sets a certain age regarding neglect.  Below is the specific language defining neglect that I believe would apply.

 in providing the child with proper supervision or guardianship, by unreasonably  inflicting  or  allowing  to  be  inflicted  harm, or a substantial risk thereof.
