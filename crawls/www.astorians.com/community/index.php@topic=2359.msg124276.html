I think I must be becoming more easily frustrated with folks.  I almost said something unkind today on my way into the office.  I get to the bottom of the stairs at the Broadway station just in time to get cut off by a woman a few years older than me, maybe early 40s.  She's clearly not dressed for work, unless it's a very casual office, and opts to go up the stairs incredibly slowly, in the middle of the stairs so you can not get past, on both flights.  I hear the train coming, I'm already late and eager not to be later, and tried hard not to get too close, but to be ready to move past should the opportunity present itself.  Alas, she made sure that me, and the other unfortunate people behind us got to take a very leisurely walk up the stairs, then had the great idea to stop at the top of the stairs and turn around to give me a nasty look, what joy!  I got around her at that point and just made the train.  What makes a person park themselves in the MIDDLE of a staircase blocking people clearly dressed for work (in a suit) and goes slowly when they hear the train moving into a station?  I almost never say something, I just think it's rude, but what in the world could this person be thinking?  If I'm going to move slowly - say I had an intense leg workout or sprained something, I would move to the side of the staircase and let folks pass - wouldn't most folks do that?
