Interesting responses on this thread.

Just focusing on the narrow point of bookstores, the store on Broadway seems to have had at least four or five names over the past ten years and a few owners. Seaburn, the current occupant, has three locations during that period of time.

There was for a month or two, a discount bookstore in the old babies r us space on Northern and 48th.

The future of small private bookstores is grim. AOL did an interesting little sidebar earlier this week and highlighted 5 things that may not last the decade, and inde book stores, like payphones and film stores do not have good prospects. Today you can go on abebooks.com and the like and order just about anything out of print.

To truly understand the prospects of B&N we should examine the direction of both local retailing and our community in general. This thread skirted the issue, then pulled back.

On one hand, some claim that freshly minted yuppies are pouring into the community, then on the other, the local shopping districts are aligning themselves with other shopping areas in Queens by becoming ethnicly oriented.

Can both be happening at the same time?

Certainly the recent declining quality of both stores and merchandise is evident, something becomes glaringly obvious when compared to other parts of the city that successfully compete with us on B&N locations. The trash that swirls around the retail districts certainly does not help.

There are a lot of complaints that we all read about on this board on local shopping, complaints that have oddly not been picked up on this thread.
   
I find it chilling, and a bit sad, that a segment of our community breezily has written off local shopping in favor of big boxes outside of our community. Some Astoria boosters there.

What will happen to our community when they roof over the Sunnyside railyards?

Yes, we can get a B&N into our community. But this requires a lot of work, and fresh leadership. Couching arguments in terms that were discussed decades ago certainly makes this glaringly obvious.

The current construction going on in the Sunnyside railyards is the extension of the 63rd street tunnel (the one that the F train runs through) to link the up with the LIRR  allowing LIRR trains to access Grand Central station.

The deck over the railyards idea is a project that’s been either ‘threatened’ or ‘promised’ depending on your viewpoint, since at least the early 70s but nothing has ever come of it.

There was a semi-serious plan floated in the 80s to build a deck and a stadium but it thankfully fell through.
