I posted earlier in this thread and since then, have become a member of Matrix and I have to say that I'm quite happy with it.

I was initially going back and forth debating between the seeming convenience of The Rock and the cheaper prices of Matrix.  I live around 33rd/Ditmars and discovered that in spite of it seeming to be quite a hike, it's really only an extra five minute walk (hey, good cardio!) for significantly lower monthly fees.  The people who work at the gym are all very friendly (I dealt with Jessica when I got my membership), they are very "hands off" and not constantly badgering you about training and classes and things of that matter, the facility is good, they have great machines, usually at least two for each muscle group (and for a few, two of the same machine) meaning less wait times for a machine, the equipment seems new, they have quite a few large-screen TVs for the viewing pleasure of people doing cardio, and the music doesn't suck.  There's different pricing plans, but I currently pay the $59/month for my month-by-month contract.  The gym also offers a large amount of classes, and there's a bunch of little perks in regards to personal training and freezing one's account, etc. the specifics of which I forget but that they'll talk to you about should you decide to join.

I don't work for them and no one told me to say any of this.  In fact, to be real, I'll list a few cons.  For me, perhaps the biggest con on Rock vs. Matrix is simply the hours.  Matrix is open til 11pm M-Th, which is late, but for someone like me who waits until late in the evening to work out, I wish it were open an hour or so later.  Ditto for Friday (open til 10pm) and Sat-Sun (open til 8).  The gym is also rather stuffy and humid so prepare to sweat, even if just standing around.  Other than that, no complaints really.  I'm very satisfied with my membership and think everyone should check it out, at least for the free trial run to see how they like it.
