What about a locavore Indian place? Source regional organic vegetables and beans, pastured lamb and poultry, fish from the region, combined with best-quality spices and great, simple recipes. Rustic, low-key decor, pleasant and informal service. That could be a winner. Neon, chrome, and 12" tall $40 entrees? Maybe not.

thank you guys for all your input , it is very exciting for me to see everybody so psyched about having a new Indian place in Astoria, i know i  seem to have contradicted myself in this issue , i guess i am having a little trouble communicating my vision to you guys. Well first of all i am a Indian woman chef originally from India , born and raised. and i have seen lot of people mentioning jackson heights and also the fact that you seem to find the same kind of foods in Indian restaurant even though we have different regions with different flavors, it is also good to know that most people would love to experience spicy Indian food. well for starters , even though i am planning to put authentic foods in restaurant which are found in other places , i would also like to introduce food from other regions of India, i mentioned Indian food with American twist i did not realise that i was causing confusion, i come from a original Punjabi family and have learnt cooking from my grandmother and mother , who cooks dishes not experienced by my American fellows, one example i will give you is something called egg curry which i am sure is never seen in any Indian restaurant in new york but has been a recipe used in my family for generations. i take the word American twist back, trendy for me means a restaurant when you enter you can see its cultural influence in the place as well as food. i am definitely going to focus more on food and quality. thank you for input.
