Title: Re: Clearly not a cat lover
Post by: Harlan on September 21, 2006, 12:46:55 PM
Merm, to me the difference is the reason why the animal exists. If people didn't eat beef, drink milk, or wear leather, there would be essentially no cows in North America. The reason why a cow exists on this planet, and (if it's lucky and is going to be eaten by a yuppie) has the pleasure of eating grass on a pasture, is because we bred it. So humanely killling the cow in order to eat it is part of its existence, and is not a violence against its existence.

For a cat, however, it was bred to be an unbearably cute companion animal for people. Its reason for being is to sit on the couch and purr while we watch TV. Without us, there would be a lot fewer cats, and they would mostly not look like inbred Persians. 

It's a violation of our moral contract with a cat to kill it in a fight with our girlfriend, and thus violate its reason for being, just as it would be a violation of our moral contract with a cow to shoot it and let it rot.
