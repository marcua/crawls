Hi all.

Despite the rain, we had a full house for Andrey Gritsman's reading on October 24th, and the open mic was wonderful.  Attempted sestinas, poems read in Greek and Russian, and a poem titling contest were just a few highlights.

On Monday, November 21st at 8pm, we're going to do it all over again, this time featuring Tim Suermondt, a NYC native and outstanding poet.  Tune in for more information about Tim, but in the meantime, gather a few poems or a story and come down to Waltz to share a night of great local literature!

All About Tim Suermondt
« Reply #1 on: November 06, 2005, 11:26:48 PM »
Tim Suermondt is the auther of two chapbooks, The Fangerous Women with Their Colles (The Manny Trio Press, 1998) and Greatest Hits (Pudding House Press 2002). His poetry has appeared in many journals, including Petry, Petry Northwest, Barrow Street, The Georgia Review, Indiana Review, Southern Poetry Review, Northeast Corridor, and River Styx, as well as such online journals as The Cortland Reveiw and Painted Bride Quarterly. He has poems in Poetry After 9-11: An Anthology of New York Poets (Melville House Books, 2002) and an anthology of poetry about Walt Whitman from the University of Iowa Press. He has been nominated for the Pushcart Prize and was a winner of the 63rd Street Y Open Voice Award. He lives in Queens, NY and is an executive recruiter for stockbrokers.
 
Sample Poem:

BECAUSE IT'S SPRING IN THE CITY

No one I'm jealous of,
no one I hate.
No Fear of death and taxes,
poetry readings and terrorists--
the walks I took
along the Mekong
fill me with pride
instead of shame.
     Love,
look out the window.
I'm the fool dancing in front
of Jakob's Hardware.
Don't be alarmed for me--
I'm coming up happy and soon.
