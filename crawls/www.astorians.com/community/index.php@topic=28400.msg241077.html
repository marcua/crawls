The only reason we might start to see more empty storefronts is precisely BECAUSE of new business like Tru.  They are attracting a new demographic to the area which in turn affects residential and commercial rents, which then drives out the mom and pop businesses. 

There should be a place for old AND new school businesses.  I happen to like both types,  but my fear is that...with the way things are progressing...that there's going to be less and less room for the old school.

Take Brooklyn Bagel for e.g.  Yeah, I like a good bagel like everybody else but lines out the door on the weekends?  For what?  They're not THAT special.  And just a few blocks away is the oh-so-charming old school Bagel House...with perfectly good bagels.  But no one goes there...cuz it's not as 'cool'.  Which is precisely why I prefer to patronize Bagel House.  I really value the old school version of Astoria...if I wanted all hipster stuff I would have chosen to live in Wburg.  ;-)  For me it's crucial that I  support the old school places...otherwise I'd be a hypocrite to then lament them as they close down, one by one....
