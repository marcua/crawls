There is another thread about the acropolis in this forum.  It gets poor reviews to say the least.   I've lived in the acropolis for almost 2 years.  I liked the area and the price was right so I bought the place.  Someone in the other thread said that there was no coop approval board, but I still had to fill out paperwork for it.  I put in hardwood floors before I moved in and had to completely redo the kitchen.  I had a small problem with roaches when I moved in, but haven't seen 1 roach in over a year and half now.  I've never seen a roach come up from the drain, but I put in stoppers just in case because I hear thats one of the ways they get into apts in general.  I also caulked the hell out of the place.  I rarely ever see a small spider now.  I've heard of other apts that are awful, but mine isn't.  There have been a lot of people buying these places up and fixing them up.

Someone in the other thread mentioned that the walls are paper thin, mine aren't.  Residents in the building and other dog owners in the area often don't pick up after their dogs.  I also used to have neighborhood kids hang out in front of my entrance, but they soon left after they replaced the security guards.  If you are thinking about buying then you should plan on doing some remodeling.  If you are renting then its possible to find a good place, but you'll need to make sure its been fixed up.  I don't know too much about the other apts, but a girl I dated told me that a friend of hers lived in the Acropolis and it was not somewhere she would ever lived.  When she saw my apt she changed her mind. 

I live on 35th st.  Hope I've helped you.

One thing you might want to think about is that they don't seem to really fix anything when it's broken.  I don't live there but last February/March they brought in a mobile steam unit the size of a semi trailer and parked it in front of the building on the corner of 21st Ave and 33rd Street and left it there until May when you no longer have to provide heat.  For the first week or so it was okay until the water condensation started dripping onto the ground and forming massive ice patches. 

Having to walk past this everyday on my way to the train I thought "no big deal - it's only for a couple of months until warmer weather..."  Guess it was too big of a problem to deal with as two days ago (Wednesday of this week) they brought the mobile trailer back in preparation of the heating having to be provided soon.  It makes me a little nervous having something so large and marked "flammable" a few steps from my house.

Also, it seems beyond pathetic to me that they couldn't get this issue fixed in the six months of warm weather we have had but really not that surprising from other things I have heard.
