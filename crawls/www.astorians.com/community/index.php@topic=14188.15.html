So I walked into this place on Sunday night, looked at the menu, walked right out and went to Vesta. The prices are insane; way higher than Vesta and on par with L'Incontro. And the menu is like a book. There are 34 different kinds of pizza including 3 with sliced hotdogs on it (grossness). Any menu that huge you know the ingredients aren't fresh. There's no way in a restaurant with 30 tables that they're keeping fresh fish for the 20 different seafood dishes, you're getting frozen. They would be throwing out profits like crazy if all the stuff on the menu were fresh. I live across the street and was really excited for this place but the prices will keep me away. I think there's an identity crisis going on there, they're a pizza place and a fancy restaurant and that space doesn't suit the prices. It looks like a pizza parlar, I'm not ordering a $25 veal chop there. I can get awesome Italian delivery from Michael Angelos and fancy Italian from Trattoria L'Incontro and nuevo Italian from Vesta... I feel like they're not fitting any niche that was needed in the area. I will eventually give it a shot when I'm either feeling flush with cash or feeling like pizza and will report back.

OK, you want honest feedback - here goes.   :|

Tried to come in and eat here 2x since they've opened but each time the place was roasting hot - no air conditioning, no lights turned on in the dining rooms, just doors propped open for circulation.  that should have been a tip off, but we instead decided to give their delivery a try because posts here sounded mixed & menu actually is beautifully written.  Well, trust your gut & first impression.

Yesterday, we tried margarita DOP with buffalo mozzarella & basil.  Their idea of 'with basil' - put only 1 huge, tough basil leaf in the middle of the measley pie and expect us to fight over it or something - seriously, not sure what these folks are thinking.  And, the sauce is 'dirty' = has seeds all in it - there's no way this place can expect to survive unless there's a serious intervention and fast! Oh, by the way, that cost like $14. 

Add to that 2 salads that sounded amazing on the menu, but arrived wilted & warm, one with pine nuts that weren't even toasted, they tasted raw.  Arrgh, it gets worse...a $17 chicken involtini that was pounded so thin it was completely dried out despite the stuffing of spinach & prosciutto (fontina was no where in site) - horrid scoop of homemade mashed potato on the side - seriously, how can you screw up mashed potato but these were dry & completely devoid of salt, butter, cream or taste. 

I don't even want to get into the debacle of the delivery and with no 'thank you' at all from the restaurant - I mean come on - not even a cannoli tucked into my order or take a couple dollars off the tab for the trouble of my heading downstairs to turn away your poor delivery guy who came to my place with the wrong order (but my delivery ticket address) - I feel sorry for the person who then got their food delivered only on the 2nd try!

I will add that the clams oreganata were good, nice sized portion, fresh, lemony, & not sandy at all, but that was the only palatable thing at all out of a tab that ran over $70.  Honestly, save your money folks.  these people either need to work out some serious kinks or just recognize they're in over their heads and get out now.  We really, really wanted to like this place, but were sadly disappointed.
