So I'm sitting here at 3am really annoyed as I've been dealing with a leaky radiator for the past two hours or so.  I discovered the leak at around 2am.  My room is a sty and has all sorts of junk on the floor.  It suddenly occurred to me, especially as this leak seems to be progressing, as I watched the puddle creep closer and closer to actual things on my hardwood floors, that I do not have renters insurance.  I have been in this apartment now since November 2007 but have just never gotten around to purchasing any.  The concept is new to me so I've got to ask a few naive questions.

Depends on the type of coverage you have. I have full replacement coverage, which means if my stuff is damaged, they will pay to replace my items with new stuff - regardless of how much the value of said merchandise has depreciated. Other insurance companies will compensate up to the value of the damaged items. I pay about $55/month for that and my valuable property insurance (musical instruments, expensive jewelry, i.e., engagement rings, etc. are not typically covered under regular insurance and that stuff needs protection too!).

Quote from: RayG on January 21, 2009, 09:41:04 AM
If your radiator is leaking, that's the landlords job to fix. If it creates enough damage to the building then HIS insurance should pay for it.

Yes, this.

I have renter's insurance through All State, and I pay around $100-$110 per year (it's changed depending on my address). I didn't have to verify any of my stuff, but I don't have anything particularly valuable. Keeping digital receipts for big-ticket items somewhere you can get them even if you lose everything would be a good idea.

However, usually they only want receipts for your replacement items, not the originals, and you're covered up to an amount, so as long as you stay under that amount, they won't question you too much. I think. This is how it worked when everything I owned was stolen while I was moving up here (car insurance covered the vehicle, my parents' homeowners insurance covered the contents).
