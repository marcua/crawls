I've been noticing lately that the trains are extremely slow from about Broadway all the way to Ditmars if I leave work between 5:30 and 7:00.  They always say it's because of trains in the station at Ditmars -- so what has happened that is suddenly causing the trains to pile up at Ditmars?  Did they change the schedule lately?  Did they put a new person into some kind of supervisory role somewhere on the line?  It happened again tonight, and as we sat at Broadway with the doors open and the frigid air pouring in, I started to wonder what was going on, but I caught myself.  "Oh yeah, I left work at a normal time tonight, and it's happening again," I said to myself.  I was talking with a friend tonight who said she was noticing it, too.

Just saw your new post, Tommy -- no, it's not normal for the N or W to run express in Queens.  When they introduced the W in July 2001, it was supposed to be an express train in Queens, and for the six weeks it lasted, it was sweet.  But in the chaos after September 11, they made the W a local - initially, they said it was temporary because the N was suspended briefly.  But they never made it back into a Queens express.  The W was actually only supposed to be a temporary train until some track work was finished in 2004, but that plan seems to have changed as well.
