I've just gone through the process of renting a new apartment.  Here's my story:

I initially tried the craigslist route.  I intended to find a no-fee apartment by sifting through the nonsense and seeing places with the owners.  I've lived in Astoria for a number of years so I had a pretty solid list of "must haves" for a new place.  Even though some places I saw were OK, none really fit the bill.  And some were a complete waste of time (like places for 1750 with roach traps all over the place, filthy living space, awful building, just plain scary).

Eventually I had to break down and go with a broker.  I didn't have a lot of time to waste & I needed someone who could narrow down the choices for me based on my list of "must haves."  In talking to brokers, I made it clear that if they wasted my time showing me places that didn't fit my criteria, then I'd drop them instantly.  This generally isn't something most brokers want to hear, so it didn't surprise me that some didn't call back at all. 

One of the brokers that didn't call back was Tasos from Astoria Apartments.  In his case, I barely got a chance to tell him my criteria because he just kept talking right over me & wouldn't allow me a chance to speak.  He was throwing things at me left and right ("I got a great place for you; I've got the keys right now").  When I'd explain that I wasn't interested in, say, a fifth floor walk-up and why, he just kept on talking.  When he figured out that I wasn't going to have the patience for his nonsense, he said "I'll call you later" and I never heard from him again.  So, I wouldn't recommend this guy.

I saw an apartment with a broker named Richard (of R&R Realty, I believe - the one partnered with Rashida).  Richard was an extremely nice guy & wasn't pushy at all.  I never met Rashida (who I heard isn't so nice).  He showed me a place off 30th - great location, nice place, but it was missing a couple of vital things I needed.  Afterward, I didn't get the sense that he had anything I was looking for & he said he'd pass my info along to his associates.  Again, never heard from him again.  I figured I'm being picky, which I'm allowed to be, and my being picky was going to make things more difficult for me.  Alas, I kept trying.

I saw another nice apartment off 30th, but after seeing it, there were a few things that bothered me & I wanted to see it again.  I saw this with a broker named Jamie (from Rutenberg Realty - which has hundreds of brokers so it's hard to judge the whole company based on this one guy).  Jamie was very pushy and it was obvious that he was saying whatever he could to get me to take the place.  He made it very difficult and confusing, and pushed for me to put down a $500 deposit on the apartment before I was anywhere near sure.  I wanted to see the place a 2nd time to look at some of the things that bothered me & he tried everything in his power to keep me from doing so.  I finally put my foot down and said I'm walking away unless I can see it, so he begrudgingly made the arrangement.  After confirming that the little things that bothered me were deal-breakers, I told him that I was uninterested & stopped communicating with him as I knew he wasn't the type of person I wanted to deal with.  Sneaky, shrewd, pushy, dishonest - unfortunately common traits for many brokers, but this was to the nth degree.

I spoke with a few other brokers on the phone, but none were especially helpful.  They were either loud & pushy, or just didn't have anything in the area I wanted.

I spoke with one broker named Maria (from Aidonis Realty) who also didn't have anything in the area I wanted, but was very nice and helpful.  She basically coached me - at length - on how to get what I wanted and how to deal with brokers.  She absolutely didn't have to take the time to do this, but she did because she's nice.  We both knew that she wasn't going to be able to find a place I wanted, but she helped anyway.

The broker who finally got me a place was Peter Horowitz, also from Charles Rutenberg Realty.  Although I see he's posted on this thread, I didn't find him here.  He just happened to be representing a place I found on craigslist.  In his case, I liked the place so much that it wasn't necessary for me to see anything else.  It had everything I needed, and after looking at close to 30 places, I was sure that this one was a rare breed.  Peter was never pushy, and he didn't try to give me standard broker lines like "this is the best building in Astoria" or any other line of crap that most brokers say just to get you to sign.  He basically just let me look around & was there to answer questions.  He was friendly and helpful in mediating my "interview" with the landlords, and has helped me with things (like asking the super to put in some window screens that were missing) even after I signed the lease.  This surprised me because most brokers, once they have their fee, will be out of your life forever.  With Peter, I can't speak for how he'd be if I needed to see place after place with him b/c the first apartment I saw was a winner.

I hope this info helps.  I can't guarantee that your experience with all of the people I mentioned will go exactly the same as they did with me.  The best I can do is describe exactly how my story went & you can make your own judgement from there.

Good luck.
