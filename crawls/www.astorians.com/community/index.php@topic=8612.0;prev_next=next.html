I hope you don't mind me being blunt, but sometimes we all need that.  I don't know if anyone has said this before, but get yourselves BOTH in counseling now.  Separately and together.  If he refuses to do so, then go on your own anyway.  None of us are therapists or psychologists, as far as I know.   And not to knock anyone including myself, but you really should be talking with a professional who deals with this sort of situation everyday.  You obviously need someone to talk to, and  this does seem like a subconscious cry for help.  Are your parents and friends aware of this situation?  I hope you are not suffering in silence, because there is no reason that you should be.  I have always thought women should be completely able to support themselves without being completely dependent on their spouse, know how to drive, have their own job and bank account, for exactly this situation.  

I'm not married, nor have I been, but I beleive marriage is a two way street.  It's not fair or healthy for you to have to carry all the weight of trying to make things better.  You haven't mentioned whether religion is a factor in your decision to stay.  You're asking for ways to cope.  I'm not sure how.  If he refuses to work with you to make this marriage work, then you're either going to have to see the apples for the trees and leave, or turn on blinders and ignore him every time he yells at you.  Which is not healthy or good for any marriage.  I would suggest counseling.  Get your own bank account, get a job, and start saving.  Alert your friends and family to the problem.  Put yourself in a position that you are not dependent on him, whether or not he changes.   Focus on yourself and what makes you happy.  And good luck.
