Personally, I think the street cleaning regulations in NYC accomplish two things primarily:

1) It makes lots of money for the city through parking summonses, probably a lot more than they have to spend on street sweeping vehicles and personnel.

2) It creates many additional union jobs.

Actual cleanliness of the streets has to fall somewhere way down on the list, especially since, in most cases, the streets really don't seem noticeably cleaner and quite often seem dirtier after the street sweepers pass.
