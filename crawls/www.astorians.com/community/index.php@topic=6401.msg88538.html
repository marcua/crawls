Good point.  But still, these points are part or important part of a dining experience. 

At the very least you have to admit that service in any form is integral to eating at a restaurant.  Someone has to get the food from the kitchen to your table.  How you rate that service is another thing all together.  Same for the ambiance.  The four walls, floor and ceiling plus the settings all add up to ambiance in some way or form.  You do not need music but most will allow that it can add something to the ambiance.  TV (as of yet) is not needed to eat at a restaurant.  What does it add?

And just to say it, A pub, cafe or bar serving food do not fall into the restaurant definition.

I've posted on this thread before, but I have to say it again: Valverde rocks.
