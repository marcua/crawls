I wouldn't fret none about the baseball bat thing, but the bedbugs is a real source of concern.  If I was in your position:

1.  $1300 is a fair amount of money to pay for a one bedroom You can certainly find other one bedrooms for that type of money in more prime areas.  So I'd continue looking for another place.  Look at craig's list.  You'll find alot of places for that sort of money.  

2.  If you want the place.  Ask the broker.  I'm sure he or she will stonewall you because they care little more than getting their fee.  I'd then ask to inspect the apartment again.  This is not a bad site to see for tell tale signs of bedbugs.  http://www.cbc.ca/news/background/pesticides/bedbugs.html.  Problem is that they travel easily within a building.  I'd then get a reassurance from the landlord that they'll spring for an exterminator in the event of an infestation and if they don't you'll take it out of the rent.  NYC renting laws are heavily in favor of the tennants.

Personally, even though it might be the settling of a grudge, I wouldn't take that type of chance with bedbugs unless the place was really rockin
