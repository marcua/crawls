Congrats!

Well I just wanted to say that when I became pregnant I was in the whole club/drinking scene and actually lost a few friends when i couldn't go out with them anymore. I now realize that they weren't "friends". Maybe you can't do the things that you used to with your friends but that doesn't mean you can't spend time with them doing other things.

But trust me when I tell you yes it will be hard and yes you will be tired for the next 20 years (ha ha) but no cocktail can compare, nothing in the world can compare, to the love you will feel for your child and having that child love you back. I work full time, have a 9 year old and a 2 year old. I still go out for dinner, drinks with friends at least once a month and still have "date night" with my husband at least once a month. Once a week we do something together as a family or with other friends and their kids.

Most important, enjoy your pregnancy and enjoy your baby!

Take care,
BL30

P.S. If possible, try to push the baby out without getting an episiotomy (know I spelled this wrong). I had it with kid #1 and had such a terrible time with the healing afterward. With kid #2, I did without it, took my time pushing and was able to walk around and do things so much easier. Recoup was so much faster, easier to get through.
