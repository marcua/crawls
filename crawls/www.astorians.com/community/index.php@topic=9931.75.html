I went for the 1st time this season on Saturday night...and it did NOT seem like the new BG in Williamsburg detracted any Manhattanites.  It was packed with plenty of tube top-wearing Murray Hill chicks, some of whom were ordering MIXED DRINKS by the ton from the main bar inside.  WTF?!? Anyway, I digress....

We got there around 8:15 and there was no line.  When we got in the backyard, it was packed but we were able to find a table right away (albeit a dirty one, but we're not afraid to throw some used plastic cups and plates in the trash can).  However, a friend who joined us just a half hour later said that the line was stretched down the block at that point.  Lines for beer and food were pretty long too, but what can you expect?  It was the first "nice" Saturday night since the grand re-opening.  A couple of tips worth sharing to other natives: 
-always get pitchers from the inside bar, the line is usually a little shorter.
-my friend was able to skip the line (he was by himself) by telling the bouncer that he "just ran home for one second to pick up a deck of cards", which he held up for the guy to see.  He got right in and met us at our table.  This can also apply with a pack of cigarettes ("oh, I'm already inside with my friends, I just had to run and grab some smokes"). 

As for the renovations, the new bathrooms are a bit of an eyesore, but nice.  Remember the pseudo-ladies' rooms from back in the day that were practically in a tent?  They are now replaced by ladies' and mens' rooms in a brick structure attached to the main bar with entrances inside and out.  Nice new flooring, automatic sinks and hand dryers.  I kind of miss the old ones, but oh well.  There is also a big tent that seems to be permanently up on one section of the garden, as well as smaller "umbrella'd" tables across on the other side of the yard.  And there are little "Red Bull" elevated bar-type tables on the new brick patio that look horribly out of place, but people were standing around them so I guess they serve a purpose.

All in all, of course it was a fun night...I guess the midtown kids are still coming into Astoria for their outer borough partying needs, but until a big Disneyland-esque outdoor beer hall opens on the east side, they'll come a-runnin' every Friday and Saturday during the summer.  :)
