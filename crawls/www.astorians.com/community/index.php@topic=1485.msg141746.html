Bites show up differently on different people, but as a rule, they generally come in threes (known grotesquely as the breakfast, lunch, and dinner combo, since bedbugs attach themselves, feed for a few minutes, and then move to another spot). Generally they are very red and if you're very allergic to their anticoagulant (which is what they inject you with) you'll see red welts that itch like ten thousand mosquito bites put together. The itching is unbearable and often keeps you up at night and makes your life a living hell.

It is infuriating how there is not much public awareness about bedbugs in NYC. The wimpy couple of webpages on NYC.gov don't cut it. Bedbugs should be considered more than a "pest" or a "nuisance". Ask anyone who's had them and they'll tell you it affects your ability to work, sleep, function and certainly affects your mental health. I am a survivor but I still consider myself "on the watch" and every time I see a discarded mattress with black dots on it in Astoria I wanna puke. Also, when I see signs (like in a Bangladeshi supermarket on 36th ave that say "we have spray for bedbugs") I want to run in the store and inform the owner that there is NO such thing as a simple "spray" to kill bedbugs --- they are not like fleas or ants... you cannot just spray chemicals on them. It takes a multi-lateral approach, you must leave residual spray, bag up your entire life and wash everything you own, take switchplates off, books, etc etc.


I URGE you to visit bedbugger.com as soon as possible, check out the FAQ's for identifying bedbugs and post on the forum if you wish. There is a lot of misinformation out there, as well as people who are underinformed and who deny or downplay the problem. If you are someone who severely reacts to BB bites (a lot of times in a couple or roommate situation they'll be one person who does and the other person who doesn't even react to the bites) then this can be absolutely horrifying. Seek help asap!

I just moved in to--and quickly moved out of--a guy's apartment who had bedbugs in the Ditmars area.

It was a tramautizing experience. I woke up one morning with a bite on my bicep, figured it was just a mosquito that flew in over night. Then a couple days later, I wake up and have 6 bites on one arm, 4 on the other, and one on my foot (!).

They look like mosquito bites, only they're a bit bigger and take longer to go away. If you wake up and have a line of bedbugs on any part of you, it's most likely bed bugs. Other giveaways: there will be drops of blood on the sheets and sometimes they leave casts of their body on the bed. So if you see pieces of bug, including tentacles or scales, that's it.

My advice is to move out immediately of a place if this happens. Wash everything you own. Throw anything on the bed out--not worth it. And report it immediately, as they spread.

Also, if you're renting out a room, BUY A NEW MATTRESS. Don't use one that's already there.

So. disgusting.

Please get help now. There is really no such thing as commercially available bed bug pesticide. Yes, you can get rubbing alcohol, steri fab and kleen free, but those kill bedbugs on contact and the problem is that bb's hide in the most remote places and are extremely, extremely hard to get rid of.
You need professional help- contact your landlord NOW (landlords are required to pay for treatment) and put up flyers everywhere in your building.

The reason why these bugs spread is due to human ignorance and refusal to confront the problem. Unfortunately since they don't spread disease(**) they are only considered a "nuisance" and public awareness and laws are not up to the challenge of ridding NYC of them.

(**) of course I wouldn't WANT them to spread disease; I say unfortunately because unlike cockroaches (which can contribute to asthma in children, and spread disease, as well as rats, which can bite etc), bedbugs are just considered pests and are not a high priority for NYC. Little do they know bedbugs cause psychological damage and severe itching, making you WISH you were being eating alive by mosquitos instead.

Go to bedbugger.com and read the FAQ's. This thread is helpful but bedbugger.com is the most up to date, comprehensive resource out there.

You don't have to evacuate but yes, you do need to exterminate the whole building, apartment by apartment.  They could easily have come from someone else's apartment, and it really has nothing to do with how clean or dirty you are.  You have blood, that's all they want.  Your landlord is required to pay for and to exterminate for bed bugs - and make sure it's for bed bugs and not just a roach extermination.  Each apartment has to be visited at least twice.  Wash and Dry your stuff, then put all your clothes and other washables in plastic bags throughout the extermination process so they're not contaminated.  If you ignore the problem, it only gets worse.  I had the unfortunate experience of having neighbors who did exactly that, and the whole blg became infested.  Needless to say, I was fed up with the blg due to that and other things, and just ended up moving out.  But AFTER I got the landlord to exterminate the whole building.  The precautions I took to avoid bringing them with me were numerous and exhausting.  Neighbors I confronted knew about the problem and had just chosen to live with it, like it was no big deal...  Read this thread, there's lots of good info in it.
