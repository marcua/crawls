Title: A funny thing happened ...
Post by: JustDesserts on January 14, 2007, 10:51:07 PM
... on the way to the pastry shop.  So I've just started an externship at a wholesale bakery on the lower east side. I'd worked there two days, today being my third.  The plan was to arrive there at 11 this morning.  Easy, right?  Yeah, not so much.  During the week I can take the V train straight there, from the 36th street station.  Well, who knew the V doesn't run on weekends?  I didn't.  So I have to wait about a week for an R.  I get off at 34th Street, call the chef to say I'm going to be a bit late, she's cool with it, and I head back down to the F train.  Everything's great on the F till West 4th Street, then it decides to run along the A train line.  What the hell?!  Unfortunately, I didn't figure out what was going on till Brooklyn!  I get off at High Street/Brooklyn-freakin'-Bridge and call again, only this time I'm practically hyperventilating.  At this point I'm half an hour late!  So the chef's delightful business partner tells me not to panic and get a cab.  I don't have money with me.  She says they'll take care of it.  So the driver takes me to the place, and I hurry in to get the money.  In my haste to get the driver his money, I open the door and somehow the metal rod from the canvas blind catches on the doorframe.  Naturally, as I am the world's clumsiest person, I trip directly over the stuck rod, which has now been mostly ripped from the canvas. There is a table right by the door, on which my friend Lori and the chef are decorating a very intricate wedding cake (which had a dramatic episode before I even arrived), and if I had tried to catch myself on the table, the cake would have been flung across the shop into the wall.  But instead,  I tumble out the door, the two chefs trying to catch me by my jacket, but they're a bit too late and I end up sprawled all over the street.  Fortunately, my only audience are my colleagues, the cab driver, and a dude standing by the pastry shop smoking.  He says to me, while laughing heartily, "That was nice.  Thanks for that."  The girls are laughing, the cab driver is laughing, and my buddy the smoking dude is laughing.  Not only do I feel like an arse, but I have hit my arm on the step and torn open my lovely, and on-the-severe-side burn that I achieved on Friday while removing tarts from the oven.  I'm bleeding, in pain, having proven I'm a total prat, and am now nearly an hour late.  I am so glad to say that I hit the jackpot of externship sites, and my tardiness is completely forgiven. I mean, they paid for my cab!!  We just laughed the whole thing off and I was given a few minutes to chill out before diving into the pecan bars. 

I just had to share that story.  Too ridiculous!
