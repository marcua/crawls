A few observations from my recent peregrinations in and about Astoria.   I noticed that Raleigh, the men's store on Broadway between 32nd and 33rd, is no more.   That was there when I moved to Astoria in '87 and lived literally around the corner on 32nd.   While I never shopped there, it was sad to see that establishment gone.   Those of us who have lived in Astoria for awhile may remember the other, similar men's store on Broadway between 31st and 32nd that went out of business a number of years ago -- probably the late 1990s.  They combined operations with Raleigh.   

And it looks like that Italian restaurant/pizza place that was open on 35th Avenue directly across the street from the Museum of the Moving Image is no more.   I never went in there, but it looked like a nice place.  It didn't last all that long.   It is kind of surprising that it wasn't able to make it because that area has undergone such a renaissance since the Astoria movie 'plex opened.    And there was a little Carvel store across from the multiplex that is also no longer there.   I always wondered about that place because there are these thick trees right in front of the building and I wondered how people would even know it was there.

And I was walking down 36th Avenue a couple of weeks ago -- New Year's Eve now that I think about it -- I noticed that the corner of 36th Ave. and either 33rd or 34th street is now a hole in the ground.   I can't even remember what was there and assume an apartment building is going to go up.   What recession?

And I noticed on Queensboro Plaza that that hideous parking garage is just about completely demolished.   That is indeed addition by subtraction.   And there is another now vacant lot on the south side of the plaza, or just off the south side of the plaza, next to the entrance to the trains.   It is right behind where the bar used to be (now a deli).   Who knows what is going up.

And finally I noticed some more skeletons of new buildings going up around the 39th Street subway stop.  Hard to believe that anyone can get real estate financing in this economic climate, but the real estate rules for the rest of the country never seem to apply to New York City.

The weather was nice this weekend so I decided to go running, which means I get to see what has changed in the six weeks or so since I last was able to go out.   Gone, gone, gone is the mosque on 31st Street, just north of 28th Avenue.   The building is coming down and I'm not sure what is going up in its stead, but I assume it will be an apartment building.   Why anyone would want to live on 31st Street right next to the el I'll never know, but different strokes for different folks I guess.

And the Zodiac cafe has never reopened.  I kind of the Zodiac and that other cafe that was diagonally across the street that is now a bank because it kind of livened up a pretty desolate intersection (Newtown & 31st Steet). 

And I noticed that the abandoned apartment building on Hoyt Avenue South just west of 31st Street is still for sale, but they boarded up the entrance and the overhang between the building and the sidewalk, which people were using as a sleeping alcove.

That is about it, though the track in Astoria is still in fine shape and has held up through the the winter.   It has fared a lot better than I have that is for sure.
