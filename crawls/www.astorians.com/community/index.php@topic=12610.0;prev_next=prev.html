Re: Should I pay my utility?
« Reply #1 on: October 12, 2008, 07:12:03 PM »
You know what you paid for June.  When the October bill comes, if it's more, pay just the difference between June and October.  Attach a note stating that.  Also, on the check, write in the memo line "(your name)'s share of (name of utility) bill for October 2008, PIF".  pif=paid in full  I believe if you write that and your roommates cash it, that would preclude them from suing you for the amount, as you wrote that it was paid in full.  I doubt it's large enough that they would ever do that, though.  As you obviously don't like these people and will never see them again, you do not owe them anything.  Since you had the proof that you paid them for June, that is pretty f-ed up that they would ignore that.

Also keep in mind that time warner cable bills a month ahead, if you have that.  Look at the bill and be sure you are not paying for the month ahead before you move out.  I once shared cable with a horrible girl who lived in an apartment downstairs from me.  We didn't like each other and werent even speaking, so she decided to get her own cable.  She was so annoying and insistent that I don't turn it off until the end of the month when her new cable was installed that I took a look at the bill and saw that she had only paid until the day before.  She always had her tv on so loud that it kept me up while I was sleeping.  She was watching some terrible show at full volume (seriously--I think it was a Full House rerun) and I unplugged her connection right there, then left the highlighted bill for her.  Ok, it was mean of me to do, but I got a good laugh out of it!
