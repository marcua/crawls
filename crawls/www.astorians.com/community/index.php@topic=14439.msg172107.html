I was there this afternoon from about 1:30 to 3:00.  When we got there, about 1/3 of the tables had people sitting at them, but by the time we left they were almost all full.  When we stepped outside, a server/hostess came up to us and asked us if we wanted a table, sun or shade.  It was a bit surreal - why couldn't we pick our own table?  Anyway, she seated us but didn't give us menus or say that a server would be over or anything.  We could see people getting beer at the outside bar on the right, and the grill looked like it was open, but we couldn't see clearly from where we were sitting whether they were serving food from it.  A server finally came and took orders from the table next to us, so we snagged a menu, and she took our order promptly.  They didn't have any kielbasa!!  (Or any chicken wings or a couple of other things.)

Our food came relatively quickly and was decent for the most part, although one dish (a bit of an adventure pick) that we assumed would be crispy bacon and pickles (I even envisioned deep-fried pickles, but that might be the result of having recently eaten at the Chip Shop) turned out to be cubes of cold very thick-cut bacon that was cured but didn't look otherwise cooked, and four dill pickle spears.  It was really odd.  The potato pancakes were very good, and our third dish, a sort of pickled sausage, was ok.

By the time we left things were getting crowded, and we were glad to leave.
