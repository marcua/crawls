You really don't save any money and switching to another energy supplier.  (ConEd still distributes the electricity).
Only good thing is these companies purchase energy from green sources, so when you get your electricity you are actually paying the supplier (IDT) for that service as well as ConEd for the distribution of it to your house.

I have a different ESCO called US Gas and Electric.  My energy comes from 100% renewable sources.  Electric bill still comes from ConEd and its not that cheap (green energy costs more).

IDT shouldn't make people sign and knock on doors that is pretty annoying and should stop.  If anyone wants to switch ESCOs they can do it by calling ConEd.  Also, they don't send out reps to sell services so if they say they are from ConEd they are lying.

Excerpt from the ConEd website:
"Some energy suppliers (ESCOs) have been using misleading tactics, such as saying that they represent Con Edison, or that customers must switch to a new supplier within 30 days. While we encourage our customers to shop around and explore alternative suppliers through PowerYourWay, Con Edison does not send salespeople to your door or have them call your home or business. There is no deadline, nor are customers required to choose a new supplier. Switching to a different supplier is your choice - so shop wisely and make the energy choice that makes sense for you."

Robo calls without prior written consent is a violation of the recently amended Telephone Consumer Protection Act. The FCC changed older consent rules to stop tele and SMS marketers from relying on a pre-existing business relationship to spam consumers. http://www.copilevitz-canter.com/resources/articles/get-it-in-writing-changes-to-the-fcc-express-consent-rules

"The new standard requires prior express written consent for predictive dialed and prerecorded advertising or solicitation calls or texts placed to wireless and residential numbers.  Pursuant to the amended rule, written consent must include the consumer’s signature, and must show that the sender provided the consumer notice of the consequences of providing consent and the consumer’s telephone number. The signature may be obtained electronically or digitally."

One more thing to add to the list of unethical (and nary illegal) business practices of these over-aggressive ESCO hucksters.
