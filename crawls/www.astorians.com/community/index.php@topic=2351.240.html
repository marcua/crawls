« Reply #7 on: May 10, 2006, 09:15:46 AM »

311 helps only when the establishment or individual offender is not part of some organized mafia or paying off the police.  I lived for three years above a cafe on 38th and Ditmars, which blared music and whose patrons yelled at all hours, Monday thru Sunday - it was a 24 hour spot.  One of those men's clubs where all they do is talk and drink coffee with each other, and the only women around are the waitresses.  This cafe's back windows opened into my apartment building's inner courtyard, where their loud voices and incessant nonstop music would echo all night.  We had a tenants organization, and we tried to no avail to get them out.  We called 311, one woman lived right above it - she called nightly.  They take your name, and detail of what is happening, and then you never hear back from them.  I was there for three years.  Many other people were there longer.  Nothing was ever done.  I began to think the police were just paid off. 

Incidently, now I've moved, and the entire block of establishments including the Olympic Diner, the noisy cafe, and the shoe guy were all thrown out sometime last year.  A bank owned by the landlord is moving in.
