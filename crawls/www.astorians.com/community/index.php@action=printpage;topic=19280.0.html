Our Side: A Creative Nonfiction Writing Workshop on Cultural Identity

Dates:   9 Tuesday nights, 6:30-9:00 pm
   October 19 -- December 14, 2010
   Participants’ reading: Saturday, December 18, 3 pm
      
Fee:    $275 ($250 if you apply by September 15)*

http://ourside.info (http://ourside.info)      
      
Description: Our Side is a workshop for writers of all levels to explore issues of cultural identity, such as race, ethnicity, nationality, immigration, mixed heritage, assimilation, retro-acculturation, etc.  We will read brief readings by contemporary writers, such as Amy Tan, Jhumpa Lahiri, Junot Diaz, and Edwidge Danticat; then we'll discuss the issues these writers address to prompt us to write about our own experiences.  We'll also receive feedback on our writing to help polish it into short memoir stories or personal essays.  The workshop will culminate with a reading to invited guests.

To apply: Write one page on your experience with writing and why you would like to take a writing workshop on cultural identity.   Include your name, email address, and phone number. Email your application (or any questions) to nancyagabian@yahoo.com.
Application deadline: September 27, 2010. 
Participants will be notified by October 5, 2010

*A few spots will be available for unemployed or low-income writers to pay on a
reduced-rate sliding scale. Please inquire within your application if you need assistance.

Facilitator: Nancy Agabian is the author of Me as her again: True Stories of an Armenian Daughter, a memoir on the influence of her Armenian-American family history on her coming-of-age, finalist for a Lambda Literary Award and shortlisted for the Saroyan International Prize.  Currently, she is working on a book about her experiences living in a community of artists in Yerevan, Armenia.   Since 1994, she has been teaching writing workshops in Los Angeles, New York and Yerevan.  She also teaches nonfiction at CUNY Queens College and the Gallatin School of Individualized Study at New York University.  She lives in Astoria, Queens.  http://nancyagabian.com (http://nancyagabian.com)

TOPAZ ARTS is located at 55-03 39th Avenue in Woodside, Queens.
Subway directions: #7-train to 61 St. or the R, V, G trains to Northern Blvd.
Details and directions are available at http://www.topazarts.org (http://www.topazarts.org).

TOPAZ ARTS, Inc. is a nonprofit organization founded in 2000 by artists Todd Richmond (composer/visual artist) and Paz Tanjuaquio (choreographer/dancer). TOPAZ ARTS fosters the creative process, produces the collaborative works of its founders and provides public programs for the performing and visual arts. TOPAZ ARTS maintains a 2,500 sq. ft. multidisciplinary arts center in Woodside, Queens housing a dance rehearsal studio, audio room, and gallery. Providing affordable space, technical support, residencies and presentation opportunities, TOPAZ ARTS enables artists to realize their projects and the process shared with audiences.
TOPAZ ARTS, Inc. is made possible, in part, by public funds from the NYC Dept. of Cultural Affairs;
New York State Council on the Arts, a state agency; NYS DanceForce; Mertz-Gilmore Foundation; Foundation for Contemporary Art; Material for the Arts, a program of NYC’s Department of Cultural Affairs, Dept. of Sanitation and Board of Education; and the generosity of private individuals.
