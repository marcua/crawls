Water Taxi is a bar, that happens to be outside right by the water and amazing view of Manhattan.  They imported sand to the area, and you can run around barefoot and play volleyball.  They sell hotdogs and hamburgers and sangria, among others.  During the day, kids are allowed.   There is no access to the lovely Hudson River water, so there is no swimming.  You can wear whatever.  They have weddings there sometimes.  They have a DJ too.  On weekend nights, they usually have a cover charge, and a long line out the door and around the block, from the crowds who bombard the place after the PS1 party.  This line is made worse by the fact that they actually scan everyone's ID before you can go in, so if you forget your license, you are out of luck.  Even if you are 50 years old.

Go during the week, it's much easier to deal with the annoying door issue.

Just a few minor point-

Water Taxi Beach is actually on the East River, not the Hudson.  And they have to scan ID's, even if you're 50, b/c some neighbors are accusing them of encouraging underage drinking and you can't just scan some people, if you scan anyone, you have to scan everyone.  Most popular places in Manhattan and Queens scan them too nowadays, it's pretty common.  They just protecting themselves.  The Astoria Beer Garden did this last summer too.

oh man it's ridiculously beautiful...you take the grand central/northern state to the meadowbrook parkway as if you were headed to Jones Beach.  When you get to the toll for Jones you stay to the left and bypass it.  You basically drive past all of Jones beach and get back on Ocean Parkway.  You take a beautiful drive for about 10 miles (think dunes, beach houses and water views) to a bridge which takes you over the water and onto Fire Island.  Robert Moses is basically the Western tip of Fire Island.  There are 5 fields.  Drive to the last one, park, and if you want you can do this dune walk over to the Lighthouse.  You can take a tour of the lighthouse if you want if not, just go right and plant your ass on the beautiful beach.  You have the dunes and a beautiful lighthouse behind you and a beautiful ocean in front of you....magic.

Robert Moses State Park occupies the western tip of Fire Island and is accessible by car via a bridge that links it to Ocean Parkway (and Jones Beach to the west) and to the Long Island mainland. To the east of Robert Moses, Fire Island is car-free and reachable by ferry from either Bayshore or Sayville.

RE: driving to Long Beach.

Early and early. It's mostly street parking and fills up early. When we were kids,
my dad used to get us out there by 7:30 - 8:00 to get a spot by the boardwalk.
We would leave around 2:00 - 2:30 to avoid the traffic that heats up later in the day,
esp. Sunday when you have a lot of weekender folks heading back to the city.

But it's silly to drive to Long Beach. The railroad arrives/leaves every hour. The
roundtrip fare and admission ticket are $18. That has to be a better deal than paying
the $7 admission for the beach plus gas and tolls. And that admission may have gone up -
I'm going by last year's price.

The beach is a 4-block walk from the train - it's almost too easy. I can't imagine driving there anymore.

I will second Robert Moses.  It takes around an hour to drive, depending on traffic.  We usually take Grand Central to Cross Island to Southern Parkway to the Robert Moses Causway exit.  In my opinion, it's worth the extra time.  They also have a pitch and putt golf course there if anyone is into that sort of thing.  I think it's like 10 bucks to play.

Now, if you want a truly spectacular beach and you have a full day, you should check out Cooper's Beach in Southampton.   It takes 2 - 2 1/2 hours to get there, but it's probably the best beach I've seen in the US.  In fact, they ranked it number 3 here http://travelwithkids.about.com/od/beachvacations/ss/top_beaches_4.htm.  It costs $30 per  car during season, but if you carpool that's not so bad.  We actually went there last September and, not only was it free(off season), but there was hardly ANYONE there.  And the water was perfect!

Both of these beaches are awesome, and if you have a car you should definitely check them out this summer.
