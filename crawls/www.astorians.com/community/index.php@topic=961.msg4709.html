#1 favorite film of all time

The Museum of the Moving Image is showing my all-time favorite film, so I thought to ask everyone here: what's your favorite film?

Note, I didn't say "films". Try to choose just one. No lists! (I'll save that for another thread )

Simple, but not easy, is it?

Mine: 2001: A Space Odyssey, Stanley Kubrick's anti-romance about space exploration and the role of humanity in a shrinking universe.

Why? Nothing I've seen so perfectly captures an entire experience -- in this case, space exploration -- so vividly on film. He's telling us that life is out there, note that cataract of the ova-like eclipsed sun in the first shot. But mankind may not be prepared (or want) to meet it. Even the spaceship Discovery, charged with sending formerly Earth-bound life into the deeps of space, looks like a sperm.

The film has special effects that, almost 40 years later, still stand out as ground-breaking, and constantly remind us that man is unfit for space travel, unless he puts his survival in the hands of his own creation -- technology and machines. And in trying to survive and live in the void, man becomes alienated from his humanness...

It postulates many difficult questions, and few answers. Physically fragile and minimized in the vast void of space, man can only come to know of the greater universe through the graces(?) of an unknown race of intelligent beings. But guide us to what? Servitude like animals in a zoo or toward a higher state of being?

For me, there's no other film that exhibits pure cinematic genius as this.

Your turn...
