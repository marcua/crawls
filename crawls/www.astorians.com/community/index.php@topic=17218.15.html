Easy to say move to Charlotte or move to Virginia. Maybe the people you know who moved down South love it but I know just as many who wouldn’t. What is the difference between forced to leave or forced to stay?


I think about the whole buying a house issue alot and I moan along with everyone else. Regardless of the s___ economy, some people have money, especially those who work construction and the trades who get paid in cash, professionals (i.e., doctors and lawyers), business owners who work like dogs, and families who pool their money.

Yes prices in the city are outrageously high and statistically out of whack with reality but I’m also beginning to think that part of the problem is that people in general don’t want to make the commitment that’s needed to buy a house (and I include myself to a certain degree in that group of people) for whatever reason, fear of debt, loss of mobility, etc.

When my family moved to Astoria my grandparents worked as did their (adult) children in order to pay the mortgage. It wasn’t easy then just as isn’t easy now.

Once upon a time buying a house was a sacrifice and it was an investment for life in a home and a neighborhood. Now it seems many people want easy monthly payments and an easy out to sell-up and move on, at a profit, at a time of their choosing. Maybe it’s not supposed to be that way. Maybe it’s expectations as well as prices that are skewed.

Before you say that’s not me think about it: are you willing to work two jobs? Start a business? Buy with a friend or sibling/family? Work 80 hours a week? Give up the niceties of life, vacations and nights outs? These are the questions I ask myself. I don’t have an extravagant lifestyle, I’m careful with my money and I save but to be honest, I spend on things that my parents or grandparents would find unnecessary and I wonder if I were to forgo these things would it make a difference? I tell myself no, because given my salary it's a losing battle to save for a 20% downpayment on a (mythical) typical 500K house.

My apologies if you are already working long hours for low pay but I suspect if you do you have other worries than being able to afford a house.

Anyway, these are the thoughts I struggle with.
