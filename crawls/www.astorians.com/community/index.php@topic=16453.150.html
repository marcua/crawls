What I would say is that spirituality should not be dismissed out of hand.

It was suggested that I should read St Augustine. In all honesty, I would and have read St John of the Cross and Teresa of Avila. In fact, because of this thread, I have added to my pile of books to read, "The Cloud of Unknowing" I get the journey and have, at times, wished I could take that Journey....but alas, it is not to be.

What I don't get is a bunch of people believing in a few scrolls that tell them of a world that is absolute. And taking that journey in such absolute terms that seem, to me, to limit that journey.

I think St John of the Cross got closer to what Jesus was talking about. One example I will give you is this, Jesus said that a rich man has a greater chance of getting to heaven then a camel has of getting through the eye of the needle.

A rich man is held to his riches and cannot begin the true journey to god. His possessions matter more to him.

If you want to get closer to god, don't worry about me. Instead, lock yourself into a dark closet and think. Take that journey. No bible will lead you there. No ten commandments will lead you there. No Cathedral will lead you there. You worrying about me screwing my boyfriend will not lead you there.

What Jesus was talking about was letting go. Letting your soul travel through space and time. Put down the bible. Remember when Jesus intervened when his disciples were gathering grain.
1At that time Jesus went through the grainfields on the Sabbath. His disciples were hungry and began to pick some heads of grain and eat them. 2When the Pharisees saw this, they said to him, “Look! Your disciples are doing what is unlawful on the Sabbath.”

3He answered, “Haven’t you read what David did when he and his companions were hungry? 4He entered the house of God, and he and his companions ate the consecrated bread—which was not lawful for them to do, but only for the priests. 5Or haven’t you read in the Law that on the Sabbath the priests in the temple desecrate the day and yet are innocent? 6I tell you that onea greater than the temple is here. 7If you had known what these words mean, ‘I desire mercy, not sacrifice,’b you would not have condemned the innocent. 8For the Son of Man is Lord of the Sabbath.”

Many read this as Jesus is the lord of the Sabbath. I read this as a condemnation of the rules of the Pharisees. They were the Rulers, the ones, such as Pat Robertson, that condemned me, But truly didn't understand a spiritual life.

Or, to put it another way,

Jesus said, f___ the rules.

I will close with this. St John of the Cross got it. He pretty much had sex with God in "A dark night of the soul". Not in a physical way....but in a spiritual way.

If you want to talk to me about god, talk to me in a way that makes sense. Taking that ultimate journey doesn't require a book, It requires your mind being open. And if anyone here thinks I am talking about Christians; please understand that I am also talking about myself.
