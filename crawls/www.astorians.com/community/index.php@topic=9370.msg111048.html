Hey. I had this done very recently and I have one very important peice of advice that I wish someone would have told me:

By all means, get your doctor to give you a single dose of your EXACT PAIN PRESCRIPTION *BEFORE* THE SURGERY.

I'm not allergic to pain killers. Not percocet, not codine, not nuttin'. But I am apparently allergic to what ever it is that makes pain killers liquids. Getting your stomach pumped while your throat already feels like a buttload of suck is a big no fun.

This should also go with any ANY medical procedure ever. Ask for one dose of the painkiller first so that you can be positive that the specific brand, dose and the like is one that your body can handle.

Apparently the first time I had a reaction to this medication it was in smaller doses and was blamed on me having the stomach flu (Dentist said I had the worst recovery of any wisdom teeth removal he'd ever seen but no one blamed the pain killer). When the tonsils came out, the dose was higher and it could have been much, much worse than it was.

If you want more details or have more specific questions, lemme know. I just went through this all a little more than a year ago, so it's pretty recent.

I had my tonsils removed at age 30 (nearly three years ago) and it wasn't much fun but I survived.  My ENT gave me pill form of the painkillers and I was able to swallow them with little/no trouble.  I had cryptic tonsilitis so am really glad I had the procedure done (which is surprisngly quick btw).

As for pain, etc I just felt like I had a really bad sore throat for about a week.  Not sure if your dr mentioned it but it's common in adult cases to get ear pain after a day or so which was the worst part for me (imagine someone boxing you in the ears).  Also, you will likely be able to talk the day of the procedure (I could but just sounded hoarse) but won't be able to talk for the following days.  Get a notepad and prepare to write a lot.

As for food recommendations - AVOID ice cream!  The texture really bothered my throat.

Good things to eat include:  Instant mashed potatoed (the salt is surprisingly soothing), apricot nectar, pudding, yogurt, easy mac (after a few days), jello

In summary, your life will suck for a few days but in the end you will feel much better - just keep telling yourself that!

Hope this helps!  Please feel free to PM me with any additional questions.

MagicPie - I did use a humidifier which helped IMMENSELY!  I have a warm air one and, even though it was June when I had mine done, I ran it full time in the bedroom. 

The thing that I found helped soothe my throat the most was apricot nectar - I drank about 1.5-2 bottles a day!

Also, it's a great time to guilt your loved ones into making a "sunshine basket" for you.  Have them give you a basket with a bunch of small gifts and each day you get to open one.  Breaks up the monotony if you are in bed for a few days.  My mom did this for me when I had chicken pox as a kid and I convinced her that I needed it with this as well!

Lastly, you should use the same notebook to write everything in and go back and review it a year or so later.  It's pretty comical what one writes and thinks is communication when hopped up on painkillers.  I came up with some great one liners!

Best of luck!
