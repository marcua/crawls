Title: Re: What's up with application fees?
Post by: newastorian35 on January 25, 2009, 10:45:48 AM
I believe there is an application fee on some places where there is no broker fee, and someone wants to make a little money and still say there is no broker fee.  The thing about an application fee is that you are paying to APPLY, so that sounds like you don't get it back if they give the apartment to someone else.  Much like applying to college, I guess you are paying for someone to read your application.

The apartment that I live in now had a $20 application fee (and it had to be cash only).  No broker fee.  It's owned by a management company, and they advertised on Craigslist, I met with the Super to see the apt, and gave him my $20 and application.  I was wary about the application fee thing--I probably would have not bothered to apply had the super not told me he was going to recommend that I get the apt.  He had a giant stack of applications, all with the required $20 in cash.  I have no idea where all those other application fees went.  In this case, it was only $20, though.  $300 seems excessive if you're not guaranteed you will get the apartment.
