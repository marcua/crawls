Just so you know the stimulus money is a tax credit for the following year, so you wouldn't see any of it until you file in 2011.

There are some advantages of a co-op versus condos. The property taxes are included in the maintenance fees, unlike condos. Of course there are some disadvantages too of owning a co-op.

My boyfriend bought a co-op at the Acropolis. The prices are still pretty low there still and the maintenance fees are around 400-600 depending upon your square footage. There's usually some open houses on the weekend there if you want to check them out. Trulia.com is a great source.

I have never sold anything in Acropolis.  I used to renovate units for the biggest shareholder there...for one reason or another we stopped.  They used to do their own rentals so even if I AM A RE Broker I wasn't given an opportunity to rent or sell there  .  Today is a new day  .  I was given the opportunity to rent some units and I still have 1 of them available to rent on a 5Th floor.

I am also very happy to say that i was also informed that 2 units will be available to sell.  One of them is ready but I have to wait for appliances which will come next week,  I am also renovating another 2 bedroom unit on a 2ND floor...this unit will require some brainstorming but I can tell you that the 21-38 unit is nicer than average unit you will see available in the complex.  While we will place the unit for $279 as the asking price the price is negotiable and you will be proud of this place as your place to live. 

As an extra incentive you should know that the only COOP thing about Acropolis is the name....this is the same as a Condo but cheaper.  There are rumors going around about creating two elevator banks at both extreme sides of the complex with cat walks branching to each one of the buildings.  The project may cost upwards of 6 million dollars but with that many apartment in the complex it would probably trickle down to a 5 year assessment (5% MAYBE?)  and a huge 30% appreciation in as long a time.  I can't verify this and I cannot guarantee the same but if this happens ALL BETS ARE OFF!  THIS WILL BE THE PLACE TO BE AT!!!

Location, location.

I could sell you a 3 bedroom with 2 bath 1300 sq ft apartment in Jamaica for the same price  :lol: .  One of the reasons why you'll see units stay in the market is because they maybe overpriced and the units may also be poorly maintained.  I have a list of current units that sold at the Acropolis and while agree with you that they could do better to maintain the Bldg the grounds are well kept and have 24 hr security.  Some of the fenced garbage areas are always being use because there is actually 2 to 5 renovations going on at any given time by several contractors.  I am one that I stored the equivalent of 40 yards worth of debris in 2 separate areas until today. 

As per the price...$269k as an asking price is not expensive at all, especially when you consider that you couldn't get a condo for a price anywhere close to that in that area.  Yes, 4 flights up is not for everyone but I can assure you there are not many coops you can buy with no board approval, minimal money down and investor friendly.  I remember Acropolis almost a decade ago when we were doing some lead in paint work (Before the current laws were in place).  I can say that the place has improved drastically and indications are it will continue to do so.  Acropolis was built in the late 20's and it is all brick construction.  It was built with modesty and for the people of it's day - Not large units at all but reasonably priced by all means.
