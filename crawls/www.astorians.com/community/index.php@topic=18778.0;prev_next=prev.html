Best thing to do, when you call TW, is to choose the option for downgrade/cancel service. When someone gets to you ( generally fast), they'll ask you why you want to blah blah blah. Explain the situation to them, and they can often get you better help. Their job is to make sure you don't leave, so they'll do a lot more than a regular CS person will.

Back in May I called them because I was fed up with the lousy cable I was receiving, and had the RCN flyer ($55 for 12 months of cable and internet). When I called, they got a tech to come out faster than regular CS could, and gave me a year of Showtime for free.

Just another tip - when you call, dial 1-800-OK-CABLE, not their 718 number.

You'll get transferred to the TWC center after a couple of prompts. This way you're not paying for the call.

It used to infuriate me that when I had to call about problems, they didn't have a toll-free number. I finally
chewed out a service rep and demanded an answer as to why they were the only business I deal with that
doesn't have one. She then told me to dial that number instead.

Some may not agree with my posting this, but since TWC doesn't exactly go out of their way to give good service
without a lot of arguing, I just don't think it should be on the customer's dime!

Good luck -  and keep track of how long you're without service to get your credit. I've made them credit me for
just a couple of hours of lost service. That's your right.
