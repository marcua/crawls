Post by: NYCGoalie on July 05, 2007, 11:57:55 AM
Quote from: BigBryan on July 02, 2007, 12:47:53 PM
Sometimes I see people play in that pocket park on Hoyt Ave N between 21 St and Astoria Park.
This park is commonly refered to as the "cheesebox" (due to it's triangular shape).  You usually find kids around Saturday mornings skating around.

Just to add to this BB on the history of Astoria:  Hockey used to be HUGE in Astoria.  Matter of fact, Astoria was once listed in GOAL Magazine (the former official magazine of the NHL) as one of the largest areas in the country for Roller Hockey.  The largest kids league was run out of Most Precious Blood school.  Games were held in the playground behind the school.  Matter of fact, if you get a chance to walk through it when it's open, you can still see faceoff circles painted on the ground.  Unfortunately, the league no longer exist.

Cheesebox was a popular place to play since the asphalt is very clean and smooth.  There are still nets which are supplied there by the City.  Although now I'm starting to see more and more soccer players use the area.  Sign of the times I guess.

There is of course the roller rink over by PS.204 (28th between 36th and 37th Avenue).  The league there is run by the YMCA over in Long Island City.  Some pretty good competition from what I've seen there.  I'm sure the summer league is well underway. 

By the way, Astoria has produced one NHL player and a couple of college/junior players (including myself....though I'm now relegated to my Chelsea Piers leagues in my old age).  Most people thought more would come out as time went on.  But most of the kids in the area aren't pushed into sports like the kids were when I grew up.  So hockey has, unfortunately, gone the way of the do-do bird in Astoria.
