Anyone here experienced with home-buying and is finance savvy?
« on: June 25, 2013, 05:33:26 PM »
 
I am really bad with financial stuff and get a headache as soon as talk turns to numbers and things like 'interest, taxes, pension, penalties, ammortization, etc'.  Blech.  ;-)

I am hoping to buy a 1-2 family home.  I know that currently they say you need to have 20% to put down.  However I’d also recently read that some in the industry (lenders) were realizing that maybe the 20% thing was a bit overkill….just a kneejerk reaction to the mortgage crisis from a few years ago….that you really need to look at a person’s entire financial picture…not just say ‘ok put 20% down and all is good’.  That said though, I haven’t actually heard that the 20% down payment stipulation has loosened up at all.

Like many people, I don’t have 20% available ($120k) for say a $600k home…not even close.  I can take an early withdrawal from my 401k (current balance is $600k), on which I’d be heavily taxed and penalized and then use that for the down payment.  Not ideal of course…I’m 50 years old…but I think my 401k would continue to grow to a point where I could still retire comfortably, and esp. since I’d now own a home whose value (*Astoria*) would likely continue to increase. 

I’ve no understanding of the programs out there to potentially help with a down payment and I get overwhelmed with bureaucratic jargon when I do read various things online about mortgage assistance.  I think I’d be considered a first-time buyer in NYC.  (I only owned one other home, in Boston, but sold it 12 years ago.)  I read something about Down Payment Assistance Loans but the language is so confusing.  Would mortgage lenders know about other potential forms of assistance for me, and even if they did, would they have any incentive to tell me about them (or might they have some kind of incentive to actually NOT tell me about them)?  Does anyone here know about any NYC-centric down payment assistance programs that might help 'middle class' and/or first time buyers?

Any other ideas I’m not thinking of?  Don’t want to ask friends/family for a loan….don’t want to ‘wait another ten years’ to save up for a down payment.   I realize the 401k withdrawal sounds like a really bad idea….I believe that in order to get the net $120k for the down payment, that I’ll need to withdraw around $240k…because half of that will go to taxes and early withdrawal penalties.  But I also think that at least as far as the taxes go, I would have had to pay them at some point anyway…whether I withdrew now or after retirement.  And the way I see it, the early withdrawal penalty is the price I’m paying to get a house to call my own, AND I’m no longer throwing rent away each month.  AND I’d then start allocating more of my paycheck each month to my 401k, to try and rebuild the balance faster….

What do y’all think? 

Thanks very much!

Banks want to see how much of a risk a person is when applying for a home. So when you are under that 20% you become a higher risk. If you apply for a jumbo loan your rate is  higher and more risk. Banks also want to see how much you have left after your downpayment and closing costs  are paid. I know the mortgage broker  in Sovereign bank and she told me most people need 30% in her bank and. Not trying to discourage you but things r tight with banks.
