Much to my surprise, I was quoted in this recent DNAinfo article discussing reactions to the proposed soccer field:

https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20160129/ditmars/you-told-us-should-astoria-park-get-soccer-field

At the end of the article it says:

"A spokeswoman for Constantinides said the running track would remain open under his proposal, and the soccer field would not interfere with the track. The exact design of the field — and whether a fence would separate it from the track or not — has not been determined yet, she said."

I'm sorry this is wishful thinking on Costa's part. I can't see how two separate activities can exist in the same space without conflict. Does anyone know if the Astoria Park running club have a position on this?

My main objection is that the field inside the track is intended for field events and athletics training -- hence the name Astoria Park Track and Field. It's not some grassy stretch for rollicking and picnics. Why should field events activities and training be sacrificed for a soccer field? With that logic, why not ask basketball players to sacrifice the courts for shared use?

I have a counter proposal -- replace the parking lot with a soccer field or fields. There's more than enough space. I'd be on-board with that.
