I'm in total agreement with starwind .... except the DVD aspect. Bring a book, not a DVD!   

I do wish more parents would encourage books/puzzles/art/games to entertain their wee ones rather than park them in front of a TV. It actually kind of annoys me to see the DVD players in cars on when the folks are clearly on a short trip (like to school). Do they really have to have some sort of television thing happening even during a 10-minute car ride? Long rides I can grasp, but still . . . Books, &c were my only options in the car (or restaurants, I suppose) as a kid, and I got through many a long drive just fine without Nemo. My parents actually had the audacity to play the License Plate game and I Spy with my sis and I on the 8-hour drives to Mammoth Mountain three summers in a row.... good times. (As an aside, at one point during some trip I noted with horror that my dad was driving and sipping an orange soda. In total horror I screamed, "Daddy, you're not supposed to drink and drive!!!!" My parents laughed for a good hour, then explained the alcohol thing.) I do realize that I had an extraordinarily happy childhood which may or may not be rather unusual.

But nevermind all that. Who brings young kids to a nice, date-nightish, grown-up restaurant?!  Boggles my mind. I guess props to them for trying not to disturb the other patrons, but sheesh. If the sitter cancels, change your plans! Or take them somewhere appropriate and teach them how to behave. I also agree with KidMurdock. It certainly seems as though children are being taught more and more that the people around them don't matter and that you don't ever have to consider their comfort and treat them respectfully.

Disclaimer: I don't have kids. But I'm just sayin' ...
