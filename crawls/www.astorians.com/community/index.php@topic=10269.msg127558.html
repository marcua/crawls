I remember the Black Panthers, Vietnam Vets, Rednecks, anti- and pro-war protesters, gays and lesbians, pro- and anti-government advocates, pro- and anti-drug advocates. Although not scheduled, the music started around 5:00 PM on Friday. The meetings, however, had started as soon as people arrived the evening before. Abbie Hoffman, Jerry Rubin and Paul Krassner kept a list of where each group was camping so that anyone who wanted to could find each other.

Sure there were plenty of drugs and alcohol. But the meetings were happening everywhere . . . all the time. Woodstock was the premiere planning stage of the resistance movements. Our brothers, boy friends and husbands were being conscripted into the armed services to die fighting an illegal war that we weren't going to win and no-one was listening to us. We shared our food, dry clothing and anything else we had that someone else didn't. We, yes all 400,000+ of us, respected each other even if we disagreed with their political stance. There was no name calling. I don't even remember a single fist fight.

We were grooving to the music, and planning how to overthrow the current administration for the good of the nation. Everyone who would become infamous in the future of resistance movements was there. We were seeking a voice for everyone, not just looking for the majority voices.

I look around now and realize how much has changed since then. I am personally heart sickened at the Obama backers who are calling for Clinton to drop out and leave her millions of supporters disenfranchised because they want to show their muscle without any regard for those disenfranchised. In 1969 we were not only willing to hear each other out, to the end, but to try to understand the other point of view. There were those who had started the non-peaceful demonstration movements in Chicago in 1968. It was a time for proponents of both the civil disobedience and more radical violent movements to hear each other's objectives.

The outcome of all those meetings produced the counterculture of Love, Sex and Rock 'N Roll. We had empathy and love, even for those who did not agree with us.

And one other truly remarkable thing happened on that memorable weekend. After trying and failing for years and years, I became pregnant with my now 38-year old twin sons.
