Quote from: astoria101 on October 26, 2015, 01:44:33 PM
Great news but wondering why they don't show the same love to the R train.



It's much more difficult to add capacity to the R train due to the lengthy turnaround time required at Forest Hills - 71 Ave.  Unlike the N train, which simply switches over at Ditmars and heads in the other direction, the R train must first be checked, car by car, to ensure there are no passengers remaining onboard before it can be turned.  This is because it goes through a series of switches inside the tunnel, and they don't want customers on the train while this is happening.

When the first section of the Second Avenue subway finally opens, the plan is for the Q to run on the line after 57th/7th up to 125th Street, meaning no more Q train for LIC and Astoria.

http://web.mta.info/capital/sas_alt.html
http://www.gothamgazette.com/index.php/opinion/5811-good-and-bad-news-on-second-avenue-subway-plotch-bloom
http://secondavenuesagas.com/2015/06/05/q-train-quandaries-astoria-and-the-second-ave-subway/

The big question now is what happens in Queens? The MTA seems to think they could add a few more trains to handle the ridership. The current N and Q service can't even do that during rush hour, with all the developments planned it will only get worse. There's been a suggestion to bring back the W train, but this looks unlikely. There's not enough money for it and as wannabehippie said, the MTA has never brought back a line after it's demise.

For all the talk about the new ferry service, which local politicians and the media are gaga over, it won't alleviate the problem on the subway. Let's face it, unless you live in one of the new developments on the water, the ferry won't be a viable option for most Astorians. First it's too far and inconvenient to get to and second it will be two expensive (at least for me).

What's the point of having to take a bus to the ferry and then a bus on the other side and/or a subway ride to get to work? Who has the time for that even if they could afford it? An occasional treat yes, but it won't work for commuting.
