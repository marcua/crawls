I keep starting this post, and erasing what I write, because I can't restrain a tendency to hyperbole about this event. I am tremendously looking forward to it.


I've been at least once in the past, when it was in Woodside, which was a world-class experience of food and cultural pleasure. This stuff is not available in restaurants at any real level of authenticity in NY. Don't miss the smoked tea salad, or the ginger salad.


Burmese food that I've had in the past shows some influence from India, Thailand, China - and yet it seems completely distinctive to me.


This year feels so much more hopeful politically in Myanmar (Burma) than it has been there for a long, long time. Nobel Peace Prize winner Aung San Suu Kyi was freed after 15 years of house arrest, and elected to the Burmese parliament along with many in her opposition party.


I am a powerfully food-focused (American) person, but it's meaningful to me to consider this context as I wade into a community as an outsider with limited understanding.
