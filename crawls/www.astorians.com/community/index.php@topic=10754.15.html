IMO, I don't think the war was based on lies. I do, however, believe that the war was based on taking incomplete intelligence and running on speculation, which to me seems an extremely irresponsible way to plunge a nation into war. 
NIE (National Intelligence Estimate) is an organization that draws it's speculation from various U.S. Intelligence agencies and basically makes an educated guess. To me, this does not seem like a good basis for launching the country into war. 
The WMDs were not ever physically located, nor were they even proven to have existed.
The briefing by the NIE, if I read it correctly, stated that Iraq had the capability to obtain WMD materials, but there was no hard evidence of a nuclear weapons program in the works. I also can't really find where the hard links to Al Qaeda are in the reports.
I also believe that post-invasion reports almost all affirmed that there were not, in-fact, WMDs, hard links to Al Qaeda nor was there an imminent threat. To me, this proves why you get hard evidence before running into war, and not jump in based on speculation. The cost in American lives and tax dollars seems unreasonably highfor something that may or may not be true.

This Senate analysis of pre-war intelligence is actually a pretty interesting read if you have the time: http://intelligence.senate.gov/phaseiiaccuracy.pdf
