I guess for me I think it wouldn't necessarily be about "adding jobs" although I suppose that that would happen - but more about making our neighborhood spruce up a bit and have more to offer to the community. I don't know how long any of you have lived here in Astoria but in the 3 years that I have lived here it has already vastly improved. A lot of storefronts have replaced their signs and awnings, and it looks so much better. Hopefully that will continue, but I think that the Olympics make any town decide to do a little bit of a makeover. I don't know about you, but I like it when my community makes the effort to present itself well and try to be more aesthetically pleasing. Maybe that's superficial, but I'd rather live in a community that thinks it deserves some nice upgrades every now and then (kind of like I think I deserve to dress up and be pretty sometimes   ).

In regards to displacing people with a new stadium (or any other new sports venue) - if it isn't NY it will be Paris and if it isn't going to be Paris then it will be somewhere else. No matter where the Olympics goes there are changes made that not everyone is happy about. I mean, look at Atlanta - I am sure that a few million were inconvenienced so the world could come together and play games in the spirit of peace.

So then, technically if we don't want to inconvenience a few people in NYC why should the Olympics inconvenience a few people elsewhere? Why not just stop the Olympics from happening altogether?

I know I am going to extremes - but when we ask the world to come to our home we have to accomodate them and the events. And I believe that a lot of new jobs will be created and kept long after the games are over. And even if some of those jobs don't hold, then I don't think that is that great big of a deal. A lot of people are resourceful enough to find another job if they try hard enough. If all else fails - look on craigslist. No - scratch that. Look on craigslist before all else fails. :)
