The inspector and the guest speaker were both called away so the meeting was a bit abnormal in information presentation.  A new guy (chief? somebody) is new to the 114 and he did the Q&A impressively well for having been at the precinct only a few months.

Big issues:

- Queensview residents have problems with cabs parking illegally on the street from an auto repair shop on 21st street -- cabs park in residential, garage repairs 24hours a day, and noise/parking are problematic.  This issue spills over into the Dutch Kills area as well (1 word or 2?) -- avenues in the high 30s near 21st street.

- Grafitti a problem.  Task force to clean it up through Vallone's office.  If you need help/referall for free graffiti cleaning contact Vallone.

- Motorcyle season is coming up.  New guy (chief?) plans to impound all bikes that are not legally registered/not licensed drivers and discourage them from congregating in the park with frequent patrolling at different hours.  311 also a good resource for complaints.

Announcements/Upcoming:

- United Community Civic Association is having a meeting with Dept of Sanitation Commissioner John Doherty & Commissioner Dept of Transportation Maura McCarthy -- Thurs April 19, 7:30PM @ Augustana Lutheran Church, 69-05 Ditmars Blvd

- Bus Tour of LaGuardia airport and lunch sign up (complimentary tour) -- 718.932.5415 -- deadline April 19th (tour date not mentioned)

NEXT precinct meeting: April 24th, 7pm

I also think that continued involvement, even in the seemingly silly little things, are the way to make it in this group.  Next meeting (my 4th or 5th?) I plan to ask to help with the sign in and meet the reps from the various offices.  Spoke to the new chief fellow and he's quite receptive to hearing from the community and the inspector also welcomes visitors to his office at the 114 if you'd like to speak with him personally/directly.
