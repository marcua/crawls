I'll disagree with you both. I wasn't really meaning to make this a conservative/liberal issue and I don't remember mentioning Bush at all, but if you spend a little time on Youtube or even just Google you'll find that every day there are way too many new incidents where police officers go too far in the way they treat ordinary citizens. There are so many instances of little or no respect, no regard for citizens constitutional rights, and sometimes people die for no reason at all. It's the conservative in me that doesn't like seeing how government paid employees can push a peaceful citizen to the ground, pin them, and then punch their face into the pavement when they were already cooperating, or open fire on a peaceful crowd of demonstrators. What I find even more upsetting is that in the majority of these instances the officers are placed on "paid administrative leave" or get off with nothing more than a hand slap.

In this case it doesn't matter to me if the guy wasn't liked in the town, or if he hadn't paid child support. It's a little disturbing that these things are even brought up in the context of what the video shows as if this somehow makes us feel better about this man's rights being violated by a uniformed police officer. Constitutional rights and due process should be applied to everyone in my POV. I'm discouraged that this kind of thing happens in America.

I'm not against police overall, but I do believe the incidents of abuse of the law (and constitutional rights) by police officers are statistically too high and that little is done about it. I know there are good cops out there (I know some), but that doesn't make up for the bad apples - I feel there are too many and the system needs some attention.
