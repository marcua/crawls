Astoria Bagel= ALWAYS RUDE, TERRIBLE CUSTOMER SERVICE
« on: March 14, 2009, 02:11:29 PM »
I've gone into this place on Ditmars about a dozen times in the past year (at all different times of day) and every time, I've been treated badly. The severity of it varies from just ignoring me and helping other (Greek) customers before me, or having conversations with other (Greek) customers while I stand there waiting to order, to more recently, just flat out rudeness. 

I went in there today, around noon, but it wasn't busy. Maybe one person ahead of me. I give my order. A few people order after me and get their bagels before me. After a few minutes the guy behind the counter confirms my cream cheese again. I was watching the guy making the orders and hadn't even seen him pick up the type of bagel I wanted. So I asked if he made it yet and if not I'd like to change my order if I could. Both guys stare at me and the one says, "what do you want, it's in the toaster already." "Okay," I say. "I was going to change the bagel but that's fine." I totally did not make a big deal at all b/c I also work in the food service industry, and even though I have no problem changing orders even if it's already made (call me old-fashioned, I guess) I get that it's still money and he'd probably have to toss it if it's toasted.

So even after I say that I'll take the bagel that's being made, he still stares at me (I wish I could put into words the look he gave me) he still says, "what am I supposed to do with the bagel? What do you want to do?" I said I'd just take it, it's fine...and STILL he stares at me, so annoyed. I said I'd take the bagel!!!

I was really just perplexed at why he was so nasty when I wasn't making a big deal out of anything, I just asked.

So then he's bagging it up, looking at me, and says something I couldn't hear. I said, "what?" and he just shakes his head. I asked him again what he said and he says (this is the kicker)

"You know lots of languages are spoken around here. There's many different cultures. English is not the only way to communicate."

So I said since he was looking at me when he was speaking, that's why I asked what he said. Not b/c I wanted him to translate or something. I don't even know what would provoke him to say that. He said he looks around a lot when he talks. Finally I got to leave. Pretty pissed off.

Especially when I get home and after all that, my bagel is burnt and hard as a hockey puck.

If I was being a bitch, that's one thing. But I wasn't. And after going through all of my experiences with that place, I realize that it's always been that level of rudeness and unfriendliness.

Anyone else?
