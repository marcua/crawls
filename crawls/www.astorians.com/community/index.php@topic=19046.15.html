Spoke with Benny at Marino's two weeks ago. Said that the summer killed them and it just wasn't worth (financially) staying open anymore.  Said people had been coming in to say good bye and cry on his shoulder.  They hate to leave but you can't run a business for free. 

They were my first vendor in the neighborhood.  Great guys who really cared.  Would also let me sneak past the old ladies on Friday and Saturdays to pick up my orders.  If you notice, all the old folks who lined up at Marinos, Franks bakery and International meats are all gone and the newbies in the neighborhood just don't shop the same. 

Benny lent me $100 during the summer black out of 2003.  The atms were down and I had $5 on me.  Pulled out the money and just gave it to me.  Said to pay it back whenever the banks reopened. 

Will really miss them.

NYCmac, That particular topic did come up numerous times over the years but the did not want to do it.  I do know for a fact that a few factors really hurt them:
Recession over past 5 years.
Wholesale clients going out of business, needing to go cheap or just going to bigger operations like restaurant depot for one stop shopping.
The old neighborhood that died off or moved away had been their mainstay for decades.

Also Marino's ownership may have just been too old to change their ways.  Too bad.  They are all looking for other work on Long island where they live. 

As for catering to the younger crowd, it was more than just the hours.  I know for a fact that a number of old school shops feel that the only way to get the younger crowd in would be to switch to single serve items raw or cooked.  Single theme shops like a butcher or bakery or fish store just can't compete with whole foods for marketing, packaging, mass offerings and gourmet prepared selections.

Of course in areas of Brooklyn many young foodies ARE flocking to single themed shops for top notch artisanal products such as bakeries, cheese and butchers so go figure.  Astoria is not there yet and then it will be too late.

International meats is one of the old school shops that jumped forward, made the renovations, packaging and service changes to attract more wholesale and retail clients.  Cudos to them. 

I am bringing my son over to see Benny this week and say goodbye.  They've asked about him ever day since he was born.  It's going to kill me.

Some good news.  NYCmacuser can stop crying now.  I went to see the guys yesterday and got updated.

They closed mainly because:
1. Times changed and they didnt or couldnt change with it AND still be happy doing it.  Had been on 30th ave since 1932.
2. Got a buyer for the building.
3.  The guys were getting tired of it all and wanted a change.

Now for some good news especially for those living by Best Yet supermarket on 19th ave and 37th street in Astoria.

Turns out one of Marino's son in laws does the seafood ordering for all Best yet stores.  Another son in law does the ordering of produce and groceries.  Looks like Charlie and maybe Benny will be running the seafood counter there as of September.  Why, because it is so hard to find fish mongers like these guys anymore and Best Yet wants them.  Not set in stone yet but Charlie put a sign up at Marino's suggesting people shop at Best yet for fish.

PS:  They think the possible new owner of the building might be putting in a restaurant.
