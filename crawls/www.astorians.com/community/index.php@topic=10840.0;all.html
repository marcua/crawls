Astorians.com is essentially an online publication covering Astoria , a highly-interactive one yes, but in essence its a magazine where contributing writers (i.e., the users) provide the content (i.e., the threads) within a set of editorial guidelines (i.e., the rules) set by the owner and editor-in-chief of the publication (i.e., merm) and the editors (i.e., moderators).

Some feel the editorial guidelines or The Rules are too loose, some think they are too strict, others feel they too arbitrarily enforced. You cant please everyone.

If the editor-in-chief or subeditors object to something that a contributor has written it their prerogative to either edit it or delete it from the publication or in the case of astorians.com, since it is published in real-time, ban the contributor from further contributions for a period of time.
