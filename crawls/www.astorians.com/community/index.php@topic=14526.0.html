« on: April 05, 2009, 10:14:37 AM »
So, I have a couple of containers - one glass, one plastic - which are keeping residual strong odors (one had garlic and chiles in it, one had kvass-beets) that might affect whatever goes in there next.  Does anyone know a way to remove strong odors with the result of a neutral scent?  Or at least something less objectionable (and not chemical cleaning solution odors).  Was thinking baking soda or salt and lemon.  If you have a suggestion, I'm all ears!

Re: Plastic and glass with residual odor - how to eliminate it?
« Reply #1 on: April 05, 2009, 10:50:49 AM »
I just had to do it yesterday with my creuset from the fish stock that was in there. Ok, so for plastic, usually COLD water, like almost freezing, plus dishwashing liquid will get rid of tomato stains and other odors (unless it wasn't cleaned well the first time around and you've already used it to rewarm something else and the stain has basically "cooked in" there.) For glass, I'd suggest what I did with my creuset, 1/4 of a lemon (as a sponge) + baking soda + distilled white vinegar (not a lot of this). Do not put any water into this while you're making the paste. Start with the lemon and baking soda only. I had to wash the same pot about 3-4 times with this method, but eventually, it went away, and again, COLD water. Good luck!

I think you just have to designate certain containers for certain things because the smell is so difficult, if not impossible to get rid of.

If you don't need to re-use the container right away, though, try washing and air-drying thoroughly, then putting a paper napkin or paper coffee filter in and closing the container.  The paper absorbs odors.  And/or try putting in a little plain rice or baking soda and closing.  (Don't plan to eat the rice.)
