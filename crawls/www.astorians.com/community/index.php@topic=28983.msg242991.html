Went back a second time last night, with most of the food again impressing and delicious. My hubby especially loved the salmon kababs. The ezme, the lentil soup and bread again very good. Tried the falafel, which was maybe just okay. Also enjoyed the almond pudding.


The atmosphere changed, negatively for us, with TV screens filled with Fox News' Bill O'Reilly show, and the previously enjoyed Turkish and Greek music replaced (at least during that time) by generic American golden oldies (Whitney Houston, Kansas, Elton John, etc.).


Our waiter explained that after 10 days of listening to the Turkish and Greek music, at least some of them pleaded for the change.


The owner, at the cash register, and a nice guy, said they'd stop playing Fox News.


I'm aware that Fox News and the millionth revisit of the officially sanctioned (way overplayed) commercial top hits of past decades make an enjoyable atmosphere for some people. Not for me. Was ready to leave as quickly as possible. Especially when Whitney began wailing out "The Greatest Love of All." Don't want to ever hear that again, honestly.
