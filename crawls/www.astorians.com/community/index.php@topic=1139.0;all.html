If you have a rice cooker you don't even need to measure the ingredients. You just pour however much rice you want into the cooker, then you put in water to cover the rice 1 inch. So the water line is one inch above your rice.

To make sushi rice you just add a little sugar and rice wine vinegar when it's done and still hot and you stir it a lot. The rice will become sticky since you are helping it cool off fast.

That's all there is to it. Brown rice, white rice, doesn't matter. One inch is all you need and hit start.

the recipe merm mentions goes for cooking white rice or basmati rice in a pot too.

the trick is to put in the rice first, cover it with about 1 inch of rice (or put your index finger in the pot just touching top of rice, water should come up to the second joint of your finger.) 
Just so you know, 2/1 water to rice is usually too much liquid.

So, 
saute any herbs, aromatics, veggies, oil/butter. 
mix in rice, pour in water using trick above.
bring to boil uncovered. 
lower to simmer, cover and cook 10-15 minutes.
should be fluffy when stirred at this point.

A rice cooker rocks by the way.  great thing to have in the kitchen and a no brainer to boot.
