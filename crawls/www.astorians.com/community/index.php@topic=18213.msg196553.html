You can count on it. I am already telling out-of-town family that I may have hit the jackpot of bakeries. My children grew up in the era of The Steinway Bake Shop (real dark chocolate fudge layer cake) and Avon Bakery (real butter cream frosting and fillings); I grew up is the era of Norgäards (where everything was made with such love and the finest ingredients you didn't care what it cost) on 30th Avenue and I can still remember Mama standing in front of the counter trying to make the decision of what to get for the company that Saturday night dinner. I always loved making the dessert a masterpiece of the meal. I am hoping I no longer will have to plan a day excursion out of town to get something really special to serve my guests. I also remember Leo's Bakery on 30th Avenue and 35th Street who made the best breads anywhere. They baked a real Jewish Rye and Corn Bread. Not the Southern kind, the European kind.

I miss the days of good, traditional bakeries in this neighborhood. I remember when I was very little, going into Norgaards with my parents and the nice, older women that worked behind the counter giving me cookies. Avon was where my birthday cakes came from, Steinway Bake Shop was where my dad would get fresh rolls for Saturday morning breakfast. Even Astoria Bakery, on 30th Ave between 33rd and 34th Streets was better than it is now.

Astor, if you do even half as good a job as the Astoria bakeries of yore, you'll have my business!

Well the pressure is definitely on, but I hope to meet your expectations.  As far as keeping your fighting weight, I've managed throughout the years to keep a healthy weight despite my love of ice cream, chocolate and meat (excluding my 6 yrs of vegetarianism). My rule is to eat healthful food, keep plenty of fruit, nuts and grains in my diet, and indulge in moderation. Even when indulging, I keep it natural. The less processed the better. I think our bodies naturally feel unsatisfied when we eat artificial, processed foods, but if that's all we're used to we won't look for something better.  Instead we'll overeat the junk trying to get satisfaction. I could be totally wrong, but that's my belief, and it seems to have kept me healthy and happy over the years.
