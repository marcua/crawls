The advice I've been given is to avoid dentists in Astoria entirely and find one in the city who's on your dental plan, if you have one.

Actually, at the gym recently I overheard someone extolling the virtures of a Ditmars-area dentist ('really nice, just him and his wife', etc.) that she and her husband just started going to. In the same converation I heard this person express surprise that she apparently had a few cavities, but was glad that this dentist "found" them. I was tempted to intrude and suggest she go to another dentist to get a second opinion because this was the same dentist who told me I had two cavities. (As it turned out this was patently false, on the basis of a thorough examination a city dentist did -- several months later.)

I kind of felt guilty about not warning her, but I wasn't comfortable butting in... Oh well.

Back to topic: good luck finding a reputable dentist around here. I've not heard many success stories (friends, landlord, fiancee) of those who've gone local... And if you're told that you'll need work done, absolutely get a second opinion -- don't mention to the second dentist what the first one "found", though.
