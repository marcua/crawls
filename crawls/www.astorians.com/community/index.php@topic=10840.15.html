I certainly think discussion is a good thing, but I think something is being lost in this discussion - Merm built this board, set it up, designed it, maintains it at almost entirely his own expense (save for some donations and some advertising revenue), taking a loss in keeping it up.  He spends a lot of time providing all of us this wonderful community home, and I think we need to thank him for his good work. 

While I can understand people disagreeing on specific actions, rules or policies (suspensions, moving postings, etc.), I do not understand how anyone can believe there is any conspiracy, as seems to be alleged in the link and discussion at the other board.  I'm also not sure why it is better to have a discussion of policies and feelings about this board on another site instead of discussing them here. 

The rules are posted, and the moderating team try to enforce them fairly and across the board.  When issues arrive, discussion is open and happens in public threads.  As one of the two current moderators at Astorians.com, I am not aware of any Central Committee, and while it's a bit embarrassing to admit, I do not recall any posts by daveednyc (though I could go and pull them up).  I do not regard him as a troublemaker or anything else.  I do not think either Merm or myself are afraid of dissent, I really enjoy seeing active discussion though the rules also exist to help keep discussion civil and free of personal attacks.

Quote from: wasabisam on June 13, 2008, 04:18:46 PM
What I don't get is seemingly adult and mature individuals have chosen to continually call the site's owner an asshole, etc., (even after he has apologized!) yet some of those same people have little constructive dialog to contribute to improving the board. I recognize that there has been *some* positive dialog coming out of this, but I do feel that some (not all) people involved in this situation seem to enjoy this vitriolic diatribe, and would rather cook up some sort of conspiracy than to just accept his honest apology and move on as adults.

If you and the site owner are mystified that people are upset about the response to his behavior, mystified that his "it's really not that big of a deal" apology hasn't quelled criticism of moderation here, then I suggest you consider the following.

Some people are evaluating the site admin's character in terms of his initial decision to censor "astorialogues." He makes it sound like it wasn't that big of a deal and that it was directed against specific people. But those people had already had their posts deleted and their membership suspended. So why censor the word site-wide?

When my wife asked me to try and figure out why a link from astorians to astorialogues wasn't working, it took me a while to wrap my mind around the possibility that the word had been censored. And then I asked for it to be uncensored. For how much longer was the site admin going to censor astorialogues if he hadn't been caught? For how long had it been censored? Maybe you can see how some people would think all of this is upsetting. What kind of a person does this? That's the question some people are asking. Some of these people find his apology after being caught utterly inadequate because it's not like he had a choice. Defend what he'd done? Not apologize?

After trials, people found guilty of committing crimes get to apologize for what they've done. It doesn't wash away the consequences of what they've done. Sorry, wasabisam, if some people are not as forgiving as quickly as you wish we'd be.

Some people also have a strong reaction to merm's moderating behavior because its inconsistencies produce the sense that he chooses to enforce his rules haphazardly, whimsically, unpredictably. And that's troubling for the same reason that people resent it when the laws they live by are enforced arbitrarily. Having people wonder about what's okay to express on a forum is counterproductive and upsetting. 

For example, the site owner went out of his way to praise a Freezepeach promoter(spammer?) and expressed incredulity that anyone would have a problem with somebody posting incessantly to show love for a local business, touting it over and over again in a noncommercial part of the site. On the other hand, a thread (which was started by a member of both of our sites) about astorialogues (showing love for a NON-commercial enterprise) was "moved" to the commercial part of the site almost immediately. Once there, a single bump resulted in post-deletions, a person being suspended, and the name "astorialogues" being censored site-wide.

Quote from: merm
I thought it was a harmless way to annoy the "bumper".
That's just not believable. In what other instance since he started this site has the owner censored a web link as a form of retaliation against a thread bumper?

Quote from: merm
For the record, I think it's great there's another Astoria site out there. Just wish that we didn't need to re-bump this thread to build traffic over there. It's sort of annoying (kinda like broken links maybe?).
Here the site owner is saying that the bumping of a single thread expressing enthusiasm for a local enterprise in the least trafficked part of this site is the moral equivalent of his censoring astorialogues on his entire site. Can you see how a reasonable person would find a comment like this disingenuous? He's framing what he did as a form of quid pro quo, not as simply and inexcusably wrong. 

Quote from: merm
I do ask though that you encourage your users if possible to just try and respect the decorum we have here at Astorians.
They're not MY users. They're members of YOUR site, too. You are the one treating people who have posted on astorialogues differently. Most of them probably don't view themselves as choosing sides vis-a-vis the communities. The intense negative feedback you are receiving has everything to do with your moderating approach and nothing to do with the people who make up Astorians or Astorialogues.

I'm the only person acting on behalf of astorialogues. I haven't claimed that there is a conspiracy to hurt astorialogues. I gave the site owner constructive criticism when I became aware of problems with his moderating approach. I do not like vitriolic diatribes either, but neither do I think it's wise to censor them or dismiss them as meaningless.

I realize that some people will think I'm posting here to continue a conversation that leads to people visiting astorialogues, but that's not it. No links in my message to my site. I'm trying to set the record straight. You said you were mystified. I agree with your self-assessment. You do not appear to "get it." You're focusing too much on the names the site owner is being called and too little on the underlying causes of people's dissatisfaction.

What's unfortunate in this situation is that, to many people, that one astorialogues thread out of context makes the board look like a collection of very bitter people who have an agenda against this board. In actuality, most of the people there joined that board because they wanted to be able to have potentially heated discussions about world issues without moderators going crazy when someone gets offended and hits the report button. If I remember the timing correctly, that board was created after merm threatened to get rid of the politics section altogether a few months ago because he was tired of dealing with it. It wasn't so they could go there and trash merm behind his back. If you look at the rest of the board, while there aren't tons of people posting there, most of the discussions are earnest and thoughtful and started by people who just want to talk about things.

This isn't about not being allowed to call people assholes during flame wars. merm is welcome to set the rules for this board as he sees fit, and it's clear that he doesn't want people calling people names. What set most of the astorialogues people off was the silent censoring of the name on this site, which happened just because one or two people decided to bump the thread in the Business & Services category. It's not a conspiracy theory since it did happen, and the only reasons some people (myself included) thought this misspelling of the link was targeted to specific people were 1) we didn't know there was a censored/uncensored option on this board, so we were very confused that it only affected certain people, and 2) it didn't cross our minds that astorialogues would be a censored word, since that's ridiculous.

merm at this point has admitted that he made a bad decision by doing this, and I would think that anyone with any ability to empathize would be able to understand why those people who enjoy posting at astorialogues would find this action highly offensive whether he apologized or not. But my point here is not to criticize merm further. It's that too many people are too ready to dismiss any "dissenters" as being people who just like to complain, and they'd sooner tell them to go away or just get over it than make any attempt to understand their point of view.
