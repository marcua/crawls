Quote from: Capibara on August 01, 2007, 10:35:37 PM
If you go to Europe though and stay in hostels or someplace with a lot of travellers you are bound to meet people, especially Aussies & Kiwis, they are EVERYWHERE!

I had to chuckle at your last statement.   :-) 

I definitely agree with staying in hostels or hotels that cater to backpackers - I actually met a fellow backpacker once while traveling in Korea.  He now lives with his wife in Astoria, and my husband and I get together with them from time to time!

One thing I've never gotten over when traveling solo (longest period of time for ten weeks) is feeling nervous when arriving at a new place.  It really helps me to have a map ready, to have studied it ahead of time and have in mind a couple of hostels or hotels I might try, if I haven't already made a reservation in advance.  As a solo traveler, I don't like to rely on cabs when arriving at a new destination, and so it really helps me to know how I can get to the hostel/hotel on foot or by public transportation.  Safety is a bigger concern for me than loneliness.

I would also ask around and see whether a friend of a friend is planning on being abroad, or knows someone who is living in the places that you plan to be.

I'm sorry that you had such a bad experience, it's unfortunate that men ever feel like they can take liberties with anyone, foreigner or not.

I've done alot of travelling on my own, and have both enjoyed it and hated it. The one nice thing about being alone is that you do tend to meet tons of people, and can usually find someone to tag along with on short trips. I do find that I am a lot more outgoing when I am travelling than when I am home- partly the loneliness factor,  and partly just curiosity.

The bad thing about travelling alone as a woman is that you have to be super careful b/c you are always vulnerable. I do think that how you present yourself has a huge impact however. What you do is not important, but you need to make it clear from the first moment that you are not looking for anything and that you expect to be treated with respect. The clothes you wear say a lot, as does the way you approach people. Knowing what you want and where you are going helps too.

That said,  if there is something you want to do and somewhere you want to go, I say do it. Plan it well, be careful, but do it.
