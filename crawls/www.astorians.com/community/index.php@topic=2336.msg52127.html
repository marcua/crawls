I'm really sorry to hear about the violence that your party received.  That shouldn't happen anywhere but especially not in peaceful Astoria.

I moved here from Harlem about 4 years ago and I have always remarked at how safe, clean and 'neighborhoody' Astoria is.  I don't think these few random acts can necessarily be labelled an epidemic but you have to understand what's causing that level of trouble in that one specific area.  Namely, the cluster of nightclubs, usually catering to younger audiences, that have sprung up over the last few years on Broadway from 31st Street to 34th Street.

I drove by the other night and there must have been some 'End of School Jam' going on at one of the clubs and the street was filled with loud, rambuncious 15-year olds dressed like 19-year olds.

Don't get me wrong, I love kids, but when you get more than 30 teenagers in the same cluster without adequate parental or authoratative supervision and add in the whole 'boys and girls' element to it, you're asking for trouble.

It's a dilemma because those clubs, and the commerce they attract for local businesses, is important but how do we keep it all safe and sound???

Maybe the meeting will provide some answers.

Editorials                                                 January 24, 2007

Hate Crime Solved

To The Editor:
On June 10, 2006, an individual stated he was walking down the street when eight (8) unknown white males hit him over the head with a baseball bat. This occurred at approximately 0110 (1:10 a.m.) hours at the intersection of Broadway and 34th Street. The victim sustained a deep laceration to his head and forehead. The perpetrators fled the location and a canvass of the surrounding area was conducted with negative results. Upon further investigation, this was believed to be an anti-gay incident and the Hate Crime Task Force took control of the case (carried under Complaint #2006- 114-5992).

As a result of the investigation, on January 10, 2007 at approximately 2145 hours (9:45 p.m.), Detective Caban of the Hate Crime Task Force apprehended three individuals for this incident. Their pedigree information is as follows:

+Daniel Shamenti, m/u, 22 years of age, of 175 Lincoln Ave., Albany NY- ARREST#Q07602121.

+Hector Dika, m/u, 21 years of age, of 34-07 36th Ave., Astoria, (resident of the 114th Pct.)- ARREST#Q07602076.

+Armando Uka, m/u, 20 years of age, of 70-06 Woodside Ave., Queens Village (resident of the 105th Pct.)- ARREST#Q07602066.

The perpetrators did yell anti-gay and antiblack epithets at the victim, along with several others, which resulted in a fight, at which time they proceeded to punch, kick and strike the victim with a baseball bat. The other victims who were menaced were able to flee the scene.

This appalling incident was a hate crime targeting gay men and was solved with tenacity. I would like to take this time to commend the dedicated and thorough work of the Hate Crime Task Force. I maintained a rapport with the investigators throughout and can assure you much time and effort was expended into bringing this matter to a successful conclusion.
Respectfully,
Brian McCarthy
Inspector
114th Police Precinct
Commanding Officer

I didn't see it that way. This case is far from closed.

I just researched some additional information on the cases.

Hector Dika was arraigned on January 12th before Judge Zayas in the Queens Criminal Court under Docket 2007QN002303. The District attorney asked for $15,000 bail (of which $10,000 must be cash). He was unable to post bail. He was  returned to court on January 26th before Criminal Court Judge Mullings where an Order of Protection was issued for the victim(s), and was returned to jail. His next appearance will be on March 1st.

Armando Uka was arraigned on January 12th before Judge Zayas in the Queens Criminal Court under Docket 2007QN002304. The District attorney asked for $20,000 bail (of which $15,000 must be cash). He was unable to post bail. He was returned to court on January 26th before Criminal Court Judge Mullings where an Order of Protection was issued for the victim(s), and was returned to jail. His next appearance will be on March 1st.

I was unable to find any information on Daniel Shamenti. Apparently the Gazette spelled his name incorrectly. Daniel Shameti was arraigned on January 12th before Judge Zayas in the Queens Criminal Court under Docket 2007QN002302. The District attorney asked for $20,000 bail (of which $15,000 must be cash). He was unable to post bail. He was returned to court on January 26th before Criminal Court Judge Mullings where an Order of Protection was issued for the victim(s), and was returned to jail. His next appearance will be on March 1st.

I found a fourth person arrested not listed in the article. Brian Ewald, 20 years old, was arraigned on January 12th before Judge Zayas in the Queens Criminal Court under Docket 2007QN002305. The District attorney asked for $25,000 bail (of which $20,000 must be cash). He was unable to post bail. He was returned to court on January 26th before Criminal Court Judge Mullings where an Order of Protection was issued for the victim(s), and was able to post bond. His next appearance will be on March 1st.

I am quite certain that the 3 that are being held on Rikers Island are being questioned frequently. Sooner or later they will give up the names of the others. That is, of course, if they even know who they were. This was a crime of opportunity. They may not all have been together. There is also the possibility that Brian Ewald struck a deal to plea in exchange for either testimony or additional information.

They have each been assigned a Public Defender.

Let's allow the system to work.
