The number one reason coops don't appeal to me, probably boils down to privacy.  I am very private with my life, both personal and financial, and I'm just not into the idea of my neighbors knowing such personal details about my life.  There is the notion that I don't want to have to seek approval of my neighbors to buy an apartment, to let someone move in, to renovate, to rent it out later on.   I also want to own my apartment, as opposed to owning a share of a building.  The maintenance fees are usually much higher on coops, making the monthly costs harder to handle, even after paying off the mortgage (especially since I'd like a 2br).  Although tax deductable, I would still have to pay those fees every month.  I do plan to stay in the condo for a good number of years, but would like the option of renting it out, say if I got married and moved into a house.  If I chose to sell, I'd like the process of selling to be easier and without board approval or a flip-tax (not all coops have this).

Those are most of my reasons, and I do think it's just a matter of personal preference.  I'm sure if I had to take a coop, I could make it work for me.  But if given a choice, I'd prefer a condo.

But even better, I'd love a house with space for a garden and a parking spot  :)

Daisy,

There is an unusual lack of marketing/websites.  But the good news (for current owners in the area) is that these places seem to sell themselves before the last cement is poured.  There is a lack of new, luxury condos in Astoria.  When a building goes up, the units are snatched up in a hurry.  Hence the ever rising prices.  There are a few medium scale buildings going up on 30th Ave in the vicinity of 21st St.  Perhaps signs will go up on the scaffolding soon.  Astoria Windsor www.astoriawindsor.com is on 21st St. and 31st Ave.  Cool looking bldg. but their website doesn't have sales info and no realtor seems to be representing it.  The bldg looks close to finished though.   If I were you, I'd wait for the Pistilli property on Ditmars to open for sales.  Not only is it a larger complex, but it will help to establish a new market rate pricing for the area (instead of the free for all happening now).  The Riverview (21st and Astoria Park) is a coop - but more unsettling, they are required to convert to condo within 10 years.  I don't care what anyone says - those owners are going to have a shocking expense when that time comes.  There's also Hallet's Cove condos on Vernon around 31st Ave.  I think a few units are still left.  They plan to build a second building across the road.  I also heard the Adirondack Furniture factory on Vernon is being converted to luxury loft condos.  And there's the not-brand-new Shore Towers, which is undergoing a complete renovation of lobby, halls, etc.  Prices at Shore Towers have risen substantially due to the prices being paid in LIC and nearby condos.  No condo in Astoria is going to be cheap.  But I think it's a sound investment.

Last time I had a discussion like this I was accused of being a developer, etc.  Not that it's anyone's business but I'm just a resident and property owner - who likes the changes he's seeing.
