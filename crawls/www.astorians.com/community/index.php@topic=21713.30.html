I for one agree with the protest.  Remembering the 60's when people went out of their comfort zone more often to protest, a lot was accomplished. 
 
I come from a strong background of trading, and working with Wall Street, and I'll tell you this, it's not what it used to be.  I also believe that corporations have cheated many Americans out of jobs because of greed, jobs need to come back to the U.S., products need to be fabricated HERE.  Strict regulations must be applied to EVERYONE, the stock market is a fiasco, when researching a company how do you know the financial information is ... kosher?  Unfortunately, when something goes wrong (i.e. government handouts to banks in trouble), it's the middleclass that is affected, the workers, we end up picking up the tab by paying more taxes, we lose our jobs due to lay offs, enough is enough. 
 
My daughter is about to complete four years of college, undergraduate in psychology, and she's having the hardest time finding a job!  I remember when college grads were able to find jobs right away, entry level of course, but it was there.  Then you have people that have extensive experience, have a career, are in the mid 40's and up that can't find a job (I'm in that category)!!!!  I have sent out THOUSANDS of resumes and ONLY 16 interviews, and out of those 16 interviews ZERO offers where before I would find a job within weeks.  I remember back in 2000 when I first moved up here, I worked for a place where one of the principals was horrible, she referred to any race other than her own to "you people", just to give you an idea.  Unfortunately, because it was a very small company it would have been a waste of time to do anything so I just left...after her telling me to clean up some coffee SHE spilled on the floor because "you people" do it better...anyways, I did walk out and 2 weeks later I had another job and was there for five years until they closed down, I was laid off and within 2 months I found another job.  In 2009 I was laid off (office closure) and have since been without a job, unemployment ran out, my savings are gone, my retirement fund is gone, my credit is now shot, BUT...I'm managing/surviving, but it's a very different life, thank God that I never over spent, and that I kept my borrowing on credit limited to a car loan and mortgage otherwise I do not know how I would have survived this ordeal.
 
My point is, GOOD FOR THEM for at least doing something, at least they're catching everyone's attention and that's what counts here.  Hopefully Corporate America will take a good hard look and realize that people are fed up and not going to put up with patching up their mistakes any more.  2008 was horrendous, with black October, then the Madoff scandal; the company I worked for took so many hits because of that, multiple investors withdrew their money because they were almost wiped clean because they also had investments with Madoff, they would call and say "we're so sorry we have to close our fund account but we lost so much money, we need to close our other investments to be liquid", in the end my office had to close because there just wasn't enough money to cover our operating costs. 
 
It's about time people actually do something instead of just complaining, I commend all of them and I hope something positive comes out from all this.
 
It's hard for me to make any donations right now because...I'm really in a bad spot right now BUT I can donate my time, if you need any help cooking, let me know.
