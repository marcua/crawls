Title: Re: Air quality round here
Post by: Harlan on February 09, 2009, 05:19:21 PM
Quote from: Ntrain on February 09, 2009, 01:58:24 PM
I think one of the main air quality issues in Astoria/LIC is the abundance of the power plants in our area. 

I'm not sure if that's true. To the best of my knowledge, those plants are all natural gas-fired, which is much cleaner than even well-scrubbed coal. (Excepting carbon dioxide of course, which doesn't have immediate health effects.) I'd be much more inclined to believe that the heavy truck traffic on the GWB, cross-Bronx, Triborough, and Astoria Blvd, are the causes of high particulate air pollution. Particulates are what cause asthma and such. (Ozone's bad for health too, but NYC doesn't get much of it, unlike say LA.)

Still, I'd arguably rather live here with our heavy truck traffic than in, say, Greenpoint, where ungodly toxins ooze out of the ground... At least the truck pollution will be easing a bit in the next few years as new Federal diesel requirements and local garbage-hauling methods (barges) kick in...!
