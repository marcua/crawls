The one nice thing is that they do have an office where you can call and have your complaints/problems/issues heard regarding their rental units.  I lived in one of his blgs.  Some people I contacted at the office were very nice, others were not so nice.  It helps to be very informed of your rights as a tenant when calling, and written letters help as well.  Most of my issues were resolved, but it took multiple phone calls, and full knowledge of my rights.

That said, the blg I was in was rundown, very old, and not well-updated - one 15 watt of electricity for the entire two bedroom apt without grounded outlets - which meant you blew fuses on a regular basis.  The blg also had roach, mice and all sorts of other bug problems.  The last straw was the bed bug problem, which hastened my departure.   I was probably more vocal than most about the problem, but it did result in getting the entire blg, and all units granted access to, exterminated twice for bed bugs.  I could not beleive that people knew about this problem but most had chosen not to speak up about it, whether out of fear or indifference, I think it was both.   Unfortunately, if your neighbors do not let exterminators in, the pests accumulate and just come thru the walls to every other apartment.

When I moved in, the unit was negotiated lower based on my good credit.  Subsequently, every year the rent was raised to max rent stab laws allowed, and was non-negotiable.  Upon looking for apts with new realtors, I was told of his slumlord reputation, and quite a few said they did not work with him because renting to him reflected badly on their real estate company.   Be aware.

All I can say is good luck.  I have yet to hear of one positive experience with this landlord.

are you in 38th and Ditmars?  The exterminator there came once a month, and the super was really nice about you giving the key so they could exterminate if you weren't home.  Also had the schedule as well.  This website is a great resource for finding out your rights -http://www.housingnyc.com/html/resources/faq/quality.html#exterminate

I'd be very nervous about those bed bugs, be very careful.  They just came thru the walls and invaded everyone's apartments.  The vacuum is your best friend.  If you are getting bit, you may have them.

I remember his leases being very by the book, but don't recall anything about extermination in them.  However, they did send someone the very next day after my many numerous frantic phonecalls about the bed bugs.  I admit, I freaked out when I discovered them, but who in their right mind wouldn't?  I did not pay for any of the three visits to exterminate them.  Call the office, follow up, write letters, educate yourself of your rights.  They did fix problems although it took a long time.

Also did you put down a key deposit?  They are supposed to be illegal but I did get mine back upon moving out.  Also you are owed interest on your security when you move out, but most landlords pocket the money.  I managed to get this as well, through numerous phonecalls.
