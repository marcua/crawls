[This contains a bad story about Cohens, but if you just want a recommendation for a good eye doctor, skip to the bottom]
I had a similar bad experience at Cohen's Fashion Optical on 31st a couple of years ago. Having just moved to NY, I needed to get some more contact lenses; and my prescription was considered expired in NY State. On my way home I saw a big sign in their window for a free eye exam with the purchase of glasses or contacts. I walked right in and signed up. The doctor saw me immediately and the exam felt rushed. He didn't offer samples for the contacts he was prescribing (which is very unusual for toric lenses, as the fit is difficult and they're more expensive than traditional lenses). I asked about samples, and he said he didn't do samples, but that I could return the contacts if they didn't fit.
I went to the counter and this is where the fun began. I was taken aback by the flourish of arithmetic happening in front of me. Anyone who's making calculations of how much you're going to pay, on paper, without making eye contact or seeing that you're with each step, is probably trying to decieve you. After his Good Will Hunting calculus was finished, he tells me that it will come to over 600 dollars; but that that was a discounted price! I was astonished, and asked him he arrived at that number. He told me that I had astigmatism and that the lenses were very expensive. I told him I'd been wearing toric lenses for my astigmatism for years and I knew how much they cost. At the rate he was asking, it would have been over 80 dollars a box. This is where he became very aggresive and
and condescending. I said I could get the same boxes for 40 dollars on the internet. So he said, if you don't buy here you don't get the special. Which is fair, so I offered to pay for the doctor visit and be on my way. He said the doctor was 120, but only if I bought from them. It was 200 dollars! if I wanted to take the prescription elsewhere. He had become more and more aggressive by now; it was as if he was trying to bully me into giving him my money somehow. I told him I was done and wouldn't be coming back. He yelled that I owed them money for the eye exam and that he was calling the police. I yelled back "call the police. I would love to tell them what happened here."

I was so furious, I went home and printed out a page from an internet retailer with the 40 price of a box of contacts he was trying to get me to buy for 80 dollars. Walked back into the store and slammed it on the counter, and announced to the rest of the people in the store that these people were crooks. I was pretty upset.

Anyway. I understand that a local place cannot compete with a large internet store. This was not my problem with this business. I expect to pay a little more than online if I'm buying local. I want to buy local and support these businesses. I can afford it; and would prefer to have real people making money in my neighborhood rather than stores closing up and everyone having to go into "the city" to make money.

It's just unfortunate that some people think that the best way for them to make money is to decieve and bully.

A great thing did come out of this. I found Dr. Schrier on Ditmars, between 35th and 36th. He's since been pushed out because of the Ditmars rent situation, and has moved his practice in with his brother on Broadway and 30th St. If you're looking for an honest eye doctor who will work with you until you get your prescription right; go see Dr. Schrier or his brother; I think it's called Schrier Optical now. I've been going to them ever since.
