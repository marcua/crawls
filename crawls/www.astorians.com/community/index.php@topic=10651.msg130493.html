The zoo suggestions are good.
They might be quite young. Some zoos have great petting zoos/learning sections.

I also like the Queens Hall of Science. And if children can appreciate another 'world', the Met museum.
Both have interesting playgrounds, the Ancient playground, and the hall of science has an unusual playground.

Manhattan also has a children's museum with fun activities, and an outdoor area.

There is also the AMNH for natural history. If they were older, check out the planetarium.

Enjoy.

I too think the Museum of Natural History/Planetarium would be an awesome choice, and I don't think they are too young for it, especially the six year old.  In addition to being a great museum for everyone, they are extremely well set-up to entertain and educate kids -- they have a Discovery Room for kids and families that is like a big, hands-on playroom.

Time Out New York kids' section has a listing of events taking place this week.  In the beginning it's theater-heavy, but there are many pages of other things.

If the weather's good - on Saturday, Kids Fest 2008 is taking place in Madison Square Park:

Quote
Time Out New York Kids teams up with the Madison Square Park Conservancy for this annual outdoor fair chock-full of art activities, face painting, live animals, tasty treats (courtesy of Whole Foods) and more. All ages.

Sunday - how about a puppet show?

Closer to home - Socrates would be a nice option.  The Hall of Science or the zoo would also be great.  If you don't feel like trekking to the Bronx, the Central Park zoo, while small, has enough to keep them entertained for awhile.  And the Queens Zoo is only a stone's throw from the Hall of Science.
