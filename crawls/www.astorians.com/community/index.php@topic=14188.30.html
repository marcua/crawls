Wife and I went there the other day and ordered a standard pizza, to go.  Place was pretty much empty; save for one couple eating.  I always become weary of places where there isn't more than 3 couples eating.

Well, I was there tonight and had a pretty positive experience.  There were four parties there when we got there, and more arrived as the night went on.  The service was very attentive but not smothering.

We ordered a beet salad with mesculin, toasted pine nuts, carrots, orange zest, and a light vinaigrette.  The greens were very fresh and the orange zest was a great addition.  We also had a side of mixed mushrooms sauteed with garlic in olive oil.  Oh, before this, we were given some very tasty bread, sort of in a foccacia style.

We ordered a pizza with prosciutto, gorgonzola, and arugula (there was tomato sauce and fresh mozz).  It was very nice.  Thin crust. The proscuitto was a bit fatty, but the combination of everything was very tasty.

We finished with pecan pie and a cup of coffee gelato.  For the price, the pie was too small a piece.  The gelato was delightful,though, and I'd like to try other flavors.

I took some pictures and where we were sitting, there was perfect light for the shots!  That was cool.

I'd go back.

We've been twice now.

Our first visit was around 9pm on a Thursday evening.  We ordered a pizza margarite DOP and a plate of fetuccini alfredo.  The pizza was good, but as mentioned above, was seriously lacking in the basil department and wasn't very large compared to the price.  If you're going to do a Margarite pizza, you REALLY need to add more basil and we shouldn't have to ask for more (which we did).  The alfredo was ok.  Not great, certainly not worth what they're charging ($17 if I recall).  Drop the price to $10 and you'll get a loyal and frequent customer. 

The 2nd time I was there was just for coffee and this experience was horrid.  Had to wait 15 minutes and the coffee that came out was undrinkable.  Not sure what happened, but the place wasn't full and we were front and center.

There may not be a lot of competition in the direct vicinity, but that's no reason to be charging what they are for the food and service they're providing - this isn't the area for a high-end eatery.  Especially not when they're in the shadow of L'Incontro and no where near the same quality.  If this place is going to survive in this economy they're going to have to up their game.  Better service, better food, better prices.  Otherwise people are going to go elsewhere.  I live less than 2 blocks away and was really looking forward to their opening, but I won't go back unless things change.
