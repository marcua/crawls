Title: “New York on Location” Film Tour
Post by: daisy on May 15, 2007, 12:48:28 PM
A message from: Mayor's Office of Film, Theatre and Broadcasting

“New York on Location” is a day-long outdoor festival that offers a
behind-the-scenes look at filmmaking in New York and will take place on
Sunday, May 20 from 11:00am to 5:00pm, on 36th Street between 34th and
35th Avenues in Astoria, Queens.

 The street, between the Museum of the Moving Image and Kaufman Astoria
Studios, will be closed to traffic as working movie trailers and trucks
are open for public tours, led by the Theatrical Teamsters and other
production professionals who staff these “studios-on-wheels”
everyday. These and other film reps will be available at the festival and in the
Museum to answer questions.

More details here:

http://www.nyc.gov/html/film/html/news/050107_on_location_queens.shtml

As part of the afternoon’s festival, the Mayor’s Office of Film,
Theatre and Broadcasting will present “Careers in Entertainment Production:
Paths to Opportunity,” a career panel at the Museum of the Moving Image,
from 2:00pm – 4:00pm.  Moderated by Commissioner Katherine Oliver, the
discussion features industry insiders who will discuss their experiences working in New York City’s entertainment production industry.

The Museum will be open with free admission all day, showcasing its
interactive “Behind the Screen” filmmaking exhibition.

See event and RSVP details here:

http://www.nyc.gov/html/film/html/news/060107_moftb_movingimage_panel.shtml

internet program.)
