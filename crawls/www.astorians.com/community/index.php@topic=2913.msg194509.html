First of all I want to say thank you to everyone for posting their experiences on here. I had to move to New York (better job) from Michigan within two weeks, while finishing out my two weeks at my current company. Needless to say, I didn't have the time (or money) to come apartment hunt after two flights for my interview. This board, and thread, saved my life.

Based on all the reviews, I decided to set up appointments with Neon Realty, Kim at Halvatzis and Mary at ReMax. Here are the results:

Neon Realty: Called these people first. I could barely understand the person who answered the phone. He asked me a few questions (when I needed the place, price range, etc.), took my number and said he'd call back. I finally received a call from him... yesterday. They were about a month late. Needless to say, this company didn't care.

Mary at ReMax: Oh, Mary. I apologize. I called her, and she asked me a lot of questions (all useful), and set up an appointment. She called to confirm later in the week, and unfortunately I'd already found a place the day before. I had to cancel on her, but she seemed extremely friendly and trustworthy.

Kim at Halvatzis: Needless to say, Kim got me the place. She was great all the way through. Called her up, she asked me some brief questions about price-range, area, pets, etc. and said she had a place in mind. Extremely friendly and had an immediate report with her (someone mentioned it was like working with a close friend.) She was very professional and quick the whole way through. When I met with her, she had 10 places lined up and we hit them all within about an hour and a half. She drove me around, gave me some tips on the area, and had everything ready to go. I think the best thing about working with Kim is that she is HONEST. She told me right off the bat, "I'm going to show you this place first. It's by far the best one I've got and I think you're going to love it. I've got others lined up with different price ranges and sizes so you can compare, but each one has its good and bad." Then she told me the positives and negatives of each place while we were there.

I absolutely love my place. I got a two-bedroom place in an awesome neighborhood and complex, for the price of a one-bedroom, because the landlord told me he trusts Kim, and needed to move the place quickly (I signed the next day, only because the banks weren't open the day I viewed). My super is great and I couldn't be happier. Totally worth the price of one month's rent.

Basically, go with Kim. You won't regret it.

PS. If you're in a situation like I was, Craigslist is useless. Don't even bother. Those places are gone the same day they're posted.

PPS. Don't worry about the cash deposit thing. Just make sure they give you a receipt.
