The 3 main methods of wire transfers in the US are Fedwire, Chips, and Swift. While it's mostly likely that a payment from Citibank NY to Bank of America in SF will go by Fedwire, if the banks maintain correspondent accounts with each other (many do), they will you Swift, since it's much cheaper and even easier.

Transfer payments can be complicated, since they can have several intermediate parties between the sender and receiver. You can send a transfer from a US bank to a US bank, but the beneficiary bank can be a foreign bank maintaining an account with that US bank. There no way to exclude those payments, and anyone with transfer experience worth their weight in salt knows that.

Another aspect of privacy is how we chose to define or not define ourselves. Legally, I can refer to myself as anything I want, whether it be Dr.Zeno, Mickey Mouse or Jesus Christ. It is only where there is a conflict of interest between my privacy and some other principle (such as credibility) that we run into problems. Before I expose the incidents that have led me to start posting on the subject of privacy, I will skirt around the issue for just a small amount of time more in order to give the offending party time to save face and rectify the mistake that he or she has made in regard to my privacy. The main issue indeed revolves around the use of my name in a public space expressly against my wishes. A note to the person in question: By exhibiting such behavior I will take it to mean that I also have license to use your given name in a public forum in the name of "professionalism" as well, the Law must apply equally to all after all, right?

Incident #1: I was approached by a reporter for Newsday at the weekly CSA meeting and was asked to be interviewed. Much like last year I said sure, but would I have to give my name. Question: Is privacy to be valued even cherished? Or are we better off without it?
