Title: Re: bluestone slate sidewalks......
Post by: ImWilliam on May 20, 2009, 11:30:48 PM
 I build gardens for a living and do  stonework, its how I make my living. What you have is either true blue, or blue/grey/green New england blue stone. New stone is about 6 to 8 dollars a sq ft ( randome rectangle 3in). The value of  your  stone  would be offset by the cost of moving it and getting rid of it. garbage is about 90 a yard.    The other guys option of resetting may apply. It depends on what your site conditions are. ie are their huge tree roots, are their drainage issues,  etc.. The problem with that though is also labor cost. If those stones are 5' sq. then it will take 3 people to set them. Its the size and weight.  If you get get 3 guys for under 100 an hour  you'll be doing well.  Figure it will take about 20 to 30 minutes per stone with no cutting.  First, pick up all the stone, then excavate underneath, then 3/4 crushed drain rock, then a 2 inch base of stone dust, then tamp it and re set the stone. A good guestimate  would be in the 325 to 500 range for redoing the stone.  Thats just a rough gauge , but  it will give you something to jump off of.  Some people dont add a drain base, or use a tamper so it may be less.. depends on the process, but thats how Id do it.

 Good luck
