September Concert seeking musicians and spaces to play!
« on: July 24, 2007, 05:25:17 PM »
Hello everyone!

As some of you may know, for the past 6 years, I've been the Astoria organizer for a world-wide event called The September Concert.

The goal of the September Concert is to bring people together each September 11 with a message of peace through the healing power of music.  Events take place all over the city, as well as in Japan, Ghana, Italy, Indonesia, the UK and many cities around the U.S.  More information including photos and video from past events can be found here:  http://www.septemberconcert.org.

Since 2002, I've placed volunteer musicians at about 10 small businesses in the neighborhood, mostly along Ditmars and Steinway, though I'd love to see more of the neighborhood involved.  Once again, I'm looking for volunteer musicians, and would also like to extend an invitation to any local business owners on this list who'd like to participate. 

Although in the past I've placed musicians at venues, this year, I'd like to streamline the process by inviting people to arrange their own events as well.  Are you a singer-songwriter who frequents a neighoborhood restaurant?  Why not ask them if they'd be willing to let you play?  Are you a cafe owner who knows that some of your regular customers are musicians?  Ask them if they'll stop by to share some songs.  The only requirement is that all events must be free and open to the public.  This is an all-volunteer effort.  It's really about the spirit of community.  If you plan an event, please email me at septemberastoria@allisontartalia.com, so that I can be sure your event is included on the September Concert website and in any printed materials (last year all of the events were listed in the Daily News).

Venues do not have to be traditional music venues.  Past venues have included cafes, restaurants, a take-out noodle shop, bagel shops, and a haircutting place, where the owner each year plays outside between hair appointments!  It's really open for all kinds of involvement, whether you play classical, jazz, bluegrass, pop or are a singer-songwriter; whether you make music for a living, or as a hobby.  Since many of these untraditional "venues" don't have a PA system, anything unplugged is especially welcome!!!!

Feel free to email me either here or at septemberastoria@allisontartalia.com for more information or if you'd like to be a performer or venue.

Thanks so much!
Allison
