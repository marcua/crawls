Last night we had calf's liver which, although of course beef, has a gentler flavour and texture than beef liver.

We roasted the piece whole (about 2.5 lbs) until it was medium-medium rare in the center.  For the thinner part, that was 20 minutes at 500, and an additional five minutes for the thicker end.  The texture when it's not overcooked is tender, and not grainy.  The taste was not overwhelming, either - definitely liver, yes, but in a good way.  Immediately before roasting, we rubbed it gently with a bit of olive oil, and gave it a good dusting of salt.

We were also lucky to get an extremely fresh piece.  It was really prime.

Well, I salted it immediately before roasting simply for flavour.

I don't have a great deal of experience with calf's liver, so I can't comment on salt's effect on purported metallic or other off flavours.  The liver we used was extremely fresh; I suspect that off flavours may have more to do with the freshness of the liver, but I don't really know.  Liver is rich in iron, and unless you kasher it (i.e. broil it) to remove the blood, also rich in blood.  Because of those, it will naturally have some "metallic" flavour, IMHO.

BTW, we used sea salt.
