I am a Manhattan ex-pat and could be considered a pod person, sleeping in Astoria and working and socializing in Manhattan.   Anything on the Ditmars side of the Grand Central/Triboro Bridge might as well be Siberia.   After losing my illegal sublet in the West Village way back in '87 I moved to Astoria and have been really happy here, mostly because of the short hop into Manhattan.  

I am very surprised people are so down on the Broadway area, though.   I lived on 32nd Street between Broadway & 34th Ave. between 1987 and 2001 and was very happy there.   I never felt unsafe and unlike the 30th Avenue area where I now hang my hat, I liked the option of taking the N or the R.   The shopping was great on Broadway.   The block I lived on was mostly three-story owner-occupied homes.   The block was family-oriented and if anything the kids playing in the street were too noisy.  (I don't know why they didn't build more parks in Astoria back when it was developing so the kids would have a place to go afterschool, summers, etc.)

In 20 plus years in Astoria I can't remember ever hearing any racist language, though I haven't really had many conversations with neighbors.   I do know discrimination does exist when renting, particulary when you move into an owner-occupied dwelling.   I remember back in '87 the broker telling me that the landlords like Japanese tenants because they were "clean" and "quiet."   And there is preference for women versus men.   I can distinctly remember the broker calling a landlord and wanting to send me over, but the landlord was adamant that they wanted a female tenant and I wasn't sent over.  

In more recent years I applied for a part-time job at a liquor store and the owner/manager looked at me and said "we are looking for a woman."   I said to myself "is that legal."   I guess he didn't have an HR department to advise him on the legal niceties.
