Ok, after basically stating my thoughts and disappearing due to 1. busyness 2. perhaps some irresponsibility in following my post 3. trying to move on.  I didn't want to get all worked up for days about this experience, nor did I want to beat a dead horse.  I stated what happened. It was MY side of the story.  What I posted was the best truth I know because it was my experience and I mentioned nothing of the food. In fact, I enjoyed my wrap and awesome waffle fries until I was asked to move with fork in mouth. 

For the record, I too have worked in the food industry as both a hostess and a server.  I know when it's appropriate to seat a single person at a small 2 topper vs. a larger table based on time of day, busyness etc.  Should I have assumed that I was required to to sit at a small table, PERHAPS.  But it was not crowded the entire back was empty, and a few tables in front also.  I asked as a courtesy not becaus I had to get my way! I also asked several times because the "hostess" didn't speak very good English, or so it appeared that she didn't quite understand my request.  I also didn't walk in with laptop in hand, nor did I originally plan to use my laptop. It was impromptu.  I just wanted some good food, a little relaxation and time to breathe after a "blek" day.

My point and point ONLY. As a costomer I thought it rude that I be asked to move after service commenced, food delivered, food 1/2 eaten and great comments on my beautiful laptop from the waiter.   The waiter had the chance to tell me that laptop use was not allowed. He had the chance before I ordered to tell me that I needed to move to a smaller table.  The owner was not present at this time. 

What it boils down to. There clearly is not a strong protocol as to how Igloo is run.  Mother Igloo runs the joint, as her son also did not tell me to move and he was there the whole time.  Now everyone......

let's move on. It was merely my account of my own experience.  Did I carry on with the owner, did I get upset with her? Of course I did. I wanted service and I didn't get it!
