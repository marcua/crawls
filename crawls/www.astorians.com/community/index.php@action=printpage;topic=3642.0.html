Title: Paid Detail Program
Post by: mcdirk on October 02, 2006, 12:29:46 PM
We've had a lot of discussion on the boards about noise and problems from clubs at night.  One solution that has been bantied about has been a "paid detail program" which has been tried in other cities, but never in NYC.  In this program, NYPD police would be able to work, in uniform, in front of clubs, bars and restaurants, keeping people behaving orderly.  Current law prohibits establishments with a liquor license (except for the stadiums and Madison Square Garden, for whatever reason) from hiring cops as security.  I understand the problem with cops working directly for bars, but personally think it's worth trying this program in NYC.  I'm wondering what other people think.  With all the problems we have been having on Broadway at night lately, I'd feel more secure with more police out, especially if they were assigned by either the precinct or the Merchants Association, or even a local Business Improvement District, to keeping the peace and making people behave.  I know I'd feel less at risk walking by Nu Bar at night and that the rowdies leaving Club DNA might be inclined to be less loud.
