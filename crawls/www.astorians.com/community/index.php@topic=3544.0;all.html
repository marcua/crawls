So after reading the "Tacos" posts, I started to think about dinner, and my thoughts became more refined as I saw a passing reference to Papa's Empanadas and Mundo. 

I am new to the world of empanadas (having also only recently disovered arepas), and I would love to try some in the 'hood.  The question is like this: my wife and I have certain dietary restrictions such that we don't eat meat or chicken out - we do, however, eat fish, veggies, and dairy.  Do any of the empanadas places have enough of a variety of things we can eat (e.g., rice and beans, veggie, cheese) to make it worth our while?  If so, which one?

I don't want to get into the whole question of whether lard is used in the dough or beans or whatever- that's in the "don't ask, don't tell" category of knowledge.
