Title: Lost Dog New Music Ensemble at Waltz Astoria FREE! - Thursday Apr 29
Post by: AlexJL2009 on April 25, 2010, 10:45:47 PM
Lost Dog’s last concert of the season, From the Mountains to the Sea: ELEMENTAL AMERICA, is next week! We will be performing five pieces by American composers, one for each of the elements.
 
John Luther Adams’ Among Red Mountains for piano is an over-the-top-glorious study on the majesty of Alaska’s terrain. It is a KNOCKOUT way to start a show!
 
David Lang’s quintet Sweet Air describes his young son’s first experience with nitrous oxide (at the dentist J).
 
Jennifer Higdon’s Rapid Fire is a furious, raging virtuoso solo flute piece exploring an all-too-American element – gunfire. Jennifer just won the Pulitzer prize, and this is one of her signature pieces, so it’s a great opportunity to hear some of her music.
 
George Crumb’s mysterious and luminous trio Vox Balenae (Voice of the Whale) is one of the great classics of American music, with powerful theatrical elements – the work is performed by players wearing masks, under a blue light representing the sea.
 
The concert will also feature the world premiere of emerging composer Shawn Crouch’s Quintessence, a fantastic new work written just for this concert, about the alchemical ‘fifth element’ – life itself.

We will be performing the program twice – on Thursday 4/29 at 8:00 in Astoria at the Waltz Cafe (just 15 minutes from Manhattan on the N and W trains), and on Saturday 5/1 at 8:30 in Manhattan at Kaufman Recital Hall, above Merkin Hall on W. 67th St (map links below).
 
The 4/29 show at Waltz is FREE! For the show at Kaufman on 5/1, tickets are $20 (or $15 for students and seniors) and may be purchased online at www.astoriamusic.org, or at the door.

The Astoria Music Society is a local non-profit group.  Please support us by coming out to a show.
