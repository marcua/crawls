BIG IJI FAN HERE!  Only been in Astoria for about six months (lived in the West & East Villages and Brooklyn before that-first time poster!) but have been consistently and pleasantly surprised by the high quality of dining options in Astoria.  Right now Iji is at the top of my list!  My boyfriend and I used to be big fans of the very wonderful & sweet guys who work over at Sushi Q.  Very basic, but very solid take-out sushi (with delicious green-tea and red-bean ice cream that they'll scoop for you for take-out!)  Iji, however, is on a whole different level.  We've gotten delivery once and take-out twice and have been astounded each time at the variety and quality of the items that we've eaten.  My boyfriend is a HUGE sushi fan and raves about Iji. 

All of our Iji meals have included a standard/basic roll like a california or spicy tuna that has been super fresh.  This week we tried the rainbow roll which had beautiful, thick cuts of fresh fish on top.  The rainbow roll was HUGE, in a good way!  Each time we have also tried one of the special rolls which have been adventurous and delicious.  Inventive combinations with taste and texture that stands-out every time.  So far we've tried and loved the spicy girl, yama, and what-n-roll.  Again, these rolls have been BIG and well worth the additional price.  As far as appetizers go the shumai has been a stand-out, though I thought their gyoza were very well done too (my boyfriend prefers a bigger, Chinese take-out type dumpling with much thicker dough.  But to me these gyoza are what a dumpling should be.)  Also had a chance to try the chicken teriyaki which I really liked.  Very basic and simple, but very delicious perfectly cooked chicken breast with awesome mashed potatoes and brussel sprouts.  These aren't tastes or foods I usually associate with Japanese food, but I'm not complaining as they were great!

Overall, Iji has not disappointed and while their menu'ing of our building has been a little overdone who can blame them as a new restaurant in this economy?  Plus it seems like the menu has changed a number of times and expanded so they're probably just trying to get the most recent one out there.  Mostly I don't mind because Iji freakin' ROCKS!!!

my bf and i just ordered from iji last night, and both were very happy with our meals--he's a big eater, so he got a sashimi combo deal, and a couple of sides, and thought the fish was very fresh, loved the seaweed salad, enjoyed his rolls...his only "complaint' was that the miso was only okay.  as for me, i had the vegetarian combo--14 pieces, nice selection, definitely well-thought out (unlike lots of places that thrown a couple of rolls together almost as an afterthought for us veggie lovers), and the sweet potato tempura rolls, which i was intrigued by, but ultimately not crazy about (but that could just be me and my like, not love of deeply fried things).  for takeout, the presentation was lovely, and they threw in two sides of sliced cantaloupe for free, which was a nice touch.  after ordering, my bf remarked on how pleasant and capable the phone person was, giving him the total of the meal, and the time it would arrive (25-30 minutes, and it arrived on the 29 minute mark!  how's that for service?) without prompting.  overall, a good experience.  i haven't been to linn, but have eaten in at osaka, and believe solely in terms of food quality and price, we thought the two restaurants were pretty comparable.
