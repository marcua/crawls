I would kill (metaphorically speaking, of course) for a TJs in Astoria.  I can't believe I haven't made it to the US one yet - I really have to do that soon.

That said, I went to a TJs near Bethpage State Park (I guess it's the Plainview one) on Saturday.  Except for the one I went to in Seattle, it's the biggest and best stocked one I've ever been in - much better than the ones in Commack or Hewlett.

I am very sorry to hear of the demise of their sake wasabi mustard.  We've got about 2 tablespoons left, and I must find a dish worthy to receive it.

I love their frozen edamame, and their frozen green beans.  The green beans are amazing, especially in the dead of winter.  Their light coconut milk is only 99 cents a can, and I just saw that they've started packaging the Tasty Bite Indian quick meals under their own label.  I like to keep a couple of these in my desk drawer at work in case I don't have time to eat anything else.
