Title: Re: Summer camps for elementary school kids?
Post by: lisadiane on January 03, 2013, 09:05:24 PM
This is not in Astoria, but for the summer we were very happy with Superkickers in Jackson Heights.    Our son was just under 3 when he went there, but there were older kids too - just not sure what the age limit was.   The camp is mostly indoors at PS 69 in Jackson Heights, but they have sprinklers and trampoline activities outside.   Other activities are things like yoga, dance, music/piano, soccer, drama and arts and crafts.   A great thing about it was that you could choose from week to week how many days you wanted to register for (online registration)- we sent our son 2 or 3 times a week, but skipped a few weeks when we went on vacation.  You can also choose the hours - you can have 9-11, 9-1, 9-5 , etc. And they have early dropoff at 8am and late pickup for an extra charge.   Very reasonable prices.   The only hesitation I had was that it was a commute to get there on the subway from Astoria, but we liked the camp and the owners and counselors so much that we felt it was worth the commute.   The link is www.superkickers.com - I noticed that their summer camp link isn't live yet, but you can check out the activities they have which are similar to their camp offering.
