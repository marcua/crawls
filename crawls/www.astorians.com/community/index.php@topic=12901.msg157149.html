Quote from: Billz1981 on November 04, 2008, 05:29:54 PM
Fair enough.  I also think we should investigate having a literacy test and a basic civics test at the poll, but that's a whole other story.

The problem with literacy tests, or any other method to demonstrate the competency of the voter, is that they are reminiscent of a time and a place that many Americans would like to forget, especially given Obama's historic run for the president.  Before the Voting Rights Act of 1965, Southern states used "disenfranchising" laws to make it difficult, if not impossible, for blacks, mostly poor sharecroppers to vote.  http://www.usdoj.gov/crt/voting/intro/intro_a.php 

While times have changed and I agree that voters should be more educated, making voters pass a "test" in order to vote is, in my opinion, unconstitutional.

By the way, I didn't negative nod you, Billz.  I actually enjoy reading your posts and respect your opinion and your manner of presenting your argument.
