I thought the meeting was great and would like to see more of them.  It was very organized and professional and Meg and Costa did a great job moderating.  I think the decision to have it moderated was key to having a more thoughtful discussion, and perhaps after a few times we can train our elected officials that at these forums they don't need to filibuster (talking as much as possible as a response to the filibustering you often get from the audience).  For people like myself who are already a part of an active community group things like this can be very informative.  Having elected officials and their reps come out on a regular basis to hear concerns from the community is how things get done.  It might not get done as fast as one would like but thats how it is and unless you're willing to run for office the best you can do is join a community group and harry our elected leaders and members of various departments to move faster on issues that matter to you.

Nothing from my group, TAWQ (Transportation Alternatives for Western Queens) was on the agenda per se, but I didn't attend the planning meeting so I wasn't expecting it to be.  Nonetheless I was surprised to hear George Delis mention he had raised complaints about the way 114th Precinct parks along Astoria Blvd (anyone who lives there like myself knows they block crosswalks and park past the curb on corners with their personal cars which is dangerous for peds and drivers alike on an already dangerous road) and had gotten grief from the 114th for doing so.  Of course when I tried to talk to him about it on his way out he wasn't crazy helpful, but its good to know that if TAWQ decided to tackle that issue head on (see uncivil servants to see what I mean) at the very least I can quote our CB 1 leader on the record as sympathetic.

So I think we should definitely have more of these, perhaps on a variety of issues but with the moderated format, and would like to be more involved than I was with this one.  Also since everyone is so community minded right now, all are welcome to our next TAWQ meeting at the Astoria Historical Society, Quinn Bldg 4th Fl, Monday Dec 3rd 6:30p-8p where we will be talking about Tour de Queens, the greenway, getting bike racks back on the QBX1, promoting congestion pricing and other cycling, pedestrian and mass transit issues.  Feel free to PM for more info.
