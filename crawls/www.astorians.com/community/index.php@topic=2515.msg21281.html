my wife and i own a small business which started out as freelancing. because of this direct deposit is next to impossible and thereby the 'free' checking that most banks offer doesn't apply. we started with citibank because we wanted the convenience of atm's all over town. but since our income is so variable we often had to pay a $7.50 service charge per month for our accounts.

before any of the other new 'free' banks showed up we decided to give greenpoint (now northfork) a chance. it was quick and easy to set up a checking and savings account and they truly have no minimum balance requirements. i'll admit that their service is rough around the edges and their check clearing policy is slow and baffling. but it seemed our best alternative given a universe of bad ones.

last year we decided to incorporate our company and thought that it was finally time for a change. with our business license in hand we walked into commerce and waited a good while for a csr. when we finally got one she looked at us like we were absolutely crazy and had to find a manual to figure out what was required to create an account. sure enough we were missing something but they couldn't pinpoint exactly what that item was. we left vowing never to go back.

a trip to northfork and we had a business checking account.

that said, no bank is perfect (or even close). i find it is a matter of searching for the least evils that pertain to your specific situation. and banks do reward loyalty so if you find a good one, stay with it.
