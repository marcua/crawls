I know I am a bit late for this one, but if for the future, I find walking around the industrial areas armed with an iced coffee and a pack of smokes to be very nice.  They are often deserted right in the middle of the day on the weekends.  Quiet enough that the only thing you can hear is the wind in yoiur ears.  They have interesting architecture, with great old buildings they would never build nowadays.  They are also often located near the river, so a waterfront view is never far away and finally they are often have some pretty hip hoods nestled up next to them, so you can get good refreshments after spelunking.

My favorite is the industrial neighborhood in Red Hook.  Take care not to wander directly into the middle of the housing projects (an easy thing to avoid, but they are pretty nasty).  Get off at the Smith & 9th Street stop on the F/G (which on its own is something any New Yorker ought to see) and then work your way south and then to the west along the water.  There are neat parks and piers and warehouses that smell of cinnamon.  In the heat of the summer that peculiar smell of brackish water washes over the area, which some find revolting but I find comforting.  When you are all done, work your way back up Van Brunt to Carroll and make a right and go over to Court Street.  That will put you right in Carroll Gardens for a great lunch!

p.s.- I haven't done this since the IKEA has been put into Red Hook and I am not sure how the hood has been affected by it.
