I think it's important to point out that when I suspend someone it's seldom for just one particular remark. Most of the time as I'm reading a thread I see the discussion unfold and many times a person crosses the line several times (singling someone out, calling them a name, etc). I'll reference the final post that caused me to take action to suspend, but most of the time it's because I see a pattern of someone stepping over the lines more than once. Or sometimes it's through several threads, or even over several days with one user picking at another user. There are also cases where I'll get abusive PM's reported to me by someone, and later see a member who is baiting the other member in public. As you might imagine, I have little patience with these kinds of members (not saying that is or isn't the case this time.)

Someone noting that the thread is going off topic or that the thread is being hijacked in terms of its topic isn't the same as singling someone out as a "hijacker" of threads for example. Even if someone is singled out I may not suspend the person if it's not part of an ongoing pattern.

There are users on the board that thoughtfully criticize each others ideas while being respectful. Then there are users that just criticize and name call out of disrespect without forming any broader argument. In a gray area if a criticism is thoughtful and respectful I tend to not suspend, but might say something to someone in a PM (as I did yesterday for a couple members who did not get suspended). In cases where I see disrespect, or where comments are baiting or inflammatory it's different.
