Gay 'Marriage'
« on: May 19, 2008, 01:22:10 AM »
OK, the three things we are told  one should never talk about in 'mixed' company is  sex, politics and religion. This one has all 3. :lol:  Seriously, the debate has reignited  on the matter of whether the state  should sanction Gay 'marriage.'  There is  a myriad  of  reasons  why people are for and against it. First, there is  virtually no religion on earth, starting with the major religions, Christianity, Judaism, Islam, which sanctions  same sex  marriage. "Penalties" range from Christians and Jews who don't recognise them, and at one  time, 'stoned'  and/or  ran Gay folks out of town, to Islam, which still practices  the old Christian/ Jewish penalties, where just a few weeks ago, Gay and Lesbian folks were executed and routinely are lucky to escape to the West with their lives. In Cuba, which was under Castro, for years, officially an 'Atheist/Communist' state, allowed religion, and under  Castro brother , Raul, just this past weekend, allowed  a Gay 'rights'  meeting, unheard  of during Fidel's days, since  'revolution' was not 'compatible' with being 'gay', in their view.

In our  Western society, and this is held up as  one reason the 'Jihadists' are able to recruit terrorists, much more  than any economic/military cooperation, they view  our 'loose' morals, from out of wedlock sex, and certainly Gay 'rights' or 'marriage' as to our 'decadence' and reason we must be destroyed. Of course, the latter  is  completely unacceptable and one reason we will be at war for a while with these nuts. Still, internally, reasonable  people  can agree with the need of secular  society to have some sort of recognition, or 'parity', for folks who are consenting adults, to be together. I think where the problem lies, is the use of term 'marriage', which is steeped in socio-religious views throughout the world and invokes  heated passion of the destruction of  family structure.

My view  is  that  some form of 'Civil Union' for  Gay,Straight, Bi- or what have  you folks should exist. I wouldn't call it 'marriage', but, would afford ALL rights and 'privileges' that is afforded, by law, to marriage. It would settle the problem, of the state  meddling with religious faith, and give  the  appropriate right to folks  who want to be together.

A buddy of mine, decidedly far more  'liberal' than me, which isn't too tuff :lol:, on matters of war and peace, is surprisingly against it, and finds  the thought 'disgusting.'  :|  Well, whatever I find 'disgusting' that consenting adults do in their lives is  irrelevant and really no ones business, except those folks. He said  I'm getting 'soft' and am not a 'Conservative' and he thought we would agree on this  thing? So, to him I am too 'liberal' on this  one. Go figure.  :-)  In any event, there is  enough time and energy wasted  on hating each other, and I contribute  to it, I admit it. :lol:  So, why should love, maybe, not my 'kind' of 'love', be denied to folks. This world is bad enough, why add to it?
