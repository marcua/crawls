 A Security Deposit is collected for use if something is damaged, not for rent.  If you have any kind of signed lease agreement it will clearly spell out what your and the landlords responsibility are in reference to the security deposit.  There are also laws on the books that spell out what the landlord and tenants responsibilities are in reference to notice to vacate and security deposit are even it there is no signed agreement.
If you do not pay the rent, the landlord could file with the courts an eviction proceeding for non-payment. Then he can have (for a very small fee) it reported to the different credit agencies. In the future try getting a decent apartment with a reputable landlord with that on you credit score and record.  You will also pay a premium (higher interest rate) when you try to land a loan to buy something.
As far as damage, if it exceeds any money owed (or the amount of the security deposit) the landlord could take you to court for the damages. Not good to have that type of judgment on your  record.
Best advice is to pay the rent as you are required to, clean the apartment out before you move, repair any damage or excessive wear you have cased, and document it with photos. Also ask for a pre-walk thru a few days prior to you last day to confirm things are in order (also gives you a chance to address any problems).  The day you turn your keys in ask for a final walk thru.  Get something in writing stating that everything checks out or listing only the things that are questionable.  This will protect you from false claims of damage.
Even if you have had a miserable experience with the landlord now is not the time to take revenge (like not paying rent, leaving the apartment a mess, or damaging something). Some people think this is OK but If the landlord exercises his rights thru the courts it will only hurt you in the long run.

 Security Deposits are to be paid back to us with interest less a yearly fee for managing the account ( I think it’s like 25.00 per year). With interest rates so low (way less than 1%) chance are the management fee will be larger than interest earned. A smart landlord deposits a security check in an interest bearing saving account (without co-mingling funds) and when a tenant moves he simply returns the security deposit with interest less management fees and damage (if any). Giving the tenant a copy of the bank statements eliminates any questions about how much was earned in interest.
Just as we are on paying our rent on time, a landlord is also bound by specific laws that dictate how long he has before he must give you your security deposit back or an accounting of items needing repair and return of the left over portion. If he does not return you security deposit (or an accounting) in the time allotted you can take him to court and he could face stiff penalties.
I  would never skip paying the rent unless you have it writing from the landlord that he will accept your security deposit for the last months rent. Verbal agreements only come back to bite people in the axx.

 Negative items (bankruptcy, non-payment, late payments, collections, etc.) stay on your credit report for 7 years.  You can send a letter to the three major credit reporting agencies asking for a correction but all they will do is investigate if they  made an error by including something that was not true.   If a court has found you at fault for non-payment then they will not change your report. They are not in the business to determine who is telling the truth or not, just report what is on the record.
Your only recourse is to go back to court and prove your case that the original decision was wrong and ask for a reversal.  I not a lawyer so I don’t how this would be accomplished or if it’s even possible. 
When you were first taken to court did you respond to the charges?  Did you go to court to defend yourself?  If you did not then a judgment was handed down because you ignored the court. This is the only way I could see the landlord prevailing against a tenant who says they have proof of payment for all the months of rent.  Insulting a landlord would not lead to a judgment of not paying rent. Your landlord would have had to make the accusation that you did not pay rent and you should have been there and had the opportunity to defend yourself. If you were not there then the court would make its decision based on his evidence only and you got screwed.

Rene:

If you didn't answer when the landlord sued you judgment will be entered (called a default judgment because you failed to appear). You need to go to the court where the judgment was entered and file an order to show cause to vacate the default (the pro-se or 'self-represented' clerk there will help you write it).  You need to explain to the judge why you failed to answer and show that you paid all rent. If you satisfy the judge the judgment will be vacated and you can send that order to the credit agencies to have the record removed. Alternatively, you can have your landlord sign a "stipulation to vacate default judgment" (google it) if he is willing, which you can then take to the court to obtain an order. I would not leave this on my credit record if I were you - it is a huge factor in tanking your score.

Good luck.
