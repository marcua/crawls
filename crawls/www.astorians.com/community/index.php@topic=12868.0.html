We still have a good number of weeks in 2008, but I ask what are the outstanding films and suggestions for award nominations.


Recently saw
CHANGELING.

I am a big fan of Eastwood. I knew very little about this film but was curious about the cast and period.
For me, it was a very interesting surprise.

It is an excellent production. Not one of of Eastwood's very best, but still very good.
However, my taste is not so much for such drama and heaviness. And the film/story is a long one to tell (almost 2.5 hours). 
Aspects of the story are certainly tragic.
It involves a mother in 30s Los Angeles, CA who has a missing son. And corruption of authorities who should have helped more. (That was what I understood going in.)

The film is in muted color.
It is based on true history, otherwise I could not have believed such terrible events.


I hope nominations would include as well the script. From a writer who worked on Babylon AD?
Thumbs up, for those with even a casual interest.

A couple more movie considerations.

Surprised to have enjoyed a DVD documentary from a few years back - Wordplay.
As the director notes, you probably would not expect much at all of a film project about crossword puzzles, a puzzle tournament, and the people involved.
I loved how the movie comes together, great soundtrack, very nicely done graphics (whowouldathunk). And it probably helps that the greater NY area is featured.
It includes a brief interview of Mussina from the Yankees. Also, a funnier interview makes it hard for the cameraman to keep the camera from shaking.

***
Saw Doubt. It has very fine acting performances. A very good script. But I dont know if the film will stick in my memory very long.
The writer directed the film and I liked how it appeared to me like a wonderful photo album. Around a NY church school.
