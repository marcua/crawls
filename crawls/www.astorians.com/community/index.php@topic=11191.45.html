Quote from: oliver klosoff on July 07, 2008, 05:55:49 PM
1- all-star had a very tasty 100% angus beef burger, better than anyone elses in the immediate area, maybe except McAnn's, but thats a pub.  Mikes is decent, Zorba's is greasy, Soho once is a while you get a bad one (i got sick last week), Igloo i'm not too crazy about, what else is near there?

Whether or not you liked their burgers is a matter of personal taste and I'm not judging it.  In fact I never even went to All-Star Burger.  But even before McDonald's reopened, the place never really seemed busy.  And most of the reviews I read for it were not very positive.  McDonald's *may* have been the final nail in the coffin for it, but it wasn't doing all that well to begin with...the other establishments you mentioned all survived McDonald's return and seem to be doing well.  Let's not forget that, prior to All-Star Burger, Planet Wings closed down, and prior to that, BK closed down, all in the same space.

Quote
2- 6 chain pizzerias were listed for ALL of the vast astoria sprawl.  i don't have enough fingers to count how many pizzerias are in my immediate area that are within walking distance..  chains are not squeezing them out..

Six is not really a small number, it's probably comparable to other neighborhoods in Queens.  Certainly there are many, many more independent pizzerias than chain pizzerias, but I don't think anyone was arguing that the chains are squeezing the indies out.  The original question was, I believe, why anyone would choose a chain pizzeria over a local, independently owned one, but the answer comes down once again to personal taste.

Quote
3- Soho opened, KFC got less business and closed.  "not economically viable" means there was no business, all the while soho thrived.  as the neighborhood slowly gentrified, Igloo's business grew and BK's lessened.  the locals decided the local business stays and the chain leaves.  McD's is McD's, you will never see one go out of business as long as kids like Happy Meals, but i wouldnt feed a kid that crap.  All-star closed down about a month after McD's re-opened.  maybe it was a bad omen that all-star had BK's old location..

You're right, but here I think it's important to point out that it's not just in the Ditmars area where chains have failed in Astoria.  The KFC/Taco Bell which was on Steinway Street for many years, closed a couple of years ago.  The indie fusion Japanese place which followed it closed within a few months.  Burger King, in the past two decades, has opened and closed on Steinway twice.  Other marginal chains have come and gone over the years, like Blimpie, Le Croissant Shop (I think most of them closed but there are a few scattered about in Manhattan), Roy Rogers (same story as Le Croissant), etc.  There's also many chains which Astoria has never seen but which exist elsewhere in Queens, like Arby's and IHOP, and other chains which don't seem to have made inroads anywhere in the five boroughs, like Denny's or Sonic or Dairy Queen. 

My point is that chains have traditionally not done too well in Astoria with the exception of McDonald's, which seems to be solid in all of its locations, and the Wendy's on Steinway Street.  One can probably attribute that to the abundance of local establishments but I'm not sure it's fair to say that any one single local establishment has pushed out an entire chain....e.g. Igloo and KFC.  I don't think KFC on Ditmars left because Igloo appeared specifically, but because overall there are other options and people took their business there.
