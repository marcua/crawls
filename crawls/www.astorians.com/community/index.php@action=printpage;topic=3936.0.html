Title: Re: Submitting articles to magazines?
Post by: belle on November 13, 2006, 05:12:37 PM
It's a lot of work and you'll get a lot of rejections-- no matter how good you are.  That doesn't mean you shouldn't try.

I've been pitching myself around lately and have had some luck, a lot of rejections, and a few stolen ideas.  I wanted to get into food writing so I started a food blog (just braise) just over a year ago to loosen me up and get readers responses.  A great way to start and now PR companies are contacting me to write up their restaurants (in exchange for a meal).  This is great practice and can potentially be sold (I also post on Associated Content).

I then read the book "Will Write for Food."  This book was really helpful in structuring a pitch and providing ideas.  From there I pitched the magazine world.

The trick is to be creative and think outside the box.  Just because I want to write about food doesn't mean I should stick to food magazines.  Nor do you have to stick to your geography (I just got something accepted for Time Out Chicago).  But know that you'll get a lot of rejections, as well as some push (a food mag I contacted really liked a recent pitch of mine but told me they couldn't fit it into an upcoming mag and to pitch more in the future). 

I will say this: most freelancers find it close to impossible to live off writing alone.  Many have part-time jobs.  But it's a fun hobby...

Good luck.

Title: Re: Submitting articles to magazines?
Post by: hillary10 on November 22, 2006, 12:01:43 PM
I used to be a freelance magazine writer. It is a tough business to get into. I would figure out the right magazine for your article, look at the masthead and pick an editor (sr or assoc). Send him or her a letter with your piece. You might include a second idea in your letter. If you have any published articles include them as well. Tell the editor why your piece is good for the magazine. Call them a week later. It's a crapshoot but it may work out. I think I accepted a few articles, or at least got a few writers into my rotation, through that method (I was also an editor).

Finding a connection to an editor is also helpful. Your teacher may have suggestions.

best of luck!
