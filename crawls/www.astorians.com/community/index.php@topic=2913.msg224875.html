We haven't sold anything with a local realtor, but here are my experiences: I've had Bianca Collasuono show us a few things. She seems very honest and down-to-earth and showed us the types of places we wanted to see. I've also heard very good things about her from many other people. We didn't buy from her, but it was more a case of wrong place/wrong time.
I'd stay away from any RE places near the train-they are all shysters. I went through them all pretty quickly and was completely turned off with many after just a quick phone conversation because they were trying to TELL ME what I WANTED. Since they didn't seem to GET IT, they got crossed off my list quick.
Years ago we were looking into purchasing a rental property. I can only remember that the agent's name was Tiffany and she was pushing my husband really hard when I wasn't there to buy, buy, buy. She even met with him for coffee to encourage him on the "deal of a lifetime". She even lied to us and told us she knew cousins of ours (which I guess would make her seem more trustworthy), which I later found out was not true. I got the sense that it was a bad deal, she knew that I was gonna screw it up for her and she encouraged my husband to meet up with out me to see the building a few more times. She had her own mortgage people, etc. Fortunately, our own banker put the final knife in the deal and gave it to us straight-NO GO. We would have been eating ramen noodles for the rest of our lives on that one, so I tell people to just stay away from her.
I will also say that while I did not do business with Paul Halvatzis at Amorelli, he was so helpful (spent time to help me out when he clearly wasn't getting paid to do it) and a really nice guy. I used another realtor at Amorelli for a rental-and she finally found what we were looking for when I got nasty with her. She was doing the typical-showing us stuff way out of our price range.
We purchased our latest apartment from an agent at Douglas-Elliman and I have to say that they were way more professional than any of the other realtors I've met with. Maybe that is because they aren't Astoria-based.
 
OK, hope that helps, not confuses. Good Luck!

I haven't used these realtors but have talked to them on the phone.  Worth a try:
 
Peter Horowitz at http://myastoriarealestate.com/
 
Maria Aidonis at http://aidonisrealty.com/current-rentals/  Maria generally helps owners sell their places but does have some rentals.
 
Both realtors are very nice on the phone and there website list their rentals with good photos and floor plans.
 
Next, I would try Craig's list.  Although you have to be careful, I did find two women realtors on there who were on the up and up and showed me some nice places.

What you have to watch out for is a lot of "realtors" on Craig's list are independent and basically don't have the properties they say they do.  I got one who made an appointment to show me an apartment but when we got there, it turned out he didn't have the keys and the super wouldn't let him in!  If I have the time, I'll try to find the two women advertising on Craig's list who were good and post them here.
 
I got my apartment 4 yrs. ago from Taso (can't remember the name of his rental agency) at 347-461-3378.  He's on 31st Street between Ditmars and 21st Avenue.  Not saying he's the greatest but you may find something.
 
Good luck!
