Quote from: J Roscoe on June 30, 2010, 03:08:43 PM
Jimmy makes a good point, but my issue still lies with the supers that choose to play these games, not with the people willing to "buy" a good super.

Cost vs value for tenants is subjective... the law for supers/landlords is not.

Keep in mind that a key fee is still not a guarantee that the super will be more reactive.  Without proof of payment or explanation of what the fee covers, the super is not under obligation to be more reactive for you.  Also, if the apartment is so nice, then exactly how often do you expect to actually need these enhanced super skills you've paid for?  You're basically bribing the super to provide better service for an apartment that should actually require him to do less or possibly nothing at all.

The law is clear – absolutely. I am attempting to quantify the value of a key fee vs. a broker fee to an individual regardless of the law.  I’m putting the legal aspects aside.  I’m playing devils advocate.

I’m putting myself in the position of someone who needs an apt, found one that is reasonably priced, rent stabilized and I did it without a broker.  My family and I love the apartment, I work long hours and have kids that I would rather focus on than continuing to hunt for a new apartment.  Lastly, the broker and key fee are the same and the odds of getting caught doing this illegal activity are almost 0.

Looking at it from that very practical perspective, the ROI (or even potential ROI) for a key fee seems higher than the actual ROI on a broker fee.   

Definitely – no guarantee the super would be better.  Just the potential to get better or at least standard service.

Emergencies are difficult to forecast so better safe than sorry.  That is why many people get additional insurance when they rent a car, have life insurance, tip their super during the holidays... etc.   Right or wrong, odds are if 2 tenants have the same problem at the same time the super is going to work for the person they like more first.  Creating that bond could be from taking the time to make small talk when you see each other, tipping at Christmas, baking cookies… a hundred different things but greasing the wheels (in any way – not necessarily just $’s) never hurts.

At the least, you are in a rent stabilized apartment. 

I don’t think you get any long term value from a broker despite the fact that it’s an acceptable legal process.
