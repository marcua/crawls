I've always done it on Craigslist. I found my place as a potential roommate, then when she moved out, I found new roommate on CL. I'm now on my 4th roommate in this apartment, and I haven't had any problems yet. (It sounds like a lot of roommates, but I've been in this place for a long time - I like it, and more importantly, I hate moving! All roommates have left for "good" reasons - moving in with girl/boyfriends, getting a 1BR because they could now afford it, etc.)

I know you say that you don't want to live with a stranger anymore, but if you don't know anyone who is looking for a room, what other option do you have? And who's to say that a stranger can't become a friend? I still keep in touch with all my former roommates.

Every time I've looked for someone new to live here, I've posted an ad on Craigslist *AND* sent messages to people I know (on Facebook, email, twitter, everything that was active at the moment) saying "hey, I'm looking for a roommate, here's my ad! [link to CL ad] Help me out and pass this along/post it on your wall/whatever :)" I'm sure I've posted on here a couple times too when I was looking with a link to my CL ad.

That said, the roommate market this year is tough. I was looking again back in May, and hardly anyone responded. In the past, I'd have hundreds of responses to the exact same ad. A friend told me recently that she knows someone that's having a hard time finding a place. I just don't think many people are moving right now. My advice though, remains the same - trust your gut...no matter how you find someone, and keep it as friendly as you'd like but professional. Yeah, you're living with this person, but you're also financially tied to them for the time you're living together.

Also, and I don't know your financial situation, but at one point, I thought that it would be better to pay double for one month to live alone, rather than invite someone to live with me who I didn't feel 100% cool about. I don't regret paying that month, since I ended up getting an awesome roommate the next month.
