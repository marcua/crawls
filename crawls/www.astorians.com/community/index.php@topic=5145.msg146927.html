I actually signed up for IDT.  The people were in my building and I admit it can be offputting to have people knocking on your door who haven't been buzzed in.

Just remember this job is the livelihood for some people. 

While the individuals hustling to sign you up for IDT might not all be bad, there are indications that many are making deceptive claims to snag customers, many of whom are elderly or don't speak english well.  If nothing else, the management of the marketing company who sends these independent contractors out needs to have a tighter rein over them.  If you have the time, you should read the above linked consumerist entries.  They are illuminating.

By the way, their promise of savings was potentially fraudulent in and of itself.  If you read the IDT fine print, they promise customers savings for the first two months, but beyond that, they buy their energy on the open wholesale market.  Which means that they cannot promise you lower rates.  In fact, there is nothing to say that the rates won't be higher than the Con-Ed rates.

I can appreciate that this puts food on the table for some, but shady behavior is shady behavior just the same.

Not 100 percent clear.  While the Con-Ed FAQ does say that "Con-Ed is in the energy delivery business, not the energy supply business," it also notes in one of the questions that you can switch back to Con-Ed if you want to.
