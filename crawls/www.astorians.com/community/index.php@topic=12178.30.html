As bstewart also posted, some people may have trouble getting PBS (Thirteen) until the switchover, because they're still operating at low power on their digital signal, to save money.  Come February, they should be operating at full power just like all the other major stations.  Specifically getting the box with the analog pass-through shouldn't really be necessary, since you may still be able to get Thirteen in digital now anyway, and if not, the switchover is in two months. 

The box I have (an Insignia) has options for both an RF cable (antenna cable) and the AV cables (red/white/yellow)...most boxes should, with the knowledge that many people with older TV's will be buying them.

Also as pointed out, some stations are operating subchannels and others aren't:

4 - has Weather Plus (which I think will soon become an all-news channel) and Universal Sports (track meets, swimming, volleyball, etc.)

5 - For some reason, is wasting its bandwidth rebroadcasting My 9 (co-owned) as its subchannel.  Channel 9 is doing the same thing rebroadcasting Fox 5 as its subchannel.  Not sure what the point of this is, as it takes away bandwidth from the main channel.

7 - Has WABC+ (infomercials and rebroadcasts of Eyewitness News and some public affairs shows) and WABC Now (the same one also shown in Yellow cabs around the city, with news, weather, traffic and sports)

11 - Has a station with Spanish-language music videos, LATV

13 - Has an all-children's subchannel and one in Spanish

25 - Traffic cameras, usually with really poor picture quality and tons of electrical noise.  Leave it to the city of NY to have something this shoddy on the air.  Their radio station, WNYE 91.5, was on the air but inaudible for two weeks...last year the analog Channel 25 went off the air for over a month.  They just don't care at all, it seems.

31 - One of the subchannels is qubo, with all children's programming, there's another one with gospel and religious stuff, and another one with infomercials, I believe.

50 - If you can pull in NJN, there's two subchannels with additional PBS programming.  They're trying to move their antenna to 4 Times Square, so it may soon be easy for everyone to get a signal from them.

63 - If you can pull in WMBC from New Jersey (main channel has ethnic programming and a "cult" local newscast straight out of the 70s), the subchannels feature more ethnic programming in Korean, Chinese, Spanish, etc.

The other stations, as of yet, don't have subchannels.  CBS, for instance, apparently has a company-wide mandate *not* to add subchannels, and to instead broadcast a full HD signal over the air instead.

There's also some low-powered stations that one might be able to get on digital, like the bible-thumpers on Cornerstone TV (Channel 3).  I actually have never gotten a signal from them in digital!  In the next couple of years, some more low-powered stations that are now analog and aren't bound by the switchover this February will eventually go digital.  Nothing too exciting though, most likely...the likes of HSN, infomercials, paid programming, etc.

It's really not too confusing at all once you actually experience it...I know it sounds a bit complicated.

Once you have your converter box installed, you can pretty much tune your TV just as before..."2" for CBS-2, "4" for NBC, etc.  You'll notice the subchannels if you start pressing "channel up" or "channel down" when tuning. 

However, if you are not interested in any of the channels...whether regular stations or subchannels...most converter boxes let you add and remove stations from the memory, so you can just tune to what you want and block out everything else.

I think a lot of the problems that you are having are likely avoidable:

1. The image freezing might indicate that you need a better antenna to pull in a stronger signal
2. The black line might be the fault of your TV or the cable you are using to connect the box to the TV.
3. The static at loud volumes might be due to the difference in volume between your box and your TV, since the box has its own volume control.  ALSO...another thing you can try is switching the audio output for the converter box from stereo to mono.  A lot of older TV's do not have stereo speakers, and thus the stereo setting will give you a weak, staticky sound quality, whereas you'll get the full range of audio with mono audio.
