So we went on Friday night and it was great.  The food was great at least. We were one of two couples in the front of the restaurant when we were there, and there seemed to be some more tables in the back that had some customers, as well. We were quickly seated, and given the menus, and we both ordered a beer (the beer list could stand to be much better), and started browsing the menu.  The first thing that stood out was the collard green egg rolls, which sounded awesome, but they were unfortunately out of. After going back and forth on a number of different dishes I decided on the ox tails with collards and rice and beans, and my better half had the fried whiting with the mac and cheese and the potato salad. Everything we got was really, really, surprisingly good. 

Oxtails: Super tender, I wound up sucking every last morsel of meat off the bone. They were a little sweeter than I'm used to, but they were still super rich, succulent. I would certainly order these again.
Collards: We actually commented that these were the best collards we'd had in a long time (we love our greens) big chunks of pork, just the perfect vinegar flavor and seasoning.  I was sad when I hit the bottom of the bowl.
Rice and beans: These were pretty standard, again, a tad on the sweet side, but otherwise good.
Whiting: The whiting was really good.  The batter wasn't overly greasy, which you sometimes find with fresh fish. You could still taste the fist over the batter, which was a huge plus.  Again, done surprisingly well, and was great with just a little hot sauce.
Mac and Cheese: The mac and cheese was okay. Good flavors, but pretty standard mac and cheese overall. I wouldn't come back just for the mac and cheese, but I wouldn't not order it again.
Potato Salad: Note: I like my potato salad with mustard in it, which is why I was a big fan of this.  For me, it was done perfectly, but if you're not the type who enjoys the mustard, then this isn't for you, unfortunately.

All that said, there are two things that could definitely be improved upon.  First, the music could use to be lower.  When we were there they were playing fun music, it seemed to have an 80's R&B theme which worked well, but just down to 6 from 8 or so.
Second was the service.  I don't think the food took that long, and the chef actually came out of the kitchen to see how everything was (I was super surprised by that, but it was great).  It was our waitress.  She would just disappear for a while, here and there throughout the meal, leaving the busser to take care of us (not a big deal). Toward the end of the meal when we were getting ready for our check, she was MIA for what felt like 20 minutes. When she was around she was really nice and super helpful and I don't know if it was just her on the floor dealing with the tables in the front and back, or what, but definite room for improvement.

So I found it really enjoyable, but please note that this is soul food like you'd find in Harlem, this isn't some hoity toity fusion chef nonsense.  If you're looking for a 4 star white table cloth experience, this isn't what you're looking for.  If you want a place where it's okay to suck meat off the bone, and lick your fingers when you're done, this place should be tried. And neither of us noticed any of the "thug" clientele that has been mentioned in this thread.
