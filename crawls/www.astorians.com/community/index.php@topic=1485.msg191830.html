I'm happy to report that since my last post to this thread back in November, there were some easy and inexpensive steps I took that got rid of my bed bugs. And since it's been months since then, during which I've had no more bites, it would appear the results I've gotten with my anti-bed bug measures are permanent.

First of all, I did some research online, and found out a very effective bed bug killer can be made with silicon crystal cat litter. Here's a short video that gives instructions for using it: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RUJj_kArOc8&NR=1 I took it a step further buy making a 50/50 mixture of the ground silicon cat litter and boric acid. I figured if the one doesn't get 'em, the other will. I sprinkled this between my mattress and box spring. I also used a plastic squeeze bottle (like the kind used for dispensing ketchup) to blow this mixture into all the tiny crevices of my bed frame, as well as the baseboard of the walls around my bed. Worked like a charm!

But still, even though I didn't get any more bites since taking action that first weekend, I still had the bites I'd gotten at the beginning itching the hell out of me. This led me to seek out relief measures for bed bug bites, and I found a great one that's very easy to do and which really REALLY works! All you need to do to stop the bites from itching is to apply external heat for a minute or two. The way I did it was to use a blow-dryer that I held a few inches away from the bites. I kept the blow-dryer at just the right distance that the heat was intense, but not enough to cause a burn. It should only take a few seconds to determine this optimal distance for yourself. I then kept the blow-dryer blowing on the bite area for about between one and two minutes. That's it - that's all it took to put a permanent stop to the itching. The bites I had never bothered me again.

I hope these two tips are helpful to anyone else looking to put an end to a bed bug problem.

Call 311.  They will help renters.  If your place is infested and your landlord won't bring in a BED BUG Exterminator (not a regular roach/ant guy) then file a formal call with 311.  It's against the law for a landlord to ignore the problem.  It's a major health issue in NYC.  They spread fast.  Once they come to fumigate your place, wash all your sheets, clothes and anything laying around in HOT water and dry clothes on HOT.  Vacuum thoroughly.  There are some great places in the city that sell bed protectors to wrap your box spring and mattress and pillows.  Totally worth it.

I live near 31st avenue and 33rd street and we have bedbugs in our apartment.  The building directly next to mine has had infestations in at least 3 apartments.  2 days ago, someone on my block threw out a mattress and it was infested with bedbugs.  They didn't even put a sign on it. 

The bedbugs have really screwed up my life temporarily.  I've had to miss work so that I can clean literally EVERY piece of clothing and fabric in the apartment.  I've thrown out my dresser where I found the bugs and I've spent nearly $600 on this before the exterminator has even arrived: mattress and box spring covers, taking my dog to the vet because he got bit by a bug and the bite became infected,  bug spray, plastic bags, tape, flashlights.  I've thrown out lots of random things I don't want to take any chances with.

Bedbugs are creepy.  They live in tiny crevices and they come out when you are in your deepest sleep, usually between the hours of 3 and 5am to feed.  They climb onto your skin, release a saliva to numb your skin then suck your blood for about 5 minutes. Each bug usually bites 3 times a night. Then they crawl back to their crevice full-bellied without you feeling a thing until you wake up and notice an itch.  They can also go up to 18 months without eating!  If you decide to sleep on the couch to avoid the bites for a couple nights, they will follow you to the couch and start living there!  They are highly evolved bugs and resilient.

If you think you have them, tell your landlord right away!  It's the right thing to do.  This is an epidemic and it's going out of control. 

Everyone in NYC should have a bed bug cover on their mattress.  If you wake up and have ANY type of strange bite or rash, don't assume it is a mosquito or a rash.  Check your mattress seams for any signs of the bugs.  They are as big as an apple seed and they will leave droppings of blood near their hiding place.  Also, don't lean anything against your bed.  I made the mistake of leaning my dresser against my bed and the bedbugs used the dresser as their hiding place. Fortunately, my mattress was miraculously saved.

If you have bedbugs and you throw any furniture out, make sure you spray paint or write BEDBUGS on it so nobody decides to take your furniture home and spread the epidemic to their building. 

I finally got hit with bedbugs and I realize now that it is a major problem that is not getting any better. Unless we take preventative measures it will get worse and worse.  10 years ago bedbugs were hardly even heard of.  Now, everyone knows at least 1 or 2 people who have had them.
