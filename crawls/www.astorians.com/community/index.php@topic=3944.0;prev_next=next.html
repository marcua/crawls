Astoria Telephone Exchanges
« on: October 24, 2006, 12:19:30 PM »
Long before there were area codes, all we had were telephone exchanges. An exchange was the first 3 digits of your telephone number. You could tell where someone lived by their exchange.

For example:
721 = RAvenswood 1-XXXX
932 = YEllowstone 2-XXXX
278 = AStoria 8-XXXX

Then all 5 boroughs were in the 212 area code.

Then we had to dial 1 before getting into another area code.

Now we have to dial 11 digits to call the guy who lives down the hall.

Ah! Technology!

You can still get a pretty good idea where somebody lives in the city, assuming they have POTS service (vs. cellular or VOIP):

http://www.telcodata.us/telcodata/telco

Put in the area code & exchange (yes, it's still called an exchange.)  You can get the details on the switch type that serves the number, where it's located, what other exchanges it serves, what other switches are in the building, etc.  Verizon isn't stupid when it comes to copper, people are served by switches in their area.

What I find really galling is that only the "outer" boroughs are on 10-digit dialing. Manhattan, of course, is the exception. :roll:

Nope - from a 212 number, you have to dial 1-212 to get another 212 number (which makes it 11 digit dialing, actually).  It's been that way since Feb. 1, 2003.  (In fact, it is at that time that 11 digit dialing became mandatory for 718 numbers as well.)  The New York State Public Service Commission petitioned the FCC to create an exception for both 212 and 718 numbers, but the FCC rejected the requested exception on fairness grounds.  The reason for the 10 (or 11) digit dialing is because 212 and 718 both have overlay area codes -- 646 (212 only), 917 and 347.
