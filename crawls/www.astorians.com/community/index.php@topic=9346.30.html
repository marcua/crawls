Quote from: essen on February 06, 2008, 11:39:08 PM
... Why is it left up to the customers to pay an employee's salary? ...

Because, if the restaurant was responsible for ensuring the waiter/waitress/delivery person made good money, your food would cost much more.  These restaurants run on very, very slim operating profits.  It's not like they have tons of extra dollars income per plate that they can afford food cost increases, utility increases, and rent increases in stride. 

Costs are passed on to the customer; increased employee salary is one of those costs.

As another argument is that it is a little like carrot and donkey.  The server/delivery person knows they have to make sure you enjoy your experience and the food comes out just right (many times this is not in their control) to make decent money.  Unfortunately for them, many people don't understand the work that goes into it and do not deem it necessary to pay them for it.

Now, I'm not saying you have to be ridiculous in your tipping.  But, you have to take the amount of work into account and, at least try to, put yourselves in their shoes.

Anyone that says $1 or $2 is a good tip should be required to work as a server (or delivery person) at a busy restaurant on a Friday or Saturday night; or worse, Sunday morning.  I guarantee that you'll change your views on tipping pretty quickly.
