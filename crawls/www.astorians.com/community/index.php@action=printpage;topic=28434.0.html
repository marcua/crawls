Forget all the great restaurants to visit...how about when we are being lazy and order from home?

These are my seamless choices:

Sushi:
GoWasabi - I like their quality and size of a la cart pieces, though they are pricey.
SushiX - Smaller pieces, but godo quality, and a la carte is $1 each!

Italian:
Dino's - After trying several places that sucked (Sac's too expensive, Gaudio's seemed like Ragu), Dino's was deliscious, though I wisht hey gave larger portions.

TexMex:
Fresca Tortilla (36th Ave) - No matter where I am in Astoria, they arrive in 10 minutes. It's messy, greasy food, but yummy, cheap and reliable. My most-ordered after all these years.

Burgers:
Takeout or sit-down, Bareburger is the only burger place I'll eat (beef on bread is otherwise pretty boring). Though I admit they've messed up my orders a few times.

I am looking for suggestions on Middle Eastern (namely Afghan), and a good place for various, basic chicken food. Anyone got anything?

Title: Re: Your Seamless Picks
Post by: LHT on October 13, 2014, 03:41:37 PM
Given the work schedule & hours we keep, I totally rely on pick-up and delivery options during week nights.  We live by Astoria Park and interestingly enough the delivery options will vary depending on if you're looking at Seamless vs. GrubHub vs. Eat24, etc.  Here are some faves

- Mike's Diner doesn't sound so great on paper, but honestly their food is really good.  Breakfast can be a great adventure with them - whole grain oatmeal pancakes, add walnuts.  And if you order an egg dish, try the grits instead of potatoes - a nice surprise!  Excellent burger and the steak tips saute with mushrooms & onions is a winner too!

- Mia pizza (formerly Sandros) - food is great, straightforward American Italian and the owner will even call sometimes to see how you liked the food - no joke!  Soups are excellent and totally home made, veal parm, manicotti and the white pizza with spinach is a win-win.  Even if you don't want a whole pie, they'll do the baby pizza size for you.

- Ancla - peruvian fried rice will beat any Asian fried rice, hands down!  We usually do beef or shrimp.  Ceviche is outstanding, roasted chicken spot on (ask for Garlic Sauce on the side).  There's a choloca corn and cheese dish too that's fresh, simple & great.  Seamless is a godsend as they speak terrible English over the phone.  Service is very kind if you go there live and the language barrier doesn't seem as extreme in person, but unless you speak Spanish, don't expect to get very far over the phone with any delivery mixups.
