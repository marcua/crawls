Question on living in No-Man's-Land area just beyond corner of Ditmars/Steinway
« on: June 02, 2014, 11:08:07 PM »
I currently live on 21st St. near 30th Ave in Astoria and decided I just don't want to pay the ever increasing rent in my luxury building.  I want to save money.  But I also want to stay in Astoria.  I love it here!  But to save at least a few hundred a month, I need to take my living space down a few notches.  So what do I give up....my outdoor space (I currently have a balcony with potted plants)?...do I give up a dishwasher? (nah...can't do that as I detest washing dishes).... what about the size of the apartment?...yeah I suppose I could do a bit smaller...what about the overall quality of the apartment and appliances?....yeah I guess I could take it down a little bit but I can't be in a place that feels like crap or depressing or chopped up or like they just threw the place together.... 

What about location?  Now see here's where it gets tricky.  At first I was like 'no way will I live anywhere outside the square area of Ditmars down to 39th Street, or 21st Street across to just past Steinway'.  Anything outside that square area is too remote.  And then I saw an apartment today on 21st Ave (which is one block NORTH of Ditmars) and 40th Street (just EAST of Steinway).  This is what I personally and officially consider the beginning of the dreaded.....No-Man's-Land.  ;-)

Not ideal, but then after looking at a number of apartments I knew that something had to give if I want to save money.  I'm not going to live in a depressing apartment where it's clear the LL/bldg mgmt has no sense of pride... I really don't want to give up my gardening and...I really want to stay in Astoria.  I decided that maybe even being on the PERIPHERY of Astoria is better than being in say.... Woodside (where I saw a really nice apartment yesterday).  No disrespect to Woodside of course but...it ain't Astoria.

I haven't really explored all the other directions outwards from 21st Ave and 40th Street but my strong sense is that, unless I am heading across Ditmars towards the N/Q, or heading down Steinway towards 30th Ave etc., that there is really nothing in this area...no cafes, restaurants....nada...except maybe an old school Italian pizza joint somewhere...am I right?

So what do y'all think?  On one hand I'm like 'hey, I'm gonna be saving $700/month on rent....I already use car services to get from one part of Astoria to another as it is, even living on 21st Street.  So what's to stop me from doing that in this new area?  Also, maybe this will motivate me to get a bike, and once I have a bike I can quickly be anywhere and feel more 'connected' to the active parts of Astoria...not so isolated.

But then another part of me fears that I'll still 'feel' the isolation....just knowing that there's no activity outside my building...that it's a sleepy area...with LGA nearby etc. But again, there's that car service option... I could be out and about in the nabe and back home in no time...or else by bike.  But living alone, again, there's that fear of feeling isolated.  And like, I'm a very social person...love having people over.  I suspect I just won't click with anybody in the immediate area...it's probably mainly families or very blue collar immigrants or else old-school sorta close-minded native NYers who were born in the area.  I also suspect that friends will be less apt to visit me here as it will involve the 101 bus from say the M/R train at Steinway, a car service from the N/Q at Ditmars, or else a good 15-20 min. walk from Ditmars...

So....anybody else live there, or has lived there, and who also lived ALONE, and can relate?  Thoughts?

Thanks!!!!

I realize that my line in the sand may not be the same as someone else's line but Steinway and 21 avenue is not Siberia.


21 Avenue is not a major commercial avenue, it's more residential but there are plenty of shops, supermarkets restaurants and cafes one block away on Ditmars, it's not like you'd have to trek miles. If you're used to living on 21 street, which is not really in the thick of it in my opinion, I think you'll do fine in Steinway town.

First, there is no 39th Street or 40th Street above the GCP -- it's 38 street, Steinway and 41 street -- don't ask why it's one of the charms of the Queens street grid.

Within a 10 minute or less walk of Steinway and 21st avenue you will find plenty including a small shopping center on Ditmars and between 43rd and 45th street (again, there's no 44 street above the GCP) with Berry Fresh supermarket, RiteAid, Porto Bello pizzeria, a gym. There's Krave diner and Astoria bakery on the corner of 46 street and Ditmars. There's another shopping center with Best Yet supermarket on 37 Street and 20 Avenue. Plus there are a few cafes and restaurants on Steinway itself between Ditmars and 20 Avenue.

It's not 30th Avenue but if it were you'd be paying more for your apartment. That's a trade-off.

Try it, you'll like it.

I actually lived on that block when I first moved here and it was perfectly fine (and actually live just a few blocks west of that block).  I love the quieter feel of the 'hood but still being only a block from the action on Ditmars. 


Besides, you will have many places within a 5-10 minute walk so don't think it's too much of "Siberia". Also, the Q101 runs right along Steinway if you don't want to use a car service.  If you want to rent a car you can hop the Q69 at Ditmars/Steinway and be at the LGA Enterprise location in less than 10 minutes.


If you have a pet there is an awesome vet on Steinway and Ditmars. 


Hope this helps.
