I moved to Astoria in 2007 after lurking on this board for almost a year.  This board seemed so cool, diverse, and funny.  The members seemed engaged with the neighborhood and the world.   If the real neighborhood was even remotely like the online community here, then I had high hopes that I'd find a happy niche for myself in Astoria.

Well, flash forward to now.  How do I feel about Astoria, the neighborhood in Western Queens?   DO. NOT. WANT.   Limited finances are keeping me here for the time being, but I'm plotting my escape as I type this. 

I'm in the Broadway area.  It's serviceable but nothing great.

Random things that turn me off:

- It's ugly.  If you've been anywhere on the planet outside of NYC, and you're being honest, you know how unattractive this part of Queens is.  The utilitarian architecture is a downer.   All the red brick is overwhelming.   I thought I could get over it, and the neighborhood would offer other positives that would compensate for the ugliness.  I was wrong.

- Ignorant, racist neighbors who talk about the 30-something cleaning person in my building as the "boy" and on two occasions used an ugly ethnic slur in reference to him.   Cannot stand the Archie Bunker vibe some of the residents put out.

- Lack of trees on sidewalks.  Air quality is poor.   And wow, I can walk to parks or outdoor exhibits 30 minutes to an hour away?  How convenient ... not!

- For all the food talk on this board, the restaurant scene is lame.  The high-end places are mediocre.  The budget places aren't as exciting or yummy as food I've had in other parts of New York or even in Queens - like Sunnyside, Jackson Heights or Elmhurst.

- The elevated train makes the neighborhood look drab.  (How does Chicago pull it off?)

- Nosy neighbors who keep track of my coming and going and ask me about it, but don't engage in conversation that's pleasant or sincere.

- Local businesses that don't deliver services as promised and lie! lie! lie!  Boulevard fricking Decor, I'm looking at you. 


To READERS of this message board - If this message board is part of your decision-making process to move to Astoria, here's a tip:  lower your expectations.  Lower them drastically.  Take hold of the hype and all the Astoria-love floating on this board and regard with great skepticism.
