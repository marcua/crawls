Astorians,
  I hate to admit it ( ), but I have noticed this unique aroma in my apartment as well lately.  I thought that it was my imagination and over-sensitive nose or a by-product of living in a large pre-war building.  But, I have noticed it from time to time.  I live on south side of the GCP/Astoria Bl. on 44th Street for the record.
  Again, just a minor temporary inconvenience of living in an urban environment in my humble opinion and, certainly, nothing to write home about.
  I guess that I'm just too busy focusing all of the great smells that Astoria offers like fresh baking bread from the local bakeries or the fresh smell of the air after a rain shower that wafts into my bedroom window at night to really complain about it.
  I'm probably alone on this, but I also enjoy walking into my building after a long day at work and trying to figure out what my various neighbors are cooking for dinner (fried fish aside).  I love the sights, sounds, AND SMELLS of living in such a great city and such a wonderful, vibrant neighborhood.
  If I wanted to live in a town with pristine air quality, I would have located to New Mexico, Alaska, or maybe upstate Minnesota.  I knew what I was in for before I moved here and I have no regrets...only great experiences and memories.
