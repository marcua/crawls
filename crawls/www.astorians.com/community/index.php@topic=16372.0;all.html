People accuse me of being nostalgic about the Astoria of ten or fifteen years ago. But I submit that anyone who thinks this is still a real neighborhood, a place where sane (that is to say, uncool and unhip) people would want to raise their kids, is indeed nostalgic for a neighborhood that no longer exists. Thanks to overdevelopment and the unfortunate trendy labels that have been attached  to it by twentysomethings looking for a cheaper place to crash and dorm, what was once a vibrant urban village of families and small businesses has become a college town without the college: You'll notice that there's no bookstore in sight. There's nothing hip and cool about garbage, graffiti, cracked sidewalks, traffic scofflaws and a general atmosphere of filth, slop and noise, unless you're here to live out your urban fantasies, that is. All this became evident when I invited some of my Manhattan friends to dinner one night, and, as we were walking through the shantytown of 23rd Avenue, one asked: "Hey, is this neighborhood safe?"

I always have held positive associations with the idea of "college towns":  Berkeley, Cambridge, Ann Arbor, Austin.  Lovely places, all.  I am also confident that the Astoria Of Yore was not perfect either.

As far as parents running for the hills when confronted with the idea of raising their kids in Astoria, that is simply not the case.  In fact, I have noticed a lot more people with strollers and baby carriers of all kinds.  Has anyone else noticed this?  Lots of cute babies in this town.  In the summer sometimes there is are a plethora of children that play on my block.  They like to hang out together into the late afternoon and evening - I can also hear them and their families later in the evening spending time with each other on their patios/porches.

Over the years, I've lived in both big and small cities (including the suburbs of California, the Midwest, and Long Island), and I can assure you that "garbage, graffiti, cracked sidewalks, and traffic scofflaws" can be found most places.  As far as the "filth, slop and noise", though, what are you doing about it?  How are you working to improve your community with regard to these three elements?  Are you simply bitching and complaining on an internet message board or are you going to do something about the flaws you see?
