If you are in Jackson Heights (convenient for you if you are near the V or R -- take train 4 stops from Steinway St. to 74th/Roosevelt)
there are several Indian grocers that sell very cheap nuts in large-size packages.  I think the price for a 4 pound bag of almonds is $14, which makes it $3.50 per pound.  they also have pistachios and other nuts, but I've only checked the price on almonds.

The big three are Patel Brothers, Apna and Subzi Mandi.  Addresses linked here http://www.desijacksonheights.com/grocery-stores.html

There used to be a place called Sweet Nut in that odd little commercial space on the 2nd floor right off the Ditmars stop.  They had all sorts of bulk nuts, seeds, snacks and dried fruits, as well as candies and coffee.  I miss it, and I haven't found a good local substitute.  Having a local place is good when all you want or need (or can afford) are small amounts.

Bigger health food stores (I'm thinking specifically of Perelandra in Brooklyn Heights, Back to the Land in Park Slope, and the (mostly) unlamented Healthy Pleasures on University) usually have a separate bulk section, and now that I think about it, I think Fresh Start has some bulk nuts and dried fruits (as well as grains) shoehorned in in back.  I need to check out their prices.
