I have to agree...I spent some time in Newark, and it was pretty sad to see how a city the state the city is in, and what the city could potentially be.

Newark has great connections by road, rail and air, a huge port, a nice downtown area with wide streets, parks, spacious sidewalks, some very beautiful buildings.  A clean subway system and a monorail.  Seven hospitals...quite a lot for a city Newark's size.  A pretty vibrant academic community.  Some nice cultural attractions, museums, concert halls, stadiums.  And of course, it is just a few minutes away from NYC.

And yet...it's Newark.  Crime, drug and gang-ridden, which has really kept people out and held the city back.  Though, if you're looking for cheap housing, there's plenty to be found there.  It's quite possible to find a luxury 2BR apartment in a new building right in downtown Newark, with a doorman, parking, gym, etc. for less than $1,000 a month.   Great deal, until you step outside and realize there's no stores and no one walking around after dark.  Oh well!
