It bears repeating at times like this that most people who are sexually assaulted know their attacker. This is not meant to diminish this particular crime--I hope she's getting the care and support that she needs, and that the perpetrator is caught. I just hate to see people think that our streets aren't safe because of this incident, and somehow turn it into a "there goes the neighborhood"-type of situation, when this is happening in our homes much more frequently than it's happening out there. This kind of thing is a sad exception.

Incidentally, a friend of mine was attacked in Astoria--by someone she knew--and as she ran down the street away from him, yelling, someone inside a ground-level apartment simply yelled out, "Are you okay?" My friend said no, and said that she was going to the hospital. The person in the apartment called 911, and that helped build a case against her attacker. So that's one method of determining whether a scream is drunken fun or something requiring attention.

(Astoria-WABC, August 13, 2007) - Since the early hours of the morning, police in Queens have been searching for a man who sexually assaulted a woman at knifepoint. The alleged attack happened inside an alley in Astoria overnight.

A witness described hearing the woman yell for help as she ran out of the alley and flagged down a car as she tried to get some assistance.

You can imagine the reaction to this in this neighborhood, that is described as an otherwise, quiet and safe neighborhood.

Whether it's six o'clock, or eight in the morning, there's no shortage of foot traffic on Ditmars Boulevard in Astoria. The fact is, people walk there at all hours of the day and night in relative safety.

"I live in this neighborhood and I come home at all hours of the night and I've always felt so totally safe in this neighborhood. That completely shocks me," Astoria resident Bonnie Lynn said.

"I get home at like three o'clock in the morning and honestly there's no one around. I can honestly say that's why I love living in this area. That kind of boggles my mind actually," said Astoria resident Angelica Rodriguez.

That shock is over a robbery and sex assault that occurred last night.

The victim was walking home around 11:00 p.m. when the suspect allegedly shoved a sharp object against the woman's back that she believed was a knife.

She says the man then forced her into an alley where he demanded cash, she handed over $400. Then police say he sexually assaulted her. Apparently, the victim finally ran to safety when she heard a car approaching.

"I was in the house and she's running across the street screaming 'help me, help me'. I ran ran right out and she stopped the car, the first car she stopped...they called 9-1-1 and the cops came....," said eyewitness Harry Biolsi.

The suspect fled. Police scoured the area looking for evidence. The victim was treated at an area hospital and released.

"What's most important now is to make sure the victim is taken care of as much as possible, and that we apprehend the person who did this so that they don't victimize other people going forward," said Assem. Mike Gianaris, (D) Astoria.

Meantime, women in the neighborhood say they will be more careful.

"....stuff happens and you know you have to be conscious and you have to be aware of your surroundings," an Astoria resident said.

Assemblyman Mike Gianaris and City Councilman Peter Vallone are offering $1,000 reward for any information leading to an arrest of the suspect.

The only description of the suspect so far:

In his 20s
About 5' 5"
150 lbs.
Wearing a black t-shirt, blue jeans & black sneakers
