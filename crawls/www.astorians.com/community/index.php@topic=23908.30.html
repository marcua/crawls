I was at Food Bazaar today. They are still in grand opening mode, celebrating with a DJ, a couple of people dressed up as circus performer making balloons animals  for kids, and a mariachi band. Lots of food demonstrations and samples for the the shoppers to snack on. They supermarket has also taken over the former adjoining stores by Dunkin Donuts, the pizza store and the real estate & accounting place, so it's bigger than it was before. It's at least as large as the nearby Stop & Shop now. The produce department is really big, and the aisles of the rest of the store are huge. They have a lot check out cashiers, but we'll see how long that lasts. The prices are a mixed bag, with limited sales items. I see no evidence of a food court as a one of the poster said earlier. The have an underground garage which makes it easier to park when the weather is bad like in today's snow conditions. Overall it's a lot to take in for a first visit.  I do recommend that people take a trip to check it out for themselves.

I was back again today for grand opening continued. It was like Costco in that there were food samples galore, I was glad I hadn't eaten lunch. I stand by the food court statement, it's that circular area behind the Merry Christmas sign that's there now. On Friday the sign proclaimed the food court was coming, designed by a famous chef whose name I couldn't recall. So that's just the last missing part. Have to admit I am really really impressed. It is good as a big box store for when you're looking for packaged named brands. The meat section with good Angus beef,  fresh fruit and vegetables, and the presentation is well thought out. Even though Pathmark pretended to renovate the store in the mid 2000's, this is the expenditure of capital and thought that the location needed. The parking lot was packed with lots of traffic which means it's still very accessible to all the local communities. they put in a brand new elevator down to that parking area in the basement, and hopefully that's where a lot of the cars will now go.
