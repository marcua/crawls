I wait tables at a trendy restaurant in Chelsea, and have been there for 6 years.  I serve food to a wide variety of people, but my favorite and most frequent customers are the fabulous gay locals from the neighborhood.  As my regulars, they know what they want and expect the same quality every time. This doesn't always happen. While some people are downright rude about it, most are apologetic when requesting there food be sent back and heated, recooked, etc.  My philosophy has always been one in which we work hard for our money, we spend good money on food, we should get what we want.  If it is not up to your satisfaction, you should send it back!!!!  I can't think of any restaurant that will not accommodate this.  If you get your food and you just don't like it, and I think we can all say we have ordered wrongly at one time or another, ethically you should probably suck it up, but I will ac comadate that as well, if your really nice about.  Please keep in mind that your server DID NOT MAKE THE FOOD.  So if the service is good in spite of your entree, please tip accordingly to your service, as the kitchen staff does not depend on tips.

I've sent food back before and I think what julie says is pretty much true. I'm not rich. I don't go out to eat often and if I'm spending my money on food, I want it to be right. Now, I usually don't send back something just because it's a little cold or it's lukewarm, but if my steak isn't cooked to the right temperature or they put something in that I asked to be left out, I'll send it back.

I think the important thing is to be nice to the waitstaff about it--it's not their fault--and realize that it's going to take time to fix the error. Don't send your food back if you're in a ruch and then get flustered with the waitress.

I also think that most restaurants would rather have you send food back if it's prepared wrong rather than get the wrong impression of their establishment.

I agree with Julie too. It's not the server's fault in most cases. However, if you make
a request like dressing on the side or no cheese and they bring it to you with what you
don't want, then I can understand getting a little testy - but not rude.

After all, you're paying for the food so you should get what you want. I have no problem
sending back cold or uncooked food - I don't order it that way, it's  not offered on the menu
that way, so why would I want it that way?

I grew up around the restaurant business - my dad was a chef - and most servers I knew at
his place were cool about stuff like that, esp. w/regular customers. And I've found that the more
polite you are about it, the better the service.
