When you think about how LMTs generally lose a third of their income to self-employment tax, and have just been handed a law that states that we must spend a generous portion of what's left on continuing education, never mind what we pay for space rental, laundry, oils, etc, you may understand why our prices are the way they are.  Also, few of us can perform more than 5 or 6 massages in a day without risking injury.  It's not like we're rolling in a big vat of money like Scrooge McDuck - a lot of us are struggling, especially in an economy where people are unwilling to spend money on things that they view as unneccesary.

An unlicensed person does not have some of these things to deal with, and thus can charge less. That is true.  But it does not change the fact that they are committing a felony.

Massage is health care.  You, KidMurdock, obviously place some value on it, or you wouldn't bother getting it at all.

Setting aside the issue of competition, there is also the issue of training.  There are a lot of ways for someone performing massage to accidentally harm their patient or client, and while some are fairly obvious, others are far less so.  Something as simple as varicose veins requires a change in technique.  Some schmuck who decides they know what they're doing can hang out a sign, and people can go to them because they're cheap, and they could wind up endangering their health.  It may seem like a fluffy indulgence to a large portion of the population, but it's serious business.

For people with health insurance (I am unfortunately not a member of that population) - you may be able to get your massages covered by your insurance.  Few solo practitioners accept insurance, but almost all chiropractic/multidisciplinary offices do.  It's worth checking out, and your benefits are there to be used.  Flex spending accounts can also often be used for massage, acupuncture, etc.


Please report unlicensed massage practices, especially those you believe to be fronts for prostitution, to the Office of the Professions.  There's a handy form, hotline number, and email address here.
