We had dinner there tonight.  I am not at all an expert on ramen, but we thought everything was very tasty.  We had two Tonkotsu Shoyu, one with extra cha-shu pork, and one with a poached egg; and one Nagoya.  The noodles were different, but no one explained to us why or exactly what the difference was.  (I spoke briefly to the owner last night and he told me that once they get the kitchen running smoothly, they will start making their own noodles.)  We also tried the pork belly Niku man (small sandwich on steamed bun).  Portions were very generous and we left feeling somewhere between very satisfied and stuffed.

When we got there, about 60% of the tables were full.  By the time we left, the place was packed and about half a dozen people were waiting for tables.  There were a lot of people there with kids - it seemed very family-friendly (but not overrun with small children).

Service was friendly but not very explanatory.  None of us know much about ramen beyond the basics, and it would have been really helpful to have more explanation about the different types of noodles and add-ons.  There are brief explanations of the different types of broth on both the menu and the large chalkboard near the entrance, and I think the blank space on the chalkboard would be an ideal place for that.
