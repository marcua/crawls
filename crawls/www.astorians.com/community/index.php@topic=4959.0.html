Okay, so this sounds like a partisanship type of thing.  When the democrats snare the White House and he/she:mrgreen: has to face down a bureaucracy built up over 8 years of republican rule, this action will seem a bit more palatable I'm sure. 

There is no difference between the sovereignty of a king and that of elected officials, only the manner in which they attain their power and from where this power derives its strength.  Our president is our quadrennially elected king.  Its pretty much been that way since Senator Ross found President Johnson not guilty. 

Quote
I'm not sure if I interpreted this correctly, but are we saying that as an elected official we should trust the decisions made and the addition of liasons in various non-government organizations? Doesn't this remove checks and balances from our system and empower more of an "empire"

The system of checks and balances do not lay here.  The executive branch has the responsibility to enforce the laws as passed by the legislature.  In order to allow for initiative and flexibility, the laws often general in nature.  The executive branch is permitted to operate within the parameters set up operating rules and (in essence) propogate de facto laws based on such.  If this was not the case, the day to day running of the government would be a bureaucratic nightmare.  If the legislature feels that the executive is going in the wrong direction with this latitude, or might be abusing its spirit if not its letter, it has the right/duty to set more specific guidelines.  That's the checks and balances.
