Quote from: Bob the Astorian on March 26, 2008, 12:00:49 PM
LOL! I like the thumbs up/down feature, though.  It's like having audience sound effects. 

I go back and forth on thumbs up/thumbs down.  It does add an interesting degree of interactivity, as you suggest.  But it's also a mild form of tyranny of the majority.  If I post a positive message about President Bush, for example, I will get 8 to 10 thumbs down in about two hours.  But what purpose do those thumbs down serve?  I know that I live in a predominantly liberal area, and I'm posting on a predominantly liberal board, just as well as I know I am entitled to my own opinion.

The thumbs down feature allows people to do a drive by expression of disapproval without actually intellectually engaging.  It doesn't add anything of substance to the board.  If you disagree with someone's ideas, tell them why.  That's what a board like this is all about.

Edit: By "public," I mean that it should be made public who is thumbing up and down a particular post.  You should have to own up to your expression.  That way, I would know who is constantly thumbing me down for no good reason, and they would know that I constantly use my own "thumb up" to restore myself to zero. :-)
