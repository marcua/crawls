Title: Re: Kitten seeks home
Post by: jayme on May 05, 2007, 12:28:48 PM
This update is NOT what you think.

While we waited with kitten expectations last night, it was not meant to be.  Twiggy remained sedate through most of the night moving between her spot in the box to her spot in the cat bag.  Darcee and I kept up an hourly check in schedule.  At the 6-7am check in, Twiggy vomitted and settled back down but seemed to be declining rather than improving.  We decided to let her continue to rest for the early morning hours with a plan to take her to the vet in the morning.  She continued to move between the two spots but something was still not quite right.  Darcee and I took a coffee break to talk out all the scary possibilities (thank you google) and strategize.  When we got back to the apartment to check on Twiggy, she was having seizures.

Twiggy was non-responsive and seizing so we put her in the bag and hauled ass to the vet -- calling in the three block trip to inform them again of our emergency and estimated arrival time.  Dr. Smith came to take us back as soon as we were announced and things were not looking good for Twiggy.  We discussed the limited options.  Because of her pregnancy and uncertain background and life on the streets history AND rapid deline AND severity of her current condition -- there was virtually nothing left to do.  It was clear to all of us she was suffering and at the recommendation of the vet and our conscience, we decided it was best to put her to sleep.

While there was no conclusive diagnosis it is suspected that Twiggy may have had kitty distemper -- a rapidly moving, debilating, and often fatal cat virus that can be prominent in stray cats.  This is a disease that cats are vaccinated against that is entirely preventable by taking pro-active, reasonable, and responsible measures to get your cat vaccinated.  Even after finding a good and safe home, Twiggy fell vicitim to irresponsible pet owners who abandoned her, neglected to spay her, and whose blatant abuse in refusing to vaccinate her against entirely preventable diseases cost the life of Twiggy and her kittens.

We had a great week with her and are happy that we had a chance to welcome her into a loving home.  We have no regrets about our course of action in adopting or caring for Twiggy in her much-too-short time with us.  We are sorry to bring you the sad news of the kitten astorians have grown to love.  Yes, the vet even cried.

If anything now we hope to encourage (if not demand) responsible pet ownership (and vaccinations).  We also hope you will consider supporting animal rescue groups and perhaps bring into your life a loving kitten/puppy/dog/cat beast/mother-to-be to share many happy weeks with.


Thank you for your concern and interest in the story of Twiggy.  Please, no emails or private messages as her "family" is still grieving.

I should look into the shelter situation around here since it's where I live now. But I'm very fond of the Grateful Paw shelter- it's no-kill, extremely organized, and the cats are in open rooms instead of cages (unless they're sick.) Really a nice place.

It's frustrating in general: A guy I know tipped me off to an old house on 31st St. near the car rental place. There's a cat hoarder who lives there, and he saw her dozens of cats living in the garage.

I called one agency after another, and eventually came full-circle. It was absurd. The ASPCA can't do anything unless the person who witnessed it first-hand calls in, and my friend didn't want to jeopardize his business relationship with this woman. Besides, there's no law against having a bunch of cats on the property and feeding them.

I forgot who else I spoke with- health dep't or something. Cat hoarders who feed tons of strays seem to be just outside everybody's jurisdiction. Maybe it's a problem that the elected officials need to address. A new law needs to be created.

In the meantime, if we each contribute what little we each have to offer, we can form the Underground Stray Railroad to ease the problem a bit.
