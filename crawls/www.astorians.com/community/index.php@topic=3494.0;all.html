Well, technically the whole thing is Long Island City.  Look at the post office by the Ditmars N/W stop - it says "Long Island City, 11105".  My driver license says I live in "Astoria, NY", and my husband's says that he lives in "Long Island City, NY", depsite the fact that we obviously have the same address.  Occasionally when I buy something online or over the phone, the seller's computer system will not let me put in "Astoria" for the 11105 zip code.

Here's About.com's take on it.  Just as NYC's modern form was created in 1898 by consolidating the five boroughs, Long Island City was created in 1870 by consolidating the formerly independent towns of Astoria, Hunters Point, Blissville, Ravenswood, Dutch Kills, Bowery Bay, Steinway, and Sunnyside.  The Greater Astoria Historical Society also has some information about the history and consolidation of LIC.

In my mind, Long Island City is clearly the part around Vernon/Jackson / Queens West/Gantry Park -- south of the Queensboro Bridge.  The area north of the bridge and Northern Boulevard/Sunnyside Yards is all Astoria, although I also have a tendency to refer to the areas around 36th and 39th Aves as LIC, so I'm a bit inconsistent.  The western boundary is the East River, and the eastern boundary is Hazen/51st.
