Title: Winegasm (37th Street, just North of Broadway)
Post by: rich23 on March 25, 2008, 12:47:41 AM
Hi Astorians my name is Richard and my partners and I wanted to introduce ourselves to the community board.  We heard about this resource from a few people and we see there is already some contraversy regarding Winegasm and previous posts so we thought we would adress them personally :-).   We are three wine enthusiasts and our motivation from the beginning was to build a beautiful wine bar with wonderful food that would truly become a homebase to all members of the community.  We see from the feedback regarding our name that our intentions may have been misinterpreted so I just wanted to clear a few things up.  The name came from a definition in the urban dictionary, it goes as follows: " The sensation of Euphoria over finding and experiencing the perfect bottle of wine, sought after for its region, make, or price. reveling in its first taste, while appreciating its rarity leading to a moment of pure bliss."  So in short it's just a name and the interior is actually, at least we feel, really beautiful.  We have built this place from the ground up and have put so much heart into it that we hope it will show when you come and see it.  Now about the place itself...it has a rustic feel with exposed brick walls and intricate wood work.  The wine list is approachable yet ecclectic and the food is a tapas style menu with a French and Mediteranean influence.  We have communal tables as well as intimate seating in order to reinforce the sense of community we were hoping for.  We would love for everyone to come down, we are having a soft opening this Saturday (a limited menu will be available we are perfecting it) but come down for some wine and cocktails everything will be half price.  We are really excited about this new venture in our lives and would love to share it with you.  Thanks for your support!  To check out the menu and winelist go to winegasmeatery.com.

-Richard, Laris, and Dean

Title: Re: Introducing Winegasm Bar and Eatery
Post by: geowrld on June 15, 2008, 09:20:10 AM
Finally tried this place and I have to say that I am, sadly, disappointed.  I live less than 2 blocks away and have been really excited about its opening - it's taken me so long because I just haven't been in a wine mood lately.  That being said here are my comments:

1.  It's GORGEOUS!  It's so beautiful - it's so totally different than anything else here in Astoria.  I'm very impressed with the atmosphere.
2.  We decided to get a bottle of white but I didn't want anything sweet - so I asked the waitress for advice.  While I wasn't expecting the expertise of a sommelier I think it's reasonable to expect the wait staff to really KNOW the characteristics of the very small wine list they have.  She seemed confused by my question and could only recommend 2 out of 13 white wines that wouldn't be sweet.  Really?  Out of 13 whites only 2 weren't sweet?  And here's the kicker:  the wine she recommended that we chose was SUPER fruity - not sweet, really, but over the top fruity - really should have been told that before hand.
3.  We ordered 2 apps and a mixed platter of meats and cheese. 
       a.  App 1 was bacon wrapped prunes.  I had these as a mise en bouche in France last year and loved them so much I make them at my dinner parties now.  These were good - my bf said he liked mine better.  I would recommend these even though they didn't knock my socks off.
       b.  App 2 was Tiger shrimp in garlic and pepper sauce.  WOW.  That sounds good doesn't it?  It really wasn't that great.  First off, they weren't tiger shrimp - too small for that.  Secondly, the sauce was basically watered down wing sauce - I KID YOU NOT.  I think there were MAYBE 3 shrimp on the plate (covered with a lot of garnish) and this cost $10!  There was also a sliced roll for dipping and it tasted freezer-burnt.
       c.  The platter.  They have these meat and cheese platters for $4 to $6 each and you can combine any 3 for $15.  We ordered smoked duck, maple ham, and a French cheese called Petit Basque.  Everything on the platter tasted great.  The smoked duck was sublime, the ham was sliced perfectly (I believe thin slicing enhances the ham tasting experience), and the cheese was quite nice.  HOWEVER, for $15 I was shocked by the small portions.  There were only 2 very, very small slices of cheese on the platter - they were about the size of a small tortilla chip in dimensions.  Is this worth $6 (the price of the cheese plate by itself)?  The cheese was good but that was ridiculous. 

I think this place is beautiful and I think they have some worthy foods.  I would not recommend this place to anyone watching their budget because I don't think it's a good value.  If you have a fair amount of disposable income to blow on a night out to try a pretty place I say go for it.  Otherwise, I don't think I'll be talking this place up.

I was there last night with a couple of friends and we had a great time!  We tried a number of wines and all were absolutely delicious.  I drank more than I usually do, because of how good they were.

I can't tell you what my favorite wine was because they were all excellent.  We had a sparkling white, two reds, a two dessert wines.  I would say that the most striking was the dark red dessert wine from Uruguay we had (that's the Tannat that tennat refers to above).  We ate it with bits of walnut bread, blue cheese, walnuts and red grapes, and the combination was phenomenal.  My friends and I all agreed that the wine would have been great on vanilla bean ice cream.

Food-wise, my favorite things both involved bacon - the mac and cheese topped with bacon and the bacon-wrapped prunes.  I also liked the gasm dip and the lavash was a nice touch.  Also remarkable was the molten chocolate cake with spearmint gelato.  We loved how the mint gelato complemented the chocolate cake.

I walked home tipsy and happy.  Was concerned a little that I would pass out so hard that it would be difficult to get up this morning for my marathon training run, but I had a really good 4 miles!  I believe the wine was of such good quality that it was not conducive to the evil hangover (I am a lightweight when it comes to drinking). 

One other thing that I absolutely loved was that the music was played at a reasonable level.  We could hear it alright, but we could also easily hear each other in conversation.  For me, this is very important.  I don't do well in places with super loud music, so this was a pleasant surprise.

The space was very nice and I felt comfortable there.  All the suggestions and information given to us during the evening were really good - they really know their stuff.  Thank you, Dean.

So, check it out!  I recommend it.
