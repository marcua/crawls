Roundabout the beginning of high school fed up with more religion classes (seriously if i went through 8 years of this day in day out, do i really need to relearn the holy days of obligation? and i had classmates who thought thanksgiving was one...so i guess that's why) i started reading about other religions. i thought they were all so interesting, and i loved reading about ancient religions like druidism, and wicca since the catholic church stole most of it to co opt it into their religion to make it easier to convert (look you can still have your holidays, just now you're celebrating one god instead of 10). i started noticing how close the words good and god are and evil and devil. the birth of the sun god coinciding with christmas, the birth of the son of god and i started wondering if this wasn't all just made up. then as i got more "wordly" understanding the gay community, unwed pregnant mothers with no recourse and what not i started to think the church had a very unchristian like way of interacting with the entire community. but still through high school i considered myself agnostic not yet atheist.
then comes faith and critical reasoning at Fordham...oddly taught by a rabbi. this was the class where i questioned every last thing the professor ever told us. and she could never give me an answer. my classmates would tell me whenever they saw me that they loved the questions that i would ask and that i would basically play stump the professor. there were a few times where i told her that her answer was not good enough. i wrote at least 2 papers about the bible really being just a way to subjucate women and certain parts of society and inflicting a contradictory moral code on it's followers. the more and more this woman spoke the more and more i questioned her (and for this class i actually had to read the bible from the start and i did...i've read most of it at this point, not all but most so i could quote it at her). the more i questioned her the more i questioned religion and the more i came to believe (this is my own personal opinion and is by no way an insult to any one who follows) that religion, especially christianity is bullshit. i can't believe in a god who says kill gay people but then his son says love everyone. god loves everyone...but i'm going to make you kill your son to see if you love me more than him. i mean seriously, what the hell? almost everything in the bible is a contradiction and the followers are left to choose which one to follow. one catholic will follow the love everyone teachings of jesus. other catholics will quote leviticus and say these are the people god says we can hate AND we can kill them. he says so (it really does say that god commands his followers to kill those who lie with their own sex...it also applies to animals and your relatives). the professor gave us a survey at the beginning of the term and one to fill out after our final. both identical. her theory was that her students come out with a deeper understanding of religion and they turn more into believers at the end. mine was all full of basically what i have said. knowing i was an agnostic anyway she asked me about the class when i handed in my final...i was the first to finish and the whole class stopped to hear what i would say "Rabbi Strauss, when i started i was an agnostic questioning everything about religion. Now that i've had your class, i am convinced there is no god, religion is a crock and i am now thoroughly an atheist.    Thanks!"

i think one is quite capable of having a moral compass and know the difference between right and wrong. i don't need a religion to tell me that. i don't concern myself with what happens after i die. i'm dead, there's no more thinking. rather than live for a glorious afterlife, i choose to live a beautiful life. if it's something you need then great for you. it's just not for me. i worship the human spirit, at the altar of myself. i celebrate its endurance and its beauty. i have no room for god in my life. if i did i would overlook all the beauty and bounty of nature and my fellow man. not to mention myself.

Dr. Laura Schlesinger is a radio personality who dispenses advice to people who call in to her radio show. Recently, she said that, as an observant Orthodox Jew, homosexuality is an abomination according to Leviticus 18:22 and cannot be condoned under any circumstance.

The following is an open letter to Dr. Laura penned by an East Coast resident, which was posted on the Internet. It is funny, as well as informative.

Quote
Dear Dr. Laura:

Thank you for doing so much to educate people regarding G-d's Law. I have learned a great deal from your show, and try to share that knowledge with as many people as I can. When someone tries to defend the homosexual lifestyle, for example, I simply remind him or her that Leviticus 18:22 clearly states it to be an abomination. End of debate. I do need some advice from you, however, regarding some of the other specific laws and how to follow them.

When I burn a bull on the altar as a sacrifice, I know it creates a pleasing odor for the Lord - Lev.1:9. The problem is my neighbors. They claim the odor is not pleasing to them. Should I smite them?

I would like to sell my daughter into slavery, as sanctioned in Exodus 21:7. In this day and age, what do you think would be a fair price for her?

I know that I am allowed no contact with a woman while she is in her period of menstrual uncleanliness - Lev.15:19-24. The problem is how do I tell? I have tried asking, but most women take offense.

Lev. 25:44 states that I may indeed possess slaves, both male and female, provided they are purchased from neighboring nations. A friend of mine claims that this applies to Mexicans, but not Canadians. Can you clarify? Why can t I own Canadians?

I have a neighbor who insists on working on the Sabbath. Exodus 35:2 clearly states he should be put to death. Am I morally obligated to kill him myself?

A friend of mine feels that even though eating shellfish is an abomination - Lev. 11:10, it is a lesser abomination than homosexuality. I do not agree. Can you settle this?

Lev. 21:20 states that I may not approach the altar of G-d if I have a defect in my sight. I have to admit that I wear reading glasses. Does my vision have to be 20/20, or is there some wiggle room here?

Most of my male friends get their hair trimmed, including the hair around their temples, even though Lev. 19:27 expressly forbids this. How should they die?

I know from Lev. 11:6-8 that touching the skin of a dead pig makes me unclean, but may I still play football if I wear gloves?

My uncle has a farm. He violates Lev. 19:19 by planting two different crops in the same field, as does his wife by wearing garments made of two different kinds of thread (cotton/polyester blend). He also tends to curse and blaspheme a lot. Is it necessary that we go to all the trouble of getting the whole town together to stone them? - Lev.24:10-16. Couldn't we just burn them to death at a private family affair like the one we do with people who sleep with their in-laws? (Lev.20:14)

I know you have studied these things extensively, so I am confident you can help. Thank you again for reminding us that G-d's word is eternal and unchanging.

Your devoted fan,
Moshe
