I have had 2 experiences with the 114, neither particularly good.

The first was about 3 years ago, I came home alone late at night and discovered a homeless looking guy sitting in the vestibule of my building, sort of hidden under the stairs. When he saw me coming, he picked up a newspaper and tried to look non-chalant. As it was about 2 AM, I was really freaked out, though he didn't try to talk to me or follow me or anything. He was probably just hanging out, but it was weird to have a strange man sitting in the building in the middle of the night, sort of trying to stay out of sight. I called the 114 as soon as I got inside and asked that they send a patrol car over to check it out. They told me to call 911. I told them that it wasn't a real emergency, he was just sitting there, nobody was hurt. The lady on the phone insisted I should call 911, so I did. They were put out that I was wasting their time with a non-emergency and told me to call the precinct. UGH!

The second time was much more frightening. I had been doing laundry at the place on Bway and 35th st, and was walking back to my apartment. All of a sudden, two teenagers, a boy and a girl came running around the corner, him chasing her. They were yelling. He caught up to her, literally grabbed her by the throat and threw her up against the metal gate covering the door of a closed business. Everyone on the street started yelling and about 3 of us pulled out cellphones and dialed 911. She tried to hit him back, got out of his grip and ran down the street. He chased her. 3 cops cars came about 2 minutes later and asked where they had gone. We pointed to where the kid had run. One cop asked where the girl was, we said we weren't sure, but that they should go pick up the kid who was beating the crap out of her. They said they would not go look for him until they found her and asked if she wanted to press charges. We responded that we were all witnesses to him assaulting her, were willing to give statements and they should just go arrest him. They refused. Most of them dawdled on the corner while one of the patrol cars went to find the girl. In the end, they found her, she did not want to press charges and everyone went home. I guess they see that stuff often, but their apathy made me really angry.

I've only dealt with the po-po in Astoria once when my brother's car was broken into overnight in front of my building (29th Street b/t 31st and 30th Avenues) last summer.  They smashed the passenger side window open, took his ipod, and took off.  Luckily, they didn't snoop around his car too much, as there was some cash in the glove compartment, a dime bag in the middle console, and a laptop in a backpack on the backseat.  Yeah...my brother isn't the brightest bulb sometimes.  Anyway, I called 114th over and over again, and of course, it rang off the hook.  I didn't think calling 911 to report a broken window and relatively minor burglary was necessary, but once I couldn't get through to the precinct, I finally did call 911 and it took about 2 hours for a squad car to come.  The 2 cops looked to be younger than I am (I'm 26) and while they weren't outright rude, they obviously couldn't care less and had an overall "holier than thou" attitude.  I felt like I was reporting a crime to two frat boys.  They told my brother there was nothing they could do (not even an incident report of any kind) and that insurance would probably cover the cost to repair the window, which is usually a few hundred bucks at most and can be done in your own driveway.  They drove off and the whole exchange probably took two minutes, tops.  All was over, done with, and well.

However, while not in Astoria, my purse was stolen by two a**holes in a bar once downtown (Spring Street).  After I immediately cancelled my credit cards, disactivated my cell phone, and had my fiance come pick me up in a cab (since I had no money, metro card, phone, or keys), I decided against calling the police since I figured there was nothing they could do.  Lo and behold, later that night, I received a phone call at my apartment from a cop in the West Village's precinct who was as sweet as can be.  He let me know that they found my wallet in the bathroom of some bar...it had no money, ID, or credit cards in it, just some business cards and my dry cleaning receipt, which was how he got my number.  He offered to hold onto it for me until I could go down there and pick it up, which I thought was surprisingly nice of him.  There was nothing of value in it (it was even a cheap TJ Maxx wallet) but he called me anyway to tell me that they had it.  I had messages from him on my work number the next morning from him as well (he got that # from one of my business cards).  

I guess the moral of the story is that yes, cops can be power-tripping and rude sometimes, but there are good ones out there too.  It must have been nice "back in the day" when the cops would walk around, know everyone, being active community members, but I don't think that type of behavior exists much anymore, unfortunately.
