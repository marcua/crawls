A man was found murdered early this morning inside Astoria Park and cops are questioning two people in connection with the crime.

Ruben Vargas, 32, was found by police unconscious inside the park at 19th Street near Ditmars Avenue, about 3:40 a.m. this morning, said police.

A 28-year-old woman and her 31-year-old male companion were found nearby and they were taken in for questioning, said police.

The NYC Medical Examiner has determined that Vargas had been strangled and struck in the head and his death has been ruled a homicide, police said.

The woman and her male acquaintance were still being questioned at the 114th Precinct this afternoon but they have not been charged with a crime, police said. Cops are still working to determine a motive for the murder.

When I was younger -- late teens or so -- we would all drive our cars and hang out on Shore Blvd. It's an Astoria tradition. I'd like to think, though, that we were more respectful than the people down there now. It was back in the day when the guidos would drive their Monte Carlos and Camaros slowly along the strip to check out the girls. Maybe I was just a dumb teen, but it seemed much safer back then -- no one was looking for trouble, just someplace to go and hang out on a hot summer night. The rocker guys also had their areas, and there was rarely any trouble. Music has always blasted from the cars, but there was more of a respect for authority -- if the cops came and said to turn it down, it was turned down.

I know I find myself getting grouchier at the ripe old age of 40, and maybe I am overreacting to the new kids that are hanging on the strip, but things really do seem to have gotten worse lately. Such a shame. It's a great area to walk down in the evening, but I just don't feel as safe as I used to.
