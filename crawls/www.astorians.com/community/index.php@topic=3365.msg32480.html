Christine, how many days a week do you bike to work and how far is it? My concerns are expense, showing up sweaty and gross at work, unexpected bad weather and of course the risk of injury. What are your thoughts?

I bike as often as possible when it's not raining (although I'm working up the courage to bike in the rain.) My commute is only about four miles each way into the city. If I ride every day, that's automatically 40 miles/week under my belt between the hours of 8am-6pm.

What's nice is that I work right in Central Park, so after work, I do laps. Each lap is about six miles. My best week I did a total of 100 miles (commuting + laps.) This has helped me stay in good shape for mountain bike races- I ride my mtb instead of a road bike for the added challenge. At first I didn't think riding on pavement would do much, but when I did a 40-mile mtb race on a tough course last month, I felt pretty good and even placed second in my division  :-)

As for not being too sweaty: I'll take a quick shower, dress in bike clothes, and pack work clothes. I also pack makeup and moist towelettes, and keep a pair of shoes at work (though it's casual and I can get away with black Sketchers.) I get to work and allow about 10-20 min. to stop sweating and cool down (depending on heat/humidity.)

You have to be careful and pay close attention, but city traffic isn't much faster than a bike anyway  The rush is addictive once you get used to it.
