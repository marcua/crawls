Title: Re: Absinthe!
Post by: lanseaux on April 06, 2006, 07:14:45 PM
What I've been doing for the past few years is declaring that I have food, and make sure I have enough boring stuff* so that their eyes glaze over before you get to anything remotely interesting, they cut to the chase and say, "do you have any meat?"  "Have you been on a farm?" "Do you have any vegetables?"  I look very serious and shocked and say, "no, no, no," very patiently.  Then they say have a nice day and wave me on.

I know that Jayme just didn't declare anything, so that can work, too.  That's what I used to do.  Not sure why I switched.  Jeffrey Steingarten believes firmly that at JFK, they don't care about the raw cheese issue.  He always declares in large capital letters on his customs form that he has "RAW MILK CHEESE (AGED LESS THAN 60 DAYS).  He's never been stopped, and I doubt it's because he's batted his eyelashes.

*coming back from Mexico, this consists of hot sauce, tortilla chips, spicy potato chips, and tequila.  Oh, and raw fish, which is allowed, at least from Baja, if you catch it yourself.  Not that I bring back Camembert from Mexico, but there's been random things.  Coming back from England, it begins with Marmite and lemon curd, and proceeds through tea and biscuits.  I never even got to the Harrod's chocolates, and I sailed through with my fine raw milk cheeses.  I have not yet used this technique coming back from France, but I've used it successfully at LAX and JFK.
