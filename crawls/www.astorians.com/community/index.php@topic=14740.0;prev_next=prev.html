making ice cream
« on: July 02, 2006, 12:49:14 PM »
or, the time I learned about benefits of denaturing proteins.

Made ice cream last night - vanilla bean - in the "Philadephia" or "American" style, meaning no eggs/egg yolks were used.  I heated the combination of heavy cream and half and half, flavored with vanilla bean and a little vanilla extract and some sugar.  I did this once before, but had used unheated dairy products and the resulting ice cream had a bunch of ice crystals in it.  This time, what a great texture!  Not as silky cream smooth as ice cream with the eggs, but a smooth and pleasing texture just the same.  And no ice crystals, thanks to heating up the dairy.  It reminded me of Breyer's ice cream (I think it's called Edy's out here, or maybe that is the alternate to Dreyer's; I get them mixed up).  Very pure flavor and true "iced cream."  I think it's a good base for additions.  The base ingredients are:

2 c. heavy cream
1 c. half and half
1 c. sugar (although I might lower it to 3/4)
1 vanilla bean
1 T vanilla extract (optional but I love the flavor of vanilla and like the little bit of alcohol to help with texture)

Combine and heat until 170 degrees.  Refrigerate until cool and freeze according to ice cream maker instructions.

I'm going to make this again.   I am thinking that putting a cinnamon stick or some cardamom pods in with the mixture could be tasty, too.  Thought about adding finely chopped candied ginger to the mix, too.

Another ice cream idea I read about has to do with combining equal parts of cream of coconut and milk (rice milk is a possibility for a vegan ice cream) and dumping it in the ice cream maker.  I think I'd heat it, too, at least the milk.  Could be quite delicious!

Today I made a chocolate cinnamon ice cream.  Talk about lucious!  The texture was so amazing - really smooth and creamy, as if it had been made with eggs.  I can't get over it.  My recipe is as follows:

2 c. heavy cream
1 c. half and half
1 c. sugar
3/4 c. cocoa
1 cinnamon stick
1/2 tsp. ground cinnamon
1 tsp. vanilla extract

Again, I combined all the ingredients (sifted the cocoa into the dairy so as to avoid lumpiness) and heated them to 170 degrees.  Then put the mixture in a 13x9 inch pan in the fridge and cooled it.  It cooled down much faster (took about an hour) than it did in a bowl, as I used for the vanilla ice cream. 

Churned it in the ice cream maker for 25 minutes, where at the 25 minute mark is was very thick.  My vanilla wasn't that thick.  I wonder if it had to do with how cold the ice cream base was before I put it in the ice cream maker?

This is the best chocolate ice cream I've had since lanseaux's chocolate wasabi ice cream last summer.
