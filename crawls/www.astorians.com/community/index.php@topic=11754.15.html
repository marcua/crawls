Ah, if you've lived here over 30 years (like I have), yeah, then you could.  You could even have had slices in places that don't even exist anymore!!

But I do agree with Sac's as the #1 choice.  I would follow that though with Polito's Pizza (on Broadway between Steinway and 38th Street).  They have the best "traditional" pizza in the neighborhood.  Sac's is more of a specialty pizza; it's really in a class all by itself.

Rizzo's gets props for being unique, but I never cared for it as much as the traditional triangle slices.  But that's just me.

I think the first poster, Plank, hit the nail on the head. Sac's Place has great pizza if you get it in the restaurant. The pizza in the slice area is also great, but it has nothing on the whole pies. I can finish a 10" pie by myself, so I would do that. I could also easily share a 10" pie and have some salad on the side.

Rizzo's is also terrific, but a bit different. Like Sac's, the pizza is thin crust, but the sauce is different. The people there are great. When I was there for the first time and they found out I'd never had their pizza before, they gave me a free slice. They are proud of what they make, and rightly so.
