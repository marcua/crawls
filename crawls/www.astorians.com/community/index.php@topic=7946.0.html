I know people have posted about this before, but the stray cat problem in Astoria and other areas of NYC is something that can be prevented by the (100% humane) TNR (Trap, Neuter, Return) program.  Basically, strays tend to become feral (therefore, usually not socialized properly and not easily adoptable if ever brought into a shelter), which only becomes worse if they are not spayed or neutered.  The breeding continues and the problem never goes away, it only grows. 

For more info, you can see  http://www.nycferalcat.org/ - they might come straight to your neighborhood to provide the TNR program.  I know the ASPCA also offers free instructional sessions to anyone willing to learn how to stop and possibly prevent future stray cat issues in their area.

Also, www.neighborhoodcats.org is a good resource on how to learn more.  I know it's sometimes good to have "mousers" around to control the pest problems so many of us have, but not neutering or spaying an animal can really be cruel in the long run.

Just thought this might help!
