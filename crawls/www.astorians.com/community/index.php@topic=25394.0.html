Re: Cafe Francis on Ditmars
« Reply #6 on: October 01, 2013, 12:06:06 PM »
Had a wonderful lunch yesterday at Cafe Francis - stayed for two hours with friends who brought their (well behaved) baby and all the staff were very welcoming & accommodating. I had excellent vegetable appetizers (beets, celery remoulade and lentils) and the blanquette de veau, which was one of the specials. Friends had the croque madame and crêpes, which were rated very highly. Possibly because we lingered for so long over coffee, they brought us some tiny complimentary vanilla crèmes brûlée, which were lovely!


We were told that the place is owned by Francis Staub (who owns the cookware company), who also owns 2 outposts of Le Gamin in Williamsburg and Ft. Greene. The Francis menu is adapted from their menus.


This is all borne out by a quirky profile of him in the NYer this summer: http://www.newyorker.com/talk/2013/07/01/130701ta_talk_parker, which says "he plans to open a restaurant in Queens, which he calls 'the countryside.'" Ha! Sounds like quite the eccentric, but not an unwelcome influence on the neighborhood.


They're still waiting on the liquor license, but they said they'd be starting dinner service at the end of the week.


I can't wait to try the rest of the menu!

We were there on Friday evening and had a really nice time and a delicious meal.  It was rather empty so there was plenty of time to chat with the woman who served us, but we refrained from getting into the whole "what the hell took so long".  But sure enough, another couple came in we could easily hear them venting their anger about how the place sat closed for so long.  When she returned to our table we said we shared those feelings and were curious...well - it sounds as though that first buyer sort of vanished or had a change of heart and it took a while for a new transaction to take hold.  Who knows what the story is?   I'm glad they're finally open.  It adds life to that corner.  Now - the place is very nice.  Cute decor but there are minor issues.  The music was so soft that those of us there felt the need to whisper or it would seem like we were shouting.   The chairs feel a bit like torture devices unless you're more on the dainty side.  Still no liquor license but coming very soon.  As is delivery.  The food was very delicious.  I took it in stride but there was not a leaf of spinach to be had in the spinach ravioli, but it was a delicious dish - French in style and taste and a beautiful presentation.  I asked and was told they realized they had no spinach on hand or forgot to include it.  I appreciated the honestly and it was funny in its own way.  We both had fish dishes that were superb.  My wild salmon over lentils was perfect.  Also had an amazing apple tart.  I hope they work out the minor kinks soon.  Though I think not having a website is odd and almost unforgiveable today.  Even if they really are "new owners", they're not exactly snapping to it when it comes to brining this place up to speed.  But again - I'm not looking to be a downer on the place.  It's very delicious and very geniune French cuisine and it's a fantastic addition to some great newcomers to Ditmars!
