Whats up all?! I'm looking for an apartment in Astoria. Do most of the apartments come with wood floors? I've had a broker tell me if I'm looking for non-wood floors then Astoria may not be for me.

I'm looking for good ole cheap tile sitting on top of concrete.     If anyone has leads or knows what types of buildings to look into please let me know. Thanks

Our place has both carpeting and tile - carpet in the living room and bedrooms and tile in the kitchen and dining room (oh, and bathroom).  I wasn't sure how I'd feel about the carpet, after living for 20 years with wood floors, but so far I like it quite a bit.  We replaced the living room carpet shortly after we moved in and watched the installation process - there are wood floors underneath but they are in pretty gnarly condition and it would have taken quite some time and $$$ to bring them up to an even "passable" level.

So yes, there are apartments out there that do not have wood floors and use alternate floor coverings, here in Astoria.

Most coops will have requirements of 70% floors covering with carpeting.  Most coop owners i know usully not follow that rule unless a cosomeome complains.  If you are an owner but have decided to rent out your unit it would be the wiser to carpet the floors so as ti avoid complaints about your tenants. There are plenty of apartments with laminate flooring out there but rarely see carpeting.

Outside of vinyl tiles tiling can be as expensive or even more expensive thay hardwood flooring.  Ceramic or porcelain  tiling is more expensive labor wise than hardwood floors.  Obviously a hardwood floor will typically look more attractive but thats because most people are afraid of artistic designs on flooring.
