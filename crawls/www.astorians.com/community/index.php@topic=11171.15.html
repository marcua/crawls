I had the little buggers (no pun intended) when we first moved in 4 years ago and haven't seen them since I experienced the Great Termination Weekend of 2004...

Bugs (not the huge cockroaches, but smaller brown ones) were EVERYWHERE, esp. in kitchen and bathroom.  Near the sink, bathtub, etc.  They came through the living room and into the bedroom too.  Gross.  Raid, traps, and caulk didn't work that well, they always found a way in.  I used to chase them down with a hammer and pound them to their death.  I eventually went to the hardware store on the corner (28th Street and 30th Avenue) and received some sage advice involving Boric Acid powder.  Here's what I did, and it was VERY effective:

- Bought a bottle of BORIC ACID powder...it was yellow with a red, cone-shaped, "nozzle"-like top. 
- Bottle was only about halfway full when purchased, so I put on some gloves, covered my mouth/nose with a painter's mask, unscrewed the top and filled the rest (to about 80% full) with sugar.  Replaced the top and shook it up.
- I sprinkled the Boric Acid/Sugar mixture all over behind my stove and under my sink (went underneath and shook it far away- not near any of my pots/pans/cleaning supplies - more against the wall). 
- I left town for 2 days to pick up a few things....when I returned....there had to be about 3-4 dozen of the little suckers, all belly up on my kitchen floor.  I sweeped them up, threw 'em out, mopped the floor, and have not had a single problem since. 

I have since gotten a cat but just blocked off the small space between my stove and the wall so he can't get through and get to any of the remnants.  I highly recommend doing this if you are safe about it...even just in your kitchen.  That's where they are attracted to and come from. 

Hope this helped!

YaFace has the right attitude.  You really should devote a weekend before you move in to really bring the pain.  I always tell people who move to NYC that no matter how nice the apt is, or how "cute" the building looks from the outside, how how "nice" and "clean" you neighbors/neighborhood seems, always clean, disinfect, bomb, etc. as if you were moving into a crackhouse.  Roaches are your enemy, and should be treated as such.  Don't start psyching yourself out by saying things like "oh, well they have the right to live just like any of gods creatures" or "they can't help it that they are disgusting little menaces".  Make no mistake, even their mere existence can make you ill.  In fact, roach droppings are the reason that children in NYC are more likely to develop respiratory diseases such as asthma than children in other parts of the US.

YaFace had some great ideas, but there are a couple tactics that many people neglect, but that I will share with you today.

1)  Go to the HomeDepot and buy this drain cleaner that I can't remember the name of.  It come in a big black jug and is sealed in a thick mylar bag, you can't miss it.  If you don't see it, ask one of the employees what is the strongest, most brutal drain cleaner they carry, and he/she will undoubtedly point forebodingly at it.  While you are there, pick up some rubber gloves and goggles if you don't already have some. (I know, I know.  I like to support small business as much as the next guy, but I have never come across a hardware store that has this wonderful stuff)

Before doing to work, make sure you are wearing the above mentioned accessories as this product is lye based and highly toxic.  Dump it down the drain and cover the drain with something big and flat like a pot to prevent eruption.  After 15 - 20 minutes you may be morbidly surprised.  After I first used this stuff, I removed the pot covering the drain to reveal a roach lying on its back, barely alive, but obviously in agony.  The lye had begun eating away at it's disgusting exoskeleton. I watched it twitch and squirm for a couple of minutes, then I mercilessly picked it up being very careful not to deliver the deathblow, and threw it outside as an example to those who dare follow.

2.)  Your stove.  This may be ground zero as many people rarely, if ever, clean behind or under their stove.  If you have access to it it then shut off you gas.  Otherwise command your landlord to do so.  Unhook and remove your stove and bleach everything.  Lift up the range cover and do the same. 

Further, and I can't stress this enough, get a screwdriver and remove the back of your stove.  One time I did this and found a horrifying roach graveyard.  There were so many corpses plastered to 20 years of caked on grease that you could not even see the surface.  In this case I my landlord was obligated under NYS law to buy me a new stove.  If you have the same problem and your landlord puts up a fuss, fu*k  him.  He is also your enemy and should be treated as such.  Threaten to take him to court and he will fold as they all do.

I hope this helps.  Remember, ther is no reason you should have to share your apartment with these creatures.  All it takes is a little hard work and a reasonable amount of bloodlust.  You may even begin to like it.  Good luck and happy hunting
