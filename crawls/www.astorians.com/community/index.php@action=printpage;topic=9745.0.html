Yeah, even in its final few years, up until it was sold in 2004, LIR was still far far better than almost anything on the commercial radio dial.  Even if it was not what it once was in the 80s (which I missed out on as I was too young, but have heard in audio clips and "flashback" weekends LIR did later on)....it played lots and lots of artists that are not heard on commercial radio in the United States, and still broke out the obscure 80s new wave as well.  Plus, the station had personality all day and all night, in an era where more and more stations are eliminating the DJ or voicetracking (pre-recording the DJ's words, then inserting them between songs in such a way that they sound like they are on the air "live").

Pulse 87.7 is a good station, by today's standards, though it's dance-specific.  They've already put together almost a complete lineup of DJ's...whereas in the past couple of months, there have been mass firings at K-Rock, CD101.9 (now RXP), Lite FM, Fresh 102.7, PLJ, WABC, WFAN, and other stations).

Funny thing about them...87.7 is actually the audio portion of Channel 6 TV.  Anywhere you go in the country, if there is a channel 6, you can hear its audio on 87.7 FM.  In Pulse 87's case, Channel 6 is a low-power TV station, and quite frankly, the TV signal is pretty awful....I'm not sure all of Astoria can even receive it, even though they broadcast from Citicorp in LIC.  So they are using the audio only as a radio station, and the video currently just has test bars, I think, though there is word that they might put a studio cam or music videos (synced with the music on the station) on the video portion soon.

Gotta commend them for thinking out of the box and getting something different on the air.  Now if only 92.7 LIR could make a return....  :-D
Title: Re: 101.9 went from smooth jazz to rock
Post by: neo11 on March 06, 2008, 03:34:00 PM
Ah, a couple of more things...LIR definitely did break out music long before other stations did, right up to the end.  I recall, for instance, they were playing Maroon 5's first hit a year and a half before Z100 broke it as "new" music. 

LIR's signal unfortunately wasn't that great in Astoria.  It was actually because it was broadcasting from the Queens-Nassau border (North Shore Towers...those tall buildings you see by the Grand Central Parkway), but with only 2000 watts, compared to the 6000 or so watts the stations from the Empire State Building put out.  Plus, Astoria being right next to Manhattan is blanketed really well with the signals from the ESB, the noise of which would drown out 'LIR.

I remember my home stereo on Ditmars Blvd. would get them with a fairly clear signal but always in mono.

Interestingly, as you headed towards Upper Manhattan and places like Fort Lee, NJ, or south towards Staten Island, the signal would come in clearer again, as the Empire State Building signals would begin to weaken as you went further away from it.

As for the music on CD101.9...that was NOT jazz.  Phil Collins?  Booker T?  Marvin Gaye?  Smooth, perhaps, but not jazz.  It's really just a "brand name" that radio stations across the country use, without having much to do with reality. 

If anyone is into "real" jazz...there's non-commercial stations like 88.3 and 89.9 in the NYC area and 90.1 on Long Island which play heavy doses of it...especially Jazz 88.3 out of Newark.

Well I'm not really in the "biz" but the work I do is related to radio.  I was also involved at my college radio station for four years and still help them out from time to time even though I've graduated.

101.9 did sort of switch formats a couple of years ago, like you mentioned.  They called it "chill" and it really sounded pretty funky.  The ratings didn't dip either, and if anything, they were probably pulling in younger listeners which the advertisers want.  For whatever reason though, they went back to the old smooth jazz format.

Rocky Allen got canned, unfortunately.  WPLJ was sold recently from ABC Radio to Citadel, along with the rest of the ABC radio stations across the country.  Citadel spent big bucks to buy those stations, and now they've discovered, lo and behold, that they spent too much.

So instead of firing the CEO who led them through that acquisition....they're just firing people left and right at every station they own.  I hear a whole station got canned down in Atlanta, and all of CD 101.9's airstaff was fired as well, even though some of them had rock radio experience in the past and would have probably fit in to the new format.  Sad.  Gambling got canned too....apparently he was pulling in ratings but not enough ad revenue to match his apparently high salary.  Radio is becoming a minimum-wage business...seriously.  Most people on the radio nowadays are doing double duty behind the scenes...promotions, voiceover work, all sorts of other stuff just to make ends meet....it's a horrible business to be in nowadays.
