I worked in an opthalmology lab for 3 years studying diseases caused by contact lenses.  Our main effort was to find cures other than a cornea transplant.  The lab was in Tennessee but was actually funded by George Steibrenner's wife...good to see (sorry for the pun  :lol:) 

All I can say is DO NOT WEAR CONTACTS.  I wear glasses and I will not even consider wearing contacts.  There is going to be a major shortage of corneas in the very near future because an overwhelming majority of contact lense wearers will need cornea transplants.  The need for a transplant is due to a variety of reasons, some were touched upon in that short article.  The packages that the lenses are sold in are not always sterile.  Your hands are rarely, if ever sterile.  A brand new lense from an unopened package could have the "bugs" already in them.  Add all of that to improper usage of the lenses and you have a prescription for disaster.  When my friends tell me they fell asleep with their lenses on I can only cringe in disbelief.   

I don't mean to frighten anyone, but please please take proper care when wearing contact lenses.

---end of public service message...

I'm coming into this rather late, but here's my 2 cents.

I had horrible eye sight growing up and started wearing contacts at age 11.  I had three horrible eye viruses throughout the years, all due to contact lense wear.  By age 18 my prescriptions stabilized and we started seriously talking about lasik.  At this point I could barely see how many fingers were put up in front of my face without contacts or glasses. 

So I had the surgery when I turned 20.  My parents and I did a lot of research and got a lot of recommendations before and we spent a little more because we chose a world class eye center in Miami.  (Although the insurance did not cover the surgery, my parents took advantage of the tax-free spending account for medical purposes so at least there was some benefit).   Yes it was uncomfortable, yes it was scary (and smelled bad as was mentioned earlier), and yes, they popped a blood vessel while doing my second eye so I felt everything.  Even with all of that I still say, yes, it was worth it when I woke up the next day and could see perfectly.  Four years later, I still have 20/20 vision and haven't had any issues since.
