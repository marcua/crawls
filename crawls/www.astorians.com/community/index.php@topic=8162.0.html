To add to what everyone else has been saying, there's also a Mediterranean Foods (same owner as the one on 34th street) on 31st Street just south of 23rd Avenue, in the Agora Plaza shopping center.

I will definitely second the recommendation on Zenon's koupepia.  They make two other variations of them: on Fridays, they make a vegetarian version without the ground meat, and on another day, they make koupepia stuffed with chicken and rice instead of the usual beef/pork. 

Taramosalata and kopanisti are awesome too, as well as tsatsiki, all of which make a great spread to have alongside the dolmadakia (koupepia).  Taramosalata is made from tarama, or fish roe (like caviar), with lemon juice, garlic, oil, and sometimes other ingredients combined to make a smooth spread.  Kopanisti is a spicy feta cheese spread. 

Another great complement to dolmades is Greek yogurt.  All the Greek supermarkets sell it, as well as places like Key Food and Best Yet...look for the "Fage" brand in the dairy section.

Are you looking to buy the store-bought, canned variety, or something that's actually freshly made?  I get the impression that most supermarkets, even the Greek markets, that sell grape leaves in their deli section actually just sell the canned variety, but without the actual can.

Freshly made stuffed grape leaves are available at some Greek restaurants in the area...Zenon and S'Agapo come to mind, but surprisingly not Stamatis (where one would usually find stuffed cabbage instead).

As for the canned variety, any Greek or ethnic market (Titan, Mediterranean, Greek House, Angelo's, Parrot, Euro Market, etc.) would have several varieties, which are all fairly similar.  Many area supermarkets (Trade Fair, Best Yet, Key Food) would have some varieties as well in their ethnic foods aisle.
