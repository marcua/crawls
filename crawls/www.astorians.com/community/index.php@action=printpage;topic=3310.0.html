Title: Re: Primary Day?
Post by: mcdirk on September 08, 2006, 04:12:55 PM
I think Cuomo skipped DFNYC as the voting base was already closely aligned with Green and he didn't think he would have a competitive chance, but I always think it's a good idea to go even where you won't win, just so people meet you and hear your case.

I know all three candidates, Cuomo, Green and Maloney personally and like them all.  They have different skill sets and strengths. 

I have supported Mark Green for every past election he's contested in NYC (except his loss to Congress, which was before I moved to the city).  He was a good Public Advocate and would have made an outstanding Mayor.  That being said, he can be very arrogant.  He's smart and he knows it.  He is a good man and would make a good AG, but would probably conflict with the rest of the Statewide Democrats as he did in the city, especially with our hopeful next Governor Eliot Spitzer.  Green is honest and has a good record, but probably can't win this race.  His negative campaigning has really shocked me.  I would have liked to have seen him run a positive campaign.  Lord knows he has done enough positive things to run one.

Andrew Cuomo is also a good man.  He came across as someone with a sense of entitlement when he took on H. Carl McCall in the Gubernatorial primary four years ago, and I strongly opposed him.  I've gotten to know him much better since then and have found him to be both charming and intelligent.  His record at HUD was one of reform and achievement and he would also make an outstanding Attorney General.  He took the most troubled national agency and salvaged it, a feat for which he should be praised, not attacked.  Was his record perfect, no, but then no elected official ever has a perfect record.  He is among the most tenacious people I have ever met and he is well connected and would be able to marshall resources to take on any problem he might face as AG.  I really like him and suspect he will win big on Tuesday (probably by 10%).

Sean Patrick Maloney is also a great guy.  My Democratic Club, Stonewall Democrats of NYC, endorsed him and I've been campaigning for him.  He's smart, driven, ambitious and honest.  I think he has a bright future ahead of him.  This may not be his race, but a vote for him could send a message for reform.  Sean has raised better issues in this campaign than any of his opponents and I think he really wants the job, instead of wanting to keep his political career alive (as he doesn't really have one yet - he's a first time candidate). 

We will do well whomever wins the primary, but keep in mind, Jeanine Pirro, the Republican nominee is waiting to savage whomever wins our primary.  Mark and Sean don't have the funds to fight back against her, so in that sense, Cuomo may be our best bet, but Green and Maloney might be able to beat Pirro too.  Who you vote for depends on what you want in an Attorney General.  All three are very smart, but Green is an outsider, Cuomo a coalition builder and Maloney an investigator/litigator.  All three can make credible claims to being a reformer, but keep in mind, whoever the AG is, will put their stamp on the office.  Eliot Spitzers AG office looks very different from that of Dennis Vacco, Oliver Koppell or Robert Abrams, they all have their own issues and interests and that will help flavor their tenure.
