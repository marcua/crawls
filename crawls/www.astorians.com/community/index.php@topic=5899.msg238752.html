Well, I'm a generally unafraid person so perhaps my input isn't that applicable to all people, but in my experience people tend to mind their own business first and foremost, even if it's in "spotty" areas.  A lot of this boils down to being able to see if something really is a potential problem or if it's just something one can't relate to, for lack of better wording.

So for me I don't generally have a big problem with going through "spotty" hoods because people there do their own thing. And most of the time I try to remind myself that a lot of the unease I may feel is really about me looking and being different.

I'll also point out that a huge problem when it comes to safety is alcohol consumption. I live around 30'th / Steinway and the past two years there's always been police activity at least twice in the summer because of drunk and stupid people causing trouble over nothing. It's basically the crowd most of us wouldn't even consider a "threat" normally because they live in reasonable housing and look and act like "us", until they get drunk.

If there's one concern I would have about living close to for example Ravenswood it'd be a different culture that could be annoying yet not illegal enough to warrant police action. I'm thinking about playing loud music for example, things that are disturbing and not legal but that would possibly be low priority for the police to deal with. Not the end of the world but annoying when it intrudes on something you want to call home.

Just some food for thought.
