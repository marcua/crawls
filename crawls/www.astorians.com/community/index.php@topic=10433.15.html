There's a meetup now scheduled for some upcoming sunday mornings.  Look at the calendar to see if you could go.  There are some "big group" events too, on the weekend. Those are hard, sometimes, to meet up with others-- especially if you don't know too many in the group.

When you go to your first meetup, my advice for you is if you have not met anyone else in the group-- put your cellphone # on your rsvp (and take the organizer's!) so that you can find everyone and be found.

The problem with the organizers just making an event on a weekend without getting specific time/dates/places that are good for you is that it still won't be *perfect* for you.  It will be during nap time, you don't want to go to that park-- it's too far, you have plans that particular weekend, etc.  Weekend meetups must not be something that most of the organizers are able to host, right now.  So, volunteer to host!

If you're planning on going to a cafe/museum/playground on a weekend anyway, so why not make it an "event" by emailing the idea to an organizer?  You would be the host-- and it would be exactly when you want it, and where you want it. 

For instance: (sent to an organizer)
i'd like to host a park meetup
Date: Sat - April 11
Time: 11:00-12:30 (I have to leave then for lunch/nap)
Place: Astoria Park Playground--- I'll be by the benches near the swings
here's my cell # ___________ (and if you wanted to, you could ask for the other parents to call you so that you could definitely find each other if it was crowded)
my child is named and is _____ years old (if you want to meet other parents with same aged kids-- if not, then it does not matter)

The point of ASAHM and other meetup/playgroups whatever is for you to get to meet some other people, then make friends, and also get some playdates for your kids.  There are other working moms in the group, and moms whose husbands work on sat/sun and would love to go to the park or anywhere with some other parents.  So, suggest a date, and time, and place that works for you, and I'm sure that you will get to meet other parents in Astoria.

Your other option is to make your own thing-- start a free yahoo or google group for working moms in astoria-- make a standing weekend event: noon at astoria park, or brunch at a cafe that is baby friendly.  Even a quick coffee with other moms/parents once a week if time is an issue.  I'm sure that there are other people who would join you! 
 
The problem is that the ASAHM group *is* mostly stay at home moms.  The name does not lie.  I am a WOHM, but home by 4, and can go to late afternoon meetups.  I host some, and will go to park ones this spring, and sometimes host/go to events at night (without kids!)  And, you'd be surprised-- even with the many events during the week, there are SAHM members who say that there are still no dates/times that work for them-- you can't please everyone!

The dues cover the cost of the meetup site, and also helps pay for large group events.  All of the organizers are volunteers, and VERY busy moms- some SAHMs, some WAHM or WOHMs, but all BUSY just like you!

Hope to see you around, on a weekend meetup,
Sarah

Hi there kokowillis,

I am the organizer of the ASAHM group and I am the one who removed you from the group.

For the record, I have no idea who you are based on your screen name here--all our members are asked to provide their real name, so that is what I would know you by.

I do not appreciate, nor deserve, the insinuation that we remove anyone for any reason other than inactivity or for non-payment of dues. If you must know, this is something the organizers have been working on for a long time. I have recently taken over as group organizer, and we have been planning to get on track with our members' attendance for some time (which is why you were removed only recently even though you have not attended any events for months). I can assure you that I sent everyone a "warning" email last week--if you'd like, you can email me with your name and I will gladly forward you your copy. It was a difficult decision to make to institute this "attendance policy," but in the end, we're all glad that we did.

As for your statement about it being better if the group is large, there is no sense in having 215 members when 60 of them are inactive. I would rather have a group of 150 moms who all know each other, than 300 moms who have never met. We are, after all, a MEET UP group. We are a group of moms that are in it to become friends, know one another, and build a strong parenting community. We share more than play dates--we share our, and our children's, lives. When we continuously build numbers without participation, then we run the risk of becoming an anonymous online group, and none of our very active members are happy with that. And it is an absolutely true statement that even with the number and versatility of events that we have, many SAHM mothers still cannot find the time to fit us in. We shouldn't have to beg anyone to come to our events--you joined out of your own accord. You get out of the group exactly what you put into it.

As for our initial "closed door policy," that decision was also made after much difficult deliberation, and was made only because at the time, the number of members exceeded the organizers' capabilities. We are, after all, mothers, and whether we work or not, we are all just as busy as you. I was a working mother until very recently, and I still work extremely hard to balance it all. The more members we have, the more chances there are that we receive complaints identical to yours, because truthfully, you cannot make everyone happy. All of our organizers work extremely hard to come up with fun events and activities at all hours of the day, and all days of the week. We are more than happy to do this for free, as we gain something more valuable than money--but when people either don't show up continuously, or complain without really producing any alternatives, it just plain stinks.

For what it's worth, a little more than a year ago, I received an email stating that due to my inactivity in the group, I was running the risk of being removed. I was still working at the time, and while I was initially very hurt, when I stepped back and realized the intent of the letter I received, and thought about all the hard work and dedication that the organizers put into the group, I got it together, became one of the most active members, then an assistant organizer, and now I am the organizer.

I care about our group deeply--each of the women I have met has had an impact in my life. Just this past Saturday we had my son's 4th birthday party--and all his friends there were through moms I had met as a member of ASAHM. The group is part of my heart and soul, and I put my heart and soul into it. Reading posts like yours is like a slap in the face, quite frankly.

We had an organizer meeting this past week (because yes, we do take it that seriously), and since at least one of our organizers will be working outside the home, we are hoping that we will have more events to cater to working moms. I can guarantee you, however, that there will be a working mom somewhere that will feel the events are not appropriate for her. If you had complaints or suggestions, how come none of us heard from you? All you had to do was click on our name to send an email. Clearly you have some time to send an email, as you find the time to post on these message boards.

And one last note--I hated having to remove the people I did. I don't want to remove anyone. We are not snooty or exclusive--our attendance expectation is posted clearly right on our front page. It's unfortunate that you are disappointed, and it's unfortunate that you didn't get a chance to experience how amazing our group really is.
