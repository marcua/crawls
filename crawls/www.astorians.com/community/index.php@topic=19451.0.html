Greater Astoria Historical Society - October Events
« on: September 26, 2010, 02:05:34 PM »
Sunday, October 3 at 11:00 am
Walking Tour of Old Astoria Village
Learn about the early days of this fascinating village from the Hallets to the Halsey’s.  Walk through a vanishing antebellum village and learn about Astoria ’s place in the history of Long Island City .  Meet at the Main Gate at Socrates Park Please call for reservations 718-278-0700.  $15  ($10 for GAHS Members)

Monday, October 4 at 7:00 pm
Lecture: The Centennial Celebration of Penn Station
October 1910 Pennsylvania Station opened – exactly one hundred years ago. In the opinion of a number of architectural historians, Pennsylvania Station was the grandest building ever erected in the United States . Lorraine B. Diehl, author of The Late, Great Pennsylvania Station, will bring New York City 's vanished railroad station to life. Whether you‘re interested in railroads, architecture, engineering (the story of how the railroad tunnelled under the Hudson River and built the station is fascinating in itself) or simply remember the great building itself, this lecture is a must! 

Saturday, October 16 at 1:00 pm
History Roundtable: The Stock Market Crash
The stock market crash of October, 1929 was the harbinger of one of the worst economic crises in U.S. history. Explore various theories of what caused the crash and the Great Depression that followed including Hoover ’s response to the depression, FDR and the New Deal. Discuss other aspects of the era and compare it to the Great Recession of today.                          Film viewing: PBS American Experience: The Crash of 1929.
Free Event
 
Wednesday, October 20 at 6:00 pm
Book Discussion Group: Towards An Understanding of Today’s Activism
Session #2: this month will feature a discussion of the book, Ahead of Her Time: Abby Kelly and the Politics of Antislavery, the biography of an extraordinary abolitionist and women’s rights activist..  You must have registered for this series to participate. 
Free Event

Saturday, October 30 at 3:00 pm
Walking Tour: Haunted Waters of the East River
Are the stories of the treacherous waters of the East River true? Ever wonder where the name Hell Gate came from?  Celebrate the spirits of Halloween as we share the stories and legends of this historic waterway.  Meet under the Hell Gate Bridge at 3 PM. 
Please call for reservations 718-278-0700.  $15  ($10 for GAHS Members and ghouls in costume!)
