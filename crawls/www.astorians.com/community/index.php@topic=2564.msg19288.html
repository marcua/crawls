Tonight, I used the fava beans and one of the onions to make this recipe, Pasta with Fava Beans. It's rather misleading, as the predominant flavors are the pancetta, tomatoes, basil, and mushrooms. I tweaked it a little, blanching the shelled beans for 30 seconds, then removing the skin from the beans before cooking them with the mushrooms. I also marinated for only 30 minutes instead of 60, used a bit more pancetta than called for, and used 1 16-oz can of crushed tomatoes instead of twice that much. It was very good, but the favas were few and far between, and overwhelmed by the other flavors....

I made a particularly tasty quick and easy beet recipe today, as an accompaniment to some interesting fried fish (watch my blog for more about that soon!), and I thought I'd post it here.

Vinegared Beets Nested in Their Greens
from Vegetarian Cooking for Everyone, Deborah Madison

  • 1 bunch small beets with their greens
  • 2 t butter
  • salt and pepper
  • 1 t balsamic or sherry vinegar

1. Remove and reserve all but 1" of the the greens, scrub the beets, and if larger than 1" across, cut into halves or quarters. Steam until tender, 20 minutes or so. Let cool, then peel and put in a small pan.
2. Discard the beet stems and rinse the leaves. Steam until tender, about 5 minutes. Drain, then toss with 1 t butter, salt, and pepper. Arrange on 2 plates.
3. Heat the beets with 1 t butter, add the vinegar and shake the pan until reduced. Put the beets on the greens and serve.

2 servings.
