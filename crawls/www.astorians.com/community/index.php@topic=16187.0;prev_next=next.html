Anyone else notice the last few days that the very loud freight train that used to run over hell gate during the day now runs at 5:30am?  There's a few that go over the bridge but the one that used to run during the day tended to be the longest, slowest and loudest of the lot.

We can all thank the politicians around glendale for this apparently, since they didn't want to put up a sound baffle wall between their oh so precious catholic school next to the tracks at metro ave, and instead harassed the railroad to change it's schedule.

Don't get me wrong, I knew the bridge was there when I moved over to the area from closer to LIC a few years back, but i also knew nothing all that loud has gone over it at night for at least the last 30-40 years. Amtrak zips by once or twice, you don't even notice it really... freight though, it drags, loud slow and uphill. I can't imagine I'm the only one that was woken up by this. It's a little screwed up to say the least that our community was not notified at all about this.

I should add that this is probably only the beginning. I've heard DSNY wants to ship all trash from brooklyn & queens out via the railroad. This will in all likelihood lead to an entire train just of trash going over the bridge 6 nights a week in the next few years. Hell Gate is basically the only rail route off of Long Island for this stuff. Those green containers you see composing half the train these days are trash cars.

On the one hand this is very good - it means a lot less trash trucks on our highways and bridges - a lot less air pollution. Hoyt ave was full of Kephart trash trucks heading out of town late at night. I'm all for the railroad - it's cheaper for the city and far less costly than all these trucks. But on the other hand, really? This has to be done in the middle of the night, when it was done for decades during the day without being too much of a bother? I expect noise during the day. not at 2, 3, 4, 5 AM...

I just find it rotten that every community, from here to woodside to maspeth that these tracks run though, now has to deal with this noise exclusively at night, all apparently because of a vocal politically connected private high school refused to go with another solution. It's disgusting.

Unless some politicians around here want to step up and actually represent us for a change, this is a new permanent source of late night noise pollution for this neighborhood and several others, and one that is poised to grow over the years as more trash leaves town over the bridge.

The bottom line is the school doesn't want the cars sitting there during school hours. The track that the cars are on is the "outbound" track for CSX. Long story short CSX comes over, drops off a new batch of cars, and picks up the cars that are on said track. The cars that CSX picks up are the ones that the school is complaining about, the loaded trash cars. I doubt the noise level has anything to do with it, seeing how the RR was there way before the school was even built. And yes, it's the trash cars that stink, not the construction debris cars or any other cars that sit on the outbound track.

an "amtrak problem" would have zero effect on freight traffic.  there are three tracks up there; two Amtrak with catenary wires, and a freight track that can't run electric trains. the amtrak and freights do not share tracks. there was a second freight track, to make the total four, but that was torn up in the 80's.

you get some odd ones running by sometimes.  you'll see an engine pulling LIRR cars sometimes, but that usually runs after dark, so its hard to get a pic.  i have twice seen NJ transit doing some kind of test run over the hellgate, and they ran a full train over the bridge.
