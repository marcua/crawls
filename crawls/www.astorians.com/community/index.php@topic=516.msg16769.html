After reading this thread (which has taken me quite some time!) i realized i never said "hello!"
so....
HELLO ASTORIA
  (Hello Jenn)

I'm Jenn, 26 and on my second place in Astoria. I used to live on 30th Ave and 45th street, right near the playground. If i pay real close attention i can spot my old building in certain scenes when i watch a bronx tale. for those that saw the movie, its the corner where the guys randomly beat down the black kids on their bikes.
when my ex and i split i had to give up the apartment (2 BR, the BRs were walk thru though, so a little hard to rent to a stranger. and the landlord lived there and was a little dificult).
so good ole craigslist helped me find my new place on  37th street btwn 34th and 35th ave. (and i traded difficult lives in the bldg landlord for difficulut absentee landlord). i have 2 roommates, one which has been constant since i moved in and the other moved in in january.
I've lived in astoria for 5 years now, right after i graduated with my useless BA in English from Fordham(and my 5 year reunion party-AKA free booze and food night is Friday). This current apartment has been home for 3 years and will be for at least one more.
I currently slave away as an admin/office manager for a neckwear and hosiery firm. i sum up my job as being a monkey puppet. the non fashionista working in fashion, sigh.
my bright spot has been hoping and trying to get into Hunter's graduate program to become a High School English teacher, but alas i have gotten the same "we regret to inform you.." letter 2 years in a row.
formerly grew up in Jackson Hts so i am a total queens girl. if i had any art skills what so ever i would design queens t-shirts and gear a la Brooklyn. but secretly i don't want to advertise astoria for fear i will priced out quicker. i went to HS in the neighborhood. you Fatty's fans dine in what used to be a creepy establishment i used to pass for 4 years while hiking up or down the hill to SJP...the Prep.
i find it amazing how much the neighborhood has changed since i was a teenager. and yet how some of it has stayed the same. ahhh, nostalgia-the Q19A bus ride...
anyhoo I love the recent "explosion" in fun cool things to do and places to go. and yet i feel like i haven't taken full advantage of it. which is sad since i chronicle upcoming astoria events on my website. and i read about all the nifty things going on here that i never seem to make it out to.
in other news about me...i'm about to be divorced...don't worry, i'm happy about that. it was stupid mistake/favor that will thankfully conclude soon. in good relationship news, my BF and i will be celebrating a year together tomorrow  :-D
i found him how i found my apartment, good ole list of craig (ladies do not fear the personal ad!) i forget who asked, and what thread it was on, but someone asked where to find a tool toting irish man, well mine was found on CL. other suggestions would be sunnyside where he lives. the irish woodside boys seem a little more crazy. but quite a delicious accent, yummmmmmmmm.
speaking of delicious, i LOOOOOOOOOVE food. i joke that i cheat on john with food. i'm kinda picky though and i joke that i am the whitest latina (well half, other half being irish) you will meet due to my not being able to tolerate spicy. i'd say its the irish but john eats the thai & indian food (yuck squared!) as well as the spicy mcspicy foods. i'm a plain jane. but i looooove cooking and baking. i make a mean apple pie. i also love throwing parties and entertaining. my friends swear they come to parties for the food i make. i'd love to invite you all to my summer fiesta but there wouldn't be enough room! i like trying new places to eat, but i get trapped into going to the same places b/c i like them. some placies i dig: cafe bar, cup(especially since they changed the menu), cavo, mundo, napoli(shhh keep the best cheap italian food a secret though!), michael's, sunrise(good cheap sushi, especially my fave, the boston roll)...having a brain fart....and over in sunnyside i dig Bliss and Sidetracks.
quick hits of other groovy things...star trek(TNG and Enterprise), stargate, battlestar galactica(noticing a theme?), rescue me, nip/tuck, king of queens(of course!), the office-old and new, dog the bounty hunter-totally wierd guily pleasure, but i kind of watch from an anthropological/sociological standpoint, deadwood....i watch waaaaaaaaaaaay too much TV. oh, John and i were recently on last comic standing! we're superstars!
waiting for superman to finally open. was very disappointed with X3.
love to read, but haven't pick up anything in a bit. i need to go on a good used book hunting trip. not the strand, but a good book stand. i miss the crazy old lady who sold used books on ditmars near the train. i got a lot of good stuff there.
obsessed with music, my mp3's are nearing the 8000 mark and i am always on the lookout for new additions. i'm a total rock n roll, punk, indie sorta gal.
when in the mood i write. haven't really done much of that lately. work has sucked the creative juices out of me.
oh, i'm also longwinded.
so i will shut the heck up now.
feel free to pick my brain should you choose....just leave me something behind...

Jenn

I've been posting quite a bit recently, but never formally introduced myself, so here goes.
I've been living in Astoria for 12 years, all in the same apartment on 34th Street, just South of Broadway.  I love the neighborhood - I think Queens is much more diverse and interesting than any other borough). 
I'm very involved in gay politics in NYC - currently I serve as the President of the citywide LGBT political club, Stonewall Democrats of NYC (sdnyc.org) and am active with the Lesbian and Gay Democratic Club of Queens, and more recently the Powhatan Regular Democratic Club here in Astoria (over off Newtown Road).  I like to get involved and joined a local church in the last year, Trinity Lutheran, up on 31st Avenue at 37th Street, as well as got active with the Astoria Kiwanis (though my work).  Professionally, I am the Director of External Affairs for QSAC (Quality Services for the Autism Community), which means I keep elected officials briefed on my organizations goals and needs and try and get them to do more for people with autism, as well as running autism awareness events, writing newsletters and press releases and working with disability groups and councils.  I spend a lot of time in meetings and like to network. 
I tend to get active on animal issues, environmental issues and civil rights issues.  Working with homeless youth and combatting bullying/teaching tolerance in our schools are my main two projects these days.
I love to cook, I love to hear about interesting restaurants and usually check them out (though not if they're seafood places!  ick!)  I am a big indie music fan and get to two or more concerts most months.  Recently I've been getting into Death Cab for Cutie, Tegan and Sara and Rilo Kiley, and I like my old 80s standards.  I have two cats who are the best two cats I know, though they have health issues - one is asthmatic and one is diabetic.  I got one when I was working at the ASPCA, the other found me through friends. 
That's pretty much it!  Feel free to ask questions.
