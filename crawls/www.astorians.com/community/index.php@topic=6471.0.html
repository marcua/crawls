CALLING ALL CREATIVE TYPES! 1 BR in a 4 BR loft, avail. June 15th
« on: May 28, 2007, 07:15:37 PM »
1 BR is available starting June 15th in our 4 BR loft. (This date is *somewhat* flexible.) You’d be sharing the apartment with
- Nechama, a bicycle mechanic + drummer
- Derek, a wage slave + guitarist
- Lisa, an acting student from Germany
- Spotless, the friendliest, sweetest dog you'll ever meet

Your potential roommates are all friendly, responsible and creative people and we’d like you to be the same. We don't hang out with each other constantly, but we are looking for a roommate that we can be friends with. This means that we hope that you're intelligent, politically liberal, gay-friendly, 420-tolerant if not 420-friendly- all that good stuff. And it means that we are not interested in living with you if your ideal of a perfect roommate is one who interacts with his/her roommates as little as possible.

More information about the apartment:

Our building is in the bodegas-housing projects-sheet metal fabrication part of Astoria. There’s a factory on the first floor, and on the second floor there’s an office and our loft. We have our own entrance. Because there are no other residences in our building, we can make as much noise as we want at whatever hour we want. Life is good. In the apartment there is a music studio with a PA, speakers, a drum kit, mics, etc. Between us there are three bands that practice here. If you moved in here, you/your band could practice here, too.

We’re located at 8-63 Astoria Blvd at the Astoria/LIC border. If you take Astoria Blvd west, you reach the apartment a few blocks before you hit the East River. We’re a 15-minute walk from the closest subway, which is the N/W stop at 30th Ave and 31st St. This is kind of a far walk, which means that this apartment would be ideal for someone who bikes or drives, or someone who likes walking. We’re very close to the Pulaski and Queensboro bridges. Nechama bikes to Manhattan and Williamsburg all the time. It takes her 15 minutes to get to Manhattan and 25 minutes to get to Williamsburg by bicycle. Our apartment is a block away from the Hellgate Social Club, which is a pretty cool/strange/unique bar (albiet overpriced). We're a short walk from really good grocery stores and restaurants on 30th Ave. We're also very close to Astoria Park, which has a dog run, for all you dog owners.

The apartment is HUGE, maybe about 40’ x 40’ (that’s just an estimate, though). The bedrooms themselves are about 12’ x 11’, which means that most of the space is a big, square common area. We have a full kitchen in one corner, with enough counter space to cook properly. In another corner we have a living room, with a TV and reclining chairs and such. And in the middle of one wall we have the music studio. We also have a large roof that we access by going out through the window from the living room. The roof is larger than the apartment, and it’s very suitable for parties and barbeques and such. One part of the roof has a good view of midtown Manhattan.

As far as constructing a homey apartment out of a raw loft, we’ve already put up the walls, but we haven’t yet finished spackling, putting up molding, and painting. This means the loft is live-able but unfinished. We’ve purchased most of the remaining needed construction supplies, but we’re waiting on YOU, our future roommate, to help us with the last bit of construction work. This will take a few weekends; it’s not a huge project. However, we’re definitely looking for a roommate who is willing to pitch in to work on the loft with us and get it finished. No construction experience is needed, just a willingness to get down and dirty. No prima donnas or primo dons.

The rent is $650/month per bedroom, plus an added flat utility fee of $150 (this covers electricity, heat, water and gas). We pay extra for internet and we have a wireless network. The move-in cost is 2 securities (650 + 650 = 1300) plus pro-rated rent/utilities for the last two weeks of june month’s rent and utilities (400), so $1700. You must have this money by June 15th in order to move in.

If you’re interested in living here, email Nechama at metacognizant at gmail dot com to arrange a time to come check the place out. Thanks!
