Hi, everyone: I was out of touch over the weekend. I've got some interest in the lilies but there's still plenty available.

Here are some general houseplant tips that I've picked up:

-When you put the plant in the pot, first you should add some gravel or broken pot-shards for drainage. Especially if you don't use a pot with drainage holes. Water the plant thoroughly to remove any air pockets in the soil.
-Always use sterile gravel and soil from nursery or flower shop.
-Do not over-fertilize: it's easy to panic if your plant looks sick, but you can poison your plant with too much fertilizer.
-You shouldn't have to lift the pot to see whether the soil is damp. You can stick your finger into the soil to the depth of one knuckle. If your finger comes out totally dry, you're not watering enough.
-Also, if you press your thumb against the soil and the soil seeps water like an over-saturated sponge, you're watering too much or there isn't enough drainage.
-Assuming you have drainage holes, you should water your plant evenly until you see water come out the bottom.
-Yellow leaves generally mean too much water. Brown leaves generally mean too little water (or sun damage). This rule varies by variety of plant.
-If you can lift the plant out of the pot in a clump, and the roots take up most of the space in the pot, it's time to re-plant in a larger pot.
-Some plants like direct sunlight and others indirect light or even shade. I have to move my plants around by season, because they will burn in a western window during the summer. And everything needs more water during the summer.
