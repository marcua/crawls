As far as scanner radios go, if you can find a not-too-outdated copy of "Betty Bearcat Frequency Directory" on ebaY or somewhere else - amazon.com maybe? - that's a pretty good reference for scanners.  I find most of the resources on the web are pretty outdated, and find most of the links broken.  The radioreference.com that you mentioned Bill, is pretty good. Also, cityfreq.com is pretty interesting.

A lot of agencies recently began "trunking" which I do not fully understand yet, but can be programmed into the newer scanners.  I also have the scanner app "Scanner911" for the iPhone which was free when I got it, I think it still is free, but I could be wrong.  It's pretty decent and updates their database frequently.  Tonight might be fun to listen in to Midtown North (476.5875), which I believe handles the Times Square insanity.

Trunking is a bit more complicated to learn then analog frequencies.
I have a Uniden BC346XT which you can listen to trunked frequencies.
Trunking has been around for awhile, maybe you are confusing it with digital?
I listen to DSNY, MTA BUS, NYPD(476.612)108/114 pcts(476.812)110/115pcts(476.712) 104/112pcts.

Right now, actually this past week DSNY has been very active. I listen mainly to QE(Queens East) and QW.(Queens
West). I have even heard the DSNY commissioner a few times giving out orders.

Radio Reference is the place to go, people there know a lot, some even have scanner shacks in their homes. I can only
listen to one radio at a time. I have owned only 2 other radios, both Radio Shacks(pro 94,pro164)but this Uniden which
I bought about a year and a half ago is far superior, great reception, small compact size. Programmed it myself
using software and a cable.

I didn't know there were apps for smart phones, will check that out, thanks.
