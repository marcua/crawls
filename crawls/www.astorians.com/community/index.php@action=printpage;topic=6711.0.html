Title: Broker advice - an unusual and SUCKY situation
Post by: ariana on June 18, 2007, 04:58:53 PM

In April my boyfriend and I moved into a beautiful 2br apartment in a small 2 family home the ditmars area. The landlords asked us to sign a 6 month lease, and the broker encouraged us to do so. We were told the owners had a string of atrocious tenants who did terrible things to the property, and as a result now always ask new tenants to do a 6 month lease as a "trial period" to protect themselves from similar situations. We were told that they were looking for a stable couple who would occupy the house long-term. We were also told that in the future the landlords might want to sell the property. We agreed to the 6 months.

We were awfully naive and dumb. 2.5 months after moving in the owners put the house on craigslist and are most likely going to sell it within the next month. (As a side note, they're asking a ridiculously high price and are probably going to get it... )

So now it looks like we may have to move in September. We are very upset, having spent so much on moving costs, plus the broker fee, AND being uprooted so soon, and of course, smarting from the consequences of being naive and dumb.

The question I have is - does anyone think we might have a shot negotiating a deal with the broker? It was someone highly recommended on this site, and our experience was so very positive, I'm wondering if we approach her in the right way would she be willing to have mercy? Any suggestions on what to ask or how to approach her?

I know this is a "buyer beware" situation, in general brokers and the owners are totally in bed together and the tenant/renter often gets screwed, and she has no reason to do anything for us, but it's just not fair. This was the first apartment she showed us, and the whole deal was taken care of in less than 2 hrs spread over the course in total (the night we saw the apt, and the day we signed the lease and forked over our money.) She hardly did any work and now we will have to go through the same thing all over again.


I know we don't have a leg to stand on, and we certainly learned our lesson, but i just thought I'd put the question to see what everyone thinks.

Thank you!

Post by: C-J on June 19, 2007, 11:01:27 AM
It might be worthwhile to see if the broker knew that they were planning to market the place so quickly when he/she showed you the place.  It's a long shot but may help.

When I first moved here (was naive as well) I took a basement apartment in a house and even asked the broker if it was a legal apartment and was told that it was.  What a shock four months later when ConEd came to check something out and started asking a lot of questions.  Shortly after that I was told by my landlord that the place was zoned as a "storage space" and I had to move out.  They (the landlord) not only gave me back all the rent I had paid (when I threatened legal action for being misled) but I actually went back to the broker and was able to get back the fee I paid to them since they knowingingly put me in that situation.

I don't know if this will work in your situation but figured it was worth throwing out there.

Good luck and please keep up posted on what happens!
