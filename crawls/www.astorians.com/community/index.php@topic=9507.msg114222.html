I think if this were just a site where we were talking about national sports or weather it would be a different thing. But because this site focuses on our little neighborhood I think the feel of the conversation is different. I always liken the conversation to one that I'd like to take place in a cafe - but that's not possible because of the difference in everyone's schedules and tastes.

Since we all actually live together in the same neighborhood, and see each other on the street/subway I think it takes an extra effort on all our part to respect each other and keep the discussion civil. The purpose of this site is to create a broad, positive, and constructive dialog about Astoria. Diversity of views is completely welcome, but snide and dismissive remarks are not. It's just that simple.

I do realize there are a lot of boards out there where people can hide behind the anonymity of the internet and disrespect each other in ways that they'd not ever normally do in person - this is not one of those places. I'm a strong believer in what goes around comes around - and my goal for this site is to create an honest and respectful dialog within our real, physical, geographical community.

If you're looking to debate politics, and you want to tell people off, please join one of the other thousands of sites that serve that purpose. But it is my belief that when a conversation degrades into people likening each other to Nazis, or terrorist supporters, or bigots, or broken-records, or fascists, etc - that the conversation is no longer helpful to the community in which we live.

I believe the success of this site, and the role in the neighborhood is precisely because I've (we've) tried to keep the dialog civil - and I have no plan of changing that. So - if it detracts from the site (and admittedly it does, I do see negatives in this approach and I realize this isn't a clear cut issue) so be it because I think the approach here creates a far more positive effect on the community dialog as a whole. And I think our site growth over 4 years speaks to it.

But, there are lots of other venues on the internet for people to have conversations that are more in line with what they're looking for. For many people Astorians can't be your be-all end-all discussion site (it's not for me), so I hope that for those that aren't happy they can supplement their forum experience with other sites - there are even other sites that cover Queens. And we are talking about the internet here, if it's that bad here maybe it's time for another Astoria-centered site to start up that's run differently. I welcome all these ideas.
