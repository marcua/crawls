LHT,  go to Marino and sons fish market on 30th ave between 36 and 37 streets.  They have frozen and canned.  After trying both over the years, I highly recommend using the canned.  Dont be shocked.  If using the frozen, you must clean and peel them. Lots of work for the same finished product as the canned item.  I know it sounds like blaspheme but its a good product.  Ask the fish guys, they will say the same.

As for the scungili fra diavlo, it is my favorite seafood dish too.  I would gladly bring some wine and my own napkin...  or at least post your recipe. 

There is a restaurant in Brooklyn called Fiorentino's on ave U by east 2nd street. When you order the scungili fra diavlo they ask you how you like the garlic, sliced or chopped.

When using the canned scungili, your cooking and prep time is almost nill. Use a bit of the liquid when creating your sauce (I would even sacrifice some of the scungili  at this point to add flavor).  Just before serving, add the remaining scungili into the sauce and heat through, taste and season if needed.

Thats it.

Ask the fish guys for any other tips.  I do like to pick up some shrimp while at the fish store. Either to add to the scungili or for a salad before the meal.
