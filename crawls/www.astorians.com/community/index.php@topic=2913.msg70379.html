I think you mean $100 for a credit report, not security deposit?  If so, $100 is criminal.  They pay a flat fee for unlimited credit checks per year, usually a few hundred dollars per year.  Even charging $20-$30 (which is average) is a huge markup.  I have always brought my own credit report and have insisted I would not pay the fee and have never been charged.  If you do that, make sure you bring your credit report and FICO score.  Most can't understand the credit report anyway, and simply want to judge based on the FICO number, which you have to order at the same time you get your report and sometimes cost a few dollars more.

You shouldn't have to have the FICO score, but they will try to use that as an excuse to still get you to pay their fee.  They will insists the landlord wants, it, etc.  Ultimately, it means they are too lazy to just read through the Credit Report itself and see what the details are.
