Whatever knives you choose, go somewhere you can try them in your hand before you buy them.  Handles can feel very different to different people, particularly if they have small or large hands.  We went to Macy's and tried every single knife line they carry when we were picking out our knives.  Based on that, plus research and input from friends, we ended up going with Henckels Four Star line.  Their handles and balance are particularly comfortable for people with smaller hands (at least, that has been our experience).  We've had them for over seven years and are very happy with them.

Also - don't feel locked into a set.  Consider your knife needs, and look at assembling your own "set" of what you need.  You almost always don't need all the knives in a full set, and you might be able to afford a few knives of better quality if you narrow down what you really need/use.

Meg - Macy's sells Le Creuset off the shelf, but Amazon often has better prices.

I love our Wusthoff Culinar series. Yes, we also didn't get the "set," but just the knives we needed-paring knife and actually two chefs knives-initially we thought larger one for the guy's hands and smaller chefs for me, but I end up using the bigger one for bigger things like cutting into particulary large objects-squash, watermelon, etc...it helps to have the extra weight and length. Whatever you decide to go with, definitely go and try them.

partactical re: pans. I love my large Demeyere skillet. I only have one large size so if you wanted a set, it wouldn't be very cost-efficient. Sur La Table carries the brand, as well as their own line of stainless steel. And now Creuset has come out with it's own stainless steel line that I'm curious to check out. However, if you're doing a lot more fish, maybe just get a really cheap non-stick. I have to replace my non-stick skillet every 1-3 years (and I only make eggs and crepes in it). The more expensive ones really don't last any longer but wooden and rubber utensils will also keep them lasting. I get mine at Marshalls or Home Goods for under $15 for the largest skillet.

I have only one non-stick brand that I am using now. GreenPan with Thermolon. It cleans up with a paper towel. Nothing sticks to it, no matter how high your flame is . . . and it can go right into the oven up to 450 degrees. But the part I love the best is that it is durable and highly scratch resistant with metal tools, and unlike all the other non-stick coatings, there is 0% PTFE & 0% PFOA. I have a 7" fry pan and a 12" fry pan that are my GoTos for every occasion.
