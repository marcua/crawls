I ate lunch there a few weeks ago...I think it was a Friday and I wasn't too fussed with the options. I got a pasta dish and John I think got some sort of club sandwich. My penne alla vodka was good, but very small for the price and I didn't feel as full as I did when eating the same dish in other spots. Mind you the only reason I had the penne (knowing full well this isn't an italian eatery) was because I didn't like the sound of anything else on the menu. Also, the table was soooo obviously a two top but they had 4 chairs at it, along with all the others like ours. We pushed the other 2 out of the way and could not imagine sitting there with 2 other people. If you were just having coffee maybe, but I still don't see how it could be comfortable. The two top tables were even tinier.
I've tried Grand a few times and like them, and Avenue is on my list (and after that meal I totally regretted not going to Avenue and I just wanted to try something different that day). I won't be going back to Flo though. Maybe if the menu changed I would, but for now I'm not going out of my way to go back.

After finding Grand Cafe packed on Tuesday night, a friend and I went to Flo's for a cup of coffee.  It was my first time there, but I suspect the service is the problem.  There were only 2 other people there, so they were not so busy as to leave us waiting almost 20 minutes before taking our order.  They turned it around quickly, though.  My cappuccino was good, and came with a chocolate chip cookie (bonus!).  But then it took a loooong while to get her attention for the bill, and we had to ask several times for a copy of the receipt before finally receiving it.  If 2 beverages on a slow night were an ordeal, I can only imagine how much longer it would have taken if we had ordered food as well.  It is conveniently located, so I will most likely give them another try at some point, but I'd rather go to Grand.
