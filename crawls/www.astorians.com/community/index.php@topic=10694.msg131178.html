If you live in astoria anywhere near the water long enough, you have encountered one of these. My first encounter was the first summer I moved here. I have to be honest in that I have a totally irrational fear of them and would rather come face to face with a mouse than a friggin giant roach (aka a waterbug).

Well, my mother lives in New Orleans where they have them and THEY FLY. She too has this irrational fear but loves her city and will not move.  They come in from outside so even if your house is spotless, you can still get them. And trust me they will scare the CRAP out of you when you see one climbing across your countertop or even worse..*shudder* nightstand.

Anyway, My mom suggested this Bug Vacuum that my brother got her for xmas. It sucks them up, then electrocutes them then all you have to do is dispose of the body. So I was THRILLED because usually what I do (after screaming like a maniac) is unloading half a can of raid on them (they dont die easily and I can NOT squish them). Anyway, this thing comes on its own charger and my mother swears it works. So Astorian neighbors.. thank me later.. Here is the link

http://www.amazon.com/Lentek-Koolatron-Vacuum-Control-Recharger/dp/B000A4D09E/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&s=garden&qid=1212381863&sr=1-2




Hope this helps someone in their fight against the nasty monsters. I have kitties so putting boric acid everywhere was just not an option in my house. I did put some boric acid in the kitchen in areas where the cats cant get to, but still. Its getting warmer so.. they will be coming soon.

Or worked in a restaurant.  I saw enough waterbugs the first few months that I worked the early morning shift at a restaurant downtown to make me never eat out again.  They were moving freely around the garbage bags that we had put out the night before and playing in the big old empty restaurant.

But the absolute worst were the very few waterbugs I found in the six summers I spent at my old apartment on 36th Avenue.  Sometimes I would get up in the middle of the night to go to the bathroom or get a glass of water and they would inevitably be in the worst possible places.  These are some of the situations that I found them: turning on the water and it's running up to escape the waterfall;  Moving the little basket that I have with toiletries and it scurries out from under the hairbrush; and my favorite, turning on the shower, without looking, I get in and look down to see a huge waterbug at my feet!  It's so big that it won't fit in through the holes in the drain as it runs in the tub.

Oh, and I did have a flying one; I hit it with a throw pillow and then, like a madman, began slamming the pillow on top of it.  I guess I wasn't making direct hits because it ran across the floor, although it didn't fly anymore.  I stayed up that night until I found it hiding by the radiator where I bashed it with a broom, repeatedly.
