Best vegan food I have found in Astoria is:

Himalayan Teahouse, on 31st Avenue between 33rd and 34th Streets, around the corner from Brick. It is open every evening except Tuesday, and for lunch on weekends.

They have veggie dumplings with dipping sauce, soba noodles with fresh veggies and a few excellent salads. Everything is under $10. (They also have beef and chicken dumplings.) Wine, beer and a huge tea menu.

A charming little garden in the back with shade for the hot days.

I live around the corner and am addicted to the beef dumplings, go once a week for a fix.

Stamatis on 23rd Avenue offers a variety of Vegan foods - string beans in tomato sauce, escarole, swiss chard, spinach with rice, stuffed veggies with rice, artichokes, lemon potatoes, okra in tomato sauce etc.  At any given time, they have a number of offerings.  He uses olive oil, not butter on these items.  By the way, some folks do not like 'greasy' foods.  Personally, I do not feel that anything in olive oil is necessarily 'greasy'.  The more olive oil, the better for my family!  We have maintained our weight for years by following a Mediterranean diet which includes lots of fish, lean meats, poultry, fruits, veggies and olive oil!

Himalayan Teahouse, at 33-17 31st Avenue between 33 & 34th, has "MOMO's,: steamed vegetarian dumplings that come in two types: one with organic tofu, shitake mushrooms, chickpeas and veggies with ginger & garlic; and vegetable, made from fresh carrots, white cabbage, broccoli and spinach. $7.95 & 8.95 for a generous helping with dipping sauce.

They also have two types of organic soba noodles, a vegetable noodle soup and big salads. Everything under $10.

I also just tried out the new Thai restaurant on Broadway, Leng, and had savory, highly spiced noodles in broth. Delicious. You can check out their menu at: www.lengthainewyork.com.
