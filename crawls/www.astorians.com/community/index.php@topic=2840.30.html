I can talk for days.......you have been warned....

Y'all are so awesome!! I'm so glad I know y'all!

I lol when I read the comments on Curbed - I don't have a problem with other folks opinion. enigmacat you're right they were silly. No worries anyway, I'm pretty thick-skinned. I was in the military so I've had my fair share of encounters with a**holes. Ya know sticks and stones.... :-D

And I know it's weird that I stuck around that long but like I said the first hour was desperation from the heat and by that time I was 3 STEPS - t-h-r-e-e steps, not Giant steps...just three Momma Bear steps...I could see the water and it was a magnet. Then the journalist in me kicked in and I wanted to know the ending to the story. (I actually got a couple of lousy pics on my cellphone of the madness.) Then my hard-headedness set in, which my husband can tell you is unrelenting.

Anyway, I took my statement to Vallone's office today planning on just dropping it off. Rang the bell, told 'em what I wanted and when I got up there Vallone came out and greeted me. He and his staff were very nice to me. He said I was the only person at the pool who had contacted him. Masonicboom please call or email his office with what you saw and how we were treated and anyone else PLEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEASE call/write/email. (McDirk those emails would be awesome.)

He was actually getting ready for the ConEd meeting and couldn't read my tome immediately but did want to know briefly what happened & when the cops got there. I told him about the POP...he said he hadn't heard anything about gunshots. Was it? I don't know. I do know that, before those few seconds of chaos, and before the cops had to scramble up the hill, there were no other sounds like that and imho I don't think a lot of folks bring illegal fireworks to the swimming pool. It's not like there was a GUN BATTLE or anything,  :-o and I was totally to the rear of the madness because everyone was trying to get out. I was at the gate wishin I could finish melting already, ooze under the barricade and flow to the water!! awwww, what relief it would have been....

Just like I said, one second the trash throwing, next second POPing noises...crowd freaks, the poor swimsuit guy is desecrated, two minutes later cops there telling the 50 or so people still at the gate to leave and POPing noises...all the cops scramble up the steps/hill, (and let me tell ya, LMAO, some of 'em were regretting all those donuts and lattes) more chaos up there and then everyone was gone except the 15/20 of us who wanted some damn answers!

Before it started getting out of hand I had kinda rallied the people near me in line telling them to please call 311 etc and complain and not to be lemmings, we can make a difference, blah blah and people were actually conversing whereas before they were just grumbling...and yes I was one of the few white people there - but it was just like Queens, man, diverse - all colors, all ages. And there was definite anti-police sentiment which made me think twice about calling 911 and having the crowd turn on me! However as soon as the chaos happened I regretted NOT calling 911. For the record I emphasized to 311 TWICE that they needed someone for crowd control. I mean really you would have thought there were no employees there - that is how fully everyone was ignored. It was crazy.

As far as swimming with all those folks - I've been a lot of places and honestly, uh, taken baths in dirtier tubs which didn't even have the benefit of chlorine or monitoring! However when I see people sucking water into their mouths to spray out I'm thinking about alllllll the pee!  :-P

I'll let ya know if I hear anything.

What I CAN hear right now is the sound of my husband smacking his forehead when he reads my statement and see's that it's getting attention! LOL, just kidding. He likes it.

You guys rock and I can't wait to see ya again.  (Zips mouth, locks it) eh promife nod dew dulc ud ull.
