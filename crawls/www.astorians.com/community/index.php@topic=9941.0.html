Apartment hunting blues
« on: March 27, 2008, 08:47:49 PM »
Ugh, I am looking for a new apartment now and it's really frustrating so far.  We rent a 2BR near ditmars for 1500 a month.  It's a great deal and our landlord is fantastic.

Now we are expecting twins and need a bigger place.  We have two dogs, so this is a HUGE strike.  It's really difficult because so few are pet friendly.

We had a broker take us to see a "3 bedroom" apartment the other night.  One of the "bedrooms" was actually what a sane person would consider the living room of the apartment.  In reality it was a 1 and a half bedroom.  The broker took us to see a few other apartments but none of them were suitable (all 2 bedrooms the same size or smaller than we have now).

We've both lived in Astoria since we were born and all our families are here...but I feel like it's going to be impossible to find a decent apartment at a reasonable price.  We are both professionals, but you really have to leave some leeway in case someone loses a job, etc. 

I wish buyers would be more discriminating.  It seems people will pay anything for crap.  I can't believe some of the rents I've seen asked for on utterly crappy apartments with kitchens and bathrooms that haven't been updated since 1970.  The frustrating part is that some idiot will come in and pay the asking rate. 

Oh well, just needed to blow off some steam.  I feel like the frustration of this search is just starting.

Hopefully I'll find a broker that I like working with but I'm skeptical about that too.
