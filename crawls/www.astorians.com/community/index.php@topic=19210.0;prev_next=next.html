N and Q lines
« on: October 07, 2010, 03:44:12 PM »
Does anyone else think it's nuts not to have Q service in Astoria on Friday evenings and the Weekend?  The N is crowded till past 2am on Friday and Saturday nights. And it's a 20 minute wait.

Meanwhile, the Q sits at 57th St as straphangers wait 20 minutes for the N to transfer to 4,5,6 at 59th St Lexington Av, or the 7 at Queensboro Plaza or to continue to Astoria.

They should at least run the Q to Astoria till 2am Friday and Saturday nights. It would greatly improve service, encourage mass transit and improve community spirits for a very reasonable cost. As it is, my friends and I have too often handed our money to a cab because we don't like the waits and the crowds.  The Q would cut the wait to 10 minutes and ease the congestion by half.

C'mon MTA, you got overstaffed teams collecting pay sitting on benches, and you can't cut one back to put a conductor on the Q line for a lousy extra 20 minute ride and important stretch between 57th St/7th Av and Ditmars Blvd/Astoria?

Good morning,

Thanks for your interest. The Q51 (as that limited stop route would be called) would operate along Ditmars and 21st street north of the Queensboro Bridge. The bus would make local stops as it does now along Ditmars Boulevard. Then along 21st Street before going into Manhattan make only the following stops:

21st St. & Ditmars Blvd., 21st St. & 23rd Rd., 21st St. & Hoyt Av., 21st  St. & Astoria Blvd., 21st St. & 30th Av., 21st St. & Broadway, 21st St. & 34th Av., 21st St. & 41st Av., 21st St. & 44th Dr., and 21st St. & Jackson Av.

Along 21st Street, the Q100 bus would make all Q69 stops and operate via the Q69 route south of Ditmars Boulevard. This would be faster and quicker for Q69 customers. As to why it would take the Queens-Midtown Tunnel as opposed to the Queensboro Bridge, it would be faster because there is a lot of traffic along the Queensboro Bridge, drivers at the Casey Stengel Depot already have the Q32 which goes across the bridge which is not popular with the drivers there. Also this bus would parallel the N and Q trains which would put the route at a huge disadvantage and make the MTA not want to start this route.
