This afternoon, I decided to visit St. Irene Chrysovalantou Monastery on 24th Avenue and 36th Street.  Before I got there, I spoke with a neighbor about a block away and asked her if there was any problems with church bells.  She said that church bells sound off in the morning but it was not a noise problem for her or her nearest neighbors.  Except for the railroad overpass, the area is very quiet.

When I got to the church, I spoke to a woman who was watering the front sidewalk and asked about the bells.  She said that the bells sound off on Sunday mornings and once in the evening on certain weekdays for about three minutes.  She directed me to the Church secretary, his office is across the street.

I introduced myself and we had a nice conversation.  He invited me to his office and explained that he met with Judy on the issue and they had reduced the bell ringing.  He offered to speak to the bishop about the matter and see if they can work something out.

Considering that I am a complete stranger asking about their bells, they were actually quite gracious.

I never got a chance to hear the bells, so I am going to reserve judgment.  I do hope that St. Irene's and Judy can negotiate and come to an amicable agreement.

I am willing to eat a slice of humble pie if I sounded harsh in my last message.

A sweet older lady explained to us that the watches (and other mementos, but watches were in the majority) were left when a pray was answered. These are not bad people. I doubt that their intention is to make someones life hell.

When should the bells ring and for how long....I don't know. Every now and then, as I lay on my bed, I get to hear the bells of St Joseph (if the wind is right) flow over me on a quiet Sunday Morning.

It's an interesting debate that I have seen take place over the years. Once, in a New England town, a couple sued because the Courthouse clock, which was out of commission when they moved in, annoyed them. They won and the clock was silenced.

At work, across from the Jefferson Courthouse in Greenwich Village, I get to here the hours called out during my shift (it also tells me that it's 10 am and time to open the store. I enjoy those moments when that bell intrudes on my busy life.

All the moments only say one thing, it is something I enjoy...but others may not. Should they compromise....I don't know. Should I compromise....I don't know.

I will say this though...."No Respect for Neighbors" I doubt. Maybe they and Judy will reach a compromise.... I hope so.....because in this large mosaic of people we call New York City, It is the variety and the noise and the daily annoyances that give this city life....and I doubt that will change no matter where we live.
