First, you need to determine if your building and your unit is regulated. Is the building six or more units? Is the building over 30 or so years old? Is your rent under 2k monthly? Have you lived there fewer (more or less) than 35 years? If the answers to those four questions are yes, then you most likely fall under Rent Stabilization. If you do fall under Rent Stabilization, the owner must, barring extenuating circumstances i.e. criminal activity etc., offer you a lease.

If you do not fall under Stabilization, the owner is under no mandate to offer a lease. That situation creates a month to month tenancy as noted above. If the landlord starts creating problems and threatens eviction, give him the address of your "attorney" for all written correspondence. A good choice for your attorney would be your best friend, college roommate, frat buddy or Mom. If the owner is too cheap to repair and/or maintain the  property, is he/she going to shell out a few hundred to an attorney to deal with a good tenant? 

If you don't fall under Stabilization don't worry. 311 is still available for heat, hot water and hazardous condition complaints. Again - will this slum lord want to shell out cash to deal with legitimate issues regarding the living conditions of the tenants?

I hope you're able to get a good deal on your next place.
