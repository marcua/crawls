When I was in college (circa 1963) I started a BUCKET list. We all did. All the things we wanted to do before we kicked the bucket. In September we were all buzzing about the newest buzzwords: I Have A Dream. The previous month had left my generation exhilarated and we used this mood along with the hope that the Kennedy administration was finally going to enact the Civil Rights Act in spite of George Wallace. At night we would sit around the dorm and speculate on how our lives would be different. That's when I started my bucket list.

Every chance I got . . . I took. Check one thing off. Okay.

I was in my 50s when I got myself transferred to Europe. I had already buried 2 husbands and raised 7 children. But this was a big one. It came with a contract and terrific benefits. I worked 3 consecutive weeks and had 10 consecutive days off. I saw it all, many times I might add.

I am so glad that I never missed an opportunity to check off another item from my list. Now, I am permanently handicapped and only one item remains on my list. Perhaps, it could happen you know, someone will find a cure for me and I can still do the one thing that evaded me!

Keep those dreams.
