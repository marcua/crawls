I'm graduating from SVA on Thursday, May 10th. Whee! You all already knew that because I have probably talked about it incessantly, sorry about that.

So. My family is coming in from CT and my mom has told me that I need to figure out where we should go out for dinner afterwards. There will be about 8 people (myself included), and we can't spend a redonk amount of money. (A little pricey is fine, $100/person is NOT.)

I am in charge of this because my mom and both of my aunts were originally like, "Lets go somewhere nice! Like Tavern on the Green!" Um, no. Isn't TOTG kind of like the cheesy Hard Rock Cafe of nice restaurants? And I heard there's a unicorn room? (Scroll down to the 1/13/07 review.) I don't want unicorns involved, please.

Graduation is at Radio City (6th Ave & 51st), so somewhere near there is cool, although we could probably take a cab or subway somewhere, but it'll be around rush hour (maybe a bit after, not sure exactly what time, gotta figure that out). The only dietary restriction is that one person who is coming has a SEVERE nut allergy - like, she had to go to the hospital on Thanksgiving after everyone else (not her, she was SUPER careful) ate turkey deep-fried in peanut oil - the fumes wafted into the house or something. So no Thai and all their peanutty goodness. Also, if I suggested something like Ethiopian (which I've heard good things about and would like to try one of these days), they wouldn't be too happy.

Honestly, with my family, I'm thinking steakhouse or Italian or "American" (whatever that really means when it comes to food), but which place? Help me, Astorians!

Happy Graduation.

If you are willing to go bistro french, a good place close by (and I say this even though I got canned from my waitering job there a few years ago) is Cafe Joule on 1st Avenue and 58th/59th Street.  Short cab ride or nice walk if the weather is fine.   Good food, nice wine list, very reasonable prices, entrees starting under $20.  Small kitchen, so the peanut dietary concern can be monitored closely.

The chef, Vernejoule, was a french wiz kid chef who lost his marbles while cooking at a four star hotel in France.  He became a short order cook at their version of a greasy spoon and was there for a few years until he met his American wife who was there on holiday.  They got married and opened this place.  Food has a good tenderness to it.

Cafe Joul is a great suggestion. There's also Trattoria Pesce Pasta across the street, but they don't take reservations. You do need to get out of the overpriced Rock Ctr area and it's a nice walk or quick taxi ride away. If you've never been to the east 50s, you should walk to the East River after the meal. Walk down 58th Street and at the end of the street is a little park where "Manhattan" was filmed, plus you can take a look at some of the most beautiful townhouses in the city. In case you didn't guess, I grew up in the East 50s (though sadly not in one of those houses  ).
