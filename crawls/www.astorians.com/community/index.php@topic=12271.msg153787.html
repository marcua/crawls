My first post.

Crescent Lounge is what it is.  I'm straight, and will stop in occassionally because it is the closest bar to home (Broadway Sports Bar is Second Closest).  It is a largely, but not exlucively, gay crowd.  Sometimes there is overboard gayness, but most times it is a nice place to hang for a few drinks in a casual atmosphere.  The greatest bartenders.

Broadway Sports Bar is not a dive bar.  Ear Inn in the city is a dive bar.  Kelly's on Crescent is divey. 

BSB is a complete hole.  I've been to its predecessors, Spinning Wheel and Dillon's.  If you are not originally from Astoria, and even if you are, there is nothing good in this establishment for Astorians readers.  The current owners seems well intentioned, but it is one of the bars, that no matter the climate, there is a bad element which hangs there.  I guarantee several non-suspecting hipster/yuppie 'new to Astoria' types will get hurt in this bar.  Be warned.

I don't like the new B-Way Station very much.  If only they would do something with that Brazillian joint across for Parisi's. 

Gilby's is good.

Back to BSB - who names a bar 'XYZ Sports Bar' in this day and age, anyhow???  That martketing tactic is remnant of late 80's/early 90's.  Which is probably about the time its current owners were sentenced.  Welcome home.  Astoria changed.  For better or worse.
