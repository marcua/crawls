Tacuba Cantina (in the old 5 Napkin/Cup, etc. space)
« on: September 21, 2014, 09:32:21 PM »
Went there this weekend, and am really curious what others who've tried it thought. The people sitting around us all seemed totally enchanted, but we were like "ehhh" on some things (but some stuff was nice). Admittedly, I am from Texas, so I have a lot of feelings about Mexican food... and am on a constant search for something in the city to soothe that homesick spot.


Pros: Drinks were good (and strong). Preferred the frozen mango margarita to the fancier one with hibiscus or whatnot (which came with a flower floating on it). Decor cool, energetic vibe, more people than I've seen in that spot in quite a while. Interesting entree involved skirt steak, an enchilada and some potatoes. Pretty solid, though I'd say a notch below Rosa Mexicano -- to compare it to other 'fancy Mexican food'. I think it was a bit better than Mojave, though. And a nice alternative to Mojave's dim, sexy-cave-dweller spa vibe, which sometimes is too cheesy for me to take.


Cons: Not a chip/salsa in sight, though we didn't ask. Queso appetizer even came with flour tortillas instead of chips, which still was good. Different, though I like that crunch with my tequila buzz. My dining partner (a big 'enchilada guy') wasn't too excited about his enchiladas, and the flavor of the beans just didn't seem quite right either. Also, pretty loud -- made me sort of long for the quiet vibe of Pachanga Patterson. And strange, grumpy tall blond porn star hostess in crazy heels (borrowed from a Euro cafe on 30th ave I guess?) makes for a weird first impression, though rest of staff seemed friendly and on-the-ball.


Overall, glad we went and am happy to have this option before seeing a movie, though we'd probably stick more with Pachanga Patterson or more hole-in-the-wall spots (not sure the $$ was totally worth it -- will try other stuff, though). Will always miss the ol' Cup, though!

I went on opening night and liked most of what I got. We ordered tableside guac and they said there were 2 avocados in it, but I didn't see them crush up 2 avocados, so that was weird. One thing I liked quite a bit was the octopus and chorizo taco - another tasty octopus dish to add to the roster of good octopus in Astoria. We were also served chips and salsa.


giantcookie, are you looking for Tex-Mex or actual Mexican food? As a California native, after a while of living in NY I had to give up the idea of finding great Cal-Mex food here (some are close but no cigar). The Mexican immigrants in NYC are from more southerly Mexico (mostly Puebla), whereas US border communities/states are used to the influence of immigrant food from the northern reaches of Mexico. Once I understood that, I came to understand that the Mexican food here in NYC is quite good, just different.

well other reviewers were pretty much right on.  your hostesses provide the first impression and they help set the tone.  if she rolled her eyes one more time, i don't know what i was going to do.  we were seated at a deuce and we preferred a booth, it was a monday night and we took that into consideration, not being unrealistic to ask for a table of four.the owner/chef was sitting at the bar. he owns 8 other restaurants in the city, different names.our waiter was from the restaurant on 92nd st.  he's going back in a week. he was training others.  i was disappointed that he wasn't going to be a staple as he had the sincerity and interest that i look for.the margherita didn't have enough alcohol in it.  i rarely finish an entire drink and well, i did this time and felt nothing, even the bf was surprised.  his sangria was pretty good though.we ordered, the baked empanadas, ceviche, guacamole, carne asada and carnitas tacos and two drinks, $100, with tip (lil pricey)the shrimp empanada is not to be missed, nice chunks of shrimp in a creamy sauce.  the beef empanada wasn't memorablei love ceviche. it's mixture wasn't anything i had ever had before.  the lime was the same but the coche and rocoto was different for me.  it didn't add or take away it just wasn't anything i would order again. wasn't exciting. it was also surprisingly too spicy for me.the guacamole was in a word, fresh, no one flavor overpowered, really good.both tacos were good, i'd have to say i'm partial to the carne asada. great flavors.i will definitely be back and i sure hope they find hostesses that make a favorable impact.
