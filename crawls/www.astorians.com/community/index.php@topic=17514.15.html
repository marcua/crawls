I've got a tivo hd box in the bedroom and a twc hd dvr in the living room.  Both are networked, and I have to say that both me and my wife prefer the tivo box over the twc dvr.  The only + for the twc dvr is that you have access to the "on demand" channels, as well as the "start over" and "caller id" features.  Tivo is not compatible with these twc services.  Otherwise, Tivo wins every category.  It's easy to use with better search and record functionality.  You can easily search and setup recording favorites based on a particluar shows name, or just on a specific actor you like.  Tivo will record any shows that either feature or somehow discuss or relate to that actor.  It will also automatically record other shows it thinks you might enjoy based on the other types of shows your either watch or record.

Tivo also has an integrated amazon, netflix and youtube service... yes, you can watch youtube on your tv using the tivo box.  If you're super lazy, then you can even order domino's pizza directly from your tivo.  If you take your shows on the go, then tivo has software available that allows you to share your recorded videos with your pc.  FYI-some shows are still copyrighted and will not share with your pc, but it's a nice feature for those that have a need for it.

My experience with the twc dvr has not been pleasant... the searching capability for finding shows is very basic and the dvr itself seems to need regular rebooting to avoid errors.  Several times I have lost the "start over", "on demand" and "caller id" functionality.  A simple reboot fixed the issue, but imagine if you're trying to start over a show within the last few minutes of its broadcast or you're in the middle of recording something... a reboot pretty much ruins those moments.  Also, I'm not sure of the spec comparisons, but the tivo hd box seems to have much more hard drive space available when recording in hd.

The cost of the tivo service + the cablecard needed from twc is almost equal to the cost of renting the hd dvr + service from twc, yet we are much happier with the tivo capabilities (especially if you network it.), and own the tivo box instead of just renting an inferior unit for the same price from twc.

I use a twc cable modem connected to a wireless router to run all of my household networking (pc/ps3/laptop/tivo).  The twc dvr should be running any network/on-demand features directly through it's cable signal.  However, I believe that even the newest Tivos still require an add-on device to run it wireless.  Built-in wireless is definitely a feature I'd like to see tivo integrate into the box.  You can occassionally find deals on these, but the wireless add-on shouldn't cost you more than $60 (That's a one-time cost... no additional monthly cost for wireless).  Otherwise, you can run the standard/hard-wire connection directly from the router to the tivo.

Once you get your tivo setup, you should be able to configure the internet connections to get it networking properly.  Also, you might have to install some tivo software onto your home pc/laptop if you plan on doing any type of file sharing.  You can find the downloads on tivos website.

Oh, and to my knowledge... that cablecard counts as having a unit with twc.  That's how they get away with charging you the cablecard fee + the service fee.  Therefore, getting rid of your twc dvr and keeping only the tivo w/ a cablecard or multiple tivos w/ cablecards should not cause you any penalty with your contract.

Try to become very familiar with how your tivo functions and how to properly set everything up.  This way you can avoid unnecessary visits from incompetent cable service reps if things get wacky.  All they should really have to do aside from scrolling through the setup is notify their techs of a few serial numbers that are assigned to the cablecard during the setup process.  After that, the only time you should need assistance from twc is if your cablecard decides to have a meltdown... which should be a no-cost visit/fix since that type of hardware failure is a twc issue, not a tivo issue.

If your premiere seems a bit slow at first, just be patient.  That box and it's new hd features are still in it's infancy with Tivo.  It's still being beta tested and once the bugs are figured out, you should see some performance improvements.

Enjoy.
