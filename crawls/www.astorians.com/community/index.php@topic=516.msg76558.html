No question about it.   I don't think I'd ever been to Astoria before I moved here.   The reason I knew about it was because a roommate I had in Manhattan was looking to move out and mentioned he was considering Astoria.     When I lost the lease on my Manhattan apartment my plan was to check out neighborhoods in the boroughs and select the one I liked best.   Because my roomie had mentioned Astoria, I started here first, liked what I saw, and got an apartment the same day.   

In fact, I remember that day when I came out here I had an old subway map that showed the R going to Astoria where the N now goes and the N going to where the R goes (at least I think that is what happened).   In any event, I ended up on Northern Boulevard and 48th Street and remember walking down Northern Boulevard and then walking down 31st Street to Broadway where I found some realtors.   I paid $600 in '87 for a two-bedroom apartment on the second floor of a private house on 32nd Street between Broadway and 34th Street.    I moved to another -- rent stablized -- apartment in '01 when the owner wanted to raise my rent from like $1,000 to $1,800.   I said "bye, bye."  Astoria was no longer a secret.   '01 was, if you recall, when the "Times" ran a piece about our little secret and interest in the neighborhood soared.

Ciao, tutti!

I just joined this community last night and I am very glad I found it! I have lived in Astoria for -2 months: That's right, I said *negative*.  I am planning to move there in August, and am both very excited and quite nervous about the whole thing.  I've never used a broker before and from what I've read on astorians.com and from what my friends who already live there have told me, a broker is the best way to go about finding an apartment there. I also think it'll be the best option for me since almost all of my time is being sucked away by the Intensive ancient Greek program I'm enrolled in for the summer. Intrigued? You should be! 

My interest in Astoria is multi-fold. In part the move would make my commute to school and work a lot easier that what I have to endure now (I say 'endure' because I live in Briarwood/ Jamaica now and I have to leave about 1.5 hours earlier than any appointments if I want any hope of getting there on time. My school and up-coming-fall-semester job are on the Upper East Side, and I tell ya, sitting on the F train for 45 minutes BEFORE waiting for a bus transfer is NOT good times!). But even more importantly than that, I really love the family/ close-knit neighborhood feel that comes across so clearly on these threads.  Plus: I'm Italian-American (my parents are immigrants so I always seek out chances to speak Italian. Any Italians out there in Astoria?) and my boyfriend is Greek (and I'm making him teach me as much Greek as possible). I figure: with so much Mediterraneaneity apparently running rampant in Astoria: where else could I imagine living?   :mrgreen:

I'm a 28 year old graduate student in archaeology and art history, and I do a lot of photography on the side. Well, when I find some spare moments between writing papers and digging up ancient artifacts. I've read some great things on here about the creative atmosphere in Astoria, and am really looking forward to meeting other artistic types and sharing in the creative vibes.

Reading through these 45 (!) pages of Hello! thread has also fully confirmed for me that Astoria is what I'm looking for. I was lucky enough to have had two great apartments in the last three years with great friends as roommates, but now that it is time for me and my massive library to branch out on our own, I think that we will be quite happy in Astoria.  Ain't that right, libri miei??

Also, though I'm not British, I may have to rival Preston for the title of "Person who says 'Cheers' a lot".  I picked that up a long time ago from a friend who had studied in Edinburgh and it stuck.  Other interesting/weird/ better-you-find-out-now things about me: I'm an unapologetic dork for things Ancient Greek and Roman, hence I like me a good historical fiction novel. I'm an uncloseted Star Wars junkie (I think I will fit in just fine with the massive # of other Sci-Fi freaks here: helloo!); Eddie Izzard cracks me up so hard I actually hurt myself from laughing too hard, and I tend to make up words that do not officially exist (yet) or massacre existing words to the point of utter manglage.  I attribute this both to my belief that there actually aren't enough words in the English language to cover all desired options for expressing oneself, and to the fact that I was/am obsessed with Buffy the Vampire Slayer, a show which made more fully appreciate the sublime beauty of the really bad pun. Oh, and I tend to randomly quote pop culture references I like, which is why I may sometimes be heard talking in French about cheeky monkeys driving buses, but in a British accent. 

So yes. I figure I ought to end this ridiculously long Hello post, and let you fine people get a word in edgewise.  Here's to being one of you soon! (I hope!)
-Cheers!
