I didn't know where else to put this post, but given that this thread seems to be discussing the general topic of the homeless, I thought it should be here.  Feel free to move it.

There was a sad story in the NY Times about a homeless man in Spanish Harlem who was set on fire and ultimately died because of his injuries.  Instead of focusing on the heinous nature of the crime, which was committed by a man whose father is in a shelter, the article details the relationship that the man had with an elderly woman from the neighborhood.

http://www.nytimes.com/2007/10/17/nyregion/17about.html?_r=1&ref=nyregion&oref=slogin

By telling the story of one individual, the article personalizes the plight of many of the people who, for whatever reason, are homeless.  Unfortunately, because of their actions, we tend to focus on the abusive and dangerous homeless as opposed to those who live in silence around us.
