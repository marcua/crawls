Two things:


  • Search the lease and confirm that when the preferential rent is listed it DOES NOT state that it's for 1 year only.  If the preferential rent does not specifically limit itself to a period of time it is deemed to be in effect for the entire term of the lease (including all renewals).  That makes the preferential rent the amount that all increases are based on.
  • The limits for increases for leases commencing on or after October 1, 2011 are 3% to 5.75% for 1 year leases and 6% to 9% for 2 year leases.  Given the timing of your post you're probably still in last year's window, in which case the increases are limited to 2.25% for 1 year leases and 4.5% for 2 year leases.

Griff,
 
Is this issue still ongoing, or did you reach a resolution with the LL?  If this renewal issue is still ongoing, I have a couple of observations/suggestions, based on your information:
 
1) 421-a is a program administered by HPD.  The owner gets a tax abatement on his/her new construction by participating in this program.  In return, the owner agrees to be subject to the RSL for a period of 10, 15, 20, 25 years.  HPD also establishes a maximum rent the LL can charge  (for the building as a whole, and per room), and still receive the tax benefits from the City.  The Dept. of Finance is the agency which provides the actual tax break.
 
2) You can verify the 421-a status of the building in a number of ways - check the lease provisions carefully, there should be some language that the building is under the 421-a program.  You can also contact HPD to confirm that the building is/was a 421-a construction (they may even provide you with documentation, such as the Certificate of Eligibility).  Finally, you can go to DHCR to get a rent history for your apartment, which should indicate the program(s) which it is operating under, as well as whether it's rent stabilized.
 
3) I assume you are the first tenant in your apartment, since you mentioned new construction built 2-3 years ago.  If so, you may want to determine whether being a first tenant may have granted you special rights or privileges under the RSL or RSC.  The question is obviously going to boil down to what the initial legal regulated rent for your apartment is.  The LL is claiming 2,800, and asserting your rent is preferential.  Do not simply assume your LL is in the right because he says so.  Note that the LL is required to register rents with DHCR (which is mandatory initially, and annually thereafter), but this registration does not establish this rent as the "legal rent."  DHCR HAS THE FINAL WORD ON THIS.
 
4) As I understand the RSL, it's supposed to afford great(er) protection for tenants, and supposed to create stability in tenancies through the city/community.  Assuming your apartment is RS, the owner's effort to raise the rent on you by 12% seems inconsistent with RSL.  As you mentioned, the RGB establishes percentage increases, which are set for 1 and 2 year leases (421-a also allows for 2.2% increases per year, which must be calculated off the base rent).  Allowing the 421-a owner to increase the rent by 12%, or 36% max increase in your situation, seems to either be a big loophole in the law or the LL is making a mockery of the RSL.  If you think about it, the prospective 2nd tenant in your apartment will be subject to the same issues you're currently facing, assuming the tenant pays 2,300.  What stops the LL from asking for 2,500 when the 2nd tenant's lease is up.  This is an 8.6% increase, and once again makes the RSL look like a joke.   
 
5) You may want to consult an attorney if you want to explore all your rights as a RS tenant in a 421-a building.  I think the focus should be on the 2,800 figure that the LL claims is the legal rent....
 
Hope this is helpful.
