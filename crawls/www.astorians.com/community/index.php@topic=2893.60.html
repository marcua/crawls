OK, let me address some of these concerns:

- The building is new construction which means everything comes with a warranty.  Everything.  So if something goes wrong or breaks down, guess what?  They have to replace it.  And frankly these accusations of cheap construction have been blown way out of proportion.  It's not a completely false observation, but it's not as dire as some would have you believe.

- You have a final inspection as a buyer.  So if there's a crack in your molding or the floors are not up to snuff, guess what?  They have to fix that before you move in.  I am sure that's how they plan to handle any deficiencies that have developed in particular units.  They have a lot of apartments in that building, over 100 - it's difficult to keep up with all of them at the same time while trying to get the building open. And also remember how long these units have been up.  They've been moving at a snail's pace for years.  So that's years of exposure these units have had with no owners to keep up with them.

- Exercise rooms - whatever.  We were perfectly happy with what they showed us.  Picking on the exercise rooms is just being curmudgeonly.  I suppose you're looking for a five star resort gym and spa in your $300k one bedroom apt purchase?

- Costs - we've run our numbers with mortgage, maintenance, tax deductions, the whole bit.  We're satisfied that the costs are within our budget and is absolutely not out of line with other new coop developments.   Maintenance depends on the size of your unit.  If you can't afford a big place, well, that's not the building's fault.

- Bottom line - and obviously I can only speak for us personally -  the following were important to us in an apt:  spaciousness, location, the community within and around the building and a cost that fit our budget.  We have gotten everything we wanted on those points and then some with this purchase.  And while you continue to lob insults, just remember there are people in this neighborhood and on this board who are going to make this place their home soon.  You insult the development, you're insulting us by default.   If this place isn't for you for whatever reason, that's fine.  But I refuse to sit back and watch our future home and our decisions to purchase there get ripped to shreds any longer on this board.
