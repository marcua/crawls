I'm not literally for allowing people to opt out of their taxes.  I was sort of making a modest proposal.  If universal healthcare were to pass (I don't believe it will), I would be bound to contribute.  Just as I am bound to contribute to other flawed government programs.  Further, I do not believe in taking away safety nets for people in need.  But I do believe in reining in entitlement programs.  There will always be people who need help, but the government gives right now like Santa Claus.  I have to agree with Lobber, need based aid makes much more sense.

Also, I would posit that ALL universal health care systems have sucked for some reason or another.  Doesn't matter what government runs them, or whether they are intended for minorities, non-minorities, martians, or whoever.  My objection is not that I should be forced to pay for the lower strata of society, as I already do plenty of that through vicious and overused entitlement programs.  My objection is that there is the potential for MY freedom of choice to be taken away so that someone else can have what I am sure will be substandard healthcare.

And you'd really only have to earn about a hundred grand to more than clear the upper 20 percent of income in the U.S.  I intend to be earning at least that much, and I want to maintain the right to purchase my own healthcare services.  I do not want to be locked into a government plan.
