Dissapearing bees
« on: October 28, 2007, 09:44:34 PM »
I had first heard about this phenomenon last year and found it both fascinating and disturbing.  Tonight, it was on 60 minutes in addition to a PBS special devoted to the subject  It is serious.  Without honey bees, we can potentially lose most fruits and vegetables.  Scientists are trying to find out what it is that is causing this.  The bees leave their hives and never return.  There may be a combination of factors involved - a virus, malnutrition and pesticides.  This is happening all around the world.  There is a village in China where farmers have to pollinate by hand - a long and costly process.

Another disturbing phenomenon.  Between the melting of the glaciers, natural disasters and this, I am so worried for the next generation.

I know, I know that generations in the past have said the same thing, but I feel that this is the first time in the history of man that we have been able to manipulate and abuse nature to this extent, and we will suffer the consequences.

I agree with what sweeper is saying.  While I am certain that we in no way are HELPING the environment by pumping out greenhouse gasses, pollutants, and anything else that we're tossing out there, we are not the CAUSE of global warming.  It is something that has happened for billions and billions of years and, like it or not, it will continue to happen.  We as humans with a relatively short memory/history, don't seem to grasp this fact.

Any species that is not symbiotic with it's environment and living in total harmony with it will cause damage.  We are definitely in this category and have been for quite some time.  Overpopulation and continued growth will cause us to have to live more and more carefully, but it doesn't mean we're going to kill the planet.

This planet has been around through much more tragic and cataclysmic events besides humans and SUVs.  A single large volcanic eruption puts as much toxic greenhouse gases into the environment in a day that can affect the climate more than we could in a year.  Actually, if you have 50 minutes to sit in front of the computer, watch this video on Global Dimminghttp://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=39520879762623193.  There is another article that backs this up, instead using volcanic eruptions as the catalyst that cools the globe http://www.koshland-science-museum.org/exhibitgcc/causes06.jsp.

Meteorite collisions have wiped out just about all life on earth more than once.  Most of the US was under a shallow sea at one time, and later covered in a glacier, within the last billion years or so.  Remember Pangaea?  Nope.  How about dinosaurs?  Nope.

All of these events are Katrina, wildfires, and tsunamis all wrapped up into 1 and multiplied by a million.  The extreme changes that this planet has undergone in it's lifetime are WAY more than we've seen in human history.  We are but a blip on this planetary timeline and, no matter WHAT we do, earth is going to do what it wants. 

We could become green tomorrow, never letting another PPM of CO2 out again, and still end up with ice caps melting, sea levels rising, and violent swings in weather.  This planet and all of its lifeforms are in constant flux and, as smart as we think we are, we're very likely not going to be able to stop it.  So, we have to learn to go with the changes.

Just my humble opinion.
