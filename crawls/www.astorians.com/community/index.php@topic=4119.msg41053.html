New recipe in the works. Apple ginger sweet potatoes.
2 lg sweet potatoes washed skin on (prick with fork)
1 small baby pumpkin cut off top OR 2 more sweet potatoes.
1 lg baking apple peeled and core removed
1 teaspoon ginger chopped fine
1/2 cup white sugar
1/2 teaspoon vanilla
2 eggs
1/2 stick butter melted
pinch of salt

Preheat oven to 350 degrees
Place in large microwave proof bowl: pumpkin cover with cling wrap loosely. cook till insides are scoopable. remove from microwave and repeat with sweet potatoes and apple in same bowl.

Scoop out pumpkin flesh into large bowl along with apple and scooped out sweet potato flesh. place remaining ingredients in same bowl and using hand mixer or wood spoon, mix till all ingredients are incorporated well.  taste for seasonings.

Put in greased casserole dish and place into oven for at least 20 minutes till cooked through.  

Options:  remove ginger and replace with a shot of spiced rum and teaspoon vanilla plus 1 teaspoon cinnamon.

I came up with this last year, and made it with both sweet potatoes and winter squash:

Acorn Squash Mock Souffle

8 oz. cooked squash or sweet potato
6 oz. andouille sausage, diced (to make it vegetarian, use sliced portabello mushrooms sprinkled with
Cajun seasoning)
1 onion, diced
1 Tbsp olive oil
1 oz. shredded cheddar
1 large egg
3 Tbsp half and half
kosher or sea salt
pepper

Preheat the oven to 325F. While acorn squash is cooking, brown the sausage and onion in a skillet over medium heat in the olive oil. Drain and set aside.

When squash is tender, remove flesh from skin and place in mixing bowl. Let cool. Mix thoroughly with cheese, egg and half and half. Add slightly cooled sausage and onion and mix well. Add salt and pepper to taste. Put mixture in 2 cup baking dish, and bake in oven for about 25 minutes.

2 servings.

Another one:

Winter Squash Soup with Roasted Red Pepper Puree

This is based on a pair of recipes I found on Epicurious.  They are put together in the original, but I modified each one slightly.

2 1/2 pounds winter squash, roasted
4 garlic cloves, roasted
2 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil
1 medium onion, chopped
2.5 to 3 cups good stock or broth, chicken or vegetable
2 Tbsp half and half
kosher salt to taste
ground white pepper to taste
Roasted Red Pepper Puree (below)

Cut ends off of garlic cloves but do not peel all outer paper off.  Place on a piece of foil, sprinkle with salt and olive oil.  Seal foil and bake at 350 for 45 minutes to an hour, or until completely soft.  Halve squash and place on a rimmed baking sheet, cut side down.  Bake at 350 for an hour, or until completely soft and caramelising.  After removing from oven, scrape squash meat into a bowl along with the roasted garlic cloves.

Heat oil in 3 qt. soup pot over medium heat.  Add onions and a pinch of kosher salt and saut√© until about half have caramelised.  The rest will be melty soft.  Add squash and garlic; stir.  Slowly add stock, about one cup at a time, and start pureeing carefully with an immersion blender.  (Alternatively, place onions, squash and garlic in a blender, and start blending in stock one cup at a time.  Thin to desired thinness.  I was aiming for a thick, silky soup, and I found that about 2.5 cups of stock gave me that.

Blend in half and half and adjust seasoning.  Serve in large bowl with a dollop of red pepper puree in the middle.  Do not completely stir in red pepper puree to eat; instead, enjoy the different strengths of each puree in each bite.

Roasted red pepper puree

1 large red pepper
1-2 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil
1 clove garlic
pinch salt

Roast pepper under broiler or over stove (or you can use jarred roasted peppers).  After peeling, chop coarsly and blend with olive oil, garlic and salt in small bowl of food processor.

3-4 servings.

My mom's recipe for sweet potato biscuits.  I haven't made these myself in years, but they're so good!

*

2 cups sweet potatoes (3 medium sweet potatoes)
1/4 cup sugar
1/3 cup butter
4 cups self-rising flour (or add 2 tablespoons baking powder and 1 tablespoon salt to plain flour)
2/3 cup shortening

Boil peeled and cut sweet potatoes until soft, then drain, mash, and add butter and sugar while still hot.  Cool, then sift flour into mixture.  Cut in shortening.  Roll out and cut to size and thickness desired.  Bake at 450F about ten minutes.

Makes about 4 dozen small biscuits.  Freeze well.

*

Southern goodness!!!!!!!  I swear by these.
