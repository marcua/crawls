Here’s a list of my favorite pizza places in Astoria

BRICK OVEN:

Forno Italia
43-19 Ditmars Blvd.
This is a great Italian Restaurant with a woodfire grill that makes some of the best pizza I’ve ever eaten. My wife and I are actually addicted to it, usually getting 2 Pizza Margaritas with garlic. They have some of the freshest mozzarella and tastiest sauce around. Service at the restaurant can be a bit shaky at times, but the food is always great and the fresh bread they put on the table is worth the price of admission.

Michael Angelo’s II
2911 23rd Ave.
Their regular pizza is solid enough, with a tasty and powdery crust. But their “Sophia Loren” pizza (fresh mozzarella, fresh tomatoes, garlic, basil & extra virgin olive oil) is the one worth getting excited about. It’s one of the only places in Astoria that sells pizza of this high quality by the slice.  Their thin-crust is also really great. I just wish they had more seating in the restaurant. Closed on Mondays.

Sac’s Pizza Place
2541 Broadway
Pretty good coal oven pizza. Not as good as Forno…but if you live in that part of Astoria it’s definitely worth checking out.

TRADITIONAL:

Gino’s
43-19 Broadway
Fantastic tasting pizza all around and one of the best slices of Sicilian I’ve ever had. My wife swears by the Grandma slice. We once looked at an apartment a couple of blocks away, and almost took it simply because Gino’s was nearby.

Apicius
20-72 Steinway Street
They have a pretty wide selection of pizzas, including Grandma slices and pesto pizzas.
Sometimes they roll out new creations. Pretty good Italian food too, for decent prices, that usually comes with delicious garlic knots and bread.

Pizza Palace
31st Street & Ditmars
Decent pizza in the classic no-frills sense. They also have a whole-wheat pizza, which isn’t bad at all.

Last Stop
22-35 31st Street
Not bad, but often a bit too greasy. So-so Italian food. Best benefit: Usually open late.

woo pizza!

Quote from: felisfemina on September 16, 2007, 12:42:43 PM
One of my favorites is Ani Pizza on 25th Ave and 44th St.  The guy who works there is really friendly and the pizza is delicious.  The garlic knots are good, too.

  I live across the street from Ani's and have to agree.  The owner, an Armenian, is a great guy and, during good weather, "Pizza Man Jr.", his son, can be found disassembling and reassembling his bike and playing with the kids of the patrons of the laundromat next door.  It's a real family type of operation.  I have to admit that I'm kind of biased since Ani found (while making a delivery) and returned my cell phone that I lost getting out of a cab one night.
  When I first moved to the neighborhood, it was still Gino's Piccola Italia, which was pretty decent, but I am told it wasn't as good as it used to be by the longtime locals. The restaurant really went down hill when it was being run by a group of Mexicans that took over from Gino.  I suppose the restaurant became unprofitable, when it was being run to support four or five families and the quality of the food suffered.
  Ani's is a definite improvement and everything on the menu is very tasty.  I particularly like the crust of his Sicilian slices--light and chewy like a good bread.
