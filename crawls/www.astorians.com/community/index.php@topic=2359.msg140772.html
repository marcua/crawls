Sure, it's sexist, but it's a pretty common attitude in my parent's generation (not assuming anything about the poster's age here), and I've never wanted to be one of those feminists who get pissed off when a man holds the door for me. I just smile, say thanks, and when I'm first through the door I'll hold it for whoever is behind me.

Our whole society is so interwoven with sexism, it's kind of hard for any one of us, no matter how aware we are, to be completely untainted by it. And to me this seems one of the less virulent forms. It's not as if it's a new and dangerous trend, and pretty soon if we aren't careful men will be standing up and letting women sit on the subway like mad. It's very much on its way out. This seems sweet and old-fashioned, like something my grandpa would have done, and something I would smile at even if I also see the sexist aspect of it.

As casicua says, a sexism that is not misogynistic may be a step in the right direction :)

At the risk of sounding like a 25 year old sexist dinosaur, why is it a problem to treat women differently if it's in a good way? I know it's easy getting caught up in gender equality these days, but let's not forget the good things we're letting go and overlooking in the process.
If I hold the door for you, and you're thinking in your head that I'm a sexist for it, please let me know so I can keep a look out for you and maybe you can open the door for me next time  :-)
