Posted this earlier-- I *have* worked with Lois (and hope to again in the near future). She's great, IMHO. Definitely talk to her-- she's not the sort of person who's going to charge you for talking first. Explain what you're nervous about and ask her what experience she has, what she thinks you could work together on.

My take on Jeff (aka PT w/dreadlocks) was that the drill sergeant thing he does is a role-- and that some people really eat it up. (I've watched him, and some people really enjoy whining at him.) He was also assigned to me as my orientation PT. I'm glad that I had waited to sign up for the orientation until I'd been around for a few months and had an impression of the various trainers. Instead of whining after the first hard thing we did, I jumped up, smiled, and asked if we could do it again? He blinked, said, "You're teasing me, now" and *never* gave me a hard time about anything ever again.

YMMV, and I do think that some Overly Fit people forget that us mere mortals can and do get injured doing things we're not ready for. If you don't bring it up to them, though, how can they improve? How can they learn and not mess someone else up?

(Or you could sue.)

:*)

I *am* sorry to the poster who got seriously injured. I don't know enough about the topic to have anything wise to say on it, though, so I'll just offer my sympathy.
