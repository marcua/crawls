Accident on Ditmars at 35th St.
« on: January 26, 2010, 08:38:22 AM »
Just before 8:00 this morning there was a terrible accident on Ditmars at 35th St. - I don't know for sure but I heard that someone was killed.  There is a sanitation truck in front of Ferrari Cleaners, and a milk truck midway through a turn from 35th St. onto Ditmars heading east.  A pair of gloves are in the crosswalk.  Ditmars is shut down from 36th to 33rd Sts.  News helicopters are all over the place, and I saw news trucks and at least one reporter talking to a bystander.  I suspect that a sanitation worker was hit but I didn't hear or see anything to confirm that (ETA: CBS 880 website confirms that a sanitation worker was hit).  Gothamist maps says only "serious MVA" (says 35th Ave. instead of 35th St.).  Anyone hear anything else?

Quote
(2) Local Trucks. An operator of any truck as defined in paragraph (a)(1) above, with an origin or destination for the purpose of delivery, loading or servicing within the Borough of Queens, shall only operate such vehicle over the following listed streets, except that an operator may operate on a street not designated below [the list in the first link] for the purpose of arriving at his/her destination.

C-J - Just saw your update.  So sad.  I often see delivery trucks driving too fast or aggressively (rushing lights, forcing people in crosswalks to hurry or jump back, etc.) in the morning turning onto or off of Ditmars, and it has made me nervous, especially since I'm now walking down the street with an 8 month old.  Maybe there is some way through Transportation Alternatives or Peter Vallone Jr.'s office to mount pressure to get current regulations enforced (such as they are) or new ones enacted.  I don't know, though - there seem to be the same complaints about traffic year after year and little changes.
