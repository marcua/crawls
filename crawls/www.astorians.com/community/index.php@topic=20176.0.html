Downstairs neighbor - yikes
« on: January 03, 2011, 01:32:26 AM »
Hi all

I guess I wanted people's opinion about this situation with my downstairs neighbor.  Every so often, this guy comes upstairs and bangs on my door and when I open, he's yelling that I'm constantly banging. Now, I'm not banging anything; I'm just living.  One time he came up between 7:30-8 pm and went off again while I was cleaning the floor with a vacuum. And it has never, ever been a civil exchange from him, like "Excuse me, can you please keep it down". It's just straight to yelling.

Sometimes, my night owl tendencies break out (like tonight) and I'm awake later than usual. I'm not doing chores or anything like that, I'm walking around my apartment. Around midnight, he started this awful banging on his ceiling probably with the handle of his broom for a solid minute. My wife looked at eachother and just rolled our eyes, what else can we do. I'm not about to go down there, I pretty much don't want confrontation because it seems like he's not all there.

I have a handful of stories with this guy which I'll spare you all of except for the first night he came up complaining -- which also happens to be the first night we moved in to this apartment back in mid-March 2010.  The situation was that we had a gas leak in the apartment and the FDNY were looking for the leak to seal it off. It was midnight mind you, but it's FDNY trying to stop a dangerous gas leak. He comes up yelling that there's too much noise and he looks one fire fighter which was closest to him at the door and continues his rant.

Now my thoughts are, What, I can't be awake at midnight anymore? The floors creak but that's not my fault. I rent, these aren't my floors.  Everytime he has come up to complain, his complaints were less than legitimate in my opinion.  These are hardwood floors and sound travels, sure. So if I scootch a chair, it'll make some noise. I drop a pen or whatever else, it makes some noise. But the way he describes this banging, he makes it seem like I'm hanging up portraits all over the apartment.

I see my next door neighbors from time to time and I ask them if we bother them or are disruptive but they have no complaints at all.  Should I just ignore this guy or should I be looking to pad my whole apartment with foam?

 :|

I've had this problem before when I had the misfortune of moving into a very old building where the wood floors creak, no matter if you are barefoot, wear socks, or wear shoes.  Every footstep gave a creak and there was not much I could do.  It's not like I was a noisy person or had parties - I like to think I'm pretty darn quiet.  The downstairs neighbor was old and heard every little thing.  The complaints were so irrational and became so frequent, that it became borderline harassment.  I won't bore you with the whole story, but I ended up sending a typewritten letter detailing the problem to the management company, explaining the situation and how I could not be expected to shut my lights at 9pm and go to sleep, for fear of disturbing my neighbor.  It was calm but direct letter, stating that I did not want any sort of trouble, but I would just like to live my life like any other normal person.  They gave me a call, were very understanding, and they must have talked to her, because she never bothered me again.

The best advice, short of writing a letter and documenting everything (just in case he really is off his rocker), would be to get a rug for your bedroom.  I know it sucks, but it might help muffle some sound.  Get an area rug though, as to actually have to install whole carpeting will cost a lot of money and it's not worth it if you end up moving out.  Other than those things, there is not much you can do about old creaky wood floors.  I would definitely try speaking to your super or management company though.  Sounds like he will continue to be a problem tenant.
