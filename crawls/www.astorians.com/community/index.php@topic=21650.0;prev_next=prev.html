I'd have to disagree.  I think Psari is worlds better than Elias Corner.  And I found the decor to be a nautical theme but done with class.  The prices are moderate and reasonable for the portions.  The fish can't be more fresh.  The closest thing I might compare it to would be Telly's on 23rd Ave.  But I really think Psari is a better seafood meal.  Quite exceptional, in fact.  Service was very friendly and prompt.  The place was packed when we went.  Obviously has a following.

I'm not sure they compare exactly.  Psari is definitely presenting itself as a more upscale seafood restaurant while Kyklades is a mob scene with all the hustle and bustle.  I'm personally not someone who thinks the food at Kyklades is really all that special.  But I don't dislike the place either.  I personally find the people who run Psari to care first and foremost about having the freshest catch.  The food quality and presentation comes first there.  But you also feel like you're in a nice place with professional service and a very nice menu.  It doesn't have all the noise and excitement you can get at Kyklades.  Just different, but both have it's pluses and minuses.
