Alexa, I know this is going to sound kind of silly, but here goes anyway. First of all congratulations. Be sure to keep a journal. You will find it a treasure in just a few years.

My first, almost term, pregnancy was around 4 decades ago. I was already showing by 3 months. I was walking my dog in Astoria Park and I was approached by a girl around my own age who was also pregnant. She told me she knew no-one who was pregnant and that her mother had passed away a few years prior and wondered if I could answer a question for her. I couldn't. But, she walked me home to drop off the dog, and then we went to the library together to look up the answer to her question. Before we both got back home, we bumped into 4–5 other pregnant girls and we all exchanged phone numbers. Before the end of the week, we all got together for milk shakes, yes, you read that correctly, milk shakes and a gab fest! We remained friends for many years, even as our children grew and families moved, we always exchanged holiday cards and now emails. There must have been something in the water supply that year because there were 4 sets of twins born on my block. It was wonderful to commiserate with other women going through exactly what was happening to me. When we all got together, we puked, passed gas, ran for the potty as our bladders burst, kvetched about our swollen, hurting breasts and laughed at ourselves and each other's misery. Having shared all those good and rotten memories was a remarkable time for all of us. When I was confined to bed rest (at month 6) up to 10 pregnant women would arrive at my house with pizza and sit on my bed and and we would laugh ourselves silly.

No-one is going to think you are nuts if you stop them on the street and congratulate them on being pregnant and ask to be their friend. You might even find women on your own block who are also pregnant; or in your church; or where you work. Start a little local group of your own. It's also like having built-in friends for your baby. It is also a great baby sitting exchange for weekend dinners out, dentist and doctor appointments and later on even weekend getaways.

I can remember the very first time I had 4 port-a-cribs and 2 carriages set up in my living room for a Saturday night. Two of my friends had weddings to go to and one had an out-of-town funeral and I don't remember the other reasons why I had such a crew—but it was such an experience. I enlisted the help of a couple of single friends . . . perhaps that night was the reason neither of them have had any children! I already had twins, so what's a few more?

Alexa, even if you go on to have another dozen children, this is the one you will always remember, just like the first time you . . . you know! Make it special. Journal it. Take growth pictures. Build memories.
