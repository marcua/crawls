As for the usual delays - and they are usual during a weekday rush hour unfortunately-  it has a bit to do with the fact that ditmars is the end of the line and it only has two tracks. If it were a three track station, there'd be more space to turn around the trains without backing up the line. Here's a hint: If it's morning rush hour and you see trains backed up ditmars bound at broadway or even 30th ave, bet that some of the manhattan bound ones are going to be express right past you in some effort to get things back to normal.

Folks, it is time for action.  I no longer use the trains but for years had problems on the N (which was the R back then).  I gathered a group of friends (no internet back then), found out who to call and we would make calls each and every time there was an issue.  We 'harrassed' them into adding more trains and would even go right up to the tower at Ditmars and ask all the Conductors what was going on.  Very often, they simply did not have a response.  More often than not, the problems are the result of incompetancy which starts right at the top of the Transit Authority. 

Astoria has grown tremendously.  The infrastructure however, has remained static.  Less parking now that they tore down the parking lot on Astoria Blvd, no additional busses or trains, no extra garbage cans etc.

If you have any complaints about these issues, you must make every effort to voice them to our Community Board, our Council Member and in the case of train issues, directly to the Transit Authority.

On Monday, I will post all the relevant telephone numbers and contact names.  If everyone of us makes the calls, I guarantee you, they will listen.

P.S.  One time we nearly started a Revolution on the trains at Queensboro Plaza when they told us to get off the train and we absolutely refused.  What happened?  More and more people sat down in protest and they gave up and took us to our stops.  Their excuse was the usual 'fire on the tracks' but as we discovered, there was no fire at all!  Case in point - you must challenge them.  The Transit Authority see us as sheep that they must transport.  We have to facilitate change.
