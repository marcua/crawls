You can do it yourself if you have the time. I did this about a year ago. You can get a grout pulling tool, basically a rough blade with a handle. You just zip it back and forth in the spaces between the tiles. But like someone said above, be careful of loose tiles and scraping the finish of the tiles. It might be wise to have some mortar in case tiles come loose. I used about 10 blades to do a shower with tub. Get some fast setting sanded grout, mix it with water, and using a rubber float fill in the cracks. let it glaze over and usuing a wet sponge wipe clean a few times. Let the grout dry and then seal it with a few coats of grout sealer. It took me about 2 days to get it all done, but it is much cleaner now. You have to reseal every year or so to keep the grout from getting funky. Or you could pay someone! Fun fun.
