I have eaten there a few times.

I had the mixed grill the first time.
Blood sausage isn't my thing, but otherwise, everything was pretty good.
Sweetbreads were very good, and as I recall, on their own they are cheap.
Short-ribs (i guess they were) were very good too.
Some sort of flank steak tasty, a bit on the stringy side.

French fries acceptable, but I don't remember anything particularly special.

The red table wine wasn't bad.

Most everything is too salty  (except the wine.)
Meats were, as I recall, more cooked than I asked for.

Haven't tried the pastas, or anything very adventurous.

The waiters were friendly and attentive.

If I remember more, I will post again.

Maybe I should go there tonight.. I need a glass of wine or two, and it's as nearby as anything.

La Fusta: Baxter Avenue/off of Broadway, across from Elmhurst Hospital. Excellent service/food. My first choice.

El Gauchito: 94st and Corona Ave (Elmhurst). My second choice. However, get there early on weekend because it does get crowded. And after you are done you can cross the street for Argentine pastries at Rio de la Plata Bakery.

La Porteña: 37 ave and 75st. It can be a bit pricey though and depending on the time of the day, the service varies.

Boca Juniors Argentinian Steakhouse: 81st and Queens Blvd (Elmhurst). Heaven for any Boca Junior (Soccer team) fans since the place pays homage to the team in their decor. Diverse menu: Argentine pizza, pasta and meats.

It's not the same guy as Pasta Fina. I miss them, because they were the only restaurant I knew that was absolutely unaware of the public's fear of uncooked egg.. their gloppy caesar salad was just right by me.  But I digress.

Tonight I tried cannelloni. The filling was chopped spinach & ricotta and pretty good. The meat sauce was, no surprise, too salty.  The generously filled $5 glass of Malbec was pretty good though.  I started with a spinach pie (i didn't know the cannelloni would be spinach too, but that's ok. I like the stuff.) It looked like a little pork pie, but spinach and onion inside. Pretty good.

(I also now recall, in the mixed grill way back when, there was a piece of "flap steak" aka "Vacio" that was darned tasty.)

The kitchen staff was good enough to join me to watch a futbol match: IND vs STM, whomever they may be.
