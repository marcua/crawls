Maybe people here (myself included) could be more sensitive in their language choice.  And it is probably true that this guy is mentally ill, or in the throes of a substance addiction, or both.  But, again, I don't think anyone wishes ill upon the guy.  Nor is anyone being particularly haughty.  To assume that the guy has some blood borne disease is unfair, because we don't know him.  But it is natural to feel a sense of revulsion in cases like this. 

And whether the guy HAS a plague or is merely a plague upon our streetscape isn't operative.  He needs to be in contact with the system, and it is a blessing when people call the police on guys like this.  It makes our streets safer when something is finally done, satisfying our own selfish ends.

AND it helps to usher this guy towards the help he needs.  If he is released repeatedly, persistence is the order of the day.  I don't mean to take anything away from the numerous charities that bring food and supplies to those on the streets.  But the needs of people on the streets are more complex than the immediate need for food.  I feel like the prevailing attitude is that street living is a way of life and we should respect that.  That doesn't ring as true to me in a city where there are more social service resources than perhaps anywhere else, and so many generous folks wanting to help out.  People do not belong on the streets where they are a detriment to our quality of life and theirs.
