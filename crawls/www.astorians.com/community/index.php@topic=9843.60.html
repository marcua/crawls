I didn't read through all the responses, but these are the rules if no one has mentioned them.

Cabs must take you where ever you want in the tristate area.

It is against the law to have a cab driver pick and choose who they drive and where.  So this means driving around with the off duty light and then asking people where they are going is not allowed. IF THEY ASK TELL THEM TO GET LOST(I TELL THEM THIS IN MY OWN WAY)

If they get reported then they get fined $1000.

If you have trouble getting cabs then you are doing it all wrong!  I NEVER have problems getting a cab ride back to Astoria...unless its NYE.

What you must do is never ever ever answer someone who asks where you are going.  Always get in the cab and then tell them where you are going.  If anyone ever gives you a problem for whatever reason take a pic of the medallion with your camara phone and then tell them that you will report them if they don't take you.  If they still refuse then get out and the next day report them.  I've only threaten someone  once and I was very convincing, they drove me to Astoria.

Everytime you get to Astoria by cab get out and at the very least tip the guy 2-3 bucks.  Don't be a scummer, its a pain in the ass to drive to Astoria.  If that's too expensive then you have no business taking a cab

The above only applies to yellow cabs.  Gypsy cabs have no real rules and livery cabs aren't supposed to pick you up off the street.  They get fined $1000 if they get caught picking people up like yellow cabs do.

I had a cab driver indirectly refuse my ride to Queens recently.  I work late and frequently take cabs to Astoria from the Upper West Side.  This was the first time in quite some time this had happened. Basically, the driver said because of bad weather the bridges were really jammed and I was better off just taking the train.  When I said, "Ok, then take the tunnel" he said, "Oh that's really backed-up too."  I told him if he didn't want to take me to Queens he should just come out and say it but obviously, since this is against taxi regulations, he was like "No, no, I'm just trying to do you a favor."

There are plenty of yellow cabs out there and unless you are a in a really tough spot trying to find one I find the best policy in these situations is not to force the issue but to simply find a driver who is amenable to taking you.  I do not feel safe being in a moving vehicle by myself with someone who does not want to take me where I need to do.  Moreover, why give this guy my money?  I got out and hailed a cab right across the street from where I was and the driver was more than happy to take me to Astoria.
