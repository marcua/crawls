A sukkot (let's see how many different transliterations we can get) dinner is a fantastic idea.  It could be at somebody's apartment, or if we are really adventurous, we could try to see if there is a sukkah around that we could use (maybe Astoria Center will have one?).  Of course, if anyone is interested in building a sukkah, that can be looked into, but I'm guessing that most of you have the typical Astoria front lawn/back alley situation which is not ideal for such purposes (not to mention the fact that I, at least, am not about to ask my new landlady for permission to construct and decorate a large wooden booth on the premises). 

Sukkot is Friday October 6 through Friday the 13th (actually, it ends at sundown on the 13th).  I would also add that Saturday night the 14th starts Simchat Torah, which is a great excuse for a party (actually, that's simchat torah's raison d'etre, if I'm not mistaken).  So anyway, that's a good smattering of weekend and weekday nights that we should be able to make one of them work.

Again, I just want to add that while in my original post I said that I was looking for a synagogue or a minyan, which I still am, I would always rather err on the side of social and inclusive rather than religious (if that would make it exclusive).
