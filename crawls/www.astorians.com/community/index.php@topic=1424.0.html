Rainey Park! OK, sorabji!
There are plenty of Queens maps that don't even *show* Rainey Park. Believe me, I've looked many times over the years on various maps. "So, where's Rainey Park?!" I used to say on those occasions.
My Rand McNally Street Atlas (copyright 1989), that I use for driving directions, shows it as "Rayney Park." I don't know if that's the real case, but I can tell you I grew up thinking it was "Rainey Park." Someone would have to give pretty good proof otherwise to change my thinking on the matter.
Well, at least Rand McNally shows it, which is a lot more than other map companies have done.
YAY, Rainey Park - the site of many happy picinics for me, while growing up!

I've seen it spelled "Rainy," which makes me laugh. Who would want to go to Rainy Park when so many other parks are without rain?
 
I never knew of any institutional confusion about the spelling of Rainey. But my maps don't date from 1989  :lol:. My Hagstrom atlases and wall map all show Rainey Park. I've seen Socrates Sculpture Park referred to by NYC Parks as both Sculpture and Sculptures Park. Parks gets its park names wrong all the time. Try to find the correct name for Flushing Meadows-Corona Park. Is it hyphenated? Is it not? I've seen it referred to multiple ways within the same seemingly authoratative source. Life is so full of fascinating things like this...

What I like most about Rainey Park is how Vernon Blvd. disappears when you get down by the riverside. I've spent many a long weekday sitting on the benches there hogging that whole park to myself. It's a beauty.
