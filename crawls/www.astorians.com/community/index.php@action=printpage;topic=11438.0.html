Post by: bstewart on July 21, 2008, 09:10:34 PM
Maybe 20 years ago, my former landlady had a strong fear of cats. The idea of their claws especially bothered her.

She had a soft spot for them though - she would put food out for the strays behind the building, and boxes with bedding for them. Then she'd wonder why there were so many. I guess she thought that after a good meal and a nights rest, they'd move along down the road. Some mother cat abandoned three kittens in the alleyway.  The landlady had some neighbor-kids gather them up in a cardboard box. They were all black, a male, and 2 females, in graduated sizes.

She tried to take them to North Shore Animal League, but was told they were too young.. she would have to wait until they were 5 weeks old and could be vaccinated. I came home that night to find them in the hallway, on the apartment steps, somewhat filthy. I called them little kitty, littler kitty, and littlest kitty. I volunteered to look after them for the duration of the waiting period, and I was able to stretch the 5 weeks into 15 years. Whenever the landlady was mad at me about something, (often enough -- too bright a bulb in the hallway fixture, for example,) she would mention that she never gave me permission to have those cats either! The littlest kitty had her revenge by outliving the landlady.


About 7 years ago, a week before Christmas, a young woman from 2 doors down stopped me on the street to ask a favor. Her friend had recently broken up with someone and moved back home with her mother. The mother's boyfriend hated cats, and had given her until Saturday to find someplace for them, or he would.  Since I was a known eccentric cat person, I was their natural mark. So I got a slender black and white "tuxedo" cat, and an extremely plump orange and white job.  She thought their names were Dre (as in Dr. Dre?) and Monkey, but I determined them to actually be Penguin and PumpkinBuddhaCat.

The landlady's sister became the new landlady, and she is as sweet as her sister was annoying. The cats began to visit her in her apartment, and find they prefer her company to mine. So the visit me to eat, and poop, and they seek me out when she is away. It is a mostly satisfactory arrangement.

(One day, a couple of months after this arrangement began, I noticed that Penguin was not eating very much at all. Concerned, I asked her if she had noticed anything, since he seemed to be off his feed. "No," she said, "In fact, he seems to enjoy the turkey I bring from the deli very much.")

Hope that's not too much words for too little story.

Title: Re: How did you acquire your pets?
Post by: ladywithpans on July 25, 2008, 11:54:55 PM
When I was in my 20's, I took a few mini-vacations in Woodstock, N.Y.  During one of them I befriended one of the local residents and his brother--the two of them lived in a house and nearby trailer in West Hurley.  The one who was my principal friend, who lived in the trailer most of the time, had two white cats, brother and sister.  The female had litter after litter, mostly with her brother so most of the kittens were white.  But this particular litter was probably fathered by some roving Casanova: two were white, one was a gray tabby, and two were tortoiseshell and white (or calico).  Of the calico kittens, one was healthy and one was sickly, the runt of the litter.

My friend had no trouble finding new owners for the kittens.  Of the litter I knew, the white ones were adopted first, leaving the tabby and the two calicos.  Then the healthy calico was given to a police officer who lived a few doors away.  That was when I visited my friend.

At about mid-evening I looked at the food dishes and saw three kittens where there should have been two.  I told my friend, and he saw that the healthy calico had returned.  My friend took the kitten and tried to return her to her new owners, but soon after he left her there she was back in the trailer again.  He tried to return her two or three times, but she always returned to the trailer through a pipe under the stove.  It was finally evident that her new owners had put her out for the night.  Meanwhile, her mother and sisters (my friend assumed all the kittens were female) rejected her because the familial bond had been broken, and she snuggled up to me.  I slept alone in the trailer that night while my friend slept in the house.

The next morning, as I was about to go back home to New York City, my friend said that he didn't think it right to put out a little kitten for the night.   He urged the kitten on me, saying, "I think this cat will be good for you."  I hesitated at first, but finally I agreed to take her home if the bus driver would let her on with me, in the carrier from the local vet.

When I got home, there was no one else in the apartment--I still lived with my parents.  I deposited the kitten in the apt. and went down to buy food and litter and a toy.  I left a note saying, "We have a new member of the family, can we keep her?"  My mother arrived home in the meantime, and it was love at first sight.  My sister, who already lived elsewhere, was delighted; during her late childhood and early teens she had been after our parents to get a cat.  She visited us very often after that, because she was really visiting the cat.
