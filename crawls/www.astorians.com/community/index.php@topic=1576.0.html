Gee, I guess I *AM* old!

I remember the snow of 1947. Every kid in Astoria dragged their parents (and their sled) down to Astoria Park. Even though I was just a toddler I remember all the fun we had.

The Neptune Diner used to be on the corner of 33rd Street and Astoria Boulevard. Everyone went there after all the fun and had hot chocolate and hamburgers. I remember that my sister was soaked down to her underwear. School was canceled and we all had days and days of snow fun.

When my twin sons (born 1970) were toddlers I used to wake them up -- even in the middle of the night -- if there was enough of a new snowfall to build a snowman. They tell stories today about those magical nights of fun. We would take a thermos of warm apple juice and a thermos of hot chocolate. We would build our snowman in the front garden of our apartment building. Of course, we'd wake all the neighbors, who in turn would wake their kids, and then come to join us.

Everyone, including me, also brought the dogs. They played catch the snowballs, the little ones goatherd the snow to build with, and the adults built a magnificent snow couple. There was a *very* old lady who lived on the 3rd floor who sat by her open window, covered in a blanket, and when we were ready she would throw us down potatoes for the eyes, and a carrots for the noses.

One year we got a little raunchy and built an obscene snow couple and then we poured water over them to freeze the *important* parts of their bodies!

Sorry to be so long.

I am still smiling at the memories.
