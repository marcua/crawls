Post by: stanza28 on June 25, 2008, 11:08:11 AM
Uhm, folks, you need facts. In this case the relevant facts are the law and the conditions stated in the lease you signed. Many landlords use the same "Rent stabilization Lease," which does in fact, grant a right of entry, and not only for emergencies.
The LL or Building Management here (includes me) can technically enter the other apartments in the multiple dwelling for example, when the exterminator is around or if I hear a weird noise--after all that noise could be the sound of a pipe bursting or some other condition that would affect everyone in the building.
I don't need to give written notice and it is not trespassing. (Though I don't, haven't been in anyone's unit in forever, have other things keeping me quite busy and I figure if I respect everyone's space I stand a better chance of having my space respected).
But you may have signed a different lease- start by reading the lease you signed. Having a crazy person as a landlord is a pain in the ass, but not illegal.
On the other hand, is there harassment? Are you telling us the whole story? Is she questioning your visitor because she has dementia or because your visitor represents something of which she doesn't approve?
Ex: Landlady busts your chops because you have a visitor- you remind her there is nothing in the lease that prevents this. (So far no legal issue). LL goes on to say she doesn't like that your friend is gay/Hispanic/disabled or something of that nature. (Now you have a cause of action, or at least the potential for one.)

Also, if you really want to know what goes on- install a webcam. They are cheap and efficient. Then you can see if LL is actually invading your space. For EX: You see on your webcam LL came into your unit, went near your stove and left (probably no issue);in the alternative, you see on the cam that LL came in, wandered around and took something out of your shredder or picked up your phone and pressed redial- now you have something.

Do not dismiss the possibility the LL has some illness (you say she is  shut-in)  your life may be simpler with a different LL.
If LL is sane and these perceived violations continue, get yourself one of those lawyers you don't have to pay unless you win in court. The first consultation will not cost you anything and you can fully describe the situation to them and they can tell you if there is an issue upon which relief may be granted.

Post by: Harlan on June 25, 2008, 12:36:02 PM
The thing is, although you have extensive legal rights as a renter here in NY, they're basically rights that allow you to get out of your lease. You're not going to end up with thousands of dollars of punitive damages awarded even if your landlady is crazy and breaks the laws. My suggestion* would be to write your landlady a letter indicating the things she has done that are in violation of the law and/or lease, and that if she does them again you will hold her to be in violation of the lease, will give 30 days notice, and will expect the security deposit returned per normal rules. Then move. If she doesn't send you the deposit (minus itemized deductions, with receipts), take her to small-claims court.

(* following my suggestions is usually a bad idea...! :) )
