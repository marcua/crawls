Post by: lanseaux on October 20, 2005, 06:11:38 PM
Keep a bag of pitas in the fridge, along with a container of hummus and a container of baba ganoush (like Sabra or whatever brand you like).  A container of prepared fresh veggies of your choice - lettuce, tomato, carrots, cucumber, peppers, lightly cooked broccoli or cauliflower, whatever, even avocados.  A container with sliced cheese if you want.  A bottle of Tabasco on your desk.  That gives you a base for variety every day.

If you want it hot you can nuke some of the veggies with a slice of cheese over them on a plate.  Wrap the pita up in a paper towel and nuke it for about 20 seconds to take off the chill.  Slide the warmed veggies with melted cheese into the pita.

In addition to the boca burgers Flooz mentions, I also like Quorn patties.  You heat them on paper towels, so they don't have an excessive amount of liquid.

Post by: semiastorian on October 20, 2005, 11:28:01 PM
Depending on how cleanly you can eat, you could make TVP sloppy joes - just keep dry TVP and a jar of tomato sauce on hand.

Also, some grocery stores sell packaged marinated tofu steaks - you don't need to cook them, just drain, pat dry, and shove between slices of bread.

Other options: canned refried beans; olive spread; cream cheese & cucumber; veggie pâté; etc.

And if you rotate the types of bread, too, then the possibilities are endless! :-)
