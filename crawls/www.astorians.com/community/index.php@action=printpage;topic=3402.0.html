Title: da frappe

So one of the things I keep hearing about Astoria is that it is a great place to get a frappe (an iced coffee drink especially popular in Greece - though I'm not sure if there is anything inherently Greek about it).  I've passed by cafes in the 'hood and seen people drinking them out of tall glasses.  From all appearances, it pretty much resembles iced coffee to me.  So my question is: what's the deal with da frappe?  What is it?  Do people have any particular recommendations as to where I should try one?  IS there a place known for frappes?  And am I correct in thinking that the preferred pronunciation is "frap" and not "frappay"?

Any advice concerning this matter would be appreciated.

In Astoria, at least, it's "frap-pay."

I think the main distinction is it's made with frothed Nescafe powder instead of real coffee.  I had one once and it was nice.
