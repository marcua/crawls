A Call to Action and Directions

We are asking our fellow Astoria residents to band together and send a quick email letter to the Board of Standard and Appeals about the proposed medical building at 23-25 31st Street. Be sure to send your letter before 9 a.m. Tuesday February 26th.

Please take the form letter below and either customize it (which is preferable) or send it verbatim. Please feel free to change, rework, edit and remix the sentences and focus on arguments that are important to you.

The only person we should be emailing is Jeff Mulligan at jmulligan@bsa.nyc.gov

Under no circumstances should you email BSA Board Chair Meenakshi Srinivasan directly as we need to adhere to proper rules.

Copy, modify and send the letter below via email.
Please contact normsutaria [at] gmail if you have any questions.

Thank you again for taking time out of your day to help us and prevent this situation from happening to others; words cannot express how much we appreciate it.



To: 

BSA Chairperson Meenakshi Srinivasan
in care of Jeffrey Mulligan, Executive Director jmulligan@bsa.nyc.gov

From:

YOUR NAME
YOUR ADDRESS


Subject line: Objections to BSA 315-12-BZ also known as 23-25 31st Street, Queens

Dear Chairperson Srinivasan,

I am writing you to object to BSA 315-12-BZ, also known as 23-25- 31st Street in Queens. While I don’t object to the proposed use of the building as a medical facility, I do oppose the profound effects it will have on our neighborhood here in Astoria.   

Not only will the building harm residents on 32nd Street, but the BSA’s decision will set the precedent for other situations where a commercial building is built adjacent to residences. The ruling about this building will have long-term implications for residents of Astoria when it comes to the quiet enjoyment of their homes, air quality, noise quality, light pollution and property values.

The exhaust vents for the proposed 135-car parking garage will be venting too close to homes on 32nd Street. This will inevitably lower the air quality and property values in the immediate vicinity.  Also unknown are the owners plans for the garage: how long will it be open? Will it be used for after hours restaurant parking? 

Placement of the vents also go against a Zoning Regulation (Z.R. 33-292) that requires a 30-foot rear yard of Open Space. In addition, Z.R. 33-292 does not allow for processing of any kind, and this includes the processing of air. Zoning Regulations are designed to protect residents and the character of neighborhoods. Exhaust vents directly adjacent to the rear properties will ruin the property values of homes on 32nd Street. 

The owner of the building, Yiannis Konstantinides and Pali Realty, LLC, also damaged the foundations of homes along 32nd Street during construction and have actively refused to fix the problems. This is completely unacceptable and needs to be remedied immediately. The homes and properties at the rear of his building must be restored to their original condition before construction began.

Finally, if it can happen to my fellow Astoria residents, it can happen to others, not only here in Astoria where commercial zones meet residential zones, but anywhere in New York City where this takes place.

Thank you for your time and attention.

Sincerely,

YOUR NAME
