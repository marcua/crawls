Post by: Harlan on August 22, 2006, 08:44:51 PM
OK, what I had for dinner tonight...

I used 4 plum tomatoes and some basil to make spaghetti with tomatoes and basil. Here's how that works: Start the pasta water. Peel, seed, and slice into long matchsticks the tomatoes. Slice some garlic into very thin slices (think goodfellas), then heat in a skillet with some XV olive oil until garlic just starts to color. Add tomatoes, and cook until water is released from the tomatoes. Add some salt. Cook until tomatoes are well cooked. Add a small handful of basil leaves, torn in half, and a pinch of crushed red chile peppers. Cook another couple of minutes then turn off the heat while you finish cooking the pasta. Add the cooked, drained pasta to the sauce, along with a bit more XV olive oil, and eat.

I also made a salad, with the lettuce, a couple of carrots, a half a cuke, a green pepper I had in the fridge. The lettuce with really bitter I thought, so I made a honey-mustard dressing that helped a lot. Equal parts canola oil, dijon mustard, and honey, with some salt and pepper.

Incidentally, salt makes bitter things taste less bitter. Try adding a bit of salt to black coffee some time if you don't believe me. Unless you over-salt it, it won't taste salty, just less bitter...
