I feel compelled to comment here on last night's forum since my name is attached to the DFNYC linkup here in Astoria.  As many of the people on this blog have seen, I have been posting DFNYC events here for almost 3 years.  I wear a lot of hats here in Astoria, DFNYC co-host as one of them.  I love this organization and have worked hard for the ideals that they stand for.  Last night, I was not in attendance because I was attending the rehearsal class for my son's baptism coming up.

Last night's forum was not promoted accurately.  Jeremiah knew very well that Aravella Simotas was not attending the forum weeks in advance.  His promotion of this event as a "forum" when he knew all too well that this was not going to be a forum is disappointing.  This was a "meet and greet" for Jeremiah Frei-Pearson.  It should have been promoted that way. It should have been in a non-publicly funded space that was appropriate for a meet and greet and not the Greater Astoria Historical Society.  There would have been nothing wrong with him being honest about the event.  People would have come to hear him speak!! There would be no shame in that.

So instead, he stands up and smacks an opponent for not being somewhere that he knew she wasn't going to be.  That isn't what DFNYC is all about.  A good government group like DFNYC deserves better.  The fact that he attached my name to an email promoting the event that was misleading is frustrating for me personally, but I am a big boy I'll survive  :-).  I am more disappointed that the people in attendance didn't get what they came in expecting and everyone looks silly. 

I will be strongly supporting Aravella Simotas for this seat because I think she can do the better job and bring back to Astoria the resources that we need. Jeremiah is a nice guy who I respect both personally and professionally and I have worked with him to do some good things.   But on this one, he made a mistake that disappoints me.  And he will not be the person I will be supporting for Assembly.

A life-long New Yorker, Aravella Simotas received her bachelor of arts degree, summa cum laude, from Fordham University in 1999 and her juris doctor degree from Fordham University School of Law in 2002. During law school, Ms. Simotas served as the managing editor of the Fordham Environmental Law Journal. She also worked as a district representative to the former Speaker of the New York City Council. An active community member, she has served on the Queens Community Board 1 since her May 2002 appointment by the Borough President.


Before joining Bickel & Brewer, Ms. Simotas clerked for Senior Judge Nicholas Tsoucalas and Judge Timothy C. Stanceu at the U.S. Court of International Trade (“CIT”). At the CIT, she focused on U.S. international trade law disputes between the United States and private parties. Such matters involved complex issues of administrative law. In addition, Ms. Simotas gained experience in a variety of civil and criminal matters during designation sittings at the Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit and the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York.

Ms. Simotas is admitted to practice in New York, New Jersey, the U.S. District Courts for the Southern District and Eastern District of New York, and the CIT.
