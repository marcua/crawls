A better Astoria Farmers Market
« on: March 31, 2009, 10:52:40 AM »
I sent an email to the person in charge of the farmer's markets in NYC. The Astoria Greenmarket is at 14th St. between 31st Ave and 31st Rd. on Wednesday, 8 AM - 5 PM from mid July through mid November. The hours and location for our greenmarket are less than stellar. I had asked why there wasn't a market in Astoria Park and this is what they sent back. I'm wondering if with the help of Astorians, we could come up with a better location (and maybe some weekend hours) or find a volunteer who would like to coordinate this (I work WAY too much). Here is the response I got back:


Astoria Park is a location we have looked at in past years, we also felt that it could support a vibrant farmers market.  Unfortunately we were unable to secure permission to use that site. 
 
We are always looking for new locations. As a resident, you know the neighborhood; we would appreciate your assistance identifying potential locations. Once a viable market site is identified, we may begin reaching out to individuals or agencies in control of the property, local politicians, community board members and the police precinct to begin the process of securing a permit. Please let us know of any neighborhood or business associations that we may also contact, and if you are interested in writing a letter of support. Starting early is essential, as it can take a year or more from identifying a site to opening day.
 
Greenmarket sites must be within the five boroughs of New York City. We operate markets in a wide variety of locations, from closed streets and wide sidewalks, to private plazas, parking lots, even inside parks.
The most desirable site is well known, easily visible and accessible, preferably in a neighborhood of medium to high density, which has significant existing foot traffic.

There are other important considerations in selecting a site:

Sites that can accommodate parking for farmers trucks are desirable.

Toilet access is essential for farmers. While restaurants and stores sometimes provide this, locations such as a community center are preferable. This could also provide a storage space for a table, chairs, benches, umbrellas, signs, bags, literature and other miscellany a market requires.

If you have a particular location in mind that you would like us to look at for a farmers market, please provide the following information:

Your name
Community groups or organizations you are affiliated with (if any)
Your phone or email address
Borough & Neighborhood
Brief neighborhood profile
Address or intersection
Name of property owner (if known)
Type of location (wide sidewalk, paved plaza, park, parking lot, etc)
What is the primary use of the site? 
Is it an area with a high volume of pedestrian traffic?  Is the high traffic area every day, or only certain days?
Is there anything else we should take into consideration when evaluating this site?

Markets always do better when a friendly face is present from the neighborhood. Local sponsors, such as a block association, BID, church, community center, co-op, tenants association, or even an ad-hoc committee can be invaluable. A dedicated individual, with the time, energy, and the ability to get things done, should represent the sponsoring group. It would be especially helpful if the sponsor had a nearby office, apartment or storefront where materials could be stored (fliers, a table, etc), emergency phone calls made or received and toilet facilities are available. The sponsorâ€™s representative should plan to be at the market each day. He or she will assist the manager, greeting and getting to know farmers and customers, helping and providing information to people, passing out fliers and helping with problems. The local sponsor will be depended on to circulate fliers in the immediate neighborhood.

Thanks for helping keep an eye out, and for supporting local agriculture!
