Pizza D'oh!
« on: May 07, 2006, 11:25:29 AM »
Last night I tried an experiment with Pillsbury refrigerated pizza dough.  I've seen such a product used on the Food Network on the shows that are about quick meals and taking shortcuts so I figured it could be quite tasty.  Wrong!  First, it smelled unpleasant when I opened the package.  It had a strange consistency, unpronounceable ingredients, and when I baked it, it just came out weird - kinda spongy - so I said, "fuggedaboutit" and decided to make my own pizza dough.  We spent 3 days of class on bread so I felt pretty confident about it.  I didn't even use my mixer.  I just got in there like a prairie woman and kneaded the heck out of it.  I used a recipe for Italian bread and added olive oil and a little extra salt to the dough.  Unfortunately, I cheaped out on the toppings.  Had I looked a little harder for good mozzarella and pepperoni (I decided to keep it simple), the pizza with homemade dough would have rocked.  Next time, I'm going to try a focaccia dough recipe to see if the texture is a bit different and I'll make the crust thinner, and I'll use high quality toppings, for Pete's sake!  Maybe bust out a little prosciutto, some parmesan, or sun-dried tomatoes, mushrooms, sausage . . . .

The moral of the story, kids . . . Don't use store-bought refrigerator dough.  You'll be disrespecting your pepperoni.  And if you don't want to make your own dough, I'll make it for you!  I just can't let bad things happen to good pizza.
