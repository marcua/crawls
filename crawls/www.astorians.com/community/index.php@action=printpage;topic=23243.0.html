Title: Composting at area Greenmarkets!
Post by: westernqnscomposts on July 31, 2012, 03:43:54 PM
For the Astorians out there who are always looking for new ways to save money, reduce their waste and give back to the community there is a way to easily do all three. Composting your kitchen scraps is a great way to cut back on the amount of garbage that sits on your curb once a week (and I think anyone can appreciate a reduction in this not so lovely fragrance), while at the same time contributing to making Astoria even more beautiful than it already is.

Food scraps constitute about 17.7%, approximately 650,000 tons annually, of the total waste that the NYC Department of Sanitation collects each year. In order to use this waste in the most productive way possible Western Queens Compost Initiative accepts and locally processes food scraps as part of the NYC Compost Project Local Organics Recovery Program funded by the NYC Department of Sanitation.

Kitchen scraps are collected and turned into fresh, nutritious soil. Soil which is given back to the communities who have donated their time, and former refuse, to our processing centers.

Drop by one close to you and soon you will see the fruitfulness of your donations!
