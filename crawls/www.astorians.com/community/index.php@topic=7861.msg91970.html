 I just got back from the site of the 9/11 attack in NYC. The site where thousands of Americans and others lost their lives after being attacked by barbarians from the 12th Century , who traveled from their lands thousands of miles away, lived among us, ate and drank with us and then killed us. I was here that bright, shining morning, in sharp contrast to today, which was raining and cooler. The site has now changed, building has progressed, but, six people I knew are gone forever. My childhood friend 'Benny', was on the 82nd Floor of Tower 2, I saw him the saturday before and he was to pass by in the evening, where I was to be working, at the election site that was on that day in 2001. A lot of ugly things have been said since that day, about that day, and about those who are fighting this war, but, one fact remains, those buildings were bought down by an enemy, still out there, an enemy of civilization. Now, what are you and I gonna do about it?

I find  people  who preach  'Peace' while  'wearing  pictures  of  chairman Mao', to be extremely disingenuous, and  at this  point , to be , whether  they know it or not, siding and  helping the enemy.

It  amazes  me that any  woman or  gay  or lesbian person, would  not  want to fight  these 12 century  barbarians?  I don't get  it?  Instead  many so-called  'Peace' people  put all their  energy  in shutting down the war  against  these insane  people.

I too am very sorry for your loss FZ - and the loss of many others.

I think that some just feel that perhaps there's more to be gained by society for us to figure out how to prevent stuff like this from happening again in the future. Part of that can be finding those who brought about the events of 9/11, but the discussion needs to go a lot deeper than that. It's not just gays, women, and lesbians who are interested in peace - there are many (even those who directly lost family on 9/11) who are interested in peace.

If I may post a question back to you; do you support justice at any cost?

I'm not against bringing criminals to justice, but unfortunately a lot of innocent people often die in the process - which, ironically is what those barbarians thought they were doing that day - bringing "justice" to the United States. They would argue that we invaded their land first (military bases), lived among them, and brought death to them. I'm not saying they are right, but there's a certain circular logic here.

What I observe is that this kind of justice (the retributive kind) only creates more death in its wake. I want those who live after me to not have to pay for the vengeance I'd exact today.

For this reason I find the dialog of peace an acceptable alternative. Not always, but mostly.
