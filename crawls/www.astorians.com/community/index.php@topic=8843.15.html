I do agree that most people have anything they want, but I don't like giving gift certificates or gift cards at all.  I like to try and get something that they'd want, but never really be willing to buy on their own. 

* Fancy umbrella.  Everyone likes them, but most of us just drop down a few bucks for the cheapies.  Get someone a nice one: nice patterned, curved wooden handle.
* High end Japanese Kitchen knife.  We all cook but most of us do not have great knives.  Just one or two of these knives is a great gift.
* Very, very nice bottle of wine.  Mentioned before, but doing the same with cognac, single malt scotch, brandy, is great as well.
* Pay for a year of a cable channel if they love a cable channel but haven't given over to getting it yet.
* Get them a years subscription of the Sunday NY Times.
* Very good seats at a concert/opera/ballet/dance.

Can I just say that I love getting gift cards? Especially to clothing stores. I don't spend much money on clothes for myself (I can't bring myself to get a funky new shirt when student loans need to be paid), but if there's money sitting there waiting to be spent on me, and I have no choice but to buy clothes with it, and I feel like getting a cool new pair of jeans, I'm getting my cool new pair of jeans! It's not like I'm gonna buy groceries or pay rent with it. I prefer gift cards to specific stores rather than cash or Visa gift cards because if I get cash/Visa cards, I'll convince myself to use the money for something practical - I actually used a Visa gift card I got for graduation to pay a ticket. How boring is that?

I got a bunch of gift cards last Christmas (I actually asked for them, gasp! And I told people what stores I liked), and I didn't use them all right away. I found a leftover one in August, and it was so nice to treat myself to some summer clothes that were on sale (and with the weather this year, I wore them into October).

This might not be all warm and fuzzy, but it's what I like.
