While I don't agree that riots are "the answer" -- I'm not sure I agree that they are the embodiment of the victim mentality either.  

If something is so deeply broken within the system, won't the problem continue to worsen until it reaches a critical breaking point?

That the rioting is so wide spread in France suggests that it's more than just a small group of angsty youth.

I think it's a step in the right direction not to say "this is wrong" but "this is wrong, why is it happening?".  

France is also a country with quite a history of civil unrest.  It's not quick to change -- but when change does come about, it's sometimes driven by social uprising.

Anyhow, I've been in Paris somewhat recently so it's a little fresh in my mind.
