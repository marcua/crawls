Socrates Sculpture Park 2005 Grant Application Guidelines Now Available on

our Website



Deadline: January 10, 2005



Socrates Sculpture Park offers grants and studio residencies for artists to

create and exhibit in the Park.  Artists who wish to submit an application

should visit our website at http://www.socratessculpturepark.org to download

the new guidelines or call Socrates Sculpture Park at 718-956-1819.



Artists can apply for one or more of the following exhibition categories:

_ 2005 Spring Exhibition

_ 2005 Emerging Artist Fellowship Program

_ Open Space



Grants and fellowships are not available to artists who are enrolled in a

school, college, or university program.



Artists who are invited to exhibit at the Park will be provided with

financial support, administrative and technical assistance, a one- to

six-month residency in the outdoor studio, and access to facilities,

materials and equipment. For complete details and requirements, please

review the application guidelines on our website:

http://www.socratessculpturepark.org/Applications/Grant_Application.htm



Completed applications must be received at the Socrates Sculpture Park

office by 5:00 pm on Monday, January 10, 2005.

The Bronx Museum AIM: Artist in the Marketplace



Deadline: January 18, 2005



The Artist in the Marketplace (AIM) Program was established to realize the

Bronx MuseumÂ¹s goal of providing professional opportunities to emerging

artists in the metropolitan area. The program features guest speakers:

artists, accountants, collectors, critics, curators, dealers, and lawyers

who provide information, instruction, and professional guidance by

addressing practical areas of concern to artists, such as curatorial

practice, copyright law, exhibition and public art opportunities, gallery

representation, grants writing, income taxes, and marketing.



This free seminar program is offered in the fall and the spring, and meets

for 12 weeks on Tuesday evenings, 6 - 8 p.m. Enrollment is limited to 18

artists per semester. The program culminates with an annual group exhibition

and accompanying catalogue.



Applications for this program are available on the Bronx Museum website at

http://www.bxma.org/entry.php?passeid=10

or by calling the Bronx Museum at 718-681-6000



Applications for the Spring 2005 semester, which begins Mach 2005, must be

postmarked no later than January 18, 2005.
