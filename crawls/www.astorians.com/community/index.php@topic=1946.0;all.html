The best baking supply store for pros is on 22nd street between 5th and 6th ave.  can be expensive but its a good starting place.  as for desserts with flair try zagat? maybe they have a listing for that by now and go to ny times online and search for chocolates / desserts in the dining section.  There are really many great chocolate shops and there should be a listing somewhere. 

check out jacque torres shops in tribeca and dumbo for great truffles.  go to payard upper east side for exquisite desserts. 

desserts are a big now in this town, I bet any number of friends could point you to their favorite upscale restaurant for a selection.

Id also ask your instructors for their favorite examples.  Good luck and dont forget us when you start experimenting.

Callebaut baking chocolate's not too hard to find -- it's regularly at the Garden of Eden store I frequent (for their reasonably good selection of high-end artisinal chocolate bars, on 14th St and around 5th Ave.). And a second for Jacques Torres. When it warms up a little more, you absolutely have to go to his factory store in DUMBO, get some truffles, maybe some coffee at the coffeeshop across the (cobblestone) street, then walk over the Brooklyn Bridge. A better use of two hours I can't imagine, expecially for a new transplant to the city...
