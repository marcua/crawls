Re: Sanfords. How is it?
« Reply #21 on: November 16, 2008, 10:14:55 AM »
We've visited four times now since the renovation: once for dessert, twice for dinner, and once for breakfast.  I found the previous decor and layout to be a bit "eh," but the space is much nicer now and I find it to be a pretty relaxing place to have a meal.

Some thoughts:

Weekday breakfast: $5 for french toast, sausage, juice, and coffee.  The french toast was pretty standard, the sausage was good, but not exceptional, and the juice was in a small glass.  That said, this may be the best thing you can still get for five bucks.  The place wasn't busy (it was around 9:30am), so this would be a good time to go do some work there.

Dinner: Wow, the bistro burger is to die for: pepper jack cheese, bacon, and some twisted sort of aioli.  Deluxe comes with fries, lettuce and tomato and its under 10 dollars.  My fellow diner likes trying different beers, and thought the list had some interesting choices: he ordered a Japanese beer that had a hint of pine.  We spent some time sitting at the bar and the place seems serious about wine--they have a major sealing system that keeps the bottles fresh (unlike so many places I see that just shove a cork in the top of the bottle).

Dessert: A very serviceable brownie bowl and a superb banana in a fried tortilla with ice cream.  Both around 5 dollars.

Overall: I'd say this is a good value, actually.  The food is good, the prices seem to be in line with what you're getting, and the decor is nice.

Sanford's has completed the transition from diner to trendy restaurant.  It's noisier and darker, and I suppose they've bid good riddance to the downscale types who used to eat at the counter.  I no longer feel comfortable there.

I remarked that I missed their fries, which are no longer on the menu.  I especially miss the sweet potato fries.  The guy who seems to be the manager said they still have fries, and pointed to the sides at the bottom of one page of the menu.  I pointed out it says "sweet mash, garlic mash" but no fries.  In any case, they no longer come free with wraps or panini.

But I must face it, I'm one of those downscale types.  I'll still keep Sanford's in mind for ordering out, but if I want to eat in, I'll eat at Michael's.
