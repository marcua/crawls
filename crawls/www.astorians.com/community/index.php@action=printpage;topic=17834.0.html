Yep, it's a handheld and I'm using the stock rubber antenna.  I don't think you'd need anything more, I can pick up precincts from all boroughs including SI.  Height of the antenna and local obstructions are probably the most important - I'm in a 3rd floor apt with a west-facing window. 

I have a bunch of frequencies programmed in, I used a program and a cable to load them up from my computer - I think the program is called pro92.exe and it's from like Win95 days but still works on XP.  I found all the frequencies on free online sites and I could probably export my list.  Feel free to contact me.

I do hear quite a lot, I have the 108/114 dispatch set as a priority channel so if there's any traffic it always jumps to that frequency, and it's almost always there, I think they use radio for all dispatch and communications.  I honestly haven't looked into new scanner technology in years, just pulled mine out a few days ago out of boredom.  I'm originally from Iowa where I was a volunteer weather spotter for a few years, and I was quite surprised at how open everything is over the air here.  Back in small-town Iowa, they had digital trunking for almost every agency, so I had to upgrade to this scanner to hear them.  On the other hand, the small number of radios in use meant that huge parts of the state could lease channels on one trunking system.
