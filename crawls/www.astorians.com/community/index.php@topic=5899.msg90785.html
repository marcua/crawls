Here are my thoughts as a recent small, female college graduate and 1 year resident of Astoria.  Things I've learned: it's better to live near the N/W train because it very rarely has service interruptions.  The G/R/V trains tend to run terribly on weekends, running express past your stop one way, etc.  I did live near the Steinway stop, but when I would come home late at night, I would have to walk 6-8 blocks because the R was never running properly, or was very infrequent.  Anyone else have this problem? 

I have recently moved to Broadway area (one block from the N/W) and LOVE it.  I found a huge studio in a private house for $975 in June with no brokers fee on craigslist.  If you look good on paper (good job, reliable, good references) it may be easier to find stuff on craigslist for rental by owner-- be persistent, but friendly, and try to give as much info about your character up front.  It may have helped that I'm a female, who knows. 

My first apt in Astoria was a brand new building, which I found more trouble than it was worth.  Nothing worked right, and the landlord was crazy.  He wanted to pick up rent every month at 9am (and what professionals are home at 9am? Or else very few!)or you had to leave your rent right outside your door where anyone could get it.  If you needed repairs, they didn't have a key, so you had to be there.  What a pain!  I heard all sorts of crazy stories about the landlord from other tenants (GFE holding Corp- George Eliot- anyone else have problems?) I think he owns lots of buildings--very greedy too.  We also had a terrible real estate agent-- Jennifer Liu.  Not sure what company she works for.  Very pushy and annoying.

21 Street and Broadway can be a little more desolate at night, and I personally wouldn't want to live on that particular corner.  There's not much in that area, not very residential or with restaurants, and I have not enjoyed the walks to Socrates park from there.  That being said, I haven't heard of anything bad going on, except that the motel down that way rents by the hour.  If the apartment is nice, and is a good price, I would take it and just be aware of your surroundings when walking home at night.  You are not that far away from the train and stuff to do on Broadway, so that's a plus.  I live around 21 Avenue and 27 Street and the street is always deserted at night - but it's more residential so I feel safer.  That doesn't always mean anything though, as bad things happen anywhere.  You live in the city, be aware of your surroundings, walk with a purpose, and take a cab after 2am from NYC to Astoria.  Pretty standard.

41St and 31 Ave would be great.  Nice area, lots to do in the area, although a little longer walk to train.  I would say 30 Ave and Broadway represent the heart of Astoria, and Ditmars is cool for the Italian and Greek influences.

West of 21st street
« Reply #102 on: October 01, 2007, 03:14:19 PM »
This is in response to the last post, and to the general sense on this thread that anyplace west of 21st is completely undesirable. It is true that west of 21st street is further from the train (although some people prefer being able to take the bus to the subway and having more options than the N) and has fewer stores and restaurants. What’s not true is the idea that 21st is a magic line beyond which the neighborhood becomes dangerous and sketchy.

Some of the pluses of being on the west side of Astoria: You’ll often have great views of the skyline and the river. You’re closer to the parks and the waterfront. You’re further from the noise, trash, traffic and parking problems of some of the busier  commercial streets.

As for 21st and Broadway in particular, it’s not even remotely desolate. There’s a supermarket and 24-hour diner across the street and enough foot traffic to feel safe at any  hour.
