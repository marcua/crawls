No matter where you are there will always be crap to deal with. If it isn't a boss it will be a co-worker. If not a co-worker then a client. If not a client then a project manager or consultant. I think it is difficult to really know if your boss or work environment will be a good one until you are in it. I loved the people in my last job, but they didn't handle stress very well (lots of yelling, crying, swearing) - mostly great to hang out with but not entirely great to work with. I really like my current job because I have an excellent boss who doesn't belittle me or the rest of us on our team - but there are a lot of people that are more difficult to deal with and I just have to learn how to handle it and thank the gods that they aren't my boss.

Since this is your direct boss I would agree that you should keep your eyes open. What about the company? Do you like it? If you like the company is there a way for you to stay, but move to a different department? If you can financially swing it maybe try an internship or temp until you find an environment that works for you. Companies like to hire interns and temps because that means that they are hiring someone they've already trained in and know.

Make sure that when you interview that you always ask how the work environment is and if people get along. After all - this is not just your interview, you are interviewing them as well. They may not always answer honestly, but it's good to ask anyway. I've noticed that it is a question that can take some people off-guard as they are not always expecting it. If you want to make sure someone is a good boss, just ask them, "How do you think you are as a boss?"

Good luck and I hope that you can figure out a good solution soon!
