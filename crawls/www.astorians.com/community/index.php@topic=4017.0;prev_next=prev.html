(7) Help the NY Democratic Lawyers Council protect voting rights on Election Day

A message from Cindy Warner of the New York Democratic Lawyers' Council:

It is an exciting time to be a New York Democrat - not only do we have a great top of the ticket, but we've also got a real chance to swing the State Senate blue and send a whole lot of Republican Congressmen off to retirement. But Democrats won't win unless everyone who is registered and eligible to vote can do so. That is why we at the New York Democratic Lawyers Council are organizing and training a statewide network of attorneys and others to work on Election Day to monitor the polls, inform voters of their rights, and respond to any problems swiftly and effectively. We'd like you to join us. Help us make sure that every voter can vote and every vote cast is counted. Please volunteer to help us on election day at this link: www.nydlc.org/live/signup.php

(1) Make Your Vote Count Forum: Pictures & Take Action to Protect the Votes
           - Tell the media to report voting machine problems, tell candidates not to concede

On Wednesday, Oct. 25, DFNYC & Community Free Democrats co-organized the Make Your Vote Count Forum, in which distinguished panelists discussed the possibilities for new voting machines in New York when we replace our lever machines in future elections.  To see pictures of the forum, visit this page at the DFNYC website:  http://www.dfnyc.org/cms/node/87764

Mark Crispin Miller, Professor at NYU, expressed the very real possibility that there will be problems with electronic voting machines in elections across the country tomorrow.  We hope that does not happen, but if it does, we want to ensure that the mainstream media investigates and reports on the problems.  Please take a moment to send *individual* emails to the following editors and request that their newspapers investigate and report on electronic voting machine issues and other election problems:

Bill Keller, Executive Editor -- New York Times:  billkeller@nytimes.com

Jill Abramson, Managing Editor -- New York Times:  abramson@nytimes.com

Gwen Flanders, Washington Editor -- USA Today: gflanders@usatoday.com

Marcus Brauchli, National News Senior Editor -- Wall Street Journal: marcus.brauchli@dowjones.com

Michael Silverman, Managing Editor -- Associated Press: msilverman@ap.org

Sample email:
Dear ____________,
In many places across the country, there have been reports of major problems with electronic voting machines in past elections, in this year's primaries, and even in early voting for the 2006 midterm elections. I believe it is possible that problems with electronic voting may influence the outcome of these elections.  So as a concerned citizen and newspaper reader, I urge you to investigate and report on allegations of problems with electronic voting machines and other irregularities.   
Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Your Address]

Tell candidates not to concede:  When there are significant allegations of election tampering and the losing candidate concedes, the media stops covering the problems.  Please contact the campaigns of the candidates you have been supporting and encourage them not to concede if there are election problems.  See this post by DFNYC members at Daily Kos, and please "recommend" it if you are a Kossack:  http://www.dailykos.com/story/2006/11/5/202229/433
