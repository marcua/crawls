Quote from: mirki2 on July 29, 2006, 11:16:39 AM
Doctor carry a lot of power but they are sworn to an oath to save lives and act ethically.
Doctors need to be able to exert expertise, that is true, but they also need to listen to their patients.

On this issue I think we are talking about doctors who indeed believe they are acting in the interest of their patient. Don't forget that to a doctor who believes that human life starts at conception, the doctor believes that the fetus is their human patient as well. In the doctors mind (though I see it differently) they feel that the patient is asking them to kill another patient, and this is against their sworn duty to not bring about harm to any human being. It's the doctor's sworn duty to protect both mother and child (from their perspective).

On one side you have people who "believe" that a human becomes human at conception, and on the other side you have people who "believe" that it starts at some point later on. Neither side is scientifically proved beyond a reasonable doubt so I think it's equally objectionable for either side to shove their belief down anyone else's throat. Both sides should be more accommodating until the fundamental questions of life can be answered in a scientific way.

Until that larger question is answered I can't fault a doctor for refusing to act against their conscience and oath any more than I would want to be faulted for refusing to do something against my conscience.

I'd support a law that, in the interest of both women and society at large, requires hospitals to maintain a list of local resources that an objecting doctor could refer a patient to when doctors object for reasons of conscience. Something along those lines I think would be a reasonable and freedom-preserving measure I think and enables patients to seek further treatment elsewhere.

Making it available over the counter however I think is a great step in the right direction because even if a particular pharmacist refuses to carry it it increases the chances that there are other places in the town that readily have it, including its availability over the internet.
