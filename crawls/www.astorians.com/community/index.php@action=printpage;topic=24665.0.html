Title: 10th NYC Musical Saw Festival - June 1st
Post by: AstoriaArts on March 25, 2013, 04:30:29 PM
Please mark your calenders and reserve Saturday, June 1st, 2pm for the Musical Saw Festival in Astoria:
(https://sphotos-b.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-ash3/165493_10151292983002735_699361426_n.jpg)
Concert & art exhibit revolving around the 300 year old art of playing music on a carpenter's handsaw.
This is a video Time Magazine did about the last festival: [size=78%]http://youtu.be/eON-p4afeTkF (http://youtu.be/eON-p4afeTkF)[/size][/color]
For the past 10 years Astoria became a pilgrimage spot for musical saw players from all over the world. The festival is in the Guinness Book of Records for the 'Largest Musical saw Ensemble' with 53 saw players playing together, beating the previous record from Poland (28 saw players). NYC Mayor Michael Bloomberg said he was "proud to celebrate the return of the Musical Saw Festival to Queens".
The festival includes world premiers of music written especially for the musical saw as well as the 'Chorus of the Saws' - about 40 musical saw players playing together. Many music styles (classical, contemporary, pop, folk, show-tunes, etc.) performed by musical saw soloists & ensembles from all over the world.
Also - A chamber orchestra will accompany musical saw player Natalia 'Saw Lady' Paruz in a presentation of the musical saw as an ensemble instrument.
Where: Trinity Church, 31-18 37th Street (37th Street at 31st Avenue), Astoria, NY
Admission: $10
