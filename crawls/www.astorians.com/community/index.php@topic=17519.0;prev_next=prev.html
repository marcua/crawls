Re: I-Pod Touch

I've had a 2nd Gen Touch for almost a year now and also quite happy with it.  Compared to other IPods I think all of the main functions (organizing and playing music, videos, photos, etc) are improved.  There's also lots of free or cheap apps, and surfing the internet is way easier than on any of the Verizon Touch phones I've had (curious about the Droid though).  It's easy to configure your Wi-Fi connection for you home and office and it will remember them and autoconnect when in range.  The downside is, you do need a Wi-Fi to enjoy a lot of what the Touch has to offer, and the Wi-Fi connections are surprisingly hard to come by in the city sometimes.  So having Google Maps is great, but sometimes you can't use it when you most need to.  Though, one of the smartest things I did was before going on vacation to Hungary and Poland was bookmarking a ton of locations in the Ipod - which saved all the maps I needed ahead of time.  Also it's an Ipod, so battery life leaves a little to be desired.  The only other thing that annoys the hell out of me is that it's not a phone, meaning I have to carry my cell phone and my Ipod.  In the winter it's not so bad, I can put them all in my jacket.  In the summer it drives me crazy because I have to cram all this technology in my pocket.  Not sure what it says about me that I can't leave any of it home.
