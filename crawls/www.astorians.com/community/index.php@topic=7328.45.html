Quote from: johnny4evah! on January 29, 2009, 10:05:36 AM
Egads! Sounds like Zenon needs a session with Gordon Ramsay, but I wouldn't be surprised if they have a steady flow of customers anyway. Sometimes people are simply creatures of habit and continue to patronize crappy restaurants, allowing them to get away with restaurant crimes and stay in business forever.

And sometimes a restaurant, like anything else, just has a bad day.  I've been to Zenon many, many times and have had nothing but very good experiences there.  No lukewarm food or burnt potatoes that I can remember, and I find that their menu has some unique Greek/Cypriot dishes that a lot of other restaurants in Astoria don't offer.  I particularly love their avgolemono soup and their seftalies, the latter being the best I've ever had.

Other Greek restaurants in Astoria I'd highly recommend are Philoxenia, Corner 26 (very no-frills, but very good food), and Stamatis.  Akti and Aliada aren't too shabby either.  I haven't been to Ta Vlahika (former Anna's Corner) or Aegean Cove yet.  I also think Kyklades is good, but rather overrated for what it is.  I've soured on Agnanti, after *multiple* (not just one...I understand if there's a bad day, like I mentioned above) negative experiences with food (on more than one occasion served ice cold) and service.

Loukamadis on Ditmars and 45th is really fantastic. Same chef owner that was at Ploes. Out of the way now yes, but just so nice, friendly and delicious. His regional specialty is casseroles. 

I like seeing a cat around. Without one there will be mice (or worse) around at all times.  A restaurant is not a self contained space that can be sealed off. Basements connected to other basements is the reason for rodents getting around. Same for places connected by dry wall and not brick walls.  A cat actually keeps most mice away just by their presence. Mice smell them.

As for Opa Opa, we love the tacky fake plant vine covered walls and ceilings, fake cave like construction painted bright white and all the old and dated decor.  Never had more that a plate of mixed grill there so can't really say much about the chef skills.
