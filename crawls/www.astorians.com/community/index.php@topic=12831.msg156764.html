Great topic. This has been a busy time for home cooking.

Eye round rubbed with paste (rosemary, garlic, lemon zest. salt, olive oil) sit overnight. Sear on all sides then low roast till 130F.  Squeeze over 1 lemon and some more of the paste and olive oil. Let sit 15 min before carving. I like to add small chunks of onion and potatoes to pan while cooking.

Flank steak with green rice and Peruvian green sauce.  Oil and salt/pepper steaks overnight. Sear hard and finish in oven till rare.
For rice, puree some 1/2 onion, 4 clovegarlic, 1 bunch spinach, 1/2 bunch cilantro, oil.
Sautee till cook off water. Add 1 package Sazon or similar.  Add more oil if needed.
Add rice and saute to coat with oil and color.  Add water or chix stock.
Add 1cup green olives stuffed with pimento and 1 can pigeon peas. 
Bring to boil uncovered then cover and reduce flame to simmer.

PERUVIAN GREEN SAUCE (approx recipe)
2 cups iceberg lettuce
1/2 bunch cilantro
2 jalapenos. Seeded and deribbed
1/4 cup white vinegar
 2 tablespoon mayonnaise
1/2 cup or less oil
1/2 medium onion
2 clove garlic
Juice of 1/2 lime
salt
a little bit of water (not a lot of water)
Combine ingredients in a blender. Adjust ingredients to taste.  Surprisingly it is usually more lettuce that makes the taste work so well. Should be a bright lite green. Let sit for an hour and check heat. The jalapenos heat will build a bit over time.
Do not use food processor, will not give you correct smooth consistency.
Serve with steaks, roast chicken, anything.

Rice onto platter, sliced steak over top with lime garnish. Serve sauce on side to spoon over every everything. It's freakin' fantastic.

A quick and simple soup

1-14 1/2 oz. can of stewed or diced tomatoes(juice included)
1-15 1/2 oz. can of red kidney or cannellini beans,drained and rinsed well
1/2 of an 8 oz. can of tomato sauce
3 cups of water
1 cup of frozen mixed vegetables
1 cup of uncooked elbow or other small pasta
Salt,pepper,and basil to taste
Grated cheese(I prefer locatelli) for sprinkling on soup

Put tomatoes(if they're too big,you can cut them smaller),beans,tomato sauce,and water in a 3 quart pot and bring to a boil.Season with salt,pepper,and basil.Lower heat to medium and stir in frozen vegetables.cook about 10 to 15 minutes.Lower to a simmer and stir in pasta.Soup is done when the pasta is done.Top with grated cheese if desired..

If you have leftovers,the pasta and beans will most likely soak up the liquid.Add the remaining tomato sauce and enough water to cover.Heat up.

Mojo Criollo is a Cuban marinade.  Here's a blurb from Goya's website:

Mojo Criollo
Zesty Mojo Criollo with garlic, onion, and citrus for tender, tasty meat and poultry.

Ingredients

    * 1/4 cup lime juice, fresh squeezed
    * 1/2 cup orange juice, fresh squeezed
    * 1/2 cup olive oil
    * 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    * 6 garlic cloves, pressed
    * salt, to taste
    * black pepper, fresh ground, to taste

  The actual recipe calls for sour oranges--(Citrus aurantium, L.), also known as bitter, bigarade, or Seville orange, which are hard to find here in Queens.  I pick them up at the Mercado Mi Bandera in Union City, NJ (518 32nd St, Union City, NJ 07087), a great Caribbean/Central and South American grocery store.  The combination of oranges and limes come pretty close to the flavor of sour oranges.
  Goya's product is decent and readily available in most local groceries in the "Spanish" food section.   I prefer buying imported brand--that cost about the same--in UC or Washington Heights or making my own when time allows.

  As for Mi Bandera, I would suggest a quick trip through the Lincoln Tunnel for anyone interested in Cuban and Central/South American food to check it out as well as the rest of Bergenline Avenue between UC and West New York, NJ.  There are a lot of great restaurants and markets along the B Line.  Yikes!  Queens is "bad enough", but New Jersey!
  UC/WNY is, actually, pretty accessible even if you don't have a car.  Simply, catch an NJT 156 bus at Port Authority and get off at the 48th Street and Bergenline stop ($2.80 each way) about three stops past the tunnel.  Total travel time from the PA Bus Terminal: fifteen minutes + tunnel access time.  Walk south for Union City or north for West New York, you'll be surprised at what you might find.
  When it's time to come home, simply hop on a bus or jitney back to NYCPA along Bergenline Avenue.

What's difficult is that I'm mostly a vegetarian (except for fish) and the BF is a carnivore. He's a really good sport about my cooking, since he doesn't usually eat much dinner anyway, but I could use some recipes that mimic the taste of meat.

I'm a vegetarian, and a very lazy cooker... some very easy ideas that may satisfy you and your bf's taste:

1.  Quesadillas.  If you just load it up with cheese and fry it up, very unhealthy, so I do it this way:  Chop some onion into small pieces (easier if you have a food processor).  Then I do the same thing with a few handfuls of spinach.  Mix together and add black beans.  Take a small handful of shredded mexican cheese and mix it through. Then take a big burrito shell and sprinkle some of the shredded cheese on 1/2 of it.  Put your onion/bean/spinach/cheese mix on top, then cover with some more cheese.  Fold in half and then cook your quesadilla in a frying pan coated in cooking spray.  Smother with salsa and enjoy.

2.  Veggie tacos.  Go to the store and buy powdered taco mix, and then prepare using the instructions on the envelope, and use veggie crumbles instead meat. 

3.  Omelettes are good ANY time.  Even better with pancakes or french toast!

4.  Buy any store bought mix for rice/pasta and then add veggies and fake meat (as was mentioned by someone else--quorn is amazing!  I like the naked cutlets, which aren't breaded).
