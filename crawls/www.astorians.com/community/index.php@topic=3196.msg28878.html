Places I've often recommended ...

On Long Island:
Cold Spring Harbor
Greenpoint
Montauk

Upstate:
Catskills (several little towns in the area)
Saratoga Springs
Woodstock
Kingston
(actually most of the little towns along the Hudson, including Tarrytown, Rye, and Nyack)

Out of state (3-hour drive or less):
Delaware Water Gap
Mystic, CT
New Haven, CT
various towns in Litchfield County, CT (Lake Compounce! woo hoo!)
Norwalk, CT
Baltimore, MD
Block Island, RI
Rockport/Gloucester, MA
Lancaster County, PA

I was just saying to JD that we had to set up a weekend in October for apple picking (and, yep, pumpkins, too ...  :mrgreen:); could make it an all in one day, as Bear Mountain hikes are awesome (as are any upstate mountain hikes) -- usually go with my aunt & uncle, who live up there, though, we tend to accidentally unearth (so to speak) very, very old cemeteries ...  :-o

One of the most relaxing places I know is New Hope, PA/Lambertville, NJ.  It's very touristy, but I don't mind touristy.  Quaint colonial-era houses, lots of trees, riverside inns, art galleries, a great bookstore with character.  Good food--not great, but good.  Waitresses who call you "hon."  There's not much to do at night unless you're a gay biker (no offense to any gay bikers on the board), but it's a nice place to depressurize.
