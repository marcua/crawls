I just switched vets. I was with Astoria Veterinary Group on Steinway for a few years, but
wasn't pleased with the care my dog was getting. I just took him over to the Animal Clinic
of Long Island City (corner of 36th Avenue & 32nd Street).

They have 3 vets there and we saw Dr. Alvarez. Very nice and also very frank about what's
wrong and what needs to be done. It's a nice, clean pleasant office and they even have cats
who are up for adoption.

I was impressed because my dog's had a recurring abcess that's been getting worse and worse.
My previous vet had him on many rounds of meds that didn't work and I wanted another opinion.

I not only got one that made much more sense, but the office even called to check on my dog
a couple of days later and told me to not to hesitate to bring him in anytime I'm worried.

Their fees were reasonable too.

Good luck.

Well, I had a 10:15 appointment and got in to an exam room around 11.  So I did have to wait a while, but it wasn't too bad.  It went fast.

I thought the vet was nice, and she seemed quite knowledgeable.  She took some time before she saw us to read through all the paperwork the shelter had given me and was very good about explaining what my kittens have (upper respiratory infection and tapeworms - very common in shelter animals) and why the shelter de-worming and shots didn't help (they were only treated for roundworms and the shots were not comprehensive enough or administered after they had already caught the virus). 

She de-wormed them for flatworms and gave us anti-biotics and an immune system booster for the infection.  They should be fine.

I had heard a number of comments about her staff being rude, but I had no problems with them.  Perhaps we got onto their good side when we said that we were thinking of renaming the kittens "Sneezy" and "Snotty", due to their colds...the vet assistant really seemed to be tickled by that...

We're scheduled for a follow-up visit in 10 days to see how they are progressing.

Overall, I'd say it was a good experience, and decently priced as well.

I had brought my cat to Animal Clinic of LIC (32nd St and 36th Ave) a few years back. He was really sick, unable to walk. They took x-rays, diagnosed him with cancer, and suggested we see a specialist at the Animal Medical Center in Manhattan. Brought him there and they also misdiagnosed him, said he broken his leg. Meanwhile the poor cat was getting worse. Finally, we brought him back to the AMC where he was correctly diagnosed with Cushings and had to be euthanized -- poor little guy was suffering so much. Animal Clinic of LIC, while wrong with their diagnosis, knew that it was a case they couldn't handle and suggested we see a specialist -- and they did call to check up on him (albeit after he had died). I'd rather a vet who knows their office's limitations, than one quick to make money off of desperate pet owners.

Now we get our vet care in Brooklyn at Cobble Hill Animal Hospital (Dr. Walter Maddox). Awesome place and awesome vet (albeit a bit on the odd side).

I've used both Steinway Court Vets and Animal Clinic of LI.  My, now deceased, boy was diabetic and an older cat.  Steinway Court has a better range of services and is a lot more thorough, but are very expensive.  Dr. Glasser was always a cold fish and the staff ranged from very friendly to terrible human beings.  The office manager cut my cat off from his diabetic cat food when I wouldn't pay for more tests, which I found unconscionable.  I found another source, but she is not a vet and I still do not think she should have made that call on her own.  They always promised payment plans and would never agree to one.  I always felt they cared much more for the money they made of me than for the health of my cat.

I've been taking my girl to Sunnyside Animal Clinic at Queens Boulevard and Greenpoint and find them to be much better.  They're are affordable like Animal Clinic of LI, but thorough like Steinway Court Vets, and Dr.Goldenberg is friendly, warm and smart.  It's a bit of a hike, but I think they're a better deal than either of the others.
