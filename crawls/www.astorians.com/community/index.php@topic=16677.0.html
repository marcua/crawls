Ok, so we made it (back) to the new Cooking With Jazz tonight (our early Valentine's Day dinner).

What can I say but that my mouth did a happy dance once again??

It's probably the closest I've ever come to reliving the past, in the best possible way - in the way you dream about when you think about those perfect times you will never relive.  I was almost in tears at one point I was so happy.

Most of their printed menu is devoted to their classic dishes they did so well when they were in Whitestone.  They had a good number of specials, however, that sounded fresh and updated -- as much as you want good Cajun cooking to sound fresh and updated.

We had Louisiana oysters on the half shell - outstanding, fresh, briny, meaty, needing nothing more than a squirt of lemon.  More meaty oysters in a spicy Brie sauce with jalapeno croutons.  Both oyster dishes were specials.  For our entree we went classic - the blackened steak, a taste I have carefully guarded in my mind since they closed in January 2005 (we had been loyal patrons for nearly a decade).

Well, the steak was testament to the fallacy of memory.  We both could not believe how much better the steak was than we remembered - and it's something I have thought of longingly for years.  We had toyed with getting something else as an entree, and I'm so glad we didn't.  All the flavour is there, the meat was perfectly cooked, tender, and flavourful in and of itself.  (I buy only grassfed beef to cook at home and have become accustomed to the more flavourful meat, and am often disappointed by the flavour of beef in restaurants -- not tonight.)

To finish, we had to have the bananas Foster.  We've had the original at Brennan's in New Orleans, and agreed that tonight's was just as good.

The only real drawback that we saw was that you have to drive to get there.  (They have convoluted directions to get there by transit, but it will take some dedication.  However, I will note that they are on Union Turnpike, and buses do pass right by their door - so it's possible.)  But we didn't peruse the wine list carefully because I wasn't ready to drink a bottle of wine by myself.  We did try a couple of drinks - the hurricane was disappointing, just too much like plain fruit juice.  I couldn't taste any rum at all.  The straight-up margarita was excellent -- not too sweet, and the tequila came through nicely without being overwhelming.  I also had a glass of Malbec that was quite nice and matched the steak.  They have several decent wines by the glass in addition to a two page wine list and a full bar.

So - if you have a way to get to Jamaica Estates, I more than highly recommend you go.  If you don't have a way to get there, you can always ask me for a ride.
