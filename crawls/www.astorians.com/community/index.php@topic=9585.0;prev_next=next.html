I agree with you wholeheartedly Katie. My first three ahem, dates with my boyfriend revolved around drinking, it worked out fine for us, but I still joke to this day that he never took me on a "real" date. Best first date: French bistro for dinner, trip to the top of the Empire State Bldg. and then a 3 hour walk in Manhattan in the spring. Dessert? Martha's for cupcakes.

Worst first date: Don't know if it really counts, but my high school boyfriend invited me his house for dinner he made me (no parents home). Later his friends came over with a "movie" - hardcore bestiality. OMG! Still horrified to this day.

Ideal first date: an art show opening, open mic, etc. followed by a walk and perhaps cupcakes from Martha's (you really can't go wrong there). The thought is that you aren't committed to a lonnng drawn out night, but by seeing/hearing something artistic it can give you something to talk about if you are nervous or having a hard time coming up with things to say.

I hope going forward your suitors are more creative. Good luck!

Best first date

Was waitering in Times Square a few years back and I managed to rook a waitress into meeting up after our shifts at like midnight.  It was a warm summer night and we both looked ridiculous in our black and whites.  About two minutes after we met up the sky opened up and it began raining like all hell.  Like two schoolkids we just kept on nervously talking to each other while getting soaked instead of heading for cover.  She then told me in that fantastic north london accent that I should be so lucky, as that it couldn't get any worse than that.  We then had a terrific laugh, took off our shoes and socks and walked barefoot down 7th avenue in the pouring rain to Mustang Harry's, where we drank ourselves silly.  Best 15 blocks I ever walked.  A shattering first date.
