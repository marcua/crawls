I don't see it that way.

B* and F* are both "not very nice things to say about people".

But "bitch" generally means "you are a mean woman". It's an insult to one woman in particular, not women in general.

F* - means "gay person" but is also a slur. It's basically a way of saying "You're gay and therefore bad" or "I find you as detestable as a gay person".

I would call someone a bitch if they were unkind/mean/vindictive/selfish, or if they just pissed me off.

I would never, ever, under any circumstances, call someone a f*. That is not just saying "you've pissed me off/I don't like you/etc." you're also insulting a whole 10th of the population by using that word as an insult.

Would it be an American exercize of free speech to call someone a n*, a k*, or another slur?

Coulter does not just insult John Edwards and all the gay people of America, she tells us that she really has nothing of substance to say about him. Calling him a f***, in this circumstance, was a way of dismissing him, of NOT exercising her freedom of speech, because she had nothing real to say about him. Coulter could have said something meaningful, intelligent, and well-thought-out about JE. (Stop to  :-D  :-D  :-D) She passed on the chance to say something interesting and instead used a slur that I think most of us here do not approve of and would not use ourselves.

I can't get excited about defending her freedom of speech on this. I wouldn't approve of her being jailed for it, but for Edwards to call what she said "Un-American" in no way impedes her freedom of speech. He gets to express his opinion too. At least his had *some* thought behind it.

Edit after seeing KFF's post:
I agree that she may be best ignored, but I didn't see that as being Frappe's point. I suppose you could also make an argument that to ignore extremism is to silently condone it ... but that would be another argument and another post.
