Title: Re: Fee's in general
Post by: pineapple on April 29, 2009, 03:56:52 PM
If you use a broker you can expect to pay a Broker's fee (usually 1 months rent but these days negotiable), a credit check fee, a security deposit equal to one months rent, and the first months rent.  Sometimes there is also an application fee, negotiate that that deducted from the Broker's fee.  The Broker will probably push you to put a deposit down on the apartment, this is ensure that you won't change your mind before the paperwork is signed.

Title: Re: Fee's in general
Post by: NJinNY on April 29, 2009, 04:44:39 PM
Just keep this in mind...you do NOT need to use a broker to find a decent apartment. A broker will definately make your life easier, they can show you lots of places and I'm sure are very knowledgeable about the neighboorhood. However, if you're willing to do your own research, and have the time to really go through the area and check out different neighboorhoods, scour Craigslist, and look at a lot of different places, you CAN find the apartment you're looking for. I did all this and am very happy in a 1-bed I found on CL after a 2 week hunt that wasn't really so bad, in retrospect.
oh and p.s.: don't put any money down on an apt until you're positive it's your first choice. I learned this the hard way. don't let anyone pressure you into making a decision. That said, sometimes you do have to jump on an opportunity. Just know what amenities and circumstances you absolutely want/need (ie, size of apt, close to train,etc) and what are maybe more flexible (proximity to stores, laundry,etc.)
hope this is of help!
