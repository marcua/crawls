White Bear in Flushing is an off-off-Main-St. hole-in-the-wall joint that has terrific dumplings available frozen.

Problem is, they don't have a printed menu that you can pick up, and always seem to run out of what you want, and often screw up the order. In other words, they're THAT GOOD that they could care less about customer service (a la Soup Nazi.)

I have yet to try their vegetable dumplings. Tried three times:

1) They ran out- both fresh and frozen
2) Got a parking ticket while trying to place order for co-workers
3) Co-worker ordered for me, I got pork instead of veggie

They're cursed as far as I'm concerned; I may never taste their stupid veggie dumplings  :x

I will have to try out that Asian market on 31st St, didn't know about that.  Thanks for the reference!

If you're not stuck on Chinese-chinese dumplings you could probably score some gyoza at the Japanese-run Family Market by the Broadway stop.  Otherwise, it's slim pickins here as far as I know.  The 28th Avenue C-Town actually sells a brand of dumplings called O'Tasty.  They're just okay.

For the dipping sauce I often just buy it and it can vary widely in spiciness whether or not you're going with Japanese, Korean or Chinese sauce but at home I sometimes mix soy sauce with vinegar and a drop or two of chili sauce and I'm good to go. 

And I know we're talking Astoria here but if you're in the Village I recommend m2m on 3rd Avenue between 10th and 11th.  They've got so many varieties of frozen dumplings it'll make your head spin and they're labeled pretty well in English too.
