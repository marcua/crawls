How Does Romano's Stay In Business When So Many Others Close?
« on: October 19, 2008, 06:53:31 PM »
Romano's is a Greek restaurant on Broadway.  They used to be excellent.  Gyro sandwichs so fat it fed two or made for incredibly delicious leftovers - and cheap!  A sandwich, a platter, anything you ordered, it was fantastic, and at a low price.  As time went by they got worse and worse.  Now they have ditzy waitresses.  I went yesterday - I ordered a gyro, my brother ordered a combination gyro and souvlaki.  Two gyros arrived - he sent his back.  He ordered a second Coke, instead the waitress brought the check.  He ordered a gyro to take home to his wife.  She didn't finish it all, and he ate the rest and realized this was something different from what we'd had.  This was lamb.  What had they given us?  I did recall that it was strangely pink and cut in thick pieces, not sliced.  Pork?  How does this place stay in business when so many other places have closed?
