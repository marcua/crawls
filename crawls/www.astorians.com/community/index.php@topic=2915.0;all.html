No, it's not transubstantiation, it's the Real Presence.  We believe that Christ is truly present "in, with, and under" the bread and wine.  And Christ is present in the sacrament whether we have faith or not.  But our senses do not deceive us; the bread and wine continue to exist.  Transubstantiation, which the Catholic Church teaches, means that the bread and wine literally change into the Body and Blood of Christ.

If you worship with us, you'll be able to commune, no problem.  We'll trust that you're baptized
