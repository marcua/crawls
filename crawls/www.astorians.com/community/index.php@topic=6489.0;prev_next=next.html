Here's the pizza dough recipe I use:

1 1/2 tsp active dry yeast
6 tbs warm (110 degree) water
6 tbs milk
1 tbs extra virgin olive oil
1 tbs fine cornmeal
1/2 tsp salt
1 tbs fine cornmeal
About 1 3/4 c. unbleached white flour
1-3 tbs additional flour for rolling the dough

Dissolve the yeast in the warm water and set aside in a warm place for 3-4 minutes.  Meanwhile, combine the milk, oil, and cornmeal in a 1-quart bowl.  Add the yeast mixture, then the salt and the other tbs of cornmeal; mix well.  Gradually add the white flour, making a soft, workable dough.  Turn out onto a lightly floured work surface and knead for about 5 minutes, sprinkling in a little flour as necessary to keep the dough from sticking to the surface.  Put the dough into an oiled bowl and turn it once so the surface is coated with oil.  Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm place until it has doubled in bulk, about 35-40 minutes.

Variation made without milk: The milk is replaced by increasing the water and doubling the olive oil, which the flour readily absorbs.  The additional oil makes a very soft, easy-to-work dough.

1 1/2 tsp active dry yeast
10 tbs warm (110 degree) water
1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
1 tbs fine cornmeal
1/2 tsp salt
1 tbs fine cornmeal
About 1 3/4 c. unbleached white flour
1-3 tbs additional flour for rolling dough.

Re: Ready-to-use pizza dough?
« Reply #20 on: July 24, 2007, 10:50:57 PM »
Keep refrigerated in an airtight plastic bag, and it should be good for a few days. When you take it out, be sure to let it warm up for a half hour or so before shaping--it will be a lot easier to work with. Then you can roll it out if you like, but I prefer to just work it with my hands, stretching and tossing until it's as big as I want it. Then top and bake!

Oh, and if you're using quarry tiles in your oven, make sure to let the oven heat for a half hour or so before you put the pizza in, so the tiles will be hot enough to crisp the bottom of the crust. Yeah, it's not the best thing to make in the summer...but to me it's worth sweating for delicious pizza.
