My problem is with its pervasiveness.  You simply cannot escape - every shop, every loudspeaker seems to be playing it.  Why do I need to listen to Jingle Bells or It's A Very Merry Christmas or whatever *every* time I am in Trade Fair?  I just feel that beginning the day after Thanksgiving (no, I did not go shopping on Black Friday or anytime that weekend) we are force fed xmas music *everywhere* for the next month.

A couple of years ago, I kept track one day of how much xmas music I heard:

Quote
Not counting the various pipings of canned Christmas music, today alone, I heard the following instruments playing, with various degrees of success, your pick of Christmas songs:

accordion - subway car
steel drum - subway platform - that's been going on for two weeks at least
flute - subway corridor
oboe - subway car
electronic keyboard with recorded backup band - subway platform
full band with live drums - subway mezzanine

trumpeters - courtesy of Salvation Army (accompanied by bell ringer), just outside Grand Central

And last, but not least, string quartet - in the lobby of my office building.

Basically, this meant that from the time I left for work until the time I got home that night, I heard some variation of xmas music almost nonstop.  That much of any type of music is too much.

I have some positive associations with holiday music.  Every December, my friends in SF would host an "eggnog and singing party", to celebrate the "Eggleston", which was the name her mother gave to this wonderful creamy and drunken eggnog.  Apart from the 'nog, we'd have hot cider, Brunswick stew and nibbles all night.  And we'd sing.   Holiday songs from a wide variety of faiths and traditions, plus some parodies (and non-holiday songs, like the Pagan version of "Give me that old time religion").   It got more entertaining the more eggnog we drank.  A really, really good time.
