I've got this really great recipe for lentils. Best with Puy lentils. You could certainly use veggie stock in it, and maybe add a little tomato paste for umami...

Sweet and Sour Lentils

Jill Schreiber, Mennonite Cookbook

2 T oil
1 onion, sliced thin
1 c lentils
2 c chicken stock (bouillion OK)
1 bay leaf
1 t salt
1/4 c apple or pineapple juice
1/4 c cider vinegar
1/4 c brown sugar
2 T white vinegar
1/8 t gr. cloves
long-grain white or brown rice, cooked

Saute onion in oil on medium heat until brown but not crispy.
Cook lentils in stock with bay leaf and salt until lentils are tender (45 minutes, perhaps).
Add onions and all remaining ingredients except rice. Cook until bubbly and most remaining liquid is absorbed or evaporated.
Serve over rice.

Serves 6.


Enjoy...!

Lentils and barley both take about an hour to cook, so they're easy to cook into a soup together! Here's my standard recipe.

Sautee chopped onions, minced garlic, diced carrots, diced celery, and diced potatoes in a bit of olive oil until everything is getting soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes. Add a few pinches of herbs--I like basil, oregano, and thyme. And a bay leaf or two.

Add 1 cup of rinsed lentils and 1/2 cup barley to the pot. Add one can of diced tomatoes and their juice; 2 cans vegetable broth; and 2-3 canfulls of water.

Bring everything to a boil. Cover loosely (tilt the lid) and simmer for one hour. Check to see how soft the veggies are, and whether the lentils and barley need to be cooked longer. If it's looking dry, add some extra water or broth to the pot. It can cook up to 2 hours. Taste for seasoning, and add salt and pepper if you like.

you can try lentil burgers:

saute onions, carrots, celery, and garlic in olive oil, toss in lentils.
cover lentils with vegetable broth.  overcook lentils.
let cool slightly.
through mix in food processor with seasoned bread crumbs.
puree. taste and adjust seasoning.
remove from processor and shape into burger patties.
can refrigerate for later or throw in pan with a touch of oil and pan fry from crispy outside.

As for the barley, cook it like a risotto.
sautee onion and garlic with butter, throw in barley. cover with white wine on low flame. stir occasionally.
once liquid is almost absorbed add vegetable stock to cover. again stir occasionally, add liquid a third time. same as before. once 3rd liquid addition is absorbed add parmesan cheese and butter S+P, to make nice and creamy.
