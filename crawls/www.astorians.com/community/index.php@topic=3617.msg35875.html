"Wow, there's a lot of garbage on the streets."

One of the first things my friend visiting from Chile said to me whilst in Astoria.

I think it is a cultural thing. Sad to say this, but it is mainly uneducated and/or ignorant segments of the population that litter.

It is almost an unspoken language in certain Latin and European (I guess, in a way, all cultures have something like this) cultures that the public space outside of their homes, is not their responsibility ("Someone else will clean it" or "It's the city's responsibility, not mine" mentality).

Their homes are immaculately clean; however, the very same people who keep their homes clean, are seen blatantly tossing out garbage into the streets and constantly littering. This is from my own observations.

It starts with the parents. Teach your kids not to litter, and they usually won't.
