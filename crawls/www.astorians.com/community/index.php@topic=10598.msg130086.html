I'm surprised nobody has posted about this place yet. I guess they opened on Thursday (took the place of another Indian restaurant that I never went to. They are not the same owners). It's on 34th St., right off of 30th.

Really excellent food. We had the chicken samosas, chicken vindaloo, and chicken korma. Everything was very tasty, and the nan was the best I've had. I would very much recommend checking it out. They also said they would deliver to Ditmars, which was a nice surprise.

Oh god, and I just realized they have online ordering on their website (http://www.sevaindianrestaurant.com/cafeseva/cafeseva.aspx), so I'm totally in love.

I tried Seva on Sunday night.  Mixed review.  I got take-out so I can't comment on service.

The slightly unusual dishes on the menu caught my eye, and I knew anything would be better than Meghna (the biryani I got there was the blandest food I've ever eaten.  good riddance to that s&^%hole). 

The appetizer (crab cake) was terrible.  It was imitation crab meat, and not billed as such.  Unforgivable.  And no detectable Indian seasoning.  They threw in a chicken samosa without charging me, perhaps to forestall disappointment with the crab cake.  The samosa was average.

Main course (green chili chicken) was delicious.  I have an unsophisticated palate but I could tell there was a lot going on in that sauce.  The complexity reminded me of Kalustyan's Curry Leaf restaurants, in a good way.  The whole green chilis in the dish allowed me to control the spiciness.  (hint: there were no leftover peppers.)

Very nice naan.  Buttered.  They gave me LOTS of rice, the leftovers of which I'll eat tonight.

I'll definitely try them again.  I'll skip appetizers completely (seriously, do NOT order the crab cake) and try the vindaloo.  Got to try their biryani, too.

They're definitely making an effort, which is more than I can say for Meghna.
