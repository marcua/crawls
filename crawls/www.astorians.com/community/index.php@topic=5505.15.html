I used to live in Boerum Hill in Brooklyn when there was nothing there...just a bunch of bodegas and empty stores...there was one brave soul who decided to open a restaurant called Patois on Smith Street....today it is a mecca of gourmet restaurants and specialty shops and continues to thrive...those who have been to Smith Street lately knows what I am talking about.  I remember the first time I went to see a three bedroom duplex in '98 near the Gowanus Canal in Carroll Gardens....I thought what a crappy neighborhood...and the canal reaked of something awful whenever it rained...with some apprehension, I decided to buy the apartment....I'm so glad I did...It has since tripled in value.

BTW, there is a prison on the corner of Atlantic Avenue and Smith Street...yes you read that right, a prison...been there for years...and guess what...there must be about five condos going up within a 1-2 block radius....while I'm at it...check out what is going on at Red Hook.

All I am saying is the Queens Plaza area is ripe for development...I mean it *is* 1 stop from the city.  There is a rumor that the ugly parking garage http://www.forgotten-ny.com/STREET%20SCENES/queensplaza/qplaza.html south of the L has been bought by Tishman-Speyer to be replaced by an office building....right behind it is another condo.  These condos are not cheap and will attract people who have money and will fight to keep their investments secure.  Usually its the kind of people who can make things happen.  That means making more improvements in this area....Its not going to happen overnight but it will steadily improve.
