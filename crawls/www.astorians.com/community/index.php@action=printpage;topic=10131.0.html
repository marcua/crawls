Post by: tashanyc on April 27, 2008, 11:17:17 AM
This is our new favorite Greek restaurant! They serve the food tapas style. Three to four plates is more than enough for two people.

Here are a few items we ate in our three visits.

Ntomatosalata- red,yellow tomatoes with feta, red onions, capers, black olives- We love this salad.
Panzarosalata- red & gold beets, cherry tomatoes, romaine lettuce, boiled eggs, potatoes, olives- Good
Kolokythakia Tiganita- zucchini chips with yogurt- Yummy
Lahanika Shara- Grilled Eggplant,zucchini, yellow squash- Healthy and good tasting
Kolokithokeftedes- Zucchini, potatoes, cheese croquettes with yogurt sauce- Our favorite dish. Perfect and not greasy
Kotopouol Souvlaki- Chicken Kebab- Chicken is tender and juicy. We order this every time
Saganaki- Fried cheese- You can not go wrong with fried cheese.

We love this place and hope the food with remain the same quality.

On the recommendation of flooz and foodista on Joey in Astoria (http://astorianyc.blogspot.com/2008/04/my-2-cents-about-akti.html), we went to Akti last night and had a great meal -- and this was without actually getting any of the "main" fish dishes.  In fact, my wife doesn't even eat seafood, and managed to have enough wonderful food that she wants to go back as soon as possible.  We had a wonderful fresh Greek salad and a number of appetizers - shrimp saganaki (I obviously got this one all to myself), haloumi, chicken kebab skewers, and Greek fries.  With a glass of wine, it came out to about $60 for the two of us.  Great meal, the restaurant is nicely decorated and the wait staff is extremely friendly.  Check it out!

Post by: Krizzle on September 22, 2008, 03:44:59 PM
Ate at Akti with my roommate on Saturday night...

We enjoyed the zucchini croquettes (kind of a crab cake consistency, and delicious!), and the tzatziki.  Our waiter was attentive and helpful, although it was difficult to decide on what to order with only one menu for the table. 

Our waiter recommended the Mediterranean Sea Bass for our shared entree, which was delicious, but slightly disappointing in terms of price and portion size.  If I remember correctly, it was $34 for the entree (whole fish and one side dish).

We would probably go back, but agreed we prefer Ovelia in terms of seafood pricing, sides, and portions.  If we returned, we would stick to the hot / cold appetizers for a tapas-style meal.
