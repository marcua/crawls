There were few patrons - but little by little the place filled up .... but not from the front door.  Couples seemed to be coming out of the back somewhere.  A man would come out... a little while later and woman would appear and come sit at the bar.  And then it happened again.  One of the women who appeared from the back room seemed to be dressed in belly dancing gear.  so I asked her if she was going to dancing that night... and she said - yes - but not as a belly dancer it turned out.  And not as a professional... sometimes she just ended up dancing at (on? ) the bar....

I dont think you should need recommendations on dive bars... the best thing to do is grab a friend and head to the least pretentious looking place you can find.  Sit at the bar.  Talk to the bartender and the locals....drink too much.. have  a great time.  And then keep it to yourself if you want the place to remain low key "cool."

Hmm...I'm a little surprised at the loose definition some people have of a dive bar.

Dive bars are dirty, dark, smelly and cheap. The only reason to go to them is because you don't have much money, you don't care where you're drinking and you don't care much about your personal safety overmuch or you're under 21 because they don't card and don't care. You would never in a million years eat the food there, even if it was free. One of the previous posts description about dive bathrooms (no doors on stalls, smells like barf, unclean) I'd consider applicable. Really, I can't imagine anyone on these boards wanting to go to a real dive bar.

I've only been to one in this city which was right off of the N/W 59th st. stop. That was about 3-4 years ago when I had some friends who were under 21 that wanted to go drinking. There was an entire table of kids drinking there that looked like they were in high school and the entire place looked like it had a film of grease and dirt on it. The bathrooms were a joke. The worst part is the drinks weren't even that cheap! This dive was closed last I checked.

Irish rover would never in a million years be classified as a dive bar, or Doyle's corner for that matter. I'd call them more old men's bars during the day. Most Irish pubs with older bartenders are old men's bars during the day. Not dive bars.
