Quote from: TRX on April 11, 2006, 02:35:25 PM
Agreed, we are on Long Island but we are NOT Long Island.

I presume we're talking about a LI culture of sorts, some of which people find off-putting. I guess it refers to an insular attitude, maybe tinged with racism and/or xenophobia, definitely a culturally-conservative environment. Plus all the exurban sprawl of malls, box stores, etc

 But what is "Long Island", though? I don't think it's that cut and dried. It's as diverse as any other population dense area -- from Glen Cove gated communities to Riverhead projects. You'll still find farms, orchards and vinyards, too. In addition there's a growing immigrant population in both Suffolk and Nassau counties.

But I understand the sterotype "Long Islander" (loafers and whatnot). I've encounted that certain type. And if you look around neighborhoods in Queens (particularly those closer to the Nassau line, but also right here in Astoria), I'm sure you'll find locals that resemble some sort of idea of "typical Long Islander".

I'm not from LI, but I spent good parts of my childhood there, particularly in the summer, and have fantastic memories of the place (West Islip/Babylon/Robert Moses Beach area). Compared to where I actually lived, LI was heaven. Seriously. I was in an isolated, provincial small town, therefore the LI sprawl represented a cultural mecca of sorts for this early -- and deprived -- adolescent.

Anyway, sorry for the digression, but I kind of felt the need to give props to LI, for what it's worth.
