Quote from: megc on December 23, 2009, 01:53:23 PM
jenriet, what is a "sour ale" and can you give me an example? I've not heard of this kind of drink before.  Thanks!

Oh dear, they are difficult to describe without making it sound really unappetizing. But basically, they are tart beers, often with fruit and vinegar notes, that are allowed to ferment with wild yeast, brett. Usually brett is avoided by brewers because it can "ruin" a batch of beer, but sour beers actually rely on it. A lot of people refer to the beers as having a kind of funk. My favorites that are available in New York are Ommegang Rouge (only available on draft) and Rodenbach Grand Cru. They can definitely be an acquired taste, but I love them. If you like lambics (not like Lindemanns, which have artificial sweeteners added), which have a bit of that sour profile, you would probably like a sour beer. It's always worth a shot at least!
