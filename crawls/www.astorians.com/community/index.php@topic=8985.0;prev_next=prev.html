Re: Plastic to cover windows?
« Reply #8 on: January 03, 2008, 09:14:26 AM »
It's definitely not pointless to plastic the windows if they won't lock. Good windows need two things. First, they need to be airtight. The plastic should do that. Second, they need to not radiate cold at you. They will unless there is still (non-moving) air against the window. That's why storm windows are useful in the winter. They don't seal anything, but they keep the wind off the glass, and allow old-style single-pane sashes to be nearly as efficient as expensive high-end windows. So, if you have storm windows, close them. Then do the best you can with the swollen sashes, and put that squishy foam in the cracks if you have to. Then put plastic over the top, ideally with about an inch of air between the plastic and the window.
