A friend of mine was a pharmacy auditor and also worked for the DEA as an agent that investigated pharmacies (she is a pharmacist herself). Many -- more than you would expect -- small, independent pharmacies often get their drugs from less than scrupulous sources and will stock the shelves with either black market or expired drugs. After she told us that, I'm loathe to use an independent pharmacy. Granted, there are probably many more law-abiding independent pharmacies out there than shady ones, but I just don't know who to trust, so I avoid them (except for Kings Pharmacy -- a very small chain with locations in Manhattan and Brooklyn -- awesome for hard-to-find stuff and compounds).

A lot of times the pharmacy isn't trying to short-change you, but the pill counter is off. As a college student, I worked as a pharmacy tech at both Genovese (that was ultimately Eckerd, that was then bought out by Rite Aid) and at a SuperX (bought out by CVS). At Genovese we used a mechanical pill counter, and at SuperX we used a tray and did it manually. Neither is perfect. Unless it's pre-packaged by the manufacturer in the required amount (thereby providing you with a sealed, original bottle), there may always be a mistake -- much like getting the wrong change at a cash register -- it happens. Chain pharmacies, though, get their drugs from their warehouses which get the drugs from the distributors for the manufacturers -- you know you're getting the real deal. The shelves are also checked frequently for out of date meds.
