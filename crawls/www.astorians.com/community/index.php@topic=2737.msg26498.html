Quote from: annie on July 26, 2006, 12:13:49 PM
Tacos SUPER RICA in Santa Barbara, CA is quite possibly the best thing I have ever eaten. They are featured in the article.
That article thus highlights two of the west coast's greatest offerings to mankind: the PCH roadtrip and the hole-in-the-wall taco. Together. As one. Let's all go!!!!

I have a funny story about this place: I visited Santa Barbara last year, with a rent-a-car (drove up from LA).  After a long day of watching the wind surfers at the beach and visiting the Old Mission (a must see by the way) I was starving, so I looked in my trusty Fodors for a place to eat.  Super Rica instantly popped out.

About a block away from the place I realized I passed the turnoff (I was kinda lost) so I made a U-Turn and came to a 4 way stop at the nearest intersection to the taco stand.  Being the native NYer I am (and a VERY hungry one at that), I PAUSED instead of coming to a complete stop and got nailed by Santa Barbara's finest for blowing a stop sign.  She was parked right outside of my peripheral vision, of course.

The conversation essentially went like this:

SB officer: So, where are you headed?
Me:  Super Rica tacos.
SB officer: Yes, I love that place.  I go there often.
Me: Oh?  How do you get there?
SB officer:  It's just around the corner.
Me:  Yeah, I know, I just made a U-Turn to come back here.
SB officer: You realize this is a residential area, and that's why I pulled you over.  Lotta kids around here
Me to myself: [I haven't seen one lousy kid on the street since I left the mission].  Yes officer.
SB: License and registration please, I'll be right back.

After giving me the ticket (170 bucks!) she kindly gave me directions to the place, and they were the best damn $175 tacos I ever had.

On the plane ride back to NYC I came to the realization that the place is a major tourist attraction and she probably gets her monthly quota of tickets patrolling that intersection for hungry out of towners. :-D
-Rob
