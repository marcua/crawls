HA HA HA!  The is the second time in recent memory that I singlehandedly derailed a whole thread by making a comment about pizza.  I guess I just have mozz on the brain!

As regards New York vs. Chicago pizza:  I lived in Chicago for four years, and I have lived in new York for five, and I have been a student much of that time- so let's just say that I know something about pizza.  But I want to make a point that is often overlooked.  There's a book written by the Russian literature scholar George Steiner called _Tolstoy or Dostoevsky: An Essay in Contrast_ or some such thing.  My favorite professor in college always used to say, "Why write a book like that- Tolstoy OR Dostoevsky?  We don't have to pick one.  We can have both!"  I feel the same way about New York and Chicago pizza - they're totally different pies, but why argue about which is better- in their ideal form, they're both excellent in their own way, and we can have BOTH!  Stuffed pizza at a place like Giordano's or Connies or Eduardo's or Gino's East is a beatiful thing - nothing like it.  And thin-crust pizza at Lombardi's or a Sicilian slice at DiFara is heavenly.  And, I would also say, that old school New Haven a-beets needs to be thrown into the discussion as well. But let's face it, folks- there's no shortage of pizza, and no shortage of us to enjoy it. 

And Chicago architecture does rock the house, eh Harlan?
