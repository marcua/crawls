I had to chuckle at your last statement.   :-) 

I definitely agree with staying in hostels or hotels that cater to backpackers - I actually met a fellow backpacker once while traveling in Korea.  He now lives with his wife in Astoria, and my husband and I get together with them from time to time!

One thing I've never gotten over when traveling solo (longest period of time for ten weeks) is feeling nervous when arriving at a new place.  It really helps me to have a map ready, to have studied it ahead of time and have in mind a couple of hostels or hotels I might try, if I haven't already made a reservation in advance.  As a solo traveler, I don't like to rely on cabs when arriving at a new destination, and so it really helps me to know how I can get to the hostel/hotel on foot or by public transportation.  Safety is a bigger concern for me than loneliness.

I would also ask around and see whether a friend of a friend is planning on being abroad, or knows someone who is living in the places that you plan to be.

The best was being in a country where I knew no one but was with other people in a similar situation (East Germany; we were all college students working - strangers but in the same situation).  The worst was when I was travelling alone and knew no one (Austria - no fear for personal safety whatsoever, but crushing loneliness because I wasn't with anyone I knew).

There are two issues that are completely separate from each other when you are contemplating travelling alone.  I think the most important thing is to think about the kind of person you are - do you revel in sharing things with others, or revel in being alone at the end of the day?  I'm not talking about how you feel after one day here or there -- what are you like in general?  If you know you can handle being alone for days at a time, then you can handle travelling alone.  If not - then to do so will be extremely difficult.

The issue of personal safety is different altogether, and can be dealt with in most countries by being an intelligent traveller - much of which has been addressed by anzubird in earlier posts.  I have travelled alone in a majority of European countries, including Italy and have never had an issue with personal safety.  Speaking languages other than English is probably the most useful skill I possessed anywhere, even if I didn't speak the language of the country where I was.  On a train, I once found myself attacked verbally by an old Italian woman, and it was a young Italian man who came to my rescue - and I felt completely safe once he did.

I have never been anywhere aside from Europe by myself, so I can't comment on travelling anywhere else alone.

Two years ago, I went to Sedona, Arizona (http://flickr.com/photos/lizzyvegas/sets/72157594178133021/) by myself. It was my first solo trip and I was more comfortable traveling within the United States for this trip. I flew into Phoenix and drove up to Sedona. There is nothing quite like getting off that highway and seeing the Red Rocks for the very first time. If you have been to Sedona, you know what I am talking about. Everyone I met on the trip praised me for traveling "from the big city" by myself. I actually found that many of the women I spoke to were quite envious that I had enough guts to travel alone. I loved every minute of that trip. Making my own itinerary, sleeping in a king-sized bed alone, eating what I wanted when I wanted, going to the spa, etc. It was so nice to have the "freedom" that you don't always have when traveling with others. I did take two organized tours, which helped stave off any loneliness. My first organized day trip was an all day trip to the Grand Canyon. My second was an off-road Jeep tour onto the Red Rocks. I actually got quite a kick out of my off-road tour. It was me (27 at the time) with 3 women in their late 70s. Our tour driver kept calling us the Golden Girls. The tours are run by Pink Jeep Tours... and yes, the Jeeps are really hot pink!

I am still more comfortable traveling solo to an English speaking city/country and I am planning a quick solo getaway for Labor Day weekend. For trips outside of the US, I am also looking into some more organized tour groups that don't charge a single traveler fee. All recommendations for these groups are welcome.

MaM, I say GO FOR IT!!!
