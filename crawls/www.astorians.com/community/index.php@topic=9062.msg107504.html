We went on Saturday evening around 6:15.  Soon after they opened.  So I appreciate that it was soon after they opened (which is "officially" between 5 and 6).  And I also appreciate that it was only their second day.  But I agree with what others have said.  The service is not just a little off - it's a glaring problem that needs to be rectified immediately.  Our waiter told us he had never set foot in the place.  That the owner took him from the Manhattan location and put him in Mohave for the night.  He had no training or experience in that restaurant.  He indicated that it was the same story for most of the wait staff on the floor.  He also said the computer system is not yet installed.  So they can't communicate orders to the kitchen efficiently.  They also can't calculate checks by computer (check your check - there were a few mistakes on Sat night - though ours was added correctly).  Enough with that.  Our waiter was not to blame.  This is an owner/management mistake.  Our waiter tried his best for us and we tipped accordingly.

I'll start by saying that we are not the kind of people that nitpick over prices.  But that's not to say we don't recognize value.  I do feel the drinks are priced high for our area.  If you pop in after work and have a few mojitos, you can expect a nice tab -- not exactly a casual happy hour spot.  And it doesn't take more than a few drinks to quickly escalate your total dinner check.  $35 for a pitcher of sangria is high in my mind.  We ordered the guacamole side dish.  What came was a large thimble shaped bowl of guacamole.  Not a garnish or nacho in sight.  $4 for that seemed a bit much.  Yes it was delicious.  But for 3 tablespoons of guacamole?  I should also add there is nothing to nosh on placed on the tables.  At this price point, a little basket of nachos and salsa would be nice.  They must think it would downgrade the experience.  But I think they better come up with something for people to pick on while they look at the menu and wait an eternity for service.  We also had the buffalo oysters.  A good choice.  But very heavy on the buffalo sauce.  Too much to coat an oyster.  Maybe fine for a chicken wing but oysters?  (They need to sample the fried oysters at Mardi Gras on Austin Street in Forest Hills.  They hit a homerun.)  And they were not served hot (the computer/kitchen problems?).  But I'd try them again.  I think they'll refine it in time. 

My wife had the short ribs.  Portion was very generous and it was absolutely delicious.  No complaints whatsoever.  An excellent choice.  I had the Pepita coated Atlantic Salmon.  The fish was outstanding.  Served over a bed of spinach and stewed tomatoes.  It was really delicious.  I recommend it.  We also ordered a side of onion rings.  Not good.  A soggy, greasy mess of onion and batter (not together).  The kitchen seems unfamiliar with the temperature of the grill and fryer.  I'm sure the onion rings will improve.  The couple next to us ordered a hamburger medium.  It was extra rare.  Really raw.

In the end, I think this is a great addition to the neighborhood.  We will no doubt go again - probably often in fact.  But, as a customer, you can't help but feel slighted that the owner would throw an untrained staff at our location.  As if we didn't deserve better.  Hopefully they will quickly realize that Astorians are discerning customers.  That we have a neighborhood filled with outstanding eateries.  Either they work out the bugs fast or they'll have an empty restaurant.  We will go back in a few weeks.  I'd bet money that it will be a very different experience.

I had dinner there last night and I would say that my review is definitely mixed.  I'm in complete agreement that the service was just a little off but I also think the food and drinks were a little off-the-mark as well.   

Margaritas: I had one on the rocks and my boyfriend had one frozen.  My glass was so jam packed with ice that I had maybe a sip and a half of pure "drink" before I was drinking a melted ice mix.  The frozen one was better.  If you like margaritas, stick to ordering them frozen, or better yet, straight up.

Appetizer: We ordered the guacamole with blue tortilla chips.  The guac came in a small bowl that was over-stuffed with sour cream, chopped tomatoes and lettuce.  There was just over a tablespoon of guac in the serving if that.

Main dishes:  My boyfriend had the chicken-fried steak, and I had the jumbo gulf shrimp.  His was very good and mine was just edible.  My shrimp was bland, and the rice was mushy (i.e., as in over-cooked, not creamy).

As everyone has stated, the restaurant itself is very pretty, but it reminded me of my first impression of Island Cafe, a nice-looking restaurant with only passable food.  If I had to choose between a great-looking restaurant with mediocre food and a drab-looking place with great food, I'd go for the latter. 

I'm happy to have this addition on the north end of Astoria and would be willing to try the place again, but only after a couple of months.  I would give them some time to get their act together on both the service and food fronts.
