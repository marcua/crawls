I'd head north to the Catskills and sleep in the woods while huddling with your honey for warmth. There's Kaaterskill Falls with it's dramatic 260' drop, Slide Mountain for one muscle-burning workout with spectacular views at the end and th Red Hill Fire Tower is relatively easy and has some of the best panoramas in the area. There are way too many day hikes in that area to mention. Check out the NY/NJ Trail Conference website (http://www.nynjtc.org/) for the best trail maps. And if you don't want to camp, there are plenty of B&Bs available. I've been all over those woods and can get more specific if you'd like.

Or, do like we did one weekend and head into Amish country (Lancaster County, PA) and get your fill of chow chow, shoo-fly pie and hex signs. The area also sports some of the largest outdoor flea markets in the country (Adamstown & Kutztown), if you're into that sort of thing. The highlights: Stoudt's brewery in Adamstown--with it's award-winning beers and giant flea market out back you can drink exquisitely crafted, traditional beers & ales and then haggle with someone over the price of an antique beer stein; Roadside root beer--follow along any one of the back roads in Lancaster Co. and look for the hand-painted signs offering just that; Farmland far and wide--there's nothing like the wide-open spaces of...wide open space; Have intercourse in Intercourse--there are also towns named Blue Ball, Bird-in-Hand, Virginville and Mount Joy. It's just too much fun!

This might sound too touristy, but my boyfriend and I went down to D.C. for President's Day weekend. We had a great timewe're both history buffs and loved visiting the Smithsonian Air & Space Museum, the National Archives, National Gallery, etc.

D.C. is a great walking town with lots of good restaurants and the nice thing is that all the attractions we went to were free since they're gov't run. Culture-wise there's always something on at the Kennedy Center and theatre offerings too. We saw Richard III as part of this year's Shakespeare Festival and it was great. That's not everyone's taste, but there's also sports and concerts at the Verizon Center and loads of other stuff.

We got a great deal on Travelocity. Roundtrip air on Delta shuttle, 3 nites at the Grand Hyatt - five minutes walk from the Mall -- buffet breakfast daily, all for a total of $325 each.

Granted, this was in February so that's probably gone up a bit now, but there are a lot of hotels to
choose from. The Hyatt was near lots of restaurants, the theatre, Verizon center, shopping and had
a Metro stop in the hotel.

Have fun wherever you end up.

I missed my opportunity to promote RI!  If you ever get a three day weekend again, head to Newport, RI.  It's expensive but a cute, seaside town with lots of shops and great restaurants and bars. They have live music in many places. When it's nice out there are plenty of out door spots to hang out in.  And if you want to do some touring there are the mansions and the Ocean Drive which is fun to ride a bike around. You can rent bikes and ride about 15 miles to get around the Ocean Drive.  Oh, and when it gets warm enough, we have 4 beaches that you can go to. Only one of them has an outdoor bar with live music.
