Remember IDA, the missing link?
« on: February 23, 2010, 11:46:57 PM »
Remember a few months ago, everywhere on the news was the story of this fossil that was found and that these scientists called Ida? They said how this was a landmark discovery and the biggest evolutionary find in history. It was the Missing Link, they told us. They compared it to the moon landing. It was on the cover of Time Magazine.  Evolution was finally proved true by the find of this part human-part ape fossil. Well, yeah, it was all a crock of s___. What they found was a lemur, a very well preserved one. So another evolution fraud gets exposed. And its back to the old drawing board to find the elusive "Missing Link." The most important thing you have to remember about the Missing Link is that it is MISSING!!! More exactly it does not exist. But where was the media storm after Ida was found out to be fake? (Don't listen to me. Google it.) It was nowhere to be found. Hhhhhhhhhhhhhmmmmmmmmmmmmm.

Just looking to share. :mrgreen:

Near as I can tell, it was talked about as potentially the "Missing Link", The History channel started to develop a program about it and a media frenzy ensued. When it turned out to be a lemur bone, creationists basically when nuts screaming, "HOAX!"

From reading a NYTimes article, it seems more of a case of releasing information before all the studies were done. Even some science blogs were saying that the the hype was not warranted from what they could see. A few blamed the science team also. They felt the initial paper was riddled with errors.

Two other points that the article brought out that I liked.

1. The finding of a 47 million year old fossil is still amazing.

2. One scientist said that it was pretty silly to even talk about THE missing link. He said that that phrase made you think evolution was a chain. He said it was more like a tree.

Here's the article. It's a fascinating read.
http://opinionator.blogs.nytimes.com/2009/05/20/lets-not-go-ape-over-ida/
