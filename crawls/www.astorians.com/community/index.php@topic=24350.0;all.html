Tamashii Ramen on Broadway is open and doing very well, by the looks of things.  We went last night.  I really enjoyed every item we ordered.  We ordered 3 hot appetizers - shrimp tempura, beef and mushroom stir fry, and a sliced pork that was barbecued.  All were delicious.  The meats were lean and tender and cooked perfectly.  Very nice flavoring without being drenched in sauce or swimming in soy sauce.  I had the Ramen #4 which is very spicy.  And it sure was.  But I really liked it.  The broth was rich and intensely flavored.  The meat, egg, corn, all of it perfect.  The noodles were al dente but just right to me.  Service was good.  A little ditzy and they seemed not to grasp that we wanted to enjoy the appetizers and not have all the food at the same time.  But then they were very eager to please and the whole experience was a good one.  I will be back for sure.  It's a little different from Hinomaru on Ditmars.  Different style and flavors.  Which is good that you can distinguish and choose according to mood and taste.

Went the other day with my wife.  Dinner was ok.  Nothing special really.  We started with pork buns, which were good but not necessarily authentic (I think they use a different cut of pork; same goes for what was in their ramen).  I ordered the Shoyu, my wife ordered the Miso.  When they came, the bowl they insisted was Shoyu smelled so smoky/fishy that I couldn't believe it wasn't Miso.  My wife and I ended up switching bowls, with me eating what they insisted was the Miso ramen, and my wife eating my smoky Shoyu ramen.  Maybe it was me, but this was never an issue at any of the other places I've been to.


Not to further a rumor (if it's untrue), but I've also heard that a lot of Japanese restaurants are owned/staffed by Koreans.  This might explain things seeming a bit off.  Does anyone have any further insight on this?


Finally, if you're looking for ramen on Broadway, and are content with either Shoyu or Miso options, I'd recommend Linn over this place.  I probably won't be going back.
