Hello Astorians.  I'm Kathy Fuchs, an owner and the executive chef at The Thirsty Koala.  First and foremost, I just read all of the posts about us and I'm so happy that you were excited about seeing us open.  I'd like to keep that excitement going so please believe me when I tell you that we want you to enjoy the food.  I've read your comments about brunch and lunch.  During the week our regular menu of  tapas, burgers, tacos, jaffles and crostis are available all day long.  We only did brunch for Christmas Eve and we offer brunch on Saturdays and Sundays.  At your suggestion, the regular menu will also be available during the day on the weekends.  Our dinner entree menu kicks in after 4 pm.


So we will work on the bread, we will improve the descriptions and we want you to tell us what you think of the food.  We do listen.


Warm regards and Merry Christmas.

We had dinner there tonight. We tried an assortment of (mostly small) dishes, and everything we had was quite delicious - my only complaint is that my stomach wasn't big enough to try more. Oh, and that they ran out of the Triple T (Tim-Tam tiramisu) just before we tried to order it. But we're looking forward to going back. We are also looking forward to when they get their liquor license, which hopefully won't be long now. But note that does mean that there are no brunch (or other) alcohol-based drinks yet.

They are very welcoming and accommodating to children, too. We ordered a "tapas" portion of their fish and chips for our son, and unbidden, they offered to make it a "koala kids"-sized portion, which cost less and came with a complimentary scoop of ice cream or berries and cream for dessert.
