Post by: Harlan on August 23, 2006, 12:07:43 PM
And the "funny" thing is that as far as animal cruelty goes, foie gras isn't nearly as bad as what they do in industrial chicken farms. The ducks fed for foie gras tend to be fairly free-range for all but the force-feeding phase of their life, and ducks don't have a gag reflex so putting a tube down their throat isn't all that cruel. In contrast, most chickens spend their entire lives in a cage the size of a sheet of paper in a huge barn that reeks of ammonia, and have their beaks snipped. I'd rather be a foie gras duck than a McNugget chicken any day of the week...

It was just a regular old ranch, 2000 antibiotic injected cows headed to the supermarket.  Though it should be noted that there are a few places that a cow will visit in the 2 years it takes to hit the meat case:

1. Cow and calf farm.  Cows are inseminated, adolescent calves are sold at auction.
2. Grow up on a second farm, sold at about 600 lbs.
3. Eat eat eat at the feedlot, sold at about 1000 lbs.
4. Slaughterhouse.

It's my understanding that cows are normally stunned and have their necks broken before they are slaughtered.  Not altogether pleasant, but could be worse.

I'd say the people who do the meat-packing are treated more in-humanely than the cattle.
