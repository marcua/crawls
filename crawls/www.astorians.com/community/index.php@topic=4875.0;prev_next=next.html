I'm with Trains and Buses but, if your dying to be Sammy Hagar and "Drive 55", there is a Enterprise on 31st Street by 36th Ave that has reasonable rates. A couple catches though.

1) they are closed on Sunday which means you have to pay for that day and return on Monday morning unless they let you drop off in the city or cause a big fuss about it.

2) you have to book at LEAST a week in advance.

3) you need $300 on a credit card to deposit while you drive the car.

Other then that... they are pretty pleasant to deal with.

Also, if you do want to rent a car, it's a lot cheaper if you rent outside of the city. For example, if you're heading Northeast into New England, you can take the Metro-North (from Harlem, if you take the M60 over the bridge!) to New Haven, and then walk about 3 blocks to a car rental place (Avis? I forget...) and rent a car for a lot less than renting from within the city. Also, many large employers have car rental discounts that are fairly substantial and include insurance.

you're welcome, kungfoofool.

just checked hotwire and they have cars from $34.95 per day (plus fees). a quick search from hertz's website direct shows a rate of $70.99 per day (plus fees). it's pretty incredible the difference.

also, last year i rented a car via orbitz from dollar rent-a-car. i was quoted a very reasonable rate and was shocked when i got my final bill which was double what i thought it should be. when i asked, the clerk told me that dollar (and others i presume) charge a $20 or so surcharge per day for people who have a driver's license in my zip code. certain parts of the bronx and brooklyn are charged an extra $70 per day. ludicrous.

fortunately, with hotwire you pre-purchase the car and there is no surcharge.
