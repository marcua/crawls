Seriously though, he signed into law banning mail order cigarettes. Which means my access to 25 dollar cartons is coming to an end. So my partner and I have a choice (he smokes too). Either spend 20 to 30 dollars a day on smokes or quit.

So, I am asking everyone who has gone through this for tips hints etc. I think I am going to set a stop date of August 1st. Fortunately, my partner is with me. So we can suffer together.

Many people have quit smoking successfully. It's a combination of good planning and being properly motivated. The first thing you need to do is cut back to a more acceptable amount of cigs a day. Try to knock it down to 20 a day, and then 15 and then 10. It will be easier to stop if your body is used to taking in less nicotine. Some people find the nicotine patch a good method, others don't. Once you do stop, replace your bad habit with a more acceptable one like chewing gum or hard candies.


It is doable if you really want to stop. Good luck and keep as posted on your progress.

Here's what I've heard to be an effective method:

Smoke when you normally wouldn't, and DON'T smoke when you normally would, such as while having a drink or coffee.

This breaks the psychological associations ("I always smoke while having coffee") while keeping the body from craving nicotine, since you're smoking at other times (while in the shower, for example  :lol: )

Good luck Kemp. It's really tough, but not impossible, and having the want to quit is half the battle! It's good that you and your partner are quitting together. I quit for a while, but when a smoker friend started spending weekends with us while he was in between homes... well... there went my will power. I'm now what I call friday/saturday smoker, but I know I should just quit again all together, especially after offering advice online! (Perhaps this will motivate me...) So, I personally found, especially in the beginning, that it's necessary to avoid situations that prompt me to want to smoke. This meant not hanging out with smoker friends, avoiding drinking outside of my home for a bit, and so on. If you find yourself in a situation where you are totally freaking out, just remove yourself from that situation all together. Go for a walk. Eat something healthy. Do something good for yourself. The craving will pass and each day is better. Best of Luck!
