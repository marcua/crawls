All[size=78%] of the schools have wonderful programs.  My daughter is at 150.  We chose it because (a) it is an accelerated program and they do math a full grade ahead, and (b) they have a wonderful focus on the arts.  The children get visual arts, music and drama.  The school has a large outdoor playspace, and a separate building for the kindergarten and preK.  Our PTA is very active.  You will find us at the school every morning and we have our own website. 


P.S. 150 also has other wonderful things like "Chess in the Schools" and a program with Unity Stage called "arts for recess" which provides the children with live performances a few times a week during the winter when they can't go outside.  It's great that they get music and dance rather than watching movies.


 Happy to answer any other questions you may have.

The principal has been there for a few years and there seems to be some friction between her and some of the teachers, but the principal is good with the kids and parents.  She has always been very responsive.


All public schools are subject to the same class size rules.  Our classes are all within guidelines.  A school can tell you that their sizes are smaller, but it is up to the DOE as to how many kids are assigned to the school.


I have not found our school to be over crowded, and by the books, we are right around the normal mark, if not a little better.


We had, I think, 26 in K and now in second 28.
