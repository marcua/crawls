Judging from what I read and heard, I do not think he is anti-immigration at all.  He's a pragmatic conservative on the matter (enforcement of laws, whatever they may be, concern for the strain on social services). 

Though I disagree with his interpretation of when a fetus attains the inalienable rights of an American, I'd still vote for him because I do not think he'd have much of an impact for people who reside in the bluer states.   I'm a state-rights guy and I like his stance on gay marriage:  The feds stay out of it (in either direction), let the states decide on their own.

I have to disagree with your FZ.  I think Ron Paul advocates foreign policy positions that we need in this country:  Patriotism without the Pandering.  Out of all the candidates, his statements are by far the most lucid and pragmatic.

All in all, there is a good chance this guy is my horse.
