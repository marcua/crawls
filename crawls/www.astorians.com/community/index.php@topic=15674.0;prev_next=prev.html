We were there in November- specifically, flew into/out of LV, drove to the Grand Canyon and UT for biking/sightseeing.

Absolutely go to the Hoover Dam. I didn't think I'd enjoy it as much as I did, but it's mighty impressive  :mrgreen: Also cool is the bridge that's being built over the canyon that'll ease the amount of traffic that needs to drive over the dam. Go early and take a Dam Tour. Plan on being there almost half a day.

As for Vegas, do NOT pay full price for a hotel room. Just by asking, you can significantly reduce the rate. Are you a gov't employee? Bring your ID and get a discount (that's how I did it, and every hotel was good about it.) Also works for AAA members, etc. Even Google should find some sort of discount coupons/codes.

I'd suggest spending one or two nights in Vegas, and the extra time in one of the national parks. Zion has some amazing hiking opportunities, but be prepared and stick to the easier trails if it's not your thing. The visitors' center within the park was lovely.

Restaurants.......hmmm.....in the Tropicana casino, there's a pricey restaurant downstairs, but if you go to the 2nd floor, there's a similar one for half the price......can't remember the details, though! There was also a terrific souvenir shop/lunch counter near the Grand Canyon.....will have to ask John if he remembers.....

Do a search on here for Sedona.  I went last year and put a whole list of things to do on there for someone else who was asking.

For Vegas, there is a special the Bellagio is running that if you book in advance you can save a lot of money.  That said, if you check around you should be able to get rooms at some of the higher end resorts for around $100 a night since that's night a high travel season.

One thing to remember is that one way car rentals (assuming you are picking it up in Phoenix and returning in Vegas) tend to run a little more.  Also, everything is really spread out (two hours from Phoenix to Sedona, another two hours to the Grand Canyon) so be prepared to drive a lot but the views more than make up for it!

Have a great trip!

We just returned from a similar trip in June. flew to Phoenix, rented a convertible and drove around. A great trip with good weather (mostly). In southern Utah, definitely hit Zion NP. we stayed in Kanab at the Parry Lodge (http://www.parrylodge.com/). It's close to several must see's in the area. Highly recommended if a bit pricey. Not exorbitantly so, but it's worth it. Central to town, and has Old Time Western Movie nights in the Barn. It's a lot of fun and it's free. Seats are any old chairs that they have so you might get a rocking chair (my favorite). Maybe an hour's drive to the Park. Plan on a full day at the park. We stayed 2 nights there. And across the street is a great BBQ type place, located next to "Denny's Wigwam" shop. Sorry, I can't remember the name- it may even be a part of Denny's Wigwam.

One suggestion is to be sure and get a car that can handle some of the mountain roads. The Sebring we had seemed to get passed by old ladies on bicycles when we were climbing some of the passes. And try and be up and driving early, if not only because it's cooler but the scenery in the morning light is spectacular. If not early try later near sunset- again, the colors are beautiful. We got lucky in that it had rained quite a bit a week or so before we arrived and the desert was in nearly full bloom. Yellows, purples, reds, greens... spectacular. If you're in LV for a couple of days- a day trip to Rachel, NV and the "Little Al'e Inn" is a hoot. you might catch a glimpse of a UFO or, like we did, Fighter training over the desert. Nothing like a couple of fighters screaming just over the road for a few hours. the folks there are real friendly and will chat up a storm. You can get some good UFO kitcsh-ey type stuff, and the food ain't bad, either. If you do go, be sure and stop for gas at Ash Springs (a few miles before the turnoff to Rachel), since there is NO gas in Rachel. That's right no gas.

Another suggestion- if you're going to Hoover Dam, go from the Vegas side. Til they finish the new bridge, the access from the Arizona side is horrible. Traffic backs up worse than the Van Wyck, down that narrow mountain road. Plus there is a security check point on both sides, so be aware. Car checks seem to be cursory, while campers, RV's and trucks get a good look over.

Oh... and if you are going to the South Rim of the GC before heading to S. Utah- the trip AROUND to the north side and Utah is a LONG drive. Plan plenty of time. It may look like a few miles on the map, but the road winds and winds. It's a great driving road, but it will take longer than you think. Great views and the others on the road will be slowing and stopping accordingly. Very little in the way of services as well. So be sure to top off before heading out. The Gas on the reservations can be $$$$$$.
