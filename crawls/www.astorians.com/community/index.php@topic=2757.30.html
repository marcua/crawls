I think you can only take your towel, shoes, sunlotion onto the deck so you have to put coverup, keys, etc in the locker for which you must have a lock. They check everyone to be sure you have a lock and a bathing suit. In case you don't have either there is a vendor area with $12 suits and $6 locks.  Everyone has to take a shower - two attendents make sure you do, they also scan you for open wounds, bandages, etc. which, ewwww, I'm really happy they check. And there is no monkey business, or not a lot anyway! A bunch of guys were getting rowdy - jumping off shoulders, etc and it was too crowded/too shallow for that and they kept ignoring the lifeguards. They made everyone get out of the pool for about 15 minutes while 6 or 7 cops came and took care of the problem. It was really a lot cleaner/nicer than I expected. And like I said it was just so cool lounging in the water looking at the park, bridge, sky instead of a parking lot. The snack bar with not very good hotdogs wasn't even that expensive seeing how they had a monopoly on the food. Dogs were $1.50, generous portion decent fries $1.75, etc.
