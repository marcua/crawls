Laundry - they lost my clothes, but i went back. would you?
« on: November 13, 2007, 09:33:09 PM »
so, like many of us, i utilize drop off service and get my laundry done each week.  i have been going to the same place for almost 2 years.  they're nice, service has been fine, whatever.  last week, i found about 12 pairs of mens socks in with my clean stuff, and a bunch of my clothing was missing.  i brought the socks back the next morning and said, "so.. um... anyone bring my stuff back."  no one did, but the lady at the laundromat thinks she knows who it ended up with and will get it back.  i stop back in every other day, and i am VERY nice, and VERY understanding (my roommate thought i should have made a fuss, i figure.... what's the point?). 

so because they've always been so nice, and i figure, what are the chances of it happening again, i took another load in this morning.  the woman said again that she's sorry, and will get my stuff back, and leaves a note for her husband to give me a discount when i pick it up that night.  when i went to pick it up, he would not give me a discount.  he said that if more time goes by and the man who they *think* has my stuff doesn't bring it back, then he will give me a discount.

i decided not to go back there.  there is another laundromat with drop off service not too far away, and only slightly less convenient.  i didn't like that i was told i would receive a discount, and then didn't. 

am i overreacting?  under-reacting?  what would you have done?

thanks.

 :|



oh yea, and i am predicting the question: it was the laundromat on newtown ave at 30th street.
