Hi Rene0266,

Thanks so much for the welcome! I'd love to have the chance to trade some recipes and get some more hints and tips on raw preparation, as well as participate in the potluck when it happens. I'm really disappointed that the Whole Foods in Manhattan does not sell unpackaged bulk items. They sell prepackaged, but oftentimes it's way more than I need. I've had to go all the way out onto the Island to find a place when I can buy dried beans, fruit, and nuts by the scoop for some snacks and meals. Kind of a pain. I'll usually just buy the big packages, but have been stuck with a huge can of yeast flakes from one recipe b/c I couldn't buy by the scoop. I'm not 100% raw, (can't seem to give up potatoes!) but do want to explore ways to incorporate more and more raw variety into my diet. I do find I get into a bit of a salad rut sometimes, b/c they're quick to throw together when I don't have time do full entrees.

Jonathan, I LOVE Pure Food and Wine. It is my favorite restaurant and Sarma and Matthew's cookbook, Raw Food Real World, was the first uncook book I ever bought. Their raw lasagne is out of this world and is not too hard to prepare, though I will say mine has never turned out as visually appealing as at their restaurant, but the flavor is fantastic. I'm actually lucky enough to work in Union Square, about 3 blocks from the restaurant, so have popped into the take-away for macaroons and rosemary herb crackers, my two personal favorites. I really want to take one of the classes! There is supposed to be another raw restaurant, Quintessence, in Manhattan, that I read about in Carol Alt's uncookbook, have you ever been there? I'd love to get some recommendations on what's good on the menu, if you have been.

Anyway, thanks for the replies and looking forward to the pot luck!

Ren/Renee

Hi Ren!

I've actually been to Quintessence although it is going on 2 years now since I was there. When my husband and I went we got the sampler platter so we could try everything. We also splurged and got appetizers, drinks, and desserts (we don't normally buy from each course on a menu, but it was my birthday :)). I do remember that I had baby coconut water (great - of course!), and I think I probably had a chocolate-type of dessert. What I also do remember is that the staff was very kind and helpful and the restaurant itself was attractive. We also wound up paying around $80 for two people, which is a bit pricey in my opinion, but well worth it if you're interested in trying out all of the raw food options in NYC.

I have heard that Bonobos (the Village?) is a more affordable place, but haven't tried it myself.

I am probably going to try and participate in this raw food potluck. Maybe I'll have some more ideas after this next week (going to my mom's house for Christmas and she's a raw foodist).

BTW - I second Meg on some of these health food stores. I've seen bulk items in Fresh Start and Broadway Natural.
