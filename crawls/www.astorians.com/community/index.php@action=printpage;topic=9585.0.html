Title: Re: Laundry/clothing question
Post by: lanseaux on February 23, 2008, 01:45:42 PM
What temp are you washing them in?  Hot water will cause excess bleeding, so if you're washing the shirts in hot or warm water, try washing them in cold.  That may help a little.

There is some info here (http://www.pburch.net/dyeing/FAQ/fixrit.shtml) about fixing dyes (dye fixatives (http://www.pburch.net/dyeing/FAQ/fixative.shtml)) and removing excess dyes (Synthrapol (http://www.pburch.net/dyeing/FAQ/synthrapol.shtml)) with formulated chemical products.  There are certain types of dye you are not supposed to use those on, though, which may be difficult since you probably don't know what type of dye was used to dye your shirt.  It emphasises that home remedies such as using salt or vinegar don't work.

Title: Re: Laundry/clothing question
Post by: lanseaux on February 23, 2008, 02:38:49 PM
Well, if they're staining your arms (which I did see), they're bleeding in the wash.  It basically means that they dye in the shirts isn't fixed well.  Washing them in cold water might help minimise the bleeding and subsequent arm-staining.  I don't know if it will completely work, but in my experience washing clothes in cold water does minimise bleeding.  In the links I posted, there's reference to a dye fixative called Retayne, which they say is sold at local quilting supply shops.  There are some fabric stores around in Astoria, and they might carry stuff like that.
