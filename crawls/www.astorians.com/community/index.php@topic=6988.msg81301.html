This is very similar to the whole history of the McCarren Park pool in Williamsburg, Brooklyn.  It's a huge pool that had a glorious history in its day (1930s-1950s).  It all went to hell in the 70s and was closed in the 80s for repairs.  The repairs wound up being cancelled due to an outcry from the local community who cited increased crime and "undesirables" coming to the pool.  It hasn't held water since.  But oddly, it's now filled with graffiti and hipsters who hold live music concerts in the empty pool.  Now there is talk of refurbishing the pool back to its glory days.  No doubt it might have something to do with the fact that it's now surrounded by luxury condos.  So what will become of our pool?  From the sound of this lively discussion, you can imagine that our local politicians won't go near the issues of race or economics.  If the crime increases, I believe the pool will be closed.  Problem solved and no one is accused of being racist or classist.  And furthermore, there will be a story put to the press about the pool being unsafe and requiring millions of dollars in repairs.  Maybe a unique solution will be found, which would be nice.  But ignoring it until there are murders taking place will not solve anything.
