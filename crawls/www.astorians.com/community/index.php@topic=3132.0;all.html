it is BRUTAL, but nothing eliminates excess water/fat from the stomach like the South Beach Diet. If you follow it strictly, and I mean STRICTLY, it does work. Of course, like all diets, it is not a permanent way to weight losss, but I saw results in the 1st week. Sorry, do I sound like a late-night infomercial????  :roll:

Anyway, cut out sugar, dairy (with very minimal low-fat exceptions), coffee, alcochol, simple carbs (potatoes, carrots, beets, all fruit) and breads (including whole wheat and other grains) for the 1st 2 weeks. Low-fat protein, vegetables, eggs, ricotta, and nuts (in moderation, of course) are allowed.

So, the upside is losing 2-8 lbs a week, depending on if you're a man or a woman and how much you have to lose. Yes, a lot of this is water weight, but you'll lose actual fat, too. The downside is because you're not eating carbs, by the end of the 1st week, you will feel your entire body and muscles aching, you will feel like an old granny walking up the stairs. But, it does work.

I've done this 3 times now and with each time, I have less and less enthusiasm for sticking to it. But if you can follow the plan for the 1st two weeks, you'll be amazed and how much excess you lose in the stomach area, (that's where I saw it first, anyway).

Jennifer! 
I've done master cleanse and a bunch of other juice-fasts of my own design.  I try to do two a year.  Juice-fasts are a little easier for me because you can change flavors.  They are brutal but mostly because you will be invited to more food and drink-related events than ever before and can't go and feel lonely.  And god, you miss chewing and tasting things.  You feel great afterwards, though.  I think I once compared it to working overtime at a crappy job: it sucks while you're there but it's great when you finally get the paycheck. 

Hang in there.  You'll be glad you did.  Be careful with the salt water flushes.  Don't do it every day.  They can be rough on the system.

Oh, and LOTS of people will tell you you're crazy.  Eating is natural so people are uncomfortable with the idea of not eating.  It's easier to do it with a friend, for sure.  You can think of un-food-related activities together.  The other thing is to drink lots of water in addition to the lemonade.  (I mean, don't pound water like a psycho but always be sipping water or the lemonade throughout the whole day.  You know what I mean.)  GOOD LUCK!  PM me if you have any questions.

Quote
More importantly, once someone has been restricting food for a certain amount of time, she discovers advantages, not appearance-related, in the way that her mind and body respond to starvation. After this point, the anorexic is motivated by a cost-benefit analysis that sounds very much like the calorie restrictor's: "This is worth the effort/pain/fighting with my parents, because of X benefit." In my case, there were several noticeable pluses. Being hungry gave me energy. It helped me focus on my work. It made me feel emotional and creative in a way that I enjoyed.
