Ugh (palm to forehead)....not this topic again.   Next time, please do a search.

1) There is no "definite score" that gets kids into a specific G&T program.   99 is the highest score you can get and the kids that achieve that grade get their first pick of schools.   Their G&T school does not have to be their closest school; rather, it's based on which school is the parents top choice and the availability of seats at that school.   In our area, PS 122 is the top choice because you're guaranteed admission into their middle school, whereas other middle schools still require the kid to test in even if they're in the G&T program.

2) Even if your kid gets a 99, there's no guarantee your kid gets a seat in your #1 choice school.  Again, availability of seats is the #2 criteria.   So if 100 kids score a 99 and there are only 30 seats in the upcoming class, naturally, some kids don't get in.   Oh, and location is not a third criteria.  I happen to live about 200 yards....YARDS....away from 122, yet my daughter was pushed into the G&T class at 166 (and I make that drive every g******mn morning); this IN SPITE of the fact that there are kids going to 122 from Jackson Heights.   Does this make any sense?   Of course not.   Does the DOE care?   Not at all.   

3) For sake of reference....a lot of kids scored over a 96 on the 2010 exam.   Matter of fact, there were more kids who scored over a 96 on the 2011 exam than there were seats available.   That's why they just restructured the exam and made it more difficult for 2013.   So while 99 is the magic number, it's going to be that much harder to get from now on.

4) Even though my kid is in the program, and it is good, it's not sooooo much different than general education.  If you're a parent who cares about their kid's education, you'll subsidize their education with additional tutoring, mentoring, etc.   You'll make sure that they're meeting and exceeding their requirements and you'll hold the teachers accountable for not pushing (if that's what they need to do).   It's not like the G&T is the end-all, be-all of education. 


Too much hype and stress has been made over the G&T program.   Problem is, instead of demanding a better system, most parents are trying to fit the INTO the system.  It's like the round pegs aren't questioning why they're being forced into a square hole, they're just trying to become squares themselves.   Makes no sense to me.   Instead of demanding better, we've become complacent. And that's just sad....my .02 cents

Ugh....the fact is you'll never get anywhere close to an average from this board (most of the G&T parents don't even know this board exist...from 166 or 122).   So the data you seek is pretty much unobtainable.   

Add to that, as I mentioned previously, the score is only PART of the criteria.   The second part is availability and third is sibling status (though that's changing soon from what I understand).   And all these criteria are relative to each other.  So if one year all the G&T kids score 98 or higher, but the following year a third of them get less than 95 but get in due to having a sibling in the school, then any notion of an "average" score is meaningless because now we're comparing apples and oranges, not apples to apples.

Bottom line: hope that the kid gets a 99, then hope for DOE is nice enough to give your kid a seat at the school you'd prefer they attend.   

And aside from the test getting tougher, more parents from outside the neighborhood are going to choose PS122 as their #1 choice due to the big news about last years' state test scores (we were near the top state-wide, even though the district scored lower than past years).   

What many parents (myself included) have been fighting for is more seats.   That way, even if a kid doesn't score a 99, they're still a chance for kids to get into the program.  But this also means there have to be more certified G&T teachers and space to house more kids.   That's been the tough part to negotiate.

I've been doing a lot of research into PS85's Gen Ed program because it's our zoned school, and I've yet to find a parent who's really unhappy with it. A good friend has subbed for almost all the teachers there and speaks really highly of the school environment, our tenant's son attends 3rd grade there and has improved leaps and bounds since transferring there this year (gen ed program), a neighbor teaches pre-K there, and several other friends and acquaintances have kids there (both G&T and gen ed) - I've yet to hear a story that scared me off the place.


The Parents' Association is very active and has brought programs like:


* Spanish classes for all years starting in K
* Swimming lessons (2nd grade, I think)
* Ballroom dance (3rd grade or 4th?)
* Indoor dance activities instead of recess during winter


And other enrichment programs that I can no longer remember. These are all available to both the G&T and non-G&T students.



I went on a very informative non-G&T tour there that was led by the Parent Coordinator and the Kindergarten Social Worker, and we met several teachers and parent volunteers, and peeked into some classrooms (it was during the school day). I came away very impressed and excited; it's a much smaller school than PS122 and less crowded, and others in the neighborhood and seems to have a nice, close-knit vibe.


(For what it's worth, I hear especially glowing things about the pre-K program. And the after-school program, Serious Fun.)


As far as the tours, etc. being disorganized, I think it's worth remembering that they may not have had firm plans in place for the new district G&T program until very recently - that program was only announced about 2 weeks ago.
