I think the race is starting to heat up, a bit earlier than usual - but then it's an open seat, which doesn't happen that often, so there is a lot of excitement.  County Regular Democratic Clubs endorse once the District Leaders have had a chance to assess the field,  the official Queens County Democratic Organization designation usually takes place in late May prior to petitioning. 

Unions are also starting to talk with candidates and advocacy groups (League of Conservation Voters, Planned Parenthood, NARAL, League of Humane Voters, etc.) are all starting to get questionnaires out, though they don't all endorse before petitioning (June and the beginning of July).

I don't think Aravella is a one issue candidate.  She's been a member of Community Board 1 for over 8 years and has been active with UCCA, the largest civic association in Astoria for a long time as well.  She's a lifelong resident of Astoria, over 31 years and she worked on the campaigns of the Vallones and Mike Gianaris.  I know she's been particularly involved in women's issues, immigration (she was born in Zimbabwe herself and is an immigrant) and has worked on Greek concerns for many years.  I'm very impressed with her (and in the interests of full disclosure, have endorsed her - I serve as the President of the Powhatan Regular Democratic Club which is behind her and my boss, Congresswoman Carolyn Maloney, is officially endorsing her on Saturday).

That all being said, I am a huge fan of Jeremiah as well (we're both congregants at Trinity Lutheran Church and he's also a member of Powhatan, and has been an incredible advocate on marriage equality).  He's truly one of the best people I know and would also be an outstanding Assemblymember.

I'm sure they'll be updating the website soon - I was surprised that the splashpage is only the donation page, but that happens with campaigns sometimes - they roll out the website in pieces.
