Basically, if your computer doesn't turn on at all (no lights turn on, no fans turn on, etc. when you hit the power button), it's definitely the power supply. There's nothing else that would stop it from not turning on at all. If it turns on, sputters and turns off (completely) then I'm not sure.

If a light or fan _does_ turn on, but it doesn't startup correctly (no feed to monitor or the light doesn't switch over from orange to green or switches back quickly), then it's probably something else. If it's not the power supply, I'd check the videocard next. Oftentimes if it starts up but there's no feed whatsoever to the monitor it's because the video card is broken.

If it died from a power surge it's possible, though not terribly likely, that something else got fried too.
