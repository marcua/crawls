Emily Bobo Bio:

Emily Bobo’s poetic work struggles with questions about the artist who believes she must suffer for her art and about her addictions to love, pain, and all that which enables her to (cathartically) speak; it also, however, explores issues of God, family, identity and how one, often, both builds and destroys the others.

Current obsessions include: sequential fugal forms and the figure of the Recovering Musician, an ex-pianist enmeshed in a complicated, psycho-sexual relationship with her piano.  Bobo’s poems are typically strange and dreamlike.  From crowns and classical music to jazz, letters and ecphrasis to re-envisioned biblical parables, the generous formal experimentation in Bobo’s poems creates a body of work that is as haunting as it is elegant.

A Kansas native, Bobo has published poems in The Mochila Review, The Flinthills Review, and 5 AM among other journals, and has performed her work nationally in New York, Colorado, Nebraska, and New Mexico.  She holds an MFA from Wichita State University and is currently completing a doctorate in English and teaching poetry at the University of Kansas.


SheLovesMe/SheLovesMeNot/HeLovesMe/HeLovesMeNot/SheLovesMe/SheLovesMeN

The pallbearers wore sweatpants.
They played “Peace in the Valley” and “All
            I Do I Do For You” on a tape recorder.

At the gravesite, Katherine stood on the casket,
            pulling roses from Mother’s stand-in
            bouquet, shouting, “Did y’all get
            what ya’ wanted from Grandma?”


A Resurrection Piece

0:18.  Thanksgiving in Paris and she’s sitting on a toilet, in a white room with a wooden
door, waiting for sleep to find her in a different longitude and an oblong pill
that could be a knife with blunt-round edges, thinking, “This is how you kill someone.
This is how you murder a beautiful woman.”

First.  You make her a woman.
