That is normal, but dependent on the landlord.  Most landlords will not ask you for the last month, but a few will.  I would ask him to show you apartments where the last month is not required, if you cannot afford it.  It is not that common to have all four fees, just 3.  I beleive it is just so you won't run out on the last month so that the landlord would still have your security for that.  As always, get receipts for everything.  What's illegal is a key deposit and I would watch how much they charge for a credit check.  These only cost 15-30 bucks, but some realtors tack on an extra $100 for their pocket.  My last realtor at Buy Sell accepted my current credit check that I had brought with me, so I didn't have that fee.

Re: Question about broker fees & security deposits
« Reply #5 on: November 02, 2007, 04:33:42 PM »
The security deposit is for any potential destruction to the apartment of which the tenant is determined to be the cause of   :-P  If that apartment is left in the same condition you got it in, barring reasonable wear and tear, you are to get your deposit back.  Otherwise, some or all the money will be used for repairs.  The last month's rent is your last month's rent.  Some people try and not pay the last month, thinking the landlord will use the deposit as such.  That is supposed to be up to the landlord.
