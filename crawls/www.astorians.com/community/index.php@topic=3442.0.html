I'll take the first stab at addressing your questions. Overall though you'll find that the majority of conversations here follow the same kind of "rules" that would govern a discussion group meeting in a local cafe. i.e. don't butt in in the middle of a conversation and start trying to sell something, try and not hijack the conversation for your own purposes, DON'T YELL (with all caps), if you have a problem with someone in the group take it outside (use a personal message, or email), etc. Here are some other thoughts though:

First, the board is primarily community moderated. Meaning that if you say something that crosses the line in some way, you can expect that people will speak up and let you know. You can't go back and edit what you said (more than 15 minutes later) for this reason. Take responsibility for your words, just as you would in a group setting.

Second, when discussion breaks down entirely and more than one or two people lose control of themselves then a moderator usually steps in to try and break it up. However, deleting posts, censoring, or banning users  (has only happened once in 3 years) is a last resort. Moderators do sometimes edit posts that have coding errors in them (that prevents people from clicking on a link you posted for example), or if you post the private contact information of someone without their permission.

Third, check the "News:" tags at the top of the board. It talks about some of the things that are common among nearly all online forums.

Lastly, if you're looking for some rough guidelines the membership agreement is probably the best one. You read this when you signed up for an account. :) I'll post it again here for reference:

Quote
As a new user to astorians.com you'll have full access to the board with two exceptions:

1. You won't be able to post any new events on the calendar immediately.
2. You won't be able to send any private messages immediately.

However these restrictions are removed once you've joined any of the discussions on the board and said hello. It only takes a couple posts to gain full access, so please take a moment and introduce yourself or ask a question!

By registering an account here, you agree that:

- You will not post any abusive, vulgar, or offensive information on this forum. (Just be a decent person please)

- You will respect the privacy of others, and not reveal the identity of, or post the private contact information of, or otherwise harass others in a way that invades their right to remain anonymous.

- You will abide by applicable U.S. and International law as it relates to any of your posts this forum.

- You are soley responsible for the content you post.

We'd also like to point out that:

- You may only create one account here. If you don't like your account and want to create a new one, ask a moderator to delete your old account first.

- You may create a personal "signature" to use in all your postings. However, signatures are limited to text only. No banners or other graphics allowed.

- Though the moderators subscribe to a philosophy of free and open sharing of ideas please know that if you are excessively disruptive, or otherwise abusive to others on this board we may ban you.

- We'd really appreciate it if you'd only post any given thing once. There's a tendency to think that posting the same content multiple times will be more likely to get it noticed; it won't. It will just get you noticed as a loudmouth. Please don't duplicate your posts.

- We cannot read every post, and cannot back up or investigate all the information that is posted here. Any posts that you read here do not necessarily represent the ideas of the owners or administrators of this board.

- When you are making a post please consider that what you say will be made public. You have an unlimited number of opportunities and time to "preview" and "modify" your post. Once you "post" your words publically it is sent to the public. If you need to make a correction to your post or delete it after it is made public, you will have 15 minutes to do so.

- Our software does indeed track your IP address, and keeps a log of your actions on this board (though this software doesn't go beyond the scope of any other forum software out there). If a formal legal complaint is filed as a result of some action you've taken on this board we reserve the right to share this information with legal council or authorities.

- If you give out your password and someone else logs into your account, you are still responsible for what is posted. Don't give out your password.

- This software places a cookie on your computer that is ONLY used to store your username/password (if you have that feature enabled). The software does not otherwise collect or collect or send any other information to/from your computer.
