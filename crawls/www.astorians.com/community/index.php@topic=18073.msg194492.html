Re: Pedestrian Plaza Proposal
« Reply #23 on: April 30, 2010, 11:14:06 AM »
A land grab for Athens Cafe is exactly what it would be and would majorly increase their business -- people aren't just going to sit there and people watch like so many do in Manhattan, they're going to buy food/beverages at Athens Cafe and then sit in the plaza area without going through table service. The reason why the plazas are successful in Manhattan is the combination of business people who use the plazas to relax at lunch, combined with tourists who are enjoying the sights of the city. That's not exactly the situation in the middle of Astoria. I'm sure the homeless dude who sits in the bus shelter and the various old retired guys who wander the neighborhood and sit in the Dunkin Donuts all day might appreciate it, but other than Athens Cafe business, I don't see it being used by people who are expressly heading over there to sit in the pedestrian plaza.

In all honesty, how many people have actually headed into Manhattan with the sole purpose of sitting in a pedestrian plaza without any other reason to be there? If you want to sit around and enjoy the outdoors, you head to Central Park, not the pedestrian plaza in Times Square.

Plus, it will have a negative affect on traffic in the area -- traffic at that intersection is already terrible. Key Food often uses that space in front for their home delivery service and for vendors making deliveries to Key Food. Displace those vehicles and they end up double parking on 33rd Street or taking up parking spaces for other shoppers. Parking is already difficult around there, by taking away even more parking -- between the spots removed from the plaza and the vehicles displaced by the closing of the street -- it would just make an already difficult situation worse. So while Athens Cafe may benefit from the placement of a pedestrian plaza there, many other local business will likely be harmed.

Finally, while the plazas in Manhattan tend to be well maintained, do we really think the Astoria plaza will be as well? I highly doubt it. We have a major graffiti problem in our neighborhood -- there's something new on the gate at Bargain Stop everytime I pass by there. I can totally see kids using whatever seating is available as a canvass for their "art." I can totally see stuff getting destroyed and misused. We don't have santitation workers and folks from the Ready, Able & Willing program walking around cleaning up constantly like they do in Manhattan. Without constant vigilance and maintainance, the plaza will just sit there and be a blight on the neighborhood. Maybe I'm overly pessimistic, but this isn't Manhattan -- there's not exactly the same attention to aesthetics as one would see in midtown.
