Like Jonathan, after 13+ years of eating in Astoria, I still do not have a favourite Greek restaurant.  We've eaten once at the place by the park, and I thought the grilled octopus app was very good. 

Don't care much for Elias Corner or Kyclades (sorry, Anzubird!).  I have heard that Elias Corner used to be much better.  I haven't been in about 5 years, but found that the fish was indifferently prepared and served.  Kyclades is always packed, but the last meal I had there was a grilled salmon steak that came sitting in a pool of olive oil about an inch deep.  The fish itself was ok (and I love olive oil), but I could hardly eat it, it was so oily.  I cook fish very well, so it's hard for me to justify ordering just a plain piece of grilled fish in a restaurant.  Mine is usually better than any of the Greek places around here.  We went to Scouna once and had a decent meal (corner of 31st St. and 23rd Ave.), but it's got a different name now (Aunt something, I think), so I have no idea about it.

My favourite item in any Greek restaurant around Ditmars is the tzatziki app from Zygos Taverna, under the Ditmars stop.  The rest of the menu is generic, but sometimes I just have to have their tzatziki.  I'm not even sure they make it in-house, but it's fantastically garlicky and got a great balance.  Sometimes I've gotten the mixed app platter at Mezzo Mezzo, which comes with hummus, tzatzaki, taramosalata and skordalia, all of which are tasty.  I also like the souvlaki from Elpides, the woman with the cart in front of Thessaloniki Jewelry on the corner of Ditmars and 33rd St.

Except for that tzatziki (and a meal or two at Mezzo Mezzo), it's probably been three or four years since I've eaten in any Greek restaurant in Astoria.
