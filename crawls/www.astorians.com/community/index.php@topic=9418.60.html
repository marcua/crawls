Here’s where things get tricky –
What happens if the person(s) is/are investigated and an allegation was found to be unwarranted, their names are added to a “secret” database anyway and they are forever marked – as are people who have similar sounding names?

Sometimes the story never ends.

I have mixed feelings as of late about balancing the needs of security with personal liberties. On one hand I feel we, as a people, have been all too willing to give law enforcement (and marketers I may add), especially on the federal level too much power and discretion to interfere in the lives of citizens and this must be re-balanced somehow.

But law enforcement has a duty to protect citizens and the pressures are enormous. I see no problem with an investigator interviewing a person based on a specific complaint, i.e, someone phoning in a tip about suspicious activity. If it were more random, i.e., stop and search without cause, then I would have a problem.

In regard to this thread, I find it unlikely that a member of some sleeper Islamic terrorist cell would take pictures of a power plant and pray in the open, fight with customers at their business and otherwise draw attention to himself.

But, and this is a big but, two people on this thread have witnessed pictures being taken. One account may be unreliable given the source (FZ) but the other is offered by someone who’s usually quite fair and consistent in their comments (lizluv).

When all is said and done I often feel people are too reluctant to get involved or to call the police when necessary. I see no harm in making the call in this instance.
