Title: Re: What kind of paperwork do I need to get an apartment?
Post by: Jimmy Farwest on June 13, 2008, 12:55:05 PM
I might be a little late to the party but I just moved recently so thought I could add something.  This is what I always had with me:

1. Drivers license (they'll need to photo copy it)
2. My 2 most recent pay stubs
3. My own credit report from freecreditreport.com (most brokers will insist they run one for you but if you find a place by owner this might be useful). 
4. A letter of employment with salary, title and how long i've been working there.
5. $1000 cash.  I hate having that much cash on me but when I found the place I liked it was great to pull out the $'s and put a deposit down on the spot.
6. Most recent bank statements. 

Having all this stuff on me made my life easier when I decided to take a place.  Some brokers will ask for less info but its never bad to be prepared. 

Like others mentioned, visit a lot of brokers.  They do almost nothing for their fee- usually just drive you and unlock the door to an apt you've already probably seen pictures of on craigslist - but thats the game here in Astoria.  if you can find a place you like "by owner" then good on you but I didn't have any success. 

Most brokers won't follow up with you.  Just go in to a bunch of realty places, fill out their basic info form and try to get them to show you places right then and there.  If you don't like anything you can probably walk about 2 blocks, find another realtor and do the same thing.  Of the roughly 12 brokers I worked with this time around only 2 followed up with additional apartments after my initial visit.  It seems like they want to show you 2-3 apt's that day and hope you take one.  After that they wait for the next walk in customer... just my experience. 

Good luck
