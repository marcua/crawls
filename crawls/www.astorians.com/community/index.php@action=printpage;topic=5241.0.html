Title: Re: Homemade chicken soup?
Post by: goldfish boy on February 17, 2007, 03:35:10 PM
8 quart pot, one chicken (pre-cut in parts), one celery thing, four carrots, two onions, two parsnips, salt, pepper, water, generous amount of fresh dill.

Clean the chicken thoroughly, put it in the pot, just cover it with water, cover the pot, cook it until the icky stuff rises to the top of the water, scrape the icky stuff off the top with a big spoon, throw it away, continue cooking until you don't have to scrape icky stuff off any more, then put the vegetables in, cover it all with more water, add the dill and pepper, and a little bit of salt.  Cover the pot and cook for two hours or until the chicken starts to fall off the bones and the vegetables are pretty mushy.  Meanwhile, cook a lot of noodles.  If you can find kloosky noodles, use those.

When the noodles are done, drain the water off of them and put some in your bowl.  When the soup is done, remove the chicken and vegetables and serve them on the side.  Use a ladle to put the soup on top of the noodles and you now have the best chicken soup ever.  Go easy on the salt unless you really like salt.

That's the best I remember of my grandmom's recipe.  It's about 11 years since I made chicken soup so it may all go horribly wrong.

This is something that's been passed down from my mother and I passed it down to my kids, when they got sick I made it for them and it seems to help.

1 orange
1 lemon
1 apple
cinnamon
honey

3 cups of water
peel orange (don't throw away rind) and seperate
peel lemon (don't throw away rind) and seperate
peel apple (don't throw away peel) and seperate
add orange, lemon and apple to water
add the orange rind (about 1/2 of what you peeled), lemon rind (same about 1/2 of what you peeled) and what you peeled off the apple (about half)
let the water boil then let it simmer for about 5 minutes
add honey to sweeten and cinnamon

It feels great to drink, warm or you can let it cool and drink it that way (I prefer it warm).  You're getting all the natural stuff and the rind is what has mostl the vitamin c in it.

It's old fashion remedy and maybe it's coincidence but our colds don't last for over a week.

Remember to drink lots of fluids.

Title: Re: Homemade chicken soup?
Post by: hikaruhana on February 21, 2007, 12:22:28 PM
Having been pretty sick lately I can share with you how I made chicken soup.  Turned out pretty well if I do say so myself.  Luckily I did this all before really falling ill, mindja.  Wouldn't be attempting all this if I were really down for the count.

Went out and bought an organic chicken (Bell and Evans is one brand although I like Murray's better) and roasted it one Sunday night with some potatoes and onions.  Basted it in butter and white wine, salted and peppered it a bit too.  Put bouquet garni inside before trussing it.  When it was done I let it cool, then picked the meat off the bones and put the bones and giblets in a large stock pot filled all the way to the top with water.  Threw in a bay leaf and then some onion.  Let that simmer and reduce for a few hours, then strained it and threw out the bones.  Kept the broth aside in a bowl for a moment while I sauteed some garlic and lots of diced onion in the stock pot.  Poured the broth back into the stock pot, then threw in potatoes, carrots, black rice, and shredded chicken.  Salted and peppered to taste.  I'm kind of an improv cook and I don't measure these things out, just do them on intuition, but something along these lines would probably turn out well for anyone trying it at home. 

I gotta say, there's nothing like homemade chicken soup.  After I ran out I switched to boullion cube-based broth and it tasted a bit blech.  Got my Jewish mama to hook me up with some matzoh ball soup yesterday, though!  That's not half bad either folks.   :-)
