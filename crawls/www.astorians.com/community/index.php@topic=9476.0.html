Great topic, Urrisgirl

I listened to a lot of different genres of music when I was growing up.  My closest friends were into metal bands like Anthrax, Megadeath, etc., but I listened to everything from punk to Top 40 to rap that I used to catch on the college radio station (thank god for KVSC!).  I think sometime in 1985, though, I committed myself to socio/political British bands, goth and new wave.  I wasn't crazy enough to really get into the punk scene and I honestly didn't like speed metal.  Unfortunately, most of the people I associated with didn't get my love of English so whenever we were driving around I was forced to listen to the Dead Kennedys or Slayer. 

Regarding movies, I was a fan of the Brat Pack and many of the cheesy 80's teen movies.  However, I also enjoyed films like "Sid and Nancy" "Sammie and Rosie Get Laid" "My Beautiful Laundrette" etc.  I was one of only three people who saw the one night showing of "Blue Velvet" in my backwards little town.  Apparently, someone heard about the movie and had it removed after only one night.

One of my favorite movies of all time is Goonies.  I have seen it probably 400 times, and I'm in no way exaggerating.  Best movie.  Pirates.  Stereotypical Italian criminals.  The mom from Throw Momma From the Train.  That water slide thing.  Lou Gherig baseball card.  Chester Copperpot.  Cyndi Lauper.  I can recite most of the movie, it's kind of sickening.
