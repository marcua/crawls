Explosion on 35th St. near Ditmars
« on: March 18, 2007, 12:39:45 PM »
Yesterday afternoon there was some sort of explosion on 35th St. between 21st Ave. and Ditmars.  The entire block was filled with fire trucks, as was Ditmars between 33rd and 36th Sts.  There were at least three ladder companies that responded, including one from Corona.  I saw a ladder extended to the top of one of the two family houses across from the Acropolis.  I overheard a guy on a cell phone saying that the explosion was across the street from him and that they had no electricity.  I couldn't see any actual fire, but I did smell an acrid burning smell that smelled like the burning wires last summer during the blackout. 

I went down that block today, and there are several Con Ed emergency vehicles there.  Many of the manholes on the Acropolis side are open and cordoned off and there are burnt electrical cable pieces on the street.  Partway down the block, the first floor of one of the two-family houses is completely boarded up, including the front door, and there is a lot of debris in the front yard.

Anyone have any information about what actually happened? The description of the explosion and the house being boarded up makes me think of a gas explosion, but that doesn't explain the burnt cables.
