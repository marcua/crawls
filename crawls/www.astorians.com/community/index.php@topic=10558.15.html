Unfortunately, I have never been impressed with Best Yet's selection for anything.  They do have a reasonably good fruit and vegetable department.  Haven't checked out their beer selection so that might be an exception, but although I've tried to find reasons to like their fish, meat, dairy/cheese/eggs, nothing draws me back.  They have a very poor selection of organic choices, even though I have spoken with the store manager on more than one occasion about it.

I don't buy a lot of packaged or processed food, but I found those choices to be only average as well.  They do have a great selection of British food - original HP sauce, salad cream, Heinz vegetarian beans, powdered custard or spotted dick, anyone? 

But hey - if you like it, you like it -- it's one more option for the neighbourhood.  I was, however, very upset with Best Yet's union-busting tactics when they first opened.  That's another reason I rarely go there.

Has Stew Leonards ever opened a store within NYC?  I associate them with suburban locations.  I've never made it there, although we have family members that love it.

One of my favorites in Astoria is Berry Fresh Farms, in the LaGuardia Shopping Center on Ditmars Blvd. and 43rd Street.  They have a really extensive produce section, including many fruits and vegetables that I have not seen sold at any of the other area supermarkets.  They also have a really extensive variety of ethnic products, and excellent meat and seafood markets.  I also find it to be rather inexpensive.

Of course, it also has the narrow, cramped aisles and as it is a small supermarket, it does lack in many other products that other supermarkets would carry, but for produce, meat, seafood, and ethnic products, it's my choice in Astoria.
