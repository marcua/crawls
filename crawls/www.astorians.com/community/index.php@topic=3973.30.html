Quote from: erpotown on October 30, 2006, 03:41:53 PM
I love Babycakes, they have some yummy cupcakes there.

Yes, they do.  I went there today and was happy with what I tasted and subsequently bought.  They sell "cupcake tops", which is exactly what you think they are, just the top of a cupcake (my favorite part).  I had a vegan spelt chocolate cupcake top, and took home a whole cupcake, both with chocolate frosting.  The cake itself is certainly more crumbly than a traditionally baked cupcake, and a little dryer than what I'm used to with homemade cupcakes, but I enjoyed it anyway.  The dryness is different from what I experienced at Buttercup Bakery (ick), which was just unpleasant.  I liked the taste of the cupcake, which was chocolately and not overly sweet, and the consistency of the frosting, which was nice and smooth like I like it.  They didn't pile on the frosting, another things I liked very much.  It was also not overly sugary like at other places.  Love the vegan aspect of things, too, and especially the sugar free element.  The cupcakes are sweetened with agave, which pleases me to no end.  There are some things sweetened with unrefined sugar, but I avoided them because I'm cutting out sugar these days.  I did not experience that nasty sugar low with the cupcakes, which was truly awesome!

I also bought a slice each of pumpkin bread and lemon poppyseed bread.

Overall, a very positive experience.  I'd go back gladly!

We finally went to Babycakes today, and had a spelt chocolate cupcake and brownie each. I enjoyed the cupcake but my favorite part about it was the frosting. I typically do not like frosting, but this was really quite good - smooth and creamy. The brownie was excellent -  moist and very chocolately. I definitely would recommend Babycakes, although they are a bit pricey. The brownie was all of two or three bites and was $0.95. I forget how much the cupcakes were, but seems like they were around $2.50 or so for a standard-sized (not large) cupcake/muffin.

Also tried Pomme Frites for the first time and enjoyed them as well (belgium-style fries). There was a line going out the door the entire time that we were there, so they are definitely popular. We tried their dijon mustard, curry ketchup, and mexican ketchup. I liked the dijon and mexican the most, but when I think of fries I usually think they are best sans anything or with regular ketchup.
