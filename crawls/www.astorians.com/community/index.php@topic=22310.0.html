Question on accessing Astoria Park from 21st Street near 30th Ave.
« on: February 19, 2012, 07:01:39 PM »

Hey all.  So I will be moving into my new apt in Astoria later this week...on 21st Street just south of 30th Ave. (in that new sorta fancy 30-50 building).  So today I decided to walk around and figure out how to walk to the park.  I walked what I guess would be West....encountered some a sort of convergence where some streets went at different angles and where there was a lot of really great graffiti art on the walls...think I saw some projects beyond...then I passed a church or two, then saw a few really old homes, and then (before I got to a really massive curved apartment building near the water....) I passed an incongruous home that looked like the Astoria version of a movie star's home...it was sort of Italian style....manicured garden....two huge lion sculptures in the front...signs to indicate they have 24 hour security...anybody here know the place?).....so then as mentioned I then saw that huge curved apt building, walked along the water a bit and then saw the beginning of the park.

While I stayed on the opposite side of the street, from what I could see across into the park it didn't look very park-like or open or inviting....certainly nothing like the other part of the part I'm used to seeing like when I've walked to the part from the Ditmar's subway station.

So...I guess the park is pretty huge eh?  Is it generally known that certain parts are nicer than others?  If I had walked to the park more directly by walking straight up 21st Street, would that have been any better?  Even when I was at that part as I headed back to my apartment, again, it didn't look very 'hospitable' for pedestrians.  A very wide road, then you have 21st Street with 4 lanes of traffic, the highway looming over the park....

If I had actually walked INTO the park, would it have looked nicer there?  My sense was that there couldn't be much park at that end...that it was close to the water's edge no?

Tx!

The park doesn't border 21st, it's a block off.  And the bridge is so high over the actual park, you don't even notice.   If I were to walk to the park from where you are, I would walk down 21st to the Astoria blvd intersection and make a left and go up 27th avenue and then make a right on 18th street and that will lead you directly to the park.   On 18st, you will pass Lorusso's pizza (their sicilian is excellent) and a convenience store that has really good coffee.   If you stay straight on 27th ave, you would hit Two Coves Community Garden. 

I would also try to be more positive about my new neighborhood.  (you MAY be, but you didn't come across it in your first post).
