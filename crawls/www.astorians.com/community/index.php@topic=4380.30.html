Interface != Understanding

That simply means that no single interface represents the computer or the computer's capabilities. The interface is simply a layer that sits between the computer and the end user. In Windows, the interface is fairly well understood due to it's dominance in the home market. By now, most people know how to use things like dialog boxes, buttons, menus, icons, and other trivial aspects of the overall experience. That of course, has nothing to do with software freedom. Many free operating systems and applications can be used by these click happy users while still providing the end user with the freedoms to redistribute, modify, and study the software in question. In other words, your comprehension of a computing experience has NOTHING to do with the issues I am discussing.
