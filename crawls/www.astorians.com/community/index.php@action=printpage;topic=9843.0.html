Post by: NYCGoalie on March 18, 2008, 10:55:28 AM
1) Once you are physically in the cab, he cannot force you out.  So before even saying a word, get in the cab.  There has been only one occassion where a cabbie has refused to move for me.  But in that instance, I stayed put.  I said, "either you go or we can be double-parked here and wait for a cop".  Begrudgingly, he left.

2) When you get into a cab, do not disclose the location.  Give a direction.  So, for example, instead of saying "Queens" or "Astoria", say "go down 23rd, toward 3rd avenue".  The driver then thinks you're destination is still in Manhattan.  Then when you approach 3rd, then say "make a left and toward the 59th Street bridge and take the upper/lower level".  At that point, he's done.  He can't force you out of the car since you're already in the care and he has a fair. 

3) There's a rule somewhere I heard about that Yellow cabs can't (or are limited in) pick up fairs in the outer boroughs.  I was heard this when I asked why you don't see nearly as many Yellow cabs as you do Livery one's (and why there are so few Livery cabs in Manhattan as opposed to Yellow ones).  And Livery cab drivers, at least in my experience, tend to charge more in Manhattan than Yellow cab...probably because there's no meter that you can dispute with them.  I try and avoid Livery cabs in Manhattan.

4) It is a good idea to point the Yellow cabs to local places where they can pick-up fares.  The beer garden is a good place.  I normally steer them toward 31st street along the train tracks, where they can get someone who may need to get to Manhattan faster.  And since 31st street eventually leads to the lower level of the 59th street bridge, it's an easy way to lead them home and not get lost.

5) A decent tip never hurts either.
