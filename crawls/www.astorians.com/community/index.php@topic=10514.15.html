Words matter – especially within the law.

You can call the legalization of gay relationships anything you wish but by labeling them as ‘civil unions’ or anything other than marriage means they are second-class, that they are somehow lesser, and to me that’s unequal.

Instead of simply extending existing marriage legislation to cover gay couples a whole new category of family law is being created with its own slate of rules and regulations: civil unions, civil divorce, civil child custody, etc. 

Don’t kid yourselves. One may hold the belief that the State should play no part in the regulation of marriage (or families for that matter) but the fact remains that it does -- so the point is moot.
