Title: Luna de Juarez
Post by: riteus on April 03, 2009, 10:47:26 PM
My wife and I tried Luna de Juarez tonight based on so many recommendations that we've gotten both from this board and from others.  We walked in Friday night at 9:00PM.

There were some redeeming qualities about it but overall, it plain old sucked.  This is my third review that I've posted now and I hate to be such a downer, but I simply can't endorse a restaurant that belongs on Ramsay's Kitchen Nightmares.  It's that bad.

So here we go from the beginning.

From the moment we walked in, there was a live band.  I immediately got excited until I realized that the band was awful.  Really, it was one member of the band ruining the performance for all, namely, the trumpeter.  He was horrible.  Not only did he not realize that he couldn't play a note with any kind of purity and he couldn't sustain a note, but he tried to do solos all night and routinely tried to "trump" his band for volume.  Everyone in the restaurant was squinting and wincing when he played.  And boy did he play a lot.  So the mood was bad.  I'm not blaming this on the restaurant per say, but it was BAD.

Next, the service.  They asked us if we wanted a drink.  I said, "Diet coke, please."  My wife went with a water.  After tasting my drink, I realized that I got a Jack and Coke instead.  Because I didn't want to get charged for the alcohol, I told the waiter that I had ordered a Diet Coke but got an alcoholic Jack and Coke.  He apologized and said he'd be right back.  Sure enough, he showed up 10 minutes later and brought me...what do you know...a Jack and Coke.  I tasted it again and told him it wasn't right.  I wanted a DIET COKE.  He said he messed up and would be right back.  He came back later with a JACK AND COKE again.  So I thought it was fate that I'd be drinking a Jack and Coke instead.  And I did and ended up paying for it.

Now onto the most important part: food.  My wife ordered their "famous" steak fajitas and I ordered the beef enchiladas with verde sauce, rice and beans.  The waiter told me that the verde sauce was a great choice for spicy food lovers, which I am, so I was excited.  The order took 40 minutes to get to the table.  Now ordinarily I wouldn't complain about 40 minutes for food, but we were one of only two tables that ordered food.  It was kind of funny because based on body language both my table and the other table were wondering why the food was taking so long.  Oh well.  When the food finally arrived, my wife looked horrified.  Her steak fajitas ($17.95) were completely overcooked.  The meat had shriveled along with the onions and peppers.  They almost looked dehydrated if you could get past the fact that most of it was burned.  Her side of pico de gallo was shriveled as well, most likely due to refrigerator burn.  You could instantly tell it was probably made days ago with both the tomato and onions translucent and having no juices.  On the other hand, my enchiladas ($13.95) were plump with cheese and verde sauce poured all over.  The rice and beans looked "out of the can," but it didn't matter because the enchiladas looked good.  Until I ate them.  The beef inside was completely dry to the bone, like jerky dry.  Also, the sauce was as mild as milk, there was no spice in it whatsoever.  I asked my wife to try mine to see if it was more edible than her burnt, dehydrated food.  The sad part was that she wasn't sure which one she preferred more.  I flagged the waiter down
