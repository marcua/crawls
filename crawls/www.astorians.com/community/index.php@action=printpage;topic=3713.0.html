Post by: merm on October 06, 2006, 05:07:09 PM
The only thing you need to worry about legally in a libel/slander case is to just make sure that whatever you are saying is true. The very definition of libel is saying something that you know to be false about someone that causes them hardship. If someone scams you, and you know it's true, then they can hardly sue you for libel.

If you genuinely feel that the community should know something that has happened to you, then by all means share it. You can do so without invading the privacy of someone else in our community, but can help educate people on what to do to avoid getting taken advantage of someone who tries to do something similar in the future.

But I think the general feeling here is that this board isn't a place to just wage war on someone personally. If you want to do that it's better to do it on your own turf rather than anyone else's (like this board for example). Which is why I suggested a blog as a good place to do that sort of thing.

If the community is really threatened by something this person does/did, then it might be good to share it. But if you just were to post a personal attack aimed at an individual on this board ("expose" them), that might not go over so well.

It all just depends on how you handle it I think.

No matter what I hope you'll stick around and join some of the other threads as well.
