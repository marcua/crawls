San Antonio Bakery #2
« on: January 08, 2006, 03:31:20 PM »
I have to say, this place is so excellent!  flooz and I met with the intention of dropping in to try just the Chilean hot dog, but was ended eating much more.  As I arrived I started talking to Ruben Guzman, one of the owners.  He was very enthusiastic about the hot dog (of course), and also suggested we try one of the sandwiches.  So we ended up ordering one of the hot dogs and a pork sandwich.

First, the hot dog was fabulous.  It's a dog (don't know if it's pork or beef, but I was in a "don't ask, don't tell" frame of mind) covered with tomatoes, onions, avocado, and mayonnaise, in a homemade bun.  Really delicious.  The toppings all kind of meld together as you bite into the dog, creating an amazing flavor.  So savory, wow!  It was served with pebre, a wonderful Chilean salsa made from tomatoes, onions, cilantro, chile, vinegar, oil, and salt. 

The pork sandwich was simple and delicious.  Just thin sliced pork covered with some melted cheese, on more homemade bread.  We had the pebre to add to it, which was terrific.  Such simple flavors and really good.  I liked how these savory items were so genuine; the flavors were wonderfully clean.

We did have some sweet things, a pan cake, which was thin slices of cake layered and covered with dulce de leche.   Not as sweet as I expected, which was nice.  We also tried the lemon meringue pie, and can I just say, yum?  I liked how the meringue was crunchy on top and soft below.  The tart lemon contrasted the meringue nicely.

Oh, we also had some Chilean sodas, one papaya and one cherry.  Both made with sugar and not corn syrup, and that is truly hot.

So the food is really great!  Check it out!  I look forward to going back to try their cheese empanada (they have meat ones, too), more of the dulce de leche desserts, and eat another hot dog.  I took some pictures, too.

San Antonio Bakery #2
36-20 Astoria Blvd. at 37th Street
Astoria
