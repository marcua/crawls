According to data accessed from the link I provided, for units built since 2000, coop and condo prices in Astoria have risen, although prices since mid-2007 have leveled off substantially.  The prices rise more dramatically if you include data from LIC - which ends up being from the newest buildings.  In 2006, there were no coops or condos sold in LIC built after 1996. 

Average prices per Sq. Ft.:

Astoria only:
2006 $543.76
2007 $610.88 (12.3% rise)
Last 12 months $623.60 (2% rise)

Astoria and LIC:
2006 $543.76
2007 $646.62 (19% rise)
Last 12 months $689.70 (6.6% rise)

What's really interesting is if you start looking at the price-per-square foot building by building.  For instance, over the last 12 months, the apartments at 30-80 21st St. have been going for an average of $408.50/SF.  But the apartments at 5-09 48th Ave., a block from Gantry Plaza, are going for an average of $779.36/SF.  That's a tremendous difference.  As I suspected, the newest buildings by Gantry Plaza are generally by far the most expensive, and I suspect they are the ones that are raising the overall averages.  The advertising materials for those buildings were targeting single male 20-somethings working on Wall Street, and despite the worsening economy in many sectors, I don't think their prospects really tanked until last week.  It will be interesting to look at the data in six or 12 months and see what it shows.
