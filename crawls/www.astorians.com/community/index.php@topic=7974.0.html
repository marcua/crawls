Review: The DVD version of Tarantino's "Death Proof"
« on: September 19, 2007, 10:57:01 AM »
I'm a huge Quentin Tarantino fan, and yesterday I eagerly plunked down my hard earned dollars to buy a DVD copy of "Death Proof," one half of the double feature released as "Grindhouse" a few months back.  Unfortunately, they made the decision to release the two films from the double feature, "Death Proof" and Robert Rodriguez's "Planet Terror" separately.  This was a huge disappointment to me, because the two films together were part of an experience mimicking the grindhouse double features of old, complete with phony movie trailers and advertisements.  I think I am one of maybe ten people in America who saw Grindhouse in the theatre, and enjoyed it.  None of this content carried over to the "Death Proof" DVD, and from what I hear, it won't be included on "Planet Terror" either.  I assume that they will eventually release a boxed set with all of the theatrical content included, but I bought my copy of "Death Proof" yesterday anyhow.  Mainly because they have been saying for a long time that a special edition of "Kill Bill" will eventually come out.  It has yet to materialize.  If they do come out with a Grindhouse box set, I will likely buy a copy anyhow, as I am stupid like that.  I watched the DVD version of Death Proof last night, and I think it's awful.  It is an extended version, which restores some scenes that were deleted in the theatrical cut.  And I really can see why these scenes were cut.  Their inclusion totally destroys the flow of the movie.  And guess what?  There is no option to watch the theatrical version, as some DVDs kindly include.  I was really disappointed by this DVD offering.  Die hard Quentin fans will still enjoy it, but for those who hated the long dialogue scenes of the original, expect much more of the same.  Any other Astorians out there who have thoughts on Grindhouse or other Tarantino films?

Scenes are almost always deleted for good reason from movies.  I remember when the extended Apocalypse Now Redux came out with the fabled French Plantation scene people always talked about included in the movie.  And guess what, that scene totally dragged the movie right when you're finally about to get to Kurtz's compound.  Turned out most people preferred it without.

I also saw Grindhouse in the theater.  Deathproof was definitely more enjoyable than Planet Terror but I thought they were both too long.  I know the point was to replicate the old double feature Corman-esque B-movies, but those movies were a complete story, whereas Planet Terror and to a lesser extent Deathproof were intentionally choppy.  I mean in Planet Terror they skip from being holed up in the bar to the bar totally on fire.  I get the missing scene was supposed to be a joke/style choice, but I'm more of a story over style kinda of viewer.  Deathproof was better, and Kurt Russell was great "Careful my arm!  I think its broken! Ahhhh (screams like a woman)", but there was too much build up with that first group of girls. We were all drunk and other things (cough cough) when we saw it, which I would say is part of the b-movie experience, and my one friend fell asleep.

It does suck when DVDs won't let you watch the theatrical version, and not including the trailers (Don't Don't DON'T was my favorite) is bullshit since they were supposed to be a big part of the "experience"
