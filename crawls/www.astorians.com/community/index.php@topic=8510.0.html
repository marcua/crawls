Quote from: GaffeyGirl on November 02, 2007, 11:39:41 AM
I'm looking for a good brunch spot near Astoria Blvd. Any suggestions? It would be great if they served mimosas :)

Astoria Blvd. is really long, so it would depend on exactly where you are.  We live just north of AB and usually walk up 31st just South of Ditmarsto Igloo for brunch, but they don't have liquor, I don't think. 

Mike's Diner is right across the street and isn't spectacular, but isn't bad either.  They have turkey sausage, which makes me happy. 

Neptune has liquor and it's not awful, just nothing special. 

I haven't had brunch at Sparrow, but I had a sandwich there that was great.  However, their brunch time tends to change on an irregular basis.  Last I saw, it started at noon. 

For what you want, you might want to take the walk to Michael's on Broadway and 33rd St.  It's a little bit more of a walk, but they have brunch specials that include a couple drinks.

Some people like Brick on 30th Ave and 33rd St., but I find it to be overpriced with awful service and subpar food.

That's about all the places that we've hit.  I'm interested to hear if there's anything I missed in walking distance...

Neptune is a classic, solid diner.  I've never had bad breakfast there.  Good omlettes, good waffles.  I get the belgian waffles w/strawberries.  We went there this morning, actually.....good stuff.  I've never thought of it as too expensive.  Their prices seem fairly equivalent to a lot of diners I've been to.  Sure, it may not be the BEST deal, or whatever, but it's not crazy expensive.  I think our bill for 4 people was around 40 bucks.

We also hit up the brunch at Grand Cafe(on 30th Ave.) sometimes, and I will second Alyssa's recommendation.  The eggs benedict there are pretty good, and for the money you pay, it's a great deal.

I'm surprised no one has mentioned the brunch at Ovelia - 30 Ave and 34th St. You get a mimosa/bloody mary and milk/tea/coffee with your meal and has some really good options as far as food. I'm a fan of their apple/pear home fries - it's always refreshing to get away from so much starch and they come with a nice light cinnamon sauce - and their homemade sausage is amazing. They have a selection of omelettes, pancakes (whole wheat available), crepes, and just recently expanded the menu to include eggs benedict and florentine as well as a few other dishes that look promising. My favorites thus far are the cornmeal pancakes and the three greek cheese omelette. Definitely worth at least checking out. However, the service can be quite slow though typically courteous, so be sure you have time for a leisurely brunch.
