Thanks for posting the link AlexNYC. I read the article yesterday and have been mulling it over.

I appreciate the sentiment expressed in the by the writer but my only thought upon finishing was sigh -- isn't a little late? I'm sorry Alanna Schubach, by my standards if Astoria is "over" (and I'm not sure what this means), it happened at least three years ago, which just so happens coincides with the time of your arrival.

Sorry to be so blunt, but I'm just really tired of discussing if Astoria is in, out, or in-between. I just don't see the point. How many threads have been started on astorians.com on this very topic, going back at least 10 years?

I'm old enough to know now that everything changes. Not all businesses are forced out by gentrification, many sell out because the owners are old like me and want to retire.

From a longer perspective, many of the changes have been good, some bad. The rental prices are a real concern to me, but that's across the entire city, not just Astoria. I'm lucky to live here, at least for now.

I'm curious to know how others feel whether you be long-timers or new-comers.

Thanks for sharing the link to that article. I've lived in Astoria all of my life and have been in the 30th Avenue area

months as neither of us likes how the personality of the neighborhood is changing. We also dislike a lot of the new
construction going up - 2 examples are the new buildings under way at 29th St.  31st Avenue and the one on 31 St. between
Broadway and 31st Avenue. They don't look like affordable housing for working class like us.
