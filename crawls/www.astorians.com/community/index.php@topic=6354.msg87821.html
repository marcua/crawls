The United States traditionally makes a bad occupier. And that's not a bad thing. So it's time we got back to doing what we do best and get the hell outta there. We're not by nature imperial, we suck at it. Time to pack up and go home.

Start with a drawdown and keep a temporary presence of no more than 50,000 to bolster the Iraqi army and protect Kurds until the country becomes more stable. But the IRaqis must be allowed decide whether they want to descend further into civil war or not. It;s just sh*tty that we precipitated this mess in the first place, and made it worse through incompetence/arrogance or whatever. But the only viable solution I see is a phased withdrawal. We're not accomplishing any national security objectives anymore. We're just chasing armed gangs who seem to be more intent on targeting each other (but mostly innocent civilians) than U.S. forces.

As for Al Qaeda, they won't last in Iraq because the Iraqis themselves don't want them there. Sunni tribal leaders are already turnign their militias against them, and the Shiites are mortal enemies of Al Qaeda. They'll have better success (and have already) setting up cells in North America and Europe. Especially Europe.

Best way to deal that threat is to pick up stakes, too. I'm a big believer of reinstating and bolstering U.S. isolationism. That means greatly reducing our commitment abroad and let the world fend for itself (sorry, Darfur, but that's the breaks). Why get invovled somewhere when you'll eventually be hated for doing so -- even if well-intentioned? Our history is bloodied by such adventurism, which usually led to worse repercussions: Central America, Southeast Asia, Lebanon, Iraq, Somalia...

The principles we hold near and dear seem to be left behind whenever we leave our shores. Not sure why, but that's what happens.

Besides, we've got a lot more important thigns to focus on here. Education, health care, individual liberties, privacy rights, economy, infrastructure, and on and on.

The U.S. needs to go on strike.
