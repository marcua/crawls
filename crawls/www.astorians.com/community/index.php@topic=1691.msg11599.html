Re: March Madness Meal!

The meal was great - and the food was great too!!

I had the chicken/spinach/mushroom and cheese crepe - I always get it there and it never disappoints (me and the 5 other people it can feed). Jonathan had the beef with dumplings - I think that dish is a little dissappointing compared to the other choices - but it was still good.  The keilbasa that I tasted was excellent.  French fries were okay.  Perogis are NOT my favorite food.  I think they're too bland and last nights were no exception. 

One of the shocks of the evening - a staff person came over to see how everything was!! At the Bohemian Hall!! Let me repeat - a staff person showed interest in how the customers were doing!!  Now I don't know if this kind of service is going to carry over when the fun/food moves outside - but I don't think any of us had EVER experienced THAT.  I did ask the woman if she was the new manager (I remembered seeing the job posting) and indeed that is who she was.  But besides said manager - I think our waitress was quite good as well.  At least I didn't hear any complaints. 

The long table (an issue in my world) was NOT an issue - b/c although I was very far from Marta all the way at the other end, the room we were in was empty for most of the night and so loud talking was socially acceptable.  And encouraged - at least by me. 

It was great to see everyone as always.  And it's always a pleasure to meet new folk.  Welcome to "Astorians Eat" Rachel, Amanda and Daisy I hope you all enjoyed yourselves.

I had a great time last night meeting all of you!  You're a fun crowd, and made a newbie feel very welcome.  Yeah, I was a bit surprised too by the attentiveness of the waitress - I'm so used to being ignored but that's usually outside with one waitress per 200 tables or so. 

I'm so excited I now have people to try new restaurants with!  I'll have to go thru my list and figure out where to start.  I came in late to this forum so apparently I missed the Churrascaria - but I have a few takers who don't mind going again with me.   And I don't know who might be interested come warmer weather, but I kinda like going to Cavo for dinner and drinks once in a while.  If you can see past the trendiness factor, it's really quite beautiful there, the staff are very friendly, and they have great food and drinks.  It's a bit expensive and there can be a line on the weekend - (so go early to avoid the line), but it's fun.  Good for people watching too.  Anyone who's interested is welcome to join me this summer.
