From Wikipedia:

Bodega may refer to:

    * A Spanish term meaning a warehouse; especially for the storing of wine usually located on the premises of a winery, i.e. a wine cellar. Bodegas can also store other alcoholic beverages and spirits. The term bodega is also used (at least loosely) for "wine cellars" of other types; wine bars or wineries, i.e., facilities for production rather than storage of wine.
    * A bodeguita, or general store in Latin America
    * Bodega, California, a town in the United States

Regional usages

    * A convenience store in New York
    * A brothel in Poland
    * A shoe store in Boston, Massachusetts

In New York City, independent convenience stores are almost always referred to as bodegas, a Spanish word for "storehouse". The word "Bodega" is not ordinarily applied to chain convenience stores such as 7-11. In New York bodegas tend to carry fewer healthy foods in comparison to their neighborhood's socioeconomic level [3].

____________________________

I would take this to mean that any non-chain convenience store qualifies as a bodega.   The definition says that bodegas "tend to" carry fewer healthy foods, but that doesn't mean some bodegas don't buck the trend.  Although, I am one of those people who doesn't care what word you use.  A deli is a bodega is a grocery is who cares.   :-)
