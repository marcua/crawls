Most people I know go to chain restaurants because they're consistent. The key to McDonald's success is that you can go to a McD's anywhere in the world and you know that Big Mac is going to taste the same (or close to it) as every other Big Mac you've ever had.

Not all chains are bad. I'm sorry, but I think it's ignorant to say that all chain restaurants are bad. Sure, I'd ideally prefer to give my dollars to a great local mom+pop restaurant, but they're not all good. Chain restaurants are what they are--usually mediocre but sometimes you don't want to deal with trying to figure out if a local place you've never tried before is any good.

I just moved to Astoria a few months ago and I've been pretty disappointed with the restaurants in the neighborhood (Broadway + 30th ave area). I love Aliada but a lot of other places I've been to are just *okay*. I wouldn't mind having a quality chain restaurant in the area (like Chipotle!) to give these mom+pops a little motivation to try harder.

Couple of comments here. 

Wife and I ate at an Applebees last month (we were upstate on a Sunday night and it was open).  We went for 2 different chicken over salad dishes thinking it would be fairly heavy.  Both had two types of cheese and the dish was smothered in sweet fattening dressing. And oh yes, mine had sour cream and guacamole on the side.  The place is a vehicle for fat and carbs to suit the American palate.  On the other hand the place is clean, prompt and does the trick when the mood strikes.

Grubstreet.com posted an article on Chain restaurants and their good points which I wholeheartedly understand and can agree with.  It's more about the ability through good management to maintain high standards in staffing, cleanliness, etc...

But please know that I hate most chains and always prefer a cook doing his/her best to serve up something heartfelt.
