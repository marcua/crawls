Tuscan menu for mom and family tonight.

Pork roast
with 2 day marinade of the following paste: rosemary, garlic, lemon zest, olive oil and salt. Roasted with potatoes and onions, 4hrs at 250f. Pull pork from oven when it hits 160f. Leave in veggies to finish at 400 for 15 minutes.  Pour 1/2 cup olive oil and juice 1 lemon over the meat as it rests.  Slice and put over veggies.

Tuscan white beans with Swiss chard. 
Simmer 1 bag dry beans for 1.5hrs with 4 cloves garlic, 2 bay leaves and 1/4cup olive oil. Beans should be just soft.
Boil chard in salted water till softened, drain. In pan saute lots of garlic with olive oil. Add chard to saute then add in beans with 1 cup bean water or more. Stir and simmer with 1 branch fresh rosemary and some salt.
Added bonus: Render some pork belly fat or proscuitti fat before adding in garlic. This will give a more authentic richer flavor. 

Apple and fennel salad
Thin slice 2 apples and 2 fennel bulbs. Toss with olive oil, red vinegar, salt, touch of fig jam (or similar sweet), black pepper. Toss together and let sit. Onto platter with crumbled baked and dried proscuitto (or pancetta). Top with good blue cheese.

Chaibatta bread pizza
cut bread into into two long slices. rub in garlic & oil. Thin slices of tomatoes and sea salt on top. drizzle with touch more oil. Grate over parm cheese and toast in oven.

We caught some blackfish over the weekend.  It's a traditional chowder fish, but we didn't have enough for it to be the only main ingredient.  So we bought half a dozen large sea scallops (bay scallops would be excellent but they're more expensive) and a few chowder clams to bulk it up.

I used Jasper White's fish chowder recipe as a starting point, and came up with:

4 slices bacon, diced
2 T unsalted butter
1 leek, cleaned and slivered
1 medium onion, chopped
2 bay leaves
1 1/4 tsp Old Bay seasoning
salt/pepper
1 quart chicken broth (or good fish stock)
3 smallish red potatoes, cut into small dice (~ 1 cm cubes)
3 chowder clams, rough chopped, with their liquid
2 fillets blackfish, cut into ~ 1" chunks
6 sea scallops, quartered
1 1/2 - 1 3/4 cups half and half

I browned the diced bacon and removed the pieces, leaving the rendered fat in the pan.  Added butter, leeks, onion, bay leaves, and seasoning.  Lowered the heat to medium-low and cooked until onions were soft and translucent but not browned, 5-10 minutes.  Added chicken broth, clam liquor and potato cubes, raised heat, and cooked until potatoes were soft, about 10 minutes.  Lowered heat to low.  Added the clam pieces, simmered for 3-4 minutes, and added fish and scallop pieces.  Simmered for 5 minutes, turned heat off and let sit for 10 minutes, covered.  Added half and half and adjusted seasoning (it took a surprising amount of salt for my taste, but I'm a salt fiend). 

All of the seafood was *just* cooked - the fish was tender and plump, just barely flaking in big chunks, and the scallops were just past translucent.  The proper chowder flavour was there, but it's not a strong-flavoured recipe.  It was more subtle than I expected, but it was still very satisfying.  Heavy cream would definitely add richness, but it's not necessary.

This works best with fresh fish.  One of the fillets we used was frozen from a fishing trip a couple of weeks ago, and the texture difference was obvious.  The pieces that were frozen were much drier and flakier, although they didn't taste more done.

Astorians,
  With a hint of snow in the air yesterday, I made a roast pork stew with root vegetables.
  Basically, I took what was left of last week's callas (pork shoulder)--little more than a little meat and the marrow bones and threw it into a pot of boiling water.
  I added several diced cloves of garlic (to taste), a couple of onions--coarsely sliced, a couple of thinly sliced carrots, a parsnip--cut into cubes, a diced stalk of celery, a turnip--cut into cubes, and diced curly (Italian?) parsley. 
  As this simmered away, I added six Thai bird peppers--cut into tiny pieces--for heat (I am a dyed in the wool aji fanatic), a bit of crushed red pepper, "Italian Seasoning" from the "spice rack", and salt and fresh-ground black pepper (to taste).
  Finally, I added several "handfuls" of dried pearl barley to absorb the liquid and turn a soup into a stew.
  The result of this experiment was delicious!
