For all you who cite examples from history where the U.S. and its allies or the European powers and their ally the U.S. (its not like we invented warfare) may have acted questionably I have two words: grow up.

Its war. What do you think happens during war? Two soldiers shake hands and then take turns shooting at each other until one or both are dead? The bodies are picked up and everyone goes home for dinner?

Weve all grown up with this myth, derived mainly from WWII propaganda I think, that somehow war can be clean, all soliders honorable, that civilians somehow can be avoided in the middle of a battlefield. That war is somehow fought only strategically and ordinary citizens are never targeted. Since when? In what war did this happen?

The firebombing of Dresden, for example, was a terrible chapter in WWII and European history but it was the British, not the United States, who targeted the city and dropped most of the bombs. Was it a strategic decision to do so or payback for the Blitz? Was it any worse than the German firebombing and destruction of Coventry in England? what would have made it fair -- if the Brits dropped the same number of bombs as the Germans and then stopped? Revenge is a powerful emotion and like it or not plays a role in life and during peace and during wartime.
