I don't know the age of Jiggs and I don't mean this in an offensive way--but sometimes older people are set in their ways and are unwilling to try new ways of paying, such as with a credit card.  I think of my father, who at 52 years old (not terribly old), is dead set against having his paycheck direct-deposited into his account or getting an ATM card, when clearly it would be more convenient for him.  Instead, he has to leave work to go to a bank and cash his paycheck each week, and then he takes a lump of cash home, puts it in a little cash box for his bills, and when the bills are due, he takes his cash and drives it to the bank to deposit it into his checking account.  I keep telling him there's a better way, but he won't listen!  He even cut short a trip he was taking because he knew that his bills were due and that he had to get to the bank.  There is no changing his mind, and it seems that you can't convince anyone in that mindset that using a credit card will be easier.  Credit cards are easier, more convenient, leaves a paper trail for proof of payment, and you can dispute unauthorized charges.  It is up to the user of the credit card to be responsible and watch their statements for unauthorized transactions, pay the bill on time, etc.  If one could be responsible enough to bring cash to the gym to pay each month, they are certainly responsible enough to remember to pay their credit card bill.
