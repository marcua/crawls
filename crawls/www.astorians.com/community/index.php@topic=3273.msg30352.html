Cell phone rant
« on: September 05, 2006, 05:58:58 PM »
OK, so I was at the Bouchon Bakery in Columbus Circle today trying to enjoy a splurge lunch which was interrupted by the woman standing next to me who was complaining loudly and bitterly into her cell phone about printing out pictures from IPhoto.  I was having a hard time imagining having to listen to all of this throughout my entire lunch, so I asked her politely "Excuse me, could you please keep your voice down a little bit?"  She gave me an angry glare, continued talking into her cell phone, and then started complaining (loudly enough that I could hear) that I had asked her to keep her voice down, that I should get the message that there are a lot of people in New York, how could I dare ask her to keep her voice down, etc. etc. for about five minutes.  Finally I said "Just let it go, OK?" and she accused me of eavesdropping on her, started shrieking that I was a psycho (the f-word was used during this time) and that I should read a book instead of listening in on her private conversation with a friend.  I pointed out that first of all, this was not a private space and that secondly, the only reason I could overhear was because she was talking so loudly.  She was already done eating so she packed up and wheeled herself and her baby stroller away, still complaining into her phone.

Was I in the wrong?  I guess I don't understand the logic of pretending that public spaces are private phone booths.  I work in a store one day a week, and I've had customers who are on their cell phones the entire time, even up to the cash register, to the point where I feel bad for interrupting their conversation to say "Can you sign for the charge?"
