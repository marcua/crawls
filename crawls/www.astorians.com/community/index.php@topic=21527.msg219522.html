We brought this home last night for dinner as a take out order.  They could not have been more accommodating.  Everything was very good.  We placed a large order to try a number of things - and have left overs for the work week.  I loved the burnt ends.  A slight sweetness that I liked.  A bit fatty, but that's to be expected.  But just right in terms of burnt and tender.  The brisket was delicious.  Just the right amount of smoke in my opinion.  Even the cole slaw and pickles were excellent.  The baked beans were a little too sweet, but I loved the flavor overall and would get them again.  The mac and cheese was very good but I have no business eating it!  Anyway, to sum up, I'm not from Texas and will not pretend to be an authority on BBQ.  But all I know is these people are working very hard to succeed and please their customers.  The meat was delicious.  I think the prices are fair for the product.  No, it's not a bargain.  But it's not overpriced either, if you ask me.  I think they'll do very well in this location.  It was packed last night with happy customers.  I can definitely see us being regulars at this place, without a doubt.  I want to try their burger as well.  Apparently they caught the attention of the Silvercup studio crowd and can barely keep up with the orders.  It really is that tasty.  I can't compare it to John Browns, but frankly, this address on 30th is just better for us.  But I agree, I think they both can succeed in Astoria without any problems.

I went there today and I thought it was really good! I didn't see fries on the menu, though, so they might not have fries. They have sweet potato wedges, potato salad, and a few other sides.

I had the Bob's Burger, which is a 1/2 pound burger that includes ground brisket and smoked bacon. That's right, smoked bacon in the burger. I got it with sharp cheddar cheese on it. Honestly, I thought it would be kind of disgusting even though it sounded fascinating, but it was not disgusting at all. I ordered it medium rare, and it was medium rare. And it had just enough smoked bacon taste. It wasn't even greasy, although it was juicy. It was really good, and I didn't feel gross and fat even though I ate the whole thing.

Please note that even though the description on the menu says it has BBQ sauced drizzled on it, it doesn't. I didn't want BBQ sauce drizzled on it, and when I asked the waitress that it not have BBQ sauce on it she said it doesn't. So yeah. The only thing is, I would've liked an onion slice to put on the burger, and it only came with lettuce and tomato. That's my only complaint. And I think if I'd asked for an onion slice, they probably would've provided one. The coleslaw that came with it was good too.

The place is nice and clean inside and the bathroom is nice.

I would not describe the apple pie as orgasmic, but it was better than Entenmanns. The crust was a little soft but the flavor and texture of the apples was good.
