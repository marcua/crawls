FOR RELEASE: IMMEDIATELY 06059/06E0894
PSC Announces Additional Public Statement Hearings and Educational Forums
Regarding July 2006 Outages in Long Island City Network

Albany, NY – 10/17/06 –The New York State Public Service Commission today
announced additional public hearings to take statements from affected customers and other
interested parties on issues related to the July 2006 power outages in Consolidated Edison of
New York, Inc.'s (Con Edison) Long Island City electric network. The Commission also
announced that there will be an educational forum held before each public hearing. The dates,
locations and times for the four educational forums and hearings are as follows:

Wednesday, October 25, 2006
The Hellenic Center
22-51 29th Street
Astoria, NY 11105
Educational Forum 1:00-2:00 p.m.
Public Statement Hearing 2:00-3:00 p.m.
and
Educational Forum 7:00-8:00 p.m.
Public Statement Hearing 8:00-9:00 p.m.

Thursday, November, 2, 2006
P.S. 11
54-25 Skillman Avenue
Woodside, NY 11377

Educational Forum 4:00-5:00 p.m.
Public Statement Hearing 5:00-6:00 p.m.
and
Educational Forum 7:00-8:00 p.m.
Public Statement Hearing 8:00-9:00 p.m.
Department of Public Service staff will conduct a one-hour educational forum for
consumers at each of the locations listed above. Staff will summarize the key issues being
examined, the procedural steps and the schedule for the case established by the Commission.
Informational handouts prepared by staff will be available. Knowledgeable staff also will be
available to answer questions.

Public Statement Hearings
Promptly following each consumer educational forum, interested members of the public
will have an opportunity to present their views before an Administrative Law Judge at a public
statement hearing. It is not necessary to make an appointment in advance or to provide written
materials in order to speak. Speakers will be called in the order in which they sign up at the
door, on a first-come, first-served basis. Everyone wishing to comment will be heard or other
acceptable arrangements will be made. A court reporter will transcribe all public statements.
The transcripts, as well as a summary of the public comments, will be placed in the official
record in this case.
The Public Service Commission is particularly interested in comments from customers
that address the following questions:
• How were you affected by the outages?
• Do you think you received timely, accurate and adequate information from Con Edison or
otherwise about what was happening before, during and after the service outages?
• Did you attempt to contact the company for information or assistance immediately
before, during or after the outages, and did you get what you were requesting?
• What are your overall impressions about how the company responded to the outages and
your reasons for those impressions?
• What are your recommendations about actions you believe the company should or should
not have taken before, during and after the service interruptions?
• What actions do you recommend the Commission take in light of all that you know about
these events?
Disabled persons who may require special accommodations should contact the
Department of Public Service's Human Resource Management Office at (518)-474-2520.
Other Ways to Comment
Interested persons not wishing to attend or who are unable to attend the public statement
hearings may comment (in English or Spanish) on the July 2006 power outages in Long Island
City by mailing their written statements to: Honorable Jaclyn A. Brilling, Secretary, New York
State Public Service Commission, Three Empire State Plaza, Albany, New York 12223-1350.
Statements should reference "Case 06-E-0894 - Investigation of Consolidated Edison Company
of New York, Inc.'s Long Island City Electric Network" and should be received no later than
December 12, 2006.

Other alternatives available for submitting comments for consideration include:
Toll-free Opinion Line: Interested persons may call a special toll-free Opinion Line at
1-800-335-2120. This phone line is set up to receive comments about the proceeding from instate
callers, 24 hours-a-day. Callers should select English or Spanish and press "1" to leave
comments about the Con Edison case.
Internet: Comments also may be submitted via the "PSC Comment Form," in the
"Contact Us" file, accessed through the homepage of the Commission's Consumer Web site. The
Web site address is http://www.AskPSC.com and comments are downloaded regularly. Many
libraries offer free internet access.
All statements and comments received will be considered by staff in its investigation and
by the Commission and will be placed in the public case file and made available for public
inspection in the Commission's file room.
A copy of the Commission's notice in Case 06-E-0894 can be obtained from the
Commission's Web site at http://www.dps.state.ny.us by accessing the Commission's Documents
section of the homepage. Many public libraries offer free Internet access. Also the notice is
available at the Commission's files office, 14th floor, Three Empire State Plaza, Albany, NY
12223 (518-474-2500).
