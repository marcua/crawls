Cookware is sort of dependent on what you want to cook.  Even something elementary like scrambled eggs can be a nightmare if the pan is made out of the wrong material.  So where you buy your cookware isn't as important as what you buy, and since cookware can be expensive it isn't necessarily something you buy locally just for the sake of supporting the community.  If you're new to cooking and don't know what you want to buy, look around online for information on how to choose, look at big-name stores (Macy's, Penney's, Bed Bath and Beyond are just three) online (as well as QVC and HSN) to see what's currently available and what's on sale, and go to Marshall's/T J Maxx to see what they have because it really is half the regular price of the other stores.  Also, determine in advance how much space you have to store your cookware because if you've got small space available you have to choose pots & pans that can nest inside each other well.  While you're at it, measure the inside of your oven so you know how big a roasting pan you can fit it - assuming you're going to cook a Thanksgiving turkey.  And, unless you've got lots of money, you might want to look at recipe sites that let you choose recipes based on ingredients, because it's a major drag to find out your spices cost vastly more than the main ingredient - you might want to expand your cooking repertoire slowly.  And, don't forget, you have to buy measuring cups, measuring spoons, mixing bowls, cooking utensils, pot holders, oven mitts...


I'm not trying to discourage you, it's just a reality check!  It's going to take a while to get up to speed in replacing what you're been eating in restaurants, so just do some planning in advance. :)
