Dog fight last night at Plaza Lounge
« on: May 22, 2009, 10:52:09 AM »
Hi all - haven't posted in a while, nor am I one to raise speculation/rumors/fear, but I just wanted to give everyone a heads-up about an incident last night on 29th Street, right off 30th Ave. 

At around 10:30 or so, my husband and I heard multiple loud screams and dogs barking/fighting coming from the street.  This horrific noise went on for quite some time (a couple of minutes...which is about 110 seconds too long for a dog fight)....my husband ran downstairs to see what all the noise was about, and I stayed in (to subsequently call my sister, who lives in the neighborhood and has a dog...I wanted to make sure she wasn't involved).  After a few minutes, when my husband didn't return, I myself went down to see what was going on.

Outside of the Plaza Lounge were two cop cars, and the place had pretty much emptied out onto the street (mind you, because it was so nice out last night, it was PACKED and there was quite a crowd on the street).  Lots of residents of adjacent buildings were also standing around.  A medium-to-small-sized dog (black, looked to be a mutt) was standing upright on the sidewalk and his owner was hysterical and talking to the police.  It seems this woman was sitting outside at Plaza Lounge, with her dog - on a leash - lying on the sidewalk beside her table, when a stranger and his dog walked by.  His dog, a rather large, stocky pitbull, went after her dog, possibly unprovoked, and the commotion ensued. 

Once the dog owners - and the crowd - were able to break up the fight and someone began to call for help, the owner of the pitbull fled the scene, running down 29th Street with his dog.  I'm told he took off and made a right onto 30th Road.  The poor woman with the black dog - who was bleeding from his chest - was left to speak with police and figure out what to do with her injured dog. 

Now, I love pitbulls.  My sister (who, as mentioned before, lives in the neighborhood) owns one who is very young and energetic, but is neither aggressive nor threatening.  HOWEVER, I am repulsed by this guy's behavior.  Not only is he an irresponsible dog owner who doesn't know how to properly train and/or control his pet, and takes an uncontrollable animal into such a busy environment where it could be provoked to attack, but he ran away from the scene of the incident, like a complete and total coward.  I understand that the probability of him running into a lawsuit or having his dog taken away from him (and possibly euthanized) was very high, but that does not excuse his actions or the actions of his animal. 

I am writing all of this for two reasons.  1) if anyone has any information on the black dog and how (s)he and its owner are doing, please let me know.  The woman and her dog were driven away in a squad car, presumably being taken to a 24-hour veterinarian/Animal ER.  2)  if any pet owner living around the 30th Ave/29th Street area sees a man of thin stature walking a tan, stocky pit bull with clipped ears (think "Daddy" from "Dog Whisperer", but not nearly as submissive and calm), please be mindful.  Cross the street, turn around with your dog, etc.  I see a lot of dog owners on my street who let their small (under 25-lb) dogs onto the sidewalks unleashed so they can go out, do their business, and go back inside...but you can never be too careful.

Thanks, all.  Again, if anyone has any info about the condition of the dog owner and her dog, please feel free to fill us in or PM me.
