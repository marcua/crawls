Ohhhhh drive up PCH!!

From where your hotel is at drive south on La Brea, then take the 10 highway west to Santa Monica, stop there in Santa Monica (Adjecent to the freeway which turns into Pacific Coast Highway and go to the Third Street Promenade for breakfast, hop back in your car drive back to the freeway to PCH and cruise to Malibu (Sunglasses must be involved ;). Stop at the Getty and stroll the beautiful gardens and see the amazing antiquities they have (FYI you might need reservations to park your car) then hop back in your car continue driving to Gladstones on the beach (a hop, skip and a jump from the Getty) and watch the sunset go down over a nice cool beer ahhh make a wish.

Universal Studio's and City Walk are a close ride from your hotel. I believe if, you don't want to drive or save on parking you can take the "Red Line Metro" (on the corner of Hollywood and Highland)directly there. It let's you off on the other side of the pass so, you just walk up the hill to the City Walk... They might still have Ice Skating right now if, the sun hasn't melted it.

Also, the sunset strip if, your looking for that. I would take a bus or a cab because parking there (though close to you) can cost anywhere from 25-40 bucks... they have theme bars like saddle ranch, dublins, The Argyle, The Standard and Chateau Marmont.

I also like Venice. You not really a tourist unless you check that out. If, you take Washinton Boulevarde west to the beach (you can actually park your car right next to the sand) there's a couple fun places there and a really yummy resturant called C&O. (there's two of them make sure you go to the one all the way down by the beach) C&O is an Itailian themed resturant with Chianti running ammuck in glass barrells that is on the honour system. You can get an enourmous plate of pasta that two people can share for about 12 bucks and chianti at 5 a glass you can't beat it. There are a couple bars on the same street. One is the Baja Cantina where Margarita's and Nachos are the specialty oooo' I almost forgot..... tequila, lot's of it (the Cadillac Margarita's make me forget my name) Then there's The Townhouse where all the locals go and the Whaler that usually has Karaoke on the Balcony (kinda fun).

Main Street Santa Monica is fun at night too.... But, it can be like a frat party sometimes.

My favorite Resturant is on the south end of Main Street Santa Monica called Chaya. A little pricey but, it's an absolute treat, and across the street is the Firehouse which has a pretty good selection of wines and beers to finish the night off.

I would avoid Downtown at NIGHT unless you know exactly where your going. It can be desolate at night and no help from cops if, you get lost.... If, you must go to downtown at night I would go to the Standard Downtown (there's two, one's on the strip and the other on is downtown). That place has an amazing view of downtown at night and usually has a DJ spinning.

For something you can walk to from your hotel, you can go to the Roosevelt Hotel, they have a couple Bars and a DJ spinning at night.

If, you go flat broke you can stop at my mom's house. She'll make you Gnocchi to die for.

Good Luck on the show, have fun and don't come back saying "Dude" and/or "Awesome"

2 things:

Find a Marukai - it's a Japanese "supermarket" that sells EVERYTHING and it's awesome - their English website is difficult to read because of the unfortunate font they chose but you might be able to get a glimpse of the wonderfulness that is Marukai

and
