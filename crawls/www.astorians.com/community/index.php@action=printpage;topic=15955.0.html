Title: Re: Drawing classes?
Post by: enigmacat on August 28, 2009, 10:13:02 AM
A few years ago I got back into art after a long hiatus. Here are a few things I've tried:

The Art Student League has inexpensive classes and you will almost certainly be able to find a weekend class. The league is very teacher-oriented- however they want to teach, they get to teach. I've been in classes where the instructor never spoke to us unless we specifically asked for feedback, and in others where feedback was very structured. A lot of students there seem to find instructors they really mesh with and keep working with them for years. But if you don't like the instructor or don't speak the same language you may be in for a very frustrating time (however if that's the case you can likely switch classes).

Hunter College has beginning drawing classes (look at their continuing ed catalog), generally on weeknights. Those classes are a bit more expensive, about $250 for 6 weeks, but I had a better experience there than at the ASL.

Additionally, wetcanvas.com/forums has a lot of tutorials for various mediums and forums for discussion. You can post your work there for the other members to critique and generally they'll be very helpful. Even reading critiques of other people's work can be interesting.

A couple of books I've found to be helpful are "The Creative License" and "Drawing on the Right Side of the Brain".

Good luck!
