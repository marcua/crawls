Fellow Astorians
 
Before I comment on the prior postings, permit me to tell you the week I had. I'll try to be brief.
I encountered so many of Chris so called friends saying he's alive. I asked three simple questions.
1) What's his last name? 2) What hospital? 3) What room? Easy questions, right? Wrong!
All different names, different hospitals,don't know what room. I even offered them a paid taxi ride, we will go together. No one accepted,making excuses, a few got nasty or tried to. I'm the wrong person to pick on because I fight back if need be. Before I left Astoria two more different people came over and told me he was brain dead. The other,in a private hospital in Manhattan,Chris being wired with a pacemaker. I wish he was around I would ask him, to make all of this stop. Then again to catch him in a good mood.
 
Anyways your suggestions of putting up a photo,mural, water fountain etc are heartfelt ideas but they wouldn't last very long with all the street denizens lurking about. Just a reminder, they're not homeless though portray as such.
 
Those of you who wish to remember Chris, there are two ways.
1) According to street custom, you can leave a container of coffee or a cigarette at his site.
2) Make a donation in his name, any amount is appreciated to the Saint Francis of Assisi Breadline 135 West 31 Street N.Y.N.Y. Besides feeding the homeless all are welcome and never get turned away.
 
At my next speaking engagement whether it be civic organization or educational institution I have lost one more street brethren to talk about. My new book when it's published he will be mentioned. You never forget the people who touched you in a fond way. RIP Chris
