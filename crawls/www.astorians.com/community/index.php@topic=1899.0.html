I had Dawar for dinner again last night (yup, another night working at the office).  This time, I had chicken saagwala - "boneless chicken marinated in yogurt and spices and herbs and cooked in a puree of spinach".  It is delicious - and there's enough left over for lunch today.  Saag (spinach) dishes in most NY Indian restaurants are quite mild, spice-wise, but Dawar's version has what to me is just a terrific level of heat - still on the mild side, but enough for a "warm glow" feeling.  (Now, every time I've eaten from Dawar, my colleague has done the ordering, and he has an Indian name.  I don't know whether they adjust the spice level or not (as many places with spicier cuisine do).  But I'm happy that they at least can - and do - make it authentically.)

The other dish I've had there was the goat vindaloo.  I described it like this: 

I am eating dinner.  Goat vindaloo, leftover from last night.  I am having the rice with it.  Why, you ask?  Because it's in the top five of the hottest things I have eaten in my life.  I mean, it's delicious.  But it's red-face-making, sweat-leaking-out-of-my-hair-follicles, esophagus-burning hot.  If I didn't eat the rice, I might pop like that mosquito in the Tabasco commercial.

Rice often gives me indigestion, so I usually avoid it.  But it was absolutely necessary to the enjoyment of this dish.  All in all, it was so good that my mouth is watering by my just thinking about it.

I can't remember what dishes I've had from Utsav -- I've had a few, all very good. My coworker thinks Utsav is slightly better than Dawar, but both are great.
