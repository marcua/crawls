Just wanted to share my thoughts on Queens Library as I have now been using them for about a month.

I go to the Steinway branch to pick up books, but I've requested all books that I've gotten there online on their website. Pick up is a smooth process, but their website is extraordinarily lacking.

I moved my "books I want to read" list over to Amazon, so I am not using any sort of wishlist that may exist on the library website. I have noticed that quite a few books, including some popular ones, are not available in the Queens Library system which is disappointing, but considering the size of my list I'm not too concerned about running out of books that I want to read.

As far as actually requesting the books on their website, it's not exactly the most user-friendly experience, but I've figured it out and it gets the job done. What's nice about it is that you can check back on their website periodically to see what the status of the request is - available, checked out, pending, etc. What is odd, IMHO, is that they send you a notice in the mail for each individual request when the book is available. So, let's say I've requested Twilight, New Moon, and Breaking Dawn - they send you three individual notices.

What I did was to see if I could add my email address to my online account so I could get emails instead of paper notices, but they don't let you do that. Instead they say on their website that you have to go to the circulation desk at the library and request for your email address to be added to your account. I did this one of the last times I went, and since then, I have received zero emails even though I've already requested and picked up a book since having my email address added.

Anyway, overall, it seems to be an easy process and it is saving me a lot of money. I would typically spend about $30/month to get used books off of Half.com, but free is obviously better - especially now in this ridiculous economy. They just haven't caught up with technology (which could be because of internal political crap - who knows?), and that's truly my main gripe.

I use both. 
For books: Queens Library is my first choice, they have longer check-out period.  I also noticed for popular books I'm more likely to successfully renew in Queens rather than in NYPL.  On the other hand, Queens Library seems to have better selection of books in foreign languages.  However, some more current books in English were available only in NYPL.

For DVDs: NYPL is my first and only choice.  You can request them/place on hold and they will be delivered to your branch of choice.  You can keep them out longer and the fines are not as high as in Queens Library. You cannot request/place on hold DVDs in Queens Library.

Electronic Books: NYPL.  I'm not sure if Queens Library has them.

On-line Catalog: NYPL uses a popular software that is fairly easy to use.  I've seen it at libraries in other cities.  Queens Library on-line catalog is a bit clunky.
