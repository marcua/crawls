It's definitely not a "first world" problem or one for new residents. I'm a lifelong resident and not even a senior or disabled.
I find it infuriating when there's a huge mound of snow or ice in front of a home or a working business where the residents or owners just don't bother. I almost slipped and fell in front of a lawyer's office on 31st Street near the Astoria Blvd. station.


He was in the office and saw me nearly fall. I went into the office and asked him why he hadn't shoveled.


The answer - a shoulder shrug and that was it. No apology, nothing.


A neighbor of mine - a young guy in his 30's broke his leg a couple of years ago by slipping on ice in front of a real estate office that hadn't shoveled.


This is a real problem for anyone just trying to get around. To dismiss it as something amusing just shows the wealth of ignorance of people who don't have any common sense or consideration for other people.
