Post by: goldfish on October 10, 2008, 05:25:59 PM
When I adopted my second cat, I went to Animal Care and Control: http://www.nycacc.org (http://www.nycacc.org).  This is the municipal shelter, which is required to take in every animal that someone brings in, so they do euthanize animals.  It was important to me to be saving a life as well as finding my new buddy.  It's probably also the most cost-effective way to adopt, unless you get a "free kitty" from craigslist or something.  I went to their Manhattan shelter, which is in a slightly scary neighborhood but nobody bothered me.  There are usually volunteers there on weekends to help out adoptors. 

Some friends of mine found their perfect kitty at the North Shore Animal League, which I believe is in Nassau County, so I don't know if traveling there is a possibility for you.  According to their website though, they're waiving adoption fees for cats in October:  http://www.nsalamerica.org/ (http://www.nsalamerica.org/).  They are a no-kill organization, and my friends had a very positive experience there.

We already had one cat who was found on the street and went through a few families, and spent time as an outdoor cat, before we took her in when she was two. She had been a solo cat her whole life and was independent, but lonely when we left during the day (she was always waiting at the door when we came home and circle us and stick by us all night). We decided to get a second cat for her to play with.

We went to a few shelters and spoke to people and most everyone recommended we get a kitten. We had an adult female at home and people told us females are more territorial and dominant. A kitten could acclimate to this more easily and the older cat could dominate (or become motherly) to a kitten more easily.

We were also told to get a male. Again b/c of the territorial thing and b/c male cats are more often less dominant. Basically they said combos of male-male or male-female (as long as they're fixed) work best.

From Pet Finders I got our kitten from Zani's Furry Friends: http://www.petfinder.com/shelters/NY606.html (http://www.petfinder.com/shelters/NY606.html)

Post by: YaFace81 on October 15, 2008, 03:12:58 PM
Hmmm...that's a tough one.  I know it's probably unlikely a place will let you adopt a cat on a "trial" basis but I'm sure any staff member or volunteer can give good tips on how to integrate a new cat into an already-cat living household.  Typically, cats don't play nice for the first couple of weeks but once the alpha cat figures out the other one isn't going away, they'll get over it and start getting along as best they can. 

And just because they're not playing or cuddling together doesn't mean they don't get along!  In turn, just because they're fighting often and/or avoiding one another entirely doesn't mean they hate one another.  Cats are finicky but can definitely adapt to new situations very easily.
