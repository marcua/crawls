Free Speech: How Far Is Too Far?
« on: May 28, 2008, 11:51:12 PM »
In the last decade or  so, with the explosion of  the internet and many things taken out of context, mostly on purpose, sometimes by mistake, cries  of 'too far' have been heard Left  and Right. IMO, The cries are very loud, speech codes and 'pressure groups', mostly from the Left. But, no doubt, 'decency groups' and others, mostly out West and South also still hold sway. Recently, several controversies, Hillary Clinton refrencing RFK's assassination as  her reason for staying in the race, in this case  Obama, well, you get the connection? Others have said things about McCain 'dropping dead', using it as 'joke', about his age. Throughout the Bush/Cheney years, the first 24/7 cable/internet  Presidency, endless  commentators have used  'quips' that Bush would never be killed, cause 'we' would get Cheney.  :| Certainly, if one likes  a President, they are more  'angry/upset', if such 'jokes'  are made, and certainly Hillary shot herself, no pun intended, in the foot, when she used the comparison. Still, even with some of the most vile 'jokes' I have ever seen being said about this or any sitting President and jokes about attempts on his life, very few have faced the wrath of prosecution. It should be noted, that there is  a line of decency, but, an even larger line  for prosecution, and even though, I find  many things said as borderline, in this day and age, it would be wise for one, to think before they say some things.
