Apartment Noise Question
« on: March 30, 2007, 12:42:19 AM »
I live in a first floor apartment. Recently, as in the past month or two, the landlord has had someone doing work in the basement, cleaning it out and god knows what else. The problem is, that this guy starts very early in the morning, around 6am, and wakes me up often however I've learned to live with it.

My problem (and concern) is that I can clearly hear the guys voice through the floor.  In all honesty, I think that we could carry on a conversation. This has me feeling that I have no privacy in my apartment, since voices are carrying clearly though the floor.

Now, if this is only being cleared out for storage, no big deal, but I feel very uneasy about someone moving in underneath me.

IMO, it is an invasion of privacy to have this much sound carry through from one floor to another. I can't hear voices through my walls or the ceiling, but I am disturbed that I can hear so much from below (and that if someone is down there, that they can hear me).

What is the best way to bring this up to the landlord? Do I have any rights as far as this goes?

I don't know if a letter will do much  :-(  If your landlord converted the basement into an apartment and does not want install false ceilings or some sort of insulation in the new space, I doubt if he wants to read about your complaints.  Did you check out the legal residency status of your particular building?  Is this a legal conversion?  If it isn't, I think a phone call to 311 is in order!  Personally, I wouldn't threaten him if you find out if it is in illegal conversion, because that usually ends up ugly.  Of course, if you don't mind confrontation and a contentious relationship with your landlord, you could choose that route.  I once hinted at calling the authorities to one of my former landlords for a lack of heat or hot water and he lost his damn mind!  We got hot water and some heat, but not after a couple of ugly confrontations!

Personally, jiffy, after reading your other message regarding the singer, I would consider moving at the end of your lease in six months.  I moved to a much quieter building and I found that it has brought me so much more peace and calm, especially after a hard day at work.   :-)
