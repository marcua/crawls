In the Where Are Astorians From? Poll, 63 of the 119 respondents noted that they are not from New York.  Out of those who are from New York, surely some have travelled for extended periods and picked up the culture of other areas.  Others have parents from other areas and countries.

What foods/drinks are famous in the area which you are from which might be little known around here?

In New Orleans, there are alot, but the big ones are:
1) Oyster Dressing (a stuffing that gets served alongside the fried turkey)
2) BBQ Shrimp (which is not barbequed but sauteed with a pound of butter and rosemary)
3) King Cake (a glazed breadcake that's served between Three King's Day and Fat Monday and has a baby jesus hidden in the cake.  Whoever gets the baby has good luck for the year)
4) Abita Beer (I can't find it anywhere around here, if anyone knows, please let me know!)

In Berkeley/SF Bay Area, our claim to fame is certainly sourdough bread (you can't really get it here because of the different strains of wild yeasts on different coasts).  Also, the organic/sustainably grown food movement started there, thanks to Alice Waters and Chez Panisse.  Anchor Steam beer for sure.  The glorious norteño/SF-style burrito has no match, and it's particularly good with avocado salsa.  Then there's coffee -- Peet's Coffee specifically, the place the original owners of Starbucks learned their craft.

Australia!

Lamb lamb lamb lamb lamb.. leg of lamb, rack of lamb, always with rosemary and garlic.
PIES! Savory small meat pies. Ground beef and gravy in a flaky puff pastry crust. Or steak and kidney. Or onions. Or chicken. Or in a serve of pea soup as a 'pie floater'. Essex street market does the best pies in town (that I've found so far), tho I think you have to have the accent or be in the know to find them. Sausage rolls (like pigs in a blanket but with sausage not hot dog, and flaky puff pastry - and a full-sized sausage at that). And pasties (same pastry, filled with potato and vegetables).
Vegemite. One very acquired taste. Yeast extract spread.
Tim Tams/Mint Slices/Cheezels/Cherry Ripes/Cadbury Chocolate (the real kind - in the US, its made by Hersheys, not Cadbury!). Chocolate cookies and junk food galore.
Prawns on the Barbie. In fact, anything you can throw on a BBQ - mushrooms, asparagus, chicken, steaks, seafood, lamb (did I mention the lamb?). I can even do garlic bread on the Barbie. Its the only way to cook, all summer long.. and doesn't involve all that sticky BBQ sauce that's always around here.
Fish and chips! With scallops, and potato cakes, and prawns and flake (kind of a shark) and mussels and calamari. YUM!
Burgers with 'the lot'. Ie: pickled sliced beets and fried egg with soft yolk as well as all the usuals (cooked onion, lettuce, tomato, cheese, ketchup)

...that's all I can think of for now!

Moldova, of the old U.S.S.R:

We got your vegetarian borscht-like mom used to make. Not your glass jar borscht-I mean the real deal with sour cream on the side if you want it...
Stuffed Peppers or Cabbage with rice, onions, carrots, minced beef in a sweet/sour broth-mmmmh
grandmother's version of burek with farmer's cheese (think nice ricotta salata) lots of dill, full of golden flaky goodness
beef stew from an iron pot-I don't know why but it makes a difference
fried potatoes with dill, have never had anything like my grandma's potatoes and I'm just talking root vegetable here! :  )
pumpkin stuffed phyllo pastries
smoked eggplant with tomatoes, olive oil: think babaganoush without the tahini
Russian potato salad with little pieces of ham, mayo, carrots, and peas
breaded pork or chicken cutlets
Beet Salad with egg, pickles, onions
"Chaladez," otherwise known as Aspic (chicken soup that is frozen in time with carrots, a chicken neck or some meat)-beautiful but an aquired taste
polenta cooked through, served with feta, sour cream, butter, and black cured olives (if you want them)
sour cherry filled dumplings (yes, with sour cream if you want it-believe me, you want it)
Compote (hardly any sweetness summer soup with quince, apples, pears and sometimes cranberries)
oh yes, oh yes, my grandmother's rugelach, her fresh fresh Napolean cake (pastry and cream made from scratch-no joke, hard work)
a torte that mimics a famous Russian candy called "Bear from the South," don't ask me why: it is wafer thin cake layers filled with ganache and walnuts, all this solidifies together to make the most amazing thing/cake/torte/big candy like thing

My parents and husband are from the island of Cyprus:

Our cuisine is a cross between Greek and Lebanese.  Some wonderful 'Cypriot' items that you can sometimes get around Astoria:

Haloumi Cheese - you can do anything to it, including grilling and frying.  You can get this in any Greek market around here.

Koupes - or kibbe - ground bulgar wrapped around ground lamb or beef and fried.  You can get this on Steinway Street in the Egyptian neighborhood.

Koupepia - grape leaves stuffed with meat.  You can get this at Aliada or Zenon.

Sheftalia - a wonderful Cypriot style sausage like item which you can get at Aliada or Zenon.

Daktyla - my all time favorite dessert.  A thin dough wrapped around almonds and cinnamon and then fried.  Zenon makes the best!
