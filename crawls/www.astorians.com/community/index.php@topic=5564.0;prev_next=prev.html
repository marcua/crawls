I can't reccommend an accountant, but I do have some suggestions for you.  When you have 1099s, as I'm assuming you do from the acting jobs, you can deduct business expenses such as headshots, transportation, etc.  You can/should also make estimated quarterly payments, which will make it a little easier than paying however many thousand all at once, and if you overpay you can apply it to next year or get it back as a refund.  Remember to put about 1/4 to 1/3 of your income in a savings account, where it can earn interest until you have to give it to Uncle Sam.

Maybe Backstage or one of the other industry publications would be able to help you find somebody to help you?

Incidentally, don't go to H&R Block if you have a lot of different income sources.  The more they have to enter, the more they charge you to file, and they don't tell you how much they're going to charge until the end.  I had 1 w2, 3 1099's and lots of student loan interest to deduct, and wound up paying $250 just in filing fees.  Plus the woman doing my taxes kept berating me for not having my student loans consolidated, and wouldn't listen when I told her that they already are, and that I had more than one account because I went to school twice.  Won't be going back there again.
