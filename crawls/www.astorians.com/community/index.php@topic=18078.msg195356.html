Grand Jury Duty is the best... if you can take the month off. they call you, you go. Unless you're dead, dying in less than a month, or are a sole provider. No "I'm going on vacation" (well, if you can prove it they'll give you 6 months- and they WILL call, and you WILL serve then). No sitting around waiting to be called; you get assigned to a jury, they swear you in and take you to one of the Grand Jury Rooms. That's it, you start right then- usually within about 15 minutes. There is a routine, usually bail jumping warrants are first, then the meaty cases come through. Most are done in less than a day, some go on for the full 20 days you're there. But it's not boring repeating stuff. After a few days, people got to know each other and we would bring donuts and the Box o'coffee thing. On slower days we could bring DVD's and we would vote on which one would be put on. Mostly harmless family type comedies that had the most appeal across the demographics. And you'd be surprised how everybody got along. After a month together, the last day we all went to lunch at the Chinese place across from the courthouse. We had 3 big cases that went almost the whole time, and over a hundred smaller cases that were usually finished in a couple of hours. All were put forward except for one small drug arrest case due to bad presentation by an assistant DA.

They will pay if you're employer does not. It isn't a lot, but it's something. Your employer cannot take action against you for serving on a Grand Jury- they can be heavily fined, sued, prosecuted or all three. The 20 consecutive days was changed from 1-2 days a week for 6 months... People hated that worse. There was some grumbling the first few days, but then everyone realized that many of the juicy cases could not have been properly presented in a 2 week window. We were lucky that no case needed to be extended and would have been on-call until the case was finished. They will wait to present a big case until a new Grand Jury is seated to be sure to have the most possible time and they make every effort to get it put through by closing.
I preferred it to regular JD since it was a regular schedule and could make plans around it instead of calling and maybe having to show, maybe not on a few hours notice.
