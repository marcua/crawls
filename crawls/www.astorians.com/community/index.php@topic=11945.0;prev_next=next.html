This is how I make mine:

1 lipton tea bag
1 cup of boiling water
sugar to taste

I steep the bag in the water for 2-3 minutes (there are directions on the package). Then I sweeten it with regular sugar and either pop it in the fridge or let it sit on the counter until it's room temperature. Then pour over ice.

That's what I do at work. On weekends, I make a big pitcher so I can have it all day. The important thing is to NOT overbrew it. It will get horribly bitter.

Until March of last year, I lived in Salisbury, NC. That's around 20 minutes north of Charlotte. It's a wonderful little town!

What I miss:

Driving down open country roads, with no one behind you beeping.
The strange pockets of counterculture you'll randomly come across.
Slower pace of life.
Space. Tons of it.
Sonic, of course.
Religion, though definitely a fixture, isn't as in your face. For example, you'll see Christian activists holding signs up in front of the Christian owned Chick-Fil-A's, but you won't be screamed at by Five Percenters, and people won't interrupt your lunch to give you something tantamount to Chick tracts. Religion is so much worse here.

What I don't miss:
A lack of options. Here, we have any type of food imaginable. This is truly the city for new experiences.
Since I'm on that topic, I'd really really REALLY miss Kebab Cafe. It's a small piece of heaven for me.
A lack of public transportation...though driving is wonderful too.
Everything closing so early, and the fact that nothing is open on Sunday. Seriously??
Frat boys. Yeah, they're here too...but down south, they try to dominate everything everywhere they are.
