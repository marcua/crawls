Not to be the first to disagree and hopefully not the only (but I suppose that's okay too), but I DID leave Astoria 7 years ago, and then promptly moved back a year later.  I had found a one-bedroom in Rego Park, and it was really nice.  But....     I ended up hating Rego Park.  I missed Astoria.  What did I miss?  How it only took 10-20 minutes to reach Manhattan versus 40-60 minutes from Rego Park.  The young people in Astoria.  Rego Park is full of coops which seemed to attract a much older crowd.  Nothing against senior citizens - I just don't want to be the only young person in the building.  I didn't fit in, there was no where to go, nothing to do.  All there was to do was shop, shop, and more shop.  No one comes out to Rego Park to visit you.  I was lonely, tired of the commute, tired of the lack of culture, etc.  Of course there will be people who will disagree with me, and happen to love Rego Park.  That's fine, it just wasn't for me.  My lease went up, and the couple wanted to sell me the coop.  I got the hell out and moved back to Astoria.  Realizing that I had not taken advantage of what I had here.  It's been 7 years, and it was the best move I ever did.  So many restaurants, great food, movies, cafes, clubs, 24 hour delis/laundry/grocery, 10-20 mins for nyc, houses, trees, birds, diversity, culture and bustle everywhere, yet quiet in other spots....  Even the Greek charm has worked its magic on me.  Friends come and visit all the time.    Yes, there are aspects of Astoria that I can't stand - the smoking in bars and pretty much everywhere, the lack of people pooper scoopers, sidewalk snow sweepers, the drastic rise in rents, the corporatization of stores on our streets, - but overall, I still love Astoria and don't see my moving anytime soon.  In fact, the only reason I would move is if I bought - since buying a condo in vicinity of the train is largely impossible in Astoria. 

I am happy you found a great place to live though.  Astoria is not for everyone.  And lots of people who grow up somewhere don't want to live there later.  Just like I will never live upstate again (or Rego Park).  :-)
