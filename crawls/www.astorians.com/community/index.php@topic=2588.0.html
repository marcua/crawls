Sidewalks
« on: July 14, 2006, 04:00:26 PM »
I think it was my mid-day walk down by Penn Station that got me all fired up on this, but it's been building up for a while.  What is with people not knowing how to walk or behave with courtesy on sidewalks?  Tourists can be excused, up to a point, but it just boggles the mind how many people in NYC can just tie up a block and cause incredible amounts of grief for their fellow pedestrians.  I especially like it when large groups congregate together next to a payphone, they can truly make passing near impossible.
People stop in the middle of the sidewalk to talk, not stepping out of the paths of moving traffic, oblivious to the dirty looks and occasional unkind word.  People knock the elderly or frail down without so much as an excuse me, and people cluster in packs trapping poor unsuspecting folks in their midst (this happened to me today - brought me to a total stop, breaking my gait - durn them!)  I keep seeing people walk into people carrying large packages or heavy bags with no (or at least very little) concern.  What's happening to manners in this city?
As an additional aside, people seem to be spitting gum on sidewalks lately with more regularity, which in this heat is making for nasty sidewalks.
