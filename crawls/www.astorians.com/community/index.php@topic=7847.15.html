I don't many people on this board seek 100% accurate answers from the media.  There is a healthy dose of skepticism, as well there should be.  But in certain cases, the media performs a valuable service: mediating between what is crap-o-la and what is a real story.  Example; the mainstream media filters out stories such as the 9/11 "Truth" movement, because they are non stories.  It is true that YouTube is a trove of unfiltered, unmediated footage, but that does not mean that the lack of an editorial process equals quality in all cases.  The "Truther" movement is an example of this.  They use pseudo-scientific "evidence" to prey on the public imagination.  They know damn well that very few people will pick up a reputable publication with a credible refutation.  They have crafted a story that reads well and the pieces seem to fit, especially since everyone is looking for answers about that horrible day.  And they should be ashamed.  The fact that the media reports on Britney and iPhones is as much of a commentary on us as it is on them.  You can find great news coverage (such as the Jim Lehrer Newshour on PBS.  I admit I don't like the editorial slant, but it's quality news.).  But very few people watch it, because it is a bore.  Our national lack of an attention span feeds right into YouTube.  Give me an unfiltered clip of some "expert" or "politician" of 10 minutes or less, because I am not going to read newspapers of various editorial stances, or watch C-Span to form my own views.  The media is a mirror.  They are reflecting what we want them to tell us.  But they are not a "distraction" in the same sense as "Loose Change" and all that other nonsense. The 9/11 "Truth" conspiracy might read better than what really happened, but it's still garbage.
