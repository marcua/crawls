Quote from: Telesphoros
Well I think people seem to be under the general impression that bans are bad, so I'll spring my opinion.  I wouldn't ban any drugs either.


Keeping "hard drugs" illegal is an infringement on people's rights -- and not only on users, but on other society members. It's generally accepted that part of our tax money can and should go to law enforcement since, as the argument goes, we all benefit from public safety, etc.

However, what we have in all these prohibitions on cocaine, marijuana, etc., is that our tax money is being used to suppress people's private actions. I object to my tax money being used to stop people from taking drugs. I don't use drugs, and I don't give a damn if anyone else does. But to have to endure extortion to prop up the Rockefeller Laws is immoral.

Legalize it. All of it. Regulate it if you have to, like by setting potency limits on cocaine or heroin (much like they already do for beer/wine). Protect consumers by giving them legal recource should they be given "laced" product. Make drug transactions a legally enforced contractual agreement between purchaser and seller that protects BOTH parties. Full warning labels on packaging like, "Herion may be highly detrimental to your health and can be fatal." Or "No matter how much of this you snort, you will not be nearly as cool as you think you are."

OK, I'm being facetious a bit... But let's get this country off the iron lung mentality that says the government must be allowed to tell us how to run our lives b/c we "don't know any better".
