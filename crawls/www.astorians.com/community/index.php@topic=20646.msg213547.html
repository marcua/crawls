Artgirl and I went back Monday night for our second visit, and overall had another fine and fun meal, repeating some favorites from last time and trying some new things.
  • The Pachanga Sangria (apples, oranges, mango juice, rum, full liter $24): Again, flavorful but not too sweet. I ate all the boozefruit lustily.
  • Black Bean Soup (“BB Soup”) (lime-sour cream, crispy tortillas, $6): A favorite last time, the soup itself was equally rich, smoky and flavorful this time, though there seemed to be less of the lime sour cream, or maybe it had just broken up more into the soup. We missed attacking the big dollops that had floated on the previous edition, which really set it apart, and we later told our server, who said she’d tell the chef. I’d definitely go for it again, but I might ask for “extra lime cream” (or possibly “more grace notes”). I reiterate that this big bowl and a drink and maybe some chips and salsa could be my lunch or a light supper most days.
  • The Stuffed Chili (roasted poblano, quinoa, cous cous, cranberry beans, walnut cream sauce, $6): I feared this might be rather dry and cardboard-y with all the grainy stuff listed, but it was a very good starter. The single large pepper was spread open and flat, with the stuffing on top and the sauce mostly underneath and alongside. The overall effect of the grains was kinda like a toasty, savory breakfast cereal with beans, which (like much else here) is slightly offbeat but in a good way. The pepper itself, sometimes an afterthought in stuffed peppers, stood up well, tender and tasty. The sauce was great, it was like sweet cream for my Mexi-Wheatena. Today I realized I might have enjoyed a kick to this dish; maybe I’d dash on a little of the hot sauce from the table bottle next time.
  • Steamed Mussels (chorizo, corn & jalepeno, $8): My favorite dish of the night. The delicate mussels in the broth with charred bread for dipping would be a fine dish in itself. But then you find, and scoop up, some of the many corn kernels and chorizo bits under the broth … mmmmm. Among the many ways people do mussels in a broth, this is instantly one of my favorites. This dish is listed as a small plate, but as artgirl notes, you get a fair number of mussels, and the corn makes it more substantial, so it’s suitable as a smaller entrée. And it’s awesome.
  • Roasted Pork Belly Enchilada (chocolate, fig & peanut sauce, cumin crema, $14): When I first tasted this dish, the bright, sweet, peanut and fig flavors of the sauce jumped out at me and seemed a little strange. Digging down into the tortilla, which had its own distinct flavor, and the extremely tasty pork itself, it all made somewhat more sense, with the chocolate blending into the umami element, though it still didn’t seem a totally logical combination. Artgirl liked the pork a lot but said she found the overall dish “perplexing” and probably wouldn’t want a whole plate of it. I agree that it’s a little off the wall, but I think that’s part of the point at this place, these unexpected, slightly thrown-together juxtapositions. I enjoyed the madness. I’d order it again and embrace my perplexity. You make the call!
  • Panna Cotta (peanut brittle crust) (desserts not on written menu, but recited by server): I’m a big fan of custardy desserts, and desserts with savory elements, and this is a good example of both. The custard was firm enough to be substantial, but was smooth, creamy, and not too sweet, with a floral overtone    that we were told was a Mexican flower. The brittle was basically just perched on top, and was quite salty as well as sweet. A fine complement to the dishes we’d eaten.
  • Other stuff: We arrived early, like maybe 6:45, and the place was only about a quarter full, not surprising for early on a Monday evening. Artgirl had a banquette seat, and I had a chair, which wasn’t plush or anything, but didn’t cause me any discomfort. The backyard was open and seemed thoroughly set up, and a large party trooped out there shortly after our arrival and seemed to enjoy themselves. The place was nearly full when we left, but I never felt squeezed or jostled. As has been observed by other commenters, the plate clearing was somewhat overly zealous, and I had to thwart it more than once, though I suppose that’s a good sign too, since I wanted to finish everything!

I just went for the first time. It was a seriously bipolar meal and I don't know what to make of it so I'll just break it down.

PROS
Hanger steak - my sister had this and it was seriously delicious. But when my mother had a bite she said there was sand in the spinach.
Panna cotta and diablito cake - AMAZING. Both desserts were the best part of the meal. They both had interesting flavors to them that you don't expect from your usual panna cotta and chocolate cake, which made it all the better.
Guacamole - I thought this was really flavorful and it was gone quickly, although the chips were pretty much rock-solid.

CONS
Black bean soup - my mother had this and I have to disagree with what others have written. It was seriously overwhelming and I was sick of it after two spoonfuls. I also have an embarrassingly low tolerance for spice and this was definitely the spiciest of the things we ordered.
Pork belly enchilada - I had this, and it was eh. some bites were pretty good, some weren't. I didn't like the whole peanuts and one bite I took was basically a huge mucusy ball of pure fat. They need to take out some ingredients because it's really puzzling.

Service was ok, the busboy ignored us for the first half of the meal although he was very attentive to the people next to us, strangely enough. Then I think I scared him or something because he refilled our water every 3 minutes after I asked.

Nothing was terrible, but I hope they fix up some things because the space is really cute and quirky, especially the back area.
