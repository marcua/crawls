Fortunately, I got my current apartment for $850.  It's a small studio with a separate kitchen and too many problems that the landlord doesn't seem to want to attend to.  We have actually started having civil conversations regarding some of the issues and because my personal debt prevents me from a better, more expensive spot, I will have to deal with some of the problems if I want to stay in the area.  

Despite the issues, it's a very quiet building, a few steps from the train, and a five to ten minute walk from most of the big box stores on Northern Boulevard.  I got it through George at Alexiou Realty, who showed me a bunch of places under $1,000.  I agree with the members about the difficulty in finding something that inexpensive, though, and the recommendations that were provided were right on!

Be careful for converted basements, private homes, and realtors who ask you in a "friendly" way, what country you were born in (yes, I answered the question, but I was desperate to get out of where I was living).
