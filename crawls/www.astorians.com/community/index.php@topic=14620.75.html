I think the analogy is pretty simple- the KKK believe that black people are "ruining the moral fibers and values" of this country. They believe minorities are "not what this country was founded on". I don't believe that all people who are against gay marriage are somehow morally equal to Nazis or the KKK, but the logic behind the argument seems to be the same to me.

I remember something about glass houses.

I am not sure what you are referencing here. I have seen the ad, and agree that it is silly, but what is in the ad that suggests people for same sex marriage are somehow related to genocidal maniacs?

Opinions can be expressed in any way one chooses. And when they use expressed by demonizing the other side, they should be called on it. That goes for conservatives, liberals, progressives, moderates, whomever. Let's take a look at that incident at the Miss America pageant. A contestant respectfully states her opinions. Her opinion is not the current politically correct one (although it is shared by the current president and vice president). And how is it treated? If it were just some internet bully like Perez Hilton drawing cumshots on her, that would be one thing. But it goes into to main stream media and who gets demonized? The one who respectfully stated her opinion (also shared by the majority of the state which she represents).
You say potato and I say, well, potato. It's not the "idea" of "equality in marriage" (which already exists). It is the generations old redefinition of marriage which is radical. That doesn't make it offensive in my opinion, but to claim it is not radical is willfull blindness.

Like I said, personally, have at it. I will happily attend weddings, or commitment ceremonies of my gay friends. But this browbeating of "if you disagree with me on this issue, you're a nazi" is far worse for your cause, and only pushes back the inevitable.

Quote from: 28Grand on May 05, 2009, 08:44:04 AM
If ‘live and let live’ Conservatives or Libertarians choose to sit on the sidelines and let more radically-minded people and groups hijack their agenda then I’m sorry -- it’s their own fault if they feel alienated from the public discourse.

I deliberately left out the words conservative or liberal from this statement because it applies to both camps.



My point was that you were quick to object to the Nazi and KKK labels but silent about the content of the ad.

If you are as neutral or live and let live as you claim to be I would expect that you’d be as critical of the opinion that depicts storm clouds threatening destruction and linking it to gay marriage and gay people as you are of the links between those opposed to the gay marriage and the KKK and Nazis.

But you now dismiss the ad as just ‘silly’. Others, including myself have a far more negative description for it.

Perez Hilton is silly (and idiotic). The Miss America pageant is silly. But both are entertainment vehicles, not serious discourse and in the end are inconsequential.

Equality in marriage for heterosexuals and homosexuals does not exist, regardless of what you say. I agree that to argue for its parity is radical – and I don’t think anyone would label it otherwise.

In this case I take ‘radical’ as a positive step. But when I read your comments it appears to you define radical as a negative, which leaves me with the impression that you’re arguing both sides of the issue.

You may be happy to attend weddings and civil ceremonies of your gay friends. I’m left to wonder if they would feel the same way given you consider it so such a radical redefinition of the institution.
