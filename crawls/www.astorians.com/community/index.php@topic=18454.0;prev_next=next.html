In my personal dealings with the Councilman, I have found him responsive.  I also think he has 'grown into' his position over the years.  It can't be easy.

I have three criticisms -

Once the first monstrosity went up in Astoria, bells and whistles should have gone off and that provincial narrow minded Pistilli should not have been allowed to develop anything.  The re-zoning should have gone into affect 10 years ago.

The only way any more venues in Astoria Park should be added is if they are adding the staff and necessities required to keep the venues going.  No garbage cans, no skate board park.  No staff, no thoughts of an ice skating rink.

Finally, I feel that our Councilman should acknowledge that he is not a shoe-in for this position because of his family name.  All the elderly in my neighborhood vote for him.  Two of them passed away recently.  Peter needs a new constituency and needs to win us over.  I do think he is trying to do so...

Oh, and one more thing - punctuation is important!

My apologies in advance for the longish post.

I don’t feel the need to defend any member of the Vallone family who holds or has held elected office because nobody forced them and I feel that it’s their responsibility to take the bad along with the good that goes into serving in public office.

My only comment is that the vitriol they inspire in some astounds me. On one hand they are all powerful and control the neighborhood and at the same time are ineffectual and can get anything done. I believe 100% that elected officials need to be held accountable and this will involve a fair amount of criticism but the key word is fair.

To LeavingSoon, I think most people feel (as do I) that you have valid concerns about issues affecting the neighborhood because they are the concerns echoed by many Astoria residents, old and new, for many years, even decades,

My main issue or criticism of you is that you bash everyone else for not doing enough when you have shared a grand total of 0 actions that you have taken directly. In regard to the aroma issue:

Have you written to the Department of Environmental Protection?
Have you written to state regulatory agencies?
Have you written to federal regulatory agencies?
Have you organized or been part of any direct protest action?
Are you part of any group or groups?

So far the only evidence of your involvement in addressing these quality of life issues is an open letter posted to Queenscrap (and your posts here).

As Peter said, the airport, sewage treatment plants and power plants were constructed in the 1930s . They are never, ever going away. There will always be noise and pollution associated with them and quite frankly this is not the Vallones or any other politician’s fault. What happens with these facilities  now can only be managed and though I would hope our city council member has some influence, there are many players in the game at many levels of government.

Why call the neighborhood a ghetto when it is clearly not? It seems to me that you want to bash for the sake of bashing -- everyone and everything. If taking a giant dump on the neighborhood makes you feel better knock yourself out. Just don’t expect everyone else to join in with you because despite the problems, most people who call Astoria home love it -- it‘s a part of them.
 
If you projected an attitude that you genuinely card, in a positive way,  if you took the initiative and shared what you have done to address the issues you care about and accomplished something other than ranting on internet forums you could have been in the position of not having the time to return Vallone’s call when he contacts you to join in.
