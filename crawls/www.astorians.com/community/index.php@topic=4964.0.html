weird passivity in astoria?
« on: January 30, 2007, 10:29:22 AM »
Last night I got home from work at 6:30 and there was banging and crashing coming from the apt/building next door  (attached building). It went on. At 8 pm I went next door and buzzed that apartment. No one answered but the banging stopped. I am on the 3rd floor and I know the people who lived beneath the apartment under construction were home. So why didn't they say anything? It's illegal to do construction after 6 (maybe 5, who knows). Made me think of all the people who didn't call Con Ed during the blackout and thought it would get taken care of.

I love astoria and don't miss Manhattan much--haven't lived there in about 5 years. But when things impinged on quality of life, people FREAK OUT in Manhattan -- or they did in my neighborhood, the west village. I freak out when my nice quiet life feel threatened, and I call the cops/311/etc. Although my darkest hour was when I lost it and called the cops on a cherry picker outside my window when the West Wing was shooting on my block. They laughed at me ("Lady, they have a permit") but at midnight on a tuesday with a BRIGHT light shining into my bedroom, well, I lost it--I don't miss the film crews at all).

I'm sure the same crap will be going on when I get home tonight and I'll do the same thing, just earlier. Anyway, my rant is over.

Its just a small town attitude we have here. We turn a blind eye on stuff like that if it helps out our neighbors and friends. Now I don't mean to condone really intolerable behavior-so please don't misunderstand me (if something is endangering safety you need to act on it)-its just this sort of wierd camraderie thing that happens here. Its one of the reasons I love Astoria. At my house, we have tons of little issues with our apt., and my Manhattan friends are always telling me I should complain to this agency or that agency. I would never do that and jeopardize our landlord who we are friends with. It just doesn't work that way. We try to work it out with them or work it out ourselves if necessary and our rent doesn't increase. I guess what I'm saying is that it is more than a cut and dry business relationship. You know-I scratch your back and you scratch mine. You never know when you might need someone's help. And there's also that cosmic karma thing-what you do comes back to you.

There is definitely a weird passivity here - especially after growing up in Manhattan. My take on it is that there are lots of immigrants living here and they don't want to cause a fuss or draw attention to themselves.

It's important to let your super know if there's a problem though because it could indicate something much bigger. Plus, you're paying rent!
