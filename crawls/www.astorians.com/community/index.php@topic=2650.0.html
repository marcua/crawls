Work from home? Get a UPS with AVR and keep working
« on: July 21, 2006, 12:02:54 PM »
I posted this to another thread, but figured it could use a bit wider attention...

If you work from home, like I do, and are stuck in brownout conditions here in Astoria, do yourself a favor and pickup a UPS (Uninteruptible Power Supply) with AVR (Active Voltage Regulation).  The AVR part allows it to take many different voltages in (usually 90v-140v) and give you a clean 120v for your equipment.  The UPS part allows the battery to kick in when power dips below that usable range or fails altogether.  If it's a short time, it'll keep plugging until power comes back.  If it's a long time, it gives you the time you need to shut down safely without losing any data.  Most even have a USB connection and special software that will shut your PC down for you if you aren't around to shut it down before the battery runs out.

I have an APC Back-UPS XS 900.  It's handling the 100v I'm getting now and the occasional drops without issue.
