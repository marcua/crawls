*burp*

Sorry, I was digesting.  ;D

I highly recommend all of you head down to Jonathan's for lunch whenever you can.  Even if you work in Midtown - as I do - it's only a couple of stops on the E or V (my god, I may actually have found a use for the V train).

I got there a little later than Annie and Meg, who had already ordered.  I'll let them share what exactly it was they ordered, because I'd probably mangle the information.  I perused the menu, full of really appetising-sounding sandwiches and salads, and two items leapt out at me.  The only problem was, I instantly knew I'd have to try both of them.  So I asked Jonathan which is better -the Cubano Melt (roast pork, ham, swiss, garlic mayo, pickles, hot peppers) or the Parish po'boy (flash-fried catfish, lettuce, juicy tomato, onion, spicy tartar sauce.)  He was a great help, saying they were both great.  Yes, I ordered both, and thatjimguy is going to reap the benefit tonight.

You're not going to get me to say which is better, either.  Both were fantastic.  The cheese on the cubano (which is not pressed, btw) was melted through perfectly, the roast pork was just right, and the garlic mayo rocks.  The only thing I might change is ordering it with extra hot peppers next time.  As for the po'boy - well, the catfish was probably the best I've ever had, and the tartar sauce complemented it quite nicely.  What else can I say?  Oh, the slice of key lime pie at the end wasn't bad, either.  ;)
