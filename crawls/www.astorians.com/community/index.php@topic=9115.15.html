Open primaries (like in NH) are usually unpredictable b/c it reveals a range of reasons for voting for (or against) a particular candidate. Although it can be an indication of voter behavior in the general election.

But that's not what primaries are for. They're a means by which the members of a political organization chooses their nominees. Or at least it should be. I don't think open primaries should be allowed at all -- if someone want a say in whom a party nominates then that person should have to register w/ that party.

I also think it needs to all be done on ONE day in September. Enough of this months-long jockeying.

The question in my mind is what the losers (Edwards, Richardson) who have some delegates under their belts, will do with them once they drop out. I have a hunch that Edwards will request his delegates support Obama.

edit: and yeah, I'm pissed Hilary won. I was praying for a quick implosion; but the machine is what it is; and we live in a political culture where a carefully scripted cry can sway voters.  :yuck:
