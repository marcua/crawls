Like beating your head against a brick wall - it feels so good when you stop:

For weeks I've been thinking about tidying up my hard drive by taking off files I don't have an immediate use for, reorganizing the ones I do, and then sorting through what I took off to see if I want to keep it.  I had a bunch of 3 1/2 " diskettes with only one thing on each, so I decided to consolidate them and temporarily move my little-used files there.  First I had to buy diskette label makers because I hate to have scratch-outs on labels, and if I printed my own symbols to put on the diskettes instead, I could change the meaning of the symbol when I wanted to use the diskettes for something else later.  So, for less than 30 diskettes I had to buy over 200 labels.  The labels did come out looking nice, though, and as someone on the Avery website pointed out, you can use the labels for things besides diskettes.  I thought the consolidation, label printing, and transferring of my files would take one day.  HAH!  HAH HAH!  HAH HAH HAH!  A week later....

One of the things I did was to gather all my receipts in one place, sorted by year.  When I buy something online I don't usually print the receipt, I save it on my hard drive along with a photo and the ad copy of what I bought.  No paper to lose and I can compare the thing when I get it to what I thought I was getting.  The problem is, photos take up A LOT of space on a drive, and I often use the photos for something else, so I don't delete the receipts.  I transferred 2007 onto a disk and it didn't fit.  I had to put part on another disk.  Then I transferred 2008 onto the second disk, and a third.  I started transferring 2009 onto the third disk, a fourth, a fifth... and then I decided that even though I've had bad luck writing to CD's, I was going to have to give another try.  First I had to transfer all those receipts back to the hard drive.  I had to move them one by one because I didn't see a way to merge a file with another file of the same name.  (Or any other name.)  I added up the total of MB of all the receipts and found it was a tiny fraction of the space on a CD.  I got out a CD, verified it was empty, and created a folder named "Receipts".  I copied 2007 into Receipts rather than moving it because I've had so much frustration with CD's in the past.  I verified that it was copied and checked the remaining free space on the CD.  It was full.  No way.  Not possible.  I tried deleting the folder so I could start again.  The CD refused to delete the folder.  I tried again.  I tried again.  I tried again.  Finally it deleted and I re-created the folder and transfered 2007 into it, and it took up only a tiny amount of space.  I transfered the rest of the receipts.  One week and that's about all I accomplished.
