Title: A fine pint of Guinness?
Post by: Pulled pork on August 01, 2008, 04:05:40 PM
Where oh where does one find a proper pint of Guinness? Not too cold, a thick head and goes down like a glass of milk? 

Not one to be negative on the Rover because that place has always been the salvation of the neighborhood. Great prices, awesome pour of Guinness and nice atmosphere.  But yesterday was the second time that the pint was way too cold, a bit rough and didn't go down so smooth. 

Dillingers: Goes down smooth as can be but a bit too cold and not as cheap as the Rover.  Lately though it's been a better pint that the Rover.

Blackbirds: Too cold and too much money.  Plus the annoying Houlihan's type waiter with lots of flair.

Come on folks, where does one find the best Guinness in Astoria?

Title: Re: A fine pint of Guinness?
Post by: CountingZestfully on August 03, 2008, 12:32:25 AM
I'm a fan of McCaffrey & Burke (30th Ave and 31st Street, right next to the 30th Ave station). The atmosphere is awesomely neighborhood bar and the main bartender pours an authentically Irish pint.

In the city, I love Molly's (23rd & 3rd) in Gramercy. Not only do they have the BEST burgers around, it's easily the best pint in all of NYC. I ADORE this place.

Just make sure you don't ruin them by telling everybody you know (like, say, by posting on a message board about them  :wink:)
