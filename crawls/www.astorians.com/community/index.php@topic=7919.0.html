Any Advice on a Strange Apartment Situation?
« on: September 15, 2007, 04:02:06 PM »
Hello,
I recently moved in with my boyfriend. I moved into his 2 bedroom basement apartment in a multi-family house in the Ditmars area. He had already been living there for a year. The day he moved in, the apartment was flooded. It appeared to be rain water, and we had a big storm around that time, so we shrugged it off. The landlord helped clean up and claimed that it had never flooded before.
We became friends with a neighbor, and according to her, the girls that lived there before us moved out because the place flooded six times in a year. We were worried, but a year went by without flooding.
A few weeks ago, however, our apartment flooded with raw sewage. We had many items damaged. It was disgusting. We do not have any windows (except two tiny basement windows that do not open). The landlord went away on vacation and did not tell us. None of the other tenants had his phone number (he lives right above us). We ended up calling TMobile and they connected us with him. He had a plumber come, and they snaked the system and gave us a video of the problem (roots are growing into the pipes). We were told that the entire front yard must be ripped up. They said that by snaking it they temporarily unclogged it, but it will happen again because the roots are pulling the pipes apart.
The landlord said he would fix it by Sept. 11th. He hasn't. He paid for all of our damages.
We found out that the apartment is illegal (through a lawyer I work for). There were never any permits filed for the basement to have tenants, and the amount of tenants in the building is illegal as well (it's only supposed to be 2 family, not 3 family).
Also, because he was away, the plumbers had to break the lock of the door that leads upstairs in order to reach the technical stuff or something. We were told that the door would be unlocked in case of a fire (because we only have one door that leads outside, and it is right by the stove/oven... and as I said, none of our windows open). The lock was not only locked, but it was so strong that three plumbers could not break it down, but had to get some big metal cutting device. It took three guys 15 minutes to get in. If there ever was a fire, my boyfriend and I would have been dead.
Also, the wall is starting to sink in a bit in a spot, probably because of mold (and there is no ventilation).
We are trying to find another place, but we are college students who are not trust fund babies, so we can only afford so much. So far all the real estate places we've been to haven't found us anything. Craigslist doesn't have anything either. We are college students working jobs as nannies (which actually pays quite well), we have two cats, and my boyfriend is a musician (a trumpet player). It seems like no place within our price range will even accept us.
I suppose this is more like a pointless vent, but I was wondering if anyone had any advice on the situation/our rights as tenants.
