Hi Daveed,

I'm pressed for time right now but let me start to see if I can answer some of your questions.

1) I wasn't a great fan of the Evangelion movies but I do think that the series was interesting and somewhat unique (for a robot theme). I don't much care for the robot sub-genre of Japanime'... but again, this series gave it a new spin.

2) I woudn't rate it as teenybobber, considering some of the subject matter, themes of the nature along the lines of dehumanization, mechanization, spirituality,mass killing and violence. But I would also in no way equate it with the masterpiece (IMHO) of Akira. It's been awhile since I've seen it however. So the details are fuzzy if not totally inaccurate. ;-)

3) I haven't rented DVD's or tapes in a long, long time... the reason being the lack of a selection. I stand by Netflix (pimp, pimp) for their Mission, Reliabilty, Originality, Cost, and most of all...their Selection. The people behind Netflix have been doing this kind of thing for a long time now, but it was only until recently did they become mainstream. I detest Blockbuster, and will not switch to them even if they paid me. :-)
They are the Microsoft of the video rental industry. :-O

subsection $a) Cowboy Bebop the Movie could be considered IMO just a long (additional episode) to the series. I would not judge the series by the movie. This goes even double for Inuyasha which on the big screen (for me) was a tremendous let down. You simply cannot encapsulate the multi-dimensionality of the characters and their long and complex histories spanning over 111 episodes in a feature length film. What you get is a watered down...nay "dummed-down" representational piece that was created even more expressly for mass and commercial appeal.

If you haven't seen the series of Cowboy Bebop, I reccomend that you do. It is one of the most artistically poignant and subversive things that I have ever witnessed...not to mention very very funny. Plus the music is e-Fu*k-ing-Tastic. And, in contradistinction to Inuyasha, it is a complete series with a beginning, a middle and a Final Episode (which had me positively Weeping, especially if you have grown to love Vicious as well as Spike. The ending is not pleasant, but it is apropos (I just love it when artists go out of their way to disappoint their fans!.) :twisted:

Many people have been puzzled by "Ghost in the Shell", especially the movie. Again, I recommend the series first (which, like Bebop is also complete). Ghost in the Shell is a show about how humanity is co-opted by technology and vice versa as well as the philosophical implications of Artificial Intelligence and what exactly it means to be human. "Ghost in the Shell" is a favorite of many hackers and technophiles. The details that go into the drawings come very closely to becoming engineeringly plausible. Any engineers out there who disagree? Everything from the computer workstation, to the insectoid robots to the military devices have been worked out meticulously and with great artistry. If you don't at first get Ghost in the Shell then I recommend doing what I habitually do... and that is turn off the sound and just pay attention to the images, as the dialogue can be quite distracting. OTOH, the dialogue is what makes Ghost in the Shell, as this series explores in a sort of amoral way the machinery of power and what it takes to maintain control regardless of whether such a thing is inherently "good" or "evil".

There are cultural gaps between Japanese and Western society, but I feel that these are easily overcome as Japan is a modern industrial (as opposed to Agrarian) society. As I have watched more and more anime, these differences become inconsequential. We are approaching the very same problems but from a different point of view. OTOH I have found other societies (such as that in Southeast Asia) much more difficult to comprehend.

I personally don't have a copy of Neo-Tokyo, and I only vaguely recall this feature but I am glad that you have respect for DVDs and by extension all media artifacts. If you want to borrow my copy of "Ninja Scroll" or the bootleg looking copy of some pornographic (hetero) anime then you are more than welcome.

Keep watching anime! and if you really want to be puzzled watch a few episodes of Super-Milk Chan"!!!

May the Chan be with you, Muhahahahahahah!!!,
