There was a lot of similar skepticism over whether or not Vesta would succeed on 30th and 21st back in the day.  And then again for Astor Bake Shop.  But I think it's become more and more clear that the area between Vernon and 21st can support places like Bear.  And as more of the new buildings fill up - even more so.  Because from what I saw in the dining room at Bear, it was a young "newbie/transplant" crowd.  As distasteful as that may be to some.  But there I was - and others I recognized - that were not all that either.  My head is not in the sand.  The neighborhood can be a bit sketchy and may always be.  But I think it's somewhat insulting that only a low-priced joint can make it there.  I think ELO was poorly managed and was little more than a little social club.  Bear is really proud of their menu and their food.  They are all about being a terrific restaurant.  I think it's going to make it.  And likely will do extremely well.

We tried out Bear this past Wed night.  Got there around 8:30 and we were the only people there (meeting another couple) and a little hesitant if we wanted to stick around at first.  Glad we did, our friends came and we ordered a ton of food.  On top of that the chef sent out a bunch of extra items and we tried most of the menu - was overall really impressed.  Some favorites were the cold pork belly appetizer, french onion soup, even the salad was really good.  The shortribs were amazing, as was the veal ravioli and beer battered carrots were incredible.  We also had the fried cheesecake (they were running a special for $5 so we had to try it) and that was surprisingly good too.  The chef came out a few times to talk to us and could not have been more welcoming - we will definitely be back.  This is a great little place, I really hope they do well.

I want to like this place, I really do.  The atmosphere is very nice, the cocktails are good (loved the mulled wine--so perfect for a cold night!), and it's clear that whoever's in the kitchen knows how to cook.  But....

....The pricing is very strange.  On the one hand, they have $9 burgers, $15 mussels + fries + beer night on Wednesday, and other weekly specials.  On the other hand, 3 bay scallops and some barley risotto cost me $23, and a pork belly special that was almost entirely fat (excessive even for pork belly) was $18.  These are Manhattan or Brooklyn prices, not that of an Astoria place way off the beaten path.  (And did I mention they don't even have bread service?) Two mulled wines, a small herring appetizer, and two modestly sized entrees cost me $86 with tax and tip--not a terrific value IMO.  If I do come back, it will be for one of the special nights.

I bought a Living Social deal to Bear. I had three courses (all seafood).


My favorite was The Oyster Pond: It reminds me of a dish I had at Corton. It was very creative and love the use of things like  shrimp chips and cod liver roe. If those things gross you out, they do have more accessible dishes. Love that they use locally sourced and organic. Complaint: I wish they offered more dessert options than cake and fried cheesecake. I know it's a tiny kitchen, but they could offer something healthy or even some outsourced ice cream/sorbet. I also thought it was odd they offer an amuse bouche, but not bread service.  I'm so excited to have a casual, fine dining place in the neighborhood: It's hard to find composed, contemporary, non-frozen seafood dishes without going into Manhattan. I thought it was cute that they offered free self-serve punch outside for anyone who walks by. Can't wait to share this place with other.
