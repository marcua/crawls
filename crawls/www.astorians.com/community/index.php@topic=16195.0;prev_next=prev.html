Quote from: newastorian35 on September 26, 2009, 10:01:06 PM
You'd be surprised how well it can work if you put your photo down on a table and take a picture of the picture with a digital camera.

For a quick and dirty xfer to digital I've done this too. Only don't lay it down in a table, but instead use 3M doublestick scrapbook tape and mount it on a wall or a mirror, window, or refrigerator if you're worried about the tape sticking to the wall. But as long as you use the 3M doublestick scrapbook tape sticking to the wall and the back of the photo shouldn't be a problem. Next mount your camera on a tripod or set it on a flat surface at the same height as the image. If using a zoom lens, say an 18-50mm, set the lens so it is at a medium zoom setting, about 24mm on an 18-50mm. This is important because zoom lenses introduce a slight amount of curvature at the edge of the frame and this curvature is at it's least in the middle of the lens' zoom range. Alternately you could just use a good 50mm fixed focal length lens because 50mm lenses are pretty much flat and lacking in any image curvature at all. Next light the photo with two incandescent lights, one to the left of your camera and one to the right of your camera. It is best if these incandescent light sources have lamp shades to diffuse the light and keep them from creating any glare/reflection on the image. Set your camera's white-balance to incandescent, so the photos colors will be reproduced as accurately as possible. Auto-white-balance will reproduce the colors warmer than they appear in the original photo. Also, don't use your on camera flash because it will just create a glare/reflection in the photo you are trying to capture. Frame the photo so it completely fills your camera's viewfinder. Autofocus on the center of the photo and take a picture.

Hope this helps ...
