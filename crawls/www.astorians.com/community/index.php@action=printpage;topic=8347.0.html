Title: Re: Does anyone remember Michael Kelly?
Post by: AlexNYC on October 21, 2007, 09:10:53 PM
The police had a bad policy back then of treating all children as runaways first. It's only recently that people have realized that the first few hour and days a child is missing are critical in tracking him or her down before they can come to harm. To assume first that a minor choses not to be home on their own accord is bad logic, and has cost too many lives. At age 12 a child is still basically helpless, especially against an adult. Sadly it's to late for those like Michael Kelly who may have stood a chance to be located, but at least we're taking the issue of a missing child more seriously today. If the police didn't take the matter seriously until after his body was found 10 weeks later, the physical evidence had to have been limited. Only witnesses or people talking through the grapevine could help now. Hopefully somebody remembers something.

Title: Re: Does anyone remember Michael Kelly?

I remember this case very well....there was a prime suspect however his wife provided an alibi which many people at the time believed was a lie.   There was a lengthy police investigation only after this boy's body was found and after the police being prompted by the newspapers of the time, i.e., the Journal-American, if anyone recalls that paper.  There was also coverage by the NY Daily News.  There was a journalist, Dan Brigham who reported on this case several times.  Also the case, once closed, was reopened by the Police Commissioner at the time and was a headline, as I recall it, on the front page of the Journal-American by Mr. Brigham, report of the Journal.   Older Astorians might recall this terrible tradegy in 1959 still unsolved to this date.

As my screen name says, I have close ties to Michael Kelly's family. I am dubious about the claim of wanting closure for the family; the surviving family in fact might not want to bring it up again. That being said, it is my understanding that one of them aggressively confronted the man the family suspected to have murdered Michael (maybe the same one mentioned above whose wife gave him a false alibi): a man very involved in their church, with a number of young sons of his own. I don't know what was said or if someone vowed to get revenge or justice, but it is my understanding that Michael was a very sweet boy, a good kid, the baby of the family and everybody's favorite little brother. It truly was a heartbreak to a family that had already experienced too many heartaches, and if there were a way to heal that grief, it would be a blessing from Above.
