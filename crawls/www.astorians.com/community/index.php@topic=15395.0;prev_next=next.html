Re: East Moon Asian Bistro
« Reply #35 on: September 29, 2008, 05:37:29 PM »
I've been to this place twice and I wouldn't recommend it (the second time was to give it another chance). The decor reminds me of something out of the 80s, and, as others have mentioned, the hodge-podge pan-Asian menu is a pretty poor excuse for actual Asian food. It might fool someone in the Midwest, but I would think that New Yorkers--especially Queens residents with ready access to Woodside and Elmhurst and Flushing--would know better.

I don't remember what I ordered the first time, but the second time I got the Vietnamese noodle bowl, which is known in most Vietnamese restaurants as bun. There was something oddly lacking about it and it took me awhile to realize that there was no mint, which is essential for this dish.

Also, this is my fault for not reading the fine print, but the 50% off Sushi special that they have been advertising is only valid for Sushi and regular rolls, not any other kind of roll. There's better-priced sushi down the block at Kumo.

Big thumbs down.
