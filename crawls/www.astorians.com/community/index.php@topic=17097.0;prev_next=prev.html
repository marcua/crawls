Closest resorts: Hunter (packed on weekends though), Windham, Jiminy Peak, Mountain Creek. All allow snowboards.

We go with seasonal rentals. My skis run about $175 for the season (return by July or August.)

Weekday rates are often cheaper as well.

Some ski shops sponsor shuttles (how's that for alliteration?  :roll: ) These busses make pick-ups in various locations. We've used the one for Emilio's on Queens Blvd. There's also Paragon and Sundown- Google oughta tell you more about those.

Drawbacks: Getting up in the wee hours of dawn! I stopped in Dunkin' Donuts for coffee on the way there, dressed in ski gear, and was on line behind some drunk women dressed up on their way home from a night club-hopping. Felt a little odd!

Still, the shuttles are nice, and the prices are great considering it includes gas and a lift ticket- around $80. Sometimes they even offer bagels for the ride up, and you can catch up on the sleep you lost.

We eventually decided on Hunter.  I found all kinds of online discounts for buying lift tickets and rentals ahead of time.  I feel so crafty.  Two lift tickets and one rental for board and boots came to $125.  I saved like $70.  I can invest that $70 in a helmet.   :mrgreen:

And for those who want a discount, buy through "E Ski" instead of the "buy tickets" link.  Then google the coupon codes for a deeper discount.  (They expire so this won't work forever but for now "RENTRETURN" gets you 50% off the rental.)  Can't wait!
