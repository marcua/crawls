Wow, I can't believe I missed this before.
I work for B&N Corporate, and I have actually discussed this with someone I know in our real estate department. The reason there isn't one in Astoria is mainly because of space- they have explored the possibility numerous times.
The real estate price to potential profit ratio is apparently too narrow to justify opening a B&N store here. We just closed our Astor Place location and our Chelsea location is closing at the end of the month, but we did open up our new Tribeca Store(Come on by, it's gorgeous). Despite these closures, business is actually doing great, but NYC real estate is so absurd these days that margins get ridiculously narrow when you talk about opening up the type of square footage a Barnes & Noble needs to open.
Believe me, I would be a huge proponent of having a B&N in Astoria, and I'm sure if a space that made financial sense were to open up, our real estate department would jump on it in a second.
