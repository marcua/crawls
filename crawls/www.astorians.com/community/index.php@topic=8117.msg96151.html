I think the recent uptick in vagrant behavior mentioned around Ditmars stop has to do with that scaffolding under Hell Gate at 31st Street. It's dark, dank and hidden from view, which I'm sure makes it ideal for less than acceptable behavior.

Maybe Vallone or someone can get whoever's doing the construction work to step it up and finish it already.

There are definitely newer faces around the intersection and my take is that there will always be homeless around (why some choose this particular neighborhood to loiter in I don't know -- maybe b/c it's safer?). There were a couple of regulars I don't see anymore... Jail, institution, death. Something claims them eventually, some sooner than others. So it's likely that this recent group will go away, and be replaced by someone else.

But when one of them gets potentially dangerous, then the police need to be notified. Immediately and frequently. And if anyone really feels threatened, change your route. If that's not possible and you really feel in danger, go and get yourself some mace and learn how to properly use it (I'm pretty sure it' s legal here.) And take a self-defense class. A good one will teach you how to first avoid a potentially dangerous situation, not kick someone's ass.

As for homeless sleeping in subway cars and stinking it up, well that's just an unfortunate fact of life in the city, too. I actually believe there's been less of that than in recent years. Even in winter.

Alia,
If you see any more info in your area about the emergency meeting, please let me know.  I have been thinking about taking some sort of action if no one else does.  But I don't know what course to take. I can't speak in terms of statistics, but anecdotally, there seems to be an increased presence of street homeless in this area.  There is constantly a very stinky panhandler present at the Broadway N/W station on the stairs.  I can smell him when getting off the train as soon as I get to the token booth.  And there is that guy with the dreadlocks who is usually passed out in the doorway by my apartment and leaves his food and other filth there when he goes to wander the neighborhood.  There are people who hang out by the fruit market diagonally across from Gilbey's.  I have seen them harass Gilbey's patrons, and the bouncer rebuked them and told them to go back to their own corner.  And the man who was hanging out in the vestibule of my building.  Most disturbingly, I am starting to see human excrement by the side of the bank on the corner of 32nd and Broadway.  Also, general vagrancy type behavior.  On Monday, I saw a man who was apparently not homeless passed out in front of Tacos Mexico at 2 PM.  This is to say nothing of the homeless congregations in the Ditmars area.  Some of them reminded me of people you'd see on the streets of Portland or Seattle.  I am a firm believer that these kinds of behaviors lead to the public perception that greater crimes will be accepted.  Someone needs to do something about this.

Hi Cadillac Man,
I hope you are doing well out there.  No intended disrespect for your situation, and I don't mean to equate being without a home or being out of luck with general vagrancy behavior.  But the excrement in the streets on the part of whoever is doing it has got to stop.  That sort of stuff cannot stand whether it is being done by people who are without a home in the technical sense, or not.  I think what is going on in Skid Row in Los Angeles is very instructive.  There are plenty of folks there who are "down and out" and are getting help for alcoholism, drug addictions, etc.  But they get a bad name from people who set up street encampments basically to use as a heroin shooting gallery.  They terrorize the community (to a much greater extent than the people under the viaduct), and again, they distract from the attention which should go to people who need and would accept help.  I don't ever want this area to become like Skid Row, or like San Francisco.  I just sent an e-mail to Vallone myself.  I hope to hear back from his office.
