https://www.nycgovparks.org/trees/street-tree-planting

The city and state have programs to control the spread of the Asian Longhorned Beetle. These bugs destroy trees from the inside. The city inspects street trees and cuts down infested trees. The state quarantines wood from all cut trees to stay within affected areas.

I imagine the inspection and culling occurs on some rotating schedule. That could account for the sudden carnage effect.

I don’t know about 34th St. but I recently saw a tree at 45th St and 20th cut down, and the pieces of their trunks had dramatically large hollow sections, presumably from these insects. There are a couple of stumps in the same area that have similar hollowed-out portions in their stumps.

Here’s a state Dept. of Environmental Conservation page on the Asian Longhorned Beetle,
here is a Cornell “Invasive Species Information” page,
and here is a not-very-recent NYT blog post about the progress of the program as of 2013.
