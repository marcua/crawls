BY CLARE TRAPASSO
A famed Astoria restaurant has closed its doors — possibly for good.
A sign on the door of Uncle George’s Greek Tavern said the long-time neighborhood fixture had been seized by city marshals.
The phone number for the popular restaurant, which served roasted leg of lamb and stuffed grape leaves, was disconnected on Tuesday. It was located at 33-19 Broadway.
BRUCE DIAMOND FOR DAILY NEWS/BRUCE DIAMOND FOR DAILY NEWSPedestrians walk by Uncle George's Greek Tavern in Astoria. The popular restaurant was a neighborhood institution until it closed this month.Calls to the city Marshal’s Office were not immediately returned.
“It’s the end of an era,” said Assemblywoman Aravella Simotas (D-Astoria), who used to hang out with friends there in high school.
“It was one of the original Greek tavernas,” she added. “It sold great food.”
Uncle George’s was reportedly opened in 1978.
