Here's an odd one...

Tomorrow I'm showing my coworker and his girlfriend around Astoria as they are considering a move here when their Manhattan lease is up.  I want to show them some of the things that make Astoria special so figured I would see if anyone had any advice.

We are already going to brunch at Cup Diner as I think they have a wonderful brunch then work our way north towards Ditmars. 

Does anyone out there have and suggestions for places we should casually pass by to give them a quick taste of the neighbourhood?  So far I have thought of parts of Steinway/Broadway/walk by Kaufman/The beer garden/Astoria Park.

Any other suggestions?

You've gotta eat somewhere.  If you hit Museum of Moving Image today (and if you happened to see Walking Tour of Queens) walk over to S'agapo www.queenstribune.com/archives/diningguide/reviews/sagapo.htm.  While there, chat about what you've read here, and the things you need to make life manageable and yes, even pleasant.  Notice the Bravo supermarket across the street.  Notice the 2 schools (Public school, private charter school) around the Kaufman center.  If you head to Steinway St we apologize for the congestion and the intemperate folks you'll encounter but as a shopping strip it serves it's purpose.  And apologies also for the too-small Library at Steinway and Broadway.

But I hope you'll come with the mindset that along with what attracts you here will also be the need to build what will Keep you here - libraries that match the density of folk that do/will live here. It's like a plant that needs pruning and care, and it will return a crop.  It has for others.
