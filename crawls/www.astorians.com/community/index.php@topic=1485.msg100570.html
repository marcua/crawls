One website says it works, but it takes weeks. Another site says it takes a few hours. When I had bedbugs I didn't try the freezing thing, just the boiling thing and threw out half my furniture.

It took me more than 6 months to completely get rid of them because the poison the exterminators use (at least this was the case last year, when I had them) doesn't kill the eggs, only the hatched bugs and adults, so he had to come back and spray every single week to kill all the new hatchlings and get them before they bred. What a nightmare!

I found a product online, can't remember the name of it, but it comes in a white bottle that looks like bleach (it's a powder) and is EXTREMELY toxic to humans and animals. I have 3 cats and was afraid to use it. But I lined the baseboards in an area the cats couldn't get to, and I really feel it was much more effective than the liquid spray the exterminator used. And his spray got over EVERYTHING. He assured me it wasn't toxic, but come on. How could it not be?? Nothing kills those rotten bugs.

Another site I visited said they used boric acid, thyme and tea tree oil and it seemed to work. Other people agreed thyme was affective for them as well, seemed to repel the bugs.

Hi MarisaL,

From what I have read, they are not able to jump.  They just crawl.  They get into beds by climbing up the legs of the bed or up the wall beside it.  This is also why glue traps under the legs of the bed can cut off their access point.  The ones that I saw in my own place were very slow and would only crawl along slowly after being poked and pushed.

we had a huge bedbug infestation and beat them! it took a long time, but it was worth it.  the best advice i can give is lots of sticking things in the dryer at high heat, doing laundry almost every day, storing all clothes and things in plastic bags until the infestation dies down, vacuuming every single day - floors, bed, couches, etc. - and covering mattresses with a plastic cover. oh, and exterminators. we also used a heavy duty bug killer from the hardware store called "intruder"...not sure if it works, but sometimes it just feels good to bathe your home in chemicals.  even then, it took a while for them to be completely gone. you should always consider it good news if the problem is getting better, not worse - it can take time, but this way you know the treatment is working. good luck everyone!
