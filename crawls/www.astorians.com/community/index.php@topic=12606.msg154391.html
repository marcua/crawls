When I adopted my second cat, I went to Animal Care and Control: http://www.nycacc.org.  This is the municipal shelter, which is required to take in every animal that someone brings in, so they do euthanize animals.  It was important to me to be saving a life as well as finding my new buddy.  It's probably also the most cost-effective way to adopt, unless you get a "free kitty" from craigslist or something.  I went to their Manhattan shelter, which is in a slightly scary neighborhood but nobody bothered me.  There are usually volunteers there on weekends to help out adoptors. 

Some friends of mine found their perfect kitty at the North Shore Animal League, which I believe is in Nassau County, so I don't know if traveling there is a possibility for you.  According to their website though, they're waiving adoption fees for cats in October:  http://www.nsalamerica.org/.  They are a no-kill organization, and my friends had a very positive experience there.

I adopted my kitten through Pet Finder (http://petfinder.com/). They have all ages of cats (I had to get a kitten to get along with my existing 2 yr cat to avoid total confrontation).

Quote
What made you decide to go with a kitten? Our cat has lived with other cats, but they've always been a lot younger than him.

We already had one cat who was found on the street and went through a few families, and spent time as an outdoor cat, before we took her in when she was two. She had been a solo cat her whole life and was independent, but lonely when we left during the day (she was always waiting at the door when we came home and circle us and stick by us all night). We decided to get a second cat for her to play with.

We went to a few shelters and spoke to people and most everyone recommended we get a kitten. We had an adult female at home and people told us females are more territorial and dominant. A kitten could acclimate to this more easily and the older cat could dominate (or become motherly) to a kitten more easily.

We were also told to get a male. Again b/c of the territorial thing and b/c male cats are more often less dominant. Basically they said combos of male-male or male-female (as long as they're fixed) work best.

From Pet Finders I got our kitten from Zani's Furry Friends: http://www.petfinder.com/shelters/NY606.html
