Title: baby/kid friendly restaurants etc in Astoria
Post by: sarahespanol on June 14, 2008, 09:58:31 PM
Can we make a list?  I don't want this to turn into a "b--- fest" about places that are NOT friendly to people with babies/kids, but I'm sure that we'd all like to know if there are places here that are in any way "welcoming" to people with smaller people in tow (strollers, high chairs, menu choices, etc). 

Parents with tiny babies:  Take your baby, and yourself, out while your child is still so portable in his/her cute little car seat!  Go on, treat yourself!  Go someplace a little nicer than those places that I mention in my list --- if your baby is asleep for most of the meal, who's gonna care?  If I want to go some place nice with my 2 kids (4 and 1 year-olds), I have to get TAKE OUT or get a baby sitter. 

I'll start with what I know (I live in the astoria/lic part of the hood):

CUP -- 35th ave/36th street --- it is a big space, has diner food, can accomodate strollers (and are pretty nice about it), high chairs, and will welcome big groups of parents and kids.

Cafe/Bar --- 34th ave/36th street --- if you go "early," they are incredibly accomodating to groups -- they don't seem to mind kids and babies, and we parked the strollers outside.  We used our laps to hold the kids....don't remember them having highchairs (they are a bar, didn't really think that they would)

Restau-Rant ---- 35th ave/30th st --- we've gone there at 4 in the afternoon when they first open.  They were really great to us....
didn't have highchairs (as it is really a bar, did not expect them to)

Igloo --- 31st st/ditmars --- very kid/baby friendly, they are used to big groups of people with strollers (you just need to make sure that the stroller is out of the aisle so they can get through), the back space is great for groups and usually less crowded, they will make kid plates, etc, have high chairs.....

Brick--- 31st Ave/33rd st--- They had a high chair when I last went (but I kept my child on my lap), but the last time I passed by there, I saw a kid in her stroller (but maybe she wanted to be there).  They have that nice outdoor area....the food is great (not really "kid" food, though)........I called first to ask about kids, and they said that children were welcome......

what else?

Title: Re: baby/kid friendly restaurants etc in Astoria
Post by: Newtownian on June 27, 2008, 12:16:22 PM
Tacos Mexico 38th and Broadway. - You can order a rice an bean roll up and they have good fruit shakes for the kiddies. There is no changing table. But time it right and you'll be in and out of there before you know it.

Lite Bites Steinway and Broadway. It's tight and a little cramped but they have highchairs and it's a good spot for silver dollar pancakes! Leave the stroller at home or fold it down.

Panera Bread by the Movie Theater - Not really a restaurant but a good place for lunch none the less. Large, accomodating tables are easy to find. The Bathrooms are clean and well air conditioned, and both have baby changing stations.

In the hit or miss category, i'll have to say Fatty's. Waiting for the food to arrive can be a harrowing experience with the little one. Either bring lots of entertainment or eat in the backyard. If the stars align just right and the food comes before the next administration, the you should be okay. No changing table, and tight spaces for strollers inside.

Good luck!
