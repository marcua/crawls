As it is mid-may, i'm assuming all the teachers & profs who posted and discussed in such heartfelt and eloquent words on this thread are now buried in either grading final papers and exams or prepping kids for Regents, other manners of standardized tests and losing their patience with kids/colleagues/principals/DOE bueacuracy/ random people on the street??? one of my colleagues flipped out the other day because he couldn't find a stapler and was convinced the teachers were plotting against his organizational skills ... we're all a bit testy these days ....

i work in a NYC public school - somewhere between administration and subject management ... it's a bizarre job I have... and I also teach a grad class at NYU, so between those two worlds i'm pretty mired in politics, bureacracy, red-tape and utterly fascinating conversations.  I taught at the university level for a few years while doing my PhD and after; my research is with teenagers, identity development and media education.   i find the kids fascinating - they are the most rewarding part of my day, even when they're going totally batty. i've learned so much about family, friendship, commitment, intelligence, trust, struggle, honesty and confidence from my students.   

but yes, i'm counting the days till june 27 ... with a lovely 3-day rest for memorial day.

i don't know if you folks got together for drinks when it was first proposed, but if not (or if people want to get together again), i propose an outing, sooner rather than later - maybe memorial day wknd if people aren't escaping the city?   or any early evening for that matter...
