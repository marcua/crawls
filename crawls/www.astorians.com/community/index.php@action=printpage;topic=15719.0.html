Title: Re: 16 more days and we are ASTORIANS!!!!!!! Any advise?
Post by: J Roscoe on July 31, 2009, 12:04:11 PM
I'm a newbie to Astoria, but I'll offer my impressions/feelings so far.

We moved here on July 1st, and although it's only been a month, I really love the area.  DO enjoy Astoria Park as much as possible.  The view of Manhattan from the Park is great.  There's a track for running laps, basketball, pool, and plenty of open space for picnics, sun-bathing, relaxing, etc.

Also, if you're use to shopping a big department stores or chain supermarkets... then you should get use to a more local approach.  The local markets here seem to have some much more varied choices in items and mostly cheaper prices when comparing them to the big chains.  Especially if you like fruits and veggies... the local produce stands are usually very good quality at a much cheaper price.  There are plenty of "ethnic" markets too, that carry a variety of foods/items from parts of the world that are fun to tryout.  Of course, you can always shop in Manhattan, but sometimes spotting a hard-to-find or special item on the backwall of a local shop is much more exciting.

Title: Re: 16 more days and we are ASTORIANS!!!!!!! Any advise?
Post by: violette4774 on August 09, 2009, 08:22:49 PM
WELCOME TO ASTORIA! :-D
My boyfriend and I moved from Florida too (Tampa) last July and we LOVE it here! We moved for my internship so we were in the same boat- we had to go. The locals really are quite nice for the most part.  The eats are also excellent and it a safe place to live (again for the most part). 
Generally:
-I agree with the granny cart, for sure!
-A costco membership will likely end up saving you money if you can get to the location in Long Island City.
-Also right next to Costco is the Socrates Sculpture Park, where they have free outdoor movies in the summer.  That's a blast.
Websites:
-You might try going to Whyleaveastoria.org for local events and to get a perks card. This website is a great asset as well.  I've learned so much from it and have gotten excellent recommendations on where to live, what to eat, who's/what's recommended as far as services in the area.  Also, Yelp.com is also good.
Shopping:
-Ditmars Ave is a cozy place to walk and shop.  They have independent businesses mixed in with some of the bigger ones. 
-If you are itching for a “real” mall, Queens Center Mall is in Elmhurst, it's excellent.
Winters:
-Get a winter coat, like a real-deal winter coat…preferably goose-down (Northface has some excellent options, trust me it’s worth every penny.) Also, get warm gloves, a hat and a scarf.  I also agree that snow/rainboots are a necessity. 
Florida vs. NY:
-The two are very hard to compare.  In some ways, I imagine that Miami is similar in that it is made of up of many different cultures/languages…but NYC is like, 12 X as diverse!  So take advantage of that (i.e.: learn about and eat food from different cultures!)  The atmosphere is also obviously different – I remember coming here and thinking – it’s so hilly! Florida is so flat!  Space is also at a premium, especially in Manhattan, so don’t be overwhelmed by the incredible amount of people and tall buldings. 

I wish you guys the very best!  Best of luck with the move.  Hopefully you will enjoys being “Astoria Transplants.”
