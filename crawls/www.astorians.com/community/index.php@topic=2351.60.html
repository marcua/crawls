I know from talking with the offices that they'd like to hear what we're proposing moreso than being asked to attend an additional meeting where they might be expected to formulate our gameplan for us.
I haven't, and don't, discourage calls to any of the offices - the more we can generate the better, e-mails doubly so as they don't tie up phone lines.   I do, however, think that we should know what we want them to do (aside from what they're already doing which is putting pressure on the 114th Precinct) before we pull elected officials, or their representatives, into a meeting.   I am sure they, or their staff, are reading the website, at least I hope they are! 
I know when I went to meetings (in my former capacity as Chief of Staff to a Councilmember) of community activists who didn't know what they wanted, it was often somewhat frustrating as people hadn't always thought things through ahead of time.  I usually found that when they'd gotten together before bringing me in, and had some ideas of what they wanted, I was able to help them achieve it. 
From my reading of the website and recollection, I think the purpose of this meeting was to 1 - recap what happened near the site, 2 - discuss the precinct council meeting and how precinct councils operated and 3 - to come up with a game plan for that meeting (and possibly 4 - to hear what happened at the LICA meeting taking place tonight, as well as 5 - to figure out who else to reach out to.
