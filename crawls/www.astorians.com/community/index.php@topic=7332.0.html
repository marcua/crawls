Rather than add this onto the previous post about Chinese food in Astoria, I was wondering if anyone else noticed that Hong Kong on Broadway off Crescent was closed by the Health Department recently?
On July 23rd, when the place was inspected, the following results were found (from the NYC Health Dept. website):
1.) Accurate thermometer not provided in refrigerated or hot holding equipment.
2.) Lighting inadequate. Bulb not shielded or shatterproof.
3.) Facility not vermin proof. Harborage or conditions conducive to vermin exist.
4.) Personal cleanliness inadequate. Clean outer garments, effective hair restraint not worn.
5.) Evidence of roaches or live roaches present in facility's food and/or non-food areas.
6.) Evidence of mice or live mice present in facility's food and/or non-food areas.
However, an inspection two days later (presumably after the restaurant was closed), found no violations and 0 points.
I remember eating in this restaurant in the mid-1980s (when it was called Wah Yuan) and seeing a mouse in the flour bags stacked up in the back of the restaurant when I went to use the bathroom there.  After not eating there for many years, I started again several years ago.  Despite the apparent Health Dept. violations, I still would eat there and think the food is one of the best in our neighborhood.
