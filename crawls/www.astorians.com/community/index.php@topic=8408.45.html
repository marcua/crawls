I understand the money thing - I really do.  And that's why the chains are so evil!  They offer such cheap meals because (a) they use subpar ingredients (actually, there are no ingredients, just pre-made factory manufactured pseudo-food), (b) they receive tax breaks - HUGE corporations are able to get all sorts of financial incentives because they have a legal team that specializes in this, like subsidies for "employee training", and (c) of the nature of their size they have more buying power and can get everything more cheaply.

Mom and pops CANNOT compete in that environment.  Our dollars mean something - and I understand that the majority of this country doesn't have enough of those dollars to make these kinds of decisions.  I don't know what the answer is in a broader sense, but I do know that Astoria has many inexpensive food options (for now) that really make spending those dollars on big corporations unnecessary.

Does this mean that I NEVER shop at chains?  No - when I'm in the suburbs or any area I don't know I first go to local places IF I CAN FIND THEM.  I have been in many places where there are ONLY chain restaurants and then I have no choice and I make the best of it.  To be fair I will say I have had a very good meal at PF Changs - and I would recommend them in that genre.  But when I know I will be on the Thruway, for example, which has only chains at their reststops, I try to pick up a few maki at Go Wasabi or whatever and bring my food on the road. 

I guess, like Para, I just don't get the resistance to try new things - I have no idea what brand of TP I have in the house - I really don't care.  I don't like Scotts so I don't buy that - but I'll try whatever else is on sale.  I try new things all the time, I may find a brand I like but this doesn't preclude me from trying something else.  Life is short and there's so much out there to explore!  If I stick to the same ol' same ol' I might miss something.
