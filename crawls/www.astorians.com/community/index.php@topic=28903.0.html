There is a post today on Shorpy, a vintage pictorial site, of a photo from 1949 of the block on Broadway that housed the Strand Theater and the nearby A&P. I remember the Strand, I had been there a few times, and the bowling alley. I also remember the A&P, which closed and became another supermarket which I can't recall. There are several mothers with baby buggies. It's quite a trip back in time.

Wow, thanks for that link.  What an amazing picture with so much detail.  As far back as I can remember (1980s), there was a Pioneer in the spot where the A&P is.  That supermarket closed its doors suddenly, leaving all the food inside, and when it was finally cleaned out, it had been taken over by thousands of mice/rats.  All of the stores on the block got infested.  Not sure if there was a supermarket in between A&P and Pioneer.  I believe Pioneer was the last supermarket there and then it became a junk store (current name is Olympus).

As for the other stores in that picture, I definitely remember the "five and dime" G.T. Lawe, but remember it a lot larger.  I believe it eventually expanded to include some of the smaller stores adjacent to it.  That eventually became Blockbuster Video, which is now the Strand beer garden.

I don't recall Albert's or Richard's or the store to the right of the movie theater with the awning -- can anyone make out what kind of stores they are from the picture?  And I don't recall the Strand Bakery to the right of the A&P.

I believe I have asked this on here before but I'll ask again -- does anyone know if there are any remnants of the movie theater left aside from the roof line?  There is a discount clothing store in the space currently, but there was previously a furniture store there in the 1980s, and I distinctly recall running around in the back of the furniture store as a kid in what I recall to be a movie theater or at least remnants of a movie theater.

And where does the "Strand" name come from on all of these establishments?
