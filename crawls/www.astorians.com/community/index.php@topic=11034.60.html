Para,

Like everyone else, you and Tim have my sympathies and support and if you need anything - a relaxing doggie playdate? - let me know.

I strongly object to the post criticizing Tim for not being "the hero type." Rather than offer a long diatribe against the "hero" mentality and how little it may actually accomplish in the service of the common good, let me say that it is widely documented in analyses of actual data on street robbery that physically resisting a street robber significantly increases the likelihood that the victim will be injured. Screaming and yelling, however, are always great strategies, even the tried and true yelling "fire" can interrupt the plans of even the sloppiest street robber.

I am very glad to hear you were not physically injured, but I well remember the terrible feeling of violation after being mugged so go easy on yourself!

I am happy to see that people who think in "heroic" terms are the minority in our neighborhood.

One more thing... in the movement for restorative justice many crime victim services have been implemented around the country, and New York is no exception; in fact, it may have been a leader.

I am unclear as to whether crime victim compensation is intended only for people injured during a crime (or for specific populations like children and elderly, regardless of injury), but everyone should know about the NYS Crime Victims Board:

http://www.cvb.state.ny.us/home.aspx

They provide compensation (up to $500 for essential personal property and up to $100 cash) for innocent crime victims in NYS.

Paratactical, I'm so sorry this happened to you and your boyfriend, and I'm glad you weren't hurt.  I know from experience how violating an attack can be, especially so close to your home (my mugging was in another city).
In regards to "fighting back", let me relay to you my own story...
A friend of mine and I were walking back to her car in a nice neighborhood of another city, and were grabbed from behind (well, she was grabbed- she is only 4'10" and went down easy- her "guy" grabbed her bag and was off).  My attacker jumped on my back and knocked me to the ground.  I guess "fight or flight" kicked in, and man- I fought like hell- kicking and screaming.  Beleive me, i think I am an *idiot* for doing this, and I'm really quite surprised I even had it in me.  It was a bad idea.  He kicked the crap out of me (kicked my legs so hard my shoes flew off my feet all the way across the street) and took out his knife and cut my bag right off me.  I still have the strap.  I spent the night in the ER for cuts and bruises- thank God he only used his knife on my bag.
My point is that it's just not worth it.  things are replaceable!  I don't miss my cash and credit cards, but I lost more than that that night because I "fought back".
Be well, and I hope they find your attackers, for all of our sake.
