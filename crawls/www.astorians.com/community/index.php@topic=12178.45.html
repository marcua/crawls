Re: DTV converter box - no PBS?
« Reply #47 on: January 10, 2009, 08:15:44 PM »
Yes, as was posted on the other thread, Thirteen has been operating its digital signal at reduced power, primarily to save money as they don't have the financial resources that the major networks do.  They will switch to full power when the analog signal is shut off (which means one less electric bill for them to pay!).

Something else that should be known about the switchover is that channels 7, 11 and 13 are going to be changing frequencies when the switchover takes place.  I'll attempt to illustrate this as simply as possible:

Right now, the DTV channels are operating on frequencies (channel numbers) other than the ones we are used to.  For instance, Channel 2's digital frequency is 56, Channel 4's is 28, etc.  Your DTV tuner, however, "maps" these stations back to their familiar locations, so when you press "2", "4", etc. on your remote, you see what you expect to see.

7, 11 and 13 have been operating their DTV signals in the UHF (14-69) band as well, but once the switchover takes place and the analog signal turns off, they have chosen to move back to the 7, 11 and 13 frequencies.  Also, Channel 2's DTV is going to move from 56 to 33, since, after the transition, the TV frequencies above 51 will begin to be used by other services (cell phones, wireless internet services, etc.).

What this means is that your DTV tuner may need to be "retuned" to find these stations again.  If, come February 17th, you have a DTV tuner or converter box and you are not receiving 2, 7, 11 or 13 anymore, that is going to be why.  Retuning the box is as simple as going into the menu and selecting the option to run a scan again.

Just one more thing to add to the confusion of this transition!

I've been using a digital converter since September. The channels that you can get are: 2,4,5,7,9,11 and some few others that I never watch. The quality is great, whenever there's signal. The reason why I don't always have signal, is that I'm not using a real antenna, but just a piece of wire, sticking out of the antenna port of the receiver. It works very well most of the time, but I'm sure that with a real antenna it would work perfectly all of the time.

By the way, the antenna is exactly the same one that you're using to get analog TV, it's just that you need a converter box between it and the TV.

Re: converter boxes
« Reply #51 on: February 07, 2009, 10:44:58 AM »
We've had the digital converter box for about a month and still use our old analog rabbit ears. We get

2 - CBS
4.1 - NBC
4.2 - A weather-channel style (all weather all the time thing from NBC)
4.4 - Universal sports (which basically has nonstop downhill skiing)

Two copies of FOX and My9 (5 and 9)

7 - ABC
7.2 - same weather-channel style thing only by ABC
7.3 - what seems to be an all infomercial channel

11 - PIX (old WB)

sometimes we get 25 - (local NYC channel)
25.2 - a live feed of various traffic shots

31 - ION (sometimes)
31.2 - QUBO (kid's channel - rarely comes in)
31.2 - ION Life (looks like a cheap knock off of "Fine Living", again rarely comes in)

a few Spanish channels and a 'Worship' channel.

We no longer get Thirteen/PBS for reasons people have cited above. I really hope they don't wait until June to transition too.  :oops:

Our reception has been spotty. We get frequent freezing and pixelation, which may be because we have a ground level apartment and no rooftop antenna. Our rabbit ears are actually hanging from the wall because this is where they get the best reception. Unfortunately, we've noticed that whenever a train goes by, we suddenly get terrible reception. I finally realized that the train was shaking our building slightly (we are 1 block away from the 36th Ave stop on the N/W). This shaking made the rabbit ears shake as well and totally screwed with our reception. We had no idea how many trains came through during rush hour until this started happening.

So it's been a mixed bag for us. The quality of the channels is clearer, but when they freeze or get garbled it can be incredibly annoying. I don't know if getting a digital antenna will help us, but I'm going to look into it.
