Quote from: merm on February 18, 2008, 08:47:23 PM
Adam. While I appreciate the feedback I consider this case closed for now. Moderation overall is highly subjective - and as you probably know rules regarding appropriate conversation is a difficult thing to quantify - and are difficult to enforce.
Merm. Moderation may be highly subjective, but the word has a specific and clear meaning.

"Moderation n. - Quality of being moderate and avoiding extremes
Moderate v. -
1.  To lessen the violence, severity, or extremeness of.
v.intr.
1. To become less violent, severe, or extreme; abate."

When your first response to an intense conversation is (1) to lock the thread and (2) to ban people and (3) to threaten the discontinuation of an entire topic area, and (4) to repeatedly characterize "politics itself" as a problem, you're doing the opposite of "lessening severity" or "avoiding extremes." 

It's also intimidating that you use the phrase "friendly reminder" simultaneously with a thread locking. And it's confusing that you prefer the word "discussion" to the word "debate," but then ban people in place of encouraging participants in a heated exchange to *discuss* their differences precisely and constructively.

Nobody anywhere on this site would ever have known or cared about the exchange between Billz, Debbie, and paratactical. Left alone, it wasn't going to spill over into other areas. You made this a site-wide issue because of the nature of your intervention.

Imagine if you hadn't locked the thread or banned anybody. Imagine instead that you or another moderator simply posted only the very first part of your first sentence in your first response to what was happening, (i.e., "A friendly reminder that when the conversation gets personal that I start suspending people.").

That alone would have encouraged a peaceful outcome to that exchange. There'd be no principled stands being taken against you, no annoyed people complaining to you. Debbie and Billz would have either agreed to disagree and moved on or reached a new level of understanding about each others' perspectives. And today, the Politics thread wouldn't seem like Siberia with an intimidating WARNING sign in front of it.

In this case, you really could have pleased "everybody." That is, the involved parties (very small group) would have respected and benefited from a soft touch. Because it would have been a moderate and constructive response that consumed less of your time... everybody's time and energy. 

Merm, this isn't merely venting. It's a pointed constructive criticism of your moderating approach, its upsetting and chilling effect, as well as how you come across by engaging in it.

I wouldn't keep after you about this if I weren't generally happy about what Astorians.com offers and, at the same time, genuinely convinced that you believe there was nothing wrong with or even contradictory about your retaliatory approach to moderation in this instance.

The owner of a property has the legal right to do what he wants with it, but morality applies to an owner's actions as they affect people on his property, too. On a property that thrives based 100% on user opinion sharing, thread-locking and user-banning should be undertaken as last resorts in extreme cases.

-Adam

Quote from: Ditmartian on February 21, 2008, 01:38:33 AM
