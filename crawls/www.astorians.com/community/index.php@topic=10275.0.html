The majority of the interaction I've had with police from the 114th is when I've had to cross-examine them on the witness stand or have subpoenaed them and brought them in to testify. I've met a couple who have been really nice guys, others who were totally rude. The one remarkable thing I've noticed is that many of them have no clue about the layout of the neighborhood. I was once questioning a Sgt. on the stand (not even a new beat officer!) and he had no clue that it's the Triboro Bridge that runs over the park, no clue what the Hellgate even was, and he thought Ditmars Blvd. was the next block up from Astoria Blvd.

They're also pretty lazy. Somewhere out there is a 15 year old kid with a gun who tried to rob a Chinese food delivery man. My client got arrested, though he was just standing there watching and he gave the name of the actual perp to police and the prosecutor, but the cops never followed up. So that kid is out there, while my kid got locked up, we took the case to trial, and I ultimately won. But that other kid... he's still on the loose. Thank the 114th for that.

I've had two experiences with calling the police in the past twelve or so years - once when my car was broken into, and once when I thought the business next door to me was being burglarized.
In the first case, the two officers arrived soon after I called, and were polite as they took down the information. The only thing that put me off a little was having one of them suggest to me that I get rid of my car if I didn't want to worry about it getting broken into again. That was NOT something I wanted to hear from a policeman.
In the second case, the police responded quickly, and were very polite to me as they checked out the situation. As it turned out, there was no burglary in progress. A piece of machinery had been left on in the business overnight and malfunctioned, making loud banging noises as if someone were in there with a sledge hammer trying to break into a safe or something.

So, with the limited experience I've had with the police around here [knock wood], I rate them as being OK.
