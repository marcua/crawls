Ack! The site just erased my post! This will be a truncated version of what I'd already typed...I can elaborate more if you're interested.

My husband and I had an Astoria wedding in December 2006. We tried to keep as much of it local as possible; I can't vouch for how 'green' everything is, but it was a really awesome wedding and I can recommend everyone we used very highly.

Venue: Mezzo-Mezzo on Ditmars and 33rd St.
We had 100 guests, and they fit snugly into Mezzo's upstairs space, but the atmosphere was unique and fun. You pay for the food and drinks, no separate charge for use of space (unless you want to reserve the entire restaurant). The food was terrific. We hired a friend to play iPod DJ and he set up on their little stage; the dance area was small but always felt packed as a result. The managers worked very closely with us to make sure we were happy and accomodated. The only downsides were, they have a small bathroom and it's downstairs, which might be hard for people who have trouble with stairs; and they don't have a removed space for getting ready and things like that. Otherwise, it was exactly what we wanted.

Ceremony: Performed on site by Jeddah Vailakis: http://interfaith-ceremonies.com/
She's not located in Astoria, but was happy to come here!

Flowers: FTD Florist on Ditmars and 31st St., near Eckerd
I wanted something really unusual for my bouquet, and they pulled it off perfectly. We went pretty modest and simple with the rest of our flowers, but the quality was good and having them right down the street from the venue put my mind at ease.

Cake: Martha's Country Bakery, Ditmars and 37th St.
They don't do wedding cakes per se, but we wanted a strawberry shorcake and that worked out fine. This would be a good place to go for a cupcake wedding, if that's your style!

Photographs: We hired a friend who lived in the area, but has sadly moved to Jersey!

Hair, make-up, etc: SPAce Salon and Spa, Ditmars and 29th St.
Friendly and affordable.

Everything else we either made ourselves, or had friends help us with. We're a pretty DIY couple to begin with, so it was a great wedding in our opinions! Our guests all raved it was one of the funnest they'd ever attended, so I guess we did something right!  :-D


I'll add that my sister-in-law got married at The Foundry in Long Island City. A MUCH classier joint than most weddings I've been to are held at. The Foundry is pricey, and a raw space you can do whatever you want with. Totally New York, and really, really stunning.

At our wedding 3 yrs ago, we decided that rather than a wedding cake & flower arrangements on the tables, we went with cupcakes on tiered platters as the table centerpiece. Wife and I loved it and all the kids really loved it.  Had been trying to save some money and this became a good and unique solution. We wanted to make some parts of the wedding our own.

Also put ads on Craigs list for band and photographer. Got both that way.  Loved the band, A new Orleans style swing blues band.  The photographer was billed as a photojournalist style rather than typical wedding photographer (which we did not want).  He did a very good job but the truth was at the end of the day we really would have liked some standard pics to fill in all the great candid in motion stuff.  We discovered that there is a reason for some of the tried and true methods.

We stuck to our guns also about great creative food and a non tacky looking place.  We originally wanted to do something on our own at a cool site and bring everything in. Once I ran the numbers I saw why a catering hall has appeal. They have the site, rentals, bar and food in one place so they have a good deal.  Not for everyone but we found a good fit (with family help) and we loved every bit of the day.  Big hint is to be flexible with the date.  We could only afford the place because they offered us a huge deal on a Sunday afternoon, Mothers day.  If you do this be sure to make a big speech at the wedding about mom's and how much we all love 'em.

The only big thing we would have changed was the sit down meal. Would have canceled it and extended the cocktail and apps portion. The food is always better it that part and the whole thing is more informal.

Good luck with your upcoming wedding and congratulations.
