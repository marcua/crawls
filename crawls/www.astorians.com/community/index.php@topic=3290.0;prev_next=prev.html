Nassau County Executive Tom Suozzi, who has, if nothing else during this campaign, demonstrated his penchant for breaking into song, does his best Rat Pack imitation in this video (posted on his campaign Web site).
Suozzi has seemed resigned in recent days to the inevitability of his defeat this evening by gubernatorial frontrunner/AG Eliot Spitzer. On Sunday, Suozzi penned the thank-you missive to supporters that accompanies this video, calling his outsider campaign “a lonely task.”
“While many electeds have privately said that they agree with me - I have been ostracized by my own party. That is why the intense support from so many individuals who hear my message for what it is - an effort to help people - has been so rewarding. Thank you.”

There will likely be no lack of Monday morning quarterbacking by news pundits this evening and tomorrow - and perhaps even beyond - regarding the Suozzi campaign’s fatal flaws. The New York Sun gets a leg up this morning, chastizing Suozzi for keeping such a thin campaign schedule and, in particular, passing up the potential for lots of free media upstate, where there is much political unrest.

Suozzi has said in the past that he’ll be through with politics after this loss, but the conventional wisdom is he has a future in New York. His greatest fault was being a good candidate with a horrible sense of timing.

Don't know if everyone saw the results, but Spitzer won big, as did Clinton and Cuomo (20 points on Mark Green).  I was at the Sabini campaign in Jackson Heights most of the day and it was a hard fought race, but we won, 51-49, so Senator Sabini will serve for at least another two years.  Senator Ada Smith, an embarrassment to the Democratic Party and the State Senate was defeated in her primary.  Councilmember Yvette Clarke won her race for Congress and quite possibly the smartest guy I ever knew in government, Brian Kavanagh ousted Assemblymember Sylvia Friedman in the Eastside of Manhattan.  I have to admit, I am absolutely delighted with these primary results.  I just hope the general election results are as good.
