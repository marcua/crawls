Title: Fitness
Post by: Adampowfitness on July 16, 2012, 08:39:03 PM
Join me for NY's most rewarding boot camp.  This 60-minute boot camp offers a wide variety of fun and challenging exercises to tone and strengthen your body. Plus, for every boot camp you attend, a portion of your class fee will be donated to the Feed My Starving Children Foundation which helps eradicate child hunger worldwide.  This boot camp will make you feel great on the inside and look great on the outside![/size]
When and Where:[/size]
Astoria Park Track Field, Mondays and Wednesdays, starting July 16th at 7:00 pm
We will meet at south side bleachers.  Sign in will start at 6:50 pm and class will start at 7:00pm.
Fee:
$10 per 1-hour boot camp
(Cash only, please try to have exact change)
What You'll Need:Water, Towel, Yoga Mat, and a Positive Attitude! [/size]
What to Expect:
Expect to have fun!  Class will start with dynamic stretching.  Dynamic stretching warms up the body's muscles, joints, and core temperature in preparation for the class.  The workout will consist of exercises that use body weight, resistance bands, resistance tubing, agility ladders, jump ropes, agility cones, and even resistance parachutes!  All equipment used during the class will be provided.  All exercises can be modified to fit your individual fitness level and to ensure that you get a great workout.  After the workout, class will end with a relaxing cool down and stretch. [/size]
Cancellation:
Class may be canceled because of bad weather.  Rain, thunder and lighting, or extreme heat may led to a class cancellation.  Notice of cancellation will be issued through Twitter.  Please follow me on Twitter at AdamPowFitness for the latest updates regarding possible class cancellations.
[/size]
About the Charity:
Feed My Starving Children is a non-profit organization committed to feeding hungry children. The approach is simple: children and adults hand-pack meals specifically formulated for malnourished children, and ship the meals to nearly 70 countries around the world.
