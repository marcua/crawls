KateNW & lovee01 -

I just wanted to mention that you don't need a broker to see the building and in fact they posted an offer on the residents website giving a $500 gift card to residents for any referrals that actually sign a lease BUT the referral must come in on their own (no broker). I guess they figure this saves them money in the long run since they pay the brokers fee $500 is definitely cheaper than a brokers fee. ALSO - Just 'cause AstoriaNW is paying the broker a fee doesn't mean your broker can't still try to charge you a fee and get double payment on this deal so be wary of those kinds of brokers.

We moved in at the end of October (new to New York & Queens) and we also love the building and location. Feels safe, comfortable, close to Astoria Park, about a mile from Costco, tons of places to eat, and the staff is all great. I specifically LOVE that the building uses the online BuildingLink site to connect residents, post messages and of course that there is an iphone app so you can always see if you've received a package, submit a repair request, leave front desk instructions, etc right on your phone. One of my favorite parts about the location is that we are the 2nd to last stop on the N/Q so you stand a pretty good chance of getting a seat on the train since only Ditmars people board before us heading into Manhattan.

KateNW hope to bump into you in the halls one day and Lovee01 good luck in your search!
