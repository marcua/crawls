This is my favorite EASY AND DELICIOUS salmon recipe.

Salmon en Papillote

Ingredients:
4 salmon fillets (about 4-ounces each)
4 medium Italian plum tomatoes, OR about 2 cups diced, seeded tomatoes
olive oil
1 tsp bottled chopped garlic, OR 2 medium cloves of garlic, finely chopped
Kosher salt
crushed red pepper flakes
12 fresh basil leaves
2 lemons
1/4 cup dry white wine (regular or non-alcoholic)
Aluminum foil OR Parchment paper

Preparation:
Preheat oven to 375°F.
Rinse fish and pat dry with paper towels.
Core tomatoes; slice about 1/4-inch thick.

Tear four sheets of aluminum foil or parchment paper, each about 12" X 10". Fold each sheet in half, then open again. With a paper towel, lightly coat their surface with olive oil.

To assemble bundles, place a fillet, skin side down, very close to the right hand side of the fold of each sheet. Sprinkle/spread each fillet with garlic, then sprinkle with a pinch of Kosher salt and a small pinch of the crushed red pepper. Add basil and tomatoes. Pour 1 tablespoon wine over each serving. Squeeze juice from 1/2 lemon over each serving. Place squeezed half lemon in packet. Fold foil/parchment over fish; tightly fold up all three sides to enclose fish.

Place bundles on a flat baking sheet and bake for about 15 minutes, or until the fish flakes easily when tested with a fork.

To serve, carefully unfold one side of foil (being careful not get a steam burn), and slide contents onto plate.

Makes 4 Servings. This recipe can be adjusted for any amount of servings, including just one.
