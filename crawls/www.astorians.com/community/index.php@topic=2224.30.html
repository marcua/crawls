I was shocked when I heard that Ken Lay had died. (Sounds like it was not a suicide based upon current news reports, but you never know. As megc has said we'll find out when the autopsy results are in.) No matter who it is who dies there is a part of me that is always saddened as well. Death, even when it happens to evil or corrupted people, is not necessarily a positive thing. Some people may rejoice in his death (or the death of Al-Zarqawi, for example), but I personally can't. I think that ultimately this was a benefit to Lay (still don't think it was suicide though), but I am sure that a lot of people feel like "justice" was taken away from them.

In the case of a terrorist such as Al-Zarqawi I think that I feel a certain amount of relief because he won't exert his force on others, but I still feel regret and sadness. I also think that common sense tells us that there will always be another Al-Zarqawi. We can keep killing them, but they'll keep coming back. Of course, each terrorist rises to take the place of the deceased terrorist because of the complex life choices and experiences that they have. Despite our desire to stamp out their lives and serve justice they are still individuals with families and friends, and people who were probably once innocent and "good."

I don't think that when a person dies it is so black and white. People are such complex creatures and when a person like Lay dies there is a lot of complexity there as well. In my opinion the reason why their deaths are complex are because of the living. The people who are left to reflect on the influence of that person's life are the ones who carry on the complexity.

Anyway, my view of death is not that important - but yes, I was saddened when I heard about Lay. He was probably an honest and good man at one point, and I think that despite the whole Enron thing there was probably honesty and goodness still in him when he died. What percentage of him was good and honest? That I don't know.

Just my two cents. (And no I am not trying to add to the argument going on here - this is more reacting to Lay's death and to a couple of comments that I felt like responding broadly to.)
