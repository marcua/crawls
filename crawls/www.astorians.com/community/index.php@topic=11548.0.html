Welcome vivalaastoria --
As sarah says, Berry Fresh is a good option. It's on the other side of the parkway in a small shopping center, which also contains a Rite Aid and other shops (next to the former Stern's warehouse, now converted into condos -- you can't miss it)

Most of the shops for your area on on 30th avenue. There's a Key Food at 44th Street and 30th ave and a Trade Fair on 50th Street. Also you'll find one of the best Italian bakeries in the nieghborhood, Gian Piero on 45th and 30th and Italian butcher/deli across the street, Sorriso.

If you're commuting into the city on the N/W for work it may be better to get off at the 30th stop than at the Astoria Boulevard stop. It's a longer walk to your apartment but more lively given it's a busy shopping street.

For laundromats -- there are a few on 28th avenue between 46-42 streets. I think there's also another on 44th and 25th avenue.

Hey there, welcome to the neighborhood! I just moved in last month a few blocks down at 43rd and 25 ave, so perhaps I can be of assistance.

I've been shopping at C-town at 41st and 28. It's small, but fairly clean, the produce is the best we've found in a supermarket in the area. (Key foods is rather intolerable. i found moldy produce and several-months-since expired goods just yesterday, so I would avoid). For produce I like the fruit market near the 30 Ave subway stop.

I do my laundry at 44 and 25 ave. It's small, but it's not too bad. it's not spotless, but clean enough to do your laundry in and if you don't like lugging your laundry several blocks.

and to comment on the subway stop thing. The Astoria Blvd stop isn't too bad at all. No matter what time or morning or night I've walked back from the stop, there's been people out and it's well lit. Though Safety is a matter of personal security, I would recommend saving yourself the extra 10 minutes that it would to get to 30 ave. Besides, if you're not into walking from Astoria Blvd, it's not that far of a walk to the 46th/Broadway stop.

anyway, welcome. we're practically neighbors! so far I've been happy with the area and are glad we moved there.

Quote from: Liberty on August 30, 2008, 01:43:16 PM
(pilling, buttons removed or loosened, etc.)


Pilling will happen eventually to any knit garment regardless of how it is washed.

As far as buttons being removed or loosened, that also happens regularly even in delicate settings on your home dryer.

The best thing to do is to get a sewing kit and start mending buttons and use packing tape to remove the pills.

I don't think you'll find a laundromat with machines up to the standards you're looking for. Commercial washers just aren't the same.
