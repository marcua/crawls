It may have been just about 25 kids.  As far as what I would say to them, I would simply and nicely tell them to use the trash cans.  About seven years ago, when I was an active parks volunteer, I saw someone going into the park with all of his barbeque gear.  When I informed him that barbequeing was against park rules, he put down his gear, started to threaten me, and followed me all the way from 24th Avenue to Ditmars, threatening to 'cut me up', 'rape me' etc.  I did not go home, but went to a friends house and called the police.  They came and I filed something against him, although I do not recall exactly what it was.  He wound up leaving the park.  I have also had some positive experiences, a few times, I have given kids $5 each to clean up one of the fields and they were very happy.  Also, I have given out garbage bags to people having picnics and they were very receptive.  That was my one and only bad experience.

I agree that the park was cleaner than usual yesterday, and it looks pretty good today.  One thing I've noticed, there is a lady who sells some kind of food products that I do not really recognize.  It looks like mangoes on sticks, corn on sticks, some kind of snacks etc.  Yesterday, she stood on the sidewalk outside the park and sold items to people leaving the pool.  When she moved on, she left a trail of trash behind her.  If you see corn cobs strewn around the park, her customers are the source of this.  If I see her today, I will ask her to take her trash with her, although I may need an interpretor. 

As summer winds down, unless there is a heat wave, I find that the crowds thin out.  The best time of the year is right after Labor Day.  The park is delightful!

Wouldn't a fee for using the pool at least provide enough additional funds to pay for come extra garbage cans in the park? 

Not to suggest that a fee will/won't make the pool a safer place.  Just that if the budget is tight and funds are needed, wouldn't it make sense then to get these needed funds in the form of a fee from the pool?  It just makes sense.

BTW; if the Olympics would have come to NYC in and they would have fixed up the pool (as was part of the original design presented to the Olympic committe), does anyone really think that in order to maintain the newly restructured pool in good shape a usage charge wouldn't have been implemented?  Of course it would have been.  The Olympic money would have paid for the redesign for the pool for the Olympics, not the upkeep of the pool after the games left town.

I like Vallone's idea of using the diving board section of the pool for something.  It's been dormant for far too long.  I "deep" pool section (6 feet and deeper) I think would be great.  Though I'm guessing most on the board would probably opt for something else.  Hey, at least they're finally going to do something with the place !!!
