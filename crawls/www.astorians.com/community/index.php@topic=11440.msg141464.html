What a fun thread.

'Kavalier and Clay' is currently my favorite book.  I should really give it another re-read. 

I also second duska's recommendation of the "His Dark Materials" series.  It's fabulous.   Also, anything by Neil Gaiman is a winner. I especially love American Gods.

Let's see, a few of my other favorites:

- Middlesex by Jeffrey Eugenides

- Partly Cloudy Patriot and Assassination Vacation both by Sarah Vowell

- Arcadia by Stoppard (play)

- Pride and Prejudice

- The Thursday Next series by Jasper Fforde

- Stephen Sondheim: A Life by Meryle Secrest (one of my more recent reads and really mostly appeals to Sondeim/musical theatre fans, but I still thought I'd throw it out there)
- Brideshead Revisited by Waugh (a new film adaptation is coming out soon)


Like kerrigankrewe, I'm also into graphic novels/comics.  In that catagory I'd recommend:

- second kerrigankrewe's rec. for Watchmen.  It's a must for anyone who's interested the superhero genre of comics. As even the movie trailer boasts, it's the "most celebrated [superhero] graphic novel ever."

- Y:The Last Man and Ex Machina, both by Brian K. Vaughn.  'Y' is now a completed story at ten volumes worth of trades, but Ex Machina is still ongoing.  I believe it is at vol. 6 as far as trades go.

- Alias by Brian Bendis.

-  Runaways created and written by Brian K. Vaughn (first two volumes) and Joss Whedon is currently writing it.

- Skyscrapers of the Midwest by Josh W. Cotter

- Persepolis by Marjane Satrapi (was adapted into an animated film recently)

Non-Fiction

"A Splendid Exchange: How Trade Shaped The World" - William Bernstein
I think history and economics go hand in hand - you can't truly understand one without the other. This book is great. I'm almost finished with it, so if you put it on your request list a the library you can be next in line.

"Discover Your Inner Economist" - Tyler Cowen
Less historical and more psychology, but it's still good. The guys blog is a good read - marginalrevolution.com

"Undercover Economist" - Tim Hartford
Great overview for the uninitiated.

"John Adams" - Douglas McCoullough
Awesome book, one of my favorites by McCoullough.
