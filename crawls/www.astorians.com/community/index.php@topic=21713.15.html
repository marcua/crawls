Hi Everyone.

I am a 28 year old woman who works right across from the New York Stock Exchange and has also been to Zuccotti Park (a.k.a. Liberty Park) where the people are occupying and gathering for their meetings.  I think it is easy to call this some hippie kids, but it is inaccurate and a disservice to do so.  Here are some key things that are straight-up incorrect about what has been stated above, and the links to the accurate information so you can review it yourself:

1.  This is group does have focus, and there is even a list of demands specifically related to legislative measures:
http://occupywallst.org/forum/specific-demand-and-action-list-for-washington-dc/

2.  This is not a group of trust fund brats banging drums.  While those kids are present, they are showing a level of courage in being the ones to get their hands dirty (literally) in order to draw attention to this issue.  BUT, the Occupy Wall Street movement is NOT one specific group of people.  It is everyone, and that is their point.  If you go to the park, you will see all walks of life "occupying" i.e. attending the meetings in the park.  There are men in business suits, construction workers, off duty police officers (yes, you read that right and I am not lying to you), teachers, doctors, etc.  There is a huge diversity in age, ethnicity.  The refusal to have a leader or spokesperson makes them disorganized:  FALSE.  In fact, it is actually sort of brilliant. 

This article from CNN: http://edition.cnn.com/2011/10/05/opinion/rushkoff-occupy-wall-street very eloquently orients the reader as to why the movement is structured as it is, and why it is significant.  For those who do not want to read (but really should), the article discusses the fact that this is not a movement with a traditional story arch.  This is about opening the dialogue (*which clearly it has, since we're talking about it right now), and discussing what is really wrong, why, and how to fix it.  These kids want action.  Since they weren't seeing anything getting done, they decided to start doing it themselves.  They have inspired some brilliant minds to weigh in, and show up, on their behalf.  While large media is doing that thing it always does, which is sensationalize it instead of investigate and convey, these kids have taken to the internet:  they are filming and reporting for themselves, complete with nearly constant video coverage, and a newspaper.  It is infuriating how most news programs are pretending this is kids without a message.  It is not.  There are teach-ins happening, community outreach groups being formed, and a lot of organization happening very fast.  [You have to keep in mind that this is a 3 week old movement and they're trying to change American government.  This started as a one-day protest on Wall Street.  It was small and the people that gathered realized that they were amongst others who saw the same problem.  They realized the only way to fix the problem is to unite everyone in a large-scale push towards action.  It is as much about getting everyone focused, in dialogue, and informed as anything else.  Call it as much an information war as a class issue.   People need to be educated on what actually caused the problem, and how we can all do our part to fix it.Another important point-This issue is large and contains multitudes, hence the lack of a clear point of attack....Occupy Wall Street is a recognizable slogan for those who only know of Wall Street in concept and don't understand the people and policy and money and power involved. These protesters are intelligent, educated, in financial distress, understand why, and are fed up. I expect protests to be organized against The Fed very soon.  It is just as culpable as Wall Street, and the Occupy bunch knows it. *The thing is, this is a marathon, not a sprint.  There will be no quick fix, and it is going to require support from everyone for anything to really change.  It's about getting people to understand that we all need to work together on this one.  It won't get fixed otherwise, and it needs to get done.If you would like to continue brushing it off, you are welcome to do so.  But it would be a shame . I hope this post was relatively coherent.  There are a lot of big ideas and conversations to this multifaceted issue that one blog post cannot fit.  But in the interest of wanting to have these conversations with people, and talk to people interested in actually causing change, I will be starting an Occupy Astoria group.  The purpose will not be to camp out, or even necessarily to go out in protest in the neighborhood.  The purpose will be to get people together to have these discussions and then start educating others on what we can do to effect change in our everyday lives and towards the larger goal.  Changing a deeply entrenched level of corruption where the wrongdoers have all the money, have a level of control over the media, and spend millions on lobbyists is not easy. A people united cannot be defeated.
