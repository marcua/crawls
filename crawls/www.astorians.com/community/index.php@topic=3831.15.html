I am almost 100% positive that it is not being turned into a homeless shelter.  I was involved, in my old job, in the early negotiations.  I have never heard that they are going to open any shelter there and I think they would have said that if they were.  I would imagine they find some other commercial venue.  They operate a few beds above CBGB's but are more homeless services instead of an actual shelter.  The area is very hostile to new shelters opening (they had two over the last few years within three blocks of CBGB's, so I doubt they could even get approval if they were going to go that route).

I am almost 100% positive that it is not being turned into a homeless shelter.  I was involved, in my old job, in the early negotiations.  I have never heard that they are going to open any shelter there and I think they would have said that if they were.  I would imagine they find some other commercial venue.  They operate a few beds above CBGB's but are more homeless services instead of an actual shelter.  The area is very hostile to new shelters opening (they had two over the last few years within three blocks of CBGB's, so I doubt they could even get approval if they were going to go that route). 

What I understand it that BRC is going to re-rent out the space.  Keep in mind, BRC does not own the building, they lease the building from the owners and were subleasing to CBGB's (who they inherited as tenant).  Maybe I'm being overly cynical, but I bet they give up their lease and the entire building complex along the Bowery gets developed.  CBGB's wasn't in it's prime anymore, but it was always packed when I was in it, so I don't think it was lack of a draw.  Granted, Hilly didn't pay his rent on time on many occasions, but they didn't exactly raise the issue when he was late, or irresponsible, they just moved to evict him instead of coming to terms.

Well if that's the case, it totally sucks.  But I remember listening to NPR last year, and the head of the BRC was interviewed, and it sounded like the space was going to be used for more homeless services.  Not necessarily for a shelter, but it would be part of the overall homeless services that they provide.

And yeah, I'm sad it's closing.  It's another piece of NYC that made the city unique, washed away.  Anytime that happens, it's a drag.  I hate seeing the city overrun by chains and corporate establishments.  CB's is a big part of the city's culture and history. 

But if  you walk through the village, I believe on Bleeker Street, by 6th Ave, you'll see an old sign for, I think it was the Village Gate, which was a very important Jazz Club.  A CVS now resides in that location.  It's really sad.  But I guess my point got a little muddled earlier.  What I was trying to say is that if a music venue is forced to close, better the space be turned into something to serve the community than another cookie cutter store, restaurant, or housing for rich people.

And Hilly Kristal was/is an extraordinary guy.  He did a huge amount to help a lot of musicians.  And he deserves to relax in the warmth of the West and retire comfortably.  I would imagine he'll make craploads more money in Vegas than here.

Nothing lasts forever.  Music evolves (or devolves as the case may be), and people's tastes change.  People might have still been going to CB's, but nowhere near the amount that were there for the heyday.  Those days are long gone.  And it's something I'm trying to accept as a jazz musician for the type of music I play.
