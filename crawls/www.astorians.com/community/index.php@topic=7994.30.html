The story about Carol Gotbaum bothered me on a lot of different levels, but maybe not for the same reasons that it bothered you.  From the news reports, it sounded like she was really out of control - yelling and running around the airport terminal.  I think that if I had been in that terminal (an enclosed space), I probably would have been frightened by her behavior and notified a gate agent or airport security because it sounded like she was behaving in an erratic and potentially threatening way.  Because she couldn't be calmed down by the airport police, I think it was right that she was handcuffed and taken away.  However, what I don't understand is why she wasn't given a medical intake to make sure that she wasn't just having a bad reaction to meds or suffering from a medical condition.  (From the latest news reports, it says that she was on her way to a rehab center in Tucson, so this is very likely.)  I also don't understand why someone who was clearly in distress was left in an isolation cell, which is where she died.

So I think this is a case of improper police procedure but not necessarily police brutality.
