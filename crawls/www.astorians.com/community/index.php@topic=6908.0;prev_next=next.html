I miss the fried chicken from McKenzie's on Gentilly,
I miss the bbq shrimp of docks in algiers,

I miss how friday happy hour began at the Columns at 2 in the afternoon,
I miss seeing movies at the Prytania,
I miss that great fish place over on Canal and Carrollton,

I miss those mesh enclosed porches with ceiling fans, especially the ones upriver from Napolean,
I miss the great outdoor market on Girod.  Week after week the local produce would come in.  Strawberries one week, then the blueberries et cetera.
I miss that little strip lakeside of Robert E. Lee (west end is it called?).  Having crawfish and drinks overlooking Lake Pontchartrain.
I miss those used bookshops in the Vieux Carre, and how they never charged tax once they found out you weren't a tourist.

And I wish I would find it up here, but CDM chickory coffee and creole seasonings I still can't seem to find here.

I believe we used to refer to it as Slime-Hell growing up. But then, what suburb wouldn't seem like that next to such a great city as NOLA? I was born in Mercy Hospital and lived in Old Gentilly until my folks bought the house in Slidell ("A great place to raise your kids" as a billboard entering the city read) and we moved across the lake. Still, we had lots of friends in New Orleans and spent most weekends visiting them there. Strangely enough, most of my friends in Slidell would only ever go into New Orleans to get to the airport. I never understood how they didn't want to get in there every chance they could?!?

BTW, the drinking age was 18 the entire time I was there, so this meant you could be 16 and pretty much get into any bar in Slidell or NOLA.
