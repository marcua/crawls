An interesting part of this discussion is the 30 year tenure of Sen Onorato and the changes occurring in Astoria at this moment.

My walk to the Steinway Subway station takes me along 38th st (if I want to avoid the crowds on Steinway). One portion of this walk is the block of 38th st between 30th ave and 31st Ave. This block contains 9 new apartment bulding that are going up in the place of single dwelling homes.

Now think of this. 9 homes that contained maybe 18-21 adults of voting age. Those people, who sold their homes and maybe some moved to Florida were probably solid Onorato voters, And who were they replaced by? From what I see these are 4 story walk ups that have 2 apartments each floor. So what we have is at least 9 x 8 x 2....which equals 144.....

Who are these 144 people that replaced the 21. I would hazard a guess that they are not the socially conservative democrats of old. I would hazard a guess that they are the 20-30 year olds that support marriage equality. Sen Onorato's voters are leaving Astoria and being replaced with progressive people. And his old supporters are being replaced, on that one block, by the tune of six times.

Now this isn't exact. But it is I think a good indication of the changes that have occurred. And it is one that should worry the Senator if he is faced with a strong primary challenger.
