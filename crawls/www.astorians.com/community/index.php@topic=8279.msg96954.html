Compaq Presario X1000 laptop: powerhouse with finicky hard drive

I am giving away this computer to a good home - or, ideally, to a good cause.  It's free to the first person who emails me and can DEFINITELY 100% CERTAIN pick it up TOMORROW (Sunday October 13) in the Ditmars area of Astoria, NY.

1.6 GHz CPU, 1 GB DDR RAM, 15.4" screen, finicky 60 GB 5400rpm disk, b/g WiFi, 64 MB ATI Radeon graphics - see the bottom of this post for a full hardware report.

This powerhouse laptop computer has served me well for about three years.  On the whole I have been very happy with it: it runs fast, is a little on the heavy side but still very portable, and has good battery life.

There's just one problem - about two years ago, the hard drive started occasionally losing its connection to the motherboard.  By occasionally, I mean about once a month, although more frequently if I was moving the computer a lot and carrying it places.  If Windows was running, this would cause a bluescreen, but this rarely happened.  Usually it would just prevent the computer from booting because it couldn't find the hard drive.  I have always been able to fix this issue by gently pressing the hard drive against the chassis, then booting again, but it's annoying and disruptive.

So, it's a fantastic machine and it's hard to do better on price than free, but it's not for the faint of heart or non-technologically-inclined.  It would be good for someone who wants a desktop replacement and wouldn't move it around much, because they wouldn't encounter the issue as often.

One other thing - I'm not sure whether the ethernet jack is working.  It may have broken, but I only used the WiFi (both b and g protocols), which definitely works great.

Comes with all original manuals and disks, including the system restore disk, which has a copy of Windows XP Home.
