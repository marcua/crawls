I am simply trying to understand the appeal and preference for these places.. that's all.
I think that is really the main problem here. I am not sure that it matters at all if you or I understand the appeal. Someone does or they would not stay in business. I want them to prosper in Astoria so that Astoria remains a vital and vibrant community. When these types of chains are reticent to open a business here it means that Astoria cannot financially sustain them; then how are we to be able to sustain the more expensive mom and pop places that have to be charging more because their overhead is highly. I don't want to sound redundant, but I am grateful that these chains have chosen MY Community to open their branches, and that they are good local citizens, even if I never set foot into one of them.

And if any of the local restaurants want to maintain their loyal customers, and increase their profit margins, I would suggest that they look into establishing a children's menu so that folks with families would not have to decide between them and the crap at one of the chain places. Applebees and the local diners are jam packed after the movies let out, while our finer dining establishments are starting to hurt while trying to retain their clientele based on costs and the state of the economy.
