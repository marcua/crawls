Alexa, I guess everyone is getting all riled up over this because it has been suggested that, if you tip the busboy directly and leave no tip to the waiter then this is somehow promoting theft.  It seems as if the majority of the group disagrees with this position.

Personally, I do not think, as it has been suggested, that one should know the policy of the restaurant as far as the mechanics of the tip distribution is concerned.  I think that should be the concern of the wait staff when they are seeking employment with that particular restaurant and they need to know what they are getting into.  They are the ones that need to know how "their" tip is going to be distributed, if at all.  And perhaps, the tip distribution policy of the restaurant will provide some incentive for the wait staff to do the best job they can, particulary if multiple persons are affected by the service/amount of tip. 
What I personally find offensive is the notion that I, as the patron, should somehow feel responsible for the rest of the staff, assuming the restaurant has a policy whereby other staff members are to be partially compensated via the wait staff's tip.  If I feel the bus-staff were the only ones who were attentive and provided good service then I as the patron want to make sure that person gets compensated for their good service and not the member of the wait staff that slacked off or was rude or whatever.  I feel all of these problems could be avoided if the management trained their staff appropriately and were mindful about who they hire and the kind of service they want to provide to the community.   If they want to be complacent with their staff and service then they need to be concerned, not the patron, with the possibility of a tip going directly to the busboy.
