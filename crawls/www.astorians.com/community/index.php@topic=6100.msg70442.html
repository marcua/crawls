I think that's why refer to them differently - their such different experiences. I call Manhattan "the city" and parts of Long Island that are not Queens or Brooklyn "Long Island" as if they were separate from where I live. If someone said of an acquaintance, "Oh, she's from Long Island", I would make different assumptions than if they said, "Oh, she's from Queens."

It may not make sense geographically, but the words are too entrenched in people's minds to force a change, I think. We all know what we mean, even if we also know it doesn't make sense :)
