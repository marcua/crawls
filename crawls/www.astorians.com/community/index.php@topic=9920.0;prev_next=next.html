Local Astorian author - in search of venues for readings, signings, etc.
« on: January 26, 2008, 03:35:03 PM »
I'm a soon-to-be published author of a middle-grade historical novel entitled SKIES OVER SWEETWATER about the Women Airforce Service Pilots of WWII (Yes, women did indeed fly for our country during WWII.) Based on this little-known piece of American history, it follows a fictional character named Byrd Thompson (18) who leaves her poverty-stricken Iowa home in 1944 to travel to Sweetwater, Texas--where the Women Airforce Service Pilots (WASP) training camp teaches women how to fly bombers, pursuits, trainers, and utility planes.

It is being published in March 2008, to coincide with Women's History Month and has already been named a selection of the Children's Book-of-the-Month Club, The Literary Guild, The Quality Paperback Book Club, and Crossings Book Club.

Early reviews have compared the writing to Harper Lee's TO KILL A MOCKINGBIRD.

My publisher is currently working on booking readings and signings in and around the city, but since I live in Astoria I would LOVE LOVE LOVE to do something local. Does anyone know any libraries, bookstores (yeah, I know, there aren't any really), youth community events, or venues that might be interested in having an author come by for either a reading or a signing sometime in March?

Any insight and advice would be very appreciated!

Thanks,
Julia Moberg
author, SKIES OVER SWEETWATER
Available to order at amazon.com, bn.com, etc.
