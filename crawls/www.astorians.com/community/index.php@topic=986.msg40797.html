Okay, this time - not a "psyche"!

Just spun through the new store.  It definitely feels new and clean - so refreshing to see sparkling tupperware sitting on shelves. 

Selections are pretty typical, with maybe a bit more emphasis on Spanish and Greek cuisine (big surprise?)  Some nice international cheeses and chocolates, small seafood section that beats the Food World at 36th Ave.

Today they're giving out cheese samples by the entryway and sausage out on the street from a cart.   8-)

It's an odd mix of somewhat classy (like the fancy wooden coffee display) with typical C-Town fare.  The specials this week (like those 89-cent Pepsis) look pretty good, but no telling how long they'll last.

Summary:  Nothing super special, but I'll definitely be using it to balance out my Food World visits.  I've also complained for a long time that despite my long walk home when I get off the R train at Steinway, I pass no grocery stores - they'll definitely be getting my R train business for sure.  They're not too shabby (yet).

I also think they got that graffiti cleaned up, didn't they?  I just realized I haven't noticed it for awhile.

Alas, Tupelo. It is hard to remember with real certainty where things used to be. I think it's interesting how spaces change upon re-use, or how the memory of what was there before can be erased.

Where once there was a working fireplace, now there is organic produce and an ATM machine. The sign for Aisle 2 now takes the space where a fake can of Duff beer used to sit above the bar. The posters announcing independent film night (which used to be on Sundays - tonight!) are replaced by loud signs for Chicken Cutlets and Grade A Oven Stuffer Roasts.

The area beside the fireplace doubled as the stage, and I think I remember a rounded couch (at which I had my Tupelo-esque ritual makeout session with a stranger). That space is now taken by a round table covered by bad looking bananas.

I was never really much into the scene at Tupelo, but I liked the space. I'd go on Mondays so I could mind my own business, and maybe get the prized seat at the end of the bar. That spot is now taken by a Red Bull cooler, or maybe it's where the shopping cart parking lot is. If that seat was taken I would take the soft seats inside the front door. No sitting in that space now, as it is the exit lane from the store. I think that seat reached to where there is now a scale with which you can determine your "EXACT WEIGHT."

I remember appreciating how CUP maintained some elements of what their space used to look like. The CIRKER'S sign, which used to be outside, is one thing I can name offhand.

At Bravo there are a few out-of-place seeming stained glass doors up above. I saw them through the windows closest to 34th street, through the signs for Krasdale Sugar and Hellmann's Mayonnaise. At first I thought those were left over from Tupelo, but that doesn't seem possible based on the location. Sort of an odd interior decorating flourish, or maybe it just seems that way while looking for some trace of what was there before.
