I'm graduating from there in May (go me!) and I love it there. Big ups to SVA from me, but it is $$$ (I'm gonna be paying for it until I'm dead, prolly). I don't know what your situation is, but they do have continuing ed and Summer programs - if you're ever near the main building (23rd St. between 2nd and 3rd ave), go into the admissions office (its right on the ground level) and grab some catalogs and whatever. If not call or email and have them send stuff to you. You should do this for all the other schools mentioned too. You just missed the photography open house at SVA though - that's every November. Not sure if they do one in the spring.

Also check out ICP - www.icp.org (http://www.icp.org), and click on school.

Also, I went back to school after working full time for awhile (did both and commuted from CT for my first 2 years at SVA), and while its tough, you can do it if you're really into it. If you want to get in for next fall though, get on it! I had all my stuff in at SVA in early March '03 and started in September '03, and I think I was pushing it a bit there.

Title: Re: Anyone know a good Photography school?
Post by: jwphotonyc on May 08, 2007, 09:10:29 AM
Depends on how serious you are and how much you want to spend.

ICP is good, but the classes in the continuing education program run about $700 per.  I found them frustrating because everyone was on a different level and the "photographers" who couldn't so much as work their cameras ran the show.  But, their facilities were top-notch.  SVA's C.E. programs were far cheaper, the classes smaller, the attention much more one-on-one.

If you're just getting your feet wet, try SVA.

Good luck.
