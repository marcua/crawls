Well, TYR has some of the best teachers from all over the city, including some of my personal favorites from multiple disciplines. The sheer variety of classes offered there, ranging from Iyengar to Yin to Brakan style hot Vinyasa, could help you figure out what works best for you. If you are completely new to yoga, I strongly recommend trying a beginners' Iyengar class to learn the physics and mechanics of basic postures. I see to many people, including myself when I started out, jumping into physically intensive vinyasa classes without really learning to how to support their joints, spine and neck.

I've heard great things about Yoga Agora as an alternative to Yoga for the People but can't personally vouch for it. Tone Pilates may be your cup of tea as well, so check them out. This small schools run by well liked ex-TYR instructors and is right off B'way and 38th st.

Good luck! Regardless of where you choose to go, even if it's a class at the NYSC, you're not too far from the many choices available here.
