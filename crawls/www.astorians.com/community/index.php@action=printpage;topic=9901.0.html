Title: Kitaku
Post by: Zeke on March 24, 2008, 05:13:42 PM
Anyone else been to Kitaku - the new sushi place on 23rd?

We went on Friday night and weren't too impressed.  When we saw the fish menu, we had great hopes for a good quality sushi place near us, but we were pretty dissapointed.  Quality of the fish (the Uni and Ikura was so, so and possibly a bit old which is NOT a good sign) and sizes of the portions were meager considering the prices (thin, not very big slices - hard to actually taste the fish).  The only thing we had that got high marks was the Age Dashi Dofu and the Vegetable tempura apps.  We also tried the Tom Yung Gung , but I didn't think it was worth getting again - the broth was not very spicy and they put fake crab into it, which fell apart and threw off the taste and texture.

We're not going to totally write them off just yet.  We're hoping they'll realize what's going wrong and work out the kinks.  We'll go again in a couple of weeks and see how things are going.  Until then, I guess we'll continute going to Watawa as our local joint.

Zeke

Title: Kitau (?) the new sushi on 23rd Ave.
Post by: Christine on March 07, 2009, 10:39:58 AM
Decided to try it last night. It's right next door to Club 23, yet worlds apart of course! Although I think the music seeps through, which is too bad.

Decor is fantastic, whoever's renovating the restaurants on 23rd Ave (Bangkok Tasty for example) is doing a fabulous job  :mrgreen: Kitau used to be the Greek supermarket that I liked, but you'd never know it. Creative interior. Lights above the tables could be shielded a bit more- no big deal.

Food, however, was just decent. I loved it; presentation as wonderful as the interior design, but my sushi-savvy dining companion wasn't as impressed. He tried the $13 bottle of sake and again, my less-experienced palate thought it was fine, while he deemed it "cheap." We had the black angel and spicy girl rolls (excellent); split one of the sushi entrees with the three types of sushi (came with soup and salad, pretty standard); were offered a sample appetizer consisting of cream cheese with.....oh I forget, it was tasty though.

Still, the prices are normal, service was great (they allowed us to linger which was nice) and they appear kid-friendly for those of you who like that sort of thing (biting my tongue) :wink:

Verdict: Wonderful treat on payday after a very rough week if you're not much of a sushi snob. Also, this would be a fine place to impress a date (who's not a sushi snob!) or just relax with a group.
