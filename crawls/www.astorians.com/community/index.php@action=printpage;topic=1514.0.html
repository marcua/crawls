Title: Re: Straight friendly bars?
Post by: bettyspin on February 20, 2006, 06:56:03 PM
What does one mean by Straight Friendly?  Well it doesn't matter.  Maybe we can categorize a few bars for your friend:

1.  The Quays - a great Irish pub on 45th Street and 30th Avenue.  Specializing in third drink kickbacks, crazy irish folk getting piss drunk and singing along with the REAL JUKEBOX.  Occasional live music and the best escargot in town.  Average price for a real drink: $3.50

2.  Caprice: Astoria Boulevard Train Station.  High brow, allowing age groups from 23 to 35.  Always a big huge bouncer at the door and plenty of barely clad (although untouchable by any mere male mortal) ladies inside.  Seems to have hip-hop overtones, while generally being a middle to upper Astoria white crowd.  Average price for a real drink $13

3.  The Beer Garden.  The place to go when your feeling college dorm living nestalgia.  Definitely hit this place as early as you can to avoid the line and maybe even get some serious croation cooking in the basement restaurant (get the beef goulash!).  Restaurant closes early on Fridays and Saturday nights, but they have grilled food all night long.  Don't bother in the winter and fall though - the party is outside beyond the bar where 100 picnic tables great you.  Plenty of room to mingle and meet you match, or just a hookup (relive those college days!)  Average price for a real drink: Who knows?  Go for a pitcher of beer $12/pitcher.
