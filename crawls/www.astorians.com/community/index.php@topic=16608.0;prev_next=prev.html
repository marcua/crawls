Renaissance Steakhouse
« on: July 06, 2009, 06:27:39 PM »
This new steakhouse is located in the same location as the Renaissance Catering Hall.  27-34 21st Street.  Near Astoria Blvd.  They are just starting a website so check back.  http://renaissancesteakhouse.com/

We first noticed this place after a meal at Vesta.  There are french doors open to the street and it has a rather elegant appearance from the outside.  So we took note and made a point to go back and try it.  And boy are we glad we did.  What a great place!  For me, this met all the requirements of a good steakhouse.  First off, the people are extremely warm and welcoming.  The decor is tough to describe.  Probably remnants from the catering days.  So it's oddly formal, but then has a beach resort casualness about it.  Not bad at all.  It was very comfortable and yet felt like a grand space.  And we noticed that all the tables are a generous size.  Even for two.  There was no juggling act as glasses got filled and plates began to arrive from the kitchen. The outdoor seating is quite nice.  But of course, being on 21st St is what it is.  A tire shop is the view across the street.  For now, anyway.  It's turning into condo row rather quickly.

The menu is typical steakhouse fare, which is a good thing, I think.  For appetizers, there were a number of seafood choices, a beef tartare, foie gras, crab cake, etc.  I had the Grilled Prawns coated with a garlic pesto sauce.  They were delicious.  Three jumbo prawns perfectly grilled with a nice watercress salad on the side.  My wife had the Caesar Salad to start.  It was delicious - with exceptional whole white anchovies on top.  Also a very generous salad.  I should mention that they start you off with a fantastic loaf of warmed bread on a bread board that has a block of butter dusted with sea salt.  Also a large plate of olives, four cheeses, and sliced fresh figs.  All absolutely delicious.  My wife had the rack of lamb.  I had the Renaissance Filet.  There were at least 5 - 6 nice sized lamb chops.  Incredibly tender.   We're big lamb eaters and this ranked as one of the best lamb meals we recall in a very long time.  My filet was a very nice size.  Cooked perfectly medium rare as requested.  Wrapped in bacon.  Topped with Asiago cheese.  And then topped with fresh, handmade onion rings.  It was amazing.  I managed to eat every bite and enjoyed it from start to finish.  The onion rings were exceptional.  I inquired about them.  They use a combination of seasoned breadcrumbs and panko.  And some special seasonings.  The coating was not too heavy and didn't get soggy after a few minutes.  And not greasy at all.  Onion was sweet like candy.  After my raves about them, the chef insisted that we have an additional plate of the onion rings.   We also ordered a side of creamed spinach.  Fresh baby spinach leaves with a light cream sauce poured over.  Then topped with breadcrumbs and baked.  It was a little soupy but irresistible.  The waitress and everyone there was genuinely happy that we were happy.  But never overbearing.  The prices are what you might expect.  But honestly, after eating there, I think the prices are extremely reasonable for such a great meal.  My steak was in the $30 - 40 range.  As was the lamb.  Figure hot appetizers run about $10 - 20.  Salads less.  They have an extensive wine list.  We sampled the house red.  A California Cabernet.  It was $12 a glass (huge goblets in this case).  Or $24 a bottle.  Many wines to choose from covering the globe.  And most well under $50 a bottle.  They have a very nice dessert menu but we didn't order.  I can't say enough.  It was one of the best meals we had in a while.  We can't wait to go back with some friends.  The only complaints would be that they didn't have a few key menu choices.  Such as the foie gras and their signature blue cheese salad.  And maybe others, had we ordered them.  So I hope as they become more well known, they'll be able to offer the entire menu on a daily basis.  They say they are about to start an advertising campaign and launch the website possibly this week.  I can see this place becoming a hot spot - like in a L'Incontro kind of way.
