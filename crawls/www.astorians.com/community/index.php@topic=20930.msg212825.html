Quote from: lanseaux on May 06, 2011, 10:07:15 AM
I'm noticing another issue with the "show unread posts" link - the regular one this time. I'm viewing my unread posts, and I click on one of them.  When I'm done reading it, I click the "show unread posts" again at the top of the page to bring me back to a refreshed list of unread posts.  However, the post I just read is still listed.  A hard refresh (with the shift key) clears it but not a regular page refresh.  It doesn't happen every time, but it's happened several times since yesterday.  This morning, it happened two out of three times.

This is actually a function of the new software and the way I've configured the server. It helps reduce the load on the server. What it does is the server indexes the board indexes (the topic list pages), and the unread posts pages once every 60 seconds. The alternative is that the server has to create an index of those posts every time the page is loaded or refreshed which slows things down.

Practically it means that the unread posts listing page will be about 60 seconds behind you. When you read something it will still appear as unread for 60 seconds, but then it falls off the list. This is why after you were refreshing it finally went away, the other times you probably had left the page to start reading another post anyway, and by the time you got back it was refreshed for you.

The same will now happen on the home page as well, it runs about 60 seconds behind "real-time". Individual topics and posts however are still all real-time.

There's some cool new features to the forum if you guys haven't checked them out yet.

Under your profile for example if you click on "Modify Profile" you'll see a dropdown there with lots of options.

There's an "Ignore Boards" list where you can ignore certain boards (like Random Gabble)
There's also the ability to ignore certain posters, or add people to your buddy list.
In addition there's some new "notification" options for how you might want to be notified of topics that you're interested in. It can send you a daily digest of all the topics you've posted in, or immediately notify you.

The site also now has expanded RSS options. If you prefer to read the site in an RSS reader format you can just go to whatever boards you're interested in and grab the URL and put it your RSS reader and you'll get all the posts from that board.

Also, don't forget a simplified version of the site is available on your phone by going here: http://www.astorians.com/community/index.php?wap2
