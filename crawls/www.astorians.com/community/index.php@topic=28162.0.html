Credit checks can be bought on your own online for about $30.  A good real estate broker will accept your credit check papers.  A bad one will tell you no and charge you a $100, pocketing the difference.   As for roommates, please don't let someone select a person for you.  It's best when you can speak to the potential roommate and really discuss how you will live together.  Otherwise, you may end up with someone that's not so great to live with - ie doesn't clean, etc.  Try asking around to your social networks and on Craigslist, and there may even be a few threads on here with folks looking for roommates.  Finally, if you're looking for easy Grand Central access, you might consider the 7 train line - which means Sunnyside, Woodside, and Jackson Heights.  It's a quick shot straight into Grand Central and you are more likely to find an apartment with laundry facilities.  Those are very hard to come by in Astoria, but they do exist.  Both the R and N line for Astoria require a transfer to Grand Central - a  thankfully short but crowded one.  Good luck!
