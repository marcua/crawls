Title: Celebrate the Art of Louis Pierre Rigal at the Astoria Center of Israel
Post by: Your Astoria Shul on November 08, 2009, 10:33:20 PM
The Astoria Center of Israel, a distinguished synagogue and popular community center for over 80 years, has been designated an Historic Religious Site.

Join us on Sunday, November 15, as we celebrate this honor!
27-25 Crescent Street
(718) 278-2680
Ceremony and Collation at 3:00 PM

The Astoria Center of Israel was erected in 1925 and in 1928-29 the ceiling of the lobby and the ceiling and walls of the main sanctuary were decorated with muslin canvases painted by Louis Pierre Rigal. The beautiful Art-Deco artwork was inspired by three main ideas:
- Harvest scenes of grapes, wheat, and other materials which are included in Jewish ritual observance
- A quotation from the Jewish Rabbinic literature called Ethics of the Fathers which states: "Be bold as a leopard, light as an eagle, swift as a gazelle, and strong as a lion. " The dramatic representations of these animals adorn the front wall on both sides of the pulpit looking out at the congregation in all their royal majesty.
- From the story of Noah - the centerpiece looming high over the pulpit is the famous scene of the dove with the freshly plucked olive branch in her mouth. 

You can see some of the artwork on our website - http://astoriacenter.org (http://astoriacenter.org)
