Maltitol is one of the sugar alcohols (total misnomer, as it is neither a sugar nor an alcohol) that has become popular in low carb diets to replace sugar.

The theory behind it is that sugar alcohols are only partially absorbed by the body, reducing the standard caloric effect of 4 calories per carbohydrate gram.  Each sugar alcohol is different, and each person has a different rate of absorption.

Here is a page listing the various sugar alcohols, and their respective theoretical caloric effect.

Maltitol is, from what I have seen, the most common one used.  It's deadly for me.  I once grabbed a small candy bar not realising that the sweetener was a sugar alcohol, specifically maltitol.  The resulting rumbling and general intestinal distress was most unpleasant, despite the fact that what I ate was one serving, which contained 14g maltitol.  The only way to determine your own tolerance level is to try it.  I can tolerate about 5g sugar alcohol before it starts bothering me.  This is exceedingly low.

Also, my own personal theory is that the less you absorb it, the more intestinal distress you will have.  The more you absorb it, the less trouble you have.  Thus, any benefits of having a "sugar-free" candy are largely negated, particularly if it's calories you are trying to avoid.

My recommendation is to stick to small quantities of real, dark chocolate.  Lindt 85% is an excellent choice.

My friend promised to bring back chocolate (I did not specify what kind) and this is what he brought for me.  It was sweet (no pun intended) and thoughtful on his part.  However...

I am not trying to avoid any kind of sugar.  I am somewhat of a purist - I despise Equal, Sweet N'Low and the most dreadful tasting Splenda.  For my sugar intake I prefer it in the form of granulated cane sugar (beet sugar sometimes, but it has a slightly different taste than the cleaner tasting cane sugar - btw, can you get C&H sugar here in NYC?), honey, maple syrup.  I try to avoid corn syrup, as it is an intruder in the body that reminds me of a lying SOB.  It is difficult in America to completely eliminate it from one's diet, so I do give myself a break when I am left choiceless (like being presented with conventional "pancake syrup").

As far as chocolate goes, favorites of mine are the 71% Valhrona bar, the Dagoba Lavender bar, and the Chocolove bar with bits of orange peel in it.  I prefer to eat chocolate with at least a 65% cacao content.  That Lindt 85% is pretty awesome.  All the chocolate I ate from Antwerp was amazing, too, and that experience opened my eyes and palate up to appreciating and loving dark chocolate over milk.   I also love the caco nibs from Scharffenberger (produced in my lovely Berkeley, CA!) and enjoy them especially on Haagen Dazs Vanilla ice cream.  Chika at Chikalicious in Manhattan rolls her pink peppercorn ice cream in the nibs and boy is it tasty.  That is a place we need to visit sometime - it is fabulous.

I've taken the Scharffenberger tour twice and it is super fun.  If you are ever in the Bay Area, you should check it out!
