Title: Re: blaring music from Astoria Park
Post by: unsurpAstorian on July 01, 2009, 07:09:54 PM
Last Saturday evening I finally had enough of the extraordinarily loud music blaring from the Park at about 2:00am so I walked down to investigate and found that people park their cars along the water on Shore Boulevard behind the swimming pool, open their car doors and crank their car stereos. It was a proper impromptu (and of course totally UNAUTHORIZED) block party going on, complete with drunken people dancing and partying all along Shore Boulevard, each car attempting to out blast the car next to it with it's own awful reggaeton remix or some horrid pop house track maxed to 11. Without engaging anyone, I discretely wrote down some license plate numbers and went home to call the police.

The officer on duty at the 114th was very accommodating and extremely understanding as I explained (very calmly mind you) the situation as I found in detail. He confirmed what was happening, although a common occurrence (especially early on in the summer) was indeed illegal and he would send a unit out to investigate. Shortly thereafter the park became silent. Thank you NYPD.

SO - in this scenario, anytime any of you hear excessively loud, unsanctioned music or noise coming from the park late at night, I urge you to just phone our precinct rather than try to navigate the red tape of 311. The more calls they get, the more likely the issue is to escalate into a some sort of policy change. (Perhaps a petition to close that stretch of Shore Boulevard to motorized vehicles on the weekends could help?) Organization and information exchange is the key.
