Randall's Island/Ward's Island is actually very easy...I do it most mornings (at least 3 a week right now) in my commute to Harlem. At about 28th St on the North side of Astoria Blvd. there is a stair to the pedestrian bridgeway. It has a rail for pushing your bike up the stairs, but I find it easier to just pick up the bike & climb. There are 2 of these easy stairs going up and 1 going down. It takes me about 7 minutes to cross the bridge to Randall's.

I made the Astoria to W. Harlem (next to the Hudson) commute this morning in half an hour...much better than it was for the poor suckers on the M60 who were in one of the worst 125th Street traffic jams I've seen.  :-D

Interested cyclists should consider joining TAWQ's google group at http://groups.google.com/group/TAWQ to keep up on our updates on cycling related news for Queens as well as rides we are putting together or just passing the word on for.  We just had a ride from Astoria to the Far Rockaways last Sunday and we're having the trial TdQ ride Sat 11/17 (check out the info here http://groups.google.com/group/TAWQ/web/tdq) 

Not sure how many more rides we'll be having now that the weather is getting cold, but our goal starting next spring is to have at least 1 ride a month and possibly a ride a week in May, which is Bike Month.  Best way to keep on top of all that is to join the google group.
