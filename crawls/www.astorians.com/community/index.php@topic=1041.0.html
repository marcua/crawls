New video gaming community in Astoria free for everyone....
« on: November 01, 2005, 11:35:36 PM »
I posted not too long ago in www.videogamebuddy.com for teenagers as well as colleagues such as myself to join in an open video game community. I decided if anyone would be interested in joining somethin gnew

As posted in Video Game Buddy's new store inside the Ditmars Blvd. arcade across the Internet Cafe, the owner and I have organized a get together in Thursdays from 4 p.m on. Anyone is welcomed to join, watch, view, comment, heck even just join in for the company.

 The games are basically fighting/action/multiplayer games NOT including Halo/Halo 2, Super Smash Brothers Melee, and PC/Store games that have already been posted in VideoGame Buddy's site as their own leagues or competitions.

The reason of this get together as part of a gaming community is to have a casual get together (refreshments and snacks all to be paid for), play some games, whether from Dreamcast, Playstation, Xbox, etc. and despite talent of playability, skill level, and location in Astoria. Although this has been posted in VGB's website for the time being for players who are interested in not only contemporary games, but also classical games from Capcom, Taito, and Midway to name some of our beloved companies who have revolutionized the way how games are played today. Plus, there hasn't been one gaming community like this ever since we've seen many companies: Gamestop, Electronics Boutique, heck even the new Bonus Stage and Video Game Buddy pop up, have started this with specifically Fighting games in particular, which is the community's main theme. Hopefully if we get enough people joining in there will be more ideas popping up and we can expand our groups so we can all head to Chinatown once per month to play some of New York's finest in our games we love.

As of now we are currently organizing a seasonal, mutli-league Round Robin tournament for those who are willing to play for a nice prize the next
upcoming Spring. I shall be the one organizing this major event and am always looking forward for players and gamers willing to join at any time for registration and/or interest of the games to be competing on. To check the formalities as well as the rules, go to www.videogamebuddy.com for the Forums inside Gamings Discussion. The forum is about Third Strike Casuals. For those who are interested, the registration for this event shall be open until end of November, and then it shall be a registration fee after that month on. Anyone who would like to know where I am there, my name is Lord Bishop in the forums.

Likewise my information, whoever is interested, and would like to talk to me about joining or anything else, please either post here or find me in videogamebuddy.com

Many thanks for those who are interested, and would like to get to know gamers in Queens that are dying to play some casual games after work or school. I look forward in seeing new faces and players that are willing to join hopefully every Thursday afternoon.

See you there...
