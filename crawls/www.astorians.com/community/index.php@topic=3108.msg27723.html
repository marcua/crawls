Although its a corruption of the traditional "lahmahjoon" (taught to me by my Armenian college roommate, freshman year), this is the best little wrap-sandwich ever! I just call it "Sandwichian", or Armenian Sandwich.

1 lb. ground lean lamb
1 1/2 cup yellow onion chopped fine
1/2 cup green bell pepper chopped fine
1/2 cup chopped Armenian or Italian parsley
1 teaspoon chopped garlic
1 teaspoon of fresh sweet basil chopped
1 tablespoon fresh mint leaves chopped
1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
1 can diced tomatoes
1 small can tomato paste (or 1/2 a tube)
Cayenne pepper to taste [optinal]
Salt and pepper to taste

1) Saute up the lamb (or beef or chicken, etc although lamb is best) along with all the herbs and stuff (its not haute cuisine, just cook it until its done and smells good!)

2) pile into a soft warm flatbread (here's where the Lavash comes in), roll up like a burrito

3) eat and be happy

optional: a dollop of yogurt, some Armenian cheese (feta will do) and/or a squeeze of lemon

I also like to stick some alfalfa sprouts in it but that's just the californian in me

Instead of the fuss and endless searching, why not just purchase lamajoun pre-made.  There are a number of places here in Astoria that sell them.  Titan & Agora Plaze on 31st and the Greek-Italian Deli on Ditmars.  They literally take 5 minutes to cook in a toaster over.  Packages contain a dozen lamajouns and cost about $11-12 each. 

As for the 'Armenian Parsley' thing, I have to say I'm 100% Armenian-American (2nd generation American born) and this is news to me.  In fact, I remember when I was young, we would have a lamajoun-making party when my grandmother came to visit and make what seemed like 100's of these tasty little treats, but it was always flat-leafed parsley that was used in the meat mixture.
