Legally if the owner wants you out and you dont have any lease or contract saying you are entitled to a specific time frame , he has to give you 30 days written notice and then you would have to leave . Once an eviction notice is presented , a Marshall can come and kick you right out the door . You can also get fined and have to pay Marshall fees just for the Marshall coming there and wasting his time . Any court fees you might be resposible for also . I only say this cuz i know at least 3 people that this has happened to . Remember a lease isnt just for a landlord locking someone in to a year , its also to protect the tenant so that after a couple of months the landlord cant say my brother is moving from California and needs the apt , so you have to leave right now . Without a lease though , its his property and if he doesnt want your there , how can you expect to stay >?    No court has a right to tell someone they have to keep you .   Find a new place and hopefully you wont have to pay any of the above i mentioned .

You should also be aware that the landlord can continue to raise the rent to even more levels and without a lease you cant refuse . He can raise it to $1000 more a month and if you dont pay or move out you would owe him the rent . It may sound crazy but there is no limit someone can raise the rent in a non rent controlled or non leases apt . Even if you end up leaving and if you dont pay , he can get you for back rent by having a judgement to garnish your wages . In order to avoid this , i would find a place ASAP .
