Okay here goes again!  I was born in Flushing Hospital in August of 1943.  I lived the first seven years of my life in Astoria, 33-10 33rd Street.  It was a three story walk up.  Does anyone remember this apartment house?

I remember so many things about living in Astoria during this time.  Christopher Walken's father had the local bakery.  Walken and I are the same age, I think we went to the same PS.

I also remember the monkey grinder, plying his tunes under our apartment window, with his monkey.

I remember black out curtains in our apartment before the war was over.  I rememer hot summers when we slept on the fire escape and when the police opened the fire hydrants in the streets where we would play in the water.  And then come home when we were covered with filth.

I remember my mother told me about "graft" -- I said , what was that?  She told me about the "bad guys" who would throw bricks through the windows of the store fronts if they weren't paid.

I remember the pink balls that were sold at the local drug store that we would bounce on the apartment building walls.  I remember egg creams, that cost a dime.  Egg creams, no eggs, but chocolate syrup and milk and seltzer!  How yummy!

So, we moved out to Long Island in 1950.  To West Hempstead.  To have a better life?  I don't know.  My fondest memories are not from Long Island, but from Astoria.

I haven't been in Astoria for over 50 year.  But it still has my heart.

Any response to this!

Hello All -

I joined Astorians last week. I've actually been posting on some topics but I should have introduced myself first. Sorry about that! Certain posts had me going so I had immediately responded to those.   Im a 28 yr old female working in the Finance industry who has lived in Astoria since 2003.

I came to Astoria by chance. I was living in the Bronx in a really miserable little apartment, when a friend of mine told me she was moving back to Astoria, and asked me if I wanted to be her roommate. I immediately broke my lease and took whatever little money I had and moved in with her. We found an apartment within 3 weeks. It was on 38th street near Steinway. Ok apartment, it was somewhat brand new but my landlord was a bit shady (but nice). It was a 3 bedroom. Actually 2.5 bedroom bc the 3rd bedroom was tiny. We wound up finding a third person on craigslist.com to move in there so our rent was cheaper.

Anyways we stayed there for 1.5 years and then we all decided to go our separate ways because moving in with boyfriends and such.  So I wound up by chance (and I do mean by chance) getting a 1 bedroom apartment over on Crescent Street. Im like 4 long blocks from the subway, but hey I can't complain. I have a friend living in Manhattan whose apartment is the size of my bedroom, so things could have been worse.

So although Ive been here for almost 4 years, im still learning the area. I made that a new years resolution because im always hanging in Manhattan and not taking advantage of whats around the corner from me. Im a big foodie so already this week ive tried many different restaurants and exploring the many different streets in Astoria.

Im a Bronx girl at heart, but Astoria has definately become my adopted hometown!  :-)
