I am against all thing that are considered bipartisan, so this didn't appeal to me but others may like it. He spoke of "compassionate conservatism" as if conservatism is inherently not compassionate.  But he also ran as a small government budget hawk, and that appeals to me also. And he ran against Al Gore, who came across as hugely unlikable in my view.
Then 9/11 Happened
Then we were asked wether or not to change courses in the War on Terrorism. I was not willing to do that with John Kerry. So I voted for Bush again. I held my nose and ignored the huge increases in the federal deficit and justified it by thinking that Kerry would do the same. I ignored the incredible usurpation of responsibility in the area of education because that was a "bipartisan" issue. I figured that the largest government expansion of government entitlements since Roosevelt is the way the nation wants to go anyway. So the prescription plan wasn't enough to throw me overboard.
I am not normally a one issue voter. And I will change my mind when presented with a better argument. But for better or worse we were in the middle of a war that was badly managed, but needed to be won.
I still feel with all the problems we have today we are better off by not electing John Kerry.
I wish our new President the best, but the fundamental difference between beings conservative / libertarian vs being liberal is too great. Not only in philosophy but also in tactics. And it raises real alarms as we anoint a new President rather than appoint one.
