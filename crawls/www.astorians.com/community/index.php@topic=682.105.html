The politics of fundraising
« Reply #108 on: October 06, 2005, 11:21:48 PM »
I was informed recently by Zeno that there was an initiative to contact a local Republican politician about supporting our relief efforts.

I would like to strongly oppose this initiative. I don’t think I could support a fund raising group that did allow it self to be the product of a political contribution because it would mean to me that I was supporting that politician de facto. While I recognize that it could enhance our ability to raise funds, it would do so with a political price tag. In essence we would become the tag and the politician would get to be the hat.

Some may argue that it does not matter if the object is to raise money and we left it open to all politicians. This is a fair argument, however I will still oppose the initiative since it is late in the game to give all politicians a fair run at representation, and because the object is not just about raising money, it’s about helping people. In my opinion I think humanitarian efforts should attempt to remain politically unbiased. Our goal is not to get people to vote. Our goal is not to make a political statement. Our goal is to help the relief effort. If a politician has an unbiased desire to help he/she should give directly to the organization that we are supporting.  There is no need to go through our group.

This reminds me of another pet peeve I have. I don’t get the feel that this group that we’re giving the money to is really actively behind us. I could be wrong here, so correct me. But are they helping at all besides proving a link on the web page?

I would appreciate more discussion on this issue as well as some kind of justification on the part of the board if they decide to go through with this decision.
