I just joined yesterday.  So far I like the place.  But then again, what it has going for it is convenience.  There's not much out here by Ditmars and The Matrix is much farther for me.  I joined with WellQuest which is like GlobalFit, you go through your health insurance (Oxford or others) or your job, and it's $57 a month.  And a month to month contract.  Which is great.  The gym is clean, the staff have been friendly if not very helpful to newbies, and they have good equipment.  The pilates class I took today was awesome, and most importantly, nonjudgmental.  I used to think it was a gym for trendsters, but so far I haven't encountered them.  Most of the girls I've noticed are not all decked out, wearing makeup and the like, rather they are just in Tshirts working out just like everyone else.  Doesn't appear to be much of a scene at least in the morning.  Honestly haven't noticed the music much but then again, who doesn't work out with their ipod?

Basically, everyone just joins the gym that works for them, cost and convenience-wise.  If it's not working for you, move on.  I do wish this area would get more gyms though.  We don't have many choices by Ditmars.

They've made a lot of changes in the last year and a half. When I joined in 2004, a lot of the desk staff had been there for awhile. They knew their business, could answer a question, and weren't rude. Most of them were let go about a year ago and a lot of young, inexperienced, snotty kids were hired. They really couldn't care less whether you stay, go, or even drop dead in front of the desk.

Another thing that's annoying now is that they've cut the air conditioning since they put in ceiling fans. On hot, humid days they cut the power on the AC and pretend that nothing is different. Their official story is that the system is old, needs to be replaced, etc. etc. Yet, they spent  money for these fans instead of upgrading the system.

The reason I go there is that the equipement is very good and maintained well. Plus I do a few of the classes, which saves me money. But when it comes to good, basic people skills and customer service, the owners are more concerned with their new offices there - which they moved into to save money renting a space on 30th Ave. & Crescent - than they are with how their staff are treating the members.

Is that specific enough?
