Quote from: essen on June 28, 2008, 12:01:57 PM
I hate Starbucks' coffee as much as the next person, but people, the coffee does not cost $4. Their coffee isn't much more expensive than most coffee shops. It's the specialty drinks that end up in the $3-5 dollar range, depending on the size of the drink and whatever extra crap you put in it, and similar drinks cost similar prices just about anywhere else you go. 
Just on a side note:  the reason Starbucks is higher cost than other coffee shops (including other chains that carry "gormet" coffee like McD's and Dunkin) is that all of it's employees - including part timers - receive full medical coverage.  60 minutes did a story on them not too long ago and the CEO mentioned that they pay more for medical coverage than they do for coffee beans at wholesale.

Some may knock them for this practice and hate that this is reflected in the price.  However, I kinda like the fact that it's corporate structure is thinking about the employees and thier needs.  A lot fo U.S. businesses have forgotten about this and put the medical burden on other government agencies (like medicare/medicade) to pick up the slack.  This cost all U.S. taxpayers and increases our overall tax burden.  So I like the fact that there are still companies out there who take care of it's workers.

Just something to think about.
