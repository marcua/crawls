I just found a beautiful apartment with Jenna from Astoria Apartments... This was the second time I've used her and I think if I ever have to move again I am going straight to her. I spent a long two weeks going through various craigslist posts, trying to avoid a broker's fee. Some were actually pretty decent but for the most part it was a nightmare. Even the decent ones just weren't good enough.. Either the neighborhood wasn't all that great or the landlord looked like a tight-ass. Anyway, my roommate and I decided to finally give up and go through a broker. I figured since I had used Jenna before, she would be our first try this time. We told her our budget and exactly what we were looking for (smoke/pet friendly, etc). She singled out the apartments she thought we would be interested in and showed us only three. All of them were great and we ended up taking the first one shown. We even got a nice little discount on the broker fee for being a second time customer  :-)

on a side note - when i found my last apartment through Jenna i also posted in here somewhere... There was a lot of talk about how so many people were 'one-time posters' and there was suspicion that these mysterious one-timers were employees of Astoria Apartments. So I come back to the broker section of the forum again today (about a year and roughly 10 posts later) only to notice that people are still being warned about the post count of the people posting positive things about Astoria Apartments.. I just don't get it. I'm not trying to be a poster boy for any broker or company or anything, just posting my experience and what I went through. Good luck house hunting everyone
