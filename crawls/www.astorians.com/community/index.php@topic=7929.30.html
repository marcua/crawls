I think the point mcw raised is a big issue with the full sit down dinners.  Often times, people arrived and sat down with the friends they already knew, and I usually never met a lot of new folks because I had to sit at one spot the whole time as others were already taken.  The dinners are fun and all but they do take a lot of planning.  If you did want to do this, my best advice would be to pick a firm date and time and place, otherwise the free for all planning by everyone would go on for pages as people debate the merits of a place, and why they can't make a particular day, etc.  Also, inevitably, people who have rsvped will cancel, and it's often last minute.  So dealing with the rsvps, the restaurant deal, and making sure people give you the right amount of money can be a big hassle.  Bills for 20 people are pretty big.  I find the mixers more social and freeing, in that everyone can pay their own bill, and show up or not show up and leave whenever they want.
