Tim Suermondt really knocked our socks off on the 21st of November, and he was just warming things up for our December reader - Melinda Thompsen!

Melinda teaches at Queens College and is an accomplished poet, most well-known for her accomplishments in that little-known genre - "basesball poetry."

So come on out and bring some poems or a story to share.  Waltz now has its beer and wine license, so if you can't have another cup of caffeine during this hectic holiday season, come in and relax with a bit of the vino or some suds!

Mel's Bio and Sample Poem
« Reply #1 on: December 03, 2005, 10:27:24 PM »
Hi all.  Here's some information about Mel and a sneak preview of one of her poems.  Hope to see you there on the 19th!

Melinda Thomsen’s poetry has been published in the New York Quarterly, Rattle, Main Street Rag, Spitball: The Baseball Literary Magazine and the anthology Blues for Bill: A Tribute to William Matthews.  In the 2004, she was a semi-finalist for the “Discovery”/The Nation poetry award. She lives in Manhattan and teaches Vocational English as a Second Language at LaGuardia Community College in Queens.  Her article “Art Appreciation and Vocational Training” is in the current issue of In Transit, a teaching journal published by LaGuardia Community College.  Another article, “Writing for Audiences: Interviews of Working New Yorkers” is forthcoming in the TESOL Series on ESL in Community Colleges.



Major League Salaries


Now, I find a 10 million dollar

salary reasonable for players

that stand sixty feet from Clemens

or Martinez. Their pitches spin



forward in white with red seamed

swirls like peppermint candies

that change into jawbreakers

and could shatter a face



like what happened to your

poor cheekbone.  The one

that pleats like a fan as you

smile or presses against mine



when the world  descends

in leaden sheets, was crushed

by a high school pitcher in Ohio

who had no clue of its value.



 - First published in Rattle, Summer 2005
