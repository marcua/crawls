Oh, the smoking at outdoor cafes irks me to no end.  The smoking regulations extend to outside cafes (only a small percentage--I think 25%-- of the tables can be a smoking section), and so many of the establishments in Astoria disregard the law.  I've pointed out the issue to cafe managers and on occasion have reported cafes to 311.  In addition to Grand Cafe and Brick, there's no smoking at the outdoor tables at Bare Burger, and I've never seen anyone smoke at the outdoor tables of Il Bambino or Fattys or Wave Thai.  Cafe Bar will enforce the smoking regulations if they aren't too busy or if pressed (they've been fined in the past).
