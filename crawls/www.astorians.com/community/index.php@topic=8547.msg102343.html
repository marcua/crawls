from one of my many random newsletters:
'Heroes' Creator Sorry For This Season...

The creator of the TV show 'Heroes' has apologized to fans for
shortcomings in the new season's plot.

Speaking to Entertainment Weekly, the brains behind the television
program, Tim Kring, stated that events in the second series were
taking too long to unfold and vowed to set right the show's problems,
which have resulted in a ratings slide.

He told the magazine: "We assumed the audience wanted season one a
build-up of intrigue about these characters and the discovery of their
powers. We taught [them] to expect a certain kind of storytelling.
They wanted adrenaline. We made a mistake."

Kring added that the show, about people with supernatural abilities
coming together to save the world, needed to reveal this season's
mission earlier. Subplots involving the love interest of blonde
cheerleader Claire and the time-bending adventures of time-bender
Hiro had also been mishandled, he said.

The maker of the show, which is currently out of episodes due to
the writers' strikes in Hollywood, added that the script was being
changed to rejuvenate viewer interest.

He stressed: "The message is that we've heard the complaints and
we're doing something about it."
