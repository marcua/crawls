Title: Why so much trash?
Post by: bennyt on September 30, 2006, 05:42:53 PM
Some people are going to be offended by this rant, and will probably perceive me as a snotty ex-Manhattanite who is part of some kind of problematic gentrification that is ruining your otherwise "authentic" outer borough experience, but...

Why does there have to be so much damn trash around here?  I took a long walk today, from 23rd and Ditmars all the way into the upper 40's.  And there's just trash everywhere, on the street, the sidewalk, on peoples' lawns.  In Manhattan, they have wastebaskets on EVERY street corner.  When I walk to the subway in the morning eating my banana, I have to carry the banana peel all the way to 31st st. before I have the chance to throw it out, unless it happens to be trash collection day and I can throw it on top of someone else's heap of stinking garbage.  I'm just a little annoyed that there ALWAYS seems to be broken glass in the middle of the street.  When there's a breeze, these little trash whirlwinds of crap eddy around and frankly, it's really not very nice.  Just get some damn trash cans out there, and I think that would solve half the problem- I mean, do people really need to be chucking Big Gulp cups and snapple bottles on the street?  I think if there were proper trash receptacles, this wouldn't be such a problem -- people wouldn't be shlepping their trash all over Astoria looking for a trashcan, only to give up after five blocks and dump it in the street.  I understand that it's a residential neighborhood, and most people probably dispose of their trash in their homes and then "take out the trash" or whatever.  But obviously, there are people who are buying things in the commercial part of Astoria and discarding the packaging in the residential part.  Or something.

There are many, many reasons why I prefer living here to Manhattan.  But the trash situation can be improved upon.  Believe me, I don't give a rat's ass that there's no Barnes and Noble here.  But the trash thing is a bit ridiculous.

ok, rant over.
bennyt
