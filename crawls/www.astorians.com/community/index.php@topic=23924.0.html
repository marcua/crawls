Quote from: Chinaski on January 10, 2013, 06:11:07 PM
Sorry, but a $2 tip on a $21 order is very stingy.


Maybe it is, but I look at things differently than most people. I have to. I'm 25, a full time college student with a full time job. I can't afford to enrich the lives of these delivery boys. Anyways, I think we can all agree that ordering for delivery is convenient, yes? Some may even go as far as saying, "the restaurant is doing us a favor by delivering." In my opinion, the whole point of delivery is to generate and serve more customers, which induces a better income, and to keep up with the competition. If all of that was of little concern, they wouldn't deliver. Delivery = more money for the restaurant. With that being said, restaurants employ "delivery guys" and most of the time these guys only have one job task, delivering food. These guys are getting paid to deliver. If you have more than one job task, one of which is delivery, you're getting paid to carry out your tasks, one of which is...again, delivery. So gratuity is not a necessity. It's just a token of appreciation. I always tip, but how much depends on the following: order total, distance, delivery time, extreme weather conditions, and the overall attitude/personality of the delivery guy. The first time I ordered from them, the delivery guy had an attitude before I even handed him a tip, thus a $2 tip was rewarded and I felt that was sufficient, especially considering I only live 3 min. away. The second time I ordered from them, he had a bigger attitude, thus another $2 tip was rewarded, only this time he decided to mumble something to himself before setting off into the sunset.

That's my interpretation of the delivery system. Maybe I'm fit for a straight jacket.
