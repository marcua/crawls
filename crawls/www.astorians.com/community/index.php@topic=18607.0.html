Glad this subject came up.  This is the letter I sent out last week:

To:
Queens Borough President Helen M. Marshall
Council Member Peter F. Vallone
Commissioner Adrian Benepe
Deputy Commissioner Therese Braddick
Queens Borough Commissioner Dorothy Lewandowski

I would like to begin this letter by stating that as a resident who lives in the vicinity of Astoria Park, I was against the concept of a skateboard park from the beginning.  At this point, it is well on the way to becoming a reality and I am resigned to the fact that those of us who live in the area will have to find a way to deal with the noise and the crowds it will bring.  Overall, I do agree with Council Member Vallone that the idea of a place for kids to practice their skills and have fun is a good one. 

We have other problems to contend with however; as summer approaches once again we are astounded to find our park full of trash and overflowing trash cans.  Memorial Day weekend was gruesome.  There was trash everywhere.  Imagine what the Fourth of July will bring. 

Simply put, it is amazing that so much money is being spent on another venue in Astoria Park which will bring more crowds and more trash at a time where it is obvious that the Parks Department is not capable of keeping up with the current trash problem.

Capital Expenditure Budget vs. Operations Budget is irrelevant to the residents of Astoria.  What we see is that a very expensive venue is being built and yet there are not enough trash cans, not enough staff and not enough trash pick-ups.

It is nice that your names are being memorialized at this skate park but it would be nicer if the park was cleaner.  The bottom line is that if the Parks Department can afford to build the skateboard park, you can afford to keep the park clean.  Please do not send a letter explaining how your budget has been cut.  In good years and in bad, the park is overwhelmed with litter during the summer months.  Also, to add salt to the wound, it is embarrassing to see so much money and work put into the new venue while noticing all the litter and lack of trash cans.

I personally spend a lot of time cleaning up the small field across the street from my house.  Right now, I fill two large trash bags daily.  As soon as the pool opens, I will need to use four or five trash bags every day.

Finally, the park gates are in a terrible state.  The gates that run between the Parking Lot and 24th Avenue were stripped by park staff but no one has appeared to paint them, so now they are down to the original metal without any paint!

Surely, the park will be made spotless for the grand opening ceremony of the skateboard park.  You will all stand proudly for the photographers and local press.  The reality is however that the park is filthy in the summer.  Our team will be documenting and photographing the situation so that we too can present our case to the local media during whatever ceremony occurs in the fall.

Please do something about this before the pool opens – we need more trash cans, more staff and more trash pick-ups.  And, before you say ‘we can’t afford it’, just remember that your names are going to be engraved somewhere in that very costly skateboard park!

Sincerely,
