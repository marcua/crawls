I don't remember that specific incident but it doesn't surprise me.  There was A LOT of bad blood between those two cafes, especially when Kolonaki first opened around 1996-97.  There was even a fire at Kolonaki a few months after it first opened, in the spring of 1997, that mostly gutted the second floor...it was rumored at the time that it was arson but I don't know if that was ever proven.  But regardless of what happened, there was definite bad blood between the two.

Kind of silly when you think about it...looking at the 30th Avenue example, each new cafe just brought more visitors to the area, and each new cafe after that increased the visibility of 30th Avenue as a cafe destination even more.  If something similar had happened on Broadway, everyone (Omonia, Kolonaki, the various cafes that have passed through the "cursed corner" at 34th and Broadway, etc.) would all have likely benefited.

Cafes do not make a vibrant community any more than restaurants make Chinatown a particularly desirable place to live. Indeed, for the people actually LIVING here they are a noisy pain. Crowds at all hours. I personally know a number of people who moved because they cannot put with with either hookah smoke, or noisy neighbors. Almost all of them were long term residents. Go to a community board meeting and on a regular basis see their representatives (at least on paper) all but laughing that them when they complain.

The point is a stroll around NYC and you see no place that has the sick shopping areas that we have in Astoria. Every place has landlords. Everyone has to pay rent. Every place the mom and pop guys are suffering, but why is Astoria afflicted with this uniquely to this degree?  :?

Because this problem is not isolated, but is systamatic. It is linked to a whole host of issues, from the chronic transit mess, the Con Ed grid, the cheap and shodding new buildings, terrible schools, littered streets, overcowded tenements, to the trashing of entire communties like Dutch Kills. We have a new element in our community that does not give two sh*ts about it.  :x

Take a close look at these merchant associations, civic groups, community board. What do you see?

The same people that have sat in these positions for decades.  :|

Do you see anyone fighting for the little guy? Anyone decrying a community slipping beneath the waves? 

No, nothing but wooden smiles and awards in the press. Every week, an enless series of articles extolling the same developers and old pols as heroes. Over and over again the same faces. A fantastic Alice in Wonderland quality that would be a dark comedy if it were not our home being trashed by the indifferent and rapacious.

We need new faces. We need new ideas. We need new directions. We need a host of Obamas.  :mrgreen:
