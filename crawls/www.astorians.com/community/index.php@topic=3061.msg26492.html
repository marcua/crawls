The Lesbian Landsmanschaft on 31st Avenue seems to have closed, and I don't know what's going to replace it.  I call it that because it seems to have been a club for Greek men from the island of Lesbos, and they had their sign only in Greek lest they attract gay women.  I used to point it out as a curiosity.

On 34th Avenue, across the street from the S'Agapo Restaurant, a Bravo International Supermarket is being set up.

The Corona Bakery on 31st Avenue has bitten the dust, and where the Miles Deli used to be the Astoria Candy Store is going to open.  Meanwhile, up the street, the Two Star Candy store has taken down its sign.  Is it going to move to the new place, I wonder?

Not that Two Star was good for much.  I used to go there for Diet Coke when the 20 oz. bottle was cheaper there than anywhere else.   But now that the price there is exactly the same, why bother?

The little Colombian bakery on the same block as Michael's has closed, and a French-style patisserie is supposed to open there.  A consolation for the absence of Edelweiss?
