Harissa on 30th Ave
« on: March 31, 2011, 02:29:17 PM »
I'm surprised no one has mentioned this place yet. I'm lurv with it--tiny little Moroccan-French cafe with hip jazz and a very sweet chef-owner. The menu is crazy cheap for what it is (organic eggs!). His omelette with french fries and merguez is genius--it's open face, and the fries are scattered on top, kind of cooked in to the omelette. Also, the brik are delish--nice Moroccan-spiced meat in crispy-fried phyllo. Everything else I've had has been really yummy too.

Oh AND: the guy sells Balthazar croissants and baguettes!  (Truly, this is the real reason I'm lobbying for him--it is freakin' fantastic to have a source for great French bread and pastry right around the corner from me, and I'd cry if he closed.)

The only drag is that he doesn't open till 10am, so you can't cruise by early for a croissant. But he's open way late into the evening usually.

Tried this place out last weekend.  We got the lamb tagine and a chicken kebab sandwich and everything was terrific.  The lamb tagine was part of their daily special that included soup, salad and bourek.  The bourek was very unique with eggs and steak.  The soup was a choice between lentil and chicken.  We chose the chicken and it was rustic and really hit the spot.  The lamb tagine was also very homestyle with peas and two plump artichokes.  It was an excellent dish, especially for the price.

It's a tiny place and even though there's some nice jazz playing, there isn't much ambiance.  Service was prompt and kind.

Here's a link to their yelp reviews:

http://www.yelp.com/biz/harissa-queens

And here's a link to their menu through seamless:

http://www.seamlessweb.com/food-delivery/Harissa-Cafe-NY-Metro.16681.r

Great cafe food at a very affordable price. :mrgreen:
