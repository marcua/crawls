Being that I've riden the subways here in Astoria since I was about 8-9, I would agree with those that say this is not a big deal.  Then again, when I was thinking about it, I only rode the RR and NN (yes, back when they used double letters and the "W" train didn't even exist) trains and buses in and around Astoria.  At that age, I never went to Manhattan by myself and I would never have gone to 39th Avenue or Queensboro Plaza by myself at that age.  And I don't think - now as a parent myself - I'd allow a child that young to travel the train into those areas either.

So I'm ok with the idea with a kid taking the train at that age...so long as the train line was ok, they're traveling for a definate purpose (school, friends, etc.) and his/her travel was limited to a certain area.  I wouldn't think a 9 year old kid taking the N train from Broadway to Ditmars would be a big deal at all.

I think 9 years old should be okay.  I see posts mentioning they wouldn't go to certain areas.  I do believe it is up to the parents to discuss limits.  In the OP's story, it was a downtown 4/5/6 train from 59th Street.
