I hope I'm just being paranoid, but as of about five days ago, I am starting to suspect I might have brought bed bugs into my house somehow. All this time I thought I was safe because I live in a fully detached private house.

The reason I'm not sure it's bed bugs is because my bites don't seem to look like the pictures of bb bites I've seen pictures of. My bites are tiny, about the size of a pin head. They itch like crazy - although for only a few minutes once or twice a day. Then, for the rest of the day, they don't bother me but for the fact that I've got tiny but visible bumps on my skin. Still, unlike mosquito bites, these bites seem to be lasting a helluva long time. At first, I thought that one of my three cats brought fleas in from out in the back yard. But none of my cats are scratching, so I don't suppose that's it. It seems to be something that much prefers humans over felines.

This past weekend, I stripped my bed down and washed everything in hot water, and dried it all bone-dry in my dryer at the highest temperature setting. I took my mattress and box-spring off the bed as well, and vacuumed the hell out of everything. As I did all this, I carefully inspected everything for the tell-tale signs of bed bugs, but saw nothing at all. And, just to be sure, I dusted boric acid all over my bed frame and box-spring before putting an entirely different set of sheets and blanket on my bed. So far so good. I don't seem to have had any more bites during the night since then.

BUT... now I'm starting to wonder if whatever is biting me is something lurking around at my job. These past few days, after my getting-ready-for-work shower in the morning, I've been giving myself a good all-over inspection - making a mental note of where my bites are and how many. But when I got home from work today, I notice I have a few new bites on my ankles that were not there this morning. So can it be there are bed-bugs or something else that have been attacking me at my job? If this is so, then I guess it's a given that whatever it is is probably hitch-hiking home with me.

I guess what I'd like to know is if anyone else here has had a similar experience. And, if so, did it turn out you had bed bugs or something else? And what did you do to solve the problem? All Ideas and suggestions are welcome because right now every few minutes I'm thinking I feel something on me, but when I look, can see absolutely nothing.

I have found bites to be a completely unreliable way of figuring out if you have bedbugs. I discovered them two months ago and only did so because I saw a spot of blood on my sheets. Neither I, nor my boyfriend, nor my roommate ever had any bites. We were just not reacting to them.

So no, I would not freak out if you have bites. It could be a lot of things. The best thing you could do would be to contact your landlord (if you rent) or call in an exterminator (if you own) to have an inspection done. They know what evidence they're looking for, and you don't. Same thing goes for work.

Oh, and it's too late now, but NEVER wash anything, move the mattress, etc. unless an exterminator has told you to. If you do have bedbugs it is very possible that you just drove them away from your bed into a harder-to-locate hiding place.

Bedbugs are hard to detect and even harder to get rid of. Don't wait.

2) I am going to move without a doubt. I will notify my landlord tomorrow so he can get an exterminator in. My question, to what extent do I have to worry about my things? Are my dishes safe? cd's, dvd's, books? framed photos on the wall? computer/electronics? Or is everything contaminated?

DO NOT MOVE. There is no reason to. With a proper schedule of treatment your belongings and apartment can be bedbug-free again, and if you move without proper treatment you will probably just end up bringing the problem to your new apartment.

Bedbugs aren't like fleas; it would be very unusual for them to hitch a ride on a cat.

Quote
4) Should I sleep on my couch or will they find me there too?

If you remove their food source (you) they will either go dormant or find a new food source (neighbor, your cats, or yes, they could follow you out to the couch).

I've had bed bugs before in the summer of 2008 and it wasn't fun. Eventually we ended up having to call Freedom Pest Control (www.expelpest.com) and paid alot of money to have them exterminated. We had to move most of the clutter out of the rooms sealed into black garbage bags so they could start the extermination. On the first meeting they steam cleaned, vacuumed, and poisoned all the cracks, bedding and crevices. They came back twice more to poison everything. They really know what they're doing and make sure to do a thorough job.

However, all the stuff we put in the garbage bags we had to wait for a hot day (95F or hotter) and put them out on the back porch so all the bed bugs got roasted inside :P.

After having bed bugs before, I'd say the best protection is to encase every mattress and box spring in your home, so that if you do get bed bugs they can't hide inside your bed.

If you'd like a cheap (and inexpensive way) to deal with bed bugs, remember their biggest weakness... They have to crawl to bite you! If you make everyplace where the bed touches the floor slippery (by putting the legs in a glass cup for example), they won't be able to get to you if your mattress is encased. You can also try using Diatomaceous Earth as well. (http://www.bedbugsguide.com/diatomaceous-soil.htm)
