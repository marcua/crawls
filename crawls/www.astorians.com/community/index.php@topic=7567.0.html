Finding a caterpillar in my food in my plate would freak me out, but I wouldn't swear off the food for the next four to five decades.  That's like me not going to the movies ever again because I got railroaded into going to see Runaway Bride (shudder!)  It is an extremely rare restaurant that does not have little critters and loads of bacteria.  Go look at the hands of the guy who puts together your salad.  That's alot worse then any caterpillar.

But the difference between chinese food for americans and chinese food for chinese is immense.  As different as Irish and French cuisines, no kidding.  So if you're thinking of giving the "cuisine" another go, go on over to Flushing.  There is a pretty clean place called Sweet-n-Tart Cafe, on 38th Avenue just off of Main Street.  The entire kitchen is exposed and can be seen by all the customers from the dining area.  Its not the best real chinese out there (because the best is from the dirty little hole in the walls where most americans wouldnt want to eat at) but is good stuff.  Alot healthier than american chinese food.  Their congee is excellent, noodles so-so.
