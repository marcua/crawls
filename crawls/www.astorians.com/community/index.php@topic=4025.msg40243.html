One addendum- you asked where the closest place to go would be.  Part of that depends on whether by "Jewish deli," you also mean "kosher deli."  If non-kosher works for you, I suspect that there is something good on the Upper East Side.  Kosher-wise, I've had fantastic sandwiches at Mendy's at 34th and Park: see my post here http://www.astorians.com/index.php?topic=3710.0 and also at Mr. Broadway (38th and Broaday) is quite good, if a bit expensive.  Both places have excellent corned beef, pastrami, chopped liver, etc.  Also, Kosher Marketplace on the Upper West (Broadway and 90th, I think) sometimes has half-price sandwich days, and it's pretty good (not as good as the other two places I mentioned, though).

Zura, do you want glatt kosher, plain old kosher, or is non-kosher OK with you?  If you don't keep kosher, try:

Arties Delicatessen and Caterers
2290 Broadway At West 83rd St
New York, NY 10001

(212) 579-5959

They're not kosher--they even serve ham--but they do serve those wonderful old-time Jewish dishes (but not giblet fricasee, alas).  And they deliver all over NYC.

If kosher's what you want, and you're on the Upper East Side, try

Pastrami Queen
1269 Lexington Ave
New York, NY 10028

(212) 828-0007
 
Isn't it a bitch that the Second Avenue Deli is no more?  Isn't a bitch that Phil's, the last kosher deli in Astoria, is no more?  And isn't it a bitch that there are no kosher restaurants in Astoria?  I feel sorry for Orthodox Jews who live or work in the neighborhood.
