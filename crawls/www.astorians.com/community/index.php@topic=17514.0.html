One of my best friends is a Tivo devotee who's been trying to convert all of our friends to Tivo users.  She has flat out said that she'll buy us a Tivo as a wedding present if we put it on our registry - which would be hard to do since our registries are with Macy's and BB&B.  Anyway, we've been weighing the pros and cons of Tivos vs our TWC DVR.  Basically it comes down to the fact that I hate our TWC DVR because we have the Scientific Atlanta Explorer 8300HDC box and although we get 100 hours of standard definition storage space, the damn DVR functions are so, so SLOW.  Just deleting an episode of a tv show takes 30 secs of more, while with our last box it was almost instantaneous. 

However, my friend wants to get us the new Tivo box because, even with 100 hours we fill it up really quick, and the Tivo box has a terabyte of memory.  This would be awesome, especially when we upgrade to an HD tv.  However, the biggest downside is with TWC's cable card that you put in the Tivo.   We've been told that you lose all On Demand channels.  This would suck.  We watch a whole lot of stuff from all the On Demand channels.

As for the savings.  Yeah, it would be almost a wash.  The cable card from TWC was $5 month (it may have gone up recently with all the other increases), but then you'd have to worry about the Tivo service fee.  So, I suppose if you pay either the 3 year or lifetime service, it'd be cheaper than going with TWC.
