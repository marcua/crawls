I was there for that.  I saw the whole entire thing, start to finish.
In my opinion, calling the police was absolutely appropriate.  The girls were obviously drunk, and if the one girl that was insisting on driving was allowed to operate that vehicle and mowed somebody down, surely that would have been more tragic than a few of us losing some sleep.

The problem with so many of these incidents is that when you call the police, they arrive on the scene WAY too late.
I've been in Astoria for quite some time, and with all the bars and lounges and young people around, I can't (and won't) call the cops for every little noise- but when there is something dangerous brewing, I call.  In this instance, I called the precinct directly and was advised by the desk officer to call 911.  I called 911, told them that there was a fight in progress (these girls were cursing at and punching eachother at one point) and that the possibility of a drunk driver getting behind the wheel existed, and of course, the girls were long gone when the police cruiser showed up.

These girls were camped out nearby, because I was observing for quite some time- one girl returned IN ANOTHER CAR to recover her smashed cell phone in the middle of the street, and a couple of these girls came back (discreetly) to the car to get some clothing and other items.

The other problem with these incidents is that you are at an extreme disadvantage being the home-owner/renter/resident.  These drunks/disorderlies KNOW WHERE YOU LIVE.  You have no idea where they live.  So if you yell out the window, or notify them that you're calling the police, they could retaliate, throw a brick through your window or worse.  Your best bet is to just call the police RIGHT AWAY so that they can respond ASAP, but do it discreetly so that the people in question have no idea who alerted the cops about their reckless behavior.

So to answer your question, I think calling the police is very appropriate.
