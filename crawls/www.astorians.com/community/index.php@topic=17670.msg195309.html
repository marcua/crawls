I haven't got a horse in this race one way or the other. While I'm grateful for Frei-Pearson's participation in the Marriage Equality movement, it always seemed somewhat of a political opportunist move rather than a sincere fight for the cause. It was (and is) a hot issue with Onorato as an easy target to go after, while gaining the support of Astoria's growing gay community and giving him the opportunity to get his name out there. As a member of that Astoria LGBT community, I always found myself somewhat suspicious of his motives, though I could very well be wrong.

And for what it's worth, I work in the child welfare field and among my colleagues, no one has a clue who he is, nor do I recall seeing him presenting at any professional conferences. Granted, while he would in all likelihood not have a clue who I am either, I probably wouldn't try to make my professional achievements seem like more than they are if I were running for office. It's a fairly small legal field. I know other people who work for his organization, and hear the names and know the reputations of some of his colleagues, but I can honestly say his name has never come up.

As for Simotas, I haven't a clue who she is, what she's done or what she stands for. I know she's gotten a number of endorsements, but that's about it.

If the only information a voter gets is from the two sides presented on the Astorians board from the warring factions, then I suspect a lot of voters might just stay home. The nitpicky fighting really doesn't help and certainly doesn't provide anyone with the tools to make an informed decision on election day.

Just my two cents.

Here in New York, we've had enough scandals to turn anyone into a cynic. But I can honestly say that I have no doubt in my mind that Jeremiah is a true friend and ally to our community. It's a rare individual that can reignite idealism in a State as beleaguered by corruption as New York. But I truly believe in Jeremiah's sincere devotion to the cause of equality as well as his devotion to a brighter future for all of us.
