Quote from: Christine on August 06, 2010, 09:43:39 AM
That really is a mystery: I can understand how a place like Costco could put a good fish market out of business; but how do the mediocre ones last longer than the quality shop??  :? Must be cheaper inventory and more of it.

That's probably part of it. Marino's seemed to me to put quality ahead of selection, and way ahead of customer service. Their hours probably dramatically limited their business (as other long, contested threads can attest to!). I also think that NYCMacUser's point about the increasing cost of fish is insightful. As overfishing has caused supplies of many fish to dwindle, and prices to increase over the decades, and as crop subsidies have slashed the cost of terrestrial proteins, it becomes more difficult for high-quality fishmongers to compete. Remember that lobsters used to be thought of as nearly inedible! Prisons used to be prevented from serving lobster to inmates too often, as it was thought of as cruel and unusual punishment! That's not the case anymore, and I suspect the fish industry, from sea to retailer, will see lots more stress in coming decades...

This is a serious issue in Astoria.  We try to do as much as possible locally - in Astoria - for dining, shopping, entertainment.  But so often it's the business hours that are a problem for us.  And it was the sole reason we could never enjoy shopping at Marino's.  Honestly, closing at even 7pm is too early for me.  I wish some shops would try a staggered schedule.  Maybe open 8 - 5 Mon - Wed, and then noon until 8pm Thurs - Sat.  This goes for a pharmacy, hair salon, or food related business.  I think MacUser is right.  It's back from housewife days - but it's time to realize current lifestyles are different.  I bet some shops get an early morning rush hour crowd and can even afford to close down a few hours midday and then reopen late afternoon into the evening.  That way the shopkeepers can do their own business during the day.  And like someone else said, for some of us, the work week is long and tiring.  I'm not looking to have to cram all my shopping in on Sat and Sun.  I actually like to relax and have some fun on my time off.  Needless to say, we do lots and lots of online shopping - including Fresh Direct - which also is a good source of fresh seafood.  And they have great hours.
