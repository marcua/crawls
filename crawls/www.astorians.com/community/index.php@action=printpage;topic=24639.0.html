NYU anthropologist Robin Nagle (http://www.wnyc.org/people/r/?n=Robin+Nagle) is a big fan of NYC’s garbage collectors and makes the case that theirs is the city’s most essential and most dangerous job.  She is a clinical associate professor of anthropology and urban studies (http://draper.fas.nyu.edu/object/RobinNagle.html) at NYU and the Department of Sanitation's first official "anthropologist-in-residence".  She shares the inside knowledge she picked up studying and working as one of "New York's Strongest" in her new book: Picking Up: On the Streets and Behind the Trucks with the Sanitation Workers of New York City.
