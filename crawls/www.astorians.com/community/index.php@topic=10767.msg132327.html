A little un-related, but not really:

Every year, I fundraise and participate in the MS Walk NYC to support my mom who was diagnosed 10 years ago.  For the past two years, the walk route (we go 10 miles) took us over the Brooklyn Bridge, through Brooklyn Heights, and back over into Manhattan.  Thousands of people participate and the pedestrian walkway on the bridge is not closed, marked, or roped off in any different way than it normally is. 

For the past 2 years, several asswipe bikers yelled at us as they rode by for taking up "their lane" on the bridge while they passed.  For the past 2 years, someone in my team has yelled back some choice words to them, something along the lines of we're so sorry to be walking 10 miles and taking up their precious bike lane - for ONE Sunday - in support of those who can barely move due to the crippling, painful illness.

All of this reminds me of that...just a little.  Count your blessings and pick your battles, people!
