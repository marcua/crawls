Hi, jr4266,

Based on my recent experience studying the rental housing market in Astoria, the approach a person uses to procuring an apartment can be dictated most dramatically by how much time as well as money he or she has. For example, since we live in Pennsylvania and I work full-time, I searched the Internet late at night for leads on apartments and roommates that we could check out during a one-day blitz visit to NYC. I lined up six apartments for us to see through a broker for convenience. If we had chosen one of the apartments shown to us by the broker, we would have paid the equivalent of one month's rent to the broker and one month's rent as the security deposit immediately, and the first month's rent by August first. That is the price of convenience (!).

If I had had more time, I would have tried lining up properties for which there was a "middleman" (locator company) because I figured that the company would have a vested interest in dealing with reputable landlords. (I know that that assumption may be naive.) My understanding is that you would have to contact the landlords directly and make appointments to see the apartments, but the locator companies provide a service by listing some properties that are exclusive to them and you can type in your criteria for location, price range, etc., so they are saving you the trouble of combing through the ads and making a list. Below are two companies that I found during my research (I am sure that there are more). I corresponded briefly with RDNY (rent-direct.com) and liked their tone and willingness to please.

http://www.rent-direct.com/ - The last time that I checked their fee was $179 for 30 days' complete access, but I think that they have raised it since then.

http://www.nycapartmentlocators.com/ - $39.99 for six months unlimited access to database with LANDLORDS PHONE NUMBERS UNBLOCKED which is updated 24 hrs a day 7
days a week in real-time

Of course, you can always make a list of broker-free apartments from craigslist and comb the on-line ads. Our ultimate "winner" was an apartment we found through an on-line ad with The Village Voice.
