Astorians,
  It good to see that there are a lot of "recombiners" (my word ) here--people that take pre-prepared products and recombine them into a totally different dish.  I do this myself, since it's really time and cost efficient.
  I'm always reminds of the time when I was unemployed a few year back and when I lived on "99 cent store" chicken parmigiana and other delicacies.

Recipe (serves six)

1 99 cent store package of six frozen breaded chicken patties divided into individual portions
1 pkg 99 cent store imitation mozzarella cheese product divided into individual portions
1 jar 99 cent store spaghetti sauce
1 box 99 cent store spaghetti

Boil the spaghetti
Cover the chicken patties with the imitation mozzarella cheese product and nuke until the faux cheese is melted
Plate the spaghetti, chicken patties, and sauce
Re-nuke until everything bubbles and smells delicious
Serve and enjoy!

Total cost: $3.96  Price per portion: $0.66

You can serve a great dish for six for the price of McDonald's "Value Meal"!  :lol:

But I digress...
To keep this on topic and Astoriacentric:
 Tomorrow's lunch, made this evening--Roasted Cherry Peppers stuffed with double smoked bacon, goat cheese feta, and onion and garlic.

  Yesterday, I bought eight largish cherry peppers from Berry Farms and the rest of the ingredients came from  either C Town and EuroMarket.

Recipe:
Roasted Cherry Peppers stuffed with double smoked bacon, goat cheese feta, and onion and garlic.

Cut off the top and stem of the cherry peppers (or any medium sized pepper of your choice)
Deseed and devein the pepper creating a hollow cavity
Line the inner wall of the pepper with Euromarket's double smoked bacon--one slice goes a long way
Insert goat cheese feta (also available at EuroMarket) into the cavity of the pepper
Press minced garlic and onion into the feta cheese
Place stuffed peppers on a cookie sheet and brush them with olive oil
Pop then into a pre-heated oven at 425F
Roast until the peppers are fork tender
Serve with Ajvar to taste and enjoy

  While, I'm sure that this isn't an original recipe--it is a recipe that I developed by using ingredients that are locally available in Astoria.
