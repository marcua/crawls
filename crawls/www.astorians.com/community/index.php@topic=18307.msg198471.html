I had dinner here last night.  I had the turkey burger with the original 5 nap burger fixins instead of the Italian fixins it comes with.  My sig other had the lamb burger.  Both were really flavorful, tasty and extremely thick but at the same time well cooked and juicy.  They were both impossible to eat with hands so the utensils (especially the steak knife) was useful.  I can't really say either one was my utmost favorite but they were different from the other choices in Astoria and I greatly enjoyed them both.  The fries were thin but too salty. 

We also ordered dessert.  The brownie sundae was typical, not mind blowing but satisfying and not too big.  The cheesecake was an absolute disappointment.  It was completely sour which seemed really odd.

They did a great job with the space with a huge bar area, lots of booths through out the edge of the restaurant and floor to ceiling windows.  It did remind me of chain restaurants, especially the big box space and the over-coddling of the bus and wait staff but I guess I still enjoyed the ambiance and being able to spread out in a way that is difficult in city life.  It was busy and pretty loud but there didn't seem to be much of a wait time for getting a table.  All in all, it wasn't a bad experience and if I'm in the mood for this type of burger and atmosphere, I would go back.  :mrgreen:
