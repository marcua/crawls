I was watching the show at the beginning of the season. 

I think he has a decent voice, but is a terrible performer.  He has no charisma onstage at all.  It's hard to describe.  Even when he's dancing around and trying to be theatrical it still lacks something.  It's like he's a robot. 

I'm hoping he wins, though, because I think the show is stupid.

What I don't get is that every season the winner is a stellar singer with a booming voice.  That's fine, but the thing is: that's not really what America wants.  That person is not America's idol.  Look at the Billboards.  Nobody on top of the charts is an outstanding singer.  It's rap, rock bands with weird names and pop stars like Justin Timberlake.  I like Justin Timberlake a lot, but he's not Luther Vandross when it comes to vocal ability.  There are some exceptions - Mariah Carey, Faith Hill, blah blah blah, - but for the most part, having the voice to perform "I Will Always Love You" is not what sells.

So, you get guys like Reuben, Fantasia, Clay Aiken, that Jay Leno looking guy, winning - or in Clay's case, coming in the top 2 - and then they vanish.  Maybe their first album does all right, but after that they're gone.  The two most financially and commercially successful winners - Kelly Clarkson and Carrie Underwood - were the two who were the most attractive and the two who sound the most like everything else on the radio. 

If I actually invested as much time in the show as what I just wrote implies, I would be pulling for Lakisha, because I think she has an amazing voice.  And there's a chance she'll win.  But what's the point?  She doesn't fit the criteria for commercial success, so she'll disappear, as well.
