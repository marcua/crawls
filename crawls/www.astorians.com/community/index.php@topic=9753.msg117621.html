To also respond to Bryan, I think the bias I was detecting regarding Greece and Cyprus had to do with playing down what those countries have been through....the "my tragedy is worse than your tragedy" scenario.  That, and the negative posts that always seem to crop up whenever, say, Cyprus is mentioned.  Certainly negative nods can't be quoted, but they're there for all to see and they do reflect the feelings of some people on this board who are choosing to remain nameless.  Since this is not the first thread where this has happened (I seem to recall the "Save Astoria" thread a couple of months back)....I'm detecting a negative pattern.

I don't think anyone in this thread is trying to diminish what the Jewish people have been through.  Quite the contrary.  I think, however, that some people are also trying to put things into perspective, by saying that...yes, the Jewish people have been through a lot, innocent people are still being killed, but the Israeli government, as well as the Palestinian and Arab governments, all share blame to a large extent for not solving their differences.  And certainly, violence on either side, as has been the case for over half a century, has made a solution even more difficult to achieve.  I also think, however, that there is nothing wrong with bringing in other conflicts and tragedies, such as what happened in Cyprus, in what is in essence a discussion that has a large historical and regional context to it.  It seems, however, that some people not only strongly disapprove about that, but have tried, either consciously or unconsciously, to belittle those other tragedies.
