I would actually say the quality of dialog overall has improved since the nods were added. And, I also don't assume that just because someone gets negative nodded that it's the same as saying "F U". For me it's a bit of a stretch to assume it means that because it can be just as easily argued that a neg nod simply expresses general disagreement in a respectful way. I have no reason to suspect that people's use of the nods is hostile.

The argument that nodding is "hiding behind anonymity" and "cowardly" doesn't fit for me because this entire forum is anonymous. I would argue that many of the posts that get negative nodded in the first place are ones that the poster would never express in a public setting because of the fact that they are posting on an anonymous site and "hiding behind the internet" (to borrow the concept). Even if we forced people to display their "names" when they nodded they'd still be anonymous.

The nods I think can help expose well thought out and respectful dialog vs trollish and immature conduct, and keep the conversation moving without people just posting "I agree", or "I disagree".
