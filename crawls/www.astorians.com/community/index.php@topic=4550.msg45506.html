Stir-Fry Related Dishes
You can throw in all kinds of leftover veggies or food into a kind of stir fry dish. It's pretty fast to make too when you have ingredients on hand.

1. Rice Cooker
2. Rice
3. Frozen Stir-Fry Vegetables
4. Leftovers

Mexican Nacho, Burrito, or Taco Salad
1. Tortilla Chips or Tortillas
2. Cans of pinto beans
3. Various veggies
4. Cheese of some kind
5. Dairy of some kind (sour cream, cottage cheese, ranch dressing)
6. Rice of some kind

Throw it all into a burrito or on top of tortilla chips.

Sandwich:
1. Mayo
2. Tomatoes
3. Bread
4. Salt
5. Some seasoning like dill

And of course Spaghetti:
1. Dry noodles
2. Sauce in a can
3. Leftover red wine
4. Veggies

Throw all the veggies in a pan and fry them, add red wine. Pour on the canned sauce. Makes a decent "home style" sauce for use with pasta.

Salad:
1. Hearts of Palm
2. Olives (Green/Black)
3. Nuts of some kind

I keep those on hand so I can just grab some tomatoes, onions, lettuce on the way home to throw together a salad.

That's some of the main ones I try and keep on hand I think.

Here's a good representation of what I keep around:

General

garlic
onions
extra virgin olive oil
toasted sesame oil
kosher salt
full array of spices
mustards
vinegars
hot sauces
condiments (ketchup, mayo, soy sauce etc.)
chicken broth (usually Pacific organic in the box)

Canned goods

tomatoes in various forms (whole, crushed, diced, sauce, paste)
Trader Joe's light coconut milk
hearts of palm
garbanzo beans
great northern beans
black soy beans
light tuna (not white because it has higher mercury content)
Trader Joe's chunk white meat chicken (good in a pinch for chicken salad-type lunch)
other canned fish -- sardines, mackerel, etc.
water chestnuts
things like capers, giardiniera (especially the pickled cauliflower), pickled jalapenos.

Also a few ready meals, mostly from TJ's -- their version of Tasty Bite Jaipur Vegetables, for instance, and a couple of tuna curry meals they offer.

Dried goods

quinoa
chana dal
green split peas
several kinds of lentils
wakame
nori
bonito shavings
krupuk (secret vice)
falafel mix

In the freezer I keep assorted frozen vegetables, which I try to get from TJ's.  I love their frozen green beans, for instance -- they are incredibly fresh and good.  I also have extra broths and sauces for quick meals. 

I buy vegetables and protein on a daily or every-other-day basis, unless I'm in one of my periodic states of trying to be organised and plan our meals, in which case I buy a bunch of stuff over the weekend and cook up batches of food.  The problem with that is by the end of the week I'm not in the mood for what I planned, so it's awfully tempting to end up ordering in, and what I've carefully planned and made ends up being thrown out.

This is a topic very close to me heart. As most of you know, I can't go outdoors in extreme weather. That makes shopping quite an ordeal for me. A few years ago I finally had to concede that my kitchen and pantry were not conducive to my getting a good meal together for myself. So I did the following:

a) I ripped out my old kitchen (which I know most of you can't afford to do) and started with a new floor. Keeping the kitchen clean can be a huge chore if you don't have easy to clean appliances and surfaces. I put in a self-cleaning oven and a refrigerator with a huge freezer.

b) I broke down my little kitchen pantry and rebuilt a closet across from the kitchen as a pantry. I put in shelves from top to bottom and then placed plastic, expandable shelves on every shelf. That way I can just look in and see everything because nothing is hiding behind something else.


c) I made inventory lists for everything important to me; pantry, freezer and spices.

I use coupons prodigiously. If I can get something at half price or less (I really like free!), I buy it as an extra. I only buy stuff that I use. That way I know I won't run out. If I had only been doing this when I worked and had my children living at home I could have saved myself a whole lot of money (on delivery and take-out) and I wouldn't have had to stop at the grocery store every night on my way home trying to figure out what to make for dinner. Keeping organized has changed my life—and saves me a lot of money. Also, finding out that I can freeze whole meals allows me to cook in big batches that I *don't* have to eat 4–5 days in a row!
