From the MTA website:
"MTA New York City Transit (NYCT) will be performing major upgrades to the Astoria’s N Line equipment, including the replacement of switches and other track work that will increase reliability and reduce delays.  The new switches will also reduce noise along the 31st Street corridor."

Here is the link to the full MTA notice: http://www.mta.info/news/2017/05/10/astoria%E2%80%99s-n-line-undergo-major-track-upgrades

As far as the replacement bus service, I was dreading it. On Saturday, I took the Q69 down to the F train. It was very crowded. On Monday, I decided to brave the replacement bus and we made very good time down to Queens Plaza, though traffic was very light due to the holiday. Coming back from Queens Plaza, it was very busy because everyone arrives at the same time.

I know you can't please everyone but the MTA pulled out all stops to ensure riders were well served. Buses left every two minutes or less. If there weren't enough buses or if people had to wait for them, there would be an uproar.
