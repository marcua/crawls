I respectfully disagree!  Once a parent feels comfortable meeting indoors with other parents and kids (some parents/kids are worried about germs and very young babies, for instance), it's time to get out!

Good for the parents AND the babies!  Good for bonding, too......

I wouldn't want to speak for anyone else, but I will say that the classes that were discussed so far here (parent and baby at the yoga room, music for aardvarks) could be attended by moms with young babies.  The classes at "my gym" are for slightly older kids.

Parents need opportunities to socialize, too!  The classes are an opportunity to bond in a different way with your child, as well as to meet other parents of young babies/kids.

I had a new baby, no family near by, few friends with young kids, and was home not working for the first time in my adult life.......I needed to get out so I signed up for mom/baby yoga class in Williamsburg when my son was about 2 months or 3 months old.  It was great!  I got to exercise, and got a chance to talk to other people going through the same thing that I was going through, and got to bond in a very fun and loving way with my baby.

If the weather is good, it's nice to find another parent to go on a stroller/carrier walk with when the babies are young.......

If I stayed in my house alone with a tiny infant, I would have gone crazy!
