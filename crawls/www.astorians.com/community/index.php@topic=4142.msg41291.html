Supreme Flounder (best fish dish)?
« on: November 16, 2006, 01:45:01 PM »
What's the single best fish dish you have ever had at a restaurant in New York?  I'm not sure how I would answer this myself - I remember thinking that Milos Estiatorio (Greek, midtown) was incredible, but I went there so long ago I don't even remember what I had.  The tastiest fish experience in recent memory may very well have been the devilled (grilled) kingfish at Sigiri, a Sri Lankan restaurant in the East Village.  The fish was grilled with a sweet (almost barbecue-esque) but HOT sauce (we ordered mild, and it was the hottest mild I'd ever had - my wife and I were sweating, and we're used to spicy food; I can't even imagine what "spicy" at Sigiri would entail). The fish is cut into pieces and tossed with grilled onions and tomatoes.  Niiiice (in Borat accent).

And no, I don't want to hear about salmon poached in the dishwasher.

The problem is, I can't really decide.  Part of wants me to say the matjes herring at Aquavit (which you can also get in the cafe at lunch for quite a reasonable price), but I can also quote myself from a couple of years ago, also from the bite tasting menu at Aquavit:

Sautéed halibut served with seafood (including scallop) sausage with marjoram and bacon topped with sautéed nettles; yellow pea puree and raisin-red wine sauce. The sautéed halibut was possibly the best fish I have ever tasted. The top of it was deeply seared, and I swear it tasted like a French fry. The seared part was perfectly golden brown, and then the rest was just perfectly cooked – the exact instant of being cooked. Buttery, meltingly tender and rich. The sausage was almost a mousse – or mousseline. Wasn’t too sure about the nettles.

I know both of those are Aquavit, and I've had incredible fish other places, too.  I'll be back.

Ok, that didn't take long.  Right now, my favourite sushi is at Sushi Seki -1st Ave. between 62nd and 63rd.  Recently, I said:

Everything - the rice, the fish, the garnishes, the wasabi - was harmonised in a way that had never occurred to me.  The mackerel and hamachi were both exemplary -- the mackerel had its typical deep savoury flavour that only lasts while the fish is very fresh, and the hamachi was perfectly presented, tender and flavourful.  I couldn't believe the umeshisu roll [ok, no fish in that one].  It's usually a pungently sour in a way I find very attractive - it's almost a palate cleanser.  This one was different.  Oh, it still had the same flavours, but there was a deepness and smokiness I had never tasted - and somehow, the flavours just blended together, nothing standing out, it was fantastic.  It was definitely partly the rice, which was perfectly balanced.

Hmm, also the salmon at Lil Bistro 33, for which we all must wait for the reopening on Ditmars.  That make me happy.
