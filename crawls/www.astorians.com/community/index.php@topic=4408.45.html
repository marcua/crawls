Not sure what your experience at Stamatis has been, but my husband and I have been going for at least 20 years, since we met.  It was in a different location back then, up the block.  We had two bad experiences with service.  Otherwise, it has been consistent.  The food is as good as it was 20 years ago.  The octopus is moist, the porgy grilled well, the lamb excellent, the moussaka and pasticho is outstanding.  Anna and Stamatis are there, watching what's going on and tasting the food to make sure it's good.  Whenever people come from out of town, we take them there and they wind up asking to go again.  In fact, we are taking a friend from California there tomorrow night, who is a fourth generation Greek American and wants to see Astoria.  Stamatis is always there for us when we would like friends to taste authentic, down to earth, no fuss Greek food.

Quote from: essen on June 02, 2007, 03:50:13 PM
I love the food and atmosphere at Agnanti, and the people who work there are nice, but the service is really inconsistent in that you never really know if you're going to get what you ordered. Was that your experience too or something else?

I loved the food at Agnanti as well, as their menu offerings deviated from the norm for most Greek establishments in Astoria and were quite yummy too.  That being the case, my problems with the place resulted from a couple of separate visits.  One time, when two separate appetizers I ordered came to the table cold and hard, as if they had been taken out of a refrigerator or freezer, plopped on a plate and sent to our table.  What compounded the situation was when the waitress insisted that those appetizers should be served cold, when I had eaten those same appetizers before and they most certainly were never cold.

Another time, someone at my table ordered the porgy, which the waitress said was excellent that day.  The fish that was brought to the table was most definitely not a porgie, and in fact, we tried to figure out what it was, to no avail.  The restaurant did take it back, but shame on them for serving a different fish than what was advertised.

Worst experience and which is what did it for me and the people I went with regularly was when we arrived one evening, and of course, the place was packed, both inside and out.  We stood by the door to wait for a table and were never acknowledged by the manager, who was sitting at one of the outside tables chatting with friends the entire time.  This is after we had become fairly regular customers.  However, when other customers came after us, she immediately got up and acknowledged them, still without acknowledging us in any way.  It was pretty rude and at that point we decided to leave. I believe we ended up at Stamatis that evening.

Ultimately, it was the service that did the place in for us, and it's a shame because I enjoyed the food there (when it was warm, that is!).
