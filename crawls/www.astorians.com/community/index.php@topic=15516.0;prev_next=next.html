Broadway show that an ultra-suburban, jock'ish 25 year old guy would like ... Rock of Ages! Can't go wrong with this one. :mrgreen:

Rock of Ages is a fun show - I saw it a few months ago and it's great.

Check broadwaybox.com - they have a section where they list discount codes for all shows,
both Broadway and off-Broadway.

There was one for Rock of Ages when I went, but check and see the expiration date.

I have to say though -  it's worth the face value of the ticket. It's fun, energetic, great songs
and the whole ensemble is super.

Another show  is Hair - that also has discount codes. Great energy.

Wasn't sure about your brother's dates, but the US Open starts at Flushing Meadows the last week of August
The week or two weeks before they have qualifying rounds where you can see up and comers try to qualify
for wild card spots. And the top players are often around practicing. I think the qualifying rounds are free.
Check usopen.org for info.

Good luck.

When he comes up for a 4th visit, I'd suggest Blue Man Group as a show- Letterman tickets are usually sold out years in advance while BMG is much easier (unless it's a holiday or a weekend.)

Cheap, great restaurants: Hellgate on the Park, Pita Hot, Seva, Sunswick. If he wants more trendy-ish, then Cavo or Plaza Lounge. For drinks, the Beer Garden on a weeknight, or Studio Square. The Ravel hotel next to the Queensboro Bridge has a rooftop bar that's pretty cool on a nice evening.

Stuff to do: Rent bikes in Flushing Meadows Park, or a free walking tour in Queens or Central Park (search online- usually kinda early on Saturday or Sunday mornings.) Water Taxi has $20 hop on/off tickets that are a blast (and bike-friendly)- great way to explore parts of Brooklyn and see the city from a different perspective.
