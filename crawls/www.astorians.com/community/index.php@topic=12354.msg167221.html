Congrats on purchasing a Canon point and shoot -- Canon has consistently made some of the best point and shoot cameras pretty much since digital photography began. But for anyone who might check out this thread looking for suggestions I'm going to second the recommendation for the Panasonic Lumix line of point and shoot cameras since they are pretty much the cream of the crop these days when it comes to digital point and shoot cameras. I'm also going to add a mild warning that while I own and love my Nikon DSLR, Nikon doesn't make the best point and shoot cameras available and in fact many people might even rank their current offerings pretty poorly. However as I just said if you're looking to step up to a DSLR Nikon is the current king of the crop.

Hope this info helps someone.  :-)

If you want more check out dpreview.com or imagingresource.com -- they're both great online sources for reliable reviews and digital photography news.

You might consider the new micro-four-thirds format of electronic-viewfinder interchangable-lens cameras. The Panasonic Lumix DMC-G1 is getting pretty good reviews, and it has a really nice combination of features. It's basically an SLR camera, but without the "R" part, so no heavy mirrors and lenses. But you get a larger CCD than with a standard point-and-shoot, you get interchangable lenses, you get an external flash mount, and it's a bit cheaper than an entry-level SLR. This is the camera I want to get to replace my Lumix FZ-20...

Camera lens are what makes a camera, not the body.  Always try to get the best lens you can afford.  Good lenses have special glass that that improve color and sharpness.  Having a nice camera body with all the bells and whistles is an expense you really don't need.  You will probably never use half the features anyway.  I would buy a cheaper camera body and save up for a better quality lens.  I use Canon.  Since I bought better lens (the L lens) my photography has improved 100%.  My pictures are much more sharp with accurate color.
