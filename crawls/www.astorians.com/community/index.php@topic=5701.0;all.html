The vendors do not care at all about your taxes.  They just want to make sure you have the paperwork so if anything comes down the pipe, their butts are covered.  If you are producing your own product and you only take cash, you aren't going to have to report dime one, of course, everyone is always advised to properly pay their taxes.  The tax ID number is only going to come into play if you buy product wholesale and wish to avoid being double taxed.  The system isn't built to catch folk in your position. 

Costs are cheap.  One time registation fee of $40, plus about $125 per event.  Tax ID and getting a vendor license are quite cheap.  For info on how to get a license, check out the NYC government website, Department of Consumer Affairs.

Hi, everyone! I just joined this community (about an hour ago, actually!) and I love the ideas you are putting out!  I actually don't live in Astoria yet, but I'm planning to move there by August 1st. I am a part-time photographer, full-time Ph.D. student, and I have wondered for a long time how I could go about selling some of my photographs in NY.

I do have a NYS Vendor's License and a Sales Tax ID #. (In response to one person who mentioned making the quarterly phone call: The NYS tax department started a new thing either this year or last where if you have 0 sales tax to report over a certain period of time you can qualify to make only ONE call a year: in March during tax season.  I love this, because I haven't had any sales tax to report since I got my license for a street fair in Nyack, NY back in 200... something, and I keep a note reminding myself to make that annual call in with all my other tax stuff: so when tax time comes around, it all gets done at once!) And I agree with the statement that the Street Fair administration doesn't care about whether or not you collect the sales tax, they just want to make sure you are properly documented in case any inspectors show up. I found this out in Nyack: I went a little crazy to get my license in time (it turned out to be the easiest bureaucratic process I have ever experienced) only to have no one at the fair ever inspect my booth, even though the application had clearly stated that every vendor had to have their license displayed at all times.  Plus, regardless of whether you get inspected or not, the license is a good to have and it doesn't cost much of anything to get, and almost nothing to keep.  But be warned: if you get your license, let your reporting lapse and then want to start it up again, there is a $50 fee to get it activated again. This applies any time you miss filing or calling in a report.  But since reported a Zero Tax Due report is only one phone call, it makes sense to just keep it active even if you aren't doing any business. I have keep my license active, filing Zero Tax Due, for the last five or so years, and so now it's ready and waiting for me to get off my butt and actually sell some art!

About my photography: I should really call myself an amateur because I don't have my own darkroom and don't really sell my work professionally, for my living, or even often. I shoot a lot (I mean, a LOT) during my travels, but getting prints made is always put on a back-burner due to my busy school schedule. I am a Ph.D. candidate in Art History/ Archaeology, and I mostly shoot ancient and historical monuments and artifacts.  My first (and only) street fair experience was thrilling, but was also a bunch of work since I did it alone.  However, because of it I am now a proud owner of a severely under-used Costco Canopy of my very own that I would be happy to share, should the need arise. (It's about 10 ft. square? with a white canopy. I think we have the same one!)

Of course, since I haven't moved yet I don't think I'll be able to participate in anything this summer: But I'm posting this reply because I really like the idea and would love to stay in the loop for future possibilities. And if all goes as planned and I become a newly-minted Astorian by August, I'd love to meet some of you and share artistic ideas like this.

So, Thanks! Hello to All! And here's to future conversations!
-Rose
