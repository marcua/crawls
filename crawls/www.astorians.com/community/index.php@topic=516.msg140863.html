thanks for the welcomes!

I do love Astoria Park, elkelorraine. there is always so much to see, whenever you go!

Bentleys Dad, wow, 7 dozen zucchini flowers!!! amazing. did you fry them up? yum. The squash borer is a flying insect that deposits its eggs actually inside the squash plant's stems. so when the larvae hatch, they start chowing down on the insides of the stems, nasty. once they are there there's not much you can do, although we've found sites that recommend trying to kill the larvae by poking pins into the stems or using a razor blade to excise them. The problem is that although you can tell they're in the plant somewhere (flowers don't lead to healthy fruits, stems begin to wilt and blacken for no apparent reason) it's hard to know exactly where -- and if you go poking & slashing everywhere it's not so good for the plant either :-( Apparently you can also try wrapping tin foil around the stems when they're still small, which theoretically prevents the adult from poking her ovipositor through the stem. Maybe we'll try this with a second crop, there should be time if we do it now!
good luck with the tomatoes!

as for the books -- agreed, but we've gotta make some room for the tot :-)
