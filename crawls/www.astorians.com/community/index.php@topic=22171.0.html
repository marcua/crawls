Running in Astoria?
« on: January 17, 2012, 02:32:44 PM »
Hello, all.

I lived in Astoria back in 2007.  At the time, I was a smoker and didn't do much for exercise.  In 2008, I moved to Brooklyn Heights, quit smoking and took up running.  Since then, running has been a regular part of my life.

I am considering moving back to Astoria (not set on it, but it's a possibility) and am wondering about the possibilities for running in the neighborhood.  Obviously, you can run anywhere, but right now I run about six miles when I run and it's a pretty consistent run, since I run over the Brooklyn Bridge and on one way streets in Brooklyn Heights.  By that, I mean it's not stop and go at intersections. 

I know Astoria has Astoria Park, which is, according to a Website that I looked at, around 1.5 miles around the perimeter.  Doing that twice, I'd make half my regular run here.  Not bad, but not enough.  I would think that running around it four times would be a little boring.

I guess I'm sort of looking for advice or stories or routes or whatever from other runners about the "running in Astoria" experience.  This is an important factor in whether or not I move, believe it or not.   

Thanks for any help.

For my long run - I go from Astoria Park up 21st street - over the 36th ave bridge to Roosevelt island and then around the island (4 mile loop around island, 8 miles or so total).  It's a nice run - all on paths and right by the water.  Noone bothers you - very peaceful.
 
Hells Gate Runners meet at Astoria park on Wednesdays I believe if you want to meet up with other runners.
 
Running from Astoria park down by the powerplant and over to the Steinway factory is another nice run.
 
If you hop in your car or on the 7 train and go over to the Worlds' Fair Grounds in Flushing you can get quite a good run in.

I am by no means a marathon runner, but I do a standard 5k or 4 miles a couple of times a week.

Right now I am on the treadmill due to the winter, but when its nice I do enjoy the scenery running in Astoria.

I live in the Ditmars area, so there are many different options for me. Sometimes I run down to the park, and then run over to like 30th ave, back up past 31st St, and back home.

Sometimes when I want a quiet, peaceful run I go uptown, towards the airport. It turns very residential up there, and is a nice change of pace.

Other times I run down 31st St all the way to 36 ave and back- its fun to run the length of Astoria and see how it changes with each avenue.

I always try to run a slightly different route each time- eventually I will have covered just about all of Astoria.

The only downside to running through the neighborhood is the fact that its very hilly in certain areas. A 3 mile run on flat land can be a breeze, but doing it uphill is BRUTAL

I used to run/walk the RFK/Triborough. I gave up mostly because of the bikes. It is just too dangerous. Biking over that bridge is illegal, with signs conspicuously posted, but why would that stop anybody? That particular path is too narrow for both biking speedsters and pedestrians -- yet bikes simply command more space and privilege than is equitably available on that path. I don't bike, and thus I don't use that bridge much any more.
Someday the city might open the path on the south side of the bridge? There was development on that side last  summer (I think). Maybe then we could, like bridges in other cities, have one pathway for bikes, and one for pedestrians?
The RFK/Triborough walkway is probably safer this time of year, with the cold air and 40mph wind gusts, but the first part of the Bronx-bound walkway is pretty hairy. You are like 4 or 5 feet from trucks and buses hurtling past at 70mph ... and you get to ingest those tasty bus fumes and truck exhausts blowing straight into your beautiful face. Good times. (Thank you, Robert Moses.) With the tailpipes and carbon-monoxides the first part of the RFK/Triborough is a lung-polluting and thus somewhat counter-productive exercise route, if you ask me.
The upper level of the bridge is pretty cool, once you get there (leave your suicidal tendencies behind). The top of the bridge offers a pretty sweet view of the Hell Gate Bridge, but a better view is from the park on Randall's Island.
This is likely of no interest to anyone here but me, but I usually do not carry a cell phone. When I do carry one it for some reason does not work over or around the East River. The last time I was there I found no emergency phones for pedestrians on the RFK/Triborough, as there are on the Ed Koch/Queensboro. Just sayin'...
For running and prancing and Skipping-to-Ma-Lou these days I choose Rainey Park. Lots of hills and during weekdays the place is mostly deserted. Once in a while I stomp on a sacrificed chicken or rooster, though, so watch out.
Unfortunately, the last time I tried to visit that park (few days ago) it was closed to the public.

I'm a big fan of running along 20th ave and (for long runs) connecting that to Vernon Blvd via Shore Blvd -> 12th St, and so on.  20th Ave has a gradual incline which turns into a long decline, and the rest (except for going between Shore Blvd and the start of Vernon Blvd) is pretty flat.  Another great plus is the low amount of cross streets you have to deal with (running past the entrance to Costco can be a pain though, depending what time you do the run).  Sites like runningahead.com and mapmyrun.com are good to check for existing routes that other runners have made (or to plan your own route).
