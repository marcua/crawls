Post by: mm on September 04, 2015, 12:46:17 PM
Part of the problem is that rather than request decent stuff at regular older Astoria venues people go to "fancy" places which are really overpriced and surprisingly shallow. Their uniqueness is rooted in a 'rustic' interior, fancy names of dishes, and a much higher price; meaning they're all different in exactly the same pretentious way essentially. So they charge more because of the perception by the newbies that it's somehow just as hip as Bushwick, eh, I mean Williamsburg, eh, I mean Park Slope, eh, I mean Dumbo, eh, I mean East Village once was..... but it's just overpriced hipsterness.

Pancakes are an excellent example (yeah, I know it's a coffee house thread). You go to a greek diner and order pancakes and you get average stuff. Then you get fancy crepes, which are pancakes but thin, at $10, and that's some sort of progress for society. End result is that the diner raises its prices for their (now relativ to price) crappy pancakes. So now we have overpriced crappy pancakes, and overpriced thin pancakes.

Same with coffee. Rather than get better stuff at regular places with less price increase the newbies want the overpriced fancy stuff because of the hard to resist thin-jeans-clad bearded lumberjack serving the beverage (or 60's-wardrobe-inspired lady).

Speaking of sub-par local coffee, this appears to be me at my bitterest..... but looks can be deceiving... I'm sure I can do worse...
