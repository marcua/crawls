Ok, so after a year or so of construction and the such, the Staples on 31st finally opened last Sunday.  I have to say, it was probably the weakest opening of a store I've ever seen in my life.  Did anyone else notice this?  Staples has got to have a marketing/ advertising department right?  I think they dropped the ball on this one.  I only knew it was open because I saw someone walk in as I was walking past.  The most impressive thing about it is the mere fact that it looks like a million bucks inside compared to the skeezy MET that used to be there.  The transformation is really pretty incredible.  Past that, it's a Staples with triple the normal amount of employees, many of whom roam the aisles and try to get you to take surveys.  I walked out after about 2 minutes.  Nothing great about it.  I really wish they would've put a CVS or something there.  Probably would have been a lot more functional of a store.

A lot of stores open before the official opening.  If a store is ready early, it starts generating income and let's the staff (especially staff members new to a company) work more hands on.  A "soft" opening before whatever more major promotions happen. 

Having said that, I wonder what they were thinking location wise...and why build over the windows on the street that could have been used for display?  Seems like they didn't adapt the store layout very much to the location.  And with a lot of foot traffic, why no put an opening in the fence closer to the door/subway?

I would guess in stores in this type of location they plan inventory mostly around home users. I'm sure there is some formula for city center stores vs residential areas etc.  Still, I don't know how long it will last unless they have a great deal on the rent.
