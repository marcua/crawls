These are the community guidelines for this forum. They are a work in progress.

Formal Rules:

Proposed - Spamming is not allowed under any circumstances. A spammer is someone who posts an unsolicited advertisement outside the business category. Posts that are considered "spam" will be moved or removed immediately and the spammers account may be suspended.

Informal Guidelines:

Proposed - Statement to the effect that since this is an online community that everyone's opinions and ideas are welcome here. There is no social requirement to be active and participate online.

Proposed - Ask people to self-edit. To try and stay on topic in the thread, and to put their threads in the proper category.

Greetings all,

The moderator group has been thinking that it might be helpful if we (everyone) were to start thinking about some basic formal and informal guidelines for etiquette on astorians.com. It might be helpful for us to talk about what we think this online community should be, and how we'd like people (including ourselves) to engage each other. We think this is better than just coming up with guidelines on our own and imposing them.

I'd like to divide these into two categories to start with.
Formal: Formal rules for the forum that moderators will work to maintain throughout the forum. These are offenses that could result in immediate action from a moderator, including banning or censoring of individual posts or users accounts. These are the hard rules.

Informal: Informal rules of conduct for the forum that both new and existing members can use as general guidelines. These might be matters of formatting, topic placement/organization, debate style, or pointers to help the conversation flow well. These are "soft" rules that we as a group generally agree on as principals we'd like to try and stick to as a community. These are informal guidelines that may or may not result in moderator action.

As the conversation plays out, I'll update the first post in this thread to reflect the consensus of the group on each point.

If you have suggestions for either formal or informal guidelines, please suggest and discuss them here. State whether or not you think it should be a formal or informal rule, and give a brief reason for proposing the guideline. It's okay to start from scratch and assume that there are currently "no" rules in place for what you are suggesting. It's also okay to be a hypocrite and suggest something that you haven't been doing yourself. The purpose here is to discuss some formal and informal guidelines that may help shape the feel of the community going forward.

I'll be actively watching this thread and keeping it on topic so please do your part to keep this thread consise.

Let's come up with some guidelines as a community that we can feel good about.

I'll start:

I propose that we come up with a definition for what a "spammer" is, and that as a hard rule we not tolerate spamming on this board.

I personally consider a spammer to be someone who just posts an advertisement to an inappropriate category without any attempt to engage anyone in discussion. I think of sitting in a cafe sipping a tea with a friend and having someone come storming in trying to sell pirated DVD's at the top of their lungs.

I also consider a spammer someone who posts advertisements that are unrelated to "astoria" in any reasonable way. Like someone who posts random links to porn sites.

Someone who has a legitimate local business though I don't consider a spammer if they are a) talking about their business or service in the context of an existing conversation where people are looking for that service [i.e. "I'm looking for ____"] or b) are posting their advertisement to the business section or the calendar (if they are having an event)

I think that commercial advertising that it not in the context of an existing conversation should be limited to the "business" section, and that those who are in violation should be warned and their post moved. If they don't get it and keep spamming the general categories I suggest we consider banning them. In the case of random porn spammers I think they should be banned immediately.

I think it's perfectly okay to talk in general about something you experienced. However, our current "policy" is to draw the line and invading someone's individual privacy.

What that means to me is:

1. It's okay to talk about a "business", but not okay to post the full name (or home address etc) of someone who works there.
2. It's okay to talk about any situation with an individual, but you should not use the forum as a tool to invade that person's privacy. Anything you talk about should be in general terms.

If someone wants to "out" someone else, and post personal information etc, I think the most appropriate place to do that is in a personal blog (which you might link to from this site - just not host it here).
