Title: Halloween Carrot Salad
Post by: Starwind51 on October 30, 2014, 12:37:50 AM
This is actually "Gujerati Carrot Salad" from "Indian Cooking" by Madhur Jaffrey, but the colors, bright orange and black, are perfect for Halloween.  The directions say to grate the carrots coarsely, but I put chunks of carrots in a food processor and grind them fine, in batches.  I also make about 3 times the amount, using 3 1-pound bags of carrots, or 1 3-pound bag, and tripling the other ingredients.  You don't have to measure exactly, everything's to taste.  My 7 oz bag of Maya brand black mustard seeds, purchased about 6 months ago, cost $1.29 in Trade Fair's Indian section, and will make many many batches of salad and are used in other Indian dishes, so all the ingredients are inexpensive.  You can use just about any cooking oil.  If you're new to mustard seeds, don't go anywhere while they're on the fire, though you won't be the first cook to have them fly out of the pan if you have the fire too high or don't take the pan off fast enough.  The mustard seeds add a smoky flavor, and they don't taste anything like the mustard you're used to putting on hot dogs.  The only other things I can think to tell you is that 3 pounds of ground carrots take up a lot of room so plan on a big bowl if you make that much, and that it's hard to judge what one carrot looks like when ground so it's very easy to over-indulge in this instantly addicting salad.  Because the salad is finely ground you'll probably eat it with a spoon, not a fork.
The recipe as written in the book:
Serves 4
5 carrots, trimmed, peeled, grated coarsely
1/4 teaspoon salt
2 tablespoons vegetable oil
1 tablespoon whole black mustard seeds
2 teaspoons lemon juice
In a bowl toss the grated carrots with the salt.  Heat the oil in a small pan over a medium flame.   When hot, put in the mustard seeds.  As soon as the seeds start to pop (which happens quickly), pour the oil & seeds over the carrots.  Add the lemon juice and toss.  Serve right away (slightly warm from the oil) or chill.
