Title: Prices of "Fresh Start" Health Store on 23rd Ave
Post by: merm on November 07, 2005, 09:56:03 AM
My wife and I went into the healthfood store this weekend (our 3rd time) and were suprised to see that on a couple items that we bought a week or so ago the prices have gone up about 15%. This saddened me a lot because for me personally it is pricing me out of shopping there.

For example, the Vegenaise which I normally buy at another health food store runs about $6.50 for the quart size. This is already a high price as you can get it some places in Manhattan for less. However - it's still within my price range. Well, our local store here was charging $7.19 for it last week. Yesterday we go in and it's now $8.99 (!!) $9 for a jar of Mayonaise is a little much folks. $7.19 was already a squeeze for me. Prices on other items have gone up as well.

I think maybe the owners are wanting to bring in more revenue, and are raising prices in order to do that, but I think they should consider lowering prices to bring in more revenue instead. There are a lot of people walking in and out of that place not buying anything because the prices are simply too high.

I hope they have enough capital for the start up, because one must realize that when you start a business you need like an entire year of operating capital in order to succeed. The key is to build your customer base in the first year, if anything, to have your prices abnormally low. Introducing new customers to your store with unreasonably high prices is a sure way to keep people from coming back, so even if you do lower your prices in the future, the customers you've driven away won't know it. They are making it so hard on themselves in my opinion.

I really want to support local health food, however there's a point at which the additional price subsidy just isn't feasable for me. Which saddens me a lot.

Is there anyone else who is experiencing thoughts or feelings over this?
