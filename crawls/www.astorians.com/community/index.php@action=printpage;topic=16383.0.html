Title: Chapbook Reading
Post by: Nevart on October 16, 2009, 04:19:45 PM
"Our Side", a Creative Nonfiction Writing Workshop on Cultural Identity

presents a publication launch reading of their new chapbook

Monday, November 2, 7 pm

Little Theater
LaGuardia Community College Performing Arts Center

Free Admission

During the summer of 2009, thirteen writers of all levels and with roots all over the world came together in Our Side to write about the worlds they live in, past and present.  In this ten-week workshop, they helped each other create memoirs, narratives and personal essays which address issues of emigration, dislocation from homeland, assimilation to a new land, maintenance of cultural identity, and transnationalism. 
Spearheaded by Our Side member Beatriz Gil (editor/designer), they produced a publication of their work, which they will present for the first time to the public. 

The free reading takes place at 7 pm on Monday, November 2

at LaGuardia Community College Performing Arts Center's Little Theater,
31-10 Thomson Ave, Long Island City Queens, NY  11101.

Directions: Take the 7 train to 33rd/Rawson or the EGVR trains to Queens Plaza
or visit http://www.lagcc.cuny.edu/visit/ for more directions.

Chapbooks will be available for donations of $5 to $10.

Featured readers will include Milena Abrahamyan, Claro de los Reyes, Tenaz H. Dubash, Beatriz A. Gil, Soniya Munshi, Deena Patel, K Sherbetdjian, Margarita Soto, Hannah Szteinbok, and facilitator Nancy Agabian.

Nancy Agabian is the author of Me as her again: True Stories of an Armenian Daughter, a memoir on her Armenian American family history.  Since 1994, she has been teaching community-based writing workshops in Los Angeles, New York and Yerevan, Armenia.  A resident of Astoria, Queens, she teaches writing at LaGuardia Community College, Queens College, and the Gallatin School of Individualized Study at New York University. 

The Our Side Workshop, during the summer of 2009,  was made possible in part with funds from Poets and Writers, Inc., and by the Queens Council on the Arts with public funding from the New York City Department of Cultural Affairs.  It took place at Topaz Arts in Woodside, Queens.  Our Side now meets regularly at LaGuardia Performing Arts Center.

For more info contact nancyagabian@yahoo.com
