Title: New Music in Astoria 5/20
Post by: rebb_ on May 14, 2011, 01:11:48 PM
 
  New York-based composers' group Random Access Music presents its second program of the season, entitled "Electric Ayres: Songs for Voices and Electronic Devices" The concert of world premiere works composed by RAM members and to be performed by the URSULA ensemble 
 
Friday, May 20, 2011, 8:00 p.m.
the eGarage
4402 23rd Street, Long Island City, Queens

Adults $20. Seniors/Students $15.
 
The lineup includes new works by Random Access Music member composers B. Allen Schulz, Andrew Lee, Manly Romero, David Fetherolf, Stefan Weisman, and URSULA guitarist David Nadal. The concert will feature six world premieres that run the gamut of musical styles--from modernistic to classical-rock fusion. [/size]T[/size]he URSULA ENSEMBLE was founded in 2005 by Holly Nadal as a vocal duo performing new works and new arrangements of medieval chant with looping and other electronic effects. URSULA has since expanded to its current form, which includes electric guitars, percussion, four singers, and a host of electronic effects. URSULA continues to expand and explore the musical possibilities of the human voice. URSULA is Holly Nadal and Megan Schubert, sopranos; Silvie Jensen, Alto; Jeffrey Gavett, Baritone and Keyboards; David Nadal and Andrew Lee, Electric Guitars; and Michael McCurdy, Drums and Percussion.[/color][/size][/font]
[/size][/color]
[/size]RANDOM ACCESS MUSIC is a consortium of composers who pool their energy and resources to produce concerts of their own music. Founded in 2005, RAM's programs are frequently designed in collaboration with specific chamber groups and artist collaborators such as Lost Dog New Music Ensemble, Iktus Percussion Quartet, Anubis Saxophone Quartet, and the Gotham Ensemble. RAM's seven members together have earned a long list of awards, commissions and critical acclaim from coast to coast, including kudos from periodicals such as the New York Times and Time Out New York: "remarkably inspired," "personal, moody and skillfully wrought," "quirky, sparkling...fresh sounds," "powerful and intense; :cheesy: "  "diverse and rousing;" and "causes you to prick up your ears."
