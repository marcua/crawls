I was there over the weekend.  Here are some details:

They’ll be open from 7:30am-4:30 on weekdays, 9am-4:30pm on weekends.

Their bread comes from Balthazar Bakery, as do their pastries.

Their coffee is from Coffee Labs Roasters in Tarrytown NY.

They use Hudson Valley Fresh milk, which comes from cows in Dutchess and Columbia Counties, so it’s pretty local.

Their soda syrups are from P&H Soda Company, based in Queens.

A number of their cheeses (mozzarella, feta) originate in the neighborhood.

Craft beer and kombucha are coming this summer, along with extended hours.

The space is rife with visually engaging, beautiful and sturdy reclaimed materials.

They’ll do espresso drinks, coffee brewed over ice, pour overs, and are considering doing their iced coffee in a cold brewed system.  Sodas are simply soda water and syrup.

Food is all made in house.

Everyone involved is super nice!

More here:  http://weheartastoria.com/2011/03/the-queens-kickshaw-is-awesome/

Went there yesterday at around 2.  Got a lovely coffee (Ethopian Yirgacheffe) and croissant.  The line was long - they were totally slammed by that point.  Part of the way someone let us know that the kitchen had temporarily run out of food, which was a bummer but not too surprising.  What impressed me was how they handled it.  They clearly took that seriously and passed out business cards with a handwritten note from co-owner Jennifer Lim offering $2 off for us to come back another time so they could make it right. I appreciated that.  So I'll be back all right.  I think they're a fabulous addition to the neighborhood and, judging from the comments on their Facebook page, so do a lot of other Astorians.  :-)
