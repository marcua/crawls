For our wedding, my mom's second cousin (who I've met maybe three times in my life), gave us a pair of heavy bronze elephant-statue bookends (each one weighs about ten pounds).  At first glance, these looked like the tackiest things you have ever seen.  They were the butt of all of present-related jokes.  We kept thinking of how to re-gift them (and we DID end up re-gifting a couple of other things), but in the end, we just thought they were so hilariously random that regifting would be totally transparent, and we couldn't even bring oursleves to give them to our not-so-favorite people.  So we held on to them, and they sat in a box for two years.  I finally rediscovered them when I was unpacking after our move to Astoria a few months ago, and realized how campy and amazing they are.  I set them up on a bookcase in the living room, and they really "tie the room together" (hee hee).  So from now on, that's the kind of thing I'm buying people.  No more "I don't give a damn" certificates (in the immortal words of Jerry Seinfeld).
