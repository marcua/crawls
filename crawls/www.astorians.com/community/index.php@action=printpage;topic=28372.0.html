Full disclosure: The worm bin is disassembled, still covered in dried worm castings, bagged up, and boxed, so it's going to be a little funky by now.  You're going to have to wash it.  Sorry about that.

If the idea of touching a dirty worm bin grosses you out, then this is not the hobby for you.

For reference, this is the exact model:
http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002Q6HMSY

I had six good years with this bin.  As long as you do your homework, worm composting is easy and doesn't smell at all when done properly.  I've had plenty of friends sitting next to this fully loaded bin in my living room without realizing the earthy weirdness it contained.

I'm more than happy to share some tips as I want the new owner to be successful with this endeavor.  However, this is not a lifetime tech support type of deal.  You really do need to do some research so that you and the worms that you buy/acquire are not traumatized by the experience. 

Please shoot me an email if you're interested.  I'll tell you where the funky box will be left for pick up as I have already left town and have tasked my awesome neighbor with giving away the worm bin.  Happy composting!
