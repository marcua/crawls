I have a wonderful collection of Wusthofs and Henckels. I learned to sharpen and steel from my Mom and I inherited her stones and her steel.


However, every few years I would shlep a couple of knives to:

Henry Westphal Company
115 West 25th Street
(between 6th and 7th Avenues)
New York NY 10001-7245
212-563-5990
Monday–Friday 9:30 AM – 6:00 PM

They are not cheap. But, they are excellent and they also do scissors.

One of the other places I have used is:
Broadway Panhandler
65 East 8th Street
(between Broadway and University)
New York NY 10003-6450
212-966-3434
Monday–Saturday 11:00 AM – 7:00 PM
Sunday 11:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Take your knives to the knife counter. They charge around $4.00 per knife, but they don't seem to stay sharp as long as when Westphal does them.


This past holiday season one of my children gave me this:


I was really afraid to use it and thought I might return it until I asked a chef friend. He encouraged me to try it by telling me that it has a decent reputation. That the reason why it is ok to use is that it has a fixed angle to run the knives over. In his opinion it should always give a better angle of attack than doing it on a stone by yourself. I decided I would try it with a couple of less expensive knives.

WOW!

I am not sure I would have made the investment myself, but I will get a whole lot of use from it. And the best part, no more slicing my fingers while using the steel and no more scheping into Manhattan and looking for parking!

We had are knives sharpened yesterday at Chelsea Market.  She charged us $4-6 a knife.  We brought in 7 knives, she tested them out and said we only needed 6 of them sharpened but she would touch up the other one for free.  The total bill was $32.  She is there from 12-6pm on Weds and Sat.  We got there about 12:30 and she had them done by 1:15pm.  She did a great job.  Plus we were able to eat our way through the market while we waited:  a lobster roll from the lobster place, an ice cream cone from the Ronnybrook store, and a Stumptown latte from the coffee place.

She gave us a leaflet with her information.  You can check out her website at www.samuraisharpening.com
