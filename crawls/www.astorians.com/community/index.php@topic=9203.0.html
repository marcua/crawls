Oleput has reopened
« on: January 16, 2008, 07:48:27 PM »
I saw them open on my way home tonight so I stopped in.  They've removed most of the bookshelves and shelves with canned goods and used the extra space for more tables.  They are serving a limited food menu of a few salads, sandwiches, soup, and dessert items like cookies and tarts, and expect to be serving wine and beer in a few weeks.  I think they will also offer things like cheese plates, and plan on eventually expanding the menu to include more options.

For the time being, they will be open Wednesday through Sunday, noon to 9pm.  Hours and days likely to expand, particularly when they start serving wine and beer.   :mrgreen:

I gotta say... I went to Oleput last night with a friend for the first time since they re-opened. We both got tea and sat there chatting for about 40 minutes. The place was so comfy and the tea was good and reasonably priced for nice, loose tea. They have two varieties of chai, a darjeeling, and several other teas. We sat there watching the snow blow around outside, and inside it was cozy and warm and we didn't want to leave.

I can understand people upset about it not so much being what it was before, but now it feels like a nice little coffee shop. Good music was playing. The girl behind the counter was friendly. They have books under the counter that I'm guessing one can read while there. There were a few other people in there, either hanging out alone or talking to a friend. All in all just a nice, chill atmosphere, yes a little hip, but not "too cool for you." My hope is that they keep this atmosphere once they start serving beer and wine, that people just go there to chill out and not to shout at each other. There are places that pull this off, so hopefully Oleput will be one of them.

Yup, I was there the night they opened with the license (was that fun!!   :lol: ), as well as tonight.  They are retooling their menu a bit to expand their offerings -- they expect to be offering cheese and meat plates within the next few days, as well as speck for the grilled cheese and a few other things.  The wine list is relatively small, but most of the wines are good (if my memory from last Friday night serves me at all....), and the beer list is quite interesting.

Music has been great, everyone is really friendly, and I really like the atmosphere.  We held a small meeting there, and I spoke to them beforehand about our needs and they were very accommodating. 

I do still wish that they were open for coffee in the mornings when I'm on my way to work, but I'm really happy with what they've got now.  Oh - and they are indeed now open 7 days a week - hours about what essen mentions above; I don't remember exactly.

I learned last night that they are selling mostly Ronnybrook ice cream, as opposed to Mary's Dairy ice cream.  MD ice cream went out of business it seems.  They have a little bit left, but mostly it's Ronnybrook now.
