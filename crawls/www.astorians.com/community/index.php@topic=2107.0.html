Hey. Yeah, I heard mention of this book on NPR this morning, as part of their fundraising shpiel.

I would definitely not recommend it. It was a book for housewives in 1927 France, and thus has no modern conveniences, and it also probably assumes you've been cooking at your mother's knee. Also, meats are decidedly different now than they were then (especially pork), and we have a vastly larger array of vegetables available.

If you're looking for a general-purpose cookbook, here are three recommendations...

The New Joy of Cooking
Mark Bittman Cooks Everything
Vegetarian Cooking for Everyone, Deborah Madison

The last one is obviously meat-free... I don't own the middle one, but lots of people have praised it.

Did you listen to radio lab today?  All about the universe, carl sagan, the team he headed producing the record sent into space.  A terrific segment.

Josh,  here are some good cook book basics:
Mark Bitmans: how to cook everything.  covers a lot and is very well written.

Tom Colicchio: think like a chef. The focus is on some important techniques and shows you some recipes around them.  His idea is that learn the techniques and you dont need recipes, just ideas.

Molly Oneill: new york cookbook: a collection of recipes from famous restaurant owners, famous ethnic food places, firemen, pasta shop owners, etc...  A wonderful variety of very solid and delicious recipes from the multicultural world that is NYC.  It was my first cookbook as an adult.

Lidia Bastianich:  Lidia's Italian American kitchen: Simply because she is the Italian grandmother chef you always wanted that can talk to you in english. Solid good home cooked food.

Any cook book by james Peterson for more serious cooking:  "Sauces" is everything you want to know from mother sauces to every single derivitive from them.  well written and a resource to have.  His vegetables cook book is a must as well. It even has a veg seasons chart.

For very serious learning pick up the "professional chef series number 6" from CIA.  its the sourse book for their cooking school.  very very good resource.

Nigella Lawson of lovely bubbly british fame produces well written and delicious cook books.

Please do not buy the balthazar or les halle cook books, good looking but not the easiest to read, prepare or shop for.  its a good coffee table book at best. No offense to their great restaurants.  Also do not buy any Bobby Flay books for same reasons.
