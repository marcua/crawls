« on: December 20, 2006, 07:19:39 PM »
hey -

i'm more of a lurker than an active poster, but wanted to get the word out about a great little cafe that just opened across the street from my apartment building called Il Bambino. It's in the space where Martha's and, more recently, Corona Bakery used to be. In my opinion, it's an improvement over both. I got a mozarella & pesto panini to go tonight, and it was fantastic - best i've had since moving here. The baked goods all looked amazing, and they have something called "warm maple milk" i'll be trying on my way to work tomorrow. It's a relatively small space, but the design aesthetic is great and the variety of food is terrific.

I mostly despair over the culinary options in my neighborhood; when my fiancee and I go out to eat, it's almost always in manhattan or Brooklyn. I'm hopeful that Il Bambino will fill the huge need for cafes/tapas restaurants that dont think booming techno is suitable dinner music.

I finally stopped by this place tonight to try it out (hey, I've been out of town!). I chatted for a bit with the owner - he's really nice. I think I remember he said his name is Darren - I have horrible memory with names.

Anyway, he was cool enough to go through the long list of panini on the menu with me and explain all the meats and cheeses. I tried the prosciutto with gorgonzola dolce and fig spread. Yum! Apparently he had been working as a chef in Manhattan for a long time before he decided to open a place in his own neighborhood. I told him about Astorians and we swapped notes on our favorite restaurants in the area.

I want to go back soon and dine in. It's BYOB right now, so you can take a bottle and settle in with a pile of tapas. Nice!  :mrgreen:

I went for dinner yesterday.  Met the owner, nice guy from the neighborhood. 
They do not have a wine beer license yet so went two doors down to the liquor store and got a bottle of wine. Love BYOB.  Snagged one the the NY times sitting on the counter and settled in for some good eats.
Told the chef that I had to kill some time so he sent out some samples.

-Proscuitti, fig jam and dolce gorgonzola panini. Delicious and well done. Very good ingredients.
-White bean, Parmesan, proscuitti and rosemary soup was sooooooo good. Chunky too.
-Tasting app. of slow roast tomato with riccota and lemon, roast beet and dry sausage, truffled potato.  All very good and very pretty little items.
-speck panini that I brought home for wife.

Everything was great and the place is warm and music was not too loud or intrusive.  Will definitely be going back.  Sort of blew my diet but it was worth it.
