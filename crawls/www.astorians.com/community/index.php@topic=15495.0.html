I had a chance to visit this Greenmarket on a couple of occasions last summer.   It pretty small, with maybe 3 vendors, but a few nice items.  Would have gone a lot more if it was in a more central location and better hours.   Perhaps they would reach a larger audience if they relocated to say somewhere like Athens Park, brought on some more vendors, and extended their hours either a couple hours before or after the time when most of us have to go elsewhere to work.

Otherwise I'd say its often worth the trouble to hit Union Square on during the week or on Saturdays.   Or if you happen to have a car, drive to the Jackson Heights Greenmarket (34th Ave at 77th St) on Sundays.

I just got back and I have to say that I'm really glad I checked this market out.  I pretty much echo pollyanna's sentiments (great pics, btw).  The tomatoes were disappointing but it's a little early yet for those anyway.   It's not that cheap, but the quality of the produce, in my opinion, is outstanding.  Their selection was very similar to what I get in my CSA share already so I didn't get a whole lot, but I did pick up some cherries, apricots, corn and a 1/2 gal of apple cider.   So far I tried the cherries, which were nice and sweet, and the apple cider was seriously probably the best cider I've ever had.  So as long as I'm around on Weds, I'll definitely be coming back.

BTW - I know some aren't crazy about the location, but I hope more people who live around here really support this because our produce selections are limited.  The produce dept. at the Associated on 21st sucks, and the closest greengrocer on Broadway is cheap but not that great quality.  So this is a very welcome addition to the neighborhood.
