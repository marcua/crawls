I used to say "frosten the cake".  I have been told repeatedly that that phrase is wrong in a number of ways.  No bother, though.  If my brother has kids, I'm totally going to teach them this phrase.

We say "bag" not "sack" where I come from (although my Canadian grandmother says "sack.")  "Shakes" not "frappes".  "Ohrnge" not "Awrange".

I still say "freeway" for those nasty multi-lane monster roads carrying cars and trucks, that out here are called "expressways".  I do enjoy the difference out here between the parkway and expressway, rules-wise.  Nice to be able to drive without scary trucks on either side of you or behind you, when you're in a passenger car on the parkway. 

There's the oh-so-annoying "on line" vs. my preference, "in line".  But we've hashed that out and lanseaux and I agree to disagree.  :D

I believe that California is the home of the ubiquitous "like" and "you know".  Thank you Moon Unit Zappa and "Valley Girl!"
