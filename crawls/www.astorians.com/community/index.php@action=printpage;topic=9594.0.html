Title: Re: Dog run
Post by: NYCMacUser on February 25, 2008, 11:29:23 AM
There is a dog run on Astoria Boulevard right near the river.

However, I am concerned that 'newly acquired' might mean puppy. If so, please have the owner check with a vet before taking any puppy to any dog run. If the dog is not fully vaccinated the run is a dangerous place for the dog. A 'newly acquired' dog may not be responsive to commands by the owner and might be a target of other, perhaps older, dogs who see the pup as an interloper. If the dog is not well-socialized, any dog run can be a dangerous place.

Dog runs are for well-behaved dogs who do not play roughly with other dogs; are not fearful of new things and do not intimidate others.

They are also the filthiest places.

I have been using the same vet for 30+ years, and he makes clients sign a form that you will not allow your dog to use a public dog run as many of the diseases caught there cannot be successfully treated.

I take my dogs to parks during off-leash hours, and to many well-kept facilities out of the City, where they are maintained by local municipalities in pristine conditions, but never to a run here in the City which are in disgusting shape. My dogs are fully vaccinated, middle-aged, completely socialized and are (relatively) well-behaved.
