I've only flown on AA once and it wasn't all that bad.  However, I did have a bad experience with USAir (most of which probably wasn't their fault).

My first year of grad school I flew from WI to FL with USAir.  The flights there were uneventful but the flights back were intense.  The first leg of the journey was from FLL to PHL.  There was no running water on the plane so no ice and you couldn't wash your hands if you used the restroom.  Compunding that was some nasty turbulence which we never seemed to be able to get out of due to traffic congestion around us.  The pilot would get on the PA during the worst of it and tell us to look out one side or another to see the plane pass which was forcing us to ride out the turbulence.  My feet/legs/butt went numb from all the shaking.

The second leg from PHL to MKE was delayed with us inside the plane on the runway for AN HOUR AND A HALF.  That was incredibly frustrating.  I nearly missed the bus back to Madison from the Mil. airport because of that (I gave myself about a 2 hour window so I could eat after flying).

The kicker was that a few days after flying I came down with the Norwalk virus which I assume I contracted en route back to WI. :|

Argh - sorry to hear of your experience!  I can sympathise, that's for sure (I think I'm not alone that, either). 

While we're at it, I would recommend staying away from America West as well.  A couple of years ago we were flying them to LA with a change of planes in Las Vegas.  (That was the first problem -- I had booked a nonstop flight, which they cancelled a couple of weeks before we flew, replacing it with this one.)  The flight was significantly delayed leaving JFK, and we nearly missed our connecting flight in Las Vegas (that had been delayed, too, or we would have missed it).  We flew to LA, started to descend -- and then climbed again and flew back to Las Vegas.  We could see the roof of our airport hotel in LA, we were so close.  But there were thunderstorms and wind shear, and we didn't have enough fuel to circle. 

So far, that's just weather.  But when we landed again at Las Vegas, they proceeded to cancel the flight and told us to rebook on the 7:00, 8:00 or 9:00 flight the next morning.  But we had a connecting flight to Mexico in the morning, and only the 7:00 could possibly get us there on time.  Here we were stuck at the airport in the middle of the night, next to the clanging slot machines, along with a hundred or so other disgruntled passengers trying to get to LA. 

Earlier, in what turned out to be an incredible stroke of luck, I had had the gate agent at JFK double book us on the first America West morning flight from Vegas to LA in case the flight from JFK was delayed even more.  They had never cancelled the double booking, which is the only reason we got a seat on the 7am flight and made our flight (with sprinting and minutes to spare involved) to Mexico.
