A bit of background:
I grew up in the Ravenswood projects. In the late 80s to early 90s, it was not a terribly unusual occurrence for there to be gunshots. I called the police many times on these occasions. Sometimes it seemed they responded, other times it seemed like my report was ignored.
To make a long story short, I started reading up on police radio communications. From what I learned through various sources, I figured it'd be a good idea to get myself a radio scanner so I could monitor police activity in my area. I got one and installed an antenna on the roof of my building. It not only made interesting listening to hear live accounts of police calls in the 114th Precinct, I was able to track any complaints of gunshots I made to see if they were being resopnded to or not.

I have since moved out of Ravenswood into my own house in the Dutch Kills area. Even though my general surroundings are now nothing at all as perilous as the projects were, I still have my scanner set up so I can listen to police calls any time I feel like it.
It's always damn interesting stuff to listen to.
And if you'd have only had a radio scanner, you'd have just had to tune in to the 114th precinct to know firsthand what was going on at Steinway and Broadway.
Just saying.
