Why isn't presidental election day a national holiday?
« on: October 29, 2008, 06:12:10 PM »
I'd like to ask this question because it baffles me why it has not been so all along.  If the vote is our citizens birthright then why is not taken more seriously as a day to be honored.  We have the day off for presidents day, MLK day, Christmas, Easter, July 4th, Thanksgiving and so on. 

It should be a day to vote, period.  I also believe voting rules should be nationalized. One method, one plan. The most basic reason for this is look at all the bullshit that happens every election. The fighting, the teams of lawyers, the lost votes, the long lines of people trying to vote after work. 

And please leave partisan reasons out if it.

You know, I just don't like the concept of early voting or voting by mail (though for the latter there are obviously circumstances where it is necessary).  For early voting, it just doesn't seem to make sense for me for some people to vote earlier than others, when a lot can happen between the start of early voting and election day itself.  A more sinister thought is that by having ballots laying around for so long, you're also creating a situation where there is a greater chance that they would be tampered with (though electronic voting machines and their constant malfunctions seem to ensure problems no matter when voting takes place!).  And as for voting by mail...honestly I just wouldn't trust sending in a ballot by mail when I could cast it in person.  Judging by the amount of other people's mail that I receive almost on a daily basis, I can't say I have much faith in the USPS!
