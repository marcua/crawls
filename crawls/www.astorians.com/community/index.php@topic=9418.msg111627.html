Muslims in Astoria
« on: February 09, 2008, 03:54:48 PM »
Let me  preface this by saying I have lived with people who are Muslim, in close quarter  situations, for years so this is  NOT about  ALL  Muslims here or anywhere  else. 


I find it disturbing, several incidents, and just general observation, that the open door and arms that has been allowed  in the US, NY and Astoria in particular, may be inviting future trouble. Yes, there  have been and are folks who like to lead separate lives  in our  fair city, due  to cultural  taboos and  differences, but, all  seem to intermingle at some point. Since, 9/11, I have noticed an explosion of immigration of  Muslims  into the Astoria  area, Mosques and Shops  on Steinway street.  I go into stores, some refuse to  have 'gambling'  or sell 'beer/alcohol' when they once  sold it, cause as one put it, it is  'against  our laws.'  Fair enough, you work or own it, you can sell what you want, but, the guy, whenever I go back is constantly praying, has a photo of Mecca and 'leaders' on his wall, and sometimes  I'm waiting so long I just leave. I was in gas station  recently, on 20th Avenue, near  the entrance  to the Con Ed Power  plant. Several  times in the late  evening I saw  two of those  guys  taking photos  of the plant, one guy with a beard and  hat, it seems whenever  I pull in, is on his rug praying in the back. This afternoon, I went to get gas, another  guy, I never saw, praying again, comes out and I was going to buy gas, and fill my tires with air, so I asked him for 4 quarters for my dollar. He said I have  no qaurters and 'You Go', waving me off. So, I said I'm a costumer here  all the time, no reason to be rude, where upon I heard this guy mutter something about ALLAH and  'Kafir!'  Now, having lived with Muslims, I know this means infidel, where upon I launched into  my own version of outrage and said all you had to say was 'sory , sir I have no change... "why I say  sorry  to you"... and he yelled 'Kafir' in my face.  "You NO speak bad to me".. OK!! said  this guy and he went in       to the shop. So I left.  I have had several run ins lately,and it appears  there  are  people  in this area, if not Al Qeada or Taliban, certainly sympathisers. I think at some  point, the Islamic  community, much as the Germans and Japanese  during WW 2, would do well to step up and help weed out these individuals who may be infilitrating. I know the  suspicion will breed  well-founded  fear, and this could lead to an ugly situtation. I know, I used  to frequent some stores  for Falafell  and  deserts, even in the past  traveling to Atlantic Avenue in Brooklyn, before  some opened  here, but, I'm reluctant lately and very uncomfortable  with the 'new' arrivals, especially  of the last  few years.
