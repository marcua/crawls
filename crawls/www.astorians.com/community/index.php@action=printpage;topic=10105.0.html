4. This ties in the first three points, in a way.  Every person that I have befriended who doesn't speak English as their first language, yet serves in a capacity where they have to interact with English speaking customers, have told me that they want to learn (Yes, I'm sure there are folks who don't, but I have never met them)  However, as was the case of my Dominican friend and most of the people who worked in the restaurant, there was not enough time in the day to learn.  They would constantly ask me how to say things in English and I would try my best, but my grammar is far from perfect (have you read my posts lately  :-D ) so I would tell them they should find some sort of ESL program.  That's easier said than done, especially if you're working six days a week, ten hours a day, which many of them were.

4. Also, the fact that we live in NYC makes our situation unique.  If you were in Minnesota, this would be a moot issue.  You have to know English to run a business, bottom line, because virtually no one from your vendors to your customers, speaks anything but English.
