Title: Re: Irish Famine Cemetery - gone?
Post by: Jiggs on December 17, 2007, 02:48:54 AM
You mean place on 27th street, just before Astoria Boulvd, if you are coming from Astoria Park.      It was gone fast, months ago.  I did not know it was Irish Famine Cemetary, you sure?  I do know Chuch sold it, which sucks.  I used to go in there, took my nephews once, to give  them idea of history of area.  They had tombstones in horrible shape, some as old as late 1700's.   Most people buried there died around Civil War time.   There were  family plots, with several generations goig back to farmers from 1820's, and a few people who died in this century.  95% were from pre-1900.   Some tombstones could not be read, some were kept up by family's.  A few were knocked over, a few were sinking way into ground.   I thought it would be fixed up by historical society, it was amazing when you realize these people lived here when it was like--well, woods??  Farms? 

So I'm driving  of Astoria Park 5 or 6 months ago to show it to a friend, and I see it's gone.
It was there a couple of weeks earlier, then BAM!  Developers started building.  Nothing in newspapers, just gone.  Don't know what they did with bodies.  It should have been made a landmark.

Title: Re: Irish Famine Cemetery - gone?
Post by: lanseaux on December 17, 2007, 09:40:54 PM
The Irish Famine cemetery that is still around is on 21st STREET at the corner of 26th Avenue.  It is almost equidistant between the Triboro Bridge and Astoria Blvd.  It is the one that is in the link Melee posted.

Bentleys Dad, are you talking about the tiny, closed-off cemetery that is on 20th Road and about 35th St.?*  There is no cemetery on 21st AVENUE between 37th and 38th Streets -- that block is completely taken up on the north side by IS 141 (http://www.schoolsk-12.com/New-York/Astoria/IS141STEINWAYSCHOOL.html), and across the street it is residential.

This page (http://www.forgotten-ny.com/STREET%20SCENES/Astoria/astoria.html) on Forgotten NY has pics of a couple of Astoria cemeteries, including one that is described as "St. George's churchyard on 12th St between Astoria Blvd. and 27th Avenue".  It sounds like it is St. George's that is gone.

*ETA:  Here (http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9E0CE2D81038F934A2575AC0A9669C8B63&n=Top/Reference/Times%20Topics/Subjects/C/Civil%20War%20(US)%20(1861-65)) is an article about that cemetery.  The article mistakenly says that it is on the corner of 20th Road and 35th Avenue; it is at 20th Road and 35th STREET.  Ah!  Another bit (http://www.forgotten-ny.com/CEMETERIES/queenscems/morequeens.html) on the Lawrence Family Graveyard.
