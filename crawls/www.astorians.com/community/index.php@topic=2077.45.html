Your ears are not genetically different because you are a lifetime New Yorker and claim a greater tolerance for noise.  Noise pollution is not a quality of life issue, it is a human health issue. 

Long term exposure to noise pollution results in reduced hearing, increased stress levels, lost sleep and productivity.  The cacophony of noise that many of us are exposed to is harmful.  Noise pollution is nerve shattering to adults and frightening to young children.

For some people, the levels of noise are so great that they are forced to move from their apartments or suffer in silence.  A few even resort to medications as a means to cope with the noise even though there is nothing wrong with them.

Ten years ago, no one knew what a boom car was, now they are everywhere.  Motorcycles and hot-rods are much louder than they were because the EPA has not enforced noise emissions from aftermarket exhaust systems manufacturers in twenty years.  For all the screaming noise of car alarms, they just don't work - thieves intentionally trip the alarm to mask the sound of breaking glass.

Astoria is flooded with noise and if you live near a busy thoroughfare as many of us do, it is a nightmare.

Since the civility of this thread is starting to wane I decided to read through it again from the beginning.

Upon first viewing my feelings were of sympathy and amusement, mainly because longer-time residents have been complaining about this same issue for decades and I’m more than happy to give you younger (or maybe not so young), whippersnapper (or maybe not so snappy) newcomers a go at tackling it.

But let’s tally the list of items that need to be banned (in order of mention):

Ice cream trucks
Motorcycles, specifically Harleys
Car alarms
Motor scooters
The subway
Neighbors
Restaurants, cafes, or bars
Construction
Street fairs
Auto dealership parties
Saint Irene, Saint Anthony and Saint Rocco
Beeping trucks
Yelling in the street
Playground noise

Can some of you understand why the complaints have bothered some? This is life in the city. It’s noisy and compromises have to be made. If you start eliminating items from the above list you forever change the essence of city living -- and it seems why live here if you are so affected by such things? 

Now I totally sympathize to individuals who have to suffer living above or near a business that generates continuous noise, especially late into the evening. It’s very detrimental to quality of life and there have been good suggestions posted here to help you alleviate the problem (though in my opinion the only way to deal with it is to move).

And during the summer it’s especially not fun for the people who live around the edges of Astoria Park. I did for a couple of years and it was really rough to deal with the nightly car-cycle-traffic cruising noise. (Though I did feel it was payback for all the summers I did the same exact thing in my teens-20s). For a couple of summers the 114th set up road blocks around the park and it seemed to help (or at least push the problem to other areas). I’m not sure why they stopped.

I’m not saying anyone should stop being annoyed or bothered by the noise but a perhaps a different perspective would be helpful.
