Ok my good people, the next fabulous 114 Precinct Meeting is scheduled for Feb 27th. After going to a couple of these- i feel like we need to organize. Last night there were 7 of us from the board i attendance. We sat, we ate, we (or i did anyway) scowled, we came out with nothing. I am planning on going to the next meeting and would love if our numbers could grow a bit. A "younger" presence is SOOOOO needed. I won't get into it here, but the whole feel of the place is kinda "stale" if you know what i mean....

First thing is first- we need to take notes with us. Talking points. Questions. Concerns. They open up the floor to questions at the end and thats pretty much the only time there is a chance for us to speak up about whatever it is that's bugging us. And if there is noone to ask anything? Well then its 50/50 time and pass the cookies!

But seriously folks, we can't keep complaining about things and not try at least to get some answers. Use this thread, give us the info and we'll try to formulate some kind of sheet of bullet points to bring up at the next meeting. For instance- last night the one cop sir man mentioned how we were getting more officers in the area. now this is in direct contradiction to what we were told about the huge cuts to the precinct numbers. When asked about this- we were pretty much shooed and given some bull runaround answer. I know there were exact numbers from last summer's meeting but i didn't take them with me to argue the matter. Thats the kind of info i want to have available at the next meeting.

Looks like i have 2 long posts in one month, must be slow at work!
Sorry for going on about this but i think this might be our foot in the door with neighborhood goings ons and i think we need to take advantage of it  :mrgreen:

Concerning the meeting last night. Something curious happened today.

To recap; last night during the open floor questions I asked questions and commented on the Broadway crime and problems, more cops on patrol at night, a task force to crack down on underage drinking in  bars/clubs.  One of the answers from the captain mentioned that they are and have been cracking down on clubs in the neighborhood. He even brought up a case in point. Club  Phenomenon was recently shut down and closed after years of aggressive actions by the police and safety commission.  Cops had been killed there, underage drinking and many other violent issues. This was his big example of the 114 in action.

Well, today while shopping at a supply store at 30th ave and 68th street (right by the 30th ave entrance to the BQE), what was next door but Club  Phenomenon. Turns out to be a huge seedy strip club.  So what the captain was talking about was a seedy crime ridden strip club in an out of the way part of industrial Astoria. This is definitely not a regular bar or club in the central Astoria community filled with residential buildings. 

I was polite and expressive in my concerns last night.  The captain could have talked straight.  Between this example and his run around about police force levels, we were pretty much placated and sent on our way.
