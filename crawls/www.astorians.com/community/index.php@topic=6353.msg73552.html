Most of my friends have been experiencing the same reaction over this - excited, and yet a little embarassed to be excited about it, because everyone knows it's not nice to be glad that someone has died. 

In this case, though, this was not a good man.  This was a man who sought to widen the culture divide by brewing hatred.  He took good but naive people and turned them into hateful bigots in the name of Jesus.  This was a man who inspired intolerance and bigotry and turned families against each other and made outlandish statements to gain media attention for himself. 

This man and others like him were the reason why kids at my hometown liberal Protestant church didn't believe they were real Christians, because their classmates at school were telling them what real Christians were and weren't, and those messages were full of hate, so Christians couldn't possibly be tolerant and loving and open-minded like our group. 

I'm not glad that a person is dead, but I am delighted that I won't have to keep turning off the TV because I hear his name.  I just hope no one tries to turn him into a martyr, and that the effects of his brand of "Christianity" aren't a permanent effect on the sad culture divide in this country.  I wish I could say it was a first step towards fixing the problem, but we've got a long way to go to undo the damage.
