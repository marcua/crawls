Busking the rails
« on: April 06, 2007, 09:35:22 PM »
Just got to thinking on a smoke break today what fun it would be to strap on a battery-amp and ride the rails playing some tunes. The more I thought about it, the more excited I got- especially after seeing Dick Dale's advice to Indie Artists.

So, does anyone know anything about doing this? I called 311 to get information on obtaining a permit, but they were unable to help me until the MUNY office opened on Monday- I then looked up MUNY, and it's not exactly what I'm looking for, especially since their once-a-year audition day has come and gone.

I guess I'd like to know (if you know):

a) do you need a permit?
a2) if yes, how do I get one?
a3) if no, then I'm going out there tomorrow!

b) if no, because it's a crime:
b2) what if you're not asking for money?

I really want to get out there, subway stops, trains, even street corners. Anyone have experience with this?

Thanks,
Preston

I've debated busking in NY but often wondered what the permit entailed. I did it in San Francisco and London. If you're busking in stations you have to go through the MTA/transportation service and if you're doing it in the street you have to go through city & county. That seems to be the basic rule no matter where you go. In London you need a permit and I noticed that a lot of NY is modeled after London so its no surprise to me that you'd have to wait forever to get an audition. I'm still waiting for my London audition after 3 months.
