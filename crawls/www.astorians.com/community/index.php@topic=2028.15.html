Just a note on pasta -- you South Astoria dwellers may not know that there's a fresh pasta store on 23rd Ave, between 31st and 32nd Sts., whose name I can't quite recall but it starts with a C. All sorts of ribbon pasta, some tubes, and ravioli. If you just need fresh pasta in a pinch and don't want to go up to the Bronx...

This said, sign me up for a trip! All that sausage, fantastic!

The pasta shop on 23 Ave is called  Cassinelli's a staple in Astoria for many years it is owned and operated by locals, also a great pork store on ditmars blvd off 37 st selling great sausages both fresh and dried as well as some of the freshest basket cheese in this city. Both of these shops serve some of the best restaurants in NYC.

I would definitely recommend waiting for cooler weather.

Highlights of the trip included sampling really good cheese, having your cannoli filled right there in the shop (so the shell doesn't have time to get soggy), stuffed zucchini flowers, and the little vendor-cart oyster bars out in front of a couple of the fish markets.
