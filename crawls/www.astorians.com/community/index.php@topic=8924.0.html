Bag Checks on the Subway
« on: December 14, 2007, 12:41:30 PM »
Everyday for the past month, I get on the train both ways with a very large green backpack.  Sometimes I'm also carrying cardboard boxes or plastic containers.  For the first time yesterday, some cop tells me he wants to look into my bag when I try to enter the Time Square Subway Station.   I quickly tell him I work for the MTA, flash him my badge, which he could barely see and keep walking.

Last time a cop wanted to look in my bags I argued with him so much that he barely looked.  Time before that I just turned around and entered the station from a different entrance.  Don't we think that if a terrorist has their mind made up to cream a subway station that they'd know how to argue or reproduce an MTA Badge or just decline being checked and use another entrance?

Its a freakin joke.  How exactly does this sort of enforcement protect us from terrorism?  Does the minute chance that a very dim terrorist might be caught warrant the wrecking of our civil liberties?  Are we just being warmed up to accept the idea that being an American includes accepting bag checks?
