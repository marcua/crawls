Bravo to you for wanting to adopt from a shelter!  Please continue to seek out reputable shelters and rescue organizations and do NOT purchase from a pet store. 

NSAL, Humane Society, Kitty Kind/PETCO, and ACC are all great places to find wonderful animal companions.  Animal Care and Control is a very harsh place, as they are a kill shelter, and you will probably see animals who are about to be put to sleep there - but from what I am told, the staff there is great and very helpful. 

If I can give a recommendation for the non-profit, no-kill animal shelter I volunteer with, it's OLLIE'S PLACE www.olliesplace.org - located in Murray Hill (26th St and 3rd Ave) and filled with wonderful, loving cats waiting for their forever home.  You can visit any evening from 6-8 and they are open all day on weekends.

Hmmm...that's a tough one.  I know it's probably unlikely a place will let you adopt a cat on a "trial" basis but I'm sure any staff member or volunteer can give good tips on how to integrate a new cat into an already-cat living household.  Typically, cats don't play nice for the first couple of weeks but once the alpha cat figures out the other one isn't going away, they'll get over it and start getting along as best they can. 

And just because they're not playing or cuddling together doesn't mean they don't get along!  In turn, just because they're fighting often and/or avoiding one another entirely doesn't mean they hate one another.  Cats are finicky but can definitely adapt to new situations very easily.
