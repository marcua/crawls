Title: Dan Bullock, Youngest American Serviceman to Die in the Vietnam War
Post by: Starwind51 on May 31, 2011, 05:46:33 AM
I was at Vietnam Veterans Plaza, downtown Manhattan, recently and saw the plaque for Dan Bullock, the youngest member of the United States military to die in that war.  At age 14 he altered his birth certificate, enlisted in the Marines, and at age 15 was killed in action.


http://www.vietnamveteransplaza.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/bullock-plaque2.jpg (http://www.vietnamveteransplaza.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/bullock-plaque2.jpg)


http://www.virtualwall.org/db/BullockDx01a.htm (http://www.virtualwall.org/db/BullockDx01a.htm)


Although Memorial Day is over, please take a few moments to consider the bravery of this young man.  At any point in his training, to the day he was sent overseas, he could have said "Really - I'm just a kid", but he went through training and he went into combat.  How many of us would have been that brave?
