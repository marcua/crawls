Shady Lady is alright for a date but the food is a bit of a turnoff to me.


As for the douchey thing about Rapture, I just find the crowd there to be a bit over the top and annoying, like everyone in there is trying too hard to be cool or hip or something.


Pachanga is a great idea. The tables are small and intimate and I like the outdoor area.


I think we also need to discuss what constitutes a good date spot to begin with. For me, I think that a first date is best when seated at the bar. You can have more personal conversation since you are closer, and body language is MUCH easier to give and receive. Dinner is usually annoying for a first date, because dining etiquette and the distance from the table can hinder your ability to get to know that person's natural behavior and mannerisms, and make it difficult to really establish a connection and feel out that body language. Very small tables are pretty good though.


But that's just me.

Brick Cafe on 33rd St. & 31st Ave. - great wine and beer selection, also sangria and a wide selection of mixed drinks. No TV, there is
music but never too loud. Nice crowd, never any trouble.


Also on 31st Avenue, I've seen people having drinks in Pachanga Patterson. The bar area is not that big, but again, I don't see any TV there.
But there are always a few people there and the atmosphere seems inviting, comfortable - I pass it on my way home from an exercise class
a couple of times a week.


I know you said not just a wine bar, but Winegasm (terrible name yes) is nice. It's next door to the exercise studio where I go. Definitely a good
date place - quiet, friendly and they do have a menu.


We've also gone to Crescent Lounge on Crescent St. just off Broadway. Not very fancy, but they have wine, beer and I've seen people drinking
mixed drinks. No tv, good music and the bar staff is always very friendly, but mellow, laid back.
