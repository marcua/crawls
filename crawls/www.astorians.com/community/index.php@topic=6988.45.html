The pool I went to as a kid had memberships.  You paid a fee and you got an ID card.  Later, I believe they had a computer database where you would sign in as you entered the pool.

If the Astoria pool charged a small fee, they could do this.  If someone breaks the rules, the membership is revoked and he or she is banned. 

Going back to the argument a few pages ago, I think that charging a fee would eliminate some of the misbehavior.  People, regardless of where they come from, feel like they have a stake in a community when they are charged money to participate in the community.  There's an, "I'd better not f___ this up, or I just blew x amount of money."  Plus, because they invested money into the community, they feel like they are actually part of the community.  Like it's more exclusive, even if the fee is only a few dollars.  And people feel more unity with other members, because they're all part of this big super duper club.

Membership cards exist partly for security purposes, but also because they have a psychological effect, as well.

At least this is my opinion...
