Rally for Marriage Equality - from 6-8 on May 31 in Athens Park
« on: May 27, 2009, 08:17:11 AM »
We're having a rally on May 31 where citizens of Western Queens will team up with local Broadway stars, clergy, and elected officials in support of civil rights and equal marriage. 

Date & Location:
Sunday, May 31, 2009
6:00pm - 8:00pm
Athens Park (take the N/W train to 30th Avenue)
30th Avenue & 30th Street
Astoria, NY

Summary:

Citizens of Western Queens, including Broadway and television stars and government officials, will rally this Sunday at 6:00 in Athens Park to support of Governor Paterson’s Marriage Equality Bill.  The rally will feature music by Broadway performers and stories from elected officials and members of the community who want equality.
 
In addition to providing for equal marriage, the Marriage Equality Bill will provide over 1,300 rights which are currently denied to LGBT New Yorkers, including essential rights such as emergency room visitation for one’s spouse. Western Queens for Marriage Equality (WQfME) is a coalition group that believes marriage is a human right that should be available to all New Yorkers, regardless of sexual orientation.  Queens is the most diverse place in America; this diversity is a strength and we want to demonstrate that the majority of people in Western Queens, like the majority of New Yorkers, believe in equal marriage.

This rally is sponsored by a coalition of groups, including: Western Queens for Marriage Equality, Democracy for New York City, Long Island City Alliance, New York State Young Democrats, Queens County Young Democrats, West Queens Greens, the Jewish Alliance for Change, IntegrityNYC, Marriage Equality New York, Broadway Impact, Civil Rights Front, The Power, Civil Marriage Trail Project, OUT Astoria, Astorians United Against Hate Crimes and Astorians United for Peace and Justice.  The performers at the rally have appeared in the following Broadway shows:  The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee, Flower Drum Song, Rent, The King and I, Spelling Bee, Mamma Mia, Good Vibrations, Mary Poppins and Jersey Boys and on the television shows Guiding Light and Hi-5.
