I went for brunch a couple weeks ago and while the food wasn't bad and the unlimited mimosas/bloody mary's were a nice treat I had a problem with how the food was represented on the menu.  My boyfriend got the burger which was on a pita, a little different and not horrible, but this wasn't explained on the menu and it also came with a McDonald's type hashbrown even though the menu said it came with french fries.  I ordered what was called the "Southern Bennie" which listed sausage gravy a topping.  I am from a southern background so I did expect white sausage gravy even though I know it is not the norm on the east coast.  Since the word Southern was in the title, I expected it to be southern style gravy.  When it arrived I saw it was a brown gravy, not a huge deal except I ordered it expecting white gravy.  I did not complain because I already heard the table next to me send back their southern bennie because it was not white sausage gravy.  There was only one guy working there and we had already heard one person complain about their food and since ours did not taste bad we didn't complain. Again, the food wasn't horrible but I did not like how the food was mis-represented on the menu. 
I have also been once in the evening and I agree with other posts that the drinks were somewhat overpriced, but sometimes you pay for atmosphere.
