BTW has anyone read anything by Haruki Murakami? I read Norwegian Wood and I really liked it but I haven't read anything else by him for fear that his other works would make my brain hurt. I'm a big Murakami fan and have read almost everything he's written. If you liked Norwegian Wood, which is by far his most accessible work, you might want to try South of the Border, West of the Sun next. It is also pretty accessible. Hard Boiled-Wonderland is a little stranger, but still not as out there as Wind-Up. His short stories in Dance, Dance, Dance and After the Quake are also pretty easy to get into. Try those and then Hard-Boiled before you get into the more bizarre Wind-Up and Kafka on the Shore.
