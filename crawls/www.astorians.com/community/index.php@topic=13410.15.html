I hate this "if you don't love the Yankees, you're just jealous" crap.

There are multiple teams in any given sport because (drum roll please).... that makes it more fun.

What fun would it be if every single person on the planet rooted for the same team? Where's the competition, where's the drive, where's the sportsmanship?

Also, I think, worldwide, Manchester U is probably more famous than the Yankees. And I think in the US, the Cowboys are probably just as famous as the Yankees.

Plus, say what you want about Boston, but a big part of the reason I miss that city and think about going back there is because *everyone* there is a baseball fan. That is a baseball town.

Besides, why would I be jealous of a team that, while great in the past, hasn't won a series in 8 years, while my team has won two, and came in third place in their division?

Multiple years and multiple teams have proven that money doesn't buy the world series. Most of the Yankees 26 titles came from before 1960 and can mostly be accredited to a stupid Boston owner wanting to fund a musical and the White Sox and their gambling putting the Yankees in the forefront.

I'm not interested in bragging about baseball victories or basing my fandom on things that happened 25 years before I was born, I'm interested in the future of the sport and a team that is going somewhere because of a solid farm system and smart management.
