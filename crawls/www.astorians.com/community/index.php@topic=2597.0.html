Hello~
I was happy to see this post...I'm a painter living in Astoria as well (and love living here!) It is funny that so many artists want to be in Brooklyn nowadays. I like visiting Brooklyn of course, but feel that Astoria is the place to be

I also feel some of the artistic isolation though. A few of my artist buddies are in grad school- for their MFA's- and so are automatically immersed in the art world. But being out of school for a few years now- I find that it's harder to make the time to paint and discuss art in general with other artists. The Astorian artist get-together mentioned above looks like a great idea though and I'm thinking about going- I think the next one is Aug. 3rd?

I'm curious about different ways that you guys stay connected to your art- whether it's classes, crit groups, museum visits....I just haven't had the motivation and focus lately in my studio- and it's starting to get to me. (that and a full time job getting in the way). But really, I know that I've still painted and worked full time before- how do you guys juggle the two different worlds?

Anyway- enjoy the day and looking forward to hearing more...
Sara
