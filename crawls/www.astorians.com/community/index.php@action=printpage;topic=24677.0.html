Title: Re: Public Schools in Astoria?
Post by: luchamos on March 27, 2013, 01:06:30 PM
I'm sure others will weigh in here (most parents are on spring break right now!) but the public schools in Astoria are very, very good.  There are probably some that are stronger than others but for the most part Astoria has some of the best public elementary and junior high schools in the city.  When you get to high school (and to some extent junior high) students go all over to different districts -- you wouldn't pick a place to live based on the high schools.


I don't know why you say the area is considered a "bad district".  I don't know what information you're working off of, but that's just not true. 


Try looking at Insideschools.org.  You can put in your address and find out your zoned school, and learn all about it.
There are also links to more information about the schools.  You can also get information from the NYC Department of Education website, though it can be tough to navigate. 


Hope this helps.

Post by: thanos27 on March 28, 2013, 12:33:42 AM
Astoria isnt considered a bad school district and actually has some very good schools . Starting with elementary you can find some great schools at that level . PS 122 located on Ditmars and 21st is the best one and one of the best in all of Queens .  I grew up in Astoria all my life and i know plently of teachers there and tons of my friends as well as myself went there ,   PS 2 located in Upper Ditmars is also a very good school . Dont get me wrong , there are bad ones too ,but as the grades get older the schools get worse . Some of the schools at the higher aves like 36th ave  37th ave are really bad at all levels . LIC and Bryant the two high schools are both very bad . They were both about to be closed this past summer due to horrible grades and kids basically not showing up to school.   
If you really want great schools and even though you dont have kids yet , you seem to really have this on your mind you should maybe look in LI or the Westchester area where the schools are excellent .
