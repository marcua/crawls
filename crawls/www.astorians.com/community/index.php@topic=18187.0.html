Re: Soccer clubs for kids?

The Cosmopolitan Junior Soccer League I think predominantly serves this part of Queens.   You may want to start by contacting the league directly (http://www.eteamz.com/CJSL/) or some of their clubs (http://www.eteamz.com/CJSL/news/index.cfm?cat=198343).  You can also try contacting the Cosmopolitan Senior League to see what they recommend in terms of youth soccer (http://www.cslny.com/index.php).

There's also the Big Apple Youth Soccer League, but I don't know much about them - http://www.baysl.com/members.htm. 

A lot of the Catholic schools used to have CYO teams, so you may want to check into some of the local Catholic schools to see if they still have teams.

If you want to go a little further east (Flushing/Bayside), Auburndale Soccer Club has a pretty large intramural league, which may be a good start.  They play (or used to play) most of their games in Cunningham Park on 73rd Avenue, not far from Francis Lewis Boulevard.  You may want to consider them - http://www.auburndalesoccerclub.org/. 

Chelsea Piers also has youth soccer if you want to trek into the city - http://www.chelseapiers.com/fhYtSoc.htm. 

I think that sums it up as best as I can.  Good luck!
