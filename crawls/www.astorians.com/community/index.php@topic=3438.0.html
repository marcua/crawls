Hijacking Posts with Personal Conversations
« on: September 17, 2006, 05:59:37 PM »
<start rant>
When I sign on to a board—this and others—I usually click on Show Unread Posts Since Last Visit. When I see the listing for things that have taken my interest I get all excited. Ohhhhh! Something new and wonderful has been added. I hope it is as interesting as the last poster. And then I get the hijacked personal conversations. UGH! Why can't people who are friends use the PM function. After all, that's what it's there for! Or if you are already buddies, exchange telephone numbers and call each other. Why do I (and all the other folks) have to read your personal conversations that waste our time. It is just as frustrating as the over-development gang! They were/are obnoxious. What makes people think I want to read their little personal chats. If a topic is about blueberry pancakes, you can certainly stretch that to other types of pancakes—or for that matter—other types of breakfast foods, but for heaven's sake who cares if you and your friend are going to be going to the movies on the weekend. OT. So very OT. (This is just being used as an example.)

If people have nothing to add to a thread, why do they post in the thread?

Astorians.com has the best spelling checker anywhere, why do people refuse to use it? Some posts are like reading 2nd-grade papers. Well, not *all* 2nd-grade papers are really written with such poor spelling!

I just needed to get this off my chest. That's what this section is for, isn't it? Ranting.

<end of rant>

And please don't follow this up with your recipe for blueberry pancakes. I already have the perfect blueberry pancake recipe, thank you!

I guess I look at these threads as something different.  I think they take on a fairly conversational tone.  And conversations naturally go off into different tangents.  I agree that it's better when threads stay on-topic.  But it's not always going to.  I guess it's more about when it's a complete random thought that comes out of nowhere that changes the topic that is the real issue.  But if a thread naturally diverges into a different subject through natural conversation, I don't know how that is really avoided.  As far as spelling goes, I am certainly guilty of bad spelling.  But it's not as though we're being graded on here.  This is a casual board, not meant as a forum to form personal opinions on people based on what kind of speller they are.  We're not writing doctoral dissertations here.  There are plenty of grammatical errors as well, but people are trying to simply get their thoughts on here. 

I would be more interested in seeing people use caps correctly before spelling issues.  But it really doesn't bother me at all.  I'm not reading these posts to critique people's spelling or grammar.
