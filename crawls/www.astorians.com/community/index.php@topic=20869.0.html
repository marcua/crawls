Queens Comfort - Yummy food but not too kid friendly
« on: April 23, 2011, 03:26:19 PM »
Let me preface this by saying that we don't allow our kids to be annoying and disruptive in restaurants.  We only take them to places that seem fitting families.  We are a young family in Astoria and are friendly with many other great families in the neighborhood.

My family went to Queens Comfort today - the food was actually quite yummy.  I did not expect them to have a kids menu, I just asked if I could get a scrambled egg plain.  I was told that they could not do that and that this is the menu they have.  I was told that they worked hard to create a special menu and the chef would be "offended" at this request.  I did not understand this - I was not asking for myself but for my 5 year old.  I ended up ordering 2 different items to try to accommodate my daughter.  Although I would love to go back to try the different dishes, this left me feeling disappointed especially when there are so many restaurants in Astoria that are family friendly.  I hope they rethink this position.

This seems to be a new trend now among chefs. The NYT recently did a front page piece in a Friday edition about various restaurants around Manhattan that proudly refuse even the simplest 'special' request. It was in the last few weeks.

Basically, these chefs and the establishments that own these restaurants feel that if you don't want what's on the menu,
then you should go elsewhere. No room for compromise, nothing about pleasing the customer.

I grew up around the restaurant business - my dad was a chef. Granted, he wasn't at a 4-star restaurant, but they always
accommodated requests. And I think the original poster's plain scrambled egg was a simple request.

My feeling is we all work hard for our money and shouldn't spend it in places where our business isn't appreciated.
So even though I've never tried Queens Comfort, I'm one of those people who make special requests so I just won't go there now.

I also think for a new business to treat customers that way in this economy is a big mistake.
