Greetings,
I always browse this site.  I have never felt the need to join till today. 

I am the devoted mom to 4 beautiful canines, they are my everything.  Three of them come from US Pets, one is a rescue. Two were purchased at the Steinway St. location, the oldest of the 2 is 9 years old.  My 4 year old German Shepherd is from  the Flushing location. He is the smartest most beautiful Shepherd in the universe.  He even talks, says "oh mom" when he doesn't get his way.  He is able to let himself out into the backyard and let himself in as well.  My elderly Mom lives with me, when she fell, he rapidly came to get me. 

Knock on wood.  I have never experienced any behavioral problems nor health problems with any of my dogs.  Like all dogs, occasionally they get an upset stomach or patch of dry skin.

My sister also owns two dogs from US pets, a Chihuahua  and a Yorkie. Both beautiful and healthy and really good with her young children.

Just thought I would tell you about my good experience. I am not saying valid points have been addressed. I am aware that my kids are probably from puppy mills.  But when you think of it, buying a puppy from a pet store, aren't you in fact rescuing it. 

Just my opinion.

Thanks to all of you who write on the is blog and have helped make Astoria the great place it is becoming. I was born in Ravenswood and have been a life long resident.

Pretty much all pet stores are inherently 'bad'...and I would imagine particularly a pet store on Steinway.  Blech!
Pet stores don't sell 'used' puppies so to speak, which means they essentially come from puppy mills.  Then you think about the kinds of people a pet store on Steinway is going to attract....not exactly 'enlightened' people who would instead opt to rescue animals from shelters.  Most people who buy from these outer-borough type pet stores....it is 'impulse' type buying...the little kids cry 'mommy....daddy...can we please get a puppy?  Please????'  These same shops also often offer layaway or payment plans.  (They sure do know their customer base!)  So then the family eventually takes the puppy home....has no sense about proper nutrition, or preventative vet visits.....then maybe within a year the puppy is either sick or they realize they just can't afford to take care of it...and next thing you know the pet is either dropped off on a street corner to fend for itself, or dropped off at a shelter.
Then you have the other end of the spectrum....pet stores in Manhattan for the rich...who drop thousands on some fancy purebreed, meanwhile perfectly good dogs in shelters go unadopted.
The whole thing is just repulsive.

'...or the un-bought animals are left to rot in stores just as equally when they get ignored by those who go to shelters.'

You are comparing apples to oranges.

When animals are bought at pet stores, the pet store owners place ORDERS with the breeders to CREATE new puppies.  When the incentive is profit, you can be sure (as we've all seen in the news from time to time) that many breeders and pet shop owners will turn a blind eye to the conditions in which these animals are bred, born, weaned and ultimately sold.  All they care about is making an easy buck.

When animals are adopted from shelters, it's not like new ORDERS are placed for new animals to be CREATED to take their place. 
So by buying animals from pet shops, people are indeed part of the vicious cycle of creating more animals on this planet, which means that the animals who have the misfortune of ending up in shelters have LESS chance of being adopted.

If pet stores were made illegal (as I think they should be), people would have no other option but to then look to shelters in order to get a pet so net net, fewer animals would be put to sleep.
