The only reason I felt the statement was a bit odd is because my research and experience with dry food vs. wet had been vastly different up to the point when I switched to the Broadway Vet to have their teeth cleaned. My cats didn't have any dental problems at the time the Vet made the suggestion to switch to dry food, I had only taken them in for a routine cleaning. I guess I should've been more clear about that. I respect the Vets advice as well as any other pet owner, because I feel that our furry kids deserve only the best we can possibly provide for them and sometimes the best advice is the one we get from other people in similar situations.

I rescued my 2 cats from a shelter and when I first got them they were in pretty bad shape, the shelter had been feeding them Purina One so maybe the food wasn't up to par?. They were overweight, shedding a lot of hair, had no shine to their coats at all, were very lethargic, and the male cat had a UTI. The female cat kept getting bald patches and would scratch a lot. I ended up taking her to the Steinway Vet only to find out she was having an allergic reaction which we later figured to be a grain allergy. I decided to take both cats off of the dry diet the shelter had them on and researched grain free wet foods and their ingredients for about a month. I tried 8 different high quality brands and ultimately wanted to feed them EVO, but ended up going with the Wellness Grain Free line of wet food. It was the most palatable to my furry kids (the female wouldn't touch the EVO) and is free of preservatives and by-products. I give them about 1/4 cup of the Wellness Core Grain Free dry Cat Food once a day as a treat more then anything, but the wet food remains their main source of diet. Since doing that they are like totally different cats, so i don't really see a need to change their feeding routine. They are the ideal weight, have shiny healthy looking coats, and are full of spunk, no health or allergy problems since switching them to this regime. Besides that, I brush their teeth about twice a week, get them cleaned by the Broadway Vet once a year, and have an automatic bubbler fountain for them to drink out of... Very, very spoiled kitties but they deserve it! lol...  :-)
