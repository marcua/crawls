Quote from: essen on January 26, 2008, 01:28:30 AM
I don't know how to explain a radiator, but he replaced whatever it was with a new part that is much shinier than the old one.
That's called an "air valve".  It lets air out of a steam radiator so that steam can get in.  When the air valve gets warm enough, it shuts off so steam and water don't get out. When it gets old or damaged, either the radiator will never get hot, or it will sputter and drip.

There are various sizes and speeds, (and some are adjustable) to balance radiators near the boiler with those further away.  (Turning down the faucet valve on steam heat doesn't work so well, except as an on off switch. If one radiator is too hot, it's better to get an appropriate air valve for it.)
