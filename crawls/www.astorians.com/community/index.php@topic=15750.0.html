What's With the No A/C?
« on: August 03, 2009, 10:38:00 AM »
I realize that we have a huge number of European/Mediterranean inspired tavernas and cafes.  And I can understand that by having all the doors flung open, it gives that open-air look of a small village cafe on a cliff overlooking the breezy Mediterranean Sea.  But are you serious?  Astoria isn't that place.  NYC in July and August can be downright disgusting.  Between the heat, high humidity, dirt, pollution, cars, buses and overhead roaring subway - it ain't pretty! 

I can see a day that is warm and sunny with low humidity.  But this weekend wasn't about that.  We turned down numerous restaurants due to the air conditioning factor until we found a place that was cool and comfy and had a menu to our liking.  It just strikes me as odd.  And the economy is not a very good argument.  Because most people in the hospitality business realize that people may be cutting back on home cooling and look to a public place to cool off and relax.  I just don't get it.  But I'm not entering a restaurant in NYC on a hot day if they don't have the A/C on.  I don't care what style is supposed to be.

Re: What's With the No A/C?
« Reply #4 on: August 03, 2009, 03:40:28 PM »
It's not limited to Astoria and European/Mediterranean restaurants, and I would guess it's more about saving money than trying to pretend their restaurant is located on a beautiful waterfront. I was in a Japanese restaurant in the east village yesterday that was the same way, and a couple weeks ago I was in an American place on the lower east side that had no AC either, and it was a pretty hot and sticky day. But what I've found is, while it's generally disappointing to walk into a place on a hot day and find that it's not air-conditioned, once I'm sitting down in there and eating and drinking, the temperature usually doesn't bother me (unless it's extremely hot and there's no circulation in the place). And I'll take that over the freezing temperatures I've encountered both times I've eaten in Mezzo Mezzo during hot weather - initially it's refreshing, but then I end up shivering and wishing I brought a sweater, which makes the meal less enjoyable.

I do agree that sensible AC usage is very nice, but, if done right, no AC is not necessarily a bad thing.
