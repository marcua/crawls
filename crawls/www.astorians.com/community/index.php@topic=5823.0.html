As long as you can find an apartment in Astoria that is within a 10 minute walking distance to the train, you should be fine without a car.  It's certainly not a necessity here.  The subway runs the same as in Manhattan, 24 hours a day.  There are of course, areas of Astoria in which you would need a car.  But unless you are going to be working in Queens, and do not foresee going into Manhattan much at all, then I would not move to those areas.

Pretty much all of Astoria is convenient to Manhattan as along as you stay within walking distance of the subway. 36th Avenue is three stops from  the 59th and Lexington Station in Manhattan, Broadway is four stops, 30th Avenue five stops, Astoria Blvd six stops and Ditmars is seven stops. All from the N/W train.

Steinway street is I believe 4 stops to 59th and Lexington Ave. (though I could be wrong- hardly ever ride the R train).

Are you looking for a more quiet type of neighborhood- or a more bustling type of neighborhood? That would give us a better idea of what you are looking for.
