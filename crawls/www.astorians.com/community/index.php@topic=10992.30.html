I was born in Astoria, I grew up in many of the places which people on this bulletin board have claimed as their own.  There were ALWAYS kids everywhere, so the issue is not weird for me.

For example, the Beer Garden was a very familiar place for us as kids, and we often played pick-up wiffleball and soccer games while our parents enjoyed the Spaaten, Czech Bud, or the soccer games on the televisions all over the place.  While my parents had coffee and desserts at Omonia Cafe, I ran around many of the cafes which are still around.  We learned to show of our cuteness as kids to score free tiramisu pieces as well as tiny bakalava.  Gilbey's used to be called Gibney's, and before it was a happening place, it was an old man's bar.  My grandfather used to take me there, and, lo and behold, there were kids there to play with too.

There were many things which I learned from these outings - always tip the waiter well, always offer to buy the first round, as well as learning how to play with other children who I first met.  I owe my parents and grandparents a great debt as these outings helped me to develop my social skills, and has made me the person who I am.  This is the way that I will raise my daughter.

If this offends any of you, it would be good for you to leave the establishments which, for decades has welcomed children.  Please send a strong message to these establishments, and go somewhere else.  There are many other places that have no-children policies - I know that if you search hard enough, you can find these places in Manhattan.  Please, do us all a favor, hop on the N (or R) train and go there.
