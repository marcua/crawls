Title: Famous People From Astoria
Post by: Chris on June 14, 2007, 11:01:06 AM
I've always been surprised to learn which famous people grew up in Astoria.  Here are a few off the top of my head.

Whitey Ford, the former Yankee great pitcher who retired in the late 1960s.

Singer Tony Bennett.

Less famous

Baseball player Henry Cotto, a journeyman who I believe played briefly for the Yankees.

Film Society of Lincoln Center head Richard Pena

In recent times (late 1980s maybe) Morton Downey Jr. was reportedly courting a woman who lived on 40th Street or 41st Street I think near Broadway.  Supposedly, people saw him there with his limo.   Downey has completely faded from the public eye, but back in the 1980s he was a Gerry Springer type talk show host and is the son of a noted Irish tenor, also named Morton Downey.

I think Ron Artest, the psycho basketball player currently wearing out his welcome with the Sacramento Kings, grew up in the Queensbridge Houses.

Tony "Red" Bruin, a Syracuse University star, and Vern Fleming, who had a long NBA career, both went to that private or maybe Catholic school whose name escape me.   They attended in the 1980s.  They were both high school All Americans and high school teammates.  The school is near Ditmars Blvd. in the twenties.   I don't know if they grew up in Astoria, but they went to school here.

Dito Montiel, who helmed the film A Guide to Recognizing Your Saints, grew up in Astoria, but until the movie came out I had never heard of him, though he reportedly was famous, or at least well known, before the movie came out.
