from the Times:  http://www.nytimes.com/2007/02/08/nyregion/thecity/09railcnd.html?ref=nyregion

Cracked Rail Delays Thousands on Subway
By JOHN HOLUSHA
Published: February 8, 2007

A cracked rail, probably due to the cold weather, between the Queensboro Plaza and the 59th Street and Lexington Avenue subway stations delayed thousands of commuters today on the N and R subway lines in New York City.

Paul J. Fleuranges, a spokesman for the Metropolitan Transportation Authority, said some trains had been re-routed to avoid the damaged track as a safety precaution, resulting in delays throughout much of the subway system.

He said repairs would be made during the day and that the system should be functioning normally by the evening rush hour.

I heard about it this morning right when I got up, so I left for work early as I had an important meeting this morning.  There was a lot of dithering at the Ditmars station about trains/buses, saw some people trying to work out a deal with a couple of gypsy cabs.  Just as I was about to head for the Q19A stop (along with the rest of humanity), a train pulled in.  Ran up the steps and got on, figuring it would leave eventually.  I did move to another car at one point, and that's when I ran into Jtrane.

It did leave, but not before a long delay, during which they told us repeatedly that they were going to wait until it was as full as possible.  Right -- that would be helpful to everyone else at the other stops.  Sure enough, once we hit Broadway, they announced we were skipping 36th Ave. and 39th Ave., presumably because the train was too full.  (Our car wasn't even jam-packed -- there was definitely room in the middle.  I felt like the people getting on at 30th Ave. and Broadway didn't realise there was room beyond the crowd of people at the door, like they were subway newbies or something, because some people were just staying on the platform.)

Then, without fanfare, at QBP, the conductor simply said that the next stop was Lex.  Ok . . .?  I made it to 5th Ave. (my regular stop), and walked into my office at 9:32, only two minutes late.  Odd.
