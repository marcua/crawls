I got lucky and found a 1 bedroom apartment last year for $1050, no broker (I found it on Craigslist).  My one year lease has expired and my landlords have not raised my rent (yay!).  Thing is though, the apartment was a fixer-upper (a dump) when I moved in. . The building hallways and entry are clean but have threadbare carpet and dingy walls. The landlords told me that they keep the price low because they "don't want to do anything cosmetic."  I figure before I took this apartment I was about to sign a lease on a $1350 apartment with a broker's fee and if I've got to put some money into my rental, it is still worth it. So, I bought paint and hired a painter (they reimbursed me for the price of the paint) and negotiated new carpet. My appliances are old but all work  And my landlords are really nice, as are all my neighbors.

As rg says, "you have to make tradeoffs."
