In Astoria, a Grill for Neighborhood Folks

By PETER MEEHAN
Published: March 22, 2006
WE all have places that fill the inglorious role of providing a meal when you're on the way home, or in a hurry to get somewhere else, or otherwise unwilling to trek for sustenance.

BZ Grill, which bills itself as a "Greek grill" on its menu, appears to fill that need for a cross-section of Astorians. Kids stop by on their way home from school for a pita sandwich and a Coke. Guys with their names stitched on their work shirts let their vans idle outside while they wait for one of the restaurant's hefty platters to go.

Neighborhood folks sit at one of the small tables near the front of the restaurant to inhale a sandwich before darting off to the nearby N and W station or, especially at night, take over one of larger tables in the back and sit down to a proper, if modest, meal.

If BZ Grill were in my backyard, I'm sure I'd do the same. Because unlike the places most of us rely on when we are looking for a quick bite, BZ Grill goes the extra mile. How? In details, like homemade mayonnaise — not that the accommodating staff would go out of its way to tell you they make it themselves — and by offering a focused, reliable roster of dishes.

The menu is terse enough to render appetizers a footnote on its back page. One item was worth turning to: haloumi, a simple dish of grilled slices of salty and firm goat cheese. I'm certain the group I visited BZ Grill with one night would have ravenously polished off a second order if weren't for an avalanche of pork that was on the way.

It was an avalanche of pork in myriad forms: gyro meat, souvlaki, seftalia, bifteki gemista, belly and more. All the meats are available as sandwiches from $4.25 to $5.25 (with toppings like tzatziki on the pork gyro or ketchup and mustard on the loukaniko, a firm Greek wine sausage, sandwich), or on platters with fries or rice, chopped veggies, pickles and a sauce or two to dress the meat with, ranging from $7.75 for a single meat selection to $14.50 for the mixed grill for two. For the money, the variety of the mixed grill is the way to go.

It will include pork gyro that is meaty and full-flavored, tasting mainly of pork but with a discernible and subtle underpinning of Greek seasonings. If you get it at the right time of day, a portion will yield both crispy bits from the outside of the spitted meat and tender flesh from within. Seftalia, small pork meat patties stuffed with mint and wrapped in caul fat, will be nestled atop the gyro meat. Dense, moist and perfumed with the aroma of mint, seftalia have a ratio of meat to plant matter that will satisfy even the most conspicuous carnivore. Bifteki gemista, oblong patties of well-seasoned meat stuffed with molten goat's milk cheese that the menu describes as "Greek burgers," are included too.

The only meat offering that I found wanting was the pork belly, which was oddly dry. It is available in sandwich form only ($4.80), in pita bread with chopped lettuce, tomatoes and mustard. After I dismissed it, one of my more industrious dining companions devised a way to moisten the sandwich and make it nearly irresistible: by adding a dollop of the mayonnaise from the mixed spreads platter ($7.50; it also included tzatziki and an herbed cheese spread called kopanisti). The result was like a souped-up Cypriot B.L.T.

Though it's a no-frills place, it's worth noting that BZ Grill serves beer and wine, including an inexpensive, light and acidic Macedonian red that does an admirable job of cutting through the meatiness of the high-quality, good-value meals it dishes out.

BZ Grill

27-02 Astoria Boulevard (28th Street), Astoria, Queens; (718) 932-7858.

BEST DISHES Haloumi; pork gyro; seftalia; bifteki gemista; spreads platter; mixed grill for two.

PRICE RANGE Appetizers, $4 to $5.50; salads, $4.75 to $5.50; sandwiches, $3 to $5.25; platters, $7.50 to $15.95.

CREDIT CARDS Cash only.

HOURS 11 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily.

WHEELCHAIR ACCESS Three steps down into back part of dining room, where larger tables and the restroom are located.
