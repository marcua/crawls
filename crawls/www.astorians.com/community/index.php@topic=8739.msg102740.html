First, thanks to the astorians, again, for advice and recommendations.  I appreciate all of the help.  This whole process of choosing a PCP is making me sick!

Second, I saw Dr. Discala earlier today.  The wait to actually get in the examining room wasn't long at all.  Granted, there weren't very many people there, but I was really quite pleased and surprised about the relatively short amount of time that I had sit in the waiting room.

A very nice and engaging student physician assistant saw me first and did the initial exam and questioning, so I was all ready for Dr. Discala when he came in.  He was nice and very helpful.  The student stayed in the room and it was interesting to hear their "give and take."  He asked her what she thought my diagnosis was and how it should be treated.  For some reason, they both agreed that I should quit smoking.  They might be on to something there.

Anyway, I would recommend Dr. Discala for those who want to stay in the area for a PCP.
