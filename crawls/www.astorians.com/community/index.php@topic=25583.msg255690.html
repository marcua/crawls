Associated Remodeled & New Services 21st Street at Broadway
« on: November 25, 2013, 01:40:47 PM »
The Associated in the shopping plaza at 21st Street and Broadway has remodeled, changed their layout, and will have new food services.  The deli is now located closer to the entry, they will have by-the-pound serve-yourself hot foods, and a fresh fish counter.


I was at the store yesterday and it looks as if the hot food service isn't available yet and the fish section is still being installed, but they have nice looking tiles on the walls.

Nice to see that it didn't close. I find it odd that as the density of Astoria increases, a number of supermarkets have closed. The area west of 21st Street is a supermarket wasteland and I don't know what I would do if I lived over there.


There used to be another Associated on 21st St. -- not all that long ago -- between Newtown & 30th Avenues. It is now a 99 cents shop. I don't think I ever went in there, but people on this board complained about shoddy products. It would seem to be an ideal location for a supermarket, given all the apartment buildings that have gone up on 21st St.


There was also a supermarket in the building which now houses Staples, behind the Neptune Diner, near the Astoria Blvd. subway stop.  And I still miss the King Kullen on Steinway & 35th Ave. that was in the building that now houses PC Richards, across from the Astoria movie 'plex. That was a nice store. It was a real supermarket, with a huge selection and, best of all, wide, wide aisles.


More recently, the Pathmark on Northern Blvd. closed. That was another real supermarket.
