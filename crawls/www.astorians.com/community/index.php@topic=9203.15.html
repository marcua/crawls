We had the chance tonight to try most of the new menu -- cheese plate (piave, gorgonzola dolce and either manouri or brie) and charcuterie plate (speck, something like a mild sopressata, grilled chorizo), with either very good lightly toasted country bread or slices of decent baguette, several kinds of olives (spicy garlic by themselves, or oil cured and baby kalamata along with manouri and fig jam and toasted country bread), a wonderful pheasant pate (with bread), along with the usual paninis/grilled cheese, salads, and daily soup (not to mention the desserts).  They've got several tiny bites (including the aforementioned garlic olives) -- hard boiled egg with olive oil and balsamic (which we didn't have), and butter beans in tomato sauce (which we did have - they were tasty).  They've now got speck to add to the grilled cheese as well.  It was all delicious!

When I was at Oleput, it felt like a nice casual neighborhoody kind of place, with a different ambiance than Il Bambino (though Il Bambino has done a lot recently to alter its vibe to a warmer feel - I like the changes very much).  I would say that they are similar with regard to having an interesting menu consisting of small plates and sandwiches, which I think is your conjecture, edava. 

Actually, on second reading of the menu, it looks as though Oleput is pulling the best elements from various local haunts:  a nice wine and beer list similar to Waltz; sandwiches and small plates like Il Bambino; interesting teas and good coffee like Freeze Peach; and sweets like the previous incarnation of Oleput.  All in all, I think the menu is a lovely addition to the neighborhood and to Astoria overall.  I'm glad they're here, and I look forward to spending some quality time there once work settles down a bit.
