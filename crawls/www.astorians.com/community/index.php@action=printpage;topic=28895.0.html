Title: Re: Jimmy's Puerto Rican Food
Post by: TJT on February 09, 2015, 08:15:05 AM
We ate at Jimmy's last night and were very glad we did.  The food is excellent.  The shrimp bisque was incredible and filled with tender shrimp.  My red snapper was a thick filet and cooked perfectly and served with crispy tostones with no trace of grease whatsoever.  Jimmy Rodriquez is the founder of the Don Coqui family of restaurants.  One or more are named "Jimmy's" in one way or another.  It was smarter here until the "unofficial" Don Coqui on Newtown changes its name.  From what I understand, that formal business relationship is over, so the one on Newtown should not be using the Don Coqui brand.  They did a nice job with the Jimmy's space near Ditmars.  It's a stylized like a lounge, but I was told the focus is going to be food and not so much music and dancing.  If the food remains as delicious as it was last night, this place will be a hit.
