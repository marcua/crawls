Quote from: mirki2 on September 14, 2006, 12:47:12 AM
does building an even larger sports complex really prove itself in the long term?

The key word isn't so much "bigger" as "newer."  When the Baltimore Orioles built Camden Yards a few years back, with a nice retro-designed stadium, the effect on ticket sales was immediate and lasting -- they went through the roof.  Even through mediocre to bad seasons for the team, their ticket sales are strong.  (Of course, the big money in any professional sport is cable TV deals ...)  Chances are, the same would happen with a renovated Yankee Stadium, especially if they install a Metro-North stop, which I seem to recall being part of this plan.

I'm a little put off by this new Yankee stadium concept, mainly because it wipes out a beautiful recreation area that served both the community and the work-force from the nearby courthouse.  (When I lived there and did jury duty, I'd often go to the runnnig track and walk laps.)  I'd have to see the full plan on what the Yankees intend to do to replace that area.  Same thing is going to happen to Shea Stadium in a few years, although there aren't many (if any) community issues associated with that.

In terms of the Brooklyn Brewery, and bars dumping their beer because of the owner's support of the new Yankee Stadium, that sounds a little strange to me.  One, bar owners and beverage distributors aren't known for their political stances, and two they'll stock their taps and bottle reserves with whatever the customers demand.  (There's a whole book waiting to be written on how beers end up on tap in a bar -- unfortunately, I'm not the one to write it, although the topic interests me greatly.)  If there's been some grass roots anti-BB movement recently, I'd love to hear about it, as that would be a fascinating issue.  If the owner supports the Yankee Stadium renovation, chances are pretty good he also supports the Nets wiping out a nice little portion of Brooklyn to build their new stadium, i.e., he's got bigger fish to fry in his home borough.

As for getting a keg, I'd also recommend if you have a car, hitting the BQE and going to straight to the Brewery itself on Friday night to get one.  Used to be a fun place to hang on Fridays after work, too, before it got obnoxiously over-crowded.  Like getting drunk in a K Mart Garden Center.  But they do a healthy keg business during those hours, too.
