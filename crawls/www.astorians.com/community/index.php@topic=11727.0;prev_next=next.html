I was there of course.  We had about 40+ people on the Astoria feeder ride, which went very smoothly.  I had a little bit of a trial in that I got there and found out that I had broken my handlebar stem, as I was going through the Met Life/Grand Central area my handlebars came loose and would turn when I had no intention of turning, and the wheel would stay in place or wobble. So I walked it to the next station at 24th St and got one of the free bike shares to cruise down to Spring St and visit some friends there. 

One note on the bike share, I was under the impression that it would be in the style like in Paris and what they are thinking of bringing to NYC, where you get a bike, take it to the next bike station and return it there.  But each of the 3 stations were run by a different bike place, so there was no swapping out from station to station.  Kind of a bummer, but the bike I got was in great condition, rode well and was free.

Altogether a beautiful day for the first Summer Streets, and a great turnout.  I hope next week is as beautiful.
