Trader Joe's carries dried fruit and nuts that I like.  Also butter and some spices.  Oh, and almond butter.  Coffee, chocolate, cheeses - it probably would end up having the best cheese selection of any place around Astoria, actually.  I used to eat a lot of their pre-made foods, but don't partake in stuff like that anymore.

I was thinking about this while on the train - where in Astoria would there be a parking lot next to a building for something like Trader Joe's?  Perhaps off of 21st Street (boonies) or north toward Best Yet (also out of the way); neither are very practical.  It would need to be near transit and have room for cars.  Best location I think would be on Broadway in the low 30s, so relatively near the N/W and the R.  It's too bad that Staples moved into that old Met grocery story - that would have been an awesome location for a TJs - right near transit AND a parking lot.

We'll see!

Oh, and the Podunk, Iowa comment?  WTF?  Trader Joe's started out in the metro Los Angeles area, in Pasadena, which is hardly the boonies. TJ's is located in a lot of cities.  Give me a break.   :roll:

Quote from: alyssa726 on March 11, 2010, 10:31:55 AM
i thought trader joe's and whole foods were similar, no?  i've only been to each once or twice.  how are they different, and where is the closest whole foods?

Trader Joe's and Whole Foods really aren't anything alike, and I shop regularly at both. Trader Joe's carries mostly their own line of products at very low prices, which makes them more similar to ALDI than Whole Foods. They also have some brand name products for very reasonable prices too - organic bath & body products way cheaper than I see anywhere else. The prices and the overall quality/selection of the things they sell is why the line is so ridiculous at all times. I don't shop there often because it's kind of exhausting to navigate through the droves (the clogged aisles are worse to me than the waiting-in-line aspect), so I think of what I need ahead of time and buy stuff in bulk so I don't need to go back there too often.

By the way, they sell honey in a bear that has the best lid I've ever seen on a honey container. It looks ridiculous, but it is amazingly functional as well as being cheaper than the honey in a bear that's sold at Trade Fair.

The closest Whole Foods is probably the one at Columbus Circle, but the Union Square location is the closest one to an N train stop, as far as I know.
