I walk down Newtown from 30th Avenue to 21st street almost every day, at all times of the day and night, and have never even felt uncomfortable.  21st can be sketchy once you go south of 30th Avenue, but walking down Newtown is great.  I've actually been hoping to find an apartment in that area.  And you'd be in walking distance of Trade Fair on 30th & 31st, which is the best grocery store I've been to in Astoria.
I think that your apartment will be near 2 schools, so there may be noise early in the morning, but probably not any problems walking home at dark.

Honestly, yes, I think Astoria has a lot of young people and old people.  I've always thought of it as fun.  I moved here 10 years ago straight out of college, and I love it here.  My friends love it here.  I went to school in Greenwich Village for 4 years and Astoria reminded me of the village because there was a lot to do and there were a lot of young diverse people around.  Sure, there are not as many punkrockers or bohemians, but that's not what I was looking for.  It was what I could afford, there was stuff to do and young people in the area, and it was extremely convenient to the city.  After my first year here, I moved out to Rego Park for one year.  I could not stay another, it was full, literally full, of old people.  I called it Florida without the palm trees.  There was nothing to do, and I had to go to Manhattan or Astoria for everything.  I felt very out of place.  I came running back to Astoria when my lease was up, and haven't left since.  It's the perfect mix of young and old, families, and neighborhood feel.  And I have a lot of fun here, to the point where I don't have to go to the city on the weekends for socializing, and where when friends visit from out of town, we can stay local for activities.  Astoria can please everyone,  and that's what I love about it.

Williamsburg might be full of young people, but there are families that have lived there a long time as well.  But it's definitely a lot more expensive than Astoria is, although Astoria is catching up fast.   There are deals to be had everywhere, but brokers are always reluctant to show you them.  It really is luck of the draw.
