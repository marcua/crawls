my wife and i went there about a week ago i think - it was within the first week they opened.  we both enjoyed our food, but if i had to nitpick, there are little things that we're attributing to being so new but we'll try them again after they've had time to refine.

the minor infractions:

  • music was sometimes too loud.  they started playing some nice italian sounding music at a reasonable level, but then changed to something loud and annoying, some kind of pop music that didn't really go with the vibe of the place.  the music is played on speakers out on the street and a friend who lives across the street from them said he can hear it all day
  • the wait service needs some refining, but they're probably just new - for example they had one person bringing around individual pieces of bread and then later we got dipping oil after we finished our bread.  with the whole show of individually serving a piece of bread by one dedicated person, i thought the bread itself was boring, i was expecting something warm i guess

don't get me wrong:

  • the food was great
  • the interior was nice - cozy and lots of windows
  • the staff was friendly and attentive

 we'll try them again.

Went there once.  I have to say - it was packed, and I think it's going to do very well.  There aren't that many nice restaurants in the Astoria/Woodside border - Stove and the new French place on 48th and 30th ave pretty much cover it - and I think the area is ripe for this kind of thing.

On the basis of one, early visit, I'd say that it's a pretty old-fashioned Italian joint.  A lot of old standards on the menu.  In some ways the menu felt like I had time-warped back to 1980, which isn't an uncommon occurrence in Astoria, but rarely happens in a NEW restaurant at that price point.  If you enjoy that type of cuisine--and a LOT of people do--you'll like this place.  Speaking personally, mediocre old-fashioned Italian food is one reason I left New Jersey.  I was a bit shocked at the quality of the fish I ordered, as well as with the simple flavor profile.  The risotto my wife ordered fared better and was nicely cooked with some great flavors (if you like cheese and cream that is!).

We'll probably go back and stick to the pastas, which seem like they have more potential.  The price point makes me wonder though.  There are so many good restaurants in Astoria that we certainly won't return for awhile.  I would make the trek to Forno on Ditmars long before I'd go back to Cara Bella.
