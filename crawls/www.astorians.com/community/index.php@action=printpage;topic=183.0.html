Title: Knitting for men (and other humans) - a primer
Post by: jayme on December 15, 2004, 12:08:52 AM
ACK (or Astoria Crafty/Knitty Group) is meets every Thursday at 8pm at the Freeze Peach.  While all are welcome (crafty and non-crafty alike), and knitting is NOT a requirement.

For those that would like to partake in the stick and string fun, think of this as your back to school shopping list.

Knitting is, at a simple level, transferring bits of string (also known as yarn) from one stick (or needle) to another.  It's best to start out with a simple practice project, like a scarf or pixel cat blanket or scarf.  For this you'll need:

1 ball (skein) of yarn
1 set of knitting needles

The yarn, ideally, should be a "chunky" weight -- something not super thin and not super thick, relatively uniform throughout.

It's good to start on larger (but not huge) knitting needles.  Look for a 10.  Bamboo, plastic, or metal will work (I like bamboo).

If you're shopping for the goods in the neighborhood there are 2 yarn carrying stores on Ditmars.  I'm most familiar with the one on Ditmars between 31st and 32nd (a mere 2 blocks from the Freeze Peach).  There's a second store further down Ditmars in the direction of Steinway and I've heard they have something of a yarn selection as well.  You should be able to buy basic yarn and needles for $10 (or so).

Buy yarn, bring sticks, and show up on Thursday.  I'll provide basic knitting instructions weekly to aspiring knitters.  I've also planned to bring spare yarn bits and needles for those who haven't committed to a first project yet.  

Sign up for the ACK (Astoria Crafty/Knitty) mailing list at:
http://lists.astorians.net

ACK isn't just knitting.  It's sharing of craftiness.  Bring your own or learn from the group in Astoria's own "circle of craft".
