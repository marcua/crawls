Just ate at Seva last night, Thursday, April 9 at 8:00PM.

This is the first positive review that I'm writing since I've eaten here.  That's not to say that this is the only good restaurant in this area.  It's just that this is the first new restaurant I've been to since I've moved in the area where it's been a great experience.  Here we go:

We ordered a tandoori mixed grill, lamb saag, and an aloo mottor gobi (potato and cauliflower) with a naan.  The total was $33.  It was a relatively quiet dinner service.

First off, I immediately noticed that it was mostly women there, so at first I was a little hesitant.  I was one of only two men all night.  Because most women I know don't really go to places "for the food," it's more about the atmosphere, I was thinking already that I wouldn't like this place.  But as I looked around, I was wondering to myself really?  what atmosphere?  That's not to say that the place doesn't have atmosphere, it's just very standard Indian restauranty, so it's not like a clubbing scene to find guys.

But on second look, I realized that there was a table with a young, attractive girl eating by herself.  There was a group of women who weren't anorexic enjoying themselves and gorging on a lot of food.  There was a couple sitting next to us having a great time.  Two women came in and did take out.  And all of the women looked like they knew how to enjoy themselves.  And that's when I realized it.  This place isn't for women, it's for foodies!  At that single moment, even before our order had arrived, I was happy.  All of a sudden, all of the women looked a lot more attractive to me (I'm married, so I'm speaking from a purely platonic sense, if that's possible) AND the restaurant looked that much more attractive as well.

In terms of food, it had a full-bodied flavor with a lot of spices going on.  They were not subtle about a lot of the spicing and it was really a "big flavor" place.  All the dishes were actually spicy, and in this case, I mean spicy hot, which is AMAZING.  My wife complained that it was on the verge of being too hot, but it was just right for me.  The tandoori was moist served with sliced onion and green pepper and raita, and the saag had a not-so-subtle kick in your face of ginger and green chilis.  The aloo gobi was bathed in a hearty curry.  The naan was light and crispy.  The basmati was spiced with fennel.  And, for the first time in my life (I never thought I'd ever say this about Indian), it wasn't greasy.  WHAT?!  Yeah, you read it right.  It's Indian food that isn't greasy!  WOW.  That made the experience that much better.

My wife and I suddenly started debating how this place stacks up to our favorite Indian places.  As it turns out, we both agree that while it's not as good as Kabob King in Jackson Heights or Banjara in East Village, it's right up there with Ghandi Cafe on Bleecker Street (not to be confused with Ghandi Cafe on Broadway).  So although it's not top top tier, it's a solid low top tier restaurant, which is really good in my book.  And for $33, it's a steal.

Portions: Portions were decent, not a lot, but enough.  Indian food tends to sit heavy anyway, so you really don't want a behemoth mound of it.  You want enough to satisfy without overwhelming, and Seva pulled that off.

Value: It's value dining.  This is my favorite kind.  It's not cheap eats but it's not mediocre greasy food either.  It's high quality food for a mediocre price, a price totally justifiable and worth every penny.

Food: A- (lots of flavor, spicy, not greasy)
Portions: B (no complaints)
Value: A (good food at $10 an entree)
Recommend: Yes, I'd recommend this place any day of the week.  This place is definitely all about the food, something I really appreciate.
