Argh, love pickled jalepenos...!

People braise with all sorts of strange things. Beer, of course. Dr. Pepper reportedly makes a good component to a marinade. Heinz Chili Sauce is good stuff.

Oh, here's one. Pickle some beets (or buy some). Store in the fridge for a very long time. Particularly good minced as an accent to cooked chard or other greens, with some extra pickling liquid drizzled over the top, and some crumbled feta.

Possibly relevant -- just picked up a new trick for making polenta without as much stirring. Pour 3 1/2 c of water in a ceramic bowl. Microwave until boiling, about 9 minutes. Whisk in 1 c coarse cornmeal. Microwave on medium (60%) for 2 minutes. Whisk again. Microwave for another 2 minutes on medium (60%). Whisk in a pat of butter and some black pepper. Cover with a plate and let sit for a couple minutes. Serve while goopy, or pour into a flat container, refrigerate, then slice and grill or pan-fry in butter.

I have a whole cupboard shelf devoted to assorted mustards, hot sauces, vinegars and other condiments, including soy sauce, mirin, sriracha, nam pla, anchovy paste, etc.  In the fridge I have another shelf devoted to condiments and sauces - different curry sauces, Asian sauces (black bean, garlic, XO, oyster sauce, noodle sauce etc.), peanut butter, assorted jams, jellies and pickles, chipotle peppers, jerk marinade, horseradish, wasabi paste, anchovies, tomato paste in a tube, pickled ginger, taramosalata, and yes, pickled jalapenos (which provides a startlingly interesting flavour combination when topped with taramosalata).   8-)  Um... I don't always have all of the above, but I do like being able to have the ability to go off in different flavour directions for any given meal.

Mayo and ketchup are always present as well.  Two kinds of butter, sunflower or safflower oil, olive oil and toasted sesame oil for cooking.  I also always keep a jug of maple syrup in the fridge.

I don't use a lot of canned goods, but I do try to keep organic chicken broth (Pacific brand) on hand, as well as different types of tomato products (whole canned tomatoes, tomato sauce and paste -- the whole tomatoes and tomato sauce I canned myself last summer), assorted beans (usually Goya - chickpeas, pink beans, black beans, pinto beans, great northern beans and/or cannelloni beans).  I don't keep a lot of grains around, but I do have quinoa, amaranth and a couple of types of oatmeal, and also several types of lentils and dal (right now I think I have regular lentils, black lentils, red lentils and chana dal).  I have small amounts of white flour and turbinado sugar as well; also cornstarch, several types of cocoa powder, yeast (probably expired), baking powder, baking soda, and other baking ingredients.

Because I get most of my meat through local farms, I have different kinds of meat in the freezer - right now I've got ground beef and chorizo, chuck for pot roast, boneless pork butt, pork chops, hanger steak, lamb chops and riblets, smoked ham hock, marrow and soup bones, and probably a few other things but I don't feel like getting up and checking.

I also get veggies from the CSA, so I've got assorted winter vegetables in storage - potatoes, onions, garlic, carrots, squash, turnips, rutabaga, possibly others.

I won't go into my separate cupboard shelf of spices.

Oh, and chocolate ice cream.   :mrgreen:

Now, do you actually want to know what I do with all of that?   :-P

Thank you all. This has actually been a ruse to see who has the best stocked homes. I would now like to invite myself and family to your place for some delicious food.   Better get to work.

Ha ha, actually I tested three ideas today.  Chicken three ways, incorporating basic items and not your standard ethnic fare (well, at least for the rest of the country).


I used chicken breasts as the main ingredient for faster cooking.

Peruvian chicken with cilantro aioli dressing:
Seasoned breasts with cumin, coriander, salt and lime juice. Sauteed till done and let rest.
Sauce (in blender): fresh cilantro sprigs, some iceberg lettuce, little mayo, white vinegar, pickled jalapeno slices and juice, veg oil, cumin, lime juice. Blend till smooth. I like to drain out a bit of the liquid so it's a little thicker.

Sliced breast and drizzle over sauce. Served with white rice or fried yucca.  Works even better with whole roasted chicken.

Buffalo chicken salad
Bread and fry chicken breast
Sauce: Simmer 1 cup hot sauce and 1/2 cup butter, 1/8 cup OJ, 2 tablespoon honey, salt, 2 clove garlic.  Whisk and remove from heat.

Chopped salad of iceberg, tomato, cucumber and celery.  Layer on sliced chicken strips and crumbled blue cheese.  Serve sauce on the side as dressing.

Chicken Cubano (thank you Edava!). Makes two sandwiches.
Slice breast in half horizontally.  Salt and pepper both  breasts. Saute till done and remove from heat to rest.
Roast garlic mayo: Mash together roast garlic cloves, some of the oil, mayo and salt.
Cut open two Portugese rolls (or Italian hero), butter insides and lightly toast open faced.
Remove roll from toaster and brush insides with roast garlic mayo.
Layering: one half of roll face up. Top with 1 breast, pickled hot pepper slices, pickle chips, 1 slice deli ham, 2 slice Swiss cheese.
Top with other half of roll.  Press down sandwich with both hands to press and schmoosh.
Heat dry pan on stove, place one sandwich in pan and take your heaviest cast iron pot or brick and put on top of sandwich.  Press and toast then flip and do other side.

It was all gooey warm with cheese and garlic mayo. Crunch and heat of peppers and pickles made the dish even better.

Enjoy and thanks all again for the help.
