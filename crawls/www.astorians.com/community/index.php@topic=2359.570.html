Might be blatant, but every day I see dozens of people on the way to or on the subway train who are oblivious to their surroundings while they openly flash devices (mostly iPhones, Blackberries and iPods) that are worth hundreds of dollars.  Once upon a time, flashing even a watch on the subway was considered risky behavior.  In my mind, a lot of these people are perfect candidates for bash-and-grab theft, especially after dark.  Many times, it's been made clear to me, coming up from behind on someone using one of these things (and I can always tell, the way the arms are hunched in and raised, the nodded head, the slower/meandering gait, the obvious decrease of that cognitive "recognition of surroundings" physical alterness, list goes on ...), it would be relatively easy to clock/rob them before they knew what happened.  I'm not even criminal-minded and I see this.  (Then again, if I was a thief, I'd make myself aware of GPS cababilities on some of these things ...)

As it is, elbowed a guy in the face the other night who came up on me, after dark, in a way that suggested he was going to jump me from behind.  He was an iPod user and shocked that I would do something like that.  A short lecture on respecting personal space and a barbed apology helped ease the tension -- but I'd do the same thing again if someone approached me like that.  And I have and use an IPod every day!  One of the worst sins of city life is being oblivious of your surroundings:  no excuses.
