Entrapment is a term that is often not understood by the public.  The penal code for NY State follows:

40.05 Entrapment.
    In  any  prosecution for an offense, it is an affirmative defense that
  the defendant engaged in the proscribed conduct because he  was  induced
  or  encouraged  to  do  so by a public servant, or by a person acting in
  cooperation with a public servant, seeking to  obtain  evidence  against
  him  for  purpose  of criminal prosecution, and when the methods used to
  obtain such evidence were such as to create a substantial risk that  the
  offense  would be committed by a person not otherwise disposed to commit
  it. Inducement or  encouragement  to  commit  an  offense  means  active
  inducement  or  encouragement.  Conduct  merely  affording  a  person an
  opportunity to commit an offense does not constitute entrapment.

The key words to the law are in bold. 

Lawyers correct me if I'm wrong, but isn't the legal reasoning is that law abiding individuals would not, under normal conditions, commit the criminal act.  I guess it would turn into entrapment then, if the undercover would have threatened the man or maybe even continued to pursue him after he said no.

An entrapment defense would be what the Florida state politician tried to use: He felt so intimidated by the mere presence of a black male that he gave him twenty dollars for oral sex.  I guess he thought that the old school racism argument always work.
