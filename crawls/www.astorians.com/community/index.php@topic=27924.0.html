Astorians,
Has anyone else but me found ths place by chance? I was heading down to C Town yesterday and ran across this place by accident. It's a pretty strange location but is pretty accessible if you know where to find it. I think it used to be a beauty parlor(?). <--OK archaic and offensive 

Saigon Cafe
25-90 41st St.
Astoria, NY 11103
718.6371207

This seems to be a "Saigon"-style Banh mi and Pho place with limited seating. So, take away seems to the bulk of their future business.
I talked with owner(?) briefly who was still getting everything in order. Strangely, he did not strike me as Vietnamese or even southeastern Asian at all. He seemed to be from Middle East. Hopefully, he can deliver a decent Banh Mi or bowl of Pho.
I asked for a menu and was given a Xeroxed word document with exactly 13 options in the "sandwich" and "soup" categories.
I look forward to trying this out in the near future.
If anyone beats me to this in the near future, please post here. While Jackson Heights and Elmhurst are only a few minutes away, it would be nice to have even a marginal Vietnamese options a few blocks from home.

Re: Saigon Grill
« Reply #5 on: May 10, 2014, 12:00:23 AM »
Tried their beef sandwich today.  The meat was tender, a little fatty but not excessively so, and you did not have to gnaw at it.  The mix of ingredients was pleasantly different.  I think they said something was pickled but I have have pickled vegetable at Vietnamese places before and they tend to be overpickled for my taste, this was just right.  The bread was a little too crumby (toasted), but overall a good experience, I will definitely try them again.

They seem to eschew MSG for the most part, which I like.  And in talking to the owner as to what is in the different dishes, if there is something you don't like, they have no problem leaving it out.

The only thing is their location on a residential street, I worry that people won't give them a chance, and if the rest of their dishes measure up I want them to succeed.

Hence, the above.
