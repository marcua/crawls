Quote from: doglover on June 24, 2008, 03:28:32 PM
And isn't the law 18 to enter 21 to drink?

No:

Quote
Minors in Licensed Establishments

According to Section 260.21 of the Penal Law, persons under the age of 16 must be accompanied by a parent or guardian to enter an on premises establishment.

In other words, under state law, you can enter a bar if you are under 16 if your parent or guardian is with you.  You can enter a bar if you are over 16 unaccompanied by a parent or guardian.  The specific language of Section 260.21 is:

Quote
§ 260.21 Unlawfully dealing with a child in the second degree.
    A  person  is  guilty of unlawfully dealing with a child in the second
  degree when:
    1. Being an owner, lessee,  manager  or  employee  of  a  place  where
  alcoholic beverages are sold or given away, he permits a child less than
  sixteen years old to enter or remain in such place unless:
    (a)  The  child  is  accompanied  by  his parent, guardian or an adult
  authorized by a parent or guardian; or

Found here.

As Bentleys Dad notes, prohibitions otherwise are up to an individual establishment and are not mandated under state law.
