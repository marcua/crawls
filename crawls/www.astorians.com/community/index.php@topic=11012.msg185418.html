My fiance and I just moved a week ago and used, "White Glove Movers". This was the second time that my Fiance has used them, and because of how well the first move went he insisted on using them again. They showed up with 4 guys, one was a foreman who oversaw the whole move. I have to say, they were extremely professional! They wrapped up my huge flatscreen T.V. as well as all my computer equipment (2 pc's total) and only charged $100 for it.
I guess my only complaint is that the move ended up costing alot more then we had thought it would. This moving company has a minimum of 4 hours and charges $150 an hour + 150 for travel time. We figured since we were moving from a small 2 bedroom apartment that was on a second floor walkup, to a 2 bedroom in an elevator building, that the move would end up taking no more then the 4 hours. I mean we were only moving 4 blocks away and there was plenty of parking in the front of the building. Not to mention we had pre-packed all our belongings so the movers didn't have to pack anything but our T.V and PC's. To our suprise, the move ended up taking 5 hours wich after all the extra costs (travel fee and packing costs) brought us to a grand total of almost $1200 wich is exactly what we were quoted at on the phone. I guess I feel like that extra hour could've been avoided if they had worked a little faster.
Do I think they were worth it, yes. They are the type of moving company you hire if you want a stress and free move with no problems, and are not concerned as much with costs, not to mention they are extremely nice. We had to change the move date and they didn't give us a hard time at all about it. The foreman pretty much oversees and handles everything, and even double checks everything rite down to your packing job to ensure nothing gets broken. They didn't break, dent, or scratch one item, door, or wall. They even put my bed together for me without even asking. At the end of the move a manager will call to ask how the move went and if you are happy with the outcome of everything. So all in all, great move but big dent in our pocketbook...
