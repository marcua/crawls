You can also join a CSA, or order through one. Hellgate lets you order their meats through them.
I've talked to International Meats in the past. They don't carry grass-fed, tho they will get you a grass-fed cow -- you have to buy the whole thing. So if you go in on it with a few people, could be worth it.


Or, just eat lamb. No one has figured out how to CAFO lamb. They are often grain-finished, but always grass-fed most of their lives so a good bet -- even at restaurants that don't have other sustainable options.

I want to make a plug for the meat CSA I belong to, High Point Farms: http://highpointfarms.net/nyc-area-csa-2013.html.  Unlike other CSAs, you choose the cuts you want and the delivery dates you want.  This way, you're not stuck with cuts you don't like and you can stock up at your convenience.  Plus, you can keep adding on $$ throughout the year.  The meat's not cheap, but everything I've gotten from them is good.  They have beef, pork, lamb, chicken, eggs, and variety cuts.
