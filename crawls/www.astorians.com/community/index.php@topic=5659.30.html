If the tradition were switched up in history, and instead, husband were addressed as Mrs. Brooke Shields or whatever, I'm positive they would have the same issues about losing their identity through marriage.  It's an old-school formality, that's probably lost its meaning over the decades, but it's rooted in the very old-school belief that you are your husband's property once you get married, and your realm is the house and the kids, and the husband's is everything else.  Granted not many people buy into this anymore, thank God, but there are remnants of this still around.   Even your father giving you away on your wedding day, or the fact that you wear a white dress.  Society still hangs on to these traditions, but the traditions do come from something.  And that is why, some women do struggle with the idea of losing their identity through marriage.  And if men had gone through the same thing instead, then they would have the same issue.
