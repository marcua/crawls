Title: Dear Local Coffee Shop Owner...
Post by: 28Grand on November 20, 2015, 11:40:29 AM
Dear local coffee shop owner

Last weekend, I stopped by your café for a coffee and pastry. When I walked through the door, I was seduced – the aromas and menu were very enticing as was the setting. 

Given the time of day, I expected it to be busy and it was. I looked around for a place to sit, but alas, there were none. So, regretfully, I turned around and walked out. The same thing happened the weekend before.

Congratulations to you that your business is bustling and I wish you much success, but I would like to know what your secret is?

For the last time I was in your shop, it seemed odd that even though every seat was occupied by stylish patrons with expensive computer hardware sporting a glowing piece of fruit, hardly any were actually drinking or eating your delicious offerings. It was a room of warm seats and cold cups and empty plates, or at least it seemed so to me.

I waited around for a while hoping a spot would open up, but I was unlucky. Maybe one day, I’ll be able to visit you, and perhaps actually give you some money but until then …

Sincerely,
Thwarted customer
 
Title: Re: Dear Local Coffee Shop Owner...
Post by: AlexNYC on November 21, 2015, 07:08:04 AM
This is typical of most coffee shops. Customers that stay for a lengthy periods of time nursing a coffee cup that has long gone cold or empty while they engage on their electronic device or chat away with friends. The owners can't just ask them to leave, that just the way cafes are. This is more of a problem at the smaller stores of course because prospective customers either have to take their order to go or just go someplace else. This basically comes down to how considerate the sitting customers are about overstaying their welcome.
