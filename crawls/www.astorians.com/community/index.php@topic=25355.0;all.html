Re: New Thai -- Enthaice?
« Reply #5 on: March 05, 2014, 11:51:18 AM »
We went last night.  They have their liquor license now and have a full array of cocktails, wines, and beers/ciders.  I generally liked what we had, though the portion size of the summer roll appetizer was a bit chintzy.  The khao soi was properly sour and contained 2 chicken legs plus some interesting pickled greens.  My partner had the larb on the special menu and it had a good "slow burn" (but it wasn't nearly as spicy as the server made it out to be).  The Thai iced tea is really sweet, more so than others I've had, but if you don't mind that it's a good foil for the sour & spicy dishes.

Yes, it's more expensive than the smaller places in Woodside and Elmhurst, but it's a good option if you want Thai that's a little outside the norm and don't want to leave Astoria and/or you'd like something a little fancy.

Re: New Thai -- Enthaice?
« Reply #15 on: March 09, 2014, 05:44:32 AM »
Finally tried Enthaice. Brought our Thai food loving one year old with us. Though they had high chairs, they didn't seem all that kid friendly. The kid loved the food, though, so we'd likely order from them to go in the future. I had the chicken pad Thai which was some of the best I've ever had. My wife had pineapple fried rice, which she devoured, along with some steamed noodles (not a fan). The kid had tofu and noodles - a meal we cobbled together from our main courses and a couple of side dishes since there was no kid's menu.

Only complaint about the food was he inability to modify items to make them vegetarian-friendly. We love Thai Pavilion and they've always been accommodating about making pad Thai without the fish sauce for my vegetarian wife. Enthaice said it couldn't be done, without even checking with the chef (as Thai Pavilion had done the first time we ordered it that way). Overall a good, albeit pricey meal.
