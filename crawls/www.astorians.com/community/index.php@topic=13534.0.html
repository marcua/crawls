I have HUGE closets, so I stock up on all the non-perishable items from there. In addition to the stuff that paratactical already mentioned I also buy aluminum foil and plastic wrap in bulk.

I buy my calcium and vitamin tablets at a great cost savings.

If you have refrigerator and freezer space their frozen food section is like a fairyland. The frozen shrimp, vegetables and some prepared foods are sold at terrific discounted prices.

But my favorite buys are the meats, fresh fruits and vegetables, and the bakery products. As soon as I get home, I repackage everything for the freezer. I am not a young dude, errr, ummm, I am an old dudette who lives on a fixed income. Wherever I can conserve my budget I avail myself of. Costco has been a great asset in my life. I learned not to buy on impulse just because something is a great bargain. You need to understand what you will really use so that you don't have to throw away spoiled foodstuff because you bought too much.

The VERY BEST BARGAIN at Costco is their pharmacy. Their prices are minimally 45%–70% cheaper than any other local drug store and the products are identical.
