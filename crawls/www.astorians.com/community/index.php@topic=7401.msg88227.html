Questions about heating costs and month-to-month leases
« on: August 06, 2007, 07:48:54 PM »
We're paying $1550 for a 2br in a small private house. Our lease is up at the end of September. The landlords want to raise the rent by $50/month to cover increased heating costs, which they cover, not us. $50 per month seems excessive, does anyone know if this is reasonable? It seems totally unfair.

Also, they are trying to sell the building and therefore want us to pay month-to-month and NOT give us a year long lease. I was wondering if we have any rights in terms of rent increases if we have not signed a lease?

Since we're in a 2-unit house, not a rent-stabilized building, I know the landlords can do anything they please, but I'm hoping we can figure out a way to get a lease so at least we are protected under the sale.

Thanks!
