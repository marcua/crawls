I don't see many celebrities (and never in Astoria or on the N train!), but I have seen a few:

On the Upper East Side: Mike Wallace and James Earl Jones
At the 42nd St. Library: Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis
In Harlem: Maya Angelou

Two incidents that involved speaking--sorry they aren't the real big names:

While photocopying a newspaper article at the Tompkins Square library I got into a conversation with a guy at the copy machine; then I looked up and saw it was Tuli Kupferberg (of the Fugs; sorry, you probably have to be a baby boomer to know who he was).

At a concert at the Brooklyn Academy of Music, before the show started, I learned from the conversation that the man sitting next to me was Norman Rosten, who had written a charming novel which I had recently read (Under the Boardwalk).  He had also written a book about his friend Marilyn Monroe but it wasn't a bestseller, probably because it wasn't lurid enough (Marilyn: An Untold Story), as well as an elegy upon her death ("Who Killed Norma Jean?").  So I introduced myself.

How have I never posted here?

Ok, so I watched my friend Shannon have a seizure as Ice Cube walked by with a ho on his arm back in 2000, I watched my mom twirl in a full circle as she saw Richard Belzer walk into NYSC, and I almost knocked over Paul Simon (he's so little!) while walking on 21st Street over to Manhattan Color Lab with a few of my friends from school. I didn't realize it was him until I looked up. Note to everyone: pass on the right!

OH! Also, Usher winked at me...my friend Diana used to work for Macy's, and she was a "floatmaster" for the 2001 Thanksgiving parade, so I latched onto that so I could be in the parade. haha! Anyway, we were in the staging area up on Central Park West, and Usher went by on a golf cart, and some girls started squealing, and then I made fun of the girls to another friend of mine and Diana's, Usher heard, and winked and pointed as he zoomed by. The squealey girls were jealous. Haha!

Also, this isn't New York related (it happened at my old bookstore job in CT), but Emeril Lagasse once put his arm around me and said, "ok...you ready to go, babe?" He was there for a book signing and I was intro-ing him to the crowd of mallrat soccer moms :) Also, other authors that I met at that job - James Patterson asked me where he could get a drink (not many people showed up for him, I think he was pissed. I directed him to a Ruby Tuesday's in the mall - hey it was suburbia, and they have a full bar and it was right around the corner), and Anne Rice looked at me and said, "hmmm...interesting."
