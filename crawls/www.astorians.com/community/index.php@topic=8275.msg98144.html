I hate to be the one to tell you, but at $200,000 you will still be short about another $300,000. Remember that you will be spending down from the day you retire, and depending on your investments, you might be paying taxes as you spend down your own money. From 30–45 you need to be VERY aggressive in your investments. They need to double in the 15 years you are investing in your future. From 45–60 you need to be a little more conservative and stay away from anything that might adversely affect your capital investment. At 60 you need to put all your monies in simple jumbo savings.

The goal is to croak and still have money left. If you don't plan it that way, you stand a chance of outliving your money.

Don't keep a losing investment in your portfolio. Get rid of it, even if it is a loss, before it wipes out. Put that money somewhere else.

Please don't spend today like there is no tomorrow. When tomorrow comes it is too late to get worried about how you are going to survive.
