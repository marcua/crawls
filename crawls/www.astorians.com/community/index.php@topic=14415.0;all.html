Twitter -- I don't get it. Do you?
« on: March 25, 2009, 11:46:51 AM »
So it seems since the election of Barack Obama everyone has been all a twitter about the new internet service, Twitter. I'm on Facebook, Flicker, Bloglines, a variety of message forums, and I even gave Digg a shot a while back (it wasn't useful for me), so I recently signed up for Twitter. But I just don't get it. It is just a random stream of people's, websites' and businesses' extremely random and varied posts. All the articles I have read encourage people to "follow" everyone that "follows" you, but I'm getting the feeling that if I did that Twitter would be even more a jumble of random content.

In an attempt to make Twitter more useful for me I signed up for a service called Friendfeed. Friendfeed is basically a site that takes all your postings from other sites and posts them to Twitter. So now all my Facebook and Flicker posts get populated to Twitter as well.

What am I not getting? Do you Twitter?

I don't get it either, but I've been resistant to this type of thing for quite some time.  I don't have email service on my phone for the very reason that I like that separation between work and personal life.  I'm in the freelance biz and I'm afraid of that line becoming very blurred.  I don't want to feel the need to answer emails if I'm not at my computer.  I'm already being bombarded with tons of email as it is, I feel like if I add email, web, and twitter to my phone, I'll be overwhelmed with feeling the need to read it, respond, write my own, and to be quite honest, I have things that have to take priority over that.  If I am so distracted with tweets, it means I'm not getting work done.  Also, with the growing popularity of Facebook and Twitter, I find that status updates quickly get buried under all the other updates, rendering the use of getting information out there potentially moot, as in when you list an ad on Craigslist, and it gets buried within hours and no one sees your ad anyway.  I'm sure people love it, but for me, I can't be bothered.  No offense to anyone :)
