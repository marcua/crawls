Title: Re: living in astoria(new comer!)
Post by: kerrigankrewe on January 10, 2009, 08:37:35 AM
I moved here with my husband in July of last year. We came from out of state too. I must say, it's extremely difficult to find what you want for the right price at what the broker deems you can afford. I had to seriously lower my standards, but I must say that I'm happy without a dishwasher & washer/dryer. With the cost of electricity, trust me, you'll be happier without. Not to say you can't find apartments with these - they're out there with their own special price tag.

Also, you may be required to have a guarantor. Although we had 40x the rent saved, the broker still wanted to see us working in NYC already. I would hear all the time, "That's great you had a job in Florida, but that doesn't mean you can have the same here." Just prove them wrong. :)

As paratactical suggested, that brokers thread is a HUGE help. There are also tons of threads regarding which areas are safe(r) in Astoria. Since you're coming from VA, I suggest you find an apartment that is close to a N/W or R/G/V stop. If you're coming with a car but don't plan on using it everyday, get a parking space the same way you got an apartment: Avoid alternate side parking and vandalism at all costs.

Post by: paratactical on January 11, 2009, 03:41:54 PM
Things I've Learned that I Might be able to get Elsewhere in Apartments that I won't get (without a big price hike) in NYC:

- Central Air/Heat
- Washer/Dryer in Building
- Dishwasher
- Garbage Disposal
- Yard
- Big Bathtub
- Walk-in Closet
- Parking Space Included with Apt

The list goes on and on.

Also, if you don't have a job lined up in NYC, you either need to have your parents sign on as guarantors or you need to sublet an apartment and save money until you get a steady job with good pay. I did the former when I came to NYC, but I wish I had done the latter. You get the benefit of having more time to find a place and you can get to know the area before you decide. Also, when you have a steady job, you can figure out if you might be able to afford more than the $1200 you're looking for now.

$1200 is possible, but be watchful for illegal apartments (often basements) and shady landlords.

Yes, go with a broker. I would definitely make a few (3 or more) appointments with brokers in Astoria. This site gives several broker referrals, so don't sweat that part - you're already in the right place. I didn't read about this site until after moving here.

I came up once before moving last July. I came up the second week of June and met with a broker in Forest Hills who claimed to have the "what what" on Astoria. When we arrived, she had one crappy apartment to show us and then dumped us off with an Astorian broker friend. That guy was cool and showed us an apartment we fell in love with. We gave him a cash deposit and about a week before we were to move, we find out we lost that apartment because the broker got into a verbal fight with the landlord. So, one week before we were to move, we had no place to go to. Luckily, we have family up here who were able to physically walk around and view apartments for us. PLEASE - NO MATTER HOW DESPERATE YOU BECOME - DON'T SIGN A LEASE WITHOUT SEEING THE APARTMENT. Sorry for the caps, but I was almost pushed to that edge.  :evil:

As was said, people are flabbergasted to find out just how hard it is to move to NYC. I certainly was. It's humbling, though, and gets you prepared for your strangely fabulous new surroundings.

Also can anyone give me definite emails of the brokers recommended on here? A few of them don't have a website and i just wanted to maybe shoot them an email to keep us in mind since we'll be there in march.

Most brokers deal by phone, not email. Check out the threads 28Grand posted to get ideas of who to call.

Even if you do get emails, March is way too far ahead for them to start keeping you in mind for an August 1 move-in date. Even June is too early.

I know that it's scary to get ready to move up here with no idea where you're going to live. I did it, and it was very stressful. But the real estate market here is very different from everywhere else in the country--good apartments are rented within days, and brokers have dozens or even hundreds of apartment-seekers for immediate move-in at any one time. Until you are in front of them, they will not remember you.
