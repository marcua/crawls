Abusive Cabbie Complaint Hearing
« on: September 03, 2011, 12:08:49 PM »

A few months ago, I had an incident with a cab driver that was so serious I filed a complaint. Yesterday was the hearing and I want to share my experience in case anyone is wondering. I've seen posts on here about cabs refusing service, but my story is a little different.


I was picked up in the West Village and brought back to Astoria. When I tried to pay my card wasn't going through at first. I showed the cabbie who was getting increasingly mad, that I was swiping but it wasn't working. I didn't want him to think I was trying to skip out. He got out of the car, calling me a stupid b*tch, and came to where I was sitting and went through the process on the screen again and it went through (turn out I wasn't on the right screen to swipe, I had one more to go). Again he called me a stupid b*tch, accused me of trying to "f*ck the cabbie" and handed me my receipt to sign. At this point I took out my phone to take a picture of his medallion, which of course made him even more mad and so he tried taking a picture of me with his camera. I got out of the car and went to take a picture of his plate also (I figured more evidence the better) and he got out to take another picture of me. I put my arm up to cover my face which he GRABBED violently to pull down. When I broke free he dropped his phone and when he went to pick it up, I ran into my apartment.  I should have called the cops, I know. But I was just thinking "flee" and I figured after the fact it wouldn't matter. So I filed a complaint.


The hearing was kind of nerve-wracking for me, probably b/c I had to wait for 2 hours to go in. Plus it takes place in this little office where you are 2ft away from the driver and attorney, and right across from the judge. First I told my side of the story and then came the cross examination from the driver's attorney. The driver's attorney was the meanest b*tch I ever met! I know it's her job to intimidate me, but she was yelling at me during the whole cross-examination. The judge actually told her to stop badgering me. She even insulted and yelled at my TLC rep, saying if she went to law school she would know the difference between cross examination and blah blah blah. After the cross-examination was done, I had to leave the room for the cabbie to tell his side of the story. I don't like how I have to leave the room during this b/c he was clearly telling lies and the only way I could defend myself was when they asked me more questions after. I guess that's what the TLC person is for, but I would love to hear his side of the story. Anyway, the whole process probably took 45 min (not including all the waiting).


I'll find out the verdict by mail. I hope the judge rules in my favor. I mean, why would I go all the way down there, miss half a day of work, sit there for 2 hours, just to lie? The driver and the attorney actually tried to make a deal earlier that day, but b/c of the charges they wouldn't let them. So when I was sitting there, going on hour 2, someone came out to talk to me saying that the deal the driver was willing to strike involved very high penalties like several points, high fines, etc. which pretty much proved he was guilty--if he'd be wiling to pay such a high price--and the wait could be a lot longer to go in. But I said I'd sit there all day if I had to, I wanted to go through with the hearing. The judge really likes it when people come in person to give statements and it carries more weight.


The medallion number is 2v49 so stay away from him if you see him! and if this ever happens to anyone, make sure you call the cops! I think it would have helped my case tremendously. The attorney kept arguing if I was so scared why didn't I call the cops? But I was so shook I wasn't even thinking!


I'll let you know the verdict when I get it...
