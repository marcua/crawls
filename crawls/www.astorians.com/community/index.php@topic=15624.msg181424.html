This is a mixed review.  I love this place.  We've been ordering and going there to eat rather often from the start of their business.  But there has been some inconsistency with the food that has been almost jarring.  I often get the same dish every time (among other things).  In this case, the Moo Shoo Crab.  Sometimes it's really hot and spicy.  Other times very mild.  Sometimes loaded with onions.  Sometimes loaded with cabbage, no onions.  Sometimes neither and just a spicy lump of crab.  So we finally started talking to them some more.  There has been a chef change.  So the new chef is trying to learn the ropes and get a groove.  Which is a shame.  They seem to have a hard time delivering on brown rice when you request it instead of white.  And they are sadly giving the impression that business isn't exactly booming.  So the future may not look so good for them.  I think this is a shame.  Spiced stands out in my mind as a restaurant that could really have a stronghold if they'd just get their act together a bit.  The menu is unique (I think) for the Ditmars area.  I love the Spicy Fish and the Fried Chili Lamb.  And the partner/owner we spoke with is truly concerned that the customers are pleased, and they seem to be trying the best they can.  But obviously what goes on in the kitchen needs some work.  But I hope they don't fail.  I would really miss their food.

Finally tried Spiced for the first time last night. Overall, we weren't too impressed. My husband and I each got soup to start - I with the corn soup with crab and he the Spiced soup with shrimp. My soup had very subtle flavors, but I enjoyed it. His soup, on the other hand, was full of flavor and he really liked it - a sweet and sour flavor that balanced well with the cilantro they mounded on top. Then I ordered the chicken szechwan. After the waitress made a point of warning me about its spice level, I expected to receive a dish full of flavor and spice. Not so. It was. . . bland. Pretty much lacking any flavor and the spice was nonexistent. Also, barely any chicken was present in the dish - almost the entire thing was vegetables and it was stingy on the sauce. I was really disappointed. My husband ordered the chicken manchurian and his also came out weak (even though both of these dishes are rated amongst their most spicy) and mostly bland. His had slightly more flavor than mine, but he also ended the meal disappointed. Overall, his soup was the highlight of the meal and everything else was so-so. We may go back and try different dishes, but with all the other fantastic dining options here it won't be any time soon.
