I live in Queensview and have lived there for my entire life.  My grandparents and mom moved in there in 1953, three years after it was built.  I have nothing but good things to say about Queensview.  The buildings, while not fancy or "luxury," are very well maintained.  Apartments are very spacious and come in basically three sizes:  1 bedroom (3.5 rooms), 2 bedroom (4.5 rooms), and 3 bedroom (5.5 rooms).  There is a children's playground and a basketball court on premises as well as plenty of open space.  There is a lot of grass and trees plus plenty of places to sit and enjoy the grounds.  The residents are mostly older, but a lot of younger people have been moving in recently (WE NEED MORE!!!).  For the senior citizens, there is a NORC -- a Naturally Occurring Retirement Community -- which offers social services and social programming.  There is also an excellent nursery school on the grounds.  Queensview is comprised of 14 buildings and covers one square block between 21st and Crescent and 33rd Road and 34th Avenue.  Queensview has its own security force which patrols the area almost 24 hours per day.  I'd say that the area is safe.  Prices of apartments are extremely reasonable -- they are actually NOT on the open market, so when a cooperator wants to sell, he or she can only sell to the coop, which pays a fixed price based on the current share price, which increases from time to time.  I don't know exact prices, but a one bedroom is under $200,000.  However, income requirements tend to be high.  It was started as a middle-income, affordable housing development and has been maintained as such today.  There has been some movement lately toward moving to open market sales.  Although such a proposal was voted down in the last few years, it will likely come up again.  Maintenance has doubled in the last 5 years, but is still reasonable.
Finally, I want to correct an earlier post -- the buildings across 21st St. are NOT part of Queensview.  They are in what is technically called "North Queensview Homes," but which most people in Queensview call "Queensview West."  Those apartments ARE on the open market, and consequently cost more than Queensview to purchase, but I believe the maintenance is lower.
If anyone has any questions about Queensview, I would be happy to answer.

I also live in Queensview, I've lived here for six years this month. I have what is called a 3 1/2 room apartment. The size is roughly 770 square feet. I love it here, the apartments are VERY well laid out, every apartment in the complex is a corner unit with 2 exposures and really nice cross ventilation. I have a beautiful view of the Triboro Bridge and I can see the N/W train from my windows. It is a VERY quiet apartment. They allow cats but no dogs. The maintenance when I moved in was something like $330 a month including gas and electric. Then 9-11 happened, and the city needed cash and raised property taxes quite a bit and thats what prompted the first big increase. It hasn't quite doubled, but it has gone up since I bought in. Mine is still below $600 a month (including Gas and Electric) which I think is well below average for a Co-Op apartment in NYC.   

You can't just walk up and buy an apartment, there is a waiting list. For me, it took about 9 months to get offered an apartment. I have no idea what the list is like these days, I do know for a while a couple of years ago, the list was closed with too many people waiting to get in. A lot of the shareholders have been here from day one, so there are a lot of retirees. Just like anywhere else, I have neighbors that I adore and people that I run from when I see them coming. I have never felt unsafe here at any hour but just like everywhere else in NYC, things do happen and you need to pay attention to what you are doing. 

For more info call the office (718) 728-5090 and they will send you an application or google for the website.
Hope this helps.

Billhill is absolutely correct -- the maintenance has almost doubled because of increased property taxes as well as huge increases in electric, water, and fuel oil (heating) bills.  All utilities are included in the maintenance, but for years there has been talk of "submetering," which is putting individual electric meters in each apt. so each person pays for what they use.  But it is still a great deal, and I really can't see why more young people don't move in given the low cost of the apartments and the proximity of the co-op to Manhattan.
