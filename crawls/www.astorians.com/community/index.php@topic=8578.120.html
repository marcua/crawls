There is no name-calling happening here, Sweeper. I mentioned the word fascism as something we're going to experience more of in coming years, edava posted a dictionary definition of the word. Pretty coherent so far. No major thought crimes committed, yet. :-P

If you consider the second definition in edava's post (2. a tendency toward or actual exercise of strong autocratic or dictatorial control), you'd be hard-pressed not to find that "tendency" manifest in Republican and Democratic policy proposals currently at work (Patriot Act). Certainly, this tendency characterizes the trend in health care (i.e., from HMOs to Hillary's or Obama's "universal" health care proposals). The government role is increasing, the costs are getting higher, and doctors and patients are pushed farther away from one another.

Hillary is seriously talking about the national government "mandating" that citizens have health insurance. So we're clear, the issue is not whether it is a good idea for people to have health insurance, but rather, is it a good idea to have the national government FORCE people to have health insurance. Of course, once you accept that the national government has the right to force people to have one thing, what would be the basis for limiting the government from forcing us to have other things? Or to not have other things? It seems to get easier and easier for government to force us to do things, easier and easier for each of us to be found to be breaking one law or another.

Hillary pays for her plan by "mandating" that large employers provide health insurance/help pay for it. You know what that means, right. It means products and services will be more expensive. Any new taxes on businesses are passed along to you and me through prices. Hillary's law would be the primary cause of the our cost of living increasing. Over time, policies like this make businesses, large and small, question whether they want to expand their businesses here. Don't worry, politicians will offer them subsidies or tax abatements to stay, and you and I will end up paying the difference once again. You and I, people who work for a living, will always be there to pay the ever-increasing costs that result from all of these mandates.

Hillary views her current proposal as an evolutionary step toward what she really prefers, a single payer system (where everybody gets their health care through the government). This is a terrifying proposal. Not merely because of the impact of socialized medicine on the quality and availability of care, but also because of demographic realities this country faces. I'm referring to the retiring baby boomers who are not millionaires like Bill and Hillary, the huge number of people over the age of sixty who will be especially vulnerable in a socialist health care system, where the tragedy of the commons quickly results in the government having to reign in costs by imposing absolute limits (quotas) on the accessibility of life-saving procedures, leading to waiting lists and unnecessary suffering and death.

Of course, Hillary and her friends won't be affected in the same way because she and Bill have amassed a fortune as well as the political pull that comes in so handy when the government is deciding who gets care and when. Most of us, however, will not be so lucky.

By the way, New York state does not allow you or me or anyone who lives in its borders to invest in a health savings account. This is an example of fascism.  :x It's f---ing outrageous. Why is the state not allowing me to buy a financial product that would enable me to save up money in a tax deductible account that will grow and be there for me and my family when we really need health care. Where does Hillary stand on health savings accounts? She's opposed to them. Who the hell does she think she is?  :x

I strongly disagree that Hillary's goal is a single payer plan.  She receives far too much money from HMO's to support a single payer plan.  And also keep in mind, yeah, she might want universal health care, but it's not the executive branch that passes laws.  Someone in congress would have to bring this to the floor.  Her only true responsibility would be to either sign or veto the legislation.  It's actually somewhat moot to discuss a presidential candidate's health plan.  It will give you an idea of what legislation they will support, but it won't give you an actual idea of what will realistically reach their desk from the legislative branch.  And I have a hard time believing there is enough support in congress, or a filibuster-proof democratic majority to enact universal health care legislation.

And also, I actually do trust the gov't over corporations to run health care.  If we don't like what the gov't is doing, we have the option to vote and make a change.  We have absolutely no say in corporations leadership and decision making.  Their bottom line is money, not the patient or customer.  I'm much more concerned with corporate greed and run-away CEO salaries siphoning money out of actual patient care and into their profit margins than I am of the government, which is by no means a perfect situation.

But I do have friends in both Canada and France that have single payer systems, and they can't even imagine living in a situation where their health care situation is in limbo depending on whether or not they have a job.  Their is a greater sense of community support and understanding that by paying taxes, you are buying into a better society.  The possibility of bankruptcy as a result of unforeseen medical expenses does not exist in other countries, and I consider it morally reprehensible that this country allows its citizens to lose their life savings as a result of medical issues.  And at the same time the previous republican congress made it more difficult for individuals to claim bankruptcy resulting from these very medical emergencies, yet corporate welfare continues. So if god forbid you should get hit by a car tomorrow, you can count on being in debt the rest of your life and no chance to really ever get back on your feet financially.  So yeah, I think there are certain "entitlement" programs that should be in place to protect people.
