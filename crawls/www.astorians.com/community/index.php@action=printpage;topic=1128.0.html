Post by: artgirl on November 18, 2005, 02:28:35 PM
Favorite in Astoria... El Boqueron, hands down.


In all of NYC... that's a tough one. I've also had great dining experiences at Aquavit and Balthazar. (Aquavit gets my vote for one of the most inventive and unexpectedly delicious desserts I've eaten-- raspberry-yellow bell pepper sorbet. Sounds a bit odd, but the flavors were perfectly matched.)

For great pasta... Massimo al Ponte Vecchio, on Thompson St. They have an appetizer of grilled portobello, creamy buffalo mozzarella & eggplant that is wonderful-- the portobello just melts in your mouth. And what's great is you can order their pasta dishes by the half order, so dinner for 2 people you can sample 4 different ones. I was especially impressed by the rigatoni with grilled shrimp & curry cream sauce, and the spaghetti with an egg yolk sauce and bits of crisp bacon.

Perhaps my most extravagant dinner experience in NYC was at Daniel... the tasting menu with wine pairings. It truly was a food-gasm :-)

it's just so hard to answer!  :-)

Trattoria L'Incontro - I have never been disappointed

Pita hot makes the best lamb pitas.  drrooool.

there's a morrocan place that I really like but can't remember the name of - it's near Astor place.

And there's Al Omda.  I went there with a friend one hot summer day, one of those days when it's almost too hot to breathe.  The place was nearly empty except for a few Egyptian guys at the back.  We asked for the fish to share, and the waiter said that they didn't have fish that day.  I said, "Oh, but it was on the specials board" and with no more fuss he left the restaurant and came back a few minutes later with a fish from the market.  They cooked it with lemon and tomatoes and spices.  We had foul madama, pita and other stuff.  It was delicious.  At the end he brought us mint tea.  I was hesitant to drink hot tea in the hot weather but to my surprise it cooled me off.

More recently i went there with a crowd of friends and got an amazing shrimp tajine and smoked hooka.
