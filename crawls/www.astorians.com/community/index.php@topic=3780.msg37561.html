Glaucoma has nothing to do with the lens of the eye, which is what Lasik deals with, so they probably don't have much of an interaction. Ah, here's a web page about it; looks like you can still have Lasik if you have mild, treated glaucoma.

My parents have both had Lasik, and like it, although my mom had complications. She sleeps with her eyes open sometimes, and went into REM sleep and knock a corneal flap out of place and had to have it reseated the next day. Ouch, it makes me woozy just thinking about it! Note that if you're of a certain age, Lasik will fix your nearsightedness, but you'll still need reading glasses.

I, personally, am happy with my glasses. They'd have to knock me clean out before I'd let anyone get close to my eyes with a razor blade...
