Re: Moonstruck Diner coming to Astoria?
« Reply #41 on: April 21, 2010, 03:22:02 PM »
Tried Moonstruck last night.

The atmosphere is very nice.... but then you think "hey I'm in a Moonstruck Diner".  The menu was typical to Moonstruck--regular diner food, though more expensive than I remember.  Though the Steak and Seafood was under 25, the chicken dishes were still 15 and up.

We were one of two tables there, and the waitress was not the most attentive, but very possesive.  She didn't want the male bus boy to get our drinks and yelled at him in front of us, which was awkward.

I ordered  a Lettuce Wrap... which I had read meant it was a sandwich wrapped in lettuce, but it was just a regular wrap.  With a ton of fries (which defeated the low carb wrap LOL).  My partner ordered their Fundango Salad.  The waitress proudly brought the food, and remarked about how BIG the food was.  I asked for some Mayo (because oddly I dip my fries in mayo) and was given a thimbles amount for the 3/4 plate of fries.  I tried their milk shakes, and they weren't too bad...not as good as bareburger, despite being the same price. 

My partner said his salad was good.. a lot of chicken on it, and it was well plated.  He wasn't offered any additional coffee from the one cup that came.

My biggest problem, is that we came in, took of our jackets, and then through the meal put them on because they had all the windows and doors open at 10pm at night.  THe other table also joined us in putting on jackets.... What that meant to me was that the host didn't really care about the customers or observe them to notice they were cold.  Not a good sign. 

With the place as big as it is, and only 2 tables filled... we'll see how long it lasts.

3/5 stars thus far.
