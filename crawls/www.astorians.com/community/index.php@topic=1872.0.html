One of the things I love about this board is that folks will turn me on to a place here in Astoria that I never knew existed. Probably not a Tivo candidate for you, but for me - yes.

Cash in the Attic is an Antiques Roadshow-type program. It is where real people want to sell some of their junk/family heirlooms/boot sale finds, etc to raise cash to go on vacation/buy a car/repair their home/etc. I like it because almost all the stuff these people have is REALLY old, like 16th century. I like antiques and old crap, er, stuff. Anyway after the Attic team comes in, rounds up a bunch of stuff, the antique expert tells where/who/why it was made and the approximate value, then they take the stuff to auction. Sometimes the smallest item that the antique appraiser values low goes for HUGE sums of money at the auction. Of course, my hope is I will come across some similar items in some tiny old junk store, buy it for pennies and then auction it off for a ton of money. Hasn't happened yet.

Sometimes it is so funny just because of how reserved the British act. The appraiser will find something that has been in a drawer for 50 years and it turns out to be worth 1000 pounds and the person, with no facial reaction at all will say something like "I'm totally gobsmacked". That is hiliarious to me! At the auction the appraiser, show host and the family are all very quiet, stand at the back of the room while things are being auctioned and don't bring a lot of attention to themselves.

The American show, on the other hand, especially the host, just irritate the crap out of me. The host talks way too much and has so much fake enthusiasm I want to smack him. The stuff people have is rarely even 100 years old and then they act like fools at the auction, sitting right up front and something sells for 20 bucks and you'd think they had hit the jackpot - screaming, jumping up and down, etc.

Frasier was very good, Niles extremely funny, but I am a Seinfeld junkie - I can never get enough. I have probably seen every episode, except for one, 20 times. The episode where Susan (George's fiance) dies from licking the envelopes for the wedding invitations has eluded me.......
