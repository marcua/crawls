Debbie, since I think some comments I made in the thread regarding the service at Pita Pan prompted you to start this thread, let me say this.

First of all, I am Greek myself.  So there's no anti-Greek bias coming from me.  But at the same time, I'm not going to favor one place over another just because it is Greek or Cypriot owned if I don't like the service or the product there.

Second, I was referring more to the type of service, prices and ambiance that you get at these places.  I've been to every single one on 30th avenue, starting from Plaza Lounge and going all the way up to Flo.  I've also been to Omonia, Kolonaki, Tre, and Lefkos Pirgos.  I'm quite familiar with all of them, though I am not someone that you'll see at the cafes every day or even every weekend.

What I find obnoxious about them, and which prevents me from going more often, as I mentioned, is the service and the overall attitude of some who are there (both employed and as patrons). 

I'll use Athens Cafe for one example.  I find their prices to be obnoxiously high, the highest of any of the establishments on 30th.  A souvlaki sandwich, for instance, without any fries or chips or anything else, costs $8.25 there.  I haven't seen it anywhere else for less than $6 and usually less than $5.  Combine that with the fact that I've found the service there to often be very slow and subpar, and it's really not worth it.  If I want to people-watch, there's many other places I can do so.

As far as the loud music/vibe and so forth....it is true that you see a lot of people who really are there to "impress", with their fast cars which they make a point of revving up in front of the cafes to impress everyone sitting outside, guys in muscle tees and so on just being loud and obnoxious.  Some places more than others blast the music as well, though nothing as loud as I heard yesterday at Pita Pan.  And yes, some music is nice and you don't expect these places to be a library, but when it gets to the point where you can't hear anything anyone with you is telling you, when you can't have a conversation and are reduced to just sitting there and staring at other people, including people who seem to be there exclusively to be stared at, then it's just not an enjoyable experience anymore.  One of the reasons people go out is to socialize, but under those circumstances, I find it hard to do so.

Astoria is full of wonderful places.  As someone else pointed out, the cafes on 30th serve a specific niche.  But, Debbie, as someone who is also of Greek descent and indeed who has traveled to Greece many, many times and will be going again in a week, I can say also that the cafes on 30th give a certain impression of Greek people and establishments that is not wholly accurate.  Just because I'm Greek, doesn't mean that I will follow the herd and go to these places just because other Greeks do.  And having experienced Greece, including living there for up to a few months at a time, I know that there's many other types of Greek establishments that can exist.  Sometimes I like to go to a place where yes, I can enjoy a frappe or a baklava or a freddo but without the showoffs in flashy clothes and flashy cars, the loud music, the high prices, and the slow service.  And while it hasn't been reflected so much in this thread, I've seen many tongue-in-cheek comments made about the places on 30th by other posters on this site, so I know I'm not alone.  Ultimately, people will go to the places they enjoy, and fortunately, Astoria has plenty of establishments for all tastes.  If I am not crazy about the 30th Avenue cafe scene, I'm entitled to that feeling and it shouldn't be construed as some sort of bias or anger.  I'm sure that everyone here has places they enjoy and places that they typically avoid for whatever reason.

I live on 33rd Street between 30th and 31st Avenues. While I can appreciate the safety that Athens Cafe has brought to the area -- it's presence has made 30th Avenue alive late at night, making a walk down the avenue from the train seem less dark and scary -- it, along with the other cafes along 30th Avenue, have also increased traffic and made parking in the area more difficult for those of us who live there. Last night (Saturday night) for example, I spent 45 minutes trying to park my car. It's worse during the summer, and worse on the weekends. Cars go zooming down the block and careen around the corner, making crossing the street at Newtown/30th Avenue very dangerous.

The foot traffic is pretty bad too. People walk down the street late at night, talking really loudly, inconsiderate of the fact that they're walking down a residential street where there are people trying to sleep at 2 a.m.

I have nothing against the cafes, but it would be great if they could or would send the message to their patrons to be considerate of the folks who live in the neighborhood. Or it would be great if the 114th Precinct could maintain more of a presence in the area on weekend nights to address the traffic and noise concerns -- it seems bizarre to me that there are NEVER any cops around 30th Avenue on the weekend nights when throngs of people flock to the area.

I was born in Astoria, and live in the apartment I was raised in, so believe me when I say that it wasn't always like this -- the increase in both pedestrian and vehicular traffic has come along with the influx of cafes in the area. Coincidence? I think not.

Lots of interesting replies.

Some comments:

Neo - I do not care for the term 'Euro-trash' and I do not care about any comments regarding Pita Pan.  I have never been there and will probably never go.  Also, what restaurant would allow you to sit there for a couple of hours sipping coffee, hence the high prices.  And, you know as well as I do that these days a Frappe in a suburb of Athens would set you as back as one in Astoria. 

Flinxt - Since we moved here in 1973, there has actually been an INCREASE in Greek owned businesses, vs a decrease!  Some examples:

Christos Steakhouse (used to be something else)
Demetris
Anna's Corner
Stamatis on Broadway
Kyclades (I think the owner is an Albanian Greek)
Mezzo Mezzo (I think they are Greeks)
Mediterranean in the little mall
Artopolis
Titan
Martha's (George is a Greek American)
Astoria Fish Market
Zenon
Zodiac
Athens Restaurant

Just add to the list folks.  Greeks may have moved out, but they come here to shop, eat and party.

Alexa - What prompted this thread are the deragatory comments regarding the cafes and those who frequent them.  Anyone can write whatever they wish, however, I am feeling a hint of disdain and perhaps a bit of bias.  Cafes have been around for years - remember Hilton, Edelweiss on Broadway, and the ever lasting Lefkos Pirgos?  Athens has also been around for years.  There are new ones like Grand, Flo, and semi new ones like Avenue.  I think they are part of the fabric of the area and are here to stay.  If they were not successful, they would close down; obviously, they are doing well. 

My eighty year old mother and I visit a cafe every Saturday.  We sit there, chat, have an expensive frappe, get smoke blown in our faces, languish in the sun, make friends with the people at the table next to us, listen to too loud obnoxious music and have a good time.  We are not Euro-trash and love our Saturday afternoons at Astoria cafes!
