Interesting you should bring this up. My husband and I did this for a month last summer (inspired by Alissa Cohen's 30-day challenge), and as a result my mom became interested in it and has now been a raw foodist for over a year now, although she does not live here. Also as a result, we are vegan (previously vegetarian).

We struggle with the idea of doing it again (me more than husband), because we do like cooked food so much but I also recognize how healthy that diet is. When we visit my mom for Christmas we have told her that we'll eat what she eats, so for a few days next month we will be!

I don't know of any other raw foodists in Astoria or NYC - at least personally. Clearly there's some good raw food restaurants in the city, but they can be very pricey. There is a raw food group on Meetup.com that meets regularly in Manhattan...not Astoria, but at least you'd get to know other people who are on the diet or who are interested.

Hi Harry,
well I am not a great Raw Foodist yet since I just receantly started, but I can tell you where I get my food. I was trying to find a place with all organic vegetables and fruit in the neighbourhood...I like Fresh Start on 23rd ave and 31st street which has great organic groceries, but fresh produce choices are pretty modest...( lol..just while I was typing this, my big pineapple fell off my fridge and scared me to death  :-) )
Anyway, then I found a small store on Broadway and not sure exactly what street but I think like 29 or 28 that has pretty nice choices of organic produce, but is kind of expensive though. So, I decided to go with Fresh Direct since I discovered on their site that they have a great choices of fresh organic produce...and I have had delivery two times already and have been extremely happy with the fruit and veggies - they ARE very fresh! I have delivery on Saturdays and I have all I need for the whole week...it is ususally around $100 but have in mind that I also order some food for my boyfriend who is not raw but is vegetarian though. So, thats not too bad, and I would rather pay a little bit more and have fresh products delivered to me.
Also, since I am relatively new to this board 2-3 months, I have found out about CSA that people from this board are involved in...it is great thing and I am soooooo looking forward to become a member in 2007.
Hope this helps.
p.s: I am doing prety great so far on Raw, love it and do feel good, but again I just started so the improvements are yet to come  :mrgreen:

Rene,

I have made quite some Raw recipes so far and all were great especially deserts - my boyfriend and his mom were enjoying them and asked for more :-)
I do not think you should be intimidated with the appliances you need...All I use all the time is blender ( I still have the old Black and decker which I payed $30...and is working great it blends well all my greens even kale and swisschard with the hard stalks, once it dies I will get more expensive one to last longer ), small food processor - Cuisineart which I payed $47 and is awesome and citrus juicer - $ 15...two great knives and you good to go!
I higly recommend a book for people like you ( and me ) who have little time to prepare food by Jennifer Cornbleet "Raw Food Made Easy" it really is easy and fast - you just need to know a day in advance what you want to "cook" tomorrow and make sure you soak the nuts if needed...other than that it takes 15 minutes to make the dish...and the food is pretty awesome!
