That's very pretty, Harlan!

Today I roasted my ramps with potatoes.  I seasoned the potatoes with fleur de sel and paprika with olive oil, and lightly oiled the ramps, roasting them all together in a 400 degree oven for 30 minutes.  Well, can I just say WOW???  Man were those ramps tasty!  The green parts cooked until crispy and sort of melted in your mouth with a similar texture as Ali's deep fried spinach.  The blubs got all carmelized, too.  The potatoes browned nicely and cooked perfectly.  They were little German heirloom potatoes from the Greenmarket, from the stall that sells burdock and jerusalum artichokes and such.

I wish I had more potatoes and ramps so I could do it all over again!  This is my favorite kind of food - very simple preparation of top notch ingredients where the genuine flavors of the ingredients shine though.

Quote from: JustDesserts on April 25, 2006, 10:30:07 PM
Where is this magical Greenmarket place?

North end of Union Square, Monday, Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday, from 8am until about 6pm! Year-round, rain or shine! It's not the biggest farmer's market ever, but it's got very very good stuff. Produce, bread, plants and flowers, honey and maple syrup, some really good pretzels, natural meat and fish. Some chefs will go there at dawn and grab the best daily produce for their restaurants...

There are smaller greenmarkets all over the city, including one in Long Island City, but it's easier to hop on the N and go to the main one...

The market in Madison, WI goes in a huge ring around the capitol building, so it's eight linear blocks long. My mom used to take me and my sister there as kids, most every Saturday morning to get vegetables and sweet rolls and all sorts of great stuff... One of the odd things about that market is that everyone walks counter-clockwise, and always has, and if you try to walk clockwise, people glare at you.

The market in Urbana, IL is really really tiny. Takes about 5 minutes to walk through it once to see what's good, then another 10 minutes to buy what you want. Which is mostly huge bags of sweet corn.
