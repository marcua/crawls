After a couple of months looking via Craigs list and a couple of really bad experiences. There were two instances where I put down a deposit and filled out all of the paperwork and run the credit report only to find that the apartment wasn't available or, in the second instance that they didn't take cats after I'd made it clear that I had one. These were both with the same guy - I don't think he was a Real Estate agent, I think that he was a runner.  It was all very unprofessional.


I walked into Page off of the street. They showed me 5 apartments and the last one was the charmer. Unlike the experience of an earlier poster who used to live in a Page building, all of the apartments that I saw were in pretty decent condition. I saw some horrible places during my previous searches.


They don't officially manage my building but do go-between me & the landlord and employ people who have worked on my apartment (I don't know how that works).  Everything gets taken care of in a pretty timely manner. My biggest problem was with my upstairs neighbor who wouldn't allow access to her apartment so that a water leak could get repaired and there was a strange gas smell but they did their best to work around her. Fortunately, she moved and the issues have been resolved. I don't have much of a roach problem since she moved out either, so I can't speak to that.

jayhawk, I got a pretty good 1BR near Ditmars in January this year for $1200. I can't make any promises but the broker I used was Peter Horowitz who runs this website: http://myastoriarealestate.com/
A lot of the stuff he posts is higher end, but he may have some other things to show you that are perhaps not quite as glamorous with their remodeled kitchens and everything. Worth a shot.
I had tried calling Kim at Halvatzis during the same time period, and she had nothing to show me under $1400 and sort of acted like lower priced apartments didn't exist and wasn't interested in my business unless I would pay $1400.

Peter showed me a wide range of apartments and I was happy working with him. He's a broker where I feel like he actually takes his job seriously and puts a lot of time and effort into it. I'm shy with dealing with random landlords and stuff and so I definitely appreciate a friendly person who does that legwork for me. YMMV. I personally don't mind using a broker for that reason, although yeah, the fee is painful on top of all the other costs involved in moving.

Another broker you can try is Jenny at Avanguard, who got us a cheapo 3BR a couple years ago but I didn't have any luck with her this time around: http://www.avanguardrealty.com/

Hi everybody,


I have had so much trouble getting into Astoria! I love the neighborhood, and I've had three apartments fall through for various reasons over the last three months, for reasons as varied as a moldy floor disintegrating under my feet (I'd seen it furnished, and they'd hidden a standpipe under a bed--avoid any basement apartments going for under a grand on 21st-24th ave!!) to an agent getting offered more and deciding to make up some lies about the owner changing his mind..:(


Luckily I recuperated all my money but was still on the hunt for a good place. I called Cesar at MQ realty late one night and he was all business. He showed me a place that was right in my budget and was considerably nicer than what I'd seen before. His realtor fee was closer to a month and a half, but I can't say I regret the expense. He really must get the best stuff. He was professional and courteous the whole way through. Not to mention funny and friendly.


I couldn't recommend him more. Not sure this is the right place for it, but he had asked me to put in a good word if the experience was a good one. And it sure was. I'd ring him now. Don't be afraid of the professional looking website. You're not getting some sharky agent who's never been around the block.
