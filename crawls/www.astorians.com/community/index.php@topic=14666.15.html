It's not just the caliber of the stores, though that has been on the decline for years.  It's the vacancies, which began quite a few years ago, when the economy was still "strong," thanks to the greedy landlords who seem to be content with having their stores vacant for years at a time, rather than lowering rent by a couple thousand dollars a month to bring some businesses into the area.

Someone here pointed out a few weeks ago that Pamela's Pizza, which had been on Steinway for at least twenty years, closed because the landlord kicked them out in order to double the rent from $7,000 to $14,000 a month.  Except that after many weeks of being vacant, the landlord has been forced to reduce the asking price back down to $7,000 with still no takers in sight.  In the meantime, an established local business was lost, due to short-sightedness and greed.
