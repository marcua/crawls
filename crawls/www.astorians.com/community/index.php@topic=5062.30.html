I've always liked the frigidly cold weather over the brutally hot weather.   When it's like this, you keep moving and wear lots of layers while when it's oppressively hot, there's only so much you can do to cool off.

I'm on the fourth floor of my building with a southern exposure.   I get the sun beating down in my apt all day so it's nice and toasty when I need it to be.    I have risers in my rooms only except my bedroom which I haven't opened more than three times in the 2 years I've been here.    I have to replace a radiator on the 5th floor and I'm thinking of giving them mine, just so I won't have to lug one up from the basement.

My sympathies tho to those of you with heat issues in your apts.   There are days when I wish I could use that as an excuse to bundle up and do nothing.    (I do that anyways, I just like to have an excuse sometimes to avoid the guilt)

You know, I try to be positive when it gets cold like this. "Oh, it's 4 degrees out? Look at how pretty the sky is!" "Aaah, at least I'm not sweating through my clothes by the time I get to the subway," etc etc etc.

But you know what? Screw it. I HATE HATE HATE cold weather. Anything below 40 is annoying, anything below 20 is un-freaking-bearable. And the wind! My god, the wind. The constant static electricity! The runny noses! I can't stand the fact that I can't throw the windows open and get fresh air flowing through my apartment, or through classrooms, or in my car (on the, like, one day a week that I drive somewhere, which is an epic journey because if I don't let my car warm up it'll stall). I simply can not deal.

And all you people from colder places, thanks - you've just given me a reason never to visit those places except in July.  And for what its worth, I'm from New England, so I *should* like this, but...gah! It's HORRIBLE. Horrible, horrible, horrible.
