It is hard to answer your question and give a number without details or viewing the job.  Straight forward doesn’t mean the same thing to everyone.   Some painters will base an estimate by room charge ($ x dollars per room) and others will give an estimate for the entire job.  Is there prep work (patching, sanding, taping, priming, or furniture moving) involved?  Is it just painting walls or ceilings too?  Do you want one color or multiple colors?  Will it take one coat, two, maybe even three or four coats for proper coverage to get to the color on the can?  The brand/type of paint will also be a factor.  Brands like Benjamin Moore paint will give better coverage then a cheaper brand of paint. Are you looking to paint white over white which would be considered the most straight forward job?  Are you supplying the paint or is the painter?  A professional painter will take all these factors (and probably more) into consideration before giving an estimate.  I don’t expect you to answer all my questions but you should be aware that painting isn’t always as straight forward as it seems.
