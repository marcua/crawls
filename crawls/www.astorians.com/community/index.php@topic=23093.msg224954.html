I am going to try not to misspeak and unintentionally insult anyone like I did earlier.  I am a bartender at Bud's and I will do my best to clear anything up.  We are not a chain.  Chains are usually defined as restaurants with national locations, but the same quality of food, service, etc.  Essentially, in a chain, everything, from the staff to the food, is regulated and very systematic.  If you go to an Applebees in Colorado, you will get the same food and experience as you would if you went to one in, say, Idaho. 


Bud's Ale House is part of a restaurant group.  A restaurant group is a collection of people that have more than one store in the same state.  This is the first Bud's Ale House in the USA...so there is no way it is a chain.  And please don't bring Hooters into this discussion.  We are not a Hooters.  The members of the group run a few Hooters in NY, but Bud's does not equal Hooters.  How can I prove this?  Everyone, men and women, will be wearing jeans (full length) and crew shirts.  Argument settled.  The owners of this place know what it means to have a successful restaurant, they have several in NY already and I have been treated as an individual employee, not just another face as corporations often do. 


This is the first Bud's in a hopefully long line of Bud's to make their rise in the NY area.  I hope this cleared up any questions.  Basically, all I am saying, is stop judging, jumping to conclusions, and assuming what will be and just come in when the doors open and see for yourself.  I think it is exactly what the opposite end of Astoria (from Ditmars) needs.  A down home, neighborhood sports bar without the corporate glitz and glam.  The place is going to rock and roll and I hope you are going to join us when it does.


I hope this helped.


ps - I can't think of any national chains that have 80 beers on tap.  Just saying.
