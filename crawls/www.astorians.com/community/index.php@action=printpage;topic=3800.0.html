Title: Re: Would ya stop with the litter in my hallway already?
Post by: Gleason on October 17, 2006, 09:52:43 AM
Nice idea, but it will not work. Like advocates of guns who hide behind the Bill of Rights, this litter that masquerades as advertising is a "freedom of speech" issue. Recall newspaper articles going back several decades on this problem.

As said before, nothing ever gets done.

In NYC everything is geared to commercial interests. If you open a McDonalds, you get a traffic light. If you complain that a crossing is dangerous, nothing happens until a kid gets run over. If you a fighting noise from a cafe, or a builder who bricks up your windows, you will almost always lose.

Apathy from our leadership and stubborn bureaucracy from the city is honed to wear you down every time. Every rare victory that you read about is eclipsed by a torrent of unsung failures. 

Someone has to ask our leadership why, despite all our glaring problems that the posters on this board notice, nothing gets done (except the odd reassuring headline). As long as our community is shy in taking its leadership to task, nothing, I repeat, nothing will ever change.

And these problems are just getting worse and worse with each passing season. Recall a quote from Ovid, "add little to little and soon you have a great pile."
