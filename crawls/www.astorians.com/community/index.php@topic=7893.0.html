Reviving this thread.  Went there for dinner and it was deee-lish.  Had a yummy grilled chicken with spinach and goat cheese.  To start, a 3 dip appetizer with hummos, baba ganoush and skordalia (which was yummy but not thick enough).  It was very comfortable although there was hardly any one having dinner when I was there.  Looking forward to the beautiful outdoor garden when the weather gets nicer.  They don't have wine by the bottle which I don't understand but the mixed drinks are yummy.

I went last night and had a Magic Punch, which really was magical - that sneaky combination of fruity delish and oh-so-well-concealed vodka that makes the effects felt only 10 minutes after you've quickly sipped down your juice. Righteous.

We had the platter of hummus, skordalia and baba ganoush. Pretty darn tasty and perfect if you like your skordalia heavy on the garlic as I do. I then managed to happily consume an entire pound of sirloin in the form of a perfectly cooked burger on a deliciously seasoned but ineffective flat bread (what flat bread could hold up a pound of meat and survive?!). The accompanyinig fries were expertly seasoned; so much so that my dining companion could not keep his (grubby) mitts off them. All those meat and potatoes for $10 - hooray! My companion had the chicken palliard with spinach and liked it a lot. I also noted that they have a $10 veggie stir-fry on the menu for the meatless among us.

Only disappointment was that the wait person told us that they had "run out of spinach artichoke dip" but it was 7:30 pm on Easter Sunday and we were the ONLY diners in the place. Ran out? The staff ate it all? You had a food fight? I would have preferred her to say "we ain't got none," but that's my fuss bunny way. Oh, and they "do not yet do wine by the bottle" - I don't really know what that means except we could not buy a bottle of wine, only by the glass.

The place is SWANKY!!! I love the swank. Very artfully crafted backyard garden in which I look forward to dining soon. Comfortable benches. Remarkable fake round chia pet-looking greenery on the semi-retro wooden wall sculpture.

The only thing that may turn some off is the disco-thumping tunage, but I liked it.
