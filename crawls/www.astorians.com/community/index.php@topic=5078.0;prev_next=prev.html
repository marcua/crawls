Now I have a second credit card with some rewards program so that I can earn points towards airline tickets. I don't understand how you have a balance if you pay it off in full every month.  Paying it off in full every month means not having a balance.

You get charged interest on any item that isn't paid off in full during the first cycle it's on the card.  You charge $10 to the card and pay off $4 when the bill comes, you'll owe interest on the remaining $6.

Essentially, you will pay interest unless you pay off your balance to $0 in full every month.  That is the only way to avoid interest fees.  It is the way credit card companies make money.  They are counting on most people just paying the minimum amount of what they owe, instead of the total balance every month.  Think of it as a loan, and if you can't pay it back within a month, then start seriously monitoring your purchases.  It's easy money, but it has it's consequences.  Don't let the interest build up, it only gets worse over time.  Avoid debt, don't play the game.  Make sure to call your company and read the fine print, to better understand all the policies.

It sounds like your card issuer uses double-cycle billing, which charges interest on any amount charged from the moment it's charged until the bill is paid, meaning you wind up paying interest charges on debt that has already been paid off. Do a search on the forums at CreditBoards.com for more information.

PJ--if you can pay off the entire balance, that would be best. Paying more than the minimum due is a good idea because it will reduce the actual amount you charged, thus reducing what you will eventually pay in interest. As far as your credit report or score goes, I'm not sure whether paying double the minimum is better or worse than paying the minimum--either are definitely better than paying less than the minimum or paying late.

So I figured out what was happening with my credit card and why I was paying interest even though I payed my balance in full.

It turns out that I have a 25 day grace period for all of my purchases. That means if I pay off my balance within 25 days, I don't get charged interest. Well I pay all of my bills on the same day every month. And as some of you might know, a month is between 28 and 31 days. So any purchases I made in those first 3-6 days after I pay my previous bill wouldn't get payed off in the 25 day grace period. So that is where the interest charges came from. It is pretty lame if you ask me. It almost seems like they did it intentionally for this exact reason.

Thanks everyone for your input. And thanks Purplecurlygirl for bringing up double cycle billing, i didn't know what that was and it is a good thing to look out for.
