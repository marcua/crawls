Post by: Frappe on April 20, 2007, 12:11:59 PM
Happy Graduation.

If you are willing to go bistro french, a good place close by (and I say this even though I got canned from my waitering job there a few years ago) is Cafe Joule on 1st Avenue and 58th/59th Street.  Short cab ride or nice walk if the weather is fine.   Good food, nice wine list, very reasonable prices, entrees starting under $20.  Small kitchen, so the peanut dietary concern can be monitored closely.

The chef, Vernejoule, was a french wiz kid chef who lost his marbles while cooking at a four star hotel in France.  He became a short order cook at their version of a greasy spoon and was there for a few years until he met his American wife who was there on holiday.  They got married and opened this place.  Food has a good tenderness to it.
