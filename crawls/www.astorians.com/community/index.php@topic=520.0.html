"Don’t miss
OUTDOOR CINEMA 2005
Cuba Night
Wednesday, July 13 at 7:00 pm
At Socrates Sculpture Park
 
FREE ADMISSION!
 
Join us for an evening of Cuban film, music and food this Wednesday at Socrates Sculpture Park!
 
 
Music:            Son Amigos
Food:              La Cabanna
Film:               Buena Vista Social Club
 
The Buena Vista Social Club, the album and the film, is the story of a remarkable re-living of the classic era of Cuban popular music, the resurrection of musical forms and personalities that have wielded an extraordinary influence over Western music. It is the story of one of the most unique musical narratives of our time. Its impact has been not only of bringing a crew of great musicians out of ignominy on the island, but also of providing, for audiences in the U.S. and Europe, a kind of Rosetta Stone, a way to interpret the Latin influence on world music over the last half century.
 
Son Amigos is made up of José Luís Martínez (co-founder of the internationally renowned Los Van Van), Kenny Kozol (percussionist and composer), and Josh Levine (bassist and singer).  Through three-part vocal harmonies, acoustic guitar, double bass and percussion, they beautifully interpret son, guaracha, cha cha cha, bolero, rumba and other Cuban rhythms. To hear a sample of their music or learn more, visit Son Amigo’s website at http://www.sonamigos.com
 
La Cabanna Restaurant serves delicious Cuban specialties.  Visit them at 34-44 Steinway Street.
 
 
OUTDOOR CINEMA 2005: Celebrating the Cultural Diversity of Queens
Wednesday evenings through August 31
Performances at 7 pm, films at sunset
Admission is free.
 
Bring a blanket or a chair and picnic with us in the Park! Enjoy the spectacular sunset over the Manhattan skyline, watch live music and dance performances, and enjoy international films set amid the Park’s latest exhibition of contemporary sculpture, Sport.
 
Prescreening performances begin at 7:00 pm, films begin at sunset.  All performances and screenings take place in the Park.


Please check our website to confirm performances and films.  Films are subject to change or cancellation due to weather.  For directions, please visit our website:  www.socratessculpturepark.org
Outdoor Cinema 2005 is curated by Museum of the Moving Image."

Don’t miss
OUTDOOR CINEMA 2005
India Night
Wednesday, August 24 at 7:00 pm
At Socrates Sculpture Park
Long Island City, NY
 
FREE ADMISSION!
 
Enjoy the waterfront breeze and the sunset over the Manhattan skyline as you take in an evening of Indian American film, music and food this Wednesday at Socrates Sculpture Park!


Dance/Music:     Natya Raga
Food:                     5 Star Punjabi
Film:                      Pather Panchali
 
Wednesday, August 24   India: Pather Panchali
1955, 112 mins. Directed by Satyajit Ray. The tough life of a poor Bengali family is dramatized with great realism and humanity by Ray, in the first and most powerful film of his "Apu Trilogy," featuring music by Ravi Shankar. Not rated. Bengali with English subtitles.
 
Natya Raga: Visitors are sure to be delighted by this special performance of Indian dance and music. The program will include two classical dances, Allaripu and Om Nama Shivaya, followed by a mix of traditional and modern music including Veena,  Maithreem Bhajatha and Bhangra!


Five Star Punjabi will serve a variety of delicious Indian dishes. Visit them at  1315 43rd Avenue in Long Island City!




OUTDOOR CINEMA 2005: Celebrating the Cultural Diversity of Queens
Wednesday evenings through August 31
Performances at 6:30 pm, films at sunset
Admission is free.
 
Bring a blanket or a chair and picnic with us in the Park! Enjoy the spectacular sunset over the Manhattan skyline, watch live music and dance performances, and enjoy international films in the outdoor setting of Socrates Sculpture Park.
 
Prescreening performances begin at 6:30 pm, films begin at sunset.  All performances and screenings take place in the Park.


Please check our website to confirm performances and films.  Films are subject to change or cancellation due to weather.  For directions, please visit our website:  www.socratessculpturepark.org
Outdoor Cinema 2005 is curated by Museum of the Moving Image.


FINAL NIGHT:
 
Wednesday, August 31    DOUBLE FEATURE!!
PERFORMANCES BEGIN AT 6:30 TONIGHT!
Greece:         Zorba the Greek
1964, 146 mins. Directed by Michael Cacoyannis. With Anthony Quinn, Alan Bates, Irene Pappas. In his definitive performance, Quinn plays the earthy, larger-than-life peasant Zorba. Sponsored by Commerce Bank.
Followed by    
South Korea: Take Care of My Cat
2001, 112 mins. Directed by Jae-eun Jeong. The vitality of contemporary South Korean cinema is shown in this coming-of-age film about five young women in the port city of Inchon navigating the hazards of young adulthood. Sponsored by Korean Cultural Service New York and the Korea Times.
Traditional Korean dance performance by Song Hee Lee Dance Company at 6:30
Greek music by Mikrokosmos at 7:00


 
The 2005 Outdoor Cinema program is supported by funding from the New York City Council, Commerce Bank, City Parks Foundation, Partnerships for Parks, The New York Times Company Foundation, Korean Cultural Service New York, Loudmouth Records, Consulate General of Slovenia, and Sambazon.
 
The film series is made possible by the generosity of Scharff Weisberg.
 
Socrates Sculpture Park is grateful for the generous contributions made by Costco Wholesale, The Korea Times, ImageKing Visual Solutions, and Spacetime C.C..
