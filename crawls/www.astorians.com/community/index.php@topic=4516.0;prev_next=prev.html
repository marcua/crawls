I agree with McDirk - I really like Jackson Heights, and it was the other area in Queens I was considering when I first moved here (6 years ago). I like all the different ethnic restaurants, the diversity, the garden apartments, the train lines, etc.

However, now that I've been here longer, I'm not sure I'd want to live there and could definitely see what firelight is talking about. I think it might not have as much of a community feel as Astoria does (but that could just be b/c I'm here in Astoria and have a better feel for it), and I think it can be dirtier and noisier. Plus, the lack of green space is a definite downside. I really love having Astoria Park and the waterfront closeby (though of course that will depend where in Astoria you are).
