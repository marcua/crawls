I agree that the guy is messed up. The violence here is clearly uncalled for. But I'm not sure that it's any less justified a reason to kill the cat out of rage, than to kill one so you can have some nice cat burger and cat wings down at the local "Cat n' Chips"

I think the think that makes us cringe at this is because we're able to see this event through the perspective of the cat. It clearly didn't deserve to die, so we identify (side) with the cat. If it's the act of considering the cat's perspective that makes us cringe at this story I'm not sure killing a cat for fast-food vs. out of rage is any different - from the cat's perspective. Both are completely unnecessary.

What we're talking about is the taking of unnecessary life, and that's upsetting to us. Especially when it can be easily shown that an animal based diet is completely unnecessary in developed countries.

I'm not entirely against eating meat, but I just see a bit of irony (even hypocrisy) in how we treat people that kill unnecessarily out of rage, vs. killing unnecessarily out of fast/processed food addiction.

Merm, to me the difference is the reason why the animal exists. If people didn't eat beef, drink milk, or wear leather, there would be essentially no cows in North America. The reason why a cow exists on this planet, and (if it's lucky and is going to be eaten by a yuppie) has the pleasure of eating grass on a pasture, is because we bred it. So humanely killling the cow in order to eat it is part of its existence, and is not a violence against its existence.

For a cat, however, it was bred to be an unbearably cute companion animal for people. Its reason for being is to sit on the couch and purr while we watch TV. Without us, there would be a lot fewer cats, and they would mostly not look like inbred Persians. 

It's a violation of our moral contract with a cat to kill it in a fight with our girlfriend, and thus violate its reason for being, just as it would be a violation of our moral contract with a cow to shoot it and let it rot.

Read it again, Wasabisam, and answer for yourself. Would you rather (a) I kill you tomorrow, or (b) have never lived. For animals raised for food, that's the question. Chosing to live to old age and die of natural causes is not an option for a Black Angus.

Yeah, but what gives us the right as Human Animals to kill other animals? Look - my view is skewed and will never reflect yours because I am a life-long vegetarian, and over the last year or more have become more ethically interested in the treatment of animals no matter their human-imposed category (food or pet). So it is difficult for me to say, "yeah your argument makes sense because cows really are just beef that haven't been killed yet." To me cows are cows, not meat (and on some farms are very large pets).

It is just interesting what categories we arbitrarily put animals in. In this country dogs are considered pets, but in some parts of the world they are food just as much as cows are food in America. Other animals, like horses are functional/practical so they are neither food nor pet - used for work on the farm, racing, etc. (Although I think they do wind up as dog and cat food, but I don't have anything to back that up with.) Yet in other countries horse meat is just a normal way of life. Then you have the whole display animals thing with zoos. Monkeys, tigers, elephants, all sorts of exotic animals wind up in zoos. But in other parts of the world monkeys are prized for their delicious (sarcasm) brains, tigers for their colorful coat, and elephants for their ivory.

Sure - I own a cat, and I suppose you could just come back to my argument and say, "well, you're not being fair because you own a cat. If fair was fair, then you would not support the domestication of animals." And maybe that would be the right thing to do in conjunction with my argument that we shouldn't place arbitrary labels on animals just so we can do whatever we want with them - eat them, pet them, use them for their assets or abilities.

While I don't think that merm is saying this guy should be roaming the streets, I do think that his point is valid - why treat any animal different than another animal? Saying, "because that's just the way it is and the way humans have set up the cycle of life - cows=beef, cats=pets" isn't much of an argument, IMHO.
