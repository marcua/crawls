We must live near each other - that's my stop too. My boyfriend and I regularly go to that
theatre. I don't think it's a big trip at all - about 15 minutes total for the two stops on the
train and the quick walk.

But yes, that is the only one in the area.

I go to the movies often and do go in the city as well. The easiest one to get to are the two
on 42nd Street - the AMC and the Regal E-walk. They're directly across the street from each
other on 42nd between 7th & 8th. Just take the last, rear car of the train and get off at Times
Square. Go up and walk around by where the number 1 train and exit there. They're both just up
the block.

Total travel time is about 25 minutes.

I think the timing and movie choice more than the movie theatre determine how awful your crowd is.  I really try to steer clear of Saturday night movies anywhere, as the crowds are always atrocious and act so as well.

I go to a lot of early matinees, or late-night films when I want to see something that would appeal to kids (Harry Potter, for example).  For cult movies, I go early to try to catch the cult crowd; for popular movies I go later to avoid the masses.

I've found myself much less annoyed by people around me that way - no matter what theatre I'm at.

The occasional pointed glare also works in many circumstances (both at the movies, and in live theatre).

I love the fact that the Kaufman Astoria almost always has the movies I want to see.  They tend to have a good mix of new, popular releases and slightly more obscure, limited release films.
