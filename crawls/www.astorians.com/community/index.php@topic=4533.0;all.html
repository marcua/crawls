Quote from: urrisgirl on January 26, 2007, 10:53:06 PM
I have never tried Brazillian food. Is it mostly meat dishes?

Even though I've been to two Brazilian restaurants, I still consider myself ignorant of what exactly Brazilian food is.  Although, when I hear Brazilian food, I always think of grilled meats.  Both places that I've been too are churrascarias (steak houses).  It's where you keep eating all sorts of grilled meats until you either give up, explode, or die of heart failure.  Basically the way it works is that you are given a signal card, one side is colored red, the other side green.  There are several "waiters" walking around with a long skewer of grilled meats....I think they call this rodizio style...each waiter will come to your table and slice meats for you if you have your signal card green side up...if you want to take a rest...make sure to turn the card red side up.  It's quite impressive for someone who has never been to one of these places.

One of the places that I've been to is Churrascaria Plataforma in the city.  It's pricey...I went there for one of those team meeting dinners and I think it cost about $50 a person....It's a buffet so make sure you skip several meals before going  :-D.  What is nice about Plataforma is that they have this huge salad buffet that non-meat eaters can enjoy...and if you like shrimp...the buffett has them already peeled  :mrgreen:.  The other thing that they are also know n for are their mojitos.

Hi All!

So, I popped into the new Brazillian place yesterday for lunch. It's called Barril grill... I'm new to the whole brazillian food thing but, was willing to try especially since there is so much meat involved.

I was a little confused when I just walked in. I wasn't sure if, i sat down first and someone was going to bring me a plate or I just grabbed one. I asked the waitress and she said just grab a plate and fill her up.....Then, she pointed to a window at the end of the buffet and said that's where you get your meat. So, I jumped right in. 

The buffet consisted of 3 diffrent types of salad, regular lettuce, a shreaded mustard green with carrots and another one was that kinda oily looking (couldn't really identify what was in it) smoothered potatoes, pasta and a couple meats marinated in different sauces, brown beans, black beans and 2 diffrent kinds of rice. Then the meat window (heavens to barbarians unite)Chicken, Pork, Beef, sausage made in so many diffrent variations it was insane. The guy at the meat window was kind enough to show me some of the diffrent kinds of meat before I actually asked for it which was nice and man, did they look good.


The second excellent part.. after filling my plate with black beans, salad and 2 meats (one was an entire steak) I walked over to the weighing scale remembering all those times I was in the city and went to a salad buffet and ended up paying 14 dollars but, instead the cost was.....drumroll please 6.50. Yup 6.50. 1 pound of food = 6.50 That might be the lunch price and the dinner price might be higher but, man, I had so, much food on my plate it was insane.

The steak was Ahhh-Mazzzz-zing! So, yummy! I paired it with the mustard greens and carrots and I was in heaven.

I'm going back for dinner soon and taking friends (there is also a full-bar which is totally helpful for dinner).

I give it a big-two thumbs up. I'm not a brazillian food expert by any means and will absolutly try those other two but, this steak was pretty damn good.

Only weird thing, I was a little confused when I first walked in, the tables and chairs are scattered everywhere so, you kinda don't know what direction to head for. wiggle wag your way through them and grab a plate at the buffet (on your left and up some stairs from the entrance) and don't forget to hit the meat window, then back down the stairs and to the bar (on your right from the entrance)to get your food weighed. They also take credit cards which is nice.

Ole, Ole, Ole, Ole  I just realized I love brazillian grub   8-)

I got back from work late tonight and my wife is at work so I decided to try Barril Grill.  Its on the southwest corner of of 31st and Broadway.  It was late (a little after 9) when I got there.  Probably not a good idea to eat at a buffet this late.  The place was clean and well lit.  As urrisgirl pointed out, it was confusing as to what you need to do.  I asked the attendant what to do and she led me to the salad bar.  At that time of the night, the bar was sparse (again, I should not come here this late)...I grabbed some greens, rice, and some fried pork (ughhh...I can feel  my arteries clogging up).  Then to the rodizio grill window where a very friendly gentleman tells you what is available...theres chicken, pork, steak, and turkey with bacon....YUMMY!  :mrgreen:  I asked for moderate portions of the steak and pork and a sausage...I love this stuff...but I have to be careful not to overdo it.

After you have your fill, you go to the register and weigh your plate.  Mine came up to a whopping $5.63 ($0.94/lb).  WOW!  I ordered a Corona along with it...total cost of dinner was $10.63+tax.  I will defintely go again...but not this late.  The barbeque was really good.  Oh and you can also do take out.
