So my partner and I went to brunch this morning with a few other people.  We got there just after 11, and apparently they had opened only a few minutes before, despite the advertised opening time of 10:00.  Also, they don't have a brunch menu yet--all they're serving is their lunch/dinner menus, which consists mostly of soups, salads, sandwiches, and sides. So if you're looking for breakfast-type things, you're out of luck (except for biscuits and doughnuts).  Coffee is Stumptown, which was really nice.  Hot tea is not on the official menu, but you can ask for it--you'll just get Lipton's though. 

Ah, but how was the food, you may ask?  Well, the cheddar & scallion biscuit was flaky, if not overly cheesy (a plus in my book).  We had to wait for a fresh batch of the maple & bacon biscuits, but hot out of the oven they were excellent--peppery and salty.  Definitely ask for a side of the maple butter.  I had a taste of someone's mac & cheese.  They give you a huge portion and it's very rich.  The cinnamon-sugar doughnuts are just like the previous review described.

It's clear that the food holds promise, but they're not quite ready for action.  The delayed opening time led to a slam of people, so there was a bit of a wait for food and service was a little disorganized.  By the time we left, around 12:40, they were out of biscuits and a few other things--they were turning people away even though the crowds had thinned out.  I'd give it a couple of months before returning.

P.S.  They had bags of Zapp's Spicy Creole potato chips behind the counter--I bought one for $2.

We officially have the worst luck with this place. EVER. So we went last night at 8, and the place was pretty full.  We got in, sat down, and then proceeded to wait for a while while our waiter sat around talking to some other tables.  As I mentioned we knew what we were ordering before we walked in the door. So after a while the hostess noticed that we hadn't had our drink order taken, so she came back and took the order, and dropped off our Mexican coke and ginger ale.  Luckily for me, I carry a bottle opener, because the coke wasn't opened (mexicokes are a pry top, not a screw top), so I could enjoy my sugared beverage.
While we were eating we took in the ambiance, which is fairly cute, the music was that kind of cool indie rock at a level where you can hear it, but it's not oppressive. Throughout the night that all changed, they music was turned up to a horribly annoying level, the level of the lights were monkeyed with and tweaked. The staff did that annoying party trick of switching songs halfway through.
So the waiter finally comes by, and takes our order, which was a maple bacon biscuit, fried green tomato side, potato salad side, meatloaf sandwich, and the fried chicken sandwich. We were getting really excited because everyone around us was getting this food that just looked amazing.  My mouth was watering when I saw other people's meatloaf and chicken sandwiches. So the biscuits came out after about ten minutes (biscuits plural was odd, since we had only ordered one to split, but whatever, I can handle my own biscuit). At this point, we noticed a very hip couple come in sit at one of the other tables and order. So after we'd finished our biscuits, we waited for probably 20 minutes.  The couple that came in while we were biscuit'ing got an amazing looking meatload sandwich and an amazing looking chicken sandwich (something my better half joked about, saying, "I bet they got our order") while we waited.
So then our food came out: potato salad, chicken sandwich and a fried green tomato sandwich. We told the waiter that this clearly wasn't our order, and that we had ordered the meatloaf sandwich and the tomato side. He apologizes, goes back to the kitchen and then comes back and informs us that there's no more meatloaf, and was there anything else we wanted, and that he was going to comp the screwed up sandwich (I will give him points only for this). I didn't want anything else, but we begrudgingly settled on the pork belly sandwich.
So while we waited for our less than suitable replacement, we split the chicken sandwich, which was over fried, to the point that the chicken was almost black, it lacked any semblence of the hot sauce that was listed on the menu, or really any seasoning at all - no salt, no pepper, and was just really dry. The worst part about this is that every other chicken sandwich we saw had these light, golden fired chicken pieces that looked wonderful. That said the potato salad was really good (the secret ingredient is dijon) and probably the best thing I ate while we were there.
We had been finished with the chicken for about 5 more minutes when the correct tomato order came. These were pretty well cooked, but again lacked any sort of seasoning.  I had to salt and pepper each one of the slabs of fried tomato, which made for an overall underwhelming experience. It was a note that we noticed other patrons salting pretty much every dish that came their way (mac & cheese included).
As we picked at these, the waiter took my plate (and my fork) leaving me to finish the potato salad with a spoon and a knife, and brought out the pork belly sandwich. I've eaten a lot of pork belly, and this was hands down the most disappointing version thereof ever experienced.  In fact it was downright disgusting. We opened the sandwich to not find pork belly (which is like bacon, in that there is actual meat / muscle interspersed with the fat) but two HUGE slabs of pure fat. I think there may have been 1 miniscule piece of actual meat on one of the slabs. At this point we felt defeated by a cut rate version of southern food, and decided it wasn't worth our time to make a fuss over just how disgusted we were by the pork belly (and to be fair, the very hip young lady to our left had a pork belly sandwich that looked downright meaty and delicious).
At this point, i don't ever see myself going back, which is a shame because as a fat pothead who loves southern food this place would be great, you know being 3 blocks away and all, but there are things the kitchen should catch.  Serving large slabs of fat. Well overcooked chicken, no seasoning on anything, that I just can't get by. And to me, the most disappointing part of all of it was that every other dish that came out of the kitchen looked effing amazing. I can't even express how disappointed I was when we left that place last night.  But based on the bike messengers, the youngs in weird clothes and s___ in their faces, who were literally eating the place up, it doesn't seem like they'll care about losing the business of a crotchety fart who is old beyond his years.
