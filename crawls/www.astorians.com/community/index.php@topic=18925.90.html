Quote from: Harlan on August 05, 2010, 03:40:55 PM
I agree that this is alarming, but I don't know that there's any evidence of a crime wave. In fact, if I may relink to something I posted at the beginning of this thread, there's evidence that violent crime in Western Queens is slightly down from the previous year. Murders happen occasionally, and two of them a few months apart is not close to enough data to conclude that there's a systematic problem.

Actually, according to Compstat for the 114 (link to PDF), however, the murder rate is up sharply from not only last year (YTD increase from 1 to 7) or the year before (YTD increase from 1 to 7), but from as long ago as 2001 (YTD increase from 5 to 7).  For nearly the past 10 years, the murder rate has been lower than it is this year.  It's true that it's still much lower than it was 20 years ago, but the current increase is notable.  Overall, for the precincts referred to in the Queens Gazette article, the murder rate is up YTD close to 30% from last year.

In terms of crime generally being up or down - it's a mixed bag (some crime categories up, some down), although overall slightly up over last year, and down nearly 10% from two years ago. That said, crime rates overall jumped over 20% for July 2010 compared to July 2009.  It's too soon to tell whether the recent sharp increase in murders portends a longer term trend, but that's no reason not to be wary.
