Title: Re: Endocrinologists?
Post by: enigmacat on June 24, 2008, 10:56:42 PM
Wasabi, I've had my own thyroid issues, though not hyperthyroidism. I'm very happy with the endocrinologist I'm seeing now, Dr. Katz (http://www.nyp.org/FPHTML/1168360089075.html).

All endocrinologists are not created equally. The range of normal for TSH (thyroid stimulating hormone, which is what they generally test when a thryoid disorder is suspected) is currently under debate, and whether other tests such as T3 and T4 should be part of the diagnostic tests is also a hot topic.

The bottom line is, if you feel like crap and all your doctor is telling you is that the numbers say you are perfectly healthy, get a second opinion, a third, and a fourth until you find someone who pays as much attention to your symptoms as they do to the test results.

Best of luck, and feel free to PM me for more thyroid chatter :)

Post by: lanseaux on July 19, 2008, 01:02:24 PM
Here (http://www.med.umich.edu/1libr/aha/umioddef.htm) is a list of symptoms of iodine deficiency.  It generally would cause hypothyroidism rather than hyperthyroidism, though.  Besides iodized salt (which I don't use either), I think the most common sources of iodine are animal-based (mostly seafood and dairy), so you may need to take an iodine supplement (or eat a lot of kelp) on a vegan diet.  Here (http://www.veganoutreach.org/health/stayinghealthy.html#iodine) and here (http://www.veganhealth.org/articles/iodine) is specific information about iodine supplementing on a vegan diet - they note that in Europe, where salt is not iodized, many vegans show signs of abnormal thyroid function.  I don't know how much soy you eat, but the second link notes that "large amounts of soy combined with inadequate iodine intake can exacerbate iodine deficiency."
