I hear you - if I eat anything carb heavy at lunch, I'm ready for my mid-afternoon nap.  If I stick to protein and veggies, though, I don't have any afternoon dip in energy at all.  I've been staying away from carb-heavy lunches for 10 years, and it's very consistent.

I alternate between bringing my own and buying something nearby.  I get bored with it, but I try to stick with salads -- a mix of greens, other veggies, and a protein (which for me is usually an egg, occasionally bacon, often chicken, rarely other kinds of meat), basically.  If you're a sandwich person, try having your favourite filling on top of a salad.  Sometimes I'll have a salmon fillet, or tuna steak; sashimi with soup and salad is a good option so long as you don't eat the whole side of rice.

If I bring my lunch, I will try to make extra of whatever I'm making for dinner the night before and bring that.  In the winter, I often make up different kinds of stews on the weekend and bring that for a few meals.  A favourite is a chicken stew that has lots of veggies -- tomatoes, cabbage, cauliflower, onions, garlic, carrots, etc., but no potatoes or pasta.  Today I brought homemade cole slaw (no added sugar) and chicken breast.

If you want some grains, quinoa or other whole grains are good options; I prefer to use them as an accent rather than the main focus, but others are less sensitive to them.  Lentils, beans and chana dal are also good.  I make a great split-pea type soup in the winter in which I mix split peas with both lentils and chana dal (you can get chana dal at the Key Food on 30th Ave., or at the Bangladeshi grocery on Ditmars across from Dunkin' Donuts).  I usually put smoked turkey or ham hock into it, but it's also very easily made vegetarian.

I always bring my lunch and a few healthy snacks to munch on throughout the day. Almonds are a great snack, but the serving size is only about 17 almonds. I also bring baby carrots and hummus to snack on mid-afternoon. Popcorn is great too and they now make 100 calorie bags of just about everything. I also buy frozen bags of edamame, cook it the night before, and bring it with me to munch on. They taste yummy cold with a little salt.

I drink a ton of water throughout the day and always have flavored seltzer at lunch. I buy the seltzer in 12 packs and just pop one in my bag every day. Cutting out soda at lunch is a great way to cut down on unnecessary sugars. If you do need the caffeine fix, stick to tea.

I know making your lunch the night before or the morning of work can be a pain, but I eat much healthier and I am saving about $50 a week. (I work in midtown where there aren't many cheap choices for lunch). Same goes for snacks. I will buy a big bag of SoyCrisps and ration them out for the week. It is much better than buying a $2.50 bag at Duane Reade and then eat them all in one sitting.
