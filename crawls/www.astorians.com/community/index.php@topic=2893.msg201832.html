It's important to know if your security deposit is in Pistilli's bank for many reasons. The bank, First Central Savings Bank, was recently fined $100,000 by the FDIC and ordered to pay restitution to account holders affected by the bank's failure to disclose an "interest-floor" on applicable accounts. First Central is on multiple industry lists of "troubled" banks and the March, 25, 2010 rating and review by The Street, gives First Central Savings Bank a Safety Rating of E, Very Weak Financial Strength.

    “The E rating means that, in our opinion, this institution currently demonstrates what we consider to be significant weaknesses and has also failed some of the basic test we use to identify fiscal stability. Therefore, even in a favorable economic environment, it is our opinion that depositors or creditors could incur significant risks.”

For more information, you can read the blog post http://thetruthaboutpistillirivervieweast.wordpress.com/2010/08/02/is-your-security-deposit-in-pistillis-bank/ or click on the individual information sources listed below.
