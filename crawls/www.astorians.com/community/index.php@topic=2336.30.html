Hi Annie -
I've been to many precinct council meetings in Manhattan, but not to one in Queens, not yet anyways.  I served as the Chief of Staff to a Manhattan City Council member for a few years and had to cover five precincts.  
I haven't been to the 114th Precinct Community Council, but I'm sure it works very similar to the ones in Manhattan.  It's important for us to get a large crowd, to have people talk politely, but forcefully and to talk about not only the incident, but the problems as a whole, though we have to make sure the people who committed this attack are caught.  As a gay man, I am horrified to have an attack like this take place in my neighborhood, let alone on my own block, especially to a close friend.  The 114th Precinct needs to take action immediately.  We need to make the officers understand the gravity of the situation and how concerned the neighborhood is about the entire situation.  Broadway is becoming unsafe and if we don't take action, we could have other attacks.
