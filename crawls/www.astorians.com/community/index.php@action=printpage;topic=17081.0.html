Title: Re: plugging holes near radiators to stop the roaches?
Post by: C-J on January 08, 2010, 04:12:34 PM
We just did something similar in our place and used steel wool to fill in the holes and then used spray foam from Home Depot (the single use bottle is about $4.00) for the top.  You stuff the wool into the hole (I verified with my stepdad that it wouldn't be an issue with the radiator pipe) then filled in the gap with the foam.  Then you wait about an hour and take a knife to level off the foam so you don't have a giant bubble.

Hope this helps!

And don't forget Heloise's roach cookie dough recipe. Works for me:

This recipe is slower to work than some insecticides, but it keeps on working for longer and is effective as long as it is kept dry.

1/4 cup shortening or bacon
1/8 cup sugar
8 ounces powdered boric acid
1/2 cup flour
1/2 small onion, chopped (optional)
Enough water to form a soft dough

Mix the shortening and sugar together. Mix together the boric acid, flour and onion, then add to the shortening-and-sugar mixture. Blend well, then add water to form a soft dough. Shape the mixture into small balls, or drop blobs into open plastic sandwich bags to keep it moist longer. Make sure you label the bags clearly so someone doesn't think you have made a tasty snack! When the dough gets hard, replace it. Place the boric-acid balls under sinks, dishwasher, refrigerator, stove and water heater, in the garage and behind outlets on your walls.

Keep the mixture away from children and pets because it can be toxic.
