Please use snap traps or humane traps instead of glue traps. Animals caught on glue traps take many, many hours (often days) to die and in the meantime suffer physical pain and often go crazy and try to chew off the parts that are stuck to the trap. You can use humane traps to capture them and release them far from the apartment, or snap traps which kill instantly. Also, both humane and snap traps are reusable. I battled mice in my old apartment so bold they would walk across your foot while you were sitting on the couch. Snap traps got rid of them MUCH more quickly than the glue traps my roommate had tried before I moved in.

In general, most of dealing with mice is prevention--leave no food out, no crumbs, etc. Put tempting food on a piece of furniture that stands alone (like a table) or in the refrigerator. Block holes as you're describing (though I've found the expanding hardening foam works better than steel wool). Put snap traps where you've seen droppings, and move furniture a little bit away from the wall (so they have fewer places to sneak/hide). Not all cats are great mousers, especially cats who've been raised indoors. Usually the scent of the predator is enough to keep mice away, so maybe put a cat toy or something that smells like the cat near where you've seen mice?

We have a parrot, and we checked VERY carefully before we bought those sonic mouse-be-gone things that they do not affect other pets...   He didn't seem to notice them at all... Unfortunately, neither did the mice.

We haven't seen any in months (knock wood) -- we cleaned out our closets (they'd been hiding out in there), took everything off the floor, plugged steel wool at the bottom of the closet door, plugged up the holes, and used snap traps with peanut butter and caught two of them that way. 

Also, with soap (dish soap is fine) and warm water, wash ALL of the baseboards and areas of floor near the baseboards where the mice have been running.  Mice will urinate in a spot to signal to other mice that food is nearby.

Ditto on the recommendation for the plug-in sound wave devices...

My boyfriend discovered a pair of mice in his apartment a while back. They were quite brazen, and actually came out to look for food while he was sitting on his couch watching tv one night!

He trapped one of them in a homemade Wile E Coyote-type contraption (and released it back outside into the wild). Then he got one of those plug-in sonic devices. Seemed to do the trick: post plug-in, he saw a mouse come in from its secret passage (behind the oven), and the strangest thing happened-- it came in a few feet, stopped dead in its tracks, and started screeching. Turned around and bolted back out its little hole, and never came back.

The only subsequent time he ever saw a mouse again was after some workers had been in his apartment. They had unplugged the mouse device so they could plug in a fan. Without the device, a mouse came back, but since he plugged it back in, he's never seen another one.
