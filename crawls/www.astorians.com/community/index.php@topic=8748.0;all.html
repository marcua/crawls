Businesses handing out fliers.....
« on: November 28, 2007, 10:00:19 AM »
This should be banned. I have sworn to never take any fliers unless it is from a business I am genuinely interested in (ie. outside a new restaurant, need a menu). Ninety percent of the time I tell the person handing them out, "no thanks" because I don't like ignoring them outright. Case in point, the Sprint store on 31st street near Ditmars. Every day, they have someone handing out these fliers, and a good percentage of these end up on the ground. The ground is littered with them. Sure, a good chunk of the blame belongs to those people who take them and throw them on the ground to begin with, but the business' themselves should be partly to blame.

Last night, I was on my way to the gym, and sure enough, there was a guy handing out fliers for Sprint. Based on his appearance, he was probably in need of some extra cash and was handing out the fliers to make a little dough. So as per my norm, as I approached the guy, he held out a flier in front of me. I shot him a smile and said "no thanks". The guy got pissed, started walking along side of me "I don't understand it, why not just take the flier?"

"Because I am not interested"

Well then you can just throw it out".....this got me fuming. I stopped, turned to him..

"I'll tell you why. Because half of your fliers end up on the floor, and I am hoping that if more people don't take them, eventually the Sprint store will realize how ineffective they are".

"Wow, thanks man".

Now sure, I felt alittle bad, cause the guy prob isn't getting paid till he is done handing them out, but its pretty unprofessional to harass those who don't take them.

Ok, my rant is done.

adamwc

Re: Businesses handing out fliers.....
« Reply #17 on: November 29, 2007, 01:51:44 PM »
So how does this work, exactly?

Ban the business from handing out ads, but don't ban me for handing out political statements? Or ban anyone from handing out paper stuff because that's a waste. It only isn't a waste if, according to some board of govt. bureaucrats, it falls into a category of being valuable. I don't think I like where this is headed. I see this as a first amendment issue, first and foremost. Think about what you're unleashing when you get the state in the business of justifying the suppression of the dissemination of printed materials deemed to be "wasteful."

I say, deploy meter maids to spend an hour or two or three a day near the people handing out the ads. As soon as they see somebody drop the ad onto the ground, give a ticket to that polluter, a $50 fine.

Maybe fewer people would take the ads if they realized that just dropping them on the street is going to cost them big-time. Or, maybe they'll take the ads, evaluate them, and then properly dispose of them to avoid getting ticketed.

The reason the streets are littered is because there are no consequences for littering here. We have anti-littering laws with no teeth.

If you think a business should find another way of promoting itself, then you should give that business direct feedback. But asking the government to BAN handing out paper ads because you think it's a waste of paper, even though there certainly are people who will take those ads, which sometimes include coupons, and make good use of them, is a myopic solution.
