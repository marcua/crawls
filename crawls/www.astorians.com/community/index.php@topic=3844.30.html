Wow. I haven't looked at this thread in a long time, it's one of those that slipped my attention for some reason.

Unfortunately for me Benny I've not found any Mexican food in Astoria/LIC that I find acceptable. I haven't had what I'd define as "good" Mexican for several years now - I've sadly stopped trying to find a good place after visiting many. The "best" burrito I've found so far is at Burritoville, which honestly isn't quite good enough to write home about. Still, I'd give Burritoville about a 6.5/10. For reference I'd give Taco Bell a solid 5/10.

I think part of the reason that I might not like the Mexican food around here is that I grew up in California where a lot of the Mexican food there is Baja-style, and coastal Mexican. What I'm finding around here is "Central" Mexican goo; overcooked un-fresh veggies, and spicy mystery-meat to provide flavor, not to mention the lard-laiden beans. Since I'm a vegetarian I depend on good beans, fresh salsa, and fresh high-quality veggies in a burrito (or in any Mexican dish really) to make it work for me - I haven't yet found any place that does this. Most of them are serving salsa from a can/jar, and grilling the crap out of all the veggies until they are mush. At least Burritoville makes their own fresh salsa.

I'm looking for Baja California-style Mexican in NYC and I've not yet found it. Such a change from the little burrito shops and hole-in-the-wall restaurants you find all over California where pretty much anywhere you go is going to be a 7+/10.

merm- While I'm guessing this place doesn't serve your typical authentic Mexican burrito, I'm fond of a very, very tiny place in Manhattan called Burrito Box. It's on 9th Avenue, between 57th and 58th. I haven't been to California or Mexico, but this place has delicious and inexpensive burritos that are extremely filling. I usually just eat half and save the rest for later. They also have salsa that I'm pretty sure is homemade. It's really garlicky and has a lot of cilantro. And they have a lot of vegetarian options too.
http://www.menupages.com/restaurantdetails.asp?areaid=0&restaurantid=4267&neighborhoodid=0&cuisineid=0

I wouldn't say you should make a special trip out there to go to Burrito Box unless you've got nothing to do and feel like trying a new burrito, but it's definitely worth checking out if you're in the neighborhood and feel like having an unglamorous but delightful burrito experience.
