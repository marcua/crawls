Urban huh?  You do need your timeline.  Astoria, along with Ravenswood, Hunter's Point, Steinway, etc. were all included in the city of Long Island City, established in 1870.  Steinway moved his factory from Manhattan to it's current location in the same year.  L.I.C. remained a city until 1898, when it, and the rest of Queens County became part of the City of New York.  L.I.C's most notable figure was Mayor Patrick "Clubber" Gleason...now that was a pol!

I was doing some research for my personal history while living in Astoria and ran across this web site, saw many remarks about the F.W. Woolworth store, and just had to chime in to those many friends who remembered the F.W.Woolworth store on 31st and Steinway Street. My father was the manager of that store in 1934 and 1935. We lived in an apartment on 38th street and I attended PS 6, 4th grade. Store managers were transferred every couple of years, just like the army, so we moved to MA in the summer of 1935. I do have many memories of Astoria but one of my fondest memories was of a man by the name of Rapaport (sp), who was supposed to have owned a night club in the city. I guess maybe today we might classify him as a "gangster" but in those days he was one of my fathers best friends and did many kind favors for my mother and father and that is how I remember him. It was a pleasure chatting with you. Best wished to all.
Chuck B.
