I think this same argument happens every year on Astorians.com.  I tend not to be around Steinway so I don't hear it so much.  I think people play if for the commercialism aspect rather than any religious one.  I mean, really, since when have the holidays been about religion for consumers, anyway?  The whole holiday has been made into one big marketing opportunity, just like Valentine's Day, Halloween, and Easter.  Go to any mall and the music is all over the place too.  Steinway is just one long mall.

I happen to like some Christmas songs.  I happen to enjoy Christmas because of the family time I get.  I usually like this time of year because of the parties, and spending happy times with friends and families.  So music outside, as long as it's not all night long, tends not to bother me, but I certainly understand how it could get annoying.  Hell, when the card shop on Ditmars and 33rd St put up their holiday decorations and that god awful horse in their store in August, I was disgusted.  I still am, by the sheer tackiness of it.  It's an eyesore.

On the same topic, I was annoyed with holiday music playing in restaurants.  Most of the restaurants around here have a bad taste in music as it is, but I actually had to ask them to put on some traditional Thai music this weekend.   The holiday music there was a bit much.

Wow, I just joined and posted a note about this same topic. Guess I should have looked to see if anyone else was asking about this issue first!

Like some others of you, I also live on Steinway, and I also work from home. My complaint about the Christmas music has nothing whatsoever to do with political correctness; rather, I am being driven insane by trying work while being forced to listen to loud music all day. Since I've never seen this in any other public space anywhere in the city, I'm guessing whoever is responsible for the music has done this without seeking municipal approval. Steinway Street is not a shopping mall--it's a city street on which many taxpaying New Yorkers reside, and as such, I think it's inappropriate that the music is being played outdoors. I just called in a complaint to 311. If any of my fellow Steinway residents share this feeling, I'd ask that you consider doing the same. Maybe if a few of us raise the issue at the same, we can draw some attention to it.
(I don't want to be grumpy about the holiday season; I just want to be able to work from home without being bombarded by this stuff all day.)
Thanks, and happy holidays everyone.
