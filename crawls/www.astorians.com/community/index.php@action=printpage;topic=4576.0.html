Is it really more expensive to eat healthy?  I think that healthy convenience foods are more expensive, but if you are doing your own cooking with raw ingredients it's cheaper to eat better.  Certainly for many people there isn't enough time to seek out produce bargains and cook, but I'd be interested to see the statistics on how many low income households contain one or more non-working adults (i.e. grandparents, adult children)--in my limited experience I have found that it's pretty common.  I think that a public initiative including meal planning classes and food stamp-like vouchers for produce (you can use food stamps for produce, but these would be addtional) would be a great step--heck, I want to be part of that program.
