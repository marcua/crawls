Hello fellow Astorians,
My name is Frankie and as you can tell by my username I am the manager at Tony’s Bicycles here in Astoria.  Although I am a new member, I have browsed this site many times before and appreciate its significance and wealth of local information. I would like to make my first post count by announcing evening bicycle rides throughout Astoria, Manhattan, Queens and many other destinations throughout the cycling season.  My goal is to organize as many riders as possible on a nightly basis to participate, meet locals and tour the city.  After speaking to Meesh today at the FREEZEPEACH café, we have decided that the café will be the meeting point for these rides. All persons of different skill levels and tolerances are welcome to ride with us each evening. I, myself, will be riding with the group on a nightly basis, so no need to worry about being stuck on the road with a bike that needs repair- you will be riding with a seasoned mechanic!  There is no sign-up requiremt for these rides due to the fact that we all lead busy lives there is also no commitment to ride. As the number of participants rise, different groups will be formed that will ride different distances and ride to different destinations if the demand is such.  The rides will start at 8:15 each night and other times may be added once the whole event is sorted out. These rides will be great for training for upcoming centuries and the five borough tour. If you would like more information you can contact me at:

I would like to thank you all for your interest in advance. I would also like to thank the founders of this forum for their contribution to the community.

Thank you,

If anybody has any interest in trail riding, be sure to check out the following boards:

www.climbonline.org -> trail building/protection/maintenance and general advocacy

www.nycmtb.com -> the new group for NYC mountain bikers

Last Saturday, there was a Cannondale Demo at Stillwell, where you could test ride some high-end mtb/road bikes for free. We then went to a bar that night and watched a couple of mountain bike movies, about as much fun as we could ask for in one day! The bar did loads of extra business, and CLIMB raised quite a bit of money for their cause.

It would be nice to see a bike community develop out here, most of these things take place out on L.I.  With Highbridge and Cunningham Parks now clear for building new trails, I'm hoping to see more mtb closer to the city.
