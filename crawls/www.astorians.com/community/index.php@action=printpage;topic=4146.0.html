Title: Pearl Meatballs
Post by: LHT on November 17, 2006, 10:01:22 AM
Can't remember who I promised this recipe to quite some time ago, but here it is.  Hope you enjoy and please let me know if it's similar to the family recipe you remember.  Happy Cooking!

Ingredients

Group I
1 cup sweet/glutinous rice
2-3 cups very warm tap water
2 dried Chinese pork sausages (no clue where to find these around Astoria)
6 medium size dried Shiitake mushrooms
Group II
1-2 slices fresh ginger (optional)
2 cups lean cubed pork
1 scallion
1/4 pound fresh waterchestnuts
Group III
1 egg beaten
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon sugar
1 teaspoon sesame oil
1 tablespoon soy sauce

Prep all ingredients - Soak sweet/glutinous rice in very warm water approx 1-2 hours or as long as it takes you to prepare the meatball mixture.  Soak the Chinese sausages in hot water until soft and pliable.  Soak the dried Shiitake mushrooms in hot water until soft.  Peel waterchestnuts and cut into chunks.  In a small cup, mix together all ingredients in Group III

In food processor pulse ingredients in Group II until coarsely chopped.  Don't overprocess and be sure to layer ingredients in the following order starting from the bottom of the food processor bowl - pork, ginger, sausages, mushrooms, scallions, waterchestnuts.  A food processor works great here, but the real way to do this step is to use 2 large cleavers and coarsely chop by hand, starting with the pork and adding one ingredient at a time until all are incorporated together. 

Remove meatball mixture to a bowl and combine with ingredients in Group III.

Drain rice very well and spread on a plate.

Line a steamer with wax paper.

Shape the meatball mixture into one inch balls and roll each ball in rice to coat.  Arrange meatballs in a steamer approx. 1/4" apart.  Steam for 30 minutes and serve hot or cold with your favorite dipping sauce.

Yield - approx 3 dozen meatballs
