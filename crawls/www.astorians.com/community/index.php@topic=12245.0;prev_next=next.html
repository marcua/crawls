While 22 points isn't a lot (failing is 28 or more), the trans fat violation really stood out to me.  I only came across one other restaurant that got busted for it, from the many that I checked in Astoria and LIC...Pizzeria Uno.  It's unfortunate, as Porto Bello is one of my favorite eateries in the area.

Granted, the trans fat "ban" is a joke as well.  It just limits usage to ingredients which have less than 0.5 grams of trans fat per "serving."  Given that serving sizes are somewhat arbitrary, the rule is pretty much meaningless.  Case in point:  I've seen a muffin that, on the package, claimed it had zero trans fat.  However, the ingredients included hydrogenated oil (trans fat).  However, that one muffin was, in the nutrition facts, listed as four servings.  Since food manufacturers are allowed to round down/up to the nearest zero, that means that the muffin could, theoretically, have 0.49 grams of trans fat per serving, multiplied by four servings = 1.96 grams of trans fat in a muffin that legally is allowed to say it has zero trans fat!  NYC's ban is much the same, and is essentially meaningless as a result, unfortunately.
