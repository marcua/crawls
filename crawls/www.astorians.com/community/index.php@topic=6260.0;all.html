The groups of 3 (Doing Life Together groups, DLT's) are people just getting coffee, talking about life and how God is intersecting in the daily context of being and creating each individual to be better husbands, wives, brothers, sisters, moms, dads, friends and you name it. The groups of 12 (community groups) will connect to serve and love on the community of Astoria as a whole, people who live out the teachings of Jesus to help those in the community connect relationally with a loving God. Then once a week 5 "community groups" will meet in a centralized location to listen to how God is working, loving and connecting through the service projects in Astoria. The cool thing about this is the organic DNA of each group is built on relationships, people connecting with people, and people connecting with God. We want to see each person plug into God, so that they can become who they are naturally wired to be.

I know that's a long description, but in short we want to "serve God through loving people." We believe that everyone belongs to God, and hope that as they begin to connect with Jesus relationally that they will subscribe to the concept that he was divinity in human form and was perfect, and in turn, hope that those concepts propel people forward to become what they were always intended be... people living, learning and loving in community with others for God.

People like Jesus, they just can't tolerate Christians and can't seem to see how Jesus can put up with them as well.

I hope people will try not to misconstrue our intentions. We are not moving to the area to tell people they need a doctrine or to place blame or guilt. There are enough Christians in the world for that matter, that already give people, including myself, a headache. My wife and I live to see people living out Christ's teachings, because that means issues like 80% of the deaths in Africa due to waterborne diseases will slowly decline because people care more about others than themselves. We don't want to see people "Coming to or Doing" church, again there are enough selfish-Christians already doing that. We want to see those who subscribe to Jesus' teachings, externalize their lives, live for others and help people connect with God.

Gravity,

There is a thread on finding a broker (and broker suggestions).  Your price range is reasonable but it's very very difficult to find an apartment without a broker and even more difficult to find one without a broker without being here. 

I'd also advise against paying 1 year in full.  Most landlords would jump at this chance but a few months rent in advance should be sufficient.  Paying for a year opens you up to the possibility that your landlord might not turn out to be *the best* in the end.  If you end up with a slumlord who neglects to provide/correct problems with heat/hot water -- you'd be left with no real recourse.  I believe withholding rent is in some instances allowed under NY housing law if a landlord neglects to maintain basic living necessities (heat/hot water/electric).

Anyhow definitely look into it a bit before you mention it to your broker or your prospective landlord.
