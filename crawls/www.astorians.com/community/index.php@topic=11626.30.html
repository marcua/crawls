Quote from: enigmacat on August 07, 2008, 11:36:28 PM

With all of the linguistics literature about how men and women communicate differently, I wonder how long until someone writes a dissertation on how men and women troll differently :P

As an "expert" in the field of online communication, I haven't seen a specific piece yet about men and women trolling differently because it's way too difficult to study accurately. It's hard to contact trolls and be assured that what they are telling you is the truth. There are case studies here and there (as in the NYT piece), but it's difficult to get enough people for a testable sample.

The closest thing I can think of are some qualitative studies with adolescents that examine the types of bullying each gender tends to carry out online. It's a lot like it is offline--boys go for the random ad hominem attacks (sort of equivalent to a fistfight or scuffle offline), but girls are vicious such that they systematically attack the target in such a way that undermines their relationships, pretty much dismantling someone's self-esteem and confidence bit by bit. A boy might simply flame someone, but a girl will pretend to be someone's friend, have a long and intimate chats or email exchanges with someone, and then save the deep dark secret parts and show them to everyone else or otherwise use them against the person. It's much more devastating than someone simply calling names (see: Meghan Meier).

Para one aspect that I think you haven’t considered is that the reason why Astorians.com (and other sites) has guidelines or rules is not necessarily to protect individuals from name calling. Name calling not only affects the person being called a name, the general negativity of in-fighting sets a tone and often drives other people away from the site. Some people don’t think it all fun and games and their needs should be considered as well.

By all means, I know there are reasons that the rules here exist. I just disagree with them.

Part of heckling is the ability of a group to self-select. The easiest way to keep trolls and spammers off of a board is to harass them. The easiest way for a bar to keep out assholes is if no one in the bar talks to them. Sure, there will be the occasional guy that needs to get called out by a mod or bartender, but I think for the most part that adults don't need supervision constantly and it's kinda creepy that we expect people to do it.

If there's an asshole in a bar, do you really expect him to get bounced out of the bar because he's an asshole? Wouldn't the more adult, more responsible thing to do be to ignore the asshole and not talk to him? If he gets too drunk or too unruly, the bartender will deal with him because it's his job to not let anyone get too drunk or too unruly. However, it is not the bartender's job to make sure that everyone has pleasant nice conversation and is protected from being called a names.
