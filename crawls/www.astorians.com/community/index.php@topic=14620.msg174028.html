Quote from: JoeyC on May 22, 2009, 02:05:44 AM
What do you expect, he supported every gay measure in past. Now he disagrees on one, and suddenly he's "old" or "out of touch."       He was out of touch when he supported those past bills, and I hope he sees where it's gotten him. 

There are several differences, the greatest of which is that, while Marriage Equality bestows upon the LGBT community a right that heterosexuals have (and take for granted), the other measures that Onorato supported --  Sexual Orientation Non-Discrimination Act (SONDA), New York's hate crimes law, Dignity for All Students Act and the Gender Expression Non-Discrimination Act (GENDA) are protections. 

You don't have to be in or out of touch to know that it's wrong to allow lesbians and gays to be discriminated against in the workplace or for housing. You don't have to be in or out of touch to know that it's wrong to beat or even kill someone because they're LGBT. You don't have to be in or out of touch to get that gay students shouldn't have to face bullying or menacing at school (and DFAS isn't aimed specifically at LGBT students -- it encompasses race, color, weight, ethnicity, religion and disability as well). You don't have to be in or out of touch to recognize that it's wrong to discriminate against someone because of the way they express their gender.

In an area as diverse as NYC, yes, these are necessary protections. Onorato pats himself on the back for supporting these measures, but when it comes time to giving the LGBT community a basic, fundamental right that heterosexual people have and we don't, he balks. To me, that speaks volumes about where he stands with respect to the LGBT community.
