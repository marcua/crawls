I'll agree that there seems to be an increase in both intrusive behavior and attitude on the subways lately... but I suspect that might be a function of my traveling during rush hour while working 9-5 during the summer. Everything is just "more" when people are in a rush.

But here's a question for you - people rarely complain about people talking to each other on the subway. They might complain about people yelling or making a scene or being idiots.. but not for talking. Why? Because everyone considers that normal. We all do that - if we're riding with a friend, we're probably going to be talking to them about something. Right? We aren't expected to sit in silence, next to each other. This isn't a funeral, it's the morning commute.

So then, why are people annoyed when people talk, -at the SAME volume as an in person conversation-, on a cell phone? It isn't necessarily the content of the conversation that bothers the overhearer, right? It isn't really the volume..(I don't believe that people are always louder when talking into a phone, just because it's a phone. Some people do- but not ALWAYS). So what is it about watching/hearing people on the phone that is intrinsically annoying to people?

Is it because:
- It's still new and we still haven't gotten used to people talking on the phone in public yet?
- The listener feels left out/excluded from the conversation because you can only eavesdrop on one side?
- It breaks the peaceful silence (in this case, the tranquility of the screeching, thumping aboveground subway with loud but incoherent announcements, occasionally screaming children and -other- people's conversations to each other in person? *grin*)
- Because some people have been rude about the usage of their cell phones, so now all cellphone use is presumed annoying?

Don't get me wrong - I'm annoyed by people on their phones sometimes too:
- if they're being too loud for their environment,
- if they're talking about topics that are annoying,
- if they aren't paying attention and therefore run into or inconvenience people...

But all those activities stand alone from cell phone use. It is possible to use a cell phone without being rude, just as it's possible to talk to your friend on the subway without being rude.

I guess I'm just trying to pose the question about -why- we're annoyed by particular things. Why is someone having a one-sided conversation an intrusion on your well-being if two people having a two-sided conversation aren't?
