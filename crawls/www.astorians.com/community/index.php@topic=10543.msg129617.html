I think we need to stop categorizing and demonizing each other as "drivers" or "bicyclists" or "pedestrians".  We're neighbors, and we all need to work with each other, with respect and understanding to achieve a better Astoria.  I'm sorry if my earlier comments made you feel like I was demonizing you TJT.  I think a lot of people feel the way you do.  I understand your objections, but I think the bile lane is a good thing.

I walk/bus/subway 99% of the time.  I only ride my bike recreationally on weekends.  I don't think I qualify as a "hard core" cyclist.

I think one of the reasons why drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians get on each other's nerves is due to the fact that the street doesn't assign cyclists a given space.  As a driver I admit I get startled and annoyed when I suddenly look in my right hand mirror and I see some guy on a bike, 12 inches from my car, going at 20 mph!  Legally, he has a right to be there, but my mind set is that the streets are for cars and there was nothing warning me to look out for this guy.  Likewise, when I'm riding my bike, even if I'm hugging the parked cars (which is dangerous to me due to doors flying open!) there are still some drivers who buzz way too close to me on purpose to show me whose boss!  It happens a lot.  And as a pedestrian I'm sick and tired of getting harassed at corners by both drivers and bicyclists (but more often drivers to tell you the truth, Livery car drivers).

Bike lanes do a great deal to solve these problems.  It lets me know, as a driver, that there are bikes around here and that I'm supposed to share the road, it also lets me know how much space to give them.  I know exactly where they are going to be.  As a pedestrian it lets me know when I'm jaywalking or in the cross walk that I should look out for bikes too!  I think bike lanes let everyone know where everyone else should be and they reduce the stress on drivers, cyclists and pedestrians alike.  Right now, its a free for all.  So quite frankly, I think that all of this aggravation is due mostly to a street design which leaves a prominent mode of transportation without a defined space.  They don't have these problems in Europe, because European cities define seperate lanes and even streets, just for bicyclists.

Finally, I don't think asking for one street to be dedicated full time for cyclists is such a sacrifice.  All of the roads in Astoria are dedicated to cars 100% of the time.  All of the curb space in Astoria is dedicated to car parking.  Asking for one side of one road is not an unreasonable sacrifice, its not even a compromise, it is a crumb off of the table, a token gesture.  Cars still have 99.9% of the curbside street space for parking.  Is it too much to ask that 0.1% of the curb NOT be dedicated to cars?  

I don't think its the cyclists who are being unreasonable here.

Hi all,

I ride my bike along Vernon Blvd every morning and every afternoon.  It is an ideal bike route through Astoria, as the traffic is not too bad and the road is wide.  I believe the average driver (definitely the average taxi) chooses 21st Street over Vernon as a pipeline through western Astoria, but I think a lot of bikers use this route because of the parks.

A few points:

-A good bike line may consolidate a lot of commuter (but not food delivery guy) bike traffic to one street, which is good for everyone on the road.  I know I will gladly bike a few minutes longer or a few blocks further to ride in a safe environment, and I'm sure I'm not the only one who wants to feel safe when they bike.

-The purpose of plans like these is not necessarily to meet the needs of the majority.  Clearly, we have a traffic problem in NYC, and if we're going to be forward-thinking, we need to pursue plans like these.  The easier and safer it is to bike, the more people will choose that mode of transportation.  Then the road is clear for those of you for whom biking is not an option.

-Garciavision: I read your post and all I see referenced are YOUR needs, not the "needs of the area".  Very weak post, for reasons others have pointed out better than I.  That said, THANK YOU to all you motorists who are willing to share the road.  Every time you slow down rather than run me off the road, or give me the time to move to the shoulder while you pass, I feel a little better about humanity in general.  You have my LOVE!
