Title: Les Moules
Post by: Harlan on December 22, 2005, 09:57:00 AM
Les Moules is the Belgian tapas/mussels/frites/beer place on Ditmars and 35th St. Apparently it's a "location-of-death", although I haven't been in the nabe long enough to see that. (Or so I thought...)

I and my dining companion have been twice. The first time was a few days after they opened, and the second time was a few days ago. Oddly, the experiences were remarkably similar... Even down to the same waiter...

On our recent visit, we had a coupon from Clipper for free tapas and salad with purchase of an entree. We ordered an apple-rutabega soup, and the steak frites (medium), plus the tapas and salad. The tapas were these crisp baked puffed-up pita cracker things, a failed attempt at hummus, a cup of soup, and a salad. The crackers were OK, but the hummus was awful. Horribly dry and tasteless. The "chunky style" hummus in the little deli case near the front of the nearby Key Foods is really excellent, incidentally. The soup, oddly, was the apple-rutabega soup we'd ordered additionally. The salad was simple, but pretty good. Speaking of incidentally, I highly recommend the salad with walnuts, feta and balsamic vinegar at Philoxenia... OK, we finished that, and... a bowl of soup came out! This is the one we'd specifically ordered, but they'd gotten confused and given us the cup of the apple-rutabega, and the bowl of the "tapas" soup, which was a pretty decent creamy pumpkin. OK. Then we got the steak. It was a New York Strip, cooked medium-well to well, with a decent sauce. The fries were good.

The waiter was exceptionally friendly, and a bit less manic than on our first visit. There were only a couple of other guests. The bill ended up being about $28. Their credit card machine wasn't working, so I had to run to the ATM (and pay an extra surcharge) to get cash.

Overall, as I commented to my dining companion, it seemed as if they were having the same start-up confusion that they had 6 months earlier!

And now I know why! Les Mousles' web site is http://www.lesmoulesny.com/ . If you go there, you get the following:

The Restaurant at this location
has one more  time
Changed management...
will keep you updated when new plans are made public


Hah! And indeed, their current menu (as photocopied and posted on their windows) is significantly changed from their old menu (http://www.lesmoulesny.com/dinner.html), which is still available on their website if you go through Google's cached version (http://64.233.161.104/search?q=cache:NaDTU3rVE04J:www.lesmoulesny.com/+les+moules+astoria+menu&hl=en&client=firefox-a). Their old menu was rather better.

I guess that explains a few things...
