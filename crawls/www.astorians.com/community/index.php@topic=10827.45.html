The Rules: “no name calling” -  I don’t think you should be allowed to call anyone an ass or tell them they’re stupid just because you don’t agree with them.  If you do call someone an offensive name, you should get a warning via a PM and the post should be deleted. with an explanation as to why it's being deleted posted in the thread.  Re: suspensions:  Maybe three warnings = suspension for a clearly specified time. Whatever it is, I think it should clearly be written in The Rules regarding cause and effect because it is not there now.  And that seems to be a big part of the problem.

“no ad hominems”  -  This is a bit tricky because many arguments do fall into this category.   For people like handyman (who said he was too lazy to look it up) this is an example:

   1.   Person A makes claim X.
   2.   Person B makes an attack on person A.
   3.   Therefore A's claim is false.
The reason why an Ad Hominem (of any kind) is a fallacy is that the character, circumstances, or actions of a person do not (in most cases) have a bearing on the truth or falsity of the claim being made (or the quality of the argument being made).

Example of Ad Hominem:
   Bob: "I believe that we need rules. Rules are good for these reasons: _______________ (fill in the blank)." 
        Ken: "Of course you would say that, you're one of the Mods."  
        Bob: "Never mind about me, what about the reasons I gave to support my position?"
        Ken: "Those don't count. Like I said, you're a Mod and you don’t like to argue, so you would say that rules are okay. Further, you are just an ass kissing sheep, so I can't believe what you say."

Again, I think a person should receive a warning via a PM, the post deleted with a post stating why, and after so many warnings a suspension is in order. 

The next section is  “Avoid repetition and duplicate posting":  When people forget this a mod should post in the thread telling them not to do this.   “This includes "quoting" an earlier message for the sole purpose of bumping the thread or expressing agreement. Please use the "nod" feature instead.”  Dislike the nods.  Think they should not be part of conversation because they divert attention from the conversation.  Also think they’re a passive aggressive way to argue with the poster’s comments.   And re: bumping, I don’t understand this myself.  I understand the definitions given but I don’t understand how wanting to continue a conversation is bumping. 
 
“Act appropriately: We do not permit comments that we deem abusive, baiting, defamatory, insulting, harassing, inflammatory, hateful, obscene, inappropriately rude or personal, retaliatory, threatening, invasive of privacy or violative of any law.”
BAITING:  It’s my understanding that a poster wasn’t banned for saying/typing the word asshole.  The person was banned for baiting because they wanted to call another poster asshole. 

Splitting hairs: some members have written that the poster in question DIDN’T REALLY call anyone an asshole.  Only ASKED if they could call someone an asshole.  That’s still baiting in my understanding of the word. It might be baiting by coming thru a back door instead of coming thru the front door, but it would still be defined as baiting.

Bottom line for me:  Somewhere in The Rules the punishments/consequences of breaking them should be clearly stated.  When people break them, they should get warned.  Many people break rules unintentionally in the heat of discussion so a warning is in order.  I think the warning should be inserted into the thread so others can know what's going on and not speculate.  The amount of warnings leading to suspension should be stated in The Rules.

Sorry this took up so much space. Decided to post it all at once instead of going back and forth in the thread.

Other boards (where I moderate) have a warning system in place.  There is a board function that allows moderators to warn a member about behavior.  Five warnings lead to a suspension.  The member is aware of the warnings and the specific reasons through PM.  But only the moderators can see the rating.  So, for example, I post:

"Mr. Peanut is a fartface."
Merm or another mod sees that I've clearly broken the board rules by name calling so he raises my warning level by one notch.  The next time I log in, I get a PM that says:
"Your warning level has been raised due to: name calling."
If, after a while, I haven't name called anyone or had another warning, it can be lowered to nothing.  The moderators can always see the reasons for raising or lowering the status so if a member is confused, all of the details are available. 

Would something like that work for this board?

My issue with the private warnings is that it increases the perception of inconsistent application of the rules.  It does not allow other users to see which posts have generated warnings (or suspensions/bannings) and which haven't.  There are apparently many posts that cause warnings to the poster, but the post itself remains.  Much of the time, I have no way of knowing whether a particular post has crossed the line because it isn't deleted and I don't know what actions may have been taken against the poster.   

I've thought long and hard about my response to this discussion, and I'm still not sure I have the time to write fully what I want to say.  I do want to say that there is a lot that is wonderful about this board, and I'm really grateful that merm started it.  I've watched Astorians grow from a board with 200 members and a handful of posts a day, to a board with thousands of members and hundreds of posts a day.  I've met an incredible community of people and have formed close friendships with a number of people I've met through Astorians.  It has indeed become very personal for me.  That said, as I've seen other people reference, I also think it has grown beyond its original conceived purpose and ideas and doesn't necessarily belong to one person anymore.  Of course, the domain and such still belongs to one person, but this website is so much more than its domain and software.  It has become a marketplace of ideas and connections for Astoria.

I'm going to respond to a few key things that stick out to me in the rules currently.

Quote
- Act appropriately: We do not permit comments that we deem abusive, baiting, defamatory, insulting, harassing, inflammatory, hateful, obscene, inappropriately rude or personal, retaliatory, threatening, invasive of privacy or violative of any law.

"that we deem".  That's awfully vague and subjective.  What one person may deem abusive or insulting or rude, another person may deem New York humor.  Of course, there will always be some subjectivity in a rule of this sort, and some of the prohibitions I agree with (particularly the last few).  However, several of the prohibitions can be interpreted in a number of legitimate ways, and I don't think there's a clear consensus on the board as to how we should interpret them.  For instance, what's insulting?  Many people on this board come from far-flung communities that may have different standards as to what's insulting.  I'm acquainted (online, as it happens) with a woman from the South.  She recently wrote of being insulted when a man - a family member - didn't stand up to greet her when she entered the room.  I'm not sure I would have noticed.  There are similar parallels in speech/online behaviour.

In law, there is a theory called the "eggshell plaintiff" theory.  It basically means that if you hurt someone and they have an underlying weakness (a preexisting condition) that makes the injury you inflict worse, you are liable for the worse injury.  I think there's a broad analogy here in the inverse - the site rules are attempting to cover all sorts of preexisting conditions of its members (for instance, people who are easily insulted) that results in a broader unspoken entreaty to "play nice" so we can all be happy.

Quote
- Avoid repetition and duplicate posting: We encourage a diversity of viewpoints; however once a point has been made the discussion needs to move forward. Excessive repetition and filibustering are not permitted - this also applies to "announcements".

This is a category I often see violated - or at least, it seems to me that it's violated.  I see multiple posts essentially saying the same thing that are not deleted.  While the poster may have been warned or suspended, there's no indication to me that the admin/mods of the board thought the pattern of posting was inappropriate, despite this in the rules.  So I don't know whether I'm misunderstanding the rule, or whether it's being applied inconsistently.

Quote
- Avoid repetition and duplicate posting: We encourage a diversity of viewpoints; however once a point has been made the discussion needs to move forward. Excessive repetition and filibustering are not permitted - this also applies to "announcements". You might think this will give more exposure to your content; it does, but in a negative way because this is seen as rude. Post your content once, to the correct category. This includes "quoting" an earlier message for the sole purpose of bumping the thread or expressing agreement. Please use the "nod" feature instead.

(Emphasis added)  I'm not sure when this was incorporated into the rules, but it contradicts your stated purpose of the nod feature:

Quote from: merm on November 07, 2006, 02:55:14 PM
It doesn't really serve any "purpose" in that it's goal is not "utility" but more just fun.

I am on record as not liking the nod feature, and I have declined to use it since it was implemented.  I'm a bit surprised that its use seems to be mandated now by the rules, since I'm probably not the only person who doesn't use it.  I'm also not sure how nodding fits into most of the rest of the rule, which is about repetition and duplicate posting, not about agreeing or disagreeing with a post.  I choose to engage a poster by telling them openly that I agree or disagree with their post (or a part of it) - why should this be against the rules?

Quote
Use the "report to moderator" feature

In terms of reporting a post - there seems to be a bit of a disconnect as to whether this is a board moderated by a selected group of moderators, or a board that is self-moderated.  Of course, many, if not most, boards have a "report post" feature, and many large boards rely on the input of regular posters to help moderators do their job.  But different people have different comfort levels of reporting posts and may prefer to report for different things.  And here, while we are encouraged to report posts, the actions of doing so and any possible responses are invisible to most members.

Quote
Additionally, public debate and protest of moderation or administrative decisions is discouraged because it seldom is helpful to the community as a whole.

For me, this has been counterproductive since it is seldom evident which posts trigger moderator responses.

* * *

Quote from: merm on June 11, 2008, 09:04:59 PM
We don't "ban" anyone for violating most of the site guidelines. In most cases people get a warning, then a short suspension, then a long suspension, then a longer one, then they get "banned". Of course it's up to the moderators how to handle each case as it's typically different. Spamming porn is one thing that will get you permanantly banned instantly for example, bumping a thread, or duplicate posting, or using all caps etc will just get you a warning. But technically speaking if you had already been suspended a couple times for other things you could I suppose be "banned" for doing it. I'm not sure it would be the straw that breaks the camels back, but then again I've never been pushed to the limit of my patience on thread bumping so who knows.

This is the first time that I have seen anything like this close to being explained.  There is a long list of prohibited conduct and conduct guidelines, but the warning and suspension policy is not spelled out anywhere.  In fact, until the past few days, I had no idea that there even was a pattern of a "warning, then a short suspension, then a long suspension, then an longer one, then they get 'banned'." How long is a "short", "long", "longer" suspension?  I also had no idea that it is "most of the time".  What's most of the time?  When is it not?   These are all issues the rules should address; their silence on these issues enhances the appearance of their inconsistent application.

The end result is that there is the appearance of a great deal of inconsistency in the way the rules are enforced.  This is caused because (1) there fair amount of ambiguity in the rules currently, and (2) rules transgressions are dealt with very nontransparently.  The relatively large size of the board and small number of moderators also likely contributes to this - they don't see everything, and not everything is reported to them, so there are posts that violate one or more rules that aren't acted on.  This is inadvertent, but it still increases the appearance of inconsistency.

A final note - I really like MagicPie's suggestion based on her other board - an escalating system of warnings that a member can choose to disclose or not, that can set back to zero after a specified period of time, or result in a suspension if it escalates far enough.  It reminds me of the DMV's system of points on a license -- the violation is clearly spelled out (you know you're going too fast, because the speed limit is posted - the sign says "Speed Limit 45", not "you will get a ticket and points on your license if you go too fast", leaving you to guess what "too fast" might be), too many of them result in a suspension, but your slate is also wiped clean if enough time passes.  At the same time, I agree that there should also be a provision for immediate suspension/banning (to continue the analogy, as there is for DUI), for instance, as merm noted above, for spamming porn or other specifically enumerated cases.
