Title: Re: Agnanti/Trattori
Post by: lanseaux on March 07, 2006, 04:48:05 PM
I agree that Trattoria L'Incontro is excellent, but I wouldn't call it old school at all (at least, I'm interpreting old school to mean a red sauce place).  I suppose you could say that their main menu does contain a number of traditional dishes, but in addition to that, they've got excellent dishes I've never seen anywhere else - my favourite is their ostrich "tenderloin".  They also often offer venison and duck.

The salads and pizzas are also good, and they've usually got a couple of unusual pasta dishes as specials.

Everything's good.  It does get noisy, and I wish they would put some of their specials in writing (the list can be exhausting to listen to), but to me those are minor quibbles about what is very good food.

Is Agnanti the "Greek place by the park"?  If so, we went there once, a long time ago.  We felt a little like we were treated a bit carelessly (because we're not Greek? - that was the vibe), but the food was good.  I can't remember my main course, but my grilled octopus app was the best octopus I've ever had.

Title: Re: Agnanti/Trattori
Post by: daveed on March 07, 2006, 07:17:26 PM
Agnanti is fantastic -- IMO the best Greek restaurant in the Ditmars area. I've been there several times and always impressed with the quality of the food, the freshness, the variety, etc. It's not at all what I call "sloppy greek" cuisine where it's a bunch of greasy grilled meat and starch drownging in yoghurt sauce.

My one beef is the smoking section -- outside on the sidewalk. I've found there's no way to eat on the sidewalk and be free of it, regardless of how windy it is, unless you go when it's totally empty (which, judging by the times I've walked past it, is never.) There are just too many chain smokers puffing away for hours. Bleech! It's too bad b/c it's a fantastic spot -- right by the Park.

Sometimes inside isn't much better. In the warmer months they leave the windows wide open which pulls that cigarette smoke inside. It's particularly bad if you're seated next to a window.
