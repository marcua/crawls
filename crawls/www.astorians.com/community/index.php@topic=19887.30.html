I miss Blue, as well.  It was great comfort food, a no frills atmosphere and, perhaps most important relative to Queens Comfort- it would actually be OPEN when you try to go there.

I have failed at getting food or coffee at Queens Comfort more often than I have succeeded.  At least they have taken down the sign with their "hours" as they never seemed to keep to them anyway, and I got tired of going into their open door to be told by the people hanging out that they were either not open yet, or were closed for some period of time in the middle of the day, and I was usually given a look like I was intruding, or was somehow foolish for thinking that a place with prominent Stumptown coffee signs might want to sell a cup of coffee before noon on a Friday.   So, I think today was my last attempt.  From my experience, and the posts I have read here, it seems like this is more of a hobby than a restaurant.  Which is a shame, because the work they did renovating was impressive.  It looks great inside, and the menu sounds good, but it is a shame they have not gone the final step of properly staffing and maintaining consistent hours.  Oh well.
