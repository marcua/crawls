You should start by raising the issue within your building.  Apprise the Board (assuming it's a coop) and/or management of the situation.  You can ask the Board/management to canvas the building for people with similar complaints (or you can do it yourself).  If you have a block of people affected, that will help you with your next steps.  If enough people in the building are affected, maybe the Board will take action on their behalf.

You can also try to attend some Community Board meetings to put them on notice of the issue.  If you have a petition demonstrating how many people you represent that can only help. 

If you are going to pursue a legal remedy (i.e., obtaining an injunction ordering the backyard area closed), you will want to obtain independent sound verification from a professional which demonstrates that the backyard is emitting noise in violation of NYC law.

1) He is quoted in DNAInfo.Com as saying he wanted to make a family friendly place for mothers with strollers, yadda yadda yadda FALSE. Now we have hipsters out back drinking and smoking and talking loudly.Opening night featured a live band with loud music, dancing, drinking, rowdiness, and nary a stroller in sight.
2) Once again, quoted to be a family oriented restaurant for children and moms and dads to enjoy chicken and ribs. FALSE> It is a full fledged whiskey bar WITH LIVE MUSIC. ( Never any mention of live music) AND dancing, Most likely without a cabaret license which is not legal). ( Already the blogosphere is alive with young hipsters telling each other how "awesome dude! The bourbon and dancing are wild). Chicken and ribs my ass.
3) The place does not even have a kitchen. Food is ferried back and forth from BAKEWAY to THE STRAND in the open air garden at least 50 to 75 feet. There are pidgeons and other birds sitting right above the heavy steel sliding doors which bang shut every 60 seconds which could defecate in the food, not very healthy. It's raining tonight, what happens when it snows, or when the wind blows dust and dirt around.  Just not very healthy at all.And the place calls itself a restaurant but it DOESN'T EVEN HAVE A KITCHEN. Oh I said that already. Just call it what it is, a bar/club. If it looks and smells like a pig..........
4) 40 to 50 feet of bi-folding glass doors which were supposed to be closed at all times and no hanging heavy velvet curtains to mitigate the sound of the sound system which was going to play "smooth jazz in the background". Or so people we led to believe. Last Friday night we all came home to the loud strains of a LIVE BLUEGRASS band and the doors where wide open and people outside smoking and drinking.
5) There was talk he was going to close the "garden" at 11PM , a so called "reasonable hour". ( reasonable unless you have to  go to work at 6 AM and go to sleep by 10 and have kids etc), but on opening night and subsequent nights tenants have witnessed people outside  drinking and smoking at 11:30PM, 12:00AM 2:30 AM etc. Not so reasonable when you are trying to get to sleep to get up for the early morning meeting or the baby is cranky and won't go down because of the noise.
6) He is storing his beer kegs, food carts and other metal materials out back as well where his workers bang them around constantly day and night. Mind you this is affecting over 120+ apartments which are within 10 to 15 FEET of this space. In some cases the fence to his garden is just 5 to 6 feet away from someones windows!
7) In the same DNA Info story he is quoted as saying he wants to be a good neighbor but he is anything but. He promised the local tenants who live right  behind him, who watched his project going up, and even wished him success, that he would be a good and conscientious neighbor.
8) We are not looking to close down the restaurant, we just don't want 100 plus hipsters hanging out 10 feet from our BEDROOM windows smoking, drinking, yelling, talking loudly and generally getting rowdy. We don't want to have to hear his workers banging beer kegs around, or slamming heavy steel doors shut every 60 seconds for hours on end every day and night. We simply want our peace and quiet back. That's all. We are entitled under the law to a certain quality of life. That's what the NYC Noise Code was established for by Lindsay and them amended by Bloomberg to make it stronger. Noise pollution is the number one complaint to 311 in the city. We don't want to add to the statistics. The police have greater problems to solve.
 
So the truth be told, he is not being a neighborhood friendly owner nor establishment at present. Think of what is going to happen in the summer when the tables out front (forget the back!) are filled with loud noisy eaters,  drinkers,and smokers. Think of the hoards of people who will come on the weekends to listen to live bands hanging out on YOUR streets, blocking your path, puking on your sidewalks, getting rowdy in front of your kids as you walk on by. Those big glass doors will be wide open. All the noise and music will be spilling out and it will not just be the people who live behind the garden, but the those who live across the street who already have to deal with Montes.

We are all for progress but at what price? There has to be a happy medium here somewhere. If the owner is not willing to work with the neighborhood to find it, then the neighborhood should not work with him by supporting his businesses.

I beg all who are reading this. If you are affected by this , call 311 , go to your local community board leader, go the local precinct and ask for the community affairs officer and tell him what you have witnessed, your concerns etc, take fotos, videos, recordings, call consumer affairs, call the SLA, do not sit down and take it! This is OUR community. It is a residential community. It is, WAS, nice and quiet and peaceful and safe. Don't let him turn this into Jersey City or Dumbo, or SOHO, . We LIVE HERE. For those of you who are NOT affected, please take pity on those of us who are living with what is slowly turning into our worst nightmare. Please support us in any way you can. Tell your neighbors, help those of us who are most affected. 

There already is a small but growing band of neighbors who are beginning to take action. You can too by doing some of the things I mentioned above. Our little band is growing and will grow larger and stronger. Again, we don't want to shut the restaurant down, we want to be good neighbors too.
