Best way to check is with the NYC DOB or HPD websites.

You can input the address and it will tell you what type of dwelling it is, or how many units it's supposed to have.

www.nyc.gov/hpd
www.nyc.gov/bis

Nine and a half times out of ten, if the utilities are included it's an illegal apartment.  These are most times in the basement.  I really doubt that a top floor apartment would be an illegal one.  Some smaller private houses aren't (and don't have to be) registered with the city so maybe that's why you couldn't find much info.   I think it's 6 family and up, but I could be wrong.

We have no lease and we live in a perfectly legal apartment.  Some landlords prefer to do a month-to-month tenancy, which is legal although it provides fewer protections for the tenants in a potential eviction situation.

Here is basic information about month-to-month tenancies:

Quote
In New York City, the landlord must serve the tenant with a written termination giving 30 days notice before the expiration of the term. The notice must state that the landlord elects to terminate the tenancy and that refusal to vacate will lead to eviction proceedings. (Real Property Law § 232-a)

Why a lease is important.

Very often, although not always, basement apartments are illegal.

The NYC Rent Guidelines Board has a tremendous amount of information on renting information.
