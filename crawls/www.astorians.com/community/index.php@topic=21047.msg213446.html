the new queensboro plaza has puzzled me since soon after it was announced. the space badly needs a re-do, but who builds a park under an above-ground subway train? what what? i guess that for those with passive suicidal tendencies a park in this area might be appeal to dreams of cars hurtling down upon them from the new-and-improved queensboro bridge.
i like the new crosswalks, though. i ignored them at first because i  assumed these carved-out expanses of roadway were reserved exclusively as bike lanes (for to accommodate the millions upon millions of bicyclists raiding this borough every hour [it will not surprise me if/when those crossings are eventually designated exclusively as bike lanes]) but for now the path from QPN to QPS is way less of an adventure for pedestrians than before. being able to move through that maelstrom of traffic without having to sprint is kinda cool, as is not having to hide from traffic behind those subway pillars. then again, neither of these QP endposts is much of a destination, is it... bah.
the graveyard is almost complete. the plaza has looked like a war zone for years and now that the place is starting to take shape our apathetic voting populace is looking at it and saying "WHAT WHAT". i still can not believe that this city's voters let The King purchase an illegal third term. all hail the Emperor, and when you're tired of kissing up to the mayor, hail Sadik-Kahn. i'd like to name a bike lane after each of them, preferably two of the bike lanes that will be removed by the next administration.
