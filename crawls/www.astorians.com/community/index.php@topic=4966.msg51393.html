Hey Astorians,

So, I have this weird fascination with apartments in NYC.  They are all so different, and though I love my apartment and have no intention of moving, I am constantly perusing Craigslist to see what's out there.  I thought it might be interesting for people to describe their Astoria apartments: type/rooms/location/price/roommates/benefits/downfalls, etc.

(I put this in the Local Chat section rather than the real estate section, because this thread is not intended to discuss available apartments or people looking for them.  It's rather a forum to share what your living situation is like.)

I'll start.

I live with my girlfriend in a 2-bedroom apartment around 36th Ave and 24th St.  It's a 3 family house, we live on the top floor, and my landlord lives on the second floor.  The large bedroom is quite sizeable, and the smaller bedroom is used as an art studio.  The living room is large, and there is a separate dining room.  The kitchen is not huge, but quite adequate.  The building is a 1965 rowhouse in decent condition.  The view from our bedroom is a fantastic one of Manhattan - Queensboro Bridge, Empire State Building and Chrystler Building, etc.  We have two cats and parking is easy...90% of the time on our block...with street cleaning only once a week per side.  We pay $1500/month and our rent has blessedly not increased in the three years we've been living there.  All in all, it's a great situation.  The biggest frustrations are living so close to a slightly-OCD landlord and the fact that we cannot control the heat.  That's not abnormal in NYC, but our landlord will often turn it off all together when he and his family are not home.  At least we don't pay for the heat.

What's your living situation?

Cool thread idea.

We're at 35th Ave and 35th Street.  My sis and I LOVE our apartment.  Loathe and despise our neighbors, but love our actual living quarters.  The bedrooms are huge - here is a list of furniture I have managed to cram into my bedroom with loads of room to spare: long desk, filing cabinet with big printer, bookcase, twin bed, 6-drawer horizontal dresser, night table.  My sister's bedroom is even bigger.  It has a giant living room, good-sized (small, but workable) bathroom, nice entryway, and hall closet.  The kitchen is small and narrow, but well-designed (meaning the fridge, oven, and sink are in convenient places), with more storage space than you'd expect for such a wee kitchen.  Plus, it had all been renovated just before we moved in so all the appliances, fixtures, paint, and hardware are brand new.  It's close to the train,  Arharn Thai (my new favorite Thai place - sorry Wave), Bravo, laundry facilities, little delis for quick shopping, Pathmark, and makes for a healthy walk when I come up to visit you Ditmars people.  Broadway is only two blocks away.  If something horrible happened and I had to walk home from Queensboro Plaza, it wouldn't be too far.  The Studios are really close, AMMI is right there, and Sunswick is right up the street.

Disadvantages include - and sorry if you're tired of me saying this - my noisy, obnoxious neighbors (who play in the courtyard despite several signs and who have raucous parties at least once a week in the vestibule and on our landing, complete with beer cans and cigarette butts everywhere the next day), the construction of the Frank Sinatra High School of the Arts across the street, the renovation at all hours of the apartment in the next building which shares a wall with my sis' bedroom and our living room, and the Floridian heat. 

Other than that, we're tolerably happy here, and we love our kitty, Bella.   :-)  Oh yeah, and rent is $1600 a month and includes water and heat.

I think that is a symptom for ALL New Yorkers. Real estate is a hobby here. I love to look up at the lit windows at night as I'm walking home and try to sneak a peak into people's homes/lives. Not in a pervy way, more just fascination. SO many people live on top of each other in this city. Every window is a story, you know?

I moved into my place at 31st Ave and 34th St in the fall of 05 after living with a friend in a small 2 bedroom in Brooklyn for two years. I started paying 1,050, but it went up as per the rent-stabilized percentage, last fall. It's a one-bedroom, which is more than I need or can afford (originally I was looking for a studio), but it's worth it to me because it's all my own and it feels like a home and for a homebody like me, that's key to staying sane. And of course, that's priceless. :)

It's a small building (7 apartments), and most of the people in it have lived there forever. The landlord is an elderly couple that live upstairs. They don't speak very good english but I adore them as they keep the building immaculate and everything working properly and are very friendly. Everyone's really quiet, except for the psycho woman who lives in the building next door, but she's only an irritant during the warmer months, when she goes outside and hollers at everyone/thing in the street. But she means well...

Pluses: huge eat-in kitchen (if you've ever seen Jen & Jonathan's, it's about that size) though I use the eat-in part as an office; windows in every room and facing both the front and back of the building; fabulous & large laundromat across the street; lots 'o storage space including massive closet in bedroom, plus coat closet, and large storage spaces above both of them; the pretty floors and original wood trim around the doorways and the doors themselves, most of which have escaped being painted over.

Negs: Street noise, as my bedroom/living room face the street & I'm on the 2nd floor; creaky floor boards - SOO creaky my fat cat even makes the occasional noise as he walks around; ugly carpet in the bedroom, but necessary to help with noise; ugly block, in my opinion (There just aren't many trees on it, and all the buildings are flat-front and tall.); erratic water temperature in the shower - I call it playing air traffic controller in the morning as I'm constantly adjusting the knobs; the high gloss white paint my dear landlord slapped up on the walls as I was moving in. Yes, I STILL haven't mustered the strength/energy to prime and paint over it.

Ooh, neat.

I live by myself in a 1Br on the top floor of a building at 19th/Ditmars.  The building is, I believe, pre-war.  It's only 8 units plus an occupied basement and some commercial on the bottom floor.  My apartment is huge and cheap (1200, heat+hot water).  The ceilings are really high and the walls/floors are thick.  In the hallway I can hear my neighbors having parties or blasting their TV but inside my apartment I can't hear anything.  Most of the people in my building are older and are all very friendly.  I often find myself carrying heavy items up- and downstairs for the old lady who lives underneath me.  I figure it's a tradeoff for the noise I make (assuming she can even hear it!).  The whole apartment is hardwood+tile.  I have a lot of closet space.

pros: I have an amazing view of Astoria Park and Ward Island.  I can go on my roof to watch planes take off and land at LGA.  I have more room than I know what to do with.  The previous tenants had the place painted before they moved and it's gorgeous.  My landlord isn't into jacking up the rent on his tenants and my rent has stayed steady since I moved in over 1.5 years ago.  My kitchen is bigger than most people's living rooms and my living room is bigger than most studio apartments.

cons: Retards on motorcycles and in loud cars all summer long tearing up and down Ditmars.  I fantasize about being on my roof with a sniper rifle every night during the warm seasons.  The heat in the winter isn't very hot.  I usually run a space heater at night when I go to sleep (I have a Vornado - very safe).  I have more room than I know what to do with.  There is a lot of empty space and it's also expensive to cool in the summer or heat with a space heater in the winter.  My landlord is hard to get in touch with.  It's about 3/4 of a mile from the subway and the bus service isn't the best.  At one point the building had a mice problem.  I wasn't affected too much being on the top floor but a couple got into my apartment and ate some food before he got an exterminator to lay down some poison (I have my own out now).

I just stumbled on this thread and want to revive it!

My roommate and I are on 36th Street and 21st Avenue. We have a three bedroom on the top floor of a 2-family home (our landlords are downstairs). We have a Goldilocks set-up--the bedrooms are large, medium and small. Mine's the big one, my roommate has the medium one, and we've turned the small room into a hamster kingdom/office/guest room. We have a very nice sized living room, small bathroom and smallish kitchen, and an enormous private balcony. We pay $1750 per month.

Pros:
--The apartment was in unbelievably gorgeous condition. When they renovated it, they did it right--high quality tile and wood flooring, all-tile bathroom, granite counters in the kitchen, screens in every window--just really, really nice. It's still practically gleaming and is very easy to keep clean because every surface is smooth.
--The block is quiet and residential, and people are friendly.
--The balcony. We can (and have) easily fit 15 people, a table, and a grill out there. We can watch planes take off and land from it.
--Our landlords are adorable and really great. They gave us a beautiful rose bush when we moved in (which I am trying desperately not to kill), have brought goodies up to us several times, and are always happy to lend us outdoor chairs when we have parties.

Cons:
--The large bedroom has no closet, the medium bedroom is right off the kitchen, and the small bedroom is Manhattan-sized and shaped. It's not our ideal layout.
--The walk to the train is a bit further than I would have preferred--but parking is great, and my roommate loves that.
--We just saw our third giant roach in two weeks (one outside, two inside). I can't figure out how the place would be SO clean but have these big roaches, so I'm hoping they're maybe coming in from outside somehow. Still...ugh.
--Despite our lovely landlords, I think I would still prefer to live in a building instead of a private home. We don't have parties often, but I'd still rather piss off neighbors than someone with the power to evict me.
