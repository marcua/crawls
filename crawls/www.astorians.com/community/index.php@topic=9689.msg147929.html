I like Indigo's brunch, good food and it's pretty reasonable--$13.95, which includes 2 drinks. They have a killer bloody mary (I was never really a bloody mary person until I had Indigo's, now I get one every time I go for brunch). I usually get the eggs benedict.

And they have a nice pseudo-outdoor area in the back (large room in the back with a removable roof in the nice weather).

(Indigo is on 31st St. betw. 30th Ave & Newtown, right off the 30th Ave N/W)

Just breezing through the posts and came across this thread, and thought I'd put in my 2 cents.

We have been to this place 4 times for brunch and it has been on the money all 4 times. The Service was friendly and on cue with the dishes they recommended. They have a broad offering of dishes and the ones we had were indeed fresh including the Sushi, and it was comforting to have seen an Asian preparing it (it just confirmed the way it tasted).

Micheal one of the managers their was very friendly and down to earth and always greeted us when we visited.

Oh and they have this nice red Greek wine, all i remember is that it's 1st on the list and comes from some monastery.

Joe had a fresh extracted apple and carrot juice which was better than your usual OJ. Which they by the way squeeze fresh.

One of the hits was the choclate french toast with apples, just ask waiter/res for extra apples!

So enjoy brunch at FLO. :mrgreen:

Grand Ave. has great food. The service is hit or miss. The one constant is that the music is always offensively loud. I've eaten there over 100 times and never after 8pm. I used to grab a quick omelet about twice a week on week day mornings and the music was still at "night club" volume.


Sanford's on Broadway is can't miss.

Indigo on 31st street is very reliable.

I liked Locale on 35th ave. the times I was there but it was never crowded while I was a patron. Also - the last time I was there was over 6 months ago.

Cronin's on Broadway is the best hangover food hands down for those who enjoy excessive weekends. The owner is a real character and I like him. I've spoken to him several times and he told me he buys nearly all of the food himself. His fish comes from Marino's on 30th ave.

I did Indigo today. Not especially crowded but I came within the last 45 mins. of Brunch.

Sat in the back with the retractable arched translucent roof open. I can't think of any place else that has such a thing. A little humid But pretty cool. A gas fireplace (not on) and two flat screen TVs.

My well competent newbie waitress was a dark eyed beauty named Daniela. I had asked the pointed question regarding the maple syrup touted on the menu.  She checked it out for me inside...Guess what...it was 'table syrup'. At least she was honest. I had the Ranchos Huevos (sp?), they were quite good. The two bloody mary's I had were quite good too.

Daniela brought me some comp flat bread and tomato / herb / olive pate, very good..and free. She also brought a comment card with the check. Hey, they're open to suggestions. I really have a pet peeve about table syrup being passed off as advertised Maple Syrup....absolutely a deal breaker with me.
