Well why is it necessary to write USA?  Naturally, we can expect Vantage buildings to know they are operating on US soil.  If you are writing the note in English, then that on its own expresses the desire to see the annoucement written in English.  So...... what exactly is the point of putting USA on the note?   Is it to say, "Hey this is the USA and I'll learn you to write in English"?  If that's the case, then that's pretty obnoxious and that's not what the USA stands for. 

We do not need English to be our national language, because, unlike some other cultures, ours is not embattled nor struggling.  The spread and maintenance of English is not a defining factor of American culture (McDonalds is more representative than English).  This is not Belgium, or Greece, or Georgia.  So why make it a national language?  The laws and the courts operate in English as it is and shall do so for the far forseeable future.  Same with tax forms, political debates and Judge Judy.  What are the official language folks worried about?  You said yourself the meat and potatoes of this country aren't even close to getting to ten in a language other than English.  Are they worried about  comparative pittance spent on translating this stuff out into other languages?  About subways signs in Korean?  About having to refer to their favorite Chinese restaurant on Mott Street by its address rather than its name?  About the Russian language services provided by Commerce Bank in Brighton Beach?  Specifically, what is the goal you wish to attain by having English declared a national language? 

There are some folks who think that America is not weakened, but rather strengthened, by the proliferation and maintenance of languages other than English.  While the languages of immigrants normally do get lost rather quickly by their offspring, having English declared the national language hastens this process.  How nice would it have been if we didn't stamp out Cajun in the Louisiana Bayou, or the Gullah of South Carolina were strong and viable, or the numerous Indian languages weren't on the verges of extinction?
