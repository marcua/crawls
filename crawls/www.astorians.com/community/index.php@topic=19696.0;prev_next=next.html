i can't help you - but I find this really interesting. 

Few uncomfortable seats was Starbucks original coffee shop plan.  At the original location there are NO seats.   The locations that followed had uncomfortable seats - the thinking was - BUy your coffee and get out of here.  If there are any Seattle transplants on the board - you remember the BeeLiner Diner sign "eat it and beat it".... that was Starbucks idea too - but they didn't even want you to drink in the store.

There's a place a door or two down from the library called Havana Cafe - when I've been it's been pretty quiet, and although I've never stayed and read myself, they are very welcoming and don't seem to be the type to mind someone hanging out for a while.  The coffee is decent, too - it's a house blend of Brazilian, Colombian and Ethiopian beans.  As a plus, in addition to sweets and savoury Cuban and Brazilian treats, they also have salads and sandwiches.

It's a little bit of a hike from 31st Street, but Bistro 33's Side Door Cafe a couple doors from its corner spot at 21st and Ditmars might fit your description.
