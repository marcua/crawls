It's funny - I don't consider the upper midwest to be corn country...but some of the best corn I've ever had was in Michigan growing up.  I've also had excellent corn in Ohio.  Midwest does produce the best sweet corn in the country, as far as I'm concerned (and I'm no midwest booster).  Today I think of the upper midwest as more dairy country.  Harlan?  What do you think?

Corn country:  definitely Iowa and Indiana.  Ohio to some extent, too.  Illinois is a funny state because it's part urban city, part industrial midwest and part southern!

I lived in central Illinois for 7 years, and the vast majority of the land was used to farm corn and soybeans (and occasionally wheat). Urbana had a Sweet Corn Festival every August, but most years they had to import the corn from other states! I recall one year the sweet corn was from Colorado! The problem is that almost all of the corn grown in the corn belt is for livestock consumption or to make HFCS and other processed products. So if the timing of the small amounts of sweet corn grown is off by just a little, there's none for the festival!

I do think of Western parts of the upper Midwest as corn country, like Iowa and Southern Minnesota, although not as densely as Iowa, Illinois, and Indiana... Yes, there's lots of dairy in Wisconsin, and fair amount of land that's still lakes and forests, especially once you get North of Madison and Milwaukee...

Oh, and I still think of Illinois as mostly agricultural. :) There's only that little corner up there with skyscrapers, factories, and people!
