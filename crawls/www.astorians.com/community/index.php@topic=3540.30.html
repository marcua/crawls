You know, I can't stand kareoke either but I will just avoid beer garden on those nights.  I have never considered it a form of art exactly, and never heard it referred to as such, but I suppose for those with great voices, it is a great chance to practice, get noticed, have fun, and just plain sing, perform, etc.  I cannot sing for the life of me, nor perform, so I tend to avoid these things - mostly because I've seen a lot of bad performers like myself the times I have gone.  But I do know people love the stuff, as evidenced by how many times I get asked to go with friends to kareoke.  So I would just avoid the BG on those nights (which hopefully is only one night, like most bars).

As far as the beer garden being loud, it definitely has changed immensely over the years.  I've been here 9 years, and you used to be able to walk in at 8 or 9 pm on a weekend night and still have the place be a comfortable enjoyable spot to chill for the night.  These days, weekends are packed to the nines, with people resorting to standing up because there are no seats left, and I would think it is much louder.  But I would never want this institution to close.  It would be nice if the staff were friendlier and if they would hire more bartenders and waitresses, to accomadate the surge in customers.  All they seem to hire is more security guards, which they never used to have either.  I suppose the change in popularity of the place was inevitable and we're all going to have to learn to live with it.  The majority of the people there on weekends are definitely not from Queens, and yeah, I would be pretty upset if there was a line, and they decided to start charging a cover without a band or the kareoke going on.  It's just a bar, no dj, and they certainly don't need more money by charging an extra cover.  But if there's a band I can certainly understand.
