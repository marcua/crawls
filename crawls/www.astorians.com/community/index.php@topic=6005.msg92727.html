Quote from: Shelby on November 25, 2007, 11:20:38 AM
My problem is that I am burning through light bulbs incredibly fast in my kitchen overhead fixture.  [...]
Is there something wrong with the fixture?
Here are some guesses:

Could it be running too hot? Something like a recessed fixture with thermal insulation behind it?  Or using a higher wattage bulb than the fixture is designed for? If this is the case, maybe a compact fluorescent bulb would give the light you crave without the problem.

Poor contact between the socket and the bulb? If the thing flickers or dims for no reason, or makes little sounds when it's operating, there could be a poor connection socket-to-bulb, or in the wiring, or a faulty switch.

Abnormally low voltage or use of a dimmer can shorten the life of halogen type bulbs - the process that makes them "long-life" depends on maintaining a particular range of operating temperature.

Abnormally high voltage. A symptom might be that the light gets brighter when some appliance like the refrigerator switches on. This would be unusual, and indicate a serious flaw in the wiring that had better be corrected.

Actually, any of these problems need to be corrected.
