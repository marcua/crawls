NYPD has it's wires crossed
« on: January 19, 2008, 06:03:43 PM »
Wednesday night we went to use our car, and it was gone.  We called the NYPD and they looked to see if the car was towed.  Nothing came up.  So, they sent officers out to our house so we could file a report, and they looked the car up, as well.  Nothing.  They put in a stolen vehicle report on our car and said if anything comes up, they'll let us know.  So, a couple of days pass, and this morning, my wife's dad became curious and searched for the car on the internet.  To our surprise, the very first place he looked said that our car was towed.  Unbelievable!  When I asked the NYPD about it, they were like "well, sometimes it takes a little bit for towed cars to show up in the system".  No excuse.

The thing is, it was towed and put in the system on the 14th at noon, and we did not find the car missing and file a report until the 16th at like 10 PM!  Surely it wouldn't take 2 and a half days for a towed vehicle to show up in their system!  I mean, if I can buy something on Amazon and it immediately reduces their inventory, you would think the NYPD could get their stuff straight.  I mean, we're talking about computers here, not the Pony Express.  And, even if there WAS a lag in the system, it DID eventually show up, because my father-in-law found it 1200 miles away!!(not that mileage matters since it's the internet)  So, when the towed vehicle decided to show up, you would THINK that the system would be like "Oh, wait........this is marked stolen.......maybe I should put 2 and 2 together and give notice to the cops"!!! 

Not to mention, to get an officer to come to our apartment, we had to call 911.  WTF?  Isn't 911 for emergencies?  It wasn't like someone was being attacked.....we just needed to take care of our car situation.  Why take up the 911 bandwidth?  Why couldn't the precinct just dispatch someone themselves?  It just seems really messed up to me.  To top it all off, half of the people that we had to speak to to handle the issue were very rude.  I don't think they always stand by the whole "Courtesy, Professionalism, Respect" motto.  These types of situations really make me lose faith in the police department.  I wish I could feel like they are providing a service to me, a tax paying citizen, as opposed to feeling like I am some kind of annoyance to them.  Seriously, is this what we pay taxes for?
