Quote from: edhopper on November 05, 2008, 11:21:08 AM
There are no real potatoes in the fries from McD and BK. Just potato starch and corn starch and other "stuff"

No - McDonald's fries are made from whole potatoes.  They have additives - including wheat and milk - and are dipped in a sugar solution prior to frying (and McDonald's has lied about these additives), but they start with and are cut from whole potatoes.

Burger King, in an attempt to make their fries more like McDonald's fries, developed a process about 10 years ago that coats each fry with a thin layer of other ingredients designed to make them more crispy.  But the interior is also cut from a whole potato.

They both store (this is how they ensure that all the potatoes they use end up with the same percentage of water, ensuring a consistent end fried product) clean, cut, process, blanch and freeze - their fries at a central location before they are shipped to each McD's or BK location in a frozen state.
