I was there last night with a couple of friends and we had a great time!  We tried a number of wines and all were absolutely delicious.  I drank more than I usually do, because of how good they were.

I can't tell you what my favorite wine was because they were all excellent.  We had a sparkling white, two reds, a two dessert wines.  I would say that the most striking was the dark red dessert wine from Uruguay we had (that's the Tannat that tennat refers to above).  We ate it with bits of walnut bread, blue cheese, walnuts and red grapes, and the combination was phenomenal.  My friends and I all agreed that the wine would have been great on vanilla bean ice cream.

Food-wise, my favorite things both involved bacon - the mac and cheese topped with bacon and the bacon-wrapped prunes.  I also liked the gasm dip and the lavash was a nice touch.  Also remarkable was the molten chocolate cake with spearmint gelato.  We loved how the mint gelato complemented the chocolate cake.

I walked home tipsy and happy.  Was concerned a little that I would pass out so hard that it would be difficult to get up this morning for my marathon training run, but I had a really good 4 miles!  I believe the wine was of such good quality that it was not conducive to the evil hangover (I am a lightweight when it comes to drinking). 

One other thing that I absolutely loved was that the music was played at a reasonable level.  We could hear it alright, but we could also easily hear each other in conversation.  For me, this is very important.  I don't do well in places with super loud music, so this was a pleasant surprise.

The space was very nice and I felt comfortable there.  All the suggestions and information given to us during the evening were really good - they really know their stuff.  Thank you, Dean.

So, check it out!  I recommend it.
