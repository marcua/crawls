Am I the only person who recognizes that aside from Rare Books stores, physical book stores aren't going to exist in 10 years time?  I think this was the first year that e-books outsold physical books in Christmas sales on Amazon, a trend that's surely going to continue and grow.  The price of Kindles and such will come down a lot in the next few years.

You could probably swing a small indie book store in a college town or major city, but I suspect even that's going to be hard to pull off.  The chains are going to go completely online, either digital books or mail order (which I gather represents a large portion of physical sales now). Bookstores now are where record/CD stores were at the turn of the century -- still functioning, but aware of this new digital thing coming down the road.  I'd be very afraid if I owned an indie book store right now, and concerned with transition to digital if I was at a chain.  (For the record, haven't bought a book in years -- the NYC Public Library really is one of the best in the world, and I use it constantly.  For free, along with my donations.)

Back to our regularly-scheduled pining for Trader Joes ...
