The place is called Oceano.  We tried it yesterday late in the afternoon.  They had a few tables being served.  We really liked this place a lot.  We'll go back for sure.  The softshell crab appetizer is amazing.  They had a special fish soup special that was very delicious.  I had the skirt steak entree with a special Latin sauce on the side.  It was excellent.  My wife had a similar entree that was also very delicious.  Two big guys seated behind us had the hot shellfish combo special.  It's a specialty of the house.  A large variety of shellfish.  Includes a lobster tail, scallops, king crab legs, etc.  About $35 per person.  They each received a serving platter sized dish.  They were blown away and ate every bit.  I hope this place makes it.  The prices are very reasonable for what they serve.  They also gave the tables a complementary mashed plantain appetizer that was very tasty.  And they gave us a complementary creme brulee that was amazing.  It's a great location and the staff couldn't be nicer.  After the service fiasco at Mohave, this place shined. :mrgreen:

My boyfriend and I recently ate at Oceano. I'm going to suggest that anyone even slightly interested, try it. It seems like it might fill a niche in the neighborhood and here's why:

I had the jalea appetizer; fried calamari, fried shrimp, fried yucca (a potato like vegetable) and a pickled onion and corn coulis. It was FANTASTIC and its obvious that they are very good at frying- each item retained its own flavor, the seasoning was fabulous and it was light. Honestly delicious. I just had the lobster bisque for my entree, and that was pretty standard. My boyfriend had a skirt steak and bean dish that needed a sauce-it was too dry- but there was a lot of flavor and we both decided that we would come back- many of the appetizers were very interesting sounding and I'd go back in a heartbeat if I had a calamari craving, as I'm known to have.

So on a scale of 0-10, we gave it a 6, which is pretty solid. The service was sort of awful- we had maybe 4 waiters, and they arent very familiar with their menu and when delivering the skirt steak to my boyfriend, waiter #3 yelled "HERE'S YOUR COW! IT'S DELICIOUS!"

Pretty standard pricing in my opinion, especially for the crab legs. They are heavy on the crab combos.

I'd go back if I was feeling casual and wanted fried seafood. The restaurant has garage-door style windows so it will probably be an outdoor cafe come summer, which might be nice. I'm happy it's here as a decent option, although it wont make my top five restaurants in the area any time soon (of which mine are JJ's Fusion, Wave Thai, Bistro 33, Watawa and Fattys.)
