Title: Queens Greenmarkets, Open 'till Dec, Pumpkin Composting & Thanksgiving Turkey
Post by: AnastasiaP on November 01, 2010, 03:15:03 PM
Queens Markets Open For Winter!
Jackson Heights and Sunnyside Greenmarkets will remain open until Saturday December 18 and Sunday December 19th respectively. There is also a proposal under consideration to make Jackson Heights a year-round market. A determining factor for the farmers and for Greenmarket will be market attendance in December, so come shop, tell your neighbors and fellow Queens foodies, and let's get our borough its first year-round Greenmarket. The farmers will be there no matter snow, sleet, or chilly temps so come out and keep us company this December. Stock up on all your favorite winter veggies, as well as locally produced bread, wine, cheese, and meat for all your holiday celebrating.

Pumpkin Composting
Don't be haunted by dead Halloween jack-o-lanterns or bury them in the landfill. Let them rest in peace at our compost drop-off events. The following Queens Markets will be accepting Pumpkins and yard debris, including leaves, for composting. Western Queens Compost Initiative (http://www.facebook.com/wqnscompost) in conjunction with Project LeafDrop (http://nycleaves.org/) will be accepting yard waste and pumpkins for compost and will turn them into beautiful soil for next seasons neighborhood community gardens.

Turkey Time!
Hard to believe Thanksgiving is right around the corner! Be prepared with a pre-ordered, local, humanely raised turkey from one of our Greenmarket producers. Come to Jackson Heights Greenmarket , located at 34th Ave and 78th Street, on Sunday November 7th or 14th to order a turkey for pick-up at market the Sunday before Thanksgiving. Order forms are available from Dipaola's Turkey or Hoosic River Poultry at their respective market stands. There are a limited number of birds so sign-up early!


Jackson Heights Greenmarket is located on 34th Avenue between 77th and on 78th between Northern Blvd and 34th Ave every Sunday through December 19. For more information, visit us at http://www.grownyc.org/
