When I joined this online Community of Astorians.com—and yes, I know that this is the internet and that capital letters are not needed, but I prefer to always put my hometown in a respectful light whenever possible—somewhere around March of 2005, it was a promising avenue into the soul of the Astoria Community at large. The only other Community-oriented site at the time was MyAstoria (http://www.myastoria.com/). Astorians.com had a few hundred members on the forum and the hot discussion was about the best bagel place around! Now Astorians.com boasts over 4000 members and averages around 140 unique posts per day. The site had far less software features and far less dissension. There was also little to no moderation. (I recently read something which profoundly resonates on this discussion: A thing moderately good is not so good as it ought to be. Moderation in temper is always a virtue; but moderation in principle is always a vice. —Thomas Paine) There were no little red and green thumbs-up and thumbs-down. There were no nods. I would personally very much appreciate it if this remained a nod-free thread. If you want to add to this thread, please post your comments. IMHO, the prolific nodders have added nothing to this forum and have in fact detracted from far too many vibrant, interesting and necessary conversations. To the neg nodders who go from thread to thread, who haven't got the pertinaciousness (READ: balls) to post their own opinions, who always neg nod certain people for whatever their petty reason, please go back to lurking because I am asking that either the nods feature be disabled or people start being held responsible for their neg nodding usage so that those that are offending the system can be held accountable for their actions to disrupt the forum. When you see a whole lot of nodding going on in a thread, doesn't it change the thread's tone into a we-against-them kind of thing? Their presence alters the discussion itself and discourages people from posting. When did flaming politely become so acceptable? No-one can identify what neg nodding ultimately produces, except a lot of hurt feelings and I hardly think that's what this forum is all about.

Merm has done an outstanding job in the development and upkeep of Astorians.com, and I personally want to take the time to applaud him on that. mcdirk recently, unbeknownst to him, got a standing ovation from me on his recognizing that a thread was getting too hot and locking it up until cooler minds prevailed, and on a few occasions asked that a conversation get toned down. That should be the extent of how this board needs to be handled. Have you noticed that since it was recently stated that it is okay to use curse words spelled out in posts that they are occurring now more often? Besides being somewhat amusing, it also succeeded in calling peoples attention to the little known fact that this, just like any other, forum has default software parameters and that they can be changed by changing the fields of your own personal profile. (Here is a little test for you: f___ if the preceding word is PANSY you should go to your Profile —> Look and Layout Preferences and put a check mark next to Leave Words Uncensored.) Then you will be able to read the word F•U•C•K and all discussions in their entirety without any artificial sweeteners.

Until now I have been a strong supporter of this forum up to and including urging people to financially support this site. For the requested donation of $12.00 per year we supporters were promised an advertising-free site and some additional perks. I was of the opinion that supporters would have a stronger voice in the goings on around here. After all, how many private clubs allow non-members to vote? But apparently membership in this club is supporting the non-donating member, ass-kissing flocks of sheep that will maintain the status quo regardless of the consequences.

We have been reminded time-after-time that this site is the private property of the owner of the site. I've always read that like, "it's my ball and I'll make up the rules for the game or I'll just go home with my ball." Somewhere among the threads there is even an analogy comparing this site (or was it all internet forums) to a magazine with an editor and a publisher and how things get published at that type of venue. I recently read a very interesting true story about public rights on private property. Back in the 1970's a gentleman who was quite the radical about building a nuclear plant on Long island, went with his little local anti-nuclear plant group and started passing out leaflets inside the Smith Haven Mall in Lake Grove, Long Island. They got themselves kicked out. The Mall rules stated that since the Mall was private property, the management can say who stays and who goes. The leaflet group initiated a lawsuit in New York State and they won the suit based upon the concept that a mall is pretty much the same thing as the "Town Square" of old. The Town Square was where people gathered to meet and discuss happenings of the day and where the local Government posted things of Community interest. The mall may have been private property but it had become more than that.

So has Astorians.com.

It is now so much more than a privately owned website. It has become a public space where local residents of the Astoria Community meet to discuss and share information. This suspending and banning of members of the forum is absurd. Even in the days of old, when one was punished they were put into stocks in the Town Square where they still had access to their Community. Suspension and banning denies the basic principle of this forum: to meet and share thoughts and ideas through input with friends and neighbors. This online Community has both the intelligence and the tenacity to moderate itself without any High School hall monitors like the simple- little-minded snitches who obviously find use for the 'report to moderator' link. I was recently told that suspensions are usually 24 hours, then 48 hours, then 7 days, then 30 days, escalating with each suspension. But, that doesn't seem to be the case. And why, among the pages of the Board's Rules, is this not mentioned? The rules themselves may be fine, it just seems that they are very unreasonably and inconsistently enforced, and that their enforcement has become a subjective statement by the enforcer rather than any other issue. Inconsistent moderating has led to so much discontent of this site, leaving the only consistent issue being the inconsistent acts of the Administrator and the Moderator(s).

How telling is it that in the Open Discussion of Board Rules and Policy (http://www.astorians.com/community/index.php?topic=10827.0;topicseen), as of this writing, there are a mere 37 posts expressing their opinions and yet there have been 50 people who responded to the poll. As I perceive it, the problems that this forum is currently experiencing is not so much from the having of rules as it is the way these rules are both enforced and interpreted, and the inconsistent way they are moderated.  I am constantly reminding myself that we would not have racial integration if it were not for the minority voices being louder and more willing to suffer the consequences of their actions to just be heard. Even more interesting than the aforementioned poll, is the poll in the nods (http://www.astorians.com/community/index.php?topic=10081.0;topicseen) thread. With only 38 posts in the thread there have been 54 people who voted and almost 25% of those voted I don't really care for it, but I use it anyway for those times when I disagree but don't feel like posting about it. Establish rules that remind people that they are free to express themselves and responsible for the content of what they say, they will become better communicators, achieve empathy with those who hurt them or whom they offend, and they will mature, and their relationships will be deeper, truer. 

Does this work perfectly every time? Of course not. But perfection isn't the point. Respecting people's rights as adults to learn from the effects of their choices is a modus operandi I can live with. You see a vicious tantrum, I see a genuine crisis that is a moment along a continuum, one that can only balance itself out in a meaningful way if its participants are left free to communicate and held responsible for what they say by each other.

I told merm in February that he was chasing a degree of control that was neither desirable nor feasible, and I stand by that.

i've been thinking on this whole thing and i think this medium caters to misunderstandings.  when you write something (as opposed to a face-to-face) things are more easily misconstrued.  at my last job that was one of the main comments made to me from my superiors.  they thought my emails were a bit abrupt at times, and although they knew it wasn't meant in any negative way, they warned me to constantly edit myself so that there aren't any misunderstandings.  a simple sentence can be read a lot of different ways, depending on the "voice" or "tone" that one puts on it.

astorians.com is pretty informative, and i think that's the main reason i check in so often.  on that note, i think it serves it's purpose.  on the otherhand, i think when it comes to the issues that are debatable, these recent events have hindered the forum...especially more recently.

since reading all of these recent comments about the rules and regulations, i find that i'm more tentative in the postings that i make, and i'm not so sure that's a good thing.  it almost defeats the purpose of some of these threads.  it's one thing to be blatantly disrespectful or insulting toward others, but it's another to be passionate about one's (differing) opinion.  i can understand how the former wouldn't be tolerated, but the latter?

i'll be the first to say that i don't know all the facts that started this line, but it just seems that a lot has been made of some comments made on a forum where there should be a lot A LOT of room for expression.  i think it's an almost impossible task for the moderators to be accurate all of the time, as what's insulting to one person, may not be insulting to another.  instead of having the moderators making these decisions, it should be more dependent on the the community reporting/commenting what they deem offense.  then in those grey areas, the moderators can be the final word.  i think when it's left up to the people, things eventually sort themselves out.  and i also think this is far more effective than the "nod" system, which i'm not convinced works at all, really.
