No it wouldn't.  Your example is not a direct parallel, once again.  Different language, different culture, people from different continents.

Modern Greek is a completely different language from Ancient Greek which is different from Medieval Greek - not to mention all the varieties and dialects that shifted and disappeared across the eons.  They're in the same Indo-European family of languages.  But they're not the same language.  They're not even mutually intelligible on the level of related languages such as Romanian and Italian, for example.

There was much migration from Asia to Europe and vice versa in that region.

Between the times of ancient Greece & modern Greece, the people and culture were displaced by new, completely different civilizations at least three times.  Roman. Byzantine. Ottoman.  Upheaval, destruction, rebirth each time.  It's not the same culture.   Just because some dudes in the 19th century decided to call it "Greece" again doesn't make them equivalents.
