The program through my insurance company is www.globalfit.com.  I don't know if it's open to everyone, but you can try.  You didn't say what part of Astoria you live in, as location is so important when you choose a gym!  

I have 2 gym memberships:

Fitness Point:  Insanely cheap... Last summer I signed up for some kind of "buy a year, get 2 months free" thing and I remember it broke down to be about $17 a month.  Of course, you get what you pay for...   It used to be full of broken machines, but recently I have noticed all new treadmills and they've fixed their ellipticals.  Honestly, I'm afraid to try the weight machines because I'd rather not injure myself.. but I'd let someone else comment on them who has used them.  Gym is kind of dirty, but you should know better than to touch anything and then not wash your hands...  It's located on 34th Ave/28th Street and is really close to my apt, so I rush in there when I don't have that much time or it's raining.  They have 2 friendly staff members, and the rest will ignore you.  Locker rooms are creepy (at least women's is) and I wouldnt recommend showering there.  I've never noticed meatheads, but you will see the occasional idiot on their cell phone while doing cardio (which means they need to move their fat a** a little faster), but unfortunately, those are everywhere.  They don't seem to realize that others really, really do not care to hear their conversation!  I also saw a woman walking on the treadmill while holding her purse on her shoulder, which I thought was really odd..

Synergy:  I like Synergy, but many people do not.  Staff there is friendly, but several also will try to sign you up for personal training at an additional cost (solution:  politely say no--they've stopped asking me awhile ago).  I originally was going to join Synergy through the globalfit insurance program, and the price on there was something like a  $50 activation plus $14 every 2 weeks, I think.  I decided to go in there and check it out first.  They had some kind of deal going on for $35 a month (you probably see the flyers everywhere!) but I said I'd go through globalfit if they couldnt match the price.  Final price was $30 a month with no activation fee.  I've heard about problems with Synergy auto-renewing you and continuing to charge your credit card if you do the monthly charge (don't know if this is true), so I just prepaid the entire year-$360.  As far as the gym goes, I like it.  They have nice, new cardio equipment (including those dumb tvs on top of the machines that I have no use for!) and a decent, well-maintained selection of weight machines/free weights.  There are some meatheads, but I don't mind them--at least they're there really working out!  A few dumb girls in spandex not really doing anything... not so many that they really get in your way, though.  I have seen some idiots on cell phones... and have encountered a few that offended with their extremely strong perfume or old man odor.  Again, though--that's everywhere.  Oh and Synergy is located at Broadway and Crescent St.

No matter what they say, you can always negotiate.  They do not want you to walk out the door without buying.  Even if you really don't go to the insurance website, say you did and that you will not pay more.  At least get them to drop that stupid activation fee (which, if you think about it--you're paying a fee so that you can start paying them every month?).  

Good luck and gyms are one of my favorite topics--so feel free to ask anything else!

I've belonged to the Rock for five years. As someone said, yes, it is pricey but it's clean,
well-maintained and the variety of equipment and classes is great.

members -  I know SAG was one, and I think police department staff also. You'd have to ask.

two cash payments, one in the late fall and another a few months later.

workout area, roaches in the locker room, etc. Plus they had very few classes and when I asked a
trainer if he was ACE certified, he asked what that meant.

I toured Evolution just before it opened and it looked good. Nice layout, lots of equipment and the
people I talked to were friendly. If it was closer to me, I'd have considered it more seriously.

Location is key - luckily now in Astoria you have a few choices. You should go in and visit the
clubs you're interested in. The Rock will give you a three-day pass to try it out. I think others will too.

You have to go when you would normally use the club to get a feel for it.

Good luck.
