Re: Time Cafe?
« Reply #8 on: April 05, 2007, 11:31:27 PM »
We checked out Time Cafe tonight. 

First of all ... there's no food... (yet) so why do they call it a cafe?  They said that they're working on the food and that it will eventually be "American."  The plan is to serve breakfast, lunch and dinner (I wouldn't hold my breath at least for the breakfast part).

The atmosphere... it's really beautiful.  in a beautiful upscale lounge sort of way.  Recessed lighting.  Nice fixtures.  Recessed shelves that don't actually make sense to me b/c i don't know how they pull bottles from the back row out without knocking stuff off the front shelf. 

There's quite a few books on shelves across from the bar... not sure what the idea is... I mean it looks good - but it doesn't go with the upscale vibe.  I didn't see anyone browsing or reading.  It doesn't really seem like a plant yourself in the back and read a little Hunter Thompson for 5 hours kind of place.

Electronica music.  Soft... good. 

Two flat screen t.v.s by the bar....   maybe more that i didn't notice

price list
makers $7
Jamison $6
Guinness $6
Large Mojito... (fishbowl size that some folk mentioned) freshly muddled before your eyes... $8.50

bar snacks... pretzels and cheese puff balls.

Owners are Croatian and therefore I think a lot of the clientele is also. 

Our bartender... Artemis... was sweet and lovely. 

overall....
too upscale for my cup of tea.  definitely friendly ... worth checking out.  Hoping they get a real chef in the kitchen.

Went there this past Sunday at noon for brunch and had quite a pleasant time.  The kitchen is now open big time and offers a $12 brunch which includes a mimosa or bloody mary.  Not too crowded, attentive service and nice atmosphere.  I had mozarella toast with tomato, home fries and sausage.  Very tasty and well-presented.  A bloody mary before and coffee with a shot of Bailey's Irish Cream after helped me enjoy some excellent live jazz, played by the Jacob Varmus Quartet.  Brunch for two and two drinks came out to $33.00 plus tip.  I didn't want to leave!
