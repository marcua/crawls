There a few of us race fans scattered throughout the city, but I suspect that there is no money to made in showing races; I've been to a couple of bars over the past few years that advertised racing, and either the bar closed or they switched to showing something, anything, else.  My best luck has been rolling into a bar that is likely to be fairly empty on Sunday afternoon, and ask the bartender nicely if we can watch the race.  East Village dives have been amenable to this in the past.  But these days I like to watch at home; if one of those follow-the-leader, no-passing stretches comes up in the middle of the race, at, say, an aero-dependent cookie cutter, or if DW starts prattling on about some bull plop, then I can take a nap or at least turn down the TV and read the paper. 

But, if anyone does figure out a decent place in Astoria to watch the race, do share.  It'd be fun to have such a joint around.

On a related note, has anyone noticed the proliferation of Nascar gear among the young Latino and African-American demographic in the city?  I'd be surprised if they are Dale Jr or Elliot Sadler fans.  Is there any other significance to the trend?
