A Man Is Killed Execution-Style on a Street in Queens
By AL BAKER and DYLAN LOEB McCLAIN
Published: July 24, 2010
 
A 41-year-old man with a long arrest record was fatally shot, execution-style, just before dawn on Saturday on a street in Astoria, Queens, officials said.

Gunshots rang out on 28th Street, off Ditmars Boulevard, at about 5 a.m., startling neighbors, some outside for a morning walk and others who looked out of their windows to see the victim lying on the ground.

A man who lives on the block said he and his wife had been up for a few minutes when they heard the shots outside.

He said his wife went to the window and saw the silhouette of a man standing over another man. By the time the man in the house, who asked not to be identified because he feared for his safety, got dressed and went outside, the gunman was gone.

The body was on the ground by the rear wheel of a car belonging to the residentâ€™s father-in-law. Detectives later dusted the car for fingerprints, the resident said, and lifted footprints from blood on the pavement.

Investigators said the victim had been shot at least five times in the head. By the afternoon, there had been no arrests, and detectives from the 114th Precinct were still piecing together what had occurred.

Detectives said that they did not believe the victim was robbed, and that they were pursuing the possibility that the killing was drug-related.

Officials said the dead man, whom they did not immediately identify by name, was a resident of Long Island who had been arrested several times since 1987.
