Never give the cabbie too much information - they are strictly NEED TO KNOW, in my opinion.  They are not doing you a favor; you are paying them, so they need to go where ever you ask.

I used to live a couple of block north of Ditmars, in an area that a lot of drivers didn't seem familiar with.  So I would just tell them, "going to Astoria, take the upper level". After we were across the bridge, and already going up 21st Street I would then give them the rest of the directions.

I think I may have had one or two cabbies complain, but I've never gotten out of a cab because of it.  I have found that if you stand your ground when they try to kick you out, and make it known that you are a local and you know that what they are trying to do is illegal, they will just give up and drive you where you're going.

Hope this helps!

I've lived in Western Queens (first Sunnyside, now Astoria) for the last 12 years and EVERYTIME I get into a cab in Manhattan I hold my breath after I tell them where I want to go.  I haven't gotten a refusal lately but I always expect one.  One of the things I do now is after I get into the cab, I smile and greet them with, "Good Morning" (or evening or whatever).  I do this because I suspect that they experience very little civility and I imagine that that wears the soul down.  I think of it as a little bit of "pay it forward" decency.  I then say, "I'm going to Astoria, please, thank you so much" - in a really nice way.  At this point, the only conflict I run into is the chosen route.  If I'm downtown many cabbies want to take the Williamsburg and I've done the math and invariably it adds on at least $4 to my ride, so unless I know for a fact that the 59th is a parking lot I ALWAYS insist on it and 99 times out of a 100 it's fine.  Plus - when was the last time the BQE WASN'T a nightmare?  I got into such a bad fight with a cabbie a few weeks ago about the route I called the police because I felt endangered - I had jumped out of the cab at this point and he came after me - he was clearly crazy.

If I'm every refused again I will report it.  However, late at night I don't like the idea of pissing off a taxi driver if I'm riding alone.  Regardless, I usually have them drop me off a few houses away from where I live and I wait until they're out of sight before I go to mine.  There are many many creepy drivers out there.

  One of the most suspicious parts of your cabbie's behavior is that he told you he was off duty, and I believe you when you said you saw his taxi light on.  Given that many, if not most, cab garages are around the Western Queens/Brooklyn area, most cabs are more than happy to drive you into Astoria in the hopes of picking up one last fare on their way home.  Most cabs either turn in their cars to a garage, or go switch up with the driver for the next shift.  If he was really going off duty, he was probably heading your way, and he most likely wouldn't have had a problem taking you to Queens, especially Astoria.
  Next time, get all the necessary information. Medallion #, cabbie's name, date, time, location etc.  Trust me, a substantiated complaint is something medallion cab drivers don't want.  I have made complaints against cabs in the past, and I would not hesitate to do so again, if one refused to take me to Queens.
