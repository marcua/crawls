Hunter Mountain is fun and the nightlife in Hunter/Tannersville during the winter is surprisingly good too. Not sure if they have much snow right now, though.

Windham Mountain is not far from Hunter, so it should take about 2 hours as well. http://www.windhammountain.com/

When I was young, I used to go to this very small mountain in Connecticut called Mohawk Mountain. It would probably be good if you are just learning to snowboard. http://www.mohawkmtn.com/

Camelback is in the Poconos and was a lot of fun too. Stay off the mountain at a Days Inn if you are staying over night, it's a lot cheaper. http://www.skicamelback.com/

The problem with Hunter is that it is full of people who think it's a good idea to drink and ski, not a good place for beginners.

Windham is nice and usually not too crowded, esp if you get there early.

But if you planln on staying overnight anyway, I would suggest going to one of the bigger places in VT. I know Mt. Snow has a great ski school and some great learn to ski/board packages.

I warn you though, learning to snowboard was one of the most painful experience of my life (well, before the torn ACL). Buy volleyball kneepads and wear them!
