Title: Re: Ancestry.com - has anyone used it?
Post by: lanseaux on January 03, 2007, 08:52:06 PM
There's a lot of free genealogical data out there you should check through first -- a great place to start is Cyndi's List (http://www.cyndislist.com/).  Cyndi is a woman who has been cataloguing genealogical sites on the Internet for over 10 years now.  She also has a section on how to approach Ancestry.com (http://www.cyndislist.com/ancestry.htm) (that link is not to Ancestry.com, but to Cyndi's page about it).

From my experience, Ancestry.com does have some stuff that other sites don't, or that other sites also charge for, like certain census, birth/death, and ships' registries information.  But through careful searching you can either find what you need, or find the direction you need to go in before paying Ancestry.com anything.

Title: Re: Ancestry.com - has anyone used it?
Post by: Josh on January 04, 2007, 11:54:56 AM
I recently installed ancestry.com's software packages for my aunt and it's a very powerful tool.  She got the software and access to all the national databases for something around $100.  It offers an incredible amount of resources including some partners websites for helping to manage your family trees and all the information is readily available from a great search interface they built on top of the databases.  It could be overkill for a small project, but it was the best product we saw out there.  Make sure you're familiar with everything you're getting.  They do sell a few different packages bundled with a different depth and breadth of information, just make sure you understand all that you're getting and how to use it it could be cumbersome. 
We did have a problem initially installing it because there was a conflict with an older version, but their help line was actually very helpful and friendly.  They were even nice enough to point out that we had purchased a few items individually (the software and the database access) for more than it would cost for the bundle, so she was refunded the difference.
