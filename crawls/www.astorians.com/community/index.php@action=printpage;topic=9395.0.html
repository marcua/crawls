Do I have to bring envelopes with money? 

My ex's parents were OTB Hong Kong and worked very humble jobs, yet they insisted on giving us red envelopes of money every new year and taking us for tea* at Ocean Pearl.  We would then have to slip the money back to her mother, so she could then slip it to him, so he could have the honor of paying for the meal. 

It was odd because it was like everyone (including him) was in on the secret, but no one gave it away, because it just made everyone so much more comfortable.  My ol' gal from a million years ago was OTB Japan and that sort of thing happened in her family alot as well.  Its such a fascinating form of human interaction: A lie wherein there is mutual participation and understanding.  Kinda like the dance you have to do with a girl's pop:  Officially, he's the boss, but in reality he's not and the understanding arises that he gets honored as one without him acting like one.

Anyways, it is worth it to goto the one in Chinatown.  I haven't been to the one in Flushing yet.  Just don't eat starting at the end of this sentence if you are going to either one of them.  I might go, just because I'm a Rat!

* Most call it dim sum, but HK folk call it tea and it just stuck in my head.  To them, Dim Sum were quick dumplings you'd eat on the run or after a night out, akin to burgers at a diner.
