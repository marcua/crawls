Re: De Mole opening a second location in Astoria - 30th Ave & 43rd St
« Reply #8 on: April 30, 2012, 10:34:59 AM »
Went yesterday and was extremely impressed.  It's still very new.  Just painted and not fully decorated.  Liquor license not yet approved.  But the kitchen is going full blast and the food is spectacular.  The ceviche is incredible.  Rich and buttery from the avocado.  The scallops were fresh and delicious.  No rubbery squid.  Just a very rich and tasty ceviche with only bay scallops, onions, fresh herbs, and citrus.  The corn tamales with cheese are very good.  Hot chili peppers and red sauce down the middle gives them a really spicy bite.  The chicken enchiladas with mole sauce is really outstanding.  It's not one of those moles with a bunch of melted chocolate in it to appeal to Americans.  It's peppery and complex.  A hint of cocoa to round it out.  I loved it.  There were fish tacos as a special yesterday. Outstanding.  Simple, fresh fish and soft warm tortillas.  In my opinion, it's the best Mexican I've had anywhere in Astoria.  It think this place is going to be very popular. Especially after they add beer and sangria.  The waitress was super friendly and offered some very good advice about our choices.

« Reply #9 on: April 30, 2012, 02:44:26 PM »
TJT, you said it brother!  We went last week for dinner and loved it.  Yes the place is not finished yet, yes it looks a bit bare, yes it is not the cozy bistro setting found at their original Sunnyside location.  But it is delicious.  We brought in some beer from deli next door and settled in for a chill meal. 

Fish ceviche was in a corn tortilla bowl with finely diced red onions, tomato and avocado plus the usual herbs and lime juice.  Was a bit mild but so fresh and clean.  We loved it. 

Guacamole was a decent portion.  Butter smooth and fresh.  Served with paper bag of tortilla strips.  My son loved that part best.

We had two burritos.  Carne asada and the pork.  Both were full sized, rich and nicely balanced with a very creamy crema. 

Over all two thumbs up and an extra thumb for good prices.  Child friendly too.  We were there at 630 and it was mostly families.
