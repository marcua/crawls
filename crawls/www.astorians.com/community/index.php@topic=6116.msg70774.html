Quote from: mcdirk on May 12, 2007, 06:46:01 PM
I do have a question for the cyclists on the board - what is the protocol when passing a pedestrian on one of these shared passageways?  At least 10 bikes passed me and they were all easy to avoid, and they didn't have to slow down, so there was no problem, except for one guy who came right at me, and had some sort of winged object on his back that if I had not ducked, would have hit me in the face.  Is there any standard way to interact with pedestrians?

I rode to Randall's island yesterday and last weekend as well.  Love that new ramp!!

As far as passing - if you're going towards each other everyone should stay to the right.  If you're coming upon pedestrians or slower riders you should give warning ahead of time with a horn or bell if you have one and call out "on your left."  And pass on the left.  If the person is on the left side of the path "on your right" will do as well.  Though I think most people who aren't used to being passed by bikes generally get startled and can't figured out which way to move to get out of the way... so... it doesn't always work.  I slow WAY down when passing - unless the person is WAAy over to one side and I don't think they're going to suddenly move into my path.  Then you also have the problem of people walking (and riding) with headphones on - no one can hear the warnings.   

As a pedestrian I'd try to stay out of the middle of the path - but the riders really should be slowing down in such a narrow area. 

That new path cut the the travel time to my job down to 25 minutes.  I'm trying to get up the energy to start riding to work.  Maybe by June....

I think people don't go there because it's kind of seedy and run down. It would be really easy to clean up and turn it into an amusement park of sorts. Not the roller-coaster kind, but a good place for a family or couple to spend the day.
