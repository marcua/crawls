We will be looking for a large studio or normal/small 1 bedroom in the Queens area for my son who is going to college at Cooper Union by NYU.  We'll only have 11 days to look for a place, from July 12-23, so I know we'll need to get a broker.  I've been reading some of the posts of the good/bad brokers in the area, and that's helped, but I haven't seen anything about Dakota Realty. ( http://www.dakotarealty4u.com/rent.html )   They advertise on craigslist a lot and they seem to have good prices on their listings.  We're looking for somewhere up to 1400 a month, preferably with a laundry area.  I haven't seen anything good or bad about Dakota Realty here.  Does anyone have any experience with them?

Also, how is the roach problem?  Are roaches something that everyone has to deal with, or are there actually apartments around up there that don't have them.  I've read about the bedbugs there, and that's a bit scary in itself.  Since I'm not from NY, does anyone have any tips on how to avoid these two pests when apartment hunting. 

Thanks for your help!

Hello there - roaches and bedbugs are prevalent throughout this city.  I have no idea why people think they are exclusively an Astoria problem.  I lived in Manhattan for eight years and will not describe what sort of critters I encountered in the streets and the buildings!  Anyway, bedbugs like to hide in wood.  Get your son a brand new box spring and bring in an exterminator before he moves in.  If you travel, do not put your suitcase on your bed when you return.  Bedbugs travel with you if you encounter them in a hotel room.  Best of luck to you and your son.  This is a great area for him to live in.

in general though, in regards to roaches, i think it really is building to building. 2 of my manhattan apartments had roaches, but i have never had an issue in astoria. use your gut all always VIEW any apartment you are going you put a deposit on. (don't trust that "this one will look just like this one").

In regards to bedbugs, well thats a toss up. i would make sure he wraps his mattress BEFORE you move in, and go to a local hardware store and just as a precaution, spray "steri fab" over the floorboards and any wood paneling. It will kill anything on the surface on contact.

and fyi, for 1400 you should find a great 1br. If he just needs a studio you should be able to get down to 1100-1200. Personally look at as many places as you can. Astoria has some great apartments but if brokers sense you are desperate you won't see any of the good places, they'll just take you around to the stuff they can't unload on anyone else. My boyfriend and i were in the same predicament last summer, we have a dozen friends in astoria with great apartments yet all we were being shown was literally CRAP because we had to move out of our previous apartment ASAP. Finnally we saw a brand new building and jumped.

Good luck to you and your son.
