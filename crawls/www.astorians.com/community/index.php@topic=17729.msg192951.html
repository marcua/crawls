Jennifer,
thank-you for starting a great discussion.  I also share your frustration with parents sending their children to a parochial school.  I really can see why people do send their children to parochial schools but I've looked around and I am not convinced all parochial schools are better than some of the better public schools. 
To answer the questions about what schools are great, I must say a lot of schools in Astoria are very good.  Please review Insideschools.org and the board of ed website for details about schools in District 30.  Some of the better schools in Astoria are 122, 85, 2.  I think some other schools do not have the reputation of being fantastic schools but if you visit you'll find community schools that are working very hard to make a difference.   Do not go by test scores alone.  Try to talk to the parent teacher coordinator.  Talk to parents who have their kids there.  Ask questions and you'll learn a lot about your community school.  I think the other thing to note is that what is good for your child and family may not necessarily be good for someone else's child.  So it's a little unfair that judgement be passed around.  It is is highly personal decision and a hard one. 
Having said that I really wish more parents would send their children to their zoned schools.  My zoned school, for example, is a school that is trying really hard to be great school.  The administration is open and warm.  They listen to parents and want involvement at all levels.  Many families with higher resources in my neighborhood opt to send their children to parochial schools or send them to other schools in District 30 (it is possible to do with some work).  So I made the unfortunate decision to not send my child to our zone school because I did not feel like there were enough families with whom I could roll up my sleeves and work with to make it a fantastic school.
We are still trying to decide where to send our child.  We are deciding between two public schools and a parochial school.  It is a very hard decision and I have spent countless hours researching the options.  I am sure many parents do the same and we hope that all end with the best choice for their child.
