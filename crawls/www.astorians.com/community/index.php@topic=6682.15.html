I have mixed feelings about the ads. The thing is, I am happy to support the board as I think it's a valuable tool for the community and I don't mind giving a few dollars to it. However, as someone who did read the board but maybe wasn't terribly thorough about it, I didn't know there was a simple donation thing to become a member or whatever until I started getting ads shoved in my face in the middle of threads. That said, I might've donated before if it was made clearer that this was an option, without having to put these ads in place.

merm, maybe the board would be better off if there were just an obvious line of text at the top of the screen that said something like "Support Astorians.com and get more features" or something, and donating would get rid of that line at the top of the screen (along with the other benefits). That and the occasional ad at the top of the screen, which would also go away upon donating, seems like it would be better.

While the mid-thread ads did get me to donate simply because I never thought of it before, I think they do take away from the look and feel of the board, and I don't think people should have to put up with that if they don't give money to the site. Realistically, how many people actually click on those things, especially when they're not even specifically relevant to Astoria or Queens? The ads are ugly and obtrusive, and I can definitely identify with the people who don't like them while they're reading the board at work. Furthermore, some people don't like logging in at work, so they'll have these ads in the middle of threads whether they donated or not. This might actually drive some people away, and I don't think that's what you are going for.
