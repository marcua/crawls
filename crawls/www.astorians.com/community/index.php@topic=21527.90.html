Finally tried Butcher Bar today. We went to John Brown's first, but they were sold out of burnt ends, rib tips, and pork belly by the time we got there, so left and decided to try BB. Overall consensus, eh. We got a sampler of 5 meats from the carvery along with a side of mac and cheese. Burnt ends weren't really burnt - just really ends. The few pieces that did have that nice crisp char on the outside were really good, but the rest was just solidly good. Best meat of our sampler though. Next was the pork belly, which was also good, but not great. Unfortunately the rest of the meats were dry, dry, dry. We got the hot sausage, which was  (dry) and primarily heat with not a lot of flavor to balance it. The brisket was dry and had barely any smoked flavor. "Smoked" turkey was worst of all. No smoke flavor whatsoever and dry as dust. It was terrible. The cranberry sauce that went with it was way too sweet to be eaten, though the original bbq sauce did help redeem the brisket and turkey - or at least make them edible. Needing to put sauce on these meats was a shame - it's definitely a step down from Fette Sau in Brooklyn, where all of the meats are so good they stand on their own without any sauces whatsoever. Considering the price point to quality ratio we received at Butcher Bar, we won't be back. Here's hoping JB's will be better once we finally get over there when they have food to sell. Oh, and mac and cheese tasted like the Stouffer's mac and cheese that you get in the frozen food aisle, which, I can't lie, I do like, but wouldn't pay that much for at a restaurant.
