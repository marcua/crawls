Finally had a chance to try this place. Got a few different kinds of pizza for the wife and I to split, and an order of the garlic breadsticks. Pizza-wise, got a margherita, a vodka sicilian, and an artichoke. I got not even halfway through the margherita and was stuffed -- so the artichoke will be for lunch tomorrow. The breadsticks were good -- simple, but good.


Would I get it again? Absolutely.


Price-wise, four slices and the garlic sticks came out to $26, which, for traditional pizza would be pricey, but these $4 slices are massive -- wide and super thick and filling. The breadsticks were WAY overpriced, though -- $10 for what is basically pizza dough with garlic on it and a tub of sauce on the side. When garlic knots at any pizza joint run 4 for $1 or $2, and garlic bread runs about $2-$3, $10 for the garlic breadsticks were overpriced, and though good, can't justify spending the money on them.


So other than the price (which really should be posted on the menu board), I have two main complaints, neither of which have to do with the quality of the food: 1) the wait for food. They ran out of some types of pizza, and rather than have a new pie ready to go, they repeatedly were telling people that whatever it was they were out of would take 20 minutes. The garlic breadsticks, I was told, would also take 20 minutes. Now I love knowing my food is fresh, but 20 minutes to wait for anything other than a whole pie at a pizza joint is ridiculous, and it took closer to 40 minutes than 20. Had I not already paid, I may not have been so willing to wait because of... 2) the music. It was loud. I mean, so loud you couldn't hear the guys behind the counter, and they couldn't hear you place your order unless you YELLED it out. I would never stay in to eat because there's no way to have a conversation with your dining companion. And during my 40 minute wait, I kind of wandered around the place, thinking I was underneath a speaker or something, but no, it was loud everywhere -- my ears were ringing by the time I left. Really unacceptable. (And it was some of the lamest music the 1990s had to offer, so not only was it loud, it was bad and loud.)


Not sure what their delivery looks like or what their delivery zone is, but if they head to my part of the neighborhood, I would definitely do that and avoid the music.


That area is really saturated with pizza joints, so I kind of wish they had opened up south of Astoria Blvd. instead where we have a bunch of burger places but little good pizza joints that sell single slices.
