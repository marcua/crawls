Title: Central Park Zoo
Post by: nycatexas on September 30, 2006, 11:57:39 PM
We went to the Central Park Zoo today and saw some awesome animals. The polar bears were really cute, huge but cute; penguins were really fun to watch swimming then launching themselves back to the rocks. They have a pretty good Tropical Rain Forest exhibit with beautiful birds, an animal called a deer mouse (which is neither a deer nor mouse, very strange with pencil-like legs and is the smallest known hooved-animal), several species of tamarinds, snakes, those giant Madagascar Roaches (which I steered well clear of) and other interesting creatures. It was pretty busy so I'm looking forward to going back in the winter when there are fewer tourists/families (those strollers are a killer). The zoo is actually open 365 days a year and one of the Zoo employees was telling me about how playful the "snow" monkeys and polar bears are in the winter. She pointed out the giant (to me anyway) bats that hang from the ceiling - they have like a 3-foot wingspan! Freaky! They also have a glassed-in display for these tiny little bats with a tiny Rhinocerous-type horn growing out of their nose that helps them navigate. Had to feed the sheep/llamas/goats at the Children's Zoo - they are sooooo cute! It's hilarious to me though that they have a cow on display! And people were really excited about it!

Can't compare to big zoos but for a couple of hours it's a great place to visit. The walk through the park afterwards was great too. They are doing extensive restoration work at Bethesda Terrace and the outcome should be impressive. And, damn, I think some of those people around "Imagine" in Strawberry Fields are still tripping from acid they took in like 1976. I liked John Lennon too, but geez.....move ON!

We headed to one of my favorite places - Zabar's - to do some sampling and get a couple of things. Of course it was a nightmare there but well worth the trip. If you ever get a chance sample the smoked salmon pate - deeeelish! And since we were in the neighborhood we HAD to go to Gray's Papaya - yummy and cheap. It always comes back to food........
