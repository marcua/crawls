10 children and those living at  have endured no working lock to the front door. Complaints have been filed with the city and inspectors have come out and issued violations. I will take the doors off the front of the building. I can do whatever I want."  He made it clear that he was going to get her thrown out for calling 311. All this tenant has wanted (since she moved in late June) was a working lock on the front door. Other tenants have verbally requested the same. Their requests have gone unheard.

We need to do something about bad landlords. What is it going to take for the front door to have a working lock? How can this not be criminal? He is putting the children and adults at risk? How long will these tenants have to wait for their building to be secure? How long will the neighborhood have to wait?

From the HPD website, the complaint history for your building lists 10 complaints (with multiple problems cited within each) starting from September 2 -- all from one apartment, which I assume is you. I didn't see anything earlier than September 2 listed. The process needs to take its course. The HPD can either send out an inspector to confirm your complaints and issue a violation and additionally, if the landlord doesn't comply you have the right to take them to housing court. From the HPD website:

TENANTS: If your landlord does not correct the condition(s), you have the right to initiate a tenant action against him/her in Housing Court. After 113 days, a front door lock has been installed. All of us will sleep better. It took 7 calls to 311.Gov, 4 inspections, DHCR, Costa Constantinides office in particular Josefina Germosen, Queensborough President Melinda Katz and her team Gloria Scala, and Lisa Actons, Attorney Pedro Rivera of DHCR, Governor Cuomo's office and all of you to help make this happen.  Not all the violations have been fixed but the most important has.


I want to thank the Astoria Police Department 114 Precinct for helping to keep us safe over these last 113 days and coming to help us when the landlord was harassing us for calling 311 for assistance.


I have learned an enormous amount and will write a future post later this week.


Thanks to all of you for your advice and support. You helped enormously at moments when I thought I should run from this nightmare.
