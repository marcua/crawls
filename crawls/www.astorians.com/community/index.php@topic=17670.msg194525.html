I do not agree with the personal attacks on either side, or at individuals specifically. 

I am curious why JFP's camp (which I know you're part of MJM since I know you personally), has not responded to stings accusations.  I do not feel it is my place to inflame the situation, but I am curious why there has been no clarification.  If the accusations are truly without merit, they should be easily defended against. 

So rather than this being about JFP or AS, this has turned more into a supporter-vs-supporter attack session that is not helpful.

1. It is fact that JFP sent an email inviting folks to a debate between him and AS and "signed" Costa's name in the email.  Costa has posted on the board a rather direct comment addressing this, confirming that it was done, and saying that the premise of "debate" was incorrect and that JFP knew that AS would not be able to attend well in advance, despite the letter leading folks on to the contrary.  As of date, no response has been posted explaining this from JFP or a rep.

2. Sting alleges that JFP has moved into the district 5 or 6 months ago with the sole intent to run for the Assembly.  I am not sure how one could prove or disprove intention, but I think if JFP DID move into the district he should own up to it.  It is my understanding, as a friend of his, that he had moved in that time period, related to his wedding.  I cannot confirm or deny any special intent on why he moved to where he did within Astoria.  I can confirm that our Assembly district lines are very oddly shaped, that I recently moved, and that it took me some time to figure out which Assembly District I live in.  Some clarification from JFP would be helpful.

3. Sting alleges that JFP did not vote in some important elections and primaries.  JFP has not refuted this on the board.  Those who speak against sting's allegations focus more on why the voting record is public.  I would be curious why he did not vote in the presidential primary, especially with his public statements of support for Obama. 

4. I have raised concern that JFP sent an email blast about "lulu"s claiming that none of the folks in Albany deserved them, despite the good work that our Assembly did, especially Mike Gianaris (emphasis is mine).  He said in his blast that none of them deserved a bonus.  I have to disagree, as I think Mike Gianaris has done an excellent job as an Assemblyman, bringing Astoria from Onorato-style politics to a mainline democratic politics--at a time when it was not yet popular in this constituency.  He voted the right way on many issues in the past, but most notably he voted FOR marriage equality both times it came up last year.  He DID a good job, does a good job, and I believe will do an even better job as our Senator.  Blanket statements like JFP made in his blast are concerning.   I know that several folks on JFP's camp have expressed that they intend to support Gianaris personally and JFP personally.  I am curious if JFP is going to do the same by endorsing Gianaris even though he knows the endorsement is not returned.  My challenge is that the endorsement is altruistic and not a political ploy...will he endorse our presumed democratic Senatorial candidate before the primary?  Similarly, will he endorse Maloney who has also endorsed AS?  I am a big fan of Gianaris and Maloney (in addition to Simotas), and it is of interest that any Assembly candidate saying they're doing this out of good-will support those who have done great things for us already.

5. I am curious about petitioning.  It is very likely that ballot petitions will be including Maloney, Gianaris, and Simotas on one petition.  Will JFP be petitioning with opponents to Maloney and Gianaris?  Or will he have a petition that is only his, aware that anyone who signs the M/G/S petition would not be able to sign his and vice versa? 

6. Maloney took a major stand against the Credit Card businesses.  She helped pass one of the most important Acts protecting citizens from exploitation by the same.  As a result she has a Wall St. well-financed opponent challenging her.  What is JFP's opinion on this and will he stand by Maloney?

7. Sting has raised allegations regarding the ConEd settlement, offering documentation that JFP's website refers to it in a different manner than the official press release.  Though it is often semantics, several folks who were on the fence on this issue also admitted to this.  I've still not seen any explanation refuting the allegations of Sting other than attacks on his tone and presentation.  I was not part of either side on this issue, though I do see the merit in questioning the settlement, as $100 electric credit does seem like a hollow gesture, and I think there are more important issues facing Astoria than trees, especially if we may still have remaining problems that could cause another black-out.  Similarly related, a newspaper asked AS her opinion on the settlement, and she spoke positively of it but mentioned that Mt. Sinai could use some of that money to become a better facility in the area.  I received an email from JFPs camp accusing her of hijacking "his win".  I rolled my eyes, as it appeared they were clearly reading into the situation--and it made them appear like they were trying to be victims.  (That was early on in the campaign, and probably what initially led me to be a Simotas supporter, as I do not like feigned sensationalized victimization).

8. Several folks including the JFP camp mention "political insider" clearly referencing that politicians have already endorsed Simotas.  I offered defense of these politicians, especially Dromm and Van Bramer.  I have also spoken highly of Maloney, Gianaris, and Quinn for the work they have done, and the respect they deserve for it.  I have said I thin it is unfair and a misnomer to consider Aravella a political insider because of support by these progressive politicians.  The real question is if her politics conform to the progressive democratic party's platform.  And they do.  If JFPs do also, then a new argument should be made by him. 

9.  I am ashamed that anyone would compare the discussions on this board with Karl Rove tactics.  That again smells of victimization talk rather than rational discussion.  It makes mountains out of mole-hills.  Additionally, one should really be thinking, that after one of the two wins the primary, they still have to win the election, and a divided democratic base would hurt either candidate.  Additionally, in the long run, we have to be a community afterwards.  Accusing anyone of of Karl Rove tactics seems a bit out of line.  It is like comparing someone to Hitler when they may have done a few rotten things in their life.  Hyperbole, and exaggeration.  It does not bring sympathy to a side, and is said to be inflammatory.  NEITHER SIDE has engaged thus far in tactics that warrant being called Karl Rove-esque.

Now that I've spent 25 minutes ranting, can we actually discuss the issues at hand? I'd love some answers to some of the questions posed to JFP's side.  I also want to be clear, that though I am a supporter of Simotas, I am not connected to her campaign.  And I speak without any prompting by them.  I believe what I believe because of what I've observed, in this race, observed in my friendships with both AS and JFP, and how I've sen both perform in civic and marriage equality-related associations.  All things tallied up, I'm supporting Simotas.
