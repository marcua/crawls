Title: Re: Another CSA for Astoria (central/souther astoria) Meeting (Organizational)
Post by: mirki2 on October 18, 2006, 07:42:37 PM
Hey, for anyone that wasn't able to make it last night, here's a summary of our meeting:

1) Farmer Update:
Paula from Just Food updated us that the farmer serving the Hell Gate CSA is overextended and therefore declined adding us for delivery. She therefore contacted other farmer and the Golden Earthworm Farm was the farm that she found for us. Per Paula, the farm is certified organic. Also, working with another farm, whether that's Golden Earthworm or another Farm, means reverting back to a larger share (the traditional size share of most CSA's). The smaller size share of the Hell Gate CSA was designed orginally to meet the needs of lower income families but it ended up serving everybody. Also, the CSA grew significantly in size this year from last, from approx 40 people to 90 people this year.
Back to the larger share, it means a higher price per person, I think she said $475 for membership, but also larger and more varied share each week.
We should get more information about the share delivered at the Forest Hills CSA (who also get their shares from Golden Earthworm).

(2) Organizing and running a CSA:
We had a discussion with the Hellgate CSA about how they have run and organized their CSA. They indicated to us that at first they followed the suggested organization model presented by Just Foods but that roles were adjusted once the CSA was up and running. They also suggested that we should first focus on getting a core group together to run the CSA and build from there. Once we have a core group in place - we are currently three  - we can then concentrate on establishing a location and by February, begin signing people on. Since the number of people that get the Hellgate CSA newsletter is larger than the actual number of members, they don't think we'll have trouble getting people to sign up for getting shares. The interest is clearly there.

Next Steps:
Expanding our Core group to plan and organize the CSA
Location, Location, Location (we need to secure a location for share delivery/pick-up)

Next Meeting: TBA

In the meantime, to get involved, please please please add yourself to our e-list (info posted above by Jayme)

peace out,
Miri
