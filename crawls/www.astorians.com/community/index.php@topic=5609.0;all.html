I'm really surprised that I haven't seen anyone singing the praises of Michael Angelo's II pizzeria. They do super thin crust pizza - to your requested level of crispiness - and it is delicious. By far the best pizza I've yet had in Astoria, including Rizzo's. We had the Sophia Loren after we saw it praised on Joey in Astoria and saw the mouthwatering picture posted there. The gravy was light and flavorful, they use fresh basil they pick off of their own basil plant sitting in the kitchen, and great mozzarella as well. The chefs all obviously know what they are doing since the kitchen is open so you can see them at work, which I always enjoy. It also has a good atmosphere and doesn't feel like a typical pizza parlor. They serve regular entrees too, so you actually feel like you are sitting down at a real restaurant when you eat there rather than at places like Rizzo's, which are not conducive to a dining experience. Oh by the way, the regular entrees are very good as well - and HUGE portions. My veal was the size of my head the last time I went there. I gave away half of it to other people at my table and still had about twice the normal portion found at other restaurants. Very well cooked too, which was great. All in all, I definitely recommend a trip - it's not far at all from the Ditmars stop off the N/W line.
