Complaining about complaining...   my head spins!

I think complaining is a pretty useful activity.

Sometimes people complain about things and I realize that I'm guilty of the annoying behavior and I try to be more aware. Like noisy headphones. If no one ever griped about noisy headphones on astorians, I wouldn't think to check and make sure the volume isn't so loud it's disturbing those around me. There are a lot of little points of NY ettiquette that I didn't know about until someone on astorians complained.

Sometimes people complain and find that they are not alone in being disturbed about something - that's not a small thing. Sometimes just being able to say "I'm so pissed off" helps you let go of it and move on -until the next time, anyway. Sometimes you might learn about a solution that otherwise you would never have known about if you kept your mouth shut and assumed "this is just how it is."

I think people should be free to speak their minds about what's going on in their lives and how they feel about it - even if ultimately there's no solution except to try to live around it. A lot of things in life DO suck, and won't change. That doesn't mean that it's wrong to complain about them.

But sometimes complaining about things (sometimes for a very long time, to a lot of people) can help bring about change. And the truth is you just don't know until you open your mouth and start bitchin!

I think the issue for some people is that the "newbies" (or transplants or whatever term you prefer) sometimes seem to have an attitude of entitlement, that is that they seem to expect change simply based on their presence. While I'm not familiar with it being an issue in Astoria, I know there has been issues in some Brooklyn nabes (Williamsburg comes to mind) where there has been friction between the "natives" and the "newbies" regarding the public schools. Apparently the newcomers are very adamant about change and the attitude they have been getting has been "nobody gave a crap about these schools before you showed up, but now they have to?".

I don't think that even the most surly, miserable New Yorker, would prefer streets to be dirtier or less safe, there have been a lot of changes for the good. I believe the issue to be more about the way the changes are brought about and the people starting those changes. I mean since I live on 31st Street very close to the el the MTA should spend billions burying the subway so I don't have to hear it go by, right?
