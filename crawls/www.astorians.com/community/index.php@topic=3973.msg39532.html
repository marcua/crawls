Restaurants in Manhattan:

Vegetarian Paradise II (W. 4th at Broadway)
Candle Cafe (E. 70th and 3rd), not all vegan
Zen Palate (can't remember address)
Angelica's Kitchen (can't remember address)
Gobo (401 6th Ave)
Kate's Joint (4th Street and Ave B), can have dishes customized veg/vegan
Galaxy Diner (15th and Irving), bbq seitan, textured soy dishes
and Burrito Box, which has options for making your burrito vegan.

Of all the places I've listed, I've heard rave reviews about Candle Cafe, and I personally really liked Gobo.  I do not care for Zen Palate.  In Astoria, you can go to Fresh Start on 23rd Ave for good vegan deli takeout.  Baked goods often are wheat-free and vegan.

I'm vegan and have had good dishes at Wave Thai and Thai Elephant (both on 31st north of Ditmars).

As far as Manhattan goes my first recommendation would be Vegetarian's Paradise 2 (located off of Washington Square Park), followed by Red Bamboo (owned and operated by VP2 people - and just a couple doors away). Both are most excellent. I also like Zen Palate (Union Square, UWS, and I think one other location), but if you go there make sure you eat upstairs (or off their more expensive menu). The food is far better, albeit more expensive.

If you want to splurge on dessert a block and a half or so off of Union Square is a raw food take out place with a divine chocolate tart. It's about $10 a slice, but pretty amazing and totally worth it. They have a restaurant around the corner called Pure Food and Wine, but the take out place is called Pure Juice and Takeaway (126 E. 17th St.). I have also eaten at Quintessence, which is an expensive raw food restaurant. When we went it was about $40/person, of course we ordered several things as well.

Oh and Burritoville has great options for vegans! I love their Lost in Austin burrito - spinach and mushrooms. You can also substitute any of their dairy with soy items. Mmmm...

Good luck!
