Thanks for sharing the article.
 
One of my favorite diners in Manhattan, La Parisienne on 7th and 57th street, closed this year. It was just a diner, but I loved the neon sign and the name, which I thought meant "the Parisian" but later found out also referred to an ancient fresco painting in Crete.
 
The location was good and I often met friends there for a bite after work. We'd often try to think of somewhere new to go to but La Parisienne, which should be pronounced faux french style, was always our fallback.
 
This was the first time I really felt a kick about an old haunt closing. I'm not one to be nostalgic and try to look forward and find something new to try, but it's sad to me that nothing new replaces these types of "cheap and cheerful" places in the same way.
 
 I felt the same about one of my favorite pizza parlors, Stromboli, on University Place which also closed (it was in the same building as Bowlmor) to make way for another useless luxury condo conversion. At least Joe's Pizza on Bleecker Street and 6th is still going strong.
