KidMurdock, I don't see any reason to toss all your stuff or to move just yet. It sounds like all the bites you have had could have easily been mosquitoes - unless there was a week that you didn't really spend much time outside but still found yourself having bites? One unidentified bug on a box spring does not an infestation make.

I think the glue traps are a good idea. If you want to take an extra measure of caution, I would do the following:
- thoroughly caulk the apartment
- put mattress covers on your bed. you can get them at bed, bath and beyond, and they should zip up around your mattress and box spring (one for each). if your apartment does become infested, the mattress and box spring will be safe.
- ask to have your apartment inspected for bedbugs
- take notice of whether you get any bites when haven't been hanging out in any place where you could get mosquito bites.

Another test you can do is to change your bedding and put the used sheets in a plastic bag. Let it sit for a few days, then open it up and take quick sniff before the enclosed air disappates. If it's a sickenly sweet, yeasty smell, you may have bedbugs. BUT BE WARNED - this is not a foolproof test! When you are good and paranoid you will smell it everwhere.

I don't think it is necessary to get rid of all your furniture. It's tempting, when you have no idea where they are hiding and you are panicking, to just toss everything. But I don't think you need to do that.

At this point, you just need to find out whether you have bedbugs, and avoid getting them if you don't already have them.
