Re: Businesses handing out fliers.....
« Reply #17 on: November 29, 2007, 01:51:44 PM »
So how does this work, exactly?

Ban the business from handing out ads, but don't ban me for handing out political statements? Or ban anyone from handing out paper stuff because that's a waste. It only isn't a waste if, according to some board of govt. bureaucrats, it falls into a category of being valuable. I don't think I like where this is headed. I see this as a first amendment issue, first and foremost. Think about what you're unleashing when you get the state in the business of justifying the suppression of the dissemination of printed materials deemed to be "wasteful."

I say, deploy meter maids to spend an hour or two or three a day near the people handing out the ads. As soon as they see somebody drop the ad onto the ground, give a ticket to that polluter, a $50 fine.

Maybe fewer people would take the ads if they realized that just dropping them on the street is going to cost them big-time. Or, maybe they'll take the ads, evaluate them, and then properly dispose of them to avoid getting ticketed.

The reason the streets are littered is because there are no consequences for littering here. We have anti-littering laws with no teeth.

If you think a business should find another way of promoting itself, then you should give that business direct feedback. But asking the government to BAN handing out paper ads because you think it's a waste of paper, even though there certainly are people who will take those ads, which sometimes include coupons, and make good use of them, is a myopic solution.
