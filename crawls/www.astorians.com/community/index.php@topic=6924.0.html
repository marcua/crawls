Did you read the tip sheet provided by the court? I actually found it useful.  Since it is your word against the landlord's document everything you can to remove as much doubt as you can and avoid problems over who really said what and how things could be interpreted by each party.  Also, narrow your argument down to only what is really relevant to the issue, and explain it in the simplest terms possible.

My former roommate and I took our old landlord to small claims court. I'm not going to lie - it was a pain in the ass but we did get our money back. She withheld about $600 from our deposit after telling me on the phone that the apartment looked great and she would put our checks in the mail. Meantime, weeks go by and no checks. My former roommate, a bit of a difficult person to put it kindly, promptly got on the phone and left harrassing messages about our money. I firmly believe this is what caused the landlady to go crazy and not return the money.

So my old roommate (RM) threatens to get a lawyer and take her to small claims. All of a sudden the apartment was referred to as "trashed" with a broken window, moldy tub and shower, broken ceiling fans, and filthy kitchen. It was ridiculous - all lies. I personally scrubbed, on my hands and knees, the entire kitchen and bathroom. And I stood on a ladder to clean the ceiling fan. It was insane. Oh yeah - to top it off the landlord (LL) and our lawyer (RM's boyfriend) began a series of crazy phone wars with the landlord saying that everyone complained about us, our place was filthy and that my roommate cleaned more than I did. Then she made a comment about being in our apartment several times without us being present. Can you believe that s___?

So onto small claims - first date, she didn't appear and didn't send a lawyer. Second date she sent a lawyer who got schooled for not having her there. Third time she shows and gives a song and dance about having cancer and never received the court notices b/c she was getting chemo. The judge was pissed that we both had lawyers. She came with pictures and phony receipts. Pictures were of stuff my roommate threw in the trash. We ended up getting all of our money back. My advice - go for it, but be prepared that it won't be resolved quickly, and while we were lucky and she ended up sending us our checks shortly after the judgement, that is not usually the case. For us, it turned into a matter of not letting her get away with lying, even though is was only for a small amount of money.
