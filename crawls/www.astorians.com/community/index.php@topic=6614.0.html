Yay, my turn.

First off, I'm going to miss this show.  I don't think any ending would have made me happy, because I didn't want to see this series end ever.  But I didn't think it was so bad.  I read in the Sopranos edition of Vanity Fair that David Chase, the shows creator, had no intention of wrapping the whole thing up in a neat bow.  And he didn't.  There's a conceit that when a show ends that world the characters inhabited must end, like the idea that when you close your eyes the world disappears.  But thats not how things happen in real life.  Sure Sil and Bobby (poor Bobby) are gone but Tony lives on and his life goes on.  Carm's going back to the spec house business, Meadow's getting married and AJ may finally be on the right track, and Tony is probably going to be indicted.  Paulie is taking over the cursed crew (which I have always pointed out to my friends that capos of that crew always end badly) despite reservations.  New York got rid of Phil but whose gonna run it now?  We don't know the answer to any of these questions and probably never will.  Like a friend who moves far away and loses touch.

I liked the cat, does anyone else think its supposed to be the spirit of Chrissy?  And did anyone notice that one of the suspicious guys in the diner, the one who uses the bathroom, was wearing the same Members Only jacket as Eugene in his final episode?  Speaking of the final scene, here's what I think it means.  That look up, cut too black, tense moment is a reminder that this is Tony's life and always will be, a world of suspicion and deadly peril around every innocuous moment.  Because of what he does Tony, and his family, can never truly relax.  Chase was always good at showing us the bad side of being a mobster and not just the money and power.  To me this was his last reminder to us that being a mob boss isn't all its cracked up to be.  But the first thing I thought was that my girlfriend's cable went out.

Also I'm glad that Phil's face got mangled just like he did to Vito's, no open casket for him.  Karma... although I think that from a different show.
