operation soccer chopper
« on: March 29, 2007, 02:31:09 PM »
hello - this information comes from my friend's husband....  if you are interested in participating please let me know through the PM system.   (the balls do NOT need to be new, just in good condition.)



Hello everyone! I've been flying a lot around here and I've noticed that as we fly over most of the villages there are a bunch of people that love to play soccer and volleyball. Who would've thunk it...volleyball? I can see soccer being a popular sport since so many people play it all across the world, but volleyball? Seems strange.

A few weeks ago another helicopter from our battalion that's based out of a different airfield stopped by to drop off and wait for their passengers. While they were waiting, the crew walked up to the fence and chatted with the kids that live just outside the base. The kids kept asking for a ball to play with. They had a football that some soldier gave them but they have no idea what to do with a football much less try to throw that thing. Volleyballs and soccer balls are easy...you just hit them or kick them. Baseballs require too many other pieces of equipment (gloves, bats, bases and such). I won't even try to mention bowling. Can you imagine the trouble I'd get into if I dropped a bowling ball from my helicopter? Yeah, I mean you'd first have to have someone set up the pins first, then the ball would start veering off and it'll end up being a gutter ball. All the guys would laugh at me.

I hope that what I'm trying to do is something that makes "us" a little less intimidating from the air and more of a friend. I don't have much of an opportunity to land and interact with the local people. They only get to see us when we fly past and most of the kids try to throw rocks at us as we fly by. I'd like to try to change that image so that they are hoping that we'd drop something useful instead of fearing that we're there to cause harm.

During WWII, COL Gail Halverson was known as the "Candy Bomber". It would be neat to do something like that. Instead of candy, I think Operation Soccer Chopper would be a fun thing to do. I've talked to the other soldiers in my unit and they'll tell their family and friends about it and hopefully in a few weeks, we'll be dropping all sorts of volleyballs and soccer balls all over Afghanistan.

Rules: the balls are not to be labeled with any type of religious symbols or decoration. References to Jesus, Christ, God or other type of religious symbols or icons shouldn’t be made. This Operation will be a concerted effort to bring a sense of friendship to the local people from the citizens of the U.S. without any expectation other than fostering a sense of goodwill.

If you plan to participate, you may send the balls deflated. I will inflate them when they arrive and give them a swift kick to make sure that they all work.  .


The helicopter crew that has the volleyballs/soccer balls on board will first identify the area that they will want to drop the "gifts". Then they will slow their airspeed to a manageable level and descend to a safe altitude determined by the crew (about 100 feet) to let the balls fall vertically. They will pick an area clear of people but close enough so that the people on the ground can see what is going on. If the helicopter is going too fast, there is a chance that the balls will fly into the tail rotor and that would be very bad for the ball, the tail rotor and the person expecting a free volleyball/soccer ball.

Lastly, I plan to end the Operation at the end of October 2007 so there’s lots of time for everyone to participate.
