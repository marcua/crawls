Title: Cheese in Astoria?
Post by: lanseaux on August 27, 2005, 12:54:57 AM
Jonathan, like I mentioned at dinner, Rosario's doesn't have an incredibly wide selection, but I think what he does have shows his care in what he has selected.  My favourite is Corsignano.  It is indeed a pecorino toscano, and the name of the town where it comes from is now Pienza.

Oh, and the name of the bleu cheese I was thinking of is Mirableu.  Does that ring a bell?  My description of it shortly after I ate it was "it's a Spanish sheep's milk bleu cheese called Mirableu. Stunning. We've never tasted anything like it. Definitely a bleu cheese, but rich, dense and creamy beyond belief, nicely counterbalancing the sharpness that bleu can have."

I spoke with Rosario several times about whether he would be able to order it, but I never followed through.  He was willing to look into it, though - and may be willing to consider carrying other cheeses if you speak with him.

I should add that there is another shop with quite a number of cheeses on Ditmars between 31st and 33rd, next to the Dunkin Donuts.  I think it's called something like "Greek and Italian Delicatessen".  I haven't been in there in quite a few years, though.  About 10 years ago I was able to find both real Gruyere and Emmentaler there, although the owner was quite peeved that I insisted he cut from both wheels.

Title: Cheese in Astoria?
Post by: lanseaux on September 01, 2005, 10:37:49 AM
Rosario's is an Italian gourmet deli under the Ditmars train stop, across the street from the fire yesterday.  His cheese selection isn't huge, but it's carefully selected.  He focuses on Italian cheeses, but not necessarily the standards, although he has them, too.  I think it's the best offering of its kind in the Ditmars area.

They make their own mozzarella and fresh ricotta, both of which are very good.  They also have a good olive selection, including one incredible bright green, round olive that explodes with a salty buttery flavour.  I've never tasted anything like it anywhere else.

Plus an interesting selection of imported grocery items.
