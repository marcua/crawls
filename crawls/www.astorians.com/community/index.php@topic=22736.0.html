Re: Kaufman Astoria Studios closing of 36th Street goes forward
« Reply #6 on: May 21, 2012, 06:58:31 PM »
I'm not sure what else any company (tv/film production or not) can give back to a community except money in the cash registers at local businesses.   I would be happy to hear some suggestions (seriously, not being sarcastic). 



But there is more being spent on than just food:  local hardware stores, PC Richardson for electronics, lumber, paint, dry cleaning, art supplies, flowers, hotel rooms, etc.  And endless car services, every day.  I've frequently had to order 20-25 cars in a single call, so 2 car services accounts are necessary, sometimes 3 (Astoria-based, of course).  That is all just what is spent in the vicinity of the studio, you obviously have more vendors all over the city.


About that food though, it's not just a few people grabbing some sandwiches for lunch.  It's probably around $250/day, every day at whatever restaurant someone is in the mood for.  Plus $500/week to stock the kitchen.  If shooting is going late, there is dinner which would be $500+ to feed an entire crew.  150 tacos from Tulcingo on Broadway, for example.  $300 pastry spreads from Omonia.  The go-to for last minute, gigantic and delicious meals is Bel Air Diner, and they're amazing at it.  They knew us well, I could call and say I want to spend X amount for 70 people and they'd have a burger bar/Greek feast/Italian spread there within the hour.


I'm not trying to figure drop, but my point is there is a lot of money being spent locally and if Kaufman closed its doors tomorrow you would see a lot more businesses closing down as well.
