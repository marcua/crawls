Title: Dry Cleaning Factory near 39th Avenue Station
Post by: NickNSakai on December 09, 2005, 10:35:13 PM
Dry cleaning your suits/coats can be expensive, especially if you get it done at your neighborhood cleaner.  When I have time and have lots of suits to clean, this is where I go to.  I found this place when I used to moonlight as a limousine driver years ago.  Although I no longer drive, I still go to the factory to get my dry cleaning done whenever time allows.

The place has no name.  No sign.  Not even an address at the door.  Don't even expect it to be on Yellow Pages.  You won't find it.  It's just a factory.  So you just have to go find it.  It is approximately at 3730 30th Street, Long Island City, NY  11101, near 38th Avenue.  You can get off at either N,W train 39th Avenue or 36th Avenue station.  It is right across from Local 580 Training Facility, right next to limousine garage (yes, that's where I used to work for)

No worker speaks English (just some basic words... like "clean", "tomorrow", "what time", etc...)  Mostly either Spanish or Chinese (I believe owners are Chinese) so if you speak either language, you'll have an easier time.  

And it is cheap!  I recently had 3 of my suits ($5 x3) get done along with my winter coat ($5) and I only paid $20.  This would have been double if I had had them done at a neighborhood cleaner.  One set of suits (jacket and pants) can be cleaned at around $5.  

Supposedly, this is the place where many of neighborhood cleaners "outsource" their work,  My guess is that is why they're so cheap...  Plus most workers appear to be illegal.  I guess that's the reality of this country.  

They don't do fixing/tayloring.  Just cleaning.  Keep that in mind. They are open between 7am to 8pm everyday.  Good luck finding it... Actually it's not as hard as you would think.
