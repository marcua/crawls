There are 2 different issues here. 

The first is having ATV's in city parks.  That is illegal and shouldn't be condoned at all.  I don't even think Parks people should have them unless it's absolutely necessary - put them on horses or bicycles instead.  The parks are too small and used by too many people for ATV riders to use them safely.  They shouldn't even be ridden in the streets of NYC unless they're street legal and I don't know of many ATV's that are considered street legal.  Too many of these guys use their ATV's and bikes in and around Astoria Park like it's their own playground and the police don't seem to do anything about it when it's not only a quality of life issue, but a safety issue as well.  I've spoken to several people who have been buzzed by these guys going 25 miles an hour, screaming down the paths.  One of these days they're going to hurt someone and it's going to be bad.

The second issue is the use of ATV's outside the city and that's an entirely different conversation.  I do know that one of the big issues is the lack of legal areas for ATV riders to use  and the unwillingness of some groups to give them places to ride.  Maybe if there were more places that were legal, they'd be less likely to ride illegally on trails that are made for walking on.  Just a thought.  The same issue has plagued dog owners when people in the parks were  unwilling to make room for the dog owners.  Make the pie bigger, everyone is happy.

Re: ATV's in Astoria Park
« Reply #21 on: January 30, 2009, 12:01:51 AM »
Not when you consider the cost of ripping up the terrain. Hiking and biking are sustainable forms of recreation; ATVs and dirt bikes chew up the trails and require constant maintenance.

Peds and bikes can squeeze through trees just a handlebar's width, but when motorized vehicles come along, they take down the trees to make the path wider, for example.

ATVs aren't given the same access as hikers and mountain bikers because you can't stop them from abusing the priviledge. What PJ said, unfortunately, reflects the typical attitude of ATV owners: "what an amature, how do you get cought on that thing ?? alittle dip and dash and your gone!!!! im about to go to the pound and bid on that actually !!!!" They can't be caught b/c they outrun everybody.

Meanwhile, bicyclists are constantly reminded of how careful they need to be. If you hit the trails with this kind of attitude, you risk losing your rights, it's that simple. So IMBA's mission is to teach all bikers how to build sustainable trails, how to ride with less impact, how to deal with non-bikers, etc. Our goal is to build and maintain trails with the least impact to nature as possible.

Granted, there are the jerks who ruin it for everybody else, but even they can't damage the trail the way an ATV or dirt bike can. And EVERY ATV rider I've come across riding illegally on our bike trails has flat-out ignored me when I reminded them they didn't belong there. Funny how you never see them out there early on a Saturday morning with shovels and Pulaskis, fixing erosion damage or re-routing a fall-line trail.

Yeah I know I'm getting preachy. But the green space we have on L.I. is not only limited in size, but fragile. Why ATVs aren't allowed in more places off-island, I don't know- probably b/c they have no reason to obey the rules, and there's no way to stop them from tearing up land wherever they want.
