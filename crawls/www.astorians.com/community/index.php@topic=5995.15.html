Just spent some time working on my tub drain, choosing to treat the slow drain with white vinegar and baking soda.  I did two rounds of it, and things are definitely running quicker!  I poured in 1/2 c. baking soda and then 1/2 c. white vinegar, let it set for 15 minutes and then dumped boiling water down the drain to wash away the residue. Soooo much better than the draino stuff I've used before.  That stuff is so toxic, it scares me a little.  Much prefer vinegar/baking soda together.  I may have to do another round, but we'll see.

Tip from apartmenttherapy:

http://www.apartmenttherapy.com/ny/how-to/how-to-unclog-your-drain-without-toxins-020259

Quote
1. Get as much water out of the area as possible
2. Pour 1/2 cup of baking soda down the drain
3. Follow with 1 cup of HOT white vinegar
4. Wait 10 minutes or until it stops bubbling and wash with boiling water
5. Use a plunger if necessary

Slightly larger proportions than I've used before.
