barrister15, Avon Bakery was on the same block closer to the el. And you are so right about that 7-layer cake. It really had 7 layers; it melted in your mouth. They did my sweet 16 party cake in 7-layers. Norgaard was a made-up name. They were really Hungarian! Do you remember their strudel? OMG. They also did a chocolate kugelhopf to die for.

Do you remember Sokol's on Steinway Street? Or the two five and dime stores we had? Woolworth was one, the other I think was a McCrory's.

I went to PS 6, which is now the parking lot, and my girlfriends and I used to go once a week to Woolworth's for lunch. We always got the same thing: the BLT plate. It came with coleslaw, a slice of pickle, french fries and an orange soda. We each came with our 50 cents allowance and, after lunch, we used to pool the rest of our money and buy a pound of potato chips from the candy counter which they would put in a brown paper sack. My parents' would have killed me if they ever found out. I was raised in a Glatt Kosher home!

I'm probably talking long before your time. Hell, I am so old, I dated Moses in High School!
