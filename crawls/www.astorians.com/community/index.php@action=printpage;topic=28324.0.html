Title: "Gourmet" In The Regular Grocery Stores
Post by: Starwind51 on August 12, 2014, 12:24:26 PM
Of all the major chain grocery stores near where I live, for a long time there was only one that sold chocolate yogurt.  I don't think buyers of that product are a niche market, other than people who are willing to pay a lot for a little container of yogurt, but grocery stores will stock different branches of their stores with different items depending on their perception of the population.  The Trade Fair across the street from me typically sells only one Phillips frozen seafood product at a time.  Over the years they've sold a variety of Phillips products, but only one product at a time.  So, they believe there are people around here will pay a lot for a little seafood but they don't believe there are people around here who will buy more than one variety of it?  I found Droste cocoa powder in a Manhattan chain grocery store when I wasn't even looking for it, and there were plenty of boxes on the shelf, but around here not one major supermarket carries it.  And I bought a can of Bar Harbor overpriced Lobster Corn Chowder and a soft-pack container of Campbell's Thai Tomato Coconut Gourmet Bisque at Waldbaums in Bayside, but are there any chain grocery stores in this neighborhood that carry either?


I'm not advocating all the products I've mentioned.  Some, the Droste cocoa and the Campbell's Thai soup are very good, but when you want to go a little crazy in the grocery store aisles, isn't there a regular grocery store around here that sells all these things?
