Trendy areas will always attract foot traffic and 'outsiders', which is why 114th Precinct's been beefing up plain-clothes patrols and drive by's. What we're seeing now is a neighborhood undergoing some major growing pains from both the business/property owner and resident sides.

I think it's reasonable for residents to take precautions to protect their safety and property in an up-trending area. It's also reasonable for property owners to be aware enough to help manage these risks. For example, after a break-in on the ground floor my last landlord was cited for using and lying about a dummy security camera. And for not replacing a frequently broken main entrance lock.

There's only so much that renters can do to ensure their safety in a non-coop building, but blaming local businesses for bringing attention to the neighborhood is counterproductive. That is until these businesses crap all over on the community they're supposed to be a part of.
