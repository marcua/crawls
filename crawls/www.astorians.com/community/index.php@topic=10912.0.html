Hi Sarah-

Any minute you're going to get the "get out hipster" type post.  Please ignore.  There are plenty of artists, academics, and hip people all around Astoria and what makes Astoria so great is that they (we) are right on mixed up with the working class families, retirees, etc.  So, while we don't have a hip/arty enclave (thank god), we do have plenty of such wonderful folks.  I live in the Broadway/Steinway area and love it.  We're near both sets of trains (N/W and R/V), near Cafe Bar (a very cool place to hang), near Museum of the Moving Image (now closed for renovation), and walking distance to all that Astoria has to offer including Socrates Sculpture Park.  We're also a short bike ride, car ride or subway ride away from PS 1 in Long Island City.

Yeah, I don't think hip and artsy is really how I would describe any parts of Astoria. I've lived all over this area. Older people, families, and younger people tend to coexist. There are hip places (like Sparrow) but they're spread out. Astoria is also a bit far away from the hipper parts of Manhattan, so if that's a huge concern for you, you might want to think twice.

You'll pretty much always be within a 5 minute walk of multiple cafes, laundromats, etc. A lot of Astoria is very dense. Especially for what you're spending (if you get a 1 bedroom) you should expect access to every amenity. You should also expect to be quite close to the subway.

In addition to Ditmars where I am now.
I like the area around the Broadway subway stop. If you go East a few blocks, it's more leafy residential which I like.
Astoria Boulevard is an eyesore and it's hard to cross on foot--there isn't really a crosswalk. It's more like a highway than a street.

Anything east of 21st st is not somewhere I'd want to walk alone at night--not enough people around.

Definitely visit before you rent. When you visit, walk from your house to the subway station, don't let your broker drive you . If you have the time, walk it at night as well as during the day.

Good luck, and welcome to NYC!

Please do not be fooled by all the cafes, lounges, dive bars, coffee houses and restaurants of all types here in Astoria. Nor should you be put off by the invasion of the yuppies coming here in the last 10 or so years, or the growing vibrant population of the gay and lesbian population.

The growing arts community, galleries, PS1, outdoor sculpture parks, Noguchi Museum , art spaces and studios, community theatre, cultural activites should not be a factor about this at all.

The growth and expansion of the Astoria Studios and film production here is not to be considered hip or beneficial.

Historically the early founders set up a community gathering spot named Bohemian Hall with a beer garden gives no hint to Astoria's progressive working class roots.

That around the community, jazz greats - including Louis Armstrong chose to name this place home is just coincidental, as well as the fact that nearby Sunnyside was considered Greenwich Village's nursery, or Jackson Heights as well was an early planned urban community is purely coincidental also.

Close proximity to Manhattan, the LI beaches Fire Island and the Hamptons  are not necessarily essential for any hipster.

The fact that many in this progressive Cyber community debate and at times even bicker among themselves on all things political, commercial, or even who has the best sushi should not dissuade you. Nor the fact that young and old here have even been banned for stretching the boundaries of free speech.

Do not let any of this influence your decision making process. Whatever you do - please do not tell
others of your discovery of Astoria. Especially do not tell your real estate agents that you want to live in hip Astoria as they will take advantage of you and keep driving up real estate costs for the rest of us here.

Please do not accuse us of being hip as we are too, em  - dare I say hip to admit it.

Realtors use these to get people in the door. I think it's a good rule of thumb to use CL and other websites to get a feeling for the "low" end of the market.
