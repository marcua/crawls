Here's a quick rundown of the carts in Astoria:

The King of Falafel and Shwarma: Near the Broadway station, on Broin front of the supermarket.  Really good, and among my favorite carts in the city, and a finalist for last year's Vendy award. This isn't greek, and there's a ton of info here: http://www.thekingfalafel.com/

The Souvlaki Carts: Broadway and Steinway.  On any given day there are at least 3 souvlaki carts, and usually one chicken and rice cart, filling up all 4 corners. The cart in front of the Rite Aid also offers a decent gyro that I have drunkenly purchased more than a few times.

Steinway and 34th Ave Halal Carts: There's typically one in front of the furniture store (across from the salvation army), and I've seen one pop up in front of where blockbuster used to be.  The one across from the SA is pretty good, and definitely different from most of the other halal carts.

Steinway and 28th Ave Halal Cart: Pretty standard fare, this guy and his customers seem to think his hot sauce is hotter than it is.  I like this cart, but if it were in Midtown it wouldn't stand out.

Other notable NYC carts:
53rd and 6th Ave: The grandaddy of halal carts in NYC.  They have a discreet cart there during the day, but at night, you can bet on a line halfway down the block by 8PM.  This is the chicken and rice that's so good that someone got stabbed for cutting in line.  Personally, I wouldn't stab someone over it, but it's worth the wait, just look for the yellow bags. www.53rdand6th.com

Sammy's Halal Jackson Heights: Without a doubt, my favorite cart in the 5 boros.  He won the Vendy's a few years ago, and you can find some information here: http://streetvendor.org/public_html/staticpages/index.php?page=2006101300295585  Sammy has begun opening satellite carts in Manhattan, but I find that his cart in JH tops them all.  Very, very good.

My 2 cents...

I work in midtown and eat from a street cart on average 3 times a week.
I always get lamb over rice, because it is so amazing, so my opinions are based on this one item.

King of Falafel is quite possibly the worst Lamb and Rice I have had. Its possible I just hit him on a bad day when the lamb was dry and flavorless, but he charged $7 for it which is too much for a rice plate.

That is the extent of my Astoria street cart experience. In Midtown however, my all time favorite lamb and rice is from Sarah's Halal on the north west corner of 45th and 6th. Only $5 and it tastes amazing. They also have this slightly sweet hot sauce which is fantastic. My almost all time favorite lamb and rice is from a cart on the north east corner of 7th and 40th. $4.50 for a gigantic portion of lamb on some slightly greasy rice.

I know the cart you speak of on 7th and 40th.  When I first started working in Midtown it used to be run (or at least it had a sign on it) by someone named Mr Shadaq.  It has since changed hands, and unfortunately isn't quite as good (but I still eat there once a week, because it's close). 
As for The King, he uses real lamb, sliced and put on a spit, as opposed to the big cylinders of meat that most carts use.  The few times I've been there it hasn't been dry, but I can imagine if he hasn't cut any off the spit in a while, it would get dry quickly. 
If you're in Midtown I would also recommend Kwik Meal (45th and 6th).  Another Vendy finalist, their chicken / lamb and rice is fresh and light and relatively greaseless compared to most other midtown carts.  It's a nice change of pace, for sure.
