I'm surprised no one has mentioned Gabriel Garcia Marquez.

One Hundred Years of Solitude is one of the most moving, comprehensive, well-written peices I've ever read. It's a heafty book and requires a pretty good commitment, but unlike the Russian Literature I love, it's perfect for this weather and this time of year. Reading about South America in the summer just fits really well. If Hundred Years is too big, Love in the Time of Cholera is equally amazing. These are two of the best books that have ever been written--in my opinion and by countless lists and authors. The surrealism, the way he makes all of his characters--the men, the women, the children, the banana trees--strong and weak, naive and weathered, is phenominal.

I just cannot speak highly enough of Marquez. If you've never read his work, you really really need to.
