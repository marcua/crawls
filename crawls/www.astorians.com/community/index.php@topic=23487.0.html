It just opened.  It's very "Brooklyn" in many ways: artisanal crackers & condiments and fancy cheeses, salumi, and bottled beers and soda.  They also have a selection of cheeses by the pound.  There's seating at the bar and at tables near the front, and on the back wall behind the counter they have about 8 or so taps, with pints going for $5-8.  It looks like you can get cheese plates too.  It's still very much a "work in progress" (problems with the scales, prices on beers on the shelf but not on corresponding bottles in the refrigerator, etc.)  The two physical spaces of the beer/cheese shop and wine store are not connected, probably because shops can't sell beer & wine/spirits from the same space in NYC.

« Reply #5 on: October 01, 2012, 06:32:27 PM »
I stopped by on Saturday and I have to say I'm a little disappointed with the direction they went in with the beer.  The only way I've been able to rationalize the (relatively high) prices is to compare this place to a bar rather than a supermarket.  Most, if not all, of the beers they sell are sold on a per bottle basis, not as a 6-pack.  As such, expect to pay (sometimes quite a bit) more than you might pay if you saw the same beer in a 6-pack in a supermarket (Bravo on 34th Avenue, for instance).  I also was sort of hoping they'd have different prices for the beer depending on whether you were staying with the bottle (slightly higher cost) or going (slightly lower cost).  It would also be nice to have some reference guide (a book or QR codes or something) to the beers they sell, as the selection is quite overwhelming.

With that said, the cheese looked great and I don't begrudge those prices at all.  The condiments were a bit on the hipster side ($11 pickles, $5 relish, etc.), but if you want McClure's pickles you can now go here to get them and factor the time you'll save into the price.

And notwithstanding my previous comments about the beers, their draft selection/prices were right on the mark so that might be the best way to go.

« Reply #6 on: October 21, 2012, 08:24:11 AM »
We stopped in yesterday, and while I can't quite figure out what a hipster condiment is - a moustached mustard, mayhaps? I tend to agree with True Blue's take on the place. That said, I absolutely loved it. We both had a beer flight of stuff that I knew about, but hadn't seen (DFH's Bitches Brew, for example) anywhere else, and got to sample 4 beers for $10. The staff was super friendly, and helpful and we felt really welcome while we were there which was awesome (we're prolly hipsters, though, whatever that means).
That said, I will echo the price remarks. A lot of the bottled offerings you can get at Triboro Beverage for less on a per-bottle basis if you buy a 4 or 6 pack, and I felt that the meats could be had at Sorriso's for less per pound (I don't buy cheese like I used to, so I can't really speak to that aspect but Sorriso's also has a damn fine cheese selection). All of that said, if you want to go out and enjoy yourself, and try some beers that you're not going to find at the pub on the corner (unless your pub is Sunswick) this place is great. All in all, I think it's a great addition, and I can see myself getting off at the Broadway stop for a flight once in a while.
