GAHS & Forgotten NY Walking Tour – TRINITY CHURCH AND WALL STREET
Saturday, June 2, 2012 at 12:00 noon
 
Tour NYC’s oldest cemetery at Trinity Church, walk on Wall Street to the water’s edge, then south to the Ferry Terminal, describing historic sites and buildings along the way. (Why is there a plaque showing the state of Ohio at Federal Hall? Find out in this tour.) Meet just inside the Trinity Cemetery fence at Broadway and Wall Streets.  Two hours.  Fee:  $15.00 to GAHS members, $20.00 non-members
 
 
GAHS & Forgotten NY Walking Tour – MADISON SQUARE PARK, 5th AVENUE
Saturday, June 10, 2012 at 12:00 noon
 
Who were Roscoe Conkling and William Seward? This tour will fill you in, as well as other semi-obscure American political and military notables, in Madison Square Park. We’ll also take a walk down Fifth Avenue to 14th Street and look at some of NYC’s most beautiful architecture, including the Flatiron Building. Meet at the NE corner of Fifth Avenue and 23rd Street, Madison Square at William Seward statue. Take the R/N train to 23rd Street. The tour will be about two hours.  Fee:  $15.00 to GAHS members, $20.00 non-members
 
 
Greater Astoria Historical Society Walking Tour - OLD ASTORIA VILLAGE
Saturday, June 23, 2012 at 11:00 am
 
Walk from the former mouth of Sunswick Creek at Hallet’s Cove, through the streets of Astoria Village, to Pot Cove overlooking the Hell Gate.  See remnants of the antebellum community and the great hill where scores of magnificent houses still exist.  There are a couple of hills and some rough terrain and uneven sidewalks.  Meet at Socrates Sculpture Park main gate at Vernon Blvd. and Broadway. Tour ends at Astoria Park’s south end. Tour is about three hours.  Fee: $10.00 for GAHS, Astoria Park Alliance and Green Shores NYC members, $15.00 for non-members
