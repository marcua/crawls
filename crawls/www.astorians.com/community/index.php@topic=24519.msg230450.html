Mer, what was it like adopting your cat from the Humane Society?  We adopted our cat from the North Shore Animal League many years ago when they had brought some cats over to Petco in Manhattan for an adoption event.  They checked two references and we had to pay about $100 donation fee I think.  Is it pretty similar at the Humane Society?  We have used their veterinary clinic and had good experiences - they really seem to just do what is necessary and not try to get you to do expensive procedures that your pet doesn't need.

Yeah, it was a very similar process.  They didn't ask for any references from me, but that was because I adopted with my reference.  I went to the Humane Society with my boss and friend when she went to adopt two cats.  The Humane Society already knew her because she's a big donor and volunteer.  The Humane Society folks decided that she alone was all I needed a reference.  And yes, I paid $100 donation.

I have to say, the experience was such a great one.  The staff spent a lot of time with me finding the right fit.  At the time, I wanted a single cat, but one that was ok being a lone cat.  Because the staff and volunteers know their cats so well, they were able to identify which cat had the personality that fit best.  And they were absolutely right.  My cat liked (likes) to play by herself, but also loves her humans.

If/when we get another cat (or two) we'll definitely go back to the Humane Society.

Count me in as another person who accidentally mistook this thread as being about the Animal Clinic of LIC on 36th Ave.


And I have to second the good experiences there, especially since their actual Yelp reviews are mixed. My 5 year old cat had a serious medical issue recently, and while I can't say I was 100% impressed with the experience (we had to take a side trip to the emergency vet in Forest Hills, who did some very expensive imaging scans and finally ID'ed the actual problem), I felt like they cared and were doing the best they could. I live very close by, and nothing beats being able to rush your beloved pet in for emergency surgery on 15 minutes notice, which happened.


Their prices were reasonable and they dealt with us outside of normal business hours more than once throughout the ordeal. They were also helpful in helping me troubleshoot the pilling process during his recovery, and did some necessary followups (like a quick exam when he somehow managed to bust a couple stitches) free of charge, which was a relief after the high cost of the whole crisis.


I do wish their exam room walls went all the way up to the ceiling. I had to listen to one person being told they should put their cat down, and another family watching their dog be put down, on two separate visits. Very sad for them, and emotionally difficult to hear when we were dealing with a life-threatening crisis ourselves.


So in short, are they the most amazing vets in the city? Probably not, but we will definitely continue to go there in the future, and I hope they're around for a long time to come.
