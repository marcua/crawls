comments:

  While I've been baulking at playing Antimeme Radio because it is not technically a technological show, I felt I could hold out no longer. The creator is a great guy who resides in Phoenix, Arizona whom I personally admire and is even a member and contributor to our humble forums here in Astoria. Don't let the cultural content of the episodes deceive you, XlogicX has a strong background in technology and is well respected in the hacking scene. I feel he wanted to do something different, so every month for 26 minutes there appears a show in which content is king and b.s. & dead air is kept to a minimum if not outright eliminated. The shows that really grabbed me were the shows about logic (reflecting his namesake)... which does btw have profound technological implications... but has more important value IMHO when it comes to hacking wetware, in other words, in how we think.

  So if you missed today's brunch or found the information difficult to absorb during the first run through, go home and listen to them again... yes all 26 minutes of it!, and tell me that before you did so you weren't seriously deprived. The tools you will be exposed to just may save your life one day or if you happen to work in the Executive Branch of the U.S. Government may even prevent a holocaust. Then the next step is to write him a letter (he even read one by me!) and tell him what you think. He welcomes intelligent criticism as well as positive feedback, and spatial distances nonwithstanding, he is a part of this Community, so go ahead, show him your support as I have repeatedly suggested when it comes to all Free Radio.

Tech Brunch Update -Tech Brunch is on Hiatus-
« Reply #46 on: March 20, 2006, 01:49:14 AM »
Fellow Astorians,

It is with great sorrow to announce that our fledgling Tech Brunch's evolution must go on temporary hiatus. The reason for this interruptus comes from the Realm of the Mundane and concerns a little thing which I like to refer to (euphemistically)as workenslavement whose ultimate aim is none other than the pursuit of bio-survival tickets which can just about purchase an empty existence in a shallow society.

Let us remember the proposition that all true "progress", technological or otherwise takes root and blooms in a "leisure society" and since leisure societies until now are founded on the exploitation of those who find themselves unfortunate enough not to own oil companies, it is here where I must do my part. And while I am I will continue to dream of a sustainable agriculture, a Land where the fruits of labor are equal to or greater than the time of labor. :-)
  Before you scoff... there is evidence in the field of Anthropology that some such cultures have indeed existed (until recently, that is).

If Fortune is ... well er.. fortunate towards me and the other consistent attendees of our hour, then later on in the year Fate will find this thing not only more organized, informative and entertaining... but perhaps even better executed.

I've learned alot from this little venture and in contradistinction to one valuable friends comment that I  also "have a life", I must respond that things such as these which are not tied solely to amorous relationships or work related survival stuff are in fact the better aspects of our lives. In other words... the things which we enjoy doing... not have to do.

In the mean time... there are plenty of shows to relisten to, and far far many more to be exposed to as well as many many words yet to be defined, mulled over and eventually grasped, i.e. assimilated into our very living organic brain tissue as well as countless questions to be asked if not ultimately answered. So, if you are rich and can afford barrels of audio tapes at Barnes & Nobles because the public library can't, or you happen to like commercial radio or tv where there are more ads than actual content... then more power to you. Just remember that there is a universe of free information out there; "free", as in no fee needs to be attached to measure it's value and Free as in, you are free to say and listen to what you want without censure. But then again maybe this term itself needs to be "redefined".

Good luck! and hope to see you all again real soon!

-Zeno

P.S.: If any of you has any ideas on how to make Technological Brunch more better (or even more logical) in the future, please don't hesitate to email me at zeno63217@gmail.com  All ideas or criticisms will be welcome.

Note: Meesh gratefully agreed to keep the hour going provided that I select and burn the show to CD. I thought I could do this... but my schedule is such that all things not immediately linked to my immediate survival must for the moment be kept out of focus. Thank you for your understanding.
