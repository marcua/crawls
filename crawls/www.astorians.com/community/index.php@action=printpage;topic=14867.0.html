Title: Queens Juror Qualification Questionnaire
Post by: dullnboring on May 04, 2009, 07:59:09 PM
I came back from work today only to see a very serious looking envelope with the words "Juror Questionnaire" stamped on the front.  My heart sunk when I opened it and saw that tis true, tis an official Juror Qualificiation Questionnaire courtesy of Queens County.  Gulp. 

Now let me just say that in theory, I have no problems doing jury duty.  In fact, having never been called to do it before, I'm actually curious to do it.  I'm just one of those people.  However, the reason why my heart sank upon receiving this is the timing.  I have a three week trip scheduled to India that I have been planning for over a year.  The trip is from June 23rd - July 11th.  I am really really worried now that potential jury time may interfere with said trip.

So I have many questions about this now.

Firstly, while this form stresses it is not a summons and that I am not required to appear for service, am I right to assume that after filling this out, that since I would likely qualify as a juror (US citizen, Queens resident, over 18 years, speak English fluently, no criminal record, never served jury duty before), I will in fact be called to come in for jury duty?  I'm hoping that this form is sent to all new Queens residents (I have been here for 1.5 years, but with this most recent tax season, it's the first time there's been an official record of it) for their own recordkeeping but somehow I doubt that. 

I have ten days to turn this form in and I'm really worried now about the timing.  How can you get out of this?  How long after I turn this form in can I expect a response one way or the other?  What happens if I don't respond?  Can you give the court an IOU of sorts and postpone service for another time?  And is non-refundable airfare sufficient cause for dismissal from a jury?

Post by: snowball119 on May 04, 2009, 09:37:38 PM
I got one of those questionnaire notices last summer and after ignoring it twice, they sent me the same questionnaire labeled with some sort of "bad juror" language in February.  So, this time, I sent it in and got selected as a telephone juror in April.  On the notice, they said that it was possible to reschedule your jury service at least a week from the date you are required to call in, through mail, online and phone.  You have to reschedule your jury service dates within 6 months of the notice date.

There are the LIC, Kew Gardens and Jamaica courts.  I got selected for Jamaica and I too was worried about my upcoming trip but they were very accommodating.  Vacation/Travel is one of the issues you can talk to them about for rescheduling.  Ultimately, I was just called for one day, so the trip became a non-issue.

Title: Re: Queens Juror Qualification Questionnaire
Post by: Bartow Street on May 07, 2009, 04:02:23 PM
Queens Criminal Court in Kew Gardens treats potential jurors like criminals. The central jury room accommodates about 1,000 people with seating. The problem is that they call 1,200 people or so every Monday and Tuesday for duty. People stand for hours. They act like the people called in are under arrest i.e. you can't leave the building, get coffee or simply get some air.

I don't recommend driving there and planning to park in the municipal lot either. They won't let you leave to add quarters. If one is called to report there, I recommend bringing something  to drink and read.

The two cases that I was interviewed for were attempted murder cases. During breaks in the corridors, juror intimidation was overt by acquaintances of the defendant(s.) Thankfully I was disqualified from both juries.

When you call they'll read ranges of juror numbers and for each one tell you 1) if that range of numbers has to report and 2) where to report. As far as where, the odds definitely favor ending up at the main courthouse out in Jamaica: more cases to try = greater need for jurors.
