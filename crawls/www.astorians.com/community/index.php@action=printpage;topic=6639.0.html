I love Boston, lived in Boston, visit Boston, etc.

The MFA there has the largest exhibition of Ed Hopper's stuff ever put together. I will def be going to that.

My two favorite bars in Boston:

http://boston.citysearch.com/profile/4742908/allston_ma/sunset_grill_tap.html

http://boston.citysearch.com/profile/4728127/boston_ma/bukowski_s_tavern.html

Bukowski's does awesome burgers and dogs and at great prices after midnight. The Sunset's food is mediocre to okay, but if you want some good eats on Newberry St there's always the Trident, the restaurant I worked at in college:

http://boston.citysearch.com/profile/40860182/boston_ma/trident_booksellers_cafe.html



Sunday nights this place has the awesomest show--
For $5, it's a poetry night with a jazz band that improvs with the poets. If you're brave enough, you can perform there too. Good band, good people, good beer.

http://boston.citysearch.com/profile/4745814/cambridge_ma/lizard_lounge.html


These two restaurants are a bit off the beaten path (think going to Arthur Ave. for a deli-30min on the train to goooooood eats) but worth it.

Red Bones BBQ: (lunch or dinner)
http://boston.citysearch.com/profile/4751824/somerville_ma/redbones_barbecue.html

Renee's (best. breakfast. eva.)
http://boston.citysearch.com/profile/4751809/somerville_ma/renee_s_cafe.html

Those are both in Davis Sq in Somerville. That area is really cool and has a great vibe. Davis Sq also has a JP Licks and it is a sin to go to Boston and not have JP Licks ice cream. They are, by far, the best ice cream I've ever had. (Coldstone is gross compared to this stuff). There are several locations around the city.  This is the one right downtown, near the Trident. There's also one in Davis by Redbone's.

http://boston.citysearch.com/profile/4728088/boston_ma/j_p_licks.html


One of the best things to do in Boston is go wander around the North End. There are only three things that I would say Boston does unequivocally better than NYC: 1) Beer, 2) Baseball & 3) The Italian 'hood.  The North End wallops Little Italy in every sense imaginable. Go there. Walk around. Get a canoli at Mike's and espresso at Gio's. There are some great cigar shops, old men love to lounge about outside and they speak Italian when they do. Hanover Street is *the* street to be on in Boston. Here's a map of the North End.

http://www.northendboston.com/art/Parking.gif

Any other questions, send 'em my way!

Title: Re: Boston: what's to see?
Post by: heff on June 12, 2007, 11:19:06 AM
I spent a weekend in Boston once, it was a lot of fun.  If you like walking around, historic sites and not spending a lot of money, let me recommend the Freedom Trail to you.  Its a red line painted or sometimes a red brick line along the the sidewalk that runs continuously through the sity to all sorts of historic sites.  I did it with a friend when I went, took us most of the day, she wasn't into going into all the individual locations so much.  But we did see the Old North Church, famous for the one if by land etc etc, Faneuil Hall, the Bunker Hill Memorial, site of the Boston Massacre, the USS Constitution aka Old Ironsides and a bunch of other sites.  Takes you through a lot of Boston's neighborhoods too.  You can look up the Freedom Train here (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_Trail).

Have fun and avoid driving in the city if you can, its a mess.
