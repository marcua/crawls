Heather Mills adds a spark to 'Dancing with the Stars’
By GARY STRAUSS, USA TODAY
Posted Monday, March 19, 2007 at 1:31 pm

The fourth season of Dancing With the Stars (ABC, tonight, 8 p.m.) boasts an undeniably diverse cast. But the Heather factor may be the hit competition’s biggest attraction ever.

Many viewers probably will tune in to see Heather Mills — vilified in the worldwide press over her nasty divorce from beloved ex-Beatle Paul McCartney — try to burnish a gold-digging image. Others will be driven by morbid curiosity.

“What I want to do is show that you can get out there and do anything with an artificial leg,” Mills, an amputee, told ABC’s Good Morning America.

Online gaming site bodog.com is taking bets on her prosthetic leg flying off during competition.

“This is getting an extraordinary amount of attention,” says public relations guru Robbie Vorhaus of Vorhaus & Co. “This should be great for ABC and for her. Even if she falls on her face, people will see that she has a lot of guts.”

Mills sees it as a chance to spotlight personal causes, including animal rights and land-mine awareness. She declines interview requests that don’t include assurances the divorce won’t be discussed. “This woman has gone through so much crap,” says Mills’ publicist, Michele Elyzabeth. “She has been done so wrongly by the press.”

Choreographer Carrie Ann Inaba, one of Dancing’s three judges, says she’s unsure whether viewers — whose votes count for 50 percent of dancers’ scores — will embrace Mills. “Will they look at her just like the other competitors? Will they judge her from what they know about her, or what they get to know about her on the show?”

Inaba and fellow judges have yet to talk about how to gauge Mills. “We don’t want to treat her differently,” Inaba says. “I don’t think that would be fair. I don’t think (Mills) wants that, either.”

Still, Mills faces challenges with demanding Latin-style dances. “This type of dancing is difficult,” Inaba says. “It’ll be even harder for her. But people with disabilities know how to work hard. She’s used to dealing with challenges. Some celebrities aren’t.”

Dancing already is a proven draw: It averaged nearly 21 million viewers for last season’s performances and 27.5 million for the finale Nov. 15.

Mills’ Dancing rivals are also viewer lures: talk- show host Leeza Gibbons, ex-Miss USA Shandi Finnessey, model Paulina Porizkova, Olympic gold medalist Apolo Anton Ohno, former NBA great Clyde Drexler, country star Billy Ray Cyrus, ex-Cheers barfly John Ratzenberger, former Beverly Hills, 90210 star Ian Ziering, ’N Sync’s Joey Fatone and boxer Laila Ali, daughter of Muhammad Ali.

“It’s a great group that cuts across every aspect of entertainment,” says Larry Novenstern, who oversees ad buying for Optimedia. Adds Barry Lowenthal of marketing strategy firm The Media Kitchen, “This show has a lot of mismatched bodies that make for interesting entertainment.”
