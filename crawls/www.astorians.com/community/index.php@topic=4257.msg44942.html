Quote from: Alexa on December 22, 2006, 06:38:50 PM
I never thought about it until I read this, but is this is violation of privacy? If it is on display for all to see, then isn't it public? I don't feel one way or another about it, but as far as the law goes, I believe your privacy is related to situations when you "reasonably" expect privacy...like in a restroom or port-o-potty...I don't think the front yard falls under this...

As a photographer, I can say that its completely within your rights to take a photo of someone's house (decorated or not) from the sidewalk and post it here. Basically, if you can see it from a public space (as a sidewalk or a street is), you can do what you want with it, EXCEPT if you want to sell it (and even then, if you can prove you shot it for artistic reasons, and you sold it as fine art, you don't need a release. But that's something else entirely). If it were for commercial gain, that would be an entirely different matter (and you would need a property release, I believe). But for something like this, its a non-issue. This is how paparrazi can hide in trees next door to a famous person's house and shoot them sunbathing in their backyard. They can sell them to celebrity rags because they're the "press" (freedom of press), but they wouldn't be able to make a poster or calendar of it and sell it at the mall. I know the local paper back home does a spread on decorations (and publishes street names and a map for those who want to drive around and see the best displays in town), and when I asked a lawyer who visited my photo business class (I'm at SVA) about it a couple weeks ago, she said it was completely legal, even if they didn't ask permission.

All the law babble aside...ethically, I'd say if its out in the front yard or on the front of the house, then its fair game. However, just to be decent, if you really want to put pictures here, you should knock on the door and let them know. I probably would, and if they're not home, I might leave a note on the door with my name and email or something. If they're not cool with it, you can still do whatever you want with it (except make it into postcards or posters to sell...you can even use it as your personal Christmas card next year, that's within your rights), but you might feel bad.
