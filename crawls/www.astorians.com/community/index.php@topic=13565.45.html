So I thought I'd give an update after making the move.  I just got my internet connected today, so I wasn't even aware that some Astorian neophyte (yay, I'm not alone in this club) needed my help.  First with the update:

The move went relatively smoothly although the apartment lost electricity on the first day I moved in.  Yay, NOT.

The tub was re-glazed the day I moved in, meaning that I couldn't shower for the first two days unless I wanted to ruin the glaze job and risk them re-glazing again some other time.  Yeah, that sucked, I really needed a shower and shave.  Needless to say, I took an extra long/thorough shower job when I finally could jump in the tub.

I'm 90% unpacked but now need to do a thorough cleaning of the apartment.  There was a good layer of dust on the floor.  And since I'm pretty much a neat person, expect a good dousing of Tilex and Clorox in the near future.  Ahh...nothing quite like a good strong waft of bleach to guarantee the area is nuked of dirt and germs.

I needed to sell some of my stuff in order for the apartment to fit my furniture, including two TVs.  I just sold 1 today and I have a potential buyer for the second one lined up.

Overall, it's taking a little while to get adjusted to the area.  I'm blaming it on the transition, but being in Queens when most of my friends are in Manhattan has made me feel somewhat isolated.  And since I work from home, it's just really really quiet.  Also, my dog is going on a peeing spree in the streets since he's completely unfamiliar with the area and needs to mark his scent everywhere to make sure he finds his way home.

I've made two meals so far:

1. Lamb shank tagine (cinnamon, ginger, tomato sauce, white wine) + couscous (raisin, cucumber, red onion, tomato, mint, lemon zest)
2. Chinese stewed pork (dark and light soy, sugar, star anise, wild pepper, garlic, cinnamon) + green beans (made with the leftover tomato sauce I had with the lamb tagine) served over rice

I don't have a dish rack yet and I've been eating on my coffee table since I don't have a dining table either.  As I mentioned earlier, I finally got connected with cable and internet today.

I haven't met many of my neighbors yet.  My wife bumped into a young girl from Miami, I've seen a fellow asian girl come out of my building, and two girls welcomed us to the neighborhood the other day.  I introduced myself to the landlord.  Oh, it's worth mentioning that my next door neighbors seem to do quite a bit of shouting.  They're either really rowdy or there's definitely some occasional inter-family "miscommunication" going on.  Also, a hispanic/latino group of guys had a really loud Superbowl party till wee into the hours of Sunday night.  Although I introduced myself to them and then asked them nicely to keep it down, they eventually pumped the music back up after about 5 mins of lowering the volume (there's nothing quite like Latin honkytonk blasting at 3AM preventing a good night's sleep).  That's when my next door neighbor stepped in.  I heard some shouting going on, not really sure what was said.  But after that, the music just stopped abruptly.  Although I haven't met my next door neighbor yet (and I'm not sure that I really want to), I am sure he is VERY convincing when he starts yelling.

As for help, Camuri -

From my experience, Astoria pretty much breaks down into the following areas:

1.  Broadway/30th Ave: young, fun, lots of dining, is the area most similar to Manhattan.  This area also features a lot of relocated Manhattaners.  In general, the rent here seems to be the highest in all of Astoria.  If you're on a budget, either expect a smaller or more run-down unit, or you're going to have to pay more for a nicer unit.  Also, this area seems to be less pet friendly.  Ethnic makeup seems to be predominantly Greek, Italian, general Caucasian, and some traces of Hispanic and Asian.

2.  Ditmars: More families, quieter, very new and semi-upscale area with good restaurants.  Housing seems to be larger here, but not necessarily cheaper.  This area has lots of nice restaurants as well.  Also a lot of relocated Manhattaners.  Ethnic makeup seems to be largely general Caucasian with some Greek.

Both the above areas either east or west of the N/W are nice (read: more affluent).  On the west side is Astoria Park and the Beer Garden.  On the East side is Steinway street and access to the R/V/G trains which are a good backup in case you need added transportation options.

3.  Steinway: Busy, lots of shops, eateries, lower end stores, very ethnic with lots of mixed ethnic minorities.  East of Steinway street seems to be the cutoff point between local Queens natives and relocateed Manhattan young professionals, although I'm not sure of this.  Ethnic makeup seems to be mostly Hispanic and Middle Eastern with some traces of Greek and Italian.

Based on this assessment, I'd say that as a young professional, you're most likely going to be attracted mostly towards the Broadway/30th Ave area, Ditmars (in terms of North-South geographic border), or the area west of Steinway including perhaps Steinway itself and a few blocks east (in terms of East-West), but not more.

There seems to pretty much be decent dining options on every street, but most of the best (or at least more well-known) eateries are closer to the N/W train.  There also seems to be a convenience store and laundromat on every corner.

Apartment in review:

So I guess I felt like I was on top of the world after I managed to get a lease.  After moving, it's taken a little bit to settle in.  This being my second week at this apartment, I thought I'd give some impressions:

The unit itself is in good condition, not great but not bad.

Cons:
Uneven flooring - it's hard to notice until you start setting things up but this wood floor, as glossy and new as it looks, is in pretty bad condition.  The heat from the radiators is wreaking havoc on the wood, causing it to expand and contract, warping the floor and making the apartment un-level.  It's hard to notice to the naked eye, but looking around, you notice that everything is always at a slight slant.  I recently bought a leveler at the hardware store and it was fun applying it to everything and seeing just how uneven my desk, bed, TVs are.  More importantly, the wood fractures and splinters easily forcing us to walk around in slippers for fear of getting splinters in our feet.

The uneven floor also extends to the bathroom.  The tile in the bathroom seems to be unlevel as well, perhaps because it rests on top of warped wood floor.  The bathtub is at a slight slant.  Whenever water gets on the rim of the bathtub (everytime we shower), the water then flows onto the ground, causing a semi-substantial amount of water to be all over the floor.  Imagine filling up 3 large cups of water and dumping it on the floor everytime you wash.

The water temperature in this building is actually out of control.  In the middle of showers, the water suddenly becomes boiling hot.  I actually had to jump out of the shower (and into the pool of water on the ground) the other day.  I thought the water was actually going to leave a mark.  I adjusted the water and then it steadied.  I jumped back into the shower and then the water dropped to freezing cold.  It left me gasping for air.  I had to jump out of the shower again.  My skin was definitely confused going from boiling to freezing in a matter of seconds.

Pros:
The neighborhood is actually pretty fun for a foodie.  The halal butchers and Middle Eastern delis are starting to recognize me.  The people are actually quite nice.  I do wish their stores had a more neutral smell when going (it always has such a strong smell of spices), but in general I've found my way around.

The coffee shop on Astoria Blvd called Soliel is really great.  They have good coffee and excellent pastry.

I also seemingly bought out the Middle Eastern pastry shop, Lazizzi(sp?), last time I went.  They have one donut-like dessert that's just flat out amazing.

The hole-in-the-wall Mexican restaurant on Astoria Blvd is also fun.  It's SUPER cheap and it serves, good, honest, authentic food, something I appreciate very much.

Other thoughts:

I still wish my apartment was a couple of blocks closer to the train.  I'll work on that for my next move.  There are still logistical issues here and there, but at least my apartment is coming together slowly.  I have to find a solution to the water on the bathroom floor, however.
