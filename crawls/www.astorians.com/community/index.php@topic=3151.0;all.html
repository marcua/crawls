um, well yea, we went through a little problem around here last month, in case you hadn't noticed. Talking about throwing some high rise buildings on to the power grid is just a little early for some of us.

But kidding aside, if you went around the area and talked, you would discover that there are already many such buildings in western Queens.

Shore Towers, the Queensviews, Boulevard Gardens, Phelps Gardens, Celtic Park, Queens West. Sunnyside is filled with large pre-war apartment buildings: many are absolute art-deco gems. Some of these complexes are considered among the finest model communities in the country and are still studied by students of urban planning.

Astoria, by comparison, is designed low scale.

It is good to have a variety of textures in communities. People like it that way. Its good urban planning.
