Without any knowledge of what it means to be a part of a church and subscribe to a set of religious beliefs, though, I've never really understood where that fear comes from. However, the strong reactions people have when it comes up continue to fascinate me.

Clarification of terms:

Quote
Agnosticism is the philosophical view that the truth or falsity of certain claims—particularly theological claims regarding the existence of God or gods—is unknown, unknowable, or incoherent. Some agnostics infer from this that these claims are irrelevant to life

Gnosticism is a historical term for various mystical initiatory religions, sects and knowledge schools which were most active in the first few centuries of the Christian/Common Era, around the Mediterranean and extending into central Asia.
Quote
These systems typically recommend the pursuit of mysticism or "special knowledge" (gnosis) as the central goal of life. They also commonly depict creation as a mythological struggle between competing forces of light and dark, and posit a marked division between the material realm, typically depicted as under the governance of malign forces (such as the demiurge), and the higher spiritual realm from which it is divided, governed by God (the Monad) and the Aeons.

Atheism, in its broadest sense, is an absence of belief in the existence of gods. This definition includes both those who assert that there are no gods, and those who make no claim about whether gods exist or not. Narrower definitions, however, often only qualify those who assert there are no gods as atheists, labelling the others as agnostics or simply non-theists.

Quote
Although atheists often share common concerns regarding empirical evidence and the scientific method of investigation and a large number are skeptics, there is no single ideology that all atheists share. Additionally, there are certain individuals whose religious or spiritual beliefs some might describe as atheistic, though those holding such beliefs do not normally describe themselves as atheists.

Atheism includes, but is not equivalent to, the position of antitheism, the active opposition to theism.

above taken from Wikipedia

I do think that most people would agree with me that the vernacular definition of Atheism is a complete lack of belief.

I do think the definitions are an issue in this discussion. 

It seems to me that (from the 1st and 2nd postings) people may be considering "religion" to only mean "organized religion".  (See comparisons of practicing catholics to non-practicing, practicing religious people to atheists, etc.)  People don't have to practice together or organize in order to be religious.  Many people are religious in their own ways.  I don't think our society considers practicing a form of your own religion as atheism. 

In order to properly discuss such a deeply spiritual (pun intended) and intensely personal issue we must first agree on the terms that are used.  If we are to say that atheism encompasses non-belief as well as agnosticism and gnosticism then we must create another term for the absense of belief.  Only once we have agreed on our meanings can we proceed with an intelligent discussion.
