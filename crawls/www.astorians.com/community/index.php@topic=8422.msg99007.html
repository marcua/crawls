I really love Greenport; we've been going there for years -- I think it and the whole North Fork is a great weekend destination.  Aside from that, early last fall we went to Beacon on the spur of the moment and had a really lovely time.  I posted about it on the board somewhere.  It's about 60 miles from Astoria, so definitely under two hours.  Cold Spring is nearby, and also very nice, but it's more self-conscious (and more expensive) than Beacon is.  Much of the main street in Beacon has been really spruced up, and the west end is now full of art galleries, and the east end has a number of funkier shops.  There's also Dia:Beacon, which is a museum for the Dia Art Foundation's art collection.

In general, Beacon has developed quite an arts scene (Beaconarts.org has a lot of information.)  Many of the galleries and other businesses participate in something called Second Saturday, where shops and galleries stay open late on the second Saturday of the month, and many restaurants offer something extra - a free glass of wine, for instance.  We had no idea it was going on, and really lucked into it.  A lot of the galleries offer free wine and cheese, and you can meander from gallery to gallery, checking out the offerings.  There are several very good restaurants, too.

When we went, we stayed at Botsford Briar Bed & Breakfast  (They don't have a website but I found a few photos of the house here) - it's a beautiful Victorian house that has been almost completely renovated.  We found their number on a little pamphlet with local business listing in a funky coffee shop, and it turned out to be a great place to stay.  They are on top of a hill close to the river and train station.  Their phone number is 845.831.6099
