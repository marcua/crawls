I totally would be interested in seeing some young Jewish activity in Astoria, but sometimes I feel like the only Jew around.  Good to know there are others out there.  I tried to buy Kosher wine to take to a friend's Shabbat dinner on the UWS a few months ago at the wine store on Bdway and 34th or 35th and the guy sort of just laughed.  He said to try the big store on 30th ave.  I couldn't find Matzoh (or any other Jewish food) during passover.  Any good grocery stores for that sort of thing? 

I tried this: http://berniesservices.org/ for free services.  It's in a church on 51st and park, and they ask you to "send it a donation" after and just bring an ID for security purposes.  It's a typical Reform service, with a really good choir and a rabbi.  Very much like the congregation I grew up at a few states away.  There are a lot of older people, so it's not younger, but it worked. They even do Kol Nidre with a cello if you're used to that.  I would recommend it if you're looking for a free, Reform experience in the city (not so far from Astoria).
