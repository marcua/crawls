Absolutely. Sometimes the number on the renewal letter is just the opening bid.  Talk to them. Make an offer lower than your deal-breaker amount. Good luck! Tell us how it goes.

Here are the legally allowed increases from this year's rent stabilization board:
For a one-year renewal lease commencing on or after October 1, 2007 and on or before September 30, 2008:                 3.0%

For a two-year renewal lease commencing on or after October 1, 2007 and on or before September 30, 2008:                 5.75%

http://www.housingnyc.com/html/guidelines/apt.html

Please check and make sure your apartment is rent-stabilized, and that your landlord is abiding by the law.  He cannot raise your rent more than these amounts if it is rent-stabilized.  I rented from a major slumlord in the past, and he was not negotiable no matter what.  In fact, they were very rude when I asked.  He would raise it each year the maximum legal amount because, well, he could.  He'd always find someone to take my place and wasn't interested in keeping good tenants.  I think it depends on your landlord if they are willing to negotiate.
