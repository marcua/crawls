I hope it sticks around too- and yes there isnt anyone ever there! I went with a friend awhile back on a fri nite- only one other couple there.  i Have become somewhat of an addict- went twice in 8 days- hahaha- but I used to live on the UES where there was a great Indian place and I missed good Indian grub.
If you are watching your waistline- they use very little ghee in their cooking and they have this dish that I love called Chana Saag. Its basically spinach with ginger. I actually am not a big spinach fan but if u eat sag paneer u will love it but ALOT less fattening and spicy. Its probably the most diet friendly thing u can eat. Chicken Tandoori is also good as is green chili chicken. My bro had the lamb vindaloo and it wasn't spicy enough. The paratha is the best!
I also like that they are BYOB- saves money. Three of us ate for $35 incl appys. U cant beat that!

I had Seva for the first time last night.  I ordered in Chana Masala and Vegy Samosa, and they were both very good.  I'd prefer the Chana Masala a little less tomatoey but since the chick peas were cooked to the right tenderness and the spices were well balanced, I'm happy.  The Veg Samosa was flavorful with an excellent mush/crunch factor and as un-greasy as a fried thing can be. 

I'm a vegetarian  not a vegan, but I prefer not to eat the Indian cream and cheese, so I really appreciate that they identify vegan choices from among their vegetarian options.  That's very cool.
