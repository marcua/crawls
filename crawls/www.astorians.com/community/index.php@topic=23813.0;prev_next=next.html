Quote from: queenzbwai on January 30, 2009, 09:34:03 AM
The management of my apartment complex (Central Astoria, LLC) posted and distributed announcements yesterday addressing the problem of dog sh!t not being picked up.  They mentioned that harboring a pet is against the lease agreement but, although they're not enforcing it now, if the dog sh!t doesn't start getting cleaned up, they'll be forced to take drastic actions.

Also, they said that some people complained about dog sh!t being left in the stairways of the buildings themselves.  Disgusting.

That isn't going to solve the problem - your buildings threat is totally unenforceable.  Regardless of what the lease states, Pet Law states that if a dog or cat lives in the building "open and notorious" for 90 days, then the no pet clause of the lease is null and void as long as the person lives in the building.  The only way to void it is to show a pattern of nuisance attributable to each individual dog or cat.  By the building posting that sign, they acknowledge that they know about the dogs and aren't enforcing the clause, thus nullifying it.  They could try and fight it, but there is so much case law in NY supporting the law, that it's almost unbeatable.

Your management company should think about installing a couple hidden cameras in order to catch the offenders and post their pictures in the lobby.  Most offenders do it because they think they can't get caught.  Public ridicule generally solves the issue pretty quickly.

Z.
