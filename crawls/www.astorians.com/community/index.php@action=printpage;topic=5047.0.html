Quote
So what about arranging a time and place to swap stories?
-betheroonie06

In college we had a creative writing class which I'm sure alot of others have had and it might work:

Between two and four people would be assigned to submit a short story/poem.  Copies would be made for everyone else in the group.  When the group met the next week, those stories would be critiqued for discussion.  At the end of that discussion, another two to four people would submit their work and the process would repeat itself.

I thought it was a good, low key way to write, get reviewed and get ideas.  Can also make it theme based (like one month our prof had us all write stories/poems that centered on a color).

I'd be interested in getting a local group going.

I think 2-5 pages is really too short for any kind of workshop. I also think that limiting a group to memoir writing would probably mean missing out on some great works!

The last workshop I ran met twice a month. We were limited to 3 mid-length poems/1 long poem or no more than 15 pages of prose. We would start off with a 10 minute free write before taking turns editing. the group had 8 people, including me, and it worked pretty well. We were all serious writers intent on publishing and it worked well. We talked about editing, but also about where to send work, what magazines fit other's styles, things like that. If anyone would be interested in a format like this, I could easily be persuaded to organize.
