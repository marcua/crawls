Ok, I can't believe I am doing this...but I have to disagree somewhat with Anthony Bourdain.  I work in purchasing for a hotel.  While it is true that most places won't have a lot of fish for a Monday delivery you can get fresh stuff in on a Monday morning.  When we need a lot (60-100lb) of something on a Monday, we preorder it on Thursday so the fish company can work on making sure they have it in on Monday morning.  Also, for fish that we only get 5-10lb of, they always have it and it is always fresh.  We can get salmon any day as well as sushi grade tuna, stripe bass, halibut, etc. 
While it is true that food isn't as fresh on Mondays (and why you shouldn't go grocery shopping on Mondays also), if you have good relations with your vendors, you can have fresh product.  If your buying fish from a market on Monday...probably stay away from it.  If you order it in a restaurant...it is probably safe.  Worse case, you can ask the manager when they got in their delivery.

If you want to buy really fresh fish in NY, unless you catch it yourself, you can only buy it on Thursdays and Fridays.  The reason for this is that the Fulton Fish Market gets it's deliveries early Thursday morning.  This has always been the day Fulton gets it's deliveries and if it has changed (it may have, I'm not claiming to be an expert) I have not heard about it.    

The Fulton Fish Market is the largest seafood marketplace in the NY metropolitan area (perhaps even the largest on the east coast) and it's pretty safe to assume that 99% of area restaurants and fish markets either get their seafood from The Fulton Fish Market directly, or through a wholesaler (middleman) who gets it from FFM.

So in answer to your question, no.  The restaurants and the local fish markets ultimately get their fish from the same place on the same day.  The only difference is that the fish market is more likely to store it's fish properly, which is packed on/in ice.  But the bottom line is that, while fish remains safe/edible for about a week at most (unless it's frozen which is just gross), the absolute best time to eat fish is Thursday (providing the place you are eating/buying from got their delivery on Thursday) and Friday.

Here is a great link to an old article on how to buy fresh fish:
http://www.nytimes.com/1982/02/24/garden/a-consumer-s-guide-to-fresh-fish.html?&pagewanted=all
