Well, I mean, it's a cloud. They kinda, y'know, move on when the wind blows. It might've only been above the plant for a little bit. So I'd guess no one else noticed it.

I wasn't there on Sunday, but the OP description of a yellow / brown cloud over the site would be from the small simple cycle gas turbines. These are run when the city doesn't have sufficient  backup capacity in the NYC area. They are also run periodically to verify that they will work when called upon. You will see it on those 90 degree + days in the afternoon, or when a larger plant is down for maintenance. Because of the age, and the classification, they do not have pollution control, and exhaust a rather unpleasant cloud that was described by the OP.
