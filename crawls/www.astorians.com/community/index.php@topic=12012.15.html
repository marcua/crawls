Its been love at first site for me here at Soleil and I wanted to pass on the location and recommendation to y'all because its a wee bit out of the way in a place one might not necessarily go searching for a cute little coffee/food shop. 

Its tucked away on Astoria Blvd., I think between 37th and 38th Streets.  They have a ton to offer, all made with love, its cheaper than any coffee shop I've ever visited, and the staff is uber friendly.  They offer hand made sandwiches (I had myself a their Bruschetta sandwich ($4) which comes with mozzarella, tomato, and home made pesto and they gave me the extra on the side), breakfast eats like bagels with your choice of topping, lots of baked goods, pastries, and breads, plus a nice assortment of coffees, teas, a large array of espresso, Iced coffees, and fresh made juices.  I had myself a carrot apple and ginger juice which was served up in an enormous mug for about $3.  They have soy options of those who like to avoid dairy, and a few vegetarian lite bites as well. 

The interior is very cute.  I'm pretty sure they just remodeled because the place closed down for a bit earlier this year and recently reopened.  They offer free wi-fi as well, and couches and even an outdoor area to curl up in.  I highly recommend it, its an oasis in the middle of Astoria Blvd. and a fantastic find.

We stopped into Soleil this morning on the way to the city and were very pleased.  The coffee (latte) was pretty good and the almond croissant was wonderful.  Flaky, not overly sweet, and in that more cornetto shape rather than the puffy crescent a lot of croissants resemble.  I asked where they got them and the told me that they get them from a "french bakery".  I saw a lot of other little pastries, too.

While we didn't stay long, I noticed that the space is nice and a little funky, very much like it was when they first opened.  They have also put the back garden in order.

I'll definitely be back - I wonder if they would be open to having knitters come there and spend a couple hours knitting and gabbing, drinking and eating.  I did see people with laptops, so that gives me hope.  It could be a good alternative to Freeze Peach, while they are closed and looking for a new home. 

Check it out!
