I can only really talk about  122. My son is in 1st grade there. Overall I am happy with the school. The teachers seem good, his teacher in particular is very good, he is learning a lot and he really loves her. I've had a few issues here and there but they are always addressed immediately, either by meeting with the teacher or the assistant principal. Even though the school is huge, the assistant principal, principal and tons of the teachers know my son by name.


Now, negatives are:
1) they never go outside if the weather is anything short of perfect.
2) they cut the dance program for all but the "gifted" students
3) if your child isn't in the "gifted" program, he/she will not get as many resources (see #2)
4) I was less than impressed with the kindergarten curriculum. It was geared toward kids who had never gone to pre-K and never done any academic work at home either (literally learning letters, letter sounds, etc). My son was VERY bored. (1st grade has been much better!)
5) the school is HUGE. It can seem less-than-welcoming when you literally dump your child at the door instead of being allowed into the building in the AM.


Overall, though, I would say that 122 is a better-than-average neighborhood school, it certainly is sought-after by parents.


I have been in PS 85 a few times and the only thing I can say with certainly is that when the subway goes by, it is LOUD. I can't imagine how the kids can concentrate with that going on all day!

My eldest son went to 85 for pre K then went to 122. My youngest is now in 122 in third grade.
I was told when I moved into the area that PS 122 was a really good school. If you go on the school website you can see the grades each school received. I have enclosed the links for both schools:
http://schools.nyc.gov/OA/SchoolReports/2010-11/Progress_Report_Overview_2011_EMS_Q085.pdf


http://schools.nyc.gov/OA/SchoolReports/2010-11/Progress_Report_Overview_2011_EMS_Q122.pdf


You can see for yourself the ratings.
As for personal experience I think both of my children have done well at 122. My older son went from K-5. He is now in the honors program at IS 141.
I think the key thing is you have to know your child. If you have a bright and academically gifted child you can test to be in the Academy program at PS 122 which goes through to 8th grade. Be warned it is alot of hard work. If your child doesn't have the personal motivation to keep up, it can be difficult.
I think my one problem with 122 has been that the arts classes do get cut ( specifically dance) , and they are very rigid about going outside for recess. I think though with the new playground they are more likely to send the kids outside. Both of my sons have had wonderful teachers. But as with all situations in life there is one who may not work well with my child. If ever you have that problem just go in and talk to the administration.


I will say as a parent , if you stay on top of your child, you will have a very good experience at PS 122.
Check homework, ( the new math is sooo much fun) see what your child is doing in class, talk to the teachers. But above all, take responsibility for your child. I have seen too many parents blame teachers , when they never hold their children accountable for their actions. yes even at 5 years old they have their personalities and they come shining through.


One lesson I learned having my children: Never be the parent that says my child would never.......
