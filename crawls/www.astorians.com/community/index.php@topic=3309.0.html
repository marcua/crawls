I moved lots in Astoria.  Just walk into any liquor store, drugstore, or grocery store, and ask a worker to save some boxes for you.  They'll either give them to you right on the spot or tell you when you can come pick them up.  I have no idea if my being a girl and smiling sweetly helps, but it can't hurt.  You can also just take them off the street as well.  There is also a packing supplies store on Ditmars and 39th for certain size boxes.

Sure, you can use anything you can find on the street.  There are a couple of bug-related caveats, though.

First - cardboard boxes can harbour roach eggs.  If you're just using them to box things up to throw out or something, it wouldn't be a problem, but I'm leery of picking them up when they're on the street.

Second - and this is more for residential pickings - there's been a resurgence of bedbugs in Astoria, and elsewhere in NYC in the past couple of years.  So if you see what looks like half an apartment out on the street, with stuff in good condition, be wary.

That said, dumpster diving and trash picking can net some good yields.  But I'm very cautious these days for the above reasons.
