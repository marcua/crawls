I can't help but think most of this discussion and anger comes from the newer resident's defending their right to be here and feeling attacked. I'm sure most of them are aware that their presence here has altered the demographics. It's not that there haven't been demographic shifts before and there have always been new immigrants and nationalities but Astoria was solidly middle class and blue collar for the most part. The shift is in the fact that now the people moving in are white collar professionals with money. The appearance of cool new bars and nice restaurants is great for the people who live here but it's made it more desirable for people with more money who are willing to pay more for rent and therefore it raises rent prices across the board. This in effect makes the residents who live in the area already and are renting unable to find new apartments in their neighborhood or are being pushed out by landlords who want to re-rent at a higher price. It's great for the homeowners but a lot of older homeowners sell when the prices get higher and they sell to people who have enough money to afford the more expensive prices and bam... no longer a middle class neighborhood. Yes, this is all the way this city works (I lived in Williamsburg and got priced out in the 90s) and it's the way gentrification works but it doesn't stop the older residents from feeling resentful and angry and territorial. The fact that Queens is one of the last bastions in NYC of affordable middle class living for families makes people especially weary of change.
