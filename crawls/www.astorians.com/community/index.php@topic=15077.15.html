Y'know, I consider myself a fairly intelligent, well-educated person and I like to think that I can see valid points on both sides of almost any argument, but I truly don't understand what your issue is about what children are or aren't seeing. Kids see gay people all the time. They'll have gay friends and they already probably have gay people in their lives -- you just don't realize it. I'm an attorney. My partner is an attorney. I highly doubt our clients realize either of us are gay. I have two gay male colleagues and women are always asking if they're single and available because people don't realize they're gay either. So I'm not really sure what it is you're talking about kids being "subjected" to -- because as I said before, with or without the passage of this bill, kids are already subjected to gay people -- that's not going to change.

Now, if you're worried about having kids subjected to gay couples in long term, committed relationships raising families and caring about one another, well then, I can absolutely see how that can be so threatening! But don't worry, just keep your television on a continuous loop of the Maury show's "Whose my baby daddy?" episodes and they will be exposed to some fine upstanding heterosexuals behaving in a completely appropriate and acceptable manner that they can use as role models.

I'm sorry to have this thread turn into a debate, but I think it's a great example of why it's so important for people to come out to this rally and show their support. There will always be people like Joey C. out there -- people who can't articulate a legitimate argument against same sex marriage, but feel the need to interject their commentary anyway -- we need to be better than them, we need to be louder than them, and we need to show them that we are people who are entitled to the same rights as everyone else.
