This whole conversation needs to stop.  Our society favors economic interests.  This should not come as a surprise.  With that said, there are legal means available to those affected by alleged unreasonable noise.  The noise code is very specific about what constitutes a violation, regardless of whether a violation is actually issued by the city.  If your coop/landlord won't take action, then you need to pool your resources with other similarly situated people to pay for a noise inspection/test and for a lawyer.  If the noise inspection/test reveals violations of the noise code, you can take appropriate legal action for an injunction and possibly damages.  If you are successful against the Strand, you can probably also sue your coop/landlord for failing to take steps to address breaches of your warranty of habitability.


TL;DR: You have clear, legal avenues to seek redress.  You are either reluctant or unable to afford those avenues and are seeking political ones.  Those are going no where.  Sue someone or STFU.  And I mean that in the kindest way possible.
