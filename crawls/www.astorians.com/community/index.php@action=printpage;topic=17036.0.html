Hi-- Welcome to Astoria.

PS 122 is considered the best of the local public schools.  It is a huge school.  For kids not in the G&T program, the school goes up to grade 5 I beleive, and up to grade 8 for those that qualify for G&T.  the other public schools in the area generally do not compare as favorably.  If you find a home you really like that is not zoned for 122, you can try to get in on a magnet basis or look at some of the other local school options other than public.  (That is the route my family went-- I was looking for a smaller school.) PS 85 also has a G&T program.

I do not know any realtors, though there apepars to be one on every block.

Best of luck!

Title: Re: Seeking info about Astoria public schools and other family-related questions
Post by: mamidesamuel on January 28, 2010, 07:41:36 AM
Hello & welcome to Astoria
We love Astoria. 
PS 122 has a strong reputation but the reputation of PS 85 is growing.  They have STEM, the only citywide G & T program in Queens and they also have district wide G & T seats.
Many of the smaller schools do not have as strong reputations and not rated as highly on Insideschools.org but if you visit them they have a certain small town feel to them that may work for your family.
Astoria has 3 charter schools:
1.  Our World Charter School
2.  Growing Up Green Charter School
3.  Voice Charter School
They are all lottery and they are all taking applications now through March or April check each school for the time frame.
I know many parents are thrilled with several religious schools in Astoria.  I only know about Grace Lutheran school.  My son is in PReK there and I would have never considered it for kindergarten but we're having such a positive experience there that we are now considering it.  It is a small school with enthusiastic families and staff. 
For preschool/ PreK options I know other parents are fond of: Little Ones (at the citylights building),  St Francis, Immaculate Conception, Most Precious Blood, Les Enfants, and Blossoms.
There are several parent communities but not one dominates (like Park Slope Parents).  I am familiar with Astoria Moms Meetup Group and Astoriafamilies.org  - look around old discussions here to see a listing where people have discussed the groups.  I think if you just make it to the playgrounds and chat up the families you will find people are eager to share about the neighborhood. 

I was recommended Bianca Colasuonno as a realtor by several friends who bought their home with her.  She also helps with the rental market.
Best of Luck as you consider your options.

Post by: alia on May 20, 2010, 09:50:16 PM
Danielle: your kid takes the G&T test, and if they get a hig enough scorte, they are offered a seat where there is room for them. There does seem to be some effort made by the ed dept to place kids in schools that are physically close to where they live, but it's no guarantee. (One could test into the G&T program, live next to ps 122, but be offered a seat at ps 85 if that's where there's room.)

My child is finishing kindergarten at PS 122 after doing nursery and pre-K at a local parochial school. PM me if you have specific questions or would like my take on his experiences there. :)

PS 122 has a very strong general ed program, but is overcrowded. The PTA there is very active and supports the school well.  On the downside: 122 has a terrible playground that badly needs repair. Their after-school program is the pits.

PS 85 is not as crowded, but like most schools is going to get hurt on class size some because of teacher layoffs. Their science and music programs are great. The playground is amazing.
