Re: Psitopolio Ta Vlahika - opening at former Athens Tavern & Anna's Place
« Reply #9 on: February 22, 2009, 04:56:08 PM »
I went there today. Its definitely a family type place, solid-citizen. We felt comfortable there with kids and were able to take our time and not be rushed out the door. Mostly older Greek people were dining there (probably has more to do with the time of day than anything). We ordered saganaki, zuchini fritters, grilled octopus, salad, feta and souvlakia. I am happy to report it wasn't greasy. This is my main issue with most of the Greek food in Astoria. I know places like Pita Pan and BZ Grill are more authentic with the real Greek style stuff, but they are repulsively greasy and my stomach is in a shambles for days after eating this stuff. I remember Anna's Corner was a little more greasy. The tzatziki was a little heavy for me, but this is personal preference thing. They have a lot of other selections on the menu, but we were in the mood for the standard stuff.

Oh yeah... the owner who was there today is the same owner from Anna's Corner. She's a really nice lady.

OK, that's my review.
