question on buying an 'attached' home in Astoria
« on: May 12, 2013, 04:03:34 PM »

I am hoping that maybe, just maybe, someday I could buy a house in Astoria.

I've seen descriptions of many homes as 'attached', which I assume means it shares a wall with another building on one or both sides of it.

I know this is pretty much the norm in Astoria but for me it brings up fears of potential problems...that just by being attached, you have this inextricable connection to your neighbor, and what if you don't end up getting along?  What if they are an irresponsible homeowner etc.?

So I'm curious if anyone has experience with this....DOES it really make much difference if your neighbor's home is ATTACHED to yours versus just 'next door' (separate) from yours?

I assume all attached homes in Astoria have firewalls between them (assuming they were built up to code)?  Are there more rules in instances of attached homes, whereby if I want to make a change to the exterior of my home or my yard...where I have to consult with the person's home that is attached to mine?

I naturally have some concerns/paranoia about buying an attached home...that it could bring with it the potential for more conflict/problems versus an UNattached home.  Or maybe that's rarely the case, and that so long as the other home/homes attached to mine look like they aren't totally run-down or abandoned, that I should likely be fine?

Tx!

In an attached home what your neighbors do can definitely make a difference. In the 3 years in our home, we've had several issues relating to our shared walls with neighbors:


* Our neighbors used oil heat and had a leak on their side, which leaked through the wall into our side and rendered our basement unusable for a year. It was a long and difficult process, but happily we resolved it (at their expense). This is something that could have ended very badly, so I'm grateful that we were able to work it out and still be on friendly terms with the neighbor.
* We share a retaining wall in the backyard that's currently collapsing into our yard, and we're having to negotiate with them to hire someone to replace a good part of the wall. Again, we've been able to negotiate terms that are to everyone's satisfaction (mostly at our expense), but this is another issue that could have led to bad blood.


It's interesting to note that both of these issues arose because their house is about 4 feet higher than ours, so our basement walls actually adjoin the soil underneath their property. I didn't see this as cause for concern when we first looked at the house, but perhaps it's something to look out for.


It's *certainly* worth paying attention to your potential next door neighbors when you look at a house. Are they absentee landlords? How well do they keep up their house? And it's also very worthwhile to make friends with your neighbors and to handle all conflicts carefully. They can definitely affect your quality of life, both for good and for bad.
