Thoughts...

I think allowing the option for Citibike in Astoria is good.  However the location of the stations clearly needs to be given a lot of thought, and imho, no one in their right mind should be wanting or considering riding a bicycle, on places like Bway, 30th Ave., Steinway, 31st Street, and the like.  Those streets are already utter chaos, and narrow, and that's WITHOUT the double-parking that occurs on Steinway, or the slimeballs on 31st Street, who try to sneak over to the parking lane in order to make a 'quick right turn'.

I'd relish the opportunity to occasionally ride a bike around the park, or across some of the lesser-travelled avenues (i.e., 20th Ave.).  I don't have space for owning my own bike and am worried about theft, and most folding bikes seem too heavy/cumbersome for me.  So Citibike or a similar rent-by-the-hour option is the only thing that would work for me.

As for the suggestion that maybe with Citibike, current car-owners might then consider giving up one of the family cars, or NOT buying a 2nd or 3rd car for the family, to me that's apples and oranges.  Those who would use Citibike are generally not car people, and vice versa...those who own cars generally aren't interested in bicycling throughout the nabe.

Net, net, what this all boils down to imho is the Old Astoria vs the New Astoria....the long-term residents (generally more 'blue collar' and who own cars) vs the new residents (generally relatively well-off - considering their ages - young people, 'hipsters' if you will... and less likely to own a car and who are more into saving the environment...).  We can argue between ourselves all we want but it won't get us anywhere.  We have to come to some type of a compromise. 

So for starters, maybe we don't criticize people for simply owning a car, but perhaps we can instead address some of the bad behaviors of 'some' car-owners.  At the top of that list (imho) would be the aforementioned double-parking on Steinway (which btw our illustrious DoT is also largely to blame, for seemingly allowing it to run rampant, and drivers being utterly nonchalant about sitting even in MTA bus stops??!)... and also the dangerous mode of driving under the el on 31st Street, and then suddenly pulling into the right lane to sneak a right turn...that too must stop.

As for those who want Astoria to be more bike-friendly, well perhaps they can start by acknowledging that for the most part, they are relatively new to this neighborhood...that the neighborhood isn't going to change for them overnight, and that they cannot expect that main thoroughfares are going to have bike lanes added, and that they just may have to use less congested/trafficked streets....  (Vernon Blvd. also seems to make sense to me.... also 14th Street....  and  PERHAPS 21st Street due to how wide it is... but 21st would really have to be looked at seriously due to the fact is has 2 lanes of traffic in each direction, plus MTA buses...)

Below is my personal use case for biking which I believe is practical for most people in good health:[size=78%] [/size]


If the trip is less than a mile or so one way I'll walk.  If less than 5-7 miles one way I'll ride my bike.  If rainy, snowy or icy weather or more than 5-7 miles, or if I need to carry large items I'll take the subway or drive a car.
Once you have a family or if you like to reach beyond city limits you probably need a car (to make trips to other family members outside of town or just trips to fun destinations where mass transit isn't available).


For some people, weekly, weekend or even hourly car rentals work.  For others car ownership is more practical.


I personally believe that you do need access to a car to maintain a good lifestyle in this city.  If you own the car just keep it in a garage and think twice before using it!


I can't wait for lots of well placed Citibike stations throughout the city.  Even though there are quite a few bike riders today, adding more bike riders via Citibike will get car drivers more used to seeing bikes and increase awareness and safety for all.
