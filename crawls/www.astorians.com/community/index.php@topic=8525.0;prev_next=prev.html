Astoria Park Alliance Meeting
 :-)

What: The Astoria Park Alliance represents local residents and
organizations who are working together to ensure the overall health of
the park. Please join us for a discussion on current issues,
organizational development, and upcoming events

When: Tuesday, September 6, 2007 6:30pm. Next meetings are 10/02 &
11/06

Where: Steinway Reformed Church at corner of Ditmars Ave and 41 Street
Who: All are welcome!   8-)

More info: If you have questions please call Helen 

or e-mail helen.ho  at  parks.nyc.gov

Looks like I'm going to be too busy to make it tonight, so I hope someone reads this before the meeting.

Aside from a desire to live IN this world instead of just ON it, my main reason for wanting to go to the meeting was this...

From hanging out with one of the Parks Dept Captains on Sun mornings I found out that they plan on placing a shipping container that they use for storage in the parking lot with a fence around it.  It currently resides under the Triboro with it's twin.   The twin is heading to Socrates and both are being moved to make room for the skate park.  I believe the perfect place for this container is the area between the field house and the little, manned TBTA booth behind the tennis courts.   It's out of the way, adjacent to a road, close to the field house and tho it's not their job, it would be/could be watched by the TBTA guys in the booth.   My suggestion to the Captain was met with a, "that's what I thought" conversation.   The problem is that the commissioner has a "Not on any grass" policy.   While this is admirable, in this case it isn't very practical.  This is certainly not any patch of grass that anyone would ever put a blanket on for a picnic.

So could someone in my place please tell the commissioner (or whoever will listen) to not be an idiot.   Lay some gravel, drop the thing on it, paint it green... end of story.    You could probably get a local hardware/paint store to donate the paint, or maybe hire/get a local artist to paint a mural on it.  Put some posies or light landscaping around it in the spring and it'll look nice.   

The people that can then use the 3-5 parking spaces this thing will eat up will thank you and not complain at all that they can't picnic behind a tennis court.

How hard is that?

Thanks in advance... here's what I meant in case my description was insufficient... (it's probably not that big, but you get the idea)
