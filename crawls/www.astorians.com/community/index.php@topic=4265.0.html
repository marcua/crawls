Cinema Paradiso
« on: November 29, 2006, 03:31:29 PM »
Cup's new neighbor (on 35th Ave) has a name now, so I figured it was worthy of a new thread.

While passing the place today, I noticed that they have a new sign up (promising brick oven pizza and gelato, among others, which is promising).  I went to take a closer look and the guys inside started waving me in.  Even after I confirmed that I wasn't looking for a job there, they still proceeded to give me a tour of the whole place, both floors, brick oven and everything.   :mrgreen:

It does look nice, and a step above some of the other restaurants in the area.  It's two stories with an open upstairs seating area and the balcony will be open in nice weather.  They have some nice flourishes inside, gold and wood finishes.  They don't have a menu printed yet, but it's mostly finalized and will be primarily Italian, obviously.  (I asked about whole wheat pizza - they said that's just a Greek thing.  I never realized that...  :-D)

Wine and beer will be available, but no liquor, from what I could gather. 

Looks like a nice place to gather with friends, or potentially a nice date spot.  I definitely want to check it out once it opens, and it seems like management is eager to win over the neighborhood.  Always a plus.

I'm also all for less empty space over there in the old studio buildings - as far as I'm concerned, this pattern of turning empty warehouse-like space into slightly upscale commercial neighborhood spots is all right by me.  Can't wait to see how the food is.

They mentioned a connection with a Cipriani in Manhattan through their cook, but I'm not sure which Cipriani that was.  There are a handful of them from what I can tell.

"Two to three weeks" is the timeline on opening, and they are hiring now.

Sorry, but this is another long-winded post.

My mom and I went to Cinema Paradiso last Wed., 1/17. Very quiet, empty really.
Not surprising since it was pretty cold out and a weeknight. We sat upstairs - much warmer there FYI!

We had a nice young guy as our server. He brought us what we asked for, but we're pretty
easy customers. We ordered off the regular menu, not the specials. I asked for a glass of
pinot grigio - listed on the back of the menu as $5 per glass. They don't have pinot or any of
the white or red wines listed. All they have is some kind of white which isn't very good and chianti.

We asked twice for water before we got it. And funny thing - they don't have water glasses, wine glasses,
etc. Just one type of goblet glass for everything.

Anyway, the food itself was good and since the service was ok, we went back Friday nite for my
birthday w/my boyfriend.

This time we had a different waiter and totally different experience.

I ordered a bronzonni fish from the specials. Choice was whole fish or filet. I asked for the filet.

My boyfriend order some kind of chicken special.

My mom ordered a veal parmesan and asked for pasta on the side since she was having a mixed
green salad as a starter.

Here's what we got:

Me - the whole fish with the head, not the filet.

BF - regular grilled chicken, not the special.

Mom - another salad,  not the pasta, w/her veal.

This waiter was a young Italian guy who didn't speak very good English. When I told him there was no
way I would eat the fish this way, he just shrugged. I asked for the manager who told me "the kitchen didn't
understand." I told him that since on the menu, the choice was offered, what was the problem. My mom asked
for the pasta and it took a couple of minutes to convince them that she wouldn't eat another salad.

My boyfriend didn't bother about his - he was just annoyed.

Finally, after dinner - mine took almost another 30 minutes to get - we asked for a cappucinno and a
chammomile tea. We got regular coffee and lipton regular tea - no apologies offered.

Dinner came to $103 and really wasn't worth it.

I may sound picky, but I grew up in restaurant kitchens - dad was a chef.

For the prices they're charging, the service is very poor and the staff very unprofessional.

Save your $$$ and go to L'Incontro or Sacs instead.
