Megc, there are few things as glorious as Le Creuset cookware (except perhaps All-Clad) so I feel very comfortable in encouraging you to go ahead and get that French oven!  I have several Le Creuset pieces and I absolutely love them.  They're incredibly expensive, but so worth the money because with proper care they'll last forever.  You won't even believe the even cooking you'll get when you roast or braise with your French oven.  I had to leave all my Le Creuset and All-Clad at home because it was too heavy to cram in my suitcases and way too expensive to ship, but I intend to bring it here to New York a little at a time because it actually hurts to be away from it.  I'm sorry if I'm rambling, but your post just got me all excited!  It's easy to clean, too, which is a huge plus for me as I hate washing dishes.  I would just suggest going easy with the metal utensils on the surface, like a whisk if you're stirring a sauce, or metal tongs when removing large pieces of meat or vegetables.  The surface is pretty durable, but you wouldn't want to scratch it accidentally.  I hope you get it and enjoy it as much as I love my Le Creuset. I even mix the colors!  I have red and blue.

I've only used my cast iron skillet a few times, but it's always yielded good results.  I love making cornbread in it, and it's brilliant for fritattas.

Good luck with your cooking adventures!!

If you're looking for great cast iron pans and happen to go to thrift shops or flea markets, be sure to check the pans they might have for ones marked "Griswold" and "Erie, PA" stamped on the bottom. Griswold pans are very collectible now, but there is always the chance that some place may have one not knowing what it is.

Also, the All Clad factory is in Pennsylvania and they have an annual clearance sale of seconds and such, and can be worth the trip.
