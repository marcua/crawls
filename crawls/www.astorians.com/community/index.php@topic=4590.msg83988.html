Just my 2 cents here because I have no friends who rent or own there. While it's great that the management has changed recently, what will always be an issue in a HUGE coop complex like that if there is no board to go through, even for renters, is the people living next door to you. Living near buyers or other renters might be an issue in terms of dogs, other noise, cleanliness, bed bugs, etc... I think it's pretty difficult to control who lives around you unless they go through a board approval process, and even then....  While you may buy your own place, renovate it and it can look fantastic, there are other things to consider than just your own 600 sq. foot of space. I would be weary of getting into a complex of that size with no board screening and a managment that has just changed hands, I think I'd want to see a couple of great years go by for the majority of the tenants and a low % of renters to buyers before investing in this kind of property. Unfortunately, there aren't a wealth of places to buy in Astoria, but there are more now than there were 3 years ago. Just to add, I rent a 2 bedroom in the acropolis. It was newly renovated when I moved in a little over a year ago, it is owned by a realty group, not related to the management company there. It is definitely not a luxury building, but it really isn't that bad either. My apartment, which is on the H line, is actually quite spacious, and has a lot of closet space. Some of the apartments I looked at there were really small. While the hallways leave a lot to be desired, overall living there is nice. I find that it is pretty quiet at night, and the maintenance people usually come in a timely manner if you have a problem. We had a leak once, and they came at 11:30 on a Sunday night. The dog crap on the streets is definitely a problem, but I usually just walk on the other side. I am not looking to buy the place, so I can't help with that. I definitely see apartments being renovated all the time.
