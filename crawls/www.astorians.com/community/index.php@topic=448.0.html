I don't think the answer lies in making rules for the majority that are based on the rude acts of the minority. There are many customers for example who use a cell phone responsibly, talking quiet - and shortly enough that it's of little or no bother to other customers. I think we're talking about a handful of people that talk loudly, and at length on their cellphones with no regard for others around them.

Another solution could be that any annoyed customer could politely ask the person to take their conversation outside, or to talk a little more quietly on their phone. A jammer could be another solution except some might argue this would also penalize courteous customers from even making or recieving calls outside the cafe (depending on the range of the jammer.)

I personally don't find cell phone use any more rude than people tuning out reality through iPods, or singing (or dancing) along with the music in their heads or ears - but as long as it's not blaringly loud or obnoxious I find it tolerable. I pretty much feel the same way about cigarette smoke. As long as there's enough fresh air to breathe around me it doesn't bother me.

Just an opinion. Are there other opinions on this matter?

It is amazing how cooperative people can be when you just ask.

Last summer on my vacation to the beach, an idyllic weekend picked up with a group of us enjoying a nice, calm, quiet summer evening in a little beach town.  The following day we'd hoped for more only to discover a large family, including once loud, outside playing obnoxious child had settled in next door.  We complained that evening at the racket  and into the next, amongst ourselves, until another "hardened" lifetime New Yorker showed up.  He'd been there under an hour, when upon noticing the volume level of the offending child, he calmly walked out to our front porch stoop and yelled "WILL YOU SHUT THAT KID UP?!"

Maybe it wasn't the most polite way of asking but it worked.

The rest of the week was as quiet as the first day.  The kid playing outside, enjoying summer in lower decibles.

It seems like there's something inbetween the extremes though.  That instead of letting a problem irritate you to the point of yelling or not asking for a conversation to be moved outside for fear of seeming impolite, we just get over ourselves already and learn to say "would you please take that outside."
