Title: Re: Internet service
Post by: artgirl on August 21, 2006, 05:04:25 PM
I've had dial-up through Earthlink for ages, but just upgraded to their high-speed cable. They get the cable service through your cable provider-- in my case Time Warner (but I got to keep my existing earthlink e-mail address, a plus for me)

It was a super easy set-up. Since I was already a Time Warner customer, all they had to do was come to my apartment, split the cable line and run it into the room where my computer is. Plugged it into a router, into my computer and like magic I was online immediately. I'd say the technician was there for 20 minutes, tops.

Earthlink has a promotion for new cable customers-- $29.99 a month for the first 6 months ($41.95/month thereafter) It just gets tacked onto your cable bill.

So far I'm thrilled with the service-- it's super fast   :-)

If you're starting from scratch, you could check out the service offered from Time Warner directly (Road Runner I think). They probably have a comparable offer price-wise. I only went thru Earthlink because I wanted to keep the e-mail address I've had for years.

hope that helps!

The only difference between Earthlink Broadband and Time Warner Roadrunner is if you want channel service.  If you don't, go thru Earthlink and they'll set you up with Time Warner, and you don't have to order channel service.  You can get a few channels by splitting the cable into your tv.  It's not a lot of channels, but it's basic and good enough for some people.  Go thru Time Warner, and you will have to order a channel service package.   So it depends on how much money you want to spend.
