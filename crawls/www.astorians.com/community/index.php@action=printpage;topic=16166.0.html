Title: 21st & Broadway Mail Delivery
Post by: jc138 on September 19, 2009, 10:45:54 AM
Hi all,

After moving to Long Island City, close to the 21st Street & Broadway post office, I have been appalled by the unreliable mail service.  I see that there are a couple threads acknowledging that the area served (or at least part of it) by this USPS branch has had trouble for years. 

I do not get very much mail -- at least not very much that I care about.  But I do find that when I am actually expecting something the mail is lost with an alarming frequency.  My magazine subscriptions routinely disappear.  I have had personal letters get lost.  And most recently a new credit card never made it to my apartment.

Now I realize that you get what you pay for and that forty-some-cents does is a great price to have a letter delivered across the county, even if it only gets there part of the time.  But nevertheless, the postal service is the only game in town and every so often I actually need it.

Who here else shares the problem?  Anyone managed to solve it?

I had this kind of trouble for a long time with the branch at 21st Street near 30th Ave. For a long time they left my
magazines laying in my lobby so that many were stolen, as were packages that used to just be left in the lobby.

A friend of mine who worked for the post office advised me to contact the postmaster general for LIC. This was back
in 2002, so it may be a different person now but you can probably check. You have to write to them - e-mailing and calling
does no good.

I got so fed up that I started sending him invoices for my missing magazines. I kept at it weekly until someone spoke to
our carrier. He hated me - told me that it wasn't their fault that the mags didn't fit in my box, blah blah blah.

My response: this is merchandise that I have already paid for. If it doesn't fit in the box, leave a call card and I'll pick it
up promptly.

But my friend had told me that the carriers get a lot of flack from their managers if they come back with anything but an
empty bag.

But here's the name/address I had then. Like I said, the location must be the same, but check the name:

Mike Menasche

46-02 21 Street

Post by: BoredTemp on September 21, 2009, 02:47:49 PM
More recently - the procedure is this.

1) file a complaint at your local post office with the supervisor on duty
2) call 800-275-8777 to file an official complaint
3) call the regional supervisor (718) 321-5570 to follow up with the complain number you receive from step 2.
4) if this fails - write to:Consumer Avocate, 475 L'enfant Plaza, Washington, DC 20260-2200
