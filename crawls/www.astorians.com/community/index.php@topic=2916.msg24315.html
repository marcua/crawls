Well, before moving here in July from Boston, I was on the faculties of Northeastern University, Gordon College, and the New England Conservatory (with whom I'm still involved)-yes, all at the same time. I also guest lectured at numerous schools, including Harvard and the California Institute for the Arts. I'm not an academic in the literal sense-I taught mostly performance studies and composition (with a little Jazz History thrown in).
I should be doing some lesson teaching privately here in the Fall (privately and at one or two colleges).
Personally, very little of what I do professionally I actually learned in school-to be honest, I tend to discourage students from pursuing higher education (especially advanced degrees) if they want to perform. I always believed that experience was far more important then reading about what others did-needless to say, this didn't make me particularly popular with college administrations.
Which is why I left more college affiliations then most people have in a lifetime (I've quit 9 faculty positions-granted all part time, but for the same reasons-not enough emphasis on experience and helping individual students gain insight to the reality of a performing career).
