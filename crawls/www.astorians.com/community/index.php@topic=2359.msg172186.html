I had a good end seat this morning, but the trip was ruined by these two very short ladies.  They couldn't reach the bar above the seats, so they had to share the lower pole next to my seat.  The thing was, they clearly didn't know about personal space.  Now I normally don't care if someone is close to me if the subway was crowded, but it's just weird when it is not, which was it was this morning.

So anyway, they are standing WAY too close to me.  Like, one of their stomachs was in my face.  She could take two steps back.  They begin having a conversation right over my head because one of them is leaning against the door while the other one is hanging onto the pole.  Then the one leaning against the door rests her hands on the small bar right next to my head, so her hands are REALLY close to my head.  AND SHE BEGINS PICKING HER NAILS!  I turn to her and gave her a shocked look and she quickly pulled back.  Then when I got up for my stop, I turn to her and her hands are back, VERY close to my head!  Like I turned and I had a hand right next to my eye.  She pulled back again.  Jeez, how could some people be so unaware of that?

I got on the N at Canal Thursday night and sat down when I was assaulted by the smell of garlic. It was almost like inhaling a pickle. I was all WTF?
