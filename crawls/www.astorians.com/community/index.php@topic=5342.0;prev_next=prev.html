If you are in a building with 4 or more units the landlord cannot raise the rent more than is allowed by the rent regulations, which is set by the state.

If you stay past your lease in New York City it becomes a month to month.  you can break the lease by giving notice as follows.  if you want to leave at the end of April you must give your notice before April starts.

The landlord can make you sign a lease for the terms of the initial lease, with any statutory rent increase if he wants by serving notice on you.

If your landlord did not, you have a month to month buddy, enjoy.

Quote from: NYCMacUser on February 27, 2007, 11:34:02 AM
How new does it have to be? That really sucks. That means that not all tenants are covered equally.

New buildings, generally anything built after the early 1970s, are only rent stabilized if the landlord chooses to accept tax breaks in return for placing the building under rent stabilization. In these cases the tax breaks are for a specified period of time, say 10 or 15 years, and when the tax breaks expire the rent stabilization status of the building goes with it and the landlord is allowed to raise the rent as much as he or she chooses.

In response to the original poster, if the apartment is rent stabilized then the landlord is technically required to give you a renewal lease, and you are required to either sign it or move out. If the apartment isn't rent stabilized then once the lease expired it became a month-to-month tenancy. This means that either the tenant or the owner has to give at least 30 days notice to move out. Note that if you pay your rent on the first of each month and you wanted to give notice today, you would be liable for rent through all of March. This would have been the case if you gave notice anytime after February 1 (when you could have moved out February 28).

Quote from: NYCMacUser on February 27, 2007, 03:06:26 PM
If a tenant wants one, can they ask for a lease renewal?

In a non-stabilized aparment everything is a negotiation between a landlord and a tenant. You can ask for a lease, but if the landlord refuses then the landlord refuses. The converse can also happen. There are a million scenarios that can happen regarding the amount of rent paid, leases, etc. It's all negotiation.

In a rent stabilized apartment the landlord is required to offer you a renewal lease 90-150 days before your present lease expires. If they don't, you can put in a complaint with DHCR who will force the landlord to provide the renewal lease to you. Of course I would also start with the landlord and ask them why the lease was late before I went for a formal complaint, but the option is there. If the rent stabilized landlord sends you a renewal lease and you don't sign it within 60 days (there's a part of the lease renewal form where you can specify that you won't be renewing, but you still have to return it within 60 days stating that) it is either grounds for eviction, or if the landlord so chooses they can make it an implied 1 year renewal lease because you failed to respond.

Hope this helped  :-)
