WARNING: DO NOT MOVE INTO THIS BUILDING!!

All the residents are facing major problems. I'm the one with the posting from October in a different topic. When I first moved in, in Sept, the issues seemed minor.

But by Dec. it started to become clear to all of us residents the construction problems causing all of our heating bills to be around the range of $300 - $600! Ridicolous and way over priced for apartments our size. 

The building got sold to new owners. The entire transaction was very shady, and the new owners are an investment company that hired a mgmt company to take care of the building.

Mgmt company isn't able to address any questions in regards to the resident's complains about the heating and electricy bills and faulty construction.

Besides say they have hired an engineering company and issue will be resolved eventually.

The balcony doors swing open due to the vents blowing air through the buidling!!

Lots of people got sick due to these conditions and when I lived there I was always freezing. I was way too scared to turn on my heat due to the high cost.

I moved out already! Many tenants have with held rent in hopes we get answers to our issues. And a few our considering moving out as well.

How did they help with this situation. They sent the residents letters saying they need to pay up within three days or move out! When did it become legal to issue three day evaction notices wihout a judgement from a judge. IT DIDN'T BUT THAT is Astoria NW for you!!

It is terrible conditions, and these apartments are way over priced for what they are worth.

Again I warn you, DO YOURSELF A FAVOR, DO NOT MOVE THERE! 

And no this is not just one upset resident, i wish! We have a facebook group and goolge group dedicated to discussing our issues. Everyone is suffering!

i have to agree. these apartments SEEM like a dream come true - balconies, washer/dryer, dishwasher, etc.... but wait until the winter

i have NEVER been so cold in an apartment while STILL being slammed with $300+ gas bills. there is NO insulation in this building and we are responsible for paying for gas, which out heating units use a ton of. i could see my breath inside the apartment when i got home waiting for it to warm up. over 50+ tenants got together having this issue, so i know it's not just me. i would come home in the winter and spend an hour and  half in the bathtub because it was the only way to stay warm. i could see my breath...

origionally the building was owned by criterion, but they sold it (during the middle of winter and with all these issues) to TCAM property. and even though TCAM is now in charge, they claim no responsibility (WHO may i asks, spends millions on a building without knowing all the problems going on in it!). Now that tenants want compensation for outrageous bills, they are coming down hard on the tenants. they say they will fix the problems - but it's been 4+ months since they told us that and nothing has happened. all these people care about is money. i would break the lease early if i could, but they've made abundantly clear that we can't! so i am stuck

it's summertime now, and these problems seem smaller... but NYC winter lasts oct - april so i would think twice before being lured in. only move in if you can afford to pay $200-$650 (yes, someone's bill was that high for a 2 bedroom) extra a month for gas.

DO NOT LIVE HERE! and if you already do live here, i am sure you agree
