This is not an Astoria option, but if you're downtown and it's convenient, OM Yoga has a Basics class which is great.

I've taken it twice because while I've been doing pilates for about 8 years, I've only done yoga a handful of times.
Very nice studio, laid back, no pressure to buy anything. And Basics gives you a really good 'feel' for yoga, pace is
slower and the instructor always asks what level each person is at and what they want out of the class. I really like it.

They offer it most days, if not daily. So if you're in the 12th & B'way area, check them out:

www.omyoga.com

I did this beginner series years ago.  It will set you up well technically and teach basics of yoga philosophy.  There were only 5-7 of us in the class so there was a lot of individual attention.  The people (instructor and students) are friendly and approachable.  

http://nyc.laughinglotus.com/ongoing_absbeg.html

Just a disclaimer, I don't practice regularly.  Typically, I drop-in my local gym's sessions during the winter months.  For me, it's the most cost effective and since I'm not hardcore it satisfies my needs.  That's not something I would ever suggest to a beginner because you won't get the proper instruction or attention in a class of 20+.  And, if you don't know what you are doing it's easy to hurt yourself.  I think you are on the right track--find a yoga studio for your first few sessions. Be sure to tell them ahead of time you are a beginner so they put you in the right class.
