Re: Freezepeach & Donations
« Reply #12 on: May 06, 2008, 08:18:13 PM »
I can't speak for the Freeze Peach, but it's not unheard of for a small business to seek donations.  For instance, Ravelry.com just raised over $50K from their users, more than enough to cover outstanding start-up costs and an expansion of their business in a user organized drive.  As a Rav user it stunned me really, how much value people placed on the site.

Now I doubt anyone associated with the Freeze Peach expects that kind of response.  However, for a small business like the Peach, the cost of operating compared to their proceeds likely means that they could use a little extra help when they want to improve the place.  As several people noted in the thread after the Freeze Peach was robbed last month, it's tough for small shops like it to survive in Astoria right now.

I love the Peach and frequent it often, so it's no hardship to give some money to improve the place, and help insure that it will be there down the road.  For me, it is a bit of a community center.  So I value it.  A LOT.  :-)  In fact, several of us have been discussing fundraising options just like this.  I'm glad that for those of us who've requested it, Matt has given us the option to help out voluntarily.

Hello All,

I am right now, as I write this, hanging out at Freeze Peach and the place is more lively than it's been in months!  There are tons of lights, and things are happening.  There are wonderful candy bars on the counter for sale and great music.   It's really happenin' so stop by for tea!   

Do make a point of giving even $5.00 to them.  I just fixed the PayPal button on the home page of their website: www.freezepeach.org.   Freeze Peach is a real community service.  Today I went to Oleput and was really upset to find the "No Laptops" signs all over - this was something I noticed after I paid $3.00 for an iced tea.   They were a bit uppity about it too.   So I went to Starbucks where I promptly paid $11 for three hours to be online.  That's when I found that Freeze Peach is open for business.   

My point is that there is no place in Astoria, or the City for that matter, like Freeze Peach.  It's a really valuable service so let's all do what we can to make it work.  I'm broke right now myself - really broke -but I just gave them $10.00.   I'm going for a job interview tommorrow and if I get the job I'm giving $20 the minute they say they want me.

I applied for the job while I was here, I answered a series of questions while I was here and they called me last week.   So this place means so much to me.

Keep it lively, keep it in town, keep it up!
