The train ground to a halt between the Ditmars and Astoria Blvd. stations and sat there for four hours.  I just got into work a few minutes ago: my hair is now white and I can’t get my teeth to stop chattering.  A coworker told me Kung Fu just died in Bangkok, and I’m thinking I’ve seen better days on the train.

Re: wanting to sit when you see someone taking up more than one seat...

If I want to sit down, and someone has their bag(s) taking up a seat, I always just ask politely with a smile. No one has ever given me an issue about it.
However, I got an unusual response last week...

It was evening rush hour (probably 6:00-ish), and I got on a W at 23rd St. There was one unoccupied seat, which was being taken up by a guy's plastic shopping bag, which he was holding onto. So I went over, gestured to the seat, and said "excuse me, may I sit?". Guy took his headphones off because he didn't hear me, so I politely repeated myself. His response: "I'll let you sit if you tell me where you're from". I was in no mood, so I rolled my eyes, said "ok, buddy", turned and stood across the way until another seat opened up down the car a couple stops later.

Cheesy pickup line, or ploy to hog 2 seats? If it was the latter, it worked  :roll:
