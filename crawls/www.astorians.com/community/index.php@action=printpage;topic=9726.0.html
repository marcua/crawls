Hello there - I know that once upon a time, there was a Mano's Papaya on Steinway and 30th, and it was truly heartbreaking when it closed. 

When it came time to quench a craving for a "street-grade" frank served up in an actual "restaurant/building" (as opposed to an open air cart) Mano's was the "go to" on Steinway when I first moved here.  Wow! greasy frozen french fries and cheese, cheap low quality burgers, and the legendary egg sandwiches with faux bacon served at all hours kept me coming back time and time again as I made my way from the subway to home in both directions. I really miss the place.  I was always amazed on how the guy that ran the grill could make two perfectly square egg and cheese sandwiches with one pour of his bowl and a couple flips of the spatula.
Is any Astorian but me upset about losing such cool little piece of architecture on Steinway Street?  Mano's Papaya made that triangle unique.  Now it's been remade as a Bank of America ATM in the typical corporate style of a lot aluminum. 
Why couldn't the Bank just move in, gut the interior, and leave the exterior alone?  Now, the corner is so very boring.
