I love JJ's. It's especially great for "unadulterated" sushi (where the fish isn't covered in a pile of spicy sauce, tempura flakes, green clovers and lucky horseshoes). I've ordered nigiri and sashimi style sushi from them a number of times and have been very happy.

Their wonton soup is great too... the broth is more like chicken stock than the yellow tinted water you get at many places. It also tastes like they finish it off with a little bit of butter. It's a really comforting soup for chilly weather like this! :)

« Reply #6 on: November 20, 2008, 02:14:22 AM »
So I just went to JJs tonight for the first time.  The pot stickers are everything they are hyped up to be and more.  Dessert (banana spring rolls w/ vanilla ice cream) was also quite good.  The rolls, however, did not seem to so clearly be "the best in Astoria." 

Don't get me wrong, they were very tasty and I will definitely be going back.  I just dont think JJs was the end-all-be-all of sushi in this neighborhood.  In fact, I like Kitaku (on 23rd Ave, b/w 29th and 31st) a little more... and they are cheaper.
