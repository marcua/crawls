Art may be necessary for you to have a certain kind or style of life, but it is not necessary for life itself--the physical which keeps the body alive.

I think it's important to keep in mind that all of these things we're debating about (jewelry, coffee, brownies, dog treats) are things which we are lucky to have to debate about. There are people in parts of the world who can't argue about such things because they have to worry about sustaining life itself. It's not that we should feel bad or we shouldn't have these things. I support things that are necessary to the kinds of lives people want, but it's important to remind oneself that these are not the things that make life possible, these are the things that enrich the life once its physical needs are met. These are first world problems and we're really lucky to have them and not the others that we can.

I'd also like to say that in my opinion, the most vital and important of art in ones life is that which makes nor demands money. The art that you make and that you share with those in your lives is the most vital, at least to me, and that doesn't have to cost a thing.

As for the bags, even buying in bulk from Pennsylvania (with all of the pulp refineries there, it is a center for paper products, including a neat outfit called Dunder Mifflin based out of Scranton), nice, attractive, debbie sized bags run 22 cents per unit before tax and delivery, whereas cheap plastic "Thank you" bags run a penny or two.  Is it overcharging to pass this luxury along to the consumer?

The ability of humans to see in the abstract and conjure questions beyond the here and now has come with the price tag of needing art for our mental well being.  But what is art?  What makes it so necessary?  Is it limited only to things produced for art, either art for art's sake or art for money's sake (or a combo of the two)?  I certainly don't think so and I think this is what the disagreement is based on.

For me, art produced for either is wholly unnecessary, be it paintings, jewlery or music concerts/cd's, because they are artistic expressions for the creator, not me (though creating the art itself is extremely satisfying).  I find my own art, be it hearing the muffled wind in my ears at the beach, a header goal in soccer, multicolored leaves on the pavement, the smell of aquanet hair spray (weird, but I grew up in the 80's and I have good associations with it), old derelict factories, clam sauce, et cetera.  It is from those things I get my mental well being.  The art I need mixes in with life so much that it is hard to tell the two apart.
