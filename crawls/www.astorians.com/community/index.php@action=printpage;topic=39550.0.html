Post by: yippee1999 on November 15, 2016, 03:44:18 PM
Try walking on a nice day, up and down the various side streets.  I imagine you'll encounter a resident or two coming or going  from their house or car.  Approach in a very friendly way, asking if they happen to know of anyone in the nabe that might be looking to rent an apartment.

There are also some small, older apt buildings that typically list the name and phone number of the in-house property manager (no broker fee) and/or the building Super's info.  That's another way to try and contact a building owner/manager directly to ask about vacancies.  In such buildings there obviously won't be driveway parking for you but then, you could always consider getting rid of your car.  (With the MTA, taxis, Uber, Zipcar, etc., you can easily manage.  It's an adjustment for car-owners, but eventually very freeing to not have to deal with car expenses, parking, shoveling, etc.)

You can also check here for a list of Astoria buildings, and contact info/rental availability....  You will have to call these places often though, as newly-available apartments will constantly be becoming available...
