People thought they were gunshots -- it's not like a siren, which is necessary to warn that an emergency vehicle is coming, or the elevated subway which, despite still being loud, has gotten considerably quieter over the years. They were fireworks -- ILLEGAL in NY -- shot off in the middle of the night in a residential neighborhood. Not cool. It woke me up and I am not an insomniac. I assumed it was for Greek Easter, though, after living in this neighborhood for 30+ years.

The idea that it's once a year and it's a holiday is a slippery slope. Most fireworks displays occur at a more reasonable 9-10 p.m. If everyone started shooting off fireworks for every holiday in the middle of the night, there'd be fireworks going off constantly -- remember, this IS a very diverse neighborhood with many cultures and religions, there's always a holiday to celebrate. I don't recall the other Christian denominations setting off fireworks the week before.

This city is loud enough, why contribute to it?
