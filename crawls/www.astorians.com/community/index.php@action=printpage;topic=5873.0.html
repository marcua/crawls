Post by: Ditmartian on August 18, 2007, 03:42:12 AM
OK. Star Wars geekiness warning...

I have an meta idea abouit technology in the Star Wars universe... that the galaxy is actually engaged in a technological retreat, meaning that over time, less and less technology is being used. And eventually will resort to primitivism before it turns and begins advancing again toward what we now understand of technological innovation and application. Like an inverse parabola.

Think about it... The whole saga takes place in the past. "A long time ago" blah blah... And as someone said above, we're constantly reminded that the central conflict is humanity vs. technology. Or rather de-humanizing technology, which seems to be inevitable no matter how good the intentions. Men become anonymous clones to combat drone robots. Warriors are literally fused with machinery to make them more powerful.

I was playing one of my SW video games (I warned you, this is SW geekiness here  :mrgreen: ) which take place several thousand years before the films. And I'm noticing that there's the same, if not better technology to be used: weapons, armor, ships, droids, and so on. And the archetypes in the game (Sith lords, Jedi, others) seemed way more powerful than what we know of them through the films.

Seemed kinda odd to me that over thousands of years so little would change. Then I thought, what if it's some sort of technology entropy?

Central to the game is a once-super advanced race that held immense power through some form of 'alien technology'. Something like 15,000 years before the timeframe of the films. But their race eventually failed and their technology mostly lost. So you see, the galaxy starts off at a technological high point, and then it's all downhill from there...

And yes, I gave it way too much thought perhaps, but such is the manner in which this brain works.
