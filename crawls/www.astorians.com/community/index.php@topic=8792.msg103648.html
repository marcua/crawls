It's worth supporting local shops when they deserve your support. In my experience, PC isn't one of those places.

Try B&H Photo or Adorama in Manhattan. Both pro (and amateur), internationally-known photo shops that would find it as crazy as you did to not allow someone to hold a camera before buying. And employees who know what they're talking about, and can likely point you at a better choice (not necessarily a more expensive one either).

B&H is quite a spectacle actually, and worth visiting almost just to see it, though that means big crowds often. Adorama is smaller and cozier and a shorter walk from the train (unless you can easily get to the A/C/E at 34th).

http://bhphoto.com/
9th ave 33rd/34th street. N/R/W to 34th and a 5-10 min walk.

http://www.adorama.com/
42 West 18th Street , N/R/W to 14th

Note that since they're both run by orthodox jews their weekend is not the norm: they close Friday around noon and are closed Saturdays. But you don't have to be jewish to shop there ;-)

If you know exactly what you want and don't need any advice, I imagine a BB or CC would be fine.

I have never had anything but pleasant experiences with PC. But, I would only use them for appliances. I always arrange to go there or call them when I know that one particular salesperson will be there. Her name is Nancy. I don't know her last name. I've bought 2 refrigerators, a gas stove, a high-end front-load, under-counter washing machine, 2 microwave ovens, 2 low-end televisions, and 2 air conditioners. I was pleased both with the price and with their delivery and set-up. Most recently, when my refrigerator dropped dead, I went online to see what they had, called Nancy, and I had a new refrigerator in my kitchen overnight . . . and it was $250 less expensive than any other place.

However, when it comes to anything that could be bought from B&H, that's where I go. Not only is their floor staff extremely knowledgeable and professional, their telephone staff is the very best around. They talked me through setting up my first digital camera with a sense of humor and followed up a few days later to find out if there was anything else they could do. I've also bought a high-end TV from them, 2 CD players, 2 DVD burner/players, and another high-end digital camera, to name a few of the things that I remember. I am not the touchy-feely kind of person, so I have no problem first checking with Consumer Reports and then going online and ordering something from B&H. Their regular standard delivery via UPS arrives overnight. I especially love their open box or used items. Talk about cost effective!
