Post by: Threemoons on January 05, 2008, 05:39:43 PM
Hey all.  I, too, was there last night.  My take on the place:

Upsides:

*Food was delicious, different, and had "just right" portions
*My Margarita served "up" was fantastic and strong, and not full of filler to weaken it like a lot of places pour "up" drinks.  It made it worth the extra dollar.
*Gorgeous atmosphere and furniture
*Bathrooms are actually on the same floor as the restaurant (this is a big issue not so much for me but for some of my mobility-impaired friends and family)

Downsides:

*Strictly amateur night on the service--took me forever to get a waitress, and then, to get a glass of water.  Also, my waitress spoke bad enough English and didn't know the menu well enough to tell the difference between buffalo oysters and buffalo wings.   I had to point to the menu and say "NO, BUFFALO OYSTERS!!" a few times.
*A contributing factor to the above may have been the volume level.  Music was too loud.
*I usually dine solo in Astoria--if I dine out with friends, we usually meet up after our jobs in Manhattan.  As such, I had every single service person in the place ask me if I was dining alone, and I got the feeling that at first, the  matire'd was trying to shunt me off to the crowded bar area.  Granted, I would have LOVED to have eaten at the bar--it's always more social for solo diners-- but that area's current configuration would have made it impossible.
*Prices a bit high for the 'hood.

Would I go back?  YES.  I had the buffalo oysters, the guac, and two margaritas for a light dinner and it was the best thing I'd done for myself all week.  The guacamole was a super-special standout and was just perfect, and the oysters were like nothing I've ever had.

My suggestions if the management is reading this:

--Get your staff to learn the menu
--Get water on the table PRONTO and don't make me wave around like a drowning person to get more--you serve spicy food; stay on top of it.
--My money is just as green as the next person's; if you don't want to give 2-tops to solo diners (which I DO NOT recommend unless you want me to never come back) then at least expand the seating at the bar area so that the people sitting at the railing behind the bar aren't butt-cheek to butt-cheek with the people sitting on the stools behind them.  If you DO decide to seat solo diners, don't try to shunt us off near the door or have every last person ask me if I am expecting someone else. Note that I have NEVER had this kind of treatment eating alone anywhere else in the area.  Then again, Fatty's, Crescent on Vine, and most of the other places have large and well-situated bar areas that are usually packed with solo diners.  Take the cue.
