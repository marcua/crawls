I'm getting tired of the 3D craze myself.  The only times I've thought it's added something to the film was with Avatar and How to Train Your Dragon (because there are a lot of flying scenes so it's like a ride).  I think it's a fad and a way for Hollywood to make money.  They charge so much extra to see these films and now tickets are $20.  It's crazy.  No wonder no one goes to the movies anymore.  A family could easily spend $100 seeing a 3D film.  With films costing so much now, I weigh whether or not the film is worth going to the theater for or just waiting for the DVD to hit Netflix.  And with rude patrons at most theaters, it makes it even worse.  I'd rather just watch my netflix from the comfort of my own home.

My other gripe - how Hollywood is successfully ruining every childhood 2D cartoon by making it a 3D film (whether or not it's for 3D glasses or just 3D computer generated as opposed to 2D hand-drawn).  They've ruined Scooby, Smurfs, Garfield, Chipmunks, and now Yogi Bear.  And the characters are butt ugly.  They were so much better hand-drawn.  I'm not opposed to 3D movies but at least have an original idea.  And don't make butt ugly characters.
