Wow, I wish I'd had your experience, mangopaco! I've been and was thoroughly underwhelmed.


I agree. I went a few weeks ago and was fairly underwhelmed.
I went with a friend and started by ordering a couple drinks, that were put in at the bar, and then promptly ignored by our waitress. After waiting, and seeing them at the bar, and watching our waitress walk past them for 10 minutes, we went up and got them. At some point one of the other waitresses realized we were being essentially ignored, so she took over our table, and the service instantly got better.
We split a cobb salad that was pretty good. The produce was fresh, and for some reason the salad had a slight sweetness to it that I'm not used to in cobb salads. All in all, a pretty solid salad.
For mains I got a burger, and I believe my dining partner got a steak (my memory isn't the greatest) and we split a side of onion rings. I didn't try her dish, but my burger was okay at best. I ordered it medium and it was more toward the well done end of the spectrum, and lacked any seasoning, which honestly only needs to be salt and pepper. The onion rings on the other hand were gloriously oversalted, to the point that I was not a fan (and I love some onion rings). The fries were pretty standard steak cut fries, no complaints, but nothing special either.
All in all, the space is amazing, and I'd definitely go back for drinks, and I'm sure after a few drinks I would be much less nit picky about the food.

This place rules! Best things about William Hallett:
 
1) Best grits I have ever eaten (and I lived in the south for 2 years). So buttery and cheesy and dreamy. Perfect side dish.
 
2) They have Ithaca Apricot Wheat on tap- never seen that before. Its an amazing beer, and great out of the tap.
 
3) Every single entree I tried was awesome. I ordered the steak sandwich, which was awesome, but I tasted the Turduken as well as a few other dishes, and everything was great.
 
4) Cool decor- I feel like I am in a speakeasy or something, which makes drinking more fun. Yay!
 
This is my new favorite restaurant/bar in Astoria since Old Man Bar Hellsgate on the Park closed and Emilio was wrongfully banished from the area. If you havent gone yet- go tonight!
 
One thing though, dont expect a true traditional restaurant. This is definitely a classy bar with a small area for sitting patrons to eat. But trust me, its worth it. Now that I have typed this up my stomach is yelling at me to go get some grits and Ithaca Apricot. Dammit....

I hadn't been here in a long time and thought we would give it one last chance.  And that it was ...


I will say this:
Service was friendly and attentive.
Great atmosphere
Interesting beers


food - it all looks good on paper.  But they just miss the mark.  I had posted earlier that we had re-named this place "William DON'T HAVE IT" - that's because each time we had gone they were out of something we wanted.  Can I tell you that was the case last night, too?  WTH? 


We tried the foie gras.  I always love foie gras.  This time, I just ate the liver.  It was served on hockey pucks (aka 10 day old stale bread) and with a kumquat compote.  I have had foie gras all over the world.  Kumquat doesn't pair well with it.  And toast points are toast points.  These giant hunks of unbreakable bread were a joke - for a moment I thought I might have to call my dentist.


I also ordered the white wine/parsley moules frites.  It wasn't parsley - it was thyme.  That was fine - but maybe say something.


Everything else we ordered was the same - just not as good as we were hoping it would be.


So, we have decided to just not eat there ever again.  We would go there for drinks but that's it.
