Hey there welcome to Astoria and congratulations on the pregnancy. 

Delivering in Manhattan is completely realistic.  Traffic can definitely be a hassle - but look at those people in the parts of the country who live 3 hours away from their hospital.  Manhattan is totally doable.  I just delivered at NY Presbyterian in Aug.  Unfortunately I ended up being induced so the ride over was a nonissue.  I spoke to someone recently who did the ride to St Luke's... I believe she said that the ride over was the worst part of her entire labor. 

You're going to pay for a private room anywhere in the city.  On the other hand...  they're not necessarily going to throw your husband out of a non-private room at 11 pm - but they're not going to tell you that.

I spent 6!!??? nights at the hospital when I was delivering.  The first two were in labor and delivery.  Private room.  but no 2nd bed.  My husband could have slept in the recliner that first night... but we knew he'd be more comfortable and get better sleep at home in bed.  And because I needed him to be 100% with me the next day - we decided for him to spend that first night at home.  I hadn't gone in to active labor yet.  They didn't start inducing me until 10pm. 

The next 4 nights I was in a double - and I just lucked out and didn't get a roommate until the last night.  Again - there was no bed for my husband to sleep in (they don't let you touch the 2nd bed in the double) and we agreed that getting real sleep was more important than being there overnight.  He would stay late - and get there first thing in the am... but he went home to get about 5 - 7 hours a night.  He did taxi back and forth - so the commute didn't take him too long.  And NY Pres is a little closer than St. Lukes. 

The last night - my roommate's husband was there pretty late.  It would have been fine with me had he stayed the night - but he didn't. 

I think it's all just luck.  If you get a roommate - hopefully she'll be low key and not care if there's a man in the room.  Sell her this way - it's another person there to help soothe the crying baby.

having the dad sleep in the hospital sounds really important and all - but when you're there - you may just realize that he'll get much better sleep at home - and you're really going to need him to be wide awake. 


aside: my sister in law, her husband and the new baby walked into their house after arriving home from the hospital - they were all exhausted.  a few minutes later the husband tells her - "oh Honey - I just took a couple of sleeping pills - I REALLY NEED SOME SLEEP."  She didn't kill him.  She's a better woman than I. 

Good luck

I have signed up to deliver at St. Lukes Roosevelt on 10th ave btwn 58th and 59th street....hope to make it to the hospital on time =)
