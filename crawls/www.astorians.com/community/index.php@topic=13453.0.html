Why I am mad at Barnes & Noble
« on: December 22, 2008, 05:36:39 PM »
I just wanted to share the interesting shopping experience I had with ordering a Christmas gift online through Barnes & Noble.

On their website it said the item I ordered at the beginning of last week would ship within 24 hours and arrive within 2-3 business days because the order qualified for "Fast Delivery."

I added a little msg saying "Merry Christmas!" to it and had it sent to my parents house (it was a gift for my mother) with the assumption she would receive it last Friday or today at the latest. Then I received a message saying it would be shipped out on Friday 12/19 (way later than their promised 24 hours later, but eh - it should still arrive before Christmas so I didn't care).

I then received a message on SATURDAY (a day after it was supposed to be shipped) saying that it had not been shipped yet and it would be shipped within 1-5 additional business days. I logged in to check the new projected SHIPPING date and it was 12/26 (i.e. the day _after_ Christmas and wouldn't arrive until sometime midweek after Christmas).

Now, I was down in Florida visiting my parents at this point (I was hoping it would arrive while I was down visiting them) and the delay email included this message: "We assume that you still want to purchase this merchandise.  However, if you would like to cancel this portion of your order, you may do so online at blah blah blah"

So I immediately went online on Saturday within an hour of receiving this message (it arrived around 2pm) and canceled the order (around 3pm) and then proceeded to go out to the store and buy the gift to give to her in person.

The next morning I received the following message:

-------------------------------
Dear Michael,
                       
We have received your request to cancel your order # #########.

Unfortunately, we are unable to complete this request, as your order has entered the shipping process or has already shipped. We apologize for any inconvenience.

We look forward to your next visit.

Sincerely,
-------------------------------

So I go online thinking maybe they HAD shipped it on Friday and they had just messed up. Nope, still said projected shipping date 12/26.

So I decided to call their customer service line and inquired about it. They said that it had "entered the shipping process" and could not be cancelled. I once again asked when the estimated SHIPPING date was. They told me 12/26. I then got a little mad. The item was not available to be shipped until SIX days after I attempted to cancel it because it wasn't in stock, yet it had MAGICALLY already entered the shipping process and couldn't be cancelled despite an email saying that I could, in fact, cancel it because they dropped the ball and wouldn't be able to ship it for 1-5 business days. I told the woman that was BS and asked to talk to a supervisor.

The supervisor reiterated what the original person said. Said they were very sorry, but they couldn't cancel the shipping process on an item they DIDN'T HAVE IN STOCK and that WOULDN'T BE SENT FOR 6 MORE DAYS, ALMOST TWO WEEKS AFTER IT WAS SUPPOSED TO BE SHIPPED OUT because it had ENTERED THE SHIPPING PROCESS. Now, I hate things that don't make sense. This did not make sense. How could something they didn't currently have in stock somehow already have "entered" the shipping process (whatever that means), and which wouldn't be sent out for 6 days NOT be cancelable. She couldn't answer that question.

She just kept repeating that they couldn't stop its shipment and that I would have to either mail it back to them or refuse acceptance of the item when it arrived or return it manually to a barnes & noble store when it finally arrived in order to get my money back (now remember I live in NYC and I had it sent to my parents house in Florida as a Christmas present so this would cause difficulties).

I won't lie, I was not very nice to the supervisor. I didn't say anything mean, but my tone of voice was not pleasant while I explained that the situation was complete and utter bullsh;t. After hearing my rather unhappy and mildly threatening tone of voice she then changed her story and said that it would magically be shipped out the next day (Monday, a.k.a. today). So, apparently, she _could_ change the shipping date, but not cancel it? And send me something "out of stock" (i.e. they're now bumping someone else's order to fill mine because I was an irate customer) which means they DO have control over the shipping process at that stage (or else they'd be sending me one today and Friday).

Of course, I had already bought the item in the store (and received it immediately! should teach me something) and the customer service supervisor was well aware of this and i repeated it and then sarcastically told her how incredibly happy I was with the service and I couldn't wait to share the news (to family, friends, co-workers and you good people).

So I'm doing my part to spread the news. The only reason they c(w)ouldn't cancel the order was to get my money in the hopes I would be too lethargic to send back the items or would still want them a week after Christmas and find that preferable to buying it in a store. i.e. shooting themselves in the foot for a small immediate monetary gain. Because you can be sure I buy books, lots of books, and I won't be doing it through them ever again and I'm telling everyone I know about my horrible experience. Do not support terrible customer service. Buy from Borders/strand/anywhere else that fine books and dvds are sold.

Thank you,
Mike

B&N.com's shipping service is TERRIBLE.  They use some outside, random shipping company (not a well-known one like UPS, FedEx, or DHL...name escapes my mind) that is - in my opinion- incredibly incompetant. 

I ordered a test-prep book for a NY State Teacher Certification Exam I had to take last Spring.  I ordered it with plenty of time to receive it and prepare for my test (about 3-4 weeks in advance).  I ordered it to be delivered to me at work (midtown Manhattan). After not receiving my package for a few days, I tracked it and realized that the book was indeed delivered, but to the wrong floor of my building.  Someone else at another company signed for it and took it.  I don't know who they were (the signature scan was un-readable) or what the hell they wanted with my Kaplan LAST/CST/CSW exam prep manual, but I digress.

Well, my test date was rapidly approaching so I called B&N customer service, explained the situation, and was told to call the shipping company.  I called the shipping company and they told me to re-order the book through B&N.  Without wanting to pay another $24.95, I started getting frustrated and was shuffled back and forth once more and finally was told - by the shipping company - that they'd work on re-tracking the order and I should call back their next business day.  This was on a Friday, so 3 days later, I called back and they said because they aren't open on the weekend, no progress was made.  I was told to call back the next business day.  Tuesday rolls around and I call, furious, and am told that they sent the messenger back to my building to try and locate the exact person the package was delivered to, and I would hear back from them on their next business day with the results of the delivery person's findings.  Wednesday rolls around, I don't hear from them, so I call, FUMING, and am told - SURPRISE SURPRISE - the delivery person was unable to exactly locate the book, so they are going to contact B&N for me and have it re-sent, no charge.

I finally got my book - about 2 weeks after I ordered it and 1 week before my exam.  The whole thing was caused by a mess-up on their end (I double checked my original order and never made any errors in the floor # of my building) but it was drawn out for way too long, in my opinion.  The representatives over the phone were always very polite and cordial, but I felt like what should have taken 5 minutes (re-order the book for FREE over the phone and process a new delivery) took 2 weeks.  I won't use B&N.com ever again. 

I passed the exam, btw.
