Thank you everyone - for your support and interest.  These are my thoughts - the folks in Astoria who have been 'running the show' have been around for years.  Believe me, cronyism is rampant.  I do not necessarily mean our representatives, who we can vote out of office if we wish, but the folks who belong to the Associations, Boards etc.

There is a new wind blowing in Astoria.  People moving in are concerned with environment, health, beautifying the area, clean ups etc.

I am an Astorian, here since 1973 so I have been witness to the status quo and I am now experiencing something new and exciting.  I am delighted with the folks who are moving into the area, and I do not feel that they are simply 'transients'.  I believe that if the people moving here were able to invest in the area, they would.  It has simply become much more expensive these days to buy a home here.

Rather than joining the same old board and civic meetings, I would like our Representatives to meet and chat with the new residents.  They  need to see what's happening and what our concerns are.

I do not mean to be disrespectful of the older residents, in fact, I consider myself one of them.  I feel however, that while remaining respectful of the past, we must embrace change and adapt to new experiences.  The old and the new can learn from each other.
