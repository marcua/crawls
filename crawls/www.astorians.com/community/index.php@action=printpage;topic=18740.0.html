Any ideas?

Can I sublet a co-op?

This depends. Co-ops are not truly "owned" by the purchaser. Rather, shares of stock in co-op buildings are purchased, giving the "owner" tenancy rights in the unit purchased. The lease is known as a "proprietary lease" and operates generally the same way as a regular lease. Thus, the shareholder of a co-op is also a tenant. However, co-op tenants do not have statutory rights to sublet like other tenants. Whether a co-op unit may be sublet depends on the policies of the co-op board.

Some co-ops allow shareholder/tenants to sublet their apartments. Others strictly forbid or regulate such practices. Co-op sublets are not subject to rent stabilization. The rights and obligations of the parties are generally spelled out in the sublet agreement subject to the terms of the shareholder/tenant’s "proprietary lease" and the co-op’s governing rules.

Title: Re: Illegal subletter evicted with 4 days notice --- legal?
Post by: 28Grand on July 02, 2010, 07:05:11 AM
This doesn't seem to be a typical 'illegal' sublet where a tenant tries to get one over on his or her landlord. It's appears to be a case of a landlord taking advantage of his business partners, the other owners in the building.

If subletting isn't allowed in terms of the by-laws that govern the building where the apartment is located then it's shame on the owner. This is common with coops and it’s the owner/landlord who violated the terms of his lease -- not your friend the tenant (and the owner knew it hence no lease).

From the link that BentleysDad posted, by law, a person has four business days (not counting weekends and holidays) to vacate an apartment after being served an eviction notice (that was news to me). Your friend does have legal recourse though to stop the eviction but the clock is ticking and he needs to go to court and file papers to stop it so maybe a lawyer is a good idea.

Most likely your friend would get a hearing and a judge will decide how long to give him to find another place to live -- it could be four days or it possibly could run up to six months.

It doesn’t matter if the apartment is rent regulated or in a co-op or in a house.

The coop board and what they do is the owner/landlord’s problem. I imagine they have the right to fine him or her for violating the building’s by-laws by illegally subletting. Your friend should keep out of it and concentrate his efforts on finding a new place to live because he will have to go eventually.

Lesson learned: sign a lease!

I hope it works out for your friend DnB.
