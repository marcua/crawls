A date with Astoria
« on: June 12, 2008, 12:48:26 PM »
Well, a friend of mine recently moved to the neighborhood and she (gasp!) doesn't like it! This is very upsetting to me because I told her how awesome Astoria is and really encouraged her to move here. Thus, on Saturday we are having a date with Astoria so she can fall in love with our 'hood too. She lives off the Astoria Blvd stop on the N/W and I live above Ditmars on 21st Ave, so we'll be focused on that general area. I want to do some fun things with her and show her some great places to eat/hang out/purchase fun items.

Already on the list are a trip to Socrates Sculpture Park in the AM for Yoga/Pilates and a visit to Astoria Park for people watching and some sunbathing. We'll also stop by Waltz Astoria, Bistro 33, Fatty's Cafe, Crescent and Vine, Mimi's Closet, various bakeries on Ditmars (you can never have enough bakeries, ha ha), Mojave, and Oleput. Oh, and Michael Angelo's for pizza and that natural food store right next to it.

She's already been to Neptune and the Sparrow and has seen, but not visited the Beer Garden.

Any other suggestions or glaring omissions? I'm especially interested in places around Astoria Blvd because I'm not as familiar with that area.

Thanks Astorians! I'll post again and let you all know how the date goes, although I'm sure it will be a rousing success.

A few places come to mind:

Kebab Cafe on Steinway (Ali's a neighborhood celebrity) and that whole Egyptian stretch south of Astoria Blvd.

Titan Foods on 31st St. (quite close to Astoria Blvd. station).

Valverde restaurant on 37th Ave. & Steinway is a hidden gem.

Costco, right next door to Socrates Sculpture Park, is some people's idea of a great attraction (including me!). 

For a general walking loop, 30th Avenue to Broadway, between Steinway and 31st Street, gives a good sense of this side of the neighborhood. 31st Avenue is more sparcely commercial, but there are some fun spots there, too.

Good luck!
