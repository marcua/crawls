Title: Re: Recommendations for a Good Seafood Restaurant
Post by: TJT on October 09, 2014, 05:13:32 PM
I'm not a fan of Kyklades at all.  Mainly the whole "tourist trap" thing they have going on.  I am a fan of Okeanos.  ATB24, I agree about the prices but I think I went by recently and saw they revamped the menu and are a little more down to earth now.  I could be wrong.  I also think they're great with fish but maybe don't know how to cook a steak.  We've been many times and loved it.  They do an amazing seafood chowder.  The fish is always fresh and prepared beautifully.  Vegetables and sides are also very good - like surprisingly good.  Atmosphere is decent and they have a full bar.  Another option not too terribly far from Ditmars (on 28th near Rapture) is Off the Hook.  Delicious.  Love that place.  A slightly more nightlife-ish vibe depending on the time you go, but nice place and delicious food.  Elias Corner is a good choice in terms of very fresh seafood.  But the atmosphere is old and beat up a bit.  The servers are famously grumpy.  There isn't really a menu and prices are relative to the fish ordered so things can be a surprise when the bill comes unless you ask everything up front, which their waitresses will love.  Consider Telly's on 23rd Avenue.  They just remodeled very nicely and even added a bar.  The only draw back is it attracts a lot of extended families.  The servers can get easily overwhelmed and you can feel forgotten.  If it's a good day, you can have a wonderful time there and a very delicious meal.  I have to mention it because it's really my favorite seafood place in Astoria but far from Ditmars.  It's Psari on 36th Avenue.  Great raw bar and always amazing whole fish and shelf fish options.  Atmosphere is upscale/casual if that makes sense.  The only thing is that it can be tight when crowded and maybe a little noisy but never that bad.

Flounder with crabmeat stuffing is something I order often.  This dish is very well done at Okeanos.  You will not be disappointed.  But there was mention of Gregory 26 Taverna.  Their crabmeat stuffing is truly extraordinary.  You have to try it to understand.  As for Okeanos, I think they do a poor job at boosting their own image.  Maybe online, Facebook, events, food specials, showing up more in Boro magazine, street fairs, etc.  I think the owner is a bit standoffish and doesn't bother much with marketing.  They have a full bar with a lot of stools.  If they played some upbeat music and made better use of lighting, people might be more inclined to go have a few drinks on a Fri/Sat night.  Their food is very good.  The service is very attentive and pleasant.  There's so much potential.  It's a shame.  But I think most people who dare to try it are really glad they did.
