[Probalby should change this thread to Aroma 31]  Tried it last night.  It's the same owner from Eco Table.  But new menu and new business model.  Which is good news because we had a delicious meal.  And everyone else there enjoyed their food, as far as I could tell.  I think they toned down the decor slightly but didn't do a gut renovations (which would have been a horrible waste).  The sausage appetizer served over cous cous is a must have.  Very unique and incredibly flavorful.  I had the mint tagliatelle with lamb ragout.  It was perfect.  The chef came to explain that the pasta is made there, by him.  It was a dish you won't find anywhere else and I really enjoyed it.  And the portion was more than I could finish and that doesn't happen much to me.  My wife had the roasted small chicken. He brines it first.  She was surprised how juicy and delicious.  Service was very helpful and friendly.  I think they might have gotten it right this time. I will be back for sure.  It's also a fun dining corner now with all the activity with Bareburger, Pachanga Patterson, and Cafe Bugatti.  Lots of foot traffic and activity but without the store shoppers of Broadway.  It's becoming a nice restaurant row.

I checked this place out and want to give them a thumbs up.  I started off with the Turkish Bean Salad and had the Butternut Squash, Smoked Pancetta and Sage Risotto as my main course.  My friend had the Stewed Octopus with Olives, Ouzo and Fava to start and the Red Snapper with Baby Clams, Merguez Sausage, and Romesco.  I didn't try my friend's dishes but he said they were really good.  What I had was really good too and was nicely seasoned with great flavors.  They give you warm pita and hummus to start which was also quite good.  The chef checked up on us a couple of times and was very pleasant as were the hostess and wait staff.  They will be getting their liquor license next month but are serving complimentary wine for now.  The space was comfortable.  The only thing I didn't like was the music.  They were playing the radio which was fine but hearing the commercials was distracting.  They just openend not too long ago so I'm sure they are still ironing out things.  All in all it was a good dining experience and the menu is more varied than what we have in the hood right now.  It's a nice addition to the neighborhood.  Go check it out.
