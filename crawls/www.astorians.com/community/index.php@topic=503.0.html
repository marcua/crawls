Assuming you're taking the subway to work, I think the key is how far you are from a grocery store. If you're happy walking a few blocks for groceries, no problem, as long as there's a grocery store within a few blocks. That's true for most of Astoria, but not for all of it...

I have a car for now, since I'm working in Connecticut, but I only drive it to other places in the city once every couple of weeks. When I start working in Manhattan this Fall, I'm selling my car. (Anyone wanna buy an '02 Elantra?)

RE: Car and groceries --

Most of the local neighborhood grocery stores will delivery, sometimes free (Trade Fair - I think) or for a small fee (Key Food).

Fresh Direct also delivers to the neighborhood (an online grocier).

There really isn't the need to rely on a car for grocery duties.

And there are always those Astoria granny carts.

As has been said, it really depends on how close you are to basic necessities and the subway, and if you plan on doing a lot of traveling to places that aren't by the subway. I've lived here for about 5 months with no car, and I love it. However, I live very close to the subway and on a block with tons of shopping. I have all sorts of grocers, drug stores, laundromat, bed&bath, dollar stores, restaurants, florists, etc. right outside my door, and the subway is convenient to where I work in Manhattan.
