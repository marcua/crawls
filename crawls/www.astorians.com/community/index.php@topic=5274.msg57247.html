Oh dear!

I suffered from a pre-ice sprained ankle a few weeks ago.  I landed myself in the ER the day after my new insurance kicked in (nice, right?) and an X-Ray confirmed that it wasn't broken, despite the fact that I couldn't put weight on it at the time.  They handed me a set of crutches and made me an appointment with a podiatrist and I figured not broken, no big deal, right?  (It didn't even look swollen.)  

I've learned a lot about ankles in the last three weeks, including that things that didn't seem like a big deal really are.  My foot was swollen in places I didn't notice, and I don't have a full range of motion.  The podiatrist referred me to physical therapy and also a follow up x-ray because frequently, ERs can miss minor breaks.  (Mine was okay.)

Anyway, I don't know what your situation is, but if it's purple, I wouldn't take "it's not broken" for an answer if you can help it.  There can still be lasting problems and a need for work on it.  My foot is feeling better now, but after almost three weeks I'm still having difficulty with balance and range of motion issues.  PT has been great for getting it back into place, though right now my whole leg is kinda burning from the work the PT just did on me tonight.

Feel better.  If you need to borrow crutches, I'm actually almost done with mine?  (I think?) If you wanted to borrow one (?) I could definitely spare a single.  Might hold on to the second in case it gets icy again - it was great for moving around over slush piles with optimal balance.

Oh, also, the mantra that my PT/podiatrists have been drilling into my head:  "Elevate and ice!  For god's sakes, ELEVATE AND ICE!!!!"
