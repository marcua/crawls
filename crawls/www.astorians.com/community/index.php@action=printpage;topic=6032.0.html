Quote from: Frappe on April 19, 2007, 09:10:27 AM
I'll echo Yaxpac's comments.

Jeez, do we have to be politically correct here too, when someone askes a frank question about the neighborhood? 

Statistically, the areas around the housing projects in Astoria/LIC have a fairly high crime rate.  We can argue for days and days why that it is, but for right now, it's reality.  In terms of drugs, for instance, which in turn often leads to violence, open-air sales are much more prevalent in the projects e.g. hallways, in front of buildings, in stairwells, than in the "nicer" areas of Astoria.  One can attribute other acts of crime, such as robbery, vandalism, gang assault to so many sociological and psychological factors, but the truth is that they are more prevalent in and around the housing projects.  

Personally, I'm not "scared" of the projects, having lived in the Martin Luther King Homes in the early 90's when kids were still robbing people for their sneakers and crack dealers controlled the hallways, but if I had a choice, and call me crazy, I would prefer not to live across the street from the Astoria Houses or Queensbridge Houses.  Currently, I live across the street from Woodside Houses, and they are considered "nice" and in the three months that I've been there, I've witnessed one very tense racial conflict, two hookers have propositioned me, and many small, street disputes that didn't turn into much.
