Icaraway seeds.

I use them all the time. One of my favorite meals is made with ground caraway seeds.

I buy a quart of fresh sauerkraut from the barrel on Orchard Street. Isour sauerkraut! I dump the whole quart, liquid included, into a 3 quart heavy saucepan. I add one small can of tomato paste and one large can of whole plum tomatoes. I grind up 3 tablespoons of caraway seeds, and dump them in. Then I decide which I want more; a few thick boneless pork chops; a (don't get grossed out) small, smoked beef tongue; a small smoked ham hock; sometimes a few cubed Kosher franks. Let it all simmer for a while (if there is meat . . . until it is cooked well) and mmmmmmm have a couple of really good meals. I eat it with boiled little red potatoes and I usually get a fresh pumpernickle bread to go with it. Leftovers freeze beautifully for another feast.

Well, I was wrong. They're not in the recipe (All those C-spices sound alike to me!). But maybe you could experiment....

Hummus (from A New Way to Cook by Sally Schneider)

1 tsp cumin seeds
1/2 tsp coriander seeds
1 Tbsp sesame seeds
3 cups cooked chickpeas, drained
3 Tbsp tahini
3-4 Tbsp lemon juice
1 garlic clove, minced
2 tsp kosher salt
pinch of cayenne pepper
1/2 reserved bean cooking liquid or water
2 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
1/2 chopped fresh cilantro
1/2 tsp silvered lemon zest

Toast cumin and coriander over low heat in small skillet until fragrant. Crush coarsely with mortar and pestel or spice grinder. Add sesame seeds to skillet and toast, shaking frequently so they won't burn. When golden, crush them or coarsely grind and set aside.

Put drained chickpeas into a food processor. In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, garlic, 1 1/2 tsp salt, cayenne, spice mixture, and 2 Tbsp liquid. Add tahini mixture a tablespoon at a time to the chickpeas, processing until you have a medium-coarse puree. Stir in enough liquid to make a soft fluffly mixture with the consistency of potatoes. Add remaining 1/2 tsp salt, adjust seasoning, and transfer to serving bowl. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with chopped cilantro and lemon zest, and serve.
