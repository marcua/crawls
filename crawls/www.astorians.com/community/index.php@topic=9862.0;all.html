Quote from: Picasso on March 19, 2008, 09:19:45 PM
How do brokers increase demand? That sounds implausible.

Well, if they advertise a place for 1000 when the Landlord is really asking for 1200-1300 (and assuming it is basically worth that much), presumably more people will be interested in the apartment as advertised.  Therefore, more people are viewing it and, potentially, making offers.  Demand for that apartment increases. That is what was intended by my statement-- not necessarily that demand in the entire market would be increased.  They then can either play competing bidders off of each other or just tell a potential renter that they have someone else interested that is willing to pay more money (or as a last resort, tell the lessee that it was listed incorrectly).

I have a Marathon horror story. When I was moving to New York in late 2005, I crashed with a friend in Williamsburg for three days in order to find an apartment. When I stepped off the N/W at Broadway, I fell in love with Astoria. (It looked so nice compared to bombed out South Bburg!)

I called brokers based on apartments I saw on Craigslist, and ended up working with Marathon on a 1-br for $1,000 (believe it or not, it was pretty nice). I went back home -- thousands of miles away -- and started packing. The next day, I got a call from Marathon. They were sorry, but they couldn't honor the lease I signed. The person who owned the apartment had decided to rent give his cousin, the current tenant, an extension. The lease I'd signed had actually been a dummy lease.

That was it. That was the money I'd had to spend on a trip to New York to find an apartment. I had no options. I didn't move to New York until a year and a half later!

Stupidly, I tried Marathon again the next time. They tried to convince me I "had" to move into the best apartment they'd shown me, and that I'd have to leave my pets at home because they'd forgotten I specifically said I had pets until AFTER I liked a place. (UGH.)

Thanks, but I moved to the city instead. Now, we're looking in Astoria again, but we're not dealing with brokers -- and definitely not with Marathon!

I needed a good vent. Sorry it was so long. :)
