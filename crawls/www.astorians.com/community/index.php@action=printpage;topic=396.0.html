Post by: lanseaux on August 28, 2005, 12:17:14 PM
That's so neat that Osprey's won NY winery of the year!

Osprey's has consistently been the LI winery with the best wines in our opinion (despite the fact that it's one of the smaller vineyards) - we've been visiting and tasting LI wines since '98 or so.  As far back as the mid-90s they had a very good cabernet.  Their sauvignon blanc is also nice.

In the past 3-4 years things have improved quite a bit on LI.  It's still a young wine-producing region, so there are still some misses out there.  But it's getting better.

Lenz whites are quite good, Meg, in particular their silver label chardonnay, which is a good medium of price and quality.  Macari also has some good whites, and Bedell Cellars has some nice reds.  Jim and I served a Macari chardonnay and a Bedell merlot at our wedding.  The merlot was given a very strong review by our French guests.
Title: NYS wine
Post by: iLoveCheese on August 28, 2005, 03:45:55 PM
I just have to chime in here that Osprey's Dominion is indeed a pretty decent LI wine. Pelligrini is also very good and Pindar's Pythagoras is very good tho most of Pindar stinks.

I happened to have writtten my master's thesis about NYS wine (wine dork!) and thought you'd be interested to know that there is a store and new accompanying wine bar on Broome street called Vintage New York that only carries NYS products. I have my beef with the owners, but it's still a fun place to check out. They also own Rivendell (yes, like in Lord of the Rings), a Hudson Valley winery, that stinks but buys some decent Finger Lakes wine that they sell and recieve awards for under thier own label.

Other NYS wine to look for is Millbrook reserve (hudson valley), Borghese reserve (LI) and the godfather of NYS wine, Dr. Frank Reisling (Seneca Lake).

Think global, buy local! ~
