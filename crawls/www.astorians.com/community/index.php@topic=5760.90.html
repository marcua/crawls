Re: Anyone know what a hipster is?
« Reply #97 on: September 11, 2007, 01:45:08 AM »
a hipster is term used about those who aspire to unique forms of expression.  irony being what it is, the concept is exceptionally ironic and sad in that hipsters tend to collect in groups and aspire to the SAME forms of unique expression.  one could call it a movement (akin to an art movement of yesteryear - dada, expressionism) yet the hipster ethic does not necessarily include expression of artistic desire but rather can be limited to the conspicuous consumption of culture as a form of expression.

i would venture to say that those who are hipsters now would have been punks in the 80's after the clash rocked the casbah or grunge kids in the 90's after pearl jam's second album or neo punk kids in the late 90's after dookie went platinum.  the truth is that there were few people in williamsburg, dumbo, LES etc who were making art and having a great time doing it and people jumped on the scene like new met fans in the mid 80's.

it is not the artistic who make hipster out to be a bad thing but the second wave of hangers-on that bring the concept to its knees.

as a disclaimer - i hung in the les in 1988, move to avenue c in 1992, worked in williamsburg in 1993, moved my studio to dumbo and my living situation to red hook in 1995 and now i am in gowanus.  i may not be artistic enough to call myself a hipster or part of the vanguard that makes a scene great but i have seen a number of changes that help me understand that the conception is not all bad, it is the successive waves that make is smell.

hipsters are the modern hippies. some live the ethic and some just appear to.

After reading all of these posts, I went on a mission around Astoria to see what sort of hipster population we actually have -- the results were surprising.  I started my walk on Ditmars -- now in my head I figured I would find hipsters on 30th ave, or down towards the movie theater -- low and behold, I spotted a hipster couple leaving their apartment building right of of Ditmars.  It took me all of 30 seconds to find a hipster (2 in fact) -- here is how I classified them.  HIM:  longer hair (not too long) sort of messy, but obviously worked on to get the look, wearing a "miller time" t-shirt, army type shorts (frayed at the bottom of course), horizontal multicolored  socks and whatever shoes that hipsters wear, OH and can't forget the oversized paris hiton shades.  HER:  very faded jeans, looks like they came from salvation army but I bet they cost over $100, a tank top (normal), and similar socks and shoes as her beau, she was wearing the quintessensial hipster eyewear -- the thin black rimmed glasses that may or may not have been prescription.  I decided to do some recon -- I followed them as they made there way down Ditmars, swinging a left on 29th and heading straight to Freeze peach.  I decided to investigate further and entered freeze peach -- Hipster HQ -- wow, I must have had my eyes closed for the past few years -- as I strolled down to the park and back there were hipsters everywhere -- Waltz, Fatty's, Oleput, Brooklyn Bagel -- I never knew.  Oh well.
