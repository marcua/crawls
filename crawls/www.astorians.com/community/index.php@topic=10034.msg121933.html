Great topic, betheroonie.  So, here's my silly story about my screen name.

I don't remember exactly when I heard it first, but when I lived in Chicago I would hear kids who were either in gangs or were perpetrating that they were, greeting each other with the name of their gang and end it with "luv" and then came the obligatory five minutes of handshakes, hand signs and gestures (called stacking). 

When I came to NYC, several of the people that I hung out with in the projects that I lived in had street names that ended with "luv" e.g. Tone-luv, Mo-Luv, etc. It was easy to do with my real name and for a very brief time,thankfully, my friends would greet me with **- luv.  To them it was a term of affection; like you belonged.

And then came the 2Pac and , "California Luv" and suddenly, using "luv" spread to across the nation.  I don't know if it really took off in my hometown in Minnesota, though.

I really don't know why it has to be misspelled.  I guess it looks "cool" if you don't know how to spell.  Or it might have to do with people, especially boys and men who hang out in the neighborhood wanting to express some sort of emotion with their male friends that isn't "love."

Anyway, that's my ridiculously verbose explanation of my name.  There's a couple of screen names that I have been curious about their origin, like Alison or Ntrain.
