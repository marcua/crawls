I was a member there for 5 years - left 3 years ago.

I left because they were constantly doing never-ending construction.
The place was filthy all the time and they mice and roaches running around
from all these big, open holes.

I also didn't like how they  moved the cardio machines up to this platform
that you had to walk up a rickety flight of stairs to get to. There was no air
up there at all and the ceiling was so low you could touch it if you were on an
elliptical machine.

However, they did have good, basic equipment and you can definitely get a
decent workout. Many of my friends there said I was going fussy about not
liking the dust, dirt and rodents, but if that's fussy, then I guess I am.

Another plus is their hours - 24 hours during the week and early opening
on the weekends. Club Fitness doesn't have that.

You should go check it out and see. To be honest, the couple of people I run into
now from there have gone on to other gyms. I belong to the Rock, my friends go to
NYSC and Dolphin.

My brother belongs to Fitness Point on 34th Ave. and he's been very happy with it.
Smaller than Synergy I think, but he says the managers and desk people are very
good and he likes the atmosphere there.

Check them out and see - it's your money and your workout so do what's right for you.

I belong to both Synergy gym and Fitness Point.  I joined Fitness Point last summer because it was extremely cheap (I prepaid for 14 months because of a deal they had--I think it worked out to be about $17 a month) and it is very close to where I live.  Fitness Point is terrible, so that is why a couple of months ago, I checked out Synergy and decided to join there also, even though my FP membership is still running. 

My problems with Fitness Point:
1.  The machines are in poor working order.  I used to run for an hour on the treadmill everyday (at my previous gym), but stopped when I noticed that the treadmills at FP frequently pause or slow down while you're running.  If you're not paying attention, you could easily stumble and fall because of this.  If you look at the treadmills, it is clear that they are old and not well maintained.  The belts show visible wear and look old.  The treadmills are also preset to stop at 30 minutes, and I couldn't figure out a way to fix it.  I decided to switch to the elliptical machines for my cardio, and those are also in poor working order.  When you switch the incline around, many of the ellipticals will make a loud banging noise for about a minute.  I've also had several shut off on me while I'm working out.  Just suddenly, the screen turns black and it's not as easy to move.  Not dangerous on the elliptical, but annoying.  I've also had the timer pause while I'm working out.
2.  Weight equipment.  If you're looking for free weights, I assume you'll be fine, but as far as the weight machines go, I'm afraid to even try them because they look poorly maintained and based on the condition of the cardio equipment,I would assume they could be in poor working order.  I'm not willing to risk getting hurt.
3.  CREEPY locker room.  I don't know about the men's locker room, but the women's locker room is downstairs and big, empty and dirty-looking.  I don't really feel safe in there.  There are showers that are dusty and don't look like they've been used in years and one of the toilets has tape across it to keep it closed and there is rarely anyone in there.


Because of my unhappiness with Fitness Point, I decided to look into Synergy.  After deciding I would join if it was satisfactory, I went in there to check out the gym, but you have to know that when you go into a gym, the staff there is trained to make sure you don't leave without signing up.  I've spent a lot of my time in gyms, and know how this works, and you just have to realize it's their job to try to sell you.  I went in, and the person there was very nice.  He showed me around the gym and I liked what I saw:  a wide variety of well-maintained equipment and cleanliness.  He was still trying to sell me, telling me about things I don't care about or need (for example, tvs coming on all the cardio equipment--but if you're really getting a good cardio workout, you can't concentrate on the tv).  He did bring up the trainers (FREE first session.. mmmhmmm....), but I just said "No thanks" and that was that.  I joined on the spot, but I said I would pay no more than $30 a month because that was the deal I would be able to get through my insurance company (globalfit program) and he did that for me, with no activation fee (and really, the activation fee is a ridiculous fee, paying so that you can start paying them every month?).  I prepaid for the year (no worries about auto-renewal or continuing to charge my credit card if I move).  So far, I'm very happy with Synergy.  Yes, they often ask me if I'd like a trainer when I come in, but all I say is "no, thanks" and keep walking.  The staff is very friendly, but I don't feel bothered by anyone.  I like the cardio equipment (well maintained treadmills and ellipticals) and the weight and ab equipment.  The locker room is small, but clean.  I go at night, usually around 8pm and while it's a bit crowded, I haven't had any major problems with getting access to the equipment I want to use.  I just do my workout, and nobody really bothers me.  I see all these bad reviews, and I don't really understand why.  If you're serious about maintaining your fitness, Synergy is great.
