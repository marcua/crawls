Yeah, it is a bit of a walk to the subway but then, right now where I live I already have to deal with a bus to get to/from the subway (otherwise it's a 15-20 min. walk to subway).  On top of that, I must take two different trains also to get to/from work.  Living at this place in Astoria, it will now be one train to my job at Rock Center. 

I'd already been looking for a few months...first to rent, then to buy (first looking at co-ops, then condos, and finally when I realized even condos have rules that could stop me from having couchsurfers, I decided a small house is the only way to go for me.  I love having guests and absolutely don't want to run the risk of any 'board' telling me down the road, that I cannot do so or have to limit the number of guests I have).  But obviously finding a house to buy would require a longer search and, wanting to get out of my present nabe sooner rather than later, I decided to find a rental, and THEN spend that time looking for a place to buy.

It definitely is more than I want to pay for rent, but I figure it's just temporary, and either way living in Astoria will be a nice change for me that will affect other parts of my life...shorter commute, I already have 4 friends in Astoria (Ihave none where I live now in the middle of nowhere on the Qns/Bk border), and I'll certainly get in better shape due to the onsite gym AND the nearby bike path (I'll now be motivated to buy a bike).

Also, having outdoor space is a MUST for me, so that made my search that much harder.  I realize yeah, I probably could find something equally nice, perhaps without ALL those amenities but with access to a balcony or yard, and for a few hundred less.  But I guess I'm ready to stop searching....ready to just MOVE.  ;-)

I've yet to sign a lease..still doing paperwork.  I currently pay $1500 for a huge 1BR WITH a private yard and large deck, and with much nicer hdwd floors and all SS kitchen and gorgeous bath.  Course, with that, I don't get the Manhattan skyline view, AND my current commute involves a bus and two trains (50 min. commute each way).  So sure, I'm gettign some things with this bldg that I don't currently get, but now I'll be paying $900 more per month single-handedly (no roommate).  I don't know that it's quite worth all that but...I'm just more than anything wanting to get out of my current nabe which I don't like.
 
But there's another variable that the building doesn't fully disclose.  Do you know about the PTAC units that heat/cool the apartment?  Well I just had to learn up on it.  Apparently in some instances the building will supply the heating fluid and the coolant, and the PTAC unit essentially just runs as a fan, to help circulate the cool or hot air.  And so you are paying for electricity to run those fans.
 
Apparently in this bldg however, I learned that they do NOT supply heating fluid or coolant for the PTAC units, and so the units themselves must create their own heat (via gas) or cool air (via electricity I thing), and then also run fans to then circulate that air.  So I understand that what that means is that, because the PTAC units are doing so much work, and they run on electricity, our electric bills will be that much higher.  It was difficult for others to tell me exactly what we might be paying each month, but the concensus seemed to be that our electric bill could be $200 in the higher use months.  So as far as I'm concerned, this is just another additional part of the 'rent' cost (most rental apartments DO provide heat).
