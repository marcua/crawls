I'm a once and future Philadelphian.  By International House Theatre do you mean the International House that's out near the Penn campus?  If so, that's a long walk from the bus station.  About 20 blocks, and these aren't little Manhattan blocks.  I would take a cab.  There's also an el (subway) but you need exact change.

The station itself is average for a bus station.  There are the usual weirdos hanging around, but if you get half a block away in any direction, you're in a good area--Chinatown, or the convention center, depending on which direction you walk.  I've been to the bus station late at night and survived.  But I'm not sure why you would take the bus instead of the train.

If you take the train, you can get out at 30th Street Station, which is a few blocks from International House.  There's the expensive Amtrak option, or the much cheaper NJ Transit/SEPTA option, which takes longer but is otherwise fine.  You take the NJ Transit Northeast Corridor line to Trenton then change to the R7 train to Philly.  Get out at 30th Street Station.  It's worth it even just to see the station.

Philly is great, if you're in the downtown area or certain other good areas.  You should go early and spend some time walking around the historic area and/or Center City.
