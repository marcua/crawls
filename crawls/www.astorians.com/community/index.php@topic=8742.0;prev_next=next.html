Movers?
« on: November 07, 2007, 10:49:58 PM »
I can't really find any new threads about movers, most posts are over a year old. And none of them mention prices.

I found a site called citimove.com, and the concept is great -- customers evaluate their experience. But there aren't a lot of companies listed there yet, and most of the bids I'm getting for my move (from that same site) are $100+ per hour (usually 3 guys), at an average 5 hours to move. That seems an awful long time to move from Brooklyn to Astoria, even in really bad traffic ... I see flyers taped to street lights all over the place, but those "man with a van" deals are awfully risky ...

Does anyone have a good experience they want to share, or recommend a mover? Especially if you can mention what their hourly rate is. I'm moving in a week (next Thurs). The cheapest bid I got on that site was for $80 an hour ... the flyers on the streets mention $19/hr per man, which is a lot cheaper than my bids (even with 3 guys it's less than $60/hr) ... but that's so risky! Not sure I want to do business based off a paper taped to a sign. Hmmmm.

I saw Shea mentioned a few times in other threads. Any recommendations there? Any idea what they cost? I plan to call them but they have to actually come to your home, and that could take a few days.

Thanks!!

Monica

About a year and a half ago, I had a fantastic experience with moving12go@yahoo.com. I have no idea what the name of the company really is, or even where I got it from, but these guys were awesome. It was $60 an hour, two hours minimum. Two guys came with a truck, blankets, dolly, etc. They carried everything down two flights at my old place, up three flights at my new place, broke nothing, were cheerful, friendly, super fast and all around awesome.

Six months ago I rented a Uhaul and had my friends help. I'll never do that again. It was more expensive (rental plus gas plus food and drinks for everyone), stuff got broken, it took all day, people were angsty, and it was all around a stressful day. I'll go with moving12go from here on out.

About 9 months ago my girlfriend and I moved in together, and we hired Park Slope Movers (recommended by a co-worker) to take everything from both of our old apartments and haul it up four flights to our new one.  They were excellent: prompt, quick, courteous, and very careful with our stuff even though it's pretty obvious that our furniture isn't the nicest.  The rates were as quoted, and it took them exactly as long as expected.  Their prices were reasonable but not the cheapest (IIRC it was ~100/hr for four guys). 

http://www.parkslopemovers.com/index.html

Agreed about roping in friends and renting a truck and all that; it's a huge pain, and it doesn't end up being a whole lot cheaper.  Did it a couple of times, but never again.
