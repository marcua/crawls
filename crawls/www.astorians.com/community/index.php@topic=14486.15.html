There's only 3 ways your bill can be that high. #1) Someone is tapped into your service, b/c I'm in a similar setup (gas & electric 2BR) and my winter bill is always around $100. #2) Con Ed can't gain access to your meter, so you're getting estimated readings, which are based on prior years consumption rates. #3) Dwarfs enter your apt in the middle of the night and charge admission for the coolest laser light show in Astoria.

Is that electric baseboard heating?

The energy cost for electric heating is generally a lot higher than that for gas or oil.

Basically, electricity is sold by the kilowatt-hour (kWh), and gas is sold by the Therm, a unit that measures the available heat from burning the gas.  The conversion factor is 29.3 kWh = 1 Therm.

Near as I can figure from my bill and their billing site, the marginal cost of a therm of gas delivered to a residential customer is around $1.60.

The cost for a kilowatt-hour of con-ed brand electricity is about $0.19.  Times 29.3, that's $5.57 per therm, if I got the rates right.

Another factor would be, do you live on the top floor?  Top-floor apartments have to deal with heat loss through the roof. The apartments below them get a free ride on this part of the heating load. The increased exposure to the wind in winter and the sunlight in summer adds to top-floor energy costs.

It could be cheaper to heat a whole three family building with gas or oil than to heat the top apartment with electricity!
