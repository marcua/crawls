I got this message from greenshoresnyc, too.  There are a few issues that stand out for me:  the potential loss of wetlands (an area that still supports a remarkable cross-section of wildlife, including pheasants -- and quite possibly some of those opossums and raccoons that people have reported seeing over the past few years), loss of waterfront property and waterfront access, loss of community playing fields, and increased traffic.  FedEx intends this new location to replace their Maspeth facility - which, if you've ever had the pleasure of visiting, you know is not a pleasant place to be.  Not only that, but it will employ nearly double the number of workers.  The only access to the proposed location is limited and runs directly through the residential streets of Astoria -- we're talking about large semis delivering the packages to be processed, numerous UPS delivery trucks arriving and departing the facility, as well as the traffic from the workers themselves.

This is really important to the community - I plan on attending if my work permits.

For those interested in a little background, please see this thread.  From that thread, here are a couple of posts summarising what happened at the community meeting in April:

Quote from: heff on May 02, 2008, 12:38:55 AM
(Well i took the time to write this up so I'm going to post it anyways)

State Senator Onorato sent his man Teddy to represent him, and he expressed Onorato's opposition to the sale.  Peter Vallone Jr also came and expressed his opposition as well as mentioning the rally against the new power plant which was already mentioned in another thread.  Lucille Hartman the "acting" District Manager of CB1 (she's humble) attended, and she said that the CB supports the community but their hands were tied.  She also let us know that the Mayor is intent on keeping all of this property between 20th Ave and the waterfront zoned industrial.  We also had folks from all the sponsoring community groups.  Since FedEx, Steel Equities and ConEd all declined to appear I made a presentation on what we do know, mainly gleaned from Daily News articles and the folks who attended the United Civic forum in February.

The area in question is about 21 acres.  Steel Equities plans to build the FedEx depot on 225,000 sq ft of the area, which is about 5 acres.  I do not know what Steel Equities plans to do with the rest of the land.  Lucille Hartman had a sheet of paper that she said she got from FedEx or someone (she had a hard time remembering), which listed some of the pros they were willing to do, one of which was to preserve 24,000 sq ft of wetland (less than an acre) although I do not know what that means.  Bear in mind that this area in question is vacant, unused land, so in a sense it already is preserved wetlands, just wetlands that the public hasn't been able to access in 100 years (no joke this property has been owned by a power company since the turn of the 20th century).

The ball fields, as we currently understand it, are mostly safe.  Some of the soccer field to the west will be lost in the sale, and about 60 feet will be lost off the back of the fields, I'm guessing to provide access to 19th St where the entrance is supposed to be.  However, the fields remain in the possesion of ConEd, and some folks associated withe the fields came and expressed concern that ConEd could sell them if it benefits them, seeing as they have no concrete guarantee from ConEd otherwise.

The Maspeth location will be closed down, this is the new location.  FedEx has said that the new location will create 150 new FedEx jobs, the Maspeth location employs 188.  FedEx earlier threw out 300 as the total number of jobs at the new location, so I'm guessing it will be the folks that currently work at Maspeth plus new hires from wherever.

The trailers will run over night and the trucks will leave in the morning from 6a-10a and returning 4p-6p.  FedEx has said that the drivers will stay to the truck routes (Steinway, 21st St, Vernon Blvd and Hazen) but have previously said that they can't control the employees commuting and can't control the freelance delivery guys that they hire (kind of like a franchise for FedEx drivers).

A lot of the folks who attended have issues with the increase in traffic and the effect it could have on asthma rates of children living along these already busy truck routes.  Folks also expressed a desire to access the waterfront in this area, and to preserve and expand the fields.  Erik Baard of the LIC Boathouse explained how the Steinway Creek is one of the last few inlets that haven't been completely erased with landfill and how the calmer waters there would make it a more accessible location for boaters who might not be comfortable with the fast moving current of the East River.

The next immediate step is to write the Public Service Commission and let them know you opinion about this parcel of land.  The PSC is the governing body that decides when ConEd can raise rates and sell it's property.  They are indeed the final authority on this matter.  The address for the PSC is:

New York State Public Service Commission
3 Empire State Plaza
Albany, NY 12223-1350

Meanwhile the Coalition for a Better Astoria is shoring up support in the community, if you and/or your community group wants to get involved send me a PM.

I will say personally I think it was a mistake for Steel Equities and FedEx not to attend, I think a lot of people would have been assuage with promises of opening the waterfront up to the public and a little more info about the future of the rest of the property.  Also (once again personal opinion) getting at minimum public waterfront access sets a nice precedent for future industrial property that goes up.

Sorry it took some time to reply to this.  Basically they showed the plan for the area, the FedEx depot isn't going to use the whole 21 acres, but it was unclear what would happen with the rest of the property.  Although they have planned for about what they said is 6 acres of green belt around the FedEx depot, when asked if there would be public access to the waterfront the rep for Steel Equities said it was up to the city and parks (which is sort of a dodge).  Rosemarie Porvelone of the United Community Civic said they were working to make Steel Eq also buy this parcel of property next to the main one to be a park for water front access.  This property is would be wedged between 20th ave and the 19th ave entrance to the depot, and not actually on the waterfront.  Its also significantly smaller than the unused section of the waterfront property being sold to Steel Eq.

There was a lot of opposition to the arrival of FedEx.  Vallone sent a staffer with a letter he wrote to the PSC objecting to the sale.  State Senator Onorato spoke at the beginning promising to do "everything they can legislatively"..."if there is indeed anything they can do".  Assemblyman Gianaris was also against the plan, saying they had to wait until ConEd filed with the PSC, which hasn't happened yet.  Congresswoman Carolyn Maloney also sent a staffer to read a letter of objection she sent to the DEP.  The Mayor however is committed to the area remaining industrial and it was pointed out that the property is zone M31, which means pretty much anything can be built there, including stuff like waste disposal.  Of course for me the question isn't could something worse than FedEx move in but why would you zone vacant, waterfront property M31?

Also although our group is having a hard time getting the EAS (Environmental Assessment Study) collected by Steel Equities (we're filing a FOIL request for it) its been confirmed by Parks and a Environmental consultant for the United Community Civic that the property is not heavily polluted and could be used for a park if that's what ends up happening.
