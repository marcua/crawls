what do bed bug bites look like?

If you're asking because you think you have them, please visit bedbugger.com or go to flickr and search for bedbug bite photos. Please nip the problem in the bud even if you THINK you may have them. You can spread them to other people, people who might be highly allergic to the bites; people with kids (who tend to react violently to the bites) or with pets (who sometimes cannot treat bedbugs normally because of the pesticides).

Not to be paranoid, but if you've had them once, you know what a miserable, expensive, and psychologically damaging process this is...

Quote from: paratactical on August 12, 2008, 12:48:04 PM
I'm thinking about hiring movers for my Aug 31/Sept 1 move but I'm concerned about bed bugs. Anyone have any hints on what I can do to safeguard my stuff? The movers come highly recommended from this site, but I figure it's not like they'd be able to tell.

Bedbugs can, and have been known to hitch a ride on those blankets that movers use to wrap up your furniture. I would buy my own and use it rather than risk using theirs.
Also, bedbugs can fall off other peoples' stuff (meaning the previous client's stuff) and hang out in the van for a while, before hitching on your stuff. Maybe you can ask to be first of the day, or do it really early in the morning? This is a tough one .
lastly, don't use used boxes. I know- not great for the environment, but you can't be too careful. I would never, ever pick up used packing boxes off the street and put my stuff in them.
Maybe buy some sterifab and/or kleen free to kill stuff on contact.
