To elaborate on the points:
Aggressive dogs that dominate a dog run and prevent other people from feeling comfortable enough to allow their dogs to go in at the same time.

Someone, who had worked on establishing a dog run in another park also sited the following things happening.
-Drug dealers using the dog run as a way to throw off drug dogs by hiding the drugs under rocks / chairs/ etc in the dog run.
-Couples who became attracted to a well kept, seemingly private area of the park to become more acquainted with eachother.
-People abandoning dogs in the dog run, expecting that someone would start taking care of them and they would be safe there.

-Lack of running water to the area (there is a work around now with the water jugs, but for better cleaning and fresh water running water would be best).
-Insurance / liability issues.  I'm not sure this has been discussed, but since it is private property, I would be concerned about the risk Tex has exposed himself to if something were to happen.
-The fence along the water looks like it crumpled from people (kids) climbing over it get down to the water front where there is some spray painting going on.  This might require more than just a fence put up to try and stop it from happening again.  Also, it almost looks like the sea wall in some areas might be collapsing...

Excellent response.

One of the other issues that most people don't discuss is that this particular dog run is a germ infested dump. I have used the same vet for the past 35+ years and he hands all new clients a severe warning about the use of most dog runs. There are so many diseases spread in these places by careless and negligent pet owners who don't get their dogs vaccinated; who encourage their dog's aggressiveness; who find it cute that their dogs (male and female) will hump anything that is vertical (please remember that humping is not sexual with dogsâ€”it is a very aggressive, and manipulative power move); that come with intact males or females in heat; that spread intestinal worms through their feces that owners don't clean up . . . dog runs are confined spaces that need to be clean, safe and healthy places for both dogs and dog owners. The only times I have seen this in New York City are the few that have a full time attendant or full time volunteers to help maintain both a clean and safe environment.

Dogs need to run freely to use up all their energy and, along with a proper diet, it also helps keep them in condition; as a result they tend to live longer and healthier lives and are happier pets. In my personal experience, people who will only take their dogs to a dog run are dog owners who have not succeeded at training a well-behaved dog. A well-trained dog is a well-behaved dog; a well-behaved dog can be allowed off-leash in a public space because they will follow their owners' commands to sit, stay come, etc. A well-behaved dog is not aggressive. A well-behaved dog is non-reactive. A well-behaved dog is a pleasure to be around. The only way to achieve having a well-behaved dog is through a healthy diet, reinforced obedience training, daily breed- and personality-specific exercise, consistent discipline, and lots and lots of affection.
