I ate at John Brown's yesterday and I was EXTREMELY impressed.  What a classy operation there.  The brisket and burnt ends were DELICIOUS.  Some of the best BBQ I've ever had.  The side I had were also out of this world -- sweet potato mash and collard greens (usually hard to make tasty).  For those who complained about the fries on the board, I am happy to report that they are now made in-house like all of the other items, including the amazing apple cobbler.  There are so many other types of meat to try with all of the regular menu items and specials.
 
On top of the incredible food, the owner (former pitmaster at Hill Country) and his staff are very friendly and accomodating.  There's music there Tuesday and Thursday evenings.  I would encourage everyone to check out John Brown's on 37th Avenue at Crescent St.  It's worth the trek.

The wife had a stomach virus yesterday and could only eat toast... so yes, the fine folks at John Brown delivered a meatilicious meal for me (+ the dog and cat who benefitted from the copious amount of leftovers).
 
Got the three meat meal -- pork ribs, pulled pork, and turkey (figured I might as well go big!). Ribs were good, though they could have used a bit more flavor. Pulled pork -- fantastic, as usual. Turkey -- better than the turkey at Butcher Bar, nice smoky flavor but retained the moistness well.
 
The new fries are okay, albeit greasy, and the packaging of said fries could be better for a to-go order. Still preferred the old crinkle fries. The mac & cheese wasn't as good as it has been in the past -- not especially cheesy. Still good, but a bit off.
 
The cat definitely approved, as he busted into the trash last night while we slept to get the rest of the leftovers. The dog wasn't smart enough to follow along, and missed out on all the fun.
