I'd start by making a list of my criteria. If you need someone to smile at you when you enter your bank, then go local. If you travel, and need to use ATMs a lot, go big.

I bank with Citibank. No, I am not happy, but I am loyal. They meet *all* my criteria. I opened my checking account with them when they were First National City Bank in 1960, when I was 16 years old and leaving for college. They have branches all over the world. Wherever I traveled to, they had a presence. That was very important to me. Converting currency or withdrawing cash whenever I needed to was a great influence in my staying with an impersonal financial institution. It has also had a few fringe benefits. I got terrific rates on car loans, consolidation loans and educational loans. I also have a 6.5% interest rate on a MasterCard which I hardly ever use, and their Preferred Money Market accounts now have a 5.50% APR.

My sister always changed banks to get better deals. So did my parents. Remember all those freebies? They supplied the whole neighborhood with toasters and glassware! Just don't pick a bank based on its proximity to your workplace—find one nearer home.

You know, I know a lot of people think that they're not eligible to join a credit union, but chances are, you can. Finding one might take a little bit of poking around, but go here: http://www.creditunion.coop/ and search under "Locate a Credit Union". I clicked on this one: http://www.nymcu.org/ at random, and to become a member, you must be one of the following:

Quote
*  A New York City employee
* A Federal or State employee who works in the five boroughs
* A Health Care professional (private or public) working in New York State
* An employee of an insurance company that offers health related insurance in the State of New York
* An employee of a private college located in the City of New York or an employee of a private or public college in the counties of Nassau, Suffolk and Westchester
* An employee of the City of Yonkers
* A member of certain private industry groups

That's a pretty broad spectrum of people, when you think about it, and a lot of credit unions also extend membership to members of family or the household of people who are eligible, so, depending on the credit union, if your roommate or someone in your family is eligible, you are too.

A lot of CU's have much more lenient membership policies too - the one I'm a member of in CT (which used to just serve employees & families of my dad's company) is now open to anyone who lives, works, or goes to school in Fairfield & New Haven Counties in CT. So EVERYONE is SW CT can join, and once you're a member, you are always a member, no matter if you move or switch jobs or whatever (this is true for all CU's).

Anyway, if its not obvious, I love my credit union. Yeah, there's no branch down here that I can go to so I have to mail checks to be deposited (which, for me, is usually within a couple days), and I have to use out of network ATMs. But the ONLY fees I've ever, ever, ever paid are other banks $1 or $1.50 ATM fees. I don't have to pay for checks, I get free everything, they do online banking, the loan rates are quite good, and every time I've talked to someone (including the time that another bank's ATM shorted me $40 - I went into the bank and told the manager and she basically said "not my problem," so I called my CU and they refunded it without question), they've been friendly and helpful.
