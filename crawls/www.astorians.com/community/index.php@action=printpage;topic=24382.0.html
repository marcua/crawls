Title: Has anyone hear heard about Mealku..a home-cooked meal cooperative?
Post by: yippee1999 on January 25, 2013, 11:32:20 PM

I learned about Mealku a month or so ago from an article in the NYTimes.  It may sound a bit odd at first, but I think folks who are into the whole social networking/couchsurfing/meetup/CSA/Food Co-op thing might like it.

Basically it's a locally-based website for people who love to cook and share their food with others.  Each person creates a profile.  You pay $10 per month to be a member.  The website then has their own 'currency' called Ku.  You earn Ku by offering up a home-cooked meal on the site for others to 'order'.  Mealku then has their own delivery people who go between the people who cook (to pick up the meals) and the people who ordered the meals (to deliver them).  I think from the time the meal is picked until it's delivered is about two hours.  (Most people generally order a meal 1-3 days in advance.)  The food is kept in a foil-lined sealed bag. 

Anyway I think it's a really cool concept and so far I myself have gotten and given a few meals.  The delivery charge (also based in the 'Ku' currency) is based on the distance between you and the cook.  So the website gives you the option to search for cooks/meals within a 3, 5 or 10 mile radius.  Naturally if you prefer to spend less Ku, you want to find a cook that lives nearby.

And this is part of the reason why I'm here talking about how great Mealku is.  It would be great if more people in the Astoria/LIC/Sunnyside areas were to become members....then I and other Astorians on Mealku would have more options to pick from.  Right now I suspect most of their members are from Manhattan.... ;-( ...as there were a number of meals I wanted to order, until I saw that the cook was more than 4 miles away and so the delivery charge would be higher.

People leave reviews after they've received a meal, so in this way you can get a sense of whether they are a good cook or not.  And I HAVE seen some constructive criticism in some of the reviews, which is a good thing IMHO.  I'd rather that than people giving fake raves for food, when in fact they didn't like it at all. 

Anyway check it out....it's a great alternative to traditional takeout ...and they will also be hosting periodic gatherings where you'll get a chance to meet other cooks.

I think if you love cooking, and especially cooking for others and then receiving nice feedback on your meals, that Mealku will be right up your alley!

Note that the website just went through an update so they are still working out a few kinks, but overall the system has been workikng well for me...

https://www.mealku.com/
