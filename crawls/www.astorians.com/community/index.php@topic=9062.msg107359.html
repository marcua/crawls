Checked out Mojave last night and I'm happy to report that it met my expectations!  We stuck with simpler things, and hoped the lower priced options could still be good.  I was wowed by the buffalo style chicken wings app - so fresh and crispy, great sauce.  Shared the steak soft tacos and crab quesidillas and they were both impressive for a "standard" choice.   Our entrees were $13 and $15, I believe, and definitely filling.  When we go back we'll explore the menu further.   The fajitas were priced at $22 (!) but I suspect it's a larger portion and worth it.  Definitely lots of great options and the 3 things we did have were great.

About the price, it is the same as Agave and at first I would have expected them to come down for Astoria.  But when you think about it, why lower the bar?  That makes is easier for disasters like Oceano to think they have a chance.  It's time for an upgrade around here.  I'm not saying upgrade means expensive - but Mojave has tasteful decor, a unique menu, great atmosphere and (so far) delicious - just like some of our other favs around here.  And there is a range on the menu.  For an app and two entrees, the bill was $39.  That was without drinks - lots of options there too! 

The bar was packed and had a nice atmosphere.  I think it would have been wise to make the bar area larger and have less tables.  I think The space really is enormous. 

Looking forward to going back!

My 2c on Mojave
« Reply #22 on: January 05, 2008, 05:39:43 PM »
Hey all.  I, too, was there last night.  My take on the place:

Upsides:

*Food was delicious, different, and had "just right" portions
*My Margarita served "up" was fantastic and strong, and not full of filler to weaken it like a lot of places pour "up" drinks.  It made it worth the extra dollar.
*Gorgeous atmosphere and furniture
*Bathrooms are actually on the same floor as the restaurant (this is a big issue not so much for me but for some of my mobility-impaired friends and family)

Downsides:

*Strictly amateur night on the service--took me forever to get a waitress, and then, to get a glass of water.  Also, my waitress spoke bad enough English and didn't know the menu well enough to tell the difference between buffalo oysters and buffalo wings.   I had to point to the menu and say "NO, BUFFALO OYSTERS!!" a few times.
*A contributing factor to the above may have been the volume level.  Music was too loud.
*I usually dine solo in Astoria--if I dine out with friends, we usually meet up after our jobs in Manhattan.  As such, I had every single service person in the place ask me if I was dining alone, and I got the feeling that at first, the  matire'd was trying to shunt me off to the crowded bar area.  Granted, I would have LOVED to have eaten at the bar--it's always more social for solo diners-- but that area's current configuration would have made it impossible.
*Prices a bit high for the 'hood.

Would I go back?  YES.  I had the buffalo oysters, the guac, and two margaritas for a light dinner and it was the best thing I'd done for myself all week.  The guacamole was a super-special standout and was just perfect, and the oysters were like nothing I've ever had.

My suggestions if the management is reading this:

--Get your staff to learn the menu
--Get water on the table PRONTO and don't make me wave around like a drowning person to get more--you serve spicy food; stay on top of it.
--My money is just as green as the next person's; if you don't want to give 2-tops to solo diners (which I DO NOT recommend unless you want me to never come back) then at least expand the seating at the bar area so that the people sitting at the railing behind the bar aren't butt-cheek to butt-cheek with the people sitting on the stools behind them.  If you DO decide to seat solo diners, don't try to shunt us off near the door or have every last person ask me if I am expecting someone else. Note that I have NEVER had this kind of treatment eating alone anywhere else in the area.  Then again, Fatty's, Crescent on Vine, and most of the other places have large and well-situated bar areas that are usually packed with solo diners.  Take the cue.

My review:

Left the apt at 6:15 and got to Mojave about five minutes later. Hostess sat us immediately without a reservation.

Water was brought to the table immediately.

A waitress, or who we thought was our waitress, took our drink orders. I ordered a frozen margarita and my boyfriend ordered a prickly pear mojito.

Drinks came, the waitress seemed very unsure of herself while placing the drink in front of me- it was dripping full and slightly pink. Turns out they had made me a prickly pear margarita and a plain margarita for my boyfriend. No problem. I stuck with my margarita and she left to remake BF's drink. My margarita was great- really refreshing and fun. Although adding the prickly pear made my margarita $10 instead of 9. BF finally got his mojito and it was honestly...disgusting. Super sweet and not minty. Fatty's mojitos are lightyears better.

Along with our drink orders, we had also placed our meal orders. This included the guacamole appetizer ($11), my entree Fried Buffalo Oysters and BF's entree was Blue Corn Chicken Enchiladas (18). I stressed Oysters when I ordered ("I would like the fried buffalo oysters...Oysters.") because of earlier posts. She grinned and wrote this information down.

While we sat, the couple to the left of us were getting worked up. BF and myself go out to restaurants frequently, BF is a chef and there is a restaurant in my family so we were prepared to wait. Not this couple. Their meal came after waiting for an hour and their appetizer arrived with their entree, and the couple sent the appetizer back. They wanted to speak to a manager because they seemed convinced that they gave their orders to a waiter who they watched leave the restaurant and never come back. Also while we were waiting for our appetizer, the four top next to us who had been seated with menus got up and left after waiting for about 25 minutes.

After about a 70 minute wait our entree and appetizers arrived together. BF's enchiladas were cold and I had received Buffalo wings. Sent them back and asked for OYSTERS. The guacamole appetizer was great. No filler, wonderful cilantro and delicious fresh tomatoes. This also included a green salsa sauce that was so warm and delicious. Oysters came out. They were rushed but they tasted phenomenal with the blue cheese dressing. I only wish they had been a bit more crispy. Other than being cold, BF said his entree was fine. We were comped the appetizer and the bill came to $50. It doesnt look like our waitress actually gave our ticket to the line, and I don't even think she was a waitress- I believe she probably works in the bar because she didn't really wait on anyone but us and she was bringing drinks back and forth to tables..dropping them multiple times-and we had at least 5 different servers handle us after the appetizer issue.

Music wasn't too loud. The place was PACKED. Great menu selection, food was very fresh, drinks were GREAT and fun.

PROS: Diverse menu selection with emphasis on seasoning- this means the menu is packed with flavor. There are vegetarian options.

Drink selection- they use a high quality margarita mix and fresh fruit.

Quality of ingredients: Nothing was bagged or prepackaged, and if it was...bravo. It tasted great.

Kind and attentive waitstaff.

CONS: The restaurant has hired waitstaff who have obviously never worked in a restaurant before.

The kitchen is possibly understaffed.

There is a lack of communication between the front and back of the house, given that waiters oftentimes were seen without plates. It was a rarity that I saw a waiter with a plate of food.

The hostess, although sweet, is a bit flighty and not really willing to actually acknowledge customers who are upset at that wait time. She would apologize but beyond that you could tell that that was all she was going to do.

The seating for the booths along the restaurant's walls are about 3 inches too high so I towered over BF throughout the meal and made me slouch forward.

Although BF disagrees, I think that the menu is overpriced by about $2 on appetizers and $3 on entrees.

Wondering- are there dessert options? If so, we weren't made aware of them.

SO. From a scale of 1 to 5 I gave Mojave a Golden Three. The golden means they did GREAT considering they have been open for two days and I have made room for growth. We will probably go back in a month, since the novelty of waiting 70 minutes for our appetizer will probably wear off before then. But we were prepared. The quality of the food is really awesome, the menu seemed inspired and the decor was fine-not too tacky or theme like. I really appreciate the way the designer has hid the waiter stations.

It's a huge restaurant and I have a feeling that the kitchen probably needs 1-2 more people on the line.

All in all...great..GREAT addition to the neighborhood. I was honestly impressed, and quite optimistic.
