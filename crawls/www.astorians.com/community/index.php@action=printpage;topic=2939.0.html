Oy, I've lost track of weeks! 10? 11?

Anyway, a quick recipe from that CSA cookbook (at least one extra copy still for sale! PM me if you'd like to buy it!) that was quite good this evening...

Clean and halve some tomatoes. Clean, quarter lengthwise, and slice some cucumber. You should have about as much tomatoes as cucumber. Put in a bowl. Add some rice wine vinegar (maybe 1-2T per serving?), a pinch of sugar, a pinch of salt, and a little bit of minced cilantro. Let sit for 15 minutes or longer at room temperature. Eat.

I made stuffed peppers with the sweet peppers we received. I didn't really follow a recipe and it turned out pretty well.

Here's my concoction:

Sweet Bell Peppers, halved lengthwise (or just take the top off)
2 cups or so Brown Rice, cooked
1 cup or so of vegetable broth
About 1/4 cup Vegan Cheese Substitute (recipe found here: http://vegweb.com/index.php?topic=7108.0 - this stuff is fantastic, we make it ALL the time)
1/2 large white onion, diced
Several fresh basil leaves

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Mix together the last five ingredients and stuff the peppers. Bake in the oven for about 45 minutes.

It turned out pretty well. I think next time I might add some mushrooms and maybe some sort of tofu or meat substitute.

Here's a recipe for vegan "cheddar cheese" that is also raw. It's my own invention so, if you want, you can vary the ingredients. Aren't you glad I gave you my permission?

Cashew "Cheese"
1 C raw cashews
1/3 large orange or red bell pepper
Juice of one lemon
1/2 medium onion
1/2 tsp. sea salt
Water

Put all ingredients in blender with water just to cover the
cashews, and  blend together.

I just take the cashews out of the bag and throw them
in the blender with the rest of the ingredients.
You can vary the thickness by using more or less water.
It can be used as a sauce on raw "pasta" or "pizza", or
to spread on crackers, or as a dip for corn chips.
It's versatile.
