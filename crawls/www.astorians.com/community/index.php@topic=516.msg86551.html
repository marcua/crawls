Hi everyone. My girlfriend found this board and started posting earlier this summer. I've found myself looking at it frequently, so I figured I might as well sign up too. I was born and raised in Florida but feel at home with the variety and culture of New York. My girlfriend and I have lived here (just off the Astoria Blvd. stop on the N/W line) since September '06. We're both doctoral students who rarely have any free time during the school year but have been fortunate to have the time to explore Astoria this summer. My favorite finds (some thanks to this board):

1. Agnanti (though I swear that dessert tastes like honey nut cheerios in cake form. This is sort of a good thing)
2. Il Bambino (not just the food, but the iced coffee is phenomenal!)
3. Pasta Fina (can be inconsistent, but the personal margherita pie is outstanding)
4. Astoria Park (I love frisbee)
5. Bohemian Hall and Beer Garden (somehow have only been once, but the name says it all)

I want to say thanks to everyone here who ended up helping us have a fantastic summer exploring this great little place!

Dear Folks:

Hello.  I've been in Astoria for one year (this time around), and finally got focused enough to register on this board. 

I love it here.  I can't say enough good things about this neighborhood.  The people, the architecture (there are some very lovely houses here, despite all the recent development), the food, the bars, the park.  Not to mention the convenience of living in a nabe full of supermarkets, low-cost stores, and cafes that aren't Starbucks, and being able to commute to Manhattan or Brooklyn in about 30 minutes.

I'm a film person - I write and direct (doesn't earn the bread), and production manage and line produce (does earn the bread).  I didn't realize when I moved here that there were so many artists, musicians, writers, and fellow film-folk already out here.

Okay, that's enough love for now.
