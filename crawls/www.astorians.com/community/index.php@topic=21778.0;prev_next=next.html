Quote from: TJT on July 31, 2011, 02:39:40 PM
What's up with this?  Meli also comes to mind. So many new places open and then the brown paper appears in a very short time.  I never saw this they way it's happening in the past 9 months or so.


I've been wondering the same thing.  I think it's happening because Astoria is changing.  It's always been a thriving neighborhood but there's been a significant cultural shift in the last 3 years or so - a shift away from the Eastern European and towards younger Americans who crave a more diverse offering of restaurant choices.  I think the competition IS stiff - there is SO much to choose from and the people who live here now need more from their restauranteur (sp?) than run-of-the-mill.  We want interesting, unique food - moving away from Greek Coffee Cafe - something more "hip" and we definitely expect the owners to listen to us - e.g. by reading what we say here on Astorians.com and doing something about it.


It's really all about Facebook, Twitter, a functional website, getting into the local blogs, getting good posts here (and responding positively to critical ones), and really promoting their unique selling point any and every way that they can.  There has to be some personality there. 


I don't think Eco Table or Meli did any of those things - and if they did  it wasn't obvious.  There's a lot of mediocrity out there - consumers today (and especially Gen Y'ers) want to know:  "WHY SHOULD I SPEND MY HARD EARNED MONEY ON YOU?"  The message from Eco Table was mixed - natural and maybe organic (when possible) - that doesn't fly with people who care about that stuff.  And on top of it all, they didn't have their act together.  And Meli?  Since when did this area need another bakery?  It made no sense to me and there was nothing about the place that said that what they had was worth exploring. 


It's a tough market out there - people are holding onto their money tighter and tighter.  We'll spend it - but only when we know it's worth it.


Sorry for the diatribe .... JMHO.
