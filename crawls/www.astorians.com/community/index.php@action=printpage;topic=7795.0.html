Post by: Ditmartian on September 06, 2007, 07:13:28 PM
Impeach Bush and Cheney becomes president. Think about it.

**just to clarify, the House votes on articles of Impeachment; if approved, the person is "impeached'. The articles must then be submitted to the Senate for approval and, depending on the articles (I believe), what punishment is meted out.**

Perjury is certainly an impeachable offense, Indeed it's what got Clinton in the hot seat (not the bj). But unless Bush is brought before a grand jury or court of law to testify that he told the truth about WMD evidence (and thus under oath), he can't be accused of perjury.

We have no provision to impeach an official under international law. I don't think any country does.

He could be impeached on the whole prisoner torture thing, but only if it's demonstrable that he ordered it, and what he ordered was in direct violation of US law. But he knew that (or rather his handlers), which is why he issued that executive order basically leaving it vague and open-ended about what kind of treatment can be conducted upon prisoners.

Bush is merely the straw man anyway. If the Dem controlled Congress wanted to make some sort of statement they can censure the president, but it's mostly an empty gesture and does not carry any legal penalty.

I know censures of Bush were proposed in the House and Senate recently, but I don't know of the outcome.

**OK, apologies for the longish and ramblign post.... Passing an article of impeachment in the House requires a simple majority. Conviction upon the basis of the articles (conducted in the Senate) require a 2/3 majority. So while it might be likely an article of impeachment brought against Bush or Cheney could pass the House, I highly doubt it will garner 2/3 majority. Plus the length of time to conduct a legal investigation prior to Impeachment probably means that the clock will run out first -- ie Bush & Co. will be out of office. Look for citizens to file civil action against them, though.....
