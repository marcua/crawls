Tried Ovelia for the first time last night.  It was awesome!  Didn't see the hot waiter though  :-(  But the owner was friendly and talkative.  We had the shrimp wrapped in eggplant, the red snapper, salad, grilled octopus, and the zuchini fritters.  The zuchinni was amazing, so different than regular fritters, you have to try them.  All the food was great, down to the bread.  And the atmosphere and decor was fun too.

I do think they need to do something about that back wall, it looks like a kitchen with all its cabinets, yet it's just being used as decoration.  It doesn't really fit with the rest of the place.  The Coronas were served warm.  And I still have yet to find a place that will fillet my fish for me.  Cavo remains my favorite restaurant - they are the only place that has ever filleted my fish right in front of me, and expertly at that.  I don't think a lot of people know how to do it well.  The red snapper was amazing though, just be careful for the bones.

I highly recommend the place.  Thumbs up.

Thanks for the recommendations, folks! Victorine and I and a friend of hers went there last night for dinner, and it was very good! We had the taramosalata, which comes with great pita and bread, and was just about the best I've had. (And I tend to order the taramosalata at any Greek restaurant...) Based on recommendations from here, we also got the surprisingly tasty (despite the dill) zucchini fritters and the excellent sausages. And the three of us split the Red Snapper with potatoes and broccoli rabe. The fish was excellent, with a salty, spicy rub, but the sides were fairly uneventful. As also reported, the gratis zeppolli was excellent. :)

It also had excellent decor and service. All of the waitstaff greeted us as we walked in and out. They clearly spent time and money on the interior, and it shows. Very comfortable, trendy, and eclectic, and would not feel out of place in SoHo.

As a geek, I also was amused by the (possibly accidental) quipu behind the bar...

I have been to Ovelia a few times and it is quite good, although the service was rather slow at times (but always friendly).

For those of you who want good Greek food, you also should try:

Stamatis on 23rd Avenue - Great Pasticho and Moussaka, very nice Tispoura, which is a Porgy.

Zenon Taverna - on 31st Avenue.  Good Cypriot cuisine, particularly the souvlaki and Sheftalia (Cypriot sausage-like item).

Agnandi - on Ditmars Blvd.  You can't go wrong.

We braved the awful weather and went to Ovelia for dinner.  What's funny is that my mom and brother were babysitting and they ordered in from Ovelia, but had no menu, so they just talked to the woman on the phone and figured out what they should order.  [They don't have a website... yet, she said.]

And while they were getting that, we went and ate there.  We had Skordalia and zucchini croquets.  The skordalia was good, but really about normal for the neighborhood.  I've made it as good, but it was a big old mound of it and I didn't have to make it.  The croquets were yummy and there were EIGHT of them.   Oh, I also got a cocktail called "The Tango" that was tasty.

Erin had a mixed lamb and beef thing and I had the grilled salmon with lemon potatoes.  It definitely lived up to the hype on this thread!  Great stuff and the staff was super friendly and made sure to make us feel welcome and want to come back.  Oh and the gratis dessert (which was like zeppolis, but the waitress called it something else) was soooo good!  They were super hot and the first one I bit into spewed honey all over the place, but after I stabbed and flattened it, I could eat it without making too much of a mess. mmmmmmmmmmmmmmmm 

My mom and brother were happy with what they got, too.  They left leftovers in the fridge.  I'll have to check that out tomorrow.
