Screwing wardrobe into apartment wall?
« on: July 26, 2007, 02:09:42 PM »
I'm starting to buy new bedroom furniture and wanted to get a wardrobe to make use of some vertical space.  I was looking at stuff from Ikea and they have a matching wardrobe for the bedroom set I wanted to buy.  The only problem is, the wardrobe says it needs to be wall mounted (is sits on the floor, but probably needs to be screwed in because it is tall with a small footprint).

What is the consensus on putting something like this into an apartment?  Should I ask the landlord first or should I just do it?  Is it anything that can come back to haunt me when I move out?  I imagine that when I move out LL could just patch the screw holes while painting the walls.

Your thoughts?

Re: Screwing wardrobe into apartment wall?
« Reply #3 on: July 26, 2007, 04:39:03 PM »
It's not a bad idea to mount it to the wall. Bookshelves especially, since they tend to be narrower, and floors sometimes tilt up towards the walls. You don't want it falling on your head.

What I'd recommend is that when you remove the item when you move, to get a $3 tub of spackle and a $1 putty knife and to fill the hole yourself. Be sure to sand it after it's dry, so that it's relatively smooth. That way when they repaint your apartment they won't have to charge you $30/hour to fix the holes before they paint.

Yeah, just do it and spackle when you leave like Harlan said. No biggie! Make sure you mount it into a stud though - don't know what your walls are like, but my cheap drywall could probably barely hold up a feather :) You can get a stud finder cheap, no huge deal (here's one for $11). Add that to the few bucks for spackle and whatever, and that's less than $15 for a guarantee that you will not be crushed by your clothes, haha.
