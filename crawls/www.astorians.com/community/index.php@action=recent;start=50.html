Community Groups, Activities, Events / Newtown Literary Seeking Submissions for Issue 17!
« Last post by allisones on June 14, 2020, 04:04:45 PM »
Now open for submissions
for our 17th issue!
Newtown Literary was founded in 2012 by a small group of Queens
writers who wanted to create a written record of the literary work that
was being done in the borough. We are dedicated to sustaining a journal
that showcases excellent poetry and prose from a diverse group of writers
either born or living in Queens, NY.
Queens is home to one of the most diverse communities of people in the
world, and Newtown Literary strives to be a literary forum reflecting our
borough. Emerging and experienced scribes of all types will find a home
in Newtown Literary, and we are especially interested in making space
for words from those who are marginalized elsewhere. Furthermore, we
hope to reflect the linguistic variety of Queens. We also seek to include
the entirety of the geographic space of Queens and encourage
submissions from Far Rockaway to Astoria, Bayside to Ridgewood.
www.newtownliterary.org/submit
Submissions deadline: July 10th, 2020

Local Chat & Issues / Power Plant pollution
« Last post by AlexNYC on November 05, 2019, 06:40:02 AM »
For the fifth time in under two weeks I am waking up to the strong smell of the air pollution by the power plant(s) at the northern end of Astoria. The smell is very similar to that of what an insect smells like when it's been burnt by a bug zapper. I am just under 2 miles form the plant but I that is where this smell is coming from since I used to go for early morning walks in Astoria Park and that is where the strong smell emanated. Some nights or early mornings you can also hear the loud sound of a rumbling similar to a jet engine which would go on at the same noise level for several minutes (maybe 10-15) and then shut off instantly. The brunt smell is even managing to infiltrate my double glass windows. Whether they are releasing steam or exhaust it is a hazard to our health to be exposed to this in our breathing air. Why is nothing being done about this?
