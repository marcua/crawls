I don't have a specific camera recommendation as mine is probably three years old at least (but I like it, a Panasonic Lumix), but what I will recommend is that you look for a camera with a greater optical zoom. Most digital cameras have a 3x optical zoom, and then a digital zoom. You want to try to avoid using the digital zoom if you can -- it lowers the picture quality. My Lumix has a 6x optical zoom and I bought it specifically for that reason. Also, the trend seems to be to offer more and more megapixels, which is fine, but most people don't really need that many megapixels unless you're planning to print posters. And the more megapixels, the more space the pictures take up on your hard drive and memory card (assuming you use the highest resolution in the camera). So, this is basically a long-winded way of saying that I would focus more on the optical zoom and less on the MP of the camera, especially as you specifically mentioned nature shots which often require zooming significantly.

Camera lens are what makes a camera, not the body.  Always try to get the best lens you can afford.  Good lenses have special glass that that improve color and sharpness.  Having a nice camera body with all the bells and whistles is an expense you really don't need.  You will probably never use half the features anyway.  I would buy a cheaper camera body and save up for a better quality lens.  I use Canon.  Since I bought better lens (the L lens) my photography has improved 100%.  My pictures are much more sharp with accurate color.
