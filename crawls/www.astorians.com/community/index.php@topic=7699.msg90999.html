My reaction to this overlaps significantly with Frappe's, but I'll comment because I have a slightly different take and emphasis.

Frappe, when you describe and reiterate the karmic rationale for Democrats not celebrating this particular senator's downfall, you do a disservice to your main point, which is THE point: the govt. is doing things it shouldn't, those things endanger the liberties all of us depend on, we really need to get the govt. out of the sex-monitoring business.

That one person receives a single taxpayer dollar to prevent consenting adults from seeking sex with other consenting adults is abominable, morally reprehensible, outrageous, unconstitutional, and unAmerican in the truest sense of the word. Infuriating, on principle.

That any citizen of a country born of a principled and intransigent revolt against illegitimate use of government power is eager to exploit the results of this sting operation is positively Orwellian.

Sure, a person could say that, one the one hand, the flagrant hypocrisy of a gay sex seeking senator fighting against gay-friendly public policy reform is irresistible fist-pumping fodder. On the other hand, the same person could simultaneously hold that the government really is wasting valuable resources and, by the way, undermining our liberty by intruding in a fellow citizen's sexual behavior. But what do people choose to emphasize?

What I hear from left of center folks about this case is pure and simple delight. They're relishing every moment. Rarely I hear a grudging, eye-rolling nod to the idea that maybe our government shouldn't be doing this kind of thing. The same Left capable of defending a Nazi's First Amendment rights celebrates law enforcement's "work up" of this sexually repressed schmuck? Hmmm. Hypocrisy?

I've heard more than one person remark that there's something especially fulfilling about Republican's being outed as hypocrites when it comes to sexuality issues.

I am especially disturbed when Democrats, who generally prize their reputation as members of a more reasonable, more scientific, more empirically grounded group, eschew data about the abject failure of central planning of the economy as they trumpet their advocacy of free lunch programs. There is something abominable, morally reprehensible... just mind-boggling infuriating about the Left's blind faith in government.

If it's pathetic that three of the Republican presidential candidates don't "believe" in evolution, it's pathetic that all of the Democratic presidential candidates recommend centrally planned socialistic solutions after the historical record or the 20th century and despite contemporary economic theories (see Public Choice, see Experimental Economics, see Hayek and von Mises, see Julian Simon, etc.).

When I hear people talk about the Democrats as the party of civil liberties, I imagine what it would be like to be in prison for the nonviolent crime of drug use/possession/trading and to believe that my champions are the current leadership of the Democratic Party. Ouch.

Sept. 2, 2007

(CBS) Sunday Morning commentator Ben Stein has a few words for the Minneapolis police's behavior in the arrest of Sen. Larry Craig, and for the Republican leadership's response - and they're not very flattering.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The whole story of Senator Larry Craig, the Republican of Idaho who announced his resignation yesterday, is a nightmare of out-of-control police and weak politicians.

Senator Craig has been the innocent victim of a set-up by the evil-minded police of Minneapolis-St. Paul that Stalin would have admired.

Here's the deal: Senator Craig went into a men's room at the Twin Cities airport. He entered a lavatory stall. He tapped his foot. He may or may not have reached his hand down under the lavatory partition to pick up a piece of paper or to make a signal.

That's it. That's the whole thing. There was no sex act of any kind.

So the policeman who happened to be in the next stall walks Senator Craig off, starts bullying him, accusing him of lying and, implicitly, threatens to ruin Senator Craig's career.

ALL FOR A MAN TAPPING HIS FOOT IN A PUBLIC BATHROOM.

This is insane.

If a police officer can wreck a man's career over this trifle, then we might as well not have a Constitution or a Bill of Rights.

Look, I spend a lot of time in Idaho. These are good people. I can readily see why a Senator from there would quietly plead guilty to a small charge rather than risk a public fight - even though the police seem to have wrecked Craig's career anyway.

What I don't understand is why the GOP is tossing Senator Craig overboard as if he were a terrorist.

Even if it turns Senator Craig is gay, so what? Barney Frank is gay and he's one of the best members of the Congress. There are lots of fine gay public servants.

Senator Craig has been the victim of a police lynching. A party that believes in individual rights should be rallying to his defense, not making him walk the plank.
