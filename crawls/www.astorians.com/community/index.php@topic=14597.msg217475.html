I wanted to jump in here to say I just had lunch at Bareburger today and found the service and quality to be well above average.  My burger was perfectly cooked, topped with tasty Applewood bacon and tangy cheddar.  The fries were crispy and not overly-salted. I dined with my friend Jared, a notoriously picky eater who was instantly won over by his black & white shake and chicken pickle sandwich. I might add that the tomato was more than edible, and it is hard to find an edible tomato in January.  This was only my second trip to Bareburger but it will not be my last.  Their attention to quality, cleanliness and service has won me over.

Finally went here for the first time this weekend.


My wife had the lamb burger which she said was ok but not something she would necessarily return for.  It was overcooked.


I got a beef burger with grilled onions.  Not sure if they season the onions with something, but the burger tasted like it was seasoned with cumin or similar seasoning.  It was kind of bizarre.  My burger was also overcooked and the texture of the burger was not all that great.  We both ordered are burgers medium and they came out well done.  There wasn't even a hint of pink anywhere in the burgers.  Just grey throughout.


The fries were really good.  The onion rings were also good but probably could have come out of the fryer a bit sooner.  They were just a tad too dark.


The vanilla milkshake was good.


Overall, I don't really see the need to return here.  Maybe the standouts are the elk or ostrich but I'm not particularly interested in that. 


I applaud the concept and what they are trying to do but if you're charging $10+ for burgers at least hire a grill man who knows how to cook them properly.
