For me the problem with people asking for money is that too many of them are just scammers, especially those asking for money to get back home. I was working in midtown in the 1980s and one day on my lunch hour on Park Avenue a young guy around 20 asked me for money to get back home to Cincinatti. He said he needed $55 for a train ticket, I only had $30 on me and I gave him $25. A week later I saw the same guy asking a tourist couple for money to get back home to Virginia. I asked him how his home changed from Cincinatti, called him out as a con artist, and told the tourists not to give him any money. He went into a tirade and told me to mind me own business. I saw a police officer and called him over, of course the young con-man ran away. Since then I take everything a panhandler tells me with a grain of salt.

Even this week I saw a girl in front of Grand Central on Tuesday asking for money to get home to New Haven. Friday she was still asking momey to get home to New Haven I told her I will buy her the ticket to New Haven myself so long as I see her get on the train. She refused. Need I say more?
