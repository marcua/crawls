It is what it is, a big sports bar with bar food, beers, and a lot of TVs. I must say that it's an upgrade over what else the neighborhood has to offer. I watched a hockey game with a buddy there, and the wings were big and delicious and the service was solid and we each had a great view of a big TV. That's more than I can say about some of the other bars we've tried to watch games at. It's not gourmet or original, but its a good sports bar and a welcome edition to the neighborhood.

I really have to add to my prior comments.  I saw their Facebook announcent that they were opening their outdoor beer garden last Friday afternoon.  We had other plans cancel due to the on and off rain.  So we said what they hell, lets see what it's like.  We wound up having the best time.  The outdoor space is really beautifully done.  Our server was incredibly friendly and accomodating.  And food is actually really good.  The coconut shrimp is a must have, in my opinion.  The chicken fingers are real white meat chicken strips.  A friend had the portobello burger and loved it.  All that said - yes, it feels more like a franchise place than an independent bar or restaurant.  But we had fun and the back garden is a winner.  Close to neighbors but with a sound barrier and more distance than Strand for sure.  They do, however, plan to open an outdoor bar. So I think they will have much more noise and activity outside than Strand.
