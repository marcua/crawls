Read all the points in the board and gather facts.

A pretty clear picture emerges that this is the result of poor management of our community by the elected officials, community boards and city planning.

Remember, do to their ineptness (or greed depending on how charitable you are) public services (sanitation, schools, etc) in this area is in a steep decline. With all those illegal conversions that CB1 so much likes (perhaps unique as community boards go in NYC), no one even has an idea of how many people live in the Con Ed's local service grid.

Con Ed, which depends on many of these same people for oversight, is getting set up as the fall guy (besides they are counting on the public with a short memory and if they play along with the culprits, stand to reap big rewards anyway in the continuing efforts to overdevelop the area)

Some say that a 100 year old utility never learned the simple act of delivering energy. My personal fantasy is that a UFO hovered over Big Alis and sucked up all the power.

The point is that we are expecting a big effort to be made to blame the utility. Clumsy orchestrating public opinion will not work this time. Other people are just as diligent in directing attention to the real parties that did this to us.

Even on this board, when confronted with facts (which you will never read in the paper or hear anyone on the community board or elected office tell you) opinion has changed dramatically in the past few days.

The crisis seems to be winding down. The echoes from this, we assure you, will take a long time to go away.
