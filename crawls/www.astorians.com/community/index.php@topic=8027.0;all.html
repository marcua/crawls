Good morning, all.  Ladywithpans, the potentially obtrusive TV screen/GPS screen, etc. can be turned off from the view of the passenger.  So that is a good thing, as it leaves viewing the screen as a matter of free choice.  But what is at issue right now is whether or not the GPS technology is linked to the fare meter, and whether the credit card reader will actually work.  My friend who worked for a few years for TLC got back to me:

As far as I know GPS is not tied to the meter’s charge, but GPS and meter info may be transmitted together to the TLC – not sure. 

The credit card reception issue may be a real problem, but cabbies may be able to move a few feet in a direction to re-establish connection.

Sorry that he wasn't more conclusive.  I will see if there is anyone else I can ask.  Sounds like the credit card reader will be a problem as far as everyone is concerned, but I still don't get why the meter would be connected to the GPS technology.  Maybe to verify that the car traveled the distance claimed by the meter?
