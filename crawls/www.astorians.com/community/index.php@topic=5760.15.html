I would say that Astoria's, if not teeming with, at the very least, riddled with hipsters. Just ride the N/W with your eyes open and try not to spit your coffee out. And this is coming from someone my wife jokingly describes as an "aging hipster" (though she may be borrowing the nomenclature from The Onion), i.e. someone old enough to be the parent of a "Bills-burg" hipster yet whose thrift store vibe is actually just really broken-in clothes he's owned for decades and whose vinyl record collection contains a high percentage of wax owned since childhood. And, yes, I despise them. IMHO, the only thing that saves Astoria from the throngs of would-be hipsters that swarm the L train is it's inconvenient distance from Williamsburg, the East Village and the LES, since those swaths of real estate are the hipster's spiritual epicenter. Let's just hope there's never a Southpaw/North Six/Warsaw in our parts. BTW, I saw Hank III at North Six and he shouted to the crowd at one point "We're playing three shows in Brooklyn and NONE in Manhattan!" as if that statement were somehow defiant. The poor guy thought he was being punk or something. He couldn't have picked a better representation of "the establishment" in which to play...

Nevertheless, I'm so ready for the BG to be written off as passe so that we can get back to enjoying it with out the 900+ Manhattanites wrapped around the block. (not that I have anything against Manhattanites--several of my friends are Manhattanites. In fact, I used to be one :lol:). Thankfully we have the Sparrow if the line's too long...

I'm on the fence with the whole "hipster" label.  In all honesty, many of my friends and myself, while walking down the street, based on appearance only, could be labelled as hipsters.  What does it really mean?  When I first heard the term, I was all too quick to join in with the chants of dislike toward this segment of society.  But come on...  When you get right down to it, any Caucasian in their early 20's to, in some cases, mid 40's walking down the street could be labelled as a hipster.  But all of my friends work day jobs and in most cases are just scraping by in this city.  They aren't funded by their parents.  They are really cool people that care about the community they live in and try to contribute something to it.

Of course I wouldn't label myself as a hipster, but who would?  I'm a jazz musician.  Does that make me a hipster?  My very good friend is a web designer who works from home in the East Village.  Does that make him a hipster?  How do you identify by appearance only which of these people are the rich spoiled brats you think they might be?

Just remember that when you point a finger at someone else, you have three pointing back at yourself, and a thumb, but that just points off in some random direction most of the time.
