Title: Loukoumi Taverna 4507 Ditmars Blvd, Astoria
Post by: loukoumi on October 24, 2008, 10:49:41 PM
My past experience of Greek restaurants in NYC was that the menu is not very interesting and that the food is rather greasy and not very fresh-tasting. However, I found that this new restaurant, Loukoumi, serves very different style of Greek food. We overjoyed when finding our favourite Tarama (Greek fish roe dip) on the menu, and it was so delicious; light and lemony. Fish roe may not sound very appetizing but, if you have never tried it before, I highly recommend to try it there. Also, they give you an option to have Calamari either fried or grilled. We ordered the grilled ones along with grilled octapus and sepia in a plate called thalassomeze if am not mistaken, and they were divine! They were tender and sweet, which is a good sign of freshness of squids used. The main courses (we ordered ) red snapper for my husband served with rice, potatoes or vegetable of the day, and the greek lamb stew topped with some kind of Greek cheese and berries ,were perfectly cooked and delicious, we were impressed with their lemon potatoes. It is completely different from what I had before in other restaurants. It was so nice to taste Greek food which is fresh-tasting, flavourful, and not heavy in your stomach.
We saw quite a few Greek wine that we have never seen or heard of. So, we are looking for going back to try them.

We also found the service very good our server Leonidas and the owner, who is Greek, were both very friendly and generous.

I highly recommend this new Greek restaurant and hope that many of you will try it.
