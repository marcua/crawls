Re: best pizza in astoria?
« Reply #40 on: September 10, 2008, 10:14:57 PM »
the best pizza by far is provided by one of the newest players in the astoria pizza game, MichaelAngelo's on 23ave just off 31st.  whether you go with the plain slice, or a wedge of the Sofia Loren fresh mozz drizzled with basil, or a square of the grandma's, you will be getting the best in astoria.  other notables in astoria are Rizzo's on steinway, Porto Bello in ditmars shopping center, and Polito's on broadway.

being i'm in the ditmars area, heres how i break it down:
- Frankies on 31st - better than average.  the health code thing is an issue, but i've never seen anything bad or had any bad experiences.  the sicilians are especially tasty as the sauce is made with onions.
- Alba's on ditmars - mediocre at best.  exactly middle of the road.  i remember when they downgraded to cheaper chesse instead of raising prices.  i stopped going.
- Porto Bello - you will like it.  better than alba's.
- Apecius on steinway between 20/21 aves - a hidden gem.  if in the area and hungy, you must give them a try and take a to-go menu because you will call them for delivery.
- Sandro's on ditmars/crescent - any pizzeria that in the window advertises their spanish food specials instead of their italian specials and has cooks that look like they only cook spanish food will slap some pizza together if you want some.  save your money.
- Astoria Park Pizza ditmars btw 21/19 st. - see sandro's above.  i've head many stories of people getting sick from this place, myself included.
- Teresa's 21st/21av - meh.  if your hungry, it'll satisfy, but its nothing to go out of your way for.  dirty and no AC.
- La Vita Mia 24ave 28st - had it once and it was real good.  the person who bought the pie was raving how good this place is.  the menu looks good.
- Rose and Joe's bakery ditmars train station - not like it used to be.
- the pizza deli on 48/ditmars - bwahhhhhhhhhhhhhhh, how is this place there so many years?
- Rosario's deli ditmars train station - good, but Michaelangelos is right around the corner and great.
- John's crescent / astoria blvd. - havent been there in years because the pizza was atrocious back then.
