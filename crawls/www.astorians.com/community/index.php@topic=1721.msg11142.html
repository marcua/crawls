I usually do tip around 15%, but then your question prompted me to go to Google and find that tipping is not really expected by massage therapists. This is a good online discussion that you might want to read.

After seeing that, I think I'll continue tipping Maria at SPAce, since I've already established that practice. But with anyone else, I'll take into account whether or not they're already receiving the full fee and whether I'm there for relaxation or therapy. It seems that some therapists feel just a little insulted if you tip, whereas no one feels insulted if you don't.

You might like to try The Emerald Spa http://www.emeraldspa.net/
What's good about it is that you can use the spa facilities before topping it all off with a massage.
The best massage I've gotten there was from a woman named Dina. You might want to ask for her.  If you don't specify, there's a chance you might get a male therapist, which might make some women a tad uncomfortable.

One big drawback they have, though, is that the showers in the women's locker room have NO COLD WATER - just scalding hot water, which renders them unusable. Apparently, this weird situation has been this way for a long time because there is a permanent sign in the locker room warning of this. Why they don't address the situation is beyond my understanding. I'd go there more often if I could use the shower to wash the pool chlorine from my hair before leaving. As it is now, I have to go right home afterwards to shower, shampoo and dry my hair. I only live two blocks from there, but still it's a drag. I'm sure it'd be an even bigger drag for anyone who lives further away.
