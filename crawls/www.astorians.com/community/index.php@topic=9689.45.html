I like Grand Cafe. They have outdoor seating, $11 for food, coffee, and drink (i think), and the best mini-muffins EVER. Service is slow though, and it's a total scene. For a diner brunch, the place on the corner of Broadway and Steinway is good, high-quality ingredients, good coffee, service, etc.

718 - Food was just so-so, drinks were terrible (WAY too much booze in the bloody mary, it was just red-colored vodka) and high prices. Nice to be able to sit outside on Ditmars, but not worth it, IMO.

Igloo - ok, but definitely get the home fries. Hash browns are a soggier, straight-from-the-freezer version of McD's.

Diners - why not? No booze though.

Stove - pretty good, but the coffee isn't very high quality (but it IS expensive), the options are limited, and it's a bit expensive for what you get.

Eurodelights - I like it, but the atmosphere leaves something to be desired.

That place on Broadway at 21st is terrible! It's glorified diner food
