I've belonged to the Rock for five years. As someone said, yes, it is pricey but it's clean,
well-maintained and the variety of equipment and classes is great.

They do belong to Global Fit if you have that option. They also give discounts to certain union
members -  I know SAG was one, and I think police department staff also. You'd have to ask.

One thing they do allow is that if you want to pay in cash, they'll give you a cash discount. I make
two cash payments, one in the late fall and another a few months later.

I also belonged to Synergy for a few years, but left because it was filthy. Mice running around in the
workout area, roaches in the locker room, etc. Plus they had very few classes and when I asked a
trainer if he was ACE certified, he asked what that meant.

I toured Evolution just before it opened and it looked good. Nice layout, lots of equipment and the
people I talked to were friendly. If it was closer to me, I'd have considered it more seriously.

Location is key - luckily now in Astoria you have a few choices. You should go in and visit the
clubs you're interested in. The Rock will give you a three-day pass to try it out. I think others will too.

You have to go when you would normally use the club to get a feel for it.

Good luck.

I've been members of NYSC and Synergy and I'm currently at Club Fitness.  I liked NYSC but it's a little out of the way for me.  I joined Synergy as a supplement to NYSC (since I was mainly going to the one near my office at the time), but only ended up going about 8 times because the staff there is so obnoxious.  Plus, I felt very claustrophobic there.  It's not the worst gym and seems cleaner as some of the other ones described, but I really don't like to be hassled when I'm trying to just trying to mind my own business and get my workout done.  

Club Fitness is wonderful!  I made the sales team promise me when I joined that they'd never hassle me about a trainer and they've kept their word - not they're in the practice of hassling people to begin with, but still I wanted to make sure I never had a repeat of my Synergy experience.  I joined Sept. of last year and my fee was $79/month, but I paid it all upfront.  I'm guaranteed to have the same rate again this year, but hopefully I can get some kind of discount for paying up front again.  It's a little pricey, but it'll be especially pricey for me this year because my work no longer helps subsidize our gym memberships.  Stupid economy!

Pluses of Club Fitness:
brand new, top-of-the-line equipment
- very clean
- nicest locker rooms I've ever seen - they each of jacuzzis along with steam and sauna rooms
- lots of aerobic classes to choose from
- almost never any waits for cardio machines
- friendly staff

Minuses of Club Fitness:
- hand towels tend to run out often, esp. during the 7pm weekday peak time
- no hand towels at the front check-in desk
- not enough stretching areas - there's only one large mat and that only typically holds about 4 people
- music can sometimes be a little too loud for my tastes - I don't like that my iPod has to compete with it.
