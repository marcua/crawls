I and most of family (aunts and cousins) were taught that abstinence before marriage is important (and that to do otherwise is a sin). Some of us adhered to that and some of us didn't, but we all seem to be happy with ourselves, as far as I can tell (I haven't taken a poll, but sometimes people offer a lot of information :P)

People can make a choice to not have sex before marriage and be happy with that choice.

On the darker side, I did know one girl, a minister's daughter, who got pregnant when she was 18. She actually managed to hide her pregnancy until her 3rd trimester. Once her mother figured it out, she was very much taken care of by her family. They supported her and as far as I could tell the people in the church more or less minded their own business. I'm sure there was gossip, but I never heard any of it. It's all water under the bridge now. Her son was thoroughly spoiled and adored by everyone in the community.

There are two problems that I see here:
1) I have no idea whether or not the girl was aware of safe sex practices and whether or not they were used, but that question does come up
2) That (to certain minds) not only is sex a sin, but that being sinful (or faulty, if you prefer) and human are inadmissable crimes. People are welcome to promote chastity as far as I'm concerned, but they should give their children adequate information to take care of themselves and they should make it VERY clear to their children that they will love and support them regardless of their choices.

Of course, not everyone does love and support their children regardless of their choices. Heck, some people beat their kids. Some people don't give a flying f___ what their kids even do. At least the abstinence-only crowd seems to care what their children do - but for the right reasons?
