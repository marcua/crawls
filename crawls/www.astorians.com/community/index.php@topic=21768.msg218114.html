Re: Live Greek music in Astoria?
« Reply #3 on: October 13, 2011, 02:24:58 PM »
Lafayette grill is more grungy, but their repertoire is more Balkan, not only Greek. Always had a blast there. It's lively. Rafina is more upscale, and also caters to the older crowd. Good times have been had there too. Jimbos can be more exciting though if you can look past the "old timers".

Most live Greek music places don't draw younger crowds anymore. The younger generation mostly frequents the cafes, bars, lounges, clubs.


I would suggest starting at a place in the city, and ending up at Jimbos or Filarakia, that way you can see for yourself the differences. Also the Astoria places tend to get busy later on in the night.
