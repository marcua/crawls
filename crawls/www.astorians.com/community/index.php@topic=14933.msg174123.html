Stopped in after work on yesterday.  We got there at 4, right when it opened, so we basically got our pick of any table in the joint. My first impression of the place, that was better verbalized by another member of the group I was with that this was TGI Beergarden's.  It's obvious that it's still very new, and that both a lot of money and attention to detail have been put into this place.  We settled into our drinking, I started with a personal pitcher (a liter) of beer while I waited for more people to show up, and tried to take in the space a little more.  I loved that there are more places to buy booze, and from the computerized registers, I incorrectly assumed that they took cards, which my girlfriend later found out was not the case, which is weird.  Why bother with crazy computerized registers and receipts if you're not going to take cards?  There are enough bars that they could easily have a dedicated charge only line.  But I digress.  I was really enjoying it, until about 9 or so.  It got to the point where it was really crowded, and between the ambient noise of the crowd, the music (which was tolerable, but not great) and my poor hearing, I was having a really hard time carrying on a conversation. I was also not totally sold on the restrooms.  The private stalls can and likely will become an issue.  It was a great idea, but in practice, there's pretty much always going to be a line when you're expelling all that delicious beer.
All in all, it's nice having another large outdoor space where I can drink and smoke and conversate (if I'm there early enough).  I just think that this place will be utilized in the same way as Bohemian Hall for me, early Sunday drinking, as I'm too tired to put up with a lot of the antics (beer pong, flip cup, push up contests, dancing on tables) that seem like are on their way. 
My list of pros and cons:

Pros:
+ Closer to home.
+ More beer selection and a wider range of non-beer drinks (the sangria on tap was a hit with my group).
+ Enough space, it seems both inside and outside.
+ Lots of space
+ It may shrink the crowd at Bohemian Hall
+ Outdoor, walk up grill
+ Nice, new everything, for now

Cons:
- Nice, new everything (I like a little dinginess)
- Separate bathrooms
- Slightly more expensive - although the liter option seems like the more economical way to go vs pitchers
- The music was a little loud
- Too much Ed Hardy / bro dawg for my tastes
