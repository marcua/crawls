It's short for LIMOUSINE.  You ever heard of that?"  Not wanting to agitate her any further, I resigned to the fact that it would be a tight ride and said, "It was just a simple question.  Forget about it."  Any sane person would have ended it there, but instead she screamed, yes SCREAMED, "SHUT UP!!!".  I couldn't help it, I had to laugh.

She got off before me in Astoria and on her way out, swung her heavy bag at me in an attempt to physically hurt me.  She missed.  Man, I wish I had tripped her.  Is that bad?

She probably would've attempted to murder you. You gotta assume in situations like that that the lady is crazy as soon as she sits down. You knew this before you said anything because she squeezed into a space that was too big for her along with a big, heavy bag that she hit you with. Clearly your comfort was not a concern of hers, and the fact that you spoke up was an affront to her. If you must protest in situations like that, I find it's best to do so in a way that appears oblivious.

A few days ago, I was on a rush hour R train and there was a dude leaning against the one pole I could get to. Now, there was a slight gap between one section of his back and the pole that I could grab onto, but unfortunately he was wearing a big messenger bag with the strap running down the full length of the pole. Instead of asking this guy to hold onto the pole properly, or asking him to move his bag so I could hold onto the pole without his strap pressing against my hand, I held onto the strap and the pole at the same time. This meant that if he swayed at all, he would get pulled back because I was essentially holding on to his bag. He glanced over at me a couple times when this happened, but I just stared blankly. He apparently wasn't bothered enough to move, or he realized that he was being a dick for standing like that in the first place and he didn't want to move regardless, but for me the aggravation turned into vague amusement on my way to work.

As for how this strategy would apply to your scenario.. I dunno, I probably would've found a way to disgust her without making it look like I was doing it on purpose. I liked Yaxpac's idea of the involuntary spasm. Sometimes the only way to fend off crazy people is to out-crazy them.
