Can they give you 3 tickets for the same thing?
« on: July 11, 2010, 11:29:28 AM »
I renewed my NYS registration online before it lapsed.  Received an email notice back stating that my new sticker would come in the mail in 1-2 weeks.  It came a week later, but in the meantime, I was given 3 tickets 3 days in a row for not having a valid registration sticker.  I contested the tickets with the explanation and proof that my registration had never lapsed and that the new sticker was not available at the time of the tickets.  Thought that should be a sufficient and valid explanation to get the tickets dismissed, but I received notices back that my appeal was DENIED due to the tickets being for "not having a valid registration sticker" not the inspection being overdue (the fact that the sticker was unavailable at the time was never addressed in the appeal denial explanation). 

Extremely angry, and I'd like to fight this further, since the new sticker wasn't available, and that's why it wasn't on the car.  These tickets are for $65 each ($195 total) and I really don't have the extra money to pay when I did nothing wrong.  Paying just one will still make me angry, but 3 for the same thing?!  I tried to research if they can give me three tickets for the same thing but didn't see any definitive answer.  I was wondering if anyone had any experience with this and could tell me if they are allowed to give me three tickets for the same thing?  I'd understand if they caught me speeding 3 days in a row or parking in a handicapped spot 3 days in a row, but for what I supposedly did, it seems excessive and duplicative.

Yes, they can and will.  The meter maids tend not to think when issuing tickets.  I guess they assume that the "judicial process" will take care of it if you are actually innocent.  Unfortunately, it seems that the judges who review these things either don't believe anyone or don't really read the defenses (I've never seen a ticket dropped unless there was an error in the writing of the ticket itself, like the wrong plate #).  There really isn't much to do about it unless you want to contact a lawyer.

When I first moved, I had a registration sticker but not an emissions sticker.  I had gotten an inspection but failed one of the tests.  When that happens, you have 10 days to fix the issue and get re-tested, and they give you a little slip of paper to put in your window in lieu of the inspection sticker.  Despite the fact that it was dated, I got 5 or 6 $65 tickets from meter maids who clearly do not care whether you are actually in violation of anything. They do not think.  I contested every one (online) with the same explanation, and some were dropped and a couple were reduced.  It's an extremely poopie system.
