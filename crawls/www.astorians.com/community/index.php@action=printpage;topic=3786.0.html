Post by: lanseaux on October 13, 2006, 12:01:32 PM
This is what you have to do:

On Fridays between 8am and 1pm, you have to go to the warehouse of the Acme Smoked Fish Company - that's right, the folks who make most of the lox, etc. that you see in the supermarkets, and also the company that smokes the house brand for places like Zabar's.  You can get it whole or sliced, although I recommend the whole.

The texture and taste is like nothing I have ever experienced.  I guarantee it is better than Zabar's.

Here (http://www.acmesmokedfish.com/retail/fridays.html) are the details.  Take the Pulaski Bridge into Greenpoint, go straight on McGuinness to Meserole, turn right on Meserole, and they are at the end of Meserole on Gem St.

I think they're called "appetizing" (n.b.: it's a noun, not an adjective) because they were originally served as pre-meal snacks on special occasions in the Old Country (I'm thinking the English term is analogous to the Yiddish/German "forshpeis").  I know few people outside of the Eastern European Jewish matrix who dig this stuff, but if you're open-minded, then I encourage you to give it a try.  I think lox (smoked salmon) is probably the safest place to start.  The hard thing is that there is a HUGE world of difference between mediocre lox and really good lox.  I would advise getting a bagel-creamcheese-and-lox sandwich at Zabar's Cafe (80th and B'way, I think)- they have a breakfast deal where you get one of those sandwiches, a coffee, and an orange juice for five bucks (great deal).  Or, as Jonanthan suggested, Russ and Daughters at 1st and 1st (nexus of the universe) will also make you such a sandwich.  If you like that, you can move on to-- whitefish salad!  And then whitefish!  and then chubbs.  and then sabe, herring....  ahhhhh
