Time to derail the hyperbole wagon and get down to real facts.

28grand, first you want to blame the banks for supposedly skimping on security. Next it's the criminals. Which is it?

The chance of a violent incident occurring during a bank robbery is very low, and bank robberies only make up around 2% of total robberies committed.

http://www.fbi.gov/ucr/cius2006/offenses/violent_crime/robbery.html

I'm trying to find more recent stats, but these are from 2002 regarding bank robberies:

Quote
The incidence of violence and injury is very low. NIBRS data show that violence occurred in only 2.34 percent of incidents and BCS shows 4.84 percent over the time period. Given the low rates of violence, it should not be unexpected that the injury levels displayed in Table 5.8 are also quite low—5.58 percent for NIBRS data and 2.00 percent for BCS.

Regarding other crimes present in the incident, murder is very low at less than 1.0 percent in both databases, as are kidnapping and hostage-taking. Both NIBRS data and BCS data show that kidnapping/hostage-taking occurs in less than 2.0 percent of reported bank robberies.

Bank robbery happens. There is no "blame". Be grateful we don't have people getting shot and killed on a regular basis on our neighborhood streets.

I'd rather be "victim" of a bank robbery, frankly. You are less likely to get injured in a bank robbery than in a mugging -- and of course more likely to get your money back, too. Add to that the draconian laws in the city which prohibit honest citizens from adequately protecting ourselves in our own homes, and being caught in a bank during a robbery is almost a picnic.

And if banks were more secure and deterred would-be robbers, I wonder how many would instead resort to more violent crime for their need for cash?

In general, I blame criminals for committing crimes.

Bank robberies are on the increase. Do a search "robbery, increase" and you'll find numerous articles dating back to 2003 documenting triple digit increases in the number of them so this is hardly a new phenomenon.

Some theories have been put forth and one of them postulates that the reason for the increase in robberies is that because banks are easier to rob. Banks should correct this either by better physical design and layout and/or increased security measures.

This may be your opinion but it isn't fact and frankly, for myself, I'd prefer not be a victim of, or involved in, any crime including bank robbery. I also feel I shouldn't have to feel grateful for being able to walk the streets without being shot or killed. I may be a dreamer but I still feel I live in a city where this is an anomaly, not the norm.
