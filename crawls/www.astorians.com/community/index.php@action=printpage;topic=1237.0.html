Appeal to Majority
Ex1:
Quote
"Nine out of ten dentists prefer Bleeding Gums Toothpaste:-)(TM)... therefore Bleeding Gums Toothpaste:-)(TM) is good"
Ex2:
Quote
"Everyone believes that the Earth is Flat...therefore the Earth is Flat."

Appeal to Emotion
Ex1:
Quote
"A man stands before a Jury of his peers. It is their job to find whether he is guilty or not.
The defending Counsel looks them all in turn, slowly says...
...with tear in eye </sniffle>...
'When my client was a child,
His father...
beat him with a Log! Therefore my client is innocent.'
Now although this argument does have a certain validity and may (or may not)  move the hearts of the jury to go easy on him, it still has no bearing on the question of guilt or innocence."
Appeal to Ignorance
Ex1:
Quote
"There are weapons [of mass destruction] because Iraq cannot prove that they don't have weapons."-XlogicX (recapitulating argument for the Invasion of Iraq)
Ex2:
Quote
"No one has yet disproved the existance of the Mighty yet curiously shy Yeti. Therefore the Yeti exists."
Ex3:
Quote
"In the above example replace Yeti with Lochness Monster."

Quotes:

Ad Hominem (toward the man)
Ex1:
Quote
"Aristotle said it, therefore it is true."
Ex2:
Quote
"Aristotle said it, therefore it is false."

Begging the Question Repeating the premise as a conclusion
Ex1:
Quote
"The world is the way it is because that's the way it is."
Ex2:
Quote
Parent: I told you not to play video games now!
Child: Why can't I play video games?
Parent: You can't because I told you so. Now go to bed!

Post Hoc Ergo Propter Hoc (after this because of this)
Many supertstitions fall under this heading.
Ex1:
Quote
"I walked under a ladder the other day and then a bucket of paint fell on my head. Walking under ladders is bad luck."

The following two fallacies will accept the following statement as true:
"If it rains then the street gets wet."

Denying the Antecedent denying the 'if' part of the statement
Ex1:
Quote
It's not raining therefore the street is not wet."

Affirming the Consequent justifying the 'then' part of the statement
Ex1:
Quote
The street is wet, therefore it is raining."

Note: Fallacies are not necessarily wrong, for example in the above fallacy it would be incorrect to assume it is raining by the information given.  It would also be illogical to assume that it must not be raining because the above statement is a fallacy.
"Just because it's a fallacy doesn't mean the conclusion is false."-XlogicX
Note: Upon further inspection XlogicX communicated to me that this may also in fact turn out to be a fallacy... and aptly named the "fallacy fallacy". [!]

Hypothesis Contrary to Fact
Ex1:
Quote
If those kids didn't see that [violent] movie then they wouldn't have done that shooting.

counter example:
Quote
If those kids mothers and fathers had never copulated then they would never have been alive to commit that shooting.

more of this?:
Quote
if there was never a Big Bang then Hitler would have never risen to power.

Non Sequiter This fallacy is difficult to explain. The components of the argument are random and have no causal relationship to each other.

Example:
Quote
Butterflies in Fields of Wheat in pre harvest season look pretty. Drink Coke!
