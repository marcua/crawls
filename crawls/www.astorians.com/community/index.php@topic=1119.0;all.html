To answer a few questions:

wustoff or henkel knives are great and easy to find all over town.  

My personal opinion is that wustoff knives are better than henkel simply because the blade has a more pronounced curve and therefore has a better rocking motion for chopping.

A good knive or 3 can be a great thing to have and will last for years.

What you want is a knife that has one piece of metal running from the point of the blade right back to the end of the handle.  the handle should form around that back piece of metal.  does not matter if the handle is metal or composite (most are not wood anymore).

NEVER BUY A MACHINE STAMPED KNIFE (like the ginsu)

You should never machine wash a good knife (not that any of us have a dishwasher anyway.

What you need are the basics:
1. 8" chef knife,  if you have small hands then a regular 8" chef is fine.  if you have big hands then you might want to look into a wide or double wide blade to accomodate your chubby fingers.

2.  paring knife (not used so much but good to have)

3.  either a serrated bread slicer (preferably an offset model) OR a boning knife for fish and meats.

Im assuming that most of you are not butchering your own meats and fish so stick with the serrated knife.  this offset knive is also great for slicing and dicing (especially when your knife dulls a bit and you are too lazy to get it sharpened.  the offset is serrated so it will never dull.  well actually it will in about 5 yr, then just buy another.)


go to bed bath and beyond or macys cellar.  look at a chef knife, paring knife set, then either buy a matching offset for about 30-40 bucks OR take a field trip down to bowery and houston streets. right there on the corner is chef supply. buy a $12 offset and use the extra savings to buy a few cool little gadgets for your kitchen and impress your friends.  Maybe buy a cheap $15 boning knife too. I love mine.

The last thing two items you need are:
1. a diamond steel for keeping your knive sharp and alligned. $30-$40.  why a diamond one rather than a regular steel?  a regular steel is 1/2 the price but not as good.

2.  Smith's Knife Sharpener: Smith's JIFF V SHARP Knife Sharpener, SM-JIFFSM-JIFF  $6.97  (go online and find this item. its cheap and works better than any high end home knife sharpener)

3. bonus item would be a wood knife block or magnetic strip to keep your knives save and away from a drawer where they will bang around and stab you when you go in to get something else.

If you are really into this then go to JB Prince and look around at what they have.  better knives at very good prices.


Hope this helps.

First, a knife with the blade through the handle is called a "Full Tang" blade, meaning the blade continues through the handle for strength and balance. This applies to swords and hunting knives too.

Balance, that is something that hasn't been brought up in this thread.Part of the appeal of a really well made knife, besides the quality of it's edge, is its balance. How it feels in your hand. This is especially true in a larger knife like a 10 inch Chef knife that is "rocked" when used for cutting.

About the wood handles. They are not as sanitary, so you see them less, although the Japanese still seem to like them for their sushi preps. Globals are all steel. BTW they have two lines, stamped and hand forged w/ differing prices for apparently similar knives.

Knife blades come in various types of stainless steel with differing Rockwell hardness ratings. If memory serves, stainless steel incorporates chromium into the alloy. No rust but not the sharpest. The sharpest metal is high carbon steel blades. Very sharp and hard but prone to rust so care needs to be taken to oil them after cleaning....like an iron skillet. Also available in sharp metal would be "Damascus Steel" It is very distinctive with what looks like tree rings or swurls of purple and blue in it. Expensive but very, very sharp. The steel is folded over and over during forging so that it approaches lamination. Hard to find too. The very sharpest is the ceramic knives. Scalpel sharp! Kyrocera (sp?) sells them. No rust but brittle.
