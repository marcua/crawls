I would have to strongly disagree with this statement. Yes, some homeless people are mentally ill (about 15%), but it is completely untrue that they will always remain homeless and asking for money. There is an amazing organization in New York (that is also expanding to other major cities) called Pathways to Housing that deals exclusively with homeless individuals with psychiatric difficulties. They put the homeless into housing first and foremost, then deal with mental illness - though treatment is not mandatory. Pathways has support from both Democrats and Republicans because it costs far less than typical homeless programs - the public cost for each person in Pathways is about $16K a year rather than the $40K+/per person it costs taxpayers for a person to live on the streets and/or in shelters - and has been shown to be incredibly effective - about 85 percent remain housed. FYI, those are facts I pulled from their website, http://www.pathwaystohousing.org/, but have also been published in peer-reviewed psychology journals.

I'm not not affiliated with or seeking donations for Pathways, just trying to illustrate that these kinds of dismissive attitudes about the homeless just further the negative stereotypes that keep so many from receiving the help that is needed.

In order to begin a job, all applicants (US born or otherwise) have to prove that they are legally able to work.  To do so, we must show (from a list of documents) something that proves identity (like a drivers license) AND something that proves citizenship or permanent residence (like a birth certificate from an US hospital).  A "Green Card" and a Passport establish both.  For many people, homeless or otherwise, it is difficult to pull together the paperwork (if you are poor chances are you haven't the need for a passport, how many people have an original passport in their posession, etc). 

Of course, there are under the radar jobs, but that is another story.  McDonalds and other large corporations require this, by law, as do all other legit jobs.
