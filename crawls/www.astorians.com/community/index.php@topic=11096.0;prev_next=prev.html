Italian dressing in United States cooking is a vinaigrette-type salad dressing consisting of an emulsion of water, oil, chicken stock or the juice from a cooked chicken, vinegar or lemon juice, salt, pepper, minced onions and bell peppers, often sugar, or high fructose corn syrup, and a variety of herbs and spices including garlic, oregano, fennel, and dill. It is often bought bottled, or prepared by mixing oil and vinegar with a packaged flavoring mix consisting of dehydrated vegetables and herbs.

The U.S.-style Italian dressing is unknown in Italy, where salad is normally dressed at table with olive oil, vinegar, salt, and sometimes black pepper, and not with a pre-mixed vinaigrette.

Quote from: enigmacat on June 27, 2008, 03:28:46 PM
A bar called Burp Castle?



I love this city.

It's a freakin' great place. Burp Castle serves mostly strange imported beers that are harder to find on tap. They tend to lean toward the Belgian variety, with some German, Czech and small American breweries. They only have 9 taps, but they rotate with each keg, so you can go two days in a row and have a totally different drinking experience. They do beer tastings with local home brewers, free pomme frites and cheese and beer pairing nights.

Next door is Standings, a sports bar with 12 taps that rotate just like Burp Castle, but theirs focus mainly on American small company IPAs, stouts and lagers. They show all your favorite sporting events with a focus on non-NYC and collegiate teams. They frequently have nights with free pizza, burgers and the like to celebrate certain sporting events or special tap nights. They had an Abita night and a Smuttynose night not too long ago.

Underneathe the two is Jimmy's No. 43, which has a variety of great taps, but also makes a boss dinner.
