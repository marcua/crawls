I just moved into Astoria a couple of weeks ago.  The building is old - but thankfully I haven't seen a single bug - especially no water bugs.   My old place had a serious water bug problem. While it was no where near the water, we had excessive humidity from constantly leaking steam pipes and large service basements where the bugs must have thrived.

No matter how many times you encounter these creatures, the shock factor never diminishes.  However, with so many encounters, one does have the opportunity to prefect extermination techniques.  And baring the interesting device which started this post, I've tried killing these bastards in a lot of different ways.

The two methods I have found most effective are as follows:

Using the sticky glue mouse traps - which can catch and kill up to 6 water bugs when placed in an area of known bug traffic.  As long as the traps are out of sight (under a sink, couch, or cabinet) you only need get grossed out when you go to throw away the trap and replace it with another.

For those face to face encounters I found that a handful of moist paper toweling makes an excellent bug missile.  The water makes the paper towel heavy enough to smash the water bugs with.  It  completely stuns and immobilizes them (sometimes it can kill them instantly if thrown hard enough).  Then, simply scoop and flush.

But I sure hope I never have to do that gain; I'm looking forward to more peaceful times in my new digs.
