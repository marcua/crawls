2 ~ The new tenant has no responsibility to me, so if they give me a check that bounces, I have very little recourse in taking them to court to get that money.  Whereas if the landlord didn't give me back my deposit, I have something substantial to fight for.


the new roommate does have a financial responsibility to you as they are assuming your responsibilities for the apartment. we have one room in our apartment that we have turned over 5 times. If the roommate you are leaving behind is getting a new roommate then you ask that person for your share of the security (as they will assume getting the security from the landlord or the next tenant) and ask for it in cash. we've never had a problem getting the old roommate the security from the new roommate. you'll just have to talk to your current roommate about what she plans to do in terms of replacing you.
your landlord owes you nothing unless both of you move out ending your rental agreement. as long as one original tenant is there the landlord has every right to keep the deposit in full. if your current roommate will assume the whole apartment herself then she needs to buy out your share of the security deposit.
