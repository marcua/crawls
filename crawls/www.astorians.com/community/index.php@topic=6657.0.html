Arugh! I had a big 'ol response written, and then I accidentally closed the browser. D'oh! Lemme see if I can remember what I wrote...

My friends and I went to Block Island for 4 nights last summer around this time (last week of June after they were done with school, they're teachers). We stayed at a B&B called The Island Home, it was at Ocean Ave & Beach Ave. There are a ton of B&B's on the island, and one big hotel (The National). Since its their high season, everything's a bit pricey, so shop around. It worked out for us since there were 4 of us to split it. The Island home is definitely recommended by me, Dina & Dave were totally nice. I'm usually weirded out by B&Bs, but I was comfortable there.

It is a ferry to Block Island. From Rhode Island, you'd get it in Port Judith. You can put the car on the ferry (I forget how much extra it costs, but if its more than just you, it can be split), or you can just go over without the car and walk/use cabs/rent a bike. Cabs are fairly pricey there, so use sparingly. For whatever its worth, I wish we had taken the car onto the ferry, but we had a pregnant friend with us and ended up taking more cabs then we expected. The beach is a little out of the way too, and you may want to car/cab/bike it there. I guess the car decision depends on how long you plan on staying (we stayed for 4 nights), if there will be other people with you, and if you plan on going out at night.

Oh! Also! If you're staying for more than a night or so and anticipate buying snacks/beer for your room/whatever, buy it on the mainland. Supermarkets there are really expensive since everything has to be shipped in.

There's not much nightlife, but there are places to go out to eat, and there's a bar where it seems like the younger, 20s-ish crowd (who probably work the downtowny area by day) hangs out at night. If I recall correctly, its on Ocean Ave near Connecticut Ave. I forget what it's called though (something with a Pirate maybe?)

Even if you do bring a car, rent a bike one day and bike around the island. It was so much fun...just make sure you bring water with you, convienence stores are hard to come by outside of the towny area.

Overall, Block Island was cute and low-key. If you're looking for lots and lots to do, you're not going to find it there, but if you're looking to get away and hang out in a beachy town, then its a good spot to go. I'd definitely go back for a few days to unwind.

If I remember anything else, I'll post again!

I grew up in Rhode Island and actually vacationed on Block Island for three years.  One thing that pops up first is that it is very difficult in the summer to make a reservation to get your car on the Ferry, though we always took the Ferry, with car, from Pt. Judith to Block Island.    You could also I think take the Ferry from Providence to Block Island, but that is an awful long time to be on a ferry and you would miss Newport.  You don't really need a car on Block Island.   You can rent bikes right near where you get off the Ferry

In Newport, you can actually tour some of the mansions and see what America was like before the days of the income tax.    What is even better is to take a walk on the cliffwalk (which is free), which is a pathway that runs along the cliffs with the water below on one and the back of the mansions on the other.   

More locally, I get a kick of out this restaurant chain that billed itself as part of "The New York System."   These chains served hot weiners (specially cooked hot dogs smothered in mustard, meet sauce, onions and celery salt).   Trouble is there isn't a "New York System" system in New York.

And if you go to a water fountain we called it a bubbler in Rhode Island.   And I grew up near that coffee milk plant, which was on the Warwick/Cranston line.   The coffee milk was called "Eclipse."    A friend's mother worked in the plant.

Finally, there is another New England brand of soda called Moxie.   I have never been brave enough to try it.   You can get it in bottles in supermarkets.

And don't forget Narrangansett beer, which was billed as "Our Own New England Beer" when I was growing up.   It was brewed in Cranston, but the owners sold it to Falstaff, the new owners closed the brewery and began brewing it in Indiana and then proceeded to run the brand into the ground.   An entrepreneur has bought the name and is now brewing 'Gansett once again.

And recommendations on restaurants, hangouts, and sights would be awesome! 

There is not much in Downtown Providence proper, though the waterfire is there.   There is a great Italian "ghetto" called Federal Hill within walking distance of downtown.   The city had an Italian Mayor -- the notorious Vincent "Buddy" Cianci -- and he threw a lot of money Federal Hill's way and it is charming.   Lots of good Italian restaurants.   I grew up with the guy who (now) runs the family restaurant "The Old Canteen" so that one is as good as any.

The other part of Providence that is probably worth your time is the East Side, where the rich people live and where Brown University and the Rhode Island School of Design are located.   Lots of streetlife, bookstores, bars, restaurants et al.
