Quote
Make it more expensive for companies to do business in a place, they'll move. Make it more expensive for people to live in a place, they'll move.
Well said.  This is an immutable fact of business.

So when it costs a trucking company a $125 an hour to keep a truck on the road (between salary, fuel, insurance, vehicle amoritization, maintenance), $8 is a drop in the bucket.  If congestion pricing saves them 10 minutes throughout the entire day, it just paid for itself.  Why do people bother to pay for the Queens Midtown Tunnel when they have the free Queensboro Bridge right next to it?  Because its pennywise/pound foolish for a business to spend 10 minutes to save $4.50.  Megc's chart shows how congestion costs the city's busuinesses & residents real money.  Any business you drive out of Manhattan with this plan probably should not have been located there in the first place.

Also, municipalities are not all the same, just as Bergdoff Goodman and Kmart are not the same.  Though expense of product can make a difference, there are two different markets here.  We used to go bananas around here because our market share of manufactured products dropped like a brick between the 50's and 90's.  And then we realized, we could never compete with the Jersey suburbs on this and the service/media industry which took its place is the real moneymaker.  People pay for convienence.  Congestion pricing is doing the same thing.

Take the $8 per car, plough it into rapid transit and make our already great transit system even better.
