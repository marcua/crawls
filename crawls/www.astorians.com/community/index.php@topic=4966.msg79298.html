Corner of 21st street and 24th Drive (last house before the TriBorough Bridge). We live on the 1st floor of a 3 family (originally it was a single family) detached house. (Landlady, landlady's son, occasionally landlady's son's gf, and since December Landlady's daughter and new twin grandchildren live upstairs. Someone with a sliding trombone lives downstairs but he only plays during daylight and i can't say the same for our beloved toddler, so i don't mind) Our private entrance opens up to a weird entryway room. On the right, two equally large bedrooms (we have a kingsize bed and a toddler bed next to each other on one wall, three dressers on the opposite wall, and two square closets in each bedroom) and a regular small bathroom with pink and black tile. To the left, one large enough walk through kitchen (completely unrenovated) to the awe-inspiringly huge, light living room with nice wooden floors.

Rent was 1500 when we moved in (three years ago next month!), got raised last year to 1650 and ready to argue if landlady tries to raise it again this summer. For a few days longer we have a subletter in the second bedroom, but he's found a better place and we're happy for him. Sniffle. (Maybe it's time beloved toddler had his own room...)

Pros: Huge apt, we enjoy the first and second sunsets the TriB gives us. (Really confused the first time the sun blinked out-- as the passing tractor trailers blotted it out briefly) Landlady takes care of things the second time we mention them (after I say, "shall we take care of it ourselves and send you the bill?"), and a mere block away from Astoria Park. Short enough commute to everything we need and have been here long enough that the neighbors who grew up here are starting to be friendly and involve us in neighborhood stuff. We have access to the big pleasant yard but not allowed to mess with it, so have a huge container garden to ease my need for green.

Cons: TriB and 21st St loud. Twins loud. (But then, so is our beloved toddler) Carpet in bedrooms not loud enough. Urge to *own* domestic situation, very loud. :*)

There are different configurations in NYCHA apartments, and I have the worst - when you open the front door you are right in the traffic pattern of the apartment - there's nowhere to step, right, left, or forward, to take off your shoes, so every time we walk in we are tracking dirt.  The idiotic arrangement also makes it difficult to bring furniture in, because when you open the apartment door, immediately opposite you is a closet.

The apartment is small.  Unlike other NYCHA apartments I don't have a dining area.  I have to use half my living room for the dining area.  My kitchen is half the length of a normal NYCHA kitchen, and wholly inadequate because the numbskulls (can you tell I'm angry?) who approved the "modernization" plan a decade or so ago put in box style lower cabinets instead of L shaped ones.  I have a lower cabinet about 4' wide with a door opening only 11" wide.  This replaced a cabinet with not only a normal sized door, but access through it's own door and the double door under the sink.  I now have a 4 foot long space perfect for storing things I never intend to use again! 

If the housing authority sold the buildings I would buy my apartment, though.  As the originator of this thread knows, the intersection of 36th Ave and 24th St is an ideal place to live!  About 5 blocks from the train, about 5 blocks from the river.  3 stops from Manhattan.  Great view of Manhattan from street level (I used to see it from my bedroom in my previous apartment.)  Now I have a great view of the first branch of a big tree.  It's like living in a tree house.  I have squirrel-scape.  In the Eastern distance I see the northward landing approach of LaGuardia Airport.  I have big windows and get sunlight almost all day.  But I absolutely needed to buy two air conditioners.  All my windows face the same direction - no cross ventilation. 

The sidewalks on my block are wide with open spaces at the corners.  I have a good view of the moon and there is enough open space to see constellations.  The housing projects are planted with many tall trees, so it is like living in a park.  If only more people thought of it that way and didn't drop their garbage and cleaned up after their dogs.  There are many comfortable benches to sit on.  Ravenswood is on 4 big city blocks, and, going north, are Queensview, also with many trees and benches, and Queensview West, which changed its name to Queensview North, also tree lined with benches.   

My taste in decoration is nautical cottage.  It was greatly helped along by a lighthouse shaped lamp I bought at the home furnishing store on Ditmars at 31st St for my living room.  I always have my walls painted white because it reflects light and keeps the apartment bright.  If I bought my apartment I would have furniture custom built to maximize storage and wall space with white wainscoting going half way up the walls and the upper half left bare to keep the airy feeling.  My livingroom chairs are too big for my space, but I love them - big pillowbacks from Sears in blue gingham and blue floral, and I love my blue gingham ottoman.  I have a white coffee table with wicker baskets that slide out from either the front or back of the table.

My bedroom has blueberry colored sheets and a blue and white delft comforter.  I had to buy cabinets on wheels for the bedroom because there is so little room to move furniture around when washing the floor.  I have wood furniture in a colonial style, and a collection of liquor bottles on a book shelf and various Barbies, including a princess in pink and an Irish princess in green.

I have many different fragrances of incense.  The fragrance I most recently burned is Ocean, which I bought at Target.  It is light and refreshing and smells the way I want my apartment to look.
