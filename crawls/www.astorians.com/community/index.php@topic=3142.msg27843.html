I once took a course in legal proofreading - I too was having problems finding work when I first moved here.  The woman who teaches this course also teaches the same course at NYU.  If you're interested, you can take a look at http://www.learnproofreading.com/index.html.

Basically, it's a two day course that prepares you for the tests given by proofreading agencies.  After the course is finished, you go on interviews at proofreading agencies and take their tests.  If you sign up with 3-5 agencies, it's likely that you'll be able to find steady work.  Now, proofreading didn't really pan out for me because I wasn't willing to work 2nd or 3rd shift (which have the most jobs available), but I did score pretty well on the tests after taking the course and I could have started working right away.  Having a certificate from this course counts for a lot, and the instructor also does a resume review.

So if typos and bad grammar get on your nerves, maybe this would be a good option for you!
