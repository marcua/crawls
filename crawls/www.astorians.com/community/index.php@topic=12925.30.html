What's with all the bitter commentary?  I am shocked that so many people seem so attached to what is an inherently generic and purely geographical name: Triborough. 

Naming landmarks, roadways, and major public infrastructure after historical figures enriches us all.  Millions of children will see or cross this bridge now and ask their parents who RFK was (or better yet, Googles him on their own).  And that result, is invaluable.

I went to college out in the midwest, and virtually no roadways have a name.  It's all I-76, I-470, I-80, etc.  Is that really the sort of empty culture that so many of you really want to live with?  For those of you who grew up in the NY area, think back to when you were in elementary school and learned about the exploration of Verrazzano, and remember that lightbulb moment when you connected the man to the bridge.  For those of you who were born after 1963, think of when you learned about JFK, and made the connection to the airport.

Oh, and this matter of the $4M the name change will cost is a pretty silly argument if you ask me.  That is a drop in the bucket of the state's overall budget.  So everyone just relax and enjoy the celebration of history.
