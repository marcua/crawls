Quote from: round2 on August 09, 2010, 02:05:29 PM
I think I'd be more likely to be with you on this if we were talking about Applebee's or McDonalds's, or even Five Napkin Burger.  These large well-capitalized corporate ventures have the resources for large teams of consultants and managers to plan their operations and staff training on a completely different scale than the incredibly brave, risk-taking entrepreneur(s) behind a labor-of-love like the Astor Bake Shop. 


I completely disagree.  It's about the people - not the corporate BS training.  As a small business owner you have way more flexibility to hire and fire people than one of the big chains.  I used to be involved with a small business and we got rid of people at the first sign that they weren't living up to our customer service goals.  Always greet and acknowledge customers (with eye contact) - no matter how busy you are.  Tell them you will be a minute if you have to - most people will understand.  You can't ignore your customers.

If I owned the bakery I'd take the opportunity to discuss this with my employees before it becomes an institutional problem.  If you see someone ignoring customers they get a warning.  Do it again and they can hit the road.  You can find plenty of people to do the job the way you want it done.  Just gotta be willing to do the work to make it happen.

I'm not here to pile on, but I also had an unpleasant experience at Astor Bake Shop last weekend.  While I was seated right away for lunch, no one returned to take my order for over ten minutes.  I found the staff to be aloof, glancing in my direction without ever wandering over. There were only three other customers in the whole place.   When I finally ordered, it was for the Warm Chicken Sandwich with a side of cole slaw and the aforementioned Sea Salt Lemonade. The Lemonade arrived first, and was bland, not citrus-y at all, and salty.  Well okay, I knew when ordering that it was flavored with salt, but the condiment added nothing to the drink whatsoever, and reminded me of a lemony dental rinse.  Then the sandwich arrived.  Having ordered off the blackboard, I looked forward to all the sandwich ingredients I saw listed there, including tomatoes.  Mine arrived without tomatoes, so when I was able to flag my server down (not once did any staffer come to the table on their own) I asked about the tomatoes and was bluntly informed that the sandwich did not come with tomatoes.  I pointed to the blackboard, where it was clearly listed, and my server deadpanned, "Well the board is wrong."  Not only that, I wasn't even offered an apology, or a couple of tomato slices.  I found this to be rather rude, and I assure you that my manner was calm and polite.  I finished the tomato-less sammy, which was pretty good otherwise, and moved on to the salad.  One bite told me it had been wrongly seasoned, was WAY too citrus-y (the citrus missing from my lemonade, perhaps?) with no balance of oil or sweetness.  It was barely edible, and I ate it only due to the small portion of the lunch.  At the end of the meal, I again had to flag down my server to get a check.  I left disappointed, although I always leave a good tip for some reason.  I could even have put up with the so-so food had the service been a little more on-point.  They acted as though they were doing me a favor each and every time.  I think they have more to work on than just staffing issues.  I found the whole experience to be regrettable and I won't be going back anytime soon.  Astoria is filled with delicious alternatives run by people who are actually glad you gave them a try.  For $15, this lunch was a bust.

I have never had any problem with Astor Bake Shop and I have been there at least twenty times since they have opened. 

The food is always great-the quality surpasses the majority of neighboring establishments.

They offer counter service.  They do not have waiters and most certainly do not have a host so I don't understand why anyone would expect table service?  The menu is tiny, decide what you would like to eat and order at the counter like everyone else.  If it is slow and you are lucky enough to receive table service, then count your blessings. 

I also think its unfair to go to a new establishment just once before going onto the internet and writing a negative review about it. 

If you absolutely hate the place and find yourself scared for life then simply don't go back. 

If you find room in your forgiving heart, give the place another shot and refrain from leaving negative feedback until then.

Regular customers who leave negative reviews after just one bad experience should be ashamed of themselves. 
If you have a problem more than once then take it up with the owner.

These reviews are not constructive, they are overly critical, especially for a new business, which the neighborhood is in terrible need of! 

I have met and spoke with the owner on several occasions.  He is approachable and he cares. 

There is a lot more room for business like Astor Bake Shop to open in Astoria if the community is more understanding and supportive. 

We need Astor Bake Shop just as much as Astor Bake Shop needs us.
