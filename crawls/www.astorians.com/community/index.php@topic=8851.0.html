I used to go to school near White Plains and I interviewed at a couple of jobs there, which were relatively near the train station.  I withdrew my application for one position because it would have been too much of a hassle for the salary to get to work.  

If you're job is close to the White Plains stop, you'll be ok; if not, it's a pain in the butt.  They have the Bee-line buses, which stop about a block and a half from the train station, but are infrequent and only run down the major streets.

In terms of your commute, I think you have the best time you will get, as far as the train option goes.  Depending on where you live in Astoria, it will take you approximately 20 to 30 minutes, maybe less, to get to Grand Central and, if you catch an express to White Plains, at minimum, it will be about 35 minutes.  Of course, you have to consider the wait time for departing trains at GCT, but they are running more "opposite direction" trains during rush hours as more people, like yourself, are working in Westchester and Connecticut.  

I would consider the laptop.  You should be able to get a seat at Grand Central going to work and coming back and you could always check newspapers (saves money) etc. before you get in.  Just don't do your work on the train!

Hope this helpful and good luck on your new job.

PS: Although people tend to be negative on White Plains because of the high cost of living there and the sort of sterile, corporate feel in some areas, I really liked going there during my four years at Purchase.  It has really "improved" (if you think giant, towering condos and more malls are an improvement) and they have some nice bars and restaurants there that you might want to check out.

I was gonna vote for the car too, but also take into consideration tolls over the Triboro/Whitestone ($8 a day on ez pass, $9 plus time in line with cash) plus gas (right now, gas is about $3.25, and if you get a 30mpg car, that's $5-6ish a day to White Plains). I'd hate the long train commute, but if my math is about right, that's a little under $300 a month in gas and tolls alone. Is the extra hour of sleep worth that? I'm not even taking into account car payments (whatever that is) + insurance (mine works out to about $105 a month) + maintenance, and I just had to put $1,200 into my car in the past 2 weeks - new tires then my shocks decided to suddenly go bye bye! All expected stuff (in the sense that I'd expect to have to get new shocks/mounts sometime since the car isn't even close to new, and yeah, those tires have needed replacing since the spring), but still, gah.

Anyway, I drive up to Westchester all the time for my job, and it is an easy drive in the morning. (Work does reimburse me for tolls and mileage though.) If you do end up driving, I prefer the Whitestone, even though the Triboro will save you a few miles. The few times I've taken the Triboro, it's been a mess, unless was like 7am. If you take the Whitestone, you'd only hit a bit of traffic before exit 13 on the Hutch and then some near the Cross Westchester.

As much as I love driving and owning a car, I vote for taking the train, unless someone is willing to give you a car with decent mileage for no dollars. Even if a cab from the train station to the office is $5-10 (and it probably won't be), you'd still be saving money. I commuted to the city from Bridgeport, CT for a couple years, and it isn't that bad. Also, use a laptop! You can play DVDs on it with headphones (I'm thinking Netflixing TV shows would be perfect since they can work out with the time of your commute), or do other stuff on it. But like Astoria Luv said - no work on the train! That's what work is for!
