Mount Sinai Sexual Assault and Violence Intervention Program Seeks Volunteers

Offers Training To Become Hospital Emergency Room Advocates For Survivors of Sexual Assault and Intimate Partner Violence

Become Certified By the New York State Department of Health Rape Crisis Program

The Mount Sinai Sexual Assault and Violence Intervention (SAVI) Program is recruiting volunteers to advocate for rape and domestic violence survivors in the Emergency Room of Hospitals in Queens. You bring your compassion and we’ll train you to offer emotional support and help survivors navigate medical and legal systems while in the ER. After completing the 40 hours of training (dates below), you will be on-call one shift a month to the following hospitals: Queens Hospital Center, Mount Sinai Hospital of Queens, Elmhurst Hospital Center.

No previous experience is required, must be at least 21 years old, and bilingual/bicultural women and men are encouraged to apply.

Why volunteer with SAVI?
SAVI is constantly creating a team of people who are helping, sharing and are making a positive impact in ways you may have never imagined. By joining our team you can help transform people’s lives that are truly in need!

Our volunteers come from different backgrounds: some are students, lawyers, housewives, psychologists, social workers, doctors, actors some are survivors of sexual assault and/or domestic violence, while others are people who are simply passionate about making a contribution.

We are looking for volunteers who live in Queens. Queens Advocates must live in western Queens (see zip codes below).  

Please call (718) 736-1288 or www.mssm.edu/savi
