Abusive, Dangerous 'Homeless.'
« on: September 30, 2007, 01:46:30 AM »
OK, I know  people are already rolling their  eyes, here comes  this  'right-wing'  guy now 'dumping' on the 'homeless.'   It's not  true.   I am talking about a different set of people.  It is not my intention and I would never hurt anyones family who were in such hard times. I don't want to  talk about myself and  my philanthropic 'charity' events, but, I do spend a good part of what  free time I have, contributing time and what little  money I have going to events helping poor children who are ill and for mentally and physically disabled Veterans and others. I also spend many a holiday season going to VA hospitals and Childrens Hospitals, and even worked  in  pantry. I'm not writing this to show what a 'great guy' I am, but, that my intention and anger was directed to those who would abuse the system doing so much for people. Also, I was jobless and homeless ,I lived out of a Van for a year in a dead end warehouse area, far from people cause it was EMBARRASSING and didn't want to go home to my parents. So, I'm not unsympathetic, but, this is quite  different.

On Tuesday, this  week, after working a 17 hour day, I feel asleep, only to awakening to a  drunken, disorderly crowd outside  a few hours later. I see these guys roaming the neighborhood and I give them cans  and bottles on occasion, they have shopping carts.  This morning they had congregrated outside my home by the tree, but, one of them I didn't recognise. Since, I had to go to work in about an hour, and having any contact with these guys  could become involved, I ignored it and went about my business. At NOON, fully 4 hours after I had left the house, I came back, only to find the two had become 4 and one was streched out on the sidewalk. I went over and told them to please pick up the bottles and trash and leave. Three  complied and started to get going, the one guy lying on the ground in his own feces and urine,suddenly  became a sidewalk 'Thomas Jefferson' and started yelling about his 'rights' to be on the sidewalk. Told me 'F. U' and  was lying in the thin strip  of  sidewalk between driveways, where  I have  to get my van out.  Now, no doubt  thanks to the  organizations like the ACLU, this  lunatic  probably would end up on page one of newspaper and I would be arrested,If anything happened, and G-D forbid  you didn't  see him some  morning and backed up. So, I called an ambulance and the cops also  came, tried to talk to this person to move, whereupon he  feigned 'No Comprende', all of a sudden he spoke no English. :roll: Eventually, the ambulance people  delicately lifted him up and he was taken to a Hospital, and I was questioned, cause he claimed to them that  I KICKED HIM?! I DID NOT touch this person, first, he was filthy, second, I don't hit people lying on the ground. Anyway, he was taken away, and I was left with the cans and bottles and potato chips wrappers to clean up. I have unfortunately, over the past several years  been ticketed  for debris left behind  by these  guys.

Now, my question is  this, what can be done  about these people, besides  calling ambulances and cops? I saw this  guy tonight, the abusive guy, with other  guys with shopping carts at  Ditmars and 31st, by the train. He was  yelling and even these  homeless guys looked  'threatened.'  It's  an ongoing problem and it's  getting worse in  Astoria not better, I think these  guys  are from outside the community. What can be done? Thanks.

I agree that you can't force someone into the system, but you can probably "nudge" some people in that direction.  At very least, it's probably a good thing for 311 to be aware of street homeless, as it helps the authorities estimate the scope of the problem.  And as I understand it, again from the folks I know who work at DHS, they are pretty good at coaxing people to at least get some medical care.

As far as folks remaining on the streets by choice, I am not sure that it is a morally valid choice for them to be making.  Granted, I have very strong libertarian leanings, and I am not unsympathetic to the plight of the homeless.  But most of the street homeless either have the substance abuse issues or mental health issues we have discussed above.  Being on the streets is a detriment to their own health and safety, as well as to the overall area.  None of us want the litter, rotten food, or human waste on the streets.  And to a certain extent their "right" to be on the streets ends when it infringes upon the rights of others.  I'll admit that the Giuliani "shelter or jail" solution comes across as draconian, and I am not entirely comfortable with that.  But I do think that the "system" has to aggressively confront the homelessness situation.

Also, we always debate on this blog about whether the area is in a state of urban decline, whether crime is up despite statistics, etc.  Aggressive pan handling, public intoxication, garbage on the streets, etc. are all things that are associated with larger urban decline. And if you believe the Broken Windows theory, they are precursors to worse crimes.
