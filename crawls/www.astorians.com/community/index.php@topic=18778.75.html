Quote from: JoeyC on July 09, 2010, 02:03:00 PM
shows that homeless people are not to always be overlooked as harmless.  Not saying kill the homeless or anything,   but many are like that due to own behaviour, drug use, or mentel illness.

You're right.  Before the term "homeless" came into fashion, they were called bums.  And bums were known as people who didn't work or try to help themselves.  Or drank themselves to oblivion or were "crazy".  Like other addictive behaviors and mental disorders, the reasons why bums were bums were rightfully determined to be much more complex.  And so they began to be classified as "homeless" for a whole host of reasons.  But as with many other "causes", it became the "in thing" to defend the homeless and speak out for these "victims".  And anyone who uttered a bad word about the homeless were frowned upon.  Well, now it seems time has come to double back a little and recognize that some are dangerous and either are - or can become - violent criminals.  Ideally we should continue to help those who really need it - be it mental illness or drug addiciton.  But they should not be permitted to loiter and/or live in public spaces.  We should not accept that these people are "ill" and so it's okay that they yell and scream and harrass pedestrians all day long.  We need to stop blindly looking at a potential dangerous threat with sympathy.
