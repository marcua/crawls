I'm George, the owner of Astor Bake Shop. I've had the space leased since 02/09, and have been physically working on it since 09/09. It does however feel like much longer. I'm glad you asked because I'm very excited about our choice of coffee roasters. After a long search I came upon a micro roaster in Red Hook, Brooklyn (though I'd love to use a Queens company, Dallas coffee is a big company that roast there beans by the tons, and I wanted something a bit more personalized). A relatively new boy on the block, like me, Stone Street Coffee is who we're going with. First off the owners, Stew and Mitch, worked for large well known italian coffee company for many years and die hard coffee connoisseurs . They live coffee. They started with a 12Kilo roaster and have grown up to a larger, but still relatively small operation. They only roast beans to order, so when I get my beans they are completely coated with fresh oils (FYI, a bean that looks dry is a sign of a bean that was roasted many days/weeks/months earlier).

My House Blend is a combination of fair trade organic Indonesian, African (Rwandan) and South American beans roasted to a dark mahogany. It's a really well rounded full bodied cup of coffee. Just writing about it makes me want a cup. I'm also going to offer a "Featured" coffee which will change bi monthly and will generally be a lighter roast. I know any thing I get from Stone Street will be excellent, because of their genuine love of coffee. I'm really glad to know there are other lovers in the neighborhood because I'm very proud of what we're offering.

Thanks again,

George
