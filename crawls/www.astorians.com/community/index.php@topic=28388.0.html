Kaufman Astoria Studios' donation to Mt Sinai Hospital
« on: September 10, 2014, 02:34:10 AM »
George S. Kaufman, chairman of nearby Kaufman Astoria Studios:
“When you are a member of a community, you help your neighbors,” Kaufman said. “We are thrilled to support Mount Sinai and look forward to helping provide better healthcare for the people of western Queens and throughout the borough.”

I agree 100% -- neighbors should help neighbors.

Kaufman Astoria Studios’ neighbors,  the citizens of the city of New York, funded their latest expansion with a $5 million grant from the New York City Economic Development Corporation and $2 million in grant and loan assistance from the Empire State Development Corporation. In fact, much of the film and television production that takes place here is heavily subsidized by the city and state, which I wholeheartedly support.

But if Kaufmann Astoria Studios relies on tax breaks and grants from taxpayers and then donates $700,000 to the Mount Sinai Hospital expansion for a new  Kaufman Astoria Studios Entrance, why do they need the tax-payer subsidies in the first place?

I'm just asking the question. Am I bothered about nothing here?


Nothing I've read so far has said this was a personal donation from Mr Kaufman.
