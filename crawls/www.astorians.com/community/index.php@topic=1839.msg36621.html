I honestly don't know what everyone has against Starbucks.  I used to work for them in the late 90's and it wasn't that bad of a job.  How many companies will give healthcare for part time employees.  The job actually helped to build up my self esteem and work skills.  A lot of what I know now for my job I learned from Starbucks.

I know, I know...everyone thinks of them like Mc Donalds because they are on every block.  But did you ever think why that is.  First off, their product isn't as bad as McDonald's is. (This is a whole other discussion I think).  Second, they give back quite a bit to the communities.  And does anyone know anything about Howard Schultz, they guy that made Starbucks a household name.  He came from a poor working class family from Canarsie Brooklyn.  He grew up in the projects.  It isn't like some rich guy did all this for money.  When he started running Starbucks he did it for the love of coffee.

I know Starbucks has lost site of what they stand for over the years, but over all, they aren't a bad company.  I know some people might not like their coffee and that is fine.  I know some won't like the company and that is fine also.  But before anyone "bashes" something, find out a little about it first.

....Just my 2 cents.   I can't wait to get the feedback from this.   :-)

And they do treat their employees very well - I've worked for several big retail companies (and my sister has as well, so she has expierences to compare Sbucks to), and from what she's told me, its a great place to work.

I don't go often because it could add up, but every time I've gone into any Starbucks, they've been nice, and whatever I got is tasty. How can you go wrong with that? And once when I went, they were out of one of the ingredients needed for my original drink order so I got something else, and a manager came over a few minutes later (I was sitting waiting to meet someone) and gave me a coupon for a free drink and apologized that they couldn't make my original order. I didn't even complain - I was like, "Oh...oh well, I guess I'll have [something else] instead." Now that's customer service!

Yeah, they're pricey, and yeah, they're a big company, but I don't think either of those alone is a reason to dislike them.

Also, if no one has ever tried it - the Passion tea Lemonade is an AWESOME warm day drink. Just get it unsweetened, the sweetener is too much, there's enough sugar in the lemonade :)
