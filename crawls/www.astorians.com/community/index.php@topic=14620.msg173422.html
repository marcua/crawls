This is the letter my partner sent to the Senator.




I believe all New York families should be treated equally under the law.

I am proud to live in a state like New York where everyone is treated with respect and dignity, and it is time that our laws reflect that respect and dignity.

You recently stated that your district does not support equal rights for gay people.  Sir, I urge you to take a look around Astoria today.  We are not the same population who voted you into office two decades ago.  Within the last five years Astoria has been literally flooded with young men and women.  We are pouring out of Manhattan in search of more affordable rents.

We love this village, and the swell of our population has demanded the creation of many new businesses, and revived many older establishments.  So many of us are young actors, singers, and dancers.  So many of us are gay; and so many of the young straights have gay friends and relatives whom they know and love.  Senator, your district has radically changed.  Please take a walk down 30th Ave or Broadway between Steinway and 31st Street on a Saturday or Sunday. The population of Astoria has changed.  I urge you to vote in favor of same sex marriage - Equality for All Astorians.  That is what we want.

Sincerely,
Cameron Folmar
