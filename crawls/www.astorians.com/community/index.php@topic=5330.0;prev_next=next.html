Whatever you do, don't go with Nationwide. I had been with Nationwide since I was 16, but when I had my first accident last spring, they were horrible to deal with (long story, won't bore you, but I will say that the accident involved me sitting, completely stopped, at a red light and getting rear-ended by a drunk 18 year old. They tried to get me to admit that it was somehow my fault). Then when I registered my car in New York, the Astoria agent basically refused to do business with me even though my NW agent from CT told me he would take care of everything (again, long story). I dropped them, and went with Geico. Better rates, and all the people I've dealt with have been helpful.

Of course, I haven't had an accident yet while with Geico, so this could all change. But I think they're a good company.
