 When I think of Chinese food, I don't think of Astoria. You really have to travel to Flushing, Elmhurst, 6th Ave in Brooklyn, or Chinatown in the city. All other places outside of that are Chinese take-out, I wouldn't even call them a restaurant. I think it's tough for someone to open an authentic Chinese or any Asian restaurant in Astoria because there isn't a large Asian population, and secondly the taste of the food would need to please the local platelets which would mean deviating from authentic. From my experience whenever anyone deviates from authentic, it taste terrible and fake.

I agree that really good Chinese food is lacking and that someone could make a killing if they opened a great Chinese place here. I also agree that it's easy to live with the lack of terrific Chinese when we have so much good food around here.

I was surprised to find, quite by accident, that Dragon Gate across from Singlecut is very, very good. Have had several of their dishes including General Tso's Chicken and Beef w/Broccoli on multiple occasions and been surprised at how good they were. Unfortunately I don't live near Singlecut so I don't go there too often, but if that place was local to me it's be my pick. Over by 30th Avenue, I like Sampan. There are a lot of dumpling options on their menu, which is a selling point, and some of them spicy-ish. Still, it's in the category of "Good, perfectly decent, but nothing to write home about" as are most of the Chinese takeout spots I've tried here over the years.
