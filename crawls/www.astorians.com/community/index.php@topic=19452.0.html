We went last night - I've been dying to try this place since seeing the sign last week ...

The decor is great - it feels very different from other restaurants in the neighborhood.  Between the look and the soundtrack I assume they're trying to appeal to a different crowd than the other places on 30th Avenue (much to our relief!). 

Since it's new we had very low expectations of the staff - and they did better than we expected.  They needed one more waitress last night - I think there were only 2 and 1 more would have sped things up a bit.  The busboys were plentiful - we never ran out of water.  It was nice to hear about the restaurant's background but I would have preferred our waitperson be more knowledgable about the beer list.

OK - the food:  we didn't order an app but all of them looked good.  The chips they brought to our table were disappointing, they didn't seem fresh.  My bf had the ribs which were DELISH - although it was a smaller than expected portion.  Their flavor was interesting without being weird, or too experimental.  They were really really good and cooked perfectly.  I ordered the fried chicken and let me say this:  HOLY FREAKIN' CHICK'N - SOOOOOOOO good.  I have never in my life had better - EVER.  The coating and skin were delicious and perfectly crispy and the meat was so juicy I couldn't believe it - really, I have never had fried chicken like this. 


We're definitely going back - I think this place is a great addition to Astoria and I'm looking forward to trying the other items on their menu.  We're particularly happy to see something open up that isn't another pseudo-cafe offering mediocre food and instant coffee to 180 bpm.

I went last night.  I think the name is pretty gimmicky, but that aside, the place is pretty good.  It's obvious that they're still ironing out their opening week kinks, but I was impressed with the food and the service.  The beer selection is also worth mentioning.

We had a dish that featured smoked brisket, braised short ribs, and pulled pork.  It came with corn tortillas, guacamole, crema, salsa and pickled red cabbage.  All the meats were excellent, and were portioned out very generously.  There was too much food for my dining companion and I to finish, and we ended up taking a lot home with us.

Total cost with drinks and appetizer was around $40 - not bad!
