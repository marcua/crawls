Title: Re: "Green" cleaners
Post by: caroslime on March 14, 2008, 05:54:33 PM
Good on ya!  I'm switching over to natural cleaners too.  I'm replacing all my cleaners as the run out.  Here's a brief review of what I've tried so far:

Holy Cow All Purpose Degreaser (it's bright pink with a dancing cow on the label) is pretty amazing...works great in both the kitchen and bathroom.  I've also used it on really stubborn set-in grease spots on my husband's shirts, and it even worked great on them!  I originally bought it at Berry Fresh Market (43rd and Ditmars), but they didn't have any this past Sunday when I went back for more.  They also make a window cleaner and a concentrated multipurpose cleaner, but I haven't tried them yet.  Has anyone see this product anywhere else?  They have a website, but I'd rather shop locally.

Bon Ami Cleanser is a good powder cleanser that I use for the tub..it's cheap, natural, does the job, and easy to find.   

Natrics makes a good toilet bowl cleaner, but I haven't tried their other products.  I also haven't seen them locally.

I'm still looking for a good dish washing detergent (for hand washing)...I've tried Seventh Generation, Trader Joe's, and Planet, but none of the natural ones cut grease as well as I'm used to or get sudsy enough for my liking.  Planet is the best so far, but still kind of...eh.  Anyone have any suggestions on that front?
