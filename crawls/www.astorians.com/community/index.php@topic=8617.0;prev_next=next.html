I'm in the same boat, no money and plenty of people to buy for.  I'm making some of my gifts.  I plan on making herb infused olive oil for my parents.  All you need is some little glass bottles, some fresh herbs and olive oil.  I also bake treats for everyone in my family.

What about Costco?  You could find some gifts there for decent prices (if you have/buy a membership, of course).  My boyfriend's mom always buys our gifts there (and at TJ Maxx).  She's given me two cashmere sweaters from there that are really nice and definitely not typical cashmere price.  She also gave us a spice rack carousel and pepper grinder. 

I also tend to shop at drugstores, at holiday time you can find some fun gifts.  One year, I made my sister a giant stocking full of shampoo, lotion, body wash, etc.  She was just about to have her first child and I figured it would be nice for her to have all those things on hand so she didn't have to worry about shopping for it.  All that stuff can be bought in bulk at Costco as well.

Can you tell I've been poor for a while?

there's a lot of cheap stuff on amazon.com, but watch the shipping fees.

If you give your brother a much-loved book with a personal note explaining why it means so much to you and why you think he'll like it ... that sort of thing.
