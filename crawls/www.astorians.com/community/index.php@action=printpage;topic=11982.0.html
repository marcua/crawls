People can fall into a trap of never being satisfied with where they live, when in fact it's a state of mind. Title: Re: Adjusting to NYC
Post by: JustDesserts on August 28, 2008, 12:36:10 PM
My experience here has been a little weird. I loved it here for my first year, but after that I just wanted out, and still do for various reasons. I'm certainly glad I gave NY a shot, though, because I think I'd regret later in life never taking the chance. Turns out I can't survive here, both financially and mentally, which makes me a bit sad because I thought after that first year I'd be in for the long haul. There are hundreds of things I love about living here and will dearly miss, but for me the stuff that sucks far outweighs the wonderfulness. The feeling of not belonging here is difficult to live through. I often feel like a misfit anyway, but to feel so out of place day-to-day is taking its toll. I believe everyone should give New York a shot at some point in their lives, though. All that I've learned through living here about myself, my personal happiness, money, relationships, how much I love home and family, and a million other things are invaluable and will follow me wherever I go. New York is a challenge, and if you can last more than six months here, you can survive anything!

I think London will be my next big-city experience, after I've sufficiently enjoyed my beloved California again and start feeling restless. I always feel at home among the British. God save the Queen!
