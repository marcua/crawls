Shelves!!

Seriously.  I have a good friend who's good at shelf installation, thank goodness.  We ran a shelf along the length of my entry hallway for all my books, another one in the alcove by my kitchen for my old notebooks/photo albums, and a cute one from Home Depot with a little rail around it that hangs above my TV and holds my DVDs.

Also, I have an older studio that came with two closets, one average-sized and one nearly walk-in.  I also held on to my wardrobe, so I have three closets.  I store unusued clothes and bags in the wardrobe (and suitcases on top of it), household stuff (linens, filing cabinet, vacuum, camp chairs, etc) in the small closet, and clothes/jewelry/laundry in the giant closet.  I use the upper shelves on the giant closet for my Christmas stuff and unused collections.

And I got risers for my full-sized bed, so I keep a few more things (granny cart, old video tapes in big tupperwares) underneath there.

Phew.

Vertical space is your friend in these tiny spaces...  :lol:

If I do get a bike, I'm worried about storage spaces.  They have those wall mounts for bikes, but it would be awkward in my place to say the least.  I might get a folding bike, I think I could make room for it in one of my closets!

Hah, I got all that beat.   I'm using my one bedroom as a studio with a big, really big walk-in closet.   Actually when I moved in, I had intended on finishing off the bedroom, but a busy schedule and a persistant leak has kind of sidelined that project.   My building is from 1920 if I'm not mistaken and has those not-very-deep, cabinet type closets in my bedroom.   When I moved in there were no doors on the bigger sections and no drawers below.  Oddly enough they're still in that state.   When I do finally turn it into a bedroom, I hope to have the time and ambition to build a storage bed which should hold a lot of stuff.   Someone who had the apartment before me put a large shelf over the bathroom door (in the bathroom) which holds quite a bit.   My coffee table has storage in it.  I built-in bookshelves under my big tripple window in my living room and have plans for a storage cabinet/side table.    If you get someone handy that you trust to check how well your kitchen cabinets are attached to the way, there's lots of room on top of the cabinets to keep things.    Hard to give an idea specifically geared twards your apt without seeing it.   Best thing to remember is confidence.  Never think you can't do something and don't be afraid to try.   
Good luck with your stuff.
