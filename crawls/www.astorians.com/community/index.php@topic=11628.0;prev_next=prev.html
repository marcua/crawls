Stove Restaurant, 28th Ave and 46th St.
« on: May 19, 2007, 02:27:38 PM »
Stove opened some time around the end of 2006 and it has fabulous food!  It's a good mix of European and American fare, fish and chips (so good!), shepherd's pie, hangar steak and shoestring potatoes, chicken marsala (another good choice) and some good seafood dishes to name just a few.  The specials always sound so good that I end up ordering one instead of off the regular menu.  I had an oregano-crusted trout last night, and we started with some buffalo wings that were delish.  It's a little off the beaten path but well worth the walk/cab ride. 

Guinness and Smithwicks on tap, moderate prices (entrees are about $10.95 to $18.95, totally worth it!), good service and not usually crowded.  So far, I've never been disappointed!

My boyfriend and I ate at Stove for the first time last night and it was amazing. Although the restaurant and bar were packed, we were seated within 5-10 minutes. I love Stove!!! I have been there twice now. The first time was awesome, but last night took the cake.
We sat outside, lovely atmosphere, and started off with the stuffed clams appetizer, which was yummy! It had a paprika crust going on - so delicious.
I have an embarrassing phobia of bees. I'm fine around any other insect or pest, but if I see a bee, I flip out like a crazy person. I run away with no regard to who or what might be in my path, I claw at people, and I twitch like a crazy person. I dunno why, but it's just how I react to the mere sight of a bee going about it's bee business. So when my friend noticed a wasp lingering around our table, he did his best to lie to me about it's presence. But then it came on my side of the table. I freaked (only a little thankfully) and the girls at the next table consoled me by saying they'd have reacted the same why. So while I'm crouched on the bench contemplating an escape route that involves jumping over the fence, my friend looks around to figure out why this particular wasp was loitering around us. Well, this is why:



 
Paper wasps has been building a nest under the very table we were sitting at!! How crazy is that! It was awesome. When he saw it, everyone came over to look at snap pictures! I'd never seen one before. I calmed down a reasonable amount when I realized that this particular wasp was just taking a break from constructing his new home, and I marveled at the wonder of nature for while. To top it off, the waitress's eyes got big as golf balls when she saw it! It was awesome! I explained that I'm crazy when it comes to bees (wasps, bees, they look exactly the same!) and she was very nice about it, and offered to move us inside, but we just switched to another outside table, right before our food came out. It was an overall entertaining experience, and I'm glad it happened. They decided to deal with removing the nest after everyone left, it was around 8pm last night, and they close at 11, so they probably just didn't let anyone sit there for the rest of the night. Smart choice, considering paper wasps only attack when they feel their nest is being threatened  :lol: as I have learned from my morning bee research. http://en. wikipedia. org/wiki/Paper_wasp
I had the trout, which was not only delicious but fun to eat!! It only had a few bones, and the way it was served, (tailfin and all) was just entertaining in itself to eat! The chopped it's head off, sliced it in half to remove the nasty bits, and put the seasoning (French, can't remember exactly, meneuivre? and lemon glace) in there, like in between the two halves of fish. Delicious beyond explanation. We really wanted to stay for dessert, baily's irish cream cheesecake!!!!!!! but unfortunately we had no time or space in our bellies.
Stove Rocks!!!!
