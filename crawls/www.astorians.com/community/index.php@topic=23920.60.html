We tried it out on a Friday evening around 7/8.  It was not that loud or playing hip hop music at that point but I have no doubt it would later on.  I saw the outfits on the waitresses.  Definitely going for a sexy look but I actually didn't think they were trashy.  I was impressed with the bourbon and whiskey selection.  My buddy thought the beer menu was pretty interesting and balanced.  I really like what they did with the space.  The bridge to the rear dining area is cool and I like that they retained the Off Track Betting lettering.  They use lighting and textures very well.  Whoever designed the space shows skill and talent.  The restrooms are spacious and clean.  I would go back for sure, but I would not go late night, nor would I go in the height of a big game.  More like a happy hour and try a burger or sandwich plate and just relax.  I completely agree about the differences between Katch and Sweet Spot.  I like Katch and I think their food is quite delicious.  But I think Sweet Spot can be cool if you're in the mood for something a little darker and more loungy.  Both seem very popular and that's good news for Astoria in general.
