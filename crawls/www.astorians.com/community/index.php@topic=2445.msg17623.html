Wow - ferris, out of the four topics you are referring to three are topics I started. So naturally, I have to defend myself.

Okay number one - the children's lit post was posted only AFTER I had already tried to figure out on my own what would be good to buy. I knew that Astorians would be a good place to ask about this because we have several teachers and book lovers that participate on this board. I have gained an amazing amount of advice on this topic and am very grateful. I don't regret starting that post at all.

Number two - AOL. I don't see how this ISN'T related to Astoria. Many people in Astoria subscribe to AOL or may be considering it, and if you read the thread more closely you will see that people are helping each other out by suggesting ways to unsubscribe. While I did post it originally as an interesting anecdote, it did turn into something beneficial.

Number three - Cat videos and links. Yes, this is a complete thread about the love of cats. Amazingly enough (or not amazing at all), many of our Astorians are animal lovers. If you will notice I directed one of my videos at megc and Jayme who happen to both be active Astorians participants and cat lovers. A few other people jumped in and started talking about their cats - who live in Astoria. (Besides, my cat can only meow at this point, unlike some of the cats on the videos I shared who can say "Mama" and "I love you," so I may have been inadvertantly trying to give him a voice in the Astorians community by posting that thread. :))

While I understand that you and others are not entirely enthusiastic about some of the conversations that take place on Astorians, I think that you may want to consider that some Astorians' lives do not revolve exclusively around Astoria. The name of the board is Astorians, thus implying it is a forum for the people who live here - not for Astoria, a place, that happens to have people who live here.

I spend an incredible amount of time away from Astoria every day at my job, so to some of you I suppose I owe an apology for some posts that stray into Manhattan or into other parts of the Internet universe. I suppose that some posts that I have started in the Politics section (i.e. Why Did the Chicken Cross the Ocean Twice?, Social Security numbers, etc.) would be considered inappropriate as well because the chicken post is referring to China and Social Security problems originate in Washington DC. I also have to apologize for my promotion of an online survey that had absolutely nothing to do with Astorians, but rather was associated with my occupation.

I think, though, that while some posts may seem like they have strayed away from Astoria they really do still have merit here. We're all Astorians and a lot of us have become friends online, so instead of blasting out an e-mail with a link to cat videos or asking for advice to only a select few about children's lit, I felt like my Astorians friends could be amused for a few minutes (after all, many have mentioned that they have boring jobs in Manhattan or elsewhere), or could offer me advice that I knew I could trust.

Anyway, if you sincerely have a problem with how people post then you should make a request to the admin group for more stringent monitoring of appropriate/inappropriate posts. I have a feeling, though, that they will likely not agree with you as merm has pointed out on multiple occasions that he prefers an online environment that acts as a natural conversation and exchange, and other admin have been guilty of participating in some of these types of so-called inappropriate or non-Astoria threads.
