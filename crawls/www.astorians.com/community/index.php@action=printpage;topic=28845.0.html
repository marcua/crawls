Post by: johnny4evah! on January 18, 2015, 05:28:12 PM
Why not make some simple Japanese soup yourself? Buy some miso paste ( I lke Miko miso with dashi), wakame dried seaweed and tofu. Add 2 Tbsp miso paste to 2 or 3 cups water, simmer few minutes, add chopped tofu, and rehydrated wakame (place a little wakame in bowl, add water, let  sit until thoroughly moist, drain water, use) simmer a few more minutes. Makes a great warming winter soup. Optional: garnish with chopped green scallions. Not too filling, but very warming and comforting.

Post by: LHT on February 03, 2015, 09:46:55 AM
It's a trekking adventure for soups in Astoria, especially if you want something beyond avgolemone - here are some suggestions
- NY Bagel on 23rd Ave - call ahead to check daily selection but New England Clam Chowder and Hungarian Mushroom are excellent
- Bartolinos on Broadway - best stratachella in town IMO
- Kholiba on 23rd Ave - garlic soup has both cheese and an egg in it - pure wow - and their onion soup is also good (up the block from NY Bagel)
- Titan grocery - in the fridge section check out the lentil and yogurt/barley soups to go
- Create on 30th avenue - nearly always great, not starchy
