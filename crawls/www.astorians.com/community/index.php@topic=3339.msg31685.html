My new job is on the corner of Liberty and Broadway and last week I noticed something while I was standing outside (supporting my bad habit). I usually stand just inside Liberty Park and face Broadway while I smoke and I noticed that almost every person who passes that corner looks toward Ground Zero. I didn't pay attention at first, then I noticed obvious non-NY'ers doing it but then I realized regular, eyes-to-the-ground NY'ers were doing it too. Maybe it's just because today was so close, but I'm going to keep watching. Just an observation.

It's been hard for me to walk past there every day going to work and not look towards where the towers should be and think about what it must have been like that day. It makes me very sad. We could hear the bells all day in our offices and everyone was very somber.

Today is also my best friend's birthday. Last year I wasn't here on 9/11 because I went back to Texas to surprise her for her 30th. So even though this is my second year in NYC it's only my first time to be here on September 11.

I loved reading everyone's posts, even though most of them made me cry.

I'm glad I know y'all. In this short time you have left a mark on my heart and I can't imagine NOT knowing y'all. I won't get too sappy here, but next time you see me, remind me to tell you how great I think you are!  :-)

I wasn't going to read this thread today, but I'm really glad I did. I would describe myself as a pretty emotional person - wear my heart on my sleeve and such - but for such an emotional person, I'm really good at finding ways to avoid and repress it. So that's one reason why I was avoiding it. The other is that, this is just one of those things about being a New Yorker that I can't really share with the people who were here on that day. I've spoken with a few people about it, about what it was like, but always feel awkward doing so. I can't imagine living through something like that, can't even begin to put myself in your shoes, or in the shoes of those that lost people they knew and loved.

Therefore, in all honesty, I've avoided a lot of things that might trigger the sadness. I won't watch the movies or the specials on TV. Won't dwell. But reading this was good. It reminds me that I can still be affected by it. It's like a pin prick. Yes, it's still there, inside me. I hope I always feel that sting.
