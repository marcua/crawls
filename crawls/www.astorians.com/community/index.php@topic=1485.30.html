I used the DE in powder form. After a couple of months of having the exterminator come again and again and again, we finally did one big push and that seemed to make the big difference.

This is what we did:

-caulk the entire apartment
-wash everything hot water and dry with highest heat.
-anything that can't be washed was steamed or put into plastic bags to be stored for 18 months.
-vinyl covers on the mattresses and futon
-vacuum everything
-threw out everything suspicious that couldn't be adequately cleaned or stored long term. mark up anything thrown out "BEDBUGS DO NOT TAKE" to avoid spreading to other apts.
-used a spray for mattresses and furniture
- put DE around the edges of the room.
- put glue traps under each bed leg.

I've only had one bite since.

http://www.astorians.com/index.php?topic=2323.0

Check out the "Bugs in my apartment" thread - other astorians have had the same problem.

I would suggest:

1) talking to the landlord and any neighbors before signing the lease to make sure there are no bedbugs in the new apartment.
2) wash and/or treat everything you can before your move and pack in clean plastic bags or plastic boxes (lots of advice on this in the other thread)
3) anything that can't be satisfactorily washed or treated prior to moving, put into storage for at least 12 months (sealed up good to avoid infecting other storage units)
4) have an exterminator treat the new apartment before you move in, along with your boxes if possible.
