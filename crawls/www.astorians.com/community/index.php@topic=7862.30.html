Saying one place has a better pizza than another is a difficult thing to do.
It actually doesn't make sense, since everyone has a different palate and while one person may say that Grimaldi's  http://www.grimaldis.com/brooklyn.htm has the best pizza ( though I wouldn't stand in line for 3 hours waiting for it) others say that their pizza is too liquidy in the center and isn't always cooked enough. Others might say that Totonnos http://www.totonnos.com/ in Coney Island is the best since they've been around the longest, longer in fact than any other family owned & operated pizzeria in the U.S.!( since 1924)  Then you have Difara's ( no website 718-258-1367    1424 Avenue J, Brooklyn, NY 1123, in the Midwood section of Brooklyn, surrounded by a bunch of kosher food establishments. If you go there be prepared to wait at least an hour and a half for a pizza, but the man is a pizza artisan.... yes only one person makes the pizza there .But , alas, it's all subjective. The fact of the matter is that here in NYC's 5 boros there are MANY fine establishments serving fantastic pizza!

Hey this place is no where near Ditmars, but their pizza is great.  It's called Sac's Place and it's on Broadway a couple of blocks below 31st Street.  The pizza is cooked in a wood burning oven and they have an open (and working) cast iron wood burning stove in the dining area.  The smell of the burning wood is great, the pizza is thin with delicious sweet tomatoes and home made mozzarella.  The grilled asparagus with proscuitto is great too. 

The fireplace made the place - made you feel like you were in a country inn in the middle of Astoria!
