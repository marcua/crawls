Review: 718
« on: June 28, 2007, 10:01:48 AM »
I took a ladyfriend there the other night. It's called 718, on the corner of 35th and Ditmars: http://www.718restaurant.com

The food was excellent, they have a great wine selection, and the service was on point. It's described as a French Bistro with a Spanish twist. They have a lot of seafood and they serve good sized portions for a place that is supposedly French. It is a bit pricey for Astoria, but about on par with what you'd pay for most 'regular' places in the city. I would definitely consider this a gourmet restaurant- if not for the food, then for the atmosphere and wine pairings.

We had a problem where they messed up our order, but they immediately took it back, gave us some complimentary shrimp ceviche to tide us over, and we were back on track 5 minutes later. I definitely had a great experience there. Would recommend it to anyone.

The place is very nice and is a welcome addition to Ditmars.  But I'm not a huge fan of the food.  They make delicious brick oven pizzas of all different varieties. And other great appetizer dishes.  And the bar is nice.  We ordered meals once and the entrees were awful.  Would never do it again.  But if you're looking for more of a yuppieish happy hour scene and just want to have cocktails and a few light dishes, it's great.  In fact, if that's what you want, it's one of the few places in Astoria that offer that kind of atmosphere.  Believe me, I continue to search.
