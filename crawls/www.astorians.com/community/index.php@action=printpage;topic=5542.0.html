Title: Graffiti Reporting
Post by: eblack on March 14, 2007, 02:32:13 PM
I noticed pretty quickly that someone had put graffiti on one of the walls of the new public construction near my house.  I called 311 to report it (probably a couple of weeks later), figuring that they would get someone to clean it off.  They gave me a service request number, so I could check on the progress.  I called last night to check on it.

The operator said that there was an arrest made in relation to the graffiti!  How that happened two weeks later, I have no idea.  Anyway, I asked when it would be cleaned up and he said I hadn't requested a clean up.  So I said I'd like to do that and he said there is no way to do it.  It went back and forth for a little bit and was very frustrating.  Basically, it seems like no one will clean up graffiti on public land and they leave it for "local civic groups" to do and they can request paint and supplies from the Mayor's office.

Does this sound odd to anyone else?

Title: Re: Graffiti Reporting
Post by: Christine on March 14, 2007, 04:23:02 PM
http://www.nyc.gov/html/sbs/nycbiz/html/managing/graffiti.shtml

You may be in luck:

GRAFFITI REWARD PROGRAM
Members of the public are eligible for up to a five hundred dollars ($500.00) reward for reporting graffiti vandalism in progress through 911 or providing information regarding graffiti vandalism to the City-Wide Vandals Task Force through 311. The information must result in the arrest and conviction of individual(s) for violations of Administrative Code Provisions or Penal Law Statues regarding graffiti vandalism. This program will be administered by the City-Wide Vandals Task Force.  :lol:
