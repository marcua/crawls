With the Costco liquor store, they can afford to buy a huge quantity at once which means they can get it for cheap. With liquor pricing and pricing on the big brand products, the more you buy, the cheaper the price so the mom and pop stores aren't trying to rip you off, they just can't afford to buy a pallet of booze to get it at the same price as a huge mega-store so in order for them to make a profit and still sell the products people are asking for. They can either take a loss or charge more. Liquor and big brand wines are all sold by 2 distributors that have a monopoly on the brands and they are the only ones that can sell those brands and they charge what they want because they know people have to buy from them. That hurts the little guys and favors the big guys. The prices posted online are often in different states and the liquor distributors are only allowed to operate in one state so from state to state different distributors charge vendors different prices. That's why prices vary from store to store and product to product. A vodka that sells very well in one store, the store owner might see that it makes sense to buy a huge quantity of it and can offer it at a lower price but something they don't sell too much of they might get a smaller quantity and have to charge more to make their money back. Hope that explains why the prices vary.
