Seva is definitely your best bet.  Unfortunately, there are no Indian restaurants at all around Ditmars, although you can buy spices, herbs and some prepared foods at the little Bangladeshi grocery on Ditmars across from Dunkin Donuts (between 32nd and 33rd Sts.).  Seva does deliver to the Ditmars area, although you should confirm before ordering that they deliver to your block, as it's on the edge of their delivery zone.

Definitely worth the trip to Jackson Heights.  If you have a car, it's a very quick drive (mapquest--37th Ave & 74th Street) and as this area is known as "Little India", you will find many, many options.  Or, as other poster said, take the R or V train uptown to the Roosevelt Ave stop (E F and 7 trains also go to that stop, if you're coming from somewhere else).  My new favorite is Mehfil (37th Ave and 75th Street, across from Met supermarket).  They (along with many of the area places) have a 7 day a week lunch buffet, very inexpensive (most under $10), if you'd like a little bit of everything.

I may get in trouble for suggesting this, but I would say there are no good Indian restaurants in Astoria.   As noted previously in this thread there are quite a few Bengali places, but I don't know that there are any proper "Desi," joints, and even in the Bengali places, I rarely see any that are packed with people of any sort of South Asian patrons which would indicate to me that those, "in the know." would rather eat elsewhere.   Personally I think you're better off taking the train to Murray Hill or the around 58th St between 3rd and 2nd Ave than even bothering with the stuff around here.   Astoria is a great place to live, and it may be only a short car ride away from some of the largest Asian communities in America, but whatever reason none of those culinary traditions seem to travel to this part of Queens.
