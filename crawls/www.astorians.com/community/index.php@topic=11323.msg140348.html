« Reply #41 on: July 16, 2008, 10:00:53 PM »
The reasons for the nods being put in place were thoroughly discussed when they were implemented - and the idea really hasn't changed. (threads have already been referenced)

Speaking personally I think nods are helpful because they are a way for people to see how readers are responding to each point that is being made in the thread all the way down. If each person that was participating in a thread were to quote and respond to every person that they "agree" with and every person they "disagree" with the thread would be insanely long.

For the most part the nods are working in that they demonstrate the tone of the conversation and how it's being received by readers. Like it or not, this site has many more readers than posters - and the nods give them a voice as well - as is demonstrated in many polls where there are far fewer posts than votes. I also find the nods I think are also positive in that they help shape and provide feedback about the conversation (take this one for example).

I'm not saying there isn't a down side to them - there is, and I recognize that.  But most of the time, the negative nods are given for a reason (immaturity, personal attacks, grand standing, poor taste, bad argument), some times they are given for no reason at all or out of immaturity (someone doesn't "like" another person etc). However, I'm still of the opinion that their utility outweighs the negatives.

For whatever reason when I get neg-nodded I don't take it too personally.
