One thing that strikes me is that given you're a RE broker this discussion most likely is more than just fun – it’s business – which is perfectly fine. It's an open forum, but I think you’ll find that if you’re more upfront about your intentions, many people here would gladly help you out with the information you need.

As I said previously, I’d be happy with anything other than an empty storefront. But the fact remains that if it's true that the rent will be $25,000 per month, it is top dollar for oddly shaped 2,700 sq. ft store and automatically means that only a restaurant, bar, or high-traffic food business can open in that space because that’s really one of the few businesses that can generate that kind of turnover and per sq. footage sales needed to pay that sort of rent.

So what’s the point of asking the question when you already know the answer? In fact you answered the question in your question when you said what type of restaurant would people like to see.

It's all fruitless – pun intended. It’s like being asked: “What do you want for dinner? You can have anything your heart desires . . . as long as it’s chicken.”
