Hi Big Dog Lover

Can I work with more than one broker at one time?  Yes
Can I look at no-fee apts on the side?  Yes
Can a broker show me apartments in a new building that is being exclusively leased by another broker?  If so how does my broker get paid?  Yes, they might have some sort of a deal going on.  Don't worry about it if they do
Can a broker from company A show me apartments that are listed by company B?  Yes, many apartments are listed through multiple borkerages
How much time and effort should I expect for the $2000-$3000 fee that they will earn off me?  That's tricky.  Some really are honest and do great jobs and work their a$$ off for you, and others barely do anything.  There are threads here which folks talk about the good and bad brokers. 

As a tip with the broker fee, drop a hint or two when looking at the apartments that you'd be willing to give the broker fee in cash if you get a discount.  So far, this has worked for me twice and I've gotten half off of the fees.

Good luck in your search for you and your pups!

Work with as many as possible, each has their own apartments that they show, but if one takes you to an apt you have already seen, tell them immediately.  Keep looking for no-fee apartments.  You should never sign be asked to sign a document saying you will only work with one broker.  Remeber, the brokers work for the landlord (even though you pay the fee).

Do not pay in cash for the security deposit without a receipt.  And do not hand over the deposit until you have a signed contract stating the correct rent.

Some will ask to run credit checks before they can show you anything, I always avoided these places to avoid the fee / added hits to my credit report.

Expect to put in a lot of effort to find the right place, and don't expect to be given a lot of time to make a decision.  Most apartments come open for showing the last two weeks prior to the month they will be rented.

I've never lived in a rent-stab apartment.  Two large non-stabilized buildings (one was a co-op that allowed leasing and one was too new), and two brownstones (one owner-occupied), before my current apartment. 

The interest isn't that much, especially at current interest rates -- although if I knew I was entitled to it I'd make sure I got it just on principle.  The landlord is supposed to deposit the security deposit in a NY account offering prevailing rates.  If you had a $2000 security deposit, a 3% interest rate would give you $60/year.  The landlord is also allowed to keep 1% of the deposit as an administrative fee, which in this case is $20, so you'd pocket $40 for one year.  Of course, if the landlord owns many apartments and keeps all the security deposits in one account so the balance stays high, they might get a higher interest rate.

I believe that the law regarding interest on security deposits is based on the number of units in a building, not private house/building.

And yep:

http://www.housingnyc.com/html/resources/faq/security.html

Should I be getting interest on my security deposit?

If the building has six or more units the landlord must pay interest, if any, on your security deposit. The owner may keep one percent of the deposit amount each year as an administrative fee. If the building has fewer than six units and the owner deposits the security in a bank, the interest also belongs to the tenant, less one percent for administration.

For more information see the security deposit section of the NY State Attorney General's Landlord/Tenant Guide on our site.
