Title: Take Root w/ Esther m Palmer & aemp:dance | amiti perry+company
Post by: Dance Entropy on May 17, 2011, 02:46:43 PM
Esther m Palmer & aemp:dance | amiti perry+company
June 11th at 8:30 pm
Palmer   and Perry intentionally challenge their joint audience to undergo two   divergent dance experiences, allowing their distinct relationships to   body and space to merge into an evening of unforgettable dance.
 
(http://www.greenspacestudio.org/images/EMP-OS6MiguelAnaya-73.jpg)
Photo by: Miguel Anaya
Esther m Palmer straddles   the boundaries between performance and workshop in her new project only   shallow people, an improvised dance inspired by the relationship   between the unique and the universal body, and between the experiential   and the visual body. Palmer and dancers Erin Cairns, Sarah Lehman, Emily   Leonard, Joanna Nobbe, and Alexandra Taylor welcome the audience to   share in a half hour performance that attempts to look at these bodies   full of curiosity and without judgment. Splitting their time between the   dance floor and the audience circle, the cast shares honest movement as dancers and talks candidly as viewers, taking the audience on a quick educational journey of the non-judgmental vision and improvisation that they developed in the creation of this interactive work. Audience are encouraged to speak alongside the dancers and participate actively in the conversations that result from this heart-opening experience. only shallow people  is supported in part by the Queens Council on the Arts   with public funding from the New York City Department of Cultural Affairs.
 
(http://www.greenspacestudio.org/images/Perry_EricBandiero.JPG)
Photo by: Eric Vandiero
“Amiti   Perry's unique and challenging choreography consistently inspires and   throws out of the window what you may have thought you knew about   dance…”
Amiti Perry’s STUCK: in the IN-betweens  |head,   heart, gut, body, place| peers into a realm of the intellectual and   physical process of being constrained, restrained and bound to something   or someone. This company of powerful females, including Emily Bass, Amy Campbell, Perry, and Sophie Torok, embraces   the elements of these situational constrictions and finds their way to   complete release through challenging choreography that explores the   spectrum of intimate gesture and full force movements. A voyeuristic   audience will be consumed as the provocative vignettes carry them   through the journey of finding function within dysfunction in a highly   charged environment.


Tickets:  $15
