Nicest thing about poetry is that you walk around and live your life and there are sometimes you just need a new way to scream what has been dominating your mind.  If you have one rolling around in your brain, post it.

Stuck in an office on a day like today instead of being at the beach, always brings this to mind

Denial by Giorgos Seferis

On the secret seashore
white like a pigeon
we thirsted at noon;
but the water was brackish.

On the golden sand
we wrote her name;
but the sea-breeze blew
and the writing vanished.

With what spirit, what heart,
what desire and passion
we lived our life: a mistake!
So we changed our life.

An old favorite of mine:

Lending Out Books by Hal Sirowitz

You're always giving, my therapist said.
You have to learn how to take. Whenever
you meet a woman, the first thing you do
is lend her your books. You think she'll
have to see you again in order to return them.
But what happens is, she doesn't have the time
to read them, & she's afraid if she sees you again
you'll expect her to talk about them, & will
want to lend her even more. So she
cancels the date. You end up losing
a lot of books. You should borrow hers.

ADIEU

"Sayonara"
is always harder than "How are you, nice to meet you"
...unless I'd like to see you
nevermore, and my life can breach you,
bleach you,
delete you,
and make you disappear
in a wisp of air
to a place of which noone is aware.
Which or where it is? I care not,
as long as it's a place that we share not,
that is near not,
where nothing touch my interests,
and it gets nothing but indifference.
And to this rare spot,
you can make your humble entrance
and there rot
until beneath your burial plot, you hear God;
and at the moment that you walk with Him,
it's only then that we will talk again.
