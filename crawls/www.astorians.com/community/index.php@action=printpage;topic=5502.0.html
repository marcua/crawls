NYC March to End the War
Sunday, March 18
Assemble at 1:00 p.m.
35th St through 39th St. East of 6th Ave.
March at 2:00 pm.

Assemble on 35th through 39th Streets, east of 6th Ave at 1pm

March will kick off at 2pm

Peace and Justice Fair at Dag Hammerskjold Plaza 3:30-6:30pm


say NO MORE!

March 19th will mark the 4th anniversary of war and occupation of Iraq which has now become one of this nation's longest military engagements. 4 years of war and occupation; 4 years of death and destruction; 4 years of a war that never should have happened.

We will march at the time of the biggest showdown between the Congress and the Bush administration. We are calling upon our Congressional representatives and Senators to take action for:

•    an immediate withdrawal of all
      U.S. forces from Iraq;

•    an end to the funding of this war except for the
      safe withdrawal of all our armed forces;

•    no military attacks on Iran;

•    full funding of veterans benefits;

•    aid to reconstruct Iraq under Iraqi control;

•    the redirection of our tax dollars
      for social programs at home.

This march is part of United for Peace and Justice’s national call for a massive outpouring of opposition to the war in locally based, decentralized actions throughout the U.S.

March 18, 2007

The group United for Peace and Justice organized the afternoon rally in Midtown, which is passing by the offices of Senator Hillary Clinton and Charles Schumer.

A group called the "Peace Actions Coalition" is planning a protest near Wall Street Monday morning.
