The apprehension over that scolding is one reason I kept delaying going to the dentist, which dragged out into a 10 year gap of not visiting.  My friend finally convinced me to go to her dentist (this is when I lived in Park Slope).  I could only screw up the courage to say that it had been a "long time" since I had been to the dentist, and he said, "so what you mean is that you're really scared."  Then he smiled, but it was a kind smile.  He took one look at my teeth and was able to tell that I had had a broken bone "around the age of 11 or 12" (it was actually 11 years and 9 months) because of the discoloration of some of the enamel on my teeth.  He's great - I still make the trek to Grand Army Plaza twice a year.  If anyone ever needs a recommendation for a dentist in Brooklyn, I'd recommend him in an instant.  (Oh, and the good news was that I had no cavities or other problems despite my 10 years of not going to the dentist.)
