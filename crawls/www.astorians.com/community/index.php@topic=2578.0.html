Cutting Nails on Subway

Hello Everyone,

I am rather new to this board and to Astoria.  I moved here from Manhattan in the middle of February and I have been loving it ever since.    I don't think I could ever move back to the city...but that is another topic.

Anyway, I just felt the need to vent my frustrations about subway etiquette, specifically cutting one's finger/toenails (yes I have seen toe's clipped on the train  :? :x). 

I feel that I am one of the few people that is truly repulsed by this practice.   Why do people feel that it is okay to do this in a public place...never mind a very crowded public space like the subway. 

About 2 weeks ago this guy was standing in the doorway of the N/W train.  He began to clip his fingernails for several stops.  I could tell that they were rather large nails by the loud sound of the "clip".   I traded pissed off looks with another man on the train who was also bothered by this clipping.  Finally, I couldn't take it anymore and I asked him to do that somewhere else and offered that it was disgusting to do this in public.  Granted I did not say it that nicely but also said that I did not want to be hit with one of his grotesque, airborne fingernails.   This S*^&bag of a person replied that he does not care about me or anyone else on the train.  We bickered back and forth and he DID stop cutting his nails.  The other man did not feel the need to chime in I guess...which didn't help any. 

I just had to share this story...maybe there was someone on this board that was on the same train.  I just am amazed at how insensitive people are towards others.  I am a native NYer so I am not shocked by this, but I am surprised at times.
