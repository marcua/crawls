Just checked out this place tonight.  A million thumbs up!  We sat in the beautiful garden.  I'm endlessly impressed by what creative restaurant owners can do with a backyard.  It was very zen and cozy.  The lighting was a little dark but I loved that it was just candles.  I wish I were still there right now. 

I had a salad that I highly recommend: Yum Ma-Muang.  It had mango and avocado and red onion in this amazing limey dressing.  Salads never surprise me.  This salad was good enough that I'm writing about it on the internet. 
 
Service was good but slowish.  It seemed really busy so maybe the service is catching up with demand.  This place deserves to be busy and get a lot of business - it's amazing. 

I will definitely go there again.  I want to move my bed in to the back area with the bamboo plants and the trees and the candles.   Ah, so soothing even to think about it.

Go there!

Went tonight for the first time.  The food was great!  We had the steamed shrimp dumplings that were very good and the sauce with it was delicious.

I got one of the specials, that wasn't particularly well described, but was seafood in a hotpot.  I would say it was a little too salty, but then I remembered the description was "baked seafood with Asian black olives and jasmine rice".  I should have expected salt with the olives, but I forgot about that because the olives weren't visible in the dish. They were probably mixed into the rice, though.  My wife got the Pad Thai, which is her judge of good Thai food.  She found it hard to stop eating, it was so good, but there was plenty left to still take some home.  I ate all the seafood in mine and only left some rice. (there were mushrooms and onions in it, too)

The atmosphere was great, too.  The outside garden was full, but we sat in the back area right next to it, so it was almost like being outside.  The service was fine, but the numerous bus boys or waitstaff (there seemed to just be general waitstaff) were actually a little too omnipresent.  One asked if we were done with the crispy rice thing they serve to you at the beginning and I said we'd keep it and he took it away.  Odd.

Overall, a great dinner!

Dined at Leng Thai tonight. Perhaps one of the best decorated and lit restaurants in Astoria and beyond. Warm woods, interesting artistic pieces. and very beautiful garden dining with tall bamboo along the rear wall. Can be a tiny bit noisy from music but overall a fantastic setting. The food is very tasty but probably a tiny bit bland across all spectrum of taste, regionality and spice.  Would still recommend it for a evening out (date or small group). This may sound odd but to most everyone the place would be a huge hit.  For those in the know, Poodums or Spicy Mina or Tofu natural would be the foodie pick for authentic regional fare.

Leng Thai
moderate prices
nice service
very good food
excellent decor
awesome bathroom
