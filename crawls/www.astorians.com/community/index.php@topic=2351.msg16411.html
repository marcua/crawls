Interesting comment on development re LICA.

I do not know anyone that wants to tear down a 150 year old church for Fedders junk, and, about 90% of Queens (including {gasp} Peter Vallone) is looking at down zoning, so do not write off LICA over those issues.  They seem to be doing things that are pretty much standard for the rest of the borough (although not yet Astoria, which makes some of you bashful, I am certain)

But the thing that impresses me is the scope of their interest, from sanitation, to noise, to crime. I do not understand how they can effectively cover all this, but if they have any degree of savvy, then they had better accept that no one wants everything on their site (I certainly don't) but everyone want something there (I think you all agree to that)

Besides, it gives different groups a chance to learn about each others concerns. There may be some impact that these different strand have on each other.

Grassroots? God bless 'em. Lets see what they have to say.
