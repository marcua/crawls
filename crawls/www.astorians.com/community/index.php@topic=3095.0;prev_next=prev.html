Mostly it's just pizza and other Italian food near where I live.

Kebab Cafe is vegetarian friendly and oh-so-good.  Wave Thai has some vegetarian dishes, but you have to make a special request if you don't want fish paste in your food. There's a Chinese take-out place on Steinway just north of Ditmars that makes General Tso's bead curd. It's...meh. And it probably has fish in it too. Sometimes I just don't feel like asking. If I can't taste it and don't know about it, it won't bother me.

I tend to make do with altering whatever is on the menu. Most places are very accommodating.

Wave has the vegetarian duck, which I always order, that I find to be quite delicious.

I also like the cheese empanadas at San Antonio Bakery #2.  There is a terrific roasted vegetable sandwich at Sunswick.  They also serve you a generous portion of tasty guacamole with their chips when you order them together.

Oh, I can't forget some of the dumplings and various appetizers at Balkh Shish Kabob, too.

Watawa has a good lunch special that includes vegetarian roll options.  $7.50 for salad, soup, and 2 rolls.

Best grilled cheese is the grilled cheese and avocado at Cup.

I hear Mundo has some delicious vegetarian options.

Another enthusiastic vote for Kabob Cafe!

I try to eat vegetarian as much as possible. Just had a veggie burger at Sanford's Diner that was pretty good- they tend to fall apart pretty easily, this one was no exception. They also make sweet potato fries btw, something you don't see very often  :lol:

Love the whole wheat pizza @ Pizza Palace. Not that it's that much healthier or any more vegetarian than regular pizza, but the whole wheat adds a nuttier flavor/texture which is a nice change of pace.

Goes without saying that the veggie burrito at Tacos Mexico is amazing, like two meals in one for $6. Fresca Tortilla also has nice black bean tortillas etc.

Too bad Zen Palate doesn't branch out to Astoria.
