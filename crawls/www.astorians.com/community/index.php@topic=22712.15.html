Second, cars are racing to not get stuck at the next light -- they do anyway, and end up slamming on their brakes or fly through the red light. We could definitely use a speed bump or two -- people need to slow the hell down.

The problem with 33rd street is that it's 2 lanes in one direction.  This encourages a highway mentality, wherein drivers are driven (excuse the pun) to pass the car they perceive as going too slow, even if the 'slow' car is more than likely speeding to begin with.  To actually slow cars down, I think a road diet (taking the road from two lanes down to one) would be more effective than speed bumps.  Because cars are already prone to racing from one red light to the next, it's easy to see them doing the same thing with speed bumps.
