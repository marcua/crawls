Title: Re: Bareburger on 31st Ave
Post by: Mark on June 14, 2009, 11:27:21 AM
My wife and I went yesterday as well.  We were really excited for this place to open as there aren't too many restaurants in the neighborhood that focus on organic ingredients.  All in all we both really liked the place and will definitely go back.

We got there about 5pm yesterday and the place was packed.  We got the last table and there was even a small line for to go orders.  I am sure that the owners were very happy with the opening - i can't imagine how excited/nervous small business owners must be when they first open the doors - but they seemed slightly overwhelmed by the amount of people.  They only had one wait-person for the floor and she was running all over taking orders - she did a good job under the circumstances.  It took a little while to order but this was obviously due to just opening and I suspect it will be sorted out. On the other hand they seemed to have enough people behind the counter and at the grill. Once we ordered we did not have to wait very long for our milkshake and then food. A couple of the people behind the counters were running food out to the tables which helped out a lot. The staff was very friendly and attentive. Several people came over to our table during the course of our meal to see if everything was okay.

They obviously put a lot of time and energy into the atmosphere: garage door walls, metal chairs, cool light fixtures with forks and bar glasses hanging from them and funky green high back booths.  It seems like they have plenty of space for an outdoor seating area if they need it and if they can get the required cafe license.  The atmosphere was very fun and almost celebratory with a lot of energy.

We ordered an original beef cheeseburger with fries, a California Burger (topped with avocado, onion and tomato) with onion rings, a peanut butter chocolate milkshake and an iced tea.  We liked the Original Burger much more than the California and the onion rings and the milkshake were excellent.

The Original Burger comes with there signature special sauce which seemed like it was a bbq mayo.  It had a subtle smokey flavor that did not overwhelm the rest of the burger.  It was a great compliment to the other condiments - jack cheese, lettuce, tomato and red onion. We both thought the Original Burger was very good with some issues with the actual burger patty (see below).

We ordered the California burger with beef - they suggest the veggie burger.  We found this one a little on the bland side but maybe it is better with the veggie burger. We thought that the avocado needed a little something more - salt, pepper, and/or mayo.   

We ordered both burgers medium as suggested by the waitress. The flavor of the beef was good and the beef patties are handmade which is nice. As m_bart said above, the burgers came out a little overcooked -maybe medium well with very little pink- and pretty dry.  I would suggest ordering the burger medium rare.  I am not positive but I think they use a fairly lean mix of beef that is finely ground.  I would like to see a little fattier mix with more juice - especially when they have other lean options on the menu like bison and elk.  On the other hand, the burgers both had a really nice charred crust that added a nice grilled flavor and good texture. 

The Peanut Butter milkshake was excellent and tasted very fresh.  The milkshake tasted homemade and not from a mix.  The ice tea was excellent as well.  It was light, refreshing and slightly sweet.

The onion rings were also another standout and probably the best single item we had.  The rings were crisp on the outside with juicy soft onions on the inside.  They had a really nice think crunchy covering.  I thought the fries were okay but I have to admit that I am usually not a big fan of the fresh cut fries as they often come out too dark for my liking.  To Bareburgers credit, they seemed a little better than the fresh cut/dark fries I have had in the past.

As m_bart said above, the place is definitely on the pricey side. It was about $50 for two burgers, two sides, milkshake, drink and tip. Unfortunately for us the price puts Bareburger into the occasional category - maybe 4 or 5 times a year. It is nice, however, to support a restaurant that focuses on the environment with quality ingredients and a fun atmosphere.  I hope they do some sort of a discount on weekday nights.  Maybe a two for tuesday - two burgers for the price of one - or possibly $1 sides, something like that.

All in all I highly recommend this restaurant as a fun burger joint with cool decor, quality food and friendly staff.

I had Bareburger over two weeks ago and am only getting to the review now.  The place did make an impression on me though, enough that I can remember the details quite lucidly.

First off, the place is gorgeous.  It was designed and decorated well.  The bathrooms are state of the art.  Every aesthetic exudes of modern and eco-friendly green.  Seriously, I thought the place looked awesome.

And that's where the compliments end.  Hey, I said the place made an impression, not the food.

So here we go.

I ordered the regular beef burger, the original, with cheese medium rare, and my wife ordered the one with guacamole -- we were and still are in a huge avocado kick -- cooked medium well along with a vanilla milkshake.

For all their pride in using "natural" or top grade meat, they sure add a lot of seasoning, which is fine, if that's the chef's personal taste.  More than any other spice, the meat was loaded with pepper.  It just happens to be that I love my pepper, so it was tasty in that regard, but I can see how purists would see this as a cowardly way out of showcasing the supreme quality of the meat.  As well-seasoned as it was, the fact that they didn't know how to control the temperature of the grill is inexcusable.  My "medium rare" was clearly cooked well-done.  My wife's "medium well" was chewy, dry, and a bit like crumbled jerky.  But, it was well-seasoned, so in her case, it was well-seasoned jerky.

To me, a burger should be seared shut over high heat to retain all of its moisture.  Layers of richness (usually the cheese and sauce/dressing) are then added to ensure max moistness and juiciness.  An inherently excellent and juicy burger doesn't even need sauce, because the sear on the meat did its job of maintaining the moisture.  In Bareburger's case, not having the sauce would have been painful to swallow.

The toppings on my burger were fine.  Nothing to brag about, nothing to snicker at either.  On my wife's, the guacamole was probably the saddest "fresh" guacamole I've ever seen comparable only to grocery store-bought artificial varieties.  The most obvious shortcoming of their guacamole was that it was made a while ago.  The tomatoes were soggy and limp, which made the guacamole runny.  The avocados had oxidized, turning it much darker and less appealing.  Thank god they were not generous with it as it only comprised of a small, thin layer on the burger.  Both the amount of guacamole and the way in which it was made/tasted could not justify the extra couple of dollars they charged for it.

The bread was fine.  The brioche was neither inspiring nor deflating.  It did its job although it's nothing to write home about.

Then comes the shake.  It was unanimously the best part of the meal.  It was cold, rich, creamy, and in a reasonable (slightly small) portion enough to satisfy those who can easily satisfy their sugar tooth with moderate servings.  Of course, for those of us who like a heaping mound of dessert, it definitely won't curb those joneses.  The biggest disappointment with the shake was that it didn't really taste of vanilla.  It was just smooth, creamy, and dense.  But the flavor was lacking.  It might behoove them to add a little more vanilla to their vanilla or by giving the shake a good douse of extract before serving.

The service was good.  I actually liked it quite a bit.  Kind waiters and waitresses (different people waited on us).  In the end and except for the food (which wasn't bad, it just wasn't good either), it was a very pleasant experience.


By the end, I paid $30 for a pleasant experience but lackluster food.  You can tell that they put a lot of thought into the menu and that in theory it should all taste amazing.  They clearly spent money on the location and carefully sourced their food.  But the cooks in the kitchen is where it all starts to unravel.  They should have spent a little more money there and hired themselves some better burger flippers instead of the space age bathroom.

Food: C
Portion Size: B-
Value: C-
Would I Recommend?  I appreciate how the owners/designer but a lot of time and thought into the place.  I was sorely disappointed with the execution of the food.  That being said, because my experience was pleasant overall, I would give them another chance.  If they disappoint me again...well then never again...can't fool me twice.
