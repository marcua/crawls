Hi Everyone,

Thanks for all the feedback, most of which was delivered in a very constructive and objective way.  The remainder of the comments veered into uninformed/non-productive territory so, as a writer, they ranked pretty low on the relevance scale for me.

In response to some of the comments, I'd like to address the grammatical issue.  As a professional writer, I must adhere to strict editorial standards that are pretty universal across the publishing field.  That is my day job and I couldn't be happier to operate within the confines of those rules.  However, just as we all change clothes at the end of the day (I hope), I also like to stretch my legs a bit and use a more colloquial tone when I'm writing for a personal outlet like NY Knife and Fork.  Otherwise, the line between paid labor and creative outlet become hopelessly blurred.  Simply put, while spelling mistakes are never really acceptable, more playful and conversational styles are how I contribute my voice to the symphony of foodie conversations on Astorians.com. 

Yes, there are fragments.  Yes, there are slang terms, pop culture references and even the occasional text message abbreviation.  Consider them the Tourette's syndrome of an overly plugged-in generation, whose ranks I'm glad to join.   The point is that my writing style is constantly evolving as it is honed through the lens of my experience and sensibilities.  I'm glad your comments are now an additional ingredient in my writing process. 

As new members of this community we’re dedicated to keeping the conversation elevated and constructive by refraining from mean-spirited criticism that serves only to incite, rather than inform or entertain.  Try to keep up.   

Again, I appreciate all the well-intentioned feedback.  Cheers!

-Marissa
