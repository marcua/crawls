Thank you for the link!  :-)

Back about 12 years ago a few friends and I set out to find Hoyt Ave Tunnel (Linden Brook). The opening is right underneath the dog park next to Shore Towers. Upon finding it, we we're disappointed that the water was too high and we couldn't go in. We checked the NOAA site to see when low tide was and did it all over again. A very interesting site to say the least! The walls and floor were brick lined, but the tunnel ended right at the intersection of Hoyt Ave. and Shore Blvd. There are 2 large flood gates that were installed by the DEP, and right above us were 6 or so square manhole covers. The flood gates are held in to place by large hinges, and are forced open when a storm hits. The flood gates also have a large chain attached to them that is secured to a large concrete block. Two of us pulled on the chain while the other peeked in. The tunnel runs right down Shore Blvd. and ends up at the Bower Bay sewage treatment plant.

There are/were a network of tunnels that connected many homes (min-mansions) in the Old Astoria Village.  As kids we accessed one tunnel through a friends home (sub basement).  The network of tunnels ended with one main tunnel leading to the water which Shore Towers Condo sits above.  It was said that the tunnels where used by the servants to get the food-stuff from the boat(s) at the shore line and then bring it back to the homes through these tunnels - all being done unseen, like ants.  Many of the homes still have the access, but the entrances have been cemented shut.  The Astoria historical Society CLAIMS that it is untrue, but I've played in these tunnels as a child.  All you have to do is ask some of the old time home owners of these houses & they will tell you that they exsist

I hope you don't live on 14th place by the park.  That area was the 1st Astoria Village Cemetery which was in use from 1842 to 1849 then closed - no mention if the bodies were ever relocated.  The Second Astoria Village Cemetery was in the Broadway area which was in use from 1850 to 1869 then closed - Bodies moved to St Michael's Cemetery, Flushing.  My grandmother told me that the B'way cemetery was between 29thst/crescent street (she believed).
