Wow, I know Oceano is the latest incarnation on the "cursed corner" and all, but I was especially amazed while driving between the two places last evening: Oceano had one couple inside the whole restaurant that I could see. I think they need to hire a priest or a shaman or take SOME sort of desperate measures to un-curse that corner. If a big, open seafood restaurant *still* can't draw a few people from an overcrowded seafood place directly across the street... are they really that bad? that expensive?

The BF says Oceano needs to run some specials to start with. That might help lift the curse.

In my opinion, there is absolutely no comparison between Kyclades and Oceano.

One is established with a clientele base and classic, fresh, well prized, consistent Greek cuisine.

The other is completely lost and has no clue how to run a restaurant. To begin with – Oceano doesn’t know who they want to be. First it was Latin, then just seafood, now they changed their logo to Mediterranean. Their original menu when they first opened was a mess. You didn’t know what you were looking at.  Even though they were pushing Latin food – half of their menu consisted of Greek cuisine. Most likely influenced by a Greek owner. He was running this restaurant like a diner – reflected by décor, menu, server’s uniform, etc.

I was very excited when the Latin place opened and went few times, but it felt uncomfortable and the food was mediocre.
