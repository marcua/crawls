I actually prefer Long Beach as the nicest - less clam shells to cut your feet on.  It's only a 45 min ride on the LIRR from Woodside, you walk down the street from the station and you are there.   The price of admin is included in the roundtrip tic, and is about $17 total.

As opposed to Jones Beach which is a longer ride and then you have to board a shuttle bus.   And more crowded, as it is more popular, but either one is crowded anyway.

http://www.mta.nyc.ny.us/lirr/Timetable/Branch.htm?Folder=Branch&Branch=Long%20Beach&Direction=East&Period=Weekend

Trains leave at the 50 of the hour in the mornings, I would say 9:50 or 10:50am would be good, and we could meet 20mins beforehand at the Woodside LIRR station at the ticket booth for where you purchase the beach package.  It's the 61st stop on the 7 train, and it's in the same station as the 7.

Bring a cooler with water, food, fruit, towel, books and mags.  They sell food there too.  I usually go to the bagel place on Ditmars and 28th in the morning to load up.  Hope it's still open!

We are going to Governor's Island tomorrow, my home for the first four years I lived in NYC!! I'm so excited, so many great memories!

 Y'all should check it out sometime. Time is running out for this year - think the tour stops like Sept.3? But it's all free - the ferry ride and somewhat self-guided tour. Governor's Island had been a military base since the Dutch took over and when I was stationed there it was the largest Coast Guard base in the world. I can't even begin to tell y'all how fantastic it was livin there.... Take W or (weekend) R to Whitehall and it is next to the Staten Island Ferry terminal.

From what I can tell they don't let you run rampant around the whole Island or anything,  :-( but I hope I get to see the building I lived in. I can still smell it. LOL, that sounded bad, I mean it had a good smell! I love those moments, especially when out of nowhere, a smell from your past finds you and floods your whole being with something so glorious, or profound, or even something so sad that it takes your breath away......I love that.

Views of the whole harbor - Statue of Liberty, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Jersey  - can be quite stunning depending on the weather, sun, your frame of mind.....
