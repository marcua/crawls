Am I the only one who didn't have such a bad experience with MQ?  I used them twice - the first time, they were on the pushy side as others have reported, but I figured that's just how the whole broker thing works.  The apartment I got was good, in a great location, but kinda old and after 2 years I had a bigger budget and wanted a nicer place.  But overall, I was satisfied with using MQ.

The second time I used them, which was this summer, I contacted them after seeing a listing on Craiglist for a place that looked amazing and was within my budget (albeit at the high end of it).  I saw the place, fell in love, and put down a deposit that day, with no pressure from MQ.  This was 2 months before the lease on my old place was up so I had the luxury of time and knowing exactly what I wanted.

I dunno, maybe I just lucked out, especially considering that Craigslist posts are a crapshoot.  But with any broker I would give the advice to give yourself time when you look for a place if you can.  That way, you are in the driver's seat and don't feel as pressured by broker-speak.
