I've lived in Queensview for 6 years.   

Pros: 
Nice Apartments
Great Views
Cheap Parking
Quiet
Cheap (great for buyers)

Cons:
Cheap (bad for sellers)--and the board will not take the open market issue to vote again
No dogs allowed
Sometimes it feels like a retirement home--new obituary seemingly every week
No assigned parking spots
Across from the projects
Bad heating system:  cannot regulate heat in individual units
Maintenance staff that used to be very tardy/uncaring.  They seem to be getting better though
Security staff spend more time inside that patroling the grounds.  After a recent mugging, they are more vigilant though

I started in the coop as a young buyer 6 years ago, perhaps the youngest buyer they had in a long time.  The price and the views have been great perks, but there are negatives too.

NYC and NYS transfer tax is usually paid by the seller. However, because queensview and not the previous owner sells the apartment there's some weird loophole that makes the buyer pay it. I just bought in Aug 09 and my bank messed up the forms at closing and we had to get new ones faxed to us, so if you aren't done closing you may want to remind your bank of this so they can fill the forms out correctly.

The open market question is interesting. When my mother passed away in the early eighties my brother and I had to dispose of her 5 1/2 room apt.  We debated over trying to hang on to it, but we were both living out of state by then and there was a one year limit on sub-lets (of course there were some illegal sublets being done). We were also told that if the apt. hadn't been specifically left to us in her will it would go back to the co-op! For those interested in history, the monthly maint. fee at the time was in the three hundreds. I forget if we received 5 or 10 thousand for it but is wasn't more than that. You didn't pay 25% to Queensview but you were responsible for restoring the apartment to its original condition when you sold.  Do sellers make money if the price increases between the time they buy and sell or does the co-op just charge the new buyer more?
