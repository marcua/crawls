A little history for the corner where Table 30 is now opening. In the 1950s (before my time) it used to be a pharmacy (Harris Drugs, you could see the old tile mosaic sign during the renovation). Around 1967 it became Grand Avenue Pizzeria, easily the best pizzerias in the neighborhood. Around 10 years ago it moved across the street to the west corner. Since then it was a fruit and produce store with 2 different owners. I hope Table 30 is more of a restaurant like Ovedia or Pinnocchio. Too many "eateries" on 30th avenue are trendy fancy cafe's, last thing we need is another one. Lets stop the insanity.
