Rikers jail inmate accused of trying to hire hit man to kill mom

http://www.amny.com/news/local/wire/ny-bc-ny--hitmanformother0410apr10,0,5593844.story

April 10, 2007, 5:36 PM EDT
 
NEW YORK (AP) _ Thinking his stint in jail was a perfect alibi, an inmate tried to hire a hit man to kill his mother so he could have early access to his inheritance, prosecutors said.

Joseph Salvio was charged at his arraignment Tuesday with conspiracy to commit murder, the Queens district attorney's office said in a release. The Astoria resident, who was being held without bail, could face up to 25 years in prison if convicted.

Salvio, 30, was jailed at Rikers Island on drug possession charges when he met an undercover police officer posing as a hit man, prosecutors said. He handed over the address and telephone number of his mother, Susan Allogramento, and detailed how he wanted her death to look like a robbery, prosecutors said.

He offered three of his mother's fur coats valued at $10,000 in exchange for the hit, they said.

A woman who answered a phone registered to S. Allogramento refused to comment Tuesday. A phone message left for Salvio's lawyer was not immediately returned.
