the way you wrote "the many people I know who are on 'disability'", I'm not sure if you meant that in a good way or in a sarcastic way. But I decided to respond based on what I've learned as a social worker both in the classroom and also out in the field...

The truly disabled are not just people on wheelchairs and they are not just people injured while on the job. Take people that randomly suffer a car accident for example (I choose them both because their numbers are on the rise and because they are not what you think of commonly when you think dissability):

what if they suffered a head injury that has left them with a neurologic defect that is virtually undetectable to the human eye (for example, as is common among car accident victims, reduced motor ability leading to no longer being able to quickly add numbers, or no longer being able move one's hands or feet at a "normal" pace without a huge amount of effort).

Those people don't get very much in the way of disability insurance, public or private, if they weren't well off to begin with. In fact, they have to go through (excuse my language) hell and then some to get public dissability assistance, which in most states also adds up to very little (since in 1980 one of the first things Ronald Reagan did was severely cut funding for disability).

Unions are for the most part the only way many people are guaranteed a decent form of protection (since most people can't afford or don't think of even purchasing disability insurance) so that they won't have to go on public assistance (which in this day and age is only temporary)

as an asside, in NY State, one has access to only if one's income is less than $600 a month. anyone that has worked with DSS (department of social services) in NYC knows that it's not a fun picnic trying to get PA in any case.

so sorry for the mini social welfare lecture but my point was that every topic is more complex than one line opinions, whether that's Unions (and yes the Teamsters are an example of a corrupt union, but the California Healthcare Worker's Union is an example of a wonderful supportive group that helps people out) or corporate American (which more and more is offering workers less and less benefits due to the rising cost of such benefits, in particular health insurance).
