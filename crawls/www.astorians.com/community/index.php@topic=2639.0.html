[N.B. Read some anti-Con Ed rants on the general thread, so posting this pro-Con Ed rant on the general thread.]

Heard Terri Delis this morning on the radio. You know, the wife of George (Demolition Man) Delis, your rabid pro-development community board district manger? Well it seems as it the fault of the energy problem is (roll the drums) Con Ed.

What? Are the developers trying to shift blame to Con Ed? You bethca.

Ok, as commentator Dave Ross, says, lets play that one slow.

For decades, did Con Ed send shoddy equipment to Astoria out of malice? No.
For years, did Co Ed send the dregs of its workforce to Astoria out of contempt? No.

No? Well, then good people, what is the mysterious source of Con Ed’s failure in this one tiny area of our great metropolis?

Their crime and the source of your aggravation?

Con Ed did not keep up with the crushing blow from the sky-rocketing ‘ill’-legal conversions and development schemes that seem to have seized the imagination of those who lead our community. Even worse, this cult of development has not only transfixed our community’s leadership, but, with their active encouragement, has trickled down to the local homeowner.

Everyone sits on a gold mine. All you have to do is make a big hole and dig for it. Right?

Any rosy picture, however, always has that pesky dark cloud. Development is now so out of control, that no one, not even Con Ed, has a handle on how big it is or what the utility demands are.

Not the sewage system. Ever wonder about that smell and the inability to do anything about it despite calm assurances?

And of course, everyone’s favorite, not the transit/transportation network. Even those who are in favor of development (for what ever reason) acknowledge that to drive around, or to take the train service, is, shall we kindly say, not what it used to be.

The root cause of all these? Just remember one word. Overdevelopment.

And you are just seeing the tip of big changes, we can assure you that.

BTW, the really scary thing is that it took several days for the news from Astoria to get out to the rest of the city. Even now, everyone else in NYC has only the vaguest idea of what you are going through.

The problem is not only overdevelopment, it is also increasing demand made by the existing customers, or rather, existing units.  

A couple of years ago the blackouts were in Harlem and the Bronx, not Manhattan or Astoria. They were there because all of a sudden, in the middle of an economic boom, people who had not been able to afford air conditioners, could. and they went out and bought them, and they turned them on.

And suddenly, a neighborhood that had an infrastructure that had been dealing with only fans and refigerators found itself trying to deal with an A/C in every apartment... needless to say it couldn't.

In the same way, the population of Astoria is changing, and more people are making a greater demand on the infrastructure.. with technologies that were not being used before.

Let's be honest, how many of us have as many electrical outlets as we "need" in our homes? I know almost every outlet in my apartment is home to a surge protector which triples the number of appliances I can plug into it... and I still need more! Our homes were simply not built for so much technology! My building has attachments for screen doors on every apartment door so that the residents could keep their doors open during the summer to have some air circulation- that's the A/C it was built for.

So yes there is overdevelopment, and it is something we should be concerned about. And yes,  we are overtaxing the system with our increasing dependence on electronic devices. And yes, Con ed has not kept up with the growing demand and has not renewed the infrastructure for which it is responsible.

But the most important thing we need to realize is that this is not a simple equation, and there is not only one villain.
