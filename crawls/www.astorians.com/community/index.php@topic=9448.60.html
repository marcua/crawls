Quote from: pink_floyd on February 25, 2008, 06:13:06 PM
I don't think that the money being made is small.
a busy location like that serving lunch and dinner can sell 500-700 plates a day multiply that by average of $5 a plate you are talking about over 16K (5 days only) a week now you tell me how much can the ingredients and salaries cost him per week? When you are not paying rent electric telephone, insurance the profit margin is HUGE.


but you're ignoring the gas generator, a cell phone for emergencies, propane for the grill, gasoline, insurance and parking on the truck or van needed to haul the cart to wherever it's sitting.  You've neglected to add in the costs of the plates, bags and silverware.  The fines any given vendor can expect from the city are costly. These guys don't have health insurance, so they're paying for their medical bills out of pocket, and they work through the cold, snow, rain, and heat.  And you're grossly underestimating the cost and availability of vendor permits, and yes, you do need to register your "spot", and it needs to be documented and visible on your cart.
Even using the formula that 1 pound of raw chicken equates to 1/2 cup of cooked, and half of your 500-700 plates (which is a gross overestimation in my experience) is chicken, expecting 1 cup of chicken on any given plate, if only half of the plates sold were chicken you're talking at least 500 lbs of chicken each day, and that's ignoring the lamb, the rice, the lettuce and tomatoes, the onions, peppers, seasoning, hot sauces, whatever goes into the white sauce. 
If these guys take a day off, they've just lost at least $2500, by your calculations.  Honestly, I don't think that the profit margin is huge, and I think that these guys work their asses off for every penny they earn.
