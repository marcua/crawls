Based on the cleanliness of most stations AND trains... why is anyone surprised that the bathrooms are at least the same, if not worse?

It seems if you're drunk, partied out or homeless, then the entire transit system becomes your toilet anyway.  After living in NYC for 2 years, I've witnessed several times people vomitting or urinating both within the stations and on trains.  In one case, a bum walked between cars while we were in motion and urinated off the side of the car/tracks while on an elevated/outdoors area of the route.  If I were either walking or driving underneath the train at the time, I'd be pretty pissed off for getting pissed on.

For the rest of us, the rationale forced upon us by the lack of sanitation bascially gives us a series of unwanted choices... use a disgusting bathroom, use a public/inappropriate space, or hold it (at the cost of increasing discomfort, possible health risks or eventually having an "accident").

I know with the vast population of NYC that uses the transit system on a daily basis, it must be almost impossible to keep the bathrooms very "fresh"... but maybe a more concerted effort by the MTA to keep things at least considerable for people in times of an emergency would help keep the rest of the station and city a little more sanitary.
