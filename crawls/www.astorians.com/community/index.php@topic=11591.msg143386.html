You know we cork intersections for the Tour de Queens, Tour de Brooklyn and the safety pack for the Tour de Bronx.  It's actually the safest and fastest way to get a group of cyclists through a light quickly instead of forcing them to be strung out between traffic and trying to regroup while drivers try to get around them.  Would you rather have 1 group of cyclists to get past or 5 individual smaller groups.  The major difference between our rides and CM is that we get the parade permits and the cops assist us in corking.

Thanks for the good info, Heff.  My problem with Critical Mass has always been their failure to secure permits, and their disregard for traffic laws.  I agree with you, it seems like they are trying to be counter-cultural, or even openly defiant for the sake of being openly defiant.  I get that some of them are trying to be part of a "radical" movement, but the motorists they disdain have just as much of a right to the road, provided they use it in a lawful and responsible context. 

Again, I am all for biking, and I understand that drivers have to learn to do a better job of sharing the road with those who are on bikes.  And that bikers are highly outnumbered by motorists. But those on bikes need to follow traffic laws, too.  It's a two way street.  I know this argument has been made before in a thread about waterfront parking, but the attitude of some (I stress some, not all!) bikers seems to be that since they are using a green method of transportation, they should be allowed to disregard the law, and hold motorists in contempt.  I read a posting on some other board that put it very well - some Critical Massers have just as much of a sense of entitlement as Hummer drivers, but with more of a sense of self righteousness.
