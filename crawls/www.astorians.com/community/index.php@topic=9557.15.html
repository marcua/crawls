Quote from: safebydaylight on April 30, 2008, 06:23:22 PM
Do I always need a phone line to get high speed internet w/ a wireless router?
Most people connect their wireless router to the internet via DSL on a phone line, or via Cable Modem on a cable TV connection.  Verizon's FIOS uses a fiber-optic connection instead of a phone line (or tv cable).

Some service providers are beginning to offer wireless internet service, in which the service provider maintains wireless routers all over the place, and the customer doesn't. I heard an ad for this sort of thing the other day refer to "decafinated" internet service, since it frees nomadic types from continuously lurking in coffee shops.


By way of analogy, your own wireless router with DSL, cable, or fiber is similar to having a cordless telephone. This newer service is more like a cellular phone.
