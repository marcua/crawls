I just got done trying some food from Sakura - I went there and got take out.  I had:

-ume shiso roll
-uni sushi
-yellowtail (hamachi) sashimi (came with two pieces)
-mackerel (saba) sashimi (came with one piece)
-snapper (tai) sashimi (came with one piece)

The above came to $11.41.  I wanted to try a cross section of their sushi menu - not just the popular items, and not the fancy rolls with lots of ingredients that tend to hide the true flavour of the fish. 

As befitting a newly opened restaurant, all the fish was very fresh.  The rice was average neighbourhood quality.  The uni was not the best I've ever had, but quite acceptable with a moderate oceany flavour and good texture (and at $3.00, probably the cheapest uni I've ever had).  The yellowtail was really good - great flavour and buttery but not mushy texture.  I really liked the mackerel, too - it was marinated very nicely (no idea whether they do it on site, though).  The snapper was fresh and mild.

One of the things I was most excited about was that they have the ume shiso roll - Watawa does not, and they don't seem interested in making it on request.  I really like to end a sushi meal with it as a palate cleanser.  Sakura's has cucumber in it, which I've never seen, and which was ok but not necessary.   

I was quite pleased with everything for a neighbourhood sushi joint, and I'm looking forward to trying more of their (quite extensive) menu.  They told me that they should have a license to serve sake in a couple of weeks.
