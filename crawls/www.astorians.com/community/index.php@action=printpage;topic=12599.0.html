Title: Re: 23 yr old new to Astoria... where does everyone go out on weekends??
Post by: paratactical on October 09, 2008, 08:26:05 PM
What part of Astoria are you in? It seems like each area has different kinds of hot spots.

Some of my favorite places to hang:

1. 30th Ave east of Steinway - You've got Blackbird's, Dillinger's, the Quays all right there. These places have lots of very friendly people and are easy to go to alone. I recommend these most strongly for weekday nights, though.

2. 28th Ave around 36th St - Rapture and the Irish Rover are right there. Also, on 28th Ave across Steinway is Stove. See what I wrote about number one. The reason these spots are better is that most of the younger crowd likes to go to Manhattan on the weekends.

3. Sunswick on 35th Ave and 35th St - This place is good any night of the week and has lots of events.

4. HellGate Social near 21st St and Astoria Blvd (forget the exact spot) - Very VERY social, especially during Sunday movies and Wednesday night Rock Band.

5. 30th Ave - the cafes between 31st St and Steinway can be a lot of fun to hang out at, but they're more fun if you have friends. Not good to go alone.

Title: Re: 23 yr old new to Astoria... where does everyone go out on weekends??
Post by: paratactical on October 09, 2008, 10:08:37 PM
I've lived in both the Ditmars and 30th Ave area and my impression, in terms of the "scene" is that Ditmars is more upscale and mature. There are wine bars and Mojave and even the sports bar there--McCaan's--is very nice (clean bathrooms, new TVs, cocktail list, nice menu, etc). Ditmars is also a much quieter, darker, tree-lined neighborhood.

While there are trees on some Broadway streets, Broadway and 30th Ave seem more urban. After spending a year and a half on Ditmars, being back at 30th Ave is nice. Lots of places are open a lot later. Heliopolis and the Taco Truck are great. Most of the bars seem to be more divided than Ditmars (i.e. you're either with other 20-somethings or it's more of an old townie place--I like 'em both, personally). Broadway has more clubs and late-night spots.

I guess what I'm saying is that from Broadway to Ditmars, there really isn't an area that I think you can go wrong with. Pick the apartment that suits you and you'll find that walking from B'way to Dit isn't really that far at all.  :mrgreen:
