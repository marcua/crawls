I can indeed verify that it wasn't myastoria that took our site down :)

It kinda amazes me at how fast Astorians on this site are to pick up new stuff happening in the neighborhood. I think it's cool, but in this case Flooz discovered something before I had the chance to post on the board what was going on with myastoria and astorians.

Over the last couple years the founders of MyAstoria and I have been talking about working together to link the discussions our sites in some way. Since there was a discussion board on each site we were originally thinking how cool it could be if there was a way that information could be shared across the two sites while allowing each site to maintain its unique flavor. The problem was that although this idea was a great one in principle, the effort that would have been required to conversationally sync the too sites was just too much for either of us to undertake, so it never happened.

As some of you know, over the last few months the MyAstoria boards have gotten a bit overrun with spammers and as a result the community feel has really suffered. The founders have also had an addition to their family, and have had to work on other projects, so the site hasn't been updated in a long time. A few weeks ago I got an email from the founders of myastoria asking if I (we) would be willing to take over management of myastoria in collaboration with them. The only think they asked was that I (we) involve them in the re-development of the site, and that they'd like it to remain live and useful to the community. I agreed - and I've really enjoyed working with them so far in the brainstorming process.

The discussion boards on myastoria were discontinued (but preserved for the information there), and we're planning to (over time - not really soon probably) transform the site into a kind of directory for Astoria - basically a wikipedia for Astoria and LIC.

The sites won't "merge" or anything like that because I'd like Astorians to keep its feel. But MyAstoria, with our help will become a kind of knowledge base for Astoria, while Astorians will remain focused on community conversation. So don't worry, Astorians.com won't change. :)

The change will take quite a bit of time as it will require a lot of work on the part of myself or volunteer coders. But once the site is up and running it's my hope that some astorians.com users will play an active role in writing about some of this neighborhood's great restaurants, events, history, etc.
