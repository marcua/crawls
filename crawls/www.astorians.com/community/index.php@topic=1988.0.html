Cafe Review
« on: April 22, 2006, 04:15:57 PM »
So we're back from vacation and it's COLD and rainy and we're hungry...

so off we go to get brunch...

1st stop Grand Cafe - we know what to expect.  The $10 brunch includes: average to okay food, a lousy drink and good to great service.  We can handle that.  Once we sit, we realize that what we can't handle is the music.  Sometimes the music at Grand is okay - but today it was T - H - U - M - P - I - N - G and ultra loud and... there was no way we were going to be able to relax and enjoy our average food, lousy drink and great service...

so onward we strolled down 30th....

and there it was... the newly renovated... super chic looking .... reopened Avenue Cafe.  Now I have to say that we used to go to Avenue Cafe regularly - but stopped going a year or so back b/c the smoke was usually so heavy that we couldn't take it.  I'd heard they'd stopped allowing smoking - but any time we strolled by... there seemed to be a cloud wafting if not - through the place - at least around the place. 

Now it's renovated and yellow - and it beckoned... so we approached.  WOW - it's beautiful - a really beautiful room (smoke free), the music was still that thumping disco garbage that every euro style  place plays - but it was low enough to (almost) be considered background music.  So we got a table.  As we entered the manager, looking suprised, said "oh you're back."  I hate that he thinks that it's the new renovation that got us in (I guess, it was in a way) rather than the old smoke that drove us out. 

The design is really lovely - in a soho kind of way. Obviously money was poured into the renovation. There's a decent size bar in the front (beer, wine and coffee only I think).  Lots of little tables and they saved the raised area in the back for a lounge area with couches and low tables.  The atmosphere is warm - I can see why the place was packed 3 days after reopening.  The fact that the designer forgot to leave an aisle to a number of the tables...must have been an oversight.  I guess they thought they needed to squeeze in a couple more tables. 

I'm not sure if our terrible service was due to the fact that we had a terrible waitress or maybe she would have been a very good waitress had we been sitting at a table that she could have gotten to with out climbing over 3 patrons.  The climbing issue didn't stop all the servers from getting to us, just our assigned waitress. 

Did I mention the service??  Yeah - it was terrible - before the renovation - it was bad - today ... it was terrible.  They weren't short staffed. There was lots of staff walking up and down the aisle looking for something to do - just make sure you sit at a table ON an aisle. 

The Food:  Really the main focus of the cafe should be the food  - well, perhaps not at the euro cafes of Astoria.  Ave Cafe used to have a decent kitchen that put out great wraps and i'm sure other tasty treats - but I never got beyond the veggie wrap myself.  It was soo perfect.  Well, they've destroyed it.  What was grilled veggies slathered in humus and wrapped in pita - became jarred roasted peppers, eggplant and zuccini, tossed on the grill for a minute to get the "grilled look" and then tossed in a pita - oh... and with a dab of humus that was totally lost in the jarred marinated oils that seeped out of the veggies.  It was unedible.  The potato chips were replaced by tasteless undercooked fries. A small pile of greens and feta was added to the plate (a definite plus) but are served undressed. 

The burger - has great onions, mushrooms and cheese - the meat itself comes from a previously frozen (I think), tasteless ( I know) patty. 

I know that the main reason that people go to the cafes is to be social, to drink coffee (though most drink that frothy nescafe stuff - that hardly  counts as coffee) and to see and be seen.  I just thought that as long as the 30th Ave cafes seem to be engaged in an all out war to see who will win the customers' wallets (note - Flo is currently closed for renovations even though they've been open less than 1 year) maybe it would occur to a couple of the owners that by improving the food and service business will improve.  Apparently this strategy has not yet been applied.

Actually, the best brunch deal in the area that I'm aware of is Fatty's Cafe on the corner of Ditmars Blvd. and Crescent Street. Perhaps one of the few that actually "Get" the idea of a Brunch. I believe that it is also about $10.00. You get a choice of several dishes and one: Bloody Mary, Mimosa: orange, mango or another exotic citrus fruit that escapes me now or a virgin home made limeade. Not a bad deal. They have their Brunches on Saturdays and Sundays. The Saturdays tend to be a little less crowded. They also have a backyard that should be opening soon too. Sue and Fernando are the friendly proprietors. Cathy is Fern's sister and the short cute Greek Chick is Reeni (Sp?) working her way through nursing school, sometimes you'll see Ziggy, a Scottsman who has his own band...an interesting mix. There is no Television, anywhere, and it is not missed.  The music is usually Jazz, Blues or something Latin...no blaring Euro Disco. This keeps the Sports Bar crowd at the Remote, McCanns, etc. Nothing wrong with them but this place draws a different kind of crowd. A great date bar. I prefer the  Pressed Cuban sandy with the salad,( I've ordered it so often they have come to calling it "Red's usual" or just a "Red") the coffe con leche is quite good.
 There is another new bar called Dillinger's, 45th Ave? I was there once and noticed its Brunch menu. $14.95 with bottomless drinks and usual brunch fare. I have yet to try it.
