In our apartment search, we probably saw at least 8-10 different brokers (I took the Craigslist approach, but all of the properties we were interested in were through brokers).

We would up getting ours through Elena from Barskaya Group. She was non-pushy and very responsive. She was very hands off - she just allowed us to look around as much as we pleased and said nothing to sway our opinion one way or the other. Her fee was only half a month's rent, which was a very pleasant surprise!

My favorite of the brokers we worked with was Yvonne of Castle Realty. She was just such a genuinely nice and caring person. She was able to see me with very little notice, numerous times in a day, to show me an apartment. She also very willingly negotiated with the landlord for me, since there were some very specific requests I had, and I feel she really gave her all to try to get me the apartment. It didn't work out (which was for the best, since the one we got is nicer!), but I feel she really went the extra mile. She even called me back a week or two after we found out the landlord had decided to give it to a family friend to see if I had found anything.

I recently dealt with Astoria Apartments LLC on 31st st and Ditmars.  DO NOT use them!  Their business practices are shady and unethical.  The owner wanted me to pay a broker fee before ever meeting my prospective landlord, signing a lease, etc. 
They are more interested in closing a deal as quickly as possible and putting your money in their pocket than making good tenant/landlord relationships that would lead to repeat business. 
They have also had the name Maximum Exposure Realty LLC in the past which makes me wonder why the name change?

On a positive note, Liz Realty on Ditmars and Steinway has been very professional, warm and listens to your needs.  Try them.
