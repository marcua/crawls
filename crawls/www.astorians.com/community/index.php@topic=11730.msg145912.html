Marilyn Monroe was a size 8. That's not fat, but she'd be considered too big to act or model now.

It's true that she was heavier than many models now (and I agree that she'd be considered "too big" now, which is almost a travesty), but in terms of sizing, vanity sizing plays a role.  Women's clothes sizing has changed dramatically over the last century.  What was size 8 then is now a size 4 or less -- she was about 117 pounds when she died.

I have a flapper dress that my grandmother wore in the 1920's (when she was in her mid-20s).  The waist of it is about 23".  My grandmother was tiny -- about 5'2" and probably not more than 100 pounds.  It's a size 14 1/2.
