I'm using this rather than start a new thread.  But the moderators might want to change the subject to read Dekko Cafe.

We tried this place over the weekend and were very impressed.  Had the fried calamari and the chicken dumpling appetizers.  Calamari is not the most tender in town (but Astoria is extraordinary in that regard).  But the breading was light and it was not greasy.  Clean taste and very crisp and delicious.  The chicken dumpling are a mixture of chicken and spices that are dipped in a batter and lightly fried.  Shaped like lollipops and have small chicken bones inserted to grab them.  Great presentation and very good.  Split a spinach buttermilk salad with was very nice.  Freshly crumbled blue cheese and pecans in a buttermilk dressing.  Might be heavy on the dressing for some.  And it's huge.  I got the short rib raviolis.  They were really impressive.  Filled with shredded short ribs.  In a wild mushroom mascarpone sauce.  This was an outstanding dish.  The other meal was the organic chicken which was incredibly moist and served over very rich mashed potatoes.

The prices are very reasonable.  The place is creatively designed but still casual and relaxing.  Service is very attentive and friendly.  Not expert but no complaints.  They want very much to be sure you're happy.  I'm a little disappointed that there aren't stools at the bar.  Rather it's a full bar but just for waiters to pick up drink orders.  I like to be able to sit at a bar until everyone in our group makes it off the subway after work.  Then relax and sit at the table.  So it's a little weird to me to have to sit at a table drinking alone until the rest of your group arrives.  But I think I can survive that if I have to!  This was a very nice experience.  I know they'll do well.

We also went over last weekend on Friday night.  It was my first time there but my hubby's second.  The food is really spectacular and the service is attentive without being intruding.  There were three of us in the party and with drinks, two apps (the saganaki and the chicken dumplings) and three entrees (two burgers, cavatelli) and one dessert the bill was barely over $100.  The owner even sent over a round of tequila shots to welcome us since he had met my hubby the week before.  The thing I really appreciated is that the server, Joyce, was more than willing to check with the chef about my food allergies since blue cheese wasn't listed as part of the dumpling appetizer (for the record, it's in the sauce so they just put it on the side for us).

Afterwards we were offered a tour of the new space, Sienna, which is set to open this weekend.  Beautiful doesn't even begin to describe this new space.  It was designed by the same team who did Greenhouse in Manhattan and it is simply stunning.

I agree with TJT in that this place will do really well.  My only minor complaint was the train going but even after a few minutes we forgot all about that.

I'll second (third?) the positive reviews.

Went to Dekko for lunch over the weekend, and was impressed. I got the cuban sandwich, and my companion got the short rib ravioli. Both were delicious! Also tried both the red and white sangrias. The red had a spiced quality that was very nice--kind of like sangria and mulled cider had a baby. And the white sangria was refreshing and peachy. Service was friendly and attentive, without being overbearing.

I love the design of the place--it's sleek yet welcoming. I agree that it's a bummer that you can't sit at the bar, but it's not a huge deal.

I was very pleasantly surprised by the menu (both the food options and prices). Was expecting another greek cafe-type place, which this definitely is not. I feel like Dekko fills the void left by Indigo (since it turned into the Astoria Brewhouse and changed into a more bar-food type place).

FYI, Dekko has a live Jazz group that plays for brunch on Sundays. I finally checked it out yesterday, and really enjoyed it!

It's more the style of Jazz that you'd find in Paris, than American Jazz, if that makes sense (bass, guitar, and accordion player, with a guest percussionist who sat in).

They play from 1:00-4:00 on Sundays.
I asked the waitress about it, and she said they plan on continuing this for the foreseeable future.

The brunch food is good too.
I had a spinach & feta omelet, and my bf had the steak & eggs. Everything was yummy (and the breakfast potatoes on the side were very flavorful). Bloody marys had a nice spicy kick to them. And since we were enjoying the music so much, lingered with some cocktails after our meal (fig martini & cherry mojito. both delicious)
