Seriously, there are elements of city life that can be controlled, and those that cannot. Excessive noise isn't necessary from each individual who can prevent it. We can't necessarily control every truck bouncing over a pothole or the Amtrak train noise- hopefully someday, but not immediately.

Living in the city is even MORE of an excuse to exercise self-control. In the countryside, churchbells ringing from miles away is no big deal, nor is an occasional firetruck. But around here, there are fires/ambulances/police cars every two seconds- it's CONSTANT. There are so many people, litter will happen *without* people purposely contributing to it- garbage cans blowing over, etc.

There's also ten billion guys who love their ultra turbo car and motorcycle engines. Again, on an open highway in Nebraska, fine. In a dense urban environment, HELL NO.

And New Yorkers aren't purposely rude, they're just in a hurry, and that *appears* to be rudeness to non-cityfolk. Doesn't give New Yorkers the RIGHT to actually BE rude.

So the real response is, if you love to be loud, rude and obnoxious, you have no business living among millions of other people in close proximity, because you simply don't have the discipline to live in society. You belong on a farm in Armpit, Arkansas.
