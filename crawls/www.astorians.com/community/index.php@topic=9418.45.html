As somebody who looks to some people as if he is Arabic, who could regale you with thrilling tales such as being pursued in a high-speed (120mph) car chase by Homeland Security agents in unmarked cars outside Philadelphia, who has been suddenly surrounded by siren-blasting police cars on the Mall in Washington DC (halfway between the Washington Monument and Capitol Building), who has been asked by policemen NOT to take pictures in the middle of the day in public places like Union Square... all of this life-threatening and oppressive behavior a reaction to my taking pictures....

Is taking pictures "suspicious activity"? In my experience, it depends on the appearance of the person taking the pictures.

I know for a fact that there are police who make snap judgments based on nothing more than physical appearance and that there are people who report other people to the police based on nothing more than physical appearance.

I wish it weren't the case, but it is. Maybe your perspective on this would be difference if you had had police surround you, check your ID, ask you why you're taking pictures of the JEFFERSON MEMORIAL (e.g., Who are you? Why are you so interested in taking pictures here? Why now, at sunset? Stop telling us about 'the lighting' and tell us what your business is here?, etc.", scribble notes about how you're a professional photographer and video producer, and then order you to stop taking pictures even after their background check is complete and you're supposedly "cleared."

In other words, "suspicious activity" just became an excuse to intervene in my life and tell me what I could or couldn't do, even after I was supposedly no longer suspicious and should have enjoyed the same rights as any of my fellow American citizens. Of course, my lilly-white photographer friends are surprised by all of this. I am off-white, it turns out.

Intimidating, enraging, and demoralizing. Has the country become safer as a result of law enforcement behavior like this? I think it's become more dangerous. At least for people who look a certain way.

Is buying a one-way, first class airplane ticket in cash at the gate suspicious activity? Yes, I think that's odd. And there were rules against doing this before 9/11, which, if they had been followed, would have saved thousands of lives that day. Is it suspicious that not a single individual was ever held accountable for the gross mistakes and negligence that made it possible for the 9/11 attackers to get away with murder? Yeah, I think that's pretty damned suspicious.

Is it suspicious that NYC and the U.S. had years to improve first-responder communications systems after the first WTC attack and did absolutely nothing? Yeah, absurdly suspicious. Makes one wonder what the hell these security people are doing for a living? 

***

"Muslims in Astoria"?  :roll: 

You don't mean me, because I'm a capitalist free-thinker born in America. But you do mean me, because if you walked by me on Steinway St. I might be mistaken for "them." Maybe you saw me and didn't recognize me and thought I was one of the new ones flooding into the neighborhood.

FZ, ultimately, it's not a crime for a person to call another person... ANYTHING, let alone an infidel. It's not a crime to love or hate people for completely irrational reasons. It's not a crime to sympathize with Osama bin Laden. It's not a crime to hate the government of the U.S. or to attack the Western way of life. As soon as we make these things crimes... we don't want to live in that kind of country, do we?

We report crimes to the police. Or we make them aware of people who we have reason to believe may be planning to commit crimes.

It's not a good business practice to insult prospective customers, but it's not a crime either. Suspicious? What do you suspect? That the gas station is a front for a terrorist cell? Because you saw a guy praying, taking pictures of a structure across the street from him, and a guy was a jerk to you?

FZ, you should follow your best judgment, of course. I don't trust your judgment, but I hope the police will take you at your word and do a decent job of looking into your complaint without violating anybody's liberty rights.
