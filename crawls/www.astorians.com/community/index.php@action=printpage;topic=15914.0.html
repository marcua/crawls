Wedding photography is everything they say it is, terrifying, hard work, demanding, rewarding. I have had the experience only once, as I agreed to bail out a friend who waited too long to book a photographer, then could not find anyone affordable. (I declined the first time around, he came back and asked me again!) I learned a lot in those 11 hours that I was on my feet clicking shutters to my heart's content. The most important thing for me was that the bride & groom understood that this was my first professional gig, I was less nervous, and they already knew my work. Will I shoot weddings again? If I decide to make this more of a career and less of a hobby, yes. Cause it's a great way to make money, and professional photographers need to pay rent too. The hardest part of the whole thing was post processing. I work a day job that usually consumes 50+hrs of my week. Add 1 wedding a month (just one) and you will have no life. Second shooting is something I've also considered, as the primary photog will take the CF cards at the end of the day. But, they also own the photos you take, so, you know, there's that. But to touch back on your original statement, yes. It can be extremely rewarding. Not all brides are crazy. And couples look to the photographer to help them pose for those unique shots, as they are not used to being in front of a camera like most people.

Just ask around and tell them exactly that! :) I'm a photographer too, and while I haven't shot any weddings yet, I've definitely worked/assisted on some that were just a "shoot and drop 'em on CDs" type of job. Do expect, however, to pay a markup for getting all the high-res images on CD - the photographer is essentially giving you ALL the images to use how you please (without control of how many you print, where the images are used, etc), rather than having you choose from proofs and printing you an album. How much they charge you may/will vary greatly by photographer.

I would suggest not looking strictly at photographers/studios that shoot ONLY weddings, since they're more inclined to work with someone who buys a full package.

We just booked our photographer.  We wanted just about the same thing you described.  We're getting married in San Diego, so I had to go with photographers there.  Honestly, I didn't really look at too many because I had to rely on recommendations since we wouldn't be able to meet the photographer in person.  However, when I did speak to photographers, I did specify my parameters and that we wouldn't pay over X amount.  Many of the websites I saw didn't include their cheaper, under 4 hours packages, but a lot of photographers do offer them, esp. if the wedding is off season or on a week day.  We got a pretty good deal because our wedding is both in the off season and on a Thursday evening (the benefit of having small family destination wedding). 

So, basically our package is this: 2 1/2 hours of shooting (before the wedding - bride and groom separately; during the ceremony and then after the wedding down on the beach). We receive a dvd of all pictures with the copyrights waived within 4 weeks of the wedding and a hosted website for 3 months of all wedding photos where friend and family can order their own prints. We get $100 credit towards purchasing photos for ourselves. 

I'd recommend that you stipulate when the cd/dvd of photos is sent to you.   As mentioned is someone else's post, the post production work is labor intensive, so it can take a really long time for you to get that cd/dvd, esp. during the wedding season.  I have friends who still haven't received their cd/dvd and it's been two years since they got married.

We're having our reception here (well, White Plains) and I don't know if we're going to do with a photographer.  I can only imagine how much that can run for a 4 to 5 hour event.
