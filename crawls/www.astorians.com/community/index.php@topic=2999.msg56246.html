That's awesome!  I'm trying to figure out when it was -- the World Wide Web graphical interface was introduced at CERN in May or June of 1991, but there's no mention of that in the video.  On the other hand, internet connections in 1992-1993 were still pretty much non-graphical - and in fact, the WWW wasn't made free until April 30, 1993, and Mosaic, as the first widely accessible graphical browser, was also introduced in 1993. 

All that, plus the mention in the video of the sensational Canadian manslaughter case in which dissemination of information was restricted makes me think this broadcast is from sometime in the first half of 1993.  The case at issue was likely the Karla Homolka case - that link talks about "the resultant media ban instituted by Judge Frances Kovacs [that] sparked controversial debate and a flurry of unanticipated activity on small bulletin board services (BBSs) and on Usenet, a worldwide distributed bulletin board system available on university campuses."  The link discusses the extensive attempts at banning various Usenet newsgroups and BBS's by Canadian universities, and how people got around the ban.  Her plea bargain was in May 1993.
