Assuming this is a rent stabilized place, there are very specific guidelines under NYC law, about how far back a landlord can charge increases (not very far), what the requirements are for when a new lease must be offered and what happens when it is not offered timely.  Also, the landlord can only fail to renew rent stabilized places for very specific reasons.  (All info available on-line w/ a google search under NYC rent stabilized apartment.)
 
If you go to NYC housing court (look up address on-line) they have free lawyers there to assist you, clinic-style.  From what you say, sounds like you are in the right legally.  But legal stuff is always stressful and a pain in the neck.  Plus, do you want to continue to live in a place with that particular landlord? (I guess depends on how good a deal you are getting and your pain threshold.)

Hi Dee

If you do indeed have a Rent Stabilized (RS) apartment, the only circumstance that I can think of where your LL would be allowed to charge rent retroactively is if they’ve been awarded a rent increase due to a Major Capital Improvement (MCI) by the agency that oversees RS, the NYS Division of Housing & Community Renewal (DHCR).   A LL can do certain building wide capital improvements then apply to DHCR for a rent increase.  If approved, the LL can increase the rent under a specific formula based on the value of the improvement, and is also allowed to charge for the time it took for DHCR to process the application (commonly called ‘retroactive arrears’).  This does not seem like your circumstance however, so I’m only mentioning it as a possibility:  it may simply be that your LL is not expressing it correctly since MCIs often involve some rocket science to implement.

That being said, it sounds to me that your LL did not appropriately renew your 09-11 lease, somehow forgot about it for two years, and is offering you a renewal based on a higher rent that they never actually collected as well as trying to collect what they would have gotten if they hadn’t screwed up.  If this is the case, under RS this is BS.  Under RS for lease renewals there’s something of a ‘you snooze you lose’ proposition for landlords:  if you were offered a renewal starting in 2009, you didn’t reply, kept paying the lower rent, AND your LL accepted those payments, they lose.  The lower rent you paid is still the legal rent.  If your LL were trying to do what they’re doing only 2-3 months after the expiration of your last renewal they MIGHT have had a case.  But after 2 years of doing nothing and accepting your payments as they were, they really don’t have a leg to stand onNow with THAT being said your best course of action, short of hiring an attorney, is to file a complaint with DCHR for failure to renew your lease the form for which is here: [url=http://nysdhcr.gov/Forms/Rent/ra90.pdf.  I think the most appropriate complaint in Part III is #6, that the owner refuses to renew on the same terms & conditions of the prior lease, since they appear to be basing the renewal rent on a rent that they never actually collected, but check off whatever seems appropriate, DHCR will figure it out.  Be sure to provide them with all the evidence they request on the form, and don’t be afraid to go into detail in the written section and add sheets as necessary.

It may take months, but I’m guessing that DHCR will order your LL to give you a lease renewal based from the rent that you’ve been paying (which has to be offered with at least 90 days notice so it will start at some future date), and to cut the crap about the renewal they missed.  In the meantime you should probably keep paying the rent you have been until you’re advised otherwise.
