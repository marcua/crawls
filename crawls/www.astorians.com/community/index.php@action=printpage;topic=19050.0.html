Title: Re: Is big business trying to control what's avaliable on the internet?
Post by: Harlan on August 08, 2010, 11:48:54 AM
To clarify/update this story, it seems as if content-specific throttling being discussed is limited to wireless networks, not to wired home/business internet connections. Here's a story about it (http://news.cnet.com/8301-31322_3-20012822-256.html?tag=mncol;6n):

Quote
Google and Verizon aren't saying much more, for now, and The New York Times appears to be tacitly sticking to its story. But if you read between the lines, and read CEO Eric Schmidt's comments on Net neutrality from this week's Techonomy conference, it's clear that the real traffic battleground is wireless networks.

My guess: Google and Verizon are almost certainly negotiating in a way to keep wired networks neutral and open, but to preserve Verizon and other telcos' ability to tier and shape their wireless networks as they see fit. It's bad news for mobile broadband in the United States, but it's clear that Google's not standing in the way.

FWIW, I'm opposed to this too, although I'm not opposed to modest preferential treatment of certain protocols. Companies should be able to register certain data streams as particular types of content (streaming video, etc, based on protocol only), and ISP should be allowed to make data quality guarantees for specific types of content, but ISPs should not be allowed to engage in financial relationships that would yield faster load times for one company's web page versus another company's web page, period, regardless of wired/wireless issues.
