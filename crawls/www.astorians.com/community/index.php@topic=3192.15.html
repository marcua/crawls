I'd say in this case "fishy" = bad management.  Both saunas have worked in the past, despite occasional down times.  The gym has been thriving enough that it's branched out to two floors.  Folks with Astoria-only memberships have stated that they've been informed of a rate increase (and most people I know are already spending anywhere form $75 to $95 a month).  It could be a case that the project of "fixing" (again, both are broken at the same time?  Come on ...) this is in a long line of overall NYSC renovations to various gyms and simply has to wait its turn.

Nearly every NYSC I've ever been in has had sauna issues at one time or another.  A few times, there was clearly a Health Dept notice stating the sauna was closed for failing inspection, or because of "inappropriate use" (mental image of Caligula-style roman orgy).  If I'm not mistaken, haven't seen that sort of notice posted in Astoria.  When it's not Health Dept related, it always seems  to be some sort of unexplained mechanical malfunction, although there's usually something in the posted notice asking users not to change settings themsleves, implying that someone somehow screwed up the sauna by setting it too high?  I don't know.

Before NYSC, I went to a social club in midtown that had a gym attached, with a gigantic sauna and steamroom next to each other.  Which may have broken down twice in my five years there, and was fixed in a matter of days.  As noted earlier, I'm not a sauna person at NYSC due to the cramped quarters, but when you're paying a nice monthly fee to go there, this is the sort of thing that should be fixed in a few days, not months.  And I have a hard time believing money is an issue.  Ergo, I'm thinking "bad management."  And that's what I mean by "fishy"!
