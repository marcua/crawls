Report on Astoria Park Cleanup, July 5
« on: July 05, 2008, 02:33:19 PM »
Just wanted to report back on this morning's cleanup of Astoria Park.  5 of us from the Astoria Park Alliance moved through the entire park, picking up garbage - from empty potato chip bags and water bottles, to some more disgusting stuff - and filled probably 10 large black garbage bags.  The cleanup lasted from about 7:00 - 9:30 a.m.  It was a really nice time, very relaxed, and it felt very good to help beautify the park. 

A number of people in the park thanked us for taking the time to clean up the garbage.  Some lamented the fact that so much garbage accumulates in the park, and one told me of times past when the garbage was not really an issue there. 

I also wanted to remind people that on Saturday July 12 is the date of the next Shoreline Cleanup at Astoria Park.  Cleanup will go from 1-4 p.m., and meet up in front of the war memorial at Shore Blvd.  This is also organized by the Astoria Park Alliance.

Anyway, congratulations to those that helped clean up Astoria Park, and I also want to recognize and thank the leaders of the APA who worked to make this cleanup event become a reality.
