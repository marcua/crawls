If you have a 4th floor Apt, like I do- I would shy away from a $99 Target job. the bike will weigh about 45+ pounds and that in itself is a workout hucking it up and down the stairs. If you have access to a basement or storage space, it will make it simpler- and easy on the back. But a 45 lb bike is a 45 lb bike and will be a beast climbing any hills- like 20th ave between 31st and the park in each direction. If you are fairly serious about getting around by bike, spend the extra money on a lightweight bike- used or otherwise. you WILL appreciate the lack of bulk.

It was a big difference when I went from my generic 10 speed 45lb steel frame tanker to the RT300 Aluminum 24 pounder.
Purchased at Astoria bikes/Tony's about 12 years ago, BTW.

For your purposes, sounds like the $80 big box special will be fine. Just don't lock it outside w/anything besides a U-Lock or Kryptonite quad chain. Cable locks are about as reliable as silly string.

I have three mtbs and a fixed gear track bike (no brakes). No worries about "accidentally" purchasing a fixie  :-D (and no, I'm not a hippie poseur, I adore the fixie for all the right reasons: stealthy quiet, pure beauty, super responsive, lively, lightweight, low maintenance, theft deterrent, cheap.)

If you find that bicycling becomes a passion, you'll eventually crave something lighter, sturdier, nimble and versatile. I started out with a rigid steel mtb; a decade later, splurged on a hardtail (front suspension); not long after that, went nuts and got the full-suspension so I could ride crazier trails while keeping up with the guys. But I'm obsessed with mountain biking :lol: and each of my bikes has a purpose:

Rigid: Now a street/commuter bike w/slick tires, back rack, and theft-resistance. Can be locked up.
Hardtail: Burly, low-maintenance trail bike that feels amazing riding in the city. Can take on potholes, curbs, steel plates, etc. Also a great bike to travel with and training on the road.
Full-suspension: For long-distance mtb races (35-50 miles), riding upstate where the trails are much harder, keeping up with the faster guys/gals!
Track fixed: Got it for the velodrome, then got bored w/the track and started riding it around the park....then around town....then to work.....then to Belmont......then to Ft. Totten........makes the most mundane rides terrifying  :-o
