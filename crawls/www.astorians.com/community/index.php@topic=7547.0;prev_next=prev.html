Maybe we can help you....Can you describe the space where your sofa would hang out?

Do you want it to plop down for TV, would it be paired with other sofa/chair seating? Do you like sectionals (would a sectional fit?) How much do  you want to spend? Do you care whether this is something you'll carry with you into your next space? Are you finnicky in terms of style, do you often change your mind about the kind of style you like? etc... Do you want sleek and sexy or lots of pillows to fall into, formal or casual? Color or monotone? Wood/metal? What else is in  your space? Maybe you can load up a picture of your living room space.

We've just moved into our space, so I'm in "interior designer" mode, shopping for a sofa myself and will second Room and Board and Crate and Barrel for quality and price.

newell, definitely measure your doorway, esp. the width - the movers will probably stand the sofa on end and bring it through your doorway either upright or tilted.  We have a 7' long sofa and moved it to our 3rd floor walkup with the help of a friend.  The tight turns in the stairwells were the toughest - we had to open the window at one point to get an inch or so clearance.  Also, the back and arms of our sofa curve outward, so that also added to the total size.  We got our sofa on sale from Crate and Barrel and have been very happy with it.

I don't know what kind of colors you like, but based on your living room description, have you thought about beige/white or an orange-y red color?  Either would really pop against wood and blue.

We also have a hallway, narrowish, when you walk through our apartment. I think CB mentioned a 45" measure for the hallway if they needed to angle the sofa in. The height of the sofa, from feet to heighest point-back of sofa or arms, is what you'll need to look at. I think most doors are a standard 35"?. You might have to remove the door in order to get more inches if your front door opens onto a wall and won't really give you the 35" consistently. I'd check with the particular store, they should be able to help you determine the minimums for gettings things through your place. I second the orangy/red or beige sofa idea-sounds like you can really go either way-something very traditional with the rest of your stuff or something more square and mod-I guess it depends on your taste, n'est pas?
