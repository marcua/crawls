OK, that was a long cocktail hour - too bad, because I didn't have any alcohol.  Anyway, here's the scoop on the hotel:

My younger brother travels a lot on business, and since he owns his company and can do his work from anywhere, when he travels on business he always takes side trips for pleasure.  He uses one credit card for all his purchases and the card is tied to reward points with United Airlines and Starwood hotels, so he accumulates a lot of points.  The more you travel the more likely you'll run into problems, such arriving at a hotel to find out it's still under construction, or the pool isn't in working order, the hotel restaurants are closed, or the hotel didn't tell you a major sporting event would be taking place in town and the hotel is overrun with whooping sports fans.  The airline might bump you from a flight.  When this happens and he speaks to management about the problem, they frequently credit back the points he spent, or most of the points, or sometimes more than the points he spent.  Since his kids travel so much, their idea of a good time involves spending a night in a hotel.  Some time ago the family spent a night at the Waldorf Astoria, partially on points, as a celebratory splurge.  It was a nice suite, with a living room, but more of a regular type Waldorf room/suite.  When they left they were given a zero balance receipt, showing that their bill was paid in full, but later my brother saw that his credit card had been billed for video games and movies.  He called the hotel and said those charges were not his, the family had not used those services.  As part of their apology the Waldorf said that on their next stay they would be upgraded to a full suite.  For his 15th birthday my older nephew said he wanted to stay at the Waldorf.  When my brother called to make the reservation (using points) he reminded them of the upgrade and asked for a two bedroom suite.  The person taking the reservation looked over the situation and said the hotel could upgrade them even further to a three bedroom suite so my brother called me up and I packed my bag!  As I described, we hadn't expected an APARTMENT.  Since my brother and I from New York, and his wife and kids have been here often, none of us felt compelled to go out and do touristy things - we were all quite content to stay in our living room, our dining room, our bedrooms.  We were on the 41st floor, and on Saturday during the day and at night had fantastic views of the city (each suite in the Towers has a view of all four directions) and on Sunday when it rained we were quite happy to stay where we were and just enjoy the ROOMS.  The complimentary breakfast is served buffet style in the Astoria room on the 26th floor.  It's strange because the "waitresses" are wearing hotel business suits.  There was salmon, hardboiled eggs, hams, cheese, blueberries, pineapple, croissants, muffins, bagels, granola, milk, coffee, teas, fresh orange juice, and more.  Sometimes it's abundance that gives a feeling of luxury.
