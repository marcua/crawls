woo hoo! so this is the first  totally random thread huh? i actually wasn't sure if this would fall under Off Topic or Random Notes since i think this does kinda pertain to Astoria.

Well anyway, i lurves me some ink! I have been going to the NY Int Tattoo Con every year for the past 8 or so years, and have only allowed myself to get tattoos that one time a year. (i went alittle overboard with silly tats when i turned 18 for awhile and needed to regulate myself). But anyway-lately i've been feeling like branching out and building on my half sleeve. Yeah there are a ton of places in Manhattan, but i thought it would be easier and cooler to establish a relationship with an artist here, that way i could stop in every once in awhile with ideas and we could bounce stuff off each other and build my pieces that way.

Did a little research and found 3 local shops, Fat Cat (2810 23rd Ave.), Painless Steel (2315 Steinway) and an unamed one on Ditmars and 38th next door to the sushi place Kumo. Haven't been to any. Do you know of any others? Has anyone here been? What were your experiences? Can i see your tats for reference? Who's your fav artists and why? CLEANLINESS?? (yes, that one is uber important) and any other info you'd have i would appreciate.

Weird, i didn't realize there wasn't a topic on this anywhere on the boards!

edited to say that when i started this post, there really was nothing else in here!

Eric and I had our most recent inking done by a woman on Ditmars, but I don't think her shop is as far as 38th... I think its on the block between 36th and 37th. Its a non-descript little storefront with a blue awning, I think. Next to a bodega. We got them a few months ago. I would never even have seen this particular parlor if it wasn't for the fact that Eric knew of this woman from a past parlor he was loyal to... My personal opinion was this:

well-priced (not expensive, but not so cheap that it makes you wonder what the hell is going on)
clean & sterile (new needles, an autoclave that she actually KNEW how to use)
no wait, no appt needed
specialization in religious ephemera

Although I prefer to support female artists, this one was a bit surly in my opinion. She is really no-games about stuff. You're in and you're out. No small talk. I am accustomed to dealing with artists (in LA) who will work with you a bit more to develop the image and get a feel for your personality... but honestly, that's only important if you've got some art you need input on. If you've got a simple flash or easy iconic design (like we did), she's great.

I'm curious about Fat Cat and the other place!
