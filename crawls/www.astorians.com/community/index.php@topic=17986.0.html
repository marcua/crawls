Jeremiah Frei-Pearson Campaign Kickoff on Monday, April 26 6pm
« on: April 20, 2010, 10:53:09 AM »
I got an email yesterday that Jeremiah Frei-Pearson will formally announce his candidacy for State Assembly at a free concert in Athens Square Park on Monday, April 26 at 6pm.

It looks like it should be fun, informative, and for a good cause as well.


Read Jeremiah's note below:

Next Monday, April 26, I will formally announce my candidacy for the State Assembly at a free concert in Athens Square Park that begins at 6:00. The dysfunction in Albany has gone on for too long.  It's led to a massive budget crisis, serious cuts to education and financial strain for our small businesses.  Instead of working for our families and our children, the legislature cuts funding for schools, healthcare and jobs.  There are serious challenges that we face and we need new leadership and a new direction in Albany. 
 
For my entire life, I have been standing up for children and families and I am running for Assembly because you deserve a leader that will fight for your family and our community.  I hope that you'll join me on April 26th to have your voice heard and take back Albany!
 
The concert will feature Broadway Stars and local performers.  Performers include:  Karla Mosley Frei-Pearson of Guiding Light and Hi-5 (my favorite!), Brent Carter of Hot Feet, Erica Dorfler of Avenue Q, Thom Warren of the Lion King, and many more!  We'll have free refreshments and lots of fun and games for the kids as well.  And, because change must start now, we'll be holding a canned food drive to help the more than 80,000 children in Queens who live in poverty. 

I'm running for State Assembly to bring honest leadership to Albany and deal with the crushing effect that the budget crisis is having on middle class families.  Athens Square Park, with its statues celebrating Greek history, symbolizes Astoria's heritage and diversity and has been a gathering point for our neighborhood.  I would be honored if you stood with me on the 26th as we work to reclaim our government and give voice to our entire community.

Got a message about a change of venue due to rain.

Due to rain, we are moving the concert and my announcement in-doors to Sissy McGinty's at 25-67 Steinway Street (between 25th and 28th Avenue). As before, people of all ages are welcome.
 
I will formally announce my candidacy for the State Assembly today because it's time to fix the dysfunction in Albany.  This dysfunction led to a massive budget crisis, serious cuts to education and financial strain for our small businesses.  Instead of working for our families and our children, politicians ignored the budget crisis, did nothing while hospitals closed, and played petty political games while subway lines were cut and our children are forced to go to increasingly overcrowded schools.  We deserve much better.  Together, we will do better.  I would be deeply honored if you stood with me today, as we kick off the campaign.
 
As before, the concert will feature Broadway Stars and local performers.  Performers include:  Karla Mosley Frei-Pearson of "Guiding Light" and "Hi-5", Brent Carter of "Hot Feet" and lead singer of Tower of Power, Erica Dorfler of "Avenue Q", Marva Hicks of "Caroline or Change" and backup singer for Michael Jackson, Stevie Wonder and Lena Horne, N'Kenge Pacurar of "Sondheim On Sondheim," Thom Warren of the "Lion King", and many more!

Because change must start now, we'll be holding a canned food drive to help the more than 80,000 children in Queens who live in poverty. 

I'm running for State Assembly to bring honest leadership to Albany and deal with the crushing effect that the budget crisis is having on middle class families.  I hope you stand with me today as we work to reclaim our government and give voice to our entire community.
 
I am deeply thankful for your suport.
