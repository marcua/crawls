I'm not an Applebee's fan... I don't really like their menu (no vegetarian-friendly choices), and agree that other chains do it better. I do, however, like Uno's right across the street. I like Panera, right next door. My gf loves Chili's (and makes me trek to Long Island so she can get their black bean burger).

If someone likes the food at Applebees, why condemn them for going there or any other chain restaurant? Chain restaurants are accountable to the home office to provide a certain degree of reliability, quality of service, quality of food, and level of cleanliness. Typically they're consistent -- you can go into any Uno's and know what's on the menu, how it will taste, and what it will cost. Chains bring more jobs into the neighborhood, boost the economy, and, with the support of their home offices, chains can withstand a bad month or two in profits without having to lay off their staff and shutter up. Chain restaurants also contribute to their neighborhoods -- I haven't been to this Applebee's, but others have walls dedicated to the local schools, and local sports teams.

I've had some very bad, very expensive meals, at local Astoria establishments... should I really have to continue eating at those places just because they're not owned by a large corporation?
