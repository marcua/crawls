I thought the cat talk was funny, but also got the impression that no tip was left.  Also, at first it sounded as if you were charged for bread you ordered but didn't get. 

If the waiter asked what kind of bread the person wanted it does seem to imply that the bread is included, but if this was an Indian restaurant, then bread often isn't included.  It's like when you go to McDonalds and they ask if you want apple pie with that or when you tell the White Castle cashier you want the #whatever and they ask if you want cheese on that.  It sounds like it's included in the price, but it's not.  I think it would have been better to tell the waiter that you didn't know the bread was not included.  He might or might not have had the power to remove the charge from the bill, and even if the manager/owner isn't around the waiter might have phoned him/her.  Intelligent management will take off the charge and explain that in the future you'll know that it's not included.  Better to lose a tiny amount of money that a whole customer!

When I go to an Indian restaurant and order bread I always choose naan because it takes more effort to make.  You can do chapattis with your eyes closed, and poori is simply chapattis that have been cooked in oil.  As for the price of bread in an Indian restaurant, it's long been a rip-off!
