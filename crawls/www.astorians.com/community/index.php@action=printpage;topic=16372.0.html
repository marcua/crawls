Title: Re: Astoria Featured on Today's AM New York Newspaper (Astoria: A Gem in Queens)
Post by: John b on October 17, 2009, 03:35:58 PM
People accuse me of being nostalgic about the Astoria of ten or fifteen years ago. But I submit that anyone who thinks this is still a real neighborhood, a place where sane (that is to say, uncool and unhip) people would want to raise their kids, is indeed nostalgic for a neighborhood that no longer exists. Thanks to overdevelopment and the unfortunate trendy labels that have been attached  to it by twentysomethings looking for a cheaper place to crash and dorm, what was once a vibrant urban village of families and small businesses has become a college town without the college: You'll notice that there's no bookstore in sight. There's nothing hip and cool about garbage, graffiti, cracked sidewalks, traffic scofflaws and a general atmosphere of filth, slop and noise, unless you're here to live out your urban fantasies, that is. All this became evident when I invited some of my Manhattan friends to dinner one night, and, as we were walking through the shantytown of 23rd Avenue, one asked: "Hey, is this neighborhood safe?"
