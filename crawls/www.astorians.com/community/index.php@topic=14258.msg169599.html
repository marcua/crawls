Sorry, I have to disagree with all of you.

Sac's pizza on Broadway is by far the best pizza in town.  One of the few places in the neighborhood that has a working brick-oven hearth and makes thier own sauce.  Definately the best.

Polito's pizza on broadway is also very good.  Not "artisan" pizza like Sac's, but more of the "classic" New York slice that most people remember.  Great stuff.

Rizzo's is unique.  I'm not a tremendous fan of thier pizza, but they are such a part of Astoria they can't go without mention in any "Astoria pizza" topic.  You have to respect thier longevity and fanbase.

I am not a big fan of Sac's.  I find the sauce to be lacking in flavor and the cheese is too thick and chewy despite it being "fresh".  I also think that they need to clean out their oven a bit.  While I enjoy the taste of a cool oven crust, I do not want to scrape off black charred bits from the bottom.  It makes the whole pie taste like burnt crust.  I don't consider this artisan pizza in any way.

Had a couple slices Bleecker Street pizza a few weeks ago. Sampled the Nonna Maria and a regular slice. I liked it. A lot. Especially the regular. The crust was out of this world- thin, crunchy, chewy. It wasn't oily at all, like when you first have to blot the surface with a napkin before eating. I hate that. The sauce was spicy just enough, the cheese was actually full of flavor-probably the parmesan they use. My boyfriend got a third slice-pepperoni. The entire slice was totally covered in pepperoni. A pepperoni lover's dream come true. Next time we head downtown by car we might just have to pick up a pie and bring it on home!

http://www.bleeckerstreetpizza.com/images/menu-w1.htm

Is there any place in Astoria comparable to Bleecker's?
