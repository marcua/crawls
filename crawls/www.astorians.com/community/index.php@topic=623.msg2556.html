You sir!  Cake or death?  Yeah, all this sucks, no doubt.  Of course, what the heck does one do about it?  I took a look at the aclu's patriot act primer to refresh my memory on the evil things this bill causes ...

http://action.aclu.org/reformthepatriotact/primer.html

I figure I can't do much about #1.  Suggestions besides "sell your worldly  posessions and become an ascetic priest" are welcome.

For #2, I offer 2 ideas.   a) drop the credit cards if you think you are under government scrutiny.  use cash.  b) Start a community wide library system.  Some people in Richmond were setting up a library is their commune-ish house, and it seemed like a good idea ... you can meet people, AND read Harry Potter 6 for free!

Suggestions are welcome for #3

For #4, I can make a few observations and suggestions.  Use gpg to encrypt your email, and set up a trust ring with the people you email the most.  Honestly, I don't do this myself yet, but i also have not been able to find anyone willing to join me.

Use freezepeach to do your browsing of the aclu website.  :)

Consider using Tor: http://tor.eff.org/ for your home machine(s).  Tor is a protocol/system which facilitates anonymity while sending data over the web.

This is nutty, but may someday be necessary: create a community level local network ... everyone is connected together via linked wireless networks, and one responsible residence has a huge pipe to the real isp.

Even more nutty, entirely possible, but less legal ... big community network, connected wirelessly, with Tor set up internally.  Each participant in the network has a cable modem connection to the internet.  With some routing fun (which at this point is beyond the scope of my knowledge), everyone in the network would be able to anonymously share each other's cable modem connection to the internet.   If you request a web site, your encrypted data may leave the network via several different cable modems ....

But all this requires effort.  I'm not convinced that everyone (me included) is ready to pull out all the stops, and dissapear from the feds.

OK, i'm done.

Congress Re-convenes.

In the intervening months since this topic was addressed, I've had alot of time to think. Although I am a firm believer in the institution of laws to counteract terrorism and am also a realistic believer in security practices (in general) and a healthy awarness of one's surroundings, I have become even more convinced through re-reading this 132 page + tract that this particular law is inimical to human rights and our Constitution. I believe it to be in fact... illegal.

However, (personally speaking) I have also lost the initial fire of emotion that I had first felt when the law was re-proposed. Lawyers from many different backgrounds have assured me that even if the law is passed in all it's all-encompassing Glory that there is yet still hope, and that it is not yet the end of civil liberties as we know them. The Act can always be modified or even overturned... and it is up to each Judge to determine in each case how prosecution is to be applied, if in fact it is to be applied at all. I still fail to see however how the War in Iraq has turned into the War against our own Citizens, but nevermind.

What puzzles me the most is that this law has gained approval on the grounds that since it has been instituted we have not yet had another terrorist attack. Well, this is like saying (and let me wax whimsical here) that since a law was passed against earthquakes we haven't had any earthquakes, therefore the Act against Earthquakes has been successful.
While the rest of us cower in fear and let our bodies and homes be searched (an open book to the government) we are led to believe that the fact of an earthquake has become prevented by our Decree.

Let me direct your attention, my fellow human beings to Beirut in Lebanon, or Palestine and Israel or Northern Ireland "in" England, or Athens in Greece. Or Cyprus. When did any law cause someone who was resourceful enough, determined enough and willing to die for a cause to desist?
Is it proper Logic to offer the Death Penalty to those who would attempt a Suicide bombing?

And isn't the idea of American Freedom the very institution that we are trying to protect? In what world does the giving up of freedom bring freedom? In what universe does the initiation of war bring about peace? In what Dimension does the snuffing out of light mitigate darkness?

These are philosophical questions that can best be posed when we are alone at home with our thoughts in front of the fireplace or when we are in prison awaiting torture or crucifixtion. These questions would have no place in a society that was under a Theocracy or (God forbid) a Leftist dictator's Regime :twisted:... too even think such thoughts would be most grievous treason <hysterical emphasis>. For now, I think such thoughts openly.
For now.
