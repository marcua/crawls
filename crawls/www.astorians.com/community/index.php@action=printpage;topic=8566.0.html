The true Bush believers are the neocons, who are decidedly a minority in the conservative movement.  Theirs was the dominating influence in this administration.  But it's clear that this has not been a successful administration, and I predict that neocon influence will wane.  True movement conservatives don't have much use for the neocons, other than for the fact that conservatives rarely air their dirty laundry and criticize each other in public. 

The evangelicals are rightfully having second thoughts about the neocons.  They have been poor stewards of the conservative mantle, and have damaged the good name of the movement.  But evangelicals certainly are not going to vote towards the left.  If anything, they are more likely to vote for a third party candidate far to the right of whoever the Republicans put up.  So it's a miscue to say that they are having second thoughts about right wing politics. 

Some of Rudy's schtick is far too liberal for the religious conservatives.  There are "social justice" Christians who vote to the left, and support "progressive" causes, but they are not the same as the conservative evangelicals, who I don't see coming together for Clinton, Obama, etc.  As repugnant as I personally find Robertson, I think that the endorsement is a huge coup for Rudy.  I am not sure what Robertson's angle is, unless he just thinks RWG is the most electable.
