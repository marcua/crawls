The class I went to on Monday night was a bit crowded. I think there were 18 people, maybe 20. But there was enough room for everyone.

I'm not that great at yoga either. I'm very fit and normally do pilates 3x/week, so I have good core strength. But yoga poses don't come easily for me since my muscles are often tight and I'm not flexible.

That said, I didn't find it too hard to keep up. And the instructor was very emphatic about telling us to listen to the body, give it what it needs. "If you need to come out of a pose, just take a stretch or go into child's pose," was something she said several times. Also, she tried doing some advanced variations on some things, but immediately said not to try them if you weren't up for it or didn't feel comfortable doing it.

If you're up for it, I'd give it a try. They do have a beginners class which I think is on Thursday in the afternoon  - check their schedule. But if you show up early, just speak to the instructor and let them know you're a beginner. I found the atmosphere very friendly and welcoming.

And I plan to go back tomorrow night and give it another go.

Finally, if you find you don't care for it, you've only spent $5.

Good luck.

I stopped into Yoga Agora last night, and it was kind of a mixed bag (for me at least). I'm definitely new to the Yoga thing, but I'm enjoying it so far. Prior to my class at Yoga Agora I had taken two beginner classes at the Yoga Room to get a feel for whether or not I'd even like yoga. 
So I got to the studio last night, and it's literally a one room operation, a few cubbies for your baggage and a couple small changing rooms. By the time the class started I think there were about 25 people ready to practice.  The class itself was, for me, somewhat fast paced, and I didn't really get a chance to concentrate on my form.  I think that, from a beginner's standpoint (and I am probably the epitome of a beginner) I appreciate a little more guidance, but with 25 people in a class, that gets understandably difficult.
All that said, I got through the whole class without too much trouble, the instructor wasn't one to really push you to do anything that you couldn't or weren't comfortable doing.  All in all, for a $5 class, I can see myself going back once or twice a week, because it's really easy for me to stop in after work. If it's your first time trying yoga, I would definitely recommend trying a beginner's class (Agora offers one during the day) before jumping into Yoga Agora's $5 classes.
