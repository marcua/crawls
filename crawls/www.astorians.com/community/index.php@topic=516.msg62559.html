Welcome from another Shampoo-Banana refugee! Who will you be translating for? A friend of mine is an interpreter for the UN...

There are lots and lots of other foodies on this board...

As for movies, you'll find that it's very easy to get down to the Village where most of the foreign/indie films are. There are half a dozen theaters that show nothing but. (This said, cool as the Tribeca film festival is, I do miss Ebertfest...)

It's possible to bike to Manhattan, certainly, but it's not exactly pleasant. Traffic is extremely heavy, and NYC has not yet figured out how to make bike commuting very easy, especially from Queens. (There are some wonderful bike paths along the Hudson.) Do a google search for "transportation alternatives", though, and you can find some activism in that area.

I think you'll be very impressed by the green grocers in Astoria. Wide variety, usually very good quality, and pretty inexpensive. For organic/natural foods, the Union Square greenmarket (40 minutes away on the N train) is 4 days a week, and is one of the better greenmarkets in the country. And you should definitely consider one of the two Astoria CSAs...

Hope your move goes well!
