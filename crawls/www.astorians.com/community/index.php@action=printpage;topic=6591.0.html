Count me in!   I'm a native New Englander who has accepted everything in New York City except for the sports teams.   I've retained my affiliation to the Boston teams, with the BoSox nearest and dearest to my heart.

I go way back too.   My father took me to my first game at Fenway in 1966 and throughout my youth we would make the trek to Fenway several times each summer.    I suffered through the '78 collapse while in college and can still remember sitting in my frat house when Bucky Dent hit the ball over the wall off Mike Torrez.   Then there was '86 with Buckner letting the ball slip through his legs.   I viewed that one in San Francisco while on a business trip.   And of course there was '03 when dumbo Grady Little left Pedro in way too long.   I was at the New York Film Festival and had my walkman and periodically would turn on the radio to get updated scores.   I remember watching the end of the game at that bar diagonally across the street from Carnegie Hall.   It is an Irish pub.   I remember Boone's homer. 

Finally came '04 and the great comeback against the Yankees.    The World Series was an anti-climax.   How could you top the BoSox-Yankees?

The Yankees finally awoke from their '07 slumber and have now won 7 in a row, while shaving about 4 games or so off the lead.   It is now 9 1/2 I think.   As someone who suffered through '78 I remain paranoid.   The Yankees are never out of it.
