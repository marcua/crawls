CPAs do taxes. Financial Planners do budgeting and investment analysis. Those who do both are considered to have a conflict of interest.

Last year I decided to seek out the advice of an independent Accountant. I have been using the same firm for 25+ years. I wanted a review of my tax situation now that I am older and my tax preparation has become greatly simplified. I did telephone interviews to determine whether I would be better off paying a flat fee or paying by the hour. A flat fee was in my best interest. There would have been no way to get out all my questions, and subsequent answers in 30 minutes, but I found that many older independent accountants are more than willing to do it for nothing if they feel that they might be lucky enough to geet you as a client.

I discovered that the firm I had been using was doing an excellent job for me, and will continue to use them at a negotiated flat rate until I croak.

I can tell you one thing that I did discover. Those computer tax programs do *not* ask all the right questions for everyone. I thought that now that my filings are simplified, one of those programs might suit me fine. My CPA showed me the difference between TurboTax, TaxCut and one other whose name I can't remember, and what he produced. Quite a savings. I was truly impressed.
