Re: Spiced on Ditmars to be new place called Cozy Koala
« Reply #27 on: December 24, 2012, 03:33:11 PM »
braun, isn't brunch just a combination of breakfast food and lunch food? Did you look at the brunch menu to see what they offer? Maybe there are things that could be eaten for your lunch meal.

I ate here last night. I was out of town since Friday and didn't realize yesterday was actually their opening day. There were some hiccups in service, but since it was their opening day I won't bother going into detail about that. I will say that the fish tacos I got were delicious. They were impossible to eat as tacos - they were piled high on soft tortillas and had to be eaten with knife and fork - but the quality and flavor were very good, and I was happy to be eating them and would eat them again.

There were a couple other items we ordered that should probably be described more clearly on the menu to avoid unhappy customers in ways that can be prevented. The panini are served on thick, very crunchy bread; not what I think of when I think of panini. I was imagining something more along the lines of what Il Bambino serves, or most places in New York that serve panini, being a grilled flatbread sandwich. Also, what is described as "apple pie" on the menu didn't resemble pie at all. It was cooked/seasoned apples and grapes and cream on a slice of thick bread, possibly the same bread used for the panini.

Neither of those items was bad, but if they'd been more clearly described we would've ordered different things, as I was there with a rather picky eater. It was the sort of thing where we thought we knew what we were getting based on the menu description (they seemed straightforward enough) but when the items arrived we were surprised by what we got, as it was not in line with our expectations. I've been to a lot of restaurants and am not used to that happening.
