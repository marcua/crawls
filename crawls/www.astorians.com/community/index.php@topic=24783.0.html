Here is a link providing more info  http://weheartastoria.com/2013/04/off-the-hook-oyster-bar-in-astoria-is-now-open/
We stopped in last Friday night and were really glad we did.  The place is on 28th Avenue.  Around the corner from Favela and diagonal from Rapture Lounge - more or less.  It's still very young.  Liquor license is expected any day.  The place is really cute.  Pretty decent size and done up nicely with a nautical theme without being overdone.  Very clean with a fully visible kitchen up front.  Very nice owner and staff.  We were given a free sample of a few items just for coming in and looking around.  If that was to tempt us, it worked because we sat and ordered two pots of steamers.  One was little necks steamed with Pernod and some cream.  The other was Manilla clams steamed in Belgian Ale and vegetables.  Both were really delicious.  The clams were clean and very fresh.  Steamed perfectly.  And the flavors were really impressive.  Another curious visitor was offered a plate of raw oysters to sample.  There is no doubt we will be visiting this place fairly regularly.  Admitedly, more so when there are adult beverages to go with their incredible shellfish.   But for now, they are serving some really tasty herbal iced teas made fresh.  I also look forward to trying their bigger seafood entrees.  We know already that there is a lot of talent in that kitchen so I look forward to trying more menu items.

We went here the other night.  We were the only ones at first, so the server (who may have been the owner) was almost comically solicitous.  The appetizer of bay scallops with pancetta, cream, bread crumbs, and two small pieces of toast was very good; the scallops were sweet and the sauce wasn't too rich.  It was a little steep at $12, though.  The lobster roll is probably smaller than those served at places in the city (e.g., Pearl Oyster Bar), but it's also cheaper. The meat was tender and the sandwich was suitably rich; the cole slaw was lightly dusted with Old Bay.  The one disappointment was the crab cake sandwich special.  Not only did it barely taste of crab, there were chickpea pieces embedded in the patty.  I did like the salad that came on top, though, and both sandwiches were served with freshly made potato chips, still warm and ever-so-slightly oily (but in a good way).

They keep running 20% off coupons in BORO magazine that can be used anytime.  They also have $1 clam Tuesdays and all-you-can-eat mussels for $15 on Wednesday.  On Sundays, if you buy 2 dozen oysters you get another dozen for free.

We finally tried this place.  I recommend it.


Details:  I love the look of it and it smells really good - like a coastal shrimp shack, a little bit of fried seasoning, or I don't know what, but I liked the smell. 


We had hangar steak and a scallop risotto special, along with raw oysters to start (malpeque, blue point, and something from the west coast).  The oysters were a little disappointing but I can't explain why - we didn't get sick, they just weren't as fabulous as we would have liked.  The steak was great and came out with a ridiculous (and appreciated!) amount of asparagus.  The scallop risotto thing was tasty, if a little skimpy on the scallops (only 4 smallish).


The service was ... meh.  It took a long while for the waitress to clear the oyster tray and plates, and while she was pleasant she just wasn't that focused. 


I am definitely going back.  There's nothing like it in the neighborhood and I saw many items on the menu for future exploration.
