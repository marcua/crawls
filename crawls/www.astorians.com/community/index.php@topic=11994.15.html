« Reply #26 on: August 29, 2008, 12:29:38 AM »
The hipsters I know personally who've lived in Astoria have all moved to Brooklyn. I'm serious. In my observation, hipsters are attracted to Astoria as a decent, moderately-priced place to live but then find it doesn't support the hipster lifestyle. Traveling to the lower east side or Brooklyn just to hit a bar/music scene they're interested in is a drag. The rents aren't low enough to wait for suitable commercial establishments to follow them here as opposed to moving to Williamsburg or Greenpoint, where the scene already exists. Thus concludes essen's theory on why there are hipsters in Astoria, but they don't stay for long and there isn't an intense concentration of them.
