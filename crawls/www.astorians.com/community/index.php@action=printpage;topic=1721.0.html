There's a really good meditation class at Grace Lutheran Church on 21st Ave @32nd St on Sundays at 12 noon.  Its based on the Kadampa school of Buddhism, the most accessible to most Westerners, and led by a former Wall Street trader who's very funny.  Normally we go in, sit for a while (1/2 hour or so) to clear our heads, then the leader speaks for a while on that topic, and then we sit again for 1/2 hour or so.  I know he's got another class at Grace Lutheran on Mondays at 7pm, but I haven't been to that one, and I believe its more of a classy-class with books and all.  Hope that helps.

Title: Re: Meditation and/or Massage?
Post by: schmennifer on March 10, 2006, 05:47:06 PM
I usually do tip around 15%, but then your question prompted me to go to Google and find that tipping is not really expected by massage therapists. This (http://frappydoo.com/forum/showthread.php?threadid=2622) is a good online discussion that you might want to read.

After seeing that, I think I'll continue tipping Maria at SPAce, since I've already established that practice. But with anyone else, I'll take into account whether or not they're already receiving the full fee and whether I'm there for relaxation or therapy. It seems that some therapists feel just a little insulted if you tip, whereas no one feels insulted if you don't.
