Nice, frugal and summery, served at room temp. very satisfying.

salad:
--6 medium redskin potatoes, boiled and cooled, then sliced
--about 2 cups of green beans, dropped into boiling water until just done and then transferred immediately to a bowl of ice water
--1 can, drained or about 2 cups of cannelini beans - optional, i didn't do this but I think it would be good
--about 1/2 cup of grape tomatoes, halved
--1 can of tuna, drained (and rinsed, if you like)
--a handful of thinly sliced red onion

use the potato slices as a not too precise base and casually pile the other ingredients on top.

dressing (I didn't measure at the time but I think this is pretty close. maybe a little less mustard, maybe a little more dill)
--juice of one lemon
--some red wine vinegar. probably about a tablespoon
--1 garlic clove, minced
--1 tablespoon? minced shallot
--1 teaspoon dijon mustard
--one teaspoon minced dill
--maybe a teaspooon of capers
--salt and pepper to taste

I let the dressing sit for about 30 minutes, then dressed the salad and let it sit for 15 minutes, then served on lettuce. It was so, so good. I think it should be easy to adapt - leave out the tuna, add some olives, etc.

I use a whole chicken whenever I want to save some cash.

For Two:
Meal #1 Roasted chicken w/ roasted baby potatoes. Just rub the bird and potatoes with a mixture of olive oil, juice of one lemon, salt and pepper. I add rosemary (fresh) if I have it and put the lemon halves inside the chicken as it roasts. We both eat the thigh/leg quarters.

Lunch:
Slice off some of the breast to use in a salad. If it was a big bird, I can get enough for two lunches.

Meal #2
Pick all the meat off the bird and shred it. Grab a skillet and heat it up on the stove with some bbq sauce. Grab two buns from the deli and you have bbq pulled chicken sandwiches.

Take the carcass and make a stock, though I usually skip this step.

Whole Foods has them on sale a lot, so you can get good quality ones too!
