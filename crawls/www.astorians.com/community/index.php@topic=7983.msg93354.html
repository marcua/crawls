I am not a mom, but I live right by the park (name is escaping me) on the corner of 31st street and Astoria Blvd., just down the stairs from the N/W Astoria Blvd Station. This park has two playgrounds. One for younger kids (I've seen toddlers on the slides and such) and one for slightly older kids. There are separate swings as well. There is also handball courts and basketball courts for teens, adults, but I've never seen any problems there whatsoever and I walk through there everyday at all different times. There is also a water dragon in the summer! It may be worth checking out.

The best time of day to be at any park without a lot of big kids is during school - morning till about 2ish.  After that, you're going to have bigger kids who need to blow off some steam.  Of course, there might be some larger or rowdier (sometimes my 3 year-old can get crazy at the playground) preschooler at any time of day.....

For toddlers, I recommend the playground at Queensview at 34th ave and crescent.  It is "private"- it belongs to those who live there and is maintained by their groundskeepers, but many people go there from around that neighborhood.  I don't live in Queensview, but I use that playground all the time.  There are lots of "just walking" kids at all times of the day, nice, friendly parents.....

I really like the TriBorough Bridge park-- and not just because it's next door to me. ;)

It's the only park I've seen that had a *really* toddler friendly slide. It's only 2 feet off the ground, and the stairs leading up to it are nice and shallow. Plus plenty of baby swings (6 to Astoria Parks' 4).

The basketball courts are nearby, but the older kids have always been pretty friendly (even letting my toddler play with their basketballs sometimes)... 10 am, the parks are pretty quiet, in my experience.
