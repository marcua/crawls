I wondered about the title, and now it makes me smile.

"Throughout the film, Grant's reflections on his marriage are woven ... , and influence his eventual decisions regarding Fiona's happiness."
Curious now about the short story that it is based on. I wonder if it was difficult adapting a short to a feature length. The first half works far better to me, and towards the end it seems a bit more forced and abrupt. It was filmed in rural snowscape country of Canada and has a leisurely melancholy and perhaps serene  pace. It is far better than what I usually see, a poetic film.

I also thought of living cases of Alzheimers that agree with some story events.

I had worried that a film involving Alzheimers would just be too depressing. But there is so much excellence and love in the talent and story, that it was satisfying.
