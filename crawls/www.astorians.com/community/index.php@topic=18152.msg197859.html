Not quite.  You are conflating the tools available with the actual use or misuse of those tools.  My problem is with how the various agencies involved in defending the coast against the oil spill seem to be working at cross-purposes-when they are working at all. 

No legislation or bullet points about inadequate oil company response plans will change the fact that we are failing to use all the available resources we do have to reduce the damage done to coastal ecosystems.  The problems you point out are chronic.  The one I am concerned with right now is acute.

I am sorry if I confused you.

P.S. A widely reported example is the USCG standing down skimming barges to do safety inspections (i.e. life jackets, etc).  I don't just assume this is the Coast Guard's fault, since professional mariners are supposed to know and follow the regulations.  The problem is that, whatever combination of actors is responsible, this sort of failure in elementary Disaster Response 101 really pisses me off.
