Re: mounting a lcd flatscreen ?
« Reply #19 on: September 06, 2008, 08:24:04 PM »
All you have to do is get yourself a stud finder, locate the metal studs, get some toggle bolts with washers, affix the lcd mount to the wall with the toggle bolts, mount the tv and you're done. It will hold. If you're not sure you'll trust a few toggle bolts, then mount a piece of 1/2" plywood, then the tv mount on top of that and then the lcd. Trust me, it'll hold. I've done it hundreds of times.

Thanks for the input guys , i finally got it all done last night . i used 4 -5inch toggle bolts  and  4 wall anchor screws into the drywall said to hold 50lbs each(which they prob dont ,but im sure they will help). guess the thing that got me nervous was the total weight of the tv with the bracket which is 89lbs plus around 25lbs equaling 114lbs going into metal studs . i was lucky enough that i was able to get behind the wall i was mounting on and remove a center piece of sheetrock . that way i made sure the toggles went right thru the center of the stud. i also was able to do all my wiring thru that opening as i also installed a home theater in a box system 5.1 . my xbox 360 sounds amazing as well . since i was able to get in back of the mounting wall , which is actually my boiler room wall , i had 2 sets of metal studs parallel  next to each other to work with .  one stud held the outer sheetrock and the other the inner sheet rock which was the inside of the boiler room .  i got the 5 inch toggler so that i would go thru both sets .   that made me feel alot more secure/
