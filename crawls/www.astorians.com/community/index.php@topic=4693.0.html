Rent is $400 monthly, deposit is $375, so to move in is $775 altogether (two seperate checks, one to management office -- the other to current roommate moving out)

I have a small room available in Long Island City off the Broadway line on 9th Street between 34th and 35th Avenue.  The apartment is 100 feet away from the last stop on the Q104, so getting to both the 7 and the N/W train is extremely easy.  The dimension of the room is on the smaller size 12'x8', but can fit a twin/full bed, a dresser, and bookself.  There is a lot of storage (walk-in closet and storage compartments overhead) in the room.  There are two large windows, the coolest room in the summer, warmest room in the winter.  You're on the other side of the apartment so you won't have to interact with my boyfriend and I if you don't want to.  WE HAVE A CAT.  I've had roommates that have locked him in the bathroom before and cannot possible go through that again, so you HAVE to be CAT FRIENDLY.  He loves affection, is declawed, and well-trained.  He won't go into your room what-so-ever.

Cool places around our neighborhood:  Laundromat across the street, Trade Fair (5 minute walk on 36th Avenue), Socrates Sculpture Park and Costco down the street, Noguchi Museum down the street, Bars all up and down Broadway.  Steinway is a 10 minute bus ride on the Q104.  Roosevelt Island and the F train is 6 blocks away (Roosevelt Island stop).  Broadway N/W stop is 12 blocks away, but there is a bus at the corner of 31st and Broadway that takes you directly to 9th Street and 34th Avenue (4 minutes).

This area is safe.  A lot of Hispanic families as well as some artsy people.  I'm a photographer and poet, boyfriend is also a photographer.  Cat is a cat.  The entire building is being exterminated this month for everything you can imagine, so you don't have to worry about pests. 

My boyfriend and I are both students.  However, I work full-time in Midtown (very early) and my boyfriend is a full-time Sociology major at Hunter College.  We're friendly, love our privacy, do not like having our food eaten, and like to stick to the cardinal rules of a roommate.  We're not anal about being clean, but we don't like having food/dishes in the sink overnight, a mess in the bathroom.  We tend to keep the common areas well kept.

If you're interested, please message me here or email me at sen.ogulnik@gmail.com
