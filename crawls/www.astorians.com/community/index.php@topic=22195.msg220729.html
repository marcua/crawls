Kathleen, if the other residents of the block are just as annoyed as you are perhaps you can get together and do something about it? There's strength in numbers: one of you can cut the lock, ring the selfish idiot's doorbell and hand it to him while someone else drives a bulldozer-that everyone chipped in for-and tears the damn gate down. Then have a block party and celebrate.
No seriously-you and your neighbors need to raise holy hell. You got nothing to lose. Make lots of phone calls, take pictures. You might try calling the FDNY: 718-999-2000, and they'll connect you to Field Public Communications Unit. They might be able to send an inspector out and possibly issue a violation. After all, what if someone's garage, or unfortunate car parked in the middle of the alley blocking the way catches fire, or there is a medical or other emergency which requires access to and from the street?  Also call the DOB-Customer Service 718-286-0781 tell them your problem and they might connect you to Construction. That'll most likely be the supervisor of Construction who might or might not tell you to call 311. If you have to call 311 (you should do it anyway in addition to the other calls) give them the address of the person who put up the gate. They'll probably ask if a permit was issued for the construction of this gate. Say no. There's no way a permit would ever be issued for such a thing unless they lied about where they were going to put it-and that it's blocking the other residents' from getting through-at this moment, right now. Call Vallone's office, call Community Board 1. Be a pain in the ass and don't give up!
Of course it would've been less of a hassle to stop someone from putting up a gate in the alley before or during construction, but hopefully the person who paid to put up this gate will soon have to pay to take it down. Good luck and let us know how it all turns out.

Check it out:         https://www.facebook.com/blox.drvway.1

If you live across a private establishment with high client traffic or city community center or daycare and the parents and/or employees of the establishment have blocked your driveway:
 
 1. Take plates make and color of vehicle
 
 2. File a complaint through http://www.nyc.gov/html/mail/html/mayor.html][url]http://www.nyc.gov/html/mail/html/mayor.html[/url] and give them dates and times those vehicles block. This office sends a notice to NYCHA and trickles down to the city community center/daycare. They end up having a parent meeting over it (but be prepared to file annually- new kids attending new parents dropping off.)
 
 3. Even if it is just a regular person or neighbor i would still file a complaint with the mayor's email. The more you file the more a pattern is formed and the more folks like you and I have a chance of changing and having the law enforced.
 
 4. Write to dmv in albany about the law "Parking or standing is not allowed: In front of a driveway." It is not sufficient and needs to be more detailed like: Parking or standing is not allowed: In front of a driveway and needs to be 5 feet away from the driveway on either side.
 
 If a hydrant can have a safety distance why can't a blocked driveway. There are some folks that park right up to the curb cut of a driveway and the vehicle owner can't open their car door without having to block traffic to do so in order to open their gate.
 
 5. Have the number of a good towing company that tows within 5 minutes usually. Call them as the officer is writing the ticket.
 
 6. Place a private reflective: No Parking in Driveway Violators will be towed at owner's expense sign.
 
 7. Request from DOT a No Parking anytime sign be placed on either side of a driveway. Forewarned they deny the first request. So you refile stressing the problem and your experiences and take pictures of you driveway and either side of it and of the infraction violators. Once the request is approved check on its placement. One person checked and DOT records showed that the sign was placed when it wasn't. The individual had to file with DOT and 311 a complaint and make a request for a replacement. Even when the sign was to be placed the city employee was unsure about placing it and was not going to.
 
 a. either sweet talk or calmly explain the problem and why the sign is being placed to convince the employee or
 b. call DOT where you requested the sign and report the individual immediately or for afterhours 311 and the next day call DOT. Take his name or description, date, time, plate of vehicle s/he's driving file it with the complaint.
 
 8. Don't bother talking to folks about them blocking your driveway. They disrespect you by blocking, they might end up cussing you out as I've seen. They will respect a ticket and tow more than you talking to them. You do not owe them anything. They owe you for blocking your driveway in the first place!
 
 Now a days no one knows the definition of respect. If you block a driveway you are disrespecting the owner then don't expect respect... expect a ticket and possibly a tow.
 
 Get the number to your local precinct for nighttime service because through 311 the cops take longer in certain areas and boroughs. Get the number of traffic in your borough because they can come quicker in the daytime.
 
 If the cops give you problems, and some will, then file a complaint with all: the mayor's office email internal affairs and the civilian complaint board and 311.
 
 And you can always put them to shame by messaging me or by placing the info personally here! I do not give out my sources. I just list the vehicle info of the person who causes the infractions.
