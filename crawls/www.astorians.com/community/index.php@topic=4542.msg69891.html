I'm sure I mentioned it somewhere on the board, but in case you missed it let me explain. Frankie has a very rare form of nailbed cancer. It has been diagnosed as either Onychomadesis or Symmetrical lupoid onychodystrophy. The only way to make a definitive diagnosis is by tissue and bone examination, and that involves amputation. I elicited the help of the teaching and research staff at The College of Veterinary Medicine at Cornell University when Frankie was just 8 months old. They saw him twice and have been working with my vet each step of the disease. They suspect a genetic factor, but the breeder has *not* been cooperative. She is a champion breeder and this would ruin her reputation and the lines she has developed. We tried to research his letter-mates, but without her cooperation we got nowhere. If just one of his siblings was diagnosed with the same thing, the AKC would stop his breeder's breeding program and therefore stop her from registering any more Bichons. This would cause havoc in the dog world. Cornell and my vet seem to agree that if it *was* present in any of the breeder's other dogs, they have already been put down because you cannot breed an unhealthy animal and you cannot show a dog in this condition.

I plan on fighting to keep him alive as long as he wants it.

Today he entered a new phase of the disease. He grew 2 nails from one toe. One of the nails grew in sideways. It needed to be surgically removed. On the way to the vet, he sloughed another nail on the same paw. I pulled over near Shea Stadium, grabbed a roll of paper towels, and held the paw while I tied a tourniquet further up the paw to stop the bleeding. When he sloughs a nail, it exposes the raw nerve, bleeds like hell and hurts a lot. I wasn't there but for 30-45 seconds, when a cop pulls up along side of me and tells me I am not allowed to stand there. The driver gets out of the car, walks around to the passenger side, where the window was open, and bends down to see what I am doing. I guess they thought I was shooting-up drugs or something. Frankie, bleeding like a stuck pig, pulls away from me and lunges at the cop. I didn't get a ticket, and after asking one of them to hold the bloodied paper towels, I politely refused their offer of an escort to the vet. I called the vet and they were waiting for me outside the office when I pulled up and they took Frankie straight into surgery.

He is resting now. He ate a hard boiled and and some yogurt, drank at least 6 ounces of cold water, took his vitamin and pain meds and he'll probably sleep through the night. As soon as he wakes up, I'll change the bandages. G-d bless him, he is so used to the routine that he just lays there and let's me do what I have to.

Frieda sometimes gets jealous, so I give her a vitamin too, and I let my little tubby girl have some hard boiled egg whites.

Kids are so much easier. They cry you feed them, change a diaper and put them down for another nap. Even as teens, they are easier. You bribe them, threaten them, punish them and they get the idea.

In my next lifetime, the only damn thing I am going to raise will be . . . cactus. Water them twice a year and that's it.
