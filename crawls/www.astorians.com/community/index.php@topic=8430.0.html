The best way to make vanilla sugar is putting *used* vanilla beans in the sugar. (Otherwise too strong.) You'll have the vanilla bean bubbling away w/ the cream when making your ice cream and should scrape out all the sticking bean center you can into the cream. There is plenty left you can't get at, which is why it's perfectly okay to still use. Just rinse the bean well, let it dry out overnight and stick it in your jar of sugar. I've had one in my sugar for almost a year and it's still making deliciously flavored sugar. Every time I use a vanilla bean I stick another bean in there... But as you say, vanilla beans are expensive, and you should pay for them. You want to make sure the beans you are buying are top of the line and most importantly, not dried out. I don't know any place around Astoria that has them, but Whole Foods carries them for $10-14. Also, if you get one that is dried out, soak it in bourbon for a bit.
