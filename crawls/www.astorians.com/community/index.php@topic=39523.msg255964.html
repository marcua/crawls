We're on 32nd off 20th Rd and have one who hangs out in our backyard a lot - I'm pretty sure it lives next door. It is completely unfazed by human presence, and goes about its business even 2-3 feet away from me. I've seen it year-round, and in the winter watched it gather a huge pile of leaves with its prehensile tail, then carry them over a 6 foot fence, presumably to insulate its nest. I love it - I find it marvelous that we have a specimen of the only native North American marsupial living in our backyard!

Keep in mind:  the Opossum/Possum are harmless and rather be left alone and not confront us humans.  They will NOT attack.  If they happen to "show" teeth or hiss at you, it's only because they are FRIGHTENED.  Same goes for playing "dead" hoping you'll leave it alone.  Their lifespan is 1-2 years, sadly because of dogs,cats and us humans!!!


They've been called, "Natures Little Sanitation Engineers,"  their diets includes mice, crickets, bugs (they love snails) to name a few (oh yeah and any type of pet food left outdoors and overripe/rotting fruits).



I've had them pass by in my yard as well as raccoons (that's another story).  Okay let me get back to work .....

It's funny cuz when I moved to NYC 15 years ago I never would have thought there'd be any wild animals here.  When I lived in Harlem, I had a ground floor apartment in like a 'rowhouse', and my kitchen door led out to a small cement patio with 8' high cinderblock walls.  I used to let my cats out there, knowing they couldn't ever escape or scale those walls.

So one night I had the door open and assumed my cats were still out there (when in fact they were inside somewhere...) and as I peered out the door to call my cats in, I saw this huge set of eyes along the top of the patio wall.  I thought 'wow, that's a huge cat!' (there was a colony of feral cats in the other yards surrounding mine....)...then as I looked some more, I realized I was seeing black rings around the animals' eyes, and it was THEN I realized 'omg, it's a RACCOON!!".  My first time ever seeing a raccoon.

I never would have thought that I'd have to move from Boston to NYC to start seeing wildlife.  lol

Yes, I think I've seen those green parrots as well ...over by me on 21st Ave and Steinway.

I love birds and feed them on my balcony...know many of them just by their calls...and I remember one day hearing an uncommon call...then I was like 'I know that call...but it's not a typical one around here....'...then I looked out for the birds and saw something bright green a few houses away on the roof!

On my Astoria balcony, I have no problem getting blue jays.  Boy are those little guys smart.  All I have to do is open my squeaky balcony door (I swear they recognize the sound now...), I put out a few peanuts IN THE SHELL (their fave food) and within ten or so minutes, they swoop in, grab a peanut in the shell, fly to a nearby tree, hold the shell against a branch with their claws, and peck away to get the peanuts inside.

I also get house finches and dark-eyed junco, but for some reason no other birds, not even cardinals or mourning dove, seem to know of my feeders on my balcony.  (and btw, I do know the different foods they like...it worked when I had a yard...but not on my current balcony.  Maybe certain birds are simply less likely to fly onto a building balcony?)

When I lived in Glendale/Ridgewood in Queens, and had an entire yard with bird feeders, I got mockingbirds/catbirds (can never differentiate between the two), woodpecker, white-breasted nuthatch, cardinals, mourning dove and dark-eyed junco.

When I lived in Harlem, I actually got Tufted Titmice, a really beautiful bird...but sadly, I haven't seen any Tufted Titmice in a good ten years or so.
