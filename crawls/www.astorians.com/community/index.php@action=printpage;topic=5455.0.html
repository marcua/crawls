Post by: wasabisam on March 08, 2007, 11:58:26 AM
I'll agree with tuesday as well, however that's really the risk you take by going out to eat, or for that matter, buying anything that has been processed in a factory. We really do put a lot of trust in total strangers, whether it is at a restaurant, grocery store, or factory, for what we put into our bodies.

Most restaurants don't want to shut down. It is very very costly to open, own, and operate a restaurant, so it is in their best interest to keep violations down as much as possible. I think that many restaurants are in a constant struggle with the rat infestation, and they are bound to have evidence of that no matter what because this is a city with very old or poorly made buildings. They also run risks by who they hire and many restaurants likely have high turnover for jobs that focus on cleaning (dishwasher, mopping, etc., etc.). So there is always a challenge to keep the balance and make sure that ultimately the consumer is happy and/or the inspectors happy.

Sadly some of my favorite restaurants have also received terrible scores, but to be honest, I'll likely go back to them. The food is good, the staff are pleasant and accomodating, and the atmosphere is nice. If I see something that has me worried as a patron, then I might be less likely to return, but so far in the years that I've frequented these restaurants I haven't had any real negative experience...that I know of. :roll:
