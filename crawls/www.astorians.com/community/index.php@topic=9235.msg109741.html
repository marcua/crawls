Quote from: 28Grand on January 22, 2008, 11:06:16 AM
Why is it that Trader Joe’s elicits cheers of almost orgasmic intensity but mention Applebees and the sound of wrecthing is deafening. Is it because one caters to the rich and the other doesn’t?

They're totally different stores. For one thing, Trader Joe's is actually inexpensive for what they sell, as far as I saw the one time I looked around the one in Union Square. Plenty of rich and non-rich people like Trader Joe's because it is a place that offers good products, and some fairly unique products, for a reasonable price. As opposed to Applebee's, which offers food that is mediocre at best and isn't even all that cheap. And yes, I went to Applebee's several times during my poor college years with my poor college friends, so I am speaking from experience. We saw it as a place to go when we felt like splurging on something more than dining hall food, and we were often disappointed. Denny's, on the other hand, was never disappointing! Neither was Arby's, Pizza Hut, or McDonald's. Neither was I disappointed when the burned out Ditmars McDonald's finally re-opened a few months back. Now I can get delicious McDonald's fries and their strange little burger if I want them, and occasionally I do.

Also, people have often complained about not having any nice/big supermarkets in the area aside from Best Yet at 19th Avenue or Stop & Shop somewhere on Northern Blvd. I wouldn't necessarily want a Trader Joe's in Astoria, but that would be why a lot of the same people who scoff at Applebee's think it would be great to have a Trader Joe's. Sort of off the main topic here, but I don't get why one would think that just because you hate one particular chain, you must hate all chains of all sorts.

Raleigh is a classic example of the type of store that used to define Broadway and Steinway Street.

Store after store is being turned over to clubs, 99 cent stores, banks, nail salons. Although their are still quality places, like the community in general, they are declining.

You might talk about gentrification in Astoria, but until the retail areas reflect this, its little more than a myth.

Poorer immigrant families, kids, and members of the Greek community (that increasingly do not live here) define the store space in our shopping 'mecca' (and yes, I disagree with some of you - upper Steinway would be nice if it offered more choices than hookah place after hookah place.)

Every attempt to improve the area, falls flat. Look at the new lightposts put up. The bases are open, the columns covered with LaBomba fat reduction posters, and no seems to care.

How about those overflowing garbage cans? And the swirling papers at Steinway and Broadway? Makes for some great studies of wind tunnels - and how to feed rats.

We all see this. The lack of interest in solving these problems speaks volumes about the people that run this community.

The only things that the city seems to be efficient is handing out parking tickets and building more and more that just adds to traffic and congestion.
