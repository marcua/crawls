STRANGER DANGER
« on: March 13, 2009, 11:29:04 AM »
Hi Everyone, I just wanted to make sure woman in the neighbourhood is aware of what happened to me yesterday. A guy followed me home to my doorstep.  It might have been his first attempt but I'm worried that he might do it again to someone else and get right what he thought he could do to me.

This is what happened:-

I got to the top of the  stairs at our house when I realised some crazy guy had followed me and 
was standing right behind me. I turned around thinking how did I not  know he was there. At first I thought he was maybe a delivery guy or dropping ads or something, I turned and faced him and waited for him to explain himself but he stayed quiet and then he moved closer to me and raised his arms as if to hug me and he was in my personal space, too close for a stranger to get, so I shoved him and shouted at him. He  remained quiet, blank stare, totally freaky. I was at the door and yanked it open trying to form the words fast enough telling my husband to see what this guy was wanting cause he was acting very strange. My husband took a couple seconds before he understood what was happening but by that time the guy was moving backwards and everytime I looked in his direction he had moved further away from me and when I looked again he was running away down the street. My husband asked me a couple questions and then went after him but just a bit too late, he was gone. My husband found 2 policemen and brought them back with him and I gave a run down of what happened and the guys description. They said they could do nothing except keep a look out for him but still he didn't really do anything. I was pretty shaken up afterwards. Still can't believe it happened.

So bottom line, he is in Astoria, Ditmars Boulevard area and he followed me home and he got away.  He is late teens, early 20s, +-5.5 ft tall, pale complexion, skinny, well dressed, was wearing a backpack and his jacket was open at the time even though it was cold, he wasn't wearing any bright colors that I can remember and was very quiet when he approached me from behind.

Be careful ladies... PLEASE! I thought it was someone I normally saw on the street walking behind me when I took the turn onto my street and didn't realise that someone else was actually walking behind me from who knows when and had started walking quieter behind me when I got to the walkway of my house. The other person that I knew didn't make the turn on my street, he must have continued walking somewhere else. My mind was on other things too, and I didn't think anything of the footsteps behind me.  Oh my god, I can't even imagine what could have happened if I was living alone.

That's scary that he followed you up to your door. Also scarier that you didn't even notice until you got home.

 I do my own kind of (gender) profiling, when a man-- no matter what he looks like-- is walking too close to me, I pay attention. If a man walks toward me, I pay attention. If some men are behind me, I walk alongside the parked cars on the road and every one in awhile glance in the windows to see my reflection and to see who's behind me and how close they are. When I get off the train at night (especially if I have my briefcase and work laptop which I am forced to carry) I peek around to see who's getting off as well. I walk alongside a woman or a group if possible.

Anyone could be a possible predator. There is no "profile" except for one-- MALE. The guy who followed you was well-dressed; it doesn't have to be a dirty-looking man in baggy jeans--it could be anyone.

 People can call me paranoid, whatever, but I have been victimized before.
