Before you judge, you should at least go and see what is happening for yourself. I, along with a large number of people participating, am neither white, nor rich. I attended public schools all my life, and then attended a public college on a bit of scholarship. I am not a hippie (although I have no bias against anyone for being a hippie) and I am pro-capitalist. I have the good fortune to have a job, but I know for many people who do not, and cannot find work, amongst them, my own father, who was laid off a few years before retirement by a company he'd worked for about 25 years. The corporation moved the jobs overseas to earn more, putting a number of Americans out of work. There is a diverse population of people who are involved who are all colors, ages, genders, sexual orientations, all political beliefs, the working and the unemployed, but there are certain ideas that unite us all. For example, most people there want an end to corporations being given the same rights as a human being. We are also for corporations being held accountable for their actions, and corporate support of our politicians to be regulated, if allowed at all. For example: why did our government bail out private banks that created the housing bubble and subsequent financial crisis? Why did they allow BP to spray U.S. coastal waters with toxic chemicals that are banned in their home country of the UK? Why are factory farms making meat allowed to resell meat infected with e. coli back to us to eat? Sometimes the best place to start change is by articulating your opinions, to engage your fellow Americans in a dialogue about how we need to fix the issues in this country.


Are there "hippies" there? Yes, there are, but you can't deny that there are more types of people than that, just come down and check it out for yourself, talk to people, instead of dismissing an entire movement because of some edited images you saw on the news. How are they supposed to "get a job" if there are no jobs to be had? Our public schools are a shame in most places. How is anyone who is a product of a broken public schooling system supposed to complete for limited jobs? I met a young woman who was just finishing her first college semester, but it was unfortunately going to be her last, her father had been laid off and could no longer afford to help her with school.


On the other side of the spectrum, here are some interviews with people who are not inarticulate hippies:


http://www.democracynow.org/2011/9/30/inside_occupy_wall_st_a_tour



http://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm


Please read the Bureau of Labor Statistics report above also. We have bailed out the banks on our tax money, but they have not created jobs in return. To express dissent to unjust and unfair practices is to love our county, because we see the potential for positive growth that will benefit more Americans.
