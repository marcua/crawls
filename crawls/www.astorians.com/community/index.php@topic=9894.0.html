Honestly, and some might disagree, I think it would be a mistake to get the cat used to going outdoors. I've lived with indoor cats my whole life, and in my experience, the less you let them outside, the less they are interested in going outside. My two most recent cats were even scared of going outside since we never familiarized them with it. The cat we had when I was a kid would be allowed outside at times on a harness or in the car from when she was a kitten, and she managed to escape from the house on several occasions, which was never fun.

Since your cat is still very young, he will get accustomed to whatever surroundings you keep him in. And especially since you have a small apartment, he might be even unhappier to stay there much of the time the more he sees the outdoors. It might seem cruel to do this, but cats are generally pretty easy going. Get him some toys and play around with him to keep him active indoors. Give him a nice cardboard box or paper bag to sit in, as cats love these things. It's too bad you don't have any good windows for him to look out - maybe some day you will?

I do appreciate what you're saying, but I'm not going to have any direct sunlight anytime soon (I work in the arts).  He's got plenty of toys and play furniture, but I'm more concerned with him spending the entire time in artificial light, which I think is a little unhealthy for any living creature.

I grew up in the country with outdoor cats, and while I don't recommend it now even in the country because of the dangers (we only did because of allergies in the family, and they had much shorter lifespans than indoor cats) they're generally pretty territorial.  This one in particular doesn't really like to get more than ten feet away from me on a regular basis - he'll run out the door into the hall, but then he stops and waits.  He's also got some experience with the mean streets since he was found as a baby stray - he was rescued because he was more interested in making a beeline for the nearest human and befriending them.   :roll: 

Don't get me wrong, I'm not taking any unnecessary risks here, but I'm more concerned about having a living creature that never gets direct sunlight than I am about the chance that he would try to run away.  My windows are always closed and locked, and when he does get out the front door, he's still got to get through two more closed doors before he would be outside.  We're also going to our destination in the carrier before getting out on the harness - 'walking' the cat on the sidewalk is not happening (mostly because I think the traffic would freak him out, though he's fine when he's confined to the carrier).

So while I understand and do appreciate the concern, I think I've weighed the risks in this situation.
