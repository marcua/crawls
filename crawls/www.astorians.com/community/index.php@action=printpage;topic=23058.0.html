Title: Re: Nissan Maxima tires stolen - 36th street (between 21st and 20th Ave)
Post by: J Roscoe on June 25, 2012, 03:23:54 PM
some of the tricks to getting around wheel locks are to spot-weld a bolt head onto the lock so that a normal socket will work to get the bolt out, or to hammer a slightly smaller socket over top of the lock and use a breaker bar to work it loose.

spot welding can be easily and quickly done with the right equipment, but it's very conspicuous if seen by someone, as is the noise made by having to hammer a socket over the lock.  wheel locks + parking in a well lit and/or populated area might help deter thieves from taking the extra time/work and move on to an easier target, but it's no guarantee either.  if they really want your wheels, they will get them.

Title: Re: Nissan Maxima tires stolen - 36th street (between 21st and 20th Ave)
Post by: J Roscoe on June 26, 2012, 01:56:36 PM
not sure i understand your question.  wheel locks come with a key.  that key is used to either remove the lock and expose the standard bolt, or to insert into the "lock" portion of the bolt which creates a standard bolt head that can be removed with a typical socket.

if you have the key, then when you get a flat, use it to properly remove the bolts and change your tire.  if you're at a shop, then give the key to the mechanic so that they can do the work.

now, if you loose the key, then you'll probably end up using a method similar to what I posted previously.
