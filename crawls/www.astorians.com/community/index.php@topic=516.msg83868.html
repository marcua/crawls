Hello all; I'm new.  I actually created my first post in the 'where you're from' thread, but I think it is also pretty applicable here, so I'll just go ahead and add an expanded version to this thread.  SO....

I graduated from school in the summer of '06 (Washington State Univ.), and which point I moved from Seattle to Hoboken with my college girlfriend who had already lined up a cush job for herself.  Essentially, I tagged along in the name of "love."  In record time, however, I managed to break up with the girlfriend, and had to decide whether to stay in NY or go back to Seattle.  By then I had landed a good job in the city, which made the decision a bit more difficult.  Suffice to say, I decided to keep the job and stay, even though I knew relatively no-one in NY beyond my coworkers.  Upon searching Craigslist for a good 2 months, I eventually found a reasonably priced studio in Astoria, NY.  Hadn't heard much about the place, but the rent was right and the commute is a beautiful thing, so to Astoria I came.

In my short stint in Astoria I'd have to say there are definitely more positives than negatives.  Much is undiscovered as of yet.  I'm in the Ditmars area right by the Beer Garden.

Also, my ancestors are Greek, so my last name apparently affords me some instant street-cred around the 'hood.  I know nothing of Greek culture, however, but that's hardly common knowledge.

See ya around the forum, all.

Quote from: jasonsr on July 21, 2007, 10:14:28 PM
Is there a way into Brooklyn other than taking the Metro into Manhattan, then out again?


If you're coming from Ditmars, the best way to Pacific Street, which I believe is close to downtown Brooklyn, is actually the N train as it goes directly into Brooklyn after Canal Street, skipping the downtown stops.  If you're going to Williamsburg/Greenpoint and don't mind transfers, you can do the following: Take the N to Queensborough Plaza and catch the 7 going to the city across the platform.  Take this train one stop to 45th Road and follow the signs for the free transfer at 23rd/Ely which is below ground to the G.  I have attached the link to the NYC subway map.

http://www.mta.info/nyct/maps/submap.htm
