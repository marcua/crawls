Post by: TJT on March 09, 2007, 03:16:24 PM
The house seems to be privately owned and only occupied by one family.  And it is probably the finest of the homes in the area.

Unfortunately Astoria Village does not seem to be incorporated or organized by any oversight committee with monthly fees and bylaws - such as with Forest Hills Gardens.  As a result, while some of the older homes were beautiful in their hayday, they are pretty shabby today.  I am not one to defend something just because it's old.  There is a new multifamily dwelling a few doors up from the Tara mansion (same side - going towards the Greek church on the corner) that I think is a beautiful example of a new construction.  It's brick and multi level with beautiful large windows.  While the new brick construction on 14th is very generic and not beautiful.  So I think if the developer is more creative, something new can be a lot nicer than a totally run down old house.  But if the village were incorporated and had rules about upkeep, paint jobs, modifications, etc., then all the houses would be beautifully maintained.  In this village, they are not.  And I DO realize that this area was not a planned/semi-gated community like Forest Hills.  But still, if preservation was a concern, something of a local home owners association could have been formed by like minded neighbors to protect the status and appearance of the village.

Post by: Pawgny on March 09, 2007, 09:09:14 PM
The mansions on 12th street were all connected to each other (at one time) by underground tunnels.  The tunnels were accessed through subbasements.  The tunnels then led to a main tunnel which led to the river (where Shore Towers is now).  The "Tunnels" where always considered to be a myth, but I've been in them when I was a child & I use to access them through a friends house.  People would say the tunnels were part of the underground railroad, but that wasn't the intention on its design.  My Grandmother told me that they were used by the servants of the mansions.  The servants would go through these tunnels to get to the river & get goods (Dried/salted) from the ship(s).  They would then take their bounty up through the tunnels w/o being seen by the wealthy above.

That's my Story & I'm sticking with it

If u notice in front of the white house the 2 large stepping bricks, when they got out of the carriages, used them. All of that area from 12th street down to 1st street were all beautiful homes that were none down. You will never find these homes again, big  rooms. Well have to getback to work for now.
