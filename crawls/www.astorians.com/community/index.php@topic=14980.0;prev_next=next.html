I had dinner there. Well, first of all, the place looks really cool; nevertheless, my kudos must stop there.
Cafe Ole, features Spaniard food yet nobody is from Spain. I noticed this when I had a couple of questions about the "jamon serrano" which tasted like prosciutto (the Italian version). But Nobody was from the region of Catalan or Spain for that matter.

Second, they have no chef!
For the prices they charge (very expensive) nobody's to blame for---

Third, I noticed 18 kind of proteins in their menu. How can they keep all that protein fresh?

Overall, it seems these folks have tried hard, but not hard enough to keep fresh food instead of doing the classic move of diners: the bigger, the frozen the more money!

I ordered the paella, it took really long, my partner in crime didn't like it at all...
The wine was ok but nothing out of the ordinary.

My advice is ---they need a happy hour...
They must learn from the best of the best:
Tratoria Il Incontro
Il Bambino
Pollos Mario (only the bandeja Paisa)
BareBurger (new.. but kick ass)
The taco truck in Broadway
The Kebab at 30 ave (the best ever in this world)

There I said it all...

I tried this place for the 1st time and I must say the food was awesome!!

 Not sure why the fact that no one was from Spain or "the region of Catalan" makes a difference in the food. Paella takes a long time to cook btw

I had a bunch of tapas and some wine. The wine tasted like it had been open for over a day, but I think that's expected at most restaurants unless you get your own bottle. They try really hard with service, a bit much at times I think, but kudos to them for trying so hard.

The prices were not the cheapest, but I don't know of any other Spanish places that are cheaper. Btwn my wife and myself, 1 glass wine, 4 apps and 2 soups. Came out to $60, not bad imo. They also offer a lunch special for $13 which includes wine and a full course meal, I'll certainly be back.
