I am a very lazy cook and only ever cook with extra virgin olive oil.  Yes, there is a science to it (e.g. one kind of olive oil for salads, another kind for cooking), but even if you ignore it everything turns out just fine.  I just use it in the place of other oil when it's needed for sauteeing, etc. Basically just add as needed - you don't want to add so much that it's dripping, but you want just enough to give the food a little bit of moisture.

To answer your specific questions:
I've found that brand doesn't really matter all that much, but you could try googling olive oil brands and see what the die-hards say.
In hot pasta, I don't usually add it, but if I did, I would add just a tiny bit to the water before putting the pasta in.
In cold pasta, just add a little bit as needed.  When food is cold, you don't need very much.
With veggies, any way you like it!  Saute with olive oil, or throw some olive oil into a salad.  Experiment and play with it.  Have fun.

Right, extra virgin olive oil starts to smoke at a lower temperature than light olive oil. So if you try to use XV for sauteeing, you'll get an unpleasant smokey flavor. Also, light olive oil is a lot cheaper!

There's a pretty big difference among the different types of XV olive oils, at least if you're using them straight on bread or a salad. For a tasty treat, infusing olive oil with fresh herbs is super easy...

-Put some extra virgin olive oil in a small pan, and add a handful of fresh basil (or whatever herbs you like)
-Heat on the stove on low for a few minutes, basically to gently let the herbs "steep"
-Fish out the leaves, let the oil cool, and use it for dipping bread (I like ciabatta)

I've also found that the basil oil is fantastic over tomatoes & fresh mozzarella.
