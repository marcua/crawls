We just came from dinner at Wave.

Ahhhh,  what a pleasant experience.  They were nicely fully but not pack.  Dim yet well lit and warm too.

Ordered the spanish white wine and after a taste (too sweet) it was replaced with a spanish red that was very simple and nice. Was not to overpowering for my seafood dish.

Jens dish was "healthy ginger with beef" (a wok dish) a fine dish but average and underwhelming.

I ordered and Jen helped me enjoy my dish of "mixed seafood street noodles"  The smell of dark soy and fermented black beans mixed with garlic and smokey mussels wafted up right away from this pleasing pile of steaming food.  Big plump mussels, nice sized shrimp, curled and flavorful squid plus some fish really made the dish interesting and well rounded.  We got real quiet and started in.  It all came together so simply. Your mouth filled with the smell, taste and care of it.  Our waitress comented on how fast we ate.  I looked around and saw that the two women served before us were barely half way through and still yaking. What came to mind is that they didnt deserve the meal.  If you want to talk away go to a cafe and have coffee. 

I say this place is a winner and a well deserved addition to our neighborhood.
