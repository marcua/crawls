I think 3% is pretty reasonable. It doesn't hurt, however to ask for a lower rate. Worst case scenario: they say no, and you get your rent increased as expected.

Given the current economy, I don't think it's unreasonable for anyone to save money where they can. It's not only fair, it's the law:

For a one-year renewal lease commencing on or after October 1, 2009 and on or before September 30, 2010: 3%
For a two-year renewal lease commencing on or after October 1, 2009 and on or before September 30, 2010: 6%

Note, these increases are for apartments where the landlord provides heat and hot water.

www.housingnyc.com/html/guidelines/orders/order41a.html

You can try to negotiate a smaller or no increase with the landlord but the guidelines do allow for 3% on a one-year lease.


See this link for Fact Sheet #1: Rent Stabilization and Rent Control: www.housingnyc.com/html/resources/dhcr/DHCR1.html

From the fact sheet: "Rent stabilization generally covers buildings built after 1947 and before 1974, and apartments removed from rent control. It also covers buildings that receive J-51 and 421-a tax benefits."

In general, apartments in buildings with 6 or more units are usually covered by rent stabilization guidelines, which limit how much a landlord can raise the rent at the end of a lease period.
