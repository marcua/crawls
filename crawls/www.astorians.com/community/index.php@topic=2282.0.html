I love NYC Transit
« on: June 02, 2006, 08:52:45 PM »
NOT!!  So I left work at 6pm today, and am just now getting home at 8:15pm.  I walked from the not running NW at 49th to 42nd to the 7, waited 2 trains to fit into the 7, sweated bullets, pushed my small 5'1 and half inch frame out of the train at Queens Plaza, only to be told the N wasn't running there either.  It was a mob scene.  We were all herded out like cattle to the street, the police weren't letting people even in Queens Plaza, only out.  No cabs in sight, buses??  I start the long walk home in the pouring rain to 27St and 20th Ave, proceeding to call every friend possible who has a car.  I finally reach a friend who proceeds to inform me he just got home on the now running N train!!  I get back on at 36th Avenue having walked from Queens Plaza, sweat some more, and after another half hour finally arrive home.

Now why is it this happens every f-in time it rains in this city??  The tracks flood and the transit system fails.  We have a drainage problem, nyc transit knows this, yet our money doesn't ever go to fix the underlying problem.  Hello politicians??  Hello Bloomberg??  Does anyone care about the lowly commuter??  So we just have to suffer every time it rains and our antiquated drainage system can't handle it.  My usual 20min commute home took 2 hours and 15 minutes.  Lovely.    :-P :-(

I apologize for the outburst.  That will be all for the evening.

At a little after 5pm, I took the W from 34th Street, thinking that I was lucky that our train wasn't on The Problem List on the e-mail that I got at the office. Of course, they waited until the train got to Lex to tell us that it wasn't going any further. They did say that the train I was on would head back south and that we were to take the 7 if we wanted to go to Queens. So I sat there for about half an hour (maybe longer) waiting for the damn train to leave the station. Of course, it never happened! After everyone packed in, getting ready to head to 42nd, they announced that that train was now out of service. No N or W trains were running south, so I took the 6 to Grand Central, hoping to catch the 7 from there. When I got there, the 7 was no longer running. Or at least not stopping at Grand Central. So I went back to Lex where the N and W were now up and running. Slowly. And only for a little while, apparently, since my husband now informs me that he's trying to take a bus to get home.

I miss Tokyo. I miss train schedules. I miss knowing that, if I missed a train, another would be by in three minutes. I miss people who know how to deal with adverse weather conditions. And I miss train cars that don't stink. F*ck the MTA.

Last time this happened (transit problems), me and two other Astorians had to hijack a cab in Queensboro Plaza, where the trains stopped.

By "hijack," I mean the cabs refused to go to Queens, even though they were IN Queens, all in a hurry back to NYC. It's illegal for them to refuse on that basis, as if that makes a difference.

Anyhoo. A gypsy cab offered to drive us for $15 *each.* We weren't that desperate.

Finally got a cab to stop, and the guy going to Broadway jumped in and said, "I'm going to Broadway." The driver thought he meant Manhattan until me and the other guy pounced in, asking to go to Astoria Blvd. The cabbie started to protest and we were like, "GO!! JUST GO!!!!" Including tip, we did this for $5 each! Small victory. Took 1-1/2 hrs total commute home.

Hence, my love for bike commuting. I don't ride on rainy days (and lucked out only when our basement office began flooding and we had to, uh, bail ASAP. N train wasn't too bad before 5pm.) But I'd like to start braving the elements just so I can avoid the crazy delays when the spun-sugar train tracks melt in the drizzle.

Trains to Queens out Friday evening
« Reply #10 on: June 02, 2006, 11:03:30 PM »
There were no trains running from Manhattan to Queens this evening. I left my job in midtown at 6pm and found all trains in every direction, one big mess. The 4,5,6,7, F,V,B,E,W,N were all either having major delays or not running at all. I heard lots of reasons, but none of them from train officials. Nobody would tell the passengers anything, this includes conductors, engineers, dispatchers, transit police, and token booth clerks. I heard various rumours from other passengers, such as sick passenger, switch problems, signal problems, fire in the tunnel, flooded tracks, power outages, stalled train, terrorist attack, impromptu strike, and my favorite: there's a big party somewhere and they forgot to invite us.   :-D

To top it off there were no buses running into Queens from the other side of the 59th Street Bridge. I waited over 1 hour for the Q101, and got fed up and ended up walking home over the bridgein the rain with other pissed-off Queens residents. My poor dress shoes didn't even last one month this time  :-(

According to NY1, the main reason was the heavy rains we had late today. So why couldn't one single person announce there are massive delays and the recommend we find a different way home? I got home just before 10pm tonight. I could have walked home, back then back to work, and then back home in the 4 hours it took me to get home today.

Why can;t the MTA get their act together and communicate to the riding public? If there is ever a serious life and death emergency, the riders don't stand a prayer in the subway because there nobody tells us anything.
