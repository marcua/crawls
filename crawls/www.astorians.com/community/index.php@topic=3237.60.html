Miri,

I'm glad you realize I was not trying to be insulting or insensitive at all - not even to jcy, well, not till he got a little nasty with me. So often things become misconstrued here, esp. when you haven't met someone face to face. Before I met you or Jtrane or Lanseaux or Daisy or anyone here, each of your posts were just black letters in a white space. Empty kind of - interesting (of course!) or I wouldn't have stuck around - but they didn't mean that much. Now, when I read y'all's posts I hear and see you. I know how ya move when ya talk, your inflections, accents, sense of humor and yes, even your smiles, and that makes all the difference in the world in understanding one another - the wonderful human connection. I love that. Goldfish Boy said he doesn't smile a lot. His isn't often a big toothy grin, but he does smile - it's subtle and cute and sweet! Maybe if jcy and I had met before in RL, the thread might not have ended in the infamous Black Hole. We'll never know.

Of course I know different cultures have different customs and I totally respect that, I'm always curious and interested in the ways of each kind of people. I find it fascinating, and this is one reason I love NYC - there are so many different kinds of people. How boring it would be if we were all the same.

When I ask questions about things it's because I really want to know, not because I'm giving my disapproval or trying to be condescending, god knows half the time I feel like I just fell off the turnip truck! I've always wanted to know why, and I've driven people crazy with it. My dad used to get so frustrated with me ("But why is Pi this (insert) number?" I still suck at math), and on and on till now and why doesn't he/she smile?. This is why I became a reporter, so I could ask a million questions. I'm sure last night one Astorian just wanted to enjoy her tamale instead of answering my questions about the different customs of a particular religion, but it's because I want to know why and I felt comfortable asking her.

Sometimes some of us, and I'm first in line, need to be told to hush (with a  :-) of course.) If anyone gets through this whole post I'm sure you WILL be saying that to me.

When I pass the mosque near my house do I look at the men's faces or smile at them? No, I'm not a total hayseed. But when I go to the Ditmars Dunkin Donuts, where I've gone almost daily for more than a year, and all of the employees are of middle eastern/Indian/Pakistani/I'mtoodumbtoknow descent, do I smile and greet them wholeheartedly - yes, and they do the same to me. They ask about my husband, I ask about their families and it is a very pleasant exchange. They enjoy it as much as I do. When my husband goes in they always tell him to say hello to me.

When I shop at a store, whether it's on 5th Avenue or the LES, do I expect respectful service with at least a hint of a smile. (Okay, it is NY, at least not a furrowed angry stare then). YES, or like someone already said, I will gladly take my business elsewhere. And I won't have a problem telling the manager, with a  :-), why I'm not shopping there anymore. It's pretty obvious managers know this, almost every want ad I read, no matter the business, wants applicants who are  "friendly/personable/upbeat".

I know all of my immediate neighbors. (Part of this is from my policeman dad - "Cathy Jo, go meet those folks so if you have to pound on their door at 3 a.m., without a stitch between you and the world, they'll know ya and open the door!" Which has turned out to be good advice....but that's another story.) But also because I don't want to be the "average" New Yorker, being so removed from their neighborhood, just existing and not living WHERE they live. Ya know?

I eased into it. I started by smiling at them a few times, then exchanging "Good Morning's" and finally introducing myself when I saw them outside. Did they all think I was el loco? Of course! Italians next door, Greeks across the street, Spanish and Indian next door. We all have accents! None of them knew what to think of me, but now, a year later, we wave and have actual conversations. Talib, the little boy next door always asks questions about Texas and, like any good Texan, I'm always willing to brag share. Do I talk to his dad and uncle? Not usually more than a respectful nod and hello, but I've gotten pretty friendly with his mom and aunt. It's not always easy - their English is a lot better than my Urdu!

Do I walk down the mean streets of NYC with a huge grin directed at every passerby? Of course not. But I often do have a big smile because my husband is making me laugh, or we see an awesome old building, or if we get lucky and have one of those magical only-in-New-York moments or simply because I'm happy. I am in love with life, my husband and New York City and I express that, I don't understand how one can't. And, if one is enjoying life, how and why keep those wonderful feelings bottled up inside? Then what is life? Does being constantly solemn and void of expression make one more pious? Some may see this as the musings of a simpleton, but it kind of is simple....if you're happy and you know it - show it! Not for others but for yourself - to feel.

Now, to the root of this thread, who by the way looks like an average white guy, no outwardly religious signs, overhead him talking, he's from Upstate......

When I've worked in a small office for two days as the main, up-at-the-front receptionist and you've had to ask for my help a thousand times and I've smiled a thousand average, pleasant, how-can-I-help-you-smiles AND done everything you wanted, perfectly I might add, AND you aren't even the head guy, do I expect you to show some kind of response that resembles an upright-walking, breathing being who recognizes me as the same? F**K YES. It doesn't matter what religion/heritage/sexual orientation/disablility/gender/other EOE designation you fall under. I don't care if you just came in from Pluto to protest being knocked off the planets list. If you are capable of working in an office then you should be capable of this as well.

And if you aren't, it makes me think you are nothing but a soul-less vessel who has no empathy or regard for others and I have no trust in you. IMHO, if I never mentioned that.... :-D

And you probably lied on your Solar System Visa anyway and came from Uranus.

Love,
Cathy
