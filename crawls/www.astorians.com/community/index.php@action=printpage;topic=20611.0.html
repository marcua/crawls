Post by: lanseaux on February 16, 2011, 11:00:34 AM
Aldi and Trader Joe's are separate corporate entities owned by two brothers (that is, one brother owns Aldi, and the other owns TJ's).  Whereas TJ's is viewed as selling niche and specialty house-branded items, Aldi often sells house-branded staple items in bulk.  I think they are considered to be a no-frills, cost-cutting chain that sells decent quality items.  I've never been in one; this is based solely on what I've read.

Because they are owned by members of the same family, I think some of TJ's popularity has rubbed off on Aldi, although that's pure speculation on my part.

Title: Re: Aldi Food Store Rego Park
Post by: essen on February 18, 2011, 11:15:14 PM
There was an Aldi in my college town. It's nothing like Bravo, a lot like Trader Joe's except without the specialty market feel to it. It's basically a regular grocery store that only sells generic items. You can tell just by looking at the items what they're imitating - Triscuits, Pantene, Frosted Mini Wheats, Mountain Dew and whatever else. They also have different levels of items. Some are cheapo, and others are "gourmet" products that aren't really cheap, but they are generally better quality than the cheapo stuff - like all-natural ravioli or something. I'm going by what I knew a few years ago, but I would be surprised if it's changed all that much aside from specific products coming and going. That and you have to pay to use their grocery bags, so it's best to bring your own bags.
