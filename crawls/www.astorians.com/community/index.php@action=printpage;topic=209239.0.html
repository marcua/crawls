Title: Shutting down astorians.com
Post by: merm on November 16, 2021, 09:39:12 PM
Dear Astorians,

I'm writing to announce that this website will be closing down on December 31st.

It's hard to believe this forum is over 17 years old. It all started in 2004 (http://www.astorians.com/community/index.php?topic=2.msg2#msg2) when a small group of us realized there wasn't really a good online forum to share information about some of the great spots in Astoria and just talk about the neighborhood we loved. At first it was just a few of us posting on here, but as the "Freeze Peach Cafe (https://www.yelp.com/biz/freeze-peach-astoria)" (managed by my partner Meesh and I) opened and the community around the cafe grew, this online community also grew bigger and bigger.

For many years astorians.com was the most active community discussion site in the community, and it thrived with lots of active posts, and some awesome volunteer moderators that kept all of us relatively well behaved. Lots of people met here (some even got married!), and we talked A LOT about a lot of stuff.

But here in 2021 it feels that there are more options than ever for discussing community issues online, and that the time for a site like this has largely faded. The activity level on the site is much lower than it was at its peak, and it's a significant struggle to try and keep things going and fend off spam (which admittedly I haven't done a great job of for a long time). Rather that let it get taken over by spammers and hackers I've decided it's time to put the site to rest. All good things come to an end.

Many thanks to all who have made this site possible and giving it life all these years:
Moderators or major contributors: mcdirk, daisy, AJTNYC, Astoria Luv, 28Grand, megc, wasabisam, jayme and others

And of course; thank you.

I'd love if you took a moment to share a memory or two about how the site was useful to you through these years, or maybe a memory from your interactions with the people here.

Sincerely,
merm

Wow. I have not been active on this board in close to a decade, and actually moved out of Astoria in 2015 to purchase a house (on the other side of Queens) and start a family. But when I saw the email notifying me that this message board was shutting down, I felt the need to log on and post one more time.


Astorians.com was instrumental for me and my now-wife when we were looking for apartments together back in 2008. We knew we wanted to live in Astoria, but didn't know much about the neighborhood at all (other than our friends lived there). This board gave us tons of insight on the different sections of Astoria, and great feedback on certain buildings, landlords, realtors,  etc. It really helped us find the right apartment, and we lived there very happily for 7 years, becoming entrenched in the neighborhood. I came back to this board many times for food and bar recommendations from others, and to give my own as well, as Astoria blossomed into one of the "it" neighborhoods in all of New York City.


I came to Astoria as a 26-year old who felt 22, and grew from a post-college party animal into a career-oriented, family-focused 33-year old soon-to-be father (who still liked a good beer or three). Hard to believe I have been away from Ditmars for 6 years now (and my son is in first grade!), but I come back to visit often. The area will ALWAYS be home for me, and this message board was always a small part of that chapter in my life.


I am saddened to see it will be shut down, but happy it existed in the first place, and proud of everything the moderators did to keep it going.   :smiley: :embarrassed:


I wish you all nothing but the best going forward.


-Andrew
