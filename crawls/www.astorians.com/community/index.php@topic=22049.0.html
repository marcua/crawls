Re: Re: Overdevelopment in Astoria

If you suddenly had to give up your car, you'd adjust.

I've never had a Costco membership and do not feel like I'm missing anything by not having one. I have gone to the Home Depot in Manhattan (via subway) to pick up what I need (gardening supplies, mostly) and it's worked out just fine.


When I really need a car, I call a car service or rent one. It's infrequent, though.


With car payments, car insurance, the price of gasoline, tolls, upkeep, registration, and other various costs, combined with my income flow, it's hard for me to justify owning a car in NYC. I plan to avoid having a car for as long as possible. For me, paying for all of that makes no sense at all, seeing as I can fulfill my transportation needs in other ways. For my personal needs, it would be like throwing money away.


I feel fortunate that I can live my life in NYC without needing a car.

Ok, we get it - you are anti vehicle.  I know many people who are and feel completely the same way, that the need for a car in NYC isn't necessary.

I take the train to work and sometimes I bike to work from Astoria to Manhattan.  Public transit, walking and biking are three great ways to get around the city.

But on weekends and taking three kids out I wouldn't think of anything else than having a car.  Things like going to the Bronx zoo take 25 minutes in a car but at least 2 hours on the subway.  Food shopping would be a real hassle unless I wanted to take two shopping carts with me.  Bringing the kids to daycare before work would mean an hour or more head start.  There are a lot of things that having a car makes a lot easier when you have a family and young kids.

I agree that a big problem is that every family has 2 or 3 cars now which I feel is excessive and unnecessary.  People on my in-laws block get new cars and then leave the old cars parked in the street completely unused.  Every day I see these cars parked in the same spots, just moved for alternate side parking.  I do think there are few instances where having more than one car is an absolute necessity and would never plan on owning more than one.
