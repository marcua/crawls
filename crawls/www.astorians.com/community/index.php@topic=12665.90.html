I finally had the opportunity to try Petey's Burger today.   I had a cheeseburger and an order of fries, and later on, a chocolate shake.  My verdict:  very close to complete satisfaction.  I was craving a fast food-style burger and fries, and Petey's delivered (figuratively!  I ate in-house).

I think it's important to note, as a number of posters have, that this isn't a thick gourmet burger; it's definitely in the thin, fast food family of burgers.  And while my usual preference is for thicker burgers done rare, this was one of the best burgers of its style that I have had.  Note:  I have never had an In-n-Out burger, so I can't make that comparison.  But although well done, the meat was tasty and surprisingly juicy.  I thought the flavours of the burger, sauce and toppings melded nicely (although as a few people mentioned, a pickle wouldn't be amiss).  I ate the cheeseburger and fries around 4pm, after having had nothing but a glass of kefir this morning around 10:30, so I was very hungry, but I found the size of the burger to be quite satisfactory.

I thought the fries were excellent.  The order was huge - it could easily satisfy two people, and I actually wish it were half the size and maybe $1 cheaper.  It was really unnecessarily large.  (Or hey - they could have two sizes.)  But they were nice and thin, with a good potato flavour.  The browning and crunch were just right for my taste.

The chocolate shake was a good fast food shake, and is made to order with Fox's U-Bet syrup.  I don't know what the other flavours are made with.  I am normally a chocolate fiend - I would say this is moderately chocolatey, which fits with the style of the food and service.

Criticisms:  mostly small details relating to toppings and condiments:
  • I thought the onion slice was way too thick - but I don't like raw onion, so I just removed the whole thing.  My husband, who normally does like raw onion, found it to be too much as well. 
  • The lettuce and tomato were on the bottom.  There's a reason they are called "toppings", IMO, but I acknowledge that that is a personal preference, and it was easy enough to put them on top myself.  On a positive note, one of my tomato slices was actually cored, which I thought was a nice attention to detail.
  • The mayonnaise provided in packets was of generic food service quality and was way too sweet (and also contained both HFCS and regular corn syrup).  It was more like Miracle Whip (although I haven't tasted Miracle Whip in years) rather than proper mayo.  It didn't match the quality of the other ingredients.
  • The salt provided is too coarse to stick properly to the fries.  It sinks to the bottom, leaving half the fries undersalted and half the fries oversalted.  The fries themselves come unsalted, which is fine - I don't mind adding salt myself, but I wish it was proper fry salt.

The service was adequate - not stellar, but nothing was wrong with it.  You place your order at the counter and they call you up when it's ready, although they'll bring something small out to you if it comes out after.  But it's not really table service, which is fine.

The best part was really how I felt afterward.  On the rare occasions I have fast food (McDonalds or equivalent), I feel like crap afterward - like I've eaten something that my body barely recognises as food.  I had none of the feeling after this meal, and I think it's because the food quality is far better and fresher. 

Now, if they would only deliver to north of Ditmars!!!   :-D
