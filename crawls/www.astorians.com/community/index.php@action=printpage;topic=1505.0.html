Post by: windowsrefund on January 29, 2006, 12:15:53 PM
I think it is worth pointing out that a person does not need to be "good" in order to value and protect freedom. All that is required is an appreciation for one's own freedom. Once that is in place, it is only natural to work toward empowering others; even if only to protect one's own sense of freedom.

In the context of technology, this is even more obvious. Let's imagine (for a moment) that I have equipped myself with all I need in order to use my computer in freedom and that everyone else on the planet has allowed themselves to become part of the subscription based, "All your information is belongs to us" model offered by today's vendors of freedom-restricting technology. The end result is that allthough I may be "free", I have in fact, become the prisoner as a direct result of my inability to interact with others. Maybe this is because I am the only one who doesn't have a valid Passport login, or I never made it to my RFID implant appointment, or maybe I'm just the only fool on the planet who doesn't own approved (e.g. TCPA) hardware. Regardless, the most logical path for me to take in order to protect my own freedom is to try and empower others.

Best,
