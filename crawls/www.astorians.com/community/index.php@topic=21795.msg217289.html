Don Coqui Restaurant
« Reply #3 on: October 11, 2011, 11:07:23 AM »
We tried Don Coqui over the weekend.  Very impressed with the food and the chef and the staff.  This is a very well established and successful restaurant with a history in New Rochelle.  So they basically took that business model, teamed up with the owners of Dekko and Cienna and changed it over.  The Dekko part is still closed but will be a more casual cafe more for lunch and brunch.  But it seems like they're still giving it thought.  The old Cienna space is the main dining and drinking part of Don Coqui.  Unfortunately, the over-the-top, garish decor of the old Cienna space takes away from the more down-to-earth dinner menu.  But I'm sure it fits well when it's after midnight and the place becomes more of a club.  We tried several small plate items.  Everything was delicious.  Get the tostones with beef and also with codfish.  Both were great. The king crab legs were amazing.  Fresh and cooked perfectly.  My guess is that all the food is prepared equally as well. The place definitely has a following and is already drawing crowds.  Dekko and Cienna never quite found a niche there.  I have a feeling this will be a huge success.   We ate at the bar and the servers could not be more helpful and pleasant.
