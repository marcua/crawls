It was only a matter of time till a thread was made about loud motorcycles.

Yes I do have a motorcycle. Yes it is a sport bike. Yes it does have a aftermarket exhaust system on it. Yes it does make it louder.

I understand where everyone is coming from when they say that the noise is becoming a nuisance. I live in ear shot of Hoyt Ave. and hear them as well. Do I approve of it? No, not at all. Are they morons for doing it? Of course. But here's the best part, close to none of the a-holes who make all this excessive noise even live in Astoria. I will say this though, my loud exhaust has saved me on numerous occasions from becoming a hood ornament. If I feel the need to speed and let loose I'll take my bike to the track, where it was intended to be ridden. These sport bikes are street legal by means of a headlight, turn signals, a horn and mirrors. Aftermarket exhausts are made to produce more horse power. I laugh at these squids (see definition http://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=squid) because the majority of them like to show off. Never even realizing what these bikes can do when put on a track.

If I want to make noise I'll take my motorcycle to a track. Not on house lined streets in the wee hours of the morning, where in turn I'll set off multiple car alarms and wake up the entire block while doing so.
