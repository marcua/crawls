i've been living in a three-story building for nearing 7 years. it took some getting used to, but i have to admit i kind of prefer it to other options in the city. it's very quiet (except for the occasional laguardia noise), laid back and relatively convenient to city and parts outside. i also like that there are trees and lots of them.

i'll be the first to admit it's not for everyone, though. the train is a 20 minute walk or a bus ride. basic services are nearby (ie. dry cleaner, delis, liquor store, etc.), though.

if you are looking to buy, i would recommend staying near ditmars in either the three-story buildings to the south or the two-story buildings to the north.

I have been subletting a condo  here for three years.  I thought it was a temporary situation since I own a home in NJ but my gf and I like it here. I know some residents recently squawked about a maintenance increase but heating costs are going up everywhere so I understand the increase. 

Pros:
Really quiet here and nice tree lined blocks.

In the two story buildings  each apartment has a storage room.  They vary by apartment type but ours is 12 by 15 or so with a washer dryer hookup.  Great for storage or bikes.

Some local stores for necessities as mentioned in an earlier post.

Street parking is not bad before 8PM especially if you take your car(s) to work and don't have to worry about alternate side parking in the morning.

Cons:

If you need to take the train you have to take a bus to it.  It is walking distance on a nice day but about 30 blocks.

No bars or restaurants in walking distance (nearest restaurant about 12 blocks, nearest bar about 20 blocks)

The planes fly low when it it raining or foggy  so it can make it hard to hear the TV.

Closet space is very small (thank God for the storage room).

PM me if you have specific questions.

We have lived in Garden Bay since 1986.  We recently moved out of our apt. not for any other reason than we purchased a home three blocks away.  Garden Bay is a a suburban community within Astoria.  32 minutes to midtown door to door, trees, ample parking and surprisingly no interference from LaGuardia in terms of noise, traffice or loss of cell phone service.  It's hard to believe that the airport is only blocks away because one never hears the planes...the soundwall does a commendable job in that regard.  And if anyone is a frequent business traveler like we are, how sweet it is to know that you are literally HOME within 15 minutes of landing.

To give you a sense of community in the area....during the Queens blackout a couple years back the owners and tenants of the complex contributed to "community BBQ's" and the local residents pulled together to check in and assist the older residents.  Every July 4 several of the blocks continue to hold community block parties.

We had the option to sell when prices were at their peak but we decided not to because the neighborhood has held it's value and we've rented it to family and friends who wanted to be closer to the city without the cost and congestion.

It's a great value in many ways and I would recommend the area to anyone interested.
