Ordered pick up/take out last night.  Very nice place and delicious food.  Every single item we ordered was better than the next.  First off, it will be obvious from the start that the menu reads like a dozen other places in town.  But don't be fooled.  The food here is unique and exceptional, in my opinion. The Trahana soup and the Avegolemono were easily the best we've had anywhere in Astoria.  The Koupes are similar to Kibbehs but these are much more fragrant of allspice/cinnamon that is more common with Anatolian varieties.  They're almost like a dessert!  The Prasini salad is a traditional Greek village salad.  Fresh and finely chopped lettuce with tons of lemon, fresh dill, mild feta, and some oil.  It should be cold, crisp, clean and refreshing and this one was for sure.  The Koupepia are also very delicious.  The grape leaves were nicely brined but firm and the ratio of meat and rice was perfect.  Again, more of an Armenian or Constantinople style rather than the very oily grape leaves filled only with rice.  Along the same lines, they offer bulgur pilaf rather than white rice and theirs is very delicious.  The most incredible dish was the Kleftiko described as spring lamb wrapped in foil and baked for 6 hours.  This was somewhere between a roasted leg of lamb and burnt ends you'd get at Butcher Bar. The meat was moist but with a good crust.  The flavor was complex and smoky.  I never had lamb like this at any Greek or Cypriot restaurant in Astoria.  This place is not a typical take out place with a counter and gill up front.  It's more upscale with white linens and a very nice dining room.  But the people are warm and friendly and eager to please.  They made it very clear that their mission is to appeal to non-Greeks as much as Greeks.  I think they'll do well and next time I'd like to sit inside and have a meal.  They have wine and beer I believe.  And they plan to have live music Fridays and Saturdays.  And starting at 8 or 9 rather than at 11 like some of the Greek clubs in town.  Being walking distance from both the Ditmars or the Astoria Blvd stations, I have a feeling this place will be a hit with locals and people coming in by subway.  I have no doubt that word of mouth will travel fast when people try this food.  They said the Wall Street Journal was coming today to do a profile on them.

It is cool to learn that some things are different because they're supposed to be.  It's not fair to say that one Trahana is better than another if it's a regional difference.  But many of us wouldn't know that and just know what we like better.  Same thing with the pizza at Tuffino vs. Basil Brick Oven.  The discussion here about it made us understand that they're very different styles of pizza.  Back to this place, I also tried their Sheftalia at Kopiaste and preferred it over others I had in Astoria by a long shot.  I find this can be pretty fatty and gamey at most places.  At Kopiaste, they are small in size, like an American breakfast sausage.  More heavily spiced and not so fatty or greasy.  Again, it could just be a different style but this was more to my liking.
