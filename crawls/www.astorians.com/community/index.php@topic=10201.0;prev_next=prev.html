Re: Another Q. about paying rent in Cash?
« Reply #1 on: March 11, 2008, 09:42:09 PM »
Banks can sell you a "bank check", or "cashier's check" - you give them money, and they write a check that you can give to someone else. The recipient can deposit or cash them like any other check - typically the check clears and they get their money faster than they would for your personal check.

The Post Office can sell you a "money order". Some other courier and communications companies may also sell them, Western Union perhaps.

A landlord who wants to be paid in cash is possibly too lazy to go to the bank, or more likely, is hoping to evade paying taxes. He'll likely prefer the money order because it's easier to cash if he has no bank account, and more anonymous.

If you use bill pay through your bank's website, the bank automatically sends the payment as a bank check.  I prefer this because my bank allows free bill pay if you have direct deposit.  With that, I don't have to worry about going to the bank to purchase a bank check nor have to buy a money order.  Any of the "check cashing" places will also do money orders for you.  Check with your bank about bill pay first.

A bank check is the same thing as a certified check.  The purpose is that the money is taken out at first to guarantee that the check will not bounce.  Some landlords probably prefer the money order/cash thing because of bounced checks.  Using bill pay deducts directly from your account when it sends the check.  So much easier to keep track of your bills/payments this way.

When the $ person here gets a check or cash, we issue an e-receipt.
If you live in a smaller dwelling, maybe you should check out the dept of buildings and find out if there is a certificate of occupancy for your place. My friends had this problem- they used to live by the park in a "three bedroom" and the LL wanted cash- turns out it was an illegal apartment- they took the realtor (V. Kourtakis) and the LL (don't remember his name) to queens civil court- with no lawyers, just some advice from a law student and a knowledge of research methods.
My friends won- they got back every penny they paid in rent (for eight or nine months) plus the fee they paid to the realtor.
Tell your LL you can't pay in cash and it's not stipulated in the lease, so you are under no obligation to do so.
If he wants to play hardball, go check on that certificate of occupancy- you need to go to the records department.
As a side note- my friends got "blacklisted" and no one near the park would rent to them- they dispersed- Astoria's loss.
But this was eight years ago- it would be pretty difficult to get "blacklisted" like that today- and the internet wasn't the way to find places back then.
If you do not like confrontations, write the LL a note with your next rent check.
The only conceivable reason to ask for cash is if you bounced a check to him already-if that's the case, get a money order.
If a person wants to pay cash, that is fine, but there are extremely limited conditions under which a LL is ENTITLED to such a thing- none comes to my legal mind at the moment...
