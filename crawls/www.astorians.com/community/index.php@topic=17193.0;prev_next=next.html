Regarding measuring, that's true for cakes and such, but it's not particularly true for bread, except for the flour/water ratio. Yeast is much more tolerant of variation than are chemical leaveners.

Here's my standby memorized bread recipe. Proportions are perfect for a bread machine too.

13.3 oz (by weight) flour (bread flour or King Arthur all-purpose and/or whole wheat bread flour)
1 c water at about 100 F
1 t yeast
1 t salt
1 T vegetable oil

1. Stir yeast into warm water and let sit until dissolved (takes longer with non-instant yeast). Meanwhile, mix flour(s) and salt with a whisk in a big bowl.
2. Make a hole in the flour and pour in yeast mixture and oil. Mix with a spoon until a thick dough. Knead for 3-10 minutes. The more you knead, the more the gluten will develop. Sometimes you want a bread with less bubbles, like when making dinner rolls, so knead less in that case.
3. Lightly oil another large bowl, put dough in that bowl and turn to cover with oil. Cover with a clean dish towel and let sit in an 80 degree spot for an hour or so.
4. When springy, punch down, then let rest 10 minutes. Form into the shape you'd like (there are specific techniques for doing this, but they're hard to describe), put on a baking sheet dusted with flour, cover with the dish towel, and let rise again, maybe 45 minutes.
5. Preheat oven to 375 F. Heat up a 12" cast iron skillet and invert it inside the oven, on an oven rack near the bottom of the oven. (Ersatz baking stone!)
6. Put the bread on the baking sheet onto the inverted hot skillet, spray the inside of the oven walls with a few squirts of water from a spray bottle, close the oven. Wait two minutes. Spray the walls again. This creates steam which condenses on the cold bread dough, preventing it from forming a thick dry crust. You want a crisp crust instead.
7. Bake for 30-60 minutes, depending on the shape of the loaf, until browned and hollow-sounding if you knock the bottom. Let cool almost completely before slicing. Serve with good quality lightly salted butter.
