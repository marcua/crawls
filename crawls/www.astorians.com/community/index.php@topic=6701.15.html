Quote from: Frappe on June 20, 2007, 09:40:22 AM
I don't think pizza was saying he thought it was wrong to want or strive to have only one spouse.  I think he meant it more like marriage is a human construct originally developed for sake of passing along property, developing alliances and making sure your progeny is your progeny (this is why I always give a laugh when I hear the anti-gay folk defending the "traditional" marriage).  Love and compatibility of companionship are relatively new requirements for a sucessful marriage.

None of the other higher primates demonstrate remote leanings to even temporary monogamy.  Its not in our nature to have monogamous relationship from child bearing age to grave.  Especially in this age, where folk live into their 80's and the former roles which we used to adhere to, a certain seperation of duties and activities between the sexes is greatly eroded.

I always admired the relationship spency tracy and katherine hepburn had though.

Yes, that's an eloquent way of putting it.

I think it's certainly possible to have a lifelong relationship - while we're using celebrity examples, I'll cite Paul Newman and Joanne Woodward, - but I don't think it's mankind's natural inclination.  Even when you're with someone you're deeply in love with, you still look at other men or women.  You're still attracted to other men and women.  And if you weren't inhibited by social constraints, you would probably want to have sex with other men or women. 

I think monogamy is a fine idea and if you can make it work, that's wonderful.  But I don't think it's reasonable to expect it to happen; and I don't think one should be truly upset if it doesn't happen.
