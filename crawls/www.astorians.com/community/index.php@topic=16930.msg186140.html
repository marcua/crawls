Another silly conversation (like the Peter Valone thread above).

I've lived here all my life, went to school here, now raising a family here.  And while things change, very little of it is negative.  The "youthful energy" of the community is not a bad thing...certainly brings in more new business and attention to the neighborhood.

Yes, I can understand that people have a problem with overdevelopment.  But that's the complete opposite of the "Detroit" analogy offered earlier.   The development has happened because people want to live here; good neighborhood, relatively low crime, access to public transportation, good schools, etc.  And what you're probably going to see happen over the next few years is that those same people who live here with a roomate eventually will marry someone, then move into a bigger apartments.  Then, when they start a family, they'll look to own here.  There are plenty of young families all around the neighborhood already in that situation; all you have to do is look around the street at any given moment.

So unlike Detriot, NYC (and Astoria itself, by extension) benefits from more people moving in and eventually seting up roots in the community.  That doesn't create Detroits' urban blight; that creates prosperity.

How this is somehow a "bad" thing is beyond me.  Then again, if I wanted a nice quiet communities with very few young people and wide open spaces, isn't that what Florida's designed for?



Again, this is a silly thread.  Do people have nothing else to talk about than "you young people get off my lawn"???
