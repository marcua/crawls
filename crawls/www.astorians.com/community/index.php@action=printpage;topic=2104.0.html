Title: Re: What's that smell??
Post by: lanseaux on May 11, 2006, 04:01:46 PM
It's courtesy of our neighbourhood sewage treatment plant - the Bowery Bay WPCP (Water Pollution Control Plant), located at 43-01 Berrian Blvd. (http://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&hl=en&q=43-01+Berrian+Blvd.,+astoria,+ny&ll=40.778713,-73.894794&spn=0.004346,0.012188&t=h&om=1)  Where is that, you may ask?  Go north on Steinway St. to Berrian Blvd, which is one block north of 19th Ave.  Make a right and go three blocks.  Until recently, the facade of the building had a really fantastic public works bas-relief.  Unfortunately, it's recently been covered over with a maze of piping that obscures nearly the whole thing.

It always seems to smell more when it's damp or humid out - I think the smell doesn't dissipate as quickly in the moister air.

Here's (http://www.dec.state.ny.us/website/ohms/decis/nycdepspdesr2.html) a list of the 14 WPCPs owned by New York City.  If you can wade through the legalese, the decision deals with nitrogen effluent reduction schedules at all 14 plants.

BIG WHIFF OF ANGER

 By ANGELA MONTEFINISE

 May 13, 2006 -- This place stinks.

 That's what furious Queens residents are saying about a city sewage-treatment plant in Astoria that's been emitting an "intolerable" stench for months.

 "It smells like 20 dogs took a dump in my yard," said Nick Arakas, 35, who lives two blocks from the plant. "You can't keep your windows open. You can't stay out more than a minute. I have a 3-year-old, and my wife doesn't take him outside."

 The city has been aware of the problem since November, when the plant's boilers - which heat sludge and reduce odors - were destroyed in a fire.

  But it has taken months to order parts and prepare for the repair, and a contractor defaulted on the project, said Charles Sturken, a spokesman for the city Department of Environmental Protection.

 "How long are we supposed to wait?" asked Sandra Martin, 55, who lives near the plant. "It's been months already. "

 Sturken said the boilers would be fixed in the next month.

New York.- Astoria residents were not pleased to find that the aromas wafting from the dozens of eateries in the neighborhood have recently been replaced by a stench emanating from a sewage treatment plant on Berrien Avenue, state Assemblyman Michael Gianaris (D- Astoria) said Tuesday.

The assemblyman, who said he could smell the odor as far as 78th Street near LaGuardia Airport, said there was a malfunction at the Astoria plant in November, during which a fire destroyed some of its boilers. He said there has been no contingency plan to deal with the emissions and that the boilers still have not been repaired.

In a letter sent to Emily Lloyd, Commissioner of the Department of Environmental Protection, Assemblyman Gianaris stated:

Dear Ms. Lloyd,
I write regarding a situation that concerns the sewage-treatment plant located in Astoria as it relates to the residents living in the nearby community.

Recently, as the New York Post reported in a May 13th 2006 article, noxious odors have been emanating from the sewage-treatment plant located on Berrien Avenue in Astoria causing a number of residents to contact me regarding the disturbing stench. As a resident of the area, I personally observed the stench and can attest to its foulness and detrimental effect to the community.

While I understand that the odors are caused by the destruction of odor reducing equipment in a November 29th 2005 fire, there has been no contingency plan initiated to deal with the odor emitting sewage. Now, the odor is more potent than ever and is threatening the quality of life in our western Queens community.

The smell is too much to bear for western Queens residents, who already suffer from the many industrial facilities located in our neighborhood. The pollution emitted from local power plants already makes breathing difficult. The last thing we need is this rotten stench as well.

Accordingly, I ask that you make every attempt to remedy the situation as soon as possible.

I appreciate any assistance you could provide regarding this situation. Thank you for your attention to this matter. Please call me if you have any questions”.
