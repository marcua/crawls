My family and I moved out of a rental unit there in July 2014 in favor of a better commute and a pet friendly place. I can't speak from an owner's perspective, so keep that in mind. Our rental period was three years, from 1 August 2011 to 31 July 2014.


During our stay there, we had very few problems, and most of those were resolved quickly. When we suspected our kids had a bedbug issue in their room, the management was quick to apply treatment, no questions asked (it turned out to be poison ivy from one of the nearby parks). When the dishwasher broke, they replaced it with a new one (I assume because the one there could no longer be repaired.) It would have been nice if they could have acted sooner to address the mice, but that did eventually get resolved as well. The only thing that exasperated me about the place was that we couldn't keep the light bulbs in good working order; they would burn out much faster than they should have, and the very high ceilings made them a pain to change. Again, that's pretty minor.


The space we had (2 bedroom) was adequate, and the large windows did allow lots of light, though our position on the SE corner of the building made for some hot summers. Be prepared to invest in blackout curtains if you don't want to pay through the nose for AC. The floors appear to be covered in a laminate wood flooring, which sometimes slides and warps, leaving small gaps and such. Once again, this is a minor thing, but I wouldn't have been pleased to own such a floor. Your experience might end up being different. Overall, the place was quiet, in part because we lived in the back corner. Occasional visits to other floors/halls yielded similar results, or at least nothing stood out to me.


It's true that the building is a good hike to the train. But you can surmise that already. If you own a car, there's a parking garage with spaces you can rent, but be forewarned that the kind of snow we've been getting here in the recent past made getting a car out of the garage something of a challenge. The lower level (covered) spaces cost more for a reason.


What else? The doormen were all nice (or nice enough), and the super was responsive most of the time. Had we not decided to seek out more space, we probably would have stayed.
