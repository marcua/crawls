Title: "Like"
Post by: Jtrane on July 18, 2007, 02:30:04 PM
This has really been bugging me lately, and I'm not sure why I've become so much more sensitive to it.  But it's the use of the word "like" used as every 2nd word when people talk.

Like, I don't know, like, what I'm, like, trying to say.  It's like, crazy, how, like, incapable people are, like, to not use, like, like.

Can you imagine if people actually wrote the way they actually spoke?  The word "like" has become the most used word in the English language.

Ah, I now remember.  I heard a commentary on NPR about the fact that the current generation lacks conviction and it's apparent in our speech patterns.  Use of the word "like" de-emphasizes arguments.  It was really a great piece, and I wish I could remember it.

But come on people, aren't we capable of forming thoughts and translating them to speech without that dreaded word?
