http://www.astorians.com/index.php?topic=6988.0

Once again it seems that astorians.com, for better or worse, does reflect this community.

Some of you have been to a community board meeting or a precinct council meeting, and, as you have observed yourselves, shouting down someone and trying to silence them as they bring in some thoughtful points is less than helpful. 

Remember when the lights went out last summer? More than one person has said to me how shocked they were to see cameras panning around  an all but empty meeting room in public hearing after hearing. The whole city witnessed that sad spectacle – and took careful notes.

Where were all of you?

And simply parroting what the politicians said, as so many residents did, while café after café took down its walls and pumped air-conditioning into the open air, or by turning a blind eye as your neighbors subdivide their basements, or by remaining mute as some developer takes down a house for a 12 story monster in your backyard says volumes about a community – your silence speaks loudly.

Thousands of residential units have been proposed for this community last summer. Silvercup is even taking down a generator to put up a number of 40 story buildings …. And not even a puff of concern for the grid?

We have all noted that time and again, when something happens, everyone is all fired up and ready to go to the community council or the like, then disappoint us as it peters out in a few weeks. We have seen groups around the community hold meetings to discuss issues. How many of you show up?

In politics, the squeaky wheel gets the grease. Doormats gets the shaft. And yes, I agree with a central tenant in his essay: a passive community is a dying community.

Whether you accept the obvious or not, there are problems in this community and there are trends that are not so positive. Just read the papers and listen to the local politicians for confirmation.

Rather than BLOGGING to gloss over or ignore everything, or sadly attack those that even raise talking points, as you probably will me,  why don’t you GET INVOLVED by actually looking into issues, thinking about them beyond your immediate experience, discussing them with others, and trying to come up with ideas?

A successful community does not happen on its own. Democracy is not a spectator sport. And if you love something, be it a car, a child, or a neighborhood, you will get passionate about it and take care of it.

I never met the fellow that wrote this, nor do I agree with everything he says, but I respect what he says. Because, unlike his naysayers, he makes me think.
