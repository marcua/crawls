Hi Y'all, I only found this board a few months ago but liked it right from the beginning.

On July 4 it will be a year since my husband Max (54) and I (43) moved here from Amarillo, Texas. Despite some health problems we both have faced, the last year has been the happiest of our lives. I've wanted to live in NYC since I was 9 years old. I joined the U. S. Coast Guard after high school in order to have a chance to live here and lived on Governor's Island for 4 incredible years. (If you haven't been on the free tour you HAVE to go - it's awesome.) Also lived in Italy, Connecticut, California and Boston thanks to Uncle Sam.

Had to leave the military to care for my terminally ill father, then my terminally ill mother, then raised my 1-year-old niece because my sister is a low-life criminal and a permanent guest of the Texas Dept. of Corrections. Though I do consider my niece my daughter, I don't have other children (thank God, because one is enough for anybody!) We moved here after she graduated high school. She actually was accepted to a prestigous NYC acting school but after the first semester decided it wasn't for her and she's back in Texas going to college.

I got really sick and almost died from having Diabetes and not knowing it. I spent the entire month of January in Wyckoff hospital in Brooklyn and now am in the best health of my life. My husband had to have heart surgery in April and now he is in the best health of HIS life. So... we are both really happy and really healthy!  :-D He is a RN at Hospital for Special Surgery and I am an unemployed newspaper reporter and freelance writer.  My husband and I also published a history magazine about the Texas Panhandle for a couple of years named "Yesterday's Panhandle". That was a lot of fun!

I love everything about New York City - the history, the architecture, the noise, the tourists, the subway, the quirky stores, I just love it....everyday I walk out the door and think about how happy I am living here! On "our" trains I always sit so I can see the Manhattan skyline coming and going from Astoria. I relish the moments when people ask me for directions because I love knowing NYC. I love telling tourists little known info that makes their trip here better - like they don't have to pay $15 to get into the Met... they can pay as little as a penny. (Get "The Cheap Bastard's Guide to New York City" for more great tips like this!) My husband gets so embarrassed when I do that so he makes me buy the tickets, but I have no qualms about embracing my inner cheap bastard! I'm devouted to my NFT book and don't go anywhere without it.  I still have my Texas accent but often it is hard for people to understand me so I find my enunciation is getting better or, as my friends in Texas say, I sound more like a Yankee everyday.

Other interests are history, reading, trivia, playing yahtzee and card games, beach, people-watching, football (Go Cowboys!), music (both kinds - Country AND Western). Just kidding, that's just one of my little country jokes. Dig the blues (Hound Dog Taylor esp) and also a big Ani DiFranco fan. (So bummed I'll be out of town when she plays SummerStage). Some folks, esp. at first, probably think I am nosey because I ask a lot of questions but that's cause I love meeting people and finding out things about them... their stories, why they do what they do, their experiences...hence the reporter thing.  I love to laugh, sing at the top of my lungs (though my voice could peel paint off the wall) and generally have a good time. Also not afraid to voice my strong opinions.

I also like to talk.....a lot......

I have a couple of NYC friends from the job I had before I got sick but really am desperate to meet new friends, which is one reason I like this board so much. I was really happy to meet some of you at the clothing swap and look forward to meeting everyone! I plan on joining some of your get togethers and hope a Beer Garden outing happens soon. 

Whew.... :-)


Cathy
