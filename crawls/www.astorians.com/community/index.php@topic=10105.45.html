It's a nasty word and idea, and entirely accurate.  I find it less insulting than the actual concept of white kids mimicking the most absolute inane, silly, stereotypical and insulting aspects of ghetto culture.  The word is dead on, which is why it makes people feel so uncomfortable.  (I first heard it in the early 90s, when the nephew of a friend in Delaware used it to describe the kids in his high school going through these motions for the first time.  His reaction was to become a huge Misfits/punk rock fan, complete with purple mohawk.)

I felt like Handyman about 10 years ago, when this nonsense seemed to be at its absolute apex.  And most of the definitions have it wrong in isolating this as the trend of wealthier suburban white kids.  I've surely seen plenty of rural and working-class kids going there, too.  Just about every white kid in Astoria I was coming across in 1997!

What I've seen and realized since then is that this is just a phase for most kids, usually right after professional wrestling, and just before the stark realization that acting this way isn't going to get them laid in college, thus the sensitive Dave Matthews Band (now Jack Johnson) frat boy phase ensues thereafter.  Its a phase, and a regrettable one at that, unless there are millions of 30-year-old, stone-faced white guys out there with size 58 pants, XXXXL Oakland Raiders football jerseys, sideways visors, etc.

I think it's a real odd mix of emotional development (hopefully not stunted), white self loathing and, let's face it, a sense of "coolness" as it does readily offend parents and serve as a sort of code to other kids who indulge.  My anger turned to befuddled amusement a long time, but that applies to a lot more things than this.
