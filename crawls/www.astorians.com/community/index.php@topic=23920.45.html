Quote from: braun on October 25, 2013, 03:00:58 PM
Has anybody tried this place yet?  I'm curious as to how it is

Yes, the wife and I checked it out last night. 

It's amazing that this used to be the old OTB, they've really done a nice job with it.  I walked by it on Friday night and peeked in--there are a ton of television screens and it had a sports bar atmosphere, so I thought it would be a good place to have a burger and watch college football the next evening.

We got there around 8:30 and were shown to the back room.  The place has a "club" like feel to it.  Scantily clad waitresses made it a bit uncomfortable and I suddenly didn't feel like I was in a sports bar anymore.  There were television screens everywhere and loud music.  Unfortunately, there were no college football games on in the back, but it was the type of place that is too intimidating to ask them to switch the game.  My burger was good (and large) and we ended up watching the World Series instead. 

If this is your scene (hoochy waitresses and a club-like atmosphere), then I'm sure you'll enjoy it.  It's not at all my scene and I very much doubt I'll ever be back.

I pretty much agree with smiddlem.  We stopped in yesterday just to see what it was like inside.  Granted, HUGE crowd for Sunday Football, so it was very busy and noisy.  I agree that the atmosphere is clubby and Vegas-like.  And I'd agree that I may not go in very often at all, BUT...I was very impressed and very glad to see people enjoying it.  I asked a very down-to-earth guy smoking out front what he thought and he was beaming and said he was having the greatest time.  There was a steady stream of people going in.  From the looks of it, it's wildly popular already!  I'm happy to see it and thrilled that OTB is gone for good.  I consider this a great addition to the Ditmars area.  I'm not a die-hard sports fan to start with, but Katch is a bit more my speed and less with the club/mirrors vibe.  I think Sweet Spot could seem less welcoming to some people, but it will not hurt their business in the least.  I might add that I looked across the street and both Ditmars Station and O'Hanlons were also crowded for Sunday football, so it goes to show that Sweet Spot is meeting a demand and not killing business for others.
