So I finally got around to trying out this place.  I got the aspargus and mushroom panini, and my parents tried the italian sausage and cheese, and the chicken cutlet with spicy mayo.  The spicy mayo was too much for my mom, probably should just be a side order, it did look like a lot of mayo on that thing.  But the other paninis were awesome!  I have to say I was rather disappointed in the decor and atmosphere there, it's really cold, little heat, and the music was blasting.  It was hard to hear each other.  And the decor on the walls, the color paint, the set up of the back eating area, it's just kind of boring and drab.  It could use some color, instead of dullish gray and yellow.  The setup looked cheap, like the walls could fall apart if you kicked them kind of cheap.

At $3.50 each, the brownies aren't cheap, but they are pretty darn good, and those cupcakes look to die for.

Thanks to you guys, I tried Bambino and had it twice last week.  First night I had the chicken cutlet with olive spread panini and a red lentil soup.  The panini was good but I fell in love with soup at first taste.   A few nights later I had the proscuttio with  hot peppers panini and cream of cauliflower soup.  I'm not sure what I was thinking with the hot peppers because they really were hot (I have a low tolerance for very spicy), but once I removed most of them, there was still enough hot pepper seasoning left that it made for a perfect sandwich.   I'm not usually a cream of whatever/blank fan, but this soup was pretty darn good. And hey, I still have the leftover in my fridge. 

Haven't gotten around to the cupcakes or brownies yet, oh but I will, I surely will!
