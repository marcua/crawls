I've got a pretty nifty "sidewalk fair game" story:  Some years ago, on a Sunday, I was walking though a public seating area in Manhattan (right near MoMA) and saw a small Tiffany bag sitting on a bench all by itself.  No one was around anywhere.  So, I walked over to the bag and stood next to it (not blocking it from view) for a few minutes thinking maybe someone forgot it but would some come rushing back.  Then I decided I would sit on the bench next to it for 30 minutes to see if someone would come back.  No one did, so I carried it to the adjacent buildings security desk and left my name and number in case someone came looking for the bag.  I was now in a quandry as to what to do (I figured I had something valuable, but I didn't feel right "claiming" it).  Inside were two tiffany jewlery boxes wrapped with their white bows, no receipt in the bag.  I still hadn't opened the boxes.  I decided that I would go to Tiffany (only a few blocks away) and have them open the boxes and see if they could somehow trace the purchaser.  I told a clerk my tale, she got a manager, and the manager opened the boxes.  A silver braclet and a silver ring: small potatoes to Tiffany and no way, she said to trace it.  I left my name and number with her in case anyone came in saying they lost this merchandise.  After not hearing anything for 2 weeks, I took the braclet and ring back to Tiffany and exchanged them for a gift card whose value exceeded $500! 

I feel comfortable with how I handled the situation.  If I hadn't picked-up the bag, obviously someone else would.  I waited for the owner and left my contact info with two logical sources and really hoped that the owner would get in contact.  People have said that I should have gone to the police, perhaps.  But I didn't think of it.  By the way, I sold the $500 gift card to an acquaintance for a deep discount and he bought a very special present for his god-son. :-)
