A-1 Courier Services/ A-1 International
« on: October 25, 2010, 10:22:55 PM »
In the past we've had discussions about UPS, FedEx, and the United States Postal Service, but does anyone have anything good to say about A-1? 

I'd never even heard of them till I saw their name on my latest Amazon order.

They claim to have tried to deliver my package last Friday at 9:10 at night.  A pretty damn unusual time for residential deliveries.  They didn't offer any further information about when their next delivery attempt would be, and Amazon has a two year history of complaints about this company, which apparently doesn't return phone calls.  There was no update till this morning (Monday) when they updated with "Out for delivery 9:03 AM.  That was about 11 hours ago and I still don't have my package and have no idea if they are going to make an attempt to deliver tonight.  Of course I've been letting Amazon know about this, but with a two year history of complaints, it's clear Amazon doesn't care. 

The thing about UPS, FedEx, and the USPS is that you can almost always get someone on the phone.  You might not like their answer, but someone does pick up the phone. 

To people who don't order things over the internet it might seem strange that anyone in New York City would shop online since you supposedly can get anything here.  Shopping on line gives you the opportunity to comparison shop, know for certain that what you want is in stock, and frees you from carrying bulky items on mass transit or attempting to fit them in your car.  So, neither Amazon or A-1 is going out of business any time soon, but if you do place an order with Amazon you might want to tell them that if they ship it by A-1 you will cancel your order, and then follow through.

I'm giving A-1's web address because if you do a net search for A-1 Courier Services, which is how Amazon refers to them, you're likely to come to a company that has nothing to do with the A-1 Amazon uses.

http://www.aoneonline.com/pages/customers/amazon.html
