Dakin Farms - yum!

We're moving to Burlington - we are so psyched! Vermont is expensive, largely because so many New Yorkers are buying up real estate for vacation homes. Burlington is actually a City, tho a small one. But I wouldn't call it remote. Though there is remoteness in Vermont and if I thought we could find work out there, we might do it.

Vermont is still very progressive compared to other states, but it's a farm state and has all the struggles of familiy farmers across the country. And there's conflict between punk over-educated liberal types like me moving into the state and the dairy farmers that have lived there for generations. In fact, I'm pretty much guaranteed to be considered a New Yorker for the rest of my life there. I have a lot of respect for farmers (and people who grew up in trailers) and I think the name of the game is respecting and trying to understand each other. Not calling each other names. I think one of the reasons I like Vermont so much is because the culture is so much different and more compassionate. But it's not perfect. Nothing is.
