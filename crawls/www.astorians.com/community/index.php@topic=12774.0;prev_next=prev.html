Ahhhh! In it's heyday, when psychics were all the rage, I was raising a rather large family alone after I lost my last husband. With a very demanding full time Corporate position, and evenings and weekends doing homework while working on my MBA, cooking, doing laundry, grocery shopping, housecleaning, kissing booboos and helping the kiddies finish school projects, running to swim and track meets a second job was practically out of the question until a good friend offered me a part time job taking calls in the privacy of my own home from the pitiful who were seeking advice on their futures from a psychic. I answered questions from the callers and predicted their love lives, the gender of their babies, and I kept them on the phone for as long as I could because I got paid for the period of time they spent on the call . . . and they were paying $3.99–$6.99 per minute. I counseled young girls trying to find out who their baby daddy was to seek legal advice; gang bangers who wanted to get out of the life to seek the help of any social work program in their community; young women who had been raped to call the police and get psychological counseling; cheating husbands to give up the girlfriend unless they were prepared to let their wives find out; cheating women to get medical testing before resuming intimate relationships with their mates; a child molester to turn himself in to the police; drunks to find AA meetings . . . I heard it all over a period of 15 months. After graduation, I dedicated the same amount of time to a suicide hotline and found it just as gratifying.

Sure I felt a little guilty about scamming the public. But, I figured that if I didn't get the money, someone else would, and I needed it just as badly.

Then I found out that the sex lines paid double what I had made as a psychic . . .

When I was a kid I read palms in the play yard of PS 111 on 21st Street, after lunch. 

There were two almost identical looking structures I would pass by every day on my way to the yard - one was a drinking fountain, but the other looked like it never had the metal parts of a fountain put in - there were no holes where things might have been in the past, it was just a one-piece conglomerate basin/pedestal that didn't seem to have a purpose.  I thought about this non-purposeness every day, and one day I just started reading palms, and I would take the read-ee over to the structure and read their palms over the basin.  The non-purposeness of the structure made it seem so mysterious, it seemed the ideal spot.

I never charged anything for the reading, and nobody ever confronted me on my bullshit.
