I decided to post this, because so many people here ask this same question, and I have an email I send to friends to help out - so here it is:
The following is an email I send to my friends interested in renting in Astoria, and is based on 9 years of doing so, making mistakes, and learning from them.

It's hit or miss with Craigslist, a lot by owner is overpriced, and a lot by realtor are bait and switch to get you to call.  Newsday on Friday, Voice on Tuesday, and Sunday Times are great.  Always ask the fee, stick to a month because that's what most realtors do here, do not let them charge 12.5 % of year, if they think they can get away with it they will.  I have walked out of realtors offices over this, and found someone else who charges me a month.  Remax is okay but stress your price, they will show you stuff out of your range if they can.   I cannot stress the importance of a good landlord, they will make your living situation so much happier.

Christina Thermos - She's the realtor that got me my studio.  She is really nice and attentive.  22-77 31 St.  Astoria, NY 11105
718-204-5000
www.buysellrealestate.com

Here are two others that were nice
Alex Kouzouloglou
Metropolis Realty
718-545-9700
29-17 23rd Ave
Astoria

Global City Realty
35-10 Broadway Ste.207 Astoria, NY 11106   Telephone: 718 932-4097

I did not have a good experience with Capital or Defalco realty.

Credit checks only cost $20-30 so be careful they don't sneak an overcharge for you.  If you have your own, you might not get charged at all.  Key deposits are illegal.  I have at least 4 friends with one-bedrooms for under $1000, they do exist but are hard to find, and with luck and perseverance you will find one.  Bring your atm card, most realtors do not accept personal checks, be ready to put down a deposit if you like something, or else it will be gone.

Good luck!

Things to look for that you might want-

1. Ample closet space (at the very least, a closet in the bedrooms)
2. No electric heat, would like to have own thermostat but not necessary
3. Good access to sun and ventilation, above 1st Floor, and have view of something other than another building wall
4. Full KIT and bathroom, no efficiencies
5. Updated electrical wiring – for example, more than one 15 watt fuse for entire apartment, more than one (grounded) outlet per room
6. Safety of area
7. Proximity to transit
8. Laundry in blg?  Where?  More than one machine (if communal)?
9. Nearest grocery store
10. Thickness of surrounding walls, does all noise travel easily?
11. Rules?
12. hardwood finished floors
13.  Plumbing, condition of walls - no mildew
14.   Landlord who cares about building, and fixes things
15.  Cleanliness of blg, extermination, no roaches, etc.
