Vinegar has become a kind of magic ingredient in all sorts of cleaning ways.  I love it, and it helps that I don't mind the smell of vinegar, too (although it was quite off-putting at first).  Here are some alternatives you might find works for you

Homemade Toilet Cleaner: Mix ½ cup unscented castile soap and 2 cups baking
soda together. Dilute with ¼ cup water and 2 tbsp. Vinegar. This will make it
foam. Mix and pour into a squirt bottle. Squirt inside the toilet bowl, under the
rim and scrub with a toilet brush. Rinse and deodorize bowl with scented vinegar.

Baking soda in the bowl will also scrub out stains. Add a few drops of tea tree,
pine or eucalyptus oil for a disinfectant.
+++++

Borax might work for you also.  While mildly toxic to humans and pets, it is gentle to the environment.  I'm not completely comfortable with it but might become in the future.

Oh, and if the smell of vinegar is grody for you, here is an option.  I may do it myself.

Citrus Vinegar: Put your citrus rinds to good use. Cut lemon, orange, lime or
grapefruit rinds and put them in a jar. Cover rinds with white vinegar. Brew for
four weeks. Strain. Use your citrus vinegar in any of your cleaning recipes, or
dilute in water for a great floor cleaner, window washer, laundry
freshener/whitener, etc.
+++++

It could be fun to meet up over tea or something and chat about these things in person!  I know there was talk at one point about having a green cleaner workshop, too.
