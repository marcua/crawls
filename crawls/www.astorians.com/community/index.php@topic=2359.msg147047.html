But really, does anyone react gracefully to being admonished in public by a stranger? It's something that inevitably evokes an emotional response. One may feel wronged and indignant, or feel guilty and humiliated. It's not likely one will be sanguine, and even less likely, grateful.

If indeed you were "less than polite", the person may have concluded that you are of no better class than she is. Hence, race must be the issue.  It's probably immaterial - I don't think Americans consider their class to be inferior to any other. Most individuals calibrate their sense of what's appropriate to place themselves squarely in the center of a spectrum, with uncouth asses in one direction, and priggish asses in the other.
