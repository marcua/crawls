Title: Did Giuliani Fix NYC?
Post by: kempsternyc on October 26, 2009, 10:50:00 PM

http://www.nytimes.com/2009/10/26/nyregion/26dinkins.html?_r=1&ref=nyregion (http://www.nytimes.com/2009/10/26/nyregion/26dinkins.html?_r=1&ref=nyregion)

I moved to NYC January 2002. At that time, I had been hearing about how Giuliani had been responsible for fixing NYC. I even seem to recall that the revival of Times Square was a trophy of the Giuliani Administration. Yet, according to this article, It was the Dinkins Administration that had gotten Disney to move into the New Amsterdam Theatre. Which was, from what most people seem to say, the turning point of Times Square.

And since living here, it seems I keep hearing that many key statistics pointed to as proof started to change in the Dinkins administration.

I wanted to ask those who lived here in the 90's, how much does either argument seem true. When did it seem to turn around in the city?

Title: Re: Did Giuliani Fix NYC?
Post by: 28Grand on October 27, 2009, 01:54:46 PM
What I find most interesting in the article you posted is this quote:

“If you elect the Democratic mayoral candidate, Mr. Giuliani, the former Republican mayor, warned a mostly Orthodox Jewish audience in Brooklyn, New York could well return to a time when a feckless liberal Democrat let services decay and crime and homelessness run rampant.”

Why it’s interesting is that to those of us who lived in the city during the 90s know, it’s a not so thinly veiled indictment of Dinkin’s handling of the Crown Heights riot in 1991, which for some reason the piece neglects to mention!

Whatever good Dinkins did for the city, and as the article states there were many things, was negated by his actions, inaction really, during the Crown Heights riot – it ended his political career.

As for Times Square, the transformation didn’t happen overnight, it was years in the making. Proposals were floated even before Dinkins became mayor. Having Disney come in was a turning point though and a major achievement, which Dinkins deserves credit as does he does for the expansion of the police force.

I always thought that much of Giuliani’s success as mayor was due to being in the right place at the right time, as it often is for many people considered successful. His brash, take no prisoners attitude was exactly what was needed to bring the city under control. But as the city changed for the better, Giuliani didn’t and in the end he was just another bully, at least in my opinion.

I wish we saw more of the Giuliani of 9/11– I remember asking myself who is this person? Who knew the man had such compassion?
