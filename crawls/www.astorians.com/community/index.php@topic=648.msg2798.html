i don't know why i didn't think of this when i posted before, but cup was sold recently, probably corresponding with the change in menu and prices. so the cavo/brick owners are, i assume, not involved any more. i don't know who the new owners are because i don't care any more, but  the catering space is still there one of the managers told me the new owners don't do that stuff. and there's an art gallery (or something like it) in the building. the surprising thing about cup was that in my opinion they had the best pint of guinness around, assuming you like it very cold. surprising because it doesn't look like a good beer place. i don't do diners as much these days but i did happen to hit Michael's last night for a 3am reuben and as far as diner food goes i think it's as good as it gets around here. i also recommend their gyro deluxe.
the astoria diner on steinway used to be the Modern Age, and either before or after that I think it was called Millennium or something Y2k-esque. the tables were wobbly when i was there, and the interior looks strangely like a cross between Jimbo's on Astoria Blvd. and a Morrison's Cafeteria somewhere down south.

I have a love/hate relationship with cup. If their mac and cheese is really gone, I probably won't go back, since I love that dish. And I like it for late fall/early spring date night because I like the walk back and the cavernous space is comforting after so many tiny New York restaurants.

I would never compare it with authentic diner food, though. If I want that I'll go to Michael, or Tastee Corner (man, I love that place, and the renovation, but why the hell don't they call it Blue Sea Cafe again? So much more dignified).

However, here's something funny to do at Cup if you hate them - order the "bottomless tea". Both times I've done it, the waitress has thrown a snitfit saying I can't have bottomless tea, but it's THERE ON THE MENU, the morons, so I keep ordering it. If they've changed the menu, they might have wised up on that, but if they haven't, TRY it.

It's not that I like harassing waitstaff, but if an establishment isn't paying enough attention to realize that bottomless tea is different than coffee, they deserve me taking advantage of it.
