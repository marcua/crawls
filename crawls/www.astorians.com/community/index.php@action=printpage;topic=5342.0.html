here's what i have wanted for the 5+ years i have been living in astoria: a decent bar.

that said, i have some very specific criteria:

1. a selection of draft beer served by people who really like beer.
2. as few baseball / football watching queens thick-necks as possible.
3. music at appropriate volume levels.
4. a generous amount of space to comfortably accommodate crowds.
5. very few comfy couches or other requisite astoria 'lounge' amenities.
6. a 7-days-a-week happy hour.

i've been encouraged by the transformation of the neighborhood population in recent years, but find it disheartening to wait for the bus on ditmars and watch every new resident going to the train to go out in another neighborhood. in order for this trend to stop there has to be some amenity or genuinely unique place(s) to keep the residents here and draw others to the neighborhood (other than greek restaurants).

a recent visit to the east village / alphabet city showed me just how far we need to come. but name a desirable neighborhood and you will find a watering hole (or ten) that the residents go to 'all the time'.
