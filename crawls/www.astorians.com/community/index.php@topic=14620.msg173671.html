A friend of mine may be moving to Canada because of marriage equality issues.

Her partner is a native of Trinidad, and has been in the US on a student visa for several years.  When the visa is up, she's going to have to leave the country.  They want to get married, not just for the immigration aspect, but because they love each other and want to spend the rest of their lives together.  But without recognition on a national level of their union, even if they get married in a state that permits it, she won't be able to stay.  The rate things are progressing at the state level around the country is giving them hope that they may be able to stay here, near family and friends who love them, but it's still really up in the air.

It makes me sick that an American citizen would have to move to another country in order to have the freedom to marry the person she loves.

Don't let the form letters discourage you- your voice, as small as it may seem, does get heard. Keep writing your representatives about this issue.
