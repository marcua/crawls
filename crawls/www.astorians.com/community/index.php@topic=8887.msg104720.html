The REALITY is that in Europe, Germany, France, the two heads of the so-called 'Anti-War' movement recently elected decidedly Pro-War and Pro-USA leaders. Anyone can now see that the war is turning the tide and the extremists are on the run. The only ones who refuse to see this are people who are tired of war, understandable, and unrepetent Anti-Semites like Pat Buchanan, who have teamed up in an 'unholy' alliance of assorted America haters, righties and lefties. In the end, we have no choice, but, to fight this war. Retreat is surrender. My nephew is in Iraq, and I want him home NOW, but, when I send him articles of what is being written and shown from Hollywood/media about his and his fellow soldiers efforts, he is angry and disgusted and tells me how it bears little to reality. Yes, there have been lies in this war, from the media, like the Museum 'looting' story early on, and how the whole war was based WMD's in Iraq, and not the 17 resolutions Saddam violated. How the incidents in Abu Ghraib were the norm and not the exception, and finally a slap in the face of tens of millions of Americans who elected Bush TWICE and the media cast doubt and disparaged it. The President did the right thing, and I believe history will prove it.

With regardss to war, I am not a pacifist at all.  For me, war is strictly a cost/benefit analysis:  Will the engagement of war result in the best possible outcome for a nation given the circumstances?  Will the benefits of global positioning and access to natural resources outweight the cost of dollars, life, global goodwill and blowback?  If the answer is yes, I support war.  If not, I am against it.  Keep in mind, even if it is beneficial, the CBA of diplomacy, as well as United Nations actions must be entered into calculations, as if they are more beneficial than war, then I am against war. 

As it stands now, I see the balance of this war decidely in the red.

Just as you seem to be a realist in this matter, not giving over to emotion, neither am I.  Human right violations on the part of Saddam play no part in my calculations, given that we are active allies with dictatorships who have engaged in human rights violations.

I am open to change my mind should I be shown in a logical manner that this war is beneficial to this country.  I can appreciate your opening post as a way to bring up the subject matter, but it is not logical nor compelling.  So, as Agent Kujan said to Verbal Kint, "Convince me".
