The atmosphere is a strange mix...it's a deli by day, so you could be sitting next to a refrigerated case, but somehow they manage to pull it off with elegance. The whole place has a sort of rustic elegance to it--venetian plaster, overflowing candles, that sort of thing. I wouldn't call it home style but it's not fancy. I was there in jeans and a hoodie and wished I'd at least had a decent blouse and non-sneaker shoes, but I didn't feel like I was getting the eye or anything, and the server was very attentive and friendly without being overbearing. The (owner? manager?) lives in Astoria, as did our server, and they were excited to hear that we were all neighbors.

I went to Sac's last week.  It was OK - not great.  I thought it was pricey for pasta in Queens.  They have really tasty pizza-like bread in their bread basket - gorged myself on it.

I felt like I was sitting in a diner that was pretending to be a nice restaurant.  I didn't try the pizza itself - I hear it's fantastic.  I had rigatoni with tomato and basil with 2 meatballs which were fatty tasting.  My friends had linguini with seafood in a white wine garlic sauce - it seemed like a good deal - there was mucho seafood and it was $22 - but apparently it had no flavor.

I want something rustic and charming (oh, and delicious, too) - is there anything like that here in Astoria?

Plus, I felt like I was on the set of the Soprano's - the table next to us was TOTALLY mobbed up.
