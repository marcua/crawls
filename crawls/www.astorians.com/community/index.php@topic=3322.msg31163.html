Lucid Dreaming
« on: September 09, 2006, 09:28:06 PM »
Okay this may sound strange, but I've just been reading a really interesting website that talks about lucid dreaming. It's basically when you train yourself to become "aware" during your dreams, remember them, and then try and interact with them. It sounds really cool!

I'm wondering if anyone has ever done this before, had a dream where they became aware that they were dreaming and interacted with their dream. After reading the instructions on that website I think I'm going to try their suggestions. One really interesting concept is "dream spinning", where if you are having a dream you want to change you just "spin" and when you stop spinning the scenery has usually changed. There are tons of other interesting tips there.

I think I'm going to try and keep a journal in an attempt to become aware of what I dream. Anyone have any dream experiences to share?

edit after I typed this all out: Gah, I've obviously spent too much time thinking about this...

I used to do this when I was a teenager, maybe 14 or 15. It was probably because I was always reading about it, and my friends were way into astrology and whatnot (not that they're the same things, but they were in the same section at the bookstore :) ) I used to be so good at it that if I wanted to interact with a specific person in my dream, I'd say to myself something like "Johnny Depp will walk through that door now, and we're gonna be at 6 Flags and go on roller coasters" and there he'd be. But honestly, for me, the absolute best thing to do lucid dreaming is to fly.

I don't remember exactly how I trained myself to do it. Tthe fact that I read about it a lot and talked to my friends about it probably had a lot to do with it, because for me, a lot of my everyday life gets filtered into my dreams so at some point during my dream, I'd become aware that I was dreaming. One technique I remember was to ask yourself whenever you remember, "am I dreaming?" and then to test yourself. If you're dreaming, you may be able to force strange things to happen just by "willing" them to happen. (A specific thing I remember was to try to make the time on a clock change...if you look at it and it says 10:00, look away and "force" it to be something else when you look back.) However, I did get to one point when I did this back in the day where I would ask myself if I was dreaming, and since my answer when awake was always automatically, "no, I'm awake," it became my answer in my dream.

You know how in dreams, completely bizarre things seem totally normal? I think the key to lucid dreaming is REALLY thinking about what's going on around you, and when you realize that something strange isn't normal, you'll realize that you're dreaming.

Despite the fact that I've become lucid in my dreams a few times in the past couple years, I've never been able to take advantage of it - I usually start thinking something like "AWESOME! Now I'm gonna go fly and do this and that and hang out with this person, and its going to rule..." and I get so excited that I wake myself up.

Lucid dreaming is something academically reported and still being studied, actually.
Specific to what you're talking about, Merm, a recent study in the Peer Reviewed Journal "Dreaming" reviewed techniques for inducing lucid dreams. The study is small, only 20 participants, so they admit the limitations of the study straight of the bat but according to the abstract, the researchers found that the reflection-intention technique (using a journal) was to some degree effective. researchers again note that the study sample size was small and it would have to be repeated in similar conditions repeatedly until a large enough sample size was attained before any conclusions could be reached...

here's the reference (from PsychInfo, which I am thankful to still have access to as an academic nerd):
Paulsson, Timo; Parker, Adrian. The Effects of a Two-Week Reflection-Intention Training Program on Lucid Dream Recall. [References]. [Journal; Peer Reviewed Journal] Dreaming. Vol 16(1) Mar 2006, 22-35.

I did indeed wake up and write down 8 separate dreams, and there were an additional 4 dreams that I woke up but was too lazy to write down. I had no idea I dreamed so much. On most I just wrote a one liner and went back to sleep evidently.

1. Praying in a Home Depot store (I don't remember this dream at all)
2. Going to Walmart and discovering when I was inside the store I had to hold my breath. (Don't remember it. But scribbled on my paper nonetheless.)
3. Sitting on a committee discussing the various logs running on this server. We were arguing over the logs. (I barely remember this one)
4. I had forgotten the SMTP "address" for my mail server and so I had to go and meet with my mom and dad and wife to get them to tell me what the SMTP address was for the server. (Don't remember this)

Next I had a longer dream that I do remember better (after looking at my notes)

5. I decided that something had to be done to let Bush know that the war in Iraq was a big mistake. So I just traveled back in time to let him know. Somehow I got a meeting with him and I was trying to convince him that I really was from the future and that if he went to war in Iraq that it would be really bad for our country. After I was convinced he finally heard me I went into a nearby church and started whispering to everyone in the pews the same thing.

The next thing I knew I was on some bridge and I came across a kid sitting there who I recognized as the inventor of some really popular open-source piece of software. He was all depressed sitting there, so I told him that he shouldn't give up because he was going to be famous one day and that his work would have a good impact on the world. I still don't remember what software he was supposed to develop.

6. Next I dreamed that after the dream I told you above that I woke up and discovered my night stand and lamp was all broken to pieces and the pen I was trying to write the dream down on wasn't working. So I stood up out of bed and stepped on some glass. Then I thought I heard someone in my house. This dream was quickly becoming a nightmare so I woke up for real and wrote down this dream, and number 5.

7. Next I dreamed that my mom called me (from the past) and told me that she was having a hard time watching me (as a baby) and that she needed me to come and babysit myself. So I traveled through time (again, which didn't seem like any big deal) and I babysat myself for an afternoon. I remember thinking what a smart baby I was because I (as a baby) was talking and I couldn't even walk yet. I tried to think of what advice I could give myself, but I don't remember if I did give myself any advice.

8. I'm in some kind of big compound where there's all these buildings and some kind of communal dwelling. But outside there's an airshow going on with all these stunt pilots doing really crazy maneuvers. Like cutting the grass with their propellers and stuff. I get the sense that a bomb is going to go off in the building imminently, and then I wake up.

9. I then have notes that say "false awake". I'm guessing that I was dreaming that I was waking up, but not really awake.

I wasn't lucid in any of my dreams though last night. I totally thought everything was normal, even time traveling. I'm going to do it again tonight and see what I dream. It's strange to me that I'm dreaming about Walmart, Home Depot, and churches so much. I seldom go to any of them.
