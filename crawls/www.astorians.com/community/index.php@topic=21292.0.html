After reading these posts, I decided to check it out myself.  Foul smell coming from backyard.  Don't know if perhaps homeless people have tried to seek refuge there.  It is completely accessible, but will all the junk and rotting garbage piled up everywhere, I can't imagine anyone actually WANTING to go back there.  The windows facing the backyard were completely covered, with the exception of the top left window.  Someone placed a painting of a lady looking out towards the backyard.  Very creepy. 

Around the front, it looks just as abandoned.  Windows covered, however, there is a glass door on the right which is partially broken and missing a large piece. A curtain was hung in it's place, but that wouldn't protect it from the harsh elements (rainstorm, snowstorm, etc.), which means that there probably is a significant amount of damage to floor and walls surrounding that area. The front doors have junk piled up in front of both of them and neither look like they have been touched for some time. I did see the car parked there, though, which is kind of odd.

While I was checking this out, some lady walked past me carrying grocery bags and jokingly asked me if I wanted to buy it.  I told her that I didn't think so, but was curious about the house since I had heard some things about it.  I asked her if she knew anyting about the house or the former/current occupants.  She sort of half shrugged and said there used to be a lady who lived there with 2 big dogs.  I wanted to ask her more, but she seemed in a hurry so I just let it go. 

Would love to find out more about that places and see what it looks like inside. I'll probably never get the chance though. At least, not without bringing an exorcist, a Rabbi, a santero, a couple of big guys with wooden stakes, etc.. LOL

Maybe this is obvious, but I'll say it anyway: Anyone poking around a "haunted" house should be veeeeery careful.


While tales of ghosts, poltergeists and the like in old houses are campfire hogwash, the weird phenomena reported at these sites often have very real, earthly causes -- and those causes can be very dangerous:


Rotting floors and ceilings that creak spookily -- and then collapse suddenly.


Pockets of gas from decaying dead things, leaking utilities, etc., that produce bad smells and strange glows -- and then explode, or poison or smother human visitors.


Feral animals (or people) that emit mournful cries -- and then attack you for invading their territory.


One can also get arrested for trespassing.
