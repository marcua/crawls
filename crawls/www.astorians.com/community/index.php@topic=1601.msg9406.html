Quote from: annie on February 17, 2006, 01:51:26 PM
Yeeeeeeah I totally thought the same thing-- but admittedly I've lost faith in NYC's ability to procure anything rare for me after seeing what people out here will call a fish taco. Damn, people, its supposed to be a CORN tortilla and no its not supposed to cost $12 at Rosa Mexicana or whatever!

Now I'm a little confused.  Every fish taco I've ever had in Baja (and it's quite a lot  8-)) has been in a flour tortilla, not a corn tortilla, and restaurants in the small towns where I have spent time automatically serve flour tortillas as well.  The taquerias generally offer both flour and corn, and consumption seems to be split about 50-50.  Because of Baja's unique history, flour tortillas are more common than corn, unlike the rest of Mexico - and that includes back on remote ranches, where I've had homemade flour tortillas made fresh as I watched.   BTW, the area I'm talking about stretches from San Ignacio (south of Guerrero Negro) to Ciudad Insurgentes, a little north of Constitucion -- all in Baja California Sur. 

Maybe it's different closer to the border?
