I remember the Woolworth's at Steinway & 31st Avenue, they did have a lunch counter, during the lunch rush. I recall it was often times hard to find empty stools. They did close it down a few years before the store closed. At the candy section they had a popcorn machine and made snow cones, also they had and a pet store section in the back. I used to spend most of my time in the toy section when my mom was busy shopping around. Simple pleasures.

A had read recently that the Woolworth stores which are still open in the UK are now closing. The Kmarts and H&M stores have taken away too much of their regular shoppers.

Wow!  Thanks for all the Woolworth's memories!  I remember the one on Steinway and 31st Avenue - they had the best frankfurters, on a split-top toasted roll, and a small Snyder's Orange Drink in the paper cone cup...  mmmmmm!

Thanks all for jogging my memory.  The parakeets and the jumping beans - LOL!  I remember them.  They also had a great record section for albums and 45's - bought most of my Beatles and Stones albums there. 

I remember they had those chrome bells with the button on top, on each aisle to call for a salesperson.  What kid couldn't resist that?  And a great fabric and yarn section in the back where my Mom went to get stuff to make clothes and blankets.  And rainbow colored yarn for shawls, mittens and stuff.  What different times.  They had a machine with a clock-like dial where the salesperson would run the fabric through to measure the lengths.  Each section of the store had its own smell and feel to it.

I miss that place alot!  Whew!  Thanks, guys...

When did Woolworth's close?   If it was before '87 I would have missed it.  I do miss Woolworth's, though.  It was one of those stores that would have anything.   They stocked a little of everything.   They even sold pets!  You cold buy tropical fish there.   They had a record department.   They were all over New York City back in the 1980s and 1990s.   They also were a national chain and we had many of them in Rhode Island, where I grew up.

I remember other national chains that went defunct when I was a kid.   Was there ever a W.T. Grant's in Astoria?   And there was an Atlantic Department Store chain.   I don't know if that was local or national.  First National/Finest and A&P were the two big grocery store chains.  And did you have supermarkets that dispensed S&H Green Stamps.   When I was a kid you would get these green stamps if you shopped at the Almacs supemarket chain.   You would put them in books and then when you had enough of them you could redeem them for a gift.   The ladies who worked in the S&H Green Stamps store in my town -- and they were all ladies -- would go over that book with a fine tooth comb.  If you skipped a page, fuhgetabout.
