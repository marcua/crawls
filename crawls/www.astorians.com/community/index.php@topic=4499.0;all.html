Funny little anecdote...
My great-grandfather fled to America from Europe fleeing religious persecution at the beginning of the last century.  He went to temple every day, studied Jewish scripture, and led his life with his faith as a fundamental backbone.  But, he loved this country and he went out of his way to be accepted as an American.  He celebrated Christmas.  A devout Jew, he didn't do this because Christmas was a Christian holiday, he definitely did not believe in Jesus as his saviour, he did it because it was an American holiday.  Over the years there has been a waxing and waning to the emphasis of its Christianity, but its roots will always lie in paganism.  December 25th was originally the day pagans celebrated the winter solstice.  The pope who declared it to be the day of christ's birth did so to make it easier for the pagan Romans to convert.
