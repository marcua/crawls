I'm a human guinea pig for playing all 1000000 games of Microsoft Freecell.  I'm up to game 135.  I keep track of what games I played on an Excel spreadsheet because the sheet is numbered on the left, to the side of the actual spreadsheet.  The spreadsheet itself I have decorated so I have something other than blank cells to look at.  I took a look at the spreadsheet for the first 100 games to see how many kb the spreadsheet was using up, to see if I was going to have to eventually move it off my hard drive.  The MB count for 1000000 games was so high that I realized I would have to periodically Delete Contents of the previous 100 games to keep the KB count low.  (I can't simply delete previous rows or I would lose the count at the left hand side.)  By deleting contents of previous games I lose the visual impact of having the decorations for all 1000000 games, but I figured, am I really going to scroll up 1000000 rows to look at them?  No.

My decorations on the spreadsheet look something like this, and are repeated every 10 games, changing color schemes every 50.  On the actual spreadsheet the thing that looks like an a with a degree symbol over it is a Webdings martini glass.  On this copy & paste the right side martini glasses are out of alignment.  They should be in a straight column, and some of the card symbols are out of alignment.  The whole thing is actually symetrical.
