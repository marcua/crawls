There is a distinction between movies made in Astoria and movies made and set in Astoria.  Under the Hellgate Bridge, Astoria and  A Guide to Recognizing Your Saints would fall into the latter category.

I'm surprised no one has mentioned Kiss of Death, from the late 1940s that featured Richard Widmark as the pyscho of all pyschos.   In the film's most famous scene, Widmark throws a woman in a wheelchair down a flight of stairs.   In any event, Victor Mature, the film's star, lives in Astoria in the movie.   They filmed some exterior shots of Mature's apartment building and they used an apartment on one of the streets just south of Astoria Park because you can see the park and the bridge in the background.
