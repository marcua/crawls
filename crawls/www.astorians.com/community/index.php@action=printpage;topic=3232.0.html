Title: Re: An Astoria Party, Of Sorts
Post by: nycatexas on September 08, 2006, 12:51:38 AM
It's real hard......

Breakfast sausage
Bisquick
Grated cheddar, preferably Longhorn
Chopped green chilies

Eyeball everything till texture is right. Roll em up, not too big, good space in between cause they expand, bake at 350, don't burn em, cool and eat em. Feel free to sub jalapenos or any of the other exotic things you foodies mention  - y'all could probably turn these Texas balls into an international smorgasbord of balls!

I like mine cold and chewy but most people like them warmed up. Sorry, I don't think HGS wants to nuke my balls so you'll have to eat them cold. I like my balls plain but some like hot mustard. I hope Uncle Telly reads this post....

Yeah Lanseaux Jimmy Dean has probably caused more Texas heart attacks than any other brand....from this little town near Amarillo - Plainview. Yum it is so good and spiced just right!

This is what makes it a little of a pain, and really only worth doing in large batches. It's a bit of a process at first getting the consistency right, but I could make them in my sleep, I've just never measured it.
In a BIG bowl you just squeeze the sausage out of the wrapper, add cheese, chilies. Mix this well before you add the 'Quick, mix again thoroughly. You have to get your hands in it.
