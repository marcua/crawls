 PIZZA STONES -   A baking stone should be placed on the lowest oven shelf and preheated with the oven. Once pre-heated, the stone evenly transfers intense heat to the food being cooked, ensuring a particularly crisply baked base.

As to if it might crack, this I do not know, as I have never had it happen. I always place the cold pizza stone in a cold oven. I then turn on the oven to 400 degrees F. to preheat oven and stone. Once the stone is hot, I do not remove it from the oven. I place the pizza on the hot stone in the oven. Following is my tip that I use when making pizza and using the pizza stone:

 PIZZA TIP - A common problem is that the topped pizza sticks to the baker's peel. Giving the peel a quick jerk may work, but it may also jerk your toppings off the pizza as well. A helpful hint is to put a piece of parchment paper on the peel instead of using cornmeal. I place the rolled-out pizza dough on the parchment paper and then add the toppings. The parchment goes into the oven with the pizza. This makes it easier to slide the pizza off the peel and onto the hot baking stone.

CLEANING PIZZA STONES - Think of your pizza baking stone as a sponge; it will soak up everything put on it. These "stones" are actually molded sand, tightly compacted under high pressure. Like sand on the beach, they will suck in any liquid exposed to the surface. Anything else in the water -- including soap -- goes right into the stone. Manufacturers warn you to use only clear, plain water to clean a baking stone.

First, completely submerge your baking stone in warm, clear, plain water for 15-20 minutes. This should thoroughly saturate the stone with clean water and dilute the soap residue. Next, remove the stone from the water and place it on a pie cooling rack on your kitchen counter. Allow the stone to dry completely overnight.

Repeat the same soaking and drying process five or six nights in a row. After the last round, bake some pizza dough on the stone. If the pizza stone still smells, I'm afraid your stone is destined for the trash

Hi all,

I'm noticing a new trend on this board that I'd like to comment about. It's that whereas we used to be able to have intelligent conversations on the board, lately people have started using personal attacks and name calling to try and get their point across. People are calling each-other trolls and it appears that they actually think that they are "right" enough in their opinions that it makes it okay for them to act immature and name call.

I want to be clear on what a troll is: it's anyone that resorts to name calling and provocation in an attempt to try and make themselves look right. What does that mean? It means, that if you read someone's post and interpret it as a personal attack on you, that this does not give you a license to mimic what you see as their immaturity. Yet on three separate occasions in the last week we've had conversations go from normal, intelligent, adult conversations, to immature high-school level shouting matches.

What makes this worse is that the normal even-tempered and intelligent people of this board are chiming in from the sidelines and provoking people to go even further into their rants. We have already lost more than one member in the last week because of this and it is of concern to me.

It seems to me that if we are to foster a sense of community, both online and offline we're going to have to cut people a little slack. That also means that those of us that are the most intelligent, and who most loudly shout the values of tolerance and inclusiveness are going to have to start practicing what we preach a little better. It means that if you perceive that someone is attacking you personally that you are going to just have to try and be the bigger person and let it slide, and respond NOT in kind, but in kindNESS and in the context of reason and intellect.

Moderating this board is becoming a burden because as a group we are sliding into intolerance and immaturity. The moderators shouldn't be having to get "resignation" letters from members, and "reported posts", and "moderation requests" so often from adults.

I'd like to request that we all take it up a notch or two and try and do three things:
1. Give each other the benefit of the doubt, don't assume someone is "attacking" you and you have to defend yourself or lose your reputation - you lose your reputation through your immaturity and by mimicking troll-like behavior, not through your ideas.
2. Do not think that just because someone else "started it", that you somehow have a license to lose your sanity and lay into someone. Responding in-kind to a troll doesn't fix the problem, it makes two trolls. If you can't engage someone in intelligent and restrained conversation then don't.
3. Do not egg people on, or otherwise enable people who have lost their mind and started slinging mud. Posting to simply say that you agree with one of the trolls is like standing on the sidelines yelling "fight, fight". Please don't do it. If you have something intelligent to contribute please do so however.

Overall I'd like to remind people that this site isn't a "club" or a clique, it's a community. You may not get to choose your neighbors in a community, but you do get to choose what kind of neighborhood you create. I prefer a tolerant and intelligent one.

That being said it's important also for me to express that I'm going to personally be looking at ways I may have contributed to this atmosphere, and will be trying to address any ways that I can help prevent it in the future.

Thanks.
