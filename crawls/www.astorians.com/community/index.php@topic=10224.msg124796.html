Quote from: Pharaduse on April 21, 2008, 11:46:14 PM
Cool.  What's the general rule of thumbs when it comes to sharing?  I mean you know YOU are dependable....what right's do I have knowing whether the person I'm shacking up with is cool?  Or is this an all risk sort of agreement?

Usually you email someone a few times. Introduce yourself. Tell them the kinds of things about yourself that you want to know about them. After an email or two, you'll probably go to check the place out. Pay attention to the vibe you get there. You can usually tell.

When I was looking for sublets, I asked people to meet me at a bar they recommended in their neighborhood for a drink before we went to see their place. It made me feel better to meet someone somewhere else, though you might want to pick coffee. I also knew, though, that anyone who wouldn't be cool with a mid-week early-evening beer is probably not the kind of person who'd want to live with me. There's lots of ways that you can tell, just figure out what's important to you and start meeting people.

FYI- if you move into a place that's rent stabilized your house mate(s) cannot charge you more than the pro-rata share- like if it's a two bedroom and the rent is 1800, they are supposed to not charge you more than 900 for 1 bedroom- illegal to do so otherwise.
But if it is rent controlled they can charge you whatever- go figure. (Source- nyc rent stabilization FAQs on the web)
good luck.
