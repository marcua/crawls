Taxes I get... but that $15 fee for delivery and "maintenance" of their delivery system?  Isn't that supposed to be already covered by their rates?  To my knowledge, conEd doesn't create all of the energy it supplies... it also buys energy from other sources and forwards it onto us (like wholesale to retail), at a cost increase.

Logically, one would think that the profit margin of internal costs vs consumer costs is what a business uses to maintain itself.  The fact that they charge an additional monthly fee for maintenance and delivery that is typically 3x higher than my cost of consumption seems like this is just a blatant way of screwing the consumer for not sucking the gas lines dry.

I doubt conEd or any other business would be so altruistic as to sell it's product at a loss for the company's needs.  I would be happier if they raised my rates and dropped all the bullshit fees.  I'd rather pay $15 for consumption alone, than $5+$15 for consumption and fees.

And although I get the taxes part of the equation, I don't get why the taxes alone are sometimes more than my cost as well.  I have to assume that the taxes are not solely based on my usage... and therefore I must also be paying taxes on conEd's fees.  Hence the reason why the government has no interest in controlling any of these costs for us.  It's a very screwed system with the consumer always on the bottom.
