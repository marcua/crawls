It's okay. I'm not so much worried about getting in trouble for defamation of character, there's actually a legal precedence that says that ISPs/websites are not responsible for what people post online anyway. The person who says the things are responsible. However my real concern is that since the individuals that are being talked about are probably not members here they don't really have a chance to respond or answer any charges levied against them (as individuals). For this reason I think it's better if things are kept on a more professional level (for the sake of everyone).

However, if you feel you have a legitimate complaint about how a specific business, or even your building is managed, I think it's okay if you tell your story here and give recommendations to other people. As long as it's not "John Doe is a loser". Instead, I would recommend you make specific recommendations, or talk about your building in general, or even your apartment and its location if you wish. You could also write about your experience in any way you please on your own personal blog, and then mention the blog post on this board (again, without naming names).

It's just the idea of creating a public forum here where individuals are "charged" and then not given the chance to respond that concerns me. One of the coolest things about the internet is that we can learn from each-other and be smart consumers, but it can also just be a way to backstab or slander someone without their knowledge. Since posts on this board are permanant and I have no intention of deleting them or letting them expire I think it's best if we try not to just use the board to sling mud (rightly or wrongly) at individuals (unless they consent to a public discussion, in which case I'd welcome it).

So yes, feel free to share stories, advice, and other information - just try and not make it personal unless your "opponent" wants to join in the discussion.
