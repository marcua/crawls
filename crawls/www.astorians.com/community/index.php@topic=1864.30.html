Add me to the list of those who don't like the Astoria Diner. I had eggs benedict there back in April of this year - figured I'd try them out. It was pretty bad. The sauce tasted vinegary!

Actually, I've tried that diner several times over the years when it'd changed owners a few times. I kept hoping someone would take it over and do it right, but that's yet to happen.

I have a theory the location is cursed since about twenty years ago when a set of new owners wrecked the gorgeous classic original Art Deco exterior (it used to be called the Modern Age Diner then) when they did a cheap tacky faux Mediterranean remodeling job. It broke my heart when I saw what they did to it! How I regret I never shot some photographs of it while it was still in its original Art Deco glory.
The only bigger crime against Art Deco is what happened to the old Narden Fragrances factory down by the railroad tracks at 42nd Street.

Anyway, I flat out refused to set foot in that diner out of principle while the people who wrecked it were running it. When it was later taken over by new management about eight years ago, I gave it another try and was not impressed. Now the newest owners seem to be keeping right with the new tradition of not very good food.
It's cursed, I tell ya!  :?
