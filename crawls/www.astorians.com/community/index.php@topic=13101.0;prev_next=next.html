I don't know anything except what was forwarded to me below. I thought maybe someone might be interested:


A foundation called the Thomas and Jeanne Elmezzi Private Foundation
services the Tri-State area and New York City. Lately, the foundation
is looking to hire a community activist to do some grassroots work in
"Long Island City/ Astoria, particularly in the most disadvantaged zip
codes of 11101, 11102, and 11106". We're to look for community
changes in 12 - 18 months!

Interestingly, the reason Tom and Jeanne left behind a $5 million
foundation that gives $400,000 in grants per year, and is now focusing
on LIC and Astoria, is because:

Tom at age 16 worked for a boss who was very savvy and bought the
rights to the Pepsi Cola company in 1931, after it went bankrupt in
Virginia. The headquarters were then located in the industrial
neighborhood of Long Island City. Two years later, at age 18, Tom
streamlined the formula for the syrup in Pepsi Cola, was the man
behind Diet Pepsi Cola, and readjusted the formula again 1951 to match
consumer's tastes. He also helped introduce Pepsi Cola to other
countries like Mexico.


Love 'em or hate 'em, Tom Elmezzi made a lot of money and made a lot
of differences.
