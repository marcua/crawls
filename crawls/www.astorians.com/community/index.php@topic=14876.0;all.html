Miriam's Southern Cuisine
« on: May 05, 2009, 11:56:03 AM »
Just stopped in to Miriam's Southern Cuisine on 21st Street just south of Broadway.  It's a restaurant in that little strip mall on the opposite side of 21st from White Castle.  Seen the storefront a few times walking by - and knew from the first time I'd seen it - that it had to be investigated further... mmm... chicken and ribs...
A trip to a new pediatrician yesterday - Dr Diane Liu at Mt Sinai on 21st Street - who btw gets a good review from us for the kid set even though we waited over an hour in the waiting room (why do you always have to wait so long at Dr's offices??) provided me with an opportunity to check it out.
Pushing our 5 month old by Miriam's it was pretty empty - not a good sign for any restaurant - and I was a little hesitant - but cravings for the possibility of good southern cooking won out.
Walked in - took a look at the menu - fried chicken, smothered chicken, pork and beef ribs, oxtails, etc..  All looke good.  Decided on the fried chicken and pork ribs - each which came with 2 sides - collard greens, mac and cheese and candied yams were the choices - and bam - quickly I came away with a heavy bag full of goodies for under $20.
When I got home - the food was understandably a little cold - but as I opened my order of pork ribs - I found 3 generous size ribs - slathered in what looked like and was a deliciously tangy bbq sauce.  I started with the mac and cheese and greens - and was intrigued - certainly miles better than that watery fast food mac and cheese you get from Popeye's or KFC - and the greens though a little soggy from the travel were tangy - and well - if you like collard greens they were good.
But the pork ribs - were unbelievable - fall off the bone - meaty, and delicious.  I'm not the best at food you eat with your hands - especially ribs - I've seen people who seem to be able get every piece of possible meat off a rib - doesn't seem to happen when I'm done - but in this case...  I had to get every succulent piece of rib meat off that bone - and it complied with tender juicy ease.
The fried chicken was not bad either - definitely a bit more worse for the wear in the 15 minutes it took to get back home - but those pork ribs - I will be back for more.

Interested in seeing if anyone else has tried Miriam's - I'll certainly be back for the ribs and may give some of their other options a try.  My recommendation - run don't walk if you have a hankering for some tender juicy rib action.
