Finally, bloody, bruised and beaten down.  I made it to work a half hour late.

I took a bus that runs down 30th Avenue (where I normally pick up the N) and then turns down 31st St. under the elevated tracks and stops at QB Plaza.  I didn't even know this bus existed.  My original plan, when I learned the subway wasn't running, was to walk down to 21st St., pick up the bus there and take it to LIC where I could pick up the E or the 7, but as I was walking by a bus stop I noticed this bus and hopped on.   By the time it got to the corner of 31st St. and 30th Ave., it was full.   

In an era a budget shortfalls, I just wonder why the transit authority is running a bus that goes under the subway tracks.  Isn't that a duplication of effort?   Why not stop the bus at 30th Avenue and let people take the N, though now that I think about it I got off the bus and picked up the E at Queens Plaza.   What the transit system should do is find a way to connect QB Plaza (N, Q 7) with Queens Plaza (E, F, M).   Now that would be a good idea.

Things like this happen.  That I can accept.  But this is what REALLY upsets me.....

With the N train out, the Q69 bus now becomes more packed as people travel to the Queensbridge train station.  Along the same route though, the Q100 express bus is still working.  But while I'm waiting at 21st Street, about five Q100 buses pass by - VIRTUALLY EMPTY - and DO NOT BOTHER TO STOP AND PICK UP PASSENGERS ALONG THE Q69 ROUTE!!!!

This makes absolutely no sense!!!  THe bus is traveling the same route as the Q69, there's no reason I can tell that the bus drivers can't stop and pick up some additional passeners; or even alter it's route to go through Ditmars and take some of the load off of the Q69 train.  It would help eleviate congestion, and the MTA would make life easier for a lot fo people.  While I see the bus dispatcher at the corner of Ditmars and 31st Street, it doesn't occur to him to tell the Q100 operators to stop along the Q69 route and then direct passengers to the buses. 

I hope Peter Valone is reading this.  This is a quick fix in an emergency situation that takes little effort to undertake.  There's no added cost to this move and no added buses to find. 

Please, contact the MTA board and let them know that in an emergency situation, they need to make this happen.
