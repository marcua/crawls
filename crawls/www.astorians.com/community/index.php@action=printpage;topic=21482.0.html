Title: Psychedelic Freecell
Post by: Starwind51 on August 13, 2011, 11:25:46 PM
A couple of years ago I mentioned that it was my intention to play all the games of Freecell in almost chronological order.  What I do is, for every 10 games, I play the game that ends in a 0, then the preceding game that ends in 1, and play the eight other games in between in any order.  To keep track of what I've played I use an Excel grid, since the left side of the grid has numbers in consecutive order, starting with 1 and presumably going to infinity.  I only need it to go to 1,000,000.  Originally I decorated the grid with symbols representing the 4 card suits - hearts, diamonds, clubs, spades, in their proper colors, and used a martini glass symbol as a marker next to the actual game numbers that I played (and won).  I kept this grid open as a background for the game as I played it.  After a while I started making the symbols other colors, and later started making the whole grid a color.  (The grid by itself is not considered to have a color.  Making it white is different from leaving it "unfilled".)  Every 50 games I change the grid color, and/or the symbol colors.  This summer, in sweet remembrance of having seen the Monkees twice, at the Beacon Theater and outdoors at Coney Island, I started making the grid black, representing the darkened theater/night sky, and using red hearts, representing the 6 hearts in their guitar logo, to mark the played games.  Often I use a mix of the martini glass and the heart icons to mark the played games, and now, in addition to red hearts I use a psychedelic mix of heart colors.  Groovy.  The Wikipedia says there are 8 known unsolvable games in the Windows XP 1,000,000 Freecell version.  So far I have encountered 2 games that I spent so much time on I had to make a note to come back to and move on in my efforts.  I have seen reference to one of the them that confirms it is winnable.  Currently I am in the 6500's, and my grid look like this:
