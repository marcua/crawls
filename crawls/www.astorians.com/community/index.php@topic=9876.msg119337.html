I vote Opa! I don't know much about out-of-town parents, but I have noticed that just about every single weekend evening I've gone to Opa, there is one table that has exactly that; some 20-something Astorian with their out-of-town parents and siblings. The waitress seems to love that type of thing, too, as I've seen her explain the different dishes, just ask her to recommend something, tell her that you're parents are visiting and they're not familiar with Greek food, and she will totally help! Personally, I would recommend the Saganaki for an appetizer, it's fried cheese (one big thing piece) dosed in rum and flambéed in front of you, so it's exciting and delicious. And the lamb there is to DIE for. They have fish, steak, burgers, and best of all - kebab.

I sound like a commercial. Did I mention its awesome conversation atmosphere?

31st between 30th ave and newtown road, near zodiac.
