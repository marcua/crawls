I like the little Bangladeshi place across Ditmars from J & T (which I also like).  There are a lot of great finds there for dried spices and herbs, dried beans, that sort of thing.  Also Bangladeshi/Indian/Pakistani snacks and condiments.  I recently got mango pickle there -- the very salty pickled condiment you get in Indian restaurants - it's got chili, fenugreek, fennel, etc. in it.  Very tasty.  (Total aside - I love the translation on this particular jar -- the ingredients are listed in a number of languages, and the French translation comes out as "Mango in gasoline" - they chose pétrole instead of huile when translating the word oil.)

I also love the end caps at Trade Fair (Ditmars/38th).  Each one is a different country/region, and you can find some interesting things.

Mediterannean Foods II, in the same weird shopping center as Artopolis (31st St. near 23rd Ave.), is also good for all the Greek stuff.  They also have frozen quail, for some reason.

Heliopolis is a little deli near Ommonia on Broadway (at 33rd). It used to be dingy but in the last year, these Indian brothers have taken it over and transformed the space into a bright, welcoming interior.

Its got lower Astoria's best selection of belgian ales (all the Lindemann's lambics!), homemade dips (the tzaziki and baba ganoush are really good), and sandwiches and the Indian brothers make a pretty darn good spanakopita. Of course, a mini olive bar and all the nuts in big jars in the front. There are also exotic condiments like "Heinz Exotic Sauce" on the shelves in the back. Their stock of  Mediterranean brand pita is always the freshest, I find.

I tried the new French bakery/patisserie on Broadway by 34th?  Very good baguette.  Didn't not try the desserts.  

30th ave:
Eleniki? for produce between 32nd / 33rd streets.  very good selection  and price.

Grocer/produce corner of 35th and 30th ave.  Excellent produce, Some interesting exotic produce choices. Ritter's chocolates. They are starting to carry some gourmet items such as goat cheese, spreads, soy products, etc...

Mediterranean on 34th just south of 30th ave for all things Greek. About 12 feta's, all types of olives, spreads, dips, smoked fish, tinned items, syrups, honeys, etc...

Astoria bakery 33rd/34th streets for great range of breads.

Olympus Bagels  33rd/ 34th streets.  Bagels. very good.

Dave and Tony Salumeria for fresh mozz, brick oven breads from  Brooklyn, pastas, sausage, ravioli, Prosciutto and Italian cheeses/meats. Ask for Joe or Ralph.
by corner of 36th street.

Franks bakery for decent croissants, very good Italian bread, very very good pignoli cookies and meringue cookies.
corner of 36th street

Sai Organics for all things organic, very good coffee, fresh made juicings. And he is a nice man.
36th/37th streets

Marino and sons for fish. Charlie does the buying at the fish market every morning. If you need something special ask him. All the guys there are one family and Yankees fans (keep it in mind)
36th/37th streets

International meats. 36th/37th streets
John & John are the owners so that makes it easy.  They have the cleanest best butcher shop anywhere.  They also supply most of the kebob stands/shops in NY.  Great selection, very helpful and will cut to order.  They also carry Mediterranean brand pita (blue label is best), its the best pita every!

Manos papaya corner of Steinway and 30th ave for mango/OJ/banana smoothies.

The Bangladeshi place is quite good, if small! They have a good selection of daal, rice, pickle, oil, and a decent selection of spices. Hello All-

Don't forget Ya Ya's Bakery on 31st st near 30th ave.  All their stuff is baked in a wood burning oven.  Their multi grain bread is excellent, as are all their greek pastries.  Most importantly- you Must try the spanikopita triangles- at .50 cents a piece there are fewer bargain snacks around.


I also love to browse in Artopolis Bakery- also on 31st street but more towards Ditmars in a shopping center.  This place is pricey but beautiful.  Tony and Daves is my fave all around provision food store- as is Sorriso's down towards 45th street.
