Teen and Assoc. Rob Bank in Queens

ASTORIA, N.Y. (1010 WINS)  -- A 16 year-old girl and three accomplices allegedly tried to rob a Commerce Bank at 37-28 Ditmars Blvd. in Astoria Friday afternoon ... and failed.

They then went six blocks down the street to the First Central Savings Bank at around 2:30 p.m. ... And successfully robbed it.

The girl, identified as Chrystie Almestica of Astoria, passed a note to the teller at the first central bank and got away with the cash. She allegedly managed to pass a bubdle of money off to another accomplice before the bank manager, who was getting coffee nearby, was able to chase her down with a customer and get her to surrender.

Almestica is in custody, charged as an adult with attempted bank robbery, bank robbery and menacing, at the 114th Precinct along with another accomplice. The male suspect is unapprehended and the investigation is ongoing.

Police think the money got away with the other two people. Police are still investigating if she was a willing accomplice or was cooerced into the robbery.
