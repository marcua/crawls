Quote from: Debbie on January 01, 2009, 08:23:33 PM
I am new to this - those of you who have one, how do you use it and do you like it?  Have you seen any results as far as being and feeling more fit?

Yeah, there's a "balance board" and you stand on it, it takes your weight, and you can set a target weight loss/gain for yourself. It'll prompt you through the setup process. You can even log in hours that you've exercised outside of the Wii fit, like if you belong to a gym or anything like that. Your Mii character will get skinnier or larger depending on whether you lose or gain weight. It'll also tell you if you're in a healthy range or not. I have one, and it's great, you have to keep up with it though, or it'll scold you for not coming back for awhile. It not only helps you do aerobics, but it also helps you have a better sense of balance. It can be an intense workout, but the only 2 things I find deficient with it is that the exercises, like running in place is weird and I don't feel as though the remote can gauge movements properly. Also my older sisters tried it, and they're all rather thin for their respective ages and my nephew's wii fit told them they were over weight. I don't really know why it says that but anyway...I felt better working out with it regularly, it did help but you need a lot of self discipline to keep at it. Oh and the Wii fit is smart, it'll wish you happy birthday on your b'day and tell you not to eat too much at Thanksgiving and things like that. Ha ha, it's fun. It is helpful, and nice to be able to exercise at home with, essentially, a personal trainer.
