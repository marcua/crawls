I've seen Food Lions in other parts of the country. Tennessee and North Carolina, and I think in Jacksonville. I don't think I've ever seen one around New York, but I lack an encyclopedic memory when it comes to supermarkets. I always thought of it as a discount grocery store, if that's the right term. Lots of store brand products.

Annie: Are you saying Gibney's has a kitchen now? Guess I haven't been there in too long a time cause they didn't used to have one. Broadway Station, nearby, has pretty reliable pug grub and the occasional scuzzy overnight scene. Oh, wait, that might have been me you saw stumbling out of there...  :?  They do have the wood, the jukebox, but I don't know about the milk shakes. They have tea.

I think they should combine grocery store and restaurant like they do over at the Fairway on the upper west side. I'd be fine with a burger joint but a decent steakhouse would suit me better. I'm still traumatized (oh, ho) by the abrupt closure of the Ranch Steakhouse on 21st Street, and the Butcher Bros. on Newtown is stupid expensive. S'agapo, across from the old Tupelo, has a decent steak. Looking forward to Cesco (formerly Gables) opening. Guess they're behind schedule, as they told me they were scheduled to open around Christmas week.

There used to be a retro-style burger place on 36th Ave. next to Veronica's, but it didn't last long.
