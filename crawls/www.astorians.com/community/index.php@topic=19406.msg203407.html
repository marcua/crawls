Young Adult Mass, Greek Dinner & The King's Men talk on Journey of Self-Mastery
« on: September 20, 2010, 08:44:18 PM »
Young Adult Mass on Sunday October 17th at 5pm at:

Our Lady of Mount Carmel Church
23-25 Newtown Avenue
Astoria, NY 11102
www.mountcarmelastoria.org

Following the mass, please meet the group in front of the church to head to dinner to:

Opa! Souvlaki Greek Restaurant
28-44 31st Street
Between Newtown Avenue & 30th Avenue
Astoria, NY 11102

Opa! Souvlaki Restaurant is a good Greek Restaurant in the heart of Astoria. Our group has been here several times, and the food/service was excellent. $25 (tax/tip included) for dinner includes the following:
- 2 appetizers (1 hot; 1 cold)
- Greek salad
- Main entree
- Dessert
- Wine/Drink

During dinner, our guest speaker will be Mark Houck, President of The King's Men (www.thekingsmen.org), and he will give a talk on "The Journey of Self-Mastery." Mark Houck powerfully explains in this timely presentation how the journey of self-mastery is worthwhile and life long pursuit. In a culture that promotes sexual license, tolerance and indulgence of every kind of disordered behavior and lifestyle, this presentation will open the minds of any man or woman who desires to grow in holiness and virtue.

Directions to the church:
***Subway***
Take the N or Q train uptown to Astoria, and get off 30th Avenue Station. Walk down 31st Street and turn left on Newtown Avenue. The church is located on the corner of Newtown Avenue and Crescent Street

***Car***
From Westchester & NJ, take the Triboro/RFK Bridge and exit 29th Street (first exit in Queens). From 29th Street, turn right on Astoria Blvd.

From Long Island, take the Grand Central Park Way and exit Hoyt Avenue (last exit before Triboro/RFK Bridge). Turn left on 31st Street (below elevated subway line) and right on Astoria Blvd.

The entrance to the church parking lot is on Astoria Blvd. (pass Crescent Street).

Contact Toan Chu at catholicfellowship@ymail.com or at 917.653.2140 with any questions.

This event is hosted by The Catholic Fellowship.  To learn about us, go to www.cfnetwork.org.
