My husband had requested meatloaf and instead of going out and buying stuff for my mother's rather complicated (but yummy) meatloaf, I searched for something simpler to make the most of what I had in my kitchen. I found this basic easy meatloaf recipe that was credited to Katie Lee Joel and made it tonight.....

And it actually was very very good!!! So I am passing it on to the meateaters out there. I halved everything and it turned out fine.

INGREDIENTS

    * 1 Tbsp. olive oil
    * 1/2 medium yellow onion, diced (about 3/4 cup)
    * 1 garlic clove, minced
    * 1 medium red pepper, finely diced (about 1 cup)
    * 1 bay leaf
    * 2 Tbsp. chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
    * 2 tsp. chopped fresh thyme
    * 2 pounds lean ground beef
    * 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
    * 3/4 cup dry bread crumbs
    * 1 cup ketchup
    * 1 Tbsp. Worcestershire sauce
    * 2 tsp. kosher salt
    * 1 tsp. freshly ground black pepper

 Preheat the oven to 350°. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Spray lightly with oil. Heat the olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Sauté the onions, garlic and bay leaf until the onions are tender, about 3 minutes. Add the red pepper and cook until the red pepper is tender, about 5 minutes. Stir in the parsley and thyme and cook for another 2 minutes. Remove pan from the heat and let the onion mixture cool. Discard the bay leaf.
In a large bowl, combine the beef, eggs, bread crumbs, 1/2 cup of ketchup, the Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper and the cooled vegetables. Use your hands to mix everything together.

Transfer the mixture to the center of the baking sheet and form into a loaf. Coat the meat loaf with the remaining 1/2 cup ketchup.

Bake for 1 to 1 1/2 hours (depending on the shape of your loaf), until the meat loaf is firm. Let set for about 5 minutes before slicing.


I served it to my hubby with string beans and a baked tater.  :mrgreen:

Astorians,
  Another simple recombinista recipe and today's lunch.

Astoristani's simple Asparagus Putanesca

Recipe:  Total Prep Time:  About 20 minutes

1. Cut the asparagus into inch long pieces and discard the white woody bit of the stock
2. Coarsely dice an onion of your choice
3. Steam the asparagus and onion until fork tender
4. Add diced sun-dried tomatoes, capers, diced Kalamata olives, and Euro Market's double smoked bacon minced into tiny pieces
5. Sprinkle with black pepper, kosher or sea salt, crushed red pepper, and garlic powder to taste
6. Coat with your favorite commercial Italian dressing
7. Nuke it when you get to work and enjoy--everything comes together.

"Haute" Cuisine doesn't get much easier than this!

Tuscan menu for mom and family tonight.

Pork roast
with 2 day marinade of the following paste: rosemary, garlic, lemon zest, olive oil and salt. Roasted with potatoes and onions, 4hrs at 250f. Pull pork from oven when it hits 160f. Leave in veggies to finish at 400 for 15 minutes.  Pour 1/2 cup olive oil and juice 1 lemon over the meat as it rests.  Slice and put over veggies.

Tuscan white beans with Swiss chard. 
Simmer 1 bag dry beans for 1.5hrs with 4 cloves garlic, 2 bay leaves and 1/4cup olive oil. Beans should be just soft.
Boil chard in salted water till softened, drain. In pan saute lots of garlic with olive oil. Add chard to saute then add in beans with 1 cup bean water or more. Stir and simmer with 1 branch fresh rosemary and some salt.
Added bonus: Render some pork belly fat or proscuitti fat before adding in garlic. This will give a more authentic richer flavor. 

Apple and fennel salad
Thin slice 2 apples and 2 fennel bulbs. Toss with olive oil, red vinegar, salt, touch of fig jam (or similar sweet), black pepper. Toss together and let sit. Onto platter with crumbled baked and dried proscuitto (or pancetta). Top with good blue cheese.

Chaibatta bread pizza
cut bread into into two long slices. rub in garlic & oil. Thin slices of tomatoes and sea salt on top. drizzle with touch more oil. Grate over parm cheese and toast in oven.
