I was born in Flushing Hospital, my first major league baseball games were Met games. For a team that began in 1964 their championship history is quite impressive. Apparently the Cubbie's 'goat' curse is real. I'm glad that they have embraced the colors and uniforms of the National League teams that they were built to replace...the Giants and Dodgers....the Met 'NY' logo is the New York Giants old logo...before they went to San Francisco....and Met Blue is Dodger Blue...the orange is from the Giants...as is the black.....the original Mets home uniform aped the Yankee pinstripes...now the all white is pure Dodger. It's about time.

Unlike most Yankee fans that I know I've never rooted against the Yankees in a series just because the Mets weren't there.

I've wondered in 1986 ( I was at game seven ).....with the Mets and Red Sox in the World Series...a GREAT series BTW...what did whimpering Yankee fans do? Lock themselves in a closet?

I do credit the Yankees on their great storied history of championships...and resisting the marketing pressure to have a "Night Yankee Hat"...a "Day Yankee Hat" or a "Sunday Yankee Hat". The home uniform is the home uniform...the away uniform is the away uniform...there is a continuity of tradition there. I tip my hat to that. They are one of the few remaining teams that do not put players names on the back of their jerseys.

Yet.....

I'm still, solidly, a Met fan.

.....is there a better or more ferociously played inter-league series than the Mets-Yankees? Since it's inception...neither team has sweeped. That's pretty cool.
