Across the street from (your old address) the saloon was the A&P, where Key Food is now. I can vividly remember when my Mom found out that all the Ann Page canned foods were made and packaged by Del Monte. She went shopping on their annual canned goods sale and we had no room to put all the stuff she bought! But, she said she saved a fortune by buying Ann Page instead of Del Monte and that made it all worth while.

You know something, I just this second realized why I can't stomach canned anything. I always hated canned vegetables and fruits. I have such a passion for fresh produce. When I left home, in 1964, I had a very small, Italian green-grocer around the corner from me on 30th Avenue between 29th and 30th Streets. He would save for me bushels of too-soft-to-sell egg tomatoes and I would make my own sauces. I would give him a few jars and some home baked breads and pies in exchange for the tomatoes. I can even remember the day I discovered that instead of jarring my sauces I could freeze them. What a time saving. I still do it that way.

This whole thread makes me melancholy.
