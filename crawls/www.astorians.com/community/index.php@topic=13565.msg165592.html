Thanks for all the great tips!

The one thing I absolutely can't complain about are my dining and grocery options.  I'm a BIGTIME foodie and an iron chef in training.  I'm just thrilled with this opportunity to work with so many ethnic ingredients.

I plan on doing a good amount of grocery shopping at the C-Town on 28th Ave and 41st street.  For olive oil and meats, I'm going to try hard and purchase mostly from the local, ethnic small businesses because I want to support the local trades as much as possible.  For seafood, I hope to be purchasing from the local fishmongers near 30th Ave.

I actually passed by Luna de Jaurez last time.  I believe they had a buffet for $9.95 if I'm not mistaken.  I ate nearby last time I was checking out apartments at Little Morocco. 

I'm surprised that there's an American restaurant nearby.  When I walk out of my apartment, all I see are the aforementioned Egyptian/Moroccan/Middle Eastern eateries.  I'll be sure to check out Stove especially when I'm craving a burger.

My recommended restaurants in the area thus far are (in no particular order): Agnanti, Tierras Colombianas, Zenon Taverna, Last Stop Restaurant, and Arhan Thai.  I already have a huge list of other places I want to try and am excited.  I'm not excited about all the hours that I'll be spending at the gym (NYSC) in order to burn off all this good food.

My wife has already complained to me that there's a lack of good Chinese restaurants in the area.  I envision myself making the trek out to Flushing on a semi-consistent basis for both produce and good eats.

All this talk about food has actually made me hungry...doh!

Well, as you've read from my posts, it's pretty clear that landlords are asking for approximately $1,800 give or take for decent 2 bedroom apartments.  Obviously, the pet situation was quite a curve ball.  But I will say this: landlords seem to be a lot more tolerant of cats than dogs.  So at least you have that on your side. 

Given the economic meltdown we're facing, I anticipate rent to continue dropping over the next 6~12 months with perhaps a flat curve or even a slight price increase during the summer.  I know a lot of landlords are trying to "make it past" the winter and spring and hope that movement will pick up during the summer.  In my amateur estimation, I'd try and time the market to rent in March/April and resist the urge to move during the summer from May~September.  After things cool down and depending on whether or not the economy picks back up, I'd start to go apartment hunting again.

Also, and I'm sure that many of you guys are well aware of this, Astoria rent has really skyrocketed over the last two years.  If you're living in an apartment that you locked into a lease from 2005, your rate is most likely going to seem incredible compared to current market prices.

Case in point: my wife's friend has a 1 bedroom for $1,100/mo.

IT DIDN'T EXIST, as you read it.

I truly wanted to believe that the prices in Manhattan were going down, but it was hard to. Despite of finding one or two other REAL apartments through other realtors, once I checked the units I realized that although they were asking a lower price for what otherwise would have been WAY out of my budget, the apartments in the city were nothing else than closets with inside bathrooms. At this point I don't know what areas are best, where is more convenient or safer, where is more hectic or quiet. I know Nothing, Zip, nada and wouldn't want to go to my appts falling desperately victim of my own ignorance. So, I decided to ask for your help, hoping you don't mind me to.

Camuri--

After living in Astoria for a while, I had a little "box" of Astoria where I would like to live when I looked for my most recent apartment.

I tend to prefer the N/W to the R/G/V because I often take the train alone late at night and being a 5'1" girl, I feel like the N/W is safer. I know lots of people on the board disagree with me, but it's my preference.

I made a box that had a western border of 21st St, an eastern border of Steinway or 45th St (if I had found the perfect place east of Steinway, I would have taken it, but it would've had to be *perfect*), a northern border of 21st Ave and a southern border of Broadway. While it's perfect safe to live south of Broadway, I tend to prefer the 30th Ave or Ditmars areas. Ditmars is a little more "town-y" with lots of tree lined streets and it gets pretty quiet at night.

I ended up moving off of the 30th Ave N/W stop, though, because I prefer to be around places with restaurants and convenience stores that are open late at night.

Here's the map I made for my search: http://maps.google.com/maps?q=astoria&oe=utf-8&rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&client=firefox-a&um=1&ie=UTF-8&split=0&gl=us&sa=X&oi=geocode_result&resnum=1&ct=image

Go to that link, click "My Maps" and look for "Astoria Apartment Search" to see the borders I made for myself.

Like Paratactical, I also prefer that box of area of Astoria (although, I think I could stretch my Southern border to 36th Ave.).  When I first moved here I lived in the Ditmars area on one of those cute tree-lined street in an apt. that was part of a private home.  It was great, but when I moved out from that share to get my own place I looked for apartments in rent stabilized buildings.  And although I wanted to stay in the Ditmars area, I ended up just off of Broadway because the apartment was exactly what I wanted.   Broadway is much livelier than where I use to live but I like that there's a whole lot more around me (Ditmars had a lot of stuff too, but I felt it was more disconnected from the rest of Astoria) and I have easy access to the R line (which comes in handy for those Target trips or when the N/W are down) and I'm much closer to the big commercial stores like Cost-co (in one direction) and Home Depot (in the other direction).  That said, I do miss Ditmars and would definitely look for places there if my fiance and I decide to upgrade to a 2br.  Oh, and one huge advantage to Ditmars, you always get a seat on the train. Speaking of which, I'm also a youngish professional (at what age do you stop being a young professional?) and work in lower Manhattan (Rector St. is my stop).  The subway trip is around 35 -45 mins but, it's mostly a relaxing trip since I always get a seat by at least the 49th St.  I've gotten a whole lot of reading done over the past 6 years.
