For Immediate Release

February 22nd, 2006
Contact: Steve Greenfield (www.greenfieldforsenate.org)
845-532-0280, greenfieldforsenate (at) earthlink.net

Greenfield Expands Senate Bid To 3 Additional Parties

Steve Greenfield, a candidate for United States Senate in New York against
incumbent Hillary Clinton, who announced for the 2006 Democratic Party primary
in October, 2005, has announced that he is officially seeking the ballot lines
of the Independence Party, the Green Party, and the Libertarian Party.

"Millions of New Yorkers who don't participate in major party primaries are
anxious for the opportunity to support the simple platform of Peace, Freedom,
Prosperity, and Justice as outlined at www.greenfieldforsenate.org, in the
General Election," said Greenfield, 44, of New Paltz, New York. "And millions of
Democrats want to know that they will have the opportunity to vote to stop the
war in Iraq and reject the right-wing voting record of Hillary Clinton
regardless of the outcome of their own primary. My campaign must transform
itself to establish this option for all New Yorkers on an equal basis regardless
of party affiliation."

Greenfield, who has been exploring the possibility of additional ballot lines
since late January, was motivated to move openly towards the multi-party option
with the early February announcement that eight House Democrats had filed a
proposal, HR 4694, that would basically end the rights of third parties and
independent candidates to participate in Congressional elections.

"With roughly 50% of Americans routinely reporting that they are dissatisfied
with the direction of both the Democratic and Republican Parties, continuing
efforts by both major parties to suppress access of American citizens to their
own electoral process is an affront to Democracy and our essential human
rights," noted Greenfield, who ran for Congress as a Green in 2002 in a campaign
that galvanized public opinion against the USA Patriot Act and preceded the
adoption of five pro-civil liberties municipal resolutions in New York's 22nd
Congressional District. "Without fair access to elections, debate is restricted,
stagnation assured, and democracy is denied."

Greenfield believes that the agendas of the Libertarians, the Greens, and the
Independence Party coincide strongly around the traditional American values of
rejecting aggressive military action, providing real security that does not
require the sacrifice of the liberty and justice we pledge to defend, ending
corporate welfare, restraining public spending and balancing the budget, and
holding public officials to the rule of law. He also believes that a single,
combined candidacy on multiple party lines will provide a pro-peace voting
option of equal standing with the pro-war candidacies of the two major parties
in 2006. In reaching this belief, Greenfield  draws upon his experience running
for County Legislature as a Green with the Libertarian endorsement in 2003, a
union that brought an unprecedented 21% vote total against well-funded and
heavily favored Democrats and a Republican incumbent.

"Considering the known majority degree of support by New Yorkers for the issues
advocated by the Greenfield For Senate campaign, which are rejected in the
platforms of the incumbent and her likely Republican opponent, a joint effort by
multiple parties will make it impossible for the traditional practice of
exclusion of third party candidates from debates to continue," Greenfield added.
"When the voters discover that voting their conscience has become a viable
choice, and that "spoiling" has become a statistical impossibility, the trap of
"lesser-evil" voting and the harm it has caused to America will be a thing of
the past. All New Yorkers will finally have the genuine right to vote."

Steve Greenfield's campaign website is www.greenfieldforsenate.org.

For further information, call 845-532-0280
