That's a fantastic area of the neighborhood to be in. I haven't been inside that particular building, but walk by it all the time and it seems well-managed, and the residents I've met there have all been friendly. I tend to prefer (*well-maintained*) pre-war buildings to new construction myself: in general more solid walls, larger rooms, more interesting layouts, more character.


Have you looked at the listings at www.urbanedgeny.com? They have apartments in the new rental building under the train at 31st St and Broadway. Most (though not all) are above your stated max, but you don't pay a broker fee for those apartments so might balance out.


If you plan to stay in the area long-term, for $1800 a month or just a bit over you can buy an apartment in the neighborhood and enjoy the tax benefits. Of course you'll need a hefty down payment.


None of those options (including the apartment you saw, I assume) have guaranteed parking though.
