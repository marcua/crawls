Some of my favorites:

Memories of my Melancholy Whores by Gabriel Garcia Marquez

Timoleon Vieta Come Home by Dan Rhodes

Snow Flower and the Secret Fan by Lisa See

The Secret Life of Bees by Sue Monk Kidd

The Known World by Edward P. Jones

Blackbird by Jennifer Lauck

Children of the Flames by Lucette Matalon Lagnado

The Bluest Eye by Toni Morrison

Night by Elie Wiesel

I am a Pencil by Sam Swope

One True Thing by Anna Quindlen

Sybil by Flora Rheta Schreiber

Memory Keepers Daughter by Kim Edwards

These are only a few but I definitely recommend. Children of the Flames and Night are books that chronicle the experience of those during the Holocaust. Timoleon Vieta didn't have good reviews online which is what drew me to it initially and I loved it. It created lots of emotion from within. The Known World is a fictional historical look at slavery. Snow Flower and the Secret Fan is an emotional journey into the lives of Chinese girls within their traditional cultural expectations. As a mother, after reading I am Pencil I felt compelled to do all that this teacher did for his 5th graders. I wish I had a teacher like him when I was in school at that age. Reading Sybil was difficult but only because it made me sad and angry for the main character. Memory Keepers Daughter which reminded me that no one can disturb the connection a mother has with those that she brings into this world. There is nothing stronger than the unknown that connects us to our children. One child can't ever replace another. Last, One True Thing hit home since my mom was ill as well as I read this book. I could relate to all that is involved with taking care of the mother that goes from supermom to frail mom.

I hope this helps and truly enjoy all that you read.

More Books I Love:

-The Tender Bar by J.R. Moehringer: a memoir about drinking, literature and the ways people can be surprising
-Nickel and Dimed by Barbara Eirenreich: nonfiction story about the struggles of the working class
-The Real War on Terror by Jeff Ott: funny political satire written by a punk rocker
-Einstein's Violin by Joseph Eger: Non-fiction book comparing beethoven to einstein +string theory
-Beasts of No Nation: Incredible fiction account of warfare in Africa and a child who was forced into it
-The Unbearable Lightness of Being by Milan Kundera: one of my favorite books of all time
