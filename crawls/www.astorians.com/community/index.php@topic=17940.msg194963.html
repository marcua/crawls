I think Astoria's a great place to raise kids.  I find the suburbs bland, boring and isolating in comparison.  Here, we have the world at our fingertips - all the fantastic places in Astoria and nearby in Queens and Manhattan and Brooklyn (and beyond!) to explore on the weekend - foods to try and people to meet and museums and parks and plays to go to and music to hear.  We don't need a big house - we've got life all around us.  Every day when we go out we see people we know on the street and in their yards and in the shops - and I don't have to drive anywhere to get to it all.  As for schools - zoned schools are not the only option.  I haven't fully waded into the fray yet, but I'm actually excited about all the possibilities.  I don't think the suburbs have as many options.

There are actually very good schools in Astoria.  And taxes on Long Island are very high-- 3 to 4 times as high as taxes in NYC.  Commuting costs are also high.  If you actually do the math Astoria is one of the best values in the area. 

For a 2-3 BR big enough for a family of 4 you'd probably pay between 1500 and 2500 depending on what you feel like you need. Prices also vary based on the the state of the apartment.

Astoria is great for renters who want to enjoy the social life. When you have kids, you're not eating out as often, plus you want to save for their college. A house in NHP can cost about HALF of what a house costs in Astoria, with maybe 1/2 hr extra commuting each way. That's $400k in savings.

Taxes are more, but you don't have to pay for private schools, plus there's less garbage/fewer potholes/less crowding. And you can always visit Astoria.

Yeah I hate the blandness of the 'burbs myself, but we're trying to stay in Queens since we don't need a whole lotta house or yard. We just want more room, peace/quiet and freedom to fix and update our surroundings.

I can't argue that anyone else is wrong for the housing choices they make for themselves and their families-- we all are doing the best we can. For me, I will say that I hope we can afford to stay in Astoria until we're old and wrinkly. I have two children, one is already in the public school. I love that I can walk down 24th Ave and the shopkeepers smile and wave at me and my children; when the toddler attempts to run into the street, there is always someone to stop him (he stops himself, but they don't know that and I'd rather they stopped him than they didn't, you know?). I am pleased to live a carless live that is full and interesting. I love that my neighborhood is tiny but my world is vast. Why do I need a huge yard when I have Astoria Park? Why do I need to paint my walls when I can take my kids to the Met? Times change, minds change, maybe later we'll leave Astoria without a backward glance. But for now, for my little family, Astoria fits us just fine.
