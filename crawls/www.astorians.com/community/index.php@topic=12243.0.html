My wife and I ate here tonight, and I must say that it was absolutely amazing!  To start we shared a vegetable chopped salad and duck truffle potato gnocchi.  The salad was really good and the gnocchi was to die for.  For the main course, I had the Braised Lamb Shank and my wife had the Organic Strip Loin.  Both dishes were outstanding; the lamb was falling off the bone.  We had chocolate cake for dessert which was really good, as well.  I would HIGHLY recommend this place.  They had a large selection with a wide range of prices, and I thought the prices were very reasonable for the quality of the food.  It's one of the most satisfying meals I've had in New York.  I'm excited to try their brunch!

My boyfriend and I went last night to try the special $15 Tuesday Prix Fixe. We brought a nice French bottle from Grand Wines and strolled down to the restaurant.

The decor didn't thrill me from the outside, as it looked incredibly blue lit, but once inside, the candles and accent lighting filled the place very warmly and created a casual, comfortable atmosphere. The tables are spaced reasonably and they don't seat people right on top of each other.

The food was amazing. The carrot soup we had for the first course is easily the best soup I've ever had. The chicken main course had a delightful sauce and was especially tender. The spinach and tomato bed that the chicken was presented on was also amazing. We shared duck gnocchi, which was tender and succulent and he had the snapper as his main course. I usually do not like fish, but I was jealous that I had not risked it and gotten the snapper. It was light, flaky and flavored wonderfully on a bed of what I think were pine nuts, finely chopped. For desert, we had crepes, which were good, but the soup and entrees were much better.

This is definitely some of the best food I've had in Astoria, and might be the best overall.

The servers were nice enough and very attentive.

However, there was one problem which really rubbed  me the wrong way.

We had brought a rather expensive, nice bottle of French wine as we had been invited to do when we made our reservations (which you don't need). When we took the bottle out, they took it behind the bar to open. They corked the bottle. Not only did the cork a nice bottle of wine, neither the bartender nor the waitress so much as mentioned this to us. I understand that particularly with older bottles, corks can and do break. I also understand that they do not have a wine license, so I cannot expect their servers to be particularly trained with regards to wine. However, to not even say something, to just set it on the table, was a real bummer.

I certainly recommend the restaurant, I would just recommend that if you bring your own bottle, you also bring your own wine key.

I've been coming here for brunch on a regular basis now when Grand Cafe ($12 for brunch with coffee and one drink) is just too loud or crowded. Boite is cheaper than Bistro 33, Ovelia, or Indigo, and has a completely different atmosphere. If you're not looking for a "scene" with your eggs, this is the place. Their homemade basket of muffins and pastries is something I was reluctant to pay for but it's a nice treat once in a while and actually the pastries (scones, muffins, etc..) are really good. Their waitstaff is very sweet and accomodating and since they don't have a huge clientele yet are always happy to greet me and my hubby. We brought a few friends here, individually, and in groups, to try to get the word out and they were really appreciative of that. I haven't tried their 18 pre-fixe yet, but have had dinner and am equally happy about price/value of food.
