megc, LIC is my hands down favorite bar in "the neighborhood."  It's kind of a pain in the ass to get to, but they have great beer, a nice patio, and a panini press behind the bar you're feeling peckish.  Oh, and a photobooth.  All the best bars have photobooths. Aside from the Beer Garden it's the only bar in the area my non-Astoria friends will come to out here. 
I'd throw out some restaurants but I'm so bad with names, I just sort of know where the places are. 
For example, the bakery near 28th Ave and Steinway which my girlfriend now tells me is Laziza. My mother from Syracuse sends requests for when I head up there.
There's a great French spot on Broadway between 44th and 45th. 
I recently fell in love with JJ's Sweet Potato Tempura roll, but that place wins for area sushi in my book.
There are more, I just can't keep them all straight.

What a great thread!

After 5+ years in Astoria, I've definitely got a few favorites!

* Telly's Taverna - great grilled fish, lamb chops, and shrimp. And free honey-covered dough-balls (mmm, dough-balls...) on Friday nights (I think it's Fridays).
* Trattoria L'Incontro for special occasions
* Le Sans Souci
* Malagueta is great!! (MandaT, you should definitely check it out)
* Igloo for casual fare
* Balkh Shish Kebab (I always get bolani kadu - pumpkin-filled turnover appetizers, fish kebab and rice with raisins & carrots and extra white sauce!)
* Fatty's Cafe for burger & sweet potato fries

New favorites
* Watawa - had good Japanese food there once
* Nebuta - excellent new cheap Japanese take-out
* Lil Bistro 33
* Wave Thai
* San Antonio Bakery
* Little Colombian bakery on Steinway near Broadway (can't remember the name)

Also really want to try
* Tournesol
* Film Chefs
* Sunswick
* Locale
* Kabab Cafe

And I know there are others I'm forgetting but these are the first that come to mind! I just got a book - the Chowhound's Guide to the New York tri-state area - for my birthday, which is great - and includes several restaurants in Astoria (like Balkh, Telly's, Malagueta, Kabab Cafe). But a wee bit out of date since VOLO is still on there, and I would have included that on my list as a favorite - who remembers that place? It was so great, alas, now it is Tutankhamun Cafe, which I can't say I've tried, but just does not appeal to me as much for some reason...

-Abby
