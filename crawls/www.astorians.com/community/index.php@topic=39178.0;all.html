Re: Falafel King

Quote from: DudeCity on March 22, 2016, 03:57:53 PM
Would you recommend any places to eat around that area besides Duzan?
Depends what you want.  Duzan may be authentic Palestinian but I am not a fan of Falafel with Cole slaw

Little Morocco is great for Sandwiches, the meat is marinated overnight and tasty
Flames is a new Afghan place that opened a few months ago.  Good and their chicken kofta is a pleasantly spicy .
Mombar is a beautiful hand crafted art place that specializes in South Indian clay pot cooking with a some other influences.  The cooking process is slow though, so be prepared to wait.  But the chef can prepare the dishes to taste or tolerance (salt, spice) with no diminution of taste
Kabab cafe is the ORIGINAL middle eastern place on the block, but Ali accepts influences from all over the world.  But part of the charm(or not) of the place is Ali's personality as well as the food.  chacun a son etc.
I keep on thinking that I should try other places, but I like the owners of all the above.  When I call in a order to little Morocco, and I say no olives and no cucumbers they say "hello professor!" <grin>
All are laziza.

bismillah.
