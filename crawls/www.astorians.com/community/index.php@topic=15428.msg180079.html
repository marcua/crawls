Last night we tried Aphrodite (33rd/Ditmars) for the first time & really enjoyed.  We tend to enjoy Cypriot style and the sheftalia diner platter, dolmades (hot) appetizer, beet salad & halloumi were all winners.  The Loukaniko sausage sandwich with Halloumi was also delish - great bread, not pita.  Loukaniko can vary from place to place with very different flavors.  This one didn't have the orange flavor, but instead just a nice robust spicy blend.  Definitely all home made food, not something that came from Krinos.  Will be calling them again soon!

I am in the same boat - so close, and yet so far.... I save Petey's for a special treat when I'm around 30th Ave.  At least the fries are always fresh and crisp.   :mrgreen:

We have been consistently impressed with Tasty's, the new one on Ditmars.  Their salads are not what you would expect from a diner; they are fresh, with a good variety of ingredients, and they do a great job cooking the protein - grilled chicken and grilled salmon are always moist and flavourful, never dry.  They're big, too - two of us can share one salad for a meal.  Burgers are not gourmet, but several cuts above the average diner burger, and they've always been cooked as ordered.  The panini are also very good, and they will substitute a full garden salad for the fries for no extra charge.  Don't get me wrong - the fries are good, too.   8-)

Like everyone else says, Petey's is great.  I usually order from places on 30th Ave bcs I live right off it.  Chicken Shack (souvlaki, big salads, ribs, roast chicken) on the 30th Ave/35th St has tasty food and is always open late.  Akti (Greek seafood--great whole fish, best fish croquettes EVER), across the street, is also great.  They're a little pricier, but we often eat at their restaurant and found that when we ordered delivery, the portions were actually larger than what you'd get at the restaurant, for the same price.  Great deal.
