I hope you’ve already voted!
 
Since you strike me as someone who is civically engaged, why not continue with a phone call to Senators Gillibrand and Schumer about amendments to the FAA Reauthorization Bill (H.R. 636) that would reduce helicopter noise over Astoria?
 
Does helicopter noise in Astoria affect your quality of life?
Do helicopters affect your enjoyment of time outside, perhaps at Astoria Park?
Do they keep you up late at night and then wake you early in the morning?
Most importantly, do you want to do something about it?
 
Now is your chance! And all it takes is a quick phone call or email today.
 
U.S. Senator Kirsten Gillibrand (D-N.Y.) has authored two amendments to the FAA reauthorization bill before the Senate - and the implementation of both would invite community input regarding helicopter routes and help to reduce helicopter noise over Astoria. 
 
Please call Senator Gillibrand’s and Senator Schumer’s offices today and express support for Senator Gillibrand’s Amendments #3605 and #3554
See the details below!
 
Both calls would take less than 10 minutes combined!
Gillibrand’s Office phone number: (202)224-4451
Schumer’s Office phone number: (202) 224-6542
 
If you feel more comfortable writing a note:
https://www.schumer.senate.gov/contact/email-chuck
http://www.gillibrand.senate.gov/contact/
 
Information:
Sen. Kirsten Gillibrand (D-N.Y.) Amendment #3605
This amendment calls for a final FAA rule in three years that creates a requirement that all existing aircraft must be retrofitted to comply with stage 3 noise levels (an international standard); any helicopters not in compliance with stage 3 noise levels by December 2024 must be retired. Government and medical helicopters are exempt.
This amendment would help to reduce the noise impacts of a large number of helicopters that fly the THROGS helicopter route over Queens, including Astoria.


Sen. Kirsten Gillibrand (D-N.Y.) Amendment #3554
This amendment calls for establishment of a process within one year to determine whether minimum altitude requirements for helicopter routes over populated areas can be safely set to reduce noise effects on communities and whether existing routes should be modified, restricted, or eliminated due to excessive noise. The FAA would establish a process to consult with politicians and communities and to evaluate specific helicopter routes to reduce noise.
Under this amendment, communities will get input into existing routes and have a stronger voice in rulemaking. This could lead to a reduction in helicopter noise, which negatively affects the health of those on the ground.
Right now helicopters overfly Astoria at 1,500-2,000 feet with no speed restrictions. Helicopter operators say they are “Flying Neighborly”, but they operate their rotorcraft at speeds higher than manufacturers recommend for Fly Neighborly operations. Fast flights at low altitudes result in excessive noise.
Emergency operations and situations would not be impacted - medical evacuation and police helicopter operations would be understandably excluded.
Let me know if you have any questions!

Hello All,


It depends on where you live in Astoria. Some are not affected by the flight path.
We are talking here of commercial/ charter helicopters and seaplanes mostly flying to the Hamptons.
Since 2013 or so there has been a huge uptick in the number of commercial/ charter helicopter and seaplane flights over Astoria.
For those under the flight path, 80% of what you are hearing are these.
Here's why:
In 2012, after many complaints from constituents on Long Island, Senator Chuck Schumer put pressure on the FAA to mandate that all commercial helicopter and seaplane flights to the Hamptons use the North Shore Route, that is, get there via the North Shore of Long Island. Previously heli/ seaplane air traffic was more dispersed getting there, with most going down the center of LI, but they had more options.
Now, with the new mandate, to get to the North Shore Route they fly over LIC, Astoria, LGA, Whitestone, etc. and then out to open water.
As a result ALL the traffic going out to the Hamptons is concentrated over us.
There is a coalition of residents from the affected areas banning together to put pressure on our elected officials and the FAA to address this issue.
I live directly under the flight path. On some mornings in the summer months there can 20+ an hour of low flying, loud helicopters and seaplanes starting at 7AM - they wake us up every morning.
One of our members built a website with a LIVE map and an easy to use complaint form to collect data about the problem to provide to elected officials and civic groups.
https://www.airnoisereport.com/complaints/new


The only other complaint website one could use is owned by the industry and the data is not available to the public.
311 only accepts complaints about Tour Helicopters.


Attached is an annotated map of the flight path over us (more or less) marked in red.
If any of you would like to join our group please PM me (I hope that's an option on this website)


Best,
Scott.
