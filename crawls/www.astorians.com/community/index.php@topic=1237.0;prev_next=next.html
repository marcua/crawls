The Second is the Technological, which I have seen holds the keys to many solutions. Not exclusively the technologies themselves, but rather the "mindset" of those who explore and invest time into these technologies.

I'm not against electronic voting. But I do find this deeply disturbing. If we're going to use electronic voting procedures I'd like to see an open-source solution implemented. In this particular issue there's no question that it's in the interest of the public that free and open software be used to tabulate votes. Anyone, of any political party should be able to look at the underlying software and data that is used to tabulate votes to ensure that votes are being counted properly. The actions of this company, and the majority republican party in AK are saddening, not to mention anti-democratic.

I can't see this standing up in any reasonable court of law, and I hope it does get to go before a court.
