Title: These are some of my favourite eats in Astoria after 1.5 mos
Post by: sass4prez on November 17, 2005, 04:38:11 PM
After 1.5 months in this shockingly pleasant neighbourhood called Astoria, here are my favourite eats, in no particular order:

Papa Empanadas --rice and beans stuffed empanada, nice and hot out of the fryer, yummm...

El Manara --best sujok ever.  That's seasoned sausages in a pita, then heated in a hot press thing like a panini.  SO GOOD.

El Rawsharaja (sp??) --hummus sandwich is delicious and cheap and quick.  Excellent babaganoush!

Kebob Cafe --pumpkin dumplings, Hawashi (mushroom paste in pita), daily specials...

Laziza Pastry Shop --the kanafe (sp), I like the cream one, not so much the cheesy one.  Plus all the pastries, and great steamed milk in the coffee... they do coffee just right.

Italian Bakery on 45th st. and 30th ave. --excellent pignoli, crossaints, cannoli, and for some reason I love getting toasted bagels with cream cheese and jelly from there.  

Brooklyn Bagels --lox sandwhich, generous cream cheese, big crusty muffins to pick on, yummy bagel chips

China Fun --fast delivery, and a ton of veggies in their soups.  And their generic veggie mix includes big savoury mushrooms!  

Jour et Nuit -tagine is delicious!  Soooo good.  

Baskin Robbins --mocha blast and turtles blast and plain chocolate ice cream.  

Haagan Daaz --mint chocolate chip ice cream!

The Columbian Place outside of the Steinway subway entrance by Broadway --delicious fresh juices with exotic fruits that I cannot remember, mixed with milk or water, I think.

Rizzo's --I think I overate their sicilian slices in my first week here, and haven't really been back since.  But I can't fault the pizza for my own recklessness.  =)

Um, that's it I guess.  I love Astoria.  The end.
