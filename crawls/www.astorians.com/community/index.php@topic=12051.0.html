Ate here before once.  The house wine we had was very standard for the area and Greek restaurants.  We had the Bouyiourdi Saganaki (melted feta with hot peppers), the Garides Methismenes (Shrimp sauteed with mushrooms) and the Ortikia (grilled Quails).  Everything tasted really good.  The Saganaki wasn't what we expected but ended up being a nice dip like appetizer we enjoyed.  I also really liked the spinach side I had with my Shrimp.  I'm usually not that excited about side dishes but I thought this one was really tasty.  We sat in the back garden and it wasn't bad.  It accommodated about 15 - 20 parties and seemed like a neighbor's patio.  A good number of people were smoking cigarettes, which may be a concern for some people.  Service was friendly but kind of slow.  All in all it reminded me of very good rustic/home style meal and environment.

We like the place. The owner/chef is the same guy that owned Ploes on Broadway until a couple of years ago.  I think it's a family run place as one of the waitresses is his wife and a son is a waiter.  The food is just so darn good and comforting.  His casseroles seem to be the specialties.  I had one with veal and my brother had one with lamb and lemon. Both delicious. The starters we all very very good and the fish dishes were roasted beautifully.  As for the slow service, yes it can be but what isn't in this neighborhood.  I would not let that be a show stopper.
