Local Chat & Issues / Queens-based rescue needs fosters for adorable kittens!
« Last post by cubemonkey on September 29, 2019, 01:35:13 PM »
Advocat (advocatrescue.org), a volunteer-run and funded cat rescue based in Forest Hills, Queens, needs fosters! Kitten season is now year-round, and there are too many homeless kittens and not enough space in shelters or rescues.

My bf and I started fostering for Advocat a few months ago while experiencing acute kitten fever. We have 2 adult cats of our own, so at first, we were concerned about having enough space in our 1-bedroom apt, and how our kitties would adapt to it. But it turned out to be so better than we expected! Advocat provides everything to get started, including an enclosed playpen to keep the fosters separated. They also cover all the vet bills, and find furrever homes for the kitties. We had our first set of fosters for about 3 weeks, and our cats loved them. We watched them grow from tiny, scared babies to confident, playful, affectionate kittens. The hardest part is saying goodbye, but that also means that we're able to take in new fosters and save more lives.

The little nugget pictured here was from our first set of fosters in May, and quite possibly the cutest thing I'd ever seen.

Fostering doesn't take much time -- we both have 9-5 jobs and are able to leave the kittens for 8 hours while we're at work. You feed them, clean their litter box, and give them affection and play. Some kittens may require oral or eye meds for a few days, but administering them is easy since they're so small.

Please consider fostering, and fill out the application on advocatrescue.org! Reply or DM me with any questions.

Creativity & Artists / Storytelling show and Open Mic in Jackson Heights
« Last post by nancygoldman on September 09, 2019, 09:14:54 PM »

“The Long and Short of It” is a monthly storytelling show and open mic in Jackson Heights. Come to hear master storytellers tell and share stories that will put you in suspense, change your thoughts on the world as you think of it today, or just give you another perspective on what somewhat else has experienced. And you can put your name in our bucket to participate in our open mic by telling a story of your own.
 
 “The Long and Short of It” is hosted by Nancy Goldman, producer and host of “Come Over For Coffee,” a storytelling home concert in Jackson Heights. In addition to performing at The New York Story Exchange and Word The Storytelling Show she has presented a TedX talk about the power of personal storytelling and teaches a course about storytelling in organizations at New York University.
 
We are often asked who attends these shows and what to expect. If you like stories, are inquisitive about other people's lives and enjoy meeting new and interesting New Yorkers then this is for you. Shows are held on the third Monday of the month (9/16) from 7pm to 8:30pm at Terraza 7 located at 40-19 Gleane Street, near 83rd Street and Roosevelt Avenue. You can purchase tickets at the door or email heightshub@gmail.com for ticket information.
