Hi everyone,

I'm Meaghan, and I'm moving to Astoria in just a few days around 36th Avenue.  From what I've seen so far, this website is a great place to pick up the best (and some of the worst) the area has to offer.  I'm very excited to move to Astoria, as I've heard many many positive things. 

I know there are many posts with many good places to go and shop and whatnot but, I was hoping to maybe save myself some time and was wondering if you all could each recommend maybe one restaurant, grocer, gym, bookstore, fun place, etc. that I might want to try in Astoria or in Manhattan.

Thanks All!

Hey Meaghan, here are my own choices, based on seven years on 36th Avenue and eighteen years in the NYC area.

Supermarket: Bravo Supermarket. 34-12 34th Avenue (if you need something quick, go to Food World on 36th Avenue and 31st Street, but make sure you check the expiration dates  :x.  I was burnt too many times).

Restaurant: There are so many choices in the area.  However, since we don't know what your particular taste is, and since you limited it to one, I would recommend The Cup 35-01 36th St.  They have decent food at affordable prices.  They have a good weekend brunch, but expect to wait.

Bookstore: Astoria lacks a chain bookstore, which has been point of contention on the board.  There is a small, family operated (I believe) bookstore on Broadway called Seaburn, 33-18 Broadway, Astoria.  Broadway, in case don't know, is the next stop north on the N/W from 36th Avenue.  There is a Borders on Park Avenue and 57th Street and a Barnes and Noble in Forest Hills on 70th Avenue and Austin Street.

Fun Place: Depends really on what you consider fun.  I've become sort of a regular at a pool/lounge/bar called Clic, at 32-04b Broadway, again one stop away or a quick 8 to 10 minute walk from 36th Avenue and 32nd Street.

Gym: Somebody else is going to have to help you out on that one. 

Hope this helps.  I'm sure people on the board have different ideas and it really depends on what your tastes/interests are.  The great thing about living in NYC, in particular, is that there seems to be something for everyone.

Hey Meaghan -

Welcome to Astoria.  The best gym is the NY Sports Club, corner of Steinway St & 30th Ave.  If you want to get a trainer, get Brooke - she's the best.  Kind of pricey, but really knows what she's talking about and teaches a great Pilates class.  For yoga, if you're into that, Paul on Saturday mornings is excellent - but you have to sign up a day in advance.

Like someone else said - restaurants - there are so many it's hard to recommend one.

For cool clothes - try Candy Plum - I forget where it is exactly - but it has a lot of handmade, unusual clothes and jewelry.
