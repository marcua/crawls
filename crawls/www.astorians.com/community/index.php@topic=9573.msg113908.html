Avoid Locale restaurant
« on: February 21, 2008, 07:27:59 PM »
For the most part the service appears to be attentive but is really quite a mess. They try to give the illusion that they care but for the most part they could be bothered. They have had one or two good waiters/waitress' but they have gone down hill since. Avoid the waiter with the wolverine sideburns at all costs. The tables are ridiculously small for the amount of things they want to put on them. If you order a bottle of wine and bottle of water prepare to balance your main course on your nose. You would think they would at least have wine stands by now since they fancy themselves as a more sophisticated restaurant. Recently they have added glass bowls the size of a small goldfish bowl to house one lone floating rose (not a great use of space to say the least, especially since there is already a candle on the table). Surprisingly, the staff is not accommodating at all.  The ambiance is wonderful once you get past the cramped quarters and the food is OK, not great. Don't break your back trying to have dinner in the "lounge". The seats are great for having a cocktail but deadly when trying to have a meal. The better choice for this place is going for drinks not dinner...

Having gone to Locale pretty consistently for the last year...the table comments-well, to me, that's really a minor issue, not even an issue. I can't even think of more than 5 restaurants in Astoria that have bigger tables. If you want a bigger table, go to Michael's, any Chinese take out place, Bistro 33 has nice big tables, but seriously table size????!!! I think the Rose thing might have been a holdover from their Valentines Day week/day; they don't usually have that.

As for the service, we've had different waiters, dinner and brunch service, and have never had to wait as long as we do at say "Grand Cafe," at their busiest times (GC also seems way understaffed when they're busy.) When we've been itching to go or trying to catch a movie, we can get the attention of any other service person there and they would normally get our waiter. We've never gone there to eat when we need to leave within 1 hour, so maybe this is just different people's experience of this. In comparison, the service at Brick is the worst we've had in Astoria on a CONSISTENT basis. From what I've read, Mojave doesn't have a lot of good points stacked in that area, either, but I'm curious to try it.

As for food, having sampled lots of places in Astoria from diners to the more chi chi (what's that, like 2?) the food is really great for the value. It is less expensive than a lot of other places for brunch and dinner of the same caliber in Astoria. I've never seen Locale try to market itself as anything other than a really good new Italian/Med place. Cavo, on the other hand....

All I can say to people who haven't gone there is, go when you're not in a rush, and make the judgment for yourself. You might be pleasantly surprised.
