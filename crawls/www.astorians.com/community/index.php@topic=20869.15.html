If someone opened up a bar where I could play video games and drink in Astoria, I'd be more than happy. I love Break and wish they'd just put some extra video games in there. Maybe I'm just the kind of drinker that gets bored with sitting at a bar and staring at the bartender or making awkward conversation with other regulars but if I could play Street Fighter and drink with friends at the same time, I'd be very happy. I guess that makes me a hipster.

Anyway, I think a restaurant not taking requests off the menu is normal and in many of the places I've worked at they won't do that either. Some places do, some don't. It's really up to the management and whether or not they want to invent prices for each request that comes up which opens up a whole load of other issues (when you come back if you say you were charged $7 not $10 last time, etc.). Also, in a busy kitchen, to suggest something off the menu can throw off the line. The places I've worked in if I would have come in and asked for a substitution or an off menu-item, I'd get my head bitten off by the nasty chefs I've had the good fortune to work with. That said, there's no reason to go off on some pretentious rant about how it's offensive to the chef. That's ridiculous. You have to respect that it's the business's right to set it's own policies and what's ok for some restaurants is not ok for others. As a server though, special requests are annoying because you have to stop what you're doing, ask the manager what they want to price it at, get the manager to override the automatic ordering system, manually type in the order into the ticket so it shows up in the kitchen, double check with the chef to make sure he can do it, then manually input the price into the individual check. When you're swamped during a rush, it's pretty annoying and I was always glad to work places where they had a policy of not allowing them because you could just nicely say, "I'm sorry, we can't do any orders off the menu" and move on.

If Queens Comfort truly didn't want children to patronize their establishment, they could have gone the Lucas route and posted a notice that infants / toddlers / youths / young'uns are not welcome.
