ABC7 Investigative Report on ConEd Maintenance
« on: July 21, 2006, 06:27:53 PM »
Can't find a link to this story on their site, hopefully it will be up soon - so I tried to jot down what they were reporting to bring over some figures and exact quotes from the report.  They headlined their 6 o' clock news by toting this "special investigation", though it didn't seem all that revealing.

They compared this week to the July 1999 power outages in Washington Heights, which were also caused by feeder cables and left 70,000 in the dark.  In that case, it was learned that managers had been told to reduce their operation and maintenance budget for four years prior to the outage.

ABC7 called this week's outage worse than 1999 with nearly half of all feeder lines into Queens burned out, and they found continued underspending on maintenance.

In 2000-2002, ConEd budged 32 million for maintenance and only spent 27 million.

A fired ConEd worker was interviewed who blames this outage on years of neglect, saying "It's not only the feeders, it's the whole infrastructure."  ConEd disputes that, claiming they have spent "billions" upgrading, and are "Constantly looking at ways to make the system better."  One ConEd worker started to say that they were replacing "old" lines, and then corrected himself to say "damaged".  (Ed. - hmmmmm.)

ABC finished off by pointing out that experts consider ConEd to be one of the best providers in the world, but toted the "reduced maintenance spending" ominously one last time before ending the report.
