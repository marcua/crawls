"Dreamgirls," "Babel" lead Oscar nominations
 


"Dreamgirls," a musical drama inspired by Motown group the Supremes, scored a leading eight Oscar nominations, but was omitted from the coveted best picture and directing categories, organizers said on Tuesday.

"Babel," a bold examination of the woes afflicting humanity in the early 21st century, followed with seven nominations, followed by Spanish-language adult fairy tale "Pan's Labyrinth" and the British royals drama "The Queen," with six each.

The mob thriller "The Departed" and the Africa-set drama "Blood Diamond" picked up five nominations each.

The best picture nominees were "Babel," "The Departed," "Letters from Iwo Jima," "Little Miss Sunshine" and "The Queen."

The directing nominees were Mexican filmmaker Alejandro Gonzalez Inarritu for "Babel," Martin Scorsese for "The Departed," Clint Eastwood for "Letters from Iwo Jima," and British directors Stephen Frears for "The Queen" and Paul Greengrass for "United 93."

Nominated for lead actor were Leonardo DiCaprio for "Blood Diamond," Ryan Gosling for "Half Nelson," Peter O'Toole for "Venus," Will Smith for "The Pursuit of Happyness," and Forest Whitaker for "The Last King of Scotland."

The best actress nominees were Penelope Cruz of Spain for "Volver," Britons Judi Dench for "Notes on a Scandal," Helen Mirren for "The Queen," and Kate Winslet for "Little Children," and the sole American contender, Meryl Streep, for "The Devil Wears Prada."

The highest-profile nominations for "Dreamgirls" were in the supporting acting races, where veteran comic Eddie Murphy and newcomer Jennifer Hudson were cited.

The 79th annual Academy Awards, the top honors in cinema, will take place on February 25 in Hollywood.

My View:
The Oscars: Resistance is useless

by elliott kalan

MAR 9

It took some time and effort, but I finally convinced myself that I agree with this years Academy Award selections. Sure, I wasnt happy when they were first announced, but the time for cinematic divisiveness is over. Weve got to put the Oscar race behind us, and pledge our support to The Departed. Its our Best Picture now, right or wrong, and it deserves our respect as Americans.

You may see this as selling out. And yes, when The Departed won best picture, I also won $60 in my office pool. But theres more at stake than just that. By refusing to believe that the unlikable, nausea-inducing closing theme from An Inconvenient Truth was the best song of last year, youre calling our entire government into question. Because if p.r.-manipulated voting by a minority of businessmen with a strong financial interest in the result isnt the best way to recognize creative achievement, then perhaps it isnt the best way to choose a president, either. Now your difference of opinion has cast doubt on the efficacy of democratic elections, and brought us to the brink of anarchy. Nice going.

Reaffirming democracy isnt the only service the Oscars perform for us. Great movies, like all works of art, must be placed against each other in competition, so that we know which one is the best. This scientific winnowing allows the casual filmgoer to save time by only sitting through the absolute cream of the crop. This saved time can then be used for more profitable enterprises, such as contributing to our economy, killing our enemies and, if possible, both.

Maybe your fancy film school education looks down on letting the Hollywood proletariat decide what constitutes art. But what would you suggest to replace it? A film-watching supercomputer? OK, thats a really stupid idea. Would a computer have the heart to nominate Little Miss Sunshine, an independent, underground, non-mainstream film that only had three previously Oscar-nominated actors in it? I doubt it. Its taste in movies would probably be too good. When the Academy honors the best of American filmmaking, its a sign that America is working properly. Therefore, to insure that America is always on the right track, we merely need to agree that the Academy Awards are always right. To do otherwise would be unpatriotic. I bet Guantanamo Bay is full of guys who thought Babel deserved to win.
