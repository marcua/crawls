Real estate is a hot topic around here, b/c of the disparity between overwhelming demand for decent, moderately-priced dwellings and supply of overpriced, pseudo-luxury.

When something is posted with all sorts of marketing-speak, of course we all get our hackles up. Especially when the price and location (two extremely important factors) are left out, even if it was a mistake.

Also a little sneaky to try and pretend it's a trendy neighborhood when in fact it's anything but. Like I said before, Corona is full of nice-looking houses, and there are certainly great people living there, but it is not Astoria!!  :-(

Anyway, that should explain our reactions. Who am I to discourage people from seeking reasonable living space elsewhere?? We're buying a place in Flushing- also not as sought-after as Astoria, but much more reasonable. Realtors could get away with being more honest- "small patio for a backyard, just enough room for a dozen friends and a BBQ, but sunny most of the day" for example.
