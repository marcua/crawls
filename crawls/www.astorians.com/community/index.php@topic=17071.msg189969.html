My fiance and I left Dolphin Gym a year ago and joined Club Fitness on Broadway. I think that it is probably the best gym/health club in all of Astoria!

~There are a lot of pros to joining:

The gym is a LOT bigger then it looks from the outside so don't let that fool you! It has 3 floors and each one has various workout rooms and equipment. There is one HUGE floor dedicated to weights, 3 separate cardio areas/rooms, a personal training room, and a cafe area. You NEVER have to wait to use weights, machines, or other equipment, and almost all of the cardio machines are equipped with TV monitors. They also have a room dedicated to their spin class as well as a separate room where they hold various other classes. I have to say that Club Fitness has some GREAT classes, you never run out of things to do that's for sure.
If that wasn't enough, they also have a cafe area with tables and a flat screen, they have their own Spa where you can get facials, massages, pedicures, etc., and 2 separate rooms with tanning beds. I also heard that they offer daycare, but have never used it personally.
I also need to mention that their locker rooms are simply amazing! they are by far the cleanest I have EVER been in and have a personal attendant that is there all the time to clean up after the slobs lol.. The locker rooms also have private showers, a separate area with the toilet stalls and sinks, a steam room, jacuzzi, and a sauna! Right outside from both the men's and women's locker rooms there is a lounge area with couches where you can relax, watch TV, read a magazine, or talk on your cell phone.

~The biggest cons about joining Club Fitness:

The monthly membership price is REALLY expensive! It's around $90 a month for a membership, that's the highest i have seen for Astoria gyms! If you prepay a year you can get a really good deal though, that's what we did.  They even threw in 2 free months since we had 2 months left on our Dolphin contract, I thought that was pretty cool.
The other con is gym hours, they are not open 24 hours which is kind of a bummer sometimes.

In my opinion, the pros outweigh the cons if you can afford the membership fee. I workout 5 days a week so for me it's worth it paying the extra money for the perks they offer!
