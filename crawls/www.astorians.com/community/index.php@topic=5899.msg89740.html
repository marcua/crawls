Wow. I was quite stunned by the vehement responses from people who mostly have never even been to this area. Yeah, it looks a little rough, but as a resident of Welling Ct. for 6 years, I have to say that appearances are deceiving. I do live with my family, and have a car, so I don't tend to walk home late at night - I think a woman should be careful about that anywhere in Astoria. But please don't slam Welling Ct. Here are some great things about it:
Every Dec. 11th, the residents throw a big street party to celebrate the Feast of the Virgin of Guadalupe, invite everyone on the block, serve great Mexican food, and we all have a good time.
The Gotham Girls Roller Derby team rehearses in a warehouse at the end of the block.
We're two blocks from the Socrates Sculpture Park, the Noguchi Museum, Astoria Park and free Kayaking on the river on Sundays.
Several of my neighbors and I are planning a block party soon. We're going to get a permit to close off the street, have a big BBQ and invite everyone to join a block association.
There are many historic homes on the block, and there's a movement going on here for everyone to fix up them up and it's catching on.
Anyway, yeah, people do refer to the block as "Little Mexico," but my only response to that is: Viva Mexico! (by the way, where do you think my member name comes from?)

Quote from: lookingatastoria on August 25, 2007, 09:12:28 PM
I read in a thread about another young professional looking for an apt, someone said they should focus on the area between 21st st and steinway st, and broadway and ditmars blvd....agree/disagree?
Those are all great areas in Astoria.  Pretty much anything on the R line from Steinway stop to 46th stop, and N train 36th Ave stop to Ditmars stops are all fine.  Just stay within a 10 minute walk of the train and you'll be fine.  There's a wealth of information in these boards, take the time to look around and read through posts.   This question is asked quite often.  Anything beyond 21St towards the water is going to be a long lonely walk.  And I think you can find a 3BR for much less than what you're willing to pay.
