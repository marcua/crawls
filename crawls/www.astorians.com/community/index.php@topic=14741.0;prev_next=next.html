Petfinder is a great source.  We used it to identify potential pets when we were looking to adopt/rescue.  Do be careful & know that not everyone on petfinder is honest or up-to-speed, so just like anything else, it's buyer beware.  We did encounter organizations that would try to push immediate adoption by guilting us into the situation saying that if we didn't adopt today, the animal would have been put down. 

We did end up adopting from this group who has a special arrangement with NYC Animal Care & Control.  They were very reasonable to deal with, really screened the pets & placed them in foster care to be sure they were indeed adoptable & really got to know each pet so they could help place them in the best home.  I believe in the end we made a $100 donation in honor of our new family addition - Good luck!

Let me just add that puppies at pet shops frequently if not always come from puppy mills, where dogs are bred in filthy and inhumane conditions. 

If there is a specific breed that you want, look for a reputable breeder in the area and ask to meet the parents.  If you're not picky, head over to the shelter of your choice, where many wonderful animals are awaiting new homes.  Good luck!
