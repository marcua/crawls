But the problem here is that you have to make a call and draw the line at some point where it makes more sense to renovate rather than just maintain. Just how cruddy does a station have to get before you renovate it? Comparing a lot of NY's transportation system, all means included, to that of some other major cities in other countries it's pretty clear that it's really not that great, relatively speaking. So the question is just how good do you want it to be.

Personally, I liked the way it looked in the 70's-80's. Yes, it was "ugly" and run down, but it had character, and so had the city. But now, after increased affluence and gentrification, people want something else. So within that latter context revamping stations make total sense. I don't really care either way (because NYC already lost a lot of its charm), but it seems to me that the objections really are solely based on the sense of personal inconvenience, which I find curious considering other opinions many people have.

And, no offense, but walking a bit more is good for a lot of people who are getting way too little exercise. And 'no', I'm not talking about the elderly, injured or disabled.
