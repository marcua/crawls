as for the quality of food, i think they are more or less all the same.  the only difference is who you choose to give your money to.  the same goes for the stuff you buy in the supermarket and cook/prepare at home.

of course each place might have their own vendor/distributor/supplier (or regionally, they might be the same), which may or may not have foods of a different quality (ie: fatty or lean, more or less salt or byproduct, etc), but that is up to the store's buyer to choose the product that will best represent their store/restaurant/deli/supermarket (and/or turn them a profit).  and then it's up to you as the consumer to choose where/how you want to spend your money.

as a full-on city girl, i know that unless you are growing/raising and killing/processing your own food, it is still being processed and made by someone else; and ultimately, you have no idea what they are doing with it or where the corners are being cut.  the vast majority of us don't live in an environment where we are particularly "close" to our food, and as someone who prefers to eat/take out (than cook), i stick by the places that serve me the best...some happen to be chain restaurants and others happen to be independent places.

i think that is the bottom line with this ENTIRE discussion.  first it's all about your personal taste/choice, and then secondly, buyer beware (as we've all experienced with all of these recent food and product recalls).

Here's a shocker... some people really do like chain restaurants. They're kid friendly, consistent, and reasonably priced. Many of the restaurants in our neighborhood are not kid friendly, tend to be on the pricier side, and are less consistent with food quality and service.

One of the other pros to the chain restaurant is selection. Go to many restaurants around here, you get one chicken choice, one pasta choice, one meat choice, one or two types of salad and a couple of dressing options. Limited vegetarian options. You get stale bread, some frou-frou dessert selection, and a snobby waiter.

Sometimes you just want a gooey cheesy pizza, a greasy burger, buffalo wings, and a chocolatey, decadent dessert that doesn't involve poached pears and some sort of french-sounding glaze. Sometimes you want to go someplace with a kid where they're going to get crayons, a drink that comes in a plastic cup, and a kid friendly portion of something that a kid would actually eat, where their squirming and fidgeting won't get you a nasty look from the diners around you.

Chain restaurants aren't all that bad. Applebees is not one of my favorites, I'll admit, and frankly, I think the addition of another chain restaurant to that area, where parking is limited already, is going to be a traffic nightmare, but I'm all for chains opening around here in general. They bring jobs to the area and are more likely to stay in business in the long run -- too often around here we see smaller places come and go, it'd be nice to have someplace that actually sticks around. It seems hypocritical for people to want so desperately for a Barnes and Noble to open, but yet eschew the opening of a chain restaurant -- they're essentially the same thing, just peddling different wares.
