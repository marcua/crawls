I have not participated in this web forum before but I'm a resident of Astoria and a vocal supporter of Jeremiah's candidacy. I am not a paid spokesman for his campaign but I wanted to be clear about where I stand on this race before I said my piece. I cannot respond to any of the accusations--many unsubstantiated--made on this forum. I can only speak for myself and why I support Jeremiah.

Jeremiah Frei-Pearson has the courage and integrity to be a true leader in a time when our government in New York is known more for scandal and corruption. He has a strong public record of fighting for the little guy. He works as an attorney for Children's Rights, where he helps get children out of broken foster systems into caring, safe, and healthy homes. He's well known in Astoria as part of the legal team who won a $63 million dollar settlement from ConEd to compensate the victims of the 2006 Queens Blackout and fund neighborhood Greening projects to improve the quality of life in our communities. Jeremiah rallied with the gay and lesbian community to fight for marriage rights and helped found Western Queens for Marriage Equality. He works with the Long Island City Alliance to keep poorly-planned development from disfiguring our neighborhoods.

It's been mentioned on this forum several times that Jeremiah's opponent is a lifelong resident of Astoria. Jeremiah may not have lived in Astoria his entire life but in a community as rich with immigrants as ours, I don't feel being a lifelong resident is necessary to hold public office. Neither does the law, which only requires 5 years of State residence and 12 months in the District to run for the Assembly. Jeremiah more than meets both qualifications. Many of us have moved here from somewhere else but few have worked as hard for our communities as Jeremiah. His candidacy has been met with enthusiastic support by many residents--myself obviously included.

I have no desire to speak against Jeremiah's opponent in this race. She has the endorsement of many worthy figures in our community. But it takes more than endorsements to win the support of this Astorian. Our government is making headlines for scandal practically every week and we need candidates with a strong public record of working for our communities, a history of fighting when the odds are tough, and a willingness to stand up to authority when necessary. Jeremiah Frei-Pearson has all of those qualities in abundance and I am proud to support him.
