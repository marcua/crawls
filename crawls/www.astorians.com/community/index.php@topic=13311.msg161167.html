If you are talking about the Foundry in LIC, I have been there for a wedding. It was absolutely beautiful space with exposed brick with a loft type of feel to it. It's not a huge place, so your reception party would prob have to be under a 100. They have a nice garden in the back. The wedding was in December so they had a bon fire in the back cuz it was freezing. I bet if you have it during the spring or fall the backyard would be lovely. They had a bar set up back there and had their cocktail/appetizer in the garden. IMHO it's probably the nicest and most unique venue in the area that isn't tacky.

We were recently at a wedding at the Foundry and it was very nice...I'd guess about the same number of people that you are having. The one thing that we noticed is that their was no real space designated for dancing. The floor towards the back (and in front of the doors to the backyard) is where people were dancing but at this particular wedding it seemed a little disconnected from the entire venue. However, we loved the bricks, the outdoor area, and the overall vibe of the place! It's classy without being pretentious! We had it easy for transportation (could practically walk there from our apartment if I hadn't been wearing heels :-D) but I do understand that for some, transportation could be a bit trickier. However, any wedding I've ever been to (except that one!) has been a little work to get to...usually comes with the territory.

keep us updated on your venue thoughts!
