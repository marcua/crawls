We play according to what the general feel of the players at the particular game are. 

Ok - that sounds really confusing.
 
I'll clear it up.  Our first game we just played with a $20 buy-in because two of the 5 players had not really played before.  We don't want to 'break' anyone or make anyone uncomfortable. 

We're hoping to play with up to a $100 buy in eventually.  We would go higher, but for the safety of our players and our piece of mind it is in our best interest to cap it at that.

Of course, each game could be different depending on who is coming.  I would like to keep a friendly, safe and fun atmosphere.  In order to do this I interview the players who are coming to find out what they've played in the past, what levels they are comfortable playing at and how skilled they are.  Then I determine a middle level for the game and suggest it to the players. 

I would definitely be up for a rotating game from house to house.  However, not a lot of people want to take on the responsibility of organizing a game.  Who ever hosts, ususally must organize as well.  I have quite a few poker player friends and access to a few online poker networks that help me to generate players for our games. 

Since there really hasn't been that much interest in weekday games, we're going to try to schedule another game for Saturday April 1 at 4:00 PM.  This gives the players who have evening plans a chance to play before hand and gives the more latenight folks a chance to come later.

Let me know if you're interested.
