I must disagree with the argument in this blog for one key reason:

You don't have to strike to preserve the pension provision.

I don't think people understand correctly that when a contract runs out the old contract continues to be in effect for all existing and new employees. So, the TWU could continue working getting their old pay (and pension benefits), and all new hires would get the same deal until a new contract is signed.

This whole argument about workers going on strike to "save" the new workers is just an "out" to a really ugly situation and makes them look only a little better than they are.

If these people were really about public service then they would never go on strike, and if they were really about saving the rights of the future worker they could do so by just keeping the old contract.

No - this is about MONEY, and the fact that they want their raise on their own terms or else they will push the big red STOP button and screw everyone else. What's sad is that it doesn't even screw the MTA bosses because the MTA isn't a corporation, it's a PUBLIC UTILITY. So that means the PUBLIC gets screwed.

It's not like when contracts expire you just have to work for free. That's the only way that someone could say that they just "had" to strike to "save" workers pensions. If you want to save pensions then DON'T STRIKE, NEGOTIATE. If you have to work for a couple months on your old contract (and health, pension, etc) then do it - if indeed it's all about "public service" and being in the interest of the new hires.

But don't strike and then claim you're doing it for the "good" of anything but your own greed.

I can think of no reason that it's acceptable for a public employee to strike unless their purpose is to cause pain to the general public. But these guys claim they don't have a problem with the general public so a strike seems totally inappropriate.

I feel VERY different about corporate employees that go on strike against corporations that are not public utilities.
