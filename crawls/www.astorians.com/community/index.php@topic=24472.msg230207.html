I think it is an overstatement to say that they are going to "destroy" the Academy.  My understanding is that they are reducing the middle school spots available in the Academy effective with the incoming 2014 kindergarteners, in order to add regular non G&T junior high school spots. The reasoning, as I have heard it, is that 122 is the only school with a junior high ONLY for gifted kids, and not in line with the rest of the city.

As a parent with one kid in the G&T and another kid in the REALLY GREAT rest of the school, I think the implication that G&T students are 'entitled' to more is really unfortunate.

As Cosmic posted above -- most of these kids came into the G&T in kindergarten.  It is simply not scientifically possible to test a 4 year old for "giftedness".  Actually what is being tested for is "test preparedness."
 http://www.nytimes.com/2013/02/18/nyregion/new-york-city-schools-struggle-to-separate-the-gifted-from-the-just-well-prepared.html?pagewanted=all

Even if you could legitimately test for "giftedness" at such a young age, I think very serious questions have yet to be answered about the effectiveness or usefulness of the G&T.  While I  relish an opportunity for my kids to do enriched schoolwork and be given extra opportunities in the arts (some of which the G&T provides, and some of which PS 122 provides for all students), the main goal of the G&T is set in a citywide curriculum, and appears to be little more than acceleration of the current regular curriculum.   Both the G&T and the regular curriculum, in turn, are focused almost entirely on preparing for standardized tests.

While acceleration of the curriculumin the G&T may prevent boredom in some kids (for instance those who read at a young age, or those with really good math skills) I personally think we'd be better off letting the teachers (in the G&T and elsewhere) design their own enriched curriculum. 

Finally, focusing on having or not having a G&T is really a subterfuge for the real issues -- the citywide standardization of curriculum for the goal of "teaching to the test".  Just to be clear, this is the fault of NYC Schools.  We have excellent teachers all over Astoria who are not allowed to alter the curriculum because they are forced to teach to the test.  Where's the outrage about that, guys??

Luchamos, my understanding (as a 150 parent) was, as you say (and like most schools), 122 has one G&T class per elementary grade.  This will remain the same with the proposed changes.  However, currently at the Academy, there are 4 classes per grade (1 from 150, 1 from 122, and TWO  classes who test in from Gen Ed).  Gen Ed kids make up 50% of the incoming middle school population.  This is where the massive change has its effects.  I 100% agree with you that there should be more opportunities for mobility, both in and out of the G&T program.  This becomes IMPOSSIBLE with a reduction in seats, particularly for the Gen Ed population, who didn't test in at age 4.  They have a huge opportunity to advance into the program at middle school, which will cease to exist with the proposed changes.  The proposal states that the G&T from 122 will have priority into the one remaining middle school G&T class--without testing.  The remaining seats at I.S. 126 will be fought over bitterly between kids from 150, 166, and Gen Ed.  This is a recipe for zero mobility, and zero opportunity for Gen Ed kids.  I still don't understand how they plan on fitting the current 7-classes-per-elementary-grade into the middle school space that currently is maxed out at 4-classes-per-grade.


Additionally, no one has a plan for kids who are a year ahead in curriculum, and aren't given an appropriate middle school seat.  Luchamos, it is too much to ask teachers with upwards of 27 kids in a class to give individualized attention to every learning level; they end up teaching to the middle.  This is as detrimental to a G&T child as it is to any other child with special needs.


I have a 2-year-old, zoned for 122.  I definitely see the appeal for her to be guaranteed a K-8 option at 122.  But not at the price of the G&T program.  It means *her* fate will be decided at 4, which currently would not be the case.  And who knows what the culture and climate at a K-8 122 will be.


I sincerely hope that non-G&T parents understand that this is an issue that affects everyone.
