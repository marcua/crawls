Post by: mridude on August 16, 2006, 09:25:41 PM
A bit off topic, but was mentioned earlier in this thread.

"Hellgate on the Park"

It was Wednesday night, the Chef was in.

Four people at the bar. Another six or seven at a table in the back.

Everybody and everything, juke box and TV was............. LOUD !!!

The menu was short but the standards were there.

A friendly waitress suggested that everything may not be available like no ravioli.

Frank Sinatra and I believe Julio Igleses were singing......LOUDLY over the boisterous group at the table who were discussing Viagra, drunk driving and the merits of Dean Martin's singing and where Jerry Lewis would be without him.

I'll have the gnocci and meat sauce, I told the waitress.

She smiled but shortly returned and announced that rigatoni, linguine and capallini were my pasta choices.....................Capallini and meat sauce, please.

Hot Italian bread and butter were brought out. My bottle of Becks came, no tap beer, sorry, and shortly there after I asked for a glass of water.

A couple of gentlemen wandered by throughout the meal and asked if everything was all right.

"Fine" I answered, hoping that they could hear me.

The capallini and meat sauce was pretty good. If you can't make a decent meat sauce you've got no right to call yourself an Italian Restaurant. Frank Sinatra was now singing "The Summer Wind" when a gray haired bespeckled man asked me if I wanted an after dinner drink. I indicated that I was actually quite full, it was a generous serving. He watched me for a while before smiling and saying I had a rain check on the drink.

So, how was it..........did I mention that it was loud? Okay, but it was friendly too. Maybe not a great choice for a first date where you want to speak softly and stare into each others eyes......but.....if you are out with the guys and want to chow down on some pretty good Italian food without breaking the bank...my meal was $12.00.......try the Hellgate on the Park.

I stopped in the night before and was told that it was 'Wings" night when the chef was not there...at least on Tuesdays anyway.

This has turned into a little Hellgate on the Park string of sorts.  I will be there tomorrow night and go most every Friday, and then some, depending.  I'm the guy who admitted to being loud.  And as for loud, I have to laugh.  The review posted earlier sited the volume.  For WEEKS I was complaining that it was too quiet.  I am a corporate type and when I want a few drinks after work, I want a total distraction from my day.  Some lively action, music, etc.  At the beginning they would have the TV on.  I can do that at home.  Finally they are playing the jukebox at a reasonable volume and the place sounds like a bar (to me).  Indeed there is a local crowd that could seem comical.  But I probably know all those guys that were mentioned.  They're not about rocket science but they're generous and sincere.  In time the crowd may get younger and more professional.  Just like the neighborhood itself.  Things have a way of evolving on their own.  For now, rest assured that it's safe, clean, fun, and great food.  But agreed, not a date place.  Not a gourmet restaurant.  And you must go with a sense of humor.  They're also trying to find experienced bartenders so the staff and service will be hit and miss for a little while.
