Title: Re: Arrrrrrrrgggggghhhhhhh someone stole my bike!!!
Post by: heff on April 03, 2007, 01:36:53 PM
Thanks for the kind words.  It was like 75% my fault and 25% the dregs of society.

When the weather is nice I like to leave my bike locked up in my front yard, which never was a problem before.  I live in a quiet residential street and its not real high traffic.  Got lazy the last time I locked it up and only used my u-lock to lock the front wheel to the fence in the yard and left my chain lock hanging on the handlebars, which is where I leave it when I ride.  I guess I never figured someone would just walk into my bldg's yard and yoink it, but I was wrong.  They must have come along and removed the wheel from the fork, then pulled the fork up out of the u-lock, which was also my fault b/c I usually use the chain to lock the bike to something via the back wheel and then use the u-lock to lock the front wheel to the frame.  Had I used the chain as well as the u-lock like I usually do, I'm sure it wouldn't have happened.  So it's extra frustrating b/c I could have easily prevented it by spending a extra minute before going inside.  And since I got it used I never bothered to write down the registration number, although the cops don't actually go looking for lost bikes, so I don't even have that.  I was so frustrated and stunned, I think I stared at the lonely wheel for a minute before unlocking it.  

So the lesson here is always make sure you lock your bike up completely, even at place that you feel is safe.

On the bright side I still have the u-lock and now I have a spare wheel.

Oh and its a Trek hybrid (forgot the exact model) with a chipped orange paint job, but I don't imagine it'll turn up.

Title: Re: Arrrrrrrrgggggghhhhhhh someone stole my bike!!!
Post by: megc on April 03, 2007, 04:51:27 PM
I have always bought Kryptonite u-locks.  They were an excellent investment, and yes, about as much as I paid for my last bike.  When I lived in Berkeley, there were a rash of thefts of bikes that used the "wide" u-lock.  Thieves were able to fit a small car jack between the two posts of the u-lock and jack it apart.  Crazy.  That's when we were advised to buy the "narrow" u-locks, and do the lock-front-wheel-to-back-wheel-to-post kind of locking.  I never had a bike stolen that way.  But this is NYC and everything is different!

Used to be that you could unlock a u-lock with a ballpoint pen.  Thank goodness the Kryptonite people learned how to make locks that foiled that.

The return policy on Kryptonite locks is sweet, too.
