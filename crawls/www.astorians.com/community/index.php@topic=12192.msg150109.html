Excellent tailor in Astoria?
« on: September 09, 2008, 11:15:19 PM »
I'm looking for a good tailor.  I have a couple of favorite (men's) shirts that I would like to have the sleeves taken up.  Most typical tailors will just take it up from the cuff.  A good tailor will take it up at the shoulder area, thereby preserving the cuff openings.  This requires taking the sleeves off the shirt and reattaching them after the excess length is removed.  Of course, it requires more expertise--but not that much more difficult from, say, hemming some pants.  Obviously, I'd be willing to pay a more for a good tailor.  So, what say ye, expert Astorians?

So, I went to San Remo today.  Looks like the shop is run by two old school Italian guys, San and Remo.  From what I gathered, one does Men's and the other does Women's clothing.  After talking to Remo (or was it San?) and being called "short," :oops: it was determined that I should take up the sleeves from the cuffs up.  But, he will move up the openings as well, so the sleeves don't look out of balance.  I brought two shirts there and was encouraged to try both on while being measured.  It turned out and even though both of my shirts were the same size, from the same brand, one actually has slightly longer sleeves than the other.  Needless to say, I appreciate the old school thoroughness that's hard to find these days.  I'll pick up my shirts sometime next week and will give my impression of the workmanship.  Each shirt costs $20 to have both sleeves taken up.

On a side note, while I was in the dressing room my girlfriend witnessed a funny exchange between San (or was it Remo?) and another lady.  She wanted to have this circa 80s leopard print faux fur jacket relined because it has sentimental value to her.  He, on the other hand, thought that the jacket was "ugly" and refused to do it.  After some back-and-forth exchanges, the lady brought the jacket home, unchanged.  My gf had a good laugh about it all.   :-D

Old school dudes are awesome.   :mrgreen:
