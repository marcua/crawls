Title: Artists of Astoria, Unite!…a night of literary readings, Sunday, October 9th
Post by: hellgatereview on October 08, 2011, 02:28:08 PM
Artists of Astoria, Unite!…a night of literary readings of new work by Catherine Kapphahn (reading from “The Grief Book: Meditations on Loss, Learning, and Motherhood”) and René Georg Vasicek (reading from his short story “The Defectors”). Plus Special Guest, Nita Noveno.

Date: Sunday, October 9th at 7 PM
Location: The Giving Tree Yoga Studio’s Community Events
22-56 31st Street (Between Ditmars and 23rd Avenue)
Astoria, NY 11105 (N or Q train to Ditmars)

Catherine Kapphahn received her MFA from Columbia University. She teaches writing at Lehman College, City University of New York in the Bronx.  She also teaches yoga at the Giving Tree Yoga Studio in Astoria. Her writing has appeared in CURE Magazine, Sunday Salon, and Ars Medica: a Journal of Medicine, the Arts, and Humanity, The Wanderlust Review, and the anthology This is the Way We Say Goodbye (Feminist Press). She is the 2011 recipient the Individual Artist Grant from Queens Council on the Arts. She lives in Astoria with her husband and son.

René Georg Vasicek lives in Astoria with his wife and son. His writing has appeared or is forthcoming in Antique Children, Camera Obscura, The Delinquent, Gargoyle, Mid-American Review, Post Road, The Prague Revue, and elsewhere. He is a 2011 Individual Artist Grant recipient from the Queens Council on the Arts; a 2009 Artists’ Fellowship recipient of the New York Foundation for the Arts; and a Pushcart Prize nominee.

Nita Noveno was born and raised in Southeast Alaska. She is a graduate of the New School MFA Creative Writing Program in Creative Nonfiction, and is the founder and co-host of Sunday Salon (www.sundaysalon.com), a monthly prose reading series in its ninth year in NYC. She also edits the online literary zine, Salonzine. Her writing has appeared in The MacGuffin and Ducts.org, amongst other places. She is working on a collection of stories that merge tropical and temperate landscapes and the disparate lives of a father and daughter. Nita teaches English Composition at Lehman College and LaGuardia Community College.

This event is made possible (in part) with funds from the Decentralization Program, a regrant program of the New York State Council on the Arts, administered by the Queens Council on the Arts. Special thanks to Anne-Margaret Redding and The Giving Tree Yoga Studio for providing this beautiful space. $5 suggested donation.
