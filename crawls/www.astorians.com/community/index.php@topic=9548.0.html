I don't either, especially now that his next best buddy (and oil benefactor) is Hugo Chavez. :|

Whatever happens, I really hope it goes peacefully. The last thing I want is Raul to send the tanks against a bunch of demonstrating moms or something horrible like that.

Unfortunately, opening up the economy will have dire (and inevitable) short-term consequences. Prices will skyrocket, Cuban currency will become worthless, and the economy will be up for grabs for a few, connected individuals. It will be many steps backward before Cuba moves forward, and all kinds of things can happen in the meantime.

I'd like to see Cuba look toward becoming a hyper-modern economy. Forget the service/tourist industry and get right into technology, banking, organic agriculture, etc. Microenterprises, media companies, and on and on. However, I seriously question whether they have the intellectual capital to do that, though. Decades of indoctrination aren't conducive to establishing -- and fostering -- meaningful transformation.

Hate to say it, but Cuba's in for a world of hurt. Having seen firsthand the pains of transition in Eastern Europe, I don't see how they can avoid it.  :|
