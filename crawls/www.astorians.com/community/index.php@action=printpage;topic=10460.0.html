Title: Astoria Symphony Concert
Post by: rebb_ on May 13, 2008, 05:02:42 PM
The ASTORIA SYMPHONY and Manhattan’s EMPIRE CITY MEN’S CHORUS will present a dramatic and controversial concert at the LaGuardia Performing Arts Center in Long Island City, Queens, on Sunday, May 18, 3:00 p.m. The program explores topics including the Holocaust, the Iraq war, death, and forbidden love. Silas Nathaniel Huff, music director of the Astoria Symphony, and Jonathan Babcock, Artistic Director of the Empire City Men’s Chorus share the podium and conducting duties. The LaGuardia Performing Arts Center is in LaGuardia College’s “E” building at 31-10 Thompson Avenue in Long Island City, Queens (subway: 7-train to 33rd Street).

Tickets to all ASTORIA SYMPHONY concerts in Queens are $18 at the door for adults, $12 for seniors, students, and kids 12-18, and FREE for kids under 12. Discount tickets are available in advance at http://www.AstoriaMusic.org. One half of the tickets sales from this concert on Sunday, May 18, will support AIDS support, education, and research via Classical Action: Performing Arts Against AIDS.

This event might be of particular interest to the LGBT community in Astoria for the reasons described below. 

The concert was banned by the venue that it was originally intended to be performed in because of it's homosexual theme (specifically David del Tredici's Queer Hosannas and Stefan Weisman's Jonathan and David, about those two Biblical characters who may have been much more than friends).

In response to this attempt at censorship, half of the proceeds will be donated to Classical Action: Performing Arts Against AIDS.
