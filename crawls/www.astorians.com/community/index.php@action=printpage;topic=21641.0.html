Title: Re: Waterbug season
Post by: lanseaux on September 12, 2011, 01:33:07 PM
I saw one over the weekend for the first time in several months.  We had a horrible run of them in the early summer despite spraying and taking other preventive measures.  I generally expect I may see one or two when the weather changes seasonally, especially when it rains.  Given the weather last week (lots of rain, cooler temps), I wasn't surprised.

If you don't have pets you can put down boric acid in the areas you suspect they're entering from.  It doesn't kill them instantly, but they will die if they walk through it.  Also try to plug (steel wool works well) or caulk any holes/gaps along baseboards/walls/radiator pipes.

Title: Re: Waterbug season
Post by: yippee1999 on June 01, 2016, 11:49:41 AM

They're baaaack... 

Is this indeed an Astoria thing....if so I wonder why...

In my other apts that I've lived in in other nabes for 12 years, I think I saw one waterbug ever.

I've been in Astoria for 4 years, 2 in a modern high-rise bldg. (where I saw no waterbugs) and 2 years in a small family-owned apt bldg.  And in this family-owned apt bldg., I've noticed waterbugs, and they seem to be seasonal, as in ....RIGHT NOW.  Granted, we're not over-run with them but....  in the past few weeks I've noticed maybe 5 waterbugs in the hallways.

Last night I went for a walk in the nabe and noticed a tree that for some reason had a bunch of what appeared to be waterbugs on them.  I was a bit traumatized that I might step on one in the dark..

Then I come home and saw a live one in the hallway.  Sprayed it with bug spray and killed it.

Come inside my apt and as I'm getting ready for bed...see something on the floor.  A waterbug running away behind my couch!!!  I was afraid however to touch anything with  my hand...didn't want to try and move the couch to get to him, in case he was ON the couch.  So I used a long-handled duster to try and 'bang' things to scare him into moving  out into the open, where I could spray him.  No such luck.  Finally had to go to bed but not after closing my BR door and pushing a box up against the door, AND a laundry bag, all in an effort to plug up the gap under the door so he couldn't come into my bedroom.  lol

Anyway, I had trouble sleeping cuz I kept wondering what the little bugger was up to...where he was.  I decided I'm gonna try a hypnotist I  used many years ago (he was good) to see if he can help me with my fear of bugs.  Intellectually I find insects fascinating...it's just that...I hate being 'surprised' by sudden movements in my home and my biggest fear is that if I see a bug and am NOT able to kill it, that a day or two later I'll go to pick something up, and  the bug will be there and crawl over my hand  or something.  I really want to get rid of this (admittedly senseless) fear.  My biggest fear however is centipedes.  I really really dislike them.

Anyway, besides some of the other tips mentioned previously in this thread, also be sure to add weather strips under the bottom edge of your apartment door.  It not only helps keep out sound from the hallway, but helps keep hot air out in the summer and cold air out in the winter, and also prevents bugs from slipping under the door.  Note that many metal apartment doors also have a RECESSED area within the bottom edge of the door, so you need to feel with your hand that you are indeed filling in all the gap with weather stripping.  I buy rolls of the weather stripping that are made of like grey or black 'felt' and where one side has 'sticky tape'.

Also helps to keep your home very clean so there are no other bugs (i.e., spiders) that serve as food for other bugs like centipedes or water bugs.  Spiders obviously create little webs so you want to dust often and vacuum especially in crevices, corners, behind and  under furniture etc. 

When I first moved into this apt I also paid a guy to seal up every little crack.  He even pulled out the fridge and  the stove and sealed up big gaps between the wall and the floor...
