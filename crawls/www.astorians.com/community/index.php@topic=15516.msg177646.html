Quote from: Christine on July 09, 2009, 09:21:42 PM
Grand Central is one of my favorite landmarks, yet this is the first time I've noticed colors on that clock. How beautiful! What's your camera set-up? Macro lens?

Thanks Christine. Grand Central is pretty amazing. I need to spend some more time exploring it before I make any more shots of it. It's photographed so much that it's hard not to capture a cliche image. Just stand in the Main Concourse for a few minutes and count hot many people walk up the stairs at the east or west ends and stop directly in the middle and take a picture. Or how many times people stand in the middle of the busy commuter traffic and snap a pic while dragging their shutter, so the crowd becomes a blur of commotion. I've got a couple ideas. I just hope they turn out the way I want.

My setup is pretty simple actually. I have a Nikon D90 and just three non-pro grade lenses -- Sigma 10-20mm, Nikon 16-85mm, and Nikon 70-300mm. My most unusual piece of gear is a Singh-Ray variable neutral density filter. It was a little pricey and was a treat to myself, but it's also one of those items that I need to take capture some of the images I make. There's a lot of equipment I would like, but I try really hard not to become a gear head. I prefer to work with what I have and work the limitations and boundaries into the images that I produce. I'm pretty sure if I could afford the $5k+ cameras and a dozen $2k+ lenses I wouldn't spend as much time thinking about how I'm going to capture some of the images that I take because I would probably be too absorbed in acquiring gear. Now having said that, if anyone wants to give me big fancy lenses, camera bodies, and other equipment I won't in a million years turn them down. ;-)
