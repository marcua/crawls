When I got serious about paying off my credit card debt (way higher than yours, incidentally), I made up an Excel spreadsheet that listed the balance of each in one column, a blank column for monthly payments, and a third column with my checking account balance in another.  I hid all the cards away (you can cut them up, but closing the accounts hurts your credit score) and started carrying and using ONLY my debit card for purchases.  I focused on one card at a time (on the others I would pay more than the minimum, but something like $100 or $200), starting with the card with the highest balance (even though it wasn't the highest interest rate, it was the highest interest charge every month).  For whatever card I focused on, I paid the absolute maximum I could afford every month.  I was able to pay off my entire credit card debt in 12 months doing this.

It does take discipline, but it works.  Right now credit is extremely tight - I don't know how available bank loans are right now.  Personally, instead of messing with yet another line of credit, I'd recommend something like the above, especially if you don't need to consolidate for financial reasons.

Like you, I also bank with HSBC, and had multiple credit cards with high balances... last year, I took out a personal loan from HSBC to consolidate everything. The whole process was very easy-- I brought all of my statements to the bank, sat down with the bank guy and answered some questions. Then he was able to tell me what kind of loan amount and interest rate I could get-- and even worked with me to see how we could get the best deal. In some cases, if you agree to close certain credit card accounts on the spot, they can get you a better interest rate deal for the loan-- because it is unfavorable in their eyes for you to still have all of that credit available to you after they clear it out and put it on the loan.

I got a loan that covered paying off everything, so now I've just got one lump payment each month (which turns out to be less than I had to pay to cover all of the different cards before). The interest rate I got wasn't the greatest, but at least it's less than what the credit cards were charging me.

The bank did all the work for me-- contacted all of my credit cards and sent the payment in and everything. So all I had to deal with was any tiny leftover interest amounts on my next credit card statements (a word of advice-- slightly overpay your credit card balances, that's the only way to zero them out in one billing period-- they'll mail you a check back for the overage). I only use ONE credit card now, as sparingly as possible (my HSBC Mastercard, which has the lowest credit limit of all my previous cards, to force myself to limit using it).

For what it's worth, the folks at the Union Square HSBC branch were extremely nice and helpful. And trust me, however self-conscious you may feel about the amount of your debts, they've seen much worse :)
