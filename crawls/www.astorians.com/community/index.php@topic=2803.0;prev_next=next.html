I quit smoking in 1981because I am prone to upper respiratory infections.  I was 23 at the time.  I think it may be helpful to pause and look at why people smoke.  In my opinion, I think since 1981 there has been a great change in how people view smoking.  It is not as popular.  It is also outlawed in office space.  I think there are always exceptions, people with their own offices who close their door and have a smoke or go outside the restaurant or work place.

Looking at the reasons why people smoke, is not excuse for saying it is okay to smoke.  But it provides reasons.  By opening up that discussion, can there be a way to not smoke.  For instance, if I said that I smoke because it really calms my nerves.  Or I smoke because it helps me to not overeat.  Okay, so the reasons for smoking can be to calm the nerves or not overeat.  one can saw it is just an excuse.  But what if we looked at the information and say why do I need to calm my nerves, what is causing me to make my nerves shot and what can I do to calm myself instead of smoking.  For instance, am I totally drained from my job? 
Can I try meditation or belly breaths?

Anyway these are just suggestions.  For someone who used to smoke, I don't find it helpful to repeatedly hear how horrible smoking is.  I already know that.  If one is expressing their feelings which includes disliking smoking, that I can relate to.  But as a way to get someone to stop smoking, I am not sure how effective it would be. 

As for me, I loved smoking.  It totally calmed my nerves.  In my heyday, it was a cool thing to do.  I thought of quitting.  getting pneumonia when I was young, made up my mind for me.
