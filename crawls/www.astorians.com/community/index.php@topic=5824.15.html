This is a neat post guys  :mrgreen:
I was thinking- if you're willing to drive to Boston then why not continue just a little further North and explore the Cape Ann/Northshore area? My boyfriend and I actually both have our families there- (Salem and Gloucester) so we go fairly often- but it's still like a mini vacay!
Salem is fun- but my favorite areas are Gloucester and Rockport. The ocean is beautiful!!!! There are great scenic drives complete with New England lighthouses and places with great lobster and clam chowder! ( I actually don't eat lobster- but just going by what everyone else orders and says when we're there)...Also- if you're into art, Gloucester has the oldest working artists's colony in the U.S.- called Rocky Neck artist's colony. http://www.rockyneckartcolony.org/artists.php

I don't know- I'm an ocean gal, and whenever I'm in that area I feel so relaxed and happy. Spring and summer are great times to go! I'd say Spring is even better because you won't have to compete with the tourists headed towards the beaches. Cape Ann is made up of Gloucester, Rockport, Essex, and Manchester by the Sea. And there are a lot of other coastal towns that are equally wonderful in that area too.

Here's the general Cape Ann link too: http://www.cape-ann.com/
and one more: http://www.capeannvacations.com/

I think it's about time that I figure out a weekend getaway with my guy too!
