Haha!  You crack me up, flooz! 

To answer your question, it's a thin layer of genoise-type spong cake on the outside and underneath, and the filling is a raspberry bavarian cream (raspberry puree, creme anglaise, whipped cream, gelatin).  "The mirroir" is a glaze made from more raspberry puree, sugar syrup, and gelatin.  Sanesha (a lady in my class) made it and she did an amazing job.  It was delicious.

I'll be done with classes in December, and will hopefully be hired on full time by whatever establishment at which I will have done my externship . . . unless I hate it, of course.  It's a certificate program in Pastry Arts, so it's just a 4-quarter program, but I'll only be taking 3 quarters to complete it since I have a lot of credits that transfer from my old school, The Art Institute of California - Orange County.  The school here, The Art Institute of New York City (formerly the New York Restaurant School) is in a big building at Canal and Varick Streets.  Eventually, I'd like to own a little patisserie, but that is years down the line.  For now, I'd just be really happy to earn a decent living (i.e. being able to at least pay rent) by working in a bakery or doing desserts for a hotel or restaurant.  That will be my big task for when I get back here from my trip home - employment!!
