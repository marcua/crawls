I used to live in LA too, so I've seen my share of celebrities. But even though she's not A-list, I like to tell people my Bea Arthur story.

When I was doing props for a play at the Tiffany Theater in LA, I had to wash some of the dishes from a food scene, and the only sink was in the ladies room. Bea Arthur and her friend were in there one time, talking to each other from different stalls.

Friend: ...and he said that people who get sick the most are people who don't wash their hands after going to the bathroom. (exits stall, washes hands)
Bea: And you had to tell me that?

The friend laughed and left the restroom. And then Bea Arthur exits the stall, looks in the mirror, puffs up her hair a little, and leaves without washing her hands.

I know. It's not a juicy story and she's not Robert Smith or anything. I just want this to be a sort of public service announcement. If you ever meet Bea Arthur, you'll want to think twice before shaking her hand. Or licking it.

If that's not exciting enough for you, there was also that one time that the drummer from the Dead Kennedys grabbed my derriere in a bar in Tokyo and tried to kiss me. I thought about just letting it happen, not because he was in the Dead Kennedys but because I was drunk and apathetic. But then I remembered that that would be kind of slutty of me, so I made some excuses and left. Poor guy. I think he was bored, and I was one of the few people there who could speak English.

Great topic! I had totally been planning to start this thread at some point, but never got around to it. Not that I have all that much to report. I really don't know actors/actresses/famous people well at all to recognize them, so I'm sure I've been in the presence of more than I actually know - and usually have to have them pointed out to me.

That said, this summer I saw Jimmy Fallon eating dinner with 2 other people right by the big open window in a restaurant on maybe 25th St, between 5th & Madison or around there. I walked by and thought it was him, but doubted myself since I never see celebrities. So I turned back around and then walked slowly past a second time. Yup it was him! So cute!

I also saw Katie Holmes and some other famous kid several years ago (before she was part of "TomKat") outside at a bar near the 57th St N stop- had to have them pointed out to me though.

Oh and I used to work at World Music Institute producing concerts, and a couple famous people came to our Flamenco Festival. Specifically I remember Viggo Mortensen, who played Aragorn in the Lord of the Rings trilogy! Very exciting, though I never would have recognized him with his short hair and soccer shirt on, if it weren't for my friends. We were all so excited, and kept track of his whereabouts, and he even bought a CD from our sales table, so we got his AUTOGRAPH when he signed his credit card receipt ;-)

Oh, also saw Jason Bigg (is that his name?) and Woody Allen when they were filming by Ditmars a few years back - they were doing a driving scene, which was really interesting to see because I saw them in the little red car, on top of a flat bed truck that pulled them along so they didn't have to really drive. What movie was that - I never checked it out, but they filmed some scenes in Astoria.
