I just wanted to create a thread to discuss and share information about the current financial atmosphere.

Citigroup to buy Wachovia

Wall Street bail-out up for vote

Quote
The deal addresses several of the key concerns raised by both Democrats and Republicans:

  • The government will release the money in tranches - $250bn straight away, and $100bn at the request of the White House; Congress can veto the release of the remaining $350bn
  • Banks that accept bail-out money will have to hand over shares in return, which allows tax payers to benefit from the banks' recovery
  • Top bankers, meanwhile, will see their pay limited, and "golden parachutes" - huge payments when they leave the firm - will be banned
  • The banking industry will have to help finance the bail-out if the money can not be recovered from the struggling banks themselves
  • Four agencies will monitor the deal, including an independent Inspector General and a bipartisan oversight board
  • Banks will be obliged to join an insurance programme to protect them against the losses of mortgage-backed securities

Quote from: NYCMacUser on October 23, 2008, 09:10:05 AM
I guess that means that the this Republican administration is basically at fault for this crisis.

Both parties have economic blood on their hands for approving a huge nationalization of private banking functions and a massive 700 billion dollar bailout. 

But the roots of the subprime collapse go back to the Clinton administration, which rejiggered the Community Reinvestment Act to boost minority and poor home ownership.  While boosting minority and poor home ownership is a laudable goal that I am sure we all support, this is an example of government intervention gone wrong.  And community activist groups played right into the opportunity to put people in homes who could not afford to pay for them.  Here is a must read from way back in 2000 which basically predicts the whole mess: http://www.city-journal.org/html/10_1_the_trillion_dollar.html  The Dems were warned I believe back in 2004 about the impending Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac risk, but claimed at the time that Fannie and Freddie were stable.  Presumably because those minority voters and community activists tend to break for the dems.

I was at a conference yesterday on the economic crisis and someone brought up the G.I. bill, which also had the goal of boosting home ownership.  They asked a panelist how this differed from the government aim to boost poor home ownership, and what went wrong.  But, as the panelist answered, the difference back then was that they did not change the rules for veterans who could not afford to buy homes.  If they didn't meet the same basic accounting standards as anyone else, they would not be permitted to buy homes.

The chicanery with mortgages, plus shady securitization vehicles caused the current economic mess.  To suggest that the Republicans (or any one cause) is behind this is grossly inaccurate.  Also, to attribute subprime, a key democratic goal, to the Republicans is just silly.

If one good thing will come of this, it's that the notion that everyone is entitled to own a home will be corrected.
