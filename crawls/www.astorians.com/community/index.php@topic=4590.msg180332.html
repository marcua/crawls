As a two year resident of the Acropolis, I'll offer my insight on day-to-day living for those interested.

The Acropolis is not a horrible place to live.  I have heard that there are great variations between apartments, but having just seen my own, I can't say for certain what others look like.  For my roommate and I, this has been a good starter apartment.  We were both moving from far away, so having to conduct our apartment searches over Craig's List, and at the time, we got what we thought was a somewhat decent deal in paying $1800 for a two bedroom and no broker's fee.  The market has changed a lot over the past two years and I have now seen our equivalent unit going for $1550 on Craig's List, and if we do in fact stay, plan to renegotiate our rent.  In general however, we probably could have found better deals even two years ago, but we were on a time crunch and searching from afar, so we ran with our first offer.  Now that I have been in Astoria for two years and seen a good deal of other people's apartments, I've realized that we could buy more for less and do not intend to stay here once our lease expires.

My biggest gripe with my current apartment is the size. My bedroom has a full size mattress.  It touches three walls.  If there were no other furniture in my room (and if the radiator didn't monopolize a whole other corner), there would maybe be room for one other full-size mattress next to it.  Of course, that's not the case, as I have a dresser and nightstand in my room, meaning that all in all, I have about a 2x5 patch of clear space, and half of my closet is inaccessible.  My roommate's room is a little larger with a different layout so she's got some more room (perhaps a 7X5 patch, with full access to her closet, and radiator in a less obstructive spot).  Our kitchen is just a row of cabinets.  It standing at the kitchen counter, if you take one step back, you're in the living room.  Our stove's door cannot be opened the whole way without hitting the cabinets behind them.  The bathroom is teeny tiny as well.  We've just quickly outgrown the space.  The novelty of our "cute tiny apartment" was worn off.

Our apartment was allegedly renovated before we got here, but there were still random holes in the wall which we had to caulk and tape over.  When we first moved in, we had roaches.  Thankfully, after the first month or two, I believe the building hired an exterminator and now we say maybe one every two months, which I kind of feel is inevitable in this city.  While I have seen the mattresses out my the curb, we have NOT had any problems with bedbugs. 

Other daily gripes include the noise.  The stairwells here absorb noise like no other I've heard.  Every footstep sounds like a bowling ball being dropped and you hear every word of conversation when someone is going up and down.  Thankfully, in each building I believe there are only 10 apartments, but still, that can get tiring.  Similarly, the walls and ceiling and floors are quite thin.  Just hope that you don't get a neighbor with little kids, because those little feet running around above you get old really quick.  There is also the street and courtyard noise.  The usual apartment building shenanigans of drunk girls yelling, music blasting, and kids skateboarding every once in awhile.  Summer weekend evenings are especially bad. 

I personally have also found the management company and super to be very unresponsive. 

Overall, in spite of the gripes there are plenty of good things about the apartment.  It is pretty secure, requiring multiple keys for entry and now with a video buzzer system.  It is also in a terrific location, just a five minute walk to the train station.  Basically, it's an okay apartment for an okay price (now that they've dropped it).  I think most people who can afford to live here can do better, but they could also do a lot worse.
