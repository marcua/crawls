Hey everyone. Long time reader, first time poster. Based on the reviews on here, I opted to stop by SpaHydra today for a Brazilian wax. Needless to say, I was not disappointed. I've had one wax before, at a terrible place in the Village, and have been incredibly resistant to the idea of trying it again. I'm going away on a beach vacation next week, and this seemed a good a time as any to give it another go.

Everyone mentioned that the place was hard to find, but I had no trouble. They have a huge banner outside their building that says "Spa Hydra". It's impossible to miss. Perhaps this is new?

The waiting area is serene and beautiful, and I was the only person waiting. I got there 20 minutes early because their site said that first-timers needed to fill out paperwork. There was no paperwork handed to me, but I did get taken in early.

Due to my sensitive skin, I opted to pay the extra $10 for the supposedly gentler "hard wax" rather than the strip wax. As mentioned in previous posts, all of their products are organic. The hard wax is essentially a wax that dries on your body, and then the hair is peeled off with the actual wax. Crystal worked on me, and she was very sweet and thorough. Where other places have left stray hairs, she did her best to catch everything she could. My body kept tensing (naturally) and she was very understanding and accommodating. She did a wonderful job of making me feel comfortable in an uncomfortable situation.

The entire process was done within 30 minutes and, considering the procedure, was relatively pain free. Yes, it hurt, but much less so than it had the previous time, and than it could have.

The end result was so fabulous and the process/price so temperate that I plan to start having them done monthly. If you're looking for a great relatively-inexpensive wax in Astoria (did I mention that it took me 10 minutes to get there?) then SpaHydra is HIGHLY recommended. Ask for Crystal!

I highly recommend Marina at Natura Spa. She is extremely nice and friendly, but also super professional. She focused on getting the job done exactly how you want it. She listens to what you want and doesn't try to tell you "how it is" or charge you extra for making the line look neat, doing the back, etc. If you've gone to a few wax places before, you know why this is crucial.

I have had my share of Brazilian wax experiences, and until now I thought the best, most meticulous job was done by a flaky, self-aggrandizing Italian woman in a small salon in Rome. Now I know that you can have a great wax close to home without the social or physical pain that can accompany the process.

Natura Brazilian Spa looks nice and clean. I went straight to the back for an overdue Brazilian. Marina warned me that after 3 months of neglect, it would be a bit more painful. But she did it so quickly that it was relatively easy to take. Also, she is a perfectionist - she treats the wax like a work of art, and her attention to detail resulted in the best down-there haircut ever! And she was as gentle as she was efficient. She put several soothing creams on at the end that smelled really great, and there is no wax residue left at all. I cannot say enough good things about my wax experience with Marina at Natura Spa. And I am very, very picky.

Did I mention that I paid $30 for the best Brazilian wax imaginable? I am actually feeling vain about how I look down there right now! Never thought it could look this good. I gave her a 50% tip! She works 6 days a week and definitely needs it - she is older and her hands are always getting cramped (though you don't see her complaining).

I say stay in Astoria and head over to see Marina, unless you like to ride the train and overpay to have Manhattan "beauticians" do a mediocre job and make you feel like you owe them for going down there.
