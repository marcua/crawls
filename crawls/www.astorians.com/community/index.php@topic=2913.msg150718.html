I recently found my first apartment in Astoria. Here's what I learned from the experience:

1. Have all the documents you will need to rent a place prepared before you start searching. Including: W2s from past 2 years, two or three pay stubs, a letter of employment, bank statements, divers license (or other form of photo ID), references (phone numbers of friends, old landlords, etc.), and guarantor's info if necessary. You may also want to run your own credit report for free from one of the credit reporting websites to avoid this fee.

2. Take some time to familiarize yourself with the neighborhood on google maps, or by walking around. Go all the way up and down 31st street, from LIC to Ditmars.

3. It is possible to rent from owners, and anyone who has some time to search should begin this way. The by owner section of craigslist should do the trick, or look for open house signs in the neighborhood.

3. If you are short on time, or cannot find any by owner rentals, using a broker is a reasonable choice. The STANDARD RATE for brokers in Astoria is one month's rent (~8% of a year's rent). If anyone charges you more than that, you should neotiate them down before signing a contract with the broker.

4. We met with several brokers. I would recommend using multiple brokers, as they don't all have access to the same listings. Ask about specific details from their postings to make sure they aren't bs-ing. Be sure you don't sign any statements saying you cannot use other brokers in the area when you initially meet with brokers.

- Angelica, whose office is on 36th and broadway, is a hot mess who consistently forgets important details (eg. how much we were willing to pay for an apt.) She posts exact locations, but her apartments are usually far away from where she says they are.
- Buy Sell Realty had excellent customer service, and was clean and professional. I wish we could have rented an apartment through them, but unfortunately they didn't have what we were looking for.
- We ended up using R&R Realty (Richard and Rashida). They were very good, but tried to charge 11% for a broker's fee. We negotiated it down.

5. See as many apartments as you can, even if they don't sound like exactly what you're looking for. You'll have a better idea of what is overpriced and what isn't if you just look at many apartments.

In general astoria is safe, beautiful and a really nice place to live. I'm happy with our decision to live here, and I hope these tips ease the stressful process of finding an apartment.
