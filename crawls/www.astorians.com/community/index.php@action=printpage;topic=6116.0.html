- Link to ped path to the Bronx

I rode to Randall's island yesterday and last weekend as well.  Love that new ramp!!

As far as passing - if you're going towards each other everyone should stay to the right.  If you're coming upon pedestrians or slower riders you should give warning ahead of time with a horn or bell if you have one and call out "on your left."  And pass on the left.  If the person is on the left side of the path "on your right" will do as well.  Though I think most people who aren't used to being passed by bikes generally get startled and can't figured out which way to move to get out of the way... so... it doesn't always work.  I slow WAY down when passing - unless the person is WAAy over to one side and I don't think they're going to suddenly move into my path.  Then you also have the problem of people walking (and riding) with headphones on - no one can hear the warnings.   

As a pedestrian I'd try to stay out of the middle of the path - but the riders really should be slowing down in such a narrow area. 

That new path cut the the travel time to my job down to 25 minutes.  I'm trying to get up the energy to start riding to work.  Maybe by June....

Title: Re: Anyone walked to Randall's Island
Post by: Bowbridge on August 01, 2008, 06:32:37 PM
I run over the Triborough a few times a week.  As others have said, you get on the bridge at 27th and Hoyt.  The construction crews have the temporary staircases in place on both the Queens side and the Randall's side; if you are biking over, there are those steel rail things to wheel your bike up, but it's still a push to get the bike up.  Once you get off the bridge, you go down the ramp and at the bottom, you will find more construction.  The best way to continue is to keep heading north for about a 1/4 mile and you will see a road going to the right (goes to the picnic areas) and the new bike/walk/run path to the left.  Going to the right isn't a fun as it used to be, because it's all fenced off where you used to go back under the Triborough.  If you go left, you can use the b/w/r path to go all the way to the green pedestrian bridge that goes into Manhattan at about 102nd St.  They did a nice job on the path; separate paths for bikes and peds with some nice flower beds inbetween.  If instead of going right or left, you continue straight, you go past the track stadium, the golf range, and the mini golf.  If you make a left after the stadium (between the stadium and the golf) you can follow the road down to the river and go left (which will hook you back into the b/w/r path in about 1/2 mile) or go right which will put you under the Manhattan part of the Triborough.  You can go over that span or continue around the north part of Randall's and go into the softball fields area.  You get a nice view of North Brother Island (where the Slocum wound up) and that whole bay area.  Keep looping and you will wind up back by the Stadium.  If anyone wants the mileages for different sections, I'll post them.
