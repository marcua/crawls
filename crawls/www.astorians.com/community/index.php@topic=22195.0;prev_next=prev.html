Army Pictorial Center
« on: April 12, 2007, 10:21:08 AM »
In 1966 I returned from Vietnam and found myself assigned to the Army pictorial Center in Long Island City. Here I was, a young man from the hills of Eastern Kentucky, living in one of the largest cities in the world. I was totally lost and confused but with the help of some very good New York friends, I manager to survive.

We (my wife and I) found an apartment in Astoria, just off Ditmars. We lived there for a year or so and then we moved to Fort Totten, which then was housing for military people who were assigned to duty in the New York City area.

We made lots of friends while living in Astoria, everyone was so friendly.

As I mentioned, I was assigned to the Army Pictorial Center, in Long Island City (link to their site is below). The Army Pictorial Center made all of the Armies training films.  While there I was lucky enough to work with several big named movie stars, such as: John Wayne, Darin McGavin & Danny Kaye.

I understand now that the Center is a Film Museum, I hope to visit it some day soon. Anyway here is the link, there are pictures of myself there, taken back then.
Let me know if you have had a chance to visit the museum and what it was like.

Larry
Lived in Astoria 1966,67 & 68

Actually the Army Pictorial Center is now the Kauffman Astoria Studios and the American Museum of Moving Image is one of the buildings in the complex. It originally opened as the Famous Players/ Lasky Studios and the became Paramount Pictures. When Paramount moved al Studio l operations to the West Coast, the Army took it over during WWII and it became the Army Pictoria Center.
