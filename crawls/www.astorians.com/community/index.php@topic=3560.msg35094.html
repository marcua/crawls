<snip>

Artificial trans fats are found in some shortenings, margarine and frying oils and turn up in foods from pie crusts to french fries to doughnuts.

Well, it is called heart-stopping goodness.  ;)

But yes, it's true.  Krispy Kreme donuts average about 5g trans fat per donut, according to the nutritional information on their website.  Incidentally, most of their donut varieties contain trivial amounts of cholesterol.

(The NY Times also has an article today that notes that the NYC Board of Health voted unanimously yesterday to ban trans fats in NYC restaurants.  There's a comment period and public hearing scheduled, though, and no guarantee that the measure will be fully adopted.  There are naturally-occuring trans fats in many foods, but the use of partially hydrogenated oils is the primary source of trans fats in Americans' diets.)
