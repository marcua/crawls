Home Depot not a hardware store?!?!?!
« on: July 07, 2006, 01:53:59 PM »
So, I go into Home De(s)pot at 3rd ave and 59th Street earlier this week looking for a small part to connect my shower curtan rod to the ceiling mount.  I speak with at least 6 different employees for help in locating the part.  Each tells me to look in a different part of the store, and with no luck from anyone.  Each employee esentially doesn't really want to help me find anything, and just passes me off to someone else.  I finally get to what I believe is the proper area of the store, and find the employee responsible for that section (bathroom accesories).  After waiting about 15 minutes for her to help a customer on the phone, I ask her for the part.  With a baffled look on her face, she looks briefly through a store catalogue, and doesn't find the part I am looking for.  And here's the kicker...

She says to me, "We don't have this part.  You need to go to a HARDWARE STORE!"

To which I replied, "So what the hell is this place?!?!"  She said, that Home Depot was too big to stock such small parts.

WOW!!!  Well, I guess I'll be supporting the little guys from now on, and don't really plan on shopping there at all.

Knowing and not knowing your home repair stuff has little to do with your sex.  Much more to do with wether or not you ever had to do any home repair.  Or if you had a shop in the basement growing up.  Or if you had shop class.  Or if you just decided that you wanted to know about hardware.

I grew up in a hardward family - Grandpa and great uncle owned the store and then my dad and uncle owned the store.  And now in it's third generation in my family (4th I think overall) "the store" has had to morph into something more niche market b/c the "little guy" has such a hard time competing with the prices of the monster/box stores.  So... people are left with generic/impersonal service all over the country in lots of arenas.  Hardware... books....pharmacy come to mind.

As for our local Home Depot: UGH!!!  I hate that place possibly more than any other store - anywhere.  I swore off it years ago and haven't been back.  I like the hardware stores on Broadway (there are two - one in the 30s and one in the 20s.  I like the store on Steinway between 31st/28th.  And as someone mentioned Metropolital Lumber - which I'd forgotten about.  I don't remember what made me go in a few years back - I'd assumed it was just Lumber - but they do have the largest selection of all the ones in the neighborhood.
