This fall I set up a small bird feeder in my backyard to attract cardinals. One day when a couple of cardinals, mourning doves, sparrows, and not-invited Mr. Squirrel were feasting on the sunflower and safflower seeds I heard a strange "kak-kak-kak-kak-kak-kak". I looked out the back window and sitting on my neighbor's laundry pole was a small hawk-like bird. Stupid me went out to the backyard to get a closer look and of course it flew away to a tree across the alley, but nearby was another one sitting on an apartment building's TV antenna. To this day I'm not sure if they were cooper's hawks or sharp shinned hawks, but since I live near the park perhaps it was a sharp shin...or cooper! They definitely weren't red tails because these were about the size of crows. Red tails are bigger. Both sharp shin and cooper's like to lurk near bird feeders and snatch smaller birds, but that was the only day they came by.
