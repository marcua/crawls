Re: the southeast corner of Ditmars and 35th, the mystery corner, where every restaurant that comes in there inexplicably fails.  Under a few of the incarnations the place has been, one of the big signs that's always been placed in the front window has been "Under New Management" -- regardless of whatever different kind of restaurant it was.  I've always heard vague rumors of mafia associated with that property and wouldn't be surprised if there never really was any "new management."  All I know is, whatever's moved in there has always failed miserably, often after months of appearing almost devoid of customers when I've passed at various times of day and night.  It makes no sense -- that's prime real estate, and the re-design from clunky video store in the late 90s to whatever that next restaurant was -- was pretty good -- looks like an open, airy space, with a big seating area and a full bar.  I could only hope someone sees the light and opens up a really good Chinese restaurant -- in that part of Astoria, which is crying out for one?  It would be a goldmine.

Re: first music store in Astoria. It came and went.  I can't recall the name of the place, but it was a small walk-in CD/vinyl store on Ditmars, was it around 28th St?  Was there up through the late 1990s, but not sure when it opened?  Either near where or in the old space that became the tattoo parlor around there.  Which, no doubt, will one day make a fine tatoo laser-removal parlor.  But unless you were solely into hiphop and 90s soul, there wasn't much going on there. 

Anyone who wanted to open a small indie music store these days would be better off building a bonfire and shoveling his money into it.  These things may not exist in a few years, unless they branch out and figure out other ways to bring customers in that don't involve the purchase of CDs.  These days, even large chains either go under (Sam Goodys, HMV, and now possibly Tower) or struggle to survive -- dark days for brick-and-mortar music stores, which is a shame.  When I first moved to NYC in the late 80s, there were literaly dozens of indie record/CD stores in the Village, all thriving -- not anymore.

I noticed there's a new Japanese restaurant on, I think, Ditmars between 37th and 38th, south side of the street.  Haven't gone there, but the menu dropped on my doorstep the other day didn't look bad at all.
