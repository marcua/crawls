If you guys are looking for frames, you might want to try what I did - I ordered a bunch of (un-lensed) frames from eyeglasses.com, and tried them on at home. They really do have a huge selection. I always have trouble with eyeglass stores because I have a big head (I'm not kidding, like my skull is bigger than average, lol), so it was nice to be able to go online and look up glasses by size, type and shape. You might want to double check their current return policy, but when I ordered, they let you return up to 8 pairs without a restocking fee. Plus, they're nearby (Norwalk, CT), so I had my order the next day, and when I returned the frames I didn't want, the refund was processed before my credit card billing cycle closed, so I didn't have to carry a balance.

Then, when you decide what frames you want, you can either send them back and have eyeglasses.com put the lenses in, or you can bring it to a shop and have the lenses put in there. It might be a big more expensive that way, but it worked for me and my situation (bizarre ogre head and really bad vision, so I needed the special thin lenses, which are still pretty thick).

As far as eye docs go, I don't have any recommendations, sorry. I went to a Lenscrafters back home - the nice thing about them is that the eye docs that are connected to the stores are independent doctors, and they really couldn't care less if you buy glasses next door or not. (Or, at least that was the attitude of the one that I went to.)

My very good eye doc who moved out of the area recently, had always advised me against lasik because of the unknown long term consequences and the fact that my astigmatism probably could not be corrected with lasik. I know another person with very poor vision and other assorted eye problems and the doctor recommended lasik. Well, I went to Optimum Eye Care (formerly Cosmo's) over on 23rd Ave, and had a very positive experience.  I saw Dr. Laurie Vu, and she was terrific!  She spent time with me and explained everything she was doing, as I had to get my eyes dialated to see what the problem was.  She also had cool eye models and showed me where things are in the eye, when I asked.  Turns out I have a scratch on my right cornea, which is most likely causing my vision problems.  I guess it is quite common among contact lens wearers.  I have drops to help heal it.  My exam and eventual fitting is $75.  She's got some new fangled oxygen flowin' contacts to show me when I go in next time, which is cool.

So anyway, so far I give this place a big thumbs up for service and professionalism.  Plus, it's so close to my apartment!  Love it!
