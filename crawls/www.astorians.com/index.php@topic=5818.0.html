OK, the Igloo thread actually gave me the idea to post this. 

I grew up all of my life around the Ditmars area.  A series of events led me to move to the Broadway area, Flushing, back to Broadway and now back to Ditmars.  I noticed a lot of new eatieries and what not that I have not had the pleasure to check out and I'm not sure how they cater to strollers/high chairs. 

I've always wanted to go into Igloo but the look of it, per their store front, made it look really small and I couldn't fathom wheeling in a stroller.  I've only now read that they have a back room.  Last Stop didn't look too accomodating and other places along 31st Street looked particularly small, as well. 

Does anyone know of restaurants that DO NOT cater to children (i.e. space for strollers and high-chair accomodations)?  I'm guessing it'd be easier to state those that do not rather than those that do...but either answers are welcomed!  Thanks in advance.   :-)

Re: Kid Friendly Eateries?
« Reply #3 on: April 04, 2007, 12:26:24 AM »
Igloo is great for kids!  They've got high chairs available and the back room isn't necessarily a must.  They love kids there.

Neptue is also good and often has kids.

Telly's Taverna is kid friendly and I think most of the Greek restaurants in the area seem to appreciate having kids around.

Cup is also a great place for kids.  We've had a couple of Astoria Dad's Group meetups there and they're very friendly and love the kids.  Lots of space, too.

I think the main thing is to judge the size and if you can fit a high chair (or if they have them).  Then it's usually better to go on the early side of whatever meal you're going for.  Diners are much emptier at 10:00 or so on the weekends when the younger, single folks are still in bed recovering and parents have already been up for a couple hours with their kids.  Dinner at 5 or 6 usually means a relatively empty restaurant and no one to mind if your kid decides to act up.

Hope this helps!  I'll try and remember more places as we go to them.

Oh, we went to Dillinger's for brunch one time and they were great, too.

And Sparrow has a lot of kids in it on Saturday and Sunday at brunch time (12-5).
