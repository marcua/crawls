I was about to respond to the post about the Italian/Greek deli and its new feline employee Panda, but decided this deserves a thread of its own.

Panda is an adopted kitten, after the former store cat Tiger passed away on Christmas last year. The owners paid around $1,000 to have emergency surgery for Tiger, and were especially upset wondering if the holiday caused any delay for his treatment, something like that.

Anyway I love that store for the simple fact that they're cat lovers and don't worry about scaring potential customers away because {{gasp}} there's a CAT near the food!!!  :roll:

WHen the Othello Deli on 24th Ave. moved half a block away to a larger, newer store, Romeo the cat was suddenly absent. I asked about him, and the cashier lamented that the owner didn't want Romeo to be seen in the new store, and was banished to the basement. The clerk said he was "depressed" and I was really bothered by this, asking about Romeo everytime I stopped in. Finally, he's allowed upstairs now and then, sometimes even sits in one of the chairs at the corner table.  :)

Somewhere near Broadway there's a diner that has a cat named Blueberry who keeps to the back- was only in there once so I don't know much more about him/her.

It would be wonderful if every store employed a cat, as it would improve customer relations (mostly), keep rodents and roaches at bay, and give otherwise homeless cats a home. There are too many strays around here.
