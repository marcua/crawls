Title: Re: refinishing furniture
Post by: Lilybell on March 16, 2015, 11:08:33 AM
OK, back with my report!  I had two different quarts of white chalk paint; the brands are Annie Sloan and Renaissance (I was able to buy Renaissance on Amazon). Both were $35 a quart.

I preferred the Renaissance brand, but they are almost the same to be honest. Either would have been fine. I was surprised at the consistency - it was like sour cream or greek yogurt and was the first time in my life I was actually tempted to eat paint. It looked delicious! It was so thick and I barely needed to use any paint (I did two coats). I'm going to do one more coat but I could easily have just done two. I think the Annie Sloan caused more brush marks than Renaissance but after the first coat you don't see them so it doesn't really matter.

I am now a believer - I don't think you need to sand or prime when using this (but I had primed the item I painted because I wasn't expecting to use chalk paint). I was really impressed by the coverage. The finish is very matte. They recommend waxing and buffing afterwards but I did some research and am going to use polycrylic instead (I would like a bit of sheen if possible). 

Tip: Do not use a roller. Use a brush with natural bristles (manufacturer recommends this as well).
