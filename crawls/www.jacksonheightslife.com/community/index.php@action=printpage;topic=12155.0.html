Title: Re: where to buy meat?
Post by: Grabey on July 03, 2014, 08:09:32 AM
Yes, the Greenmarket is great. It's pricey, for sure, but not that different from what you'd pay for similar meat (e.g., grass-fed) at Whole Foods. I've been getting beef from Sun Fed beef for a couple of years now and the quality is outstanding. There are also duck, turkey, pork, seafood, and sometimes chicken vendors at the Greenmarket. Most of the meat vendors are there year-round. The only potential downside is that it requires some planning (can't just run down the street the day you decide to make burgers!).

Title: Re: where to buy meat?
Post by: Perkalator1 on July 03, 2014, 03:51:25 PM
We've been buying meat from the Sun/Grass Fed farmer's market vendor for the past year and have enjoyed the clean taste of their meats.  They will also take special requests.  I ordered beef tongue and about 20 steaks for a large barbecue.  The only thing about the Farmer's Market is that you can't get chicken there.

As for a butcher, I've stopped in the Boina Roja butcher (next to the restaurant) and ended up not buying anything.  It smelled a little too gamely for me.  For a legit butcher shop I've frequented International Meat Market in Astoria, they are fantastic. It's a little pricey but they have good meats and have some items that are hard to find (e.g., slab bacon, whole rabbit, fresh beef liver cut to order, lamb shoulder & ribs, D'Artagnan foie gras, excellent souvlaki skewers, etc.).  They even grind meat for you on the spot.  Get there early and get a number, otherwise you'll be waiting a while.  PS, they are closed on Sundays.

Title: Re: where to buy meat?
Post by: Lilybell on November 29, 2017, 11:14:59 AM
I buy from the Sun/Grass-fed guy at the Farmer's Market - he's cheaper than the grass-fed stuff at the grocery stores and I've liked everything I've gotten. He also has one of those cards where you get holes punched for everything you buy and then you'll get a free pound of ground beef after the card is filled.

I also buy meat from Fresh Direct - they have a lot of organic and grass-fed options and their prices are the same as the greenmarket or a bit lower.  I think the quality is much better than at the grocery stores because Fresh Direct uses local sources. The grass-fed options at FoodTown are ridiculously overpriced.
